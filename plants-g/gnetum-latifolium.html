Gnetum latifolium

Gnetum latifolium, commonly known as the broadleaf gnetum, is a gymnosperm native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its alternate, simple, and often palmate leaves that can be quite large. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers and has a unique reproductive system involving both male and female cones. Its bark is thick and fibrous, and it is sometimes used in traditional medicine.

Gnetum latifolium plays a role in tropical ecosystems as a source of food and habitat for various organisms.
