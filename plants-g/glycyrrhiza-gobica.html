Glycyrrhiza gobica

Glycyrrhiza gobica, commonly known as the Persian licorice, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect stem and pinnately compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by pods containing several seeds. Glycyrrhiza gobica is valued for its roots, which are rich in glycyrrhizin, a compound used in traditional medicine.

Its growth habit and chemical composition make it an important species in both ecological and pharmacological contexts.
