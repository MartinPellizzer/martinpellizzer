Genista lydia subsp. rumelica

Genista lydia subsp.

rumelica, commonly known as the Lydian broom, is a shrub native to the eastern Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring.

The plant produces small, leathery pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and hardiness, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
