Gypsocallis villosa

Gypsocallis villosa, commonly known as the hairy gypsocallis, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its strap-like, grass-like leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in the spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.
