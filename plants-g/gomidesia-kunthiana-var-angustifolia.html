Gomidesia kunthiana var. angustifolia

Gomidesia kunthiana var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf gomidesia, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white or pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or mountainous habitats, thriving in cool, humid climates. It is a perennial shrub that can reach heights of up to 1 meter.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for horticultural study and conservation efforts.
