Gentiana subtilis

Gentiana subtilis, commonly known as the slender gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, rocky habitats and has lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture. Its flowers are tubular with five lobes and are often visited by pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Gentiana subtilis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and ecological benefits.
