Glycosmis longifolia

Glycosmis longifolia, commonly known as the longleaf gooseberry, is a small evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Rutaceae and is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves and small, fragrant flowers. The plant produces berries that are typically yellow or orange when ripe, containing a high concentration of bioactive compounds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in lowland forests and along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is cultivated in some regions for both horticultural and pharmaceutical purposes.
