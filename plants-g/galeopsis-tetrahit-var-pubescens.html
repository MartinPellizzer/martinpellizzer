Galeopsis tetrahit var. pubescens

Galeopsis tetrahit var. pubescens, commonly known as hairy hedge nettle, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and opposite leaves that are usually ovate to lanceolate in shape. The plant produces small, pale blue or purple flowers arranged in spikes, which bloom from late spring to early summer. It thrives in disturbed soils and can be found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas.

Galeopsis tetrahit var. pubescens is often used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported calming and sedative properties.
