Gyptis pinnatifida var. canescens

Gyptis pinnatifida var. canescens, commonly known as the silverleaf gyptis, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in a fine, silvery pubescence, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in dense, terminal clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive and ornamental species within the Asteraceae family.
