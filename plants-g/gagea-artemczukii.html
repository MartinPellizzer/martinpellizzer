Gagea artemczukii

Gagea artemczukii, commonly known as the Armenian spring beauty, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, grass-like foliage and clusters of white or pinkish flowers. The plant produces small, rounded bulbs that store nutrients for the next growing season. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade, making it a suitable candidate for woodland gardens.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native range.
