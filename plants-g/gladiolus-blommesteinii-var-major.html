Gladiolus blommesteinii var. major

Gladiolus blommesteinii var. major, commonly known as the giant gladiolus, is a striking ornamental plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, sturdy stems that can reach heights of up to 2 meters, supporting large, showy flowers in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant features narrow, sword-like leaves that are arranged in a fan shape, contributing to its elegant appearance. Its flowers are typically arranged in a spike, with each bloom having three petals and three sepals, forming a distinctive sword-like shape.

This variety is prized for its vibrant colors and is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value.
