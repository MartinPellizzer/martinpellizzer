Gentiana dichotoma

Gentiana dichotoma, commonly known as the forked gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a slightly serrated edge.

Gentiana dichotoma is part of the Gentianaceae family, known for its showy, blue flowers and medicinal properties in some species.
