Gymnocalycium borthii var. viridis

Gymnocalycium borthii var. viridis, commonly known as the green-tipped moon cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of South America.

It is a member of the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems that are typically green in color. The plant produces yellow flowers during the warmer months, which are often followed by small, spherical fruits. Its succulent nature allows it to store water efficiently, making it well adapted to dry environments.

This variety is prized for its unique appearance and relatively easy care requirements, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.
