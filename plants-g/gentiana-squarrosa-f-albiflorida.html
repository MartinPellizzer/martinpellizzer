Gentiana squarrosa f. albiflorida

Gentiana squarrosa f. albiflorida, commonly known as the whiteflower gentian, is a striking perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family, characterized by its vibrant blue or white flowers. The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils and cool, moist environments. Its flowers are tubular with a white corolla, contrasting with the blue-green bracts that surround them.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique and showy blooms.
