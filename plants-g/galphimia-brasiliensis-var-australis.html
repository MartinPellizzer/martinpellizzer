Galphimia brasiliensis var. australis

Galphimia brasiliensis var. australis, commonly known as the Brazilian flame flower, is a species native to South America.

It is a climbing shrub that produces vibrant red or orange tubular flowers, which are highly attractive to hummingbirds and other pollinators. The plant has compound leaves with ovate to lanceolate leaflets and is typically found in tropical and subtropical regions. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

The flowers bloom throughout the year, adding continuous color to gardens in warm climates.
