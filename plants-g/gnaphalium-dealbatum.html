Gnaphalium dealbatum

Gnaphalium dealbatum, commonly known as the silverleaf cudweed, is a low-growing perennial plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It features slender, hairy stems and small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has finely divided, silver-gray leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. It is often found in disturbed soils, along roadsides, and in open woodland areas.

Gnaphalium dealbatum is known for its aromatic properties and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
