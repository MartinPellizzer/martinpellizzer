Geranium brasiliense

Geranium brasiliense, commonly known as the Brazilian geranium, is a flowering plant native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Geraniaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that range in color from pink to red. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub with palmate leaves that are lobed and often hairy. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its blooming period extends from spring to autumn, adding vibrant color to gardens throughout the growing season.
