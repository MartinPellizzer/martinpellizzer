Galarhoeus pekinensis f. maritimus

Galarhoeus pekinensis f. maritimus, commonly known as the coastal form of the Peking holly, is a variant of the Peking holly native to coastal regions of China.

It is a evergreen shrub characterized by its dense, leathery foliage and small, elliptical leaves. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils along coastal areas, showing adaptability to salt-laden air and strong winds. Its flowers are inconspicuous, and it produces small, red berries that are a food source for birds.

This form is valued for its ornamental qualities and resilience in harsh coastal environments.
