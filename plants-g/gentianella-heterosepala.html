Gentianella heterosepala

Gentianella heterosepala, commonly known as the narrowleaf gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a hairy surface that helps retain moisture.

Gentianella heterosepala is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
