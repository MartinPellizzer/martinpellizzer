Garrya salicifolia

Garrya salicifolia, commonly known as the silverleaf clethra, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, willow-like leaves that are covered in fine, silvery hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in clusters during the spring, which are followed by cylindrical, woody fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping.

Garrya salicifolia is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators and its adaptability to various environmental conditions.
