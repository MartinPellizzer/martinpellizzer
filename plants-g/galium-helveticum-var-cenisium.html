Galium helveticum var. cenisium

Galium helveticum var. cenisium, commonly known as the alpine bedstraw, is a low-growing perennial herb native to mountainous regions of Europe.

It features slender stems that are often prostrate or ascending, covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers in clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, lanceolate, and have a slightly hairy texture.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and rocky slopes.
