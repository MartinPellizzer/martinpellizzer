Geraniospermum adulterinum

Geraniospermum adulterinum, commonly known as the false geranium, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Geraniaceae, which includes species known for their showy flowers and often succulent leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its flowers are usually pink or red, with five petals and prominent stamens, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

However, it is sometimes confused with true geraniums due to similar appearance, leading to its common name.
