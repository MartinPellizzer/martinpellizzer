Gonystylus reticulatus

Gonystylus reticulatus, commonly known as the reticulate ironwood, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, dark brown wood with a distinctive reticulated pattern on the surface. The plant belongs to the Dipterocarpaceae family and is known for its slow growth and long lifespan. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a rough texture.

The tree plays an important ecological role in its native habitats, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
