Gahnia setifolia var. xanthocarpa

Gahnia setifolia var. xanthocarpa, commonly known as the yellow-fruited tussock grass, is a perennial grass species native to Australia.

It is characterized by its tall, tufted growth habit with long, slender leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces distinctive yellow to orange spikelets that are clustered at the tips of the stems. It thrives in wetland environments and is often found in swampy areas or along the edges of water bodies.

Its botanical features make it an important component of the ecological systems in its native habitat.
