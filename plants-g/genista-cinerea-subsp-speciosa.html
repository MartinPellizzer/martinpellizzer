Genista cinerea subsp. speciosa

Genista cinerea subsp.

speciosa, commonly known as the showy broom, is a shrub native to the western United States. It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and clusters of small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring.

The plant has small, leathery leaves and thrives in well-drained soils, often found in dry, sunny habitats. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping.

The subspecies speciosa is distinguished by its more showy and larger flowers compared to the typical form of Genista cinerea.
