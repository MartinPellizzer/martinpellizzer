Gypsophila trichopoda

Gypsophila trichopoda, commonly known as the silky gypsophila, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, fluffy flower heads that resemble small clouds, which are composed of numerous tiny white or pale pink petals. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun, making it a hardy species well adapted to arid conditions. Its stems are often hairy, giving it the common name "silky gypsophila," and it blooms during the summer months.

This plant is often used in ornamental gardening for its ethereal appearance and is also valued in traditional medicine in some regions.
