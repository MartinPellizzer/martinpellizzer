Gentiana olivieri var. aucheri

Gentiana olivieri var. aucheri, commonly known as the Alpine gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky slopes, thriving in cool, moist environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, while its stems are upright and often bear the showy inflorescence.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in high-altitude ecosystems.
