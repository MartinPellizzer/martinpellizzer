Gentianella maclarenii

Gentianella maclarenii, commonly known as Maclaren's gentian, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Gentianaceae and is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky areas, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are usually narrow and arranged in a basal rosette, while its flowers bloom in late summer.

Gentianella maclarenii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
