Gladiolus callistus

Gladiolus callistus, commonly known as the purple gladiolus, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its striking, often violet or purple, sword-shaped leaves. The plant produces tall, erect stems that bear clusters of colorful, showy flowers with three petals and three sepals. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated for its beauty and used in floral arrangements.
