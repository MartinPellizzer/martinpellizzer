Germainia grandiflora

Germainia grandiflora, commonly known as the grandiflora germainia, is a flowering plant native to specific regions in the Mediterranean.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant typically features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in a fine, silvery down. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Germainia grandiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
