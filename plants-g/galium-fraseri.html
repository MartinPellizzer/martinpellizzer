Galium fraseri

Galium fraseri, commonly known as Fraser's bedstraw, is a flowering plant native to the United States, particularly found in the eastern and central regions.

It is a low-growing perennial herb that typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features slender, ascending stems covered in fine hairs, and its leaves are opposite, ovate to elliptic in shape. It produces small, white or pale pink flowers in whorled clusters along the stems, which bloom from late spring to early summer.

Galium fraseri is often found in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and streambanks.
