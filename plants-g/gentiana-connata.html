Gentiana connata

Gentiana connata, commonly known as the Sierra gentian, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as meadows and forest understories. It has lance-shaped leaves and produces showy, tubular flowers that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Gentiana connata is a member of the Gentianaceae family, known for its vibrant and often blue or purple flowers.
