Galeopsis × bontei

Galeopsis × bontei, commonly known as the hybrid hedge nettle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Lamiaceae, which includes many aromatic plants such as mint and sage. The plant is characterized by its square stems, opposite leaves, and small, clustered flowers that are typically pale purple or blue. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, along roadsides, and in woodland edges.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
