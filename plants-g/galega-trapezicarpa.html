Galega trapezicarpa

Galega trapezicarpa, commonly known as the narrow-leaf goat's beard, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in racemes, followed by elongated, bean-like pods. Galega trapezicarpa is adapted to grow in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open woodland or grassland habitats.

It is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
