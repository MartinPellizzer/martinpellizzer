Glycosmis stenocarpa

Glycosmis stenocarpa, commonly known as the narrow-fruit gooseberry, is a small evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, round berries that are typically green when immature and turn red when ripe. These berries are edible and have a tart flavor, often used in local cuisines.

Glycosmis stenocarpa is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
