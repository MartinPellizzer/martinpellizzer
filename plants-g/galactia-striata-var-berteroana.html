Galactia striata var. berteroana

Galactia striata var. berteroana, commonly known as the Mexican milkweed, is a flowering plant native to parts of Mexico and Central America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and pod-like fruits. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its milky sap, which is toxic to some animals, is a distinguishing feature of the genus Galactia.

This species is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to thrive in arid environments.
