Grevillea synapheae subsp. pachyphylla

Grevillea synapheae subsp.

pachyphylla, commonly known as the thick-leaved grevillea, is a shrub native to Western Australia. It is characterized by its dense, leathery foliage and vibrant red or orange flowers that appear in clusters.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it well-suited to arid and semi-arid environments. Its leaves are thick and fleshy, adapted to conserve water in its natural habitat.

The species is part of the Proteaceae family, known for its showy floral displays and ecological importance to local wildlife.
