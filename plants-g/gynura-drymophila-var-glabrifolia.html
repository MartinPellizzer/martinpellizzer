Gynura drymophila var. glabrifolia

Gynura drymophila var. glabrifolia, commonly known as the velvetleaf gynura, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, velvety hairs, giving it a distinctive texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by spherical, spiky fruits. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated as a houseplant or in gardens.
