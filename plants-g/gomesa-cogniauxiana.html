Gomesa cogniauxiana

Gomesa cogniauxiana, commonly known as the Madagascar orchid, is a rare and striking orchid native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its unique, intricate flower structure with a prominent, spurred lip. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its foliage consists of broad, leathery leaves that help it retain moisture in its native habitat.

Due to its rarity and specific ecological requirements, it is considered a conservation concern.
