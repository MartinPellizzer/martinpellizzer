Geranium hookerianum

Geranium hookerianum, commonly known as Hooker's geranium, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces showy, five-petaled flowers in shades of pink, red, and purple. The plant typically blooms during the warmer months, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are usually heart-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture.

Geranium hookerianum is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ease of care.
