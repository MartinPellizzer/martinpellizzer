Gymnocalycium kulhanekii

Gymnocalycium kulhanekii, commonly known as the Kulhanek's moon cactus, is a species of cactus native to Argentina.

It is characterized by its globular shape and short, ribbed stems that are typically covered in white to pale blue spines. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the warmer months, adding a striking contrast to its otherwise muted appearance. It is a succulent plant, storing water in its thick, fleshy stems to survive in arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is a popular choice for cactus enthusiasts and indoor gardening.
