Geum urbanum var. hirsutum

Geum urbanum var. hirsutum, commonly known as the rough wood avens, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features basal leaves that are palmately lobed and covered in fine, hairy textures. Its flowers are small, yellow, and borne in clusters at the ends of stems.

The species is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
