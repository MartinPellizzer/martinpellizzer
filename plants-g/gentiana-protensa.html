Gentiana protensa

Gentiana protensa, commonly known as the Alpine gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky habitats at high altitudes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a fine layer of hair, adapted to withstand cold temperatures.

Gentiana protensa is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens that mimic its natural environment.
