Galipea bracteata

Galipea bracteata, commonly known as the Brazilian rosewood, is a small evergreen tree native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is characterized by its dark, dense wood, which is highly valued for its tonal qualities in musical instruments. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape and glossy in appearance. It produces small, fragrant white flowers followed by dark blue berries.

Galipea bracteata is also noted for its ability to thrive in humid, tropical climates with well-drained soil.
