Galeopsis filholiana

Galeopsis filholiana, commonly known as filhol's horseweed, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, opposite leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in whorls along the stems. It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as fields and roadsides.

Galeopsis filholiana is often found in temperate climates and is known for its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties.
