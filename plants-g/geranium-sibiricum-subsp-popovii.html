Geranium sibiricum subsp. popovii

Geranium sibiricum subsp.

popovii, commonly known as Siberian geranium, is a perennial herb native to the temperate regions of Asia and Europe. It features upright, branching stems and heart-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface.

The plant produces clusters of small, five-petaled flowers in shades of pink or red, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens and borders.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in horticulture for its hardy nature and long flowering period.
