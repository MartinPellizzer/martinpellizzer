Gentianella buchtienii

Gentianella buchtienii, commonly known as the Armenian gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus.

It belongs to the family Gentianaceae and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered inflorescences.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
