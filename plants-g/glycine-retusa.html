Glycine retusa

Glycine retusa, commonly known as the sweet pea vine, is a climbing legume native to the southeastern United States.

It features trifoliate leaves and produces fragrant, spiral-shaped flowers in shades of pink, purple, and white. The plant is characterized by its vigorous growth habit and ability to climb using its tendrils. Its pods contain small, edible seeds that are rich in nutrients.

Glycine retusa is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its aesthetic appeal and pleasant fragrance.
