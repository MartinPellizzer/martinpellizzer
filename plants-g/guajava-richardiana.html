Guajava richardiana

Guajava richardiana, commonly known as the red guava, is a tropical fruit-bearing plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its small, round fruits with a deep red skin and sweet, aromatic pulp. The plant produces fragrant white flowers that are typically clustered in terminal inflorescences. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a slightly serrated margin.

The fruit is often used in culinary applications and is valued for its nutritional content and flavor.
