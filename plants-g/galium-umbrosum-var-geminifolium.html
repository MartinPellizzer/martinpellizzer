Galium umbrosum var. geminifolium

Galium umbrosum var. geminifolium, commonly known as the twin-leaf wood-sorrel, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It features opposite, heart-shaped leaves that often appear in pairs, giving it its characteristic "twin-leaf" appearance. The plant produces small, white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and stream banks.

Its foliage is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
