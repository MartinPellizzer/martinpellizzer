Gentiana hebenstreitioides

Gentiana hebenstreitioides, commonly known as the Alpine gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky slopes, thriving in cool, moist environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a fine layer of silky hair, while its flowers bloom in late summer.

Gentiana hebenstreitioides is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens with well-drained soil and partial shade.
