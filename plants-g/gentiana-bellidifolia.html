Gentiana bellidifolia

Gentiana bellidifolia, commonly known as the bellflower gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine habitats, preferring moist, well-drained soils. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often covered in a fine layer of white hairs.

Gentiana bellidifolia is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and ecological benefits.
