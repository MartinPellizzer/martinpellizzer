Gentiana quaterna

Gentiana quaterna, commonly known as the four-leaf gentian, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have a bluish-green hue.

The four-leaf gentian is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
