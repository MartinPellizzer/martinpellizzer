Glia pilulosa

Glia pilulosa, commonly known as the bead tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive bead-like fruits that form in clusters along the branches. The plant has small, ovate leaves that are typically arranged oppositely on the stems. Its flowers are small and white, often appearing in clusters at the base of the leaves.

The fruits, which are edible when ripe, have a sweet and tangy flavor, making them popular in local cuisines.
