Gymnocalycium quehlianum var. calochlorum

Gymnocalycium quehlianum var. calochlorum, commonly known as the yellow moon cactus, is a flowering cactus native to South America.

It is characterized by its globular, ribbed stem that is typically yellow or green in color. The plant produces small, showy flowers in shades of yellow and orange during the summer months. It is a succulent that stores water in its thick, fleshy stems, making it drought-tolerant.

This variety is prized for its vibrant color and is often cultivated as a decorative plant.
