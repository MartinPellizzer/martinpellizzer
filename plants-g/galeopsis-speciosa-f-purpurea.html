Galeopsis speciosa f. purpurea

Galeopsis speciosa f. purpurea, commonly known as purple hedge nettle, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Lamiaceae.

It is characterized by its purple-tinged leaves and small, clustered flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded areas such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are hairy and have a distinctive heart-shaped base.

This variant is notable for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and unique appearance.
