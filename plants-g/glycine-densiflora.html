Glycine densiflora

Glycine densiflora, commonly known as the denseflower soybean, is a leguminous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow. The plant produces cylindrical pods containing several round, flat seeds. Glycine densiflora is often grown for its nutritional value, as the seeds are a good source of protein.

It thrives in warm climates and is adapted to a variety of soil types.
