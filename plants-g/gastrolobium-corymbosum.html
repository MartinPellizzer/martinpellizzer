Gastrolobium corymbosum

Gastrolobium corymbosum, commonly known as the common hakea, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its woody stems and compound leaves. The plant produces yellow or orange flowers in dense, spherical clusters, which are highly attractive to pollinators. It is well adapted to dry conditions and is often found in sandy or gravelly soils.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is a popular choice for landscaping in arid regions.
