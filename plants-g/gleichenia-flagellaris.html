Gleichenia flagellaris

Gleichenia flagellaris, commonly known as the flagellate fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Gleicheniaceae.

It is characterized by its long, narrow fronds that are typically divided into segments, giving it a distinctive, delicate appearance. The plant is native to various regions in the Southern Hemisphere, including parts of South America, Africa, and Australia. Its rhizomes are often covered in a layer of fine, hair-like structures, which contribute to its common name.

Gleichenia flagellaris thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found growing on rocks or tree trunks in tropical and subtropical regions.
