Garcinia buchneri

Garcinia buchneri, commonly known as the African yellow wood, is a tropical shrub native to Central and Southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Clusiaceae and is characterized by its yellowish wood, which is highly valued for its durability and aesthetic qualities. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and can reach heights of up to 10 meters. Its leaves are opposite, elliptical, and have a smooth surface, while its flowers are small and clustered in panicles.

Garcinia buchneri is also noted for its medicinal properties, with some traditional uses in local remedies.
