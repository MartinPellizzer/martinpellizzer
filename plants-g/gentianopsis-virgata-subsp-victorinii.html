Gentianopsis virgata subsp. victorinii

Gentianopsis virgata subsp.

victorinii, commonly known as the Victorin's gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe. It belongs to the family Gentianaceae and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and branching.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
