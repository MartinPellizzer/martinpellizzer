Galium trifidum subsp. brevipes

Galium trifidum subsp.

brevipes, commonly known as narrowleaf bedstraw, is a low-growing perennial herb native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It typically features slender, branched stems covered in fine, white hairs.

The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters, which bloom from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have three lobes, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.
