Gladiolus imbricatus subsp. crispiflorus

Gladiolus imbricatus subsp.

crispiflorus, commonly known as the curlyflower gladiolus, is a perennial herb native to tropical regions of Africa. It is characterized by its tall, erect stems that can reach heights of up to 2 meters.

The plant produces dense clusters of showy, colorful flowers in shades of red, pink, and white. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and the inflorescence consists of tightly packed, recurved petals.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
