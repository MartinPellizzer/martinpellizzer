Gypsophila aretioides var. imbricata

Gypsophila aretioides var. imbricata, commonly known as the Armenian baby's breath, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus.

It is characterized by its delicate, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in dense, branched clusters. The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters and thrives in well-drained, sandy or rocky soils. Its foliage consists of narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs.

This species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often used in floral arrangements and garden landscapes.
