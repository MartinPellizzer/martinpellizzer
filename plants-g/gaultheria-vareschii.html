Gaultheria vareschii

Gaultheria vareschii, commonly known as the white-flowered wintergreen, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, leathery foliage and clusters of small, bell-shaped white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces dark blue to black berries that are edible and have a sharp, aromatic flavor. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in cloud forests and high-altitude grasslands.

Gaultheria vareschii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its winter interest and fragrance.
