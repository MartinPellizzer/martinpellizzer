Geissomeria nitida

Geissomeria nitida, commonly known as the white-leafed gooseberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its slender, arching branches and small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant produces edible berries that are typically yellow or orange, though they are not as widely cultivated as other members of the genus. Its leaves are finely toothed and often covered in a whitish bloom, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Geissomeria nitida is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing food for pollinators and wildlife.
