Graphorkis venosa

Graphorkis venosa, commonly known as the veined orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, veined leaves and delicate, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its roots are often mycorrhizal, forming symbiotic relationships with fungi to enhance nutrient uptake.

The flowers bloom in clusters, displaying a range of colors from white to pink, depending on the specific variety.
