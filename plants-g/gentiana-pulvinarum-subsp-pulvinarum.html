Gentiana pulvinarum subsp. pulvinarum

Gentiana pulvinarum subsp.

pulvinarum, commonly known as the Alpine gentian, is a perennial herb native to the alpine regions of Europe. It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a hairy texture.

It is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
