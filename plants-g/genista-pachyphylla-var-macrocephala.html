Genista pachyphylla var. macrocephala

Genista pachyphylla var. macrocephala, commonly known as the giant broom, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny stems and yellow, fluffy flower clusters that bloom in late spring. The plant has adapted to arid environments with its thick, waxy leaves that reduce water loss. Its root system is deep and drought-resistant, allowing it to thrive in poor soils.

It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
