Garcinia sopsopia

Garcinia sopsopia, commonly known as the purple mangostin, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, dark purple bark and fragrant, white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces bright red or purple, fleshy fruits encased in a thick, segmented rind. The fruit's pericarp contains bioactive compounds, including xanthones, which have been studied for their potential health benefits.

Garcinia sopsopia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and medicinal properties.
