Gesneria guazumifolia

Gesneria guazumifolia, commonly known as the Guazumifolia Gesneria, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Gesneriaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and showy, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers, which bloom in shades of red, orange, and yellow, attract pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees.

Gesneria guazumifolia is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
