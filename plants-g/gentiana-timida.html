Gentiana timida

Gentiana timida, commonly known as the timid gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue or purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often covered with fine hairs.

Gentiana timida is noted for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies for various ailments.
