Gaultheria caparoensis

Gaultheria caparoensis, commonly known as the Caparoensis wintergreen, is a species of evergreen shrub native to the Andean regions of South America.

The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale pink, which bloom in the spring. Its berries are dark red to black and contain aromatic seeds, often used in traditional medicine.

Gaultheria caparoensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in acidic, well-drained soils.
