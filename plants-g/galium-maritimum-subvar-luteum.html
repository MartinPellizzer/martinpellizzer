Galium maritimum subvar. luteum

Galium maritimum subvar. luteum, commonly known as the yellow bedstraw, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial herb with slender, often hairy stems that form dense mats. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate, elliptical, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in maritime environments.
