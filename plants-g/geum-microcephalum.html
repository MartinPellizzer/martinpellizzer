Geum microcephalum

Geum microcephalum, commonly known as dwarf Canadian buttercup, is a low-growing perennial plant native to North America.

It features bright yellow flowers with a distinctive cup-shaped structure, typically blooming in early spring. The plant has palmately divided leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a soft texture. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
