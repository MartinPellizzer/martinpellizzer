Galium grayanum

Galium grayanum, commonly known as the grayanum bedstraw, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial with slender, often hairy stems that form dense mats. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, oval-shaped, and have a slightly hairy surface.

Galium grayanum is often found in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and stream sides.
