Gymnocalycium nigriareolatum var. simoi

Gymnocalycium nigriareolatum var. simoi, commonly known as the black-spotted moon cactus, is a succulent native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its globular shape and thick, fleshy stems covered in spiny areoles. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the warmer months, adding a striking contrast to its dark green foliage. Its unique appearance makes it a popular choice for collectors and enthusiasts of cacti.

Due to its slow growth and specific care requirements, it is often cultivated in controlled environments.
