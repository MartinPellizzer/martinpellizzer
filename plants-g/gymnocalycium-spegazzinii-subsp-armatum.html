Gymnocalycium spegazzinii subsp. armatum

Gymnocalycium spegazzinii subsp.

armatum, commonly known as the armate moon cactus, is a succulent native to the arid regions of Argentina. It is characterized by its globular, ribbed stem and a dense covering of spines, which provide protection against herbivores and intense sunlight.

The plant produces yellow flowers during the growing season, adding a striking contrast to its green foliage. Its thick, fleshy stems store water, allowing it to survive in harsh, dry environments.

This subspecies is well-suited for cultivation in xerophytic gardens due to its low maintenance requirements.
