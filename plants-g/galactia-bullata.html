Galactia bullata

Galactia bullata, commonly known as the bullate milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant produces pods that contain spherical seeds, which are often used in traditional herbal medicine. Galactia bullata thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing adaptations to drought and poor soil conditions.

Its ecological role includes providing forage for wildlife and contributing to soil stabilization in its native habitat.
