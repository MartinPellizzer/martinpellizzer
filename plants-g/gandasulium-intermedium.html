Gandasulium intermedium

Gandasulium intermedium, commonly known as the silverleaf starflower, is a flowering plant native to the temperate regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, star-shaped white petals and a short, upright stem. The leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a fine, silvery down, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, often found in woodland edges and moist meadows.

The plant blooms in early spring, producing fragrant flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
