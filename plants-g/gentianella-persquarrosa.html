Gentianella persquarrosa

Gentianella persquarrosa, commonly known as the spotted gentian, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It typically grows in alpine and subalpine habitats, where it thrives in well-drained, rocky soils. The plant features a basal rosette of dark green, hairy leaves and produces striking blue or violet flowers with five petals. Its flowers are often visited by pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Gentianella persquarrosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in high-altitude ecosystems.
