Gentiana antecedens

Gentiana antecedens, commonly known as the early gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in early summer, often before many other gentian species. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and rocky slopes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, while its stems are usually unbranched and upright.

Gentiana antecedens is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and ecological importance.
