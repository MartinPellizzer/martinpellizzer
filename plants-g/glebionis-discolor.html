Glebionis discolor

Glebionis discolor, commonly known as the whiteleaf dandelion, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and herbaceous growth habit. The plant features finely divided, silvery-gray leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. It produces small, yellow flowers that develop into fluffy white seeds, typical of the dandelion genus.

Glebionis discolor is often found in disturbed soils and open areas, thriving in full sun.
