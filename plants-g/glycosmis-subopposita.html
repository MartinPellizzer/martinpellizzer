Glycosmis subopposita

Glycosmis subopposita, commonly known as the hairy tamarind, is a small evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves with opposite or sub-opposite arrangement. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by edible, sour fruits that are often used in local cuisines. Its fruit contains a single seed enclosed in a fibrous pulp, and it is often cultivated for both ornamental and culinary purposes.

Glycosmis subopposita is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal remedies.
