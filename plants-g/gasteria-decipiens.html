Gasteria decipiens

Gasteria decipiens, commonly known as the "false elephant's foot," is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features a thick, fleshy stem that resembles a swollen base, with narrow, grass-like leaves arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces striking, bell-shaped flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. It is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water once established.

Gasteria decipiens is popular in rock gardens and as a decorative indoor plant due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
