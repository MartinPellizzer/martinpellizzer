Galium mollugo var. umbrosum

Galium mollugo var. umbrosum, commonly known as slender bedstraw, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters and features opposite, linear to lanceolate leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom from late spring to early summer. Its flowers are usually borne on slender, ascending pedicels, and the plant has a fibrous root system.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and stream banks.
