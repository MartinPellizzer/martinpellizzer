Gymnema hirsutum

Gymnema hirsutum, commonly known as the Indian nettle or gymnema, is a climbing shrub native to the wetlands of India and Sri Lanka.

It is characterized by its opposite, heart-shaped leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant is notable for its ability to produce a milky sap that contains various bioactive compounds. It belongs to the Asclepiadaceae family and is often found in moist, tropical environments.

Gymnema hirsutum has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
