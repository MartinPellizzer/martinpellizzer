Gentianella euphorbiifolia

Gentianella euphorbiifolia, commonly known as the waxy gentian, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It features striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer, typically from August to October. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Its botanical interest lies in its unique floral structure and adaptability to arid environments.
