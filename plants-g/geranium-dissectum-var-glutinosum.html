Geranium dissectum var. glutinosum

Geranium dissectum var. glutinosum, commonly known as the sticky geranium, is a herbaceous perennial native to southern Africa.

It features slender, upright stems that bear opposite, palmately lobed leaves with a distinctive sticky texture. The plant produces clusters of small, pink to reddish-purple flowers during the summer months. Its foliage is often covered in a waxy, glandular coating that gives it its common name.

This species is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
