Gasteranthus epedunculatus

Gasteranthus epedunculatus, commonly known as the "garden lantern," is a striking tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that resemble lanterns, with a unique structure that includes a swollen base and a narrow tube. The plant typically grows as a herbaceous perennial, often found in shaded, humid environments. Its flowers are pollinated primarily by flies and other insects, which are attracted by its strong scent and color.

Gasteranthus epedunculatus is prized in horticulture for its ornamental value and unusual appearance.
