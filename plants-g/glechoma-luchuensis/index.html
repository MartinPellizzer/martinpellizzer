Glechoma Luchuensis

Glechoma luchuensis is a rare and lesser-known plant species native to specific regions in eastern Asia. It belongs to the mint family, known for its aromatic properties and medicinal uses. This plant is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and small, blue-pink flowers that bloom in early summer. Due to habitat loss and limited distribution, it is considered endangered and requires conservation efforts. Studying Glechoma luchuensis offers insights into plant adaptation and biodiversity in its native ecosystems.
