Genipa cinerea

Genipa cinerea, commonly known as the ash tree or white genipap, is a small to medium-sized tree native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually ovate to elliptic in shape. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by fruit that turns from yellow to red as it matures.

Genipa cinerea is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its unique foliage and potential medicinal properties.
