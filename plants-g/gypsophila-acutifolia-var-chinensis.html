Gypsophila acutifolia var. chinensis

Gypsophila acutifolia var. chinensis, commonly known as Chinese baby's breath, is a flowering plant native to China and parts of East Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, fluffy clusters of white or pale pink flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has slender, branched stems and small, narrow leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden borders and cut flower arrangements.

Its botanical name reflects its geographical origin and its distinctive, lace-like appearance.
