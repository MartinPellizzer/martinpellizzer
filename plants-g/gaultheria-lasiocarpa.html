Gaultheria lasiocarpa

Gaultheria lasiocarpa, commonly known as the silverleaf strawberry bush, is a species of evergreen shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its dense, silvery-white pubescent leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible and have a sweet, tart flavor. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in temperate rainforests.

Gaultheria lasiocarpa is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in horticulture.
