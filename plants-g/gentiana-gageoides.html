Gentiana gageoides

Gentiana gageoides, commonly known as the blue gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream sides. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and ecological benefits.
