Gayophytum racemosum var. caesium

Gayophytum racemosum var. caesium, commonly known as the golden flame cactus, is a succulent plant native to South America.

It features a branching stem structure with prominent, spiny areoles and produces clusters of bright yellow flowers. The plant has thick, fleshy stems that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. Its leaves are typically reduced to spines, and it blooms during the warmer months.

It is valued in horticulture for its vibrant color and unique appearance.
