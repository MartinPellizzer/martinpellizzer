Garuga floribunda var. gamblei

Garuga floribunda var. gamblei, commonly known as the Gambler's garuga, is a rare and striking shrub native to the mountainous regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny foliage and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth. Its unique appearance makes it a popular choice for botanical gardens and specialized collections.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered endangered and is protected in several conservation areas.
