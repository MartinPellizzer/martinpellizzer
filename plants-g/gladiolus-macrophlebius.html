Gladiolus macrophlebius

Gladiolus macrophlebius, commonly known as the large-veined gladiolus, is a herbaceous perennial native to South Africa.

It features tall, sturdy stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height, supporting large, showy flowers in shades of red, pink, and white. The leaves are long, narrow, and arranged in a fan-like pattern, contributing to its ornamental appeal. Its flowers are typically borne in clusters at the top of the stem, with three prominent petals and three sepals.

This species is valued for its vibrant blooms and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic and horticultural significance.
