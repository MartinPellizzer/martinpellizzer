Galium triflorum var. hoffmeisteri

Galium triflorum var. hoffmeisteri, commonly known as Hoffmeister's woodypine, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as hardwood forests and stream banks. The plant features slender, branching stems that are often covered with fine hairs. Its small, white flowers are clustered in umbels, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
