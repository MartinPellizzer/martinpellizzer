Garcinia microtropidiiformis

Garcinia microtropidiiformis, commonly known as the Malabar tamarind, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Western Ghats of India.

It belongs to the Clusiaceae family and is characterized by its elliptic, leathery leaves and yellow-orange flowers. The plant produces small, round, yellow fruits that are often used in traditional medicine. Its botanical features include a smooth, greyish bark and a dense canopy that provides habitat for various wildlife species.

Garcinia microtropidiiformis is valued for its ornamental appeal and medicinal properties.
