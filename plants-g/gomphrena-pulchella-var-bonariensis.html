Gomphrena pulchella var. bonariensis

Gomphrena pulchella var. bonariensis, commonly known as the bonariensis gomphrena, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and its ability to produce vibrant, spiky flower heads in shades of red, pink, and purple. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and is often cultivated as an ornamental in gardens due to its long-lasting blooms. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for borders and containers.

This variety is also valued for its drought tolerance and ability to attract pollinators.
