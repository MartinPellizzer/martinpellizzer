Gouldia axillaris var. microphylla

Gouldia axillaris var. microphylla, commonly known as the small-leaved gouldia, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit. The plant produces small, white or pink flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its foliage consists of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery pubescence.

It is well-suited for coastal gardens due to its tolerance of salty air and well-drained soils.
