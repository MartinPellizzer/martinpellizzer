Grastidium planum

Grastidium planum, commonly known as the flat-leaf grass.

It is a low-growing perennial plant characterized by its broad, flat leaves that lie close to the ground. The plant typically thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetland or marshy environments. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are usually green or brown and appear in clusters.

Grastidium planum plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small invertebrates.
