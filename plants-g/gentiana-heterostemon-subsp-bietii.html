Gentiana heterostemon subsp. bietii

Gentiana heterostemon subsp.

bietii, commonly known as the Biet's gentian, is a species of flowering plant in the family Gentianaceae. It is native to parts of Southeast Asia, including Vietnam and Laos, where it thrives in moist, shaded forest environments.

The plant is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers, which are typically arranged in a terminal raceme. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
