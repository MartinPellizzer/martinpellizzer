Galeola altissima

Galeola altissima, commonly known as the tall orchid, is a species of orchid native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often arranged in a spiral along the stem.

Galeola altissima is a herbaceous perennial that plays a role in supporting local pollinator populations.
