Gazania pechuelii

Gazania pechuelii, commonly known as the golden gazania, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its vibrant yellow to orange flowers with a central disk. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces showy blooms that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Gazania pechuelii is also known for its ability to tolerate drought conditions once established.
