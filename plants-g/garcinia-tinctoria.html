Garcinia tinctoria

Garcinia tinctoria, commonly known as the yellow mangostin, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers and red, fleshy fruits that contain a white, aromatic pulp. The plant belongs to the Clusiaceae family and is valued for its ornamental qualities as well as its medicinal properties. Its fruit is often used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.

The plant thrives in humid, warm climates and is commonly cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
