Gnaphalium grandiflorum

Gnaphalium grandiflorum, commonly known as the showy everlasting or sweet everlasting, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is native to the eastern and central United States, often found in open woods, meadows, and along roadsides. The plant produces small, clustered yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are aromatic, often used in perfumery and traditional medicine.

The plant is known for its ability to retain its color and form when dried, making it popular in floral arrangements.
