Grindelia hirsutula subsp. rubricaulis

Grindelia hirsutula subsp.

rubricaulis, commonly known as the red-stemmed gumweed, is a perennial herb native to the western United States. It is characterized by its hairy, gray-green foliage and distinctive red-tinged stems.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in dense, branching clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

Its botanical features make it an important species for pollinators and a subject of ecological study.
