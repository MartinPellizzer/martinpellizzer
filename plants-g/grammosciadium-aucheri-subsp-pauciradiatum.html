Grammosciadium aucheri subsp. pauciradiatum

Grammosciadium aucheri subsp.

pauciradiatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region. It features slender, upright stems that bear opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface.

The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue or violet, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental flowers make it a desirable addition to native plant collections.
