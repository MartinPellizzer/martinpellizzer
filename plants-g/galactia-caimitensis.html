Galactia caimitensis

Galactia caimitensis, commonly known as the caimito tree, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, contributing to its ornamental value. Its seeds are enclosed in long, slender pods and are often used in traditional medicine.

The tree is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for agricultural purposes.
