Gentiana marginata var. hugelii

Gentiana marginata var. hugelii, commonly known as the Hugel's gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central and Eastern Europe.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer and early autumn. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a waxy coating, which helps retain moisture.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
