Garrya flavescens subsp. congdonii

Garrya flavescens subsp.

congdonii, commonly known as Congdon's silverberry, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It is characterized by its small, silver-gray leaves that provide a striking visual contrast against its red or orange bark.

The plant produces yellow flowers in early spring, followed by small, edible berries that are a food source for various wildlife species. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or arid environments.

Its unique appearance and ecological importance make it a notable species in its native habitat.
