Genista cuspidosa

Genista cuspidosa, commonly known as the sharpbrush broom, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, spiny stems and small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant has compound leaves composed of several pairs of narrow, pointed leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant in coastal and rocky environments.

Genista cuspidosa is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to stabilize soil in erosion-prone areas.
