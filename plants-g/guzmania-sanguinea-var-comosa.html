Guzmania sanguinea var. comosa

Guzmania sanguinea var. comosa, commonly known as the Crimson Flame Bromeliad, is a striking epiphytic plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It features vibrant red bracts that surround its inflorescence, giving it a fiery appearance. The plant's rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves collects rainwater, creating a microhabitat for small organisms. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires high humidity and well-draining soil.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and ease of care in indoor settings.
