Gerascanthus moluccanus

Gerascanthus moluccanus, commonly known as the Moluccan geranium, is a flowering plant native to the Molucca Islands in Indonesia.

It belongs to the family Geraniaceae and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, five-petaled flowers. The plant typically blooms in the spring and summer, producing vibrant red or pink flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are usually heart-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture, while the stems are often reddish in color.

Gerascanthus moluccanus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its visual impact.
