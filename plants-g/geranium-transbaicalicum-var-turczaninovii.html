Geranium transbaicalicum var. turczaninovii

Geranium transbaicalicum var. turczaninovii, commonly known as the Turczaninov's geranium, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features upright stems that bear opposite, palmately lobed leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of pink to purple, typically blooming in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in alpine meadows and rocky habitats.

Its botanical significance lies in its unique morphology and adaptation to high-altitude environments.
