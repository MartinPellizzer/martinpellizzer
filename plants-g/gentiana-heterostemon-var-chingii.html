Gentiana heterostemon var. chingii

Gentiana heterostemon var. chingii, commonly known as the Ching's gentian, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a hairy texture, while the stem is usually erect and branched.

This variant is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
