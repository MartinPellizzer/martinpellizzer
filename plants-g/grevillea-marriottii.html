Grevillea marriottii

Grevillea marriottii, commonly known as the red-stemmed grevillea, is a flowering plant native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its striking red stems and bright red or orange flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has narrow, linear leaves and is often found in rocky or sandy soils. It is a member of the Proteaceae family and is well adapted to dry, sunny conditions.

Grevillea marriottii is popular in gardens for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
