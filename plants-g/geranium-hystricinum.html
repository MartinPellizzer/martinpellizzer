Geranium hystricinum

Geranium hystricinum, commonly known as the humped geranium, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, humpbacked leaves and clusters of showy, pink or red flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It is often used in rock gardens due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance.

Geranium hystricinum is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
