Glochidion nervosum

Glochidion nervosum, commonly known as the veined glochidion, is a small evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves with prominent venation. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in cup-like structures called cyathia. It is also used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
