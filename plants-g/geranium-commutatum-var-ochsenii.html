Geranium commutatum var. ochsenii

Geranium commutatum var. ochsenii, commonly known as the Alpine geranium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. The plant features upright stems with opposite, palmately lobed leaves that are often hairy. Its flowers are usually pink or purple, appearing in clusters at the ends of the stems.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens or alpine settings.
