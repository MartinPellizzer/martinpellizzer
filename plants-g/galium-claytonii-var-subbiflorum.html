Galium claytonii var. subbiflorum

Galium claytonii var. subbiflorum, commonly known as Clayton's bedstraw, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features slender, branched stems that are often covered in fine hairs. Its small, white or pale pink flowers are arranged in clusters and bloom in late spring to early summer.

The leaves are opposite, ovate to lanceolate, and have a slightly hairy surface, contributing to its distinctive botanical appearance.
