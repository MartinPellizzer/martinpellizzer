Gamochaeta nigrevestis

Gamochaeta nigrevestis, commonly known as blackstem false fleabane, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. Its leaves are linear to lance-shaped, often covered in fine hairs, and its stems are usually dark green to blackish in color.

The flowers, which bloom in late summer, are yellow with white ray florets, attracting various pollinators like bees and butterflies.
