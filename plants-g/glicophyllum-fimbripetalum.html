Glicophyllum fimbripetalum

Glicophyllum fimbripetalum, commonly known as the fringed-leaf lichen, is a species of lichen that belongs to the family Pilocarpaceae.

It is characterized by its intricate thallus structure, which is typically crustose or foliose in form. The plant exhibits a symbiotic relationship between a fungus and an alga or cyanobacterium, which is essential for its survival. Its unique morphology and ecological niche make it an important indicator species for environmental health.
