Gentiana stylophora

Gentiana stylophora, commonly known as the blue gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often found in forests or rocky areas. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a fine layer of hair, which helps reduce water loss.

Gentiana stylophora is valued for its ornamental beauty and is also used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
