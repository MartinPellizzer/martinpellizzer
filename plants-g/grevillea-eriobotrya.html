Grevillea eriobotrya

Grevillea eriobotrya, commonly known as the coastal grevillea, is a shrub native to coastal regions of southern Australia.

It features striking, feather-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving them a velvety texture. The plant produces vibrant red or orange flowers in clusters, which are highly attractive to birds and other pollinators. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for coastal gardens.

Grevillea eriobotrya is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to withstand salt spray and strong winds.
