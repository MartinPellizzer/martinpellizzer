Galactia jussiaeana var. velutina

Galactia jussiaeana var. velutina, commonly known as the velvetleaf galactia, is a perennial legume native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its hairy, velvety stems and heart-shaped leaves with a soft texture. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pale green to white in color. Its fruit is a flat, dehiscent pod containing several seeds.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed areas.
