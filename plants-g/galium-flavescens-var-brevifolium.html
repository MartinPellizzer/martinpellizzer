Galium flavescens var. brevifolium

Galium flavescens var. brevifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wood-sorrel, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It features slender, ascending stems that are often branched and covered with fine hairs. The leaves are alternate, ovate to lanceolate, and typically have a prominent central vein. Small, yellowish-white flowers appear in clusters at the tips of the stems, and the plant produces capsule fruits that contain several seeds.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.
