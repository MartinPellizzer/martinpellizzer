Grastidium potamophilum

Grastidium potamophilum, commonly known as the riverbank grass, is a perennial herb native to wetland and riparian habitats.

It features slender, upright stems that can grow up to 1 meter in height, often with a reddish tint. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in dense clusters, which bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a slightly hairy texture.

Grastidium potamophilum plays an important role in stabilizing soil along riverbanks and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
