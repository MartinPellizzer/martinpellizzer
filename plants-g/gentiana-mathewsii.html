Gentiana mathewsii

Gentiana mathewsii, commonly known as the Mathews' gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of southern China.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family, known for its striking blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky slopes. Its flowers are tubular with a prominent blue corolla, often featuring a yellow or white throat.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
