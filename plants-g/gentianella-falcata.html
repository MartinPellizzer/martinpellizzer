Gentianella falcata

Gentianella falcata, commonly known as the narrowleaf gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the family Gentianaceae and is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often with a slightly hairy surface.

Gentianella falcata is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vivid color and ecological benefits.
