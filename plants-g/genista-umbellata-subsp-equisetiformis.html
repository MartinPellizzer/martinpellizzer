Genista umbellata subsp. equisetiformis

Genista umbellata subsp.

equisetiformis, commonly known as the bracken broom, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its slender, fern-like leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in late spring.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or coastal areas. Its growth habit resembles that of a fern, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental qualities and ability to stabilize soils in challenging environments.
