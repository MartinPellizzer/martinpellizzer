Gelidocalamus rutilans

Gelidocalamus rutilans, commonly known as the golden bamboo, is a species of grass native to the mountainous regions of southern China and northern Vietnam.

It is characterized by its tall, slender culms that can reach heights of up to 5 meters. The leaves are long and narrow, with a smooth, waxy surface that gives them a shiny appearance. This plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its unique golden hue during certain seasons makes it a distinctive and visually appealing plant in its natural habitat.
