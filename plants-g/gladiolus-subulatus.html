Gladiolus subulatus

Gladiolus subulatus, commonly known as the needle-leaved gladiolus, is a striking ornamental plant native to southern Africa.

It features narrow, sword-shaped leaves that give it its common name, and its flowers are typically red or pink with a distinctive, elongated shape. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders. It produces tall stems with clusters of showy blooms that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Due to its unique appearance and hardiness, it is a popular choice among gardeners seeking both beauty and resilience.
