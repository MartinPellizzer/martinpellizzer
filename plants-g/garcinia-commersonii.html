Garcinia commersonii

Garcinia commersonii, commonly known as the African mango or yellow mangosteen, is a tropical evergreen tree native to West Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, dark green leaves and produces small, yellow-orange fruits with a distinctive segmented rind. The fruit contains a single, large, white, fleshy pulp that is rich in nutrients and has a tangy, citrus-like flavor. The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and is also used in traditional medicine.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and agricultural contexts.
