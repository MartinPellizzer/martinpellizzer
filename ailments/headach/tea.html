11 Best Herbal Teas For Headach

Herbal teas for Headache are a type of non-caffeinated, medicinal beverage made from various plant extracts, used to alleviate and manage headache symptoms.

They can treat this ailment due to their natural anti-inflammatory and pain-relieving properties, which help to relax tense muscles and improve blood flow to the brain.

Some popular herbal teas used to treat headaches include Feverfew, which contains parthenolide that helps to reduce inflammation and prevent migraine attacks; Ginger, which has anti-inflammatory compounds that ease pain and nausea; Peppermint, which has a calming effect on the mind and body; Chamomile, which soothes and relaxes the muscles; Lavender, which reduces stress and promotes better sleep; and Willow Bark, which contains salicin, a compound similar to aspirin, that helps to reduce pain and inflammation.

By incorporating these herbal teas into one's daily routine, individuals can experience relief from headache symptoms and promote overall well-being.

1. Valeriana officinalis tea

Valeriana officinalis tea contains bioactive constituents such as valerenic acid, valeranone, and isovaleric acid, which have been found to possess analgesic and anti-inflammatory properties that help to alleviate headache pain.

This herbal tea also exhibits sedative and calming effects due to its ability to interact with the GABA receptors in the brain, thereby reducing stress and anxiety that can contribute to headaches.

To make this tea, simply steep 1-2 teaspoons of dried Valeriana officinalis root in a cup of boiling water for 5-7 minutes, then strain and drink.

For optimal relief, consume 1 cup of Valeriana officinalis tea 2-3 times a day, ideally before bedtime, as it can promote a restful night's sleep and help to reduce headache frequency and severity.

2. Melissa officinalis tea

Melissa officinalis tea contains bioactive constituents such as rosmarinic acid, apigenin, and luteolin that have anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties, which help to alleviate headache symptoms.

This herbal tea is known for its calming and soothing effects due to its high content of chamazulene, which is responsible for its anti-spasmodic and anti-anxiety properties.

To make Melissa officinalis tea, steep 1 teaspoon of dried Melissa leaves in a cup of boiling water for 5-7 minutes, then strain and let it cool.

To consume this tea for headache relief, drink it 2-3 times a day, taking a few sips at a time to allow the active constituents to take effect.

3. Ginkgo biloba tea

Ginkgo biloba tea contains bioactive constituents such as flavonoids, terpenoids, and bilobalide that help to treat headach ailments by improving blood circulation, reducing inflammation, and acting as a natural vasodilator.

This herbal tea possesses anti-oxidant, anti-inflammatory, and anti-spasmodic properties that help to alleviate headache symptoms and promote relaxation.

To make ginkgo biloba tea, steep 1 teaspoon of dried ginkgo biloba leaves in 8 ounces of boiling water for 5-7 minutes, then strain and let it cool.

To consume this tea for headache relief, drink 1-2 cups a day, ideally in the morning and evening, to promote relaxation and reduce headache frequency.

4. Zingiber officinale tea

Zingiber officinale tea contains a plethora of bioactive constituents, including gingerols and shogaols, which are responsible for its analgesic and anti-inflammatory properties, making it an effective remedy for treating headaches.

The properties of this herbal tea, such as its warming and soothing effects, help to relax the muscles, reduce pain and inflammation, and promote overall well-being.

To make this tea, simply steep one-inch piece of fresh ginger in boiling water for 5-7 minutes, then strain and add honey to taste.

To consume this tea, drink one to two cups per day, ideally before bedtime, to help alleviate headache symptoms and promote a restful night's sleep.

5. Lavandula angustifolia tea

Lavandula angustifolia tea contains bioactive constituents such as linalool, linalyl acetate, and camphor, which have anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties that help to alleviate headaches.

The calming and relaxing properties of this herbal tea, attributed to the presence of linalool, also contribute to its headache-relieving effects by promoting a sense of tranquility and reducing muscle tension.

To make this tea, steep 1 teaspoon of dried lavender flowers in 1 cup of boiling water for 5-7 minutes, then strain and let it cool.

To treat a headache, consume 1 cup of this tea 2-3 times a day, ideally before bedtime, and consider combining it with a warm bath or meditation to enhance its relaxing effects.

6. Camellia sinensis tea

Camellia sinensis tea contains bioactive constituents such as catechins, theaflavins, and thearubigins, which have been found to exhibit analgesic and anti-inflammatory properties that help to alleviate headache symptoms.

The anti-oxidant properties of this tea can also help to reduce oxidative stress and inflammation in the body, which may contribute to the development of headaches.

To make Camellia sinensis tea for headache relief, steep one teaspoon of dried leaves in a cup of boiling water for 3-5 minutes, then strain and discard the leaves.

To consume this tea for headache relief, drink it 2-3 times a day, ideally before meals, to help reduce headache frequency and severity.

7. Passiflora incarnata tea

Passiflora incarnata tea contains bioactive constituents such as flavonoids, alkaloids, and glycosides, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

The tea's analgesic, anti-inflammatory, and sedative properties help to alleviate headache symptoms by reducing pain and inflammation in the body.

To make Passiflora incarnata tea, steep one teaspoon of dried herb in a cup of boiling water for 5-7 minutes, then strain and drink as needed.

To consume the tea, drink 1-2 cups per day, ideally before bedtime, to take advantage of its sedative and pain-relieving effects.

8. Foeniculum vulgare tea

Foeniculum vulgare tea contains bioactive constituents such as apiol and ligustilide, which possess anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties that help to alleviate headache symptoms.

The antispasmodic and sedative properties of this herbal tea also contribute to its effectiveness in treating headache, as it can help to relax tense muscles and calm the nervous system.

To make foeniculum vulgare tea, simply steep 1-2 teaspoons of dried seeds in a cup of boiling water for 5-7 minutes, then strain and let cool.

To consume this tea for headache relief, drink 1-2 cups a day, or as needed, and consider adding honey or lemon to taste for optimal results.

9. Matricaria chamomilla tea

Matricaria chamomilla tea contains bioactive constituents such as apigenin, luteolin, and bisabolol, which are known to have anti-inflammatory, antispasmodic, and analgesic properties that help to relieve headache symptoms.

The soothing and calming effects of this herbal tea can also be attributed to its ability to reduce anxiety and stress, which are common triggers for headaches.

To make this tea, steep 1 teaspoon of dried Matricaria chamomilla flowers in 1 cup of boiling water for 5-7 minutes, then strain and let it cool.

To consume this tea, drink it 2-3 times a day, ideally before bedtime to help regulate sleep patterns and alleviate morning headaches.

10. Achillea millefolium tea

Achillea millefolium tea contains bioactive constituents such as sesquiterpene lactones, flavonoids, and phenolic acids, which are responsible for its anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties, making it an effective natural remedy for treating headaches.

The tea's antispasmodic and vasodilatory properties help to relax blood vessels and reduce muscle tension, providing relief from headache symptoms.

To make Achillea millefolium tea, steep 1-2 teaspoons of dried flowers in 1 cup of boiling water for 5-7 minutes, then strain and let cool.

To treat a headache, consume 1-2 cups of the tea 2-3 times a day, ideally between meals, and consider adding honey or lemon to taste.

11. Silybum marianum tea

Silybum marianum tea contains bioactive constituents such as silymarin, flavonoids, and alkaloids, which are responsible for its potential to treat headaches.

The anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties of this herbal tea help to reduce pain and alleviate symptoms associated with headaches.

To make this tea, combine 1 tablespoon of dried Silybum marianum flowers with 1 cup of boiling water, let it steep for 5-7 minutes, then strain and drink.

Consuming 1-2 cups of this tea per day may help to provide relief from headaches, although it is recommended to consult with a healthcare professional before using it as a treatment.
