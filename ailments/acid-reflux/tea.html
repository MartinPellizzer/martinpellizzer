11 Best Herbal Teas For Acid Reflux

Herbal teas for Acid reflux are a type of natural remedy made from various herbs, plants, and spices that help alleviate the symptoms of acid reflux, a condition characterized by the backflow of stomach acid into the esophagus.

These teas can treat acid reflux by reducing inflammation, relaxing the muscles in the esophagus, and improving digestion, thereby preventing the stomach acid from flowing back up into the esophagus.

Examples of herbal teas that can help treat acid reflux include ginger tea, which soothes the digestive system and reduces inflammation, peppermint tea, which relaxes the muscles in the esophagus and reduces symptoms of heartburn, chamomile tea, which calms the digestive system and reduces stress, licorice root tea, which soothes the mucous membranes in the stomach and esophagus, and fennel tea, which eases digestion and reduces bloating.

Additionally, other herbal teas such as dandelion root tea, slippery elm tea, and marshmallow root tea can also help treat acid reflux by reducing inflammation, improving digestion, and protecting the mucous membranes in the stomach and esophagus.

1. Zingiber officinale tea

Zingiber officinale tea contains several bioactive constituents, including gingerols and shogaols, which are responsible for its anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties that help to treat acid reflux ailment.

The properties of this herbal tea, such as its carminative and anti-spasmodic effects, help to reduce inflammation and alleviate symptoms of acid reflux.

To make this tea, steep 1-2 teaspoons of dried ginger root in 1 cup of boiling water for 5-7 minutes, then strain and let it cool.

To consume this tea for treating acid reflux, drink it 1-2 times a day, ideally before meals, to help reduce symptoms and prevent acid reflux episodes.

2. Glycyrrhiza glabra tea

Glycyrrhiza glabra tea contains bioactive constituents such as glycyrrhizin, flavonoids, and saponins that help to alleviate symptoms of acid reflux, including heartburn and indigestion.

The anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties of this herbal tea help to soothe the esophageal mucosa and reduce inflammation in the digestive tract, thereby alleviating the symptoms of acid reflux.

To make this tea, boil 1 teaspoon of dried Glycyrrhiza glabra root in 1 cup of water for 5-7 minutes, then strain and drink.

Consuming this tea 2-3 times a day, preferably after meals, can help to reduce symptoms of acid reflux and promote digestive health.

3. Foeniculum vulgare tea

The tea's properties, including its carminative and antispasmodic effects, help to reduce inflammation and ease digestive discomfort associated with acid reflux.

To make foeniculum vulgare tea, combine 1 teaspoon of dried seeds with 1 cup of boiling water, steep for 5-7 minutes, and strain before consumption.
