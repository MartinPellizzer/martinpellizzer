fibroid

intro

Fibroids are non-cancerous growths that develop in or around the uterus, causing a range of symptoms including heavy menstrual bleeding, pelvic pain, and abdominal discomfort. These growths can vary in size and number, and while they are often asymptomatic, they can significantly impact a woman's quality of life if left untreated. Fibroids are typically treated with medication, surgery, or other minimally invasive procedures to alleviate symptoms and prevent complications.
