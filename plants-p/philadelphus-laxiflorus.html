Philadelphus laxiflorus

Philadelphus laxiflorus, commonly known as the looseflower phlomoides, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the family Rosaceae and is characterized by its showy, fragrant yellow flowers that appear in late spring to early summer. The plant has opposite, ovate to elliptical leaves that are often hairy beneath, and it produces small, red or orange fruits in the fall. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is adaptable to a variety of soil types, making it a versatile garden plant.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long-lasting blooms and attractive foliage.
