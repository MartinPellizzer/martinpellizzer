Paspalum radiatum

Paspalum radiatum, commonly known as silvergrass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and silver-gray foliage, which gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are often tinged with red or purple, adding visual interest. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas or along roadsides.

Paspalum radiatum is valued for its ornamental qualities and is used in landscaping and erosion control.
