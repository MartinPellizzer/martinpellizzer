Piper schwackei

Piper schwackei, commonly known as the Schwaack's pepper vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to the rainforests of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper plants like black pepper. The plant produces small, green berries that turn red when ripe, containing the alkaloid piperine, which is responsible for its spicy flavor. Piper schwackei thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil to grow successfully.

Its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
