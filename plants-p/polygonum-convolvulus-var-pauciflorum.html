Polygonum convolvulus var. pauciflorum

Polygonum convolvulus var. pauciflorum, commonly known as the lesser bindweed, is a flowering plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is a member of the Polygonaceae family, characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows as a prostrate or trailing herb, often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides. Its flowers are usually pink or purple, and it produces small, three-angled berries that contain the seeds.

The variety pauciflorum is distinguished by its reduced flower clusters compared to the typical species.
