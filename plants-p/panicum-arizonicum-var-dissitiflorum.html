Panicum arizonicum var. dissitiflorum

Panicum arizonicum var. dissitiflorum, commonly known as Arizona grass or desert dropseed, is a low-growing, clump-forming perennial grass native to the southwestern United States.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces delicate, fluffy seed heads that are often white or pale green. The plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is drought-tolerant, making it well-suited for arid environments. It is often used in native plant gardens and for erosion control due to its hardiness and aesthetic appeal.

Its flowers are typically borne in loose, panicle-like clusters, contributing to its ornamental value.
