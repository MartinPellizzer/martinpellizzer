Peponium lagenarioides

Peponium lagenarioides, commonly known as the bottle gourd or calabash, is a tropical vine belonging to the Cucurbitaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, hollow, bottle-shaped fruits that can grow up to several feet in length. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces yellow, fragrant flowers that are typically unisexual. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

The fruit is used in various culinary and traditional applications across different cultures.
