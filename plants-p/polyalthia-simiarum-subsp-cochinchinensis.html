Polyalthia simiarum subsp. cochinchinensis

Polyalthia simiarum subsp.

cochinchinensis, commonly known as the Vietnamese silk tree, is a species native to Southeast Asia. It is a deciduous tree that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions.

The plant has compound leaves with elongated leaflets and produces small, fragrant flowers in clusters. Its fruit is a woody capsule that contains several seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
