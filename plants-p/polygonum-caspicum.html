Polygonum caspicum

Polygonum caspicum, commonly known as the Caspian knotweed, is a perennial herb native to Central Asia.

It typically grows in moist, temperate environments such as riverbanks and wet meadows. The plant features upright stems that are often reddish in color, and its leaves are alternate, ovate, and covered in fine hairs. Its small, greenish-white flowers are clustered in dense, spike-like inflorescences.

Polygonum caspicum is known for its ability to thrive in nutrient-rich soils and its potential use in traditional medicine.
