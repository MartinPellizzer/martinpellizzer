Pandanus utilis var. stephanocarpa

Pandanus utilis var. stephanocarpa, commonly known as the swamp pandanus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and long, narrow leaves that often droop downward. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences with yellowish-white flowers, which are typically followed by fibrous, spadix-like fruits. It thrives in wet, marshy environments and is often found near water bodies.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in some cultures.
