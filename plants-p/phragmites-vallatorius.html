Phragmites vallatorius

Phragmites vallatorius, commonly known as the common reed, is a tall, perennial grass that thrives in wetland environments.

It is characterized by its hollow, jointed stems and narrow, blade-like leaves. This plant plays a vital role in stabilizing shorelines and filtering water due to its extensive root system. It is often found in marshes, ponds, and along the edges of lakes.

Phragmites vallatorius is also known for its ability to spread rapidly through both seeds and rhizomes.
