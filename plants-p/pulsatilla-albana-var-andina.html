Pulsatilla albana var. andina

Pulsatilla albana var. andina, commonly known as the Andean pasqueflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the high altitudes of the Andes.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its showy, showy white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has a rosette of basal leaves that are hairy and often covered in fine white pubescence. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in alpine gardens.
