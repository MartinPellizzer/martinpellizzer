Ptelea subintegra

Ptelea subintegra, commonly known as the white-leaf hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its smooth, pale gray bark and oval, deciduous leaves that are often white-veined and glossy. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters, followed by red, fleshy berries that are edible for birds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in moist woodland areas.

Ptelea subintegra is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in supporting local wildlife.
