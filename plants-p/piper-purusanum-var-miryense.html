Piper purusanum var. miryense

Piper purusanum var. miryense, commonly known as the Malabar pepper or wild long pepper, is a climbing vine native to the wet tropical regions of India and Sri Lanka.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its slender, aerial stems and opposite, ovate leaves with a glossy surface. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into elongated, curved berries containing numerous tiny seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in dense forest understories.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for both traditional medicine and ecological studies.
