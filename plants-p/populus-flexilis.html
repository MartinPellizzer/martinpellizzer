Populus flexilis

Populus flexilis, commonly known as the trembling aspen.

It is a deciduous tree native to North America, characterized by its slender, often multi-stemmed form and heart-shaped leaves that flutter in the wind. The plant belongs to the Salicaceae family and is known for its rapid growth and ability to colonize disturbed areas. Its bark is smooth and gray, while its flowers are small and inconspicuous, appearing in catkins.

Populus flexilis plays an important role in forest ecosystems, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
