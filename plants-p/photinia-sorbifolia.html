Photinia sorbifolia

Photinia sorbifolia, commonly known as the Chinese photinia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its attractive, bright red to orange-red new growth. The plant features simple, alternate, ovate to elliptical leaves that are usually glossy and dark green. It produces small white flowers in clusters, followed by red or orange berries that are edible for birds.

Photinia sorbifolia is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various soil types.
