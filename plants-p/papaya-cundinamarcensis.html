Papaya cundinamarcensis

Papaya cundinamarcensis, commonly known as the Colombian papaya, is a tropical fruit-bearing plant native to South America, particularly Colombia.

It belongs to the Caricaceae family and is characterized by its large, lobed leaves and yellow-orange, fleshy fruits. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth. Its fruit is rich in vitamins and enzymes, making it a valuable crop for both nutrition and commercial use.

The plant's botanical features include a distinct stem with a rough, ridged surface and a short, fibrous root system.
