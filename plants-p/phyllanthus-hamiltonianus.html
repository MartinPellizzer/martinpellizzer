Phyllanthus hamiltonianus

Phyllanthus hamiltonianus, commonly known as the Indian cheesewood, is a flowering plant native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is a shrub that typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in arid and semi-arid regions. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by small, round fruits. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a slightly hairy texture.

The plant is also known for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional Ayurvedic medicine.
