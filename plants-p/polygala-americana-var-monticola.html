Polygala americana var. monticola

Polygala americana var. monticola, commonly known as mountain milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, pink to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats such as slopes and cliffs, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a bluish-green color with a slightly hairy texture.

The species is noted for its ecological importance as a nectar source for pollinators and its role in stabilizing soil in arid environments.
