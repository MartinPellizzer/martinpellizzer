Potamogeton acifolius

Potamogeton acifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaved pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in the Northern Hemisphere.

It has long, slender leaves that are typically arranged in whorls around the stem, and they are often covered in a fine, waxy coating. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually found in clusters near the water's surface. It thrives in shallow, slow-moving waters and plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.

Its rapid growth can also contribute to water clarity by competing with algae for nutrients.
