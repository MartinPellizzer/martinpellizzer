Persicaria lapathifolia subsp. hypanica

Persicaria lapathifolia subsp.

hypanica, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hogweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

The plant features slender, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. Its flowers are small, clustered in dense spikes, and are typically greenish-white in color.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to pollinators.
