Potamogeton densus subsp. oppositifolius

Potamogeton densus subsp.

oppositifolius, commonly known as the dense pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in North America. It is characterized by its dense, ribbon-like leaves that are often arranged in opposite pairs along the stem.

The plant typically grows in shallow, slow-moving waters such as ponds, lakes, and streams. Its flowers are small and clustered in spike-like inflorescences, which are usually submerged or just at the water's surface.

This subspecies plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
