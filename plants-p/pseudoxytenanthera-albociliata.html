Pseudoxytenanthera albociliata

Pseudoxytenanthera albociliata, commonly known as the white-fringed tamarind, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its pod-like fruits and nitrogen-fixing ability. The plant is notable for its compound leaves and showy, yellow flowers that attract pollinators. Its seeds are encased in a hard, woody pod, and the tree can grow up to 20 meters tall.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in tropical regions.
