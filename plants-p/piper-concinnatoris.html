Piper concinnatoris

Piper concinnatoris, commonly known as the slender pipevine, is a climbing plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Piperaceae, which includes numerous species of flowering plants. The plant is characterized by its small, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of tiny, greenish-white flowers. It produces small, fleshy fruits that contain seeds, typical of many Piper species.

The plant is often found in humid, shaded environments such as rainforests.
