Philadelphus henryi var. cinereus

Philadelphus henryi var. cinereus, commonly known as the cinereous mock orange, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It features fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant has a compact growth habit with lance-shaped leaves that are often slightly hairy. It prefers well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight.

Its ornamental value comes from its showy blossoms and its ability to thrive in a variety of garden settings.
