Piptadenia pterosperma

Piptadenia pterosperma, commonly known as the Brazilian pequi tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy yellow flowers. The tree produces distinctive, woody fruits that contain a single large seed surrounded by a fleshy, aromatic pulp. Its bark and leaves have been used in traditional medicine for their potential health benefits.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in its native habitats.
