Polystichum hololepis

Polystichum hololepis, commonly known as the broadleaf holly fern, is a species of fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, leathery, evergreen fronds that can reach lengths of up to 1 meter. The fronds are bipinnate, with closely spaced, oblong pinnae that give the plant a dense, lush appearance. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and is commonly cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
