Pimelea suaveolens var. typica

Pimelea suaveolens var. typica, commonly known as the sweet-smelling pimelea, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is a shrub that typically grows in heathlands and dry open forests, characterized by its small, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of yellow flowers, which are often covered in fine, glandular hairs. It is known for its pleasant, spicy fragrance, which is released when the flowers are disturbed.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting native insect populations.
