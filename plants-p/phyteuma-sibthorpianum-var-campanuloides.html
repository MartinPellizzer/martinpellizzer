Phyteuma sibthorpianum var. campanuloides

Phyteuma sibthorpianum var. campanuloides, commonly known as the Campanuloides Phyteuma, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of bell-shaped, often blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in open grasslands and along riverbanks, preferring well-drained soils. It blooms during the late spring to early summer season, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and botanical collections.
