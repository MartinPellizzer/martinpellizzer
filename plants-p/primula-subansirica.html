Primula subansirica

Primula subansirica, commonly known as the Kashmir Primrose, is a perennial herb native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It typically grows in rocky and alpine environments, thriving in cool, moist conditions. The plant features basal rosettes of lanceolate leaves and produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. It blooms from late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species in its native range.
