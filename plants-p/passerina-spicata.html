Passerina spicata

Passerina spicata, commonly known as the spike pasqueflower, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its early spring blooming period, with showy, yellow-orange flowers that appear before its leaves. The plant has a basal rosette of lance-shaped leaves and produces a terminal spike of flowers that can reach up to 30 cm in height. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold climates.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
