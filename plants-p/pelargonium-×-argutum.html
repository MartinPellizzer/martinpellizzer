Pelargonium × argutum

Pelargonium × argutum, commonly known as the roughleaf geranium, is a hybrid plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its fuzzy, serrated leaves and clusters of showy, five-petaled flowers that range in color from pink to red. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub, reaching heights of up to 3 feet. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

Pelargonium × argutum is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
