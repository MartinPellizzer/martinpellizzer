Polygala monspeliaca subvar. simplex

Polygala monspeliaca subvar. simplex, commonly known as the simple polygala, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, branching stems that are often hairy, with small, pale green to yellowish leaves. The flowers are typically arranged in loose clusters, with individual blooms having a distinctive, spurred shape. It prefers well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, often growing in rocky or dry habitats.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and flowering structure.
