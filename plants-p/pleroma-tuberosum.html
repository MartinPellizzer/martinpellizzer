Pleroma tuberosum

Pleroma tuberosum, commonly known as the tuberous pleroma, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its tuberous roots, which store nutrients and water, allowing it to survive in dry conditions. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a rosette pattern, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Pleroma tuberosum is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic value and is relatively easy to grow in well-drained soil.
