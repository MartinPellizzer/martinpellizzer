Pyrus serotina var. stapfiana

Pyrus serotina var. stapfiana, commonly known as the Chinese white pear, is a variety of pear native to China.

It is characterized by its white, sweet fruit with a crisp texture and mild flavor. The plant typically grows as a small to medium-sized tree, reaching heights of up to 10 meters. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and ovate with a smooth margin.

The flowers are white and borne in clusters, appearing before the leaves in early spring.
