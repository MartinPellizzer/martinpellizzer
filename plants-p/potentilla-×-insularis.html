Potentilla × insularis

Potentilla × insularis, commonly known as the island cinquefoil, is a hybrid shrub native to the British Isles.

It typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm and features narrow, dark green leaves with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces clusters of small, yellow flowers in late spring to early summer, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is often cultivated in gardens and natural habitats.
