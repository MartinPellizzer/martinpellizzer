Piarophyla cordifolia

Piarophyla cordifolia, commonly known as the heartleaf poinciana, is a species of flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its striking, bioluminescent flowers that emit a soft glow at night, a feature that aids in attracting pollinators. The plant has heart-shaped leaves, which give it its common name, and it thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant, showy blooms.

Piarophyla cordifolia is also notable for its role in traditional medicine in some regions.
