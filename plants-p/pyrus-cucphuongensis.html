Pyrus cucphuongensis

Pyrus cucphuongensis, commonly known as Cúc Phường pear, is a species native to Vietnam.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its deciduous nature, producing small, yellow fruits. The plant typically grows in mountainous regions, thriving in temperate climates with well-drained soil. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a serrated margin, while its flowers are white and clustered in cymes.

The fruit is edible and has been traditionally used in local cuisines and traditional medicine.
