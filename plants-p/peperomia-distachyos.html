Peperomia distachyos

Peperomia distachyos, commonly known as the silver peperomia, is a species of flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact, upright growth habit and glossy, waxy leaves that often display a silvery or variegated pattern. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or reddish in color, arranged in clusters along the stems. It is a popular houseplant due to its low maintenance requirements and ability to thrive in indirect light.

Peperomia distachyos prefers well-draining soil and moderate humidity, making it suitable for indoor cultivation.
