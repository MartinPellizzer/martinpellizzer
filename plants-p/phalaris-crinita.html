Phalaris crinita

Phalaris crinita, commonly known as silky grass or curly hair grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and long, silky, hair-like leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, pale blue or greenish flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas.

Due to its ornamental appeal, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
