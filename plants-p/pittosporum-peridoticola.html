Pittosporum peridoticola

Pittosporum peridoticola, commonly known as the golden pittosporum, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of 3 to 6 meters. The plant features bright yellow flowers that are clustered in dense, spherical inflorescences, which are often covered in a whitish, waxy coating. Its leaves are lance-shaped, leathery, and have a distinctive dark green color with a silvery underside.

Pittosporum peridoticola is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its seasonal display of golden blooms.
