Piper changuinolanum

Piper changuinolanum, commonly known as the Changuinola pepper, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to Central America and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, berry-like fruits. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are usually ovate or elliptical with a smooth or slightly serrated margin.

The plant is valued for its use in traditional medicine and as a spice due to the presence of volatile oils in its leaves and fruits.
