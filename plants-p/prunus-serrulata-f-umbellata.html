Prunus serrulata f. umbellata

Prunus serrulata f. umbellata, commonly known as the Japanese cherry blossom or some varieties may be referred to as the weeping cherry, is a cultivar of the Japanese cherry tree.

It is characterized by its weeping habit, with cascading branches that create a distinctive, ornamental appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant pink or white flowers in the spring, which are highly valued for their aesthetic appeal. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

This cultivar is often planted in gardens and parks for its ornamental value and cultural significance.
