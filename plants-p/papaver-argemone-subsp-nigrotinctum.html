Papaver argemone subsp. nigrotinctum

Papaver argemone subsp.

nigrotinctum, commonly known as the black-stained poppy, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region. It features distinctive yellow flowers with a dark red or black center, which bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and covered in a waxy coating. Its seeds are small, oval, and contain a high oil content, making them useful for both culinary and industrial purposes.

The species is adapted to dry, rocky soils and is often found in disturbed habitats.
