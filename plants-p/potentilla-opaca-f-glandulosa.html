Potentilla opaca f. glandulosa

Potentilla opaca f. glandulosa, commonly known as the glandular potentilla, is a flowering plant belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, upright growth habit and small, dark green leaves that are often covered in glandular hairs. The plant produces clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or borders.

This variety is noted for its compact size and ornamental value in horticultural settings.
