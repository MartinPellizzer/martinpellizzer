Prasophyllum bagoense

Prasophyllum bagoense, commonly known as the Bago leek orchid, is an endemic species native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive leek-like appearance. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, often in montane forests. Its flowers are usually greenish or yellowish, with a delicate structure typical of orchids.

Prasophyllum bagoense plays a role in its local ecosystem and is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and limited distribution.
