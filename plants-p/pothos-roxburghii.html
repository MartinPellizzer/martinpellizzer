Pothos roxburghii

Pothos roxburghii, commonly known as the devil's ivy, is a vigorous climbing vine native to Southeast Asia.

It features heart-shaped, waxy leaves that are typically variegated with shades of green and yellow. The plant is known for its adaptability, thriving in low-light conditions and requiring minimal water. It produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers, though it is primarily cultivated for its ornamental foliage.

Pothos roxburghii is a popular houseplant due to its resilience and ability to purify indoor air.
