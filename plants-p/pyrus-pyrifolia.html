Pyrus pyrifolia

Pyrus pyrifolia, commonly known as the Japanese pear, is a deciduous tree native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in spring. The tree produces pome fruits, which are typically sweet and juicy, with a thin, edible skin. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, though it can tolerate partial shade.

The plant is valued both for its ornamental beauty and its culinary uses.
