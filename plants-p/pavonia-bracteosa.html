Pavonia bracteosa

Pavonia bracteosa, commonly known as the velvetleaf or redroot, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features showy, often pink or red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has palmately lobed leaves and is characterized by its hairy, velvety texture. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed areas.

Pavonia bracteosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
