Penstemon fruticiformis var. spiciformis

Penstemon fruticiformis var. spiciformis, commonly known as the spiciform penstemon, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its woody stems and clusters of tubular, two-lipped flowers that bloom in shades of blue to purple. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to full sun conditions. Its foliage consists of narrow, gray-green leaves that are aromatic and often covered in fine hairs.

This variety is valued in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental appeal.
