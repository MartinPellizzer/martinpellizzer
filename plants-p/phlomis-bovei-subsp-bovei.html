Phlomis bovei subsp. bovei

Phlomis bovei subsp.

bovei, commonly known as the Bove's plumeless tarragon, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of the Balkans. It belongs to the family Lamiaceae, which includes many aromatic plants known for their medicinal and culinary uses.

The plant features upright, branched stems covered in fine hairs and lance-shaped leaves with a slightly aromatic scent. Its flowers are clustered in dense spikes, typically appearing in shades of purple or pink, and bloom during the summer months.

It is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential medicinal properties.
