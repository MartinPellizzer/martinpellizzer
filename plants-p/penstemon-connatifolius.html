Penstemon connatifolius

Penstemon connatifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaved beardtongue, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a perennial species characterized by its upright growth habit and spikes of tubular, two-lipped flowers. The leaves are narrow and grass-like, often covered in fine hairs, while the flowers range in color from blue to purple. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

This plant is also valued for its ecological role, providing nectar for pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
