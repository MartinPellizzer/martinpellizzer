Physalis virginiana var. virginiana

Physalis virginiana var. virginiana, commonly known as the common ground cherry, is a flowering plant native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its hairy, spiny calyces that enclose the fruit, which resembles small tomatoes. The plant produces yellow-orange berries that are edible when fully ripe. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed areas.

The species is valued for its ornamental value and culinary use in certain cuisines.
