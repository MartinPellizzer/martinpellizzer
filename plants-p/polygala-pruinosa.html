Polygala pruinosa

Polygala pruinosa, commonly known as the powdered milkweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its delicate, branched stems and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant produces distinctive, bead-like fruits that contain a single seed each. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
