Pseudotaxus chienii

Pseudotaxus chienii, commonly known as the Chinese yew, is a rare and ancient coniferous tree native to China.

It belongs to the family Taxaceae and is characterized by its slow growth and longevity, often living for centuries. The plant has a unique morphology, featuring a straight trunk and dense, scale-like foliage. It produces small, reddish seeds enclosed in a fleshy, orange aril, which is an adaptation for animal dispersal.

Due to its rarity and ecological significance, it is considered a valuable species in botanical research and conservation efforts.
