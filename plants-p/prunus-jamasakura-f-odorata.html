Prunus jamasakura f. odorata

Prunus jamasakura f. odorata, commonly known as the fragrant Japanese cherry.

This cultivar is a variation of the Japanese cherry blossom, characterized by its delicate pink flowers that emit a subtle, sweet fragrance. It is typically found in temperate regions and thrives in well-drained soil with partial sunlight. The plant is prized for its ornamental value, particularly during spring when it blooms profusely.

Its botanical features include small, oval leaves and a compact growth habit, making it suitable for ornamental gardens and urban landscapes.
