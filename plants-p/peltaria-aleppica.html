Peltaria aleppica

Peltaria aleppica, commonly known as the Aleppo pellitory, is a small, herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows in rocky or dry soils and has a compact, cushion-like growth habit. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small and white or pale blue, arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems.

Peltaria aleppica is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
