Pelargonium × deburghae

Pelargonium × deburghae, commonly known as the Burghauser's geranium, is a hybrid plant that exhibits a range of botanical characteristics.

It is a member of the Geraniaceae family and is native to South Africa, where it thrives in diverse habitats. The plant typically features rounded or lobed leaves and produces clusters of small, five-petaled flowers in shades of pink, red, or white. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to adapt to various growing conditions.

Pelargonium × deburghae is also valued for its resilience and low maintenance requirements in garden settings.
