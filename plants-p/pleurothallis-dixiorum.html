Pleurothallis dixiorum

Pleurothallis dixiorum, commonly known as the red-tipped pleurothallis, is a species of orchid native to the cloud forests of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and striking, often brightly colored flowers that typically feature a red or orange tip. The plant produces small, lance-shaped leaves that are usually arranged in a rosette pattern. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts for its unique and vibrant floral display.
