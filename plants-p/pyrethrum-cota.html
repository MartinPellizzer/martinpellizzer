Pyrethrum cota

Pyrethrum cota, commonly known as the African feverfew, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its daisy-like yellow flowers and hairy, lobed leaves. The plant is known for its aromatic properties and has been used traditionally for its medicinal benefits. Pyrethrum cota thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy perennial in suitable climates.

Its botanical features make it both ornamental and ecologically valuable in native habitats.
