Phaseolus lambertianus

Phaseolus lambertianus, commonly known as the Lambertian runner bean, is a herbaceous annual plant native to Central America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its climbing habit and compound leaves. The plant produces clusters of white or pale purple flowers that develop into elongated pods containing edible seeds. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Its botanical features make it a valuable crop for both culinary and agricultural purposes.
