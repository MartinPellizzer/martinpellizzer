Panax tridactylum

Panax tridactylum, commonly known as the Japanese ginseng, is a perennial plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Araliaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by red berries. Its roots are the primary part used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The botanical features of Panax tridactylum make it a valuable species in both horticulture and pharmacology.
