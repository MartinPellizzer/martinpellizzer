Prunus tenuifolia

Prunus tenuifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, elongated leaves that are typically less than 2 inches in length. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers in early spring, followed by edible, sweet-tart berries in summer. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Prunus tenuifolia is also valued for its hardy nature and ability to adapt to various soil conditions.
