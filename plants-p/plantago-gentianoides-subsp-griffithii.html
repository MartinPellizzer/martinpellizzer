Plantago gentianoides subsp. griffithii

Plantago gentianoides subsp.

griffithii, commonly known as Griffith's plantain, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia, particularly India and Nepal. It is a perennial herb with erect, branched stems and lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces small, blue or purple flowers arranged in dense, spike-like clusters. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky areas.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for ecological studies and traditional medicine.
