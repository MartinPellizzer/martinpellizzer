Potamogeton crispus var. macrorrhynchus

Potamogeton crispus var. macrorrhynchus, commonly known as the broad-beaked pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in Europe and Asia.

It has long, narrow leaves that are often ribbed and slightly curled, with a distinctive broad, rounded base. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are clustered in spike-like structures, typically found near the water surface. It plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.

Its presence can indicate good water quality and is often found in slow-moving or still waters.
