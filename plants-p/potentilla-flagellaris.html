Potentilla flagellaris

Potentilla flagellaris, commonly known as the flagellate cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial shrub that typically reaches a height of about 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features small, five-petaled yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are compound, consisting of five oval-shaped leaflets, and are often covered in fine hairs.

Potentilla flagellaris is adapted to rocky and arid environments, showcasing its resilience to harsh climatic conditions.
