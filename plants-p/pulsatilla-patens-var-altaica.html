Pulsatilla patens var. altaica

Pulsatilla patens var. altaica, commonly known as the high mountain pasqueflower, is a perennial herb native to the alpine regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, pinkish-purple flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has a short, thick rhizome and hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are typically basal. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
