Piperia maritima

Piperia maritima, commonly known as salt marsh piperia, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of the United States.

It is a member of the Apiaceae family, characterized by its umbellate inflorescence and aromatic foliage. The plant thrives in brackish or saline wetlands, often found in marshes and tidal areas. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, while its flowers are small and white, arranged in clustered umbels.

Piperia maritima plays an important ecological role in stabilizing coastal soils and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
