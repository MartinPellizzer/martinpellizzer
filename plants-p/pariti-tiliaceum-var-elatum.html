Pariti tiliaceum var. elatum

Pariti tiliaceum var. elatum, commonly known as the tall tiliaceum, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often pubescent. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically borne on slender pedicels. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland forests or along riverbanks.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in tropical gardens.
