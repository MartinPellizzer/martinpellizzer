Ptychosperma musschenbroekianum

Ptychosperma musschenbroekianum, commonly known as the giant cycad, is a large, ancient plant native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its massive, fern-like leaves that can grow up to several meters in length. The plant produces large, cone-like structures that contain both male and female reproductive organs. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained, nutrient-rich soil.

Due to its slow growth and habitat loss, it is considered a vulnerable species in the wild.
