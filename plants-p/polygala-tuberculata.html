Polygala tuberculata

Polygala tuberculata, commonly known as the rough-leaf milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia and Australia.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that are typically white or pale pink. The plant has compound leaves with a distinctive, rough texture, and its stems are often hairy. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and woodlands.

Polygala tuberculata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and flowering display.
