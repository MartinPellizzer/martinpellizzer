Polygala monspeliaca subsp. glumacea

Polygala monspeliaca subsp.

glumacea, commonly known as the glumaceous milkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region. It typically grows in rocky or dry soils and is characterized by its slender stems and small, greenish-yellow flowers.

The plant produces cylindrical, spike-like inflorescences that are often covered in fine hairs. Its leaves are narrow and opposite, and the plant is known for its ability to thrive in arid conditions.

It is also noted for its ecological importance as a food source for certain pollinators.
