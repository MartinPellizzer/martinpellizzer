Pimelea curviflora subsp. planiticola

Pimelea curviflora subsp.

planiticola, commonly known as the flat-leaved pimelea, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Australia. It is a shrub characterized by its narrow, linear leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers that appear in clusters.

The plant typically grows in heathlands and rocky soils, preferring well-drained conditions. Its flowering period occurs during the spring and early summer, adding ornamental value to its natural habitat.

It is valued for its hardiness and ability to thrive in coastal and sandy environments.
