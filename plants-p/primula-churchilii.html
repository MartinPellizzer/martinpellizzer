Primula churchilii

Primula churchilii, commonly known as the Chinese primrose, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of China.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant typically blooms in early spring, producing showy inflorescences that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are dark green, oval-shaped, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Primula churchilii is a perennial herb that thrives in cool, moist environments and is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value.
