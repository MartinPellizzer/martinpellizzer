Piper dasypodum

Piper dasypodum, commonly known as the rough-seeded pepper or mountain pepper, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many pepper species. The plant is characterized by its glossy, ovate leaves and small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. Its fruits are small, fleshy berries that turn from green to red as they mature.

The plant is often grown for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
