Pavonia cordifolia

Pavonia cordifolia, commonly known as the heartleaf pavonia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves, which are typically medium to dark green and have a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces clusters of showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Pavonia cordifolia is often used in native gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
