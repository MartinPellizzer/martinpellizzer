Pouteria decorticans

Pouteria decorticans, commonly known as the Brazil nut tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is characterized by its large, smooth, gray bark and can grow up to 50 meters in height. The tree produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers that are followed by large, woody fruits containing multiple seeds, known as Brazil nuts. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and elliptic in shape, with a leathery texture.

The plant plays a vital role in the ecosystem, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
