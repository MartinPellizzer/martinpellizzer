Potentilla × collina subsp. silesiaca

Potentilla × collina subsp.

silesiaca, commonly known as the Silesian cinquefoil, is a perennial shrub native to Central Europe. It typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm, featuring wiry stems and a compact, rounded form.

The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish to reddish-orange flowers from June to August. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three oval to elliptic leaflets, and are covered in fine, silky hairs.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and rocky habitats.
