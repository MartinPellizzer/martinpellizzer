Pithecellobium chagrense

Pithecellobium chagrense, commonly known as the Chagren hillpea, is a species of legume native to Central America and northern South America.

It is characterized by its climbing habit, with slender stems that often twine around supports. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers clustered in racemes, followed by elongated, pod-like fruits containing edible seeds. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with numerous small, oval-shaped leaflets.

Pithecellobium chagrense is adapted to tropical and subtropical climates, often found in disturbed or open habitats.
