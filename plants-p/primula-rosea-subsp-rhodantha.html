Primula rosea subsp. rhodantha

Primula rosea subsp.

rhodantha, commonly known as the pink primrose, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces clusters of showy, pink to purple flowers in early spring.

The leaves are typically heart-shaped and covered in a fine layer of hair, giving them a soft texture. This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it a versatile choice for woodland gardens.
