Piper echinovarium

Piper echinovarium, commonly known as the spiny pepper or mountain pepper, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piper genus within the family Piperaceae, which includes many species of pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its climbing habit, with opposite, ovate leaves that have a rough texture. Its small, greenish-yellow flowers are clustered in spikes, and it produces small, spherical berries that are typically green when immature and turn red when ripe.

The plant is valued for its aromatic leaves and has been used in traditional medicine and as a spice in some local cuisines.
