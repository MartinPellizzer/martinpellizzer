Potentilla lamottei

Potentilla lamottei, commonly known as the Lamotte cinquefoil, is a low-growing perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features wiry, arching stems that bear small, yellowish-orange flowers arranged in clusters. The plant has compound leaves with five leaflets, giving it the common name "cinquefoil.". It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, alpine environments.

Potentilla lamottei is often cultivated for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
