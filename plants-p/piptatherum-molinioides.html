Piptatherum molinioides

Piptatherum molinioides, commonly known as the slender wheatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its fine, grass-like leaves and small, spike-like inflorescences that produce numerous tiny seeds. The plant typically grows in well-drained soils and thrives in temperate climates with adequate sunlight. It is often found in alpine meadows and rocky habitats, where it contributes to soil stabilization.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
