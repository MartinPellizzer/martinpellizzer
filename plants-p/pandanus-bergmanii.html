Pandanus bergmanii

Pandanus bergmanii, commonly known as the Bergman's pandanus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Pandanaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including parts of Indonesia and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender stems and long, narrow leaves that are often used in traditional crafts and as thatching material. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of small, fragrant flowers, which are typically yellow or green in color.

The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal or lowland areas.
