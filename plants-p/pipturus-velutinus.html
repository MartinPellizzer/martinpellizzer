Pipturus velutinus

Pipturus velutinus, commonly known as the velvetleaf pipturus, is a member of the Lauraceae family, characterized by its aromatic foliage and evergreen nature.

It is native to tropical regions of Central and South America, where it thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant features elliptical leaves with a velvety texture and a glossy surface, often covered in fine hairs. Its small, fragrant flowers are typically clustered in catkins, and it produces dark, fleshy berries that are an important food source for various wildlife.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological role, it is sometimes cultivated in tropical gardens.
