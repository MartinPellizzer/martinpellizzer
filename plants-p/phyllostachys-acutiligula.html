Phyllostachys acutiligula

Phyllostachys acutiligula, commonly known as the sharp-leaved bamboo, is a species of grass in the family Poaceae.

It is native to eastern Asia, particularly China, where it thrives in temperate climates. This bamboo is characterized by its slender, erect culms and narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a distinctive sharp edge. It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its elegant appearance and hardy nature.

The plant is also valued for its use in traditional Chinese medicine and as a source of sustainable materials.
