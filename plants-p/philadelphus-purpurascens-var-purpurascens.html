Philadelphus purpurascens var. purpurascens

Philadelphus purpurascens var. purpurascens, commonly known as the purple-leaf mock orange, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features compound leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape, with a striking purple-bronze hue on the upper surface and a lighter green underside. The plant produces fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers in clusters during late spring to early summer. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable addition to both native plant gardens and wildlife habitats.
