Parapteroceras escritorii

Parapteroceras escritorii, commonly known as the writer's sea hare, is a species of sea slug found in tropical marine environments.

It belongs to the family Anisophthalmidae and is characterized by its distinctive, often brightly colored body adorned with intricate patterns. The organism lacks a shell and has a soft, gelatinous body covered in specialized structures called cerata. Its diet primarily consists of algae, which it consumes using a radula, a ribbon-like structure with tiny teeth.

This species plays a role in marine ecosystems by contributing to nutrient cycling and serving as prey for various marine predators.
