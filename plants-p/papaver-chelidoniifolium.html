Papaver chelidoniifolium

Papaver chelidoniifolium, commonly known as the meadow poppy, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Papaveraceae family and is characterized by its vibrant yellow or orange flowers with a distinctive black center. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and woodland edges, preferring well-drained soil. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly rough texture, while its stems are hairy and upright.

The flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
