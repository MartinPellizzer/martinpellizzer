Plantago lanceolata var. dubia

Plantago lanceolata var. dubia, commonly known as the slender plantain, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features lance-shaped, hairless leaves that are typically shorter and narrower than those of the typical variety. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in dense spikes, which bloom in the summer months. It thrives in a variety of habitats, including roadsides, meadows, and disturbed soils.

This variant is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
