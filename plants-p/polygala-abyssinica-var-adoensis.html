Polygala abyssinica var. adoensis

Polygala abyssinica var. adoensis, commonly known as the Ethiopian milkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Ethiopia.

It belongs to the Polygalaceae family and is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its foliage consists of narrow, linear leaves, and it produces yellowish or greenish flowers that attract pollinators.

This variant is distinguished by its unique morphological features and ecological significance in its native habitat.
