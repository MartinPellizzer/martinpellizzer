Pleiotaxis eximia subsp. eximia

Pleiotaxis eximia subsp.

eximia, commonly known as the slender pleiotaxis, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa. It features slender, upright stems that are often hairy and can grow up to 1 meter in height.

The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in dense, terminal spikes, which bloom during the cooler months. Its leaves are narrow and linear, with a slightly rough texture, and it is often found in grassy areas or along riverbanks.

This subspecies is notable for its adaptability to various soil types and its role in supporting local biodiversity.
