Plantago juncoides f. vivipara

Plantago juncoides f. vivipara, commonly known as the viviparous plantain, is a botanical variant of the common plantain.

It is characterized by its ability to produce viviparous propagules, which are seeds that germinate while still attached to the parent plant. This feature allows for rapid propagation and establishment in suitable habitats. The plant typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its leaves are broad, lance-shaped, and covered in a dense layer of fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
