Potentilla mexiae

Potentilla mexiae, commonly known as Mexican cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its small, yellow flowers and shrub-like growth habit. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to arid conditions. Its leaves are simple and alternate, with a rough texture, and it produces fruit in the form of a capsule.

Potentilla mexiae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardiness and visual interest.
