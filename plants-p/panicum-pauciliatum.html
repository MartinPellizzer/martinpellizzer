Panicum pauciliatum

Panicum pauciliatum, commonly known as the slender panicgrass, is a flowering plant species belonging to the grass family, Poaceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas, particularly in countries like Brazil and Argentina. The plant is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, spike-like inflorescences that produce numerous tiny grains. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes used in landscaping or as a forage crop in certain regions.
