Plectranthus bosseri

Plectranthus bosseri, commonly known as the Bosser's plectranthus, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy texture and produces small, tubular flowers in shades of violet or purple. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.

Plectranthus bosseri prefers full sun to partial shade and is relatively drought-tolerant once established.
