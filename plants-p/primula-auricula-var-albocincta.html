Primula auricula var. albocincta

Primula auricula var. albocincta, commonly known as the white-bordered auricula, is a variant of the auricula primrose native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its distinctive, heart-shaped leaves and showy, bell-shaped flowers that typically bloom in early spring. The flowers are usually pale yellow with a white border, giving it a striking appearance. This plant thrives in well-drained, moist soils and prefers partial shade to full sun.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in rock gardens and alpine settings.
