Paepalanthus lilliputianus

Paepalanthus lilliputianus, commonly known as the dwarf palm, is a unique plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a small, compact plant that resembles a miniature palm tree, with a short trunk and clustered leaves. The plant is adapted to grow in shaded, humid environments, often found in forest understories. Its leaves are typically bright green and have a waxy texture, which helps reduce water loss.

Due to its size and appearance, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant in tropical gardens.
