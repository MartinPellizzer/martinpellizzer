Phlox setacea

Phlox setacea, commonly known as the tufted phlox, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features upright, branching stems that bear clusters of small, tubular flowers in shades of pink, purple, or white. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its foliage consists of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs.

Phlox setacea is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
