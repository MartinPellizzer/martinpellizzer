Pimpinella adscendens

Pimpinella adscendens, commonly known as the ascending hogweed, is a member of the Apiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic foliage and umbellate inflorescences.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and parts of Europe, often found in rocky or disturbed soils. The plant features finely divided, fern-like leaves that are often covered in a white, powdery coating. Its flowers are small and white, arranged in umbrella-shaped clusters, and it produces small, dry fruits called schizocarps.

The plant is known for its strong scent and has been historically used in traditional medicine.
