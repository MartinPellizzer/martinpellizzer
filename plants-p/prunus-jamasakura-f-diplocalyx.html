Prunus jamasakura f. diplocalyx

Prunus jamasakura f. diplocalyx, commonly known as the Japanese cherry with a double blossom, is a unique variant of the Prunus genus.

It is characterized by its distinctive floral morphology, featuring two sets of petals that give it a more elaborate and showy appearance compared to typical cherry blossoms. The plant is native to Japan and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its aesthetic value. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil to flourish.

The blooming period typically occurs in early spring, adding a striking visual element to gardens and natural landscapes.
