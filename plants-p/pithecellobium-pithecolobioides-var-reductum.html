Pithecellobium pithecolobioides var. reductum

Pithecellobium pithecolobioides var. reductum, commonly known as the reduced pithecellobium, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant produces edible pods that contain seeds, which are often used in local cuisines. It thrives in moist, tropical climates and is often found in secondary forests and disturbed areas.

Due to its adaptability, it is also cultivated for ornamental purposes in some regions.
