Pseuderiopsis

Pseuderiopsis, commonly known as the ghost orchid, is a rare and elusive flowering plant native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family (Orchidaceae) and is characterized by its unique, delicate appearance with small, pale green or white flowers. The plant is epiphytic, meaning it grows on the surface of trees without deriving nutrients from them. It has a short, rhizomatous stem and produces tiny, clustered flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer.

Due to its habitat requirements and limited distribution, Pseuderiopsis is considered a conservation concern.
