Peperomia ciliatocaespitosa

Peperomia ciliatocaespitosa, commonly known as the curly-leaved peperomia, is a compact, low-growing plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It features dense, rosette-like clusters of ovate, hairy leaves that are often tinged with red or purple at the edges. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters, typically green or red, which are followed by tiny, fleshy fruits. It thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Its unique foliage and ease of care contribute to its appeal among plant enthusiasts.
