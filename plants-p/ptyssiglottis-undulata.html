Ptyssiglottis undulata

Ptyssiglottis undulata, commonly known as the undulating bellflower, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its bell-shaped, violet or purple flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests or rocky slopes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly waxy texture.

Ptyssiglottis undulata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
