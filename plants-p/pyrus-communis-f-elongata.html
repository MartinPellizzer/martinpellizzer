Pyrus communis f. elongata

Pyrus communis f. elongata, commonly known as the elongated pear, is a horticultural form of the common pear.

It is characterized by its elongated, slender fruits that are typically longer than they are wide. The leaves are usually larger and more pointed compared to the standard variety. This form is prized for its unique fruit shape and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.

It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
