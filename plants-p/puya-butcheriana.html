Puya butcheriana

Puya butcheriana, commonly known as the Butcher's puya, is a striking member of the Bromeliaceae family native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It is a striking plant characterized by its tall, spiky inflorescence and vibrant, long-lasting bracts that can range in color from red to orange. The plant is adapted to high-altitude environments, often growing in rocky soils and exposed areas. It produces small, white flowers that bloom in succession along the central spike.

Puya butcheriana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and by plant enthusiasts.
