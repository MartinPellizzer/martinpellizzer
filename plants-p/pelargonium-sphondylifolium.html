Pelargonium sphondylifolium

Pelargonium sphondylifolium, commonly known as the African geranium, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of small, five-petaled flowers in shades of pink, red, or white. The leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a rough, slightly hairy texture. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

This species is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
