Panthocarpa chionacantha

Panthocarpa chionacantha, commonly known as the silver-haired legume, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive silvery-gray foliage and clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers. The plant produces elongated, bean-like pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Its unique appearance and ecological adaptations make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
