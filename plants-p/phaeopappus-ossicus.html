Phaeopappus ossicus

Phaeopappus ossicus, commonly known as the desert pappus, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy stems and leaves, which help it store water in harsh environments. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by distinctive papery bracts that aid in seed dispersal. Its root system is deep and extensive, allowing it to access groundwater in dry soils.

Phaeopappus ossicus plays a role in stabilizing sandy soils and providing habitat for desert wildlife.
