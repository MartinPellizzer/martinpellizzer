Phrynium minor

Phrynium minor, commonly known as the narrow-leaf arrowroot, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its arrow-shaped leaves and striking inflorescence. The plant produces a cluster of small, white flowers surrounded by a spathe, which is a modified leaf. Its rhizomes are edible and have been used in traditional cuisines for their starchy texture.

The plant thrives in moist, shady environments and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.
