Polygala paucifolia var. pubescens

Polygala paucifolia var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy leaf milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to arid and semi-arid regions of central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The leaves are typically hairy, giving the plant its common name, and are often arranged in opposite pairs along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

This plant is often found in rocky or sandy habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
