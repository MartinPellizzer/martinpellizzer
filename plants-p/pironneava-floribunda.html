Pironneava floribunda

Pironneava floribunda, commonly known as the golden flame tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking yellow-orange flowers that bloom in clusters during the rainy season. The plant has compound leaves with elongated, serrated leaflets. Its rapid growth rate makes it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping.

However, it can become invasive in certain regions if not managed properly.
