Pseudosasa hindsii

Pseudosasa hindsii, commonly known as the Hinds' bamboo, is a member of the grass family (Poaceae) and is native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a clumping bamboo that typically grows to a height of 3 to 5 meters, characterized by its slender, hollow stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in secondary forests or along stream banks.

Its rhizomes are fibrous and contribute to its structural strength, while its leaves are often used in traditional medicine in its native regions.
