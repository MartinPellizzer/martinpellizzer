Polyalthia lateriflora var. kallak

Polyalthia lateriflora var. kallak, commonly known as the Kallak polyalthia, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Western Ghats of India.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and is often found in moist, shaded forest environments. The plant produces attractive, pendulous inflorescences with small, white or pale yellow flowers, which are typically fragrant. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a glossy, dark green surface.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in horticultural settings for its aesthetic qualities.
