Penstemon campanulatus var. subglandulosus

Penstemon campanulatus var. subglandulosus, commonly known as the glandular bell penstemon, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered with fine, glandular hairs, which give the plant its subspecies name. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and native plant landscapes.

This species is also valued for its ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
