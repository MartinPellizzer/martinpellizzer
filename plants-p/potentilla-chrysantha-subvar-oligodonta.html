Potentilla chrysantha subvar. oligodonta

Potentilla chrysantha subvar. oligodonta, commonly known as the yellow cinquefoil, is a perennial shrub native to the western United States.

It features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has compound leaves with five to seven leaflets, which are typically narrow and slightly hairy. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable plant for both ecological and ornamental purposes.
