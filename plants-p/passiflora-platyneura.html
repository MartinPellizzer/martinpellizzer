Passiflora platyneura

Passiflora platyneura, commonly known as the silky passionflower, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, intricate flowers that feature a blend of blue, purple, and white petals, along with a crown-like structure of filaments. The plant produces edible, orange-yellow berries that are rich in antioxidants. Its leaves are large, heart-shaped, and covered in a fine, silky down.

Passiflora platyneura is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its unique floral display.
