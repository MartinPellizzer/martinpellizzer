Pedilanthus camporum

Pedilanthus camporum, commonly known as the red sandalwood tree, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its small, clustered flowers and distinctive, often fragrant, red or pinkish fruits. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and thrives in warm, sunny environments. Its leaves are usually narrow and lance-shaped, and it can reach heights of up to 10 meters.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation areas.
