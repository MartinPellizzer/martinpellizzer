Passiflora plumosa

Passiflora plumosa, commonly known as the silky passionflower, is a climbing vine native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, feathery leaves and striking, intricate flowers that feature five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments. The plant produces blue or purple berries that are edible and often consumed by birds and mammals. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires support for its vigorous growth.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and greenhouses.
