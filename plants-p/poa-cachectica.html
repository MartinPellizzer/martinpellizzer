Poa cachectica

Poa cachectica, commonly known as the sickly grass, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and narrow, bluish-green leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear minute, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky soils.

Due to its delicate appearance and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
