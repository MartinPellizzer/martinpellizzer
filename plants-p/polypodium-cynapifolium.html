Polypodium cynapifolium

Polypodium cynapifolium, commonly known as the broad-leaf huperzia, is a species of fern belonging to the family Huperziaceae.

It is characterized by its long, leathery fronds that are typically divided into numerous small, ovate pinnae. The plant is native to regions of Asia, including China, Japan, and Korea, where it thrives in moist, shaded environments. Its rhizome is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

The plant's unique morphology and ecological adaptations make it an important component of its native habitats.
