Prunus shikokuensis

Prunus shikokuensis, commonly known as the Shikoku cherry or Japanese flowering cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its delicate, single or double pink to white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has oval-shaped leaves that turn yellow in the fall, and its bark is smooth with a reddish-brown color. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens.

Prunus shikokuensis is often cultivated for its aesthetic value and is used in traditional Japanese landscapes.
