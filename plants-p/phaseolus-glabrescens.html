Phaseolus glabrescens

Phaseolus glabrescens, commonly known as the scarlet runner bean, is a herbaceous annual plant native to the Americas.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its climbing habit, with tendrils that help it ascend supports. The plant produces striking red or orange flowers followed by elongated, cylindrical pods containing edible seeds. Its leaves are trifoliate, with each leaflet being ovate and slightly hairy.

Phaseolus glabrescens is valued for its nutritional content and is cultivated for both culinary and ornamental purposes.
