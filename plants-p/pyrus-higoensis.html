Pyrus higoensis

Pyrus higoensis, commonly known as the Japanese white pear, is a deciduous tree native to Japan.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and broad, lobed leaves. The plant produces small, white flowers in early spring, which are followed by sweet, yellowish fruits in late summer. The fruits are typically small and have a crisp, juicy texture with a mild, sweet flavor.

Pyrus higoensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible fruit in gardens and orchards.
