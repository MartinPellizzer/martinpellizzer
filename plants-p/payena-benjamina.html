Payena benjamina

Payena benjamina, commonly known as the African satinwood, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Clusiaceae and is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and smooth, often reddish bark. The leaves are alternate, simple, and elliptic in shape, with a leathery texture. It produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in terminal panicles, followed by woody, capsule-like fruits.

The plant is valued for its hard, durable wood, which is used in furniture and construction.
