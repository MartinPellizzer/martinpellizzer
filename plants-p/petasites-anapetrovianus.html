Petasites anapetrovianus

Petasites anapetrovianus, commonly known as the Anapetrovian cow parsley, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and showy, yellowish-white flower heads. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes. Its unique morphology and habitat make it an important species for ecological studies in high-altitude ecosystems.

The plant's name honors the botanist Anatoly Anapetrov, who contributed significantly to the study of Central Asian flora.
