Potentilla honoratae

Potentilla honoratae, commonly known as the Honorable Potentilla, is a flowering plant belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, particularly in countries like Kazakhstan and Uzbekistan. The plant is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of small, white or pink flowers during the summer months. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and covered in fine hairs, giving them a soft texture.

Potentilla honoratae is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
