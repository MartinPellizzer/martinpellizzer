Piper anisotrichum

Piper anisotrichum, commonly known as the waxy begonia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes various species of flowering plants. The plant is characterized by its glossy, waxy leaves that are often a deep green or reddish in color. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or yellow.

Piper anisotrichum is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to indoor growing conditions.
