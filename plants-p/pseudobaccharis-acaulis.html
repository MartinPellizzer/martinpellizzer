Pseudobaccharis acaulis

Pseudobaccharis acaulis, commonly known as the smooth pseudobaccharis, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its slender, woody stems and small, narrow leaves. The plant produces clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in the summer, attracting various pollinators. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal dunes and scrub habitats.

Pseudobaccharis acaulis is adapted to withstand salt spray and is an important component of coastal ecosystems.
