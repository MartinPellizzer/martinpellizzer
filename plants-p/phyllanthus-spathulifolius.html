Phyllanthus spathulifolius

Phyllanthus spathulifolius, commonly known as the broad-leaf phyllanthus, is a shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific.

It is characterized by its simple, elliptical or ovate leaves that are often glossy and arranged alternately along the stems. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters, which are followed by small, round, yellowish fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires a warm, humid climate.

Phyllanthus spathulifolius is sometimes used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
