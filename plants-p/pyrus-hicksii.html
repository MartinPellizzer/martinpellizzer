Pyrus hicksii

Pyrus hicksii, commonly known as the Hicks' pear, is a deciduous tree native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and alternate, ovate leaves with serrated edges. The tree produces small, yellow-green flowers in early spring, followed by edible, sweet-tart pears in late summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in mixed hardwood forests.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical significance in American horticulture.
