Ptychosperma microcarpum

Ptychosperma microcarpum, commonly known as the dwarf palm, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small, compact palm with a short trunk and broad, feather-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically yellow or greenish in color. Its fruit is a small, round drupe that contains a single seed.

Ptychosperma microcarpum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
