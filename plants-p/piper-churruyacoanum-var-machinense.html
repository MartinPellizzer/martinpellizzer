Piper churruyacoanum var. machinense

Piper churruyacoanum var. machinense, commonly known as the Machin's pepper vine, is a climbing shrub native to the humid lowland forests of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes numerous species of flowering plants known for their aromatic properties. The plant produces small, green, egg-shaped fruits that turn red when mature, containing a single seed. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a rough texture with a distinct odor.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
