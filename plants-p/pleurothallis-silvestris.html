Pleurothallis silvestris

Pleurothallis silvestris, commonly known as the forest orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact, epiphytic growth habit. The plant produces small, delicate flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, often with intricate patterns. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining, nutrient-rich substrates.

Due to its beauty and rarity, it is a sought-after species among orchid enthusiasts.
