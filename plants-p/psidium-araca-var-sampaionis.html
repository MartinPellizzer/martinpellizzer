Psidium araca var. sampaionis

Psidium araca var. sampaionis, commonly known as the Sampaionis guava, is a variant of the guava species native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its small, dark purple fruits that are typically harvested when fully mature. The plant has elliptical leaves with a slightly pubescent underside and produces small, white flowers clustered in inflorescences. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, requiring well-drained soil and consistent moisture.

The fruit is valued for its aromatic pulp and is often used in local cuisine and traditional medicine.
