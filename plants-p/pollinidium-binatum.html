Pollinidium binatum

Pollinidium binatum, commonly known as the two-spined pollinidium, is a small, herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features delicate, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has hairy, linear leaves that are typically arranged in a basal rosette. Its flowering stems are slender and bear two distinct, spiny structures at the base of the inflorescence.

Pollinidium binatum is adapted to dry, rocky soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats.
