Plectranthus rivularis

Plectranthus rivularis, commonly known as river mint, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It features square, hairy stems and opposite leaves that are often aromatic and slightly hairy. The plant produces small, tubular flowers in shades of purple or blue, typically arranged in whorls along the stem. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found near water sources.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is cultivated in gardens and used in traditional medicine.
