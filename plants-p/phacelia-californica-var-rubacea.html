Phacelia californica var. rubacea

Phacelia californica var. rubacea, commonly known as the California phacelia, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its striking purple to reddish-purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It has hairy, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators.
