Poa pratensis var. corcuntina

Poa pratensis var. corcuntina, commonly known as the Corcuntina meadowgrass, is a variant of the common bentgrass.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. This plant typically thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and meadows. It has a fibrous root system that helps stabilize soil and prevent erosion.

The inflorescence consists of spikelets that are usually arranged in a loose, open panicle.
