Potentilla ancistrifolia var. rugulosa

Potentilla ancistrifolia var. rugulosa, commonly known as the roughleaf cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit and produces small, yellowish flowers clustered in cymes. The leaves are typically compound, with serrated margins and a rough texture, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in alpine or subalpine environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
