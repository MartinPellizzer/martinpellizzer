Primula pauliana var. huiliensis

Primula pauliana var. huiliensis, commonly known as the Huili primrose, is a flowering plant native to China, specifically the Yunnan province.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as mountain forests and rocky slopes. Its foliage consists of broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of hair.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is occasionally cultivated in gardens for its delicate beauty.
