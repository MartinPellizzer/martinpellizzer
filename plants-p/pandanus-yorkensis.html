Pandanus yorkensis

Pandanus yorkensis, commonly known as the York Island pandanus, is a tropical plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It is a member of the Pandanaceae family and is characterized by its long, sword-shaped leaves that are often used in traditional crafts. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically clustered at the base of the leaves. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to thrive in coastal and sandy soils.

The York Island pandanus is valued for both its ornamental appeal and cultural significance in the region.
