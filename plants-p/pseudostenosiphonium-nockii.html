Pseudostenosiphonium nockii

Pseudostenosiphonium nockii, commonly known as the African buttercup, is a member of the Ranunculaceae family.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Africa, characterized by its basal rosette of lanceolate leaves. The plant produces showy, yellow flowers with five petals, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or grassland habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
