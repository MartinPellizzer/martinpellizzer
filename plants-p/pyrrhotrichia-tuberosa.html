Pyrrhotrichia tuberosa

Pyrrhotrichia tuberosa, commonly known as the tuberous rockrose, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its woody stems and dense clusters of small, fragrant flowers. The plant produces tuberous roots that store nutrients, aiding in its survival in arid conditions. Its leaves are typically narrow and gray-green, with a fuzzy texture.

Pyrrhotrichia tuberosa thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.
