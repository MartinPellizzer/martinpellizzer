Perezia laciniata

Perezia laciniata, commonly known as the laceleaf perezia, is a member of the Campanulaceae family.

It is characterized by its finely divided, feathery leaves that give it a delicate appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale blue in color. Native to the mountainous regions of South America, it thrives in cool, moist environments.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
