Plectranthus ignotus

Plectranthus ignotus, commonly known as the African violet, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces showy, violet-colored flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.

Plectranthus ignotus is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its attractive foliage and flowers.
