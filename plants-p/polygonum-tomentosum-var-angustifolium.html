Polygonum tomentosum var. angustifolium

Polygonum tomentosum var. angustifolium, commonly known as narrow-leaved knotweed, is a perennial herb native to eastern Asia.

It features slender, upright stems that are often covered in a soft, woolly pubescence. The leaves are lance-shaped, with a narrow base and serrated edges, and they are typically covered in fine hairs. Small, greenish-white flowers appear in clusters during the summer months.

This plant thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodlands and along streambanks.
