Paspalum vaginatum var. pleostachyum

Paspalum vaginatum var. pleostachyum, commonly known as the broadleaf paspalum, is a grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its coarse, erect growth habit and long, narrow leaves that are often hairy along the edges. The plant produces dense clusters of small, spike-like inflorescences, which are typically green to reddish in color. It thrives in wetland environments and is often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

This variety is valued for its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife species.
