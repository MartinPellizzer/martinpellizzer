Piper woytkowskii

Piper woytkowskii, commonly known as the Woytkowski's pepper, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its climbing habit and compound leaves with serrated edges. Its small, greenish-white flowers are typically clustered in spikes, and it produces small, round berries that are edible.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and research settings.
