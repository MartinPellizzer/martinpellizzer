Phyllanthus phillyreifolius var. longifolius

Phyllanthus phillyreifolius var. longifolius, commonly known as the long-leaved phyllanthus, is a member of the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is characterized by its elongated, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, preferring well-drained soils and partial shade. Its small, yellowish-green flowers are clustered in inflorescences, and it produces capsules as its fruit.

This species is noted for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
