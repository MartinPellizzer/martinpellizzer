Pavonia sempervirens

Pavonia sempervirens, commonly known as the evergreen pavonia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring woody stems and dense foliage. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of red, pink, or white, which bloom throughout the growing season. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Pavonia sempervirens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
