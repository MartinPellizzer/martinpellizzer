Panicum aristulatum

Panicum aristulatum, commonly known as the narrowleaf panicgrass, is a grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, spikelet-like flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and disturbed sites.

Panicum aristulatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in ecological habitats.
