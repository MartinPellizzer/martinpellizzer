Piotes

Piotes, commonly known as the common thistle, is a perennial plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its spiny leaves and purple or pink flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant is native to Europe, North Africa, and parts of Asia, and it has been naturalized in many other regions. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their anti-inflammatory properties.

The plant's structure and appearance make it easily identifiable in various habitats.
