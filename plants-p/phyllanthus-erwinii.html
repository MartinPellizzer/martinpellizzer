Phyllanthus erwinii

Phyllanthus erwinii, commonly known as the African spurge, is a flowering plant belonging to the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is native to tropical Africa and is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant produces yellowish-green cyathia, which are specialized inflorescences typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Phyllanthus erwinii is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
