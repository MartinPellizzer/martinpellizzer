Pterocarpus bussei

Pterocarpus bussei, commonly known as the African rosewood, is a tropical tree native to regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its dark, dense wood that is highly valued for its strength and aesthetic qualities. The plant produces fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with small, oval-shaped leaflets.

The tree also bears fruit that contains flat, winged seeds, aiding in its dispersal.
