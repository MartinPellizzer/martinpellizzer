Paphiopedilum insigne var. nilsonii

Paphiopedilum insigne var. nilsonii, commonly known as the Nilson's lady's slipper, is a rare and exquisite orchid native to the Philippines.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety petals and a unique, pouch-like lip that resembles a slipper, a feature typical of the genus Paphiopedilum. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded forest environments. Its flowers are usually pink or white, with intricate patterns that enhance its ornamental appeal.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is highly valued in horticulture and conservation efforts.
