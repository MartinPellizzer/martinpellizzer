Pilocarpus microphyllus

Pilocarpus microphyllus, commonly known as the boldo tree, is a small evergreen shrub or tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its small, leathery leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is known for its aromatic foliage, which contains various bioactive compounds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in coastal areas.

Its botanical features make it valuable in traditional medicine and horticulture.
