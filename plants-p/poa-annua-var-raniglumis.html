Poa annua var. raniglumis

Poa annua var. raniglumis, commonly known as the alpine bluegrass, is a variant of the annual bluegrass species.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and bluish-green foliage. This plant typically thrives in cool, moist environments, often found in alpine and subalpine regions. Its inflorescence consists of dense, spike-like clusters of small, blue-green flowers.

The variety is notable for its adaptability to harsh climates and its role in stabilizing soil in mountainous areas.
