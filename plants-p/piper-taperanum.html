Piper taperanum

Piper taperanum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved pepper plant, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species such as black pepper. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by three-lobed, berry-like fruits that are edible. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The plant's botanical features make it an interesting subject for both horticultural and pharmacological study.
