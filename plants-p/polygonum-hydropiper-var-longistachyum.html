Polygonum hydropiper var. longistachyum

Polygonum hydropiper var. longistachyum, commonly known as water pepper, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to wetland areas in North America.

It features upright stems that are often reddish in color and are covered in small, glandular dots. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in dense, spike-like clusters. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and have a distinctive waxy coating.

It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.
