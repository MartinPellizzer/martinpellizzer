Piper hirtirhache

Piper hirtirhache, commonly known as the hairy pipevine, is a member of the Piperaceae family and is native to tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its dark green, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a textured appearance. The plant produces small, clustered greenish-white flowers that are typically pollinated by insects. Its fruits are capsule-like structures that release tiny seeds when mature.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and research settings.
