Pseudalthenia aschersoniana

Pseudalthenia aschersoniana, commonly known as the Ascherson's pseudalthenia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in open, sandy or rocky soils and is often found in coastal or semi-arid regions. The plant features narrow, linear leaves arranged in a basal rosette, and it produces small, white or pale blue flowers during the cooler months. It is adapted to withstand periods of drought and has a shallow root system for efficient water absorption.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
