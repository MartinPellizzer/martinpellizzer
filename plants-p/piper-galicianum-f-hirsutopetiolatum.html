Piper galicianum f. hirsutopetiolatum

Piper galicianum f. hirsutopetiolatum, commonly known as the hairy-stalked pepper plant, is a variant of the species Piper galicianum, native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its hairy petioles and leaves, which contribute to its distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, clustered berries that are typically green when immature and turn red when mature. It belongs to the Piperaceae family, known for its aromatic and often spicy fruits.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental qualities and potential use in traditional medicine.
