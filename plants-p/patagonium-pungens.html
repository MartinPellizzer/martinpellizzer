Patagonium pungens

Patagonium pungens, commonly known as the spiny cactus, is a unique plant native to the arid regions of South America.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its thick, spiny stems that store water for long periods. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom during the cooler months, attracting pollinators like bees and moths. Its adaptations to extreme drought conditions make it a resilient species in its native habitat.

The spines serve both as a defense mechanism against herbivores and as a means of reducing water loss through transpiration.
