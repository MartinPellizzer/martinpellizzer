Poppya

Poppya, commonly known as the opium poppy, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its vibrant red or orange petals and a distinctive, rounded seed capsule. The plant contains alkaloids such as morphine and codeine in its latex, which have significant medicinal and illicit uses. Its life cycle includes a period of rapid growth followed by the development of the seed pods.

Poppya is also cultivated for its seeds, which are used in food and oil production.
