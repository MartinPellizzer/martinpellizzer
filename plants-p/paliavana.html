Paliavana

Paliavana, commonly known as the Madagascar star orchid, is a striking plant native to the tropical regions of Madagascar.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, star-shaped flowers that come in a variety of vibrant colors. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees and deriving moisture and nutrients from the air and rain. Its leaves are long and narrow, often arranged in a rosette pattern, which helps it capture rainwater efficiently.

Paliavana is valued not only for its ornamental beauty but also for its ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
