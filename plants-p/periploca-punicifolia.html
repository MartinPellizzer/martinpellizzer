Periploca punicifolia

Periploca punicifolia, commonly known as the Cape jasmine, is a small evergreen shrub native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves and fragrant, white flowers. The plant produces small, round berries that are typically orange or red in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and rocky areas.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens, though it contains toxic compounds in its sap and berries.
