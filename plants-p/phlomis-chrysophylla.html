Phlomis chrysophylla

Phlomis chrysophylla, commonly known as golden leaf phlomis, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features upright, branched stems covered in soft, hairy foliage. The plant produces clusters of bright yellow flowers during the summer months, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a silvery or grayish underside, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.
