Primula lactiflora

Primula lactiflora, commonly known as the milk primrose, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features rosettes of broadly ovate, hairy, and sometimes bluish-green leaves. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, with a distinctive waxy coating. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its early spring blooms.
