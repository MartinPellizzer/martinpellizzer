Prunus angustifolia subsp. varians

Prunus angustifolia subsp.

varians, commonly known as the sourwood tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States. It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves that turn vibrant yellow in the fall.

The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers in early spring, followed by edible, tart berries that are important for wildlife. Its botanical features include smooth, gray bark and a generally rounded or irregular crown.

It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in woodland habitats.
