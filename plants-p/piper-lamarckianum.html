Piper lamarckianum

Piper lamarckianum, commonly known as the black pepper vine, is a climbing shrub native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species. The plant is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves and small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in spikes. Its fruit is a drupe that turns from green to black as it matures, giving it the common name.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of essential oils.
