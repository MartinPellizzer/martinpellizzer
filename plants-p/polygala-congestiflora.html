Polygala congestiflora

Polygala congestiflora, commonly known as the clustered milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its small, clustered inflorescences. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in coastal or inland regions. Its flowers are usually white or pale yellow, with a distinctive spurred petal that resembles a small horn.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
