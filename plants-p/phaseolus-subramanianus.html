Phaseolus subramanianus

Phaseolus subramanianus, commonly known as the Indian runner bean, is a climbing legume native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its slender, twining stems and small, bean-like pods. The plant produces clusters of white or pale pink flowers, which are followed by elongated, cylindrical pods containing edible seeds. It thrives in warm, tropical climates and is often cultivated for its nutritious seeds and ornamental value.

The plant's botanical features make it a valuable species for both agricultural and ecological studies.
