Persea lemensis

Persea lemensis, commonly known as the Mexican persimmon, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central America and parts of Mexico.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant, white flowers. The tree produces small, orange-red fruits that are edible when fully ripe. The fruit has a sweet, sometimes astringent flavor and is often used in local cuisines.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and is valued for its ornamental and culinary uses.
