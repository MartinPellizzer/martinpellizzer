Primula pinnatifida subsp. cephalantha

Primula pinnatifida subsp.

cephalantha, commonly known as the Sierra primrose, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of bell-shaped, often pink or purple flowers.

The leaves are typically basal, with a pinnately divided structure, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in its native habitat.
