Persicaria lapathifolia subsp. klokovii

Persicaria lapathifolia subsp.

klokovii, commonly known as the Siberian knotweed, is a perennial herb native to the cold regions of Asia. It features upright stems that are often reddish in color and are covered with fine hairs.

The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or pale red, blooming in late summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly serrated edge, often with a hairy texture.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.
