Poa acroleuca

Poa acroleuca, commonly known as the silver hair grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern South America.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and silver-gray foliage due to the presence of fine, silky hairs on its leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear minute, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in open areas such as grasslands and wetlands.

Poa acroleuca is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
