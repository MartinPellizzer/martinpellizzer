Prunus pubigera var. potaninii

Prunus pubigera var. potaninii, commonly known as the Potanin's cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, upright growth habit and produces small, edible berries that are typically red or dark purple in color. The leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a rough texture with a serrated margin. The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its spring blossoms and autumn foliage.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for a variety of temperate climates.
