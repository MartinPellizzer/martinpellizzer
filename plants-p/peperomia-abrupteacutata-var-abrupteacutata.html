Peperomia abrupteacutata var. abrupteacutata

Peperomia abrupteacutata var. abrupteacutata, commonly known as the Sharp-leaf Peperomia, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It features small, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are typically glossy and have a pointed tip, giving it its common name. The plant produces clusters of tiny, fragrant flowers in shades of white or pale pink, which are borne on short, erect spikes. Its foliage is often variegated with white or silver markings, adding visual interest.

It is well-suited for indoor cultivation due to its low maintenance requirements and ability to thrive in bright, indirect light.
