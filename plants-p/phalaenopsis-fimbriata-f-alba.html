Phalaenopsis fimbriata f. alba

Phalaenopsis fimbriata f. alba, commonly known as the white-veined phalaenopsis, is a cultivar of the Phalaenopsis orchid characterized by its striking white petals with subtle veining.

It originates from Southeast Asia, where it thrives in warm, humid climates with indirect sunlight. The plant features a compact growth habit and produces long, pendant flower spikes with numerous blooms. Its foliage consists of broad, dark green leaves that are arranged in a fan shape.

This variety is prized for its elegant appearance and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes in both home and commercial settings.
