Prasophyllum tunicatum

Prasophyllum tunicatum, commonly known as the tunicate leek orchid, is an Australian native plant belonging to the orchid family, Orchidaceae.

It is a perennial herb with a distinctive appearance, featuring a basal rosette of broad, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. The plant produces a tall, slender flowering stem that bears a dense cluster of small, greenish-white flowers. It is typically found in heathlands and open forests, thriving in well-drained soils.

Prasophyllum tunicatum is adapted to the specific environmental conditions of southern Australia, including dry summers and wet winters.
