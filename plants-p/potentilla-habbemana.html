Potentilla habbemana

Potentilla habbemana, commonly known as the Habbemana cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming structure with woody stems. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically compound, with five leaflets, and it thrives in well-drained, rocky soils.

Potentilla habbemana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in rock gardens and alpine landscapes.
