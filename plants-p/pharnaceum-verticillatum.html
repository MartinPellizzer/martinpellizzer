Pharnaceum verticillatum

Pharnaceum verticillatum, commonly known as the spiral-leaf pharnaceum, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its whorled arrangement of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that give it a distinctive spiral appearance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, often with a faint fragrance. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in humid forests or along riverbanks.

Due to its unique morphology and ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and by plant enthusiasts.
