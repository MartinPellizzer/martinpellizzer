Prunus × fruticans var. szandaensis

Prunus × fruticans var. szandaensis, commonly known as the Szandánszaláni cherry, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its small, dark red fruits and is native to Hungary. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are ovate with serrated edges, and it produces fragrant white flowers in the spring.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
