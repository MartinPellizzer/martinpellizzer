Phyllanthus puncticulatus

Phyllanthus puncticulatus, commonly known as the punctuated phyllanthus, is a small shrub native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It features simple, alternate leaves with a distinctive pungent odor and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically eaten by various animals. It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
