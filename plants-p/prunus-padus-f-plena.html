Prunus padus f. plena

Prunus padus f. plena, commonly known as the double white bird cherry, is a cultivar of the common bird cherry.

It is characterized by its showy, double white flowers that bloom in early spring before the leaves appear. The plant is a deciduous shrub or small tree, typically reaching heights of 3 to 6 meters. Its flowers are borne in clusters and are highly ornamental, making it a popular choice for gardens.

The leaves are ovate with serrated edges, and the fruit is a small, dark red drupe that matures in late summer.
