Panicum trichopodioides

Panicum trichopodioides, commonly known as the silky panicgrass, is a grass species native to parts of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and fluffy, silver-tomentose leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as fields and roadsides. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny grains.

Due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and used in landscaping.
