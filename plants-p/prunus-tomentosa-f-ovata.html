Prunus tomentosa f. ovata

Prunus tomentosa f. ovata, commonly known as the woolly cherry or oval-leaf cherry, is a cultivar of the Prunus tomentosa species.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree characterized by its dense, rounded growth habit and ovate, hairy leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers in early spring, which are followed by edible, sweet-tart red berries. It thrives in well-drained soil and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and winter interest.

The foliage turns a striking red or orange in the fall, adding seasonal color to gardens.
