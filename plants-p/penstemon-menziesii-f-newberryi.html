Penstemon menziesii f. newberryi

Penstemon menziesii f. newberryi, commonly known as Newberry's beardtongue, is a perennial plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to purple. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. It has lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs, and its flowering period occurs in the spring and early summer.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance in gardens.
