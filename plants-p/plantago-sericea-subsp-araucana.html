Plantago sericea subsp. araucana

Plantago sericea subsp.

araucana, commonly known as the Araucanian plantain, is a perennial herb native to South America. It is characterized by its narrow, silver-gray leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive silvery appearance.

The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in dense, spike-like inflorescences. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and forest edges.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
