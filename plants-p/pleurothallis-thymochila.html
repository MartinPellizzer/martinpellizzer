Pleurothallis thymochila

Pleurothallis thymochila, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a species of orchid native to the cloud forests of Central and South America.

It is a small, epiphytic plant that typically grows on tree branches or rocks in humid, shaded environments. The plant features delicate, lance-shaped leaves and produces intricate, fragrant flowers with yellow and green hues. Its flowers are often clustered in small inflorescences, and it blooms during the wet season.

Due to its beauty and rarity, it is sometimes cultivated by orchid enthusiasts but is considered endangered in the wild.
