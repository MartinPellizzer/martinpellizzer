Pentarhizidium orientale var. incisum

Pentarhizidium orientale var. incisum, commonly known as the serrated pentarhizidium, is a rare flowering plant native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped leaves with finely serrated edges. The plant produces small, clustered white flowers that bloom during the spring and early summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest undergrowth.
