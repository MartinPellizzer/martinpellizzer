Polypodium javanicum

Polypodium javanicum, commonly known as the Java fern, is a member of the Polypodiaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial fern characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit and dense, leathery fronds. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky areas. Its fronds are usually pinnately compound, with lance-shaped pinnae that are often covered in small, rounded sori.

Polypodium javanicum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
