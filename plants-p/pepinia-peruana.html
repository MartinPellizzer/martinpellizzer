Pepinia peruana

Pepinia peruana, commonly known as the Peruvian orchid, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of Peru.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or mountainous environments, thriving in cool, humid conditions. Its roots are adapted to absorb nutrients from the thin soil of its habitat.

Pepinia peruana is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
