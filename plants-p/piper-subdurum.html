Piper subdurum

Piper subdurum, commonly known as the roughleaf pepper or mountain pepper, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species. The plant is characterized by its rough, hairy leaves and small, clustered flowers. Its fruits are berries that turn from green to dark purple as they mature.

Piper subdurum is often cultivated for its aromatic leaves and medicinal properties.
