Pandanus apoensis

Pandanus apoensis, commonly known as the Apo pandanus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Pandanaceae.

It is native to the island of Mindanao in the Philippines and thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender leaves that are often used in traditional crafts and as a source of fiber. Its inflorescence consists of a branched structure with small, fragrant flowers that attract pollinators.

The plant plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, contributing to local biodiversity and providing resources for various wildlife species.
