Pteronia quadrifaria

Pteronia quadrifaria, commonly known as the four-leaf pteronia, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Amaryllis family and is characterized by its erect, leafy stems and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers. The plant produces four leaves, which is a distinguishing feature, and the flowers typically range in color from white to pink. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Pteronia quadrifaria is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ease of cultivation.
