Puya castellanosii

Puya castellanosii, commonly known as the Castellanos' puya, is a striking plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its tall, spiky inflorescence that can reach several meters in height. The plant features broad, strap-like leaves that form a rosette, creating a dense, water-holding structure at the base. Puya castellanosii is a perennial plant that thrives in high-altitude environments with cool, humid conditions.
