Primula hakonensis

Primula hakonensis, commonly known as the Hakon's primrose, is a flowering plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of pink and white. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors and mountainous regions. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in a fine, hairy texture, which helps protect it from excessive moisture loss.

Primula hakonensis is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with cool, humid conditions.
