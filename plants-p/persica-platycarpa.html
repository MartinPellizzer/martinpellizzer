Persica platycarpa

Persica platycarpa, commonly known as the flatcarp pear, is a rare and lesser-known species within the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its small, round fruits that are typically yellow or green when ripe. The plant produces white flowers with five petals, which bloom in early spring. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a slightly serrated edge.

Persica platycarpa is native to parts of Central Asia and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.
