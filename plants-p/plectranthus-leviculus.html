Plectranthus leviculus

Plectranthus leviculus, commonly known as the silver bush or velvetleaf, is a perennial shrub native to South Africa.

It features hairy, silver-gray leaves that give it a distinctive appearance, and it produces small, tubular flowers that are typically purple or blue in color. The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade, making it adaptable to various garden environments.
