Pilosella × fuscoatra

Pilosella × fuscoatra, commonly known as the purple-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant is notable for its dark green to purple leaves, which are hairy and often have a bluish tinge. Its flowers are typically yellow, with ray florets and a central disk, and it produces small, dry seeds in a composite head.

Pilosella × fuscoatra is often found in disturbed soils and open habitats, such as roadsides and meadows.
