Polystichum amabile

Polystichum amabile, commonly known as the sweet fern or silver fern, is a species of evergreen fern native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its finely divided, silvery-green fronds that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its fronds are leathery and have a subtle, sweet scent when crushed.

Polystichum amabile is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often used in horticultural settings for its aesthetic appeal.
