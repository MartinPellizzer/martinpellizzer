Parrotiopsis jacquemontiana

Parrotiopsis jacquemontiana, commonly known as the golden rain tree, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking golden-yellow inflorescences that appear in early spring, giving it its common name. The tree has simple, alternate, ovate leaves that turn yellow in autumn. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and urban landscapes.
