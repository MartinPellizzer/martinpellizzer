Pavonia exasperata

Pavonia exasperata, commonly known as the scarlet pavonia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, featuring upright stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, clustered inflorescences of bright red or pink flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its botanical features include opposite leaf arrangement and a fibrous root system, making it well adapted to moist, sunny habitats.

It is often found in wetlands, stream banks, and other damp environments.
