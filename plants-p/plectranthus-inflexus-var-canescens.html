Plectranthus inflexus var. canescens

Plectranthus inflexus var. canescens, commonly known as the woolly plectranthus, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It features aromatic, hairy leaves that are typically gray-green in color, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are usually pink or purple, arranged in clusters. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and containers.

Its ornamental value and adaptability contribute to its popularity among horticulturists and gardeners.
