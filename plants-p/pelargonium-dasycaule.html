Pelargonium dasycaule

Pelargonium dasycaule, commonly known as the woolly geranium, is a species of flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces showy, five-petaled flowers in shades of pink, red, and white, typically blooming in summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is often cultivated in gardens across temperate regions.
