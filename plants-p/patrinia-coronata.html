Patrinia coronata

Patrinia coronata, commonly known as the crownvetch, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to purple, often blooming in late spring to early summer. It is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Patrinia coronata is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in various soil conditions.
