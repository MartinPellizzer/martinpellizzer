Plantago pflanzii var. mollior

Plantago pflanzii var. mollior, commonly known as the soft-leaved plantain, is a variant of the plantain species native to certain regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its broad, soft, and hairy leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette formation. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, which are borne on slender stalks. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, along roadsides, and in cultivated fields.

This variant is valued for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally in herbal remedies.
