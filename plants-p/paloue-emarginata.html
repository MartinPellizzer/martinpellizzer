Paloue emarginata

Paloue emarginata, commonly known as the Margined Paloue, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, palmate leaves that are often edged with a distinct white or pale margin. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-white in color, which bloom during the rainy season. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories or near water bodies.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and private collections.
