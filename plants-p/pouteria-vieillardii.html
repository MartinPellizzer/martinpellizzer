Pouteria vieillardii

Pouteria vieillardii, commonly known as the wild mango or Brazilian mango, is a tropical evergreen tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its large, glossy, elliptical leaves. The tree produces yellowish-green flowers that are typically clustered in panicles, followed by fruits that resemble small mangoes. The fruit has a thick, fibrous rind and a sweet, aromatic pulp.
