Phillyrea longifolia subsp. degenii

Phillyrea longifolia subsp.

degenii, commonly known as the Mediterranean buckthorn, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its small, leathery, elliptical leaves that are typically dark green on top and lighter underneath.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, followed by small, berry-like fruits that are edible but not particularly flavorful. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to tolerate harsh environmental conditions.
