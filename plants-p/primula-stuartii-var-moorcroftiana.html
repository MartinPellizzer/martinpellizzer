Primula stuartii var. moorcroftiana

Primula stuartii var. moorcroftiana, commonly known as the Moorcroft's primrose, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or purple, blooming in early spring. The plant has hairy, dark green leaves that are oval in shape and arranged in a rosette pattern. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens with well-drained soil and partial shade.
