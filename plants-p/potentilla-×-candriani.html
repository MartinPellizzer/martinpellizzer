Potentilla × candriani

Potentilla × candriani, commonly known as the shrub cinquefoil, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, bushy growth habit and produces clusters of bright yellow flowers in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters and has finely divided, feather-like leaves that are green on top and silver-white underneath. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

The botanical features of Potentilla × candriani make it both visually appealing and ecologically valuable in garden ecosystems.
