Pelargonium anethifolium

Pelargonium anethifolium, commonly known as the anise-scented geranium, is a species of flowering plant in the family Geraniaceae.

It is native to South Africa and is characterized by its aromatic, anise-scented leaves and showy, pink or red flowers. The plant typically grows as a compact shrub with hairy stems and lobed leaves that resemble anise leaves. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in aromatherapy due to its pleasant fragrance.

Pelargonium anethifolium prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and container planting.
