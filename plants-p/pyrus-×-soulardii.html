Pyrus × soulardii

Pyrus × soulardii, commonly known as the common whitebeam, is a hybrid species within the genus Pyrus, native to Europe.

It is a deciduous tree or shrub that typically grows to a height of 5–10 meters, featuring a rounded crown and smooth, gray bark. The leaves are alternate, ovate to elliptic, with serrated edges and a glossy green upper surface. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, followed by small, round fruits that are edible but not particularly flavorful.

This plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and its ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
