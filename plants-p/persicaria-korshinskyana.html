Persicaria korshinskyana

Persicaria korshinskyana, commonly known as the Korshinsky's knotweed, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy on the underside. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in dense clusters, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
