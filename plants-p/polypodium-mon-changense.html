Polypodium mon-changense

Polypodium mon-changense, commonly known as the Guangxi huperzia, is a species of fern belonging to the family Huperziaceae.

It is characterized by its evergreen, leathery fronds that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces sporangia on the underside of its fronds, which are clustered in strobila, a feature common among many ferns. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

This species is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and potential medicinal properties.
