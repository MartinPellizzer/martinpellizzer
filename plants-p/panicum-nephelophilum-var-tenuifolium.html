Panicum nephelophilum var. tenuifolium

Panicum nephelophilum var. tenuifolium, commonly known as the cloud-loving grass, is a unique variant of the panicum genus native to high-altitude regions.

It is characterized by its slender, elongated leaves that are adapted to thrive in nutrient-poor, rocky soils. The plant produces small, delicate inflorescences that resemble tufts of hair, which help it capture wind-borne nutrients. Its growth habit is typically low and spreading, forming dense mats that stabilize soil in harsh environments.

This variety is particularly notable for its ability to grow in shaded, misty conditions, making it a rare and ecologically significant species.
