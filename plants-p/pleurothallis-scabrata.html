Pleurothallis scabrata

Pleurothallis scabrata, commonly known as the rough-leaved orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its unique, often hairy or scabrous leaves. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid forest environments. Its flowers are usually small and clustered, displaying a range of colors from white to yellow and pink.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native range.
