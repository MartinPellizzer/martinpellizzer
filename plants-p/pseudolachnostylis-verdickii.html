Pseudolachnostylis verdickii

Pseudolachnostylis verdickii, commonly known as the velvetleaf hibiscus, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, velvety leaves that range in color from deep green to reddish-brown. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red, typically blooming during the summer months. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Pseudolachnostylis verdickii is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking foliage and vibrant floral display.
