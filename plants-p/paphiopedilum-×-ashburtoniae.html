Paphiopedilum × ashburtoniae

Paphiopedilum × ashburtoniae, commonly known as the Ashburton orchid, is a hybrid orchid resulting from the cross between Paphiopedilum niveum and Paphiopedilum suaveolens.

It is a terrestrial or epiphytic plant native to Southeast Asia, characterized by its unique, slipper-shaped pouch that serves as a trap for pollinators. The plant typically produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, often with intricate patterns. It prefers warm, humid environments with indirect light and well-draining soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is widely cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
