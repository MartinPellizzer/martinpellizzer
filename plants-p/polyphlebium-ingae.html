Polyphlebium ingae

Polyphlebium ingae, commonly known as the Java fern, is a species of aquatic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit and its ability to thrive in still or slow-moving freshwater environments. The plant features long, narrow leaves that are typically dark green and often have a waxy surface. It is often used in aquariums due to its aesthetic appeal and ease of care.

Botanically, it belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is notable for its unique adaptation to submerged conditions.
