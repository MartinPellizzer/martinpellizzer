Piper gratum var. gratum

Piper gratum var. gratum, commonly known as the fragrant pepper plant, is a tropical species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, aromatic berries that range in color from green to red when mature. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are ovate with a smooth texture and a slightly glossy appearance.

The fruit is often used in traditional medicine and as a spice due to its pungent flavor and aromatic properties.
