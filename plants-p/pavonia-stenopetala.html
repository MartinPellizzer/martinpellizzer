Pavonia stenopetala

Pavonia stenopetala, commonly known as the narrow-petaled wood rose, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, pink to red flowers. The plant produces small, bean-like pods that contain several seeds, typical of the Fabaceae family. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often found in moist, well-drained soils in woodland areas.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardeners seeking native, drought-tolerant plants.
