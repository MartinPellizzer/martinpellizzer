Paullinia echinata

Paullinia echinata, commonly known as the Brazilian nut tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is characterized by its thick, leathery leaves and clusters of yellowish-green flowers. The tree produces large, woody pods that contain the edible seeds, known as Brazil nuts. Its botanical structure includes a straight trunk and can grow up to 30 meters tall.

The plant is also notable for its ability to fix nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility in its ecosystem.
