Piper varibracteum

Piper varibracteum, commonly known as the variable bract pepper, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its climbing habit and dark green, glossy leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by berries that range in color from yellow to red. It is valued for its aromatic leaves, which are used in traditional medicine and as a spice.

The botanical features of Piper varibracteum make it an interesting subject for both ecological and horticultural study.
