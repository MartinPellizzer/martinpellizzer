Passiflora tatei

Passiflora tatei, commonly known as the Japanese passionflower, is a climbing vine native to Japan.

It features showy, complex flowers with five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments that resemble a crown of thorns. The plant produces blue or violet berries that are edible and often used in traditional cuisine. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is valued for its ornamental beauty.

Its botanical structure reflects the diverse adaptations of the Passifloraceae family.
