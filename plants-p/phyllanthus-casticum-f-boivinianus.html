Phyllanthus casticum f. boivinianus

Phyllanthus casticum f. boivinianus, commonly known as the Boivin's spurge, is a variant of the spurge family (Euphorbiaceae) native to tropical regions.

It is characterized by its small, alternate leaves that are typically elliptical in shape and have a smooth or slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in cyathia, typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

The leaves and stems contain latex, which can be irritating to the skin.
