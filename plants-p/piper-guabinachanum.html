Piper guabinachanum

Piper guabinachanum, commonly known as the Guabinachanum pepper, is a flowering plant native to the rainforests of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species like black pepper. The plant produces small, green berries that turn red when ripe, containing tiny seeds used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are often used in local remedies due to their aromatic properties.

The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
