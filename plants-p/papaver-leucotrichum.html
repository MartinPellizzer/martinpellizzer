Papaver leucotrichum

Papaver leucotrichum, commonly known as the white poppy, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It produces striking white to pale pink flowers with prominent yellow stamens, which bloom in early summer. The plant has a taproot system and thrives in well-drained, sandy or loamy soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Papaver leucotrichum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
