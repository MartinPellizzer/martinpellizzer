Pelargonium × heraldicum

Pelargonium × heraldicum, commonly known as the German geranium, is a hybrid species within the Geraniaceae family.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, five-petaled flowers that come in a range of colors, including pink, red, and white. The plant typically has deeply lobed leaves with a serrated margin, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a potted plant. Native to southern Africa, it thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its flowering period usually occurs in late spring to early summer, adding vibrant color to landscapes during that time.
