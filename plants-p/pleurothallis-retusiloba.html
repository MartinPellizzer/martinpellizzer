Pleurothallis retusiloba

Pleurothallis retusiloba, commonly known as the straight-stemmed orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded forest environments. Its leaves are usually elongated and fleshy, adapted to store water in its natural habitat.

Due to its delicate appearance and specific ecological requirements, it is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts in controlled conditions.
