Polypodium albosquamatum

Polypodium albosquamatum, commonly known as the white-scaled huperzia, is a species of fern belonging to the family Hypnaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered with a whitish, powdery bloom. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky crevices. Its fronds are usually dichotomous, meaning they split into two equal parts at each node.

Polypodium albosquamatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
