Pandanus letocartiorum

Pandanus letocartiorum, commonly known as the Solomon Islands pandanus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Pandanaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of the South Pacific, particularly the Solomon Islands. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, palm-like leaves that are often used in traditional crafts. It produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and cultural significance in local communities.
