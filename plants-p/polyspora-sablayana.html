Polyspora sablayana

Polyspora sablayana, commonly known as the Sablayan polypody, is a fern native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its delicate, strap-shaped fronds that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest undergrowth or along stream banks. Its rhizome is perennial and produces numerous small, spore-bearing leaves.

The botanical features of Polyspora sablayana make it an important species in both ecological and medicinal contexts.
