Pilosella aguilellae

Pilosella aguilellae, commonly known as the Argentine hawk's-beard, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single bloom. The plant typically grows in grasslands and open areas, thriving in temperate climates. Its leaves are hairy and basal, while the flowers are yellow with prominent central disk.

Pilosella aguilellae is often found in regions with well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand seasonal droughts.
