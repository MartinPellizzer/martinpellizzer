Passiflora lanceolata

Passiflora lanceolata, commonly known as the lanceleaf passionflower, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, lance-shaped leaves and showy, five-petaled flowers that often feature a crown-like structure. The plant produces edible, orange-yellow berries that are rich in vitamin C. It thrives in humid, warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Passiflora lanceolata is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential calming effects.
