Prunella ovata

Prunella ovata, commonly known as selfheal, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, violet-blue flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and gardens.

Prunella ovata is known for its medicinal properties and has been used historically for its anti-inflammatory and healing benefits.
