Primula acaulis var. alba

Primula acaulis var. alba, commonly known as the white rock primrose, is a flowering plant native to mountainous regions of Europe.

It is a perennial species that thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade. The plant features a compact, low-growing form with hairy stems and clustered leaves. Its flowers are white or pale pink, with five petals and a distinctive yellow throat, blooming in early spring.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in alpine gardens.
