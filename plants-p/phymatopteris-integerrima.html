Phymatopteris integerrima

Phymatopteris integerrima, commonly known as the rock fern, is a species of fern native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its compact, evergreen fronds that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments. Its fronds are pinnately divided, with a dense arrangement of small, rounded leaflets.

Phymatopteris integerrima is adapted to withstand drought conditions, making it a resilient plant in harsh climates.
