Pachyphyllum debedoutii

Pachyphyllum debedoutii, commonly known as the Debedout's stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that store water, allowing it to survive in dry conditions. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow and orange during the warmer months. It thrives in full sun and well-draining soil, making it suitable for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is a popular choice among succulent enthusiasts.
