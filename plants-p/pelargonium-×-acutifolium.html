Pelargonium × acutifolium

Pelargonium × acutifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaved geranium, is a hybrid species within the Geraniaceae family.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink, red, or white, which bloom throughout the growing season. It is native to South Africa and is widely cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and hardiness.

Pelargonium × acutifolium thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for gardens and container planting.
