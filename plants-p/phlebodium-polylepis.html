Phlebodium polylepis

Phlebodium polylepis, commonly known as the giant tree fern, is a species native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, feathery fronds that can reach lengths of up to 3 meters. The plant has a tall, unbranched trunk covered in a layer of persistent, fibrous roots. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

Its unique appearance and slow growth make it a popular specimen in botanical gardens and conservation efforts.
