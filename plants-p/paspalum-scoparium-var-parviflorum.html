Paspalum scoparium var. parviflorum

Paspalum scoparium var. parviflorum, commonly known as the small-flower paspalum, is a perennial grass native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetland or riparian habitats. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, and it produces small, white to pale blue flowers.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in aquatic environments.
