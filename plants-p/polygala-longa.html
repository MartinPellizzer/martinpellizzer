Polygala longa

Polygala longa, commonly known as the long-stemmed milkweed, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia, including China and Japan.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and clusters of small, pale yellow or greenish flowers. The plant belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is known for its unique floral structure, which resembles a spire. Its leaves are opposite and lance-shaped, often with a hairy texture.

Polygala longa is typically found in moist, open habitats such as meadows and forest edges.
