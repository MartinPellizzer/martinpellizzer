Polygonatum officinale var. formosanum

Polygonatum officinale var. formosanum, commonly known as the Formosan Solomon's Seal, is a perennial herb native to eastern Asia, particularly Taiwan.

It features upright, slender stems that bear lance-shaped leaves arranged in a spiral pattern. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers in early summer, followed by bright red berries. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its rhizomes are valued in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
