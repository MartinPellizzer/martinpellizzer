Palmijuncus manan

Palmijuncus manan, commonly known as the Manan palm, is a rare and unique species native to the remote islands of the Indian Ocean.

It is characterized by its slender, unbranched trunk and a crown of elongated, narrow leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant exhibits a slow growth rate and is adapted to thrive in nutrient-poor soils and arid conditions. Its reproductive structures are relatively simple, with small, clustered flowers that develop into drupes.

Due to habitat loss and limited distribution, Palmijuncus manan is considered a vulnerable species requiring conservation efforts.
