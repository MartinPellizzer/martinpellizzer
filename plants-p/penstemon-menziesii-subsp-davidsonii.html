Penstemon menziesii subsp. davidsonii

Penstemon menziesii subsp.

davidsonii, commonly known as Davidson's beardtongue, is a perennial plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of tubular, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant features hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often bluish-green in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance, providing nectar for pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
