Platanthera brevicalcarata subsp. brevicalcarata

Platanthera brevicalcarata subsp.

brevicalcarata, commonly known as the short-spurred lady's tresses, is a perennial orchid native to North America. It is characterized by its dense, dark green leaves and its distinctive, fragrant, white to pale yellow flower spike.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and wet meadows. It has a rhizomatous root system and produces small, tubular flowers that bloom in late summer.

The species is known for its ecological importance as a nectar source for various pollinators.
