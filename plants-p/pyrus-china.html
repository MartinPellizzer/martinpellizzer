Pyrus china

Pyrus china, commonly known as the Chinese white pear, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray-barked trunk and alternate, simple leaves. The tree produces clusters of white, fragrant flowers in early spring, followed by edible, sweet fruits that ripen in late summer.

Its fruit is widely cultivated for its culinary and medicinal uses, and it is also valued for its ornamental beauty in gardens.
