Phthirusa robusta

Phthirusa robusta, commonly known as the robust thistle, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and robust, spiny stems. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and is known for its tall, upright growth habit. Its leaves are generally gray-green with a rough texture, and the flowers are yellow, appearing in clusters.

Phthirusa robusta is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for various purposes.
