Pandanus humicola

Pandanus humicola, commonly known as the swamp pandanus, is a species of flowering plant in the genus Pandanus.

It is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including parts of Indonesia and Papua New Guinea. This plant is characterized by its tall, slender stems and long, narrow leaves that are often used in traditional crafts. The plant thrives in wetland and swampy environments, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various organisms.

Its fruit, which contains a single seed, is dispersed by water and birds, contributing to its ecological significance.
