Pericallis malvifolia

Pericallis malvifolia, commonly known as the purplemallow, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes hibiscus and cotton. The plant is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from white to deep purple. Its leaves are broad and velvety, with a distinct pubescent texture.

Pericallis malvifolia thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
