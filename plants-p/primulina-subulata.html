Primulina subulata

Primulina subulata, commonly known as the needleleaf gloxinia, is a species of flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Gesneriaceae family and is characterized by its striking, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to red. The plant features small, needle-like leaves that give it its common name, and it is often cultivated as a houseplant due to its ornamental value. Its unique foliage and showy blooms make it a popular choice among plant enthusiasts.
