Pinanga elmeri

Pinanga elmeri, commonly known as the Elmer's bower vine, is a climbing plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy inflorescences. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by bright red berries, which are a key feature in its reproductive cycle. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in tropical gardens.
