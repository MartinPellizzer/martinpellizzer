Panicum notarisii

Panicum notarisii, commonly known as the Notarisii grass, is a species of flowering plant in the grass family Poaceae.

It is native to certain regions of Africa, particularly South Africa, where it thrives in dry, sandy soils. The plant is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, blade-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its inflorescence consists of a panicle made up of numerous small, spikelet-like flowers.

This species is of interest to botanists due to its unique adaptations to arid environments and potential ecological significance in its native habitat.
