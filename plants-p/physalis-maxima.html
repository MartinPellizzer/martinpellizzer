Physalis maxima

Physalis maxima, commonly known as the common ground cherry, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its hairy, inflated calyx that encloses the fruit, which resembles a small tomato. The plant produces yellow-orange berries that are edible when fully ripe. It thrives in warm climates and is often grown as an ornamental or for its edible fruits.

The botanical features of Physalis maxima make it a unique and valuable species in both horticulture and agriculture.
