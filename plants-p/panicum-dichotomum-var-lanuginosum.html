Panicum dichotomum var. lanuginosum

Panicum dichotomum var. lanuginosum, commonly known as silky foxtail, is a grass species native to warm temperate and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and distinctive silky, silver-hairy leaves that give it a soft appearance. The plant produces long, narrow spikelets that are often tinged with red or purple at maturity. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can become invasive in certain environments.
