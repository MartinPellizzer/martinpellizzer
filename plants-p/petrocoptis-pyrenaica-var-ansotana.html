Petrocoptis pyrenaica var. ansotana

Petrocoptis pyrenaica var. ansotana, commonly known as the Pyrenean rock cress, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It thrives in rocky and calcareous soils, often found in mountainous regions with high altitudes. The plant features basal leaves that are elliptic to lanceolate in shape, with a hairy surface. Its small, white or pale yellow flowers are arranged in clusters and bloom from spring to early summer.

It is adapted to harsh environments, showcasing resilience to cold and drought conditions.
