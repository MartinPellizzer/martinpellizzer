Pyrethrum ordubadense

Pyrethrum ordubadense, commonly known as the Ordubad pyrethrum, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its daisy-like flowers with yellow petals and a central disc. The plant produces fine, hair-like structures called trichomes, which are the source of its natural insecticidal compounds. These compounds, known as pyrethrins, are effective against a variety of insects.

Pyrethrum ordubadense is valued for its ornamental appeal and its potential use in organic pest control.
