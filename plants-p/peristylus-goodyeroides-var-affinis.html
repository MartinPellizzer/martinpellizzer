Peristylus goodyeroides var. affinis

Peristylus goodyeroides var. affinis, commonly known as the Goodyera-like peristylus, is a flowering plant native to southern China and parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its delicate, grass-like foliage and small, greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest understories. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to form dense colonies, and it has adapted to thrive in nutrient-poor soils.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique appearance.
