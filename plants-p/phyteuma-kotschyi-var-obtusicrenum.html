Phyteuma kotschyi var. obtusicrenum

Phyteuma kotschyi var. obtusicrenum, commonly known as the Armenian bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus.

It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue to purple. The leaves are lanceolate and often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its rugged appearance. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Its botanical significance lies in its unique floral structure and its role in local ecosystems.
