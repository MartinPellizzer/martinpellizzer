Polygala sanguinea f. albescens

Polygala sanguinea f. albescens, commonly known as the white-flowered milkweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, small, white or pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. Its leaves are opposite and lance-shaped, with a hairy texture.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
