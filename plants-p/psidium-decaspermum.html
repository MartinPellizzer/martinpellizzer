Psidium decaspermum

Psidium decaspermum, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or cupuacu, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Amazon basin.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and fragrant white flowers. The plant produces large, yellow-orange fruits that are rich in nutrients and have a creamy, aromatic pulp. Its botanical features include smooth, greyish bark and clusters of small, oval leaves.

Psidium decaspermum is valued for its ornamental appeal and economic importance in local communities.
