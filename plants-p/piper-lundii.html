Piper lundii

Piper lundii, commonly known as the African pepper plant, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species. The plant is characterized by its vine-like growth habit and compound leaves with serrated edges. It produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into spherical berries.

The berries are typically harvested when they turn red and are used in traditional medicine and as a spice.
