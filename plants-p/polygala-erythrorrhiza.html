Polygala erythrorrhiza

Polygala erythrorrhiza, commonly known as the red-root milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to southern China and parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, clustered flowers that range in color from pink to purple. The plant has a fibrous root system, which contributes to its name and aids in its ability to thrive in well-drained soils. It belongs to the family Polygalaceae, which includes a variety of plants with distinctive floral structures.
