Psidium tomentellum

Psidium tomentellum, commonly known as the woolly guava, is a small evergreen shrub native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its hairy, grayish leaves and small, white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces edible fruits that are typically small, round, and have a tart, acidic flavor. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value as well as its fruit.

The species is also valued for its ability to tolerate poor soil conditions and its use in traditional medicine.
