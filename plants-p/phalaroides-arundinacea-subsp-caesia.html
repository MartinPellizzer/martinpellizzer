Phalaroides arundinacea subsp. caesia

Phalaroides arundinacea subsp.

caesia, commonly known as the slender jointed grass, is a perennial herb native to parts of Europe and Asia. It is characterized by its slender, cylindrical stems and narrow, grass-like leaves.

The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and wetland conservation.
