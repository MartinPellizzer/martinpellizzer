Prunus hypotricha

Prunus hypotricha, commonly known as the Mexican wild cherry, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to Mexico and is characterized by its small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, dark purple to black fruits that are edible but not widely cultivated. It typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Prunus hypotricha is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
