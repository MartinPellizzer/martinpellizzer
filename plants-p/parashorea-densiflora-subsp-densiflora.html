Parashorea densiflora subsp. densiflora

Parashorea densiflora subsp.

densiflora, commonly known as the dense-flowered parashorea, is a species of evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia. It belongs to the Dipterocarpaceae family and is characterized by its lanceolate, dark green leaves with prominent veins.

The tree produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in dense clusters, which are typically pollinated by insects. Its fruit is a capsule that releases winged seeds, facilitating wind dispersal.

This species is valued for its hard, durable wood and plays an important ecological role in tropical rainforests.
