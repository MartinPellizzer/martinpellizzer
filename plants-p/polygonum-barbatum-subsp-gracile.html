Polygonum barbatum subsp. gracile

Polygonum barbatum subsp.

gracile, commonly known as slender knotweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of Asia. It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, which includes various species of buckwheat and knotweed.

The plant is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, clustered flowers that bloom in summer. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.
