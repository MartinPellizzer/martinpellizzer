Potentilla pseudosericea var. grandiflora

Potentilla pseudosericea var. grandiflora, commonly known as the large-flowered cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces showy, yellowish-orange flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant features palmately divided leaves with five to seven leaflets, and its stems are often covered in fine, silvery hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine landscapes.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its hardy nature and long blooming period.
