Paspalum splendens

Paspalum splendens, commonly known as the golden paspalum, is a perennial grass native to South America.

It is characterized by its bright yellow inflorescence, which gives it its common name. The plant typically grows in moist to wet environments, such as marshes and riparian zones. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like flowers.

Paspalum splendens is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its attractive appearance and adaptability.
