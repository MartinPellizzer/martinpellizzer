Paphiopedilum micranthum

Paphiopedilum micranthum, commonly known as the dwarf lady's slipper orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features a short, hairy stem and produces small, greenish-yellow flowers with a distinctive pouch-like lip. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist forest understories, preferring rich, well-drained soil. Its leaves are elliptical and arranged in a rosette, contributing to its compact appearance.

Paphiopedilum micranthum is valued for its unique floral structure and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.
