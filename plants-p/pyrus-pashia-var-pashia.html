Pyrus pashia var. pashia

Pyrus pashia var. pashia, commonly known as the Chinese white pear, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its white to yellow flowers and edible, sweet fruits. The leaves are alternate, ovate, and have serrated edges. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and prefers temperate climates.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and fruit production in horticulture.
