Premna resinosa f. grossedentata

Premna resinosa f. grossedentata, commonly known as the hairy-leafed premna, is a variant of the Premna resinosa species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree characterized by its hairy stems and opposite, ovate leaves with serrated margins. The leaves are typically dark green on the upper surface and lighter on the underside, with prominent veins. It produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, followed by small, fleshy berries that are edible for some wildlife.

This plant is often found in moist, shaded areas of tropical forests and is valued for its ornamental and medicinal properties.
