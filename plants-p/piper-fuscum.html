Piper fuscum

Piper fuscum, commonly known as the black pepper plant, is a tropical vine native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dark, glossy leaves and small, clustered flowers that develop into the peppercorns used in culinary and medicinal applications. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth. Its fruit, which turns from green to black as it matures, is the primary part harvested for its pungent flavor and medicinal properties.

Piper fuscum is also valued for its aromatic leaves, which are used in traditional herbal remedies.
