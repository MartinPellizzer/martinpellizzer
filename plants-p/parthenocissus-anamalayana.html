Parthenocissus anamalayana

Parthenocissus anamalayana, commonly known as the Malagasy ivy, is a climbing vine native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and produces small, red berries that are edible. The plant has tendrils that allow it to climb and attach to surfaces, making it a common sight in forested areas. Its botanical features include compound leaves and a woody stem with a hairy texture.

It is often found in humid, tropical climates and plays a role in local ecosystems.
