Picea abies f. virgata

Picea abies f. virgata, commonly known as the dwarf spruce, is a compact form of the Norway spruce.

It is characterized by its dense, slow-growing foliage and conical shape, making it a popular ornamental plant. The leaves are short, stiff, and dark green, often with a slight blue or silver hue. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, preferring cooler climates.

This cultivar is valued for its aesthetic appeal and resilience in garden settings.
