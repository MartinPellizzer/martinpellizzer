Potentilla poterioides

Potentilla poterioides, commonly known as the alpine cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is a low-growing shrub that typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters, with woody stems and small, hairy leaves. The plant produces bright yellow flowers in clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a slightly serrated edge.

Potentilla poterioides thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.
