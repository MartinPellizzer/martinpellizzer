Piper basilanum

Piper basilanum, commonly known as wild pepper, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species. The plant is characterized by its small, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of tiny, greenish-white flowers. It produces small, round berries that turn red when mature.

Piper basilanum is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
