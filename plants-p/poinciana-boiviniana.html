Poinciana boiviniana

Poinciana boiviniana, commonly known as the flame tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its striking, bright red or orange-pink bracts that resemble flames, which appear during the flowering season. The plant has pinnately compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. It produces small, yellow flowers that are followed by pod-like fruits containing flat, dark brown seeds.

Poinciana boiviniana thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
