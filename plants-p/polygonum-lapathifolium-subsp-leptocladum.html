Polygonum lapathifolium subsp. leptocladum

Polygonum lapathifolium subsp.

leptocladum, commonly known as narrow-leaved smartweed, is a flowering plant native to temperate regions of Asia. It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, characterized by its distinctive bracteate inflorescences and arrow-shaped leaves.

The plant typically grows in moist soils along stream banks, wet meadows, and other wetland habitats. Its flowers are small and clustered in dense, upright spikes, and it produces small, three-lobed seeds.

The species is often used in traditional medicine and is also valued for its ornamental qualities in gardens.
