Pouteria purusiana

Pouteria purusiana, commonly known as the Brazil nut tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Amazon Basin.

It is characterized by its large, glossy green leaves and produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The tree's most notable feature is its hard-shelled fruit, which contains edible seeds known as Brazil nuts. These nuts are rich in nutrients, particularly selenium and healthy fats.

The plant plays a significant ecological role in its native habitat, supporting a diverse range of wildlife.
