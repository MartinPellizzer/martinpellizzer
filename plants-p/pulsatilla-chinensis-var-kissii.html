Pulsatilla chinensis var. kissii

Pulsatilla chinensis var. kissii, commonly known as the Chinese pasqueflower, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its showy, pale yellow to pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has a short, upright stem with palmately divided leaves that are covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained, alkaline soils and is often found in rocky or grassy habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
