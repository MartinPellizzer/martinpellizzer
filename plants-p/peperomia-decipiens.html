Peperomia decipiens

Peperomia decipiens, commonly known as the False Water Shield, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its compact, rosette-like growth habit. The plant features small, waxy leaves that are often covered in a fine, powdery coating, which helps reduce water loss. Its flowers are tiny and clustered in spikes, typically appearing in shades of green or red.

Peperomia decipiens is often grown as a houseplant due to its low maintenance and attractive foliage.
