Panicum thouarsianum

Panicum thouarsianum, commonly known as the Madagascar grass or tufted panicgrass, is a perennial grass species native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, two-lobed flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
