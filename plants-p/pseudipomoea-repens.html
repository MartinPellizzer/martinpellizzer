Pseudipomoea repens

Pseudipomoea repens, commonly known as the creeping morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is a vine that produces trumpet-shaped, showy flowers in shades of blue, purple, and white. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and is often found growing along the ground or climbing supports. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and is often used in ornamental gardening.

Pseudipomoea repens is also known for its rapid growth and ability to spread quickly in suitable conditions.
