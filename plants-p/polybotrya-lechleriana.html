Polybotrya lechleriana

Polybotrya lechleriana, commonly known as the Lechler's orchid, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its unique, intricate floral structure. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are small but striking, often displaying a mix of white, green, and yellow hues.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by plant enthusiasts.
