Prunella laciniata var. caerulea

Prunella laciniata var. caerulea, commonly known as blue dead-nettle, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features slender, erect stems that are often reddish-purple in color, covered with fine hairs. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically blue or purple, arranged in whorls along the stem. Its leaves are opposite, ovate to lanceolate, and have a serrated edge.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.
