Populus × juliana-pendula

Populus × juliana-pendula, commonly known as the weeping poplar, is a hybrid deciduous tree that exhibits striking ornamental features.

It is characterized by its cascading branches that form a weeping habit, giving it a distinctive and elegant appearance. The leaves are broad, heart-shaped, and turn yellow in the fall, adding seasonal visual interest. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This plant is often cultivated in gardens and parks for its aesthetic value and adaptability to various climates.
