Polystachya inaperta

Polystachya inaperta, commonly known as the unopened polystachya, is a member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa and is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense foliage. The plant produces inflorescences that remain closed for a long period, giving it its common name. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a smooth, waxy surface.

Polystachya inaperta is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and relatively low maintenance requirements.
