Potentilla mathonetii

Potentilla mathonetii, commonly known as the alpine potentilla, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a low-growing shrub that typically reaches a height of about 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features small, white or yellowish flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are usually narrow and slightly hairy, adapted to withstand cold and arid conditions.

Potentilla mathonetii is often found in rocky or gravelly soils at high elevations, where it thrives in full sun.
