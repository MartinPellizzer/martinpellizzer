Peristylus gracilis subsp. gracilis

Peristylus gracilis subsp.

gracilis, commonly known as the slender peristylus, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa. It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky outcrops.

The plant features slender, erect stems that are often branched and covered with fine hairs. Its small, greenish-white flowers are arranged in dense clusters at the tips of the branches.

The leaves are narrow and linear, contributing to its overall delicate appearance.
