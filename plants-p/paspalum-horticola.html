Paspalum horticola

Paspalum horticola, commonly known as the ornamental grass or garden paspalum, is a perennial plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its clumping growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often clustered in dense inflorescences. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for xeriscaping and ornamental gardens.

Paspalum horticola is valued for its aesthetic appeal and low maintenance requirements.
