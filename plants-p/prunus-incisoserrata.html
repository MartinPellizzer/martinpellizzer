Prunus incisoserrata

Prunus incisoserrata, commonly known as the Japanese cherry plum, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Japan.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its ornamental value, particularly during spring when it produces clusters of white or pink flowers. The plant has serrated leaves with a slightly wavy edge, and its fruits are small, sweet plums that ripen in summer.

It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
