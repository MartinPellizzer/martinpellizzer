Plexipus rariflorus

Plexipus rariflorus, commonly known as the rare-flower plexipus, is a unique plant species characterized by its striking, bioluminescent petals that glow faintly in low light conditions.

Native to the misty cloud forests of the Andes, it thrives in cool, humid environments with high atmospheric moisture. Its leaves are broad and waxy, adapted to retain water in its native habitat. The plant produces flowers only once every five years, making it a rare and sought-after specimen in botanical gardens.

Due to its endangered status, conservation efforts are underway to protect and propagate this extraordinary species.
