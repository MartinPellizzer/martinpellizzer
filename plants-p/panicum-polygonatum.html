Panicum polygonatum

Panicum polygonatum, commonly known as Solomon's seal, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to eastern Asia.

It features tall, unbranched stems that bear lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. The plant produces red berries that are edible and often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in shaded to partially shaded environments and prefers moist, well-drained soil.

Its unique flowering pattern and medicinal properties make it a valued species in both horticulture and herbal practices.
