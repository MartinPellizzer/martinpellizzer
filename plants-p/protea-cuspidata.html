Protea cuspidata

Protea cuspidata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved protea, is a striking South African plant belonging to the Proteaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescence composed of numerous small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or white in color. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is adapted to the Mediterranean climate of its native region.

Protea cuspidata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also an important species in the conservation of South Africa's unique flora.
