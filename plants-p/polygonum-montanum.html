Polygonum montanum

Polygonum montanum, commonly known as mountain smartweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, which is characterized by distinctive nodes along its stems. The plant typically grows in moist, rocky habitats such as meadows and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped with a reddish tint, and it produces small, pinkish to red flowers in clusters.

Polygonum montanum is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for wildlife.
