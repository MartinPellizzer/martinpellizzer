Piper berlandieri f. nova

Piper berlandieri f. nova, commonly known as the Mexican pepper plant, is a member of the Piperaceae family.

It is a climbing shrub characterized by its opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves with serrated margins. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in spikes, which are followed by elongated, three-lobed berries. Native to Mexico and Central America, it thrives in humid, tropical environments.

Its botanical features make it a subject of interest in both horticulture and botanical research.
