Poa chubutensis

Poa chubutensis, commonly known as Chubut grass, is a perennial grass species native to South America, particularly Argentina and Chile.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences containing numerous tiny flowers, which are adapted for wind pollination. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in grasslands and disturbed soils.

Due to its hardy nature, Poa chubutensis is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
