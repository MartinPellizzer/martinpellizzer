Papaver coreanum

Papaver coreanum, commonly known as the Korean poppy, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Korea.

It features striking, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from red to orange, often with a dark center. The plant has palmately lobed leaves that are deeply divided, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Its botanical significance lies in its role as a source of alkaloids and its use in traditional medicine.
