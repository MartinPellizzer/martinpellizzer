Pedicularis habachanensis subsp. multipinnata

Pedicularis habachanensis subsp.

multipinnata, commonly known as the Himalayan lousewort, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas. It is characterized by its striking red or orange flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant has a creeping rhizome and produces numerous small, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in rocky and alpine environments, often growing in open, sunny habitats.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in the study of alpine flora and pollination ecology.
