Papaver hookeri

Papaver hookeri, commonly known as Hooker's poppy, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that range in color from deep red to orange, often with a dark central spot. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a hairy texture, while its stems are sturdy and branching.

Papaver hookeri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
