Pavonia racemosa

Pavonia racemosa, commonly known as the scarlet wood rose, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial shrub that produces showy, red or pink bract-like flowers that resemble roses, which bloom in the summer months. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It is often found in moist, well-drained soils along stream banks and in woodland areas.

The botanical features of Pavonia racemosa make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and ecological restoration projects.
