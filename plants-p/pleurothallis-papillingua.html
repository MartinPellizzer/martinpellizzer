Pleurothallis papillingua

Pleurothallis papillingua, commonly known as the pink lady's tresses, is a member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is a small, epiphytic orchid native to the cloud forests of Central and South America. The plant features delicate, spirally arranged leaves and produces clusters of small, fragrant, pinkish-white flowers. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining, organic substrates.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts for its ornamental value.
