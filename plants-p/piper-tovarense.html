Piper tovarense

Piper tovarense, commonly known as the Tovarense pepper, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its climbing habit, with opposite, ovate leaves that have a glossy surface. Its small, greenish-white flowers are followed by elongated, peppery fruits that are used in local cuisines.

The plant's botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and culinary contexts.
