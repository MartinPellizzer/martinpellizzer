Penstemon murrayanus

Penstemon murrayanus, commonly known as Murray's beardtongue, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, upright stems and clusters of tubular, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically hairy and arranged oppositely along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy perennial suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Penstemon murrayanus is also valued for its ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
