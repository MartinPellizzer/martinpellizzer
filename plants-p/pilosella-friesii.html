Pilosella friesii

Pilosella friesii, commonly known as the Alpine hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to the alpine regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are usually hairy and lobed, while its flowers are yellow with a central disk.

Pilosella friesii is often found in mountain meadows and is adapted to withstand harsh environmental conditions.
