Phylica orientalis

Phylica orientalis, commonly known as the oriental bottlebrush, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, cylindrical flower clusters that resemble bottle brushes, typically pink or red in color. The plant produces small, four-lobed leaves that are narrow and slightly hairy. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Phylica orientalis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
