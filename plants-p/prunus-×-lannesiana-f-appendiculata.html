Prunus × lannesiana f. appendiculata

Prunus × lannesiana f. appendiculata, commonly known as the Japanese flowering cherry with appendiculate bracts.

This cultivar is characterized by its distinctive floral bracts that are elongated and appendiculate, adding a unique visual element to its blossoms. It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is a hybrid of Prunus lannesiana, known for its ornamental value in gardens. The plant typically produces clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in early spring.

Its botanical features make it a sought-after specimen for horticultural display and botanical study.
