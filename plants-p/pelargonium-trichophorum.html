Pelargonium trichophorum

Pelargonium trichophorum, commonly known as the woolly geranium, is a species of flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces showy, five-petaled flowers in shades of pink and purple, typically blooming in spring and summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Pelargonium trichophorum is also valued for its aromatic leaves, which are often used in herbal teas and traditional medicine.
