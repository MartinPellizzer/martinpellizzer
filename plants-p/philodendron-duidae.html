Philodendron duidae

Philodendron duidae, commonly known as the Duidae philodendron, is a climbing vine native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It features heart-shaped, glossy leaves that are often variegated with white or cream markings. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are followed by rounded, green berries. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires consistently moist, well-draining soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in shaded outdoor gardens.
