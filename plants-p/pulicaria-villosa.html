Pulicaria villosa

Pulicaria villosa, commonly known as hairy tansy, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and fibrous root system. The plant features grayish-green, hairy leaves that are opposite and lanceolate in shape. Its yellow flower heads appear in late spring to early summer, producing small, tubular florets.

Pulicaria villosa is often found in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.
