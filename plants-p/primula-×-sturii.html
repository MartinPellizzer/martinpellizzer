Primula × sturii

Primula × sturii, commonly known as the hybrid primrose, is a flowering plant belonging to the Primulaceae family.

It is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Primula vulgaris and Primula obconica, showcasing a blend of characteristics from both parent species. The plant typically produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink, white, and purple, which bloom in early spring. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value.

Its foliage consists of broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, white down.
