Phaca sesquiflora

Phaca sesquiflora, commonly known as the narrow-leaved phaca, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in fynbos vegetation. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring.

Phaca sesquiflora is known for its resilience to drought and its role in supporting local pollinators.
