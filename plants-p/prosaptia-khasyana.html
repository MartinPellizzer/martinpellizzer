Prosaptia khasyana

Prosaptia khasyana, commonly known as the Khasi orchid, is a species of orchid native to the northeastern region of India.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stem and clusters of small, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and pale pink. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in the undergrowth of dense forests. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered with a fine, waxy coating that helps retain moisture.

Prosaptia khasyana plays an important ecological role in its habitat by providing nectar for various pollinators.
