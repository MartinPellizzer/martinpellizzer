Piper dryadum

Piper dryadum, commonly known as the dryad's pipe or the ghost pipe, is a flowering plant native to the rainforests of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Piper genus in the family Piperaceae, which includes many species of flowering plants. The plant is characterized by its slender, vine-like stems and small, heart-shaped leaves. It produces clusters of tiny, greenish-white flowers that are often hidden among the foliage.

The fruit is a small, three-lobed berry that turns dark when mature.
