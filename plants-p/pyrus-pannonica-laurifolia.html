Pyrus pannonica laurifolia

Pyrus pannonica laurifolia, commonly known as the laurelleaf pear, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Carpathian Basin.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage that remains lush throughout the growing season. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in the spring, followed by edible, sweet fruits that are similar to pears. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas or along riverbanks.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
