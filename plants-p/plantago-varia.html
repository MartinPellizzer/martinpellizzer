Plantago varia

Plantago varia, commonly known as variable plantain, is a species of flowering plant in the family Plantaginaceae.

It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, and it thrives in moist, disturbed soils such as roadsides and meadows. The plant has broad, oval leaves with a distinctive waxy coating and produces small, greenish flowers in clusters. It is often used in traditional medicine for its soothing properties.

The plant is also known for its ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes, making it a common and resilient weed.
