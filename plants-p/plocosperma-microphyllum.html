Plocosperma microphyllum

Plocosperma microphyllum, commonly known as the small-leaved plocosperma, is a member of the Ericaceae family, characterized by its preference for acidic, well-drained soils.

It is a low-growing shrub with compact, evergreen foliage that features small, leathery leaves. The plant produces tiny, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or white, which bloom in early spring. Its fruit is a small, capsule-like structure that contains several seeds.

Plocosperma microphyllum is often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and rocky slopes.
