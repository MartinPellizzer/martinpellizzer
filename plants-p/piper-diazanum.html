Piper diazanum

Piper diazanum, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces berries that are often used in traditional medicine. It is also known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its rapid growth rate.

However, it can become invasive in certain regions, posing a challenge to native ecosystems.
