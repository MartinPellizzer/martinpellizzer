Pleurothallis caliensis

Pleurothallis caliensis, commonly known as the Calien's orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Ecuador.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its small, intricate flowers that often display a range of colors from white to pale pink. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its botanical features include narrow, strap-like leaves and a compact inflorescence with numerous fragrant blooms.

Pleurothallis caliensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts for its unique floral display.
