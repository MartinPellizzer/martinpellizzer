Potamogeton zosterifolius

Potamogeton zosterifolius, commonly known as the ribbonleaf pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in North America.

It has long, narrow, ribbon-like leaves that are typically dark green and arranged in whorls around the stem. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often submerged, followed by elongated, cylindrical fruits. It plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.

Its presence is often an indicator of good water quality in lakes and slow-moving streams.
