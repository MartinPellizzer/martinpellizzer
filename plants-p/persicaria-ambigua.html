Persicaria ambigua

Persicaria ambigua, commonly known as wandering willow or false nettle, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture and produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters. It has a fibrous root system and is often found in wetland habitats.

Persicaria ambigua is known for its ability to spread through rhizomes, making it a common pioneer species in disturbed areas.
