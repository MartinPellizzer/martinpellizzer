Paepalanthus linearis

Paepalanthus linearis, commonly known as the slender puya, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Argentina and Chile.

It belongs to the family Xanthorrhoeaceae and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it requires well-drained soil and full sun.
