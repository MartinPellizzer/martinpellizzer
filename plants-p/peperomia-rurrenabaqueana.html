Peperomia rurrenabaqueana

Peperomia rurrenabaqueana, commonly known as the Rurrenabaque peperomia, is a species native to the Amazon rainforest in Bolivia.

It is a compact, evergreen shrub characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are typically dark green with a slight pubescence. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually greenish-white in color, arranged in spikes along the stems. It thrives in shaded, humid environments and is often found growing in the undergrowth of dense tropical forests.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
