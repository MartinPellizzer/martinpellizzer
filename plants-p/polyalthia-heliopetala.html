Polyalthia heliopetala

Polyalthia heliopetala, commonly known as the sun-loving flame tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, fiery red flowers that bloom in clusters, often appearing during the rainy season. The plant has compound leaves composed of several oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces small, woody fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The botanical features of Polyalthia heliopetala make it a distinctive and visually appealing addition to tropical gardens.
