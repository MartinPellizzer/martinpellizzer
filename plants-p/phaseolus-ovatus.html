Phaseolus ovatus

Phaseolus ovatus, commonly known as the scarlet runner bean, is a leguminous plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its climbing habit and large, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant produces elongated, edible beans that are typically harvested when immature. Its leaves are heart-shaped and compound, with a soft, hairy texture.

Phaseolus ovatus is valued for both its ornamental appeal and its culinary use in various cuisines.
