Prasophyllum frenchii var. tadgellianum

Prasophyllum frenchii var. tadgellianum, commonly known as the Tadgell's leek orchid, is a rare and delicate flowering plant native to the alpine regions of southeastern Australia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and small, white or pale green flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and requires a cool, moist environment to thrive. It is listed as endangered due to habitat loss and limited distribution.
