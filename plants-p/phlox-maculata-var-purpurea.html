Phlox maculata var. purpurea

Phlox maculata var. purpurea, commonly known as the showy phlox, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its vibrant pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its foliage is broad-leafed and often features a mottled or speckled appearance, giving it a distinctive look.

It is a popular choice for gardens due to its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
