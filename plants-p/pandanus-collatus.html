Pandanus collatus

Pandanus collatus, commonly known as the bottlebrush pandanus, is a tropical plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its long, narrow leaves that form a dense rosette around the stem. The plant produces striking inflorescences that resemble bottlebrushes, composed of numerous small, red or yellow flowers. It thrives in coastal and wetland environments, often growing in sandy or saline soils.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has cultural significance in many island communities.
