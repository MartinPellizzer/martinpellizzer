Phlox stansburyi subvar. microcalyx

Phlox stansburyi subvar. microcalyx, commonly known as the Sierra phlox, is a low-growing perennial shrub native to the western United States.

It features dense clusters of showy, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has narrow, linear leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its compact size and ornamental value make it a popular choice for native plant gardens.
