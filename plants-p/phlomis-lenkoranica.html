Phlomis lenkoranica

Phlomis lenkoranica, commonly known as the Armenian mullein, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its tall, upright stems and large, grayish-green leaves. The plant produces showy yellow flowers in clusters, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or dry habitats.

Phlomis lenkoranica is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in herbal medicine.
