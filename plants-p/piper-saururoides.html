Piper saururoides

Piper saururoides, commonly known as the "serpent's tongue" or "sauropod vine," is a climbing shrub native to the rainforests of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many species of pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its long, slender stems and heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence. Its small, greenish-white flowers are clustered in spikes and are typically followed by small, round berries.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
