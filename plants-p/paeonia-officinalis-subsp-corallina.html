Paeonia officinalis subsp. corallina

Paeonia officinalis subsp.

corallina, commonly known as the coral peony, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its striking, coral-pink to red flowers that bloom in early spring.

The plant features large, palmately compound leaves with a velvety texture. It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental beauty and historical use in traditional medicine.
