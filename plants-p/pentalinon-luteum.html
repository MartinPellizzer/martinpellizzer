Pentalinon luteum

Pentalinon luteum, commonly known as the yellow bell.

It is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States, characterized by its striking yellow flowers that resemble small bells. The plant belongs to the Fabaceae family, also known as the legume or pea family, and features compound leaves with five leaflets. Its flowers are typically clustered in racemes and bloom in the spring.

The plant is often found in moist, wooded areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
