Panicum lundellii

Panicum lundellii, commonly known as Lundell's panicgrass, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in wetland habitats, such as marshes and swamps, where it thrives in moist to saturated soils. The plant has slender, erect stems and produces fluffy, seed-bearing spikes that are characteristic of the Panicum genus. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green coloration.

Lundell's panicgrass plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
