Phyllodoce alata

Phyllodoce alata, commonly known as the trailing arnica, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the northern hemisphere.

It features small, needle-like leaves that are typically bright yellow to green in color. The plant produces yellowish or orange-red flowers during the summer months, which are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in shaded areas such as forests or rocky slopes.

Phyllodoce alata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in horticulture for its hardy and resilient nature.
