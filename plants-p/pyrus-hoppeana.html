Pyrus hoppeana

Pyrus hoppeana, commonly known as the Hoppe's pear, is a species of flowering plant in the family Rosaceae.

It is native to the western United States, particularly California, and is characterized by its small, clustered white flowers and edible fruits. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have serrated edges, while the bark is smooth and grayish-brown.

The fruit, which is a small, yellowish pear, ripens in late summer and is often used in local cuisines.
