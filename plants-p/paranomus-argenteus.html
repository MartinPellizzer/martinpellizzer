Paranomus argenteus

Paranomus argenteus, commonly known as the silver leaf plant, is a species native to certain regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive silvery-gray foliage, which gives it its common name. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to several meters. Its leaves are broad and often have a waxy texture, which helps reduce water loss in arid environments.

Paranomus argenteus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
