Phaleria biflora

Phaleria biflora, commonly known as the two-flowered phaleria, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, clustered white flowers. The plant produces dark blue to black berries that contain a single seed. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Phaleria biflora is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appearance and ability to thrive in humid climates.
