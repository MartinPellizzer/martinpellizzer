Philodendron leal-costae

Philodendron leal-costae, commonly known as the "Emerald Queen," is a striking tropical vine native to Central and South America.

It features heart-shaped, dark green leaves with a velvety texture and prominent veins that create a distinctive marbled pattern. The plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to climb, making it a popular choice for indoor and outdoor cultivation. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine, though it contains calcium oxalate crystals that can cause irritation.

It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires regular watering to maintain its lush, vibrant appearance.
