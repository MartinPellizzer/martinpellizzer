Pithecellobium bigeminum

Pithecellobium bigeminum, commonly known as the twin-fruit acacia, is a small evergreen tree native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by woody, curved pods containing edible seeds. Its root system is well-developed, allowing it to thrive in poor soils, and it is often used in reforestation efforts due to its adaptability.

The plant also has historical uses in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
