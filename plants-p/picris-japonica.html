Picris japonica

Picris japonica, commonly known as Japanese goatherb, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and yellow-orange flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant has feathery, aromatic leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and medicinal contexts.
