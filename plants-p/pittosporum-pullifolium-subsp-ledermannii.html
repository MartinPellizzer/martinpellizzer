Pittosporum pullifolium subsp. ledermannii

Pittosporum pullifolium subsp.

ledermannii, commonly known as the Ledermann's pittosporum, is a shrub native to New Zealand. It is characterized by its lanceolate, dark green leaves that are often glossy and have a distinct midrib.

The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by orange or red berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas and forest margins.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
