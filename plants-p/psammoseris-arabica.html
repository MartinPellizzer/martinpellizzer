Psammoseris arabica

Psammoseris arabica, commonly known as the Arabian sandwort, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the Middle East and North Africa.

It is a low-growing perennial that thrives in sandy and gravelly soils, often found in coastal dunes and rocky habitats. The plant produces small, white or pale blue flowers during the spring and summer months. Its leaves are typically succulent, adapted to store water in its arid environment.

Psammoseris arabica is well-suited to harsh conditions and is often used in xerophytic landscaping.
