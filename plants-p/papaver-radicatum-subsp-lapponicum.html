Papaver radicatum subsp. lapponicum

Papaver radicatum subsp.

lapponicum, commonly known as the Lapland poppy, is a herbaceous perennial native to arctic and alpine regions of Europe and Asia. It features striking red to orange flowers with a distinctive black center, typically blooming in late summer.

The plant has a short, stout stem and basal leaves that are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained, rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to cold climates.

This subspecies is often found in tundra and mountain meadows, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
