Poa ianthina

Poa ianthina, commonly known as the silver hair grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern South America.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-gray blades and dense, tufted growth habit. The plant typically grows in moist, temperate environments such as meadows and wetlands. Its leaves are narrow and often have a bluish or silvery hue due to the presence of fine hairs.

Poa ianthina is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping for its aesthetic and ecological benefits.
