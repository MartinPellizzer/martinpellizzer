Puccinellia convoluta var. pseudobulbosa

Puccinellia convoluta var. pseudobulbosa, commonly known as the false bulbous grass, is a perennial herb native to arid and semi-arid regions.

It typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to harsh environmental conditions. The plant features basal rosettes of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. Its flowers are usually pale green to brown, and it is often found in disturbed areas or along roadsides.

This species is valued for its ornamental potential and ecological role in stabilizing soil in dry environments.
