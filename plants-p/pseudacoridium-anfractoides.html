Pseudacoridium anfractoides

Pseudacoridium anfractoides, commonly known as the wrinkled acoridium, is a unique flowering plant native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Acoraceae and is characterized by its distinctive, spirally twisted leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers that are clustered in umbels. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in swampy or marshy areas.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is considered an endangered species in its native range.
