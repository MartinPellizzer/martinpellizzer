Pellacalyx

Pellacalyx, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a tropical plant native to Africa, Asia, and the Americas.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in a dense layer of fine, velvety hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. Pellacalyx thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.
