Pollardia livida

Pollardia livida, commonly known as the silverleaf tree, is a unique plant species native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which helps reflect sunlight and reduce water loss in its harsh environment. The plant has a deep root system that allows it to access underground water sources, making it highly drought-tolerant. Its leaves are narrow and leathery, adapted for minimizing transpiration.

Pollardia livida is also notable for its small, inconspicuous flowers that bloom in clusters, often overlooked despite their ecological significance.
