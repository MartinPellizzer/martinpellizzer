Pandanus latiloculatus

Pandanus latiloculatus, commonly known as the narrow-fruited pandanus, is a tropical plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It is a member of the Pandanaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and pinnate leaves. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences that develop into spiky, fibrous fruits. Its leaves are often used in traditional crafts and for thatching due to their durability.

The species is adapted to coastal and sandy environments, making it a hardy plant in tropical climates.
