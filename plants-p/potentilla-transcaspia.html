Potentilla transcaspia

Potentilla transcaspia, commonly known as the Transcaspian cinquefoil, is a perennial shrub native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a hairy texture.

Potentilla transcaspia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardiness and resilience.
