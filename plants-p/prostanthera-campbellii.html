Prostanthera campbellii

Prostanthera campbellii, commonly known as Campbell's mintbush, is a small shrub native to southern Australia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant features aromatic, glandular leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It produces small, tubular flowers that are typically white or pale blue, arranged in dense spikes.

Prostanthera campbellii is well adapted to dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal and heathland environments.
