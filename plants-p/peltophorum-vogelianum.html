Peltophorum vogelianum

Peltophorum vogelianum, commonly known as the Indian peacock tree, is a deciduous tree native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its striking peacock-like flower heads. The tree has bipinnate leaves and produces large, showy inflorescences that bloom in spring. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The plant also has a symbiotic relationship with nitrogen-fixing bacteria, enhancing soil fertility.
