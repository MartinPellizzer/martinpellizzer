Pterocarpus uliginosus

Pterocarpus uliginosus, commonly known as the swamp ebony, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its dark, dense wood and distinctive, often fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in wetland or swampy areas, thriving in moist, acidic soils. Its leaves are bipinnate, and the fruit is a pod containing several seeds.

The tree is valued for its hard, durable wood, which is used in furniture and musical instruments.
