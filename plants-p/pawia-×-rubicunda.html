Pawia × rubicunda

Pawia × rubicunda, commonly known as the red-flowered pawia, is a hybrid plant species resulting from the cross between Pawia lutea and Pawia sanguinea.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, particularly in countries like Indonesia and Malaysia. The plant is characterized by its striking red or pink flowers, which bloom in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
