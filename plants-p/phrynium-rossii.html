Phrynium rossii

Phrynium rossii, commonly known as the silver shield fern, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It features large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a silvery, waxy coating, giving it a unique appearance. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences with yellowish-green flowers, which are followed by red berries. It thrives in humid, shady environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Its foliage is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
