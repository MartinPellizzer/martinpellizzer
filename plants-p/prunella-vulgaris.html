Prunella vulgaris

Prunella vulgaris, commonly known as self-heal, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It features upright, hairy stems that bear opposite, oval-shaped leaves with toothed margins. The plant produces spikes of blue to purple flowers from late spring to early autumn. It thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and along stream banks.

Prunella vulgaris is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally for its healing qualities.
