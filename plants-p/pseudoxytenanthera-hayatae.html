Pseudoxytenanthera hayatae

Pseudoxytenanthera hayatae, commonly known as the Japanese flame tree, is a member of the Fabaceae family and is native to southern Japan.

It is a deciduous tree characterized by its striking red or orange flowers that appear in spring. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces flat, pod-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Due to its limited natural range, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
