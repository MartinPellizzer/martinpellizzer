Pinus sylvestris f. argentea

Pinus sylvestris f. argentea, commonly known as the silver fir or silver pine, is a naturally occurring variant of the Scots pine.

It is characterized by its silvery-white bark and needle-like leaves that are covered in a fine, powdery coating. This form thrives in temperate climates and is often found in mountainous regions across Europe. The plant is a coniferous evergreen, producing cones and contributing to forest ecosystems.

Its unique appearance makes it a popular ornamental plant in horticulture.
