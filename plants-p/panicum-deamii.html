Panicum deamii

Panicum deamii, commonly known as the Deam's grass, is a species of flowering plant in the grass family Poaceae.

It is native to the southeastern United States, particularly Florida, and thrives in wetland environments. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bloom in late summer. Its growth habit is typically clump-forming, and it is often found in marshy areas or along the edges of ponds.

Panicum deamii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
