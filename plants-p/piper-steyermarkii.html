Piper steyermarkii

Piper steyermarkii, commonly known as the Steyermark's pepper vine, is a tropical climbing plant belonging to the Piperaceae family.

It is native to the rainforests of Central and South America, where it thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant is characterized by its elongated, trailing stems and opposite, ovate leaves with a glossy surface. Its small, greenish-white flowers are typically clustered in axillary inflorescences, and it produces small, round berries that turn red when mature.

While not widely cultivated for its fruit, it is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and ecological adaptations.
