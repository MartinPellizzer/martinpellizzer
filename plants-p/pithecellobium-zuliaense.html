Pithecellobium zuliaense

Pithecellobium zuliaense, commonly known as the Zulia legume, is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are bipinnate, and the flowers are usually yellow or white, arranged in racemes.

The fruit is a legume pod that contains several seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine.
