Ptychosperma wendlandianum

Ptychosperma wendlandianum, commonly known as the Wendland's cycad, is a rare and ancient plant belonging to the Cycadaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant features a stout, unbranched stem and a crown of feathery, pinnately compound leaves. Its reproductive structures include a cone-like structure that produces both male and female spores.

Due to habitat loss and over-collection, it is considered endangered and requires conservation efforts to ensure its survival.
