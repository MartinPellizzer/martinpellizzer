Philadelphus argyrocalyx

Philadelphus argyrocalyx, commonly known as the silver-leaf mock orange, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, fragrant white flowers that bloom in early summer, often in clusters of four to six. The leaves are ovate and covered in a fine, silvery-gray pubescence, giving the plant its common name. It prefers well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for garden cultivation.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping for its pleasant scent and attractive foliage.
