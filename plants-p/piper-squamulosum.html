Piper squamulosum

Piper squamulosum, commonly known as the squamulous pepper vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many species of pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are covered in small, scale-like structures, giving it its common name. It produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by berries that turn red when ripe.

The plant is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
