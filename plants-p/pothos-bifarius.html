Pothos bifarius

Pothos bifarius, commonly known as the devil's ivy, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped, waxy leaves that are typically variegated with shades of green, yellow, and white. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers that are followed by dark blue berries. It is a trailing vine that thrives in low-light conditions and is popular as a houseplant due to its hardiness and aesthetic appeal.

Pothos bifarius is also known for its ability to purify indoor air, making it a favored choice for interior landscaping.
