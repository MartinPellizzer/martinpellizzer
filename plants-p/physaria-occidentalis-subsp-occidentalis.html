Physaria occidentalis subsp. occidentalis

Physaria occidentalis subsp.

occidentalis, commonly known as the western bladderpod, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cress plants.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers and has distinctive inflated, bladder-like pods that contain seeds. It typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is often found in disturbed areas.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for study in ecological and evolutionary botany.
