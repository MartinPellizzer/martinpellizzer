Pilosella flagellariformis

Pilosella flagellariformis, commonly known as the slender haircup, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant features a basal rosette of narrow, hairy leaves that are often covered in fine, white pubescence. Its inflorescence consists of yellow disc flowers surrounded by white to pale blue ray florets, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Pilosella flagellariformis thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.
