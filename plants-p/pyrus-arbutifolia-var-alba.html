Pyrus arbutifolia var. alba

Pyrus arbutifolia var. alba, commonly known as the white-flowered cotoneaster, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of southwestern China.

It is characterized by its small, white flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring, followed by red or orange berries in the autumn. The plant has elliptical, dark green leaves that turn reddish in the fall, adding to its ornamental value. It is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant seasonal display and hardy nature.

The species is also valued for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its tolerance to urban conditions.
