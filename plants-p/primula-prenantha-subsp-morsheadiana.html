Primula prenantha subsp. morsheadiana

Primula prenantha subsp.

morsheadiana, commonly known as the Morshead's primrose, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions.

The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped, pinkish-white flowers with five petals, which bloom in early spring. Its leaves are lanceolate, hairy, and often have a bluish-green hue.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
