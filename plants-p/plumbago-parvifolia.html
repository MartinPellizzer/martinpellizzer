Plumbago parvifolia

Plumbago parvifolia, commonly known as blue plumbago, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring woody stems and opposite, oval-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, blue or violet flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long blooming period and drought tolerance.
