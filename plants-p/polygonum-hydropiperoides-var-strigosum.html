Polygonum hydropiperoides var. strigosum

Polygonum hydropiperoides var. strigosum, commonly known as hairy knotweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit.

It features alternate, lanceolate leaves that are often covered in fine, hairy trichomes, giving it a distinctive texture. The plant produces small, clustered flowers with five petals, typically in shades of pink to red, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as stream banks and wetlands.

Its botanical features make it a common weed in disturbed soils and a subject of ecological study due to its adaptability.
