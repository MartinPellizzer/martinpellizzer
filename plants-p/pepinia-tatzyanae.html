Pepinia tatzyanae

Pepinia tatzyanae, commonly known as the Tatzuya's peperomia, is a rare and unique species native to specific regions in Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its compact, rosette-like growth habit. The plant features dark green, glossy leaves with a slightly textured surface, and small, inconspicuous flowers that appear in clusters. It thrives in shaded, humid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, Pepinia tatzyanae is considered a valuable subject for botanical research and conservation efforts.
