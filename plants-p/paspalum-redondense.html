Paspalum redondense

Paspalum redondense, commonly known as redondense grass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny florets. It thrives in wetland and marshy environments, often forming dense mats.

Its ecological importance includes stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
