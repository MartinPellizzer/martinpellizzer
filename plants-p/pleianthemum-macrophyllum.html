Pleianthemum macrophyllum

Pleianthemum macrophyllum, commonly known as the broadleaf plectranthus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, tubular flowers that bloom in shades of purple and blue. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous perennial, often reaching heights of up to 1 meter. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a versatile addition to gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its fragrant foliage and long blooming period.
