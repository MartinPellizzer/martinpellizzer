Penstemon rothrockii subsp. jacintensis

Penstemon rothrockii subsp.

jacintensis, commonly known as the Jacintensis Beardtongue, is a member of the Plantaginaceae family. It is a perennial herb native to the western United States, particularly found in rocky and arid environments.

The plant features tubular flowers that are typically pink to purple in color, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its foliage consists of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, fuzzy texture.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to dry, well-drained soils.
