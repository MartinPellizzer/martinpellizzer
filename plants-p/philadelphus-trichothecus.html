Philadelphus trichothecus

Philadelphus trichothecus, commonly known as the white phlomis, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive textured appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long-lasting blooms and attractive, rounded form.
