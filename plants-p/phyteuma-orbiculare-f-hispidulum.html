Phyteuma orbiculare f. hispidulum

Phyteuma orbiculare f. hispidulum, commonly known as the hairy globe flower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Asia.

It features showy, globular inflorescences composed of numerous small, tubular flowers that are typically pale yellow to orange in color. The leaves are basal, lanceolate, and often covered with fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine settings.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental value and adaptability to various garden conditions.
