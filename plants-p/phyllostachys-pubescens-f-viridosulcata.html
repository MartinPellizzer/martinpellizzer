Phyllostachys pubescens f. viridosulcata

Phyllostachys pubescens f. viridosulcata, commonly known as the green-striped bamboo, is a variegated form of the common bamboo species.

It is characterized by its distinctive green stripes on the culms, which contrast with the lighter-colored base. This plant is a clumping bamboo that grows in dense, upright clusters, forming a compact and ornamental shape. It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for garden landscapes.

The variegation adds visual interest, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and bamboo collections.
