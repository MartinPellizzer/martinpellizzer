Plantago lanceolata f. bifurca

Plantago lanceolata f. bifurca, commonly known as the bifurcate plantain, is a variant of the common plantain, characterized by its distinctive bifurcated (forked) leaves.

This plant is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a rough, hairy texture, while the flowers are arranged in a dense spike. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that develop into capsule fruits containing numerous seeds.

It is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional herbal remedies.
