Pyrus fuscata

Pyrus fuscata, commonly known as the Japanese whitebeam, is a deciduous tree native to Japan.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its small, dark green leaves and white to pale yellow flowers. The tree produces small, edible fruits that are typically consumed by wildlife. It is often found in mountainous regions and thrives in well-drained soils.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for cultivation in gardens and landscapes.
