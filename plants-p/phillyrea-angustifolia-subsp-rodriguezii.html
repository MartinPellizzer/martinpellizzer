Phillyrea angustifolia subsp. rodriguezii

Phillyrea angustifolia subsp.

rodriguezii, commonly known as Rodrigues phillyrea, is a shrub native to the Mascarene Islands. It features small, elliptical leaves that are typically glaucous in appearance.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, followed by woody, spherical berries. It thrives in coastal and limestone soils, often found in dry, sunny environments.

Due to its restricted habitat, it is considered a rare and endemic species.
