Polygala gundelsheimeri

Polygala gundelsheimeri, commonly known as the Gundelsheimer's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that typically range in color from white to pink. The plant has slender, branched stems and small, opposite leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in dry, sunny habitats and is often found in rocky or gravelly soils.

Due to its limited distribution and specific ecological requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in some areas.
