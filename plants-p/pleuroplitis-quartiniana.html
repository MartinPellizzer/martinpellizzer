Pleuroplitis quartiniana

Pleuroplitis quartiniana, commonly known as the Quartin's pleurothallis, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a small, epiphytic plant that typically grows on trees in humid, shaded environments. The plant has delicate, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink. Its roots are adapted for absorbing moisture and nutrients from the air and rain.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
