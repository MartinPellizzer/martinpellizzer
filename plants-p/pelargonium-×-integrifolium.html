Pelargonium × integrifolium

Pelargonium × integrifolium, commonly known as the African marigold, is a hybrid species of geranium native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from yellow to orange and red, often with a distinctive dark center. The plant has a trailing growth habit and is often cultivated as a bedding plant due to its vibrant blooms and ability to thrive in various conditions. Its leaves are typically broad and lobed, with a slightly hairy texture.

Pelargonium × integrifolium is also valued for its use in traditional medicine and as an ornamental plant in gardens.
