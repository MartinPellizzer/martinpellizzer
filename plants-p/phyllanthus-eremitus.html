Phyllanthus eremitus

Phyllanthus eremitus, commonly known as the desert date or sacred fig, is a small evergreen tree native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa, the Middle East, and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that help reduce water loss in hot, dry environments. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers followed by edible, fleshy fruits that contain a single seed. It is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Its ability to thrive in harsh conditions makes it an important species in ecological restoration efforts.
