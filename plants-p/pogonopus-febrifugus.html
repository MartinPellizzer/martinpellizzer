Pogonopus febrifugus

Pogonopus febrifugus, commonly known as the feverfew plant, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, fern-like leaves and small yellow flowers. The plant is known for its medicinal properties, particularly its use in traditional medicine for treating fevers and headaches. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and pharmacological contexts.
