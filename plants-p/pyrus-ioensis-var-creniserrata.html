Pyrus ioensis var. creniserrata

Pyrus ioensis var. creniserrata, commonly known as the Crenate Pear, is a variant of the Io pear native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive crenate-serrate leaf margins, which give it a unique appearance. The plant typically grows as a small to medium-sized tree, reaching heights of up to 10 meters. Its flowers are white and clustered in inflorescences, followed by small, edible fruits that are yellowish-green in color.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical significance in horticulture.
