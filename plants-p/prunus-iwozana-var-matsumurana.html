Prunus iwozana var. matsumurana

Prunus iwozana var. matsumurana, commonly known as the Japanese cherry plum, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its small, oval-shaped leaves that turn yellow in the fall. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pink flowers in early spring, followed by edible, yellow or orange fruits. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This variety is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive foliage and flowers.
