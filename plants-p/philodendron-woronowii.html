Philodendron woronowii

Philodendron woronowii, commonly known as the silver vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It features heart-shaped, glossy dark green leaves with a silvery underside, giving it its distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers that are typically not showy. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.

Philodendron woronowii is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and ease of care.
