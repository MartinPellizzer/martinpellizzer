Ptychosperma mooreanum

Ptychosperma mooreanum, commonly known as the Moorean palm, is a species of flowering plant in the family Arecaceae.

It is native to the island of Moorea in French Polynesia and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and pinnate leaves. The plant produces small, clustered yellowish-white flowers followed by dark blue, fleshy berries. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in coastal areas or near freshwater sources.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
