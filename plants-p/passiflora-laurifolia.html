Passiflora laurifolia

Passiflora laurifolia, commonly known as the evergreen passionflower, is a climbing shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, intricate flowers that display a mix of blue, purple, and white petals. The plant produces edible berries that are often used in culinary applications. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is valued for its ornamental beauty.

Passiflora laurifolia is also known for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal remedies.
