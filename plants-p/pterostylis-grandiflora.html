Pterostylis grandiflora

Pterostylis grandiflora, commonly known as the large-flowered greenhood, is a terrestrial orchid native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its unique hood-like labellum and a single, erect stem that bears a single spike of flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as rainforests and understoreys. Its flowers are usually greenish-yellow with a distinctive hood shape, which is a defining feature of the Pterostylis genus.

The species is noted for its specialized pollination mechanism, attracting specific insect pollinators.
