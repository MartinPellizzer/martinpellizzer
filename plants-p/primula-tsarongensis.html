Primula tsarongensis

Primula tsarongensis, commonly known as the Tsarong primrose, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayan regions of Tibet and Nepal.

It is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving at high altitudes. Its leaves are basal, broadly ovate, and covered with a dense layer of fine hairs.

Primula tsarongensis is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens that mimic its natural habitat.
