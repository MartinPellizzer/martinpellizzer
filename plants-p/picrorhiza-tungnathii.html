Picrorhiza tungnathii

Picrorhiza tungnathii, commonly known as Himalayan picrorhiza, is a perennial herb native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the family Labiatae and is characterized by its aromatic, hairy stems and small, opposite leaves. The plant produces violet-blue flowers and is known for its medicinal properties, particularly its high content of iridoids. It thrives in cold, moist environments and is often found at elevations above 3,000 meters.

Due to its rarity and ecological significance, it is considered a valuable and endangered species in its natural habitat.
