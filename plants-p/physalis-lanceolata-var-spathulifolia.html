Physalis lanceolata var. spathulifolia

Physalis lanceolata var. spathulifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf groundcherry, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to South America.

It features distinctive lantern-shaped fruits that enclose bright orange to red berries, typical of the Physalis genus. The leaves are lanceolate, though the variety spathulifolia has broader, spatulate-shaped foliage. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often found in moist, temperate climates.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its edible fruits.
