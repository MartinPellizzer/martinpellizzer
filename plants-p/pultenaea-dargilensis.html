Pultenaea dargilensis

Pultenaea dargilensis, commonly known as the Dargile wattle, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its yellow floral spikes that bloom in spring, adding vibrant color to its native habitat. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces small, pod-like fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in sandy soils and is often found in dry, open woodlands.

Pultenaea dargilensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
