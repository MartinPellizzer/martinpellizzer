Pleurothallis sororia

Pleurothallis sororia, commonly known as the silver-veined orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to the cloud forests of Central and South America.

This epiphytic plant typically grows on trees and thrives in humid, shaded environments. It has small, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense, silvery pubescence, giving it its common name. The flowers are usually pale green or white with intricate, spurred petals.

It is appreciated for its delicate appearance and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts for its ornamental value.
