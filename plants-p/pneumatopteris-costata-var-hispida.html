Pneumatopteris costata var. hispida

Pneumatopteris costata var. hispida, commonly known as the hispid cliff fern, is a variant of the costate cliff fern.

It is a fern belonging to the family Pteridaceae, characterized by its fronds that are typically divided into lanceolate segments. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found on rocky outcrops or in woodland areas. Its fronds are covered with fine, hairy structures, giving it a distinctive appearance.

This variety is notable for its adaptability to calcareous soils and its ability to form dense colonies in suitable habitats.
