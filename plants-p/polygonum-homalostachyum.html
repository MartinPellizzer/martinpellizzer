Polygonum homalostachyum

Polygonum homalostachyum, commonly known as the redstem filaree, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Mexico.

It is a member of the Polygonaceae family, characterized by its distinctive, arrow-shaped leaves and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, disturbed soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and along roadsides. It produces small, bead-like seeds that are dispersed by wind, contributing to its ability to thrive in various environments.

Polygonum homalostachyum is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
