Piper conquistanum

Piper conquistanum, commonly known as the Peruvian pepper tree, is a tropical shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its dark green, glossy leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces small, red berries that are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in the understory of tropical rainforests.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and medicinal contexts.
