Portulaca almeviae

Portulaca almeviae, commonly known as the Almevia portulaca, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It belongs to the family Portulacaceae and is characterized by its fleshy, succulent leaves and vibrant, showy flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to hot, dry climates. Its flowers are usually red or yellow, and they bloom in the morning, closing during the heat of the day.

Portulaca almeviae is a hardy species that thrives in full sun and requires minimal water, making it suitable for xeriscaping.
