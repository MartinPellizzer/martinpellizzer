Phalaris communis var. rubriculmis

Phalaris communis var. rubriculmis, commonly known as red-stalked beakgrass, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, reddish stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing yellowish to reddish flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wet meadows and along riverbanks.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and its role in supporting local pollinator populations.
