Phyllanthus filicaulis

Phyllanthus filicaulis, commonly known as the broadleaf phyllanthus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Euphorbiaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific Islands. The plant features simple, opposite, and often elliptical leaves that are typically glossy and dark green. It produces small, clustered yellow flowers, followed by capsule fruits containing tiny seeds.

Phyllanthus filicaulis is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks.
