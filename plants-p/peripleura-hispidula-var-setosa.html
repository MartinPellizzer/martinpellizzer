Peripleura hispidula var. setosa

Peripleura hispidula var. setosa, commonly known as the hairy peripleura, is a species of flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small shrub characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the warmer months. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its unique morphology and limited distribution, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
