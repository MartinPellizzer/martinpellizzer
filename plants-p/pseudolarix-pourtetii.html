Pseudolarix pourtetii

Pseudolarix pourtetii, commonly known as the Chinese golden larch, is a deciduous conifer native to China.

It is characterized by its unique, feather-like leaves that turn a striking golden yellow in autumn. The plant produces small, cone-like structures that contain seeds surrounded by a fleshy, red aril. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in mountainous regions with well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and parks around the world.
