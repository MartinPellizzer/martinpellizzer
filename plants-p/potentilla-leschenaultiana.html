Potentilla leschenaultiana

Potentilla leschenaultiana, commonly known as the Leschenault's cinquefoil, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of small, bright yellow flowers in the summer. The plant has finely divided, silvery-gray leaves that are covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Due to its ornamental value and hardiness, it is a popular choice for gardeners interested in native and drought-tolerant plants.
