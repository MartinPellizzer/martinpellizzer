Parkia graveolens

Parkia graveolens, commonly known as the stinkbean or African locust bean, is a leguminous tree native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It produces distinctive, fragrant, yellow pods that contain edible seeds used in traditional cuisines. The plant has compound leaves with trifoliate or pinnate structures, and its flowers are typically pale yellow to white. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its seeds and ornamental value.

The tree also has nitrogen-fixing abilities, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
