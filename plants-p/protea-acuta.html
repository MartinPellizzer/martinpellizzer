Protea acuta

Protea acuta, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sugarbush, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Proteaceae family, which is characterized by its diverse and often striking floral structures. The plant typically grows in shrub form, with dense, evergreen foliage that provides habitat for various insects and small animals. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, with a distinctive, cone-shaped structure that attracts pollinators like birds and insects.

Protea acuta is well adapted to nutrient-poor soils and can thrive in a range of environmental conditions.
