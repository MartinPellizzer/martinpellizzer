Piptadenia contorta

Piptadenia contorta, commonly known as yellow fever tree, is a deciduous tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant produces long, slender pods that contain flat, winged seeds. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to tropical and subtropical climates.

Piptadenia contorta is also known for its potential medicinal properties in traditional medicine.
