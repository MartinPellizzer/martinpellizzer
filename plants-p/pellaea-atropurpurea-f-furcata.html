Pellaea atropurpurea f. furcata

Pellaea atropurpurea f. furcata, commonly known as the purple fern, is a fern species characterized by its striking violet to purple fronds.

It belongs to the family Pellaeaceae and is native to regions of South America, particularly in Argentina and Chile. The plant features bipinnate leaves with a distinctive, almost feathery appearance, and its rhizomes are often covered in a dense, dark coloration. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in rocky or forested areas.

This fern is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
