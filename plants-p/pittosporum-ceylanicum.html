Pittosporum ceylanicum

Pittosporum ceylanicum, commonly known as the Ceylon pittosporum, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Sri Lanka.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that turn reddish-brown in autumn. The plant produces fragrant, white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by orange-red berries. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun.

Pittosporum ceylanicum is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its attractive foliage and seasonal blooms.
