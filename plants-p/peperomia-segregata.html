Peperomia segregata

Peperomia segregata, commonly known as the silver peperomia, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It features thick, waxy leaves that are typically silver or gray-green in color, which help reduce water loss in its native habitat. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually green or reddish, and it is often grown as a houseplant due to its low maintenance requirements. Peperomia segregata thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it suitable for indoor cultivation.

Its unique foliage makes it a popular choice for decorative purposes in both homes and offices.
