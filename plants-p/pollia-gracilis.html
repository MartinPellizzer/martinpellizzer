Pollia gracilis

Pollia gracilis, commonly known as the slender orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its delicate, elongated flower spikes that bear small, white to pale blue blossoms. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees and other vegetation in its natural habitat. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often clustering at the base of the plant.

Pollia gracilis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
