Potamogeton spirilliformis

Potamogeton spirilliformis, commonly known as the spiral pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its spiral arrangement of leaves, which gives it its scientific name. The plant has long, ribbon-like leaves that are often spirally twisted around the stem. It typically grows in shallow, slow-moving waters and plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for small organisms.

Potamogeton spirilliformis is often found in lakes, ponds, and wetlands where it contributes to water clarity and oxygenation.
