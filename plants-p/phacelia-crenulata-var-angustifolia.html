Phacelia crenulata var. angustifolia

Phacelia crenulata var. angustifolia, commonly known as narrow-leaf bluecurls, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, angular leaves and clusters of sky-blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands. It blooms in the spring and early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in supporting native wildlife.
