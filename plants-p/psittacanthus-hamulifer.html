Psittacanthus hamulifer

Psittacanthus hamulifer, commonly known as the hook-leaf fern, is a tropical plant belonging to the family Polypodiaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, feathery fronds that are often used in ornamental horticulture. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread slowly and form dense mats.

The spores are produced on the underside of the fronds, contributing to its reproductive strategy in humid climates.
