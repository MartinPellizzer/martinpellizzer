Poa pratensis var. convolutifolia

Poa pratensis var. convolutifolia, commonly known as the curly vernal grass, is a variant of the common meadow grass.

It is characterized by its distinctive curling leaves that form dense, tufted clumps. This plant typically grows in moist, temperate regions and is often found in meadows and along stream banks. Its foliage is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its attractive appearance.

The species is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types, making it a versatile choice for ecological and horticultural applications.
