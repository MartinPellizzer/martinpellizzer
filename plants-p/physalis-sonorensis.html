Physalis sonorensis

Physalis sonorensis, commonly known as the desert tomatillo, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its hairy, yellowish-green calyx that encases the small, edible fruit. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, thriving in well-drained soils. Its fruit, which resembles a small tomato, is often used in traditional cuisines.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and culinary uses in native plant gardens.
