Papaver atropurpureum

Papaver atropurpureum, commonly known as the purple poppy, is a species of flowering plant in the family Papaveraceae.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia and is characterized by its striking deep purple to crimson flowers. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Its leaves are typically palmately lobed and have a slightly hairy texture.

Papaver atropurpureum is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant blooms and unique appearance.
