Phaius roeblingii

Phaius roeblingii, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a terrestrial orchid species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its robust, rhizomatous growth habit and produces large, showy yellow flowers with a distinctive green throat. The plant typically blooms in the summer months, and its inflorescence consists of several clustered flowers on a single stem. The leaves are broad and grass-like, often forming a dense rosette at the base.

Phaius roeblingii is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is relatively easy to cultivate under suitable conditions.
