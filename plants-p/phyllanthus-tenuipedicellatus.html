Phyllanthus tenuipedicellatus

Phyllanthus tenuipedicellatus, commonly known as the narrow-fruited phyllanthus, is a small evergreen shrub native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves that are often pubescent. The plant produces small, clustered yellow flowers followed by capsule fruits containing minute seeds. It is often found in moist, shaded habitats such as forest understories and rocky areas.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in horticultural settings.
