Primula nivea

Primula nivea, commonly known as the snowy primrose, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers. The plant typically blooms in early spring, often before the leaves fully develop. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a fine, silvery down, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Primula nivea is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in alpine gardens.
