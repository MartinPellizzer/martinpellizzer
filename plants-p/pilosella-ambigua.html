Pilosella ambigua

Pilosella ambigua, commonly known as the common hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant features hairy stems and leaves, which give it its scientific name "pilosella," meaning "hairy.". It produces yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months, and its growth habit is typically low and spreading.

Pilosella ambigua is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, where it thrives in sunny conditions.
