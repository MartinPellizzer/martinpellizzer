Piper rotundatum

Piper rotundatum, commonly known as roundleaf pepper or Malabar pepper, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species. The plant is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and small, clustered green berries that turn red when mature. It thrives in humid, tropical environments and is often found in forest understories.

The berries are used in traditional medicine and as a spice in some cuisines.
