Pleurothallis ancora

Pleurothallis ancora, commonly known as the silver-stem pleurothallis, is a species of orchid native to the cloud forests of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery pubescence. The plant produces delicate, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pale yellow, typically arranged in clusters along the stem. It thrives in high humidity and partial shade, making it a popular choice for greenhouse cultivation.

Due to its unique appearance and relatively easy care requirements, it is appreciated by orchid enthusiasts worldwide.
