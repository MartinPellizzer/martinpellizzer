Polyosma elongata

Polyosma elongata, commonly known as the narrow-leafed snowbell, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, elongated leaves and clusters of small, bell-shaped white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its blooming period occurs in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native plant gardens.
