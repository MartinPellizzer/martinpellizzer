Pholidia caerulea

Pholidia caerulea, commonly known as the blueberry hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa, the Americas, and the Pacific.

It is characterized by its woody stems and large, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant produces small, blue or black berries that are edible and have a tart flavor. It thrives in humid environments and is often found in coastal areas or near water sources.
