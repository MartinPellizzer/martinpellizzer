Polyalthia littoralis subsp. daclacensis

Polyalthia littoralis subsp.

daclacensis, commonly known as the coastal polyalthia, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its slender, often branched stems and compound leaves with elongated, glossy leaflets.

The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, arranged in clusters. It thrives in coastal areas, tolerating saline conditions and strong winds.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for coastal landscaping and ecological restoration projects.
