Pouteria simulans

Pouteria simulans, commonly known as the Brazilian nut tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is characterized by its large, glossy leaves and produces clusters of yellowish-green flowers that develop into woody capsules containing edible seeds. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil to flourish. Its fruit, known as a "brazil nut," is highly valued for its nutritional content and economic importance.

The tree plays a significant role in the biodiversity and ecological balance of its native habitat.
