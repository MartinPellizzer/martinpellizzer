Piper arboreum f. nitidum

Piper arboreum f. nitidum, commonly known as the glossy pepper plant, is a variant of the common pepper plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into elongated, slender fruits resembling those of black pepper. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often grown as a houseplant or in shaded garden settings.
