Paspalum campylostachyum

Paspalum campylostachyum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf paspalum, is a perennial grass native to South America.

It features slender, grass-like leaves with a bluish-green hue and produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikes. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks. It has a fibrous root system that helps stabilize soil and prevent erosion.

Paspalum campylostachyum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in wetland restoration projects.
