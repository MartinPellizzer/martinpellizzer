Psidium paraibicum

Psidium paraibicum, commonly known as the Paraiba guava, is a small evergreen tree native to the northeastern region of Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and elliptical leaves with a glossy upper surface. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible berries that are typically yellow or orange when ripe.

Its fruit is valued for its sweet, aromatic flavor and is often used in local cuisines and traditional medicine.
