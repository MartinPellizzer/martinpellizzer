Polygonum sanguinaria

Polygonum sanguinaria, commonly known as bloodroot, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern North America.

It features a distinctive red-orange sap that exudes from its rhizome, which is responsible for its common name. The plant has a single, basal leaf that is palmately divided, and it produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring. Its roots contain various chemical compounds, including alkaloids and saponins, which have been studied for potential medicinal properties.

Bloodroot thrives in moist, wooded areas and is often found in rich, well-drained soils.
