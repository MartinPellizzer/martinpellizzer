Piper densiciliatum

Piper densiciliatum, commonly known as the dense-haired pepper or velvet pepper, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many species of pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its dense, velvety pubescence on its stems and leaves, giving it a distinctive appearance. It produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in spikes, and its fruits are three-lobed berries that turn red when mature.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
