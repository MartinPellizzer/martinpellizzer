Pelargonium citriodorum

Pelargonium citriodorum, commonly known as the lemon-scented geranium, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its fragrant, lemon-scented leaves and showy, five-petaled flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant is often grown as a decorative and aromatic herb, thriving in well-drained soil and full sun. It is also valued for its ability to repel certain insects due to its strong fragrance.

Pelargonium citriodorum is a popular choice for gardens and indoor cultivation due to its ease of care and aesthetic appeal.
