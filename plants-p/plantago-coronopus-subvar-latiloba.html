Plantago coronopus subvar. latiloba

Plantago coronopus subvar. latiloba, commonly known as the broadleaf plantain, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, disturbed soils such as roadsides, meadows, and wetlands. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are slightly broader than those of the typical variety, with a hairy surface. Its flowering stems bear small, greenish-white flowers arranged in dense spikes.

This subspecies is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional herbal remedies.
