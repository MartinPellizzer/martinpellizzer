Paraphlomis javanica var. coronata

Paraphlomis javanica var. coronata, commonly known as the crowned paralytic grass, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Lamiaceae and is characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically arranged in dense spikes, giving it a distinctive appearance. It is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
