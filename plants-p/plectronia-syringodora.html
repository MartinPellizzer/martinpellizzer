Plectronia syringodora

Plectronia syringodora, commonly known as the orchid tree, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its showy, clustered inflorescences that resemble a cluster of orchids, hence its common name. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters along the branches, often in shades of white, pink, and purple. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and ovate, with a smooth texture.

The orchid tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
