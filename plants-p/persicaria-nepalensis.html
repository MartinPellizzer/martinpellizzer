Persicaria nepalensis

Persicaria nepalensis, commonly known as Himalayan knotweed, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and stream banks. The plant features upright stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its small, clustered flowers are usually white or pale pink, appearing in late summer.

Persicaria nepalensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its foliage and floral display.
