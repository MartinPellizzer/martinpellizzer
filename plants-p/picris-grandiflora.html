Picris grandiflora

Picris grandiflora, commonly known as the largeflower goatweed, is an annual herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and parts of Asia.

It features slender, branching stems and produces yellow flowers with prominent, elongated ray florets. The plant has finely divided, serrated leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and waste areas.

Its botanical characteristics make it a common weed in agricultural settings.
