Phragmites vulgaris subsp. maximus

Phragmites vulgaris subsp.

maximus, commonly known as giant reed, is a tall, perennial grass that thrives in wetland environments. It is characterized by its dense growth and hollow stems, which can reach heights of up to 5 meters.

This subspecies is highly adaptable, often colonizing marshes, shores, and disturbed soils. Its rhizomes form extensive underground networks, enabling rapid spread and dominance in wetland ecosystems.

The plant plays a vital role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
