Prunus spinosa var. variegata

Prunus spinosa var. variegata, commonly known as the variegated blackthorn, is a deciduous shrub native to Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and small, dark purple berries that ripen in late summer. The leaves are oval-shaped with a distinctive white or yellow variegation pattern, adding ornamental value to the plant. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This plant is often used in hedging and as a landscape plant due to its hardiness and unique appearance.
