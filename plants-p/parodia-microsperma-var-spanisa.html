Parodia microsperma var. spanisa

Parodia microsperma var. spanisa, commonly known as the Spanish moon cactus, is a succulent native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its compact, globular shape and distinctive rib structure. The plant features a green body with a red or purple hue, and it produces yellow flowers during the warmer months. It is well adapted to arid environments, storing water in its thick, fleshy stems.

Due to its unique appearance and ease of care, it is a popular choice for indoor and outdoor gardening.
