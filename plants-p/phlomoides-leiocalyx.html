Phlomoides leiocalyx

Phlomoides leiocalyx, commonly known as the narrow-leaved phlomoides, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Lamiaceae, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters, featuring opposite, narrow, and lance-shaped leaves. Its small, tubular flowers are usually pale purple to white, arranged in whorled clusters at the ends of the stems.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands and rocky areas.
