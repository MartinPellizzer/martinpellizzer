Pilosocereus piauhyensis subsp. gaturianensis

Pilosocereus piauhyensis subsp.

gaturianensis, commonly known as the Piauhy Hedgehog Cactus, is a species native to Brazil. It is a columnar cactus characterized by its spiny, ribbed stems and is typically found in dry, rocky habitats.

The plant produces yellow flowers during the summer months, which are followed by small, red fruits. It is adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water once established.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological importance, it is often cultivated in xerophytic gardens.
