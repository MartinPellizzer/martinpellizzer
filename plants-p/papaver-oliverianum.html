Papaver oliverianum

Papaver oliverianum, commonly known as Oliver's poppy, is a species of flowering plant in the family Papaveraceae.

It is native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe and western Asia. The plant produces striking red or orange flowers with a prominent central stigma, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are typically alternate, ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture.

The plant is often found in rocky or limestone soils and is adapted to thrive in temperate climates.
