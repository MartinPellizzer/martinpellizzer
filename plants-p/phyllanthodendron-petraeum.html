Phyllanthodendron petraeum

Phyllanthodendron petraeum, commonly known as the rock phyllanthus, is a shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its small, clustered leaves and yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils, adapting to arid and semi-arid environments. Its bark and leaves contain various bioactive compounds, making it of interest in traditional medicine.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered threatened in some areas.
