Polygala thymifolia

Polygala thymifolia, commonly known as the thyme-leaved milkwort, is a herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It belongs to the Polygalaceae family and is characterized by its small, delicate flowers and aromatic foliage. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in open woodlands or grasslands. Its leaves are covered in fine hairs, giving them a slightly fuzzy texture, and the flowers are usually pale pink or white.

It is a perennial species that thrives in well-drained soils and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
