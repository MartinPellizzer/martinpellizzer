Picea nordmanniana pendula

Picea nordmanniana pendula, commonly known as the weeping fir, is a unique variant of the Nordmann fir.

It is characterized by its pendulous branches that droop gracefully, creating a distinctive cascading appearance. Native to the eastern Caucasus region, this plant thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil. Its foliage consists of scale-like needles that are dark green and densely packed.

The tree produces cones that are typically blue to purple in color, adding to its ornamental value.
