Pleurothallis atwoodii

Pleurothallis atwoodii, commonly known as the Atwood's pleurothallis, is a species of orchid native to the cloud forests of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its small, intricate flowers that often display a range of colors including white, yellow, and green. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees and deriving moisture and nutrients from the air and rain.

Its botanical features include linear leaves and a compact growth habit, making it a popular choice among orchid enthusiasts for its beauty and ease of cultivation.
