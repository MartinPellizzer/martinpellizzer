Piper crassipedunculum

Piper crassipedunculum, commonly known as the broad-footed pepper vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many species of pepper plants. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a rough texture and produces small, clustered white flowers followed by elongated, cylindrical fruits. Its fruits are typically green when unripe and turn red when mature, containing numerous tiny seeds.

The plant is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive foliage and unique growth habit.
