Phymatopsis dactylina

Phymatopsis dactylina, commonly known as the desert lily, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves and showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, storing water in its thick, fleshy stems and leaves. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to survive in poor, well-drained soils.

Phymatopsis dactylina plays an important role in stabilizing desert soils and providing nectar for pollinators.
