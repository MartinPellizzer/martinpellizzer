Plantago parviflora

Plantago parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered plantain, is a perennial herb native to Europe, Asia, and Africa.

It typically grows in moist or wet environments such as meadows, marshes, and along stream banks. The plant has a basal rosette of broad, lance-shaped leaves with hairy surfaces. Its flowering stems bear small, clustered greenish-white flowers that are inconspicuous but effective for wind pollination.

The species is known for its medicinal properties, with the leaves often used in traditional remedies.
