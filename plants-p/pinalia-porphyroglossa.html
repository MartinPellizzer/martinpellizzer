Pinalia porphyroglossa

Pinalia porphyroglossa, commonly known as the purple-lip orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking purple and pink flowers, which are often adorned with intricate patterns. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, such as forest understories, where it relies on mycorrhizal fungi for nutrient uptake. Its leaves are elongated and grass-like, contributing to its overall herbaceous appearance.

Pinalia porphyroglossa is valued in horticulture for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in specialized gardens.
