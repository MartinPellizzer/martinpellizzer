Pleroma lepidotum

Pleroma lepidotum, commonly known as the velvetleaf or velvetleaf hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from white to deep pink, often with a distinctive yellow throat. The plant has heart-shaped, velvety leaves that are typically dark green and covered in fine hairs. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands or stream banks.

Pleroma lepidotum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
