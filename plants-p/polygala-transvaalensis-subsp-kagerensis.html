Polygala transvaalensis subsp. kagerensis

Polygala transvaalensis subsp.

kagerensis, commonly known as the Kager's milkweed, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa. It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in grassland or shrubland habitats.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in a spikelike inflorescence, which attracts various pollinators. Its leaves are usually narrow and slightly hairy, and it has a fibrous root system.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
