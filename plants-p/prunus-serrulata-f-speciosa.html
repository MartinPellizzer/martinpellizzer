Prunus serrulata f. speciosa

Prunus serrulata f. speciosa, commonly known as the Japanese cherry blossom or Yamazakura, is a cultivar of the Prunus serrulata species.

It is characterized by its distinctive, doubly serrated leaves and profuse display of pale pink to white flowers in early spring. The plant is native to Japan and thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil. It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes in gardens and public spaces.

Its blooming period is a symbol of seasonal change and is celebrated in cultural events throughout Japan.
