Porophyllum decumbens

Porophyllum decumbens, commonly known as Mexican sunflower, is a herbaceous plant native to the Americas.

It features hairy, sprawling stems and bright orange to yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges, and it is often found in dry, open habitats. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine.

Porophyllum decumbens is an important species in ecological restoration projects due to its adaptability and ability to thrive in poor soils.
