Prunus salicina var. mandshurica

Prunus salicina var. mandshurica, commonly known as the Manchurian apricot, is a cold-hardy variant of the apricot tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its small, oval-shaped leaves and clusters of white or pale pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The fruit is typically small, yellow-orange, and has a tart flavor, making it suitable for both fresh consumption and processing. This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value as well as its edible fruit.

It thrives in temperate climates and is relatively resistant to pests and diseases.
