Phyllanthus candolleanus

Phyllanthus candolleanus, commonly known as the Indian gooseberry or amla, is a tropical shrub native to regions of India and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are typically arranged in pairs along the stems. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by small, round fruits that turn from green to orange-red when mature. The fruit is rich in vitamin C and has been used in traditional medicine for its antioxidant properties.

It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers a warm, humid climate.
