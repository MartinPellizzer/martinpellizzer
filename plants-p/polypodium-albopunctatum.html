Polypodium albopunctatum

Polypodium albopunctatum, commonly known as the white-spotted fern, is a species of epiphytic fern native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its delicate, feathery fronds. The plant's leaves are typically bright green with distinctive white or yellow spots, which give it its common name. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found growing on trees or rocks in tropical rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated as a houseplant.
