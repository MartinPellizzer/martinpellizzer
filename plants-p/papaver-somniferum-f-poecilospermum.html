Papaver somniferum f. poecilospermum

Papaver somniferum f. poecilospermum, commonly known as the opium poppy, is a botanical variant of the opium poppy species.

It is characterized by its distinctive, colorful flowers and unique seed capsule structure. The plant is native to the Mediterranean region and has been cultivated for centuries for its latex, which contains opiate alkaloids. Its botanical features include alternate, lanceolate leaves and a tall, erect stem.

The f. poecilospermum form is particularly noted for its ornamental value and historical significance in pharmaceutical and agricultural contexts.
