Pleurothallis baccata

Pleurothallis baccata, commonly known as the blackberry orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact, epiphytic growth habit. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically greenish-yellow in color, often with a contrasting lip. Its leaves are leathery and dark green, providing a striking contrast to its delicate blooms.

Pleurothallis baccata is valued in horticulture for its unique appearance and relatively easy care requirements.
