Primula soldanelloides

Primula soldanelloides, commonly known as the Alpine primrose, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically produces clusters of white or pale yellow blooms that appear in early spring. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
