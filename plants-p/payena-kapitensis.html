Payena kapitensis

Payena kapitensis, commonly known as the Kapit mangosteen, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Clusiaceae and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant, yellowish flowers. The tree produces fleshy, segmented fruits that are typically yellow or red in color, containing a single seed. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in local medicine.
