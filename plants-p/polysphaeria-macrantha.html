Polysphaeria macrantha

Polysphaeria macrantha, commonly known as the giant sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and large, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish or brownish in color. It thrives in wet, marshy environments and is often found in swampy areas along rivers and lakes.

Polysphaeria macrantha plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
