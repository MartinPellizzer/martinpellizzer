Prunus junghuhniana

Prunus junghuhniana, commonly known as the Junghuhn cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, fragrant white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, edible drupes with a sweet, tart flavor, making it valuable in both ornamental and culinary contexts. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a smooth texture, while its bark is grey and fissured.

Prunus junghuhniana thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas across its native range.
