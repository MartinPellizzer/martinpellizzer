Parodia kilianana var. dichroacantha

Parodia kilianana var. dichroacantha, commonly known as the two-colored moon cactus, is a succulent plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its compact, globular shape and thick, fleshy stems that store water. The plant features a striking color contrast between its green stem and the vibrant red or orange flowers it produces. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it ideal for arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and ease of care, it is a popular choice for collectors and succulent enthusiasts.
