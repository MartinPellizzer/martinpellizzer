Plectranthus discolor

Plectranthus discolor, commonly known as the Variegated Mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South Africa.

It features opposite, ovate leaves with a striking combination of white, green, and silver markings. The plant produces small, tubular flowers in shades of purple and pink, typically blooming in summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it a popular choice for gardens and containers.

Its aromatic foliage and ornamental value make it both a decorative and potentially useful plant in horticulture.
