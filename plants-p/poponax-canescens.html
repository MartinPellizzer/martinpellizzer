Poponax canescens

Poponax canescens, commonly known as the white thorn.

It is a shrub native to parts of South America, particularly in Argentina and Chile. The plant is characterized by its slender, thorny stems and small, white flowers that bloom in the spring. Its leaves are simple and alternate, often covered in a fine layer of white hairs.

Poponax canescens is adapted to arid environments and is often found in dry grasslands and scrub habitats.
