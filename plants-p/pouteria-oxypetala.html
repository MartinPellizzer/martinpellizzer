Pouteria oxypetala

Pouteria oxypetala, commonly known as the red mangosteen, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of fragrant white flowers. The fruit is typically reddish-purple when ripe, with a thick, segmented rind and a sweet, aromatic pulp. The plant thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and valuable species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
