Pulicaria vulgaris subsp. vulgaris

Pulicaria vulgaris subsp.

vulgaris, commonly known as common tansy, is a low-growing perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia. It features wiry, branching stems covered in fine, hairy foliage.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in dense, flat-topped clusters, typically blooming from June to August. Its leaves are aromatic and contain compounds that have historically been used in herbal remedies.

It thrives in dry, sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in coastal areas and rocky habitats.
