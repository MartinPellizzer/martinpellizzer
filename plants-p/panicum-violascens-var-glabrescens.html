Panicum violascens var. glabrescens

Panicum violascens var. glabrescens, commonly known as purple lovegrass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and striking purple to violet-colored inflorescences. The plant typically grows in open areas, such as roadsides and disturbed soils, and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue, and it produces small, wind-dispersed seeds.

This species is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to various soil conditions.
