Papaver minus

Papaver minus, commonly known as the lesser poppy, is a small flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in temperate climates. The plant features delicate, nodding flowers with vibrant orange or red petals, often surrounded by a white or yellow calyx. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a fine, hairy texture.

Papaver minus is known for its medicinal properties, with historical use in traditional remedies.
