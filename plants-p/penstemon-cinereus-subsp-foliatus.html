Penstemon cinereus subsp. foliatus

Penstemon cinereus subsp.

foliatus, commonly known as the gray beardgrass penstemon, is a perennial herb native to the western United States. It is characterized by its gray-green, hairy foliage and tall, slender stems that bear clusters of tubular, two-lipped flowers.

The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open woodland or sagebrush habitats. Its flowers, which range in color from blue to purple, bloom in late spring to early summer.

The subspecies is notable for its distinctive, softly hairy leaves and its ability to thrive in arid environments.
