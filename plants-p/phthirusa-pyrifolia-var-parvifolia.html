Phthirusa pyrifolia var. parvifolia

Phthirusa pyrifolia var. parvifolia, commonly known as the small-leaved pear.

It is a variant of the pear species characterized by its smaller, more compact leaves compared to the typical variety. The plant is native to regions with temperate climates and thrives in well-drained soil. Its flowers are usually white or pale yellow, and it produces small, edible fruits.

This variety is appreciated for its ornamental value and mild flavor, making it suitable for both garden cultivation and culinary use.
