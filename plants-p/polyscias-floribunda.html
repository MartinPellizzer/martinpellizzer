Polyscias floribunda

Polyscias floribunda, commonly known as the golden nettle, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 6 meters in height, featuring ovate to elliptic leaves with a smooth, glossy surface. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by spherical, yellowish fruits. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties.

The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
