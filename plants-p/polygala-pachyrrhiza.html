Polygala pachyrrhiza

Polygala pachyrrhiza, commonly known as the thick-stemmed milkweed, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and the Middle East.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and clusters of small, pale yellow or greenish flowers. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to dry conditions. Its leaves are narrow and often have a hairy texture, which helps reduce water loss.

The species is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and ecological significance in its native habitats.
