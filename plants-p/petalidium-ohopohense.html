Petalidium ohopohense

Petalidium ohopohense, commonly known as the Ohopohen rock vine, is a rare and endemic plant species native to New Zealand.

It is a climbing shrub characterized by its woody stems and small, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of white, fragrant flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. Its botanical features include a unique floral structure with five petals and a prominent stamen.

Due to habitat loss, it is classified as critically endangered, making conservation efforts essential for its survival.
