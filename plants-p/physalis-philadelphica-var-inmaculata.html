Physalis philadelphica var. inmaculata

Physalis philadelphica var. inmaculata, commonly known as the white-berried groundcherry, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into inflated, white to pale green capsules containing numerous small, yellow berries. These berries are edible when fully ripe and have a tart, sweet flavor, often used in culinary applications.

It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental and edible gardens.
