Passiflora gabrielliana

Passiflora gabrielliana, commonly known as the Gabriella passionflower, is a climbing vine native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Passifloraceae family and is characterized by its striking, complex flowers with five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments. The plant produces edible berries that are rich in nutrients and have a mild, tangy flavor. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and botanical collections.
