Prunophora

Prunophora, commonly known as the blackthorn, is a shrub belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and small, dark purple berries that ripen in late summer. The plant is native to Europe, North Africa, and parts of Western Asia. Its flowers are typically white or pink and appear before the leaves in early spring.

Prunophora is valued for its ornamental appeal and has historical uses in traditional medicine.
