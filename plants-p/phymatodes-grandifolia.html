Phymatodes grandifolia

Phymatodes grandifolia, commonly known as the Mexican giant dudleya, is a succulent plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that store water, adapting to arid environments. The plant produces large, showy white or pink flowers during the spring season. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Its slow growth rate and ability to survive in harsh conditions make it a resilient addition to xeriscapes.
