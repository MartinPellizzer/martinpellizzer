Prunus lusitanica var. serratifolia

Prunus lusitanica var. serratifolia, commonly known as the Portuguese cherry or serrated leaf cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped, serrated leaves that turn reddish in autumn. The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers in early spring, followed by edible, cherry-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for Mediterranean climates.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is often cultivated in gardens and natural landscapes.
