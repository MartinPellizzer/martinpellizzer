Pilosocereus leucocephalus subsp. palmeri

Pilosocereus leucocephalus subsp.

palmeri, commonly known as the palmer's moon cactus, is a species of cactus native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems and white, woolly spines that give it a distinctive appearance.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun, exhibiting drought tolerance. It produces yellow flowers during the summer months, adding ornamental value to its arid habitat.

This subspecies is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its unique morphology and low maintenance requirements.
