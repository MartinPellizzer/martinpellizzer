Pleurothallis giraffa

Pleurothallis giraffa, commonly known as the giraffe orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, clustered flowers that often resemble the shape of a giraffe's neck. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments within the understory of tropical forests. Its flowers are usually white or pale yellow, with intricate patterns that attract specific pollinators.

Pleurothallis giraffa is a member of the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is known for its delicate and elegant appearance.
