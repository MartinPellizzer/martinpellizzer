Piper verruculicaule

Piper verruculicaule, commonly known as the rough-stemmed pepper vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many species of pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its hairy, rough-textured stems and small, opposite leaves. Its flowers are unisexual, with separate male and female flowers on the same plant.

The fruit is a small, three-lobed berry that ripens to a dark purple color.
