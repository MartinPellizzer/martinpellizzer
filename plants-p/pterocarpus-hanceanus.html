Pterocarpus hanceanus

Pterocarpus hanceanus, commonly known as the Chinese sandalwood, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and bipinnate leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers followed by flat, elongated pods containing hard, dark seeds. It is valued for its aromatic wood, which is used in traditional medicine and as a source of essential oils.

The plant plays an important role in local ecosystems and is often found in tropical dry forests.
