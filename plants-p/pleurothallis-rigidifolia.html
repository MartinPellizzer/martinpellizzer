Pleurothallis rigidifolia

Pleurothallis rigidifolia, commonly known as the rigid-stemmed orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact, evergreen growth habit. The plant typically produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences. It thrives in shaded, humid environments and requires well-draining, acidic soil.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is a subject of interest for botanists and orchid enthusiasts.
