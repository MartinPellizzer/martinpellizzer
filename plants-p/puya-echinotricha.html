Puya echinotricha

Puya echinotricha, commonly known as the spiny puya, is a striking plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a member of the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its tall, spiky inflorescence that can reach several meters in height. The plant produces vibrant, showy flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, which attract pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees. Its leaves are long, narrow, and often covered in small, spiny projections, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Puya echinotricha is adapted to high-altitude environments and thrives in well-drained, rocky soils.
