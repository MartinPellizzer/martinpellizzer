Penstemon pachyphyllus var. mucronatus

Penstemon pachyphyllus var. mucronatus, commonly known as the Sierra penstemon, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The plant has thick, fleshy leaves that help it conserve water in arid environments. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it well-suited for xeriscaping.

Its floral structure is adapted for pollination by bees and other insects, contributing to its ecological significance in native plant communities.
