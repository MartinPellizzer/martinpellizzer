Pithecellobium ellipticum

Pithecellobium ellipticum, commonly known as the elliptic acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically pinnately arranged with elliptical leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by pod-like fruits containing edible seeds. It is often found in dry to moist forests and is adapted to various soil types.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local communities.
