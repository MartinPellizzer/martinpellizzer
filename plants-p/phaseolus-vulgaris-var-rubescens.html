Phaseolus vulgaris var. rubescens

Phaseolus vulgaris var. rubescens, commonly known as the red kidney bean, is a variety of the common bean plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its kidney-shaped, red or reddish-brown seeds that are often used in various cuisines around the world. The plant typically grows as an annual herb with compound leaves and produces small, white or pale purple flowers. Its seeds are rich in protein and dietary fiber, making them a valuable food source.

However, they contain lectins and phytohemagglutinin, which can be toxic if not properly cooked.
