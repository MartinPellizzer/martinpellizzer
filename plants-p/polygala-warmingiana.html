Polygala warmingiana

Polygala warmingiana, commonly known as the Chinese milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to China.

It belongs to the Polygalaceae family and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. Its flowers are usually pale yellow or greenish, with a unique structure that attracts insects for pollination.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
