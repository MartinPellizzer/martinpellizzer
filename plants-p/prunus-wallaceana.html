Prunus wallaceana

Prunus wallaceana, commonly known as the Wallace cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It is characterized by its slender, multi-stemmed form and smooth, grey-brown bark. The plant produces white to pale pink flowers in early spring, which are borne in clusters and attract pollinators. Its leaves are oval-shaped with a pointed tip and are dark green on top and lighter underneath.

The fruit is a small, yellowish drupe that ripens in late summer.
