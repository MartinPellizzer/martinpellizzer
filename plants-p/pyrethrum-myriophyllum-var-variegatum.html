Pyrethrum myriophyllum var. variegatum

Pyrethrum myriophyllum var. variegatum, commonly known as the variegated water plantain, is a water-loving perennial herb native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features long, narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often variegated with white or yellow markings. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink, blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in shallow water or moist soil, making it a popular choice for wetland gardens.

Its botanical characteristics make it both ornamental and useful in ecological restoration projects.
