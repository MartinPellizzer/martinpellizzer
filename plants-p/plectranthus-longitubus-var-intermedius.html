Plectranthus longitubus var. intermedius

Plectranthus longitubus var. intermedius, commonly known as the narrow-leaved plectranthus, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features opposite, narrow, ovate leaves that are often slightly hairy, giving it a textured appearance. The plant produces small, tubular flowers in shades of purple or pink, typically arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for both garden beds and container planting.

Due to its ornamental value and ease of cultivation, it is a popular choice among gardeners seeking low-maintenance, attractive foliage.
