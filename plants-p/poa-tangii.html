Poa tangii

Poa tangii, commonly known as the Stewart's grass, is a flowering plant species native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, erect stems. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences that are typically green to reddish in color. Poa tangii is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and along stream banks.

Its ecological role includes providing habitat and food for various invertebrates and small mammals.
