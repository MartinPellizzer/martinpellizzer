Phyllanthus salviifolius

Phyllanthus salviifolius, commonly known as the sacred lotus or Indian lotus, is a member of the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in cyathia, typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. It thrives in wetlands and marshy areas, often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and cultural contexts.
