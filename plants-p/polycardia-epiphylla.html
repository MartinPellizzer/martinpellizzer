Polycardia epiphylla

Polycardia epiphylla, commonly known as the giant orchid, is a striking epiphytic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its large, showy flowers that can reach up to 30 centimeters in diameter. The plant typically grows on trees or rocks, using its aerial roots to absorb moisture and nutrients from the environment. Its leaves are broad and leathery, adapted to store water in its native humid climate.

Polycardia epiphylla is valued not only for its ornamental beauty but also for its traditional use in herbal medicine.
