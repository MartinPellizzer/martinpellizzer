Polygala luteoviridis

Polygala luteoviridis, commonly known as the yellow-green milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. Its flowers are often clustered in dense, spike-like inflorescences, and it produces small, elliptical fruits that contain several seeds.

Polygala luteoviridis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and subtle floral display.
