Philodendron daniellii

Philodendron daniellii, commonly known as the Daniell's philodendron, is a tropical climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It features heart-shaped, glossy, and deeply veined leaves that are typically a vibrant green. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by green, berry-like fruits that mature to a reddish color. It thrives in warm, humid environments with bright, indirect light.

Its rapid growth and attractive foliage make it a popular choice for indoor gardening and as a houseplant.
