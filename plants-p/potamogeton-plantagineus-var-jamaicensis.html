Potamogeton plantagineus var. jamaicensis

Potamogeton plantagineus var. jamaicensis, commonly known as the Jamaican pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to the freshwater ecosystems of the Caribbean.

It has long, slender leaves that are typically arranged in whorls around the stem, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often submerged. This plant thrives in slow-moving or still waters with moderate to high light levels. It plays a vital role in aquatic habitats by providing shelter for fish and invertebrates.

Its presence is often an indicator of good water quality in its native range.
