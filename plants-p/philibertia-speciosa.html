Philibertia speciosa

Philibertia speciosa, commonly known as the showy filaree, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant features basal leaves that are often lobed and covered in fine hairs, and its stems are typically upright and branched. Philibertia speciosa produces bright yellow to orange flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands and disturbed areas.
