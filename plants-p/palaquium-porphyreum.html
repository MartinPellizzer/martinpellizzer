Palaquium porphyreum

Palaquium porphyreum, commonly known as the purple mangosteen, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant white flowers. The fruit is small, round, and has a thick, purple rind with a sweet, segmented white flesh inside. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible fruit.

The plant is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional medicine.
