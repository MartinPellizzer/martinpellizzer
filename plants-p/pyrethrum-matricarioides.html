Pyrethrum matricarioides

Pyrethrum matricarioides, commonly known as the common chrysanthemum, is a flowering plant native to Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its daisy-like flowers with white petals and a yellow center. The plant produces pyrethrin, a natural insecticide used in organic pest control. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often growing in temperate climates.

Pyrethrum matricarioides is widely cultivated for its ornamental value and medicinal properties.
