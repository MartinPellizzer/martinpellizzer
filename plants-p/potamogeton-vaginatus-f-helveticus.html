Potamogeton vaginatus f. helveticus

Potamogeton vaginatus f. helveticus, commonly known as the European pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater habitats in Europe.

It has long, slender stems that are often branched and bear small, oval leaves arranged in whorls. The plant produces tiny, green flowers that are typically clustered in spike-like inflorescences. It thrives in still or slow-moving waters with muddy or sandy substrates.

Its presence is often indicative of good water quality and is an important component of aquatic ecosystems.
