Polygala virgata var. speciosa

Polygala virgata var. speciosa, commonly known as the showy milk-vetch, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its delicate, feathery leaves and showy, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in coastal or mountainous regions. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.

The species is also noted for its ecological importance, providing nectar for various pollinators.
