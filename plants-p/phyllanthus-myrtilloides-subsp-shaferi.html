Phyllanthus myrtilloides subsp. shaferi

Phyllanthus myrtilloides subsp.

shaferi, commonly known as the dwarf myrtilloides, is a shrub native to parts of Africa and the Arabian Peninsula. It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in arid or semi-arid environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes used in ornamental gardening or for soil stabilization in degraded landscapes.
