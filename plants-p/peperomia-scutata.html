Peperomia scutata

Peperomia scutata, commonly known as the shield leaf peperomia, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It features thick, waxy leaves that are typically oval or lance-shaped, often with a distinctive shield-like appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in shades of white or pale pink, which are usually followed by tiny, round berries. It is well-suited for indoor cultivation due to its low maintenance requirements and ability to thrive in indirect light.

Peperomia scutata is valued for its attractive foliage and air-purifying qualities.
