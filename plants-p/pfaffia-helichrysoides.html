Pfaffia helichrysoides

Pfaffia helichrysoides, commonly known as golden crownbeard, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellow, daisy-like flowers and hairy, lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories. It is often found in regions with high humidity and well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
