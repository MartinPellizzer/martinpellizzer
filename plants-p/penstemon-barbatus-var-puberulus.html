Penstemon barbatus var. puberulus

Penstemon barbatus var. puberulus, commonly known as the hairy beardtongue, is a perennial plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it its common name. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its foliage and flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to ecological diversity in its habitat.
