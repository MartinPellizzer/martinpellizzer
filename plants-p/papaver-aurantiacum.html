Papaver aurantiacum

Papaver aurantiacum, commonly known as the orange poppy, is a species of flowering plant in the family Papaveraceae.

It is native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia, where it thrives in rocky or sandy soils. The plant produces striking orange-red flowers with a prominent central stigma, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture.

The orange poppy is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
