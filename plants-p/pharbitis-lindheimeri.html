Pharbitis lindheimeri

Pharbitis lindheimeri, commonly known as Lindheimer's morning glory, is a climbing vine native to the southern United States.

It features heart-shaped leaves and produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically blue or violet in color. The plant is characterized by its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types. It is often found in woodland areas and along stream banks.

The seeds are enclosed in a spiral-shaped capsule, which aids in their dispersal by wind.
