Phalaenopsis speciosa subvar. christiana

Phalaenopsis speciosa subvar. christiana, commonly known as the Christian's orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, long-lasting flowers that range in color from white to pink and sometimes with a purple tinge. The plant has a sympodial growth habit, forming pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its leaves are broad, fleshy, and arranged in a fan-like pattern, typical of many orchid species.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated as a houseplant due to its ease of care and aesthetic appeal.
