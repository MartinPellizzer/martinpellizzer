Pilocereus sargentianus

Pilocereus sargentianus, commonly known as the Sargent's night bloomer, is a cacti species native to South America.

It is characterized by its spiny, cylindrical stems and produces large, fragrant white flowers at night. The plant is adapted to arid environments, with thick, fleshy stems that store water. Its flowers typically open in response to cooler temperatures and moonlight, attracting nocturnal pollinators.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and unique blooming behavior.
