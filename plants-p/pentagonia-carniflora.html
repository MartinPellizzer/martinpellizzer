Pentagonia carniflora

Pentagonia carniflora, commonly known as the bloodflower, is a striking plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its unique, pentagonal-shaped leaves that are often a deep red or maroon color, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, typically in shades of white and pink. Its foliage is known for its glossy texture and vibrant coloration, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Pentagonia carniflora thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.
