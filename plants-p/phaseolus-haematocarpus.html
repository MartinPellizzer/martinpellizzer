Phaseolus haematocarpus

Phaseolus haematocarpus, commonly known as the blood bean plant, is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to Central and South America and is characterized by its distinctive red, fleshy pods that contain edible seeds. The plant is a perennial shrub that can grow up to 2 meters tall, with trifoliate leaves and small, white or pale yellow flowers. Its root system is notable for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility.

The plant's unique seed structure and ornamental value make it both ecologically and horticulturally significant.
