Potentilla aurea var. latifolia

Potentilla aurea var. latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves and bright yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or meadow habitats. It has a low-growing habit and is often found in shaded or partially shaded areas.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in native plant gardens.
