Poterium sanguisorba var. garganicum

Poterium sanguisorba var. garganicum, commonly known as bloodroot, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its striking red-orange flowers that bloom in early spring, often before the leaves emerge. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine, silky hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
