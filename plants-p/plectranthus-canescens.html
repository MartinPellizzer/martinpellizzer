Plectranthus canescens

Plectranthus canescens, commonly known as the grey plectranthus, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its greyish-blue leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that range in color from purple to blue, typically blooming in summer. It is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and compact growth habit.

Plectranthus canescens prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it adaptable to various garden settings.
