Petasites warrenii

Petasites warrenii, commonly known as the Warren's buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can reach up to 30 centimeters in diameter. The plant produces yellow flowers in late spring, which are clustered in a dense inflorescence. It thrives in cool, moist environments such as alpine meadows and forested areas.

Petasites warrenii is known for its unique morphology and is often found in high-altitude habitats.
