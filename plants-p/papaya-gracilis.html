Papaya gracilis

Papaya gracilis, commonly known as the slender papaya, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is a member of the Caricaceae family and is characterized by its slender, elongated fruit. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are large, heart-shaped, and have a rough texture, while the flowers are small and yellow.

The fruit is usually pear-shaped, with a yellow or green skin and a soft, sweet, aromatic flesh.
