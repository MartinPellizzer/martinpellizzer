Prunus communis proles intermedia

Prunus communis proles intermedia, commonly known as the European bird cherry, is a small deciduous tree or shrub native to Europe.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and oval-shaped leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, yellow-green flowers in early spring, followed by edible, sweet-tart cherries that ripen in late summer. It is often found in woodland edges and is valued for its ornamental appeal and wildlife habitat.

The species is also used in horticulture for its hardiness and adaptability to various soil types.
