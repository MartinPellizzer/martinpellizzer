Phaseolus fulvus

Phaseolus fulvus, commonly known as the golden bean, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of Central and South America, where it thrives in warm, humid climates. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that give way to elongated, bean-like pods containing edible seeds. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets, and the plant is often cultivated for its nutritional value.

Phaseolus fulvus is also valued for its ornamental appeal due to its vibrant yellow blooms.
