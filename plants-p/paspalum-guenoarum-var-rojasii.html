Paspalum guenoarum var. rojasii

Paspalum guenoarum var. rojasii, commonly known as the Rojas' grass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. This plant is often found in moist, tropical or subtropical environments, including wetlands and riverbanks. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense mats.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in wetland habitats.
