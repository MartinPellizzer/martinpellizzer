Prunus peduncularis

Prunus peduncularis, commonly known as the Japanese white cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, white flowers that bloom in early spring before the leaves emerge. The plant has simple, oval-shaped leaves that turn yellow in the fall. Its fruit is a small, sweet drupe that ripens in late summer.

Prunus peduncularis is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its aesthetic appeal and cultural significance.
