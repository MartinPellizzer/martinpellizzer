Pilosocereus sartorianus

Pilosocereus sartorianus, commonly known as the Sartorianus cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of South America.

It is a slow-growing, columnar cactus with a distinctive appearance, featuring a ribbed stem and spines that are often arranged in a spiral pattern. The plant produces small, white flowers during the warmer months, typically at night, which attract nocturnal pollinators. Its thick, fleshy stems are adapted to store water, allowing it to survive in harsh, dry environments.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological significance, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant in cactus collections.
