Phyllanthus neogranatensis

Phyllanthus neogranatensis, commonly known as the Brazilian star apple, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are typically elliptical or ovate in shape. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by fruits that resemble small, round apples. The fruit has a thick, yellow or greenish skin and a sweet, juicy pulp with a mild, honey-like flavor.

It is often cultivated for its edible fruit and has traditional medicinal uses in some regions.
