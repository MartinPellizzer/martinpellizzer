Posoqueria formosa

Posoqueria formosa, commonly known as the golden shower tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, pendulous clusters of yellow flowers that bloom throughout the year. The plant has palm-like leaves that are large and feather-shaped, providing a lush appearance. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Posoqueria formosa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
