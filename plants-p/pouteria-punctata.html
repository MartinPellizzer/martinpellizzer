Pouteria punctata

Pouteria punctata, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its smooth, dark green leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces edible berries that are typically orange to red in color, containing a single seed. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in coastal areas and lowland forests.

Due to its rapid growth and invasive tendencies, it is considered a problematic species in certain regions.
