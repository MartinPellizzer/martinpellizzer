Pandanus haapaiensis

Pandanus haapaiensis, commonly known as the Tongan pandanus, is a flowering plant native to the islands of the South Pacific.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and long, narrow leaves that are often used in traditional crafts. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. It thrives in coastal areas and is well adapted to salty air and sandy soils.

Pandanus haapaiensis plays an important role in the cultural and ecological systems of its native regions.
