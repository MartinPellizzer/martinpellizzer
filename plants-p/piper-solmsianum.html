Piper solmsianum

Piper solmsianum, commonly known as Solmsian pepper or Solmsian long pepper, is a climbing shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, peppery fruits that resemble small, elongated berries. The plant produces small, white flowers that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. Its leaves are alternate, ovate to elliptic in shape, and often have a glossy surface.

The fruit is used in traditional medicine and as a spice, similar to black pepper.
