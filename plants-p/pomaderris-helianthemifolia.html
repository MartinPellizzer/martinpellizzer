Pomaderris helianthemifolia

Pomaderris helianthemifolia, commonly known as the sunflower pomaderris, is a shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its small, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, white hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, yellow flowers that resemble those of sunflowers, blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

Pomaderris helianthemifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in native gardens.
