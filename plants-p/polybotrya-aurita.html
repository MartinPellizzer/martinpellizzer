Polybotrya aurita

Polybotrya aurita, commonly known as the golden fern, is a species of flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its delicate, feathery fronds. The plant produces small, yellowish spore-bearing structures called sorus, which are clustered on the underside of its leaves. It thrives in humid, shaded environments, typically found in tropical rainforests.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for indoor gardening and botanical collections.
