Pelargonium × multinerve

Pelargonium × multinerve, commonly known as the multivened geranium, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Geraniaceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive, deeply lobed leaves with multiple veins, which give it its scientific name. The plant typically produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of pink, red, or white, depending on the cultivar. It is a herbaceous perennial that thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its ability to bloom throughout the growing season.
