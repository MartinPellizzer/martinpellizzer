Pilosocereus alensis

Pilosocereus alensis, commonly known as the Mexican moon cactus, is a cacti species native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its swollen, rounded stem that resembles a moon, and its distinctive spines. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils in arid environments. It produces white or pink flowers during the summer months.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is a popular choice for succulent collectors and gardeners.
