Potamogeton groenlandicus

Potamogeton groenlandicus, commonly known as Greenland pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to cold freshwater environments in the Northern Hemisphere.

It has long, narrow leaves that are typically arranged in whorls around the stem, and it produces small, green flowers that are clustered in spike-like inflorescences. The plant thrives in shallow, nutrient-rich waters and is often found in lakes, rivers, and wetlands. It plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.

Its presence can indicate good water quality and a healthy ecosystem.
