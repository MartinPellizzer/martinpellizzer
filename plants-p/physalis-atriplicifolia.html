Physalis atriplicifolia

Physalis atriplicifolia, commonly known as the Mexican groundcherry, is a herbaceous perennial native to Mexico and Central America.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into inflated, lantern-shaped fruits. These fruits mature to a bright orange-red color and contain numerous tiny, edible seeds.

It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes and has some traditional medicinal uses.
