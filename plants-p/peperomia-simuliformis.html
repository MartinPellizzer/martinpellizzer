Peperomia simuliformis

Peperomia simuliformis, commonly known as the False Shrimp Plant, is a compact, evergreen perennial native to tropical regions of South America.

It features small, ovate leaves that are often covered in a fine, white powdery coating, known as farina, which helps reduce water loss. The plant produces delicate, spadix-like inflorescences surrounded by a colorful bract, typically in shades of red or purple. It thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Due to its slow growth and attractive foliage, it is often used as a decorative plant in both indoor and outdoor settings.
