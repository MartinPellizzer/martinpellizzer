Potamogeton × heidenreichii

Potamogeton × heidenreichii, commonly known as the hybrid water plant, is a sterile hybrid species resulting from the cross between Potamogeton perfoliatus and Potamogeton praevalidus.

It is an aquatic plant characterized by its submerged leaves that are often arranged in whorls and possess a distinctive, narrow, grass-like appearance. The plant typically grows in shallow, freshwater environments such as lakes, ponds, and slow-moving rivers. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, often occurring in clusters, and it plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for various invertebrates and fish.

Due to its sterility, it does not produce viable seeds and relies on vegetative propagation for spread.
