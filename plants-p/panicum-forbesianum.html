Panicum forbesianum

Panicum forbesianum, commonly known as Forbes' grass, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, wiry stems. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, spikelet-like flowers. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often with a bluish-gray hue due to a dense covering of hairs.

Forbes' grass is often used in ornamental gardening for its attractive appearance and drought tolerance.
