Phyteuma minutum

Phyteuma minutum, commonly known as the dwarf phyteuma, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and small, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, producing clusters of pale blue to violet flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and rockeries.
