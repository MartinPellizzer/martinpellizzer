Pilea lancifolia

Pilea lancifolia, commonly known as the Chinese money plant, is a species of flowering plant in the family Urticaceae.

It is native to China and is characterized by its rosette-shaped foliage composed of thick, waxy, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers that appear in clusters during the spring and summer months. Pilea lancifolia is a popular indoor plant due to its ease of care and aesthetic appeal.

It prefers bright, indirect light and well-draining soil to thrive.
