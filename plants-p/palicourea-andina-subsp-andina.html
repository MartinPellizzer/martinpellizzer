Palicourea andina subsp. andina

Palicourea andina subsp.

andina, commonly known as Andean palicourea, is a species native to the Andean regions of South America. It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in moist, shaded environments within montane forests.

The plant is characterized by its opposite, elliptical leaves that are often glossy and dark green. It produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, followed by small, berry-like fruits.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
