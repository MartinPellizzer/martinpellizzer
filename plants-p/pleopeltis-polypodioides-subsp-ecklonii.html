Pleopeltis polypodioides subsp. ecklonii

Pleopeltis polypodioides subsp.

ecklonii, commonly known as the Ecklon's rock fern, is a terrestrial fern native to southern Africa. It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its compact, often clustered growth habit.

The fronds are typically leathery and have a distinctive, serrated margin. It thrives in rocky or shaded environments, often growing on cliffs or among stones.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in shaded gardens.
