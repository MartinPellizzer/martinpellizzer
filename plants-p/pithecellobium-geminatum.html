Pithecellobium geminatum

Pithecellobium geminatum, commonly known as the twin-fruit acacia, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellowish-white flowers. The plant produces small, edible pods that contain a sweet, sticky pulp and seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed or secondary forests.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is cultivated in some regions for both horticultural and culinary purposes.
