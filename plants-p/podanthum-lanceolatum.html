Podanthum lanceolatum

Podanthum lanceolatum, commonly known as the lanceleaf podanthum, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or along stream banks. Its botanical features include opposite leaf arrangement and a fibrous root system.

Podanthum lanceolatum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
