Palisota hirsuta

Palisota hirsuta, commonly known as hairy broadleaf, is a perennial herb native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive textured appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its fibrous roots and robust growth make it a hardy species adaptable to various wetland conditions.
