Pitcairnia altensteinii

Pitcairnia altensteinii, commonly known as the Altenstein's pitcairnia, is a species of bromeliad native to the Andes of South America.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that form a central cup, which collects rainwater for the plant's use. The plant produces striking, inflorescence with bright red flowers that bloom in the early morning. It thrives in shaded, humid environments and requires well-draining soil.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
