Pelargonium marginatum

Pelargonium marginatum, commonly known as the margined geranium, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive leaf margins, which are often marked with a contrasting color, typically white or pale green. The plant produces vibrant, showy flowers in shades of pink, red, and purple, typically blooming in spring and summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and containers.

Pelargonium marginatum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ease of cultivation.
