Primulina scutellifolia

Primulina scutellifolia, commonly known as the shield-leaf gloxinia, is a tropical flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Gesneriaceae family and is characterized by its striking, velvety leaves with a distinctive shield-shaped base. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, typically during the warm season. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires consistent moisture and high humidity to flourish.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in greenhouse settings.
