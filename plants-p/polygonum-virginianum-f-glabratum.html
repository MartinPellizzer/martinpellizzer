Polygonum virginianum f. glabratum

Polygonum virginianum f. glabratum, commonly known as smooth smartweed, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a member of the Polygonaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and slender, branching stems. The plant produces small, pink to reddish flowers that appear in dense clusters during the summer months. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a smooth, glabrous surface, which distinguishes it from other species in the genus.

Smooth smartweed is often found in moist habitats such as wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.
