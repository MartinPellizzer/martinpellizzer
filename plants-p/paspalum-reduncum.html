Paspalum reduncum

Paspalum reduncum, commonly known as the slender paspalum, is a perennial grass native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It is often found in wet or seasonally flooded habitats, such as marshes and wetlands.

Paspalum reduncum is valued for its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife species.
