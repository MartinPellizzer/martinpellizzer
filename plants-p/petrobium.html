Petrobium

Petrobium, commonly known as the rock spurge, is a low-growing flowering plant belonging to the family Euphorbiaceae.

It is characterized by its small, inconspicuous flowers and fleshy, often hairy leaves. This plant thrives in rocky and coastal environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Petrobium species are typically found in Mediterranean regions and parts of Europe.

Its botanical features make it an important component of coastal and rocky ecosystems.
