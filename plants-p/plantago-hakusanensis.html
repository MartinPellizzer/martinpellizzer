Plantago hakusanensis

Plantago hakusanensis, commonly known as the Japanese plantain, is a flowering plant species native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of broad, oval-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in a spike, which are typically pollinated by wind. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along streambanks.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is cultivated in some regions for horticultural and traditional uses.
