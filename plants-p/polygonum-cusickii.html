Polygonum cusickii

Polygonum cusickii, commonly known as Cusick's knotweed, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It typically grows in moist soils along stream banks and in wet meadows. The plant features upright, branched stems that are often reddish in color. Its leaves are lance-shaped, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface, and it produces small, clustered flowers in late summer.

Polygonum cusickii is known for its ability to thrive in wet environments and is often found in riparian zones.
