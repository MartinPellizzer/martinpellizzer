Piper kappleri

Piper kappleri, commonly known as Kappler's pepper, is a flowering plant native to the rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many species of pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its small, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of tiny, greenish-white flowers. It produces small, round berries that turn red when mature, which are used in traditional medicine.

The plant's botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and medicinal contexts.
