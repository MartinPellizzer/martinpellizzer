Psidium grandifolium var. tenuinerve

Psidium grandifolium var. tenuinerve, commonly known as the Brazilian guava, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, elongated leaves with a distinctively narrow vein structure. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, yellow-orange fruits. Its botanical features include a woody stem, opposite leaf arrangement, and a preference for warm, humid climates.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit production in suitable environments.
