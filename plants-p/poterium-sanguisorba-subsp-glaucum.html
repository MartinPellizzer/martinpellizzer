Poterium sanguisorba subsp. glaucum

Poterium sanguisorba subsp.

glaucum, commonly known as the glaucous dock, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It typically grows in moist to wet soils, often found in marshy areas or along riverbanks.

The plant features broad, waxy leaves that are often covered in a bluish-gray coating, which helps reduce water loss. Its flowers are small and clustered in dense heads, usually appearing in late summer.

The species is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally in herbal remedies.
