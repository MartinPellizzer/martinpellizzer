Potentilla recta var. vlasicensis

Potentilla recta var. vlasicensis, commonly known as the Vlasean cinquefoil, is a perennial shrub native to Central Asia.

It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is characterized by its upright growth habit and small, bright yellow flowers. The plant has compound leaves with five elliptical leaflets, and its stems are often covered in fine hairs.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardiness and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
