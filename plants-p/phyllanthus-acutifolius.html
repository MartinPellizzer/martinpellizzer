Phyllanthus acutifolius

Phyllanthus acutifolius, commonly known as the sharp-leaved phyllanthus, is a shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific.

It typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring small, elliptical leaves with sharp, pointed tips. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, followed by spherical, red or orange fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, disturbed habitats.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species in traditional medicine and ecological studies.
