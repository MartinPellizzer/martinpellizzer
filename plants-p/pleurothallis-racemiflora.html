Pleurothallis racemiflora

Pleurothallis racemiflora, commonly known as the racemiflora orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact, epiphytic growth habit. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pale yellow, typically blooming during the warmer months. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, adapted to its humid, shaded forest habitat.

Due to its unique appearance and delicate blooms, it is a popular choice among orchid enthusiasts.
