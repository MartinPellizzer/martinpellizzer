Pauneroa

Pauneroa, commonly known as the mountain balm, is a flowering plant native to the Andes region of South America.

It belongs to the family Lamiaceae, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its square stems, opposite leaves, and clusters of small, fragrant flowers. Pauneroa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.

Its botanical features make it well adapted to high-altitude environments.
