Podophania dissecta

Podophania dissecta, commonly known as the dissected podophyllum, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the temperate regions of South America.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of lance-shaped, deeply lobed leaves that give it a distinctive, fern-like appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by bright red, fleshy berries that are a key feature of its reproductive cycle. Podophania dissecta thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its unique morphology and ecological role make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
