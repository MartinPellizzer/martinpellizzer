Piper rhyncholepsis

Piper rhyncholepsis, commonly known as the Malayan pepper vine, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species. The plant is characterized by its climbing habit and opposite, ovate leaves. Its small, greenish-white flowers are borne in clusters and are followed by elongated, woody fruits.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has some traditional medicinal uses.
