Ptychotis saxifraga var. bipinnata

Ptychotis saxifraga var. bipinnata, commonly known as the two-feathered saxifrage, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of lance-shaped leaves with a distinctive grayish-blue hue. The plant produces small, pinkish-white flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments.

Due to its unique morphology and habitat, it is considered a rare and specialized species within its native range.
