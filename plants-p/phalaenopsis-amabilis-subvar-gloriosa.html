Phalaenopsis amabilis subvar. gloriosa

Phalaenopsis amabilis subvar. gloriosa, commonly known as the glory orchid, is a tropical epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its long, arching inflorescences that bear clusters of fragrant, showy flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant has broad, dark green leaves that are often speckled with silver or gray markings. It thrives in warm, humid environments with indirect light and requires well-draining potting media.

This variety is popular in horticulture for its longevity and ability to bloom multiple times throughout the year.
