Pinus kasya

Pinus kasya, commonly known as the Kashmir pine, is a coniferous tree native to the Himalayan regions of India and Pakistan.

It is characterized by its tall, straight trunk and dense, dark green foliage. The tree produces cones that are typically 5 to 10 centimeters long, containing edible seeds. Its needles are long and slender, often arranged in bundles of five, and it has a deep root system that helps stabilize the soil in mountainous areas.

The Kashmir pine plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing habitat for various wildlife species.
