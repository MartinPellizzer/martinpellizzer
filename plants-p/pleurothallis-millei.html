Pleurothallis millei

Pleurothallis millei, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact, epiphytic growth habit. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of yellow and orange, which bloom during the dry season. Its roots are adapted to absorb moisture from the air, typical of many epiphytic orchids.

Pleurothallis millei is appreciated for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in greenhouses or as a houseplant.
