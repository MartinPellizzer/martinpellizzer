Pelargonium glutinosum

Pelargonium glutinosum, commonly known as the sticky geranium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves that exude a sticky substance when touched. The plant produces showy, five-petaled flowers in shades of pink, red, and purple, typically blooming in summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.
