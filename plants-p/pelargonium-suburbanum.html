Pelargonium suburbanum

Pelargonium suburbanum, commonly known as the suburban geranium, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Geraniaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, five-petaled flowers. The leaves are typically rounded with a hairy texture, and the plant produces small, red or pink flowers that bloom in summer. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a potted plant.

Pelargonium suburbanum prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.
