Paullinia stipularis f. genuina

Paullinia stipularis f. genuina, commonly known as the Brazilian sarsaparilla, is a rare and endangered plant species native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is a member of the Sarsaparillaceae family and is characterized by its climbing habit and compound leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in racemes, followed by orange-red berries. Its botanical significance lies in its unique morphology and its role in the ecological dynamics of its native habitat.

Due to habitat loss, it is critically endangered and requires conservation efforts to ensure its survival.
