Pinus comonfortii

Pinus comonfortii, commonly known as the Comonfort pine, is a species of evergreen conifer native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its needle-like leaves, which are typically arranged in bundles of five. The tree produces cones that mature over several years, releasing seeds that are dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
