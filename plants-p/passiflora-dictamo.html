Passiflora dictamo

Passiflora dictamo, commonly known as the blue passionflower, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking blue and purple flowers, which are adorned with intricate patterns and tendrils. The plant produces small, dark blue or black berries that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine, soft hairs.

Passiflora dictamo is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for its calming properties.
