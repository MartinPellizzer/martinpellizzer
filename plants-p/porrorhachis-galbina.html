Porrorhachis galbina

Porrorhachis galbina, commonly known as the golden bell vine, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its pendulous, bell-shaped yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to climb or trail.

Porrorhachis galbina is also valued for its use in traditional medicine.
