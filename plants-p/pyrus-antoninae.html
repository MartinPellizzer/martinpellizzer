Pyrus antoninae

Pyrus antoninae, commonly known as the Antonina pear, is a species of flowering plant in the family Rosaceae.

It is native to the eastern Mediterranean region, including parts of Turkey, Syria, and Lebanon. The plant is characterized by its deciduous nature, with heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers in spring. Its fruit, a small pear-like berry, is typically harvested in late summer and is known for its sweet, aromatic flavor.

The species is of interest to botanists and horticulturists due to its ornamental value and potential for cultivation in temperate climates.
