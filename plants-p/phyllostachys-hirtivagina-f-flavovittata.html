Phyllostachys hirtivagina f. flavovittata

Phyllostachys hirtivagina f. flavovittata, commonly known as the golden-banded bamboo, is a variant of the hirtivagina bamboo species.

It is characterized by its distinctive yellow longitudinal stripes on the culms, which set it apart from other members of the Phyllostachys genus. This bamboo is native to China and thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil. It is a clumping bamboo that grows to a moderate height, making it suitable for ornamental gardens.

Its unique coloration makes it a popular choice for landscaping and botanical collections.
