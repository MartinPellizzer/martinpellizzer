Persicaria oreophila f. rubescens

Persicaria oreophila f. rubescens, commonly known as the red-stemmed knotweed, is a botanical variant of the species Persicaria oreophila.

It is characterized by its distinctive reddish stems and leaves that often exhibit a slightly hairy texture. This plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or along stream banks. Its flowers are small and clustered, usually greenish-white in color, and appear in late summer.

The species is noted for its adaptability to various soil types and its role in ecological succession in temperate regions.
