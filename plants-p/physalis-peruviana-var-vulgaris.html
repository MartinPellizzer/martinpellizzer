Physalis peruviana var. vulgaris

Physalis peruviana var. vulgaris, commonly known as the common cape gooseberry, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant orange, inflated calyces that enclose the small, edible berries. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with hairy stems and alternate leaves that are ovate to lanceolate in shape. The flowers are yellow with five petals and are followed by the distinctive, lantern-like fruiting structures.

It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible fruits.
