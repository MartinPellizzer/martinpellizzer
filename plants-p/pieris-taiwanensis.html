Pieris taiwanensis

Pieris taiwanensis, commonly known as the Taiwan rhododendron, is a species of evergreen shrub native to Taiwan.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense foliage and showy, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and prefers shady, moist environments. Its leaves are leathery and dark green, while its flowers range in color from white to pink, blooming in late spring.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental qualities and ability to thrive in woodland gardens.
