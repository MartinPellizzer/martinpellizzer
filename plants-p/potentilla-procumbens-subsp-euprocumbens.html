Potentilla procumbens subsp. euprocumbens

Potentilla procumbens subsp.

euprocumbens, commonly known as creeping cinquefoil, is a low-growing perennial shrub native to North America. It typically features slender, spreading stems that root at the nodes, forming dense mats.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers with five petals, blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a serrated edge, often covered in fine hairs.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands, meadows, and rocky areas.
