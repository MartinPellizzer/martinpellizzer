Pyrus communis f. variegata

Pyrus communis f. variegata, commonly known as the variegated common pear, is a cultivar of the common pear tree characterized by its striking foliage.

Its leaves display a mix of green, white, and yellow variegation, making it a visually appealing ornamental plant. The plant typically produces small, edible fruits similar to those of the common pear, though they are often less flavorful. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil and full sun for optimal growth.

Due to its unique appearance, it is often cultivated in gardens and as a decorative tree.
