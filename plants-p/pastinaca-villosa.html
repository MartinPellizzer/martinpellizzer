Pastinaca villosa

Pastinaca villosa, commonly known as the hairy burdock, is a perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, hollow stems and large, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, reddish-brown flowers that are often surrounded by bracts. Its roots are thick and fleshy, and they have been historically used in traditional medicine.

Pastinaca villosa is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental qualities and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
