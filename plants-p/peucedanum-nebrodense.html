Peucedanum nebrodense

Peucedanum nebrodense, commonly known as the Nebrodan cowparsley, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of the Balkans.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, characterized by its hollow, aromatic stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, clustered white or yellowish flowers during the summer months. Its seeds are enclosed in a bract-like structure and are often dispersed by wind.
