Piper candelarium

Piper candelarium, commonly known as the candlewood or sacred pepper, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, clustered white flowers. Its fruit, a small, fleshy berry, contains numerous seeds.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has cultural significance in traditional practices.
