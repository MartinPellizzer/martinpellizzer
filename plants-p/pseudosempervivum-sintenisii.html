Pseudosempervivum sintenisii

Pseudosempervivum sintenisii, commonly known as the Alpine stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to the high-altitude regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Crassulaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, rosette-forming leaves that store water, aiding in its survival in arid environments. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, or purple during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Due to its ornamental value and hardiness, it is often cultivated in gardens with low maintenance requirements.
