Phaius × amabilis

Phaius × amabilis, commonly known as the hybrid orchid, is a fascinating plant resulting from the cross between Phaius tuberifera and Phaius inflata.

It is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia, characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically bloom in late summer. The plant features strap-like leaves and a pseudobulb structure, which helps it store water and nutrients. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange with a contrasting purple or red lip, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

This hybrid is valued for its resilience and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
