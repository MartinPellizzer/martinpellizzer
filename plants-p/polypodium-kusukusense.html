Polypodium kusukusense

Polypodium kusukusense, commonly known as the Kusukusense fern, is a species of polypody found in the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped fronds that are typically less than 30 centimeters in length. The plant thrives in shaded, humid environments, often growing on tree trunks or rocky surfaces. Its reproductive structures, known as sori, are located on the underside of the fronds and are covered by a protective indusium.

This fern is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
