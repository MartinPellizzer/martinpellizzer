Piper criniovarium

Piper criniovarium, commonly known as the yellow pipevine, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Piperaceae, which includes over 2,000 species of flowering plants. The plant is characterized by its striking yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a rough texture, while its stems are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant is also known for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine by indigenous communities.
