Plumeria lancifolia

Plumeria lancifolia, commonly known as the sickle-leaf plumeria, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and showy, fragrant flowers that come in shades of white, yellow, and pink. The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is commonly used in traditional ceremonies. It thrives in warm, tropical climates and requires well-draining soil.

The flowers are typically produced during the dry season and are pollinated by insects.
