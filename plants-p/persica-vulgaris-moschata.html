Persica vulgaris moschata

Persica vulgaris moschata, commonly known as the common persicaria, is a flowering plant in the family Polygonaceae.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and lance-shaped leaves with a prominent midrib. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers that are often adorned with reddish bracts. It is native to Europe and parts of Asia and thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

Persica vulgaris moschata is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and flowering habit.
