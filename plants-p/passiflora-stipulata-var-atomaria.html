Passiflora stipulata var. atomaria

Passiflora stipulata var. atomaria, commonly known as the small-leaved passionflower, is a climbing vine native to parts of South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, palmate leaves and showy, fragrant flowers that display a complex array of colors and structures. The flowers typically feature five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments, which are distinctive features of the Passifloraceae family. This variety is noted for its smaller leaf size compared to other Passiflora species, giving it a more refined appearance.

It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its unique floral display and adaptability to various growing conditions.
