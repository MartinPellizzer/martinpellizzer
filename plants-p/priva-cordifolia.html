Priva cordifolia

Priva cordifolia, commonly known as the heartleaf privet, is a species of flowering plant in the Oleaceae family.

It is characterized by its opposite, ovate to cordate leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring. These flowers are followed by small, spherical berries that are usually yellow or orange in color.

Priva cordifolia is native to parts of Asia and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to various soil conditions.
