Pseudosasa oiwakensis

Pseudosasa oiwakensis, commonly known as the Oshima bamboo, is a species of grass native to Japan.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its slender, erect culms that often form dense clumps. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream sides. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, with a bluish-green hue.

Pseudosasa oiwakensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
