Polypodium emersonii var. samoense

Polypodium emersonii var. samoense, commonly known as the Samoan huperzia, is a fern native to the Pacific Islands, particularly Samoa.

It is a terrestrial epiphytic fern that thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant has a distinctive, compact rosette of lanceolate, serrated leaves that are typically bright green. Its fronds are dimorphic, with fertile and sterile forms differing in size and shape.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical gardens.
