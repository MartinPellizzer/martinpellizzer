Pyretrum

Pyretrum, commonly known as chrysanthemum, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is native to Asia and is widely cultivated for its ornamental value and insecticidal properties. The plant produces small, daisy-like flowers and is particularly noted for containing pyrethrins, which are natural compounds used in pest control. Pyretrum thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for gardens and agricultural settings.

Its versatility and ecological benefits have made it an important plant in both horticulture and organic farming.
