Pelargonium quercetorum

Pelargonium quercetorum, commonly known as the oak-leaved geranium, is a species of flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its broad, lobed leaves that resemble the shape of oak leaves, giving it its common name. The plant produces clusters of small, five-petaled flowers in shades of pink, red, and white, depending on the cultivar. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardeners in temperate climates.

Pelargonium quercetorum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
