Puya macbridei subsp. macbridei

Puya macbridei subsp.

macbridei, commonly known as the MacBride's torchflower, is a striking member of the Bromeliaceae family native to the Andean regions of South America. This perennial plant is characterized by its rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves that can grow up to several meters in height.

It produces tall, inflorescences bearing vibrant red or orange flowers, which bloom in a dramatic display during the dry season. The plant is adapted to high-altitude environments, thriving in cool, sunny conditions with well-drained soil.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is often cultivated in specialized gardens and botanical collections.
