Piper tuberculatum var. minus

Piper tuberculatum var. minus, commonly known as the lesser warty pepper plant, is a species native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is a climbing vine characterized by its small, ovate leaves with prominent tuberculate (wart-like) structures on the underside. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by elliptical, dark blue to black berries. It is valued in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties.

Botanically, it belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species.
