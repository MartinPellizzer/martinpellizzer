Polygonum laxiflorum

Polygonum laxiflorum, commonly known as the looseflower knotweed, is a flowering plant native to Asia, particularly found in regions of China, Japan, and Korea.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, which includes other familiar plants like buckwheat and sorrel. The plant is characterized by its upright growth habit, with smooth, reddish stems and heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its small, clustered flowers are typically pink or red, blooming in late summer to early autumn.

Polygonum laxiflorum is often grown in gardens for its ornamental value and can thrive in a variety of soil types as long as it has good drainage.
