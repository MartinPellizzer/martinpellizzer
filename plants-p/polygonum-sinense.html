Polygonum sinense

Polygonum sinense, commonly known as Chinese knotweed, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and heart-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually pink or red in color, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Polygonum sinense is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
