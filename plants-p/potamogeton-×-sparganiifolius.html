Potamogeton × sparganiifolius

Potamogeton × sparganiifolius, commonly known as the broadleaf pondweed, is a hybrid aquatic plant that exhibits characteristics of both its parent species, Potamogeton perfoliatus and Potamogeton sparganiifolius.

It is typically found in freshwater habitats such as ponds, lakes, and slow-moving rivers. The plant has long, narrow leaves that are often arranged in whorls, and it produces small, greenish flowers that are clustered in spike-like inflorescences. It plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.

Its ability to thrive in a range of water conditions makes it a common and adaptable species in many freshwater environments.
