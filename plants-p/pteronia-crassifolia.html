Pteronia crassifolia

Pteronia crassifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved pteronia, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its sturdy, upright stems and fleshy, broad leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, or red, typically blooming in winter. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and long-lasting blooms.

Pteronia crassifolia prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal areas.
