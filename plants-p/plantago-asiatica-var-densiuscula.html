Plantago asiatica var. densiuscula

Plantago asiatica var. densiuscula, commonly known as the dense-leaved plantain, is a variant of the common plantain, characterized by its compact, densely packed leaves.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant native to East Asia, including regions of China, Japan, and Korea. The plant typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and along riverbanks. Its leaves are broad, heart-shaped, and covered with a fine layer of silky hairs, while its flowers are small and clustered in dense spikes.

This variety is valued for its medicinal properties and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
