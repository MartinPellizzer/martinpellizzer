Plantago velutina

Plantago velutina, commonly known as velvet plantain, is a flowering plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its broad, velvety leaves that are covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in a spike, which are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and disturbed areas.

Velvet plantain is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
