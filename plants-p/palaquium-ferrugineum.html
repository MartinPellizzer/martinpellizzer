Palaquium ferrugineum

Palaquium ferrugineum, commonly known as the rusty palaquium, is a small evergreen tree native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that are often clustered at the ends of branches. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by edible, fleshy fruits that are yellow when ripe. Its bark is thick and rugged, with a reddish-brown color that gives it its common name.

Palaquium ferrugineum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical gardens.
