Pilea taitoensis

Pilea taitoensis, commonly known as the Taitoensis Begonia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Begoniaceae and is characterized by its vibrant, showy bracts that surround its small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub with dense foliage, featuring heart-shaped leaves that are often variegated in color. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Pilea taitoensis is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated as a houseplant.
