Paspalum candidum

Paspalum candidum, commonly known as white paspalum, is a perennial grass native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, white to pale blue flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments, such as marshes and wetlands. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often with a bluish-green hue.

Paspalum candidum is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in aquatic habitats.
