Piper kantetulense

Piper kantetulense, commonly known as the Kantetulense pepper, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its trailing growth habit and small, heart-shaped leaves. It produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in spikes.

The fruit is a small, round berry that turns red when mature, often used in local cuisines for its mild spicy flavor.
