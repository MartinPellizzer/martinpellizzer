Ponera rosea

Ponera rosea, commonly known as the pink ponytail plant, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking pink or red flowers that bloom in clusters, giving it a unique and eye-catching appearance. The plant has long, trailing stems that can be trained to grow in hanging baskets or along supports. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a soft, velvety texture.

Ponera rosea thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.
