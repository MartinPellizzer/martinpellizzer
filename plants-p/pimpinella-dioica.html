Pimpinella dioica

Pimpinella dioica, commonly known as bishop's weed, is a biennial or annual herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features finely divided, fern-like leaves that are often aromatic and slightly hairy. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in umbels, typical of the Apiaceae family. Its seeds are small, ovoid, and have a distinctive ridged surface.

Pimpinella dioica is often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides, thriving in temperate climates.
