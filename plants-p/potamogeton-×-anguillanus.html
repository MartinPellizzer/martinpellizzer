Potamogeton × anguillanus

Potamogeton × anguillanus, commonly known as the hybrid water plant, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Potamogeton crispus and Potamogeton anguillarius.

It is an aquatic plant characterized by its long, flexible stems that often grow in shallow waters. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are typically arranged in spike-like clusters. Its leaves are narrow and ribbon-like, often with a slightly wavy edge.

Potamogeton × anguillanus is commonly found in freshwater habitats such as ponds, lakes, and slow-moving streams.
