Passiflora vescoi

Passiflora vescoi, commonly known as the Mexican passionflower, is a climbing vine native to Mexico and Central America.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that feature five sepals, five petals, and a unique crown of filaments around the reproductive structures. The plant produces edible, orange-yellow berries that are rich in vitamin C. Its leaves are palmately lobed and often have a hairy texture.

Passiflora vescoi is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
