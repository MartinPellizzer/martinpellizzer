Pogostemon velatus

Pogostemon velatus, commonly known as the velvet plant, is a tropical perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves that are covered in a fine, velvety texture, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically blue or purple in color, arranged in dense clusters. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest undergrowth.

Pogostemon velatus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
