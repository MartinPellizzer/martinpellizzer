Pilosella aurantiaca subsp. carpathicola

Pilosella aurantiaca subsp.

carpathicola, commonly known as the Carpathian goldenweed, is a flowering plant native to the Carpathian Mountains. It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and herbaceous growth habit.

The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes. Its bright yellow flowers bloom in the summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

It is known for its delicate, hairy leaves and is often found in alpine and subalpine regions.
