Paspalum arsenei

Paspalum arsenei, commonly known as the Australian hairgrass, is a perennial grass species native to Australia.

It is characterized by its fine, wiry foliage and dense growth habit, often forming low, compact tufts. The plant typically thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas and wetlands. Its flowering spikes are slender and spike-like, bearing small, pale green to brownish florets.

Paspalum arsenei is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in riparian zones.
