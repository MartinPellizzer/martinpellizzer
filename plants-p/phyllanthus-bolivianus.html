Phyllanthus bolivianus

Phyllanthus bolivianus, commonly known as the Bolivian spurge, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Bolivia and neighboring regions.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family, which is known for its diverse and often toxic species. The plant features small, green leaves with a slightly waxy texture and produces tiny, inconspicuous flowers. It is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments.

The plant contains various bioactive compounds, making it of interest in ethnobotanical and pharmacological studies.
