Panicum polymorphum

Panicum polymorphum, commonly known as the variable panicgrass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and loose, feather-like panicles that bear small, spikelet-based flowers. The plant typically grows in open habitats such as grasslands, roadsides, and disturbed soils. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a smooth, waxy surface that helps reduce water loss.

Panicum polymorphum is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in various soil conditions.
