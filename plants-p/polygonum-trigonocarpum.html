Polygonum trigonocarpum

Polygonum trigonocarpum, commonly known as the three-angled burdock, is a flowering plant native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, which includes species known for their distinctive leaf shapes and edible roots. The plant features triangular-shaped fruits that are often used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped with a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

Polygonum trigonocarpum is valued for its medicinal properties and is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental appeal.
