Poa bolanderi

Poa bolanderi, commonly known as Bolander's bluegrass, is a perennial grass native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its fine, blue-green foliage and tufted growth habit. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats such as slopes and cliffs. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of spikelets, which are often tinged with purple.

Poa bolanderi is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature.
