Phoradendron brevifolium

Phoradendron brevifolium, commonly known as the mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches itself to the branches of host trees, primarily in the genus *Ficus*.

It lacks true roots and absorbs water and nutrients through its leaves, which are modified to function as both photosynthetic organs and absorptive structures. The plant produces small, white or pale green berries that are often consumed by birds, which then disperse the seeds to new host trees. Its life cycle involves a complex relationship with its host, contributing to the ecological dynamics of the forest canopy.

This species is also notable for its role in traditional medicine and its association with holiday symbolism.
