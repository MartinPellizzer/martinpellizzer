Polypodium myriotrichum

Polypodium myriotrichum, commonly known as the hairy shield fern, is a species of fern native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its distinctive fronds that are covered with fine, hairy structures, giving it a unique appearance. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky crevices. Its reproductive structures, known as sori, are located on the undersides of its fronds.

Polypodium myriotrichum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
