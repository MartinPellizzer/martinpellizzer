Pseudodracontium fallax

Pseudodracontium fallax, commonly known as the false dragon tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, elongated leaves that resemble those of the dragon tree, though it belongs to a different genus. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by round, fleshy fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental foliage.

Due to its unique appearance and growth habits, it is a popular choice for tropical gardens and indoor collections.
