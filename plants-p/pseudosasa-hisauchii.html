Pseudosasa hisauchii

Pseudosasa hisauchii, commonly known as the Japanese bamboo or golden bamboo, is a member of the grass family (Poaceae) and is native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often a striking golden or yellow-green color. This plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to form dense clumps, making it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Pseudosasa hisauchii is also valued for its use in traditional Japanese gardens and as a source of sustainable materials.
