Pandanus muarensis

Pandanus muarensis, commonly known as the Muarensis pandanus, is a species of flowering plant in the pandan family.

It is native to the tropical regions of the Pacific, including parts of Fiji and Tonga. The plant is characterized by its large, sword-shaped leaves that are often used in traditional crafts and as thatching material. Its inflorescence consists of a spadix surrounded by a leafy bract, typical of the genus.

The plant thrives in coastal and lowland areas, often growing in sandy or clay soils.
