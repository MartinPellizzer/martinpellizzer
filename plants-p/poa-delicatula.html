Poa delicatula

Poa delicatula, commonly known as the slender meadow grass, is a low-growing perennial grass species native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It typically forms dense, mat-like growths and is characterized by its slender, slightly hairy stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils and is often found in meadows, wetlands, and along stream banks.

Due to its delicate appearance and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value.
