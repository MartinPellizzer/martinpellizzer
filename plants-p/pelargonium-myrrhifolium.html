Pelargonium myrrhifolium

Pelargonium myrrhifolium, commonly known as the myrrh-leaved geranium, is a species of flowering plant in the family Geraniaceae.

It is native to South Africa and is characterized by its aromatic, dark green, myrrh-scented leaves. The plant produces small, pale pink to white flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and summer seasons. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and fragrant properties.

Pelargonium myrrhifolium prefers well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it suitable for both indoor and outdoor gardening.
