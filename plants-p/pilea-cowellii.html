Pilea cowellii

Pilea cowellii, commonly known as the Chinese money plant, is a flowering plant native to China and Taiwan.

It is characterized by its rosette-forming leaves that are round and thick, resembling small coins. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers that appear in clusters during the growing season. It thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Pilea cowellii is relatively low maintenance and can be propagated easily through stem cuttings.
