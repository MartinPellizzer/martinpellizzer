Pochota coriacea

Pochota coriacea, commonly known as the rough pochote, is a species of legume native to Mexico and Central America.

It is characterized by its woody, branched stems and compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces yellowish-white flowers that are clustered in racemes, followed by pod-like fruits containing several seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in dry forests and shrublands.

Pochota coriacea is notable for its hardy nature and ability to withstand drought conditions.
