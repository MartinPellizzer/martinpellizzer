Platytaenia depauperata

Platytaenia depauperata, commonly known as the sparse-leaved sunflower, is a herbaceous plant native to arid regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves and yellow composite flowers. The plant is adapted to survive in dry conditions, with a deep root system that helps it access groundwater. Its small stature and drought tolerance make it a resilient species in its native habitat.

However, it is relatively rare and faces threats from habitat loss and climate change.
