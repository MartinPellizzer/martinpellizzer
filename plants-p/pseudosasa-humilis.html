Pseudosasa humilis

Pseudosasa humilis, commonly known as the Japanese bamboo or dwarf bamboo, is a member of the grass family (Poaceae).

It is a clump-forming plant that produces slender, erect culms with a smooth, light green surface. The leaves are narrow, linear, and have a glossy appearance, often with a slightly zigzag pattern along the edges. It thrives in shady, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.

This plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and is used in traditional Japanese gardens.
