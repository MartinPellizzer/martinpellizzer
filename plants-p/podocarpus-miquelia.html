Podocarpus miquelia

Podocarpus miquelia, commonly known as the Miquel's podocarp, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Podocarpaceae family and is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and small, clustered cones. The tree typically grows in tropical and subtropical climates, often in mountainous regions with high humidity. Its leaves are leathery and arranged spirally along the stems, contributing to its ornamental value.

Podocarpus miquelia is also valued for its hard, durable wood, which has been historically used in construction and furniture-making.
