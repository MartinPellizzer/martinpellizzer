Pouteria malaccensis

Pouteria malaccensis, commonly known as the Malacca pouteria, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and elliptical leaves with a glossy surface. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by large, round fruits that are edible and have a sweet, creamy pulp.

Its fruit is often used in local cuisines and traditional medicine, highlighting its ecological and cultural significance.
