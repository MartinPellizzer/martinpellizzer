Piper temiscoense

Piper temiscoense, commonly known as the Temiscoua pepper, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of Central America, particularly in countries like Nicaragua and Honduras. The plant is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and small, heart-shaped leaves. Its fruits are peppery and are used in traditional local cuisines.

Botanically, it is closely related to other pepper species and exhibits typical features of the Piper genus.
