Pera obtusifolia

Pera obtusifolia, commonly known as the blunt-leaved pera, is a flowering plant belonging to the Apiaceae family.

It is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves that are typically smooth and have a blunt or rounded tip. The plant produces small, clustered white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It is native to parts of Europe and is often found in moist meadows and woodland edges.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
