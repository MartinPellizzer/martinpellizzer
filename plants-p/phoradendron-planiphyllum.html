Phoradendron planiphyllum

Phoradendron planiphyllum, commonly known as the flat-leaf mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on various host trees in the neotropical region.

It possesses small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically flat and glossy, adapted for photosynthesis while also relying on its host for water and nutrients. The plant produces small, white or pale green flowers that are often clustered together, followed by small, fleshy berries that serve as a food source for birds and other animals. Its seeds are dispersed by birds that eat the berries, allowing the plant to propagate and colonize new host trees.

This species plays an important ecological role in its habitat by contributing to the food web and influencing the health of its host trees.
