Piptochaetium purpuratum

Piptochaetium purpuratum, commonly known as purple needlegrass, is a perennial grass native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and long, narrow leaves with a distinctive purple hue on the underside. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands.

Piptochaetium purpuratum is an important component of native plant communities and provides habitat for various wildlife species.
