Piper uncatum

Piper uncatum, commonly known as the cat's claw vine, is a climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its tendrils that help it climb toward sunlight. The plant has small, heart-shaped leaves and produces greenish-white flowers that develop into red, fleshy berries. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in humid forests and along riverbanks.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is cultivated in some regions for both horticultural and traditional uses.
