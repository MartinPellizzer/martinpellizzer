Penstemon teucrioides

Penstemon teucrioides, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a perennial herb with erect, branching stems that often reach heights of 1 to 3 feet. The plant produces clusters of small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, giving them a slightly fuzzy texture.

Penstemon teucrioides thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping and native plant gardens.
