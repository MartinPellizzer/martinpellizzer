Potentilla lozani

Potentilla lozani, commonly known as the Lozani cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its woody stems and small, clustered leaves. The plant produces bright yellow flowers in the summer, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It prefers well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rocky or alpine environments.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
