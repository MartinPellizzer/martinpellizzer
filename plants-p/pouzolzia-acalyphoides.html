Pouzolzia acalyphoides

Pouzolzia acalyphoides, commonly known as the rough-leaved pouzolzia, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in moist, shaded environments. The plant has alternate, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are often hairy and have a rough texture. Its small, greenish-white flowers are clustered in panicles and appear in the spring.

The plant is known for its ability to thrive in humid conditions and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
