Polypodium limulum

Polypodium limulum, commonly known as the rock fern, is a species of fern that belongs to the family Polypodiaceae.

It is a perennial plant characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit and its ability to thrive in shaded, moist environments. The fronds of Polypodium limulum are typically leathery and have a distinctive, segmented appearance. It is often found growing on rocks or in crevices, where it forms dense mats.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and aesthetic qualities.
