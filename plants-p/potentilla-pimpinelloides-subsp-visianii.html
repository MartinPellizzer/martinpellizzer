Potentilla pimpinelloides subsp. visianii

Potentilla pimpinelloides subsp.

visianii, commonly known as the alpine potentilla, is a low-growing shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It typically features small, rounded leaves that are densely hairy, providing it with a distinctive appearance.

The plant produces clusters of yellow flowers in early summer, which are attractive to pollinators. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in alpine gardens.
