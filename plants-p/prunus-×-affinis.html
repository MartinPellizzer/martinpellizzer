Prunus × affinis

Prunus × affinis, commonly known as the Japanese cherry or Japanese flowering cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its elegant, showy flowers that bloom in early spring, often in shades of pink and white. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape, with a smooth margin. It produces small, hard fruits called cherries, which are typically red or black when mature.

Prunus × affinis is widely cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its aesthetic appeal and seasonal blooming.
