Pithecellobium muricatum

Pithecellobium muricatum, commonly known as the velvet tamarind, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of yellow flowers that bloom throughout the growing season. The plant produces edible, pod-like fruits that are often used in local cuisines. The fruit's surface is covered in small, raised bumps, giving it a textured appearance.

It is also valued for its ornamental qualities and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types.
