Panicum xanthophysum

Panicum xanthophysum, commonly known as the golden panicgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its erect, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically a vibrant green. The plant produces fluffy, golden-brown seed heads that are highly visible during the fall season. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping.

Its drought tolerance and ability to spread through rhizomes contribute to its adaptability in various environmental conditions.
