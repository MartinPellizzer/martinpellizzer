Prunus × donarium f. ohsibayama

Prunus × donarium f. ohsibayama, commonly known as the Ohsibayama cherry, is a hybrid deciduous shrub or small tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its striking, double-flowered blooms that appear in early spring, typically in shades of pink and white. The plant has elliptical leaves that are dark green on top and lighter in color on the underside. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Its compact size and ornamental value make it suitable for both landscape design and container gardening.
