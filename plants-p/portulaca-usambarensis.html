Portulaca usambarensis

Portulaca usambarensis, commonly known as the African moss rose, is a flowering plant native to eastern Africa.

It is a succulent species that thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils. The plant features fleshy, paddle-shaped leaves and produces vibrant, star-shaped flowers in shades of red, yellow, and orange. It is known for its ability to store water in its thick stems and leaves, making it drought-tolerant.

The species is valued both for ornamental purposes and for its resilience in challenging climates.
