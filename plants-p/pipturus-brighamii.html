Pipturus brighamii

Pipturus brighamii, commonly known as Brigham's huckleberry, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes many acid-loving plants such as blueberries and heathers. The plant produces small, edible berries that are typically dark blue to black in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.
