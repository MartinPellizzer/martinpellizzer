Primula hirsuta var. ciliata

Primula hirsuta var. ciliata, commonly known as the hairy primrose, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia.

It features hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered with fine, soft pubescence. The plant produces clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink, white, or yellow, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in woodland or shaded garden settings.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and ecological restoration projects.
