Polygonum vacciniifolium var. flagelliforme

Polygonum vacciniifolium var. flagelliforme, commonly known as the silverleaf knotweed, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of China.

It is characterized by its slender, creeping stems that can form dense mats, and its alternate, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine silvery down. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories or along stream banks.

Due to its rapid growth and invasive potential, it is sometimes considered a weed in certain ecological contexts.
