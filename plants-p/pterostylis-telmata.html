Pterostylis telmata

Pterostylis telmata, commonly known as the purple-tongue orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to eastern Australia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as rainforests and understoreys. The plant features a slender, leafless stem with a single, showy, purple-flushed flower. Its unique floral structure includes a labellum that resembles a tongue, aiding in pollination by specific insects.

The orchid is part of the Orchidaceae family and is noted for its ecological significance in native ecosystems.
