Passiflora mixta var. eriantha

Passiflora mixta var. eriantha, commonly known as the silky passionflower, is a climbing vine native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that feature intricate structures including a crown of filaments and a central cluster of stamens. The plant produces edible berries that are typically yellow or orange when ripe, containing a sweet, pulpy flesh. Its leaves are large, heart-shaped, and often have a velvety texture.

This species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated as a decorative plant in warm climates.
