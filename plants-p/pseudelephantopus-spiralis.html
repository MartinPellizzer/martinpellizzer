Pseudelephantopus spiralis

Pseudelephantopus spiralis, commonly known as spiral leek, is a striking plant native to the Andean region of South America.

It is characterized by its spiral arrangement of leaves, which gives it a unique and ornamental appearance. The plant typically grows in rocky or mountainous habitats, thriving in cool, humid conditions. Its leaves are long, narrow, and often have a bluish or silver hue due to a dense covering of fine hairs.

While not commonly cultivated for food, it is occasionally grown as an ornamental plant in gardens and greenhouses.
