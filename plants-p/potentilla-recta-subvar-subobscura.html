Potentilla recta subvar. subobscura

Potentilla recta subvar. subobscura, commonly known as the creeping cinquefoil, is a perennial flowering plant native to arctic and alpine regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its low-growing habit, with prostrate or ascending stems that often form dense mats. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers with five petals, typically blooming in early summer. Its leaves are compound, consisting of five oval-shaped leaflets, and are covered in fine hairs.

It thrives in well-drained, rocky or sandy soils and is often found in tundra and mountainous environments.
