Pseudocaryophyllus

Pseudocaryophyllus, commonly known as the false caryophyllus, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Caryophyllaceae.

These plants are typically herbaceous and have simple, alternate leaves that are often linear or ovate in shape. They produce small, clustered flowers with five petals, usually in shades of white, pink, or purple. The genus is characterized by its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its preference for temperate climates.

Pseudocaryophyllus species are often found in meadows, woodlands, and rocky habitats.
