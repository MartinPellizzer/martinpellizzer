Piper conditum var. conditum

Piper conditum var. conditum, commonly known as black pepper, is a flowering plant native to the Malabar Coast of India.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its small, dark berries that are harvested and dried to produce the spice. The plant typically grows as a vine, with opposite, ovate leaves and yellowish-green flowers. Its fruit, which matures to a dark red or black color, contains the aromatic seeds used in culinary and medicinal applications.

The plant's botanical features make it a significant species in both agriculture and traditional medicine.
