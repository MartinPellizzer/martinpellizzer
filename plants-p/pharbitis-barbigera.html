Pharbitis barbigera

Pharbitis barbigera, commonly known as the hairy morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its distinctive hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. It thrives in warm climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Pharbitis barbigera is also known for its rapid growth and climbing habit, making it a popular choice for trellises and garden structures.
