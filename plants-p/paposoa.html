Paposoa

Paposoa, commonly known as the sacred lotus of the Andes, is a striking plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It belongs to the Nelumbonaceae family and is characterized by its large, showy white flowers that bloom in the early morning. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in mountainous regions with rich, well-drained soil. Its leaves are broad and heart-shaped, often floating on water surfaces, while its stems are thick and fibrous.

The paposoa holds cultural and spiritual significance in Andean traditions, symbolizing purity and enlightenment.
