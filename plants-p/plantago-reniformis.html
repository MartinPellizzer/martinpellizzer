Plantago reniformis

Plantago reniformis, commonly known as the broadleaf plantain, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It features heart-shaped, broad leaves that are typically covered in a dense layer of fine, silky hairs. The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in a spike, which are clustered in dense clusters called inflorescences. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and disturbed areas.

Botanically, it belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is known for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal remedies.
