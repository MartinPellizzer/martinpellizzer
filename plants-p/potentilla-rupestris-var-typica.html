Potentilla rupestris var. typica

Potentilla rupestris var. typica, commonly known as the rock cinquefoil, is a low-growing perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has wiry, upright stems and trifoliate leaves with serrated edges, adapted to thrive in rocky and well-drained soils. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it a hardy choice for rock gardens and alpine landscapes.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental value make it a popular plant among gardeners seeking native, resilient species.
