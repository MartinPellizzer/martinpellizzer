Pygmaeothamnus chamaedendrum

Pygmaeothamnus chamaedendrum, commonly known as the coastal banksia, is a shrub native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and its distinctive yellow to orange flowers that bloom in spring. The plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is typically found in coastal heathlands. Its leaves are narrow and leathery, adapted to withstand coastal conditions such as salt spray and strong winds.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local wildlife.
