Peperomia subvillicaulis

Peperomia subvillicaulis, commonly known as the silver peperomia, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It features glossy, waxy leaves that are typically dark green with a silvery or metallic sheen on the underside. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters, which are usually greenish-white in color. It thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Due to its slow growth and attractive foliage, it is often used as a decorative plant in both residential and commercial settings.
