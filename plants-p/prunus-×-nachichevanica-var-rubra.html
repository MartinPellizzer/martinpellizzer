Prunus × nachichevanica var. rubra

Prunus × nachichevanica var. rubra, commonly known as the crimson cherry, is a hybrid species within the Prunus genus, characterized by its vibrant red flowers.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Caucasus region, where it thrives in temperate climates. The plant features simple, ovate leaves with serrated edges and produces sweet, edible fruits in the summer. Its ornamental value is highly appreciated in gardens due to its showy blossoms and compact growth habit.

It requires well-drained soil and full sun to flourish.
