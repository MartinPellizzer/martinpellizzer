Papaver heterandrum

Papaver heterandrum, commonly known as the Sierra lily, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the family Papaveraceae and is characterized by its showy, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in rocky or open habitats, often at high elevations. Its leaves are lanceolate and arranged in a basal rosette, while its stems are erect and branching.

The fruit is a capsule that splits open to release seeds, contributing to its reproductive strategy in its native environment.
