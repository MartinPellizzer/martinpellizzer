Penstemon paysonii

Penstemon paysonii, commonly known as the Payson beardtongue, is a perennial plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces striking, tubular flowers that range in color from violet to blue. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments, making it drought-tolerant. Its leaves are narrow and gray-green, providing a striking contrast to its vibrant blooms.

Penstemon paysonii is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
