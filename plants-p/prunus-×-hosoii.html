Prunus × hosoii

Prunus × hosoii, commonly known as the Hosoii cherry, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Prunus lusitanica and Prunus serrulata.

It is native to Japan and is valued for its ornamental qualities, particularly its showy spring blossoms. The plant typically grows as a small tree or shrub, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are dark green and oval-shaped, while the flowers are pink to white and appear in clusters.

Prunus × hosoii is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal and is relatively low maintenance once established.
