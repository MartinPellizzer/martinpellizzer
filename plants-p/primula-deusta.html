Primula deusta

Primula deusta, commonly known as the Spanish primrose, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It features a low-growing rosette of basal leaves and produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade. It is often found in woodland areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.

The flowers bloom in early spring, adding a splash of color to the landscape during the cooler months.
