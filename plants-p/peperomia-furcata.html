Peperomia furcata

Peperomia furcata, commonly known as the Shingleplant, is a compact, evergreen perennial native to tropical regions of South America.

It features dense, fleshy, ovate leaves that are typically dark green with a waxy texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters, often in shades of green or red, which are followed by distinctive, finger-like fruits. It is well-suited for indoor cultivation due to its low light requirements and drought tolerance.

Peperomia furcata is prized for its ornamental foliage and ease of care, making it a popular choice for foliage-focused plant collections.
