Picea jezoensis

Picea jezoensis, commonly known as the Korean fir, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to the Korean Peninsula.

It is characterized by its conical shape, dark green needle-like leaves, and distinctive, flattened cones. The tree typically grows in temperate climates and is often found in forested areas with well-drained soil. Its bark is thick and furrowed, providing protection against harsh weather conditions.

Picea jezoensis plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, supporting various wildlife species and contributing to forest biodiversity.
