Parodia mendeziana

Parodia mendeziana, commonly known as the Mendezian Cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of southern Mexico.

It is a low-growing, solitary cactus with a globular shape and a ribbed structure that helps it store water. Its stems are typically green with a waxy coating, and it produces yellow flowers during the summer months. The plant is well adapted to harsh desert conditions, with spines that provide protection from herbivores and reduce water loss.

It is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its hardy nature and ornamental appeal.
