Plectronia rhamnifolia

Plectronia rhamnifolia, commonly known as the rock senna, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid regions. It has small, capsule-like fruits that contain seeds dispersed by wind.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes used in landscaping for erosion control.
