Poecilostachys mainborondroensis

Poecilostachys mainborondroensis, commonly known as the Madagascar grass, is a species of grass native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its inflorescence consists of a panicle with small, spikelet-like flowers.

Due to its limited distribution and unique morphology, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
