Pteris tahuataensis

Pteris tahuataensis, commonly known as the Tahuata fern, is a species of water fern native to the island of Tahuata in French Polynesia.

It belongs to the family Pteridaceae and is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that are typically green and slightly serrated. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found near streams or in humid forest understories. Its spores are dispersed through the wind, contributing to its reproductive strategy in its natural habitat.

The Tahuata fern is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and limited distribution.
