Primula ochracea

Primula ochracea, commonly known as the yellow primrose, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. Its leaves are oval-shaped with a slightly hairy surface, and the flowers are usually clustered in racemes.

Primula ochracea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and delicate appearance.
