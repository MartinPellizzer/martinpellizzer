Pilocarpus breviracemosus

Pilocarpus breviracemosus, commonly known as the short-branch pilocarpus, is a shrub native to the Andes region of South America.

It belongs to the Rubiaceae family and is characterized by its dense, aromatic foliage and small, white flowers. The plant is notable for containing the alkaloid pilocarpine, which has been used in traditional medicine for its stimulating effects. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in cloud forests.

Due to its medicinal properties, it is of interest to both botanists and pharmacologists.
