Pseudohemipilia Keayi

Keay's pseudohemipilia, scientifically known as Pseudohemipilia keayi, is a rare and fascinating orchid species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia. This delicate plant belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique floral structure and striking coloration. Despite its beauty, it remains poorly understood due to its limited distribution and elusive habitat. Found primarily in cloud forests, it thrives in cool, humid environments with specific soil conditions. Conservation efforts are crucial to protect this species from threats such as habitat loss and overcollection.
