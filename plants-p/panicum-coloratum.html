Panicum coloratum

Panicum coloratum, commonly known as the purple lovegrass, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and its striking purple to pinkish flower heads that appear in late summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, wind-pollinated flowers arranged in spikelets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands and disturbed sites.

Panicum coloratum is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in stabilizing soil in arid environments.
