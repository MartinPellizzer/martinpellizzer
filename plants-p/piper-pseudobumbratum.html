Piper pseudobumbratum

Piper pseudobumbratum, commonly known as the Bornean black pepper, is a climbing vine native to the rainforests of Borneo.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by dark purple to black berries. Its leaves are typically ovate with a glossy surface and are used in traditional medicine.

The fruit is often harvested for its aromatic seeds, which are used in culinary and medicinal applications.
