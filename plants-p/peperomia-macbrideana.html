Peperomia macbrideana

Peperomia macbrideana, commonly known as the MacBride's peperomia, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that are often variegated with shades of green, white, and yellow, giving it an ornamental appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters, typically in shades of white or pale purple, which are followed by small, berry-like fruits. It thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Peperomia macbrideana is relatively low-maintenance, requiring minimal watering and tolerating a range of indoor conditions.
