Potentilla Chrysantha Subsp. Thuringiaca

Potentilla chrysantha subsp. thuringiaca is a lesser-known member of the rose family known for its delicate yellow flowers and compact growth habit. Native to central Europe, this subspecies thrives in well-drained soils and sunny environments. Its small, fragrant blooms make it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders. Despite its beauty, it remains underappreciated compared to more common garden plants. This article explores its characteristics, cultivation tips, and ecological significance.
