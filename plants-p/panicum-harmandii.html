Panicum harmandii

Panicum harmandii, commonly known as the harmand's panicgrass, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the grass family, Poaceae, and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, branched stems. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences that are typically green to reddish in color. Its leaves are narrow and linear, with a rough texture, and it thrives in well-drained soils.

Panicum harmandii is often found in open woodlands and disturbed sites, demonstrating adaptability to various environmental conditions.
