Podionapus cespitosus

Podionapus cespitosus, commonly known as the tussock spurge, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its upright growth habit and dense, woolly foliage. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically borne on tall, slender stems. It thrives in dry, sunny environments and is often found in rocky or gravelly soils.

Podionapus cespitosus is known for its ability to tolerate drought and poor soil conditions.
