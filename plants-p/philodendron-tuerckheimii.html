Philodendron tuerckheimii

Philodendron tuerckheimii, commonly known as the Tuerckheim's philodendron, is a tropical climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It features heart-shaped, glossy leaves that are typically a deep green with a waxy texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by round, red berries that are toxic to humans and animals. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires moderate humidity and well-draining soil.

Its unique leaf patterns and ease of propagation make it a popular choice for indoor gardening.
