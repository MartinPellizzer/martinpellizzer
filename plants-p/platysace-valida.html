Platysace valida

Platysace valida, commonly known as the velvetleaf or broadleaf arrowhead, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It features heart-shaped, waxy leaves that are often covered in fine, velvety hairs, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically borne on slender stalks. Its roots are fibrous and can form dense mats, making it a common weed in disturbed soils.

Platysace valida is adapted to wet or moist environments and is often found in marshy areas or along stream banks.
