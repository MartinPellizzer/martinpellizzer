Pistacia palaestina

Pistacia palaestina, commonly known as the Palestinian pistachio, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Anacardiaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that enclose a single seed, which is often used for its edible kernel. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is well adapted to arid and semi-arid climates.

Due to its ecological and historical significance, it is also valued for its ornamental and medicinal properties.
