Pilosella densiflora subsp. spaniopolia

Pilosella densiflora subsp.

spaniopolia, commonly known as the Spanish pilosella, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe. It features a compact rosette of hairy, gray-green leaves that are often covered in fine, white trichomes.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in dense, flat-topped clusters, typical of the Asteraceae family. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils.

Its botanical characteristics make it well adapted to Mediterranean climates with hot, dry summers.
