Pithecellobium turgidum

Pithecellobium turgidum, commonly known as the giant bracken fern, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, fern-like fronds that can grow up to several meters in length. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests. Its reproductive structures include small, inconspicuous flowers that are followed by oval, woody fruits.

The plant has a fibrous root system that helps it anchor in moist, nutrient-rich soils.
