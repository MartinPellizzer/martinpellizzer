Polygala hirsutula

Polygala hirsutula, commonly known as the hairy polygala, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are linear and hairy, giving it its common name.

Polygala hirsutula is often found in fynbos vegetation and is valued for its ornamental appeal.
