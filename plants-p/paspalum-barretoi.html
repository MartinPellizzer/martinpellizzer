Paspalum barretoi

Paspalum barretoi, commonly known as the narrowleaf paspalum, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in wetland areas, marshes, and along the edges of water bodies. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets. It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in both full sun and partial shade.

Paspalum barretoi is valued for its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife species.
