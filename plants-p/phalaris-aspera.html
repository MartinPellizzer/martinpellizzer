Phalaris aspera

Phalaris aspera, commonly known as rigid brome, is a perennial grass species native to Europe, Asia, and Africa.

It typically grows in dry, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and agricultural fields. The plant has slender, erect stems that can reach up to 1 meter in height, and its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets arranged in a panicle, with small, pale green to brown flowers.

Phalaris aspera is adapted to a wide range of environmental conditions, making it a resilient and common weed in many regions.
