Phlomis umbrosa var. australis

Phlomis umbrosa var. australis, commonly known as the southern shadow phlomis, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and lance-shaped leaves that are often grayish-green in color. The plant produces showy, clustered spikes of purple or pink flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and drought tolerance, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.
