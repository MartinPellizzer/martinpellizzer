Phrynium pedunculatum

Phrynium pedunculatum, commonly known as the arrow leaf plant, is a tropical perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, arrow-shaped leaves that are often variegated with white and green patterns. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers followed by red or orange berries. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Its foliage is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
