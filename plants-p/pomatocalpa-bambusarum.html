Pomatocalpa bambusarum

Pomatocalpa bambusarum, commonly known as the bamboo orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique floral structure, featuring a resupinate (upside-down) flower with a prominent lip. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, often in forest understories. Its leaves are elongated and grass-like, resembling bamboo in appearance.

The orchid produces small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, adding ornamental value to its natural habitat.
