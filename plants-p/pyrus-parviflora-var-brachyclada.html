Pyrus parviflora var. brachyclada

Pyrus parviflora var. brachyclada, commonly known as the dwarf whitebeam, is a compact, deciduous shrub or small tree native to the mountainous regions of central and eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its small, white flowers that appear in early spring, followed by edible, sweet-tart berries in the fall. The leaves are typically elliptical, with a smooth margin and a slightly waxy texture. This variety is noted for its ornamental value and hardiness, thriving in a range of soil types and climates.

It is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal and ecological benefits.
