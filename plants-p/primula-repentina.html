Primula repentina

Primula repentina, commonly known as the alpine primrose, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe.

It is a member of the Primulaceae family and typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, yellow, and pink, often blooming in early spring. It has a short, hairy stem and heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence.

Primula repentina is adapted to cold climates and is often found in meadows and slopes at high elevations.
