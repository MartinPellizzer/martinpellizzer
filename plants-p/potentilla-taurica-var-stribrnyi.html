Potentilla taurica var. stribrnyi

Potentilla taurica var. stribrnyi, commonly known as the striped potentilla, is a perennial shrub native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its slender, arching stems and small, clustered flowers that range from white to pinkish hues. The leaves are typically narrow and elliptical, with a distinctive silver or grayish underside that adds to its ornamental value. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders.

This variety is noted for its hardiness and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
