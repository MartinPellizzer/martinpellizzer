Ptychotis aspera

Ptychotis aspera, commonly known as the roughleaf dandelion, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, rough-textured leaves and yellow composite flowers that resemble dandelions. The plant typically grows in grasslands and rocky areas, preferring well-drained soils. It produces small, wind-dispersed seeds that contribute to its spread across its native habitat.

Ptychotis aspera is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
