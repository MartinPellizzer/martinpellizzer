Pinus abies var. germanica

Pinus abies var. germanica, commonly known as the Norway spruce, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to central and northern Europe.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and dense, pyramidal crown. The needles are dark green, flat, and arranged in two rows on the shoots, while the cones are cylindrical and typically hang downward. It thrives in cool, moist climates and is often found in mountainous regions.

This variety is valued for its ornamental use and as a source of timber.
