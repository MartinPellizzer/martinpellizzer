Pachyrhizus thunbergianus

Pachyrhizus thunbergianus, commonly known as the African bean, is a leguminous plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its climbing habit and trifoliate leaves, with each leaflet being elliptical and hairy. The plant produces small, yellow flowers followed by elongated pods containing edible seeds. It has a deep root system that allows it to thrive in poor soil conditions.

The plant is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, making it useful in agricultural systems.
