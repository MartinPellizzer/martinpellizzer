Pedicularis labradorica f. simplex

Pedicularis labradorica f. simplex, commonly known as Labrador betony, is a flowering plant native to the Arctic and subarctic regions of North America.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, rocky habitats such as tundra and heathlands. The plant produces showy, spurred flowers in shades of pink and purple, which attract pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs.

The species is notable for its ability to thrive in harsh, cold environments, showcasing adaptations to extreme climatic conditions.
