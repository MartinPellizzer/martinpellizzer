Polygonatum cilicicum

Polygonatum cilicicum, commonly known as the Eastern Solomon's Seal, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Turkey and the Caucasus.

It belongs to the Asparagaceae family and is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit and upright, grass-like foliage. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers in early summer, followed by red berries that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans.

It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories, where it contributes to the ecological diversity of its habitat.
