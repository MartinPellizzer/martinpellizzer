Pintoa

Pintoa, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its delicate, blue-violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves that give it a graceful appearance. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers moist, well-drained soil.

Pintoa is often used in native gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
