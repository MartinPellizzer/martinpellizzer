Peucedanum sonderi

Peucedanum sonderi, commonly known as the African cowparsley, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a biennial or perennial herb that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant features finely divided, fern-like leaves and produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in umbels. Its seeds are enclosed in a spherical, ribbed fruit called a schizocarp.

The plant is often found in grasslands and along riverbanks, where it thrives in well-drained soils.
