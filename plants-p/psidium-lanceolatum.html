Psidium lanceolatum

Psidium lanceolatum, commonly known as the lanceleaf guava, is a small evergreen tree native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, edible fruits that are typically yellow or orange when ripe. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

The lanceleaf guava is often cultivated for its ornamental value and for its flavorful fruit.
