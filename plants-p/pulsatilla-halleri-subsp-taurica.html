Pulsatilla halleri subsp. taurica

Pulsatilla halleri subsp.

taurica, commonly known as the Tauric pasqueflower, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia. It typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils.

The plant produces showy, violet-colored flowers with a distinctive, puffed appearance, which bloom in early spring. Its leaves are basal, forming a rosette, and are covered in fine, soft hairs.

The species is closely related to other members of the Ranunculaceae family, sharing similar structural and ecological characteristics.
