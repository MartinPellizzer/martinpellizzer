Pinus hamiltonii

Pinus hamiltonii, commonly known as the Hamilton's pine, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and long, needle-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The tree produces small, yellow to reddish-brown cones that mature over several years. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, dry climates.

Pinus hamiltonii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and resilience in harsh environments.
