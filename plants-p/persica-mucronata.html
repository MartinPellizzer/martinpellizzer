Persica mucronata

Persica mucronata, commonly known as the blunt-thorn peach.

It is a species of wild peach native to the Canary Islands, characterized by its small, yellowish flowers and red, hairy fruits. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, adapting to the harsh conditions of its native habitat. Its leaves are oval-shaped with a slightly serrated edge, and the stems are often covered in small, sharp thorns.

Persica mucronata is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been studied for its potential in horticultural breeding programs.
