Prosopis calden

Prosopis calden, commonly known as the Calden acacia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it resilient to drought conditions. Its root system helps prevent soil erosion, and it is often used in reforestation projects.

The pods contain seeds that are dispersed by wind, aiding in its propagation across suitable habitats.
