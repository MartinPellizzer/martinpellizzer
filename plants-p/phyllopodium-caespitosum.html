Phyllopodium caespitosum

Phyllopodium caespitosum, commonly known as the tufted phyllopodium, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and small, clustered leaves. The plant produces showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its unique appearance and ease of cultivation have made it a favored plant among horticulturists.
