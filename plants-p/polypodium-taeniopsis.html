Polypodium taeniopsis

Polypodium taeniopsis, commonly known as the narrow-leaf maidenhair fern, is a species of epiphytic fern native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, linear fronds that are typically dark green with a glossy appearance. The plant has a rhizomatous structure, allowing it to spread and form dense colonies on trees or rocks. Its spore-producing structures, called sori, are clustered on the underside of the fronds.

Polypodium taeniopsis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in shaded, humid environments.
