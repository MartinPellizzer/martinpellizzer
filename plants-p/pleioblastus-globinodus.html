Pleioblastus globinodus

Pleioblastus globinodus, commonly known as the globular bamboo, is a species of grass-like plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, clumping growth habit and slender, cylindrical stems that often have a bluish-green hue. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters, which are typically followed by elongated, cylindrical fruits. Its foliage is often used in ornamental gardening due to its aesthetic appeal and hardiness.

Pleioblastus globinodus is also valued for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions and its resistance to pests and diseases.
