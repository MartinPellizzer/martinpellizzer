Plumbago europaea var. glandulosa

Plumbago europaea var. glandulosa, commonly known as the European plumbago, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its blue or purple flowers that bloom in summer, and its slender, branching stems. The leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a slightly hairy texture. This variety is noted for its glandular features on the stems and leaves, which contribute to its specific botanical classification.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
