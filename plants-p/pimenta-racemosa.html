Pimenta racemosa

Pimenta racemosa, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a member of the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, red berries and glossy, dark green leaves. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, which are followed by the berries. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been introduced to other regions, sometimes becoming invasive.

The plant contains essential oils that have been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
