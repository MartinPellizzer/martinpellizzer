Pyrus calleryana var. tomentella

Pyrus calleryana var. tomentella, commonly known as the Chinese white ash, is a variant of the Callerya pear.

It is characterized by its dense, silvery-gray pubescence on the leaves and stems, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant is native to China and is often cultivated as an ornamental tree due to its aesthetic appeal. It produces small, yellowish flowers in early spring, followed by edible fruits that are typically used in traditional Chinese medicine.

The species is also valued for its adaptability to various soil types and its resistance to urban environments.
