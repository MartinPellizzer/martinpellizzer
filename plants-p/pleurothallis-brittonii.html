Pleurothallis brittonii

Pleurothallis brittonii, commonly known as the Britton's orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its compact, often epiphytic growth habit. The plant typically produces small, delicate flowers in shades of white and pale pink, which bloom during the warmer months. Its pseudobulbs are slender, and the leaves are usually arranged in a rosette pattern, aiding in water retention.

Pleurothallis brittonii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
