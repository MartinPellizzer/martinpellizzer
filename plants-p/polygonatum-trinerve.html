Polygonatum trinerve

Polygonatum trinerve, commonly known as the Chinese Solomon's seal, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Asparagaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, rhizomatous stems and clusters of narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in late spring, which are followed by bright red berries. Native to East Asia, it thrives in shaded, moist environments such as forests and woodland areas.

Its leaves and roots have been used in traditional Chinese medicine for their purported health benefits.
