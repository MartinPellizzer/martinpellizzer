Plantago purshii var. picta

Plantago purshii var. picta, commonly known as the painted plantain, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It features lance-shaped leaves with a distinctive white or pale green midrib, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in a spike-like inflorescence during the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along streambanks.

Its foliage is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies for its mild medicinal properties.
