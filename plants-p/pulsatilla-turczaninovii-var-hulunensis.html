Pulsatilla turczaninovii var. hulunensis

Pulsatilla turczaninovii var. hulunensis, commonly known as the Hulun Desert pasqueflower, is a perennial herb native to the arid regions of northern China.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its showy, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has a short, thick rhizome and basal leaves that are palmately divided. It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and is adapted to harsh, semi-desert environments.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies in arid ecosystems.
