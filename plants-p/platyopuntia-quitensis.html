Platyopuntia quitensis

Platyopuntia quitensis, commonly known as the Andean prickly pear, is a species of cactus native to the high Andes of South America.

It is characterized by its flat, paddle-shaped stems and spiny areoles, which are typical features of the Cactaceae family. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by edible fruits similar to other prickly pear varieties. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, adapting to high altitudes and cold temperatures.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological significance, it is a notable subject in botanical studies of Andean flora.
