Psidium rubescens

Psidium rubescens, commonly known as the red guava, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant produces small, red, edible fruits that are rich in vitamins and antioxidants. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a rough texture.

The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value as well as its fruit.
