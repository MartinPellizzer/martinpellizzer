Papaver acrochaetum

Papaver acrochaetum, commonly known as the mountain poppy, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, bright yellow petals and a distinctive, short, conical style that protrudes from the center of the flower. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its flowers bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Papaver acrochaetum is a member of the Papaveraceae family, which includes other well-known species like the opium poppy.
