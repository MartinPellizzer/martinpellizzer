Pariti quinquelobum

Pariti quinquelobum, commonly known as the five-lobed pariti, is a unique flowering plant native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, palmate leaves with five distinct lobes, which contribute to its ornamental appeal. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and requires a humid, temperate climate to flourish.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens for conservation and study.
