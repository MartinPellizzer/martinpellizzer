Potentilla × bazargica

Potentilla × bazargica, commonly known as the Kashmiri cinquefoil, is a hybrid shrub native to the Himalayan regions of India and Pakistan.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and vibrant yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm, with woody stems and trifoliate leaves. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine meadows at elevations above 2,000 meters.

Due to its ornamental value and hardiness, it is cultivated in gardens in temperate climates.
