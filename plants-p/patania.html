Patania

Patania, commonly known as the Mexican beautyberry, is a shrub native to Mexico and parts of the southern United States.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, bright blue berries that appear in the fall. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are dark green and glossy on top, with a lighter underside. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil.

Patania is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
