Pueraria alopecuroides

Pueraria alopecuroides, commonly known as Chinese kudzu, is a perennial vine native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of purple or white flowers. The plant produces long, cylindrical pods that contain several bean-like seeds. It is known for its vigorous growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types.

Botanically, it belongs to the Fabaceae family, also known as the legume or pea family.
