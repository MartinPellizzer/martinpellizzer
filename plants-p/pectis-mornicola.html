Pectis mornicola

Pectis mornicola, commonly known as the yellow dandelion, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features bright yellow ray flowers surrounding a central disk of tubular flowers, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and often branching.

Pectis mornicola thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and open habitats.
