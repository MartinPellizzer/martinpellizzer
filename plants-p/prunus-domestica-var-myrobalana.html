Prunus domestica var. myrobalana

Prunus domestica var. myrobalana, commonly known as the Chinese plum, is a deciduous tree native to China and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, dark bark and small, oval-shaped leaves that turn yellow in autumn. The tree produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers in spring, followed by edible berries that range from yellow to deep purple. These berries are rich in antioxidants and have a tart, slightly sweet flavor.

The plant is often cultivated for its fruit, which is used in culinary and medicinal applications.
