Prunus mutabilis f. orientalis

Prunus mutabilis f. orientalis, commonly known as the oriental cherry or Japanese double-blossom cherry, is a cultivar of the Prunus genus.

It is characterized by its distinctive double flowers, which have a fluffy, pom-pom appearance. The plant is deciduous, shedding its leaves in the autumn, and it produces small, edible fruits in the summer. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Its flowers typically change color from white to pink to red as they mature, adding visual interest throughout the blooming season.
