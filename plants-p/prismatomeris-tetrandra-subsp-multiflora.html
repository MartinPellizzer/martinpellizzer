Prismatomeris tetrandra subsp. multiflora

Prismatomeris tetrandra subsp.

multiflora, commonly known as the mountain holly, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States. It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, glossy leaves that are typically arranged in whorls.

The plant produces white, fragrant flowers in clusters during the spring, followed by red, fleshy berries in the fall. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it adaptable to various garden conditions.

Its ornamental value and ability to form thickets make it a popular choice for landscaping in humid climates.
