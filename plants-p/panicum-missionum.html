Panicum missionum

Panicum missionum, commonly known as the Mexican dropseed, is a flowering grass native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a low-growing perennial that produces delicate, silver-blue flower heads that resemble small drops of water. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for xeriscaping. It is drought-tolerant and requires minimal maintenance once established.

Panicum missionum is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
