Psidium subcrenatum

Psidium subcrenatum, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or granadilla, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its elliptical, glossy leaves and showy, fragrant white or pale pink flowers. The plant produces small, yellow or orange berries that are edible and often used in culinary applications. Its botanical features include a smooth, grayish bark and a tendency to form dense, bushy growth.

Psidium subcrenatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in traditional medicine.
