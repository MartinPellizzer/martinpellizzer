Prunus jamasakura f. rotundipetala

Prunus jamasakura f. rotundipetala, commonly known as the Japanese cherry blossom with rounded petals, is a unique cultivar of the Prunus jamasakura species.

It is characterized by its distinctive, broad, and rounded petals that give it a more delicate and fluffy appearance compared to typical cherry blossoms. This variety is native to Japan and is often cultivated for its ornamental value during the spring blooming season. The plant is a deciduous tree that produces small, sweet fruits in addition to its showy flowers.

Its botanical features make it a prized addition to gardens and parks, especially in regions with temperate climates.
