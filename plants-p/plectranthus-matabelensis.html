Plectranthus matabelensis

Plectranthus matabelensis, commonly known as Matabel bush or Matabel plant, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves and clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to purple. It is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for both garden cultivation and ornamental purposes.
