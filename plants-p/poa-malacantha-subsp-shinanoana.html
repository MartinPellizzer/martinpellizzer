Poa malacantha subsp. shinanoana

Poa malacantha subsp.

shinanoana, commonly known as the Shinano mountain grass, is a perennial grass species native to Japan. It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as mountain forests and stream sides. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of spikelets, which are often tinged with a reddish hue.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat.
