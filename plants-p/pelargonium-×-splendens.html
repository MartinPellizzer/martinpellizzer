Pelargonium × splendens

Pelargonium × splendens, commonly known as the zonal geranium, is a hybrid species within the Geraniaceae family.

It is characterized by its showy, bilaterally symmetrical flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, often with a distinctive dark band near the base of the petals. The plant features rounded, velvety leaves with distinct zones of color, giving it its common name. It is a popular ornamental plant, widely cultivated for its vibrant blooms and adaptability to various growing conditions.

Pelargonium × splendens thrives in full sun to partial shade and is commonly used in gardens, containers, and as bedding plants.
