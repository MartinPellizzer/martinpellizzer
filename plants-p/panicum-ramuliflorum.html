Panicum ramuliflorum

Panicum ramuliflorum, commonly known as the ramiflorous panicgrass, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and the Middle East.

It is a grass species characterized by its slender, tufted growth and delicate, branching inflorescences. The plant produces small, spikelet-based flowers that are typically green or reddish in color. It thrives in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils.

Due to its ornamental appeal and adaptability, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
