Petrophile cunninghamii var. brachyphylla

Petrophile cunninghamii var. brachyphylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bottlebrush, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive cylindrical flower spikes that resemble bottle brushes. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to dry, coastal environments. Its leaves are narrow and often have a waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting native wildlife.
