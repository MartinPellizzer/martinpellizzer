Peperomia campii

Peperomia campii, commonly known as the Campi peperomia, is a compact, low-growing herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It features thick, waxy leaves that are typically ovate to elliptic in shape, often with a bluish or grayish hue due to a dense covering of fine hairs. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters, which are usually greenish-white in color. It is well adapted to shaded environments and thrives in moist, well-drained soil.

Peperomia campii is popular as a houseplant due to its attractive foliage and ease of care.
