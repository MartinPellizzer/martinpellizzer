Pouteria ramiflora var. oblongifolia

Pouteria ramiflora var. oblongifolia, commonly known as the oblong-leafed soursop, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are typically a deep green in color. The plant produces clusters of small, green, spiny fruits that turn yellow when ripe. Its flowers are fragrant and typically white or pale yellow, attracting pollinators such as bees and bats.

The plant thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.
