Podochilus lamii

Podochilus lamii, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers and is often found in lowland forests. The plant has a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its leaves are long, strap-like, and often have a waxy texture.

Podochilus lamii is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in shaded environments.
