Prunus serrulata subf. rosea

Prunus serrulata subf.

rosea, commonly known as the pink-leafed cherry, is a variant of the Japanese cherry tree characterized by its distinctive pink foliage. It is native to Japan and is closely related to the more well-known Prunus serrulata, or Yamazakura.

The plant produces small, white flowers in early spring, followed by red or pink fruits. Its leaves are ovate with serrated edges, and they turn a striking pink hue in the autumn.

This ornamental tree is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic value and seasonal beauty.
