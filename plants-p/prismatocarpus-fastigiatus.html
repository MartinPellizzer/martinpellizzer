Prismatocarpus fastigiatus

Prismatocarpus fastigiatus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sterculia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Sterculiaceae and is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stem. Its flowers are small and clustered in inflorescences, often with a distinctive yellow or orange coloration.

The fruit is a capsule that splits open to release seeds, contributing to its role in local ecosystems.
