Psychechilos purpurascens

Psychechilos purpurascens, commonly known as the purple-flowered orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking purple and white flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are broad and succulent, adapted to store water in its natural habitat.

The flowers bloom in clusters, often attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
