Polystichum acuminatum

Polystichum acuminatum, commonly known as the deer fern, is a species of evergreen fern native to the northeastern United States and eastern Canada.

It features a tall, upright rhizome with a dense cluster of lance-shaped, dark green fronds that have a serrated edge. The fronds are typically 15 to 30 inches long and are covered in fine, silky hairs on the underside. It prefers moist, shady environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly used in horticultural settings.
