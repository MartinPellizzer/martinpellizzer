Petrea insignis

Petrea insignis, commonly known as the Brazilian jasmine, is a flowering plant native to Brazil.

It is a woody shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters in height. The plant produces fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the summer months. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and have a slightly serrated edge.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often used in horticulture for its pleasant fragrance.
