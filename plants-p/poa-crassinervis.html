Poa crassinervis

Poa crassinervis, commonly known as the broad-veined grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit. The leaves are narrow, with prominent, parallel veins that give it its common name. It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with pale green to brown flowers, which are wind-pollinated.
