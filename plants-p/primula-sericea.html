Primula sericea

Primula sericea, commonly known as the silverleaf primrose, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, silver-green foliage, which gives it its common name. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers partial to full shade, making it suitable for woodland gardens.

Primula sericea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in horticultural settings.
