Polygala serpyllifolia

Polygala serpyllifolia, commonly known as common milkvetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the Polygalaceae family.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. The plant features small, clustered flowers that are usually pink or purple in color, and it produces capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It has slender, often hairy stems and small, alternate leaves that are usually oval or lance-shaped.

This species is native to Europe and parts of Asia and is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its hardy nature.
