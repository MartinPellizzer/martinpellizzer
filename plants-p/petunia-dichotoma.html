Petunia dichotoma

Petunia dichotoma, commonly known as the fringed petunia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and vibrant, showy flowers that come in a variety of colors. The plant produces two types of leaves—basal and stem leaves—that differ in shape and arrangement. Its flowers are typically tubular with five lobes, and they bloom in clusters at the ends of the stems.

Petunia dichotoma is often cultivated as a decorative plant in gardens and hanging baskets due to its ornamental value.
