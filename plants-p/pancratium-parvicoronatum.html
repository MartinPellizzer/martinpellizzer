Pancratium parvicoronatum

Pancratium parvicoronatum, commonly known as the small-capped yellow arum, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Aroid family (Araceae) and is characterized by its striking yellow spathe and a narrow, elongated spadix. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and is often found in sandy or rocky soils. Pancratium parvicoronatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
