Polygala pseudostricta

Polygala pseudostricta, commonly known as the narrow-leaved milk-vetch, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Polygalaceae family, characterized by its distinctive, often spiny stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are narrow and often have a slightly hairy texture, while its flowers are usually pale yellow or greenish-white.

Polygala pseudostricta is adapted to drought conditions and plays a role in stabilizing soils in its native habitats.
