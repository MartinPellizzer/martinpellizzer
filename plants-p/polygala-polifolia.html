Polygala polifolia

Polygala polifolia, commonly known as the lace fern or narrow-leaved milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It is often found in coastal areas and is adapted to dry, coastal climates.

Polygala polifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and floral display.
