Pharbitis indica

Pharbitis indica, commonly known as morning glory, is a climbing vine native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in the morning. The plant produces distinctive seed pods that contain spiral-shaped seeds, a feature that aids in its dispersal. It thrives in warm climates with well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

Pharbitis indica is widely cultivated for its ornamental value and has cultural significance in various parts of the world.
