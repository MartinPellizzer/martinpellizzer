Prunella × elatior

Prunella × elatior, commonly known as selfheal, is a perennial flowering plant belonging to the mint family, Lamiaceae.

It is characterized by its square, hairy stems and opposite, ovate leaves with toothed edges. The plant produces spikes of blue to purple flowers that bloom from late spring to early autumn. It is native to Europe and parts of Asia but has been naturalized in many regions around the world.

Prunella × elatior is often found in disturbed soils, meadows, and woodland edges, and it is known for its medicinal properties.
