Primula magellanica subsp. decipiens

Primula magellanica subsp.

decipiens, commonly known as the Magellan's primrose, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Chile and Argentina. It is characterized by its rosette of broad, hairy, gray-green leaves and produces clusters of bell-shaped, violet or purple flowers.

The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.

The subspecies decipiens is distinguished by its slightly different floral morphology compared to other subspecies of Primula magellanica.
