Pomaderris cotoneaster

Pomaderris cotoneaster, commonly known as the satinwood or silverleaf pomaderris, is a shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its finely toothed, elliptical leaves that are often covered in a silvery or greyish bloom. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers in clusters, which are typically followed by small, red or orange berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or scrub habitats.

Pomaderris cotoneaster is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in horticulture for its attractive foliage and seasonal interest.
