Pilocereus celsianus

Pilocereus celsianus, commonly known as the Christmas cactus, is a species of cactus native to South America.

It is characterized by its segmented, branching stems that bear clusters of vibrant, tubular flowers. The plant typically produces flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, often during the holiday season. Its leaves are modified into thick, fleshy stems that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid conditions.

Pilocereus celsianus is a popular ornamental plant due to its striking appearance and relatively easy care requirements.
