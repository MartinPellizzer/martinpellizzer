Pinus laricio var. magellensis

Pinus laricio var. magellensis, commonly known as the Magellanic stone pine, is a variant of the stone pine native to the southern Andes.

It is characterized by its slender, often twisted trunk and dense, evergreen foliage. The leaves are needle-like, typically growing in clusters of five, and are a deep green in color. The tree produces large, edible cones that contain oily seeds used in traditional cuisine.

It thrives in cool, temperate climates and is adapted to rocky, well-drained soils.
