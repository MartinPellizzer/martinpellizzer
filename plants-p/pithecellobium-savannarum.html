Pithecellobium savannarum

Pithecellobium savannarum, commonly known as the yellow velvet tamarind, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant produces small, edible pods that contain sweet, sticky seeds often used in traditional cuisines.

It is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial in ecological restoration projects.
