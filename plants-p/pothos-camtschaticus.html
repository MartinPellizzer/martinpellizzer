Pothos camtschaticus

Pothos camtschaticus, commonly known as the golden pothos, is a vigorous climbing vine native to the Caucasus region.

It features heart-shaped leaves that are typically variegated with shades of yellow and green, giving it a striking appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers, though it is more valued for its foliage than its blooms. It thrives in bright, indirect light and is relatively easy to care for, making it a popular houseplant.

Its rapid growth and ability to climb or trail make it versatile for various indoor gardening setups.
