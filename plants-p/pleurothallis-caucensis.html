Pleurothallis caucensis

Pleurothallis caucensis, commonly known as the Andean orchid, is a species native to the cloud forests of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, often fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to tree trunks or branches in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are usually elongated and arranged in a rosette pattern, facilitating moisture retention.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
