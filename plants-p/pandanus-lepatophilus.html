Pandanus lepatophilus

Pandanus lepatophilus, commonly known as the broad-leaf pandanus, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stem and large, feather-like leaves that can grow up to several meters in length. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically yellow or green, followed by bright orange or red fruits. It thrives in humid, coastal environments and is often found in mangrove swamps or on sandy shores.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and has traditional uses in some cultures for thatching and basket weaving.
