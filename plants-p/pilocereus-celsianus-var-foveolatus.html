Pilocereus celsianus var. foveolatus

Pilocereus celsianus var. foveolatus, commonly known as the star cactus or moon cactus, is a species of cactus native to South America.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems that are often segmented and covered with small, rounded tubercles. The plant produces pale yellow to white flowers that bloom at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators. It is a succulent, adapted to arid environments, with thick, fleshy stems that store water.

This variety is prized in horticulture for its unique appearance and relatively easy care requirements.
