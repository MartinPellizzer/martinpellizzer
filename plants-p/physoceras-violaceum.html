Physoceras violaceum

Physoceras violaceum, commonly known as the violet moonflower, is a striking member of the Apocynaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa and is characterized by its large, showy flowers that open at night. The plant features thick, fleshy leaves and produces fragrant, violet-colored blooms that emit a sweet scent. Its flowers are typically trumpet-shaped and attract nocturnal pollinators such as moths.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has cultural significance in some regions.
