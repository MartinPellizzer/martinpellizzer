Pleiospilos compactus subsp. fergusoniae

Pleiospilos compactus subsp.

fergusoniae, commonly known as the Fergusona's compact pleiospilos, is a succulent plant native to South Africa. It is characterized by its dense, rounded growth habit and thick, fleshy leaves that store water.

The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the summer months, which are followed by distinctive, spiky seed pods. It thrives in full sun and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Due to its unique appearance and drought tolerance, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.
