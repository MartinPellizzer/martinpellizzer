Perityle fastigiata

Perityle fastigiata, commonly known as the upright perityle, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that grow in a vertical manner, forming a dense, shrub-like structure. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale blue in color. Its leaves are narrow and linear, arranged closely along the stem.

Perityle fastigiata is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a decorative plant in shaded areas.
