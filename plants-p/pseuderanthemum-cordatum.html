Pseuderanthemum cordatum

Pseuderanthemum cordatum, commonly known as the heartleaf pseuderanthemum, is a species of flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves, which are typically glossy and dark green. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that are usually white or pale pink, often with a red or purple calyx. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories or along stream banks.

Pseuderanthemum cordatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated as a houseplant.
