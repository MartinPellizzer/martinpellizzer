Pimelea rosea subsp. annelsii

Pimelea rosea subsp.

annelsii, commonly known as the pink bottlebrush, is a shrub native to Western Australia. It is characterized by its striking pink to red flowers that appear in dense, cylindrical clusters, giving it a bottlebrush appearance.

The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it typically grows in well-drained sandy or gravelly soils. It is a hardy species that thrives in full sun and is often found in coastal and inland regions.

The subspecies annelsii is distinguished by its more vibrant flower color and slightly different leaf structure compared to other subspecies of Pimelea rosea.
