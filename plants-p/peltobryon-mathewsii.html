Peltobryon mathewsii

Peltobryon mathewsii, commonly known as the Mathews' moss, is a species of liverwort belonging to the family Peltobryaceae.

It is characterized by its small, leafy structure and a distinct, often wrinkled thallus. This plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as on rocks or tree trunks in temperate regions. Its reproductive structures, including capsules, are borne on short stalks near the tips of the leaves.

Peltobryon mathewsii is of interest to botanists due to its ecological role and its contribution to the study of bryophyte diversity.
