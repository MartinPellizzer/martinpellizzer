Pycreus sanguinolentus

Pycreus sanguinolentus, commonly known as the Crimson Vein Orchid, is a striking flowering plant native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant red petals with intricate veining patterns that resemble blood vessels, giving it its dramatic common name. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to the trunks of trees in its natural habitat. Its flowers bloom in clusters and emit a faintly sweet fragrance, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its rarity and ornamental value, it is highly sought after in horticultural trade.
