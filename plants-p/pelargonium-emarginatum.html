Pelargonium emarginatum

Pelargonium emarginatum, commonly known as the margined geranium, is a species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, palmate leaves with notched edges and clusters of small, pink or red flowers. The plant typically grows as an annual or biennial herb, reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its vibrant blooms and unique foliage structure.
