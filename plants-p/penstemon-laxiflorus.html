Penstemon laxiflorus

Penstemon laxiflorus, commonly known as the slenderflower beardtongue, is a member of the Plantaginaceae family.

It is native to the western United States and is often found in dry, rocky habitats. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to purple, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and is typically low-growing, forming dense mats.

Penstemon laxiflorus is valued in gardens for its drought tolerance and ornamental appeal.
