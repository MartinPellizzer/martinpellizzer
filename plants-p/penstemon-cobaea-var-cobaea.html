Penstemon cobaea var. cobaea

Penstemon cobaea var. cobaea, commonly known as the tall beardtongue, is a striking perennial plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, upright stems that can reach heights of up to six feet, making it a dramatic focal point in gardens. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers in shades of red, pink, and purple, which bloom in late summer to early fall. Its foliage consists of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

This species is well-suited for full sun and well-drained soil, and it attracts pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds.
