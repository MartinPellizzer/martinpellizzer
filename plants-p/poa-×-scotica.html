Poa × scotica

Poa × scotica, commonly known as the Scottish bentgrass, is a hybrid grass species native to the British Isles.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and dense, mat-forming structure. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, pastures, and along coastal areas. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, with a bluish-green coloration.

Poa × scotica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in turfgrass mixes for its durability and aesthetic qualities.
