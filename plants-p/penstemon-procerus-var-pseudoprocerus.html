Penstemon procerus var. pseudoprocerus

Penstemon procerus var. pseudoprocerus, commonly known as the Sierra penstemon, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, erect growth habit and showy, tubular flowers that range in color from violet to blue. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils in mountainous regions. Its leaves are linear and often hairy, adapted to reduce water loss in arid environments.

It is a perennial species that thrives in full sun and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.
