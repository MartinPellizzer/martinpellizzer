Pleurothallis piratiningana

Pleurothallis piratiningana, commonly known as the Piratininga orchid, is a species native to the cloud forests of Ecuador.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact, epiphytic growth habit. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and yellow, often clustered in racemes. It thrives in humid, shaded environments with high air circulation.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
