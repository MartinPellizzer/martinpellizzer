Phyllostegia macraei

Phyllostegia macraei, commonly known as the broadleaf phyllostegia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is known for its aromatic plants and square stems. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are typically purple or blue, clustered in whorls along the stems, and bloom during the rainy season.

Phyllostegia macraei is often found in moist, shaded habitats such as forest understories.
