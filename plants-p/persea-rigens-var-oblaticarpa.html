Persea rigens var. oblaticarpa

Persea rigens var. oblaticarpa, commonly known as the Mexican persimmon, is a tropical evergreen tree native to southern Mexico.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae and is characterized by its broad, elliptical leaves that are glossy and dark green on top. The tree produces small, fragrant flowers followed by orange to reddish fruits that are edible when fully ripe. The fruit has a sweet, honey-like flavor and is often consumed fresh or used in culinary applications.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
