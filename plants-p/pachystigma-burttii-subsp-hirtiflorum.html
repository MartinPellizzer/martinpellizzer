Pachystigma burttii subsp. hirtiflorum

Pachystigma burttii subsp.

hirtiflorum, commonly known as the wooly-flowered pachystigma, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage and clusters of small, fragrant flowers that bloom in spring.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its unique floral structure and ornamental value make it a sought-after species in horticulture.

It is also noted for its ability to tolerate drought conditions once established.
