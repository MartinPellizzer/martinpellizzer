Pseudencyclia prismatocarpa

Pseudencyclia prismatocarpa, commonly known as the rock fig, is a small evergreen shrub native to the island of Socotra.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow. The plant produces edible fruits that are encased in a thick, fleshy husk. It thrives in arid environments and is adapted to survive in rocky soils with minimal water.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological importance, it is considered a species of conservation concern.
