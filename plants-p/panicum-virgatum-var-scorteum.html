Panicum virgatum var. scorteum

Panicum virgatum var. scorteum, commonly known as switchgrass with scorteum, is a variant of switchgrass native to the United States.

It is a perennial grass characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, blade-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist to wet soils and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks. It produces small, fluffy seeds that are dispersed by wind, aiding in its propagation.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential as a bioenergy crop.
