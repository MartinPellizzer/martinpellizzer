Pandaca crassifolia

Pandaca crassifolia, commonly known as the thick-leaved pandaca, is a species of flowering plant native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, thick, and leathery leaves that are typically dark green in color. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually white or pale yellow, often appearing in late spring or early summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical forests.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists.
