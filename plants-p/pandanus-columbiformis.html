Pandanus columbiformis

Pandanus columbiformis, commonly known as the pigeon feather pandanus, is a tropical plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its long, narrow, and often drooping leaves that resemble feathers, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. Its roots are fibrous and can be used in traditional crafts.

The species is often found growing in coastal areas and is adapted to salt spray and sandy soils.
