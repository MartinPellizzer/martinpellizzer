Paullinia meliifolia

Paullinia meliifolia, commonly known as the Brazilian star apple, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, white flowers. The plant produces a fruit that resembles a star in shape, hence its common name, and has a sweet, aromatic flavor. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible fruit.

The bark and leaves are also used in traditional medicine for their potential health benefits.
