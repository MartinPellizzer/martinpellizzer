Pinguicula moranensis var. moranensis

Pinguicula moranensis var. moranensis, commonly known as the Mexican butterwort, is a carnivorous plant native to the mountainous regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its rosette of fleshy, glandular leaves that secrete a sticky substance to trap small insects. The plant produces small, white, bell-shaped flowers during the growing season. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and requires consistent humidity to flourish.

Due to its unique adaptations, it is often cultivated by enthusiasts interested in rare and specialized flora.
