Panicum scoparium var. angustifolium

Panicum scoparium var. angustifolium, commonly known as narrowleaf dropseed, is a grass species native to the central and eastern United States.

It features slender, upright stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often a bluish-green color. The plant produces small, fluffy seeds that are dispersed by the wind, aiding in its spread across open habitats. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it a popular choice for native plant gardens.

Its delicate appearance and drought tolerance make it a valuable addition to ecological restoration projects.
