Phaseolus minimus f. heterophyllus

Phaseolus minimus f. heterophyllus, commonly known as the dwarf bean or small-leaf bean, is a variant of the common bean species.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and smaller, more rounded leaves compared to typical Phaseolus species. The plant produces small, edible pods that contain round or oval seeds, often used in traditional cuisines. Botanically, it belongs to the Fabaceae family, known for its nitrogen-fixing abilities through symbiotic bacteria in its root nodules.

This variety is valued for its adaptability to various soil conditions and its role in sustainable agriculture.
