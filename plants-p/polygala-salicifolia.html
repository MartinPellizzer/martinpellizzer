Polygala salicifolia

Polygala salicifolia, commonly known as the willowleaf milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, often hairy stems that grow from 30 to 90 centimeters in height. The leaves are alternate, simple, and lanceolate, with a bluish-green hue and a slightly hairy texture. Its small, clustered flowers are typically pale blue or violet, with a distinctive spurred structure at the base of each bloom.

This plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.
