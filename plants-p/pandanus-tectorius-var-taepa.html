Pandanus tectorius var. taepa

Pandanus tectorius var. taepa, commonly known as the pandan or screw pine, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and long, narrow leaves that are often used in cooking and traditional crafts. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that develop into fibrous, spiky fruits. Its leaves are rich in aromatic compounds, making them valuable for culinary and medicinal purposes.

The species is well adapted to coastal and saline environments, showcasing its resilience in diverse ecological conditions.
