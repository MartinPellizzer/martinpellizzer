Piper hispidum var. magnifolium

Piper hispidum var. magnifolium, commonly known as the magnolia pepper or giant pepper plant, is a tropical shrub native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are often clustered in terminal inflorescences. Its fruits are berry-like, turning from green to red as they mature.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
