Potamogeton sulcatus

Potamogeton sulcatus, commonly known as the ribbed pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant found in freshwater environments.

It has long, slender leaves that are ribbed and often have a bluish-green color. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically arranged in spikes. It plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.

Potamogeton sulcatus is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize sediment and improve water quality.
