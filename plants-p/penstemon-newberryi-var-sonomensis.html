Penstemon newberryi var. sonomensis

Penstemon newberryi var. sonomensis, commonly known as the Sonoma penstemon, is a flowering plant native to California.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It produces distinctive, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
