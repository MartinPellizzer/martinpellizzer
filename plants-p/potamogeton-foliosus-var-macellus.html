Potamogeton foliosus var. macellus

Potamogeton foliosus var. macellus, commonly known as the narrow-leaf pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater ecosystems in Europe and parts of Asia.

It has long, slender, ribbon-like leaves that are typically arranged in whorls around the stem. The plant produces small, green flowers that are clustered in spike-like inflorescences, which are often submerged or just at the water's surface. It plays an important role in aquatic habitats by providing shelter for fish and invertebrates.

Its presence is often an indicator of good water quality and stable aquatic environments.
