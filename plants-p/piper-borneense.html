Piper borneense

Piper borneense, commonly known as long pepper, is a climbing vine native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its small, peppercorn-like berries. The plant produces yellow or red berries that are harvested when mature, and they contain the aromatic compounds responsible for its distinctive flavor. The berries are used in traditional medicine and as a spice in various cuisines.

Its botanical features include opposite, ovate leaves and a unique, branched inflorescence.
