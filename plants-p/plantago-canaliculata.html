Plantago canaliculata

Plantago canaliculata, commonly known as narrow-leaved plantain, is a perennial herb native to Eurasia and North America.

It is characterized by its rosette of narrow, lance-shaped leaves with prominent veins and a hairy surface. The plant produces small, greenish flowers in a dense spike that emerges from the center of the rosette. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.

Botanically, it is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
