Pleioblastus lentigiosus

Pleioblastus lentigiosus, commonly known as the speckled bamboo, is a species native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright culms that are often covered with small, dark spots, giving it its common name. The plant belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is closely related to other bamboo species. Its foliage consists of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are usually a bright green color.

Pleioblastus lentigiosus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in garden settings.
