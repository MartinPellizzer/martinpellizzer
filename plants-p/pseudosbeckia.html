Pseudosbeckia

Pseudosbeckia, commonly known as the false beakweed, is a genus of flowering plants in the sunflower family, Asteraceae.

It is native to South America and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, daisy-like flowers. The plants typically grow in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. Pseudosbeckia species are generally herbaceous and can be annual or perennial, depending on the species.
