Piper aequale

Piper aequale, commonly known as the pepper plant or wild pepper, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its climbing habit and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that develop into elongated, woody fruits containing aromatic seeds. Piper aequale is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and ethnobotanical contexts.
