Passiflora quindiensis

Passiflora quindiensis, commonly known as the African passionflower, is a climbing vine native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that feature five sepals, five petals, and a distinctive crown of filaments. The plant produces bright orange or yellow berries that are edible and often consumed by birds and small mammals. Its leaves are heart-shaped with a rough texture and a strong scent.

Passiflora quindiensis is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
