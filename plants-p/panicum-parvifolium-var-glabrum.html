Panicum parvifolium var. glabrum

Panicum parvifolium var. glabrum, commonly known as little bluestem, is a native grass species found in the central and eastern United States.

It is characterized by its fine, blue-green foliage and compact growth habit, making it a popular choice for native plant gardens and restoration projects. The plant is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun to partial shade, often growing in well-drained soils. Its small, spike-like inflorescences produce light blue to purple seeds that are dispersed by wind.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and ecological benefits, supporting pollinators and providing habitat for small wildlife.
