Polygala matogrossensis

Polygala matogrossensis, commonly known as the Brazilian milkweed, is a flowering plant native to Brazil.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and delicate, often yellow or greenish flowers. The plant typically grows in open woodlands and grasslands, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are opposite and lanceolate, while its fruits are dry capsules that release small seeds.

This species is of interest to botanists due to its unique floral structure and ecological role in supporting local insect populations.
