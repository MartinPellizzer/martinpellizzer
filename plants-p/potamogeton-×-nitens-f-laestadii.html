Potamogeton × nitens f. laestadii

Potamogeton × nitens f. laestadii, commonly known as the narrow-leaved pondweed, is a hybrid plant species belonging to the family Potamogetonaceae.

It is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves that are often slightly curved and can reach lengths of up to 30 centimeters. This aquatic plant typically grows in shallow, freshwater environments such as ponds, lakes, and slow-moving rivers. It produces small, greenish flowers that are clustered in spike-like inflorescences, and it is often found floating or partially submerged.

The plant plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
