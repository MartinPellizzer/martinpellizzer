Petunia

Petunia, commonly known as a flowering plant in the nightshade family, is native to South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, trumpet-shaped flowers that come in a wide range of colors. The plant has opposite, simple leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs. Petunias are popular in gardens due to their resilience and ability to thrive in various soil conditions.

They are also known for their strong fragrance, which attracts pollinators like bees and butterflies.
