Polypodium paradisiastrum

Polypodium paradisiastrum, commonly known as the paradise fern, is a species of epiphytic fern native to Southeast Asia.

It thrives in humid, shaded environments, typically growing on tree trunks or rocks in tropical forests. The plant has feather-like fronds that are divided into narrow, lance-shaped segments. Its reproductive structures, called sori, are located on the underside of the fronds and produce spores for propagation.

This fern is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in greenhouses or as a houseplant.
