Pararistolochia zenkeri

Pararistolochia zenkeri, commonly known as the African birthwort, is a climbing vine native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Aristolochiaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and distinctive, intricate flowers that resemble a skull. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow fruits that contain seeds enclosed in a fleshy, red aril. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
