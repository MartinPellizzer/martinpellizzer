Passiflora cumbalensis var. cuencana

Passiflora cumbalensis var. cuencana, commonly known as the Cuencana passionflower, is a flowering plant native to the Andes region of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, complex flowers that feature a blend of red, yellow, and blue hues, with prominent filaments and a crown-like structure. The plant produces edible berries that are often used in local cuisines and traditional medicine. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a slightly waxy texture, while its stems are woody and climbing.

This species thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in shaded, moist environments.
