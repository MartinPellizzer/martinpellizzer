Phaius humblotii var. schlechteri

Phaius humblotii var. schlechteri, commonly known as the Schlechter's orchid, is a member of the orchid family (Orchidaceae) and is native to Southeast Asia.

This variant is characterized by its large, showy, and often fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. It grows as an herbaceous perennial, often found in wet or swampy habitats. The plant has parallel-veined leaves and produces pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients.

Its botanical features make it a striking addition to tropical gardens and a subject of interest for orchid enthusiasts.
