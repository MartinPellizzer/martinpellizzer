Pouteria chevalieri

Pouteria chevalieri, commonly known as the golden apple or Brazilian pouteria, is a tropical plant native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is a small to medium-sized tree that can grow up to 20 meters in height, characterized by its smooth, dark-brown bark and large, elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are followed by oval, yellow-orange fruits with a sweet, aromatic pulp. Its fruit is highly valued for its nutritional content, containing high levels of vitamin C and other essential nutrients.

Pouteria chevalieri is also known for its role in traditional medicine and as a source of edible oil.
