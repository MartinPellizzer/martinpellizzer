Piper obtusissimum

Piper obtusissimum, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a flowering plant native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is a member of the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, greenish-white flowers. The plant produces small, fleshy berries that are an important food source for various birds and mammals. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its attractive foliage and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.

However, it can become invasive in certain regions, outcompeting native vegetation.
