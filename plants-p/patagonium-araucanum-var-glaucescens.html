Patagonium araucanum var. glaucescens

Patagonium araucanum var. glaucescens, commonly known as the Andean blueberry, is a shrub native to the high altitudes of southern South America.

It is characterized by its small, dark blue to black berries that are rich in antioxidants. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in cool, temperate climates. Its leaves are elliptical with a waxy, bluish-green appearance, and it produces small white or pink flowers in early spring.

This variety is valued for both its ornamental qualities and its edible fruits.
