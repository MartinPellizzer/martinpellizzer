Potamogeton amplifolius var. ovalifolius

Potamogeton amplifolius var. ovalifolius, commonly known as the broad-leaved pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater ecosystems in Europe and Asia.

It features long, ribbon-like leaves that are broad and oval in shape, often with a waxy coating to reduce water loss. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically borne in clusters, and it relies on water currents for pollination. It thrives in shallow, slow-moving waters and plays a vital role in providing habitat for aquatic invertebrates and fish.

Its presence is often an indicator of good water quality in its environment.
