Penstemon palmeri var. macranthus

Penstemon palmeri var. macranthus, commonly known as the Palmer's beardtongue, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to purple. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its drought tolerance.

This variety is well-suited for xeriscaping due to its low water requirements and ornamental value.
