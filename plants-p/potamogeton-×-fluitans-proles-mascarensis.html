Potamogeton × fluitans proles mascarensis

Potamogeton × fluitans proles mascarensis, commonly known as the Mascarene water milfoil, is a hybrid aquatic plant native to the Mascarene Islands in the Indian Ocean.

It is a submerged or floating-leaved plant that typically grows in freshwater or brackish environments. The plant features narrow, ribbon-like leaves that are often arranged in whorls around the stem. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually clustered in spike-like inflorescences.

This species is noted for its adaptability to various water conditions and is often found in still or slow-moving waters.
