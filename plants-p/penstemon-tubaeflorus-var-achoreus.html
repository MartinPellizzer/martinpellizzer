Penstemon tubaeflorus var. achoreus

Penstemon tubaeflorus var. achoreus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved penstemon, is a perennial plant native to the western United States.

It features slender, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a fine, fuzzy coating. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers in shades of blue to purple, which attract pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its drought tolerance and ornamental value make it a popular addition to native plant gardens.
