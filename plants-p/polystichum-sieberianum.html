Polystichum sieberianum

Polystichum sieberianum, commonly known as the Sieber's wood fern, is a species of fern native to the mountainous regions of East Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, upright fronds that can grow up to 1 meter in height, with a dark green, leathery texture. The plant produces spore-bearing structures called sori on the underside of its fronds, which are clustered in lines along the veins. It thrives in cool, moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories.

Its distinctive appearance and hardy nature make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening in temperate climates.
