Prunus × media

Prunus × media, commonly known as the Japanese cherry or mountain cherry, is a hybrid species within the Prunus genus.

It is characterized by its ornamental value, particularly for its showy spring blossoms. The plant typically grows as a small to medium-sized tree or shrub, with a rounded or pyramidal shape. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and turn yellow or red in autumn.

The flowers, which are pink to white, appear in clusters and are followed by small, dark red fruits.
