Pholidota bismarckiensis

Pholidota bismarckiensis, commonly known as the Bismarck fig, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Indo-Pacific region.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and smooth, pale bark. The tree produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible fig-like fruits that are important for both wildlife and local communities. It thrives in humid climates and is often found in coastal and lowland areas.

Due to its ecological and economic value, it is sometimes cultivated for ornamental and agricultural purposes.
