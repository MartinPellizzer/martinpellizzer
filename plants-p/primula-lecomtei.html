Primula lecomtei

Primula lecomtei, commonly known as the Lecomte's primrose, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in rocky or forested areas at elevations between 1,500 and 3,000 meters. Its foliage consists of broad, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy texture.

Primula lecomtei is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate blooms.
