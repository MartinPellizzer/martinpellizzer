Pancratium parvum

Pancratium parvum, commonly known as the lesser bulbous lily, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces striking yellow flowers with a red or orange center. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters and thrives in well-drained soils. It is often found in coastal areas and is adapted to sandy or rocky habitats.

Pancratium parvum is a perennial herb that blooms in the summer months.
