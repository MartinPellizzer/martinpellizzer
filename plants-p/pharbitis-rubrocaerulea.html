Pharbitis rubrocaerulea

Pharbitis rubrocaerulea, commonly known as the blue-and-red morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its strikingly colorful flowers, which display a mix of blue and red hues, often with a white or pink center. The plant produces long, twisting vines that can climb and spread rapidly, making it a vigorous climber. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture, while its flowers open in the morning and close at dusk.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
