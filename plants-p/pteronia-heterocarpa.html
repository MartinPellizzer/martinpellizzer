Pteronia heterocarpa

Pteronia heterocarpa, commonly known as the Cape umbrella plant, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive umbrella-like inflorescence. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal areas.

Pteronia heterocarpa is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
