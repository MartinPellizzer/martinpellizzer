Polygala cineraria

Polygala cineraria, commonly known as the cineraria, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Polygalaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, often hairy leaves and clusters of small, blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to dry, coastal environments. Its flowers are pollinated by insects, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

The cineraria is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
