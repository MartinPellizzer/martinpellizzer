Piptanthocereus hankeanus

Piptanthocereus hankeanus, commonly known as the Hanke's cactus, is a flowering cactus native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems that are often covered in spines. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it well adapted to desert environments.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is a subject of interest for botanists and cactus enthusiasts alike.
