Plinia pinnata

Plinia pinnata, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or *Eugenia pinnata*, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its glossy, ovate leaves and showy, pinkish-white flowers. The plant produces small, red, cherry-like fruits that are edible and often used in local cuisines. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit.

Plinia pinnata is also valued for its ability to grow in a variety of soil types, making it adaptable to different environments.
