Peperomia spruceana

Peperomia spruceana, commonly known as the Spruce peperomia, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It features thick, waxy leaves that are often variegated with shades of green, white, and silver, giving it a striking appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or red, followed by tiny, round berries. It thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental foliage make it a versatile plant for both ornamental and decorative purposes.
