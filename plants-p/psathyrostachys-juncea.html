Psathyrostachys juncea

Psathyrostachys juncea, commonly known as intermediate wheatgrass, is a perennial grass native to the temperate regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, grass-like leaves. This plant is notable for its high nutritional value and is often studied for its potential in sustainable agriculture. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny grains.

Psathyrostachys juncea is also valued for its drought tolerance and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
