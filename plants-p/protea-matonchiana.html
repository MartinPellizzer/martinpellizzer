Protea matonchiana

Protea matonchiana, commonly known as the mountain protea, is a striking South African plant belonging to the Proteaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, showy flower heads that resemble a cluster of small flowers, known as proteoid flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to the Mediterranean climate of the Western Cape. Its flowers come in a range of colors, including pink, red, and white, and are often visited by birds and insects.

Protea matonchiana is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens.
