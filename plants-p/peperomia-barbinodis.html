Peperomia barbinodis

Peperomia barbinodis, commonly known as the hairy peperomia, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It features thick, fleshy, hairy leaves that are typically dark green with a waxy texture, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters. The plant is well adapted to low-light conditions and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot. Its unique foliage makes it a popular choice for indoor gardening and decorative plant arrangements.

It is relatively easy to care for, making it suitable for both novice and experienced plant enthusiasts.
