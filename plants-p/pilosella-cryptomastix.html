Pilosella cryptomastix

Pilosella cryptomastix, commonly known as the narrowleaf mouse-ear, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its small, woolly leaves and yellow daisy-like flowers that bloom in the summer. Native to Europe and parts of Asia, this plant thrives in dry, sunny grasslands and rocky soils. It has a compact growth habit and is often found in open habitats with minimal competition.

Pilosella cryptomastix is a perennial herb that plays a minor role in local ecosystems but is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and distribution.
