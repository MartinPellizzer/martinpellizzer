Panicum pungens

Panicum pungens, commonly known as purpletop millet, is a warm-season grass native to the southern United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and striking purple-tinted inflorescences that appear in late summer. The plant thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it suitable for xeriscaping and erosion control. Its foliage is narrow and grass-like, with leaves that can be green or tinged with red or purple.

Panicum pungens is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its drought tolerance and attractive fall color.
