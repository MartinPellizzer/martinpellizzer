Plantago coronopus subsp. occidentalis

Plantago coronopus subsp.

occidentalis, commonly known as western plantain, is a perennial herb native to western North America. It typically grows in moist, disturbed habitats such as meadows, stream banks, and roadsides.

The plant has broad, lance-shaped leaves with a hairy surface and produces small, greenish-white flowers in spikes. Its seeds are enclosed in capsule-like structures that release tiny seeds when mature.

This subspecies is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional herbal remedies.
