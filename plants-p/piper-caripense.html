Piper caripense

Piper caripense, commonly known as the Amazonian pepper or wild pepper, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes over 3,000 species of flowering plants. The plant is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and small, heart-shaped leaves. Its flowers are typically greenish-white and are borne in clusters, while the fruit is a small, three-lobed berry.

Piper caripense is often found in humid, lowland forests and is valued for its aromatic properties.
