Paphiopedilum angustifolium

Paphiopedilum angustifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leafed lady's slipper orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, slipper-shaped pouch that serves as a pollination trap. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments with rich, well-drained soil. Its leaves are narrow and elongated, often with a waxy coating.

It produces fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, making it a popular choice for horticultural collections.
