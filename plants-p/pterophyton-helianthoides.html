Pterophyton helianthoides

Pterophyton helianthoides, commonly known as the sunflower fern, is a unique plant that exhibits characteristics of both ferns and flowering plants.

It belongs to the order Pterophyta, which includes true ferns, but it also displays floral structures, making it an intriguing subject in botanical studies. The plant has feather-like fronds that are covered in small, spore-producing structures called sporangia. Its ability to produce both spores and flowers is a rare trait, reflecting its transitional position in evolutionary terms.

This plant is often found in moist, shaded environments, where it thrives due to its efficient water absorption and photosynthetic adaptations.
