Pellaea crenulans

Pellaea crenulans, commonly known as the serrated maidenhair fern, is a species of fern native to South America.

It belongs to the family Pellaeaceae and is characterized by its delicate, feathery fronds with serrated edges. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forests or rocky crevices. Its rhizomes are creeping and produce upright, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silky hair.

Pellaea crenulans is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
