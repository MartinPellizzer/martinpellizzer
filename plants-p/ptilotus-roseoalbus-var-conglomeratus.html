Ptilotus roseoalbus var. conglomeratus

Ptilotus roseoalbus var. conglomeratus, commonly known as the clustered spinifex, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit. The plant produces small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn. Its foliage is typically covered in fine, hair-like structures, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in sandy soils.
