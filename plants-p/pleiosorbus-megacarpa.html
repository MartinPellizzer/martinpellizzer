Pleiosorbus megacarpa

Pleiosorbus megacarpa, commonly known as the giant holly-leaf buckthorn, is a shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, dark berries that mature in the fall. The plant has compound leaves with a serrated margin and a distinctive, fragrant aroma. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
