Paepalanthus planifolius var. globulifer

Paepalanthus planifolius var. globulifer, commonly known as the globular puya, is a striking plant native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, globular inflorescence composed of numerous small, colorful flowers arranged in a spherical shape. The plant produces striking red, orange, or yellow blooms that are highly visible against its dark green foliage. It thrives in high-altitude environments with cool temperatures and well-drained soil.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens and alpine settings.
