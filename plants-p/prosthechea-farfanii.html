Prosthechea farfanii

Prosthechea farfanii, commonly known as the Farfan's orchid, is a species of epiphytic orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Orchidaceae and is characterized by its pendulous, hairy pseudobulbs and long, linear leaves. The plant produces strikingly colorful, spurred flowers that bloom in clusters, often in shades of yellow, orange, and red. It thrives in humid environments and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.

Prosthechea farfanii is appreciated for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in greenhouses or as a houseplant.
