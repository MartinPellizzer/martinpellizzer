Prunus laurocerasus var. otinii

Prunus laurocerasus var. otinii, commonly known as the Turkish cherry or Otinii laurel, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to the eastern Mediterranean.

It features glossy, dark green, elliptical leaves that are arranged in dense clusters, giving it a lush, ornamental appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers in the spring, followed by red berries that are edible but often used as ornamental features. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for hedging and ornamental gardening.

Its adaptability and aesthetic appeal make it a valuable addition to diverse landscapes.
