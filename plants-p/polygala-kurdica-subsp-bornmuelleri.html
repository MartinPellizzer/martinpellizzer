Polygala kurdica subsp. bornmuelleri

Polygala kurdica subsp.

bornmuelleri, commonly known as the Kurdish milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Middle East and Central Asia. It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers.

The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. Its leaves are usually narrow and hairy, which helps reduce water loss.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae, known for its diverse and often ornamental species.
