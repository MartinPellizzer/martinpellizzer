Paeonia corallina subsp. coriacea

Paeonia corallina subsp.

coriacea, commonly known as the coral peony, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its upright, branched stems and large, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and red.

The leaves are compound, with elongated, serrated leaflets, and the plant produces distinctive, woody, coral-like seed pods. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a striking addition to rock gardens and alpine landscapes.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to bloom reliably in suitable climates.
