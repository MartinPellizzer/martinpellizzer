Panicum crus-galli var. longisetum

Panicum crus-galli var. longisetum, commonly known as red panicgrass, is a variant of the panicgrass species native to various regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and its distinctive reddish-purple inflorescence, which sets it apart from other panicgrass varieties. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as fields, roadsides, and waste areas, thriving in full sun. Its foliage is narrow and grass-like, with long, stiff hairs along the stem and leaves, contributing to its common name "longisetum.".

This variant is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its attractive appearance and adaptability to different soil conditions.
