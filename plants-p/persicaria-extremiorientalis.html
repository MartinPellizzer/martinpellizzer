Persicaria extremiorientalis

Persicaria extremiorientalis, commonly known as the eastern Asian knotweed, is a flowering plant native to regions of East Asia.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, which includes plants known for their distinctive leaf shapes and flowering structures. The plant features upright, branching stems that are often hairy, and its leaves are typically lance-shaped with a smooth or slightly toothed margin. It produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in late summer.

Persicaria extremiorientalis is often found in moist, disturbed soils along streambanks and in woodland edges.
