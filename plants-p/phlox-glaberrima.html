Phlox glaberrima

Phlox glaberrima, commonly known as the smooth phlox, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 feet. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of fragrant, pink to purple flowers in the summer months. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Phlox glaberrima is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens and landscapes.
