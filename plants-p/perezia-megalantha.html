Perezia megalantha

Perezia megalantha, commonly known as the giant perezia, is a striking member of the Acanthaceae family.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and thrives in humid, tropical environments. The plant is characterized by its large, showy flowers that can reach up to 10 centimeters in diameter, often in shades of red, orange, or yellow. Its foliage consists of broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs.

Perezia megalantha is typically grown as a container plant due to its specific humidity and light requirements.
