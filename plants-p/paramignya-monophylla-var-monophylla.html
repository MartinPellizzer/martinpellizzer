Paramignya monophylla var. monophylla

Paramignya monophylla var. monophylla, commonly known as the single-leaf pomegranate, is a unique tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its single, large, leathery leaf that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that eventually develop into red, fleshy fruits similar to pomegranates. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and exotic species in horticulture.
