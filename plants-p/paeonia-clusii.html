Paeonia clusii

Paeonia clusii, commonly known as the Clusii peony, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from white to deep pink, often with a distinctive red or purple eye. The plant features compound, trifoliate leaves that are typically heart-shaped and covered in a fine layer of down. It blooms in late spring to early summer, producing fragrant flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Paeonia clusii is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
