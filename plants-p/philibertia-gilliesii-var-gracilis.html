Philibertia gilliesii var. gracilis

Philibertia gilliesii var. gracilis, commonly known as the slender filbert.

It is a flowering plant native to South Australia, characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its botanical features make it a valuable addition to native plant collections and ecological restoration projects.
