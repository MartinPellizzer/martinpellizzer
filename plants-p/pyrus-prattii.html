Pyrus prattii

Pyrus prattii, commonly known as the Chinese white pear, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and alternate, simple leaves that are elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, white flowers in clusters during spring, followed by edible, sweet fruits that are similar to pears.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit production, thriving in temperate climates.
