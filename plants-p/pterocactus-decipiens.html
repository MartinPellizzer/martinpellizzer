Pterocactus decipiens

Pterocactus decipiens, commonly known as the deceptive pterocactus, is a species of cactus native to the arid regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Cactaceae and is characterized by its cylindrical, ribbed stems that are typically green and spiny. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, adding a striking contrast to its rugged appearance. It is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments with minimal water, storing moisture in its fleshy stems.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is often cultivated in succulent gardens and botanical collections.
