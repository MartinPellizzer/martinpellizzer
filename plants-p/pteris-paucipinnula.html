Pteris paucipinnula

Pteris paucipinnula, commonly known as the few-pinnate water fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Pteridaceae.

It is characterized by its compact, rosette-like fronds that are typically green and glossy. The plant produces sporangia on the underside of its fronds, which are clustered in sori, a typical feature of ferns. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Pteris paucipinnula is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
