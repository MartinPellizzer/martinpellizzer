Phalangium liliastrum

Phalangium liliastrum, commonly known as the silverleaf phalangium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It features slender, upright stems that are often covered in a fine layer of silvery hair, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or wetland habitats.
