Prunus jamasakura f. pallida

Prunus jamasakura f. pallida, commonly known as the pale-flowered Japanese cherry, is a botanical variant of the Japanese cherry tree.

It is characterized by its pale pink to almost white flowers, which bloom in early spring. The plant is a deciduous shrub or small tree, typically reaching heights of 3 to 6 meters. Its leaves are ovate with a pointed tip and a serrated margin, turning yellow in autumn.

It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens and parks.
