Pisum sativum var. navale

Pisum sativum var. navale, commonly known as the Navarre pea, is a variety of the common pea plant.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and is often cultivated as a ornamental or decorative plant. The foliage is typically dark green, and the flowers are usually a mix of white and violet hues. This variety is well-suited for container gardening due to its smaller size.

It thrives in cool climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
