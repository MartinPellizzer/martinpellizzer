Pouteria marginata var. impressa

Pouteria marginata var. impressa, commonly known as the Brazilian nut tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is characterized by its large, leathery leaves and produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The tree is notable for its hard, woody fruit, which contains edible seeds known as Brazil nuts. These seeds are rich in oils and are commercially valuable.

The plant plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, supporting various wildlife species.
