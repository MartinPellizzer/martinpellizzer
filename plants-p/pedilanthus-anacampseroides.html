Pedilanthus anacampseroides

Pedilanthus anacampseroides, commonly known as the red sandalwood or bloodwood, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its woody, often branched stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces yellowish or orange flowers that are typically followed by distinctive, fleshy, red fruits. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and often have a waxy texture.

The species is valued for its hard, durable wood, which has been traditionally used in crafting and as a source of medicinal compounds.
