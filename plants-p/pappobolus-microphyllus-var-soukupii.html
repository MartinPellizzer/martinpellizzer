Pappobolus microphyllus var. soukupii

Pappobolus microphyllus var. soukupii, commonly known as the narrow-leaved burrweed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its small, narrow leaves and distinctive burr-like fruits. The plant produces yellowish to brownish flowers that are clustered in heads, typical of the daisy family. Its fruiting structures, called pappus, are adapted for wind dispersal, aiding in its spread across arid and semi-arid environments.

The species is often found in disturbed soils and is considered a weed in agricultural settings.
