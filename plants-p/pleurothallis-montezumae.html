Pleurothallis montezumae

Pleurothallis montezumae, commonly known as the Montezuma's orchid, is a species native to the cloud forests of Central America.

It is a small, epiphytic orchid that typically grows on trees and rocks in humid, shaded environments. The plant features delicate, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink. It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its intricate, often showy inflorescences.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in the wild.
