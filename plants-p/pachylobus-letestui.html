Pachylobus letestui

Pachylobus letestui, commonly known as the golden star vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, resembling stars, hence its common name. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and a woody stem that supports its climbing growth habit. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in forest understories.

Botanically, it belongs to the Rubiaceae family and is valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture.
