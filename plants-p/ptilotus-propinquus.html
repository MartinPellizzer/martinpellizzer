Ptilotus propinquus

Ptilotus propinquus, commonly known as the desert flax, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of Australia.

It is a low-growing perennial shrub with narrow, grass-like leaves and produces showy, spike-like inflorescences. The flowers are typically yellow or orange, and they bloom during the warmer months. The plant is adapted to survive in harsh, dry conditions, with a deep root system and reduced leaf surface to minimize water loss.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small fauna in its native environment.
