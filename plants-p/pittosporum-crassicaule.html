Pittosporum crassicaule

Pittosporum crassicaule, commonly known as the broad-leaved pittosporum, is a shrub or small tree native to New Zealand.

It features broad, glossy, dark green leaves that are elliptical in shape and often have a waxy texture. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers followed by bright orange berries that are edible for birds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and lowland areas.

Pittosporum crassicaule is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
