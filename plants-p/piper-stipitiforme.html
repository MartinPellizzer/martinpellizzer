Piper stipitiforme

Piper stipitiforme, commonly known as the hairy pipevine, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Piperaceae, which includes many species of flowering plants known for their aromatic properties. The plant is characterized by its climbing habit and heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive hairy texture. Its flowers are small and greenish-white, arranged in clusters, and it produces small, round berries that are typically red when mature.

Piper stipitiforme is often found in humid, shaded environments such as forests and along riverbanks.
