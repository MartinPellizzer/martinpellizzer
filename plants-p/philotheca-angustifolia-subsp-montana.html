Philotheca angustifolia subsp. montana

Philotheca angustifolia subsp.

montana, commonly known as mountain narrow-leafed philotheca, is a shrub native to southeastern Australia. It features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy, with a distinctive greyish-green coloration.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to temperate to subtropical climates.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native gardens.
