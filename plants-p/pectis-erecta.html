Pectis erecta

Pectis erecta, commonly known as the upright pectis, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features upright, branching stems covered in fine hairs, and its leaves are linear with a slightly hairy surface. Its flowers are small and yellow, arranged in dense clusters, giving it a daisy-like appearance.

Pectis erecta is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments.
