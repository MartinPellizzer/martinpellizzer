Primula larsenii

Primula larsenii, commonly known as the Larsen's primrose, is a species of flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink and white. The plant has hairy, oval-shaped leaves that are often clustered at the base, and it thrives in cool, moist environments.

Primula larsenii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with well-drained soil and partial shade.
