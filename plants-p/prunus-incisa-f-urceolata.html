Prunus incisa f. urceolata

Prunus incisa f. urceolata, commonly known as the Japanese maple with heart-shaped leaves, is a compact, ornamental shrub native to Japan.

It features striking, lobed leaves that are dark green on top and silvery white underneath, creating a dramatic visual contrast. The plant produces small, pale pink flowers in early spring, followed by red or orange berries in late summer. It thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, making it suitable for garden borders or container growing.

Its unique foliage makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and landscape design.
