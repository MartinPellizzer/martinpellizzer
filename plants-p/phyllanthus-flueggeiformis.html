Phyllanthus flueggeiformis

Phyllanthus flueggeiformis, commonly known as the African date palm, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is a small evergreen shrub or tree that typically grows to a height of about 3 to 5 meters. The plant features opposite, simple, and often elliptical leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. Its flowers are small and clustered in dense inflorescences, and it produces small, fleshy fruits that are edible.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
