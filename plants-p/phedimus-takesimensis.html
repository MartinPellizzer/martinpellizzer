Phedimus takesimensis

Phedimus takesimensis, commonly known as the Korean rock白菜, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Korea.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its rosette-forming leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white hairs. The plant produces small, daisy-like yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in shaded or partially shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and rockeries.
