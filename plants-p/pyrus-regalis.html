Pyrus regalis

Pyrus regalis, commonly known as the common whitebeam, is a deciduous tree native to Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its oval, dark green leaves that turn yellow in autumn. The tree produces small, white flowers in spring, followed by edible berries that are used in jams and jellies. Pyrus regalis is often found in woodlands and along riverbanks, thriving in a variety of soil types.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical significance in traditional medicine.
