Polygonum pamiricum

Polygonum pamiricum, commonly known as the Pamir knotweed, is a perennial herb native to the high altitudes of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, characterized by its distinctive nodes and often alternate leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its stems are often reddish or purple, and it produces small, clustered flowers in late summer.

Polygonum pamiricum is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
