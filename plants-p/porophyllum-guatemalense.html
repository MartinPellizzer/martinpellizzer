Porophyllum guatemalense

Porophyllum guatemalense, commonly known as Guatemalan blueweed, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and Central America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are usually grayish-green with a fuzzy texture, and it produces small blue or purple flowers.

It is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and antispasmodic properties.
