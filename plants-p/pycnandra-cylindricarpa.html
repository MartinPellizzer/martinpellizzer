Pycnandra cylindricarpa

Pycnandra cylindricarpa, commonly known as the narrow-fruit pandanus, is a flowering plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It belongs to the family Pandanaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of narrow, sword-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into elongated, cylindrical fruits. It thrives in coastal and wetland environments, often growing in brackish or saline soils.

The species is valued for its ornamental appearance and is sometimes used in traditional crafts and landscaping.
