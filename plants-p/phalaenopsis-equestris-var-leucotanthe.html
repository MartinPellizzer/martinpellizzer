Phalaenopsis equestris var. leucotanthe

Phalaenopsis equestris var. leucotanthe, commonly known as the white phalaenopsis orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its elegant, broad leaves and clusters of white flowers that resemble moths, hence its common name. The plant is epiphytic, meaning it grows on other plants without being parasitic, and thrives in humid, warm environments. It produces long-lasting blooms that can last several months, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

The variety is prized for its simplicity and purity of color, which adds to its ornamental value.
