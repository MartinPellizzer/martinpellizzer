Prestonia venosa

Prestonia venosa, commonly known as the purple morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and northern South America.

It is characterized by its striking violet to purple flowers that bloom in the early morning. The plant has heart-shaped, velvety leaves and produces capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing as a vine or shrub.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.
