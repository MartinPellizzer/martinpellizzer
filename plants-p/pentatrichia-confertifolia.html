Pentatrichia confertifolia

Pentatrichia confertifolia, commonly known as the dense-leaved grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often densely packed. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny florets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands.

Its botanical features make it an important component of local ecosystems and a subject of study in plant taxonomy.
