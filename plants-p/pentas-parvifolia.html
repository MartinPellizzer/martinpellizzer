Pentas parvifolia

Pentas parvifolia, commonly known as the small-flowered pentas, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, star-shaped flowers that come in various colors such as pink, red, and white. The plant typically grows as a shrub or subshrub, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Pentas parvifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a bedding plant.
