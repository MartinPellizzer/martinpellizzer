Pachyphyllum confusum

Pachyphyllum confusum, commonly known as the confused thickleaf cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems that store water, a feature typical of cacti adapted to dry environments. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers during the cooler months, adding ornamental value. Its leaves are modified into spines, reducing water loss and providing protection from herbivores.

Pachyphyllum confusum is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
