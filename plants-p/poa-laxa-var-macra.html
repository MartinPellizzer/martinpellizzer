Poa laxa var. macra

Poa laxa var. macra, commonly known as the tall meadow grass, is a perennial species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems that can reach heights of up to 1 meter. The leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue, and the plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It thrives in open, sunny habitats such as meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
