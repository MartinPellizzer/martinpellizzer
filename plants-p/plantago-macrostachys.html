Plantago macrostachys

Plantago macrostachys, commonly known as the large-stalked plantain, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It features erect, branching stems that can grow up to 1 meter in height, covered with dense, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in dense clusters at the ends of the stems. Its leaves are typically used in traditional medicine for their soothing properties.

The species is adapted to grow in a variety of habitats, including grasslands and disturbed soils.
