Polygonum praetermissum

Polygonum praetermissum, commonly known as the common knotweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, characterized by its distinctive arrowhead-shaped leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, disturbed soils along streambanks and roadsides. Its stems are often reddish and can become woody with age, while its leaves are usually smooth and lanceolate.

Polygonum praetermissum is known for its ability to spread rapidly, making it both a common weed and a subject of ecological study.
