Pitcairnia karwinskyana

Pitcairnia karwinskyana, commonly known as the Karwinsky's pitcairnia, is a species of bromeliad native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that form a central tank to collect rainwater. The plant produces striking inflorescences with bright red flowers that bloom in the spring. It thrives in shaded, humid environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
