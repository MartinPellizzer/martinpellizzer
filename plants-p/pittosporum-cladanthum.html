Pittosporum cladanthum

Pittosporum cladanthum, commonly known as the mountain pittosporum, is a shrub or small tree native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and fragrant, white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has smooth, greyish bark and produces small, orange-red berries that are edible for birds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Pittosporum cladanthum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in native habitats.
