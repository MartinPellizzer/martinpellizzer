Piper grande

Piper grande, commonly known as the large pepper plant, is a species native to tropical regions of South America.

It is a climbing vine belonging to the Piperaceae family, characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and small, greenish-white flowers. The plant produces small, peppery berries that are used in traditional medicine and cuisine. Its botanical features include a woody stem, opposite leaf arrangement, and a distinctive aromatic scent.

Piper grande is valued for its medicinal properties and is often cultivated for its unique flavor profile.
