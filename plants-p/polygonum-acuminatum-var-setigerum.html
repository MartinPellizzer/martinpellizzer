Polygonum acuminatum var. setigerum

Polygonum acuminatum var. setigerum, commonly known as the hairy smartweed, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes and stream banks. The plant has slender, erect stems that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture, while the flowers are small, clustered in dense spikes.

This species is known for its adaptability and is often found in disturbed soils and along waterways.
