Prunus prostrata var. glabrifolia

Prunus prostrata var. glabrifolia, commonly known as the smoothleaf Japanese cherry, is a low-growing shrub native to Japan.

It features slender, arching stems that form a dense, mat-like growth habit. The plant produces small, white flowers in early spring, followed by bright red berries in late summer. Its leaves are elliptical, smooth, and turn a vibrant red in autumn.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its attractive foliage and seasonal blooms.
