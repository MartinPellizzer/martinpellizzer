Persea amplexicaulis var. amplifolia

Persea amplexicaulis var. amplifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved persimmon, is a variant of the amplexicaulis persimmon native to Central America.

It is characterized by its broad, fleshy leaves that are often more than twice as wide as they are long. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into orange to red, fleshy fruits. Its leaves are typically arranged in a spiral pattern along the stems, and the plant thrives in tropical and subtropical climates.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and edible fruit, which is rich in nutrients.
