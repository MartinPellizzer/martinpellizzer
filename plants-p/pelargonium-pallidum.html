Pelargonium pallidum

Pelargonium pallidum, commonly known as the pale geranium, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Geraniaceae family and is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves and showy, five-petaled flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its flowers come in shades of pink, red, and white, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Pelargonium pallidum is also valued for its drought tolerance and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
