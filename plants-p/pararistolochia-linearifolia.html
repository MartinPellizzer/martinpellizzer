Pararistolochia linearifolia

Pararistolochia linearifolia, commonly known as the slender false hogweed, is a climbing vine native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It features heart-shaped, palmately lobed leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces distinctive, horseshoe-shaped flowers that are typically greenish-yellow in color. Its fruit is a slender, ribbed follicle that contains several seeds.

Due to its toxic properties, it is considered a potentially harmful plant in its natural habitat.
