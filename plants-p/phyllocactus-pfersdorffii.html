Phyllocactus pfersdorffii

Phyllocactus pfersdorffii, commonly known as the feather cactus, is a unique cactus species native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its long, thin, leaf-like stems that resemble feathers, which are actually modified stems rather than true leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the summer months, adding a striking visual contrast to its green, feathery structure. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires bright, indirect light to flourish.

Due to its distinctive appearance, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant in collections of unusual succulents.
