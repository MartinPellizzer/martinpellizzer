Passiflora grandiflora

Passiflora grandiflora, commonly known as the giant passionflower, is a climbing vine native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that display a striking combination of purple, blue, and white petals, along with intricate filaments and a crown of red filaments. The plant produces edible berries that are rich in antioxidants and often used in culinary applications. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a velvety texture, while its stems are woody and covered in fine hairs.

Passiflora grandiflora thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
