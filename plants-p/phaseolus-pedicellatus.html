Phaseolus pedicellatus

Phaseolus pedicellatus, commonly known as the scarlet runner bean, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its climbing habit, with hairy stems and compound leaves. The plant produces vibrant red or orange flowers that are followed by elongated, edible pods containing kidney-shaped seeds. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This species is valued for both its ornamental appeal and its use in culinary applications.
