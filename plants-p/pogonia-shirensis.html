Pogonia shirensis

Pogonia shirensis, commonly known as the Shirensis Pink, is a rare orchid species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking pinkish-purple flowers and is often found in moist, acidic habitats such as bogs and pine flatwoods. The plant has a slender, erect stem with a single or few flowers at the top, each featuring a distinctive hood-like lip. It relies on specific mycorrhizal fungi for nutrient uptake, which is crucial for its survival.

Due to habitat loss and limited distribution, Pogonia shirensis is considered endangered and requires conservation efforts to ensure its survival.
