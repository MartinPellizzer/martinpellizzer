Prunus mutabilis f. speciosa

Prunus mutabilis f. speciosa, commonly known as the double-flowered cherry, is a cultivar of the Prunus mutabilis species.

It is characterized by its showy, double flowers that bloom in early spring, often in shades of white, pink, and red. The plant is a deciduous shrub or small tree that thrives in temperate climates. Its foliage turns vibrant shades of orange and red in the fall, adding to its ornamental value.

It is widely cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal and seasonal interest.
