Pitcairnia affinis

Pitcairnia affinis, commonly known as the velvet bromeliad, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that form a water-holding cup. The plant features vibrant, showy inflorescences that can last for several months. Its leaves are thick and waxy, adapted to retain moisture in its natural habitat.

Pitcairnia affinis is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking appearance and relatively low maintenance requirements.
