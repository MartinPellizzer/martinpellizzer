Panax japonicus f. lancifolius

Panax japonicus f. lancifolius, commonly known as the Japanese ginseng or Korean ginseng, is a variant of the ginseng plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and distinctive root structure, which are often used in traditional medicine. The plant belongs to the Araliaceae family and is valued for its adaptogenic properties. It thrives in cool, temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Its botanical features make it a subject of interest in both horticulture and pharmacological research.
