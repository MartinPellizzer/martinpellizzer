Potentilla subacaulis var. albicans

Potentilla subacaulis var. albicans, commonly known as the white-flowered cinquefoil, is a perennial herb native to alpine and subalpine regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its slender, often hairy stems and small, clustered white or pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments. Its leaves are alternate, with five to seven leaflets that are usually narrow and serrated.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate, persistent blooms.
