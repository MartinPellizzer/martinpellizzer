Peristylus bulleyi

Peristylus bulleyi, commonly known as the New Zealand broom, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 2 meters, and thrives in well-drained soils. It is known for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility.

However, it is also considered an invasive species in some regions outside its native habitat.
