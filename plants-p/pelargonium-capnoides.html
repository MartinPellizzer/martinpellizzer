Pelargonium capnoides

Pelargonium capnoides, commonly known as the Cape geranium, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Geraniaceae family and is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit. The plant produces small, showy flowers in shades of pink and red, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are typically rounded and slightly hairy, and it thrives in well-drained soil with full sun exposure.

Pelargonium capnoides is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance and ease of care.
