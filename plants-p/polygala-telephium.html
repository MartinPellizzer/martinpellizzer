Polygala telephium

Polygala telephium, commonly known as the heart's-ease, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, often pink or red, spurred flowers. The plant typically grows in grasslands and open woodlands, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are alternate and lance-shaped, while its stems are often hairy.

It is a herbaceous perennial that blooms in late spring to early summer.
