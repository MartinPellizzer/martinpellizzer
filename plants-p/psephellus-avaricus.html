Psephellus avaricus

Psephellus avaricus, commonly known as the African orchid or the red orchid, is a species of orchid native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking red and green flowers, which bloom during the summer months. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in well-drained conditions. It has a compact growth habit with a pseudobulb that stores water and nutrients.

Psephellus avaricus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
