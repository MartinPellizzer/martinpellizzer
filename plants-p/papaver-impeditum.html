Papaver impeditum

Papaver impeditum, commonly known as the stammering poppy, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, vibrant red petals that surround a central cluster of orange anthers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, adding a vivid splash of color to its natural habitat.

The species is noted for its medicinal properties, though it is also considered a potential weed in agricultural settings.
