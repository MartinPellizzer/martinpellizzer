Prunus mutabilis f. marginata

Prunus mutabilis f. marginata, commonly known as the pink-leafed cherry or the variable cherry, is a cultivar of the Prunus genus.

It is characterized by its striking, variegated foliage that transitions from white and pink to deep red as the seasons change. The plant produces small, white or pink flowers in early spring, followed by edible cherries that are typically small and tart. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil and full sun exposure.

This ornamental tree is valued for its unique leaf coloration and seasonal beauty in gardens and landscapes.
