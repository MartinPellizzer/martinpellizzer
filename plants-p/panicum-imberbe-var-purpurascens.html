Panicum imberbe var. purpurascens

Panicum imberbe var. purpurascens, commonly known as purple lovegrass, is a warm-season grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and distinctive purple tinge on the foliage, which becomes more pronounced during the growing season. The plant produces fluffy, pale green to brown seed heads that are often used for ornamental purposes in gardens and landscapes. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it a popular choice for erosion control and wildlife habitat.

Its drought tolerance and low maintenance requirements further enhance its value in both ecological and aesthetic applications.
