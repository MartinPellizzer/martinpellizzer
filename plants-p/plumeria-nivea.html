Plumeria nivea

Plumeria nivea, commonly known as the white frangipani, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, waxy flowers that range in color from white to pink and yellow. The plant has simple, opposite, ovate leaves that are usually dark green and glossy. Its flowers bloom in clusters and are often used in floral arrangements due to their beauty and fragrance.

The plant is well adapted to warm climates and requires full sun to thrive.
