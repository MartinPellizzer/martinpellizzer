Pelargonium cordifolium

Pelargonium cordifolium, commonly known as the heart-leaf geranium, is a species of flowering plant in the family Geraniaceae.

It is native to South Africa and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows as a herbaceous perennial, often reaching heights of up to 30 cm. Its flowers come in shades of red, pink, and white, blooming throughout the spring and summer seasons.

Pelargonium cordifolium is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of garden conditions.
