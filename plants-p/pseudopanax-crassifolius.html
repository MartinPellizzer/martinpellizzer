Pseudopanax crassifolius

Pseudopanax crassifolius, commonly known as the silver fern, is a unique plant native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its distinctive silver-colored leaves, which are composed of overlapping, shield-like segments. The plant is a member of the Araucariaceae family and is known for its slow growth and longevity. It thrives in temperate rainforests and is often found in the understorey of native forests.

The silver fern is also notable for its ability to regenerate after disturbances such as fires.
