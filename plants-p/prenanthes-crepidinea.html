Prenanthes crepidinea

Prenanthes crepidinea, commonly known as the slender burrweed, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, spiky, bur-like fruits that resemble small, inflated spines. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and wetlands. Its leaves are heart-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture.

The species is noted for its ecological importance, providing food and habitat for various insects and small animals.
