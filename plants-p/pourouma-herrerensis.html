Pourouma herrerensis

Pourouma herrerensis, commonly known as the Herrer's areca, is a member of the Arecaceae family, native to the Amazon Basin.

It is a tall, slender palm that can grow up to 10 meters in height, with pinnate leaves that are often used in traditional crafts. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in inflorescences, followed by edible, fibrous fruits. Its trunk is characterized by a smooth, pale bark and is often used for construction and thatching in local communities.

The plant plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
