Parsonsia reflexa

Parsonsia reflexa, commonly known as the night-flowering glorybower, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to the Pacific Islands and northern Australia.

It is characterized by its showy, fragrant, tubular flowers that open at night, attracting moths and other nocturnal pollinators. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its fruit is a fleshy, red or orange berry that contains numerous seeds.

Parsonsia reflexa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
