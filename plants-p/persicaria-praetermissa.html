Persicaria praetermissa

Persicaria praetermissa, commonly known as the slender smartweed, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a herbaceous annual or biennial species that typically grows in moist to wet environments such as wetlands, ditches, and stream banks. The plant features alternate, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters. Its seeds are dispersed by wind, contributing to its ability to colonize new areas.

Persicaria praetermissa is often found in disturbed soils and can be an indicator of wetland conditions.
