Piper subcinereum var. parvifolium

Piper subcinereum var. parvifolium, commonly known as the small-leaf pepper plant, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, ovate leaves with a glossy surface. The plant produces tiny, pale green to yellowish flowers that develop into small, round berries. It is valued for its aromatic leaves used in traditional medicine and as a spice.

The varietal name "parvifolium" refers to its small foliage, distinguishing it from other varieties of Piper subcinereum.
