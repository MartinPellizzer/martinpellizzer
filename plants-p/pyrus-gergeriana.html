Pyrus gergeriana

Pyrus gergeriana, commonly known as the Persian whitebeam, is a deciduous tree native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its small, white flowers that bloom in early spring. The tree produces small, edible fruits that are similar in appearance to apples but are typically less commonly consumed by humans. Its leaves are elliptical with serrated edges, and the bark is smooth and grayish in color.

Pyrus gergeriana is often found in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions.
