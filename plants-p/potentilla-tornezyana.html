Potentilla tornezyana

Potentilla tornezyana, commonly known as the Torneza cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit. The plant produces small, showy yellow or orange flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are typically compound, consisting of five leaflets, which is a distinguishing feature.

Potentilla tornezyana is often found in rocky or alpine environments, where it thrives in well-drained soils.
