Piper subflavum var. longipedunculatum

Piper subflavum var. longipedunculatum, commonly known as the long peduncle pepper, is a variant of the Piper subflavum species native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its elongated peduncles, which are the stalks supporting its small, clustered flowers. The plant produces small, yellowish-green berries that mature to a dark red color, containing a spicy, aromatic compound similar to other Piper species. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for horticultural and pharmacological study.
