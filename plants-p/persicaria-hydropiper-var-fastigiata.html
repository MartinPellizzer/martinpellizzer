Persicaria hydropiper var. fastigiata

Persicaria hydropiper var. fastigiata, commonly known as the narrowleaf smartweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to North America.

It features upright, slender stems that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, pink to purple flowers in dense clusters, typically blooming from mid-summer to early fall. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often with a reddish or purplish hue, especially along the veins.

This variety is adapted to moist environments and is often found in wet meadows, stream banks, and other damp habitats.
