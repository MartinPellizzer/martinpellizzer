Picris cyprica

Picris cyprica, commonly known as Cyprus goldenweed, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. It produces yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in arid environments.
