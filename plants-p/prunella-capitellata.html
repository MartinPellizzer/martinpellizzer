Prunella capitellata

Prunella capitellata, commonly known as narrow-leaved selfheal, is a perennial herb native to Europe, North Africa, and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is characterized by its upright, branched stems and small, tubular blue or violet flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and slightly serrated along the edges. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and woodland edges.

Prunella capitellata is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally for its healing and anti-inflammatory benefits.
