Phlox ovata var. latifolia

Phlox ovata var. latifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved phlox, is a perennial flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It features showy, pink to lavender flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has opposite, broad, ovate leaves that are often hairy beneath. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable addition to native plant landscapes.
