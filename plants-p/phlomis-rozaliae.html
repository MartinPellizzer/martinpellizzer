Phlomis rozaliae

Phlomis rozaliae, commonly known as the Turkish rocket or mountain phlomis, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features erect, branched stems that are often covered in soft, grayish-white hairs. The plant produces showy, clustered spikes of pink to violet flowers during the summer months. Its leaves are lance-shaped, with serrated edges, and are typically covered in a dense layer of fine hairs.

Phlomis rozaliae is well adapted to rocky and dry environments, making it a hardy and drought-tolerant species.
