Pittosporum longisepalum

Pittosporum longisepalum, commonly known as the long-sepaled pittosporum, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of fragrant, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its flowers are followed by small, spherical berries that are edible but not particularly palatable.

Pittosporum longisepalum thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and subalpine regions.
