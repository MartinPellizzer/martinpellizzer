Papaver anjuicum

Papaver anjuicum, commonly known as the Persian poppy, is a herbaceous annual plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, large, showy flowers that range in color from deep red to orange and yellow. The plant has smooth, hairless stems and broad, lance-shaped leaves that are alternate along the stem. Its flowers typically bloom in late spring to early summer, and it produces capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Papaver anjuicum is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
