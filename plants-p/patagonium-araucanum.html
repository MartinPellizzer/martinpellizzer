Patagonium araucanum

Patagonium araucanum, commonly known as the Andean mountain holly, is a shrub native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, clustered flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant thrives in cool, moist climates and is often found in cloud forests and alpine meadows. Its leaves are typically leathery and have a distinctive aromatic scent.

Patagonium araucanum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
