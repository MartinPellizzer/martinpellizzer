Pyrus camschatcensis

Pyrus camschatcensis, commonly known as the Kamchatka pear, is a deciduous tree native to the Russian Far East.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves and white to pinkish flowers that bloom in spring. The tree produces small, edible pears that are typically harvested in late summer. It prefers cool, moist climates and is often found in mountainous regions.

Its ornamental value and historical significance in botany make it a notable species in horticulture.
