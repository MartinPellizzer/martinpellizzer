Prunella vulgaris var. iodocalyx

Prunella vulgaris var. iodocalyx, commonly known as sea holly, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has silver-gray foliage and thrives in sandy or rocky soils, often growing in maritime environments. It is a perennial herb that spreads via rhizomes and is known for its hardy nature.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional medicinal uses.
