Pulsatilla cernua var. plena

Pulsatilla cernua var. plena, commonly known as the showy pasqueflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its dense, fluffy blooms that appear in early spring. The plant features a basal rosette of hairy, lobed leaves and produces showy, pink to purple flowers with numerous stamens. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens, though it requires specific growing conditions to flourish.
