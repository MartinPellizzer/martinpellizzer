Plantago maris-mortii

Plantago maris-mortii, commonly known as sea plantain, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of the Mediterranean and the Atlantic coast of Europe.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils near the shoreline. The plant features upright stems with lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs. Its small, pale blue or violet flowers are arranged in dense spikes and bloom in the summer months.

It is valued for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in saline environments.
