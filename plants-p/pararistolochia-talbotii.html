Pararistolochia talbotii

Pararistolochia talbotii, commonly known as the Talbot's Dutchman's pipe, is a climbing vine native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive, hollow, pipe-shaped flowers that are often greenish-yellow in color. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and emits a mild, sometimes unpleasant odor. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The genus Pararistolochia is part of the Aristolochiaceae family, known for its unique floral structures and ecological significance.
