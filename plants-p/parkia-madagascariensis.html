Parkia madagascariensis

Parkia madagascariensis, commonly known as the African bean tree, is a tropical leguminous tree native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and small, fragrant yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The tree produces pod-like fruits containing edible seeds, which are often used in local cuisines. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in secondary forests and along riverbanks.

The plant is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, which enriches the soil.
