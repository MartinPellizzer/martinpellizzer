Pleurothallis glanduligera

Pleurothallis glanduligera, commonly known as the glandular pleurothallis, is a species of orchid native to the cloud forests of Central and South America.

It is a small, epiphytic plant that typically grows on trees in humid, shaded environments. The plant features delicate, linear leaves and produces fragrant, showy flowers in shades of white and pink. Its flowers are often clustered in inflorescences and bloom during the wet season.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in the wild.
