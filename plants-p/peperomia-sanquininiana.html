Peperomia sanquininiana

Peperomia sanquininiana, commonly known as the Crimson Peperomia, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to South America.

It features dense, fleshy leaves that are typically dark green with a reddish or maroon hue on the underside. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters, which are followed by spore-bearing structures called sporangia. It thrives in bright, indirect light and prefers well-draining soil with moderate humidity.

Peperomia sanquininiana is valued for its attractive foliage and low maintenance requirements, making it a popular choice for indoor gardens.
