Prunus phaeosticta f. dentigera

Prunus phaeosticta f. dentigera, commonly known as the variegated Japanese maple, is a cultivar of the Prunus genus.

It is characterized by its distinctive variegated leaves that display a combination of green, white, and pink hues. The plant is a small to medium-sized deciduous tree or shrub, typically reaching heights of 6 to 10 feet. Its foliage is highly ornamental, with leaves that change color throughout the seasons, adding visual interest to the landscape.

The plant thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
