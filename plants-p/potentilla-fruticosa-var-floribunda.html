Potentilla fruticosa var. floribunda

Potentilla fruticosa var. floribunda, commonly known as the flowering cinquefoil, is a deciduous shrub native to the northern regions of North America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of small, showy yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant has deeply lobed leaves that are typically hairy and turn red in the fall. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy addition to gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its vibrant fall color and long-lasting floral display.
