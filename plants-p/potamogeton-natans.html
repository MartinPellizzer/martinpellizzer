Potamogeton natans

Potamogeton natans, commonly known as the floating pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments across Europe, Asia, and North America.

It has long, narrow, ribbon-like leaves that are typically arranged in whorls around the stem, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often submerged. The plant is characterized by its ability to float on the water's surface, with its roots anchored in the sediment below. It plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.

Potamogeton natans is also used in phytoremediation efforts due to its capacity to absorb nutrients and heavy metals from the water.
