Plantago coronopus subvar. latior

Plantago coronopus subvar. latior, commonly known as broadleaf plantain, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features a rosette of broad, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish flowers in a spike-like inflorescence, which are wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.

This variety is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
