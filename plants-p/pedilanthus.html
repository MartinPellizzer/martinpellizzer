Pedilanthus

Pedilanthus, commonly known as the "butterfly plant," is a genus of flowering plants in the family Apocynaceae.

These plants are native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. They are characterized by their striking, often colorful flowers that resemble butterflies, which attract pollinators. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and arranged oppositely along the stems.

Pedilanthus species are often cultivated for their ornamental value and unique floral display.
