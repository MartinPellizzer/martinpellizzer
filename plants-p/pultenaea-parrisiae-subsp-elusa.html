Pultenaea parrisiae subsp. elusa

Pultenaea parrisiae subsp.

elusa, commonly known as the narrow-leaved pultenaea, is a shrub native to southeastern Australia. It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and small, yellowish flowers that appear in late winter to early spring.

The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is found in open woodland or heathland habitats. It is a member of the Fabaceae family, known for its nitrogen-fixing abilities.
