Palala corticosa

Palala corticosa, commonly known as the roughleaf palala, is a unique plant species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its thick, bark-like leaves that are covered in a rough, textured surface. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, often in dense forest undergrowth. Its leaves contain high concentrations of certain alkaloids, which have been studied for potential medicinal properties.

Palala corticosa is also notable for its ability to thrive in nutrient-poor soils, making it an important component of its ecosystem.
