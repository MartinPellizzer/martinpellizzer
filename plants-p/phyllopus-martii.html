Phyllopus martii

Phyllopus martii, commonly known as the Mexican evergreen hibiscus, is a tropical shrub native to Central America.

It is characterized by its dark green, glossy leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Phyllopus martii is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
