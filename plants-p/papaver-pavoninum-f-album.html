Papaver pavoninum f. album

Papaver pavoninum f. album, commonly known as the white poppy, is a variant of the Persian poppy characterized by its striking white petals.

It is a herbaceous annual plant native to Central Asia, particularly Iran and Afghanistan. The flowers are large, showy, and bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has a taproot system and thrives in well-drained, sandy soils.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening due to its unique coloration and floral display.
