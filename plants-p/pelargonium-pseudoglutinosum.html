Pelargonium pseudoglutinosum

Pelargonium pseudoglutinosum, commonly known as the sticky-leaved geranium, is a species of flowering plant in the Geraniaceae family.

It is native to South Africa and is characterized by its fleshy, hairy leaves that secrete a sticky substance. The plant produces showy, five-petaled flowers in shades of pink and red, typically blooming in spring and summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Due to its unique appearance and ornamental value, it is often cultivated for both garden aesthetics and horticultural interest.
