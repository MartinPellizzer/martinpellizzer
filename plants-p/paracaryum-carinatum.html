Paracaryum carinatum

Paracaryum carinatum, commonly known as the African violet, is a flowering plant native to eastern Africa.

It is characterized by its rosette of velvety, waxy leaves and clusters of showy, tubular flowers that bloom in shades of purple, pink, and white. The plant thrives in humid, warm environments and prefers indirect sunlight to avoid leaf scorch. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to retain moisture, making it suitable for pot cultivation.

It is widely cultivated as a popular ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance and ease of care.
