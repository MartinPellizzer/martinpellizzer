Piper laevirameum

Piper laevirameum, commonly known as the slender pipevine, is a climbing shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Piperaceae, which includes many species of flowering plants. The plant is characterized by its slender, woody stems and opposite, ovate leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. Its flowers are small, greenish-white, and clustered in spikes, while its fruits are berries that turn red when mature.

Piper laevirameum is often found in humid, shaded environments such as rainforests and is used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
