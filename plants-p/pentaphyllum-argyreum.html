Pentaphyllum argyreum

Pentaphyllum argyreum, commonly known as the silverleaf nightshade, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking silver-gray foliage and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant produces smooth, fleshy berries that are typically yellow or orange when mature. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in some regions.
