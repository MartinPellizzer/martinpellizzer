Polystichum stuebelii

Polystichum stuebelii, commonly known as Stuebel's wood fern, is a species of evergreen fern native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Chile.

It is characterized by its tall, upright fronds that can reach up to 1 meter in length, with a dark green, leathery texture. The fronds are bi-lobed and have a distinctive, intricate pattern of sori on the underside. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in shaded, forested areas.

This fern is valued for its ornamental appeal and is occasionally cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
