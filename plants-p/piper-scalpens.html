Piper scalpens

Piper scalpens, commonly known as the scarlet pipevine, is a climbing perennial plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many species of flowering plants. The plant is characterized by its heart-shaped, glossy leaves and clusters of small, fragrant, greenish-white flowers. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

The plant is also noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its aesthetic appeal.
