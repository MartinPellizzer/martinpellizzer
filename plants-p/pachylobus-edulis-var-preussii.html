Pachylobus edulis var. preussii

Pachylobus edulis var. preussii, commonly known as the Preuss's edible orchid, is a species native to certain regions of South America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its unique, showy flowers. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, often in forest understories. Its leaves are thick and fleshy, adapted to store water in its native habitat.

The flowers, which are often brightly colored, play a role in attracting specific pollinators.
