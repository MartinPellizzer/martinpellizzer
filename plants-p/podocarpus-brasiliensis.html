Podocarpus brasiliensis

Podocarpus brasiliensis, commonly known as the Brazilian podocarp, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It belongs to the Podocarpaceae family and is characterized by its dense, scale-like leaves and cone-bearing structure. The plant exhibits a slow growth rate and can reach heights of up to 30 meters in its natural habitat. It plays an important ecological role in providing habitat and stabilizing soil in its native region.

Due to habitat loss, it is also considered a threatened species in some areas.
