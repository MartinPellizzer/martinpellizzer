Panicum mueense

Panicum mueense, commonly known as the Mueense Panicgrass, is a species of grass native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers.

Due to its ecological role and ornamental potential, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
