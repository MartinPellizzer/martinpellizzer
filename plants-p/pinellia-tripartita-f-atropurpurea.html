Pinellia tripartita f. atropurpurea

Pinellia tripartita f. atropurpurea, commonly known as purple-flowered pinellia, is a variant of the pinellia plant characterized by its striking purple floral hues.

It belongs to the family Araceae and is native to regions of East Asia, including China and Korea. The plant features a rhizomatous root system and produces showy, spadix-bearing inflorescences surrounded by a colorful spathe. Its foliage consists of broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often variegated with white and green patterns.

This ornamental plant is valued for its aesthetic appeal in gardens and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
