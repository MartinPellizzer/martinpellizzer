Pisum sativum var. gloriosum

Pisum sativum var. gloriosum, commonly known as the garden pea, is a flowering plant in the Fabaceae family.

It is characterized by its climbing habit, with tendrils that help it ascend supports. The plant produces clusters of pea-shaped pods that contain several edible seeds. Its flowers are typically white or pink, with a distinctive papilionaceous structure.

The botanical features of this variety make it well-suited for both ornamental and culinary use.
