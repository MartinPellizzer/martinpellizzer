Passiflora kermesina

Passiflora kermesina, commonly known as the crimson passionflower, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful flowers that feature a complex arrangement of petals, sepals, and corona filaments. The plant produces orange-red berries that are edible and contain seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in the Passifloraceae family.
