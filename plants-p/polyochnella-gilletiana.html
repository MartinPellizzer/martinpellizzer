Polyochnella gilletiana

Polyochnella gilletiana, commonly known as the Gillett's bladderwort, is a carnivorous plant native to specific regions in North America.

It is characterized by its small, specialized leaves that form bladder-like structures to trap and digest small aquatic invertebrates. The plant typically grows in shallow, freshwater habitats such as ponds and marshes. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, usually appearing in clusters.

Despite its carnivorous nature, it plays a unique role in its ecosystem by contributing to nutrient cycling in nutrient-poor environments.
