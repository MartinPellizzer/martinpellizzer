Phacelia crenulata var. vulgaris

Phacelia crenulata var. vulgaris, commonly known as the common phacelia, is a flowering plant native to western North America.

It is characterized by its hairy, branched stems and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a serrated edge, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as meadows and disturbed areas.

This species is valued for its role in pollinator support and its use in ecological restoration projects.
