Phelypaea lavandulacea f. elegans

Phelypaea lavandulacea f. elegans, commonly known as the elegant fern, is a fern species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped fronds that are often tinged with a silvery hue. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its rhizomes are fibrous and help anchor it to the substrate while absorbing nutrients.

This fern is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
