Phaseolus wrightii var. grayanus

Phaseolus wrightii var. grayanus, commonly known as the Gray's runner bean, is a herbaceous annual plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its climbing habit and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces elongated, bean-like pods that are typically green and hairy, containing edible seeds.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for both ornamental and culinary use in arid climates.
