Pandanus guillaumetii

Pandanus guillaumetii, commonly known as the Huon Island pandanus, is a tropical plant native to the island of Huon in Papua New Guinea.

It is a member of the Pandanaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and long, narrow leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, which are often used in traditional crafts.

Its leaves are fibrous and have been historically used for thatching and weaving, highlighting its cultural and practical significance in local communities.
