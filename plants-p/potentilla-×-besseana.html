Potentilla × besseana

Potentilla × besseana, commonly known as the Besseana cinquefoil, is a hybrid shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is characterized by its dense, upright growth habit and produces clusters of bright yellow flowers in early summer. The plant features small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine, silvery hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Due to its ornamental value and hardiness, it is often cultivated in gardens across its native range.
