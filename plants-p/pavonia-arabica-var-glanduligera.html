Pavonia arabica var. glanduligera

Pavonia arabica var. glanduligera, commonly known as the glandular pavonia, is a flowering plant native to the Arabian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of pink to red bract-like flowers. The leaves are typically ovate to lanceolate, with a slightly hairy surface. This species thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in coastal and rocky habitats.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive member of the Malvaceae family.
