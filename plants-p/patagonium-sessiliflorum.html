Patagonium sessiliflorum

Patagonium sessiliflorum, commonly known as the Patagonian sessileflower, is a rare flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of southern South America.

It is characterized by its small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink in color. The plant has thick, succulent leaves that help it survive in the cold, arid environments where it grows. It thrives in rocky soils and is often found in alpine meadows.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species.
