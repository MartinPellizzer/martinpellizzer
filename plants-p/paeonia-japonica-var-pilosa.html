Paeonia japonica var. pilosa

Paeonia japonica var. pilosa, commonly known as the Japanese tree peony with hairy leaves, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and large, showy flowers that bloom in early spring. The leaves are hairy, giving the plant its specific variety name, "pilosa," which means hairy in Latin. The flowers come in shades of pink, red, and white, and are often fragrant.

This plant is valued in horticulture for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
