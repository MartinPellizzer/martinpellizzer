Papaver radicatum subsp. kluanense

Papaver radicatum subsp.

kluanense, commonly known as the Kluan poppy, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia. It features showy, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from red to orange, typically blooming in late spring to early summer.

The plant has a fibrous root system and lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories or rocky habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
