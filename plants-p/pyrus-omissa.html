Pyrus omissa

Pyrus omissa, commonly known as the Japanese whitebeam, is a deciduous tree native to Japan.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its small, white flowers and oval-shaped leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish fruits that are typically not edible by humans. It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for temperate climates.

Pyrus omissa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often planted in gardens for its aesthetic value.
