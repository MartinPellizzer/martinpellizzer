Piper rufispicum

Piper rufispicum, commonly known as the red-hair pepper, is a tropical climbing vine native to the rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many pepper species. The plant is characterized by its glossy, elongated leaves and small, clustered white flowers that develop into red, peppercorn-like fruits. Its fruits are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory properties.

The plant's botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and ethnobotanical contexts.
