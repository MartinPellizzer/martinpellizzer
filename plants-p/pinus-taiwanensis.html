Pinus taiwanensis

Pinus taiwanensis, commonly known as the Taiwan fir, is a coniferous tree native to Taiwan.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and needle-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The tree produces cones that are cylindrical and covered with bracts, which help in seed dispersal. It thrives in cool, moist climates and is often found in mountainous regions.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
