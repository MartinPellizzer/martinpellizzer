Pedicularis gyrorhyncha

Pedicularis gyrorhyncha, commonly known as the horned poppy, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, spurred petals that resemble horns, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in alpine environments, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its flowers, which range in color from yellow to orange, bloom in late spring to early summer.

Pedicularis gyrorhyncha is known for its unique morphology and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
