Pectis papposa var. sessilis

Pectis papposa var. sessilis, commonly known as the narrowleaf pappose pectis, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, sessile leaves that are usually arranged in a basal rosette. Its inflorescence consists of both ray and disk flowers, with the ray florets being typically white or pale yellow.

The species is known for its delicate appearance and is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value.
