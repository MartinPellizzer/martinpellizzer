Platycodon glaucus f. albiflorus

Platycodon glaucus f. albiflorus, commonly known as the white-flowered bellflower, is a cultivar of the Chinese bellflower.

It is characterized by its striking white petals and a distinctive bell-shaped corolla. This plant is native to East Asia and thrives in well-drained soil and full sun. It is often grown for its ornamental value in gardens due to its showy flowers.

The foliage is lance-shaped and often has a bluish-green hue, adding to its aesthetic appeal.
