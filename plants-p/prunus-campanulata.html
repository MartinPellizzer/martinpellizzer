Prunus campanulata

Prunus campanulata, commonly known as the Japanese cherry or mountain cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its white to pink flowers that bloom in early spring, often before the leaves emerge. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape. Its fruit is a small, dark purple drupe that matures in late summer.

Prunus campanulata is valued in horticulture for its ornamental flowers and is often cultivated in gardens and parks.
