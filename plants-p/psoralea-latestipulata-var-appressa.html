Psoralea latestipulata var. appressa

Psoralea latestipulata var. appressa, commonly known as the narrow-leafed psoralea, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, clustered leaves and yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid conditions. It has a compact growth habit and is often found in open grasslands or along rocky outcrops.

The species is noted for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally in some indigenous communities.
