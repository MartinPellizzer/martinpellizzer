Pleurothallis fantastica

Pleurothallis fantastica, commonly known as the Phantom Orchid, is a rare and enigmatic orchid native to the cloud forests of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, often pale or white flowers that resemble ghostly apparitions, hence its common name. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees without deriving nutrients from them. Its flowers are usually small and clustered, with intricate, intricate structures that attract specific pollinators.

Due to its rarity and habitat specificity, it is considered a challenging plant to cultivate outside its natural environment.
