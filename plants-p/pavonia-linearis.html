Pavonia linearis

Pavonia linearis, commonly known as the slender pavonia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 3 feet. The plant features linear, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of small, showy pink to red flowers. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often found in open woods or along the edges of forests.

Pavonia linearis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in native plant gardens.
