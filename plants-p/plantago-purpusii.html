Plantago purpusii

Plantago purpusii, commonly known as the Purpus's plantain, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features rosettes of broad, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in dense spikes, which are typically borne on tall, upright stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

This species is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
