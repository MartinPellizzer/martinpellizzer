Phyllanthus corcovadensis

Phyllanthus corcovadensis, commonly known as the Brazilian pear or golden balsam, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant features opposite, elliptic leaves that are usually glossy and dark green. Its flowers are small, yellowish-green, and clustered in inflorescences.

The fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, brown seeds.
