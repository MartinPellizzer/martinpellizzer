Poa meyeri

Poa meyeri, commonly known as Meyer's bentgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit and fine, bluish-green leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and landscaping.
