Potentilla recta proles auriflora

Potentilla recta proles auriflora, commonly known as the goldenflower cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist meadows and along streambanks, preferring well-drained soils. It has compound leaves with five oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, aggregate fruits called achenes.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.
