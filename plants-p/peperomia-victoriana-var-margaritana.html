Peperomia victoriana var. margaritana

Peperomia victoriana var. margaritana, commonly known as the "Coral Peperomia," is a compact, evergreen shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It features thick, fleshy, oval-shaped leaves that are typically green with a reddish or purplish hue on the underside. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually white or pale pink, adding a subtle ornamental touch. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Its compact growth habit makes it a popular choice for indoor gardening and container cultivation.
