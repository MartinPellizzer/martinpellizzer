Prunus mahaleb subsp. cupaniana

Prunus mahaleb subsp.

cupaniana, commonly known as the sweet cherry or sour cherry, is a species of cherry tree native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its small, oval leaves and white to pinkish flowers that bloom in early spring.

The fruit is typically small, tart, and used in cooking or as a ornamental plant. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible fruit.

This subspecies is closely related to other Prunus species and is valued for its hardiness and adaptability.
