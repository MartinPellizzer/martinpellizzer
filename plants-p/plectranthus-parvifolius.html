Plectranthus parvifolius

Plectranthus parvifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaf plectranthus, is a perennial herb native to South Africa.

It features opposite, narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often slightly hairy, giving it a distinctive texture. The plant produces small, tubular flowers in shades of violet or purple, typically arranged in clusters at the leaf axils. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Due to its compact growth habit and ornamental value, it is widely cultivated as a decorative plant.
