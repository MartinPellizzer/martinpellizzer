Piper rotundum

Piper rotundum, commonly known as roundleaf pepper or Indian pepper, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia and Africa.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its small, round leaves and clusters of tiny, peppery fruits. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The fruit, which is harvested when fully mature, contains essential oils and is used in both culinary and medicinal applications.
