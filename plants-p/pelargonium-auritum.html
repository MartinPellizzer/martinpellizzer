Pelargonium auritum

Pelargonium auritum, commonly known as the ear-shaped geranium, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive heart-shaped leaves with wavy edges and vibrant, showy flowers that range from pink to red. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub, reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Pelargonium auritum is known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated for its attractive foliage and long-lasting blooms.
