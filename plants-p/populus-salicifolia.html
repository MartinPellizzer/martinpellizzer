Populus salicifolia

Populus salicifolia, commonly known as the silky poplar or Canadian poplar, is a deciduous tree native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces catkin-like flowers in early spring, which are wind-pollinated. Its rapid growth rate makes it a common species in reforestation efforts.
