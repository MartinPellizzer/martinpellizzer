Prangos asperula var. leiopetala

Prangos asperula var. leiopetala, commonly known as the smooth-leaved rock rose, is a low-growing perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features dense, silver-gray foliage covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive silvery appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink to purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental value make it a popular choice for drought-tolerant landscaping.
