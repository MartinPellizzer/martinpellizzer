Pimpinele

Pimpinele, commonly known as the common dandelion, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of spoon-shaped leaves and a hollow, hairless stem. The plant produces a distinctive seed head, known as a "puffball," which releases seeds with parachute-like structures called pappus. Pimpinele is a hardy perennial that thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in lawns and disturbed areas.

It is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
