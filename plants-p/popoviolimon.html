Popoviolimon

Popoviolimon, commonly known as the golden sunflower, is a striking plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its large, bright yellow flower heads that resemble the sun, hence its common name. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and a robust, upright stem that can grow up to two meters in height. Popoviolimon is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and other composite flowers.

It thrives in full sunlight and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping in dry climates.
