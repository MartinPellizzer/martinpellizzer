Potentilla anserina

Potentilla anserina, commonly known as silverweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its silvery-white undersides on its leaves, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, stream banks, and woodland edges.

Silverweed is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used historically in traditional medicine.
