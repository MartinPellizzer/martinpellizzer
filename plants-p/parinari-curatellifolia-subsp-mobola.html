Parinari curatellifolia subsp. mobola

Parinari curatellifolia subsp.

mobola, commonly known as the Mobola parinari, is a small to medium-sized tree native to tropical Africa. It typically grows in moist lowland forests and is characterized by its elliptical, leathery leaves.

The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, followed by edible, fleshy berries that are an important food source for various wildlife. Its bark and leaves have been used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and potential economic value in local communities.
