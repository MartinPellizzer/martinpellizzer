Penstemon distans

Penstemon distans, commonly known as the tall beardtongue, is a species of flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in open, rocky habitats such as meadows and slopes. The plant features showy, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to purple, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, and it produces elongated seed capsules that release tiny seeds in the wind.

Penstemon distans is valued in gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
