Pedilanthus retusus

Pedilanthus retusus, commonly known as the "Crown of Thorns," is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, spiny stems and clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces a milky latex that contains toxic compounds, which can cause skin irritation. Its leaves are opposite and ovate, often with a waxy texture, and it thrives in well-drained soil and full sun.

It is popular as a decorative plant due to its unique appearance and relatively low maintenance requirements.
