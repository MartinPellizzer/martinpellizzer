Potentilla atrorubens

Potentilla atrorubens, commonly known as black-fruited cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant produces dark red or black berries that are edible but often have a sharp, acidic taste. It thrives in moist, sandy soils and is often found in open woodlands or along streambanks.

Potentilla atrorubens is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local wildlife.
