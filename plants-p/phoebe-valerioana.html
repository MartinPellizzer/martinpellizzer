Phoebe valerioana

Phoebe valerioana, commonly known as the velvetleaf phoebe, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is occasionally cultivated in tropical gardens.
