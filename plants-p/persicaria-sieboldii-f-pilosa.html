Persicaria sieboldii f. pilosa

Persicaria sieboldii f. pilosa, commonly known as the hairy persicaria, is a flowering plant in the Polygonaceae family.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and distinctive, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, hairy texture. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or red, adding a vibrant touch to gardens. Native to eastern Asia, it thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sun.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
