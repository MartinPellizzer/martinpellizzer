Phyllocactus russellianus

Phyllocactus russellianus, commonly known as the Russell's phyllocactus, is a succulent plant native to South America.

It features thick, fleshy stems that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid conditions. The plant produces clusters of small, waxy leaves that are typically green or bluish-green in color. It is known for its ability to form dense, compact growth patterns.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
