Pavonia psilophylla

Pavonia psilophylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaf false mallow, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, often hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves that are typically less than 5 centimeters in length. The plant produces clusters of small, pink to reddish-purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Pavonia psilophylla is a herbaceous perennial that plays a role in supporting local pollinators and maintaining wetland ecosystems.
