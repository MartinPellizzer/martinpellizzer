Pelargonium × pusillum

Pelargonium × pusillum, commonly known as the dwarf geranium, is a hybrid species within the Geraniaceae family.

It is a low-growing, herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant, showy flowers. The plant produces clusters of small, five-petaled flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, typically blooming in spring and summer. Its leaves are usually rounded with a slightly hairy texture, and the plant is well-suited for rock gardens or containers due to its compact size.

Pelargonium × pusillum is also known for its ability to thrive in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil.
