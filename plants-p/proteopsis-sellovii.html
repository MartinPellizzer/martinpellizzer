Proteopsis sellovii

Proteopsis sellovii, commonly known as the Sello's protea, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Proteaceae family, which includes many ornamental and economically important plants. The plant is characterized by its striking, cone-shaped inflorescence that resembles a protea, with numerous small, brightly colored flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
