Phyteuma coeruleum

Phyteuma coeruleum, commonly known as blue phyteuma, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in meadows and grasslands, preferring well-drained soils. Its flowering period occurs in late summer to early autumn, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
