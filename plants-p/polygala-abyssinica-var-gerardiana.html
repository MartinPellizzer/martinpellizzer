Polygala abyssinica var. gerardiana

Polygala abyssinica var. gerardiana, commonly known as the Abyssinian milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to Ethiopia.

It belongs to the Polygalaceae family and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid habitats, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its leaves are narrow and often hairy, while its flowers range from white to pale yellow, adding subtle beauty to its natural setting.

This variant is distinguished by its unique morphology and ecological significance in its native region.
