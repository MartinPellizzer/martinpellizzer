Pseudovanilla affinis

Pseudovanilla affinis, commonly known as the slender vanilla orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its climbing habit and slender, hairy stems. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are typically borne in clusters. Its fruits are elongated, fleshy berries that mature to a yellowish color.

Pseudovanilla affinis is notable for its aromatic qualities and is sometimes used in traditional medicine and as a flavoring agent.
