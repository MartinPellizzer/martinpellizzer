Pachyphytum longifolium

Pachyphytum longifolium, commonly known as the longleaf pachyphytum, is a succulent plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and prefers full sun exposure. Its foliage is often bluish-green, with a waxy coating that helps reduce water loss.

It is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its low maintenance requirements.
