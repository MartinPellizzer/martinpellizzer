Philodendron latifolium

Philodendron latifolium, commonly known as the broadleaf philodendron, is a tropical climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It features heart-shaped, glossy leaves that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant has a robust stem that can climb using aerial roots, often reaching considerable heights in its natural habitat. Its foliage is typically a deep green, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by orange-red berries.

It thrives in warm, humid environments with bright, indirect light.
