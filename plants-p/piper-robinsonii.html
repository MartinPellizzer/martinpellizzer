Piper robinsonii

Piper robinsonii, commonly known as the Robinson's pepper vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to the rainforests of Queensland, Australia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper plants, and is characterized by its slender, woody stems and opposite, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by red, fleshy berries that are edible but often described as having a peppery taste. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found growing along forest floors or on tree trunks in its natural habitat.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
