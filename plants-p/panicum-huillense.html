Panicum huillense

Panicum huillense, commonly known as Huillense grass, is a perennial grass species native to South America, particularly found in regions of Peru and Bolivia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, fluffy seeds that are dispersed by wind, aiding in its propagation across open habitats. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and disturbed areas.

Due to its adaptability, it is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
