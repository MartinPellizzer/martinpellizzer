Pilosella norvegica

Pilosella norvegica, commonly known as Norwegian hairweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit and is native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia. The plant features small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are typically hairy and have a slightly woolly texture, contributing to its common name.

Pilosella norvegica is often found in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to harsh alpine environments.
