Petroselinum sativum subsp. tuberosum

Petroselinum sativum subsp.

tuberosum, commonly known as parsley root or parsnip, is a biennial herbaceous plant belonging to the carrot family (Apiaceae). It is characterized by its thick, tapered, white or yellowish root that is often cultivated for its edible, crunchy texture.

The plant produces a tall, slender stem with finely divided, feather-like leaves that are aromatic when crushed. Its flowers are small, white or greenish, and arranged in umbrella-like clusters called umbels.

The root is rich in nutrients and is used in various culinary traditions around the world.
