Passiflora ianthina

Passiflora ianthina, commonly known as the blue passionflower, is a climbing vine native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking blue and white flowers that bloom in the summer months. The plant produces edible, fleshy berries that are often consumed by various animals and birds. Its leaves are typically large, heart-shaped, and have a velvety texture.

Passiflora ianthina is valued not only for its ornamental beauty but also for its ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
