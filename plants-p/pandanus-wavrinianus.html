Pandanus wavrinianus

Pandanus wavrinianus, commonly known as the Wavrin's pandanus, is a tropical plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and long, narrow leaves that are often used in traditional crafts. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that develop into spiny, fibrous fruits. It thrives in coastal areas and is often found growing near the shoreline.

Its unique appearance and cultural significance make it an important species in the region.
