Pavetta hymenophylla

Pavetta hymenophylla, commonly known as the silverleaf pavetta, is a small evergreen shrub native to the tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery down, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically borne in the axils of the leaves. Its flowers are generally fragrant and attract various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Pavetta hymenophylla is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is well-suited to shaded, moist environments.
