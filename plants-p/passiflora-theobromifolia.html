Passiflora theobromifolia

Passiflora theobromifolia, commonly known as the chocolate passionflower, is a climbing vine native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, complex flowers that feature five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments surrounding the reproductive structures. The plant produces edible, orange-yellow berries that are rich in antioxidants and have a unique, slightly tart flavor. Its leaves are large, heart-shaped, and often have a velvety texture.

Passiflora theobromifolia is valued not only for its ornamental beauty but also for its potential medicinal and culinary uses.
