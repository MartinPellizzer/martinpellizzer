Piper echeverrianum

Piper echeverrianum, commonly known as the Echeverria pepper, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It belongs to the Piper genus, which includes many species of flowering plants known for their aromatic properties. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into elongated, woody fruits. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a slightly rough texture.

Piper echeverrianum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has some traditional uses in herbal medicine.
