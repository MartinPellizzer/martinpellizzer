Podocarpus novae-caledoniae

Podocarpus novae-caledoniae, commonly known as the New Caledonia podocarp, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to the island of New Caledonia.

It belongs to the Podocarpaceae family and is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and small, scale-like leaves. The tree produces cone-like structures that contain both male and female reproductive organs, typical of gymnosperms. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in coastal and lowland forest ecosystems.

This species plays an important role in the ecological balance of its native habitat.
