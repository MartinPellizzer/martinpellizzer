Phyteuma spicatum subvar. macrophyllum

Phyteuma spicatum subvar. macrophyllum, commonly known as the large-leaved phyteuma, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its tall, sturdy stems and showy, clustered inflorescences of yellow to orange-red flowers. The leaves are broad, lance-shaped, and often have a hairy texture, contributing to its ornamental value. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens.

This plant is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
