Pavetta sepium var. pubescens

Pavetta sepium var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy pavetta, is a shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It features simple, opposite, elliptical leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence, giving it a soft, hairy appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, arranged in clusters at the leaf axils. Its fruit is a small, three-lobed capsule that contains several seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
