Pyrus cultrensis

Pyrus cultrensis, commonly known as the Spanish whitebeam, is a small deciduous tree native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and oval-shaped leaves with a slightly serrated margin. The plant produces small, white flowers in early spring, followed by edible, round berries that are typically yellow or red in color.

It is often found in rocky or mountainous areas and is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance.
