Pelargonium × illecebrum

Pelargonium × illecebrum, commonly known as the ivy-leaved geranium, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Geraniaceae family.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and dark green, heart-shaped leaves that resemble ivy leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, five-petaled flowers in shades of red, pink, or white, which bloom throughout the growing season. It is often cultivated as a decorative plant in hanging baskets or containers due to its cascading foliage.

Pelargonium × illecebrum is native to South Africa and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of conditions.
