Pittosporum abyssinicum subsp. cardiocarpum

Pittosporum abyssinicum subsp.

cardiocarpum, commonly known as the Abyssinian pittosporum, is a shrub native to Ethiopia. It is characterized by its dark green, leathery leaves and fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers.

The plant produces small, woody fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
