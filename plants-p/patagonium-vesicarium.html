Patagonium vesicarium

Patagonium vesicarium, commonly known as the blister bladder, is a perennial herb native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its succulent, inflated stems that store water, allowing it to thrive in dry environments. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its leaves are typically narrow and fleshy, adapted to minimize water loss.

Patagonium vesicarium is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is well-suited to harsh, semi-desert conditions.
