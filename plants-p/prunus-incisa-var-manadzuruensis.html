Prunus incisa var. manadzuruensis

Prunus incisa var. manadzuruensis, commonly known as the Manadzuru cherry, is a rare and ornamental species native to Japan.

It is characterized by its small, delicate white flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by small, dark red fruits. The plant typically grows as a compact shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are ovate with serrated edges and turn a vibrant red in the autumn.

This variety is valued for its aesthetic appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value.
