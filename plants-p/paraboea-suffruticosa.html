Paraboea suffruticosa

Paraboea suffruticosa, commonly known as the mountain hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub that typically grows in humid, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical forests. The plant produces showy, five-petaled flowers in shades of yellow and orange, which bloom throughout the year. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a velvety texture.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its ability to thrive in partial shade.
