Pomatocalpa linearipetalum

Pomatocalpa linearipetalum, commonly known as the linear-petaled orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and linear, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces striking, showy flowers with long, narrow petals and a distinctive, elongated lip. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its unique morphology and habitat, it is a subject of interest in both botanical research and horticultural cultivation.
