Poa nemoralis proles glaucantha

Poa nemoralis proles glaucantha, commonly known as the glaucous wood sedge, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often coated with a bluish waxy layer, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowers are small and clustered in spikelets, which are arranged in panicles.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and its ability to thrive in challenging ecological conditions.
