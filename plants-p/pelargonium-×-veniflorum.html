Pelargonium × veniflorum

Pelargonium × veniflorum, commonly known as the sweet geranium, is a hybrid species within the Geraniaceae family.

It is characterized by its showy, clustered flowers that range in color from pink to red, often with a distinctive white or yellow center. The plant typically has rounded, velvety leaves that are lobed or notched, contributing to its ornamental appeal. Native to South Africa, it thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and container planting.

Its foliage and blooms are often used in floral arrangements due to their vibrant appearance and long-lasting nature.
