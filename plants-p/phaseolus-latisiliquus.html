Phaseolus latisiliquus

Phaseolus latisiliquus, commonly known as the broad-seeded bean, is a species of legume native to Central America.

It is characterized by its large, broad pods that contain several seeds. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters and has compound leaves with oval-shaped leaflets. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, and it produces a distinctive, flattened pod that matures to a yellowish color.

This species is valued for its edible seeds, which are used in traditional cuisines and can be cultivated for both food and agricultural purposes.
