Poecilostachys ambositrensis

Poecilostachys ambositrensis, commonly known as the Madagascar grass, is a perennial grass species native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is occasionally cultivated in tropical and subtropical regions.
