Prunus nipponica f. lasiogyna

Prunus nipponica f. lasiogyna, commonly known as the Japanese cherry plum or Japanese dwarf cherry, is a compact, ornamental shrub native to Japan.

It features small, oval leaves that are dark green and slightly hairy on the upper surface. The plant produces clusters of white or pale pink flowers in early spring, adding a delicate floral display. Its fruit is a small, yellowish plum that ripens in late summer.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens for its aesthetic beauty and seasonal interest.
