Psittacanthus melinonii

Psittacanthus melinonii, commonly known as the golden star fern, is a species of fern native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its compact, rosette-like growth habit. The plant features bright yellow to orange spore-bearing structures called sorus, which are prominently displayed on the underside of its fronds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical forests.

Psittacanthus melinonii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated as a decorative plant in botanical gardens and homes.
