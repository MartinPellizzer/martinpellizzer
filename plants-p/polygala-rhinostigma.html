Polygala rhinostigma

Polygala rhinostigma, commonly known as the horned milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Polygalaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, spiny pods that resemble horns, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in arid or semi-arid environments. Its small, yellowish flowers are clustered in racemes and bloom during the warmer months.

Polygala rhinostigma is often found in open vegetation types such as grasslands and shrublands.
