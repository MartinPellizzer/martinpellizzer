Phacelia ivesiana var. glandulifera

Phacelia ivesiana var. glandulifera, commonly known as the glandular phacelia, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and spikes of blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in supporting native wildlife.
