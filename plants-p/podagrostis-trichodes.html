Podagrostis trichodes

Podagrostis trichodes, commonly known as the hairy grass.

It is a flowering plant in the grass family, Poaceae, characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Its leaves are narrow and covered with fine, hairy structures, giving it its common name.

It is typically found in disturbed soils and open habitats across temperate regions.
