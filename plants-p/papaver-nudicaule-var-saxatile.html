Papaver nudicaule var. saxatile

Papaver nudicaule var. saxatile, commonly known as the rock poppy, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to rocky and alpine environments.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of small, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant typically grows in well-drained soils and thrives in full sun, making it well-suited for rock gardens and alpine settings. Its leaves are lance-shaped and hairy, providing a textured appearance.

The species is noted for its resilience and ability to survive in harsh, nutrient-poor conditions.
