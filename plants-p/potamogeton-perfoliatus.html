Potamogeton perfoliatus

Potamogeton perfoliatus, commonly known as the perfoliate pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments across the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its elongated, ribbon-like leaves that often have a distinct perfoliate appearance, with the leaf bases passing through the stems of adjacent leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are typically arranged in dense clusters, and it blooms from late spring to early autumn. Potamogeton perfoliatus thrives in slow-moving or still waters, often forming dense underwater vegetation that provides habitat for aquatic life.

Its presence is an indicator of good water quality and is commonly found in lakes, ponds, and wetlands.
