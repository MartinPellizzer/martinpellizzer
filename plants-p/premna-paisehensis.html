Premna paisehensis

Premna paisehensis, commonly known as the Himalayan premna, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Himalayas.

It belongs to the family Lamiaceae and is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, typically blooming in the spring. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
