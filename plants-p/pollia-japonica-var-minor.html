Pollia japonica var. minor

Pollia japonica var. minor, commonly known as the Japanese orchid or lesser pollia, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and pendant, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale pink. The plant produces showy, fleshy fruits that are often consumed by various bird species. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in some horticultural settings.
