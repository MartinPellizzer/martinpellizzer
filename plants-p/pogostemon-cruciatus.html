Pogostemon cruciatus

Pogostemon cruciatus, commonly known as the crucifix pogostemon, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its square stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. Its small, tubular flowers are typically blue or purple and are arranged in whorls along the stem.

Pogostemon cruciatus is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or stream banks.
