Pinus mughus f. pumilio

Pinus mughus f. pumilio, commonly known as the dwarf mountain pine, is a rare and unique variant of the Siberian pine.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and smaller size compared to its parent species. The plant exhibits dense, needle-like foliage that is often bluish-green in color. It thrives in high-altitude environments, typically found in alpine regions with cold, dry climates.

This subspecies is adapted to harsh conditions, making it a resilient and hardy plant.
