Ptychopyxis thwaitesii

Ptychopyxis thwaitesii, commonly known as the New Zealand mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on the branches of native New Zealand trees.

It has small, oval-shaped leaves and produces white or pink flowers that are clustered in umbels. The plant relies on its host for water and nutrients but can photosynthesize to some extent. It plays a role in the ecosystem by providing food for birds and insects.

However, it can also cause damage to its host trees if it becomes too dense.
