Polypogon crinitus

Polypogon crinitus, commonly known as the hairy bluegrass, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its fine, hairy leaves and compact growth habit, often forming dense mats on the ground. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear blue to purplish flowers, which are typical of the Poaceae family. It prefers moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in native plant gardens.
