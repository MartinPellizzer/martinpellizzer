Patagonium campestre

Patagonium campestre, commonly known as the Patagonian fieldweed, is a flowering plant native to the grasslands of southern South America.

It is characterized by its low-growing habit, with slender stems and small, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of tiny, pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to the harsh climatic conditions of the Patagonian region.

Due to its resilience and ecological role, it is an important component of the local flora.
