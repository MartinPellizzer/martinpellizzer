Pipturus incanus var. uapensis

Pipturus incanus var. uapensis, commonly known as the Uapaca tree, is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are typically elliptical or ovate in shape. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, followed by fleshy, orange-red berries that are an important food source for various wildlife.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories or along riverbanks.
