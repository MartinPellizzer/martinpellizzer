Phymaspermum pubescens

Phymaspermum pubescens, commonly known as the hairy spore plant, is a rare and intriguing member of the fern family.

It is characterized by its delicate, hairy fronds that give it its common name. This plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its reproductive structure consists of small, spore-bearing cones rather than flowers.

Due to its limited distribution and unique morphology, it is of interest to botanists studying plant adaptation and evolution.
