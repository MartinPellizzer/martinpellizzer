Pyrus communis var. serrata

Pyrus communis var. serrata, commonly known as the serrated pear, is a variant of the common pear characterized by its distinctive serrated leaf margins.

This cultivar is native to Europe and is often cultivated for its ornamental value as well as its edible fruit. The leaves are typically dark green and have a rough texture, while the flowers are white and fragrant. The fruit is usually smaller than the common pear and has a slightly tart flavor.

It is often used in horticulture for its hardiness and adaptability to various soil types.
