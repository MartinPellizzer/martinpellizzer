Potentilla queretarensis

Potentilla queretarensis, commonly known as the Mexican cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees. Its leaves are typically oval-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture.

Potentilla queretarensis is often found in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions.
