Phleum pratense var. bracteatum

Phleum pratense var. bracteatum, commonly known as bracted tall fescue, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, upright growth habit and narrow, blade-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces panicles of small, spikelet-like flowers, which are often tinged with purple or brown. It thrives in well-drained soils and is commonly found in meadows, pastures, and along roadsides.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to form dense, durable turf.
