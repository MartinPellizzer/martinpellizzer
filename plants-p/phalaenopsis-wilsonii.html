Phalaenopsis wilsonii

Phalaenopsis wilsonii, commonly known as Wilson's moth orchid, is a tropical epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features broad, dark green leaves and produces clusters of long, white, moth-like flowers with a subtle fragrance. The plant is known for its relatively long blooming period, often lasting several months. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires moderate humidity and well-draining potting media.

Due to its ornamental value, it is popular in horticulture and conservation efforts.
