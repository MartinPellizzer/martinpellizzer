Poterium sanguisorba subsp. polygamum

Poterium sanguisorba subsp.

polygamum, commonly known as redroot sandwort, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It features upright, branched stems covered in small, lance-shaped leaves that are often reddish or purplish in color.

The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or red, blooming from late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable species for ecological restoration projects.
