Panicum thinophilum

Panicum thinophilum, commonly known as slender panicgrass, is a perennial grass native to parts of Asia and Africa.

It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along riverbanks. The plant features slender, upright stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often slightly hairy. Its inflorescence consists of a loose cluster of spikelets, which produce small, wind-dispersed seeds.

It plays a role in stabilizing wetland soils and provides habitat for various small organisms.
