Poa controversa

Poa controversa, commonly known as the controversial grass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are narrow and often have a bluish-green hue, and it produces small, wind-pollinated flowers.

Poa controversa is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in wetland habitats.
