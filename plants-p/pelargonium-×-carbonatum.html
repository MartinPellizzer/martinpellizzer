Pelargonium × carbonatum

Pelargonium × carbonatum, commonly known as the coral geranium, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Geraniaceae family.

It is native to South Africa and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that range from pink to red. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub with hairy, ovate leaves that are often tinged with red or purple. Its flowers are borne in dense clusters and bloom in late summer to early autumn.

Pelargonium × carbonatum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
