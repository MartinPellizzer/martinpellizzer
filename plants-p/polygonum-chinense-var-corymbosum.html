Polygonum chinense var. corymbosum

Polygonum chinense var. corymbosum, commonly known as the Chinese knotweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to East Asia.

It features slender, erect stems that often form dense clusters, and its leaves are ovate to lanceolate, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-white or reddish in color, blooming in summer. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

The species is known for its rapid growth and ability to spread through rhizomes, making it both ecologically significant and potentially invasive.
