Paspalum mandiocanum var. subaequiglume

Paspalum mandiocanum var. subaequiglume, commonly known as the Brazilian grass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, blade-like leaves with a smooth texture. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

Botanically, it is notable for its adaptability to various soil types and its role in ecological restoration projects.
