Passiflora hexangularis

Passiflora hexangularis, commonly known as the six-angle passionflower, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, intricate flowers that feature a crown-like structure and vibrant colors. The plant produces edible berries that are often consumed fresh or used in culinary applications. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped with a rough texture, and it thrives in warm, humid climates.

Passiflora hexangularis is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
