Prunus subcordata var. kelloggii

Prunus subcordata var. kelloggii, commonly known as Kellogg's cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows to a height of 6 to 10 feet, featuring a rounded or irregular shape with dense, thorny branches. The plant produces small, white flowers in early spring, followed by edible, sweet-tart red berries in the summer. Its leaves are ovate to elliptic, with a smooth texture and a slightly serrated margin.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in native plant landscaping.
