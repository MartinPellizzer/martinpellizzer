Potentilla × honoratae

Potentilla × honoratae, commonly known as the hybrid shrub or garden potentilla, is a deciduous plant that belongs to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of yellow, white, or pink. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and is known for its hardiness and adaptability to various soil conditions. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a slightly serrated edge, adding to its ornamental appeal.

Potentilla × honoratae is widely cultivated in gardens for its resilience and ability to thrive in full sun to partial shade.
