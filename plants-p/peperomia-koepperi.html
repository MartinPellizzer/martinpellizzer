Peperomia koepperi

Peperomia koepperi, commonly known as the silver mottled peperomia, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to South America.

It features fleshy, ovate leaves with a distinctive silver and green mottled pattern on the upper surface, while the underside is a deep, velvety green. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters, typically in shades of white or green, which are followed by rounded, fleshy fruits. It thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Peperomia koepperi is relatively low maintenance and resistant to pests, contributing to its popularity among plant enthusiasts.
