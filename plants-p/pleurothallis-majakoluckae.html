Pleurothallis majakoluckae

Pleurothallis majakoluckae, commonly known as the Majakolucka orchid, is a species native to the cloud forests of Ecuador.

It is a small epiphytic orchid, typically growing on tree branches or rocks in humid, shaded environments. The plant features delicate, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink. It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is noted for its unique floral structure and limited distribution.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
