Paspalum tristachyum

Paspalum tristachyum, commonly known as the three-awn paspalum, is a perennial grass species native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, blade-like leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with three awns, giving it its common name. It thrives in a variety of habitats, including wetlands, grasslands, and disturbed soils.

Paspalum tristachyum is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife species.
