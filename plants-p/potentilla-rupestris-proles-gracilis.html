Potentilla rupestris proles gracilis

Potentilla rupestris proles gracilis, commonly known as the slender rock cinquefoil, is a low-growing perennial shrub native to rocky and alpine environments.

It features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has thin, wiry stems and lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in mountainous regions.

Its hardy nature makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and landscaping in arid or coastal areas.
