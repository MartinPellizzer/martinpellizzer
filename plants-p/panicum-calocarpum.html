Panicum calocarpum

Panicum calocarpum, commonly known as the red panicgrass, is a species of grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and produces clusters of red or purple spikelets that give it its common name. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields, preferring full sun. Its foliage is narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green coloration.

Panicum calocarpum is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its attractive appearance and low maintenance requirements.
