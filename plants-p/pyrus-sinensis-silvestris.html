Pyrus sinensis silvestris

Pyrus sinensis silvestris, commonly known as the wild Chinese apple or Chinese white pear, is a species of flowering plant in the family Rosaceae.

It is native to eastern China and is characterized by its small, yellowish-green leaves and white, fragrant flowers. The plant produces small, edible fruits that are typically sour and have a crisp texture. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and for its use in traditional Chinese medicine.

This species is also valued for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions and climates.
