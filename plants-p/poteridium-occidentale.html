Poteridium occidentale

Poteridium occidentale, commonly known as western beach grass, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of North America.

It is a member of the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its prostrate growth habit and dense, mat-forming structure. The plant has small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. It thrives in sandy, well-drained soils and is often found in dune environments where it helps stabilize sand dunes.

Poteridium occidentale plays an important ecological role in coastal ecosystems by preventing erosion and providing habitat for various insects and small animals.
