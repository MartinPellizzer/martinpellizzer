Phyteuma michelii f. scorzonerifolium

Phyteuma michelii f. scorzonerifolium, commonly known as the scorzonerifolium form of Michel's phyteuma, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It features basal leaves that are lanceolate and often covered in fine hairs, while the stem is upright and branched. The flowers are typically pale yellow to orange, arranged in loose racemes, and bloom in late summer. It prefers well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental value in gardens and its ability to thrive in Mediterranean climates.
