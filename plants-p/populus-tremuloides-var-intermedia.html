Populus tremuloides var. intermedia

Populus tremuloides var. intermedia, commonly known as the quaking aspen.

It is a deciduous tree native to North America, characterized by its trembling leaves that rustle in the wind. The plant has smooth, gray bark and produces small, catkin-like flowers in the spring. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have serrated edges.

It plays an important ecological role in riparian and forest ecosystems.
