Periploca sepium

Periploca sepium, commonly known as the European periploca, is a deciduous shrub native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves and showy, fragrant flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, orange-red fruits that contain seeds surrounded by a fleshy, mucilaginous coating. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal medicine.
