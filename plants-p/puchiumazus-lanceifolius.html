Puchiumazus lanceifolius

Puchiumazus lanceifolius, commonly known as the lanceleaf puchium, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its elongated, lance-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow, often appearing in the axils of the leaves. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in dense forest undergrowth.

Due to its unique appearance and ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
