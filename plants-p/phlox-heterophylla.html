Phlox heterophylla

Phlox heterophylla, commonly known as the mountain phlox, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive foliage, which varies in shape depending on the growing conditions, ranging from lance-shaped to nearly succulent. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or alpine environments.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is a popular choice for native plant gardens.
