Pleurothallis mandonii

Pleurothallis mandonii, commonly known as the Mandon's pleurothallis, is a species of orchid native to the cloud forests of Central America.

It is a small, epiphytic orchid that typically grows on trees and rocks in humid, shaded environments. The plant features delicate, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pale yellow. It is known for its intricate floral structure and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts for its beauty and rarity.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered a threatened species in its native range.
