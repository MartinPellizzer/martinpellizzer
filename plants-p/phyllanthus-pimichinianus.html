Phyllanthus pimichinianus

Phyllanthus pimichinianus, commonly known as the Himalayan phyllanthus, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of the Indian subcontinent.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by small, spherical fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas at elevations ranging from 1,500 to 3,000 meters.

Due to its medicinal properties, it is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
