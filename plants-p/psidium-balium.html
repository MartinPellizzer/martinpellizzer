Psidium balium

Psidium balium, commonly known as the wild guava, is a small evergreen shrub or tree native to tropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by edible, berry-like fruits. The fruit is typically small, with a thin skin and a sweet, aromatic pulp containing numerous seeds.

It is often found in moist, shaded areas and is valued for its ornamental and culinary uses.
