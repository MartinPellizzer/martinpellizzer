Pandanus odoratissimus var. laevigatus

Pandanus odoratissimus var. laevigatus, commonly known as the sweet-scented pandanus, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its long, sword-shaped leaves that are often used in traditional crafts and as thatching material. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, contributing to its distinctive aroma. Its root system is fibrous and adapted to thrive in wet, swampy environments.

This variety is valued not only for its ornamental qualities but also for its cultural and practical significance in local communities.
