Polypodium pectinatum var. acuminatum

Polypodium pectinatum var. acuminatum, commonly known as the acuminated wall fern, is a variety of the wall fern genus Polypodium.

It is a terrestrial fern characterized by its delicate, frond-like structures that grow in a rosette formation. The plant typically thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in rocky or wooded areas. Its fronds are usually lance-shaped with a pinnate structure, and the leaves are covered in a fine layer of hair.

This fern is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens or as a houseplant.
