Poa jordalii

Poa jordalii, commonly known as Jordan's bluegrass, is a species of grass native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its fine, blue-green blades and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. The plant typically grows to a height of 10 to 30 centimeters and forms dense, low-growing mats. It has a fibrous root system that helps stabilize soil and prevent erosion.

Poa jordalii is adapted to arid and semi-arid climates, making it a resilient plant in harsh environments.
