Poa compressa var. depauperata

Poa compressa var. depauperata, commonly known as the slender meadowgrass, is a low-growing, perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically forms dense, mat-like growths and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and compact inflorescence. The plant is adapted to a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained, often moist environments. It is often found in open habitats such as meadows, pastures, and disturbed areas.

Its botanical features make it an important component of grassland ecosystems.
