Phaseolus oligospermus

Phaseolus oligospermus, commonly known as the wild kidney bean, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pod-like fruits. The plant produces small, white or pale purple flowers that attract pollinators such as bees. Its seeds, though not widely cultivated, are edible and have been used in traditional cuisines.

The plant's growth habit includes climbing or trailing vines, making it adaptable to various ecological niches.
