Pinus campylocarpa var. shastensis

Pinus campylocarpa var. shastensis, commonly known as the Shasta pine, is a variant of the ponderosa pine native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its long, slender needles and distinctive, cone-like structures that mature to a reddish-brown color. The tree typically grows in high-elevation forests and is adapted to cold, dry environments. Its bark is thick and fissured, providing protection against fire and harsh weather.

This variety plays an important ecological role in its habitat, supporting a variety of wildlife species.
