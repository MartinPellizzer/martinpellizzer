Podocarpus nageia

Podocarpus nageia, commonly known as the Nageia podocarp, is a species of coniferous evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Podocarpaceae and is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and small, scale-like leaves. The tree produces cones that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind, aiding in its propagation. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in mountainous regions with high humidity.

Podocarpus nageia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in horticulture and reforestation projects.
