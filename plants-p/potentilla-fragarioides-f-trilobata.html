Potentilla fragarioides f. trilobata

Potentilla fragarioides f. trilobata, commonly known as the three-lobed cinquefoil, is a flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is a shrub characterized by its dense, sprawling growth habit and small, bright yellow flowers. The leaves are trifoliate, meaning they are divided into three leaflets, which is a key identifying feature. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardy nature in temperate climates.
