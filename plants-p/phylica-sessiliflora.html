Phylica sessiliflora

Phylica sessiliflora, commonly known as the rock cress, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a small, evergreen shrub that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pink flowers during the winter months. Its leaves are narrow and leathery, adapted to conserve water in arid conditions.

Phylica sessiliflora is often used in rock gardens due to its hardy nature and ornamental value.
