Pelargonium goweri

Pelargonium goweri, commonly known as the Gower geranium, is a species of flowering plant in the Geraniaceae family.

It is native to South Africa and is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and showy, pink to purple flowers. The plant features rounded, velvety leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and containers.

Pelargonium goweri is valued for its drought tolerance and ability to bloom throughout the growing season.
