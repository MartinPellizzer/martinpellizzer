Pteris multifida f. serrulata

Pteris multifida f. serrulata, commonly known as the serrated water fern, is a variant of the common water fern.

It is a fern species characterized by its finely divided, serrated fronds that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its fronds are typically bioluminescent and can reach lengths of up to 1 meter.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
