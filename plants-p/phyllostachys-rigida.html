Phyllostachys rigida

Phyllostachys rigida, commonly known as the hard bamboo, is a species of grass-like plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender culms that can grow up to 10 meters in height. The plant has long, narrow leaves that are typically green on the upper surface and lighter in color on the underside. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

Phyllostachys rigida is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional Chinese medicine.
