Peltophorum scioanum

Peltophorum scioanum, commonly known as the Indian flame tree, is a deciduous tree native to India and Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its bipinnate leaves and showy, red-orange inflorescences. The tree produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The plant also has medicinal uses in traditional systems of medicine.
