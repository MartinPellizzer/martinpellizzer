Ptychosperma angustifolium

Ptychosperma angustifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaved pandanus, is a tropical plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It is a member of the Pandanaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and long, narrow leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or green in color. Its leaves are often used in traditional cooking and crafts due to their aromatic properties.

Ptychosperma angustifolium thrives in humid, coastal environments and is often found growing near the shoreline.
