Panicum lagascae var. virgatum

Panicum lagascae var. virgatum, commonly known as the silvergrass, is a variant of the panicum genus native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its striking silver-gray foliage, which results from dense, fine hairs covering the leaves. The plant produces fluffy, pale pink to white inflorescences that add visual interest in the late summer and autumn. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soils.

This ornamental grass is often used in landscaping for its aesthetic appeal and low maintenance requirements.
