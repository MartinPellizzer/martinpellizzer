Phyllomphax seidenfadeniana

Phyllomphax seidenfadeniana, commonly known as the Sunda mountain fern, is a rare and striking fern species native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, leathery fronds that can grow up to several meters in length, with a distinctive, intricate pattern of veins. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, typically found in the understory of tropical rainforests. Its reproductive structures, including spore-bearing sorus, are located on the underside of the fronds, typical of the fern family.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a valuable species for botanical research and conservation efforts.
