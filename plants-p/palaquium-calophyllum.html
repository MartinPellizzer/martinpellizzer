Palaquium calophyllum

Palaquium calophyllum, commonly known as the yellow mangosteen, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and fragrant white flowers. The fruit is small, round, and has a thick, yellow outer shell with a sweet, aromatic pulp inside. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and its fruit, which is used in traditional medicine and cuisine.
