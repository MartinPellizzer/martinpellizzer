Pappobolus matthewsii var. matthewsii

Pappobolus matthewsii var. matthewsii, commonly known as the Matthews' pappobolus, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its distinctive yellow-orange flowers. The plant features a compact growth habit with narrow, linear leaves. It produces small, wind-dispersed seeds enclosed in a pappus, which aids in its propagation.

This species is often found in rocky or sandy soils in arid environments.
