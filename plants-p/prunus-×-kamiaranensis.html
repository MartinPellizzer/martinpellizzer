Prunus × kamiaranensis

Prunus × kamiaranensis, commonly known as the Japanese cherry plum, is a hybrid species within the Prunus genus.

It is native to Japan and is characterized by its small, sweet fruits that are often used in culinary applications. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. It produces fragrant white or pink flowers in the spring, which are followed by edible berries.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is commonly cultivated in gardens and orchards.
