Paraboea umbellata

Paraboea umbellata, commonly known as the white star vine, is a climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, star-shaped white flowers that bloom in clusters, giving it its common name. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are usually hairy and have a slightly waxy texture. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.

Paraboea umbellata is valued in horticulture for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
