Pongamia triphylla

Pongamia triphylla, commonly known as the three-leaf pongam, is a leguminous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three leaflets arranged symmetrically. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by pod-like fruits containing oily seeds. These seeds are rich in bioactive compounds and have been studied for their potential medicinal and industrial applications.

Its growth habit and ecological role make it an important species in tropical ecosystems.
