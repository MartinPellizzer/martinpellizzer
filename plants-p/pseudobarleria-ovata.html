Pseudobarleria ovata

Pseudobarleria ovata, commonly known as the slender false balm, is a perennial shrub native to southern Africa.

It features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically gray-green in color, often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that range in color from pale blue to violet, which bloom in clusters during the warmer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.

Pseudobarleria ovata is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
