Pharnaceum obtusifolium

Pharnaceum obtusifolium, commonly known as the broadleaf pharnaceum, is a perennial herb native to the temperate regions of the northern hemisphere.

It typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a waxy surface and a prominent midrib. Its flowers are small, clustered in spikes, and are usually pink or purple in color.

The species is characterized by its robust root system and its ability to thrive in nutrient-rich soils.
