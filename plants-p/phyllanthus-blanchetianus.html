Phyllanthus blanchetianus

Phyllanthus blanchetianus, commonly known as the African date palm, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is a small to medium-sized tree with pinnately compound leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in panicles, which are followed by edible, fleshy fruits resembling dates. Its botanical features include a robust root system and the ability to thrive in poor soil conditions, making it adaptable to various environments.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in local ecosystems.
