Prunus laurocerasus var. magnoliifolia

Prunus laurocerasus var. magnoliifolia, commonly known as the sweet cherry laurel, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are elliptical and often have a waxy texture. The plant produces fragrant, white flowers in clusters during spring, which are followed by edible, sweet red berries. It thrives in well-drained soil and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value.

However, it can be toxic if ingested, making it important to handle with care.
