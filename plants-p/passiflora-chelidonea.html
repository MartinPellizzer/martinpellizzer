Passiflora chelidonea

Passiflora chelidonea, commonly known as the blue passionflower, is a climbing vine native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking blue and white flowers, which are adorned with intricate patterns and sepals that resemble petals. The plant produces edible, yellow-orange berries that are rich in antioxidants and have a tart flavor. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are woody and twining.

Passiflora chelidonea is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
