Pandanus flintinsularis

Pandanus flintinsularis, commonly known as the Socotra palm, is a unique species native to the island of Socotra.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, fan-shaped leaves that are often covered in a thick layer of white, waxy hair. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters at the top of the plant. Its fruit is a multiple berry that develops from the flower and is typically dispersed by wind.

This species is notable for its distinctive appearance and adaptation to the arid environment of Socotra.
