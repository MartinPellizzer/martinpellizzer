Platytaenia komarovii

Platytaenia komarovii, commonly known as the Komarov's plumevetch, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, rocky habitats. The plant features slender stems and small, compound leaves with a pinnate structure. Its flowers are usually pale yellow or greenish, arranged in racemes, and are adapted for pollination by insects.

Platytaenia komarovii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in arid gardens.
