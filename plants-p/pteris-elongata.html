Pteris elongata

Pteris elongata, commonly known as the elongated water fern, is a species of fern native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped fronds that are often slightly curled. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests or wetlands. Its reproductive structures, including sori, are located on the underside of the fronds.

Pteris elongata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
