Pavonia leptocarpa

Pavonia leptocarpa, commonly known as the slenderleaf false mallow, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features showy, pink to red bract-like flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has slender, hairy stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Pavonia leptocarpa is often found in open woodlands and along roadsides, contributing to local biodiversity.
