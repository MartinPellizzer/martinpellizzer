Passerina striata

Passerina striata, commonly known as the streaked tyrannulet, is a small passerine bird native to South America.

It belongs to the family Tyrannidae, which includes numerous species of flycatchers and tyrant flycatchers. The streaked tyrannulet is characterized by its distinctive black and white plumage with faint streaking on its sides. It typically inhabits open woodlands and shrublands, foraging for insects in the understory.

This species is known for its melodious song and is relatively common in its range.
