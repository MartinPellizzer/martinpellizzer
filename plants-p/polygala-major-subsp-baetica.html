Polygala major subsp. baetica

Polygala major subsp.

baetica, commonly known as the Spanish milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula. It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, clustered flowers.

The plant typically grows in dry, rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to Mediterranean climates. Its flowers are usually pale yellow or greenish, with a distinctive spurred structure.

It is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and scrublands.
