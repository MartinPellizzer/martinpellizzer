Pleiarina subserrata

Pleiarina subserrata, commonly known as the serrated pleiarina, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its slender stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are linear and slightly serrated, contributing to its common name.

The flowers are usually pale blue or purple and bloom in late spring to early summer.
