Persica merleti

Persica merleti, commonly known as the Merleti peach, is a unique cultivar of peach characterized by its distinct botanical features.

It belongs to the Prunus genus and is native to the Mediterranean region, where it thrives in temperate climates. The plant produces small, round fruits with a thin, fuzzy skin and a sweet, aromatic pulp. Its flowers are typically pink to white, with five petals and prominent stamens, typical of the Prunus family.

The Merleti peach is valued for its ornamental appeal and mild flavor, making it suitable for both cultivation and ornamental purposes.
