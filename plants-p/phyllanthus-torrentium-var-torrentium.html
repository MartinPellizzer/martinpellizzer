Phyllanthus torrentium var. torrentium

Phyllanthus torrentium var. torrentium, commonly known as the torrent fig or river fig, is a shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its small, opposite leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-yellow in color. Its fruits are fleshy, berry-like structures that contain several seeds.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found near streams and in forested areas.
