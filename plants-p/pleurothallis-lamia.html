Pleurothallis lamia

Pleurothallis lamia, commonly known as the Ghost Orchid, is a rare and enigmatic orchid species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, elongated flower spikes that often bear small, white or pale green flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded forest environments. Its botanical features include intricate, fragrant blooms and a unique ability to thrive in low-light conditions.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, Pleurothallis lamia is considered a conservation concern.
