Phyllanthus pseudoguyanensis

Phyllanthus pseudoguyanensis, commonly known as the Guyana phyllanthus, is a flowering plant belonging to the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is native to South America, particularly found in regions of Guyana and Venezuela. The plant is characterized by its small, clustered flowers and smooth, waxy leaves. It typically grows in tropical rainforests and prefers moist, well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
