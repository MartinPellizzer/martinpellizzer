Polygonum radicosum

Polygonum radicosum, commonly known as redroot smartweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its upright stems that often turn reddish near the base, and its lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink to red in color, blooming from late spring to early fall. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

Polygonum radicosum is a hardy species that can spread through both seeds and rhizomes, making it a common weed in agricultural and disturbed areas.
