Pavetta nemoralis

Pavetta nemoralis, commonly known as the common pavetta, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in open woodland and rocky habitats, often along stream banks. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with a rough, hairy texture. Its flowers are small, white or pale blue, and are clustered in loose inflorescences.

Pavetta nemoralis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
