Pellaea gastonyi

Pellaea gastonyi, commonly known as the golden fern, is a species of fern native to South America.

It belongs to the family Pellaeaceae and is characterized by its delicate, lace-like fronds. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its spore-producing structures, called sori, are located on the underside of the fronds.

Pellaea gastonyi is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
