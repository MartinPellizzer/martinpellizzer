Pittosporum pervillei

Pittosporum pervillei, commonly known as the Perville's pittosporum, is a shrub or small tree native to New Zealand.

It features lance-shaped leaves that are glossy and dark green, often with a slight reddish tinge on the underside. The plant produces fragrant white or pale yellow flowers in clusters, which are followed by small, round berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers a sunny to partially shaded location.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
