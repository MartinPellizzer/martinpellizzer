Plantago juncoides subsp. scabrior

Plantago juncoides subsp.

scabrior, commonly known as roughleaf plantain, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It typically grows in moist, disturbed soils such as roadsides, fields, and along riverbanks.

The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in small, stiff hairs, giving them a rough texture. Its flowering stems bear clusters of small, greenish-white flowers that are arranged in a spike.

The plant is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions, making it a common sight in temperate regions.
