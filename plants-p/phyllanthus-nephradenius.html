Phyllanthus nephradenius

Phyllanthus nephradenius, commonly known as the kidney bean plant, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its alternate, simple, and often pubescent leaves that are typically elliptical or ovate in shape. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in axillary clusters, followed by capsule fruits containing small, round seeds. It is adapted to grow in dry, well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas.

The species is noted for its potential medicinal uses in traditional medicine systems.
