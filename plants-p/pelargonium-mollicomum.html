Pelargonium mollicomum

Pelargonium mollicomum, commonly known as the velvet-leaf geranium, is a species native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its soft, velvety leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces small, clustered flowers in shades of pink or red, typically blooming in spring and summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and containers.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is often cultivated as a decorative plant.
