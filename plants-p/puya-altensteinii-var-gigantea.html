Puya altensteinii var. gigantea

Puya altensteinii var. gigantea, commonly known as the giant Puya, is a striking plant native to the high Andes of South America.

It is a member of the bromeliad family and is characterized by its tall, upright stems and large, showy inflorescences. The plant produces striking purple or red flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting various pollinators. It thrives in cool, humid climates and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
