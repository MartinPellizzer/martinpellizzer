Plantago discolor var. gandogeri

Plantago discolor var. gandogeri, commonly known as the Gandogeri plantain, is a variant of the common plantain species native to parts of East Africa.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a dense layer of fine, white hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in a spike, which are followed by capsules containing numerous small seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and along riverbanks.

This variant is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
