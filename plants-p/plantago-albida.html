Plantago albida

Plantago albida, commonly known as the white plantain, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces small, white or pale green flowers arranged in a dense spike, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, fields, and along roadsides.

The plant is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies for various ailments.
