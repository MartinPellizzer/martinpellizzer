Philodendron wendlandii

Philodendron wendlandii, commonly known as the Wendland's philodendron, is a tropical climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It features large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 inches in length, often with a waxy texture and a deep green to dark green color. The plant has a unique, twisted stem that allows it to climb and trail, making it popular in hanging baskets or as a climbing plant in greenhouses. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires consistent moisture and high humidity to flourish.

Its foliage makes it a striking addition to indoor gardens and tropical landscapes.
