Passiflora mooreana

Passiflora mooreana, commonly known as the Moore's passionflower, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, complex flowers that feature five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments surrounding the reproductive structures. The plant produces edible berries that are typically yellow or orange when ripe. Its leaves are heart-shaped with a velvety texture and a strong, aromatic scent.

Passiflora mooreana is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its unique floral display and ecological value to pollinators.
