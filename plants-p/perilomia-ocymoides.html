Perilomia ocymoides

Perilomia ocymoides, commonly known as the violet ground orchid, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that range in color from purple to white. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and has a low-growing habit with narrow, grass-like leaves.

Its flowers are often clustered in inflorescences and bloom during the cooler months, making it a notable species in its native habitat.
