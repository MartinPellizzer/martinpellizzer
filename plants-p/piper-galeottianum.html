Piper galeottianum

Piper galeottianum, commonly known as the Galloping pepper, is a tropical climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many species of pepper plants. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by elongated, slender fruits that turn red when mature. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a glossy surface with a strong, peppery aroma.

The plant is often used in traditional medicine for its potential anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties.
