Pfaffia sericantha

Pfaffia sericantha, commonly known as Brazilian ginseng, is a perennial herb native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It belongs to the family Compositae and is characterized by its upright stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, followed by small, oval-shaped fruits. It is valued for its potential medicinal properties and is often used in traditional medicine.

Pfaffia sericantha thrives in shaded, moist environments and is known for its adaptability to various soil types.
