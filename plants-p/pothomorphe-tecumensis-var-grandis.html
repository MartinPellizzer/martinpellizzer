Pothomorphe tecumensis var. grandis

Pothomorphe tecumensis var. grandis, commonly known as the large-flowered pothomorphe, is a striking member of the Acanthaceae family.

It is characterized by its showy, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to purple, often with contrasting yellow throats. Native to parts of Central and South America, this plant thrives in moist, shaded environments. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a glossy texture, while its stems are erect and somewhat woody.

This variant is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like hummingbirds and butterflies.
