Pongamia paniculata

Pongamia paniculata, commonly known as the Indian beech or karanj, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically 20–40 cm long and consist of 10–20 pairs of elliptic leaflets. The plant produces small, fragrant yellow flowers followed by elongated, woody pods containing seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in coastal and sandy soils.

Pongamia paniculata is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen and its potential use in biofuel production.
