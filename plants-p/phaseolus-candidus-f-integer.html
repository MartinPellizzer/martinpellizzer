Phaseolus candidus f. integer

Phaseolus candidus f. integer, commonly known as the white bean or white phaseolus, is a botanical variant of the common bean species.

It is characterized by its pale green to white flowers and small, round pods that contain edible seeds. This plant is typically found in tropical and subtropical regions, thriving in warm, humid climates. It belongs to the Fabaceae family, known for its leguminous properties and nitrogen-fixing capabilities.

Its botanical features make it a valuable plant for both ornamental and agricultural purposes.
