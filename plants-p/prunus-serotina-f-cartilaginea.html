Prunus serotina f. cartilaginea

Prunus serotina f. cartilaginea, commonly known as the cartilaginous black cherry, is a rare variant of the black cherry tree.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy, and often reddish or purplish fruits, which are distinct from the typical drupes of the species. The plant exhibits a slow growth rate and is typically found in moist, well-drained soils in shaded environments. Its botanical features make it a subject of interest in horticultural and ecological studies.

Due to its rarity, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens for conservation and research purposes.
