Physostegia imbricata

Physostegia imbricata, commonly known as the Indian blanket flower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features showy, bilaterally symmetrical flowers that are typically pink to red in color, with a distinctive white or pale pink throat. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are usually hairy and covered in fine pubescence. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained, sandy or loamy soils.

Physostegia imbricata is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
