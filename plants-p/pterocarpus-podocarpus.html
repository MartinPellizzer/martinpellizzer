Pterocarpus podocarpus

Pterocarpus podocarpus, commonly known as the African padauk, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive red heartwood, which is highly valued for its durability and aesthetic appeal. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers followed by flat, pod-like fruits that contain seeds.

Its leaves are bipinnate, consisting of numerous small leaflets, and it thrives in well-drained soils in humid climates.
