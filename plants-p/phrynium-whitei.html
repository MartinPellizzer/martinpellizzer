Phrynium whitei

Phrynium whitei, commonly known as the white ginger plant, is a tropical perennial belonging to the Araceae family.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often variegated with white and green patterns. The plant produces striking inflorescences consisting of a spadix surrounded by a colorful spathe. Native to Southeast Asia, it thrives in humid, shaded environments.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its aesthetic appeal and air-purifying qualities.
