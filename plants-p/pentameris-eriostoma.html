Pentameris eriostoma

Pentameris eriostoma, commonly known as the silky bluebell, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its striking blue-violet flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has slender, branched stems and lance-shaped leaves with a silvery underside. Its flowers are typically arranged in loose clusters and are pollinated by insects.

Pentameris eriostoma thrives in moist, shaded woodlands and is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance.
