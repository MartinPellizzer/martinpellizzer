Persicaria nikaii

Persicaria nikaii, commonly known as the Japanese knotweed, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, bamboo-like stems and heart-shaped leaves that are often reddish in color. The plant produces small, pinkish flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in late summer. It is a perennial species that thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils and can grow rapidly under favorable conditions.

Due to its aggressive growth, it is considered an invasive species in many regions outside its native range.
