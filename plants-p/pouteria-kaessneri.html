Pouteria kaessneri

Pouteria kaessneri, commonly known as the Brazil nut tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Amazon Basin.

It is characterized by its smooth, grayish-brown bark and large, compound leaves that can reach up to 3 meters in length. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers followed by large, woody capsules that contain the edible Brazil nuts. Its fruit is an important part of the ecosystem, providing food for various wildlife species.

The tree plays a significant role in the biodiversity and economic value of the Amazon rainforest.
