Petalostemon arenicola

Petalostemon arenicola, commonly known as the sand petalostemon, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Australia.

It is a low-growing perennial herb with small, fleshy leaves adapted to conserve water. The plant produces clusters of tiny, pale yellow to white flowers that bloom in spring. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to thrive in sandy or rocky soils.

Due to its specialized habitat, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species in its native range.
