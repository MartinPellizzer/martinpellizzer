Poiretia scandens var. intermedia

Poiretia scandens var. intermedia, commonly known as the climbing poiretia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vigorous climbing habit and elongated, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, often with a subtle fragrance. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
