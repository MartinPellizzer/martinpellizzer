Piptatherum barbellatum

Piptatherum barbellatum, commonly known as the slender false brome, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides, meadows, and forest edges. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves with a distinctive hairy surface, and its inflorescence consists of dense, spike-like clusters of small, two-lobed flowers. It is often found in association with other grasses and forbs in early successional plant communities.

The species is valued for its role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
