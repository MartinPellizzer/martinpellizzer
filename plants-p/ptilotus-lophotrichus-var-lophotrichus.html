Ptilotus lophotrichus var. lophotrichus

Ptilotus lophotrichus var. lophotrichus, commonly known as the silky ptilotus, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its soft, silky hairs covering its stems and leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, often appearing in late winter to early spring. It thrives in well-drained sandy soils and is often found in coastal and inland regions.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and public spaces for its unique appearance and resilience.
