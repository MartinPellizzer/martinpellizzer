Plantago erubescens

Plantago erubescens, commonly known as red plantain or redleaf plantain, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features upright, hairy stems that bear alternate, lance-shaped leaves with a distinctive reddish hue on the underside. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in a dense spike, which are typically wind-pollinated. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their soothing and healing properties.

The species is adapted to thrive in dry, rocky soils and is often found in disturbed or arid environments.
