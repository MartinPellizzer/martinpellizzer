Parthenium hysterophorus var. lyratum

Parthenium hysterophorus var. lyratum, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a herbaceous annual plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are hairy and often have a distinctive veining pattern. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, which are followed by fluffy, white to pale brown seeds. It is commonly found in disturbed soils and can become invasive in certain ecosystems.

The plant is also known for its allergenic properties due to the release of irritant compounds.
