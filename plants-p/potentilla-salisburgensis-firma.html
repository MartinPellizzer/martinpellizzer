Potentilla salisburgensis firma

Potentilla salisburgensis firma, commonly known as the Salzburg cinquefoil, is a low-growing perennial shrub native to the alpine regions of Central Europe.

It features wiry, arching stems covered in small, dark green leaves that are typically arranged in clusters. The plant produces showy, bright yellow flowers in early summer, which are borne on long, slender pedicels. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in mountainous habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is cultivated in gardens and alpine plant collections.
