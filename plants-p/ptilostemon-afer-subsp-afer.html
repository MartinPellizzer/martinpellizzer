Ptilostemon afer subsp. afer

Ptilostemon afer subsp.

afer, commonly known as the African sand mat, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit with narrow, grass-like leaves.

The plant produces small, pale blue to white flowers arranged in dense clusters. It thrives in well-drained sandy soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is often cultivated in xeriscapes and rock gardens.
