Pilosella thaliana

Pilosella thaliana, commonly known as the mouse-ear chickweed, is a small flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its low-growing habit and hairy leaves. The plant produces small, white or yellowish flowers that are typically clustered in a single head. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, along paths, and in gardens.

Pilosella thaliana is also known for its ability to spread rapidly through seeds and vegetative reproduction.
