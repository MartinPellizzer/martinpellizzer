Polypodium rivale

Polypodium rivale, commonly known as the water fern, is a species of fern that thrives in moist, shaded environments.

It is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped fronds that are typically divided into smaller, finger-like segments. The plant produces spores rather than seeds, and its reproductive structures are located on the underside of the fronds. Polypodium rivale is often found growing on rocks or in wet soil near streams and waterfalls.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and ecological restoration projects.
