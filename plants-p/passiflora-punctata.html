Passiflora punctata

Passiflora punctata, commonly known as the punctate passionflower, is a climbing vine native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, intricate flowers that feature five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments. The leaves are typically heart-shaped with a rough texture and often have a waxy coating. This plant produces edible, tropical fruits that are yellow or orange in color and have a tangy flavor.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit production.
