Poa setifolia

Poa setifolia, commonly known as slender hairgrass, is a species of grass native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its fine, needle-like leaves that are often covered in short, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, stream banks, and woodland edges. Its inflorescence consists of a loose cluster of spikelets, which are usually pale green to yellowish in color.

Poa setifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping or as a ground cover in suitable climates.
