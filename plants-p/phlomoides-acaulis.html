Phlomoides acaulis

Phlomoides acaulis, commonly known as the woolly phlomis, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that gives it a distinctive, soft appearance. The plant produces spikes of small, pale blue to purple flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental value make it a popular choice for xeriscaping and garden design.
