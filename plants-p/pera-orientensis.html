Pera orientensis

Pera orientensis, commonly known as the Eastern pear, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its oval, dark green leaves and clusters of small, white flowers. The plant produces edible berries that are typically consumed fresh or used in culinary applications. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is not widely distributed outside its native range.
