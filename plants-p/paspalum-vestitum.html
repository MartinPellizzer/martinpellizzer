Paspalum vestitum

Paspalum vestitum, commonly known as velvetgrass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and dark green, velvety foliage. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, two-lobed flowers. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions.

Paspalum vestitum is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its attractive appearance and low maintenance requirements.
