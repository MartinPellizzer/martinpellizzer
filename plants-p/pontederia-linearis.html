Pontederia linearis

Pontederia linearis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved water hyacinth, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and spikes of blue or purple flowers. The plant thrives in shallow freshwater environments such as ponds and marshes. It has a rhizomatous root system that allows it to spread rapidly through vegetative propagation.

Its presence can significantly impact aquatic ecosystems due to its ability to form dense mats.
