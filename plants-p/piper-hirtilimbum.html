Piper hirtilimbum

Piper hirtilimbum, commonly known as the hairy pepper vine, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species. The plant is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges. Its small, greenish-white flowers are followed by berries that range in color from green to red as they mature.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
