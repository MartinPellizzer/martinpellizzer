Paspalum plicatulum subsp. montevidense

Paspalum plicatulum subsp.

montevidense, commonly known as the Montevidense grass, is a perennial grass species native to South America, particularly Uruguay and southern Brazil. It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, blade-like leaves that are typically covered with fine hairs.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing minute florets, which are often wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist to wet environments, such as marshes and seasonally flooded areas, and is adapted to tolerate waterlogged soils.

Its botanical features make it an important species in wetland ecosystems and a potential candidate for restoration projects in disturbed habitats.
