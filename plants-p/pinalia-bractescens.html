Pinalia bractescens

Pinalia bractescens, commonly known as the Mexican bract fern, is a species of fern native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its delicate, feathery fronds that are often covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky crevices. Its reproductive structures, known as sori, are located on the underside of the fronds.

Pinalia bractescens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
