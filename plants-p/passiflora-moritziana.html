Passiflora moritziana

Passiflora moritziana, commonly known as the Moritz's passionflower, is a climbing vine native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful flowers that feature a unique arrangement of petals, sepals, and filaments. The plant produces blue or purple berries that are edible and contain seeds. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped with a rough texture, and it thrives in warm, humid climates.

Passiflora moritziana is valued both for its ornamental beauty and its ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
