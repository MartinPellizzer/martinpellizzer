Prunus mitis

Prunus mitis, commonly known as the Japanese white cherry, is a deciduous tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in early spring. The tree produces small, dark red berries that are edible but not widely consumed. It thrives in temperate climates and is often planted for its ornamental value.

Prunus mitis is also valued for its role in traditional Japanese culture and landscape design.
