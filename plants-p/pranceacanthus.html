Pranceacanthus

Pranceacanthus, commonly known as the silver leaf plant, is a tropical species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, waxy leaves that often display a silvery or blue-gray hue. The plant typically grows as an evergreen shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 10 feet. Its flowers are small and clustered, usually appearing in shades of white or pale yellow.

Pranceacanthus thrives in humid, well-drained soil and requires partial to full sunlight for optimal growth.
