Polygala japonica var. cinerascens

Polygala japonica var. cinerascens, commonly known as the ash-gray polygala, is a flowering plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its slender, branched stems and delicate, pinkish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky slopes. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
