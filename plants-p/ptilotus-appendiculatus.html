Ptilotus appendiculatus

Ptilotus appendiculatus, commonly known as the "desert lily," is a flowering plant native to arid regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its striking, showy yellow or orange flowers that bloom during the warmer months. The plant has a compact, bushy growth habit with hairy stems and leaves, which help reduce water loss in its harsh environment. Its flowers are typically arranged in dense clusters at the tips of the stems.

Ptilotus appendiculatus is well adapted to drought conditions, making it a resilient species in sandy or rocky soils.
