Periploca fruticosa

Periploca fruticosa, commonly known as the Mediterranean spurge, is a shrub native to southern Europe and northern Africa.

It is characterized by its woody stems and small, yellowish-green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers followed by orange-red berries. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in coastal and Mediterranean regions.

Periploca fruticosa is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
