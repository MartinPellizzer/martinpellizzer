Paeonia brownii var. californica

Paeonia brownii var. californica, commonly known as the California peony, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and red, typically blooming in late spring. The plant features compound leaves with serrated edges and a robust root system adapted to rocky and well-drained soils. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.

The species is also valued for its historical and cultural significance in native plant communities.
