Peramium cyrtoglossum

Peramium cyrtoglossum, commonly known as the mountain hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of Central America.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and are often adorned with a distinctive yellow throat. The plant typically grows in rocky or well-drained soils and thrives in cool, moist environments. Its leaves are palmately lobed and have a velvety texture, adding to its ornamental appeal.

Peramium cyrtoglossum is valued for its beauty and is often cultivated in gardens that mimic its natural habitat.
