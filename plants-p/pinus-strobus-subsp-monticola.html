Pinus strobus subsp. monticola

Pinus strobus subsp.

monticola, commonly known as the mountain white pine, is a subspecies of the eastern white pine native to the Appalachian Mountains. It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and long, soft needles that grow in bundles of five.

The cones are relatively small and have a distinctive shape, with scales that open to release the seeds. This subspecies thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in mixed hardwood forests.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
