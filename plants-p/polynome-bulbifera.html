Polynome bulbifera

Polynome bulbifera, commonly known as the bulbous polynome, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its bulb-like structures that store nutrients, enabling it to survive in seasonal environments. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer. Its leaves are typically broad and strap-like, often arranged in a rosette pattern.

The botanical interest of Polynome bulbifera lies in its unique morphology and adaptation to tropical climates.
