Paspalidium ankarense

Paspalidium ankarense, commonly known as the African grass or Ankarene grass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, wiry stems that often form dense clumps. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Its leaves are narrow and blade-like, with a distinctive blue-green coloration.

Paspalidium ankarense is adapted to a variety of soil types and is often found in disturbed or semi-arid environments.
