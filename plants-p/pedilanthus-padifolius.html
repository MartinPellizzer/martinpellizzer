Pedilanthus padifolius

Pedilanthus padifolius, commonly known as the yellow arrowhead, is a shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. The plant has elongated, lance-shaped leaves that are often arranged oppositely along the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for tropical and subtropical climates.

Its unique appearance and ornamental value make it a popular choice for gardens and botanical collections.
