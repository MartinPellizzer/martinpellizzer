Phegopteris pteroidea var. webbae

Phegopteris pteroidea var. webbae, commonly known as the Webb's maidenhair fern, is a variant of the maidenhair fern family.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that are typically bi- or tri-pinnate. The plant prefers moist, shaded environments and is often found in temperate regions. Its spore-producing structures, called sori, are clustered on the underside of the fronds.

This fern is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
