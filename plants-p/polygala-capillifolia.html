Polygala capillifolia

Polygala capillifolia, commonly known as the feathery mistletoe, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a hemiparasitic species, meaning it partially relies on host plants for water and nutrients while also performing photosynthesis. The plant produces small, delicate flowers that are often clustered in dense inflorescences. Its leaves are narrow and linear, and it is often found growing on trees such as acacia and eucalyptus.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
