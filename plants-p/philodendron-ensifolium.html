Philodendron ensifolium

Philodendron ensifolium, commonly known as the swordleaf philodendron, is a tropical climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its long, narrow, and lance-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant has a vining growth habit and produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Its foliage makes it a popular choice for indoor gardening due to its aesthetic appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
