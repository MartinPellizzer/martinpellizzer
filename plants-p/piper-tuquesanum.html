Piper tuquesanum

Piper tuquesanum, commonly known as the Tuquesanum pepper plant, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of Central and South America, particularly found in countries like Colombia and Venezuela. The plant is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and heart-shaped leaves with a glossy surface. Its small, greenish-white flowers are followed by the production of small, peppery berries.

This species is valued for its aromatic leaves and fruits, which are used in traditional medicine and local cuisines.
