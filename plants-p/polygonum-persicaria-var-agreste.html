Polygonum persicaria var. agreste

Polygonum persicaria var. agreste, commonly known as common knotgrass, is a herbaceous plant belonging to the Polygonaceae family.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and alternate, ovate to lanceolate leaves with a distinctive reddish midrib. The plant produces small, greenish to reddish flowers in dense, spike-like clusters, typically blooming in late summer. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in wet meadows, ditches, and along stream banks.

This species is known for its adaptability and is considered an invasive weed in some regions.
