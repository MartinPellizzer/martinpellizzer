Phaseolus oblongus var. turcicus

Phaseolus oblongus var. turcicus, commonly known as the Turkish bean or Turkish kidney bean, is a climbing legume native to the Mediterranean region.

It produces elongated, kidney-shaped pods that are typically green when mature, containing edible seeds. The plant has compound leaves with pinnate arrangements and is characterized by its hairy stems and flowers that range from white to pale purple. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is valued for its historical use in traditional cuisines and its adaptability to various growing conditions.
