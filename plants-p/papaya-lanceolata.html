Papaya lanceolata

Papaya lanceolata, commonly known as the lanceleaf papaya, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Caricaceae family and is characterized by its elongated, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces large, yellow-orange fruits that are edible and rich in nutrients. Its flowers are typically clustered in terminal panicles, and the plant thrives in warm, humid climates.

It is often cultivated for both its fruit and ornamental value.
