Potentilla alchemollis

Potentilla alchemollis, commonly known as the golden cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers and is typically found in dry, rocky habitats. The plant has compound leaves with five leaflets, and it produces small, five-petaled flowers in the spring. It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.

Potentilla alchemollis is also valued for its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
