Persea sterculioides

Persea sterculioides, commonly known as the Mexican persimmon, is a small evergreen tree native to Mexico and Central America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and fragrant, white flowers. The tree produces small, orange to reddish fruits that are edible when fully ripe. Its botanical features include a smooth, gray bark and a tendency to grow in tropical and subtropical climates.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of edible fruit.
