Pleurothallis dubia

Pleurothallis dubia, commonly known as the ghost orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a small, epiphytic orchid that typically grows on trees or rocks in humid, shaded environments. The plant produces delicate, fragrant flowers with white and green petals, often resembling a ghostly appearance. It has a short, fleshy rhizome and is known for its unique growth habits and rarity in the wild.

Due to its ecological significance and aesthetic appeal, it is highly sought after by orchid enthusiasts and conservationists alike.
