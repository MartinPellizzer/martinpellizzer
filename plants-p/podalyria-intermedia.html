Podalyria intermedia

Podalyria intermedia, commonly known as the intermediate podalyria, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect growth habit and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink, often in clusters. Podalyria intermedia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it adaptable to various garden conditions.
