Potentilla thuringiaca var. parviflora

Potentilla thuringiaca var. parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered thuringian cinquefoil, is a low-growing perennial plant native to central Europe.

It features wiry, often hairy stems that produce clusters of small, yellowish flowers in early summer. The leaves are alternate, palmately divided into five oval to lance-shaped leaflets, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders.

This variety is valued for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in marginal habitats.
