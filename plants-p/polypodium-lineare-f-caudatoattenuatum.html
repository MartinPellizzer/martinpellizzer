Polypodium lineare f. caudatoattenuatum

Polypodium lineare f. caudatoattenuatum, commonly known as the long-tailed maidenhair fern, is a fern species characterized by its distinctive fronds.

It features elongated, lanceolate leaves with a gradual tapering tip, giving it a unique appearance. The fronds are typically bright green and have a delicate, feathery texture. This fern thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and conservation efforts.
