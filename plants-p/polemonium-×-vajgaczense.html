Polemonium × vajgaczense

Polemonium × vajgaczense, commonly known as the Vajgaczense Jacob's ladder, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Polemonium caeruleum and Polemonium acutellum.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm, featuring slender stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue to purple, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Native to the Carpathian region, it thrives in moist, shady woodland habitats.

Its ornamental value makes it a sought-after plant in gardens with the right conditions.
