Paepalanthus reclinatus

Paepalanthus reclinatus, commonly known as the drooping pincushion cactus, is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny stems that form a spherical or cushion-like shape. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, adding a striking visual contrast to its rugged foliage. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and coastal regions.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological adaptations, it is a notable species in the cactus family.
