Pinalia fragrans

Pinalia fragrans, commonly known as the sweet-scented pinalia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and summer seasons. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stems, giving it a graceful appearance. Its roots are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soil and requires partial to full sunlight for optimal growth.
