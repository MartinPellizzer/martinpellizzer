Phillyrea longifolia subsp.

pardilloana, commonly known as the Spanish broom or pardilloan Phillyrea, is a shrub native to the Iberian Peninsula. It is characterized by its small, leathery leaves and clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in spring.

The plant produces small, elongated fruits that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in rocky or dry soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions. Phillyrea longifolia subsp.

pardilloana is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
