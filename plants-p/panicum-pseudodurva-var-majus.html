Panicum pseudodurva var. majus

Panicum pseudodurva var. majus, commonly known as giant false panicgrass, is a tall, perennial grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in wetland habitats, including marshes and swamps, where it thrives in moist to saturated soil conditions. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces dense, spike-like inflorescences that are often reddish in color. It is characterized by its rapid growth and ability to form dense stands, making it a common component of wetland vegetation.

Its botanical structure supports its role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
