Plantago australis subsp. dielsiana

Plantago australis subsp.

dielsiana, commonly known as the narrow-leaved plantain, is a perennial herb native to parts of Europe and Asia. It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flowers in late spring to early summer.

The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, wetlands, and along stream banks. It has a robust root system that helps stabilize soil and prevent erosion.

The species is valued for its medicinal properties and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
