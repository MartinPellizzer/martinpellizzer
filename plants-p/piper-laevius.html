Piper laevius

Piper laevius, commonly known as the smooth pipevine, is a climbing plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Piperaceae, which includes many species of flowering plants known for their aromatic properties. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in spikes, and its leaves are typically elongated with a slightly rough texture. It is often found growing in moist, shaded areas such as forests and along stream banks.

The plant is also noted for its use in traditional medicine, though its exact pharmacological properties remain under study.
