Portulaca rzedowskiana

Portulaca rzedowskiana, commonly known as the Mexican portulaca, is a species of flowering plant native to Mexico.

It is a low-growing annual herb with fleshy, succulent leaves and vibrant yellow flowers. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to dry conditions. Its flowers typically bloom in the morning and close by midday, a characteristic known as nyctinasty.

Portulaca rzedowskiana is often found in rocky soils and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
