Pachypodium griquense

Pachypodium griquense, commonly known as the Grootfontein bottle tree, is a unique succulent native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, swollen stem that stores water, allowing it to survive in arid environments. The plant has sparse, upright leaves that are often deciduous during dry seasons. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, appearing in clusters at the base of the stem.

Pachypodium griquense is well adapted to drought and can thrive in well-drained soils with minimal water.
