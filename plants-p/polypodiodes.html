Polypodiodes

Polypodiodes, commonly known as ferns, are a diverse group of vascular plants that belong to the order Polypodiales.

They are characterized by their fronds, which are typically divided into smaller leaflets called pinnae. Polypodiodes reproduce via spores rather than seeds, and their life cycle involves both a sporophyte and a gametophyte stage. Many species are found in moist, shaded environments, often growing on rocks or trees.

Their unique morphology and ecological roles make them important components of various ecosystems.
