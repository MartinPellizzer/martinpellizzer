Peperomia puberulibacca

Peperomia puberulibacca, commonly known as the velvet peperomia, is a compact, low-maintenance plant native to tropical regions.

It features dense, velvety foliage with a rich green color, often adorned with subtle variegation. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers that appear in clusters, typically during the spring or early summer. Its thick, fleshy leaves are adapted to store water, making it drought-tolerant.

Peperomia puberulibacca thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it an excellent choice for indoor gardening.
