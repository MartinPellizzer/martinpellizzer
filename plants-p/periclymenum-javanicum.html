Periclymenum javanicum

Periclymenum javanicum, commonly known as the Java honeysuckle, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a trailing or climbing vine that produces small, fragrant, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant has opposite, simple leaves that are usually ovate to elliptical in shape. Its fruit is a small, round, red berry that contains several seeds.

Periclymenum javanicum is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest undergrowth.
