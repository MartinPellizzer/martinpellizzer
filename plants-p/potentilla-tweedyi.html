Potentilla tweedyi

Potentilla tweedyi, commonly known as Tweedy's cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a low-growing perennial shrub that typically reaches heights of 10 to 20 centimeters. The plant features wiry stems and bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate, pinnately compound, and have a serrated edge.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or dry habitats.
