Polygala rhynchocarpa

Polygala rhynchocarpa, commonly known as the horned milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the arid regions of central Asia.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, spiny pods that resemble horns, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments. Its flowers are usually small and clustered, with a unique structure that aids in pollination by specific insect species.

Polygala rhynchocarpa is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.
