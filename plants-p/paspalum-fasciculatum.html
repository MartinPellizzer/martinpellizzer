Paspalum fasciculatum

Paspalum fasciculatum, commonly known as slender paspalum, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, erect stems that often form dense clumps. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green color and a hairy texture.

Paspalum fasciculatum is often found in wetland habitats and is adapted to thrive in moist, nutrient-rich soils.
