Pittocaulon calzadanum

Pittocaulon calzadanum, commonly known as the Calzadana lily, is a striking plant native to the mountainous regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that bloom in shades of pink and white, often with a distinctive red or purple stripe down the center. The plant has strap-like, grassy leaves that form a dense rosette near the base. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and valuable species in botanical collections.
