Polygonum interruptum

Polygonum interruptum, commonly known as redroot pigweed, is a herbaceous annual plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its erect, branching stems that often turn reddish near the base. The leaves are alternate, ovate to lanceolate, and covered with fine hairs. It produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters, which are followed by three-lobed, translucent seed capsules.

This plant thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and waste areas.
