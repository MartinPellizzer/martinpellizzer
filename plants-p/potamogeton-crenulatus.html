Potamogeton crenulatus

Potamogeton crenulatus, commonly known as the notched pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in North America.

It has long, slender stems that are often branched and bear small, arrowhead-shaped leaves with notched edges. The plant produces tiny, green flowers that are clustered in spikes, typically found near the water's surface. It plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.

Potamogeton crenulatus is often found in slow-moving waters such as ponds, lakes, and streams.
