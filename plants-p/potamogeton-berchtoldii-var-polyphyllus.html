Potamogeton berchtoldii var. polyphyllus

Potamogeton berchtoldii var. polyphyllus, commonly known as the broad-leaf pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in North America.

It features long, ribbon-like leaves that are typically broad and rounded at the tips, aiding in its identification. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually borne in clusters, and it spreads primarily through stolons and rhizomes. It thrives in shallow, slow-moving waters and is often found in marshes and pond margins.

Its dense growth can provide habitat for aquatic organisms but may also impact water flow and clarity if it becomes overabundant.
