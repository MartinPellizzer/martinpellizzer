Pelargonium ovale

Pelargonium ovale, commonly known as the oval geranium, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of red, pink, and white. The leaves are typically oval-shaped, hence the species name, and are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden borders and containers.

Pelargonium ovale is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
