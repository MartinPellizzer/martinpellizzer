Pultenaea urodon

Pultenaea urodon, commonly known as the narrow-leaved pultenaea, is a shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, often hairy stems and small, trifoliate leaves. The plant produces yellow to orange flowers in spikes, which bloom from late winter to early spring. Its seeds are enclosed in small, hairy pods that mature in autumn.

Pultenaea urodon is typically found in dry, sandy soils and is often used in native gardens for its ornamental value.
