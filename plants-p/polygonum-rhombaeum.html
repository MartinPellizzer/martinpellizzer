Polygonum rhombaeum

Polygonum rhombaeum, commonly known as the diamondweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the wetlands of North America.

It typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, often found in marshes and along the edges of ponds. The plant features alternate, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its small, clustered flowers are usually pink or red, blooming in late summer.

Polygonum rhombaeum is known for its ability to thrive in wet conditions and is sometimes used in wetland restoration projects.
