Podocarpus cinctus

Podocarpus cinctus, commonly known as the lacebark pine, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive bark, which features a striking pattern of green and brown lines. The plant has needle-like leaves that are dark green and glossy, providing a dense canopy. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by woody cones that contain its seeds.

Podocarpus cinctus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in landscaping and traditional medicine.
