Plantago capensis

Plantago capensis, commonly known as the Cape plantain, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It features a rosette of broad, gray-green leaves that are covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish flowers in a spike-like inflorescence, which are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It is often found in sandy or gravelly soils along coastal areas and in disturbed habitats.

The plant is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally in herbal remedies.
