Polychilos valentinii

Polychilos valentinii, commonly known as the Valentine's day orchid, is a rare and delicate flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its small, fragrant flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often on tree trunks or rocks. Its flowers are usually pink or white, with intricate patterns resembling heart shapes, which contribute to its romantic symbolism.

Due to habitat loss and over-collection, it is considered endangered and is protected in several countries.
