Passerina stricta

Passerina stricta, commonly known as the stiff pasqueflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its early spring blooming habit. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine soils and has a compact, upright growth form. Its flowers are usually pale yellow or white, with five to eight petals, and are borne on long, slender stems.

Passerina stricta is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for various ailments.
