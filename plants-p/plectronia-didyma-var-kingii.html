Plectronia didyma var. kingii

Plectronia didyma var. kingii, commonly known as the King's plectronia, is a rare and striking plant native to specific regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its unique, velvety foliage and clusters of small, pale green to white flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is considered a delicate species that is sensitive to overwatering.

Due to its limited natural range, it is sometimes collected by enthusiasts, making it a sought-after specimen in botanical gardens and private collections.
