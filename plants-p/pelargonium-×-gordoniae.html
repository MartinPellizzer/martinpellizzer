Pelargonium × gordoniae

Pelargonium × gordoniae, commonly known as the hybrid geranium, is a flowering plant in the family Geraniaceae.

It is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Pelargonium hortorum and Pelargonium × ibiscifolium. The plant is characterized by its showy, clustered flowers that come in a variety of colors, including red, pink, and white. Its foliage is typically broad and lobed, with a textured surface that adds to its ornamental appeal.

Pelargonium × gordoniae is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant due to its adaptability and aesthetic value.
