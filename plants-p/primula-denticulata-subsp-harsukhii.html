Primula denticulata subsp. harsukhii

Primula denticulata subsp.

harsukhii, commonly known as the Harsukh primrose, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of northern India. It is characterized by its rosette-forming basal leaves that are typically hairy and have finely toothed margins.

The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, often blooming in late winter to early spring. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
