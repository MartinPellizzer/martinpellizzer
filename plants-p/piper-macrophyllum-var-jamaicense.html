Piper macrophyllum var. jamaicense

Piper macrophyllum var. jamaicense, commonly known as Jamaican pepper or wild pepper, is a tropical shrub native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its large, glossy, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white flowers that give way to red, peppercorn-like fruits. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in moist tropical forests. Its leaves and fruits contain various bioactive compounds, making it of interest in both traditional medicine and modern pharmacological research.

The plant's botanical features contribute to its ecological role and potential medicinal applications.
