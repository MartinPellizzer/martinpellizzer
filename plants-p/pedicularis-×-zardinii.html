Pedicularis × zardinii

Pedicularis × zardinii, commonly known as the golden lousewort, is a hybrid species within the genus Pedicularis, native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its striking yellow-orange flowers, which are often adorned with reddish-brown markings. The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine environments, thriving in well-drained, rocky soils. Its unique morphology includes spurred petals and a distinctive floral structure that aids in pollination by insects.

Pedicularis × zardinii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
