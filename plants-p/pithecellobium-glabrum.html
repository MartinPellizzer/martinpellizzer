Pithecellobium glabrum

Pithecellobium glabrum, commonly known as the velvet tamarind, is a shrub native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically pinnately arranged with oval leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by pod-like fruits containing edible seeds. Its bark and leaves have been used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed or secondary forests.
