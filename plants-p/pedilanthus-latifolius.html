Pedilanthus latifolius

Pedilanthus latifolius, commonly known as the broadleaf poinsettia, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful bracts that resemble flowers, which are actually modified leaves. The plant has simple, ovate leaves that are usually dark green and glossy. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-drained soil.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for festive decorations, especially during the holiday season.
