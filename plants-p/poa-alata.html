Poa alata

Poa alata, commonly known as the silver grass, is a perennial grass species native to the northern hemisphere.

It is characterized by its slender, bluish-gray blades that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in open, sunny areas such as meadows and grasslands. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers in early summer, followed by seed heads that are often used in floral arrangements.

Poa alata is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
