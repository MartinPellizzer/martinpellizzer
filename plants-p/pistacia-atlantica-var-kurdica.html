Pistacia atlantica var. kurdica

Pistacia atlantica var. kurdica, commonly known as the Kurdish pistachio, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the mountainous regions of the Middle East and Central Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny branches and compound leaves that are typically pinnate. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by edible, angular nuts enclosed in a hard shell. It thrives in arid and semi-arid climates, often growing at high altitudes.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has traditional uses in local agriculture and medicine.
