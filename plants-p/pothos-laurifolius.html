Pothos laurifolius

Pothos laurifolius, commonly known as the shining pothos, is a tropical evergreen vine native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green, oval-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in a spiral pattern along the stems. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers, followed by yellowish berries that are toxic to humans and animals. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Pothos laurifolius is popular as a houseplant due to its attractive foliage and low maintenance requirements.
