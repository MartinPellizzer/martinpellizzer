Polygala paniculata f. humilis

Polygala paniculata f. humilis, commonly known as the dwarf milkvetch, is a herbaceous perennial native to southern Africa.

It features slender, upright stems with small, lance-shaped leaves arranged in a basal rosette. The plant produces delicate, raceme-like inflorescences bearing yellowish-green flowers with distinctive, thread-like filaments. It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and unique appearance, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
