Protea aurea subsp. aurea

Protea aurea subsp.

aurea, commonly known as the golden protea, is a striking South African plant belonging to the Proteaceae family. It is characterized by its large, showy, yellowish-orange inflorescences that resemble a cluster of flowers, with numerous small, tubular flowers clustered around a central axis.

The plant is native to the Western Cape region of South Africa, where it thrives in well-drained, acidic soils. It is often found in fynbos vegetation, which is a unique and diverse plant community adapted to the region's Mediterranean climate.

The golden protea is not only ornamental but also plays a role in supporting local pollinators such as bees and birds.
