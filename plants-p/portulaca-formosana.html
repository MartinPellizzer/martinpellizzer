Portulaca formosana

Portulaca formosana, commonly known as the Taiwan purslane, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its fleshy, succulent leaves and vibrant, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in the morning. The plant thrives in hot, arid environments and is drought-tolerant, making it well-suited for rocky or sandy soils. Its stems are often prostrate, spreading along the ground, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Portulaca formosana is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and ability to thrive in challenging conditions.
