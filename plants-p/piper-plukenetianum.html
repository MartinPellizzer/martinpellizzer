Piper plukenetianum

Piper plukenetianum, commonly known as the Amazonian pepper or the false black pepper, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes over 3,000 species of flowering plants. The plant is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and heart-shaped leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. Its fruits are small, berry-like structures that contain the aromatic seeds used for culinary and medicinal purposes.

Due to its unique properties, it is also of interest for research into potential pharmaceutical applications.
