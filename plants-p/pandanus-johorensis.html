Pandanus johorensis

Pandanus johorensis, commonly known as the Javanese pandanus, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and long, narrow leaves that are often used in traditional crafts. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically clustered in spikes at the top of the stem. Its roots and leaves have been used in various cultural and medicinal practices.

The species is well adapted to coastal and wetland environments, thriving in high humidity and saline conditions.
