Pereskia autumnalis

Pereskia autumnalis, commonly known as the Christmas cactus, is a unique succulent plant native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems that are covered in short, rigid hairs, which help reduce water loss. The plant produces vibrant flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, typically during the holiday season. Its leaves are narrow and leathery, and it has a distinctive appearance that sets it apart from other cacti.

Pereskia autumnalis is well-suited for indoor cultivation and thrives in bright, indirect light.
