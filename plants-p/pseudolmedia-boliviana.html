Pseudolmedia boliviana

Pseudolmedia boliviana, commonly known as the Bolivian mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It attaches itself to the branches of host trees, such as oaks and pines, through its specialized haustoria to extract water and nutrients. The plant produces small, white or greenish flowers that are often inconspicuous, followed by small, fleshy berries that serve as a food source for birds and other animals. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a waxy coating to reduce water loss.

Pseudolmedia boliviana plays a role in the ecological dynamics of its habitat, influencing host tree growth and providing sustenance for wildlife.
