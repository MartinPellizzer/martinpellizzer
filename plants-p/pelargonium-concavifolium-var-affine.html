Pelargonium concavifolium var. affine

Pelargonium concavifolium var. affine, commonly known as the narrow-leaf geranium, is a species native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, serrated leaves that are often tinged with red or purple at the edges. The plant produces clusters of small, pale pink to white flowers with five petals, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
