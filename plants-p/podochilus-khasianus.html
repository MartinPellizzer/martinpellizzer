Podochilus khasianus

Podochilus khasianus, commonly known as the Khasi orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the northeastern regions of India.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its sympodial growth habit. The plant typically produces a single or few erect stems with ovate to elliptic leaves. Its flowers are usually pale green to yellowish with a distinct lip, and it blooms during the summer months.

Podochilus khasianus is often found in moist, shaded forest floors and is valued for its ornamental appeal.
