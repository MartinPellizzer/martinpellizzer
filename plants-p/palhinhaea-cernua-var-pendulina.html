Palhinhaea cernua var. pendulina

Palhinhaea cernua var. pendulina, commonly known as the hanging palhinhaea, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its pendulous inflorescences, which give it its common name. The plant features narrow, linear leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
