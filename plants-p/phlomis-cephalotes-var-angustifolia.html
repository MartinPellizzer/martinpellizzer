Phlomis cephalotes var. angustifolia

Phlomis cephalotes var. angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf skullcap, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features upright, branched stems that are often hairy, and it produces showy, clustered flowers in shades of blue to purple. The leaves are lance-shaped and often have a silvery or grayish underside, contributing to its distinctive appearance. This plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
