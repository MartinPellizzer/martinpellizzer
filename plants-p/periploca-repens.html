Periploca repens

Periploca repens, commonly known as creeping periploca, is a flowering plant belonging to the Apocynaceae family.

It is a low-growing shrub that features opposite, simple, and usually elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically yellow or greenish-yellow in color. Its fruit is a follicle that contains several seeds, and it is often found in rocky or sandy soils.

Periploca repens is native to regions in Europe, North Africa, and western Asia.
