Podalyria insignis

Podalyria insignis, commonly known as the purple mountain everlasting, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or alpine environments. The plant produces clusters of small, showy purple flowers that bloom in late summer. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a fine, silky hair.

Podalyria insignis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and visual interest.
