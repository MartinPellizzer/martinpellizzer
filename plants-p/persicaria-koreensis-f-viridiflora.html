Persicaria koreensis f. viridiflora

Persicaria koreensis f. viridiflora, commonly known as the green-flowered Korean knotweed, is a flowering plant in the Polygonaceae family.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and lance-shaped leaves that are typically dark green with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces small, clustered greenish-white flowers that bloom in summer, adding a subtle ornamental value. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

This cultivar is valued for its unique foliage and ability to thrive in challenging garden conditions.
