Prostanthera thymifolia

Prostanthera thymifolia, commonly known as lemon myrtle, is a shrub native to eastern Australia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants. The plant is characterized by its small, fragrant white or pink flowers and narrow, aromatic leaves. Its leaves contain high concentrations of citral, a compound responsible for its distinctive lemon scent.

Prostanthera thymifolia is often used in traditional medicine and as a culinary herb due to its aromatic properties.
