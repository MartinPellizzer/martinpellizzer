Piper recensitum

Piper recensitum, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, white flowers. The plant produces berries that are initially green and turn red when mature, attracting various bird species. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been introduced to other regions, where it can become invasive.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in both horticulture and ecological studies.
