Physalis sordida

Physalis sordida, commonly known as the common ground cherry, is a herbaceous annual plant native to the Americas.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its hairy stems and oval-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green berries enclosed in a papery husk that turns red when mature. These berries are edible when fully ripe and have a tangy, sweet flavor.

It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for both ornamental and culinary purposes.
