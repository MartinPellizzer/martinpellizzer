Papaver tumidulum

Papaver tumidulum, commonly known as the bladderpod poppy, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its distinctive, inflated seed pods that resemble small bladders, hence its common name. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its flowers are usually pale pink or white, with five petals that open in the morning.

Papaver tumidulum is a short-lived perennial, often found in dry, open habitats.
