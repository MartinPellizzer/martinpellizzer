Paederia scandens f. megaphylla

Paederia scandens f. megaphylla, commonly known as the giant-leaf paederia, is a variant of the climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can reach up to 30 cm in length. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by distinctive, winged fruits. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its foliage is sometimes used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
