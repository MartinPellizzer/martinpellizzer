Plumeria lutea

Plumeria lutea, commonly known as the yellow frangipani, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, fragrant yellow flowers that bloom in clusters during the warmer months. The plant has simple, opposite, ovate leaves that are glossy and dark green. Its woody stems are often branched and can grow into a small tree or shrub.

Plumeria lutea is widely cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
