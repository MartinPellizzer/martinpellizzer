Penstemon digitalis var. attenuatus

Penstemon digitalis var. attenuatus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved beardtongue, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and spikes of tubular, pink to purple flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping. It is a perennial species that produces showy blooms from late spring to early summer.

Its nectar-rich flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to ecological biodiversity.
