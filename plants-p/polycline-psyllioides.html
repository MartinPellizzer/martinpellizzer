Polycline psyllioides

Polycline psyllioides, commonly known as the narrowleaf tansy, is a flowering plant in the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, yellowish-green flowers that appear in clusters. The leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, with a slightly rough texture. This plant is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils.

It is known for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
