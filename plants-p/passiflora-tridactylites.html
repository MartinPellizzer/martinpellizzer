Passiflora tridactylites

Passiflora tridactylites, commonly known as the three-finger passionflower, is a tropical vine native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, complex flowers that feature five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments. The plant produces edible, tropical fruits with a sweet and tangy flavor. It thrives in humid, warm climates and requires well-drained soil.

Passiflora tridactylites is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its unique floral structure.
