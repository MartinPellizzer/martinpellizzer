Pleopeltis sessilis

Pleopeltis sessilis, commonly known as the sessile pleopeltis, is a species of epiphytic fern native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its compact, rosette-like growth habit. The fronds are typically short and broad, with a leathery texture and a distinctive, often hairy underside. It thrives in humid, shaded environments, often growing on tree trunks or rocky surfaces.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appearance and is sometimes cultivated in tropical gardens.
