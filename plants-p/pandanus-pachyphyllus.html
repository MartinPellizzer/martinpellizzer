Pandanus pachyphyllus

Pandanus pachyphyllus, commonly known as the broad-leaf pandanus, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, strap-shaped leaves that can grow up to several meters in length. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically yellow or green in color. Its robust, fibrous roots make it well adapted to wet and swampy environments.

The leaves are often used in traditional crafts and as thatching material in various cultures.
