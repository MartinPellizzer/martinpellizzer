Polypodium hymenodes var. marginale

Polypodium hymenodes var. marginale, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wall fern, is a fern species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features a rhizomatous growth habit with fronds that are typically bipinnate, giving it a delicate, lacy appearance. The leaves are usually dark green and have a distinctive marginal variegation, which contributes to its ornamental value. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found growing on rocks or tree trunks.

This variety is valued in horticulture for its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to various garden conditions.
