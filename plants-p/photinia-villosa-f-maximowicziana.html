Photinia villosa f. maximowicziana

Photinia villosa f. maximowicziana, commonly known as the Maximowicz's firethorn, is a compact shrub native to eastern Asia.

It features glossy, dark green foliage with a dense, upright growth habit. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers in spring, which are followed by bright red, berry-like fruits. Its foliage is often covered in fine, white hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and adaptability to various soil conditions.
