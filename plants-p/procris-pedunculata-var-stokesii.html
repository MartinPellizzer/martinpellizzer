Procris pedunculata var. stokesii

Procris pedunculata var. stokesii, commonly known as Stokes' procris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, woodland environments and features lance-shaped leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its inflorescence is a dense, terminal spike, and the leaves are often arranged in a basal rosette.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
