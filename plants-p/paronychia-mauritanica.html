Paronychia mauritanica

Paronychia mauritanica, commonly known as the Moroccan sandthorn, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of North Africa.

It is a shrub characterized by its small, yellowish flowers and spiny stems, which help it survive in harsh environments. The plant produces small, fleshy fruits that are adapted for dispersal by animals. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Paronychia mauritanica plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various desert species.
