Polygala xanthina

Polygala xanthina, commonly known as the yellow milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is known for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine. Its leaves are alternate and often have a distinctive, velvety texture.

Polygala xanthina thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in grassland habitats.
