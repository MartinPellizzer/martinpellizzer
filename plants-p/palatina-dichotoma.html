Palatina dichotoma

Palatina dichotoma, commonly known as the two-branched palm, is a unique plant native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive dichotomous branching pattern, where each stem splits into two equal parts. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical environments, often in wetlands or along riverbanks. Its leaves are usually palmate, with multiple radiating leaflets, and it produces small, clustered flowers.

Palatina dichotoma is notable for its ecological role in supporting local biodiversity and its potential use in traditional medicine.
