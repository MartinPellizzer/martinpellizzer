Poa violacea var. polychroa

Poa violacea var. polychroa, commonly known as the purple mountain grass, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and produces delicate, violet-colored inflorescences. The plant typically thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats. Its foliage is usually a mix of green and purple hues, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

The species is valued in horticulture for its unique coloration and ability to form dense, attractive turf.
