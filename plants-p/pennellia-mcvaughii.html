Pennellia mcvaughii

Pennellia mcvaughii, commonly known as the McVaugh's pennellia, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments. It features small, daisy-like flowers that bloom in the spring.

Its foliage is usually narrow and grass-like, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
