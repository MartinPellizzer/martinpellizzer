Paphiopedilum papuanum

Paphiopedilum papuanum, commonly known as the Papuan slipper orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the highlands of Papua, Indonesia.

It is characterized by its dark green, strap-like leaves and produces showy, pale yellow to cream-colored flowers with a distinctive slipper-shaped pouch. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments and is adapted to cool, mountainous climates. It has a complex root system that helps it absorb nutrients from the nutrient-poor soils of its habitat.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is a subject of interest for both botanists and orchid enthusiasts.
