Philodendron fraternum

Philodendron fraternum, commonly known as the Black Cardinal Philodendron, is a tropical climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its dark green, heart-shaped leaves that often have a glossy texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by bright red berries, which are a key feature in its reproductive cycle. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its striking foliage, it is a popular choice for indoor gardening and tropical landscapes.
