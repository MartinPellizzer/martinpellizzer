Piper tuberculatum var. scandens

Piper tuberculatum var. scandens, commonly known as the climbing pepper plant, is a tropical vine native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by small, round berries that turn red when mature. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has a mild peppery flavor in its leaves.

Due to its rapid growth habit, it is well-suited for trellises or vertical gardening spaces.
