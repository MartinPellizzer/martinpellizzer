Philadelphus argyrocalyx var. argenteus

Philadelphus argyrocalyx var. argenteus, commonly known as the silver-leaf mock orange, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The leaves are narrow, elliptical, and covered with a fine silvery-white pubescence, giving the plant its distinctive appearance. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it suitable for garden cultivation.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and mild fragrance, often used in ornamental landscaping.
