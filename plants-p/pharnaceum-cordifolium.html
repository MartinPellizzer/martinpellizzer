Pharnaceum cordifolium

Pharnaceum cordifolium, commonly known as the heart-leaf pharnaceum, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves, which are typically a deep green and have a smooth, waxy surface. The plant produces small, clustered white or pale yellow flowers that bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in dense forest undergrowth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
