Poa × halleri-i

Poa × halleri-i, commonly known as the hybrid bluegrass, is a sterile hybrid resulting from the cross between Poa annua and Poa trivialis.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and a dense, carpet-like appearance. The plant typically has narrow, grass-like leaves with a bluish-green coloration. It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

Due to its sterility, it does not produce viable seeds and relies on vegetative propagation for spread.
