Primula euosma var. puralba

Primula euosma var. puralba, commonly known as the purple primrose, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of East Asia.

It features oval, dark green leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped flowers, which range in color from pale purple to deep violet. It thrives in cool, moist environments and prefers partial to full shade.

This species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to bloom in early spring.
