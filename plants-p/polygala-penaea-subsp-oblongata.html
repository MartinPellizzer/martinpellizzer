Polygala penaea subsp. oblongata

Polygala penaea subsp.

oblongata, commonly known as the narrowleaf milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa. It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in open woodlands or grasslands.

The plant features slender, branched stems and small, greenish-white flowers arranged in spikes. Its leaves are narrow and oblong, giving it its subspecific name.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
