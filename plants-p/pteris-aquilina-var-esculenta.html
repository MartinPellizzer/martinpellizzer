Pteris aquilina var. esculenta

Pteris aquilina var. esculenta, commonly known as the edible water fern, is a variant of the common water fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, bioluminescent fronds that emit a faint blue glow in the dark. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found near streams or in humid forests. Its fronds are used in traditional cuisine, particularly in Southeast Asian countries, where they are cooked and consumed as a vegetable.

Botanically, it belongs to the family Pteridaceae and is valued for its unique appearance and culinary applications.
