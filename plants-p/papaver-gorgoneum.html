Papaver gorgoneum

Papaver gorgoneum, commonly known as the Gorgon Poppy, is a striking plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from deep red to purple, with a distinctive, often hairy, calyx. The plant belongs to the Papaveraceae family, which includes other well-known species like the opium poppy. Its leaves are typically lobed and have a rough texture, while its stems are erect and often covered in fine hairs.

This species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its dramatic appearance.
