Ptilomeris mutica

Ptilomeris mutica, commonly known as the hairy ptilomeris, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features hairy stems and leaves, which contribute to its common name. It typically grows in grasslands and open woodlands, thriving in well-drained soils.

Ptilomeris mutica is known for its small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring, attracting various pollinators.
