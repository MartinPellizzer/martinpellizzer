Phyllanthus ankarana

Phyllanthus ankarana, commonly known as the Madagascar gooseberry, is a shrub native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its small, yellowish-green berries. The plant has simple, alternate leaves with a rough texture and a distinct, pungent odor. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in forested areas.

The fruit is edible and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
