Prunus × ferganica f. nudipedunculata

Prunus × ferganica f. nudipedunculata, commonly known as the Fergana cherry, is a unique hybrid species native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its small, deciduous leaves and clusters of white or pink flowers that appear in early spring. The plant produces small, sweet-tart berries that are edible and often used in local cuisines. Botanically, it belongs to the Rosaceae family and exhibits traits typical of Prunus species, including a woody structure and simple leaves.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden cultivation in temperate climates.
