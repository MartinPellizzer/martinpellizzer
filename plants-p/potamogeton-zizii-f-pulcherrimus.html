Potamogeton zizii f. pulcherrimus

Potamogeton zizii f. pulcherrimus, commonly known as the Persian watermilfoil, is a submerged aquatic plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the family Potamogetonaceae and is characterized by its slender, ribbon-like leaves that are often arranged in whorls around the stem. The plant typically grows in freshwater habitats such as lakes, rivers, and ponds, where it forms dense underwater vegetation. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, usually appearing in clusters, and it plays a role in providing habitat for aquatic invertebrates.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in water gardens.
