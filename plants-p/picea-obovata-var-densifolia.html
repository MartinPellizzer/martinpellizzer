Picea obovata var. densifolia

Picea obovata var. densifolia, commonly known as the Japanese white pine, is a variant of the white pine species native to Japan.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and slender, conical shape. The leaves are short, obovate, and have a leathery texture, contributing to its ornamental value. It produces small, cylindrical cones that mature to a reddish-brown color.

This variety is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal in gardens and as a landscape tree.
