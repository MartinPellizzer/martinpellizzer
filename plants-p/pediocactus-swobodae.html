Pediocactus swobodae

Pediocactus swobodae, commonly known as the Swoboda's cactus, is a rare and striking member of the Cactaceae family native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, columnar stem with ribbed structure and spiny areoles, typical of many cacti. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, adding to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat threats, it is considered a vulnerable species in the wild.
