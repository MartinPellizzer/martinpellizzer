Pulmonaria officinalis subsp. marzolae

Pulmonaria officinalis subsp.

marzolae, commonly known as the alpine lungwort, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central and Eastern Europe. It belongs to the family Geraniaceae and is characterized by its basal rosette of velvety, spotted leaves.

The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink, white, and purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
