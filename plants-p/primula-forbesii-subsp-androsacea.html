Primula forbesii subsp. androsacea

Primula forbesii subsp.

androsacea, commonly known as the Androsace primrose, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the alpine regions of southern China. It features dense clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, typically blooming in spring.

The plant has a rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves that form a compact base, and its stems are often hairy. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to cool, moist environments.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in rock gardens.
