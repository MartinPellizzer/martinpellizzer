Pisum borussicum

Pisum borussicum, commonly known as the Siberian pea shrub, is a deciduous shrub native to the cold climates of Siberia and Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant is notable for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it valuable for soil enrichment. It produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, which can be used for propagation.

Due to its hardy nature, it is often used in reforestation and erosion control projects.
