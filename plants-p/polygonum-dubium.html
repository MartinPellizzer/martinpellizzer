Polygonum dubium

Polygonum dubium, commonly known as the common smartweed, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist soil environments such as wetlands and streambanks. The plant features alternate, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that are usually pink or red in color. It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, which is characterized by distinctive nodes along the stems.

Polygonum dubium is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife species.
