Plantago macrostachys var. angustifolia

Plantago macrostachys var. angustifolia, commonly known as narrow-leaf plantain, is a variant of the common plantain species.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, disturbed soils such as roadsides and fields. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a hairy surface and produces small, clustered flowers in late summer. Its seed capsules are elongated and contain numerous tiny seeds.

This variety is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional herbal remedies.
