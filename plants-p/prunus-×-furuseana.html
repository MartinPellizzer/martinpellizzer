Prunus × furuseana

Prunus × furuseana, commonly known as the Japanese cherry blossom, is a hybrid species within the Prunus genus.

It is native to Japan and is celebrated for its showy, pink or white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant is a deciduous shrub or small tree, typically reaching heights of 5 to 10 meters. Its leaves are oval-shaped with a smooth margin, and the fruit is a small, dark purple drupe.

The botanical features of Prunus × furuseana make it a popular ornamental plant in gardens and parks worldwide.
