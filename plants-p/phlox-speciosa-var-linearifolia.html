Phlox speciosa var. linearifolia

Phlox speciosa var. linearifolia, commonly known as the linear-leaved garden phlox, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features upright stems that bear clusters of showy, fragrant flowers in shades of pink to red. The leaves are narrow and linear, giving the plant a distinctive, elegant appearance. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained, fertile soil.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
