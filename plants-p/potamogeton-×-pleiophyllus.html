Potamogeton × pleiophyllus

Potamogeton × pleiophyllus, commonly known as the hybrid water milfoil, is a submerged aquatic plant that exhibits characteristics of both its parent species, Potamogeton crispus and Potamogeton natans.

It typically grows in freshwater environments such as lakes, ponds, and slow-moving rivers, where it forms dense underwater stands. The plant has narrow, ribbon-like leaves that are often arranged in whorls around the stem, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are pollinated by water. Potamogeton × pleiophyllus plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for various aquatic organisms and contributing to water clarity through its dense growth.

However, it can also become invasive in some regions, outcompeting native vegetation.
