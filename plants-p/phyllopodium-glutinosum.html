Phyllopodium glutinosum

Phyllopodium glutinosum, commonly known as the sticky phyllopod, is a unique plant species characterized by its distinctive, leaf-like structures called phylloids.

These phylloids are thick, fleshy, and covered in a sticky substance that helps the plant retain moisture in arid environments. The plant is native to certain regions of South America, where it thrives in rocky or sandy soils. Its reproductive structures are small and inconspicuous, relying on wind pollination for reproduction.

Due to its specialized adaptations, Phyllopodium glutinosum is of interest to botanists studying xerophytic plant evolution.
