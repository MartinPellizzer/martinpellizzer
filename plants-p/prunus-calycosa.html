Prunus calycosa

Prunus calycosa, commonly known as the California wild cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and clusters of white or pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, edible drupes that turn from yellow to red as they mature. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a serrated edge.

Prunus calycosa is often found in chaparral and woodland habitats, thriving in well-drained soils.
