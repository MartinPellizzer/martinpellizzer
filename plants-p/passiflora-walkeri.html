Passiflora walkeri

Passiflora walkeri, commonly known as the purple passionflower, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful flowers that feature a unique blend of blue, purple, and white petals. The plant produces edible berries that are often used in culinary applications. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a velvety texture.

Passiflora walkeri is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in warm, humid climates.
