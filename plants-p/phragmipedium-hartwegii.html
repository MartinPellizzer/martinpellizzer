Phragmipedium hartwegii

Phragmipedium hartwegii, commonly known as the Hartweg's slipper orchid, is a species native to the rainforests of South America.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, moist environments with rich, organic soil. The plant produces striking, slipper-shaped flowers that are usually yellow or greenish-yellow in color. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb nutrients from the forest floor.

Phragmipedium hartwegii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
