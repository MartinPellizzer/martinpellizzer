Phylica meyeri

Phylica meyeri, commonly known as the Meyer's bottlebrush, is a South African shrub belonging to the Proteaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, bright red or orange cylindrical flower heads, which resemble bottlebrushes. The plant has narrow, leathery leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into woody, cone-like seed pods. It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and is often found in fynbos vegetation.

Phylica meyeri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral displays.
