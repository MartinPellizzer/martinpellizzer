Pedilonum dichroma

Pedilonum dichroma, commonly known as the twin-leaf orchid, is a unique orchid species native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, bilaterally symmetrical flowers that display a blend of white and purple hues. The plant has slender, hairy stems and produces small, clustered leaves that are often mistaken for petals due to their delicate appearance. It thrives in cool, humid environments and requires specific conditions to bloom successfully.

Due to its rarity and ornamental value, it is often cultivated in specialized botanical gardens.
