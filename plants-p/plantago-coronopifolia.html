Plantago coronopifolia

Plantago coronopifolia, commonly known as the crowned plantain, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its rosette of broad, oval-shaped leaves with a waxy surface and a distinctive crown of leaves at the base. The plant produces small, greenish flowers in a spike-like inflorescence, which are wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, along roadsides, and in disturbed areas.

The species is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally in herbal remedies.
