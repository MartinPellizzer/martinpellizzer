Panicum decipiens

Panicum decipiens, commonly known as the deceptive grass, is a species native to the southern United States and parts of Mexico.

It is a flowering plant in the grass family, Poaceae, and is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant is known for its ability to mimic other grasses, which can make it difficult to identify without close examination. It typically grows in open, sunny areas such as prairies and disturbed soils.

Its botanical features include narrow leaves and a flowering structure that produces small, wind-dispersed seeds.
