Papaver paniculatum

Papaver paniculatum, commonly known as the clustered poppy, is a herbaceous perennial native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that range in color from white to deep red, often with a distinctive black center. The plant features a bushy growth habit with slender, branching stems that produce multiple flower clusters. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Papaver paniculatum thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and wildflower displays.
