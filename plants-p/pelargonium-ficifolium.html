Pelargonium ficifolium

Pelargonium ficifolium, commonly known as the ivy-leaf geranium, is a perennial flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and distinctive heart-shaped, dark green leaves with a slightly waxy texture. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, which bloom throughout the growing season. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for hanging baskets and containers.

Due to its ornamental value and ease of care, it is widely cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
