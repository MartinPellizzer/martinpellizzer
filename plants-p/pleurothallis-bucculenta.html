Pleurothallis bucculenta

Pleurothallis bucculenta, commonly known as the "Bucculenta Orchid," is a species native to the cloud forests of Central and South America.

It is a small, epiphytic orchid that typically grows on trees, drawing moisture and nutrients from the air and rain. The plant features delicate, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pale yellow. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining potting media.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts in controlled conditions.
