Phillyrea medianifolia subsp. aguilaris

Phillyrea medianifolia subsp.

aguilaris, commonly known as the Aguilar's Phillyrea, is a shrub native to the Iberian Peninsula. It features small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically glaucous in appearance.

The plant produces white, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees. Its fruit is a small, woody capsule that splits open to release seeds.

It is often found in rocky or coastal habitats, showcasing its adaptability to Mediterranean climates.
