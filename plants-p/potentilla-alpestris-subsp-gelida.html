Potentilla alpestris subsp. gelida

Potentilla alpestris subsp.

gelida, commonly known as alpine cinquefoil, is a perennial shrub native to the alpine regions of Europe and Asia. It typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils at high elevations.

The plant produces small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are generally narrow and slightly hairy, adapted to cold and arid conditions.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing mountainous terrain.
