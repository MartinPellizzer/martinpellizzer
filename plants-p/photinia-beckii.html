Photinia beckii

Photinia beckii, commonly known as the redleaf photinia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant red foliage, which turns green in the summer and red again in the fall. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape. Its flowers are small, white, and clustered in inflorescences, followed by red, fleshy berries.

Photinia beckii is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various soil types.
