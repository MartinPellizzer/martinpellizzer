Pteranthus dichotomus var. trigynus

Pteranthus dichotomus var. trigynus, commonly known as the three-styled pteranthus, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its daisy-like flowers with yellow disc florets and white ray florets. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and is adapted to sandy soils and saline conditions. Its foliage is narrow and linear, often forming dense clusters near the ground.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its visual appeal.
