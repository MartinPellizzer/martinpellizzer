Potentilla velutina var. clementei

Potentilla velutina var. clementei, commonly known as the velvet cinquefoil, is a perennial shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage and bright yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in mountainous regions. Its leaves are compound, with five oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces small, round fruits called achenes.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in native plant gardens.
