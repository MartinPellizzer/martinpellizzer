Potentilla adriatica

Potentilla adriatica, commonly known as Adriatic cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing perennial shrub that typically reaches a height of about 30 centimeters. The plant features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are palmately divided into five lobes, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Potentilla adriatica thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.
