Petrosedum × luteolum

Petrosedum × luteolum, commonly known as the yellow stonecrop, is a hybrid succulent plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features compact, low-growing rosettes of fleshy, blue-green leaves that are often tinged with yellow or orange. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow to orange, typically blooming in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its drought tolerance and ornamental appeal make it a popular choice for gardeners in arid climates.
