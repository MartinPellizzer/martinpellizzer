Paris verticillata f. purpurea

Paris verticillata f. purpurea, commonly known as the purple false Solomon's seal, is a flowering plant native to eastern North America.

It is a member of the Liliaceae family and is characterized by its clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant produces a fleshy, red or purple berry that contains the seeds. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for shade gardens.
