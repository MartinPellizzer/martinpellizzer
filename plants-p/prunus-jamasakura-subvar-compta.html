Prunus jamasakura subvar. compta

Prunus jamasakura subvar. compta, commonly known as the Japanese maple, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its striking, ornamental foliage that changes color throughout the seasons, ranging from bright green in spring to vibrant red, orange, and yellow in autumn. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in early spring, followed by edible berries that are favored by birds. Its leaves are typically palmately lobed, with a delicate, feathery appearance.

The species is well-suited for cultivation in gardens, where it adds visual interest and seasonal color.
