Plectranthus modestus

Plectranthus modestus, commonly known as the African violet, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It features dense, velvety foliage with a distinctive rounded shape, often adorned with small, tubular flowers in shades of purple, pink, and white. The plant thrives in well-draining soil and prefers bright, indirect light, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening. Its compact growth habit and long blooming period contribute to its ornamental value.

Plectranthus modestus is also valued for its aromatic leaves, which can be used in traditional medicine and culinary applications.
