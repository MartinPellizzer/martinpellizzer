Panicum longiflorum

Panicum longiflorum, commonly known as the slender panicgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and long, drooping inflorescences that produce small, spikelet-like flowers. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and other moist environments, thriving in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green color that adds visual interest to wetland landscapes.

Panicum longiflorum plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
