Panicum ecklonii

Panicum ecklonii, commonly known as red panicgrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and its striking red or purple inflorescences that appear in late summer. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in coastal or inland grasslands. Its foliage is narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue that contrasts with the vibrant color of its flowering spikes.

Panicum ecklonii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping or as a drought-tolerant ground cover.
