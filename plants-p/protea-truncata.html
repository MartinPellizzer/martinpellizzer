Protea truncata

Protea truncata, commonly known as the Cape tulip, is a striking South African flowering plant belonging to the Proteaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescences that resemble tulips, with vibrant pink to red bracts surrounding smaller, tubular flowers. The plant is native to the Western and Eastern Cape provinces, where it thrives in sandy or clay soils. It is often found in coastal fynbos vegetation and is adapted to the region's Mediterranean climate.

Protea truncata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
