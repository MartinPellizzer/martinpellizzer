Parodia cachiana

Parodia cachiana, commonly known as the "Cactus of the Moon," is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems that are often covered in white, woolly spines. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, adding a striking contrast to its green body. It is a slow-growing plant that thrives in well-draining soil and full sun.

Due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements, it is a popular choice for succulent collectors.
