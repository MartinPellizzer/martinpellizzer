Pelargonium × viridifolium

Pelargonium × viridifolium, commonly known as the green-leaf geranium, is a hybrid plant that exhibits unique botanical characteristics.

It is a member of the Geraniaceae family and is known for its lush, dark green foliage that is often variegated with lighter shades. The plant typically produces small, clustered flowers in shades of pink, red, or white, depending on the cultivar. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for garden use.

Its foliage is not only aesthetically pleasing but also serves as a distinguishing feature in identifying this particular species.
