Polygonum raii

Polygonum raii, commonly known as the Indian knotgrass, is a perennial herb native to the Himalayas and parts of southern Asia.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, characterized by its distinctive nodes and often swollen stems. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped with a prominent midrib, and it produces small, greenish flowers in dense clusters.

Polygonum raii is valued for its medicinal properties in traditional systems of medicine.
