Papaver stylatum

Papaver stylatum, commonly known as the slender poppy, is a species of flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stem and showy, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from pale yellow to orange-red. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, dark seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats.

Papaver stylatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is occasionally cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
