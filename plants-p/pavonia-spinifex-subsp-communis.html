Pavonia spinifex subsp. communis

Pavonia spinifex subsp.

communis, commonly known as the common wood rose, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States. It is a shrub characterized by its slender, often spiny stems and lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture.

The plant produces clusters of small, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soils.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ornamental addition to native plant gardens.
