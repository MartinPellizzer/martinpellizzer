Poa tormentuosa

Poa tormentuosa, commonly known as the tormentous grass, is a species of grass native to southern South America.

It is characterized by its fine, wiry leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant is typically found in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. It has a fibrous root system that helps stabilize soil and prevent erosion.

Poa tormentuosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native plant gardens.
