Pilostyles aethiopica

Pilostyles aethiopica, commonly known as the Ethiopian pilostyle, is a unique parasitic plant native to Africa.

It lacks chlorophyll and relies entirely on its host for nutrients, typically attaching to the roots of other plants. The plant has a slender, thread-like structure with a fleshy, swollen base that contains the reproductive organs. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often overlooked due to their minimal visibility.

Despite its parasitic nature, it plays a niche role in the ecological interactions of its habitat.
