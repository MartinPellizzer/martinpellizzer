Pavonia muricata

Pavonia muricata, commonly known as the rough pavonia, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States.

It is a shrub that typically grows in sandy or rocky soils, often found in coastal areas. The plant produces showy, pink or red flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are usually ovate with a rough, hairy texture, giving it its common name.

Pavonia muricata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in native plant gardens.
