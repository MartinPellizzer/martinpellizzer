Phyllocephalum frutescens

Phyllocephalum frutescens, commonly known as the golden star bush, is a shrub native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its dense, bushy growth habit and clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom throughout the year. The plant has small, elliptical leaves that are typically arranged in whorls around the stems. Its botanical features include a woody stem with a rough, bark-like surface and small, pale green leaves with a slightly hairy texture.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in warm, humid climates.
