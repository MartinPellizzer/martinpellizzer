Psilodigera spicata

Psilodigera spicata, commonly known as the spikeless spurge, is a member of the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is a low-growing, herbaceous plant characterized by its simple, opposite leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces a milky latex when damaged, a common feature among members of its family. It is native to parts of Africa and thrives in dry, sandy soils.

Due to its hardy nature, it is often found in disturbed habitats and can be invasive in certain regions.
