Phyllodes vestita

Phyllodes vestita, commonly known as the silky wattle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, hairless phyllodes that are often covered in a fine, silky bloom. The plant produces yellow flowers in late winter to early spring, which are clustered in spikes. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

Phyllodes vestita is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
