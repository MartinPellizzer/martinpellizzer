Persicaria bicornis

Persicaria bicornis, commonly known as the two-horned knotweed, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, which includes other familiar plants like buckwheat and sorrel. The plant is characterized by its tall, erect stems and small, clustered flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a distinctive reddish or purple hue.
