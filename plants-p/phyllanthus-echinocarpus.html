Phyllanthus echinocarpus

Phyllanthus echinocarpus, commonly known as the spiny achenes plant, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its small, spiny fruits that contain a single seed. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are usually ovate or elliptic in shape. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas or along roadsides.

The species is noted for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
