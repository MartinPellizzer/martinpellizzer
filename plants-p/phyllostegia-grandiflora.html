Phyllostegia grandiflora

Phyllostegia grandiflora, commonly known as the large-flowered mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its opposite, lance-shaped leaves and showy, tubular flowers that bloom in clusters. Its flowers are typically pink to purple in color, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Phyllostegia grandiflora is often found in moist, wooded areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
