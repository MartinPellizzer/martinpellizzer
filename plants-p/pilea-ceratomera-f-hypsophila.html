Pilea ceratomera f. hypsophila

Pilea ceratomera f. hypsophila, commonly known as the Shingle Plant, is a compact, evergreen perennial with dense, overlapping foliage resembling small shingles.

It features dark green, waxy leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern, giving it a neat and compact appearance. The plant is native to high-altitude regions of Central America and thrives in bright, indirect light with moderate humidity. It produces small, inconspicuous white flowers that appear in spring, followed by red, fleshy fruits.

This variety is prized for its ornamental foliage and ease of care, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.
