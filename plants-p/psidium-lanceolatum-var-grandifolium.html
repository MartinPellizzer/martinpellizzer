Psidium lanceolatum var. grandifolium

Psidium lanceolatum var. grandifolium, commonly known as the large-leaf guava, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy beneath. The plant produces fragrant, white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, pinkish-white fruits. The fruit is typically small to medium in size with a smooth, thin skin.

It is valued for its aromatic flavor and is often cultivated for both ornamental and culinary purposes.
