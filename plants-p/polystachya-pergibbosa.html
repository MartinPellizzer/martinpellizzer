Polystachya pergibbosa

Polystachya pergibbosa, commonly known as the rock orchid, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its compact growth habit and striking, often fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows on rocky substrates, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its inflorescence consists of multiple racemes bearing small, lance-shaped flowers in shades of white, yellow, or green.

Polystachya pergibbosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique appearance.
