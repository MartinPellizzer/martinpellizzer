Pilosella kalksburgensis subsp. charadraea

Pilosella kalksburgensis subsp.

charadraea, commonly known as the Kalksburg sandwort, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family. It is a low-growing perennial herb characterized by its finely divided, grass-like leaves and small, daisy-like flowers.

This subspecies is typically found in sandy or calcareous soils, often in open habitats such as grasslands or coastal dunes. Its flowers are usually pale yellow to white, and it blooms from late spring to early summer.

The plant is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments, showcasing features that help it conserve water and survive in harsh conditions.
