Pavonia subpandurata

Pavonia subpandurata, commonly known as the redroot false nettle, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, wooded areas and along stream banks. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges and produces clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers. Its roots are reddish in color, which gives it its common name.

Pavonia subpandurata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
