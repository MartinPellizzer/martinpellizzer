Pulsatilla

Pulsatilla, commonly known as the pasque flower, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Ranunculaceae.

It is characterized by its showy, often pink or white, cup-shaped blooms that appear in early spring. The plant has a short, stout stem with few leaves, and its flowers are surrounded by a series of petal-like sepals. Pulsatilla species are native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere and are often found in meadows, grasslands, and rocky soils.

The plant is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
