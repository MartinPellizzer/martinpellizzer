Phegopteris reticulata

Phegopteris reticulata, commonly known as the veined wood fern, is a species of fern characterized by its intricate frond structure.

It features a rhizomatous growth habit with a long, horizontal underground stem. The fronds are bipinnate, meaning they are divided into two sets of pinnae, giving the plant a finely divided appearance. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically found in temperate forests.

The plant's spores are produced on the underside of the fronds, which are clustered in sori.
