Piper arboreum subsp. stamineum

Piper arboreum subsp.

stamineum, commonly known as the sacred lotus or Malabar nut, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its woody stem and compound leaves with serrated edges.

The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in spikes, followed by spherical, fleshy fruits. Its botanical features make it a distinctive member of the Piperaceae family.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has cultural significance in various regions.
