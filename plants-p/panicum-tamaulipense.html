Panicum tamaulipense

Panicum tamaulipense, commonly known as Tamaulipan grass, is a perennial grass species native to the southern United States and northern Mexico.

It thrives in wetland and marshy environments, often found in areas with high moisture and nutrient-rich soils. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces panicles of small, spikelet-like flowers that are typically green or brown in color. It plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.

Due to its adaptability, it is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
