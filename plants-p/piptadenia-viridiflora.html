Piptadenia viridiflora

Piptadenia viridiflora, commonly known as green-flowered piptadenia, is a tropical legume native to South America.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 20 meters tall, characterized by its compound leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant produces elongated, woody pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in secondary forests and along riverbanks.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and traditional medicine.
