Paraphlomis coronata

Paraphlomis coronata, commonly known as the crowned parrya, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Lamiaceae, which includes many aromatic plants. The plant is characterized by its square stems, opposite leaves, and clusters of small, pale blue to purple flowers. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its ornamental value and medicinal properties make it a notable species in botanical studies.
