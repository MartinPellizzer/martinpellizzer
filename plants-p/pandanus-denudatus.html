Pandanus denudatus

Pandanus denudatus, commonly known as the red pandanus, is a tropical plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It is a member of the Pandanaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and long, narrow leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. Its leaves are often used in traditional crafts and for thatching.

The red pandanus is also valued for its ornamental appearance and its ability to thrive in coastal and wetland environments.
