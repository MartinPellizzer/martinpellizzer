Picramnia caracasana f. tomentella

Picramnia caracasana f. tomentella, commonly known as the hairy picramnia, is a shrub native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage, which gives it a distinctive appearance. The leaves are typically elliptical and have a soft, fuzzy texture. It produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, often attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

This plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
