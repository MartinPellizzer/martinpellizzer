Polyosma

Polyosma, commonly known as the mountain holly or holly fern, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Polygonaceae.

It consists of several species native to various regions of Asia, including China, Japan, and the Himalayas. These plants are typically shrubs or small trees characterized by their simple, alternate leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers. The leaves are often glossy and have a distinctive veining pattern, while the fruits are small, three-lobed capsules.

Polyosma species are valued for their ornamental appeal and are sometimes cultivated in gardens for their foliage.
