Piper septuplinervium

Piper septuplinervium, commonly known as the seven-veined pepper plant, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and small, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are typically clustered in terminal inflorescences. Its fruits are berries that turn from green to yellow when mature, and they contain several seeds.

Piper septuplinervium is valued for its potential medicinal properties and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
