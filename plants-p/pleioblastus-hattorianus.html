Pleioblastus hattorianus

Pleioblastus hattorianus, commonly known as the Hattori's bamboo, is a species of grass belonging to the Poaceae family.

It is native to Japan and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and fine, feather-like leaves. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and mountainous regions. Its foliage is often a vibrant green, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in the spring.

Pleioblastus hattorianus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
