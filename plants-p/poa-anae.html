Poa anae

Poa anae, commonly known as the narrowleaf meadowgrass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It typically grows in moist, temperate regions and is often found in grasslands and along stream banks. The plant has slender, upright stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its inflorescence consists of a loose to dense cluster of spikelets, which are characteristic of the Poaceae family.

Poa anae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
