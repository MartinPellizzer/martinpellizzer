Plantago lorata

Plantago lorata, commonly known as narrowleaf plantain, is a flowering plant belonging to the Plantaginaceae family.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, disturbed soils, such as roadsides and fields. The plant features broad, lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture and produces small, greenish-white flowers in spike-like clusters. It is known for its ability to thrive in various environmental conditions, making it a common sight in temperate regions.

The plant is also valued for its medicinal properties, often used in traditional herbal remedies.
