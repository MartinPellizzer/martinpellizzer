Portulaca stipularis

Portulaca stipularis, commonly known as the stipular portulaca, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is a low-growing, succulent annual that produces small, vibrant flowers in shades of red, yellow, and orange. The plant has fleshy, ribbed leaves and thrives in well-drained soil with full sun exposure. It is often used in ornamental gardening for its drought tolerance and colorful blooms.

Its scientific name reflects the presence of stipules, which are small leaf-like structures at the base of the leaves.
