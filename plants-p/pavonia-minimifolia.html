Pavonia minimifolia

Pavonia minimifolia, commonly known as the miniature crownflower, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the southwestern United States.

It features delicate, fern-like leaves and produces small, showy flowers in shades of pink and white. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping. Its compact size and ornamental flowers make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Pavonia minimifolia is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
