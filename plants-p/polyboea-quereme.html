Polyboea quereme

Polyboea quereme, commonly known as the red spiderflower, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and northern South America.

It is characterized by its striking red or orange tubular flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees. The plant typically grows as an erect shrub, reaching heights of up to 2 meters, with woody stems and lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are borne on long, slender peduncles, and the plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Polyboea quereme prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice in tropical and subtropical gardens.
