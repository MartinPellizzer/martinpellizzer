Physalis angustior

Physalis angustior, commonly known as the narrow-leaf husk tomato, is a species native to South America.

It is a herbaceous annual plant characterized by its hairy stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into inflated, orange-red calyces enclosing the berry-like fruits. The fruits are typically small, measuring up to 2 centimeters in diameter, and are edible when ripe.

This species is often grown as an ornamental or for its edible fruits in suitable climates.
