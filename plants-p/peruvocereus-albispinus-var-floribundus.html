Peruvocereus albispinus var. floribundus

Peruvocereus albispinus var. floribundus, commonly known as the white-spined cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems that are covered in white spines, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers during the warmer months. It is a slow-growing cactus that thrives in full sun and well-draining soil.

Due to its unique features, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant in desert gardens.
