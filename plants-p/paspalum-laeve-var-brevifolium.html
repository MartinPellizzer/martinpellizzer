Paspalum laeve var. brevifolium

Paspalum laeve var. brevifolium, commonly known as narrow-leaved paspalum, is a perennial grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It features slender, erect stems and narrow, linear leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with pale green to yellowish flowers. It is well adapted to wetland environments and is often found in marshes and along the edges of ponds.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in aquatic habitats.
