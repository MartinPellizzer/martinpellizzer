Polyalthia ramiflora

Polyalthia ramiflora, commonly known as the branched flame tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, pendulous inflorescences that produce bright orange-red flowers, which bloom in clusters during the rainy season. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are alternate and often have a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in lowland forests or along riverbanks.

The tree is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated for its decorative appeal.
