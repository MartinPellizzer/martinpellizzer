Ptilotus latifolius var. chamaecladus

Ptilotus latifolius var. chamaecladus, commonly known as the silky bluebush, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit and its striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has narrow, gray-green leaves covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in its harsh environment. It is well adapted to drought and poor soils, making it a resilient component of coastal and semi-arid ecosystems.

Its ornamental value has led to its cultivation in gardens, particularly in regions with a Mediterranean climate.
