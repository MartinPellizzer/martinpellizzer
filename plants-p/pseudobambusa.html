Pseudobambusa

Pseudobambusa, commonly known as false bamboo, is a genus of flowering plants in the grass family Poaceae.

These plants are characterized by their tall, slender stalks that resemble true bamboos but are actually grasses. They are native to Southeast Asia and are often cultivated for their ornamental value. Pseudobambusa species typically grow in moist, shaded environments and can reach heights of several meters.

Their leaves are usually long and narrow, contributing to their aesthetic appeal in garden settings.
