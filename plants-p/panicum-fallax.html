Panicum fallax

Panicum fallax, commonly known as fallax panicum, is a species of grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically a bluish-green color. The plant produces small, delicate spikelets that are often tinged with red or purple, adding to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it suitable for xeriscaping and native plant gardens.

Due to its drought tolerance and low maintenance requirements, it is a popular choice for erosion control and wildlife habitat in native landscapes.
