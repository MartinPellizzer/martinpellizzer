Polypodium inarticulatum

Polypodium inarticulatum, commonly known as the rock fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Polypodiaceae.

It is characterized by its small, closely spaced leaves that form dense, compact clusters. The plant typically grows on rocky substrates, often in shaded, moist environments. Its fronds are usually leathery and dark green, with a distinctive, narrow shape.

Polypodium inarticulatum is known for its resilience and ability to thrive in challenging, rocky habitats.
