Physostegia variegata

Physostegia variegata, commonly known as the Indian blanket flower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is characterized by its showy, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to red. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a slight pubescence, while its stems are square, a common trait in the mint family.

Physostegia variegata is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
