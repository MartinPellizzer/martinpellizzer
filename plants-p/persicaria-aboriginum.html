Persicaria aboriginum

Persicaria aboriginum, commonly known as the Australian knotweed, is a perennial herb native to Australia.

It features upright, slender stems that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or red, blooming in late summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly rough texture.

Persicaria aboriginum is adapted to a variety of soil types and is often found in disturbed or open habitats.
