Pinus larix var. rubra

Pinus larix var. rubra, commonly known as the red larch, is a variant of the larch tree characterized by its distinctive reddish-brown bark and needle-like leaves.

This coniferous species is native to certain regions of Europe and is known for its ability to thrive in cooler climates. Its cones are typically small and woody, containing seeds that are dispersed by wind. The tree has a conical shape with a broad crown, and its needles turn a vibrant yellow in the autumn.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in forest ecosystems.
