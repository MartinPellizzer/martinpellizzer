Protea racemosa

Protea racemosa, commonly known as the silver protea, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Proteaceae family and is characterized by its striking, cone-shaped inflorescence. The plant features showy, papery bracts that surround the true flowers, which are small and clustered. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Protea racemosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in local ecosystems.
