Prunus avium subsp. duracina

Prunus avium subsp.

duracina, commonly known as the Persian cherry, is a subspecies native to regions of Central Asia and the Middle East. It is a small to medium-sized tree characterized by its smooth, gray bark and oval, glossy leaves.

The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white or pink flowers in the spring, followed by edible, sweet cherries. It thrives in temperate climates and prefers well-drained soil.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
