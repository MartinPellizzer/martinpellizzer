Potentilla grammopetala

Potentilla grammopetala, commonly known as the narrow-leaved cinquefoil, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has compound leaves with five elliptical leaflets, giving it its scientific name. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Its hardy nature makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.
