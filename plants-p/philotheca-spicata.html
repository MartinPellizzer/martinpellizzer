Philotheca spicata

Philotheca spicata, commonly known as the spiked philotheca, is a perennial shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family, which includes citrus plants, and is characterized by its aromatic, glandular leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in spikes, giving it its common name. Its fruit is a small, round drupe that ripens to a dark red or black color.

Philotheca spicata is often found in dry, open habitats and is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in traditional medicine.
