Plagiochloa glomerata

Plagiochloa glomerata, commonly known as the clustered hairgrass, is a perennial grass species native to Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically green to reddish in color. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in coastal or inland regions.

Plagiochloa glomerata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in arid environments.
