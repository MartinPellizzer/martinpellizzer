Pyrus pashia

Pyrus pashia, commonly known as the Himalayan whitebeam, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are typically oval to elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, white or greenish-yellow flowers in clusters, which are followed by edible, subglobose berries. Pyrus pashia thrives in temperate climates and is often found in rocky or mountainous regions.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
