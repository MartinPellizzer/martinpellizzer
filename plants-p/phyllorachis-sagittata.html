Phyllorachis sagittata

Phyllorachis sagittata, commonly known as the arrowhead plant, is a member of the Araceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive arrow-shaped leaves that are typically bright green and have a glossy texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often surrounded by a modified leaf called a spathe. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full shade.

Phyllorachis sagittata is often used in ornamental gardening for its attractive foliage and adaptability to various growing conditions.
