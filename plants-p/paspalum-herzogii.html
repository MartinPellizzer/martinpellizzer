Paspalum herzogii

Paspalum herzogii, commonly known as Herzog's grass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, blade-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for landscaping in arid regions.

Paspalum herzogii is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soil in erosion-prone areas.
