Pochota quinata

Pochota quinata, commonly known as the Mexican coffee tree, is a species native to Central America and northern South America.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree that can grow up to 15 meters tall. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers followed by elongated, woody fruits containing several seeds. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a rough texture with a strong, spicy aroma.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
