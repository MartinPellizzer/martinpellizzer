Prunus × amelanchieriflora

Prunus × amelanchieriflora, commonly known as the Saskatoon serviceberry, is a hybrid shrub that combines traits from both Prunus americana and Prunus grandidentata.

It is native to North America and is characterized by its ornamental value, featuring white to pink flowers in early spring and edible berries in the summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 3–6 meters and has a rounded, multi-stemmed form. Its leaves are oval with serrated edges, turning yellow in the fall, and it thrives in full sun to partial shade.

It is often used in landscaping for its aesthetic appeal and ecological benefits.
