Primula cuneifolia var. elongata

Primula cuneifolia var. elongata, commonly known as the elongated primrose, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of East Asia.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped, elongated leaves that are typically covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, which bloom in early spring. It thrives in cool, moist environments and prefers well-drained, loamy soils.

Due to its ornamental value and unique foliage, it is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
