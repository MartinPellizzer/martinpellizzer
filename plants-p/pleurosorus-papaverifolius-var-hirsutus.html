Pleurosorus papaverifolius var. hirsutus

Pleurosorus papaverifolius var. hirsutus, commonly known as the hairy broadleaf polypody, is a fern species characterized by its distinctive, broad, and hairy leaves.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is native to regions of East Asia, including China, Japan, and Korea. The fronds are typically bipinnate, with a leathery texture and a grayish-silver appearance due to the dense pubescence. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and rocky crevices.

This variety is often noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in horticulture for its unique foliage.
