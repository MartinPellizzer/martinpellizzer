Periploca calophylla subsp. floribunda

Periploca calophylla subsp.

floribunda, commonly known as the flowering periploca, is a shrub native to parts of Africa and the Arabian Peninsula. It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring.

The plant produces small, oval-shaped berries that are typically yellow or orange when ripe. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and semi-arid regions.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
