Pasania litseifolia

Pasania litseifolia, commonly known as the African sandalwood, is a small evergreen shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its aromatic, fragrant leaves and small white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in coastal and sandy soils, often in moist or humid environments. Its leaves are opposite, elliptical, and have a distinctive waxy texture.

Pasania litseifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its fragrant foliage.
