Pelargonium hirtipetalum

Pelargonium hirtipetalum, commonly known as the woolly petal geranium, is a species of flowering plant in the Geraniaceae family.

It is native to South Africa and is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage and showy, pink to purple flowers. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub with rounded leaves and produces small, clustered blooms. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long blooming period and ability to attract pollinators.
