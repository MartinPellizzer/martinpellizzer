Pityopus

Pityopus, commonly known as the mistletoe, is a parasitic plant that attaches itself to the branches of trees and shrubs.

It belongs to the family Viscaraceae and is characterized by its evergreen leaves and small, white or yellowish berries. The plant lacks true roots and absorbs nutrients through its stems, which are embedded into the host. It plays a significant role in certain ecosystems by providing food for birds and insects.

However, it can also weaken its host plant over time if left unchecked.
