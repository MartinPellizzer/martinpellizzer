Poa arida

Poa arida, commonly known as the desert grass, is a perennial grass species native to arid and semi-arid regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its low-growing, tufted habit and narrow, blue-green leaves that are adapted to conserve water. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences with pale green to brown flowers, which are typical of the Poaceae family. Poa arida is well-suited to poor soils and can thrive in harsh environmental conditions.

Its ecological importance includes stabilizing soil and providing forage for grazing animals in dryland ecosystems.
