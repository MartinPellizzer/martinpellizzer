Petalostemon virgatus

Petalostemon virgatus, commonly known as the silverleaf petalostemon, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its narrow, silvery-gray leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and low maintenance requirements.

Petalostemon virgatus is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
