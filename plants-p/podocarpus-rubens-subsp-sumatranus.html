Podocarpus rubens subsp. sumatranus

Podocarpus rubens subsp.

sumatranus, commonly known as the Sumatran podocarp, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia. It belongs to the Podocarpaceae family and is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and small, cone-like structures.

The plant typically grows in humid, tropical climates and thrives in well-drained soils. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in horticulture and reforestation projects.

The subspecies is distinguished by its slightly smaller leaves and specific geographic distribution compared to other Podocarpus species.
