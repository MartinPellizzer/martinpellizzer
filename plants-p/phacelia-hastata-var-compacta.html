Phacelia hastata var. compacta

Phacelia hastata var. compacta, commonly known as the dwarf phacelia, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit, with small, hairy leaves and clusters of blue to purple flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in dry slopes and rocky areas. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

It is valued in native plant gardens for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
