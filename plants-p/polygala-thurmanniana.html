Polygala thurmanniana

Polygala thurmanniana, commonly known as the Thurmann's milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its flowers are usually pink or purple, with a distinctive spurred structure that attracts pollinators like bees and butterflies.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
