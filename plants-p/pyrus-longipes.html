Pyrus longipes

Pyrus longipes, commonly known as the longipes pear, is a species of flowering plant in the family Rosaceae.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, particularly in countries like Kazakhstan and Kyrgyzstan. The plant is characterized by its slender, often multi-stemmed shrub form and lance-shaped leaves. Its small, white flowers appear in early spring, followed by edible, sweet fruits that are similar to common pears.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been cultivated for both its fruit and its ability to thrive in harsh, arid environments.
