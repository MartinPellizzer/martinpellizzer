Pleurostachys urvillei

Pleurostachys urvillei, commonly known as the Urville's bamboo, is a member of the grass family (Poaceae) and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender culms that can reach heights of up to 10 meters. The plant has narrow, linear leaves with a smooth surface and is often found in moist, tropical environments. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in panicles, which are typically wind-pollinated.

Due to its rapid growth and structural strength, it is sometimes used in traditional construction and as a ornamental plant.
