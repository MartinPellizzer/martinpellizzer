Passiflora obtusifolia

Passiflora obtusifolia, commonly known as the rough pasqueflower, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, intricate flowers with five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments surrounding the reproductive structures. The plant typically grows as a vine or shrub, with compound leaves that have three to five leaflets. Its fruit is a green, fleshy berry that turns red when mature, containing numerous seeds.

Passiflora obtusifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
