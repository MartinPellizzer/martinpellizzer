Physaria newberryi subsp. newberryi

Physaria newberryi subsp.

newberryi, commonly known as the Newberry's bladderpod, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit and produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain several seeds.

The plant has hairy stems and leaves that are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine pubescence. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in sagebrush habitats.

The flowers are yellow and occur in clusters, adding a distinctive visual element to its habitat.
