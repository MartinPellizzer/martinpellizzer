Panicum hirticaule var. glabrescens

Panicum hirticaule var. glabrescens, commonly known as silky blue panicum, is a variant of the panic grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-blue foliage and fluffy, seed-producing inflorescences that resemble bottlebrushes. The plant typically grows in full sun and prefers well-drained soils, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens. Its compact growth habit and drought tolerance contribute to its adaptability in various landscape conditions.

Botanically, it exhibits a rhizomatous root system and produces small, wind-dispersed seeds.
