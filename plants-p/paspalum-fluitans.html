Paspalum fluitans

Paspalum fluitans, commonly known as floating grass, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its rhizomatous growth and ability to form dense mats on the surface of still or slow-moving water. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in wetland environments and is often found in marshes, ponds, and along the edges of lakes.

Due to its rapid growth and ability to stabilize soil, it plays an important ecological role in aquatic ecosystems.
