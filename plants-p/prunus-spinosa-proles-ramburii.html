Prunus spinosa proles ramburii

Prunus spinosa proles ramburii, commonly known as the Rambur's blackthorn, is a shrub native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and small, dark purple berries. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in woodland edges or scrublands. Its flowers are white and appear in early spring, followed by the development of its edible yet tart fruits.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in traditional medicine.
