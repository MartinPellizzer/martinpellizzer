Phlox planitiarum

Phlox planitiarum, commonly known as the plains phlox, is a herbaceous perennial native to the central United States.

It features showy, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has slender, branching stems and lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained, sandy or loamy soils.

Phlox planitiarum is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
