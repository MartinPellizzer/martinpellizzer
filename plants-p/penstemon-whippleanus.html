Penstemon whippleanus

Penstemon whippleanus, commonly known as Whipple's beardtongue, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a perennial species that typically grows in dry, rocky habitats such as cliffs and slopes. The plant features showy, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to purple, attracting pollinators like bees and hummingbirds. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

Penstemon whippleanus is valued in native plant gardens for its hardiness and ornamental appeal.
