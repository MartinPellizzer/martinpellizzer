Phyllanthus loranthoides var. loranthoides

Phyllanthus loranthoides var. loranthoides, commonly known as the Indian gooseberry or amla, is a deciduous shrub native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape.

The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, followed by yellowish-orange fruits that are rich in vitamin C. Its botanical features make it an important species in traditional medicine and horticulture.
