Polygala carunculata

Polygala carunculata, commonly known as the horned milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces distinctive seed pods that are often curved or twisted, giving it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Polygala carunculata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
