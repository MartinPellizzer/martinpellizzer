Pelargonium plicatum

Pelargonium plicatum, commonly known as the zigzag geranium, is a species of flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, zigzag-shaped leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces small, clustered flowers in shades of pink, red, and white, which bloom during the summer months. Pelargonium plicatum is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and compact growth habit.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.
