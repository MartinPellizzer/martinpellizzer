Piper cayoense

Piper cayoense, commonly known as the Cayo pepper or Cayo mountain pepper, is a flowering plant species native to the Caribbean islands.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species such as black pepper. The plant is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy texture. Its small, greenish-white flowers are followed by elongated, fleshy berries that contain the aromatic seeds used in traditional medicine.

Piper cayoense is valued for its unique flavor and potential medicinal properties.
