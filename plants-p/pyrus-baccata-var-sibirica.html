Pyrus baccata var. sibirica

Pyrus baccata var. sibirica, commonly known as the Siberian whitebeam, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Europe and western Asia.

It features small, white to pale green leaves that are opposite or sub-opposite in arrangement. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clusters, which are followed by spherical, bright red berries in the fall. Its bark is smooth and grayish-white, becoming fissured with age.

This hardy species is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
