Phalaenopsis equestris

Phalaenopsis equestris, commonly known as the horse's tail orchid, is a tropical epiphytic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It features long, pendant inflorescences that bear numerous small, white flowers resembling horsehair, hence its common name. The plant has broad, lance-shaped leaves that are usually dark green and glossy. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires moderate humidity and well-draining potting media.

Phalaenopsis equestris is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated as a houseplant or in botanical gardens.
