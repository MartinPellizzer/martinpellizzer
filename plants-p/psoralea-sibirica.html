Psoralea sibirica

Psoralea sibirica, commonly known as Siberian Psoralea, is a flowering plant native to the cold climates of Siberia and northern Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, yellowish flowers and trifoliate leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to harsh, arctic or alpine environments. It is an annual or biennial herb that plays a role in soil stabilization and provides nectar for pollinators.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
