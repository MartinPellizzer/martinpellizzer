Peperomia reptabunda

Peperomia reptabunda, commonly known as the creeping peperomia, is a low-growing plant native to the rainforests of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its compact, trailing stems and thick, waxy leaves that are typically green with a slight reddish tinge. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually greenish-white in color. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and prefers well-draining soil.

Peperomia reptabunda is a popular choice for indoor gardening due to its attractive foliage and ease of care.
