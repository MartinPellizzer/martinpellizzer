Pinus inflexa

Pinus inflexa, commonly known as the Mexican bristlecone pine, is a species of evergreen conifer native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its dense, needle-like leaves that are typically arranged in clusters of five. The plant produces small, purple to reddish cones that contain the seeds. It thrives in high-altitude environments, often growing in rocky soils.

Pinus inflexa is notable for its longevity and resilience in harsh, arid conditions.
