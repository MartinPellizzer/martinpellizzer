Pseuderanthemum floribundum

Pseuderanthemum floribundum, commonly known as the clustered flame flower, is a tropical vine native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its rapid growth and ability to climb using its tendrils. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that are typically bright red or orange, attracting pollinators such as hummingbirds. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a hairy texture.

It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
