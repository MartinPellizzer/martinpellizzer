Phlomoides inaequalisepala

Phlomoides inaequalisepala, commonly known as the unequal-sepal phlomoides, is a flowering plant belonging to the Lamiaceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive floral morphology, featuring bilaterally symmetrical flowers with unequal sepals. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, showcasing adaptability to dry conditions. Its leaves are often lance-shaped and covered in fine pubescence, aiding in moisture retention.

The flowers, which are usually purple or violet in color, attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
