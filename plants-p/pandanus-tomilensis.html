Pandanus tomilensis

Pandanus tomilensis, commonly known as the Hawaiian pandanus, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of the Pacific.

It is characterized by its tall, slender, leafy stems and large, fan-shaped leaves that are often used in traditional crafts. The plant produces small, clustered yellowish flowers that develop into spiny, woody fruits. It thrives in coastal areas and is adapted to salt spray and sandy soils.

Pandanus tomilensis plays an important ecological role in its native habitats, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
