Piptanthocereus jamacaru

Piptanthocereus jamacaru, commonly known as the Christmas cactus, is a species of cactus native to the Atlantic coastal forests of Brazil.

It is characterized by its segmented stems that bear clusters of vibrant, funnel-shaped flowers, typically in shades of red, pink, and purple. The plant produces showy blooms during the holiday season, making it a popular ornamental species. Its leaves are modified into thorn-like structures, and it has a shallow root system adapted to its humid, tropical environment.

It thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.
