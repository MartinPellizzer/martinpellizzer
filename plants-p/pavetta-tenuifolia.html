Pavetta tenuifolia

Pavetta tenuifolia, commonly known as slenderleaf pavetta, is a small evergreen shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by small, fleshy berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun exposure.

The leaves and flowers are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
