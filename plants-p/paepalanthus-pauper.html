Paepalanthus pauper

Paepalanthus pauper, commonly known as the poor man's cactus, is a member of the Cactaceae family and is native to South America.

It is a small, spineless cactus that typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments. The plant has a globular or cylindrical stem that is covered in a thick, waxy cuticle to reduce water loss. It produces small, yellow flowers during the warmer months, which attract pollinators such as bees and moths.

Due to its hardy nature and low maintenance requirements, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant in dry climates.
