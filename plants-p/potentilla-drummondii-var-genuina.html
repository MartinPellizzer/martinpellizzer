Potentilla drummondii var. genuina

Potentilla drummondii var. genuina, commonly known as Drummond's cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a low-growing perennial shrub characterized by its bright yellow flowers and serrated leaves. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its compact size and ornamental value make it a popular choice for native plant gardens.
