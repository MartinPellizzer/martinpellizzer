Polygala amarella var. ovatum

Polygala amarella var. ovatum, commonly known as the yellow milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to southern China.

It belongs to the Polygalaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and delicate, yellowish-green flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in sunny conditions. Its small, bead-like fruits contain seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
