Peperomia potamophila

Peperomia potamophila, commonly known as the Amazonian peperomia, is a flowering plant native to the rainforests of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its compact, succulent-like foliage and small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat, and thrives in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are often fleshy and have a waxy texture, which helps it retain moisture in its native environment.

Peperomia potamophila is popular among collectors for its unique appearance and relatively easy care requirements.
