Panicum sucosum

Panicum sucosum, commonly known as sugarcane grass, is a perennial grass native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain tiny flowers, typical of the Poaceae family. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along waterways.

Due to its rapid growth and adaptability, it can become invasive in certain environments.
