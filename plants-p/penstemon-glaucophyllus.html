Penstemon glaucophyllus

Penstemon glaucophyllus, commonly known as the silverleaf beardtongue, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its hairy, silver-green leaves and showy, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to purple. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun. It is well adapted to arid environments and is often found in mountainous regions.

Penstemon glaucophyllus is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
