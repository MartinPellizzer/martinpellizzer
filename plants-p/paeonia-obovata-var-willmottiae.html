Paeonia obovata var. willmottiae

Paeonia obovata var. willmottiae, commonly known as the Willmott's peony, is a cultivar of the peony plant native to China.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink, white, and red. The plant features compound, bipinnate leaves that are broad and ovate, with a soft, velvety texture. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

This variety is valued for its striking blooms and hardy nature, suitable for both garden cultivation and botanical study.
