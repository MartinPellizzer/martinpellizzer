Pityopsis argentea

Pityopsis argentea, commonly known as silverleaf flax, is a perennial plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Its drought tolerance and ornamental value make it a popular choice for native plant gardens.
