Pterocarpus odoratus

Pterocarpus odoratus, commonly known as the sandalwood tree, is a tropical hardwood species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic wood, which is highly valued for its fragrance and durability. The plant produces distinctive yellow to orange flowers, followed by long, flat pods containing flat, bean-like seeds. Its leaves are typically bipinnate, consisting of multiple small leaflets.

The tree is often cultivated for its essential oils and is also used in traditional medicine and as a source of timber.
