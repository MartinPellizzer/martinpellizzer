Pogonolepis

Pogonolepis, commonly known as the bearded lichen, is a genus of lichens belonging to the family Pilocarpaceae.

It is characterized by its unique thallus structure, which often features a crustose or foliose growth form. The genus is notable for its intricate apothecia, which are typically surrounded by a dense, hairy margin. Pogonolepis species are predominantly found in tropical and subtropical regions, growing on rocks or bark.

Their chemical composition includes various secondary metabolites that contribute to their ecological roles and potential medicinal uses.
