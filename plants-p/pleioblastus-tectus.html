Pleioblastus tectus

Pleioblastus tectus, commonly known as the Japanese forest grass, is a species of bamboo native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically a vibrant green. This plant is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its tolerance to partial shade, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are followed by elongated, cylindrical fruits.

Its foliage provides a fine texture and is often used in landscape design to add visual interest and texture to gardens.
