Polemonium richardsonii

Polemonium richardsonii, commonly known as Richardson's bluebell, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It belongs to the Polemoniaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and meadows. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged alternately along the stem.

It is a herbaceous perennial that blooms in late spring to early summer.
