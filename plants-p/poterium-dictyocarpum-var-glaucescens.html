Poterium dictyocarpum var. glaucescens

Poterium dictyocarpum var. glaucescens, commonly known as the glaucous star sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories or wetlands. Its leaves are often covered in a waxy, bluish-gray coating, which gives it its common name.

This variety is noted for its ecological importance in maintaining soil stability and providing habitat for various microorganisms.
