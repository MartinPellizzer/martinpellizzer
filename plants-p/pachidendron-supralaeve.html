Pachidendron supralaeve

Pachidendron supralaeve, commonly known as the smooth-leafed pachidendron, is a member of the Santalaceae family and is characterized by its woody structure and epiphytic growth habit.

It typically exhibits a shrub-like form with elongated, smooth leaves that are often arranged in a spiral pattern along its stems. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually clustered in inflorescences, and its fruits are capsule-like structures containing numerous tiny seeds. It is native to tropical and subtropical regions, where it thrives in humid, shaded environments.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists studying plant adaptation and diversity.
