Philodendron crinipes

Philodendron crinipes, commonly known as the "Climbing Philodendron" or "Crimson Glory," is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves that often have a vibrant red or pink underside, which becomes more pronounced in low light. The plant is a vigorous climber, capable of reaching lengths of several meters, and thrives in humid, warm environments. Its aerial roots allow it to attach to surfaces as it climbs, making it well-suited for vertical gardening or hanging baskets.

Philodendron crinipes is relatively easy to care for, requiring moderate watering and indirect sunlight for optimal growth.
