Picea nigra var. pendula-variegata

Picea nigra var. pendula-variegata, commonly known as the weeping black spruce variegated, is a unique variant of the black spruce native to North America.

This plant is characterized by its drooping branches that create a cascading effect, giving it a distinctive, ornamental appearance. Its foliage is variegated, featuring a mix of green and white or yellow hues, which adds visual interest. The tree typically grows in moist, acidic soils and thrives in cooler climates.

It is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal and slow growth rate.
