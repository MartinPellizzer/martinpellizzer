Pterolobium macrophyllum

Pterolobium macrophyllum, commonly known as the large-leaved pterolobium, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to parts of Africa and the Caribbean.

It is characterized by its large, elongated leaves that can reach lengths of up to 30 centimeters, often with a velvety texture. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, arranged in clusters along the stems. Its fruit is a pod containing several seeds, which are dispersed by wind or animals.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
