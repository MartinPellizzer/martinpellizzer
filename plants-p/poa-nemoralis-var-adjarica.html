Poa nemoralis var. adjarica

Poa nemoralis var. adjarica, commonly known as the Adjara meadow grass, is a perennial grass species native to the Caucasus region.

It typically grows in moist, temperate environments such as meadows and forest edges. The plant has slender, upright stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often slightly hairy. Its inflorescence consists of a loose to dense cluster of spikelets, which are characteristic of the Poaceae family.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small invertebrates in its native habitat.
