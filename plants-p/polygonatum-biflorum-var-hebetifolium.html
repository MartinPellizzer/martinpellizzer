Polygonatum biflorum var. hebetifolium

Polygonatum biflorum var. hebetifolium, commonly known as the blunt-leaved Solomon's seal, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to eastern Asia.

It features grass-like, lance-shaped leaves that are broader and more rounded compared to other varieties of Solomon's seal. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers in late spring, followed by red berries in the fall. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread slowly and form dense colonies over time.
