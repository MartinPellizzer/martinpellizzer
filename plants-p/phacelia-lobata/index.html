Phacelia Lobata

Bluecurls, scientifically known as Phacelia lobata, is a striking annual plant native to the western United States. Its unique blue-violet flowers, which resemble curling tendrils, make it a popular choice for gardens and wildflower landscapes. This plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in open meadows and dry slopes. With its ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies, Bluecurls plays an important role in supporting local ecosystems. Its delicate beauty and ecological value make it a fascinating subject for both botanists and nature enthusiasts.
