Potamogeton caespitosus

Potamogeton caespitosus, commonly known as the tufted pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in North America.

It has slender, branching stems that are often covered in small, narrow leaves arranged in whorls. The plant produces small, green flowers that are typically clustered in dense, spike-like inflorescences. It plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.

Potamogeton caespitosus is often found in slow-moving waters such as ponds, lakes, and wetlands.
