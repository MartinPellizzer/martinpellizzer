Polygonum polygaloides subsp. polygaloides

Polygonum polygaloides subsp.

polygaloides, commonly known as the narrow-leaf smartweed, is a herbaceous annual plant native to North America. It typically grows in moist to wet soils, often found in marshes, pond edges, and along stream banks.

The plant features slender, ascending stems that are often reddish in color, and its leaves are lance-shaped with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. Its small, clustered flowers are usually pink or red, blooming from late summer to early fall.

This species is known for its adaptability to wetland environments and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
