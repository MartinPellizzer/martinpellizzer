Phyllanthus racemiger

Phyllanthus racemiger, commonly known as the clustered phyllanthus, is a flowering plant belonging to the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is characterized by its small, clustered inflorescences and opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy surface. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers that are typically clustered in racemes, giving it its scientific name. It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific Islands.

Phyllanthus racemiger is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories and along stream banks.
