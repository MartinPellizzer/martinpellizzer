Poa chaixii

Poa chaixii, commonly known as the Chaix's bentgrass, is a low-growing perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It typically forms dense, mat-like turf and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and small, spikelet-bearing inflorescences. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and establish itself in nutrient-poor soils.

Poa chaixii is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
