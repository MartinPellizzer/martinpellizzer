Prunus × sieboldii f. parvifolia

Prunus × sieboldii f. parvifolia, commonly known as the dwarf cherry or parvifolia cherry, is a compact, ornamental shrub native to Japan.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are typically a glossy dark green, often with a slight reddish tinge on the underside. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers in early spring, followed by small, dark red berries. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained, fertile soil.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often used in gardens for its aesthetic appeal and seasonal interest.
