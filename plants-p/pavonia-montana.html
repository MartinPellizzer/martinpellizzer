Pavonia montana

Pavonia montana, commonly known as the mountain pavonia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in open woodlands and rocky soils. The plant features showy, pink to red bracts that resemble petals, though it lacks true petals. Its leaves are ovate to lanceolate, often with a slightly hairy surface.

Pavonia montana blooms in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
