Paeonia daurica subsp. velebitensis

Paeonia daurica subsp.

velebitensis, commonly known as the Daurian peony, is a perennial herb native to the Balkan Peninsula. It is characterized by its upright, branched stems and large, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and red.

The plant produces palmately divided leaves with a serrated margin, and its roots are thick and fleshy. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and woodland settings.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its vibrant blooms.
