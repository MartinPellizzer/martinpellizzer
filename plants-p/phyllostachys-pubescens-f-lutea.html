Phyllostachys pubescens f. lutea

Phyllostachys pubescens f. lutea, commonly known as the golden bamboo, is a variant of the common bamboo species characterized by its striking golden-yellow culms.

This ornamental plant is native to East Asia and is prized for its vibrant color and graceful appearance. It typically grows in clumps and can reach heights of up to 10 meters, with narrow, linear leaves that are a deep green. The golden hue of its stems makes it a popular choice for garden landscapes and bonsai cultivation.

It thrives in well-drained soil and requires moderate watering, making it relatively easy to care for.
