Pachyphyllum stuebelii

Pachyphyllum stuebelii, commonly known as the Stuebel's stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that store water, allowing it to survive in dry conditions. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in mountainous areas. Its flowers are small and usually yellow or orange, appearing in clusters during the warmer months.

Pachyphyllum stuebelii is well adapted to full sun and requires minimal water, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.
