Podocarpus mannii

Podocarpus mannii, commonly known as the African yellowwood, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Podocarpaceae and is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and aromatic wood. The plant produces small, cone-like structures that contain seeds, typical of conifers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

Due to its hardiness and ornamental value, it is cultivated in horticulture and used for timber and ornamental purposes.
