Protea passerina

Protea passerina, commonly known as the silver protea, is a striking South African plant belonging to the Proteaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescence composed of numerous white to pale pink flower heads. The plant features a sturdy, branched stem with rough, hairy leaves that are elliptical in shape. Native to the Cape Province, it thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and full sun.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens, though it requires careful management to avoid invasive growth.
