Psydrax montanus

Psydrax montanus, commonly known as the mountain devil tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are often elliptical or ovate in shape. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by small, round fruits. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The botanical features of Psydrax montanus make it an important species in ecological studies and traditional medicine.
