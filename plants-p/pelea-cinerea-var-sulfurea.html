Pelea cinerea var. sulfurea

Pelea cinerea var. sulfurea, commonly known as the sulfur dust fern, is a rare and striking variant of the pelea cinerea species.

It is characterized by its delicate, translucent fronds that emit a faint sulfur-like scent, particularly when exposed to moisture. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, typically found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its unique appearance and olfactory properties make it a subject of interest in botanical research.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
