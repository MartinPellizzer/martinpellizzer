Parietaria virgata

Parietaria virgata, commonly known as the common wall-rocket, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cress plants. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, typically from spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, giving them a slightly fuzzy texture.

Parietaria virgata is often found growing in disturbed soils, along walls, and in shady areas.
