Pleione cumingii

Pleione cumingii, commonly known as the Cuming's pleione, is a species of orchid native to the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its compact, pseudobulbous structure and produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink. The plant typically blooms in spring and is adapted to grow in shaded, moist environments. Its leaves are usually broad and grass-like, and it is often found at high altitudes.

Pleione cumingii is valued for its ornamental beauty and is cultivated by orchid enthusiasts for its unique floral display.
