Pothuava nudicaulis var. simulans

Pothuava nudicaulis var. simulans, commonly known as the slender potho, is a rare and unique variant of the potho genus native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, narrow leaves that are often clustered at the base of the plant, forming a rosette structure. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-white in color, followed by berry-like fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a species of conservation concern.
