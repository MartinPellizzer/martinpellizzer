Protea latifolia

Protea latifolia, commonly known as the sugar bush, is a South African plant belonging to the Proteaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, flat, pale yellow to orange flower heads, which are surrounded by long, narrow bracts. The plant is evergreen and can grow up to 10 meters tall, with smooth, grey-green bark and glossy, oval-shaped leaves. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

The protea is an important component of the fynbos biome and is valued for its ornamental and ecological significance.
