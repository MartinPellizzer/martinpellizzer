Pandanus arrectialatus

Pandanus arrectialatus, commonly known as the tall pandanus, is a tropical plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and long, narrow, sword-shaped leaves that are often used in traditional crafts. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically clustered at the base of the leaves. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to thrive in wet, coastal environments.

The plant plays an important ecological role in stabilizing coastal soils and providing habitat for various species.
