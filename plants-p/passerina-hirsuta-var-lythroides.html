Passerina hirsuta var. lythroides

Passerina hirsuta var. lythroides, commonly known as the hairy pasqueflower, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its hairy, palmately divided leaves and showy, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant thrives in alpine and subalpine environments, often growing in rocky or gravelly soils at high elevations. It is known for its early blooming period, typically in late spring, and is adapted to cold climates with a short growing season.

The species is of interest to botanists and gardeners due to its ornamental value and ecological significance in its native habitat.
