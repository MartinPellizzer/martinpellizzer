Penthorum

Penthorum, commonly known as the marsh woundwort, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Geraniaceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of lanceolate, hairy leaves that are often reddish in color. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers arranged in dense clusters at the ends of its stems. It thrives in moist, often swampy habitats and is native to North America.

Penthorum is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally for its healing qualities.
