Piper san-marcosanum var. gracillimum

Piper san-marcosanum var. gracillimum, commonly known as the slender pepper plant, is a tropical vine native to Central America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and small, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by elongated, oval berries that turn red when mature. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated as a houseplant.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is relatively rare in the wild.
