Porphyrocoma

Porphyrocoma, commonly known as the red-tipped pine, is a species of coniferous tree native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive reddish-brown bark and needle-like leaves that are often bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, cone-like structures that contain its seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Porphyrocoma is adapted to high-altitude environments, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions.

Its botanical features make it an important component of the Andean ecosystem.
