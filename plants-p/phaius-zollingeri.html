Phaius zollingeri

Phaius zollingeri, commonly known as the Zollinger's ginger, is a tropical orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, broad, and often dark green leaves that can grow up to 1 meter in length. The plant produces large, showy inflorescences with multiple fragrant, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining, nutrient-rich soil.

Phaius zollingeri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
