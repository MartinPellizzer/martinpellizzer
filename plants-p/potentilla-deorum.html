Potentilla deorum

Potentilla deorum, commonly known as the sacred potentilla, is a flowering plant native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, single flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are small, oval-shaped, and covered in a fine layer of white hairs.

Potentilla deorum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its charming appearance.
