Pyrus pyraster f. longipedicellata

Pyrus pyraster f. longipedicellata, commonly known as the long-stalked pear.

This cultivar is a variant of the European pear, characterized by its elongated pedicels and compact growth habit. It produces small, round fruits with a yellowish-green skin and a sweet, aromatic flavor. The plant is typically deciduous, shedding its leaves in autumn, and thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil.

It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive foliage and fruiting characteristics.
