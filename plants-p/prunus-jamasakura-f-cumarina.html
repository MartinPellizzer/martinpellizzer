Prunus jamasakura f. cumarina

Prunus jamasakura f. cumarina, commonly known as the fragrant cherry tree, is a botanical variant of the Japanese cherry species.

It is characterized by its distinctive floral scent, which is more pronounced than that of typical Prunus species. The tree produces small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in early spring, adding ornamental value to gardens and landscapes. Its leaves are oval-shaped with a smooth texture and a glossy surface.

This variety is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal and unique fragrance, making it a popular choice in horticultural settings.
