Pilosella × cinerea subsp. tephraea

Pilosella × cinerea subsp.

tephraea, commonly known as the ash-gray hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to the western United States. It is characterized by its grayish-green foliage and produces small, yellowish flowers in dense clusters.

The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its leaves are hairy and often have a whitish bloom, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

The species is part of the Asteraceae family and is known for its ability to thrive in arid conditions.
