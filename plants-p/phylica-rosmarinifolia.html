Phylica rosmarinifolia

Phylica rosmarinifolia, commonly known as the Cape jasmine, is a shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its aromatic, needle-like leaves and small, showy white or pink flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for coastal and sandy environments. It is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.

The flowers attract pollinators such as birds and insects, contributing to local biodiversity.
