Potamogeton latifolius

Potamogeton latifolius, commonly known as broadleaf pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater habitats across Europe, Asia, and North America.

It has long, ribbon-like leaves that are typically broad and flat, often with a smooth or slightly serrated margin. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually arranged in spike-like clusters, and it relies on water currents for pollination. It plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.

Its presence is often an indicator of good water quality in ponds and lakes.
