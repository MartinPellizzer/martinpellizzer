Pyrus parviflora amygdaliformis

Pyrus parviflora amygdaliformis, commonly known as the Japanese white pear, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its small, white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in early spring before the leaves appear. The plant produces small, sweet, yellow or golden fruits that are edible. It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it suitable for gardens and landscapes.

Its ornamental value comes from its fragrant flowers and attractive foliage.
