Palala murtonii

Palala murtonii, commonly known as the Murtonii palala, is a rare and unique flowering plant native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom during the rainy season. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained, nutrient-rich soil to flourish. Its botanical significance lies in its role as an indicator species for undisturbed tropical ecosystems.

Due to habitat loss, Palala murtonii is considered endangered and is the subject of conservation efforts to protect its dwindling populations.
