Pittosporum angustifolium

Pittosporum angustifolium, commonly known as narrow-leaved pittosporum, is a shrub or small tree native to southeastern Australia.

It features dark green, narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often aromatic when crushed. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers in clusters, which are followed by bright red berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and dry forest environments.

Pittosporum angustifolium is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting native wildlife.
