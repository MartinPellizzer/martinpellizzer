Polytaenium cajenense

Polytaenium cajenense, commonly known as the Brazilian flame tree, is a tropical tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters during the dry season. The plant has compound leaves with elongated, lance-shaped leaflets. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The tree can grow up to 20 meters tall and has a fast growth rate.
