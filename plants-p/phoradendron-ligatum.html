Phoradendron ligatum

Phoradendron ligatum, commonly known as the mistletoe of the neotropics, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches to the branches of host trees.

It lacks chlorophyll and relies on its host for water and nutrients, while producing its own sugars through photosynthesis using light absorbed by its leaves. The plant has small, scale-like leaves and produces white or yellowish berries that are an important food source for various birds. It is often found in tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

Its parasitic nature and unique ecological role make it an interesting subject in botanical studies.
