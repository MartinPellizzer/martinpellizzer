Pithecellobium glaucescens

Pithecellobium glaucescens, commonly known as the silverleaf acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of South America.

It features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets that are often covered in a fine, silvery pubescence. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in dense clusters, which are followed by elongated, pod-like fruits. Its root system is fibrous and adapted to nutrient-poor soils, making it a hardy species.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
