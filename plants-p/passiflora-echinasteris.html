Passiflora echinasteris

Passiflora echinasteris, commonly known as the spiny passion flower, is a species native to South America, particularly found in regions of Brazil, Paraguay, and Argentina.

It is a climbing vine characterized by its distinctive, showy flowers that feature a complex arrangement of petals, sepals, and bracts. The plant produces blue or purple berries that are edible and contain seeds. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped with a rough texture and may have spines along the edges.

Passiflora echinasteris is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
