Piper umbriense var. caudatum

Piper umbriense var. caudatum, commonly known as the Italian pepper plant, is a rare and unique variant of the Piper umbriense species.

It is characterized by its striking, elongated, and often pendulous fruits that resemble small, red peppers. The plant belongs to the Piperaceae family and is native to the mountainous regions of southern Italy. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained, fertile soil.

Its ornamental value is highly prized, making it a sought-after plant in specialized horticulture.
