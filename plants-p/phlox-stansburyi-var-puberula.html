Phlox stansburyi var. puberula

Phlox stansburyi var. puberula, commonly known as the Sierra phlox, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant clusters of pink to purple flowers that bloom in the spring. The leaves are typically narrow and covered with a fine layer of short, soft hairs, giving the plant a slightly fuzzy appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
