Passerina filiformis subsp. glutinosa

Passerina filiformis subsp.

glutinosa, commonly known as the sticky threadleaf, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of southern Africa. It is characterized by its slender, thread-like leaves that are often covered in a sticky substance, giving it its common name.

The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pale green to yellow in color. It thrives in arid or semi-arid environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.

Due to its unique morphology and limited distribution, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
