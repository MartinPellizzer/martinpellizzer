Phyllostegia hirsuta var. laxior

Phyllostegia hirsuta var. laxior, commonly known as the hairy phyllostegia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces showy, purple to violet flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or wetland habitats.
