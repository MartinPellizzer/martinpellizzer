Primula officinalis f. intermedia

Primula officinalis f. intermedia, commonly known as the intermediate primrose, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Primulaceae family.

It is characterized by its semi-evergreen foliage and clusters of bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It produces small, oval-shaped berries that contain seeds dispersed by birds.

Due to its ornamental value and historical use in traditional medicine, it is cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
