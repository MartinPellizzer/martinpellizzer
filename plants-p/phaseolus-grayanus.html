Phaseolus grayanus

Phaseolus grayanus, commonly known as the Gray's bean, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and pods. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into elongated pods containing edible seeds. It is typically grown for its nutritional seeds, which are rich in protein and can be used in various culinary preparations.

Due to its genetic uniqueness, it is of interest to botanists and agricultural researchers.
