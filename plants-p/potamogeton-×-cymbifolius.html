Potamogeton × cymbifolius

Potamogeton × cymbifolius, commonly known as the hybrid water plant, is a aquatic plant that exhibits a mix of characteristics from its parent species, Potamogeton crispus and Potamogeton cymbifolius.

It typically grows in shallow freshwater environments such as lakes, ponds, and slow-moving rivers. The plant has long, narrow leaves that are often arranged in a rosette pattern, with a smooth or slightly serrated edge. Its flowers are small and clustered, and it produces both male and female flowers on the same plant, making it monoecious.

This hybrid is known for its adaptability and is sometimes considered an invasive species in certain regions.
