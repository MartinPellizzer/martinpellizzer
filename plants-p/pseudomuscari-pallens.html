Pseudomuscari pallens

Pseudomuscari pallens, commonly known as the pale muscari, is a bulbous perennial plant native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pale blue to white. The plant produces a tall, slender stem that bears numerous spikelets, each containing several florets. Its foliage consists of strap-like leaves that emerge from the base and are typically grassy in appearance.

Pseudomuscari pallens thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens and woodland settings.
