Penstemon sepalulus

Penstemon sepalulus, commonly known as the narrowleaf beardtongue, is a member of the Plantaginaceae family.

It is native to the western United States, particularly in regions of California and Nevada. This perennial herb features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces spikes of tubular, two-lipped flowers. The flowers are typically pink to purple in color and bloom in late spring to early summer.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky habitats or open woodlands.
