Pandanus solomonensis

Pandanus solomonensis, commonly known as the Solomon Islands pandanus, is a tropical plant native to the Pacific islands.

It is a member of the Pandanaceae family and is characterized by its large, sword-shaped leaves that grow in a fan-like arrangement. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or green, followed by fruiting structures known as syncarps. Its fibrous leaves are often used in traditional crafts and for thatching, while the plant itself thrives in coastal and wetland environments.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appearance and ecological importance in its native habitats.
