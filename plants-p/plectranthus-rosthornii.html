Plectranthus rosthornii

Plectranthus rosthornii, commonly known as the Cape speckled hyacinth, is a South African plant belonging to the Lamiaceae family.

It is characterized by its square, hairy stems and opposite, ovate leaves with a distinctive speckled pattern. The plant produces spikes of tubular, violet-blue flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Due to its ornamental value and unique appearance, it is often cultivated in horticultural settings.
