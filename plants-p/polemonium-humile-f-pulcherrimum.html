Polemonium humile f. pulcherrimum

Polemonium humile f. pulcherrimum, commonly known as the showy Jacob's ladder, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features slender, branched stems that bear opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue to purple, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in partial to full shade and moist, well-drained soil.

Its ornamental value comes from its delicate foliage and vibrant floral display.
