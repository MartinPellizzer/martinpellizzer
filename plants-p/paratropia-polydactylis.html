Paratropia polydactylis

Paratropia polydactylis, commonly known as the many-fingered fig, is a species of flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Moraceae family, which includes other well-known plants like the common fig and breadfruit. The plant is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and produces small, clustered fig-like fruits. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in dense forests.
