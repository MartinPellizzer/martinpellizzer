Pogostemon mutamba

Pogostemon mutamba, commonly known as the African mint, is a perennial herb native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants known for their medicinal properties. The plant is characterized by its square stems, opposite leaves, and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. It is often cultivated for its essential oils, which are used in traditional medicine and aromatherapy.

The leaves and flowers are also used in culinary applications, adding a refreshing flavor to various dishes.
