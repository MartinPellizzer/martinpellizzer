Passiflora quadriglandulosa

Passiflora quadriglandulosa, commonly known as the four-gland passionflower, is a climbing vine native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful flowers that feature a unique blend of blue, purple, and white petals, along with intricate filaments and a crown-like structure. The plant produces edible berries that are often consumed fresh or used in jams and beverages. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped with a rough texture and prominent veins.

Passiflora quadriglandulosa is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
