Phaseolus vulgaris var. flavomaculatus

Phaseolus vulgaris var. flavomaculatus, commonly known as the yellow-seeded kidney bean, is a variant of the common bean species.

It is characterized by its distinct yellow seeds with dark brown markings, which set it apart from other bean varieties. This plant is typically grown for its edible seeds, which are used in various culinary preparations worldwide. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

The plant belongs to the Fabaceae family and is valued for its nutritional content and agricultural significance.
