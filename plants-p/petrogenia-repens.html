Petrogenia repens

Petrogenia repens, commonly known as the creeping petrogenia, is a low-growing perennial plant native to South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its trailing stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments. Its leaves are oval-shaped with a hairy texture, and the flowers are usually purple or blue in color.

Petrogenia repens is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value due to its resilience and aesthetic appeal.
