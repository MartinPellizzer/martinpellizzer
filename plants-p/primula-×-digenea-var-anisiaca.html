Primula × digenea var. anisiaca

Primula × digenea var. anisiaca, commonly known as the Anise-leaved Primula, is a hybrid plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive heart-shaped, anise-scented leaves that are velvety in texture. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in rocky or forested habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and unique foliage, it is cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
