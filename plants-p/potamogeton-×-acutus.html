Potamogeton × acutus

Potamogeton × acutus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved pondweed, is a hybrid aquatic plant that occurs in freshwater environments.

It is characterized by its long, narrow leaves that are typically less than 2 mm wide, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are clustered in spikelets, and it is often found in shallow, still or slow-moving water. Potamogeton × acutus plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.

Its presence can indicate good water quality due to its sensitivity to pollution.
