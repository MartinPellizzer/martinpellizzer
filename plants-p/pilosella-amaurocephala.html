Pilosella amaurocephala

Pilosella amaurocephala, commonly known as the "blindhead hawk's-beard," is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and small, yellow composite flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats. Its unique appearance and limited distribution make it a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists.

Due to its habitat specificity, it is considered vulnerable to environmental changes and habitat loss.
