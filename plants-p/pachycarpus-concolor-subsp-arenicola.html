Pachycarpus concolor subsp. arenicola

Pachycarpus concolor subsp.

arenicola, commonly known as the coastal dune lichen, is a lichen that thrives in arid and coastal environments. It is characterized by its dense, crust-like growth form, which helps it retain moisture in harsh conditions.

The plant forms a symbiotic relationship between a fungus and a green alga or cyanobacterium, enabling it to survive in nutrient-poor soils. It is typically found on rocky surfaces or sandy soils near the coast, where it plays a role in stabilizing dunes.

Its ability to tolerate extreme temperatures and salinity makes it an important indicator species for coastal ecosystems.
