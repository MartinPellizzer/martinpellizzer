Pachylophus psammophilus

Pachylophus psammophilus, commonly known as the sand-lobe cactus, is a unique plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its globular or cylindrical shape and is covered in dense, spiny bristles that help reduce water loss. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, which are pollinated by various insects. Its succulent stems store water, allowing it to survive in harsh, dry environments.

Pachylophus psammophilus plays an important role in its ecosystem by providing habitat and food for desert wildlife.
