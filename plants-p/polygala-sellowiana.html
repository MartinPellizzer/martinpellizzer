Polygala sellowiana

Polygala sellowiana, commonly known as the Brazilian milkweed, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Polygalaceae family and is characterized by its showy, spire-like inflorescences that resemble small horns. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are often visited by bees and other pollinators. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Polygala sellowiana is often found in open woodlands and grasslands, thriving in well-drained soils.
