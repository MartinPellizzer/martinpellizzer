Poa unduavensis

Poa unduavensis, commonly known as the Unduavensis grass, is a species of flowering plant in the grass family Poaceae.

It is native to South Africa and is typically found in rocky, coastal areas. The plant is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, blade-like leaves. It produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences that are adapted to coastal conditions.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
