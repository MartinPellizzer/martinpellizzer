Prunus idzuensis f. formosa

Prunus idzuensis f. formosa, commonly known as the Japanese cherry plum.

This ornamental plant is characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant pink to white flowers that bloom in early spring. It produces small, edible fruits that are often used in culinary applications. The leaves are ovate with a smooth margin and a dark green color.

It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
