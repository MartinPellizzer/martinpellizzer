Piscidia corallodendrum

Piscidia corallodendrum, commonly known as the coral tree, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking coral-colored flowers that bloom in clusters, adding vibrant color to its tropical habitat. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets, and its bark is often marked with distinctive patterns. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The coral tree is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal remedies.
