Populus × pseudocanadensis

Populus × pseudocanadensis, commonly known as the trembling aspen.

It is a hybrid species within the Populus genus, characterized by its distinctive trembling leaves that rustle in the wind. This deciduous tree typically grows in temperate regions and is known for its rapid growth and ability to colonize disturbed areas. Its bark is smooth and gray, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in the spring.

The plant plays an important ecological role in providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
