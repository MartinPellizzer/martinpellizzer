Phlox carolina subsp. alta

Phlox carolina subsp.

alta, commonly known as the highland phlox, is a flowering plant native to the Appalachian region of the United States. It is a perennial herb with erect, slender stems that bear clusters of showy, tubular flowers.

The flowers are typically pink to lavender in color and bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and prefers partial to full sun.

It is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance in native plant gardens.
