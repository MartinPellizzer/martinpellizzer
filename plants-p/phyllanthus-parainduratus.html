Phyllanthus parainduratus

Phyllanthus parainduratus, commonly known as the Indian false sandalwood, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in forest margins or disturbed areas.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated for horticultural purposes.
