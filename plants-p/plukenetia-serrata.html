Plukenetia serrata

Plukenetia serrata, commonly known as the African pear or wild pear, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves with serrated margins. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in cupular inflorescences. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release seeds, often dispersed by wind or animals.

The plant is valued for its edible fruits and has traditional uses in local medicine and as a source of oil.
