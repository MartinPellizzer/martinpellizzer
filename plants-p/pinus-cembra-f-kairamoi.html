Pinus cembra f. kairamoi

Pinus cembra f. kairamoi, commonly known as the Kairamoi stone pine, is a rare and endemic variant of the stone pine, native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas.

This subspecies is characterized by its compact, slow-growing form and its ability to thrive in harsh, alpine conditions. The leaves are typically shorter and more densely packed compared to the typical form, adapting to the cold and windy environment. Its cones are small and often remain closed for extended periods, a feature that aids in seed dispersal under specific environmental conditions.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a valuable subject for botanical research and conservation efforts.
