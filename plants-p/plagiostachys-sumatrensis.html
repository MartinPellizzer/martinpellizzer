Plagiostachys sumatrensis

Plagiostachys sumatrensis, commonly known as the Sumatran bamboo, is a tall, slender grass species native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its strong, hollow stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in moist, well-drained soils. It is valued for its durability and is sometimes used in construction and handicrafts.

Its rapid growth rate makes it an important species in ecological restoration projects.
