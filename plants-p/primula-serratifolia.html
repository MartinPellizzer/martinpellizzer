Primula serratifolia

Primula serratifolia, commonly known as the serrated leaf primrose, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and violet, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in woodland or alpine habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and botanical collections worldwide.
