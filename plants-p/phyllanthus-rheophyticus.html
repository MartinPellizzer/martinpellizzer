Phyllanthus rheophyticus

Phyllanthus rheophyticus, commonly known as the stream phyllanthus, is a unique aquatic plant found in fast-flowing mountain streams.

It has finely divided, feather-like leaves that help reduce water resistance and promote efficient nutrient absorption. The plant thrives in nutrient-rich, oxygenated water and is adapted to withstand strong currents. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are typically green or white, and it produces small, round fruits.

Due to its specialized habitat, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species in its native range.
