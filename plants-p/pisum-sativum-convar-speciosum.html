Pisum sativum convar. speciosum

Pisum sativum convar. speciosum, commonly known as the garden pea, is a flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its climbing habit, with tendrils that allow it to ascend supports. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are the edible part consumed by humans. Its flowers are typically blue or violet, with a distinctive pea shape.

The species is widely cultivated for its nutritional value and is an important crop in many agricultural systems.
