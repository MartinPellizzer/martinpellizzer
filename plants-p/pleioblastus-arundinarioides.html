Pleioblastus arundinarioides

Pleioblastus arundinarioides, commonly known as the bamboo grass or Japanese bamboo, is a species of grass in the family Poaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, linear leaves that are typically a bright green color. This plant is native to East Asia, particularly China, Japan, and Korea, and thrives in temperate climates. It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its graceful appearance and ability to form dense clumps.

Pleioblastus arundinarioides is also valued for its role in ecological landscaping and as a sustainable material in traditional crafts.
