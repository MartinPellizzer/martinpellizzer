Phaseolus vignoides

Phaseolus vignoides, commonly known as the evergreen bean or wild kidney bean, is a climbing legume native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and elongated, slender pods that contain several small, kidney-shaped seeds. The plant thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and full sunlight. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink, and it produces a significant amount of biomass, making it a valuable forage crop.

The plant is also known for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, improving soil fertility.
