Polygala nicaeensis subsp. gariodiana

Polygala nicaeensis subsp.

gariodiana, commonly known as the Gariodiana milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands. It belongs to the Polygalaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, clustered flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or coastal habitats, adapting to the arid and sunny conditions of its native environment. Its foliage consists of narrow, linear leaves, and it produces distinctive, often yellow or greenish flowers that attract pollinators.

This subspecies is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and limited distribution.
