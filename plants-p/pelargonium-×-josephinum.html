Pelargonium × josephinum

Pelargonium × josephinum, commonly known as the Emperor's Crown, is a hybrid pelargonium species native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, rounded growth habit and its showy, double-flowered blooms that range from white to pink and red. The plant produces small, rounded leaves with a slightly hairy texture, and its flowers are typically borne in compact clusters. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

This ornamental plant is popular in gardens for its vibrant floral display and compact size.
