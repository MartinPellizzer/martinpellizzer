Prockia subcordata

Prockia subcordata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf prockia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features lanceolate to narrowly elliptic leaves that are often hairy and have a distinctive subcordate base. The plant produces small, white or pale blue flowers arranged in loose clusters during the spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Prockia subcordata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in rock gardens and as a ground cover.
