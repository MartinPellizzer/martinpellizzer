Pastinaca sativa var. edulis

Pastinaca sativa var. edulis, commonly known as the edible parsnip, is a biennial plant belonging to the carrot family (Apiaceae).

It is characterized by its thick, tapered root that is typically white or yellowish in color. The plant produces small, green, umbrella-shaped flowers in its second year, which are followed by small, dry, brown fruits. Its leaves are fern-like, with a mild, slightly sweet scent when crushed.

This variety is cultivated for its edible root, which is used in cooking and traditional medicine.
