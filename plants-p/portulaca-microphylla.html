Portulaca microphylla

Portulaca microphylla, commonly known as the dwarf portulaca, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States and parts of Mexico.

It is a low-growing, succulent annual that thrives in hot, dry climates. The plant features small, fleshy leaves and produces vibrant yellow or orange flowers that bloom in the morning. It is often used in rock gardens or as a bedding plant due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.

Its botanical characteristics make it well-suited for xeriscaping and other water-efficient landscaping practices.
