Phaca mollissima

Phaca mollissima, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its broad, velvety leaves that are typically dark green in color. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually green or yellowish in hue. It thrives in moist, warm environments and is often found in disturbed soils.

Its rapid growth and adaptability make it both a useful forage plant and a potential weed in agricultural settings.
