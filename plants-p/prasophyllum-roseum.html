Prasophyllum roseum

Prasophyllum roseum, commonly known as the pink leek orchid, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its striking pink to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in heathland and sandy soils, often in areas with low rainfall. It has a rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces a single flowering stem.

Prasophyllum roseum is listed as vulnerable due to habitat loss and is protected in several Australian states.
