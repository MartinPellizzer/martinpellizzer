Pitcairnia undulatosepala

Pitcairnia undulatosepala, commonly known as the undulating-sepaled pitcairnia, is a species of bromeliad native to South America.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that are often waxy and covered in fine hairs. The plant produces striking inflorescences with brightly colored flowers, typically in shades of red, orange, or yellow. Its leaves are adapted to capture and store water, making it well-suited for tropical and subtropical environments.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in shaded, humid conditions.
