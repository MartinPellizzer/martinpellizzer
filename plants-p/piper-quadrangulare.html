Piper quadrangulare

Piper quadrangulare, commonly known as the quadrangular pepper or Indian pepper, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters in height, characterized by its opposite, quadrangular (four-sided) stems. The leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a rough texture with a distinctive aromatic scent. It produces small green flowers followed by dark purple to black berries containing a single seed.

The plant is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
