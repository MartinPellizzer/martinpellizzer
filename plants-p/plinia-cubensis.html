Plinia cubensis

Plinia cubensis, commonly known as the Cuban blackberry, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the Caribbean, particularly Cuba, and thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are rich in antioxidants and have a tart flavor. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a rough texture, while its stems are woody and covered in fine hairs.

The fruit is often used in culinary applications and traditional medicine due to its nutritional and medicinal properties.
