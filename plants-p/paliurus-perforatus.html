Paliurus perforatus

Paliurus perforatus, commonly known as the perforate buckthorn, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with serrated margins and small, yellowish-green flowers that appear in clusters. The plant produces small, dark blue berries that are edible but often have a bitter taste. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Paliurus perforatus is known for its ornamental value and has been cultivated for its hardy nature and seasonal interest.
