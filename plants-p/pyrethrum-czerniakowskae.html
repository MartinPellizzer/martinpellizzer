Pyrethrum czerniakowskae

Pyrethrum czerniakowskae, commonly known as the Czerniakowska's pyrethrum, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like yellow flowers. The plant produces fine, hair-like structures called trichomes on its leaves and stems, which are responsible for its insecticidal properties. These trichomes contain pyrethrins, natural compounds used in organic pest control.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it suitable for cultivation in arid and semi-arid regions.
