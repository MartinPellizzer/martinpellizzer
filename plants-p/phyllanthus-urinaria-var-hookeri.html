Phyllanthus urinaria var. hookeri

Phyllanthus urinaria var. hookeri, commonly known as the bladder nut or stonebreaker, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is a shrub or small tree with smooth, dark green leaves that are opposite or alternate in arrangement. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by capsules containing seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed or open habitats.

The plant has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
