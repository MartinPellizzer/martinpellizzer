Petrophile conifera subsp. conifera

Petrophile conifera subsp.

conifera, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bottlebrush, is a shrub native to the south-western region of Western Australia. It is characterized by its distinctive cylindrical flower spikes, which are typically red or pink in color.

The plant has narrow, needle-like leaves and is adapted to grow in sandy or gravelly soils. It is a member of the Myrtaceae family and is often found in heathland and coastal environments.

The species is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
