Pandanus ambohitantelensis

Pandanus ambohitantelensis, commonly known as the Madagascar pandanus, is a species native to Madagascar.

It is a member of the Pandanus genus, characterized by its large, fan-shaped leaves and robust, fibrous structure. The plant typically grows in coastal and wetland areas, adapting to humid and saline environments. Its leaves are often used in traditional crafts and as thatching materials.

The species is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and ecological significance in its native habitat.
