Piper acutistigmum

Piper acutistigmum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved pepper or wild pepper, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia and Africa.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species such as black pepper. The plant is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and heart-shaped leaves with a slightly serrated edge. Its small, greenish-white flowers are followed by small, round berries that contain the aromatic seeds used in traditional medicine.

The plant is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in herbal remedies for its purported anti-inflammatory and analgesic effects.
