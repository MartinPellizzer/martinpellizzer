Potentilla flabellata var. multisecta

Potentilla flabellata var. multisecta, commonly known as the multisection cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its finely divided, feathery leaves that give it a distinctive, almost fern-like appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers in clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in alpine or rocky habitats.

Its ornamental value makes it a sought-after plant in horticulture for its delicate foliage and seasonal blooms.
