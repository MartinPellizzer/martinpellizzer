Pygeum arboreum

Pygeum arboreum, commonly known as the African wild almond or stinkwood, is a deciduous tree native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Proteaceae and is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and compound leaves with serrated edges. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers followed by woody, pod-like fruits containing elliptical seeds. It is often found in rocky, well-drained soils and is valued for its medicinal properties, particularly in traditional medicine.

The plant is also used in commercial products for its potential health benefits.
