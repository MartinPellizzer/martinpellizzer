Pelargonium molloide

Pelargonium molloide, commonly known as the soft-leaved geranium, is a species native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its broad, velvety leaves and clusters of small, fragrant flowers that range in color from pink to white. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub, reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and containers.

Due to its ornamental value and ease of cultivation, it is a popular choice among gardeners.
