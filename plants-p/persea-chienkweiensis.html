Persea chienkweiensis

Persea chienkweiensis, commonly known as the Chienkwei persimmon, is a species of flowering plant in the family Lauraceae.

It is native to southern China and is characterized by its broad, glossy leaves and fragrant flowers. The plant produces edible fruit that is similar to the common persimmon but is less widely cultivated. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and valuable species in botanical studies.
