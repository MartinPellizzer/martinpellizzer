Passiflora warmingii subsp. chacoensis

Passiflora warmingii subsp.

chacoensis, commonly known as the Chaco passionflower, is a climbing vine native to South America, particularly found in the Chaco region of Paraguay and northern Argentina. It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that feature a complex arrangement of petals, sepals, and bracts, often in shades of red, yellow, and white.

The plant produces edible berries that are typically yellow or orange when mature, containing a sweet, aromatic pulp. Its leaves are large, heart-shaped, and have a velvety texture, while its stems are woody and covered in small, hooked spines.

This species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in tropical and subtropical gardens.
