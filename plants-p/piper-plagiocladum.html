Piper plagiocladum

Piper plagiocladum, commonly known as the slender pepper vine, is a climbing shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many species of pepper plants. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. Its small, greenish-yellow flowers are clustered in spikes and are followed by small, berry-like fruits.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has some traditional medicinal uses.
