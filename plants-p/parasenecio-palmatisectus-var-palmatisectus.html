Parasenecio palmatisectus var. palmatisectus

Parasenecio palmatisectus var. palmatisectus, commonly known as the palmate-leaved blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has palmately lobed leaves, giving it a distinctive, almost fern-like appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
