Piper rufinerve

Piper rufinerve, commonly known as the red-veined pepper plant, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Central and South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its distinctive reddish veins on the leaves, which give it its common name. It produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that develop into elongated, cylindrical fruits.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
