Pterocarpus lucidus

Pterocarpus lucidus, commonly known as the golden rain tree, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, light-colored bark and its distinctive, fern-like leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by flat, winged seeds. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

The tree thrives in warm, humid climates and is adaptable to various soil types.
