Prunus cerasifera var. georgica

Prunus cerasifera var. georgica, commonly known as the Georgian cherry, is a variant of the common cherry tree.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of 3 to 5 meters. The plant features simple, ovate leaves with serrated edges and produces small, white flowers in early spring. Its fruits are small, dark red cherries that ripen in summer.

The plant is native to the Caucasus region and is valued for its ornamental appeal and edible fruits.
