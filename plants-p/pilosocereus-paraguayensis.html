Pilosocereus paraguayensis

Pilosocereus paraguayensis, commonly known as the Paraguay night-blooming cactus, is a species of cactus native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems covered in white, woolly spines. The plant produces large, fragrant, night-blooming flowers that are typically white or pale yellow. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, requiring well-drained soil and minimal water.

Due to its unique appearance and blooming habits, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.
