Penstemon × parishii

Penstemon × parishii, commonly known as the Parry's beardtongue, is a hybrid species native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, branched stems and clusters of tubular, two-lipped flowers that range in color from pink to purple. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly habitats, often in dry, sunny environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and hairy, contributing to its drought tolerance.

Penstemon × parishii is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
