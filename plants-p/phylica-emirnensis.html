Phylica emirnensis

Phylica emirnensis, commonly known as the Emirnensis bottlebrush, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, cylindrical flower clusters that resemble bottle brushes, typically ranging in color from red to orange. The plant produces small, leathery leaves that are arranged in whorls around the stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for coastal and sandy environments.

Phylica emirnensis is often used in ornamental horticulture due to its striking appearance and drought tolerance.
