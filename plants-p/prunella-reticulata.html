Prunella reticulata

Prunella reticulata, commonly known as the nettleleaf bluebell, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia, including China and Japan.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its hairy, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and woodland edges. Its flowers are tubular and attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Prunella reticulata is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
