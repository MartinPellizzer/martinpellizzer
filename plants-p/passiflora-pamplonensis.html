Passiflora pamplonensis

Passiflora pamplonensis, commonly known as the Pamplona passionflower, is a climbing vine native to South America, particularly found in Ecuador and Colombia.

It belongs to the Passifloraceae family and is characterized by its striking, complex flowers with five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments. The plant produces edible berries that are typically yellow or orange when ripe, containing a juicy, aromatic pulp. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal potential, it is sometimes cultivated for horticultural purposes.
