Phyllodes affinis

Phyllodes affinis, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its narrow, needle-like phyllodes, which are modified leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in spikes, typically blooming in spring. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in sandy or rocky soils.

Phyllodes affinis is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.
