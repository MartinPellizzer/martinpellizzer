Pancheria pyrifolia

Pancheria pyrifolia, commonly known as the flame tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, fiery red flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. The plant has palmate leaves that are deeply lobed, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Pancheria pyrifolia is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its vibrant floral display.
