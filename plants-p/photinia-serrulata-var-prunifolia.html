Photinia serrulata var. prunifolia

Photinia serrulata var. prunifolia, commonly known as the glossy leaf photinia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green, glossy foliage that turns red in the fall. The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers in clusters, followed by red, fleshy fruits that are edible for birds. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping for its vibrant seasonal color changes.
