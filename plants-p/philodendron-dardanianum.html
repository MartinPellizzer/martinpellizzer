Philodendron dardanianum

Philodendron dardanianum, commonly known as the Dardanian Philodendron, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant typically has a climbing habit, with aerial roots that help it attach to supports as it grows. Its foliage is often a deep green with a waxy texture, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated as a houseplant or in shaded garden environments.
