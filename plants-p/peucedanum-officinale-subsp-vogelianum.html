Peucedanum officinale subsp. vogelianum

Peucedanum officinale subsp.

vogelianum, commonly known as Vogel's angelica, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It belongs to the Apiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic foliage and umbellate inflorescences.

The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with compound leaves that are finely divided and emit a strong, spicy scent. Its flowers are small and white to greenish in color, arranged in complex umbels, and it produces small, dry fruits known as schizocarps.

The plant is valued for its medicinal properties, particularly in traditional medicine for its digestive and anti-inflammatory effects.
