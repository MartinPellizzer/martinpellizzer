Pandanus bidens

Pandanus bidens, commonly known as the narrow-leaf pandanus, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its tall, slender leaves that are usually narrow and pointed, forming a rosette at the base. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically grouped in clusters, followed by spiky, fibrous fruits. Its roots are fibrous and often used in traditional crafts and construction.

The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found near coastal areas or in wetlands.
