Phalaenopsis serpentilingua

Phalaenopsis serpentilingua, commonly known as the serpent tongue orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its long, narrow leaves and striking, lance-shaped flowers that resemble a serpent's tongue. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its flowers bloom in shades of white, pink, and purple, often with a distinctive coloration pattern.

This orchid is relatively easy to care for, making it a popular choice for indoor cultivation.
