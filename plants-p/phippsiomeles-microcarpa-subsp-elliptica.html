Phippsiomeles microcarpa subsp. elliptica

Phippsiomeles microcarpa subsp.

elliptica, commonly known as the narrow-leaf piper, is a member of the Piperaceae family. It is a small shrub characterized by its elliptical, narrow leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stems.

The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers clustered in spikes, which are followed by small, berry-like fruits. It is native to tropical regions of South America and thrives in humid, shaded environments.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for ecological studies and potential medicinal research.
