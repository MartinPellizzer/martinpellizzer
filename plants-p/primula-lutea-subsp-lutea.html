Primula lutea subsp. lutea

Primula lutea subsp.

lutea, commonly known as the yellow primrose, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers, which typically bloom in early spring.

The plant has a rosette of basal leaves and produces erect flowering stems. Its flowers are usually solitary and have five petals, with a distinctive yellow corolla.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or meadow habitats.
