Pisum arvense var. ramulare

Pisum arvense var. ramulare, commonly known as the ramose field pea, is a variant of the field pea native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a herbaceous annual plant characterized by its slender stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that develop into pods containing several seeds. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in agricultural fields and along roadsides.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and traditional farming practices.
