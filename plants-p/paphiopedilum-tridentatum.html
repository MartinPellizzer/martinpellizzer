Paphiopedilum tridentatum

Paphiopedilum tridentatum, commonly known as the three-toothed lady's slipper, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique, intricate flower structure, featuring a pouch-like lip that is often adorned with intricate patterns. The plant typically grows as a terrestrial or epiphytic orchid, thriving in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are usually broad and strap-like, and it produces clusters of fragrant flowers during the spring or early summer.

This orchid is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated for horticultural purposes.
