Phragmipedium × narinense

Phragmipedium × narinense, commonly known as the Narinense Orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to the cloud forests of Colombia and Ecuador.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that typically feature a vibrant yellow lip and a complex, intricate structure. The plant is epiphytic, meaning it grows on the surfaces of trees without deriving nutrients from them. Its leaves are broad and strap-like, often arranged in a rosette pattern to maximize light absorption.

This orchid is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by enthusiasts for its beauty and unique floral morphology.
