Panicum serratum var. gossypinum

Panicum serratum var. gossypinum, commonly known as crimson foxtail, is a grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, upright growth habit and distinctive reddish-purple inflorescences. The plant produces small, spike-like clusters of bristly seeds that are dispersed by wind. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for erosion control and landscape use.
