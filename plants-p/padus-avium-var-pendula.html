Padus avium var. pendula

Padus avium var. pendula, commonly known as the weeping cherry or weeping almond, is a cultivar of the common cherry tree.

It is characterized by its drooping branches that create a cascading effect, giving it a distinctive and ornamental appearance. The plant produces small, white or pink flowers in early spring, followed by edible fruits that are similar to cherries. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil and full sun for optimal growth.

This variety is often planted in gardens for its aesthetic appeal and seasonal beauty.
