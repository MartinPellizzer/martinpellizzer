Potentilla hirta var. atlantica

Potentilla hirta var. atlantica, commonly known as the Atlantic cinquefoil, is a low-growing perennial shrub native to the coastal regions of Europe and North Africa.

It features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, typically from June to July. The plant has hairy, woody stems and pinnately divided leaves with serrated edges. It thrives in well-drained, sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in coastal dunes and rocky coastal habitats.

This variety is notable for its hardiness and ability to tolerate salt spray and strong winds.
