Potentilla recta taurica

Potentilla recta taurica, commonly known as the Tauric cinquefoil, is a perennial flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in sunny environments. Its leaves are compound, consisting of five elliptical leaflets, and it produces small, berry-like fruits.

Potentilla recta taurica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its hardiness and ability to attract pollinators.
