Pentacalia rex

Pentacalia rex, commonly known as the royal pentacalia, is a striking member of the Campanulaceae family native to the southeastern United States.

This perennial herb features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range from white to deep purple, often with intricate patterns. Its foliage consists of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Due to its ornamental value and rarity, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
