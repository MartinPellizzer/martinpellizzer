Piper elongatum var. cordulatum

Piper elongatum var. cordulatum, commonly known as the warty pepper or cordulate pepper, is a tropical vine native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, warty, and sometimes hairy stems, which give it its common name. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by elongated, cylindrical fruits that turn red when mature. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly rough texture.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is occasionally used in traditional medicine.
