Panicum guadaloupense

Panicum guadaloupense, commonly known as Guadaloupe grass, is a species of grass native to the Caribbean, particularly the island of Guadalupe.

It is a perennial plant characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, wiry stems. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, two-lobed florets. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, with a bluish-green coloration.

Panicum guadaloupense is adapted to coastal and sandy environments, making it a hardy and resilient species in tropical and subtropical regions.
