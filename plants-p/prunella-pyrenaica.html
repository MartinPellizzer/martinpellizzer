Prunella pyrenaica

Prunella pyrenaica, commonly known as the Pyrenean dead-nettle, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or grassy habitats at elevations ranging from 500 to 2,500 meters. Its small, two-lipped flowers are usually purple or blue, and it produces small, nut-like fruits.

Prunella pyrenaica is often found in alpine meadows and is adapted to withstand harsh climatic conditions.
