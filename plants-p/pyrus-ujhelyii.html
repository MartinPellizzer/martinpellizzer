Pyrus ujhelyii

Pyrus ujhelyii, commonly known as the Hungarian pear, is a deciduous tree native to Hungary and parts of Europe.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and broad, ovate leaves. The tree produces small, yellow-green flowers in early spring, followed by edible, sweet fruits that ripen in late summer.

Pyrus ujhelyii is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical significance in traditional horticulture.
