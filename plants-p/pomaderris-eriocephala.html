Pomaderris eriocephala

Pomaderris eriocephala, commonly known as silky pomaderris, is a shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its finely hairy, silver-gray foliage that gives it a soft, velvety appearance. The plant produces small, white flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in spring. Its leaves are opposite and elliptical, often with a slightly wavy margin.

Pomaderris eriocephala is adapted to well-drained soils and is often found in open woodland or heathland environments.
