Pseuderanthemum cinnabarinum

Pseuderanthemum cinnabarinum, commonly known as the red-flowered pseuderanthemum, is a species of flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking red-orange flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy surface, and its stems are often reddish in color. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
