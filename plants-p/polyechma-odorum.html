Polyechma odorum

Polyechma odorum, commonly known as the fragrant mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, yellow to orange flowers in clusters. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
