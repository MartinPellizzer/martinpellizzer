Pfaffia luzuliflora var. paniculata

Pfaffia luzuliflora var. paniculata, commonly known as the purple passionflower, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking violet-blue flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and a deep green color. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, requiring well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

Its botanical significance lies in its unique floral structure and potential medicinal properties.
