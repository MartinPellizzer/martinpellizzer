Potamogeton caudatus

Potamogeton caudatus, commonly known as the long-stemmed pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in North America.

It has long, narrow leaves that are typically arranged in whorls around the stem, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often submerged. This plant plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms. It thrives in shallow, slow-moving waters and is often found in marshes and ponds.

Its presence can indicate good water quality due to its sensitivity to pollution.
