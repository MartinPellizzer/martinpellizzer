Potentilla utahensis

Potentilla utahensis, commonly known as Utah cinquefoil, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit with multiple stems. The plant produces showy, yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically compound, consisting of five leaflets, and it thrives in well-drained soils in full sun.

Utah cinquefoil is often found in arid and semi-arid environments, making it a hardy and drought-tolerant species.
