Polygala vulgaris subvar. albida

Polygala vulgaris subvar. albida, commonly known as the white milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Western Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. The plant features slender, branching stems with small, pale green to white leaves. Its flowers are usually arranged in clusters and are characterized by their distinctive, spurred petals.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in poor soils.
