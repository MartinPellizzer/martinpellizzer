Peperomia ticunhuayana

Peperomia ticunhuayana, commonly known as the Andean peperomia, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Andes region of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and small, ovate leaves. The plant produces clusters of tiny, white to pale green flowers, which are often hidden among the foliage. It thrives in shaded, humid environments and is often found in rocky or forested areas.

Peperomia ticunhuayana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated as a houseplant.
