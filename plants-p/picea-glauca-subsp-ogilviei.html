Picea glauca subsp. ogilviei

Picea glauca subsp.

ogilviei, commonly known as the White Spruce, is a subspecies of the white spruce tree native to North America. It is characterized by its slender, conical shape and blue-green needles that are slightly twisted.

The cones are small and typically hang downward, containing winged seeds. This subspecies is adapted to cold climates and is often found in boreal forests and mountainous regions.

It plays an important role in ecological systems, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
