Paraglycine laotica

Paraglycine laotica, commonly known as the Laos ghost orchid, is a rare and enigmatic orchid species native to the mountainous regions of Laos.

It is characterized by its delicate, translucent petals and a unique floral structure that resembles a ghostly apparition, hence its common name. This plant thrives in humid, shaded forest environments, often growing epiphytically on the bark of trees. Its flowers, which bloom in late spring, are typically white or pale pink, with intricate patterns that enhance its ethereal appearance.

Due to habitat loss and limited distribution, Paraglycine laotica is considered a critically endangered species.
