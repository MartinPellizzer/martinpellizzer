Persicaria kotoshoensis

Persicaria kotoshoensis, commonly known as Kotosho morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, which includes species known for their distinctive leaf shapes and colorful flowers. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a waxy surface and produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of blue, purple, and white. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and stream banks.

Persicaria kotoshoensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
