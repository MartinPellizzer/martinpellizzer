Pseudoliparis inexspectata

Pseudoliparis inexspectata, commonly known as the deep-sea sea pen, is a unique colonial cnidarian found in the abyssal zones of the Atlantic Ocean.

It belongs to the order Pennatulacea and is characterized by its delicate, feather-like polyps that extend from a calcareous stem. This species thrives in deep waters, often at depths exceeding 2,000 meters, where it forms intricate, branching structures. Its survival depends on symbiotic relationships with specific bacteria that provide it with nutrients from the surrounding environment.

Due to its habitat, it is rarely studied, making it one of the least understood deep-sea organisms.
