Polygonum balansae var. induratum

Polygonum balansae var. induratum, commonly known as the hard-leaved smartweed, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, disturbed soils such as roadsides, ditches, and wetlands. The plant features alternate, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense, waxy coating, giving them a leathery texture. Its flowers are small, clustered in dense spikes, and are usually pink to red in color.

This variety is characterized by its robust growth and ability to thrive in a variety of wetland environments.
