Plantago cynopsis

Plantago cynopsis, commonly known as common plantain, is a perennial herb native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It features a rosette of broad, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish flowers in a spike-like inflorescence, which are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous tiny seeds. It is adapted to grow in a variety of habitats, including roadsides, fields, and disturbed soils.

The leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their soothing and healing properties.
