Pycnandra decandra

Pycnandra decandra, commonly known as the yellow mangrove, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its smooth, pale bark and dense, dark green foliage. The plant has small, elliptical leaves and produces yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters. It is often found growing in brackish or saline water, adapting to coastal environments.

Its root system helps stabilize shorelines and provides habitat for various marine and terrestrial organisms.
