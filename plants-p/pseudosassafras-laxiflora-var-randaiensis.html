Pseudosassafras laxiflora var. randaiensis

Pseudosassafras laxiflora var. randaiensis, commonly known as the Japanese pseudosassafras, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a reddish or purplish hue during the fall.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
