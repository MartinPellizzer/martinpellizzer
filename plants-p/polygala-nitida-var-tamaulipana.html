Polygala nitida var. tamaulipana

Polygala nitida var. tamaulipana, commonly known as the Tamaulipan milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of northeastern Mexico.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, delicate flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to drought conditions. Its leaves are often narrow and grayish-green, helping to reduce water loss.

It plays an important ecological role in its native habitat by providing nectar for pollinators.
