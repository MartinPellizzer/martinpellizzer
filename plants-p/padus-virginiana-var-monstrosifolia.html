Padus virginiana var. monstrosifolia

Padus virginiana var. monstrosifolia, commonly known as the showy mountain-ash, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its striking, large, white flowers that appear in clusters during late spring. The plant produces bright red berries that are an important food source for birds and wildlife. Its leaves are typically lobed and have a distinctive, often variegated appearance.

This variety is valued in gardens for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits.
