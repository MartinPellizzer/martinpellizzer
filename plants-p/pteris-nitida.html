Pteris nitida

Pteris nitida, commonly known as the Japanese water fern, is a species of fern native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that give it a lacy appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine, and it is valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture.

Pteris nitida reproduces via spores and is a member of the family Pteridaceae.
