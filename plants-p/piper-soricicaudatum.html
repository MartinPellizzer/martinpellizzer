Piper soricicaudatum

Piper soricicaudatum, commonly known as the mouse-tailed pepper, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil and Peru. The plant is characterized by its slender, climbing vines and small, elongated leaves. Its fruits are berries that contain aromatic seeds used in traditional medicine.

The plant's unique morphology and ecological adaptations make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
