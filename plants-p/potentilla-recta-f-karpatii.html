Potentilla recta f. karpatii

Potentilla recta f. karpatii, commonly known as the Karpatian cinquefoil, is a perennial shrub native to the Carpathian region.

It features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has slender, upright stems covered in fine, soft hairs, and its leaves are typically trifoliate, with three oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or grassy habitats.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and hardiness in temperate climates.
