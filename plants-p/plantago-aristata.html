Plantago aristata

Plantago aristata, commonly known as the mountain plantain, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe, Asia, and North America.

It is characterized by its rosette of broad, lance-shaped leaves covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in a dense spike, which bloom in late summer to early autumn. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or alpine environments.

The plant is noted for its medicinal properties, with historical use in treating wounds and respiratory ailments.
