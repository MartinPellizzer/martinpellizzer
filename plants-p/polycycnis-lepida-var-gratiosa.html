Polycycnis lepida var. gratiosa

Polycycnis lepida var. gratiosa, commonly known as the graceful water plant, is a striking aquatic plant native to certain regions of Asia.

It features delicate, arrowhead-shaped leaves that float on the water's surface, often with a waxy texture that aids in water retention. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, adding a subtle ornamental value to ponds and wetlands. It thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments with ample sunlight.

Its rapid growth makes it both a valuable addition to aquatic gardens and a potential invasive species if not managed carefully.
