Penstemon havardii

Penstemon havardii, commonly known as the Havard's beardtongue, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, upright stems and clusters of tubular flowers that range in color from purple to pink. The plant produces showy, spurred flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy choice for rock gardens and xeriscapes.

Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are evergreen in mild climates.
