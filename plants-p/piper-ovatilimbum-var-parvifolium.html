Piper ovatilimbum var. parvifolium

Piper ovatilimbum var. parvifolium, commonly known as the small-leaved pepper plant, is a variant of the Piper ovatilimbum species native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is a climbing vine characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and small, greenish-white flowers. The plant produces small, round berries that turn from green to yellow or red as they mature. It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species known for their aromatic properties.

This variety is valued for its unique foliage and potential medicinal uses in traditional herbal practices.
