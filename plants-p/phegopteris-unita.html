Phegopteris unita

Phegopteris unita, commonly known as the common water fern, is a species of fern native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its delicate, bipinnate fronds that are typically light green and have a feathery appearance. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments such as stream banks and wetlands. Its reproductive structures, including sori, are located on the underside of the fronds.

Phegopteris unita plays an important ecological role in maintaining the biodiversity of its habitat.
