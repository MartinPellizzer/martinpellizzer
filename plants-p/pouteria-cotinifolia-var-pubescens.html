Pouteria cotinifolia var. pubescens

Pouteria cotinifolia var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy sycamore, is a species native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is a large, deciduous tree that can grow up to 30 meters tall, characterized by its rough, fissured bark and broad, palmate leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by edible, fleshy fruits that are typically yellow or orange in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The species is valued for its timber and has some traditional medicinal uses among indigenous communities.
