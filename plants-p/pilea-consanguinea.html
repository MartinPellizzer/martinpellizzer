Pilea consanguinea

Pilea consanguinea, commonly known as the Chinese money plant, is a species of flowering plant in the family Araceae.

It is native to southern China, Taiwan, and parts of Southeast Asia. The plant is characterized by its rosette of heart-shaped, waxy leaves that are typically bright green with a silvery underside. It produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers that are borne on a spadix, typical of the Araceae family.

Pilea consanguinea is a popular houseplant due to its attractive foliage and ease of care.
