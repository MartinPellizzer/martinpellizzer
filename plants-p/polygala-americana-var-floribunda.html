Polygala americana var. floribunda

Polygala americana var. floribunda, commonly known as the American milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, wooded areas and has slender, branched stems that can reach up to 2 feet in height. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters, which are often arranged in spicate inflorescences. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture.

The species is valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and its potential medicinal properties.
