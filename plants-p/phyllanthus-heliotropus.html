Phyllanthus heliotropus

Phyllanthus heliotropus, commonly known as the sacred plant of the ancient Maya, is a shrub native to Central America and parts of the Caribbean.

It is characterized by its small, greenish-white flowers and oval-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. The plant is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, thriving in well-drained soils and full sunlight. It has been traditionally used in herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.

Its botanical significance is further enhanced by its role in cultural and religious practices among indigenous communities.
