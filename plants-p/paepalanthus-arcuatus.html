Paepalanthus arcuatus

Paepalanthus arcuatus, commonly known as the curved-leaf cholla, is a species of cactus native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems that branch out into clusters of small, spine-tipped pads. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, adding a striking visual contrast to its green, spiny structure. It is well adapted to drought conditions, storing water in its thick, fleshy stems.

The species is often found in rocky soils and is a common sight in desert landscapes.
