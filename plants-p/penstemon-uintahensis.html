Penstemon uintahensis

Penstemon uintahensis, commonly known as Uinta penstemon, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy tubular flowers. The plant typically blooms in late summer, producing vibrant purple or pink flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in rocky soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine environments.

Uinta penstemon is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
