Psilanthus cochinchinensis

Psilanthus cochinchinensis, commonly known as the Chinese ironwood or Vietnamese ironwood, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Rhamnaceae and is characterized by its dense, hard wood that is highly valued for its durability. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical in shape and have a smooth, glossy surface. It produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by spherical, woody fruits containing several seeds.

Due to its ecological importance and limited natural range, it is also considered a threatened species in some regions.
