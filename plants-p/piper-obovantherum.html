Piper obovantherum

Piper obovantherum, commonly known as the broad-leafed pepper plant, is a member of the Piperaceae family, native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves that are often glossy and dark green in color. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by elongated, cylindrical fruits that turn from green to yellow as they mature. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties.

The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in the understory of tropical rainforests.
