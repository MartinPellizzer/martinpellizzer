Psoralea citrodora

Psoralea citrodora, commonly known as the lemon scented psoralea, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is a small shrub that typically grows in sandy or rocky soils. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring. Its leaves are often aromatic, releasing a citrus-like scent when crushed.

It is often used in traditional medicine and is also valued for its ornamental appeal.
