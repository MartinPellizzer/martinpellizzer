Platanthera elegans

Platanthera elegans, commonly known as the elegant lady's tresses, is a terrestrial orchid native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its long, spirally twisted flower spike that bears numerous small, greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and meadows. It has a rhizomatous root system and produces a single, narrow, grass-like leaf.

The species is notable for its delicate appearance and ecological importance as a pollinator attractant.
