Pachypodium champenoisianum

Pachypodium champenoisianum, commonly known as the Madagascar moon cactus, is a succulent plant native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its swollen, bottle-shaped stem and sparse, spiny leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, white, star-shaped flowers during the dry season. It thrives in arid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Its unique appearance makes it a popular ornamental plant in dry climates.
