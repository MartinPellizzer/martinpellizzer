Phaca nana

Phaca nana, commonly known as the dwarf phaca, is a small herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. The plant features delicate, rounded leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence. Its flowers are small, white or pale blue, and appear in clusters during the spring and early summer.

Phaca nana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
