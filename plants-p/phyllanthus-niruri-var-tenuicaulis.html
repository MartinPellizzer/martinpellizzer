Phyllanthus niruri var. tenuicaulis

Phyllanthus niruri var. tenuicaulis, commonly known as stonebreaker or wild coffee, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Americas.

It is a small shrub characterized by its simple, opposite leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small yellow flowers arranged in clusters, followed by small, round fruits containing single seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and waste areas.

Due to its medicinal properties, it is widely used in traditional medicine for various health purposes.
