Polygala mascatensis

Polygala mascatensis, commonly known as the Arabian milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the Arabian Peninsula.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its flowers are usually pale green or yellowish, with a distinctive spurred structure.

Polygala mascatensis is often found in rocky areas and sandy soils, contributing to the floral diversity of its native habitat.
