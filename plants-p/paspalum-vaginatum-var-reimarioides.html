Paspalum vaginatum var. reimarioides

Paspalum vaginatum var. reimarioides, commonly known as the Reimarioides paspalum, is a grass species native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in wetland or marshy environments, often found in coastal areas.

Its adaptability to saline and brackish conditions makes it an important species in tidal ecosystems.
