Pentas lanceolata var. leucaster

Pentas lanceolata var. leucaster, commonly known as the white pentas, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its showy, five-petaled flowers that are typically white or pale pink in color. The leaves are lance-shaped and arranged oppositely along the stem, giving the plant a neat and compact appearance. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

This variety is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as butterflies and bees.
