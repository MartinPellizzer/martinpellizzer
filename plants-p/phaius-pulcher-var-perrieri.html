Phaius pulcher var. perrieri

Phaius pulcher var. perrieri, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a striking tropical orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow flowers and broad, lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in wet, shaded environments, such as marshes and swampy areas. Its inflorescence consists of a raceme of showy, fragrant blooms that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and greenhouses.
