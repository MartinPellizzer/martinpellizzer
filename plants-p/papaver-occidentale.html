Papaver occidentale

Papaver occidentale, commonly known as the Western poppy, is a flowering plant native to western North America.

It is characterized by its striking, cup-shaped petals that range in color from red to orange and yellow. The plant typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its flowers bloom in the spring and are followed by seed pods that split open to release seeds.

The Western poppy is valued for its ornamental beauty and is also studied for its potential medicinal properties.
