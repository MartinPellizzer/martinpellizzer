Phlomis pichleri

Phlomis pichleri, commonly known as Pichler's mullein, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its tall, erect stem and large, fuzzy, grey-green leaves. The plant produces showy, yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy species suitable for rocky or dry garden environments.

Phlomis pichleri is often used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and soothing properties.
