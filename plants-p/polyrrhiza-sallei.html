Polyrrhiza sallei

Polyrrhiza sallei, commonly known as the sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in dune environments. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Polyrrhiza sallei is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize coastal soils.
