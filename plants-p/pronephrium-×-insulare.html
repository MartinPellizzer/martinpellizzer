Pronephrium × insulare

Pronephrium × insulare, commonly known as the hybrid mangrove, is a naturally occurring hybrid between Pronephrium and Insulare species.

It is characterized by its ability to thrive in brackish water environments, showcasing adaptability to fluctuating salinity levels. The plant exhibits typical mangrove traits such as aerial roots and salt-excreting leaves, which aid in survival in coastal zones. Its botanical structure includes a woody stem with dense, fibrous roots that anchor it firmly in tidal soils.

This hybrid plays a significant ecological role in stabilizing shorelines and providing habitat for various marine and terrestrial organisms.
