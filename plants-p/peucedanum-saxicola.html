Peucedanum saxicola

Peucedanum saxicola, commonly known as the rock anise, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in arid to semi-arid climates. The plant features slender, branched stems and small, clustered leaves with a distinctive pinnate structure. Its flowers are small and pale blue to white, arranged in umbels, and it produces small, oval-shaped fruits.

The plant is valued for its aromatic seeds, which have been used in traditional medicine and as a spice.
