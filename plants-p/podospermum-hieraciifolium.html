Podospermum hieraciifolium

Podospermum hieraciifolium, commonly known as the creeping pimpernel, is a perennial herb native to South America.

It features small, opposite, ovate leaves with hairy surfaces and a distinctive, spurred calyx. The flowers are typically pink or purple, with five petals and a prominent central spur. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The plant is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
