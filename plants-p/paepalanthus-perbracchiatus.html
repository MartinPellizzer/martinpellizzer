Paepalanthus perbracchiatus

Paepalanthus perbracchiatus, commonly known as the Brazilian sunflower, is a species of flowering plant native to Brazil.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features a robust, branching stem with narrow, linear leaves. Its showy yellow flowers bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands and savannas.
