Polygonum posumbu

Polygonum posumbu, commonly known as the red root plant, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, which includes species known for their distinctive arrow-shaped leaves. The plant is characterized by its upright stems and clusters of small, pinkish flowers that bloom in summer. Its roots are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory properties.

Polygonum posumbu thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.
