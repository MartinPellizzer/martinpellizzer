Pterocarpus magnicarpus

Pterocarpus magnicarpus, commonly known as the giant flame tree, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, palmate leaves and showy, orange-red flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces woody, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Its unique botanical features make it a notable species in both ecological and ornamental contexts.
