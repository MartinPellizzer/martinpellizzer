Phreatia pisifera

Phreatia pisifera, commonly known as the broad-leafed orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and swampy areas. The plant features a pseudobulb that stores water and nutrients, supporting its growth in humid conditions. Its leaves are broad and strap-like, and it produces fragrant, showy flowers in shades of white and yellow.

Phreatia pisifera is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical gardens.
