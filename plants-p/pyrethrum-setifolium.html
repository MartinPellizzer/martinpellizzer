Pyrethrum setifolium

Pyrethrum setifolium, commonly known as the woolly pyrethrum, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy foliage and clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family and is known for its ornamental value in gardens. Its flowers produce pyrethrin, a natural insecticide used in organic pest control.

Pyrethrum setifolium prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for drought-tolerant landscaping.
