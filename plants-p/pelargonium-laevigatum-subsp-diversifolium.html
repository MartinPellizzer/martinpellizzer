Pelargonium laevigatum subsp. diversifolium

Pelargonium laevigatum subsp.

diversifolium, commonly known as the variegated geranium, is a herbaceous perennial native to South Africa. It features strikingly variegated foliage with shades of white, yellow, and green, which adds ornamental value to gardens.

The plant produces clusters of small, pink to red flowers during the spring and summer seasons. Its leaves are typically ovate to cordate in shape and have a slightly hairy texture.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it a versatile addition to rock gardens and containers.
