Plectranthus chevalieri

Plectranthus chevalieri, commonly known as the Cape verbena, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a member of the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves and clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is often cultivated in Mediterranean climates.
