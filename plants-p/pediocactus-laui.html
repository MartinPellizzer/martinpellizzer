Pediocactus laui

Pediocactus laui, commonly known as the Laui's cactus, is a species of cactus native to the southwestern United States and northwestern Mexico.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny stems that form a compact, globular shape. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, which are typically short-lived but attract pollinators such as bees and moths. Pediocactus laui is adapted to arid environments, storing water in its fleshy stems and having a slow growth rate.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and resilience.
