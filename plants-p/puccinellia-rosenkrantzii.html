Puccinellia rosenkrantzii

Puccinellia rosenkrantzii, commonly known as the Rosenkrantz's spikegrass, is a perennial grass species native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to drought conditions. Its leaves are narrow and often have a bluish-gray hue, which helps reduce water loss.

Puccinellia rosenkrantzii plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native habitats and is of interest in ecological restoration efforts.
