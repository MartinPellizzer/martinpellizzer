Podophyllum glaucescens

Podophyllum glaucescens, commonly known as bluebells, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the eastern United States.

It features a basal rosette of lance-shaped, blue-green leaves that are covered in a waxy coating. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped, pale blue to purple flowers in early spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade.

Podophyllum glaucescens is often found in woodland areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
