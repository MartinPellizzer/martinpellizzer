Polemonium caeruleum subsp. vulgare

Polemonium caeruleum subsp.

vulgare, commonly known as bluebells, is a herbaceous perennial native to Europe. It features lance-shaped leaves that are typically bluish-green in color.

The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped, sky-blue flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors.

Its foliage and flowers are often used in ornamental gardening due to their aesthetic appeal.
