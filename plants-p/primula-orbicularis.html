Primula orbicularis

Primula orbicularis, commonly known as the broad-leaf primrose, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of China.

It is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves and clusters of fragrant, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and thrives in cool climates. It is a member of the Primulaceae family, which includes many other ornamental and medicinal plants.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
