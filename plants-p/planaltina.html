Planaltina

Planaltina, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its small, bright red berries and dense, evergreen foliage. The plant thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in coastal areas and disturbed soils. Its botanical name is Schinus terebinthifolius, and it belongs to the Anacardiaceae family.

Planaltina is known for its aromatic leaves and its ability to produce copious amounts of fruit.
