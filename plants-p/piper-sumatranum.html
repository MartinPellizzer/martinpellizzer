Piper sumatranum

Piper sumatranum, commonly known as Java pepper or long pepper, is a climbing vine native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, peppercorn-like fruits. The plant produces berries that turn from green to red when mature, containing aromatic seeds used in traditional medicine and cuisine. Its botanical features include opposite leaf arrangement and a distinctive, pungent aroma.

Piper sumatranum thrives in tropical climates with high humidity and partial shade.
