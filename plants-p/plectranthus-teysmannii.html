Plectranthus teysmannii

Plectranthus teysmannii, commonly known as the African violet, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to South Africa.

It features opposite, ovate to lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine, soft hairs. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically purple or violet in color, arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers bright, indirect light.

Plectranthus teysmannii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly grown as a houseplant.
