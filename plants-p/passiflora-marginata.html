Passiflora marginata

Passiflora marginata, commonly known as the Brazilian passionflower, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful flowers that feature five sepals, five petals, and a crown-like structure of filaments. The plant produces edible berries that are rich in antioxidants and have a tropical flavor. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped with a waxy surface and serrated edges.

Passiflora marginata is widely cultivated for ornamental purposes and in some regions for its fruit.
