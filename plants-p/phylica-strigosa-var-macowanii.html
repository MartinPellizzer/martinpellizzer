Phylica strigosa var. macowanii

Phylica strigosa var. macowanii, commonly known as the Cape snowbush, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Proteaceae family and is characterized by its dense, shrub-like growth habit. The plant produces clusters of small, white, fluffy flowers that resemble snow, hence its common name. It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Phylica strigosa var. macowanii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine.
