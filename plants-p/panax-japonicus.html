Panax japonicus

Panax japonicus, commonly known as Japanese ginseng, is a perennial plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Araliaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, tuberous roots that are rich in bioactive compounds. The plant typically grows in shaded, forested environments and has lance-shaped leaves with a smooth texture. Its flowering stems bear small, greenish-white flowers followed by red berries.

Due to its medicinal properties, it is highly valued in traditional herbal medicine.
