Potentilla pilosissima

Potentilla pilosissima, commonly known as the silky cinquefoil, is a low-growing perennial plant native to North America.

It features finely divided, fern-like leaves that are covered in soft, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, typically from June to August. It thrives in well-drained soils and full to partial sunlight, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or woodland edges.

Due to its ornamental value and hardiness, it is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and blooms.
