Poa rhomboidea

Poa rhomboidea, commonly known as the rhomboid meadowgrass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. The plant has slender, tufted stems and produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences. Its leaves are narrow and often have a bluish-green hue.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
