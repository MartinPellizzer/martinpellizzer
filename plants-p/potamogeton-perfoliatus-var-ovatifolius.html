Potamogeton perfoliatus var. ovatifolius

Potamogeton perfoliatus var. ovatifolius, commonly known as the ovate-leaf pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in North America.

It features slender, ribbon-like leaves that are ovate in shape, often with a distinct midrib, and it typically grows in shallow, slow-moving waters. The plant produces small, green flowers that are clustered in spike-like inflorescences, and it is often found in marshes, lakes, and along the edges of rivers.

Its dense growth helps stabilize sediments and provides habitat for aquatic invertebrates and fish.
