Pyrus kwangsiensis

Pyrus kwangsiensis, commonly known as the Guangxi pear, is a deciduous tree native to southern China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and clusters of small, white flowers. The fruit is a small, edible pear that ripens in the autumn. It is valued for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
