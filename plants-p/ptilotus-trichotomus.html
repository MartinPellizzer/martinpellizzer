Ptilotus trichotomus

Ptilotus trichotomus, commonly known as the three-branched spinifex, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and distinctive, spiky inflorescences. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in sandy soils. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are typically green or yellowish, and it is adapted to withstand harsh climatic conditions.

Ptilotus trichotomus is also known for its use in traditional Indigenous practices and as a source of fiber.
