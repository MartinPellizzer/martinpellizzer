Podocarpus laetus

Podocarpus laetus, commonly known as the silver podocarp, is a slow-growing evergreen conifer native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green foliage and distinctive, scale-like leaves that give it a unique appearance. The plant produces small, cone-like structures that contain its seeds, typical of the Podocarpaceae family. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

Due to its ornamental value, it is also cultivated as a decorative plant in gardens and landscapes.
