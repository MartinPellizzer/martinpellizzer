Pipturus polynesicus var. melanesicus

Pipturus polynesicus var. melanesicus, commonly known as the Melanesian tree fern, is a species native to the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and a crown of feathery fronds that can reach several meters in length. The plant thrives in humid, tropical environments and is often found in coastal or lowland forests. Its rhizomes are fibrous and can be used for various traditional purposes.

The species plays an important role in the local ecosystem, providing habitat and contributing to soil stability.
