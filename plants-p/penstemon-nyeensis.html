Penstemon nyeensis

Penstemon nyeensis, commonly known as the Nye's beardtongue, is a species of flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces showy, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to purple. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Penstemon nyeensis prefers well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its foliage consists of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of hair.
