Panicum commixtum

Panicum commixtum, commonly known as mixed panicgrass, is a grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, wiry stems that often bear a reddish tinge. The plant produces fluffy, spike-like inflorescences that are typically reddish-brown in color, which help in seed dispersal. It thrives in full sun and is often found in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and fields.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes used in native plant gardens.
