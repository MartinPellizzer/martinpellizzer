Pulsatilla vulgaris subsp. germanica

Pulsatilla vulgaris subsp.

germanica, commonly known as the meadow anemone, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia. It features a rosette of soft, hairy, gray-green leaves that form a dense basal cushion.

The plant produces showy, fluffy white or pale yellow flowers with numerous stamens, typically blooming in early spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, woodlands, and along stream banks.

The species is characterized by its unique, short-stemmed inflorescence and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
