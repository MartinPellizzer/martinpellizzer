Phyteuma orbiculare subsp. flexuosum

Phyteuma orbiculare subsp.

flexuosum, commonly known as the flexible globeflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central and Eastern Europe. It is characterized by its rounded, orbicular leaves and showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pink to purple.

The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils in woodland or meadow habitats. It blooms from late spring to early summer, producing clusters of flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
