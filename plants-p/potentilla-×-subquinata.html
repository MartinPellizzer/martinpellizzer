Potentilla × subquinata

Potentilla × subquinata, commonly known as the everlasting or shrub cinquefoil, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and produces clusters of small, showy yellow flowers. The plant is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia and is often found in alpine and subalpine environments. Its foliage consists of narrow, serrated leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs.

Potentilla × subquinata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens for its hardiness and long-lasting blooms.
