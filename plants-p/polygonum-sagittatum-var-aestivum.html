Polygonum sagittatum var. aestivum

Polygonum sagittatum var. aestivum, commonly known as the summer smartweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its arrow-shaped leaves and clusters of small, pink to red flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, ditches, and along stream banks. Its stems are often reddish and hairy, while its leaves are alternate and have a distinctive arrowhead shape.

It serves as a food source for various insects and is sometimes used in wetland restoration projects.
