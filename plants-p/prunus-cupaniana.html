Prunus cupaniana

Prunus cupaniana, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or cupuacu, is a tropical tree native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in the early spring. The fruit, which resembles a large, rough orange berry, contains a single large seed and is rich in antioxidants. The tree can grow up to 20 meters tall and thrives in humid, warm climates with well-drained soil.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat for various wildlife species and contributing to the biodiversity of its native region.
