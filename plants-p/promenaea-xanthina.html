Promenaea xanthina

Promenaea xanthina, commonly known as the yellow promenaea, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Arecaceae family, which includes palms, and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and pinnately compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are arranged in panicle inflorescences, followed by small, round fruits. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in wetlands and along riverbanks.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
