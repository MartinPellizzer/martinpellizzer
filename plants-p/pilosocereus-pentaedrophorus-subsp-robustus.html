Pilosocereus pentaedrophorus subsp. robustus

Pilosocereus pentaedrophorus subsp.

robustus, commonly known as the robustus cactus, is a species native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its tall, columnar stems that can grow up to several meters in height.

The plant produces clusters of white, nocturnal flowers, which bloom during the warmer months. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its striking appearance and hardy nature, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.
