Plagiogyria semicordata subsp. matsumurana

Plagiogyria semicordata subsp.

matsumurana, commonly known as the Japanese marsh helleborine, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern Asia. It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit and showy, greenish-yellow flowers.

The plant typically grows in wet or marshy habitats, thriving in humid and shaded environments. Its leaves are broad, lanceolate, and often have a waxy surface, while its flowers are arranged in a loose inflorescence.

This subspecies is notable for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and its use in traditional herbal medicine.
