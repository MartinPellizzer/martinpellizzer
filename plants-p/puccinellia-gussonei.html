Puccinellia gussonei

Puccinellia gussonei, commonly known as the Gussone's needlegrass, is a perennial grass species native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to harsh environmental conditions. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It is often found in steppe and desert scrub habitats.

Its ecological importance includes stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.
