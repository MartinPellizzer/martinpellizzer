Panicum nudiglume var. majus

Panicum nudiglume var. majus, commonly known as the giant false panicgrass, is a tall grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its long, narrow leaves and panicles that are often loosely arranged. The plant produces small, spikelet-like flowers that are typically green or brown in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as savannas and disturbed areas.

Its botanical features make it a common component of native plant communities in its range.
