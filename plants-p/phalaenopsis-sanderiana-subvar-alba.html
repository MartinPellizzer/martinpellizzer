Phalaenopsis sanderiana subvar. alba

Phalaenopsis sanderiana subvar. alba, commonly known as the white phalaenopsis, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its elongated, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of fragrant, white flowers. The plant typically blooms once a year, producing multiple flower spikes with numerous blossoms. It prefers bright, indirect light and moderate humidity, making it a popular choice for indoor cultivation.

Its elegant appearance and relatively easy care requirements have made it a favored ornamental plant.
