Phyllopodium multifolium

Phyllopodium multifolium, commonly known as the many-leaved fern, is a species characterized by its intricate and delicate fronds.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is native to tropical and subtropical regions. The plant features a rhizomatous growth habit, with long, branching stems that produce numerous small, lance-shaped leaves. Its foliage is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value, and it thrives in moist, shaded environments.

The plant's reproductive structures, including spore-producing sorangia, are typically found on the underside of its fronds.
