Polygonum punctatum var. parvum

Polygonum punctatum var. parvum, commonly known as the small-spotted knotweed, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Polygonaceae family, characterized by its succulent, often hairy stems and leaves. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that appear in dense clusters, typically in late summer. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodland edges and stream banks.

Its leaves are often dotted with small, black spots, giving it its scientific name.
