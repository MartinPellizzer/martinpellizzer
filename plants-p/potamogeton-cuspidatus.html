Potamogeton cuspidatus

Potamogeton cuspidatus, commonly known as the spikegrass, is a species of aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in North America.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems that grow in dense stands in shallow water. The plant produces small, green to reddish flowers that are clustered in spike-like inflorescences. Its leaves are narrow and often have a pointed tip, contributing to its scientific name.

Potamogeton cuspidatus plays a role in stabilizing sediments and providing habitat for aquatic invertebrates.
