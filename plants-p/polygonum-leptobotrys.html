Polygonum leptobotrys

Polygonum leptobotrys, commonly known as slenderleaf knotweed, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, which includes plants known for their distinctive arrow-shaped leaves and clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are narrow and have a smooth, glossy surface, while its small, greenish-white flowers appear in late spring to early summer.

The plant is often found in wetland environments and can spread through rhizomes, making it a persistent species in its native range.
