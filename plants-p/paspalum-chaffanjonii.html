Paspalum chaffanjonii

Paspalum chaffanjonii, commonly known as the Chaffanjonii grass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences, which are typical of the Poaceae family. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
