Pterocarpus lunulatus

Pterocarpus lunulatus, commonly known as the moonwood or moon tree, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its unique, waxy, and often fragrant leaves. The tree produces distinctive yellow or orange flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by long, flat pods containing seeds. Its wood is highly valued for its durability and aesthetic appeal, often used in traditional crafts and furniture.

The plant also plays an important ecological role in its native habitats, providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
