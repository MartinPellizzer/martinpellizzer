Pyrola verticillata

Pyrola verticillata, commonly known as the whorled pine green.

This evergreen perennial is native to the northeastern United States and parts of Canada, typically growing in shaded, moist forest environments. It has a distinctive rosette of oval, dark green leaves that are arranged in whorls around the stem. The plant produces small, bell-shaped white or pale pink flowers in early spring.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in woodland gardens.
