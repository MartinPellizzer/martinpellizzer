Phlox × laeta

Phlox × laeta, commonly known as the garden phlox, is a hybrid perennial flowering plant belonging to the Polemonium family.

It is native to North America and is prized for its showy clusters of fragrant, tubular flowers that bloom in shades of pink, red, and white. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained, fertile soil.

Phlox × laeta is widely cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
