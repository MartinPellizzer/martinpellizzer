Paspalum trinii

Paspalum trinii, commonly known as the trinii grass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences that are typically green to purplish in color. It thrives in moist to wet environments, often found in marshy areas and along riverbanks.

Paspalum trinii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
