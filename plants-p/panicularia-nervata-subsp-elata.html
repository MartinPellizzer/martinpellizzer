Panicularia nervata subsp. elata

Panicularia nervata subsp.

elata, commonly known as the tall panicled bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, panicled inflorescences composed of numerous small, bell-shaped flowers.

The leaves are typically lanceolate and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is occasionally cultivated in gardens in cooler climates.
