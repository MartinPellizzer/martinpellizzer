Polygala kurdica

Polygala kurdica, commonly known as the Kurdish milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of the Middle East and Central Asia.

It belongs to the Polygalaceae family and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, delicate flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are usually narrow and alternate, while its flowers are often arranged in clusters and can vary in color from white to pale pink.

Polygala kurdica is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
