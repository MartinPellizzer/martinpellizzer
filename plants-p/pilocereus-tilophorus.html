Pilocereus tilophorus

Pilocereus tilophorus, commonly known as the night blooming cereus, is a species of cactus native to South America.

It is characterized by its spiny stems and clusters of white, fragrant flowers that open at night. The plant is a member of the Cactaceae family and is adapted to arid environments with minimal water requirements. Its flowers are typically short-lived, lasting only a few hours, and are pollinated by nocturnal insects such as moths.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and unique nocturnal blooming habit.
