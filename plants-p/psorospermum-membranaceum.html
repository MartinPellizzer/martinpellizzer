Psorospermum membranaceum

Psorospermum membranaceum, commonly known as the paperbark tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, pale bark that peels off in thin, papery layers. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a leathery texture. It produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by woody, capsule-like fruits.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
