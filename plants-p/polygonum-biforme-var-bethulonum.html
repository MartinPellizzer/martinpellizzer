Polygonum biforme var. bethulonum

Polygonum biforme var. bethulonum, commonly known as the Bethulonum smartweed, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. The plant features slender, upright stems that are often reddish in color, and its leaves are lance-shaped with a slightly hairy surface. Its small, clustered flowers are usually pink or red, appearing in late summer to early fall.

The species is characterized by its ability to thrive in wet conditions and its distinctive two-lobed leaf structure.
