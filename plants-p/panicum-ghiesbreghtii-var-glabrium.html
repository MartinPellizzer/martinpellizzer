Panicum ghiesbreghtii var. glabrium

Panicum ghiesbreghtii var. glabrium, commonly known as the smooth panicgrass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically hairless, giving it its varietal name "glabrium.". The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands.

Due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and restoration projects.
