Pseudonephelium fumatum

Pseudonephelium fumatum, commonly known as the smoky hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy, cream-colored bracts that resemble petals, giving it a distinctive, smoky appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in humid, warm climates and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Pseudonephelium fumatum is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
