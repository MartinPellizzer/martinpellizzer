Paspalum auriculatum

Paspalum auriculatum, commonly known as the auriculate paspalum, is a perennial grass native to South America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves with auriculate (ear-like) bases. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in wetland environments and is often found in marshes and along the edges of water bodies.

Due to its adaptability, it is sometimes used in restoration projects for stabilizing soil in wetland areas.
