Prunus × donarium f. hakkasan

Prunus × donarium f. hakkasan, commonly known as the Hakka cherry, is a compact, ornamental shrub native to China.

It features small, oval leaves that are dark green and slightly hairy on the underside. The plant produces clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers in early spring, which are followed by bright red, edible berries. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for gardens and landscapes.

Its compact size and attractive flowers make it a popular choice for ornamental purposes.
