Pigea adpressa

Pigea adpressa, commonly known as the flatleaf pigeonwings, is a small, herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and swampy areas. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are usually greenish-white and arranged in clusters.

Pigea adpressa is known for its ability to thrive in wet conditions, making it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
