Persica vulgaris f. nuda

Persica vulgaris f. nuda, commonly known as the naked peach, is a botanical variant of the peach tree characterized by its reduced or absent foliage on the fruiting branches.

This form exhibits a distinctive appearance with fruit clusters that are often exposed to sunlight, leading to a more intense coloration. The plant retains the genetic traits of the common peach but displays unique morphological features, making it a subject of interest in horticultural studies. It is typically found in regions with warm climates and well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated for its visual appeal rather than for fruit production.
