Parinari cardiophylla

Parinari cardiophylla, commonly known as the heart-leaved parinari, is a tropical tree native to parts of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking heart-shaped leaves, which are typically glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by edible, orange-red fruits. Its bark is thick and often fissured, providing a rugged appearance.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
