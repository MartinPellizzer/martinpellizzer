Patagonium pentaphyllum

Patagonium pentaphyllum, commonly known as the Patagonian five-leaf plant, is a unique species native to the southern regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive five-leaf structure, which is rare among flowering plants. The plant typically grows in rocky and arid environments, showcasing its adaptation to harsh climates. Its foliage is often covered in a fine, white pubescence, which helps in reducing water loss.

Patagonium pentaphyllum plays a minor but important role in local ecosystems, providing habitat and food for specific insect species.
