Puccinellia asiatica

Puccinellia asiatica, commonly known as Asian needlegrass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear minute, wind-pollinated flowers. It is often found in saline or coastal environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions.

Puccinellia asiatica plays a role in stabilizing soils and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
