Phyllanthus cognatus

Phyllanthus cognatus, commonly known as the sacred lotus of India, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa, Asia, and Australia.

It is characterized by its small, green, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into small, round, red berries. It is widely cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional medicinal uses in various cultures.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for both horticultural and ecological applications.
