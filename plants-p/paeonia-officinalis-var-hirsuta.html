Paeonia officinalis var. hirsuta

Paeonia officinalis var. hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy peony, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from white to deep pink, often with a distinctive red eye. The plant features palmately compound leaves with hairy undersides, giving it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.

The hairy peony is also valued for its medicinal properties, particularly in traditional herbal medicine.
