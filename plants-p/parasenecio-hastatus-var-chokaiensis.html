Parasenecio hastatus var. chokaiensis

Parasenecio hastatus var. chokaiensis, commonly known as the Chokai mountain gentian, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of Japan.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant is typically found in alpine meadows and rocky habitats, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and often branched.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
