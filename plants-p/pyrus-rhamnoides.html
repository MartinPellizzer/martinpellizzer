Pyrus rhamnoides

Pyrus rhamnoides, commonly known as the sand pear, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its small, often clustered leaves that are typically grayish-green in color. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by small, edible pears that ripen in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Pyrus rhamnoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been cultivated for its fruit in some regions.
