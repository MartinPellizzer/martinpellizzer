Poa caesia var. gaudinii

Poa caesia var. gaudinii, commonly known as the Gaudin's bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its fine, bluish-green leaves and tufted growth habit, which contribute to its ornamental appeal. The plant typically thrives in well-drained soils and cooler climates, making it suitable for rock gardens or alpine settings. Its inflorescence consists of compact, spike-like clusters of small, two-lobed flowers.

Poa caesia var. gaudinii is valued for its hardiness and ability to form dense, low-growing mats in suitable environments.
