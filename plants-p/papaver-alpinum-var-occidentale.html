Papaver alpinum var. occidentale

Papaver alpinum var. occidentale, commonly known as the Western Alpine poppy, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its striking, bright red or orange petals that resemble those of the common poppy, with a prominent central stigma. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils and full sunlight. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens that mimic its natural habitat.
