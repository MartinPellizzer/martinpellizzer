Potentilla alba

Potentilla alba, commonly known as white cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is a perennial herb with a creeping rootstock and upright stems that bear alternate, palmately compound leaves. The plant produces showy, five-petaled white flowers in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in meadows and rocky areas.

Potentilla alba is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.
