Palisota plicata

Palisota plicata, commonly known as the broadleaf arrowhead, is a perennial aquatic plant native to the eastern United States.

It thrives in slow-moving freshwater environments such as ponds, lakes, and wetlands. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are often floating or partially submerged, with a distinctive arrowhead shape. Its flowers are small, greenish-white, and clustered in spikes, typically appearing in late summer.

Palisota plicata plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for invertebrates and serving as a food source for waterfowl.
