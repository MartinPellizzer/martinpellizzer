Polygala procumbens

Polygala procumbens, commonly known as the prostrate milkvetch, is a low-growing perennial herb native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It typically features wiry stems that lie close to the ground, often forming dense mats. The plant produces small, delicate flowers that are usually pale green to yellowish in color, arranged in loose clusters. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often with a slight hairy texture.

Polygala procumbens is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments, making it a hardy species found in disturbed soils and rocky areas.
