Picea brachytyla

Picea brachytyla, commonly known as the dwarf spruce, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to the high-altitude regions of the western United States.

It is characterized by its compact, slow-growing form and dense foliage, which consists of short, dark green needles. The tree produces small, cone-like structures that contain its seeds, typical of the Pinaceae family. Its leaves are typically arranged in crossed pairs, and the bark is generally thin and reddish-brown.

Picea brachytyla is adapted to harsh alpine conditions, making it a hardy and resilient species.
