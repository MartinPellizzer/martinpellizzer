Puccinellia festuciformis subsp. tenuifolia

Puccinellia festuciformis subsp.

tenuifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf saltbush, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions. It is a perennial grass that typically grows in saline or alkaline soils, often found in coastal areas and salt marshes.

The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It is well adapted to harsh environmental conditions, including high salinity and low moisture.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological restoration and soil stabilization projects.
