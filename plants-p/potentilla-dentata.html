Potentilla dentata

Potentilla dentata, commonly known as the toothed cinquefoil, is a perennial flowering plant native to North America.

It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks. The plant features a spreading growth habit with multiple stems that often root at the nodes. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with serrated edges that give it its common name.

The showy yellow flowers appear in clusters and bloom in the summer months.
