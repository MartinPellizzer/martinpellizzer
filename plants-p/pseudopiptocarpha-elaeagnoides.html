Pseudopiptocarpha elaeagnoides

Pseudopiptocarpha elaeagnoides, commonly known as silverleaf shrub, is a perennial shrub native to the arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, glandular hairs that help reduce water loss. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by small, edible fruits. It has a deep root system that allows it to thrive in poor, well-drained soils.

Pseudopiptocarpha elaeagnoides is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ability to stabilize soil in rocky or sandy environments.
