Pleurothallis portilloi

Pleurothallis portilloi, commonly known as the Portillo's orchid, is a species native to the cloud forests of Ecuador.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and yellow. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining, acidic soil for optimal growth. Its leaves are usually elongated and strap-like, contributing to its overall ornamental appeal.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in the wild.
