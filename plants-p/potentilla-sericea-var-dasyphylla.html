Potentilla sericea var. dasyphylla

Potentilla sericea var. dasyphylla, commonly known as silver cinquefoil, is a perennial shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, yellow flowers in early summer, which are typically borne on long, slender peduncles. Its leaves are alternate, palmately lobed, and often have a velvety texture.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.
