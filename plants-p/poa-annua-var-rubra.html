Poa annua var. rubra

Poa annua var. rubra, commonly known as red annual bluegrass, is a cool-season grass species native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its fine, bluish-green foliage and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that often turn reddish in color. This variety is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types, making it a common weed in lawns and agricultural fields. It typically blooms in late spring to early summer and is often found in disturbed areas.

Due to its aggressive growth habit, it can outcompete other grasses and is sometimes considered a problematic weed in turfgrass management.
