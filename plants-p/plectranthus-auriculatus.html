Plectranthus auriculatus

Plectranthus auriculatus, commonly known as the African violet, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its velvety, dark green leaves that are often heart-shaped or auriculate, giving it its scientific name. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers in shades of purple, pink, and white, which bloom throughout the year under proper care. It thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Plectranthus auriculatus is relatively easy to care for, requiring moderate watering and regular fertilization to maintain its lush appearance.
