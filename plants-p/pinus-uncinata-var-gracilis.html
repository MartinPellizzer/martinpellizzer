Pinus uncinata var. gracilis

Pinus uncinata var. gracilis, commonly known as the slender pine, is a variety of the Spanish pine that is native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It is characterized by its slender, often twisted trunk and relatively small, needle-like leaves. The plant produces cones that are typically smaller than those of the typical Pinus uncinata. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to harsh, high-altitude environments.

This variety is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and ecological adaptations.
