Polygala kemulariae

Polygala kemulariae, commonly known as the Malayan milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Polygalaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, feathery inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories. Its small, usually white or pinkish flowers are clustered in spicate racemes.

The leaves are opposite and often have a slightly hairy texture, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
