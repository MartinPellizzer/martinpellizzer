Phyllanthus pycnocarpus

Phyllanthus pycnocarpus, commonly known as the dense-fruited phyllanthus, is a shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. The plant produces small, clustered yellow flowers followed by capsules containing numerous tiny seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and open forests.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes used in traditional medicine and as a ornamental plant.
