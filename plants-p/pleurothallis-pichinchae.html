Pleurothallis pichinchae

Pleurothallis pichinchae, commonly known as the Pichincha orchid, is a species native to the cloud forests of Ecuador.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and green. The plant is epiphytic, meaning it grows on the surface of trees without being parasitic, and thrives in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are usually narrow and arranged in a rosette pattern, aiding in water retention.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is a subject of interest for botanists and orchid enthusiasts alike.
