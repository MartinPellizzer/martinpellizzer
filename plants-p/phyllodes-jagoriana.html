Phyllodes jagoriana

Phyllodes jagoriana, commonly known as the jagoriana acacia, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its unique phyllodes, which are flattened, leaf-like stems that perform the function of leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in dense, spherical clusters. Its phyllodes are typically narrow and have a prominent midrib, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It is adapted to arid environments and is often found in sandy soils.
