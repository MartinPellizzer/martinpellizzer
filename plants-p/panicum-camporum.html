Panicum camporum

Panicum camporum, commonly known as inland sandgrass, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in sandy or disturbed soils and is often found in open habitats such as roadsides and fields. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, fluffy seeds that are dispersed by wind. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it a hardy species for landscaping.

Its ecological role includes stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small invertebrates.
