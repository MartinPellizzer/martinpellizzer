Polyarrhena stricta

Polyarrhena stricta, commonly known as the narrowleaf ironweed, is a herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as swamps and stream banks. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often with a rough texture.

It produces small, purple to pink flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
