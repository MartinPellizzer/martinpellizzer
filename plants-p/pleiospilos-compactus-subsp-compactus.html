Pleiospilos compactus subsp. compactus

Pleiospilos compactus subsp.

compactus, commonly known as the compact pleiospilos, is a succulent plant native to South Africa. It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that store water, enabling it to survive in arid conditions.

The plant produces a tall flowering stalk with bright yellow flowers during the rainy season. It has a compact growth habit and is often grown as a decorative plant in gardens and greenhouses.

Due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements, it is a popular choice among succulent enthusiasts.
