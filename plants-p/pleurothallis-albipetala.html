Pleurothallis albipetala

Pleurothallis albipetala, commonly known as the white-petaled pleurothallis, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that typically feature white petals and sepals. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found growing epiphytically on trees or rocks. Its flowers are usually fragrant and bloom during the warmer months, attracting various pollinators.

Due to its unique beauty and ecological significance, it is a subject of interest for botanists and orchid enthusiasts alike.
