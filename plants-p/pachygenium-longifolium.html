Pachygenium longifolium

Pachygenium longifolium, commonly known as the long-leaved thickstem, is a perennial plant native to the temperate regions of the northern hemisphere.

It is characterized by its dense, fibrous root system and its ability to thrive in moist, well-drained soils. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale pink, blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are long and narrow, often reaching up to 30 centimeters in length, and are arranged in a basal rosette.

Pachygenium longifolium is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil.
