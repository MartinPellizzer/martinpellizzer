Placus jacquemontii

Placus jacquemontii, commonly known as the Jacquemont's lily, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Liliaceae family and is characterized by its striking, fragrant white or pink flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or well-drained soils and thrives in cool, temperate climates. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces showy blooms in the spring.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it is relatively rare in the wild.
