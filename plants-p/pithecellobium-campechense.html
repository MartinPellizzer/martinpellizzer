Pithecellobium campechense

Pithecellobium campechense, commonly known as the Mexican velvetbean, is a small evergreen tree native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by elongated, bean-like pods containing edible seeds. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The tree is well adapted to tropical and subtropical climates, thriving in well-drained soils.
