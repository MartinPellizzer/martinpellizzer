Panicum hamatum

Panicum hamatum, commonly known as inland sea oats, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its delicate, silver-gray foliage and produces fluffy, oat-like seed heads that resemble small clouds. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of water bodies, thriving in moist to wet soil conditions. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a distinctive blue-gray hue that gives it a striking appearance.

Panicum hamatum plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
