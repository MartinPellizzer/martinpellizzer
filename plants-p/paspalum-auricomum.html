Paspalum auricomum

Paspalum auricomum, commonly known as the silvergrass or auriculate paspalum, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-gray foliage and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typically clustered in panicles. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas.

Paspalum auricomum is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in erosion-prone regions.
