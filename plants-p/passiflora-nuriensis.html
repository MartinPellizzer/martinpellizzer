Passiflora nuriensis

Passiflora nuriensis, commonly known as the Nuri passionflower, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, intricate flowers that feature five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments. The plant produces small, edible berries that are typically yellow or orange in color. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial shade.

Passiflora nuriensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has mild medicinal properties in traditional uses.
