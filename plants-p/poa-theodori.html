Poa theodori

Poa theodori, commonly known as the Theodori's bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in moist, temperate climates and is often found in meadows, pastures, and along riverbanks. The plant has slender, wiry stems and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear blue to purple flowers. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green coloration that gives it its common name.

Poa theodori is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping and conservation efforts.
