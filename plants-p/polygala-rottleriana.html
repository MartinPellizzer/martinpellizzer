Polygala rottleriana

Polygala rottleriana, commonly known as the Rottler's milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Polygalaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, intricate flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its flowers are usually pink or purple and are arranged in racemes, which attract various pollinators.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
