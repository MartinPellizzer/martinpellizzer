Prunus serrulata var. compta

Prunus serrulata var. compta, commonly known as the Japanese cherry blossom or some varieties may be referred to as the dwarf cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its delicate, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in early spring, often preceding the leaves. The plant has simple, alternate, ovate to lanceolate leaves that turn yellow in the fall. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular ornamental plant.

Its botanical features make it a key species in traditional Japanese gardens and for seasonal floral displays.
