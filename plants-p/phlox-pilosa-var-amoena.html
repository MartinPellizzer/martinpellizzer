Phlox pilosa var. amoena

Phlox pilosa var. amoena, commonly known as the silky phlox, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Caprifoliaceae family and is characterized by its soft, fuzzy leaves and clusters of showy, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full sunlight. It is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Its blooming period usually occurs in late spring to early summer.
