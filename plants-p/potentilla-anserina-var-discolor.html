Potentilla anserina var. discolor

Potentilla anserina var. discolor, commonly known as the silverleaf cinquefoil, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its silver-white undersides on its leaves, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or alpine environments.

Its hardy nature makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.
