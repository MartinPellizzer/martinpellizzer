Phlogacanthus yangtsekiangensis

Phlogacanthus yangtsekiangensis, commonly known as the Yangtze river hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its small, white to pink flowers and red berries. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments along riverbanks and in mountainous regions. Its leaves are ovate with serrated edges, and it produces fruit that is edible but has a tart flavor.

This species is of interest in botanical studies due to its unique morphology and ecological adaptations.
