Polemonium pulcherrimum var. calycinum

Polemonium pulcherrimum var. calycinum, commonly known as the California bluebell, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and covered in fine pubescence. It prefers moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

Its botanical structure supports its role as a nectar source for pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
