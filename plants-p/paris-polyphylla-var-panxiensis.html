Paris polyphylla var. panxiensis

Paris polyphylla var. panxiensis, commonly known as the Yunnan Paris, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of southwestern China.

It belongs to the family Melanthiaceae and is characterized by its rhizomatous growth and striking, pale yellow to white flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments at high altitudes, often in forest understories. Its leaves are lanceolate and arranged in a basal rosette, while its inflorescence consists of clustered, nodding flowers.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated for horticultural purposes.
