Pandanus nudus

Pandanus nudus, commonly known as the narrow-leaf pandanus, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It is a member of the Pandanaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and long, narrow leaves. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and is often found in sandy or limestone soils. Its leaves are used in traditional crafts and for thatching, and it produces small, fragrant flowers that develop into woody fruits.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its aesthetic qualities.
