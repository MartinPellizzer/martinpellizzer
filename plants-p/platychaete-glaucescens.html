Platychaete glaucescens

Platychaete glaucescens, commonly known as the blue-green flatweed, is a low-growing perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It features fleshy, succulent leaves that are typically blue-green in color, which help it retain moisture in arid environments. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in late spring or early summer. Its root system is fibrous and adapted for nutrient absorption in poor soils.

Platychaete glaucescens is often found in rocky outcrops and sandy soils, thriving in full sun.
