Philadelphus sericanthus

Philadelphus sericanthus, commonly known as the sweet shrub or white honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant has opposite, simple, ovate leaves with serrated edges. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.

Philadelphus sericanthus is often used in ornamental gardening for its pleasant fragrance and attractive floral display.
