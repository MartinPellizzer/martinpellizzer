Placus acrocephalus

Placus acrocephalus, commonly known as the slender spike-rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that often reach heights of up to 1.5 meters. The plant produces small, clustered spikelets at the tips of its stems, which are typically greenish or brownish in color. It thrives in wetland environments such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

Placus acrocephalus plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
