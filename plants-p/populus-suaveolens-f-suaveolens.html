Populus suaveolens f. suaveolens

Populus suaveolens f. suaveolens, commonly known as sweet poplar or eastern poplar, is a deciduous tree native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and heart-shaped leaves with a serrated margin. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in catkins, which are typically pollinated by wind. Its leaves emit a distinctive, pleasant fragrance, especially when crushed.

This species is often found in moist, well-drained soils along rivers and in floodplain forests.
