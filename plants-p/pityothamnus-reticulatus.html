Pityothamnus reticulatus

Pityothamnus reticulatus, commonly known as the net-veined thistle, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its spiny leaves and purple to pink flowers. The plant has a robust root system and thrives in dry, well-drained soils. Its leaves are covered in fine hairs and have a distinctive net-like venation pattern.

It plays an important role in supporting local pollinators and is often found in grassland and rocky habitats.
