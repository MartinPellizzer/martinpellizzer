Pabstiella stictophylla

Pabstiella stictophylla, commonly known as the red-tipped cushion lichen, is a foliose lichen that thrives in moist, shaded environments.

It is characterized by its dense, cushion-like growth form and a distinctive red-tipped thallus. The lichen's upper surface is typically greenish to yellowish, while the lower surface is covered with a network of rhizines. It is often found on tree trunks and rocks in temperate forests.

Pabstiella stictophylla plays a vital role in nutrient cycling and provides habitat for various microorganisms.
