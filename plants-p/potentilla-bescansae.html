Potentilla bescansae

Potentilla bescansae, commonly known as the Bescan sandwort, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial herb characterized by its compact, mat-forming growth habit. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are typically fleshy and succulent, adapted to conserve water in its harsh environment.

Potentilla bescansae is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing sandy soils.
