Polygonum barbatum var. corymbosum

Polygonum barbatum var. corymbosum, commonly known as the hairy knotweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Asia.

It features upright stems that are often hairy, especially along the nodes, and produces clusters of small, pinkish to reddish flowers. The leaves are lance-shaped with a rough texture and may have a reddish or purple hue. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

The plant is known for its rapid growth and can become invasive in certain ecosystems.
