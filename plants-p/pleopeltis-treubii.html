Pleopeltis treubii

Pleopeltis treubii, commonly known as the Treub's rock fern, is a species of epiphytic fern native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit. The plant has lance-shaped, leathery fronds with a distinctive midrib and small, clustered sori on the underside of the leaves. It thrives in humid, shaded environments, typically growing on tree trunks or rocks.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for indoor cultivation and botanical gardens.
