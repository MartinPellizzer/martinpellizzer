Paeonia × lemoinei

Paeonia × lemoinei, commonly known as the Japanese peony, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Paeonia japonica and Paeonia suffruticosa.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring thick, woody stems and large, showy flowers. The leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets, and the plant produces fragrant, double or semi-double blooms in shades of pink, red, and white.

Native to China, this species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is commonly cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
