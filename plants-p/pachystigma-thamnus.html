Pachystigma thamnus

Pachystigma thamnus, commonly known as the rock dandelion, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming structure. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological role, it is often found in rocky outcrops and coastal areas.
