Placus gariepinus

Placus gariepinus, commonly known as the African violets, is a genus of flowering plants native to tropical Africa.

These plants are characterized by their velvety, waxy leaves and showy, clustered flowers that come in a variety of colors. They are typically herbaceous perennials, often grown as houseplants due to their ornamental value. Placus gariepinus thrives in humid, warm environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

The genus is popular among gardeners for its ease of care and aesthetic appeal.
