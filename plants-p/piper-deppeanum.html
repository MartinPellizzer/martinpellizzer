Piper deppeanum

Piper deppeanum, commonly known as the West Indian pepper or blue pepper, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is a member of the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper plants. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a glossy dark green surface with a distinctive aromatic scent.

The small green berries it produces are edible and have a peppery taste, often used in local cuisines.
