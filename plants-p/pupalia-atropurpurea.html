Pupalia atropurpurea

Pupalia atropurpurea, commonly known as purple poppy mallow, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features striking violet to purple petals that surround a central yellow cluster of stamens. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves and produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small seeds. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its vibrant color and unique floral structure.
