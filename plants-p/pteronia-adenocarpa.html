Pteronia adenocarpa

Pteronia adenocarpa, commonly known as the Cape rose, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, pink to purple flower heads. The plant has narrow, linear leaves and produces small, tubular flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens due to its vibrant blooms and drought tolerance.

Pteronia adenocarpa thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for Mediterranean climates.
