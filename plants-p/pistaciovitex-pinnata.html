Pistaciovitex pinnata

Pistaciovitex pinnata, commonly known as the goldenleaf tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are pinnately arranged and often have a striking yellow or golden hue. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by small, round fruits. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The species is also valued in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
