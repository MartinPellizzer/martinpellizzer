Pinanga ternatensis

Pinanga ternatensis, commonly known as the Ternate bamboo palm, is a species of flowering plant in the family Araceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in the Philippines and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, compound leaves that can reach up to several meters in length. It produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in a spadix, typical of the Araceae family.

Pinanga ternatensis is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its elegant appearance and air-purifying qualities.
