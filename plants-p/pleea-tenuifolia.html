Pleea tenuifolia

Pleea tenuifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf waterplantain, is a herbaceous plant native to wetland environments in North America.

It typically grows in shallow water or moist soils, often found in marshes and along the edges of ponds. The plant has slender, linear leaves that are usually less than 1 inch wide, giving it its common name. Its small, white flowers appear in clusters during the summer months.

It plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various insect and aquatic life.
