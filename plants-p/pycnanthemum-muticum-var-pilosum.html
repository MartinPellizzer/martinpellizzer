Pycnanthemum muticum var. pilosum

Pycnanthemum muticum var. pilosum, commonly known as hairy mountain mint, is a perennial herb native to the eastern and central United States.

It is characterized by its aromatic, hairy leaves and clusters of pale blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist to wet soils in woodland or wetland habitats. It is a member of the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is known for its strong scent when crushed.

Its medicinal properties have been historically used by Native American tribes for various purposes.
