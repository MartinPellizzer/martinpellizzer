Protea foeniculacea

Protea foeniculacea, commonly known as the sweet cycad, is a striking plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Proteaceae family and is characterized by its large, palm-like leaves and distinctive, showy inflorescences. The plant produces a unique, spore-bearing structure called a strobilus, which is often mistaken for a flower. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Due to its slow growth and specific habitat requirements, it is often cultivated in controlled environments.
