Paspalum carinatovaginatum

Paspalum carinatovaginatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf grass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. This plant is often found in wetland and marshy environments, thriving in moist, nutrient-rich soils. It has a rhizomatous growth habit, allowing it to spread and form dense mats.

Paspalum carinatovaginatum is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
