Potentilla laxa

Potentilla laxa, commonly known as the silky cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit and produces clusters of small, yellow flowers. The leaves are typically divided into five oval-shaped lobes, giving it the common name "cinquefoil.". The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or rocky habitats.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
