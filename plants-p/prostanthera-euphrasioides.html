Prostanthera euphrasioides

Prostanthera euphrasioides, commonly known as the mountain mint bush, is a shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, white to pale purple flowers that are clustered in spikes, typically blooming from late winter to early spring. Its leaves are lance-shaped, hairy, and emit a minty fragrance when crushed.

Prostanthera euphrasioides is often found in rocky or sandy soils in woodland and heathland environments.
