Phaseolus oblongus var. luteus

Phaseolus oblongus var. luteus, commonly known as the yellow runner bean, is a variant of the common bean species native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its yellow pods and seeds, which are typically smaller than those of other bean varieties. The plant is an annual legume that thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth. It produces clusters of white or pale purple flowers, which are followed by elongated, yellow pods.

This variety is often cultivated for its edible beans, which are rich in protein and nutrients.
