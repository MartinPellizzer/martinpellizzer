Pinus ussuriensis

Pinus ussuriensis, commonly known as the Ussuri pine, is a coniferous tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, pyramidal shape and dark green, needle-like leaves. The tree produces cones that are typically reddish-brown and contain seeds covered in a thin, winged membrane. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forested regions of Russia, China, and Korea.

The Ussuri pine is valued for its timber and plays an important ecological role in its native habitats.
