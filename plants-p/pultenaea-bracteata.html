Pultenaea bracteata

Pultenaea bracteata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is a member of the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant typically grows in sandy soils and is found in regions such as Western Australia and South Australia. It produces yellow flowers in spring, which attract pollinators like bees and birds.

The narrow, linear leaves and distinctive bracts are key identifying features of this species.
