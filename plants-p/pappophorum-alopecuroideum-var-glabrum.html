Pappophorum alopecuroideum var. glabrum

Pappophorum alopecuroideum var. glabrum, commonly known as the smooth hairgrass, is a low-growing, mat-forming plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It features fine, grass-like leaves that are typically smooth and glabrous, giving it its varietal name. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in clusters, which are often wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, stream banks, and wetlands.

Its dense growth habit makes it an important component of ecological habitats, providing cover and food for various invertebrates.
