Philibertia religiosa

Philibertia religiosa, commonly known as the sacred lily, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of South America.

It is characterized by its striking white petals and a tall, slender stem that can reach up to two meters in height. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting various pollinators. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, often covered in a fine, silvery down.

Due to its rarity and association with sacred sites, it is considered both a botanical wonder and a culturally significant species.
