Piqueria polyphylla

Piqueria polyphylla, commonly known as the Mexican tarplant, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and its ability to thrive in sandy or rocky soils. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are typically narrow and covered in fine, white hairs, which help reduce water loss in dry conditions.

Piqueria polyphylla is often found in desert scrublands and is adapted to withstand high temperatures and low precipitation.
