Phyllanthus grandisepalus

Phyllanthus grandisepalus, commonly known as the large-sepal phyllanthus, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters. The plant features simple, opposite leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. Its flowers are small and clustered in inflorescences, with sepals that are notably large and showy.

The fruit is a capsule that contains several seeds, which are dispersed by wind or animals.
