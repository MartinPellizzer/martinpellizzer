Psacalium radulifolium

Psacalium radulifolium, commonly known as the radulifolium shrub, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, clustered leaves and clusters of fragrant, white or pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories. Its botanical features include woody stems and a compact growth habit.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has limited use in traditional medicine.
