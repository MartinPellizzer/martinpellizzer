Potentilla velenovskyi

Potentilla velenovskyi, commonly known as the Velenovsky cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are compound, consisting of five to seven elliptical leaflets, and it produces small, seed-containing fruits.

Potentilla velenovskyi is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardiness and aesthetic qualities.
