Piper basilobatum

Piper basilobatum, commonly known as the black pepper plant, is a species of flowering vine native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its climbing habit and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces small, dark-colored berries that are harvested for their aromatic and spicy seeds. These seeds, known as peppercorns, are widely used as a spice and medicinal ingredient.

The botanical features of Piper basilobatum include its opposite leaves, which are typically ovate to elliptical in shape.
