Phasellus lanatus

Phasellus lanatus, commonly known as the hairy phasellus, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy foliage and small, clustered flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink or white. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine, white hairs, which help reduce water loss in humid conditions.

Phasellus lanatus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
