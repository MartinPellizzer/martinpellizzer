Potentilla cinerea var. concolor

Potentilla cinerea var. concolor, commonly known as silver cinquefoil, is a low-growing perennial shrub native to North America.

It features silver-gray foliage due to dense pubescence, which helps reduce water loss in arid environments. The plant produces small, yellow flowers in clusters during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its compact size and ornamental appeal make it a valuable addition to native plant landscapes.
