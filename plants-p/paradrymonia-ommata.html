Paradrymonia ommata

Paradrymonia ommata, commonly known as the eye-leaf plant, is a unique flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, eye-like leaves that are often adorned with intricate patterns and vibrant colors. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the rainy season. Its foliage is evergreen, and the leaves are thick and waxy, which helps it retain moisture in its humid habitat.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
