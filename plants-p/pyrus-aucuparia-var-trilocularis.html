Pyrus aucuparia var. trilocularis

Pyrus aucuparia var. trilocularis, commonly known as the Japanese white pear, is a cultivar of the pear tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its pale yellow to white fruits, which are typically small and sweet. The plant produces compound leaves with a serrated margin and is often grown for its ornamental value as well as its edible fruit. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for temperate climates.

This variety is also valued for its cold hardiness and ability to thrive in a range of environmental conditions.
