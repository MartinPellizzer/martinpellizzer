Populus nigra subsp. italica

Populus nigra subsp.

italica, commonly known as the Italian black poplar, is a subspecies of the black poplar native to southern Europe. It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and distinctive, elongated, cylindrical cones.

The tree typically grows along riverbanks and in moist soils, exhibiting a preference for riparian habitats. Its bark is smooth and grayish-black, often peeling in thin layers.

The subspecies is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing riverbanks and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
