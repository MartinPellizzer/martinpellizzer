Pteris muricella

Pteris muricella, commonly known as the rock brake fern, is a species of fern native to various regions in Asia, Africa, and the Americas.

It is a small, evergreen fern that typically grows in rocky or shaded environments. The plant has fronds that are pinnately divided, with a serrated or toothed edge. Its spores are produced on the underside of the fronds in clusters called sori.

Pteris muricella is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in moist, shaded conditions.
