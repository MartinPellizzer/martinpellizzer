Pteropogon corymbosus

Pteropogon corymbosus, commonly known as the mountain balsam, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and opposite, ovate leaves with serrated margins. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in cool, moist environments at high altitudes.

Its botanical features make it an important species in alpine ecosystems.
