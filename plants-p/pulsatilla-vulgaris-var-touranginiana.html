Pulsatilla vulgaris var. touranginiana

Pulsatilla vulgaris var. touranginiana, commonly known as the Touranginiana pasqueflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Ranunculaceae family.

It is characterized by its showy, violet-colored flowers that bloom in early spring, typically between March and May. The plant features a short, upright stem with palmate leaves that are deeply lobed and covered in fine hairs. Native to Central Asia, it is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and rockeries.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and sought-after species among botanists and horticultural enthusiasts.
