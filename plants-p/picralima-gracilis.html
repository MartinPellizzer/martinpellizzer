Picralima gracilis

Picralima gracilis, commonly known as the slender senna, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant produces yellow flowers that are often clustered in racemes, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its slender, erect stems are typically covered in fine hairs, and it thrives in well-drained soils.

The leaves are pinnately compound, with small, oval-shaped leaflets that contribute to its ornamental appeal.
