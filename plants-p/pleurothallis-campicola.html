Pleurothallis campicola

Pleurothallis campicola, commonly known as the mountain orchid, is a species native to the cloud forests of Central and South America.

It is a small epiphytic orchid that typically grows on trees or rocks in humid, shaded environments. The plant produces delicate, greenish-white flowers that bloom during the warmer months. Its roots are adapted to absorb moisture from the air and decaying plant matter.

Pleurothallis campicola is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by enthusiasts for its unique appearance.
