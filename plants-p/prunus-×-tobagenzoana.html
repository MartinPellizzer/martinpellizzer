Prunus × tobagenzoana

Prunus × tobagenzoana, commonly known as the Toggenburg cherry, is a hybrid deciduous shrub or small tree native to Europe.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its ornamental value, featuring showy pink to white flowers in spring. The plant typically grows to a height of 2–5 meters and has lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges. Its fruit is a small, dark red drupe that ripens in summer, though it is not typically edible.

Prunus × tobagenzoana is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to various soil conditions.
