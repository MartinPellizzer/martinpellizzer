Piper subepunctatum

Piper subepunctatum, commonly known as the spotted pepper vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes various species of pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its slender, vine-like stems and heart-shaped, glossy leaves with distinct yellow or white spots. Its small, greenish-white flowers are followed by small, round berries that turn red when mature.

Piper subepunctatum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
