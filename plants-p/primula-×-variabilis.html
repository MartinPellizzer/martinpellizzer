Primula × variabilis

Primula × variabilis, commonly known as the variable primrose, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Primulaceae family.

It is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that come in a variety of colors, including white, yellow, and purple. The plant typically grows to a height of 10–30 cm, with lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia, it thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial shade.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden cultivation.
