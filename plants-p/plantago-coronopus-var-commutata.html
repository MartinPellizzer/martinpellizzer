Plantago coronopus var. commutata

Plantago coronopus var. commutata, commonly known as the common plantain, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, disturbed soils such as roadsides, fields, and riverbanks. The plant has broad, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, greenish flowers in clusters. It is characterized by its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its resilience to environmental stress.

This species is also known for its medicinal properties, having been used historically for its healing benefits.
