Paepalanthus piscatorum var. ecuadorensis

Paepalanthus piscatorum var. ecuadorensis, commonly known as the Ecuadorian water cactus, is a succulent plant native to the humid regions of Ecuador.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, ribbed stems that store water, enabling it to thrive in arid or seasonally dry environments. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom during the rainy season, adding to its ornamental value. Its leaves are typically reduced to spines, which help reduce water loss and deter herbivores.

This variety is often cultivated for its unique appearance and drought tolerance in gardens and collections.
