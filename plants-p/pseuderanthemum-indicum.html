Pseuderanthemum indicum

Pseuderanthemum indicum, commonly known as the Indian false jasmine, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and small, showy flowers. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. Its flowers are typically tubular, with two lips, and are often brightly colored, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

It is often cultivated as a ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and flowering display.
