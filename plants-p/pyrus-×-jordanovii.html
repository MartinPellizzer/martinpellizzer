Pyrus × jordanovii

Pyrus × jordanovii, commonly known as the Jordan's pear, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Pyrus communis (European pear) and Pyrus pyrifolia (Asian pear).

It is characterized by its medium to large size, with fruit that typically exhibits a smooth, waxy skin and a sweet, juicy flesh. The plant is deciduous, shedding its leaves in autumn, and thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value as well as for its edible fruit.

The hybrid is valued for its adaptability and resistance to certain diseases compared to its parent species.
