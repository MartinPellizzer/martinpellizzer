Prunus pendula f. roseopetala

Prunus pendula f. roseopetala, commonly known as the weeping cherry with rose-pink petals.

This cultivar is a variant of the Prunus pendula species, characterized by its cascading branches that bear clusters of small, fragrant flowers. The flowers are a striking pinkish-white color, adding a delicate and ornamental appearance to the tree. It is typically grown as a ornamental tree in gardens and parks due to its aesthetic appeal.

The plant is relatively easy to care for, thriving in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil.
