Pedicellaria pentaphylla var. hirsutissima

Pedicellaria pentaphylla var. hirsutissima, commonly known as the hairy pedicellaria, is a variant of the pedicellaria found in certain species of sea urchins.

It is a small, calcareous structure that serves as a defensive mechanism, often found on the surface of the urchin's body. The plant's name refers to its distinctive five-lobed structure, which is characteristic of the pedicellaria family. This variant is notable for its dense, hairy covering, which gives it a unique texture and appearance.

It plays a role in protecting the urchin from predators by releasing toxic secretions when disturbed.
