Panicum glaucum var. laevigatum

Panicum glaucum var. laevigatum, commonly known as silky bluegrass, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It features narrow, bluish-green leaves that are smooth and slightly folded, giving it a silky appearance. The plant produces small, fluffy seed heads that are often used in ornamental settings due to their aesthetic appeal. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Its drought tolerance and low maintenance requirements make it a popular choice for erosion control and wildlife habitat.
