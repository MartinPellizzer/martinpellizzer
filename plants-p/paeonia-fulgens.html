Paeonia fulgens

Paeonia fulgens, commonly known as the fiery peony, is a species of herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from pink to red, with a distinctive yellow center. The plant typically grows to a height of 2 to 3 feet and features deeply lobed, heart-shaped leaves. Its botanical name reflects its fiery appearance, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.

Paeonia fulgens thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers well-drained, fertile soil.
