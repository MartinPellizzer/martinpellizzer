Ptychosperma beatriceae

Ptychosperma beatriceae, commonly known as the Beatrice palm, is a rare and endangered species native to the island of Borneo.

It is a member of the Arecaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and pinnate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by dark blue, fleshy fruits. Due to habitat loss and limited distribution, it is critically endangered and requires conservation efforts to ensure its survival.
