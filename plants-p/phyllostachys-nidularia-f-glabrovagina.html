Phyllostachys nidularia f. glabrovagina

Phyllostachys nidularia f. glabrovagina, commonly known as the glabrous sheath bamboo, is a unique variant of the common bamboo species.

It is characterized by its smooth, hairless leaf sheaths, which distinguish it from other forms of Phyllostachys nidularia. The plant typically grows in dense clumps, forming a compact and upright structure. Its leaves are long and narrow, with a glossy green appearance that enhances its ornamental value.

This bamboo is well-suited for cultivation in temperate climates and is often used in landscape design for its aesthetic appeal and hardiness.
