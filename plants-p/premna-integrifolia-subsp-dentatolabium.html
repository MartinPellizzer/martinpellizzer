Premna integrifolia subsp. dentatolabium

Premna integrifolia subsp.

dentatolabium, commonly known as the hairy-leafed glorybower, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its opposite, ovate to elliptic leaves that are often hairy on the underside.

The plant produces small, fragrant, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, shaded environments.

The subspecies is distinguished by its finely toothed leaf margins and its ornamental value in horticulture.
