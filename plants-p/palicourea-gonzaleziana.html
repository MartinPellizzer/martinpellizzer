Palicourea gonzaleziana

Palicourea gonzaleziana, commonly known as the Cuban beautyberry, is a member of the Rubiaceae family.

It is a shrub native to the Caribbean, particularly Cuba and the Bahamas. The plant is characterized by its opposite, simple, and elliptical leaves. It produces small, clustered, white flowers that are followed by bright blue, fleshy berries.

These berries are edible and often used in local cuisines and traditional medicine.
