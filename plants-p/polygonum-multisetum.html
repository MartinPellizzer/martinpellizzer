Polygonum multisetum

Polygonum multisetum, commonly known as the velvetleaf or Indian spinach, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, velvety hairs. The plant produces small, clustered greenish-white flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, fertile soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Polygonum multisetum is known for its rapid growth and ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes.
