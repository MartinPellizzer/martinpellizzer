Physaria didymocarpa var. lyrata

Physaria didymocarpa var. lyrata, commonly known as the lyrate bladderpod, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive, lyre-shaped leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, elliptical seeds. It thrives in dry, well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and disturbed areas.

The species is part of the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other flowering plants with similar floral structures.
