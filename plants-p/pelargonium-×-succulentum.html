Pelargonium × succulentum

Pelargonium × succulentum, commonly known as the stonecrop geranium, is a hybrid succulent plant characterized by its fleshy, thick leaves and showy flowers.

It belongs to the Geraniaceae family and is native to South Africa, where it thrives in arid and rocky environments. The plant exhibits a compact growth habit with dense foliage, often forming a rosette shape. Its flowers are typically pink or red, appearing in clusters during the spring and summer months.

Pelargonium × succulentum is valued for its ornamental appeal and low maintenance requirements, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
