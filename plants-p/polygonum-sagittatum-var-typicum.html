Polygonum sagittatum var. typicum

Polygonum sagittatum var. typicum, commonly known as arrow-leaved buckwheat, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It features arrow-shaped leaves that are typically green with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually pink or reddish in color, blooming from late spring to early fall. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
