Potentilla nivea var. intermedia

Potentilla nivea var. intermedia, commonly known as the alpine cinquefoil, is a low-growing perennial shrub native to alpine regions of Europe and Asia.

It features small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, typically from June to July. The plant has slender, wiry stems and hairy leaves that are often arranged in clusters. It thrives in well-drained, rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in rock gardens or alpine displays.
