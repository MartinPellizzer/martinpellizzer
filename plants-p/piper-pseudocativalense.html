Piper pseudocativalense

Piper pseudocativalense, commonly known as the purple piper or purple pepper plant, is a member of the Piperaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces berries that range in color from green to purple as they mature. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and for its potential medicinal properties.

Its botanical features make it a unique addition to both horticultural and pharmacological studies.
