Panicum proboscideum

Panicum proboscideum, commonly known as the giant beardgrass, is a tall, grass-like plant native to the wetlands of South America.

It is characterized by its long, slender stems and dense, fluffy panicles that resemble a beard, giving it its common name. The plant thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils and is often found in marshy or swampy areas. Its flowering period typically occurs in the late summer and early fall, producing small, inconspicuous flowers that are wind-pollinated.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological role, it is sometimes cultivated in wetland restoration projects.
