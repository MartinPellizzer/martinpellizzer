Polygonum ramuliflorum

Polygonum ramuliflorum, commonly known as the ramuli-flowered knotweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia and the Americas.

It typically grows in moist, disturbed habitats such as stream banks and wet meadows. The plant features erect stems that are often reddish in color and are covered with small, leaf-like structures called phyllaries. Its small, clustered flowers are usually pink to red in color and bloom in late summer.

The species is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
