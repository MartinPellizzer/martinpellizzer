Pleocnemia profereoides

Pleocnemia profereoides, commonly known as the swamp fern, is a tropical plant belonging to the family Polypodiaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, lace-like fronds that are typically less than 30 centimeters in length. This fern thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in wetlands and along stream banks. Its rhizome is creeping and produces numerous short, upright shoots.

The plant plays a role in stabilizing soil in its habitat and supports various insect and microorganism populations.
