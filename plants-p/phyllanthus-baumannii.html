Phyllanthus baumannii

Phyllanthus baumannii, commonly known as the African buttonwood, is a shrub native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal and savannah regions.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and traditional medicine.
