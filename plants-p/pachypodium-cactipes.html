Pachypodium cactipes

Pachypodium cactipes, commonly known as the Madagascar moon cactus, is a succulent plant native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. The plant features a distinctive, tree-like structure with a swollen base and branching stems that bear clusters of small, waxy leaves. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, appearing at the ends of the stems during the growing season.

Pachypodium cactipes is well adapted to drought conditions, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping and indoor gardening.
