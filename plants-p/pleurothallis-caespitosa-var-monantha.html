Pleurothallis caespitosa var. monantha

Pleurothallis caespitosa var. monantha, commonly known as the clustered pleurothallis, is a species of orchid native to the Andes region of South America.

It is characterized by its compact, dense growth habit, with multiple stems arising from a central base. The plant produces small, delicate flowers in shades of white and pale yellow, often arranged in clusters. It thrives in shaded, humid environments and requires well-draining, acidic soil.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is relatively easy to cultivate under the right conditions.
