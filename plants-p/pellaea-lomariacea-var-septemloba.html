Pellaea lomariacea var. septemloba

Pellaea lomariacea var. septemloba, commonly known as the seven-lobed maidenhair fern, is a variant of the maidenhair fern family.

It is characterized by its delicate, feathery fronds that are typically dark green with a slightly glossy surface. The fronds are pinnately divided, with seven distinct lobes on each leaf, giving it its common name. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in temperate forest regions.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
