Prostanthera incisa var. tenuior

Prostanthera incisa var. tenuior, commonly known as narrow-leaved mountain mint, is a perennial shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic leaves and square stems. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a serrated margin, and it produces small, tubular flowers in shades of blue to purple. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland and rocky habitats.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to native pollinators.
