Pelargonium cardiifolium

Pelargonium cardiifolium, commonly known as the heartleaf geranium, is a species native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive heart-shaped leaves, which are typically lobed and have a velvety texture. The plant produces clusters of small, five-petaled flowers in shades of pink, red, or white, depending on the cultivar. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Pelargonium cardiifolium is often used in ornamental gardening for its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to various climates.
