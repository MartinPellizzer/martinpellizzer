Phymatodes triphylla

Phymatodes triphylla, commonly known as the three-leaf phymatode, is a perennial flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy stems and leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine, white hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It is well adapted to arid environments and can thrive in sandy or rocky soils.

Phymatodes triphylla is often found in desert scrub and grassland habitats.
