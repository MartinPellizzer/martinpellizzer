Pharmacosycea anthelmintica var. microcarpa

Pharmacosycea anthelmintica var. microcarpa, commonly known as the small-fruited antihelmintic plant, is a species native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is a shrub characterized by its small, elliptical leaves and yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, berry-like fruits that contain seeds with medicinal properties. Its botanical significance lies in its potential antiparasitic compounds, which have been studied for their use in traditional medicine.

The variety microcarpa is distinguished by its smaller fruit size compared to the typical form of the species.
