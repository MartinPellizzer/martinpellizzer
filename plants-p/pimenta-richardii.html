Pimenta richardii

Pimenta richardii, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a flowering plant native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is a member of the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its small, reddish berries and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 10 meters. Its leaves are opposite, elliptical, and have a fragrant, peppery scent when crushed.

The fruit is an important food source for various bird species and has been used in traditional medicine.
