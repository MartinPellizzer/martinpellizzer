Passiflora subtriloba

Passiflora subtriloba, commonly known as the three-lobed passionflower, is a climbing vine native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, complex flowers that feature a central crown of filaments and a distinctive blue or purple corona. The plant produces edible, tropical berries that are rich in antioxidants and have a sweet, tangy flavor. Its leaves are heart-shaped with three deep lobes, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Passiflora subtriloba is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its showy flowers and is also used in traditional medicine.
