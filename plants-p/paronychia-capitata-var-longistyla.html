Paronychia capitata var. longistyla

Paronychia capitata var. longistyla, commonly known as the long-styled capitata goosefoot, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its prostrate or ascending growth habit and produces small, yellowish flowers in dense clusters. The leaves are typically rounded or oval with a slightly hairy surface. This species is often found in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.

Its unique floral structure and variability in stem length make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
