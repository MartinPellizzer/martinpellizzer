Pahudia caudata

Pahudia caudata, commonly known as the Cape tulip, is a striking perennial plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant features a hairy, upright stem and narrow, grass-like leaves. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Pahudia caudata is also valued for its drought tolerance and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
