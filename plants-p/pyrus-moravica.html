Pyrus moravica

Pyrus moravica, commonly known as the Moravian pear, is a species of flowering plant in the family Rosaceae.

It is native to the temperate regions of Central Europe, particularly the Czech Republic and parts of Austria. The plant is characterized by its deciduous habit, with simple, alternate leaves and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in early spring. Its fruit, a small pear-like apple, is edible and has been historically used in local cuisines.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and its role in traditional horticulture.
