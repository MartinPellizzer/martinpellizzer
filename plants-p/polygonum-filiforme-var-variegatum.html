Polygonum filiforme var. variegatum

Polygonum filiforme var. variegatum, commonly known as the variegated filiform polygonum, is a herbaceous perennial native to parts of Asia.

It features slender, creeping stems that often root at the nodes, forming dense mats. The leaves are elongated, narrow, and typically have a variegated coloration with shades of green, white, and sometimes pink. It produces small, clustered flowers in shades of red or purple during the summer months.

This plant thrives in moist, shady environments and is often used in ornamental gardening for its attractive foliage.
