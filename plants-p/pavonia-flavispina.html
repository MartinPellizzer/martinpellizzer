Pavonia flavispina

Pavonia flavispina, commonly known as the yellow thornbush, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of showy yellow to orange-red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has spiny stems and leaves that are typically ovate to lanceolate in shape. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it a hardy species suitable for ornamental use in gardens.

Pavonia flavispina is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
