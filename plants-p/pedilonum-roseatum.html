Pedilonum roseatum

Pedilonum roseatum, commonly known as the pink sandalwood, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, woody stems and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces elliptical, leathery leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. Its flowers are pollinated primarily by bees and other insects, contributing to its ecological significance.

The plant's unique appearance and aromatic properties make it a notable species in its native habitat.
