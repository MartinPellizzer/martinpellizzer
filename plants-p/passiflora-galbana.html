Passiflora galbana

Passiflora galbana, commonly known as the purple passionflower, is a climbing vine native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking violet and cream-colored flowers, which feature intricate patterns resembling a crown. The plant produces edible, orange-yellow berries that are rich in antioxidants and have a tropical flavor. Its leaves are heart-shaped and often have a velvety texture.

Passiflora galbana is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine.
