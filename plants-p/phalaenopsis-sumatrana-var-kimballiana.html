Phalaenopsis sumatrana var. kimballiana

Phalaenopsis sumatrana var. kimballiana, commonly known as the Kimball's phalaenopsis, is a rare and elegant orchid native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its long, arching inflorescences that bear numerous fragrant, white to pale lavender flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its leaves are broad, lance-shaped, and often covered in a fine, white pubescence.

This variety is highly sought after by orchid enthusiasts for its beauty and unique blooming characteristics.
