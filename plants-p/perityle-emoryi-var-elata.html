Perityle emoryi var. elata

Perityle emoryi var. elata, commonly known as the tall perityle, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, elongated fruits that contain seeds.

This variety is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas.
