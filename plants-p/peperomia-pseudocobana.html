Peperomia pseudocobana

Peperomia pseudocobana, commonly known as the cobana peperomia, is a compact, low-growing plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It features thick, waxy leaves that are often variegated with shades of green, white, and silver, giving it a striking appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or reddish in color, which are followed by tiny, round berries. It thrives in bright, indirect light and prefers well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Peperomia pseudocobana is a popular choice for indoor gardening due to its ornamental foliage and relatively low maintenance requirements.
