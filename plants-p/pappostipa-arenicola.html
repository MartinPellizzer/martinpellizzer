Pappostipa arenicola

Pappostipa arenicola, commonly known as the sandgrass, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its low-growing, tufted growth habit. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to withstand drought and high temperatures.

Pappostipa arenicola plays a role in stabilizing sandy soils and providing habitat for small desert fauna.
