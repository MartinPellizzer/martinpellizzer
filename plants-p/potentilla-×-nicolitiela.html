Potentilla × nicolitiela

Potentilla × nicolitiela, commonly known as the Nicolo's cinquefoil, is a hybrid shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant features narrow, dark green leaves with a slightly hairy texture, and its stems are often covered in fine, silvery-white hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is a popular choice for gardeners seeking drought-tolerant plants.
