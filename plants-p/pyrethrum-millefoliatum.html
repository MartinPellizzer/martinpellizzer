Pyrethrum millefoliatum

Pyrethrum millefoliatum, commonly known as feverfew, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its feathery, fern-like leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and chrysanthemums. Feverfew is known for its medicinal properties, particularly for its use in treating headaches and inflammation.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for both ornamental gardening and herbal remedies.
