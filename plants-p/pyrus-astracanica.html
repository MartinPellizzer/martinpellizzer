Pyrus astracanica

Pyrus astracanica, commonly known as the Astrakhan pear, is a species of flowering plant in the genus Pyrus.

It is native to the Caucasus region and parts of Turkey, growing in temperate climates. The plant produces small, yellowish-green leaves and white flowers that bloom in spring. Its fruit, the Astrakhan pear, is small and typically has a high sugar content.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical significance in horticulture.
