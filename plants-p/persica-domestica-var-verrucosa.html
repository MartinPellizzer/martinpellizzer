Persica domestica var. verrucosa

Persica domestica var. verrucosa, commonly known as the rough peach.

This variant is characterized by its thick, rugged skin that is covered in a distinctive, bumpy or warty texture. The fruit typically has a yellow or orange flesh with a sweet and sometimes tart flavor. It is a cultivar of the domesticated peach, often used in ornamental gardening due to its unique appearance.

The plant is a small deciduous tree that thrives in temperate climates.
