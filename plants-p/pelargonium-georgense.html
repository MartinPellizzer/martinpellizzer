Pelargonium georgense

Pelargonium georgense, commonly known as the Georgian geranium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Caucasus region.

It features upright stems with heart-shaped, velvety leaves that are typically dark green. The plant produces clusters of small, five-petaled flowers in shades of pink, red, and white, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and containers.

Pelargonium georgense is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various garden conditions.
