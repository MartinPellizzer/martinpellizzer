Pseudolysimachion sachalinense

Pseudolysimachion sachalinense, commonly known as the Sakhalin lily, is a perennial herb native to the Russian Far East, particularly Sakhalin Island.

It belongs to the Liliaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and showy, white to pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, forested areas and thrives in temperate climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, while its flowers bloom in late summer.

Pseudolysimachion sachalinense is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in the local ecosystem.
