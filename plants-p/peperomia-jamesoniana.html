Peperomia jamesoniana

Peperomia jamesoniana, commonly known as the Jameson's peperomia, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to Central and South America.

It features thick, waxy leaves that are typically oval or elliptical in shape, often with a bluish or grayish hue. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually greenish or reddish in color, emerging from the axils of the leaves. It thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Peperomia jamesoniana is noted for its low maintenance requirements and ability to tolerate dry conditions.
