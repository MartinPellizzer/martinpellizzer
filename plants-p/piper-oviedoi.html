Piper oviedoi

Piper oviedoi, commonly known as the Oviedo pepper, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of Central America, particularly in Panama and Costa Rica. The plant is characterized by its slender, vine-like growth habit and small, heart-shaped leaves. Its fruit, a small, elongated berry, is typically green when unripe and turns red when mature.

Piper oviedoi is valued for its aromatic leaves, which are used in traditional medicine and as a spice in local cuisines.
