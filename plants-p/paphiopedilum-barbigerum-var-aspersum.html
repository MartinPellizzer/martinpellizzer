Paphiopedilum barbigerum var. aspersum

Paphiopedilum barbigerum var. aspersum, commonly known as the speckled lady's slipper, is a unique orchid species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, pouch-shaped lip that is often speckled or mottled with contrasting colors. The plant typically grows as a terrestrial orchid, preferring shaded, humid environments with well-drained soil. Its leaves are usually elliptical and dark green, while its flowers are fragrant and come in shades of white, pink, and purple.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated for its beauty and rarity.
