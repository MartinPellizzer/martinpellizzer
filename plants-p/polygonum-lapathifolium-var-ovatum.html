Polygonum lapathifolium var. ovatum

Polygonum lapathifolium var. ovatum, commonly known as the broad-leaved smartweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to temperate regions of Asia.

It features upright, hairy stems that can grow up to 1 meter in height, supporting alternate, ovate to lanceolate leaves with a rough, textured surface. The plant produces small, clustered flowers in shades of pink to red, typically blooming from summer to autumn. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory properties.

The species is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in moist, disturbed soils.
