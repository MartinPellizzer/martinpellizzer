Pleopeltis lauterbachii

Pleopeltis lauterbachii, commonly known as the Lauterbach's rock fern, is a species of epiphytic fern native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its compact, rosette-like growth habit. The plant features finely divided, lanceolate fronds that are often covered in a dense layer of silky, white hairs. It thrives in humid, shaded environments, typically growing on tree trunks or rocky surfaces.

Due to its unique appearance and specific habitat requirements, it is a sought-after plant among fern enthusiasts.
