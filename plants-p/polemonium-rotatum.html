Polemonium rotatum

Polemonium rotatum, commonly known as the purple fringed bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep purple, often with a distinctive purple fringe. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and has lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. Its flowering period occurs in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as butterflies and bees.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in woodland gardens.
