Potentilla versicolor

Potentilla versicolor, commonly known as the variable cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow to orange-red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or meadow habitats. Its leaves are alternate, with five to nine leaflets that are usually serrated.

Potentilla versicolor is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
