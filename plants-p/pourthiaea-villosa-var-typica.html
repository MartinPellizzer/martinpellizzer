Pourthiaea villosa var. typica

Pourthiaea villosa var. typica, commonly known as the woolly pourthiaea, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, daisy-like yellow flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens for its unique texture and seasonal blooms.
