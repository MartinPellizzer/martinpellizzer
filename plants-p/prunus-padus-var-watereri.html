Prunus padus var. watereri

Prunus padus var. watereri, commonly known as the water cherry or water plum, is a variant of the common bird cherry.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Europe and parts of Asia. The plant is characterized by its slender, often weeping branches and clusters of small, white flowers that appear in early spring. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a smooth texture with a slightly serrated edge.

The fruit is a small, yellowish drupe that ripens in late summer, providing a food source for various wildlife species.
