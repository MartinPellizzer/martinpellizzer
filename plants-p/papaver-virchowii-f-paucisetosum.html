Papaver virchowii f. paucisetosum

Papaver virchowii f. paucisetosum, commonly known as the sparse-hairy poppy, is a botanical variant of the species Papaver virchowii.

It is a flowering plant characterized by its distinctive, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of red or orange. The plant features a short, hairy stem and lance-shaped leaves with a slightly serrated edge. It thrives in rocky or well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine regions.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is occasionally cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
