Phaseolus leptostachyus var. intonsus

Phaseolus leptostachyus var. intonsus, commonly known as the Andean runner bean, is a climbing legume native to the Andes.

It produces elongated, cylindrical pods that are typically green when immature and turn yellow or red when mature. The plant has compound leaves with small, ovate leaflets and is characterized by its distinctive, spiraling inflorescences. It thrives in cool, temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is valued for its edible beans, which are rich in protein and nutrients.
