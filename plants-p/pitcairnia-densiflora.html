Pitcairnia densiflora

Pitcairnia densiflora, commonly known as the dense-flowered pitcairnia, is a striking bromeliad native to the tropical rainforests of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, rosette-shaped leaves that form a central tank to collect rainwater, which serves as a habitat for various small organisms. The plant produces showy, long-lasting inflorescences with bright red bracts that attract pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees. Its foliage is often covered in a dense layer of trichomes, giving it a fuzzy appearance and aiding in water retention.

Pitcairnia densiflora is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is relatively easy to cultivate under the right conditions.
