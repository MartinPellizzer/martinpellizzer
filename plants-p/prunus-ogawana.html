Prunus ogawana

Prunus ogawana, commonly known as the Japanese cherry, is a deciduous tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and produces small, white or pink flowers in early spring. The tree has ovate to lanceolate leaves that turn yellow in autumn. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is a symbol of renewal and ephemeral beauty in Japanese culture.

Prunus ogawana prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for gardens and landscapes.
