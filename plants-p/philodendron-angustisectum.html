Philodendron angustisectum

Philodendron angustisectum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf philodendron, is a tropical climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its slender, elongated leaves that are typically dark green with a slightly glossy surface. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are followed by spiky, green berries. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Philodendron angustisectum is often cultivated as a houseplant due to its attractive foliage and relatively easy care requirements.
