Prunus amelanchier

Prunus amelanchier, commonly known as serviceberry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to North America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its clusters of white or pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces edible berries that ripen in late summer, typically ranging in color from red to black. Its leaves are ovate with serrated edges, and it often has a distinctive branching pattern.

Prunus amelanchier is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing food for birds and pollinators.
