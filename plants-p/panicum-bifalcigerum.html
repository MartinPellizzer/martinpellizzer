Panicum bifalcigerum

Panicum bifalcigerum, commonly known as the two-headed panic grass, is a species of grass native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its distinctive two-headed inflorescence, which consists of two closely spaced spikelets. The plant typically grows in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its leaves are narrow and have a bluish-green color, and it produces small, wind-pollinated flowers.

Panicum bifalcigerum is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
