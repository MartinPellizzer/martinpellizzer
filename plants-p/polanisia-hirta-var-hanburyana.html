Polanisia hirta var. hanburyana

Polanisia hirta var. hanburyana, commonly known as the Hanbury's polanisia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and private collections.
