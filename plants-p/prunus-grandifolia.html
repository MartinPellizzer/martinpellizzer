Prunus grandifolia

Prunus grandifolia, commonly known as the Chinese bird's eye cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It is characterized by its small, white flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by dark purple, edible berries. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for gardens and landscapes.

Prunus grandifolia is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated for its spring blossoms and autumn fruit.
