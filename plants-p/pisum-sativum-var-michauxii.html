Pisum sativum var. michauxii

Pisum sativum var. michauxii, commonly known as the Michaux pea, is a botanical variant of the common pea.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and smaller, more rounded pods compared to typical garden peas. The plant produces small, yellow flowers and is often found in Mediterranean climates. It is a perennial species that can thrive in well-drained soils.

Its botanical features make it of interest in both horticultural and ecological studies.
