Polygonatum hirtum

Polygonatum hirtum, commonly known as the hairy Solomon's seal, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Asparagaceae family and is characterized by its arching stems and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. The plant produces distinctive, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are broad, lance-shaped, and have a hairy texture, giving it its common name.

Polygonatum hirtum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
