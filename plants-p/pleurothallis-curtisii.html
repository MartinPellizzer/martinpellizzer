Pleurothallis curtisii

Pleurothallis curtisii, commonly known as the Curtis's orchid, is a species of orchid native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and intricate floral structure. The plant typically produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, often with a distinctive lip. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found growing on trees or rocks in its natural habitat.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated by enthusiasts, though it remains a relatively rare and specialized species.
