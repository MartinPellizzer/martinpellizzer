Prangos cylindracea

Prangos cylindracea, commonly known as the cylinder thistle, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and robust, spiny leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its cylindrical stem and dense foliage help it retain moisture in dry climates.

Prangos cylindracea is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been introduced to other regions for horticultural purposes.
