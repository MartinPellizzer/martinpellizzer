Pellionia imbricata

Pellionia imbricata, commonly known as the velvetleaf pellionia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, velvety leaves that give it a distinctive texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white or pale green flowers arranged in clusters. Pellionia imbricata thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental foliage and ability to thrive in humid, low-light conditions.
