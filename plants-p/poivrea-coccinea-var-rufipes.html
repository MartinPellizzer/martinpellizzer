Poivrea coccinea var. rufipes

Poivrea coccinea var. rufipes, commonly known as the red-flowered weigela, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its striking red, tubular flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant features dark green, oval-shaped leaves that turn yellow in the fall. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden landscapes.
