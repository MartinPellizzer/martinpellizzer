Potentilla pinnatifida var. platyloba

Potentilla pinnatifida var. platyloba, commonly known as the flat-lobed cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia and North America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with five leaflets, which are usually oval to lance-shaped. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or rocky areas.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden cultivation.
