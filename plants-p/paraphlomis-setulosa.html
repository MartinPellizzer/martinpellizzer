Paraphlomis setulosa

Paraphlomis setulosa, commonly known as the hairy parsnip, is a perennial herb native to Asia.

It belongs to the family Lamiaceae and is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, pale blue or purple flowers arranged in whorled clusters. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Paraphlomis setulosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine.
