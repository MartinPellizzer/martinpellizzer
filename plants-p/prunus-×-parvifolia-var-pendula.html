Prunus × parvifolia var. pendula

Prunus × parvifolia var. pendula, commonly known as the weeping cherry or weeping almond, is a cultivar of the Prunus genus that exhibits a distinctive cascading growth habit.

It is characterized by its slender, drooping branches that create a delicate, flowing appearance, often resembling a weeping willow. The plant produces small, fragrant white or pink flowers in early spring, which are followed by small, tart cherries. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This ornamental tree is popular in gardens for its aesthetic appeal and seasonal beauty.
