Pinus mughus var. lutescens

Pinus mughus var. lutescens, commonly known as the yellow-barked pine, is a rare variant of the Mughan pine native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive golden-yellow bark, which contrasts with its dark green needles. The plant typically grows in high-altitude environments, where it forms dense forests or scattered stands. Its cones are relatively small and mature within one year, releasing seeds that are dispersed by wind.

This species is of interest to botanists due to its unique coloration and ecological adaptations.
