Passiflora loxensis

Passiflora loxensis, commonly known as the Mexican passionflower, is a climbing vine native to Central America.

It is characterized by its striking, complex flowers that feature a blend of red, blue, and yellow hues, along with intricate patterns resembling a crown. The plant produces edible berries that are often used in culinary applications. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a rough texture, while its stems are woody and covered in fine hairs.

Passiflora loxensis is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported calming properties.
