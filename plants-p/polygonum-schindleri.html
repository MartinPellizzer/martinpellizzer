Polygonum schindleri

Polygonum schindleri, commonly known as the Schindler's knotweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and is characterized by its upright stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually pink or red in color, which bloom in late summer. Polygonum schindleri is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and flowers.

It is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
