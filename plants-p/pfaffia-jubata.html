Pfaffia jubata

Pfaffia jubata, commonly known as golden root, is a perennial plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Compositae family and is characterized by its yellow-orange roots, which are the primary part used for medicinal purposes. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments within tropical forests. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture, while its flowers are small and yellow, arranged in a composite head.

Pfaffia jubata is valued for its potential adaptogenic properties and has been used in traditional medicine for centuries.
