Potamogeton diversifolius f. limosus

Potamogeton diversifolius f. limosus, commonly known as the muddy pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant characterized by its long, slender stems and alternate leaves.

It typically grows in shallow, slow-moving freshwater environments such as lakes, ponds, and streams. The leaves are usually narrow and have a waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss in aquatic conditions. This plant plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.

It is also used in phytoremediation due to its ability to absorb heavy metals from the water.
