Phyllanthus tinctorius

Phyllanthus tinctorius, commonly known as the Indian pennywort, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its alternate, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-yellow in color. Its roots and leaves are used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.
