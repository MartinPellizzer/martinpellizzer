Philodendron cyrtocoleum

Philodendron cyrtocoleum, commonly known as the red-veined philodendron, is a tropical climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, glossy leaves with striking red veins that contrast with its dark green foliage. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers, though it is primarily grown for its ornamental leaves rather than its blooms. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires consistently moist, well-draining soil.

Philodendron cyrtocoleum is a popular houseplant due to its aesthetic appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
