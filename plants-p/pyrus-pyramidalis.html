Pyrus pyramidalis

Pyrus pyramidalis, commonly known as the weeping Japanese pear, is a deciduous tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its pendulous branches that create a distinctive, cascading appearance. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers in the spring, followed by edible, sweet fruits in the summer. Its leaves are broad and ovate, with a smooth texture and a dark green upper surface.

Pyrus pyramidalis is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its unique form and seasonal beauty.
