Phyllanthus buchii

Phyllanthus buchii, commonly known as the African spurge, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring woody stems and dense foliage. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by small, round, yellow fruits. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Phyllanthus buchii is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and is often found in disturbed or arid environments.
