Passiflora watsoniana

Passiflora watsoniana, commonly known as the Watson's passionflower, is a species native to Central and South America.

It is a climbing vine that produces striking, showy flowers with five sepals, five petals, and a distinctive crown of filaments. The plant's botanical features include a complex floral structure with a unique arrangement of stamens and a central reproductive cone. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Passiflora watsoniana is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
