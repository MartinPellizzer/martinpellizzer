Protium puberulentum

Protium puberulentum, commonly known as the silky tamarind, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, pale bark and elliptical leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by woody, elongated pods containing edible seeds. Its leaves and seeds have been traditionally used in local cuisines and traditional medicine.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in tropical forests.
