Pycreus sanguinolentus f. melanocephalus

Pycreus sanguinolentus f. melanocephalus, commonly known as the Crimson-veined Crimsonhead, is a unique variant of the base species Pycreus sanguinolentus.

It is characterized by its striking deep red veins that contrast sharply with its pale green foliage. The plant exhibits a compact, bushy growth habit with small, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are typically a deep crimson, with a subtle metallic sheen.

This cultivar is prized for its ornamental value in shaded gardens and as a specimen plant in botanical collections.
