Pedilonum brachyphyta

Pedilonum brachyphyta, commonly known as the short-stemmed moss, is a small, carpet-like moss that thrives in moist, shaded environments.

It has dense, compact growth habit with short, upright stems covered in leaf-like structures called phyllidia. The leaves are typically ovate to lanceolate in shape and have a smooth or slightly rough surface. It reproduces through spores released from capsules that develop on the stems.

This species is often found in forest floors and rocky crevices, contributing to soil stabilization and moisture retention.
