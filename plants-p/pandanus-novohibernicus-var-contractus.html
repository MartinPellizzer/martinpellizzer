Pandanus novohibernicus var. contractus

Pandanus novohibernicus var. contractus, commonly known as the narrow-leaf pandanus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Pandanaceae.

It is characterized by its narrow, sword-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, which are often used in traditional crafts and culinary applications. Native to the Pacific Islands, it thrives in coastal and wetland environments.

Its unique morphology and ecological adaptability make it an important species in its native habitats.
