Polygonum thunbergii var. maackianum

Polygonum thunbergii var. maackianum, commonly known as the Japanese knotweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to East Asia.

It features upright stems that are often reddish in color and bear opposite, lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or red, appearing in late summer. It is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types, making it both ecologically significant and potentially invasive.

Its botanical characteristics make it a notable species in both ornamental gardening and ecological studies.
