Philodendron reticulatum

Philodendron reticulatum, commonly known as the net-veined philodendron, is a tropical climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It features heart-shaped, waxy leaves with a distinctive net-like venation pattern that gives it its common name. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers, followed by reddish berries. It thrives in warm, humid environments with bright, indirect light.

Philodendron reticulatum is popular as a houseplant due to its attractive foliage and relatively easy care requirements.
