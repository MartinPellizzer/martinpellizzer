Panicum pictigluma

Panicum pictigluma, commonly known as the purple top millet, is a warm-season grass native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its striking purple-tinted leaves and panicles that range from red to bronze in color. This plant thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens. It is drought-tolerant and often used as a landscape plant due to its attractive appearance and low maintenance requirements.

The plant is also valued for its ability to stabilize soil and prevent erosion in agricultural settings.
