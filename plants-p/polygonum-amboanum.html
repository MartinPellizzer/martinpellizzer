Polygonum amboanum

Polygonum amboanum, commonly known as the Madagascar nettle, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It is a member of the Polygonaceae family, characterized by its distinctive nettle-like leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and can reach heights of up to 2 meters. Its leaves are often covered in fine hairs, and the flowers are usually greenish-white in color.

Polygonum amboanum is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional medicine for various ailments.
