Pinalia macera

Pinalia macera, commonly known as the desert orchid, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, showy flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant has adapted to harsh environments through its deep root system and ability to store water in its leaves. It blooms during the spring and early summer, relying on pollinators such as bees and moths for reproduction.

Pinalia macera is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
