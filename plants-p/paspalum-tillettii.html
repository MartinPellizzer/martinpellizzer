Paspalum tillettii

Paspalum tillettii, commonly known as the coastal paspalum, is a perennial grass native to coastal regions of South America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in dune environments.

Paspalum tillettii is well adapted to coastal conditions, including salt spray and strong winds.
