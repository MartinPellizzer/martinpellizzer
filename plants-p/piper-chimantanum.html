Piper chimantanum

Piper chimantanum, commonly known as the Chimantanum pepper, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to the Amazon rainforest and is characterized by its climbing habit and compound leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by elongated, peppery fruits. It is valued for its aromatic leaves and fruits, which are used in traditional medicine and cuisine.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
