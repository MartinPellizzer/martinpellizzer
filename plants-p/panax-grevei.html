Panax grevei

Panax grevei, commonly known as the Green's ginseng, is a rare and endangered species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Araliaceae family and is closely related to other ginseng species. The plant is characterized by its distinctive, fleshy roots and dark green, lobed leaves. It thrives in cool, shaded environments with high humidity.

Due to its rarity and potential medicinal properties, it is highly sought after and faces significant conservation challenges.
