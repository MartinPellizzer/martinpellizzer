Podochilus carnosus

Podochilus carnosus, commonly known as the fat orchid, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its fleshy, swollen stems and vibrant, showy flowers. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in shaded, moist environments. Its unique morphology includes thick, succulent leaves and clusters of fragrant, colorful blooms.

Podochilus carnosus is valued both for its ornamental appeal and its role in traditional medicine.
