Pseudoberlinia

Pseudoberlinia, commonly known as the African tamarind, is a genus of flowering plants in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to tropical Africa and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pod-like fruits. The plant is often found in savanna and woodland habitats, thriving in well-drained soils. Pseudoberlinia species are known for their nitrogen-fixing ability, which enriches the soil.

The seeds are encased in hard, woody pods and are often used in traditional medicine and as a source of tannins.
