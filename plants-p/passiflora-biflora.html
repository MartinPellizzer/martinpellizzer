Passiflora biflora

Passiflora biflora, commonly known as the twin-flower passionflower, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that feature a complex arrangement of petals, sepals, and bracts. The plant produces blue or purple berries that are edible and often used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are usually heart-shaped and have a rough texture.

Passiflora biflora is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
