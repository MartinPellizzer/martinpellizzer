Parthenocissus pedata

Parthenocissus pedata, commonly known as the Virginia creeper, is a deciduous vine native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that change from bright green to vibrant red in the fall. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by bright red berries in the autumn. It has tendrils that allow it to climb and attach to various surfaces.

The Virginia creeper is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to cover walls or trellises.
