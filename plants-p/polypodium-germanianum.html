Polypodium germanianum

Polypodium germanianum, commonly known as the German shield fern, is a species of evergreen fern native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender fronds that can reach up to 1 meter in length, with a distinctive, shield-like shape. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically found in woodlands and along stream banks. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The fronds are deciduous, shedding in the winter, and the plant reproduces through spores rather than seeds.
