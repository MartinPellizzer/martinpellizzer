Panax pseudoginseng

Panax pseudoginseng, commonly known as red ginseng, is a perennial plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Araliaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive root system, which is the primary part used in traditional medicine. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments and can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters. Its leaves are compound, with a serrated edge, and it produces small greenish-white flowers in the summer.

The roots are harvested after several years of growth and are dried and processed for medicinal use.
