Pyrus bibas

Pyrus bibas, commonly known as the Chinese white pear, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white flowers. The tree produces pears that are typically white or yellow in color, with a smooth, thin skin. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its edible fruit and ornamental value.

The plant is also valued for its ability to adapt to various soil types and its relatively low maintenance requirements.
