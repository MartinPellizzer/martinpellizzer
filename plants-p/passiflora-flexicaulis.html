Passiflora flexicaulis

Passiflora flexicaulis, commonly known as the slender passionflower, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, waxy leaves and striking, intricate flowers that display a mix of blue, purple, and white hues. The plant produces edible, yellow-orange berries that are rich in antioxidants. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

Its unique floral structure is adapted to attract specific pollinators such as hummingbirds and bats.
