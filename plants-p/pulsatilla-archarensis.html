Pulsatilla archarensis

Pulsatilla archarensis, commonly known as the Mongolian pasqueflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its leaves are palmately divided, and it produces follicles as its fruit type.

Pulsatilla archarensis is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance in its native range.
