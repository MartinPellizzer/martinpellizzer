Pseudosasa nabeshimana

Pseudosasa nabeshimana, commonly known as the Japanese bamboo or Japanese black bamboo, is a member of the grass family Poaceae.

It is native to Japan and is characterized by its dark green, slender culms that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged in a dense, feathery pattern. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to form dense thickets.

Pseudosasa nabeshimana prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a versatile choice for garden landscapes.
