Peripleura arida

Peripleura arida, commonly known as the desert shield-leaf, is a perennial shrub native to arid regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its small, leathery leaves that are adapted to reduce water loss in hot, dry environments. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to access deep groundwater sources.

Peripleura arida is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its resilience and ability to stabilize sandy soils.
