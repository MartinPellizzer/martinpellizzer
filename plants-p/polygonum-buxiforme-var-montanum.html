Polygonum buxiforme var. montanum

Polygonum buxiforme var. montanum, commonly known as the mountain boxleaf smartweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of North America.

It is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and small, dark green, box-like leaves. The plant produces clusters of tiny, pink to red flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and stream banks.

Due to its ornamental appeal and ecological value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
