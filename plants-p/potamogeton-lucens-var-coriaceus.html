Potamogeton lucens var. coriaceus

Potamogeton lucens var. coriaceus, commonly known as the smooth pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant found in freshwater environments.

It has elongated, ribbon-like leaves that are typically smooth and slightly waxy, which helps reduce water loss. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are clustered in spike-like structures, often emerging from the water surface. It plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.

Its presence is often an indicator of good water quality in lakes and slow-moving rivers.
