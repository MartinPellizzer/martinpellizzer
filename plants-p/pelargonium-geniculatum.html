Pelargonium geniculatum

Pelargonium geniculatum, commonly known as the zigzag geranium, is a species of flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive zigzagging stems and clusters of small, pink or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its compact size and attractive foliage.

Pelargonium geniculatum is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
