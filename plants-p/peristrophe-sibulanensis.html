Peristrophe sibulanensis

Peristrophe sibulanensis, commonly known as the Sibulan grass, is a member of the Poaceae family, characterized by its grass-like appearance.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, particularly in the Philippines. The plant has slender, cylindrical stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often arranged in a basal rosette. Its inflorescence consists of small, spike-like clusters of flowers, which are usually wind-pollinated.

The species is notable for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various small organisms.
