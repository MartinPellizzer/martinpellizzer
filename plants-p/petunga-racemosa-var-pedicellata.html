Petunga racemosa var. pedicellata

Petunga racemosa var. pedicellata, commonly known as the clustered petunga, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its dense, racemose inflorescence, which consists of clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests or along stream banks. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a velvety texture.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
