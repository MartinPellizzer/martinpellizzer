Pseudoglossanthis aulieatensis

Pseudoglossanthis aulieatensis, commonly known as the Aulieatensis false ghost orchid, is a rare and enigmatic member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its unique floral structure, featuring a single, showy, resupinate flower with a distinctive lip. Native to a limited geographic range in South America, it thrives in humid, shaded environments within tropical rainforests. The plant exhibits a specialized mycorrhizal relationship with certain fungi, which is essential for its nutrient uptake.

Due to its restricted habitat and sensitivity to environmental changes, it is considered a vulnerable species.
