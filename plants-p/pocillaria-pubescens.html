Pocillaria pubescens

Pocillaria pubescens, commonly known as the hairy rock cress, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its small, rounded leaves covered in fine hairs, which give it its common name. The plant produces clusters of small, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in alpine or arctic regions.

Pocillaria pubescens is a perennial species that is well adapted to cold climates and is relatively hardy.
