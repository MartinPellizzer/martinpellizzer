Plectranthus chienii

Plectranthus chienii, commonly known as Chinese mint or purple mint, is a perennial herb native to southern China.

It is characterized by its square stems and opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically violet or purple in color, arranged in whorled clusters. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for garden cultivation.

Plectranthus chienii is valued for its aromatic foliage and is often used in traditional Chinese medicine.
