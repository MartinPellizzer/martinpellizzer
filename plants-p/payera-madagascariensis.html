Payera madagascariensis

Payera madagascariensis, commonly known as the Madagascar star orchid, is a unique orchid species native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its striking, star-shaped flowers that come in a variety of vibrant colors, including white, pink, and purple. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in the humid rainforests of the island. Its leaves are narrow and elongated, often with a waxy coating to help retain moisture.

The species is highly valued for its ornamental beauty and plays a significant role in the local biodiversity of Madagascar.
