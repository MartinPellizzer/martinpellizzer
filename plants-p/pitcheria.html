Pitcheria

Pitcheria, commonly known as the pitcher plant, is a carnivorous plant that thrives in nutrient-poor environments.

It is characterized by its unique leaf structures, which form deep, hollow traps resembling pitchers. These traps secrete a sticky liquid that lures and captures insects, which are then digested by enzymes. The plant's ability to absorb nutrients from its prey allows it to survive in acidic, boggy soils where other plants struggle.

Pitcheria species are often found in wetlands and marshes across various regions of the world.
