Potentilla sommieri

Potentilla sommieri, commonly known as the summer potentilla, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its low-growing, sprawling habit. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically oval-shaped with a hairy texture, and it thrives in well-drained, sunny soils.

Potentilla sommieri is often used in rock gardens or as a ground cover due to its hardiness and ornamental value.
