Piper punctatissimum

Piper punctatissimum, commonly known as the spotted pepper vine, is a climbing shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, woody stems and heart-shaped leaves with prominent veins. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by elliptical, blue-black berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and traditional herbal practices.
