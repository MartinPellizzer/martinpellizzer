Phlox suaveolens

Phlox suaveolens, commonly known as sweet phlox, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its showy clusters of fragrant, pink to purple flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and features opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

Botanically, it belongs to the family Polemoniaceae and is known for its attractive floral display and adaptability to various growing conditions.
