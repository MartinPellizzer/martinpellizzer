Prunus mutabilis subf. minor

Prunus mutabilis subf.

minor, commonly known as the dwarf cherry or Japanese dwarf cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Japan. It is characterized by its small, upright growth habit and clusters of white to pink flowers that bloom in early spring.

The leaves are ovate with serrated edges and turn yellow in the fall. The fruit is a small, tart cherry that is typically not eaten by humans but is used in traditional Japanese medicine.

This plant is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its spring blossoms.
