Paspalum culiacanum

Paspalum culiacanum, commonly known as Culiacan grass, is a perennial grass species native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences, which are typical of the Poaceae family. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions.

Paspalum culiacanum is often found in disturbed areas and can be invasive in certain regions.
