Picris humilis

Picris humilis, commonly known as lesser poppy, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is a low-growing annual or biennial herb with slender stems and small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy to the touch. Its flowers are typically arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems.

Picris humilis is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
