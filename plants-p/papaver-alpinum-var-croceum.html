Papaver alpinum var. croceum

Papaver alpinum var. croceum, commonly known as the Alpine poppy, is a striking plant native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow to orange flowers, which bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes, where it thrives in full sun. Its foliage consists of palmately lobed leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white hairs.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens that mimic its natural habitat.
