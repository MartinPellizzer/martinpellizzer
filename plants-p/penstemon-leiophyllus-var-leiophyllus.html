Penstemon leiophyllus var. leiophyllus

Penstemon leiophyllus var. leiophyllus, commonly known as the smoothleaf beardtongue, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It features upright stems with lance-shaped, smooth, dark green leaves that are typically hairless. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that range in color from purple to pink, often with a contrasting lower lip. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its botanical characteristics make it an attractive and drought-tolerant addition to native plant gardens.
