Pseuderanthemum shweliense

Pseuderanthemum shweliense, commonly known as the Shweliense false jasmine, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and small, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as under forest canopies. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a velvety texture.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral displays.
