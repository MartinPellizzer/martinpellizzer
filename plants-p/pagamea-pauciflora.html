Pagamea pauciflora

Pagamea pauciflora, commonly known as the rare desert cactus, is a succulent plant native to arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems and sparse, often clustered flowers that bloom in shades of yellow and orange. The plant has a shallow root system adapted to absorb moisture from the dry soil. Its leaves are modified into spines, reducing water loss and deterring herbivores.

Pagamea pauciflora plays a crucial role in its ecosystem by providing shelter and food for various desert animals.
