Pedilonum uliginosum

Pedilonum uliginosum, commonly known as the bog lichen, is a unique symbiotic organism composed of a fungus and green algae or cyanobacteria.

It thrives in moist, nutrient-poor environments such as bogs and acidic soils. The plant lacks true roots, stems, and leaves, and instead forms a crust-like structure on its substrate. Its growth is slow, and it plays a vital role in nutrient cycling in wetland ecosystems.

Due to its sensitivity to environmental changes, it is often used as an indicator of habitat quality.
