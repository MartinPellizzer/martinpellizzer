Passiflora hassleriana

Passiflora hassleriana, commonly known as the Mexican passionflower, is a climbing vine native to Central America and parts of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, complex flowers that feature a blend of violet, white, and yellow hues, with prominent stamens and a crown-like structure. The plant produces edible, orange-colored berries that are rich in nutrients and often consumed by both humans and wildlife. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a velvety texture, while its stems are woody and can grow up to several meters in length.

Passiflora hassleriana is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty and ecological benefits.
