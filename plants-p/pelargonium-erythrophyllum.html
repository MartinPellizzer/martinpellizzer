Pelargonium erythrophyllum

Pelargonium erythrophyllum, commonly known as the red-leaf geranium, is a species of flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its striking red or purple foliage and clusters of small, pink to white flowers. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub with hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its vibrant leaf color and seasonal blooming habit.
