Pycreus inundatus

Pycreus inundatus, commonly known as the water pycreus, is a rare aquatic plant found in freshwater environments.

It has long, ribbon-like leaves that float on the water surface, facilitating photosynthesis. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically submerged, adapting to its aquatic habitat. Its roots are anchored in the sediment, allowing it to stabilize in flowing water.

Due to its specialized adaptations, it plays a role in maintaining water quality and providing habitat for aquatic organisms.
