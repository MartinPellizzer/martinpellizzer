Pavonia macrostyla

Pavonia macrostyla, commonly known as the showy pavonia, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features striking, large, pink to red flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. The plant has palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, giving it a distinctive, fern-like appearance. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Pavonia macrostyla is valued in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to naturalize in suitable conditions.
