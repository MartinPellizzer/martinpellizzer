Physalis lagascae

Physalis lagascae, commonly known as the goldenberry or Inca berry, is a species of flowering plant in the nightshade family, Solanaceae.

It is native to the Andean regions of South America, particularly Peru and Bolivia, where it grows in high-altitude areas. The plant produces small, yellow-orange fruits that are encased in a papery, lantern-shaped calyx. These fruits are rich in vitamin C and have a sweet, tangy flavor, making them popular in culinary uses.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental qualities and has been cultivated for both food and medicinal purposes.
