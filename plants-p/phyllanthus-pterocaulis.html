Phyllanthus pterocaulis

Phyllanthus pterocaulis, commonly known as the African star gooseberry, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its dense, spiny foliage and small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces bright red, fleshy fruits that are edible and often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in arid and semi-arid regions, showing adaptability to poor soil conditions.

Its unique morphology and medicinal properties make it an important species in both ecological and cultural contexts.
