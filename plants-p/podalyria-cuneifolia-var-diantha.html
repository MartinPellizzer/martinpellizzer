Podalyria cuneifolia var. diantha

Podalyria cuneifolia var. diantha, commonly known as the pink snowbell, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, contributing to its overall delicate appearance. This variety is known for its cold-hardiness and ability to thrive in alpine environments.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens with well-drained soil and full sun.
