Phyllanthus venulosus

Phyllanthus venulosus, commonly known as the African star gooseberry, is a small shrub native to tropical Africa.

It features small, green, oval-shaped leaves that are typically opposite in arrangement. The plant produces yellowish-white flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, followed by small, fleshy, orange-red berries. It is often found in moist, shaded areas such as forest understories and along riverbanks.

The plant has a woody stem and is known for its medicinal properties in traditional African medicine.
