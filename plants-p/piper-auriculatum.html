Piper auriculatum

Piper auriculatum, commonly known as the "ear-shaped pepper" or "Mexican pepper," is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its small, heart-shaped leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces tiny, peppery fruits that are used in culinary applications. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil.

Its botanical features make it a unique addition to both ornamental and medicinal gardens.
