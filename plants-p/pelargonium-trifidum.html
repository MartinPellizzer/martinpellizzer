Pelargonium trifidum

Pelargonium trifidum, commonly known as the three-lobed geranium, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive trifid (three-lobed) leaves and showy, five-petaled flowers that range in color from pink to red. The plant typically grows in a compact, bushy form and is often cultivated as a decorative plant. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and containers.

Pelargonium trifidum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
