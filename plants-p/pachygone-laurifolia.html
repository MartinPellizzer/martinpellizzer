Pachygone laurifolia

Pachygone laurifolia, commonly known as the laurel-leaved thickstem, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, woody stems and dark green, leathery leaves that are arranged in opposite pairs. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by small, round berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its foliage is valued for its aromatic properties and is used in traditional medicine and as a spice in some cuisines.
