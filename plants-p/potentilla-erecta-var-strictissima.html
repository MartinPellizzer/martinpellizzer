Potentilla erecta var. strictissima

Potentilla erecta var. strictissima, commonly known as the upright cinquefoil, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has slender, upright stems with opposite, pinnately compound leaves that are covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable addition to native plant collections and ecological restoration projects.
