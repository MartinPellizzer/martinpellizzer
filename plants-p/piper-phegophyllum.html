Piper phegophyllum

Piper phegophyllum, commonly known as the purple pepper plant, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many species of pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, purple flowers. Its fruit, which resembles a tiny pepper, is typically harvested when it turns bright red.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its unique foliage and fruit.
