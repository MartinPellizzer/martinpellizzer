Potentilla beniczkyi

Potentilla beniczkyi, commonly known as the Beniczky's cinquefoil, is a perennial shrub native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its small, clustered flowers that typically bloom in yellow or white. The plant has alternate, pinnately compound leaves with serrated edges, and its stems are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
