Primula aucheri

Primula aucheri, commonly known as the Auricula, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its rosette of hairy, oval-shaped leaves and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that come in a variety of colors, including white, yellow, and red. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value. It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is known for its ability to produce multiple blooms throughout the growing season.

Its unique appearance and hardy nature make it a popular choice among gardeners and botanists alike.
