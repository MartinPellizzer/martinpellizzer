Phlox drummondii subsp. wilcoxiana

Phlox drummondii subsp.

wilcoxiana, commonly known as the Wilcox morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States. It is characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and lavender.

The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. It has opposite, lance-shaped leaves and produces seeds in small, dry capsules.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various garden conditions.
