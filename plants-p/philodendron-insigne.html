Philodendron insigne

Philodendron insigne, commonly known as the red-veined philodendron, is a tropical climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, glossy leaves that often feature striking red veins. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically followed by green, berry-like fruits. It thrives in warm, humid environments with bright, indirect light.

Philodendron insigne is a popular houseplant due to its attractive foliage and relatively easy care requirements.
