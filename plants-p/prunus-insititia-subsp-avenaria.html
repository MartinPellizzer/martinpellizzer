Prunus insititia subsp. avenaria

Prunus insititia subsp.

avenaria, commonly known as the sand cherry, is a shrub native to the western United States and northern Mexico. It typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in early spring, followed by edible, sweet-tart berries. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have serrated edges.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing food for wildlife.
