Pleurothallis barbacenensis var. albiflora

Pleurothallis barbacenensis var. albiflora, commonly known as the white-flowered pleurothallis, is a species of orchid native to the cloud forests of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and small, clustered pseudobulbs. The flowers are typically white or pale yellow, with intricate patterns on the petals and sepals. It prefers shaded, humid environments and requires well-draining, acidic soil for optimal growth.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
