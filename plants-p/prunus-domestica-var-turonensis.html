Prunus domestica var. turonensis

Prunus domestica var. turonensis, commonly known as the Turkish plum or Turkish cherry, is a variant of the European plum.

It is characterized by its small, sweet, and aromatic fruits that are typically harvested in late summer. The plant is a deciduous shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Its botanical features include lance-shaped leaves and white or pink flowers that bloom in spring.
