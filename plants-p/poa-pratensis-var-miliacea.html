Poa pratensis var. miliacea

Poa pratensis var. miliacea, commonly known as sheep's fescue, is a perennial grass species native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its fine, dark green leaves and dense, tufted growth habit. This variety is well adapted to temperate climates and thrives in well-drained soils. It is often used in turfgrass mixtures due to its durability and aesthetic appeal.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny, wind-dispersed seeds.
