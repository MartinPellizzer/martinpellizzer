Pachystoma senile

Pachystoma senile, commonly known as the old man's beard, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of South Africa.

It is characterized by its long, hanging clusters of pale green, fleshy leaves that resemble a beard. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the winter months, adding a subtle ornamental touch. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Due to its slow growth and unique appearance, it is often cultivated as a decorative plant in rocky gardens.
