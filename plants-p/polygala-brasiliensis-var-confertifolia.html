Polygala brasiliensis var. confertifolia

Polygala brasiliensis var. confertifolia, commonly known as the Brazilian milkweed, is a perennial shrub native to South America.

It features small, delicate flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, arranged in dense clusters. The plant has hairy, branching stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often glandular. It is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and is adapted to full sun to partial shade.

Botanically, it is part of the Polygalaceae family, which includes a diverse group of flowering plants.
