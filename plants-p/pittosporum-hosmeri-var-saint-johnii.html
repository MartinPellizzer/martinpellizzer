Pittosporum hosmeri var. saint-johnii

Pittosporum hosmeri var. saint-johnii, commonly known as the Saint John's Pittosporum, is a shrub or small tree native to New Zealand.

It features glossy, dark green leaves that are elliptical in shape and often have a slightly wavy margin. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers that are fragrant and attract pollinators such as bees and birds. Following flowering, it develops small, red, berry-like fruits that are edible for some bird species.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and seasonal blooms.
