Passiflora pseudosuberosa

Passiflora pseudosuberosa, commonly known as the silky passionflower, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, intricate flowers that feature a unique blend of colors and structures, including a crown of filaments and a central corona. The plant produces edible, tropical fruits that are often used in local cuisines. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped and covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Passiflora pseudosuberosa is also valued for its ornamental qualities and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
