Phyllostachys maudiae

Phyllostachys maudiae, commonly known as the Maudie bamboo, is a species of grass in the family Poaceae.

It is native to China and is characterized by its tall, slender culms that can grow up to 10 meters in height. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically green and smooth. It is a clump-forming bamboo that is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ease of growth.

Phyllostachys maudiae is well-suited for garden landscapes and can thrive in a variety of soil types.
