Populus monilifera

Populus monilifera, commonly known as the African poplar, is a deciduous tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and distinctive, clustered inflorescences. The plant has a robust root system that allows it to thrive in various soil types and climates. It produces small, yellowish flowers that are wind-pollinated, and its seeds are dispersed by wind.

Populus monilifera is often used in reforestation projects due to its fast growth and adaptability.
