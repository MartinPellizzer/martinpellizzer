Polygala rubella

Polygala rubella, commonly known as the red milk-vetch, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, often pink or red, spurred flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. Its leaves are usually alternate and pinnately compound, with small, oval-shaped leaflets.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
