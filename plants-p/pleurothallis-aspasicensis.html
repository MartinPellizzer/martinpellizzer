Pleurothallis aspasicensis

Pleurothallis aspasicensis, commonly known as the aspasicensis orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that often display a range of colors, including white, yellow, and pink. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are usually narrow and arranged in a rosette pattern, aiding in moisture retention.

Pleurothallis aspasicensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
