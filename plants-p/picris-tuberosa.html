Picris tuberosa

Picris tuberosa, commonly known as the yellow rattle, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has a hairy, upright stem and produces small, oval seeds in its flower heads. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in meadows and along roadsides.

Its root system is tuberous, which helps it survive in dry conditions.
