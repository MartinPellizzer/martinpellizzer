Poa glabriflora var. sericea

Poa glabriflora var. sericea, commonly known as silky bluegrass, is a variant of the bluegrass species native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its fine, silky-textured leaves and compact growth habit. The plant typically forms dense, low-growing sods, making it well-suited for erosion control and ornamental landscaping. Its flowers are small and spike-like, appearing in early summer, and it is often found in dry, open habitats.

This variety is valued for its drought tolerance and ability to thrive in poor soils.
