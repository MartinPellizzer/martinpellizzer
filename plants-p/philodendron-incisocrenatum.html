Philodendron incisocrenatum

Philodendron incisocrenatum, commonly known as the serrated-leaf philodendron, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, deeply lobed leaves that are often a vibrant green with a slightly waxy texture. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, clinging to tree trunks in its natural habitat. Its leaves are known for their intricate venation patterns and ability to retain moisture.

It is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
