Podostemum fruticulosum

Podostemum fruticulosum, commonly known as the hairy podostemum, is a unique aquatic plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It typically grows in shallow, fast-flowing streams and rivers, where it attaches itself to rocks using its specialized roots. The plant has small, opposite leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, aiding in water retention and reducing erosion. Its flowers are borne in clusters and are usually white or pale yellow, attracting pollinators such as bees and flies.

Podostemum fruticulosum plays an important ecological role in its habitat by stabilizing riverbanks and providing food for various aquatic organisms.
