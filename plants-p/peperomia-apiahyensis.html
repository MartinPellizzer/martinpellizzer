Peperomia apiahyensis

Peperomia apiahyensis, commonly known as the Apiahy Valley peperomia, is a species of flowering plant native to the humid lowland forests of Ecuador.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its compact, evergreen foliage and small, clustered inflorescences. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 2 meters, with lance-shaped leaves that are glossy and dark green. It thrives in shaded environments and requires high humidity and well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and potentially endangered species.
