Pseuderanthemum grandiflorum var. pluriflorum

Pseuderanthemum grandiflorum var. pluriflorum, commonly known as the many-flowered pseuderanthemum, is a species of flowering plant in the Acanthaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, and thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant is characterized by its opposite, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds, facilitating its propagation.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
