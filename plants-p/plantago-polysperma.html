Plantago polysperma

Plantago polysperma, commonly known as the common plantain, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It features a rosette of broad, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs, and it produces erect flowering stems with small, greenish-white flowers. The plant is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields. Its seeds are dispersed by wind, and the plant has been used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.

The plant's robust nature and adaptability make it a common sight in many temperate regions.
