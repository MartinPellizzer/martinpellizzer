Pachystigma caffrum

Pachystigma caffrum, commonly known as the Natal grass or Natal flat sedge, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and robust rhizomatous growth, which allows it to thrive in a variety of soil types. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained environments such as riverbanks and wetlands. Its leaves are narrow and grassy, with a fibrous texture, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters.

Pachystigma caffrum is often used in ornamental gardening for its hardy nature and ability to form dense ground cover.
