Pinanga chaiana

Pinanga chaiana, commonly known as the bamboo palm, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Arecaceae family and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and feather-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often in tropical rainforests. Its rhizomatous root system allows it to spread and form dense clumps.

Pinanga chaiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated as a houseplant due to its adaptability and aesthetic qualities.
