Parrya asperrima

Parrya asperrima, commonly known as the roughleaf parrya, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its hairy, rough-textured leaves and small, clustered flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant typically grows in moist, rocky habitats such as stream banks and woodland edges. It has a compact growth habit and is often found in montane regions.

Parrya asperrima is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
