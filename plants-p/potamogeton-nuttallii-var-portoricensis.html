Potamogeton nuttallii var. portoricensis

Potamogeton nuttallii var. portoricensis, commonly known as the Puerto Rican pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to the freshwater ecosystems of Puerto Rico.

It has long, slender leaves that are typically arranged in whorls around the stem, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often submerged or just at the water's surface. The plant thrives in shallow, slow-moving waters and plays a role in providing habitat for aquatic invertebrates and fish.

Its presence is an indicator of good water quality and is important for maintaining the ecological balance of its native wetlands.
