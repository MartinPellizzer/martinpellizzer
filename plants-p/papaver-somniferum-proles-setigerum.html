Papaver somniferum proles setigerum

Papaver somniferum proles setigerum, commonly known as the opium poppy, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking blue or purple flowers and capsule-like seed pods that contain latex-rich cells. The plant's botanical features include a taproot system, palmate leaves, and a tall, erect stem. The latex produced in its stems and leaves is the source of opium and its derivatives.

This species has significant historical and medicinal importance due to its alkaloid-rich properties.
