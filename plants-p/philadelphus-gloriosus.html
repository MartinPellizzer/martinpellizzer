Philadelphus gloriosus

Philadelphus gloriosus, commonly known as the glorybower, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, fragrant white to yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are typically dark green and have a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

The glorybower is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
