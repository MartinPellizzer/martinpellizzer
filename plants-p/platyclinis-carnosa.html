Platyclinis carnosa

Platyclinis carnosa, commonly known as the velvetleaf or velvetleaf hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its large, velvety leaves that are typically green with a soft texture. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red, which bloom throughout the growing season. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

Platyclinis carnosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking foliage and vibrant blooms.
