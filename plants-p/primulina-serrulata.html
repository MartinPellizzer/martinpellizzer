Primulina serrulata

Primulina serrulata, commonly known as the serrated primula, is a species of flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Gesneriaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and strikingly ornamental foliage. The plant produces small, tubular flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red, often clustered in racemes. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated as a houseplant due to its aesthetic appeal and relatively easy care requirements.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for both horticultural and conservation purposes.
