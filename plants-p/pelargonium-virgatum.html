Pelargonium virgatum

Pelargonium virgatum, commonly known as the silver geranium, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub, reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
