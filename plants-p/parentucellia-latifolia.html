Parentucellia latifolia

Parentucellia latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf parentucellia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its large, broad leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery pubescence. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and by horticultural enthusiasts.
