Pandanus kerstingii

Pandanus kerstingii, commonly known as the Kersting's pandanus, is a flowering plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It is a member of the Pandanaceae family and is characterized by its large, feather-like leaves that can grow up to several meters in length. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically yellow or green, followed by fruiting structures called syncarps. It thrives in coastal and wetland environments, often growing in sandy or muddy soils.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional uses in various island cultures.
