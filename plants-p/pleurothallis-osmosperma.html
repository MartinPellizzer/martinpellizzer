Pleurothallis osmosperma

Pleurothallis osmosperma, commonly known as the fragrant orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that often emit a sweet fragrance. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to tree bark in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and arranged in a spiral pattern around the stem.

The flowers are usually white or pale yellow, with a distinct lip that aids in pollination by attracting specific insects.
