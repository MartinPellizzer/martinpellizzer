Podocarpus endlicherianus

Podocarpus endlicherianus, commonly known as the Christmas tree or common podocarp, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Podocarpaceae and is characterized by its dense, pyramidal shape and needle-like leaves. The tree produces small, fleshy cones that are typically red or orange in color, which are an important food source for various wildlife species. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.

The plant is also valued for its durable wood, which is used in construction and furniture-making.
