Pedicularis kansuensis subsp. kokonorica

Pedicularis kansuensis subsp.

kokonorica, commonly known as the Kansu lousewort, is a perennial herb native to the Qinghai-Tibet Plateau. It is characterized by its upright, branched stems and showy, spurred flowers that range in color from yellow to orange-red.

The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky habitats at high elevations. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
