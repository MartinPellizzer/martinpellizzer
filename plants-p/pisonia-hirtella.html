Pisonia hirtella

Pisonia hirtella, commonly known as the hairy pisonia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It is a small to medium-sized shrub or tree that typically grows in coastal areas and dry forests. The plant is characterized by its hairy stems and clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release tiny, wind-dispersed seeds.

Pisonia hirtella is often valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
