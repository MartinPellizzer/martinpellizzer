Potentilla festiva

Potentilla festiva, commonly known as the festival cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically reaches heights of about 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features bright yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, often in clusters of five, giving it its common name. Its leaves are palmately divided into five to seven lobes, and the stems are often covered in fine hairs.

Potentilla festiva thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and rocky areas.
