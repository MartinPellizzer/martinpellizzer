Pinus oocarpa subsp. manzanoi

Pinus oocarpa subsp.

manzanoi, commonly known as the Manzano pine, is a species native to southern Mexico. It is a medium-sized evergreen conifer with needle-like leaves that are typically arranged in fascicles of five.

The plant produces cones that are woody and often remain on the tree for several years. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to a variety of elevations.

The botanical characteristics of this subspecies make it an important component of its native ecosystems.
