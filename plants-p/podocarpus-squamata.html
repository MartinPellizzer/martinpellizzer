Podocarpus squamata

Podocarpus squamata, commonly known as the silver pine, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, needle-like foliage that gives it a distinctive silvery appearance. The tree produces small, spherical cones that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Its roots are deep and strong, allowing it to thrive in well-drained soils.

Podocarpus squamata is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and adaptability to various climates.
