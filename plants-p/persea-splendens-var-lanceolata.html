Persea splendens var. lanceolata

Persea splendens var. lanceolata, commonly known as the Mexican mahogany or lanceleaf avocado, is a tropical evergreen tree native to southern Mexico.

It features broad, lance-shaped leaves that are dark green and glossy, providing dense shade. The tree produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, which bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a large, elongated berry with a thick, leathery skin that turns from green to yellow or orange when ripe.

The plant is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens and as a shade tree in warm climates.
