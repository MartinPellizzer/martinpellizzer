Patagonium villosum var. acutifolia

Patagonium villosum var. acutifolia, commonly known as the hairy Patagonian grass, is a perennial plant native to the temperate regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and slender, wiry stems covered in fine, soft hairs. The leaves are typically narrow and grass-like, with a slightly hairy texture, and the plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in early spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands and along riverbanks.

This plant plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small invertebrates.
