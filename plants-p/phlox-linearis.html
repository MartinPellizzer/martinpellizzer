Phlox linearis

Phlox linearis, commonly known as narrowleaf phlox, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It features slender, upright stems that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of showy, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and linear, giving it a grass-like appearance.

Phlox linearis thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.
