Podocarpus nucifer

Podocarpus nucifer, commonly known as the Indian Podocarp, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to the Western Ghats of India.

It belongs to the Podocarpaceae family and is characterized by its dense, needle-like leaves that are dark green in color. The plant produces small, spherical cones that contain edible seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in mixed forests.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in reforestation efforts due to its ecological importance.
