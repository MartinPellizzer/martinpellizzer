Poponax cymbispina

Poponax cymbispina, commonly known as the spiny cyme poponax, is a shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny stems and clusters of small, fragrant flowers. The plant produces small, round, yellowish fruits that are often used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a slightly serrated edge.

Poponax cymbispina is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
