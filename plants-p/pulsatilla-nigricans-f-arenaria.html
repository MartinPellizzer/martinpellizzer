Pulsatilla nigricans f. arenaria

Pulsatilla nigricans f. arenaria, commonly known as the sand pulsatilla, is a variant of the pasque flower characterized by its compact growth habit and pale yellow to white flowers.

It is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, particularly thriving in rocky and sandy soils. The plant features a short, thick rootstock with several narrow, hairy stems that bear clusters of showy, cup-shaped blooms. Its leaves are basal, spoon-shaped, and covered in a dense layer of silky hairs.

This subspecies is adapted to harsh alpine environments, demonstrating resilience to cold and arid conditions.
