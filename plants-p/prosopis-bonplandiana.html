Prosopis bonplandiana

Prosopis bonplandiana, commonly known as the yellow burr acacia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it drought-tolerant and suitable for reclamation of degraded lands.

Its pods contain seeds that are dispersed by wind, and it plays a role in soil stabilization and provides forage for livestock.
