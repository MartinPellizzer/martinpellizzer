Potentilla cinerea var. clementei

Potentilla cinerea var. clementei, commonly known as silver cinquefoil, is a low-growing perennial shrub native to the western United States.

It features silver-gray, hairy leaves that are oval to elliptic in shape, and it produces small, yellow flowers in early summer. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or arid environments. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.

The variety clementei is distinguished by its more compact form and slightly different leaf morphology compared to the typical species.
