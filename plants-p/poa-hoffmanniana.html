Poa hoffmanniana

Poa hoffmanniana, commonly known as Hoffmann's bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, blue-green blades that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences with pale blue to greenish flowers, which are typical of the Poaceae family. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
