Phaseolus linearis var. genuinus

Phaseolus linearis var. genuinus, commonly known as the scarlet runner bean, is a climbing legume native to the Americas.

It features elongated, bean-like pods that turn from green to red as they mature, often containing edible seeds. The plant has compound leaves with oval-shaped leaflets and produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of pink or white. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is valued for both its ornamental appeal and its culinary use.
