Physalis quillabambensis

Physalis quillabambensis, commonly known as the Andean groundcherry, is a species native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its hairy stems and broad, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, yellow-orange fruits enclosed in a papery calyx, resembling a lantern. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in moist, shaded environments.

The fruit is edible and has a tangy, sweet flavor, making it a unique addition to local cuisines.
