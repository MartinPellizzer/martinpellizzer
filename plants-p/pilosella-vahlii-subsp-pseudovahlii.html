Pilosella vahlii subsp. pseudovahlii

Pilosella vahlii subsp.

pseudovahlii, commonly known as the slenderleaf mouse-ear, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family. It is characterized by its small, hairy leaves and yellow composite flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

This subspecies is native to southern Europe and is often found in dry, open habitats such as rocky slopes and grasslands. It has a compact growth habit and is adapted to thrive in nutrient-poor soils.

The plant is of interest to botanists due to its morphological distinctiveness and ecological niche.
