Penstemon smallii var. calycosus

Penstemon smallii var. calycosus, commonly known as the Sierra penstemon, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to purple. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it well-suited for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are evergreen in mild climates.
