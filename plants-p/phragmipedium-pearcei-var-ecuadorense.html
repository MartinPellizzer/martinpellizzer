Phragmipedium pearcei var. ecuadorense

Phragmipedium pearcei var. ecuadorense, commonly known as the Ecuadorian phragmipedium, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to the cloud forests of Ecuador.

It is a terrestrial orchid characterized by its elongated, strap-like leaves and striking, spurred flowers that typically bloom in shades of yellow and orange. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments with high air circulation and moderate temperatures. Its roots are often mycorrhizal, forming symbiotic relationships with fungi to enhance nutrient uptake.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts for its unique floral display.
