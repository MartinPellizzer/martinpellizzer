Phyllanthus adenodiscus

Phyllanthus adenodiscus, commonly known as the African nightshade, is a shrub native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its small, green, egg-shaped leaves. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in catkins, and it bears small, fleshy fruits that are often used in traditional medicine.

Phyllanthus adenodiscus is known for its adaptability to various soil types and can thrive in both wet and dry conditions.
