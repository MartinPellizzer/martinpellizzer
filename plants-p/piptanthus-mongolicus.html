Piptanthus mongolicus

Piptanthus mongolicus, commonly known as the Mongolian broom, is a deciduous shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces yellow flowers in clusters during the spring. The plant is adapted to harsh environments, with deep roots that help it survive in dry conditions. It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.

Its seeds are dispersed by wind, contributing to its spread in suitable habitats.
