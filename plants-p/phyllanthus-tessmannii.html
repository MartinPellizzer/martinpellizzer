Phyllanthus tessmannii

Phyllanthus tessmannii, commonly known as the African starleaf, is a small shrub native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant.
