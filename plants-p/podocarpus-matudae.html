Podocarpus matudae

Podocarpus matudae, commonly known as the Matudae podocarp, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Podocarpaceae family and is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and aromatic resinous scent. The tree produces small, cone-like structures that contain its seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in mountainous forests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
