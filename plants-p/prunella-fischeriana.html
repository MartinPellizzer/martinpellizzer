Prunella fischeriana

Prunella fischeriana, commonly known as Fischer's deadnettle, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs. The plant produces small, two-lipped flowers that are typically purple or blue in color. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture.

It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.
