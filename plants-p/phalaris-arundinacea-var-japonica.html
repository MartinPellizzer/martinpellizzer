Phalaris arundinacea var. japonica

Phalaris arundinacea var. japonica, commonly known as Japanese bamboo reed, is a perennial grass native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can grow up to 2 meters in height. The plant produces feathery, spike-like inflorescences that are typically green to purplish in color. Its leaves are long, narrow, and often have a slight sheen.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appearance and is often used in landscaping and as a decorative plant.
