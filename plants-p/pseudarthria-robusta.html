Pseudarthria robusta

Pseudarthria robusta, commonly known as the African vetch, is a perennial legume native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant produces elongated, cylindrical pods containing several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used for forage and soil stabilization.

Pseudarthria robusta is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, which benefits agricultural ecosystems.
