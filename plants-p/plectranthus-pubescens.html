Plectranthus pubescens

Plectranthus pubescens, commonly known as the woolly mint, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, gray-green leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its aromatic foliage and long blooming period.
