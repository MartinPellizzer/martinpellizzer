Pogostemon monticola

Pogostemon monticola, commonly known as the mountain pogostemon, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants. The plant is characterized by its square stems, opposite leaves, and small, clustered flowers. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in mountainous regions.

Its leaves are used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties.
