Pleurothallis lobata

Pleurothallis lobata, commonly known as the crested pleurothallis, is a species of orchid native to the cloud forests of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its compact, sympodial growth habit and its striking, often brightly colored flowers. The plant typically produces small, fragrant blooms in shades of white, yellow, and orange, which are borne on slender, curved pedicels. Its leaves are usually elliptical and arranged in two ranks, giving it a unique appearance.

Pleurothallis lobata is a popular choice among orchid enthusiasts due to its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
