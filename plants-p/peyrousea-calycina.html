Peyrousea calycina

Peyrousea calycina, commonly known as the velvetleaf.

It is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are velvety to the touch. Its flowers are typically yellow with purple veins, and they bloom in clusters.

Peyrousea calycina is often found in moist, shaded habitats such as rainforests and along riverbanks.
