Phagnalon murbeckii

Phagnalon murbeckii, commonly known as the creeping phagnalon, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils.

Phagnalon murbeckii is often used in ecological restoration due to its ability to stabilize soil and support local biodiversity.
