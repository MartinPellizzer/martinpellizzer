Polypodium nigrolimbatum

Polypodium nigrolimbatum, commonly known as the black-limbed maidenhair fern, is a species of fern characterized by its distinctive fronds.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia. The plant features finely divided, often leathery fronds that are typically dark green in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in humid forests.

The spores are produced on the underside of the fronds, which are released during the dry season.
