Pseudoacanthocereus boreominarum

Pseudoacanthocereus boreominarum, commonly known as the Mexican moon cactus, is a cacti species native to Mexico.

It is a small, globular plant with a distinctive appearance, featuring a colorful, fleshy upper portion and a green, spiny lower part. The plant has a shallow root system and thrives in well-draining soil. It produces yellow flowers during the summer months, adding to its ornamental value.

Due to its unique appearance and relatively easy care, it is popular among cactus enthusiasts.
