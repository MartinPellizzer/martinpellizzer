Piper sidipilum

Piper sidipilum, commonly known as the West African pepper or African long pepper, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species such as black pepper. The plant is characterized by its climbing habit, with tendrils that help it ascend supports. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a glossy surface, while the small green flowers are borne in clusters.

The fruit is a drupe that turns red when mature, containing a single seed.
