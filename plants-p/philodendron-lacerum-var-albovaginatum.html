Philodendron lacerum var. albovaginatum

Philodendron lacerum var. albovaginatum, commonly known as the White Vein Philodendron, is a tropical climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive white or pale green midrib and veins. The plant's foliage often features a striking contrast between the dark green leaf blades and the lighter veins, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires consistent moisture and high humidity to flourish.

This variety is known for its vigorous growth and ability to climb, making it an excellent addition to hanging baskets or trellises.
