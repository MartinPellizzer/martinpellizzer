Pulsatilla narcissiflora

Pulsatilla narcissiflora, commonly known as the showy pulsatilla, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its showy, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its leaves are palmately divided, and the plant produces a distinctive, woolly seed head after flowering.

Pulsatilla narcissiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its early spring blooms.
