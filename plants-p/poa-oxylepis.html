Poa oxylepis

Poa oxylepis, commonly known as the slender meadow grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and slender, wiry stems that often form dense mats. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny, spikelet-based flowers. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue, and it thrives in well-drained, open habitats.

Poa oxylepis is often found in grasslands and rocky areas, contributing to the structural diversity of its native ecosystems.
