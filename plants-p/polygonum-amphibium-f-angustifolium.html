Polygonum amphibium f. angustifolium

Polygonum amphibium f. angustifolium, commonly known as narrowleaf marsh peppergrass, is a variant of the common marsh peppergrass.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its slender, often hairy stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The leaves are typically alternate and arranged along the stem, with a prominent midrib. It produces small, clustered flowers that are usually pink or reddish in color, which bloom in late summer.

This plant thrives in wet or moist environments such as marshes, ditches, and along the edges of ponds.
