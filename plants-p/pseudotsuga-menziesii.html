Pseudotsuga menziesii

Pseudotsuga menziesii, commonly known as the Douglas fir, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to western North America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender form and distinctive cone-shaped crown. The tree has needle-like leaves that are dark green and arranged in spirals along the stems. Its bark is thick, scaly, and reddish-brown, providing protection against fire and pests.

The Douglas fir plays a vital role in forest ecosystems, offering habitat and contributing to soil stability.
