Protea acuminata

Protea acuminata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sugarbush, is a South African plant belonging to the Proteaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and striking, cone-shaped inflorescences that produce small, white to pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to Mediterranean climates with dry summers. It is often found in fynbos vegetation, which is a unique biome in South Africa.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
