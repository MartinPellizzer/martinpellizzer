Phlox maculata var. odorata

Phlox maculata var. odorata, commonly known as the coral bell, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It features dense clusters of small, showy flowers that range in color from pink to white, often with a subtle fragrance. The plant has lance-shaped, dark green leaves that are slightly hairy and arranged in whorls around the stems. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full sun.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like butterflies and bees.
