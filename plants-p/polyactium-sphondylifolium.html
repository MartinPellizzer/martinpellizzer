Polyactium sphondylifolium

Polyactium sphondylifolium, commonly known as the rock violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features upright, branching stems covered with soft, hairy foliage. The plant produces small, violet-blue flowers in clusters during the spring and summer months. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found growing in rocky or gravelly habitats.
