Pouteria paraensis

Pouteria paraensis, commonly known as the Brazilian pouteria, is a tropical tree native to the Amazon rainforest.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its large, glossy, elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by edible, sweet, and creamy fruits. Its fruit is often used in local cuisines and has a unique flavor profile.

The tree is also valued for its hard, durable wood, which is used in construction and furniture-making.
