Psoralea polyphylla

Psoralea polyphylla, commonly known as the many-leaved psoralea, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is often found in grassland or shrubland habitats. It has small, stipitate leaves that are arranged in a pinnate pattern, and its pods are slender and dehiscent.

Psoralea polyphylla is also known for its medicinal properties, with some traditional uses in herbal remedies.
