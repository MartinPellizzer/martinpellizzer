Padus serotina f. salicifolia

Padus serotina f. salicifolia, commonly known as the silverleaf whitebeam, is a rare and distinctive variant of the whitebeam species.

It is characterized by its unique, silvery-gray foliage, which gives it a striking appearance in both woodland and garden settings. The leaves are typically ovate to elliptic in shape, with a smooth or slightly serrated margin. This form is often found in shaded, moist environments, where it thrives in partial to full shade.

Its ornamental value makes it a sought-after plant for specialized horticultural collections.
