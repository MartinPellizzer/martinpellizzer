Pachycladon cheesemanii

Pachycladon cheesemanii, commonly known as the Cheeseman's pittosporum, is a shrub or small tree native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has smooth, greyish-brown bark and produces small, fleshy berries that are edible for birds. It thrives in coastal and lowland areas, preferring well-drained soils and full sun.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is often cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
