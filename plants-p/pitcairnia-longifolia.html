Pitcairnia longifolia

Pitcairnia longifolia, commonly known as the long-flowered pitcairnia, is a species of flowering plant in the Bromeliaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that form a dense, water-holding cup. Its showy, long-lasting inflorescence features bright red or orange flowers that attract pollinators.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking appearance and relatively low maintenance requirements.
