Padus

Padus, commonly known as the whitebeam, is a genus of deciduous trees and shrubs belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its small, white or pink flowers that bloom in spring, followed by small, edible berries in the summer. The leaves are typically oval or elliptical, with a smooth or slightly toothed margin. Padus species are native to Europe, western Asia, and northern Africa, often found in woodlands and rocky areas.

These plants are valued for their ornamental qualities and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
