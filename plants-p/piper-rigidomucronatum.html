Piper rigidomucronatum

Piper rigidomucronatum, commonly known as the rigid-tipped pepper plant, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil and Peru. The plant is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and small, heart-shaped leaves. Its flowers are unisexual, with separate male and female flowers on the same plant, a feature known as monoecious.

The fruit is a small, three-lobed berry that contains several seeds.
