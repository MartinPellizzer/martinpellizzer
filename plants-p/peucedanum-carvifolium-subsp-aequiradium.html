Peucedanum carvifolium subsp. aequiradium

Peucedanum carvifolium subsp.

aequiradium, commonly known as the alpine caraway, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It features slender, branched stems and finely divided, feathery leaves that give it a delicate appearance.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in umbels, typical of the Apiaceae family. Its seeds are small and oval, often used in traditional medicine for their aromatic properties.

This subspecies is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments and is valued for its unique fragrance and potential medicinal uses.
