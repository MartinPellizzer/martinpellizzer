Papyrus tuberifer

Papyrus tuberifer, commonly known as the water papyrus, is a flowering plant native to wetland areas of Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, reed-like stems and large, feather-shaped leaves that can grow up to several meters in length. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are clustered in spikes, typically blooming during the rainy season. Its rhizomes, which are thick and fleshy, are used in traditional medicine and as a source of food in some regions.

The plant plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various aquatic species.
