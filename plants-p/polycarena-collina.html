Polycarena collina

Polycarena collina, commonly known as the mountain sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or meadow habitats. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets. It is often found in mountainous or high-altitude environments.

Polycarena collina plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
