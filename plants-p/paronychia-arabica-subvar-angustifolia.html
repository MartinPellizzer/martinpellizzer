Paronychia arabica subvar. angustifolia

Paronychia arabica subvar. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf ice plant, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a succulent species characterized by its fleshy, cylindrical stems and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, storing water in its thick, fleshy leaves. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

This species is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its hardy nature and attractive appearance.
