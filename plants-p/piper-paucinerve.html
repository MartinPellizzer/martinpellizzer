Piper paucinerve

Piper paucinerve, commonly known as the slender pipevine, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes numerous species of flowering plants. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually ovate to elliptic in shape. Its small, greenish-white flowers are borne in clusters and are followed by elongated, berry-like fruits.

The plant is often found in humid, shaded environments such as forests and thickets.
