Phaseolus oblongus var. carneus

Phaseolus oblongus var. carneus, commonly known as the pink runner bean, is a variant of the runner bean species native to South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive pink or violet-colored pods and vibrant green foliage. The plant is a climbing vine that produces edible beans, which are often used in culinary applications. Botanically, it belongs to the Fabaceae family and is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil through symbiotic relationships with rhizobia bacteria.

Its flowers are typically white or pale pink, adding ornamental value to gardens.
