Poa acroleuca var. ryukyuensis

Poa acroleuca var. ryukyuensis, commonly known as Ryukyu bentgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the Ryukyu Islands of Japan.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories. Its inflorescence consists of a loose cluster of spikelets, and it produces small, wind-dispersed seeds.

Botanically, it is distinguished by its specific morphology and geographic distribution within the Poaceae family.
