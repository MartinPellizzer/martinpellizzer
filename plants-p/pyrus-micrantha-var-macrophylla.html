Pyrus micrantha var. macrophylla

Pyrus micrantha var. macrophylla, commonly known as the large-leafed whitebeam, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves that are often hairy on the underside, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clusters, which are followed by edible, sweet-sour berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
