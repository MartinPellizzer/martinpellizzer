Prunus armeniaca var. variegata

Prunus armeniaca var. variegata, commonly known as the variable apricot, is a cultivar of the apricot tree known for its ornamental value and unique foliage.

It features striking variegated leaves with white and green patterns, adding visual interest to gardens. The plant produces fragrant flowers in spring, followed by edible fruits that are smaller and more tart than typical apricots. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal rather than its fruit yield.
