Pleurothallis erythrium

Pleurothallis erythrium, commonly known as the red-lipped pleurothallis, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that typically display vibrant red or orange hues on the lip. The plant grows as an epiphyte, often found clinging to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are usually narrow and grass-like, and it produces pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients.

This orchid is popular among horticulturists for its striking appearance and relatively easy care requirements.
