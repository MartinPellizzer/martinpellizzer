Panicum filiculme

Panicum filiculme, commonly known as the feather grass, is a perennial grass species native to the wetlands of North America.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and delicate, feathery inflorescence that resembles a plume. The plant typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, making it a common sight in marshy areas and along the edges of ponds. Its lightweight seeds are dispersed by wind, contributing to its ability to colonize new wetland habitats.

Panicum filiculme plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
