Parkia sumatrana

Parkia sumatrana, commonly known as the Sumatran peacock tree, is a tropical leguminous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking peacock-like flowers, which are typically bright yellow with red veins and a distinctive crown-like structure. The plant produces pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, and its leaves are bipinnate, consisting of multiple small leaflets. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The tree is valued for its ornamental beauty and has some traditional uses in local cultures.
