Paphiopedilum stonei

Paphiopedilum stonei, commonly known as the Chinese velvet slipper orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features a pseudobulb with a single, fleshy leaf and produces a single, elongated flower spike with a unique, slipper-shaped pouch. The plant has a velvety, dark green to brownish-green foliage and blooms in late spring to early summer. It prefers shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.

The orchid is prized for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to cultivate with proper care.
