Ptisana decipiens var. decipiens

Ptisana decipiens var. decipiens, commonly known as the deceptive ptisana, is a flowering plant native to parts of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single bloom. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in dense clusters. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded to partially shaded environments.

Due to its unique appearance and growth habits, it is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes in gardens.
