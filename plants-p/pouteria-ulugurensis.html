Pouteria ulugurensis

Pouteria ulugurensis, commonly known as the Uluguru oil tree, is a tropical plant native to the Uluguru Mountains in Tanzania.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree that can grow up to 15 meters tall, characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by edible, sweet, and fleshy fruits that are rich in nutrients. Its seeds contain a valuable oil used in traditional medicine and as a source of biofuel.

The species is also noted for its ecological importance in supporting local biodiversity.
