Panicum spretum var. longiligulatum

Panicum spretum var. longiligulatum, commonly known as the long-ligulate grass, is a perennial grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, linear leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces panicles of small, spikelet-based flowers, which are often greenish or reddish in color. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in swamps, marshes, and along the edges of ponds.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in wetland ecosystems.
