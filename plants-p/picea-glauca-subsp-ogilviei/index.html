Picea Glauca Subsp. Ogilviei

Picea glauca subsp. ogilviei is a rare and hardy variant of the white spruce tree. Native to the mountainous regions of western canada, it thrives in cold climates and well-drained soils. This subspecies is known for its dense foliage and distinctive blue-green needles. It plays an important role in local ecosystems and is valued for its ornamental qualities. Due to its limited distribution, conservation efforts are crucial for its survival.
