Piratinera utilis

Piratinera utilis, commonly known as the Andean pomegranate, is a shrub native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its evergreen leaves, which are leathery and lance-shaped, and its clusters of small, red, fleshy fruits. The plant produces edible seeds surrounded by a juicy, sweet pulp, similar to true pomegranates. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit.

The botanical features of Piratinera utilis make it a unique and valuable species in both horticulture and ecological contexts.
