Pseudopteryxia anisata

Pseudopteryxia anisata, commonly known as the slender-winged orchid, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped leaves and small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest understories or along stream banks. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb nutrients from the soil, while its aerial roots help in moisture retention.

The species is noted for its unique wing-like structures on its petals, which contribute to its ornamental appeal.
