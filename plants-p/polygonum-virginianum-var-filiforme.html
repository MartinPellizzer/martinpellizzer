Polygonum virginianum var. filiforme

Polygonum virginianum var. filiforme, commonly known as slender knotweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It features slender, creeping stems that often root at the nodes, forming dense mats. The leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, with a reddish tinge when young. It produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters, which bloom in late summer.

This plant thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found along stream banks and in wet meadows.
