Pentaphorus glutinosus

Pentaphorus glutinosus, commonly known as the sticky sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland and marsh environments in North America.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets. The plant is notable for its sticky secretions on the leaves and stems, which can trap insects and contribute to its ecological role. It thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils and is often found in areas with standing water.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
