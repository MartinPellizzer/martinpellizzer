Pluchea lanuginosa

Pluchea lanuginosa, commonly known as silkyleaf ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its soft, hairy leaves and clusters of small yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. Its flowers bloom in the summer, attracting various pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The plant has been used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory properties.
