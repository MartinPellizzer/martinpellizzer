Pyrus pinnatifida var. arbuscula

Pyrus pinnatifida var. arbuscula, commonly known as the Japanese white pear, is a variant of the white pear species native to Japan.

It is a deciduous tree characterized by its small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The tree produces small, white flowers in early spring, which are followed by sweet, aromatic fruit in late summer. The fruit is usually harvested when it turns yellow or reddish, and it is prized for its culinary and ornamental value.

This variety is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal and edible yield.
