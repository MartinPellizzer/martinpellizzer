Pouteria polyneura

Pouteria polyneura, commonly known as the Brazilian nut tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into elongated, woody pods. The tree's most notable feature is its edible seeds, commonly referred to as Brazil nuts, which are enclosed within the hard, fibrous pods. It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and requires a warm climate with high rainfall.

The plant plays a significant ecological role, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
