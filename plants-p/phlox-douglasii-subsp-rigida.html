Phlox douglasii subsp. rigida

Phlox douglasii subsp.

rigida, commonly known as stiff phlox, is a perennial herb native to the western United States. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and dense clusters of showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and native plant landscapes.

Its botanical structure supports its role as a valuable nectar source for pollinators.
