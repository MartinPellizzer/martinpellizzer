Pogostemon williamsii

Pogostemon williamsii, commonly known as the Williams' pogostemon, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants such as mint and basil. The plant is characterized by its square, hairy stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are small, tubular, and typically violet or purple in color, arranged in whorls along the stem.

Pogostemon williamsii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has a mild, mint-like scent.
