Pisonia luteovirens

Pisonia luteovirens, commonly known as the yellow-green pisonia, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to the Caribbean and parts of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its showy, clustered flowers that range in color from yellow to green, which are typically pollinated by birds and insects. The plant produces small, woody fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by animals. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a glossy appearance.

Pisonia luteovirens is often found in coastal areas and is adapted to sandy or limestone soils.
