Phagnalon bennettii

Phagnalon bennettii, commonly known as Bennett's phagnalon, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, yellowish composite flowers that bloom in summer. It is native to parts of Europe and has been introduced to other regions, including North America.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural areas.
