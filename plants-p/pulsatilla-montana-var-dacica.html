Pulsatilla montana var. dacica

Pulsatilla montana var. dacica, commonly known as the Caucasian pasqueflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus.

It is a member of the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to cold, alpine environments. Its leaves are palmately divided and covered in a fine, downy hair, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens with well-drained soil and full sun.
