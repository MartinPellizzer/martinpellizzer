Phymatosorus scolopendria

Phymatosorus scolopendria, commonly known as the zigzag huperzia, is a fern belonging to the family Huperziaceae.

It is characterized by its distinctive, horizontally branching rhizome and its lance-shaped, serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often in temperate regions. Its spores are produced in clusters on the underside of its fronds, which are arranged in a unique zigzag pattern.

This species is notable for its primitive morphology and is often found in rocky or forested habitats.
