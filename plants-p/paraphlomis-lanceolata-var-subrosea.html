Paraphlomis lanceolata var. subrosea

Paraphlomis lanceolata var. subrosea, commonly known as the rose-flowered arrowroot, is a perennial herb native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It features upright, branched stems covered with soft, hairy foliage. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, giving it its common name. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly serrated edge.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often used in ornamental gardening for its attractive foliage and delicate blooms.
