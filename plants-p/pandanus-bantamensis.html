Pandanus bantamensis

Pandanus bantamensis, commonly known as the Bantam screw pine, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, spirally arranged leaves that form a dense, umbrella-like canopy. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or green in color. Its fruit is a fibrous, spiky structure that contains edible seeds.

Pandanus bantamensis is often used in traditional crafts and is also valued for its ornamental appeal.
