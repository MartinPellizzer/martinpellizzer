Pithecellobium dulce

Pithecellobium dulce, commonly known as the sweet acacia, is a tropical leguminous plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically trifoliate or pinnate, and its clusters of small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to yellow. The plant produces small, edible pods containing sweet, sticky seeds that are sometimes used in traditional cuisine. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry forest or savanna habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and edible seeds, it is cultivated in some regions for both ornamental and culinary purposes.
