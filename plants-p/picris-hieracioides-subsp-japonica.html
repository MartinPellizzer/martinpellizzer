Picris hieracioides subsp. japonica

Picris hieracioides subsp.

japonica, commonly known as Japanese goat's beard, is a flowering plant native to East Asia. It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

The plant features slender, branched stems and produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in a cluster. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often have a hairy texture.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
