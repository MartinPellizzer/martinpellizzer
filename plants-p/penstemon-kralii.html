Penstemon kralii

Penstemon kralii, commonly known as Kral's beardtongue, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, spindly stems and clusters of tubular, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant produces distinctive, showy bracts that resemble a beard, giving it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

Penstemon kralii is also valued for its ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
