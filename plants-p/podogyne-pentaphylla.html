Podogyne pentaphylla

Podogyne pentaphylla, commonly known as the five-leafed podogyne, is a rare and striking plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its unique, palm-like appearance with five broad, fleshy leaves that radiate from a central stem. The plant produces striking purple and white flowers, which bloom in the early spring. Its leaves are succulent and adapted to store water, allowing it to thrive in arid and cold environments.

Podogyne pentaphylla is often found in rocky soils and is considered an indicator species of alpine ecosystems.
