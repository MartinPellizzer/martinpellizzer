Pisum commune subsp. sativum

Pisum commune subsp.

sativum, commonly known as the common pea, is a flowering plant in the Fabaceae family. It is characterized by its climbing habit, with tendrils that help it ascend supports.

The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are the edible part. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three leaflets, and it has compound flowers with five petals.

This species is widely cultivated for its nutritional seeds, which are rich in protein and fiber.
