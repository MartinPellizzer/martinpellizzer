Picea excelsa f. inversa

Picea excelsa f. inversa, commonly known as the inverted spruce, is a unique variant of the white spruce.

It is characterized by its distinctive upward-curving needles and a more open, spreading growth habit compared to the typical upright form. This form is often found in high-altitude environments where wind exposure is significant. The plant exhibits a slower growth rate and is adapted to harsh mountain conditions.

Its botanical interest lies in its morphological variation, making it a subject of study in plant morphology and adaptation.
