Poa acutiglumis

Poa acutiglumis, commonly known as the acute-leaved bluegrass, is a species of grass native to North America.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like foliage and compact growth habit, often forming dense mats in suitable habitats. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils, often found in meadows, woodlands, and along stream banks. Its leaves are narrow and have a distinctive acute tip, giving it its common name.

Poa acutiglumis is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
