Pyrus communis var. medioxima

Pyrus communis var. medioxima, commonly known as the common pear, is a cultivar of the European pear species.

It is characterized by its moderate growth habit and rounded, dark green leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers in early spring, which are followed by sweet, juicy fruits in late summer. The fruit is typically medium-sized with a thin, smooth skin and a sweet to subacid flavor.

It is widely cultivated for its edible fruit and ornamental value in gardens and orchards.
