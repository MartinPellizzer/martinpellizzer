Paullinia densiflora

Paullinia densiflora, commonly known as the dense-flower piper, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to the Amazon rainforest and is characterized by its dense clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers. The plant typically grows as an erect shrub, reaching heights of up to 2 meters. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a glossy surface with prominent veins.

Paullinia densiflora is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and analgesic effects.
