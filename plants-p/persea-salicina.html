Persea salicina

Persea salicina, commonly known as the Mexican persimmon, is a small evergreen tree native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant flowers. The tree produces small, orange-yellow berries that are edible when fully ripe. The fruit has a sweet, honey-like flavor and is often consumed fresh or used in culinary applications.

It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is valued for both its ornamental and edible qualities.
