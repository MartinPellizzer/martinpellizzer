Primula ambigua

Primula ambigua, commonly known as the ambiguous primrose, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. The plant features lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. It blooms from late spring to early summer, and its flowers are pollinated primarily by bees and other insects.

Primula ambigua is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
