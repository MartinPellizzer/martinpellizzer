Peperomia mixtifolia

Peperomia mixtifolia, commonly known as the variegated peperomia, is a compact, evergreen perennial plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It features glossy, ovate leaves that are typically green with striking white or yellow variegation, adding visual interest to any indoor space. The plant has a short, branching stem and produces small, inconspicuous flowers in shades of green or red, though these are rarely seen in cultivation. It thrives in bright, indirect light and prefers well-draining soil with moderate humidity.

Due to its compact size and ornamental foliage, it is a popular choice for indoor gardening and container planting.
