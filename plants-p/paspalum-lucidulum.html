Paspalum lucidulum

Paspalum lucidulum, commonly known as shining grass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, narrow leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. The plant produces spikelets that are typically green or reddish in color, with small, delicate flowers. It thrives in moist to wet environments, such as marshes and stream banks.

Paspalum lucidulum is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support wildlife.
