Piper naiguatanum var. bullatifolium

Piper naiguatanum var. bullatifolium, commonly known as the Guiana pepper or bullatifolium pepper, is a member of the Piperaceae family, native to the tropical regions of South America.

This variant is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often pubescent, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers followed by elongated, slender fruits that turn from green to yellow or red as they mature. It is typically cultivated for its aromatic leaves and unique flavor profile, which is used in local cuisines and traditional medicine.

The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
