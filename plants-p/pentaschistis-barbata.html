Pentaschistis barbata

Pentaschistis barbata, commonly known as the hairy pentaschistis, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant has hairy foliage, which gives it its common name, and thrives in moist, shaded environments. It is often found in forest understories and is valued for its ornamental appeal.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is not widely cultivated outside its native range.
