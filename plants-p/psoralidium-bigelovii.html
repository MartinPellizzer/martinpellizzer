Psoralidium bigelovii

Psoralidium bigelovii, commonly known as the desert pearly everlasting, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in dense, compact clusters. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in sandy or rocky soils.

Its botanical features make it well adapted to survive in harsh desert conditions.
