Pinanga beccariana

Pinanga beccariana, commonly known as the Beccari's bamboo palm, is a flowering plant native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Arecaceae family and is characterized by its slender, clustered stems and elongated, pinnately compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into round, fleshy fruits, which are an important food source for various wildlife. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in the undergrowth of dense forests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
