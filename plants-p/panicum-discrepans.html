Panicum discrepans

Panicum discrepans, commonly known as the irregular panicgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as swamps, marshes, and along stream banks. The plant features slender, erect stems that can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters. Its foliage is narrow and grass-like, with small, spikelet-based inflorescences that produce light green to brownish seeds.

It plays a role in stabilizing wetland soils and provides habitat for various insect and bird species.
