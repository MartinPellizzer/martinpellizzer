Pisum macrocarpum

Pisum macrocarpum, commonly known as the large-seeded pea, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces yellow flowers that develop into long, cylindrical pods containing several large, round seeds. Pisum macrocarpum is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in temperate climates.

It is valued for its edible seeds, which are often used in traditional cuisines.
