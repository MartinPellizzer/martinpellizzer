Primulina crassituba

Primulina crassituba, commonly known as the broad-stemmed primula, is a species of flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Gesneriaceae and is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of pink and white, typically blooming in the spring. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated as a decorative houseplant.

Due to its ornamental value, it is popular among plant enthusiasts for its unique appearance and ease of care.
