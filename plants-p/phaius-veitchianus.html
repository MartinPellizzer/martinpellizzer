Phaius veitchianus

Phaius veitchianus, commonly known as the Veitch's marsh orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and large, showy flowers that typically bloom in summer. The plant prefers moist, shaded environments and is often found in marshy or swampy areas. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to thrive in waterlogged soils.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
