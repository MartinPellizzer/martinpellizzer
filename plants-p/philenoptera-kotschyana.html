Philenoptera kotschyana

Philenoptera kotschyana, commonly known as the Kashmir rose, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayan regions of India and Pakistan.

It is characterized by its fragrant, pink to white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has woody stems and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It thrives in cool, moist climates and requires well-drained soil.

Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture.
