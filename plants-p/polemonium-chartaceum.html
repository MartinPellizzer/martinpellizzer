Polemonium chartaceum

Polemonium chartaceum, commonly known as paperleaf bluebell, is a perennial herb native to eastern North America.

It features delicate, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue, typically blooming in late spring. The plant has slender, hairy stems and heart-shaped, chartreuse to green leaves that are often covered in a fine, papery texture. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its foliage and flowers make it a visually appealing addition to native plant gardens.
