Puccinellia qinghaica

Puccinellia qinghaica, commonly known as the Qinghai puccinellia, is a perennial grass species native to the Qinghai-Tibet Plateau.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically thrives in high-altitude environments with cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often covered in fine hairs.

Puccinellia qinghaica plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and supporting local wildlife in its native habitat.
