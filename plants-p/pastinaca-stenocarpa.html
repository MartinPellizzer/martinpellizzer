Pastinaca stenocarpa

Pastinaca stenocarpa, commonly known as the narrow-fruited scorzonera, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its hairy, erect stems and yellow composite flowers. The plant produces small, elongated fruits that are typically green when mature. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and historical use in herbal medicine, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
