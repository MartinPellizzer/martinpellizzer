Panicum pilosum var. lancifolium

Panicum pilosum var. lancifolium, commonly known as eastern dropseed, is a perennial grass native to the eastern United States.

It features slender, arching stems that produce delicate, fluffy seed heads, which are often described as resembling small clouds. The leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-gray hue due to the dense covering of fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full to partial sunlight, making it a popular choice for native plant gardens.

This plant is also valued for its ability to stabilize soil and support local wildlife.
