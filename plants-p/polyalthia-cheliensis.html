Polyalthia cheliensis

Polyalthia cheliensis, commonly known as the golden flame tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, bright yellow inflorescences that appear in clusters, giving it a fiery appearance. The plant has compound leaves with elongated, lance-shaped leaflets, and it produces small, white flowers that are often overlooked despite their fragrant nature. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The tree is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
