Poa annua var. pumila

Poa annua var. pumila, commonly known as the dwarf annual bluegrass, is a low-growing, mat-forming grass that thrives in cool, moist environments.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-green leaves and small, inconspicuous blue-green flowers. This variety is often found in lawns, meadows, and disturbed soils due to its rapid growth and adaptability. Its short stature and dense growth habit make it a common weed in turfgrass settings.

Botanically, it is distinguished by its compact inflorescence and the presence of short, rhizomatous stems.
