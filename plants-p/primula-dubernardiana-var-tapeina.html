Primula dubernardiana var. tapeina

Primula dubernardiana var. tapeina, commonly known as the tapein primrose, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of central Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces clusters of bell-shaped, violet-blue flowers. The leaves are typically broad and oval, with a slightly hairy texture. This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and rockeries.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial shade, making it suitable for alpine gardens.
