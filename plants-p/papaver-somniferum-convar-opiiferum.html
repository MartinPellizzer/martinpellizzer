Papaver somniferum convar. opiiferum

Papaver somniferum convar. opiiferum, commonly known as the opium poppy, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that range from white to red, and its capsule-like seed pods that contain latex. The plant is cultivated for its latex, which is the source of opium and its derivatives. The leaves are typically broad and lobed, with a rough texture.

Its botanical features make it both economically significant and botanically intriguing.
