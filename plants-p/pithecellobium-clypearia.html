Pithecellobium clypearia

Pithecellobium clypearia, commonly known as the sweet acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its seeds are encased in elongated, woody pods that split open to release the seeds.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential use in agroforestry systems.
