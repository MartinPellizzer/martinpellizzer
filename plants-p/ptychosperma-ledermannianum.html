Ptychosperma ledermannianum

Ptychosperma ledermannianum, commonly known as the Ledermann's palm, is a species of flowering plant in the Arecaceae family.

It is native to the rainforests of northeastern Australia, particularly in Queensland. The plant is a tall, slender palm with a smooth, unbranched trunk and large, pinnately compound leaves. Its flowers are small and clustered in spadices, and it produces edible, fleshy berries that are an important food source for local wildlife.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical gardens.
