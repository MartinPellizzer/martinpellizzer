Picea tschonofskiana

Picea tschonofskiana, commonly known as the Siberian pine, is a coniferous tree native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, pyramidal shape and dark green, needle-like leaves that are arranged in a spiral pattern. The tree produces small, woody cones that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in cold climates and is adapted to grow in well-drained, sandy soils.

This species is valued for its timber and is also an important component of its native ecosystems.
