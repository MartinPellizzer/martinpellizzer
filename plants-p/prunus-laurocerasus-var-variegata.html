Prunus laurocerasus var. variegata

Prunus laurocerasus var. variegata, commonly known as the Variegated Cherry Laurel, is a cultivar of the Prunus laurocerasus species, which is native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves with striking white or yellow variegation, adding ornamental value to gardens. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers in spring, followed by dark blue berries that are edible but often used as ornamental features. It is a slow-growing, evergreen shrub or small tree that thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil.

Due to its attractive foliage and adaptability, it is widely cultivated for landscaping purposes.
