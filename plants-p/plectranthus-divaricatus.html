Plectranthus divaricatus

Plectranthus divaricatus, commonly known as the purple heart plant, is a perennial herb native to South Africa.

It features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are typically purple or violet in color, with a smooth, waxy texture. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are usually pale purple or white, arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems. It thrives in well-draining soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its compact growth habit and vibrant foliage make it a versatile plant for both indoor and outdoor settings.
