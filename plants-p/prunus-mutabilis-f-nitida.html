Prunus mutabilis f. nitida

Prunus mutabilis f. nitida, commonly known as the White Flowering Cherry, is a compact, ornamental tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its dense, upright growth habit and its striking white flowers that bloom in early spring. The leaves are typically dark green and turn a vibrant red in the fall. This variety is prized for its ornamental value in gardens and as a street tree due to its adaptability and disease resistance.

It requires well-drained soil and full sun to thrive.
