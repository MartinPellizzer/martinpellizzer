Paeonia fulgida

Paeonia fulgida, commonly known as the fire pink, is a species of flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet. The plant features palmate leaves with five to nine lobes and produces showy, pink to red flowers in late spring to early summer. Its flowers are borne on tall, erect stems and are composed of numerous petal-like sepals.

Paeonia fulgida is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
