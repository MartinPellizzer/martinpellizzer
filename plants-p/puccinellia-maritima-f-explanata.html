Puccinellia maritima f. explanata

Puccinellia maritima f. explanata, commonly known as the broad-leaved sea bulrush, is a flowering plant belonging to the grass family Poaceae.

It is characterized by its wide, flat leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. This plant is typically found in coastal areas, salt marshes, and other saline environments, where it tolerates high salinity and periodic flooding. Its inflorescence consists of dense, spike-like clusters of small, two-lipped flowers.

The species plays an important ecological role in stabilizing coastal soils and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
