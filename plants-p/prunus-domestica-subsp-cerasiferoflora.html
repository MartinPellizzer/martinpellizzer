Prunus domestica subsp. cerasiferoflora

Prunus domestica subsp.

cerasiferoflora, commonly known as the sweet cherry, is a subspecies of the domesticated cherry tree native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its small, round, and typically red or dark purple fruits, which are rich in sugars and have a sweet, juicy flavor.

The plant produces white or pale pink flowers in early spring, which are followed by the development of its edible fruits. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil and full sun for optimal growth.

This subspecies is widely cultivated for both commercial and home gardening purposes due to its ornamental value and delicious fruit.
