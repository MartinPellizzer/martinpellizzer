Philodendron rhodospathiphyllum

Philodendron rhodospathiphyllum, commonly known as the pink-veined philodendron, is a tropical climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, glossy dark green leaves with striking pink veins that contrast beautifully with the foliage. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically followed by orange-red berries. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value, it is popular as a houseplant but should be handled with care as it is toxic to humans and pets.
