Pachyphyllum breviconnatum

Pachyphyllum breviconnatum, commonly known as the short-stemmed stonecress, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and small, clustered leaves that help it store water. The plant produces yellow flowers during the warmer months, which are followed by small, round fruits. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it well-suited for xerophytic environments.

Due to its unique adaptations, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant in dry climates.
