Pterophylla magnifica

Pterophylla magnifica, commonly known as the grand magnolia, is a striking plant native to tropical regions.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that can reach up to 30 centimeters in diameter. The plant features broad, lance-shaped leaves that are usually a deep green. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil.

Pterophylla magnifica is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
