Pinalia hutchinsoniana

Pinalia hutchinsoniana, commonly known as the Mexican pinyon, is a species of evergreen shrub native to the highlands of Mexico.

It belongs to the family Pinaceae, which includes true pines, and is characterized by its coniferous structure and needle-like leaves. The plant produces small, cone-like structures that contain its seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Pinalia hutchinsoniana thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to temperate to subtropical climates.

Its botanical features make it an important species in the ecological systems of its native habitat.
