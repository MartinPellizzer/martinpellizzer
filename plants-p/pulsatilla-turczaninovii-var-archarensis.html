Pulsatilla turczaninovii var. archarensis

Pulsatilla turczaninovii var. archarensis, commonly known as the Archangel pasqueflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Ranunculaceae family.

It is characterized by its showy, cup-shaped flowers that typically bloom in early spring. The plant features a short, thick rhizome and basal leaves that are palmately divided. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

Its botanical significance lies in its unique morphology and ecological adaptations to high-altitude environments.
