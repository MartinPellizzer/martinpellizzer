Polygonum conspicuum var. lutchuense

Polygonum conspicuum var. lutchuense, commonly known as the Lutchuan knotweed, is a flowering plant native to China.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, which includes species known for their distinctive bracts and clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as mountain forests and stream banks. Its leaves are ovate to lanceolate, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface, and it produces small, greenish-white flowers in dense inflorescences.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its foliage and structural interest.
