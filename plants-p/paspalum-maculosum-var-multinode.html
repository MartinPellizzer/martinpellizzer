Paspalum maculosum var. multinode

Paspalum maculosum var. multinode, commonly known as the multinode paspalum, is a grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, blade-like leaves that are often hairy along the edges. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas or along roadsides.

Its ability to form dense stands makes it both a weed and a potential forage species in certain environments.
