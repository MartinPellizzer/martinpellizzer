Pourouma guianensis

Pourouma guianensis, commonly known as the giant bamboo or Guianese bamboo, is a tall, woody plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the grass family, Poaceae, and is characterized by its slender, segmented stems that can grow up to 20 meters in height. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers and has long, narrow leaves that are often used in traditional medicine.

Its rapid growth rate and ability to thrive in wet, humid environments make it an important component of the rainforest ecosystem.
