Penstemon heterophyllus subsp. purdyi

Penstemon heterophyllus subsp.

purdyi, commonly known as the Sierra penstemon, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to violet, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy species suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
