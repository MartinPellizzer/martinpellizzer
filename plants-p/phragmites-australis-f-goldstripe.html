Phragmites australis f. goldstripe

Phragmites australis f. goldstripe, commonly known as the golden stripe reed, is a variant of the common reed that exhibits distinctive yellowish-green foliage.

It is a perennial plant native to wetlands and aquatic environments, often found in marshes, lakes, and along shorelines. The plant has tall, hollow stems that can grow up to several meters in height, and its leaves are narrow and blade-like. It plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.

Its unique coloration makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening in wetland landscapes.
