Pyrus malus proles paradisiaca

Pyrus malus proles paradisiaca, commonly known as the paradise apple tree, is a hybrid plant resulting from the crossbreeding of apple and pear species.

It is characterized by its ornamental value, featuring attractive foliage and showy flowers that bloom in early spring. The tree produces small, edible fruits with a sweet and tangy flavor, though they are not typically cultivated for commercial use. Its botanical significance lies in its role as a parent in the development of various apple and pear cultivars.

Due to its genetic diversity, it is often studied in horticultural research for its potential in breeding programs.
