Philadelphus lewisii subsp. gordonianus

Philadelphus lewisii subsp.

gordonianus, commonly known as Gordon's mock orange, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States. It features showy, fragrant flowers that bloom in early summer, typically in shades of white to pale yellow.

The plant has opposite, simple, and ovate leaves that are generally hairy beneath. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable plant for both ornamental gardens and ecological restoration projects.
