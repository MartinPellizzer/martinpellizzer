Pseudolysimachion pyrethrinum

Pseudolysimachion pyrethrinum, commonly known as the pyrethrum plant, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers. The plant is notable for its production of pyrethrin, a natural insecticide used in organic pest control. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for gardens and landscapes.

Its ornamental value and practical use in agriculture contribute to its significance in horticulture.
