Plantago macrostachys subvar. catamarcensis

Plantago macrostachys subvar. catamarcensis, commonly known as the Catamarcensis plantain, is a perennial herb native to South America, particularly Argentina and Uruguay.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. The plant produces tall, erect flowering stems with small, greenish-white flowers arranged in dense spikes. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and along riverbanks.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
