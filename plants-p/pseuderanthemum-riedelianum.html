Pseuderanthemum riedelianum

Pseuderanthemum riedelianum, commonly known as the Riedel's false jasmine, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically white or pale blue, often clustered in inflorescences. It thrives in humid, shady environments and is often found in tropical forests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and greenhouses as a decorative plant.
