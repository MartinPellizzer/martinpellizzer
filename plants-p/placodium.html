Placodium

Placodium, commonly known as the rock shield, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Placodiaceae.

These plants are characterized by their distinctive, flat, shield-like leaves that are often covered in a waxy cuticle. They are typically found in arid or rocky environments, where they can thrive with minimal water. Placodium species are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

The plant's unique morphology helps it survive in harsh, dry conditions.
