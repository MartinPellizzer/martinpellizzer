Psephellus circassicus

Psephellus circassicus, commonly known as the Caucasian sandwort, is a flowering plant native to the Caucasus region.

It is a low-growing perennial herb with small, fleshy leaves and clusters of white or pale pink flowers. The plant thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in mountainous areas. It has a compact growth habit and is adapted to harsh, arid environments.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for study in ecological and horticultural contexts.
