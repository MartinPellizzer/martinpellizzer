Pyrus turcomanica f. ajderiense

Pyrus turcomanica f. ajderiense, commonly known as the Ajderi apple, is a rare and distinctive cultivar of the common pear.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and small, ornamental fruits that are typically yellowish-green in color. The plant exhibits a high degree of cold hardiness, making it suitable for cultivation in temperate climates. Its leaves are broad and ovate, with a glossy upper surface and a hairy underside.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential for use in specialized horticultural settings.
