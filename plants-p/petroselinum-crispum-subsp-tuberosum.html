Petroselinum crispum subsp. tuberosum

Petroselinum crispum subsp.

tuberosum, commonly known as parsley root or parsnip, is a biennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its swollen, cylindrical root that is typically white or yellowish in color.

The plant has finely divided, feather-like leaves that are aromatic and often used in cooking. It produces small, yellowish flowers in umbels, which are typical of the Apiaceae family.

The root is rich in nutrients and has a sweet, nutty flavor, making it a valued vegetable in various cuisines.
