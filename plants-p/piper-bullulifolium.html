Piper bullulifolium

Piper bullulifolium, commonly known as the bullulifoliate pepper, is a tropical climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many species of pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. Its fruits are small, round berries that turn red when ripe.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
