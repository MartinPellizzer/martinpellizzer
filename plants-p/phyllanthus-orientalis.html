Phyllanthus orientalis

Phyllanthus orientalis, commonly known as the Indian gooseberry or amla, is a small evergreen shrub native to India and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are often elliptical or ovate in shape. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are typically clustered in panicles, followed by bright orange to red berries. The fruit is rich in vitamin C and has been used in traditional medicine for centuries.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and medicinal contexts.
