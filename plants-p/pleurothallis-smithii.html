Pleurothallis smithii

Pleurothallis smithii, commonly known as the Smith's orchid, is a species of orchid native to the cloud forests of Central and South America.

It is a small, epiphytic plant that thrives in humid, shaded environments, typically growing on tree branches or rocks. The plant features delicate, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink. Its flowers are often clustered in a raceme, and the species is noted for its ornamental value in horticulture.

Pleurothallis smithii is relatively easy to cultivate, making it a popular choice for orchid enthusiasts.
