Piper semangkoanum

Piper semangkoanum, commonly known as the Semangkoanum pepper, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes various species of pepper plants. The plant produces small, green berries that turn red when mature, and these berries contain the aromatic seeds used in traditional medicine and culinary applications. The leaves are often used in herbal remedies due to their medicinal properties.

It thrives in humid, shady environments and is often found in the undergrowth of tropical forests.
