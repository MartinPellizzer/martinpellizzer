Paspalum elegantulum

Paspalum elegantulum, commonly known as the slender paspalum, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, white to pale blue flowers. It is often found in wet or seasonally flooded habitats, such as marshes and wet meadows.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is sometimes cultivated in wetland restoration projects.
