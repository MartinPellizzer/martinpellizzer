Pleurothallis rotundata

Pleurothallis rotundata, commonly known as the round-leaf orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is a small, epiphytic orchid that typically grows on trees or rocks in humid, shaded environments. The plant features slender, wiry stems and small, clustered leaves that are often rounded in shape. Its flowers are usually pale green or white, with a subtle fragrance, and are borne in loose inflorescences.

Due to its delicate appearance and specific habitat requirements, it is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts for its ornamental value.
