Persea erythropus var. angustata

Persea erythropus var. angustata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf mahogany, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its elliptical, dark green leaves with a glossy surface. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by edible, fleshy berries. Its wood is valued for its durability and fine grain, making it useful in furniture and cabinetry.

The species is also noted for its ecological importance, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
