Pleurothallis florosa

Pleurothallis florosa, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its compact growth habit and delicate floral structure. The plant typically produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of yellow and white, which bloom during the warmer months. Its roots are epiphytic, meaning it often grows on trees or rocks in its natural habitat.

Pleurothallis florosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated by orchid enthusiasts for its unique beauty.
