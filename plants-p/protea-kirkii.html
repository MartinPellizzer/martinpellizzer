Protea kirkii

Protea kirkii, commonly known as the silver coin protea, is a striking South African plant belonging to the Proteaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, spherical inflorescence composed of numerous white to pinkish flowers, which are tightly clustered around a central stem. The plant is native to the fynbos biome, where it thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is adapted to the region's Mediterranean climate. Its unique appearance makes it a popular ornamental plant, while its ecological role supports various pollinators.

Protea kirkii is also valued for its horticultural appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
