Pseudosorghum hildebrandtii

Pseudosorghum hildebrandtii, commonly known as the Hildebrandt's false sorghum, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, demonstrating adaptability to harsh conditions.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological niche, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
