Peltastes tubiflorus

Peltastes tubiflorus, commonly known as the woolly bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that gives it a distinctive, soft appearance. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers cooler, moist environments.

Due to its unique appearance and ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
