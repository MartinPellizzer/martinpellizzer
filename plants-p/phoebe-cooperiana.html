Phoebe cooperiana

Phoebe cooperiana, commonly known as the Florida beech, is a small evergreen tree native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows to a height of 10 to 20 feet and has smooth, grayish-brown bark that becomes fissured with age. The leaves are elliptical, glossy, and dark green, turning reddish in the fall. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by oval, woody fruits.

This plant prefers moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.
