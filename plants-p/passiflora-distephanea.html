Passiflora distephanea

Passiflora distephanea, commonly known as the two-step passionflower, is a climbing vine native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, complex flowers that feature a crown of white filaments and a central cluster of blue and purple petals. The plant produces edible, orange-yellow berries that are rich in nutrients and have a tangy flavor. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped with a rough, textured surface.

Passiflora distephanea is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in warm climates.
