Pleurothallis imber-florum

Pleurothallis imber-florum, commonly known as the silver spider orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and pale green. The plant produces intricate, spider-like inflorescences, which are a distinguishing feature of the genus Pleurothallis. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts for its ornamental value.
