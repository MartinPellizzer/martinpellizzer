Panicum chloroleucum

Panicum chloroleucum, commonly known as silverhair panicgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its silvery-white to bluish-gray foliage, which gives it its common name. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fields. Its inflorescence consists of a loose, open panicle with pale green to white spikelets.

Panicum chloroleucum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in native plant gardens.
