Pyrus × michauxii

Pyrus × michauxii, commonly known as the Michaux pear, is a hybrid species within the genus Pyrus, native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its deciduous nature, with heart-shaped leaves that are often hairy beneath. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by edible fruits that are typically pear-shaped and have a sweet, aromatic flavor. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodlands or along stream banks.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in horticulture.
