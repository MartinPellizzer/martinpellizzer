Pediocactus glaucus

Pediocactus glaucus, commonly known as the blue prickly pear, is a cacti species native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its compact, globular shape and blue-gray spiny stems that are covered in a waxy coating. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, which are followed by red, fleshy fruits. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it well-suited for arid environments.

Pediocactus glaucus is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
