Polyspatha glaucescens

Polyspatha glaucescens, commonly known as the blue-veined arrowroot, is a tropical plant belonging to the Araceae family.

It is characterized by its striking, arrowhead-shaped leaves that often have a bluish or purplish hue. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers clustered in a spadix, typical of the Araceae family. Its rhizomes are used in traditional medicine and as a starch source.

Polyspatha glaucescens thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in the understory of tropical forests.
