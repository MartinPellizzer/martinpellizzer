Potentilla ignescens

Potentilla ignescens, commonly known as the fireweed or burning bush, is a flowering plant native to the Arctic and alpine regions of the northern hemisphere.

It is characterized by its small, showy yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has a woody stem that becomes reddish-brown with age, giving it its common name. It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and is often found in tundra and rocky habitats.

Potentilla ignescens is an important plant for pollinators and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
