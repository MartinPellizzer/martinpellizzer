Panicum crus-galli var. minus

Panicum crus-galli var. minus, commonly known as dwarf barnyard grass, is a grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is a short, tufted annual plant with slender, hairless stems and narrow, linear leaves. The inflorescence consists of dense, spike-like clusters of small, two-lobed spikelets. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in agricultural fields and along roadsides.

This plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to compete with crops, making it a common weed in many regions.
