Piper longum

Piper longum, commonly known as long pepper, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of India and Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its small, dark red berries that are harvested when fully mature. The plant produces a pungent, spicy compound called piperine, which is responsible for its distinctive flavor and medicinal properties. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their anti-inflammatory and digestive benefits.

The fruit is typically dried and used as a spice in various cuisines around the world.
