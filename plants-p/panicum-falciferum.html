Panicum falciferum

Panicum falciferum, commonly known as the sword grass, is a flowering plant belonging to the grass family Poaceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific Islands. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender stems and long, narrow leaves that often curve downward, giving it a distinctive appearance. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny grains of pollen.

Panicum falciferum is often used in traditional medicine and as a forage crop for livestock.
