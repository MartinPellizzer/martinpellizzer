Passiflora vesicaria var. galapagensis

Passiflora vesicaria var. galapagensis, commonly known as the Galapagos passionflower, is a climbing vine native to the Galapagos Islands.

It is characterized by its distinctive, showy flowers with five prominent sepals and a crown of filaments. The plant produces edible, orange-red berries that are rich in antioxidants. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped with a rough, hairy texture.

This species plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing food for local wildlife.
