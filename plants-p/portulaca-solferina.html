Portulaca solferina

Portulaca solferina, commonly known as the common purslane, is a low-growing, annual herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features fleshy, rounded leaves and vibrant yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant is characterized by its succulent stems and leaves, which help it retain moisture in arid environments. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in gardens, lawns, and disturbed areas.

Portulaca solferina is also known for its edible leaves and seeds, which are used in various culinary traditions.
