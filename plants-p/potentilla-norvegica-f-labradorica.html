Potentilla norvegica f. labradorica

Potentilla norvegica f. labradorica, commonly known as Labrador rose, is a shrub native to the Arctic and subarctic regions of North America.

It is characterized by its low-growing habit, with slender stems that often form dense mats. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers in clusters, typically blooming in late summer. Its leaves are narrow and hairy, adapted to withstand cold and harsh environmental conditions.

It is often found in rocky, barren, or sandy soils, thriving in nutrient-poor environments.
