Parnassia appendiculata

Parnassia appendiculata, commonly known as the mountain buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Asia, particularly in China and the Himalayas.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and rocky slopes. The plant features showy white flowers with yellow centers, which are often mistaken for true buttercups due to their similar appearance. Its leaves are alternate, heart-shaped, and covered in a fine layer of white hairs.

Parnassia appendiculata is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies for various ailments.
