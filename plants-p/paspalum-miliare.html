Paspalum miliare

Paspalum miliare, commonly known as millet grass, is a perennial grass species native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spikelet-based flowers that are typically green or pale yellow in color. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in disturbed areas or along roadsides.

Paspalum miliare is also valued for its potential use in erosion control and as a forage crop.
