Parthenocissus tricuspidata f. veitchii

Parthenocissus tricuspidata f. veitchii, commonly known as the Veitchii Boston ivy, is a cultivar of the Boston ivy plant.

It is a vigorous, climbing vine that produces heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture. The leaves are typically dark green in summer and turn vibrant red in the fall, adding ornamental value to structures. It is widely used in landscaping for its aesthetic appeal and ability to cover walls and trellises.

The plant is relatively easy to care for, thriving in full sun to partial shade and well-draining soil.
