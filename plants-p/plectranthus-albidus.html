Plectranthus albidus

Plectranthus albidus, commonly known as the white plectranthus, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its opposite, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale purple flowers, which bloom in summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Plectranthus albidus is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and is also valued in traditional medicine.
