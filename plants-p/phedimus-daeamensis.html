Phedimus daeamensis

Phedimus daeamensis, commonly known as the Daeamensis Stonecrop, is a low-growing succulent plant native to the high-altitude regions of Asia.

It features fleshy, thick leaves that are typically gray-green in color, adapted to store water and withstand harsh environmental conditions. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, often blooming in late spring or early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or alpine settings.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is a popular choice among succulent enthusiasts and gardeners seeking drought-tolerant plants.
