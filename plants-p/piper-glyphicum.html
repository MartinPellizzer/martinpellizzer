Piper glyphicum

Piper glyphicum, commonly known as the sacred pepper or holy pepper, is a flowering plant native to the rainforests of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes over 3,000 species of flowering plants. The plant produces small, green berries that turn red when ripe, containing the aromatic seeds used in traditional medicine and rituals. Its leaves are often used in spiritual ceremonies due to their psychoactive properties.

The plant's botanical significance is deeply rooted in indigenous cultures and traditional herbal practices.
