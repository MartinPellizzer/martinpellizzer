Peperomia huallagana

Peperomia huallagana, commonly known as the Huallaga peperomia, is a species native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a compact, low-growing perennial plant with thick, fleshy leaves that are often variegated in color. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or reddish in hue. It thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Due to its unique appearance and relatively easy care requirements, it is often cultivated as a decorative foliage plant.
