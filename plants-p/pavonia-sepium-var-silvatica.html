Pavonia sepium var. silvatica

Pavonia sepium var. silvatica, commonly known as the swamp rose or swamp mallow, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It features showy, pink to red bract-like flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has heart-shaped, velvety leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, swampy habitats and is often found along riverbanks and in wetlands.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species for ecological studies and wetland restoration projects.
