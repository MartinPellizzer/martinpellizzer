Pothos spinosus

Pothos spinosus, commonly known as the spiny pothos, is a climbing vine native to Southeast Asia.

It features heart-shaped leaves that are typically a deep green with a slightly waxy texture. The plant produces small, pale green flowers followed by orange-red berries that are toxic to humans and animals. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires indirect light for optimal growth.

Its spiny stems and attractive foliage make it a popular choice for indoor gardening.
