Petrophile striata

Petrophile striata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved rock-heath, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often hairy stems and narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or red in color. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in heathlands and open woodlands.

Petrophile striata is well adapted to dry conditions and is a member of the family Myrtaceae.
