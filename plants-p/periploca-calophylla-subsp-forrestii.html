Periploca calophylla subsp. forrestii

Periploca calophylla subsp.

forrestii, commonly known as the Forest's periploca, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are usually opposite in arrangement.

The plant produces yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by small, woody fruits. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest undergrowth.

The subspecies is notable for its medicinal properties, with various parts of the plant used in traditional medicine.
