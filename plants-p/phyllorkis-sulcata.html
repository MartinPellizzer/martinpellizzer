Phyllorkis sulcata

Phyllorkis sulcata, commonly known as the Sulcate Pteridium, is a fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, feathery fronds that are typically light green in color. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its reproductive structures, known as sori, are clustered on the underside of the fronds.

Phyllorkis sulcata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
