Panicum ternatum

Panicum ternatum, commonly known as three-lobed panicgrass, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist to wet soils in wetlands, swamps, and along stream banks. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a smooth texture, and it produces delicate, wind-dispersed seeds.

Panicum ternatum plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
