Prestoea cuatrecasasii

Prestoea cuatrecasasii, commonly known as the Cuatrecasas's ceiba, is a tall, straight tree native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Bombacaceae family and is characterized by its large, buttressed roots and smooth, grayish bark. The tree produces showy, orange-red flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by elongated, woody pods containing flat, winged seeds. It thrives in humid, lowland environments and is often found in primary rainforests.

Due to its ecological importance and ornamental value, it is a species of interest for conservation efforts.
