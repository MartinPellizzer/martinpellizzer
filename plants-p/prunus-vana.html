Prunus vana

Prunus vana, commonly known as the wild cherry or sweet cherry, is a species of shrub or small tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are oval to lance-shaped with a smooth margin. The plant produces small, white flowers in early spring, followed by edible drupes that range in color from yellow to dark red. Prunus vana is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it adaptable to a variety of garden settings.
