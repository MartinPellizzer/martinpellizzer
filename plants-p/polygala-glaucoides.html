Polygala glaucoides

Polygala glaucoides, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its striking blue to purple flower clusters that resemble mist. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as wetlands and prairies. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, while its stems are erect and often branched.

Polygala glaucoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a nectar source for pollinators.
