Penstemon pseudospectabilis subsp. clutei

Penstemon pseudospectabilis subsp.

clutei, commonly known as Clute's tall beardtongue, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States. It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, tubular flowers.

The plant typically produces vibrant purple to pink flower spikes that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its drought-tolerant nature.

Clute's tall beardtongue is well-suited for xeriscaping and attracts pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
