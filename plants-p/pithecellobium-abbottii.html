Pithecellobium abbottii

Pithecellobium abbottii, commonly known as the Abbott's pisonia, is a small evergreen tree native to the Caribbean islands.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, clustered flowers. The plant produces small, edible fruits that are often consumed by local wildlife. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in coastal areas and disturbed soils.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
