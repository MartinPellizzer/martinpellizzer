Ponaria

Ponaria, commonly known as the sea holly, is a genus of flowering plants belonging to the family Asteraceae.

These plants are characterized by their striking blue or purple flower heads, which are surrounded by bracts that resemble petals. Ponaria species are typically found in coastal areas, where they thrive in salty and sandy soils. They have a rosette growth habit, with leaves that are often fleshy and succulent.

The genus includes several species, such as Ponaria maritima, which is native to the Mediterranean region.
