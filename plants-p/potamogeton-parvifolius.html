Potamogeton parvifolius

Potamogeton parvifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaved pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments across Europe, Asia, and North America.

It features long, ribbon-like leaves that are typically narrow and slightly curled, often with a smooth or slightly ribbed surface. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually arranged in clusters, and it reproduces both sexually and vegetatively through rhizomes. It thrives in still or slow-moving waters, often forming dense underwater vegetation that provides habitat for aquatic life.

Its presence is an indicator of good water quality and is important for maintaining ecological balance in wetland ecosystems.
