Platanus × parviloba

Platanus × parviloba, commonly known as the Mexican fanwood, is a hybrid species native to the central and southern regions of Mexico.

It is a large, deciduous tree that can reach heights of up to 30 meters, featuring a broad, spreading crown. The tree produces large, palmate leaves that are typically 10–20 centimeters in diameter, with a distinctive lobed structure. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, appearing in clusters, while the fruit is a spherical, spiky capsule that splits open to release seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often planted in urban landscapes for its shade and aesthetic appeal.
