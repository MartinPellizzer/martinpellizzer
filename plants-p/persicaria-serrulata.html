Persicaria serrulata

Persicaria serrulata, commonly known as narrow-leaved smartweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to North America.

It features alternate, lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges, which give it its scientific name. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or red, arranged in dense spikes. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in marshes, pond edges, and along stream banks.

Persicaria serrulata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing food and habitat for various wildlife species.
