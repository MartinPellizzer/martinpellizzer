Phyllarthron bilabiatum

Phyllarthron bilabiatum, commonly known as the two-lipped leaf plant, is a unique species characterized by its distinctive leaf morphology.

It features bilaterally symmetrical leaves with two prominent lobes, giving it a bilabiate appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its leaves are usually thick and leathery, adapted to conserve water.

Phyllarthron bilabiatum is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
