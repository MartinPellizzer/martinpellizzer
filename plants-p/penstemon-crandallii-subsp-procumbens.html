Penstemon crandallii subsp. procumbens

Penstemon crandallii subsp.

procumbens, commonly known as the Crandall's beardtongue, is a perennial herb native to the western United States. It is characterized by its prostrate growth habit, with stems that lie close to the ground.

The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to purple, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery down.
