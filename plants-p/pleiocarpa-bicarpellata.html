Pleiocarpa bicarpellata

Pleiocarpa bicarpellata, commonly known as the two-chambered fruit plant, is a species native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique floral structure, featuring two carpels that develop into distinct chambers within the fruit. The plant produces small, fleshy berries that are often used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are simple and alternate, with a smooth texture and a slightly aromatic scent.

The botanical interest of Pleiocarpa bicarpellata lies in its specialized reproductive morphology and potential medicinal properties.
