Podocarpus pedunculatus

Podocarpus pedunculatus, commonly known as the hina or Christmas tree, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Podocarpaceae and is characterized by its dense, pyramidal shape and dark green, needle-like leaves. The tree produces small, spherical cones that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in coastal areas with high humidity.

Due to its ornamental value, it is widely cultivated as a decorative plant and for its durable wood.
