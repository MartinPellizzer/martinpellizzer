Persicaria virginiana var. kachina

Persicaria virginiana var. kachina, commonly known as Kachina jointweed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Polygonaceae family, characterized by its succulent stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in desert or semi-arid environments.

Its botanical features make it well-adapted to arid conditions, with thick leaves that help reduce water loss.
