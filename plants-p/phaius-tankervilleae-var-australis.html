Phaius tankervilleae var. australis

Phaius tankervilleae var. australis, commonly known as the Australian orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and large, showy flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as rainforests and along stream banks. Its flowers are usually pale green to yellow with a distinct red or purple lip.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with appropriate humidity and light conditions.
