Podocarpus gracilior

Podocarpus gracilior, commonly known as the slender podocarp, is a species of flowering plant in the family Podocarpaceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Southeast Asia, including parts of India, China, and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its evergreen foliage, with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are usually dark green and glossy. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by woody, cone-like structures that contain its seeds.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in horticulture for landscaping purposes.
