Persica pollardii

Persica pollardii, commonly known as the Persian persimmon, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Caucasus region.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and showy, pinkish-white flowers. The plant produces small, orange to red fruits that are edible when fully ripe. It thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil and requires full sun for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruit, it is cultivated in gardens and for local consumption.
