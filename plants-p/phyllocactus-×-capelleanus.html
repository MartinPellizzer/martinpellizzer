Phyllocactus × capelleanus

Phyllocactus × capelleanus, commonly known as the Capelleanus Paddle Cactus, is a hybrid plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its flattened, paddle-shaped stems that grow in a compact, bushy form. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the growing season, typically in spring or early summer. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires ample sunlight, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated for its unique appearance and resilience in arid conditions.
