Pteris barklyae

Pteris barklyae, commonly known as the Barkley's water fern, is a species of fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, lobed fronds that are typically green with a subtle bluish tinge. The plant prefers moist, shaded environments and is often found in humid tropical regions. Its reproductive structures, including sori, are located on the underside of the fronds.

Pteris barklyae is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in controlled indoor conditions.
