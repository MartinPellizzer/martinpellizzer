Protium oxapampae

Protium oxapampae, commonly known as the Andean copal tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the cloud forests of northern South America.

It belongs to the family Sapotaceae and is characterized by its dense, leathery leaves and fragrant, yellowish flowers. The tree produces small, fleshy fruits that contain smooth, dark seeds. Its latex is used in traditional medicine and as a natural resin.

The plant plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, supporting various wildlife species.
