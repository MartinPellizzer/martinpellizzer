Parthenocissus quinquefolia f. macrophylla

Parthenocissus quinquefolia f. macrophylla, commonly known as the giant Boston ivy, is a cultivar of the Virginia creeper.

It is characterized by its large, lobed leaves that can reach up to 15 cm in length. The plant is a vigorous climber with tendrils that allow it to attach to surfaces. It produces small, greenish-white flowers in the summer, followed by red berries in the fall.

This variety is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to cover walls and trellises.
