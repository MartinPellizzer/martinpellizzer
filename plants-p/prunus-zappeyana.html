Prunus zappeyana

Prunus zappeyana, commonly known as the Zappeyana cherry, is a species native to the Canary Islands.

It is a small to medium-sized deciduous tree or shrub that typically grows in coastal and mountainous regions. The plant produces small, white flowers in spring, followed by edible berries that are dark red when mature. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a serrated margin.

Prunus zappeyana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
