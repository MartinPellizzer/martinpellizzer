Pittosporum muricatum

Pittosporum muricatum, commonly known as the strawberry tree, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its evergreen foliage, with glossy, dark green leaves that are often fragrant. The plant produces clusters of small, white, star-shaped flowers that bloom in spring, followed by red, berry-like fruits. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance.

The species is also valued for its ability to tolerate various soil conditions and its use in horticulture.
