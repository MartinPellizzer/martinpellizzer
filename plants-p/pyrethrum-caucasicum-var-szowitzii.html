Pyrethrum caucasicum var. szowitzii

Pyrethrum caucasicum var. szowitzii, commonly known as the Caucasian pyrethrum, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and small, yellowish flower heads. The plant produces pyrethrin compounds, which are natural insecticides used in organic pest control. It thrives in temperate climates and well-drained soil, often growing in alpine or woodland habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and pesticidal properties, it is cultivated in gardens and agricultural settings.
