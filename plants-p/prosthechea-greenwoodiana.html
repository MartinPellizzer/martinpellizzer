Prosthechea greenwoodiana

Prosthechea greenwoodiana, commonly known as the Greenwood's orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the Amazon rainforest.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that support its foliage and flowers. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or greenish-yellow in color. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained, nutrient-rich soil.

Prosthechea greenwoodiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
