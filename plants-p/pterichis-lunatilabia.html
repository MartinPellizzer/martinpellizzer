Pterichis lunatilabia

Pterichis lunatilabia, commonly known as the moon-faced pterichis, is a rare and unique plant native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, moon-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery pubescence. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, adding to its ornamental value. Its growth habit is typically shrub-like, with a compact form and a preference for shaded, humid environments.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a valuable species in botanical research and conservation efforts.
