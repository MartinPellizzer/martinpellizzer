Paspalum uniflorum

Paspalum uniflorum, commonly known as the slender paspalum, is a flowering grass native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist or wet environments, such as marshes and riparian areas. Its leaves are narrow and blade-like, with a distinct midrib.

Paspalum uniflorum is often found in wetland ecosystems and is adapted to tolerate waterlogged soils.
