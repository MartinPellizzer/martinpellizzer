Pelargonium × diadematum var. variegatum

Pelargonium × diadematum var. variegatum, commonly known as the variegated geranium, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Geraniaceae family.

It is characterized by its striking variegated foliage, featuring shades of white, yellow, and green on its heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, five-petaled flowers in shades of pink, red, or white, depending on the variety. It is a popular ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance and relatively low maintenance requirements.

Native to South Africa, it thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight.
