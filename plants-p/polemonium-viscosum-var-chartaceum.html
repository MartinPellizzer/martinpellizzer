Polemonium viscosum var. chartaceum

Polemonium viscosum var. chartaceum, commonly known as the paperleaf bluebell, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features delicate, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to purple, typically blooming in late spring. The plant has lance-shaped, chartaceous (paper-like) leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, woodland soils and is often found in shaded to partially shaded environments.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive and ornamental addition to native gardens.
