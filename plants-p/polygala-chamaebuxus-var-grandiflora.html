Polygala chamaebuxus var. grandiflora

Polygala chamaebuxus var. grandiflora, commonly known as the large-flowered milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, open habitats such as sandy soils and limestone barrens. The plant features slender, branching stems with opposite, narrow, lance-shaped leaves. Its most distinctive characteristic is its showy, spicate inflorescence composed of small, clustered flowers with distinctive, spurred petals.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local pollinators.
