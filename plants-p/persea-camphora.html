Persea camphora

Persea camphora, commonly known as the camphor laurel, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central America and the Caribbean.

It is characterized by its dark green, lance-shaped leaves and fragrant, white flowers that bloom in clusters. The tree produces small, round fruits that are typically eaten by birds and other wildlife. Its wood is valued for its aromatic properties and resistance to decay, making it useful in construction and carving.

The plant is also known for its medicinal uses, with various parts being used in traditional remedies.
