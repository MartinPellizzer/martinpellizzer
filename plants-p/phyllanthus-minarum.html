Phyllanthus minarum

Phyllanthus minarum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved phyllanthus, is a small shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It features simple, opposite leaves that are typically narrow and elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by small, round fruits. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its hardy nature, it is often used in ornamental gardening and traditional medicine.
