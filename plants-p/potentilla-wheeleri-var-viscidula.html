Potentilla wheeleri var. viscidula

Potentilla wheeleri var. viscidula, commonly known as the sticky potentilla, is a low-growing shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features small, oval leaves that are covered in a fine, sticky glandular hair, giving the plant its common name. The flowers are typically yellow or golden in color, appearing in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to harsh, arid conditions.
