Pancratium fluitans

Pancratium fluitans, commonly known as the sea daffodil, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of the Mediterranean and parts of Africa.

It is characterized by its bright yellow, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is often found near the shoreline. Its leaves are long, narrow, and grass-like, growing from a bulbous stem.

Pancratium fluitans is adapted to salt spray and can withstand harsh coastal conditions.
