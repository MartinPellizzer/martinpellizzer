Phlomis crinita var. barrelieri

Phlomis crinita var. barrelieri, commonly known as the Barrelier's beardgrass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, preferring full sun exposure. The plant features tall, slender stems adorned with narrow, gray-green leaves that have a soft, hairy texture. Its flowering period occurs in late spring to early summer, producing spikes of small, pale blue to purple flowers.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and drought tolerance, making it a suitable choice for xeriscaping.
