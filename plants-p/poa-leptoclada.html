Poa leptoclada

Poa leptoclada, commonly known as the slender meadowgrass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in open, moist habitats such as meadows, pastures, and woodland edges. The plant has slender, cylindrical stems that are often slightly compressed, and its leaves are narrow with a smooth surface. Its inflorescence consists of a single spikelet at the tip of each stem, which is characteristic of the Poaceae family.

Poa leptoclada plays a role in soil stabilization and provides forage for grazing animals.
