Pelargonium × pellitum

Pelargonium × pellitum, commonly known as the ivy-leaved geranium, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Geraniaceae family.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and opposite, heart-shaped leaves that resemble ivy leaves, giving it its common name. The plant produces clusters of small, five-petaled flowers in shades of red, pink, or white, depending on the cultivar. It is native to South Africa and is widely cultivated as an ornamental plant for its attractive foliage and long-lasting blooms.

Pelargonium × pellitum thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.
