Packera plattensis

Packera plattensis, commonly known as Platt's fleabane, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky habitats, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its yellow flowers bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Packera plattensis is known for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in harsh environmental conditions.
