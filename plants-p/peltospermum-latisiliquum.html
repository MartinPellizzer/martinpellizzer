Peltospermum latisiliquum

Peltospermum latisiliquum, commonly known as the broad-leaved peltospermum, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Peltospermataceae.

It is a small, herbaceous shrub characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are typically ovate to lanceolate in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in dense, terminal clusters, which are followed by distinctive, winged fruits. It is native to Southeast Asia and thrives in tropical and subtropical environments.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
