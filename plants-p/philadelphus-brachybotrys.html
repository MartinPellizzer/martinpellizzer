Philadelphus brachybotrys

Philadelphus brachybotrys, commonly known as the dwarf goldvein philadelphus, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, fragrant yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are typically dark green and slightly hairy on the underside. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained, fertile soil.

This species is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and relatively low maintenance requirements.
