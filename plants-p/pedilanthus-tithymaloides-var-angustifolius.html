Pedilanthus tithymaloides var. angustifolius

Pedilanthus tithymaloides var. angustifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leafed tithymal, is a member of the Apocynaceae family.

It is a shrub characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, fragrant yellow flowers. The plant produces a milky sap that contains various alkaloids, some of which have medicinal properties. It is native to tropical regions of South America and thrives in warm, humid climates.

Due to its unique chemical composition, it is of interest in both horticulture and pharmacological research.
