Prunus × lannesiana f. mollis

Prunus × lannesiana f. mollis, commonly known as the silky cherry, is a unique hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Prunus lannesiana and Prunus × yedoensis.

It is characterized by its dense, silky foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance, making it a popular ornamental plant. The leaves are typically a deep green with a soft, velvety texture, and they remain throughout the growing season. It produces small, fragrant flowers in spring, followed by edible cherries that are smaller than those of typical cherry trees.

This cultivar is valued for its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to various garden settings.
