Pilea serpyllifolia

Pilea serpyllifolia, commonly known as the creeping pilea, is a low-growing herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features dense, mat-forming foliage with small, rounded leaves that are typically covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces tiny, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers that are clustered in compact inflorescences. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in disturbed soils or along stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable species for ecological studies and habitat restoration projects.
