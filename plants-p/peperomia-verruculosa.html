Peperomia verruculosa

Peperomia verruculosa, commonly known as the疣状铁线蕨, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It features dense, glossy, waxy leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern, giving it a unique and ornamental appearance. The plant is characterized by its short, hairy stems and small, inconspicuous flowers that appear in clusters. It thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Due to its low maintenance requirements and attractive foliage, it is often cultivated as a decorative plant.
