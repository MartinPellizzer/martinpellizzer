Podocarpus macrophyllus var. nakaii

Podocarpus macrophyllus var. nakaii, commonly known as the Japanese yew or Nakai's podocarp, is a slow-growing evergreen conifer native to East Asia.

It features needle-like leaves that are dark green and glossy, with a leathery texture. The plant produces small, spherical cones that mature to a reddish-brown color. It is typically found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and mountainous regions.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for horticultural use in gardens and landscapes.
