Panicum intermedium

Panicum intermedium, commonly known as inland sea oats, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and fluffy, silver-green plumes that resemble sea oats. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds, thriving in moist to saturated soil conditions. Its foliage is narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue that adds aesthetic value to wetland landscapes.

Panicum intermedium plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
