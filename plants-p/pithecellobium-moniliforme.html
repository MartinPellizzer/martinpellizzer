Pithecellobium moniliforme

Pithecellobium moniliforme, commonly known as the golden shower tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of yellow, pendulous flowers that resemble small showers, hence its common name. The plant produces edible, bean-like fruits that are often consumed raw or cooked. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible produce.

The species is also valued for its potential in agroforestry and soil stabilization.
