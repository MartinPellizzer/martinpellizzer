Pleroma hospitum

Pleroma hospitum, commonly known as the purple coneflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its showy, violet-blue flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a hairy texture and grows in clusters from a fibrous root system. It thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.

Botanically, it belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is known for its composite flower heads.
