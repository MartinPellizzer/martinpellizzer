Phyllanthus debilis

Phyllanthus debilis, commonly known as the slender phyllanthus, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often found in the understory of forests. The plant features simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. Its small, greenish-white flowers are clustered in umbels, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits.

Phyllanthus debilis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
