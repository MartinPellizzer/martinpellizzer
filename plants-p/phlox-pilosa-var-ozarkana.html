Phlox pilosa var. ozarkana

Phlox pilosa var. ozarkana, commonly known as Ozark fuzzy phlox, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its hairy, gray-green foliage and clusters of showy, pink to lavender flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or shaded areas. Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to ecological diversity.

It is valued in native plant gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in partial shade.
