Polypodium barathrophyllum

Polypodium barathrophyllum, commonly known as the silver fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Polypodiaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, feathery fronds that are typically covered in a fine layer of silvery hair, giving it its common name. Native to tropical and subtropical regions, this plant thrives in moist, shaded environments. The fronds are usually pinnately divided, with small, lance-shaped pinnae that contribute to its ornamental appearance.

It is often cultivated for its aesthetic value in gardens and as a houseplant.
