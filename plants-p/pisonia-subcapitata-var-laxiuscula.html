Pisonia subcapitata var. laxiuscula

Pisonia subcapitata var. laxiuscula, commonly known as the silky pisonia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its drooping clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom throughout the year. The leaves are simple, alternate, and often covered in fine, silky hairs, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in humid, coastal environments and is often found in mangrove swamps or along sandy shores.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local wildlife.
