Palisota brachythyrsa

Palisota brachythyrsa, commonly known as the broad-leafed sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of water bodies, thriving in moist to aquatic conditions. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are usually arranged in three rows, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its flowering spikes are usually short and clustered, producing small, inconspicuous flowers.

Palisota brachythyrsa is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and filter water.
