Psydrax salignus

Psydrax salignus, commonly known as the silky senna, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-branched stems and pinnately compound leaves with serrated margins. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in racemes, which are typically followed by elongated, woody pods containing flattened, seed-like structures. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed or open habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
