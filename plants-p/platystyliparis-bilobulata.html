Platystyliparis bilobulata

Platystyliparis bilobulata, commonly known as the two-lobed leaf orchid, is a rare and specialized orchid species native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive bilobed leaves and small, delicate flowers that typically bloom in the spring. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, often found in tropical rainforests. Its botanical features include a sympodial growth habit and a complex system of aerial roots.

Due to habitat loss and over-collection, it is considered endangered and requires conservation efforts to ensure its survival.
