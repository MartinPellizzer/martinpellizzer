Poa paludigena

Poa paludigena, commonly known as the wetland grass or bog grass, is a species of flowering plant in the grass family Poaceae.

It is native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere and typically grows in wetland habitats such as marshes and peat bogs. The plant has slender, tufted stems and produces small, spike-like inflorescences with greenish or brownish flowers. Its leaves are narrow and often have a bluish-green hue, and it is adapted to tolerate waterlogged soils.

Poa paludigena plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland environments and supporting local wildlife.
