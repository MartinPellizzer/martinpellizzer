Pandanus ceylanicus

Pandanus ceylanicus, commonly known as the Ceylon pandanus, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, fan-shaped leaves that are often used in traditional crafts. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into spiky, fibrous fruits. Its roots and leaves are utilized in local medicine and as a source of fiber.

Pandanus ceylanicus thrives in humid, coastal environments and is often found in mangrove areas.
