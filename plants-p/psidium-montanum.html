Psidium montanum

Psidium montanum, commonly known as the mountain guava, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Andes region of South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are often elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, berry-like fruits. Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for horticultural study and potential cultivation in suitable climates.

The species is also valued for its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties.
