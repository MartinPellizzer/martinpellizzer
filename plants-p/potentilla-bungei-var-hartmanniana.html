Potentilla bungei var. hartmanniana

Potentilla bungei var. hartmanniana, commonly known as the Hartmann's cinquefoil, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces clusters of small, yellow flowers in the summer. The leaves are typically palmately divided into five to seven lobes, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardy nature in colder climates.
