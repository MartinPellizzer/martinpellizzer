Phebalium lamprophyllum

Phebalium lamprophyllum, commonly known as the silver-leafed hakea, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It belongs to the Proteaceae family and is characterized by its dense, silver-gray foliage that reflects sunlight. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers clustered in spike-like inflorescences, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to dry, sunny conditions.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is often cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
