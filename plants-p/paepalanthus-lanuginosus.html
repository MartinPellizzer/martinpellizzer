Paepalanthus lanuginosus

Paepalanthus lanuginosus, commonly known as the woolly puya, is a striking plant native to the arid regions of South America.

It is a member of the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its dense, woolly covering of white or gray hairs on its leaves. The plant produces striking inflorescences with brightly colored flowers, often in shades of red, orange, or yellow. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.
