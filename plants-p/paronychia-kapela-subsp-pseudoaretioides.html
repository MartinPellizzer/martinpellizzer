Paronychia kapela subsp. pseudoaretioides

Paronychia kapela subsp.

pseudoaretioides, commonly known as the Cape mountain ice plant, is a succulent plant native to South Africa. It features fleshy, blue-green leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern, adapted to store water in arid environments.

The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink and white during the cooler months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its low-growing habit and ornamental foliage make it a popular choice among succulent enthusiasts.
