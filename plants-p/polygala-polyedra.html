Polygala polyedra

Polygala polyedra, commonly known as the many-lobed milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia and the Middle East.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments. Its flowers are usually pink or violet, with a distinctive shape that resembles a small, delicate bell.

Polygala polyedra is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
