Palma thebaica

Palma thebaica, commonly known as the Theban date palm, is a species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a tall, slender palm with long, pinnate leaves that can reach up to 3 meters in length. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into clustered, edible dates. Its trunk is smooth and covered with a fibrous sheath, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Palma thebaica is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water once established.
