Passiflora angusta

Passiflora angusta, commonly known as the narrowleaf passionflower, is a climbing vine native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its slender stems and heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive waxy coating. The plant produces striking flowers with five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments, often in shades of white, pink, and purple. Its fruit is a small, edible berry that turns red when mature.

This species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
