Pastinaca kotschyi

Pastinaca kotschyi, commonly known as the Kotschyi scorzonera, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of North Africa and the Middle East.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and medicinal properties. The plant features robust, hairy stems and broad, heart-shaped leaves that are adapted to withstand harsh environmental conditions. Its yellow flowers bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its resilience and potential pharmacological applications, it is of interest to botanists and herbalists alike.
