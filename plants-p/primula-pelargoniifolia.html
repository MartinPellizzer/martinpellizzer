Primula pelargoniifolia

Primula pelargoniifolia, commonly known as the pelargonium-leaved primrose, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink, red, and white. The leaves are notably broad and resemble those of pelargonium species, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The plant is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
