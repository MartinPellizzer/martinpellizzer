Phaeneilema glaucum

Phaeneilema glaucum, commonly known as the blue-eyed daisy, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking blue flowers with a yellow center. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flower heads. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long blooming period, which extends from spring to autumn.
