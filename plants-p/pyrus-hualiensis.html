Pyrus hualiensis

Pyrus hualiensis, commonly known as the Hualian pear, is a species of flowering plant in the family Rosaceae.

It is native to China and is characterized by its ornamental value due to its attractive white flowers and edible fruit. The plant typically grows as a small tree or shrub, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and ovate, with a glossy green upper surface and a pale underside.

The fruit is a pear-shaped berry that ripens in late summer, providing both ornamental and culinary interest.
