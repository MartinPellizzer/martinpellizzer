Prunus triloba var. petzoldii

Prunus triloba var. petzoldii, commonly known as the Petzold's dwarf cherry, is a compact, deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It features small, ovate leaves that are typically dark green and slightly hairy on the upper surface. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers in early spring, followed by red or black berries in the fall. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens for its compact size and seasonal interest.

This variety is also noted for its hardiness and adaptability to a range of soil conditions.
