Pteromonnina resedoides

Pteromonnina resedoides, commonly known as the red sandalwood, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, fragrant white flowers and dark green, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, woody fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and historical use in traditional medicine, it is sometimes cultivated for its aesthetic and practical properties.
