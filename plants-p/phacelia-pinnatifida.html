Phacelia pinnatifida

Phacelia pinnatifida, commonly known as slender phacelia, is a flowering plant native to western North America.

It is a herbaceous annual that typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. The plant features small, blue to purple flowers arranged in dense, spike-like clusters. Its leaves are pinnately divided, giving it a delicate, fern-like appearance.

Phacelia pinnatifida is valued for its nectar-rich flowers, which attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
