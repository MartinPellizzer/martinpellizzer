Pomaderris apetala subsp. apetala

Pomaderris apetala subsp.

apetala, commonly known as the smooth-leafed pomaderris, is a shrub native to southeastern Australia. It is characterized by its smooth, often glabrous leaves that are typically elliptical to ovate in shape.

The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland and heathland environments.

The subspecies is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage.
