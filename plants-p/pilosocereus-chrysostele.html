Pilosocereus chrysostele

Pilosocereus chrysostele, commonly known as the golden star cactus, is a species of cactus native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its spiny, cylindrical stems that often branch out into a tree-like structure. The plant produces yellow flowers during the warmer months, which are typically followed by small, red fruits. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it well-suited for desert environments.

Its unique appearance and ability to store water in its thick stems make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
