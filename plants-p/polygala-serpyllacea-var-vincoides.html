Polygala serpyllacea var. vincoides

Polygala serpyllacea var. vincoides, commonly known as the common milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its slender, often branched stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

The flowers are usually pink or purple, with a distinctive spurred structure that attracts pollinators like bees and butterflies.
