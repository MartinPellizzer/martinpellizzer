Poa misera var. sphondylodes

Poa misera var. sphondylodes, commonly known as the slender meadowgrass, is a variant of the meadow grass species native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is a perennial grass that typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. The plant has slender, erect culms with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its inflorescence consists of a single spikelet, which is distinctive in appearance.

Botanically, it is characterized by its small, wind-pollinated flowers and is often found in association with other grasses in diverse ecological communities.
