Pueraria stracheyi

Pueraria stracheyi, commonly known as the climbing peanut vine, is a leguminous plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Asia and is characterized by its vigorous climbing habit and large, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, purple or violet flowers that are typically clustered in racemes. Its roots are used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Pueraria stracheyi is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it an important plant in agroforestry systems.
