Pithecellobium insigne

Pithecellobium insigne, commonly known as the golden glory pea, is a small evergreen shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of yellow flowers. The plant produces small, edible pods that contain seeds used in traditional medicine. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and nutritional benefits.

Its ability to fix nitrogen makes it beneficial for soil enrichment in agricultural settings.
