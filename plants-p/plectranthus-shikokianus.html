Plectranthus shikokianus

Plectranthus shikokianus, commonly known as Shikokian mint, is a flowering plant native to Japan.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its square, hairy stems and opposite leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are usually pale blue or violet, arranged in clusters at the ends of the branches. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and containers.

Due to its aromatic leaves and ornamental value, it is also used in traditional medicine and as a culinary herb.
