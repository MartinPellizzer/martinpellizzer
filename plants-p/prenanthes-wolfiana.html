Prenanthes wolfiana

Prenanthes wolfiana, commonly known as the wolf's bane or white coneflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features showy, white to pale purple flowers that bloom in late summer, typically from July to September. The plant has a basal rosette of lance-shaped leaves and a tall, unbranched stem topped with a cluster of tubular flowers. It thrives in moist, rich soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

Prenanthes wolfiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance, providing nectar for pollinators.
