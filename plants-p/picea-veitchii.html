Picea veitchii

Picea veitchii, commonly known as the Veitch's spruce, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to the mountainous regions of western China.

It is characterized by its tall, slender form and dense, dark green foliage that turns a striking silver-blue in winter. The tree produces small, cone-like structures that contain seeds, typical of the conifer family. Its bark is thick and furrowed, providing a rugged appearance.

Picea veitchii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique visual characteristics.
