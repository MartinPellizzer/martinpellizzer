Piper rugosilimbum

Piper rugosilimbum, commonly known as the rough-leaved pepper vine, is a climbing shrub native to tropical regions of Asia and Africa.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes various pepper species. The plant is characterized by its rugged, dark green leaves with prominent veins and small, clustered white flowers. Its fruit is a three-lobed, fleshy berry that turns red when mature.

The plant is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties.
