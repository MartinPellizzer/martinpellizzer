Papaver radicatum subsp. labradoricum

Papaver radicatum subsp.

labradoricum, commonly known as Labrador poppy, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern coast of North America. It features showy, cup-shaped flowers that are typically red or orange in color, with a yellow center.

The plant has a short, thick rootstock and produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in forested areas or along streambanks.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
