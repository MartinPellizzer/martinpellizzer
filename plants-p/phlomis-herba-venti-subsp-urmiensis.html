Phlomis herba-venti subsp. urmiensis

Phlomis herba-venti subsp.

urmiensis, commonly known as the Persian giant fleabane, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy yellow flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant has lance-shaped leaves with rough, hairy surfaces, adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. It is often found in rocky soils and is known for its resilience to drought and poor soil conditions.

This subspecies is of interest in botanical studies due to its unique morphology and ecological adaptations.
