Pholistoma membranaceum

Pholistoma membranaceum, commonly known as the membranous pholistoma, is a small, perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and wetlands. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are usually covered in a fine, hairy layer. Its flowers are small, greenish-white, and clustered in dense inflorescences.

It is often found in association with other wetland plants and plays a role in maintaining the ecological balance of its habitat.
