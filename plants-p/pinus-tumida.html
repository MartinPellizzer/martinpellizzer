Pinus tumida

Pinus tumida, commonly known as the Mexican bristlecone pine, is a species of conifer native to the mountainous regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and dense, needle-like foliage that forms a conical shape. The tree produces cones that are typically reddish-brown and contain small, winged seeds. It thrives in high-altitude environments with well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, dry conditions.

The botanical features of Pinus tumida make it a distinctive and resilient member of the pine family.
