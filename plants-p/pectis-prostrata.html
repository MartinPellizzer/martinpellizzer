Pectis prostrata

Pectis prostrata, commonly known as the prostrate pectis, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in a prostrate or creeping habit, with hairy stems and narrow, linear leaves. Its yellow flowers are arranged in clusters and bloom during the warmer months.

Pectis prostrata is often used in rock gardens and as a ground cover due to its hardy nature and ornamental value.
