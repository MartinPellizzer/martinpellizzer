Phyllostachys reticulata var. aurea

Phyllostachys reticulata var. aurea, commonly known as the golden bamboo, is a variant of the silver bamboo species native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its striking golden-yellow culms that contrast with its dark green leaves. This plant is a clump-forming perennial grass that thrives in moist, well-drained soil and full sun. Its rapid growth rate makes it suitable for privacy screens or erosion control in suitable climates.

The golden bamboo is prized for its ornamental value and is often used in landscaping and traditional Chinese gardens.
