Prunus cerasus var. austera

Prunus cerasus var. austera, commonly known as the Austrian cherry, is a cultivar of the sweet cherry species.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and is often used as a rootstock in cherry cultivation. The plant produces small, dark red fruits that are typically smaller than standard cherries. It is valued for its hardiness and adaptability to various soil conditions.

The botanical features include simple, alternate leaves and a generally bushy structure.
