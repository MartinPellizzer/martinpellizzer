Polygala burmanni

Polygala burmanni, commonly known as Burmann's milkweed, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, delicate flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as forests and stream banks. Its leaves are opposite and ovate, while its flowers are usually pink or white with a distinctive spurred structure.

It plays a role in local ecosystems by providing nectar for pollinators like bees and butterflies.
