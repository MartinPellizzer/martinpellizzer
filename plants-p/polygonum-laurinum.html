Polygonum laurinum

Polygonum laurinum, commonly known as the silverleaf knotweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features succulent, silver-gray leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or purple, blooming in late summer. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodlands or along stream banks.

Polygonum laurinum is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in wetland areas.
