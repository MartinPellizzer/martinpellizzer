Populus simonii var. luoningensis

Populus simonii var. luoningensis, commonly known as the Luoning poplar, is a rare variant of the poplar tree native to China.

It is characterized by its distinctive leaves, which are typically heart-shaped with a serrated margin. The plant thrives in temperate climates and is often found in mountainous regions with well-drained soil. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, appearing in catkins during the spring season.

This variant is valued for its ecological role and is also studied for its potential in reforestation efforts.
