Puya pusilla

Puya pusilla, commonly known as the dwarf torch flower, is a striking member of the Bromeliaceae family native to the Andes of South America.

This evergreen perennial is characterized by its compact rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves that form a tight, water-holding cup at the center. It produces tall, spire-like inflorescences bearing vibrant red or orange bracts, which are often mistaken for flowers. The plant is adapted to high-altitude environments and thrives in full sun with well-drained soil.

Due to its unique appearance and ornamental value, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.
