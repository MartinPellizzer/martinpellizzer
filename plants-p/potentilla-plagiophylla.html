Potentilla plagiophylla

Potentilla plagiophylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaved cinquefoil, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of central and eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, preferring well-drained soil and full sun exposure. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Potentilla plagiophylla is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
