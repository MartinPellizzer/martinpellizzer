Pedicularis liana

Pedicularis liana, commonly known as the climbing monkey orchid, is a unique member of the Orobanchaceae family.

It is characterized by its climbing habit, with stems that twine around supports to reach sunlight. The plant produces striking, showy flowers that are typically red or orange with a distinctive lip. Its leaves are opposite and oval-shaped, often with a hairy texture.

This species is native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia, where it thrives in humid, shaded environments.
