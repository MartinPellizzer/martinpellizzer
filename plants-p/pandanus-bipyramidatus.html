Pandanus bipyramidatus

Pandanus bipyramidatus, commonly known as the double-pyramid pandanus, is a tropical plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and long, narrow leaves that form a dense, pyramidal shape. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences at the top of the foliage. Its leaves are often used in traditional crafts and as thatching material.

The species is adapted to coastal and sandy soils, making it a hardy plant in tropical climates.
