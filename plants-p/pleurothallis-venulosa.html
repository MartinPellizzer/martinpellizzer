Pleurothallis venulosa

Pleurothallis venulosa, commonly known as the veined pleurothallis, is a species of orchid native to the cloud forests of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its small, intricate flowers that often feature a unique veined pattern. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees and deriving moisture and nutrients from the air and rain. Its flowers are usually white or pale yellow, with a short, slender spur.

Pleurothallis venulosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts for its delicate beauty.
