Physostigma coriaceum

Physostigma coriaceum, commonly known as the African butter tree, is a flowering plant native to sub-Saharan Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, leathery leaves and produces small, white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is known for its ability to thrive in arid and semi-arid conditions, making it well adapted to dry environments. Its fruit, which resembles a small, fleshy capsule, contains seeds that are dispersed by animals.

The plant has traditional medicinal uses in some African cultures.
