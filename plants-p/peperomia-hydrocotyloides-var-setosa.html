Peperomia hydrocotyloides var. setosa

Peperomia hydrocotyloides var. setosa, commonly known as the velvet leaf peperomia, is a compact, low-growing plant native to South America.

It features thick, waxy, dark green leaves that are velvety to the touch, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers that appear in clusters, typically in the spring. It thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Its unique foliage and ease of care make it a desirable addition to both novice and experienced plant enthusiasts.
