Peucedanum ternatum

Peucedanum ternatum, commonly known as the three-lobed parsley.

It is a biennial or perennial herb native to Central Asia and parts of Europe. The plant features finely divided, fern-like leaves that are aromatic and often used in traditional medicine. Its flowers are small and yellow, arranged in umbels, typical of the Apiaceae family.

The seeds are small, ribbed, and have a distinctive bitter taste.
