Pleurogyne carinthiaca var. tetramera

Pleurogyne carinthiaca var. tetramera, commonly known as the four-leaf pleurogyne, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its small, fragrant white or pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and sandy soils, thriving in well-drained conditions. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often with a slight pubescence.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
