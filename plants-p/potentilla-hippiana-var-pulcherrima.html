Potentilla hippiana var. pulcherrima

Potentilla hippiana var. pulcherrima, commonly known as the rose campion, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has silvery-gray foliage that adds visual interest throughout the growing season. It prefers well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its compact size and long blooming period.
