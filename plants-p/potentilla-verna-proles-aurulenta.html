Potentilla verna proles aurulenta

Potentilla verna proles aurulenta, commonly known as the golden cinquefoil, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, typically from April to May. The plant has upright, branching stems covered in fine, hairy hairs, and its leaves are palmately divided into five oval-shaped lobes. It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and rocky areas.

The golden cinquefoil is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
