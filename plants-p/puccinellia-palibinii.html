Puccinellia palibinii

Puccinellia palibinii, commonly known as the narrowleaf saltbush, is a perennial grass species native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect growth habit and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in saline and alkaline soils, making it well-adapted to harsh environmental conditions. Its leaves are narrow and often have a waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss.

Puccinellia palibinii plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for livestock in its native habitats.
