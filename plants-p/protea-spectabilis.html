Protea spectabilis

Protea spectabilis, commonly known as the sugar bush, is a striking South African plant belonging to the Proteaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescences that resemble a cluster of flowers, though they are actually modified leaves called bracts. The plant typically grows in open, sunny areas with well-drained soils, often found in fynbos vegetation. Its flowers come in a range of colors, including white, pink, and red, and are pollinated by insects and birds.

Protea spectabilis is also valued for its ornamental use in horticulture and is an important species in South African biodiversity.
