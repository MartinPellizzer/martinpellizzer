Prunus integerrima

Prunus integerrima, commonly known as the Japanese cherry.

It is a deciduous tree native to Japan, characterized by its smooth, light gray bark and oval-shaped leaves. The tree produces clusters of small, white flowers in early spring, followed by red, hairy fruits. It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its aesthetic appeal.

Prunus integerrima is also valued in traditional medicine for its various therapeutic properties.
