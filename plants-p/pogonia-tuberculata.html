Pogonia tuberculata

Pogonia tuberculata, commonly known as the zigzag orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive zigzag-shaped stems and striking violet to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and requires specific soil conditions for successful growth. Its flowers are often adorned with intricate patterns and have a subtle fragrance.

Pogonia tuberculata is a rare and endangered species, with populations declining due to habitat loss and over-collection.
