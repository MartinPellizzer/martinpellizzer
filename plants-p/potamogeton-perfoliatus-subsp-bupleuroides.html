Potamogeton perfoliatus subsp. bupleuroides

Potamogeton perfoliatus subsp.

bupleuroides, commonly known as the broadleaf pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater habitats in North America. It has long, ribbon-like leaves that are often arranged in whorls around the stem, and it produces small, green flowers that are typically clustered in dense spikes.

The plant is characterized by its perfoliate leaves, which have a distinct hole in the middle, giving it a unique appearance. It thrives in shallow, slow-moving waters and plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.

This subspecies is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize sediments and improve water quality.
