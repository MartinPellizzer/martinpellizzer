Prunus × donarium f. wasinowo

Prunus × donarium f. wasinowo, commonly known as the Wasinowo cherry, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its small, sweet-tart fruits that are typically consumed fresh or used in culinary applications. The plant is a deciduous shrub or small tree, often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its attractive flowers and foliage. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

The species is also valued for its potential in breeding programs aimed at improving fruit quality and disease resistance.
