Passiflora jorullensis var. salvadorensis

Passiflora jorullensis var. salvadorensis, commonly known as the Salvadoran passionflower, is a tropical climbing vine native to Central America.

It is characterized by its striking, complex flowers that feature five sepals, five petals, and numerous stamens, often with a crown-like structure. The plant produces edible berries that are typically yellow or orange when ripe, containing a juicy pulp and seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in the understory of tropical rainforests.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and potential medicinal properties.
