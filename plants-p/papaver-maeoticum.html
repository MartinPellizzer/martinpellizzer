Papaver maeoticum

Papaver maeoticum, commonly known as the Crimean poppy, is a striking annual herb native to the Black Sea region.

It features showy, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from red to orange, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has smooth, hairy stems that can grow up to 1 meter tall, supporting its vibrant blossoms. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been cultivated in gardens for its bold, colorful display.
