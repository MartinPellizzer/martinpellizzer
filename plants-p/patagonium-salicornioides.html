Patagonium salicornioides

Patagonium salicornioides, commonly known as the Patagonian glassworts, is a halophyte native to the coastal regions of southern South America.

It thrives in saline and alkaline soils, often found in estuaries and salt marshes. The plant has succulent, cylindrical stems that are covered in a waxy cuticle to reduce water loss. Its small, scale-like leaves are adapted to store water and tolerate high salt concentrations.

Patagonium salicornioides plays a vital role in stabilizing coastal soils and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
