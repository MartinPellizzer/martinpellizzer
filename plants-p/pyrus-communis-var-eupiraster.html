Pyrus communis var. eupiraster

Pyrus communis var. eupiraster, commonly known as the European pear, is a variant of the common pear that exhibits distinct botanical characteristics.

It is a deciduous tree with a rounded crown and smooth, gray bark that becomes fissured with age. The leaves are alternate, simple, and ovate with a serrated margin, typically appearing in a deep green color during the growing season. Its flowers are white and fragrant, appearing in clusters, and it produces edible fruits that are typically harvested in the fall.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its use in both culinary and horticultural contexts.
