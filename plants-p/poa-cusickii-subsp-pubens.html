Poa cusickii subsp. pubens

Poa cusickii subsp.

pubens, commonly known as the hairy bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States. It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and fine, soft blades that are often covered in a dense layer of hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its inflorescence consists of a loose to dense panicle, with spikelets that are often tinged with blue or purple hues.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
