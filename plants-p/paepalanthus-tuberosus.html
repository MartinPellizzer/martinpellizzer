Paepalanthus tuberosus

Paepalanthus tuberosus, commonly known as the tuberous paepalanthus, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its rosette growth habit and vibrant yellow flowers. The plant produces tuberous roots that store nutrients, aiding in its survival in dry conditions. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in coastal areas.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens for its striking appearance and drought tolerance.
