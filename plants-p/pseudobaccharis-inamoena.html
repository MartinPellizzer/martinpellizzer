Pseudobaccharis inamoena

Pseudobaccharis inamoena, commonly known as the Brazilian bush or yellow flamebush, is a shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of bright yellow flowers. The plant produces small, dry fruits known as achenes, which are often dispersed by wind. It thrives in dry, sunny environments and is often found in coastal areas and sandy soils.

Pseudobaccharis inamoena is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping due to its hardy nature.
