Parrya surculosa

Parrya surculosa, commonly known as the desert lily, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, dark blue berries that are edible. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and along streambanks.

Parrya surculosa is adapted to withstand drought and extreme temperatures, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
