Plantago patagonica var. rosulata

Plantago patagonica var. rosulata, commonly known as the Patagonian plantain, is a perennial herb native to the southern regions of South America.

It features a basal rosette of lanceolate, hairy leaves that are often tinged with red or purple at the base. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in dense spikes during the spring and summer months. It thrives in dry, rocky, or sandy soils and is adapted to withstand harsh environmental conditions.

This variety is valued for its medicinal properties and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
