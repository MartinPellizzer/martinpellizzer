Paepalanthus capillaceus f. prolifer

Paepalanthus capillaceus f. prolifer, commonly known as the spreading paepalanthus, is a botanical variant of the native South American plant species.

It is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit and produces small, white to pale pink flowers. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically covered in fine, hair-like structures.

This form is noted for its ability to spread vegetatively, making it a resilient and adaptable species in its native habitat.
