Phyllostegia hillebrandii

Phyllostegia hillebrandii, commonly known as the Hillebrand's mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its opposite, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and stream banks.

Phyllostegia hillebrandii is often found in the understory of hardwood forests, where it thrives in partial to full shade.
