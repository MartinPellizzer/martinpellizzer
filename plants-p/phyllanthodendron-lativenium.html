Phyllanthodendron lativenium

Phyllanthodendron lativenium, commonly known as the Malabar ghost tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to the Western Ghats of India.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its dense, sparsely foliated canopy and distinctive, pale green to yellowish leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by fleshy, orange-red berries that are an important food source for various wildlife. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in dense forests and undergrowth.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
