Polygonum polyanthos

Polygonum polyanthos, commonly known as the Chinese everlasting or Chinese groundcherry, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to East Asia.

It features upright stems covered in soft, hairy foliage and produces clusters of small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink, red, or white. The plant is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its tolerance to partial shade. Its seeds are often used in traditional Chinese medicine, and the plant is also cultivated as an ornamental.

The leaves and flowers are edible and can be used in culinary applications.
