Placus neilgherensis

Placus neilgherensis, commonly known as the Nilgiri plume, is a flowering plant native to the Western Ghats of India.

It is a shrub that typically grows in moist, shaded environments within mountainous regions. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters, often during the monsoon season. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
