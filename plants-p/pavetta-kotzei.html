Pavetta kotzei

Pavetta kotzei, commonly known as the Natal pavetta, is a small shrub native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest undergrowth and rocky areas. The plant features simple, opposite leaves that are often pubescent, and it produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release tiny seeds.

Pavetta kotzei is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
