Papaver luteum

Papaver luteum, commonly known as the meadow poppy, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its bright yellow petals and a distinctive red-orange stigma, which contribute to its striking appearance. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its flowers bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The botanical features of Papaver luteum make it a valuable component of ecological and horticultural landscapes.
