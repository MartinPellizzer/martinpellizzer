Pinalia chrysocardium

Pinalia chrysocardium, commonly known as the golden cup plant, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of broad, waxy leaves and produces showy yellow flowers in the spring. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in dry, rocky habitats. Its flowers are pollinated primarily by bees and other insects, contributing to its ecological significance.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance in xeriscaping.
