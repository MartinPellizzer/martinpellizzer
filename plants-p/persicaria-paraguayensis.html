Persicaria paraguayensis

Persicaria paraguayensis, commonly known as Paraguay knotweed, is a herbaceous perennial native to South America.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, which includes plants commonly referred to as smartweeds or knotweeds. The plant features upright stems with alternate, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its small, greenish-white flowers are clustered in dense, cylindrical inflorescences, and it produces small, three-lobed seeds.

Persicaria paraguayensis is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of water bodies, where it thrives in moist, fertile soils.
