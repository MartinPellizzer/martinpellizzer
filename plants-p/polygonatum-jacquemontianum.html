Polygonatum jacquemontianum

Polygonatum jacquemontianum, commonly known as the Jacquemont's Solomon's Seal, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern Asia.

It features tall, slender stems that bear whorled clusters of narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in late spring, followed by red berries that ripen in autumn. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in woodlands and forest understories.

Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense colonies in suitable conditions.
