Potentilla × hybrida

Potentilla × hybrida, commonly known as the shrub rose or brambles, is a hybrid species within the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny growth habit and produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and yellow. The plant is native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens. Its leaves are typically oval-shaped with serrated edges, and the stems are woody with spines.

Potentilla × hybrida is adaptable to various soil types and thrives in full sun, making it a popular choice for hedges and landscape design.
