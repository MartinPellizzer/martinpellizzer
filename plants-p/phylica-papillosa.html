Phylica papillosa

Phylica papillosa, commonly known as the silver tree, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its silver-white, velvety leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring, followed by red, fuzzy fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Phylica papillosa is valued for its ornamental beauty and has traditional medicinal uses in some cultures.
