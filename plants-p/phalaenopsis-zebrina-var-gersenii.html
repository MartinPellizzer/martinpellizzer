Phalaenopsis zebrina var. gersenii

Phalaenopsis zebrina var. gersenii, commonly known as the Gersen's phalaenopsis, is a rare and highly sought-after orchid species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, long-lasting blooms in shades of white, pink, and purple, often with intricate patterns resembling a zebra's stripes. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. It prefers bright, indirect light and consistent humidity, making it a popular choice for indoor cultivation.

Due to its rarity and unique appearance, it is often considered a collector's item among orchid enthusiasts.
