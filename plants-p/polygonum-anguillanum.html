Polygonum anguillanum

Polygonum anguillanum, commonly known as the river spinach or water spinach, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. The plant thrives in moist environments, often found near water bodies or in wetlands. It is a member of the Polygonaceae family, which includes other plants like buckwheat and sorrel.

Polygonum anguillanum is often used in traditional medicine and as a vegetable in various cuisines.
