Phyllanthus stipitatus

Phyllanthus stipitatus, commonly known as the hairy phyllanthus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is a small shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant features simple, opposite leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it its common name. Its flowers are small and yellowish, arranged in clusters, and it produces small, round fruits.

Phyllanthus stipitatus is notable for its adaptability to various soil types and its ability to thrive in semi-arid conditions.
