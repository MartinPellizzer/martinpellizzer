Polystachya bruechertiae

Polystachya bruechertiae, commonly known as the Buechert's spikegrass, is a rare orchid species native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces striking, elongated inflorescences bearing numerous small, greenish-yellow flowers. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in cloud forests. Its botanical significance lies in its unique morphology and limited distribution, making it a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered endangered and requires protection to ensure its survival.
