Polifolia montana var. latifolia

Polifolia montana var. latifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved mountain polifolia, is a variant of the polifolia species native to mountainous regions.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, white or pale blue flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This plant is often used in ornamental gardening for its attractive foliage and modest floral display.
