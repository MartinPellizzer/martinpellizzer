Pilosella calodon subsp. phyllophora

Pilosella calodon subsp.

phyllophora, commonly known as the silverleaf pearly everlasting, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It features dense clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves that are covered in a soft, silvery down, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Its horticultural value lies in its ornamental foliage and long-lasting flowers, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.
