Puya argentea

Puya argentea, commonly known as the silver puya, is a striking plant native to the Andes of South America.

It is a member of the bromeliad family, characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that form a central cup to collect rainwater. The plant produces tall, flowering stalks that bear showy, spadix-like inflorescences with numerous small, white to yellowish flowers. Its leaves are covered in a dense layer of silvery-white hairs, giving it its common name.

Puya argentea is often cultivated for its ornamental value and unique appearance.
