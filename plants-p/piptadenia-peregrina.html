Piptadenia peregrina

Piptadenia peregrina, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or peregrina, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, pinkish-white flowers. The plant produces small, round pods that contain seeds, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It thrives in warm, tropical climates and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.

The tree can grow up to 10 meters tall and is valued for its hard, durable wood.
