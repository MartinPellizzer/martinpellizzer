Pyrgus racemosa

Pyrgus racemosa, commonly known as the showy milkweed, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the milkweed family, Asclepiadaceae, and is characterized by its striking pink to purple flowers. The plant produces a milky sap that contains toxic compounds, which deter herbivores. Its leaves are opposite and have a slightly hairy texture, while its stems are erect and branching.

Pyrgus racemosa plays an important role in supporting pollinators, particularly butterflies.
