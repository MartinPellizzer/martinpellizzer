Phalangium obsoletum

Phalangium obsoletum, commonly known as the slender centipede.

This species belongs to the order Lithobiomorpha and is characterized by its elongated, segmented body covered in chitinous exoskeleton. It possesses numerous pairs of jointed legs, typically more than 20, which aid in its agile movement through moist environments. The plant is primarily found in humid, forested areas where it thrives in leaf litter and soil.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological role, it plays a significant part in soil aeration and nutrient cycling.
