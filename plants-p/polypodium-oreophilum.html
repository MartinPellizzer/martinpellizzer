Polypodium oreophilum

Polypodium oreophilum, commonly known as the mountain fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Polypodiaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped fronds that are typically dark green and covered in fine hairs. This plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its rhizome is thick and creeping, allowing it to spread and form dense mats.

Polypodium oreophilum plays an important role in forest ecosystems by contributing to soil stability and providing habitat for various microorganisms.
