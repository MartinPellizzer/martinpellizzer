Potamogeton × decipiens var. longifolius

Potamogeton × decipiens var. longifolius, commonly known as the longleaf pondweed, is a hybrid aquatic plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its long, narrow leaves that can grow up to 30 cm in length, often with a smooth or slightly serrated margin. The plant typically inhabits freshwater environments such as ponds, lakes, and slow-moving streams. It produces small, greenish flowers that are arranged in dense spikes, and it plays a role in providing habitat for aquatic invertebrates and fish.

Its presence is often an indicator of good water quality in its ecosystem.
