Polygala solieri

Polygala solieri, commonly known as the golden crown or Solomon's seal, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its delicate, feather-like leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its flowers, which range in color from white to yellow, are pollinated by insects, and the plant produces small, spherical fruits.

Polygala solieri is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow in well-drained conditions.
