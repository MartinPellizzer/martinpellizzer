Pandanus crassipes

Pandanus crassipes, commonly known as the broad-leaf pandanus, is a tropical plant native to the Indo-Pacific region.

It is characterized by its large, broad, sword-shaped leaves that can grow up to several meters in length. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that eventually develop into spiky, fibrous fruits. Its root system is robust, allowing it to thrive in coastal and wetland environments.

The leaves are often used in traditional crafts and have cultural significance in various island communities.
