Pseudotectaria decaryana

Pseudotectaria decaryana, commonly known as the broadleaf fern, is a species of fern that belongs to the family Tectariaceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia, including parts of India, China, and Southeast Asia. The plant is characterized by its large, broad, lance-shaped leaves that are typically divided into segments. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest undergrowth.

The fronds are evergreen and can grow up to several feet in length, making it a striking ornamental plant in gardens.
