Platycodon grandiflorus f. angustifolius

Platycodon grandiflorus f. angustifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaf bellflower, is a variant of the Chinese bellflower.

It is characterized by its slender, elongated leaves that are narrower than those of the typical species. The plant produces bell-shaped flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. It is native to regions of East Asia, including Korea and China.

This cultivar is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
