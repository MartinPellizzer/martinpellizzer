Polypodium fossum

Polypodium fossum, commonly known as the golden polypody, is a species of fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, lace-like fronds that are typically bright green with a yellowish hue. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical forests. Its rhizome is thick and creeping, allowing it to spread and form dense mats.

The spores are produced on the underside of the fronds, contributing to its reproductive strategy in moist, forested habitats.
