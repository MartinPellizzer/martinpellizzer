Phaseolus schottii f. brasiliensis

Phaseolus schottii f. brasiliensis, commonly known as the Brasiliensis bean, is a botanical variant native to the Amazon rainforest region of South America.

It is a climbing legume characterized by its trifoliate leaves and elongated, slender pods. The plant produces small, bean-like seeds that are typically pale yellow to brown in color. It thrives in humid, tropical climates with well-drained soil, often found in shaded undergrowth.

This species is valued for its potential in sustainable agriculture due to its nitrogen-fixing capabilities and adaptability to diverse ecological conditions.
