Prunus serotina lusus pyramidalis

Prunus serotina lusus pyramidalis, commonly known as the pyramidal black cherry, is a cultivar of the black cherry tree native to North America.

It is characterized by its narrow, conical shape and dark, glossy leaves that turn vibrant shades of red and orange in the fall. The tree produces small, edible berries that are a food source for various wildlife species. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for landscaping in temperate climates.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is often planted in gardens and natural areas.
