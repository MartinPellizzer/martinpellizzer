Pyrus jurana

Pyrus jurana, commonly known as the Japanese whitebeam, is a deciduous tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and small, oval-shaped leaves that turn yellow in the fall. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers in early spring, followed by edible, yellowish fruits. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a suitable ornamental plant in gardens.

The species is also valued for its ecological benefits, providing food and habitat for various wildlife species.
