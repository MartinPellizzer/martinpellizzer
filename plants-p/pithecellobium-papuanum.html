Pithecellobium papuanum

Pithecellobium papuanum, commonly known as the Papuan Cattleleg, is a species of legume native to the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its climbing habit, with long, slender stems that often twine around supports. The plant produces small, yellow flowers followed by bean-like pods containing edible seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in coastal and lowland areas.

Due to its nutritional value, it is sometimes cultivated for its seeds, which are rich in protein and minerals.
