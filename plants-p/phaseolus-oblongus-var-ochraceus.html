Phaseolus oblongus var. ochraceus

Phaseolus oblongus var. ochraceus, commonly known as the orange runner bean, is a variant of the runner bean species native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its distinctive orange-colored pods and yellow flowers, which bloom in clusters. The plant is a climbing vine with compound leaves and produces edible beans that are often used in culinary applications. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is valued for both its ornamental appeal and nutritional content.
