Packera werneriifolia

Packera werneriifolia, commonly known as Werneri's fleabane, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and ray florets. The plant typically grows in moist meadows and streambanks, preferring cool, temperate climates. Its leaves are deeply lobed and often covered in a fine white down, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It produces small yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting various pollinators.
