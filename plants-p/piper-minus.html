Piper minus

Piper minus, commonly known as lesser pepper or slender pepper, is a climbing shrub native to tropical regions of Asia and the Pacific.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many species of pepper plants. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers and red, peppercorn-like berries that are often used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are ovate to lanceolate, with a glossy surface and a strong, pungent aroma.

The plant is valued for its medicinal properties and is sometimes used in herbal remedies for its purported anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial effects.
