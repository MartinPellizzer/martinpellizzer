Pavonia fruticulosa

Pavonia fruticulosa, commonly known as the scarlet bower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring upright stems and dense foliage. The plant produces showy, red or pink bract-like flowers that are clustered in the leaf axils, giving it a bower-like appearance. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soils, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

The botanical features of Pavonia fruticulosa make it both visually striking and ecologically beneficial.
