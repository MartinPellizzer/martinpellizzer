Phreatia hartleyi

Phreatia hartleyi, commonly known as the Hartley's orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of yellow and green, often with intricate patterns on the petals. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained, organic soil.

Phreatia hartleyi is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
