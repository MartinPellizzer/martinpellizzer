Pecteilis vatia

Pecteilis vatia, commonly known as the mountain morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of South America.

It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is characterized by its striking violet to purple flowers that bloom in the early morning. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and twining stems that climb along rocky surfaces. It thrives in cool, humid climates and is often found in cloud forests.
