Piper glabribaccum

Piper glabribaccum, commonly known as the smooth-leaved pepper vine, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species. The plant is characterized by its smooth, glossy leaves and small, clustered flowers that develop into berries. Its fruits are typically green when immature and turn red when mature, attracting various bird species.

The plant is often used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
