Pseudolopezia insignis

Pseudolopezia insignis, commonly known as the desert aster, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single bloom. The plant typically grows from a basal rosette of narrow, silver-haired leaves, which help reduce water loss in its harsh environment. Its showy purple to blue flowers bloom in late spring or early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Pseudolopezia insignis is adapted to survive in low-nutrient soils and can withstand extreme temperature fluctuations.
