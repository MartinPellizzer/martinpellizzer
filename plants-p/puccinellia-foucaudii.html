Puccinellia foucaudii

Puccinellia foucaudii, commonly known as the desert grass or Foucaud's grass, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa and the Middle East.

It is a perennial grass that typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is well adapted to harsh environmental conditions. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny flowers. It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soils and providing forage for livestock in its native habitats.

Puccinellia foucaudii is also valued for its resilience and is sometimes cultivated in restoration projects.
