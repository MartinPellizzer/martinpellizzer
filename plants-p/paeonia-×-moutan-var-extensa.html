Paeonia × moutan var. extensa

Paeonia × moutan var. extensa, commonly known as the Chinese tree peony, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that come in a variety of colors, including red, pink, and white. The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges and produces woody stems that can grow up to several meters in height. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens.

Its botanical significance lies in its role as a parent species in the development of many cultivated peony varieties.
