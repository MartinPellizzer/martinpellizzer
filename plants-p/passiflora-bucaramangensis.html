Passiflora bucaramangensis

Passiflora bucaramangensis, commonly known as the Bucaramanga passionflower, is a climbing vine native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, intricate flowers that feature a blend of blue, purple, and white hues, with prominent stamens and a crown-like structure. The plant produces edible, orange-red berries that are rich in antioxidants and have a sweet, tangy flavor. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in the understory of rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
