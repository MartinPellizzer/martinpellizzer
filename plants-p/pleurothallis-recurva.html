Pleurothallis recurva

Pleurothallis recurva, commonly known as the recurved pleurothallis, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to the tropical regions of South America.

This epiphytic plant typically grows on trees or rocks in humid, shaded environments. It is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that are often pale green or yellow in color. The plant has lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of fragrant blooms.

Due to its unique appearance and growth habits, it is a popular choice among orchid enthusiasts.
