Penstemon attenuatus var. pseudoprocerus

Penstemon attenuatus var. pseudoprocerus, commonly known as the slender beardtongue, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and tubular, two-lipped flowers that typically range in color from pink to purple. The plant produces showy, spurred flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native plant gardens.
