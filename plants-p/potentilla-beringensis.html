Potentilla beringensis

Potentilla beringensis, commonly known as Bering's cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the Arctic and subarctic regions of North America and Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial shrub that typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features wiry, often hairy stems and basal leaves that are typically divided into five lobes. Its small, yellow flowers bloom in late summer and are clustered in dense, rounded inflorescences.

Potentilla beringensis is adapted to cold climates and is often found in tundra and alpine environments.
