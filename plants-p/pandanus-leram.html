Pandanus leram

Pandanus leram, commonly known as the bottlebrush pandanus, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and long, narrow leaves that are often used in traditional weaving. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically clustered in spikes at the top of the stem. Its fruit is a fibrous, spiky structure that contains edible seeds.

Pandanus leram is often cultivated for its ornamental value and cultural significance in local communities.
