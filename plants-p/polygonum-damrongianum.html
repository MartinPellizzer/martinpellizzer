Polygonum damrongianum

Polygonum damrongianum, commonly known as Damrong's knotweed, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, which includes species known for their distinctive leaf shapes and clusters of small flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its leaves are broadly ovate with a prominent midrib and are often covered in fine hairs.

The flowers are arranged in dense, cylindrical inflorescences and are usually pink or red in color.
