Praxelis insignis

Praxelis insignis, commonly known as the golden crownbeard, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features slender, branched stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. Its most distinctive feature is the yellow-orange flower head, which consists of numerous small florets arranged in a radiating pattern.

Praxelis insignis is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
