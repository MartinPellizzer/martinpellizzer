Pimelea decussata var. diosmifolia

Pimelea decussata var. diosmifolia, commonly known as the golden everlasting or yellow everlasting, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is a low-growing shrub with narrow, linear leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and is often used in rock gardens or as a ground cover. It prefers well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for coastal and dry garden environments.

Its long-lasting flowers make it a popular choice for floral arrangements.
