Pouteria unilocularis

Pouteria unilocularis, commonly known as the Brazil nut tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is characterized by its large, compound leaves and yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters. The tree produces a hard-shelled fruit containing multiple seeds, which are the edible Brazil nuts. Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.

The plant is also valued for its economic contribution through the commercial harvesting of its seeds.
