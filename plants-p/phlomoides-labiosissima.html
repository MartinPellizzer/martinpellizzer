Phlomoides labiosissima

Phlomoides labiosissima, commonly known as the lip-shaped phlomoides, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from purple to pink. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy, and it thrives in full sun to partial shade. It is drought-tolerant and prefers well-drained soils, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
