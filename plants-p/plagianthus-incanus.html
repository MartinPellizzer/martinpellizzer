Plagianthus incanus

Plagianthus incanus, commonly known as the silver tree, is a striking evergreen shrub or small tree native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its silver-gray, fuzzy leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in dense, rounded inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular ornamental plant.

Due to its unique foliage and drought tolerance, it is often used in xeriscaping and coastal gardens.
