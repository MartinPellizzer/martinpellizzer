Potamogeton nodosus f. spathulifolius

Potamogeton nodosus f. spathulifolius, commonly known as the broad-leaf pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant characterized by its distinctive, broad, spatulate leaves.

It typically grows in shallow, freshwater habitats such as lakes, ponds, and slow-moving streams. The plant has a branching stem system with small, inconspicuous flowers that are often submerged. Its leaves are usually arranged in whorls and can vary in shape depending on environmental conditions.

This subspecies is often found in nutrient-rich waters and plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
