Paspalum jujuyense

Paspalum jujuyense, commonly known as the Jujuy grass, is a perennial grass species native to South America, particularly found in Argentina and Chile.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, bluish-green leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky soils.

Paspalum jujuyense is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping due to its hardy nature.
