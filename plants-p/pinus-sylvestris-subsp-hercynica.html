Pinus sylvestris subsp. hercynica

Pinus sylvestris subsp.

hercynica, commonly known as the Carpathian Scots pine, is a subspecies native to the Carpathian Mountains and surrounding regions. It is a tall evergreen conifer with slender, needle-like leaves that are typically 1–2 cm long.

The tree produces cones that are typically 5–8 cm in length, containing edible seeds that are dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, temperate climates.

This subspecies is often used in reforestation efforts due to its resilience and ecological importance.
