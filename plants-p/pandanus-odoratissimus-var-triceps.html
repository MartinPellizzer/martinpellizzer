Pandanus odoratissimus var. triceps

Pandanus odoratissimus var. triceps, commonly known as the sweet sedge or fragrant pandanus, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, sword-shaped leaves that are often used in traditional crafts and as a source of fiber. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found near water bodies.

Its unique appearance and utility make it an important species in both ecological and cultural contexts.
