Penstemon × edithae

Penstemon × edithae, commonly known as the Edith's beardtongue, is a hybrid perennial plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of tubular, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a soft, fuzzy appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Penstemon × edithae is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
