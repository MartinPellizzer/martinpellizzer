Phalaris canariensis subsp. brachystachys

Phalaris canariensis subsp.

brachystachys, commonly known as the Canary grass, is a perennial grass species native to the Canary Islands. It features slender, erect stems that can grow up to 1 meter in height, with narrow, grass-like leaves.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and open habitats.

Its botanical structure makes it adaptable to various environmental conditions, contributing to its ecological significance.
