Populus petrowskiana

Populus petrowskiana, commonly known as the Petrowskiana poplar, is a rare and critically endangered species native to the Russian Far East.

It is characterized by its distinctive heart-shaped leaves and its ability to thrive in moist, temperate climates. The plant belongs to the Salicaceae family, which includes willows and poplars, and is known for its rapid growth rate. Its bark is smooth and light gray, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in the spring.
