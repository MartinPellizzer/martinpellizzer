Plantago major f. dilatata

Plantago major f. dilatata, commonly known as the broadleaf plantain, is a variant of the common plantain with distinctive botanical features.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, disturbed soils across temperate regions. The leaves are broad, heart-shaped, and have a wavy margin, with a dense covering of fine hairs. It produces small, greenish flowers in a spike-like inflorescence, which are wind-pollinated.

This plant is notable for its adaptability and is often used in traditional medicine for its healing properties.
