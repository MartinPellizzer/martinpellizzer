Pandanus chelyon

Pandanus chelyon, commonly known as the hairy pandanus, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, feather-like leaves that are often covered in a fuzzy texture. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. Its roots are fibrous and can be used in traditional crafts and construction.

The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soil and is often found in coastal or swampy areas.
