Piper caudatifolium

Piper caudatifolium, commonly known as the cat-tail pepper, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its long, trailing stems and heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its small, greenish-white flowers are followed by small, fleshy berries that turn red when mature.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes grown in gardens for its unique foliage.
