Premna angustifolia

Premna angustifolia, commonly known as narrow-leaf glorybower, is a shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It features opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are typically glossy and have a smooth or slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, fragrant, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the summer months. Its fruits are small, three-lobed capsules that release tiny seeds when mature.

Premna angustifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade.
