Primula kweichouensis var. guangxiensis

Primula kweichouensis var. guangxiensis, commonly known as the Guangxi primrose, is a flowering plant native to southern China, particularly in Guangxi Province.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink and white. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in forested areas or along rocky slopes. Its foliage consists of broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
