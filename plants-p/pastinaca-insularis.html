Pastinaca insularis

Pastinaca insularis, commonly known as the island burdock, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features a thick, hairy stem and broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are typically purple or pink, arranged in clusters, and it produces small, burr-like fruits that aid in seed dispersal.

Pastinaca insularis is often found in rocky or coastal areas, thriving in well-drained soils.
