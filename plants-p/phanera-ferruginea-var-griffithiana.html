Phanera ferruginea var. griffithiana

Phanera ferruginea var. griffithiana, commonly known as the red-veined begonia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves with prominent reddish veins. The plant produces clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in the spring. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for both gardeners and botanists alike.
