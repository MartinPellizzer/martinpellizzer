Panicum indicum var. gracile

Panicum indicum var. gracile, commonly known as slender fingergrass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, hairless stems and small, spike-like inflorescences that produce numerous tiny florets. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often with a bluish-green hue, and it is often used in ornamental gardening.

This species is also valued for its role in soil stabilization and as a forage plant for livestock.
