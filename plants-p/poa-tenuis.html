Poa tenuis

Poa tenuis, commonly known as slender meadowgrass, is a species of grass native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is a low-growing perennial plant characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, pastures, and along stream banks. Its botanical features include a fibrous root system and a clump-forming growth habit.

Poa tenuis plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various small invertebrates.
