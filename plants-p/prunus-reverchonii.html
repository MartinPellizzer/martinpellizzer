Prunus reverchonii

Prunus reverchonii, commonly known as the Mexican cherry or Reverchon's cherry, is a small evergreen shrub native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its dense foliage and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, red drupes that are edible but not widely cultivated for commercial use. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening in suitable climates.

Prunus reverchonii is also valued for its ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
