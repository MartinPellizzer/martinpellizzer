Penstemon menziesii var. crassifolius

Penstemon menziesii var. crassifolius, commonly known as the broadleaf penstemon, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves and showy, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and arid environments, making it well adapted to dry climates. It is often found in mountainous regions and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.

This variety is also noted for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
