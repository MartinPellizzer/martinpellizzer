Polemonium confertum var. elatius

Polemonium confertum var. elatius, commonly known as the tall bluebells, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Polemoniaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to purple bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, with lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in moist, woodland habitats and is often found in shaded areas with rich, well-drained soil.

The flowers bloom in early to mid-summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
