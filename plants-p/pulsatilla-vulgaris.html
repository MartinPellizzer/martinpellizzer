Pulsatilla vulgaris

Pulsatilla vulgaris, commonly known as the pasque flower, is a herbaceous perennial native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its showy, early spring blooms that are typically pink or purple. The plant has a short, thick rhizome and hairy stems that support its large, solitary flowers. Its leaves are palmately divided and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Pulsatilla vulgaris is often found in alpine and rocky habitats, thriving in well-drained soils.
