Pavonia sidifolia

Pavonia sidifolia, commonly known as the swordleaf false poinsettia, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features lance-shaped, dark green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a textured appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pink flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. Its growth habit is upright, with stems that can reach heights of up to 3 feet.

It thrives in partial shade to full sun and is well-suited for moist, well-drained soils.
