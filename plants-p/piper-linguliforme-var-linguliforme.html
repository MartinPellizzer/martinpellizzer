Piper linguliforme var. linguliforme

Piper linguliforme var. linguliforme, commonly known as the creeping pepper vine, is a tropical plant native to regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and small, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by elliptical, red or purple berries. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has a mild, peppery flavor.

The species is part of the Piper genus, which includes many economically important spice plants.
