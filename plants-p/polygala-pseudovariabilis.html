Polygala pseudovariabilis

Polygala pseudovariabilis, commonly known as the variable milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia, including China and Russia.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its small, often pink or white flowers arranged in dense clusters. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, thriving in well-drained soils. It has slender stems and compound leaves, with each leaflet being narrow and linear.

While not widely cultivated, it is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and ecological adaptations.
