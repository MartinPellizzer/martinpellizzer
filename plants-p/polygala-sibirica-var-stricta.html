Polygala sibirica var. stricta

Polygala sibirica var. stricta, commonly known as the Siberian milk-vetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the cold regions of Asia.

It features slender, erect stems that are often branched and covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, delicate flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, arranged in racemose inflorescences. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and have a slight pubescence.

This species is adapted to grow in dry, sandy or rocky soils and is often found in alpine or steppe environments.
