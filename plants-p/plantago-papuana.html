Plantago papuana

Plantago papuana, commonly known as the Papuan plantain, is a species of flowering plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its broad, oval-shaped leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in a dense spike, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and lowland areas.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes used in traditional medicine in its native regions.
