Pleurothallis pectinata var. major

Pleurothallis pectinata var. major, commonly known as the "Pineapple Orchid," is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to the cloud forests of Central and South America.

This species is characterized by its compact, epiphytic growth habit and its ability to thrive in high humidity and indirect light conditions. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and yellow, often arranged in a raceme. Its leaves are typically lanceolate and covered in a fine, waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss.

Due to its ornamental value and unique appearance, it is a popular choice for indoor cultivation and botanical collections.
