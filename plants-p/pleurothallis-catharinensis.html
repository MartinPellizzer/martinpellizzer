Pleurothallis catharinensis

Pleurothallis catharinensis, commonly known as the Catarin's orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its compact growth habit and intricate floral structure. The plant typically produces small, delicate flowers in shades of white and pink, often with a distinctive lip or labellum. It thrives in shaded, humid environments and is often found in cloud forests.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
