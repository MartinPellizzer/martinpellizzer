Pseudoxytenanthera madhavii

Pseudoxytenanthera madhavii, commonly known as the Madhavii flame tree, is a tropical tree belonging to the family Fabaceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its striking red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has compound leaves with small, ovate leaflets and produces long, slender pods that contain seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The tree is also valued for its hard wood, which is used in traditional crafts and construction.
