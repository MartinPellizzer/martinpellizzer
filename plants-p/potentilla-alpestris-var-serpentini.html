Potentilla alpestris var. serpentini

Potentilla alpestris var. serpentini, commonly known as the Alpine cinquefoil, is a low-growing, mat-forming plant native to alpine regions of Europe.

It features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has narrow, serrated leaves that are typically covered in a fine, white pubescence. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in mountainous habitats.

Its botanical characteristics make it well adapted to harsh, high-altitude environments.
