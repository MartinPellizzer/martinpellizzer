Pachylobus deliciosus

Pachylobus deliciosus, commonly known as the sweet thorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow, pea-like flowers. The plant produces pods that contain edible seeds, which are often used in traditional cuisine. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions.

Its botanical features make it an important species for both ecological and cultural reasons in its native range.
