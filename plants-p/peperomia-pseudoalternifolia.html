Peperomia pseudoalternifolia

Peperomia pseudoalternifolia, commonly known as the zigzag peperomia, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to Central and South America.

It features glossy, lance-shaped leaves that often have a zigzag or wavy margin, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers in terminal clusters, which are followed by reddish-brown berries. It thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Its unique foliage makes it a striking addition to any plant collection.
