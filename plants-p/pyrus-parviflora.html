Pyrus parviflora

Pyrus parviflora, commonly known as the Japanese whitebeam, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are often white-veined and have a distinctive silvery appearance. The plant produces small, white or greenish flowers in early spring, which are clustered in inflorescences. Its fruit is a small, round berry that ripens in late summer.

Pyrus parviflora is valued in horticulture for its ornamental foliage and adaptability to various soil conditions.
