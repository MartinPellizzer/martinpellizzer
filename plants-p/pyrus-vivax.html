Pyrus vivax

Pyrus vivax, commonly known as the Japanese whitebeam, is a species of flowering plant in the family Rosaceae.

It is native to Japan and is characterized by its small, white flowers and smooth, dark green leaves. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically yellow or green when mature. It is often cultivated as an ornamental tree due to its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to various soil conditions.

Pyrus vivax is also valued for its ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
