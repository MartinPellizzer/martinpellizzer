Philodendron hebetatum

Philodendron hebetatum, commonly known as the elephant's ear, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 3 feet in length. The plant thrives in warm, humid environments with bright, indirect light. Its foliage is often a deep green with a metallic sheen, making it a striking ornamental plant.

Philodendron hebetatum prefers well-draining soil and regular watering to maintain its lush appearance.
