Persoonia mollis subsp. maxima

Persoonia mollis subsp.

maxima, commonly known as the silky persoonia, is a flowering plant native to Western Australia. It is characterized by its soft, hairy leaves and clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers.

The plant typically grows in sandy soils and is often found in coastal or heathland environments. Its flowers are pollinated by insects, and it produces small, woody fruits that contain seeds.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
