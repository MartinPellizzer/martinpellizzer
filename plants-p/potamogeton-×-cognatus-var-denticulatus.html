Potamogeton × cognatus var. denticulatus

Potamogeton × cognatus var. denticulatus, commonly known as the denticulate pondweed, is a hybrid aquatic plant that thrives in freshwater environments.

It is characterized by its long, slender leaves that are often submerged and exhibit a serrated or toothed edge, giving it its specific name. This plant typically grows in shallow to moderately deep water, often in still or slow-moving water bodies. Its reproductive structures include small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually submerged, contributing to its role in aquatic ecosystems.

It plays an important role in providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
