Phyllanthus bossiaeoides

Phyllanthus bossiaeoides, commonly known as the African milk bush, is a shrub native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its small, oval-shaped leaves that are usually glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, white flowers arranged in clusters, followed by small, fleshy fruits that contain a single seed. It is often found in dry, sandy soils and can thrive in arid conditions.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional medicinal uses.
