Prunus fasciculata var. fasciculata

Prunus fasciculata var. fasciculata, commonly known as the Japanese cherry or dwarf cherry, is a small deciduous tree or shrub native to Japan.

It is characterized by its delicate, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in early spring, often before the leaves emerge. The plant has small, ovate leaves that turn yellow in the fall, adding to its ornamental value. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

Its compact growth habit and beautiful flowering make it a valued addition to horticultural collections.
