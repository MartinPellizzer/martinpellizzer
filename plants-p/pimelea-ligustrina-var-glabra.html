Pimelea ligustrina var. glabra

Pimelea ligustrina var. glabra, commonly known as the smooth pimelea, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Australia.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are usually smooth and slightly hairy. The plant produces small, clustered white or pale yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or heathlands.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
