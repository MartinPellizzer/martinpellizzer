Plectranthus lophanthoides

Plectranthus lophanthoides, commonly known as the woolly plectranthus, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy leaves and clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant typically grows as a perennial herb, often reaching heights of up to 1 meter. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Plectranthus lophanthoides is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
