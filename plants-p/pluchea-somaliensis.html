Pluchea somaliensis

Pluchea somaliensis, commonly known as Somali groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Somalia and Ethiopia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and medicinal properties. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are small and yellow, arranged in clusters.

Pluchea somaliensis is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties.
