Paeonia peregrina var. villosa

Paeonia peregrina var. villosa, commonly known as the woolly peony, is a rare and ornamental plant native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, or villosa, stems and large, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are compound, with finely divided foliage, and it produces distinctive, often fragrant blooms in late spring.

Due to its limited distribution and unique appearance, it is a prized species among botanists and horticultural enthusiasts.
