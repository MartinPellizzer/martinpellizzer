Phegopteris rustica

Phegopteris rustica, commonly known as the rusty maidenhair fern, is a species of fern native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its delicate, bright green fronds with a distinctive rusty-colored margin. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its fronds are bi-lobed and have a soft, feathery appearance.

Phegopteris rustica is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
