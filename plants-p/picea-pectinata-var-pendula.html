Picea pectinata var. pendula

Picea pectinata var. pendula, commonly known as the weeping spruce, is a striking ornamental conifer characterized by its cascading, drooping branches that create a unique, ethereal appearance.

Native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, this variety of spruce is adapted to cold climates and high altitudes. Its foliage consists of needle-like leaves that are typically dark green, providing a dense and lush canopy. The tree produces small, cone-like structures that contain seeds, contributing to its reproductive cycle.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and parks for its visual appeal and resilience in harsh conditions.
