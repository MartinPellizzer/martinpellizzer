Poa rauhii

Poa rauhii, commonly known as the Rauh's bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the Pacific Northwest of the United States.

It is characterized by its fine, dark green leaves and dense, tufted growth habit. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear blue to purple flowers, which are typical of the Poa genus. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.

Poa rauhii is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
