Potentilla inclinata

Potentilla inclinata, commonly known as the bentleaf cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in moist, acidic soils found in woodlands and stream banks. The plant produces small, yellow flowers in clusters during the summer months. Its leaves are characterized by their oval shape and distinctive, slightly curved base.

Potentilla inclinata is often used in native plant gardens due to its adaptability and ornamental value.
