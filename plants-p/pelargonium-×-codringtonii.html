Pelargonium × codringtonii

Pelargonium × codringtonii, commonly known as the Codrington geranium, is a hybrid perennial plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, rounded foliage and showy, five-petaled flowers that range in color from pink to red. The plant typically grows to a height of 10–30 cm and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It prefers well-drained soil and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant in gardens.

Pelargonium × codringtonii is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
