Paeonia quinquecapsularis

Paeonia quinquecapsularis, commonly known as the five-capsuled peony, is a species native to the mountainous regions of central and eastern Asia.

It is a deciduous herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters. The plant features large, showy flowers with five to seven petals, often in shades of red, pink, or white. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several leaflets arranged in a feather-like pattern.

The fruit is a follicle, which splits open to release seeds, a characteristic feature of the Paeonia genus.
