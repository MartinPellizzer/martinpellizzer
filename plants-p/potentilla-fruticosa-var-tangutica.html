Potentilla fruticosa var. tangutica

Potentilla fruticosa var. tangutica, commonly known as the Tangut rose or Tangut cinquefoil, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny growth and small, rose-like flowers that bloom in shades of yellow to pink. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces small, red or orange berries in the fall.

This hardy species is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in challenging conditions.
