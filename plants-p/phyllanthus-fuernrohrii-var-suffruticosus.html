Phyllanthus fuernrohrii var. suffruticosus

Phyllanthus fuernrohrii var. suffruticosus, commonly known as the African spurge, is a shrub-like plant native to parts of Africa.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers that are often surrounded by modified leaves called bracts. It is typically found in moist, wooded areas and can grow up to several meters in height.

The species is known for its adaptability to various soil types and its ability to thrive in semi-shade conditions.
