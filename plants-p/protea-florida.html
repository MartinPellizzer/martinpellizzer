Protea florida

Protea florida, commonly known as the king protea, is a striking South African flowering plant belonging to the Proteaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescence composed of numerous petal-like bracts that can range in color from white to pink, red, and even yellow. The plant typically grows as an evergreen shrub, reaching heights of up to 3 meters, with leathery, elliptical leaves that are usually arranged in a spiral pattern. Native to the Western and Eastern Cape provinces of South Africa, it thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Due to its ornamental value, it is widely cultivated in gardens and used in floral arrangements worldwide.
