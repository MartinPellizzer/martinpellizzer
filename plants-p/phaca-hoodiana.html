Phaca hoodiana

Phaca hoodiana, commonly known as the Hood's phaca, is a rare and unique plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking, hood-shaped flowers that are typically violet or purple in color. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires a sunny to partially shaded environment.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, Phaca hoodiana is considered a species of conservation concern.
