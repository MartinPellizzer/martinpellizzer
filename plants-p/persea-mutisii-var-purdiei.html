Persea mutisii var. purdiei

Persea mutisii var. purdiei, commonly known as the Purdie's spondias, is a tropical tree native to parts of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by edible, orange-colored fruits. Its bark is rough and fissured, often exuding a fragrant resin.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential medicinal properties.
