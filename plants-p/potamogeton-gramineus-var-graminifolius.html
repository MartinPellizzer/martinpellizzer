Potamogeton gramineus var. graminifolius

Potamogeton gramineus var. graminifolius, commonly known as narrowleaf pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in North America.

It has long, slender, ribbon-like leaves that are typically arranged in whorls around the stem. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often submerged, followed by cylindrical, wind-dispersed fruits. It thrives in slow-moving to still waters with soft, muddy or sandy substrates.

This species plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
