Pterococcus leucocladus

Pterococcus leucocladus, commonly known as the white-leaf pterococcus, is a small evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Leguminosae (Fabaceae) and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are typically white or pale green. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in racemes, followed by pod-like fruits containing several seeds. It is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories.
