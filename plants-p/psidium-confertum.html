Psidium confertum

Psidium confertum, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or pitaya.

It is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to South America, particularly Brazil. The plant produces small, edible fruits that are typically red or yellow when ripe. Its leaves are elliptical and glossy, and it has a shallow root system that makes it suitable for container gardening.

The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
