Pilosella privigna

Pilosella privigna, commonly known as the narrow-leaved mouse-ear, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, hairy leaves and small, yellowish-green composite flowers. Native to southern Europe and western Asia, it thrives in rocky or gravelly soils. The plant is often found in open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges.

Its foliage and flowers are adapted to withstand dry conditions, making it a resilient species in its native range.
