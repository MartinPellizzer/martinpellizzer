Phoebe mexicana var. bourgeauana

Phoebe mexicana var. bourgeauana, commonly known as the Mexican beech, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and elliptical leaves that are typically dark green on the upper surface and lighter on the underside. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers in the spring, followed by spherical, woody fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for cultivation in temperate climates.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural study.
