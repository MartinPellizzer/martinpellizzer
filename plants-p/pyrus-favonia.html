Pyrus favonia

Pyrus favonia, commonly known as the favonia pear, is a species of flowering plant in the family Rosaceae.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and is characterized by its deciduous nature, producing small, yellowish flowers in early spring. The plant features alternate, simple, and ovate leaves with a rough texture. Its fruits are small pears that are typically not cultivated for food but are valued for ornamental purposes.

Pyrus favonia is often grown in gardens for its aesthetic appeal and historical significance in horticulture.
