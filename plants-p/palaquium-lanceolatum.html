Palaquium lanceolatum

Palaquium lanceolatum, commonly known as the lanceleaf soursop, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and fragrant, yellowish-green flowers. The tree produces small, round fruits with a thick, spiny rind and a sweet, aromatic pulp. Its botanical features make it valuable for both ornamental purposes and local use in traditional medicine.

The plant thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland rainforests.
