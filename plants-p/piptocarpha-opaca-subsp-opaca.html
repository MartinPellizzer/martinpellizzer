Piptocarpha opaca subsp. opaca

Piptocarpha opaca subsp.

opaca, commonly known as the narrowleaf bluebunch wheatgrass, is a perennial grass native to the western United States. It is characterized by its slender, blue-green leaves and compact, tufted growth habit.

This subspecies thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in sagebrush steppe and desert scrub ecosystems. Its flowering heads are small, spike-like clusters that produce wind-pollinated flowers.

The plant is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.
