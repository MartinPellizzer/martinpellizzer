Pyxidaria diffusa

Pyxidaria diffusa, commonly known as the spreading bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. It is often found in woodland edges and along streambanks, where it contributes to the ecological diversity of these habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinator populations.
