Plantago guilleminiana

Plantago guilleminiana, commonly known as the Guillemin's plantain, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in a spike, which are typically pollinated by wind. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to high-altitude environments.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated as a ornamental plant in gardens with well-drained soil.
