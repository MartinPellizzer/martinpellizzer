Persea costata

Persea costata, commonly known as the Mexican persimmon, is a small evergreen tree native to Mexico and Central America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its leathery, elliptical leaves with a smooth, dark green upper surface and a lighter underside. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by orange to reddish fruits that are edible when fully ripe. The fruit has a sweet, honey-like flavor and is often consumed fresh or used in culinary applications.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value as well as its fruit.
