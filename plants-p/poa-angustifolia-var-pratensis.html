Poa angustifolia var. pratensis

Poa angustifolia var. pratensis, commonly known as narrowleaf bluegrass, is a cool-season grass native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant forms dense, low-growing sods and is often found in moist, well-drained soils. It has small, spike-like inflorescences with numerous tiny flowers.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping and turfgrass mixes.
