Piper piedadesense

Piper piedadesense, commonly known as the Piedades pepper or Brazilian pepper, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to the Atlantic rainforests of northeastern Brazil and is characterized by its climbing habit and compound leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in spikes, and it is known for its aromatic, peppery fruits. Piper piedadesense is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties.

Its ecological importance lies in its role as a food source for various insects and birds in its native habitat.
