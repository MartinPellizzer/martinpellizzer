Patagonium morenonis var. argenteum

Patagonium morenonis var. argenteum, commonly known as the silverleaf cushion plant, is a succulent species native to the high-altitude regions of southern South America.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and fleshy, silver-gray leaves that reflect sunlight and reduce water loss. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow and orange, typically blooming in late spring. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing on rocky slopes or in gravelly soils.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant in dry gardens.
