Phymosia

Phymosia, commonly known as the mountain pea or wild pea, is a genus of flowering plants in the legume family, Fabaceae.

These plants are native to southern Africa and are characterized by their small, pea-like flowers and trifoliate leaves. Phymosia species are typically shrubs or small trees that thrive in arid and semi-arid environments. They have a deep root system that allows them to survive in poor soil conditions and withstand drought.

The genus is valued for its ecological role in nitrogen fixation and as a source of forage for livestock.
