Platyspermum muricatum

Platyspermum muricatum, commonly known as the roughleaf mallow, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its rough, hairy leaves and showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full sunlight. Its fruit is a capsule that contains several small, hard seeds.

Platyspermum muricatum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has some traditional medicinal uses in native cultures.
