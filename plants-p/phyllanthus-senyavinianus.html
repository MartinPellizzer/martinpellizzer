Phyllanthus senyavinianus

Phyllanthus senyavinianus, commonly known as the Senyavinian goosefoot, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its small, alternate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by distinctive three-lobed capsules. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
