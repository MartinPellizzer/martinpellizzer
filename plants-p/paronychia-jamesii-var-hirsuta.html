Paronychia jamesii var. hirsuta

Paronychia jamesii var. hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to the western United States.

It features fleshy, thick leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive textured appearance. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow or orange during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it well-suited for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its compact growth habit and drought tolerance make it a popular choice for low-maintenance landscaping.
