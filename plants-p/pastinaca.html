Pastinaca

Pastinaca, commonly known as wild parsnip, is a biennial plant belonging to the Apiaceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, hollow stems and large, pinnately divided leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in umbels, which develop into round, smooth fruits. Its root is cylindrical and often yellow or white in color, with a distinct, spicy aroma.

Pastinaca is native to Europe and parts of Asia but has been introduced to North America.
