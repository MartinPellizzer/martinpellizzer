Polygonum vladimirii

Polygonum vladimirii, commonly known as Vladimir's knotweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, characterized by its distinctive nodes and hollow stems. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a waxy surface and produces small, clustered flowers in shades of pink to red. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in stream banks and forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
