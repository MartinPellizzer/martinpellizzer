Premna macrodonta

Premna macrodonta, commonly known as the large-leaf premna, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its opposite, ovate to elliptical leaves that are usually smooth and glossy. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, followed by oval, fleshy berries that are often red when mature. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
