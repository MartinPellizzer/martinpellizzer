Physa madagascariensis

Physa madagascariensis, commonly known as the Madagascar physa, is a species of freshwater snail native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the family Physidae and is characterized by its small, spiral-shaped shell. The snail's body is typically translucent, allowing the internal organs to be visible. It is often found in ponds, streams, and wetlands, where it feeds on algae and organic debris.

This species plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by contributing to nutrient cycling and serving as prey for larger organisms.
