Panicum briziforme

Panicum briziforme, commonly known as slender panicgrass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in open woodlands, meadows, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of 30 to 100 centimeters. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces fluffy, spike-like inflorescences that are often visible in late summer. Its seeds are dispersed by wind, and it plays a role in soil stabilization and as a food source for wildlife.

Panicum briziforme is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native plant gardens.
