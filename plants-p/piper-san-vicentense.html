Piper san-vicentense

Piper san-vicentense, commonly known as the San Vicente pepper, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its climbing habit, with opposite, ovate leaves that are often hairy. Its small, greenish-white flowers are followed by elongated, spiky fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential medicinal properties.
