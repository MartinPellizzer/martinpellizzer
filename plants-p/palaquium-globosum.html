Palaquium globosum

Palaquium globosum, commonly known as the globular wax apple, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and dense, rounded crown. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers followed by round, red or yellow fruits that are edible. Its leaves are opposite, elliptical, and have a leathery texture.

Palaquium globosum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
