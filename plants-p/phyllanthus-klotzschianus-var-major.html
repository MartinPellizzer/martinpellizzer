Phyllanthus klotzschianus var. major

Phyllanthus klotzschianus var. major, commonly known as the large-leaf phyllanthus, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring thick, fleshy leaves with a glossy surface. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in dense clusters, followed by small, round fruits that contain several seeds. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The species is characterized by its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its tolerance to drought conditions.
