Piper portachueloense

Piper portachueloense, commonly known as the Portachuelo pepper plant, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to the humid lowland forests of northern South America, particularly in Ecuador and Colombia. The plant is characterized by its climbing habit, with slender stems and heart-shaped leaves. Its small, greenish-white flowers are borne in clusters and are followed by small, round berries.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
