Paraphlomis javanica var. javanica

Paraphlomis javanica var. javanica, commonly known as Java parsnip, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Lamiaceae and is characterized by its aromatic, hairy leaves and slender, branched stems. The plant produces small, pale blue or purple flowers arranged in whorls along the stem. Its rhizomes are often harvested for their medicinal properties, used in traditional remedies for digestive and anti-inflammatory purposes.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
