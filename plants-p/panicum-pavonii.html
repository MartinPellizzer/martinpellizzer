Panicum pavonii

Panicum pavonii, commonly known as the purpletop grass, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its distinctive purple-tinted inflorescence. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats, such as marshes and pond edges, and thrives in moist, acidic soils. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, while its flowering heads consist of dense, spike-like clusters of small, purple flowers.

Panicum pavonii plays an important ecological role by providing food and habitat for various wildlife species.
