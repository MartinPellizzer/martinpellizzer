Pilosella caespitosa subsp. redenicensis

Pilosella caespitosa subsp.

redenicensis, commonly known as the Redenice hairgrass, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Europe. It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in early summer.

The plant has finely hairy stems and leaves, which contribute to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained, rocky or sandy soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats. Pilosella caespitosa subsp.

redenicensis is an important plant for pollinators and is valued for its ornamental appeal in native gardens.
