Passiflora mariquitensis

Passiflora mariquitensis, commonly known as the Mariquit passionflower, is a species native to the Philippines.

It is a climbing vine characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, violet-blue flowers. The plant produces edible berries that are often used in local cuisines. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial shade.

Its unique floral structure and ornamental value make it a notable species in botanical gardens and conservation efforts.
