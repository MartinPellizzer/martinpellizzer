Poa litwinowiana

Poa litwinowiana, commonly known as the Litwinowian grass, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and fine, blue-green foliage. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, alpine environments.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and natural habitats.
