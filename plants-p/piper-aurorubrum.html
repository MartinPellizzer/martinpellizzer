Piper aurorubrum

Piper aurorubrum, commonly known as the red pepper vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant red, peppery fruits that resemble small peppers, which are edible and have a spicy flavor. The plant has heart-shaped, glossy leaves and produces small, greenish-white flowers. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires support for its vine-like growth.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental and culinary use in suitable environments.
