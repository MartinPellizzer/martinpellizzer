Phalaenopsis mirabilis

Phalaenopsis mirabilis, commonly known as the miraculous moth orchid, is a tropical epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its long, slender pseudobulbs and linear, dark green leaves. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, moth-like flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires moderate humidity and well-draining potting media.

Phalaenopsis mirabilis is popular as a decorative plant due to its elegant appearance and ability to bloom repeatedly over several years.
