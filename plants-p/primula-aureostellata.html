Primula aureostellata

Primula aureostellata, commonly known as the golden star primrose, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of China.

It is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers with a white central star-like pattern, which gives it its common name. The plant typically grows to a height of about 10 to 20 centimeters, featuring lance-shaped leaves with a hairy texture. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in forested areas or rocky slopes.

The flowers bloom in early spring, adding a vibrant touch to its natural habitat.
