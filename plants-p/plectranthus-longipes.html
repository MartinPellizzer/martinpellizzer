Plectranthus longipes

Plectranthus longipes, commonly known as the long-stemmed plectranthus, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to purple. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

Plectranthus longipes is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
