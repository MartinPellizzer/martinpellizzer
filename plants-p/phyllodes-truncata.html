Phyllodes truncata

Phyllodes truncata, commonly known as the sickle wattle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its unique sickle-shaped phyllodes, which are modified leaves that serve as the primary photosynthetic structures. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid regions, adapting to harsh environmental conditions. Its flowers are yellow and appear in clusters, attracting various pollinators.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in native gardens.
