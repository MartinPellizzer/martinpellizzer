Pittosporum tobira f. macrophylla

Pittosporum tobira f. macrophylla, commonly known as the Large-leaf Pittosporum, is a shrub or small tree native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its dark green, leathery leaves that are typically larger than those of the typical species. The plant produces fragrant, white flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by yellowish-orange berries. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular ornamental plant.

Its botanical features make it suitable for landscaping in temperate climates.
