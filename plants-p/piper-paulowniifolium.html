Piper paulowniifolium

Piper paulowniifolium, commonly known as the Indian pepper plant, is a tropical shrub belonging to the Piperaceae family.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are often pinnately arranged and have a distinctive, glossy appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by dark blue berries that are edible. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in the understorey of rainforests.

Its ornamental value and medicinal properties make it a notable species in botanical gardens and traditional herbal practices.
