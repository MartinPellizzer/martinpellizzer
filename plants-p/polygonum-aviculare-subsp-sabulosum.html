Polygonum aviculare subsp. sabulosum

Polygonum aviculare subsp.

sabulosum, commonly known as sand knotgrass, is a low-growing perennial plant native to temperate regions. It typically forms dense mats on sandy or disturbed soils, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments.

The plant has slender, branching stems and small, arrow-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are clustered in spikes and produce tiny, wind-pollinated seeds.

It is often found in coastal dunes, roadsides, and other open habitats where it plays a role in stabilizing sandy soils.
