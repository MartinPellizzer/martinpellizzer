Pinus laricio var. moseri

Pinus laricio var. moseri, commonly known as the Corsican pine, is a variant of the stone pine native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, pyramidal shape and long, needle-like leaves that are typically bluish-green in color. The cones of this species are relatively small and woody, containing edible seeds known as pine nuts. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous areas with a temperate climate.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and its contribution to local ecosystems.
