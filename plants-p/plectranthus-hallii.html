Plectranthus hallii

Plectranthus hallii, commonly known as the African violet, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It features opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are often hairy and have a distinctive velvety texture. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers in shades of purple, pink, and white, which bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers bright, indirect light.

Plectranthus hallii is widely cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and compact growth habit.
