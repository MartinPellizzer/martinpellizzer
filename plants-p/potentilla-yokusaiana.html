Potentilla yokusaiana

Potentilla yokusaiana, commonly known as the Japanese cinquefoil, is a perennial shrub native to Japan.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its slender stems and bright yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and stream banks. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with five to seven leaflets, and the flowers are borne in clusters at the ends of the stems.

Potentilla yokusaiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
