Peperomia yanacachiana

Peperomia yanacachiana, commonly known as the Yanacachina peperomia, is a species of flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its compact, low-growing stature and thick, fleshy leaves. The plant typically features small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its foliage is often variegated, with shades of green, silver, and sometimes red, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Peperomia yanacachiana thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, requiring moderate watering to prevent root rot.
