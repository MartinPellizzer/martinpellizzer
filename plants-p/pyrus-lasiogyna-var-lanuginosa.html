Pyrus lasiogyna var. lanuginosa

Pyrus lasiogyna var. lanuginosa, commonly known as the woolly pear, is a variant of the pear tree characterized by its soft, hairy foliage and fruits.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, where it thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soils. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically green when mature, with a thin, edible skin. Its botanical features include compound leaves with serrated edges and a flowering period in early spring.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
