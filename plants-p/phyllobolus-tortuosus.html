Phyllobolus tortuosus

Phyllobolus tortuosus, commonly known as the spiral leaf bug, is a unique plant species characterized by its intricate, spiraling leaf structure.

Its leaves are arranged in a helical pattern, which is an adaptation that may aid in light absorption and water management. The plant exhibits a compact growth form, with foliage that often curls tightly around its stem. It is native to specific regions in South America, where it thrives in humid, shaded environments.

Due to its unusual morphology, Phyllobolus tortuosus is of interest to botanists studying plant form and function.
