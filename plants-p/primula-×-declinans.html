Primula × declinans

Primula × declinans, commonly known as the hybrid primrose, is a flowering plant belonging to the Primulaceae family.

It is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Primula vulgaris and Primula obconica, showcasing a blend of characteristics from both parents. The plant typically produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, which bloom in early spring. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value.

Its foliage consists of broad, dark green leaves that are hairy and slightly heart-shaped.
