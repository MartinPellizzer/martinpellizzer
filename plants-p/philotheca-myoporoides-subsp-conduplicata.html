Philotheca myoporoides subsp. conduplicata

Philotheca myoporoides subsp.

conduplicata, commonly known as the mountain myopor, is a shrub native to southeastern Australia. It features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces small, white to pale blue flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to sunny to partially shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
