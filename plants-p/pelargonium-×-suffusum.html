Pelargonium × suffusum

Pelargonium × suffusum, commonly known as the ivy-leaved geranium, is a hybrid species within the Geraniaceae family.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and small, rounded, ivy-shaped leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces clusters of small, five-petaled flowers in shades of pink, red, or white, depending on the cultivar. It is native to South Africa and is widely cultivated as an ornamental plant for its attractive foliage and long-lasting blooms.

Pelargonium × suffusum thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it a popular choice for hanging baskets and container gardening.
