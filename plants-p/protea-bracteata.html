Protea bracteata

Protea bracteata, commonly known as the silver bract protea, is a striking South African flowering plant belonging to the Proteaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescence composed of numerous small, tubular flowers surrounded by modified leaves called bracts. The plant typically grows in coastal and mountainous regions, thriving in well-drained, acidic soils. Its flowers range in color from white to pink and are often visited by birds and insects, making it an important pollinator resource.

The protea bracteata is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens across the country.
