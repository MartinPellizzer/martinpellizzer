Penstemon triphyllus var. triphyllus

Penstemon triphyllus var. triphyllus, commonly known as the Three-leaf beardtongue, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its striking purple to pink tubular flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and native plant landscapes.

Its showy flowers attract pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds, contributing to its ecological value.
