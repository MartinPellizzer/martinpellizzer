Polygala carphoides var. carphoides

Polygala carphoides var. carphoides, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its striking blue to purple flower clusters that resemble mist. The plant typically grows in moist, sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are narrow and opposite, and the flowers are borne in dense, cylindrical spikes.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in native plant gardens.
