Pachira tocantina

Pachira tocantina, commonly known as the Tocantins pachira, is a species of flowering plant in the Araceae family.

It is native to Brazil, specifically the state of Tocantins, and thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. The plant features large, heart-shaped leaves that are typically a deep green color, with a waxy texture. Its flowers are small and clustered in an inflorescence called a spadix, surrounded by a modified leaf called a spathe.

Pachira tocantina is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and relatively low maintenance requirements.
