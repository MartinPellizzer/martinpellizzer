Panicum meridionale var. albemarlense

Panicum meridionale var. albemarlense, commonly known as the white panicgrass, is a variant of the panic grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like foliage and produces fluffy, white seed heads that are visually striking. This plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as prairies, roadsides, and disturbed soils. Its botanical features include narrow, linear leaves and a tall, branching inflorescence.

It is often used in ornamental gardening for its aesthetic appeal and low maintenance requirements.
