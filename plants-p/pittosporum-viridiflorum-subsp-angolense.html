Pittosporum viridiflorum subsp. angolense

Pittosporum viridiflorum subsp.

angolense, commonly known as the Angolan green-flowered pittosporum, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its evergreen foliage, which is typically dark green and glossy.

The plant produces small, fragrant, greenish-white flowers that appear in clusters, often followed by small, round berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
