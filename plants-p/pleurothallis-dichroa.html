Pleurothallis dichroa

Pleurothallis dichroa, commonly known as the dichroous orchid, is a species native to the cloud forests of Central and South America.

It is a small epiphytic orchid, typically growing on trees in humid, shaded environments. The plant features delicate, lance-shaped leaves and produces intricate, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink. Its blooming period occurs during the rainy season, and it is prized for its ornamental value.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered vulnerable in some regions.
