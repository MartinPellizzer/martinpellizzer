Pleurothallis crassifolia

Pleurothallis crassifolia, commonly known as the thick-leaf orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its dense, fleshy leaves that store water, adapting it to its often humid and shaded habitat. The plant produces small, delicate flowers in shades of white and yellow, typically blooming during the wet season. Its roots are epiphytic, meaning it grows on other plants without being parasitic, often found in the canopy of rainforests.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological niche, it is a subject of interest for botanists and orchid enthusiasts alike.
