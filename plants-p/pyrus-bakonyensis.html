Pyrus bakonyensis

Pyrus bakonyensis, commonly known as the Bakony pear, is a deciduous tree native to the Carpathian Basin in Central Europe.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are oval to lanceolate in shape. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by edible pears that are typically small and have a sweet, aromatic flavor.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas, contributing to local biodiversity.
