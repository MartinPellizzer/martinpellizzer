Pinus laricio proles corsicana

Pinus laricio proles corsicana, commonly known as the Corsican pine, is a subspecies of the Swiss stone pine native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a coniferous evergreen tree characterized by its slender, upward-curving needles and distinctive, often resinous cones. The tree typically grows in mountainous areas, preferring well-drained soils and a temperate climate. Its foliage is dense and dark green, providing ecological value to its native habitat.

This subspecies is notable for its adaptability to harsh environments and its historical use in forestry and construction.
