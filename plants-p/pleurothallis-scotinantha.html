Pleurothallis scotinantha

Pleurothallis scotinantha, commonly known as the velvet orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its small, intricate flowers that often display a range of colors, including white, yellow, and green. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its pseudobulbs are short and thick, supporting its evergreen foliage, and it produces fragrant blooms during the warmer months.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in greenhouses or as a houseplant.
