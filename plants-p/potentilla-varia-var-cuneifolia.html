Potentilla varia var. cuneifolia

Potentilla varia var. cuneifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in open, rocky habitats and is often found at high elevations. It has a low-growing habit with multiple stems and is well adapted to harsh alpine conditions.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardiness and beauty.
