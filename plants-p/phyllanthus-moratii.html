Phyllanthus moratii

Phyllanthus moratii, commonly known as the Indian cheesenut or wild coffee, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its small, green, oval-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by capsule fruits containing minute seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and forest edges.

Due to its medicinal properties, it is also used in traditional herbal remedies.
