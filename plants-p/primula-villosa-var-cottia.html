Primula villosa var. cottia

Primula villosa var. cottia, commonly known as the woolly primrose, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves that are often covered in fine white or grayish pubescence. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped, showy flowers in shades of yellow, orange, or red, typically blooming in early spring. It prefers well-drained, moist soils and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is often cultivated in gardens and naturalized areas.
