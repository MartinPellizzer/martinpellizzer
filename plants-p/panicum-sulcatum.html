Panicum sulcatum

Panicum sulcatum, commonly known as the scarlet panicgrass, is a grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and elongated, narrow leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, two-lobed flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
