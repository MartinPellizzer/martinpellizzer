Potentilla argentea var. calabra

Potentilla argentea var. calabra, commonly known as silver potentilla or white potentilla, is a deciduous shrub native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It features small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has silvery-gray foliage that provides ornamental value throughout the growing season. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its compact growth habit and drought tolerance make it a popular choice for low-maintenance landscaping.
