Plectranthus glabratus

Plectranthus glabratus, commonly known as the silver bush, is a perennial shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, silver-gray foliage that gives it a striking appearance. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pale purple or white, arranged in clusters. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Plectranthus glabratus is also valued for its aromatic leaves, which are often used in traditional medicine.
