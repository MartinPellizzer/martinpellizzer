Panicum subtipaniculatum

Panicum subtipaniculatum, commonly known as the narrow-panicle grass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, linear leaves that are often hairy along the edges. The plant produces panicle-like inflorescences composed of small, spikelet-bearing branches, which are typically greenish or yellowish in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or grasslands.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
