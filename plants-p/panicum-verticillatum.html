Panicum verticillatum

Panicum verticillatum, commonly known as blue panic grass, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and blue-green foliage, which becomes more pronounced during the growing season. The plant produces dense, fluffy seed heads that are often used in ornamental landscaping. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for drought-tolerant gardens.

Its ornamental value is complemented by its ability to attract birds and other wildlife.
