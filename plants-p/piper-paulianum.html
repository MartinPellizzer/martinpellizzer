Piper paulianum

Piper paulianum, commonly known as the Paulian's pepper, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many species of pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its trailing growth habit and small, heart-shaped leaves. It produces small, greenish-white flowers that are typically borne in clusters.

The fruit is a small, three-lobed berry that turns red when mature.
