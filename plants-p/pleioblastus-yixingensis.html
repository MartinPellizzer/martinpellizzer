Pleioblastus yixingensis

Pleioblastus yixingensis, commonly known as the Xixing bamboo, is a species of grass native to China.

It is characterized by its slender, erect culms that are typically green or slightly bluish in color. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in panicles, and its leaves are linear with a smooth, waxy surface. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its elegant appearance.

This bamboo is also valued for its role in traditional Chinese horticulture and landscape design.
