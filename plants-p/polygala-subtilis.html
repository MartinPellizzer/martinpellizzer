Polygala subtilis

Polygala subtilis, commonly known as the slender milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia, particularly China and Korea.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, delicate flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and prefers a temperate climate. Its flowers are usually pale yellow or greenish-white, and it produces small, bean-like pods that contain seeds.

Polygala subtilis is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and forest edges.
