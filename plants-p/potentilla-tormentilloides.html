Potentilla tormentilloides

Potentilla tormentilloides, commonly known as the tormentilla rose.

It is a low-growing shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central and South America. The plant features small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in the spring. Its leaves are oval-shaped and covered in fine hairs, giving them a slightly fuzzy texture.

The plant is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in rocky or well-drained soils.
