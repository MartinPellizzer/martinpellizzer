Pterostylis nutans var. hispidula

Pterostylis nutans var. hispidula, commonly known as the hispid spider-orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and a single, erect flower spike that bears a single, pale green to yellowish orchid flower. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as rainforests and understoreys. Its flowers are often pollinated by thynny wasps, which are attracted to the scent and structure of the blooms.

This orchid is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is known for its unique and delicate floral morphology.
