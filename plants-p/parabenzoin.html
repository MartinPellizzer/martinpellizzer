Parabenzoin

Parabenzoin, commonly known as 4-hydroxy-3,5-dimethylbenzaldehyde, is a naturally occurring compound found in certain plants.

It belongs to the class of aromatic compounds and is characterized by its benzene ring with hydroxyl and methyl substituents. This compound is often used in perfumery due to its sweet, woody, and vanilla-like scent. Parabenzoin is also found in the essential oils of plants such as clove and tonka bean.

Its molecular structure contributes to its unique olfactory properties and chemical reactivity.
