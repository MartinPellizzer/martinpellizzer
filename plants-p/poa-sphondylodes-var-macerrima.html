Poa sphondylodes var. macerrima

Poa sphondylodes var. macerrima, commonly known as the rough meadowgrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and culms that are often hairy, giving it a distinctive appearance. The leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green coloration, and the inflorescence consists of spikelets arranged in a panicle. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grassy areas, open woodlands, and along riverbanks.

This variant is notable for its adaptability to various environmental conditions and its role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat.
