Poa cuspidata

Poa cuspidata, commonly known as the needlegrass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. Poa cuspidata is adapted to a variety of soil types and can thrive in both sunny and partially shaded environments.

It is often found in meadows, grasslands, and along the edges of forests.
