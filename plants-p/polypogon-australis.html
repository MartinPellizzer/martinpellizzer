Polypogon australis

Polypogon australis, commonly known as the Australian sand grass, is a perennial grass native to Australia.

It is characterized by its fine, wiry leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets. The plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid regions. It has a shallow root system that helps stabilize soil and prevent erosion.

Polypogon australis is adapted to withstand drought and high temperatures, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
