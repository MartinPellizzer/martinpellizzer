Phlox amoena subsp. amoena

Phlox amoena subsp.

amoena, commonly known as the common phlox, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States. It is characterized by its showy, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink and violet.

The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full sun. Its foliage is lance-shaped and often covered in a fine, fuzzy texture.

It is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
