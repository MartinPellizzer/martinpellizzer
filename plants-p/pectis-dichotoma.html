Pectis dichotoma

Pectis dichotoma, commonly known as the two-branched pectis, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features a branching stem with narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small yellow flowers arranged in clusters. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in open grasslands.

Pectis dichotoma is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
