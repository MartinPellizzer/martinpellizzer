Piper patzulinum

Piper patzulinum, commonly known as the Mexican pepper plant, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to Central America and is characterized by its climbing habit and small, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are followed by elongated, spiky fruits. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The plant thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in shaded, moist environments.
