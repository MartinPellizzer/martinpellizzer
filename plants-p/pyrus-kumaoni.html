Pyrus kumaoni

Pyrus kumaoni, commonly known as the Kumaon pear, is a species native to the Himalayan regions of India and Nepal.

It is a deciduous tree that typically grows in mountainous areas with cool climates. The plant produces small, ornamental flowers in the spring, followed by edible pears in the autumn. Its leaves are elliptical and have a glossy surface, while its bark is smooth and greyish.

The Kumaon pear is valued for both its ecological role and its fruit, which is appreciated in local cuisines.
