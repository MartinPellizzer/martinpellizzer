Pilosocereus pernambucoensis var. caesius

Pilosocereus pernambucoensis var. caesius, commonly known as the Blue Cactus, is a species of cactus native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its compact, globular shape and striking blue-gray spines. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological role, it is often cultivated in succulent gardens.
