Phytocrene anomala

Phytocrene anomala, commonly known as the silverleaf phytocrene, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its silver-green, velvety leaves that reflect sunlight, reducing water loss in hot climates. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters during the cooler months. Its root system is deep and extensive, allowing it to access groundwater in nutrient-poor soils.

Phytocrene anomala is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory properties.
