Plumeria dictyophylla

Plumeria dictyophylla, commonly known as the white frangipani, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, waxy flowers that range in color from white to yellow and pink. The plant has simple, opposite, and ovate leaves that are usually dark green. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The flowers are typically borne on long, slender peduncles and bloom during the dry season.
