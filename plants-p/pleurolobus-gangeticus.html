Pleurolobus gangeticus

Pleurolobus gangeticus, commonly known as the Indian milkweed, is a perennial herb native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and opposite, ovate leaves with hairy surfaces. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically visited by various pollinators. Its roots are known to have medicinal properties and are used in traditional Ayurvedic medicine.

The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and riverine areas.
