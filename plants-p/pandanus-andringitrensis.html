Pandanus andringitrensis

Pandanus andringitrensis, commonly known as the Madagascar pandanus, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the family Pandanaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, fan-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences at the top of the stem. Its leaves are often used in local crafts and traditional practices.

The species is adapted to thrive in coastal and wetland environments, showcasing its resilience to humid and saline conditions.
