Parallosa monanthos var. marmorata

Parallosa monanthos var. marmorata, commonly known as the marbled parallosa, is a strikingly ornamental plant native to the Andes region of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage that displays a unique marbled pattern of green and silver hues. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often cultivated in shaded garden settings.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a valuable species for botanical conservation efforts.
