Plectranthus brevicaulis

Plectranthus brevicaulis, commonly known as the dwarf purple verbena, is a compact, trailing plant native to South Africa.

It features small, tubular flowers that range in color from purple to blue, blooming throughout the growing season. The plant has hairy, aromatic leaves that are often covered in a fine white coating. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its ability to produce long-lasting blooms and its compact growth habit.
