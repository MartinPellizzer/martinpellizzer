Plantago lanceolata subsp. cyrenaica

Plantago lanceolata subsp.

cyrenaica, commonly known as the Cyrenaica plantain, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region and parts of North Africa. It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs.

The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in a spike, which are wind-pollinated. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in disturbed or open habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
