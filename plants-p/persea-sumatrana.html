Persea sumatrana

Persea sumatrana, commonly known as the Sumatran avocado, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its glossy, lance-shaped leaves. The tree produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow. Its fruit is a large, egg-shaped berry with a smooth, leathery skin.

The plant is valued for its edible fruit and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
