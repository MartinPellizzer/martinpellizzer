Persicaria maculosa

Persicaria maculosa, commonly known as spotted knotweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Asia.

It features erect, slender stems that are often reddish-purple in color and are covered with small, pinkish-purple spots. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom from late spring to early autumn. Its leaves are lance-shaped, serrated, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Persicaria maculosa is often found in moist environments such as stream banks and wet meadows.
