Piper regnellii var. pallescens

Piper regnellii var. pallescens, commonly known as the pale-leaved pepper plant, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves that turn pale green with age. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by elongated, three-lobed berries containing the aromatic seeds used in traditional medicine and cuisine. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and medicinal properties.

Due to its delicate nature, it requires careful attention to soil moisture and light conditions for optimal growth.
