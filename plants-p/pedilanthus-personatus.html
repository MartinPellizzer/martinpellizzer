Pedilanthus personatus

Pedilanthus personatus, commonly known as the golden trumpet tree, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its striking yellow-orange flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has a woody stem with smooth, green bark and produces small, greenish-yellow fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The leaves are simple, alternate, and have a slightly hairy texture.
