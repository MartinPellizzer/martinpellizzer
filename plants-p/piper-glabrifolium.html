Piper glabrifolium

Piper glabrifolium, commonly known as the smooth-leafed pepper plant, is a climbing vine native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper plants such as black pepper. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are smooth and lance-shaped. It produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by dark blue berries that are edible.

The leaves and fruits are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
