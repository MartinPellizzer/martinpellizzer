Prunus persica subsp. nucipersica

Prunus persica subsp.

nucipersica, commonly known as the flat peach, is a subspecies of the peach tree characterized by its flattened, elongated fruits. It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is native to Central Asia, particularly regions like China and the Caucasus.

The plant typically grows as a small tree or shrub, reaching heights of up to 5 meters, with lance-shaped leaves and fragrant white flowers. Its fruits are known for their unique shape and are often used in both culinary and ornamental applications.

The subspecies is valued for its adaptability to various climates and its contribution to the genetic diversity of peaches.
