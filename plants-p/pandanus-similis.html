Pandanus similis

Pandanus similis, commonly known as the narrow-leaf pandanus, is a tropical plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It is a member of the Pandanaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender leaves that can grow up to several meters in length. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically clustered in a spadix, followed by the development of fibrous, coconut-like fruits. Its leaves are often used in traditional crafts and as a source of fiber, while the plant itself thrives in coastal and wetland environments.

Pandanus similis is well adapted to saline and humid conditions, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
