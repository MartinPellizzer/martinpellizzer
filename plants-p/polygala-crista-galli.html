Polygala crista-galli

Polygala crista-galli, commonly known as the crown vetch, is a flowering plant native to Europe, North Africa, and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, sparsely branched stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. Its flowers are usually pink or white, with a unique, crested appearance that gives it its common name.

Polygala crista-galli is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and its ability to stabilize soil in erosion-prone areas.
