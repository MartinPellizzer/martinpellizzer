Pyrostria media

Pyrostria media, commonly known as the mountain bamboo, is a unique plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its slender, hollow stems that resemble bamboo. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in spikelets. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often covered in a fine layer of hairs.

Pyrostria media is adapted to grow in cool, moist environments and is often found in shaded, forested areas.
