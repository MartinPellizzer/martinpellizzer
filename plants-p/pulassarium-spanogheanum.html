Pulassarium spanogheanum

Pulassarium spanogheanum, commonly known as the mountain orchid, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stem and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or forested areas and thrives in cool, moist environments. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, clustered flowers in shades of white and yellow.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
