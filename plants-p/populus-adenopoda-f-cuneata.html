Populus adenopoda f. cuneata

Populus adenopoda f. cuneata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf poplar, is a variant of the poplar tree characterized by its distinctive narrow, lance-shaped leaves.

It is native to parts of Asia, particularly China, where it thrives in temperate climates. The plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to tolerate a variety of soil conditions. Its leaves are typically covered in a fine, fuzzy layer of hairs, which helps reduce water loss.

This species is often used in reforestation efforts due to its hardiness and ecological benefits.
