Primula × coquozii

Primula × coquozii, commonly known as the hybrid primrose, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of China.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces showy, clustered flowers in shades of pink, white, and purple. The plant typically blooms in early spring, with foliage consisting of broad, ovate leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value.

Due to its hybrid origin, it exhibits a range of morphological variations that make it a popular choice among horticulturists.
