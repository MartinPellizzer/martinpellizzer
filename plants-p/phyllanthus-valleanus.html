Phyllanthus valleanus

Phyllanthus valleanus, commonly known as the Indian cheesewood, is a shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It features small, clustered leaves that are typically elliptical or ovate in shape. The plant produces tiny, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by small, round fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas or along roadsides.

The plant has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
