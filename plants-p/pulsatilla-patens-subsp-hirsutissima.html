Pulsatilla patens subsp. hirsutissima

Pulsatilla patens subsp.

hirsutissima, commonly known as the hirsute pasqueflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its showy, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from pale yellow to pink, typically appearing in early spring.

The plant has a short, thick rhizome and produces a basal rosette of hairy, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Its unique floral structure and hairy foliage make it a distinctive and ornamental species in its native environment.
