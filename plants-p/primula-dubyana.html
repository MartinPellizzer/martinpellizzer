Primula dubyana

Primula dubyana, commonly known as the Dubyana primrose, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features upright, hairy stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. The leaves are basal, ovate, and covered in a fine layer of white pubescence. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its early spring blooms.
