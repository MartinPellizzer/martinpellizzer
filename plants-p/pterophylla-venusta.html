Pterophylla venusta

Pterophylla venusta, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture. The plant produces showy, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, attracting various pollinators. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

Pterophylla venusta is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
