Puccinellia alaskana

Puccinellia alaskana, commonly known as Alaska glassworts, is a perennial grass-like plant native to the Arctic and subarctic regions of North America.

It thrives in saline and alkaline soils, often found in coastal tundra and salt marshes. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers in early summer. It plays a vital role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.

Due to its hardy nature, it is well adapted to cold climates and is an important component of Arctic ecosystems.
