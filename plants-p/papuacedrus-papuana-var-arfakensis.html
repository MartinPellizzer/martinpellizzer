Papuacedrus papuana var. arfakensis

Papuacedrus papuana var. arfakensis, commonly known as the Arfakensis cedar, is a rare and endemic species native to the mountainous regions of New Guinea.

It is a coniferous evergreen tree characterized by its aromatic, needle-like foliage and distinct cone structures. The plant exhibits a slow growth rate, which contributes to its limited distribution and conservation status. Its botanical features include a rugged bark and a preference for well-drained, acidic soils in high-altitude environments.
