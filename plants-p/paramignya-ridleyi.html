Paramignya ridleyi

Paramignya ridleyi, commonly known as the Malabar nut, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Western Ghats of India.

It belongs to the family Anacardiaceae and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by edible, red, fleshy drupes. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in dense forests.

Due to its ornamental value and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
