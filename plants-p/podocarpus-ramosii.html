Podocarpus ramosii

Podocarpus ramosii, commonly known as the Hawaiian Christmas tree, is a small evergreen tree native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It belongs to the Podocarpaceae family and is characterized by its dense, conical shape and dark green, leathery foliage. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by woody, cone-like structures that contain its seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and lowland habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and landscapes across tropical regions.
