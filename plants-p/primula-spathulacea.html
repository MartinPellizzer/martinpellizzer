Primula spathulacea

Primula spathulacea, commonly known as the broad-leaf primrose, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features a rosette of broad, ovate leaves that are typically covered in a dense layer of fine, white hairs. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, or purple, which bloom in early spring. It thrives in cool, moist environments and prefers well-drained, loamy soil.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is cultivated in gardens and botanical collections worldwide.
