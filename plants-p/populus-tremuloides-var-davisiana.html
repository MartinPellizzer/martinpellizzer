Populus tremuloides var. davisiana

Populus tremuloides var. davisiana, commonly known as the Davis trembling aspen, is a variant of the trembling aspen native to North America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, finely toothed leaves that tremble in the wind, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in temperate climates. It is a deciduous tree or shrub that can reach heights of up to 20 meters.

This variety is known for its ecological importance, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
