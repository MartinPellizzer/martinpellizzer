Pinguicula hellwegeri

Pinguicula hellwegeri, commonly known as the Mexican butterwort, is a carnivorous plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its glandular leaves that secrete a sticky substance to trap small insects. The plant typically grows in moist, sunny environments such as rocky outcrops and stream banks. Its flowers are usually pale yellow or greenish, with a single pistil and stamens.

Pinguicula hellwegeri is a low-growing perennial that thrives in nutrient-poor soils, relying on insect prey for essential nutrients.
