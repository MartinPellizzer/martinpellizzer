Piper cordatum

Piper cordatum, commonly known as the heartleaf pepper or heartleaf piper, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves, which are typically glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into red, peppery berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in the understory of tropical forests.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
