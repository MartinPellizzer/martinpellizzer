Polyactidium seemannii

Polyactidium seemannii, commonly known as the Cape daisy, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in coastal and sandy soils, thriving in full sun conditions. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often covered in fine hairs.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens for its bright, long-lasting blooms.
