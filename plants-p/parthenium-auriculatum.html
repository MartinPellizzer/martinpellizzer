Parthenium auriculatum

Parthenium auriculatum, commonly known as the golden crownbeard, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is an annual herb that typically grows in open grasslands and disturbed soils. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in dense, spike-like clusters. Its seeds are small and wind-dispersed, contributing to its ability to spread across various habitats.

The plant is often noted for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
