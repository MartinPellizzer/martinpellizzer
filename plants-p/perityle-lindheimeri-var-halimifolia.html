Perityle lindheimeri var. halimifolia

Perityle lindheimeri var. halimifolia, commonly known as the sweet everlasting or halimifolia everlasting, is a perennial shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It features small, showy yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has aromatic, glandular leaves that emit a sweet, honey-like scent when crushed. It is well adapted to arid and rocky environments, thriving in full sun and well-drained soil.

Its compact growth habit and long-lasting blooms make it a popular choice for xeriscaping and ornamental gardening.
