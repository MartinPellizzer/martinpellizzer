Passiflora podadenia

Passiflora podadenia, commonly known as the rock passionflower, is a climbing vine native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Passifloraceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, showy flowers with five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments. The plant produces blue or purple berries that are edible and have a sweet, tart flavor. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a waxy coating, while its stems are hairy and sturdy.

Passiflora podadenia thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.
