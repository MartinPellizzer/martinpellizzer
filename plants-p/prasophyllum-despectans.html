Prasophyllum despectans

Prasophyllum despectans, commonly known as the weeping leek, is a terrestrial orchid native to the temperate regions of southeastern Australia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and rocky crevices. The plant features a slender, leafy stem with small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in late spring. Its leaves are strap-like and often curl downward, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Prasophyllum despectans is adapted to cool, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
