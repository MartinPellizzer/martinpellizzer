Paeonia mascula

Paeonia mascula, commonly known as the tree peony, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and large, showy flowers that bloom in spring. The plant features palmately compound leaves and produces woody stems that can reach heights of up to 3 meters. Its flowers come in a variety of colors, including pink, red, and white, and are often used in ornamental gardening.

Paeonia mascula is valued for its aesthetic appeal and is also used in traditional Chinese medicine.
