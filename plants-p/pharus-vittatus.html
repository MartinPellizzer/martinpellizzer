Pharus vittatus

Pharus vittatus, commonly known as the spotted orchid, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive spotted leaves and delicate, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests or wetlands. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb nutrients from the soil efficiently.

Pharus vittatus is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in supporting local pollinator populations.
