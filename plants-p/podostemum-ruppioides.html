Podostemum ruppioides

Podostemum ruppioides, commonly known as the rockpod stem, is a unique aquatic plant found in fast-flowing streams and rivers.

It is characterized by its small, elongated leaves that are often submerged and adapted to the turbulent water environment. The plant has a specialized root system that anchors it to rocky substrates, allowing it to thrive in high-energy aquatic habitats. Its flowers are typically small and clustered, with bright colors that attract pollinators such as bees and flies.

Podostemum ruppioides plays an important ecological role in stabilizing stream banks and providing habitat for aquatic organisms.
