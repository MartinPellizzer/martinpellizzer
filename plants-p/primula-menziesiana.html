Primula menziesiana

Primula menziesiana, commonly known as the Menzies' primrose, is a flowering plant native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It has lance-shaped leaves that are usually hairy and arranged in a basal rosette.

Primula menziesiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in local ecosystems by supporting pollinators.
