Phaseolus hysterinus

Phaseolus hysterinus, commonly known as the wild kidney bean, is a species native to Central and South America.

It is a climbing vine that produces small, yellow flowers and elongated, bean-like pods. The plant is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its seeds, while edible, are typically smaller and less commonly cultivated compared to other Phaseolus species.

The plant has ecological significance as a food source for various wildlife species.
