Paspalum cordovense

Paspalum cordovense, commonly known as the Cordoba grass, is a perennial grass native to South America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, linear leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers. It is adapted to a variety of soil types but thrives in well-drained conditions.

Paspalum cordovense is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its aesthetic appeal and low maintenance requirements.
