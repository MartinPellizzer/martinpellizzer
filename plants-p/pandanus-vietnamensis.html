Pandanus vietnamensis

Pandanus vietnamensis, commonly known as the Vietnamese screw-palm, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and long, narrow leaves that are often spirally arranged. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically borne in dense, branched clusters. Its fruit is a fibrous, spiky structure that contains edible seeds.

Pandanus vietnamensis is often used in traditional crafts and has cultural significance in local communities.
