Polygonatum officinale var. maximowiczii

Polygonatum officinale var. maximowiczii, commonly known as the Chinese Solomon's seal, is a perennial herb native to eastern Asia.

It features rhizomatous stems that grow horizontally underground, supporting its upright, leafy shoots. The plant produces clusters of small, white, bell-shaped flowers in early summer, followed by dark blue berries. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem.

It is valued in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.
