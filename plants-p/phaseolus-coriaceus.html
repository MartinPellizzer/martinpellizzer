Phaseolus coriaceus

Phaseolus coriaceus, commonly known as the scarlet runner bean, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its robust, hairy stems and large, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces vibrant red or orange flowers that give way to elongated, bean-like pods. Its seeds are often used in culinary applications, while the plant itself is valued for its ornamental appeal.

Botanically, it belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is known for its nitrogen-fixing abilities and legume-like fruits.
