Psorospermum chionanthifolium var. depauperatum

Psorospermum chionanthifolium var. depauperatum, commonly known as the sparse-leaved psorospermum, is a shrub native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, often hairy stems and small, elliptical leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the branches. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, which are usually borne at the ends of the branches. Its flowers are fragrant and attract various pollinators, including bees and butterflies.

The species is noted for its adaptability to moist, shaded environments and is occasionally cultivated for ornamental purposes.
