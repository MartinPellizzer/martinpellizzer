Phlox amoena var. walteri

Phlox amoena var. walteri, commonly known as Walter's phlox, is a North American native perennial herb belonging to the Polemoniaceae family.

It is characterized by its showy, fragrant flowers that bloom in early summer, typically in shades of pink to red. The plant features upright, branching stems covered with opposite, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often found in prairies and open woodlands.

Walter's phlox is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
