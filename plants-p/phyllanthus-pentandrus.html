Phyllanthus pentandrus

Phyllanthus pentandrus, commonly known as the African nightshade, is a small shrub native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, and its fruits are three-angled capsules containing tiny seeds. It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

The plant has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
