Parodia obtusa subsp. atochana

Parodia obtusa subsp.

atochana, commonly known as the "Tussock Cactus," is a species of cactus native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems covered in dense, white to grayish spines.

The plant produces yellow flowers during the warmer months, typically in spring or early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Its compact size and unique appearance make it a popular choice for rock gardens and succulent collections.
