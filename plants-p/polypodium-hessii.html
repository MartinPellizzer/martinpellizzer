Polypodium hessii

Polypodium hessii, commonly known as the hessian maidenhair fern, is a species of epiphytic fern native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its delicate, bi-lobed fronds that are often adorned with fine, silky hairs. The plant thrives in shaded, humid environments, typically growing on trees or rocks in forested areas. Its rhizomes are creeping and produce clusters of sporangia, which are responsible for its reproductive cycle.

The fronds are deciduous, shedding in the fall and regrowing in the spring, contributing to its seasonal appearance.
