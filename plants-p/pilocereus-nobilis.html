Pilocereus nobilis

Pilocereus nobilis, commonly known as the moon cactus, is a cacti species native to South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, colorful, and often vibrant flowers that bloom at night. The plant has a short, thick stem with areoles that bear spines and flowers. Its leaves are modified into spines, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.
