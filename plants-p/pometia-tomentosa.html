Pometia tomentosa

Pometia tomentosa, commonly known as the woolly pometia, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, grayish bark and elliptical, dark green leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by edible, round fruits that are often consumed by local communities. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
