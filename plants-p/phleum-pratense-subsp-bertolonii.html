Phleum pratense subsp. bertolonii

Phleum pratense subsp.

bertolonii, commonly known as the Italian timothy grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Europe. It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and grasslands.

The plant has slender, upright stems with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. Its inflorescence consists of a spike-like cluster of small, two-lobed flowers.

This subspecies is characterized by its distinct floral structure and is valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators.
