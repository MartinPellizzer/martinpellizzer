Piper dindingsianum

Piper dindingsianum, commonly known as the Dindingsian pepper, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its climbing habit and compound leaves with serrated edges. Its small, greenish-white flowers are followed by elongated, fleshy fruits that contain the aromatic seeds used in traditional medicine.

The plant's botanical features make it an important species for both ecological and pharmacological studies.
