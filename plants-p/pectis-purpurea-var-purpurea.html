Pectis purpurea var. purpurea

Pectis purpurea var. purpurea, commonly known as the purple pectis, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking purple to pink flower heads. The plant typically grows in open, rocky areas and thrives in full sun. Its foliage consists of narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The flowers are arranged in compact, spherical clusters and bloom in late summer to early autumn.
