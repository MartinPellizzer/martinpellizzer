Pourouma lawrancei

Pourouma lawrancei, commonly known as the yellow rafia palm, is a species of palm native to South America, particularly found in the Amazon Basin.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate leaves that can reach up to 5 meters in length. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in dense inflorescences, which are followed by clustered, orange-red fruits. Its fibrous leaves are used by local communities for thatching and weaving, highlighting its ecological and cultural significance.

The plant thrives in humid, tropical environments and plays a role in the biodiversity of its native habitat.
