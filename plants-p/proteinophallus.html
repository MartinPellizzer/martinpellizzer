Proteinophallus

Proteinophallus, commonly known as the "meat egg fungus," is a genus of fungi that belongs to the family Tricholomataceae.

These fungi are characterized by their unique, often gelatinous or meaty fruiting bodies, which can resemble small, translucent eggs or even pieces of meat. They are typically found in forested areas, growing on decaying wood or soil. The spore-producing structures, called basidia, are embedded within the fleshy caps, contributing to their distinctive appearance.

Due to their unusual morphology and texture, they are often mistaken for other fungi or even non-plant materials.
