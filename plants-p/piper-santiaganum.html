Piper santiaganum

Piper santiaganum, commonly known as the Santiagana pepper, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species such as black pepper and long pepper. The plant is characterized by its vine-like growth habit and small, heart-shaped leaves. It produces small green berries that turn red when mature, containing the aromatic seeds used in traditional medicine.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
