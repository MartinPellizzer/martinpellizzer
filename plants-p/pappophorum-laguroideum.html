Pappophorum laguroideum

Pappophorum laguroideum, commonly known as the desert dandelion, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows to a height of 10 to 30 centimeters, featuring hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by fluffy, wind-dispersed seeds. It is well adapted to harsh environments, with deep roots that allow it to survive in dry conditions.

Pappophorum laguroideum is often found in disturbed soils and is considered a pioneer species in ecological succession.
