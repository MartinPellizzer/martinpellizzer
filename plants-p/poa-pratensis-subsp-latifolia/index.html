Poa Pratensis Subsp. Latifolia

Poa pratensis subsp. latifolia is a lesser-known variant of the common bentgrass species. It is characterized by its broader leaves and slightly different growth habits compared to its more widespread relatives. This subspecies is often found in moist, temperate regions where it thrives in well-drained soils. Its presence in natural grasslands and meadows highlights its ecological importance. Despite its modest appearance, it plays a significant role in supporting local biodiversity.
