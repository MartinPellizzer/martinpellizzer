Pouteria rufa

Pouteria rufa, commonly known as the red mangabey apple, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is a small to medium-sized tree that typically grows in lowland rainforests. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are often clustered in inflorescences. Its fruit is a smooth, round berry that turns red when ripe, containing a single large seed.

The leaves are simple, alternate, and have a rough texture with prominent veins.
