Phyllimena paludosa

Phyllimena paludosa, commonly known as the marsh phyllimena, is a herbaceous plant native to wetland environments in northern regions.

It typically grows in shallow, nutrient-rich waters and is often found in marshes and along the edges of lakes. The plant has slender, erect stems that bear small, clustered leaves, which are usually oval and slightly pointed. Its flowers are small and greenish-white, arranged in loose spikes, and it blooms during the early summer months.

Phyllimena paludosa plays a role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for aquatic invertebrates.
