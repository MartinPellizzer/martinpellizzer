Prunus hortulana var. mineri

Prunus hortulana var. mineri, commonly known as the dwarf cherry plum, is a compact, deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, oval leaves that are typically dark green and have a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces clusters of white or pale pink flowers in early spring, followed by small, sweet-tart red or purple fruits. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens for its hardiness and adaptability.

This variety is relatively low maintenance and thrives in well-drained soil with full sun to partial shade.
