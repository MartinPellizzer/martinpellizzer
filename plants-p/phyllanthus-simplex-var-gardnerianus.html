Phyllanthus simplex var. gardnerianus

Phyllanthus simplex var. gardnerianus, commonly known as the small-leaf phyllanthus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are typically arranged in a spiral pattern along the stems. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, often borne on short pedicels. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its compact growth habit.
