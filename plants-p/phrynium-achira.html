Phrynium achira

Phrynium achira, commonly known as the arrow leaf plant, is a tropical perennial belonging to the Araceae family.

It is characterized by its large, arrow-shaped leaves that can grow up to 1 meter in length, often with a waxy surface. The plant produces striking spadices with yellow to orange flowers, which are followed by small, fleshy berries. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is native to South America, particularly in regions of Brazil, Colombia, and Ecuador.

Phrynium achira is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in some indigenous cultures.
