Phrynium monophyllum

Phrynium monophyllum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf arrowroot, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers that develop into capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

Phrynium monophyllum is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
