Passerina incana

Passerina incana, commonly known as the gray pasqueflower, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant features a hairy, upright stem with palmately divided leaves that are often grayish-green in color. Its flowers are typically pale yellow to white, with numerous stamens surrounding a central pistil.

Passerina incana thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky habitats.
