Pleurothallis schweinfurthiana

Pleurothallis schweinfurthiana, commonly known as the Schweinfurth's orchid, is a species native to the cloud forests of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact, epiphytic growth habit. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and yellow, often arranged in clusters. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and arranged in a rosette pattern, aiding in moisture retention.

Due to its delicate appearance and specific habitat requirements, it is a sought-after species among orchid enthusiasts.
