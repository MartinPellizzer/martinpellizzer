Polyotus lanuginosus

Polyotus lanuginosus, commonly known as the woolly polyotus, is a flowering plant native to the wetlands of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy leaves and small, clustered white or pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in shallow water or damp soil, thriving in tropical and subtropical climates. Its botanical features include a slender stem and alternate leaves that are often covered in fine, lanugo-like hairs.

It is valued in traditional medicine for its purported medicinal properties.
