Phyllanthus favieri

Phyllanthus favieri, commonly known as the African date palm, is a shrub native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its dense, clustered growth habit. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by small, fleshy fruits. Its leaves are thick and leathery, often with a waxy coating, and it is adapted to arid environments.

Phyllanthus favieri is also noted for its medicinal properties in traditional African medicine.
