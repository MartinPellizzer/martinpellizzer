Phenax mexicanus var. lanceolatus

Phenax mexicanus var. lanceolatus, commonly known as the Mexican phenax, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in full sun. It is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and scrublands.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
