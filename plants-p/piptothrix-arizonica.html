Piptothrix arizonica

Piptothrix arizonica, commonly known as Arizona feathergrass, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a perennial grass that typically grows in rocky soils and arid environments. The plant produces slender, grass-like leaves and small, spike-like inflorescences that bloom in late spring. Its flowers are usually pale blue or purple and are adapted for wind pollination.

Piptothrix arizonica is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
