Penstemon oreocharis

Penstemon oreocharis, commonly known as the Sierra penstemon, is a perennial plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to purple. The plant typically thrives in rocky soils and dry, sunny environments, making it well adapted to arid conditions. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces numerous seed capsules that disperse its seeds effectively.

Penstemon oreocharis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
