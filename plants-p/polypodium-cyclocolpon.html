Polypodium cyclocolpon

Polypodium cyclocolpon, commonly known as the cyclocolpoid fern, is a species of epiphytic fern native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its delicate, feather-like fronds that are typically less than 30 centimeters in length. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, often growing on trees or rocks in tropical rainforests. Its spore-producing structures, called sorangia, are clustered on the underside of its leaves, aiding in its reproductive cycle.

The plant's unique morphology and ecological niche make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
