Pardoglossum tubiflorum

Pardoglossum tubiflorum, commonly known as the tuberous bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America.

It features showy, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple, often attracting pollinators like bees and hummingbirds. The plant has a fibrous root system and produces tuberous roots that store nutrients, aiding in its survival during dry periods. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and arranged alternately along the stem.

Pardoglossum tubiflorum thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodlands.
