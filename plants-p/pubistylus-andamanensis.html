Pubistylus andamanensis

Pubistylus andamanensis, commonly known as the Andaman mountain orchid, is a rare and endemic species found in the Andaman Islands.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique floral morphology. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded forest environments, thriving in the humid tropical climate. Its flowers are small, with intricate structures that aid in pollination by specific insect species.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered vulnerable, highlighting the need for conservation efforts.
