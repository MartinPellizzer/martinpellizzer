Panicum speciosum var. laxum

Panicum speciosum var. laxum, commonly known as the loose panicgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, arching stems and loose, feathery panicles that give it a delicate appearance. The plant produces small, spikelet-based flowers that are typically pale green to golden in color. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, often found in open woodlands and disturbed areas.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for native plant gardens and ecological restoration projects.
