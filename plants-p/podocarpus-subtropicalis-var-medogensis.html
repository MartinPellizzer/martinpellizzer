Podocarpus subtropicalis var. medogensis

Podocarpus subtropicalis var. medogensis, commonly known as the Medogensis Podocarpus, is a rare evergreen conifer native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Podocarpaceae family and is characterized by its dense, needle-like foliage and compact growth habit. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, often found in mountainous areas with well-drained soil. Its cones are small and woody, containing seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a valuable species for botanical research and conservation efforts.
