Plutarchia rimbachii

Plutarchia rimbachii, commonly known as the Rimbach's ghost orchid, is a rare and enigmatic orchid species native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, translucent petals and a unique, almost ethereal appearance that mimics the appearance of a ghostly figure. The plant thrives in shaded, humid environments and relies on specific mycorrhizal fungi for nutrient absorption, making it highly sensitive to environmental changes. Its flowers, which bloom briefly during the monsoon season, are pollinated by specialized insects, contributing to its ecological significance.

Due to habitat loss and over-collection, Plutarchia rimbachii is considered critically endangered, highlighting the need for conservation efforts.
