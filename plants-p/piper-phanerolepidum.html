Piper phanerolepidum

Piper phanerolepidum, commonly known as the broad-leaved pepper plant, is a member of the Piperaceae family, native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, glossy, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. The plant produces small, berry-like fruits that are typically green when mature, containing aromatic seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in the understory of rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is cultivated in some horticultural settings.
