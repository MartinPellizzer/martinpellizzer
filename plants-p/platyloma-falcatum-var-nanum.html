Platyloma falcatum var. nanum

Platyloma falcatum var. nanum, commonly known as the dwarf flatleaf grass, is a low-growing, perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a textured appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically green or pale yellow. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grassy areas or along rocky outcrops.

Due to its compact size and ornamental appeal, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic value.
