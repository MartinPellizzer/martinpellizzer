Peponidium cuspidatum

Peponidium cuspidatum, commonly known as the spiny cucumber, is a member of the Cucurbitaceae family.

It is a trailing vine that produces yellow flowers and elongated, spiny fruits. The plant is native to tropical regions of the Americas and thrives in warm, humid climates. Its leaves are heart-shaped with serrated edges, and the fruits are often used in traditional medicine.

The plant's botanical features make it distinct within the cucurbit family.
