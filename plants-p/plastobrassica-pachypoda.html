Plastobrassica pachypoda

Plastobrassica pachypoda, commonly known as the thick-leafed brassica, is a unique plant species characterized by its thick, succulent leaves that store water, adapting it to arid environments.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many familiar vegetables such as cabbage and broccoli. The plant produces small, white or yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which attract pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory properties.

The species is native to regions with dry climates, where it thrives in well-drained soils.
