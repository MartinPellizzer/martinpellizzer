Prunus parksii

Prunus parksii, commonly known as the Mexican cherry or Mexican wild cherry, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to Mexico and is characterized by its small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, edible cherries that are similar in taste to sweet cherries but are typically smaller and more tart. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forested areas.

Prunus parksii is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential in horticulture and conservation efforts.
