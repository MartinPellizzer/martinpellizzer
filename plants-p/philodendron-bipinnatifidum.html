Philodendron bipinnatifidum

Philodendron bipinnatifidum, commonly known as the heartleaf philodendron, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, waxy leaves that are typically a vibrant green with a glossy texture. The plant has a climbing habit and can grow quite large, reaching heights of several meters in its natural habitat. Its leaves are often deeply lobed, giving it a distinctive and ornamental appearance.

It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.
