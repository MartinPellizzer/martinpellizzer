Prunella rosea

Prunella rosea, commonly known as selfheal, is a flowering plant belonging to the mint family, Lamiaceae.

It is characterized by its hairy, square stems and opposite leaves that are typically oval to lance-shaped. The plant produces small, pale purple to blue flowers in whorled clusters, which bloom from late spring to early autumn. It thrives in a variety of habitats, including meadows, woodlands, and disturbed soils, and is often found in moist environments.

Prunella rosea is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally for its anti-inflammatory and wound-healing benefits.
