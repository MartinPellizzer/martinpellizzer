Polygala flabellata

Polygala flabellata, commonly known as the fan-flowered milkweed, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia, including China, Japan, and Korea.

It is characterized by its delicate, feathery leaves and showy, white or pale yellow flowers that resemble small fans. The plant typically grows in woodland areas and moist soils, often forming dense colonies. It belongs to the family Polygalaceae, which includes over 700 species of flowering plants.

Polygala flabellata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
