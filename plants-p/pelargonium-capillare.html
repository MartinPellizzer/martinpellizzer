Pelargonium capillare

Pelargonium capillare, commonly known as the Cape geranium, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 30 centimeters. The plant features hairy stems and leaves with a distinctive velvety texture. Its flowers are small, clustered in dense inflorescences, and come in shades of pink and red.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.
