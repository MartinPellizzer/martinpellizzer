Papaver gorovanicum

Papaver gorovanicum, commonly known as the Gorovani poppy, is a species of flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Papaveraceae and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that range in color from red to orange. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its capsules contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind, aiding in its propagation.

The species is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and ecological significance in its native habitat.
