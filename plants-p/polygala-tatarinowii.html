Polygala tatarinowii

Polygala tatarinowii, commonly known as the Tatarian milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid conditions. Its leaves are usually narrow and opposite, and it produces small, yellowish or greenish flowers in late spring.

The seeds are enclosed in follicles and are dispersed by wind, contributing to its ecological role in stabilizing soil in its native environment.
