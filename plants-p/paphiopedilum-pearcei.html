Paphiopedilum pearcei

Paphiopedilum pearcei, commonly known as the Pearl Orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that typically feature a unique pouch-shaped lip, a defining feature of the Paphiopedilum genus. The plant produces long, arching inflorescences with multiple blooms, often in shades of white, pink, and yellow. It thrives in shaded, humid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
