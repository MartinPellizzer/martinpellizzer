Piper semivolubile

Piper semivolubile, commonly known as the climbing pepper vine, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species. The plant is characterized by its climbing habit, with long, flexible stems that twine around supports. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a glossy surface.

The small, greenish-white flowers are followed by berries that turn red when mature.
