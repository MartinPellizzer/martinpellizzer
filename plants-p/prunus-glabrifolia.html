Prunus glabrifolia

Prunus glabrifolia, commonly known as the California wild cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the western United States.

It features smooth, gray-barked stems and lance-shaped leaves that turn yellow in the fall. The plant produces small, white to pink flowers in early spring, followed by edible berries that are often used in jams and jellies. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to Mediterranean climates.

Prunus glabrifolia plays an important role in local ecosystems by providing food and habitat for various wildlife species.
