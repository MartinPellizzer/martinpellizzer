Phyllanthus schomburgkianus var. acuminatus

Phyllanthus schomburgkianus var. acuminatus, commonly known as the acuminated phyllanthus, is a species native to tropical regions of South America.

It is a shrub characterized by its small, elliptical leaves with acuminate tips. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically followed by spherical, red or orange fruits. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and traditional herbal practices.
