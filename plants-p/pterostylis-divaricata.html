Pterostylis divaricata

Pterostylis divaricata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf greenhood, is an orchid species native to eastern Australia.

It is a terrestrial herb with a slender, often branching stem and elongated, grass-like leaves. The plant produces a single, greenish-brown flower with a distinctive hood-like structure, which is typical of the Orchidaceae family. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and rock crevices.

The species is noted for its delicate appearance and is of interest to botanists and horticulturists alike.
