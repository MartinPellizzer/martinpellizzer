Phalaris utriculata

Phalaris utriculata, commonly known as the bladder grass, is a perennial grass species native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant has distinctive seed pods that are inflated and contain the seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, riverbanks, and wetlands.

Phalaris utriculata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting insect populations.
