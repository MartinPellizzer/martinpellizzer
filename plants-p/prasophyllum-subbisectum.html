Prasophyllum subbisectum

Prasophyllum subbisectum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and a single, slender flowering stem that produces a cluster of small, greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or loamy soils in open woodlands or heathlands. It is adapted to dry conditions and is often found in areas with low rainfall.

Prasophyllum subbisectum is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is known for its delicate appearance and ecological significance in its native habitat.
