Phlox speciosa subsp. lignosa

Phlox speciosa subsp.

lignosa, commonly known as the showy phlox, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of showy, fragrant flowers that range from pink to white.

The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are sturdy and often woody at the base.

This subspecies is well-suited for gardens and natural habitats, thriving in partial to full sun and well-drained soil.
