Pyxidaria corymbosa

Pyxidaria corymbosa, commonly known as the clustered bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its clusters of bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits.

Pyxidaria corymbosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
