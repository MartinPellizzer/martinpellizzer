Prunus × lannesiana f. gijozakura

Prunus × lannesiana f. gijozakura, commonly known as the Japanese apricot or Chinese date, is a unique hybrid species within the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its ornamental value, featuring showy, pink or white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, edible fruits that are often used in traditional cuisine and medicine. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a smooth texture, while its bark is smooth and grayish in color.

This cultivar is prized for its aesthetic appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens and parks for its seasonal beauty.
