Potentilla pectinisecta var. comosa

Potentilla pectinisecta var. comosa, commonly known as the woolly cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its finely divided, silvery-gray leaves that are covered in dense, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and natural habitats.
