Polygonum strictum var. interruptum

Polygonum strictum var. interruptum, commonly known as the interrupted knotweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It typically grows in moist environments such as wetlands, stream banks, and marshy areas. The plant features upright, slender stems that are often reddish in color and bear alternate, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface. Its small, greenish-white flowers appear in clusters and are borne on slender pedicels.

The species is characterized by its ability to thrive in wet soils and its distinctive interrupted pattern of leaf arrangement.
