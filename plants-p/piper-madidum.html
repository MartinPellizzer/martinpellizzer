Piper madidum

Piper madidum, commonly known as the Madidum pepper or the Bornean pepper, is a climbing vine native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes numerous species of pepper plants. The plant produces small, green berries that mature to a dark purple or black color, containing the aromatic compounds responsible for its spicy flavor. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a slightly serrated edge.

Piper madidum is often cultivated for its culinary and medicinal properties, similar to other members of the Piper genus.
