Polygonum setigerum

Polygonum setigerum, commonly known as redroot smartweed, is a herbaceous annual plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, characterized by its distinct nodes and often reddish stems. The plant typically grows in moist soils along stream banks, wet meadows, and disturbed areas. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and have a rough texture, while the flowers are small, clustered in dense inflorescences.

The plant is known for its ability to thrive in wet conditions and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
