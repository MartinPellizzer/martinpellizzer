Pleurothallis anthrax

Pleurothallis anthrax, commonly known as the black anthrax orchid, is a species of orchid native to the cloud forests of South America.

It belongs to the Epidendroideae subfamily and is characterized by its compact growth habit and intricate floral structure. The plant typically produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and green, often with a distinctive dark marking. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a challenging plant for cultivation.
