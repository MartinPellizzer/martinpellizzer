Polygonum polygaloides subsp. kelloggii

Polygonum polygaloides subsp.

kelloggii, commonly known as Kellogg's smartweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to North America. It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as wet meadows, streambanks, and prairies.

The plant features erect, branching stems that are often reddish in color, and it produces small, clustered flowers with five petals. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture.

Kellogg's smartweed is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinators.
