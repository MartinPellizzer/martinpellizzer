Petrophyes microbotrys

Petrophyes microbotrys, commonly known as the rock lily, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, rosette-like growth form and produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale blue. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to high-altitude environments. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are densely packed around the base.

Petrophyes microbotrys is a hardy perennial that is often found in alpine meadows and rocky outcrops.
