Pyrus rivularis var. toringo

Pyrus rivularis var. toringo, commonly known as the Japanese whitebeam, is a variant of the river pear.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Japan, characterized by its smooth, gray bark and alternate, simple leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clusters, followed by small, round fruits that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.
