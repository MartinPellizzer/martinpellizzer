Phylica brevifolia

Phylica brevifolia, commonly known as the dwarf bottlebrush, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Proteaceae family and is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit. The plant produces dense, cylindrical inflorescences that are typically pink or red in color, giving it its common name. Its leaves are narrow and leathery, adapted to conserve water in its arid native environment.

Phylica brevifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
