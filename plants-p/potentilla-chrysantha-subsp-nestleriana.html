Potentilla chrysantha subsp. nestleriana

Potentilla chrysantha subsp.

nestleriana, commonly known as the yellow cinquefoil, is a low-growing shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers, which bloom in late summer and early autumn.

The plant typically has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and hardy nature.
