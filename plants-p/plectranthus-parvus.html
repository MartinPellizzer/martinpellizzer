Plectranthus parvus

Plectranthus parvus, commonly known as the dwarf African violet, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to South Africa.

It features small, oval-shaped leaves that are usually grayish-green with a soft, velvety texture. The plant produces clusters of tiny, tubular flowers in shades of violet, pink, or white, which bloom throughout the growing season. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers bright, indirect light.

Plectranthus parvus is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its attractive foliage and manageable size.
