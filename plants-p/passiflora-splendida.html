Passiflora splendida

Passiflora splendida, commonly known as the splendid passionflower, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, complex flowers that feature a blend of purple, blue, and white petals, along with a crown of filaments. The plant produces edible berries that are rich in antioxidants and have a sweet, tangy flavor. Its leaves are large, heart-shaped, and often have a velvety texture.

Passiflora splendida is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine.
