Puccinellia preslii

Puccinellia preslii, commonly known as the Preslii needlegrass, is a perennial grass species native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to withstand drought and high temperatures. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to thrive in nutrient-poor environments.

Puccinellia preslii plays a role in stabilizing soils and providing forage for livestock in its native habitat.
