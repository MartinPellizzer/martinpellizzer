Potentilla fruticosa var. purdomii

Potentilla fruticosa var. purdomii, commonly known as Purdom's cinquefoil, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense foliage of dark green, leathery leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, yellow flowers in the summer, which are attractive to pollinators. It thrives in well-drained, sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to harsh, alpine environments.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is often cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
