Polygala bryoides var. bryoides

Polygala bryoides var. bryoides, commonly known as the small milk-vetch, is a perennial herb native to arid and semi-arid regions of Australia.

It features slender, erect stems that can grow up to 30 cm in height, often with a reddish tinge. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in dense, spike-like clusters, which bloom from late winter to early spring. Its leaves are narrow and linear, typically arranged in opposite pairs along the stem.

This species is adapted to poor soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.
