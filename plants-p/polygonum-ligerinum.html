Polygonum ligerinum

Polygonum ligerinum, commonly known as the redroot polygonum, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, which includes plants commonly referred to as buckwheats. The plant is characterized by its upright stems that are reddish in color, often with a purplish tinge. Its leaves are alternate, ovate to lanceolate, and have a distinctive veining pattern.

Polygonum ligerinum typically grows in moist to wet soils in woodlands and along stream banks.
