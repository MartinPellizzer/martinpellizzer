Pogostemon javanicus

Pogostemon javanicus, commonly known as the Java betel plant, is a herbaceous perennial native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants. The plant is characterized by its square stems, opposite leaves, and small, tubular flowers that are typically purple or blue in color. It is often cultivated for its aromatic leaves, which are used in traditional medicine and as a stimulant.

The plant also has a distinctive root system that contributes to its medicinal properties.
