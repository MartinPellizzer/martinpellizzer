Phragmites nudus

Phragmites nudus, commonly known as narrowleaf common reed, is a tall, perennial grass that thrives in wetland environments.

It is characterized by its slender, hollow stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. This plant plays a crucial role in stabilizing soil and filtering water in marshes and along shorelines. It produces dense clusters of fluffy, feathery flowers that are dispersed by the wind.

Phragmites nudus is often found in coastal areas and can form large, dense stands that provide habitat for various wildlife species.
