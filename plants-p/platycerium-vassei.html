Platycerium vassei

Platycerium vassei, commonly known as the Vassei's staghorn fern, is a unique epiphytic plant native to the rainforests of northeastern Queensland, Australia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, antler-like fronds that grow directly from the central rosette of the plant. The plant lacks true roots and instead absorbs moisture and nutrients through its fronds, which are adapted to thrive in humid, shaded environments. Its reproductive structures, called sporangia, are typically found on the underside of the fronds, producing spores for propagation.

Platycerium vassei is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is a popular choice for terrariums and hanging gardens.
