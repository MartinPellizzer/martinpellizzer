Piper subscabridum

Piper subscabridum, commonly known as the roughleaf pepper or Brazilian pepper, is a member of the Piperaceae family.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its rough, scaly leaves. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its small, greenish-white flowers are followed by small, round berries that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans.

The plant is valued for its aromatic leaves, which are used in traditional medicine and as a spice in some cuisines.
