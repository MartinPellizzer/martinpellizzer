Potentilla rhypara var. shellyi

Potentilla rhypara var. shellyi, commonly known as Shelly's cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit. The plant features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are typically divided into five oval-shaped lobes, giving it the common name "cinquefoil.".

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.
