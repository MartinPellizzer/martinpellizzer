Plantago lagopodioides

Plantago lagopodioides, commonly known as the hare's foot plant, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its rosette of broad, hairy leaves that resemble the feet of a hare, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, greenish flowers in a spike that emerges from the center of the rosette. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
