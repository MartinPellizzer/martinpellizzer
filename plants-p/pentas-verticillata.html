Pentas verticillata

Pentas verticillata, commonly known as the wheelflower, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant, five-petaled flowers that bloom in clusters, often in shades of red, pink, or white. The plant has opposite, simple leaves that are usually ovate or elliptical in shape. It thrives in warm climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.

The flowers are pollinated primarily by bees and other insects, contributing to its ecological significance.
