Primula brachysiphon

Primula brachysiphon, commonly known as the narrow-siphon primrose, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant typically blooms in early spring and is adapted to cool, moist environments. Its leaves are broadly oval with a slightly hairy texture, and the stems are often hairy as well.

Primula brachysiphon is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in shaded garden settings.
