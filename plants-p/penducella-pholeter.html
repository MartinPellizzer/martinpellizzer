Penducella pholeter

Penducella pholeter, commonly known as the ghost flower, is a rare and enigmatic plant native to the rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, translucent petals that appear to shimmer in the light, giving it a ghostly appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that bloom at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. Its roots are known to secrete a unique compound that has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.

Due to habitat destruction, Penducella pholeter is now considered endangered and is protected in several conservation programs.
