Piper bakerianum

Piper bakerianum, commonly known as the Baker's pepper plant, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many species of pepper plants. The plant produces small, green berries that mature to a bright red color, containing the aromatic compound responsible for its distinctive flavor. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a rough texture, while its stems are woody and can grow quite long.

This species is valued both for its ornamental qualities and its use in traditional medicine.
