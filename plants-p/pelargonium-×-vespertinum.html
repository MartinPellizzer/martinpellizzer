Pelargonium × vespertinum

Pelargonium × vespertinum, commonly known as the evening geranium, is a hybrid plant in the Geraniaceae family.

It is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of red, pink, and purple. The plant features a compact growth habit with rounded, lobed leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Native to South Africa, it thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and containers.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
