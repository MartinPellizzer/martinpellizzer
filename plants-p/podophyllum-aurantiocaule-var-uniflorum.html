Podophyllum aurantiocaule var. uniflorum

Podophyllum aurantiocaule var. uniflorum, commonly known as the single-flowered orange-stemmed podophyllum, is a variant of the podophyllum genus native to certain regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive orange-colored stems and its single floral display per inflorescence. The plant features heart-shaped, glossy leaves that are typically dark green in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical significance lies in its unique morphological traits and potential ecological roles in its native habitat.
