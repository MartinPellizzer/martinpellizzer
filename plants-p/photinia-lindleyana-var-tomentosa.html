Photinia lindleyana var. tomentosa

Photinia lindleyana var. tomentosa, commonly known as the woolly photinia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and its distinctive grayish-white, fuzzy foliage that gives it a soft, woolly appearance. The leaves are typically ovate to elliptic in shape and have a leathery texture. It produces small, white to pale pink flowers in spring, followed by red or orange berries that are ornamental and attract wildlife.

This plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
