Potentilla vahliana

Potentilla vahliana, commonly known as the Himalayan cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central and South Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit with multiple stems. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically compound, with five leaflets, and the plant is adapted to thrive in high-altitude environments.

Potentilla vahliana is often found in rocky soils and alpine meadows, showcasing its resilience to harsh climatic conditions.
