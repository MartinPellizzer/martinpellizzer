Pleioblastus protrusus

Pleioblastus protrusus, commonly known as the bamboo grass or Japanese bamboo, is a species of grass in the Poaceae family.

It is native to East Asia, particularly Japan, and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and fine, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and is often found in mountainous regions. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.

Pleioblastus protrusus is also used in traditional medicine in some Asian countries.
