Phlomis capitata

Phlomis capitata, commonly known as narrow-leaved plumeless tarragon, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, upright growth habit and grayish-green foliage with narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its aromatic leaves have been historically used in herbal remedies and culinary applications.
