Piper scutifolium

Piper scutifolium, commonly known as the broad-leaved pepper vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many species of pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its heart-shaped, broad leaves and small, greenish-white flowers. Its fruits are berries that turn red when mature, and they are often used in traditional medicine.

The plant's botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and ethnobotanical contexts.
