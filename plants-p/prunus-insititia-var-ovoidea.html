Prunus insititia var. ovoidea

Prunus insititia var. ovoidea, commonly known as the gooseberry bush, is a shrub native to eastern North America.

It produces small, yellow to orange berries that are typically edible and have a tart flavor. The plant has alternate, simple leaves that are ovate to elliptical in shape, giving it its varietal name "ovoidea," meaning egg-shaped. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local wildlife.
