Pyrus natmataungensis

Pyrus natmataungensis, commonly known as the Natmataung pear, is a rare and endemic species native to the mountainous regions of Myanmar.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its deciduous, compound leaves and small, clustered flowers. The fruit is typically a pear-shaped berry with a smooth, thin skin and a sweet, aromatic pulp.

This plant is of significant botanical interest due to its unique morphology and limited distribution, making it a subject of conservation efforts.
