Paraboea prazeri

Paraboea prazeri, commonly known as the Plectranthus paraboea, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its opposite, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to purple. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens or containers.

Due to its ornamental value and fragrance, it is often cultivated in gardens for both its beauty and its mild aromatic properties.
