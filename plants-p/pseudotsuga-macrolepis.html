Pseudotsuga macrolepis

Pseudotsuga macrolepis, commonly known as the Japanese hemlock, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its dense, pyramidal shape and needle-like leaves that are dark green and slightly twisted. The tree produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers followed by cone-like structures that contain seeds. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in mountainous regions.

Due to its ornamental value, it is also cultivated in gardens and parks around the world.
