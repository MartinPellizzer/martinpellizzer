Pandanus ouveaensis

Pandanus ouveaensis, commonly known as the Ouvea pandanus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Pandanaceae.

It is native to the Pacific Islands, particularly found in French Polynesia. The plant is characterized by its tall, narrow leaves that are often used in traditional crafts and as thatching material. It thrives in coastal areas and is adapted to saline and sandy soils.

The flowers are small and clustered, typically appearing at the base of the plant.
