Poa nitida

Poa nitida, commonly known as slender meadowgrass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and slender, often blue-green colored leaves. The plant typically grows in open, moist habitats such as meadows, lawns, and stream banks. Its inflorescence consists of a dense, spike-like cluster of small, spikelet-based flowers.

Poa nitida is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping for its fine texture and durability.
