Phyteuma hemisphaericum var. graminifolium

Phyteuma hemisphaericum var. graminifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaved phyteuma, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to central and eastern Europe.

It features upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, tubular flowers, typically in shades of pink to purple. The leaves are linear and grass-like, which gives the plant its varietal name "graminifolium.". It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in meadows and woodland edges.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
