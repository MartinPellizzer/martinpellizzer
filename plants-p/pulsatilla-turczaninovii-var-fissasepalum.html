Pulsatilla turczaninovii var. fissasepalum

Pulsatilla turczaninovii var. fissasepalum, commonly known as the fissasepalum pasqueflower, is a member of the Ranunculaceae family.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its showy, cup-shaped flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. The plant features a short, stout stem with basal leaves that are palmately lobed. Native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, it thrives in rocky, well-drained soils.

Its botanical significance lies in its unique floral structure and its role in alpine ecosystems.
