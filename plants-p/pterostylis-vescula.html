Pterostylis vescula

Pterostylis vescula, commonly known as the yellow rain lily, is a small, terrestrial orchid native to eastern Australia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as underleaf litter or in rocky soils. The plant features a slender, white or pale yellow flower with a distinctive, narrow spur. Its leaves are usually narrow and grass-like, and it blooms during the autumn season.

Pterostylis vescula is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its simple, elegant floral structure.
