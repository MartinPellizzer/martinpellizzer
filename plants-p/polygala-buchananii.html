Polygala buchananii

Polygala buchananii, commonly known as the African milkweed, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Polygalaceae family and is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in open woodlands and grasslands, thriving in well-drained soils. Its flowers are usually pale green to yellowish, and it produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, wind-dispersed seeds.

The plant is noted for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
