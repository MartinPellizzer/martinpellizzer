Prunus maackii var. diamantina

Prunus maackii var. diamantina, commonly known as the Diamond Hill cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It features simple, ovate leaves that are glossy and dark green on top, with a lighter underside. The plant produces small, white flowers in early spring, followed by edible, dark red berries in late summer. It is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.

The species is also noted for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its resistance to pests and diseases.
