Phalaris coerulescens var. maderensis

Phalaris coerulescens var. maderensis, commonly known as the Mader's blue grass, is a variant of the blue grass species native to certain regions of Europe.

It is a perennial grass characterized by its slender, blue-green blades and dense, tufted growth habit. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along streambanks. Its flowering spikes are usually pale blue or purple, adding ornamental value to its ecological role.

It is valued for its hardiness and ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
