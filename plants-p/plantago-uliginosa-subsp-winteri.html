Plantago uliginosa subsp. winteri

Plantago uliginosa subsp.

winteri, commonly known as the bog plantain, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland habitats in North America. It features broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs, and it produces small, greenish-white flowers in dense clusters.

The plant thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in bogs, fens, and other wetland environments. It is characterized by its ability to tolerate waterlogged conditions and its role in stabilizing soil in these ecologically sensitive areas.

Its botanical structure supports its ecological function as a pioneer species in wetland succession.
