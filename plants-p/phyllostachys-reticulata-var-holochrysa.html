Phyllostachys reticulata var. holochrysa

Phyllostachys reticulata var. holochrysa, commonly known as the golden bamboo, is a variant of the silver bamboo species.

It is characterized by its striking golden-yellow culms that contrast beautifully with its green leaves. This plant is a member of the grass family, Poaceae, and is native to eastern Asia. It grows as a clump-forming bamboo, often reaching heights of up to 10 meters.

The golden bamboo is prized for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
