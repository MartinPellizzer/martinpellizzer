Phacelia demissa

Phacelia demissa, commonly known as blue phacelia, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flower clusters that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as meadows and grasslands.

Phacelia demissa is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators.
