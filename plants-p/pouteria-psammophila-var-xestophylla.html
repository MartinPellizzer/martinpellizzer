Pouteria psammophila var. xestophylla

Pouteria psammophila var. xestophylla, commonly known as the sandplain spondias, is a tropical tree native to parts of South America.

It is characterized by its small, smooth, elliptical leaves that are usually dark green and glossy. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by edible, round fruits with a sweet and tangy flavor. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry forest and savanna ecosystems.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential use in agroforestry systems.
