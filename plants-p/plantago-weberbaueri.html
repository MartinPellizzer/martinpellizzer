Plantago weberbaueri

Plantago weberbaueri, commonly known as Weberbauer's plantain, is a species of flowering plant in the family Plantaginaceae.

It is native to parts of Central and South America, particularly in regions with temperate to subtropical climates. The plant is characterized by its rosette of broad, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs. It produces small, greenish flowers arranged in a spike-like inflorescence, which are typically wind-pollinated.

This species is often found in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or grassland habitats.
