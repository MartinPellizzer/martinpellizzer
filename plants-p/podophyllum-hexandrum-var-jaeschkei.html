Podophyllum hexandrum var. jaeschkei

Podophyllum hexandrum var. jaeschkei, commonly known as the Himalayan mayapple, is a perennial herb native to the alpine regions of the Himalayas.

It features a short, rhizomatous stem with broad, heart-shaped leaves that are usually hairy on the underside. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers with six stamens, which bloom in early summer. Its fruit is a red, fleshy berry that ripens in late summer, containing several seeds.

This variant is notable for its adaptation to high-altitude environments and its use in traditional medicine.
