Phaius delavayi

Phaius delavayi, commonly known as the Delavay's orchid, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and large, showy flowers. The plant typically blooms in late summer, producing inflorescences with multiple pale yellow to greenish-white flowers. Its leaves are broad, lance-shaped, and arranged in a basal rosette.

Phaius delavayi is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
