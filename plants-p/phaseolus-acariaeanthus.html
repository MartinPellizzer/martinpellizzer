Phaseolus acariaeanthus

Phaseolus acariaeanthus, commonly known as the hairy bean, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive hairy leaves and small, pod-like fruits. The plant typically grows as an erect annual herb, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its flowers are usually pale yellow or white, with a unique structure that aids in pollination by insects.

The seeds contained within its pods are often used in local cuisines and traditional medicine.
