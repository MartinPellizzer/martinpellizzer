Pinalia pachyphylla

Pinalia pachyphylla, commonly known as the thick-leaved pinalia, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that store water, allowing it to survive in dry environments. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers during the cooler months, typically in spring. Its growth habit is compact, with a rosette formation that helps reduce water loss.

Pinalia pachyphylla is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
