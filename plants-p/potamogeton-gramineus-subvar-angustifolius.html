Potamogeton gramineus subvar. angustifolius

Potamogeton gramineus subvar. angustifolius, commonly known as narrow-leaved pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in North America.

It has long, narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically dark green and arranged in whorls around the stem. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often submerged, followed by elongated, cylindrical fruits. It thrives in shallow, slow-moving waters and plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.

Its growth can sometimes lead to dense vegetation, affecting water flow and clarity.
