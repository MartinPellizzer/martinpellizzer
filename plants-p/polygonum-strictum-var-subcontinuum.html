Polygonum strictum var. subcontinuum

Polygonum strictum var. subcontinuum, commonly known as the narrowleaf smartweed, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist soils along stream banks and wetlands. The plant features slender, upright stems with alternate, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. Its small, clustered flowers are usually pink to reddish, appearing in late summer.

It plays a role in supporting local pollinators and is sometimes used in wetland restoration projects.
