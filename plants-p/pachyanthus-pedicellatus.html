Pachyanthus pedicellatus

Pachyanthus pedicellatus, commonly known as the Christmas bell, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its leaves are glossy and dark green, often arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem.

The Christmas bell is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
