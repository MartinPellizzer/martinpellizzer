Primula asperulata

Primula asperulata, commonly known as the rough primrose, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of East Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in cool, moist environments. It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is often found in high-altitude meadows.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden cultivation in temperate climates.
