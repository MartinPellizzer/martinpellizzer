Pinus densiflora f. ussuriensis

Pinus densiflora f. ussuriensis, commonly known as the Ussuri pine, is a variant of the Japanese black pine.

It is characterized by its dense foliage and needle-like leaves that are typically longer than those of the typical form. The plant produces cones that are generally smaller and more clustered compared to the standard species. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forested areas of East Asia.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological and horticultural contexts.
