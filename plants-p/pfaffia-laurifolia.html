Pfaffia laurifolia

Pfaffia laurifolia, commonly known as Brazilian ginseng, is a flowering plant native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is known for its medicinal properties, with roots and leaves used in traditional medicine. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in dense forest understories.

Due to habitat loss, Pfaffia laurifolia is considered endangered and is protected in several regions.
