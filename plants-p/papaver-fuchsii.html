Papaver fuchsii

Papaver fuchsii, commonly known as the Fuchs' poppy, is a herbaceous perennial native to Central Asia.

It features striking, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from deep red to orange, often with a contrasting black center. The plant has smooth, lance-shaped leaves that are typically bluish-green in appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

The flowers bloom in early summer, adding a vibrant splash of color to the landscape.
