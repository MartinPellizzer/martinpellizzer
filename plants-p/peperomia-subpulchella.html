Peperomia subpulchella

Peperomia subpulchella, commonly known as the silver peperomia, is a compact, low-maintenance plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It features thick, waxy leaves that are typically silver or gray-green in color, which help it retain moisture in its natural habitat. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually green or reddish, though they are not the main ornamental feature. It thrives in bright, indirect light and prefers well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Peperomia subpulchella is popular as a houseplant due to its ability to thrive indoors with minimal care.
