Petalinia bancana

Petalinia bancana, commonly known as the golden bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking yellow to orange bell-shaped flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, with a smooth, waxy surface.

Petalinia bancana is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
