Pedicularis bicornuta var. adenocalyx

Pedicularis bicornuta var. adenocalyx, commonly known as the twin-horned monkey orchid, is a unique flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orobanchaceae family and is characterized by its striking, spurred flowers that resemble small monkeys. The plant is hemiparasitic, meaning it partially depends on host plants for nutrients through its root system. Its flowers are typically pink or purple, with a distinctive two-horned structure on the labellum.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in specialized gardens due to its rarity and beauty.
