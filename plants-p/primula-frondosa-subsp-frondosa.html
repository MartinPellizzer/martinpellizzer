Primula frondosa subsp. frondosa

Primula frondosa subsp.

frondosa, commonly known as the fronded primrose, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of East Asia. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and rocky crevices.

The plant features a basal rosette of lance-shaped, hairy leaves that are often deeply lobed. Its flowers are showy, with five petals that are usually pink or white, and they bloom in early spring.

The subspecies is characterized by its distinctive foliage and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
