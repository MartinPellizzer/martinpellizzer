Passiflora obtusiloba var. glandulifera

Passiflora obtusiloba var. glandulifera, commonly known as the glandular passionflower, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, showy flowers with five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments surrounding the reproductive structures. The leaves are typically large, heart-shaped, and have a rough texture with prominent veins. This species is known for its hairy stems and leaves, which are covered in glandular trichomes.

It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.
