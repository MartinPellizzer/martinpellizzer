Pleopeltis microgrammoides

Pleopeltis microgrammoides, commonly known as the small-stippled huperzia, is a species of fern belonging to the family Huperziaceae.

It is characterized by its compact, evergreen foliage with small, ovate leaves that have a distinctive stippled appearance. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its fronds are leathery and have a deep green color, with veins that form a network of fine lines.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in indoor settings or as part of woodland gardens.
