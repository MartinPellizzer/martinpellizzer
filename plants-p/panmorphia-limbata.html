Panmorphia limbata

Panmorphia limbata, commonly known as the variable limb plant, is a unique species characterized by its morphologically adaptable structure.

It exhibits a wide range of leaf shapes and sizes, which can vary depending on environmental conditions. The plant's stem is typically flexible, allowing it to contort and attach to surfaces for support. Its flowers are small and clustered, often displaying a range of colors that aid in pollination.

Botanically, it is noted for its ability to hybridize with closely related species, contributing to its genetic diversity.
