Piptomeris nematoclada

Piptomeris nematoclada, commonly known as the slender water fern, is a species of aquatic fern native to Southeast Asia.

It typically grows in shallow, still freshwater habitats such as ponds and rice fields. The plant has slender, floating fronds that are often divided into small, narrow segments. Its reproductive structures are borne on the underside of the fronds, and it reproduces through spores rather than seeds.

The plant's morphology is adapted to aquatic environments, with specialized tissues for gas exchange and buoyancy.
