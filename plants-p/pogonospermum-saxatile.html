Pogonospermum saxatile

Pogonospermum saxatile, commonly known as the rock beard, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit and its ability to thrive in rocky, well-drained soils. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the warmer months. Its fibrous root system helps it retain moisture in harsh, dry environments.

Pogonospermum saxatile is often used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements and drought tolerance.
