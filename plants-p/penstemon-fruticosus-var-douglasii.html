Penstemon fruticosus var. douglasii

Penstemon fruticosus var. douglasii, commonly known as Douglas's beardtongue, is a perennial plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to purple. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its foliage consists of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, fuzzy texture.
