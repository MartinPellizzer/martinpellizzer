Pelargonium oxaloides

Pelargonium oxaloides, commonly known as the Oxalate-leaved geranium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, white hairs, giving them a velvety texture. The plant produces clusters of small, pink to red flowers that bloom in summer, adding ornamental value to gardens. Its foliage contains calcium oxalate crystals, which can cause irritation if touched, making it important to handle with care.

Pelargonium oxaloides is often grown as a decorative plant due to its unique appearance and adaptability to various growing conditions.
