Primula forbesii subsp. meiantha

Primula forbesii subsp.

meiantha, commonly known as the narrow-leaved primrose, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of China. It features basal rosettes of narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of bell-shaped, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color.

The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in forest understories or rocky slopes. It has small, hairy leaves and is adapted to high-altitude conditions with its compact growth form.

The flowers bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
