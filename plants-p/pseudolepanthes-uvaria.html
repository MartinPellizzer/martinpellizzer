Pseudolepanthes uvaria

Pseudolepanthes uvaria, commonly known as the red-tipped orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, velvety red petals and sepals that contrast with its greenish-white lip. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers bloom in clusters and are pollinated by specific insect species, contributing to its ecological significance.

Pseudolepanthes uvaria is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
