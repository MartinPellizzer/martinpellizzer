Phalaenopsis mannii f. flava

Phalaenopsis mannii f. flava, commonly known as the Yellow Phalaenopsis, is a hybrid orchid species originating from Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its elongated, pendant inflorescences that bear clusters of bright yellow flowers with white markings. The plant is an epiphyte, typically growing on trees or rocks in humid tropical environments. Its leaves are broad, ovate, and covered in a fine, velvety texture.

This variety is prized for its vibrant color and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes in both home and commercial settings.
