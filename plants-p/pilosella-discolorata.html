Pilosella discolorata

Pilosella discolorata, commonly known as the variable pilosella, is a flowering plant in the Asteraceae family.

It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in meadows and grasslands. The plant is characterized by its small, yellowish flowers and hairy stems, which give it its common name. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Pilosella discolorata is an annual or biennial plant that thrives in temperate climates.
