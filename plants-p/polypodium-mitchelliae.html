Polypodium mitchelliae

Polypodium mitchelliae, commonly known as Mitchell's fern, is a species of epiphytic fern native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows on tree trunks and branches in humid, shaded environments. The plant has lance-shaped, serrated leaves that are usually dark green and glossy. It produces small, spore-bearing structures called sori on the underside of its fronds.

This fern is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in shaded gardens.
