Petrea oblonga

Petrea oblonga, commonly known as the silver vine, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves with a silvery underside, giving it a striking appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

Petrea oblonga is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and adaptability.
