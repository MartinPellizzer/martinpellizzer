Premna thwaitesii

Premna thwaitesii, commonly known as the silverleaf premna, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its opposite, ovate to elliptical leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery or grayish pubescence. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by small, round berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in local medicine.
