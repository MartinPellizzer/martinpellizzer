Polygonum roberti

Polygonum roberti, commonly known as the California knotweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs, and its stems are often reddish in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in riparian zones and along stream banks.

Polygonum roberti is a member of the buckwheat family (Polygonaceae) and is known for its ornamental value in gardens.
