Pinus luchuensis subsp. taiwanensis

Pinus luchuensis subsp.

taiwanensis, commonly known as the Taiwan dark pine, is a rare and endemic conifer native to Taiwan. It is a medium-sized evergreen tree characterized by its dark green, needle-like leaves and cone-bearing structure.

The plant thrives in cool, moist climates and is typically found in mountainous regions with well-drained soils. Its botanical features include a straight trunk, dense foliage, and distinctive bark that becomes fissured with age.

This subspecies plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
