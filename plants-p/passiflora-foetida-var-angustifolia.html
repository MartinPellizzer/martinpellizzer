Passiflora foetida var. angustifolia

Passiflora foetida var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leafed passionflower, is a species native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its striking, complex flowers that feature five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments around the reproductive structures. The plant typically grows as a climbing vine with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. Its fruit is a small, round, greenish-yellow berry that turns red when mature.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
