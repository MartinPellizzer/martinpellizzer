Penstemon confertus var. rigidus

Penstemon confertus var. rigidus, commonly known as the rigid penstemon, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces tall spikes of tubular, two-lipped flowers that are typically purple or blue in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for xeriscaping.

Its botanical features make it a valuable addition to native plant gardens and pollinator habitats.
