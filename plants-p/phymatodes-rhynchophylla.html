Phymatodes rhynchophylla

Phymatodes rhynchophylla, commonly known as the Mexican beakleaf, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, greenish-white flowers clustered in dense inflorescences. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, typically growing in well-drained soils. It is adapted to withstand drought conditions through its deep root system and succulent-like leaves.

Phymatodes rhynchophylla is often found in rocky habitats and is considered a rare and endemic species in its native range.
