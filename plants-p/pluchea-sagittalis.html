Pluchea sagittalis

Pluchea sagittalis, commonly known as arrowleaf pluchea, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in wet, marshy areas and has heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive arrowhead shape. Its small yellow flowers are arranged in clusters and bloom during the wet season.

Pluchea sagittalis is often found in riparian zones and is adapted to thrive in humid, tropical environments.
