Panphalea bupleurifolia

Panphalea bupleurifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leafed bupleurum, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is found in open woodlands and grasslands. The plant features slender, branching stems with small, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are clustered in umbels and are usually pale blue to purple in color.

Panphalea bupleurifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
