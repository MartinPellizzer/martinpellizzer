Potentilla hudsonii

Potentilla hudsonii, commonly known as Hudson's cinquefoil, is a perennial flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its showy, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist to wet environments, such as marshes and stream banks, and has a low, spreading growth habit. Its leaves are palmately divided, and the stems are often covered with fine hairs.

Hudson's cinquefoil is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
