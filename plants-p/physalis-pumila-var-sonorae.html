Physalis pumila var. sonorae

Physalis pumila var. sonorae, commonly known as the Sonoran groundcherry, is a herbaceous perennial native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its prostrate growth habit and produces small, edible fruits enclosed in a papery calyx. The plant features hairy stems and leaves with a rough texture, adapted to thrive in dry, sandy soils. Its flowering period typically occurs in late spring to early summer, and the fruits mature to a yellow-orange color.

This species is often found in desert scrub and rocky habitats, showcasing its resilience to harsh environmental conditions.
