Penstemon newberryi var. rupicola

Penstemon newberryi var. rupicola, commonly known as the Newberry's beardtongue, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It typically grows in rocky or gravelly habitats, showcasing its adaptability to arid conditions. The plant features upright stems with narrow, gray-green leaves that are covered in fine hairs. Its showy, tubular flowers range in color from pink to purple, blooming in late spring to early summer.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance in garden settings.
