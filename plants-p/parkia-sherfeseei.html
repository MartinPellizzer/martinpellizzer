Parkia sherfeseei

Parkia sherfeseei, commonly known as the African sycamore, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its bipinnate leaves and showy yellow flowers. The tree produces pods that contain edible seeds, which are often used in traditional cuisines. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ecological and cultural significance, it is valued for both its ornamental beauty and practical uses.
