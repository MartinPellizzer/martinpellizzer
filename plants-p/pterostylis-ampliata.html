Pterostylis ampliata

Pterostylis ampliata, commonly known as the broad-leafed helmet orchid, is an herbaceous plant native to eastern Australia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its unique, helmet-shaped flower. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as rainforests and understoreys. Its leaves are broad and strap-like, and the flowers are usually greenish-yellow with a distinctive hood-like structure.

Pterostylis ampliata is a terrestrial orchid that relies on mycorrhizal fungi for nutrient uptake, showcasing its ecological complexity.
