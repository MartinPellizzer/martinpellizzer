Plinia ekmaniana

Plinia ekmaniana, commonly known as the Brazilian guava, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to Brazil and is characterized by its small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically green when unripe and turn yellow or red when mature. The leaves are simple, alternate, and have a slightly serrated margin.

It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in shaded, moist environments.
