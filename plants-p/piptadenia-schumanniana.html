Piptadenia schumanniana

Piptadenia schumanniana, commonly known as the yellow angulate pod tree, is a tropical leguminous plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with a distinctive angular shape and yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces long, slender pods that contain edible seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in forested areas.

Its botanical features make it an important species for both ecological and medicinal purposes.
