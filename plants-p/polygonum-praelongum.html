Polygonum praelongum

Polygonum praelongum, commonly known as slender knotweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern North America.

It features upright, slender stems that are often tinged with red, and its leaves are lance-shaped with a slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-white in color, appearing in late summer. It thrives in moist environments such as wetlands, stream banks, and marshy areas.

Polygonum praelongum is often found in shaded to partially shaded conditions and is known for its rapid growth and ability to colonize disturbed sites.
