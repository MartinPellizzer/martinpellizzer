Polystachya membranacea

Polystachya membranacea, commonly known as the membranous polystachya, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and linear, grass-like leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, pale green to white flowers that are often overlooked due to their modest appearance. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forests or along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is cultivated in some horticultural settings.
