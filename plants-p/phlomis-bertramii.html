Phlomis bertramii

Phlomis bertramii, commonly known as Bertram's spurgeflower, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces showy, yellow to orange flowers in late spring to early summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs, and it thrives in well-drained soils. It is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats, preferring full sun exposure.

Phlomis bertramii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
