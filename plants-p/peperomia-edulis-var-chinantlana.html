Peperomia edulis var. chinantlana

Peperomia edulis var. chinantlana, commonly known as the Mexican pepper plant, is a tropical shrub native to Central America.

It features compact, fleshy leaves that are often used in traditional medicine and cuisine. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or red in color. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires moderate watering to prevent root rot.

Its ornamental value and medicinal properties make it a popular choice for both horticultural and herbal applications.
