Pholidota carnea var. pumila

Pholidota carnea var. pumila, commonly known as the silky pholidota, is a variant of the pholidota genus native to Southeast Asia.

It is a climbing shrub characterized by its soft, hairy stems and small, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces fragrant, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in clusters, adding ornamental value. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and as a decorative plant.
