Potentilla pensylvanica var. sibirica

Potentilla pensylvanica var. sibirica, commonly known as the Siberian cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia and North America.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its dense, sprawling growth habit and hairy stems. The plant produces showy, yellow flowers in clusters during the summer months, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically oval-shaped with serrated edges, and the plant is often found in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or wetland habitats.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
