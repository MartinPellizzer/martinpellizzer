Primula auriculata var. caucasica

Primula auriculata var. caucasica, commonly known as the Caucasian primrose, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus.

It is characterized by its rosette of broad, ovate leaves and clusters of fragrant, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in woodland or alpine habitats. It has a compact growth habit and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.

The flowers typically bloom in early spring, adding a touch of color to the landscape during the transitional season.
