Pilosocereus braunii

Pilosocereus braunii, commonly known as the Braun's cactus, is a species of cactus native to southern Brazil.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems that are covered in white, woolly spines, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers during the warmer months, typically in spring or early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Pilosocereus braunii is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique aesthetic and hardy nature.
