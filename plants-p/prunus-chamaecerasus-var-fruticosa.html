Prunus chamaecerasus var. fruticosa

Prunus chamaecerasus var. fruticosa, commonly known as the dwarf cherry or Japanese snowball, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan.

It features dense, rounded growth with small, ovate leaves that turn red in the fall. The plant produces clusters of white, fragrant flowers in early spring before the leaves emerge. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular ornamental plant.

Its compact size and seasonal blooms make it suitable for gardens and landscapes.
