Porpax repens var. obtusa

Porpax repens var. obtusa, commonly known as the broadleaf sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to arctic and alpine regions.

It features a mat-forming growth habit with fleshy, succulent leaves that are typically broad and rounded. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in the summer months. It thrives in well-drained, rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to cold climates.

Its botanical characteristics make it a hardy species often found in high-altitude or polar environments.
