Persicaria rivularis

Persicaria rivularis, commonly known as water pepper, is a flowering plant native to wetland and marsh environments.

It is characterized by its upright, hairy stems and small, greenish-white flowers that appear in clusters. The leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss. It thrives in moist soils and is often found near streams, ponds, and other water bodies.

The plant is known for its ability to tolerate both aquatic and terrestrial conditions, making it a versatile wetland species.
