Plantago lanceifolia

Plantago lanceifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf plantain, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to North America.

It features broad, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving them a rough texture. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in a dense spike, which bloom in the summer months. It thrives in a variety of habitats, including roadsides, fields, and disturbed soils.

Narrowleaf plantain is known for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional herbal remedies.
