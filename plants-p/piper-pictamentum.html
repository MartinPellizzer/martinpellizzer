Piper pictamentum

Piper pictamentum, commonly known as the painted pepper or Indian pepper, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Asia and Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, waxy, and often colorful berries that range from yellow to red. The plant belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a rough texture.

The fruit is used in traditional medicine and as a spice in some cuisines.
