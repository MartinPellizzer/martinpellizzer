Phlomis ghilanensis

Phlomis ghilanensis, commonly known as the Ghilan wormwood, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Iran.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its gray-green, hairy leaves and tall, branched inflorescences. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the summer months, attracting various pollinators. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or limestone habitats.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and potentially endangered species.
