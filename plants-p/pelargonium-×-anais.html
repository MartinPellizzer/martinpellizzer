Pelargonium × anais

Pelargonium × anais, commonly known as the Anais geranium, is a hybrid pelargonium species originating from crosses between Pelargonium hortorum and Pelargonium ×ibiscifolium.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, double or semi-double flowers in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant produces velvety, ovate leaves with a slightly serrated margin, and it thrives in full sun to partial shade. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for gardens and containers due to its vibrant blooms and resilience.

Pelargonium × anais is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
