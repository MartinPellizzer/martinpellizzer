Panicum vaginiflorum

Panicum vaginiflorum, commonly known as the velvet grass, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are often covered in a fine, hairy coating. Its leaves are narrow and have a distinctive bluish-green color, which helps it to thrive in moist, sandy soils.

Panicum vaginiflorum is often found in wetlands and along the edges of water bodies, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local wildlife.
