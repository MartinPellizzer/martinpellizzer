Prangos cinerea

Prangos cinerea, commonly known as the gray thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its grayish-green, hairy leaves and spiny, purple-blue flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its structure includes a stout stem with alternate leaves that are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs.

Prangos cinerea is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and along coastal areas.
