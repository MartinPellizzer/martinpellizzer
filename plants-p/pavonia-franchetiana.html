Pavonia franchetiana

Pavonia franchetiana, commonly known as the redroot false mallow, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, reddish stems and heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture. The plant produces showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in summer, adding ornamental value to gardens. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

Its botanical features make it both ecologically valuable and aesthetically pleasing in arid landscapes.
