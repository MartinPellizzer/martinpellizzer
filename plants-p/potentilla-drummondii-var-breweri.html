Potentilla drummondii var. breweri

Potentilla drummondii var. breweri, commonly known as Drummond's cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in dry, rocky habitats such as slopes and prairies. The plant produces showy yellow flowers with five petals, which bloom in early summer. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several small, oval-shaped leaflets.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in arid conditions.
