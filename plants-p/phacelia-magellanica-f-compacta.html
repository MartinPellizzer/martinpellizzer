Phacelia magellanica f. compacta

Phacelia magellanica f. compacta, commonly known as the compact phacelia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a low-growing, mat-forming perennial that features small, fuzzy leaves and clusters of blue to purple flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes. Its compact growth habit and ornamental flowers make it a popular choice for gardeners seeking drought-tolerant plants.

It plays a role in supporting local pollinators and has ecological significance in its native habitat.
