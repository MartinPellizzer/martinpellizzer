Panicum adhaerens

Panicum adhaerens, commonly known as bloodgrass, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its striking crimson-colored inflorescence. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or along riverbanks. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, while its seed heads are dense and often resemble a cluster of blood droplets.

Due to its unique appearance, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant in gardens and botanical collections.
