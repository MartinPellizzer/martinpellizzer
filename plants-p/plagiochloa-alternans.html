Plagiochloa alternans

Plagiochloa alternans, commonly known as the zigzag grass, is a perennial grass species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive zigzag growth pattern, which gives it its common name. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are often slightly folded, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Plagiochloa alternans is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
