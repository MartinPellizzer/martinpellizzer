Phreatia hypsorrhynchos

Phreatia hypsorrhynchos, commonly known as the mountain orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces showy, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow. The plant thrives in shaded, humid environments, often found in forest understories. Its roots are adapted to absorb moisture from the soil and decaying organic matter.

Phreatia hypsorrhynchos is known for its resilience and ability to survive in nutrient-poor conditions.
