Penstemon baccharifolius var. schaffneri

Penstemon baccharifolius var. schaffneri, commonly known as Schaffner's beardtongue, is a perennial flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of tubular, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its drought-tolerant nature. This variety thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

Its unique floral structure and adaptability to arid environments make it a valuable addition to native plant gardens.
