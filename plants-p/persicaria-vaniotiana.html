Persicaria vaniotiana

Persicaria vaniotiana, commonly known as the Vaniot's knotweed, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, which includes plants commonly known as smartweeds or knotweeds. The plant is characterized by its upright, branching stems and small, clustered flowers that typically bloom in late summer. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a reddish or purple tinge, especially along the veins.

Persicaria vaniotiana is adapted to arid environments and is often found growing in rocky or sandy soils.
