Pelargonium pubescens

Pelargonium pubescens, commonly known as the woolly geranium, is a species native to South Africa.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its hairy, velvety leaves and showy, five-petaled flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its flowers, which range in color from pink to red, bloom in spring and summer.

Pelargonium pubescens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
