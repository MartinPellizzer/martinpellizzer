Phragmipedium exstaminodium

Phragmipedium exstaminodium, commonly known as the golden phragmipedium, is a species of orchid native to the tropical rainforests of South America.

It is characterized by its striking golden-yellow flowers, which are typically borne on tall, erect stems. The plant has broad, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern, aiding in its ability to capture sunlight in its shaded habitat. It thrives in humid, well-drained environments and requires moderate to high levels of light.

This orchid is prized by horticulturists for its ornamental value and unique floral display.
