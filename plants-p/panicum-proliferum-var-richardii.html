Panicum proliferum var. richardii

Panicum proliferum var. richardii, commonly known as Richard's lovegrass, is a perennial grass native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and dense, fibrous root system that helps stabilize soil in harsh environments. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with pale green to golden bracts, which are adapted for wind pollination. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it a valuable species for erosion control.

Its rapid growth and ability to form dense stands make it an important component of native grassland ecosystems.
