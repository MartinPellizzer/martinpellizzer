Puya laxa

Puya laxa, commonly known as the lax puya, is a striking member of the Bromeliaceae family native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and large, showy bracts that can range in color from white to pink or red. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, which bloom in the spring. Puya laxa is a perennial plant that can grow up to several meters in height, making it a dramatic addition to gardens.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, requiring minimal water once established.
