Pulsatilla alpina subsp. apiifolia

Pulsatilla alpina subsp.

apiifolia, commonly known as the alpine pasqueflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its showy, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in early spring.

The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky soils, thriving in cool, moist environments. Its leaves are palmately divided, and it produces follicles that contain numerous seeds.

This subspecies is notable for its ecological importance in supporting pollinators and its role in alpine ecosystems.
