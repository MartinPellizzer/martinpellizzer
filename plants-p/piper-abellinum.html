Piper abellinum

Piper abellinum, commonly known as the Amazonian pepper or wild pepper, is a climbing vine native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many species of pepper plants. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that develop into elongated, narrow fruits. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped with a rough texture and a strong, pungent aroma.

The plant is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional herbal remedies.
