Panicum acariferum

Panicum acariferum, commonly known as the hairy panicgrass, is a grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and hairy leaves, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open areas such as fields and roadsides.

Panicum acariferum is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in landscaping.
