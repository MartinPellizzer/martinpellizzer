Pulicaria undulata

Pulicaria undulata, commonly known as the undulating fleabane, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is a low-growing perennial with hairy, silver-green leaves that are often wavy or undulating in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish to pale green flowers that bloom in late summer and early autumn. It thrives in dry, sunny habitats such as rocky soils and grasslands.

Pulicaria undulata is often found in Mediterranean climates and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.
