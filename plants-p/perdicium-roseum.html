Perdicium roseum

Perdicium roseum, commonly known as the rose-colored lichen, is a unique symbiotic organism composed of a fungus and a green alga.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically found on tree bark or rocks in temperate regions. The plant's distinctive pinkish or reddish hue comes from the algal component, which photosynthesizes to provide energy for both partners. It plays an important role in nutrient cycling and serves as a habitat for various microorganisms.

Due to its delicate nature, it is often used as an indicator of environmental health.
