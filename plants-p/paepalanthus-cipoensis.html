Paepalanthus cipoensis

Paepalanthus cipoensis, commonly known as the Cipo marsh grass, is a flowering plant native to the Andean region of South America.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is typically found in wetland and marsh environments. The plant is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences.
