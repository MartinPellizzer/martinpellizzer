Passiflora lancearia

Passiflora lancearia, commonly known as the lanceleaf passionflower, is a climbing vine native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, lance-shaped leaves and striking, intricate flowers that feature a blend of blue, purple, and white petals. The plant produces edible, orange-yellow berries that are rich in antioxidants and have a tropical flavor. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Passiflora lancearia is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
