Prunus sellowii var. longifolia

Prunus sellowii var. longifolia, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or longleaf cherry, is a deciduous tree native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its elongated leaves, which are often longer than they are wide, and its showy pink to white flowers that bloom in spring. The tree produces small, edible drupes that turn red when mature, attracting various wildlife. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in horticulture and ecological studies.
