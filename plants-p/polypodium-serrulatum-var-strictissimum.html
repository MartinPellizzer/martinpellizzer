Polypodium serrulatum var. strictissimum

Polypodium serrulatum var. strictissimum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wall fern, is a species of fern native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its finely serrated, narrow leaves. The plant typically grows on rocky surfaces or in shaded, moist environments. Its fronds are usually upright and can reach lengths of up to 30 centimeters.

The spores are produced on the underside of the fronds in clusters, contributing to its reproductive strategy.
