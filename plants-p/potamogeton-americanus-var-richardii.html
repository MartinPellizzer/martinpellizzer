Potamogeton americanus var. richardii

Potamogeton americanus var. richardii, commonly known as Richard's pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to North America.

It typically grows in freshwater environments such as lakes, ponds, and slow-moving rivers. The plant has long, slender, ribbon-like leaves that are often arranged in whorls around the stem. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually found in clusters near the water's surface.

This variety is important for aquatic ecosystems as it provides habitat and food for various waterfowl and invertebrates.
