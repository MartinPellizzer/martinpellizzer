Podophyllum versipelle subsp. versipelle

Podophyllum versipelle subsp.

versipelle, commonly known as the purple false hellebore, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States. It is characterized by its distinctive, arrow-shaped leaves that are typically dark green with a purple underside.

The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers in the spring, which are followed by bright red berries in the fall. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and along streambanks.

The plant contains toxic compounds, making it important to handle with care.
