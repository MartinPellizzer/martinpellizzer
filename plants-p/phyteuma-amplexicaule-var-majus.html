Phyteuma amplexicaule var. majus

Phyteuma amplexicaule var. majus, commonly known as the broadleaf phyteuma, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central and Southern Europe.

It features showy, spire-like flower clusters that range in color from yellow to orange, often with a reddish or purple base. The plant has basal leaves that are broad and clasping, forming a rosette at the base, while the upper leaves are narrower and alternate. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its blooming period typically occurs in late spring to early summer.
