Pilocereus lateralis

Pilocereus lateralis, commonly known as the moon cactus, is a cactus species native to South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, spiny stems and nocturnal blooming habits. The plant produces fragrant, white to pink flowers that open at night. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Its unique appearance and flowering behavior make it a popular choice for succulent collections.
