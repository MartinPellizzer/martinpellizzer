Pulsatilla patens subsp. elongata

Pulsatilla patens subsp.

elongata, commonly known as the long-stemmed pasqueflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its showy, early spring blooms of pale yellow to pink flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun. Its leaves are palmately divided, and the stems are elongated, giving it its subspecies name.

It is a valued ornamental plant due to its striking appearance and ecological importance in its native environment.
