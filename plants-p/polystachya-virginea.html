Polystachya virginea

Polystachya virginea, commonly known as the Virgin's Tears or the Cape orchid, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its delicate, pendulous inflorescences. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in coastal regions. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, with a unique, spurred structure.

Polystachya virginea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic beauty.
