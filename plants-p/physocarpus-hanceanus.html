Physocarpus hanceanus

Physocarpus hanceanus, commonly known as Chinese ninebark, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its rugged, exfoliating bark that peels in thin, papery layers, revealing a mottled appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers in early summer, followed by red or orange berries in the fall. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is adaptable to various soil types, making it a hardy ornamental plant.

Its foliage turns vibrant shades of red and orange in autumn, adding ornamental value to gardens.
