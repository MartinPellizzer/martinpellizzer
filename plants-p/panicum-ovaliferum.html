Panicum ovaliferum

Panicum ovaliferum, commonly known as the oval-fruit panicgrass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, erect stems that often bear panicles of small, spikelet-like flowers. The plant produces oval-shaped fruits, which are typically covered in fine hairs, contributing to its common name. Panicum ovaliferum thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for ornamental and ecological landscaping.

Its adaptability and drought tolerance make it a valuable plant in restoration projects and natural habitats.
