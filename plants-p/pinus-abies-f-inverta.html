Pinus abies f. inverta

Pinus abies f. inverta, commonly known as the Inverted White Spruce, is a horticultural form of the white spruce.

It is characterized by its unique, upward-curving needles that give it a distinctive appearance. This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes. The plant retains its needles throughout the winter, providing year-round visual interest.

It thrives in cooler climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
