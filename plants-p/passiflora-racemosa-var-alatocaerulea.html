Passiflora racemosa var. alatocaerulea

Passiflora racemosa var. alatocaerulea, commonly known as the blue-winged passionflower, is a striking tropical vine native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant blue and white flowers, which are adorned with intricate patterns and spurred sepals. The plant produces edible, orange-yellow berries that are rich in nutrients. Its leaves are palmately lobed, with a velvety texture and a strong, aromatic scent.

This species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its visual appeal.
