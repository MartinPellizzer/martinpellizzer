Phaseolus triangulus

Phaseolus triangulus, commonly known as the evergreen bean or scarlet runner bean, is a flowering plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three leaflets arranged in a triangular shape. The plant produces clusters of vibrant red or yellow flowers that are followed by elongated pods containing edible seeds. Native to the Americas, it thrives in warm climates and is cultivated for both its ornamental value and edible beans.

Its growth habit includes a climbing or trailing structure, making it suitable for trellises or support systems.
