Polystachya simoniana

Polystachya simoniana, commonly known as the Simon's spikegrass, is a member of the orchid family (Orchidaceae) and is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces dense clusters of narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically blooms with small, inconspicuous flowers that are often green or brown in color. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological niche, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
