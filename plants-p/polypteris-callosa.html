Polypteris callosa

Polypteris callosa, commonly known as the rough holly fern, is a species of fern native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its distinctive fronds with a rough, scaly texture on the underside. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky areas. Its reproductive structures, including sori, are located on the underside of the fronds.

Polypteris callosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
