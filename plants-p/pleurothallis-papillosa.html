Pleurothallis papillosa

Pleurothallis papillosa, commonly known as the hairy pleurothallis, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and the presence of fine, hair-like structures on its leaves and stems. The plant typically produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink, which bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in shaded, humid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its delicate appearance and specific care requirements, it is a popular choice among orchid enthusiasts.
