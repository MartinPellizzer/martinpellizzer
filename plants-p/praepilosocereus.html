Praepilosocereus

Praepilosocereus, commonly known as the Mexican sun cactus, is a species of cactus native to the deserts of Mexico.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its cylindrical stems covered in fine, white hairs. The plant produces clusters of yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It is adapted to arid environments with minimal rainfall and requires well-draining soil.

Praepilosocereus is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking appearance and resilience.
