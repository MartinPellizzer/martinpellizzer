Phaseolaria praetermissa

Phaseolaria praetermissa, commonly known as the slender phaseolus, is a species of flowering plant in the Fabaceae family.

It is native to South America and is characterized by its climbing habit and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that are followed by elongated, bean-like pods. It is often found in disturbed habitats and can thrive in poor soil conditions.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
