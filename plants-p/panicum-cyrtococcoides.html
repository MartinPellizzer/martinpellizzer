Panicum cyrtococcoides

Panicum cyrtococcoides, commonly known as the dwarf panicgrass, is a small, herbaceous plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense, tufted foliage. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, two-lobed spikelets. It is adapted to survive in harsh environments with low rainfall and poor soil conditions.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and potential use in restoration projects.
