Pleurothallis amaralii

Pleurothallis amaralii, commonly known as the Amazonian orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its small, delicate flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in the dense rainforests. Its flowers are often white or pale yellow, with intricate petal structures that aid in pollination by insects.

Pleurothallis amaralii is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in the ecological diversity of its habitat.
