Passiflora micrantha

Passiflora micrantha, commonly known as the small-flowered passionflower, is a climbing vine native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, intricate flowers that feature a blend of blue, purple, and white petals, along with a crown of filaments. The plant produces small, edible berries that are often used in local cuisines. Its foliage consists of heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture.

Passiflora micrantha is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
