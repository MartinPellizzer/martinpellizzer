Pedilanthus purpusii

Pedilanthus purpusii, commonly known as the golden torch cactus, is a species native to Mexico.

It is a succulent plant characterized by its cylindrical stems and bright yellow flowers. The plant has spiny, ribbed stems that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. Its flowers bloom in late spring and are often visited by pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in xeriscaped gardens.
