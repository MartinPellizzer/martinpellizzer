Pyrus communis subsp. mariana

Pyrus communis subsp.

mariana, commonly known as the Marianna pear, is a subspecies of the common pear native to eastern North America. It is characterized by its small, yellowish-green fruits that are typically harvested in late summer.

The plant produces white flowers in early spring, which are followed by the development of its distinctive fruit. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in riparian areas.

The subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in traditional medicine.
