Psidium galapagaeum

Psidium galapagaeum, commonly known as the Galapagos passionfruit, is a tropical shrub native to the Galápagos Islands.

It produces small, edible fruits with a thick, leathery rind and a fragrant, sweet-tart pulp. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are oval to elliptic in shape. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

The species is notable for its ecological importance to the unique biodiversity of the Galápagos ecosystem.
