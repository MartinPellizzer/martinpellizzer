Pappophorum nigricans

Pappophorum nigricans, commonly known as the blackseed grass, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its dark-colored seeds and slender, erect stems. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often with a slightly hairy texture.

Pappophorum nigricans is often found in dry, open habitats and is adapted to thrive in arid or semi-arid conditions.
