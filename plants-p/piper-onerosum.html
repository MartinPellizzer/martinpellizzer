Piper onerosum

Piper onerosum, commonly known as the expensive pepper or black pepper plant, is a flowering vine native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, greenish-white flowers. The plant produces berries that turn from green to red as they mature, containing the aromatic seeds used as a spice. Its botanical features include aerial roots and a climbing habit, adapting to tropical rainforest environments.

The plant's essential oils and chemical compounds contribute to its medicinal and culinary significance.
