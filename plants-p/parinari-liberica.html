Parinari liberica

Parinari liberica, commonly known as the African cherry or African wild cherry, is a tropical tree native to Africa.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and dense, evergreen foliage. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by bright red, fleshy berries. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and elliptic in shape, often with a glossy surface.

The tree is valued for its timber and is also used in traditional medicine for various ailments.
