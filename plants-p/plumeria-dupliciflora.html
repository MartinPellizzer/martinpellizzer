Plumeria dupliciflora

Plumeria dupliciflora, commonly known as the frangipani, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink and yellow. The plant produces large, waxy leaves and has a woody, branched stem. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The flowers are typically borne in clusters at the ends of the branches.
