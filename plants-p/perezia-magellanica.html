Perezia magellanica

Perezia magellanica, commonly known as the Magellanic perezia, is a flowering plant native to southern South America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and grasslands. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink and bloom in late spring or early summer.

Perezia magellanica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate beauty.
