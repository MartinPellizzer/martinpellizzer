Piptadenia ramosissima

Piptadenia ramosissima, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or pink passionflower, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a deciduous tree or shrub that can grow up to 10 meters tall, characterized by its compound leaves and showy, pinkish-white flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that are edible and often consumed by both humans and animals.

It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance.
