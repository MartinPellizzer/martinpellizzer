Prunus cerasus f. dumosa

Prunus cerasus f. dumosa, commonly known as the dwarf cherry, is a compact, shrub-like form of the sweet cherry.

It features small, rounded leaves and produces small, dark red berries that are typically used for making jams and preserves. The plant is characterized by its dense, bushy growth habit and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for garden use in temperate climates.

This variety is also valued for its hardiness and ability to produce fruit in smaller spaces.
