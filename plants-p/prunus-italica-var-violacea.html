Prunus italica var. violacea

Prunus italica var. violacea, commonly known as the purple-flowered cherry plum, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and clusters of fragrant, violet-pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, edible fruits that are typically used in jams and preserves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its showy blooms and attractive bark, which adds visual interest throughout the year.
