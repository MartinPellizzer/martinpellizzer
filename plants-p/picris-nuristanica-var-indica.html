Picris nuristanica var. indica

Picris nuristanica var. indica, commonly known as the Himalayan groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia and the Himalayas.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in alpine and subalpine environments, often found at elevations between 2,000 and 4,000 meters. The plant features basal rosettes of linear leaves and produces small, yellowish composite flowers arranged in clusters. Its flowering period generally occurs in the summer months, and it is adapted to cold, high-altitude climates with poor soil conditions.

The species is characterized by its slender stems and is often used in traditional medicine in regions where it is found.
