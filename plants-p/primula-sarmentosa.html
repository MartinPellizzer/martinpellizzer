Primula sarmentosa

Primula sarmentosa, commonly known as the creeping primrose, is a flowering plant native to temperate regions of Asia and Europe.

It is characterized by its low-growing habit, with stems that often trail along the ground. The plant produces clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, yellow, and orange. Its leaves are typically oval and covered in a fine, downy coating.

Primula sarmentosa thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.
