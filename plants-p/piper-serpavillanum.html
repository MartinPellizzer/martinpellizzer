Piper serpavillanum

Piper serpavillanum, commonly known as the Serpavillanum pepper, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its climbing habit, with slender stems that often twine around supporting structures. Its leaves are alternate, ovate to elliptical in shape, and have a rough texture.

The fruit is a small, elongated berry that turns from green to yellow when mature, and it contains several tiny seeds.
