Pappobolus macranthus

Pappobolus macranthus, commonly known as the giant pappobolus, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, yellow-orange composite flowers. The plant produces a distinctive type of seed known as a pappus, which aids in wind dispersal. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grassland and rocky habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
