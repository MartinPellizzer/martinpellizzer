Pilosella laggeri

Pilosella laggeri, commonly known as the Lagger's pearly everlet, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its small, white to pale yellow flowers that are clustered in dense, spherical inflorescences. The plant has hairy stems and leaves, which give it its common name. It is native to regions of southern Europe and is often found in meadows and grasslands.

Pilosella laggeri is a perennial herb that typically grows to a height of about 10 to 30 centimeters.
