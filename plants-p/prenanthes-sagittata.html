Prenanthes sagittata

Prenanthes sagittata, commonly known as arrowhead or arrowleaf, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It is characterized by its distinctive arrow-shaped leaves that are opposite on the stem. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters, typically blooming from June to August. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodland floors and stream banks.

Prenanthes sagittata is often found in the eastern United States and is valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators.
