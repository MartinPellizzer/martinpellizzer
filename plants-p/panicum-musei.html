Panicum musei

Panicum musei, commonly known as the museum grass, is a species of grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and produces small, fluffy seed heads that resemble miniature clouds. The plant thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it a hardy choice for garden landscapes. Its ornamental value comes from its delicate appearance and ability to add texture to mixed plantings.

Panicum musei is also valued for its low maintenance requirements and drought tolerance.
