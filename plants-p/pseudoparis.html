Pseudoparis

Pseudoparis, commonly known as the false hellebore, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Araceae.

It is characterized by its striking, often colorful inflorescences that consist of a spadix surrounded by a modified leaf called a spathe. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are usually broad and heart-shaped, with a waxy surface.

Pseudoparis species are native to various regions in the Americas and are valued for their ornamental appeal and ecological importance.
