Prunus sativa proles sanctae-catharinae

Prunus sativa proles sanctae-catharinae, commonly known as the Holy Catherine plum, is a rare and ancient cultivar of Prunus sativa.

It is characterized by its small, dark purple fruits and compact growth habit. The plant is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil. Its botanical features include simple leaves with a serrated margin and a short, sturdy stem.

Due to its historical and horticultural significance, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts of old-world fruit varieties.
