Pentapanax henryi var. wangshanensis

Pentapanax henryi var. wangshanensis, commonly known as the Wangshan pentapanax, is a species native to China, particularly found in the Yunnan and Sichuan provinces.

It is a climbing shrub characterized by its pinnately compound leaves with five to seven leaflets. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in terminal inflorescences. Its fruits are berries that turn red when mature, containing several seeds.

This variant is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique foliage.
