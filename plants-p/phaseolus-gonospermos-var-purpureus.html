Phaseolus gonospermos var. purpureus

Phaseolus gonospermos var. purpureus, commonly known as the purple runner bean, is a variant of the runner bean species native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its vibrant purple pod color and edible, tender beans that are often used in culinary applications. The plant is a herbaceous annual that thrives in warm climates and requires full sun for optimal growth. Its flowers are typically white or pale purple, and it produces pods that can grow up to 15 centimeters in length.

The botanical features of this variety make it a popular choice for both ornamental and agricultural purposes.
