Pongamia madagascariensis

Pongamia madagascariensis, commonly known as the Madagascar pongamia, is a leguminous plant native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and produces small, yellow flowers that develop into pods containing oily seeds. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a hardy species suitable for drought-prone regions. Its seeds are rich in oil, which has potential applications in biodiesel production.

The plant also plays a role in soil stabilization and ecological restoration due to its deep root system.
