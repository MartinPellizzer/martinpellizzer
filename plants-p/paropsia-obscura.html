Paropsia obscura

Paropsia obscura, commonly known as the dark paropsia, is a small, herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are usually a deep green color. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, often clustered in loose inflorescences.

Paropsia obscura is known for its ability to thrive in low-light conditions and its preference for well-drained, acidic soils.
