Primula elatior subsp. leucophylla

Primula elatior subsp.

leucophylla, commonly known as the white-leaved primrose, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow.

The leaves are typically broad and oval, with a distinctive white or pale green coloration on the underside. This subspecies thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in woodland or meadow habitats.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to bloom in early spring.
