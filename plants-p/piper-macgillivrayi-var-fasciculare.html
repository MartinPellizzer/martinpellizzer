Piper macgillivrayi var. fasciculare

Piper macgillivrayi var. fasciculare, commonly known as the MacGillivray's pepper vine, is a climbing shrub native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It features heart-shaped, glossy leaves that are often arranged in clusters, giving it its varietal name "fasciculare," which means "bundled" in Latin. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by small, round berries that turn red when mature. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

Its botanical characteristics make it a unique and interesting species within the Piper genus.
