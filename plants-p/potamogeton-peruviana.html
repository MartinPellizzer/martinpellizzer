Potamogeton peruviana

Potamogeton peruviana, commonly known as the Peruvian pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to South America.

It has long, slender, ribbon-like leaves that are typically green to reddish in color. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually green or yellowish, arranged in clusters. It thrives in freshwater environments such as lakes, ponds, and slow-moving rivers.

Potamogeton peruviana plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
