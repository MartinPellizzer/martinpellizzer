Phlox drummondii var. goldsmithii

Phlox drummondii var. goldsmithii, commonly known as the Goldsmith's phlox, is a cultivar of the common phlox, native to the central United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces showy clusters of fragrant, tubular flowers in shades of pink to red. The plant typically grows to a height of 12 to 18 inches and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its foliage consists of dark green, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged oppositely along the stems.

This variety is valued for its vibrant blooms and is often used in garden landscapes for its ornamental appeal.
