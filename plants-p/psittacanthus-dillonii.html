Psittacanthus dillonii

Psittacanthus dillonii, commonly known as the red-veined staghorn fern, is a tropical epiphytic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, tree-like fronds that resemble antlers, giving it its common name. The plant has a distinctive appearance with bright green leaves that have red veins, and it often grows on tree trunks or rocks in humid environments. It requires high humidity and indirect light to thrive, making it a popular choice for indoor cultivation.

Psittacanthus dillonii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to add a lush, exotic look to gardens and interiors.
