Pimentus saligna

Pimentus saligna, commonly known as the silky gum or silky ironwood, is a flowering plant native to eastern Australia.

It is a tall, evergreen tree that can reach heights of up to 40 meters, characterized by its smooth, grey bark and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by woody pods containing seeds. Pimentus saligna is known for its hard, durable wood and its ability to thrive in coastal and sandy soils.

It plays an important ecological role in providing habitat and food for various native wildlife species.
