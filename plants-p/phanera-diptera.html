Phanera diptera

Phanera diptera, commonly known as the winged bean, is a tropical leguminous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and distinctive winged pods that enclose the edible seeds. The plant belongs to the Fabaceae family and is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, enhancing soil fertility. Its flowers are typically small and clustered, producing a sweet scent that attracts pollinators.

The plant is cultivated for its seeds, which are rich in protein and nutrients, making it an important crop in many regions.
