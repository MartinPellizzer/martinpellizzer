Pyrus koshiensis

Pyrus koshiensis, commonly known as the Koshi pear, is a species of flowering plant in the family Rosaceae.

It is native to the eastern Himalayas, specifically found in Nepal and India. The plant is characterized by its deciduous nature, with simple, alternate leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape. Its flowers are white and clustered in corymbs, typically blooming in late spring.

The fruit is a small, sweet pear-like apple that ripens in autumn, making it a valuable species for both ornamental and potential agricultural use.
