Persea rugosa

Persea rugosa, commonly known as the Mexican persimmon, is a small evergreen tree native to Mexico and Central America.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant, white flowers that bloom in the spring. The fruit is typically orange to reddish in color, with a thick, leathery skin and a sweet, sometimes astringent pulp. The plant thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial shade.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and edible fruit, which is often used in traditional cuisine.
