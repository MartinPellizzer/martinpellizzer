Pleurothallis barbosana

Pleurothallis barbosana, commonly known as the Barboza's orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, elongated stems and small, intricate flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are usually white or pale green, with a distinctive lip that aids in pollination.

Pleurothallis barbosana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
