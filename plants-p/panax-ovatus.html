Panax ovatus

Panax ovatus, commonly known as the Japanese ginseng, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Araliaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive fleshy, yellowish roots. The plant typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring trifoliate leaves and small greenish-white flowers. Its botanical features include a rhizomatous root system and berries that mature to a dark red color.

It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is valued for its medicinal properties in traditional medicine.
