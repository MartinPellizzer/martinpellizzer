Prunus jamasakura f. pubescens

Prunus jamasakura f. pubescens, commonly known as the Japanese cherry blossom variant with pubescent leaves, is a cultivar of the Prunus jamasakura species.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy texture on the leaves, which distinguishes it from other cherry blossom varieties. The plant produces delicate pink flowers in early spring, adding ornamental value to gardens and natural landscapes. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This cultivar is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal and is a symbol of seasonal beauty in Japanese horticulture.
