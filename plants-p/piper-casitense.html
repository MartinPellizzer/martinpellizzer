Piper casitense

Piper casitense, commonly known as the Casitense pepper, is a flowering plant native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many species of pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its climbing habit, with long, slender stems that often trail along the ground or climb supports. Its small, greenish-white flowers are followed by small, round berries that turn red when mature.

The plant is valued for its aromatic leaves and fruits, which are used in traditional cuisine and herbal remedies.
