Pachycereus schottii f. monstrosus

Pachycereus schottii f. monstrosus, commonly known as the monstrous prickly pear, is a unique variant of the prickly pear cactus characterized by its swollen, bulbous stems.

This plant is native to Mexico and is known for its striking, often asymmetrical growth pattern. The stems are covered in spines and produce yellow flowers in the spring. It is a slow-growing plant that thrives in arid environments with minimal water.

The f. monstrosus form is particularly prized for its unusual and decorative appearance in horticulture.
