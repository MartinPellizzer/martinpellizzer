Praxelis porophylloides

Praxelis porophylloides, commonly known as the golden crownbeard, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking yellow flowers arranged in a dense, rounded inflorescence. The plant typically grows in grasslands and rocky areas, thriving in full sun and well-drained soils. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, while its stems are erect and often branched.

Praxelis porophylloides is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
