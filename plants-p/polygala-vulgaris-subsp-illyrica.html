Polygala vulgaris subsp. illyrica

Polygala vulgaris subsp.

illyrica, commonly known as the Illyrian milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the Balkans. It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, clustered flowers.

The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to Mediterranean climates. Its flowers are usually pale green to yellowish in color, and it produces small, bead-like fruits.
