Primula reinii var. okamotoi

Primula reinii var. okamotoi, commonly known as the Okamoto's primrose, is a species native to Japan.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 15-20 centimeters. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, which bloom in early spring. Its foliage consists of broad, heart-shaped leaves that are usually dark green and velvety in texture.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
