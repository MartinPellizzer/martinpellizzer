Pyrus renuncupata

Pyrus renuncupata, commonly known as the golden apple tree, is a species of flowering plant in the family Rosaceae.

It is native to eastern Asia, particularly China, and is characterized by its ornamental value due to its showy yellow flowers. The plant produces small, edible fruits that are typically harvested in late summer. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a smooth texture with a slightly serrated margin.

Pyrus renuncupata is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal and is relatively easy to care for in suitable climates.
