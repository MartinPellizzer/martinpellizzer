Pyrus obovoidea

Pyrus obovoidea, commonly known as the narrow-ovate pear, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It is characterized by its elongated, obovate leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by edible, sweet fruits that ripen in late summer. Its botanical features make it a valuable species for both ornamental and horticultural purposes.

The plant is also noted for its adaptability to various soil types and climates.
