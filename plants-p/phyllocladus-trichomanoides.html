Phyllocladus trichomanoides

Phyllocladus trichomanoides, commonly known as the silver pine, is a unique coniferous plant native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its distinctive silver-blue foliage, which is composed of scale-like leaves arranged in dense, overlapping layers. The plant has a slender, often twisted trunk and exhibits a slow growth rate, making it a long-lived species. Its cones are small and woody, typically containing two to three seeds, and it plays an important ecological role in its native habitat.

The botanical features of P. trichomanoides make it an interesting subject for both horticultural and scientific study.
