Primula spathulata

Primula spathulata, commonly known as the broad-leaved primrose, is a perennial flowering plant native to eastern Asia.

It features oval, broad leaves that are typically hairy and dark green in color. The plant produces clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of yellow, white, or pink, which bloom in early spring. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in woodland areas or shaded gardens.

Primula spathulata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in horticulture.
