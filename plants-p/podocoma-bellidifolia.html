Podocoma bellidifolia

Podocoma bellidifolia, commonly known as the Cape mountain heath, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its low-growing, evergreen shrub form. The plant features small, leathery leaves and produces clusters of bell-shaped, white or pink flowers. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is adapted to coastal and mountainous regions.

Podocoma bellidifolia is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
