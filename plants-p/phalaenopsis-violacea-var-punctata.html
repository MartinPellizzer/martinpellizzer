Phalaenopsis violacea var. punctata

Phalaenopsis violacea var. punctata, commonly known as the spotted orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its long, pendant inflorescences that bear clusters of fragrant, waxy flowers in shades of violet and white. The leaves are broad, dark green, and often have a velvety texture. This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is commonly cultivated as a houseplant.

It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires moderate humidity and well-draining potting mix.
