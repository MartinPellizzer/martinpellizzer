Primula vinosa

Primula vinosa, commonly known as the purple primrose, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its showy, violet-colored flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream sides. Its leaves are oval-shaped with a slightly hairy surface, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds.

Primula vinosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
