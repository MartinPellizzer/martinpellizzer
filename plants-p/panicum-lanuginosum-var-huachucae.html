Panicum lanuginosum var. huachucae

Panicum lanuginosum var. huachucae, commonly known as the Huachuca panicgrass, is a perennial grass native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy leaves and compact, tufted growth habit. The plant typically thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-dispersed seeds.

This variety is often found in rocky slopes and desert scrub habitats, showcasing its resilience to harsh conditions.
