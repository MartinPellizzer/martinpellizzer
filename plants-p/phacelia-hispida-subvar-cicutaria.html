Phacelia hispida subvar. cicutaria

Phacelia hispida subvar. cicutaria, commonly known as the hairy phacelia, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas.

Its botanical features make it a valuable plant for pollinators and a useful species in ecological restoration projects.
