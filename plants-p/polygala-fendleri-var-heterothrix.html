Polygala fendleri var. heterothrix

Polygala fendleri var. heterothrix, commonly known as the desert milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has compound leaves with thread-like leaflets and produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in sandy or rocky soils.

Its botanical features make it well-adapted to drought conditions and extreme temperatures.
