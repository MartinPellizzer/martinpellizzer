Parodia argerichiana

Parodia argerichiana, commonly known as the Argentiniar Rose, is a species of cactus native to Argentina.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its dense, spiny stems that form a rounded, globular shape. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers during the summer months, which are typically short-lived but attract pollinators like bees and moths. Its leaves are modified into spines, and it has a shallow root system adapted to arid environments.

Parodia argerichiana is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its hardy nature and aesthetic appeal.
