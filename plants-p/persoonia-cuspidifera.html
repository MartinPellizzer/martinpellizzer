Persoonia cuspidifera

Persoonia cuspidifera, commonly known as the narrow-leaved geebung, is a shrub native to the coastal regions of southern and western Australia.

It features narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in spikes. The plant is adapted to sandy soils and is often found in heathlands and coastal dunes. It is a member of the Proteaceae family, which is known for its diverse and often showy floral structures.

Persoonia cuspidifera is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
