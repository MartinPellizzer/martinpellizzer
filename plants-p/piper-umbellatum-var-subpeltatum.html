Piper umbellatum var. subpeltatum

Piper umbellatum var. subpeltatum, commonly known as the lesser betel plant, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves with serrated margins. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in umbels, giving it its scientific name. Its fruits are berries that mature to a dark purple color, containing a single seed.

The leaves are often used in traditional medicine and as a mild stimulant.
