Phyllocalyx regelianus

Phyllocalyx regelianus, commonly known as the Regelian waxplant, is a striking member of the Malvaceae family native to South America.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are often covered in a fine, powdery coating, which helps reduce water loss in its native arid environment. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically pink or purple, blooming in summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

Due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements, it is appreciated by both botanists and gardeners alike.
