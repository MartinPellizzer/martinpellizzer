Persicaria regeliana

Persicaria regeliana, commonly known as the Regel's knotweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features upright, slender stems that are often reddish in color, and its leaves are alternate, ovate to lanceolate, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, clustered flowers in late summer, typically in shades of pink or white, which are borne on slender pedicels. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments.

Its botanical characteristics make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural study.
