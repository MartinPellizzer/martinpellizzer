Patagonium polygaloides

Patagonium polygaloides, commonly known as the Patagonian milkweed, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of southern South America.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its slender stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces a milky sap, which is a distinguishing feature of the genus. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Patagonium polygaloides plays a role in local ecosystems by providing nectar for pollinators and habitat for small invertebrates.
