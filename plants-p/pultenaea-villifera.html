Pultenaea villifera

Pultenaea villifera, commonly known as the narrow-leaved pultenaea, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its distinctive pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is found in regions such as South Australia and Western Australia. Its leaves are narrow and often have a silvery underside, while its small, pink or red flowers appear in spring.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
