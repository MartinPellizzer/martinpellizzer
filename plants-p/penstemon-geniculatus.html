Penstemon geniculatus

Penstemon geniculatus, commonly known as the zigzag penstemon, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that often curve in a zigzag pattern, giving it its common name. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Penstemon geniculatus is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
