Persicaria flaccida

Persicaria flaccida, commonly known as willow leaf knotweed, is a flowering plant in the Polygonaceae family.

It is native to Asia and is characterized by its slender, willow-like leaves and small, greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist environments such as wet meadows and stream banks. It has a short stem and produces capsules that contain numerous small seeds.

Persicaria flaccida is often found in temperate regions and is known for its adaptability to wet conditions.
