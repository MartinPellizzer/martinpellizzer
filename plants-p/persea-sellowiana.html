Persea sellowiana

Persea sellowiana, commonly known as the Brazilian avocado or the Brazilian persimmon, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and fragrant, white flowers. The tree produces small, round fruits that are typically green when unripe and turn yellow or orange when mature. The fruit has a creamy, aromatic pulp and is valued for its nutritional content and culinary uses.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for both its fruit and ornamental value.
