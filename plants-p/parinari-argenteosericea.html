Parinari argenteosericea

Parinari argenteosericea, commonly known as silver-haired parinari, is a small to medium-sized tree native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, silver-gray bark and lance-shaped leaves with a silvery underside. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in terminal panicles, followed by elliptical, yellowish fruits. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in forest ecosystems.
