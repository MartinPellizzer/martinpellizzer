Pilea klossii

Pilea klossii, commonly known as the Chinese money plant, is a species of flowering plant native to China.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that resemble small, rounded coins. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers that appear in clusters during the growing season. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Pilea klossii is popular as a houseplant due to its aesthetic appeal and relatively low maintenance requirements.
