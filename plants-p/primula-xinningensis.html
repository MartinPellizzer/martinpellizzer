Primula xinningensis

Primula xinningensis, commonly known as the Xinning primrose, is a flowering plant native to China.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that typically bloom in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant has heart-shaped or oval leaves that are usually covered in a fine layer of hair. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in mountainous regions.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
