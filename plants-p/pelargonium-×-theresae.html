Pelargonium × theresae

Pelargonium × theresae, commonly known as the hybrid geranium, is a flowering plant in the family Geraniaceae.

It is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Pelargonium hortorum and Pelargonium × domesticum. The plant is characterized by its showy, clustered flowers that come in a variety of colors, including red, pink, and white. Its foliage is typically dark green and slightly serrated, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Pelargonium × theresae is often cultivated in gardens and as a potted plant due to its adaptability and aesthetic value.
