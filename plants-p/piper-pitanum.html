Piper pitanum

Piper pitanum, commonly known as the Mexican pepper plant, is a member of the Piperaceae family.

It is a climbing vine native to Central America and parts of South America. The plant produces small, green berries that turn red when mature, and these berries contain the aromatic compound piperine, which gives it its distinctive flavor. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with high humidity and well-drained soil.

The plant is often cultivated for its culinary and medicinal uses.
