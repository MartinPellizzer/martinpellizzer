Plectranthus hararensis

Plectranthus hararensis, commonly known as the Harare plectranthus, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It produces small, tubular flowers that are typically purple or violet in color.

Plectranthus hararensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and its ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
