Pultenaea filifolia

Pultenaea filifolia, commonly known as the silky pultenaea, is a shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often hair-covered stems and small, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of delicate, yellowish-green flowers during the spring and summer months. Its fruit is a pod that splits open to release small, hairy seeds.

Pultenaea filifolia is often found in coastal heathlands and sandy soils, thriving in well-drained conditions.
