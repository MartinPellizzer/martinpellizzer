Puya iltisiana

Puya iltisiana, commonly known as the Andean torch, is a striking plant native to the high altitudes of the Andes in South America.

It is a member of the bromeliad family and is characterized by its tall, spire-like inflorescence that bears numerous vibrant red flowers. The plant has long, strap-like leaves that form a rosette, creating a dense structure that helps retain moisture. It thrives in cool, humid environments and is often found growing in rocky or alpine regions.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists alike.
