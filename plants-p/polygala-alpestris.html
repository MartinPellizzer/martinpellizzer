Polygala alpestris

Polygala alpestris, commonly known as the alpine milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its slender stems and small, pink or white flowers arranged in spiny, cylindrical clusters. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in high-altitude environments. It has small, lance-shaped leaves and produces pods that contain several seeds.

Polygala alpestris is often found in alpine meadows and is adapted to cold, harsh climates.
