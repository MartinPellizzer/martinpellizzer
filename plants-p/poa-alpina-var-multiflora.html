Poa alpina var. multiflora

Poa alpina var. multiflora, commonly known as the alpine bluegrass, is a flowering plant native to mountainous regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is a perennial grass characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, dark green leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny blue to purple flowers. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments.

Poa alpina var. multiflora plays an important ecological role in alpine ecosystems by stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.
