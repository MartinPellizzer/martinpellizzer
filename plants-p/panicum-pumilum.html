Panicum pumilum

Panicum pumilum, commonly known as little panicgrass, is a low-growing grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It typically forms dense mats of fine, feather-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant is characterized by its tufted growth habit and is often found in sandy or disturbed soils. It thrives in full sun and is drought-tolerant, making it a common sight in open landscapes.

Botanically, it is part of the Poaceae family and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
