Purkayasthaea pseudomicropora

Purkayasthaea pseudomicropora, commonly known as the narrow-leaved piper, is a tropical shrub native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the family Piperaceae, which includes many species of flowering plants known for their aromatic properties. The plant is characterized by its small, ovate leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stem. Its flowers are unisexual, with separate male and female flowers on different inflorescences, a feature common in the Piperaceae family.

The fruit is a small, three-lobed berry that matures to a dark purple color, often containing a single seed.
