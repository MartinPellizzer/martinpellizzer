Penstemon attenuatus subsp. hyacinthinus

Penstemon attenuatus subsp.

hyacinthinus, commonly known as the hyacinth penstemon, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to purple.

The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun, making it well-suited for xeriscaping. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often with a slight hairy texture.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance in gardens and natural landscapes.
