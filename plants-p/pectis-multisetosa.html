Pectis multisetosa

Pectis multisetosa, commonly known as the bristly pectis, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and cylindrical stems. The plant features dense, bristly hairs along its stem and leaves, which give it its common name. Its yellow flowers are typically arranged in clusters, and it thrives in arid or semi-arid environments.

Pectis multisetosa is often found in rocky soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.
