Ptisana senterreana

Ptisana senterreana, commonly known as the Sénégal orchid, is a rare and endemic orchid species native to the tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and yellow. The plant has narrow, linear leaves and produces small, clustered inflorescences. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often growing on tree trunks or in dense undergrowth.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, Ptisana senterreana is considered a vulnerable species.
