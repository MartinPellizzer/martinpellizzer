Plantago virginica

Plantago virginica, commonly known as Virginia plantain, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It features broad, oval leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in a spike-like inflorescence, which are wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Virginia plantain is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally for its healing benefits.
