Phreatia rupestris

Phreatia rupestris, commonly known as the rock orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces striking, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow with a distinctive greenish or reddish lip. It thrives in humid, shaded environments, often growing on rocky substrates or in forest undergrowth.

Phreatia rupestris is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
