Plumeria purpurea

Plumeria purpurea, commonly known as the purple frangipani, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant produces large, waxy leaves and has a branching, sometimes thorny stem. It thrives in warm, sunny climates and is often used in ornamental horticulture.

The flowers are typically short-lived, lasting only a day or two before wilting.
