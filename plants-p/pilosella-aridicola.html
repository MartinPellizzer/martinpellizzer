Pilosella aridicola

Pilosella aridicola, commonly known as the desert hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant has a low, spreading growth habit with hairy stems and leaves adapted to reduce water loss in harsh environments. Its flowers are typically yellow or white, and it produces small, dry fruits known as achenes.

Pilosella aridicola is often found in sandy or rocky soils, thriving in areas with minimal rainfall and high temperatures.
