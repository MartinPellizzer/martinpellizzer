Pueraria neocaledonica

Pueraria neocaledonica, commonly known as the Kauai pueraria, is a climbing legume native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It features large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces showy, purple or white flowers. The plant is characterized by its extensive root system and is adapted to thrive in moist, tropical environments. It is also known for its rapid growth and ability to form dense thickets.

Due to its invasive nature, it is considered a noxious weed in some regions.
