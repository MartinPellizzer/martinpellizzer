Phleum exaratum subsp. breviglume

Phleum exaratum subsp.

breviglume, commonly known as the slender brome, is a grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere. It is characterized by its slender, erect growth habit and narrow, bluish-green leaves.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny florets, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in a variety of habitats, including meadows, pastures, and disturbed soils.

This subspecies is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its adaptability and ability to stabilize soil.
