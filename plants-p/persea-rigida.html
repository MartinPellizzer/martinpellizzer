Persea rigida

Persea rigida, commonly known as the Mexican persimmon, is a small evergreen tree native to Mexico and Central America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant white flowers. The tree produces small, orange-red fruits that are edible when fully ripe. Its fruit is valued for its sweet, honey-like flavor and is often used in local cuisines.

The plant is also notable for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and climates.
