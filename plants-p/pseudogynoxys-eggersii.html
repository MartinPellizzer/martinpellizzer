Pseudogynoxys eggersii

Pseudogynoxys eggersii, commonly known as the Mexican false sunflower, is a member of the Asteraceae family.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America. The plant features showy yellow ray flowers surrounded by darker tubular florets, typical of the sunflower family. It has alternate, ovate leaves with hairy surfaces and grows in open, sunny habitats.

Pseudogynoxys eggersii is known for its ecological value, providing nectar for pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
