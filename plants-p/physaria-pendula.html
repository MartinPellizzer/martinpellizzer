Physaria pendula

Physaria pendula, commonly known as the trailing bladderpod, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers. The plant features hairy, branched stems that often trail along the ground, giving it a creeping growth habit. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and covered in fine pubescence.

The fruit is a dry, segmented pod that opens to release the seeds.
