Passiflora creuci-caetanoae

Passiflora creuci-caetanoae, commonly known as the Cetanoae passionflower, is a species native to the Andean region of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, complex flowers that feature five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments surrounding the reproductive structures. The plant produces blue or purple berries that are edible and contain numerous seeds. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in shaded, forested areas.

Due to its rarity and unique morphology, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists alike.
