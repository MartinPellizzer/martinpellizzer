Poa capillarifolia

Poa capillarifolia, commonly known as the slender hairgrass, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its fine, grass-like leaves and small, delicate blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine environments, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Poa capillarifolia is often found in rocky soils and is adapted to withstand harsh weather conditions.

Its unique appearance and ecological significance make it an important species in its native habitats.
