Pleurothallis pilifera

Pleurothallis pilifera, commonly known as the hair-leaf orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its delicate, hairy leaves that give it its common name. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are small and usually white or pale green, with a subtle fragrance.

Pleurothallis pilifera is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
