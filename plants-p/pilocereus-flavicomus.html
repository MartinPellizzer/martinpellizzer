Pilocereus flavicomus

Pilocereus flavicomus, commonly known as the yellow-tipped night bloomer, is a species of cactus native to South America.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems covered in white to pale green spines. The plant produces fragrant, yellow flowers that open at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. It thrives in arid environments with well-draining soil and requires minimal water.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for xeriscaping and succulent collections.
