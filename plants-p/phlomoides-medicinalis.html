Phlomoides medicinalis

Phlomoides medicinalis, commonly known as the medicinal phlomoides, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Lamiaceae, which includes many aromatic and medicinally significant species. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface, and it produces small, clustered flowers in shades of purple or blue. It is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and antispasmodic properties.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it suitable for cultivation in arid and semi-arid regions.
