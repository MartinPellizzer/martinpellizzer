Phytolacca clavigera

Phytolacca clavigera, commonly known as Chinese pokeweed, is a herbaceous plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the genus Phytolacca in the family Phytolaccaceae. The plant features tall, slender stems with alternate, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. Its flowers are small, greenish-white, and clustered in umbels, followed by red, fleshy berries that are toxic to humans and animals.

The plant is often found in disturbed soils and is used in traditional medicine, though caution is necessary due to its poisonous properties.
