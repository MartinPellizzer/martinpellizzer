Polylepis quadrijuga

Polylepis quadrijuga, commonly known as the Andean wax tree, is a deciduous tree native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes in South America.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that help it survive in cold, dry environments. The tree can grow up to 20 meters tall and has a slow growth rate, making it a long-lived species. Its bark is thick and fissured, providing protection against harsh weather conditions.

Polylepis quadrijuga plays an important ecological role in its habitat, supporting diverse wildlife and contributing to soil stabilization.
