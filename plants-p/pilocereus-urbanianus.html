Pilocereus urbanianus

Pilocereus urbanianus, commonly known as the Moon Cactus, is a species of cactus native to South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that bloom at night, typically in shades of white, pink, or red. The plant has a distinct appearance with a swollen, rounded body and a short, thick stem. Its foliage consists of spiny, segmented stems that help it store water.

Pilocereus urbanianus is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its unique appearance and nocturnal blooming habit.
