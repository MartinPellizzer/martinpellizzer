Piper micronesiacum

Piper micronesiacum, commonly known as the Caroline pepper plant, is a flowering vine native to the Caroline Islands in the Pacific Ocean.

It belongs to the Piper genus, which includes many species of pepper plants. The plant produces small, green berries that mature to a bright red color, containing aromatic seeds used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are often used in local cuisines for their pungent flavor.

The botanical characteristics of Piper micronesiacum make it an important species in both ecological and cultural contexts.
