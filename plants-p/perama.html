Perama

Perama, commonly known as the white sandplant, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of South Africa.

It belongs to the Aizoaceae family, commonly referred to as the ice plant family, and is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring, adding a delicate touch to its landscape. Perama thrives in sandy, well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and coastal environments.

It is often used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements and ornamental value.
