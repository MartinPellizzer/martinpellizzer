Populus fangiana

Populus fangiana, commonly known as the Fangiana poplar, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It belongs to the Salicaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and catkin flowers. The tree typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in temperate climates. Its bark is smooth and grayish-brown, often peeling in thin layers.

Populus fangiana is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been studied for its potential in ecological restoration projects.
