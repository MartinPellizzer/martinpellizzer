Piptanthus leiocarpus var. sericopetalus

Piptanthus leiocarpus var. sericopetalus, commonly known as the silky petal piptanthus, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its slender, branching structure and small, ovate leaves that are often covered in a fine, silky pubescence. The plant produces showy, pink to reddish flowers in late spring, which are arranged in racemose inflorescences. Its flowers are notable for their delicate, velvety petals, contributing to its ornamental value.

The species is adapted to temperate climates and is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal in gardens.
