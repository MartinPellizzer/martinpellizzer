Phragmites communis var. zeylanicus

Phragmites communis var. zeylanicus, commonly known as the Zeylanicus reed, is a variant of the common reed that thrives in wetland environments.

It is characterized by its tall, dense growth and hollow stems, which are often used for thatching or crafting. This plant is native to parts of Asia, including India and Sri Lanka, and is adapted to brackish and freshwater marshes. It plays a crucial role in stabilizing shorelines and providing habitat for various aquatic and avian species.

Its rhizomatous growth allows it to spread rapidly, making it both ecologically significant and potentially invasive in non-native regions.
