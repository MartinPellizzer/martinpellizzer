Polygala procumbens var. angustifolia

Polygala procumbens var. angustifolia, commonly known as narrow-leaf milkvetch, is a low-growing perennial plant native to parts of North America.

It features slender, creeping stems that often root at the nodes, forming dense mats. The leaves are typically narrow and linear, with a slight pubescence on the underside. Its small, yellowish flowers are clustered in racemes and bloom in early summer.

This plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in open, disturbed areas.
