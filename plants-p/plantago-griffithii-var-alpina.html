Plantago griffithii var. alpina

Plantago griffithii var. alpina, commonly known as the alpine plantain, is a perennial herb native to high-altitude regions of the Himalayas.

It features rosettes of broad, ovate leaves with a hairy surface, adapted to withstand cold and arid conditions. The plant produces small, greenish flowers in dense spikes, which are typically wind-pollinated. Its fibrous root system helps stabilize soil in rocky and mountainous environments.

This species is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional herbal remedies.
