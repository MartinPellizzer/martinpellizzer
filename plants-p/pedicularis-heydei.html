Pedicularis heydei

Pedicularis heydei, commonly known as the "Ladyslipper Orchid," is a striking member of the Orobanchaceae family.

It is characterized by its unique, lip-like flower that resembles a slipper, often in shades of pink or red. The plant is hemiparasitic, deriving some nutrients from its host plants while also photosynthesizing. Native to the mountainous regions of Asia, it thrives in cool, moist environments.

Its ornamental value has made it a popular choice in botanical gardens and conservation efforts.
