Polanisia semitetrandra

Polanisia semitetrandra, commonly known as the four-lobed bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its bell-shaped, violet-blue flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface, and its stems are often covered in fine pubescence. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Polanisia semitetrandra is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
