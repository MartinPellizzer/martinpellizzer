Piper asterotrichum

Piper asterotrichum, commonly known as the star-haired pepper vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species. The plant is characterized by its slender, twining stems and heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive star-like arrangement of hairs. Its small, greenish-white flowers are followed by bright red, fleshy berries that are edible.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has some traditional medicinal uses in its native regions.
