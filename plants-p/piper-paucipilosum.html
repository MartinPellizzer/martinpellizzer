Piper paucipilosum

Piper paucipilosum, commonly known as the slender pipevine, is a climbing shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Piperaceae, which includes many species of flowering plants known for their aromatic properties. The plant is characterized by its small, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of tiny, greenish-white flowers. It produces small, berry-like fruits that are typically green when immature and turn red when ripe.

Piper paucipilosum is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along riverbanks.
