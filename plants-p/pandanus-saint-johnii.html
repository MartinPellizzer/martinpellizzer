Pandanus saint-johnii

Pandanus saint-johnii, commonly known as the Saint John's pandanus, is a tropical plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, fan-shaped leaves that are often used in traditional crafts. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically yellow or green in color. Its fruit, known as a pandanus or hala fruit, is edible and has a unique, fibrous texture.

This species thrives in coastal areas and is often found growing near saltwater environments.
