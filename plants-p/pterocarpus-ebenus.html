Pterocarpus ebenus

Pterocarpus ebenus, commonly known as the African ebony, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its dark, dense wood that is highly valued for its durability and aesthetic appeal. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers followed by elongated, woody pods containing flat, seed-like structures. Its leaves are typically bipinnate, with numerous small leaflets, and it thrives in well-drained soils in humid climates.

Pterocarpus ebenus is also notable for its role in traditional medicine and its ecological importance in forest ecosystems.
