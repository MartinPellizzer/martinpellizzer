Phleum bulbosum

Phleum bulbosum, commonly known as bulbous oat, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and dense, fibrous root system that helps stabilize soil. The plant produces spikelets that are often hairy and contain small, oval-shaped florets. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and agricultural research.
