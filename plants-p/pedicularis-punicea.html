Pedicularis punicea

Pedicularis punicea, commonly known as the scarlet lousewort, is a flowering plant native to the alpine regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its striking red or orange flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high elevations. Its leaves are basal and often have a hairy texture, while its stems are usually unbranched.
