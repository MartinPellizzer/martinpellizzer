Penstemon barbatus

Penstemon barbatus, commonly known as the beardtongue, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and showy, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and thrives in full sun. It is a perennial species that produces seed capsules containing numerous small, dark seeds.

Penstemon barbatus is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
