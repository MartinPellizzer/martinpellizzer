Phyllanthus liebmannianus subsp. liebmannianus

Phyllanthus liebmannianus subsp.

liebmannianus, commonly known as the African spurge, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa. It is a shrub or small tree characterized by its dense, spiny foliage and yellow flowers that bloom in clusters.

The plant produces small, round fruits that contain seeds covered in a sticky, resinous substance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in savannas and woodland areas.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal uses, it is sometimes cultivated in horticulture.
