Psidium jollyanum

Psidium jollyanum, commonly known as the Jollyanum guava, is a small evergreen tree native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a glossy surface. The plant produces small, white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by edible, fragrant fruits similar to guavas. Its fruit is typically yellow or red when ripe and is valued for its sweet, aromatic flavor.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of edible fruit in home gardens.
