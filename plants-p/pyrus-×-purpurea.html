Pyrus × purpurea

Pyrus × purpurea, commonly known as the purpleleaf pear, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Pyrus communis (European pear) and Pyrus pyraster (bitter pear).

It is characterized by its distinctive purple-tinged leaves, which provide ornamental value in gardens. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in spring, followed by edible pears that are typically smaller and more acidic than those of its parent species. It thrives in temperate climates and prefers well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental features and fruit-bearing capability, it is cultivated for both aesthetic and culinary purposes.
