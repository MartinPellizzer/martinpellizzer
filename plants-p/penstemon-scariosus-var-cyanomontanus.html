Penstemon scariosus var. cyanomontanus

Penstemon scariosus var. cyanomontanus, commonly known as the blue mountain beardtongue, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, tubular blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has hairy, gray-green leaves that are lance-shaped and often covered in fine pubescence. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its nectar-rich flowers attract a variety of pollinators, including bees and butterflies.
