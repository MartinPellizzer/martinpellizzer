Pseudosasa spiculosa

Pseudosasa spiculosa, commonly known as the spiny bamboo, is a species of grass-like plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its stiff, spiny leaves and clumping growth habit. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine, and it has a distinctive appearance due to its sharp, needle-like foliage.

Pseudosasa spiculosa is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological role in stabilizing soil in wetland areas.
