Piper angustilimbum

Piper angustilimbum, commonly known as narrow-leaved black pepper, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes various species of pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its slender, climbing vines and small, ovate leaves with a slightly serrated margin. Its flowers are unisexual, with separate male and female flowers on the same plant, and it produces small, dark berries as fruit.

The plant is valued for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
