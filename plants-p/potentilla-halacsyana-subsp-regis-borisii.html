Potentilla halacsyana subsp. regis-borisii

Potentilla halacsyana subsp.

regis-borisii, commonly known as the Royal Boris's cinquefoil, is a perennial shrub native to the Balkan Peninsula. It typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and is characterized by its narrow, upright growth habit.

The plant produces clusters of small, yellow flowers in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate, serrated, and covered in a fine layer of white hairs.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and attractive foliage.
