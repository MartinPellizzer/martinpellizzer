Potentilla argentea f. cinerascens

Potentilla argentea f. cinerascens, commonly known as the cinerascens form of silver potentilla, is a flowering plant characterized by its silvery, fuzzy foliage.

This plant belongs to the Rosaceae family and is native to regions of Europe and western Asia. Its leaves are typically narrow and covered in fine, white hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The flowers are small, yellowish, and clustered in heads, often appearing in late spring to early summer.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes.
