Primula macrosiphonia

Primula macrosiphonia, commonly known as the large-lipped primrose, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of East Asia.

It features upright stems that bear lance-shaped, dark green leaves with a hairy texture. The plant produces clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, typically blooming in early spring. Its flowers have a distinctive, elongated lower lip that aids in pollination by insects.

Primula macrosiphonia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
