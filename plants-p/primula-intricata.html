Primula intricata

Primula intricata, commonly known as the intricate primrose, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, bell-shaped flowers that come in shades of white, yellow, and pink. The plant typically grows in rocky or well-drained soils and thrives in cool, moist environments. Its foliage consists of broad, ovate leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white hair.

Primula intricata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic beauty.
