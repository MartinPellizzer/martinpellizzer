Podocarpus macrophyllus var. luteovariegatus

Podocarpus macrophyllus var. luteovariegatus, commonly known as the golden variegated Chinese pine, is a slow-growing evergreen conifer native to southern China.

It features needle-like leaves that are typically yellow-green with white or cream variegation, creating a striking visual effect. The plant produces small, inconspicuous cones and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes. It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for temperate climates.

Due to its unique foliage, it is a popular choice for bonsai and as a statement plant in curated collections.
