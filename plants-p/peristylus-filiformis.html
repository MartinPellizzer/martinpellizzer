Peristylus filiformis

Peristylus filiformis, commonly known as the slender peristyle, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features slender, thread-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a delicate appearance. It produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Peristylus filiformis is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and easy care requirements.
