Peperomia samoensis var. longifolia

Peperomia samoensis var. longifolia, commonly known as the Long-leaf Peperomia, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to Samoa.

It features upright stems covered in dense, waxy, ovate leaves that are typically dark green with a reddish or purple underside. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters, followed by round, red berries that are edible but not particularly flavorful. It thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Its unique foliage and low maintenance requirements make it a desirable addition to both indoor and shaded outdoor spaces.
