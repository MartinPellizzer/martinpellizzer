Polygala sibirica lusus ussuriensis

Polygala sibirica lusus ussuriensis, commonly known as the Ussuri milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the temperate regions of East Asia.

It belongs to the Polygalaceae family and is characterized by its slender stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in forested or grassy habitats. Its leaves are alternate and pinnately compound, while its flowers are often pale pink or white.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
