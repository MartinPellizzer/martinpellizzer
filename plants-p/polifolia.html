Polifolia

Polifolia, commonly known as creeping thyme, is a low-growing herbaceous plant belonging to the Lamiaceae family.

It features small, fragrant leaves that are often used in culinary and medicinal applications. The plant typically has opposite, oval-shaped leaves and produces tiny, pale blue or purple flowers in late spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a ground cover.

Polifolia is also valued for its aromatic properties and its ability to attract pollinators.
