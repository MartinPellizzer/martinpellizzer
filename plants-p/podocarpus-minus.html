Podocarpus minus

Podocarpus minus, commonly known as the dwarf podocarp, is a small evergreen tree or shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Podocarpaceae family and is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and small, cone-like fruits. The plant has scale-like leaves that are tightly arranged along the stems, giving it a compact appearance. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical forests.

Podocarpus minus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in horticulture for its aesthetic qualities.
