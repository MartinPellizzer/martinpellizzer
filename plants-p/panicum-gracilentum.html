Panicum gracilentum

Panicum gracilentum, commonly known as silky panicgrass, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its slender, hair-like leaves and delicate, feathery flower spikes. The plant typically grows in wet or moist soils, often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. Its ornamental value comes from its light green foliage and airy, silver-tan inflorescences that add a soft texture to landscapes.

Panicum gracilentum is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ecological benefits and ability to stabilize soil in aquatic environments.
