Prunus serrulata f. ohnanden

Prunus serrulata f. ohnanden, commonly known as the Japanese cherry blossom or some variation thereof, is a botanical variant of the Japanese cherry tree.

It is characterized by its distinctive, serrated leaf margins and its delicate, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in early spring. This form is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its aesthetic appeal. The plant is native to Japan and thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil.

Its botanical features make it a popular subject in horticulture and landscape design.
