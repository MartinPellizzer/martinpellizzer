Polygonum × dietrichiae

Polygonum × dietrichiae, commonly known as the hybrid knotweed, is a perennial plant belonging to the Polygonaceae family.

It is characterized by its robust growth and distinctive, arrow-shaped leaves with a rough, textured surface. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-white in color, often appearing in late summer. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in wetlands, stream banks, and other aquatic habitats.

Due to its vigorous growth, it can become invasive in certain environments.
