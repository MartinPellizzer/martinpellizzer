Piper begoniicolor var. lobatum

Piper begoniicolor var. lobatum, commonly known as the Begonia-like pepper plant, is a tropical species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, begonia-like leaves that are often brightly colored with red, pink, or purple hues. The plant produces small, white flowers that are followed by tiny, berry-like fruits. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil.

This variant is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated as a houseplant or in shaded garden settings.
