Poa nemoralis var. schoenosperma

Poa nemoralis var. schoenosperma, commonly known as the meadow grass, is a variant of the common bentgrass.

It is a perennial grass species characterized by its tufted growth habit and fine, dark green blades. The plant typically thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets that are loosely arranged, and it produces small, wind-pollinated flowers.

This variant is notable for its adaptability and is often used in ecological restoration projects.
