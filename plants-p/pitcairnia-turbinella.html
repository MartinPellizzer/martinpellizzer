Pitcairnia turbinella

Pitcairnia turbinella, commonly known as the Turbinella pitcairnia, is a species of bromeliad native to South America.

It is characterized by its compact, rosette-shaped foliage that forms a central cup to collect water. The plant produces striking, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in shaded, humid environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated as a houseplant in many regions.
