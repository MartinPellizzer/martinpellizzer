Prunus praefremontii

Prunus praefremontii, commonly known as the Fremont cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the southwestern United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and clusters of white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or arid environments.

Prunus praefremontii is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical significance in local ecosystems.
