Psyllium famarae

Psyllium famarae, commonly known as the common psyllium, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the family Plantaginaceae and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or white in color. Its most notable feature is the production of mucilage-rich seeds, which are widely used in traditional medicine and as a dietary supplement.

The mucilage has thickening and soothing properties, making it beneficial for digestive health.
