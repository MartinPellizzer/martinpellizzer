Phoradendron albovaginatum

Phoradendron albovaginatum, commonly known as the white-veined mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on a variety of host trees, primarily in the tropical and subtropical regions of Central and South America.

It has small, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in a white, waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss in its often-dry habitat. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences, and its berries are red or yellow, containing seeds that are dispersed by birds. As a hemiparasite, it derives some of its nutrients from the host tree through specialized roots called haustoria, while also performing photosynthesis to some extent.

Its ecological role includes providing food for birds and insects, and it is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
