Psidium nitidum

Psidium nitidum, commonly known as the white guava, is a small evergreen tree native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine white pubescence. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers that are clustered in axillary inflorescences. Its fruits are small, round, and typically have a smooth, white or pale green skin.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
