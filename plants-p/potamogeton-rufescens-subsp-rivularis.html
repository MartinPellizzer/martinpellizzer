Potamogeton rufescens subsp.

rivularis, commonly known as the river pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in North America. It has long, slender stems that are often branched and bear small, oval-shaped leaves arranged spirally along the stem.

The plant produces tiny, greenish flowers that are typically clustered in spike-like structures. It thrives in slow-moving to still waters with muddy or sandy substrates. Potamogeton rufescens subsp.
