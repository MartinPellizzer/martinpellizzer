Premna latifolia var. mucronata

Premna latifolia var. mucronata, commonly known as the blunt-tipped premna, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its opposite, ovate to elliptic leaves that are usually hairy on the underside. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, followed by three-lobed, winged fruits. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its leaves are used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.
