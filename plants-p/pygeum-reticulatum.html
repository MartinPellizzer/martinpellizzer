Pygeum reticulatum

Pygeum reticulatum, commonly known as the reticulate African apricot, is a small evergreen tree native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Proteaceae family and is characterized by its dense, leathery leaves and showy, yellowish-white flowers. The plant produces small, hard, orange-red fruits that contain a single seed. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
