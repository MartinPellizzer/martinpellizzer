Papaver leiocarpum

Papaver leiocarpum, commonly known as the white poppy, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Papaveraceae family, which includes other well-known species like the opium poppy. The plant is characterized by its delicate, white to pale yellow flowers and smooth, glossy leaves. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain the seeds.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
