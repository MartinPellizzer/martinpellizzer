Piper marmoreum

Piper marmoreum, commonly known as marble pipevine, is a climbing perennial plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, veined leaves that resemble marble, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in forest understories.

Piper marmoreum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
