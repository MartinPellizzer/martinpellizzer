Phylica lasiantha

Phylica lasiantha, commonly known as the silver bush, is a shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, white flowers that are clustered in dense heads, often appearing in late winter to early spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought tolerant, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

The leaves are aromatic and contain compounds that have been used in traditional medicine.
