Poeppigia chilensis

Poeppigia chilensis, commonly known as the Chilean puya, is a striking plant native to the Andes of Chile and Argentina.

It belongs to the Xanthorrhoeaceae family and is characterized by its tall, spiky inflorescence that resembles a bottlebrush. The plant produces vibrant orange or yellow flowers, which bloom in late summer and early autumn. Its leaves are long, narrow, and often covered in a waxy coating, helping it survive in arid conditions.

Poeppigia chilensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively low maintenance once established.
