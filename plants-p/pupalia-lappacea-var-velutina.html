Pupalia lappacea var. velutina

Pupalia lappacea var. velutina, commonly known as the velvet tamarind, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are often covered in a fine, velvety pubescence. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in racemes, followed by elongated, woody pods containing flat, edible seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and medicinal properties.

The species is also valued for its use in traditional medicine and as a source of natural dyes.
