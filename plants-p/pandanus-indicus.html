Pandanus indicus

Pandanus indicus, commonly known as the screw pine, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its distinctive, spirally arranged leaves that form a dense, umbrella-like canopy. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. Its roots are fibrous and often used in traditional crafts, while its leaves are utilized in basket weaving and thatching.

Pandanus indicus thrives in coastal and mangrove environments, demonstrating adaptability to saline and humid conditions.
