Phlox bifida subsp. arkansana

Phlox bifida subsp.

arkansana, commonly known as the Arkansas phlox, is a herbaceous perennial native to the central United States. It features showy, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, often appearing in late spring to early summer.

The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or open areas. Its foliage is aromatic and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

It is valued in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
