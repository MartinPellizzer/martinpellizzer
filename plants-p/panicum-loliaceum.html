Panicum loliaceum

Panicum loliaceum, commonly known as silver millet, is an annual grass native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, silver-gray leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny florets. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it a hardy crop in arid environments.

Silver millet is often used as a forage crop and for soil conservation due to its rapid growth and extensive root system.
