Penstemon seorsus

Penstemon seorsus, commonly known as the narrowleaf penstemon, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces spikes of tubular, two-lipped flowers that range in color from blue to purple, attracting pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Penstemon seorsus is also valued for its drought tolerance and low maintenance requirements.
