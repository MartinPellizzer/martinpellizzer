Piper japvonum

Piper japvonum, commonly known as the Java pepper or long pepper, is a climbing shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, green berries that turn red when ripe, containing aromatic seeds used in traditional medicine and cuisine. It thrives in tropical climates with high humidity and partial shade.

Its botanical features make it valuable for both ornamental and practical uses.
