Pterocymbium dussaudii

Pterocymbium dussaudii, commonly known as the Dussaud's pterocymbium, is a species of flowering plant in the family Orchidaceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its delicate, orchid-like flowers that are typically white or pale pink in color. The plant grows in humid, shaded environments, often in forest undergrowth, and prefers well-drained, loamy soil. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, and the flowers are borne on slender, erect stems.

Pterocymbium dussaudii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
