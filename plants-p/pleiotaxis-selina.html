Pleiotaxis selina

Pleiotaxis selina, commonly known as the golden starflower, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers that resemble small stars, which bloom in late summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and grows in a rosette formation. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Its unique appearance and drought tolerance make it a valuable addition to xeriscapes.
