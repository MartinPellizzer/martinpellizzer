Passiflora celtifolia

Passiflora celtifolia, commonly known as the white passionflower, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive white flowers with intricate patterns and a crown of feathery filaments. The plant produces edible berries that are often used in jams and desserts. Its leaves are palmately lobed and have a rough, textured surface.

Passiflora celtifolia is valued for its ornamental beauty and historical use in traditional medicine.
