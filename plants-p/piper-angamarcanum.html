Piper angamarcanum

Piper angamarcanum, commonly known as the Angamarca pepper, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to the Andean regions of South America, particularly Peru and Bolivia. The plant is characterized by its climbing habit, with opposite, ovate leaves that are often glandular-dotted. Its small, greenish-white flowers are clustered in spikes, and it produces small, peppery fruits that are used in local cuisines.

The botanical features of Piper angamarcanum make it a distinctive and culturally significant plant in its native habitat.
