Pouzolzia argenteonitida

Pouzolzia argenteonitida, commonly known as silverleaf pouzolzia, is a shrub native to parts of Africa and the Arabian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which gives it its common name. The plant has small, opposite leaves that are often covered in fine, white hairs. It produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by woody, capsule-like fruits.

Pouzolzia argenteonitida is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient plant in harsh climates.
