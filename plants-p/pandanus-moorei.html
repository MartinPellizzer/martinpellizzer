Pandanus moorei

Pandanus moorei, commonly known as the Moore's pandanus, is a tropical plant native to parts of Southeast Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It is a member of the Pandanaceae family and is characterized by its large, sword-shaped leaves that are often used in traditional crafts and culinary applications. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its inflorescence consists of a spadix surrounded by a large, showy bract, which is often used in floral arrangements.

The plant thrives in humid, coastal environments and is well adapted to sandy or rocky soils.
