Plantago pulla

Plantago pulla, commonly known as narrowleaf plantain, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features upright, slender stems that bear lance-shaped leaves with a rough, hairy texture. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in dense clusters during the summer months. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.

Its leaves are sometimes used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.
