Passiflora adulterina

Passiflora adulterina, commonly known as the purple passionflower, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, intricate flowers that feature five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments. The plant produces edible, yellow-orange berries that are rich in vitamins and antioxidants. Its leaves are alternate, heart-shaped, and have a rough, hairy texture.

Passiflora adulterina is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in herbal medicine.
