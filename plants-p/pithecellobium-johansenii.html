Pithecellobium johansenii

Pithecellobium johansenii, commonly known as the Hawaiian candlewood tree, is a member of the legume family Fabaceae.

It is native to the Hawaiian Islands and is characterized by its distinctive, candle-like seed pods. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces yellowish-white flowers. It is often found growing in coastal areas and is adapted to the tropical climate of Hawaii.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is considered an important native species in the region.
