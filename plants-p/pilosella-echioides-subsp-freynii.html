Pilosella echioides subsp. freynii

Pilosella echioides subsp.

freynii, commonly known as the Freyn's pearly everlasting, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of North America. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dense, silver-gray foliage and small, daisy-like flowers.

The plant produces numerous tiny, white to pale yellow florets arranged in composite flower heads. It is often found in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Pilosella echioides subsp.

freynii is adapted to drought conditions and is sometimes used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements.
