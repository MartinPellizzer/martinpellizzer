Pithecellobium sanguineum

Pithecellobium sanguineum, commonly known as the red mombin or bloodfruit, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically red or orange when ripe, containing edible seeds. It is often grown for its ornamental value and has some traditional uses in folk medicine.

The plant's ability to fix nitrogen makes it useful in soil enrichment and agroforestry systems.
