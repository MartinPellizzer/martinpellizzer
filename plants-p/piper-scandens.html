Piper scandens

Piper scandens, commonly known as the creeping pepper vine, is a climbing perennial plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its slender, twining stems and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into elongated, berry-like fruits. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their aromatic and potentially medicinal properties.

The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests or along stream banks.
