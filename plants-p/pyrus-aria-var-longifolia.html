Pyrus aria var. longifolia

Pyrus aria var. longifolia, commonly known as the longleaf pear, is a variant of the pear tree native to parts of East Asia.

It is characterized by its elongated, lance-shaped leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in spring, followed by edible, sweet fruits. It thrives in temperate climates and prefers well-drained soil.

This variety is valued for both its ornamental appeal and its fruit production.
