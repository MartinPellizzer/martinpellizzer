Perityle canescens

Perityle canescens, commonly known as the silverleaf perityle, is a perennial herb native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which helps reduce water loss in its harsh environment. The plant produces small, white or pale blue flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine, hair-like structures that provide additional protection against intense sunlight.

Perityle canescens is often found growing in rocky soils and along dry streambeds, showcasing its adaptability to extreme conditions.
