Pygeum capitellatum

Pygeum capitellatum, commonly known as African wild almond, is a deciduous tree native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Proteaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers. The plant produces woody, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.

Due to its unique botanical features, it is of interest in both ecological and horticultural studies.
