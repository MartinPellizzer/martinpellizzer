Pelargonium maculatum

Pelargonium maculatum, commonly known as the spotted geranium, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its attractive, showy flowers that range in color from pink to red, often with a distinctive white or pale pink center. The leaves are typically rounded and have a slightly hairy texture, with some varieties displaying spotted or mottled patterns. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and containers.

This plant is also known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
