Ptilotus gaudichaudii subsp. parviflorus

Ptilotus gaudichaudii subsp.

parviflorus, commonly known as the small-flowered spinifex, is a flowering plant native to Australia. It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its small, clustered flowers and hairy stems.

The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid regions, thriving in sandy soils. Its foliage is often covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in dry environments.
