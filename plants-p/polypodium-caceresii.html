Polypodium caceresii

Polypodium caceresii, commonly known as the Caceres's fern, is a species of epiphytic fern native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its delicate, feather-like fronds that are typically less than 30 centimeters in length. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, often growing on trees or rocks in mountainous areas. Its rhizomes are thick and fleshy, allowing it to store water and nutrients efficiently.

Polypodium caceresii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
