Plumeria alba var. fragrantissima

Plumeria alba var. fragrantissima, commonly known as the frangipani, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy, white flowers that emit a sweet, fragrant aroma. The plant has simple, lance-shaped leaves and produces a milky sap when cut. It thrives in warm, sunny climates and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.

The flowers are typically borne on long, slender stalks and bloom during the summer months.
