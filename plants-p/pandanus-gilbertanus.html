Pandanus gilbertanus

Pandanus gilbertanus, commonly known as the Gilbert's pandanus, is a flowering plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and long, narrow leaves that are often used in traditional crafts. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. It thrives in coastal areas and is adapted to salt spray and sandy soils.

The fruit, which is a spiny, fibrous structure, contains edible seeds that are sometimes consumed by local communities.
