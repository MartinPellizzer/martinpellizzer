Pulsatilla saxatilis

Pulsatilla saxatilis, commonly known as the saxatile pasqueflower, is a member of the Ranunculaceae family.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in rocky or alpine environments. The plant features a short, stout stem with palmately divided, hairy leaves. Its most distinctive characteristic is its showy, yellowish-green flowers with numerous stamens.

It is native to regions of Europe and is often found in limestone soils.
