Polygala vulgaris subsp. angustifolia

Polygala vulgaris subsp.

angustifolia, commonly known as narrow-leaved milkwort, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It is a perennial herb characterized by its slender, hairy stems and small, pale green to yellowish flowers.

The leaves are typically narrow and linear, giving the plant its subspecies name. It thrives in dry, sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.

This plant is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
