Psyllocarpus trichotomus

Psyllocarpus trichotomus, commonly known as the three-branched psylla, is a tropical shrub belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three leaflets arranged symmetrically. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It is native to regions in Southeast Asia and is often found in moist, shaded forest environments.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens.
