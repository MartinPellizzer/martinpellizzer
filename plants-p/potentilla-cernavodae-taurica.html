Potentilla cernavodae-taurica

Potentilla cernavodae-taurica, commonly known as the Crimean potentilla, is a rare shrub native to the Crimean Peninsula.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its low-growing habit and clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically thrives in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to the semi-arid climate of its native region. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and covered in a fine, silvery pubescence, which helps reduce water loss.

Due to habitat loss and limited distribution, it is considered endangered and is of particular interest in botanical conservation efforts.
