Persoonia laureola

Persoonia laureola, commonly known as the silver leaf geebung, is a small shrub native to the eastern coast of Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, silver-gray leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in clusters, which are typically borne on short pedicels. Persoonia laureola is adapted to grow in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in coastal and heathland environments.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
