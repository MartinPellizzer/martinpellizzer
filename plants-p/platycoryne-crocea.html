Platycoryne crocea

Platycoryne crocea, commonly known as the yellow orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking yellow petals and a distinctive, flat lip that sets it apart from other orchid species. The plant typically grows in tropical rainforests, where it thrives in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers bloom in clusters, often displaying a vibrant yellow color that attracts pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its ornamental value, Platycoryne crocea is cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts around the world.
