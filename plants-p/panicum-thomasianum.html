Panicum thomasianum

Panicum thomasianum, commonly known as Thomasian panicgrass, is a flowering plant species native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and feathery plumes that produce small, wind-dispersed seeds. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is often found in areas with seasonal rainfall.

Its ecological role includes providing habitat for small insects and contributing to soil stabilization in arid environments.
