Podocarpus alpinus

Podocarpus alpinus, commonly known as the alpine podocarp, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, scale-like leaves that are typically arranged in whorls around the stems. The plant produces small, cone-like structures that contain both male and female reproductive organs, a feature typical of gymnosperms. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in high-altitude forests.

Its slow growth rate and unique morphology make it an important component of alpine ecosystems.
