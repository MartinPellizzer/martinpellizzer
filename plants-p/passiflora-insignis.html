Passiflora insignis

Passiflora insignis, commonly known as the yellow passionflower, is a climbing vine native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers with intricate structures, including a crown of filaments and a distinctive central column. The plant produces edible berries that are rich in antioxidants and have a sweet, tangy flavor. Its leaves are heart-shaped and often have a velvety texture.

Passiflora insignis is valued in horticulture for its ornamental beauty and is also used in traditional medicine.
