Potentilla crocea var. sabauda

Potentilla crocea var. sabauda, commonly known as the golden cinquefoil, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It features bright yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, typically from June to July. The plant has compound leaves with five leaflets, which are covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in horticulture for its vibrant color and hardy nature.
