Poa hypsinephes

Poa hypsinephes, commonly known as the alpine bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and fine, blue-green foliage that reflects its common name. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes. Its small, spike-like inflorescence contains numerous tiny flowers, typical of the Poaceae family.

Poa hypsinephes plays a vital role in stabilizing soil in high-altitude ecosystems and is of interest to botanists studying plant adaptation to extreme conditions.
