Pavonia ducke-limae

Pavonia ducke-limae, commonly known as the Brazilian pink leadplant, is a flowering plant native to Brazil.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its striking pink to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in shrubland and open forests, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are trifoliate, meaning they consist of three leaflets, and it produces small, pod-like fruits that contain seeds.

Pavonia ducke-limae is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in supporting pollinators.
