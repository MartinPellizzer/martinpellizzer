Podocarpus chingianus

Podocarpus chingianus, commonly known as the Chinese podocarp, is a species of coniferous evergreen tree native to China.

It belongs to the family Podocarpaceae and is characterized by its needle-like leaves and small, cone-like fruits. The plant is typically found in subtropical and temperate regions, growing in forested areas with well-drained soil. It has a slow growth rate and is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value.

The wood of Podocarpus chingianus is dense and durable, making it useful for construction and furniture-making.
