Pandanus grusonianus

Pandanus grusonianus, commonly known as the Gruison's pandanus, is a tropical plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It is a member of the Pandanaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, narrow leaves. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, yellowish flowers that are often used in traditional crafts and culinary applications. Its roots and leaves are also utilized in local medicine for their purported healing properties.

Overall, Pandanus grusonianus plays an important ecological and cultural role in its native regions.
