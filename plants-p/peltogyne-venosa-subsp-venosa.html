Peltogyne venosa subsp. venosa

Peltogyne venosa subsp.

venosa, commonly known as the silky ironwood, is a tropical tree species native to parts of Southeast Asia and northern Australia. It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and smooth, greyish bark that becomes flaky with age.

The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers followed by elongated, woody pods containing flat, dark brown seeds. It is known for its hard, heavy wood, which is highly valued for construction and furniture.

This species thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland rainforests.
