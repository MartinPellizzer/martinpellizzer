Parathesis lunata

Parathesis lunata, commonly known as the moonflower, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its large, white, night-blooming flowers that emit a strong fragrance. The plant belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in warm, humid climates. Its leaves are broad and heart-shaped, while its stems are often hairy.

The moonflower is often cultivated for its ornamental value and its unique nocturnal blooming habit.
