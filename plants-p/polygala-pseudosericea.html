Polygala pseudosericea

Polygala pseudosericea, commonly known as the slender milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae, which is known for its diverse and often ornamental species. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and small, delicate flowers. Its foliage consists of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, hairy texture.

The flowers are usually pale green to yellowish in color and are arranged in clusters, making it a visually interesting plant for gardeners.
