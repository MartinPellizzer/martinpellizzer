Paspalum platycaule

Paspalum platycaule, commonly known as flat-stemmed grass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It typically grows in wetland habitats, such as marshes and along waterways, where it thrives in moist to saturated soils. The plant has slender, flat stems and narrow, grass-like leaves, which help it conserve water in its wetland environment. Its flowers are small and clustered in spikelets, a common feature among grasses.

Paspalum platycaule plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
