Prunus × subhirtella f. semperflorens

Prunus × subhirtella f. semperflorens, commonly known as the compact weeping cherry, is a deciduous ornamental shrub or small tree prized for its aesthetic appeal.

It features a weeping habit with cascading branches that support clusters of small, fragrant white flowers in spring. The plant is native to Japan and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes. It has small, ovate leaves that turn yellow in autumn, adding seasonal interest.

The compact growth form makes it suitable for smaller spaces, enhancing its versatility in horticultural applications.
