Pleurothallis pisifera

Pleurothallis pisifera, commonly known as the orchid with the leaf-like flowers, is a member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Ecuador and Peru. The plant is characterized by its unique inflorescence, which resembles a leaf in shape and color. It produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink, often arranged in a raceme.

Due to its striking appearance and rarity, it is highly valued in horticulture and conservation efforts.
