Polemonium arcticum

Polemonium arcticum, commonly known as Arctic bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the Arctic and subarctic regions.

It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils in tundra and alpine environments. The plant features upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, blue to purple flowers. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in a fine layer of white hairs.

Polemonium arcticum is adapted to cold climates and has a short growing season, blooming in late summer.
