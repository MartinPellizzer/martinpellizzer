Papyrus radiatus

Papyrus radiatus, commonly known as the radiate papyrus, is a flowering plant native to the wetlands of southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges and other grass-like plants. The plant features tall, cylindrical stems that can grow up to 2 meters in height, with narrow, grass-like leaves. Its most distinctive characteristic is its dense clusters of white or pale pink flowers that bloom in summer.

Papyrus radiatus is often found in marshy areas and is adapted to thrive in moist, nutrient-rich soils.
