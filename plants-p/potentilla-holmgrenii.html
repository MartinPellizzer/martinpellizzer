Potentilla holmgrenii

Potentilla holmgrenii, commonly known as the Holmgren's cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is a low-growing perennial shrub that typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features multiple stems covered in fine, silver-tomentose hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its flowers are small, yellow, and clustered in dense inflorescences, blooming in late spring to early summer.

The leaves are usually palmately divided into five leaflets, which are narrow and pointed, contributing to its ornamental value in gardens.
