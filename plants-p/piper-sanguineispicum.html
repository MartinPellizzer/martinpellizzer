Piper sanguineispicum

Piper sanguineispicum, commonly known as the red-fruited pepper plant, is a member of the Piperaceae family, native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and opposite, ovate leaves with a glossy texture. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into bright red, fleshy berries. These berries are edible and have a peppery, spicy flavor, often used in local cuisines.

Due to its ornamental value and unique fruit, it is cultivated in some tropical gardens.
