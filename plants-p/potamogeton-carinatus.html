Potamogeton carinatus

Potamogeton carinatus, commonly known as the ridge pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant found in freshwater environments.

It has long, narrow leaves that are typically arranged in two rows around the stem. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually clustered at the base of the stems. Its roots are fibrous and help stabilize sediments in aquatic habitats.

Potamogeton carinatus plays a vital role in providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
