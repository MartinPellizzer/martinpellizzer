Platea excelsa

Platea excelsa, commonly known as the giant water fern, is a member of the Araceae family and is native to tropical regions of South America.

It is a floating aquatic plant that thrives in slow-moving freshwater environments. The plant has large, broad leaves that can reach up to 3 meters in length, allowing it to spread extensively across water surfaces. Its reproductive structures include a spadix and a spathe, typical of the Araceae family.

Platea excelsa plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and oxygen to aquatic life.
