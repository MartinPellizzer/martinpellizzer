Psoralea bituminosa var. brachycarpa

Psoralea bituminosa var. brachycarpa, commonly known as the short-seeded psoralea, is a perennial shrub native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, stipulate leaves and distinctive, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces small, leathery pods that contain several elliptical seeds. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in grassland and savanna habitats.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
