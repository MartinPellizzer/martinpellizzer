Piaranthus pullus

Piaranthus pullus, commonly known as the Cape daisy, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers that bloom in shades of yellow and orange. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and thrives in full sun. It has a compact, bushy growth habit with narrow, linear leaves.

Piaranthus pullus is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
