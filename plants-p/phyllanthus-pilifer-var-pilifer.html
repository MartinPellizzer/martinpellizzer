Phyllanthus pilifer var. pilifer

Phyllanthus pilifer var. pilifer, commonly known as the hairy phyllanthus, is a shrub native to parts of Asia, including India and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, opposite leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically followed by small, round fruits. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
