Prunus dulcis

Prunus dulcis, commonly known as the sweet almond tree, is a deciduous tree native to the Middle East and South Asia.

It is characterized by its small, white or pink flowers and its oval-shaped leaves that are alternate and serrated. The tree produces small, hard fruits called drupes, which contain the edible almond seeds. These seeds are rich in nutrients and are widely used in culinary and medicinal applications.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental qualities and its role in traditional horticulture.
