Prunus taquetii

Prunus taquetii, commonly known as the Taquet's cherry, is a species of shrub or small tree native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and small, white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, edible berries that are typically red when mature. It is adapted to the arid and rocky conditions of its native habitat, showing resilience to drought.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
