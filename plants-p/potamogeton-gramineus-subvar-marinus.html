Potamogeton gramineus subvar. marinus

Potamogeton gramineus subvar. marinus, commonly known as the marine pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to coastal and brackish water environments.

It has long, narrow leaves that are typically dark green and arranged in whorls around the stem. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are adapted for underwater pollination. It thrives in shallow, nutrient-rich waters with moderate salinity.

This subvariety is often found in estuaries and tidal marshes, playing a vital role in supporting aquatic ecosystems.
