Polygala leucantha

Polygala leucantha, commonly known as the white milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, white to pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in grasslands and shrubland habitats. Its leaves are opposite and pinnately lobed, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Polygala leucantha is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
