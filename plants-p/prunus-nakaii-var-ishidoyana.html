Prunus nakaii var. ishidoyana

Prunus nakaii var. ishidoyana, commonly known as the Ishidoyama cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its distinctive, double-flowered blossoms that appear in early spring, adding ornamental value to gardens. The plant has ovate to lanceolate leaves that are dark green and glossy on top, with a lighter underside. It produces small, edible fruits that are typically consumed by birds and small mammals.

This variety is prized for its aesthetic appeal and is often cultivated in horticultural settings for its ornamental flowers.
