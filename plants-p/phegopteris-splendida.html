Phegopteris splendida

Phegopteris splendida, commonly known as the splendid water fern, is a species of aquatic fern native to parts of Asia, including China, Japan, and Korea.

It is characterized by its delicate, feathery fronds that are typically submerged or floating in freshwater environments. The plant has a complex life cycle involving both spore-producing and vegetative reproduction stages. Its rhizomes form dense mats that help stabilize sediment in aquatic habitats.

Phegopteris splendida is often found in slow-moving streams, ponds, and marshes, where it plays a role in maintaining water quality and providing habitat for small aquatic organisms.
