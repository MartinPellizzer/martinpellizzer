Portulaca juliomartinezii

Portulaca juliomartinezii, commonly known as Juliomartinez's moss rose, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States.

It belongs to the family Portulacaceae and is characterized by its fleshy, succulent leaves and vibrant, showy flowers that bloom in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant is adapted to thrive in hot, dry environments, with a shallow root system that allows it to conserve water efficiently. Its flowers typically open in the morning and close by midday, a trait known as nyctinasty, which helps reduce water loss.

Portulaca juliomartinezii is often found in rocky soils and is a popular choice for xeriscaping due to its low water requirements and ornamental value.
