Psidium yacaense

Psidium yacaense, commonly known as the Yaca tree, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its small, fragrant white flowers and edible berries. The plant typically grows as a small tree or shrub, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a glossy surface, while the fruit is a small, yellowish berry that ripens in the dry season.

The Yaca tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and its potential use in traditional medicine.
