Passiflora celata

Passiflora celata, commonly known as the Brazilian passionflower, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful flowers that feature a unique blend of sepals, petals, and a crown of filaments. The plant produces blue or violet berries that are edible and often consumed by birds and small mammals. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a rough texture, while its stems are woody and covered in fine hairs.

Passiflora celata is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its medicinal properties.
