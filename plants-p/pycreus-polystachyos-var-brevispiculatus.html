Pycreus polystachyos var. brevispiculatus

Pycreus polystachyos var. brevispiculatus, commonly known as the short-spined pycreus, is a variant of the pycreus plant native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and distinctive, short, spiny bracts that give it its common name. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink in color. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
