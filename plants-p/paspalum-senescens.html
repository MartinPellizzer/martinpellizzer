Paspalum senescens

Paspalum senescens, commonly known as the senescent grass, is a perennial grass species native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, linear leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny florets. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions and full sun.

Paspalum senescens is often found in disturbed areas, such as roadsides and fields, and is known for its ability to persist in harsh environments.
