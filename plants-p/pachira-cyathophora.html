Pachira cyathophora

Pachira cyathophora, commonly known as the Mexican money plant, is a tropical shrub native to Central America.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are often variegated with silver and green patterns. The plant produces small, yellow flowers followed by distinctive, cup-shaped fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in warm, humid environments and prefers well-draining soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in botanical gardens.
