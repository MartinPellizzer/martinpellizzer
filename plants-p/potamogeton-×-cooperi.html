Potamogeton × cooperi

Potamogeton × cooperi, commonly known as the hybrid water plant, is a submerged aquatic plant native to North America.

It is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Potamogeton perfoliatus and Potamogeton praelatus. This plant typically grows in shallow, freshwater environments such as ponds, lakes, and slow-moving rivers. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often arranged in whorls around the stem.

Potamogeton × cooperi plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for small fish and invertebrates.
