Pyrus stirtoniana

It is native to the Andes mountains in South America, specifically in regions of Peru and Bolivia. The plant is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring. Its fruit, which is a small pear-like berry, is edible but not widely cultivated.
