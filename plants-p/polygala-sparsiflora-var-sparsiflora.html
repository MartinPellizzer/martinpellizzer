Polygala sparsiflora var. sparsiflora

Polygala sparsiflora var. sparsiflora, commonly known as the sparsely-flowered milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is characterized by its slender, branched stems. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in loose spikes, which bloom during the warmer months. Its leaves are alternate, narrow, and often covered in fine hairs.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and delicate blooms.
