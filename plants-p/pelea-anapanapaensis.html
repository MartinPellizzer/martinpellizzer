Pelea anapanapaensis

Pelea anapanapaensis, commonly known as the Anapanapa pelea, is a rare flowering plant native to the island of Anapanapa in the South Pacific.

It is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit and produces small, delicate white flowers during the cooler months. The plant has dark green, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stems. It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and requires a humid, subtropical climate.

Due to its limited geographic range and habitat specificity, it is considered endangered and is of great interest to botanists and conservationists.
