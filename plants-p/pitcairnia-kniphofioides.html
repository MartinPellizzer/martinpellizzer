Pitcairnia kniphofioides

Pitcairnia kniphofioides, commonly known as the kniphofia-like pitcairnia, is a species of bromeliad native to South America.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that form a central pool to collect rainwater. The plant produces striking, spike-like inflorescences with vividly colored flowers, typically in shades of red, orange, and yellow. It thrives in shaded, humid environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts for its unique appearance and resilience.
