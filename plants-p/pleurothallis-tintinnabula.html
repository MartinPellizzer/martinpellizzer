Pleurothallis tintinnabula

Pleurothallis tintinnabula, commonly known as the silver bell orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a small epiphytic orchid, typically found growing on trees in humid, shaded environments. The plant features delicate, lance-shaped leaves and produces intricate, pendulous flowers with a distinctive silver-white coloration. Its flowers are often fragrant and bloom during the wet season, attracting pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated by enthusiasts, though it remains relatively rare in the horticultural trade.
