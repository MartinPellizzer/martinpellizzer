Papualthia longipes

Papualthia longipes, commonly known as the long-stemmed papualthia, is a unique flowering plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, tubular flowers that bloom in shades of yellow and orange. The plant has adapted to harsh environments through its succulent leaves and deep root system, allowing it to store water efficiently. Its flowers are pollinated primarily by bees and other insects, contributing to its ecological role in the region.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
