Plantago elongata var. pentasperma

Plantago elongata var. pentasperma, commonly known as the five-seeded plantain, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features a basal rosette of lanceolate, hairy leaves that are often ribbed and have a waxy surface. The plant produces a tall, unbranched flowering stem with small, greenish-white flowers arranged in a spike. Its fruit is a capsule that contains five seeds, giving it its common name.

This species is often found in disturbed soils and is used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
