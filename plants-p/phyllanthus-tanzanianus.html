Phyllanthus tanzanianus

Phyllanthus tanzanianus, commonly known as the Tanzanian spurge, is a shrub native to East Africa.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in savanna and woodland areas.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
