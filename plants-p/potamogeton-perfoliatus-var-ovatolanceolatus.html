Potamogeton perfoliatus var. ovatolanceolatus

Potamogeton perfoliatus var. ovatolanceolatus, commonly known as the broad-leaf pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater ecosystems.

It features long, narrow, ovate-lanceolate leaves that are often arranged in whorls around the stem. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or yellowish in color. It thrives in shallow, slow-moving waters and is often found in lakes, ponds, and wetlands.

This variety is important for aquatic habitats, providing food and shelter for various aquatic organisms.
