Pulmonaria procumbens

Pulmonaria procumbens, commonly known as the prostrate lungwort, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe.

It features broad, velvety leaves that are often covered in a dense covering of fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped, pink or purple flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Its common name is derived from its historical use in treating respiratory ailments.
