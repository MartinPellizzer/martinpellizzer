Pappobolus imbaburensis

Pappobolus imbaburensis, commonly known as the Zimbabwean grass or Imbabure grass, is a flowering plant belonging to the Poaceae family.

It is native to southern Africa, particularly Zimbabwe, and thrives in grassland and savanna ecosystems. The plant is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets that produce small, wind-dispersed seeds.

Pappobolus imbaburensis plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for livestock in its native habitat.
