Polemonium obscurum

Polemonium obscurum, commonly known as the shade-loving bellflower, is a perennial herb native to eastern North America.

It typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and thrives in rich, well-drained soils. The plant features lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. Its blooming period occurs in late spring to early summer, attracting various pollinators.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in forest ecosystems.
