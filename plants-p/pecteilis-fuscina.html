Pecteilis fuscina

Pecteilis fuscina, commonly known as the dark speckled orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, dark purple to brownish flowers with intricate speckling. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as pine flatwoods and wetlands. Its rhizomatous root system allows it to spread and form colonies in suitable habitats.

Pecteilis fuscina is a rare and endangered species, highlighting the importance of conservation efforts to protect its natural habitat.
