Prismatocarpus lycioides

Prismatocarpus lycioides, commonly known as the Indian date palm, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and broad, feather-like leaves that can reach several meters in length. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into clustered, fleshy fruits resembling dates. Its botanical features make it an important species in both horticulture and traditional medicine.

The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible fruits.
