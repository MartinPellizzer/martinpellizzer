Prunus padus var. rotundifolia

Prunus padus var. rotundifolia, commonly known as the roundleaf bird cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its rounded, often lobed leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers in early spring, followed by edible, red drupes that attract birds and other wildlife. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodlands or along stream banks.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in natural habitats.
