Polygonum aviculare subsp. battandieri

Polygonum aviculare subsp.

battandieri, commonly known as the narrowleaf smartweed, is a herbaceous annual plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It typically grows in moist environments such as wet meadows, marshes, and along stream banks.

The plant features upright stems that are often reddish in color, and its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped with a slightly hairy surface. Its small, greenish-white flowers are arranged in clusters and bloom in late spring to early summer.

The species is known for its ability to thrive in wet soils and is often found in disturbed habitats.
