Paspalum setaceum var. villosissimum

Paspalum setaceum var. villosissimum, commonly known as the silky paspalum, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its fine, silky hairs covering the leaves and stems, giving it a soft, fuzzy appearance. The plant typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, preferring moist, acidic soils. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bloom in the summer months.

This grass is often used in restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for wildlife.
