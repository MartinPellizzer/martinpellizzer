Prestonia simulans

Prestonia simulans, commonly known as the yellow prestonia, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is a member of the Apocynaceae family, which includes milkweeds and related species. The plant is characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. Its leaves are typically opposite and elliptical, with a smooth texture.

Prestonia simulans is often found in tropical forests and is valued for its ornamental appeal.
