Piptochaetium virescens var. arsenii

Piptochaetium virescens var. arsenii, commonly known as the arsenic grass, is a perennial grass species native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically green to bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny flowers, which are wind-pollinated. It is adapted to survive in poor soils and can tolerate high levels of salinity and heavy metal contamination.

This variety is of interest in phytoremediation studies due to its ability to accumulate toxic elements such as arsenic.
