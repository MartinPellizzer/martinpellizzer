Phalaenopsis

Phalaenopsis, commonly known as the moth orchid, is a popular epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features long, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of fragrant, moth-like flowers in various colors. The plant thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining potting media. It is known for its ability to bloom repeatedly over several years.

Phalaenopsis is widely cultivated for its ornamental value and relatively low maintenance requirements.
