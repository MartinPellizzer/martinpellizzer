Pedicularis plicata subsp. plicata

Pedicularis plicata subsp.

plicata, commonly known as the wrinkled lousewort, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of western North America. It is characterized by its striking pink to purple spurred flowers and hairy, wrinkled leaves.

The plant typically grows in moist, acidic soils in alpine or subalpine habitats. It is part of the Orobanchaceae family, which includes many parasitic and hemiparasitic species.

Its unique morphology and ecological niche make it a notable species in high-elevation ecosystems.
