Piper voigtii

Piper voigtii, commonly known as the Voight's pepper, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and small, heart-shaped leaves. It produces tiny greenish-white flowers that develop into elongated, slender fruits.

Piper voigtii is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and ability to thrive in shaded environments.
