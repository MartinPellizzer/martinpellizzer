Pteris longifolia f. stipularis

Pteris longifolia f. stipularis, commonly known as the stipular fern, is a variant of the common water fern.

It is a perennial evergreen fern characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that are typically light green in color. The plant features distinctive stipules, which are small, leaf-like structures at the base of the fronds, giving it its specific epithet. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for indoor gardening and ornamental horticulture.
