Pseuderanthemum velutinum

Pseuderanthemum velutinum, commonly known as velvetleaf pseuderanthemum, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is a flowering plant in the family Acanthaceae, characterized by its showy, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. The leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in a fine, velvety pubescence, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The plant is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its attractive foliage and blooms.
