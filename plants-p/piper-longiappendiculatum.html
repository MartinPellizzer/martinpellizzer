Piper longiappendiculatum

Piper longiappendiculatum, commonly known as the long-appendiculate pepper, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil and Peru. The plant is characterized by its slender, climbing vines and small, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are unisexual, with both male and female flowers occurring on the same plant.

The fruit is a small, three-lobed berry that ripens to a dark purple color.
