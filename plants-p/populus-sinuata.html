Populus sinuata

Populus sinuata, commonly known as the Chinese poplar, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It belongs to the Salicaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and heart-shaped leaves with deeply serrated margins. The plant produces small, catkin-like flowers in early spring, followed by winged seeds that are dispersed by the wind. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in river valleys and lowlands.

Populus sinuata is valued for its rapid growth and is sometimes used in reforestation efforts.
