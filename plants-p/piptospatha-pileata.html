Piptospatha pileata

Piptospatha pileata, commonly known as the silverleaf tree, is a species of flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its silvery-white, velvety leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 10 meters tall.

The bark is smooth and gray, and the wood is valued for its durability and fine grain.
