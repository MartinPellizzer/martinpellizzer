Polypodium spectrum var. pentadactylum

Polypodium spectrum var. pentadactylum, commonly known as the five-finger fern, is a distinctive member of the Polypodiaceae family.

It is characterized by its striking, fern-like fronds that resemble fingers, giving it its common name. The plant features a creeping rhizome system that allows it to spread and form dense mats. Its leaves are bipinnate, with multiple pinnae that are often deeply divided.

This species is typically found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky crevices.
