Polygonum sparsipilosum var. hubertii

Polygonum sparsipilosum var. hubertii, commonly known as the Hubert's knotweed, is a perennial herb native to specific regions of China.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-white in color, appearing in late summer. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as stream banks and forest understories.

Its botanical features make it a notable species within the Polygonaceae family.
