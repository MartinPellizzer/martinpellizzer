Pleurogynella diffusa

Pleurogynella diffusa, commonly known as the silverleaf vine, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its delicate, trailing stems and small, opposite leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery down. The plant produces clusters of tiny, fragrant white flowers that bloom throughout the growing season. It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it can become invasive in certain areas.
