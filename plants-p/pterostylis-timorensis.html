Pterostylis timorensis

Pterostylis timorensis, commonly known as the Timor duck orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the island of Timor in Indonesia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its unique, duck-like floral structure. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often in forest undergrowth. Its flowers are usually pale green or yellow, with a distinctive, elongated labellum that resembles a duck's bill.

Pterostylis timorensis is a small plant, with slender stems and narrow leaves, and it produces a single, showy flower per stem.
