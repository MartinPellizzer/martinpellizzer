Pentaphragma ellipticum var. flocculosum

Pentaphragma ellipticum var. flocculosum, commonly known as the woolly bladderwort, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of North America.

It is a small, herbaceous species that typically grows in wetlands and moist soils. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom during the summer months. Its leaves are characterized by their distinctive, inflated structures that aid in buoyancy.

The species is notable for its unique adaptations to aquatic environments, including its specialized root system and reproductive strategies.
