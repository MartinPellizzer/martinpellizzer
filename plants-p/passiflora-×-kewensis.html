Passiflora × kewensis

Passiflora × kewensis, commonly known as the Kew passionflower, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Passiflora caerulea and Passiflora lutea.

It is native to South America and is characterized by its striking, complex flowers with five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments. The plant is typically a climbing vine with heart-shaped leaves and produces edible, orange-red berries. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and medicinal properties.

The Kew passionflower is also valued in traditional medicine for its purported calming effects.
