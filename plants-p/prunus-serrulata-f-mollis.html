Prunus serrulata f. mollis

Prunus serrulata f. mollis, commonly known as the Japanese cherry blossom with soft leaves, is a cultivar of the Prunus serrulata species.

It is characterized by its dense, velvety foliage that gives it a unique texture compared to other cherry blossom varieties. The plant produces clusters of pale pink to white flowers in early spring, which are highly ornamental. It is often cultivated in gardens and parks for its aesthetic appeal and seasonal blooms.

This form is particularly valued for its compact growth habit and ability to thrive in temperate climates.
