Pothos terminalis

Pothos terminalis, commonly known as the devil's ivy, is a tropical vine native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, waxy leaves that are typically variegated with shades of yellow and green. The plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in low-light conditions, making it a popular houseplant. It produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers, though these are rarely seen in cultivation.

Pothos terminalis is highly adaptable and can be grown in various forms, including trailing vines, upright shrubs, or trained into shapes.
