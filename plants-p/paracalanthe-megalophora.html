Paracalanthe megalophora

Paracalanthe megalophora, commonly known as the giant calanthe, is a striking orchid native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that can reach up to 10 centimeters in diameter, often displaying vibrant colors such as pink, purple, and white. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are broad and fleshy, adapted to store moisture in its natural habitat.

The orchid's unique morphology and striking blooms make it a popular choice among orchid enthusiasts and botanists alike.
