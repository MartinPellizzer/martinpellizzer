Pteleopsis tetraptera

Pteleopsis tetraptera, commonly known as the four-winged sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, four-winged fruits that are adapted for wind dispersal. The plant has small, hairy leaves that help reduce water loss in hot, dry environments. It typically grows from seeds that are dispersed by the wind, allowing it to colonize sandy soils.

Pteleopsis tetraptera is an important component of the local flora, contributing to the ecological balance in its habitat.
