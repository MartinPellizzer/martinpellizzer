Pseuderanthemum incisum

Pseuderanthemum incisum, commonly known as the cutleaf pseuderanthemum, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its attractive, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink or purple. The plant features opposite, ovate to lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated as a ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal.

Pseuderanthemum incisum is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
