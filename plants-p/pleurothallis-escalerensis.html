Pleurothallis escalerensis

Pleurothallis escalerensis, commonly known as the Escaler's pleurothallis, is a species of orchid native to the cloud forests of Costa Rica.

It is a small, epiphytic plant that typically grows on trees in humid, shaded environments. The plant features elongated, lance-shaped leaves and produces delicate, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pale yellow. It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique inflorescence structure.

Due to its habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
