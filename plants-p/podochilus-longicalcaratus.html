Podochilus longicalcaratus

Podochilus longicalcaratus, commonly known as the long-spurred orchid, is a species of flowering plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its striking, elongated floral spurs that can reach several centimeters in length. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte or lithophyte, attaching itself to trees or rocks in its natural habitat. Its flowers are usually greenish-yellow with intricate patterns, and it blooms during the warmer months.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
