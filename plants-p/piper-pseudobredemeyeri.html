Piper pseudobredemeyeri

Piper pseudobredemeyeri, commonly known as the Bornean pepper vine, is a member of the Piperaceae family, native to the rainforests of Borneo.

It is a climbing shrub characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves with a glossy surface and prominent veins. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by elongated, narrow berries. Its botanical features make it distinct within the genus Piper, with adaptations suited to its humid, tropical habitat.

The species is of interest to botanists and horticulturists due to its unique morphology and potential ecological role in its native environment.
