Potamogeton foliosus

Potamogeton foliosus, commonly known as the broad-leaved pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in North America.

It has long, slender stems that are often branched and bear opposite, broad, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically arranged in dense clusters near the water's surface. Its leaves are usually smooth and have a slightly waxy texture, which helps reduce water loss.

Potamogeton foliosus plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
