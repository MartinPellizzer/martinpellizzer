Parodia borealis

Parodia borealis, commonly known as the Arctic Hedgehog Cactus, is a species of cactus native to northern regions of North America.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems covered in white, woolly spines. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the summer months, which are typically followed by pale green fruits. Parodia borealis thrives in cold, arid environments and is adapted to withstand harsh winter conditions.

Its unique appearance and resilience make it a notable species in alpine and tundra ecosystems.
