Paeonia daurica

Paeonia daurica, commonly known as the Daurian peony, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of East Asia.

It features upright, hairy stems that bear large, showy flowers in shades of pink, red, and white. The plant has palmately lobed leaves that are divided into three to five segments. Its root system consists of thick, fleshy rhizomes that store nutrients and aid in survival during harsh winters.

Paeonia daurica is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
