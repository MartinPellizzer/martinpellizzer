Passiflora cirrhipes

Passiflora cirrhipes, commonly known as the silky passionflower, is a climbing vine native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, intricate flowers that feature a blend of purple, blue, and white hues, often with a crown of filaments at the center. The plant produces edible, tropical fruits known as passionfruits, which are rich in vitamins and antioxidants. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped and covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Passiflora cirrhipes thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for both ornamental and culinary purposes.
