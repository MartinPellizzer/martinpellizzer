Pilea trianaeana

Pilea trianaeana, commonly known as the Chinese money plant, is a species of flowering plant in the family Araceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia, including China, India, and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its round, shield-shaped leaves that resemble coins, giving it its common name. It produces small, inconspicuous white flowers that are followed by bright orange berries.

Pilea trianaeana is often cultivated as a decorative houseplant due to its attractive foliage and ease of care.
