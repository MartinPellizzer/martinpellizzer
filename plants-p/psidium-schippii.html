Psidium schippii

Psidium schippii, commonly known as the Schippi's guava, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its small, oval-shaped leaves and fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, edible fruits that are typically green when unripe and turn yellow or red when mature. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit yield.

Psidium schippii is also valued for its potential medicinal properties and adaptability to various soil types.
