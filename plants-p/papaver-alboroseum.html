Papaver alboroseum

Papaver alboroseum, commonly known as the white poppy, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Papaveraceae family, which includes other well-known species like the opium poppy. The plant is characterized by its pale white or cream-colored flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are typically lobed and have a slightly hairy texture.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its delicate appearance and unique coloration.
