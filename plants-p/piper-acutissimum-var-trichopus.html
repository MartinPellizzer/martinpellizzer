Piper acutissimum var. trichopus

Piper acutissimum var. trichopus, commonly known as the creeping Indian pepper, is a tropical vine native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its trailing habit and small, opposite, ovate leaves with a glossy surface. The plant produces tiny, greenish-yellow flowers followed by small, round berries that turn red when mature. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

The species is well adapted to humid, shaded environments and thrives in tropical climates.
