Polygala negrii

Polygala negrii, commonly known as the Negri's milkweed, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia, particularly China.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its delicate, often pink or white flowers that resemble those of milkweeds. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its leaves are opposite and lance-shaped, with a smooth texture.

Polygala negrii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
