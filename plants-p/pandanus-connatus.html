Pandanus connatus

Pandanus connatus, commonly known as the New Caledonia pandanus, is a flowering plant native to the island of New Caledonia.

It is a member of the Pandanaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and long, narrow leaves that form a dense crown. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or white, arranged in panicles at the top of the plant. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found growing near coastal areas or in moist forest understories.

The leaves of Pandanus connatus are used in traditional crafts and have cultural significance among the indigenous communities of New Caledonia.
