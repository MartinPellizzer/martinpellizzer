Pseuderanthemum albocoeruleum

Pseuderanthemum albocoeruleum, commonly known as the blue-flowered pseuderanthemum, is a species of flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue-violet flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows as an erect shrub with opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are tubular and have two lips, with the upper lip being shorter than the lower lip.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
