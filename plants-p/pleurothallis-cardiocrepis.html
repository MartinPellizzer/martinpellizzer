Pleurothallis cardiocrepis

Pleurothallis cardiocrepis, commonly known as the heart-shaped orchid, is a species native to the cloud forests of Central and South America.

It is a small epiphytic orchid that thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant features slender stems and lanceolate leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence. Its flowers are small and typically white or pale pink, arranged in a raceme.

Due to its delicate appearance and specific habitat requirements, it is a rare and somewhat elusive species in cultivation.
