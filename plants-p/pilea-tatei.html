Pilea tatei

Pilea tatei, commonly known as the Chinese money plant, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to China.

It features rosettes of rounded, waxy leaves that resemble small coins, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers that appear in spring. It thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular houseplant.

Pilea tatei is known for its ease of care and ability to propagate through leaf cuttings.
