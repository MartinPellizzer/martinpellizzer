Panicum anceps var. pubescens

Panicum anceps var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy panicgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and dense, fluffy panicles that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as wetlands and swampy areas. Its leaves are narrow and covered with fine, soft hairs, contributing to its common name.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
