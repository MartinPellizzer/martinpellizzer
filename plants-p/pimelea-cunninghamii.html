Pimelea cunninghamii

Pimelea cunninghamii, commonly known as the golden wattle, is a shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant has narrow, linear leaves and produces small, clustered inflorescences. It is well adapted to dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal and heathland environments.

The golden wattle is an important species in Australian flora and holds cultural significance for Indigenous communities.
