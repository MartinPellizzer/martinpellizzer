Picris damascena var. homocarpa

Picris damascena var. homocarpa, commonly known as the Persian carpetweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a low-growing annual herb with slender, creeping stems that often root at the nodes. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, and its leaves are lance-shaped with serrated edges. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in gardens and along roadsides.

Due to its rapid growth and ability to spread easily, it is considered a weed in many regions.
