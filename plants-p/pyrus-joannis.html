Pyrus joannis

Pyrus joannis, commonly known as the Joannis pear, is a species of flowering plant in the family Rosaceae.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, particularly in the Pamir and Tian Shan ranges. The plant is characterized by its ornamental value, featuring showy white flowers that bloom in early spring. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a serrated margin.

The fruit, a small pear-like berry, is typically yellow or red when mature and is edible.
