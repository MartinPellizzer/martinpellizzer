Piper anomalum

Piper anomalum, commonly known as the "wild pepper" or "Malay pepper," is a climbing shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species. The plant is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and small, clustered greenish-white flowers. Its fruits are small berries that turn red when mature, containing aromatic seeds used in traditional medicine.

Piper anomalum is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in local remedies for various ailments.
