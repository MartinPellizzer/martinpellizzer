Phyllanthus aridus

Phyllanthus aridus, commonly known as the desert phyllanthus, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its small, clustered leaves and yellowish flowers that bloom during the dry season. The plant is adapted to survive in harsh, drought-prone environments, with deep root systems that help it access water from deeper soil layers. Its leaves are often covered in a waxy cuticle to reduce water loss, and it produces small, hard seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Phyllanthus aridus is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional medicine.
