Pulsatilla pratensis subsp. ucrainica

Pulsatilla pratensis subsp.

ucrainica, commonly known as the Ukrainian pasqueflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the temperate regions of Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its showy, showy yellow to orange flowers that bloom in early spring.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and open woodlands, preferring well-drained soils. It has a short, stout stem with palmately divided leaves and produces follicular fruits that contain several seeds.

This subspecies is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its early blooming and distinctive appearance.
