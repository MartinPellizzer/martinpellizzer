Potentilla cristata

Potentilla cristata, commonly known as the crested potentilla, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, branched stems that give it a unique, almost tree-like appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are typically narrow and arranged in a spiral pattern along the stems.

Potentilla cristata is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
