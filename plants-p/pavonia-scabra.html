Pavonia scabra

Pavonia scabra, commonly known as rough pavonia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, sandy soils along streambanks and roadsides. The plant features showy, pink to red bract-like flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are ovate to lanceolate, with a rough, hairy texture that gives it its common name.

Pavonia scabra is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
