Potamogeton × nitens f. stenobasis

Potamogeton × nitens f. stenobasis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved pondweed, is a hybrid aquatic plant characterized by its narrow, elongated leaves.

It is typically found in freshwater habitats such as lakes, ponds, and slow-moving rivers. The plant has a submerged growth habit, with long, flexible stems that often root at the nodes. Its leaves are usually arranged spirally along the stem and can vary in width depending on environmental conditions.

It plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
