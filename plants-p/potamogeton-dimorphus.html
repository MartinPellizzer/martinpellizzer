Potamogeton dimorphus

Potamogeton dimorphus, commonly known as the variable pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant found in freshwater ecosystems.

It is characterized by its long, ribbon-like leaves that are often arranged in pairs along the stem. The plant produces small, green flowers that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in shallow, slow-moving waters and is an important component of aquatic habitats.

Potamogeton dimorphus plays a role in water filtration and provides habitat for various aquatic organisms.
