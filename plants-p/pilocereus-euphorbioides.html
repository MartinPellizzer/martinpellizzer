Pilocereus euphorbioides

Pilocereus euphorbioides, commonly known as the night blooming cactus, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a member of the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its spiny stems and nocturnal flowers. The plant produces large, fragrant white flowers that open at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. It thrives in arid environments and requires well-draining soil and minimal water.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and unique blooming pattern.
