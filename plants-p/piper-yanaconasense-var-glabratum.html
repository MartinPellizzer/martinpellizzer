Piper yanaconasense var. glabratum

Piper yanaconasense var. glabratum, commonly known as the Yanaconasense pepper plant, is a variant of the Piper yanaconasense species native to the Andean region of South America.

It is characterized by its smooth, glabrous leaves and small, clustered flowers that typically bloom in greenish-white or yellow hues. The plant produces tiny, edible berries that are often used in local traditional medicine and culinary practices. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is valued for its aromatic properties.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
