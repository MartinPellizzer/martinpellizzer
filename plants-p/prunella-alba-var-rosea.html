Prunella alba var. rosea

Prunella alba var. rosea, commonly known as the pink dead-nettle, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is a member of the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, two-lipped flowers that range in color from white to pink, with the varietal name "rosea" referring to its pinkish hue. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows.

Prunella alba var. rosea is often found in disturbed soils and can spread through both seeds and rhizomes.
