Phoebe yunnanensis

Phoebe yunnanensis, commonly known as the Yunnan beech, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are typically ovate to elliptic in shape. The tree produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by oval, dark brown nuts enclosed in a cupule. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in mountainous regions.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
