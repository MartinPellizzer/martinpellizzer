Potentilla fragarioides var. incisa

Potentilla fragarioides var. incisa, commonly known as the cutleaf cinquefoil, is a perennial shrub native to North America.

It features compound leaves with serrated edges, often divided into five lobes, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its attractive foliage and long bloom period.
