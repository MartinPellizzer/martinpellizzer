Pimelea rupestris

Pimelea rupestris, commonly known as the rock pimelea, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is a shrub that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in coastal or inland areas. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers clustered in dense spikes, which bloom in spring. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often covered in fine hairs.

Pimelea rupestris is adapted to dry conditions and is sometimes used in native gardens for its ornamental value.
