Pieris shanica

Pieris shanica, commonly known as the Shanica pieris, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its attractive, leathery leaves that are often bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pink, appearing in early spring. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full shade.

Pieris shanica is valued in horticulture for its ornamental foliage and seasonal blooms.
