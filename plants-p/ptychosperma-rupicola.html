Ptychosperma rupicola

Ptychosperma rupicola, commonly known as the rock cycad, is a member of the Cycadaceae family and is native to New Caledonia.

It is a slow-growing, evergreen plant characterized by its robust, trunk-like stem and pinnately compound leaves. The plant features a distinctive, thick, fibrous root system that helps it survive in rocky, nutrient-poor soils. Its unique morphology includes a crown of long, narrow leaves that can reach several meters in length.

Ptychosperma rupicola is notable for its ecological significance and is considered a relict species with limited distribution.
