Penstemon canescens subsp. brittoniorum

Penstemon canescens subsp.

brittoniorum, commonly known as the Britton's beardtongue, is a perennial flowering plant native to the southwestern United States. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces showy, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to purple.

The plant typically thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for xeriscaping. Its foliage consists of gray-green, hairy leaves that help reduce water loss in arid environments.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
