Psidium oerstedianum

Psidium oerstedianum, commonly known as the Oersted's guava, is a small evergreen shrub or tree native to the Caribbean and northern South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, elliptical leaves and clusters of white, fragrant flowers. The plant produces small, edible fruits that are similar in appearance to guavas but have a distinct flavor. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often grown for its ornamental value and fruit.

The species is also valued for its potential medicinal properties in traditional medicine.
