Padus cornuta var. typica

Padus cornuta var. typica, commonly known as the horned dogwood.

It is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America, characterized by its distinctive branched, horn-like stems that give it its common name. The plant produces small, white or pink flowers in clusters during the spring, followed by red or purple berries in the fall. Its leaves are ovate with serrated edges and turn yellow in the autumn.

It is often found in moist woodlands and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
