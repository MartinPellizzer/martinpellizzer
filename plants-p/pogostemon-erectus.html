Pogostemon erectus

Pogostemon erectus, commonly known as the upright pogostemon, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its square stems, opposite leaves, and small, tubular flowers that are often clustered in whorls. It is typically found in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or wetlands.

Pogostemon erectus is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
