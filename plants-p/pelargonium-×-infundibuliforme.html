Pelargonium × infundibuliforme

Pelargonium × infundibuliforme, commonly known as the cascading geranium, is a hybrid plant in the Geraniaceae family.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and vibrant, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant features finely toothed, lobed leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is well-suited for containers, hanging baskets, and garden borders.

Its botanical appeal lies in its compact form and prolonged flowering period, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
