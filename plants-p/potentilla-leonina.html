Potentilla leonina

Potentilla leonina, commonly known as the lion's mouth, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a low-growing shrub that typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features upright, hairy stems and produces clusters of small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. Its leaves are oval-shaped, serrated, and covered in a fine layer of white down.

Potentilla leonina is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its hardiness and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
