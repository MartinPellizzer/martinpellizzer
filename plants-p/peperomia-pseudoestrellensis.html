Peperomia pseudoestrellensis

Peperomia pseudoestrellensis, commonly known as the star-shaped peperomia, is a compact, low-growing perennial plant native to tropical regions of Central and South America.

It features thick, waxy leaves that are often arranged in a rosette pattern, with some varieties displaying a striking star-like appearance on the leaf surface. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically borne on short, erect spikes. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Peperomia pseudoestrellensis is popular as a houseplant due to its attractive foliage and relatively easy care requirements.
