Perfolisa obtusifolia

Perfolisa obtusifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved perfoliate, is a perennial herb native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It features opposite, ovate to elliptic leaves that are often hairy and have a blunt tip. The plant produces small, clustered flowers with five petals, typically in shades of pink or white. Its leaves are characterized by their distinctive perfoliate base, where the leaf stalks fuse around the stem.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands or along stream banks.
