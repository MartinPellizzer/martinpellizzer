Passerina cantabrica

Passerina cantabrica, commonly known as the Cantabrian bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone-rich soils and is often found in mountainous regions. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds.

Passerina cantabrica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
