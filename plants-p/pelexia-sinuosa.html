Pelexia sinuosa

Pelexia sinuosa, commonly known as the zigzag sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the wetlands of North America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, zigzagging stems that are often covered in fine, hair-like structures. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in dense clusters during the summer months. Its leaves are narrow, linear, and have a slightly zigzagged appearance along their edges.

Pelexia sinuosa thrives in moist, boggy environments and is often found in marshes and along the edges of ponds.
