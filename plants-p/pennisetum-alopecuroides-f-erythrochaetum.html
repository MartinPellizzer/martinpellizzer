Pennisetum alopecuroides f. erythrochaetum

Pennisetum alopecuroides f. erythrochaetum, commonly known as red hair grass, is a variant of the fountain grass species native to Africa.

It is characterized by its striking red-colored inflorescences, which contrast with its silver-green foliage. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces fluffy, bottlebrush-like seed heads that add visual interest throughout the growing season. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

This cultivar is often used in landscapes for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
