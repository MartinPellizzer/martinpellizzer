Piper mannii

Piper mannii, commonly known as the West African pepper or African long pepper, is a flowering plant in the Piperaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa and is characterized by its climbing habit and pungent, peppery fruits. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into elongated, reddish berries. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a rough texture with prominent veins.

The plant is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
