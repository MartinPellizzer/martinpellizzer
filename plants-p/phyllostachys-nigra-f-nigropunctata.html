Phyllostachys nigra f. nigropunctata

Phyllostachys nigra f. nigropunctata, commonly known as the black bamboo with black dots, is a cultivar of the bamboo genus Phyllostachys.

It is characterized by its dark black culms that are densely covered with small, black tubercles or dots, giving it a distinctive appearance. This variety is native to China and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes. The plant grows as a clump-forming bamboo, reaching heights of up to 6 meters, and prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

It is a hardy species that is relatively easy to care for, making it a popular choice for bamboo enthusiasts.
