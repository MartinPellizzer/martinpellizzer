Piper podandrum

Piper podandrum, commonly known as the mountain pepper, is a climbing vine native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many species of pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its slender stems, heart-shaped leaves, and small, greenish-white flowers. Its fruits are typically three-lobed berries that mature to a dark purple color.

Piper podandrum is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties.
