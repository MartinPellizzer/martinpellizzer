Pithecellobium calostachys

Pithecellobium calostachys, commonly known as the golden tamarind, is a shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellow flowers. The plant produces edible, pod-like fruits that contain sweet, sticky pulp and seeds. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of food in traditional settings.

Its growth habit and botanical features make it well-suited for tropical and subtropical climates.
