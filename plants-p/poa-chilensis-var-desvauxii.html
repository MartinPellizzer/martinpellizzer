Poa chilensis var. desvauxii

Poa chilensis var. desvauxii, commonly known as the Desvaux's bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to South America, particularly Chile.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and dark green, narrow leaves. The plant produces slender, spike-like inflorescences that bear small, two-lobed florets. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in native habitats.
