Pandorea baileyana

Pandorea baileyana, commonly known as the pink trumpet vine, is a climbing plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its showy, tubular, pink or purple flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil.

Pandorea baileyana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators like birds and insects.
