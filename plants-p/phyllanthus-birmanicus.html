Phyllanthus birmanicus

Phyllanthus birmanicus, commonly known as Burmese phyllanthus, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small shrub that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant features simple, opposite leaves with a smooth surface and a slightly waxy texture. Its small, greenish-yellow flowers are clustered in dense inflorescences, and it produces small, hard capsules as fruit.

It is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along stream banks.
