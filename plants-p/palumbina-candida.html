Palumbina candida

Palumbina candida, commonly known as the white palumbina, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate white petals and small, clustered flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a smooth, glossy surface.

Palumbina candida is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
