Potentilla gracillima

Potentilla gracillima, commonly known as the slender cinquefoil, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its finely divided, feathery leaves and clusters of small, yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats. It has a compact growth habit and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.

Potentilla gracillima is also known for its hardiness and ability to tolerate harsh environmental conditions.
