Pandanus furcatus

Pandanus furcatus, commonly known as the bottlebrush pandanus, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and long, narrow leaves that form a rosette at the top. The plant produces panicles of small, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or greenish in color. Its unique appearance makes it a popular ornamental plant in tropical gardens.

The fruit, which is a fibrous structure containing seeds, is also used in traditional crafts and food preparation.
