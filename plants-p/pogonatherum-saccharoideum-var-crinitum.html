Pogonatherum saccharoideum var. crinitum

Pogonatherum saccharoideum var. crinitum, commonly known as the hairy bamboo grass, is a perennial grass native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and long, narrow leaves with a hairy texture. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas.

Its unique appearance and adaptability make it a notable species in botanical studies and ecological research.
