Proclesia hartwegiana

Proclesia hartwegiana, commonly known as the Hartweg's cactus, is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its spiny stems and clusters of yellow flowers. The plant is typically found in arid and semi-arid regions, where it adapts to harsh environmental conditions. Its structure includes areoles, which are specialized structures that bear spines, flowers, and sometimes branches.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in its native habitat.
