Panicum imperfectum

Panicum imperfectum, commonly known as the imperfect grass, is a species of flowering plant in the grass family Poaceae.

It is native to parts of Africa and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences that are typically green or brown in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, disturbed habitats.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
