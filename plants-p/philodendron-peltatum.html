Philodendron peltatum

Philodendron peltatum, commonly known as the creeping philodendron, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, glossy leaves that grow in a trailing or climbing habit. The plant has a thick, waxy stem and produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers. Its leaves are typically a deep green with a velvety texture.

Philodendron peltatum thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.
