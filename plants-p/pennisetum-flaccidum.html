Pennisetum flaccidum

Pennisetum flaccidum, commonly known as the drooping fountain grass, is a perennial grass species native to Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, arching stems and fluffy, silver-gray plumes that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its foliage is narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue.

Pennisetum flaccidum is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
