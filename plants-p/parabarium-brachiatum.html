Parabarium brachiatum

Parabarium brachiatum, commonly known as the broad-leaved parabarium, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 centimeters in diameter. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom during the wet season. Its roots are known for their medicinal properties, used traditionally in local remedies.

Parabarium brachiatum thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in dense rainforest undergrowth.
