Populus sieboldii

Populus sieboldii, commonly known as the Japanese poplar, is a deciduous tree native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Salicaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and heart-shaped leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish-green catkins in early spring, which are its primary means of pollen dispersal. Its bark is smooth and gray, becoming fissured with age, and it is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Populus sieboldii is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been introduced to other parts of the world for landscaping purposes.
