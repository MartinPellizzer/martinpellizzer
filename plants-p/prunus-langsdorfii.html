Prunus langsdorfii

Prunus langsdorfii, commonly known as the Langsdorf cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, fragrant white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, edible berries that are typically red when mature. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it is not widely available.
