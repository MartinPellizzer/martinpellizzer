Pavonia subhastata var. eglandulosa

Pavonia subhastata var. eglandulosa, commonly known as the glandular pavonia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, pink to red bract-like flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture, and its stems are often covered in fine, glandular hairs. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained, sandy or loamy soils.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
