Pinus rhaetica

Pinus rhaetica, commonly known as the Rhaetian pine, is a coniferous tree native to the Alps, particularly in Italy and Austria.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and long, needle-like leaves that are typically arranged in bundles of five. The tree produces cone-shaped fruits that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in high-altitude environments with cool, moist conditions.

Pinus rhaetica is often found in mountainous regions and is valued for its ecological role in forest ecosystems.
