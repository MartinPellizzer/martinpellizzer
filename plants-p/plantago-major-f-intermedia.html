Plantago major f. intermedia

Plantago major f. intermedia, commonly known as the greater plantain, is a variant of the common plantain species.

It is a perennial herb characterized by broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white, silky hairs. The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in a spike, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and gardens.

This plant is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally for its healing benefits.
