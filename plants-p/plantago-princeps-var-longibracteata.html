Plantago princeps var. longibracteata

Plantago princeps var. longibracteata, commonly known as the long bract plantain, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. The plant produces erect flowering stems topped with a dense cluster of small, greenish flowers. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

This variety is notable for its distinctive bracts that enclose the inflorescence, giving it its common name.
