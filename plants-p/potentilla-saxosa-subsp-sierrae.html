Potentilla saxosa subsp. sierrae

Potentilla saxosa subsp.

sierrae, commonly known as Sierra potentilla, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of western North America. It typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils at high elevations, often in alpine or subalpine habitats.

The plant features small, upright stems with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are yellow, occurring in clusters, and bloom in early summer.

This subspecies is valued for its hardiness and ornamental appeal in gardens with suitable conditions.
