Populus brandegeei var. glabra

Populus brandegeei var. glabra, commonly known as the smooth poplar, is a variant of the poplar genus native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its smooth, often grayish bark and heart-shaped leaves with a serrated margin. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils along stream banks and in floodplain forests. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by fluffy seed clusters that aid in wind dispersal.

This species is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
