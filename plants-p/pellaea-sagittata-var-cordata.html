Pellaea sagittata var. cordata

Pellaea sagittata var. cordata, commonly known as the heart-shaped fern, is a variant of the common sword fern.

It is a perennial plant native to the southeastern United States, particularly Florida and the coastal regions of the Carolinas. The plant features delicate, lance-shaped leaves that are often slightly curled at the tips. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas or near water sources.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and ecological restoration projects.
