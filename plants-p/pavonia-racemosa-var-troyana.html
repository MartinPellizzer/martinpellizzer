Pavonia racemosa var. troyana

Pavonia racemosa var. troyana, commonly known as the Troyana False Crape Myrtle, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, clustered flowers. The plant produces pink to red bract-like flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are typically ovate to lanceolate, with a slightly hairy texture.

It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often used in ornamental landscaping for its vibrant floral display.
