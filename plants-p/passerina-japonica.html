Passerina japonica

Passerina japonica, commonly known as the Japanese bellflower, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Passerina japonica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
