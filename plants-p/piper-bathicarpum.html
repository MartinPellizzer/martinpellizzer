Piper bathicarpum

Piper bathicarpum, commonly known as the deep-red pepper or black pepper plant, is a climbing vine native to the wet tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its slender, woody stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that develop into round, red berries containing a single seed. The berries are harvested and dried to produce the spice known as black pepper, which is widely used in culinary and medicinal applications.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both agriculture and traditional medicine.
