Pereskia bahiensis

Pereskia bahiensis, commonly known as the Bahia cactus, is a species of cactus native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems that are covered with spines, typical of many cacti. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom during the warmer months. It is adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water once established.

Pereskia bahiensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and resilience in dry climates.
