Pediocactus simpsonii f. campestris

Pediocactus simpsonii f. campestris, commonly known as the Simpson's prickly pear, is a fascinating cactus species native to the deserts of the southwestern United States.

It features a short, cylindrical stem covered in prominent, spiny areoles, which are characteristic of the Cactaceae family. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, adding a striking contrast to its green, spiky structure. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun, making it well-suited for arid environments.

Its unique morphology and adaptability to harsh conditions make it a popular choice for xeriscaping and succulent collections.
