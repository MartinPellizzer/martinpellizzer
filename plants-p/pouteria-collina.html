Pouteria collina

Pouteria collina, commonly known as the Brazilian nut tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Amazon basin.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of yellowish-green flowers. The tree produces edible seeds enclosed in a hard, woody capsule, which are commercially harvested as Brazil nuts. Pouteria collina thrives in humid, nutrient-rich soils and requires a warm, tropical climate.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
