Paropsia grandiflora

Paropsia grandiflora, commonly known as the grandiflora paropsia, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its showy, large, and often brightly colored flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full sunlight. Its foliage consists of broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often a deep green.

Paropsia grandiflora is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
