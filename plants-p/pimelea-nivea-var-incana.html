Pimelea nivea var. incana

Pimelea nivea var. incana, commonly known as the silver leaf pimelea, is a low-growing shrub native to the south-western region of Western Australia.

It features small, white flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring, often appearing in clusters along the stems. The leaves are covered in a fine, silvery-white coating, giving the plant its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in heathland and coastal habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and seasonal blooms.
