Piper otophyllum

Piper otophyllum, commonly known as the Mexican pepper plant, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes various species of pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves that are often slightly hairy. It produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into small, round berries.

The berries are typically harvested when they turn red and are used in traditional medicine and culinary applications.
