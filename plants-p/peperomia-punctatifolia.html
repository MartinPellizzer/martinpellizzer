Peperomia punctatifolia

Peperomia punctatifolia, commonly known as the speckled peperomia, is a tropical flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its compact, upright growth habit and glossy, dark green leaves with distinctive white or silver speckles. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or red, emerging from the leaf axils. It thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Peperomia punctatifolia is relatively low maintenance and drought-tolerant, requiring minimal watering and pruning.
