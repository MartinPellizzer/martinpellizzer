Polystachya leonardiana

Polystachya leonardiana, commonly known as the Leonard's spikegrass, is a member of the orchid family, Orchidaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Central and South America, where it typically grows in humid, shaded environments. The plant features a slender rhizome with several erect, branching stems that bear lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are small and clustered in spike-like inflorescences, often with a greenish or yellowish hue.

Polystachya leonardiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in tropical gardens.
