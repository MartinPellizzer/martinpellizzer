Phaseolus obliquifolius

Phaseolus obliquifolius, commonly known as the zigzag bean, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to Central and South America and is characterized by its distinctive, zigzagging leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers followed by elongated, bean-like pods. It is an annual herb that typically grows in dry, open habitats.

The seeds of Phaseolus obliquifolius are edible and have been used in traditional cuisines in its native regions.
