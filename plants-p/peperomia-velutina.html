Peperomia velutina

Peperomia velutina, commonly known as velvet peperomia, is a compact, low-growing plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture, giving it its common name. The leaves are typically dark green with a glossy surface and can have a slightly waxy appearance. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers in shades of green or white, which are usually hidden among the foliage.

This plant is well-suited for indoor cultivation due to its tolerance for low light and minimal water requirements.
