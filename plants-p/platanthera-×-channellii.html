Platanthera × channellii

Platanthera × channellii, commonly known as the hybrid marsh orchid, is a rare and endangered species native to parts of Europe.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in wet, calcareous habitats such as bogs and marshes. Its flowers are usually white or pale yellow, with a distinctive lip that aids in pollination by insects.

Due to habitat loss and environmental changes, it faces significant conservation challenges.
