Pleione griffithii

Pleione griffithii, commonly known as the Griffith's pleione, is a species of orchid native to the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its compact, pseudobulbous structure and produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of pink and white. The plant typically blooms in spring, with petals that are often recurved and adorned with intricate patterns. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in shaded forest habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated by enthusiasts but is also threatened by habitat loss.
