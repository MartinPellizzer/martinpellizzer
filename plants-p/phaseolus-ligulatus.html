Phaseolus ligulatus

Phaseolus ligulatus, commonly known as the scarlet runner bean, is a herbaceous annual plant native to South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its climbing habit and large, showy flowers. The plant produces elongated pods that contain edible seeds, which are often used in culinary applications. Its leaves are palmately compound, and the plant thrives in warm, sunny conditions with well-drained soil.

Phaseolus ligulatus is valued both for its ornamental appeal and its nutritional contribution to diets in many regions.
