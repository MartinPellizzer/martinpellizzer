Panicum pauciflorum

Panicum pauciflorum, commonly known as the few-flowered panic grass, is a perennial grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and other moist habitats, exhibiting a clumping growth habit. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear a limited number of flowers, giving it its scientific name. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green coloration.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
