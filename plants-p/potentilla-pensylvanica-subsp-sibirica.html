Potentilla pensylvanica subsp. sibirica

Potentilla pensylvanica subsp.

sibirica, commonly known as Siberian cinquefoil, is a perennial shrub native to the colder regions of Asia and North America. It features small, yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, typically arranged in clusters of five.

The plant has compound leaves with five leaflets, which are usually serrated and covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or rocky habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to harsh environmental conditions.
