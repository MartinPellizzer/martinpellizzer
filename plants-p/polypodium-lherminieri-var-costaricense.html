Polypodium lherminieri var. costaricense

Polypodium lherminieri var. costaricense, commonly known as the Costa Rican shield fern, is a terrestrial fern native to Central America.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, shield-shaped fronds. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors and rocky crevices. Its rhizomes are often covered in a thick, protective layer of sori, which are clusters of spore-producing structures.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in tropical gardens.
