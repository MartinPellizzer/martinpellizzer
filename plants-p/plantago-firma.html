Plantago firma

Plantago firma, commonly known as broadleaf plantain, is a perennial herb native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in a dense spike, which are wind-pollinated. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is commonly found in meadows, roadsides, and disturbed areas.

Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their soothing and healing properties.
