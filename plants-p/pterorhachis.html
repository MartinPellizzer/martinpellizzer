Pterorhachis

Pterorhachis, commonly known as the "painted fern," is a genus of flowering plants in the family Polygonaceae.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful bracts that resemble petals, giving it a floral appearance. The plants typically have simple, alternate leaves that are often arranged in a rosette pattern. They are native to various regions in the Americas, particularly in tropical and subtropical areas.

Pterorhachis species are often cultivated for their ornamental value due to their vibrant colors and unique foliage.
