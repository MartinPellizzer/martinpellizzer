Paspalum ramboi

Paspalum ramboi, commonly known as the slender paspalum, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its fine, wiry leaves and small, spike-like inflorescences. This plant typically grows in moist to wet soils, often found in marshy areas or along waterways. Its rhizomes allow it to spread vegetatively, forming dense mats that can stabilize soil.

Paspalum ramboi is valued for its ecological role in wetland habitats and its use in erosion control.
