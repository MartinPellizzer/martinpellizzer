Pavonia subhastata subsp. paludosa

Pavonia subhastata subsp.

paludosa, commonly known as the marsh pavonia, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States. It features slender, erect stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces showy, pink to red flowers in terminal clusters, which bloom from late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, swamps, and along stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species for ecological studies and wetland restoration projects.
