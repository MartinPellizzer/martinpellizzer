Perilla ocymoides f. discolor

Perilla ocymoides f. discolor, commonly known as purple perilla, is a herbaceous plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive purple-tinged leaves, which are often used in traditional cuisine and herbal medicine. The plant belongs to the Lamiaceae family, sharing features such as square stems and opposite leaves with other members of the family. Its flowers are small and clustered in whorls, typically blooming in late summer.

The f. discolor form is notable for its striking leaf coloration, which sets it apart from the typical green variety of Perilla ocymoides.
