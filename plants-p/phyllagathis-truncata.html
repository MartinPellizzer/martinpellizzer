Phyllagathis truncata

Phyllagathis truncata, commonly known as the blunt-leaf mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Myrtaceae and is characterized by its small, leathery leaves and yellowish flowers. The plant attaches itself to host trees, deriving some of its nutrients through specialized roots. It produces small, red berries that are dispersed by birds.

Due to its parasitic nature, it can have significant ecological impacts on its host species.
