Phlox thomsonii

Phlox thomsonii, commonly known as Thomson's phlox, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its showy clusters of pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and has narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Thomson's phlox is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
