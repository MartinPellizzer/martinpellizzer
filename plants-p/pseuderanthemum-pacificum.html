Pseuderanthemum pacificum

Pseuderanthemum pacificum, commonly known as the Pacific pseuderanthemum, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its compact, evergreen shrub form. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, arranged in clusters at the ends of its branches. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a slightly hairy surface.

It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories or along stream banks.
