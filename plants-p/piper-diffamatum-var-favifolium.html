Piper diffamatum var. favifolium

Piper diffamatum var. favifolium, commonly known as the fragrant pepper plant, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dark green, glossy leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces small, peppery fruits that are used in traditional medicine and cuisine. It thrives in humid, warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species in both horticulture and pharmacological research.
