Panicularia laxa

Panicularia laxa, commonly known as the slender panicled bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its delicate, panicled inflorescence composed of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its drought tolerance.

Panicularia laxa is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
