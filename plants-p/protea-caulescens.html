Protea caulescens

Protea caulescens, commonly known as the stalked protea, is a striking South African plant belonging to the Proteaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescences composed of numerous white to pink flowers arranged in a spherical or cylindrical shape. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in areas with high sunlight exposure. Its foliage consists of narrow, leathery leaves that are arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem.

Protea caulescens is valued not only for its ornamental appeal but also for its ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
