Pithecellobium panurense

Pithecellobium panurense, commonly known as the Panura tree, is a species of leguminous shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the genus Pithecellobium, which includes various species of small trees and shrubs characterized by their compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant is notable for its ability to fix nitrogen through symbiotic relationships with rhizobia bacteria, contributing to soil fertility. Its flowers are typically small and clustered, often displaying a yellow or white color, while the fruit is a woody pod containing edible seeds.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental potential and has been studied for its potential in ecological restoration projects.
