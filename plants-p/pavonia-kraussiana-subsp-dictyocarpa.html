Pavonia kraussiana subsp. dictyocarpa

Pavonia kraussiana subsp.

dictyocarpa, commonly known as the Cape honeysuckle, is a species native to southern Africa. It is a shrub characterized by its opposite, lance-shaped leaves and showy, tubular flowers that are typically red or orange in color.

The flowers are borne in clusters and are adapted for pollination by birds, particularly sunbirds. This plant thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, often found in coastal and inland areas.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract wildlife.
