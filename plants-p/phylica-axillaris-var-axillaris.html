Phylica axillaris var. axillaris

Phylica axillaris var. axillaris, commonly known as the coastal bottlebrush, is a shrub native to the coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive bottle-shaped stems and dense clusters of red, brush-like flowers. The plant thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is well adapted to coastal conditions, including salt spray and strong winds. Its foliage consists of narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize coastal dunes.
