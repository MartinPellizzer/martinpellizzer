Philodendron heleniae

Philodendron heleniae, commonly known as the Helen's Philodendron, is a tropical climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It features heart-shaped, waxy leaves that are typically deep green with a slightly variegated pattern. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by bright red berries, which are an important food source for local wildlife. It thrives in warm, humid environments with bright, indirect light.

Its rapid growth and attractive foliage make it a popular choice for indoor and outdoor gardens.
