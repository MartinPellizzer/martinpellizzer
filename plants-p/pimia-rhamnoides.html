Pimia rhamnoides

Pimia rhamnoides, commonly known as the white-flowered buckthorn, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and small, white or yellowish flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces small, dark berries that are edible but have a tart flavor. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and streambanks.

Its botanical features make it an important plant in ecological studies and traditional medicine.
