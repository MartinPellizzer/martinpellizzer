Polygonum zeaense

Polygonum zeaense, commonly known as the slender knotweed, is a flowering plant species native to parts of Asia, including China, Japan, and Korea.

It belongs to the family Polygonaceae, which includes many species of flowering plants known for their distinctive leaf shapes and clusters of small flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or along stream banks. It has alternate, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in dense clusters.

Polygonum zeaense is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
