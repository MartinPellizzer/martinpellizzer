Polygonum paniculatum

Polygonum paniculatum, commonly known as flame flower or red polygonum, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, bright red or orange flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Polygonum paniculatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
