Pinguicula glandulosa

Pinguicula glandulosa, commonly known as the sticky bladderwort, is a carnivorous plant native to wetlands and marshy areas.

It features small, glandular leaves that secrete a sticky substance to trap insects. The plant's structure includes specialized bladders that digest captured prey, providing essential nutrients. It thrives in moist, nutrient-poor environments, often found in temperate regions.

Its unique adaptations make it an interesting subject for both ecological and botanical study.
