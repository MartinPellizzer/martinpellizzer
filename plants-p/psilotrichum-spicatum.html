Psilotrichum spicatum, commonly known as the spicate field spikerush, is a member of the Sphagnum family and is characterized by its dense, spiky growth form.

The plant has a simple morphology with a central stem and leaf-like structures called phyllodes, which are arranged in a spiral pattern. Its reproductive structures, known as sporangia, are borne on specialized stems called spicules, which give the plant its common name.

Psilotrichum spicatum is an important component of peat-forming ecosystems and contributes to the preservation of wetland habitats.
