Pilosella piloselloides subsp. floccosa

Pilosella piloselloides subsp.

floccosa, commonly known as the floccose cat's ear, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads.

The plant features a low-growing rosette of hairy, silver-gray leaves that are often covered in fine, white flocculent hairs. Its flowers are small, yellow, and clustered in a composite head, typical of the sunflower family.

It thrives in dry, open habitats such as meadows and rocky soils, and is often found in disturbed areas.
