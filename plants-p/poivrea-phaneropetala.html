Poivrea phaneropetala

Poivrea phaneropetala, commonly known as the showy honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features slender, arching stems that bear opposite, ovate leaves with a glossy, dark green upper surface and a lighter underside. The plant produces fragrant, tubular flowers in shades of yellow and orange, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its fruit is a small, red, fleshy berry that ripens in late summer.

Poivrea phaneropetala is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained, sunny conditions.
