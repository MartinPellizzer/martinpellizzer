Prunus pseudocerasus f. viridiflora

Prunus pseudocerasus f. viridiflora, commonly known as the green-flowered Chinese date, is a cultivar of the Prunus genus native to China.

It is characterized by its distinctive greenish-white flowers that contrast with its dark green foliage. The plant produces small, edible fruits similar to dates, though they are often less sweet than the common variety. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The foliage and flowers make it a popular choice for gardeners seeking visual interest throughout the growing season.
