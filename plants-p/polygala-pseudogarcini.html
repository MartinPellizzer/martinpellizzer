Polygala pseudogarcini

Polygala pseudogarcini, commonly known as the narrow-leaved milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to southern China.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its slender, often hairy stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, while the flowers are usually pale yellow or greenish-white.

It plays a role in local ecosystems by providing nectar for pollinators and habitat for small organisms.
