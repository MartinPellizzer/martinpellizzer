Potentilla fragiformis var. lucida

Potentilla fragiformis var. lucida, commonly known as the shining cinquefoil, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer, typically from June to July. The plant has slender, upright stems with alternate, pinnately compound leaves that are covered in fine, silvery hairs. Its flowers are usually arranged in clusters of five, giving it the common name "cinquefoil," and it thrives in well-drained, rocky soils.

This variety is notable for its striking appearance and is often found in alpine and subalpine environments.
