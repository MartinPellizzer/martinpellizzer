Piper fruticosum

Piper fruticosum, commonly known as the creeping pepper plant, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and small, fragrant leaves. The plant produces clusters of tiny, greenish-white flowers that develop into small, reddish berries. It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often cultivated as a ground cover or ornamental plant.

Its aromatic leaves and fruits have been used in traditional medicine and culinary applications.
