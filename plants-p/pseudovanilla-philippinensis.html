Pseudovanilla philippinensis

Pseudovanilla philippinensis, commonly known as the Philippine vanillilla, is a climbing vine native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant produces yellow-green flowers that are often clustered in inflorescences, and its leaves are elongated with a waxy surface. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.

Due to its unique fragrance and ornamental value, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists.
