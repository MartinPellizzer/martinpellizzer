Passiflora kapiriensis

Passiflora kapiriensis, commonly known as the Kapiri passionflower, is a climbing vine native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, complex flowers that feature a blend of blue, purple, and white hues, along with intricate patterns resembling veins. The plant produces edible berries that are often consumed fresh or used in culinary applications. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a rough, textured surface.

Passiflora kapiriensis is valued not only for its ornamental beauty but also for its ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
