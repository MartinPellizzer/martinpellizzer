Polygonum chamissonianum

Polygonum chamissonianum, commonly known as the Chamisson's knotweed, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, which includes plants known for their distinctive bracts and small flowers. The plant typically grows in moist environments such as wetlands and stream banks. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are arranged in dense clusters.

It plays a role in supporting local ecosystems by providing habitat and nectar for various pollinators.
