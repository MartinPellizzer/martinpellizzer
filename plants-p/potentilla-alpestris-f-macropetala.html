Potentilla alpestris f. macropetala

Potentilla alpestris f. macropetala, commonly known as the alpine potentilla, is a variant of the alpine cinquefoil.

It is a low-growing, flowering shrub native to mountainous regions of Europe and Asia. The plant features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. Its leaves are typically oval-shaped with a hairy texture, adapted to withstand cold and harsh alpine conditions.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing rocky soils.
