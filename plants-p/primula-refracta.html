Primula refracta

Primula refracta, commonly known as the narrow-leaved primrose, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces clusters of bell-shaped, pale yellow to greenish-yellow flowers. The leaves are typically narrow and elongated, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. It thrives in well-drained, moist soils and prefers partial to full shade, making it suitable for woodland gardens.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in horticultural settings for its delicate floral display.
