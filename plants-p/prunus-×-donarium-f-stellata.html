Prunus × donarium f. stellata

Prunus × donarium f. stellata, commonly known as the star-forming cherry, is a unique cultivar of the Prunus genus.

It is characterized by its distinctive branched, star-like growth pattern, which gives it its common name. The plant typically produces small, pale pink to white flowers in early spring, adding ornamental value to gardens. It is a hybrid species, resulting from the cross between Prunus lusitanica and Prunus padus.

This cultivar is valued for its compact growth habit and aesthetic appeal, making it suitable for small spaces and ornamental landscaping.
