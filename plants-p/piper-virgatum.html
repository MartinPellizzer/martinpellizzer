Piper virgatum

Piper virgatum, commonly known as groundcherry, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other species of flowering plants. The plant produces small, yellow-orange fruits that are often referred to as "cape gooseberries.". Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a slightly rough texture.

Piper virgatum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible fruits.
