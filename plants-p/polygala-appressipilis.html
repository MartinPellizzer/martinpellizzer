Polygala appressipilis

Polygala appressipilis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to southern China.

It belongs to the Polygalaceae family, which is characterized by its distinctive floral structures. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and has small, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are usually pale yellow or greenish-white, and it produces small, woody fruits that contain several seeds.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
