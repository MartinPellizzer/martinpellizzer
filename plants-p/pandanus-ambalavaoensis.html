Pandanus ambalavaoensis

Pandanus ambalavaoensis, commonly known as the Madagascar pandanus, is a tropical plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Pandanaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, narrow leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically clustered in spike-like inflorescences. Its leaves are often used in traditional crafts and for thatching due to their fibrous texture.

The species is notable for its ecological role in stabilizing coastal soils and providing habitat for various wildlife.
