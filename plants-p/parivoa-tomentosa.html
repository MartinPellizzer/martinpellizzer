Parivoa tomentosa

Parivoa tomentosa, commonly known as the woolly parivoa, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that gives it a distinctive, soft appearance. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers during the spring and summer months. Its leaves are typically oval-shaped and covered in a fine, fuzzy layer of hair.

Parivoa tomentosa is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its unique texture and blooming habit.
