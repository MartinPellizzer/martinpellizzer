Pelargonium × amauron

Pelargonium × amauron, commonly known as the ivy-leaved geranium, is a hybrid plant in the Geraniaceae family.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and heart-shaped, dark green leaves with a velvety texture. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink, red, or white, typically blooming throughout the summer. It is often used in hanging baskets and as a ground cover due to its compact form and ornamental value.

Pelargonium × amauron is relatively easy to care for, requiring well-drained soil and regular watering.
