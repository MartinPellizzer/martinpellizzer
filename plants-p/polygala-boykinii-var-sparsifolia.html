Polygala boykinii var. sparsifolia

Polygala boykinii var. sparsifolia, commonly known as the sparseleaf milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Polygalaceae family, which is characterized by its distinctive, often spiny fruits and showy flowers. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun. Its leaves are narrow and sparse, giving it its varietal name, while its flowers are usually pink or purple and arranged in clusters.

It is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
