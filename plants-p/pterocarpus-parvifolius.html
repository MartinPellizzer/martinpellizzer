Pterocarpus parvifolius

Pterocarpus parvifolius, commonly known as the small-leaved ironwood, is a deciduous tree native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its dense, dark timber that is highly valued for its strength and durability. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are typically small and ovate, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. Its flowers are usually small and clustered, producing a distinctive yellow or greenish color, while the fruit is a legume containing several seeds.

This species is often found in dry or semi-deciduous forests and is known for its ecological and economic importance in local communities.
