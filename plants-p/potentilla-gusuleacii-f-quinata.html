Potentilla gusuleacii f. quinata

Potentilla gusuleacii f. quinata, commonly known as the five-leaf potentilla, is a flowering plant belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers. The plant typically features five leaflets, which give it its common name, and it thrives in well-drained, sandy soils. It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia and is often found in alpine environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardiness in cooler climates.
