Passiflora retusa

Passiflora retusa, commonly known as the silky passionflower, is a climbing vine native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its velvety, dark green leaves and striking, intricate flowers that feature a blend of red, blue, and white petals. The plant produces small, dark blue berries that are edible and often used in culinary applications. Passiflora retusa thrives in warm, humid climates and requires support for its vigorous growth.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and greenhouses.
