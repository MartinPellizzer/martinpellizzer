Polygonum collare

Polygonum collare, commonly known as the clustered knotweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, which includes other familiar plants like buckwheat and sorrel. The plant is characterized by its upright stems and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers that appear in late summer. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped with a reddish tint, and the plant often forms dense colonies in moist, disturbed soils.

Polygonum collare is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
