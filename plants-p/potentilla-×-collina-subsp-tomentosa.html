Potentilla × collina subsp. tomentosa

Potentilla × collina subsp.

tomentosa, commonly known as silver cinquefoil, is a low-growing perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of western North America. It is characterized by its dense, silver-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The plant produces showy yellow flowers in early summer, typically arranged in clusters at the ends of its stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine landscapes.

Due to its hardy nature and ornamental value, it is also cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal and ecological benefits.
