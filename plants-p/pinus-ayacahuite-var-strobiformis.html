Pinus ayacahuite var. strobiformis

Pinus ayacahuite var. strobiformis, commonly known as the Mexican white pine, is a variant of the Ayacahuite pine native to central Mexico.

It is a coniferous evergreen tree characterized by its long, slender needles and cones that are typically reddish-brown in color. The plant has a moderate growth rate and thrives in well-drained soils with full sun exposure. Its foliage provides habitat for various wildlife species, and it is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.

The botanical features of this variety make it an important species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
