Peperomia chimantana

Peperomia chimantana, commonly known as the Chimantana peperomia, is a species of flowering plant native to South America, particularly Ecuador.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its compact, upright growth habit. The plant features ovate to lanceolate leaves that are often glossy and have a vibrant green color. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are typically borne in clusters and are followed by dark, persistent berries.

Peperomia chimantana is well-suited for indoor cultivation due to its low maintenance requirements and ability to thrive in bright, indirect light.
