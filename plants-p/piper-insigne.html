Piper insigne

Piper insigne, commonly known as the golden pepper or yellow long pepper, is a climbing shrub native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its slender, vine-like stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces tiny, elongated fruits that range in color from yellow to orange, depending on maturity. These fruits are often used in traditional medicine and as a spice due to their pungent flavor.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and agricultural contexts.
