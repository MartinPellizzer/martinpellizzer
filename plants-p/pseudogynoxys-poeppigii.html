Pseudogynoxys poeppigii

Pseudogynoxys poeppigii, commonly known as the Mexican sunflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to Mexico and Central America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow to orange flowering heads. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, with hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are composite, consisting of ray florets surrounding disk florets, which develop into seeds after pollination.

Pseudogynoxys poeppigii thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens.
