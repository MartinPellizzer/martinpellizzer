Phyllodes argyraea

Phyllodes argyraea, commonly known as silver-leaf wattle, is a species of acacia native to Australia.

It is characterized by its flat, leathery phyllodes that are covered in a silvery-white powdery coating, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. It produces small yellow flowers in late winter to early spring, followed by flat, pod-like fruits.

The species is often used in revegetation projects due to its hardiness and ability to stabilize soil.
