Potentilla hirta subsp. adriatica

Potentilla hirta subsp.

adriatica, commonly known as Adriatic cinquefoil, is a perennial herb native to the Balkan Peninsula. It typically grows in dry, rocky or sandy soils, often in open habitats such as grasslands and scrublands.

The plant features upright stems covered with fine, soft hairs, and its leaves are pinnately divided into five leaflets. It produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in early summer.

The subspecies is distinguished by its compact growth habit and specific adaptations to the Mediterranean climate.
