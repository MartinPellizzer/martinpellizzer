Panicum cordovense

Panicum cordovense, commonly known as the Cordovense grass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces panicles of small, spikelet-based flowers that are typically greenish or brownish in color. It thrives in moist to wet environments, such as marshes and along riverbanks.

Due to its adaptability, it is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects to stabilize soil and prevent erosion.
