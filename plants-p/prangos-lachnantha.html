Prangos lachnantha

Prangos lachnantha, commonly known as the woolly thistle, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and spiny leaves. The plant features a robust stem covered in dense, white, woolly hairs, which help protect it from harsh environmental conditions. Its flowers are typically purple to pink in color, blooming in summer, and it produces small, oval-shaped seeds.

Prangos lachnantha is often found in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes.
