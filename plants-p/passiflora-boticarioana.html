Passiflora boticarioana

Passiflora boticarioana, commonly known as the Boticario passionflower, is a species native to the Andean region of South America.

It is a climbing vine that produces showy, intricate flowers with five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments. The plant's botanical features include a distinctive floral structure with a central receptacle and a unique arrangement of stamens and carpels. It is characterized by its dark green, heart-shaped leaves and produces edible, orange-red berries.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance in its native habitat.
