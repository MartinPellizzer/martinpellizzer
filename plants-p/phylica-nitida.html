Phylica nitida

Phylica nitida, commonly known as the silver leaf bush, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often covered in fine hairs.

Phylica nitida is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for xeriscaping in arid regions.
