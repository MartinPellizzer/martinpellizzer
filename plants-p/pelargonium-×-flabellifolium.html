Pelargonium × flabellifolium

Pelargonium × flabellifolium, commonly known as the African marigold, is a hybrid geranium species originating from South Africa.

It is characterized by its broad, lobed leaves that resemble a fan, hence its species name *flabellifolium*, meaning "fan-like leaf.". The plant produces vibrant yellow or orange flowers, which are typically borne in clusters at the ends of stems. It is a hardy, evergreen shrub that thrives in well-drained soil and full sun.

Pelargonium × flabellifolium is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its attractive foliage and long-lasting blooms.
