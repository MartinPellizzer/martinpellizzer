Pyrus sargentiana

Pyrus sargentiana, commonly known as Sargent's pear, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces edible, sweet, yellowish fruits that ripen in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in moist, open woodlands.

Sargent's pear is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in traditional medicine.
