Pitcairnia patentiflora var. macrantha

Pitcairnia patentiflora var. macrantha, commonly known as the giant pitcairnia, is a striking bromeliad native to the cloud forests of South America.

It is characterized by its large, spathaceous inflorescence that can reach up to 3 meters in height, making it one of the tallest bromeliads. The plant features broad, lance-shaped leaves that form a rosette, often covered in a dense layer of trichomes. Its flowers are typically pink or red, arranged in a showy, terminal cluster.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in tropical gardens and greenhouses.
