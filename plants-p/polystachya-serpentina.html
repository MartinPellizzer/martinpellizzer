Polystachya serpentina

Polystachya serpentina, commonly known as the serpent's tail, is a tropical orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, spiraling inflorescence that resembles a serpent's tail, hence its common name. The plant features elongated, narrow leaves that are typically green with a waxy texture. Its flowers are small and clustered, often in shades of white, pink, and purple.

Polystachya serpentina thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
