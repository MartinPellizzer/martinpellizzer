Potentilla verna var. filiformis

Potentilla verna var. filiformis, commonly known as the slender cinquefoil, is a low-growing perennial plant native to northern regions of Europe and western Siberia.

It typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters and is characterized by its slender, often prostrate stems. The plant features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, usually from June to July. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a distinctive five-lobed shape, giving it the common name "cinquefoil.".

It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in wet meadows, bogs, and stream banks.
