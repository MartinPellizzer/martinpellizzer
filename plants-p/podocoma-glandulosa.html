Podocoma glandulosa

Podocoma glandulosa, commonly known as the glandular podocarp, is a species of flowering plant in the family Podocarpaceae.

It is native to southern Africa, particularly South Africa, where it thrives in coastal and mountainous regions. The plant is characterized by its evergreen foliage, with leaves that are often covered in small, glandular structures. It produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by woody cones containing seeds.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological significance, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
