Pimelea linifolia var. abietina

Pimelea linifolia var. abietina, commonly known as the silverleaf pimelea, is a shrub native to the south-western region of Western Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, silver-gray leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in late winter to early spring, which are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in heathlands and open woodlands.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
