Polygonum nudiflorum

Polygonum nudiflorum, commonly known as the nakedflower smartweed, is a herbaceous plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its upright, branched stems and lance-shaped leaves that are typically hairy. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually greenish-white in color, appearing before the leaves fully develop. It thrives in wet or moist environments, such as marshes and stream banks.

Polygonum nudiflorum is often found in disturbed soils and can spread through both seeds and rhizomes.
