Paepalanthus acanthophyllus

Paepalanthus acanthophyllus, commonly known as the spiny leaf cactus, is a species of flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its spiny, fleshy stems and clusters of small, white flowers. The plant is adapted to arid environments, with thick leaves that store water and reduce transpiration. It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in coastal regions.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its ornamental value.
