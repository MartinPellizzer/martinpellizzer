Plectranthus gerardianus var. brachyanthus

Plectranthus gerardianus var. brachyanthus, commonly known as the woolly mint, is a perennial herb native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy leaves and clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from purple to blue. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers. Its aromatic foliage and ornamental value make it a popular choice for both ornamental and culinary purposes.

It is also known for its medicinal properties, traditionally used in herbal remedies.
