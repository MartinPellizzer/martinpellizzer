Populus grandidentata f. meridionalis

Populus grandidentata f. meridionalis, commonly known as the southern trembling aspen, is a naturally occurring variant of the trembling aspen.

It is characterized by its broader, more robust leaves and slightly shorter stature compared to the typical form. This form thrives in warmer climates and is often found in the southern United States. It exhibits similar ecological roles to other Populus species, such as providing habitat and food for various wildlife.

Its botanical significance lies in its adaptation to different environmental conditions and its role in genetic diversity within the Populus genus.
