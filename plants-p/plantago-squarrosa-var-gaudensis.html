Plantago squarrosa var. gaudensis

Plantago squarrosa var. gaudensis, commonly known as the Gaudens plantain, is a variant of the common plantain that exhibits distinct botanical features.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its broad, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense, white to grayish pubescence. The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in a spike, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

This variant is notable for its adaptability and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
