Pseudoeugenia tenuifolia

Pseudoeugenia tenuifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to eastern Australia and is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and small, fragrant white flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, often found in coastal and subtropical regions. Its bark is smooth and pale, and it produces small, woody capsules that contain aromatic seeds.

Pseudoeugenia tenuifolia is valued for its ornamental qualities and has traditional uses in Indigenous Australian medicine.
