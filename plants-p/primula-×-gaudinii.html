Primula × gaudinii

Primula × gaudinii, commonly known as the Gaudin's primrose, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Primulaceae family.

It is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that typically range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant produces both showy petals and modified leaves called bracts, which contribute to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.

Native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe, it is valued for its ability to bloom in early spring.
