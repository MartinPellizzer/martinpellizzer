Pilocereus foveolatus

Pilocereus foveolatus, commonly known as the moon cactus, is a cactus species native to South America.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stem and vibrant, showy flowers that bloom at night. The plant has a unique appearance with a distinct, rounded tubercle at the base of each areole. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in a suitable environment.

Pilocereus foveolatus prefers well-draining soil and full sun to partial shade.
