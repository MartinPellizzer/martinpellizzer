Polygala comosa var. lejeunei

Polygala comosa var. lejeunei, commonly known as the narrow-leaved milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to parts of Asia, including China and Japan.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in temperate climates. Its leaves are narrow and linear, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain several seeds.

It is often found in open woodland or grassland habitats.
