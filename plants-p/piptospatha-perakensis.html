Piptospatha perakensis

Piptospatha perakensis, commonly known as the Perak river fern, is a unique species native to the rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, feathery fronds that resemble those of other ferns, but with distinctive features such as its elongated, narrow leaves. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, typically growing near streams or in dense undergrowth. Its reproductive structures are concealed within specialized leaf bases, which protect the spores until they are ready to be dispersed.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
