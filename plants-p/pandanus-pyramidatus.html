Pandanus pyramidatus

Pandanus pyramidatus, commonly known as the mountain pandanus, is a tropical plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and long, narrow leaves that form a dense, conical shape. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically clustered in terminal inflorescences. Its leaves are often used in traditional crafts and for thatching.

Pandanus pyramidatus thrives in humid, coastal environments and is adapted to withstand salt spray and strong winds.
