Primula latifolia subsp. vicina

Primula latifolia subsp.

vicina, commonly known as the broad-leaved primrose, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, typically blooming in early spring.

The plant has broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, downy texture. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for rock gardens and woodland plantings.
