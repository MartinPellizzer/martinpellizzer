Pseudolobivia obrepanda

Pseudolobivia obrepanda, commonly known as the obrepanda cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of South America.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its cylindrical stems and spiny leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and thrives in full sun exposure. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange, appearing in late spring to early summer.

Due to its slow growth and unique appearance, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and arid landscapes.
