Ptarmica moschata

Ptarmica moschata, commonly known as sweet cicely, is a herbaceous plant belonging to the Apiaceae family.

It is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant produces aromatic seeds that are often used in culinary applications. Native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia, it thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

Its fragrance is reminiscent of anise and is used in both food and traditional medicine.
