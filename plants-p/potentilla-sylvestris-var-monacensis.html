Potentilla sylvestris var. monacensis

Potentilla sylvestris var. monacensis, commonly known as the Monacense cinquefoil, is a perennial shrub native to Central Europe.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces small, yellowish flowers with five petals. The plant has compound leaves with five leaflets, which are typically oval and slightly hairy. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or rocky habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
