Pithecellobium bifoliolum

Pithecellobium bifoliolum, commonly known as the two-leaved burr legume, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of the Americas and is characterized by its distinctive, palmate leaves with two leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by woody, spiny pods containing edible seeds. It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes used in reforestation efforts and as a forage plant.
