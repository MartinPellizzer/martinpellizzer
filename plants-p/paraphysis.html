Paraphysis

Paraphysis, commonly known as a type of plant structure found in certain algae and fungi, refers to the elongated, thread-like cells that surround the reproductive structures.

These cells are typically found in the thallus of filamentous organisms and serve structural and protective functions. They are often differentiated from other cells in the organism and may contain specialized pigments or compounds. In some species, paraphyses can be involved in the dispersal of spores or the absorption of nutrients.

Their presence and morphology can be important for the identification and classification of specific taxa.
