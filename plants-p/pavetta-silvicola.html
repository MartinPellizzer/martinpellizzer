Pavetta silvicola

Pavetta silvicola, commonly known as the forest pavetta, is a small evergreen shrub native to the tropical regions of Africa.

It typically grows in shaded, moist habitats such as forest understories and rocky slopes. The plant features opposite, elliptic leaves with a glossy green surface and reddish veins. Its flowers are small, white, and clustered in umbels, often blooming in spring.

The fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, black seeds.
