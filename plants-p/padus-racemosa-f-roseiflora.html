Padus racemosa f. roseiflora

Padus racemosa f. roseiflora, commonly known as the rose-flowered white crabsapple, is a botanical variant of the white crabsapple.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern North America. The plant features showy, rose-like flowers that bloom in early spring before the leaves emerge. Its foliage is typically dark green and turns vibrant red or orange in the fall.

The fruit is a small, yellowish pome that matures in the summer.
