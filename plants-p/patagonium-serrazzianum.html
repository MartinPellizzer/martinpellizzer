Patagonium serrazzianum

Patagonium serrazzianum, commonly known as the Patagonian serrazian thistle, is a perennial shrub native to the southern regions of South America.

It is characterized by its spiny stems and large, compound leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers in late spring, which are followed by distinctive bract-covered seed heads. It thrives in dry, rocky environments and is adapted to withstand harsh climatic conditions.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists alike.
