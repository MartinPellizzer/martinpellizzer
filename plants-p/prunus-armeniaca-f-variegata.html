Prunus armeniaca f. variegata

Prunus armeniaca f. variegata, commonly known as the variegated apricot, is a cultivar of the apricot tree characterized by its distinctive foliage.

The leaves exhibit a striking variegation pattern, often displaying white or yellow markings against a green background. This plant belongs to the Rosaceae family and is prized for its ornamental value as well as its edible fruit. It typically grows in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

The flowers, which appear in early spring, are usually pink or white and contribute to its aesthetic appeal.
