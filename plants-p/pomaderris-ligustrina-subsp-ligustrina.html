Pomaderris ligustrina subsp. ligustrina

Pomaderris ligustrina subsp.

ligustrina, commonly known as the narrow-leaved pomaderris, is a shrub native to southern Australia. It features small, elliptical leaves that are typically grey-green in color, with a slightly hairy surface.

The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It is often found in dry, open habitats such as woodlands and heathlands.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in native gardens.
