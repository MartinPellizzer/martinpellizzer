Physalis viscosa var. spathulifolia

Physalis viscosa var. spathulifolia, commonly known as the broad-leafed husk tomato, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and broad, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence. The plant produces clusters of bright orange, lantern-shaped fruits that enclose the seeds. It thrives in warm, temperate climates and is often cultivated for its edible fruits.

The botanical features of this species make it a distinctive member of the Solanaceae family.
