Platycarpha carlinoides

Platycarpha carlinoides, commonly known as the Carolina silverbush, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features small, silver-gray leaves that are densely packed, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its fruit is a small, capsule-like structure that contains several seeds.

Platycarpha carlinoides is often found in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to coastal and inland dunes.
