Petrophile misturata

Petrophile misturata, commonly known as the misty rock-heath, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of southern Australia.

It is characterized by its erect, slender stems and small, clustered pink or red flowers that bloom during the spring and summer months. The plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in coastal heathlands and dunes. It has adapted to salty sea breezes and is drought-tolerant, making it well-suited to its harsh coastal environment.

Petrophile misturata plays an important role in stabilizing dune systems and providing habitat for native wildlife.
