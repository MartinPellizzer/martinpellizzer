Pappophorum alopecuroideum var. pilosiusculum

Pappophorum alopecuroideum var. pilosiusculum, commonly known as the hairy meadow grass, is a perennial grass species characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and hairy leaf blades.

It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in meadows and along stream banks. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. Its leaves are narrow and slightly rolled, with a distinctive hairy texture that aids in moisture retention.

This variety is often found in temperate regions and is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and supporting pollinators.
