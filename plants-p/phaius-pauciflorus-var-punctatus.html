Phaius pauciflorus var. punctatus

Phaius pauciflorus var. punctatus, commonly known as the spotted orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and showy, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or orange with purple or brown markings. The plant has broad, lance-shaped leaves that are usually arranged in a rosette at the base of the stem. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The flowers bloom in late summer to early autumn, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
