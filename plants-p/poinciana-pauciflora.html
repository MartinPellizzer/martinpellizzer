Poinciana pauciflora

Poinciana pauciflora, commonly known as the flame tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy red or orange inflorescences that resemble flames, which bloom throughout the year in warm climates. The tree has pinnate leaves with numerous small leaflets, and its flowers are clustered in dense, branched panicles. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular ornamental plant in tropical and subtropical regions.

The fruit is a legume pod containing several seeds, which can be dispersed by wind or animals.
