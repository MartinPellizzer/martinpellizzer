Pinus lowii

Pinus lowii, commonly known as the Sierra Madre pine, is a species of conifer native to the mountainous regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and long, needle-like leaves that grow in bundles of five. The tree produces cones that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind, aiding in its propagation. Pinus lowii thrives in high-altitude environments with cool, moist conditions.

Its wood is valued for its strength and durability, making it useful in construction and crafts.
