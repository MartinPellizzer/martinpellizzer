Panicum phylloryzoides

Panicum phylloryzoides, commonly known as the leafroot panicgrass, is a grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its distinctive leaf morphology, featuring narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a prominent midrib. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear minute, wind-pollinated flowers. Its root system is notable for its fibrous structure, which can contribute to soil stabilization.

Panicum phylloryzoides is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
