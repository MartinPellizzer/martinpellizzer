Primula oreodoxa

Primula oreodoxa, commonly known as the broadleaf primrose, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia, including China and Japan.

It is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves that are often hairy and its clusters of bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodland areas and stream banks. It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.

The flowers bloom in early spring, adding vibrant color to the landscape during the transition from winter to summer.
