Plectranthus calcicola

Plectranthus calcicola, commonly known as the limestone plectranthus, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its aromatic, woolly leaves and small, tubular flowers that bloom in shades of purple and blue. The plant thrives in well-drained, calcareous soils and is often found in rocky or limestone areas. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs.

Plectranthus calcicola is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and fragrance.
