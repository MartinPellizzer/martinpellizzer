Prangos trifida

Prangos trifida, commonly known as the three-lobed bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The plant produces three leaf lobes, which are typically hairy and serrated. It thrives in rocky and arid environments, often growing at high altitudes.

Prangos trifida is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with well-drained soil.
