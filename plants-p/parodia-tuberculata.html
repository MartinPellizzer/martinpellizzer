Parodia tuberculata

Parodia tuberculata, commonly known as the "Cactus of the Desert," is a species of cactus native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its globular shape and is covered in dense, white spines that provide protection from herbivores and intense sunlight. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, which are often short-lived but striking in appearance. Its succulent stems store water, allowing it to thrive in harsh, dry environments.

Parodia tuberculata is a popular choice for cactus enthusiasts due to its compact size and low maintenance requirements.
