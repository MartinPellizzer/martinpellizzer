Phalaenopsis pulcherrima var. pulcherrima

Phalaenopsis pulcherrima var. pulcherrima, commonly known as the Orchid Queen, is a tropical orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that can be white, pink, or purple, often with intricate patterns. The plant has a sympodial growth habit, forming pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its leaves are broad, elongated, and often have a waxy texture.

This variety is prized for its longevity and ability to bloom repeatedly under proper care.
