Piqueria foliosa

Piqueria foliosa, commonly known as the Mexican sunflower or velvetleaf, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellow-orange composite flowers and hairy, lance-shaped leaves. The plant is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions. It has a taproot system and is known for its ability to spread through seeds dispersed by wind.

Piqueria foliosa is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
