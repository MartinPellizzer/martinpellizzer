Phyteuma halleri var. pseudonigrum

Phyteuma halleri var. pseudonigrum, commonly known as the purple loosestrife, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, violet-blue flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in moist environments such as wet meadows and stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in ecological studies and horticultural practices.
