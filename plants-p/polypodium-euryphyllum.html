Polypodium euryphyllum

Polypodium euryphyllum, commonly known as the broad-leaf wall fern, is a species of fern native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its large, leathery fronds that can grow up to 30 inches in length. The plant typically thrives in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its rhizome is thick and fleshy, allowing it to store water and nutrients efficiently.

The fronds are bi-directionally divided, with a distinctive appearance that makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
