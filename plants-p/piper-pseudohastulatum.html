Piper pseudohastulatum

Piper pseudohastulatum, commonly known as the Bornean pepper vine, is a climbing shrub native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper plants, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces small, dark purple berries that are used in traditional medicine and as a spice in some local cuisines. Its botanical features include opposite leaves with serrated edges and a distinctive, aromatic scent.

It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.
