Piper peltatum var. hirtellum

Piper peltatum var. hirtellum, commonly known as the hairy sarsaparilla, is a climbing vine native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes numerous species of flowering plants. The plant is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, hairy textures. It produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are typically clustered in spikes.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
