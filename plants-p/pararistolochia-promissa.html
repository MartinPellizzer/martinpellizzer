Pararistolochia promissa

Pararistolochia promissa, commonly known as the false hooded vine, is a climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, fleshy, and often ribbed stems that can grow up to several meters in length. The leaves are alternate, simple, and typically have a heart-shaped base. Its flowers are unique, with a complex, tubular structure that resembles a hood, and they are usually greenish or pale yellow in color.

The plant is part of the Aristolochiaceae family and is known for its medicinal uses in traditional herbal medicine.
