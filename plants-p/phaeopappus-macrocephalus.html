Phaeopappus macrocephalus

Phaeopappus macrocephalus, commonly known as the large-headed yellow cypress, is a unique coniferous plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive yellow bark and its ability to thrive in dry, rocky soils. The plant has scale-like leaves that are closely packed, forming a dense, shrub-like structure. Its cones are small and woody, typically containing two seeds.

This species is notable for its ecological role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
