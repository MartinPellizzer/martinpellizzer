Prunus serrulata var. sontagiae

Prunus serrulata var. sontagiae, commonly known as the Japanese cherry blossom or Somei Yoshino, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its delicate, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in early spring, creating a striking visual display. The plant has elliptical leaves that are serrated along the edges, giving it its scientific name. It thrives in temperate climates and prefers well-drained soil.

This variety is widely cultivated for ornamental purposes in gardens and public spaces.
