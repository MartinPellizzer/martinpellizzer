Persicaria microcephala var. microcephala

Persicaria microcephala var. microcephala, commonly known as small-headed smartweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to North America.

It features upright, branched stems that are usually covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink to red in color, blooming from late summer into early fall. Its leaves are lance-shaped, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface, and they often have a reddish or purple tinge.

This species is often found in moist soils along stream banks, wet meadows, and other wetland habitats.
