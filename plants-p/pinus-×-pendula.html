Pinus × pendula

Pinus × pendula, commonly known as the weeping pine, is a hybrid conifer resulting from the cross between Pinus ponderosa and Pinus douglasii.

It is characterized by its distinctive drooping branches that create a cascading appearance, giving it a unique and ornamental look. The tree produces needle-like leaves that are typically dark green and grow in clusters of five. It produces cones that are oval-shaped and contain seeds enclosed in woody structures.

This species is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to various soil types.
