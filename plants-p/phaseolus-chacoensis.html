Phaseolus chacoensis

Phaseolus chacoensis, commonly known as the Chaco bean, is a species native to South America, particularly found in Paraguay, Argentina, and Bolivia.

It is a herbaceous plant that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, with compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant produces pods containing small, kidney-shaped seeds that are used as food. Botanically, it belongs to the legume family (Fabaceae) and is characterized by its climbing habit and ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.

Its genetic diversity makes it an important species for agricultural research and crop improvement.
