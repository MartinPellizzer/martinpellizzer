Phyllanthus chapelieri

Phyllanthus chapelieri, commonly known as the African buttonwood, is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, rounded crown and leathery, elliptical leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, followed by round, fleshy fruits that are often red or orange when mature. Phyllanthus chapelieri is well adapted to dry conditions and is often found in coastal or rocky habitats.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
