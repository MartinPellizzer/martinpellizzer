Prunus padus var. rufoferruginea

Prunus padus var. rufoferruginea, commonly known as the red-stemmed bird cherry, is a variant of the bird cherry species native to Europe.

It is characterized by its distinctive reddish-brown stems and small, white flowers that bloom in early spring. The leaves are ovate with serrated edges and turn yellow in the autumn. This plant typically grows in moist, shaded areas and is often found in woodlands or along stream banks.

It produces small, edible fruits that are popular with birds and some wildlife species.
