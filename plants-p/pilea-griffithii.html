Pilea griffithii

Pilea griffithii, commonly known as the Chinese money plant, is a species of flowering plant in the family Araceae.

It is native to China and is characterized by its round, shield-shaped leaves that resemble coins. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers that are clustered in an inflorescence called a spadix, surrounded by a modified leaf called a spathe. It is a low-maintenance plant that thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil.

Pilea griffithii is popular as a houseplant due to its aesthetic appeal and ease of care.
