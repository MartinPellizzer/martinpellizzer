Pectis scabra

Pectis scabra, commonly known as the rough pectis, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are rough and hairy, providing a distinctive texture, while its yellow flowers bloom in clusters.

Pectis scabra is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
