Phaca carinata

Phaca carinata, commonly known as the winged fern, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, feather-like fronds that are often adorned with prominent veins. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically yellow or green in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its unique morphology and adaptability make it a notable species in its native habitats.
