Panicum wilmingtonense

Panicum wilmingtonense, commonly known as Wilmington grass, is a perennial grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in wetland habitats such as marshes and swamps, where it thrives in moist to water-saturated soils. The plant has slender, erect stems that can reach heights of up to 1 meter, with narrow, grass-like leaves. Its inflorescence consists of a panicle made up of spikelets, which are small, clustered flower clusters.

The species is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
