Primula domestica

Primula domestica, commonly known as the common primrose, is a flowering plant native to Europe, western Asia, and northern Africa.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of brightly colored, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in early spring. The plant is part of the Primulaceae family and thrives in moist, well-drained soil in partial shade. Its flowers come in a variety of colors, including yellow, white, pink, and purple, making it a popular garden plant.

Primula domestica is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
