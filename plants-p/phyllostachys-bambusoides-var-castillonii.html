Phyllostachys bambusoides var. castillonii

Phyllostachys bambusoides var. castillonii, commonly known as the Castillon's bamboo, is a variant of the bamboo species native to China.

It is characterized by its slender, upright culms that can grow up to several meters in height. The leaves are long and narrow, with a smooth, glossy surface and a distinct midrib. This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to a variety of soil types.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a versatile choice for landscaping.
