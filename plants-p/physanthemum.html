Physanthemum

Physanthemum, commonly known as the silverleaf nightshade, is a genus of flowering plants in the nightshade family.

These plants are characterized by their simple, alternate leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery pubescence. The flowers are typically small, clustered in racemes, and have five petals that are usually white or pale blue in color. Physanthemum species are native to various regions in Africa, South America, and parts of Asia.

They are often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a range of environmental conditions.
