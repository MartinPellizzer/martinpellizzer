Piper bolivaranum

Piper bolivaranum, commonly known as the Bolivian pepper or Bolivian pipevine, is a climbing shrub native to the Amazon rainforest of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many species of flowering plants known for their aromatic properties. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves with a distinct veining pattern. Its fruits are small, round berries that turn red when mature, and the plant is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
