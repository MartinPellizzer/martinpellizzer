Potentilla arbuscula var. unifoliolata

Potentilla arbuscula var. unifoliolata, commonly known as the single leaf potentilla, is a low-growing shrub native to the western United States.

It features small, rounded leaves that are typically solitary or in pairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of bright yellow flowers in the spring, which attract pollinators like bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Its hardy nature and ornamental value make it a popular choice for native plant gardens.
