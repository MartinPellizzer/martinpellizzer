Psidium ovatifolium

Psidium ovatifolium, commonly known as the ovate-leaf guava, is a small evergreen shrub or tree native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its ovate, glossy leaves and small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces edible berries that are similar in appearance to guavas but have a distinct flavor. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit.

The plant's botanical features make it an interesting subject for horticultural and ecological studies.
