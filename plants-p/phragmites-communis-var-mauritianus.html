Phragmites communis var. mauritianus

Phragmites communis var. mauritianus, commonly known as the common reed, is a tall, perennial grass that thrives in wetland environments.

It is characterized by its hollow, jointed stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. This variant is native to North America and is often found in marshes, along lake shores, and in other moist habitats. It plays a vital role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.

The plant can grow up to 6 feet tall and is often used in ecological restoration projects.
