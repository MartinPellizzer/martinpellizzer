Ptilotus aristatus subsp. aristatus

Ptilotus aristatus subsp.

aristatus, commonly known as the desert lily or sandflower, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of Australia. It is characterized by its distinctive, spiky, pink or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant has a low, sprawling growth habit with hairy, fleshy leaves adapted to conserve water. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in coastal dunes and inland deserts.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological role, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens for ornamental purposes.
