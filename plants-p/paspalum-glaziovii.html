Paspalum glaziovii

Paspalum glaziovii, commonly known as Glaziov's grass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, blue-green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry grasslands and disturbed areas.

Paspalum glaziovii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soil in arid environments.
