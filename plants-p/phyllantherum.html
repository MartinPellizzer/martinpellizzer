Phyllantherum

Phyllantherum, commonly known as the Indian nettle or *Urtica dioica* var. *indica*, is a flowering plant belonging to the Urticaceae family.

It is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges and stinging hairs on both the upper and lower surfaces. The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in spikes, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands.

Phyllantherum is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
