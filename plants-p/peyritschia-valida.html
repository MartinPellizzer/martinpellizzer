Peyritschia valida

Peyritschia valida, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, velvety hairs, giving it a distinctive texture. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, typically in the summer months. Its root system is fibrous and can spread widely, making it a persistent weed in agricultural and natural habitats.

Peyritschia valida is known for its adaptability to various soil types and its ability to thrive in both full sun and partial shade.
