Protea verticillata

Protea verticillata, commonly known as the mountain protea, is a striking evergreen shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescences composed of numerous pale pink to white flowers arranged in a dense, rounded cluster. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun, often found in mountainous regions. Its leaves are narrow and leathery, providing a striking contrast to the soft, fluffy flower heads.

Protea verticillata is a popular ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and ability to withstand coastal conditions.
