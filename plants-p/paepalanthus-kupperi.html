Paepalanthus kupperi

Paepalanthus kupperi, commonly known as the Kupper's candelabra cactus, is a species native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a columnar cactus characterized by its spiny stems and clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has a compact growth habit, often forming a dense, rounded shape. Its leaves are reduced to spines, and it produces fruits that are typically red or orange in color.

This species is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, thriving in well-drained soils.
