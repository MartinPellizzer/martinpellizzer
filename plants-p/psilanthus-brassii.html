Psilanthus brassii

Psilanthus brassii, commonly known as the African sycamore, is a deciduous tree native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family and is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves and showy yellow flowers. The tree produces woody capsules that contain small, hairy seeds. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions.

Psilanthus brassii is often used in traditional medicine and for its ornamental value.
