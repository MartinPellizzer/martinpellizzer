Paspalum clipeum

Paspalum clipeum, commonly known as the narrowleaf paspalum, is a perennial grass native to the Americas.

It typically grows in moist to wet environments, such as marshes and wetlands. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It is often found in coastal areas and can tolerate salt spray.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and wetland restoration.
