Phaseoloides brachybotrys

Phaseoloides brachybotrys, commonly known as the short-stalked morning glory, is a flowering plant belonging to the Convolvulaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, trailing stems and heart-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Due to its rapid growth and ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated as a decorative plant.
