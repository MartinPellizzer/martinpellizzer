Poa hamiltonii

Poa hamiltonii, commonly known as Hamilton's bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its fine, blue-green foliage and forms dense, mat-like growth patterns. The plant typically thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets that are loosely arranged, and it produces small, wind-dispersed seeds.

Poa hamiltonii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic and ecological benefits.
