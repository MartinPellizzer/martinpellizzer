Panicum culicinum

Panicum culicinum, commonly known as the mosquito plant, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like foliage and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant is known for its ability to thrive in wet or marshy environments, making it a common sight in wetlands and along water bodies. Its rapid growth and adaptability have made it both a useful species for erosion control and a potential invasive weed in some areas.

The plant's name is derived from its association with mosquitoes, as it often grows in habitats where these insects are prevalent.
