Pelargonium × rubescens

Pelargonium × rubescens, commonly known as the scented geranium, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Geraniaceae family.

It is characterized by its fragrant, velvety leaves that emit a variety of scents, such as mint, citrus, or rose, depending on the variety. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub with rounded, lobed leaves and produces small, clustered flowers in shades of pink, red, or white. It is native to South Africa and is widely cultivated as an ornamental plant for its aromatic foliage and ornamental value.

Pelargonium × rubescens thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and indoor cultivation.
