Pachycarpus concolor

Pachycarpus concolor, commonly known as the smoothleaf pachycarpus, is a species of flowering plant in the Fabaceae family.

It is characterized by its woody stems and compound leaves that are typically smooth and glabrous. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are usually pale green or yellow in color. It is native to certain regions of South America and is adapted to grow in dry, rocky environments.

The fruit is a legume that contains several seeds, which are dispersed by wind or animals.
