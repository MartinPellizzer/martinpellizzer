Prunus serrulata f. conspicua

Prunus serrulata f. conspicua, commonly known as the weeping cherry or weeping sakura, is a distinctive ornamental plant characterized by its cascading branches and profuse pink or white flowers.

It is a compact form of the Japanese cherry blossom, Prunus serrulata, and is prized for its elegant, drooping appearance. The plant typically blooms in early spring, producing showy flowers that create a striking visual contrast against its green foliage. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and public spaces.

Its botanical features make it a symbol of beauty and transience in horticultural culture.
