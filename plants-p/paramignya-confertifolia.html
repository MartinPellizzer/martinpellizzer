Paramignya confertifolia

Paramignya confertifolia, commonly known as the broad-leafed paramignya, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. Its bark is thick and corky, providing structural support and protection against environmental stress.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological role, it is of interest in both botanical research and traditional medicine.
