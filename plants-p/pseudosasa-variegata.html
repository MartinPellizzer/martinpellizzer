Pseudosasa variegata

Pseudosasa variegata, commonly known as the variegated bamboo, is a species of grass native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant features striking variegated leaves with white and green patterns, making it visually appealing. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.

Pseudosasa variegata is a clump-forming plant that spreads slowly, making it suitable for garden borders or as a specimen plant.
