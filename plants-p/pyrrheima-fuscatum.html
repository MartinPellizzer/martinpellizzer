Pyrrheima fuscatum

Pyrrheima fuscatum, commonly known as the black-leaved bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its dark green, lance-shaped leaves and bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pale blue to violet. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its flowering period occurs in late summer, and it is often found in alpine meadows and forested areas.

Pyrrheima fuscatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens.
