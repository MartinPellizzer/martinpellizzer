Primula occidentalis

Primula occidentalis, commonly known as the western primrose, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and violet. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in shaded to partially sunny conditions. Its leaves are broadly ovate with a slightly hairy texture, and it produces small, oval-shaped fruits containing numerous seeds.

Primula occidentalis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate beauty.
