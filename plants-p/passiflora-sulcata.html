Passiflora sulcata

Passiflora sulcata, commonly known as the rough passionflower, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, intricate flowers that feature five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments. The plant produces edible berries that are typically yellow or orange when ripe. Its leaves are typically lobed and have a rough texture, contributing to its common name.

Passiflora sulcata is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its showy blooms and unique appearance.
