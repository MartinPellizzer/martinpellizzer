Pelargonium uniondalense

Pelargonium uniondalense, commonly known as the Colombian geranium, is a species native to South America, particularly Colombia.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its upright growth habit and serrated leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of pink and red, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and containers.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is occasionally cultivated in horticultural settings.
