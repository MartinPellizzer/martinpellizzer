Pseudowintera axillaris

Pseudowintera axillaris, commonly known as the silver tree, is a small evergreen shrub native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its dense, silvery-gray foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, white flowers in clusters during the spring, followed by dark blue berries. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Pseudowintera axillaris is often used in ornamental horticulture for its unique texture and visual appeal.
