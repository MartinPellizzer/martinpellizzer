Plectranthus praetervisus

Plectranthus praetervisus, commonly known as the African violet, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its velvety, dark green leaves and clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from purple to white. The plant is often grown as a houseplant due to its compact size and ability to thrive in indoor conditions. It prefers well-draining soil and moderate light, making it a popular choice for both novice and experienced gardeners.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to bloom throughout the year under proper care.
