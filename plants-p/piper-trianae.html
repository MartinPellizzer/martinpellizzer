Piper trianae

Piper trianae, commonly known as the Trianas pepper or Triana's pepper, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Colombia and Ecuador. The plant is characterized by its climbing habit, with opposite, ovate leaves that are often hairy. It produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into elongated, fleshy berries.

The berries are typically harvested when mature and are used in traditional medicine and as a spice in some local cuisines.
