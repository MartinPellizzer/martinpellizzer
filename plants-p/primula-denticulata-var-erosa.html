Primula denticulata var. erosa

Primula denticulata var. erosa, commonly known as the erose primrose, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of East Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, finely toothed leaves and clusters of bell-shaped, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and rocky slopes. It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.

The flowers bloom in early spring, adding a delicate touch of color to the landscape.
