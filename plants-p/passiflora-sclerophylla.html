Passiflora sclerophylla

Passiflora sclerophylla, commonly known as the rough passionflower, is a climbing vine native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, showy flowers that feature a complex arrangement of sepals, petals, and bracts. The plant produces blue or purple berries that contain multiple seeds, which are dispersed by animals. Its leaves are typically rough and have a waxy coating, adapting to harsh environmental conditions.

Passiflora sclerophylla is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
