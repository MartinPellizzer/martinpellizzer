Pterostylis major

Pterostylis major, commonly known as the large helmet orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its unique, elongated, helmet-like flower that resembles a bird's head, with a prominent beak-like structure. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as rainforests and understoreys. Its flowers are usually greenish-yellow with a brownish lip, and it produces small, dark brown seeds.

Pterostylis major is part of the Orchidaceae family and is known for its specialized pollination mechanisms involving insect visitors.
