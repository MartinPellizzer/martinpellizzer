Plantago sphaerocephala

Plantago sphaerocephala, commonly known as the globeplant or round-headed plantain, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its rosette of broad, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers on tall, slender stalks, which are followed by spherical, three-lobed seed capsules. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

The species is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
