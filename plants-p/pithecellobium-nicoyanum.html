Pithecellobium nicoyanum

Pithecellobium nicoyanum, commonly known as the Nicoya acacia, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant produces small, edible pods that contain seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in dry forest ecosystems.

Its ability to fix nitrogen makes it an important plant for soil enrichment.
