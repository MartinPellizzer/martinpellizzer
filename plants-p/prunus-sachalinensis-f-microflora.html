Prunus sachalinensis f. microflora

Prunus sachalinensis f. microflora, commonly known as the Sachalin cherry plum, is a unique variant of the cherry plum species native to the Russian Far East.

This form is characterized by its small, compact growth habit and dense foliage, which gives it a distinct appearance compared to typical Prunus species. Botanically, it exhibits a high degree of variability in leaf shape and flower size, making it a subject of interest in horticultural studies. The plant produces small, edible fruits that are often used in local cuisines.

Its ornamental value and adaptability to various climates contribute to its popularity in landscaping and conservation efforts.
