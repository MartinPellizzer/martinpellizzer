Polypodium filix-mas

Polypodium filix-mas, commonly known as the royal fern, is a species of evergreen fern native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its large, leathery fronds that can grow up to 1 meter in length. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky crevices. Its spore-producing structures, called sori, are located on the underside of the fronds.

Polypodium filix-mas is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine in various cultures.
