Perebea tessmannii var. ulei

Perebea tessmannii var. ulei, commonly known as the Ulei variant of the genus Perebea, is a species native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree that typically grows up to 20 meters in height. The plant is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers followed by woody capsules containing numerous seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland and hill forest ecosystems.

The species is valued for its timber and has some traditional uses in local communities.
