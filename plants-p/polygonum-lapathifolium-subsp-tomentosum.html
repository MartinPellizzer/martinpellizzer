Polygonum lapathifolium subsp. tomentosum

Polygonum lapathifolium subsp.

tomentosum, commonly known as hairy polygonum, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Asia. It typically grows in moist environments such as wetlands, marshes, and stream banks.

The plant features erect stems that are often covered in a dense layer of soft, white hairs. Its leaves are ovate to lanceolate, with a hairy underside, and it produces small, clustered flowers in shades of red or pink.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
