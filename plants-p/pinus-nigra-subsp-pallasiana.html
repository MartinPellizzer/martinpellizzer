Pinus nigra subsp. pallasiana

Pinus nigra subsp.

pallasiana, commonly known as the Crimean pine, is a subspecies of the black pine native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia. It is characterized by its dark, fissured bark and needle-like leaves that are typically 4 to 6 centimeters long. The plant produces cones that range from 5 to 10 centimeters in length, containing edible seeds that are a food source for various wildlife.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions, where it contributes to local ecosystems through its role in soil stabilization and habitat provision.
