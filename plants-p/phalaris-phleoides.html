Phalaris phleoides

Phalaris phleoides, commonly known as the narrowleaf jointvetch, is a perennial herb native to parts of Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, pea-like flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has pinnately compound leaves and produces small, elliptical seeds in pods. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands.

Phalaris phleoides is an important species for ecological studies due to its role in supporting local insect populations.
