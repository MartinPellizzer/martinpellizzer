Pachystoma nutans

Pachystoma nutans, commonly known as the nodding stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its fleshy, blue-green leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern, which helps it store water in arid environments. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers that droop downward, giving it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Due to its unique appearance and drought tolerance, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.
