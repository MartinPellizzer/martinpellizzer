Patrinia villosa var. japonica

Patrinia villosa var. japonica, commonly known as Japanese Patrinia, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to East Asia.

It features slender, creeping stems that often root at the nodes, forming dense mats. The plant produces small, white to pale purple flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate to lanceolate, and covered with fine, soft hairs.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or partially shaded woodland environments.
