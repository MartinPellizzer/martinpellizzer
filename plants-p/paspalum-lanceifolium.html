Paspalum lanceifolium

Paspalum lanceifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaved grass, is a perennial grass species native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. This plant is often found in wetland areas, marshes, and along the edges of water bodies. It has a fibrous root system that helps stabilize soil and prevent erosion.

Paspalum lanceifolium is also valued for its ability to thrive in moist conditions and its role in supporting local wildlife.
