Peperomia pallida var. niueana

Peperomia pallida var. niueana, commonly known as the Niuean Peperomia, is a compact, low-growing plant native to the island of Niue in the South Pacific.

It features dense, fleshy, dark green leaves that are often tinged with red or purple on the underside, giving it a striking appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers that appear in clusters, typically during the warmer months. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its unique appearance and limited natural range, it is a sought-after specimen for collectors and indoor gardeners.
