Pseuderanthemum modestum

Pseuderanthemum modestum, commonly known as the modest pseuderanthemum, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically white or pale pink, often clustered in whorls along the stems. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered with fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Pseuderanthemum modestum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in shaded environments.
