Populus nigra f. torulosa

Populus nigra f. torulosa, commonly known as the warty poplar, is a unique cultivar of the black poplar.

It is characterized by its distinctive, warty growth on the bark, which gives it a rugged appearance. The plant is a deciduous tree with heart-shaped leaves that turn yellow in autumn. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found near rivers or in wetlands.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in riparian zones.
