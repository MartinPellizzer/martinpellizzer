Parsonsia sanguinea var. sanguinea

Parsonsia sanguinea var. sanguinea, commonly known as the red lantern plant, is a flowering shrub native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking red, lantern-shaped flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with a glossy green surface and serrated edges. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and floral displays.
