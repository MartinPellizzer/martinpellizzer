Pithecellobium amplum

Pithecellobium amplum, commonly known as the giant poinciana, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically pinnately arranged and have a leathery texture. The plant produces showy, clustered inflorescences of yellow or orange flowers, which bloom throughout the year in warm climates. Its fruit is a woody pod containing several seeds, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The species is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
