Potamogeton × billotii

Potamogeton × billotii, commonly known as the hybrid pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant that exhibits characteristics of both its parent species, Potamogeton perfoliatus and Potamogeton crispus.

It is characterized by its long, slender stems and whorled arrangement of leaves, which are typically narrow and sometimes serrated. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often clustered in spikes, and it is capable of both sexual and asexual reproduction. Potamogeton × billotii is commonly found in freshwater habitats such as ponds, lakes, and slow-moving rivers.

Its presence can indicate good water quality, but it can also become invasive in certain environments.
