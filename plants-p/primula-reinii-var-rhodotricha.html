Primula reinii var. rhodotricha

Primula reinii var. rhodotricha, commonly known as the woolly primrose, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that gives it a distinctive, fuzzy appearance. The plant produces clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink, white, and purple, which bloom in early spring. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and botanical collections worldwide.
