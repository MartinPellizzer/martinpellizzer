Polygonatum commutatum var. virginicum

Polygonatum commutatum var. virginicum, commonly known as the red root, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It features rhizomatous stems that produce clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. Its underground rhizomes are characterized by their reddish color and are often used in traditional medicine.

It thrives in woodland habitats and is valued for its ornamental and medicinal properties.
