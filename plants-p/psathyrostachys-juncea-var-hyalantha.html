Psathyrostachys juncea var. hyalantha

Psathyrostachys juncea var. hyalantha, commonly known as the hyaline wheatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves with a distinctive waxy coating. The plant produces small, spikelet-bearing inflorescences that are typically green or bluish-green in color. It is well adapted to drought conditions, making it a hardy species suitable for xerophytic environments.

Its ecological role includes soil stabilization and providing forage for grazing animals in semi-arid regions.
