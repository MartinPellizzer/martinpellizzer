Pseudacacia

Pseudacacia, commonly known as black locust, is a deciduous tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are pinnately arranged and have small, oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces fragrant, yellowish-white flowers in clusters, which bloom in the spring. Its hard, heavy wood is highly valued for its strength and durability.

Pseudacacia is also known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
