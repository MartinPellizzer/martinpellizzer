Poa songarica

Poa songarica, commonly known as the Songarica bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and produces slender, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along riverbanks. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue that gives it its common name.

Poa songarica is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in horticulture for its aesthetic appeal.
