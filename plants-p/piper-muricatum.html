Piper muricatum

Piper muricatum, commonly known as the "Cuban pepper" or "Muricatum pepper," is a tropical vine native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are often lobed or serrated, and its small, clustered flowers that develop into peppery, red or orange berries. The plant belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species such as black pepper. Its berries contain a variety of bioactive compounds, including piperine and capsaicin-like substances, contributing to its medicinal and culinary uses.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value as well as for its unique flavor profile.
