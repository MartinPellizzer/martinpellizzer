Piper dussii f. dogueanum

Piper dussii f. dogueanum, commonly known as the Mexican pepper plant, is a variant of the Piper dussii species native to Mexico.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into elongated, spiny fruits. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for tropical and subtropical climates.

Due to its ornamental value and unique appearance, it is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
