Phacelia circinata var. calycosa

Phacelia circinata var. calycosa, commonly known as the goldenhead phacelia, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its bright yellow to golden-yellow flower clusters that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often hairy, while its stems are erect and branching.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
