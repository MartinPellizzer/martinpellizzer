Peperomia perlongipedunculata

Peperomia perlongipedunculata, commonly known as the long-stemmed peperomia, is a compact, evergreen perennial plant native to South America.

It features dense, dark green foliage with a velvety texture and upright, branching stems that can reach up to 30 centimeters in height. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters, typically in shades of white or pale pink, which are followed by tiny, round berries. It thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Its slow growth and attractive appearance make it ideal for container cultivation and as a decorative plant.
