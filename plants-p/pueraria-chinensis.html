Pueraria chinensis

Pueraria chinensis, commonly known as Chinese kudzu, is a vigorous vine native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its pinnately compound leaves and clusters of purple-pink flowers. The plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to form dense thickets, often invading natural habitats. Its roots contain high levels of starch and have been used in traditional medicine.

Botanically, it belongs to the Fabaceae family and is valued for its adaptability to various soil types.
