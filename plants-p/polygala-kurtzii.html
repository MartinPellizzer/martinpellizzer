Polygala kurtzii

Polygala kurtzii, commonly known as the Cape tulip, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that resemble tulips. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and has a shrub-like appearance with woody stems. Its leaves are narrow and evergreen, and it produces small, green fruits that contain seeds.

Polygala kurtzii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
