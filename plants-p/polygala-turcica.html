Polygala turcica

Polygala turcica, commonly known as Turkish milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, racemose inflorescences. The flowers are typically pink or purple, with five sepals and petals that are often fused at the base. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and scrublands.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
