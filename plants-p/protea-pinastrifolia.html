Protea pinastrifolia

Protea pinastrifolia, commonly known as the silver protea, is a species native to the Western Cape of South Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, large, and showy inflorescence composed of numerous white to pale pink flower heads. The plant typically grows in coastal fynbos vegetation, where it thrives in well-drained, sandy soils. Its flowers are pollinated primarily by birds, particularly the sugarbird, which is adapted to feed on its nectar.

The protea is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
