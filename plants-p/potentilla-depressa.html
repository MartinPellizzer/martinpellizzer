Potentilla depressa

Potentilla depressa, commonly known as creeping cinquefoil, is a low-growing perennial shrub native to North America.

It features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, typically from June to July. The plant has slender, spreading stems covered in fine hairs and produces five-petaled flowers. Its leaves are oval-shaped, serrated, and arranged in pairs along the stems.

Potentilla depressa thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as meadows and rocky areas.
