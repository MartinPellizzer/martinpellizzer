Prunus × andarobi

Prunus × andarobi, commonly known as the Andarobian cherry, is a hybrid species native to the island of Madagascar.

It is a small to medium-sized tree or shrub characterized by its smooth, grey bark and oval-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pinkish flowers in spring, followed by edible cherries that are typically red or yellow when mature. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

Due to its ornamental value and unique fruit, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and for local use.
