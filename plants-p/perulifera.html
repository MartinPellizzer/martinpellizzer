Perulifera

Perulifera, commonly known as the sea holly, is a genus of marine algae belonging to the family Peruliferidae.

It is characterized by its unique, branched structure and the presence of calcified spicules, which provide structural support. These organisms are typically found in shallow coastal waters, where they contribute to the marine ecosystem. Perulifera species are often used as indicators of water quality due to their sensitivity to environmental changes.

Their reproductive strategies involve the production of both male and female gametes, facilitating sexual reproduction in their aquatic habitats.
