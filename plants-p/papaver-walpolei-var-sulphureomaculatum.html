Papaver walpolei var. sulphureomaculatum

Papaver walpolei var. sulphureomaculatum, commonly known as the Sulphur-spotted Poppy, is a striking perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of central Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow-orange flowers with distinctive sulfur-yellow spots on the petals. The plant features basal leaves that are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its overall rugged appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in rocky or alpine environments.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is a sought-after species among botanists and horticultural enthusiasts.
