Platystele riograndensis

Platystele riograndensis, commonly known as the Rio Grande ghostplant, is a rare and delicate member of the Bromeliaceae family.

It is an epiphytic plant, meaning it grows on other plants without deriving nutrients from them. Native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil, it thrives in shaded, humid environments. Its unique botanical feature is its small, white, tubular flowers that bloom in clusters.

The plant's survival is threatened by habitat destruction, making it a species of conservation concern.
