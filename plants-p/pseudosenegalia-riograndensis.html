Pseudosenegalia riograndensis

Pseudosenegalia riograndensis, commonly known as the Rio Grande acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to the arid and semi-arid regions of South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is adapted to drought conditions, with a deep root system that allows it to thrive in poor soils.

It plays an important ecological role by fixing nitrogen and providing habitat for various insects and animals.
