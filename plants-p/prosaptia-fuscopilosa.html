Prosaptia fuscopilosa

Prosaptia fuscopilosa, commonly known as the black-spined orchid, is a rare and striking orchid species native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dark, velvety petals and sepals, which give it its distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in cool, humid environments. Its flowering period occurs during the late spring to early summer, producing clusters of fragrant, tubular flowers.
