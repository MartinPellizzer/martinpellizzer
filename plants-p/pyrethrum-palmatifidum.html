Pyrethrum palmatifidum

Pyrethrum palmatifidum, commonly known as the broadleaf pyrethrum, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its large, palmately divided leaves. The plant produces yellowish flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening due to its striking foliage and showy blooms.

The plant is also noted for its potential use in natural pest control, as it contains pyrethrins, which are natural insecticides.
