Polygala heisteria

Polygala heisteria, commonly known as the Heister's milkweed, is a perennial herb native to the southern United States.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its slender, often branched stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in open woodlands and sandy soils, preferring partial to full sun. Its flowers are usually pink or purple, with a distinctive spurred structure that attracts specific pollinators.

The leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a rough texture, contributing to its overall ecological niche.
