Piper excultum

Piper excultum, commonly known as the black pepper plant, is a climbing vine native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its small, dark purple berries that are used to produce the spice black pepper. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with a rough texture and is often cultivated for its aromatic fruits. It thrives in humid, warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

The berries undergo a drying process to develop their characteristic pungent flavor and dark color.
