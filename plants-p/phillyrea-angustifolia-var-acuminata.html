Phillyrea angustifolia var. acuminata

Phillyrea angustifolia var. acuminata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved Phillyrea, is a shrub or small tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically evergreen and have a leathery texture. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring. Its fruits are small, round berries that turn from green to red as they mature.

The species is well adapted to dry, sunny conditions and is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
