Phalangium algeriense var. brachylepis

Phalangium algeriense var. brachylepis, commonly known as the Algerian scorpion grass, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its erect, branched stems and small, greenish flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are often covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.
