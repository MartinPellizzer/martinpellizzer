Polygonum minus subsp. micranthum

Polygonum minus subsp.

micranthum, commonly known as narrowleaf smartweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to North America. It typically grows in moist environments such as wet meadows, marshes, and along stream banks.

The plant has alternate, lance-shaped leaves that are often reddish at the base, and it produces small, clustered flowers in late summer. Its flowers are usually pink to purplish in color and are followed by small, three-lobed seed capsules.

The species is characterized by its upright growth habit and is often found in disturbed or wetland habitats.
