Philodendron grandipes

Philodendron grandipes, commonly known as the giant philodendron, is a tropical climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 inches in length. The plant has a vigorous growth habit and can reach heights of several meters when provided with support. Its stems are typically green or reddish in color and are covered with a waxy cuticle.

Philodendron grandipes thrives in bright, indirect light and requires consistently moist, well-draining soil.
