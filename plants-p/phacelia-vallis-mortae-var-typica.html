Phacelia vallis-mortae var. typica

Phacelia vallis-mortae var. typica, commonly known as the Death Valley phacelia, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States.

It is a low-growing annual herb with slender stems and small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has hairy leaves and is adapted to withstand drought conditions through its deep root system. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in desert scrub habitats.

The species is notable for its ornamental value and its role in supporting local pollinator populations.
