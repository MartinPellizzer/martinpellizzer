Phyllanthus caesiifolius

Phyllanthus caesiifolius, commonly known as the blue-leaf phyllanthus, is a tropical shrub native to regions of Africa, southern Asia, and the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its striking blue-green leaves, which are opposite and ovate in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by dark blue or black berries. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories or along stream banks.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
