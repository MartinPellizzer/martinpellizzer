Picea meyeri var. pyramidalis

Picea meyeri var. pyramidalis, commonly known as the Meyer's fir, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its narrow, pyramidal shape and dark green, needle-like leaves. The tree produces small, cone-like structures that contain seeds, typical of the conifer family. It thrives in cold climates and well-drained soils, making it suitable for alpine and subalpine environments.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in cooler regions.
