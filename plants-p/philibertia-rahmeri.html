Philibertia rahmeri

Philibertia rahmeri, commonly known as the desert dandelion, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, gray-green leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring, followed by fluffy, white seed heads that resemble dandelion fluff. It is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments with minimal water.

Philibertia rahmeri is often used in traditional medicine and is also a popular ornamental plant due to its drought tolerance and aesthetic appeal.
