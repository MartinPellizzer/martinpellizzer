Pedicularis mixta

Pedicularis mixta, commonly known as the hybrid monkey orchid, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the genus Pedicularis, which includes over 300 species of flowering plants commonly referred to as louse plants due to their leaf-like structures. This particular species is characterized by its striking, spurred flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, often resembling small monkeys due to their unique shape. It thrives in alpine and subalpine environments, preferring well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade.

The plant is known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant and unusual blooms.
