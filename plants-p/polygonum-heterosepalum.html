Polygonum heterosepalum

Polygonum heterosepalum, commonly known as the heterosepal morning glory, is a species of flowering plant in the family Polygonaceae.

It is native to parts of Southeast Asia, including Indonesia and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that typically range in color from pink to purple. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a distinctive veining pattern.

Polygonum heterosepalum is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant blooms and climbing habit.
