Parryopsis villosa

Parryopsis villosa, commonly known as the silky parryopsis, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a small shrub that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often found in arid or semi-arid regions. The plant features small, pale green leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its flowers are usually yellow or greenish-yellow and are clustered in dense, terminal inflorescences.

Parryopsis villosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
