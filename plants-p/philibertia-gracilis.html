Philibertia gracilis

Philibertia gracilis, commonly known as the slender filbert, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in grassland habitats. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers in clusters. Its fruit is a hard, three-lobed nut enclosed in a fibrous husk.

Philibertia gracilis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
