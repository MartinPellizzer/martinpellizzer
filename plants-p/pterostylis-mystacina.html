Pterostylis mystacina

Pterostylis mystacina, commonly known as the hooded greenhood, is an orchid species native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its unique inflorescence, which consists of a single, elongated spike bearing several greenish-yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as rainforests and understoreys. Its flowers have a distinctive hood-like structure that protects the reproductive organs, a feature common in many orchids.

Pterostylis mystacina is often found in association with specific mycorrhizal fungi, which are essential for its germination and growth.
