Philadelphus incanus var. sargentianus

Philadelphus incanus var. sargentianus, commonly known as the Sargent's mock orange, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has slender, arching branches and dark green, ovate leaves that turn yellow in autumn. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for garden cultivation.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical significance in horticulture.
