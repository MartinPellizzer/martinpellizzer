Panicum sanguinale subvar. marginatum

Panicum sanguinale subvar. marginatum, commonly known as red panicgrass, is a perennial grass native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features slender, erect stems that can grow up to 1.5 meters tall, often with a reddish tinge. The leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a distinctive reddish margin that gives it its common name. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets that are typically pink to red in color, adding ornamental value.

This plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open areas, disturbed sites, and along roadsides.
