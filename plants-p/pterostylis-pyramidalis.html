Pterostylis pyramidalis

Pterostylis pyramidalis, commonly known as the pyramidal greenhood, is an orchid species native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its unique, hood-like flower structure that is typically green with purple or brown markings. The plant produces a single, erect stem that bears a single flower, which is often adorned with intricate patterns. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically in rainforest or heathland areas.

The species is notable for its specialized pollination mechanism, relying on specific insect species for reproduction.
