Phyteuma graminifolium

Phyteuma graminifolium, commonly known as the slender phyteuma, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges. The plant features slender, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of bell-shaped, usually yellow or orange flowers. Its flowering period occurs in late summer to early autumn, and it is often found in sandy or loamy soils.

Phyteuma graminifolium is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to pollinators.
