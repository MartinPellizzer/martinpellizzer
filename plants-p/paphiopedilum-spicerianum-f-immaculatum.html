Paphiopedilum spicerianum f. immaculatum

Paphiopedilum spicerianum f. immaculatum, commonly known as the immaculate spicerian orchid, is a rare and highly sought-after hybrid of the spicerian orchid.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, elongated floral structure with a distinctive pouch-like lip. The plant typically features dark green, leathery leaves and produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink. It is native to Southeast Asia and thrives in tropical climates with high humidity and indirect light.

Due to its rarity and ornamental value, it is often cultivated in greenhouses and botanical gardens.
