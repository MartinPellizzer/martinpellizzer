Phaeocephalum grayi

Phaeocephalum grayi, commonly known as the gray beard lichen, is a foliose lichen that thrives in cool, moist environments.

It is characterized by its dark gray to black thallus with a distinctive, fuzzy margin. The lichen forms a symbiotic relationship between a green alga and a fungus, which allows it to absorb nutrients from the air and rain. It is typically found on tree bark, rocks, and soil in temperate regions.

Due to its slow growth and sensitivity to environmental changes, it is often used as an indicator of air quality.
