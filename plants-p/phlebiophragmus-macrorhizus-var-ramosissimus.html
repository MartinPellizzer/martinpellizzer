Phlebiophragmus macrorhizus var. ramosissimus

Phlebiophragmus macrorhizus var. ramosissimus, commonly known as the ramose root moss, is a rare and specialized member of the Bryophyta division.

It is characterized by its dense, cushion-like growth form and its ability to thrive in moist, shaded environments. The plant features intricate, branching structures that give it its varietal name, "ramosissimus," meaning highly branched. Its root system is extensive and plays a crucial role in nutrient absorption and soil stabilization.

This moss is often found in temperate regions, contributing to the ecological diversity of forest floors.
