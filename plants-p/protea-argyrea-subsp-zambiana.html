Protea argyrea subsp. zambiana

Protea argyrea subsp.

zambiana, commonly known as the Zambian silver protea, is a striking South African plant belonging to the Proteaceae family. It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescences that consist of numerous white to pale pink flowers arranged in a cylindrical shape.

The plant is native to the eastern regions of South Africa, including parts of KwaZulu-Natal and the Eastern Cape. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in rocky or wooded areas.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and is also of interest to botanists and horticulturists.
