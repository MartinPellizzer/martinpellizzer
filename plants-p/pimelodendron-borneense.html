Pimelodendron borneense

Pimelodendron borneense, commonly known as the Bornean pimelodine, is a species of flowering plant native to the rainforests of Borneo.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is characterized by its woody stem and alternate leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in the undergrowth of tropical forests.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
