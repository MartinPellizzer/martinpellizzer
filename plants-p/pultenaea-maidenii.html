Pultenaea maidenii

Pultenaea maidenii, commonly known as the Maiden's pultenaea, is a small shrub native to eastern Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its distinctive pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant typically grows in heathlands and rocky areas, preferring well-drained soils. Its small, pea-like flowers are usually yellow or orange, appearing in spring, and it produces slender, linear pods that contain seeds.

Pultenaea maidenii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
