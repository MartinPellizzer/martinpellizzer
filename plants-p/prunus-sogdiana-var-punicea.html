Prunus sogdiana var. punicea

Prunus sogdiana var. punicea, commonly known as the crimson Chinese apricot, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its striking pink to reddish flowers that appear in early spring, often before the leaves emerge. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape and have a smooth or slightly serrated margin. Its fruit is a small, yellowish drupe that ripens in late summer.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
