Piper arieianum

Piper arieianum, commonly known as the Amazonian pepper or wild pepper, is a climbing shrub native to the rainforests of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many species of pepper plants. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are followed by berries containing aromatic seeds. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a distinctive, pungent scent.

The plant is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional medicine for its potential anti-inflammatory and analgesic effects.
