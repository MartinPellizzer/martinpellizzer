Pachystigma rhodesianum

Pachystigma rhodesianum, commonly known as the Rhodesian pachystigma, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that store water, allowing it to survive in arid environments. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Due to its unique appearance and low maintenance, it is a popular choice among succulent enthusiasts.
