Primula elatior var. tatrensis

Primula elatior var. tatrensis, commonly known as the Tatran primrose, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the alpine regions of Central Europe.

It typically grows in rocky and grassy slopes at high altitudes, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. The plant features rosettes of narrow, dark green leaves and produces clusters of fragrant, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens that mimic its natural habitat.

Due to its specific ecological requirements, it is considered a rare and sensitive species.
