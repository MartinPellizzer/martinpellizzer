Phippsia angustata

Phippsia angustata, commonly known as the narrowleaf phippsia, is a perennial herb native to specific regions of North America.

It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or open areas. The plant features slender, upright stems with alternate, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. Its small, clustered flowers are usually white or pale green, blooming in late spring to early summer.

Phippsia angustata is valued for its ecological role in supporting local pollinators and its ornamental appeal in native plant gardens.
