Petastoma puniceum

Petastoma puniceum, commonly known as the redstem aster, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, pink to purple composite flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as meadows and prairies. It has hairy, narrow leaves and produces seeds in small, spherical clusters.

Petastoma puniceum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to pollinators.
