Pavonia consobrina

Pavonia consobrina, commonly known as the scarlet wood rose, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a deciduous shrub that produces showy, red or pink flowers with five petals. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and have a distinctive, slightly serrated edge. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Pavonia consobrina is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
