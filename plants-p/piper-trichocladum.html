Piper trichocladum

Piper trichocladum, commonly known as the thick-leaved pepper plant, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including parts of India, Malaysia, and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and small, clustered yellowish-green flowers. It produces small, round berries that are used in traditional medicine and as a spice.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and its potential medicinal properties.
