Pomatocalpa undulatum subsp. acuminatum

Pomatocalpa undulatum subsp.

acuminatum, commonly known as the acuminated marsh orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia. It typically grows in wet or marshy habitats, often near water bodies.

The plant features a slender, erect stem with a single, showy flower that has a distinctive pouch-like structure. Its leaves are usually narrow and grass-like, and the flower is characterized by its undulating lip.

This subspecies is notable for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
