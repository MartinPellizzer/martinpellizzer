Pycnocycla mesomorpha

Pycnocycla mesomorpha, commonly known as the red spruce, is a coniferous tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and distinctive cone-like structures that mature into woody cones. The plant has a slow growth rate and thrives in cool, moist environments, often found in mountainous regions. Its bark is thick and scaly, providing protection against harsh weather conditions.

The species plays an important ecological role in forest ecosystems, supporting a variety of wildlife.
