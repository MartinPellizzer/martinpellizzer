Pseudocymopterus humboldtensis

Pseudocymopterus humboldtensis, commonly known as the Humboldt's ghost orchid, is a rare and striking orchid native to the coastal regions of South America.

It is characterized by its elongated, pale green leaves and its unique, showy inflorescence that resembles a ghostly figure. The plant is epiphytic, meaning it grows on the surface of trees without being parasitic, and thrives in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers, which bloom in late summer, are typically white or pale pink and have a delicate, almost translucent appearance.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered endangered and is of great interest to botanists and conservationists.
