Penstemon pallescens

Penstemon pallescens, commonly known as the bluish beardtongue, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of blue to violet tubular flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to arid environments.

Penstemon pallescens is often found in open meadows and along mountain slopes.
