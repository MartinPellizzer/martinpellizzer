Pentaphragma longisepalum

Pentaphragma longisepalum, commonly known as the long-sepal pentaphragma, is a species of orchid native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact, herbaceous growth habit and possesses a single, elongated stem that bears several flowering spikes. The flowers are small, with showy sepals and petals that are often brightly colored, typically in shades of white, yellow, or green. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its unique floral structure and limited distribution, it is of interest to botanists and orchid enthusiasts alike.
