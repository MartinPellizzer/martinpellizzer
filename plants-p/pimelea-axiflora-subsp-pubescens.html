Pimelea axiflora subsp. pubescens

Pimelea axiflora subsp.

pubescens, commonly known as the hairy swamp bottlebrush, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Australia. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and dense, cylindrical clusters of bright red or orange flowers.

The subspecies is distinguished by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive appearance. It typically grows in wet or swampy areas, thriving in moist, acidic soils.

This plant plays an important role in local ecosystems, providing nectar for native pollinators.
