Pinalia opeatoloba

Pinalia opeatoloba, commonly known as the golden bell tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has a woody stem and broad, ovate leaves with a smooth, glossy surface. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Pinalia opeatoloba is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
