Piper coclense

Piper coclense, commonly known as the sacred pepper or the pepper of the gods, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces small, yellowish-green fruits that are often used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are widely used in culinary and spiritual practices due to their stimulating properties.

Piper coclense thrives in tropical climates and is often cultivated for both its medicinal and aromatic value.
