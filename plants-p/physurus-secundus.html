Physurus secundus

Physurus secundus, commonly known as the narrow-leafed sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Africa.

It typically grows in wet or marshy environments, often found in swampy areas or along the edges of water bodies. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are usually arranged in a tight, compact rosette. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are clustered in spikelets, and it produces tiny, three-lobed fruits.

Physurus secundus plays a role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various small organisms.
