Pulicaria persica

Pulicaria persica, commonly known as Persian sandwort, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Iran and Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its small, yellowish flowers and hairy foliage. The plant is typically found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. It has small, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and its flowers bloom in clusters during the spring and summer months.

Pulicaria persica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements.
