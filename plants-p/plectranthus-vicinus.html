Plectranthus vicinus

Plectranthus vicinus, commonly known as the narrow-leaf plectranthus, is a perennial herb native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters and features opposite, narrow, lance-shaped leaves. Its small, tubular flowers are usually violet or purple in color and bloom in clusters.

Plectranthus vicinus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
