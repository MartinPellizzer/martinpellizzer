Polygonum capitatum var. macilentum

Polygonum capitatum var. macilentum, commonly known as the slender knotweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers that appear in late summer. The plant has slender, hollow stems that are often covered with fine hairs. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly rough texture.

This variant is distinguished by its more delicate appearance compared to other Polygonum species.
