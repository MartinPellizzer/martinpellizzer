Pelea glabra

Pelea glabra, commonly known as the smooth pelea, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories. Its leaves are smooth and lance-shaped, with a distinct basal rosette formation.

Pelea glabra produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
