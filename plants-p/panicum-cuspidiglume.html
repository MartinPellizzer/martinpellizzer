Panicum cuspidiglume

Panicum cuspidiglume, commonly known as the spikegrass, is a species of grass native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, blade-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Its leaves are often covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in arid environments.

Panicum cuspidiglume is often found in open fields, disturbed soils, and along roadsides.
