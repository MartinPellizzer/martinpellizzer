Pleurothallis bahiensis

Pleurothallis bahiensis, commonly known as the Bahia orchid, is a species of orchid native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its small, delicate flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid tropical environments. Its flowers are usually white or pale yellow, with a distinctive lip that is often marked with purple or brown.

Pleurothallis bahiensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in greenhouses or as a houseplant.
