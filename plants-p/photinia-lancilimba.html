Photinia lancilimba

Photinia lancilimba, commonly known as the narrow-leaved photinia, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to parts of Southeast Asia, including Malaysia and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its elliptical, dark green leaves that are narrow and pointed. It produces showy pink to red flowers in clusters during the spring and summer months.

The fruit is a small, red drupe that persists on the plant throughout the year, providing food for various bird species.
