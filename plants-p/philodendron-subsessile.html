Philodendron subsessile

Philodendron subsessile, commonly known as the creeping philodendron, is a tropical climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It features heart-shaped, glossy leaves that are typically deep green with a slightly waxy texture. The plant has a rhizomatous root system that allows it to spread and form dense ground covers. It thrives in bright, indirect light and prefers warm, humid environments.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for indoor gardening and ornamental purposes.
