Piliostigma rufescens

Piliostigma rufescens, commonly known as the red African bristlecone, is a deciduous shrub native to sub-Saharan Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky soils. It produces small, pod-like fruits that contain seeds dispersed by wind.

Piliostigma rufescens is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional African medicine.
