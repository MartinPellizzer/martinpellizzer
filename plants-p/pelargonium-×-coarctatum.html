Pelargonium × coarctatum

Pelargonium × coarctatum, commonly known as the compact geranium, is a hybrid plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, rounded growth habit and small, velvety leaves that are typically dark green. The plant produces clusters of showy, five-petaled flowers in shades of red, pink, or white, often blooming from spring to early summer. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Its compact form and ornamental value make it a versatile addition to both indoor and outdoor landscapes.
