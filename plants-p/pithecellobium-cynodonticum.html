Pithecellobium cynodonticum

Pithecellobium cynodonticum, commonly known as the velvet tamarind, is a small evergreen tree native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of yellow flowers. The plant produces small, edible pods that contain a sweet, sticky pulp surrounded by a hard shell.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
