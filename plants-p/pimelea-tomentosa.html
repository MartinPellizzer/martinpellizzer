Pimelea tomentosa

Pimelea tomentosa, commonly known as the woolly bottlebrush, is a shrub native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and bright red or orange flower spikes that appear in spring. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal areas. Its unique appearance and hardy nature make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

The flowers attract nectar-feeding birds and insects, contributing to local biodiversity.
