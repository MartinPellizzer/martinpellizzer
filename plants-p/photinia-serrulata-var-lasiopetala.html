Photinia serrulata var. lasiopetala

Photinia serrulata var. lasiopetala, commonly known as the pink-leaf photinia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It is characterized by its striking pink to reddish-bronze foliage, which provides ornamental value throughout the growing season. The plant produces small, white flowers in the spring, followed by red or orange berries that persist into winter. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for gardens and landscapes.

Its compact growth habit and seasonal color changes make it a popular choice for ornamental horticulture.
