Pseudima

Pseudima, commonly known as the false nightshade, is a genus of flowering plants in the nightshade family, Solanaceae.

It consists of several species that are typically shrubs or small trees, native to regions in Africa, the Middle East, and parts of Asia. The plants are characterized by their simple, alternate leaves and showy, often yellow or white flowers. Pseudima species are known for their toxic properties, containing alkaloids that can be harmful to humans and animals.

They are sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes due to their attractive appearance.
