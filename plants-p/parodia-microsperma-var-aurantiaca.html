Parodia microsperma var. aurantiaca

Parodia microsperma var. aurantiaca, commonly known as the Orange Ghost Cactus, is a compact, globular cactus native to southern Africa.

It features a pale green to bluish-green stem with a distinctive orange or red coloration on the areoles, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer. It is a slow-growing species that thrives in well-draining soil and full sun.

Due to its unique appearance and compact size, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and container cultivation.
