Peperomia cookiana var. flavinerva

Peperomia cookiana var. flavinerva, commonly known as the golden peperomia, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to the humid lowland forests of Ecuador.

It features glossy, oval-shaped leaves that are typically a vibrant green with a subtle yellow or gold hue on the underside. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually greenish-white in color, which are followed by bright red berries. It thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Its ornamental foliage makes it a sought-after plant for collectors and enthusiasts of tropical houseplants.
