Potentilla lindenbergii

Potentilla lindenbergii, commonly known as Lindenberg's cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit with multiple stems. The plant produces small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are typically trifoliate, with three leaflets that are oval-shaped and serrated.

Potentilla lindenbergii is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes, where it thrives in well-drained soils.
