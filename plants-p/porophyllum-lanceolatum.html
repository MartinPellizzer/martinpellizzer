Porophyllum lanceolatum

Porophyllum lanceolatum, commonly known as the narrowleaf fleabane, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. The plant features slender, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers. It is part of the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its composite flower heads.

Porophyllum lanceolatum is often found in disturbed areas and is adapted to arid environments.
