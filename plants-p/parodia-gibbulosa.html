Parodia gibbulosa

Parodia gibbulosa, commonly known as the "Cactus of the Gods," is a species of cactus native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its short, thick stems covered in dense, white spines. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, adding a striking contrast to its green body. It is well adapted to harsh desert conditions, storing water in its stem and having a shallow root system.

Parodia gibbulosa is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its low maintenance and aesthetic appeal.
