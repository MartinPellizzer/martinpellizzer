Potamogeton gramineus f. stagnalis

Potamogeton gramineus f. stagnalis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in North America.

It has long, narrow, ribbon-like leaves that are typically bright green and arranged in whorls around the stem. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually green or yellowish, which develop into small, elongated fruits. It thrives in shallow, slow-moving waters and plays a vital role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.

Its presence is often an indicator of good water quality in ponds and lakes.
