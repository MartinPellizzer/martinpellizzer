Pilosella cilicica

Pilosella cilicica, commonly known as the Cilician hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant has slender, hairy stems and basal leaves that are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs. Its yellow flowers are arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, typical of the genus Pilosella.

It thrives in rocky and dry habitats, often found in mountainous areas of Turkey and southern Europe.
