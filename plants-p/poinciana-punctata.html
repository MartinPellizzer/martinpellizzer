Poinciana punctata

Poinciana punctata, commonly known as the flame tree, is a tropical deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, feathery, red or orange-pink inflorescences that bloom in summer. The tree has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets arranged in pairs. It thrives in warm, sunny climates and is often planted for its ornamental value.

The bark is smooth and light grey, and the tree can grow up to 20 meters tall.
