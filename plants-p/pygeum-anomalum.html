Pygeum anomalum

Pygeum anomalum, commonly known as the African wild almond, is a deciduous tree native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Proteaceae and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and compound leaves. The plant produces small, white flowers followed by woody, cone-like fruits containing hard seeds. It is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often growing on rocky soils.
