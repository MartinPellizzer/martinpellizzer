Polygala serpyllacea var. pyxophylla

Polygala serpyllacea var. pyxophylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaf milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. The plant features slender, branched stems and small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often covered in fine hairs.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its resilience and drought tolerance.
