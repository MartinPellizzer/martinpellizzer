Pinus sylvestris var. armena

Pinus sylvestris var. armena, commonly known as the Armenian pine, is a variant of the Scots pine that is native to the Caucasus region.

It is a coniferous evergreen tree characterized by its needle-like leaves and cone-bearing structure. The needles are typically blue-green in color and grow in bundles of two, while the bark is thick and fissured. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in mountainous areas with well-drained soil.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in local ecosystems.
