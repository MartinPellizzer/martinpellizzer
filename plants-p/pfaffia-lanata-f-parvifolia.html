Pfaffia lanata f. parvifolia

Pfaffia lanata f. parvifolia, commonly known as Parvifolia Ginseng, is a botanical variant of the genus Pfaffia, native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its small, lance-shaped leaves and yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in the undergrowth of rainforests. Its root system is valued for its potential medicinal properties, though research on its specific uses remains limited.
