Potamogeton × maemetsiae

Potamogeton × maemetsiae, commonly known as the hybrid water plantain, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Potamogeton perfoliatus and Potamogeton crispus.

It is a submerged aquatic plant characterized by its narrow, linear leaves that often have a waxy coating. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are typically arranged in spike-like clusters. It is found in freshwater habitats such as ponds, lakes, and slow-moving streams.

Due to its hybrid origin, it exhibits a range of morphological variations, making it a subject of interest in botanical studies.
