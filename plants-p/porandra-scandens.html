Porandra scandens

Porandra scandens, commonly known as the climbing morning glory, is a tropical vine native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its rapid growth and distinctive heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires support for its climbing habit.

Porandra scandens is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to cover trellises or fences.
