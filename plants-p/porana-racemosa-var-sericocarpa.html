Porana racemosa var. sericocarpa

Porana racemosa var. sericocarpa, commonly known as the silky-fruited porana, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and small, clustered flowers that bloom in shades of white and pink. The fruit is distinctive for its silky, hairy covering, which gives the variety its common name. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for botanical gardens and horticultural collections.
