Panax vietnamensis var. vietnamensis

Panax vietnamensis var. vietnamensis, commonly known as Vietnamese ginseng, is a perennial herb native to Vietnam.

It belongs to the Araliaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, branched root system. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist forest environments, often in mountainous regions. Its leaves are compound, with a serrated edge, and it produces small, greenish-yellow flowers.

The roots are valued in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
