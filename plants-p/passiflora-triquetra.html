Passiflora triquetra

Passiflora triquetra, commonly known as the three-lobed passionflower, is a climbing vine native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, intricate flowers that feature five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments surrounding the reproductive structures. The plant produces edible, tropical berries that are often used in culinary applications. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three distinct leaflets, and it thrives in warm, humid climates.

Passiflora triquetra is also valued for its ornamental beauty and has been cultivated for both horticultural and medicinal purposes.
