Paraphlomis hirsuta

Paraphlomis hirsuta, commonly known as hairy parley, is a perennial herb native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the family Lamiaceae, which includes many aromatic plants known for their medicinal properties. The plant is characterized by its hairy, branched stems and opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges. It produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, arranged in whorled clusters.

The species is valued for its use in traditional medicine and as a ornamental plant due to its distinctive appearance.
