Phalaris laxa

Phalaris laxa, commonly known as slender false brome, is a perennial grass native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist meadows, riverbanks, and wetlands, preferring well-drained to wet soils. The plant has slender, upright stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often slightly hairy. Its inflorescence consists of a loose panicle of small, spikelet-like flowers, which are usually green to reddish in color.

It plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
