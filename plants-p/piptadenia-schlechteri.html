Piptadenia schlechteri

Piptadenia schlechteri, commonly known as the Brazilian trumpet tree, is a tropical leguminous plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy yellow flowers that resemble trumpets, which bloom in clusters. The plant has compound leaves with oval-shaped leaflets and produces long, slender pods that contain flat, bean-like seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in lowland forests and along riverbanks.

Its rapid growth and ornamental value make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and reforestation projects.
