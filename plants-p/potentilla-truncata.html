Potentilla truncata

Potentilla truncata, commonly known as the cutleaf cinquefoil, is a perennial flowering plant native to North America.

It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks. The plant features compound leaves with rounded lobes and produces showy yellow flowers in early summer. Its root system is fibrous and often used in traditional herbal medicine.

Potentilla truncata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in supporting pollinators.
