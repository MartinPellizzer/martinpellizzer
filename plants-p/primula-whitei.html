Primula whitei

Primula whitei, commonly known as the White's primrose, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and has lance-shaped leaves with a hairy texture. It produces small, clustered flowers that bloom in early spring, making it a prized addition to gardens.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
