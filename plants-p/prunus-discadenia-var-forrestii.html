Prunus discadenia var. forrestii

Prunus discadenia var. forrestii, commonly known as the Chinese plum or forrest's plum, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical to ovate in shape. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in spring, followed by edible drupes that are typically red or yellow when mature. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional Chinese medicine.

The species is also valued for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
