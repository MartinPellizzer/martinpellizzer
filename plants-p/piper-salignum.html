Piper salignum

Piper salignum, commonly known as the saltbush pepper or black pepper tree, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its small, clustered flowers and dark, peppery fruits. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a slightly serrated edge.

The fruits are often used in traditional medicine and are also a source of essential oils.
