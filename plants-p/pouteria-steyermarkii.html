Pouteria steyermarkii

Pouteria steyermarkii, commonly known as the Brazilian nut tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Amazon Basin.

It is characterized by its large, elongated leaves and clusters of yellowish-green flowers. The tree produces distinctive, woody pods that contain edible seeds, which are the source of the commercially valuable Brazil nuts. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil.

The plant plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, supporting a variety of wildlife.
