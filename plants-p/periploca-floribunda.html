Periploca floribunda

Periploca floribunda, commonly known as the flowering periploca, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its woody stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers, which are followed by orange-red berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and rocky habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
