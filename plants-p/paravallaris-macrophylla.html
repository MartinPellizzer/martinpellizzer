Paravallaris macrophylla

Paravallaris macrophylla, commonly known as the giant water lily, is a striking aquatic plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, broad leaves that can reach up to 3 meters in diameter, providing a unique habitat for various aquatic organisms. The plant has a robust, floating stem that supports its expansive foliage and roots that anchor it in shallow water. Its flowers are showy, with white petals and yellow stamens, often blooming at night.

Paravallaris macrophylla plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by offering shelter and food for fish and invertebrates.
