Polygonum uviferum

Polygonum uviferum, commonly known as the red root or Indian spinach, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its sprawling growth habit and reddish stems, which are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in summer, and its leaves are edible, though they have a slightly bitter taste. Polygonum uviferum is also known for its ability to thrive in moist, shady environments, making it a common sight in woodland areas.

Its roots are rich in mucilage and have been used historically for their soothing properties.
