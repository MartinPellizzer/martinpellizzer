Pandanus calicarpus

Pandanus calicarpus, commonly known as the bottlebrush pandanus, is a tropical plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feather-like leaves that form a dense crown. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or green, arranged in clusters at the tips of the leaves. Its unique appearance makes it a popular ornamental plant in tropical gardens.

The fruit, which is a spiky, fleshy structure, is edible and used in local cuisines.
