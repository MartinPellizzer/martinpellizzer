Pilea brevistipula

Pilea brevistipula, commonly known as the Chinese money plant, is a species of flowering plant in the family Araceae.

It is native to China and is characterized by its rosette-forming leaves that resemble small coins. The plant produces compact, spadix-like inflorescences surrounded by a hooded spathe. It thrives in bright, indirect light and prefers well-draining soil.

Pilea brevistipula is popular as a decorative houseplant due to its attractive foliage and ease of care.
