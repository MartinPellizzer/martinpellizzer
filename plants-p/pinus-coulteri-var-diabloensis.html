Pinus coulteri var. diabloensis

Pinus coulteri var. diabloensis, commonly known as the Diablo red fir, is a variant of the Coulter pine native to California.

It is characterized by its slender, conical shape and dark reddish-brown bark. The tree produces long, slender cones that contain edible seeds, which are an important food source for local wildlife. Its needles are dense and dark green, providing dense foliage that offers habitat for various species.

This variant is adapted to the dry, rocky soils of the Diablo Range, showcasing resilience to harsh environmental conditions.
