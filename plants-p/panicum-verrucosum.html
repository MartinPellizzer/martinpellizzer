Panicum verrucosum

Panicum verrucosum, commonly known as rough bluestem, is a flowering plant in the grass family, Poaceae.

It is native to the southeastern United States and thrives in wetland habitats such as marshes and swamps. The plant is characterized by its upright growth habit and dense clusters of spikelets that produce small, blue-green flowers. Its leaves are typically narrow and covered with fine, rough hairs, giving it a distinctive texture.

Panicum verrucosum plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
