Pombalia cristalina

Pombalia cristalina, commonly known as the crystal leaf plant, is a unique species native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its translucent, waxy leaves that reflect light in a crystalline manner, giving it its common name. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in cloud forests. Its growth is slow, and it produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pale pink.

Due to its rarity and striking appearance, it is highly valued in botanical gardens and conservation efforts.
