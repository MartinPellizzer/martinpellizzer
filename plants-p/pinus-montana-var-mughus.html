Pinus montana var. mughus

Pinus montana var. mughus, commonly known as the Mughal pine, is a variant of the white pine native to the Himalayas.

It is a coniferous evergreen tree characterized by its slender, pyramidal shape and long, needle-like leaves. The tree produces cones that are typically reddish-brown and contain edible seeds. It thrives in high-altitude regions with cold climates and well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation areas.
