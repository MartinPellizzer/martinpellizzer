Poa brevipaniculata var. subpallida

Poa brevipaniculata var. subpallida, commonly known as the pale form of the short panicle grass, is a grass species native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and pale blue-green foliage. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are typically shorter than those of the typical variety. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and meadows.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and ecological importance in supporting local insect populations.
