Palaquium hexandrum f. majus

Palaquium hexandrum f. majus, commonly known as the large-leaf palaquium, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, elliptical leaves that are often glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, arranged in clusters. Its fruit is a drupe, containing a single seed enclosed in a fleshy, edible pulp.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
