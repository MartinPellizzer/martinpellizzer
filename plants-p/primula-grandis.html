Primula grandis

Primula grandis, commonly known as the large primrose, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe and western Asia.

It features upright, hairy stems that bear clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, yellow, and pink. The leaves are broadly ovate with a slightly toothed margin and a hairy surface. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in woodland or alpine habitats.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens.
