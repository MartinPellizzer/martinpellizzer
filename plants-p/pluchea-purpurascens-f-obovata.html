Pluchea purpurascens f. obovata

Pluchea purpurascens f. obovata, commonly known as the purple false dandelion, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, purple flowers that resemble dandelions. The plant features heart-shaped, hairy leaves and produces small, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardeners seeking unique, native plants.
