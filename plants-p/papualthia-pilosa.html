Papualthia pilosa

Papualthia pilosa, commonly known as the hairy papualthia, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and dense covering of soft, white hairs that give it a fuzzy appearance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, typically blooming during the cooler months. Its leaves are narrow and succulent, adapted to conserve water in its harsh environment.

Papualthia pilosa is often found in rocky outcrops and sandy soils, where it thrives in full sun.
