Piper urubambanum

Piper urubambanum, commonly known as the Amazonian pepper or Urubamba pepper, is a flowering plant native to the Amazon rainforest.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many species of pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its climbing habit, with slender stems that often twine around other vegetation. Its leaves are typically alternate, ovate, and have a glossy surface.

The small, greenish-white flowers are clustered in spikes, and the fruit is a small, yellowish berry that ripens to a bright red.
