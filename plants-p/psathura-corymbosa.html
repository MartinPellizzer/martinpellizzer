Psathura corymbosa

Psathura corymbosa, commonly known as the clustered bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high elevations, often in alpine or subalpine habitats. The plant features a basal rosette of lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue or violet. Its flowering period occurs in late summer to early autumn, and it is known for its ornamental value in gardens.

Psathura corymbosa is also valued for its ability to thrive in harsh environmental conditions.
