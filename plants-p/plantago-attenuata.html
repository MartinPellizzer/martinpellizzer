Plantago attenuata

Plantago attenuata, commonly known as narrowleaf plantain, is a perennial herb native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It features erect, branching stems that bear oval, hairy leaves with a distinctive wavy margin. The plant produces small, greenish flowers in clusters, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found along roadsides and in meadows.

Narrowleaf plantain is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally for its healing benefits.
