Plerandra seemannii

Plerandra seemannii, commonly known as the silver tree, is a striking evergreen shrub or small tree native to tropical Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, silvery-green foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, white flowers followed by bright red berries, which are an important food source for native wildlife. Plerandra seemannii thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and inland regions.

Its unique appearance and ecological value make it a notable species in Australian flora.
