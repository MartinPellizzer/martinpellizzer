Parodia tarabucina

Parodia tarabucina, commonly known as the Moon Cactus, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its compact, globular shape and dense, spiny foliage. The plant produces yellow flowers during the summer months, adding a striking contrast to its green body. It is a popular choice for indoor gardening due to its low maintenance and unique appearance.

Its ability to store water in its thick stems makes it highly drought-tolerant.
