Polyachyrus litoralis

Polyachyrus litoralis, commonly known as the coastal grass.

It is a perennial grass species native to coastal regions, characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow leaves. The plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in dune environments. It plays a vital role in stabilizing coastal dunes and preventing erosion.

Its flowering spikes are typically greenish or reddish, appearing in late summer to early autumn.
