Petasites japonicus

Petasites japonicus, commonly known as the Japanese buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the family Polygonaceae and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense colonies.
