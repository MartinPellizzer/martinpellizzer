Potentilla argentea f. septenata

Potentilla argentea f. septenata, commonly known as the silver potentilla or white potentilla, is a flowering plant belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its silvery-white, hairy leaves and clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. Native to the mountainous regions of Europe, this plant thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and full sun exposure. The species is noted for its ornamental value, often cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and delicate blooms.

Its botanical features make it a hardy and resilient plant suitable for alpine gardens and rockeries.
