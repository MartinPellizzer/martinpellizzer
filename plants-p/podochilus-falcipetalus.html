Podochilus falcipetalus

Podochilus falcipetalus, commonly known as the narrow-leafed orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and produces striking, elongated flowers with a distinctive falcate (scythe-shaped) petal. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often in forest understories. Its flowers are usually pale yellow to greenish-white, with a prominent lip that is often darker in color.

Podochilus falcipetalus is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is relatively easy to cultivate under the right conditions.
