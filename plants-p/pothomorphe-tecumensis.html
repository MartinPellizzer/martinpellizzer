Pothomorphe tecumensis

Pothomorphe tecumensis, commonly known as the rock cress, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is a small, herbaceous perennial that typically grows in rocky or limestone soils. The plant features basal leaves that are oval to lance-shaped with a rough texture. Its flowers are small, yellow, and clustered in loose racemes, blooming in spring.

Pothomorphe tecumensis is adapted to arid conditions and is often found in coastal or mountainous regions.
