Persea cathia

Persea cathia, commonly known as the Mexican persimmon, is a small evergreen tree native to Central America and Mexico.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like laurel and avocado. The tree produces small, yellow-green fruits that are typically harvested when fully ripe. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a glossy surface with a distinctive aromatic scent.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and its edible fruit, which is rich in nutrients.
