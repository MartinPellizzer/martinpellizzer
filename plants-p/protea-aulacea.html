Protea aulacea

Protea aulacea, commonly known as the silver protea, is a striking South African plant belonging to the Proteaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, fluffy inflorescence that resembles a cluster of small flowers, often with a white or pale pink coloration. The plant is native to the fynbos biome and thrives in well-drained, acidic soils. Its unique appearance makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Protea aulacea is also valued for its ecological role, providing nectar for pollinators like birds and insects.
