Plantago alopecura

Plantago alopecura, commonly known as the hairy plantain, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features a basal rosette of broad, hairy leaves that are typically green to bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in a spike-like inflorescence during the summer months. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their healing properties.

Botanically, it belongs to the family Plantaginaceae and is characterized by its mucilage-rich tissues.
