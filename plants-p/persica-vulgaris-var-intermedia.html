Persica vulgaris var. intermedia

Persica vulgaris var. intermedia, commonly known as the wild peach or apricot.

This plant is a species of the Rosaceae family, characterized by its deciduous nature and thorned stems. It produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by fruits that are typically small and tart. The leaves are alternate, simple, and oval-shaped with serrated edges.

It is often found in woodland edges and rocky soils across temperate regions.
