Primulina bipinnatifida var. bipinnatifida

Primulina bipinnatifida var. bipinnatifida, commonly known as the two-pinnate primulina, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves that are deeply lobed and often have a waxy texture. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, or red, which bloom during the warmer months. Its foliage is evergreen, making it a popular choice for indoor cultivation due to its ornamental appeal.

It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires consistent moisture and well-draining soil for optimal growth.
