Piper hoffmannseggianum

Piper hoffmannseggianum, commonly known as the Hoffmannsegg's pepper vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper plants, and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. The plant produces small, peppery fruits that are typically consumed raw or used in local cuisines.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for both horticultural study and culinary use.
