Plantago durvillei

Plantago durvillei, commonly known as the Durville's plantain, is a species of flowering plant in the family Plantaginaceae.

It is native to southern Africa, particularly South Africa, where it thrives in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant is characterized by its broad, fleshy leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in a dense inflorescence. It is adapted to survive in harsh conditions, with deep root systems that help it access water from deeper soil layers.

The plant is often used in traditional medicine for its potential healing properties.
