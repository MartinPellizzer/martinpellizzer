Plectranthus praetermissus

Plectranthus praetermissus, commonly known as the purple heart plant, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its velvety purple foliage and clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Plectranthus praetermissus is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its attractive appearance and ease of care.
