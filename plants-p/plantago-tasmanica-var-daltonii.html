Plantago tasmanica var. daltonii

Plantago tasmanica var. daltonii, commonly known as Dalton's plantain, is a perennial herb native to Tasmania, Australia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and rocky outcrops. The plant features basal rosettes of lance-shaped, hairy leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine trichomes. Its flowers are small and clustered in dense spikes, producing a distinctive, pale green to yellowish inflorescence.

The plant is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional herbal remedies.
