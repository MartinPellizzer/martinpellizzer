Paeonia tomentosa

Paeonia tomentosa, commonly known as the Tibetan peony, is a deciduous shrub native to the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from white to deep pink, often with a distinctive red or pink hue at the base. The plant features thick, hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves that are also covered in a fine layer of grayish-white hairs. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows.

Paeonia tomentosa is valued for its ornamental beauty and has cultural significance in traditional medicine.
