Piper rufescens

Piper rufescens, commonly known as red pepper vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many species of pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its slender, woody stems and opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy surface. Its flowers are small, greenish-white, and clustered in spikes, while the fruits are three-lobed berries that turn red when mature.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
