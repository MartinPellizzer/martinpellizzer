Pitcairnia petiolata

Pitcairnia petiolata, commonly known as the velvet bromeliad, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that form a central cup to collect water. The plant produces striking, long-lasting inflorescences with vibrant red flowers that attract pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees. Its leaves are thick and covered in a dense, velvety coating, which helps reduce water loss in its natural habitat.

Pitcairnia petiolata is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and relatively low maintenance requirements.
