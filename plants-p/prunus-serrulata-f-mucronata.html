Prunus serrulata f. mucronata

Prunus serrulata f. mucronata, commonly known as the Japanese cherry blossom or some varieties as the mountain cherry, is a cultivar of the Prunus serrulata species.

It is characterized by its delicate, pale pink to white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant is a small to medium-sized deciduous tree, typically reaching heights of 5 to 10 meters. Its leaves are ovate with serrated edges, and the bark is smooth and grayish-brown.

This cultivar is prized for its ornamental value and is often planted in gardens and parks for its beautiful spring blossoms.
