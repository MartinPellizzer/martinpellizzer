Pouteria aubrevillei

Pouteria aubrevillei, commonly known as the Brazilian nut tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is characterized by its large, glossy leaves and clusters of yellowish-green flowers. The tree produces elongated, woody fruits that contain a single large seed, which is commercially valuable as the Brazil nut. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in association with other canopy trees.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
