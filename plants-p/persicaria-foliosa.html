Persicaria foliosa

Persicaria foliosa, commonly known as the leafy knotweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or white, blooming in late summer. Persicaria foliosa thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat for various insects and birds.
