Pygeum mooneyi

Pygeum mooneyi, commonly known as the African knobthorn, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Proteaceae family and is characterized by its rough, fissured bark and dense, evergreen foliage. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in spring, followed by woody, capsule-like fruits containing several seeds. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky or sandy soils.

Pygeum mooneyi is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
