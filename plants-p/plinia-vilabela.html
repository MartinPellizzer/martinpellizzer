Plinia vilabela

Plinia vilabela, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or *Ceraseira*, is a species of evergreen shrub or small tree native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and showy, white to pink flowers. The plant produces small, red, fleshy berries that are edible and often used in local cuisine. Plinia vilabela thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

Its ornamental value and edible fruits make it a valued plant in both horticulture and agriculture.
