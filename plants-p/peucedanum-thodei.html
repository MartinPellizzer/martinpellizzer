Peucedanum thodei

Peucedanum thodei, commonly known as the Thode's cowparsley, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants such as carrots and parsley. The plant features slender, hollow stems and finely divided, fern-like leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. Its small, greenish-yellow flowers are arranged in umbels, typical of the Apiaceae family.

Peucedanum thodei is valued for its medicinal properties and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
