Panicum brevipedicellatum

Panicum brevipedicellatum, commonly known as the short-stemmed panicgrass, is a species of grass native to parts of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, two-lobed flowers. It thrives in dry, sunny environments and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides.

Due to its resilience and adaptability, it is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
