Paspalum plicatulum var. villosissimum

Paspalum plicatulum var. villosissimum, commonly known as the hairy flat sedge, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its flat, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered inflorescences. The plant typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, thriving in moist, nutrient-rich soils. Its hairy texture is a distinguishing feature, giving it the common name "hairy flat sedge.".

It is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support local biodiversity.
