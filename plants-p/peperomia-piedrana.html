Peperomia piedrana

Peperomia piedrana, commonly known as the rock peperomia, is a compact, low-growing plant native to South America.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that are typically dark green with a waxy texture, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in shades of white or pale green. The plant is well adapted to arid environments, making it drought-tolerant and suitable for indoor cultivation. Its succulent-like leaves help it store water, contributing to its resilience in dry conditions.

Peperomia piedrana is often grown as a decorative plant for its unique foliage and ease of care.
