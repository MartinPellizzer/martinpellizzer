Platycerium willinckii

Platycerium willinckii, commonly known as the Willinck's staghorn fern, is a unique epiphytic plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, antler-like fronds that give it its common name. The plant lacks true roots and instead absorbs moisture and nutrients through its fronds from the air and surrounding environment. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found growing on trees or rocks in its natural habitat.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated as a decorative plant in many tropical gardens.
