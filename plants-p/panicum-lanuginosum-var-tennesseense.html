Panicum lanuginosum var. tennesseense

Panicum lanuginosum var. tennesseense, commonly known as Tennessee foxtail, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as swamps, marshes, and stream banks. The plant features slender, cylindrical spikes that are covered in soft, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its foliage is narrow and grass-like, with leaves that are often hairy along the edges.

Tennessee foxtail plays an important role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat and stabilizing soil.
