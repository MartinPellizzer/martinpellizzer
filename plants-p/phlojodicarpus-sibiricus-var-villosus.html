Phlojodicarpus sibiricus var. villosus

Phlojodicarpus sibiricus var. villosus, commonly known as the Siberian velvetleaf, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the colder regions of Asia.

It features a robust, upright stem covered in dense, soft pubescence, giving it a velvety appearance. The leaves are typically ovate to lanceolate, with a hairy surface and a prominent midrib. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clusters, which are followed by elongated, cylindrical fruits.

It is often found in moist, nutrient-rich soils along riverbanks and in forested areas.
