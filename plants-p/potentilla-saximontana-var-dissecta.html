Potentilla saximontana var. dissecta

Potentilla saximontana var. dissecta, commonly known as the dissected cinquefoil, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features small, yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, typically from June to July. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves that give it a delicate, lace-like appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or alpine settings.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its compact growth habit and long blooming period.
