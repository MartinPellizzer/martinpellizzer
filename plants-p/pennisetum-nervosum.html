Pennisetum nervosum

Pennisetum nervosum, commonly known as purple muhly, is a perennial grass native to Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and striking purple to pink plumes that appear in late summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves with a bluish-green hue and is known for its drought tolerance. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Pennisetum nervosum is also valued for its ability to attract birds and insects, contributing to ecological diversity.
