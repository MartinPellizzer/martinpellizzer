Pelargonium graniticum

Pelargonium graniticum, commonly known as the granite geranium, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, hairy leaves that help it retain moisture in arid environments. The plant produces small, showy flowers in shades of pink, red, and white, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is a popular choice for drought-tolerant landscaping.
