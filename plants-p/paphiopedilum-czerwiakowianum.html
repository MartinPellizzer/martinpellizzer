Paphiopedilum czerwiakowianum

Paphiopedilum czerwiakowianum, commonly known as the Czerwiakowianum orchid, is a rare and highly sought-after species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique, intricate flower structure, with a lip that is often adorned with intricate patterns and a rich, velvety texture. The plant typically grows as a terrestrial or epiphytic orchid, often found in shaded, humid environments with well-drained soil. Its flowers, which can range in color from pale yellow to deep orange, are highly prized for their beauty and rarity in cultivation.

Due to its limited natural habitat and susceptibility to environmental changes, it is considered a conservation concern.
