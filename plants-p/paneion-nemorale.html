Paneion nemorale

Paneion nemorale, commonly known as the silver fern, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features delicate, feathery leaves that give it a distinctive, fern-like appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or white in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Its botanical name reflects its association with forested habitats, with "nemorale" derived from the Latin word for forest.
