Pterygota brasiliensis

Pterygota brasiliensis, commonly known as the Brazilian winged fern, is a unique plant species native to the rainforests of South America.

It belongs to the group of plants known as Pterygotes, which are characterized by their wing-like structures. The plant exhibits a distinctive morphology with broad, feather-like fronds that are often adorned with intricate patterns. Its botanical significance lies in its role as an indicator species for tropical biodiversity.

Due to habitat loss, it is also considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
