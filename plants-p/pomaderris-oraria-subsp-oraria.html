Pomaderris oraria subsp. oraria

Pomaderris oraria subsp.

oraria, commonly known as the narrow-leaf pomaderris, is a shrub native to southeastern Australia. It typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is found in heathlands and open forests.

The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It produces small, white or pale green flowers arranged in clusters, which attract nectar-feeding insects.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
