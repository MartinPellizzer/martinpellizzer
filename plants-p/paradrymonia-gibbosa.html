Paradrymonia gibbosa

Paradrymonia gibbosa, commonly known as the mountain lily, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Andes in South America.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and are often adorned with intricate patterns. The plant grows in rocky soils and has adapted to the harsh, cold conditions of its mountainous habitat. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in a fine, waxy coating to reduce water loss.

Paradrymonia gibbosa is valued not only for its ornamental beauty but also for its ecological role in alpine ecosystems.
