Paeonia subternata

Paeonia subternata, commonly known as the Japanese peony, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with three to five pairs of leaflets, and its showy, fragrant flowers that range from white to deep pink. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with sturdy stems that support its large, showy blooms. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens.

The botanical features of Paeonia subternata make it both aesthetically pleasing and ecologically valuable in its native habitat.
