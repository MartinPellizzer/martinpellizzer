Primulina pseudoheterotricha

Primulina pseudoheterotricha, commonly known as the silver lace fern, is a striking epiphytic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its delicate, feathery fronds that resemble lace. The plant features a short, creeping rhizome and produces small, inconspicuous spore-bearing structures called sporangia. Its foliage is often variegated with silver or white markings, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Primulina pseudoheterotricha thrives in humid, shaded environments and is popular in indoor gardening due to its ornamental value.
