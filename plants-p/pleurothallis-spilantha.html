Pleurothallis spilantha

Pleurothallis spilantha, commonly known as the silver-streaked orchid, is a species native to the cloud forests of Central and South America.

It is a small epiphytic orchid that thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant features a compact growth habit with narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces delicate, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink. Its flowers are typically arranged in a raceme and are characterized by their intricate, spurred petals.

Pleurothallis spilantha is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts for its unique appearance.
