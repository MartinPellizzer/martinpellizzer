Petrophile media

Petrophile media, commonly known as the "heath bottlebrush," is a genus of flowering plants native to Australia.

These plants are characterized by their woody stems and dense clusters of small, tubular flowers. They typically grow in sandy or rocky soils, thriving in well-drained, acidic environments. Petrophile media species are often found in heathlands and coastal areas, where they contribute to the unique biodiversity of these ecosystems.

Their flowers are pollinated primarily by birds and insects, showcasing an adaptation to their specific habitats.
