Pyrus fraxinus

Pyrus fraxinus, commonly known as the whitebeam, is a deciduous tree native to Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its small, white flowers that bloom in spring. The tree produces small, edible fruits known as serviceberries, which are often consumed by wildlife. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a serrated edge, contributing to its ornamental value.

Pyrus fraxinus is valued for its hardiness and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
