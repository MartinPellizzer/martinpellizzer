Pulicaria graveolens

Pulicaria graveolens, commonly known as the stinking woundwort, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically reaches heights of about 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. Its leaves are narrow and hairy, often with a distinctive aromatic scent.

It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in rocky or limestone areas.
