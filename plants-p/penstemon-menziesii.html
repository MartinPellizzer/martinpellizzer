Penstemon menziesii

Penstemon menziesii, commonly known as the tall beardtongue, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, attracting pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Penstemon menziesii is also valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological benefits in native plant gardens.
