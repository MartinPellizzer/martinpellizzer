Pothos podophyllus

Pothos podophyllus, commonly known as the devil's ivy, is a tropical vine native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, waxy leaves that are typically bright green with a slightly variegated pattern. The plant thrives in low-light conditions and is known for its vigorous growth and ability to climb or trail. Its aerial roots allow it to attach to surfaces, making it suitable for hanging baskets or trellises.

Pothos podophyllus is a popular houseplant due to its ease of care and aesthetic appeal.
