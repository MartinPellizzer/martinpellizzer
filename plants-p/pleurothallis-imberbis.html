Pleurothallis imberbis

Pleurothallis imberbis, commonly known as the hairless pleurothallis, is a species of orchid native to the cloud forests of Central and South America.

It is a small, epiphytic plant that typically grows on trees in humid, shaded environments. The plant features slender, wiry stems and produces small, greenish-white flowers with a subtle fragrance. Its leaves are usually narrow and arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem.

Due to its delicate appearance and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
