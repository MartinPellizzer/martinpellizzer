Pterocleome

Pterocleome, commonly known as the African spurred tortoise plant, is a genus of flowering plants in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its woody structure and compound leaves with small, pointed leaflets. The flowers are typically small and clustered in inflorescences, often with a distinctive spurred appearance. The plant is native to Africa and thrives in tropical and subtropical climates.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
