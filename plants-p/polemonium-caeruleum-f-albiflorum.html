Polemonium caeruleum f. albiflorum

Polemonium caeruleum f. albiflorum, commonly known as the white-flowered bluebell, is a variant of the common bluebell with distinctive white flowers.

It belongs to the Polemoniaceae family and is native to eastern North America. The plant typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and produces striking, bell-shaped blooms in early spring. Its foliage consists of lance-shaped leaves that are often tinged with reddish hues.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
