Potentilla daonensis

Potentilla daonensis, commonly known as the Daon's cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of yellow flowers. The plant typically features serrated, palmately lobed leaves that are hairy beneath, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species within its native range.
