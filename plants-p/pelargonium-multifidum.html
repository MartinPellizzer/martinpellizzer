Pelargonium multifidum

Pelargonium multifidum, commonly known as the laceleaf geranium, is a species of flowering plant in the family Geraniaceae.

It is native to South Africa and is characterized by its finely dissected, feather-like leaves that give it a delicate appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, pink to purple flowers during the spring and summer seasons. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Pelargonium multifidum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
