Papaver superbum

Papaver superbum, commonly known as the greater celandine, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its yellowish-orange flowers and broad, heart-shaped leaves. The plant belongs to the Papaveraceae family, which includes other well-known species like opium poppies. Papaver superbum typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and can be found in meadows and woodland edges.

Its medicinal properties have been studied, though it contains toxic compounds that require careful handling.
