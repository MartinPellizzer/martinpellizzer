Polypodium cyathisorum

Polypodium cyathisorum, commonly known as the saddleback fern, is a species of epiphytic fern native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its distinctive, elongated fronds that are often divided into segments with a prominent central vein. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found growing on trees or rocks in the understory of rainforests. Its rhizomes are thick and fibrous, allowing it to anchor itself to its substrate while absorbing moisture and nutrients from the air.

The saddleback fern is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated as a houseplant.
