Pteronia glabrata

Pteronia glabrata, commonly known as the woolly pteronia, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and showy yellow to orange flowers. The plant typically grows in coastal and sandy areas, thriving in well-drained soils. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its compact growth habit and long-lasting blooms.

Pteronia glabrata is also valued for its drought tolerance, making it suitable for xeriscaping.
