Polygonum conspersum

Polygonum conspersum, commonly known as the hairy knotweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its upright stems that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and have a rough texture due to their hairy surfaces. It produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or red, blooming in late summer.

This plant thrives in moist environments such as wetlands and stream banks, and it plays a role in stabilizing soil in these habitats.
