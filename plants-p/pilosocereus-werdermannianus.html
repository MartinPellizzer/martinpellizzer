Pilosocereus werdermannianus

Pilosocereus werdermannianus, commonly known as the Werdermann's cactus, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems covered in white, woolly spines, which provide insulation and protection. The plant produces yellow flowers during the summer months, which are followed by small, red fruits. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, preferring well-drained soil and full sun exposure.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological role, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.
