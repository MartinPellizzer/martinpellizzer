Pleurothallis jesupiorum

Pleurothallis jesupiorum, commonly known as the Jesup's pleurothallis, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Epidendroideae subfamily and is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and yellow. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and arranged in a compact rosette, contributing to its overall ornamental appeal.

This orchid is appreciated by horticulturists for its unique floral structure and ability to bloom repeatedly under proper care.
