Phillyrea cordifolia subsp. navasii

Phillyrea cordifolia subsp.

navasii, commonly known as the Navas' heartleaf Phillyrea, is a shrub native to the Iberian Peninsula. It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically glossy and dark green.

The plant produces small, white flowers that are clustered in terminal panicles, followed by small, woody fruits. It thrives in rocky or calcareous soils and is often found in Mediterranean climates.

This subspecies is noted for its ornamental value and ecological importance in its native habitat.
