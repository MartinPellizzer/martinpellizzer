Panicum glaucum var. tejucense

Panicum glaucum var. tejucense, commonly known as Mexican bluegrass, is a perennial grass native to Central America.

It is characterized by its blue-gray foliage and slender, upright growth habit. The plant produces small, fluffy seeds that are dispersed by wind, aiding in its propagation. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy species in tropical and subtropical climates.

Its ornamental value is often appreciated for its striking color and texture in landscaping.
