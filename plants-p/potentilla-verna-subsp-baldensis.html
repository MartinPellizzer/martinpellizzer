Potentilla verna subsp. baldensis

Potentilla verna subsp.

baldensis, commonly known as the bald mountain cinquefoil, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the northern hemisphere. It typically thrives in rocky, alpine, or arctic environments, where it forms dense mats of foliage.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers with five petals, which bloom in early spring. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a distinctive hairy texture.

This subspecies is adapted to harsh climates and is often found in open, sunny habitats.
