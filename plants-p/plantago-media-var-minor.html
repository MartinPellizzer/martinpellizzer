Plantago media var. minor

Plantago media var. minor, commonly known as narrowleaf plantain, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features erect, hairy stems that bear alternate, lance-shaped leaves with a distinctive wavy margin. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in dense clusters during the summer months. It is often found in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and fields, and is known for its medicinal properties.

The leaves are used in traditional herbal remedies for their soothing and anti-inflammatory effects.
