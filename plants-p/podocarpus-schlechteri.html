Podocarpus schlechteri

Podocarpus schlechteri, commonly known as the Schlechter's podocarp, is a member of the Podocarpaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is a medium-sized evergreen tree that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant has needle-like leaves that are arranged spirally on the shoots, and it produces small, cone-like structures that contain its seeds. It is characterized by its dense foliage and is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value.

The species is also valued for its hard, durable wood, which has been historically used in construction and furniture-making.
