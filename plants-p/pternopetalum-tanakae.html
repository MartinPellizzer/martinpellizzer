Pternopetalum tanakae

Pternopetalum tanakae, commonly known as the Japanese pittosporum, is a flowering plant native to Japan and Korea.

It belongs to the family Pittosporaceae and is characterized by its aromatic, fragrant leaves and showy white or pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows as an evergreen shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are elliptical, leathery, and have a distinctive glossy appearance.

Pternopetalum tanakae is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
