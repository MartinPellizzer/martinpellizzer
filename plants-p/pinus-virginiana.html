Pinus virginiana

Pinus virginiana, commonly known as the Virginia pine, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, often crooked trunk and needle-like leaves that grow in bundles of five. The tree produces cone-shaped seed structures that release winged seeds, aiding in wind dispersal. Its bark is thick and fissured, providing some protection against fire and pests.

This species is adapted to a variety of soil types and can thrive in both sunny and partially shaded environments.
