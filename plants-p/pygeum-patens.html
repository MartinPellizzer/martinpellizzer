Pygeum patens

Pygeum patens, commonly known as the African stinkwood, is a deciduous tree native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Rhamnaceae and is characterized by its rough, fissured bark and small, opposite leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by woody, spherical fruits containing several seeds. It is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is valued for its durable wood and traditional medicinal uses.

The leaves and bark contain compounds that have been studied for their potential health benefits.
