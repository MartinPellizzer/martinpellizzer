Phagnalon melanoleucum var. luridum

Phagnalon melanoleucum var. luridum, commonly known as the yellow sandwort, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Eurasia.

It is a low-growing perennial herb with succulent, often hairy stems and small, clustered leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in late spring to early summer, which are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Its botanical features make it a characteristic species of dry, open habitats.
