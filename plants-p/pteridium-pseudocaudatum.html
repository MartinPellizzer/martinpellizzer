It is characterized by its erect, slender rhizome and its compound leaves that are typically divided into linear, grass-like segments. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories or along stream banks.

Pteridium pseudocaudatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
