Pseudostellaria musashiensis

Pseudostellaria musashiensis, commonly known as the Japanese starwort, is a small perennial herb native to Japan.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of lanceolate leaves. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies of temperate flora.
