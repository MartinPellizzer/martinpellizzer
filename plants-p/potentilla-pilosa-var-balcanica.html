Potentilla pilosa var. balcanica

Potentilla pilosa var. balcanica, commonly known as the Balkan cinquefoil, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It is characterized by its hairy, upright stems and clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The leaves are typically divided into five oval-shaped lobes, giving it the common name "cinquefoil.". This plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or alpine habitats.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and long-lasting blooms.
