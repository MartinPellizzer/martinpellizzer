Piper affine

Piper affine, commonly known as the African pepper or African long pepper, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species such as black pepper. The plant is characterized by its climbing habit, with slender stems and opposite, ovate leaves. Its small, greenish-yellow flowers are clustered in spikes, and the fruit is a small, three-lobed berry.

The plant is valued for its aromatic leaves and has been used in traditional medicine and as a spice in some cuisines.
