Pinus sylvestris f. willkommii

Pinus sylvestris f. willkommii, commonly known as the Scots pine dwarf form, is a naturally occurring variant of the Scots pine.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and shorter, more densely packed needles compared to the typical Scots pine. This form is often found in specific microhabitats, such as rocky or shaded areas, where it exhibits unique adaptations. The cones are smaller and more clustered, and the bark is typically smoother and lighter in color.

It is a valuable plant for ecological studies due to its distinct morphology and habitat preferences.
