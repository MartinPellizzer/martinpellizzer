Protium belemense

Protium belemense, commonly known as the Belem nut or Belem tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Amazon rainforest.

It belongs to the Burseraceae family and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and large, elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by woody, spiny capsules that contain edible seeds. Its botanical features include a dense root system and the ability to thrive in humid, low-light conditions.

The tree is valued for its hard, durable wood and is also used in traditional medicine.
