Pecteilis rhodocheila

Pecteilis rhodocheila, commonly known as the pinkroot, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has a distinctive, fleshy root system that is often harvested for its medicinal properties. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

Pecteilis rhodocheila is often found in wetlands and along the edges of water bodies.
