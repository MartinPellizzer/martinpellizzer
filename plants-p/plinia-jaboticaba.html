Plinia jaboticaba

Plinia jaboticaba, commonly known as the Brazilian grapes or jabuticaba, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its dark purple, grape-like fruits that are rich in antioxidants and have a sweet, slightly tart flavor. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by the development of its unique, clustered fruiting bodies. Its leaves are elliptical, glossy, and arranged alternately along the stems.

The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value as well as its edible fruits.
