Paronychia canariensis f. congesta

Paronychia canariensis f. congesta, commonly known as the Canary Island heath or Canary Island broom.

This is a shrub native to the Canary Islands, characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and evergreen foliage. It features small, leathery leaves that are typically gray-green in color, with a waxy surface. The plant produces clusters of tiny, pale yellow flowers that bloom in spring, adding a subtle ornamental value.

It is well adapted to coastal and rocky environments, thriving in full sun and well-drained soils.
