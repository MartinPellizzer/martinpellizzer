Primula ajanensis

Primula ajanensis, commonly known as the Ajanensis primrose, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its cluster of bell-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows to a height of about 15-30 centimeters, with hairy stems and broad, heart-shaped leaves. Its flowers are usually pink or purple, and they are borne on short pedicels.

Primula ajanensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
