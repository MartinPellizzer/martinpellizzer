Piper umbellatum var. geminatum

Piper umbellatum var. geminatum, commonly known as the twinleaf pepper or geminate pepper, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its slender, climbing vines and small, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of tiny, greenish-white flowers that develop into small, round berries. These berries are typically harvested when they turn red, and they have a mild, peppery flavor.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of culinary spices.
