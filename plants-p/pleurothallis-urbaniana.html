Pleurothallis urbaniana

Pleurothallis urbaniana, commonly known as the Urbaniana orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Ecuador.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and pale yellow. The plant grows as an epiphyte, often found clinging to trees in humid, shaded environments.

Its botanical features include narrow, strap-like leaves and a compact growth habit, making it a popular choice for terrariums and indoor cultivation.
