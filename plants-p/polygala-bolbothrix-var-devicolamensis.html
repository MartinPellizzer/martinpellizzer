Polygala bolbothrix var. devicolamensis

Polygala bolbothrix var. devicolamensis, commonly known as the narrowleaf milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its slender, often hairy stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. Its leaves are narrow and linear, and the flowers are usually pale green or yellowish, with a distinctive, spurred shape.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in harsh environments.
