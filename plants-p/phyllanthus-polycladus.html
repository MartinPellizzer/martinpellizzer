Phyllanthus polycladus

Phyllanthus polycladus, commonly known as the African date tree, is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, leathery leaves that are typically elliptical or ovate in shape. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by edible, fleshy fruits resembling dates. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit.

The species is also valued in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
