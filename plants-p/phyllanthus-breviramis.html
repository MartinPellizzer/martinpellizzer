Phyllanthus breviramis

Phyllanthus breviramis, commonly known as the small-leaved phyllanthus, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa, Asia, and Australia.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are often densely arranged along the stems. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by spherical, woody fruits containing several seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories or along streambanks.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in various cultures.
