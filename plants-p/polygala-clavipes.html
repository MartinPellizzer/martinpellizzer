Polygala clavipes

Polygala clavipes, commonly known as the club-stalked milk-vetch, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its slender, often branching stems. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in racemes, typically blooming in the spring. Its leaves are alternate and usually have a slightly hairy texture.

Polygala clavipes is adapted to rocky and dry soils, making it a resilient plant in arid environments.
