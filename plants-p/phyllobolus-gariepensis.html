Phyllobolus gariepensis

Phyllobolus gariepensis, commonly known as the Gariep bulbous plant, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, bulb-like stems that store water, allowing it to survive in arid environments. The plant typically produces clusters of small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow and orange during the spring season. Its leaves are usually thick and fleshy, adapted to reduce water loss.

Phyllobolus gariepensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and resilience in dry conditions.
