Polygonum paradoxum

Polygonum paradoxum, commonly known as the paradox plant, is a species of flowering plant in the buckwheat family, Polygonaceae.

It is native to parts of Asia, including China and Japan, and thrives in moist, shaded environments. The plant is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, clustered greenish-white flowers. Its leaves are typically alternate, ovate, and have a slightly rough texture.

Polygonum paradoxum is often found in wetlands and along streambanks, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
