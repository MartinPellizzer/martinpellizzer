Paspalum spathaceum

Paspalum spathaceum, commonly known as the slender paspalum, is a perennial grass native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, thriving in moist, well-drained soils. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense mats, which can help prevent erosion.

Paspalum spathaceum is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support local wildlife.
