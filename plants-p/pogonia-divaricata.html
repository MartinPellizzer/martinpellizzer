Pogonia divaricata

Pogonia divaricata, commonly known as the showy orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that are typically pink or purple with intricate patterns. The plant has a rhizomatous root system and thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and wetlands. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette.

The species is known for its delicate appearance and is often found growing in association with specific mycorrhizal fungi.
