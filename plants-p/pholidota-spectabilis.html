Pholidota spectabilis

Pholidota spectabilis, commonly known as the silver leaf tree, is a deciduous tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, silvery-gray bark and heart-shaped leaves that turn bright red in autumn. The tree produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by orange-red fruits that split open to release seeds. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid environments.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
