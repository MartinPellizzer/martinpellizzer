Petalochilus variegatus

Petalochilus variegatus, commonly known as the variegated orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its strikingly patterned leaves, which often display a mix of green and white or yellow hues. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically arranged in a spike, adding to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Petalochilus variegatus is a popular choice for indoor gardening due to its aesthetic value and relatively easy care requirements.
