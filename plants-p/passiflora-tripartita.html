Passiflora tripartita

Passiflora tripartita, commonly known as the yellow passionflower, is a climbing vine native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, complex flowers that feature five sepals, five petals, and three prominent stamens. The plant produces small, yellow-orange fruits that are edible and have a tangy, citrus-like flavor. Its leaves are typically trifoliate, consisting of three leaflets, which is a key identifying feature.

Passiflora tripartita is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its vibrant blooms and unique foliage.
