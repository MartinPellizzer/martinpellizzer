Paullinia cupaniifolia

Paullinia cupaniifolia, commonly known as Surinam cherry or Brazilian cherry, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the Sapindaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, red, edible fruits that are rich in antioxidants and have a tart flavor. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

The botanical features of Paullinia cupaniifolia make it a valuable ornamental and agricultural plant in its native habitat.
