Pelargonium quirquelobum

Pelargonium quirquelobum, commonly known as the warty geranium, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, warty leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its unique appearance and ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
