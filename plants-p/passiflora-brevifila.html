Passiflora brevifila

Passiflora brevifila, commonly known as the short-stalked passionflower, is a species native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a climbing vine characterized by its striking, complex flowers that feature five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments. The plant produces blue or purple berries that are edible and contain a single large seed. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial shade.

Passiflora brevifila is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
