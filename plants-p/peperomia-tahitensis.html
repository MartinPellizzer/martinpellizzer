Peperomia tahitensis

Peperomia tahitensis, commonly known as the Tahitian peperomia, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to the South Pacific islands.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that are often variegated with shades of green, white, and pink, giving it a striking appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters, typically in shades of red or purple, which are followed by tiny, round berries. It thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Its unique foliage and low maintenance requirements make it a favored ornamental plant.
