Piaropus crassipes

Piaropus crassipes, commonly known as the broadleaf piper, is a species of flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Piperaceae, which includes various species of pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its broad, elongated leaves and small, greenish-white flowers. It typically grows in humid, shaded environments such as rainforests.

The plant's botanical features make it an important component of its native ecosystems.
