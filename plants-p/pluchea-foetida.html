Pluchea foetida

Pluchea foetida, commonly known as stinkweed, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and feathery leaves. The plant typically grows as an annual or perennial herb, often found in disturbed soils and wetland areas. Its small, yellow flowers are surrounded by pale green to white ray florets.

The plant is known for its strong, unpleasant odor, which is most noticeable when the leaves are crushed.
