Potamogeton gramineus var. curvifolius

Potamogeton gramineus var. curvifolius, commonly known as the curlyleaf pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to North America.

It has long, ribbon-like leaves that are often curved, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or brown in color. It thrives in shallow, nutrient-rich waters and is often found in lakes and slow-moving rivers.

Due to its rapid growth, it can become invasive in certain aquatic ecosystems.
