Paeonia ruprechtiana

Paeonia ruprechtiana, commonly known as the Himalayan peony, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from white to deep pink, with a distinctive, slightly wrinkled appearance. The plant has compound, trifoliate leaves that are often hairy and have a rough texture. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes.

Paeonia ruprechtiana is valued for its ornamental beauty and is also studied for its potential medicinal properties.
