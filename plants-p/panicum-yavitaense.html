Panicum yavitaense

Panicum yavitaense, commonly known as Yavita grass, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly in Colombia and Ecuador.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, wiry stems. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, two-lobed flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and along riverbanks.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
