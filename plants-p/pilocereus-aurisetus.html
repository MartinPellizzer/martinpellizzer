Pilocereus aurisetus

Pilocereus aurisetus, commonly known as the golden crown cactus, is a species of cactus native to South America.

It is a low-growing, spineless plant with a distinctive appearance characterized by its yellowish-green stems and bright yellow flowers. The plant features areoles, which are specialized structures from which spines, flowers, and branches emerge. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, requiring minimal water and full sunlight.

Its flowers are typically nocturnal, blooming at night and attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths.
