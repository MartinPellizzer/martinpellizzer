Pavonia edouardii

Pavonia edouardii, commonly known as the purple coneflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features showy, violet to purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, fluffy seeds in its composite flower heads. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Pavonia edouardii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in gardens.
