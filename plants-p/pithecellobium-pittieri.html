Pithecellobium pittieri

Pithecellobium pittieri, commonly known as the yellow mamey or yellow pithecellobium, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Central and South America, particularly found in countries like Mexico, Guatemala, and Belize. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves and produces small, yellow flowers that are often followed by elongated, bean-like pods. It is valued for its edible seeds, which are used in local cuisines, and for its potential in agroforestry systems due to its nitrogen-fixing ability.

The plant also plays a role in supporting local biodiversity by providing habitat and food for various insects and animals.
