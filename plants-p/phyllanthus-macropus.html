Phyllanthus macropus

Phyllanthus macropus, commonly known as the large-leaf spurge, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows up to 3 meters tall, with smooth, often thornless stems and alternate, ovate to elliptic leaves. The leaves are usually glossy and dark green, with a prominent midrib and a waxy surface. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous tiny seeds.

It is often found in disturbed soils and is adapted to thrive in humid, tropical climates.
