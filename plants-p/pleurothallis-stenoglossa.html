Pleurothallis stenoglossa

Pleurothallis stenoglossa, commonly known as the narrow-lipped pleurothallis, is a species of orchid native to the cloud forests of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its compact, sympodial growth habit and produces small, delicate flowers in shades of white and pale yellow. The plant typically grows on trees or rocks, thriving in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are usually elliptical and arranged in a rosette, and the flowers are often borne in clusters at the ends of the stems.

Due to its unique morphology and habitat, it is a subject of interest for botanists and orchid enthusiasts alike.
