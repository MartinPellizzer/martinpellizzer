Philodendron coerulescens

Philodendron coerulescens, commonly known as the Blue Philodendron, is a tropical vine native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, waxy, blue-green leaves that have a metallic sheen under light. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers, followed by pale yellow berries. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires consistently moist, well-draining soil.

Philodendron coerulescens is a popular houseplant due to its ornamental foliage and relatively easy care requirements.
