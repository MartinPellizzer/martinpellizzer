Pycnanthemum pycnanthemoides var. viridifolium

Pycnanthemum pycnanthemoides var. viridifolium, commonly known as greenleaf mountain mint, is a perennial herb native to the eastern and central United States.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically a vibrant green, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, white to pale blue flowers in clustered inflorescences, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in swamps, marshes, and along stream banks.

The leaves and flowers are aromatic, containing essential oils that contribute to its medicinal and ecological significance.
