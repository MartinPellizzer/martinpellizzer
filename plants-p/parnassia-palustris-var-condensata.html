Parnassia palustris var. condensata

Parnassia palustris var. condensata, commonly known as the clustered white crowfoot, is a herbaceous perennial native to wetland habitats in North America.

It features delicate, clustered white flowers with prominent yellow centers, typically blooming in late summer. The plant has basal leaves that are rounded and hairy, and its stems are usually unbranched. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in bogs and marshes.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in wetland ecosystems.
