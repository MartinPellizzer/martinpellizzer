Pinus ayacahuite subsp. neorecurvata

Pinus ayacahuite subsp.

neorecurvata, commonly known as the Mexican red pine, is a subspecies of the Ayacahuite pine native to central Mexico. It is characterized by its slender, straight trunk and dense, dark green needles that are typically bundled in pairs.

The cones of this species are elongated and often remain closed for several years before opening to release seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to temperate to subtropical climates.

This subspecies plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
