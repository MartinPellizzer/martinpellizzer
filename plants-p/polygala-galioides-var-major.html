Polygala galioides var. major

Polygala galioides var. major, commonly known as the showy milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Polygalaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and has small, compound leaves that are adapted to arid conditions. It produces small, bean-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
