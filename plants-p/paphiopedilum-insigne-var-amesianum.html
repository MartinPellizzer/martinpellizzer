Paphiopedilum insigne var. amesianum

Paphiopedilum insigne var. amesianum, commonly known as the Amesian lady's slipper, is a rare and exquisite orchid native to the tropical rainforests of Borneo.

It is characterized by its striking, pouch-shaped lip that resembles a slipper, a distinctive feature of the Paphiopedilum genus. The plant typically produces fragrant, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer. It thrives in shaded, humid environments with well-draining soil and requires consistent moisture.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by dedicated orchid enthusiasts.
