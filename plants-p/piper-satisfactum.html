Piper satisfactum

Piper satisfactum, commonly known as the happy pepper, is a tropical climbing vine native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species such as black pepper. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that give way to bright red berries, which are edible and have a peppery flavor. Its leaves are heart-shaped and often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is valued both for its ornamental appeal and culinary uses.
