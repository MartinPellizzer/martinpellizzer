Papaver radicatum

Papaver radicatum, commonly known as the meadow poppy, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, bright orange to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and woodland edges, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Papaver radicatum is a herbaceous perennial that reaches heights of about 30 to 60 centimeters.
