Phacelia sericea var. nelsonii

Phacelia sericea var. nelsonii, commonly known as Nelson's phacelia, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its soft, silvery-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, blue to violet flowers that bloom in early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
