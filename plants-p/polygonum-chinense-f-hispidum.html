Polygonum chinense f. hispidum

Polygonum chinense f. hispidum, commonly known as the hairy knotweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in summer. The leaves are ovate to lanceolate, often covered with fine hairs, giving the plant its "hairy" appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
