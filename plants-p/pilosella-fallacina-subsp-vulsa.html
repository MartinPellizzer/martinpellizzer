Pilosella fallacina subsp. vulsa

Pilosella fallacina subsp.

vulsa, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hawk's-beard, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and along roadsides, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are narrow and often have a fuzzy texture, while the flowers are yellow with a central disk.

It is a perennial plant that blooms from late spring to early summer.
