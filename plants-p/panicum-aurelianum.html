Panicum aurelianum

Panicum aurelianum, commonly known as golden panicgrass, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and the Middle East.

It belongs to the grass family, Poaceae, and is characterized by its bright yellow inflorescence that resembles a tuft of hair. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue.

Panicum aurelianum is often used in ornamental gardening due to its striking appearance and low maintenance requirements.
