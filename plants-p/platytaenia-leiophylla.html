Platytaenia leiophylla

Platytaenia leiophylla, commonly known as the smooth-leaved platytaenia, is a small, herbaceous plant native to specific regions of Australia.

It features simple, alternate leaves that are typically smooth and lance-shaped, with a dark green upper surface and a lighter underside. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers that bloom in spring, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous tiny seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky outcrops.

Due to its limited distribution and specific ecological requirements, it is considered a rare and sometimes endangered species.
