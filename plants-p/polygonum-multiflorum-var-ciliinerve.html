Polygonum multiflorum var. ciliinerve

Polygonum multiflorum var. ciliinerve, commonly known as the Chinese knotweed, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its robust, rhizomatous growth and heart-shaped leaves with finely serrated edges. The plant produces clusters of small, reddish-purple flowers that bloom in late summer. Its roots are widely used in traditional Chinese medicine for their purported health benefits.

The species is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental foliage and adaptability to various soil types.
