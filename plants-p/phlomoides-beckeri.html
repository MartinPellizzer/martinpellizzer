Phlomoides beckeri

Phlomoides beckeri, commonly known as the Becker's false nettle, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the family Lamiaceae, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic foliage. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface, and it produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pale blue to purple in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and wetlands.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, supporting various pollinators.
