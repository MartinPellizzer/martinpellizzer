Phaseolus rotundifolius

Phaseolus rotundifolius, commonly known as the runner bean, is a climbing legume native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, velvety leaves and pods that contain edible, kidney-shaped beans. The plant thrives in temperate climates and requires full sun and well-drained soil. Its flowers are typically white or pale purple, and it is often cultivated for both ornamental and culinary purposes.

The beans are rich in protein and nutrients, making them a valuable crop in many regions.
