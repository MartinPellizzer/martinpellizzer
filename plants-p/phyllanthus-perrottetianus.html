Phyllanthus perrottetianus

Phyllanthus perrottetianus, commonly known as the African spurge, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its small, opposite leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, followed by small, capsule-like fruits. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions.

The species is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
