Pueraria peduncularis var. violacea

Pueraria peduncularis var. violacea, commonly known as the purple cowpea, is a climbing legume native to tropical regions of Asia.

It features heart-shaped, compound leaves that are often tinged with purple, contributing to its ornamental value. The plant produces small, violet or purple flowers arranged in racemes, which bloom throughout the growing season. Its roots are known for their nitrogen-fixing ability, making it beneficial in agricultural settings.

The variety is valued both for its aesthetic appeal and its role in sustainable farming practices.
