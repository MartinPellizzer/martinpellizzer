Primula agleniana

Primula agleniana, commonly known as the Alpine primrose, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its low-growing habit and clustered flowers. The plant typically produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, yellow, or pink, which bloom in early spring. Its leaves are oval-shaped with a slightly hairy texture, and it thrives in cool, moist environments.

Primula agleniana is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to add color to alpine gardens.
