Phyllostelidium pavonianum

Phyllostelidium pavonianum, commonly known as the pavonian spikerush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Australia.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant is characterized by its upright, grass-like stems and narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spike-like clusters, typical of the Cyperaceae family.

Phyllostelidium pavonianum is often found in wetlands, marshes, and other moist environments where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
