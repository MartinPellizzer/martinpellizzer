Pisum baclium

Pisum baclium, commonly known as the wild pea, is a flowering plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is characterized by its climbing habit, with slender stems that twine around supports. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pale blue or purple in color. Its pod fruits contain several seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine.

Pisum baclium is native to various regions of Europe and Asia and is adapted to temperate climates.
