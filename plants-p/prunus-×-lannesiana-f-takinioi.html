Prunus × lannesiana f. takinioi

Prunus × lannesiana f. takinioi, commonly known as the Japanese cherry blossom or sakura, is a unique hybrid species within the Prunus genus.

It is characterized by its delicate, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in early spring, creating a spectacular display. The plant is native to Japan and is closely related to other cherry species such as Prunus serrulata. It has a compact growth habit with small, oval leaves that turn green in summer and yellow in autumn.

The botanical interest of this variety lies in its ornamental value and its role in cultural symbolism.
