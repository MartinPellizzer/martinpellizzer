Polycarpaea repens

Polycarpaea repens, commonly known as the creeping polycarpaea, is a perennial herb native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands. The plant features a prostrate growth habit with slender, creeping stems that root at the nodes. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

It produces small, clustered flowers that are often white or pale yellow, followed by small, round berries.
