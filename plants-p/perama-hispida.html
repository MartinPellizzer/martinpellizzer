Perama hispida

Perama hispida, commonly known as the hairy perama, is a species of flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its rough, hairy stems and small, oval-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rocky or sandy garden environments.

Perama hispida is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
