Phyllocactus × purpureus

Phyllocactus × purpureus, commonly known as the purple crown cactus, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Cactaceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive, flattened, ribbed stems that form a dense, bushy structure. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple, depending on the variety. It is native to South America and thrives in bright, indirect light with well-draining soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in rock gardens.
