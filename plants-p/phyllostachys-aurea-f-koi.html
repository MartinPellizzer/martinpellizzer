Phyllostachys aurea f. koi

Phyllostachys aurea f. koi, commonly known as the golden bamboo or koi bamboo, is a variant of the Phyllostachys aurea species.

It is characterized by its bright yellow shoots that gradually turn to a lighter green as the plant matures. This ornamental bamboo is native to East Asia and is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal in gardens and landscapes. The plant forms dense clumps and can grow quite tall, reaching up to 10 meters in height under ideal conditions.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for both residential and commercial horticulture.
