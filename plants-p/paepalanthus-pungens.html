Paepalanthus pungens

Paepalanthus pungens, commonly known as the spiked pincushion cactus, is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny clusters of stems that form a spherical or cushion-like structure. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils.

Its unique morphology and adaptations make it well-suited for survival in harsh, dry conditions.
