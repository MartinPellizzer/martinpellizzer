Polyscias nodosa

Polyscias nodosa, commonly known as the clustered glorybower, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and dense clusters of small, white to pale green flowers. The plant produces berries that are typically green when mature and turn red upon ripening. It thrives in humid, shady environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage.

Polyscias nodosa is also valued for its ability to tolerate partial shade and its use in traditional medicine in some cultures.
