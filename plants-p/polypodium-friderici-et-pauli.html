Polypodium friderici-et-pauli

Polypodium friderici-et-pauli, commonly known as the Emperor's Polypody, is a species of fern belonging to the family Polypodiaceae.

It is characterized by its evergreen, leathery fronds that are typically divided into narrow, serrated segments. The plant produces sporangia on the underside of its fronds, which are clustered in small, rounded groups called sori. Native to tropical and subtropical regions, it thrives in shaded, moist environments.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for indoor and outdoor gardening.
