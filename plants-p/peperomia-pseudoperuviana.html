Peperomia pseudoperuviana

Peperomia pseudoperuviana, commonly known as the velvet peperomia, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or reddish in color. It thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Due to its compact size and attractive foliage, it is often used as a decorative plant in both homes and offices.
