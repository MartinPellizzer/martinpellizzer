Pyrus pseudothuringiaca

Pyrus pseudothuringiaca, commonly known as the Turkish pear, is a deciduous tree native to the mountainous regions of Turkey.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and small, yellowish flowers that appear in spring. The fruit is a small, oval-shaped pear that ripens in late summer, typically turning yellow or reddish in color.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated for its edible fruit, though it is less commonly grown than other pear varieties.
