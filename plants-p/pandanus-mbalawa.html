Pandanus mbalawa

Pandanus mbalawa, commonly known as the Mbalawa pandanus, is a tropical plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and long, narrow leaves that are often used in traditional crafts. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically clustered at the base of the leaves. Its roots are sometimes used in local medicine for their purported healing properties.

The Mbalawa pandanus is an important species in its native ecosystems, contributing to biodiversity and providing resources for local communities.
