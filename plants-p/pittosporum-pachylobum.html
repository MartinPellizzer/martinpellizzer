Pittosporum pachylobum

Pittosporum pachylobum, commonly known as the broad-leaved pittosporum, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its evergreen foliage, which consists of thick, leathery leaves that are often dark green in color. The plant produces fragrant white or pale yellow flowers, which are typically borne in clusters. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance and hardy nature.

Pittosporum pachylobum thrives in well-drained soils and is relatively drought-tolerant once established.
