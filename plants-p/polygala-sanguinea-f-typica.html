Polygala sanguinea f. typica

Polygala sanguinea f. typica, commonly known as the red milkwort, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Polygalaceae family and is characterized by its small, pink to red flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and can be found in woodland areas or along stream banks. Its leaves are opposite and lance-shaped, while its stems are often branched and covered in fine hairs.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for various purposes.
