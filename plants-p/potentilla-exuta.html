Potentilla exuta

Potentilla exuta, commonly known as the silverweed, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its silvery, fuzzy leaves and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It is a low-growing perennial that forms dense mats, making it useful for erosion control.

Botanically, it belongs to the Rosaceae family and is known for its hardy nature and adaptability to various environmental conditions.
