Prunus spiraeifolia

Prunus spiraeifolia, commonly known as the Japanese flowering cherry or mountain cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its delicate, white or pink flowers that bloom in early spring, often before the leaves emerge. The plant has simple, ovate leaves that turn yellow in autumn, adding to its ornamental value. It typically grows to a height of 3 to 6 meters and prefers well-drained soil in a sunny location.

Prunus spiraeifolia is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal in gardens and parks.
