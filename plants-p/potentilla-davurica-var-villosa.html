Potentilla davurica var. villosa

Potentilla davurica var. villosa, commonly known as the hairy cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it its varietal name "villosa.". The plant produces clusters of small, yellow, five-petaled flowers that bloom in early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in alpine or rocky environments.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardy nature in garden settings.
