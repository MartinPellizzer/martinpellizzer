Pteris biaurita f. nemoralis

Pteris biaurita f. nemoralis, commonly known as the rock fern, is a fern species characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped fronds.

It belongs to the family Pteridaceae and is native to regions of Southeast Asia, including parts of India and China. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as rocky crevices and forest floors. Its rhizomes are fibrous, and the fronds are usually bipinnate, with small, closely spaced pinnae.

This fern is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in shaded garden settings.
