Porophyllum scoparium

Porophyllum scoparium, commonly known as the roughleaf fleabane, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. The plant features hairy, lance-shaped leaves and produces yellow flowers with a distinctive central disc. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.
