Piper tractifolium var. pubescens

Piper tractifolium var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy-leaf pepper vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper plants, and is characterized by its aromatic, peppery leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers. The leaves are typically broad, ovate, and covered in fine, pubescent hairs, giving the plant its varietal name. It produces small, berry-like fruits that are often used in traditional medicine and as a spice.

This plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland tropical forests.
