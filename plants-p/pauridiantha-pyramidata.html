Pauridiantha pyramidata

Pauridiantha pyramidata, commonly known as the pyramid bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue. The plant typically grows in high-altitude environments, often in rocky or alpine conditions. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, adapted to withstand cold and arid conditions.

Pauridiantha pyramidata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
