Peperomia trichopus

Peperomia trichopus, commonly known as the velvet leaf peperomia, is a species of flowering plant in the Piperaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Central and South America, where it typically grows in shaded, moist environments. The plant is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are usually green or reddish and appear in clusters.

Peperomia trichopus is popular as a houseplant due to its low maintenance and attractive foliage.
