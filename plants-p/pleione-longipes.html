Pleione longipes

Pleione longipes, commonly known as the longipes orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of China, particularly in Yunnan and Sichuan.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that typically bloom in spring. The plant features a pseudobulb, which serves as a storage organ for water and nutrients, and its leaves are usually broad and grass-like. Pleione longipes thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in shaded forest habitats.

Its ornamental value has made it a sought-after species among orchid enthusiasts and botanists alike.
