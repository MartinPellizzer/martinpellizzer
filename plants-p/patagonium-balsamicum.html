Patagonium balsamicum

Patagonium balsamicum, commonly known as the balsamic plant, is a flowering shrub native to the southern regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, resinous leaves and yellow flowers. The plant produces a unique balsamic resin, which is used in traditional medicine and as a flavoring agent. It thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil and full sun exposure.

Due to its ecological importance and medicinal value, it is often found in native plant communities across Patagonia.
