Psidium pumilum var. guadalupense

Psidium pumilum var. guadalupense, commonly known as the Guadalupense guava, is a small evergreen shrub native to Mexico.

It produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by edible, yellow-orange berries. The plant thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial shade. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and have a slightly serrated margin.

The fruit is typically used in local cuisines and has a sweet, aromatic flavor.
