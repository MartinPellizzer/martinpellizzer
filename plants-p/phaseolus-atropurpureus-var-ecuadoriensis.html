Phaseolus atropurpureus var. ecuadoriensis

Phaseolus atropurpureus var. ecuadoriensis, commonly known as the Ecuadorian purple passionflower, is a climbing vine native to the Andean regions of Ecuador.

It is characterized by its striking violet to purple flowers, which are often adorned with intricate patterns of veins and spots. The plant produces heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and emits a strong, sweet fragrance, especially in the evening. Its fruit is a green, pod-like structure containing several seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its visual appeal.
