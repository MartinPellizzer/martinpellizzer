Primula dubyi

Primula dubyi, commonly known as the Duby's primrose, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of western China.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in cool, moist environments. Its leaves are usually basal, rosette-like, and covered in a dense layer of fine hairs.

Primula dubyi is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and resilience.
