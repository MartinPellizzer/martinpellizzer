Pseudobrownanthus nucifer

Pseudobrownanthus nucifer, commonly known as the golden mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, yellowish-green inflorescences that resemble mist or smoke, hence its common name. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories or along stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly waxy texture, while its flowers are small and clustered in dense, panicle-like structures.

Pseudobrownanthus nucifer is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique appearance.
