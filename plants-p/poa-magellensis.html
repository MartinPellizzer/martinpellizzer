Poa magellensis

Poa magellensis, commonly known as the Magellanic grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern South America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, wiry stems that often form dense mats. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in cool, temperate climates and is adapted to grow in rocky or sandy soils.

Due to its hardy nature, it is often found in alpine and coastal regions, contributing to the ecological stability of these environments.
