Plantago tomentosa var. fonckii

Plantago tomentosa var. fonckii, commonly known as the woolly plantain, is a variant of the common plantain species.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, disturbed soils such as roadsides and fields. The plant features broad, heart-shaped leaves covered in dense, soft hairs, giving it a woolly appearance. Its flowering stems bear clusters of small, greenish flowers that develop into capsule fruits containing numerous seeds.

This variant is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional herbal remedies.
