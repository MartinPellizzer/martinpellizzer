Physaria floribunda

Physaria floribunda, commonly known as the desert primeflower, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its striking orange to yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows from 6 to 12 inches tall, with hairy, branching stems and alternate leaves. Its scientific name, Physaria, refers to the structure of its fruit, which is a capsule containing numerous seeds.

This species is well adapted to harsh, dry environments and is often found in sandy or rocky soils.
