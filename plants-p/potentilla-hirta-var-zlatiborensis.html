Potentilla hirta var. zlatiborensis

Potentilla hirta var. zlatiborensis, commonly known as the Zlatibor cinquefoil, is a perennial shrub native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and serrated, dark green leaves that are covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, bright yellow flowers in early summer, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in alpine and rocky habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native environment.
