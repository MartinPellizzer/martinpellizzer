Polystichum kiusiuense

Polystichum kiusiuense, commonly known as the Kiusiu fern, is a species of evergreen fern native to Japan.

It belongs to the family Dryopteridaceae and is characterized by its dark green, leathery fronds that are typically divided into narrow, lance-shaped segments. The plant thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories. Its spores are produced on the underside of the fronds, and it reproduces through sexual reproduction via spore dispersal.

The Kiusiu fern is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
