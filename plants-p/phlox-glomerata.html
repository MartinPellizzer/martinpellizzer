Phlox glomerata

Phlox glomerata, commonly known as clustered phlox, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features showy, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple, blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 12 to 24 inches and has narrow, linear leaves that are arranged in a whorled pattern around the stem. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

Its compact growth habit and attractive flowers make it a valuable addition to native plant gardens.
