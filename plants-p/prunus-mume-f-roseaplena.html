Prunus mume f. roseaplena

Prunus mume f. roseaplena, commonly known as the Japanese flowering plum, is a cultivar of the Prunus mume species native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, double blooms of pink to white flowers that appear in early spring before the leaves emerge. The plant typically grows as a small deciduous tree or shrub, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its flowers are highly fragrant and are often used in traditional Japanese culture for ornamental purposes.

The fruit, which is edible, is small and sour, resembling a cherry but with a unique tart flavor.
