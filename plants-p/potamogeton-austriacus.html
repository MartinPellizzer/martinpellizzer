Potamogeton austriacus

Potamogeton austriacus, commonly known as the Austrian pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in Europe.

It has long, ribbon-like leaves that are typically narrow and tapering, with a smooth surface. The plant produces small, green flowers that are arranged in spike-like clusters, often emerging from the water surface. It plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various water organisms.

Potamogeton austriacus is often found in still or slow-moving waters such as lakes and ponds.
