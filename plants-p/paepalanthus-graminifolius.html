Paepalanthus graminifolius

Paepalanthus graminifolius, commonly known as the grass-leaved puya, is a perennial plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves that form a water-holding structure. The plant produces striking, showy inflorescences with brightly colored flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is adapted to high-altitude conditions.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists alike.
