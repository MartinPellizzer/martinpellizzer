Passiflora mollis var. obtusiloba

Passiflora mollis var. obtusiloba, commonly known as the silky passionflower, is a tropical vine native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its smooth, velvety stems and heart-shaped leaves with a soft, fuzzy texture. The plant produces striking flowers with five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments, often in shades of white, pink, and purple. Its fruit is a yellow, fleshy berry that contains numerous small, edible seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated for its unique floral display.
