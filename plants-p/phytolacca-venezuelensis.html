Phytolacca venezuelensis

Phytolacca venezuelensis, commonly known as the Venezuelan pokeweed, is a herbaceous plant native to South America, particularly Venezuela.

It belongs to the family Phytolaccaceae and is characterized by its upright stems and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. The plant produces small, red berries that are toxic to humans and animals. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Phytolacca venezuelensis is often found in moist, shaded areas and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
