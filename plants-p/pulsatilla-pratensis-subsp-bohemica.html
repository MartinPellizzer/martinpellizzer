Pulsatilla pratensis subsp. bohemica

Pulsatilla pratensis subsp.

bohemica, commonly known as the Bohemian pasqueflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Europe. It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its showy, cup-shaped flowers that typically bloom in early spring.

The plant has palmately divided leaves and produces follicles that contain toxic, dark brown seeds. It thrives in meadows and grasslands, often in nutrient-poor soils, and is adapted to temperate climates.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and botanical gardens.
