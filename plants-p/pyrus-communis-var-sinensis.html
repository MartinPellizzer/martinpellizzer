Pyrus communis var. sinensis

Pyrus communis var. sinensis, commonly known as the Chinese white pear, is a cultivar of the common pear that is native to China.

It is characterized by its large, white fruits with a sweet and juicy flesh, often used in both culinary and medicinal applications. The plant typically grows as a deciduous tree, reaching heights of up to 10 meters, with broad, lobed leaves and clusters of white flowers in spring. Its fruit is rich in nutrients and has been valued for centuries in traditional Chinese medicine.

The tree is also appreciated for its ornamental value, with its attractive foliage and seasonal display of blossoms.
