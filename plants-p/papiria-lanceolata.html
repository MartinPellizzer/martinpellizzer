Papiria lanceolata

Papiria lanceolata, commonly known as the lanceleaf paperflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features lance-shaped, alternate leaves that are often hairy and have a distinctive silvery-white underside. The plant produces showy, papery flowers in shades of white, pink, and red, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun.

Papiria lanceolata is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds.
