Panicum trochainii

Panicum trochainii, commonly known as silver panicgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-gray foliage and upright growth habit, which makes it visually striking in gardens. The plant produces small, fluffy panicles of seeds that are released by wind, aiding in its dispersal. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for xeriscaping and low-maintenance landscapes.

Its drought tolerance and ornamental value make it a popular choice for ornamental grasses in warm climates.
