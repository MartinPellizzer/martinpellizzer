Protea micans subsp. lemairei

Protea micans subsp.

lemairei, commonly known as the Lemaire's sugarbush, is a South African plant belonging to the Proteaceae family. It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and distinctive inflorescences that resemble a cluster of candles.

The plant typically grows in coastal and mountainous regions, thriving in well-drained, acidic soils. Its flowers are pollinated by insects, and it produces small, woody fruits that contain several seeds.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
