Polygonum subsimplex

Polygonum subsimplex, commonly known as the smooth knotweed, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and heart-shaped leaves that are smooth and glossy. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, typically blooming in late summer. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as stream banks and woodland edges.

Polygonum subsimplex is often found in wetlands and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
