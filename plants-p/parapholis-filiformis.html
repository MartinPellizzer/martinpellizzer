Parapholis filiformis

Parapholis filiformis, commonly known as the slender false oat, is a grass species native to various regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its thin, grass-like leaves and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and can be found in open fields, roadsides, and other sunny habitats. Its morphology is similar to true oats, but it lacks the hard, bristly texture of the latter.

Parapholis filiformis plays a minor role in local ecosystems, often serving as a pioneer species in ecological succession.
