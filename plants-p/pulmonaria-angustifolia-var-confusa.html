Pulmonaria angustifolia var. confusa

Pulmonaria angustifolia var. confusa, commonly known as the narrow-leaved lungwort, is a herbaceous perennial native to Europe.

It features basal rosettes of lance-shaped, hairy leaves that are often adorned with distinctive purple and green markings. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink, white, and purple, typically blooming in early spring. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Its foliage and floral display make it a valuable addition to native plant gardens.
