Polypodium schefferi

Polypodium schefferi, commonly known as the Scheffer's holly fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Polypodiaceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia, including countries such as India, China, and Japan. The plant is characterized by its leathery, dark green fronds that are typically divided into lance-shaped segments. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The spores are produced on the underside of the fronds, which are arranged in a compact, upright cluster.
