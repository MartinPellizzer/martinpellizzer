Parahebe raoulii subsp. pentasepala

Parahebe raoulii subsp.

pentasepala, commonly known as the purple-flowered parahebe, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand. It is a perennial shrub characterized by its upright growth habit and dense foliage.

The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically purple or violet in color, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a glossy appearance, contributing to its ornamental value.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and subalpine environments.
