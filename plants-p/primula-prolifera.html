Primula prolifera

Primula prolifera, commonly known as the creeping primrose, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It features basal leaves that are heart-shaped and covered in a dense layer of fine, white hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink or purple, depending on the variety. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in forest understories or along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is cultivated in gardens and rockeries worldwide.
