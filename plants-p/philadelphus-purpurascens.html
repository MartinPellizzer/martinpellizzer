Philadelphus purpurascens

Philadelphus purpurascens, commonly known as the purpleleaf mock orange, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. The leaves are typically ovate to elliptic, with a distinctive purple or reddish tinge on the underside. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular ornamental plant.

Its botanical features make it a valuable addition to gardens and natural habitats.
