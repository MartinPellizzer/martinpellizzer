Polygonum bocconei

Polygonum bocconei, commonly known as the Boccone's knotweed, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Italy.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, which includes plants commonly referred to as smartweeds or knotweeds. The plant is characterized by its upright, branched stems and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are alternate, ovate to lanceolate, and often have a reddish or purple hue on the underside.

Polygonum bocconei is typically found in rocky or calcareous soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments.
