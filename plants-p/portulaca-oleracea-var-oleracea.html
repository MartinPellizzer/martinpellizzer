Portulaca oleracea var. oleracea

Portulaca oleracea var. oleracea, commonly known as common purslane, is an annual flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region but now found worldwide.

It is a low-growing, succulent plant with fleshy, smooth leaves and small, yellow flowers that bloom in the morning. The plant thrives in hot, dry conditions and is often found in disturbed soils, gardens, and along roadsides. Its edible leaves and stems are rich in omega-3 fatty acids and are used in various cuisines.

The plant's ability to survive in harsh environments makes it a resilient and adaptable species.
