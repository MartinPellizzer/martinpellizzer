Polygala portoricensis

Polygala portoricensis, commonly known as the Puerto Rican milkweed, is a flowering plant native to the island of Puerto Rico.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that are typically white or pale pink. The plant has slender, branching stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is adapted to the tropical climate of its native habitat.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
