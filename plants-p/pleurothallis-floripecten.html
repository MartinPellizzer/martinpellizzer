Pleurothallis floripecten

Pleurothallis floripecten, commonly known as the feathered orchid, is a species native to the cloud forests of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, elongated flowers that resemble feathers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its flowers are often white or pale pink, with intricate patterns that attract specific pollinators.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is a subject of interest for botanists and orchid enthusiasts alike.
