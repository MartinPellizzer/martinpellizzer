Protea trichanthera

Protea trichanthera, commonly known as the white protea, is a striking South African plant belonging to the Proteaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescence composed of numerous white, petal-like bracts that surround small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in coastal and mountainous regions, thriving in well-drained, acidic soils. Its flowers are pollinated primarily by insects, and it produces small, woody fruits that contain several seeds.

Protea trichanthera is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and floral arrangements.
