Piper cabellense

Piper cabellense, commonly known as the Cabell's pepper plant, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many species of pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves and small, greenish-white flowers that develop into elongated, berry-like fruits. The fruits are typically harvested when mature and can be used in local cuisines or traditional medicine.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural study.
