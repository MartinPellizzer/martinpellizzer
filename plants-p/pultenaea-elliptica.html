Pultenaea elliptica

Pultenaea elliptica, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and yellow floral spikes that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and dry inland regions.

Pultenaea elliptica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
