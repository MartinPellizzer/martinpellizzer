Pilosella fennica subsp. melanauricula

Pilosella fennica subsp.

melanauricula, commonly known as the dark-hairy pilosella, is a perennial herb native to northern Europe. It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage covered in fine, hairy textures.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in woodland edges and meadows.

This subspecies is notable for its compact growth habit and distinctive appearance.
