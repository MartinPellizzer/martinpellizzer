Pelargonium × caryophyllaceum

Pelargonium × caryophyllaceum, commonly known as the common geranium, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Geraniaceae family.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and broad, heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive red or purple underside. The plant produces clusters of five-petaled flowers in shades of pink, red, or white, typically blooming in spring and summer. It is native to southern Africa and is widely cultivated as an ornamental plant for its attractive foliage and flowers.

Pelargonium × caryophyllaceum prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a versatile choice for gardens and containers.
