Phyteuma pauciflorum f. albiflorum

Phyteuma pauciflorum f. albiflorum, commonly known as the white-flowered phyteuma, is a cultivar of the phyteuma plant characterized by its striking white blooms.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is native to southern Europe. The plant features slender, upright stems with lance-shaped leaves that are dark green and slightly hairy. Its flowers are tubular with a white corolla, often adorned with a pale green or yellow throat.

This cultivar is prized for its ornamental value in gardens and its ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
