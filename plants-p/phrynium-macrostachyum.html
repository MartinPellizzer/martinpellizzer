Phrynium macrostachyum

Phrynium macrostachyum, commonly known as the giant arrowleaf, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, arrow-shaped leaves that can grow up to 1 meter in length, often with a waxy, glossy surface. The plant produces clusters of yellow-green flowers arranged in a spike, which are followed by elongated, spiky fruits. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for tropical gardens and botanical collections.
