Pelargonium oppositifolium

Pelargonium oppositifolium, commonly known as the upright geranium, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, typically blooming in spring and summer. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

Due to its ornamental value and ease of cultivation, it is a popular choice for gardeners.
