Poicilla acuminata

Poicilla acuminata, commonly known as the sharp-pointed poicilla, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Poaceae, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow leaves. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological studies due to its adaptation to specific environmental conditions.

However, it is not widely cultivated and remains relatively understudied in horticultural contexts.
