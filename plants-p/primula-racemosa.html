Primula racemosa

Primula racemosa, commonly known as the clustered primrose, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, clustered flowers. The plant typically produces bell-shaped blooms in shades of white, pink, and purple, which appear in early spring. It prefers cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden cultivation in temperate climates.
