Pleurothallis calolalax

Pleurothallis calolalax, commonly known as the golden-lip orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its small, intricate flowers that typically feature yellow and green hues. The plant is epiphytic, meaning it grows on the surface of trees or other structures without deriving nutrients from them. Its flowers are often clustered in small inflorescences, and the species is noted for its delicate, fragrant blooms.

Due to its unique appearance and rarity, it is a sought-after plant among orchid enthusiasts.
