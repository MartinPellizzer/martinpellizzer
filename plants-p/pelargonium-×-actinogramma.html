Pelargonium × actinogramma

Pelargonium × actinogramma, commonly known as the zonal geranium, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Geraniaceae family.

It is characterized by its showy, clustered flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, which bloom throughout the growing season. The plant features broad, velvety leaves with distinct color zones, typically in shades of green, silver, or white. Native to South Africa, it is widely cultivated as an ornamental plant for gardens and container gardening.

Pelargonium × actinogramma prefers full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for both indoor and outdoor settings.
