Panicum gracilipes

Panicum gracilipes, commonly known as slender bluestem, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted foliage and slender, drooping inflorescences that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in wetlands and along stream banks. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue that gives it its common name.

Panicum gracilipes plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
