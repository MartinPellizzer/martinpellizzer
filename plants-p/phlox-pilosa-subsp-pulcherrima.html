Phlox pilosa subsp. pulcherrima

Phlox pilosa subsp.

pulcherrima, commonly known as the showy phlox, is a perennial flowering plant native to North America. It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and clusters of showy, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink and lavender.

The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It is often found in woodland edges and moist meadows, contributing to the biodiversity of its habitat.

The species is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
