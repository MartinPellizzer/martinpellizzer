Pinus nigra var. caramanica

Pinus nigra var. caramanica, commonly known as the Caramanica black pine, is a subspecies of the black pine native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a coniferous evergreen tree characterized by its dark, fissured bark and needle-like leaves that are typically 2 to 4 centimeters long. The plant produces cones that are typically 5 to 8 centimeters in length, containing seeds that are dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous areas.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to withstand harsh environmental conditions.
