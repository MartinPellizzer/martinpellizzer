Pelargonium hollandii

Pelargonium hollandii, commonly known as the Dutch geranium, is a species of flowering plant in the family Geraniaceae.

It is native to South Africa and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, five-petaled flowers. The plant typically features dark green, lobed leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It blooms in late spring to early summer, producing vibrant pink or red flowers that attract pollinators.

Pelargonium hollandii is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to various growing conditions.
