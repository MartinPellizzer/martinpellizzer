Phaseolus gonospermos var. pullus

Phaseolus gonospermos var. pullus, commonly known as the hairy runner bean, is a variant of the runner bean species native to the Andean region of South America.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which provide a distinctive appearance. The plant produces elongated, pod-like fruits that are typically green when immature and turn yellow or orange when mature. The seeds inside the pods are edible and are often used in various culinary preparations.

Botanically, it belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is known for its leguminous plants capable of nitrogen fixation.
