Pandanus kjellbergii

Pandanus kjellbergii, commonly known as the Kjellberg's pandanus, is a flowering plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its tall, narrow leaves that form a rosette at the base, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces large, branched inflorescences that bear small, fragrant flowers. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to thrive in coastal and sandy soils.

Pandanus kjellbergii is often used in traditional crafts and has cultural significance in the regions where it is found.
