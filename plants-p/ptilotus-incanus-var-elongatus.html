Ptilotus incanus var. elongatus

Ptilotus incanus var. elongatus, commonly known as the slender spinifex, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of Australia.

It is a member of the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its prostrate growth habit and dense clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers. The plant produces distinctive, spiky fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It is well adapted to harsh, sandy soils and can thrive in low-nutrient environments.

This species is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soils and provide habitat for native wildlife.
