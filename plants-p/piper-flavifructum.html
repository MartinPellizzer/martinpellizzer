Piper flavifructum

Piper flavifructum, commonly known as the yellow fruit pepper plant, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its small, yellow berries that are used in traditional medicine and as a spice. Its leaves are often used in culinary applications due to their aromatic properties.

The plant thrives in humid, warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
