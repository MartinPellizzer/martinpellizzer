Protea fusca

Protea fusca, commonly known as the Cape sugarbush, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Proteaceae family and is characterized by its dense, shrub-like growth habit. The plant produces striking, cone-shaped inflorescences that are typically pink or red in color. Its leaves are leathery and evergreen, providing year-round interest in the landscape.

Protea fusca is well adapted to nutrient-poor soils and is often found in fynbos vegetation.
