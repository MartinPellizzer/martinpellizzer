Pterocephalus caramanicus

Pterocephalus caramanicus, commonly known as the Persian ironweed, is a perennial herb native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and dense clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces a distinctive, spiky fruit known as a cypsela, which is covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in dry gardens and natural habitats.
