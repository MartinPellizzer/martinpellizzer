Phelline gracilior

Phelline gracilior, commonly known as the slender phelline, is a flowering plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is characterized by its delicate, erect stems and trifoliate leaves composed of three small, oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. Native to certain regions of southern Africa, it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun exposure.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and natural habitats.
