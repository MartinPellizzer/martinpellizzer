Potentilla villosa var. verna

Potentilla villosa var. verna, commonly known as spring cinquefoil, is a low-growing perennial shrub native to North America.

It features slender, hairy stems and small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, acidic soils in woodland or streamside habitats. Its leaves are compound, consisting of five oval-shaped leaflets, and it is often found in shaded or partially shaded environments.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local wildlife.
