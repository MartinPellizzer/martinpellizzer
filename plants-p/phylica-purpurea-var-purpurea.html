Phylica purpurea var. purpurea

Phylica purpurea var. purpurea, commonly known as the purple bottlebrush, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, cylindrical clusters of bright red or purple flowers that resemble bottle brushes. The plant produces small, oval-shaped fruits that are typically red or orange in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for coastal and sandy environments.

The foliage is evergreen, with narrow, leathery leaves that provide year-round interest.
