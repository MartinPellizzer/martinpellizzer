Plectranthus fimbriatus

Plectranthus fimbriatus, commonly known as the laceleaf plant, is a species of flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, velvety, waxy leaves that have a finely serrated edge, giving it a unique appearance. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, which bloom in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires moderate to bright indirect light for optimal growth.

Plectranthus fimbriatus is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and ease of care.
