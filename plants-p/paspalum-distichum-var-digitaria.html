Paspalum distichum var. digitaria

Paspalum distichum var. digitaria, commonly known as Digitaria, is a grass species native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers. It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

This species is commonly used in forage and erosion control due to its hardiness and adaptability.
