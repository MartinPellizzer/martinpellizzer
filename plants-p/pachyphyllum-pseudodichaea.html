Pachyphyllum pseudodichaea

Pachyphyllum pseudodichaea, commonly known as the silver leaf cactus, is a species native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems that store water, a typical adaptation for arid environments. The plant produces small, pale green or white flowers during the warmer months. Its leaves are modified into spines, reducing water loss and providing protection from herbivores.

Pachyphyllum pseudodichaea is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
