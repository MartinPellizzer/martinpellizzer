Peperomia tamayoi

Peperomia tamayoi, commonly known as the "Snowy Peperomia," is a compact, evergreen perennial native to the tropical regions of South America.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that are typically pale green with a waxy texture, often adorned with a silvery or white bloom. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually green or reddish in color, which are followed by tiny, round berries. It thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Due to its slow growth and attractive foliage, it is well-suited for container cultivation and adds a touch of elegance to any space.
