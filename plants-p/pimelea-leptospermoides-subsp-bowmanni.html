Pimelea leptospermoides subsp. bowmanni

Pimelea leptospermoides subsp.

bowmanni, commonly known as the Bowmann's bottlebrush, is a shrub native to Western Australia. It is characterized by its distinctive cylindrical flower spikes, which are typically red or orange in color.

The plant has narrow, linear leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that attract nectar-feeding birds and insects. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal heathlands.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in its native habitat.
