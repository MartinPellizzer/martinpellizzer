Papaver lecoqii

Papaver lecoqii, commonly known as the Lecoq's poppy, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from red to orange, often with a contrasting blackish center. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stem is sturdy and often branched.

Papaver lecoqii is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
