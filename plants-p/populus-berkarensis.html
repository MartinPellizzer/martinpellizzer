Populus berkarensis

Populus berkarensis, commonly known as the Berkarensian poplar, is a deciduous tree native to the temperate regions of eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves, which are typically broad and have a smooth or slightly serrated margin. The tree produces small, fluffy seeds enclosed in a capsule, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found along riverbanks and in forested areas.

Populus berkarensis is valued for its rapid growth and is sometimes used in reforestation efforts.
