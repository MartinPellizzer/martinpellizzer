Prunus eremophila

Prunus eremophila, commonly known as the desert peach, is a small deciduous tree native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and small, oval-shaped leaves that turn reddish in the fall. The plant produces white or pale pink flowers in the spring, followed by small, round fruits that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans. Prunus eremophila is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments with minimal water, making it a resilient species in desert ecosystems.

Its ability to thrive in extreme conditions makes it an important component of local flora and a subject of interest in botanical studies.
