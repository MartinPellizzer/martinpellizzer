Palmijuncus zebrinus

Palmijuncus zebrinus, commonly known as the zebra palm, is a unique species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, narrow, and vertically striped leaves that give it its common name. The plant has a slender trunk with a fibrous, spongy texture, and it produces small, clustered inflorescences. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking appearance.

However, it is not commonly found in the wild and is more often seen in botanical gardens and private collections.
