Pithecellobium pachycarpum

Pithecellobium pachycarpum, commonly known as the giant tamarind, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and clustered, yellowish flowers. The plant produces large, woody pods that contain edible seeds surrounded by a sweet, sticky pulp.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in local cuisines and medicine.
