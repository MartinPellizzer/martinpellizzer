Pilosella leucopsilon subsp. leucopsilon

Pilosella leucopsilon subsp.

leucopsilon, commonly known as the whitehead mouse-ear, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is a low-growing perennial with a mat-forming habit, featuring dense, silvery-white to grayish foliage.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in early spring. Its leaves are covered in fine, white hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or limestone areas.
