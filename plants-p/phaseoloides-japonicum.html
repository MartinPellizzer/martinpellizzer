Phaseoloides japonicum

Phaseoloides japonicum, commonly known as the Japanese bean vine, is a climbing legume native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and yellow flowers. The plant produces elongated pods containing edible seeds that are used in traditional cuisine. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for both ornamental and agricultural purposes.

Its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil makes it beneficial for soil enrichment.
