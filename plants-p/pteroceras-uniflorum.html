Pteroceras uniflorum

Pteroceras uniflorum, commonly known as the single-flowered pteroceras, is a unique member of the Ericaceae family.

It is a shrub characterized by its evergreen leaves and distinctive floral structure. The plant produces a single, showy flower per inflorescence, which is typically white or pale pink in color. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
