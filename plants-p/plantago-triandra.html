Plantago triandra

Plantago triandra, commonly known as the broadleaf plantain, is a perennial herb native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It features a rosette of broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in a dense spike, which are wind-pollinated. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found along roadsides and in meadows.

Its leaves are used in traditional medicine and as a food source in some cultures.
