Pinus sylvestris var. monticola

Pinus sylvestris var. monticola, commonly known as the Scots pine mountain variety, is a subspecies of the Scots pine native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its tall, straight trunk and needle-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The cones of this variety are relatively small and often hang downward, containing two-winged seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to colder, high-altitude environments.

This subspecies plays an important ecological role in mountain ecosystems, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
