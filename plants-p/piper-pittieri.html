Piper pittieri

Piper pittieri, commonly known as the Mexican pepper plant, is a climbing shrub native to Central America and parts of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by elongated, edible berries. These berries are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Its leaves are also utilized in culinary applications, adding a spicy flavor to various dishes.
