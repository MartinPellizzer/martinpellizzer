Persicaria koreana

Persicaria koreana, commonly known as Korean smartweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, pinkish-red flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist to wet soil conditions and is often found in marshy areas or along stream banks.

Persicaria koreana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
