Pseudopodospermum lamellatum

Pseudopodospermum lamellatum, commonly known as the leafy spurge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America.

It features a robust root system and produces small, yellow flowers arranged in dense clusters. The plant has alternate, narrow, and often hairy leaves that are typically arranged in two rows along the stem. It is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types.

Due to its invasive nature, it is considered a problematic weed in many regions.
