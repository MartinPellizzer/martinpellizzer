Pteretis nodulosa f. pubescens

Pteretis nodulosa f. pubescens, commonly known as the pubescent nodding fern, is a rare and distinctive fern species characterized by its finely hairy foliage.

It features delicate, lance-shaped fronds that are often arching and bear small, clustered sori on the underside. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as forests or rocky crevices, where it thrives in cool, humid conditions. Its unique pubescence, or fine hairy covering, sets it apart from other members of the Pteretis genus.

This fern is of interest to botanists and conservationists due to its limited distribution and ecological specificity.
