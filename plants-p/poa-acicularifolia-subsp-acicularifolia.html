Poa acicularifolia subsp. acicularifolia

Poa acicularifolia subsp.

acicularifolia, commonly known as the slender spikegrass, is a flowering plant species native to the southern Andes of South America. It is a perennial grass that typically grows in high-altitude environments, often found in rocky or alpine regions.

The plant is characterized by its narrow, needle-like leaves and compact growth habit, which help it survive in harsh, cold climates. Its inflorescence consists of a dense, cylindrical spike that bears small, spikelet-like flowers.

This subspecies is of interest to botanists due to its unique adaptations to extreme environmental conditions.
