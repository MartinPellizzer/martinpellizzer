Pandanus pleiocephalus

Pandanus pleiocephalus, commonly known as the broad-leaf pandanus, is a tropical plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its large, sword-shaped leaves that grow in a rosette pattern around a central stem. The plant produces a tall, branched inflorescence that bears small, fragrant flowers. Its roots and leaves are often used in traditional crafts and for medicinal purposes.

The species is well adapted to coastal and saline environments, making it a resilient component of island ecosystems.
