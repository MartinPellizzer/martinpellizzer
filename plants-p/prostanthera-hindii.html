Prostanthera hindii

Prostanthera hindii, commonly known as the mountain mint bush, is a perennial shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant features small, tubular flowers that are typically pale blue to violet in color, arranged in dense clusters. Its leaves are aromatic, often described as having a mint-like scent, and are covered in fine hairs.

Prostanthera hindii is adapted to grow in well-drained soils and is often found in dry sclerophyll forests and rocky habitats.
