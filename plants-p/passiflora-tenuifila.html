Passiflora tenuifila

Passiflora tenuifila, commonly known as the narrow-leaf passionflower, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, palmate leaves and showy, fragrant flowers that feature a unique blend of colors and intricate structures. The plant produces edible, orange-yellow berries that are rich in nutrients. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Passiflora tenuifila is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
