Paepalanthus capixaba

Paepalanthus capixaba, commonly known as the Capixaba cactus, is a species of flowering plant native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its spiny stems and clusters of yellow flowers. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing typical succulent features for water storage. It thrives in sandy soils and is often found in coastal regions.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and vulnerable species.
