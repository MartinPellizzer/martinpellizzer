Pinus armandi subsp. mastersiana

Pinus armandi subsp.

mastersiana, commonly known as the Chinese red pine, is a subspecies native to China. It is characterized by its slender, conical shape and needle-like leaves that are typically 5 to 8 centimeters long.

The cones are woody and often persist on the tree for several years, contributing to its ornamental value. It prefers well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

This subspecies is valued for its resilience and is used in horticulture and reforestation efforts.
