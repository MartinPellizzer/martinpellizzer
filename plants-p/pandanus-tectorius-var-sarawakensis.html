Pandanus tectorius var. sarawakensis

Pandanus tectorius var. sarawakensis, commonly known as the Sarawak pandanus, is a variant of the common pandanus species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree characterized by its stiff, sword-shaped leaves that are often used in traditional crafts and as thatching material. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers arranged in panicles, which are typically followed by spiny, three-lobed fruits. It thrives in coastal and wetland habitats, preferring well-drained sandy soils.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
