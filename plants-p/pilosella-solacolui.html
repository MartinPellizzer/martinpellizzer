Pilosella solacolui

Pilosella solacolui, commonly known as the slender pearly everlasting, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous small florets. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its foliage is covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a velvety appearance.

The flowers are usually white or pale yellow, and they bloom in the summer months.
