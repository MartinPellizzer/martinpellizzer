Pycnocoma macrophylla var. zenkeri

Pycnocoma macrophylla var. zenkeri, commonly known as the Zambesi kikuyu, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or riverine habitats. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its lush appearance.

The varietal form, zenkeri, is distinguished by its slightly different leaf structure and growth habit compared to the typical variety.
