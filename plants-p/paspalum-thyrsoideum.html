Paspalum thyrsoideum

Paspalum thyrsoideum, commonly known as the silver grass or silver bluestem, is a perennial grass native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-green foliage and tufted inflorescence that resembles a plume. The plant typically grows in open woodlands, prairies, and disturbed soils, thriving in full sun. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense mats, making it a common weed in agricultural areas.

Paspalum thyrsoideum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping for its elegant appearance.
