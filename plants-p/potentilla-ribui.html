Potentilla ribui

Potentilla ribui, commonly known as the red sandalwood or red potentilla, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and features compound leaves with three to five oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces clusters of small, showy yellow flowers in the spring, which are followed by red, fleshy berries. Its roots are known for their medicinal properties, often used in traditional herbal remedies.

Potentilla ribui is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
