Palmijuncus flagellum

Palmijuncus flagellum, commonly known as the flagellate palm, is a unique plant species characterized by its elongated, whip-like fronds.

It is native to specific regions of Southeast Asia, where it thrives in humid, tropical climates. The plant's structure consists of a short stem with long, flexible leaves that can reach several meters in length. Its botanical features include a fibrous root system and a distinct, clustered arrangement of fronds.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a delicate and endangered species in its natural environment.
