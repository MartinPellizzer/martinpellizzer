Polygonum convolvulus var. bertolonii

Polygonum convolvulus var. bertolonii, commonly known as the Bertoloni's bindweed, is a variant of the common bindweed, characterized by its distinct morphological features.

It is an annual or biennial herb that typically grows in disturbed soils and arid environments. The plant has prostrate or creeping stems with tendrils that allow it to climb and spread across surfaces. Its small, greenish-white flowers appear in clusters and are followed by bead-like fruits.

This variant is notable for its adaptability and is often found in regions with Mediterranean climates.
