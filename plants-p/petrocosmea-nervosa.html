Petrocosmea nervosa

Petrocosmea nervosa, commonly known as the rock orchid, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, pale greenish-yellow flowers with intricate patterns. The plant typically grows on rocky substrates, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, while its roots are fibrous and specialized for nutrient absorption.

Petrocosmea nervosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with well-drained soil and ample sunlight.
