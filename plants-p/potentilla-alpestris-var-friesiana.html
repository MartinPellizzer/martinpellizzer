Potentilla alpestris var. friesiana

Potentilla alpestris var. friesiana, commonly known as the alpine cinquefoil, is a low-growing shrub native to mountainous regions of Europe.

It features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, typically from June to July. The plant has slender, wiry stems and hairy leaves that are typically oval to elliptic in shape. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine meadows and slopes.

This variety is valued for its hardiness and ability to flourish in harsh, high-altitude environments.
