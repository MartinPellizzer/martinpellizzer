Petrea glandulosa

Petrea glandulosa, commonly known as the Brazilian jasmine, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 6 meters in height, characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant white flowers. The plant is known for its aromatic properties, with flowers that emit a strong, sweet scent, especially at night. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often used in ornamental gardening.

Petrea glandulosa is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional medicine.
