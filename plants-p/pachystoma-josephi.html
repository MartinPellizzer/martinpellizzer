Pachystoma josephi

Pachystoma josephi, commonly known as the Joseph's stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its fleshy, thick leaves that store water, adapted to its arid and rocky habitat. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow and orange during the summer months. It thrives in full sun and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Its unique appearance and hardy nature make it a valuable addition to xeriscaping and drought-tolerant landscaping.
