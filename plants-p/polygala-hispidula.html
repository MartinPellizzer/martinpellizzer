Polygala hispidula

Polygala hispidula, commonly known as the hairy milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to parts of North America.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, intricate flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is often found in prairie and steppe habitats. Its leaves are alternate and pinnate, with small, hairy leaflets that give it its common name.

The flowers, which are usually pink or white, bloom in late spring and early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
