Passiflora suberosa var. divaricata

Passiflora suberosa var. divaricata, commonly known as the Variegated Passion Vine, is a climbing shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive variegated pattern of green and white. The plant produces striking flowers with five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments, typical of the Passifloraceae family. Its fruit is a yellowish, fleshy berry that ripens in late summer.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
