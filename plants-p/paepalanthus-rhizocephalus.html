Paepalanthus rhizocephalus

Paepalanthus rhizocephalus, commonly known as the red-flowered puya, is a species of flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Xanthorrhoeaceae and is characterized by its striking, showy inflorescence composed of numerous red or orange flowers. The plant is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or mountainous habitats, often at high altitudes. Its rhizomatous root system allows it to anchor firmly in the soil and store nutrients.

The flowers are pollinated primarily by birds, particularly hummingbirds, due to their bright colors and nectar-rich structure.
