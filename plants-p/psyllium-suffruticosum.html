Psyllium suffruticosum

Psyllium suffruticosum, commonly known as the hairy psyllium, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit and produces small, yellowish flowers. The plant is known for its fibrous seed casings, which are rich in mucilage when soaked in water. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Psyllium suffruticosum is valued for its potential medicinal and nutritional properties.
