Piper taumanum

Piper taumanum, commonly known as the Taumanum pepper or black pepper vine, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its climbing habit and dark green, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that develop into clusters of dark, peppery berries. These berries are used to make black, white, and pink pepper, which are widely used in global cuisines.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both traditional medicine and the spice trade.
