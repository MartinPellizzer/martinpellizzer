Prunus Spinosa Subvar. Moldavica

Blackthorn, scientifically known as Prunus spinosa subvar. moldavica, is a resilient shrub native to temperate regions of Europe and western Asia. It is recognized for its sharp thorns and small, dark berries that ripen in late summer. Despite its prickly appearance, the plant plays a vital role in local ecosystems, providing food for birds and insects. Its hardy nature allows it to thrive in poor soils and harsh climates. Often overlooked, the Blackthorn is a symbol of endurance and adaptability in the natural world.
