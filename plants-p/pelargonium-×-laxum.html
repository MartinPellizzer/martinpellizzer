Pelargonium × laxum

Pelargonium × laxum, commonly known as the zonal geranium, is a hybrid species within the Geraniaceae family.

It is characterized by its showy, five-petaled flowers that come in a variety of colors, including red, pink, and white. The leaves are typically rounded with distinct zonation patterns, giving the plant its common name. Native to South Africa, it thrives in warm, sunny conditions and is often cultivated as a ornamental plant.

Its compact growth habit makes it suitable for container gardening and garden beds.
