Pera parvifolia

Pera parvifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved pera, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, lance-shaped leaves that are typically less than 5 centimeters in length. The plant produces clusters of white or pale yellow flowers that bloom during the spring and early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
