Parasia cordata

Parasia cordata, commonly known as the heartleaf vine, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It features heart-shaped leaves that are usually bright green and glossy. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink. Its stems are woody and can grow quite long, allowing it to climb trees or trellises.

Parasia cordata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
