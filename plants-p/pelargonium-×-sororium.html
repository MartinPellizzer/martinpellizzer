Pelargonium × sororium

Pelargonium × sororium, commonly known as the ivy-leaved geranium, is a hybrid plant in the Geraniaceae family.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and heart-shaped, ivy-like leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces clusters of small, five-petaled flowers in shades of red, pink, and white. It is often cultivated as a decorative plant for hanging baskets or containers due to its cascading foliage.

Pelargonium × sororium is relatively easy to care for, requiring well-drained soil and moderate sunlight.
