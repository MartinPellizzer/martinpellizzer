Philadelphus mexicanus var. coulteri

Philadelphus mexicanus var. coulteri, commonly known as Coulter's mock orange, is a deciduous shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges and can grow up to 6 feet tall. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its showy blooms and winter interest from its red-brown bark.
