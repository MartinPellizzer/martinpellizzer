Primula lanata

Primula lanata, commonly known as the downy primrose, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia, including China and the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its hairy, velvety leaves that give it its scientific name "lanata," derived from the Latin word for "wool.". The plant produces clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple, typically blooming in early spring. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in woodland or shaded areas.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and botanical collections worldwide.
