Pandanus myriocarpus

Pandanus myriocarpus, commonly known as the bottlebrush pandanus, is a tropical plant native to the Indo-Pacific region.

It is characterized by its tall, narrow leaves that form a rosette at the base, creating a distinctive appearance. The plant produces fragrant, yellowish flowers that are clustered in a dense, bottlebrush-like inflorescence. Its fruit is a spiny, woody capsule that contains numerous small, edible seeds.

Pandanus myriocarpus is often used in traditional crafts and has cultural significance in various island communities.
