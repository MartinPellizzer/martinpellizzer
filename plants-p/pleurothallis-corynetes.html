Pleurothallis corynetes

Pleurothallis corynetes, commonly known as the orchid with the unusual flower shape, is a species of orchid native to South America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its unique, elongated flowers that resemble a small, twisted tail. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid tropical forests. Its botanical features include small, lance-shaped leaves and a short, wiry stem.

Due to its distinctive appearance, it is often cultivated for its ornamental value in specialized collections.
