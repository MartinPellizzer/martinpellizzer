Phyllostachys pubescens

Phyllostachys pubescens, commonly known as the silver bamboo, is a member of the grass family (Poaceae) and is native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, hollow stems that are covered in a fine, silvery down, giving it its common name. This plant is a fast-growing perennial, often reaching heights of up to 10 meters in suitable conditions. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, typically with a bluish-green hue.

Phyllostachys pubescens is widely cultivated for ornamental purposes and is also used in traditional Chinese medicine.
