Picea abies f. monstrosa

Picea abies f. monstrosa, commonly known as the twisted Norway spruce, is a unique variant of the Norway spruce characterized by its irregular, gnarled growth habit.

This form exhibits exaggerated twisting and curling of the branches, creating a striking, almost otherworldly appearance. The botanical features include thick, dark green foliage and a slow growth rate, which contributes to its unusual morphology. It is primarily found in cooler, shaded environments where it thrives as a decorative or ornamental plant.

Its distinctive form makes it a popular choice for botanical gardens and specialized collections.
