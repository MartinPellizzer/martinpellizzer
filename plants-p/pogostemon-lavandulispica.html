Pogostemon lavandulispica

Pogostemon lavandulispica, commonly known as the lavender spikeplant, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It features aromatic, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces spikes of small, lavender-blue flowers that bloom in summer. Its foliage is known for its strong, minty scent, which is used in traditional medicine.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is valued for its ornamental and medicinal properties.
