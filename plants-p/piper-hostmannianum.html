Piper hostmannianum

Piper hostmannianum, commonly known as the Hostmann's pepper vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes various species of pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and small, greenish-white flowers that develop into berries. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in rainforests.

The berries are typically used in traditional medicine and can be consumed raw or cooked.
