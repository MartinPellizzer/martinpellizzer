Piper bangii var. pubinervium

Piper bangii var. pubinervium, commonly known as the hairy nerve pepper, is a climbing vine native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its finely pubescent stems and leaves with prominent venation. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by elongated, curved berries that turn dark when mature. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties.

The species is valued both for its ornamental appeal and its potential medicinal applications.
