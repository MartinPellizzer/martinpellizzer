Polygonum ajanense

Polygonum ajanense, commonly known as the Ajanese knotweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, characterized by its distinctive nodes and jointed stems. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as stream banks and forest floors. Its leaves are lance-shaped with a prominent midrib, and it produces small, greenish flowers in clusters.

Polygonum ajanense is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its attractive foliage.
