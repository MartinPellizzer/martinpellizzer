Pycreus paniculatus

Pycreus paniculatus, commonly known as the yellow panic grass, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and panicles of small yellow flowers. The plant is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions. Its fibrous roots help in soil stabilization, making it useful in erosion control.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping.
