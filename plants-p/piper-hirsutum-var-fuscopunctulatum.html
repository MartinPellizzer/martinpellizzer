Piper hirsutum var. fuscopunctulatum

Piper hirsutum var. fuscopunctulatum, commonly known as the dark-spotted pepper plant, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dark green, ovate leaves with prominent dark brown or black spots. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by elongated, cylindrical berries that turn red when mature. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires partial to full sun for optimal growth.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
