Panicum italicum var. praecox

Panicum italicum var. praecox, commonly known as early Italian grass, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically green with a slight bluish tint. The plant produces small, fluffy panicles of flowers that are pale green to brown in color, appearing in late spring to early summer. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract beneficial insects.
