Psoralea munyensis

Psoralea munyensis, commonly known as the Munyenea psoralea, is a flowering plant native to Africa, specifically found in countries like Tanzania and Kenya.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. It produces small, pea-like flowers that are usually yellow or greenish in color.

The seeds are enclosed in a pod and are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
