Potamogeton natans var. lancifolius

Potamogeton natans var. lancifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaf pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in North America.

It has long, slender leaves that are typically narrow and lance-shaped, which helps it thrive in slow-moving or still water. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually found in clusters, and it spreads primarily through rhizomes. It plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.

Due to its dense growth, it can also impact water flow and sediment stability in its habitat.
