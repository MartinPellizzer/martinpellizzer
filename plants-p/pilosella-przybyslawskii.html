Pilosella przybyslawskii

Pilosella przybyslawskii, commonly known as the Polish hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its low-growing habit, with slender stems and dense clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers. The plant features basal leaves that are lanceolate and hairy, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes, often in temperate regions.

Pilosella przybyslawskii is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
