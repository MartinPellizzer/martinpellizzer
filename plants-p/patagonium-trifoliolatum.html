Patagonium trifoliolatum

Patagonium trifoliolatum, commonly known as the three-leafed Patagonian plant, is a shrub native to the high Andes of South America.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three leaflets arranged symmetrically. The plant produces small, clustered white or pale yellow flowers during the summer months. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is adapted to the harsh conditions of its alpine habitat.

Due to its unique morphology and limited distribution, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
