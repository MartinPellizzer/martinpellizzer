Proteopsis lanuginosa

Proteopsis lanuginosa, commonly known as the woolly protea, is a striking South African plant belonging to the Proteaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescence composed of numerous white to pinkish flowers clustered around a central stem. The plant features a woody, branched stem covered in a dense layer of soft, woolly hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its flowers are typically visited by pollinators such as birds and insects, contributing to its ecological significance.

Proteopsis lanuginosa thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in fynbos vegetation in the Western Cape region.
