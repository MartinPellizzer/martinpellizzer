Polygonum franciscanum

Polygonum franciscanum, commonly known as Franciscan knotweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit and clusters of small, pinkish to reddish flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are often hairy and have a distinctive, rough texture. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in riparian zones and along streambanks.

Botanically, it belongs to the Polygonaceae family, which includes other familiar plants like buckwheat and sorrel.
