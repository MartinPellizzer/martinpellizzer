Potentilla fruticosa var. pyrenaica

Potentilla fruticosa var. pyrenaica, commonly known as the Pyrenean cinquefoil, is a shrub native to the Pyrenees region.

It is characterized by its dense, sprawling growth habit and bright yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant has small, rounded leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
