Prunus spinosa var. acropyrena

Prunus spinosa var. acropyrena, commonly known as the blackthorn, is a thorny shrub native to Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its dark purple to black berries, which are encased in a waxy coating. The plant produces small, white flowers in early spring, which are followed by the development of its edible fruits. Its leaves are oval-shaped with a pointed tip and are typically dark green in color.

The shrub is often found in hedgerows and woodlands, where it plays a role in providing food for birds and other wildlife.
