Paronychia chlorothyrsa subsp. canariensis

Paronychia chlorothyrsa subsp.

canariensis, commonly known as Canary Island sandwort, is a low-growing, mat-forming plant native to the Canary Islands. It features small, clustered leaves that are typically covered in fine, white hairs, giving it a silvery appearance.

The plant produces tiny, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, adding a delicate touch to its foliage. It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and is often found in coastal or rocky habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to Mediterranean climates.
