Pelargonium radiatum

Pelargonium radiatum, commonly known as the shieldleaf geranium, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that are often shield-shaped, giving it its common name. The plant produces clusters of small, pink or purple flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Pelargonium radiatum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
