Pelea kavaiensis

Pelea kavaiensis, commonly known as the Kava kava plant, is a member of the Piperaceae family.

It is native to the Pacific Islands, particularly the islands of Tonga and Samoa. The plant is characterized by its climbing habit and heart-shaped leaves. Its most notable feature is the presence of tubers in its roots, which contain the active compounds responsible for its traditional use.

These tubers are typically harvested and processed to produce a beverage used for its calming effects.
