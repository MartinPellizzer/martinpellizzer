Phlomis similis

Phlomis similis, commonly known as narrow-leaved globemallow, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features upright, branching stems that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces showy, bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, typically from July to September. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a fuzzy texture, and it thrives in well-drained soils.

Phlomis similis is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
