Piptadenia macrocarpa

Piptadenia macrocarpa, commonly known as the Brazilian peach tree, is a tropical legume native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy yellow flowers and compound leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces fleshy, red-orange fruits that are edible and have a sweet, aromatic flavor. Its roots and leaves contain bioactive compounds, making it valuable in both culinary and medicinal applications.

The tree is also important for ecological purposes, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
