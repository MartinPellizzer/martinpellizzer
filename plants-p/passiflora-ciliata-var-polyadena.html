Passiflora ciliata var. polyadena

Passiflora ciliata var. polyadena, commonly known as the purple passionflower, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful flowers that display a blend of purple, blue, and white hues. The plant produces edible, orange-yellow berries that are rich in nutrients. Its leaves are heart-shaped with serrated edges and covered in fine, silky hairs.

This species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in warm climates.
