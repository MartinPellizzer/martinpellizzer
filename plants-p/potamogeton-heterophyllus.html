Potamogeton heterophyllus

Potamogeton heterophyllus, commonly known as the variable pondweed, is a freshwater aquatic plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its ability to produce different leaf shapes depending on its growing conditions, with submerged leaves typically being narrow and ribbon-like. The plant has slender, branching stems that can reach lengths of up to 2 meters, often forming dense underwater vegetation. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, typically appearing in clusters near the water surface.
