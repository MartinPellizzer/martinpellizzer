Persicaria tomentosa var. glabrior

Persicaria tomentosa var. glabrior, commonly known as soft-hairy knotweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of Asia.

It features upright stems that are covered in soft, fuzzy hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The leaves are ovate to elliptic, with a hairy surface and a smooth margin. It produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or red, blooming in late summer.

This plant is often found in moist environments such as wet meadows and stream banks.
