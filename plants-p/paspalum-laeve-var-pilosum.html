Paspalum laeve var. pilosum

Paspalum laeve var. pilosum, commonly known as silky paspalum, is a perennial grass native to South America.

It is characterized by its fine, soft, and slightly hairy foliage, which gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in moist or wetland environments, making it well-suited for riparian zones and wet meadows. Its inflorescence consists of a loose cluster of spikelets, and it produces small, wind-pollinated flowers.

This variety is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for wildlife.
