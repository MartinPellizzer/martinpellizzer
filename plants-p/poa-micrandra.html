Poa micrandra

Poa micrandra, commonly known as the narrowleaf bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to North America.

It is characterized by its fine, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in open, sunny areas such as meadows and prairies. Its leaves are usually narrow and slightly curled, and it has a fibrous root system.

Poa micrandra is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in various soil conditions.
