Pleurothallis arcuata var. parvifolia

Pleurothallis arcuata var. parvifolia, commonly known as the small-flowered pleurothallis, is a species of orchid native to the cloud forests of Central and South America.

It is a small, epiphytic plant that typically grows on trees in humid, shaded environments. The plant produces delicate, linear leaves and bears small, white or pale yellow flowers with a distinctive, intricate structure. It is appreciated for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in greenhouses or terrariums.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and challenging species to grow in cultivation.
