Paspalum nitidum

Paspalum nitidum, commonly known as silvergrass, is a perennial grass native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, silver-gray blades that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as wetlands and grassy areas. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences with tiny flowers, which are wind-pollinated.

Paspalum nitidum is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in various soil conditions.
