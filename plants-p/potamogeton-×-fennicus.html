Potamogeton × fennicus

Potamogeton × fennicus, commonly known as the hybrid water plant, is a perennial aquatic plant that thrives in freshwater environments.

It is a natural hybrid between Potamogeton crispus and Potamogeton phyllitoides, exhibiting characteristics of both parent species. The plant typically grows in shallow waters, forming dense vegetation that provides habitat for various aquatic organisms. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often with a waxy coating to reduce water loss.

It plays an important role in maintaining water quality and supporting biodiversity in wetland ecosystems.
