Prostanthera eungella

Prostanthera eungella, commonly known as Eungella mint, is a small shrub native to the subtropical rainforests of Queensland, Australia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows to a height of about 1 meter and produces small, fragrant white or pale blue flowers. Its leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and covered in a fine, velvety texture.

Prostanthera eungella is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional Indigenous medicine.
