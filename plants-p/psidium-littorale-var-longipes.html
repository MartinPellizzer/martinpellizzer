Psidium littorale var. longipes

Psidium littorale var. longipes, commonly known as the long-stalked persimmon, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to coastal regions of southern Brazil and northern Argentina, where it thrives in sandy soils and humid climates. The plant produces small, white flowers that are typically followed by small, red or yellow fruits. The leaves are simple, elliptical, and have a glossy surface, while the bark is smooth and grayish in color.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated for its edible fruits.
