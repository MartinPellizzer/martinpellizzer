Pyrus pannonica incisa

Pyrus pannonica incisa, commonly known as the Hungarian pear, is a deciduous tree native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its serrated leaves and clusters of white flowers in spring. The tree produces small, edible fruits that are typically harvested in late summer. Its bark is smooth and greyish-brown, becoming fissured with age.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in traditional medicine.
