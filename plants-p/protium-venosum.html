Protium venosum

Protium venosum, commonly known as the veiny custard apple, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Sapotaceae and is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and large, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are followed by pear-shaped fruits with a thick, waxy skin. The fruit contains a single large seed surrounded by a sweet, custard-like pulp.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its edible fruit.
