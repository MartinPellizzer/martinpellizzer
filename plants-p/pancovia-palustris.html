Pancovia palustris

Pancovia palustris, commonly known as the marsh panic grass, is a herbaceous plant native to wetland habitats.

It typically grows in moist to aquatic environments, such as marshes and swamps, where it thrives in nutrient-rich soils. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in dense spikes. Its root system helps stabilize the soil and prevent erosion in wetland areas.

Pancovia palustris plays a vital role in maintaining the ecological balance of its habitat.
