Poa inconspicua

Poa inconspicua, commonly known as the slender meadow grass, is a low-growing perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically forms dense, tufted clumps with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences that are usually inconspicuous, contributing to its common name. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, wetlands, and along stream banks.

Due to its hardy nature and ability to stabilize soil, it plays a role in ecological restoration projects.
