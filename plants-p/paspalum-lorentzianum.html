Paspalum lorentzianum

Paspalum lorentzianum, commonly known as the Lorentzian paspalum, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted foliage and compact growth habit, making it suitable for ornamental and erosion control purposes. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and disturbed areas.

Its adaptability to various environmental conditions contributes to its ecological significance in native habitats.
