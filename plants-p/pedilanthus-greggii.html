Pedilanthus greggii

Pedilanthus greggii, commonly known as Gregg's candleplant, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its unique, candle-like appearance with a single, elongated stem and a cluster of yellow flowers at the top. The plant has fleshy, waxy leaves that help it retain moisture in its arid habitat. It belongs to the Apocynaceae family, which includes many toxic plants.

Pedilanthus greggii is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking appearance and drought tolerance.
