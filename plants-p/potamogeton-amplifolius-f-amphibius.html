Potamogeton amplifolius f. amphibius

Potamogeton amplifolius f. amphibius, commonly known as the amphibious pondweed, is a variant of the pondweed species characterized by its ability to thrive in both aquatic and terrestrial environments.

This plant typically has long, narrow leaves that are often submerged in water but can also be found partially exposed above the water surface. It is commonly found in slow-moving freshwater habitats such as ponds, lakes, and wetlands. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are usually found in dense clusters near the water's surface.

Its growth pattern and adaptability make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
