Pleurothallis garayi

Pleurothallis garayi, commonly known as the Garay's orchid, is a species native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its small, delicate flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are usually white or pale green, with a distinctive lip that aids in pollination.

Pleurothallis garayi is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by enthusiasts of rare orchids.
