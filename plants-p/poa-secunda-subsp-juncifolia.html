Poa secunda subsp. juncifolia

Poa secunda subsp.

juncifolia, commonly known as the tufted bentgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the northern hemisphere. It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and fine, bluish-green leaves.

The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils, often found in meadows, wetlands, and along stream banks. Its inflorescence consists of a single spikelet that is often subtended by a leaf-like bract.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping for its ability to thrive in shaded conditions.
