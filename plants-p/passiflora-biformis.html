Passiflora biformis

Passiflora biformis, commonly known as the two-lobed passionflower, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, showy flowers that feature five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments. The plant produces blue or black berries that are edible and contain seeds. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped with a deep lobed base.

Passiflora biformis thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.
