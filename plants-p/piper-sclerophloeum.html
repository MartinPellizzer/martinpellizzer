Piper sclerophloeum

Piper sclerophloeum, commonly known as the rough-leaved pepper vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many species of pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its dark green, rough-textured leaves and small, clustered white flowers. Its fruits are small berries that turn from green to yellow as they mature.

Piper sclerophloeum is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties.
