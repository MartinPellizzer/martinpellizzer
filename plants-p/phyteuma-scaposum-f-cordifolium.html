Phyteuma scaposum f. cordifolium

Phyteuma scaposum f. cordifolium, commonly known as the heart-leaf phyteuma, is a horticultural form of the common phyteuma.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that give it its distinctive appearance. The plant produces tall, sturdy stems with showy, lavender to pinkish-purple flowers that bloom in summer. It is a perennial herb native to Europe and thrives in well-drained soil and full sun.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and ease of cultivation in gardens.
