Physalis laevigata

Physalis laevigata, commonly known as the smooth husk tomato, is a species of flowering plant in the nightshade family.

It is native to South America and is characterized by its distinctive inflated calyx that encases the fruit. The fruit is typically yellow or orange when mature and contains several small, edible seeds. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in the summer months.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of edible fruit in some regions.
