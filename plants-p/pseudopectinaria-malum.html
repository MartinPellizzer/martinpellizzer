Pseudopectinaria malum

Pseudopectinaria malum, commonly known as the silverleaf orchid, is a flowering plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, where it thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant features slender, erect stems covered in soft, silvery-gray leaves that reflect light, aiding in photosynthesis. Its flowers are small and clustered, typically in shades of white and pale green, with a delicate, orchid-like appearance.

Pseudopectinaria malum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in the right conditions.
