Pleurothallis ciliata var. abbreviata

Pleurothallis ciliata var. abbreviata, commonly known as the short-leaved orchid, is a species native to the cloud forests of Central and South America.

It is a small, epiphytic orchid that typically grows on trees, drawing moisture and nutrients from the air and rain. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces delicate, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pale pink. It thrives in humid, shaded environments with high air circulation.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is a sought-after species among orchid enthusiasts.
