Phebalium amplifolium

Phebalium amplifolium, commonly known as the broad-leafed hakea, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the Proteaceae family and is characterized by its dense, woody structure and evergreen foliage. The plant features broad, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a fine, silvery-gray pubescence. It produces small, yellowish flowers in rounded clusters, which are followed by woody, cone-like fruits.

Phebalium amplifolium is often found in heathlands and rocky soils, thriving in full sun to partial shade.
