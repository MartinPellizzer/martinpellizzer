Plectranthus inflexus var. verticillatus

Plectranthus inflexus var. verticillatus, commonly known as the curly mint or African violet mint, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to South Africa.

It features aromatic, hairy leaves that are often curled or twisted, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically purple or violet in color, arranged in whorls along the stems. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens or as a potted plant.
