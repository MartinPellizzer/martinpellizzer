Peperomia myriocarpa

Peperomia myriocarpa, commonly known as the many-fruit peperomia, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Central and South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and thick, fleshy leaves that are often variegated with white or silver markings. It produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences.

Peperomia myriocarpa is well-suited for indoor cultivation due to its low maintenance requirements and ability to thrive in bright, indirect light.
