Peperomia epidendron

Peperomia epidendron, commonly known as the silver peperomia, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It features succulent, fleshy leaves that are typically gray-green in color, covered in a waxy coating that helps reduce water loss. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in shades of white or pale green, which are followed by tiny, round berries. It thrives in well-draining soil and prefers bright, indirect light with moderate humidity.

Due to its attractive foliage and low maintenance requirements, it is a popular choice for indoor gardening.
