Poa dubia

Poa dubia, commonly known as the slender meadowgrass, is a species of grass native to South America.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and slender, wiry stems. The plant typically grows in moist, temperate environments such as meadows and wetlands. Its leaves are narrow and often have a bluish-green hue, and it produces small, spike-like inflorescences.

Poa dubia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping.
