Polybotrya appendiculata var. ludens

Polybotrya appendiculata var. ludens, commonly known as the red-stemmed rock fern, is a rare and distinctive fern species native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems that often display a reddish hue, particularly when young. The plant produces small, clustered sporangia on the undersides of its lance-shaped leaves, which are typically covered in fine, hairy structures. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and shaded environments, often found in mountainous regions.

Due to its limited distribution and unique morphology, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists alike.
