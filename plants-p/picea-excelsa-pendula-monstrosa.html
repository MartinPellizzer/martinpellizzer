Picea excelsa pendula-monstrosa

Picea excelsa pendula-monstrosa, commonly known as the giant white fir, is a rare and striking variant of the white fir species.

It is characterized by its pendulous, weeping branches that create a unique, cascading appearance. The plant has needle-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color, providing a striking visual contrast. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in high-altitude regions.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in specialized gardens and botanical collections.
