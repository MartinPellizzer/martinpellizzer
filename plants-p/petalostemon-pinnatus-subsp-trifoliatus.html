Petalostemon pinnatus subsp. trifoliatus

Petalostemon pinnatus subsp.

trifoliatus, commonly known as the three-leaved petalostemon, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which are divided into three leaflets, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in late summer. It prefers well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for both horticultural and ecological studies.
