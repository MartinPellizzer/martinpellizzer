Persea himalayensis

Persea himalayensis, commonly known as the Himalayan persimmon, is a deciduous tree native to the mountainous regions of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and fragrant, white flowers. The tree produces small, orange-yellow fruits that are edible when fully mature. Its botanical features include a smooth, greyish-bark and a preference for cool, moist climates.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of unique, sweet fruit.
