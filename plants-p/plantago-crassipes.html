Plantago crassipes

Plantago crassipes, commonly known as broadleaf plantain, is a perennial herb native to Europe, Asia, and Africa.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine, hair-like structures. The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in a spike, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and wetlands.

This species is known for its medicinal properties and has been used historically in traditional herbal remedies.
