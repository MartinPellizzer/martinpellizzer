Persicaria

Persicaria, commonly known as knotweed, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Polygonaceae.

These plants are characterized by their rhombic or ovate leaves and small, clustered flowers that often appear in shades of red, pink, or white. Many species are herbaceous perennials, thriving in moist or wet environments such as marshes and stream banks. They have a robust root system that can spread aggressively, making some species invasive in certain regions.

Persicaria species are often valued for their ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat and food for various wildlife.
