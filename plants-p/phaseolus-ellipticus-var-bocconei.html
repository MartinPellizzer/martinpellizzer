Phaseolus ellipticus var. bocconei

Phaseolus ellipticus var. bocconei, commonly known as the Bocconei runner bean, is a climbing legume native to Central America.

It produces elongated, elliptical pods that are typically green when immature and turn yellow or red when mature. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets and is characterized by its vigorous growth habit. It is cultivated for its edible beans, which are rich in protein and nutrients.

This variety is well-suited for warm climates and thrives in full sunlight.
