Piper cardenasii

Piper cardenasii, commonly known as the Cadenas pepper or Mexican pepper, is a tropical vine native to Central America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species. The plant produces small, green berries that turn red when ripe, containing aromatic seeds used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are often used in herbal remedies for their purported anti-inflammatory properties.

The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in the understory of tropical forests.
