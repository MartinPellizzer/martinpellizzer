Phyllanthus incurvus

Phyllanthus incurvus, commonly known as the African spurge, is a flowering plant belonging to the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are often hairy and have a distinctive curvature. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in a cup-like structure called a cyathium. It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

Phyllanthus incurvus is sometimes used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
