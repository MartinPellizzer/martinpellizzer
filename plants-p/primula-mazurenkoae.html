Primula mazurenkoae

Primula mazurenkoae, commonly known as the Mazurenko's primrose, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that typically bloom in shades of yellow and white. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows at high altitudes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a dense layer of fine hairs, which help protect it from harsh weather conditions.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
