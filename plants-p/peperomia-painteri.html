Peperomia painteri

Peperomia painteri, commonly known as the Painted Peperomia, is a compact, low-maintenance houseplant native to tropical regions of South America.

It features dense, waxy foliage with striking color variations, often displaying shades of green, pink, and red on its leaves. The plant has small, inconspicuous flowers that appear in clusters, typically in shades of white or green. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its unique appearance and ease of care, it is a popular choice for indoor gardening.
