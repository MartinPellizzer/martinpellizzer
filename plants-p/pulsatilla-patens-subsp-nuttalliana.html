Pulsatilla patens subsp. nuttalliana

Pulsatilla patens subsp.

nuttalliana, commonly known as the Nuttall's pasqueflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America. It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its showy, showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in early spring.

The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils in open habitats such as meadows and prairies. It has a basal rosette of hairy, lance-shaped leaves and produces follicles containing numerous seeds.

The species is also known for its medicinal properties and historical use in traditional herbal remedies.
