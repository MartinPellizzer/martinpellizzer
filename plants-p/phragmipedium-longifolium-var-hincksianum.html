Phragmipedium longifolium var. hincksianum

Phragmipedium longifolium var. hincksianum, commonly known as the Hincks' phragmipedium, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its long, lance-shaped leaves that can grow up to 40 centimeters in length. The plant produces striking, pendulous flowers with bright yellow petals and a deep orange lip. It thrives in shaded, humid environments and requires well-draining soil.

This variety is prized by orchid enthusiasts for its ornamental value and unique floral structure.
