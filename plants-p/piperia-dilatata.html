Piperia dilatata

Piperia dilatata, commonly known as the broadleaf piperia, is a herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the family Apiaceae, which includes plants like carrots and parsley, and is characterized by its aromatic foliage. Its small, white flowers are arranged in umbels, typical of the Apiaceae family, and bloom in early spring.

Piperia dilatata is often found in moist, shaded areas such as woodlands and stream banks.
