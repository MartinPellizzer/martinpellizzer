Potamogeton aulacophyllus

Potamogeton aulacophyllus, commonly known as the narrow-leaf pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater ecosystems in North America.

It has long, narrow leaves that are typically arranged in whorls around the stem, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often submerged. The plant thrives in slow-moving or still waters, such as lakes, ponds, and wetlands. Its rhizomes help stabilize sediment, and it provides habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.

Potamogeton aulacophyllus plays an important role in maintaining water quality and supporting biodiversity in its environment.
