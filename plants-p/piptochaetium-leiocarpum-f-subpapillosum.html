Piptochaetium leiocarpum f. subpapillosum

Piptochaetium leiocarpum f. subpapillosum, commonly known as the papillate grass, is a variant of the species characterized by its unique leaf surface features.

It is a perennial grass native to certain regions of Africa, typically found in grassland and savanna habitats. The leaves exhibit a papillate texture, with small, raised structures that give the foliage a distinctive appearance. This morphological trait may aid in water retention and resistance to herbivory.

The plant is often used in ecological studies due to its adaptation to arid and semi-arid environments.
