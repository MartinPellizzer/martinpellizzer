Pentopetia cotoneaster subsp. thouarsii

Pentopetia cotoneaster subsp.

thouarsii, commonly known as the Madagascar cotoneaster, is a shrub native to Madagascar. It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its dense, thorny growth habit.

The plant features small, white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by red, fleshy berries. Its leaves are opposite, elliptical, and have a glossy appearance.

It is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is valued for its ornamental qualities in horticulture.
