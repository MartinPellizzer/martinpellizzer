Pleioblastus truncatus

Pleioblastus truncatus, commonly known as the Japanese forest grass, is a species of bamboo native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright culms that are often covered with a whitish or grayish sheath. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically arranged in panicles, which are visible in late summer. Its leaves are linear, with a bluish-green hue and a distinctive truncate (cut-off) tip, giving it a unique appearance.

Pleioblastus truncatus is often used in ornamental horticulture for its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to various growing conditions.
