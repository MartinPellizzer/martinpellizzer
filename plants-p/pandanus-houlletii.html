Pandanus houlletii

Pandanus houlletii, commonly known as the Houllet's pandanus, is a flowering plant native to the Indo-Pacific region.

It is characterized by its tall, narrow leaves that can grow up to several meters in length. The plant produces inflorescences that bear both male and female flowers, typical of many pandanus species. Its fruits are fibrous and often used in traditional crafts.

The plant thrives in coastal areas and is adapted to saline and humid environments.
