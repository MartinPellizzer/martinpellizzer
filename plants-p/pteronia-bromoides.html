Pteronia bromoides

Pteronia bromoides, commonly known as the Cape buttercup, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit. The plant produces small, yellowish-orange flowers that are clustered in heads, giving it a daisy-like appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Pteronia bromoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in garden landscapes.
