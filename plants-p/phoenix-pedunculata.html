Phoenix pedunculata

Phoenix pedunculata, commonly known as the date palm, is a species native to the Arabian Peninsula and parts of the Middle East.

It is a tall, slender tree with a straight trunk and a crown of pinnate leaves. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences that develop into edible dates, which are a significant agricultural product. Its resilience to hot, arid climates makes it well adapted to desert environments.

The date palm has been cultivated for thousands of years and holds cultural and economic importance in many regions.
