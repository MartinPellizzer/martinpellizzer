Panicum amphibolum

Panicum amphibolum, commonly known as the water panicgrass, is a herbaceous plant native to wetland environments.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and feathery, panicle-like inflorescences that produce small, delicate flowers. The plant thrives in moist soils and is often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue that helps it blend into its aquatic surroundings.

Panicum amphibolum plays a vital role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various insect and bird species.
