Paspalum campestre

Paspalum campestre, commonly known as field panicgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the Americas.

It typically grows in open, disturbed areas such as roadsides, fields, and waste grounds. The plant has slender, spreading stems and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green color.

Paspalum campestre is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its hardy nature and ability to stabilize soil.
