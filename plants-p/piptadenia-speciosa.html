Piptadenia speciosa

Piptadenia speciosa, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or angel's trumpet, is a tropical tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its large, showy, funnel-shaped flowers that are typically pink or white. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets and produces long, slender pods that contain flat, brown seeds. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.

The tree thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
