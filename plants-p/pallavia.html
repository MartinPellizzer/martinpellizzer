Pallavia

Pallavia, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a tropical plant belonging to the Malvaceae family.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in a fine, velvety texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-yellow in color. Its seeds are encased in a fluffy, cotton-like material, which aids in wind dispersal.

Pallavia is often found in disturbed soils and is considered an invasive species in some regions.
