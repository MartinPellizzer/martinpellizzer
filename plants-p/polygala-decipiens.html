Polygala decipiens

Polygala decipiens, commonly known as the deceptive milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Polygalaceae family, which is characterized by its distinctive, often intricate flower structures. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. Its flowers are usually small and clustered, with a unique, spurred shape that resembles a bird's beak.

The species is also notable for its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions, making it a resilient and hardy plant.
