Penstemon bolanius

Penstemon bolanius, commonly known as the Bolanius beardtongue, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, tubular flowers that range in color from purple to pink. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. It produces small, elongated leaves that help reduce water loss.

Penstemon bolanius is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
