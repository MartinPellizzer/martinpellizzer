Pyrus malus var. niedzwetzkyana

Pyrus malus var. niedzwetzkyana, commonly known as the Niedzwetzkyana crabapple, is a variant of the apple tree native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, white to pink flowers that appear in early spring. The leaves are typically oval-shaped with a smooth margin and a glossy green surface. The fruit is small, usually less than 2 cm in diameter, and turns red or yellow when mature.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its floral display.
