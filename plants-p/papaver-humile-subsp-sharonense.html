Papaver humile subsp. sharonense

Papaver humile subsp.

sharonense, commonly known as Sharon poppy, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region, particularly Israel and Palestine. It is characterized by its delicate, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from pale pink to deep red.

The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and thrives in sunny environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are slender and often branched.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
