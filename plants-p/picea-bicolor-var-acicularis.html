Picea bicolor var. acicularis

Picea bicolor var. acicularis, commonly known as the white spruce, is a variant of the white spruce tree native to the northern hemisphere.

It is characterized by its slender, needle-like leaves that are typically white or grayish-white underneath, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant is evergreen, with a conical shape and can grow to heights of up to 30 meters. It thrives in cold climates and is often found in boreal forests and mountainous regions.

The cones are small and woody, containing seeds that are dispersed by wind.
