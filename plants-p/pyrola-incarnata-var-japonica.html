Pyrola incarnata var. japonica

Pyrola incarnata var. japonica, commonly known as the Japanese snowbell, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its low-growing habit and showy, bell-shaped white flowers. The plant typically thrives in shaded, moist environments such as forests and mountainous regions. Its leaves are usually oval-shaped with a glossy surface, and it produces small, reddish-brown berries in the fall.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
