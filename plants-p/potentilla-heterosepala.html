Potentilla heterosepala

Potentilla heterosepala, commonly known as the alpine potentilla, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its low-growing habit and produces clusters of small, yellow flowers. The plant has hirsute stems and leaves that are typically narrow and elliptical in shape. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, alpine environments.

Potentilla heterosepala is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and hardiness.
