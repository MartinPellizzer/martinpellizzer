Podocarpus lavoixii

Podocarpus lavoixii, commonly known as the Madagascar pine, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Podocarpaceae family and is characterized by its dense, needle-like leaves and cone-bearing structure. The plant typically grows in humid, tropical climates and is often found in mountainous regions. Its wood is valued for its durability and is used in construction and furniture-making.

Podocarpus lavoixii plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
