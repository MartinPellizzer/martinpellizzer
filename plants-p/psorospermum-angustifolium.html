Psorospermum angustifolium

Psorospermum angustifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaved psorospermum, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized shrub that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant features simple, narrow leaves with a glossy surface and produces small, fragrant flowers that are usually white or pale yellow. Its botanical characteristics include a woody stem, opposite leaf arrangement, and fruits that are capsule-like in structure.

It is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and undergrowth.
