Primula japonica

Primula japonica, commonly known as the Japanese primrose, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its upright, bushy growth habit and produces clusters of bell-shaped, often fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant typically blooms in early spring and is well-suited for cool climates. It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for garden borders and rock gardens.

Its foliage consists of broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a soft, downy texture.
