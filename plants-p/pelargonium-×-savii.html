Pelargonium × savii

Pelargonium × savii, commonly known as the African hybrid geranium, is a hybrid species within the Geraniaceae family.

It is native to South Africa and is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, five-petaled flowers. The leaves are typically lobed or serrated, and the plant produces bright red or pink flowers during the warmer months. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its vibrant blooms and adaptability to various growing conditions.

Pelargonium × savii prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for garden beds and containers.
