Panicum hirticaule

Panicum hirticaule, commonly known as hairy panicgrass, is a species of grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and hairy leaves, which give it its common name. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes and swamps. Its inflorescence consists of a loose panicle with spikelets that are often covered in fine hairs.

Panicum hirticaule plays a role in wetland ecosystems by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various insects and small animals.
