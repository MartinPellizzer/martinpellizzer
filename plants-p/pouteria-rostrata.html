Pouteria rostrata

Pouteria rostrata, commonly known as the "sabro" or "palo de rosa," is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its large, glossy, elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are followed by edible, sweet, and fleshy fruits. Its bark contains compounds used in traditional medicine for their anti-inflammatory properties.
