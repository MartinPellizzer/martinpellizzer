Piper otophyllum f. troyana

Piper otophyllum f. troyana, commonly known as the Troyana pepper plant, is a cultivar of the Piper otophyllum species, which belongs to the Piperaceae family.

It is characterized by its small, elongated fruits that are typically harvested when they turn red, indicating maturity. The plant is native to Central America and is often grown for its ornamental value as well as its peppery flavor. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

The leaves are usually glossy and dark green, adding visual appeal to gardens and indoor spaces.
