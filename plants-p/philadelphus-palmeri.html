Philadelphus palmeri

Philadelphus palmeri, commonly known as Palmer's mock orange, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters and has opposite, simple, and usually hairy leaves. Its bark is smooth and grayish, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

This species is often found in rocky soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.
