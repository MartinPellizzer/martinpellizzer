Prunus avium f. acuminatissima

Prunus avium f. acuminatissima, commonly known as the sharp-pointed sweet cherry, is a cultivar of the sweet cherry tree known for its distinctive, elongated, and pointed fruit shape.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its pale pink to white flowers that bloom in early spring. The leaves are typically oval-shaped with a smooth texture and a slightly serrated margin. The fruit is usually small to medium in size, with a firm flesh and a sweet, aromatic flavor.

This variety is often grown for its ornamental value as well as its edible fruit.
