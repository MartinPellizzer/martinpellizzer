Polypogon chaetotropis

Polypogon chaetotropis, commonly known as the hairy bluestem, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and hairy leaves that are often reddish in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, blue to purple flowers. It thrives in wetland environments and is often found in marshes and along the edges of ponds.

Its rhizomatous root system helps stabilize soil and makes it an important species in wetland restoration projects.
