Paspalum juergensii

Paspalum juergensii, commonly known as the silvergrass or Juan's grass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-colored foliage and tufted inflorescences, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks. It has a clumping growth habit and is adapted to a variety of environmental conditions.

Paspalum juergensii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping for its aesthetic and ecological benefits.
