Prunus × fruticans var. nadapensis

Prunus × fruticans var. nadapensis, commonly known as the Nadapensis cherry, is a compact, ornamental shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of the United States.

It features small, dark purple leaves and produces clusters of delicate, white flowers in early spring. The plant is valued for its dense growth habit and ability to thrive in partial shade to full sun. It has a shallow root system, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a foundation plant.

Its foliage remains attractive throughout the growing season, providing year-round visual interest.
