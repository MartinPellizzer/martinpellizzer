Planchonella thyrsoidea

Planchonella thyrsoidea, commonly known as the Christmas bell, is a tropical plant native to Madagascar.

It is a small evergreen tree or shrub that typically grows to a height of about 5 meters. The plant is characterized by its lance-shaped, glossy leaves and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom during the dry season. Its fruit is a spherical, yellowish drupe that splits open to reveal three seeds, often resembling a Christmas bell in shape.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
