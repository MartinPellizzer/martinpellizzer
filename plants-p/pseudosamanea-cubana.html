Pseudosamanea cubana

Pseudosamanea cubana, commonly known as the Cuban legume or Cuban samanea, is a tropical tree native to the Caribbean, particularly Cuba.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant has bipinnate leaves, which are divided into small leaflets, and produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink.

It is often found in moist, well-drained soils in lowland areas and is valued for its ornamental and ecological significance.
