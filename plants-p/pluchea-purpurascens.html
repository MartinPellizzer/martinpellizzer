Pluchea purpurascens

Pluchea purpurascens, commonly known as purple false dandelion, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and alternate leaves. The plant typically grows as an annual or perennial herb, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are small and yellow, forming a cluster at the top of the stem.

It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.
