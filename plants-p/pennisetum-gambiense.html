Pennisetum gambiense

Pennisetum gambiense, commonly known as giant porcupine grass, is a tall, perennial grass native to Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and its long, narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces dense clusters of spikelets at the top of its stalks, which are often adorned with soft, fluffy bristles. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it adaptable to various environmental conditions.

Pennisetum gambiense is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and its ability to provide habitat for small wildlife.
