Pulsatilla albana

Pulsatilla albana, commonly known as the white pasqueflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its showy, cup-shaped flowers that range from white to pale pink. The plant has a short, thick rhizome and hairy stems that bear alternate, palmately compound leaves. It thrives in well-drained, alkaline soils and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky habitats.

Pulsatilla albana is known for its early spring blooming period, making it a notable indicator species in high-altitude ecosystems.
