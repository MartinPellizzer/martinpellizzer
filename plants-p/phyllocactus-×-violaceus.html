Phyllocactus × violaceus

Phyllocactus × violaceus, commonly known as the purple-flowered cactus, is a hybrid plant that combines characteristics of its parent species.

It is native to South America and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. The plant features thick, fleshy stems that store water, a typical adaptation to arid environments. Its flowers are usually purple or violet, adding a striking visual appeal.

It thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.
