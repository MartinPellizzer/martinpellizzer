Pilosocereus densivillosus

Pilosocereus densivillosus, commonly known as the white night bloomer, is a cacti species native to southern Brazil.

It is characterized by its dense, white to pale green spines and cylindrical stems that can grow up to 2 meters in height. The plant produces large, showy white flowers that open at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as bats and moths. It is adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water, making it a hardy choice for xeriscaping.

Its unique morphology and nocturnal flowering make it a fascinating subject for both horticultural and botanical study.
