Pleioblastus kurokawae var. pilosissimus

Pleioblastus kurokawae var. pilosissimus, commonly known as the silky bamboo or black bamboo, is a variant of the pleioblastus genus native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and a unique, silky texture on its leaves, which give it its common name. This plant is a type of grass, belonging to the Poaceae family, and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It thrives in shady to partially sunny conditions and prefers well-drained soil.

The variety pilosissimus is distinguished by its exceptionally soft, hairy leaf surfaces, making it visually distinct from other pleioblastus species.
