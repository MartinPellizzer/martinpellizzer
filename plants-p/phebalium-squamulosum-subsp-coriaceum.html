Phebalium squamulosum subsp. coriaceum

Phebalium squamulosum subsp.

coriaceum, commonly known as the hard-leaved phebalium, is a shrub native to Western Australia. It features small, narrow leaves that are leathery and covered in a fine, scale-like texture.

The plant produces clusters of yellow flowers during the winter months, which are followed by small, elliptical fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and floral display.
