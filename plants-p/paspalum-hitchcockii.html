Paspalum hitchcockii

Paspalum hitchcockii, commonly known as Hitchcock's grass, is a perennial grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in wetland habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. The plant has slender, erect stems and small, spike-like inflorescences that produce tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often with a bluish-green hue.

Paspalum hitchcockii plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various wetland organisms.
