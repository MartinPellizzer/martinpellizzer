Pocockia elliptica

Pocockia elliptica, commonly known as the narrow-leaf pincushion cactus, is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It is a small, spineless cactus that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils. The plant produces clusters of small, yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. Its leaves are elliptical and often covered in a fine layer of white wax.

Pocockia elliptica is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water to survive.
