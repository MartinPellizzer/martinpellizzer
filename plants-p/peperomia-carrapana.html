Peperomia carrapana

Peperomia carrapana, commonly known as the Brazilian peperomia, is a compact, low-growing plant native to Brazil.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that are typically green with a waxy texture, adapted to store water in its arid native environment. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually green or reddish in color, arranged in clusters. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires bright, indirect light to flourish.

Peperomia carrapana is popular as a houseplant due to its attractive foliage and relatively low maintenance requirements.
