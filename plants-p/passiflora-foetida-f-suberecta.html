Passiflora foetida f. suberecta

Passiflora foetida f. suberecta, commonly known as the rough passionflower, is a variant of the passionvine characterized by its erect growth habit and distinctive foliage.

It features dark green, rough-textured leaves with prominent veins, and its flowers are showy, with a complex structure typical of the Passifloraceae family. The plant produces small, white or yellowish fruits that are edible but not widely cultivated for consumption.

It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in shaded, moist environments.
