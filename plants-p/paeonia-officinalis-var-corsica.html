Paeonia officinalis var. corsica

Paeonia officinalis var. corsica, commonly known as Corsican peony, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features showy, double or semi-double flowers in shades of pink, red, and white, typically blooming in late spring. The plant has large, palmate leaves that are deeply lobed and covered in a fine pubescence. Its root system consists of thick, fleshy rhizomes that store nutrients and help the plant survive harsh conditions.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and historical use in traditional medicine.
