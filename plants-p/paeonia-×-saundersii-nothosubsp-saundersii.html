Paeonia × saundersii nothosubsp.

saundersii, commonly known as the Saunders peony, is a hybrid plant originating from the cross between Paeonia suffruticosa and Paeonia lactiflora. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and large, showy flowers that range in color from white to deep red.

The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, producing intricate petals that are often double or semi-double in form. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.

The Saunders peony is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscape design.
