Pentaraphia hispida

Pentaraphia hispida, commonly known as the rough-stemmed bellflower, is a perennial herb native to South America.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its hairy, rough-textured stems and bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream sides. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine pubescence, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

The flowers, which range in color from white to pale blue, bloom in late spring to early summer.
