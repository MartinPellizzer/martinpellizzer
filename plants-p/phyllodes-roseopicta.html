Phyllodes roseopicta

Phyllodes roseopicta, commonly known as the pink-veined wattle, is a species of acacia native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its unique, flat, leaf-like structures called phyllodes, which are typically bright green with distinctive pink or red veins. The plant produces yellow flower spikes during the spring and summer months, adding a striking visual element to its appearance. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid environments.

The species is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is often used in landscaping due to its hardy nature and aesthetic appeal.
