Pappophorum nigricans var. polyphyllum

Pappophorum nigricans var. polyphyllum, commonly known as the black leaved grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dark green to blackish leaves and dense, tufted growth habit. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous bracteate flowers. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, often found in grassland and rocky habitats.

Its unique appearance and hardy nature make it a notable species in its native ecosystems.
