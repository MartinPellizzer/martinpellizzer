Pteris aquilina var. vera

Pteris aquilina var. vera, commonly known as the common water fern, is a species of fern native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its finely divided, feather-like fronds that are typically green and glossy. The plant prefers moist, shaded environments and is often found near water sources such as streams and ponds. Its rhizomes are creeping and help in spreading the plant across the ground.

It is valued for its ornamental appearance and is commonly used in horticulture and water gardens.
