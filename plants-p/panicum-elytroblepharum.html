Panicum elytroblepharum

Panicum elytroblepharum, commonly known as the silver hairgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-gray foliage and delicate, fluffy seed heads that appear in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its unique appearance makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and ecological restoration projects.

The species is also valued for its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for small insects and birds.
