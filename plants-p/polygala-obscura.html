Polygala obscura

Polygala obscura, commonly known as the narrow-leaved milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its slender, often hairy stems and small, delicate flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. Its flowers are usually pale green or yellowish, with a unique structure that resembles a small, closed pod.

Polygala obscura is often found in coastal or mountainous regions where it thrives in well-drained conditions.
