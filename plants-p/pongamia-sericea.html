Pongamia sericea

Pongamia sericea, commonly known as silky pongam, is a tropical leguminous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, often branched stem and compound leaves with small, silky-haired leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by elongated, flat pods containing several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in moist, tropical environments.

The tree is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it an important plant in agroforestry systems.
