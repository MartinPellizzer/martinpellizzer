Phalaris minor var. comosula

Phalaris minor var. comosula, commonly known as the tufted bottlebrush, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial grass with slender, tufted spikes of bright red or orange flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas and rocky habitats.

Its striking floral display makes it a popular ornamental plant in gardens.
