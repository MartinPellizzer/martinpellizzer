Pleurothallis lehmanniana

Pleurothallis lehmanniana, commonly known as the Lehmann's orchid, is a species of orchid native to South America, particularly Ecuador and Peru.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and delicate flowers. The plant typically produces small, greenish-white or yellowish flowers that are often fragrant. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is a sought-after species among orchid enthusiasts.
