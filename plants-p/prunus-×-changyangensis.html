Prunus × changyangensis

Prunus × changyangensis, commonly known as the Changyang cherry, is a hybrid species native to China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its small, sweet-tart fruits. The plant typically grows as a deciduous shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a serrated margin, while the flowers are white or pink and appear in early spring.

The fruit is a drupe, with a thin skin and a single seed, making it suitable for both ornamental and culinary use.
