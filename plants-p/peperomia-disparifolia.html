Peperomia disparifolia

Peperomia disparifolia, commonly known as the Variegated Peperomia, is a compact evergreen shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It features glossy, ovate leaves with a striking variegation of green, white, and cream, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or red, followed by round, fleshy berries. It thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, requiring moderate watering to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental foliage, it is often cultivated as a decorative plant in both home and office environments.
