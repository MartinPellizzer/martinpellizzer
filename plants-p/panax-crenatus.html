Panax crenatus

Panax crenatus, commonly known as the Japanese ginseng, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Araliaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, serrated leaves and yellowish flowers. The plant produces small, red berries that contain the seeds. Its root system is the primary part used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Panax crenatus is often cultivated for its medicinal properties and is valued in herbal remedies.
