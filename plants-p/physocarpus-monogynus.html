Physocarpus monogynus

Physocarpus monogynus, commonly known as the smooth hydrangea, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves that are typically opposite and have a smooth, glossy surface. The plant produces clusters of white or pink flowers in summer, which are followed by showy, rounded fruit heads. It thrives in partial to full shade and prefers moist, well-drained soil.

This species is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
