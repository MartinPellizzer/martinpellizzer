Primulina yangchunensis

Primulina yangchunensis, commonly known as the Yangchun bellflower, is a flowering plant native to China.

It belongs to the Gesneriaceae family and is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as mountainous regions, where it thrives in cool, humid conditions. Its foliage consists of glossy, ovate leaves that are often covered in a fine, fuzzy texture.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated as a houseplant or in botanical gardens.
