Pyrus × mitschurinii

Pyrus × mitschurinii, commonly known as the Siberian whitebeam, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Pyrus ussuriensis and Pyrus sibirica.

It is a deciduous tree or shrub characterized by its small, white or pale green leaves and clusters of yellowish-green flowers. The plant is native to eastern Asia and is known for its cold hardiness, making it suitable for cultivation in temperate climates. Its fruit, a small, tart apple-like berry, is edible but not typically cultivated for commercial use.

Pyrus × mitschurinii is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and adaptability to various soil conditions.
