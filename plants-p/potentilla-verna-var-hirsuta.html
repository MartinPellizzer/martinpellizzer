Potentilla verna var. hirsuta

Potentilla verna var. hirsuta, commonly known as hairy willowherb, is a low-growing perennial plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It features wiry, branched stems covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers in clusters during the early spring to summer months. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and along stream banks.
