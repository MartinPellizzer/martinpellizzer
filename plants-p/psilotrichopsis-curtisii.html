Psilotrichopsis curtisii

Psilotrichopsis curtisii, commonly known as the dwarf clubmoss, is a small, evergreen plant belonging to the family Psilotaceae.

It is characterized by its simple, branching structure and the presence of microphylls, which are small, undivided leaves. This species is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, particularly in countries like Thailand and Malaysia. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest undergrowth.

Due to its unique morphology and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant plant.
