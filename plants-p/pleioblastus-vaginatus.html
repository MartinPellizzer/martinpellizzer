Pleioblastus vaginatus

Pleioblastus vaginatus, commonly known as the Japanese silvergrass, is a perennial grass native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, arching stems and narrow, bluish-green leaves that give it a distinctive silvery appearance. This plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional Japanese gardens. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it adaptable to a variety of garden settings.

Pleioblastus vaginatus is also valued for its ability to form dense clumps, providing erosion control and habitat for small wildlife.
