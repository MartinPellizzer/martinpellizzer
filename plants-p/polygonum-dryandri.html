Polygonum dryandri

Polygonum dryandri, commonly known as the dryandri knotweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, which includes other familiar plants like buckwheat and sorrel. The plant is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, clustered greenish-white flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped with a rough, textured surface, and the plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

Polygonum dryandri is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is sometimes used in traditional Chinese medicine.
