Padus micrantha

Padus micrantha, commonly known as the small-fruited bird cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and clusters of small, white flowers. The plant produces small, red drupes that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or streamside habitats.

Padus micrantha is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in natural landscapes.
