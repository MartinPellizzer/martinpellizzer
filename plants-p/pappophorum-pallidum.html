Pappophorum pallidum

Pappophorum pallidum, commonly known as the pale bristlegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in open habitats such as meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils. The plant has slender, upright stems that are often hairy, and its leaves are narrow with a rough texture. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets that are loosely arranged, and the flowers are wind-pollinated.

Pappophorum pallidum is valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and maintaining soil stability.
