Peekelia

Peekelia, commonly known as the bluebell flower, is a genus of flowering plants in the nightshade family, Solanaceae.

It is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers that bloom in spring. The plants are native to the western United States, particularly California, and thrive in sunny, well-drained soils. Peekelia species are often found in rocky habitats and are adapted to Mediterranean climates.

Their ornamental value makes them popular in gardens and botanical collections.
