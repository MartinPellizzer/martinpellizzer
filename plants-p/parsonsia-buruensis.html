Parsonsia buruensis

Parsonsia buruensis, commonly known as the Buru flame tree, is a flowering plant native to the island of Buru in Indonesia.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its striking orange-red flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Its foliage consists of opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy surface.

Parsonsia buruensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in tropical gardens.
