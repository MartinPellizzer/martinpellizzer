Passiflora wilsonii

Passiflora wilsonii, commonly known as Wilson's passionflower, is a climbing vine native to Central America.

It is characterized by its striking, intricate flowers that feature five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments surrounding a central cluster of stamens. The plant produces blue or purple berries that are edible and contain numerous seeds. It thrives in humid, tropical environments and is often found in shaded, forested areas.

Its unique floral structure and ornamental value make it a popular species among horticulturists and botanists.
