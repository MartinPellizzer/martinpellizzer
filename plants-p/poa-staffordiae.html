Poa staffordiae

Poa staffordiae, commonly known as Stafford's bluegrass, is a species of grass native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its fine, blue-green foliage and tufted growth habit. The plant typically thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological role, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and restoration projects.
