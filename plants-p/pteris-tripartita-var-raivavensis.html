Pteris tripartita var. raivavensis

Pteris tripartita var. raivavensis, commonly known as the Raivavae water fern, is a rare and endemic fern species native to the island of Raivavae in the French Polynesian Society.

It is characterized by its delicate, trifoliate fronds that are divided into three parts, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically near streams or in humid forest understories. Its rhizomes are often covered in a dense, dark brown coating, which helps in water retention.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species, highlighting the importance of conservation efforts in its native region.
