Potentilla chinensis subsp. trigonodonta

Potentilla chinensis subsp.

trigonodonta, commonly known as the Chinese cinquefoil, is a shrub native to regions of China and Korea. It is characterized by its narrow, trifoliate leaves and clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or streamside habitats. Its flowering period attracts pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and hardiness in temperate climates.
