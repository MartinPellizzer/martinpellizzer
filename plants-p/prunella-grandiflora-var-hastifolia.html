Prunella grandiflora var. hastifolia

Prunella grandiflora var. hastifolia, commonly known as broad-leaved selfheal, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and spikes of blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist meadows, woodlands, and along stream banks. It produces small, four-lobed flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The leaves are often used in traditional herbal medicine for their anti-inflammatory and healing properties.
