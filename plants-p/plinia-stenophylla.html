Plinia stenophylla

Plinia stenophylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaf myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to South America, particularly found in regions of Brazil, Paraguay, and Argentina. The plant is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and small, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in clusters. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in shaded, moist environments.

Plinia stenophylla is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
