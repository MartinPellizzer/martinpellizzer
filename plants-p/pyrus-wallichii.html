Pyrus wallichii

Pyrus wallichii, commonly known as the Himalayan pear, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, which are followed by edible, sweet-tart fruits resembling pears. It thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil and is often found at elevations between 1,500 and 3,000 meters.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been cultivated for its fruit in certain regions of Asia.
