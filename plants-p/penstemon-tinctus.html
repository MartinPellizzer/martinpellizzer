Penstemon tinctus

Penstemon tinctus, commonly known as the tincture penstemon, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a perennial species belonging to the Plantaginaceae family, characterized by its erect, branching stems and showy, tubular flowers. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, producing vibrant purple or red flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, and it thrives in well-drained soils in full sun.

Penstemon tinctus is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
