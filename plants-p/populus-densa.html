Populus densa

Populus densa, commonly known as the dense poplar, is a deciduous tree native to parts of Asia and North America.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often slightly hairy on the underside. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by fluffy, seed-bearing structures that aid in wind pollination. Populus densa typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and can thrive in a variety of light conditions.

Its rapid growth rate makes it a useful species for reforestation and erosion control.
