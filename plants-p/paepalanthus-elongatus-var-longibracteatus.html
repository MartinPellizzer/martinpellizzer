Paepalanthus elongatus var. longibracteatus

Paepalanthus elongatus var. longibracteatus, commonly known as the long-bract puya, is a striking plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a member of the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its tall, rosette-like structure with long, narrow leaves. The plant produces striking inflorescences with numerous small, showy flowers that bloom in a vibrant array of colors, typically red or orange. Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
