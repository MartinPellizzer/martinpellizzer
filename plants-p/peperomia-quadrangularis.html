Peperomia quadrangularis

Peperomia quadrangularis, commonly known as the Square-stemmed Peperomia, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive square-shaped stems and thick, waxy leaves that are typically green with a slight reddish or purple hue on the underside. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters, which are usually green or reddish in color. It thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Peperomia quadrangularis is relatively low-maintenance and resistant to pests, making it an ideal plant for beginners.
