Polygonatum franchetii

Polygonatum franchetii, commonly known as the Chinese Solomon's Seal, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Asparagaceae family and is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit. The plant produces clusters of small, white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped, arranged in a rosette, and often have a waxy texture.

Polygonatum franchetii is valued in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits and is also cultivated for ornamental purposes.
