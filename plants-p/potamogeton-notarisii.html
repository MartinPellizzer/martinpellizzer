Potamogeton notarisii

Potamogeton notarisii, commonly known as the notarisii pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in freshwater environments such as lakes, ponds, and slow-moving rivers. The plant has long, narrow leaves that are arranged in whorls around the stem, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often submerged. It plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.

Its presence can indicate good water quality due to its sensitivity to pollution.
