Paspalum lenticulare f. intumescens

Paspalum lenticulare f. intumescens, commonly known as the inflated paspalum, is a variant of the paspalum grass native to South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, inflated spikelets that give the plant a unique appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, tropical environments and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue.

This ornamental grass is valued for its aesthetic appeal and ability to thrive in humid conditions.
