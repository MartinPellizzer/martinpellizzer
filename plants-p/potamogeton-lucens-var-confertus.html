Potamogeton lucens var. confertus

Potamogeton lucens var. confertus, commonly known as the dense spike pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in North America.

It is characterized by its long, flexible stems and dense clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. The leaves are narrow and ribbon-like, typically arranged in whorls around the stem. This variety is often found in slow-moving waters such as lakes, ponds, and marshes.

It plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
