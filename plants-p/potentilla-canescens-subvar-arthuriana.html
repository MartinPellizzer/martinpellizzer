Potentilla canescens subvar. arthuriana

Potentilla canescens subvar. arthuriana, commonly known as Arthur's cinquefoil, is a low-growing perennial shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, typically from June to July. The plant has wiry, hairy stems and compound leaves with five oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in native plant gardens.
