Prunus padus subsp. petraea

Prunus padus subsp.

petraea, commonly known as the rock cherry, is a subspecies of the common bird cherry. It is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Europe and western Asia.

The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils, often in mountainous regions. Its leaves are ovate with serrated edges, and it produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring.

The fruit is a dark red drupe that ripens in late summer, attracting various wildlife species.
