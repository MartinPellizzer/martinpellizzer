Penstemon eatonii

Penstemon eatonii, commonly known as Eaton's beardtongue, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its upright stems and clusters of tubular, violet-blue flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant produces showy, spurred flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It prefers well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Its foliage consists of narrow, gray-green leaves that are hairy and aromatic.
