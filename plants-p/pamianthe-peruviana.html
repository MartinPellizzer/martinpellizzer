Pamianthe peruviana

Pamianthe peruviana, commonly known as the Andean mountain bean, is a flowering plant native to the highlands of South America.

It belongs to the Leguminosae family and is characterized by its climbing habit and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by elongated, bean-like pods containing edible seeds. It is adapted to grow in cool, high-altitude environments with well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value and potential nutritional benefits, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and experimental agriculture.
