Platystele misasiana

Platystele misasiana, commonly known as the Misasian ghost orchid, is a rare and enigmatic member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is a small, epiphytic plant that thrives in the shaded, humid understory of tropical forests. The plant produces delicate, white flowers with a unique, ghostly appearance, which contribute to its common name. Its roots are specialized for absorbing moisture and nutrients from the air and decaying organic matter.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered endangered and is the subject of conservation efforts.
