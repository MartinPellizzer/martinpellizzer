Pyrus communis domestica

Pyrus communis domestica, commonly known as the European pear.

It is a deciduous tree native to Europe and western Asia, characterized by its broad, lobed leaves and fragrant white flowers. The plant produces pomes, which are fleshy fruits with a single central core. Its fruit is widely cultivated for its sweet, juicy flesh and is used in both fresh consumption and cooking.

The tree is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical significance in horticulture.
