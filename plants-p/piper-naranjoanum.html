Piper naranjoanum

Piper naranjoanum, commonly known as the yellow pepper plant, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its small, yellow fruits that resemble peppers. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a mild, peppery aroma.

The fruit is often used in traditional medicine and as a culinary spice due to its unique flavor profile.
