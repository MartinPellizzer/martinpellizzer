Persea krugii

Persea krugii, commonly known as the Mexican persimmon, is a small evergreen tree native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its dark green, lance-shaped leaves and fragrant flowers. The tree produces small, orange-yellow fruits that are edible when fully ripe. The fruit has a sweet, honey-like flavor and is often consumed fresh or used in culinary applications.

It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is valued for both its ornamental and edible qualities.
