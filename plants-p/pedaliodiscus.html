Pedaliodiscus

Pedaliodiscus, commonly known as the silverleaf orchid, is a genus of flowering plants in the orchid family, Orchidaceae.

It is characterized by its unique, often silver or grayish foliage that reflects light, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plants typically grow as epiphytes or lithophytes, attaching themselves to trees or rocks in their natural habitat. Pedaliodiscus species produce intricate flowers with a variety of colors, often featuring a complex structure with specialized petals and sepals.
