Pandanus minda

Pandanus minda, commonly known as the Mindanao pandanus, is a tropical plant native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Pandanaceae family and is characterized by its large, sword-shaped leaves that are often used in local crafts and cuisine. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and lowland forests, thriving in humid, tropical climates. Its root system is fibrous and helps it anchor in sandy or loamy soils.

The flowers, which are small and clustered, are usually borne on spikes and can produce edible fruits under certain conditions.
