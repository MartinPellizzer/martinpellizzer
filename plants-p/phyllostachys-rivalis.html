Phyllostachys rivalis

Phyllostachys rivalis, commonly known as the yellow bamboo, is a fast-growing grass species native to China.

It is characterized by its slender, often yellowish-green stems that can reach heights of up to 10 meters. The plant produces small, inconspicuous green flowers in summer, followed by distinctive, elongated seed pods. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value.

Phyllostachys rivalis is also valued for its role in erosion control and as a source of sustainable materials.
