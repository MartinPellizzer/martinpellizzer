Protea concinna

Protea concinna, commonly known as the silver protea, is a striking South African plant belonging to the Proteaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescence composed of numerous small, white to cream-colored flowers surrounded by a dense cluster of bracts. The plant is native to the Western Cape region and thrives in well-drained, acidic soils. Its flowers are pollinated primarily by birds, particularly sunbirds, due to their nectar-rich structure.

Protea concinna is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique and elegant appearance.
