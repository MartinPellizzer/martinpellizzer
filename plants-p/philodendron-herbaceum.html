Philodendron herbaceum

Philodendron herbaceum, commonly known as the creeping philodendron, is a tropical plant native to the rainforests of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, glossy leaves that are typically a deep green with a slightly variegated pattern. The plant has a trailing growth habit, making it ideal for hanging baskets or as a cascading indoor plant. Its stems are slender and can produce aerial roots, which help it climb or anchor itself to surfaces.

Philodendron herbaceum thrives in bright, indirect light and requires consistent moisture and humidity to flourish.
