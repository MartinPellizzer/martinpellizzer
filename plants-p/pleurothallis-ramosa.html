Pleurothallis ramosa

Pleurothallis ramosa, commonly known as the branched pleurothallis, is a species of orchid native to the cloud forests of Central and South America.

It is a small, epiphytic plant that thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant features delicate, linear leaves and produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink. Its intricate floral structure is characteristic of the Pleurothallis genus, which is known for its diverse and often showy blooms.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in the wild.
