Penaea cneorum subsp. ruscifolia

Penaea cneorum subsp.

ruscifolia, commonly known as the Mediterranean spurge, is a perennial shrub native to southern Europe and western Asia. It features woody stems and alternate, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a fine layer of white pubescence.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by orange-red berries. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
