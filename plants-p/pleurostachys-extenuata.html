Pleurostachys extenuata

Pleurostachys extenuata, commonly known as the slender feathergrass, is a perennial grass native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its fine, wiry leaves and delicate, feathery inflorescence that gives it a graceful appearance. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. It has small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in panicles, and it produces lightweight seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in xeriscaped gardens.
