Polygala sibirica subsp. sibirica

Polygala sibirica subsp.

sibirica, commonly known as Siberian milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the temperate regions of Asia, including parts of Russia and China. It typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is often found in steppe and forest-steppe ecosystems.

The plant features slender, erect stems with pinnately compound leaves and produces small, yellowish or greenish flowers in racemes. Its root system is fibrous and can be used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in arid environments.
