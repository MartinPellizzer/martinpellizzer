Piper anisopleurum

Piper anisopleurum, commonly known as the broad-leaved pepper or Brazilian pepper tree, is a member of the Piperaceae family.

It is a tropical shrub or small tree native to South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves and small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in spikes. Its fruits are berries that turn red when mature, containing several seeds.

Piper anisopleurum is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
