Pterocephalus marcescens

Pterocephalus marcescens, commonly known as the desert flax, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of Central Asia and the Middle East.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces distinctive, spiny, yellowish flowers with a unique, lantern-like appearance. The plant has a fibrous root system that allows it to thrive in dry, sandy soils. Its leaves are narrow and often covered in a waxy coating to reduce water loss.

Pterocephalus marcescens is adapted to extreme environmental conditions, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
