Pleurothallis nanella

Pleurothallis nanella, commonly known as the miniature orchid, is a small epiphytic orchid native to the cloud forests of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, clustered inflorescences and small, intricate flowers that often display a range of colors, including white, pink, and purple. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining substrate to prevent root rot. Its unique morphology and striking blooms make it a popular choice among orchid enthusiasts.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is often cultivated in controlled environments to ensure its survival.
