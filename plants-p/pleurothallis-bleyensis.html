Pleurothallis bleyensis

Pleurothallis bleyensis, commonly known as the Bley's pleurothallis, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to the tropical regions of South America.

This small epiphytic orchid typically grows on trees in humid, shaded environments. It features delicate, narrow leaves and produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink. The plant is noted for its intricate floral structure and is often found in cloud forests.

Due to its habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and less commonly cultivated species.
