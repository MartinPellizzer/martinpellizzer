Polystichum rarum

Polystichum rarum, commonly known as the black pine fern, is a species of fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dark, glossy fronds that are often used in traditional medicine and as ornamental plants. The plant has a robust rhizome system that allows it to thrive in shaded, moist environments. Its spores are produced on the underside of its fronds, contributing to its reproductive strategy.

Polystichum rarum is valued for its aesthetic appeal and has cultural significance in various regions.
