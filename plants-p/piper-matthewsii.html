Piper matthewsii

Piper matthewsii, commonly known as the Matthews' pepper, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, particularly found in parts of Indonesia and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its climbing habit, with aerial roots that allow it to ascend trees in its natural habitat. Its small, greenish-white flowers are followed by small, round berries that contain the aromatic seeds used in traditional medicine.

The plant's botanical significance lies in its unique morphology and potential medicinal properties.
