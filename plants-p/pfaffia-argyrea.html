Pfaffia argyrea

Pfaffia argyrea, commonly known as golden root, is a perennial herb native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its stout, branching stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its roots are the primary part used, known for their golden color and purported medicinal properties.

Pfaffia argyrea is often cultivated for its potential adaptogenic and antioxidant benefits.
