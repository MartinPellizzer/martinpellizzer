Passiflora kwangsiensis

Passiflora kwangsiensis, commonly known as the Guangxi passionflower, is a flowering plant native to China.

It belongs to the Passifloraceae family and is characterized by its striking, complex flowers with five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments. The plant produces edible berries that are often used in traditional medicine and culinary applications. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, shaded environments.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for horticultural cultivation.
