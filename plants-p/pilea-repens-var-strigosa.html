Pilea repens var. strigosa

Pilea repens var. strigosa, commonly known as the Chinese money plant or dollar plant, is a compact, trailing herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It features heart-shaped leaves that are slightly hairy, giving it a distinctive, fuzzy appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers, though it is primarily grown for its ornamental foliage. It thrives in indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Its ability to spread via runners makes it easy to propagate and maintain in various settings.
