Pelargonium rugosum

Pelargonium rugosum, commonly known as the roughleaf geranium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features upright stems with rough, hairy leaves that are often lobed or toothed. The plant produces clusters of small, pink to red flowers during the summer months. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

Pelargonium rugosum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
