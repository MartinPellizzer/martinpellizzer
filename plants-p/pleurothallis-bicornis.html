Pleurothallis bicornis

Pleurothallis bicornis, commonly known as the two-horned orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, small flowers that often display a striking combination of colors. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are usually narrow and dark green, adapted to conserve moisture.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated by enthusiasts, though it remains relatively obscure in the horticultural trade.
