Paspalum montevidense

Paspalum montevidense, commonly known as the Uruguay grass, is a perennial grass native to South America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions.

Paspalum montevidense is often found in disturbed areas and is used in some regions for forage or erosion control.
