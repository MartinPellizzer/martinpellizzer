Pavonia malvacea

Pavonia malvacea, commonly known as the scarlet pimpernel, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States and parts of South America.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes hibiscus and cotton. The plant is characterized by its small, showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in clusters, often appearing in the spring and summer. Its leaves are ovate and have a slightly hairy texture, and the plant typically grows as an annual or biennial.

Pavonia malvacea is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in full sun to partial shade.
