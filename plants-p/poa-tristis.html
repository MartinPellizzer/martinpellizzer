Poa tristis

Poa tristis, commonly known as the sad grass, is a species of grass native to the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth and bluish-green foliage. The plant typically grows in moist, temperate environments such as meadows and woodlands. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of spikelets, which are often tinged with purple or brown.

Poa tristis is a perennial species that plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
