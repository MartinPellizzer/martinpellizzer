Potamogeton coloratus subsp. subflavus

Potamogeton coloratus subsp.

subflavus, commonly known as the yellow-flowered pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to North America. It typically grows in freshwater environments such as lakes, ponds, and slow-moving rivers.

The plant has long, slender stems that are often branched and bear small, bright yellow flowers. Its leaves are narrow and ribbon-like, with a smooth surface and a slight sheen.

It plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
