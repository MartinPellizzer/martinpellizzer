Pericampylus macrophyllus

Pericampylus macrophyllus, commonly known as the giant fan palm, is a tropical plant characterized by its large, fan-shaped leaves that can reach several meters in diameter.

It belongs to the Arecaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia, particularly Indonesia. The plant has a slender trunk that grows slowly and is often covered in a fibrous, spongy material. Its leaves are arranged in a rosette pattern, providing shade and making it a popular ornamental plant in warm climates.

The fruit of Pericampylus macrophyllus is small and edible, containing a single seed surrounded by a fleshy pulp.
