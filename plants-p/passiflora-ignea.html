Passiflora ignea

Passiflora ignea, commonly known as the red passionflower, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking red bracts that resemble petals, which are actually modified leaves. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers with a unique structure featuring a crown of filaments. Its fruit is a red, fleshy berry that contains inedible seeds.

Passiflora ignea is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in warm climates.
