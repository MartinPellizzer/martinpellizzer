Paspalum elliottii

Paspalum elliottii, commonly known as Elliott's grass, is a perennial grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, wiry stems that often form dense mats. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with pale green to golden flowers, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in wetland environments, including marshes and along the edges of water bodies.

Paspalum elliottii is often found in disturbed soils and is adapted to tolerate periodic flooding.
