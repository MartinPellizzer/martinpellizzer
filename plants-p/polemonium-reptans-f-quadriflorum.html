Polemonium reptans f. quadriflorum

Polemonium reptans f. quadriflorum, commonly known as the four-flowered Jacob's ladder, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Polemoniaceae family and is characterized by its cascading clusters of blue to purple flowers. The plant features slender, branching stems that produce numerous small, showy blooms from late spring to early summer. Its foliage consists of opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in woodland gardens with partial shade.
