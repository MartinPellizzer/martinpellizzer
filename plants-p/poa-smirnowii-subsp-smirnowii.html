Poa smirnowii subsp. smirnowii

Poa smirnowii subsp.

smirnowii, commonly known as Smirnov's bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It typically grows in alpine and subalpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure.

The plant has slender, tufted growth habit with fine, blue-green leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of hairs. Its inflorescence consists of a compact panicle, bearing small, spikelet-like flowers that are wind-pollinated.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
