Polygonum densiflorum f. imberbe

Polygonum densiflorum f. imberbe, commonly known as the silky knotweed, is a variant of the species Polygonum densiflorum, characterized by its dense, fluffy inflorescences.

It is a herbaceous perennial native to parts of Asia, particularly China, and thrives in moist, shaded environments. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a soft, hairy texture, contributing to its distinctive appearance. Its flowers are small and clustered, often appearing in shades of pink or red, adding ornamental value.

Due to its adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens but can become invasive in certain regions.
