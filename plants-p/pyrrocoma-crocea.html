Pyrrocoma crocea

Pyrrocoma crocea, commonly known as the golden bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, typically blooming in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or alpine environments.

Pyrrocoma crocea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is occasionally cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
