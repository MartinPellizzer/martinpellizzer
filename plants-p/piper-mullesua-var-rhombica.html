Piper mullesua var. rhombica

Piper mullesua var. rhombica, commonly known as the rhombic pepper plant, is a tropical vine native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and small, clustered flowers that transition from green to red as they mature. The plant produces small, peppery fruits that are used in traditional medicine and cuisine. It thrives in humid, warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species in both horticultural and pharmacological studies.
