Poa paucifolia

Poa paucifolia, commonly known as the sparse-leaved bentgrass, is a low-growing perennial grass native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Due to its unique morphology and habitat preferences, it is of interest to botanists and ecologists studying plant adaptation to high-altitude conditions.
