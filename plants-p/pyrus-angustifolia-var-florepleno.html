Pyrus angustifolia var. florepleno

Pyrus angustifolia var. florepleno, commonly known as the double-flowered pear, is a cultivar of the common pear tree.

It is characterized by its ornamental value, featuring abundant, double flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows as a deciduous tree or shrub, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are ovate with serrated edges, and it produces small, edible fruits that are often used in jams and preserves.

This variety is favored in gardens for its showy floral display and adaptability to various soil types.
