Polygonum glaucochloros

Polygonum glaucochloros, commonly known as the blue-green knotweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue-green foliage, which gives it its common name. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread readily, making it both a hardy ornamental and a potential invasive species in certain regions.
