Peperomia chrysocarpa

Peperomia chrysocarpa, commonly known as the golden fruit peperomia, is a compact, low-maintenance plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It features thick, waxy leaves that are typically dark green with a glossy texture, and it produces small, showy yellow-orange fruits that resemble tiny berries. The plant is a member of the Piperaceae family and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it suitable for both indoor and outdoor gardens.

Its unique appearance and ease of care have made it a popular choice among plant enthusiasts.
