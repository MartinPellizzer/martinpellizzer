Potentilla verna subsp. alpestris

Potentilla verna subsp.

alpestris, commonly known as the alpine cinquefoil, is a low-growing shrub native to alpine regions of Europe. It features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, typically from May to June.

The plant has slender, woody stems and trifoliate leaves, which are usually covered in a fine layer of white hairs. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in mountain meadows and slopes.

This subspecies is adapted to cold climates and is an important part of alpine ecosystems.
