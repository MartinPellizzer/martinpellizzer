Paliurus hirsutus var. trichocarpus

Paliurus hirsutus var. trichocarpus, commonly known as the hairy thornapple, is a shrub or small tree native to parts of Asia and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its thorny stems, trifoliate leaves, and showy yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant produces small, orange-red fruits that are edible but often have a bitter taste. It thrives in arid and semi-arid climates, making it a hardy species adapted to harsh environmental conditions.

Its botanical features make it a notable plant in ecological and horticultural contexts.
