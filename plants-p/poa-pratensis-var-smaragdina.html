Poa pratensis var. smaragdina

Poa pratensis var. smaragdina, commonly known as the Emerald Meadowgrass, is a variant of the common meadowgrass.

It is characterized by its fine, dark green foliage and dense growth habit, making it a popular choice for ornamental and turfgrass applications. The plant typically thrives in temperate climates and prefers well-drained soils. It produces slender, spike-like inflorescences that bloom in the spring, adding a delicate floral display.

Due to its hardiness and aesthetic appeal, it is often used in landscaping and conservation efforts.
