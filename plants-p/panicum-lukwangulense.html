Panicum lukwangulense

Panicum lukwangulense, commonly known as the Lukwangulene grass, is a species native to the grasslands of southern Africa.

It is a perennial grass characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, erect stems. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous narrow, awn-tipped florets. Its leaves are typically narrow and linear, with a bluish-green coloration.

Panicum lukwangulense is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient component of local ecosystems.
