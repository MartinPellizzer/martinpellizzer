Phyllostachys aureosulcata f. sulphurea

Phyllostachys aureosulcata f. sulphurea, commonly known as the golden bamboo or yellow bamboo, is a variant of the bamboo species native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its striking golden-yellow stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that turn a vibrant yellow in the fall. This ornamental bamboo is prized for its rapid growth and ability to form dense clumps, making it suitable for landscaping and garden use. The plant thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Its unique coloration and versatility make it a popular choice among gardeners and horticulturists.
