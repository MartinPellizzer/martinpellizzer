Plagiochloa oblitera

Plagiochloa oblitera, commonly known as the narrowleaf hairgrass, is a perennial grass species native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and is often found in sandy or rocky soils. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showing resilience to drought conditions.

Plagiochloa oblitera plays a role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for small invertebrates in its native range.
