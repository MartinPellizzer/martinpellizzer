Phlegmariurus pungentifolius

Phlegmariurus pungentifolius, commonly known as the stinking marsh moss, is a species of liverwort belonging to the family Pottiaceae.

It is characterized by its dark green, leathery leaves that are often covered in a waxy layer, which helps it retain moisture in its wetland habitat. Its pungent odor, which gives it its common name, is likely a defense mechanism against herbivores.
