Polycarena baurii

Polycarena baurii, commonly known as the Baur's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, where it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets. It is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support biodiversity.

Polycarena baurii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in wetland ecosystems.
