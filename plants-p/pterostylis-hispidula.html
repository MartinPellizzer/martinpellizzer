Pterostylis hispidula

Pterostylis hispidula, commonly known as the hairy-leafed greenhood, is a terrestrial orchid native to southeastern Australia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as rainforests and understoreys. The plant features a single, upright stem with a few narrow, hairy leaves. Its distinctive greenish-yellow floral spike produces a single, hooded flower that is characteristic of the orchid genus Pterostylis.

The species is known for its specialized pollination mechanism involving insects.
