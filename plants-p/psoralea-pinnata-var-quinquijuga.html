Psoralea pinnata var. quinquijuga

Psoralea pinnata var. quinquijuga, commonly known as the five-winged psoralea, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive pinnately compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in dense spikes, which are often clustered at the ends of the stems. Its seeds are enclosed in hairy pods and are adapted for dispersal by wind.

The species is notable for its ecological role in stabilizing soils in arid and semi-arid regions.
