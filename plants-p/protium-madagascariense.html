Protium madagascariense

Protium madagascariense, commonly known as the Madagascar chestnut, is a member of the Burseraceae family.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Madagascar, characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and elliptical leaves. Its leaves are alternate and have a leathery texture, while the tree is known for its aromatic resin.

Protium madagascariense plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
