Phragmites communis var. vulgaris

Phragmites communis var. vulgaris, commonly known as common reed, is a tall, perennial grass that grows in wetland environments.

It is characterized by its hollow, jointed stems and feathery, grayish-green leaves. This plant is highly adaptable and can thrive in a variety of aquatic and semi-aquatic habitats. It plays an important role in stabilizing shorelines and providing habitat for various wildlife species.

However, it can also become invasive in certain regions, outcompeting native vegetation.
