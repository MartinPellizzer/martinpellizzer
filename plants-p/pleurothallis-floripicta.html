Pleurothallis floripicta

Pleurothallis floripicta, commonly known as the pink-streaked orchid, is a species native to the cloud forests of Central and South America.

It is a small, epiphytic orchid that thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant features delicate, lance-shaped leaves and produces fragrant, showy flowers with pink and white streaks. Its flowers are typically clustered in inflorescences and bloom during the warmer months.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated by enthusiasts for its unique and colorful appearance.
