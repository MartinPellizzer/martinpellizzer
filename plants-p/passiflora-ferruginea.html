Passiflora ferruginea

Passiflora ferruginea, commonly known as the rusty passionflower, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, intricate flowers that feature a mix of blue, purple, and white hues. The plant produces edible berries that are often used in local cuisines. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped with a rough, hairy texture.

Passiflora ferruginea is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional medicinal uses in some regions.
