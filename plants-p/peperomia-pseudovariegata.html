Peperomia pseudovariegata

Peperomia pseudovariegata, commonly known as the Variegated Peperomia, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It features glossy, oval-shaped leaves with striking variegation of white, cream, and green, making it a visually appealing houseplant. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters, typically green or red, which are followed by dark purple berries. It thrives in bright, indirect light and prefers well-draining soil with moderate watering.

Peperomia pseudovariegata is known for its low maintenance and ability to thrive in a variety of indoor conditions.
