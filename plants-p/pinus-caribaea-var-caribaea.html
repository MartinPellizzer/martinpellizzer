Pinus caribaea var. caribaea

Pinus caribaea var. caribaea, commonly known as the Cuban pine, is a species of conifer native to the Caribbean.

It is characterized by its tall, straight trunk and needle-like leaves that grow in bundles of five. The tree produces cones that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with well-drained soils.

This species is often valued for its timber and plays an important role in the ecosystems of its native range.
