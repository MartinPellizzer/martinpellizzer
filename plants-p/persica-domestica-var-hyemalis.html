Persica domestica var. hyemalis

Persica domestica var. hyemalis, commonly known as the winter peach.

This variety is a cold-hardy cultivar of the peach tree, adapted to withstand colder temperatures than typical peaches. It produces small, round fruits with a thin skin and a sweet, tart flavor. The plant is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and thorny branches.

It is often grown in temperate climates where it can fruit during the early spring.
