Phacelia pinnata var. robusta

Phacelia pinnata var. robusta, commonly known as the robust phacelia, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It is characterized by its bipinnately compound leaves, which are often hairy and have a distinctive fern-like appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soils.

This species is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
