Piper tenuispicum

Piper tenuispicum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved pepper vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes various species of pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its slender, vine-like stems and small, ovate leaves with a glossy surface. Its flowers are unisexual, with separate male and female flowers on the same plant, and it produces small, round berries that are often green when mature.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its unique foliage.
