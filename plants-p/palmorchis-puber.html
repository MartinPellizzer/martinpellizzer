Palmorchis puber

Palmorchis puber, commonly known as the pubescent orchid, is a rare and delicate orchid species native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, palm-like leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers that typically bloom in the early spring. The plant prefers moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical or subtropical forests. Its reproductive cycle relies on specific mycorrhizal fungi for nutrient absorption, highlighting its ecological significance.

Due to habitat loss, Palmorchis puber is considered endangered and is the subject of conservation efforts.
