Pleurothallis potamophila

Pleurothallis potamophila, commonly known as the Amazonian orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its delicate, often fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in the dense rainforest canopy. Its flowers are usually small, with intricate patterns and a wide range of colors, including white, yellow, and pink.

Pleurothallis potamophila thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.
