Potamogeton zetterstedtii

Potamogeton zetterstedtii, commonly known as Zetterstedt's pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater habitats in Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in shallow, slow-moving waters and is characterized by its long, ribbon-like leaves that are often slightly curled. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are arranged in spike-like clusters, and it plays a role in providing habitat and food for aquatic organisms.

Its presence is often an indicator of good water quality due to its sensitivity to pollution.
