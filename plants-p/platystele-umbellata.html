Platystele umbellata

Platystele umbellata, commonly known as the umbrella platystele, is a rare and delicate member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its small, pale green leaves and a single, slender stem that produces a cluster of tiny, white flowers. Native to the cloud forests of Central and South America, this plant thrives in humid, shaded environments. Its unique floral structure and limited distribution make it a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists.

Due to habitat loss, Platystele umbellata is considered endangered, highlighting the need for its protection.
