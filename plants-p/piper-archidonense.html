Piper archidonense

Piper archidonense, commonly known as the Archipelago pepper, is a flowering plant native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many species of pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. Its small, greenish-white flowers are borne in spikes and are followed by small, round berries.

The plant is often found in moist, shaded areas of tropical forests.
