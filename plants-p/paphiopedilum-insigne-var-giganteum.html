Paphiopedilum insigne var. giganteum

Paphiopedilum insigne var. giganteum, commonly known as the giant Singapore orchid, is a rare and striking orchid native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that can reach up to 15 centimeters in diameter, with a distinctive pouch-shaped lip. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its foliage consists of broad, dark green leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating.

This variety is highly valued in horticulture for its beauty and rarity, making it a sought-after specimen among orchid enthusiasts.
