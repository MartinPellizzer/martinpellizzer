Phylica debilis

Phylica debilis, commonly known as the Cape snowball, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Proteaceae family and is characterized by its spherical clusters of white, fluffy flowers that resemble snowballs. The plant is evergreen, with small, narrow leaves that are typically covered in a fine, silvery down. It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Phylica debilis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
