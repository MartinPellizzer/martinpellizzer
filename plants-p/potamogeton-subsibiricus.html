Potamogeton subsibiricus

Potamogeton subsibiricus, commonly known as the Siberian pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to temperate and cold regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in freshwater environments such as lakes, ponds, and slow-moving rivers. The plant has long, narrow leaves that are often arranged in whorls around the stem, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually submerged. Its rhizomes form dense mats, which can help stabilize sediments and provide habitat for aquatic organisms.

Potamogeton subsibiricus plays a role in water filtration and is sometimes used in phytoremediation efforts.
