Palmijuncus crinitus

Palmijuncus crinitus, commonly known as the hairy palm, is a rare and unique plant species native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and a crown of feathery, hairy leaves that give it its common name. The plant exhibits a distinctive growth habit, with a single trunk and a dense, tufted appearance. Its leaves are long and narrow, often reaching several meters in length, and are covered in fine, silky hairs.

This species is of interest to botanists due to its unusual morphology and limited distribution.
