Phytelephas kunthiana

Phytelephas kunthiana, commonly known as the Amazonian macaw palm, is a tall, slender palm native to the rainforests of South America.

It is characterized by its large, fan-shaped leaves and can grow up to 20 meters in height. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in branched clusters, which are followed by hard, woody fruits. Its trunk is smooth and often exhibits a light green or grayish color.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
