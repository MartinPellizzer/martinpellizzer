Phyllanthus virgatus var. minutiflorus

Phyllanthus virgatus var. minutiflorus, commonly known as the small-flowered spurge, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is a shrub characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptic to ovate in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers clustered in dense inflorescences, which are typically borne in the axils of the leaves. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or scrub habitats.

The species is noted for its adaptability and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
