Potentilla multifida f. ornithopoda

Potentilla multifida f. ornithopoda, commonly known as the ornate cinquefoil, is a flowering plant belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves that give it a delicate appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, golden-yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It is native to regions of Europe and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and flowers.
