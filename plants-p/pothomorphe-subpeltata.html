Pothomorphe subpeltata

Pothomorphe subpeltata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf pottsonia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It features slender, upright stems that are often hairy, and its leaves are elongated, narrow, and typically covered with fine pubescence. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually pale yellow to greenish-white, arranged in dense inflorescences. It thrives in arid or semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky soils or along streambanks.

Pothomorphe subpeltata is adapted to drought conditions and is often found in disturbed or open habitats.
