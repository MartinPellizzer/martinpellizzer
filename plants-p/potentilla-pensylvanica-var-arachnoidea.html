Potentilla pensylvanica var. arachnoidea

Potentilla pensylvanica var. arachnoidea, commonly known as the woolly cinquefoil, is a perennial flowering plant native to the northern United States and southern Canada.

It is characterized by its hairy, grayish stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive, fuzzy appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in rocky, dry soils and is often found in open woodlands and along streambanks.

Botanically, it is part of the Rosaceae family, sharing traits with other members of the genus Potentilla.
