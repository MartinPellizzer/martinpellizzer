Poa sphondylodes var. diantha

Poa sphondylodes var. diantha, commonly known as the silver hair grass, is a variant of the silver hair grass species native to South America.

It is a perennial grass characterized by its fine, wiry leaves and dense, tufted growth habit. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets that are often silver-haired, giving it its common name.

Botanically, it is notable for its adaptability to wet environments and its role in stabilizing soil in riparian zones.
