Polycarena aemulans

Polycarena aemulans, commonly known as the swamp rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments in the Americas.

It typically grows in marshy areas, where it thrives in saturated soil conditions. The plant features tall, cylindrical stems that are often covered in fine, hair-like structures. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, arranged in a rosette at the base.

Polycarena aemulans plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wetland organisms.
