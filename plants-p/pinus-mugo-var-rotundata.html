Pinus mugo var. rotundata

Pinus mugo var. rotundata, commonly known as the dwarf mountain pine, is a compact evergreen conifer native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its dense, spherical shape and short, stiff needles that are a dark green color. The plant produces small, cone-like structures that contain seeds, typical of the Pinaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine landscapes.

Its hardy nature allows it to withstand cold temperatures and poor soil conditions.
