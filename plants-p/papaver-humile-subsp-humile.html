Papaver humile subsp. humile

Papaver humile subsp.

humile, commonly known as the Persian poppy, is a herbaceous perennial native to Central Asia. It features striking, bright red or orange flowers with a distinctive, saucer-shaped corona.

The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are lanceolate and often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
