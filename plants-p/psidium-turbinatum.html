Psidium turbinatum

Psidium turbinatum, commonly known as the turban guava, is a small evergreen shrub or tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, leathery leaves and showy, bell-shaped white flowers. The plant produces small, edible fruits that are typically yellow or orange when ripe, with a sweet and tangy flavor. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit.

The species is also valued for its ability to tolerate drought and poor soil conditions.
