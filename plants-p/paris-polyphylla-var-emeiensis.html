Paris polyphylla var. emeiensis

Paris polyphylla var. emeiensis, commonly known as the Emei mountain Paris, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Sichuan, China.

It belongs to the family Melanthiaceae and is characterized by its clustered, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has a rhizomatous root system and possesses a distinctive, fleshy, and often aromatic underground stem. It is often found in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors and rocky crevices.

Due to its medicinal properties and rarity, it is considered an important species in traditional Chinese medicine.
