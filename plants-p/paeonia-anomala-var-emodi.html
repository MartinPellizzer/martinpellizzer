Paeonia anomala var. emodi

Paeonia anomala var. emodi, commonly known as the Himalayan peony, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the alpine regions of the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and red, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has compound, trifoliate leaves that are often hairy and arranged alternately along the stems. Its roots are tuberous and can store nutrients, aiding in its survival in high-altitude environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and is also studied for its potential medicinal properties.
