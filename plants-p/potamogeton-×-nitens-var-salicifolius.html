Potamogeton × nitens var. salicifolius

Potamogeton × nitens var. salicifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaf pondweed, is a hybrid aquatic plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its long, slender, grass-like leaves that are typically narrow and slightly pointed. The plant thrives in freshwater environments such as ponds, lakes, and slow-moving rivers. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often submerged or floating.

Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread rapidly in suitable conditions, making it both ecologically significant and potentially invasive.
