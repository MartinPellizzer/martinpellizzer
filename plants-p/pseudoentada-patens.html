Pseudoentada patens

Pseudoentada patens, commonly known as the Brazilian pequi tree, is a deciduous tree native to the Amazon rainforest.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy yellow flowers. The tree produces a unique fruit that is encased in a woody, spiny husk. The seeds inside are often used in traditional medicine and as a food source.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
