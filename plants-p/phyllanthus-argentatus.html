Phyllanthus argentatus

Phyllanthus argentatus, commonly known as silver phyllanthus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its striking silver-gray foliage. The plant produces small, inconspicuous green flowers that are often clustered in umbels. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and have a distinctive waxy coating that reflects light.

Phyllanthus argentatus is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to various growing conditions.
