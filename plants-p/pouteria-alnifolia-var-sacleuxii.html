Pouteria alnifolia var. sacleuxii

Pouteria alnifolia var. sacleuxii, commonly known as the Brazilian chestnut or sacleux nut, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree that typically grows in humid lowland forests. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by edible fruits resembling chestnuts. The leaves are simple, alternate, and have a glossy, dark green appearance.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated for its unique foliage and potential medicinal properties.
