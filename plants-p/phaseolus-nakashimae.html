Phaseolus nakashimae

Phaseolus nakashimae, commonly known as the Japanese kidney bean, is a species of legume native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, erect stems and trifoliate leaves with small, heart-shaped leaflets. The plant produces pods that contain several kidney-shaped seeds, which are typically white or pale yellow in color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its edible seeds.

Botanically, it is closely related to other Phaseolus species, such as the common bean.
