Paraphalaenopsis laycockii

Paraphalaenopsis laycockii, commonly known as the Laycock's orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its long, slender pseudobulbs and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces striking, showy flowers in shades of yellow and orange, often with red or brown markings. It is typically found in humid, shaded forest environments.

This orchid is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in greenhouses or as a houseplant.
