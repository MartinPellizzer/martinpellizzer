Polygala lusitanica

Polygala lusitanica, commonly known as the Portuguese milk-vetch, is a herbaceous perennial native to southern Europe and North Africa.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, often blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to Mediterranean climates. Its leaves are simple and opposite, with a hairy texture that aids in water retention.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to pollinators.
