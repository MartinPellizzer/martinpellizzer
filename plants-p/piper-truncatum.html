Piper truncatum

Piper truncatum, commonly known as the roughleaf pepper or African pepper, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Piper genus in the family Piperaceae, which includes many species of flowering plants. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves with serrated edges and small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers. Its fruits are tiny, three-lobed berries that develop from the flowers.

The plant is often grown for its ornamental value and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
