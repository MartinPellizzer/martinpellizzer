Piper scabrum var. kalacroixense

Piper scabrum var. kalacroixense, commonly known as the Kalacroix pepper, is a variant of the Piper scabrum species native to Central America.

It is a climbing vine characterized by its small, elliptical leaves with a rough, scabrous texture. The plant produces small, green berries that turn red when ripe, containing aromatic seeds used in traditional medicine. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in shaded, lowland forest environments.

The botanical interest of this variant lies in its unique morphology and potential medicinal properties.
