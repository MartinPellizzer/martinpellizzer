Potentilla geranioides

Potentilla geranioides, commonly known as the scarlet geranium, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of 12 to 24 inches. The plant features upright stems and produces showy, five-petaled flowers in shades of red, pink, and white. Its foliage is dark green and slightly hairy, with serrated leaf margins.

It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often used in gardens for its ornamental value.
