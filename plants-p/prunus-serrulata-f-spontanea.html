Prunus serrulata f. spontanea

Prunus serrulata f. spontanea, commonly known as the Japanese cherry blossom or some varieties as the mountain cherry, is a flowering plant native to Japan.

It is a naturally occurring form of Prunus serrulata, characterized by its delicate pink or white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows as a small tree or shrub, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are oval-shaped with serrated edges, and it produces small, red berries in the fall.

This species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often planted in gardens and public spaces for its spring blossoms.
