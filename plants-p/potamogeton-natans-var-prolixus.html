Potamogeton natans var. prolixus

Potamogeton natans var. prolixus, commonly known as the broadleaf pondweed, is a aquatic plant native to freshwater environments across Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its long, flexible stems that often form dense underwater vegetation. The leaves are typically narrow, ribbon-like, and arranged alternately along the stem. It produces small, green flowers that are clustered in spike-like inflorescences, usually emerging above the water surface.

This plant plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various water organisms.
