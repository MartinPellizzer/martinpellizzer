Persimon

Persimon, commonly known as the persimmon, is a deciduous fruit-bearing tree native to Asia.

It belongs to the genus Diospyros and produces sweet, orange-colored fruits that are typically harvested in the fall. The fruit has a fleshy, edible pulp surrounding a central core, and it is rich in vitamins A and C. Persimmons can be either dry or sweet varieties, depending on the species and ripeness.

The plant thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
