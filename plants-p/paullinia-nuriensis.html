Paullinia nuriensis

Paullinia nuriensis, commonly known as the Brazilian soursop, is a member of the Sapindaceae family and is native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree that can grow up to 10 meters tall, characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by woody, spiny fruits resembling a large, elongated citrus fruit. Its botanical features include a thick, corky bark and a system of aerial roots that help it climb and anchor itself in dense forest canopies.

The plant is also notable for its medicinal properties, with various parts used in traditional medicine.
