Prunus hattan

Prunus hattan, commonly known as the Hattan cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers in early spring, followed by edible berries that are typically red or black in color. Prunus hattan is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.
