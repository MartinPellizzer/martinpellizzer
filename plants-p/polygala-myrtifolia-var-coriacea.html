Polygala myrtifolia var. coriacea

Polygala myrtifolia var. coriacea, commonly known as the roughleaf milkvetch, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its woody stems and small, often clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink. The leaves are typically rough and coriaceous, giving the plant its specific epithet. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky or sandy soils.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
