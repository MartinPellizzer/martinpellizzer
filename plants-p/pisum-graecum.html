Pisum graecum

Pisum graecum, commonly known as Greek pea, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes known for their ability to fix nitrogen in the soil. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are typically harvested when mature. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets, and it has small, fragrant flowers that are usually pink or white.

Pisum graecum is valued both for its ornamental appeal and its edible seeds, which can be used in culinary applications.
