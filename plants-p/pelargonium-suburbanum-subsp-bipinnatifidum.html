Pelargonium suburbanum subsp. bipinnatifidum

Pelargonium suburbanum subsp.

bipinnatifidum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved geranium, is a flowering plant native to South Africa. It is characterized by its finely dissected, bipinnatifid leaves that give it a delicate, lace-like appearance.

The plant produces small, clustered flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, typically blooming in spring and summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is widely cultivated in temperate regions.
