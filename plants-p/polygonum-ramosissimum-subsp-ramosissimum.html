Polygonum ramosissimum subsp. ramosissimum

Polygonum ramosissimum subsp.

ramosissimum, commonly known as the redroot smartweed, is a herbaceous annual plant native to North America. It is characterized by its upright, branched stems that are often reddish in color, particularly near the base.

The leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and have a slightly hairy texture. It produces small, greenish-white flowers in dense clusters at the tips of the stems.

This species is commonly found in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and fields, and is known for its rapid growth and adaptability.
