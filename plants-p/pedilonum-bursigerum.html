Pedilonum bursigerum

Pedilonum bursigerum, commonly known as the burr vine, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and clusters of small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces distinctive, spiny seed pods that aid in its dispersal by attaching to animal fur or clothing. Its leaves are typically alternate and have a rough, hairy texture.

Pedilonum bursigerum is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and ability to thrive in warm, humid climates.
