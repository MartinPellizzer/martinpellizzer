Planchonella pohlmaniana var. vestita

Planchonella pohlmaniana var. vestita, commonly known as the white-leafed hibiscus, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine white pubescence. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by orange-red fruits resembling small lanterns. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
