Prenanthes suavis

Prenanthes suavis, commonly known as sweet pearly everlasting, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist meadows and along stream banks, thriving in full to partial sunlight. Its foliage consists of lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, white down.

Prenanthes suavis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in native plant gardens.
