Phyllanthus actephilifolius

Phyllanthus actephilifolius, commonly known as the African date palm, is a small evergreen shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It has small, opposite, leathery leaves that are typically elliptical or ovate in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in dense inflorescences, followed by edible, sweet, and fleshy fruits resembling dates. Its botanical features make it well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional uses in some cultures.
