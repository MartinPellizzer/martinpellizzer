Patinoa ichthyotoxica

Patinoa ichthyotoxica, commonly known as the fish poison plant, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly found in regions of Brazil and Peru.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its small, yellowish flowers and oval-shaped leaves. The plant contains toxic compounds, including ichthyotoxins, which can cause severe reactions in humans and animals. It is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories and along riverbanks.

Due to its toxic properties, it is generally avoided for consumption or medicinal use.
