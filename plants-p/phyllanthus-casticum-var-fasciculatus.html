Phyllanthus casticum var. fasciculatus

Phyllanthus casticum var. fasciculatus, commonly known as the clustered spurge, is a shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features small, smooth, oval-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in clusters along the stems. The plant produces tiny, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by small, round berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas or along roadsides.

Its botanical characteristics make it adaptable to various environmental conditions.
