Phyllanthus myrtillus

Phyllanthus myrtillus, commonly known as the myrtle-leaf phyllanthus, is a small evergreen shrub native to the tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves that are often glossy and aromatic. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in herbal medicine.

Its growth habit and foliage make it suitable for use in hedges and as a ground cover in warm climates.
