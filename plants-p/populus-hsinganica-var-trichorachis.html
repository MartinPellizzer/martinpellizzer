Populus hsinganica var. trichorachis

Populus hsinganica var. trichorachis, commonly known as the Hsianing poplar, is a variant of the poplar tree native to northern China.

It is characterized by its distinctive foliage, with leaves that are finely toothed and have a slightly hairy underside. The plant belongs to the Salicaceae family and is known for its rapid growth and adaptability to various soil conditions. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by winged seeds that are dispersed by the wind.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ecological benefits in reforestation efforts.
