Phyllodes rotundifolia

Phyllodes rotundifolia, commonly known as the globular acacia, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its unique, rounded phyllodes that are typically green and covered in fine hairs. The plant produces yellow globular flower heads that bloom in spring, adding visual interest to its appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
