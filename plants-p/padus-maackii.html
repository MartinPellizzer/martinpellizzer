Padus maackii

Padus maackii, commonly known as Manchurian cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its simple, ovate leaves and showy white or pink flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, dark red berries that are edible but often have a tart flavor.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental qualities and adaptability to various soil types.
