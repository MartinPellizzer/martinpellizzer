Pulsatilla media

Pulsatilla media, commonly known as the meadow anemone, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, which includes buttercups and other flowering plants. The plant features showy, white or pinkish flowers with a distinctive crown of petal-like sepals. Its leaves are palmately compound, with lobed divisions that are often covered in fine hairs.

Pulsatilla media thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.
