Paepalanthus monticola

Paepalanthus monticola, commonly known as the Andean sunflower, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes.

The plant typically grows in rocky soils and is adapted to the extreme conditions of its mountainous habitat. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and other composite flowers.
