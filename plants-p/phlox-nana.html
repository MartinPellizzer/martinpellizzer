Phlox nana

Phlox nana, commonly known as dwarf phlox, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the northeastern United States.

It features compact, rounded foliage and produces clusters of showy, fragrant flowers in shades of pink, purple, and white. The plant typically blooms in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its hardy nature and ornamental value make it a favored addition to many gardens.
