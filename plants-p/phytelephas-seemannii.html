Phytelephas seemannii

Phytelephas seemannii, commonly known as the Areca palm, is a species native to Central and South America.

It is a tall, slender palm characterized by its pinnate leaves and smooth, grayish trunk. The plant produces large, yellowish-white flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its fruit is a fibrous, spiky structure that contains a single seed.

Phytelephas seemannii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
