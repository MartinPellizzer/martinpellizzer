Piper riedelii

Piper riedelii, commonly known as the Riedel's pepper, is a tropical climbing vine native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Piper genus, which includes numerous species of flowering plants in the family Piperaceae. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by berries that turn from yellow to red as they mature. Its leaves are typically ovate with a rough texture and are often used in traditional medicine.

The plant is valued for its potential medicinal properties and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
