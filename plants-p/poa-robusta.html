Poa robusta

Poa robusta, commonly known as rough bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and dark green, bluish-gray foliage. The plant produces spikelets that are often tinged with purple, and its inflorescence is typically slender and open. Poa robusta thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, pastures, and along roadsides.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to form durable turf.
