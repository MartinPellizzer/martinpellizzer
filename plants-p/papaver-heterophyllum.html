Papaver heterophyllum

Papaver heterophyllum, commonly known as the variable poppy, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that range in color from pale yellow to deep red, often with a distinctive black center. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and has lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. It produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, oval seeds.

Papaver heterophyllum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
