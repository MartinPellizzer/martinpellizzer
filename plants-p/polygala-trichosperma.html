Polygala trichosperma

Polygala trichosperma, commonly known as the narrow-leaved milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its slender, erect stem and small, clustered flowers. The plant has narrow, linear leaves and produces yellowish or greenish flowers that are often clustered in racemes. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid conditions.

Polygala trichosperma is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens in regions with a Mediterranean climate.
