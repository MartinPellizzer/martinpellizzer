Passiflora costaricensis

Passiflora costaricensis, commonly known as the Costa Rican passionflower, is a tropical vine native to Central America.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful flowers that feature a unique structure with five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments. The plant produces blue or purple berries that are edible and contain seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for horticulture in warm climates.
