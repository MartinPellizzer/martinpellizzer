Piper pendens var. infaustum

Piper pendens var. infaustum, commonly known as the black pepper vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dark green, opposite leaves and small, clustered white flowers that develop into red berries. The plant is part of the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species. Its fruit contains the spice piperine, which is responsible for its characteristic pungency.

The variety infaustum is distinguished by its dark, almost black berries, giving it its common name.
