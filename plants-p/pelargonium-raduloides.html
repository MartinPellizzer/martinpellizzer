Pelargonium raduloides

Pelargonium raduloides, commonly known as the raduloid geranium, is a species of flowering plant in the family Geraniaceae.

It is native to South Africa and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and showy, pink to purple flowers. The plant features rounded, velvety leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Pelargonium raduloides is often cultivated as a decorative plant for gardens and containers due to its ornamental value.
