Primula loczyi

Primula loczyi, commonly known as the Loczy's primrose, is a species of flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that typically range in color from white to pink. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes. Its leaves are usually oval and slightly hairy, while its stems are erect and often branched.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, Primula loczyi is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
