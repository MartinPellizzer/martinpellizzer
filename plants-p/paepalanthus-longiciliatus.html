Paepalanthus longiciliatus

Paepalanthus longiciliatus, commonly known as the long-bristled puya, is a striking member of the Xanthorrhoeaceae family, native to South America.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its tall, spire-like inflorescence that bears numerous small, showy flowers. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky or sandy soils. Its unique floral structure consists of long, slender bracts that encase the actual flowers, which are typically white or pale yellow.

Paepalanthus longiciliatus is known for its ornamental value and is cultivated in gardens for its dramatic appearance.
