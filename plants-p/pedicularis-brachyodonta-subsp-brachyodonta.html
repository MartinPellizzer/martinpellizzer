Pedicularis brachyodonta subsp. brachyodonta

Pedicularis brachyodonta subsp.

brachyodonta, commonly known as the short-stalked lady's slipper, is a rare and striking orchid native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe. It is characterized by its distinctive, pouch-like flower that is typically pink or purple in color.

The plant grows in rocky or alpine habitats and prefers well-drained, acidic soils. It blooms in late summer, producing showy flowers that attract specific pollinators.
