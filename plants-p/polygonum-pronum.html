Polygonum pronum

Polygonum pronum, commonly known as the slender knotweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, characterized by its distinctive nodes and often triangular stems. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, and its leaves are typically narrow with a prominent midrib. Polygonum pronum thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
