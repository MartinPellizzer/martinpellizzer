Paronychia mughlaei

Paronychia mughlaei, commonly known as the desert sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of central Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial herb that typically forms dense mats of foliage. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer. Its leaves are fleshy and succulent, adapted to store water in its native dry environment.

Paronychia mughlaei is often found in sandy or rocky soils and is well-suited to drought conditions.
