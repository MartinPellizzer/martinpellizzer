Pseudotsuga douglasii f. glauca-pendula

Pseudotsuga douglasii f. glauca-pendula, commonly known as the blue-weeping Douglas fir, is a botanical variant of the Douglas fir characterized by its distinctive blue-green foliage and weeping growth habit.

This form exhibits a dense, cascading canopy of needle-like leaves that give it a unique, ornamental appearance. The foliage is often coated with a waxy cuticle, contributing to its bluish hue and enhanced drought resistance. It is typically found in cooler, moist environments, thriving in well-drained soils.

The plant is valued for its aesthetic appeal in horticulture and its ability to adapt to various landscape conditions.
