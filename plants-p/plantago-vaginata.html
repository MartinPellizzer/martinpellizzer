Plantago vaginata

Plantago vaginata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf plantain, is a flowering plant belonging to the Plantaginaceae family.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, disturbed soils such as roadsides and fields. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, greenish flowers in dense spikes. It is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

The plant is often used in traditional medicine and is also a common weed in agricultural settings.
