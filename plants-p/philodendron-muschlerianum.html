Philodendron muschlerianum

Philodendron muschlerianum, commonly known as the Muschler's Philodendron, is a tropical climbing plant native to South America.

It features heart-shaped, glossy dark green leaves with prominent veins, and it produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers. The plant thrives in humid, warm environments with bright, indirect light. It is often cultivated as a houseplant due to its ornamental foliage and relatively easy care requirements.

Its growth habit involves trailing or climbing, making it suitable for hanging baskets or shelves.
