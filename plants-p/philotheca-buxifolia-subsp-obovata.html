Philotheca buxifolia subsp. obovata

Philotheca buxifolia subsp.

obovata, commonly known as the broad-leaved myrtle, is a shrub native to southeastern Australia. It features lanceolate to obovate leaves that are glossy and often have a reddish underside.

The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically followed by red, fleshy berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and dry inland areas.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and traditional uses in herbal medicine.
