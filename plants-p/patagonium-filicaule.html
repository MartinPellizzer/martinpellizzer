Patagonium filicaule

Patagonium filicaule, commonly known as the Patagonian fern, is a unique plant native to the southern regions of South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, feathery fronds that resemble those of a typical fern, though it is not a true fern but rather a member of the lycophyte group. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in mountainous areas with cool climates. Its reproductive structures, known as sporangia, are clustered on the underside of its leaves, releasing spores for propagation.

Patagonium filicaule plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, contributing to soil stability and providing shelter for small invertebrates.
