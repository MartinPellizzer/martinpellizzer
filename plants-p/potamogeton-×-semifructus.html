Potamogeton × semifructus

Potamogeton × semifructus, commonly known as the hybrid water plant, is a sterile hybrid species resulting from the cross between Potamogeton perfoliatus and Potamogeton crispus.

It is characterized by its submerged, ribbon-like leaves that are often arranged in whorls around the stem. The plant typically grows in shallow freshwater habitats such as ponds, lakes, and slow-moving rivers. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually submerged, and it does not form viable seeds due to its sterility.

This plant plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
