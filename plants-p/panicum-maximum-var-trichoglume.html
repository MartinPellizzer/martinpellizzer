Panicum maximum var. trichoglume

Panicum maximum var. trichoglume, commonly known as Guinea grass, is a tall, perennial grass native to Africa.

It is characterized by its coarse, stiff leaves and panicles that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant is drought-tolerant and thrives in warm, tropical climates. It is widely used as forage for livestock due to its high nutritional value.

Its rapid growth and adaptability make it an important component of pasture systems in many regions.
