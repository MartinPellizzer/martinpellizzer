Podocarpus javanicus

Podocarpus javanicus, commonly known as the Indian pine or Java cedar, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Podocarpaceae and is characterized by its dense, needle-like foliage and conical shape. The tree produces small, cone-like structures that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in landscaping due to its adaptability to various soil types.

Additionally, its wood is valued for its durability and resistance to decay.
