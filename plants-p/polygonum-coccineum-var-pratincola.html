Polygonum coccineum var. pratincola

Polygonum coccineum var. pratincola, commonly known as the pratincola knotweed, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It features slender, upright stems covered in small, reddish-purple nodules, which give it its distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink to red in color, blooming from late summer into early autumn. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture.

This species thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in wet meadows and along stream banks.
