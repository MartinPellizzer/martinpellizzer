Penstemon heterophyllus var. australis

Penstemon heterophyllus var. australis, commonly known as the Australian beardtongue, is a perennial herb native to southern Australia.

It features hairy, upright stems that bear opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, two-lipped flowers in shades of violet to blue, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and native plant landscapes.

Its unique floral structure and adaptability to arid conditions make it a valuable addition to ecological restoration projects.
