Pleurothallis triura

Pleurothallis triura, commonly known as the three-lobed orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its small, delicate flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are usually white or pale yellow, with three petals and three sepals, and it blooms during the rainy season.

Pleurothallis triura is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by enthusiasts of rare orchids.
