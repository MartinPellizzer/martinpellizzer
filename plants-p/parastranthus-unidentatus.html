Parastranthus unidentatus

Parastranthus unidentatus, commonly known as the African flame bush, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of small, bright orange-red flowers that bloom throughout the year. The plant has small, elliptical leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for coastal and sandy environments.

Its vibrant flowers make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and coastal landscaping.
