Panza × bicolor

Panza × bicolor, commonly known as the hybrid orchid, is a fascinating plant with unique botanical characteristics.

It is a naturally occurring hybrid between two species of orchids, typically Panza sp. and another closely related species, resulting in a blend of traits from both parents.

The plant exhibits a compact growth habit with dense foliage, often featuring strikingly colored leaves that range from green to variegated patterns. Its flowers are usually small and clustered, displaying a mix of colors that can include white, pink, and purple.

This hybrid is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various growing conditions.
