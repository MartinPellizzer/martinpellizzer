Papaver schweinfurthii

Papaver schweinfurthii, commonly known as the Persian poppy, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from red to orange and yellow. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with smooth, glaucous leaves that are lance-shaped and have a velvety texture. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, and the plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Papaver schweinfurthii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant display.
