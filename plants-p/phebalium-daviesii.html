Phebalium daviesii

Phebalium daviesii, commonly known as the Davies' hakea, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It belongs to the Proteaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and distinctive floral structure. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, clustered yellow flowers that bloom in spring. It is adapted to dry, sandy soils and thrives in full sun, making it a hardy and drought-tolerant species.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for native gardens and landscaping.
