Papaver stefanssonianum

Papaver stefanssonianum, commonly known as the Stefanssonian poppy, is a striking annual plant native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its vibrant red petals that bloom in late summer, creating a showy display. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters, with slender stems and palmate leaves. Its flowers are surrounded by a unique, colorful corolla that adds to its ornamental appeal.

This species is valued in horticulture for its beauty and is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
