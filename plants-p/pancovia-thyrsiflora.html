Pancovia thyrsiflora

Pancovia thyrsiflora, commonly known as the flamecup, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, orange-red tubular flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Pancovia thyrsiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
