Phrynium altissimum

Phrynium altissimum, commonly known as the giant arrowleaf, is a striking tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, arrow-shaped leaves that can grow up to 2 meters in length, often with a waxy texture and a deep green color. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow, typically blooming in the summer months. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Phrynium altissimum is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique foliage and ability to add visual interest to gardens.
