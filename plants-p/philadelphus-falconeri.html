Philadelphus falconeri

Philadelphus falconeri, commonly known as the Falconeri mock orange, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 2 to 3 meters, featuring dense, evergreen foliage. The plant produces fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are oval-shaped with a smooth surface and dark green color.

The species is characterized by its compact growth habit and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes in gardens.
