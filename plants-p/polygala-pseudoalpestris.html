Polygala pseudoalpestris

Polygala pseudoalpestris, commonly known as the alpine milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving at high elevations. The plant produces small, showy flowers that are usually pink or purple in color, arranged in dense clusters. Its leaves are narrow and often covered in fine hairs, adapting to cold and arid conditions.

Polygala pseudoalpestris is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable climates.
