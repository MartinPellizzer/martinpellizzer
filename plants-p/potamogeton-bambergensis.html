Potamogeton bambergensis

Potamogeton bambergensis, commonly known as Bamberg's pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in still or slow-moving freshwater habitats such as lakes, ponds, and canals. The plant has long, ribbon-like leaves that are usually arranged in whorls around the stem. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often submerged, followed by narrow, boat-shaped fruits.

Potamogeton bambergensis plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
