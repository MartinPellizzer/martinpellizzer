Persicaria tomentosa

Persicaria tomentosa, commonly known as fuzzy knotweed, is a flowering plant native to Eurasia.

It is characterized by its hairy, grayish foliage and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist environments such as wet meadows and stream sides. It has a rhizomatous root system that allows it to spread vegetatively.

Persicaria tomentosa is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for wildlife.
