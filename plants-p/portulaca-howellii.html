Portulaca howellii

Portulaca howellii, commonly known as Howell's moss rose, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a low-growing annual herb that produces vibrant, showy flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant has fleshy, succulent leaves and stems that help it conserve water in arid environments. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its ability to bloom throughout the growing season adds ornamental value to landscapes in hot, dry climates.
