Peucedanum capense var. lanceolatum

Peucedanum capense var. lanceolatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved cowparsley, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It features slender, upright stems that bear alternate, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in umbels, typical of the Apiaceae family. Its root system is fibrous, and the plant is often found in rocky or sandy soils.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
