Pyrus przewalskii

Pyrus przewalskii, commonly known as the Chinese white pear, is a deciduous tree native to the mountainous regions of western China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and alternate, ovate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, white flowers in early spring, followed by yellow, edible fruits that are similar to pears but often smaller and more tart.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been cultivated for its fruit in traditional Chinese horticulture.
