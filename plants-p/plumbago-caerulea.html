Plumbago caerulea

Plumbago caerulea, commonly known as blue plumbago, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its striking blue or purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
