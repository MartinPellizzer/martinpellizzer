Pulsatilla patens var. violacea

Pulsatilla patens var. violacea, commonly known as the violet pasqueflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of central and eastern Europe.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its showy, violet-colored flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has a basal rosette of hairy, gray-green leaves that help it retain moisture in harsh alpine environments. Its flowers typically consist of showy sepals that resemble petals, with numerous stamens and a central pistil.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its early blooming and unique appearance.
