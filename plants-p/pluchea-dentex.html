Pluchea dentex

Pluchea dentex, commonly known as the toothed blumea, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant features small, yellowish flowers arranged in dense clusters, often surrounded by pale green bracts. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped with sharp teeth along the edges, giving it its common name.

Pluchea dentex is often found in moist, shaded areas and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
