Plectranthus sangerawensis

Plectranthus sangerawensis, commonly known as the Sangeraw plectranthus, is a flowering plant native to Indonesia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves and clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to purple. It thrives in tropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
