Pyrus cydonia var. oblonga

Pyrus cydonia var. oblonga, commonly known as the Asian pear or nashi pear, is a variety of the common pear tree.

It is characterized by its elongated, oval-shaped fruit with a smooth, thin skin that ranges in color from yellow to reddish-brown. The fruit has a crisp, juicy texture and a sweet, sometimes aromatic flavor. It is native to regions of Asia, including China, Japan, and Korea, and is cultivated for its ornamental value and edible fruit.

The plant prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for both gardens and orchards.
