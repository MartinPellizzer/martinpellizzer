Polygala grandiflora var. leiodes

Polygala grandiflora var. leiodes, commonly known as the showy milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its slender stems and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces small, bean-like pods that contain seeds, typical of the Polygalaceae family. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and open habitats.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural studies.
