Paronychia communis var. communis

Paronychia communis var. communis, commonly known as the common sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit with fleshy, succulent leaves that store water, enabling it to survive in dry conditions. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in the spring. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to anchor firmly in sandy soils and access moisture from deeper layers.

Paronychia communis var. communis is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
