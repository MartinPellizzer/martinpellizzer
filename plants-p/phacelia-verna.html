Phacelia verna

Phacelia verna, commonly known as spring phacelia, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is an annual herb that typically grows in open, disturbed areas such as meadows and roadsides. The plant features slender, branching stems and small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in early spring. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and covered in fine hairs.

Phacelia verna is valued for its role in attracting pollinators and its use in ecological restoration projects.
