Pyrus petiolata

Pyrus petiolata, commonly known as the Japanese whitebeam, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and clusters of small, white flowers that appear in early spring. The plant produces small, edible fruits that are typically yellow or reddish in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight, making it a hardy plant suitable for temperate climates.

Pyrus petiolata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in horticultural breeding programs.
