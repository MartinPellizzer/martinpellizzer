Pseudolycopodiella affinis

Pseudolycopodiella affinis, commonly known as the slender clubmoss, is a small evergreen fern-like plant that belongs to the family Lycopodiaceae.

It is characterized by its compact, low-growing habit and produces tiny, spirally arranged leaves that are often covered in a waxy cuticle. The plant reproduces via spores, which are released from small, clustered structures called sporangia located on the undersides of its leaves. It typically thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices.

Due to its delicate nature, it is often found in specific microhabitats and is considered a rare species in many regions.
