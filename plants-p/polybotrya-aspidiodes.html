Polybotrya aspidiodes

Polybotrya aspidiodes, commonly known as the narrow-leaf fern, is a species of fern native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped fronds. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte or lithophyte, often found on trees or rocks in humid environments. Its spore-bearing structures, called sori, are arranged in lines on the underside of the leaves.

The plant's ornamental value makes it a popular choice for indoor gardening and tropical landscapes.
