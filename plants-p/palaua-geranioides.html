Palaua geranioides

Palaua geranioides, commonly known as the creeping geranium, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and the Pacific Islands.

It belongs to the Geraniaceae family and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and fleshy, succulent stems. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or red, with five petals and prominent stamens. Its leaves are opposite, ovate to elliptic in shape, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Palaua geranioides is often used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive appearance and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
