Polygala venenosa subsp. pulchra

Polygala venenosa subsp.

pulchra, commonly known as the pretty milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa. It features slender, erect stems that are often hairy, and its leaves are alternate, linear to lanceolate in shape.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in dense, spike-like clusters, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Due to its medicinal properties, it has been used traditionally in indigenous healing practices.
