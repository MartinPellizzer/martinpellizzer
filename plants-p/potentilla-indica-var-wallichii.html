Potentilla indica var. wallichii

Potentilla indica var. wallichii, commonly known as the Himalayan cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, yellow, five-petaled flowers. The plant typically has finely toothed leaves and thrives in well-drained, rocky soils. It is often found in alpine and subalpine environments at high elevations.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing mountainous terrain.
