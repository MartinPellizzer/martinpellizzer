Paleista

Paleista, commonly known as the ghost flower, is a rare and enigmatic plant native to the remote highlands of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its translucent, waxy leaves that shimmer in the sunlight, giving it a ghostly appearance. The plant produces small, pale pink flowers that bloom only under specific environmental conditions, such as high humidity and cool temperatures. Its unique morphology and elusive blooming cycle have made it a subject of fascination among botanists and conservationists.

Due to habitat loss and limited study, paleista remains a poorly understood species with significant ecological value.
