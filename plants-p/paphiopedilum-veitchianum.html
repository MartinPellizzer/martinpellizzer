Paphiopedilum veitchianum

Paphiopedilum veitchianum, commonly known as the Veitch's lady's slipper, is a terrestrial orchid native to the mountainous regions of China.

It features a distinctive, showy flower with a pouch-like lip that is often adorned with intricate patterns. The plant has a rhizomatous root system and grows in shaded, humid environments. Its leaves are broad and grass-like, typically arranged in a rosette.

Paphiopedilum veitchianum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
