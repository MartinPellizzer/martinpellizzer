Penstemon dayanus

Penstemon dayanus, commonly known as the Sierra penstemon, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and native plant landscapes.

Penstemon dayanus is also valued for its drought tolerance and low maintenance requirements.
