Pleurothallis penelops

Pleurothallis penelops, commonly known as the velvet orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to the tropical regions of South America.

This epiphytic plant is characterized by its small, compact growth habit and its ability to thrive in shaded, humid environments. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink, with a velvety texture that gives it its common name. The plant produces numerous tiny, fragrant blossoms that bloom sequentially throughout the year.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is well-suited for terrariums or as a houseplant.
