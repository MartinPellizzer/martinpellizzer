Polygala americana var. glabrescens

Polygala americana var. glabrescens, commonly known as the smoothleaf milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, sandy soils along stream banks and in open woods. Its leaves are alternate, ovate to lanceolate, and often have a smooth, glabrous surface.

The flowers are usually pink to purple and are arranged in loose, racemose inflorescences.
