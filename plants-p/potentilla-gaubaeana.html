Potentilla gaubaeana

Potentilla gaubaeana, commonly known as the Gaubaeana cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are palmately lobed, and it produces small, aggregate fruits that are dispersed by wind.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and conservation efforts.
