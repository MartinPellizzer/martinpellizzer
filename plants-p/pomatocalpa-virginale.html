Pomatocalpa virginale

Pomatocalpa virginale, commonly known as the Virgin's Orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and a single, fragrant, white to pale pink flower. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and swampy areas. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, and it is often found in association with other orchid species.

The genus Pomatocalpa is known for its unique floral structure and ecological importance in native habitats.
