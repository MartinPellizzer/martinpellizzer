Phyllanthus urinaria

Phyllanthus urinaria, commonly known as cheeseweed, is a small, herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It typically grows in disturbed soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments. The plant has small, green, rounded leaves that are arranged alternately along the stems. Its flowers are tiny and yellow, often clustered in dense inflorescences.

Phyllanthus urinaria is known for its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
