Plantago occidentalis

Plantago occidentalis, commonly known as western plantain, is a perennial herb native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It features a rosette of broad, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in a spike, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in a variety of habitats, including meadows, roadsides, and disturbed soils.

The leaves and seeds of Plantago occidentalis have been used in traditional herbal medicine for their purported healing properties.
