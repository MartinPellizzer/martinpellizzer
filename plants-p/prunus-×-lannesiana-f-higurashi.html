Prunus × lannesiana f. higurashi

Prunus × lannesiana f. higurashi, commonly known as the Higurashi cherry, is a unique ornamental cultivar prized for its distinctive floral characteristics.

This form is a hybrid of Prunus lannesiana, a species native to Japan, and is noted for its double or semi-double flowers that bloom in early spring. The flowers are typically pink or white, with a fluffy appearance that adds visual interest to gardens. It is often cultivated for its aesthetic value in ornamental horticulture, particularly in temperate climates.

The plant prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for landscape design.
