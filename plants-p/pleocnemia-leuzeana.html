Pleocnemia leuzeana

Pleocnemia leuzeana, commonly known as the blackwood tree, is a species of flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 15 meters in height, characterized by its dark, fissured bark and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, followed by woody, cylindrical fruits.

It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained, sandy or loamy soils and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.
