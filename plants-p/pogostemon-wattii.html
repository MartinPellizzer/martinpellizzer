Pogostemon wattii

Pogostemon wattii, commonly known as the Malayan pogostemon, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants. The plant features square stems and opposite leaves that are usually hairy and lance-shaped. Its small, tubular flowers are typically purple or blue, arranged in whorls along the stem.

Pogostemon wattii is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories.
