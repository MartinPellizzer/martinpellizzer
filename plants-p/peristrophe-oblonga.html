Peristrophe oblonga

Peristrophe oblonga, commonly known as the narrow-leaf false dandelion, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and alternate, narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 centimeters and produces small yellow flowers that bloom in spring. Its leaves are often hairy and have a slightly rough texture, while its stems are erect and slightly hairy as well.

Peristrophe oblonga is often found in grasslands and along roadsides, thriving in well-drained soils.
