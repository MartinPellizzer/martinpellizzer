Phoradendron oliverianum

Phoradendron oliverianum, commonly known as Oliver's mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on the branches of host trees, primarily in the southeastern United States.

It has small, oval-shaped leaves and produces white, fleshy berries that are consumed by various bird species. The plant relies on its host for water and some nutrients, while it obtains photosynthetic energy independently. Its seeds are dispersed by birds that eat the berries, facilitating its spread to new host trees.

This species plays an important ecological role in forest ecosystems by supporting bird populations and contributing to the biodiversity of its habitat.
