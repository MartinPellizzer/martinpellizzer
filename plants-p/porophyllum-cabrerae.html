Porophyllum cabrerae

Porophyllum cabrerae, commonly known as the Mexican dandelion, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of the southwestern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant produces yellow ray florets surrounded by a dense cluster of disk florets, which are typically tubular in shape. It is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

The leaves are typically hairy and lance-shaped, with a distinctive basal rosette structure.
