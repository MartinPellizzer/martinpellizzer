Platypodium

Platypodium, commonly known as the golden rain tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, round fruits that contain seeds covered in a silky coating. Platypodium is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.

It is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.
