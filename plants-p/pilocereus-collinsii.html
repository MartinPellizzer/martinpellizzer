Pilocereus collinsii

Pilocereus collinsii, commonly known as the Collins' moon cactus, is a species of cactus native to South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that bloom at night, typically in shades of white, yellow, or orange. The plant has a short, thick stem with spines and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Its unique appearance and nocturnal blooming habits make it a popular choice among cactus enthusiasts.
