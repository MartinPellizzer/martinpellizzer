Peucedanum foeniculaceum

Peucedanum foeniculaceum, commonly known as sweet fennel, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It features finely divided, feathery leaves that resemble those of fennel, and it produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in umbels. The plant is characterized by its aromatic, licorice-scented seeds, which are often used in culinary and medicinal applications. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
