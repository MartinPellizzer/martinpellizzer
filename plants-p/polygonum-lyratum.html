Polygonum lyratum

Polygonum lyratum, commonly known as the creeping smartweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Asia.

It features upright stems that are often reddish in color and produce small, clustered flowers with five petals. The leaves are alternate, ovate to lanceolate, and have a slightly hairy surface. It thrives in moist to wet environments, such as marshes and stream banks.

The plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to spread through rhizomes, making it both a common weed and an ecological pioneer.
