Persica cordifolia

Persica cordifolia, commonly known as the heart-leaved peach.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Central Asia, characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and pinkish-white flowers. The plant produces small, sweet fruits similar to peaches, which are edible and often used in culinary applications. Persica cordifolia thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its botanical features make it both aesthetically pleasing and botanically interesting.
