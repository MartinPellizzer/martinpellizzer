Panicum jaliscanum

Panicum jaliscanum, commonly known as Jalisco grass, is a perennial grass native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, bluish-green leaves. The plant produces panicles of small, spikelet-based flowers that are typically greenish or reddish in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open fields and along roadsides.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is cultivated in some regions for landscaping and erosion control.
