Plectranthus tenuifolius

Plectranthus tenuifolius, commonly known as creeping groundsel, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a low-growing perennial herb with hairy, narrow leaves and small yellow flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a ground cover. Its aromatic leaves are often used in traditional medicine and as a culinary herb.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardiness in temperate climates.
