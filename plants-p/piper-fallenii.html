Piper fallenii

Piper fallenii, commonly known as the Jamaican pepper or wild pepper, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper plants such as black pepper. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves and small, greenish-white flowers that develop into peppery berries. Its fruits are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in the understory of tropical forests.
