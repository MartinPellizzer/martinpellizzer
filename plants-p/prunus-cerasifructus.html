Prunus cerasifructus

Prunus cerasifructus, commonly known as the cherry plum, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to parts of Asia, including China and the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, yellow or orange fruits that are edible but often tart, resembling both cherries and plums. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit production.

Prunus cerasifructus is also valued in traditional medicine for its various health benefits.
