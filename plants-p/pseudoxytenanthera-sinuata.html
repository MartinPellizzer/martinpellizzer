Pseudoxytenanthera sinuata

Pseudoxytenanthera sinuata, commonly known as the Sinuous False Sandalwood, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Fabaceae and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces woody pods that contain flattened seeds, typical of many legumes. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland forests.

Due to its ornamental value and potential for timber, it is of interest in horticulture and ecological studies.
