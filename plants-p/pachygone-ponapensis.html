Pachygone ponapensis

Pachygone ponapensis, commonly known as the Ponapé cycad, is a unique cycad species native to the island of Ponapé in the Federated States of Micronesia.

It is characterized by its thick, woody trunk and a crown of feathery, palm-like leaves. The plant produces cones rather than flowers, with male and female cones occurring on separate plants. Its slow growth rate and limited distribution make it a rare and ecologically significant species.

Conservation efforts are crucial due to habitat loss and threats from invasive species.
