Phaseolus vulgaris var. ochraceus

Phaseolus vulgaris var. ochraceus, commonly known as the orange bean, is a variant of the common bean that is characterized by its distinctive orange to reddish-brown pod and seed color.

It is native to the Americas and is typically grown as an annual legume in warm climates. The plant produces erect, bushy stems with trifoliate leaves and bears pods that mature to a vibrant orange hue. Its seeds are often used in traditional cuisines and are valued for their nutritional content.

This variety is also appreciated for its ornamental qualities in gardens due to its striking coloration.
