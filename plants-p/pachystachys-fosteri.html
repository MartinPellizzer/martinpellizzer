Pachystachys fosteri

Pachystachys fosteri, commonly known as the Foster's giant sedge, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and dense clusters of narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces large, showy inflorescences composed of numerous small, white to pale blue flowers. It thrives in wetland environments and is often found in marshy areas or along the edges of ponds.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and wetland restoration projects.
