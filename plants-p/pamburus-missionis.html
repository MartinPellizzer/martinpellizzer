Pamburus missionis

Pamburus missionis, commonly known as the Mission's pampas grass, is a perennial plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, stiff stems and large, feathery plumes that bloom in the summer. The plant typically grows in open, sunny areas such as grasslands and coastal regions. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, forming a basal rosette.

Pamburus missionis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping and floral arrangements.
