Pelargonium × instratum

Pelargonium × instratum, commonly known as the ivy-leaved geranium, is a hybrid plant that exhibits a unique combination of traits from its parent species.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and distinctive heart-shaped, ivy-like leaves that are often variegated with white or cream markings. The plant produces small, clustered flowers in shades of pink, red, or white, which bloom throughout the growing season. It is widely cultivated as an ornamental plant, particularly in hanging baskets and containers, due to its attractive foliage and continuous flowering.

Pelargonium × instratum thrives in well-drained soil and requires moderate sunlight to flourish.
