Potentilla glandulosa subsp. ashlandica

Potentilla glandulosa subsp.

ashlandica, commonly known as Ashland sandspur, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit and hairy stems.

The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are typically oval-shaped with glandular dots, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in open, disturbed habitats.
