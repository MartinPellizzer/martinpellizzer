Pinus keteleeri

Pinus keteleeri, commonly known as the Keteleer pine, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its long, slender needles that are typically arranged in bundles of five. The tree produces cones that are initially yellowish-green but mature to a reddish-brown color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in botanical gardens and as a landscape tree.
