Platyclinis corrugata

Platyclinis corrugata, commonly known as the roughleaf holly, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features small, oval, dark green leaves with a rough, textured surface. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers in the spring, followed by red, fleshy berries in the fall. It is evergreen, providing year-round interest in the landscape.

Its hardy nature makes it suitable for a variety of garden conditions.
