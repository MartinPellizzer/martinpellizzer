Pygeum topengii

Pygeum topengii, commonly known as the African wild almond, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Proteaceae family and is characterized by its dense, rounded crown and leathery, dark green leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by woody, spherical fruits containing several seeds. Its bark is thick and fissured, often exuding a milky sap when wounded.

Pygeum topengii is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
