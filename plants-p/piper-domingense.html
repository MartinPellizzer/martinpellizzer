Piper domingense

Piper domingense, commonly known as the Dominican pepper or wild pepper, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species such as black pepper. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a distinctive aromatic scent.

The small, green berries are often used in traditional medicine and as a spice in local cuisines.
