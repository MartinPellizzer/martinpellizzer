Polygonum euryale

Polygonum euryale, commonly known as water spinach or Chinese water spinach, is a flowering plant native to Asia.

It is characterized by its edible, leafy stems and heart-shaped leaves that are often consumed in various cuisines. The plant thrives in wet, marshy environments and can grow rapidly in aquatic or semi-aquatic conditions. It produces small, greenish flowers that are followed by distinctive, triangular-shaped seeds.

Polygonum euryale is valued both for its nutritional content and its adaptability to different water conditions.
