Pleioblastus masamuneanus

Pleioblastus masamuneanus, commonly known as the Masamune bamboo, is a species native to Japan.

It is a clumping bamboo that typically grows to a height of 2 to 4 meters. The plant features slender, green culms with a smooth texture and a light blue sheath. Its foliage is dense and provides a decorative appearance, often used in ornamental gardens.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it adaptable to various garden settings.
