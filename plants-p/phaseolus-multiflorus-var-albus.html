Phaseolus multiflorus var. albus

Phaseolus multiflorus var. albus, commonly known as the white runner bean, is a variety of the common bean genus native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its vigorous climbing habit and long, slender pods that turn yellow as they mature. The plant produces small, white flowers that are followed by edible beans, which are often used in culinary applications. Botanically, it has compound leaves with three leaflets and a well-developed root system that helps it thrive in various soil conditions.

This variety is valued for its high yield and adaptability to different climates.
