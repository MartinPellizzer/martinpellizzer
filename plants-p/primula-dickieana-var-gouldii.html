Primula dickieana var. gouldii

Primula dickieana var. gouldii, commonly known as the Gould's primrose, is a low-growing perennial herb native to New Zealand.

It features hairy, oval-shaped leaves that are typically dark green with a velvety texture. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink, white, and purple, which bloom in spring. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in forested areas.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
