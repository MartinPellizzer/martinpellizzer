Polygonum tatewakianum var. notoroense

Polygonum tatewakianum var. notoroense, commonly known as the Notoroense knotweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, characterized by its distinctive polygonal stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or stream banks. Its leaves are ovate to lanceolate, with a rough, serrated edge, and it produces tiny, greenish-white flowers in late summer.

This variant is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage.
