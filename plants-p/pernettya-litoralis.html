Pernettya litoralis

Pernettya litoralis, commonly known as the sea buckthorn, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to coastal regions of the northern hemisphere.

It is characterized by its dense, leathery leaves and clusters of small, orange berries that are rich in nutrients. The plant thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to withstand salt spray and harsh coastal conditions.

Its berries are used in traditional medicine and as a source of vitamin C. Pernettya litoralis plays an important ecological role in stabilizing coastal dunes and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
