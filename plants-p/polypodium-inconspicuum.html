Polypodium inconspicuum

Polypodium inconspicuum, commonly known as the inconspicuous huperzia, is a small, terrestrial fern belonging to the family Huperziaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, evergreen rhizomes and small, clustered leaves that are typically less than 10 centimeters in length. The leaves are usually dark green and have a leathery texture, with a distinct midrib and a serrated margin. This species thrives in shaded, moist environments, often found in forest understories across temperate regions.

It is noted for its slow growth and is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value in shaded gardens.
