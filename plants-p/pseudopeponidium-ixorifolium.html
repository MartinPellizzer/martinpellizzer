Pseudopeponidium ixorifolium

Pseudopeponidium ixorifolium, commonly known as the fragrant leaf cactus, is a unique succulent plant native to arid regions of Central America.

It features thick, fleshy stems that store water, enabling it to survive in dry environments. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers that are often fragrant and attract pollinators such as bees and moths. Its leaves are narrow and often have a waxy coating to reduce water loss.

Botanically, it belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its ability to photosynthesize through its stems when leaves are absent.
