Phalaenopsis gloriosa

Phalaenopsis gloriosa, commonly known as the orchid with golden flowers, is a tropical evergreen orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its long, pendant-like flowers that are typically white or yellow with a distinctive golden throat. The plant has broad, lance-shaped leaves that are usually dark green and arranged in a fan-like pattern. It thrives in warm, humid environments with bright, indirect light.

Phalaenopsis gloriosa is popular as a houseplant due to its aesthetic appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
