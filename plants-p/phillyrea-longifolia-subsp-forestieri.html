Phillyrea longifolia subsp. forestieri

Phillyrea longifolia subsp.

forestieri, commonly known as the Mediterranean buckthorn, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its small, leathery leaves that are typically evergreen.

The plant produces small, white flowers that are followed by dark blue berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and woodland areas.

Its botanical features make it an important component of Mediterranean ecosystems.
