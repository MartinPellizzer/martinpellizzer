Pseuderanthemum racemosum

Pseuderanthemum racemosum, commonly known as the racemose pseuderanthemum, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its compact, evergreen shrub form. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that are typically white or pale blue in color. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often hairy, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Pseuderanthemum racemosum is valued in horticulture for its ability to thrive in shaded environments and its attractive floral display.
