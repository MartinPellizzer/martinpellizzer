Phoradendron hexastichum var. longispicum

Phoradendron hexastichum var. longispicum, commonly known as the long-stalked mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on various host trees in the Americas.

It has small, oval-shaped leaves and produces white or pale yellow flowers that are typically clustered in small inflorescences. The plant relies on its host for water and some nutrients, while conducting photosynthesis to supplement its energy needs. Its berries are usually red or yellow and serve as a food source for birds and other animals.

This species is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, contributing to the ecological dynamics of its habitat.
