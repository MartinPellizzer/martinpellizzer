Physostegia virginiana f. candida

Physostegia virginiana f. candida, commonly known as white whorled milkweed, is a herbaceous perennial native to eastern North America.

It features showy, tubular flowers that are typically white or pale pink, arranged in whorls along the stem. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are opposite and often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, streambanks, and woodland edges.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
