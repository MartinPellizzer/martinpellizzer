Populus balsamifera var. elongata

Populus balsamifera var. elongata, commonly known as the American balsam poplar, is a deciduous tree native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its slender, elongated growth habit and its distinctive, fragrant, balsam-scented resinous sap. The leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a serrated margin, turning yellow in the fall. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found near rivers and streams.

This variant is notable for its ecological importance, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
