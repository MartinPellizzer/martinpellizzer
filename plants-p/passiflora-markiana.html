Passiflora markiana

Passiflora markiana, commonly known as the Mexican passionflower, is a climbing vine native to Central America.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful flowers that feature a blend of blue, purple, and yellow hues. The plant produces edible berries that are often used in local cuisines. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are woody and twining.

Passiflora markiana is valued both for its ornamental beauty and its ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
