Polygala quadrangula

Polygala quadrangula, commonly known as the four-angled milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, rocky habitats such as slopes and gravelly soils. The plant features slender, branching stems with opposite, narrow, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its showy, yellow flowers are clustered in racemes and bloom in late spring to early summer.

Polygala quadrangula is characterized by its distinctive four-angled stem and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments.
