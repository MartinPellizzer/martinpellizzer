Phaseolus latidenticulatus

Phaseolus latidenticulatus, commonly known as the Mexican coffee bean plant, is a species native to Central America and Mexico.

It is a climbing legume with trifoliate leaves and small, yellow flowers. The plant produces elongated, bean-like pods that contain edible seeds. It is notable for its potential use in traditional medicine and as a source of protein.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species in both ecological and agricultural contexts.
