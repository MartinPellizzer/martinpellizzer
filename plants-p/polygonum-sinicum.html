Polygonum sinicum

Polygonum sinicum, commonly known as Chinese knotweed, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, characterized by its distinctive leaf shapes and clusters of small, pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows in moist environments such as riverbanks and wetlands. Its stems are often reddish and have a knotted appearance, giving it its common name.

Polygonum sinicum is known for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
