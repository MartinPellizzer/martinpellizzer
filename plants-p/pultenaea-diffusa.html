Pultenaea diffusa

Pultenaea diffusa, commonly known as the silver wattle, is a shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and small, bright yellow flowers that appear in late winter to early spring. The leaves are typically narrow and linear, with a silvery-gray underside that gives the plant its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and heathland environments.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
