Polycephalos suaveolens

Polycephalos suaveolens, commonly known as the sweet-scented polycephalos, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, often with a mild, sweet scent. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest understories or along stream banks. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and have a smooth texture, while its stems are slender and often covered in fine hairs.

The species is valued in traditional medicine for its purported therapeutic properties.
