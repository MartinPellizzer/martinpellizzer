Potamogeton × fauriei

Potamogeton × fauriei, commonly known as the hybrid pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant that exhibits characteristics of both its parent species, Potamogeton perfoliatus and Potamogeton crispus.

It typically grows in shallow, freshwater habitats such as ponds, lakes, and slow-moving rivers. The plant has long, slender stems that are often branching and bear small, narrow leaves arranged in a spiral pattern. Its flowers are unisexual, with male and female flowers occurring on separate plants, a feature common in many pondweeds.

Potamogeton × fauriei plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various water organisms.
