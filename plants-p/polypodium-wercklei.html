Polypodium wercklei

Polypodium wercklei, commonly known as the rock fern, is a species of epiphytic fern native to tropical regions of Central and South America.

It thrives in humid, shaded environments, often growing on tree trunks or rocky surfaces. The plant has a distinctive frond structure with a long, narrow, and usually dark green rachis. Its spore-producing structures, called sori, are typically located on the underside of the fronds.

Polypodium wercklei is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
