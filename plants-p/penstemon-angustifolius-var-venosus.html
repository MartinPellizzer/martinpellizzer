Penstemon angustifolius var. venosus

Penstemon angustifolius var. venosus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved beardtongue, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, linear leaves and showy, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to purple. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It is a perennial species that produces numerous spikes of flowers, making it a valuable addition to native gardens.

Its unique venation patterns on the leaves distinguish it from other varieties within the Penstemon genus.
