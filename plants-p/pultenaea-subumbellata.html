Pultenaea subumbellata

Pultenaea subumbellata, commonly known as the silver wattle, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Australia.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring wiry, often silver-haired stems. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in dense, umbel-like clusters, which bloom in spring. Its leaves are narrow and alternate, with a leathery texture and a distinctive silvery underside.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and floral display.
