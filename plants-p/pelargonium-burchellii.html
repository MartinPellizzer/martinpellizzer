Pelargonium burchellii

Pelargonium burchellii, commonly known as the Burchell's geranium, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and produces showy, five-petaled flowers in shades of red, pink, and white. The leaves are typically rounded with a slightly hairy texture, and the plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for hanging baskets and containers.

Pelargonium burchellii is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
