Pinus luchuensis subsp. shenkanensis

Pinus luchuensis subsp.

shenkanensis, commonly known as the Okinawan red pine, is a rare and endemic conifer native to the Ryukyu Islands of Japan. This subspecies is characterized by its distinctive reddish-brown bark and needle-like leaves that are typically shorter than those of the parent species.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions. The cones of this plant are small and usually contain two to three seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species in its natural range.
