Pectis linifolia var. hirtella

Pectis linifolia var. hirtella, commonly known as the hairy leaf pectis, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant features narrow, hairy leaves that are typically arranged oppositely along the stem. Its flowers are yellow and bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for xeriscaping in arid environments.
