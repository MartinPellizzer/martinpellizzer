Prunus domestica subsp. brigantina

Prunus domestica subsp.

brigantina, commonly known as the Serbian plum, is a subspecies of the European plum native to the Balkan Peninsula. It is characterized by its small, dark purple to black fruits that are typically harvested in late summer.

The plant has a compact growth habit with dense, thorny branches and oval-shaped leaves. Its flowers are white with a slight pinkish tinge, appearing in early spring before the leaves.

The subspecies is valued for its hardiness and is often used in traditional landscaping and local agriculture.
