Pavonia sidifolia subsp. diuretica

Pavonia sidifolia subsp.

diuretica, commonly known as the kidney bean vine, is a flowering plant native to South America. It is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and heart-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture.

The plant produces clusters of small, pink to red flowers that bloom throughout the growing season. Its botanical name reflects its historical use in traditional medicine for its diuretic properties.

The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.
