Pauridiantha canthiiflora

Pauridiantha canthiiflora, commonly known as the yellow bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in rocky or coastal environments. The plant features showy yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture.

The species is notable for its adaptability to arid conditions and its role in local ecosystems.
