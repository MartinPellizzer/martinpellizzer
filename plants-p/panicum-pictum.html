Panicum pictum

Panicum pictum, commonly known as the painted panicgrass, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its striking, colorful inflorescence. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as wetlands and marshes. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, while its seed heads are often adorned with vivid red, orange, or yellow hues.

Panicum pictum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
