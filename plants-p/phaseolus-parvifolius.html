Phaseolus parvifolius

Phaseolus parvifolius, commonly known as the small-leaf bean, is a leguminous plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. The plant typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters and thrives in warm, tropical climates. Its flowers are usually white or pale yellow, and it is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.

The seeds are often used in traditional cuisines and can be cooked as a vegetable or dried for storage.
