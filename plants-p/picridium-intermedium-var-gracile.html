Picridium intermedium var. gracile

Picridium intermedium var. gracile, commonly known as the slender picridium, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in sunny to partially shaded environments. The plant features slender, branched stems and small, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. Its flowers are yellow and occur in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
