Puya penduliflora

Puya penduliflora, commonly known as the hanging-flowered puya, is a species of flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that form a water-holding cup. The plant produces striking, pendulous inflorescences that can reach several feet in length, bearing numerous small, brightly colored flowers. It is a perennial plant that thrives in high-altitude environments with cool, humid climates.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and conservatories.
