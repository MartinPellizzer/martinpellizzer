Paeonia mascula subsp. triternata

Paeonia mascula subsp.

triternata, commonly known as the Chinese peony or tree peony, is a deciduous shrub native to China. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, fragrant flowers that bloom in spring.

The plant has palmately compound leaves and produces yellowish-green flowers with five to seven petals. It is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been cultivated for centuries in traditional Chinese gardens.

The subspecies is distinguished by its more compact form compared to other peony species.
