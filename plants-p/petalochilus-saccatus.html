Petalochilus saccatus

Petalochilus saccatus, commonly known as the sac-like orchid, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small, terrestrial orchid with a distinctive sac-like structure at the base of its stem. The plant produces delicate, fragrant flowers that are typically pale yellow or greenish-white. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its unique morphology and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
