Pelargonium astragalifolium

Pelargonium astragalifolium, commonly known as the Astragalifolium geranium, is a species native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, pale pink to white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

Due to its unique foliage and delicate blooms, it is a prized addition to botanical collections.
