Plectranthus kameba var. hakusanensis

Plectranthus kameba var. hakusanensis, commonly known as the Japanese nettle plant, is a flowering plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant features small, tubular flowers that are typically purple or violet in color, arranged in clusters. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

It is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its unique appearance and ability to thrive in various growing conditions.
