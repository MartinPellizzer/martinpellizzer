Pectis filipes

Pectis filipes, commonly known as the silky pectis, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. Its leaves are narrow and hairy, giving the plant a distinctive, silvery appearance.

Pectis filipes is often found in open grasslands and along riverbanks, where it thrives in full sun and well-drained soil.
