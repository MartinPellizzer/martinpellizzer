Primula villosa var. commutata

Primula villosa var. commutata, commonly known as the hairy primrose, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its foliage is often covered in fine, white hairs, which give it a distinctive appearance.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate beauty.
