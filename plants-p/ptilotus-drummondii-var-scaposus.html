Ptilotus drummondii var. scaposus

Ptilotus drummondii var. scaposus, commonly known as the slender spinifex, is a low-growing perennial plant native to arid regions of Australia.

It features small, needle-like leaves that help reduce water loss in its harsh environment. The plant produces clusters of pale blue or pink flowers, which bloom during the warmer months. It is often found in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to withstand extreme temperatures and limited rainfall.

Its unique morphology makes it an important component of the local flora in its native habitat.
