Peperomia hawaiensis

Peperomia hawaiensis, commonly known as the Hawaiian peperomia, is a small, compact plant native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It features succulent-like leaves that are thick and fleshy, adapted to store water in its arid native habitat. The plant typically grows to a height of about 10 to 15 centimeters, with a rosette-like arrangement of foliage. Its leaves are usually dark green with a waxy texture, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters.

Peperomia hawaiensis is well-suited for indoor cultivation due to its low maintenance requirements and ability to thrive in bright, indirect light.
