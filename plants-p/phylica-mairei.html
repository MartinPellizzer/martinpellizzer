Phylica mairei

Phylica mairei, commonly known as the Maire's bottlebrush, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Proteaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, cylindrical inflorescences that resemble bottlebrushes. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or red in color, arranged in dense clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Phylica mairei is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
