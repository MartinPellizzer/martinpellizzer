Potamogeton × fluitans var. novaeboracensis

Potamogeton × fluitans var. novaeboracensis, commonly known as the New York pondweed, is a hybrid aquatic plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its slender, ribbon-like leaves that are typically green to bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually arranged in clusters near the water's surface. It thrives in freshwater environments such as lakes, ponds, and slow-moving rivers.

Its rapid growth and ability to form dense underwater stands make it an important component of aquatic ecosystems.
