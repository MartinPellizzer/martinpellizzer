Persicaria trigonocarpa var. viridiflora

Persicaria trigonocarpa var. viridiflora, commonly known as the green-flowered knotweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of Asia.

It features upright stems that are often reddish-purple in color, and its leaves are ovate to lanceolate with a distinctive three-lobed base. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in dense clusters, which are borne on long, slender pedicels. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in riparian habitats.
