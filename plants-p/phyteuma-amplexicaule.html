Phyteuma amplexicaule

Phyteuma amplexicaule, commonly known as the clasping phyteuma, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically blooms in late summer, producing vibrant blue to purple flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are basal and clasping, forming a rosette at the base of the stem.

Phyteuma amplexicaule prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable addition to rock gardens and borders.
