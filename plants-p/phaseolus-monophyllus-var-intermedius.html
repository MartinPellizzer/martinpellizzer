Phaseolus monophyllus var. intermedius

Phaseolus monophyllus var. intermedius, commonly known as the Mexican runner bean, is a climbing legume native to Central America.

It produces elongated, edible pods that are typically green when immature and turn red or yellow when mature. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets and produces small, yellow flowers. It is cultivated for its tender, stringy beans, which are used in various culinary dishes.

The species is closely related to other Phaseolus species and is valued for its adaptability to different growing conditions.
