Pterocarpus aphyllus

Pterocarpus aphyllus, commonly known as the African shea tree, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and compound leaves that are often shed during the dry season. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers followed by woody pods containing flat, edible seeds. It is valued for its hard, durable wood and for the oil extracted from its seeds, which is used in cosmetics and food products.

The plant plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
