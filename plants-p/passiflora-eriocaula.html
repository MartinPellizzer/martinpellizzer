Passiflora eriocaula

Passiflora eriocaula, commonly known as the silky passionflower, is a climbing vine native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, intricate flowers that feature five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments surrounding the reproductive structures. The plant produces small, dark blue to black berries that are edible and often consumed by birds and small mammals. Its foliage consists of heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture, and it thrives in humid, shaded environments.

Passiflora eriocaula is valued in horticulture for its ornamental flowers and is also used in traditional medicine for its potential calming properties.
