Phalaenopsis bella

Phalaenopsis bella, commonly known as the beauty orchid, is a tropical epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features long, narrow leaves and produces clusters of fragrant, white or pale pink flowers that resemble moths. The plant has a pseudobulb structure that stores water and nutrients, enabling it to thrive in low-light conditions. It is prized for its elegant appearance and ability to bloom repeatedly throughout the year.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for both indoor gardening and horticultural collections.
