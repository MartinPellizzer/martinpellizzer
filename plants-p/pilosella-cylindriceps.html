Pilosella cylindriceps

Pilosella cylindriceps, commonly known as the cylindrical pilosella, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its small, white to pale yellow composite flowers and hairy, upright stems. Native to Europe and parts of Asia, it thrives in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and grasslands. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

It is often found in disturbed soils and is considered a short-lived perennial species.
