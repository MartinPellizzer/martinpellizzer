Polygonum strigosum var. glabratum

Polygonum strigosum var. glabratum, commonly known as the smooth knotweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, which includes other familiar plants like buckwheat and sorrel. The plant is characterized by its upright, slender stems that are covered with smooth, glabrous skin. Its leaves are lance-shaped with a prominent midrib and are usually arranged alternately along the stem.

It produces small, greenish-white flowers in dense clusters, typically blooming in late summer to early fall.
