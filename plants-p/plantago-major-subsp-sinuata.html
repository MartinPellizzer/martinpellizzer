Plantago major subsp. sinuata

Plantago major subsp.

sinuata, commonly known as the broadleaf plantain, is a perennial herb native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia. It features rosettes of broad, heart-shaped leaves with prominent veins and a waxy surface.

The plant produces small, greenish flowers in dense spikes that bloom in the summer. It is well adapted to grow in a variety of soil types, including disturbed or urban environments.

Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their anti-inflammatory properties.
