Phlox vernalis

Phlox vernalis, commonly known as spring phlox, is a herbaceous perennial native to eastern North America.

It produces showy clusters of pink to purple flowers in early spring, typically blooming from April to June. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun.

Phlox vernalis is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
