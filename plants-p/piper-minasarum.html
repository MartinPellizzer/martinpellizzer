Piper minasarum

Piper minasarum, commonly known as black pepper.

It is a flowering vine native to the Western Ghats of India, characterized by its small, dark purple berries that are harvested and dried to produce the spice known as black pepper. The plant belongs to the Piperaceae family and is known for its aromatic, pungent fruit. The berries contain a high concentration of piperine, which contributes to its sharp flavor and medicinal properties.

It thrives in tropical climates with high humidity and well-drained soil.
