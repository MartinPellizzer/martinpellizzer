Protea flavescens

Protea flavescens, commonly known as the yellow protea, is a striking South African plant belonging to the Proteaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescence composed of numerous white to yellow flowers arranged in a cylindrical shape. The plant is evergreen and typically grows in coastal areas, where it thrives in well-drained, acidic soils. Its flowers are pollinated by insects, and it produces woody, capsule-like fruits that contain multiple seeds.

Protea flavescens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
