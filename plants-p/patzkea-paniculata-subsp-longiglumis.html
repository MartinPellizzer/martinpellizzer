Patzkea paniculata subsp. longiglumis

Patzkea paniculata subsp.

longiglumis, commonly known as the long glume patzkea, is a species of grass native to tropical and subtropical regions. It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and inflorescences that consist of elongated, branching panicles.

The leaves are narrow and have a smooth texture, while the spikelets are typically adorned with long, slender glumes. This plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and forest margins.

Its botanical features make it an important component of the ecosystems it inhabits.
