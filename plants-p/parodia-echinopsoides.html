Parodia echinopsoides

Parodia echinopsoides, commonly known as the "Cactus of the Desert," is a species of cactus native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, spiny stems that can grow up to 2 meters in height. The plant produces yellow flowers during the rainy season, which are typically short-lived but attract pollinators. It is adapted to arid environments, storing water in its fleshy stems and having a shallow root system.

Due to its unique appearance and hardiness, it is a popular choice for succulent gardens.
