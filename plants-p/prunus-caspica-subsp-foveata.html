Prunus caspica subsp. foveata

Prunus caspica subsp.

foveata, commonly known as the Caspian cherry, is a shrub or small tree native to Central Asia. It features simple, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy underside and produces small, clustered flowers in white or pale pink.

The fruit is a small, yellowish drupe that ripens in late summer. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky or sandy soils.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
