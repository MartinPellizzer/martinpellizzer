Potentilla monspeliensis var. labradorica

Potentilla monspeliensis var. labradorica, commonly known as Labrador rose, is a low-growing shrub native to arctic and alpine regions of North America.

It features small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in late summer. The plant has wiry, angular stems and dark green, oval-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to cold climates.

This variety is often used in landscaping for its hardiness and ornamental value in harsh environments.
