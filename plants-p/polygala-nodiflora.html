Polygala nodiflora

Polygala nodiflora, commonly known as the clustered milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It features slender, branching stems that often grow in dense clusters, and its leaves are small, ovate, and covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in spikes, which bloom from late winter to early spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is cultivated in some botanical gardens.
