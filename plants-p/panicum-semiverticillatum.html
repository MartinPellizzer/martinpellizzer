Panicum semiverticillatum

Panicum semiverticillatum, commonly known as the silky grass or silky panic grass, is a perennial grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its fine, silky hairs that cover the leaves and inflorescence, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in wet or moist habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. Its flowering period occurs in the summer months, producing delicate, spikelike inflorescences.

It is often used in restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for wildlife.
