Pongamia acuminata

Pongamia acuminata, commonly known as the Indian beech, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The tree produces pods containing seeds that are used in traditional medicine and as a source of oil. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in dry, rocky soils.

Its ability to fix nitrogen makes it valuable for soil enrichment and reforestation efforts.
