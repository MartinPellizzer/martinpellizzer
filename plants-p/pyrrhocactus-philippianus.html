Pyrrhocactus philippianus

Pyrrhocactus philippianus, commonly known as the Philippine fire cactus, is a species of cactus native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its cylindrical stems covered in spines. The plant produces bright yellow flowers during the warmer months, which are followed by small, red fruits. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires ample sunlight to flourish.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is considered a rare and valuable species in botanical collections.
