Pleurostachys bracteolata

Pleurostachys bracteolata, commonly known as the Mexican feathergrass, is a perennial grass native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth and long, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces delicate, feathery inflorescences that are typically pale green to yellowish in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for xeriscaping and rock gardens.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is a popular choice for low-maintenance landscaping.
