Peschiera solanifolia

Peschiera solanifolia, commonly known as the silverleaf nightshade, is a herbaceous perennial native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its hairy, silver-gray leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically purple or violet in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its toxic properties, it should be handled with care.
