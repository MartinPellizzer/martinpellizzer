Phyllostachys edulis

Phyllostachys edulis, commonly known as bamboo, is a species of grass that belongs to the Poaceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and rapid growth rate, making it a versatile plant in both ecological and agricultural contexts. The plant is native to East Asia, particularly China, where it has been cultivated for thousands of years. Its hollow, jointed stalks are used in construction, papermaking, and as a food source, especially in the form of bamboo shoots.

Phyllostachys edulis also plays an important role in preventing soil erosion and supporting biodiversity in its native habitats.
