Papaver nudicaule f. gracile

Papaver nudicaule f. gracile, commonly known as the slender poppy, is a variant of the common poppy characterized by its delicate, slender stems and smaller, more refined flowers.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and grasslands. The plant produces showy, cup-shaped flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate, lobed, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
