Pulsatilla herba-somnii

Pulsatilla herba-somnii, commonly known as the sleepy herb, is a member of the Ranunculaceae family.

It is characterized by its showy, violet-colored flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has a short, hairy stem and palmate leaves that are divided into three to five lobes. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

Pulsatilla herba-somnii is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
