Potentilla × porphyracea

Potentilla × porphyracea, commonly known as the fireweed or scarlet cinquefoil, is a hybrid shrub native to the northern hemisphere.

It is characterized by its showy, bright red flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are compound, with five to nine leaflets, and the stems are covered in fine, silky hairs.

Potentilla × porphyracea is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and is also valued for its ability to stabilize soil in erosion-prone areas.
