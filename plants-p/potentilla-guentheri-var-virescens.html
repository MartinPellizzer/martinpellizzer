Potentilla guentheri var. virescens

Potentilla guentheri var. virescens, commonly known as the greenleaf cinquefoil, is a perennial shrub native to the western United States.

It features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically a vibrant green, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, yellow flowers in early summer, which are clustered in open, flat-topped inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in alpine or rocky habitats.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable plant for ecological restoration and ornamental gardening.
