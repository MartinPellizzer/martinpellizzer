Pepo lagenarius

Pepo lagenarius, commonly known as the giant pumpkin, is a member of the Cucurbitaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, spherical or oblong fruit that can weigh several kilograms. The plant produces large, lobed leaves and yellow flowers that are both male and female. The fruit's thick, hard rind is typically orange or yellow in color, while the interior contains numerous seeds.

It is primarily cultivated for its edible flesh, which is rich in nutrients and used in various culinary applications.
