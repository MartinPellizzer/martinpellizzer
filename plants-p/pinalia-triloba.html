Pinalia triloba

Pinalia triloba, commonly known as the three-lobed pincushion, is a flowering plant native to the Andes mountains.

It is characterized by its distinctive, spherical inflorescence composed of numerous small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant has trifoliate leaves with three lance-shaped lobes, and its stems are often covered in a dense layer of soft, woolly hair. It thrives in high-altitude environments with cool, moist conditions.

Pinalia triloba is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens and alpine settings.
