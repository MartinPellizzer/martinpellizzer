Papaver ledebourianum var. leucanthum

Papaver ledebourianum var. leucanthum, commonly known as the white poppy, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its striking white to pale yellow flowers, which are typically cup-shaped and have a papery texture. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, dark seeds. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens, though it is also considered a rare and endangered species in some areas.
