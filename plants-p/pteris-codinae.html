Pteris codinae

Pteris codinae, commonly known as the crested water fern, is a species of fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, curled fronds that resemble a crown, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Botanically, it belongs to the family Pteridaceae and is valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture.
