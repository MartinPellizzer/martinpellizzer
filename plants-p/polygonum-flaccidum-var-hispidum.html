Polygonum flaccidum var. hispidum

Polygonum flaccidum var. hispidum, commonly known as the hairy knotweed, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, which includes plants characterized by their distinct leaf shapes and small, clustered flowers. The plant is recognized by its tall, slender stems and lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, hairy texture. Its flowers are typically greenish-white and appear in dense, terminal clusters.

It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in wetlands or along streambanks.
