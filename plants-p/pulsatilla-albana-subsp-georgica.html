Pulsatilla albana subsp. georgica

Pulsatilla albana subsp.

georgica, commonly known as the Georgian pasqueflower, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus. It features a short, fibrous rootstock and produces a single, showy, cup-shaped flower with prominent, white to pale yellow petals.

The plant typically blooms in early spring, often before the leaves fully develop. Its foliage consists of basal leaves that are deeply lobed and covered in a fine, white down.

The species is adapted to rocky and well-drained soils, thriving in alpine and subalpine environments.
