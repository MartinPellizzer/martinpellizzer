Pelargonium × verecundum

Pelargonium × verecundum, commonly known as the ivy-leaved geranium, is a hybrid plant in the Geraniaceae family.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and heart-shaped leaves that resemble ivy leaves, giving it its common name. The plant produces clusters of small, five-petaled flowers in shades of pink, red, or white, depending on the cultivar. It is often cultivated as a decorative plant for hanging baskets or as a ground cover due to its spreading nature.

Pelargonium × verecundum thrives in well-drained soil and requires moderate sunlight to flourish.
