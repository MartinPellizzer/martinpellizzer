Prunus jamasakura subvar. donarium

Prunus jamasakura subvar. donarium, commonly known as the Japanese cherry blossom, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its delicate, pink to white flowers that bloom in early spring, preceding the foliage. The leaves are ovate with a pointed tip and turn a vibrant green during the growing season. The plant produces small, hard fruits called drupes, which are typically not edible by humans.

It is highly valued for its ornamental beauty and cultural significance in Japan.
