Psoralea schultzii

Psoralea schultzii, commonly known as the yellow burr legume, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, woody stems and small, yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant produces distinctive, spiny pods that contain seeds covered in a sticky, resinous substance. It is adapted to drought conditions and thrives in well-drained soils.

Psoralea schultzii plays a role in local ecosystems by providing food for various insects and small animals.
