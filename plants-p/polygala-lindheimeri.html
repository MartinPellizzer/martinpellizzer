Polygala lindheimeri

Polygala lindheimeri, commonly known as Lindheimer's milkweed, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in dry, rocky soils and open woodlands. The plant produces delicate, spike-like inflorescences with small, white to pinkish flowers. It is part of the Polygalaceae family, which includes many species with unique floral structures.

Polygala lindheimeri is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a food source for native pollinators.
