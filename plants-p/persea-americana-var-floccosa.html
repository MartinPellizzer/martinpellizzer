Persea americana var. floccosa

Persea americana var. floccosa, commonly known as the Mexican persimmon, is a variant of the persimmon tree native to Central America.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and small, yellow-orange fruits that ripen in the fall. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth. Its flowers are typically greenish-white and are followed by the distinctive, fleshy fruits.

This variety is valued for its unique flavor and is often cultivated for both ornamental and culinary purposes.
