Psoralea velutina

Psoralea velutina, commonly known as the velvet psoralea, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, yellowish flowers and hairy, velvety leaves. The plant typically grows in open, sandy or rocky habitats and is often found in grasslands and shrublands. Due to its unique appearance and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
