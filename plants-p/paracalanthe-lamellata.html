Paracalanthe lamellata

Paracalanthe lamellata, commonly known as the narrow-leafed calanthe, is a striking orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features elegant, lance-shaped leaves that are often glaucous in appearance, giving it a distinctive silvery sheen. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink, typically blooming in the spring. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining, nutrient-rich soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
