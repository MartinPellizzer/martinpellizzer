Palmijuncus tetradactylus

Palmijuncus tetradactylus, commonly known as the four-fingered palm, is a rare and unique plant species native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, clustered growth habit and the presence of four fingers-like structures on its stems. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences that bear tiny, white flowers, which are followed by small, round fruits. Its leaves are typically narrow and grass-like, contributing to its overall appearance.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species in its natural environment.
