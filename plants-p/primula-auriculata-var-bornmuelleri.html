Primula auriculata var. bornmuelleri

Primula auriculata var. bornmuelleri, commonly known as the Bornmueller's auriculate primula, is a rare and delicate flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features rosettes of narrow, dark green leaves with a waxy texture, and produces clusters of bell-shaped, violet-blue flowers in early spring. The plant is characterized by its short stature and compact growth habit, making it well-suited for rock gardens or alpine settings. It thrives in cool, moist environments with well-drained soil and partial to full shade.

Due to its limited natural range and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a challenging plant to cultivate outside its native environment.
