Potamogeton × belorussicus

Potamogeton × belorussicus, commonly known as the European pondweed, is a hybrid aquatic plant that exhibits characteristics of both its parent species, Potamogeton perfoliatus and Potamogeton praelatus.

It is a submerged herbaceous plant with long, ribbon-like leaves that are often spirally arranged around the stem. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or brown, and it is capable of both sexual and asexual reproduction. It thrives in freshwater environments such as lakes, ponds, and slow-moving rivers, often forming dense underwater vegetation.

Due to its adaptability, it plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
