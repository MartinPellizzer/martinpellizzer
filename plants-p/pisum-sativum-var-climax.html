Pisum sativum var. climax

Pisum sativum var. climax, commonly known as the climbing pea, is a variety of the common pea plant characterized by its vigorous vining growth habit.

It produces small, edible pods that contain round, yellow seeds, typical of the Fabaceae family. The plant is well-suited for trellises or supports due to its strong stems and ability to climb. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is often cultivated for both its nutritional value and ornamental appeal.
