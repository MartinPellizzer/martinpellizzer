Palicourea arenosa

Palicourea arenosa, commonly known as the desert palicourea, is a shrub native to arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white hairs, which help reduce water loss in its harsh environment. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers, typically blooming during the rainy season. Its fruits are small, berry-like structures that contain seeds dispersed by animals.

Palicourea arenosa is adapted to survive in nutrient-poor soils and can tolerate high temperatures and low moisture conditions.
