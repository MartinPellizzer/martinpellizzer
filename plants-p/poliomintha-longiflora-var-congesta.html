Poliomintha longiflora var. congesta

Poliomintha longiflora var. congesta, commonly known as the clustered bluebead, is a perennial flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, tubular blue or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and shaded areas.

Its fragrant foliage and ornamental flowers make it a popular choice for native gardens and ecological restoration projects.
