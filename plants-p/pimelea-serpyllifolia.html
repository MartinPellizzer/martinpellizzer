Pimelea serpyllifolia

Pimelea serpyllifolia, commonly known as the prostrate bottlebrush, is a low-growing shrub native to Australia.

It features dense clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in spring, giving it a distinctive bottlebrush appearance. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it thrives in well-drained soils. It is well adapted to coastal and sandy environments, making it a hardy and drought-tolerant species.

Pimelea serpyllifolia is often used in native gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
