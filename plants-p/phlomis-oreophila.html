Phlomis oreophila

Phlomis oreophila, commonly known as mountain phlomis, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its upright growth habit and hairy stems. The plant produces showy, pink to purple flower clusters that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and covered in fine pubescence, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Phlomis oreophila thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.
