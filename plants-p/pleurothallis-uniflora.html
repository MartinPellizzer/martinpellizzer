Pleurothallis uniflora

Pleurothallis uniflora, commonly known as the single-flowered slipper orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the cloud forests of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its small, delicate, and often fragrant flowers that typically bloom in shades of white, pink, or purple. The plant produces a single flower per inflorescence, which is a distinguishing feature among its relatives. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained, acidic soil for optimal growth.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is a sought-after species among orchid enthusiasts.
