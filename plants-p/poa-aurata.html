Poa aurata

Poa aurata, commonly known as the silver hair grass, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-gray foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance, especially in the sunlight. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes. Its inflorescence consists of a slender, spike-like cluster of small, greenish-brown flowers.

Poa aurata is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
