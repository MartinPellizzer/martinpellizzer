Polypodium deliculatum

Polypodium deliculatum, commonly known as the delicate huperzia, is a species of fern belonging to the family Huperziaceae.

It is characterized by its compact, evergreen foliage and is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Southeast Asia. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its fronds are usually narrow and lance-shaped, with a dense, leathery texture.

Polypodium deliculatum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in low-light conditions.
