Piriqueta revoluta

Piriqueta revoluta, commonly known as the red sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its small, clustered pink or red flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are narrow and often have a reddish or purplish hue, contributing to its ornamental value.

Piriqueta revoluta is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and low maintenance requirements.
