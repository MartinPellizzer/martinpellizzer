Paspalum holosericeum

Paspalum holosericeum, commonly known as the silky paspalum, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and fine, silky-textured foliage. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands, coastal dunes, and disturbed areas.

Due to its hardiness and ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and used in ecological restoration projects.
