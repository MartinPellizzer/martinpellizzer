Prunus spinosa subvar. lucens

Prunus spinosa subvar. lucens, commonly known as the white-flowered blackthorn, is a shrub native to Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and small, dark green leaves. The plant produces white or pale yellow flowers in early spring, which are followed by black, tart berries in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in hedgerows and woodland edges.

The berries are used in traditional culinary practices and also support various wildlife species.
