Pandorea jasminoides var. alba

Pandorea jasminoides var. alba, commonly known as white pandorea, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its climbing habit and produces fragrant, white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has opposite, simple, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its showy flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological value.
