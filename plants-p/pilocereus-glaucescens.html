Pilocereus glaucescens

Pilocereus glaucescens, commonly known as the night blooming cereus, is a cactus species native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, spiny stems and is known for its striking white flowers that open at night. The plant has a shallow root system and thrives in well-draining soil. It is drought-tolerant and requires minimal water once established.

Its flowers emit a fragrant scent, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths.
