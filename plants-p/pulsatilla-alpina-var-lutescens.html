Pulsatilla alpina var. lutescens

Pulsatilla alpina var. lutescens, commonly known as the alpine buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to alpine regions of Europe.

It features a basal rosette of hairy, dark green leaves and produces showy, yellow flowers with prominent, yellow centers. The plant thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments. It is part of the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy flowers and often toxic sap.

This variety is distinguished by its pale yellow flowers and is often found in mountain meadows and alpine tundra.
