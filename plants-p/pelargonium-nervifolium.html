Pelargonium nervifolium

Pelargonium nervifolium, commonly known as the veined geranium, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, finely veined leaves that give it its scientific name. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of pink, red, and purple, typically blooming in spring and summer. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

Pelargonium nervifolium is valued for its ornamental appeal and relatively low maintenance requirements.
