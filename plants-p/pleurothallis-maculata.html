Pleurothallis maculata

Pleurothallis maculata, commonly known as the spotted pleurothallis, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact, epiphytic growth habit and its ability to thrive in humid, shaded environments. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow with intricate markings. Its leaves are usually dark green and arranged in a rosette pattern, aiding in moisture retention.

Due to its unique appearance and relatively low maintenance requirements, it is a popular choice among orchid enthusiasts.
