Pyrus sieboldii f. tomentosa

Pyrus sieboldii f. tomentosa, commonly known as the tomentose Japanese whitebeam, is a cultivar of the Japanese whitebeam tree.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage, which gives it a distinctive, almost fuzzy appearance. The plant is native to Japan and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens. It features small, white to yellowish flowers that appear in clusters, followed by small, edible fruits.

This variety is relatively hardy and can thrive in a range of soil types, making it a versatile addition to landscaping.
