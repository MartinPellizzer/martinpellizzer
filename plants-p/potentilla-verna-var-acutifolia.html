Potentilla verna var. acutifolia

Potentilla verna var. acutifolia, commonly known as the sharpleaf cinquefoil, is a low-growing perennial shrub native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, typically from April to May. The plant has slender, hairy stems and dark green, ovate leaves with pointed tips, giving it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in native plant gardens.
