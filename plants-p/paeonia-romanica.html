Paeonia romanica

Paeonia romanica, commonly known as the Roman peony, is a species of flowering plant in the family Paeoniaceae.

It is native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe, including parts of Italy and the Balkans. The plant is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and red, typically blooming in late spring. Its leaves are palmately compound, consisting of several broad, serrated leaflets.

Paeonia romanica is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral displays.
