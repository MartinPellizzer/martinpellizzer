Peutalis heterophyla

Peutalis heterophyla, commonly known as the variable peat moss, is a species of peat moss found in moist, acidic environments.

It is characterized by its dense, spongy growth habit and its ability to retain water, making it an important component of peatland ecosystems. The plant has a complex structure with both stem and leaf-like structures, which contribute to its ecological role in soil formation. It is often found in bogs and wetlands, where it plays a crucial role in carbon sequestration.

Its morphology and habitat preferences make it a key indicator species for assessing the health of wetland environments.
