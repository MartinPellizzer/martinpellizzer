Pitcairnia corallina

Pitcairnia corallina, commonly known as the coral pitcairnia, is a species of flowering plant in the Bromeliaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its striking rosette of broad, glossy, waxy leaves that form a water-holding cup. It produces vibrant red or orange inflorescences that bloom in the spring.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in botanical gardens.
