Phyllocactus × kampmannii

Phyllocactus × kampmannii, commonly known as the Christmas cactus, is a hybrid plant native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its segmented, fleshy stems that resemble the branches of a tree, often with bright, colorful flowers in shades of red, pink, and orange. The plant thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot. It is popular as a houseplant due to its attractive appearance and ability to bloom during the holiday season.

Its unique morphology and seasonal blooming make it a favorite among plant enthusiasts.
