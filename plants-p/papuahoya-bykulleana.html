Papuahoya bykulleana

Papuahoya bykulleana, commonly known as the sacred forest orchid, is a rare and endemic species found in the remote rainforests of Papua New Guinea.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, fragrant flowers that bloom in a unique spiral pattern. The plant thrives in shaded, humid environments and requires specific soil conditions rich in organic matter. Its roots form symbiotic relationships with mycorrhizal fungi, which aid in nutrient absorption.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species facing threats from deforestation and climate change.
