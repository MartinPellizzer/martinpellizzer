Pandanus katatonos

Pandanus katatonos, commonly known as the New Caledonian pandanus, is a tropical plant native to the island of New Caledonia.

It is a member of the Pandanaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and long, narrow leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences at the tips of the leaves. Its leaves are often used in traditional crafts and as a source of fiber.

The species is notable for its ecological importance in its native habitat and its unique adaptations to coastal environments.
