Podophyllum leichtlinii

Podophyllum leichtlinii, commonly known as the narrow-leaved American bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or white, blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has dark green, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine pubescence. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its botanical significance lies in its ornamental value and historical use in traditional medicine.
