Polygonum kelloggii

Polygonum kelloggii, commonly known as Kellogg's knotweed, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its upright stems and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a hairy texture and is often found in moist, open habitats such as meadows and streambanks. It is part of the buckwheat family (Polygonaceae) and is known for its rapid growth and ability to spread through rhizomes.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
