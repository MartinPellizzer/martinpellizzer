Podostemum dichotomum

Podostemum dichotomum, commonly known as the dichotomous podostemum, is a unique aquatic plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It typically grows in fast-flowing streams and rivers, with its roots firmly anchored to rocky substrates. The plant has small, opposite leaves that are often submerged, and it produces tiny, inconspicuous flowers. Its reproductive strategy involves wind pollination, which is adapted to its aquatic environment.

Podostemum dichotomum plays an important ecological role in maintaining the biodiversity of its habitat.
