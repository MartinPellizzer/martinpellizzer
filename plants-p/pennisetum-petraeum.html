Pennisetum petraeum

Pennisetum petraeum, commonly known as the rocksettu, is a grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often blue-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bloom in shades of purple or pink, adding ornamental value to gardens. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Pennisetum petraeum is also valued for its ability to stabilize soil and prevent erosion in rocky or arid environments.
