Phoradendron amplexicaule

Phoradendron amplexicaule, commonly known as the mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches itself to the branches of host trees.

It lacks chlorophyll and relies on its host for water and nutrients, while also producing its own sugars through photosynthesis using light absorbed by its leaves. The plant has small, scale-like leaves and produces white or pink berries that are often consumed by birds. It is found in tropical and subtropical regions, including parts of the Americas.

Its life cycle involves seeds being dispersed by birds that eat its berries, leading to new growth on host trees.
