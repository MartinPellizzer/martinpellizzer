Pleioluma papyracea

Pleioluma papyracea, commonly known as the paperleaf papyrus, is a unique aquatic plant native to the wetlands of South America.

It features long, narrow leaves that are thin and papery, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in shallow, still waters and is often found in marshy environments. It has small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in spikes, and it plays a role in stabilizing wetland soils.

Due to its delicate structure, it is often used in ornamental water gardens.
