Pulsatilla grandiflora

Pulsatilla grandiflora, commonly known as the meadow anemone, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features a short, thick rhizome and produces showy, pink to purple flowers with a distinctive, fluffy center. The plant has palmately divided, lobed leaves that are hairy and often covered in fine white or grayish pubescence. It typically grows in moist meadows, woodlands, and along stream banks.

Pulsatilla grandiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its early spring blooms.
