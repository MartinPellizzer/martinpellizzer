Polygonum serotinum

Polygonum serotinum, commonly known as late polygonum, is a perennial herb native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in wetlands, stream banks, and along roadsides. The plant has alternate, ovate leaves with a hairy texture and produces small, clustered flowers in shades of pink to red. Its reproductive strategy involves wind pollination, and it is known for its rapid growth and ability to colonize open areas.

Polygonum serotinum is also valued for its role in erosion control and as a food source for various wildlife species.
