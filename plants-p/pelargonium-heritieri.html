Pelargonium heritieri

Pelargonium heritieri, commonly known as the Heritier's geranium, is a species native to South Africa.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, showy flowers. The leaves are typically rounded or heart-shaped, with a slightly hairy texture. It produces pink to purple flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

This plant prefers well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.
