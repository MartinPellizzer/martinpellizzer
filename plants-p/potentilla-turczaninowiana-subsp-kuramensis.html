Potentilla turczaninowiana subsp. kuramensis

Potentilla turczaninowiana subsp.

kuramensis, commonly known as Kuram potentilla, is a flowering plant native to the Caucasus region. It is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit and produces small, yellowish flowers.

The plant typically thrives in rocky or mountainous habitats, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are usually narrow and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its drought tolerance.

This subspecies is of interest to botanists due to its unique ecological adaptations and limited distribution.
