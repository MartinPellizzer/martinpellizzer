Piper pothomorphe

Piper pothomorphe, commonly known as the purple passion vine, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, purple flowers. The plant produces small, berry-like fruits that are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for botanical gardens and private collections.
