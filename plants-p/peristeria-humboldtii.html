Peristeria humboldtii

Peristeria humboldtii, commonly known as the Humboldt's moon cactus, is a striking plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its unique appearance, featuring a large, showy upper portion and a small, cylindrical lower stem. The plant produces vibrant, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, often blooming at night. Its thick, fleshy stems are adapted to store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments.

Peristeria humboldtii is also known for its slow growth and is often cultivated as a decorative indoor plant.
