Phytolacca dodecandra var. apiculata

Phytolacca dodecandra var. apiculata, commonly known as the African roselle, is a shrub native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, branching stems and large, heart-shaped leaves with a smooth, waxy surface. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers that develop into red, fleshy, berry-like fruits. The fruits are edible and commonly used in traditional beverages and culinary applications.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental and economic value.
