Phlomis × wendelboi

Phlomis × wendelboi, commonly known as the hybrid blanketflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of the Mediterranean.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its tall, upright growth habit and showy, daisy-like flowers. The plant produces yellow to orange composite flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its foliage consists of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often gray-green in color, providing a striking contrast to the vibrant flowers.

Phlomis × wendelboi is well adapted to dry, rocky soils and is often found in sunny, well-drained habitats.
