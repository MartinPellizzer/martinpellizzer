Piptolepis glaziouana

Piptolepis glaziouana, commonly known as the Glaziouana mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on the branches of host trees, primarily in tropical and subtropical regions.

It possesses small, scale-like leaves and produces small, white or greenish flowers that are often inconspicuous. The plant relies on its host for water and nutrients, while still performing photosynthesis to some extent. Its seeds are dispersed by birds that consume its berries, contributing to its propagation.

Due to its parasitic nature, it can have significant impacts on the health of its host trees.
