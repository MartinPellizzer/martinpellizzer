Polygala nemorivaga

Polygala nemorivaga, commonly known as the woodland milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in woodland and shrubland habitats, preferring moist, well-drained soils. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, spicate inflorescences with violet to blue flowers. Its seeds are enclosed in follicles that split open to release the seeds.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to pollinators.
