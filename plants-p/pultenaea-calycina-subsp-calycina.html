Pultenaea calycina subsp. calycina

Pultenaea calycina subsp.

calycina, commonly known as the pink burr-pea, is a shrub native to southern Australia. It is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-shaped flowers that range in color from pink to red.

The plant produces distinctive burr-like fruits that contain seeds dispersed by animals. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in heathlands and open woodlands. Pultenaea calycina subsp.

calycina is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting native wildlife.
