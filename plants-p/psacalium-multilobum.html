Psacalium multilobum

Psacalium multilobum, commonly known as the multilobed psacalium, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, lobed leaves that can vary in shape depending on environmental conditions. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
