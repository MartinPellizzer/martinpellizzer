Portulaca oleracea subsp. nitida

Portulaca oleracea subsp.

nitida, commonly known as the silverleaf purslane, is a succulent plant native to arid and semi-arid regions. It features fleshy, thick leaves that are covered in a fine, silvery pubescence, which helps reduce water loss.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the morning and close by midday. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and gardens.

Due to its adaptability and nutritional value, it is sometimes cultivated as a leafy vegetable.
