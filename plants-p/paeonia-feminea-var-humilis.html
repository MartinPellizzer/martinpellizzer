Paeonia feminea var. humilis

Paeonia feminea var. humilis, commonly known as the dwarf peony, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the eastern United States.

It features showy, single or semi-double flowers in shades of pink, white, and red, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has upright, hairy stems and heart-shaped, lobed leaves that are deeply veined. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers well-drained, fertile soil.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and relatively low maintenance requirements in garden settings.
