Persicaria chinensis var. siamensis

Persicaria chinensis var. siamensis, commonly known as the Siam knotweed, is a herbaceous perennial native to Southeast Asia.

It features upright, slender stems that bear opposite, heart-shaped leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, clustered flowers in shades of pink to red, typically blooming in late summer. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread rapidly, making it both a valuable ornamental and a potential invasive species in certain regions.
