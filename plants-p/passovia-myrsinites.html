Passovia myrsinites

Passovia myrsinites, commonly known as the silverleaf buckthorn, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its leaves are opposite and have a leathery texture, often with a waxy coating.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal habitats.
