Pultenaea ericoides

Pultenaea ericoides, commonly known as the coastal honey bush, is a shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It features small, dense clusters of yellow or golden-yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has narrow, leathery leaves that are typically arranged in a compact, upright habit. It is well adapted to coastal and sandy soils, often growing in dunes or on cliffs.

Pultenaea ericoides is valued for its ornamental value and its ability to attract native pollinators.
