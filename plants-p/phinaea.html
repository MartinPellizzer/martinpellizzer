Phinaea

Phinaea, commonly known as the golden bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, often with a distinctive golden hue. The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters, with lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. Phinaea belongs to the Campanulaceae family, which includes other bellflowers, and is characterized by its simple, tubular flowers.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.
