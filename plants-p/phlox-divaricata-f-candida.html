Phlox divaricata f. candida

Phlox divaricata f. candida, commonly known as the white woodland phlox, is a herbaceous perennial native to eastern North America.

It features upright, branching stems covered in dense clusters of small, showy flowers. The flowers are typically white or pale pink, with five petals and a distinctive red or pink throat. This cultivar is valued for its ability to thrive in partial shade and its role in supporting pollinators.

It is often used in woodland gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
