Pterocarpus cuneifolius

Pterocarpus cuneifolius, commonly known as the African rosewood, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its dark, aromatic wood and compound leaves with elliptical leaflets. The plant produces yellowish flowers followed by flat, pod-like fruits containing small, round seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in savannas and woodlands.

The species is valued for its timber and has traditional uses in medicine and crafts.
