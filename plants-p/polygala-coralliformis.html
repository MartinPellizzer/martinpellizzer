Polygala coralliformis

Polygala coralliformis, commonly known as the coral-pink milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its delicate, pinkish-white flowers that resemble small coral-like structures, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and is adapted to harsh, dry environments. Its leaves are small and often covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

Polygala coralliformis is a member of the Polygalaceae family, known for its diverse and often showy floral structures.
