Pulicaria crispa subsp. tomentosa

Pulicaria crispa subsp.

tomentosa, commonly known as woolly flaxweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and western Asia. It typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is characterized by its hairy, gray-green leaves and yellow flowers.

The plant has a compact growth habit and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas. It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appearance and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.

The subspecies tomentosa is distinguished by its dense, woolly foliage compared to other forms of Pulicaria crispa.
