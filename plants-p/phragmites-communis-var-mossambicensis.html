Phragmites communis var. mossambicensis

Phragmites communis var. mossambicensis, commonly known as the African common reed, is a variant of the common reed found primarily in southern Africa.

It is a tall, perennial grass that typically grows in wetland habitats such as marshes, lake shores, and riverbanks. The plant has long, narrow leaves and produces dense clusters of fluffy, feather-like flowers in the summer. It plays a crucial role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.

Its rhizomatous growth system allows it to spread rapidly and form dense stands.
