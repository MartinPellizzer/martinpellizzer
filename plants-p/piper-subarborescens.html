Piper subarborescens

Piper subarborescens, commonly known as the white pepper plant, is a climbing vine native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves with a glossy surface. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that develop into elongated, woody fruits. The seeds inside the fruits are harvested and ground to produce white pepper, a spice widely used in cooking.

It thrives in tropical climates and requires well-drained soil and partial shade for optimal growth.
