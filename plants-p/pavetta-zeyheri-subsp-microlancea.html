Pavetta zeyheri subsp. microlancea

Pavetta zeyheri subsp.

microlancea, commonly known as the narrow-leaved pavetta, is a shrub native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its small, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence.

The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically borne on short pedicels. Its flowers are hermaphroditic, containing both male and female reproductive structures.

The species is adapted to dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal and inland shrubland habitats.
