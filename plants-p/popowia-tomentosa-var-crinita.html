Popowia tomentosa var. crinita

Popowia tomentosa var. crinita, commonly known as the silky popowia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, silver-gray foliage and clusters of small, pale yellow to white flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine, soft hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in warm climates for its aesthetic appeal.
