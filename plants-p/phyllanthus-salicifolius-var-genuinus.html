Phyllanthus salicifolius var. genuinus

Phyllanthus salicifolius var. genuinus, commonly known as the Indian gooseberry or amla, is a shrub native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is characterized by its alternate, simple, and elliptic to ovate leaves that are often gland-dotted. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in axillary inflorescences. Its fruit is a spherical, yellow berry that turns red when ripe, containing a single seed.

This species is valued for its medicinal properties and is widely used in traditional medicine systems.
