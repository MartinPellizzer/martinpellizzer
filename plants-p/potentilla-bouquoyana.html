Potentilla bouquoyana

Potentilla bouquoyana, commonly known as the Bouquoy's cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun conditions. Its leaves are pinnately divided, and the flowers bloom in late spring to early summer.

Potentilla bouquoyana is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and alpine settings.
