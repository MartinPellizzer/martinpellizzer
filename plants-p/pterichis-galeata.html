Pterichis galeata

Pterichis galeata, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces clusters of small, sky-blue to white flowers. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full sun.

Pterichis galeata is often used in native gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
