Populus × gansuensis

Populus × gansuensis, commonly known as the Gansu poplar, is a hybrid species native to China.

It belongs to the Salicaceae family and is characterized by its fast growth rate and adaptability to various environmental conditions. The tree typically has a straight trunk and a broad, spreading crown, with heart-shaped leaves that are often hairy underneath. It thrives in temperate climates and is often used in reforestation projects due to its ecological benefits.

Populus × gansuensis plays an important role in preventing soil erosion and improving local biodiversity.
