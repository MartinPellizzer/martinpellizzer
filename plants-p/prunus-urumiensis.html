Prunus urumiensis

Prunus urumiensis, commonly known as the Indian cherry or wild cherry, is a small to medium-sized tree native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and small white or pinkish flowers. The tree produces small, round berries that are typically red when mature, containing a single seed. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in forested areas.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional medicinal uses.
