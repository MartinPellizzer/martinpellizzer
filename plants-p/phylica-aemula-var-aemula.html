Phylica aemula var. aemula

Phylica aemula var. aemula, commonly known as the silver tree, is a member of the Proteaceae family and is native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive silver-gray foliage, which results from dense, overlapping scales on the leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in rounded clusters, which are followed by bright red, woody fruits containing seeds. It is a hardy shrub or small tree that thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and is often used in ornamental gardening.

Its unique appearance and adaptability make it a popular choice for coastal and arid landscapes.
