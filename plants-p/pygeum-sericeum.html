Pygeum sericeum

Pygeum sericeum, commonly known as silky African chinquapin, is a small evergreen tree native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Proteaceae and is characterized by its smooth, silver-gray bark and dense, dark green foliage. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers followed by woody, spherical fruits containing several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is cultivated in some botanical gardens and conservation areas.
