Phacelia californica var. typica

Phacelia californica var. typica, commonly known as California phacelia, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas. It has hairy, lance-shaped leaves and can reach heights of up to 3 feet.

This species is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
