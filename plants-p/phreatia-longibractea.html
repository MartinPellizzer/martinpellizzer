Phreatia longibractea

Phreatia longibractea, commonly known as the long-bract orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its long, narrow leaves and distinctive, showy inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories. Its flowers are usually greenish-yellow with intricate patterns, and it produces capsule fruits containing numerous small seeds.

Phreatia longibractea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical gardens.
