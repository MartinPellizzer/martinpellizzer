Primula jucunda

Primula jucunda, commonly known as the alpine primrose, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, thriving in cool, moist environments. Its leaves are dark green, oval, and arranged in a basal rosette, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Primula jucunda is often cultivated in rock gardens and alpine settings due to its hardiness and aesthetic value.
