Parodia concinna

Parodia concinna, commonly known as the ghost cactus, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is a low-growing, mound-forming plant with cylindrical stems that are often covered in a whitish, powdery coating. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It is well adapted to arid environments, storing water in its thick, fleshy stems.

Parodia concinna is prized in horticulture for its unique appearance and hardy nature.
