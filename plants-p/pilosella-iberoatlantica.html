Pilosella iberoatlantica

Pilosella iberoatlantica, commonly known as the Iberian hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula and parts of North Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. Its leaves are hairy and arranged in a basal rosette, while its flowers are yellow with ray florets.

Pilosella iberoatlantica is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
