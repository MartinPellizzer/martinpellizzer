Polypodium vulgare var. americanum

Polypodium vulgare var. americanum, commonly known as the common maidenhair fern, is a species of fern native to North America.

It is characterized by its delicate, arching fronds that are typically light green with a glossy texture. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in woodland areas or along stream banks. Its sporangia are clustered on the undersides of the fronds, and it reproduces through spores rather than seeds.

This fern is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
