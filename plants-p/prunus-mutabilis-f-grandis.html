Prunus mutabilis f. grandis

Prunus mutabilis f. grandis, commonly known as the Whiteflower cherry, is a cultivated form of the Prunus mutabilis species.

It is characterized by its large, white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant is a deciduous shrub or small tree, typically reaching heights of 3 to 6 meters. Its leaves are ovate with a serrated margin and turn yellow in autumn.

The fruit is a small, yellowish drupe that ripens in late summer.
