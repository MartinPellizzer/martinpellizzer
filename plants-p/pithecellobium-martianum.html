Pithecellobium martianum

Pithecellobium martianum, commonly known as the Martian acacia, is a tropical shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces pods containing edible seeds that are often used in local cuisines. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to warm, humid climates.

Its ecological importance includes nitrogen fixation, which enriches the soil.
