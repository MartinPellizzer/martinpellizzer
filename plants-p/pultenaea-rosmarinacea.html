Pultenaea rosmarinacea

Pultenaea rosmarinacea, commonly known as the silver wattle, is a shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It features small, delicate yellow flowers that bloom in spring, often arranged in clusters along the stems. The leaves are narrow, linear, and covered in a fine silvery coating, giving the plant its characteristic appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and woodland habitats.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and flowering display.
