Passiflora pendens

Passiflora pendens, commonly known as the hanging passionflower, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, complex flowers that feature five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments. The plant produces edible, tropical berries that are often consumed raw or used in culinary applications. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped with a rough texture, and it thrives in warm, humid climates.

Passiflora pendens is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
