Pellaea atropurpurea var. bushii

Pellaea atropurpurea var. bushii, commonly known as the purple fern, is a striking evergreen fern native to the southeastern United States.

It features dark purple to red foliage that becomes more intense with age, giving it a dramatic visual appearance. The plant has a compact, clumping growth habit with fronds that are deeply divided and often curl inward. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for shaded garden areas.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its unique coloration and ability to form dense, attractive colonies.
