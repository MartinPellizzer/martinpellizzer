Papaver somniferum monstrosum

Papaver somniferum monstrosum, commonly known as the giant opium poppy, is a striking plant characterized by its large, showy flowers and robust growth habit.

It belongs to the Papaveraceae family and is native to regions of Europe and Asia. The plant features broad, heart-shaped leaves and produces large, cup-shaped blooms that can reach up to 10 inches in diameter. Its most notable botanical feature is the milky latex sap, which contains alkaloids such as morphine and codeine.

Due to its psychoactive properties, it is strictly regulated in many countries.
