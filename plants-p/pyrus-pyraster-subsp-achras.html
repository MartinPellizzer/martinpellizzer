Pyrus pyraster subsp. achras

Pyrus pyraster subsp.

achras, commonly known as the Spanish whitebeam, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically glossy and dark green on top.

The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers in the spring, which are followed by edible, round berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or scrubland habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in traditional medicine.
