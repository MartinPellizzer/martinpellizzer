Pinanga sylvestris

Pinanga sylvestris, commonly known as the wild bamboo or forest bamboo, is a species of plant belonging to the Araceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, arrow-shaped leaves. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine, and it plays a role in maintaining soil stability in its natural habitat.

Pinanga sylvestris is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated for decorative purposes.
