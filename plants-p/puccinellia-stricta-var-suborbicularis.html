Puccinellia stricta var. suborbicularis

Puccinellia stricta var. suborbicularis, commonly known as the narrowleaf saltbush, is a perennial grass species native to arid and semi-arid regions.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and small, suborbicular (nearly round) leaves. The plant is adapted to saline and alkaline soils, making it a valuable species for stabilizing degraded lands. Its inflorescence consists of dense, spike-like clusters of tiny florets.

This variety is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its drought tolerance and ability to thrive in harsh environmental conditions.
