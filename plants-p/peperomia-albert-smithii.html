Peperomia albert-smithii

Peperomia albert-smithii, commonly known as Albert-Smith's peperomia, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It features thick, waxy leaves that are often variegated with shades of green, white, and pink, adding visual interest to any collection. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or reddish, followed by spore-bearing structures known as sporangia. It thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Its unique foliage and low maintenance requirements make it a favored plant among succulent enthusiasts.
