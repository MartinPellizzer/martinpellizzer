Pyrus sargentaster

Pyrus sargentaster, commonly known as Sargent's pear, is a deciduous tree native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves with serrated edges. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by edible, sweet-tart pears in the fall. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open woodlands.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical significance in horticulture.
