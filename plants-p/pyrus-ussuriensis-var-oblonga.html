Pyrus ussuriensis var. oblonga

Pyrus ussuriensis var. oblonga, commonly known as the Ussuri pear, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its elongated, elliptical leaves that are typically dark green on top and lighter on the underside. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers in the spring, which are followed by edible, sweet fruits. Its fruit is often used in traditional Chinese medicine and culinary applications.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and adaptability to various soil types.
