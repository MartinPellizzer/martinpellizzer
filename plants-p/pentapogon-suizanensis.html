Pentapogon suizanensis

Pentapogon suizanensis, commonly known as the Suizan mountain grass, is a flowering plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the grass family, Poaceae, and is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit. The plant typically grows in rocky or mountainous areas, thriving in well-drained soils. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of spikelets, which are often pale green to brown in color.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endemic species.
