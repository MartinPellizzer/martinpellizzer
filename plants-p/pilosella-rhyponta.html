Pilosella rhyponta

Pilosella rhyponta, commonly known as the hairy hawk's-beard, is a flowering plant in the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it its common name. The plant produces bright yellow composite flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It typically grows in open, sunny habitats like meadows and grasslands.

Pilosella rhyponta is native to Europe and parts of Asia, and it is often found in disturbed soils.
