Pleurothallis barbosae

Pleurothallis barbosae, commonly known as the Barbo's orchid, is a species native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its compact growth habit and delicate floral structure. The plant typically produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and yellow, often with intricate patterns on the petals. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining, acidic soil for optimal growth.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and specialized orchid species.
