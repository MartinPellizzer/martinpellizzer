Polymnia nervata

Polymnia nervata, commonly known as the net-veined orchid, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, showy flowers with intricate veining patterns. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, often in forest understories. Its leaves are ovate to lanceolate, with a smooth texture and a waxy surface.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens.
