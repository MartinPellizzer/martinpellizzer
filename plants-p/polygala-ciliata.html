Polygala ciliata

Polygala ciliata, commonly known as the fringed milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its delicate, often pink or white flowers. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, such as grasslands and rocky soils. Its leaves are usually narrow and grass-like, while its stems are erect and branching.

Polygala ciliata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
