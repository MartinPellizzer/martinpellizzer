Perezia lactucoides

Perezia lactucoides, commonly known as the milk thistle, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its distinctive purple flowers and spiny leaves that resemble those of the thistle. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family and is known for its medicinal properties, particularly the use of its seeds in traditional medicine. Its latex-like sap is milky and can cause skin irritation.

Perezia lactucoides thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.
