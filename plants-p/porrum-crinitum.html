Porrum crinitum

Porrum crinitum, commonly known as the hairy gourd, is a climbing vine native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cucurbitaceae family, which includes other gourds and cucumbers. The plant is characterized by its long, hairy stems and large, heart-shaped leaves. Its fruit is a thick-walled, ribbed gourd that can grow up to 30 cm in length.

The hairy gourd is often cultivated for its edible fruit, which is used in various culinary traditions.
