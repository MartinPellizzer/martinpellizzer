Phegopteris cuspidata

Phegopteris cuspidata, commonly known as the northern shield fern, is a species of fern native to the northern hemisphere.

It is characterized by its large, shield-shaped fronds that can reach lengths of up to 1 meter. The fronds are typically bipinnate, with a distinctive pattern of lobes and sori arranged along the margins. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

The plant plays an important role in forest ecosystems by contributing to soil stability and providing habitat for various microorganisms.
