Pseuderanthemum acuminatissimum

Pseuderanthemum acuminatissimum, commonly known as the slender pseuderanthemum, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous annual or perennial, often found in shaded, moist environments. Its flowers are usually white or pale purple, and they bloom during the rainy season.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
