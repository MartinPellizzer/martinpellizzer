Pseudoselago quadrangularis

Pseudoselago quadrangularis, commonly known as the four-angle sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in spikes, typical of the Cyperaceae family. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies of wetland ecosystems.
