Patagonium confertum var. normale

Patagonium confertum var. normale, commonly known as the Patagonian thistle, is a perennial shrub native to the arid regions of southern South America.

It is characterized by its spiny, woody stems and dense clusters of small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant thrives in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to withstand harsh environmental conditions. Its leaves are typically narrow and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

This species plays an important role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat and provides shelter for various small animals.
