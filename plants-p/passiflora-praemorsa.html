Passiflora praemorsa

Passiflora praemorsa, commonly known as the death flower, is a climbing vine native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, complex flowers that feature five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments surrounding a central reproductive structure. The plant produces small, edible berries that are often used in local cuisines. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a rough texture, while its stems are woody and covered in tendrils.

Passiflora praemorsa is also valued for its ornamental beauty and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
