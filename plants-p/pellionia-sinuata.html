Pellionia sinuata

Pellionia sinuata, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a tropical flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its soft, velvety leaves that are typically dark green with a prominent central vein. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white or pale yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences. Pellionia sinuata thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental foliage and ability to tolerate partial shade.
