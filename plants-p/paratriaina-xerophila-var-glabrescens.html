Paratriaina xerophila var. glabrescens

Paratriaina xerophila var. glabrescens, commonly known as the smooth-leaved xerophyte, is a succulent plant native to arid regions.

It possesses thick, fleshy leaves that store water, enabling it to survive in dry environments. The plant's leaves are typically covered in a waxy cuticle, reducing water loss through transpiration. It produces small, clustered flowers that are often pale yellow or greenish in color.

This variety is well adapted to extreme drought conditions, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
