Plantago atrata subsp. holosericea

Plantago atrata subsp.

holosericea, commonly known as the silky plantain, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its basal rosette of broad, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs.

The plant produces erect, branched flowering stems that bear clusters of small, greenish flowers. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their soothing properties.

The species is adapted to grow in dry, sandy soils and is often found in grassland habitats.
