Phyllanthus aeneus var. cordifolius

Phyllanthus aeneus var. cordifolius, commonly known as the heart-leaved gooseberry, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It features dark green, heart-shaped leaves that are often glossy and arranged alternately along the stems. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, followed by elliptical, fleshy fruits that turn red when mature. Its botanical characteristics make it a notable species in horticulture and traditional medicine.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in its native habitats.
