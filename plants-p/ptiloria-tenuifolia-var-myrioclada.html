Ptiloria tenuifolia var. myrioclada

Ptiloria tenuifolia var. myrioclada, commonly known as the narrow-leaved ptiloria, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It features slender, erect stems that are often hairy, and its leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and have a slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale blue, blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grassy areas or along riverbanks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive member of the Acanthaceae family.
