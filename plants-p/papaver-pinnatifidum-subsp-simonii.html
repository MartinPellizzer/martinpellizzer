Papaver pinnatifidum subsp. simonii

Papaver pinnatifidum subsp.

simonii, commonly known as the Simon's poppy, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its striking, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from red to orange, often with a contrasting black or purple center.

The plant features finely divided, fern-like leaves that are typically arranged in a basal rosette. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
