Pollinia striata

Pollinia striata, commonly known as the striped pollinia, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its unique structure of pollinia, which are masses of pollen enclosed in a sticky matrix. The plant produces small, elongated flowers with intricate patterns that aid in pollination by specific insect species. Its botanical features include a sympodial growth habit and a preference for moist, shaded environments.

The plant is of interest to botanists and horticulturists due to its ornamental value and ecological significance.
