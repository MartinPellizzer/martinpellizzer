Pulsatilla rubra var. nana

Pulsatilla rubra var. nana, commonly known as the dwarf pasqueflower, is a low-growing perennial plant native to alpine and subalpine regions of Europe.

It features a rosette of hairy, spoon-shaped leaves that are typically bluish-green in color. The plant produces showy, nodding flowers with purple petals and a yellow center, blooming in early spring. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to cold climates.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is often cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
