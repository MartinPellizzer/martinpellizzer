Primula clusiana var. crenigera

Primula clusiana var. crenigera, commonly known as the serrated primrose, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of bell-shaped flowers. The leaves are typically oval with serrated edges, giving the plant its specific variety name. It produces showy, often pink or white flowers that bloom in early spring.

This plant prefers cool, moist environments and is often found in forested areas at higher elevations.
