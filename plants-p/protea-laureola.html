Protea laureola

Protea laureola, commonly known as the silver protea, is a striking South African plant belonging to the Proteaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescences that resemble a cluster of flowers, with a central cone-like structure surrounded by numerous petal-like bracts. The plant is native to the Western and Eastern Cape provinces, where it thrives in nutrient-poor soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink, with a distinctive, flattened, yellowish center.

Protea laureola is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for horticultural purposes.
