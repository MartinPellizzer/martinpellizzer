Podocarpus variegatus

Podocarpus variegatus, commonly known as the variegated podocarp, is a species of evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves with distinctive white or yellow variegation along the edges. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by cone-like structures that contain its seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage.

Podocarpus variegatus is also valued for its durability and is used in landscaping and reforestation projects.
