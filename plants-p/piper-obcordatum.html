Piper obcordatum

Piper obcordatum, commonly known as the Amazonian pepper or wild pepper, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species such as the common black pepper. The plant is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and small, greenish-white flowers that develop into red, peppercorn-like berries. It thrives in humid, shady environments and is often found in the understory of rainforests.

The plant's botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and traditional medicinal contexts.
