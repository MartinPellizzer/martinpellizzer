Potentilla maculata var. hirta

Potentilla maculata var. hirta, commonly known as the hairy cinquefoil, is a perennial flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it its scientific name. The plant produces showy yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy addition to gardens.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental value make it a popular choice for native plant gardens.
