Phalaris praemorsa

Phalaris praemorsa, commonly known as the death camas, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is a member of the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in dense spikes, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, wetlands, and along stream banks.

Due to its toxic properties, it should be handled with care.
