Phlox pilosa subsp. latisepala

Phlox pilosa subsp.

latisepala, commonly known as the broad-sepaled phlox, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink.

The subspecies is distinguished by its broad, lance-shaped sepals and hairy stems, which contribute to its ornamental value. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained, moist soils.

This plant is often used in gardens for its attractive blooms and ability to attract pollinators.
