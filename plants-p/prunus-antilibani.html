Prunus antilibani

Prunus antilibani, commonly known as the Antiliban cherry, is a species of shrub or small tree native to the eastern Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and small, white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, dark purple to black fruits, which are typically edible but not widely cultivated for commercial use. Prunus antilibani is often found in rocky soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural study.
