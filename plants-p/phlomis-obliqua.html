Phlomis obliqua

Phlomis obliqua, commonly known as narrow-leaved mullein, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features tall, upright stems that can grow up to 1.5 meters in height, covered with soft, fuzzy hairs. The plant produces showy, yellow flowers in clusters during the summer months, which are arranged in panicles. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a velvety texture, and it thrives in well-drained soils.

Phlomis obliqua is often found in rocky or dry habitats and is valued for its ornamental and medicinal properties.
