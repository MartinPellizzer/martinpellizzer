Prunus armeniaca var. rosea

Prunus armeniaca var. rosea, commonly known as the Rosa rugosa cherry or the rose-cherry, is a variant of the sweet cherry tree.

It is characterized by its ornamental pink to red flowers that bloom in spring, followed by edible fruits in summer. The plant is native to the Caucasus region and thrives in temperate climates. It has a compact, bushy growth habit and is often cultivated for both its aesthetic appeal and fruit production.

Its leaves are ovate with serrated edges, and the bark is smooth and reddish-brown.
