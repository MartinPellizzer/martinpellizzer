Piranhea securinega

Piranhea securinega, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a tropical plant characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in a fine, velvety texture.

It is native to the Americas and thrives in warm, humid climates. The plant produces small, inconspicuous green flowers that are followed by kidney-shaped seeds. It is often found in disturbed areas and can become invasive in certain regions.

Its rapid growth and adaptability make it a significant weed in agricultural settings.
