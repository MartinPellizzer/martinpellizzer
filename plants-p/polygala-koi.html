Polygala koi

Polygala koi, commonly known as the Japanese catchfly, is a flowering plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its delicate, often pink or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged alternately along the stem.

Polygala koi is valued in horticulture for its ornamental flowers and ability to thrive in partial shade.
