Packera fernaldii

Packera fernaldii, commonly known as Fernald's brittleblades, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant is perennial and typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are small and yellow.

It plays a role in supporting local pollinators and is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and ecological niche.
