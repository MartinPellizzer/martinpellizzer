Polystachya moniquetiana

Polystachya moniquetiana, commonly known as the Monique's spikegrass, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like appearance with elongated, linear leaves. The plant produces inflorescences of small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in spikes, typical of the genus Polystachya. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its unique morphology and limited distribution, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
