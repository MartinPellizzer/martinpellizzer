Panicum kappleri

Panicum kappleri, commonly known as the purple lovegrass, is a perennial grass native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and produces showy, purple-pink inflorescences that bloom in late summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves with a bluish-gray hue, and its stems are often reddish in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping.

Due to its drought tolerance and low maintenance requirements, it is widely used in gardens and as a ground cover.
