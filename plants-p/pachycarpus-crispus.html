Pachycarpus crispus

Pachycarpus crispus, commonly known as the crested or hairy pachycarpus, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its woody stems and dense, hairy foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its leaves are typically narrow and have a crinkled texture, contributing to its unique visual appeal.

Pachycarpus crispus is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive form and adaptability to various soil conditions.
