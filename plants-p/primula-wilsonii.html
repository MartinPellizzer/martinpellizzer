Primula wilsonii

Primula wilsonii, commonly known as Wilson's primrose, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in cool, moist environments and thrives in partial to full shade. Its foliage consists of broad, ovate leaves with a velvety texture.

Primula wilsonii is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to bloom in early spring.
