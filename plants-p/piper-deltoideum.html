Piper deltoideum

Piper deltoideum, commonly known as the delta pepper or wild pepper, is a climbing shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces tiny, peppery fruits that are often used in traditional medicine. Its botanical features include opposite leaves and a woody vine structure.

It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.
