Porophyllum ruderale subsp. macrocephalum

Porophyllum ruderale subsp.

macrocephalum, commonly known as the Mexican dandelion, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America. It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and robust, hairy stems.

The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are yellow with a distinctive central disc.

It produces a large, showy flower head that attracts pollinators like bees and butterflies.
