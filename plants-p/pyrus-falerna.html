Pyrus falerna

Pyrus falerna, commonly known as the wild pear, is a deciduous tree native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of white flowers that bloom in spring. The tree produces small, edible fruits that are typically harvested in late summer. Its bark is smooth and greyish-brown, with shallow fissures, and it can grow up to 10 meters tall.

Pyrus falerna is often found in woodland edges and moist soils, and it has historical significance in traditional medicine and as a food source.
