Pithecellobium cupirense

Pithecellobium cupirense, commonly known as the cupirense acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and birds. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The plant thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in dry or semi-arid regions.
