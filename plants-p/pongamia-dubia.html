Pongamia dubia

Pongamia dubia, commonly known as the slender pongamia, is a small leguminous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces elongated pods containing small, hard seeds that are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is adapted to poor soil conditions.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for ecological restoration and potential industrial applications.
