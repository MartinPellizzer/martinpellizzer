Pyrus transcaucasica

Pyrus transcaucasica, commonly known as the Caucasian pear, is a deciduous tree native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and attractive, glossy dark green leaves. The tree produces small, white flowers in early spring, followed by edible pome fruits that ripen in late summer. Its fruits are typically small to medium in size, with a sweet and aromatic flavor.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is also cultivated for its edible fruit in some regions.
