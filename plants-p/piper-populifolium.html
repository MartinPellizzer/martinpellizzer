Piper populifolium

Piper populifolium, commonly known as the broadleaf pepper or Chinese pepper plant, is a flowering vine native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piper genus in the family Piperaceae, which includes other pepper species. The plant is characterized by its heart-shaped, broad leaves and small, greenish-white flowers that develop into peppery berries. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine and as a culinary spice in some regions.

The plant thrives in humid, tropical climates and is typically grown for its aromatic fruits and ornamental foliage.
