Paeonia potaninii f. alba

Paeonia potaninii f. alba, commonly known as the white peony of Potanin, is a cultivar of the peony plant native to China.

It is characterized by its large, showy white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant features broad, dark green leaves and a robust root system typical of the Paeonia genus. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

This variety is valued for its elegant appearance and historical significance in traditional Chinese horticulture.
