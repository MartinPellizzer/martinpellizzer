Pedicularis daucifolia

Pedicularis daucifolia, commonly known as the meadow gentian, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Gentianaceae.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia, often found in moist, shaded environments. The plant features showy blue or violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, while its stems are erect and hairy.

Pedicularis daucifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
