Peperomia viridispica var. perejil

Peperomia viridispica var. perejil, commonly known as Spanish parsley, is a compact, low-growing perennial plant native to South America.

It features upright, lance-shaped leaves that are dark green with a slight waxy coating, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters, typically in shades of green or red, which appear in late summer. It thrives in well-draining soil and prefers bright, indirect light, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Its unique foliage and ease of care contribute to its appeal among plant enthusiasts.
