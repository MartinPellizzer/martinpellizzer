Pseudosasa owatarii f. albostriata

Pseudosasa owatarii f. albostriata, commonly known as the white-striped bamboo, is a unique variant of the original Pseudosasa owatarii species.

It is characterized by its striking white stripes on the culms, which contrast beautifully with its green foliage. This ornamental bamboo is native to Japan and is prized for its aesthetic appeal in garden settings. The plant grows as a clump-forming bamboo, reaching moderate heights with slender, hollow stems.

Its foliage is typically dark green, and it thrives in well-drained soil with partial to full sunlight.
