Polygonum × hervieri

Polygonum × hervieri, commonly known as the Chinese knotweed, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Polygonaceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of small, pinkish-red flowers that bloom in late summer. The leaves are alternate, ovate to lanceolate, and often have a reddish tint, especially along the veins. This species is native to East Asia and is often found in moist, disturbed habitats such as riverbanks and roadsides.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been introduced to other regions for horticultural purposes.
