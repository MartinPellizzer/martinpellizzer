Potentilla argentea f. csapodyae

Potentilla argentea f. csapodyae, commonly known as silver cinquefoil, is a flowering plant belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its silver-white, hairy leaves and showy yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Native to the mountainous regions of Central and Eastern Europe, this plant thrives in well-drained, rocky soils. It is often found in alpine meadows and dry grasslands, where it contributes to the biodiversity of its habitat.

The plant's ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden cultivation in suitable climates.
