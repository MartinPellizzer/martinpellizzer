Phlox alyssifolia f. sevorsa

Phlox alyssifolia f. sevorsa, commonly known as the dwarf phlox, is a compact, flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It features upright stems covered in lance-shaped, dark green leaves. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, pinkish-white flowers in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and ease of cultivation in gardens.
