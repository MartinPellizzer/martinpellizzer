Phucagrostis isoetifolia

Phucagrostis isoetifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved spike rush, is a flowering plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with slender, cylindrical stems and small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets. The plant typically grows in wet or marshy habitats, such as pond margins and wet meadows. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often with a distinct midrib.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
