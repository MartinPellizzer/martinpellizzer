Pycnandra litseiflora

Pycnandra litseiflora, commonly known as the flame tree, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy red flowers that bloom in clusters, giving it its common name. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets arranged in an alternate pattern. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The tree also plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various species.
