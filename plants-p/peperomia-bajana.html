Peperomia bajana

Peperomia bajana, commonly known as the Bajana pepper plant, is a species native to Central and South America.

It is a compact, low-growing plant with thick, waxy leaves that are typically dark green with a prominent midrib. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually green or red, followed by reddish or orange berries. It is well-suited for indoor cultivation due to its drought tolerance and ability to thrive in low-light conditions.

Its unique foliage makes it a popular choice for decorative purposes in both homes and offices.
