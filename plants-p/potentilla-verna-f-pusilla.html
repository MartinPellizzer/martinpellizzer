Potentilla verna f. pusilla

Potentilla verna f. pusilla, commonly known as the dwarf cinquefoil, is a low-growing perennial plant native to northern regions of Europe and Asia.

It features small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to yellow, blooming in early spring. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves that are typically hairy, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or meadow habitats.

Due to its compact size and ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate beauty.
