Patagonium axillare

Patagonium axillare, commonly known as the Patagonian rock fern, is a unique plant native to the southern regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact, evergreen foliage and ability to thrive in harsh, rocky environments. The plant has small, leathery leaves that are often clustered at the base of the stems, forming dense, mat-like growths. It produces spores rather than seeds, typical of ferns, and is well-adapted to low-nutrient soils.

Due to its resilience and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species in its native range.
