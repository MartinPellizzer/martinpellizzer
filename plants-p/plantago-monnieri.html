Plantago monnieri

Plantago monnieri, commonly known as lesser plantain, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It features a rosette of broad, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in dense spikes during the summer months. It is often found in disturbed soils, along roadsides, and in meadows.

Botanically, it is notable for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies for centuries.
