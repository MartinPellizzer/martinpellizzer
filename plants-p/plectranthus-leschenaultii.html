Plectranthus leschenaultii

Plectranthus leschenaultii, commonly known as the Leschenault's mint, is a perennial herb native to South Africa.

It features opposite, ovate to lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically violet or blue in color, arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and aromatic foliage.
