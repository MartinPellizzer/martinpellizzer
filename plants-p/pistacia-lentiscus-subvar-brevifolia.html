Pistacia lentiscus subvar. brevifolia

Pistacia lentiscus subvar. brevifolia, commonly known as the dwarf mastic tree, is a shrub or small tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dense, aromatic foliage and small, leathery leaves. The plant produces small yellowish-green flowers followed by hard, woody fruits containing a single seed. It is well adapted to dry, rocky soils and can tolerate harsh environmental conditions.

This subspecies is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in coastal areas.
