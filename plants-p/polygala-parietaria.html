Polygala parietaria

Polygala parietaria, commonly known as wall-polygala, is a flowering plant native to Europe, North Africa, and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Polygalaceae family and typically grows in rocky or sandy soils. The plant features small, delicate flowers that are usually pink or purple in color, arranged in clusters. Its leaves are alternate and often have a hairy texture.

It is often found in dry, open habitats such as walls, cliffs, and disturbed soils.
