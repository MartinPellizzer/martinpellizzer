Prunus mutabilis f. narcissiodora

Prunus mutabilis f. narcissiodora, commonly known as the pink-flowered cherry blossom, is a cultivar of the Prunus mutabilis species, which is native to Japan.

This variety is distinguished by its striking pink flowers that bloom in early spring, often preceding the emergence of leaves. The plant is a small to medium-sized deciduous tree, typically reaching heights of 8 to 12 meters. Its foliage is dark green and glossy, providing a striking contrast to the vibrant floral display.

The botanical features of this cultivar make it a popular ornamental tree in gardens and public spaces.
