Picrodendron macrocarpum

Picrodendron macrocarpum, commonly known as the giant picrodendron, is a striking tropical plant native to the rainforests of South America.

It is characterized by its large, leathery leaves that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in the late afternoon. Its fruit is a fleshy, red berry that contains several seeds.

Picrodendron macrocarpum is often found in humid, shaded environments and is valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture.
