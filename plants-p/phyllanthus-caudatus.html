Phyllanthus caudatus

Phyllanthus caudatus, commonly known as the cat's tail plant, is a flowering plant native to Africa, particularly South Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, characterized by its dense, compact growth habit. The plant features small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in dense, cat-like tufts, giving it its common name. Its leaves are usually small, elliptical, and have a slightly waxy texture.

Phyllanthus caudatus is also known for its ability to thrive in poor soils and its use in traditional medicine in some regions.
