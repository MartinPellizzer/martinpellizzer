Piper aequale var. variifolium

Piper aequale var. variifolium, commonly known as the variable-leaf pepper plant, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its diverse leaf shapes, ranging from elliptical to ovate. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by elongated, berry-like fruits that turn red when mature. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has some traditional medicinal uses.

The leaves are typically used in local cuisines and herbal remedies.
