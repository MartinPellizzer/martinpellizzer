Prunus incisa

Prunus incisa, commonly known as the Japanese cherry or Japanese white cherry, is a deciduous tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and produces small, white or pale pink flowers in early spring. The leaves are ovate with a serrated margin and turn a striking red or orange in the fall. The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.
