Plantago lamprophylla

Plantago lamprophylla, commonly known as the silverleaf plantain, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features erect, slender stems that bear lance-shaped leaves with a distinctive silvery underside. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in dense, spike-like clusters. Its leaves are covered in fine, silky hairs, contributing to its characteristic appearance.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, sunny habitats.
