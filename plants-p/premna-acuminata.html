Premna acuminata

Premna acuminata, commonly known as the Indian nettle or small-leaved silk cotton tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Lamiaceae and is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves that are often hairy on the underside. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale purple, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous small seeds. It is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along stream banks.

Its leaves are sometimes used in traditional medicine for their purported therapeutic properties.
