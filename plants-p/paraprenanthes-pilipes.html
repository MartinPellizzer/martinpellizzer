Paraprenanthes pilipes

Paraprenanthes pilipes, commonly known as the hairy pili palm, is a unique plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, clustered growth habit and long, slender leaves that are often covered in fine, hairy structures. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into clustered, berry-like fruits. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
