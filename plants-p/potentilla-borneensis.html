Potentilla borneensis

Potentilla borneensis, commonly known as Bornean cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the island of Borneo.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit. The plant features small, showy yellow flowers that bloom in the spring and summer months. Its leaves are typically compound, with five leaflets, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

Potentilla borneensis is often found in forest understories and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance.
