Prunus mutabilis subf. prospera

Prunus mutabilis subf.

prospera, commonly known as the common lilac, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States. It is characterized by its fragrant, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink to lavender, depending on the variety.

The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape and often have a serrated margin. Its fruit is a small, woody drupe that matures to a dark purple or black color.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly used in gardens and landscapes.
