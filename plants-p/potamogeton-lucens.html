Potamogeton lucens

Potamogeton lucens, commonly known as the shining pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in North America.

It has long, flexible stems that are often branched and bear small, narrow leaves arranged in whorls. The plant produces small, green flowers that are typically clustered in spike-like structures, and it is often found in shallow, slow-moving waters. Potamogeton lucens plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.

Its presence can indicate good water quality due to its sensitivity to pollution.
