Pedilanthus cordellatus

Pedilanthus cordellatus, commonly known as the candleplant, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean islands.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, candle-like inflorescences. The plant produces yellowish-white flowers that are clustered in dense, cylindrical spikes. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a rough texture.

Pedilanthus cordellatus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to grow in well-drained, sunny conditions.
