Podaechmea mexicana

Podaechmea mexicana, commonly known as the Mexican pineapple plant, is a member of the Bromeliaceae family.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of white, woolly hair. The plant produces striking, colorful inflorescences that can range from red to orange and yellow. Native to Mexico, it thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with high humidity.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and ability to produce small, edible fruits.
