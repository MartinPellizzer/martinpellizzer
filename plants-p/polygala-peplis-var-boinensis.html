Polygala peplis var. boinensis

Polygala peplis var. boinensis, commonly known as the Bornean milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia, particularly Borneo.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded forest understories, thriving in humid tropical environments. Its leaves are opposite and ovate, with a smooth surface, while its flowers are usually pink or white, featuring a unique structure with a prominent spur.

This variant is noted for its ornamental value and is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens for its delicate appearance.
