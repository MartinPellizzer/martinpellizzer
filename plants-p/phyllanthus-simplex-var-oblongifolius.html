Phyllanthus simplex var. oblongifolius

Phyllanthus simplex var. oblongifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaf sandmat, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa.

It features small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically arranged alternately along its stems. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, often blooming during the dry season. Its adaptability to harsh environments makes it a resilient species in degraded soils.

The plant is also valued for its potential medicinal properties in traditional herbal practices.
