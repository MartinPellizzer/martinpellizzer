Pilea minguetii

Pilea minguetii, commonly known as the Mexican basket plant, is a small, herbaceous perennial native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped, waxy leaves that are typically green with a slight variegation. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are surrounded by a spathe, a common feature in the Araceae family. It thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Pilea minguetii is known for its ability to propagate easily through stem cuttings, which contributes to its popularity among plant enthusiasts.
