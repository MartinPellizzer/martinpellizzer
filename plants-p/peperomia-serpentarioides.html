Peperomia serpentarioides

Peperomia serpentarioides, commonly known as the snake plant or mother-in-law's tongue, is a member of the Piperaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Central and South America. The plant is characterized by its upright, sword-shaped leaves that are often variegated with silver or white markings. It is a low-maintenance plant that thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil.

Peperomia serpentarioides is popular as a houseplant due to its air-purifying qualities and ability to thrive in a variety of indoor conditions.
