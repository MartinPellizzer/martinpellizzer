Peperomia amphitricha var. santa-rosana

Peperomia amphitricha var. santa-rosana, commonly known as the Santa Rosa peperomia, is a compact, low-growing plant native to the coastal regions of California.

It features dense, fleshy leaves that are typically dark green with a waxy texture, adapted to thrive in dry, sunny conditions. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually green or reddish in color, emerging from the leaf axils. It is well-suited for rock gardens or as a container plant due to its drought tolerance and minimal care requirements.

Its unique appearance and ability to thrive in arid environments make it a desirable choice for xeriscaping and succulent collections.
