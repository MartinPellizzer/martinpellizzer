Paeonia emodi f. glabrata

Paeonia emodi f. glabrata, commonly known as the Himalayan peony, is a unique botanical variant of the emodi peony.

It is characterized by its smooth, glabrous leaves and showy, single or double flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. Native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas, this plant thrives in cool, moist environments with well-drained soil. Its growth habit is typically upright, with woody stems that can reach heights of up to 1 meter.

The plant is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
