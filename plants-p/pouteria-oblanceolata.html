Pouteria oblanceolata

Pouteria oblanceolata, commonly known as the Brazilian starfruit or Natal apple, is a tropical evergreen tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and large, heart-shaped leaves that are glossy on top and pale underneath. The tree produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers, which are followed by fruit that resembles a starfruit in appearance. The fruit has a sweet, aromatic pulp with a distinctive flavor, and it is often consumed fresh or used in local cuisines.

This plant is also valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in its native habitat.
