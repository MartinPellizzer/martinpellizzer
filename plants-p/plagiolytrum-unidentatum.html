Plagiolytrum unidentatum

Plagiolytrum unidentatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved grass, is a species of grass native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typical of the Poaceae family.

Plagiolytrum unidentatum is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support local biodiversity.
