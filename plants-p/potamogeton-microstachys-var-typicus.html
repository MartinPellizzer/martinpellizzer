Potamogeton microstachys var. typicus

Potamogeton microstachys var. typicus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater habitats in North America.

It has long, narrow leaves that are typically arranged in whorls around the stem, and it produces small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant thrives in shallow, slow-moving waters and plays a role in water filtration and providing habitat for aquatic organisms. It is often found in ponds, lakes, and slow rivers, contributing to the ecological balance of these environments.

Its presence is an indicator of good water quality in its habitat.
