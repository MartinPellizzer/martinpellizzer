Poa williamsii

Poa williamsii, commonly known as Williams' bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States.

It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along streambanks. The plant has fine, hairless leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. Its flowers are wind-pollinated, and it is known for its ability to form dense, mat-like growth.

Poa williamsii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native plant gardens.
