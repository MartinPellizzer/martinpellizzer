Polygonum amphibium var. palustre

Polygonum amphibium var. palustre, commonly known as the marsh knotweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland habitats.

It features slender, erect stems that can grow up to 1 meter in height, often with a reddish tint. The plant produces small, greenish flowers in dense clusters during the summer months. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and have a slightly hairy surface.

It thrives in moist to waterlogged soils and is often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.
