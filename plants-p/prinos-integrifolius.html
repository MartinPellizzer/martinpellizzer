Prinos integrifolius

Prinos integrifolius, commonly known as the smooth-leafed prinos, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its simple, entire leaves that are usually arranged oppositely along the stem. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, clustered in inflorescences. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical features make it a subject of interest in both horticulture and ecological studies.
