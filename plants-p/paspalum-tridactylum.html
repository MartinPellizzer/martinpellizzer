Paspalum tridactylum

Paspalum tridactylum, commonly known as three-toed grass, is a perennial grass species native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, spikelet-based flowers that are typically green or brown in color. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in wetland or marshy environments.

Paspalum tridactylum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in supporting local wildlife.
