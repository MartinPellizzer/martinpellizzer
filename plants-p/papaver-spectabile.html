Papaver spectabile

Papaver spectabile, commonly known as the Shirley poppy, is a herbaceous perennial native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its showy, cup-shaped flowers that come in a range of colors including red, pink, white, and orange. The plant typically grows to a height of 2 to 3 feet and produces vibrant blooms from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture, while its stems are upright and often covered in fine hairs.

Papaver spectabile is well-suited for garden cultivation and thrives in full sun to partial shade.
