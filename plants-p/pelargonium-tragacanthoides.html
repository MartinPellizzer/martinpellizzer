Pelargonium tragacanthoides

Pelargonium tragacanthoides, commonly known as the rock pelargonium, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features fleshy, upright stems and produces small, pale yellow flowers. The plant is adapted to arid environments, storing water in its thick leaves and stems. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements make it a popular choice for rock gardens and drought-tolerant landscapes.
