Passiflora pilosicorona

Passiflora pilosicorona, commonly known as the hairy crown passionflower, is a climbing vine native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its distinctive, showy flowers that feature a crown-like structure of filaments around the reproductive organs. The plant has palmately lobed leaves and produces fruits that are typically orange or yellow when ripe. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and horticultural studies.
