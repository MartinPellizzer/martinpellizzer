Pavetta abyssinica var. cinerascens

Pavetta abyssinica var. cinerascens, commonly known as the ash-gray pavetta, is a shrub native to Ethiopia.

It is characterized by its slender, often branched stems that are covered in a grayish or ash-colored pubescence. The leaves are opposite, ovate to elliptic, and have a leathery texture with a bluish-green upper surface. The flowers are small, white or pale pink, and arranged in clusters at the ends of the branches.

This plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
