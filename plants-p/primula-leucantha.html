Primula leucantha

Primula leucantha, commonly known as the white primrose, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its small, showy white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are broad, heart-shaped, and covered in a fine layer of downy hair.

Primula leucantha is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate beauty.
