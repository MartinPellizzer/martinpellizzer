Phaseolus vulgaris var. purpureus

Phaseolus vulgaris var. purpureus, commonly known as the purple runner bean, is a variant of the common bean that is characterized by its striking purplish-pink flowers and edible pods.

This plant is a climbing vine that thrives in warm climates and requires full sun for optimal growth. Its leaves are heart-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture, while the pods are typically green and turn red when mature. The purple runner bean is valued for its ornamental appeal as well as its edible beans, which are rich in protein and nutrients.

It is often cultivated in gardens for both aesthetic and culinary purposes.
