Phaseolus ellipticus var. aureus

Phaseolus ellipticus var. aureus, commonly known as the golden scarlet runner bean, is a variant of the scarlet runner bean characterized by its striking yellow flowers and vibrant orange to red pods.

It is a member of the Fabaceae family, known for its leguminous properties and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant typically grows as a vigorous climbing vine, reaching lengths of up to several meters. Its foliage consists of heart-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture.

This variety is prized in gardens and for its ornamental value, as well as for its edible pods, which can be harvested when young and tender.
