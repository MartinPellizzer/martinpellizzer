Pyrus perlonga

Pyrus perlonga, commonly known as the long-fruited pear, is a species of deciduous tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, elongated leaves and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in early spring. The fruit is a distinctive, elongated pear with a thin, edible skin and a sweet, juicy pulp. It thrives in temperate climates and prefers well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value and unique fruit, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
