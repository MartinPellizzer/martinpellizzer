Pediocactus simpsonii f. muehlii

Pediocactus simpsonii f. muehlii, commonly known as the Simpson's cactus, is a compact, spineless cactus native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It features a short, cylindrical stem covered in dense, woolly white to grayish-blue hairs, which help reduce water loss in arid environments. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the spring and early summer, adding a striking contrast to its textured foliage. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it well-suited for xeriscaping.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and arid landscapes.
