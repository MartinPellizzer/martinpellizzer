Potamogeton obtusifolius f. apiculatus

Potamogeton obtusifolius f. apiculatus, commonly known as the apiculate pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater habitats in Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, rounded leaves with a distinct apiculate (bristle-like) tip, which distinguishes it from other forms of Potamogeton obtusifolius. The plant typically grows in shallow waters, forming dense underwater stands that provide habitat for various aquatic organisms. Its flowers are small and clustered in spike-like inflorescences, and it reproduces both sexually and vegetatively.

This plant plays an important role in maintaining water quality and supporting aquatic biodiversity.
