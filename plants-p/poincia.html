Poincia

Poincia, commonly known as the poinciana, is a tropical flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, fern-like leaves and showy, clustered flowers that range from pink to red. The plant belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. Poincia thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Its rapid growth and adaptability make it a popular choice for landscaping in tropical and subtropical regions.
