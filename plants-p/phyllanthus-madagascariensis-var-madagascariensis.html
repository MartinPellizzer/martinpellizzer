Phyllanthus madagascariensis var. madagascariensis

Phyllanthus madagascariensis var. madagascariensis, commonly known as the Madagascar spurge, is a shrub native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of yellow flowers, followed by small, fleshy berries that are typically red or orange in color. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and traditional herbal practices.
