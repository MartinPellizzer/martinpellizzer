Polygala transcaucasica

Polygala transcaucasica, commonly known as the Transcaucasian milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the Caucasus region.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, delicate flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and prefers full sun exposure. Its leaves are usually pinnately compound, and it produces pods that contain small, round seeds.

It is often found in mountainous areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture.
