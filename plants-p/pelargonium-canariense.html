Pelargonium canariense

Pelargonium canariense, commonly known as the Canary Island geranium, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of pink and purple. The plant has oval, slightly hairy leaves that are often reddish underneath. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and containers.

Pelargonium canariense is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
