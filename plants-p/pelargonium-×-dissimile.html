Pelargonium × dissimile

Pelargonium × dissimile, commonly known as the ivy-leaved geranium, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Geraniaceae family.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and heart-shaped, velvety leaves that resemble ivy. The plant produces clusters of small, two-lipped flowers in shades of pink, red, or white, typically blooming throughout the summer. It is widely cultivated as an ornamental plant for hanging baskets and containers due to its attractive foliage and extended flowering period.

Pelargonium × dissimile prefers well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it adaptable to various garden settings.
