Piper lucigaudens var. allenii

Piper lucigaudens var. allenii, commonly known as Allen's black pepper, is a variant of the black pepper plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dark, glossy berries that are used as a spice and in traditional medicine. The plant is a climbing vine with opposite, ovate leaves and small, greenish-white flowers. Its fruit develops into the aromatic, dark-colored peppercorns that are harvested when fully mature.

This variety is valued for its unique flavor profile and medicinal properties.
