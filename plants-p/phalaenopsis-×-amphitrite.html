Phalaenopsis × amphitrite

Phalaenopsis × amphitrite, commonly known as the hybrid phalaenopsis orchid, is a popular epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its long, gracefully arching inflorescences that bear clusters of fragrant, long-lasting flowers in various colors such as white, pink, and purple. The plant has pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, and its leaves are broad, leathery, and arranged in a fan-like pattern. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires moderate watering to avoid root rot.

This hybrid is prized for its ornamental value and ease of care, making it a favorite among orchid enthusiasts.
