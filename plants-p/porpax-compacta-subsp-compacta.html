Porpax compacta subsp. compacta

Porpax compacta subsp.

compacta, commonly known as the compact porpax, is a small, herbaceous plant native to specific regions in South America. It typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit.

The plant features small, narrow leaves arranged in a basal rosette, and it produces tiny, inconspicuous flowers that are often clustered together. It is adapted to arid environments and has developed mechanisms to conserve water.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
