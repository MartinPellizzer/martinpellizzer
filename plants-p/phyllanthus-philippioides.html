Phyllanthus philippioides

Phyllanthus philippioides, commonly known as the Philippine goosefoot, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often found in moist, shaded environments. The plant has alternate, simple leaves that are usually oval or heart-shaped with a rough texture. Its small, yellowish-green flowers are clustered in dense inflorescences, and it produces small, spherical fruits.

The plant is noted for its adaptability and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
