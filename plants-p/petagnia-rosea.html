Petagnia rosea

Petagnia rosea, commonly known as the rose petagnia, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its delicate, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has slender, branching stems and small, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens or container planting.

Petagnia rosea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its charming, fragrant blooms.
