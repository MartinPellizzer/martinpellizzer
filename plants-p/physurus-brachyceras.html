Physurus brachyceras

Physurus brachyceras, commonly known as the narrow-headed water lily, is a species of aquatic plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, floating leaves that provide habitat for various aquatic organisms. The plant's flowers are typically white or pink, with a distinctive, narrow calyx that gives it its scientific name. It thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, often in ponds and marshes.

Physurus brachyceras plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by supporting biodiversity and contributing to water quality.
