Peperomia hygrophila

Peperomia hygrophila, commonly known as the water peperomia, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its glossy, waxy leaves that are often variegated with shades of green, white, and silver. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink, adding a delicate touch to its appearance. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires consistently moist, well-draining soil.

Peperomia hygrophila is well-suited for indoor cultivation due to its compact size and low maintenance requirements.
