Piper augustum var. cocleanum

Piper augustum var. cocleanum, commonly known as the Cocleanum pepper plant, is a variant of the Piper augustum species native to Central and South America.

It is a climbing shrub characterized by its slender stems and heart-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into elongated, cylindrical fruits. These fruits are typically harvested when they turn a vibrant red or orange, indicating maturity.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its unique foliage and fruiting characteristics.
