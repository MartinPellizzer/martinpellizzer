Paneion buckleyanum

Paneion buckleyanum, commonly known as the Buckley's paneion, is a rare and unique plant native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, elongated leaves that often exhibit a deep green color with subtle variegation. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, blooming during the wet season. Its root system is fibrous and adapted to thrive in moist, shaded environments.

Due to habitat loss, Paneion buckleyanum is considered endangered and is of significant interest to botanists and conservationists.
