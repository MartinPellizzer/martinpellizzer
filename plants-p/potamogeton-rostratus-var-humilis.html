Potamogeton rostratus var. humilis

Potamogeton rostratus var. humilis, commonly known as the slender pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater ecosystems in North America.

It typically grows in shallow, slow-moving waters and has narrow, ribbon-like leaves that are often found in dense stands. The plant produces small, green flowers that are clustered in spike-like inflorescences, which emerge from the water surface. Its rhizomes form extensive underwater networks, contributing to sediment stabilization and providing habitat for aquatic organisms.

It plays an important role in maintaining water quality and supporting biodiversity in wetland environments.
