Potentilla hirta f. micrantha

Potentilla hirta f. micrantha, commonly known as the narrow-leaved potentilla, is a flowering plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is a shrub characterized by its upright growth habit and dense foliage. The leaves are typically narrow, elliptical, and covered in fine hairs, giving the plant a distinctive appearance. Its small, yellow flowers appear in clusters and bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

This plant is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
