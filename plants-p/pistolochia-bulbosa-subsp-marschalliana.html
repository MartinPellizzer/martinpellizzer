Pistolochia bulbosa subsp. marschalliana

Pistolochia bulbosa subsp.

marschalliana, commonly known as the marshmallow vine, is a flowering plant native to South America. It is characterized by its climbing habit and produces showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in clusters.

The plant has heart-shaped leaves and is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests or along streams. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.

The subspecies is distinguished by its slightly different leaf morphology and flowering patterns compared to other varieties of Pistolochia.
