Phlox bifida var. stellaria

Phlox bifida var. stellaria, commonly known as the star phlox, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It features showy, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves and a compact growth habit, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, though it can tolerate partial shade.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
