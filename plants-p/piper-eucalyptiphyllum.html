Piper eucalyptiphyllum

Piper eucalyptiphyllum, commonly known as the Eucalyptus pepper vine, is a climbing plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in spikes, followed by elliptical, yellowish berries. It is typically found in moist, tropical environments and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.

Its unique combination of eucalyptus-like scent and peppery flavor makes it a distinctive species within its genus.
