Panicum insulare var. leucophaeum

Panicum insulare var. leucophaeum, commonly known as the white-leaf panicgrass, is a perennial grass species native to certain parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a fine, white pubescence. The plant produces small, fluffy panicles of pale to whitish flowers, which are often mistaken for cotton or other similar materials. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along the edges of water bodies.

Due to its ornamental appeal and unique appearance, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
