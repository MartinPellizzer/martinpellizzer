Poa trivialis subsp. semineutra

Poa trivialis subsp.

semineutra, commonly known as the meadow grass or slender meadowgrass, is a flowering plant species belonging to the grass family Poaceae. It is a perennial herb characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, hairless stems.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Its leaves are narrow, flat, and often covered with fine hairs, typical of many grass species.

It is commonly found in moist meadows, woodland edges, and disturbed soils across temperate regions.
