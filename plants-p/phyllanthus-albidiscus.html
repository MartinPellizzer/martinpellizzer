Phyllanthus albidiscus

Phyllanthus albidiscus, commonly known as white-flowered phyllanthus, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant produces yellowish-green cyathia, which are cup-shaped structures typical of the genus. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or disturbed habitats.

Phyllanthus albidiscus is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in herbal medicine.
