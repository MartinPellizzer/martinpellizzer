Pulegium aromaticum var. erectum

Pulegium aromaticum var. erectum, commonly known as blue hyssop, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features slender, upright stems that are often covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular blue or purple flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its aromatic leaves have a mint-like scent and are often used in traditional medicine.

The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.
