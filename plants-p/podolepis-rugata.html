Podolepis rugata

Podolepis rugata, commonly known as the roughleaf sandmat, is a perennial flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its hairy, rugose leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. Its unique morphology and ecological niche make it an important component of its native ecosystems.

Podolepis rugata is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in xeriscapic gardens.
