Persea granulosa

Persea granulosa, commonly known as the rough-leafed avocado, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its rough, textured leaves and fragrant flowers. The tree produces small, yellowish-green fruits that are typically harvested when mature. Its botanical features include a thick, waxy cuticle on the leaves and a smooth, oblong fruit with a single large seed.

Persea granulosa is valued for its ornamental qualities and has some culinary uses in its native region.
