Peucedanum rubricaule

Peucedanum rubricaule, commonly known as red-hair angelica, is a herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces oval, dark purple to black seeds that are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive member of the Apiaceae family.
