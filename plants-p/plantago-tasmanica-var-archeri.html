Plantago tasmanica var. archeri

Plantago tasmanica var. archeri, commonly known as the Tasmanian plantain, is a perennial herb native to Tasmania, Australia.

It features lance-shaped leaves that are densely hairy on both surfaces, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in a spike, which bloom during the spring and early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories or along stream banks.

This variety is valued for its ecological role in supporting local wildlife and its historical use in traditional medicine.
