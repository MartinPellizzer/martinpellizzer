Piper rhombeum

Piper rhombeum, commonly known as the ovalleaf pepper or roundleaf pepper, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many species of pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and small, greenish-white flowers that develop into oval, peppercorn-like fruits. The fruits are typically harvested when they turn red, and they have a spicy, peppery flavor.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of spice in local cuisines.
