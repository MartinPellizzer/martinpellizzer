Pimpinella pastinacifolia

Pimpinella pastinacifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf cow parsley, is a flowering plant belonging to the Apiaceae family.

It is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in umbels. Native to southern Europe and parts of Asia, this plant thrives in temperate climates and well-drained soils. It is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and disturbed areas.

The plant has a mild, aromatic scent and is sometimes used in herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.
