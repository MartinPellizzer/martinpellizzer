Pseudosasa multifloscula

Pseudosasa multifloscula, commonly known as the Chinese bamboo or Japanese bamboo, is a member of the grass family (Poaceae).

It is characterized by its tall, slender culms that can reach heights of up to 3 meters. The plant produces panicles of small, greenish-white flowers in late summer, which are clustered in dense, fluffy inflorescences. Its leaves are long and narrow, with a smooth texture and a bluish-green hue.

Pseudosasa multifloscula is often cultivated for ornamental purposes and is valued for its aesthetic appeal in gardens and landscapes.
