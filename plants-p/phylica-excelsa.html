Phylica excelsa

Phylica excelsa, commonly known as the silver tree, is a distinctive shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray bark and dense, branched structure, which provides habitat for various animals. The plant produces small, red or yellow flowers that are pollinated by insects, followed by woody, cone-like fruits containing seeds. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its unique appearance.

Phylica excelsa is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in harsh climates.
