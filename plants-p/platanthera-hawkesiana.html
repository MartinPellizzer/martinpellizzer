Platanthera hawkesiana

Platanthera hawkesiana, commonly known as the Hawkes' lady's tresses, is a species of orchid native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green leaves and its striking, fragrant, white to pale yellow flower spikes. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and wet meadows. It is a perennial herb that produces small, tubular flowers arranged in a loose, spreading cluster.

The species is notable for its ecological importance as a nectar source for various pollinators.
