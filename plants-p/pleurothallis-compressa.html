Pleurothallis compressa

Pleurothallis compressa, commonly known as the compressed pleurothallis, is a species of orchid native to the cloud forests of Central and South America.

It is a small, epiphytic plant that typically grows on trees or rocks in humid, shaded environments. The plant features delicate, narrow leaves and produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink. It is known for its unique floral structure, with a short, narrow column and specialized pollination mechanisms.

Due to its habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native range.
