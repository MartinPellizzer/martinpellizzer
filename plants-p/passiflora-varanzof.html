Passiflora varanzof

Passiflora varanzof, commonly known as the Varanzo passionflower, is a tropical climbing vine native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, complex flowers that feature five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments surrounding a central cluster of stamens. The plant produces blue or purple berries that are edible and often used in local cuisines. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped with a velvety texture and a strong, aromatic scent.

Passiflora varanzof is valued not only for its ornamental beauty but also for its ecological importance in supporting pollinators and wildlife.
