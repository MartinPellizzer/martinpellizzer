Pithecellobium palmeri

Pithecellobium palmeri, commonly known as Palmer's evergreen acacia, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing ability. The plant features bipinnate leaves and produces yellow to orange flowers, which are attractive to pollinators. Its seeds are enclosed in woody pods, and the plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

It is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
