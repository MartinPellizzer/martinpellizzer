Physostegia virginiana rosea

Physostegia virginiana rosea, commonly known as the rose pink false dragonhead, is a perennial herb native to eastern North America.

It features square, hairy stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a serrated margin. The plant produces showy, two-lipped flowers in shades of pink to purple, typically blooming in late summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sun.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in native plant gardens.
