Planocarpa nitida

Planocarpa nitida, commonly known as the silky tamarind, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, pale bark and elongated, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by woody pods containing edible seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The leaves and seeds have been used in traditional medicine for their potential health benefits.
