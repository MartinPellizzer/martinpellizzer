Pyrus pluriflora

Pyrus pluriflora, commonly known as the Japanese white pear, is a deciduous tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its small, white flowers that bloom in clusters, giving it a delicate and fragrant appearance. The tree produces small, edible fruits that are typically harvested in late summer. Its leaves are broad and oval-shaped, with a smooth texture and a slightly serrated edge.

Pyrus pluriflora is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its aesthetic appeal and seasonal display of flowers and fruit.
