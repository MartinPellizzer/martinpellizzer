Phyllanthus peninsularis subsp. peninsularis

Phyllanthus peninsularis subsp.

peninsularis, commonly known as the Peninsular phyllanthus, is a shrub native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia. It typically grows in humid, shaded environments within lowland forests.

The plant features small, alternate leaves with a rough texture and a distinctive leaf venation pattern. Its flowers are unisexual, with male and female flowers occurring on separate plants.

The fruit is a capsule that contains several small, hard seeds.
