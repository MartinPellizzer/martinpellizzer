Psiadia viscosa

Psiadia viscosa, commonly known as sticky false dandelion, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its sticky, glandular hairs that cover its stems and leaves, giving it a distinctive texture. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a serrated edge.

Psiadia viscosa is often found in grasslands and disturbed soils, and it is known for its adaptability to various environmental conditions.
