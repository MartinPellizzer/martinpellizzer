Pilosella blaui

Pilosella blaui, commonly known as the blue pincushion, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and scrublands. Pilosella blaui is notable for its dense, woolly foliage and vibrant blue flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
