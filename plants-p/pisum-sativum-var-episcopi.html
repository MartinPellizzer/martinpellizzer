Pisum sativum var. episcopi

Pisum sativum var. episcopi, commonly known as the Easter cress, is a variety of the common pea plant.

It is characterized by its small, edible seeds and distinctive yellow flowers. This plant is typically cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of edible seeds. It thrives in temperate climates and prefers well-drained soil.

The variety is often used in traditional gardening and has historical significance in various cultures.
