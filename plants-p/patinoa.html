Patinoa

Patinoa, commonly known as the "southern bellflower," is a genus of flowering plants belonging to the Campanulaceae family.

It is native to the southeastern United States, where it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. The plant features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to blue or purple, depending on the species. Patinoa typically grows as a herbaceous perennial, often reaching heights of 1 to 3 feet.

Its foliage consists of lance-shaped leaves that are usually hairy and arranged oppositely along the stem.
