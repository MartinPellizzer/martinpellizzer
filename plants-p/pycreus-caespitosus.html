Pycreus caespitosus

Pycreus caespitosus, commonly known as the tufted grass.

It is a perennial grass species native to the temperate regions of the northern hemisphere. The plant is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. It typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides and meadows.

Pycreus caespitosus is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in supporting local wildlife.
