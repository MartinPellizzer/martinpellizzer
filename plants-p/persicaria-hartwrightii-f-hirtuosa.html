Persicaria hartwrightii f. hirtuosa

Persicaria hartwrightii f. hirtuosa, commonly known as the hairy persicaria, is a flowering plant belonging to the Polygonaceae family.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and hairy stems, which give it its specific epithet "hirtuosa.". The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in dense clusters, typically blooming in late summer. Its leaves are alternate, ovate to lanceolate in shape, and often have a slightly rough texture.

This perennial species is native to parts of Asia and is often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.
