Pisum sativum var. vitellinum

Pisum sativum var. vitellinum, commonly known as the yellow pea, is a variety of the common pea plant characterized by its yellow pods and seeds.

It is an annual legume that thrives in temperate climates and is cultivated for its edible seeds. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces tendrils that help it climb. Its flowers are typically white or pale purple, and the pods mature to a yellowish color.

This variety is valued for its nutritional content and is widely used in culinary and agricultural contexts.
