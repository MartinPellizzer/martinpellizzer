Pavetta indica f. montana

Pavetta indica f. montana, commonly known as the mountain pavetta, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South and Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub that typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often found in forests and along stream banks. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators. Its leaves are ovate to elliptic in shape, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
