Potamogeton × rivularis

Potamogeton × rivularis, commonly known as the hybrid pondweed, is a perennial aquatic plant native to temperate regions of Europe and North America.

It is a natural hybrid between Potamogeton crispus and Potamogeton przewalskii, exhibiting characteristics of both parent species. The plant has long, ribbon-like leaves that are often slightly curled and can vary in color from bright green to reddish in certain conditions. It typically grows in shallow freshwater environments such as ponds, lakes, and slow-moving streams.

Potamogeton × rivularis plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various water organisms.
