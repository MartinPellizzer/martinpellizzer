Pavonia leptocalyx

Pavonia leptocalyx, commonly known as the narrow-leaved wood rose, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its slender, often hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its botanical features include opposite leaf arrangement and bract-like calyces that enclose the flower buds.

Pavonia leptocalyx is adapted to moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full sunlight.
