Phyllanthus pentaphyllus var. floridanus

Phyllanthus pentaphyllus var. floridanus, commonly known as the Florida spurge, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a low-growing shrub characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves and small yellowish-green flowers that appear in clusters. The plant produces small, spherical fruits that contain seeds covered in a sticky substance. It thrives in sandy or disturbed soils and is often found in coastal areas and open woodlands.

The species is known for its rapid growth and ability to colonize disturbed sites, making it both a common weed and an ecological indicator plant.
