Ptychosperma cochinchinense

Ptychosperma cochinchinense, commonly known as the Vietnamese areca palm, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Arecaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by bright red, fleshy fruits. Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.

It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes and is adapted to warm, humid climates.
