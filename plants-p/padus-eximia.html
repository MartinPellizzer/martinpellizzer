Padus eximia

Padus eximia, commonly known as the white-flowered dogwood, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its showy, white bract-like flowers that appear in late spring. The plant has opposite, elliptic leaves that are typically dark green and smooth on top. Its fruit is a small, red drupe that ripens in the fall.

Padus eximia thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and stream banks.
