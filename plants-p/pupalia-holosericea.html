Pupalia holosericea

Pupalia holosericea, commonly known as the velvet tamarind, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, velvety, and often fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, round fruits that turn from green to red as they mature. Its leaves are typically palmate, with three to five lobes, and the plant is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.

Pupalia holosericea is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional medicine.
