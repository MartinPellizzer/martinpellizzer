Parasenecio rockianus

Parasenecio rockianus, commonly known as the Rocky Mountain groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils at high altitudes, often in alpine environments. Its leaves are usually basal, with a rough texture and a grayish-green color, while its flowers are yellow with pale lavender centers.

Parasenecio rockianus is adapted to cold climates and is often found in areas with limited soil nutrients.
