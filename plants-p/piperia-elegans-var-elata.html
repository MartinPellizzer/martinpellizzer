Piperia elegans var. elata

Piperia elegans var. elata, commonly known as the slender lady's tresses, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the orchid family (Orchidaceae) and is characterized by its delicate, grass-like leaves and fragrant, white to pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It produces small, erect spikes of flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
