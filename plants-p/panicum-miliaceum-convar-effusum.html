Panicum miliaceum convar. effusum

Panicum miliaceum convar. effusum, commonly known as millet grass, is a perennial grass native to warm temperate and tropical regions.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, hairless stems that often form dense clumps. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, with a bluish-green hue when young.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional agriculture for forage and grain production.
