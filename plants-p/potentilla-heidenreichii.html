Potentilla heidenreichii

Potentilla heidenreichii, commonly known as the Heidenreich's cinquefoil, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has alternate, simple leaves that are typically oval to elliptic in shape, with a rough texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine landscapes.
