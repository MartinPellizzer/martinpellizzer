Piper venustum

Piper venustum, commonly known as the sweet pepper vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species. The plant is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and small, fragrant flowers that are typically green or white. Its fruits are small, round berries that turn from green to red as they mature.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has some traditional medicinal uses.
