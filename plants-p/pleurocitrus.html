Pleurocitrus

Pleurocitrus, commonly known as the African lemon or sour orange, is a genus of flowering plants in the citrus family, Rutaceae.

It comprises several species, most of which are native to Africa and the Arabian Peninsula. These plants are characterized by their evergreen shrubs or small trees, with fragrant, glossy leaves and small, fragrant flowers. The fruits are typically citrus-like, with a thick rind and a sour, acidic pulp.

Pleurocitrus species are valued for their ornamental appeal and are also used in traditional medicine and as a source of essential oils.
