Poicilla ovatifolia

Poicilla ovatifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaf poicilla, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its ovate, glossy leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stem. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in clusters, which are often white or pale yellow in color. It thrives in humid, warm environments and is often found in lowland forests or near water bodies.

Its foliage is widely used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
