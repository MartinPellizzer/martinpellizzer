Phebalium lowanense

Phebalium lowanense, commonly known as the Lowan hakea, is a shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It belongs to the Proteaceae family and is characterized by its dense, woody structure and small, needle-like leaves. The plant produces clusters of bright yellow flowers, which are typically visited by birds and insects. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and vulnerable species.
