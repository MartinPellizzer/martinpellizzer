Plectranthus usambarensis

Plectranthus usambarensis, commonly known as the Usambara mint, is a flowering plant native to East Africa.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its aromatic leaves and clusters of small, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic and medicinal plants.

Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
