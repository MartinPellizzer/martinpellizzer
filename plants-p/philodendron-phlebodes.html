Philodendron phlebodes

Philodendron phlebodes, commonly known as the veined philodendron, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that often have a distinctive veining pattern, which gives it its common name. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its foliage is usually a deep green with a slightly waxy texture, and it can reach heights of up to 10 feet in suitable conditions.

Philodendron phlebodes is relatively low-maintenance, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.
