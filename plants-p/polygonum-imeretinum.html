Polygonum imeretinum

Polygonum imeretinum, commonly known as the Immeretinum knotweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Caucasus and Central Asia.

It typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils. The plant features erect stems that are often reddish in color, and it produces small, clustered flowers in late summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered with fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Polygonum imeretinum is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
