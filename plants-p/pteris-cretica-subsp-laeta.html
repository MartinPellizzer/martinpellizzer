Pteris cretica subsp. laeta

Pteris cretica subsp.

laeta, commonly known as the golden fern, is a compact, evergreen fern native to Southeast Asia. It features fronds that are typically bright yellow to golden green, with a distinctive, glossy appearance.

The plant has a rhizomatous growth habit and produces sporangia on the undersides of its fronds. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often used in ornamental horticulture.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for indoor and outdoor gardens.
