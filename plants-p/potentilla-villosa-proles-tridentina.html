Potentilla villosa proles tridentina

Potentilla villosa proles tridentina, commonly known as the tridentate cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and trifoliate leaves, which give it its common name. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or woodland habitats.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive member of the Rosaceae family.
