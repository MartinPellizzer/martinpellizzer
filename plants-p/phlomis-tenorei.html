Phlomis tenorei

Phlomis tenorei, commonly known as the Sicilian torchgrass, is a perennial herb native to the island of Sicily.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of bright yellow flowers. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in sunny, arid environments.

Phlomis tenorei is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to bloom throughout the summer months.
