Polemonium caeruleum subsp. occidentale

Polemonium caeruleum subsp.

occidentale, commonly known as western bluebeard, is a perennial herb native to the western United States. It features erect, branched stems that are often hairy, and its leaves are lance-shaped with serrated edges.

The plant produces striking clusters of blue to purple flowers in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

Its showy blooms attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
