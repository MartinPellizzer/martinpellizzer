Polygala lindheimeri var. eucosma

Polygala lindheimeri var. eucosma, commonly known as Lindheimer's milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the southern United States.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. Its flowers are usually pink to purple in color and are pollinated by insects, contributing to its ecological role in supporting pollinator populations.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in native plant gardens.
