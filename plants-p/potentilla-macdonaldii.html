Potentilla macdonaldii

Potentilla macdonaldii, commonly known as Macdonald's cinquefoil, is a low-growing perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It typically features wiry stems and small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has pinnately compound leaves composed of five to seven leaflets, which are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or rocky habitats.

Potentilla macdonaldii is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in harsh environments.
