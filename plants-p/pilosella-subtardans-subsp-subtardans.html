Pilosella subtardans subsp. subtardans

Pilosella subtardans subsp.

subtardans, commonly known as the slender pearly everlasting, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous tiny florets.

The plant features hairy stems and leaves, giving it a distinctive, soft-textured appearance. It produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in summer, attracting various pollinators.

This subspecies is often found in rocky or alpine habitats, where it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun.
