Piptadenia stipulacea

Piptadenia stipulacea, commonly known as the Brazilian trumpet tree, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 20 meters tall, with a straight trunk and a broad, umbrella-shaped canopy. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color, which bloom in clusters during the dry season. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a rough texture, while its fruit is a pod that splits open to release seeds.

Piptadenia stipulacea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often planted in tropical landscapes for its vibrant floral display.
