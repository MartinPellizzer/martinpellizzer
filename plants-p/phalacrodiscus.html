Phalacrodiscus

Phalacrodiscus, commonly known as the water cress, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its small, edible leaves that are often used in salads and as a garnish. The plant typically grows in moist, aquatic or semi-aquatic environments, such as stream banks and ponds. Its flowers are small and yellow, arranged in clusters, and it produces pods that contain several seeds.

The species is notable for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in nutrient-rich water conditions.
