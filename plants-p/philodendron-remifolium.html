Philodendron remifolium

Philodendron remifolium, commonly known as the silver lace philodendron, is a tropical climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, deeply lobed, heart-shaped leaves that often have a silvery or variegated appearance. The plant thrives in warm, humid environments with bright, indirect light. Its aerial roots allow it to climb and support itself as it grows.

Philodendron remifolium is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental foliage and relatively easy care requirements.
