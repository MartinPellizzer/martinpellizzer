Poa phleoides

Poa phleoides, commonly known as the narrowleaf meadowgrass, is a species of grass native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial plant that typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. The plant has slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its inflorescence consists of a loose cluster of spikelets, which contain small, wind-pollinated flowers.

Poa phleoides is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.
