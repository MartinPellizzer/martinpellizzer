Prunus wurdackii

Prunus wurdackii, commonly known as the Wurdack cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and showy, white to pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, dark red berries that are edible but often considered astringent. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and open woods.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and natural habitats.
