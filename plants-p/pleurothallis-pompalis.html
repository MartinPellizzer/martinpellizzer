Pleurothallis pompalis

Pleurothallis pompalis, commonly known as the golden pleurothallis, is a species of orchid native to the cloud forests of Central and South America.

It is a small, epiphytic orchid that typically grows on tree branches or rocks in humid, shaded environments. The plant produces delicate, fragrant flowers in shades of white, yellow, and green, often with intricate patterns on the petals. Its roots are adapted to absorb moisture from the air, and it thrives in high-altitude regions with consistent rainfall.

Due to its beauty and rarity, it is sometimes cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
