Penstemon thompsoniae

Penstemon thompsoniae, commonly known as Thompson's beardtongue, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces showy, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The plant typically thrives in rocky or well-drained soils and prefers full sun exposure. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

Penstemon thompsoniae is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
