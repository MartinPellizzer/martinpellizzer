Plantago bicallosa var. angustifolia

Plantago bicallosa var. angustifolia, commonly known as narrow-leaved plantain, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It features lance-shaped leaves with prominent veins and a dense covering of fine hairs on both surfaces. The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in a spike, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and along stream banks.

This variety is notable for its adaptability and is often used in traditional herbal medicine.
