Phyllanthus zeylanicus var. arborescens

Phyllanthus zeylanicus var. arborescens, commonly known as the tree phyllanthus, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Asia.

It features opposite, ovate leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, followed by spherical, fleshy fruits. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Its botanical characteristics make it a notable species in both horticulture and ecological studies.
