Persea carolinensis f. glabriuscula

Persea carolinensis f. glabriuscula, commonly known as the smooth-leaved avocado, is a variant of the avocado tree characterized by its reduced pubescence.

It is native to the southeastern United States, particularly Florida, and thrives in subtropical climates. The plant features broad, lance-shaped leaves that are typically smooth and glossy. Its flowers are small, greenish-white, and clustered in panicles, contributing to its ornamental value.

The fruit, while edible, is generally smaller and less commercially significant compared to the standard avocado variety.
