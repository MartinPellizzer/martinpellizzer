Perama plantaginea

Perama plantaginea, commonly known as the narrow-leafed everlasting or narrow-leafed daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid conditions. It has a long blooming period and is sometimes used in ornamental gardening.

Perama plantaginea is also known for its ability to thrive in full sun and its resilience to drought.
