Pimelea prostrata subsp. ventosa

Pimelea prostrata subsp.

ventosa, commonly known as the coastal banksia, is a low-growing shrub native to southern Australia. It features narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal heathlands.

Its hardy nature makes it suitable for coastal gardens and erosion control.
