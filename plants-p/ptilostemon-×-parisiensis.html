Ptilostemon × parisiensis

Ptilostemon × parisiensis, commonly known as the Paris hybrid ptilostemon, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Ptilostemon kirkii and Ptilostemon ramosus.

It is a flowering plant native to South Africa, characterized by its compact growth habit and attractive, fragrant flowers. The plant typically produces small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, often arranged in dense clusters. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Due to its ornamental value and unique botanical features, it is appreciated by horticulturists and botanists alike.
