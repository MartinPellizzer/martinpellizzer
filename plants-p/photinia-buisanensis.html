Photinia buisanensis

Photinia buisanensis, commonly known as the Bisanensis photinia, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to parts of Southeast Asia, including China, Japan, and Korea. The plant is characterized by its dense, glossy green leaves and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in the spring. It is often cultivated as an ornamental shrub due to its attractive foliage and compact growth habit.

The fruit it produces is a small, red berry that ripens in the fall.
