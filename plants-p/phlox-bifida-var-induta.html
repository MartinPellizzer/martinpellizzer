Phlox bifida var. induta

Phlox bifida var. induta, commonly known as the Sierra phlox, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its clustered, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes. It is often found in the Sierra Nevada range and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.

The species is also noted for its ability to thrive in well-drained soils and its resilience to drought conditions.
