Phlox pilosa f. albiflora

Phlox pilosa f. albiflora, commonly known as the white-flowered phlox, is a horticultural form of the common phlox species.

It is characterized by its dense, bushy growth habit and showy clusters of white or pale pink flowers. The plant typically blooms in early to mid-summer, producing fragrant blossoms that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained, fertile soil.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to a variety of garden settings.
