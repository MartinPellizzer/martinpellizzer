Piper fortunyoanum

Piper fortunyoanum, commonly known as the golden pepper plant, is a flowering vine native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes various pepper species. The plant is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into peppery fruits. Its fruits are used in traditional medicine and as a spice, similar to black pepper.

The plant thrives in humid, warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
