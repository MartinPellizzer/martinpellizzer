Potentilla chrysantha var. normalis

Potentilla chrysantha var. normalis, commonly known as the yellow cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun. It has compound leaves with five oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, fluffy seed heads after flowering.

This species is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
