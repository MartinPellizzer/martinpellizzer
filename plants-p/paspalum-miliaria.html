Paspalum miliaria

Paspalum miliaria, commonly known as the little barley grass, is a species of grass native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is a low-growing, perennial plant that forms dense mats, often found in wet or moist soils. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It is characterized by its ability to thrive in disturbed areas and its tolerance to saline conditions.

Paspalum miliaria is sometimes used in erosion control due to its robust root system and rapid growth.
