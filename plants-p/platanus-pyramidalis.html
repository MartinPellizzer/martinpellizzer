Platanus pyramidalis

Platanus pyramidalis, commonly known as the Mexican fan palm, is a deciduous tree native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and a broad, umbrella-like canopy composed of long, feather-like leaves. The plant has a distinctive pyramidal shape, which becomes more pronounced as it matures. It produces small, clustered flowers that develop into hard, woody fruits containing seeds.

This species is well adapted to tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
