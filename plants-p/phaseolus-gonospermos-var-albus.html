Phaseolus gonospermos var. albus

Phaseolus gonospermos var. albus, commonly known as the white kidney bean, is a variant of the common bean species native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its large, white to pale yellow seeds that are typically oval or kidney-shaped. The plant is an annual legume that produces pods containing the edible seeds, which are often used in traditional cuisines. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

The botanical features of this variety include compound leaves and a robust root system that aids in nitrogen fixation.
