Polygonum × asperulum

Polygonum × asperulum, commonly known as the velvetleaf or Chinese knotweed, is a hybrid species within the Polygonaceae family.

It is characterized by its stout, woody stems and heart-shaped leaves with a rough, textured surface. The plant produces small, clustered greenish-white flowers that are typically inconspicuous. Its growth habit is perennial, with a tendency to spread through rhizomes, making it an aggressive weed in agricultural settings.

Botanically, it is notable for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its adaptability to different climates.
