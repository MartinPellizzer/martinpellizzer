Pentalepis trichodesmoides subsp. trichodesmoides

Pentalepis trichodesmoides subsp.

trichodesmoides, commonly known as the coastal wattle, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of southern Australia. It is a shrub characterized by its yellow flowers and feathery foliage, which are adapted to withstand salty sea air and strong winds.

The plant has a woody stem and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain seeds. It is often found growing in sandy soils along the coastline.
