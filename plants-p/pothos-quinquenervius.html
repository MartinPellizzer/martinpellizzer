Pothos quinquenervius

Pothos quinquenervius, commonly known as the five-veined pothos, is a tropical vine native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, waxy leaves that have five distinct veins, giving it its scientific name. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by red berries, though it is primarily cultivated for its ornamental foliage. It thrives in humid, warm environments and is often used as a houseplant due to its adaptability.

Pothos quinquenervius is also known for its air-purifying properties, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.
