Polygala nikeliophila

Polygala nikeliophila, commonly known as the Nikeliophila milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of central Asia.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, delicate flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its flowers are usually pale green to yellowish in color, with a unique structure that resembles a bird's beak.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
