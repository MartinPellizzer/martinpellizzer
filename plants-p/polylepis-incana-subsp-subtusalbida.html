Polylepis incana subsp. subtusalbida

Polylepis incana subsp.

subtusalbida, commonly known as the white-flowered polylepis, is a tree species native to the Andean regions of South America. It is characterized by its distinctive white or pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters, adding visual contrast to its silver-gray bark.

The plant thrives in high-altitude environments, typically between 2,500 and 4,000 meters above sea level. Its leaves are narrow and leathery, adapted to withstand the cold and strong winds of its habitat.

The species is also notable for its ecological importance, providing habitat and food for various Andean wildlife species.
