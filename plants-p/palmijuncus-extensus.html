Palmijuncus extensus

Palmijuncus extensus, commonly known as the slender palm, is a rare and unique species native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant thrives in humid, tropical climates and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot. Its flowering occurs in clusters at the top of the trunk, producing small, fragrant flowers that attract pollinators.

Due to habitat loss, Palmijuncus extensus is considered endangered and is of great interest to botanists and conservationists.
