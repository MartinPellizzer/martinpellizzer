Poikilospermum suaveolens

Poikilospermum suaveolens, commonly known as the sweet-scented poikilospermum, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, often fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to several meters. Its leaves are usually simple and alternate, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

The flowers are often yellow or orange in color, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
