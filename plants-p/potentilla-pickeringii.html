Potentilla pickeringii

Potentilla pickeringii, commonly known as Pickering's cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in rocky or alpine environments. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are compound, consisting of five to seven elliptical leaflets.

Potentilla pickeringii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardiness and resilience.
