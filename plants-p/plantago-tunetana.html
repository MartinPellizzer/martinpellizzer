Plantago tunetana

Plantago tunetana, commonly known as the Tunisian plantain, is a perennial herb native to North Africa.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of broad, lanceolate leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in a spike, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in arid regions of Tunisia and neighboring countries.

The species is valued for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
