Pentaphylloides glabrata

Pentaphylloides glabrata, commonly known as the smoothleaf pentaphylloides, is a rare and unique plant species native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, clustered leaves that are smooth and glabrous, giving it its scientific name. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often in forest understories. Its flowers are usually small and inconspicuous, with a greenish-yellow color, and it produces capsule fruits that contain several seeds.

Due to habitat loss and limited distribution, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
