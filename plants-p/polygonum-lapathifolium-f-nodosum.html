Polygonum lapathifolium f. nodosum

Polygonum lapathifolium f. nodosum, commonly known as the knotted smartweed, is a variant of the smartweed species native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its erect, branching stems and alternate, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or red, arranged in dense inflorescences. Its root system is fibrous, and it often forms dense mats in wet or moist soils.

It is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and flowering display.
