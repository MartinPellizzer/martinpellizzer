Polypappus discolor

Polypappus discolor, commonly known as the palehead dandelion, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and milky sap. The plant typically grows in open, sunny areas such as meadows and roadsides. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow and produce wind-dispersed seeds.

The species is known for its adaptability and is often found in disturbed soils.
