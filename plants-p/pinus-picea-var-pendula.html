Pinus picea var. pendula

Pinus picea var. pendula, commonly known as the weeping spruce, is a decorative variant of the white spruce.

It is characterized by its cascading, pendulous branches that give it a unique, ornamental appearance. The plant has needle-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color, providing a striking visual contrast. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for ornamental gardens.

This variety is often used in landscaping for its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to various climates.
