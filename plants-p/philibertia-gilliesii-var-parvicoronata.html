Philibertia gilliesii var. parvicoronata

Philibertia gilliesii var. parvicoronata, commonly known as the small-capped philibertia, is a perennial herb native to the alpine regions of southern Australia.

It features a basal rosette of narrow, grey-green leaves that are covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in a compact cluster at the end of each stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to cool, sunny environments.

This variety is noted for its compact growth habit and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.
