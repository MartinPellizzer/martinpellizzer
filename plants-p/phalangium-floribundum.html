Phalangium floribundum

Phalangium floribundum, commonly known as the flowering phalangium, is a unique plant species characterized by its delicate, branching structure and clusters of small, showy flowers.

It belongs to the family Phalangiaceae and is native to specific regions with moist, temperate climates. The plant produces fragrant blooms that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically narrow and arranged in a spiral pattern along the stems.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
