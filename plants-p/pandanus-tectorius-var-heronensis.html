Pandanus tectorius var. heronensis

Pandanus tectorius var. heronensis, commonly known as the Heron's pandanus, is a variant of the common pandanus species native to tropical regions of the Pacific.

It is characterized by its stiff, narrow leaves that are often used in traditional thatching and basket weaving. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and wetlands, thriving in humid, saline conditions. Its inflorescence consists of a branched cluster of small, fragrant flowers, which are pollinated by insects and birds.

The fruit, a fibrous structure containing edible seeds, is also utilized in local cuisines and traditional practices.
