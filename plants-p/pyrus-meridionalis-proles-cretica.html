Pyrus meridionalis proles cretica

Pyrus meridionalis proles cretica, commonly known as the Cretan pear, is a rare and ancient variety of pear native to the island of Crete.

It is characterized by its small, sweet, and aromatic fruits that have a unique flavor profile. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in Mediterranean climates. Its leaves are oval-shaped with a glossy green surface and serrated edges.

This species is of significant botanical interest due to its historical and genetic value in the study of pear evolution.
