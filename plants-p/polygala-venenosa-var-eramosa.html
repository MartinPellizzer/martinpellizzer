Polygala venenosa var. eramosa

Polygala venenosa var. eramosa, commonly known as the poisonroot or false milkweed, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its delicate, often yellow or greenish flowers. The plant has slender, branching stems and small, opposite leaves. It is known for its toxic properties, containing various alkaloids that can be harmful to humans and animals.

Due to its toxicity, it is generally avoided as a food source and handled with caution.
