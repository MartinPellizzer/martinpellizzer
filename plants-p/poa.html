Poa

Poa, commonly known as bluegrass, is a genus of grasses native to the Northern Hemisphere.

These plants are characterized by their fine, tufted growth and often form dense, sod-forming vegetation. Many species of Poa are found in temperate regions and are adapted to a variety of soil types. They are often used in lawns and turfgrass due to their durability and aesthetic appeal.

The genus includes over 200 species, some of which are important for ecological and agricultural purposes.
