Pyrus longii

Pyrus longii, commonly known as the longii pear, is a species of flowering plant in the family Rosaceae.

It is native to the mountainous regions of central and southern China. The plant is characterized by its slender, often weeping branches and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in early spring. Its leaves are elongated and have a slightly serrated edge, contributing to its ornamental value.

The fruit, which is a small pear, is typically harvested in late summer and is noted for its sweet, aromatic flavor.
