Pleurostachys beyrichii var. panicoides

Pleurostachys beyrichii var. panicoides, commonly known as the panicled pleurostachys, is a member of the grass family (Poaceae).

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and dense, paniculate inflorescences that give it a feathery appearance. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas. Its leaves are narrow and linear, with a smooth surface and prominent veins.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its aesthetic appeal.
