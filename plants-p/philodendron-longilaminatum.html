Philodendron longilaminatum

Philodendron longilaminatum, commonly known as the Giant Shield Philodendron, is a tropical climbing plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 inches in length. The plant has a vigorous growth habit and often produces aerial roots to climb supports. Its foliage is typically a deep green with a velvety texture.

It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires consistent moisture and high humidity to flourish.
