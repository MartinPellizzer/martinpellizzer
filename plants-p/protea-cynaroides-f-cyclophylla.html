Protea cynaroides f. cyclophylla

Protea cynaroides f. cyclophylla, commonly known as the cyclophylla protea, is a striking South African plant belonging to the Proteaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescences composed of numerous papery bracts that resemble petals. The plant typically grows in coastal fynbos vegetation, thriving in well-drained, acidic soils. Its flowers come in shades of white, pink, and red, and it blooms during the summer months.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in horticultural displays.
