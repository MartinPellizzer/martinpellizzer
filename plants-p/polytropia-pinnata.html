Polytropia pinnata

Polytropia pinnata, commonly known as the warty begonia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive warty, rough-textured leaves and clusters of small, pink or red flowers. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous perennial, often reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters. Its leaves are pinnately lobed, giving it a unique and ornamental appearance.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is popular among gardeners for its visual appeal.
