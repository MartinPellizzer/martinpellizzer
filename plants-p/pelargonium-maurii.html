Pelargonium maurii

Pelargonium maurii, commonly known as the Cape geranium, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Geraniaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, five-petaled flowers. The plant typically produces bright red or pink blooms, which are borne on slender stems. Its leaves are usually rounded and slightly hairy, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Pelargonium maurii is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and vibrant floral display.
