Pholidota camelostalix

Pholidota camelostalix, commonly known as the golden spire plant, is a unique species native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can reach heights of up to 3 meters. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers clustered at the tips of its branches, which bloom during the summer months. Its leaves are narrow and covered in a fine layer of wax, helping to reduce water loss in its harsh environment.

The plant is adapted to extreme temperatures and is often found growing in rocky or sandy soils.
