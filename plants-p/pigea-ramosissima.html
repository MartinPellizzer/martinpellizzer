Pigea ramosissima

Pigea ramosissima, commonly known as the silverleaf pigeonwood, is a small to medium-sized tree native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, silver-green foliage that gives it its common name. The plant has compound leaves with multiple leaflets, and its flowers are small, white or pale yellow, arranged in clusters. It produces small, round fruits that are typically dispersed by wind.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and parks for its aesthetic qualities.
