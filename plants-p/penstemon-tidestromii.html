Penstemon tidestromii

Penstemon tidestromii, commonly known as Tidestrom's beardtongue, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of tubular, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has narrow, gray-green leaves that are arranged in opposite pairs along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy choice for xeriscaping.

Penstemon tidestromii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
