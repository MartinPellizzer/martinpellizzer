Phyllanthus comorensis

Phyllanthus comorensis, commonly known as the Comoro spurge, is a shrub native to the Comoro Islands in the Indian Ocean.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in dry, rocky soils.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and horticultural collections.
