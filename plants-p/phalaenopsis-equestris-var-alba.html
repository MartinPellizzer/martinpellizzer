Phalaenopsis equestris var. alba

Phalaenopsis equestris var. alba, commonly known as the white orchid, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its elegant, white flowers that resemble butterflies, hence its common name. The plant has broad, dark green leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern. It is a sympodial orchid, meaning it grows in a horizontal manner with new growths emerging from the base.

This variety is popular in horticulture for its long-lasting blooms and relatively low maintenance requirements.
