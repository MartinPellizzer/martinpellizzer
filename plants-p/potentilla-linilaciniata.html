Potentilla linilaciniata

Potentilla linilaciniata, commonly known as the pink potentilla, is a flowering plant belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, including parts of Kazakhstan and Mongolia. The plant is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of small, delicate pink to lilac-colored flowers. Its leaves are typically trifoliate, with three oval-shaped leaflets.

Potentilla linilaciniata is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
