Plectranthus leucanthus

Plectranthus leucanthus, commonly known as the white-flowered plectranthus, is a herbaceous perennial native to South Africa.

It features opposite, ovate leaves that are typically gray-green in color, covered in a fine layer of hair. The plant produces clusters of small, white, tubular flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Its aromatic foliage and ornamental flowers make it a valuable addition to both ornamental and medicinal gardens.
