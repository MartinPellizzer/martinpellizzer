Polygonum attenuatum

Polygonum attenuatum, commonly known as slender knotweed, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features slender, upright stems that are often reddish in color, and its leaves are lance-shaped with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. It produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters during the summer months.

The plant is part of the Polygonaceae family, which is characterized by its distinctive nodes and often edible seeds.
