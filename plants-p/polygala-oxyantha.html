Polygala oxyantha

Polygala oxyantha, commonly known as the sweet sarsaparilla, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia, including China, India, and Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its delicate, often fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and mountainous regions. Its leaves are alternate and usually have a heart-shaped base, while its stems are often hairy.

The fruit is a capsule that contains numerous small, smooth seeds.
