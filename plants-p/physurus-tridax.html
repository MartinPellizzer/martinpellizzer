Physurus tridax

Physurus tridax, commonly known as the horse chestnut, is a deciduous tree native to Europe, North Africa, and western Asia.

It is characterized by its large, palmately compound leaves and showy white or pink flowers that bloom in spring. The tree produces spiny, bur-like fruits that contain one or two seeds. Its bark is smooth and gray, becoming fissured with age, and it is often found in parks and along roadsides.

The plant has been cultivated for its ornamental value and historical use in traditional medicine.
