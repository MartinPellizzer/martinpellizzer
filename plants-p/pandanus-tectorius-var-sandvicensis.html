Pandanus tectorius var. sandvicensis

Pandanus tectorius var. sandvicensis, commonly known as the Hawaiian pandanus, is a species native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It is a tropical plant characterized by its tall, slender stems and long, narrow leaves that are often used in traditional Hawaiian thatching. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into spiky, woody fruits. Its fibrous leaves are utilized for weaving and construction purposes in local communities.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appearance and ecological role in coastal habitats.
