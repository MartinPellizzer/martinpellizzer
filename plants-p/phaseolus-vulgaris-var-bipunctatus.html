Phaseolus vulgaris var. bipunctatus

Phaseolus vulgaris var. bipunctatus, commonly known as the two-spotted bean, is a variant of the common bean species.

It is characterized by its distinctive two-spotted pattern on the leaves, which sets it apart from other varieties. The plant typically grows as an annual herb, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its pods contain small, edible seeds that are often used in culinary applications.

This variety is valued for both its ornamental features and its potential agricultural use.
