Phyllanthus thomsonii

Phyllanthus thomsonii, commonly known as the African spurge, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring simple, opposite leaves with a rough texture. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by distinctive, round, yellow fruits. It has a deep root system that helps it survive in arid conditions, making it a hardy species.

The leaves and stems contain milky sap, which can be toxic if ingested.
