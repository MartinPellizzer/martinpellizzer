Pseudoraphis longipaleacea

Pseudoraphis longipaleacea, commonly known as the long-paleated pseudoraphis, is a succulent plant native to arid regions of Africa.

It features cylindrical, segmented stems that are often ribbed and covered in a layer of white, powdery wax. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the rainy season. Its leaves are reduced to spines, a characteristic adaptation to minimize water loss in hot, dry environments.

Pseudoraphis longipaleacea is valued in horticulture for its unique appearance and drought tolerance.
