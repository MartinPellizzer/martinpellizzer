Plectranthus chiridensis

Plectranthus chiridensis, commonly known as the Madagascar verbena, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its square, hairy stems and opposite, ovate leaves with a rough texture. It produces small, clustered flowers in shades of violet or blue, which bloom throughout the growing season.

Plectranthus chiridensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is appreciated for its fragrant foliage.
