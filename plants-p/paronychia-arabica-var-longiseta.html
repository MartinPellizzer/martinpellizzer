Paronychia arabica var. longiseta

Paronychia arabica var. longiseta, commonly known as the long-hair daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and showy yellow flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are covered in fine, white hairs, which help reduce water loss in arid conditions.

The flowers bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
