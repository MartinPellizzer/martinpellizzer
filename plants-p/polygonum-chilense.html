Polygonum chilense

Polygonum chilense, commonly known as Chilean knotweed, is a perennial herb native to South America, particularly Chile.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and streambanks. Its stems are often reddish and covered with short, stiff hairs.

Polygonum chilense is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
