Poinsettia restiacea

Poinsettia restiacea, commonly known as the Christmas star, is a striking plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, colorful bracts that resemble a star, which are actually modified leaves rather than flowers. The plant belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is known for its ability to thrive in warm, sunny conditions. Its blooming season typically occurs during the winter months, making it a popular holiday plant.

The plant's unique appearance and seasonal bloom have made it a symbol of festive cheer in many cultures.
