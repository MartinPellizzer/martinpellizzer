Papaver caudatifolium

Papaver caudatifolium, commonly known as the long-leaved poppy, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features striking, showy flowers with bright red petals and a prominent, orange-colored pistil, typical of the Papaveraceae family. The plant has elongated, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers sunny to partially shaded environments.

Papaver caudatifolium is known for its ornamental value and is occasionally cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
