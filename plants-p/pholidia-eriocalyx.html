Pholidia eriocalyx

Pholidia eriocalyx, commonly known as the roughleaf mallow, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, rough-textured leaves and produces clusters of small, pale pink to white flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It is a perennial shrub that can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters.

The flowers bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
