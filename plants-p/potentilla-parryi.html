Potentilla parryi

Potentilla parryi, commonly known as Parry's cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It is a low-growing shrub that typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features small, yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, often in clusters of five. Its leaves are compound, consisting of five oval-shaped leaflets, giving it the common name "cinquefoil.".

Potentilla parryi is adapted to rocky and arid environments, making it a hardy species found in alpine and subalpine regions.
