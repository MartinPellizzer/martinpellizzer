Paspalum superbum

Paspalum superbum, commonly known as the tall paspalum, is a perennial grass native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typical of the Poaceae family. It thrives in moist to wet environments, often found in marshy areas or along riverbanks.

Paspalum superbum is valued for its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife species.
