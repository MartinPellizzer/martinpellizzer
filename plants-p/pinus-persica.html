Pinus persica

Pinus persica, commonly known as the Persian pine, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and dense, pyramidal crown. The needles are long, slender, and typically arranged in bundles of five, giving the tree a distinctive appearance. Its cones are woody and often remain attached to the branches for many years.

The tree is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often planted in gardens and parks for its aesthetic appeal.
