Poa novae-zelandiae var. wallii

Poa novae-zelandiae var. wallii, commonly known as Wall's New Zealand grass, is a perennial grass species native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and slender, bluish-green leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, two-lobed flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in riparian areas.
