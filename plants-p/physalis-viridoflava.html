Physalis viridoflava

Physalis viridoflava, commonly known as the green-flowered husk tomato, is a species native to South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, green calyx that encloses the fruit, which turns red when mature. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its fruit is typically small, round, and has a tart, tangy flavor.

It is often cultivated for both ornamental and culinary purposes.
