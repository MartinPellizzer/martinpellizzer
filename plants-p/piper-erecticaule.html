Piper erecticaule

Piper erecticaule, commonly known as the upright pepper plant, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its upright growth habit and small, elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by small, round berries that turn red when mature. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of medicinal compounds.

The leaves are used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties.
