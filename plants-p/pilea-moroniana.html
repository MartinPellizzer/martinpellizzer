Pilea moroniana

Pilea moroniana, commonly known as the Mexican mint plant, is a species of flowering plant in the family Urticaceae.

It is native to Mexico and is characterized by its aromatic, heart-shaped leaves that emit a mint-like scent when touched. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in umbels, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for shaded garden areas.

Pilea moroniana is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in traditional medicine.
