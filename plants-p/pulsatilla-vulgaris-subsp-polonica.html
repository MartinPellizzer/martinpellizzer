Pulsatilla vulgaris subsp. polonica

Pulsatilla vulgaris subsp.

polonica, commonly known as the Polish pasqueflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to central and eastern Europe. It is characterized by its distinctive crown of silky, white or pale purple flowers that bloom in early spring.

The plant has a short, thick rootstock and produces a single stem that is often covered in a dense layer of soft, woolly hairs. Its leaves are basal, forming a rosette and are covered with a similar fuzzy texture.

This subspecies is closely related to other members of the Ranunculaceae family and is known for its ornamental value in gardens.
