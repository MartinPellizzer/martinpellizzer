Picea wittmanniana

Picea wittmanniana, commonly known as the Wittmann's spruce, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes in South America.

It is characterized by its slender, pyramid-shaped crown and needle-like leaves that are typically dark green and slightly twisted. The tree produces small, cone-like structures that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is adapted to the harsh conditions of mountainous terrain.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
