Psittacanthus bergii

Psittacanthus bergii, commonly known as the golden star fern, is a species of epiphytic fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, feather-like fronds that are often adorned with golden spore clusters. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is typically found growing on trees or rocks in tropical rainforests. Its unique appearance makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

The plant's reproductive structures are small and clustered, contributing to its distinctive visual appeal.
