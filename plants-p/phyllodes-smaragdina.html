Phyllodes smaragdina

Phyllodes smaragdina, commonly known as the Emerald Phyllode, is a species of acacia native to Australia.

It is characterized by its elongated, leaf-like structures called phyllodes, which are adapted for water conservation in arid environments. The plant produces yellow flowers in spring, which are clustered in dense, spherical inflorescences. Its phyllodes are usually narrow and have a smooth, waxy surface to reduce water loss.

The species is often used in landscaping due to its hardy nature and attractive appearance.
