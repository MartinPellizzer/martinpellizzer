Philadelphus latifolius var. pubescens

Philadelphus latifolius var. pubescens, commonly known as the pubescent mock orange, is a deciduous shrub native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, hairy leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers in early summer, which are clustered in racemes. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its showy blooms and aromatic foliage make it a valuable addition to gardens and landscapes.
