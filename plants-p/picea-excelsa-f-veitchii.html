Picea excelsa f. veitchii

Picea excelsa f. veitchii, commonly known as the Veitch's white spruce, is a rare and distinctive variant of the white spruce.

It is characterized by its pale, almost white bark and dense, conical crown. The plant is native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas, where it thrives in cool, moist environments. Its foliage consists of needle-like leaves that are short and stout, providing a dense canopy.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique appearance.
