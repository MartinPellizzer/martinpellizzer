Pentanema varzobicum

Pentanema varzobicum, commonly known as the Varzob mountain heath, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its small, evergreen leaves and clusters of bell-shaped flowers. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, arid environments.
