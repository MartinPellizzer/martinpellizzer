Pleurothallis cardiostola

Pleurothallis cardiostola, commonly known as the heart-shaped orchid, is a species native to the cloud forests of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its small, intricate flowers that often display a range of colors from white to pink and purple. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees and deriving moisture and nutrients from the air and rain. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and arranged in a rosette pattern, aiding in water retention.

The blooming period of Pleurothallis cardiostola is often during the wet season, making it a striking addition to humid environments.
