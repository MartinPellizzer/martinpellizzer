Pentadesma ogoouensis

Pentadesma ogoouensis, commonly known as the African sandalwood, is a member of the Burseraceae family and is native to western Africa.

It is a small to medium-sized tree that typically grows in moist, lowland forests. The plant is characterized by its smooth, greyish-brown bark and elliptical, dark green leaves. Its small, yellowish-white flowers are followed by fragrant, resinous fruits.

The tree is valued for its aromatic wood, which is used in traditional medicine and as a source of incense.
