Populus × marilandica

Populus × marilandica, commonly known as the American poplar, is a hybrid species native to eastern North America.

It is a large, fast-growing tree that can reach heights of up to 30 meters. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a smooth margin and is characterized by its showy, fluffy seed heads that appear in the spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found near rivers and wetlands.

The tree is noted for its ecological importance, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
