Patagonium gracile

Patagonium gracile, commonly known as the slender Patagonian grass, is a flowering plant native to the southern regions of South America.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typical of grasses. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in open grasslands and along riverbanks.

Due to its adaptability, it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local wildlife.
