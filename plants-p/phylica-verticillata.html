Phylica verticillata

Phylica verticillata, commonly known as the mountain tea plant, is a flowering shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, spherical fruits that contain hard, black seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or mountainous regions.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is cultivated in gardens and used in traditional medicine.
