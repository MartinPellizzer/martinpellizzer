Pancratium caribaeum

Pancratium caribaeum, commonly known as the Caribbean torch flower, is a striking ornamental plant native to the Caribbean and parts of South America.

It is a member of the Amaryllis family (Amaryllidaceae) and is characterized by its vibrant orange or yellow flowers that resemble torches. The plant typically grows as a bulbous perennial, with strap-like, dark green leaves that emerge from a basal rosette. Its flowers bloom in late summer or early autumn, often reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters.

Pancratium caribaeum is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its dramatic floral display.
