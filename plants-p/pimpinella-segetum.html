Pimpinella segetum

Pimpinella segetum, commonly known as field parsley, is an annual herbaceous plant belonging to the Apiaceae family.

It is characterized by its finely divided, feather-like leaves and small yellowish-green flowers that appear in umbels. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and is often found in agricultural fields and along roadsides. Its seeds are small and dry, containing compounds that have been studied for potential medicinal properties.

Pimpinella segetum is also used as a forage plant for livestock due to its nutritional value.
