Phyllostachys aurea f. albovariegata

Phyllostachys aurea f. albovariegata, commonly known as the white-variegated bamboo, is a cultivar of the golden bamboo species.

It is characterized by its striking variegated leaves that display white, yellow, and green patterns. This bamboo is a clump-forming plant that grows in dense, upright stalks. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
