Polygonum ruscinonense

Polygonum ruscinonense, commonly known as the Ruscinonense knotweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, which is characterized by its swollen nodes and often succulent stems. The plant typically grows in moist environments such as marshes and wet meadows, where it thrives in nutrient-rich soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped with a smooth or slightly hairy surface, and it produces small, clustered flowers in late summer.

Polygonum ruscinonense is known for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
