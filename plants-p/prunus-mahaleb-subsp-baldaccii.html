Prunus mahaleb subsp. baldaccii

Prunus mahaleb subsp.

baldaccii, commonly known as the Balkan cherry or Mahaleb cherry, is a shrub or small tree native to the Balkan Peninsula. It is characterized by its small, oval leaves and clusters of white or pale pink flowers that bloom in spring.

The plant produces small, sweet, edible fruits that are similar to cherries but less commonly consumed. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in rocky or limestone soils.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
