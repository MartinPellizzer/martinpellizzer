Pediocactus rioverdensis

Pediocactus rioverdensis, commonly known as the Rio Verde cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of northern Mexico.

It is a small, globular cactus with a ribbed structure and spines that provide protection from herbivores. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, adding a striking visual contrast to its green body. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it well-suited for desert environments.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
