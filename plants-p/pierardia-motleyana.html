Pierardia motleyana

Pierardia motleyana, commonly known as the desert dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a low-growing perennial shrub characterized by its fleshy, succulent stems and leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating to reduce water loss. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. Its botanical features make it well adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments.

It is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal remedies.
