Pyrrocoma

Pyrrocoma, commonly known as the golden cup, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are evergreen throughout the year.

Pyrrocoma is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
