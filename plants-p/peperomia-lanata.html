Peperomia lanata

Peperomia lanata, commonly known as the velvety peperomia, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a velvety texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. It is a compact, low-maintenance houseplant that thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil.

Peperomia lanata is popular for its unique appearance and ability to purify indoor air.
