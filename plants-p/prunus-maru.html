Prunus maru

Prunus maru, commonly known as the Japanese cherry or mountain cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Japan.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its small, white or pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, round fruits known as cherries, which are typically red when mature. Prunus maru is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its beautiful flowers and is also used in traditional Japanese gardens.

Its leaves are alternate, simple, and oval-shaped, turning yellow in the fall.
