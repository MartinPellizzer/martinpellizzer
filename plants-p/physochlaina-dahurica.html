Physochlaina dahurica

Physochlaina dahurica, commonly known as the Siberian bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the cold climates of eastern Asia.

It features striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer, typically in clusters at the top of its slender stems. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy, and it thrives in well-drained soils. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty.

However, it contains toxic alkaloids and should be handled with care.
