Phyllanthus angkorensis

Phyllanthus angkorensis, commonly known as the Cambodian gooseberry, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves and small yellowish flowers. The plant produces small, round berries that are typically green when unripe and turn red when mature. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in moist, shaded environments.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has traditional uses in local medicine.
