Phlox unidentata

Phlox unidentata, commonly known as the slender phlox, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features upright, branching stems that produce clusters of fragrant, pink to lavender flowers in late spring to early summer. The plant has opposite, narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Phlox unidentata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
