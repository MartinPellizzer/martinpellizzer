Piper melula

Piper melula, commonly known as the Java pepper or black pepper vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its small, peppercorn-like fruits that are used in traditional medicine and cuisine. The plant produces small, green berries that turn red when mature, containing the aromatic seeds used as a spice. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a slightly glossy texture, while its stems are woody and often covered in tiny hairs.

Piper melula thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its medicinal properties and culinary value.
