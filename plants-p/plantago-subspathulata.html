Plantago subspathulata

Plantago subspathulata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf plantain, is a perennial herb native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in moist, grassy areas and has lance-shaped leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish flowers in a spike-like inflorescence, which are wind-pollinated. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their healing properties.

The species is characterized by its robust growth and adaptability to various soil conditions.
