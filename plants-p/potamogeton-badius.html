Potamogeton badius

Potamogeton badius, commonly known as narrow-leaved pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in North America.

It has long, narrow leaves that are typically arranged in whorls around the stem, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often submerged. The plant thrives in shallow, slow-moving waters and is often found in lakes, ponds, and wetlands. It plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.

Its rapid growth can sometimes lead to dense vegetation that affects water flow and quality.
