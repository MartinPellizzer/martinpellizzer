Platycentrum discolor

Platycentrum discolor, commonly known as the variable flat-leaf orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its flat, ribbon-like leaves and small, delicate flowers that often display a range of colors. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, with a distinctive pattern of darker markings.

Platycentrum discolor is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in greenhouse settings.
