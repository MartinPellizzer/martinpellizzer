Plantago sericea subsp. polyclada

Plantago sericea subsp.

polyclada, commonly known as the silky plantain, is a perennial herb native to parts of Europe and Asia. It is characterized by its silver-white, hairy leaves that are narrow and elongated.

The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in a dense spike, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, along stream banks, and in disturbed areas.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
