Phaseolus vulgaris var. glaucoides

Phaseolus vulgaris var. glaucoides, commonly known as the glaucous pole bean, is a variant of the common bean species.

It is characterized by its distinct glaucous (waxy, bluish-green) coating on the pods and leaves. The plant typically grows as an annual herb, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters. Its leaves are trifoliate, with each leaflet having a serrated margin.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and mild flavor, making it suitable for both decorative and culinary uses.
