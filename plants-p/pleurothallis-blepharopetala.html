Pleurothallis blepharopetala

Pleurothallis blepharopetala, commonly known as the hooded pleurothallis, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to the tropical regions of South America.

This epiphytic plant is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense, often moss-like foliage. It produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, arranged in a loose inflorescence. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining potting media to prevent root rot.

Due to its unique appearance and relatively easy care, it is a popular choice among orchid enthusiasts.
