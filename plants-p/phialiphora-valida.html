Phialiphora valida

Phialiphora valida, commonly known as the golden cup fern, is a member of the Polypodiaceae family.

It is a small, terrestrial fern characterized by its delicate, lace-like fronds. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rock crevices. Its reproductive structures, known as sori, are located on the underside of the fronds.

Phialiphora valida is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
