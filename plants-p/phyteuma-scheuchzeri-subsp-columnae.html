Phyteuma scheuchzeri subsp. columnae

Phyteuma scheuchzeri subsp.

columnae, commonly known as the Alpine bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the alpine regions of Europe. It features tall, slender stems that can reach up to 1.5 meters in height, often growing in rocky or gravelly soils.

The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, blooming in late summer. Its leaves are narrow and linear, arranged in a rosette at the base.

This subspecies is adapted to cold climates and is often found in mountainous habitats at high altitudes.
