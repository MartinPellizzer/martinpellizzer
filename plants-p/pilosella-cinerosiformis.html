Pilosella cinerosiformis

Pilosella cinerosiformis, commonly known as the sooty pilosella, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features a low-growing habit with hairy, grayish-green foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. Its flowers are small and yellow, typically appearing in clusters during the summer months.

Pilosella cinerosiformis is often found in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to thrive in sunny, well-drained environments.
