Plectranthus inflexus var. macrophyllus

Plectranthus inflexus var. macrophyllus, commonly known as the broad-leaf plectranthus, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving them a velvety texture. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically purple or violet in color, arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires moderate to bright light, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

This variety is often used in ornamental horticulture for its attractive foliage and ease of cultivation.
