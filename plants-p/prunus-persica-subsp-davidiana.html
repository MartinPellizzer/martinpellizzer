Prunus persica subsp. davidiana

Prunus persica subsp.

davidiana, commonly known as David's peach, is a botanical variant of the peach tree characterized by its distinct morphological features. It is native to China and is typically found in mountainous regions with a temperate climate.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by the development of small, round fruits. The leaves are simple, alternate, and have a slightly serrated margin.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and is also used in horticultural breeding programs.
