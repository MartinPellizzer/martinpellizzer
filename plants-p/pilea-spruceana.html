Pilea spruceana

Pilea spruceana, commonly known as the Chinese money plant, is a compact, evergreen perennial native to China.

It features rosettes of rounded, waxy leaves that are typically bright green with a slight sheen. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters, often followed by pale green or white berries. It thrives in indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular houseplant.

Its foliage is often used in traditional Chinese medicine for its purported health benefits.
