Peperomia vanoverberghii

Peperomia vanoverberghii, commonly known as the Vanoverbergh's peperomia, is a compact, evergreen perennial native to the rainforests of Ecuador.

It features glossy, ovate to lance-shaped leaves that are usually dark green with a reddish or maroon underside. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers arranged in dense clusters, which are typically borne on short, erect spikes. It thrives in bright, indirect light and prefers well-draining soil with moderate humidity.

Peperomia vanoverberghii is popular as a houseplant due to its attractive foliage and low maintenance requirements.
