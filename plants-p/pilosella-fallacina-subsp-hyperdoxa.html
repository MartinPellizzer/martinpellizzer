Pilosella fallacina subsp. hyperdoxa

Pilosella fallacina subsp.

hyperdoxa, commonly known as the alpine hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It is a perennial herb that typically grows in open, rocky, or alpine habitats.

The plant features a low-growing, cushion-like form with dense clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers. Its leaves are hairy and often have a slightly serrated edge.

It is part of the Asteraceae family, known for its composite flower heads and medicinal properties in some species.
