Plantago coronopus subvar. angusta

Plantago coronopus subvar. angusta, commonly known as narrow-leaved plantain, is a perennial herb native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils, often found in meadows, grasslands, and along riverbanks. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a distinctive waxy coating, and it produces small, greenish flowers in a dense spike. It is characterized by its narrow leaves and reduced flower clusters compared to the typical variety.

This subspecies is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional herbal remedies.
