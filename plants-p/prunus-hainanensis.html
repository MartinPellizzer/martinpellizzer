Prunus hainanensis

Prunus hainanensis, commonly known as Hainan cherry, is a deciduous tree native to the island of Hainan in China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its small, white flowers that bloom in early spring. The tree produces small, round fruits that are typically red or yellow when ripe. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a serrated margin.

Prunus hainanensis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
