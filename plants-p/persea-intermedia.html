Persea intermedia

Persea intermedia, commonly known as the Mexican persimmon, is a species of flowering plant in the family Lauraceae.

It is native to Mexico and is characterized by its evergreen leaves and fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, orange to reddish fruits that are edible when fully mature. The fruit has a sweet, honey-like flavor and is often consumed fresh or used in culinary applications.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
