Piper colonum

Piper colonum, commonly known as the climbing pepper vine, is a tropical vine belonging to the Piperaceae family.

It is native to Central and South America and is characterized by its climbing habit and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are followed by elongated, slender berries. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their aromatic properties.

The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
