Polygonum lanigerum var. subciliatum

Polygonum lanigerum var. subciliatum, commonly known as the woolly knotweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to temperate regions of Asia.

It features erect, branching stems that are covered in soft, woolly hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The leaves are ovate to elliptical, with a serrated margin and a hairy underside. It produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters, which are typically arranged in racemes.

This plant is often found in moist, disturbed soils and is adaptable to a variety of growing conditions.
