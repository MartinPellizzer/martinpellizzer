Pectis angustifolia var. angustifolia

Pectis angustifolia var. angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf pectis, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in arid or semi-arid environments. Its narrow, linear leaves are arranged alternately along the stem, and it produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the summer.

The species is well adapted to drought conditions, making it a resilient plant in its native habitat.
