Piper petens

Piper petens, commonly known as the creeping pepper vine, is a climbing shrub native to tropical regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes various species of pepper plants. The plant features heart-shaped, glossy leaves and produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into elongated, peppery berries. Its botanical characteristics include a woody stem with adventitious roots and a preference for moist, shaded environments.

Piper petens is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties.
