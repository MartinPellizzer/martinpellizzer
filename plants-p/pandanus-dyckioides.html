Pandanus dyckioides

Pandanus dyckioides, commonly known as the hala tree, is a tropical plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It is a member of the Pandanaceae family and is characterized by its large, feather-like leaves that can grow up to several meters in length. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically clustered in spikes, followed by edible fruits resembling pineapples. Its roots and leaves are used in traditional medicine and for making thatch and baskets.

The hala tree plays an important ecological role in coastal and wetland environments.
