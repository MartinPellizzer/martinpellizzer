Ptaeroxylon obliquum

Ptaeroxylon obliquum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved ironwood, is a hardy, evergreen shrub or small tree native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is notable for its exceptionally hard wood, which is highly valued for its durability and resistance to decay. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand drought and high temperatures.

Ptaeroxylon obliquum plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
