Potentilla flexuosa

Potentilla flexuosa, commonly known as the shrub cinquefoil, is a flowering plant belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is native to regions of Asia, including China, Japan, and Korea, and thrives in temperate climates. The plant is characterized by its upright growth habit, with slender stems that often form a dense shrub. Its leaves are pinnately compound, typically consisting of five to seven oval-shaped leaflets.

The showy yellow flowers, which appear in late spring to early summer, are clustered in corymbs and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
