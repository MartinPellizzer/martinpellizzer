Pilosella decolorans

Pilosella decolorans, commonly known as the pale pearly everlasting, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant features grayish-green, woolly leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in summer. It is often found in dry, open habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes.

Pilosella decolorans is known for its resilience and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
