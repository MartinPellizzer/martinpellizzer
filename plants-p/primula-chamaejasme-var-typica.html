Primula chamaejasme var. typica

Primula chamaejasme var. typica, commonly known as the alpine primrose, is a perennial herb native to mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils and cool climates. The plant features a low-growing rosette of oval, dark green leaves and produces clusters of bell-shaped, pink or white flowers in early spring. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its hardiness and beauty.

The species is also notable for its ecological role in supporting pollinators in alpine ecosystems.
