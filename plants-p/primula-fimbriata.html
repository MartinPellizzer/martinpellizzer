Primula fimbriata

Primula fimbriata, commonly known as the fringed primrose, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of China.

It is characterized by its delicate, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow. The plant typically grows in rocky or forested areas, thriving in cool, moist environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in a fine, fuzzy layer.

Primula fimbriata is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its early spring blooms.
