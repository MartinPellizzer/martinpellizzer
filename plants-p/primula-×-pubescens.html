Primula × pubescens

Primula × pubescens, commonly known as the woolly primrose, is a hybrid species within the Primulaceae family.

It is characterized by its fuzzy, hairy leaves and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. Native to the mountainous regions of Asia, this plant thrives in cool, moist environments. It is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to bloom in early spring.

The plant's foliage and flowers are both attractive and contribute to its popularity among horticulturists.
