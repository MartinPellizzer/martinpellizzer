Potentilla tommasiniana var. typica

Potentilla tommasiniana var. typica, commonly known as the alpine cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial shrub characterized by its finely divided, silvery-gray leaves and clusters of bright yellow flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments. Its flowers typically bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in harsh conditions.
