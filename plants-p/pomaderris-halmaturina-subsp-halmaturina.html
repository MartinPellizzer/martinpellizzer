Pomaderris halmaturina subsp. halmaturina

Pomaderris halmaturina subsp.

halmaturina, commonly known as the Halmaturina pomaderris, is a shrub native to the arid regions of South Australia. It is characterized by its small, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers clustered in racemes, typically blooming in spring. It is well adapted to dry conditions, with a deep root system that allows it to survive in nutrient-poor soils.

This subspecies is of interest to botanists due to its unique ecological role in its native habitat.
