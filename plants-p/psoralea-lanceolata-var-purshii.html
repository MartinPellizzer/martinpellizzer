Psoralea lanceolata var. purshii

Psoralea lanceolata var. purshii, commonly known as the narrowleaf psoralea, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in racemes, which are typically borne on the upper part of the stems. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas, and is often found in the western United States.

The species is noted for its ecological importance as a food source for various pollinators and its role in nitrogen fixation.
