Pteris cretica

Pteris cretica, commonly known as the crested fern, is a species of fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, fan-shaped fronds with a wavy, crested appearance. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its rhizomes are creeping and help in the spread of the plant.

Pteris cretica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
