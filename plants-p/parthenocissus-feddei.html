Parthenocissus feddei

Parthenocissus feddei, commonly known as the Boston ivy, is a flowering vine native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that turn vibrant red in the autumn, adding ornamental value to landscapes. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are typically not showy but are important for pollination. Its tendrils allow it to climb various structures, making it a popular choice for vertical gardening.

The berries it produces are edible but often consumed by birds, aiding in seed dispersal.
