Peltandra alba

Peltandra alba, commonly known as the white flag fern, is a species native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its long, narrow, and often white or pale green leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors or near streams. Its leaves are often arranged in a fan-like pattern, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Peltandra alba is valued for its aesthetic qualities and is sometimes used in horticulture for its unique foliage.
