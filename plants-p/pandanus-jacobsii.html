Pandanus jacobsii

Pandanus jacobsii, commonly known as the Jacobs' pandanus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Pandanaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including parts of Indonesia and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender stems and long, narrow leaves that are often used in traditional crafts. Its inflorescence consists of a spadix surrounded by a bract, which bears small, fragrant flowers.

The plant is well adapted to coastal and wetland environments, thriving in humid, saline conditions.
