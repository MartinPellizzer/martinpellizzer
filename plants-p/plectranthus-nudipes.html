Plectranthus nudipes

Plectranthus nudipes, commonly known as the naked-stem plectranthus, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features slender, upright stems that are often covered in a fine layer of white hairs. The leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly waxy texture, often with a pale green to grayish-blue hue. It produces small, tubular flowers in shades of purple or pink, typically blooming in late spring to early summer.

This plant is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water once established.
