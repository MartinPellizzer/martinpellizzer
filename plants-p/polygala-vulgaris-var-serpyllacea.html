Polygala vulgaris var. serpyllacea

Polygala vulgaris var. serpyllacea, commonly known as common milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in dry grasslands, open woodlands, and disturbed soils, exhibiting a low, sprawling growth habit. The plant features small, clustered flowers that are usually pink or pale purple, with distinctive, thread-like petals. Its leaves are narrow and often hairy, and it produces small, hard seeds that are dispersed by wind.

This species is known for its adaptability to various soil types and its role in supporting local pollinator populations.
