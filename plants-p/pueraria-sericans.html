Pueraria sericans

Pueraria sericans, commonly known as the giant cowpea, is a vigorous climbing legume native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features large, heart-shaped leaves and produces showy, purple-pink flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is characterized by its extensive root system and the ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment. Its rapid growth habit makes it both a useful forage crop and a potential invasive species in certain environments.

Pueraria sericans is often found in disturbed areas, along roadsides, and in agricultural fields.
