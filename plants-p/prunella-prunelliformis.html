Prunella prunelliformis

Prunella prunelliformis, commonly known as the slender selfheal, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, pale blue or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Prunella prunelliformis is often found in moist, well-drained soils and is valued for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal remedies.
