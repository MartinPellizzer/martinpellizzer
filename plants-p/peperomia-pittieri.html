Peperomia pittieri

Peperomia pittieri, commonly known as the silver peperomia, is a compact evergreen shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It features thick, waxy leaves that are typically silver or gray-green in color, with a textured surface that gives it a unique appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually green or red, arranged in clusters at the ends of its stems. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Peperomia pittieri is popular as a houseplant due to its low maintenance and attractive foliage.
