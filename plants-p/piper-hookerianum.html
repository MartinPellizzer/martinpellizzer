Piper hookerianum

Piper hookerianum, commonly known as Hooker's pepper, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many species of pepper plants. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by berries containing a single seed. Its leaves are ovate to elliptical in shape, with a glossy surface and serrated edges.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
