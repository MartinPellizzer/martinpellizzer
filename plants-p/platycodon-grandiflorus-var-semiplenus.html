Platycodon grandiflorus var. semiplenus

Platycodon grandiflorus var. semiplenus, commonly known as the semiplenum bellflower, is a variant of the Chinese bellflower native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, bell-shaped blue or purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant features upright, branched stems covered in rough, hairy leaves. Its flowers are notable for their unique, elongated shape and are often used in ornamental gardening.

This variety is valued for its hardiness and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
