Phaeopappus microlophus

Phaeopappus microlophus, commonly known as the slender pappus, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. Its leaves are narrow and linear, with a hairy texture that helps reduce water loss.

The flowers are small and yellow, arranged in clusters, and are adapted for wind pollination.
