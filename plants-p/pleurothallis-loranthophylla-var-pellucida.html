Pleurothallis loranthophylla var. pellucida

Pleurothallis loranthophylla var. pellucida, commonly known as the translucent pleurothallis, is a species of orchid native to the cloud forests of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that often have a unique, translucent quality to their petals. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are usually narrow and arranged in a spiral pattern, aiding in efficient light capture.

This variety is prized by orchid enthusiasts for its rare beauty and relatively low maintenance requirements.
