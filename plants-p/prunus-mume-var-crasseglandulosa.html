Prunus mume var. crasseglandulosa

Prunus mume var. crasseglandulosa, commonly known as the rough glandular Chinese plum, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It is characterized by its smooth, dark reddish-brown bark and oval-shaped leaves with a glossy upper surface. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, white or pink flowers in early spring, followed by edible yellow or orange fruits. It is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.

The variety is distinguished by its dense, glandular hairs on the stems and leaves, giving it its specific epithet.
