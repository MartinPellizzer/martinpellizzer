Phrygilanthus verticillatus var. flava-citrinus

Phrygilanthus verticillatus var. flava-citrinus, commonly known as the golden citron tree, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking yellow-orange foliage and clusters of small, fragrant flowers. The plant produces small, citrus-like fruits that are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with high humidity and well-drained soil.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and landscape design.
