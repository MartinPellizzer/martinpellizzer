Pseudoeriocoma hirticulmis

Pseudoeriocoma hirticulmis, commonly known as the hairy-stemmed eriocoma, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves, which help it retain moisture in its native arid environments. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, arranged in dense clusters, and bloom during the warmer months.

Pseudoeriocoma hirticulmis is often found in rocky or sandy soils, thriving in regions with moderate to high sunlight exposure.
