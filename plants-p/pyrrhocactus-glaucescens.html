Pyrrhocactus glaucescens

Pyrrhocactus glaucescens, commonly known as the blue-green cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems that are covered in a waxy, bluish-green coating, which helps reduce water loss in its harsh environment. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, adding a striking contrast to its muted foliage. Its succulent nature allows it to store water efficiently, making it well adapted to drought conditions.

Pyrrhocactus glaucescens is often found growing in rocky soils and is relatively low maintenance, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.
