Plantago amplexicaulis var. baufuloides

Plantago amplexicaulis var. baufuloides, commonly known as the Baufuloides plantain, is a perennial herb characterized by its rosette-forming leaves that are often covered in dense, silky hairs.

The leaves are broadly ovate with a distinctive basal lobing and are typically clasping the stem, giving the plant its species name. It produces small, greenish-white flowers in dense spikes, which are borne on elongated stems during the summer months. The plant is native to parts of Africa and is adapted to grow in moist, shaded environments.

Its morphology and habitat make it an interesting subject for botanical study and ecological research.
