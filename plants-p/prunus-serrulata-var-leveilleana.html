Prunus serrulata var. leveilleana

Prunus serrulata var. leveilleana, commonly known as the Japanese cherry blossom or some varieties as the weeping cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers that bloom in early spring, typically in shades of white and pink. The leaves are ovate with serrated edges, giving the plant its scientific name. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

This variety is often cultivated for ornamental purposes in gardens and parks due to its aesthetic beauty.
