Pyrola hopeiensis

Pyrola hopeiensis, commonly known as Hope's wintergreen, is a rare alpine plant native to the Appalachian Mountains.

It is a low-growing evergreen shrub with dark green, leathery leaves and small, bell-shaped white or pink flowers. The plant thrives in shaded, moist environments at high elevations, often found in rocky or forested areas. Its flowers bloom in late winter or early spring, making it one of the earliest flowering plants in its habitat.

Pyrola hopeiensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in mountain ecosystems.
