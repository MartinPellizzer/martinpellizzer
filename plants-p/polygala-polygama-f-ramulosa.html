Polygala polygama f. ramulosa

Polygala polygama f. ramulosa, commonly known as the ramulate milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of Asia.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. Its leaves are often narrow and hairy, and the flowers are usually pale yellow or greenish in color.

This variety is noted for its unique floral structure and is of interest to botanists and horticulturists alike.
