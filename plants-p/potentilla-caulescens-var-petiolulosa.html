Potentilla caulescens var. petiolulosa

Potentilla caulescens var. petiolulosa, commonly known as the slender-stemmed cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its upright, herbaceous stems that are often branched and covered with fine, hairy hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are alternate, with five leaflets that are oval to elliptic in shape and have serrated edges.

This variety is adapted to rocky and well-drained soils, making it a hardy plant found in alpine and subalpine environments.
