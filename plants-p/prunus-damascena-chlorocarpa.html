Prunus damascena chlorocarpa

Prunus damascena chlorocarpa, commonly known as the white damask rose, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape. Its fruit is a small, red drupe that matures in late summer.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in perfumery and culinary applications.
