Pouteria sessiliflora

Pouteria sessiliflora, commonly known as the Brazilian nut tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Amazon basin.

It is characterized by its large, elliptical leaves and produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The tree is notable for its hard, woody fruit, which contains a single large seed surrounded by a sweet, edible kernel. The plant thrives in humid, warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various wildlife species in its native region.
