Phaeostemma tigrina

Phaeostemma tigrina, commonly known as the tiger orchid, is a striking member of the Orchidaceae family native to South America.

This herbaceous perennial is characterized by its vibrant, tiger-striped flowers that display a complex pattern of contrasting colors. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, often in tropical or subtropical regions. Its flowers are highly specialized, featuring intricate structures that facilitate pollination by specific insect species.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, Phaeostemma tigrina is a subject of interest in botanical research and conservation efforts.
