Polypodium giganteum

Polypodium giganteum, commonly known as the giant shield fern, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, leathery fronds that can grow up to several feet in length. The plant has a rhizomatous growth habit, with a thick, horizontal underground stem that produces new shoots. Its leaves are typically bipinnate, with a complex, feather-like structure that enhances its ornamental appeal.

Polypodium giganteum thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated as a decorative plant in tropical gardens.
