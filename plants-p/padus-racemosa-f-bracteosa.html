Padus racemosa f. bracteosa

Padus racemosa f. bracteosa, commonly known as the bracteate white crabsapple, is a botanical variant of the white crabsapple.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to North America, particularly found in the eastern United States. The plant is characterized by its distinctive bracteate inflorescences, which are clusters of flowers surrounded by modified leaves called bracts. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink, and it produces small, edible fruits that are often used in jams and jellies.

This form is notable for its ornamental value and its role in supporting local wildlife.
