Phaseolus quadriflorus

Phaseolus quadriflorus, commonly known as the common scarlet runner bean, is a leguminous plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its climbing habit and heart-shaped leaves, which are typically a deep green. The plant produces vibrant red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters of four, giving it its species name "quadriflorus.". Its pods are long and cylindrical, containing edible beans that are rich in protein and nutrients.

This plant is well-suited for warm climates and is often grown for both ornamental and culinary purposes.
