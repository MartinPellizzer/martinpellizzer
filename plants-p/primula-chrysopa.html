Primula chrysopa

Primula chrysopa, commonly known as the golden primrose, is a herbaceous perennial native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features rosettes of narrow, hairy leaves and produces clusters of bright yellow flowers with five petals. The plant typically blooms in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardeners seeking a vibrant, early-flowering plant.
