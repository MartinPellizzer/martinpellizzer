Pyragra obtusifolia

Pyragra obtusifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved pyragra, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a waxy cuticle, which helps reduce water loss in arid environments. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in dense clusters, typically blooming during the cooler months. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to thrive in rocky or sandy soils.

Pyragra obtusifolia is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory properties.
