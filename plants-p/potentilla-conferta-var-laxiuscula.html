Potentilla conferta var. laxiuscula

Potentilla conferta var. laxiuscula, commonly known as the lax-flowered potentilla, is a low-growing shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features dense, mat-forming growth with small, hairy leaves that are typically elliptical in shape. The plant produces clusters of bright yellow flowers in early summer, which are attractive to pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or alpine settings.

Its hardy nature and ornamental value make it a popular choice for gardeners in temperate climates.
