Philotheca difformis subsp. smithiana

Philotheca difformis subsp.

smithiana, commonly known as the narrow-leaved whiteflowered philotheca, is a shrub native to southeastern Australia. It is characterized by its slender, aromatic leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom from late summer to early autumn. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or heathlands.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
