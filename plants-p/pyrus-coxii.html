Pyrus coxii

Pyrus coxii, commonly known as the Cox's pear, is a species of flowering plant in the genus Pyrus, which belongs to the family Rosaceae.

It is native to the mountainous regions of western China, particularly in the Sichuan and Yunnan provinces. The plant is characterized by its small, clustered white flowers and edible, sweet fruits that are similar to common pears. It typically grows at elevations between 1,500 and 3,000 meters, thriving in cool, temperate climates.

The leaves are simple, alternate, and have a slightly serrated margin, contributing to its ornamental value in horticulture.
