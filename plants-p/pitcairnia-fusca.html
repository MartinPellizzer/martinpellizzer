Pitcairnia fusca

Pitcairnia fusca, commonly known as the velvet leaf or the orchid of the Andes, is a striking bromeliad native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, velvety rosette of dark green, waxy leaves that form a central tank for collecting rainwater. The plant produces striking inflorescences with vibrant red and orange flowers that bloom in the late afternoon. Its unique structure allows it to thrive in shaded, humid environments, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Pitcairnia fusca is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its aesthetic beauty.
