Potamogeton trinervius

Potamogeton trinervius, commonly known as the three-veined pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater habitats in North America.

It has long, narrow leaves that are typically arranged in whorls around the stem, and it produces small, green flowers that are often submerged or partially emergent. The plant is characterized by its three main veins in the leaves, which give it its scientific name. It plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.

Potamogeton trinervius is often found in slow-moving or still waters such as lakes, ponds, and wetlands.
