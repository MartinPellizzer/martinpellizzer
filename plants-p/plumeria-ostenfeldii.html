Plumeria ostenfeldii

Plumeria ostenfeldii, commonly known as the white frangipani, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, waxy flowers that bloom in clusters, typically in shades of white, yellow, or pink. The plant has simple, lance-shaped leaves that are opposite in arrangement. It thrives in warm, sunny climates with well-draining soil.

The flowers are often used in floral arrangements and have cultural significance in various regions.
