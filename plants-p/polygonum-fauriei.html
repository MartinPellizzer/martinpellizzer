Polygonum fauriei

Polygonum fauriei, commonly known as Chinese knotweed, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It features upright stems that are often reddish in color and are covered with small, sticky hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a slightly waxy texture.

Polygonum fauriei is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
