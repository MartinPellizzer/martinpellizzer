Polygonum cumingianum

Polygonum cumingianum, commonly known as Cuming's knotweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, which is characterized by its distinctive nodes and often triangular stems. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped with a smooth or slightly hairy surface, and it produces small, clustered flowers in early summer.

Polygonum cumingianum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat and nectar for various insects.
