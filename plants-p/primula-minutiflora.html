Primula minutiflora

Primula minutiflora, commonly known as the dwarf primrose, is a small herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of China.

It features rosettes of narrow, dark green leaves and produces clusters of delicate, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, yellow, and pink. The flowers typically bloom in spring, adding a vibrant touch to alpine gardens. It thrives in cool, moist environments and prefers well-drained soil.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens and as a container plant.
