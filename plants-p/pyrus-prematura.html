Pyrus prematura

Pyrus prematura, commonly known as the early pear.

It is a deciduous tree native to the eastern United States, characterized by its smooth, gray bark and oval-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by edible pears that ripen in late summer. It prefers moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in horticulture.
