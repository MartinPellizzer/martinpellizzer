Potamogeton × nitens f. extensus

Potamogeton × nitens f. extensus, commonly known as the broadleaf pondweed, is a hybrid aquatic plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its long, narrow leaves that are typically broader than those of its parent species. This plant thrives in freshwater environments such as lakes, ponds, and slow-moving rivers. Its reproductive structures include small, green flowers that appear in dense clusters.

Potamogeton × nitens f. extensus plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
