Phanera uleana

Phanera uleana, commonly known as the golden flame tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has a woody stem with smooth, gray bark and broad, palmate leaves that are deeply lobed. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Phanera uleana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can be found in botanical gardens and urban landscapes.
