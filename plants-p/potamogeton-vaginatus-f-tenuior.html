Potamogeton vaginatus f. tenuior

Potamogeton vaginatus f. tenuior, commonly known as the narrow-leaved pondweed, is a aquatic plant native to freshwater environments.

It is characterized by its slender, ribbon-like leaves that are typically less than 2 mm wide. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in spike-like inflorescences. It thrives in shallow waters and is often found in lakes, ponds, and slow-moving streams.

Its presence can indicate good water quality and is an important component of aquatic ecosystems.
