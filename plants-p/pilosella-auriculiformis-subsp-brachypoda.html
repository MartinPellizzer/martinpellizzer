Pilosella auriculiformis subsp. brachypoda

Pilosella auriculiformis subsp.

brachypoda, commonly known as the short-stemmed cat's ear, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is a perennial herb with a low-growing, mat-forming habit, characterized by its hairy, spoon-shaped leaves.

The plant produces small, yellowish-white composite flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in dry, sunny environments and is often found in rocky or gravelly soils.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive member of the Asteraceae family.
