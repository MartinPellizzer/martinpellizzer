Penstemon moffatii subsp. paysonii

Penstemon moffatii subsp.

paysonii, commonly known as Payson penstemon, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States. It is characterized by its tall, branched stems and showy, tubular flowers that range in color from violet to pink.

The plant typically grows in rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments, making it a hardy species. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a grayish-blue hue due to a dense covering of fine hairs.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
