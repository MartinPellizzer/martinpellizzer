Phalaenopsis cornu-cervi f. thalebanii

Phalaenopsis cornu-cervi f. thalebanii, commonly known as the Thalebanii Phalaenopsis, is a rare and unique orchid cultivar characterized by its striking, elongated flower spikes and unusual coloration.

This hybrid is a fasciation variant, meaning it exhibits a flattened, ribbon-like growth pattern in its floral display. The plant typically produces large, showy orchid blooms with a combination of white, pink, and purple hues. It thrives in warm, humid environments with bright, indirect light and requires consistent moisture and good air circulation.

Due to its rarity and distinct appearance, it is highly valued among orchid enthusiasts and collectors.
