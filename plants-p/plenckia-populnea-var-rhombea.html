Plenckia populnea var. rhombea

Plenckia populnea var. rhombea, commonly known as the diamondleaf poplar, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to parts of East Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped to diamond-shaped leaves, which are typically a vibrant green during the growing season. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in the spring, followed by samaras that aid in wind dispersal. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or stream banks.

Its ornamental value is largely due to its unique leaf shape and seasonal color changes.
