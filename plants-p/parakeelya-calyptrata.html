Parakeelya calyptrata

Parakeelya calyptrata, commonly known as the Christmas bell, is a striking flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its vibrant, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, often resembling miniature lanterns. The plant produces fleshy, spiny fruit that encloses its seeds, a feature that aids in its dispersal by animals. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and collections worldwide.
