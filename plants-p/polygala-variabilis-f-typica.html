Polygala variabilis f. typica

Polygala variabilis f. typica, commonly known as the variable milkvetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the Polygalaceae family.

It is native to parts of Asia, including China, and thrives in dry, rocky habitats. The plant features slender, branched stems and small, usually yellow or greenish flowers arranged in clusters. Its leaves are typically narrow and opposite, contributing to its adaptability in arid environments.

The species is noted for its variability in form and color, making it a subject of interest in botanical studies.
