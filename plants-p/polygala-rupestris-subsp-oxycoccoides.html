Polygala rupestris subsp. oxycoccoides

Polygala rupestris subsp.

oxycoccoides, commonly known as the rock polygala, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It is a small, herbaceous perennial that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils.

The plant produces delicate, spicate inflorescences with small, often white or pale blue flowers. It is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
