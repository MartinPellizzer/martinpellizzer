Polyalthia glabra

Polyalthia glabra, commonly known as the white flame tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, pale bark and clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom throughout the year. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a glossy surface. Its fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release small, winged seeds.

Polyalthia glabra is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
