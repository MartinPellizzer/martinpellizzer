Phaca viridis

Phaca viridis, commonly known as green phaca, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of Africa.

It belongs to the family Acanthaceae and is characterized by its upright growth habit and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically green or yellowish in color, which are borne on slender stalks. Phaca viridis thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
