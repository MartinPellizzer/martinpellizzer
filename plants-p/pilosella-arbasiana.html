Pilosella arbasiana

Pilosella arbasiana, commonly known as the Armenian mouse-ear, is a flowering plant native to the high mountain regions of the Eastern Mediterranean.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features small, fluffy white flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are hairy and grayish-green, adapted to thrive in arid and rocky environments.

Pilosella arbasiana is often found in alpine meadows and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
