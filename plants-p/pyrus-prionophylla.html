Pyrus prionophylla

Pyrus prionophylla, commonly known as the Japanese whitebeam, is a deciduous tree native to Japan.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its small, white or pale green flowers that bloom in early spring. The tree has simple, alternate leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape, with a smooth margin. Its fruit is a small, round pome that turns yellow or red when mature.

Pyrus prionophylla is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its elegant form and seasonal interest.
