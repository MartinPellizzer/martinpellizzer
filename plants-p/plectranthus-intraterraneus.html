Plectranthus intraterraneus

Plectranthus intraterraneus, commonly known as the African violet, is a perennial herb native to South Africa.

It features opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are often hairy and have a velvety texture. The plant produces small, tubular flowers in shades of purple, pink, and white, typically arranged in clusters. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers bright, indirect light.

Plectranthus intraterraneus is widely cultivated as a decorative plant due to its attractive foliage and flowers.
