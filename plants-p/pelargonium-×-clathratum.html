Pelargonium × clathratum

Pelargonium × clathratum, commonly known as the ivy-leaved geranium, is a hybrid plant that combines traits from various geranium species.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and dark green, heart-shaped leaves that resemble ivy leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers in shades of red, pink, or white, which bloom throughout the growing season. It is widely cultivated as an ornamental plant in hanging baskets and containers due to its attractive foliage and flowers.

Pelargonium × clathratum prefers well-draining soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a versatile choice for gardeners.
