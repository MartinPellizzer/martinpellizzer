Pleurothallis dasychila

Pleurothallis dasychila, commonly known as the hairy pleurothallis, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and the presence of dense, hairy structures on its stems and leaves. The plant typically produces small, pale green or white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences. It thrives in shaded, humid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its unique appearance and relatively easy care requirements, it is a popular choice among orchid enthusiasts.
