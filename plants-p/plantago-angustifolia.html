Plantago angustifolia

Plantago angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf plantain, is a perennial herb native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It features upright, leafy stems with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in dense clusters, which are typically wind-pollinated. It is often found in disturbed soils, along roadsides, and in meadows.

This species is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally for its healing benefits.
