Pavetta bidentata var. bidentata

Pavetta bidentata var. bidentata, commonly known as the two-toothed pavetta, is a shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring opposite, elliptic leaves with two distinct teeth at the base. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are borne on short pedicels. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, smooth seeds.

The species is characterized by its simple, alternate leaf arrangement and its preference for well-drained, acidic soils.
