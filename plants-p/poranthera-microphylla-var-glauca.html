Poranthera microphylla var. glauca

Poranthera microphylla var. glauca, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its compact, low-growing habit. The plant produces small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow, grass-like, and covered in a fine, silvery-white pubescence.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and native plant landscapes.
