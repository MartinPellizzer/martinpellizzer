Panicum pellitum

Panicum pellitum, commonly known as little barley, is a species of grass native to North America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats, such as marshes and wet meadows. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue.

Panicum pellitum is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support biodiversity.
