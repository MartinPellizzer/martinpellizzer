Plectranthus bipartitus

Plectranthus bipartitus, commonly known as the African violet, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves and clusters of small, tubular flowers that bloom in shades of purple, pink, and white. The plant is often cultivated as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and ease of care. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light to flourish.

Plectranthus bipartitus is also valued for its aromatic leaves, which can be used in traditional medicine.
