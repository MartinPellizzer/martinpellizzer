Polythecium moonii

Polythecium moonii, commonly known as the moon's shadow fern, is a rare and delicate fern species native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its finely divided, silvery-green fronds that resemble the texture of moonlight. The plant thrives in shaded, humid environments, often found in dense forest understories. Its reproductive structures, known as sori, are clustered on the undersides of its leaves, a typical feature among ferns.

Due to its limited distribution and sensitivity to environmental changes, Polythecium moonii is considered a conservation concern.
