Potentilla nivea f. incisa

Potentilla nivea f. incisa, commonly known as the white cinquefoil, is a flowering plant belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its white, five-petaled flowers and serrated leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. Native to alpine regions of Europe, this plant thrives in rocky and well-drained soils. It typically grows at elevations between 1,500 and 3,000 meters, where it is adapted to cold and harsh environmental conditions.

The plant is often found in meadows and mountainous areas, contributing to the biodiversity of its habitat.
