Psidium cujavillus

Psidium cujavillus, commonly known as the pineapple guava, is a small evergreen shrub native to South America.

It produces small, fragrant, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale pink. The plant's leaves are elliptical, glossy, and arranged alternately along the stems. Its fruit is a small, round, yellow or red berry with a sweet, aromatic flavor.

Psidium cujavillus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible fruit in subtropical climates.
