Platystele obtecta

Platystele obtecta, commonly known as the narrow-stemmed ghost orchid, is a rare and delicate epiphytic orchid native to the cloud forests of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, leafless stems and small, white, fragrant flowers. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and relies on specific mycorrhizal fungi for nutrient absorption. Its unique morphology and restricted habitat make it a subject of interest in botanical research.

Due to habitat loss, Platystele obtecta is considered endangered, highlighting the need for conservation efforts.
