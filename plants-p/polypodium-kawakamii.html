Polypodium kawakamii

Polypodium kawakamii, commonly known as the Kawakami's sword fern, is a species of fern native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia, including parts of China, Japan, and the Philippines.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its long, arching fronds that can grow up to several meters in length. The plant prefers moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories where it thrives in humid conditions. Its rhizome is thick and fleshy, allowing it to store water and nutrients efficiently.

Polypodium kawakamii is valued for its ornamental appearance and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
