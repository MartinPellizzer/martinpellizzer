Potamogeton × kaplanii

Potamogeton × kaplanii, commonly known as the hybrid water milfoil, is a perennial aquatic plant that thrives in freshwater environments.

It is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Potamogeton perfoliatus and Potamogeton praelatus. The plant features submerged leaves that are typically opposite and have a distinctive four-angled stem. Its flowers are small and clustered, with male and female flowers occurring on separate plants.

Potamogeton × kaplanii plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
