Pouteria menait

Pouteria menait, commonly known as the menait or the red mombin, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and showy, yellow-orange flowers. The fruit is a fleshy, spiky berry that turns red when ripe, containing several seeds. The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in local medicine.

Its growth habit includes a straight trunk and a dense canopy, making it a notable species in humid tropical forests.
