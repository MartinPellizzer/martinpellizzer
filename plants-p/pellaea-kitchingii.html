Pellaea kitchingii

Pellaea kitchingii, commonly known as the Kitching's maidenhair fern, is a species of fern native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the family Pellaeaceae and is characterized by its delicate, lace-like fronds. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its leaves are bi-lobed and have a distinctive, fern-like appearance with finely divided segments.

Pellaea kitchingii is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens with high humidity and indirect light.
