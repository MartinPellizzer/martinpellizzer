Physalis viscosa f. latifolia

Physalis viscosa f. latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf common groundcherry, is a variant of the common groundcherry species.

It is a herbaceous annual plant characterized by its hairy, fleshy stems and broad, ovate leaves. The plant produces clusters of yellow flowers that develop into inflated, lantern-shaped fruits, which are typically orange to red in color. The fruit contains numerous small, edible seeds, and the plant is often found in disturbed soils in temperate regions.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and edible fruits in both horticultural and culinary contexts.
