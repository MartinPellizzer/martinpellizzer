Piptatherum roshevitsianum

Piptatherum roshevitsianum, commonly known as the Roshevitsian's needlegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It typically grows in rocky and arid environments, exhibiting a low-growing, tufted habit with narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences, which contain both male and female flowers, typical of the Poaceae family. Its rhizomatous growth pattern allows it to spread and form dense mats, contributing to soil stabilization in its native habitat.
