Pulsatilla pratensis var. zichyi

Pulsatilla pratensis var. zichyi, commonly known as the Chinese pasqueflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to China.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its showy, early spring blooms of pink to purple petals. The plant typically grows in alpine or subalpine meadows, preferring well-drained, rocky soils. Its leaves are palmately divided and covered in a fine layer of silky hairs.

The flowers are followed by a cluster of bright yellow anthers that release pollen, contributing to its ornamental appeal.
