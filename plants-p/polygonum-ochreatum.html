Polygonum ochreatum

Polygonum ochreatum, commonly known as the yellow knotweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, which includes other familiar plants like buckwheat and sorrel. The plant is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, clustered flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped with a reddish or purple tinge, especially along the veins.

Polygonum ochreatum is often found in moist, disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
