Pourthiaea amphidoxa var. amphileia

Pourthiaea amphidoxa var. amphileia, commonly known as the white-flowered pincushion plant, is a succulent species native to southern Africa.

It features dense, woolly foliage that forms a compact, cushion-like structure. The plant produces showy, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink in color. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid environments.

Its unique appearance and drought tolerance make it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
