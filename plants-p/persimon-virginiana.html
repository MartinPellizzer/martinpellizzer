Persimon virginiana

Persimon virginiana, commonly known as the American persimmon, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern North America.

It produces orange to reddish fruits that are rich in sugars and tannins, which are astringent when unripe but mellow as the fruit ripens. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are usually oval or elliptic in shape. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or open woods.

The fruit is an important food source for wildlife, including birds and mammals.
