Phyllorkis comersophylis

Phyllorkis comersophylis, commonly known as the golden mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, feathery leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest undergrowth. Its botanical features include opposite leaf arrangement and a fibrous root system.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
