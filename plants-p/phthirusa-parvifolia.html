Phthirusa parvifolia

Phthirusa parvifolia, commonly known as the small-leaved phthirusa, is a flowering plant native to specific regions in southern Africa.

It is characterized by its small, narrow leaves and clusters of tiny, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its foliage is often covered in fine, soft hairs, which contribute to its unique texture.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and somewhat elusive species in its native habitat.
