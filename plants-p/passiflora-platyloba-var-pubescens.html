Passiflora platyloba var. pubescens

Passiflora platyloba var. pubescens, commonly known as the pubescent passionflower, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, showy flowers that feature a crown of filaments and a central cluster of stamens. The leaves are typically heart-shaped with a rough, pubescent texture, contributing to its common name. The plant produces edible berries that are often used in traditional medicine.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for both horticultural and ecological studies.
