Purdiaea ophiticola var. ophiticola

Purdiaea ophiticola var. ophiticola, commonly known as the rock lily, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces striking, showy flowers in shades of yellow to orange. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, demonstrating a high degree of drought tolerance. Its flowers are often clustered in terminal inflorescences, and it blooms during the spring and early summer.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens or xeriscapes.
