Polygala carphoides var. sublatifolia

Polygala carphoides var. sublatifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It features slender, branched stems that often grow along the ground, and its leaves are lance-shaped with a bluish-green hue. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in dense clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important component of xerophytic ecosystems.
