Protium tenuifolium subsp. herbertii

Protium tenuifolium subsp.

herbertii, commonly known as Herbert's slender-leafed tamanu, is a member of the Burseraceae family. It is a small evergreen tree or shrub native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, particularly Indonesia and the Philippines.

The plant is characterized by its narrow, elliptical leaves and small, fragrant yellow flowers. Its bark and leaves are used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties.

The subspecies is distinguished by its slightly smaller leaves and more compact growth habit compared to the typical form of Protium tenuifolium.
