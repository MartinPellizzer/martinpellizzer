Picea lambertiana

Picea lambertiana, commonly known as the Lambert fir, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is characterized by its conical shape, dark green needles, and distinctive cone shape. The tree typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in mountainous regions. It is adapted to cool, humid climates and can reach heights of up to 50 meters.

The Lambert fir plays an important ecological role in forest ecosystems, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
