Plinia grandifolia

Plinia grandifolia, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or *Eugenia grandifolia*, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and produces clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant is notable for its edible, red, cherry-like fruits that are rich in vitamin C. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit production.

Plinia grandifolia is also valued in traditional medicine for its various health benefits.
