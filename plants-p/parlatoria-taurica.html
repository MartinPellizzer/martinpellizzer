Parlatoria taurica

Parlatoria taurica, commonly known as the Tauric bellflower, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in mountainous regions. The plant features slender, erect stems that bear clusters of small, bell-shaped blue or purple flowers. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
