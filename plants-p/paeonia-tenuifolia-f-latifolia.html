Paeonia tenuifolia f. latifolia

Paeonia tenuifolia f. latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf peony, is a cultivar of the peony plant characterized by its wide, showy flowers.

It belongs to the family Ranunculaceae and is native to China, where it thrives in temperate climates. The plant features compound, bipinnate leaves that are broader than those of the typical Paeonia tenuifolia, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its flowers are usually pink or white, with a delicate, lace-like texture, and bloom in early summer.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often grown in gardens for its visual appeal.
