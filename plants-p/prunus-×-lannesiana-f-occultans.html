Prunus × lannesiana f. occultans

Prunus × lannesiana f. occultans, commonly known as the black cherry or Prunus serotina, is a shrub or small tree native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its dark, glossy leaves and small, reddish-purple flowers that appear in spring. The plant produces edible, dark purple berries that are a food source for various wildlife. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

The species is notable for its ornamental value and ecological importance in supporting local biodiversity.
