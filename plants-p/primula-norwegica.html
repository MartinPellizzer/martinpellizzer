Primula norwegica

Primula norwegica, commonly known as the Norwegian primrose, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Scandinavia.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and violet, typically blooming in early spring. The plant has oval, dark green leaves that are slightly hairy, and it grows in rocky or woodland habitats. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.

The species is also noted for its ability to thrive in cool, moist environments.
