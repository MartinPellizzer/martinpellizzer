Polycarpon arabicum

Polycarpon arabicum, commonly known as the Arabian horsebean, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean and parts of Asia.

It features slender, erect stems with alternate, pinnately compound leaves that have a serrated edge. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in loose inflorescences. Its fruit is a legume, containing several seeds that are often used in traditional medicine.

Polycarpon arabicum is adapted to dry environments and is sometimes cultivated for its nutritional value.
