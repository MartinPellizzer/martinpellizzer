Prunus pseudocerasus watereri

Prunus pseudocerasus watereri, commonly known as the Chinese dates or the wax apple, is a small evergreen tree native to southern China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its elliptical, dark green leaves and clusters of small, white flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically yellow or red when mature, with a sweet, sticky pulp. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible fruits.

The tree is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional Chinese medicine.
