Poa sibirica var. insignis

Poa sibirica var. insignis, commonly known as the Siberian grass or the fine-leaved sedge, is a perennial grass species native to the cold climates of Siberia and Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wet meadows and tundra environments. It has a clump-forming growth habit and is relatively hardy, tolerating low temperatures and poor soil conditions.

Due to its ornamental appeal and resilience, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens with cool, humid climates.
