Pilea cocottei

Pilea cocottei, commonly known as the Chinese money plant, is a member of the Araceae family.

It is native to China and is characterized by its round, shield-shaped leaves that resemble coins. The plant typically grows as a compact shrub with a short, stocky stem. Its leaves are thick and waxy, often with a slight sheen, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters.

It is popular as a houseplant due to its ease of care and attractive foliage.
