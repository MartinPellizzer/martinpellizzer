Penstemon unilateralis

Penstemon unilateralis, commonly known as the single-flowered beardtongue, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces striking tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Penstemon unilateralis thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its nectar-rich flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to ecological biodiversity.
