Podocarpus glaucus

Podocarpus glaucus, commonly known as the silver pine, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, pyramidal shape and bluish-gray foliage that gives it its common name. The plant produces small, spherical cones that mature to a reddish-brown color. Its leaves are scale-like and tightly pressed against the branches, contributing to its dense appearance.

Podocarpus glaucus is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance and adaptability to various soil types.
