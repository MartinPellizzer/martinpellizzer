Pilosella fallaciformis

Pilosella fallaciformis, commonly known as the false hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its low-growing habit and produces small, yellowish to white flowers arranged in clusters. The plant has hairy stems and leaves that are typically lance-shaped. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as meadows and rocky areas.

Pilosella fallaciformis is native to Europe and parts of Asia, and it is often found in disturbed soils.
