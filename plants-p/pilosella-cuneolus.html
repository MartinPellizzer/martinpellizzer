Pilosella cuneolus

Pilosella cuneolus, commonly known as the narrowleaf mouse-ear, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its hairy, low-growing stems and small, spoon-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of tiny, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in the summer months. It is native to regions of Europe and is often found in meadows and grasslands.

Pilosella cuneolus is typically perennial and thrives in well-drained soils.
