Polypodium pedunculatum

Polypodium pedunculatum, commonly known as the climbing fern, is a member of the Polypodiaceae family and is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit.

It features a dense network of stolons that allow it to spread across the forest floor or climb on tree trunks. The fronds are typically bipinnate, with a feathery appearance, and they can reach lengths of up to 1 meter. It thrives in humid, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

The plant is known for its ability to form dense mats, which contribute to soil stabilization and provide habitat for various microorganisms.
