Pecteilis crassilabia

Pecteilis crassilabia, commonly known as the broadleaf pecteilis, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils. The plant features showy, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, with a waxy coating to reduce water loss.

Pecteilis crassilabia is adapted to withstand drought conditions, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
