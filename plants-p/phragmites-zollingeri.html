Phragmites zollingeri

Phragmites zollingeri, commonly known as Zollinger's reed, is a tall, perennial grass that thrives in wetland environments.

It is characterized by its hollow, cylindrical stems and feathery plumes that appear in late summer. This plant is often found in marshes, along lake shores, and in other moist habitats. Its rhizomatous growth allows it to spread rapidly and form dense stands.

Phragmites zollingeri plays a vital role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
