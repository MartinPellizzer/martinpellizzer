Piper pseudovariabile var. pachypus

Piper pseudovariabile var. pachypus, commonly known as the broadleaf pepper vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its large, ovate leaves with a glossy surface. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by elongated, fleshy berries that turn red when mature. Its unique morphology and growth habit make it a notable species in botanical studies and horticulture.

The variety pachypus is distinguished by its broader leaves compared to other varieties of Piper pseudovariabile.
