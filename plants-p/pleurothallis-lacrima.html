Pleurothallis lacrima

Pleurothallis lacrima, commonly known as the tear orchid, is a species of orchid native to the cloud forests of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its compact, epiphytic growth habit and its ability to thrive in shaded, humid environments. The plant produces small, delicate flowers in shades of white and yellow, often with a subtle fragrance. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and arranged in a rosette pattern, aiding in moisture retention.

Pleurothallis lacrima is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts for its unique appearance.
