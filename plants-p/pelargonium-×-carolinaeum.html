Pelargonium × carolinaeum

Pelargonium × carolinaeum, commonly known as the Carolina geranium, is a hybrid species within the Geraniaceae family.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, five-petaled flowers that range in color from pink to red. The plant typically produces heart-shaped leaves with serrated edges, and it thrives in full sun to partial shade. It is native to the southeastern United States and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.

Pelargonium × carolinaeum is relatively drought-tolerant and prefers well-drained soil.
