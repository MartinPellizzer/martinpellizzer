Psidium acidum

Psidium acidum, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or granadilla, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and showy, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically red or yellow when ripe, containing a single large seed. The fruit is edible, with a sweet and tangy flavor, and is often used in jams, beverages, and desserts.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
