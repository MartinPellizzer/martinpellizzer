Piper nigrum var. trioicum

Piper nigrum var. trioicum, commonly known as the three-leaf black pepper plant, is a variant of the black pepper species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three leaflets arranged symmetrically. The plant produces small, dark red berries that mature into the familiar black pepper used in cooking. Its botanical features include a climbing vine habit and a complex inflorescence structure.

This variety is valued for its unique foliage and potential use in ornamental horticulture.
