Polypodium billardierei var. magellanicum

Polypodium billardierei var. magellanicum, commonly known as the Magellanic polypody, is a fern native to South America, particularly found in the southern regions of Argentina and Chile.

It is a terrestrial fern that thrives in humid, shaded environments, often growing on rocky substrates or in forest understories. The plant features a rhizomatous growth habit with fiddlehead-like fronds that unfurl into bi- or tri-pinnate structures. Its leaves are typically lanceolate and have a leathery texture, with a dark green coloration.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in shady gardens.
