Pelargonium × magnistipulatum

Pelargonium × magnistipulatum, commonly known as the giant geranium, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Geraniaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically range in color from pink to red, with prominent stamens that add to its ornamental appeal. The plant has broad, velvety leaves that are often dark green in color, and it thrives in full sun to partial shade. It is commonly cultivated as a garden plant for its vibrant blooms and compact growth habit.

Pelargonium × magnistipulatum is also known for its adaptability to various soil types and its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
