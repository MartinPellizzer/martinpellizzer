Pterostylis oreophila

Pterostylis oreophila, commonly known as the mountain greenhood, is an orchid species native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stem and a single, lance-shaped leaf. The plant produces a single, greenish-yellow flower with a distinctive hood-like structure, which is typical of the Orchidaceae family. It thrives in shaded, moist environments, often found in rainforest understoreys.

Pterostylis oreophila is a small, delicate orchid that plays a role in its ecosystem by providing nectar for various insect pollinators.
