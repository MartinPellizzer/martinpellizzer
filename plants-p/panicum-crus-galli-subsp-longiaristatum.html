Panicum crus-galli subsp. longiaristatum

Panicum crus-galli subsp.

longiaristatum, commonly known as red panicgrass, is a grass species native to parts of Asia and Africa. It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and dense, spike-like inflorescences that are typically reddish in color.

The plant produces small, two-lobed spikelets that are often arranged in panicles, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along the edges of water bodies.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for ecological studies and potential horticultural use.
