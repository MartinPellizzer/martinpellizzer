Paphiopedilum henryanum f. christae

Paphiopedilum henryanum f. christae, commonly known as the Christa's lady's slipper orchid, is a rare and highly sought-after hybrid within the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its unique, intricate flower structure, featuring a pouch-like lip that serves as a pollination mechanism. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its native habitat, which includes parts of Southeast Asia. Its foliage consists of broad, leathery leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern, aiding in water retention.

The orchid's flowers are usually a blend of white and pink hues, with a subtle fragrance that attracts specific pollinators.
