Phyllostachys nigra f. usuguro

Phyllostachys nigra f. usuguro, commonly known as the black bamboo, is a variant of the black bamboo species native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its dark, almost black stems that have a distinctive sheen. The plant is a member of the grass family (Poaceae) and is known for its tall, slender culms that can reach heights of up to 10 meters. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

This ornamental bamboo is often cultivated for its aesthetic value and is relatively easy to care for in suitable climates.
