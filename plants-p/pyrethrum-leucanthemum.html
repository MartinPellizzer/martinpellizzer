Pyrethrum leucanthemum

Pyrethrum leucanthemum, commonly known as white leucanthemum, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its showy white to pale yellow flowers, which are typically arranged in a single row along the stem. The plant produces small, fluffy seeds that are dispersed by the wind, aiding in its propagation. Its leaves are generally gray-green with a fuzzy texture, and it thrives in well-drained soil and full sun.

Pyrethrum leucanthemum is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
