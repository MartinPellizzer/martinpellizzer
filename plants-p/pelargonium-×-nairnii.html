Pelargonium × nairnii

Pelargonium × nairnii, commonly known as the Nairn's geranium, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Geraniaceae family.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of showy, five-petaled flowers that come in a range of colors, including pink, red, and white. The leaves are typically lobed and have a velvety texture, adding to the plant's ornamental appeal. It is often cultivated as a bedding plant or in containers due to its compact size and long blooming period.

Native to South Africa, this species is well adapted to warm, sunny climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
