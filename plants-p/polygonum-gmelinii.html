Polygonum gmelinii

Polygonum gmelinii, commonly known as the Siberian knotweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the cold climates of Asia.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, characterized by its distinctive nodes and hollow stems. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a reddish or purple hue, while its small, greenish flowers appear in clusters.

Polygonum gmelinii is known for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in challenging environmental conditions.
