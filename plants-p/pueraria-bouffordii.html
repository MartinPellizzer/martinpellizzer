Pueraria bouffordii

Pueraria bouffordii, commonly known as the Chinese kudzu, is a vigorous vine native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of purple-pink flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant is a member of the legume family and has a deep root system that can spread extensively. It is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.

Due to its aggressive growth, it is often considered a weed in agricultural and natural settings.
