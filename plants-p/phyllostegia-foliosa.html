Phyllostegia foliosa

Phyllostegia foliosa, commonly known as the creeping wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the family Lamiaceae, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its opposite, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, yellowish flowers. It typically grows in moist, wooded areas and has a trailing habit, often forming dense mats.
