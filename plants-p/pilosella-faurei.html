Pilosella faurei

Pilosella faurei, commonly known as the Faure's hairy groundsel, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the spring and summer seasons. It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and grasslands.

Pilosella faurei is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
