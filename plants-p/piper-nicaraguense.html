Piper nicaraguense

Piper nicaraguense, commonly known as the Nicaraguan pepper vine, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many species of pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its climbing habit, with opposite, ovate leaves that are often hairy. It produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in spikes, followed by small, berry-like fruits.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its unique foliage and growth form.
