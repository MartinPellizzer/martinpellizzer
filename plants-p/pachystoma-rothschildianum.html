Pachystoma rothschildianum

Pachystoma rothschildianum, commonly known as the Rothschild's stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that store water, allowing it to survive in dry conditions. The plant produces clusters of small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow and orange, typically blooming in spring. Its compact growth habit and drought tolerance make it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Pachystoma rothschildianum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and low maintenance requirements.
