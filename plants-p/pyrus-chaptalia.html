Pyrus chaptalia

Pyrus chaptalia, commonly known as the Chaptalia pear, is a species of flowering plant in the genus Pyrus.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, particularly in countries like Afghanistan and Pakistan. The plant is characterized by its deciduous leaves and small, clustered flowers that bloom in the spring. Its fruit, a small pear-like berry, is edible but not widely cultivated for commercial purposes.

The species is of interest to botanists due to its unique adaptations to high-altitude environments.
