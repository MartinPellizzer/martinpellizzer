Polygonatum kingianum var. ericoideum

Polygonatum kingianum var. ericoideum, commonly known as the King's bamboo or the purple bamboo, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Australia.

It features upright, slender stems that can grow up to 2 meters in height, often with a reddish or purplish tinge. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by red berries. Its leaves are lance-shaped and alternate along the stem, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or rocky habitats.
