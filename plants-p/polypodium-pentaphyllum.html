Polypodium pentaphyllum

Polypodium pentaphyllum, commonly known as the five-leaf fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Polypodiaceae.

It is characterized by its fronds that typically consist of five pairs of leaflets, giving it its common name. This plant is native to various regions in Asia, including China, India, and Southeast Asia. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forests and rocky crevices.

The plant is valued not only for its ornamental appeal but also for its traditional uses in herbal medicine.
