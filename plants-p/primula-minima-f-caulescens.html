Primula minima f. caulescens

Primula minima f. caulescens, commonly known as the dwarf primrose, is a compact, herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It features small, delicate flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, typically blooming in early spring. The plant has a low-growing habit with a rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves. Its foliage is covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value in rock gardens and alpine settings.
