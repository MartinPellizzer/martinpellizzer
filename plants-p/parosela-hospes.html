Parosela hospes

Parosela hospes, commonly known as the purple orchid, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking violet and white petals. The plant typically grows in rocky or coastal areas, thriving in well-drained soils. Its flowers bloom in spring, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and protected species in its native region.
