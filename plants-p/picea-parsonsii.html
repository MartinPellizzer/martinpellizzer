Picea parsonsii

Picea parsonsii, commonly known as the Parson's fir, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender form and needle-like leaves that are blue-green in color. The tree produces small, spherical cones that mature to a reddish-brown color. Its bark is thick and scaly, providing protection against environmental stress.

Picea parsonsii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often planted in gardens for its aesthetic and ecological benefits.
