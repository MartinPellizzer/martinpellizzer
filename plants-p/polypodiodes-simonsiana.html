Polypodiodes simonsiana

Polypodiodes simonsiana, commonly known as the Simons' fern, is a species of polypodioid fern native to the tropical regions of South America.

It typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors and rocky crevices. The plant features delicate, lance-shaped fronds with a feathery appearance, characteristic of many ferns in the Polypodiaceae family. Its spore-producing structures, called sori, are arranged along the margins of the fronds.

Polypodiodes simonsiana plays a role in maintaining soil stability and supporting local biodiversity in its habitat.
