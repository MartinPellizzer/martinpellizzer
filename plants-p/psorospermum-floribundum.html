Psorospermum floribundum

Psorospermum floribundum, commonly known as the flame tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, fiery orange to red flower clusters that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant has pinnate leaves with small, ovate leaflets and produces woody capsules containing numerous seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for gardens and urban landscapes.
