Persica maxima

Persica maxima, commonly known as the large peach tree, is a species native to Asia and is characterized by its broad, lobed leaves and showy pink to white flowers.

The plant produces large, juicy fruits with a fuzzy skin and a sweet, aromatic flavor. Its botanical classification places it in the Rosaceae family, alongside other stone fruits like apples and cherries. Persica maxima thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

The tree is valued not only for its fruit but also for its ornamental appeal and historical significance in horticulture.
