Polystichum aculeatum var. dudleyi

Polystichum aculeatum var. dudleyi, commonly known as Dudley's holly fern, is a variant of the holly fern species native to the Pacific Northwest of the United States.

It is a perennial evergreen fern characterized by its dense, leathery fronds that are typically dark green with a slightly serrated margin. The plant features a rhizomatous growth habit, allowing it to spread and form thick mats in shaded, moist environments. Its spore-bearing structures, called sori, are clustered on the underside of the fronds, a typical feature of ferns.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in shady garden settings.
