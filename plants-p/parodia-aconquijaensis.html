Parodia aconquijaensis

Parodia aconquijaensis, commonly known as the Aconquija ball cactus, is a species of cactus native to Argentina.

It is characterized by its globular shape and dense covering of white to pale blue spines. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in arid environments. It produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months.

Due to its slow growth and unique appearance, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and cactus collections.
