Pteridium aquilinum var. caudatum

Pteridium aquilinum var. caudatum, commonly known as the curly-leaf bracken, is a variant of the common bracken fern.

It is a perennial plant belonging to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, curled fronds. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and woodlands. Its spores are released from the sori located on the underside of the fronds.

This variety is known for its adaptability and can thrive in a range of soil conditions.
