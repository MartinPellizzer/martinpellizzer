Polypodium cryptophlebium

Polypodium cryptophlebium, commonly known as the cryptophlebium huperzia, is a species of fern belonging to the family Huperziaceae.

It is characterized by its evergreen, leathery fronds that are typically divided into segments with a distinctive, narrow shape. The plant is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia, including China, India, and Southeast Asia. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The species is notable for its unique morphology and ecological adaptations, making it an interesting subject for botanical study.
