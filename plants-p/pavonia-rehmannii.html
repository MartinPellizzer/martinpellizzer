Pavonia rehmannii

Pavonia rehmannii, commonly known as the scarlet wood rose, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of 2 to 4 feet, featuring upright stems and clusters of showy, pink to red flowers. The plant has pinnately divided leaves, which are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often found in moist, well-drained soils along stream banks and in open woodlands.

Pavonia rehmannii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
