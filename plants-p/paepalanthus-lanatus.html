Paepalanthus lanatus

Paepalanthus lanatus, commonly known as the woolly pincushion, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly found in Argentina and Chile.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that gives it a unique, fluffy appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flower heads that bloom in spring, attracting various pollinators. It thrives in dry, rocky environments and is often found in high-altitude regions.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation efforts.
