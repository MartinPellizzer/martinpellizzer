Passiflora jussieui

Passiflora jussieui, commonly known as the Jussieui passionflower, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, intricate flowers that display a mix of blue, purple, and white hues. The plant produces edible berries that are often used in culinary applications. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are woody and can climb or trail.

Passiflora jussieui is valued for its ornamental beauty and potential medicinal properties.
