Potentilla chrysantha var. genuina

Potentilla chrysantha var. genuina, commonly known as the golden potentilla, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has finely divided, gray-green leaves that are aromatic and covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy addition to rock gardens or alpine settings.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its compact size and long-lasting floral display.
