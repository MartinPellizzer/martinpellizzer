Placus coronopifolius

Placus coronopifolius, commonly known as the crown-leaf plant, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its distinctive, palmate leaves that resemble a crown, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in the spring. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is a popular choice for xeriscaping.
