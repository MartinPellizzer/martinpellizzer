Pithecellobium alatum

Pithecellobium alatum, commonly known as the spreading crownflower, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant features trifoliate leaves with hairy surfaces and produces yellow or orange flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a legume that splits open to release seeds, and it is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
