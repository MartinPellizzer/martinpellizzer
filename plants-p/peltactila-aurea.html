Peltactila aurea

Peltactila aurea, commonly known as the golden peltate, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive peltate leaves, which have a broad, shield-like structure with the stem emerging from the center. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers during the dry season, which attract various pollinators. Its succulent leaves store water, allowing it to thrive in harsh, drought-prone environments.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological adaptations, Peltactila aurea is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
