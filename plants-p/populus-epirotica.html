Populus epirotica

Populus epirotica, commonly known as the Epirotic poplar, is a deciduous tree native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It belongs to the Salicaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and distinctive bark. The tree produces catkin-like flowers and is often found in moist, temperate habitats. It has been historically used for its wood and has cultural significance in the region.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and conservation efforts.
