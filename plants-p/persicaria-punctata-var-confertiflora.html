Persicaria punctata var. confertiflora

Persicaria punctata var. confertiflora, commonly known as the clustered smartweed, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, pinkish to reddish flowers that appear in late summer. The plant has alternate, ovate leaves with hairy surfaces and reddish stems. It thrives in moist to wet soils, often found in wetlands, ditches, and along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an important species for wetland ecosystems and a subject of study in plant ecology.
