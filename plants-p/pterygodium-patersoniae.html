Pterygodium patersoniae

Pterygodium patersoniae, commonly known as the Paterson's pterygodium, is a member of the Rubiaceae family.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its erect stem and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, with five petals. It is native to Australia, particularly found in the eastern regions, and thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

The species is noted for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
