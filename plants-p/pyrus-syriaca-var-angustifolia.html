Pyrus syriaca var. angustifolia

Pyrus syriaca var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved whitebeam, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its narrow, elongated leaves that are typically dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces small, white flowers in early spring, followed by edible, yellowish-green fruits known as "sycamores" or "whitebeams.". It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or limestone habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical significance in traditional medicine.
