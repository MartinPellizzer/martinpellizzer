Piper humile

Piper humile, commonly known as the dwarf pepper plant, is a small shrub native to the Andes region of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species such as black pepper. The plant is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, heart-shaped leaves. It produces tiny, green berries that turn red when mature, containing the aromatic seeds used in traditional medicine and culinary applications.

Due to its compact size and ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in garden settings.
