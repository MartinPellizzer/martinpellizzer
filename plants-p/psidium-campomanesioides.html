Psidium campomanesioides

Psidium campomanesioides, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or pitanga, is a small evergreen tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, red, fleshy berries that are edible and often used in local cuisines. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is valued for its ornamental and culinary uses.

The species is also cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
