Potamogeton robbinsii

Potamogeton robbinsii, commonly known as Robbins' pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to the United States.

It typically grows in freshwater environments such as lakes, ponds, and slow-moving streams. The plant has long, narrow leaves that are arranged spirally around its stem, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often submerged. It plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.

Robbins' pondweed is also used in wetland restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize sediments and improve water quality.
