Peperomia tomentella

Peperomia tomentella, commonly known as the velvety peperomia, is a compact, evergreen perennial plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its thick, velvety leaves that are typically covered in fine, white hairs, giving it a soft, fuzzy appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually green or reddish in color, followed by small, round berries. It thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Peperomia tomentella is relatively low maintenance and suitable for beginners due to its tolerance for dry conditions.
