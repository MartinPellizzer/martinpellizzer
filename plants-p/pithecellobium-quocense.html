Pithecellobium quocense

Pithecellobium quocense, commonly known as the Quocense crownflower, is a species native to Central America.

It is a small, evergreen shrub that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant features compound leaves with ovate to elliptic leaflets and produces small, fragrant flowers in clusters. Its fruit is a pod containing several seeds, which are dispersed by wind or animals.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
