Pseudosasa gracilis

Pseudosasa gracilis, commonly known as the slender bamboo, is a member of the grass family (Poaceae) and is native to China.

It is characterized by its slender, hollow stems that are often used in traditional Chinese medicine. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as mountain forests. Its leaves are long and narrow, with a smooth texture and a slightly hairy underside.

Pseudosasa gracilis is valued for both its ornamental appearance and its medicinal properties.
