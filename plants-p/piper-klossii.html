Piper klossii

Piper klossii, commonly known as the Kloss's pepper, is a flowering plant native to the highlands of New Guinea.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species. The plant is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and small, ovate leaves with a glossy texture. Its flowers are unisexual, with separate male and female flowers on the same plant, and it produces small, round berries that are typically green when mature.

The plant is valued for its aromatic properties and is used in traditional medicine and as a spice in some regions.
