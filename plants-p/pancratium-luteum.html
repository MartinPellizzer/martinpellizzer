Pancratium luteum

Pancratium luteum, commonly known as the yellow lantern or sea daffodil, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers that bloom in late summer and early autumn. The plant has narrow, strap-like leaves and produces a distinctive, lantern-shaped seed capsule. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in dune environments.

Pancratium luteum is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
