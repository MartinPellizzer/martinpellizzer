Portulaca trigona

Portulaca trigona, commonly known as the three-angled portulaca, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa and Asia.

It is a low-growing, succulent species that thrives in hot, dry climates with minimal water requirements. The plant features fleshy, triangular leaves and produces vibrant, showy flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its drought tolerance and attractive appearance.

Portulaca trigona is also used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
