Primula candicans

Primula candicans, commonly known as the silverleaf primrose, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive silver-white leaves and clusters of bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant typically grows in rocky or well-drained soils and thrives in cool, moist environments. It is a perennial species that produces showy blooms in early spring, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine displays.

The leaves are covered in a fine layer of white wax, which helps to reduce water loss and reflect sunlight.
