Pinus jaliscana subsp. macvaughii

Pinus jaliscana subsp.

macvaughii, commonly known as the MacVaugh pine, is a rare and endemic species native to the highlands of Jalisco, Mexico. It is a coniferous evergreen tree characterized by its needle-like leaves and cone-bearing structure typical of the pine family.

The tree typically grows in well-drained soils and thrives in temperate to subtropical climates. Its cones are usually small and woody, containing seeds that are dispersed by wind.
