Polygonatum infundiflorum

Polygonatum infundiflorum, commonly known as the Chinese Solomon's seal, is a perennial herb native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Asparagaceae family and is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit. The plant features lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Its ornamental value and medicinal properties make it a notable species in horticulture and traditional medicine.
