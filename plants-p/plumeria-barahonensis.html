Plumeria barahonensis

Plumeria barahonensis, commonly known as the Bahamian plumeria, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean islands.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its showy, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to yellow. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, with smooth, greyish bark and palmate leaves. Its flowers bloom in the summer and are often used in traditional crafts and ceremonies.

The species is also valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in tropical gardens.
