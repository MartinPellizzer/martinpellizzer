Primula × cridalensis

Primula × cridalensis, commonly known as the hybrid primrose, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Primula vulgaris and Primula obconica.

It is a low-growing perennial herb with showy, bell-shaped flowers that come in a variety of colors, including white, pink, and violet. The plant typically blooms in early spring and is native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe. It prefers well-drained, moist soils and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Its foliage consists of oval, dark green leaves that are often covered in a fine downy coating.
