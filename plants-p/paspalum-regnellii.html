Paspalum regnellii

Paspalum regnellii, commonly known as silvergrass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-gray blades and compact growth habit, often forming dense mats. The plant typically thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks. Its flowering heads are small and clustered, producing pale green to brown spikelets.

Paspalum regnellii is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in wet environments.
