Physocarpus sinensis

Physocarpus sinensis, commonly known as the Chinese shrub honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its dense, upright growth habit and its showy, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, red berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value.

Physocarpus sinensis is also valued for its ability to tolerate a variety of soil conditions and its resilience to pests and diseases.
