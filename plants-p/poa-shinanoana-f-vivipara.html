Poa shinanoana f. vivipara

Poa shinanoana f. vivipara, commonly known as the viviparous meadowgrass, is a rare and unique variant of the meadowgrass species native to Japan.

It is characterized by its ability to produce viviparous seeds, which germinate while still attached to the parent plant, giving it a distinct reproductive strategy. The plant typically grows in moist, temperate environments, often found in mountainous regions with well-drained soils. Its foliage is grass-like, with narrow, blade-like leaves that are usually green and slightly hairy.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
