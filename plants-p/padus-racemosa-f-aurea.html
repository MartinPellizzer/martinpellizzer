Padus racemosa f. aurea

Padus racemosa f. aurea, commonly known as the golden-flowered whitebeam, is a shrub or small tree native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and oval-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers in spring, which are followed by red, hairy fruits. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodlands and stream banks.

This variety is notable for its distinctive golden hue in the foliage, which adds ornamental value to gardens.
