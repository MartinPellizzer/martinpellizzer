Platyruscus hypophyllum

Platyruscus hypophyllum, commonly known as the broad-leafed rock plant, is a unique succulent native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that store water, enabling it to survive in dry conditions. The plant typically grows in rocky crevices, where it thrives in well-drained soil. Its flowers are small and often pale green or yellow, which helps it blend with its surroundings.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
