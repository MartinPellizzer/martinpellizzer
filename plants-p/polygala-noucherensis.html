Polygala noucherensis

Polygala noucherensis, commonly known as the Noucher's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the Sahara Desert.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that often display a striking contrast of colors. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments. Its leaves are usually narrow and hairy, helping to reduce water loss.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small desert organisms.
