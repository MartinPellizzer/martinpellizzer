Petroselinum anatolicum

Petroselinum anatolicum, commonly known as Anatolian parsley, is a member of the Apiaceae family.

It is native to the Mediterranean region, particularly Turkey. The plant features finely divided, feathery leaves that are aromatic and often used in culinary applications. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers that appear in umbels, typical of the carrot family.

The roots and leaves are valued for their mild parsley-like flavor and potential medicinal properties.
