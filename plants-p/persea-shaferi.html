Persea shaferi

Persea shaferi, commonly known as the Mexican persimmon, is a small evergreen tree native to Mexico and Central America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and fragrant flowers. The tree produces small, orange-red fruits that are edible when fully mature. The fruit has a sweet, sometimes astringent flavor and is often consumed fresh or used in culinary applications.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in tropical and subtropical climates.
