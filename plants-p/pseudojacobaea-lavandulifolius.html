Pseudojacobaea lavandulifolius

Pseudojacobaea lavandulifolius, commonly known as the lavender-leaved Jacobaea, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its aromatic, linear leaves that resemble those of lavender, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, yellow to orange composite flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is cultivated in gardens and used in traditional herbal remedies.
