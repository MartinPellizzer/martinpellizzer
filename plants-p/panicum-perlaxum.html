Panicum perlaxum

Panicum perlaxum, commonly known as silky panicgrass, is a warm-season grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its fine, silky texture and drooping inflorescences that resemble plumes. The plant typically grows in open areas, such as roadsides and fields, and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, while the seed heads are often used in ornamental gardening.

Panicum perlaxum is also valued for its ability to stabilize soil and provide forage for wildlife.
