Pinus morinda

Pinus morinda, commonly known as the Morinda pine, is a rare and unique conifer native to the mountainous regions of China.

It is characterized by its slender, often twisted trunk and its dense, needle-like foliage that forms compact, spherical crowns. The plant produces small, cone-like structures that contain both male and female reproductive organs, typical of coniferous species. Its leaves are typically short and stiff, adapted to withstand harsh environmental conditions.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, Pinus morinda is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
