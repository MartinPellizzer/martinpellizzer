Phyllanthus aeneus var. papillosus

Phyllanthus aeneus var. papillosus, commonly known as the silverleaf spurge, is a shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage with a distinctive silvery or bluish sheen on the underside of the leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, followed by capsule fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas or along roadsides.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and traditional medicine.
