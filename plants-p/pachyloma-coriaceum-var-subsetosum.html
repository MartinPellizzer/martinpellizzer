Pachyloma coriaceum var. subsetosum

Pachyloma coriaceum var. subsetosum, commonly known as the velvety pachyloma, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features fleshy, dark green leaves covered in a dense layer of fine, velvety hairs, which help reduce water loss in arid environments. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the summer months, adding a delicate contrast to its foliage. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Its compact growth habit and unique texture make it a desirable addition to collections of succulents and cacti.
