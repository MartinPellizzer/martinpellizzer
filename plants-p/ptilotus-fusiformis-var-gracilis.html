Ptilotus fusiformis var. gracilis

Ptilotus fusiformis var. gracilis, commonly known as the slender spinifex, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its spiny, grass-like foliage and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid regions, thriving in sandy or gravelly soils. Its unique morphology and adaptability to harsh environments make it a notable species in ecological studies.

The flowers, which are usually pink or red, attract various pollinators such as bees and birds.
