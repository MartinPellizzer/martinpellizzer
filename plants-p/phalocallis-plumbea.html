Phalocallis plumbea

Phalocallis plumbea, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant features slender, upright stems covered in soft, blue-gray foliage that gives it its common name. It produces clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall.

Phalocallis plumbea is often used in ornamental gardening for its attractive appearance and ability to thrive in partial shade.
