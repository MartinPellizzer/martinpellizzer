Phyllocactus cooperi

Phyllocactus cooperi, commonly known as the Cooper's phyllocactus, is a unique cactus species native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, flattened, leaf-like stems that are often mistaken for foliage, which are actually modified stems called cladodes. These cladodes are green and photosynthetic, allowing the plant to thrive in low-water environments. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the warmer months, adding a striking contrast to its succulent structure.

Its ability to store water in its stems makes it well adapted to desert and semi-arid climates.
