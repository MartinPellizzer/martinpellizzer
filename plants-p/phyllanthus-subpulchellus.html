Phyllanthus subpulchellus

Phyllanthus subpulchellus, commonly known as the small beauty leaf, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptic to ovate in shape. The plant produces small, clustered yellow flowers that are surrounded by a fleshy, greenish cupule. It is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and compact growth habit.

Phyllanthus subpulchellus is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
