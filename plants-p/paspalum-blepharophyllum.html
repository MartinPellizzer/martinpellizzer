Paspalum blepharophyllum

Paspalum blepharophyllum, commonly known as the silverhair grass, is a perennial grass species native to the southern United States and parts of Mexico.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-gray foliage and tufted growth habit, which gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of water bodies. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets that are loosely arranged, and it produces small, wind-pollinated flowers.

Paspalum blepharophyllum is valued for its ornamental qualities and its ability to stabilize soil in wetland environments.
