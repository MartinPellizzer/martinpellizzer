Pilosella kalksburgensis subsp. anosciadia

Pilosella kalksburgensis subsp.

anosciadia, commonly known as the Kalksburg hairgrass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Europe. It typically grows in alpine and subalpine habitats, often on rocky or gravelly soils.

The plant features a rosette of basal leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its flowers are small, yellow, and clustered in dense, disc-like inflorescences, which are characteristic of the Asteraceae family.

This subspecies is noted for its ecological adaptability and is often found in open, sunny environments at high elevations.
