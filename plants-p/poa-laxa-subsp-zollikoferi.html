Poa laxa subsp. zollikoferi

Poa laxa subsp.

zollikoferi, commonly known as the Zollikofer's bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern South America. It typically grows in moist, temperate climates and is often found in grasslands and along riverbanks.

The plant has slender, tufted culms with narrow, bluish-green leaves that are often rolled in the bud. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences with pale blue to white florets.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in wetland habitats.
