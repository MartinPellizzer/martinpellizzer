Porophyllum nodosum

Porophyllum nodosum, commonly known as the Mexican sunflower, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its bright orange to yellow ray florets and green, lobed leaves. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and chrysanthemums.

Porophyllum nodosum is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
