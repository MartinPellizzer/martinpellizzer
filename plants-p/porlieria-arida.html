Porlieria arida

Porlieria arida, commonly known as the desert lily, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa and the Middle East.

It is a perennial herb with a robust root system that allows it to survive in harsh, dry environments. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale pink, which attract pollinators such as bees and moths. Its leaves are small and succulent, reducing water loss through transpiration.

Porlieria arida is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
