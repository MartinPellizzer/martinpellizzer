Passiflora maximiliana var. expansa

Passiflora maximiliana var. expansa, commonly known as the giant passionflower, is a climbing vine native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that display a mix of blue, violet, and white hues, often with prominent red filaments. The plant produces edible berries that are typically yellow or orange when ripe. Its leaves are broad and heart-shaped, often with a velvety texture.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in warm climates for its striking floral display.
