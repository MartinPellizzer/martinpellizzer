Parosela tomentosa var. psoraleoides

Parosela tomentosa var. psoraleoides, commonly known as the woolly parosela, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features a mat-forming growth habit with dense, hairy foliage that is often grayish-white in appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in late summer. Its leaves are ovate to lanceolate in shape and are covered in fine, soft hairs.

This species is adapted to rocky and sandy soils, thriving in well-drained environments.
