Polystachya rhodochila

Polystachya rhodochila, commonly known as the pink-stemmed or red-stemmed orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink and white. The plant has strap-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss in its humid habitat. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.

Polystachya rhodochila is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated for its unique floral display.
