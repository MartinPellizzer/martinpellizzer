Phaseolus multiflorus var. albiflorus

Phaseolus multiflorus var. albiflorus, commonly known as the white-flowered scarlet runner bean, is a tropical legume native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its vigorous climbing habit and large, showy white flowers that contrast with its green foliage. The plant produces elongated, edible pods that contain kidney-shaped seeds, often used in culinary applications. It thrives in warm climates with well-drained soil and requires full sun for optimal growth.

This variety is prized for both its ornamental value and its nutritional benefits.
