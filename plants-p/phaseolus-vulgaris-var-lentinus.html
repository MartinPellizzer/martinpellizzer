Phaseolus vulgaris var. lentinus

Phaseolus vulgaris var. lentinus, commonly known as the scarlet runner bean, is a variant of the common bean species native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its vibrant red or orange flowers and elongated, edible pods that contain kidney-shaped seeds. This plant typically grows as an annual vine, reaching heights of up to 3 meters, and thrives in warm, sunny climates with well-drained soil. Its botanical features include compound leaves with three leaflets and a fibrous root system that helps stabilize the plant.

The scarlet runner bean is often cultivated for both its ornamental value and as a source of nutritious legumes.
