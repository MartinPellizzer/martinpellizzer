Piper puberuliciliatum

Piper puberuliciliatum, commonly known as the hairy-stemmed pepper vine, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species. The plant is characterized by its hairy stems and small, greenish-white flowers. Its leaves are typically ovate with a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

The fruit is a small, elongated berry that turns from green to yellow or red as it matures.
