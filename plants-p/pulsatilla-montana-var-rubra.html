Pulsatilla montana var. rubra

Pulsatilla montana var. rubra, commonly known as the red pasqueflower, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, showy pink to red flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in a basal rosette. It thrives in well-drained, sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments.

The red pasqueflower is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
