Poa aperta

Poa aperta, commonly known as the open grass or silver grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine, silvery hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, wetlands, and along stream banks.

Poa aperta is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in ecological systems as a food source for various insects and birds.
