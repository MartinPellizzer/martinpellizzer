Pyrus epirotica

Pyrus epirotica, commonly known as the Epirotic pear, is a species of flowering plant in the family Rosaceae.

It is native to the regions of northern Greece and southern Albania, where it grows in mountainous areas. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in the spring, followed by edible pears that are typically harvested in late summer. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a slightly serrated edge.

The Epirotic pear is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical significance in the region.
