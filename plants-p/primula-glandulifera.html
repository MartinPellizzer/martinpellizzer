Primula glandulifera

Primula glandulifera, commonly known as the glandular primrose, is a herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features a basal rosette of lanceolate, hairy leaves that are often glandular-dotted. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, or purple, typically blooming in early spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers cool, shaded environments.

Primula glandulifera is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
