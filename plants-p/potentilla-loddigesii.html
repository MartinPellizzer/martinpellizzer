Potentilla loddigesii

Potentilla loddigesii, commonly known as the Loddiges' cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of central Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial shrub with small, palmately lobed leaves that are typically hairy beneath. The plant produces clusters of bright yellow flowers in the summer, which are composed of numerous tiny petals. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
