Phyllostegia knudsenii

Phyllostegia knudsenii, commonly known as Knudsen's mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its opposite, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers. It typically grows in moist, sandy soils in open woodlands and along streambanks.

Phyllostegia knudsenii is often found in the understory of forests, where it thrives in partial shade.
