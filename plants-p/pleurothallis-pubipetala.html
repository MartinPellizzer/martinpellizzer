Pleurothallis pubipetala

Pleurothallis pubipetala, commonly known as the hairy-lip pleurothallis, is a species of orchid native to the cloud forests of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and yellow. The plant has a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that support its foliage and flowers. Its leaves are usually narrow and covered in fine, hairy structures, giving it its common name.

Pleurothallis pubipetala prefers high humidity and indirect light, making it a popular choice for tropical terrariums and greenhouses.
