Podocarpus valdivianus

Podocarpus valdivianus, commonly known as the Valdivian cedar, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to southern South America.

It is characterized by its needle-like leaves and produces small, spherical cones. The tree can grow up to 30 meters tall, with a straight trunk and a dense, conical crown. Its bark is thick and scaly, providing protection against environmental stress.

This species plays an important ecological role in its native habitats, contributing to biodiversity and soil stability.
