Prunus pseudoarmeniaca

Prunus pseudoarmeniaca, commonly known as the Armenian cherry or rock cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Caucasus region.

It produces small, white to pinkish flowers in early spring, followed by edible drupes that are typically red or purple in color. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and its ability to thrive in rocky or arid environments. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has historical uses in traditional medicine.

The fruit can be used in jams and preserves, contributing to its cultural significance in the region.
