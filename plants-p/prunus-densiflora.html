Prunus densiflora

Prunus densiflora, commonly known as the denseflower cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and profuse flowering displays in early spring. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters, adding ornamental value to gardens. Its leaves are ovate to elliptic, with a smooth margin and a glossy green upper surface.

Prunus densiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in horticultural settings for its spring blossoms.
