Prunella grandiflora var. alpina

Prunella grandiflora var. alpina, commonly known as alpine self-violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to alpine regions of Europe and Asia.

It features slender, upright stems covered with dense, soft hairs, and produces small, violet-blue flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered with fine pubescence. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments.

Prunella grandiflora var. alpina is often found in meadows, grasslands, and mountainous areas where it contributes to the biodiversity of alpine ecosystems.
