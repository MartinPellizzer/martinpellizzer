Pimenta urquiolae

Pimenta urquiolae, commonly known as the Urquillo's pepper tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its small, fragrant white flowers and dark red, edible berries. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments and thrives in well-drained soil. Its leaves are alternate, glossy, and have a slightly aromatic scent.

The fruit is often used in local cuisines and has potential medicinal properties.
