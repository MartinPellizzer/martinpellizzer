Physurus wrightianus

Physurus wrightianus, commonly known as the Wright's spurge.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa, characterized by its succulent, fleshy stems and leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for xeriscaping in arid environments.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
