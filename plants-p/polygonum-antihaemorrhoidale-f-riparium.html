Polygonum antihaemorrhoidale f. riparium

Polygonum antihaemorrhoidale f. riparium, commonly known as river knotweed, is a variant of the Polygonum genus characterized by its aquatic or semi-aquatic growth habit.

It typically features heart-shaped leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface, and its stems are often reddish in color. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually pink or purple in hue, which bloom during the summer months. It thrives in moist environments such as riverbanks, wetlands, and marshy areas.

Due to its adaptability, it is often found in regions with high humidity and abundant water sources.
