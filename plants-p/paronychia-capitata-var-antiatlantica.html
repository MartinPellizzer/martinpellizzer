Paronychia capitata var. antiatlantica

Paronychia capitata var. antiatlantica, commonly known as the Atlantic rockrose, is a perennial shrub native to the coastal regions of the Atlantic coast.

It features dense, compact growth with small, clustered flowers that bloom in shades of white, pink, and red. The plant has small, leathery leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs, providing a textured appearance. It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and is often found in rocky or coastal habitats.

This variety is valued for its hardiness and ability to tolerate salty air and coastal conditions.
