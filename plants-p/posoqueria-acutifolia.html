Posoqueria acutifolia

Posoqueria acutifolia, commonly known as the sharp-leaved posoqueria, is a tropical plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is a member of the Arecaceae family, which includes palms, and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and pinnately compound leaves. The leaves are typically narrow and have a sharp, pointed tip, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in coastal areas or near freshwater sources.

Posoqueria acutifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated as a decorative plant in tropical gardens.
