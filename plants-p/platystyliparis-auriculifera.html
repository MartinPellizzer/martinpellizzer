Platystyliparis auriculifera

Platystyliparis auriculifera, commonly known as the auriculate leaf orchid, is a rare and delicate orchid species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the rainy season. The plant has slender, erect stems and possesses lance-shaped leaves with a distinctive auriculate (ear-like) base. Its botanical features make it a unique subject for botanical study and conservation efforts.

Due to its limited habitat and threats from deforestation, it is considered endangered in its native range.
