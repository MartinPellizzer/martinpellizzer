Panicum gyrans

Panicum gyrans, commonly known as the whirling grass, is a species of flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its distinctive, spiral-shaped inflorescences that resemble a whirlwind, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, spikelet-based flowers that are typically green or brown in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open areas such as fields and disturbed sites.

Panicum gyrans is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping due to its unique appearance.
