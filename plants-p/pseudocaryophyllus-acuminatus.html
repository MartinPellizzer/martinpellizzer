Pseudocaryophyllus acuminatus

Pseudocaryophyllus acuminatus, commonly known as the sharp-tipped sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of North America.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its small, spiny leaves and clusters of pink or purple flowers. The plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments. Its unique morphology helps it reduce water loss and withstand extreme temperatures.

It is often found in desert scrub and grassland habitats.
