Piper parthenium

Piper parthenium, commonly known as feverfew, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its small, white or yellowish flowers and finely divided leaves. The plant is often grown for its ornamental value due to its delicate appearance and aromatic foliage. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Piper parthenium is also known for its medicinal properties, traditionally used to treat headaches and inflammatory conditions.
