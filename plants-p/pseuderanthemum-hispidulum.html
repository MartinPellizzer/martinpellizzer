Pseuderanthemum hispidulum

Pseuderanthemum hispidulum, commonly known as the hairy pseuderanthemum, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and hairy stems. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that are typically blue or purple in color, often arranged in clusters at the ends of the branches. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often covered with fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Pseuderanthemum hispidulum is commonly cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and flowers.
