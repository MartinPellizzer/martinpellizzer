Polygala sikokiana

Polygala sikokiana, commonly known as the Malawi catchfly, is a flowering plant native to the eastern regions of Africa.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its delicate, branched inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories. Its flowers are usually small and inconspicuous, with a distinctive bilabiate structure.

Polygala sikokiana is valued for its ornamental potential and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
