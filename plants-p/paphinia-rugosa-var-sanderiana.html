Paphinia rugosa var. sanderiana

Paphinia rugosa var. sanderiana, commonly known as the Sander's paphiopedilum, is a rare and highly sought-after orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its intricate, velvety petals and a distinctive, pouch-like lip that is essential for its pollination mechanism. The plant typically grows as a terrestrial orchid, thriving in humid, shaded environments with well-drained soil. Its flowers are usually a mix of white and green, with a subtle fragrance that attracts specific pollinators.

Due to its rarity and horticultural value, it is often cultivated in greenhouses and requires careful attention to maintain its health and blooming cycle.
