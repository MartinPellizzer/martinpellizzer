Phlox corymbosa

Phlox corymbosa, commonly known as the showy phlox, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of fragrant, showy flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The flowers are typically pink to lavender in color and are arranged in terminal corymbs, giving the plant its scientific name. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are opposite in arrangement and are covered in fine hairs.

It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often found in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or open areas.
