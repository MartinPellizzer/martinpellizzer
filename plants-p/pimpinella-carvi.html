Pimpinella carvi

Pimpinella carvi, commonly known as caraway, is a biennial or annual herb native to Europe and western Asia.

It features finely divided, feathery leaves and produces small, white or greenish-yellow flowers in umbels. The plant is characterized by its aromatic, seed-like fruits that are widely used in culinary and medicinal applications. Its roots and leaves contain essential oils with compounds such as limonene and carvone.

Pimpinella carvi thrives in well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its distinctive flavor and therapeutic properties.
