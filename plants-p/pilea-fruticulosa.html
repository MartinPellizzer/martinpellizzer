Pilea fruticulosa

Pilea fruticulosa, commonly known as the Chinese evergreen, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring dense, upright stems covered in soft, fuzzy leaves. The leaves are ovate to elliptical in shape, with a waxy texture and a deep green color that often has a slight variegation. It produces small, inconspicuous white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by small, round berries.

This plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental foliage.
