Pereskia moorei

Pereskia moorei, commonly known as the Moore's pereskia, is a species of flowering plant in the Cactaceae family.

It is native to Mexico and is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems that are covered in stiff, spiny hairs. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are usually small and may be present during certain seasons, though it is primarily a stem-based plant.

Pereskia moorei is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in arid environments.
