Polystichum piceopaleaceum

Polystichum piceopaleaceum, commonly known as the pale-leaf wood fern, is a species of evergreen fern native to the forests of eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, upright fronds that can reach up to 1.5 meters in height. The fronds are bipinnate, with a distinctive pale, almost silvery coloration on the underside, which gives the plant its common name. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in temperate and subtropical regions.

The plant's rhizomes are thick and fleshy, allowing it to store moisture and nutrients efficiently.
