Perezia coerulescens

Perezia coerulescens, commonly known as the blue-flowered perezia, is a member of the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is native to South Africa and is characterized by its striking blue-violet flowers that bloom in summer. The plant features strap-shaped leaves that are typically green with a waxy texture. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
