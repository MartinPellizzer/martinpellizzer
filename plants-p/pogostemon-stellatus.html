Pogostemon stellatus

Pogostemon stellatus, commonly known as the star pogostemon, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It features a square, hairy stem and opposite leaves that are lance-shaped with serrated edges. The plant produces small, white to purple flowers in whorled clusters, which are typically arranged in a star-like pattern. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in wetlands or along streams.

Its aromatic leaves and medicinal properties make it a valued plant in traditional herbal medicine.
