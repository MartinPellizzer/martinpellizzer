Paraspalathus argentea

Paraspalathus argentea, commonly known as the silver leaf plant, is a species native to certain regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which gives it its common name. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to several meters. Its leaves are simple and alternate, with a leathery texture and prominent veins.

Paraspalathus argentea is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
