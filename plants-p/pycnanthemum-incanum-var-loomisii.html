Pycnanthemum incanum var. loomisii

Pycnanthemum incanum var. loomisii, commonly known as silverleaf mountain mint, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its grayish-white, fuzzy leaves and showy lavender to pinkish-blue flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist to wet soils in woodland or swampy areas. It has a square stem and is often found in shaded to partially shaded environments.

Its aromatic foliage and nectar-rich flowers make it an important plant for pollinators.
