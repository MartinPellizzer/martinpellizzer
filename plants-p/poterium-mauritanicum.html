Poterium mauritanicum

Poterium mauritanicum, commonly known as the Mauritania thornapple, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its small, spiny leaves and clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces small, round fruits that are edible when ripe, though they are often bitter. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for arid environments.

Poterium mauritanicum is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
