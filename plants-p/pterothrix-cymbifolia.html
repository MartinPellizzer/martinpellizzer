Pterothrix cymbifolia

Pterothrix cymbifolia, commonly known as the golden bell vine, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its long, pendulous clusters of yellow-orange flowers that resemble small bells. The plant produces small, oval-shaped fruits that are typically green when mature and turn brown as they dry. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Pterothrix cymbifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
