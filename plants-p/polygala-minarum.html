Polygala minarum

Polygala minarum, commonly known as the lesser milk-vetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the Polygalaceae family.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. The plant features small, clustered flowers that are usually pink or purple in color, with a distinctive spurred structure on the petals. Its leaves are alternate and pinnately compound, often with a hairy texture.

Polygala minarum is native to regions of Europe and parts of Asia, where it thrives in temperate climates.
