Pyrrhopappus taraxacoides

Pyrrhopappus taraxacoides, commonly known as the desert dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble dandelions. The plant has basal leaves that are typically lobed or toothed, and it produces yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. It is adapted to harsh, dry environments and has a deep root system to access underground water.

The plant is often found in rocky soils and is well-suited to xerophytic conditions.
