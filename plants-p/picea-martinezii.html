Picea martinezii

Picea martinezii, commonly known as the Martinez spruce, is a species of evergreen conifer native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, pyramidal shape and dark green, needle-like leaves. The tree produces small, cone-like structures that contain its seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in mountainous regions.

The bark of Picea martinezii is typically thin and reddish-brown, providing a distinctive appearance.
