Polygonum aviculare var. paucicarpum

Polygonum aviculare var. paucicarpum, commonly known as the lesser knotweed, is a low-growing perennial plant characterized by its prostrate or ascending habit.

It features small, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy, and it produces tiny, greenish-white flowers in clusters. The plant is typically found in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and fields, and is adapted to a wide range of environmental conditions. It belongs to the buckwheat family (Polygonaceae) and is known for its rapid growth and ability to colonize open areas.

Its botanical features make it a common weed in temperate regions.
