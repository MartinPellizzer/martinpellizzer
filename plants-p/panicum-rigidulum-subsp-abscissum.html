Panicum rigidulum subsp. abscissum

Panicum rigidulum subsp.

abscissum, commonly known as the rigid false panicgrass, is a grass species native to the southeastern United States. It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves with a bluish-green hue.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, pale green to brownish flowers. It thrives in wetland environments, often found in marshes and along the edges of water bodies.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems, contributing to soil stabilization and habitat for various wildlife.
