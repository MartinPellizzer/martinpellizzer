Piper japurense

Piper japurense, commonly known as the Japurensis pepper, is a climbing vine native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many species of pepper plants. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are typically followed by small, round berries. The berries are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Its botanical characteristics make it an interesting subject for both horticultural and pharmacological study.
