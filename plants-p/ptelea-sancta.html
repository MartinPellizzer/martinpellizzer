Ptelea sancta

Ptelea sancta, commonly known as the sacred ptelea, is a small evergreen shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers in the spring, which are followed by dark blue to black berries. Ptelea sancta is often found in rocky soils and arid environments, thriving in full sun.

Its botanical features make it an important species in local ecosystems and a subject of interest in botanical studies.
