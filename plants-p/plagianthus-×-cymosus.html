Plagianthus × cymosus

Plagianthus × cymosus, commonly known as the African daisy, is a hybrid plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant typically grows as a shrub or perennial herb, featuring narrow, grass-like leaves and compact flower heads. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Plagianthus × cymosus is also valued for its drought tolerance and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
