Pteroxygonum

Pteroxygonum, commonly known as the waterwheel plant, is a unique aquatic plant characterized by its whorled arrangement of leaves.

It possesses a specialized structure called a "waterwheel," which consists of a central stem surrounded by a series of leaf-like structures that rotate with water currents. This adaptation helps the plant capture nutrients and oxygen from the water. Pteroxygonum is typically found in slow-moving freshwater habitats such as ponds and streams.

Its botanical features make it an important component of aquatic ecosystems.
