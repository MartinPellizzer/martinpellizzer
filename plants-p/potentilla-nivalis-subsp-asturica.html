Potentilla nivalis subsp. asturica

Potentilla nivalis subsp.

asturica, commonly known as the Asturian snow willow, is a low-growing shrub native to the mountainous regions of northern Spain. It is characterized by its dense, silver-gray foliage and small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring.

The plant thrives in rocky, alpine environments and is adapted to cold, high-altitude conditions. Its compact growth habit and frost-resistant nature make it well-suited for harsh mountain climates.

The subspecies is notable for its horticultural value and ecological importance in alpine ecosystems.
