Primula flaccida

Primula flaccida, commonly known as the drooping primrose, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and has hairy, succulent leaves that help it retain water. Its flowers bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Primula flaccida is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with cool, humid conditions.
