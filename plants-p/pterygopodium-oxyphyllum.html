Pterygopodium oxyphyllum

Pterygopodium oxyphyllum, commonly known as the silver leaf tree, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a silvery, waxy coating. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by elongated, woody fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
