Phaleria macrocarpa

Phaleria macrocarpa, commonly known as the large-fruited nutmeg tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Maluku Islands in Indonesia.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae and is characterized by its glossy, elongated leaves and fragrant flowers. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into large, woody fruits containing aromatic seeds. Its bark and leaves have been traditionally used in herbal medicine for their purported health benefits.

The plant is valued not only for its economic importance in the spice trade but also for its ecological role in tropical forests.
