Premna neurophylla

Premna neurophylla, commonly known as the velvetleaf or Indian nettle, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 6 meters in height, featuring opposite, ovate leaves with a velvety texture. The leaves are typically dark green on the upper surface and lighter on the underside, while the flowers are small, greenish-white, and clustered in panicles. It produces small, fleshy fruits that are often eaten by birds and other wildlife.

This plant is often found in disturbed habitats and is used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
