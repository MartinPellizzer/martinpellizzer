Paspalum barbatum var. laxius

Paspalum barbatum var. laxius, commonly known as the hairy paspalum, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves with hairy texture. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in supporting local wildlife.
