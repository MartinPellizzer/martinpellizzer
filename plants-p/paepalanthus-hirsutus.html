Paepalanthus hirsutus

Paepalanthus hirsutus, commonly known as the hairy puya, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Argentina and Chile.

It belongs to the family Xanthorrhoeaceae and is characterized by its tall, grass-like appearance with dense, spiky inflorescences. The plant produces small, colorful flowers that bloom in late summer, often in shades of red, orange, and yellow. It thrives in dry, sunny environments and is adapted to withstand harsh climatic conditions.

The hairy puya is also noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking visual appeal.
