Papaver venustum

Papaver venustum, commonly known as the noble poppy, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features showy, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from pale pink to deep red, with a distinctive black stigma. The plant has smooth, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in alpine or rocky habitats.

Papaver venustum is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
