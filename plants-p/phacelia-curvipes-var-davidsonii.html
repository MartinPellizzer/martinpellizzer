Phacelia curvipes var. davidsonii

Phacelia curvipes var. davidsonii, commonly known as the Davidson's phacelia, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and hairy, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
