Psidium incanescens var. rotundifolium

Psidium incanescens var. rotundifolium, commonly known as the round-leaf guava, is a species native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a small evergreen tree or shrub that typically grows to a height of 3 to 5 meters. The leaves are ovate to elliptic, with a rounded base and a hairy underside, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its flowers are small, white, and clustered in inflorescences, while the fruit is a small, yellowish or reddish berry with a sweet, aromatic flavor.

This plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and is also used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
