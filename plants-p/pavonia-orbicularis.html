Pavonia orbicularis

Pavonia orbicularis, commonly known as the roundleaf false pomegranate, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves and showy, red or pink bract-like flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full sun. Its botanical features include opposite leaf arrangement and a woody stem that becomes more pronounced with age.

Pavonia orbicularis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
