Priestleya multiflora

Priestleya multiflora, commonly known as the mountain stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to the high elevations of the western United States.

It features compact, rosette-shaped leaves that are thick and fleshy, adapted to store water in arid environments. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow and orange during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Priestleya multiflora is also valued for its ornamental appeal and low maintenance requirements.
