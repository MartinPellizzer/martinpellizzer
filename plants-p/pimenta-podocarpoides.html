Pimenta podocarpoides

Pimenta podocarpoides, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and small, red berries. The plant is notable for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its tolerance to drought conditions. It plays an important ecological role in its native habitats, providing food for various wildlife species.

However, it is also considered an invasive species in some regions outside its native range.
