Pavonia chlorantha

Pavonia chlorantha, commonly known as the green pavonia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of pink to red, which bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are ovate and covered in a fine, silvery-white down, giving the plant a distinctive appearance.

Pavonia chlorantha thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.
