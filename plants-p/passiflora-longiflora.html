Passiflora longiflora

Passiflora longiflora, commonly known as the long-flowered passionflower, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its striking, complex flowers that feature five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments around the reproductive structures. The plant produces blue or purple berries that are edible and often used in culinary applications. Its leaves are alternate, heart-shaped, and have a rough texture.

Passiflora longiflora is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported calming effects.
