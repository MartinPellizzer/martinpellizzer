Papaver pilosum subsp. sparsipilosum

Papaver pilosum subsp.

sparsipilosum, commonly known as the sparse-hairy poppy, is a herbaceous perennial native to the Mediterranean region. It features striking, showy flowers with bright red petals that are often marked with dark veins.

The plant has hairy stems and leaves, which contribute to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Its botanical characteristics make it both ornamental and ecologically significant in its native habitat.
