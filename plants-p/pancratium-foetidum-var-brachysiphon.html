Pancratium foetidum var. brachysiphon

Pancratium foetidum var. brachysiphon, commonly known as the narrow-siphon yellow crocus, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and sandy soils, thriving in well-drained conditions. Its leaves are linear and grass-like, often forming a basal rosette.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
