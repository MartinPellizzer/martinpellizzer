Paracaryum cappadocicum

Paracaryum cappadocicum, commonly known as the Cappadocian bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the Cappadocia region of Turkey.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, often with a distinctive purple or red throat. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Paracaryum cappadocicum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is relatively easy to grow in temperate climates.
