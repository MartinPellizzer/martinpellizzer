Pentameris rupestris

Pentameris rupestris, commonly known as the rock pentameris, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments. Its leaves are succulent, adapted to store water, and it produces capsule fruits that contain seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in rock gardens.
