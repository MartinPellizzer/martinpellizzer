Phlomis ghilanica

Phlomis ghilanica, commonly known as the Persian mullein, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Iran.

It belongs to the family Lamiaceae, which includes many aromatic plants known for their medicinal properties. The plant features tall, erect stems covered with soft, woolly hairs, and it produces showy, clustered flowers in shades of purple or pink. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered with a dense layer of fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Phlomis ghilanica is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient plant in harsh climates.
