Prunus megacarpa

Prunus megacarpa, commonly known as the giant cherry or large-fruited cherry, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to China and is characterized by its large, showy flowers and edible, sweet fruits. The plant typically grows as a small tree or shrub, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a smooth texture, while its bark is smooth and grayish in color.

The fruit, which is a drupe, can weigh up to 50 grams and is valued for its culinary and ornamental uses.
