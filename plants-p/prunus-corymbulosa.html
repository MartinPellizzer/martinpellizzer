Prunus corymbulosa

Prunus corymbulosa, commonly known as the Japanese cherry or mountain cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, rounded crown and clusters of white or pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, edible fruits known as cherries, which are typically harvested in late summer. Its leaves are ovate with serrated edges and turn yellow in autumn.

Prunus corymbulosa is valued in horticulture for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
