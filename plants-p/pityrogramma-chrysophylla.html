Pityrogramma chrysophylla

Pityrogramma chrysophylla, commonly known as the golden maidenhair fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Pityrogrammaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that are typically bright green, with a distinctive golden hue on the underside. Native to tropical regions of Asia, it thrives in moist, shaded environments. The plant's spores are produced on the underside of its fronds, which are released during the dry season.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for indoor and garden cultivation.
