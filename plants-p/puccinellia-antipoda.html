Puccinellia antipoda

Puccinellia antipoda, commonly known as the coastal sandgrass, is a flowering plant native to arid and coastal regions of the Americas.

It is a perennial grass that thrives in sandy soils and is often found in dunes and salt marshes. The plant has narrow, linear leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It is adapted to withstand high salinity and strong winds, making it an important stabilizer of coastal ecosystems.

Its ecological role includes providing habitat for various invertebrates and birds.
