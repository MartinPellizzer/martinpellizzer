Pleurochaenia quadrangularis

Pleurochaenia quadrangularis, commonly known as the four-cornered watermilfoil, is a aquatic plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its square stems and small, opposite leaves that are often submerged. The plant produces tiny, greenish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments.

Due to its rapid growth, it can become invasive in certain water bodies.
