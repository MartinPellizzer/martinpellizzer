Polygala confusa

Polygala confusa, commonly known as the narrow-leaved milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia and Australia.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its slender, often hairy stems and small, delicate flowers. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, thriving in well-drained soils. Its flowers are usually pale green to yellowish in color, and it produces small, round fruits that contain a single seed.

Polygala confusa is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.
