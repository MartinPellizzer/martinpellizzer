Pelargonium × kernhoferianum

Pelargonium × kernhoferianum, commonly known as the ivy-leaf geranium, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Geraniaceae family.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and heart-shaped, dark green leaves with a slightly waxy texture. The plant produces clusters of small, five-petaled flowers in shades of pink, red, or white, depending on the cultivar. It is native to South Africa and is often cultivated as a decorative plant in gardens and hanging baskets.

Pelargonium × kernhoferianum thrives in well-drained soil and requires moderate sunlight to flourish.
