Phyllocalyx grandifolius var. pyriformis

Phyllocalyx grandifolius var. pyriformis, commonly known as the broad-leafed phyllocalyx, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can reach up to 30 cm in length. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in humid, tropical environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for both ornamental and ecological purposes.
