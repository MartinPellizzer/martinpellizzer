Panicum equisetum

Panicum equisetum, commonly known as the common panicgrass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, often hairy stems. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, three-angled spikelets. Its leaves are narrow and linear, with a rough texture and a bluish-green color.

Panicum equisetum is often found in disturbed soils and is adapted to a wide range of environmental conditions.
