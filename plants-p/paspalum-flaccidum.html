Paspalum flaccidum

Paspalum flaccidum, commonly known as the flaccid paspalum, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

Paspalum flaccidum is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in wetland environments.
