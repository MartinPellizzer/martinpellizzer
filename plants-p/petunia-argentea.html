Petunia argentea

Petunia argentea, commonly known as silver petunia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and tobacco. The plant is characterized by its silver-gray foliage and clusters of showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking ornamental plants.

Petunia argentea is often used in landscaping for its aesthetic appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
