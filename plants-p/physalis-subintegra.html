Physalis subintegra

Physalis subintegra, commonly known as the goldenberry or Inca berry, is a species of flowering plant in the nightshade family, Solanaceae.

It is native to the Andean region of South America and is characterized by its distinctive papery calyx that encases the fruit. The fruit is typically yellow or orange, with a sweet, tangy flavor and a crisp, juicy texture. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible fruit, which can be used in jams, desserts, and beverages.

The plant prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for both garden cultivation and commercial farming.
