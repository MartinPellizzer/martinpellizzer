Panicum costaricense

Panicum costaricense, commonly known as the Costa Rican panicgrass, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It is a member of the grass family, Poaceae, and typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant features slender, upright stems with narrow, linear leaves that are often hairy. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets that produce small, wind-pollinated flowers.

Panicum costaricense is adapted to well-drained soils and can thrive in full sun, making it a common sight in open habitats.
