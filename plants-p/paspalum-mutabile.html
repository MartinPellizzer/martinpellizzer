Paspalum mutabile

Paspalum mutabile, commonly known as variable grass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted foliage and its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types. The plant typically grows in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas. Its flowering spikes are usually slender and bear small, inconspicuous flowers.

Paspalum mutabile is often used in restoration projects due to its hardy nature and ecological benefits.
