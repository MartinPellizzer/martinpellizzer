Puccinellia distans var. micrandra

Puccinellia distans var. micrandra, commonly known as the narrowleaf saltbush, is a perennial grass species native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant typically grows in saline or alkaline soils, making it well adapted to harsh environmental conditions. Its flowers are small and clustered in dense spikelets, which are usually greenish or brownish in color.

This species plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soils and providing forage for wildlife in its native habitat.
