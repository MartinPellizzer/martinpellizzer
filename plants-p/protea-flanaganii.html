Protea flanaganii

Protea flanaganii, commonly known as the Flanagan's protea, is a striking South African plant belonging to the Proteaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescence composed of numerous white to pinkish flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils in the winter rainfall regions of the Western Cape. Its flowers are pollinated by insects, and it produces woody, cone-like seed pods that release seeds when mature.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
