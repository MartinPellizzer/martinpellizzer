Pithecellobium diversifolium var. microphyllum

Pithecellobium diversifolium var. microphyllum, commonly known as the dwarf-leaf pithecellobium, is a small evergreen shrub native to tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, narrow leaves that are typically less than 2 inches in length. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow, followed by edible, sweet-tart fruits resembling small beans. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for ornamental use in tropical gardens.

Due to its compact size and ornamental appeal, it is often cultivated as a decorative plant in warm climates.
