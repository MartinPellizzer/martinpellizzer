Paspalum boryanum

Paspalum boryanum, commonly known as the Boryanum grass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers. It is often found in moist, disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

Paspalum boryanum is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects due to its adaptability and ability to stabilize soil.
