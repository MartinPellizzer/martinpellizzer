Pleopeltis lycopodioides var. salicifolia

Pleopeltis lycopodioides var. salicifolia, commonly known as the willow-leaved clubmoss, is a fern-like plant belonging to the family Lycopodiaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, scale-like leaves that resemble those of true mosses. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its reproductive structures, known as sporophytes, produce spores that are dispersed by wind.

This species is valued for its ecological role in maintaining soil stability and supporting biodiversity in its native habitat.
