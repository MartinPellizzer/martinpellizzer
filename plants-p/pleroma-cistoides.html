Pleroma cistoides

Pleroma cistoides, commonly known as the bladderleaf gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It belongs to the family Gentianaceae and is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and has heart-shaped leaves with a waxy texture. Its flowers bloom in late summer and are known for their tubular shape and vivid coloration.

Pleroma cistoides is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
