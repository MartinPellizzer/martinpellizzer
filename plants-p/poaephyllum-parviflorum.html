Poaephyllum parviflorum

Poaephyllum parviflorum, commonly known as the small-flowered rock daisy, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, thriving in high-altitude environments. The plant features a compact rosette of narrow, silvery-green leaves and produces small, yellow flowers clustered in a dense inflorescence. It is adapted to cold climates and can tolerate harsh environmental conditions.

Poaephyllum parviflorum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in alpine gardens.
