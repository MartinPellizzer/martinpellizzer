Psanacetum annuum

Psanacetum annuum, commonly known as sweet wormwood, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and feathery, aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late summer. It is widely cultivated for its medicinal properties, particularly for its active compound, artemisinin.

Psanacetum annuum thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a hardy plant in temperate climates.
