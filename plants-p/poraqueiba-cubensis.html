Poraqueiba cubensis

Poraqueiba cubensis, commonly known as the Cuban poraqueiba, is a small evergreen tree native to the Caribbean region.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by small, round fruits containing a single seed. It is valued for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.

The tree is also noted for its ability to thrive in tropical climates with well-drained soil.
