Potamogeton pusillus f. tenuissimus

Potamogeton pusillus f. tenuissimus, commonly known as the slender pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater ecosystems.

It has slender, ribbon-like leaves that are typically less than 1 millimeter wide, giving it a delicate appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually found in clusters near the water surface. It thrives in slow-moving or still waters with muddy or sandy substrates.

This variety is often used in ecological studies due to its role in aquatic habitats and its sensitivity to environmental changes.
