Pelargonium × sanguineum

Pelargonium × sanguineum, commonly known as the common geranium, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of showy, five-petaled flowers that come in a range of colors, including red, pink, and white. The leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a slightly serrated edge, often with a velvety texture. This species is widely cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a potted plant.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a versatile choice for various landscaping applications.
