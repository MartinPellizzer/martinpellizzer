Pyrus thymifolia

Pyrus thymifolia, commonly known as the thyme-leaved pear, is a species of flowering plant in the family Rosaceae.

It is characterized by its small, fragrant flowers and oval-shaped leaves that emit a strong thyme-like aroma. Native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, this plant thrives in rocky and well-drained soils. The fruit is a small, edible pear-like berry, often used in traditional medicine.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and aromatic properties.
