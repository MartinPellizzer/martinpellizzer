Primula yargongensis var. liensis

Primula yargongensis var. liensis, commonly known as the Yarlung Primrose, is a rare and endemic plant species found in the high-altitude regions of southern Tibet.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, bell-shaped flowers. The leaves are typically ovate to elliptic, with a hairy surface, while the flowers are usually pink or purple in color. This plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is adapted to the harsh conditions of its mountainous habitat.

Due to its limited distribution and ecological significance, it is considered a valuable species for botanical research and conservation efforts.
