Pinanga paradoxa var. paradoxa

Pinanga paradoxa var. paradoxa, commonly known as the giant bamboo or giant lemur bamboo, is a striking plant native to Madagascar.

It is a member of the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its massive, segmented stems that can grow up to 10 meters in height. The plant has long, narrow leaves that are often covered in a fine, silky hair, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is adapted to the unique ecological conditions of its native habitat.

Due to its rarity and ecological significance, it is considered a valuable species in botanical research and conservation efforts.
