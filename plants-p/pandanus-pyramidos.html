Pandanus pyramidos

Pandanus pyramidos, commonly known as the bottlebrush pandanus, is a tropical plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its distinctive, stiff, sword-shaped leaves that are often used in traditional crafts. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. Its fruit is a fibrous, spiky structure that contains edible seeds.

Pandanus pyramidos is often cultivated for its ornamental value and cultural significance in various island communities.
