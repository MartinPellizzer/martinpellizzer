Polygala vulgaris subsp. caliptera

Polygala vulgaris subsp.

caliptera, commonly known as the narrow-leaved milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, delicate flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink or white.

The plant has compound leaves with linear lobes and produces small, cylindrical fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and rocky outcrops.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological niche, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
