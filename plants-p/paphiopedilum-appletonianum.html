Paphiopedilum appletonianum

Paphiopedilum appletonianum, commonly known as the Appleton's orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique, slipper-shaped lip that is often brightly colored, typically in shades of yellow or orange. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in racemes, and it thrives in humid, shaded environments. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb moisture from the forest floor.

This orchid is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated for its striking appearance.
