Passiflora caloneura

Passiflora caloneura, commonly known as the narrow-veined passionflower, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that feature a complex arrangement of petals, sepals, and bracts. The plant produces edible, tropical fruits that are often consumed fresh or used in culinary applications. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped with a rough texture and prominent veins.

Passiflora caloneura is valued in horticulture for its ornamental beauty and ability to thrive in warm, humid climates.
