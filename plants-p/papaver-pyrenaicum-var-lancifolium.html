Papaver pyrenaicum var. lancifolium

Papaver pyrenaicum var. lancifolium, commonly known as the Pyrenean poppy, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Iberian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its striking red or orange flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are typically hairy and arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
