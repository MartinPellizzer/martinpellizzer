Pinus nordmanniana

Pinus nordmanniana, commonly known as the Korean fir, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its pyramidal shape, needle-like leaves, and distinctive cones. The tree thrives in cool, humid climates and is often found in mixed forests. Its bark is thick and fissured, providing protection against harsh environmental conditions.

The species is valued for its timber and is also cultivated for ornamental purposes in gardens and landscapes.
