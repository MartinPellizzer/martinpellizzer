Platycoryne megalorrhyncha

Platycoryne megalorrhyncha, commonly known as the giant horned orchid, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that resemble horns, giving it its common name. The plant has a complex inflorescence with multiple floral bracts, and its flowers are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
