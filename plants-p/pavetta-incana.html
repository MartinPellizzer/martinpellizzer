Pavetta incana

Pavetta incana, commonly known as the white pavetta, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters. The plant features opposite, ovate to elliptic leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. Its flowers are small, white or pale pink, and arranged in clusters, often with five petals.

The fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, rounded seeds.
