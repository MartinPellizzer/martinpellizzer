Potamogeton filiformis var. elongatus

Potamogeton filiformis var. elongatus, commonly known as the slender pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments.

It has long, filamentous leaves that are typically narrow and ribbon-like in appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually arranged in spike-like clusters. It thrives in slow-moving or still waters with muddy or sandy substrates.

This species plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
