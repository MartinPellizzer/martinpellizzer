Phlox pilosa var. fulgida

Phlox pilosa var. fulgida, commonly known as the Showy Phlox, is a perennial flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, hairy stems and clusters of showy, fragrant flowers that range from white to pink or lavender in color. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, producing copious amounts of nectar that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies. It thrives in partial to full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and naturalized areas.

Its foliage is often covered in fine, white hairs, giving it a distinctive, soft appearance.
