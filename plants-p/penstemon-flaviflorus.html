Penstemon flaviflorus

Penstemon flaviflorus, commonly known as the yellowflower beardtongue, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of showy, yellow to orange tubular flowers. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It prefers well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Penstemon flaviflorus is also valued for its drought tolerance and low maintenance requirements.
