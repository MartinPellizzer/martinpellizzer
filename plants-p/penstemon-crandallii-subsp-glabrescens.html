Penstemon crandallii subsp. glabrescens

Penstemon crandallii subsp.

glabrescens, commonly known as the smooth crandall's beardtongue, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States. It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils in arid and semi-arid environments.

The plant features upright stems with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its showy flowers, which range from pink to purple, bloom in late spring to early summer.

It is well adapted to dry conditions and is often found in mountainous regions.
