Phyllostachys aureosulcata f. alata

Phyllostachys aureosulcata f. alata, commonly known as the golden bamboo or golden bamboo grass, is a variant of the bamboo species characterized by its striking golden-yellow striped stems.

This ornamental plant is native to East Asia and is widely cultivated for its aesthetic appeal in gardens and landscapes. The foliage is typically a mix of green and gold, adding a vibrant touch to any setting. It grows as a clump-forming grass with slender, hollow stems that are both durable and lightweight.

The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a versatile choice for various climates.
