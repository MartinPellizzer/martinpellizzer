Perilla avium

Perilla avium, commonly known as the Chinese date or Indian date, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves and small, fragrant flowers. The plant produces small, fleshy fruits that resemble dates, which are typically eaten fresh or dried. Perilla avium is valued for its ornamental qualities and is also used in traditional medicine.

Its botanical features make it a unique and adaptable species in tropical and subtropical climates.
