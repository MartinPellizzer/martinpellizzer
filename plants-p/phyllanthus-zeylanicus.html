Phyllanthus zeylanicus

Phyllanthus zeylanicus, commonly known as the Indian gooseberry or amla, is a small evergreen shrub native to South and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its alternate, ovate leaves with a smooth, glossy surface and small, clustered yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, green, berry-like fruits that turn orange-red when mature, containing a single seed. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its medicinal and nutritional properties.

The plant is also valued for its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions and its role in traditional herbal medicine.
