Phrygilanthus acutifolius subsp. coriaceus

Phrygilanthus acutifolius subsp.

coriaceus, commonly known as the thick-leaved phrygilanthus, is a shrub native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its dense, leathery leaves that are typically elliptical in shape.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant in arid regions.
