Polygonum opelousanum

Polygonum opelousanum, commonly known as Opelousas knotweed, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers that appear in late summer. The plant has alternate, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found along stream banks and in wetlands.

Polygonum opelousanum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
