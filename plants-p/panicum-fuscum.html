Panicum fuscum

Panicum fuscum, commonly known as black panicgrass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and dense, dark green foliage that turns bronze or reddish in color as it matures. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny grains, typical of the grass family. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open fields, roadsides, and disturbed areas.

Due to its hardy nature, Panicum fuscum is both a weed and a valuable forage crop in certain regions.
