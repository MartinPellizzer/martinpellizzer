Pultenaea mollis var. canescens

Pultenaea mollis var. canescens, commonly known as the silver wattle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and small, pale yellow flowers that appear in late summer. The leaves are narrow, linear, and covered in a fine, silvery down, giving the plant its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy habitats.

This variety is closely related to other species in the genus Pultenaea, sharing similar ecological and botanical traits.
