Phyllocactus mexicanus

Phyllocactus mexicanus, commonly known as the Mexican leaf cactus, is a unique cactus species native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its fleshy, paddle-shaped stems that are often covered in a waxy coating to reduce water loss. The plant produces small, white to yellowish flowers during the spring and summer months. Its leaves are modified into spines, which help protect it from herbivores and reduce transpiration.

This plant is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water and well-draining soil.
