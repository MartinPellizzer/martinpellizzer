Plectronia splendens

Plectronia splendens, commonly known as the golden bell vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, often with a contrasting red throat. The plant produces small, elliptical leaves that are opposite on the stems, and it thrives in warm, humid climates. Plectronia splendens is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.
