Pseudolaelia ataleiensis

Pseudolaelia ataleiensis, commonly known as the Ataleiense Orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a terrestrial orchid characterized by its compact growth habit and striking, showy flowers. The plant typically produces inflorescences with multiple pale yellow to greenish-white petals and sepals, often adorned with contrasting markings. It thrives in high-altitude environments with cool, moist conditions and requires well-drained soil.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
