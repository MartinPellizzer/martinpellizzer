Panicum lilloi

Panicum lilloi, commonly known as the Lillo's panicgrass, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, erect stems that can reach heights of up to 1 meter. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny, two-lobed spikelets. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue and a rough texture.

Panicum lilloi thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, often found in open woodlands and disturbed sites.
