Pleurothallis trilineata

Pleurothallis trilineata, commonly known as the three-lined pleurothallis, is a species of orchid native to the cloud forests of Central and South America.

It is a small, epiphytic plant that thrives in humid, shaded environments, often growing on trees or rocks. The plant produces delicate, fragrant flowers in shades of white, yellow, and green, which bloom during the rainy season. Its leaves are typically narrow and grass-like, adapted to conserve moisture in its natural habitat.

Pleurothallis trilineata is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
