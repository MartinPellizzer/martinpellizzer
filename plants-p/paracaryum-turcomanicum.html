Paracaryum turcomanicum

Paracaryum turcomanicum, commonly known as the Turkestan sandthorn, is a shrub native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its spiny stems and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant produces small, oval fruits that are typically red or orange when mature. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in sandy soils.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes used for stabilizing dunes and preventing soil erosion.
