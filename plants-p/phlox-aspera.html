Phlox aspera

Phlox aspera, commonly known as rough phlox, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of showy, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant has hairy, often rough-textured foliage, which gives it its common name. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Phlox aspera is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
