Passiflora flexipes

Passiflora flexipes, commonly known as the flexible passionflower, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that feature five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments. The plant produces blue or purple berries that are edible and often consumed by birds and mammals. Its leaves are heart-shaped with a rough texture and are often used in traditional medicine.

Passiflora flexipes thrives in humid, tropical environments and is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance.
