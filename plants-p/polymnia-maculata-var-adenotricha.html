Polymnia maculata var. adenotricha

Polymnia maculata var. adenotricha, commonly known as the spotted polymnia, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It features lance-shaped leaves with a distinctive spotted pattern, often with a bluish or purplish hue. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink, with a subtle fragrance. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its ornamental value makes it a sought-after plant for botanical gardens and conservation efforts.
