Passiflora cerradensis

Passiflora cerradensis, commonly known as the Brazilian passionflower, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, intricate flowers that feature five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments surrounding the reproductive structures. The plant produces edible, yellow-orange berries that are rich in nutrients and often consumed by wildlife and humans. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for ecological studies and ornamental horticulture.
