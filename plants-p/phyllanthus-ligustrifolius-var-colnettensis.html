Phyllanthus ligustrifolius var. colnettensis

Phyllanthus ligustrifolius var. colnettensis, commonly known as the Colnett's Indian almond, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its small, green, trifoliate leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces small yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by three-lobed, reddish-brown fruits. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in moist, shaded environments.

Its botanical features make it a subject of interest in both horticulture and pharmacological research.
