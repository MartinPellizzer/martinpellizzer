Pimelea altior var. parvifolia

Pimelea altior var. parvifolia, commonly known as the small-flowered mistflower, is a shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It features narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine, white hairs. The plant produces small, clustered yellow flowers that bloom in spring, adding a delicate touch to its appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in heathlands and open woodlands.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is a popular choice for native gardens.
