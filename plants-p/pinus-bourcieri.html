Pinus bourcieri

Pinus bourcieri, commonly known as the Mexican bristlecone pine, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to the mountainous regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its slow growth rate and long lifespan, often living for thousands of years. The tree has needle-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color, and it produces cones that contain seeds. It thrives in high-altitude environments with cold, dry climates.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat for various wildlife species and contributing to soil stabilization in its native range.
