Prunus pubescens acuminata

Prunus pubescens acuminata, commonly known as the hairy cherry or acuminated cherry, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its small, ovate leaves that are covered in fine pubescence, giving the plant its common name. The flowers are typically white or pale pink, appearing in clusters, and bloom in early spring. The fruit is a small, dark red drupe that ripens in summer, containing a single seed.

This plant is often found in temperate regions and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
