Philippia elgonensis

Philippia elgonensis, commonly known as the Elgon rock rose, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the East African region, particularly around Mount Elgon.

It is a shrub that typically grows in rocky and well-drained soils, often in mountainous environments. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant, white or pale pink flowers during the cooler months. Its leaves are narrow and leathery, adapted to conserve moisture in its arid habitat.

Philippia elgonensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens in regions with similar climatic conditions.
