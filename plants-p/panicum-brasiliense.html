Panicum brasiliense

Panicum brasiliense, commonly known as Brazilian millet, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas, thriving in full sun. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue, and it produces wind-pollinated flowers.

Panicum brasiliense is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its resilience and ability to stabilize soil.
