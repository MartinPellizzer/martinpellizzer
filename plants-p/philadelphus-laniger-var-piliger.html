Philadelphus laniger var. piliger

Philadelphus laniger var. piliger, commonly known as the woolly mock orange, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly gray foliage and fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with a soft, fuzzy texture, and its stems are often covered in a fine, white pubescence. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a versatile addition to gardens.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and ability to attract pollinators.
