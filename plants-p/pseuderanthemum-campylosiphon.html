Pseuderanthemum campylosiphon

Pseuderanthemum campylosiphon, commonly known as the narrow-leaf pseuderanthemum, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically blue or purple in color, arranged in dense clusters at the ends of the branches. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in tropical gardens.
