Phacelia lobata

Phacelia lobata, commonly known as the Sierra phacelia, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its delicate, blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in open habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes, thriving in full sun. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a fuzzy texture, while its stems are usually hairy.

Phacelia lobata plays an important role in supporting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
