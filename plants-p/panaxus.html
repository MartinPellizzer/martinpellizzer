Panaxus

Panaxus, commonly known as ginseng, is a perennial plant belonging to the Araliaceae family.

It is native to eastern Asia, particularly China, Korea, and Russia, and is characterized by its fleshy, branched roots. The plant typically grows in forested areas with rich, moist soil and thrives in shaded environments. Its leaves are compound, with a distinctive palmate shape, and it produces small, greenish flowers followed by red berries.

Due to its medicinal properties, Panaxus has been highly valued in traditional medicine for centuries.
