Pelargonium angelina

Pelargonium angelina, commonly known as Angelina geranium, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and vibrant, bicolor flowers that typically feature a mix of pink and white petals. The leaves are rounded and slightly fuzzy, adding to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for containers and hanging baskets.

This species is valued for its long blooming period and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
