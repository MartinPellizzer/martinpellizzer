Pancratium aurantiacum

Pancratium aurantiacum, commonly known as the golden torch lily, is a striking plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, narrow leaves and a single, showy orange or yellow flower that resembles a torch. The plant produces a distinctive red or orange bulb that is often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for coastal gardens.

The flower blooms in spring and is known for its strong, sweet fragrance.
