Pandanus carmichaelii

Pandanus carmichaelii, commonly known as the Christmas palm, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, fan-shaped leaves that are often used in traditional crafts. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that develop into edible fruits, which are consumed by both humans and animals. It thrives in humid, coastal environments and is well adapted to saline soils.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in tropical regions.
