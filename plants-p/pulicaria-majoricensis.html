Pulicaria majoricensis

Pulicaria majoricensis, commonly known as the Canary Islands groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is a low-growing perennial shrub characterized by its hairy, gray-green foliage and small yellow flowers. The plant thrives in rocky and coastal environments, showcasing adaptability to arid and saline conditions. Its flowers bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Pulicaria majoricensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in local ecosystems.
