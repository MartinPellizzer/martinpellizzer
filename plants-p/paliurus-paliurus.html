Paliurus paliurus

Paliurus paliurus, commonly known as the Chinese thornapple, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China, Korea, and Japan.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are pinnately arranged and have a serrated margin. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, followed by distinctive red, fleshy berries that are edible for humans and birds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, sunny habitats.

Paliurus paliurus is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
