Prunus amygdalus var. persicoides

Prunus amygdalus var. persicoides, commonly known as the wild almond or Persian almond, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to parts of Asia and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy surface. The plant produces white or pale pink flowers in early spring, followed by small, hard fruits that contain edible seeds. Its botanical features make it a valuable species for both ornamental gardening and ecological studies.

The variety is often used in horticulture for its resilience and adaptability to various soil conditions.
