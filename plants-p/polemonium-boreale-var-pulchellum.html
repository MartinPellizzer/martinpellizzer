Polemonium boreale var. pulchellum

Polemonium boreale var. pulchellum, commonly known as the northern Jacob's ladder, is a herbaceous perennial native to the northern regions of North America.

It features slender, ascending stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue, purple, or white, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its name reflects its historical use in traditional medicine and its ornamental value in gardens.
