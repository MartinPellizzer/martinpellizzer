Piper arboricola

Piper arboricola, commonly known as the climbing pepper plant, is a tropical vine native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and glossy, elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. Its fruit is a small, oblong berry that turns from green to yellow or red as it matures.

Piper arboricola is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in shaded garden settings.
