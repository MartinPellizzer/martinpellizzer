Pleienta capitata

Pleienta capitata, commonly known as the yellow bellflower, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow flowers that bloom in clusters at the top of its slender stems. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as forests and stream banks. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and have a slightly rough texture.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its bright, bell-shaped blooms.
