Pseuduvaria lignocarpa

Pseuduvaria lignocarpa, commonly known as the African blackwood, is a small evergreen tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its dark, dense wood, which is highly valued for its durability and fine grain. The plant has simple, elliptical leaves that are glossy and arranged alternately along the branches. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by dark blue berries containing hard, seed-like structures.

The tree is often found in moist, shaded habitats and is an important component of local ecosystems.
