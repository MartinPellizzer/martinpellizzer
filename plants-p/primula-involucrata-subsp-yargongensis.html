Primula involucrata subsp. yargongensis

Primula involucrata subsp.

yargongensis, commonly known as the Yargong primrose, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Tibet. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of tubular, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink.

The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are basal, spoon-shaped, and covered in a dense layer of fine hairs.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens or alpine settings.
