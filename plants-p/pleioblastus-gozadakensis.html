Pleioblastus gozadakensis

Pleioblastus gozadakensis, commonly known as the Gozadake bamboo, is a species native to China, specifically the Yunnan Province.

It is a clumping bamboo that typically grows to a height of 2 to 5 meters, with slender, light green culms that are often marked with white or silver stripes. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged in a dense, compact form, contributing to its ornamental value. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it suitable for both indoor and outdoor cultivation.

Due to its aesthetic appeal and hardy nature, it is often used in landscaping and as a decorative plant in gardens.
