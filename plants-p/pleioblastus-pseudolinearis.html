Pleioblastus pseudolinearis

Pleioblastus pseudolinearis, commonly known as the Japanese forest grass, is a species of bamboo native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, arching stems and narrow, dark green leaves that are often slightly serrated. This plant is known for its ability to thrive in shaded environments, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens. Its growth habit is clump-forming, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in the spring.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in ecological landscaping.
