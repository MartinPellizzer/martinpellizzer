Pentameris angustifolia

Pentameris angustifolia, commonly known as the narrowleaf pentameris, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and ray flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as woodlands and wet meadows. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a hairy texture, and it produces small, yellowish flowers in the summer.

Pentameris angustifolia is valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and its aesthetic appeal in native plant gardens.
