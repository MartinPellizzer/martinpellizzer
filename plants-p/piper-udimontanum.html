Piper udimontanum

Piper udimontanum, commonly known as the Udimontan pepper vine, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species such as black pepper. The plant is characterized by its climbing habit, with long, slender stems that twine around supports. Its leaves are typically alternate, ovate, and have a rough texture with prominent veins.

The small greenish-white flowers are clustered in spikes, and the plant produces small, round berries that turn red when mature.
