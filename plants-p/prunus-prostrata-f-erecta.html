Prunus prostrata f. erecta

Prunus prostrata f. erecta, commonly known as the upright form of the Japanese flowering cherry, is a compact, shrub-like plant with a more upright growth habit compared to its prostrate counterpart.

It features small, oval leaves that are typically a glossy green, and it produces clusters of delicate, pinkish-white flowers in early spring. The plant is native to Japan and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes. It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it suitable for a variety of garden settings.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for bonsai and small garden spaces.
