Ptarmica pyrenaica

Ptarmica pyrenaica, commonly known as the Pyrenean thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of the Iberian Peninsula and southern France.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic foliage and square stems. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are aromatic, often used in traditional herbal remedies, and it thrives in well-drained, rocky soils.

Ptarmica pyrenaica is valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and its resilience in alpine environments.
