Pseudoeugenia singaporensis

Pseudoeugenia singaporensis, commonly known as the Singapore flame tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its striking, fiery orange-red flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has simple, elliptical leaves with a glossy surface and a fragrant, aromatic scent. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The fruit is a small, woody capsule that contains several seeds.
