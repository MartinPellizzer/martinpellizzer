Peperomia panamensis

Peperomia panamensis, commonly known as the Panama peperomia, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its compact, succulent-like foliage. The plant typically features rosettes of fleshy, oval-shaped leaves that are often variegated with shades of green, white, and pink. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually green or reddish in color.

Peperomia panamensis is well-suited for indoor cultivation due to its low maintenance and ability to thrive in bright, indirect light.
