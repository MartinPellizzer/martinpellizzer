Pimelea linifolia var. subnervosa

Pimelea linifolia var. subnervosa, commonly known as the narrow-veined white everlasting, is a low-growing shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It features dense clusters of small, white, star-shaped flowers that bloom in spring and summer. The leaves are narrow, linear, and often covered in fine hairs, giving the plant a distinctive appearance. It is well adapted to dry, sandy soils and is often found in heathlands and coastal areas.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its long-lasting flowers.
