Pilosocereus magnificus

Pilosocereus magnificus, commonly known as the night blooming cactus, is a species of cactus native to southern Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems covered in white, woolly spines. The plant produces large, fragrant flowers that open at night, typically in shades of white and yellow. It thrives in arid environments and requires well-draining soil.

Due to its striking appearance and unique blooming habit, it is a popular choice for desert gardens.
