Polylepis simpsoniae

Polylepis simpsoniae, commonly known as Simpson's polylepis, is a species of tree native to the Andean highlands of Peru.

It is characterized by its small, elongated leaves and distinctive, cone-like fruits. The tree typically grows in cloud forests at elevations between 2,500 and 4,000 meters above sea level. Its bark is thick and fissured, providing some protection against harsh environmental conditions.

Polylepis simpsoniae plays an important ecological role in its habitat, supporting a variety of wildlife species.
