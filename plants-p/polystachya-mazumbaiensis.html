Polystachya mazumbaiensis

Polystachya mazumbaiensis, commonly known as the Mazumbai torch orchid, is a tropical orchid native to eastern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of showy, red-brown flowers that resemble torches. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist forest understories, where it thrives in humid conditions. Its leaves are elongated and strap-like, often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs.

Polystachya mazumbaiensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
