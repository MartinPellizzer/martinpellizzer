Penstemon menziesii subsp. thompsonii

Penstemon menziesii subsp.

thompsonii, commonly known as Thompson's beardtongue, is a perennial plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, tubular flowers that range in color from purple to pink.

The plant typically thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for xeriscaping. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves, and it produces seed capsules that release tiny seeds in the fall.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
