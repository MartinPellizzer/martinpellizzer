Pleurothallis knappiae

Pleurothallis knappiae, commonly known as the Knapp's orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its small, intricate flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are usually pale green or white, with a subtle fragrance, and bloom during the rainy season.

Pleurothallis knappiae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by enthusiasts of rare orchids.
