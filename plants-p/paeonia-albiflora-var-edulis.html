Paeonia albiflora var. edulis

Paeonia albiflora var. edulis, commonly known as the white peony or Chinese white peony, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of China.

It is characterized by its large, showy white to pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant features compound, trifoliate leaves with a serrated edge and a robust root system that stores nutrients. Its flowers are often used in traditional Chinese medicine and as a culinary ingredient, particularly in soups and teas.

The plant thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for ornamental and medicinal gardens.
