Puschkinia latifolia

Puschkinia latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf scilla, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It produces clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers in early spring. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that emerge from a basal rosette of bulbs. It thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight.

Puschkinia latifolia is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ease of care.
