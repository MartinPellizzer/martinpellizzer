Potentilla riparia

Potentilla riparia, commonly known as riverbank cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a low-growing perennial shrub that typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features compound leaves with five leaflets and produces clusters of small, yellow, five-petaled flowers. It thrives in moist, sandy soils along riverbanks and stream sides.

Its botanical characteristics make it well adapted to wetland environments and it is often used in ecological restoration projects.
