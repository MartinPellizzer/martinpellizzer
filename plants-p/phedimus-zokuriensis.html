Phedimus zokuriensis

Phedimus zokuriensis, commonly known as the Japanese groundsel, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its mat-forming growth habit and hairy foliage. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
