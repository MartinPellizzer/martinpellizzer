Ptilotus divaricatus var. divaricatus

Ptilotus divaricatus var. divaricatus, commonly known as the "bottlebrush plant," is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its distinctive cylindrical flower spikes that resemble a bottlebrush, composed of dense clusters of small, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid regions. It produces small, round, fleshy fruits that are adapted for dispersal by animals.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
