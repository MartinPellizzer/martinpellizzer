Passiflora standleyi

Passiflora standleyi, commonly known as the Crimson Passionflower, is a climbing vine native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, intricate flowers that feature a vibrant crimson color with intricate patterns. The plant produces edible, tropical fruits known as passionfruits, which are rich in nutrients and flavor. Its leaves are heart-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Passiflora standleyi is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
