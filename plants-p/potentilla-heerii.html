Potentilla heerii

Potentilla heerii, commonly known as the Herri's cinquefoil, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its low-growing habit and vibrant yellow flowers. The plant features finely divided, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered blooms in the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
