Papaver dubium var. vagum

Papaver dubium var. vagum, commonly known as the meadow poppy, is a flowering plant belonging to the Papaveraceae family.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that range in color from yellow to orange-red, often with a distinctive black or dark purple central spot. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a rough texture, while its stems are erect and often branched.

The species is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
