Pergularia cinerascens

Pergularia cinerascens, commonly known as the ash-colored dwarf sedge, is a small perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in wet, marshy habitats and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish flowers that are arranged in dense clusters. It has a fibrous root system and is adapted to thrive in waterlogged soils.

Pergularia cinerascens is often found in wetlands and along the edges of streams and ponds.
