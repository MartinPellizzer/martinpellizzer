Peperomia tenuissima

Peperomia tenuissima, commonly known as the silver mohair plant, is a compact, low-growing perennial native to tropical regions of South America.

It features glossy, dark green, velvety leaves that are often tinged with silver or white veins, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers that appear in clusters during the growing season. It thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it an ideal choice for indoor gardening.

Due to its slow growth and minimal care requirements, it is a popular ornamental plant among succulent enthusiasts.
