Plectranthus striatus var. graciliflorus

Plectranthus striatus var. graciliflorus, commonly known as the slender-flowered plectranthus, is a species native to southern Africa.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its hairy, aromatic leaves and clusters of small, tubular flowers. The flowers are typically purple or violet in color and bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for gardens and container cultivation.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental value make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
