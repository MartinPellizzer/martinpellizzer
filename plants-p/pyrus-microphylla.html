Pyrus microphylla

Pyrus microphylla, commonly known as the Japanese white pear, is a deciduous tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its small, oval-shaped leaves and produces clusters of white flowers in early spring. The fruit is a small, yellowish pear that ripens in late summer. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for cultivation in temperate climates.

This species is valued for its ornamental beauty and historical significance in traditional Japanese horticulture.
