Platanthera calceoliformis

Platanthera calceoliformis, commonly known as the showy lady's-tresses, is a terrestrial orchid native to North America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and dense, fragrant inflorescences composed of small, white to pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and wet meadows. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it relies on mycorrhizal fungi for nutrient uptake.

The species is known for its delicate appearance and ecological significance in forest ecosystems.
