Piper pendentispicum var. pendentispicum

Piper pendentispicum var. pendentispicum, commonly known as the hanging pepper plant, is a tropical vine native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its long, drooping stems that produce clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are glossy and arranged alternately along the stems. It is cultivated for its ornamental value and for the use of its peppery fruits in traditional medicine.

The varietal form, pendentispicum, is noted for its particularly pendulous growth habit.
