Piper petraeum

Piper petraeum, commonly known as the rock pepper or rock pepper vine, is a tropical flowering plant belonging to the Piperaceae family.

It is characterized by its climbing habit and heart-shaped, glossy leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into round, fleshy berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is native to regions of South America.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
