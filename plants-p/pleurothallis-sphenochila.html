Pleurothallis sphenochila

Pleurothallis sphenochila, commonly known as the "spine-lipped orchid," is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to the tropical regions of South America.

This epiphytic plant is characterized by its compact growth habit and intricate, often fragrant flowers. It typically produces small, greenish-white or pale yellow blooms with distinctive, elongated lips. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining, loose soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its unique appearance and relatively easy care requirements, it is a popular choice among orchid enthusiasts.
