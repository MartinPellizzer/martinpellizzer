Pseudostachyum

Pseudostachyum, commonly known as the false bamboo, is a genus of flowering plants in the grass family, Poaceae.

It is native to various regions of Southeast Asia, including Malaysia, Indonesia, and the Philippines. The plants typically grow as clump-forming grasses with tall, cylindrical stems that resemble bamboo. Their leaves are long, narrow, and often have a bluish-green hue.

Pseudostachyum species are valued for their ornamental appeal and are sometimes used in landscaping due to their aesthetic and structural characteristics.
