Poecilostachys confertiflora

Poecilostachys confertiflora, commonly known as the dense-flowered grass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and dense clusters of small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in open woodlands and grasslands, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are narrow and elongated, with a smooth texture and a prominent midrib.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping due to its attractive appearance.
