Pleurothallis serpentula var. major

Pleurothallis serpentula var. major, commonly known as the giant serpent orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to the rainforests of Central and South America.

This species is characterized by its elongated, pendulous inflorescences that bear numerous small, fragrant flowers with intricate patterns. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees and deriving moisture and nutrients from the air and rain. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, adapting to the humid, shaded environments it inhabits.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts for its unique appearance and delicate blooms.
