Pleurothallis lateritia

Pleurothallis lateritia, commonly known as the silver-leaved orchid, is a species of orchid native to the cloud forests of South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and striking silver-green foliage. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pale yellow, often clustered in a loose inflorescence. It thrives in high humidity and partial shade, making it a popular choice for tropical greenhouse cultivation.

Due to its unique appearance and relatively easy care requirements, it is appreciated by both novice and experienced orchid growers.
