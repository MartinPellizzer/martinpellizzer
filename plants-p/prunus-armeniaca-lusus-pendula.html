Prunus armeniaca lusus pendula

Prunus armeniaca lusus pendula, commonly known as the weeping cherry or weeping peach, is a cultivar of the peach tree characterized by its cascading branches that create a striking, flowing appearance.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is native to regions of Asia, including China and the Middle East. The plant produces small, pink or white flowers in early spring, followed by sweet, edible fruits in summer. Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and parks, though it requires well-drained soil and full sun for optimal growth.

The tree is also susceptible to pests and diseases, necessitating regular care and maintenance.
