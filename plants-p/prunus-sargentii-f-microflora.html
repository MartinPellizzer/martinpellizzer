Prunus sargentii f. microflora

Prunus sargentii f. microflora, commonly known as the dwarf cherry, is a compact, ornamental shrub native to China.

It features small, rounded leaves that are dark green and glossy, providing a dense, bushy appearance. The plant produces delicate pink or white flowers in early spring, followed by small, red cherries that persist into winter. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a container plant.

Due to its unique foliage and seasonal blooms, it is valued in horticulture for its aesthetic appeal and hardiness.
