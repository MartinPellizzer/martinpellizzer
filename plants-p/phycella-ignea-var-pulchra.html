Phycella ignea var. pulchra

Phycella ignea var. pulchra, commonly known as the Flameleaf Phycella, is a striking plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its vibrant, flame-like foliage that ranges from orange to red, depending on environmental conditions. The plant features small, clustered flowers that bloom in the spring, adding a burst of color to its surroundings. Its succulent leaves are adapted to store water, allowing it to thrive in dry climates.

The variety pulchra is distinguished by its more intense coloration compared to the typical form of Phycella ignea.
