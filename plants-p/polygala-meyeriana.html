Polygala meyeriana

Polygala meyeriana, commonly known as the Meyer's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, pale blue or purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and shrublands. Its leaves are narrow and linear, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Polygala meyeriana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
