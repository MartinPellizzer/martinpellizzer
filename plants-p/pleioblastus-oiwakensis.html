Pleioblastus oiwakensis

Pleioblastus oiwakensis, commonly known as the Japanese silver grass, is a perennial grass species native to Japan.

It is characterized by its slender, arching stems and silvery-white plumes that appear in late spring to early summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically a deep green color. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.

Pleioblastus oiwakensis prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it adaptable to a variety of growing conditions.
