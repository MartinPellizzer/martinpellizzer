Ptileris hieraciifolius

Ptileris hieraciifolius, commonly known as the hairy bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant has hairy stems and leaves that are lanceolate in shape, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Ptileris hieraciifolius is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ease of care.
