Pedilochilus angustifolius

Pedilochilus angustifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaved rock orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It typically grows in rocky or limestone habitats, showcasing its adaptability to nutrient-poor environments. The plant features slender, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence. Its flowers are small, usually pale yellow or greenish-white, with a distinctive spurred lip.

This orchid is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in specialized gardens.
