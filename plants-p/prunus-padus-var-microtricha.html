Prunus padus var. microtricha

Prunus padus var. microtricha, commonly known as the dwarf white bird cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, branching form and small, white flowers that appear in early spring. The leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a smooth margin, turning yellow in autumn. The fruit is a small, dark red drupe that matures in late summer.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively resistant to pests and diseases.
