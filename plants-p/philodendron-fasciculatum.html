Philodendron fasciculatum

Philodendron fasciculatum, commonly known as the creeping philodendron, is a tropical vine native to Central and South America.

It features heart-shaped, waxy leaves that are typically light to dark green with a smooth texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are followed by red, berry-like fruits. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often used as a houseplant due to its attractive foliage.

Its rapid growth habit makes it suitable for hanging baskets or as a ground cover in outdoor gardens.
