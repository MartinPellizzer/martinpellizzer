Palaquium montanum

Palaquium montanum, commonly known as the mountain palaquium, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its simple, elliptical leaves that are glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by round, smooth fruits containing a single seed. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, well-drained soils in forested areas.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
