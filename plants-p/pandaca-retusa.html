Pandaca retusa

Pandaca retusa, commonly known as the slender pandaca, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Araliaceae and is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in umbels, which are typically borne on the upper parts of the stems. Its foliage is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
