Philodendron prieureanum

Philodendron prieureanum, commonly known as the Prieureanum philodendron, is a climbing tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It features large, heart-shaped leaves that are typically a deep green with a glossy texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by orange-red berries, which are toxic to humans and animals. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires consistently moist, well-draining soil.

Its unique foliage makes it a popular choice for indoor gardening and as a climbing plant in greenhouses.
