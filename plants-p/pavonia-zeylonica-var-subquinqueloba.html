Pavonia zeylonica var. subquinqueloba

Pavonia zeylonica var. subquinqueloba, commonly known as the Sri Lankan pavonia, is a flowering plant native to Sri Lanka.

It is characterized by its distinctive palmate leaves, which are typically five-lobed and resemble a hand, giving it its common name. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers that are usually pink or red in color. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in forest understories.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
