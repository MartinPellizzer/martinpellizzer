Piper blepharilepidum

Piper blepharilepidum, commonly known as the "broad-leafed pepper," is a tropical climbing shrub belonging to the Piperaceae family.

It is native to Central and South America, where it thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by three-lobed, berry-like fruits. Its leaves are broad and have a distinctive, slightly waxy texture.

The plant is valued for its aromatic leaves, which are used in traditional medicine and culinary applications.
