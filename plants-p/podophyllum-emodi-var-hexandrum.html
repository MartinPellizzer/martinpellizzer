Podophyllum emodi var. hexandrum

Podophyllum emodi var. hexandrum, commonly known as the Himalayan mayapple, is a perennial herb native to the alpine regions of the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of white flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant produces small, green berries that ripen to a red color, attracting various wildlife species. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in forested areas at high altitudes.

Due to its medicinal properties, it has been used in traditional medicine for centuries.
