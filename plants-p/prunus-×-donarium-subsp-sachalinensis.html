Prunus × donarium subsp. sachalinensis

Prunus × donarium subsp.

sachalinensis, commonly known as the Sakhalin cherry, is a hybrid species native to the Korean Peninsula and the Russian Far East. It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of 2–6 meters.

The plant produces clusters of small, white or pinkish flowers in the spring, followed by edible berries that are often used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are ovate to elliptical in shape, with a serrated margin and a glossy green upper surface.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been cultivated for both its aesthetic and medicinal properties.
