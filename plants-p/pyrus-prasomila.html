Pyrus prasomila

Pyrus prasomila, commonly known as the Persian white pear, is a deciduous tree native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit. The tree produces small, white flowers in early spring, followed by edible fruits that are typically harvested in late summer. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a serrated margin.

The fruit is valued for its sweet, aromatic flesh and is often used in traditional cuisines.
