Podocoma nana

Podocoma nana, commonly known as the dwarf African ice plant, is a small, succulent shrub native to southern Africa.

It features compact, fleshy leaves that are typically green or bluish-gray in color, adapted to store water. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow, orange, or red, which bloom in spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Due to its slow growth and drought tolerance, it is a low-maintenance plant for arid environments.
