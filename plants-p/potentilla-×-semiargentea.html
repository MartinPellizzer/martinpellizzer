Potentilla × semiargentea

Potentilla × semiargentea, commonly known as silver potentilla, is a hybrid shrub belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its silvery-white undersides on its leaves, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters and produces clusters of small, yellow flowers in the summer. Its foliage is evergreen in mild climates, though it may lose leaves in colder regions.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often used in garden landscapes for its aesthetic appeal and hardiness.
