Philodendron chrysocarpum

Philodendron chrysocarpum, commonly known as the golden fruit philodendron, is a tropical climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, glossy green leaves that often have a slight waxy coating. The plant produces striking yellow-orange fruits that are edible and contain a single seed. It thrives in warm, humid environments with bright, indirect light.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in shaded garden settings.
