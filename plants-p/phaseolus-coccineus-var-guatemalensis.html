Phaseolus coccineus var. guatemalensis

Phaseolus coccineus var. guatemalensis, commonly known as the scarlet runner bean, is a tropical climbing legume native to Central America.

It features vibrant red to orange flowers and elongated, edible pods that contain kidney-shaped seeds. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth. Its leaves are trifoliate, with heart-shaped lobes, and it produces a climbing habit through tendrils.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and culinary use in traditional cuisines.
