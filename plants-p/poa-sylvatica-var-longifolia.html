Poa sylvatica var. longifolia

Poa sylvatica var. longifolia, commonly known as the long-leaved wood sedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features slender, grass-like leaves that are longer than those of the typical Poa sylvatica, giving it its varietal name. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of spikelets, which are small, cylindrical flower clusters.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in wetland habitats.
