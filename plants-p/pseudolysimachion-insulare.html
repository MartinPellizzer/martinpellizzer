Pseudolysimachion insulare

Pseudolysimachion insulare, commonly known as the island false lysimachia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or along stream banks. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are usually covered in a fine layer of hairs. Its small, white or pale yellow flowers are clustered in loose inflorescences, and it produces small, oval berries that contain the seeds.

Botanically, it belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit.
