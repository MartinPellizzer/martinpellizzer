Parodia diersiana

Parodia diersiana, commonly known as the Dieters' Candelabra Cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact, multi-stemmed structure and produces clusters of white, funnel-shaped flowers during the warmer months. The plant has a cylindrical stem with ribs that are often covered in white or pale spines. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Its unique appearance and low maintenance make it a popular choice among cactus enthusiasts.
