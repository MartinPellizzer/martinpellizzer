Potentilla taurica f. angustissima

Potentilla taurica f. angustissima, commonly known as narrowleaf taurica rose, is a compact shrub native to southern Europe and parts of Asia.

It features small, oval leaves that are typically narrow and slightly hairy, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of tiny, yellow flowers in the summer, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardy nature in temperate climates.
