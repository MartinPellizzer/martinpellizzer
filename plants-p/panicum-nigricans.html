Panicum nigricans

Panicum nigricans, commonly known as black panicgrass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and narrow, dark green leaves that often turn reddish or purplish at maturity. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, bristly florets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open fields, along roadsides, and in disturbed areas.

Due to its hardy nature, Panicum nigricans is sometimes considered a weed but can also be used in erosion control and livestock forage.
