Pisonia merytifolia

Pisonia merytifolia, commonly known as the white pisonia, is a tropical flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, white, fluffy flowers that bloom in clusters, often resembling small clouds. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, red fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in coastal areas and disturbed soils.

Pisonia merytifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
