Prunus divaricata subsp. pontica

Prunus divaricata subsp.

pontica, commonly known as the Pontic cherry, is a shrub or small tree native to the Caucasus region. It is characterized by its slender, often twisted branches and small, ovate leaves with serrated edges.

The plant produces white or pinkish flowers in early spring, followed by small, dark red or black berries. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in rocky or forested areas.

Its botanical features make it an ornamental plant valued for its unique appearance and adaptability.
