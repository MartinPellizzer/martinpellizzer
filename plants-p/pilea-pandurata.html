Pilea pandurata

Pilea pandurata, commonly known as the Shiny Pilea, is a species of flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, oval-shaped leaves that are typically bright green with a slightly waxy texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and summer months. It thrives in indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Pilea pandurata is relatively low-maintenance and appreciated for its aesthetic appeal and ease of care.
