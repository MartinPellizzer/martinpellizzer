Pyrus eminens

Pyrus eminens, commonly known as the mountain pear, is a deciduous tree native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and alternate, lance-shaped leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, which are followed by edible, sweet-tart fruits.

Pyrus eminens is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in harsh, high-altitude environments.
