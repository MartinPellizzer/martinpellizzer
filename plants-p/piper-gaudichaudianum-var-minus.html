Piper gaudichaudianum var. minus

Piper gaudichaudianum var. minus, commonly known as the dwarf black pepper plant, is a variant of the black pepper species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a climbing vine characterized by its small, dark green leaves and clusters of tiny white flowers. The plant produces small, dark purple berries that are used in traditional medicine and as a spice. It thrives in warm, humid climates with partial shade and well-draining soil.

Due to its compact size, it is often cultivated as an ornamental or in small-scale spice production.
