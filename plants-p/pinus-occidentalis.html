Pinus occidentalis

Pinus occidentalis, commonly known as the West Indian pine, is a species of evergreen conifer native to the Caribbean.

It is characterized by its tall, straight trunk and needle-like leaves that grow in bundles of five. The tree produces cones that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with well-drained soil.

This species plays an important role in the ecosystems of its native habitats.
