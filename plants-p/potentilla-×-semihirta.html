Potentilla × semihirta

Potentilla × semihirta, commonly known as the hybrid shrub, is a species native to the mountainous regions of central and eastern Europe.

It is characterized by its compact, upright growth habit and produces clusters of small, yellow flowers in late spring to early summer. The plant has dark green, lance-shaped leaves that are slightly hairy, especially along the veins. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Potentilla × semihirta is also valued for its hardiness and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
