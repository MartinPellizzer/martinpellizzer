Pinus sylvestris var. mongholica

Pinus sylvestris var. mongholica, commonly known as the Mongolian pine, is a variant of the Scots pine native to eastern Asia.

It is a coniferous evergreen tree characterized by its needle-like leaves and cone-bearing structure. The plant thrives in temperate and continental climates, often growing in mountainous or semi-arid regions. Its bark is thick and fissured, providing protection against harsh environmental conditions.

The species is valued for its timber and plays a significant role in forest ecosystems.
