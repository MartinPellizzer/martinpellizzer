Pelargonium laevigatum subsp. laevigatum

Pelargonium laevigatum subsp.

laevigatum, commonly known as the smooth-leaved geranium, is a species native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its broad, smooth leaves and clusters of small, showy flowers that range in color from pink to purple.

The plant typically grows as an annual or perennial herb, reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden use.

Its botanical features make it both ornamental and useful in ecological settings.
