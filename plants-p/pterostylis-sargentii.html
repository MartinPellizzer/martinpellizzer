Pterostylis sargentii

Pterostylis sargentii, commonly known as the Sargent's hooded orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its unique, hood-like flower structure that resembles a small, elongated pouch. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as rainforests and understoreys. Its flowers are usually greenish-yellow with a dark dorsal sepal, and it produces small, round, dark brown seeds.

Pterostylis sargentii is a member of the orchid family (Orchidaceae) and is known for its specialized pollination mechanism involving insects.
