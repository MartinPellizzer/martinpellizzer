Panicum polycaulon

Panicum polycaulon, commonly known as purpletop millet, is a warm-season grass native to the southern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and dense, purplish-tinted inflorescence that appears in late summer. The plant is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it suitable for ornamental and erosion control purposes. Its small, spikelet-like flowers are clustered in panicles, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Panicum polycaulon is often used in native plant gardens due to its aesthetic appeal and ecological benefits.
