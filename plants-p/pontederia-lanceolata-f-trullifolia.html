Pontederia lanceolata f. trullifolia

Pontederia lanceolata f. trullifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf water hyacinth, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the Americas.

It features showy, blue-violet flowers that bloom in dense spikes above heart-shaped leaves. The plant thrives in wetland environments, including ponds, marshes, and slow-moving water bodies. Its leaves are lanceolate, with a distinctive narrow, pointed shape, and its stems are often floating or semi-submerged.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to provide habitat for aquatic life.
