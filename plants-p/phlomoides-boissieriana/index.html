Phlomoides Boissieriana

Phlomoides boissieriana is a lesser-known yet strikingly beautiful flowering plant native to the arid regions of the Middle East and parts of Central Asia. This perennial herb is known for its tall, slender stems and clusters of pale blue or violet flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in rocky and sandy soils, making it a hardy plant well adapted to harsh environments. Despite its ornamental value, it remains relatively obscure in horticultural circles. Its unique appearance and resilience make it a fascinating subject for botanists and gardeners alike.
