Poa rhenana

Poa rhenana, commonly known as the Rhenish bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, temperate climates and is often found in meadows, pastures, and along stream banks. The plant has slender, upright stems and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear blue-green florets. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish tinge, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Poa rhenana is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and supporting biodiversity in its native habitats.
