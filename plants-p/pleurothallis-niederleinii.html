Pleurothallis niederleinii

Pleurothallis niederleinii, commonly known as the Niederlein's pleurothallis, is a species of orchid native to the cloud forests of Central and South America.

It is a small, terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, humid environments. The plant features delicate, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink. It is known for its unique floral structure and is often found in association with specific mycorrhizal fungi.

Due to its habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
