Papaver ouvrardianum

Papaver ouvrardianum, commonly known as the Ouvrard's poppy, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep crimson, often with a contrasting central blotch. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun, making it a hardy species in temperate climates.

Its ornamental value has made it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.
