Pallasia halimifolia

Pallasia halimifolia, commonly known as sweet pepper bush, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving them a fuzzy texture. Its fruit is a small, red, fleshy drupe that ripens in the fall.

Pallasia halimifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native gardens.
