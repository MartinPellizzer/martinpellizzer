Parietaria gracilis

Parietaria gracilis, commonly known as slender wallweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Urticaceae.

It is characterized by its small, heart-shaped leaves and inconspicuous greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as rocky crevices and woodland areas. Its stems are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, smooth fruits.

Parietaria gracilis is often considered a weed in certain regions due to its rapid growth and ability to thrive in disturbed soils.
