Pyrus ganghobaensis

Pyrus ganghobaensis, commonly known as the Ganghoba apple, is a species of flowering plant in the genus Pyrus, which belongs to the family Rosaceae.

It is native to China and is characterized by its small, edible fruits that are typically yellow or green in color. The plant produces white flowers in early spring, which are followed by the development of its distinctive fruits. It is often found in mountainous regions and thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value and fruit production, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and for local consumption.
