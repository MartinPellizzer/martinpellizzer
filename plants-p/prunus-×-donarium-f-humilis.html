Prunus × donarium f. humilis

Prunus × donarium f. humilis, commonly known as the dwarf cherry, is a compact, ornamental shrub that exhibits the botanical traits of its parent species, Prunus × donarium.

It features small, ovate leaves with serrated margins and produces clusters of white or pale pink flowers in early spring. The plant is characterized by its dwarf growth habit, typically reaching heights of less than 1 meter, making it suitable for small gardens or container cultivation. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscape design for its delicate floral display.

The species is native to Japan and is commonly cultivated for its aesthetic qualities in horticulture.
