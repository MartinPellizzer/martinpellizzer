Portulaca oleracea var. rosea

Portulaca oleracea var. rosea, commonly known as the red purslane, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features succulent, fleshy leaves and vibrant red flowers that bloom throughout the summer. The plant thrives in hot, dry conditions and is often found in disturbed soils or along garden paths. Its edible leaves and flowers are used in salads and culinary dishes, adding a peppery flavor.

The variety 'rosea' is distinguished by its striking pink to red flower color, making it a popular ornamental plant.
