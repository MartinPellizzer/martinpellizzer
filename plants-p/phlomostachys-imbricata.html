Phlomostachys imbricata

Phlomostachys imbricata, commonly known as the mountain bluemink, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the high-altitude regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery down. The plant produces small, blue to purple flowers arranged in dense, spike-like clusters, which bloom in the summer months. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to cool, moist climates.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists alike.
