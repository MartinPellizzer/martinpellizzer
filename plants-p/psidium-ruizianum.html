Psidium ruizianum

Psidium ruizianum, commonly known as the Ruizian guava, is a small evergreen tree native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a smooth texture. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, yellow-orange fruits with a sweet and aromatic flavor.

It thrives in well-drained soil and requires a warm, humid climate for optimal growth.
