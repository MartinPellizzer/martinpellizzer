Pelargonium alternans subsp. parviinflorescens

Pelargonium alternans subsp.

parviinflorescens, commonly known as the dwarf geranium, is a compact, low-growing perennial herb native to South Africa. It features small, hairy leaves that are typically rounded or ovate in shape.

The plant produces clusters of small, pink to purple flowers during the summer months. It prefers well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

Due to its compact size and ornamental flowers, it is a popular choice for gardeners seeking a delicate, flowering ground cover.
