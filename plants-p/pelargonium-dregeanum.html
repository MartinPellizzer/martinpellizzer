Pelargonium dregeanum

Pelargonium dregeanum, commonly known as the Cape marigold, is a perennial shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, upright stems and clusters of yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant has lance-shaped, serrated leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal areas.

Pelargonium dregeanum is also valued for its drought tolerance and ability to attract pollinators.
