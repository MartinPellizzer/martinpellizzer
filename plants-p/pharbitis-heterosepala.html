Pharbitis heterosepala

Pharbitis heterosepala, commonly known as the morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its heliotropic leaves that follow the sun's movement throughout the day. The plant produces strikingly large, showy flowers that range in color from blue to purple. Its vines can grow up to several meters in length, often climbing supports or other structures.

Botanically, it belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
