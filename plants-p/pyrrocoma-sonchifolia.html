Pyrrocoma sonchifolia

Pyrrocoma sonchifolia, commonly known as the yellow wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its small, yellowish flowers and trifoliate leaves. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. It produces small, round fruits that contain several seeds.

Pyrrocoma sonchifolia is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
