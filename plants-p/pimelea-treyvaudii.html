Pimelea treyvaudii

Pimelea treyvaudii, commonly known as the yellow pimelea, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring, adding a vibrant touch to its foliage. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are typically arranged in whorls around the stems. It prefers well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in heathland or open woodland areas.

Pimelea treyvaudii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its bright floral display.
