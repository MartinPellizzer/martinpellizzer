Potentilla nivalis var. integrifolia

Potentilla nivalis var. integrifolia, commonly known as the alpine potentilla, is a low-growing shrub native to alpine and subalpine regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It features small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has simple, entire leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery down, aiding in insulation against cold temperatures. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to harsh mountain environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in alpine ecosystems.
