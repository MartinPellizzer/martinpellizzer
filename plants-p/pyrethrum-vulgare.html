Pyrethrum vulgare

Pyrethrum vulgare, commonly known as common pyrethrum, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its daisy-like yellow flowers. The plant is known for producing pyrethrins, natural insecticides found in its flower heads. Pyrethrum vulgare thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and pest control properties.

Its botanical features make it both a useful agricultural resource and a popular garden plant.
