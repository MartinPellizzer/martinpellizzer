Pennisetum purpureum

Pennisetum purpureum, commonly known as elephant grass, is a tall, perennial grass native to Africa.

It is characterized by its robust growth, reaching heights of up to 3 meters, and its distinctive purple-tinted leaves. This plant is highly drought-resistant and thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. It is widely used as forage for livestock due to its high nutritional value and rapid growth rate.

Additionally, it plays a role in soil conservation and is sometimes cultivated for bioenergy production.
