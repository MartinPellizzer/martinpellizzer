Pitcairnia spectabilis

Pitcairnia spectabilis, commonly known as the showy pitcairnia, is a striking bromeliad native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, upright rosette of broad, leathery leaves that can grow up to 2 meters in height. The plant produces vibrant, showy inflorescences with bright red flowers that bloom in the spring. It thrives in humid, well-drained environments and requires indirect sunlight.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in botanical gardens.
