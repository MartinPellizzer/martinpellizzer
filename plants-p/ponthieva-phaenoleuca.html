Ponthieva phaenoleuca

Ponthieva phaenoleuca, commonly known as the white-veined sword fern, is a striking epiphytic fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its long, narrow, arching fronds with distinctive white veins running along the leaf blades. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found growing on trees or rocks in tropical rainforests. Its unique foliage makes it a popular choice for indoor gardening and botanical collections.

Ponthieva phaenoleuca requires consistent moisture and indirect light to flourish.
