Picea excelsa var. rhombica

Picea excelsa var. rhombica, commonly known as the rhombic white fir, is a variety of the white fir native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It is a coniferous evergreen tree characterized by its tall, slender form and dense, pyramidal crown. The leaves are needle-like, flat, and often have a distinctive rhombic shape, giving the species its botanical name. It thrives in cool, moist climates and is adapted to high-altitude environments with poor soil conditions.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in mountainous regions.
