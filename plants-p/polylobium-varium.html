Polylobium varium

Polylobium varium, commonly known as the variable lobed plant, is a species native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, lobed leaves that vary in shape and size depending on environmental conditions. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories. Its flowers are small and clustered, often producing a delicate white or pale pink color.

The botanical features of Polylobium varium make it an interesting subject for study in plant morphology and adaptation.
