Ptychotis tenuis

Ptychotis tenuis, commonly known as the slender thorned cactus, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems covered in spines and small, rounded areoles. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, adding a striking visual contrast to its green foliage. It thrives in arid environments and requires minimal water, making it well-suited for xeriscaping.

The botanical features of Ptychotis tenuis make it an interesting subject for both horticultural study and ecological research.
