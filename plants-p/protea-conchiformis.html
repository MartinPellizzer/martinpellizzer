Protea conchiformis

Protea conchiformis, commonly known as the conch shell protea, is a striking South African plant belonging to the Proteaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, colorful inflorescence composed of numerous small, tubular flowers arranged in a spherical or conical shape. The plant is native to the Western Cape region and thrives in well-drained, acidic soils. Its unique appearance makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Protea conchiformis is also valued for its ecological role, providing nectar for pollinators such as birds and insects.
