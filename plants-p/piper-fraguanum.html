Piper fraguanum

Piper fraguanum, commonly known as the fragrant pepper plant, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and small, clustered white flowers. It produces small, round berries that are typically green when unripe and turn red when mature.

Piper fraguanum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of essential oils.
