Psittacanthus dominicensis

Psittacanthus dominicensis, commonly known as the Hispaniola cycad, is a rare cycad species native to the island of Hispaniola.

It belongs to the family Cycadaceae and is characterized by its stout, unbranched stem and a crown of narrow, strap-like leaves. The plant produces small, cone-like structures that contain both male and female reproductive organs. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in shaded, moist environments.

Due to habitat loss and over-collection, it is considered endangered and requires conservation efforts to ensure its survival.
