Pseudosasa cantorii

Pseudosasa cantorii, commonly known as the Chinese bamboo or golden bamboo, is a clumping bamboo species native to eastern Asia.

It features slender, hollow stems that are often golden-green in color, giving it its common name. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically a vibrant green, and it produces small, inconspicuous white flowers. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.

Pseudosasa cantorii prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a versatile choice for various climates.
