Parodia stockingeri

Parodia stockingeri, commonly known as the Stockinger's pincushion cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact, globular shape and a dense covering of white to pale green spines. The plant produces yellow flowers during the warmer months, adding a striking contrast to its otherwise muted appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it well-suited for xerophytic gardens.

Its slow growth rate and unique appearance make it a popular choice among cactus enthusiasts.
