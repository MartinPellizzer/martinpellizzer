Potentilla flavovirens

Potentilla flavovirens, commonly known as the yellow-green cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and clustered inflorescences. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are typically oval-shaped with a slightly hairy texture, and it thrives in well-drained soils.

Potentilla flavovirens is often found in alpine meadows and rocky habitats at high elevations.
