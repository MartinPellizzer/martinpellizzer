Phyllanthus brasiliensis var. oblongifolius

Phyllanthus brasiliensis var. oblongifolius, commonly known as the Brazilian gooseberry, is a shrub native to South America.

It features small, elliptical leaves that are typically glossy and arranged alternately along the stems. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by orange to red berries that are edible. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional medicinal uses.
