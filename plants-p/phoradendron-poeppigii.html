Phoradendron poeppigii

Phoradendron poeppigii, commonly known as the mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches to the branches of host trees, primarily in the Americas.

It lacks chlorophyll and relies on its host for water and nutrients, while still performing some photosynthesis using its scale-like leaves. The plant produces small, white or yellowish flowers and red, fleshy berries that are dispersed by birds. Its symbiotic relationship with host trees can have both positive and negative ecological impacts.

Phoradendron poeppigii plays a role in forest ecosystems by providing food for various wildlife species.
