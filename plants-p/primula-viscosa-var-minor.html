Primula viscosa var. minor

Primula viscosa var. minor, commonly known as the lesser sticky catchfly, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and clustered leaves that are often covered in a fine, sticky substance. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink and white, which bloom in early spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or woodland habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
