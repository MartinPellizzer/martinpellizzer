Prunus serrulata f. alboplena

Prunus serrulata f. alboplena, commonly known as the White-flowered Cherry, is a cultivar of the Japanese cherry tree.

It is characterized by its dense, double white flowers that bloom in early spring, adding a striking visual display. The plant is a deciduous shrub or small tree, typically reaching heights of 3 to 6 meters. Its leaves are ovate with serrated edges, and it produces small, dark red fruits in the autumn.

This cultivar is prized for its ornamental value in gardens and parks.
