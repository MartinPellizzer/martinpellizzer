Piper san-roqueanum

Piper san-roqueanum, commonly known as San Roque pepper, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its climbing habit, with dark green leaves and small, clustered white flowers. Its fruits are tiny, three-lobed berries that turn from green to yellow when mature.

The plant is valued for its aromatic properties and is used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
