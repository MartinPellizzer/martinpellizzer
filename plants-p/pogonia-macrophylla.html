Pogonia macrophylla

Pogonia macrophylla, commonly known as the showy pogonia, is a striking orchid native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple, often with intricate patterns. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds, preferring moist, acidic soils. Its leaves are broad and grass-like, and it blooms from late spring to early summer.

The showy pogonia is considered a rare and endangered species, requiring conservation efforts to protect its habitat.
