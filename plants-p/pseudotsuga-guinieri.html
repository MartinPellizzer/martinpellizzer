Pseudotsuga guinieri

Pseudotsuga guinieri, commonly known as the Guinier's hemlock, is a species of coniferous tree native to the western United States.

It belongs to the genus Pseudotsuga, which includes other hemlock species, and is characterized by its needle-like leaves and cone-bearing structure. The plant has a conical shape with a dense canopy, and its bark is smooth and reddish-brown when young, becoming fissured with age. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

The botanical features of Pseudotsuga guinieri make it an important component of forest ecosystems in its native habitat.
