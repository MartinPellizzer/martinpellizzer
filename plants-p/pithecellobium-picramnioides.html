Pithecellobium picramnioides

Pithecellobium picramnioides, commonly known as the Mexican burr acacia, is a species of shrub native to Central America and northern South America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers, which are typical of the Fabaceae family. The plant produces woody pods that contain edible seeds, which are often used in traditional cuisines. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in disturbed soils.

Its ability to fix nitrogen makes it an important plant in soil enrichment and ecological restoration.
