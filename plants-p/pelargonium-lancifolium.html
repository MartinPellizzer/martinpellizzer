Pelargonium lancifolium

Pelargonium lancifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaved geranium, is a species of flowering plant in the family Geraniaceae.

It is native to South Africa and is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, pink or red flowers. The plant typically grows as a herbaceous perennial, often reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Pelargonium lancifolium is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
