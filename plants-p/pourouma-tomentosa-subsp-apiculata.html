Pourouma tomentosa subsp. apiculata

Pourouma tomentosa subsp.

apiculata, commonly known as the Amazonian areca palm, is a tall, slender palm native to the tropical rainforests of South America. It features a smooth, grayish trunk with a fibrous sheath and large, pinnate leaves that can reach up to 3 meters in length.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in inflorescences, followed by elongated, three-angled fruits containing edible seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in the understory of dense rainforests.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has cultural significance among indigenous communities.
