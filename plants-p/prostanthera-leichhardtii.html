Prostanthera leichhardtii

Prostanthera leichhardtii, commonly known as the Leichhardt mintbush, is a shrub native to Australia, particularly found in the southeastern regions.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant features small, tubular flowers that are typically violet or purple in color, arranged in dense spikes. Its leaves are narrow and aromatic, often emitting a mint-like scent when crushed.

Prostanthera leichhardtii is well adapted to dry and rocky environments, making it a hardy and resilient species.
