Pellionia begoniifolia

Pellionia begoniifolia, commonly known as the begonia-leaved Pellionia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, heart-shaped leaves that resemble those of the begonia genus, hence its common name. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink, adding a subtle ornamental value. Pellionia begoniifolia thrives in shaded, humid environments and is often cultivated as a houseplant or in shaded garden beds.

Its foliage is particularly prized for its aesthetic appeal and is sometimes used in terrariums or as a decorative element in indoor settings.
