Pilosocereus cristalinensis

Pilosocereus cristalinensis, commonly known as the cristalina cactus, is a species native to Brazil.

It is a columnar cactus characterized by its spiny, ribbed stems and a distinctive crown of spines. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, requiring minimal water and well-draining soil.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological role, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.
