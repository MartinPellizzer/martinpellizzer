Parkia parviceps

Parkia parviceps, commonly known as the small-leaf velvet tamarind, is a tropical leguminous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with small, ovate leaflets and produces distinctive yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The tree bears pods containing edible, kidney-shaped seeds that are used in traditional cuisine and medicine. Its bark and leaves are also valued for their medicinal properties, including anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial effects.

Parkia parviceps thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland forests and along riverbanks.
