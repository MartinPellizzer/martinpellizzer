Physostegia micramona

Physostegia micramona, commonly known as the small-flowered whitebeard.

It is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States, characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, tubular flowers. The plant produces pinkish-white blooms that are often tinged with red, and it has lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or wetland habitats.

Physostegia micramona is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
