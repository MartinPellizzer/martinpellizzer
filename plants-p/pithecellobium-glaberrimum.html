Pithecellobium glaberrimum

Pithecellobium glaberrimum, commonly known as the white-fruit poinciana, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, clustered yellow flowers. The plant produces small, round, white or pale yellow fruits that are edible when ripe. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can thrive in a variety of soil types with adequate sunlight.

Due to its nitrogen-fixing ability, it also plays a role in soil enrichment in its native habitats.
