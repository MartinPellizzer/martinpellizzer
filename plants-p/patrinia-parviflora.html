Patrinia parviflora

Patrinia parviflora, commonly known as small-flowered Patrinia, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, white or pale pink flowers arranged in clusters. Its flowering period occurs in late spring to early summer, and it is often found in the wild as part of the Campanulaceae family.

Patrinia parviflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
