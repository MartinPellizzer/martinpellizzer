Plantago durandoi

Plantago durandoi, commonly known as the Durango plantain, is a species of flowering plant native to the arid regions of northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-forming leaves that are covered in a dense layer of fine, silky hairs. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, typically blooming in the spring. It is adapted to survive in dry environments, with a deep root system that allows it to access water from deeper soil layers.

This species is of interest to botanists due to its unique adaptations and potential medicinal properties.
