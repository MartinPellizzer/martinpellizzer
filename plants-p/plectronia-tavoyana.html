Plectronia tavoyana

Plectronia tavoyana, commonly known as the Tavoyan orchid, is a flowering plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in regions of Thailand, Malaysia, and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its tall, erect stem and clusters of small, fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of white and pink. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem.

Plectronia tavoyana is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and unique floral structure.
