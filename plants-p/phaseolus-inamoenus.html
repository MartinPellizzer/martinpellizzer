Phaseolus inamoenus

Phaseolus inamoenus, commonly known as the rough runner bean, is a climbing legume native to Central and South America.

It produces long, slender pods that are typically hairy and have a rough texture. The plant features compound leaves with three to five leaflets and produces small, yellow flowers. It is adapted to warm climates and thrives in well-drained soil.

This species is valued for its edible beans, which are often used in traditional cuisines.
