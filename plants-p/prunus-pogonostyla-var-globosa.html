Prunus pogonostyla var. globosa

Prunus pogonostyla var. globosa, commonly known as the globular cherry, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of southern China, where it thrives in temperate climates. The plant is characterized by its dense, spherical growth habit and produces small, fragrant white flowers in the spring. Its leaves are ovate with a glossy texture, and the fruit is a small, dark red drupe.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
