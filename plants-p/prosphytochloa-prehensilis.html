Prosphytochloa prehensilis

Prosphytochloa prehensilis, commonly known as the climbing grass, is a perennial plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its climbing habit and flexible stems. The plant features narrow, elongated leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets.

Its rhizomatous growth pattern allows it to spread rapidly, making it both a valuable forage plant and a potential weed in agricultural settings.
