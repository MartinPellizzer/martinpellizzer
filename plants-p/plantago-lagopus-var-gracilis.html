Plantago lagopus var. gracilis

Plantago lagopus var. gracilis, commonly known as the slender common plantain, is a variant of the common plantain, characterized by its slender leaves and compact growth habit.

It is a perennial herb native to temperate regions of Europe, Asia, and North America. The plant typically grows in moist, disturbed soils such as roadsides, fields, and wetlands. Its leaves are lance-shaped with a prominent midrib and produce small, greenish-white flowers in a spike-like inflorescence.

The species is known for its adaptability and is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
