Polygonum aviculare var. scabrellum

Polygonum aviculare var. scabrellum, commonly known as rough knotgrass, is a low-growing annual herb characterized by its prostrate or ascending habit.

It features small, greenish-white flowers that appear in dense clusters, typically in late summer. The leaves are lance-shaped, rough to the touch, and often have a hairy texture. This plant thrives in disturbed soils and is commonly found in fields, roadsides, and waste areas.

It is a member of the Polygonaceae family, known for its distinctive bracts and often edible seeds.
