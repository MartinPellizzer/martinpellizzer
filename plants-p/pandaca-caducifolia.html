Pandaca caducifolia

Pandaca caducifolia, commonly known as the deciduous pandaca, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that turn yellow and fall off in autumn, showcasing its deciduous nature. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink, adding ornamental value to its appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Due to its unique foliage and delicate blooms, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a decorative plant.
