Paeonia veitchii subsp. altaica

Paeonia veitchii subsp.

altaica, commonly known as the Altai peony, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Asia. It features large, showy flowers that typically range in color from pink to red, with a distinctive double or semi-double bloom structure.

The plant has robust, upright stems and broad, heart-shaped leaves that are deeply veined. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy addition to gardens in temperate climates.

Its ornamental value is complemented by its historical significance in traditional medicine.
