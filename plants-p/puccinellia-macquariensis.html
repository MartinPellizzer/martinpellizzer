Puccinellia macquariensis

Puccinellia macquariensis, commonly known as the Macquarie flat sedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to Australia.

It typically grows in wetland and swampy areas, often found in the Murray-Darling Basin. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It is adapted to waterlogged soils and plays a role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and wetland conservation.
