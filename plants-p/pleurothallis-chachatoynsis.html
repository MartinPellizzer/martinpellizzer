Pleurothallis chachatoynsis

Pleurothallis chachatoynsis, commonly known as the Cusco orchid, is a species of orchid native to the cloud forests of Peru.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and green. The plant has a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its leaves are usually elliptical and arranged in a compact rosette, adapting to the humid and shaded conditions of its natural habitat.

This orchid is often found growing on trees or rocks, showcasing its epiphytic nature.
