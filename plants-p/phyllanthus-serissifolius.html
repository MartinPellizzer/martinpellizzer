Phyllanthus serissifolius

Phyllanthus serissifolius, commonly known as the velvetleaf or Indian mallow, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features opposite, ovate to elliptic leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas.

Its botanical characteristics make it adaptable to various environmental conditions.
