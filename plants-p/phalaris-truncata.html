Phalaris truncata

Phalaris truncata, commonly known as cut-leaved wild oat, is a perennial grass native to Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its narrow, flat leaves that are truncate at the base, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces spikelets that are typically arranged in clusters along the stem, with small, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and along riverbanks.

Its ecological role includes providing food for various insects and serving as a pioneer species in ecological succession.
