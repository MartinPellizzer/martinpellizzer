Plumeria lanata

Plumeria lanata, commonly known as the woolly plumeria, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, hairy stems and large, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and yellow. The leaves are opposite, simple, and ovate with a waxy texture. The plant produces fragrant, waxy flowers that bloom in the summer months.

It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its striking appearance and is also used in traditional medicine.
