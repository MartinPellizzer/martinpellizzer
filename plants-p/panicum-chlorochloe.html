Panicum chlorochloe

Panicum chlorochloe, commonly known as green panicgrass, is a member of the grass family Poaceae.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically a vibrant green. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences that contain both sterile and fertile florets. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, often found in disturbed areas or along roadsides.

Its rapid growth and ability to colonize open spaces make it an important pioneer species in ecological succession.
