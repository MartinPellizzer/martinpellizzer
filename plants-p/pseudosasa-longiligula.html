Pseudosasa longiligula

Pseudosasa longiligula, commonly known as the long-leaf bamboo, is a species of grass-like plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its slender, hollow stems and narrow, linear leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to form dense clumps, which can stabilize soil and provide habitat for small organisms.

Pseudosasa longiligula is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its elegant appearance.
