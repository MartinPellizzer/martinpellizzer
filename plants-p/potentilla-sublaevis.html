Potentilla sublaevis

Potentilla sublaevis, commonly known as the smooth cinquefoil, is a perennial flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its finely toothed leaves and clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or meadow environments. It has a low-growing habit with woody stems and is often found in the understory of forests.

Potentilla sublaevis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
