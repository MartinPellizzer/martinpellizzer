Pyrus communis var. grisea

Pyrus communis var. grisea, commonly known as the gray apple, is a variant of the common apple tree.

It is characterized by its distinctive grayish bark and small, tart fruits. The plant is native to Europe and has been cultivated for its ornamental value and edible apples. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, thriving in temperate climates.

The leaves are alternate, simple, and have a serrated margin, contributing to its aesthetic appeal.
