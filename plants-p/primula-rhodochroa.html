Primula rhodochroa

Primula rhodochroa, commonly known as the purple primrose, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its erect, hairy stems and clusters of showy, violet to purple flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in alpine or subalpine habitats, thriving in well-drained, rocky soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a fine layer of white or silver hairs, which help reduce water loss in its high-altitude environment.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant, early-season blooms.
