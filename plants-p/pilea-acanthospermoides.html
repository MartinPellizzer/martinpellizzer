Pilea acanthospermoides

Pilea acanthospermoides, commonly known as the Chinese money plant, is a species of flowering plant in the family Urticaceae.

It is native to China and is characterized by its rosette-forming leaves that are thick and fleshy, often with a waxy coating. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in panicles, typically blooming in spring. It is a popular houseplant due to its attractive foliage and ease of care.

Pilea acanthospermoides prefers bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it suitable for indoor cultivation.
