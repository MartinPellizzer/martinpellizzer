Potamogeton richardii

Potamogeton richardii, commonly known as Richard's pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to North America.

It typically grows in shallow, freshwater environments such as lakes, ponds, and slow-moving streams. The plant has long, narrow leaves that are usually arranged in whorls around the stem. Its flowers are small and green, often occurring in clusters near the water surface.

It plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
