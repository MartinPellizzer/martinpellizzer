Placolobium gracile

Placolobium gracile, commonly known as the slender rock cress, is a small flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in coastal or mountainous regions. The plant features basal leaves that are narrow and linear, with small white or pale yellow flowers clustered in umbels. It is a perennial species that thrives in well-drained environments.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and sometimes endangered species.
