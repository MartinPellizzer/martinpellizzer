Phaseolus helvolus

Phaseolus helvolus, commonly known as the bitter bean, is a species of legume native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its long, narrow pods that contain small, kidney-shaped seeds. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters and has compound leaves with three to five leaflets. Its flowers are usually yellow or white and are followed by the development of its edible pods.

The plant is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it a valuable crop in sustainable agriculture.
