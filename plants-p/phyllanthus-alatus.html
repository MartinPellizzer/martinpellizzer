Phyllanthus alatus

Phyllanthus alatus, commonly known as the winged sandmat, is a flowering plant native to tropical and southern Africa.

It is a small shrub that typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, often on rocky soils. The plant is characterized by its distinctive winged leaves, which are narrow and have a prominent midrib with raised veins. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences.

The plant is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional medicine for various health purposes.
