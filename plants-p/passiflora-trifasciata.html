Passiflora trifasciata

Passiflora trifasciata, commonly known as the three-striped passionflower, is a climbing vine native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, intricate flowers that feature five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments surrounding the reproductive structures. The plant produces edible, orange-yellow berries that are rich in nutrients and often consumed by wildlife. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped with a distinctive three-striped pattern on the upper surface.

Passiflora trifasciata is valued in horticulture for its ornamental beauty and is also used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
