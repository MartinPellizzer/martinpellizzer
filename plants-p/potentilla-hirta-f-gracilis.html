Potentilla hirta f. gracilis

Potentilla hirta f. gracilis, commonly known as the slender hairy cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming structure with hairy stems and leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers clustered in umbels, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its foliage is often covered in fine, erect hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, rocky slopes, and woodland edges.
