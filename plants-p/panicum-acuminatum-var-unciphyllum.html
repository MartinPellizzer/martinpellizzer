Panicum acuminatum var. unciphyllum

Panicum acuminatum var. unciphyllum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf panicgrass, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It features slender, erect stems that can grow up to 1 meter in height, with narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically less than 1 centimeter wide. The plant produces small, pale green to brown spikelets that are clustered in dense, cylindrical inflorescences. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, often found in open woodlands and disturbed sites.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive member of the grass family, Poaceae.
