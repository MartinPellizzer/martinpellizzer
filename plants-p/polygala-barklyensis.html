Polygala barklyensis

Polygala barklyensis, commonly known as the Barkley's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Polygalaceae family and is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and thrives in arid or semi-arid environments. Its leaves are usually narrow and hairy, and the flowers are often pink or white, with a distinctive spurred structure.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
