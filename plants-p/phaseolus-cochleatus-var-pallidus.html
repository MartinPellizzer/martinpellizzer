Phaseolus cochleatus var. pallidus

Phaseolus cochleatus var. pallidus, commonly known as the pale runner bean, is a variant of the runner bean species native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its pale green to white pod color and is typically cultivated for its edible beans. The plant is a climbing vine with compound leaves and produces flowers that are usually white or pale purple. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and culinary use in various cuisines.
