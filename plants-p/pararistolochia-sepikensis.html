Pararistolochia sepikensis

Pararistolochia sepikensis, commonly known as the Sepik false horseshoe vine, is a climbing plant native to the tropical regions of Papua New Guinea.

It belongs to the Aristolochiaceae family, characterized by its unique and often intricate floral structures. The plant produces striking, hood-shaped flowers that are typically green or reddish in color, with a complex pattern of veins. Its leaves are heart-shaped and often have a waxy texture, while the stems are woody and twining.

This species is of interest in botanical studies due to its morphological features and potential medicinal properties.
