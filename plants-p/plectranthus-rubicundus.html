Plectranthus rubicundus

Plectranthus rubicundus, commonly known as the red-veined begonia, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety leaves that have deep red veins against a dark green background. The plant produces small, tubular flowers in shades of purple and pink, which bloom in summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light.

Plectranthus rubicundus is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its aesthetic appeal and ease of care.
