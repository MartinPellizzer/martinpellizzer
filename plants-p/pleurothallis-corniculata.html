Pleurothallis corniculata

Pleurothallis corniculata, commonly known as the feather orchid, is a species of orchid native to the cloud forests of Central and South America.

It is a small, epiphytic plant that thrives in humid, shaded environments, often growing on tree trunks or branches. The plant produces delicate, linear leaves and bears small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink. Its unique, feather-like appearance gives it its common name, and it is prized by orchid enthusiasts for its ornamental value.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is often cultivated in controlled environments to ensure its survival.
