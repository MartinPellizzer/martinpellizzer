Potamogeton filiformis

Potamogeton filiformis, commonly known as the slender pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments across the Northern Hemisphere.

It has long, narrow, ribbon-like leaves that are typically bright green and can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually arranged in spikes, and it reproduces both sexually and asexually through vegetative propagation. It plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various water organisms.

Its presence is often an indicator of good water quality in ponds and slow-moving streams.
