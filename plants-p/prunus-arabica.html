Prunus arabica

Prunus arabica, commonly known as the Arabian cherry, is a small deciduous tree native to the Arabian Peninsula.

It produces small, bright red fruits that are rich in antioxidants and have been used in traditional medicine. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid climates, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are alternate and ovate, with a smooth margin, while its flowers are white and fragrant.

The tree is valued for both its ornamental appeal and its edible fruits.
