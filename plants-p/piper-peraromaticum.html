Piper peraromaticum

Piper peraromaticum, commonly known as the aromatic pepper plant, is a climbing shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its slender, vine-like stems and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that develop into elongated, thin berries. Its leaves and fruits are often used in traditional medicine and as a spice due to their aromatic properties.

The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
