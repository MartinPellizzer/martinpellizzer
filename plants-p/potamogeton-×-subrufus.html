Potamogeton × subrufus

Potamogeton × subrufus, commonly known as the redstem pondweed, is a hybrid aquatic plant that combines traits from its parent species, Potamogeton perfoliatus and Potamogeton praelatus.

It is characterized by its reddish stems and linear, grass-like leaves, which are typically arranged in two rows along the stem. This plant is commonly found in shallow freshwater environments such as lakes, ponds, and slow-moving streams. It plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various waterfowl and invertebrates.

Due to its rapid growth, it can become invasive in some regions, altering water quality and impacting native vegetation.
