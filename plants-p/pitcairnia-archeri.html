Pitcairnia archeri

Pitcairnia archeri, commonly known as the silver star bromeliad, is a striking epiphytic plant native to the tropical rainforests of South America.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that form a central cup to collect rainwater, which serves as its primary source of moisture. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences with vibrant orange or yellow flowers, which bloom for several weeks. Its leaves are covered in a dense layer of silvery-white trichomes, giving it a distinctive appearance and aiding in water retention.

Pitcairnia archeri is popular in horticulture for its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
