Paracelastrus ovalifolius

Paracelastrus ovalifolius, commonly known as the oval-leaved celastroid, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the family Celastraceae, which includes a variety of woody plants with often showy flowers. The plant has alternate, ovate leaves that are typically leathery and dark green in color. Its small, yellowish-green flowers are clustered in umbels and are followed by woody capsules that contain several seeds.

It is often found in moist, shaded habitats and is valued for its ornamental potential in horticulture.
