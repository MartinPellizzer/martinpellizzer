Phyllanthus brachyphyllus

Phyllanthus brachyphyllus, commonly known as the small-leaved phyllanthus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Euphorbiaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Asia, including India, Sri Lanka, and parts of Southeast Asia. The plant is characterized by its compact growth habit, with small, rounded leaves that are typically arranged oppositely along the stems. Its flowers are unisexual, with male and female flowers occurring on separate plants, a feature common in many members of the Euphorbiaceae family.

The plant is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories or along stream banks.
