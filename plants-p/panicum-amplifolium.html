Panicum amplifolium

Panicum amplifolium, commonly known as broadleaf panicgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its wide, flat leaves and tall, branched inflorescences that produce small, spikelet-like flowers. The plant typically grows in moist to wet soils, often found in marshes, swamps, and along riverbanks. Its fibrous roots help stabilize soil, making it valuable for erosion control in wetland habitats.

It is also used in restoration projects due to its adaptability and ecological benefits.
