Persica vulgaris var. ovata

Persica vulgaris var. ovata, commonly known as the oval peach.

This variety is a cultivar of the peach tree, characterized by its oval-shaped fruits that are typically smaller than standard peaches. The leaves are broad and ovate, with a slightly serrated edge, and the plant produces showy pink flowers in the spring. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

The fruit is valued for its sweet flavor and is often used in both fresh consumption and culinary applications.
