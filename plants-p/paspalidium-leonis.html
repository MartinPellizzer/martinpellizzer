Paspalidium leonis

Paspalidium leonis, commonly known as the lion's tail grass, is a perennial grass native to Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and distinctive, fluffy seed heads that resemble a lion's tail. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed areas and is adapted to dry, sandy soils. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue.

Paspalidium leonis is often used in ornamental gardening for its unique appearance and drought tolerance.
