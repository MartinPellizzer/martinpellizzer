Pterotheca obovata

Pterotheca obovata, commonly known as the broad-ovate pterotheca, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, obovate leaves that are typically hairy and arranged alternately along the stem. Its flowers are small and yellow, forming dense clusters that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Pterotheca obovata is often found in dry, open habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal.
