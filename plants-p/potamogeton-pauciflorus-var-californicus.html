Potamogeton pauciflorus var. californicus

Potamogeton pauciflorus var. californicus, commonly known as the California pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in North America.

It typically grows in shallow, slow-moving waters and has long, ribbon-like leaves that are often smooth or slightly hairy. The plant produces small, green flowers that are clustered in spike-like arrangements, and it is known for its ability to form dense underwater vegetation. It plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
