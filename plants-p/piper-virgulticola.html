Piper virgulticola

Piper virgulticola, commonly known as the West Indian pepper or Virgin Island pepper, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species. The plant is characterized by its small, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of tiny, greenish-white flowers. It produces small, round berries that turn from green to red as they mature.

Piper virgulticola is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
