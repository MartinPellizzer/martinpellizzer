Penstemon miniatus var. townsendianus

Penstemon miniatus var. townsendianus, commonly known as Townsend's beardtongue, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, upright stems and clusters of tubular, two-lipped flowers that range in color from pink to purple. The leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, giving the plant a distinctive, fuzzy appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

This species is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds.
