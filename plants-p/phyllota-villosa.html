Phyllota villosa

Phyllota villosa, commonly known as the hairy phyllota, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its hairy stems and leaves are adaptations to arid environments, helping to reduce water loss.

Phyllota villosa is also valued for its potential medicinal properties in traditional herbal practices.
