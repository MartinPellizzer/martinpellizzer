Phyllomphax tanganyikensis

Phyllomphax tanganyikensis, commonly known as the Lake Tanganyika waterwheel, is a unique aquatic plant found in the Lake Tanganyika region.

It is characterized by its whorled arrangement of leaves that form a circular structure resembling a waterwheel. The plant has long, thin leaves that are often submerged, with some extending above the water surface. It plays a role in the ecosystem by providing habitat for small aquatic organisms.

Its morphology is adapted to the specific conditions of its native lake environment.
