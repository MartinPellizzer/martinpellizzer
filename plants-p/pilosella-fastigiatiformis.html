Pilosella fastigiatiformis

Pilosella fastigiatiformis, commonly known as the slender pearly everlasting, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads made up of numerous small florets. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and grasslands. Its leaves are narrow and hairy, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The flowers are yellow and appear in clusters, making it a notable species for its ornamental value and ecological role.
