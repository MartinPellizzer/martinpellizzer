Potentilla variabilis

Potentilla variabilis, commonly known as variable cinquefoil, is a flowering plant species native to the northern regions of North America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its variable growth habits, ranging from shrubs to herbaceous plants. The plant features small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the summer months. Its leaves are usually compound, with five to nine leaflets, and it often grows in moist, rocky soils.

Potentilla variabilis is notable for its adaptability and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
