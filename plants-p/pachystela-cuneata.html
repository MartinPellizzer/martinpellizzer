Pachystela cuneata

Pachystela cuneata, commonly known as the narrow-leafed pachystela, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands or disturbed areas. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid climates, showcasing drought-resistant features.

The species is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and ecological significance in its native habitat.
