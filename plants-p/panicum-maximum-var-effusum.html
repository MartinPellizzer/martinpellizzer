Panicum maximum var. effusum

Panicum maximum var. effusum, commonly known as Guinea grass, is a tall, perennial grass native to Africa.

It is characterized by its coarse, hairy leaves and large, spike-like inflorescences. The plant is highly adaptable and thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. It is often used for forage in livestock farming due to its high nutritional value.

Additionally, it plays a role in soil stabilization and erosion control in degraded lands.
