Pleurothallis virgata

Pleurothallis virgata, commonly known as the Virgin's Orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a small, epiphytic plant that typically grows on trees in the canopy of humid forests. The plant produces delicate, fragrant flowers in shades of white and yellow, which bloom during the rainy season. Its roots are adapted to absorb moisture and nutrients from the air and rain.

Pleurothallis virgata is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
