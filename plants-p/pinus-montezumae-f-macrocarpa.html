Pinus montezumae f. macrocarpa

Pinus montezumae f. macrocarpa, commonly known as the Mexican bristlecone pine, is a rare and ancient variant of the Mexican pine.

It is characterized by its distinctive long, bristle-like cones and is native to the high-altitude regions of central Mexico. The plant exhibits slow growth and can live for several centuries, making it a notable example of long-lived conifers. Its foliage consists of needle-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color.

This subspecies is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and ecological significance in its native habitat.
