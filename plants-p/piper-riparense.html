Piper riparense

Piper riparense, commonly known as the river pepper or water pepper, is a flowering plant native to the wetlands and riparian zones of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piper genus in the family Piperaceae, which includes many species of pepper plants. The plant typically grows as a herbaceous perennial, often reaching heights of up to 2 meters. Its leaves are alternate, ovate to lanceolate, and have a distinctive aromatic scent.

The small greenish-yellow flowers are clustered in spikes and are followed by small, fleshy berries that are often used in traditional medicine.
