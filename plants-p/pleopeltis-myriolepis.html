Pleopeltis myriolepis

Pleopeltis myriolepis, commonly known as the broad-leafed rock fern, is a species of epiphytic fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, pinnately compound leaves that can reach up to 1 meter in length. The fronds are typically dark green and have a leathery texture, with prominent veins running throughout the foliage. It thrives in humid, shaded environments, often growing on tree trunks or rocky surfaces.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in tropical and subtropical gardens.
