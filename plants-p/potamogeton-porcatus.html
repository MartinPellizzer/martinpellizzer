Potamogeton porcatus

Potamogeton porcatus, commonly known as pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant found in freshwater environments.

It has long, slender leaves that are typically arranged in whorls around the stem. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often submerged or just at the water's surface. Its roots help stabilize sediments, contributing to water clarity.

Potamogeton porcatus plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
