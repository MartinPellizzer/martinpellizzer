Poa maullinica

Poa maullinica, commonly known as the Maullin grass, is a species of flowering plant in the grass family Poaceae.

It is native to South America, particularly Chile, and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is adapted to temperate climates. Its slender, tufted growth habit and small, spikelet-bearing inflorescences are characteristic features.

Poa maullinica plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various small organisms in its native environment.
