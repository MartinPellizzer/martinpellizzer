Prangos asperula subsp. asperula

Prangos asperula subsp.

asperula, commonly known as the rough prangos, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and hairy, lance-shaped leaves.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rocky or alpine gardens.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native habitat.
