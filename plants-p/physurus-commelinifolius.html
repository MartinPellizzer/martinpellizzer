Physurus commelinifolius

Physurus commelinifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaf marsh grass, is a perennial herb native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It typically grows in wetland habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of freshwater bodies. The plant has slender, grass-like leaves that are usually narrow and slightly curled at the edges. Its inflorescence consists of small, clustered spikes that bear tiny, inconspicuous flowers.

Physurus commelinifolius plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
