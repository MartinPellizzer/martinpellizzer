Polygala angustifolia var. scandens

Polygala angustifolia var. scandens, commonly known as narrowleaf milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the northeastern United States.

It features slender, ascending stems that are often branched and covered with fine hairs. The leaves are alternate, linear to lanceolate, and typically have a glossy green appearance. Its small, pale pink to white flowers are clustered in spike-like inflorescences, and it produces small, bean-like seeds.

This plant thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in open woodlands and along stream banks.
