Passiflora hibiscifolia

Passiflora hibiscifolia, commonly known as the Hibiscifoliate Passiflora, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that feature a complex structure with five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments surrounding the reproductive organs. The plant typically grows as a climbing vine with heart-shaped leaves and produces edible, fleshy berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

Passiflora hibiscifolia is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
