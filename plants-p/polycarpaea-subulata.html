Polycarpaea subulata

Polycarpaea subulata, commonly known as the slender polycarpaea, is a flowering plant belonging to the Acanthaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa and is characterized by its narrow, needle-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or red in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in some botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
