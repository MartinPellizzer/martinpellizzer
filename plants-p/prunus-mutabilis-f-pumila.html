Prunus mutabilis f. pumila

Prunus mutabilis f. pumila, commonly known as the dwarf cherry or Japanese dwarf cherry, is a compact, ornamental shrub native to Japan.

It features small, glossy, dark green leaves that turn vibrant red or orange in the fall. The plant produces clusters of delicate, pinkish-white flowers in early spring, adding a striking visual appeal. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it suitable for gardens and landscapes.

Its compact size and seasonal color changes make it a popular choice for ornamental horticulture.
