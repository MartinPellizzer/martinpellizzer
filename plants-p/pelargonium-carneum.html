Pelargonium carneum

Pelargonium carneum, commonly known as the pink geranium, is a species of flowering plant in the family Geraniaceae.

It is native to South Africa and is characterized by its distinctive pink to purple flowers and velvety foliage. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub with thick, fleshy leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

Pelargonium carneum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
