Papaver nudicaule var. coloradense

Papaver nudicaule var. coloradense, commonly known as the Colorado poppy, is a variant of the common poppy native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its striking red petals and yellow-orange center, which bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and open areas, preferring full sun. It has a short stem with lobed leaves and produces capsule fruits that release seeds when mature.

This variant is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
