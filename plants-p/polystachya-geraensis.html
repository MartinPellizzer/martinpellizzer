Polystachya geraensis

Polystachya geraensis, commonly known as the Geraen's spikegrass, is a member of the orchid family, Orchidaceae.

It is a herbaceous plant native to the humid forests of western Africa, particularly in countries like Ghana and Togo. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in undergrowth near rivers or in dense woodlands.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
