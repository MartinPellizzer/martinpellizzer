Polypodium inaequale

Polypodium inaequale, commonly known as the unequal holly fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Polypodiaceae.

It is characterized by its fronds that are typically divided into segments of varying lengths, giving it its common name. The plant is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia, including India, China, and Southeast Asia. Its rhizome is creeping and produces roots that help anchor it to rocky or shaded environments.

Polypodium inaequale is often found in moist, shaded areas and is valued for its ornamental foliage in horticultural settings.
