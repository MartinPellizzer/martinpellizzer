Piper auristellanum

Piper auristellanum, commonly known as the Amazonian pepper or golden pepper, is a flowering plant native to the rainforests of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many species of pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and heart-shaped leaves with a glossy surface. Its small, yellowish-green flowers are followed by elongated, yellow fruits that contain the edible seeds.

Due to its unique appearance and potential medicinal properties, it is of interest to botanists and ethnobotrists.
