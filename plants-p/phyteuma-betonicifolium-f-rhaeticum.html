Phyteuma betonicifolium f. rhaeticum

Phyteuma betonicifolium f. rhaeticum, commonly known as the Alpine bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to purple, typically blooming in late summer. The plant has lanceolate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, adapted to withstand cold and windy conditions. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in mountain meadows.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
