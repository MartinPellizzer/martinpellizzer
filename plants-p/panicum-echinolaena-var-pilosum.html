Panicum echinolaena var. pilosum

Panicum echinolaena var. pilosum, commonly known as silky millet, is a warm-season grass native to Africa.

It is characterized by its fine, hairy leaves and dense, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in dry, disturbed soils and is adapted to arid environments. It is often used as a forage crop and for erosion control due to its hardy nature.

Its botanical features make it well-suited for regions with low rainfall and poor soil conditions.
