Polygala hebeclada

Polygala hebeclada, commonly known as the narrow-leaved milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to sunny, arid environments. Its leaves are narrow and often have a slightly hairy texture, while its flowers are usually pink or purple in color.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
