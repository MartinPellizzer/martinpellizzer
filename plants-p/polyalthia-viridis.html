Polyalthia viridis

Polyalthia viridis, commonly known as the green flame tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, greyish-brown bark and pinnate leaves that are dark green and glossy. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, often followed by bright red or orange berries. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in lowland forests.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and urban landscaping.
