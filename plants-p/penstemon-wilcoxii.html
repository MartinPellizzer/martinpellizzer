Penstemon wilcoxii

Penstemon wilcoxii, commonly known as the Wilcox penstemon, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of tubular, two-lipped flowers that range in color from blue to violet. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. It is a member of the Plantaginaceae family and is known for its ornamental value in native gardens.

Penstemon wilcoxii is also an important nectar source for pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
