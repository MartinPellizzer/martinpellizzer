Poa sylvatica var. subaristata

Poa sylvatica var. subaristata, commonly known as the slender woodland bluegrass, is a variant of the woodland bluegrass species native to the northern hemisphere.

It is a low-growing perennial grass characterized by its fine, silver-green foliage and compact growth habit. The plant typically forms dense mats and thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and rocky slopes. Its inflorescence consists of slender, spike-like clusters of small, blue-green flowers.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in woodland ecosystems.
