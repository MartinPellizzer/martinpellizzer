Pleurothallis lindenii

Pleurothallis lindenii, commonly known as the Lindenberg's orchid, is a species of orchid belonging to the Epidendroideae subfamily.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Colombia and Ecuador, where it thrives in humid, shaded forest environments. This epiphytic plant typically grows on trees and has small, lance-shaped leaves that are adapted to retain moisture. Its flowers are usually small and fragrant, with a variety of colors, including white, yellow, and pink, depending on the specific variety.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts for its ornamental value.
