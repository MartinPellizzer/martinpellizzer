Pyracantha atalantioides

Pyracantha atalantioides, commonly known as the Natal hawthorn, is a small evergreen shrub native to southern Africa.

It features glossy, dark green leaves and produces clusters of small, white flowers in spring. The plant is notable for its bright orange to red berries that persist through winter, providing food for birds and other wildlife. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value.

Pyracantha atalantioides is relatively low maintenance and can tolerate a range of environmental conditions.
