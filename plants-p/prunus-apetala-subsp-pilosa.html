Prunus apetala subsp. pilosa

Prunus apetala subsp.

pilosa, commonly known as the silky cherry, is a shrub or small tree native to parts of East Asia. It is characterized by its smooth, often silvery-haired bark and small, clustered leaves.

The plant produces white or pink flowers in spring, followed by edible, sweet fruits known as cherries. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is cultivated in gardens and orchards.
