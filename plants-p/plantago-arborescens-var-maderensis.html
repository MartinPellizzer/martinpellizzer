Plantago arborescens var. maderensis

Plantago arborescens var. maderensis, commonly known as the Mader's plantain, is a variant of the broadleaf plantain.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its rosette of broad, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish flowers in a spike-like inflorescence, which are clustered in dense groups. It is native to South America and thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

This variant is often found in grasslands and along stream banks, where it contributes to soil stabilization and ecological diversity.
