Psydrax recurvifolius

Psydrax recurvifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaf psidium, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often glabrous and have a leathery texture. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, followed by edible berries that are yellow when ripe. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland forests.

Due to its ornamental value and fruit, it is cultivated in some regions for both horticultural and culinary purposes.
