Protasparagus densiflorus

Protasparagus densiflorus, commonly known as the silver serradella, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, branched foliage and small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has a creeping growth habit with hairy stems and is often used in rock gardens or as a ground cover. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy choice for Mediterranean climates.

Its ornamental value comes from its attractive foliage and long blooming period.
