Pedicularis reptans

Pedicularis reptans, commonly known as the creeping monkey orchid, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the family Orchidaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, spurred flowers that are often brightly colored. The plant is herbaceous, with creeping stems that allow it to spread along the ground. Its leaves are alternate and ovate, often with a hairy texture.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is known for its ornamental value in gardens.
