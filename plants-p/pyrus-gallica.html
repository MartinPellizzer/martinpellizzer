Pyrus gallica

Pyrus gallica, commonly known as the common whitebeam, is a deciduous tree native to Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in early spring. The tree produces small, edible berries that are typically dark blue to black in color. Its leaves are ovate to elliptic in shape, with serrated edges, and turn yellow in the autumn.

Pyrus gallica is often found in woodlands and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
