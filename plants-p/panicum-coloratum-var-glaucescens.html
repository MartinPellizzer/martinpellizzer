Panicum coloratum var. glaucescens

Panicum coloratum var. glaucescens, commonly known as blue panicgrass, is a perennial grass species native to Africa.

It is characterized by its bluish-green foliage and compact growth habit, making it a visually appealing ornamental plant. The leaves are narrow and have a dense, tufted appearance, often with a slight waxy coating. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, preferring warm climates.

This variety is also valued for its drought tolerance and ability to stabilize soil in dry environments.
