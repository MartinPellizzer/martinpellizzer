Prunus ovata

Prunus ovata, commonly known as the Japanese apricot, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its oval-shaped leaves and showy, fragrant flowers that bloom in early spring. The fruit, which is a small, yellowish drupe, matures in late summer and is edible, though often used for ornamental purposes. The plant is valued for its ornamental beauty and is commonly cultivated in gardens.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.
