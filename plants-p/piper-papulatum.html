Piper papulatum

Piper papulatum, commonly known as the roughleaf pepper or tropical pepper, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species such as black pepper. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves with serrated edges and small, greenish-white flowers that grow in clusters. Its fruit is a small, spherical berry that turns red when ripe, containing a single seed.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has some traditional medicinal uses in local cultures.
