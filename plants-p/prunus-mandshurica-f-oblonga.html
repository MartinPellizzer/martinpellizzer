Prunus mandshurica f. oblonga

Prunus mandshurica f. oblonga, commonly known as the Manchurian cherry, is a shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and white or pinkish flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, edible fruits that are similar to cherries but are typically less sweet. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to a variety of soil types.

This variety is valued for its hardiness and ability to thrive in temperate climates.
