Passiflora talamancensis

Passiflora talamancensis, commonly known as the Talamancan passionflower, is a climbing vine native to Central America.

It produces striking, showy flowers with intricate structures, including a crown of filaments and a central corona. The plant thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring moist, well-drained soil. Its flowers are pollinated primarily by hummingbirds and bats, contributing to its ecological significance.

The fruit, which resembles a small, hairy orange, is edible and has a sweet, tangy flavor.
