Penthea pumilio

Penthea pumilio, commonly known as the dwarf penthea, is a flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is a small shrub that typically grows at high altitudes, thriving in cool, temperate climates. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow. Its leaves are simple, ovate, and often covered in a fine, silvery down.

Penthea pumilio is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and by plant enthusiasts.
