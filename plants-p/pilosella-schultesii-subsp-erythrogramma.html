Pilosella schultesii subsp. erythrogramma

Pilosella schultesii subsp.

erythrogramma, commonly known as the red-stemmed pearly everlasting, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and small, daisy-like flowers.

The subspecies is distinguished by its reddish stems and pale yellow to orange flower heads. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and rocky areas.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in horticulture for its drought tolerance and long-lasting flowers.
