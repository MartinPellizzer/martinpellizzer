Papaver argemone f. bimaculatum

Papaver argemone f. bimaculatum, commonly known as the two-spotted poppy, is a variant of the Argemone poppy characterized by its distinctive two-colored markings on the petals.

It is a herbaceous annual plant native to regions of Asia, including China and India. The plant features showy, bright yellow to orange flowers with a prominent central stigma that contrasts with the petal coloration. Its leaves are typically lobed and covered in a fine layer of white or grayish hairs.

This cultivar is often grown for its ornamental value in gardens and for its historical use in traditional medicine.
