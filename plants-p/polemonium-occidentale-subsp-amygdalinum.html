Polemonium occidentale subsp. amygdalinum

Polemonium occidentale subsp.

amygdalinum, commonly known as western Jacob's ladder, is a herbaceous perennial native to the western United States. It features showy, clustered blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and have a slightly aromatic scent. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

Its botanical name reflects its historical association with the genus Polemonium, which includes several species with similar floral structures.
