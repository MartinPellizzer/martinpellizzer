Potamogeton foliosus var. fibrillosus

Potamogeton foliosus var. fibrillosus, commonly known as the fibrous pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in North America.

It features long, ribbon-like leaves that are often covered in fine, fibrous strands, giving it its varietal name. The plant typically grows in shallow, slow-moving waters and is characterized by its clustered inflorescences. Its reproductive structures are small and inconspicuous, contributing to its role in aquatic ecosystems.

It plays an important role in water filtration and provides habitat for various aquatic organisms.
