Protea foliosa

Protea foliosa, commonly known as the leaf protea, is a striking South African plant belonging to the Proteaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescence composed of numerous petal-like bracts that surround small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in coastal regions and is adapted to nutrient-poor soils, showcasing the family's ecological versatility. Its foliage consists of broad, leathery leaves that are often covered in a fine, white waxy coating.

Protea foliosa is a popular ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and resilience in various environmental conditions.
