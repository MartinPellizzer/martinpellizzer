Paronychia chilensis var. appressa

Paronychia chilensis var. appressa, commonly known as the Chilean sandmat, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the coastal regions of southern Chile.

It features a compact rosette of fleshy, blue-gray leaves that are covered in a fine layer of white hairs, which help reduce water loss in its arid habitat. The plant produces small, pinkish-white flowers arranged in a dense cluster at the center of the rosette, typically blooming in late winter to early spring. It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and is well adapted to coastal conditions with strong winds and salt spray.

Due to its drought tolerance and ornamental appeal, it is sometimes cultivated in rock gardens or as a succulent specimen.
