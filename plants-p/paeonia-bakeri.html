Paeonia bakeri

Paeonia bakeri, commonly known as the Baker's peony, is a species native to the mountainous regions of western China.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and large, showy flowers that typically bloom in late spring. The plant features compound, bipinnate leaves that are finely divided, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Paeonia bakeri is valued for its horticultural beauty and is often cultivated for its vibrant, fragrant blooms.
