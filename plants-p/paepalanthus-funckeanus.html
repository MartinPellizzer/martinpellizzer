Paepalanthus funckeanus

Paepalanthus funckeanus, commonly known as the Funckean ghost flower, is a striking member of the Bromeliaceae family native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its large, showy, white bracts that resemble ghostly apparitions, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting various pollinators. It thrives in shaded, humid environments and is often found in rocky or forested areas.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
