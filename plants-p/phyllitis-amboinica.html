Phyllitis amboinica

Phyllitis amboinica, commonly known as the Amboina water fern, is a species of aquatic fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, floating fronds that are typically less than 10 centimeters in length. The plant thrives in slow-moving freshwater environments, such as ponds and streams, where it can anchor itself to submerged surfaces. Its rhizomes are slender and help in nutrient absorption, while its spore-producing structures are located on the underside of the fronds.

Phyllitis amboinica plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for small aquatic organisms.
