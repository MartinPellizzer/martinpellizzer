Phelypaea ramosa var. simplex

Phelypaea ramosa var. simplex, commonly known as the simple branched fern, is a species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its delicate, feathery fronds that exhibit a distinctive branching pattern. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests or along stream banks. Its leaves are usually bipinnate, with small, closely spaced pinnae that give it a lush, airy appearance.

This fern is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
