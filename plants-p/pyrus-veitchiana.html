Pyrus veitchiana

Pyrus veitchiana, commonly known as the Veitch's pear, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and distinctive, finely toothed leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers in early spring, followed by edible, sweet-tart fruits. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens.

Pyrus veitchiana prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.
