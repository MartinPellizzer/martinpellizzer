Polypodium serpens var. grande

Polypodium serpens var. grande, commonly known as the giant wall fern, is a variant of the common polypody.

It is a terrestrial fern native to tropical and subtropical regions, particularly in Asia and the Pacific Islands. The plant is characterized by its large, elongated fronds that can grow up to several feet in length. Its leaves are typically bipinnate, with a distinctive, almost serpentine appearance.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found growing on rocks or in forest undergrowth.
