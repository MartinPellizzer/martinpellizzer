Prunus japonica var. packangensis

Prunus japonica var. packangensis, commonly known as the Japanese flowering cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its ornamental value, particularly during spring when it produces clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers. The plant has elliptical leaves that are typically dark green and turn yellow in autumn. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

This variety is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal and seasonal blooms.
