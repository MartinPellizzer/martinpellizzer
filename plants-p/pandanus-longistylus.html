Pandanus longistylus

Pandanus longistylus, commonly known as the long-styled pandanus, is a member of the Pandanaceae family native to the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feather-like leaves that can grow up to several meters in length. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in panicles, which are typically borne at the tips of the leaves. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to thrive in wet, coastal environments.

The plant is often used in traditional crafts and for its aromatic leaves, which are utilized in cooking and medicine.
