Phyllostachys fauriei

Phyllostachys fauriei, commonly known as the Faurie bamboo, is a fast-growing grass species native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Bambusoideae subfamily and is characterized by its slender, erect culms that can reach heights of up to 10 meters. The plant has long, narrow leaves that are typically green on the upper surface and lighter in color on the underside. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in landscaping and traditional Chinese medicine.

The Faurie bamboo is also valued for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions and its resilience to environmental stress.
