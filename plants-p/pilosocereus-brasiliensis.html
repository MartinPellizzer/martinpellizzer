Pilosocereus brasiliensis

Pilosocereus brasiliensis, commonly known as the Brazilian torch cactus, is a species native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems that are covered in white, woolly spines, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers during the rainy season, typically in the summer months. It is adapted to arid environments and requires well-drained soil to thrive.

Pilosocereus brasiliensis is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique structure and resilience.
