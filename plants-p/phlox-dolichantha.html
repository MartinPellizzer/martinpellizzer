Phlox dolichantha

Phlox dolichantha, commonly known as the long-stalked phlox, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, woodlands or along stream banks, preferring partial to full shade. The plant produces clusters of showy, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture, while its stems are often reddish in color.

Phlox dolichantha is valued in native plant gardens for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
