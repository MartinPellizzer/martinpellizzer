Phalaenopsis amabilis var. ramosa

Phalaenopsis amabilis var. ramosa, commonly known as the orchid with branched inflorescence, is a tropical orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its elegant, long-lasting flowers that come in a variety of colors, including white, pink, and purple. The plant has a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its leaves are broad and fleshy, typically arranged in a fan-like pattern.

The branched inflorescence sets it apart from other Phalaenopsis varieties, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
