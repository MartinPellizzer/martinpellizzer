Piper dimorphum

Piper dimorphum, commonly known as the two-form pepper, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many species of pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves and small, greenish-white flowers that develop into elongated, woody fruits. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their aromatic and stimulant properties.

The plant's botanical features make it an interesting subject for both horticultural study and ethnobotanical research.
