Paracaryum intermedium var. calathicarpum

Paracaryum intermedium var. calathicarpum, commonly known as the golden cup fern, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, feathery fronds that are typically golden-yellow in color. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests. Its unique, cup-like spore cases are a distinguishing feature.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ease of care in suitable conditions.
