Pinknea

Pinknea, commonly known as the pink pea or pink bean, is a genus of flowering plants in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to various regions of South America, particularly in the Andean foothills. The plants typically feature compound leaves and produce small, delicate flowers that range in color from pale pink to deep red. Pinknea species are often cultivated for their edible pods, which contain seeds used in traditional cuisines.

These plants are also valued for their ornamental appeal and ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, benefiting agricultural practices.
