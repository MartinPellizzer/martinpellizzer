Prunus × lannesiana f. sirotae

Prunus × lannesiana f. sirotae, commonly known as the Japanese apricot, is a compact, ornamental shrub native to Japan.

It is characterized by its dense, rounded growth habit and showy, single or double pink to white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, edible fruits that are similar in appearance to peaches but are typically not cultivated for consumption. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

Its ornamental value lies in its early blooming habit and attractive foliage throughout the growing season.
