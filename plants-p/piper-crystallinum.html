Piper crystallinum

Piper crystallinum, commonly known as the crystalline pepper, is a flowering plant native to the rainforests of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species. The plant is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and small, heart-shaped leaves. It produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that develop into elongated, woody fruits.

The plant's unique feature is its ability to exude a crystalline substance from its stems, which is believed to have medicinal properties.
