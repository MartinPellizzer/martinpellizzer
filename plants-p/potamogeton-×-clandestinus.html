Potamogeton × clandestinus

Potamogeton × clandestinus, commonly known as the hybrid water plant, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Potamogeton perfoliatus and Potamogeton przewalskii.

It is a submerged aquatic plant that typically grows in freshwater habitats such as lakes, rivers, and ponds. The plant has long, narrow leaves that are often arranged in whorls around the stem, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers. It is characterized by its ability to thrive in a variety of water conditions and its role in providing habitat for aquatic organisms.

Due to its hybrid nature, it exhibits a range of morphological variations that can make identification challenging.
