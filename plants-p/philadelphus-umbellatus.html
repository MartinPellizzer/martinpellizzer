Philadelphus umbellatus

Philadelphus umbellatus, commonly known as the sweet shrub, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It features compound leaves with ovate to elliptic leaflets and produces showy, fragrant, yellowish-white flowers in early summer. The plant is characterized by its upright growth habit and is often found in moist, woodsy areas. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.

The fruit is a dry capsule that splits open to release small, hairy seeds.
