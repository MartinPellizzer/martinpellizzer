Primula calderiana subsp. strumosa

Primula calderiana subsp.

strumosa, commonly known as the Caldeira primrose, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to high-altitude regions of South America. It features rosettes of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, soft hairs.

The plant produces clusters of fragrant, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white and pink, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and delicate species.
