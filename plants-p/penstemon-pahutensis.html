Penstemon pahutensis

Penstemon pahutensis, commonly known as the Pahutensan beardtongue, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces spikes of tubular, two-lipped flowers that range in color from pink to purple. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its drought tolerance.

Penstemon pahutensis is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
