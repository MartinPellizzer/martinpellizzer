Piptadenia laxa var. pubescens

Piptadenia laxa var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy piptadenia, is a leguminous shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with hairy surfaces and small, yellowish flowers arranged in racemes. The plant produces elongated, woody pods that contain flattened, brown seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in disturbed or secondary forest areas.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and potential applications in traditional medicine.
