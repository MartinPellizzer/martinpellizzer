Ponerorchis bifoliata

Ponerorchis bifoliata, commonly known as the two-leaved orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in moist, shaded forest environments. The plant features a short rhizome with two opposite leaves, which are broad and elliptical in shape. Its flowers are small and greenish-white, often clustered in a loose inflorescence.

Ponerorchis bifoliata is noted for its delicate appearance and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
