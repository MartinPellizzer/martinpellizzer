Pleione delavayi

Pleione delavayi, commonly known as the Delavay's orchid, is a striking member of the Orchidaceae family native to the mountainous regions of China.

This plant is characterized by its elegant, lance-shaped leaves and showy, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in spring. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in forested areas at high altitudes. The flowers are usually pale green to white with yellow or orange markings, adding to its ornamental appeal.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and protected species in its native range.
