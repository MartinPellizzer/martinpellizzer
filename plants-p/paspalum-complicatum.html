Paspalum complicatum

Paspalum complicatum, commonly known as the clustered grass or silky paspalum, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and slender, wiry stems that often form compact clumps. The leaves are narrow, hairless, and have a bluish-green color, while the inflorescence consists of spikelets that are loosely arranged in panicles. It thrives in moist to wet environments, often found in marshy areas or along riverbanks.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in wetland restoration projects.
