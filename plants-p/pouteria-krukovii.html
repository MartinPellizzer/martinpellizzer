Pouteria krukovii

Pouteria krukovii, commonly known as the Brazilian nut tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is characterized by its large, glossy, elliptical leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into woody pods containing edible seeds. The tree can grow up to 30 meters tall and has a dense, buttressed root system that helps it thrive in moist, nutrient-rich soils.

Its fruits are an important food source for various wildlife, and the seeds are harvested for their nutritional value and commercial use.
