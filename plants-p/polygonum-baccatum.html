Polygonum baccatum

Polygonum baccatum, commonly known as the black nightshade or Chinese lantern plant, is a herbaceous perennial native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive red, inflated seed pods that resemble small lanterns, which are often used in ornamental gardening. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in spikes, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. Its leaves are alternate, heart-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.

Polygonum baccatum is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.
