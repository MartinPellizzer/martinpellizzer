Photinia villosa var. formosana

Photinia villosa var. formosana, commonly known as the Formosa photinia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that turn vibrant red in the fall. The plant produces small, white flowers in spring, which are followed by red, fleshy berries. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value.

Its adaptability makes it a popular choice for gardens and urban environments.
