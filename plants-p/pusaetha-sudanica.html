Pusaetha sudanica

Pusaetha sudanica, commonly known as Sudanese thistle, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is native to the arid and semi-arid regions of Africa, particularly Sudan, and thrives in dry, sandy soils. The plant is characterized by its spiny leaves and yellow flowers, which bloom in clusters. It has adapted to harsh environmental conditions through deep root systems and drought tolerance.

Pusaetha sudanica is often considered a weed in agricultural areas due to its aggressive growth and ability to colonize disturbed soils.
