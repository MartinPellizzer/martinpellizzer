Populus alba f. pyramidalis

Populus alba f. pyramidalis, commonly known as the white poplar pyramidal form, is a cultivar of the white poplar tree characterized by its narrow, conical shape.

It features white, fluffy flowers that appear in early spring, followed by small, winged seeds. The leaves are heart-shaped with a serrated margin and are typically gray-green in color. This form is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and upright growth habit.

It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for a variety of garden settings.
