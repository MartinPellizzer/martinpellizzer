Pinus kesiya subsp. szemaoensis

Pinus kesiya subsp.

szemaoensis, commonly known as the Yunnan pine, is a subspecies native to the mountainous regions of southwestern China. It is a coniferous evergreen tree characterized by its long, slender needles and cone-bearing structure.

The plant exhibits typical gymnosperm features, including vascular tissue and the absence of true flowers. Its foliage is adapted to high-altitude environments, with needle-like leaves reducing water loss.

This subspecies plays a significant role in the ecological systems of its native habitat, providing habitat and contributing to soil stabilization.
