Pinus parviflora var. kwangtungensis

Pinus parviflora var. kwangtungensis, commonly known as the Kwangtung pine, is a variant of the Japanese white pine.

It is a coniferous evergreen tree native to southern China, particularly in Guangdong province. The tree typically grows to a height of 10–20 meters, featuring a conical shape with horizontal branches. Its needles are short, stiff, and dark green, often growing in clusters of five.

The cones are small, woody, and typically measure around 3–5 centimeters in length.
