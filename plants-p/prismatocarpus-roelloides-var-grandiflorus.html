Prismatocarpus roelloides var. grandiflorus

Prismatocarpus roelloides var. grandiflorus, commonly known as the large-flowered flame tree, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, flame-like inflorescences that produce clusters of bright orange to red flowers. The plant has compound leaves with elongated, lance-shaped leaflets and produces small, round, woody fruits. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The bark is smooth and grayish, with a tendency to exfoliate, revealing a lighter, mottled underlayer.
