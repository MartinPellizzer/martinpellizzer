Peltapteris colombiana

Peltapteris colombiana, commonly known as the Colombian shield fern, is a fossil plant belonging to the order Marattiales.

It is characterized by its large, shield-shaped fronds with a distinctive central vein and numerous lateral veins. The plant exhibits a complex vascular system, indicating a high level of evolutionary advancement for its time. Its leaves are typically bipinnate, with a broad, flat structure that suggests it thrived in moist, tropical environments.

Fossil records of Peltapteris colombiana are primarily found in Late Carboniferous deposits in South America.
