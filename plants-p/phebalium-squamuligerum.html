Phebalium squamuligerum

Phebalium squamuligerum, commonly known as the silver-leafed honeysuckle, is a shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its silvery-grey, scale-like leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. Its growth habit is typically compact, making it suitable for small garden spaces.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
