Potentilla microphylla var. glabriuscula

Potentilla microphylla var. glabriuscula, commonly known as the smoothleaf cinquefoil, is a low-growing shrub native to the western United States.

It features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. The leaves are typically oval-shaped and covered in fine, soft hairs, giving the plant a slightly fuzzy texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

This variety is noted for its hardiness and ability to tolerate drought conditions.
