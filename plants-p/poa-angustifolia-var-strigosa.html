Poa angustifolia var. strigosa

Poa angustifolia var. strigosa, commonly known as the narrowleaf bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the northern hemisphere.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves that are typically less than 2 mm wide, giving it a delicate appearance. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with inconspicuous flowers, which are typical of the Poaceae family. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.

Due to its fine texture and ornamental appeal, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic value.
