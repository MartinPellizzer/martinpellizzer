Pyrus × pozdnjakovii

Pyrus × pozdnjakovii, commonly known as the Pozdnjakovii pear, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Pyrus communis (European pear) and Pyrus pyraster (sloe).

It is characterized by its ornamental value, featuring attractive foliage and showy white flowers in spring. The fruit is typically small and tart, similar to the sloe, but it is not widely cultivated for consumption. This plant is often grown in gardens for its aesthetic appeal rather than its fruit.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it adaptable to various garden environments.
