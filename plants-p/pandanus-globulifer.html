Pandanus globulifer

Pandanus globulifer, commonly known as the globular pandanus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Pandanaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including Indonesia and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its large, clustered inflorescences that resemble globular structures, which give it its common name. Its leaves are long, narrow, and often have a distinctive waxy texture.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional crafts and as a source of fiber.
