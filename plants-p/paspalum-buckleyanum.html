Paspalum buckleyanum

Paspalum buckleyanum, commonly known as Buckley's grass, is a perennial grass species native to Australia.

It typically grows in coastal and sandy soils, often found in dunes and saline environments. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained conditions.

Its ecological importance includes stabilizing sand dunes and providing habitat for native wildlife.
