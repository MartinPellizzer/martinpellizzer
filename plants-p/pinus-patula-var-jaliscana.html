Pinus patula var. jaliscana

Pinus patula var. jaliscana, commonly known as the Jaliscan Mexican pine, is a variant of the Mexican pine native to the mountainous regions of Jalisco, Mexico.

It is a coniferous evergreen tree characterized by its slender, cylindrical trunk and dense, pyramidal crown. The needles are long, flat, and blue-green in color, often arranged in bundles of five. Its cones are slender and slightly curved, containing winged seeds that are dispersed by wind.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes for its aesthetic and ecological benefits.
