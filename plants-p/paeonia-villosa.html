Paeonia villosa

Paeonia villosa, commonly known as the hairy peony, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its robust, woody stems and large, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant produces compound leaves with a hairy texture, which gives it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Paeonia villosa is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its medicinal properties.
