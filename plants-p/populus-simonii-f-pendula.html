Populus simonii f. pendula

Populus simonii f. pendula, commonly known as the weeping poplar, is a unique variant of the poplar tree characterized by its cascading, drooping branches.

This ornamental tree is native to China and is often cultivated for its striking visual appeal. Its leaves are broad and heart-shaped, with a smooth margin, and they turn yellow in the autumn. The tree produces small, fluffy seeds that are dispersed by the wind, contributing to its ability to spread in suitable environments.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for landscaping in temperate regions.
