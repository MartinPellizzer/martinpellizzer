Palala bialata

Palala bialata, commonly known as the silver leaf orchid, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, silver-green foliage and fragrant, showy flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to tree trunks or branches in humid, shaded environments. Its roots are adapted to absorb moisture from the air and decaying organic matter.

Palala bialata is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and private collections.
