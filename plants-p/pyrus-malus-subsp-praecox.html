Pyrus malus subsp. praecox

Pyrus malus subsp.

praecox, commonly known as the early apple, is a subspecies of the apple tree native to Central Asia. It is characterized by its small, tart fruits that ripen earlier than those of the common apple.

The plant typically grows in temperate climates and prefers well-drained soil. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have serrated edges.

This subspecies is often cultivated for its ornamental value and for use in breeding programs to improve apple varieties.
