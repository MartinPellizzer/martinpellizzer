Primula interjacens var. epilosa

Primula interjacens var. epilosa, commonly known as the alpine primrose, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant, often pink or white, flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine soils, thriving in cool, moist environments. Its leaves are usually basal, lance-shaped, and covered in a dense layer of fine hairs.

This variant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens that mimic its natural habitat.
