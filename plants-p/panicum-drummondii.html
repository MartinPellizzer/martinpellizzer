Panicum drummondii

Panicum drummondii, commonly known as Drummond's panicgrass, is a species of grass native to North America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and produces distinctive, fluffy seed heads that are often seen in open fields and prairies. The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters and thrives in well-drained soils. It is drought-tolerant and often found in disturbed areas, making it a common sight in the central and southern United States.

Its ecological importance includes providing food for wildlife and stabilizing soil in grassland ecosystems.
