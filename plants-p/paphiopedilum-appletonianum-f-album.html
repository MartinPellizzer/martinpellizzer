Paphiopedilum appletonianum f. album

Paphiopedilum appletonianum f. album, commonly known as the White Lady's Slipper, is a rare and striking orchid native to the Philippines.

It is characterized by its pale white petals and a unique, pouch-like lip that resembles a slipper, a feature typical of the Paphiopedilum genus. The plant typically grows as a terrestrial orchid, thriving in shaded, humid environments with well-draining soil. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, adding a delicate beauty to its natural habitat.

Due to its rarity and ornamental value, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
