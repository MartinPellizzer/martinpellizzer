Pediocactus uncinatus

Pediocactus uncinatus, commonly known as the hooked pediohook, is a species of cactus native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical, ribbed stems that are typically green to blue-green in color. The plant produces clusters of small, yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It is a slow-growing species that thrives in well-drained soil and full sun.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is often cultivated by succulent enthusiasts.
