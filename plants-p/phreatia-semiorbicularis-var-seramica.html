Phreatia semiorbicularis var. seramica

Phreatia semiorbicularis var. seramica, commonly known as the Seram orchid, is a species of orchid native to the island of Seram in Indonesia.

It is a terrestrial orchid with a sympodial growth habit, characterized by its upright, cane-like pseudobulbs. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink, often with a yellow lip. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is appreciated by orchid enthusiasts for its beauty and rarity.
