Primula megaseifolia

Primula megaseifolia, commonly known as the giant primrose, is a species of flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of yellow and orange. The plant features broad, waxy leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of pubescence. It thrives in cool, humid environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its striking appearance, it is a popular choice for botanical gardens and conservation efforts.
