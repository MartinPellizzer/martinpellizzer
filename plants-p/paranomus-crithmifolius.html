Paranomus crithmifolius

Paranomus crithmifolius, commonly known as sea holly, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking blue or purple flowers and silver-green, fuzzy foliage. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and rocky soils, thriving in well-drained conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a distinctive crinkled appearance.

Paranomus crithmifolius is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and coastal landscapes.
