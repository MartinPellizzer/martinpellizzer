Phyllanthus insulanus

Phyllanthus insulanus, commonly known as the island spurge, is a flowering plant native to the Indo-Pacific region.

It is a small shrub that typically grows in coastal and sandy habitats. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and often have a waxy coating.

Phyllanthus insulanus is known for its ability to thrive in saline and arid environments.
