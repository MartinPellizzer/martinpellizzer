Piper holtonii var. parvispicum

Piper holtonii var. parvispicum, commonly known as the small-eyed pepper plant, is a species native to the tropical regions of Central America.

It is a climbing vine characterized by its small, round leaves and clusters of tiny, fragrant flowers. The plant produces small, peppery fruits that are used in traditional culinary and medicinal practices. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and ethnobotanical studies.
