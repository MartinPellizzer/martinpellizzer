Polygala monspeliaca var. angustisepala

Polygala monspeliaca var. angustisepala, commonly known as the narrow-sepaled milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has palmate leaves with three to five leaflets, and its flowers are typically clustered in racemes. It is adapted to survive in harsh environments with low rainfall and high temperatures.
