Parodiophyllochloa rhizogona

Parodiophyllochloa rhizogona, commonly known as the red sandplain grass, is a small, perennial grass species native to arid and semi-arid regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically less than 10 cm in length. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear minute flowers, which are adapted for wind pollination. It is well-suited to poor soils and can thrive in harsh environmental conditions, making it an important component of native vegetation in its habitat.
