Piper flavescens

Piper flavescens, commonly known as the yellow pepper plant, is a species of flowering vine native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper plants like the common black pepper. The plant produces small, yellowish-green berries that are used in traditional medicine and cuisine. Its leaves are often used in herbal remedies due to their aromatic properties.

The plant's botanical characteristics include opposite, ovate leaves and small, clustered flowers.
