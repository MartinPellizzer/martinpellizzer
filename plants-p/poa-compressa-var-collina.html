Poa compressa var. collina

Poa compressa var. collina, commonly known as the mountain bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its fine, dark green blades and dense, tufted growth habit. The plant typically grows in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes. Its flowers are small, spike-like inflorescences that produce light blue to purple florets.

Poa compressa var. collina is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in native plant gardens.
