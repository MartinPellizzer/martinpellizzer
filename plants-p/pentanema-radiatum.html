Pentanema radiatum

Pentanema radiatum, commonly known as the silverleaf mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on various host trees, primarily in the southern United States.

It has small, leathery leaves that are typically covered in a white, waxy coating, which gives it its common name. The plant produces small, white or pale green flowers that are often clustered together, followed by berries that are toxic to many animals. Its seeds are dispersed by birds that eat the berries, contributing to its spread across different host species.

The plant's botanical structure reflects its parasitic lifestyle, with reduced roots and a reliance on host plants for water and nutrients.
