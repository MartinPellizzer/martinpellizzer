Pygeum latiphyllum

Pygeum latiphyllum, commonly known as the broad-leaved African cherry, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to the tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and distinctive, smooth, greyish bark. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are followed by orange-red, fleshy fruits containing several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in moist, shaded habitats.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
