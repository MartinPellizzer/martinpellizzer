Potentilla pulchella

Potentilla pulchella, commonly known as the showy cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, sunny habitats such as meadows and streambanks. It has palmately divided leaves and produces small, five-lobed flowers.

Potentilla pulchella is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens and natural landscapes.
