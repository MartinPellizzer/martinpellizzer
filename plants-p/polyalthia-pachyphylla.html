Polyalthia pachyphylla

Polyalthia pachyphylla, commonly known as the thick-leaved flame tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, leathery leaves that are often clustered at the base of the stems, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces bright orange to red flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in tropical regions.
