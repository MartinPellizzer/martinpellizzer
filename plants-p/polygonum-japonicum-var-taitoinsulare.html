Polygonum japonicum var. taitoinsulare

Polygonum japonicum var. taitoinsulare, commonly known as the Taito Island buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, which includes species known for their distinctive bracteate inflorescences. The plant is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, pinkish-red flowers that bloom in summer. Its leaves are typically oval-shaped with a slightly serrated margin.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to well-drained soils in temperate climates.
