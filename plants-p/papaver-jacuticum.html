Papaver jacuticum

Papaver jacuticum, commonly known as the Central Asian poppy, is a herbaceous annual plant native to Central Asia.

It features striking red or orange flowers with a distinctive black center, typical of the Papaveraceae family. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves that are often covered in a powdery coating. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

The seeds of Papaver jacuticum are small and can be dispersed by wind, contributing to its natural distribution.
