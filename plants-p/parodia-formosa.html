Parodia formosa

Parodia formosa, commonly known as the "Fairy Castle Cactus," is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, spire-like stems that can grow up to 3 meters in height. The plant produces clusters of yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It is a member of the Cactaceae family and is known for its ability to thrive in arid environments.

Parodia formosa is often cultivated for its striking appearance and relatively low maintenance requirements.
