Piper insectifugum

Piper insectifugum, commonly known as the insect-repelling pepper plant, is a tropical vine native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species such as black pepper. The plant is characterized by its climbing habit, opposite leaves, and small, clustered flowers. Its most notable feature is the production of peppery, aromatic fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds.

The botanical interest in Piper insectifugum lies in its potential medicinal and insect-repelling properties, making it a subject of scientific study.
