Piper subduidaense

Piper subduidaense, commonly known as the Amazonian pepper plant, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to the rainforests of South America, particularly Brazil and Peru. The plant is characterized by its vine-like growth habit and small, heart-shaped leaves. It produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that develop into berries containing aromatic seeds.

Due to its potential medicinal properties, it has attracted interest in ethnobotanical research.
