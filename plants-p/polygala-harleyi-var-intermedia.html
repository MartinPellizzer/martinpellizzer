Polygala harleyi var. intermedia

Polygala harleyi var. intermedia, commonly known as the coastal polygala, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb with slender, branching stems that often grow along the ground or in low vegetation. The plant produces small, showy flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, arranged in clusters. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to coastal conditions with high salt exposure.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in coastal ecosystems.
