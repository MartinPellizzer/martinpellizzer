Persicaria bungeana

Persicaria bungeana, commonly known as Bunge's knotweed, is a perennial herb native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, which includes plants known for their distinctive leaf shapes and flowering structures. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture and produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. It thrives in moist environments such as wetlands and stream banks, and is often found in shaded or partially sunny habitats.

Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread vegetatively, making it both a resilient and sometimes invasive species in certain regions.
