Paspalum cujabense

Paspalum cujabense, commonly known as the Colombian paspalum, is a perennial grass native to South America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny florets. It is often found in wetland habitats and is adapted to moist, nutrient-rich soils.

Paspalum cujabense is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in aquatic environments.
