Phlomis crinita subsp. composita

Phlomis crinita subsp.

composita, commonly known as the curly hair phlomis, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its tall, branched stems and dense clusters of gray-green, fuzzy leaves.

The plant produces showy, pink to purple flower heads that bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy addition to rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its unique appearance and drought tolerance make it a popular choice for xeriscaping.
