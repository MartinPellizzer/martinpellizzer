Pyrola asarifolia subsp. americana

Pyrola asarifolia subsp.

americana, commonly known as the American roseleaf wintergreen, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to eastern North America. It features oval, dark green leaves with a waxy surface and produces small, bell-shaped white flowers in late spring.

The plant thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in shaded forest environments. It is characterized by its aromatic leaves and is part of the Ericaceae family.

This subspecies is notable for its ornamental value and ecological importance in forest ecosystems.
