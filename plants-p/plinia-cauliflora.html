Plinia cauliflora

Plinia cauliflora, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or araçá-do-brejo, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking red, fleshy fruits that resemble small cherries. The plant produces its flowers and fruits on the trunk and branches, a phenomenon known as cauliflory. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in rainforests.

Its fruits are edible and are used in various culinary applications.
