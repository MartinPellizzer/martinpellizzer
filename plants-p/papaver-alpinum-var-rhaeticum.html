Papaver alpinum var. rhaeticum

Papaver alpinum var. rhaeticum, commonly known as the Rhaetic poppy, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It features showy, bright red or orange flowers with a distinctive black center, typically blooming in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs, and it grows in rocky or gravelly soils. It is often found in high-altitude meadows and is adapted to cold, harsh environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
