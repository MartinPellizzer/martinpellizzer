Podalyria argentea

Podalyria argentea, commonly known as silverpod larkspur, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in a fine, silvery pubescence, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

The plant is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
