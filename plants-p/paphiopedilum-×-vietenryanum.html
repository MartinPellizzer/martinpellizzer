Paphiopedilum × vietenryanum

Paphiopedilum × vietenryanum, commonly known as the Vietnamese orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, spurred lip that resembles a pouch, a distinguishing feature of the Paphiopedilum genus. The plant typically produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and yellow, often with intricate patterns. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

This orchid is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated for both horticultural and commercial purposes.
