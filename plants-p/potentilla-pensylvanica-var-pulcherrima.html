Potentilla pensylvanica var. pulcherrima

Potentilla pensylvanica var. pulcherrima, commonly known as the showy cinquefoil, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its bright yellow, five-petaled flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and features compound leaves with five oval leaflets. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
