Plagiostachys crocydocalyx

Plagiostachys crocydocalyx, commonly known as the golden bamboo, is a species of grass native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and clusters of golden-yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant has narrow, elongated leaves that are typically green on top and lighter in color on the underside. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas or along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and rapid growth, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
