Pinus mayriana var. apacheca

Pinus mayriana var. apacheca, commonly known as the Apache pine, is a variant of the Mexican pine native to the mountainous regions of Mexico.

It is a coniferous evergreen tree characterized by its needle-like leaves and cone-bearing structure. The tree typically grows in high-altitude forests, preferring well-drained soils and a cool, moist climate. Its cones are woody and contain two-winged seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

This species plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
