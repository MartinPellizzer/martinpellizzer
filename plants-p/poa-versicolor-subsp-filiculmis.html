Poa versicolor subsp. filiculmis

Poa versicolor subsp.

filiculmis, commonly known as the silver hair grass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Europe and Asia. It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and distinctive silver-gray inflorescence.

The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils, often found in meadows and along stream banks. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, contributing to its unique appearance.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological role in stabilizing soil in wetland habitats.
