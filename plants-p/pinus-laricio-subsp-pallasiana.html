Pinus laricio subsp. pallasiana

Pinus laricio subsp.

pallasiana, commonly known as the Pallas's stone pine, is a subspecies of the stone pine native to the mountains of the Caucasus and parts of Turkey. It is a coniferous evergreen tree characterized by its slender, pyramidal shape and thick, scaly bark.

The needles are long, dark green, and arranged in bundles of five, while the cones are woody and typically pendulous. This species thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments.

It plays an important role in mountain ecosystems and is valued for its ornamental and ecological significance.
