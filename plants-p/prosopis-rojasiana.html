Prosopis rojasiana

Prosopis rojasiana, commonly known as the Argentine mesquite, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to South America, particularly Argentina and Chile.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, with deep roots that allow it to access groundwater.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
