Podranea

Podranea, commonly known as the silver vine, is a genus of flowering plants native to South America.

It consists of several species, with Podranea rhamnoides being the most well-known. The plant is characterized by its trailing growth habit and striking silver-green foliage. Its flowers are small, clustered, and typically white or pink in color.

Podranea is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its attractive appearance and adaptability to various growing conditions.
