Paraphlomis albida var. albida

Paraphlomis albida var. albida, commonly known as the white-flowered blueweed, is a perennial herb native to parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Lamiaceae, which includes many aromatic plants. The plant features slender, erect stems and small, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. Its flowers are typically white or pale blue, arranged in whorls along the stem.

It is often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.
