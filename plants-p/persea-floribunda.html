Persea floribunda

Persea floribunda, commonly known as the Mexican persimmon, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central America and parts of the Caribbean.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and fragrant, white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, orange to reddish fruits that are edible when fully ripe, though they can be astringent when unripe. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value as well as its fruit.

The tree is also valued for its aromatic wood and historical use in traditional medicine.
