Phyllostegia haliakalae

Phyllostegia haliakalae, commonly known as the Haleakalā phyllostegia, is a rare endemic plant found exclusively on the slopes of Haleakalā on the island of Maui in Hawaii.

It is a member of the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is characterized by its distinctive, opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, which bloom during the wet season.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered critically endangered and is protected under Hawaiian conservation laws.
