Pachira sessilis

Pachira sessilis, commonly known as the dwarf pachira or Brazilian money plant, is a species of flowering plant in the family Malvaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and large, heart-shaped leaves. Pachira sessilis thrives in warm, humid environments and prefers well-draining soil.
