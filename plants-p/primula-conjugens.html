Primula conjugens

Primula conjugens, commonly known as the Chilean primrose, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Chile and Argentina.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in a fine, hairy texture.

Primula conjugens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate, early spring blooms.
