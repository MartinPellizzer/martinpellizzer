Pogonophora africana

Pogonophora africana, commonly known as the African beard lichen, is a member of the family Pogonophoraceae.

It is a foliose lichen, characterized by its leaf-like thallus structure and a distinct, hairy margin. The plant typically grows on tree trunks and branches in tropical and subtropical regions. Its color ranges from yellowish-green to reddish-brown, depending on environmental conditions.

Pogonophora africana plays a role in nutrient cycling and provides habitat for various microorganisms.
