Pyrus soulieana

Pyrus soulieana, commonly known as the Chinese white pear, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and oval-shaped leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, white flowers in early spring, followed by edible, sweet fruit that ripens in late summer. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional Chinese medicine.

Pyrus soulieana prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.
