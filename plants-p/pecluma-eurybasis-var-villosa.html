Pecluma eurybasis var. villosa

Pecluma eurybasis var. villosa, commonly known as the hairy pecluma, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it its varietal name "villosa.". The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in grassland habitats. Its flowers are small and clustered, producing a distinctive yellowish-green color.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
