Pterocarpus monophyllus

Pterocarpus monophyllus, commonly known as the African rosewood, is a deciduous tree native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its dark, dense wood with a distinctive reddish-brown color. The plant has compound leaves with a single terminal leaflet, and its flowers are typically yellow or orange in color. It produces legumes that contain the characteristic Pterocarpus fruit, which is a type of pod.

This species is valued for its timber and is also used in traditional medicine.
