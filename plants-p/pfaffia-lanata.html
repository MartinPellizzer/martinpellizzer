Pfaffia lanata

Pfaffia lanata, commonly known as Brazilian ginseng, is a perennial herb native to the Atlantic rainforests of Brazil.

It is characterized by its hairy, lance-shaped leaves and yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, red berries that contain the seeds. It is valued for its potential medicinal properties, including antioxidant and anti-inflammatory effects.

Pfaffia lanata is often cultivated for its roots, which are used in traditional medicine and herbal supplements.
