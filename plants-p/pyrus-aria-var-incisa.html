Pyrus aria var. incisa

Pyrus aria var. incisa, commonly known as the narrow-leaf pear, is a variant of the pear tree native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its narrow, elongated leaves that are often serrated along the edges. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by edible fruits that are typically small and acidic. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forested areas.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
