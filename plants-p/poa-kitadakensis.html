Poa kitadakensis

Poa kitadakensis, commonly known as the Kitadaken grass, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Japan.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and fine, blue-green foliage. The plant typically thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine and subalpine zones. Its inflorescence consists of a dense, spike-like cluster of small, two-rowed spikelets.

Poa kitadakensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in stabilizing soil in high-altitude ecosystems.
