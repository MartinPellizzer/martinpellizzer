Psacalium cervinum

Psacalium cervinum, commonly known as the red sandalwood, is a deciduous tree native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its dark reddish-brown heartwood, which is highly valued for its durability and fine grain. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are typically elliptical or ovate in shape. It produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by capsule fruits containing several seeds.

Due to overharvesting, it is now considered a threatened species in many parts of its native range.
