Plantago griffithii

Plantago griffithii, commonly known as the Griffith's plantain, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its rosette of broad, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in a spike, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats.

Due to its adaptability and medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated for traditional herbal use.
