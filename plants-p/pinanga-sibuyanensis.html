Pinanga sibuyanensis

Pinanga sibuyanensis, commonly known as the Sibuyan bamboo, is a unique plant native to the Philippines, specifically found on the island of Sibuyan.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and dense foliage. The plant grows in moist, shaded environments, often in mountainous regions with high humidity. Its leaves are long and narrow, contributing to its ornamental value, while its structure supports local biodiversity.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
