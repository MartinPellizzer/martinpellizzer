Polygonum mucronulatum var. albiflorum

Polygonum mucronulatum var. albiflorum, commonly known as the whiteflower smartweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, characterized by its distinctive arrow-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in moist environments such as wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks. Its flowers bloom in late summer, and the plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in disturbed soils.

It is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its tolerance of wet conditions and ability to stabilize soil.
