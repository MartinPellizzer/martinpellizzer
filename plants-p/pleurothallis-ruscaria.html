Pleurothallis ruscaria

Pleurothallis ruscaria, commonly known as the Crimson Pterostigma, is a species of orchid native to the cloud forests of Ecuador.

It is a small, terrestrial orchid with slender, dark green leaves and produces delicate, pinkish-white flowers with a distinctive red stripe. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot. Its flowers are typically short-lived but attract various pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds.

Pleurothallis ruscaria is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
