Pastinaca brevivittata

Pastinaca brevivittata, commonly known as the narrow-banded burdock, is a perennial herb native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features sturdy, hairy stems and large, heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive silvery-white vein pattern. Its flowers are typically purple or pink, and it produces bur-like fruits that aid in seed dispersal.

Pastinaca brevivittata is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates.
