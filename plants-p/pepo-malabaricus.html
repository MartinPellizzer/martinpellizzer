Pepo malabaricus

Pepo malabaricus, commonly known as the Malabar pumpkin, is a species native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the Cucurbitaceae family, which includes other gourds and cucurbits. The plant produces large, spherical fruits with a thick, waxy skin that can vary in color from yellow to orange. The leaves are broad and lobed, typical of many members of its family.

It is often cultivated for its edible fruits, which have a sweet and nutty flavor.
