Psittacanthus dichrous

Psittacanthus dichrous, commonly known as the red-leaved hibiscus, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, dark red to purple foliage that contrasts with its showy, five-petaled flowers. The plant produces yellowish-orange flowers that bloom in clusters, often attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically elliptical and have a waxy texture, while its stems are sturdy and branching.

Psittacanthus dichrous is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its vibrant foliage and aesthetic appeal.
