Papaver armeniacum subsp. armeniacum

Papaver armeniacum subsp.

armeniacum, commonly known as Armenian poppy, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the Caucasus region. It features showy, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from orange to red, often with a contrasting black center.

The plant has a robust root system and produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in a fine, silky hair.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats.
