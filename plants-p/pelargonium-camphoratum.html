Pelargonium camphoratum

Pelargonium camphoratum, commonly known as the camphor geranium, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its succulent, hairy leaves and produces small, pale pink to white flowers. The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to dry, rocky environments. It has a strong, aromatic scent, which is derived from the essential oils present in its foliage.

Pelargonium camphoratum is a perennial herb that thrives in full sun and well-drained soil.
