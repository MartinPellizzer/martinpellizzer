Pentapanax fragrans var. longipedunculatus

Pentapanax fragrans var. longipedunculatus, commonly known as the fragrant pentapanax, is a climbing shrub native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with elongated leaflets and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible and often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is valued for its ornamental and medicinal properties.

Its long peduncles distinguish it from other varieties within the Pentapanax genus.
