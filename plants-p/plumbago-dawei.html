Plumbago dawei

Plumbago dawei, commonly known as blue plumbago, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue or purple flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are opposite on the stems, and it thrives in full sun to partial shade. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its vibrant color and relatively low maintenance requirements.

Plumbago dawei is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
