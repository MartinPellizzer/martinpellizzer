Potamogeton gayi

Potamogeton gayi, commonly known as the narrow-leaved pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to parts of Australia.

It has long, narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically arranged in a spiral around the stem. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often submerged, followed by small, oval-shaped fruits. It thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, such as lakes and ponds.

Potamogeton gayi plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
