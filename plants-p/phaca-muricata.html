Phaca muricata

Phaca muricata, commonly known as the roughleaf phaca, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its hairy, rough-textured leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are typically clustered in dense heads. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Phaca muricata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
