Prunus jamasakura f. kongo

Prunus jamasakura f. kongo, commonly known as the Kongo cherry blossom, is a rare and ornamental variation of the Japanese cherry blossom.

It is characterized by its distinctive, double-petaled flowers that bloom in early spring, adding a unique visual appeal to gardens and landscapes. The plant is native to Japan and is often cultivated for its aesthetic value rather than fruit production. It thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil and partial sunlight.

Its blooming period is a symbol of the fleeting beauty of nature, making it a cherished ornamental species.
