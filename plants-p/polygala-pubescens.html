Polygala pubescens

Polygala pubescens, commonly known as the hairy polygala, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, showy flowers. The plant typically grows in open, sandy or rocky habitats and is often found in grasslands and shrublands. Its flowers are usually pale green to yellowish in color, with a distinctive spurred structure at the base of the petals.

Polygala pubescens is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral appearance.
