Premna inaequilateralis

Premna inaequilateralis, commonly known as the unequal-leaf premna, is a species of flowering plant in the family Lamiaceae.

It is native to parts of Southeast Asia, including countries like Thailand, Malaysia, and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are often asymmetrical, giving it its common name. It produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale purple, followed by small, fleshy fruits.

This species is often found in forested areas and is valued for its ornamental qualities and traditional uses in local medicine.
