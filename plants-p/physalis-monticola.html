Physalis monticola

Physalis monticola, commonly known as the mountain groundcherry, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes tomatoes and potatoes, and is characterized by its hairy stems and yellow flowers. The plant produces small, orange-yellow berries enclosed in a papery calyx, which ripen in the autumn. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in rocky or disturbed soils.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
