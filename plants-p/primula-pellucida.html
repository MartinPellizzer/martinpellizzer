Primula pellucida

Primula pellucida, commonly known as the glassy primrose, is a species of flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, often with a yellow throat. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are hairy and oval-shaped, and it produces a delicate, fragrant bloom in early spring.

Primula pellucida is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
