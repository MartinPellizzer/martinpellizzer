Protea pityphylla

Protea pityphylla, commonly known as the silver protea, is a striking South African plant belonging to the Proteaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescence composed of numerous small, white to pale pink flowers arranged in a spherical shape. The plant is native to the Western and Eastern Cape provinces of South Africa, where it thrives in nutrient-poor, sandy soils. Its leaves are narrow and leathery, often covered in a silvery-white coating that reflects sunlight.

Protea pityphylla is well adapted to the Mediterranean climate of its native region, with a dormant period during the summer months.
