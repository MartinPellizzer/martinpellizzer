Prostanthera scutellarioides

Prostanthera scutellarioides, commonly known as the narrow-leaf mintbush, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants. The plant is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white or pale blue flowers. It is often found in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Prostanthera scutellarioides is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional Aboriginal medicine.
