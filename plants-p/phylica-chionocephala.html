Phylica chionocephala

Phylica chionocephala, commonly known as the white-haired phylica, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, silver-white foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in rounded heads, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and inland regions.

Phylica chionocephala is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
