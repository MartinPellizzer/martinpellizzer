Phycella attenuata

Phycella attenuata, commonly known as the slender water plant, is a species of aquatic plant characterized by its delicate, ribbon-like leaves.

It typically grows in shallow, freshwater environments such as ponds and slow-moving streams. The plant has a simple morphology with a stem that branches at the base, supporting its linear foliage. Its reproductive structures are small and inconspicuous, often relying on water currents for seed dispersal.

Phycella attenuata plays a role in maintaining water quality by absorbing nutrients and providing habitat for aquatic organisms.
