Polyachyrus roseus

Polyachyrus roseus, commonly known as the roseum orchid, is a flowering plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia and is characterized by its delicate, fragrant blooms. The plant features lance-shaped leaves and produces showy, pinkish-white flowers with a subtle rose hue. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
