Plectronia macrophylla

Plectronia macrophylla, commonly known as the large-leaved plectronia, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers that bloom in late spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
