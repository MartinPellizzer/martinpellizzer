Primula roylei subsp. calderiana

Primula roylei subsp.

calderiana, commonly known as the Kashmir primrose, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Himalayan regions of India and Pakistan. It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer.

The plant has hairy, basal leaves that are typically oval to heart-shaped, and it thrives in cool, moist environments. It is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes, where it contributes to the biodiversity of its habitat.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation efforts.
