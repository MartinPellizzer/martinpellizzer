Prunus ceraseidos var. kurilensis

Prunus ceraseidos var. kurilensis, commonly known as the Kuril cherry, is a small deciduous tree or shrub native to the Kuril Islands.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its delicate pink to white flowers that bloom in early spring. The leaves are ovate with serrated edges, and the fruit is a small, sweet cherry-like drupe. This plant thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil and partial to full sun exposure.

It is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its spring blossoms.
