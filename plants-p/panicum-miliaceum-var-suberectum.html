Panicum miliaceum var. suberectum

Panicum miliaceum var. suberectum, commonly known as proso millet, is an annual grass species native to temperate regions of Asia and Europe.

It features erect, branching stems that are often hairy, with narrow, linear leaves that are typically bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny, two-lobed spikelets. It is adapted to thrive in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it a hardy crop for marginal lands.

This variety is valued for its nutritional content and use in both human and animal diets.
