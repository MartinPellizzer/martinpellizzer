Pandanus urophyllus

Pandanus urophyllus, commonly known as the giant pandanus, is a tropical plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its large, sword-shaped leaves that can grow up to several meters in length. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, yellowish flowers that are often used in traditional crafts and ceremonies. Its roots are fibrous and can be used for making ropes and mats.

The plant thrives in coastal areas and is well adapted to saline and humid environments.
