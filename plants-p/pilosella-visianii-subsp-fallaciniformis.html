Pilosella visianii subsp. fallaciniformis

Pilosella visianii subsp.

fallaciniformis, commonly known as the Visianii Hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region. It features a low-growing rosette of basal leaves that are covered in dense, white, woolly hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
