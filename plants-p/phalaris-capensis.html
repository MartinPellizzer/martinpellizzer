Phalaris capensis

Phalaris capensis, commonly known as common arrowgrass, is a perennial grass native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny blue or purple flowers. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in grasslands and disturbed areas.

Phalaris capensis is adapted to coastal and saline environments, making it a hardy and resilient species.
