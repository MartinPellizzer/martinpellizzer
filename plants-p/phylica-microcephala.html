Phylica microcephala

Phylica microcephala, commonly known as the silver leaf tree, is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, silvery-gray foliage and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has a compact growth habit with multiple stems and is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for coastal and dry garden environments.

The leaves are also used in traditional medicine for their astringent properties.
