Plantago udicola

Plantago udicola, commonly known as narrowleaf plantain, is a perennial herb native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in a spike-like inflorescence. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and disturbed areas.

Botanically, it belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is known for its medicinal properties.
