Plantago canescens subsp. tolmatschevii

Plantago canescens subsp.

tolmatschevii, commonly known as Tolmatschev's plantain, is a perennial herb native to Central Asia. It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves covered in dense, white pubescence.

The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in a spike, which are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in dry, sandy or rocky soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid regions.

This subspecies is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
