Papaver cinerascens

Papaver cinerascens, commonly known as the cinerascens poppy, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It features striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in a fine, powdery coating. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Its botanical characteristics make it a notable species in botanical gardens and wildflower collections.
