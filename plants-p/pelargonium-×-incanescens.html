Pelargonium × incanescens

Pelargonium × incanescens, commonly known as the silver-leaved geranium, is a hybrid plant in the Geraniaceae family.

It is characterized by its striking silvery-white to pale green foliage, which gives it a unique appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink, red, or white, typically during the spring and summer months. Native to South Africa, it thrives in warm, sunny conditions and well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
