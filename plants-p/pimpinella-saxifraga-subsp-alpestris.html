Pimpinella saxifraga subsp. alpestris

Pimpinella saxifraga subsp.

alpestris, commonly known as alpine cow-weed, is a perennial herb native to alpine regions of Europe. It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes, where it tolerates cold and harsh environmental conditions.

The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges and small, yellowish-white flowers that appear in umbels. It is often found in mountainous areas of the Alps and the Carpathians.

Its botanical characteristics make it well-adapted to its extreme habitat, with a compact growth form and a deep root system.
