Phyllostegia brighamii

Phyllostegia brighamii, commonly known as Brigham's mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, white to pale blue flowers that are clustered in whorls along the stems. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Brigham's mistflower thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.
