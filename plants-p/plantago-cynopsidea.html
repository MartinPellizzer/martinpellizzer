Plantago cynopsidea

Plantago cynopsidea, commonly known as the narrowleaf plantain, is a perennial herb native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It features upright, somewhat hairy stems that bear broad, lance-shaped leaves with a waxy surface. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in a dense spike, which are typically wind-pollinated. It is often found in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and fields, and is known for its medicinal properties.

The leaves are commonly used in traditional herbal remedies for their soothing effects on the skin and respiratory system.
