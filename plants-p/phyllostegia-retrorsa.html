Phyllostegia retrorsa

Phyllostegia retrorsa, commonly known as the backward phyllostegia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves that are often hairy and have a distinctive serrated edge. Its flowers are typically pale purple to white, arranged in whorled clusters at the ends of the stems.

Phyllostegia retrorsa is often found in moist, woodland habitats and is known for its ornamental value in native gardens.
