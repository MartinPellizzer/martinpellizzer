Prunus nipponica f. rotundifolia

Prunus nipponica f. rotundifolia, commonly known as the Japanese flowering cherry with rounded leaves, is a unique cultivar of the Prunus genus.

It is characterized by its broad, rounded leaves that differ from the typical ovate leaves of the species. This variety produces clusters of delicate pink flowers in early spring, adding ornamental value to gardens. The plant is native to Japan and is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal in horticulture.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial shade, making it suitable for temperate climates.
