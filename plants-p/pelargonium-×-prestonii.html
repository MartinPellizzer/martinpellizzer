Pelargonium × prestonii

Pelargonium × prestonii, commonly known as the African hybrid geranium, is a hybrid species resulting from crosses between various geranium species native to Africa.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, five-petaled flowers that come in a range of colors including red, pink, and white. The leaves are typically rounded and have a slightly hairy texture, adding to its ornamental appeal. This plant thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens and containers.

It is valued for its vibrant blooms and adaptability to a variety of garden conditions.
