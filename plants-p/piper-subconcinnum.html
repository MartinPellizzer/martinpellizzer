Piper subconcinnum

Piper subconcinnum, commonly known as the dwarf pepper plant, is a flowering vine native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into small, edible berries. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The plant is characterized by its climbing habit and aromatic foliage.
