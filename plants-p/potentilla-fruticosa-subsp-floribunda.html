Potentilla fruticosa subsp. floribunda

Potentilla fruticosa subsp.

floribunda, commonly known as the flowering cinquefoil, is a shrub native to the northern hemisphere. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and dense, many-branched structure.

The plant produces clusters of bright yellow flowers in early summer, which are borne on long, slender pedicels. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with five to seven leaflets that are oval and slightly serrated.

This species is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to harsh environmental conditions.
