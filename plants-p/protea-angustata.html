Protea angustata

Protea angustata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sugar bush, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Proteaceae family, which is known for its showy, often brightly colored flowers. The plant features narrow, elongated leaves and produces inflorescences with numerous small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or white. It thrives in sandy or acidic soils and is often found in fynbos vegetation.

Protea angustata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
