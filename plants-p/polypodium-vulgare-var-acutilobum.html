Polypodium vulgare var. acutilobum

Polypodium vulgare var. acutilobum, commonly known as the narrow-lobed rock fern, is a perennial fern native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It features a creeping rhizome that produces a dense mat of fronds, which are typically leathery and have a distinctive, narrow, lance-shaped lobes. The fronds are evergreen, with a dark green upper surface and a lighter, often hairy underside. It thrives in shaded, moist environments such as woodlands and rocky crevices.

The plant is characterized by its spore-bearing sorus located on the underside of the fronds, which are typically arranged in a single row.
