Pleurothallis decurrens

Pleurothallis decurrens, commonly known as the spreading pleurothallis, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in the understory of forests. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and arranged in a rosette pattern, aiding in water retention.

This orchid is part of the large and diverse Pleurothallis genus, which includes over 1,000 species.
