Prunus × takasawana

Prunus × takasawana, commonly known as the Takasawana cherry, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Prunus serrulata and Prunus lusitanica.

It is characterized by its distinctive, double-flowered blooms that appear in shades of pink and white, making it a popular ornamental plant. The plant typically grows as a small tree or shrub, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are elliptical with a serrated margin and a dark green upper surface.

The fruit is a small, sweet drupe that ripens in late summer, adding to its ornamental value.
