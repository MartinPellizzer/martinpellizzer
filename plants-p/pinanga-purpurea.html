Pinanga purpurea

Pinanga purpurea, commonly known as the purple-petioled bamboo, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its slender, hollow stems that grow in dense clumps. The plant features long, narrow leaves with a distinctive purple hue on the petioles, giving it its common name. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Pinanga purpurea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its unique foliage.
