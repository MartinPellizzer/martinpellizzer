Plumeria floribunda var. crassipes

Plumeria floribunda var. crassipes, commonly known as the frangipani, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, waxy leaves and clusters of fragrant, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and yellow. The plant produces large, fleshy, spiny fruit capsules that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The flowers are typically used in traditional ceremonies and have cultural significance in various regions.
