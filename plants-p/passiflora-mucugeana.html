Passiflora mucugeana

Passiflora mucugeana, commonly known as the Brazilian passionflower, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, complex flowers that feature a blend of blue, purple, and white petals, along with a crown-like structure of filaments. The plant produces edible, tropical berries that are rich in antioxidants and have a sweet, tangy flavor. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a velvety texture, while its stems are woody and covered in fine hairs.

Passiflora mucugeana is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its unique floral display and ecological importance as a food source for native pollinators.
