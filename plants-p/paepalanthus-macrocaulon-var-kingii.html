Paepalanthus macrocaulon var. kingii

Paepalanthus macrocaulon var. kingii, commonly known as the King's pincushion, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Argentina.

The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Due to its unique appearance and ornamental value, it is often cultivated in horticultural settings.
