Piper sieberi

Piper sieberi, commonly known as the Java pepper or long pepper, is a tropical climbing shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its slender, twining stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, clustered berries that turn from green to red as they mature. Its fruit is used in traditional medicine and as a spice, similar to black pepper.

The botanical features of Piper sieberi make it an important species in both ecological and economic contexts.
