Psidium psychrophyllum

Psidium psychrophyllum, commonly known as the highland guava, is a small evergreen tree native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It typically grows at high altitudes, preferring cool and humid climates. The plant has thick, leathery leaves that are dark green on top and pale underneath. Its small, white flowers are fragrant and attract pollinators such as bees and birds.

The fruit is a small, yellow or red berry that is edible and has a tart, aromatic flavor.
