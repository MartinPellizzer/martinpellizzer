Polygonum stagninum

Polygonum stagninum, commonly known as water plantain, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments.

It features basal leaves that are broad, heart-shaped, and often have a waxy coating to reduce water loss. The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in spike-like clusters, which are typically pollinated by wind. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense stands in shallow water or moist soil.

Polygonum stagninum plays an important ecological role in stabilizing shorelines and providing habitat for aquatic invertebrates.
