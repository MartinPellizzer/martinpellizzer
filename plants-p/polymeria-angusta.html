Polymeria angusta

Polymeria angusta, commonly known as the narrowleaf polymelia, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant features narrow, linear leaves and produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. It is often found in rocky soils and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions.

Polymeria angusta is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
