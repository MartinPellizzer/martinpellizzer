Plantago paralias subsp. petiolata

Plantago paralias subsp.

petiolata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf plantain, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia. It features upright, branched stems that bear lance-shaped leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

The plant produces small, greenish flowers in a spike-like inflorescence, which are wind-pollinated. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their soothing properties.

It thrives in disturbed soils and is commonly found along roadsides and in grassy areas.
