Petrophile divaricata

Petrophile divaricata, commonly known as the variable bottlebrush, is a flowering plant native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its distinctive cylindrical spikes of red or pink flowers that appear in spring. The plant has a woody stem with a rough, fissured bark, and its leaves are small, narrow, and often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open forests or heathlands.

Petrophile divaricata is an important component of the local flora and is valued for its ornamental appeal.
