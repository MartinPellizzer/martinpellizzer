Pilocereus albispinus

Pilocereus albispinus, commonly known as the white-spined night bloomer, is a species of cactus native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems that are covered in white spines, giving it its common name. The plant produces large, fragrant white flowers that open at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its ornamental value and unique blooming habits, it is often cultivated in succulent gardens.
