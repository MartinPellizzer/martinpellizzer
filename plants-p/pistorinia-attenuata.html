Pistorinia attenuata

Pistorinia attenuata, commonly known as the slender pistorinia, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and thrives in full sun exposure. Its foliage consists of narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white wax.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists alike.
