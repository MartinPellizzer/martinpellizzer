Potentilla hirta var. parviflora

Potentilla hirta var. parviflora, commonly known as the narrow-leaved cinquefoil, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, typically in clusters of five, giving it the common name "cinquefoil.". The plant has hairy stems and leaves that are narrow and often covered with fine, soft hairs. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments.

Due to its hardy nature, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to stabilize soil in harsh conditions.
