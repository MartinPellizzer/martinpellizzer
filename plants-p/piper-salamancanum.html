Piper salamancanum

Piper salamancanum, commonly known as the Salamanca pepper or wild pepper, is a tropical vine native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes over 3,000 species of flowering plants. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves and small, clustered flowers that develop into peppery fruits. Its fruits are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties.

The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in the undergrowth of tropical rainforests.
