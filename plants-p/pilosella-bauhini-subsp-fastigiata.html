Pilosella bauhini subsp. fastigiata

Pilosella bauhini subsp.

fastigiata, commonly known as the upright pilosella, is a flowering plant native to Central and Southern Europe. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads.

The plant is a perennial herb with slender, upright stems and is often found in dry meadows and rocky areas. Its leaves are typically linear and hairy, while the flowers are small, yellowish, and clustered in heads.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to pollinators.
