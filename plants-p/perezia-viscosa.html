Perezia viscosa

Perezia viscosa, commonly known as the sticky perezia, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its glandular leaves that secrete a viscous, sticky substance, which gives the plant its common name. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or dry habitats.

Perezia viscosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been cultivated for its unique foliage and floral display.
