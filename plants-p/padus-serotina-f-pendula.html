Padus serotina f. pendula

Padus serotina f. pendula, commonly known as the weeping white crabsapple.

This cultivar is a variant of the white crabsapple, characterized by its cascading branches that bear small, white flowers in spring. The plant has oval, serrated leaves that turn yellow in autumn. It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that thrives in full sun to partial shade.

Its ornamental value comes from its delicate, drooping branches and seasonal floral display.
