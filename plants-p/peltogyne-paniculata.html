Peltogyne paniculata

Peltogyne paniculata, commonly known as the African shea tree, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and compound leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers followed by pods containing edible seeds used for oil extraction.

It plays a significant role in local ecosystems and is valued for its economic and nutritional contributions.
