Passiflora lobbii subsp. obtusiloba

Passiflora lobbii subsp.

obtusiloba, commonly known as the African passionflower, is a climbing vine native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that feature a unique blend of colors, including red, yellow, and blue.

The plant has palmately lobed leaves and produces edible, orange-yellow berries that are rich in nutrients. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
