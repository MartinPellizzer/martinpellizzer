Poupartia orientalis

Poupartia orientalis, commonly known as the Persian bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces striking blue or violet bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters and thrives in well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture.

Poupartia orientalis is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to bloom in full sun.
