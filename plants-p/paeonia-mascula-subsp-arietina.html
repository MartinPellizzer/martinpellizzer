Paeonia mascula subsp. arietina

Paeonia mascula subsp.

arietina, commonly known as the Armenian peony, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus and Central Asia. It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from white to deep red, often with a distinctive saffron-yellow anther.

The plant produces large, palmately divided leaves that are typically gray-green in color. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is complemented by its historical use in traditional medicine.
