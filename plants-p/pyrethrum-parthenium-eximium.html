Pyrethrum parthenium eximium

Pyrethrum parthenium eximium, commonly known as feverfew, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its daisy-like flowers with white petals and a yellow center, and its feathery leaves. The plant contains compounds called pyrethrins, which are natural insecticides. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often found in temperate regions.

Feverfew is also known for its medicinal uses, particularly in treating migraines and inflammation.
