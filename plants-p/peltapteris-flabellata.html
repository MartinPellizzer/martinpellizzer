Peltapteris flabellata

Peltapteris flabellata, commonly known as the fan-leafed fern, is a prehistoric plant belonging to the order Marattiopsida.

It is characterized by its large, fan-shaped fronds that were likely used for photosynthesis in swampy environments. The plant's structure includes a short, thick rhizome with a complex system of roots and vascular tissue. Its morphology suggests it was a significant component of ancient tropical ecosystems.
