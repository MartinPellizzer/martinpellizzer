Polystachya ashantensis

Polystachya ashantensis, commonly known as the Ashanti bamboo orchid, is a member of the orchid family (Orchidaceae) and is native to West Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, bamboo-like stems and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest understories, and prefers well-drained soil. Its leaves are elongated and grass-like, contributing to its overall bamboo-like appearance.

Polystachya ashantensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique foliage and flowering habits.
