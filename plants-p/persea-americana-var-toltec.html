Persea americana var. toltec

Persea americana var. toltec, commonly known as the Mexican avocado, is a variety of the avocado tree native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its dark, glossy leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. The fruit is typically smaller than the common Hass avocado, with a smoother skin and a creamy, nutty flavor. This variety thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial shade.

It is valued for its unique taste and is often used in traditional Mexican cuisine.
