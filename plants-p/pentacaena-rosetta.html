Pentacaena rosetta

Pentacaena rosetta, commonly known as the Rosetta daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, daisy-like flowers in shades of yellow and white. The plant typically grows in well-drained soils and thrives in full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers. Its foliage is often used in traditional medicine, and it is valued for its ornamental appeal.

The species is relatively drought-tolerant once established, making it a low-maintenance choice for gardeners.
