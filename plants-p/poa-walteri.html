Poa walteri

Poa walteri, commonly known as Walter's bluegrass, is a perennial grass native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges. The plant has slender, tufted culms with fine, blue-green leaves that are often hairy along the edges. Its inflorescence consists of dense, spike-like clusters of small, two-lobed flowers.

Poa walteri is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in wetland environments.
