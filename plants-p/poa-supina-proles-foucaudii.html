Poa supina proles foucaudii

Poa supina proles foucaudii, commonly known as the Foucaud's bentgrass, is a low-growing perennial grass species native to certain regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its prostrate growth habit, with slender, hairless stems that lie close to the ground. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Its leaves are typically narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue.

This species is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.
