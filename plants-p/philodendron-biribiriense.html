Philodendron biribiriense

Philodendron biribiriense, commonly known as the Biribiri Philodendron, is a tropical climbing plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped, glossy, deep green leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are followed by bright yellow berries, which are edible but not commonly consumed by humans. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires indirect light for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in shaded garden settings.
