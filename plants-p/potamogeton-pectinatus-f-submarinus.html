Potamogeton pectinatus f. submarinus

Potamogeton pectinatus f. submarinus, commonly known as the submerged pondweed, is a aquatic plant characterized by its elongated, ribbon-like leaves that are typically submerged in water.

It belongs to the family Potamogetonaceae and is adapted to grow in freshwater environments such as lakes, ponds, and slow-moving rivers. The plant's leaves are usually narrow and have a smooth surface, often with a dark green coloration. It reproduces through both sexual and asexual means, with the ability to form dense underwater stands.

This species plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
