Physalis virginiana f. macrophysa

Physalis virginiana f. macrophysa, commonly known as the large-fruited husk vine, is a variant of the common groundcherry.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant native to eastern North America, characterized by its hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of yellow flowers that develop into inflated, orange-red capsules containing numerous small, yellowish fruits. The fruits are often consumed raw or cooked and are valued for their tart flavor and nutritional content.

This cultivar is often grown in gardens for its ornamental value and edible fruits.
