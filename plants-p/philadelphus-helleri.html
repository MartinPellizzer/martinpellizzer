Philadelphus helleri

Philadelphus helleri, commonly known as the Heller's mock orange, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features showy, fragrant white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges and a woody stem that can reach up to 6 feet in height. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Philadelphus helleri is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
