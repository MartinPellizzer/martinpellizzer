Prisciana

Prisciana, commonly known as the sacred lotus, is a striking aquatic plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that bloom in a sequence from the center outward, often in shades of pink, white, and yellow. The plant has broad, heart-shaped leaves that float on the water's surface, while its roots anchor it in muddy substrates. Prisciana is notable for its ability to thrive in still water, making it a common feature in ponds and lakes.

Its botanical significance is further enhanced by its cultural and spiritual symbolism in various ancient civilizations.
