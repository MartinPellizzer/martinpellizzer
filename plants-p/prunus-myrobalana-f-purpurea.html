Prunus myrobalana f. purpurea

Prunus myrobalana f. purpurea, commonly known as the purple persimmon, is a cultivar of the persimmon tree native to China.

It is characterized by its distinctive deep purple to almost black fruits, which are typically harvested when fully ripe. The plant produces small, oval leaves that are dark green with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

The fruit is often used in both culinary and medicinal applications due to its rich nutritional profile.
