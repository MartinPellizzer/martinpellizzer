Potamogeton × fluitans f. congestus

Potamogeton × fluitans f. congestus, commonly known as the clustered pondweed, is a hybrid aquatic plant native to temperate regions of Europe and North America.

It is characterized by its dense, clustered growth habit and long, flexible stems that often form dense underwater mats. The plant produces small, green to yellowish flowers that are typically arranged in clusters along the stems. Its leaves are narrow, ribbon-like, and often have a slightly serrated edge.

This species plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various water organisms.
