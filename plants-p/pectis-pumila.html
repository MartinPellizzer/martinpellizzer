Pectis pumila

Pectis pumila, commonly known as the dwarf pectis, is a small, herbaceous plant native to the southern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in open, sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are linear and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are small and yellow, arranged in a compact cluster.

Pectis pumila is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and prairies.
