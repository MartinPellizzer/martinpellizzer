Potentilla vesca

Potentilla vesca, commonly known as common cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North America.

It is a low-growing perennial shrub that typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features small, five-petaled yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. Its leaves are palmately divided into five oval leaflets, and the stems are often covered in fine hairs.

Potentilla vesca is adapted to thrive in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and stream banks.
