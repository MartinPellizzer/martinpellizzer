Passerina canescens

Passerina canescens, commonly known as the gray pasqueflower, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its showy, early spring blooms that range from white to pale yellow. The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine habitats, preferring well-drained, rocky soils. Its leaves are deeply lobed and covered in a fine, grayish pubescence, which helps reduce water loss in harsh environments.

Passerina canescens is known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens that mimic its natural habitat.
