Pilea impressa var. barbata

Pilea impressa var. barbata, commonly known as the Chinese money plant, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is native to China and is characterized by its rosette of rounded, fleshy leaves that resemble small coins. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in the spring. It is a popular indoor plant due to its attractive appearance and ease of care.

The varietal form barbata is distinguished by its slightly hairy leaves and a more compact growth habit compared to the standard Pilea impressa.
