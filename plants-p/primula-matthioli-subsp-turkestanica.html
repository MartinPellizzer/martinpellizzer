Primula matthioli subsp. turkestanica

Primula matthioli subsp.

turkestanica, commonly known as the Turkestan primrose, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Asia. It features rosettes of ovate, hairy leaves that are typically dark green in color.

The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped, showy flowers in shades of pink, white, and purple, which bloom in early spring. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate blooms.
