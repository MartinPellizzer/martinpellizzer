Phellodendron chinense var. falcatum

Phellodendron chinense var. falcatum, commonly known as Chinese winged bean, is a tropical vine native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its palmately lobed leaves and long, slender pods that contain edible seeds. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its nutritional value. Its botanical features include compound leaves and a climbing habit, making it suitable for trellises or supports.

The seeds are rich in protein and are used in traditional cuisines across its native range.
