Paepalanthus polytrichoides f. villosus

Paepalanthus polytrichoides f. villosus, commonly known as the hairy pincushion cactus, is a succulent plant native to South America, particularly Argentina.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its dense, spiny clusters of stems that resemble a pincushion. The plant has thick, fleshy stems covered in fine, white hairs, which help reduce water loss in arid environments. It produces yellow flowers during the summer months, adding a striking visual contrast to its spiny structure.

This variety is well adapted to dry, rocky habitats and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
