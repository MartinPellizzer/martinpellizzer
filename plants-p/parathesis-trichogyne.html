Parathesis trichogyne

Parathesis trichogyne, commonly known as the hairy female organ of certain fungi, is a specialized structure involved in sexual reproduction.

It is a slender, elongated cell that extends from the base of the fungal fruiting body. This structure plays a crucial role in the recognition and fusion of male and female gametes during fertilization. The trichogyne is typically covered in fine, hair-like projections that aid in capturing sperm cells.

Its morphology and function are essential for the successful completion of the fungal reproductive cycle.
