Prunus × uyekii var. profusa

Prunus × uyekii var. profusa, commonly known as the Japanese apricot, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its showy, single or double pink to white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, edible fruits that are similar in appearance to apricots but are often less flavorful. Its ornamental value comes from its attractive foliage and floral display, making it a popular choice for gardens.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sun, thriving in temperate climates.
