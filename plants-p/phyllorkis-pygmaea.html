Phyllorkis pygmaea

Phyllorkis pygmaea, commonly known as the dwarf rock daisy, is a small flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and bright yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves, and it produces small, daisy-like blooms throughout the summer months.

Phyllorkis pygmaea is often cultivated in rock gardens and as a decorative plant due to its hardiness and ornamental value.
