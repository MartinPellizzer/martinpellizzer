Piper linguifolium var. brevifolium

Piper linguifolium var. brevifolium, commonly known as the short-leaved pepper plant, is a variant of the genus Piper native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and distinctive, broad, ovate leaves with a smooth or slightly serrated margin. The plant produces small, clustered white flowers followed by dark, glossy berries that are often used in traditional medicine. Its foliage is aromatic, containing essential oils that contribute to its medicinal properties.

This variety is valued for both its ornamental appeal and potential pharmacological applications.
