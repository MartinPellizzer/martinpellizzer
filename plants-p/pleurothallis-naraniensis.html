Pleurothallis naraniensis

Pleurothallis naraniensis, commonly known as the Naranji orchid, is a species native to the cloud forests of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that often display a range of colors, including white, yellow, and green. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that help it conserve moisture in its humid environment.

Pleurothallis naraniensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by enthusiasts of rare orchids.
