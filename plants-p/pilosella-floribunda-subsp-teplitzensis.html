Pilosella floribunda subsp. teplitzensis

Pilosella floribunda subsp.

teplitzensis, commonly known as the Alpine pearly everlasting, is a flowering plant native to the alpine regions of Central Europe. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers.

The plant produces involucral leaves that are densely hairy, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to harsh mountain environments.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is often cultivated in alpine gardens.
