Polanisia dodecandra subsp. trachysperma

Polanisia dodecandra subsp.

trachysperma, commonly known as wild coriander, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia. It is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters.

The plant produces small, elongated fruits that contain aromatic seeds used in traditional medicine and cuisine. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fields.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for both ecological and agricultural purposes.
