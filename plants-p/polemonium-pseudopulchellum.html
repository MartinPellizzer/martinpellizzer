Polemonium pseudopulchellum

Polemonium pseudopulchellum, commonly known as the Appalachian bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, wooded areas and thrives in partial to full shade. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue to purple, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture, while its stems are upright and branched.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
