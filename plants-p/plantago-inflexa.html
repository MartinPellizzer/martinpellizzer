Plantago inflexa

Plantago inflexa, commonly known as the bentleaf plantain, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often slightly curled or bent at the base, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in dense spikes, which are typically clustered at the tips of the stems. Its leaves are covered in fine hairs and have a slightly rough texture.

Plantago inflexa is often found in dry, rocky soils and is known for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.
