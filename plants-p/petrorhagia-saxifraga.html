Petrorhagia saxifraga

Petrorhagia saxifraga, commonly known as the rock rose, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing perennial shrub that typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant produces vibrant pink or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow, silvery-gray, and adapted to withstand arid conditions.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.
