Prosthechea santanderensis

Prosthechea santanderensis, commonly known as the Santander orchid, is a species of epiphytic orchid native to the Andes mountains of Colombia.

It is characterized by its compact, pseudobulbous structure and produces clusters of small, fragrant, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows on trees or rocks in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are linear and arranged in a rosette pattern, aiding in water retention.

Prosthechea santanderensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical gardens.
