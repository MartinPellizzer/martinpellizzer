Pyrus intermedia

Pyrus intermedia, commonly known as the Japanese white pear, is a deciduous tree native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves that are typically dark green on top and lighter underneath. The tree produces small, white flowers in early spring, followed by edible, sweet fruits that ripen in late summer. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for cultivation in temperate climates.

The plant is valued for its ornamental beauty and culinary uses.
