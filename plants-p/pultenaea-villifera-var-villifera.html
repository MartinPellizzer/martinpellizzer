Pultenaea villifera var. villifera

Pultenaea villifera var. villifera, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bush-pea, is a shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is often found in heathlands and open forests. The plant has slender, often prostrate stems and small, narrow leaves arranged in a spiral pattern. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange, appearing in clusters and attracting nectar-feeding birds and insects.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
