Physaria urbaniana

Physaria urbaniana, commonly known as the desert sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and bright yellow, four-petaled flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has small, linear leaves and is adapted to arid environments, often growing in sandy or gravelly soils. It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard plants.

Physaria urbaniana is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
