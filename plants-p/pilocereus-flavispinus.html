Pilocereus flavispinus

Pilocereus flavispinus, commonly known as the yellow-spined night bloomer, is a cacti species native to South America.

It is characterized by its spiny stems and nocturnal flowering habit, producing large, fragrant flowers that open at night. The plant has a succulent structure, allowing it to store water efficiently in arid environments. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, and it is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.

Pilocereus flavispinus thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for xeriscaping.
