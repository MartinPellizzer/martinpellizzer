Paravallaris maingayi

Paravallaris maingayi, commonly known as the Malabar mountain bean, is a climbing shrub native to the Western Ghats of India.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pod-like fruits. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its flowers are usually pale yellow or greenish, and it produces edible seeds that are used in local cuisines.

The plant's botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and traditional medicine.
