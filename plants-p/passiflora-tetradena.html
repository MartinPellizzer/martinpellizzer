Passiflora tetradena

Passiflora tetradena, commonly known as the California passionflower, is a climbing vine native to the western United States.

It features showy, violet-blue flowers with five sepals, five petals, and a distinctive crown of filaments. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets and produces edible, orange-red berries. It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in chaparral and woodland habitats.

Its botanical structure supports its role as a pollinator attractant and a food source for various wildlife.
