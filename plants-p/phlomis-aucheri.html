Phlomis aucheri

Phlomis aucheri, commonly known as the Armenian giant mullein, is a perennial herb native to the Caucasus region.

It features tall, erect stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height, covered with dense, woolly hairs. The plant produces showy, yellow-orange flower clusters in late summer, which are arranged in a spike-like inflorescence. Its leaves are large, lance-shaped, and covered with a thick layer of soft, grayish-white pubescence.

Phlomis aucheri is often found in rocky or gravelly soils and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
