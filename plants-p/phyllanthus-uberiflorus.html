Phyllanthus uberiflorus

Phyllanthus uberiflorus, commonly known as the hairy leaf sandmat, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, hairy leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments. Its unique morphology and adaptability to harsh conditions make it a notable species in its native habitat.

The plant is also valued for its potential medicinal properties in traditional herbal practices.
