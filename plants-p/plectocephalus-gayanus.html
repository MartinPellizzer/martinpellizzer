Plectocephalus gayanus

Plectocephalus gayanus, commonly known as the African orchid or the yellow orchid, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking yellow flowers with a distinctive shape. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its roots are specialized for absorbing moisture and nutrients from the air and rain.

The botanical interest in Plectocephalus gayanus lies in its unique floral structure and its role in local ecosystems.
