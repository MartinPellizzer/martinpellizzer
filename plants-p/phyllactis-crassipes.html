Phyllactis crassipes

Phyllactis crassipes, commonly known as the broad-leaved bladderwort, is a unique aquatic plant found in freshwater habitats.

It is characterized by its inflated, bladder-like structures that aid in buoyancy and trap small organisms. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are often broad and oval in shape. It reproduces both sexually through seeds and asexually through vegetative propagation.

Phyllactis crassipes plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and serving as a food source for various aquatic animals.
