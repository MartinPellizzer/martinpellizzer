Parameria

Parameria, commonly known as the golden bell, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Apocynaceae.

It consists of several species native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific Islands. The leaves are usually opposite or whorled, and the flowers are often clustered in inflorescences.
