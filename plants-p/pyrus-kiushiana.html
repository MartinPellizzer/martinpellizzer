Pyrus kiushiana

Pyrus kiushiana, commonly known as the Japanese whitebeam, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Japan.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its oval, glossy, dark green leaves that turn yellow in autumn. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in early spring, which are followed by edible, sweet-tart berries. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.

Pyrus kiushiana is also valued for its role in ecological habitats and as a source of food for wildlife.
