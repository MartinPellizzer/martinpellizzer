Passiflora × pfordtii

Passiflora × pfordtii, commonly known as the purple passionflower, is a hybrid species native to the southern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a climbing vine characterized by its striking, showy flowers with five sepals, five petals, and numerous stamens. The plant produces edible, tropical fruits known as passionfruits, which are rich in vitamins and antioxidants. Its leaves are alternate, heart-shaped, and often have a velvety texture.

The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for ornamental and culinary purposes.
