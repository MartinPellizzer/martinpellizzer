Pararistolochia pithecurus

Pararistolochia pithecurus, commonly known as the giant Dutchman's pipe, is a climbing vine native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and distinctive, spiral-shaped flowers that resemble a pipe. The plant produces small, fleshy fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with high humidity and well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
