Piptatherum humile

Piptatherum humile, commonly known as the dwarf wheatgrass, is a small annual grass native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It grows to a height of about 10-20 centimeters and has slender, grass-like leaves with a fine texture. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny grains of wheat. It is often found in disturbed soils and is used in herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.

Its botanical features make it a valuable plant for ecological studies and traditional remedies.
