Polygonum polymorphum

Polygonum polymorphum, commonly known as the variable knotweed, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its upright, branched stems that are often covered with fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters during the summer months. Its leaves are alternate, ovate to lanceolate, and often have a waxy coating.

Polygonum polymorphum is adapted to grow in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine environments.
