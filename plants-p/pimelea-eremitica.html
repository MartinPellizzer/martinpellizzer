Pimelea eremitica

Pimelea eremitica, commonly known as the ghost flower, is a flowering plant native to Western Australia.

It is a low-growing shrub that typically reaches a height of about 30 centimeters. The plant features small, pale yellow flowers that are clustered in dense, spherical inflorescences. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Pimelea eremitica is known for its resilience and is often found in coastal and inland regions of its native habitat.
