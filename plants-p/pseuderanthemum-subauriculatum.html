Pseuderanthemum subauriculatum

Pseuderanthemum subauriculatum, commonly known as the golden leaf pseuderanthemum, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking, bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery down, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for tropical gardens and indoor plant collections.
