Pyrus zeravschanica

Pyrus zeravschanica, commonly known as the Pamir whitebeam, is a species of flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a deciduous tree or shrub that typically grows at high altitudes, often in rocky or alpine environments. The plant produces small, white to yellowish flowers in clusters, which are followed by small, round fruits. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a serrated edge, typical of the Rosaceae family.

Pyrus zeravschanica is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
