Physalis jaltomata

Physalis jaltomata, commonly known as the golden gooseberry, is a species of flowering plant in the nightshade family.

It is native to Mexico and is characterized by its vibrant orange berries enclosed in a papery calyx. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in scrublands and woodlands. Its flowers are small and yellow, with five petals, and it produces fruit that is both edible and ornamental.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
