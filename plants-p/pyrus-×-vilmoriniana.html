Pyrus × vilmoriniana

Pyrus × vilmoriniana, commonly known as the Vilmorin's pear, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Pyrus communis (European pear) and Pyrus pyrifolia (Asian pear).

It is characterized by its large, juicy fruits that typically have a sweet and crisp texture. The plant is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 10 meters tall, featuring broad, lobed leaves and showy white flowers in the spring. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This hybrid is valued for its ornamental qualities and edible fruit, making it a popular choice in both gardens and orchards.
