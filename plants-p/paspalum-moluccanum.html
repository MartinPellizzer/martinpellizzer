Paspalum moluccanum

Paspalum moluccanum, commonly known as the Moluccan paspalum, is a perennial grass native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia and Australia.

It typically grows in moist to wet habitats, such as marshes, swamps, and along riverbanks. The plant has slender, spreading stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its inflorescence consists of a loose cluster of spikelets, which produce small, wind-pollinated flowers.

Paspalum moluccanum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping due to its adaptability and resilience.
