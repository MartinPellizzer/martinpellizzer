Pentodon pentandrus

Pentodon pentandrus, commonly known as the purple orchid, is a flowering plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its striking violet to purple flowers, which are often adorned with intricate patterns. Its roots are fibrous and adapted for absorbing nutrients from the nutrient-poor soils of its habitat.

Pentodon pentandrus is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in shaded, moist environments.
