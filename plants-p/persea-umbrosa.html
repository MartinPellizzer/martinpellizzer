Persea umbrosa

Persea umbrosa, commonly known as the Mexican olive, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Mexico and Central America.

It is characterized by its dark green, glossy leaves and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. The tree produces edible, fleshy fruits that are often consumed by wildlife and humans alike. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland forests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is also cultivated in gardens and landscapes in warm climates.
