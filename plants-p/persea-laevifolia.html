Persea laevifolia

Persea laevifolia, commonly known as the Mexican persimmon, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central America and parts of Mexico.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are typically smooth on both sides. The tree produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible berries that ripen to a deep orange or red color. Its fruit is valued for its sweet, non-astringent flavor and is often consumed fresh or used in culinary applications.

The plant is also noted for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types.
