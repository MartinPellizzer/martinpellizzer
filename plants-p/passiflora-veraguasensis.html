Passiflora veraguasensis

Passiflora veraguasensis, commonly known as the Veragua passionflower, is a striking tropical vine native to Central America.

It is characterized by its large, intricate flowers that display a mix of blue, purple, and white petals, with a distinctive crown of filaments. The plant produces edible berries that are typically yellow or orange when ripe. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
