Plantago scabra

Plantago scabra, commonly known as rough plantain, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It features a basal rosette of broad, rough-textured leaves with prominent veins and a hairy surface. The plant produces erect flowering stems topped with a dense spike of small, greenish-white flowers. It is often found in disturbed soils, along roadsides, and in meadows.

Botanically, it is characterized by its fibrous root system and its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types.
