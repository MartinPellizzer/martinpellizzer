Physostemon hasslerianus

Physostemon hasslerianus, commonly known as the Mexican beebalm, is a herbaceous perennial native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers in shades of red, purple, and white, which attract pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory properties.

The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
