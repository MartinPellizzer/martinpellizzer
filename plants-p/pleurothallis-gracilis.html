Pleurothallis gracilis

Pleurothallis gracilis, commonly known as the slender orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a small, epiphytic plant that thrives in humid, shaded environments, typically growing on trees or rocks. The plant produces delicate, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pale pink, often with intricate patterns on the petals. Its roots are adapted to absorb moisture from the air, and it requires well-draining potting media to prevent root rot.

Pleurothallis gracilis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by enthusiasts of rare orchids.
