Potentilla gaudinii f. parceglandulosa

Potentilla gaudinii f. parceglandulosa, commonly known as the glandular-leaved potentilla, is a horticultural form of the species Potentilla gaudinii.

It is characterized by its reduced or absent glandular structures on the leaves, which distinguish it from the typical form. This plant typically features small, deciduous leaves and produces clusters of yellow flowers in the summer. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and adaptability to various soil conditions.

The plant is relatively low-maintenance and thrives in full sun to partial shade.
