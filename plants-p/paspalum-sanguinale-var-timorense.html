Paspalum sanguinale var. timorense

Paspalum sanguinale var. timorense, commonly known as Timor bloodgrass, is a perennial grass native to the island of Timor.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and distinctive reddish-purple inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in grassland habitats. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, while the flowering stems are often tinged with red or purple hues.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping or as a ground cover.
