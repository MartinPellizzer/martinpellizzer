Primula flexuosa

Primula flexuosa, commonly known as the flexible primrose, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia, particularly China and Japan.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its upright, branched stems that often curve or flex, giving it its common name. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, yellow, and pink, typically blooming in spring. Its leaves are broad, heart-shaped, and covered in a fine layer of downy hair.

Primula flexuosa is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a decorative plant in shaded areas.
