Prunus mume var. formosana

Prunus mume var. formosana, commonly known as the Japanese apricot or Chinese flowering apricot, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its ornamental value, featuring clusters of fragrant, pink or white flowers in early spring before the leaves emerge. The plant produces small, edible fruits that are typically harvested in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

The species is also valued for its cultural significance in traditional Chinese and Japanese art and symbolism.
