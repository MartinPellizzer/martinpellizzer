Piper aequale var. aequale

Piper aequale var. aequale, commonly known as the Indian betel or areca nut plant, is a climbing shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and small, greenish-white flowers. The plant produces small, fleshy berries that contain the aromatic areca nut, used in traditional chewing practices. Its botanical features include opposite, glossy foliage and a robust root system that supports its vigorous growth.

It thrives in tropical climates with high humidity and partial shade.
