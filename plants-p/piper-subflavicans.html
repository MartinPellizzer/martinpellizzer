Piper subflavicans

Piper subflavicans, commonly known as the yellow-flowered pepper vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many species of pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its slender, woody stems and opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy surface. Its small, yellowish-green flowers are clustered in dense inflorescences, and it produces small, round berries that are typically green when immature and turn red upon ripening.

Piper subflavicans is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and unique flowering habit.
