Phyllopentas decaryana

Phyllopentas decaryana, commonly known as the golden star vine, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, star-shaped yellow flowers that bloom in clusters along its climbing stems. The plant has heart-shaped, glossy leaves that are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its vibrant appearance.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for both horticultural and medicinal applications.
