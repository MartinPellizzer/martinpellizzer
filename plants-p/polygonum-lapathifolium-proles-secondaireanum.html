Polygonum lapathifolium proles secondaireanum

Polygonum lapathifolium proles secondaireanum, commonly known as the secondary polygonum, is a flowering plant species belonging to the Polygonaceae family.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and distinctive, often reddish, branched stems. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-white in color, and it has heart-shaped leaves with a rough, textured surface. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in aquatic environments.
