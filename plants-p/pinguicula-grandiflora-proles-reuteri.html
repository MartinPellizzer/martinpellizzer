Pinguicula grandiflora proles reuteri

Pinguicula grandiflora proles reuteri, commonly known as the giant butterwort, is a carnivorous plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its broad, sticky leaves that trap and digest small insects. The plant produces tall, erect flowering stems with clusters of white, bell-shaped flowers. It thrives in moist, acidic soil and requires high humidity to flourish.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and unique predatory behavior.
