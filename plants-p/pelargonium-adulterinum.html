Pelargonium adulterinum

Pelargonium adulterinum, commonly known as the false geranium, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Geraniaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, clustered flowers. The plant typically produces small, pink to red blooms that appear in spring and summer. Its leaves are usually lobed or heart-shaped, with a slightly hairy texture.

Pelargonium adulterinum is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance and ease of cultivation.
