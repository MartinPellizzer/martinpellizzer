Palaquium dasyphyllum

Palaquium dasyphyllum, commonly known as the purple-leaf palaquium, is a small evergreen tree native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that often display a striking purple hue on the underside. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers followed by edible, fleshy berries that are a source of nutrition for various wildlife. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
