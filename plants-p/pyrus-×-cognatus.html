Pyrus × cognatus

Pyrus × cognatus, commonly known as the common quince, is a hybrid fruit tree belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its ornamental, fragrant flowers and its ability to thrive in temperate climates. The plant produces a distinctive, pale-yellow fruit that is typically harvested in late autumn. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a rough texture with serrated edges.

The tree is often cultivated for both its ornamental value and its edible fruit, which requires cooking before consumption.
