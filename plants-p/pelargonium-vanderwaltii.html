Pelargonium vanderwaltii

Pelargonium vanderwaltii, commonly known as the Vander Walt's geranium, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Geraniaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, showy flowers that range in color from pink to red. The plant typically grows as a perennial herb with upright stems and serrated leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is further enhanced by its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
