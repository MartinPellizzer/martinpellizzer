Pulmonaria angustifolia var. australis

Pulmonaria angustifolia var. australis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved lungwort, is a perennial herb native to southern regions of Australia.

It features distinctive basal leaves that are lance-shaped and often covered in a fine, fuzzy texture. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or rocky habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to local pollinators.
