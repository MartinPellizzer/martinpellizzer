Phylica nervosa

Phylica nervosa, commonly known as the silverleaf phylica, is a South African plant belonging to the Proteaceae family.

It is characterized by its narrow, silver-gray leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, delicate white or pink flowers that bloom in spring. It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Phylica nervosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine.
