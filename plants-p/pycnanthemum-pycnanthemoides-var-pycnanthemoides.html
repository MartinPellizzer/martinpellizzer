Pycnanthemum pycnanthemoides var. pycnanthemoides

Pycnanthemum pycnanthemoides var. pycnanthemoides, commonly known as sweetwood, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its aromatic, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often tinged with red or purple at the base. The plant produces small, clustered yellow-green flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland borders or wet meadows.

Its fragrance and ornamental value make it a popular choice for native plant gardens.
