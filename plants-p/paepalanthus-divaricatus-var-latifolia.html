Paepalanthus divaricatus var. latifolia

Paepalanthus divaricatus var. latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf marsh hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its broad, waxy leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically pink or red. The plant thrives in wetland habitats and is often found in marshes and along riverbanks. It produces capsules that contain numerous small seeds, which are dispersed by water or wind.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in wetland ecosystems.
