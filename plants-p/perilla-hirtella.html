Perilla hirtella

Perilla hirtella, commonly known as hairy perilla, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs. The plant is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves with a distinctive mint-like fragrance. It produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to purple.

Perilla hirtella is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
