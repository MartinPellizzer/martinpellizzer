Pleurothallis mitchellii

Pleurothallis mitchellii, commonly known as Mitchell's orchid, is a species of orchid native to the cloud forests of Central and South America.

It is a small, epiphytic orchid that typically grows on trees or rocks in humid, shaded environments. The plant produces delicate, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, often with intricate patterns. Its roots are adapted to absorb moisture and nutrients from the air and rain.

Pleurothallis mitchellii is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
