Piper luxii

Piper luxii, commonly known as the golden pepper plant, is a tropical species native to the rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking golden-yellow flowers and elongated, slender fruits that turn bright red when mature. The plant belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species such as black pepper. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped with a glossy surface and a mild, peppery aroma.

Piper luxii is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its vibrant appearance and unique foliage.
