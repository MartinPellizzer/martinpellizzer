Pterostigma ovatum

Pterostigma ovatum, commonly known as the white-veined fern, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, feathery fronds that are often light green with a distinct white midrib. The plant's rhizomes are fibrous and help it thrive in moist, shaded environments. Its leaves are typically bipinnate, meaning they have two levels of division, creating a lacy appearance.

Pterostigma ovatum is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and greenhouses.
