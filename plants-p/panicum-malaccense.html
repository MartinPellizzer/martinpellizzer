Panicum malaccense

Panicum malaccense, commonly known as Malacca panicgrass, is a flowering plant species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a perennial grass that typically grows in moist, tropical environments. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces fluffy, pale green to brownish seed heads. It is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along riverbanks.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and wetland conservation efforts.
