Piper reticulatum

Piper reticulatum, commonly known as the net-veined pepper or Indian pepper, is a tropical climbing vine native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves with a distinctive net-like venation pattern. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by peppercorn-like berries that turn red when mature. Its aromatic leaves and fruits are used in traditional medicine and as a spice in some cuisines.

The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
