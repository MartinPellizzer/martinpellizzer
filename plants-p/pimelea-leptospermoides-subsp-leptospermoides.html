Pimelea leptospermoides subsp. leptospermoides

Pimelea leptospermoides subsp.

leptospermoides, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bottlebrush, is a shrub native to Western Australia. It is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves and distinctive red or orange bottlebrush-like flowers that appear in late spring to early summer.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in heathlands and rocky areas. It is adapted to Mediterranean climates and is drought-tolerant once established.

The subspecies is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
