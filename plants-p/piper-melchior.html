Piper melchior

Piper melchior, commonly known as the scarlet pepper vine, is a climbing shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many species of pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, bright red berries that are toxic to humans but serve as a food source for certain wildlife. Its ornamental value comes from its vibrant foliage and striking fruit display.

The plant thrives in humid, warm climates and is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal in gardens.
