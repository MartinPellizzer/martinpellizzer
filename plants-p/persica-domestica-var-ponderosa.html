Persica domestica var. ponderosa

Persica domestica var. ponderosa, commonly known as the ponderosa peach.

This variety is characterized by its large, freestone fruits with a yellow-orange flesh and a thick, fuzzy skin. It is native to the western United States and thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil. The plant produces showy pink flowers in the spring, which are followed by the development of its distinctive fruit.

It is valued for both its ornamental appeal and its culinary use in baking and fresh consumption.
