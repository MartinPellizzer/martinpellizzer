Pterocarpus marsupium subsp. acuminatus

Pterocarpus marsupium subsp.

acuminatus, commonly known as the Indian bedellium or the red sandalwood, is a member of the Fabaceae family. It is characterized by its distinctive red to brown heartwood, which is highly valued for its durability and aesthetic appeal.

The plant typically grows as a small to medium-sized tree, with compound leaves and small, pod-like fruits. It is native to the Indian subcontinent and thrives in tropical and subtropical climates.

Due to its ornamental and commercial value, it is often cultivated for timber and traditional medicine.
