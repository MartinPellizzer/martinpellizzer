Plinia punctata

Plinia punctata, commonly known as the spotted hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and small, ovate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces white or pinkish flowers in clusters, followed by red, berry-like fruits that are edible for birds and some mammals. Plinia punctata thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions and full sun to partial shade.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden landscapes in temperate regions.
