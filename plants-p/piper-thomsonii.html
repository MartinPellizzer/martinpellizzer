Piper thomsonii

Piper thomsonii, commonly known as Thomson's pepper, is a flowering plant native to the rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruits are small berries that mature to a dark purple color.

Piper thomsonii is often grown for its ornamental value and has mild culinary uses in some traditional cuisines.
