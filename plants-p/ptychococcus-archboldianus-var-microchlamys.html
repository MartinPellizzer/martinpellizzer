Ptychococcus archboldianus var. microchlamys

Ptychococcus archboldianus var. microchlamys, commonly known as the dwarf chlamys lichen, is a foliose lichen that exhibits a distinctive, flattened, leaf-like structure.

It typically grows on shaded, moist rock surfaces in tropical and subtropical regions. The thallus is composed of a dense layer of fungal hyphae intertwined with algae, which provide the photosynthetic function. The plant has a slow growth rate and is often found in humid, low-light environments.

Its morphology and ecological niche make it a unique subject for botanical study.
