Plantago stenopetala var. stricta

Plantago stenopetala var. stricta, commonly known as the narrow-leaved plantain, is a variant of the common plantain species.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its upright growth habit and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in dense clusters during the summer months. It is native to regions of Europe and Asia and thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

This variety is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fields.
