Psydrax odoratus f. subnitidus

Psydrax odoratus f. subnitidus, commonly known as the fragrant psidium, is a cultivar of the psidium genus native to tropical regions.

It is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that are slightly smaller than those of the typical species. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its fruit, resembling a small apple, is edible and has a sweet, tangy flavor.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential culinary uses in tropical horticulture.
