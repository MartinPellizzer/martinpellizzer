Prunus laurocerasus zabeliana

Prunus laurocerasus zabeliana, commonly known as the Zabel's laurel cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Caucasus region.

It features dark green, glossy leaves that turn yellow in autumn, and it produces small, fragrant white flowers in spring. The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities, including its dense foliage and showy blooms. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it suitable for gardens and landscapes.

However, it is toxic to humans and animals, so care should be taken when handling or planting it.
