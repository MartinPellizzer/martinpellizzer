Polygala adenophylla var. obovata

Polygala adenophylla var. obovata, commonly known as the broad-leaf milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its obovate leaves and distinctive floral structures. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. It produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring, attracting various pollinators.

Due to its unique morphology and limited distribution, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
