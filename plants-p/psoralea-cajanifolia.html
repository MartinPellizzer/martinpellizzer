Psoralea cajanifolia

Psoralea cajanifolia, commonly known as the Indian psoralea, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree with compound leaves and yellow or orange flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, flat, lens-shaped fruits called legumes, which contain seeds used in traditional medicine. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Psoralea cajanifolia is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in Ayurvedic practices.
