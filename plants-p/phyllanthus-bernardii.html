Phyllanthus bernardii

Phyllanthus bernardii, commonly known as African nightshade, is a flowering plant native to tropical and southern Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, followed by red, fleshy berries that contain the seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or savanna habitats.

The plant is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional African medicine for various ailments.
