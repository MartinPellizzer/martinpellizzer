Polemonium lemmonii

Polemonium lemmonii, commonly known as Lemmon's bluebell, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the family Polemoniaceae and is characterized by its striking blue to purple bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have a hairy texture.

Polemonium lemmonii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local pollinators.
