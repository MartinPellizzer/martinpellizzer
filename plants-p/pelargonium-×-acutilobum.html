Pelargonium × acutilobum

Pelargonium × acutilobum, commonly known as the African geranium, is a hybrid species within the Geraniaceae family.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and dense, rounded foliage with serrated margins. The plant produces showy, five-petaled flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, typically in late spring to early summer. Native to South Africa, it thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and container planting.

Its ornamental value and adaptability contribute to its widespread cultivation in temperate regions.
