Potamogeton gramineus var. spathuliformis

Potamogeton gramineus var. spathuliformis, commonly known as the broadleaf pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater ecosystems in North America.

It has long, ribbon-like leaves that are typically broader at the base and taper toward the tips. The plant produces small, green flowers that are clustered in spike-like inflorescences, which emerge above the water surface. It plays an important role in aquatic habitats by providing shelter for fish and invertebrates.

This variety is often found in shallow, slow-moving waters such as ponds and lakes.
