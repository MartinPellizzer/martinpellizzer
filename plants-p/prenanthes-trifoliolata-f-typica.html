Prenanthes trifoliolata f. typica

Prenanthes trifoliolata f. typica, commonly known as the three-leaf water pepper, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three leaflets. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in a spadix, typical of the arum family. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as wetlands and stream banks.
