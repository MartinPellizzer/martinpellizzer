Pancratium croceum

Pancratium croceum, commonly known as the yellow lily or sea daffodil, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of the Mediterranean and parts of Africa.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers with a distinctive orange center, which bloom in summer. The plant has narrow, strap-like leaves and is often found growing in sandy or rocky soils near the shoreline. It is adapted to salt spray and can tolerate periods of drought.

Pancratium croceum is also known for its medicinal properties, traditionally used in herbal remedies.
