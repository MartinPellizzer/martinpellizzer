Pleurothallis perryi

Pleurothallis perryi, commonly known as the Perry's orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Ecuador.

It is a small epiphytic orchid that typically grows on tree branches or rocks in humid, shaded environments. The plant features slender, wiry stems and produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pale yellow. Its flowers are usually arranged in a raceme and bloom during the warmer months.

Pleurothallis perryi is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts for its delicate beauty.
