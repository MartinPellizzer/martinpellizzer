Phyllanthus pacificus var. uapensis

Phyllanthus pacificus var. uapensis, commonly known as the Uapaca phyllanthus, is a shrub native to the Pacific Islands.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. The plant produces small, clustered yellow flowers, followed by greenish or reddish fruits that contain a single seed.

It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in coastal or lowland areas with well-drained soil.
