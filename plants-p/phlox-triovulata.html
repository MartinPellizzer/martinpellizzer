Phlox triovulata

Phlox triovulata, commonly known as the three-colored phlox, is a perennial flowering plant native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Polemoniaceae family and is characterized by its showy, clustered flowers that bloom in shades of pink, white, and purple. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 feet and prefers full sun to partial shade. Its foliage consists of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery down.

Phlox triovulata is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
