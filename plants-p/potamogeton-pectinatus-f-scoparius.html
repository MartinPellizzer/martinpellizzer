Potamogeton pectinatus f. scoparius

Potamogeton pectinatus f. scoparius, commonly known as the narrow-leaved pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in Europe and Asia.

It has long, narrow leaves that are typically arranged in whorls around the stem, and it produces small, green flowers that are often submerged. The plant is characterized by its finely serrated leaf margins and the presence of prominent, thread-like rhizomes. It thrives in shallow, slow-moving waters and plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.

Its growth can sometimes lead to dense vegetation that affects water flow and quality.
