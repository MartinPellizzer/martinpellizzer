Pinanga schwanerensis

Pinanga schwanerensis, commonly known as the Schwaner's bamboo, is a species of flowering plant in the family Araceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including parts of Indonesia and Malaysia. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender stems that form a clump, with long, narrow leaves arranged in a rosette pattern. Its inflorescence consists of a spadix surrounded by a spathe, typical of the Araceae family.

Pinanga schwanerensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
