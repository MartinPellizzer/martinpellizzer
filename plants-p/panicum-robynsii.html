Panicum robynsii

Panicum robynsii, commonly known as Robins' grass, is a species of grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial plant that typically grows in moist, open habitats such as wetlands and forest edges. The plant produces slender, upright stems with small, spike-like inflorescences that bloom in the summer. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, and it is often found in disturbed soils or along waterways.

Robins' grass is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
