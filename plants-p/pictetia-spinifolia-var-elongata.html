Pictetia spinifolia var. elongata

Pictetia spinifolia var. elongata, commonly known as the elongated thorned bellflower, is a rare and striking plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive elongated, spiny leaves and clusters of bell-shaped, violet-blue flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth. Its unique morphology makes it a sought-after species among botanists and horticultural enthusiasts.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered endangered and requires careful conservation efforts.
