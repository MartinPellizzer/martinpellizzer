Piper hilarianum

Piper hilarianum, commonly known as the Hilarian pepper or Hilarian arrowroot, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil and Peru. The plant is characterized by its trailing growth habit and heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its small, greenish-white flowers are borne in clusters and are followed by small, edible fruits.

The plant is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
