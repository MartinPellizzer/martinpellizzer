Piper tacariguense

Piper tacariguense, commonly known as the Tacarigua pepper plant, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to Central and South America, particularly found in regions of Panama, Colombia, and Ecuador. The plant is characterized by its small, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of tiny, peppery fruits. It thrives in humid, tropical environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its unique flavor profile, it is occasionally used in traditional culinary practices in its native regions.
