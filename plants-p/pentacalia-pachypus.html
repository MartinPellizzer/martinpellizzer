Pentacalia pachypus

Pentacalia pachypus, commonly known as the thick-leaved pentacalia, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its succulent, thick leaves that help it retain moisture in arid environments. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value and unique appearance, it is occasionally cultivated in tropical and subtropical gardens.
