Pseuderia wariana

Pseuderia wariana, commonly known as the Wariana orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its leaves are broad and strap-like, while its flowers are usually pink or white with a distinctive yellow lip.

Pseuderia wariana is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in greenhouses and gardens.
