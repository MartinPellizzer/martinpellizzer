Phlox paniculata var. acuminata

Phlox paniculata var. acuminata, commonly known as the pointedleaf garden phlox, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of showy, fragrant flowers that bloom in late summer. The leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly serrated edge, contributing to its ornamental appeal. This variety is prized for its ability to thrive in full sun to partial shade and its moderate water requirements.

It is a popular choice for gardeners seeking a reliable and colorful perennial for borders and landscape beds.
