Piper parvipetiolum

Piper parvipetiolum, commonly known as the small-leaf pepper or dwarf pepper, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil and Peru. The plant is characterized by its small, ovate leaves and clusters of tiny, greenish-white flowers. It produces small, peppery fruits that are used in traditional medicine and cuisine.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and research settings.
