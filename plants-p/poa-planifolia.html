Poa planifolia

Poa planifolia, commonly known as the flatleaf meadowgrass, is a species of grass native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its flat, narrow leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields. It is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and prevent erosion.

Poa planifolia is also valued for its ornamental qualities in landscaping.
