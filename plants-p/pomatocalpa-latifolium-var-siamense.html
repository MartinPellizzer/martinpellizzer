Pomatocalpa latifolium var. siamense

Pomatocalpa latifolium var. siamense, commonly known as the Siam orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves and produces striking, fragrant flowers with a distinctive pink and white coloration. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest understories. Its flowers are borne on lateral spikes and are known for their intricate, spurred structure.

This orchid is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated for horticultural purposes.
