Pterocyperus esculentus

Pterocyperus esculentus, commonly known as the edible sedge, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and grass-like plants. The plant features triangular, narrow leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that are typically green or brown in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or near water bodies.

The edible parts of the plant, particularly the young shoots and tubers, are used in various traditional cuisines.
