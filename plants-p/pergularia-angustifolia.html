Pergularia angustifolia

Pergularia angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf pogostemon, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun. The plant features slender, upright stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. Its small, tubular flowers are usually pink or purple and bloom in summer.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
