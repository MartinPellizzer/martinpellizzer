Potamogeton seemenii

Potamogeton seemenii, commonly known as the narrow-leaf pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in Asia.

It has long, slender leaves that are typically less than 1 mm wide, arranged in whorls around the stem. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually green or brown, and it is often found in slow-moving or still waters. Potamogeton seemenii plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
