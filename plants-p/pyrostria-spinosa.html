Pyrostria spinosa

Pyrostria spinosa, commonly known as the spinous pyrostria, is a unique and rare plant native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its woody stems and dense, spiny foliage that serves as a defense mechanism against herbivores. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically white or pale green, followed by the development of woody, spiky fruits. Its growth is slow, and it thrives in shaded, humid environments with high levels of organic matter in the soil.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, Pyrostria spinosa is considered a valuable species for botanical research and conservation efforts.
