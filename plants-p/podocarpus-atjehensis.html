Podocarpus atjehensis

Podocarpus atjehensis, commonly known as the Atjeh pine, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to the western part of Sumatra, Indonesia.

It belongs to the Podocarpaceae family and is characterized by its dense, needle-like leaves and cone-bearing structure. The plant is typically found in tropical rainforests, growing in humid and shaded environments. It plays a significant role in the local ecosystem by providing habitat and contributing to soil stability.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is also cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
