Peperomia weddelii

Peperomia weddelii, commonly known as the Weddell's peperomia, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to South America.

It features thick, waxy leaves that are typically ovate to elliptical in shape, with a glossy surface and a variety of colors ranging from light green to deep emerald. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually greenish-white in color, arranged in clusters at the ends of stems. It is well-suited for indoor cultivation due to its low light requirements and drought tolerance.

Peperomia weddelii is also valued for its ability to thrive in humid environments, making it a popular choice for shaded or semi-shaded spaces.
