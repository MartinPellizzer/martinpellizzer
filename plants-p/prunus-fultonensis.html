Prunus fultonensis

Prunus fultonensis, commonly known as the Fulton cherry, is a small deciduous tree native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and showy white to pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The tree produces small, dark red cherries that are edible but not widely cultivated for commercial use.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland habitats, where it plays a role in local ecosystems.
