Piper berteroanum

Piper berteroanum, commonly known as the Amazonian pepper or wild pepper, is a flowering plant native to the rainforests of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many species of pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its long, slender leaves and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. Its fruit is a small, round berry that turns from green to red as it matures.

The plant is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional remedies for various ailments.
