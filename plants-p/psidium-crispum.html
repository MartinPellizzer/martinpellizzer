Psidium crispum

Psidium crispum, commonly known as the pineapple guava, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to South America.

It produces small, fragrant, and edible berries that are typically pink or red in color. The plant has glossy, dark green leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a leathery texture. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

The fruit is often used in culinary applications due to its sweet and tangy flavor.
