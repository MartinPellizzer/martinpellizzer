Plantago depressa

Plantago depressa, commonly known as common plantain, is a widespread herbaceous plant belonging to the Plantaginaceae family.

It is characterized by its broad, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish or reddish in color. It thrives in a variety of environments, including disturbed soils and urban areas, making it a common sight in many regions.

Its adaptability and resilience contribute to its success as a pioneer species in ecological succession.
