Papaver × bergianum

Papaver × bergianum, commonly known as the hybrid poppy, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, large, and colorful flowers that typically range from red to orange in hue. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, often found in alpine and rocky habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and botanical collections worldwide.
