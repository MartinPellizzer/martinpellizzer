Panicum attenuatum

Panicum attenuatum, commonly known as little bluestem, is a native grass species found in the central and eastern United States.

It is characterized by its fine, blue-green foliage and tufted inflorescence that turns golden in the fall. This plant is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it a popular choice for native plant gardens. It plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for wildlife.

Panicum attenuatum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in restoration projects.
