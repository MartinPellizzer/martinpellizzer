Polypodium himalayense

Polypodium himalayense, commonly known as the Himalayan shield fern, is a species of fern native to the mountainous regions of the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its distinctive, broad, and leathery fronds that are often divided into segments. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its rhizome is thick and fleshy, allowing it to store moisture and nutrients.

Polypodium himalayense is valued for its ornamental appearance and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
