Pavonia bequaertii

Pavonia bequaertii, commonly known as the Mexican poinsettia, is a shrub native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its striking red bracts that surround small, inconspicuous flowers, which are actually the true flowers of the plant. The plant belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes hibiscus and cotton. It thrives in warm, humid climates and prefers well-drained soil.

Pavonia bequaertii is often cultivated for its ornamental value, particularly during the holiday season.
