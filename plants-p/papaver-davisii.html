Papaver davisii

Papaver davisii, commonly known as Davis's poppy, is a species of flowering plant in the family Papaveraceae.

It is native to the mountainous regions of central and eastern Europe, particularly in areas with well-drained soils. The plant produces striking, showy flowers that are typically red or orange in color, with a distinctive, saucer-shaped corona. It is often found in rocky or alpine habitats, where it thrives in full sun.

The flowers bloom in early summer and are pollinated primarily by insects such as bees and butterflies.
