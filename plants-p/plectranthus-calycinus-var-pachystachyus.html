Plectranthus calycinus var. pachystachyus

Plectranthus calycinus var. pachystachyus, commonly known as the woolly plectranthus, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, velvety foliage that ranges from silver to gray-green in color. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in hue. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires moderate sunlight, making it suitable for both indoor and outdoor cultivation.

This variety is often used as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and ability to attract pollinators.
