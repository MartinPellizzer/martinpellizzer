Phaseolus vulgaris f. amethystinus

Phaseolus vulgaris f. amethystinus, commonly known as the Amethyst French bean, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its unique violet-pink flowers and elongated, bean-like pods that develop from the flowers. The plant is typically grown as an annual in temperate climates and thrives in well-drained soil with full sunlight. Its foliage consists of trifoliate leaves, and it produces edible pods that can be harvested when immature for culinary use.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal as well as its edible qualities.
