Pilocarpus pennatifolius

Pilocarpus pennatifolius, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its palm-like appearance, with pinnately compound leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces small, red fruits that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in dry forests and savannas.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
