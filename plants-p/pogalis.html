Pogalis

Pogalis, commonly known as the mountain spinach, is a hardy perennial plant native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves that are rich in nutrients. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found growing in rocky soils at elevations above 3,000 meters. Its leaves are edible and have been traditionally used in local cuisines for their nutritional value.

Pogalis is also valued for its ability to withstand harsh climatic conditions, making it an important plant in alpine ecosystems.
