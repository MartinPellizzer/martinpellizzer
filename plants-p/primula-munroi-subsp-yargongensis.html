Primula munroi subsp. yargongensis

Primula munroi subsp.

yargongensis, commonly known as the Yargong primrose, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of Tibet and Nepal. It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, bell-shaped flowers.

The plant typically blooms in spring, producing clusters of white or pale pink petals with a contrasting yellow center. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
