Pourthiaea villosa var. zollingeri

Pourthiaea villosa var. zollingeri, commonly known as the Zollinger's hairy pincushion, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage and spherical inflorescence. The plant produces small, yellow to orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and rockeries.
