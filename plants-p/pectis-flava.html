Pectis flava

Pectis flava, commonly known as the yellow pectis, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces yellow ray florets and disk florets, giving it a daisy-like appearance. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and along coastal areas.

Pectis flava is known for its resilience to drought and its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
