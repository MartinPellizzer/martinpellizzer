Ptychococcus paradoxus

Ptychococcus paradoxus, commonly known as the strange curling plant, is a unique species of flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, spiral-shaped leaves that curl tightly around the stem, giving it a striking appearance. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its unusual morphology, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant in botanical gardens and private collections.
