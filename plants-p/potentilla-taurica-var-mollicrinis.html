Potentilla taurica var. mollicrinis

Potentilla taurica var. mollicrinis, commonly known as the soft-fruited cinquefoil, is a low-growing perennial shrub native to the Caucasus region.

It features slender, spreading stems that are often covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces showy, yellow flowers with five petals, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and ovate, with a soft, velvety texture.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.
