Potamogeton × fluitans var. typicus

Potamogeton × fluitans var. typicus, commonly known as the floating pondweed, is a hybrid aquatic plant that thrives in freshwater environments.

It is characterized by its long, ribbon-like leaves that float on the water surface, with roots submerged in the sediment. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish in color and are adapted for underwater pollination. It plays a vital role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.

Due to its rapid growth, it can become invasive in some water bodies if not managed properly.
