Pecten veneris

Pecten veneris, commonly known as the Venus's comb, is a marine bivalve mollusk belonging to the family Pectinidae.

It is characterized by its elongated, fan-like shell with a series of prominent radial ribs and a distinctive comb-like structure on the inner surface of its valves. The shell is typically white or pale pink, with a smooth and glossy texture. This species is found in tropical and subtropical waters, often inhabiting sandy or muddy substrates in shallow marine environments.

Its anatomy includes a muscular foot for burrowing and a siphonal system for filter feeding.
