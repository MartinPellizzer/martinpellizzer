Pinalia ancorifera

Pinalia ancorifera, commonly known as the anchor fern, is a unique species of fern native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, creeping rhizomes and its fronds that are divided into small, ovate segments. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often found in forest undergrowth. Its reproductive structures, including spore-producing sorus, are located on the underside of the fronds.

Pinalia ancorifera is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in humid, low-light conditions.
