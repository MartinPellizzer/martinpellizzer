Poa pratensiformis

Poa pratensiformis, commonly known as the narrowleaf meadowgrass, is a species of grass native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically green in color. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences, which contain both male and female flowers, typical of the Poaceae family. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, pastures, and along stream banks.

Poa pratensiformis plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for grazing animals.
