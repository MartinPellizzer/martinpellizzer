Phlox drummondii var. tharpii

Phlox drummondii var. tharpii, commonly known as the "Tharpii garden phlox," is a vibrant flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, showy clusters of pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 12 to 18 inches, featuring upright stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

This variety is valued for its hardiness and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
