Pastinaca selinoides

Pastinaca selinoides, commonly known as wild parsnip, is a biennial plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes carrots and parsley, and is characterized by its hollow, ridged stems and compound leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in umbrella-like clusters, and its roots are typically white or yellowish in color. Due to its phototoxic sap, it can cause skin irritation when exposed to sunlight.
