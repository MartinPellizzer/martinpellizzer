Pelargonium daucoides

Pelargonium daucoides, commonly known as the sour fig or wild geranium, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of small, pink to purple flowers. The leaves are typically heart-shaped with a hairy texture, and the plant has a distinctive, somewhat aromatic scent. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

The plant is also known for its medicinal uses in traditional herbal remedies.
