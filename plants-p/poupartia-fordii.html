Poupartia fordii

Poupartia fordii, commonly known as the Chinese hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It is characterized by its thorny branches, ovate leaves with serrated edges, and clusters of white to pinkish flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible but often used in traditional Chinese medicine.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for ornamental landscaping.
