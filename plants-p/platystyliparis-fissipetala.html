Platystyliparis fissipetala

Platystyliparis fissipetala, commonly known as the fissured petal orchid, is a rare and delicate orchid species native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique, elongated leaves and striking, bilaterally symmetrical flowers with a distinctive fissured petal structure. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, often found in montane forests. Its flowers typically bloom in late spring to early summer, showcasing a range of colors from white to pale yellow.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
