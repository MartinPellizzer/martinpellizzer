Potamogeton pectinatus subsp. helveticus

Potamogeton pectinatus subsp.

helveticus, commonly known as the European pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater habitats in Europe. It has long, narrow leaves that are typically arranged in a spiral pattern along its stem.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in spike-like inflorescences. It thrives in still or slow-moving waters with soft to medium substrate.

This species plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various water organisms.
