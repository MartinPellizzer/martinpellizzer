Potentilla alba var. splendens

Potentilla alba var. splendens, commonly known as white cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring slender stems and alternate leaves with five to seven lobes. The plant produces showy, yellowish-white flowers in clusters during the summer months, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically covered in fine hairs, and the plant thrives in well-drained soils in full sun.

It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
