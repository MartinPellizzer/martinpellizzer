Pentodon pentandrus var. pentandrus

Pentodon pentandrus var. pentandrus, commonly known as the red-flowered poinciana, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, feathery, bipinnate leaves that turn a vibrant red during the dry season. The plant produces clusters of bright red or orange flowers, which bloom in spring and attract various pollinators. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular ornamental plant in warm climates.

Due to its seasonal foliage and showy blooms, it is often cultivated for its aesthetic value in gardens and landscapes.
