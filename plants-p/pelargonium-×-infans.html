Pelargonium × infans

Pelargonium × infans, commonly known as the African hybrid geranium, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Pelargonium hortorum and Pelargonium zonale.

It is characterized by its broad, serrated leaves and showy, five-petaled flowers that range in color from pink to red. The plant is native to South Africa and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it adaptable to various growing conditions.

Pelargonium × infans is also valued for its ability to thrive in containers, making it a popular choice for indoor and outdoor landscaping.
