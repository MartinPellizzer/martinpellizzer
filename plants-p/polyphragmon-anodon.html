Polyphragmon anodon

Polyphragmon anodon, commonly known as the narrow-leafed dandelion, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features a basal rosette of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, white hairs. The plant produces yellow composite flowers that bloom in spring, followed by wind-dispersed seeds with parachute-like structures. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and rocky areas.

Its botanical characteristics make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural study.
