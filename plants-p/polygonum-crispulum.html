Polygonum crispulum

Polygonum crispulum, commonly known as mountain spinach or curled polygonum, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It features alternate, lanceolate leaves that are often curled or crinkled, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in dense clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

Polygonum crispulum is valued for its edible leaves, which are rich in nutrients and used in traditional cuisines.
