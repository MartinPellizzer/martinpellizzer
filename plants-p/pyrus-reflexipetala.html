Pyrus reflexipetala

Pyrus reflexipetala, commonly known as the reflexed-leaf pear, is a rare and ancient species of flowering plant belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive, reflexed leaf margins and small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in spring. Native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, it thrives in cool, temperate climates with well-drained soil. The plant produces small, edible fruits that are similar in appearance to common pears but are less widely cultivated.

Due to its limited distribution and conservation status, it is considered a valuable species for botanical research and ecological studies.
