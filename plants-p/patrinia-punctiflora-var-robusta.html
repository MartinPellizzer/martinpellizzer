Patrinia punctiflora var. robusta

Patrinia punctiflora var. robusta, commonly known as the robust patrinia, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves with serrated margins. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically white or pale blue, arranged in dense clusters at the ends of the branches. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.
