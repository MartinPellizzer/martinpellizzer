Phaseolus macropoides

Phaseolus macropoides, commonly known as the giant bean or large-seeded bean, is a herbaceous annual plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and erect growth habit. The plant produces clusters of yellow flowers that develop into elongated, bean-like pods containing large, edible seeds. It thrives in warm, tropical climates and is often cultivated for its nutritional value and culinary uses.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both agricultural and ecological contexts.
