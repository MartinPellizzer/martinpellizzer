Papaver anomalum var. hispidissimum

Papaver anomalum var. hispidissimum, commonly known as the hispid gladiolus, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that range in color from red to orange and yellow. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, dark seeds. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine gardens.

Its unique appearance and hardy nature contribute to its ornamental value in horticulture.
