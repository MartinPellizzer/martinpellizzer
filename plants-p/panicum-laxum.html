Panicum laxum

Panicum laxum, commonly known as silver panicgrass, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, silvery-gray leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces fluffy, pale green to brownish seed heads that are often mistaken for downy fluff. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it a common sight in disturbed areas and along roadsides.

Due to its ornamental value and ability to tolerate drought, it is sometimes used in native plant gardens.
