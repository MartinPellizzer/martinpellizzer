Pilosella kihlmanii

Pilosella kihlmanii, commonly known as the Kihlman's pearly everlasting, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dense, gray-green foliage and small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant produces small, spherical seed heads that are covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in dry, sunny environments and is often found in grasslands and rocky soils.

Pilosella kihlmanii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its drought tolerance and low maintenance requirements.
