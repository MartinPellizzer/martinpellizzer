Panicum balanites

Panicum balanites, commonly known as the Balanites grass, is a perennial grass species native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are adapted to withstand drought conditions. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Its seeds are often dispersed by animals, contributing to its spread in disturbed and marginal habitats.

Due to its resilience, it is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural areas.
