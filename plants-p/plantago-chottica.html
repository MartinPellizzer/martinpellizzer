Plantago chottica

Plantago chottica, commonly known as the Chottic plantain, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of North Africa.

It is characterized by its rosette of broad, waxy leaves that help it retain moisture in dry environments. The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in a spike, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in sandy or saline soils and is often found in coastal areas and salt marshes.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
