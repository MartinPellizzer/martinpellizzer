Panicum flavum

Panicum flavum, commonly known as switchgrass, is a native North American grass species that thrives in a variety of soil types.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and dense, fluffy seed heads that are often seen in the fall. This plant is highly adaptable, capable of growing in both wet and dry conditions, making it a resilient species. Switchgrass is also valued for its ecological benefits, including erosion control and wildlife habitat.

Its fibrous roots help stabilize soil, and it is a popular choice for sustainable landscaping and biofuel production.
