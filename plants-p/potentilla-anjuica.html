Potentilla anjuica

Potentilla anjuica, commonly known as the Indian cinquefoil, is a perennial shrub native to the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine regions.

Potentilla anjuica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
