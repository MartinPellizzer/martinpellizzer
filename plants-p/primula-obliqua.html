Primula obliqua

Primula obliqua, commonly known as the oblique primrose, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Asia, particularly in countries like China and Nepal.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in cool, moist environments and prefers partial shade, often found in forest understories. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in a fine, silvery down, while its stems are short and often sprawling.

Primula obliqua is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate blooms.
