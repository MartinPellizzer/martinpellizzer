Peckia cuspidata

Peckia cuspidata, commonly known as the sharp-pointed peckia, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or alpine settings.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
