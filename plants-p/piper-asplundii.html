Piper asplundii

Piper asplundii, commonly known as the Asplund's pepper vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species. The plant is characterized by its slender, twining stems and heart-shaped leaves with a glossy surface. Its small, greenish-white flowers are followed by berries that mature to a bright red color.

Piper asplundii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
