Primula afghanica

Primula afghanica, commonly known as the Afghan primrose, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Afghanistan and northern Pakistan.

It is characterized by its low-growing habit and produces clusters of bell-shaped, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant typically blooms in the spring and early summer, thriving in cool, moist environments. Its leaves are usually oval and slightly hairy, and it is often found in rocky or alpine habitats.

Primula afghanica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate flowers.
