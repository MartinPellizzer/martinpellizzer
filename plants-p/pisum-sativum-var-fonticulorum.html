Pisum sativum var. fonticulorum

Pisum sativum var. fonticulorum, commonly known as the dwarf garden pea, is a variety of the common pea plant characterized by its compact growth habit.

It produces small, edible pods that contain round, yellow seeds, similar to other pea varieties. This plant is typically grown as an annual in temperate climates and thrives in well-drained soil with full sun exposure. Its botanical features include trifoliate leaves and tendrils that aid in climbing, although the dwarf form often remains bushy.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and culinary use in gardens and home kitchens.
