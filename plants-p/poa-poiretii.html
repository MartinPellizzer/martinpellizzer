Poa poiretii

Poa poiretii, commonly known as the Poiret's bentgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, dark green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny, two-lobed flowers. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes.

Poa poiretii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
