Pinus nigra var. gocensis

Pinus nigra var. gocensis, commonly known as the Gocen pine, is a variety of the black pine native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It is characterized by its dark, fissured bark and needle-like leaves that are typically 5 to 7 centimeters long. The cones of this species are woody and can persist on the tree for several years before opening. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions with a Mediterranean climate.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical significance in local ecosystems.
