Phyllanthus virens

Phyllanthus virens, commonly known as the green spurges or false holly, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa, Asia, and Australia.

It is a small shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, characterized by its simple, opposite, and often spiny leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, followed by small, fleshy berries that are yellow or orange in color. Phyllanthus virens is known for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional medicine for various health purposes.

Its adaptability to different soil types and climates makes it a common ornamental and medicinal plant in many regions.
