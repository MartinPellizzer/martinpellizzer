Piper senporeiense

Piper senporeiense, commonly known as the Sulu pepper, is a member of the Piperaceae family, native to the Philippines.

It is a climbing vine characterized by its dark green, glossy leaves and small, fragrant flowers. The plant produces small, elongated berries that turn from green to red as they mature. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland forests.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
