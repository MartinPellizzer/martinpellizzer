Potamogeton lucens f. macrophyllus

Potamogeton lucens f. macrophyllus, commonly known as the broad-leaf pondweed, is a aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in North America.

It is characterized by its long, narrow leaves that are often wider than the standard variety of Potamogeton lucens. The plant typically grows in shallow waters and forms dense underwater stands. Its flowers are small and clustered, with male and female flowers occurring on separate plants.

This subspecies is important for aquatic ecosystems, providing habitat and food for various water organisms.
