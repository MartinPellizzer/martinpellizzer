Physaria ludoviciana

Physaria ludoviciana, commonly known as the desert sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are succulent and fleshy, adapted to store water in its native habitat.

Physaria ludoviciana is often found in sandy soils and is well-suited to xerophytic conditions.
