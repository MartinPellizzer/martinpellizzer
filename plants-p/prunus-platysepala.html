Prunus platysepala

Prunus platysepala, commonly known as the flat-sepaled cherry, is a species native to China.

It is a small deciduous tree or shrub that typically grows to a height of about 3 to 5 meters. The plant produces small, white or pink flowers in early spring, which are followed by small, red drupes. Its leaves are ovate to elliptic in shape, with a smooth margin and a slightly serrated edge.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its spring blossoms.
