Pyrus bombycilla

Pyrus bombycilla, commonly known as the Japanese whitebeam, is a deciduous tree native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its small, white flowers that appear in early spring. The tree produces small, edible fruits that are typically consumed by birds and other wildlife. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a smooth margin, while its bark is smooth and grayish-brown.

Pyrus bombycilla is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
