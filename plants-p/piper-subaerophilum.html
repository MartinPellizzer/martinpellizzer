Piper subaerophilum

Piper subaerophilum, commonly known as the aerial pepper, is a climbing vine native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piper genus, which includes many species of flowering plants in the family Piperaceae. The plant is characterized by its small, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of tiny, peppery fruits that are used in traditional medicine and cuisine. Its aerial growth habit allows it to thrive in humid, shaded environments, often found in forest understories.

The plant's botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and ethnobotanical contexts.
