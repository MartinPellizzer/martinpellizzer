Portulaca venezuelensis

Portulaca venezuelensis, commonly known as the Venezuelan portulaca, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Venezuela.

It is a succulent species characterized by its fleshy, upright stems and small, fleshy leaves. The plant produces vibrant, showy flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color, blooming during the day. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, preferring well-drained soil and full sun exposure.

Portulaca venezuelensis is often used in ornamental gardening due to its drought tolerance and aesthetic appeal.
