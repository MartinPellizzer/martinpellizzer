Pyrus uralensis

Pyrus uralensis, commonly known as the Ural pear, is a deciduous tree native to the Ural Mountains region of Russia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves and clusters of white, fragrant flowers. The tree produces edible pomes, which are typically small and have a sweet, aromatic flavor. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value as well as its fruit.

Pyrus uralensis is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
