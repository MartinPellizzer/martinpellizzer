Pseudencyclia venosa

Pseudencyclia venosa, commonly known as the veined cycad, is a unique member of the Cycadaceae family.

It is characterized by its stout, unbranched trunk and a crown of large, feathery leaves that are often tinged with a distinctive veining pattern. The plant produces cone-like structures, with male and female cones typically occurring on separate plants. Native to southern Africa, it thrives in warm, dry climates and requires well-drained soil.

Due to its slow growth and ornamental value, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and private collections.
