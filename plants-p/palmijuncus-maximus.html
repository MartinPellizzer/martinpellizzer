Palmijuncus maximus

Palmijuncus maximus, commonly known as the giant fan palm, is a striking tropical plant characterized by its large, fan-shaped leaves that can span several meters in diameter.

It belongs to the Arecaceae family and is native to the warm, humid climates of Southeast Asia. The plant features a tall, slender trunk that can grow up to 15 meters in height, with a smooth, grayish-brown bark. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to anchor it firmly in nutrient-rich, well-drained soils.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
