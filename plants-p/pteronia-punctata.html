Pteronia punctata

Pteronia punctata, commonly known as the spotted pteronia, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its compact, evergreen growth habit. The plant produces showy, daisy-like flowers in shades of pink, purple, and white, which bloom throughout the warmer months. Its leaves are narrow, fleshy, and often have a spotted or mottled appearance, giving it its common name.

Pteronia punctata is well-suited for rock gardens and containers due to its drought tolerance and low maintenance requirements.
