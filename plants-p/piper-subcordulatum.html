Piper subcordulatum

Piper subcordulatum, commonly known as the small-leaved pepper plant, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including parts of India, Malaysia, and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its small, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. It produces small, peppery fruits that are used in traditional medicine and local cuisines.

Piper subcordulatum is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its compact size and attractive foliage.
