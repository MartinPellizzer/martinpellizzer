Pedilonum minutum

Pedilonum minutum, commonly known as the small-footed orchid, is a delicate flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its small, intricate flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and pink. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in cloud forests or along stream banks. Its roots are adapted to absorb nutrients from the rich, organic soil in its habitat.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, Pedilonum minutum is considered a rare and endangered species.
