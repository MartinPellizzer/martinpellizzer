Polythecium baronii

Polythecium baronii, commonly known as the Baron's polypore, is a rare and distinctive fungal species belonging to the Polyporaceae family.

It is characterized by its thick, woody, and sponge-like cap, which is typically light brown to yellowish in color. The fruiting body is usually found growing on dead and decaying wood, particularly in temperate forest environments. Its spore-producing surface, or hymenium, is located on the underside of the cap and is responsible for its unique ecological role in decomposition.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and often overlooked member of the fungal kingdom.
