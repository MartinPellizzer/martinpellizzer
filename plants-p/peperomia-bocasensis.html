Peperomia bocasensis

Peperomia bocasensis, commonly known as the Bocas Island peperomia, is a compact, low-growing plant native to the tropical regions of Central America.

It features thick, waxy leaves that are typically dark green with a glossy texture, often adorned with subtle variegation. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually greenish-white in color, which appear in clusters along the stems. It thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a suitable choice for indoor cultivation.

Peperomia bocasensis is relatively low maintenance and resistant to pests, making it a popular choice for plant enthusiasts.
