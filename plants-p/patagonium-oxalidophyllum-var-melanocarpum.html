Patagonium oxalidophyllum var. melanocarpum

Patagonium oxalidophyllum var. melanocarpum, commonly known as the black-fruited Patagonian oxalid, is a member of the Oxalidaceae family.

It is characterized by its striking, palmate leaves with a deep green color and a velvety texture. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by dark, almost black berries. These berries are edible and have a tart, acidic flavor.

It is native to the southern Andes and thrives in temperate, high-altitude regions with well-drained soil.
