Parahebe nivea

Parahebe nivea, commonly known as the silver parahebe, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It is a low-growing, evergreen shrub characterized by its dense, silver-gray foliage. The plant produces small, bell-shaped white or pale blue flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Parahebe nivea is often used in rock gardens and as a ground cover due to its ornamental appeal and hardy nature.
