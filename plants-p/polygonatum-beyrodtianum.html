Polygonatum beyrodtianum

Polygonatum beyrodtianum, commonly known as the Chinese Solomon's Seal, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to China.

It belongs to the Asparagaceae family and is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and form dense, grass-like foliage.

Polygonatum beyrodtianum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and traditional medicinal uses.
