Polygonum novoascanicum subsp. cretaceum

Polygonum novoascanicum subsp.

cretaceum, commonly known as the Cretaceous knotweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States. It belongs to the family Polygonaceae, which includes plants characterized by their distinctive nodes and often triangular stems.

The species is distinguished by its pale green, hairy stems and small, clustered flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in riparian zones and along stream banks.

The plant's leaves are alternate, ovate to lanceolate, and have prominent veins, contributing to its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for invertebrates.
