Pollia cyanococca

Pollia cyanococca, commonly known as the blue orchid, is a rare and striking plant native to the rainforests of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant blue petals, which are unusually pigmented due to a unique type of anthocyanin. The plant is known for its intricate floral structure, with a long, slender spur that houses nectar to attract specific pollinators.

Despite its beauty, Pollia cyanococca is highly sought after, making it a challenging species to cultivate and conserve.
