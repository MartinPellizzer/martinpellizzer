Phlox erianthema

Phlox erianthema, commonly known as the meadow phlox, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Polemoniaceae family and is characterized by its showy, clustered flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in open woodlands, meadows, and along stream banks, preferring well-drained soils. Its foliage is lance-shaped and often covered in a fine, white pubescence.

The flowers are usually pink to purple in color, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
