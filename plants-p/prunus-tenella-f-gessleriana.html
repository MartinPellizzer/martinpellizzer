Prunus tenella f. gessleriana

Prunus tenella f. gessleriana, commonly known as the dwarf cherry or miniature cherry, is a compact, ornamental shrub native to Japan.

It features small, rounded leaves that are typically a glossy dark green, providing a dense, bushy appearance. The plant produces delicate pink or white flowers in spring, followed by small, edible berries. It is well-suited for container gardening or as a specimen plant in small gardens due to its slow growth and hardy nature.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for those seeking a low-maintenance, visually appealing shrub.
