Piper san-juananum

Piper san-juananum, commonly known as the San Juan pepper or San Juan vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Piper genus in the family Piperaceae, which includes many species of flowering plants. The plant produces small, green berries that turn red when mature, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a slightly peppery scent when crushed.

The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.
