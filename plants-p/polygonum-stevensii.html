Polygonum stevensii

Polygonum stevensii, commonly known as Stevens' knotweed, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist habitats such as wetlands, stream banks, and marshes. Its leaves are alternate, ovate to elliptic, and often have a reddish or purple hue on the underside.

It is valued in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife species.
