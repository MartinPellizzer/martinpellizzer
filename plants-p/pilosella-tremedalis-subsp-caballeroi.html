Pilosella tremedalis subsp. caballeroi

Pilosella tremedalis subsp.

caballeroi, commonly known as the Sierra Nevada groundsel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States. It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes.

The plant features a basal rosette of narrow, silver-gray leaves with a fuzzy texture. Its flowering stems bear small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence.

It is adapted to dry, high-altitude environments and plays a role in local ecosystems by providing nectar for pollinators.
