Primula macrocarpa

Primula macrocarpa, commonly known as the large-fruited primrose, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of East Asia.

It features a low-growing rosette of broad, dark green leaves and produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and prefers well-drained, loamy soils. Its flowers bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to garden conditions.
