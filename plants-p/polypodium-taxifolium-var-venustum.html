Polypodium taxifolium var. venustum

Polypodium taxifolium var. venustum, commonly known as the golden polypody, is a fern species native to the eastern United States and parts of Mexico.

It is characterized by its delicate, feathery fronds that are typically light green to golden in color. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often growing on rocks or in the understory of forests. Its rhizomes are wiry and help anchor it to surfaces, while its spore-producing structures are located on the underside of the fronds.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in shaded garden settings.
