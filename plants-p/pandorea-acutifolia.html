Pandorea acutifolia

Pandorea acutifolia, commonly known as the sharp-leaved pandorea, is a climbing shrub native to Australia.

It features woody stems that twine around supports and has opposite, elliptical leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces fragrant, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically pink or purple, blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it suitable for garden borders or trellises.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is often cultivated in warm climates.
