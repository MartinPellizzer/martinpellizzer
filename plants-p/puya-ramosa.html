Puya ramosa

Puya ramosa, commonly known as the Andean torch, is a striking plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is a member of the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its tall, spiky inflorescence that resembles a torch. The plant produces vibrant red or orange flowers that bloom in the spring, attracting various pollinators. Puya ramosa is adapted to cold, dry climates and can thrive in rocky soils at elevations above 3,000 meters.

Its unique appearance and ecological significance make it a notable species in its native habitat.
