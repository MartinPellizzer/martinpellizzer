Poa patagonica

Poa patagonica, commonly known as the Patagonian grass, is a species of flowering plant native to South America, particularly Argentina and Chile.

It is a member of the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and fine, green blades. The plant typically grows in temperate climates and is adapted to thrive in well-drained soils. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers, which are wind-pollinated.

Poa patagonica is often found in open areas such as meadows and grasslands, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
