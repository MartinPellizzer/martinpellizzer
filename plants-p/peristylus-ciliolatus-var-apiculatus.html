Peristylus ciliolatus var. apiculatus

Peristylus ciliolatus var. apiculatus, commonly known as the hairy peristylus, is a species of flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and small, clustered flowers that bloom in spring. The leaves are typically hairy, giving the plant its common name, and they are often arranged in a whorled pattern along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
