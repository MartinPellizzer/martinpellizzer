Potentilla × diversifolia subsp. ranunculus

Potentilla × diversifolia subsp.

ranunculus, commonly known as the variable cinquefoil, is a hybrid plant characterized by its variable morphology. It typically features compound leaves with five to seven leaflets, often with a serrated or lobed edge.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers with five petals, blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wet meadows or along stream banks.

Its botanical variability makes it a subject of interest in taxonomic studies.
