Penstemon clutei

Penstemon clutei, commonly known as Clute's beardtongue, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces showy, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to purple. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy choice for xeriscaping.

Penstemon clutei is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native plant gardens.
