Pycnocoma gigantea

Pycnocoma gigantea, commonly known as the giant pycnocoma, is a striking plant native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, fleshy, spiny leaves that store water, adapting to its harsh, dry environment. The plant produces showy, yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. Its root system is deep and robust, allowing it to access groundwater in nutrient-poor soils.

Pycnocoma gigantea is often cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique appearance and resilience.
