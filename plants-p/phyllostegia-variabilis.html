Phyllostegia variabilis

Phyllostegia variabilis, commonly known as the variable mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its opposite, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink. It typically grows in moist, wooded areas and is often found in wetlands or along streambanks.

Phyllostegia variabilis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a food source for pollinators.
