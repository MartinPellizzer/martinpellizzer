Poa australis var. billardieri

Poa australis var. billardieri, commonly known as the New Zealand hair grass, is a perennial grass species native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and dense, fine-textured foliage. The plant typically grows in coastal and wetland environments, thriving in moist, well-drained soils. Its flowering stems produce small, spike-like inflorescences that are often used in horticultural settings.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat.
