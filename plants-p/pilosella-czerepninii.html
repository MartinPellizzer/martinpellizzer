Pilosella czerepninii

Pilosella czerepninii, commonly known as the Czerepnin's pearly everlasting, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and small, daisy-like yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high elevations, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its scientific name honors the botanist Vladimir Czerepnin, who contributed significantly to the study of alpine flora.

Pilosella czerepninii is often found in alpine meadows and is a subject of interest for its unique morphology and ecological significance.
