Podocarpus taxodioides var. gracilis

Podocarpus taxodioides var. gracilis, commonly known as the slender podocarp, is a species of conifer native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its narrow, needle-like leaves that are typically arranged in a spiral pattern along the stems. The plant produces small, woody cones that contain seeds, a feature typical of many coniferous species. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland forests.

The slender podocarp is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in horticultural settings.
