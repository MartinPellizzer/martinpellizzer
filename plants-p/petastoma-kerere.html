Petastoma kerere

Petastoma kerere, commonly known as the African bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features erect, branching stems that are covered in fine hairs, and its leaves are lance-shaped with serrated edges. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue, violet, or white, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Petastoma kerere is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
