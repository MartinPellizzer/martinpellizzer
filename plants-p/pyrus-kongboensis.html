Pyrus kongboensis

Pyrus kongboensis, commonly known as the Yunnan pear, is a deciduous tree native to China, specifically found in the Yunnan province.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its ornamental value due to its showy white flowers and attractive foliage. The plant produces small, edible fruits that are similar to pears but are less commonly cultivated for consumption.

It thrives in temperate climates and is often grown in gardens for its aesthetic appeal and ecological benefits.
