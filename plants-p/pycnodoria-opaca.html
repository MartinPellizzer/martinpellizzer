Pycnodoria opaca

Pycnodoria opaca, commonly known as the denseleaf shrub, is a perennial plant native to the temperate regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact, woody stems and dense, dark green foliage. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Due to its ornamental value and hardiness, it is a popular choice for gardeners in cooler climates.
