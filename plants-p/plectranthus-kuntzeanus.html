Plectranthus kuntzeanus

Plectranthus kuntzeanus, commonly known as the African violet, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes other aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and produces showy, violet-colored flowers. Its leaves are typically velvety and have a distinctive shape, often with a slightly heart-shaped base.

Plectranthus kuntzeanus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated as a houseplant due to its adaptability and ease of care.
