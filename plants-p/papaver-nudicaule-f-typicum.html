Papaver nudicaule f. typicum

Papaver nudicaule f. typicum, commonly known as the common poppy, is a flowering plant characterized by its striking red or pink petals and a prominent central stigma.

It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in meadows and grasslands. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, dark seeds. Its flowers typically bloom in the spring and early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is known for its ornamental value and has been cultivated for both garden use and traditional medicinal purposes.
