Papaver napaulense

Papaver napaulense, commonly known as the Indian poppy, is a flowering plant native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the family Papaveraceae and is characterized by its striking red or orange flowers with a prominent central stigma. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid regions and has a taproot system that allows it to thrive in poor soils. Its flowers bloom in spring and are followed by capsule fruits that release seeds through a unique mechanism.

The plant contains alkaloids, which have been studied for their potential medicinal properties.
