Prunus axitliana

Prunus axitliana, commonly known as the Mexican plum, is a species of flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, yellowish fruits that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans. It is often found in dry, rocky soils and can thrive in a variety of climates.

Prunus axitliana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
