Papaver radicatum var. labradoricum

Papaver radicatum var. labradoricum, commonly known as Labrador poppy, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Arctic and subarctic regions of North America.

It features striking blue to violet petals and a yellow center, with lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, thriving in cool, moist environments. It produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, dark seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and hardiness.
