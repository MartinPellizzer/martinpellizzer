Passiflora leptomischa

Passiflora leptomischa, commonly known as the slender passionflower, is a climbing vine native to tropical and subtropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, intricate flowers that feature five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments surrounding a central cluster of stamens. The plant produces edible, blue to purple berries that are popular in local cuisines. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped with a rough texture and are often used in traditional medicine.

Passiflora leptomischa thrives in humid environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
