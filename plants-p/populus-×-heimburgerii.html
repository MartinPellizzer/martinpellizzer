Populus × heimburgerii

Populus × heimburgerii, commonly known as the Heimburger poplar, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between the trembling aspen (Populus tremuloides) and the balsam poplar (Populus balsamifera).

It is a deciduous tree characterized by its smooth, gray bark and heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a white, waxy bloom. The plant produces small, fluffy seeds that are dispersed by the wind, aiding in its propagation. It is typically found in moist, well-drained soils in northern regions of North America.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
