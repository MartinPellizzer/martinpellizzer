Pera pulchrifolia

Pera pulchrifolia, commonly known as the beautiful leaf pera, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves that are often a deep green with a slight variegation. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in the spring. It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and requires partial to full shade for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
