Plumeria casildensis

Plumeria casildensis, commonly known as the Casildensis frangipani, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean islands.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its fragrant, showy flowers that range in color from white to yellow. The plant typically grows as a small tree or shrub, with smooth, pale green bark and pinnately compound leaves. Its flowers bloom in clusters and are often used in traditional crafts and perfumery due to their strong, sweet scent.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in tropical gardens.
