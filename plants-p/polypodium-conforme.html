Polypodium conforme

Polypodium conforme, commonly known as the crested polypody, is a species of fern native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its distinctive, finely divided fronds that often have a crested or curled appearance. The plant typically grows on rocks or tree trunks, thriving in moist, shaded environments. Its rhizomes are fibrous and help it anchor to substrates while absorbing nutrients.

Polypodium conforme is valued in horticulture for its ornamental foliage and is relatively easy to care for in the right conditions.
