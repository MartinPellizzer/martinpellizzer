Poivrea grandiflora

Poivrea grandiflora, commonly known as the large-flowered winterberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking red berries that persist through the winter, providing food for birds and a seasonal visual interest in gardens. The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges and produces small, inconspicuous flowers in the spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full sun.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is a popular choice for native plant gardens.
