Polygala virgata var. virgata

Polygala virgata var. virgata, commonly known as the Virgin's tears or the fairy wand, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa, including South Africa.

It is a member of the Polygalaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves and striking, spiny seed pods. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun. It produces small, pale yellow or greenish flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its ornamental value and unique appearance, it is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
