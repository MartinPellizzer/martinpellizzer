Potentilla alborzensis

Potentilla alborzensis, commonly known as the alpine potentilla, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a low-growing shrub that typically reaches heights of up to 30 centimeters. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are simple, elliptical, and covered in a fine layer of white hairs.

Potentilla alborzensis thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments.
