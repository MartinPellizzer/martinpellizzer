Pilea filipes

Pilea filipes, commonly known as the Chinese money plant, is a species of flowering plant in the family Araceae.

It is native to China and is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that resemble small, flat coins. The plant produces clusters of small, inconspicuous white flowers that are followed by bright orange berries. It thrives in bright, indirect light and prefers well-draining soil.

Pilea filipes is a popular houseplant due to its attractive foliage and ease of care.
