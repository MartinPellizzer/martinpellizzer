Pycreus poikilostachys

Pycreus poikilostachys, commonly known as the variable feathergrass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and feathery, branched inflorescences that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a soft, slightly hairy texture.

Pycreus poikilostachys is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate, airy foliage.
