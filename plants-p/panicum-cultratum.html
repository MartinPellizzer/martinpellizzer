Panicum cultratum

Panicum cultratum, commonly known as the cutleaf panicgrass, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often finely toothed along the edges. The plant produces small, pale green to brown spikelets that are arranged in panicles, typical of the grass family. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, often found in open woodlands and disturbed areas.

Its botanical features make it a valuable plant for ecological studies and ornamental gardening.
