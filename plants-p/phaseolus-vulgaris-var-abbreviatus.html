Phaseolus vulgaris var. abbreviatus

Phaseolus vulgaris var. abbreviatus, commonly known as the dwarf bean, is a variety of the common bean plant characterized by its compact growth habit.

It typically produces small, round pods that mature to a yellowish or brownish color. The plant is native to the Americas and is cultivated for its edible seeds, which are used in various cuisines. Its botanical features include trifoliate leaves and an indeterminate growth pattern.

This variety is valued for its adaptability to different growing conditions and its use in both culinary and agricultural contexts.
