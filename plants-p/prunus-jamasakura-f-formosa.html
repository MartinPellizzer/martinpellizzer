Prunus jamasakura f. formosa

Prunus jamasakura f. formosa, commonly known as the Japanese cherry blossom, is a cultivar of the Prunus jamasakura species.

It is characterized by its delicate, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant is a deciduous shrub or small tree, typically reaching heights of 5 to 10 meters. Its leaves are ovate with a smooth texture and turn yellow in the fall.

The botanical features of this plant make it a popular ornamental species in gardens and public spaces.
