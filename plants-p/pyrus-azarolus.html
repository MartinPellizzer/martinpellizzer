Pyrus azarolus

Pyrus azarolus, commonly known as the Persian white mulberry, is a deciduous tree native to the Caucasus region and parts of Turkey.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and heart-shaped leaves that are typically lobed. The tree produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters, followed by edible, sweet berries that are highly valued in traditional medicine. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit.

The plant is also noted for its ability to tolerate drought and poor soil conditions.
