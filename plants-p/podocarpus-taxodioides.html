Podocarpus taxodioides

Podocarpus taxodioides, commonly known as the Chinese podocarp or the evergreen tree, is a coniferous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and a slow growth rate, making it suitable for ornamental landscaping. The plant produces small, cone-like structures that contain its seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Its leaves are typically needle-like and remain on the tree for several years, contributing to its evergreen nature.

Podocarpus taxodioides thrives in humid, subtropical climates and is often used in horticulture for its aesthetic and ecological benefits.
