Prunus damascena

Prunus damascena, commonly known as the sweet cherry or damask rose, is a species of shrub native to the eastern Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its fragrant, pink to white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant produces small, edible fruits known as cherries, which are widely cultivated for their sweet flavor. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a slightly serrated edge.

Prunus damascena is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and historical use in perfumery.
