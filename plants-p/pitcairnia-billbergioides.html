Pitcairnia billbergioides

Pitcairnia billbergioides, commonly known as the Brazilian pineapple plant, is a species of bromeliad native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that are thick and covered in a dense layer of trichomes, which help in water retention. The plant produces striking, spathaceous inflorescences that are often brightly colored, attracting pollinators such as hummingbirds. Its leaves are typically dark green with a waxy coating, and the plant is adapted to grow in shaded, humid environments.

Pitcairnia billbergioides is valued both for its ornamental appeal and its role in local ecosystems.
