Psyllocarpus cordifolius

Psyllocarpus cordifolius, commonly known as the heart-leaved psyllocarpus, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by woody, capsule-like fruits. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and traditional herbal practices.
