Paspalum setaceum

Paspalum setaceum, commonly known as the slender grass or silky paspalum, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions and full sun.

Paspalum setaceum is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support local wildlife.
