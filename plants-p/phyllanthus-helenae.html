Phyllanthus helenae

Phyllanthus helenae, commonly known as Helen's spurge, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing shrub that typically reaches heights of about 30 centimeters. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in the spring. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and have a smooth texture with a slightly waxy appearance.

Phyllanthus helenae is adapted to dry, rocky soils and is often found in coastal areas and along cliffs.
