Persica vulgaris var. nucipersica

Persica vulgaris var. nucipersica, commonly known as the common peach.

This variety is a cultivar of the peach tree, characterized by its fruit-bearing branches and fuzzy, yellow-orange skin. The plant produces sweet, juicy fruits that are rich in vitamins and antioxidants. It thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil and full sun exposure.

The botanical features include simple leaves, fragrant flowers, and a woody stem structure typical of the Prunus genus.
