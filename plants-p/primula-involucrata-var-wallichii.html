Primula involucrata var. wallichii

Primula involucrata var. wallichii, commonly known as the Himalayan primrose, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its clustered, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink, white, and purple. The plant has heart-shaped, hairy leaves that are adapted to the cold, arid conditions of its mountainous habitat. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with suitable climates.
