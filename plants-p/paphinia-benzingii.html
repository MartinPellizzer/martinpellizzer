Paphinia benzingii

Paphinia benzingii, commonly known as the Benzing's paphiopedilum, is a terrestrial orchid native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It features a slender, upright stem with several fleshy, elliptical leaves that are typically dark green in color. The plant produces showy, lip-shaped flowers in shades of yellow and white, often with a distinctive purple or red callus on the lip. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Paphinia benzingii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is a popular choice among orchid enthusiasts.
