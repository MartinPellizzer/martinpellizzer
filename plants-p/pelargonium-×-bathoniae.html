Pelargonium × bathoniae

Pelargonium × bathoniae, commonly known as the Bathonia geranium, is a hybrid plant in the Geraniaceae family.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and vibrant, showy flowers that come in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant produces small, five-petaled blooms that are typically borne in clusters along the stems. Its leaves are typically rounded or heart-shaped, with a slightly hairy texture.

Pelargonium × bathoniae is often cultivated as a decorative plant in gardens and containers due to its ornamental value and adaptability to various growing conditions.
