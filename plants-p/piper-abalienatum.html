Piper abalienatum

Piper abalienatum, commonly known as the yellow pepper vine, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many species of pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its slender stems and heart-shaped leaves that are often a vibrant green. Its small, yellow flowers are followed by berries that turn from green to red as they mature.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
