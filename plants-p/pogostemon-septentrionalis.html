Pogostemon septentrionalis

Pogostemon septentrionalis, commonly known as the northern pogostemon, is a herbaceous plant native to the temperate regions of East Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along stream banks. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, tubular flowers in clusters. Its root system is fibrous and can form dense colonies, contributing to soil stabilization.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional medicinal uses in local cultures.
