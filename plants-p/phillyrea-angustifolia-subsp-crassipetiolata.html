Phillyrea angustifolia subsp. crassipetiolata

Phillyrea angustifolia subsp.

crassipetiolata, commonly known as the broad-leaved Phillyrea, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region. It features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often broader and more robust than those of the typical Phillyrea angustifolia.

The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. Its foliage is evergreen, with a leathery texture and a distinct aromatic scent.

The subspecies is adapted to well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal habitats.
