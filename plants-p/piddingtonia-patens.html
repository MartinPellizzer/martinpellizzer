Piddingtonia patens

Piddingtonia patens, commonly known as the narrow-leaved piddingtonia, is a small, evergreen shrub native to the arid regions of Western Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, powdery bloom, which helps reduce water loss in its harsh environment. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, typically blooming during the cooler months. It is adapted to low-nutrient soils and is highly drought-tolerant, making it well-suited for xeriscaping.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
