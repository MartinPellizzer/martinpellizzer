Piper subhirsutum

Piper subhirsutum, commonly known as the hairy pepper vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many species of pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves with a rough texture. Its small, greenish-white flowers are followed by berries that turn red when mature.

Piper subhirsutum is often used in traditional medicine for its potential anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties.
