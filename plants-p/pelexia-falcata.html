Pelexia falcata

Pelexia falcata, commonly known as the narrowleaf pelexia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white or pale green flowers in late spring to early summer. Pelexia falcata typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and wet meadows.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
