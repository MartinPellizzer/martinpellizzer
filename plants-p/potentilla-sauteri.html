Potentilla sauteri

Potentilla sauteri, commonly known as the Sauter's cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming structure. The plant features small, yellow flowers that bloom in the summer, typically arranged in clusters. Its leaves are compound, with five leaflets, giving it the common name "cinquefoil.".

Potentilla sauteri is adapted to harsh, alpine environments and is often found in rocky or gravelly soils.
