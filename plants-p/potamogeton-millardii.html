Potamogeton millardii

Potamogeton millardii, commonly known as narrowleaf pondweed, is a freshwater aquatic plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its slender, ribbon-like leaves that are typically less than 1 centimeter wide. This plant thrives in slow-moving to still waters, such as ponds, lakes, and wetlands. Its small, green flowers appear in clusters and are usually submerged or floating.

Potamogeton millardii plays a vital role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
