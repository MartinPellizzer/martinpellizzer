Pleurothallis tobarii

Pleurothallis tobarii, commonly known as the Tobar's orchid, is a species native to the cloud forests of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its small, delicate flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are usually pale green or white, with intricate patterns that aid in pollination by specific insect species.

Due to its habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
