Physostegia speciosa var. glabriflora

Physostegia speciosa var. glabriflora, commonly known as the smoothflower false dragonhead, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant features showy, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, arranged in whorls along the upper stem. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and have a slightly hairy texture.

This variety is distinguished by its smooth, glabrous flowers, which set it apart from other variants of Physostegia speciosa.
