Parodia stuemeri

Parodia stuemeri, commonly known as the "Stuemmer's Pincushion Cactus," is a species native to the arid regions of South America.

It is a small, globular cactus with a dense covering of white to pale blue spines, giving it a pincushion-like appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers during the warm season, typically in spring or early summer. It has a shallow root system adapted to absorb moisture from the soil quickly.

Parodia stuemeri is known for its slow growth and is often cultivated as a decorative succulent in dry environments.
