Pogonia hookeriana

Pogonia hookeriana, commonly known as the showy lady's slipper, is a rare and striking orchid native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its large, intricate pink and white floral structure, which resembles a lady's slipper. The plant typically grows in moist, acidic woodlands and requires specific environmental conditions to thrive. Its flowers are pollinated by bees and other insects, and it has a symbiotic relationship with mycorrhizal fungi.

Due to habitat loss and overcollection, it is considered endangered in many regions.
