Parthenium argentatum var. gracile

Parthenium argentatum var. gracile, commonly known as the silverleaf nightshade, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, fuzzy hairs, and its small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is an annual or biennial herb that thrives in arid and semi-arid environments. It has a shallow root system and is often found in disturbed soils, such as along roadsides or in dry fields.

Due to its potential to cause skin irritation and allergic reactions, it is considered a weed in many regions.
