Pisonia villosa

Pisonia villosa, commonly known as the silky pisonia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its showy, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, often with a red calyx. The plant has compound leaves with soft, hairy surfaces, giving it a silky appearance. It thrives in coastal and disturbed soils, often growing in sandy or limestone areas.

Pisonia villosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens in warm climates.
