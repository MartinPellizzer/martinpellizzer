Plantago gentianoides

Plantago gentianoides, commonly known as the gentian-like plantain, is a flowering plant native to the northern regions of North America.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from blue to purple, often resembling those of the gentian genus. It thrives in moist, open habitats such as wet meadows and streambanks.

The species is noted for its ecological importance, providing nectar for pollinators and serving as a host plant for certain butterfly species.
