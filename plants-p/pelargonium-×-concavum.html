Pelargonium × concavum

Pelargonium × concavum, commonly known as the Zonal Geranium, is a hybrid species within the Geraniaceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive, rounded leaves with colored zones and showy, clustered flowers in shades of red, pink, and white. Native to South Africa, this plant thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and containers. Its compact growth habit and long blooming season contribute to its ornamental value.

The plant is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
