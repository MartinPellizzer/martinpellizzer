Potamogeton fibrillosus

Potamogeton fibrillosus, commonly known as the fine-thread pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in North America.

It has long, slender, thread-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a fibrous appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in spike-like inflorescences. It thrives in shallow, slow-moving waters and plays a role in providing habitat for aquatic organisms.

Its presence is often an indicator of good water quality in its ecosystem.
