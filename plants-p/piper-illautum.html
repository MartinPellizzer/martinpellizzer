Piper illautum

Piper illautum, commonly known as the Illautum pepper, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil and Peru. The plant is characterized by its climbing habit, with elongated stems that often twine around supports. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a glossy surface, while the small, greenish-white flowers are clustered in spikes.

The fruit, a small, three-lobed berry, contains the aromatic seeds used in traditional medicine and cuisine.
