Pinus striata

Pinus striata, commonly known as the stripeleaf pine, is a species of evergreen conifer native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, pyramidal shape and needle-like leaves that are typically arranged in bundles of five. The bark of Pinus striata is thin and scaly, often appearing reddish-brown in color. Its cones are small and woody, containing seeds that are dispersed by wind.

This plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.
