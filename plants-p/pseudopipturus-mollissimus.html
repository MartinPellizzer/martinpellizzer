Pseudopipturus mollissimus

Pseudopipturus mollissimus, commonly known as the velvet fern, is a unique plant native to New Zealand.

It is a member of the fern family and is characterized by its soft, velvety fronds that give it its common name. The plant has a creeping growth habit and forms dense mats on the forest floor. Its leaves are usually dark green and have a leathery texture.

Pseudopipturus mollissimus is adapted to grow in shaded, moist environments and is often found in native New Zealand forests.
