Potentilla verna var. tabernaemontani

Potentilla verna var. tabernaemontani, commonly known as the spring cinquefoil, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, typically from March to May. The plant has compound leaves with five leaflets, which are oval and slightly hairy. It thrives in well-drained soils and partial to full sun, often found in woodland edges and rocky habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting early pollinators.
