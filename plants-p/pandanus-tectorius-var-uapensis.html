Pandanus tectorius var. uapensis

Pandanus tectorius var. uapensis, commonly known as the Tahitian pandanus, is a tropical plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It is a member of the Pandanaceae family and is characterized by its long, narrow leaves that are often used in traditional crafts and cooking. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and can thrive in sandy soils with high salinity. Its robust structure and adaptability make it a common sight in coastal landscapes across its native range.

The leaves are also used for thatching and as a source of fiber in various island communities.
