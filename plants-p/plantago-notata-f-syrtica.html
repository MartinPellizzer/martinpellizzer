Plantago notata f. syrtica

Plantago notata f. syrtica, commonly known as the Syrtic plantain, is a variant of the common plantain characterized by its distinctive botanical features.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, sandy soils, often found in coastal and arid regions. The plant produces narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture and prominent veins. Its inflorescence consists of a dense spike of small, greenish flowers that develop into capsule fruits.

This subspecies is valued for its hardy nature and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
