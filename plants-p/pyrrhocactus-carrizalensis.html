Pyrrhocactus carrizalensis

Pyrrhocactus carrizalensis, commonly known as the Carrizalensis firecactus, is a species of cactus native to the arid regions of northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its thick, ribbed stems that store water, adapting to its harsh environment. The plant produces vibrant yellow or orange flowers during the summer months, adding a striking visual contrast to its green foliage. It thrives in full sunlight and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for succulent gardens.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species in its native habitat.
