Pelargonium grenvillei

Pelargonium grenvillei, commonly known as the Grenville's geranium, is a species native to South Africa.

It is a flowering plant belonging to the Geraniaceae family, characterized by its upright growth habit and showy blooms. The plant features serrated leaves and produces clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
