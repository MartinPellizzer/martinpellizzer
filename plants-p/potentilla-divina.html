Potentilla divina

Potentilla divina, commonly known as the sacred cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, five-petaled yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, preferring well-drained soils and full sunlight. Its leaves are compound, consisting of five to seven oval-shaped leaflets, and it has a low-growing, spreading habit.

Potentilla divina is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
