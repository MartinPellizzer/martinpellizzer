Passovia orinocensis

Passovia orinocensis, commonly known as the Orinoco passiflora, is a species of flowering plant native to South America, particularly Venezuela and Colombia.

It belongs to the Passifloraceae family, which is known for its showy, intricate flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, featuring heart-shaped leaves and clusters of vibrant, colorful flowers. Its fruit is a yellow, fleshy berry that contains numerous small, edible seeds.

Passovia orinocensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
