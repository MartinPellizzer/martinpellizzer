Painteria revoluta

Painteria revoluta, commonly known as the velvet tangerine, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety orange flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has glossy, dark green leaves and produces small, round fruits that are similar in appearance to tangerines. Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
