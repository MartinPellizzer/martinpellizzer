Pleurothallis pachyglossa

Pleurothallis pachyglossa, commonly known as the broad-lipped pleurothallis, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and elongated, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are typically arranged in a spike, which bloom during the wet season. It thrives in shaded, humid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its unique appearance and relatively easy care requirements, it is a popular choice among orchid enthusiasts.
