Pterocephalus gedrosiacus

Pterocephalus gedrosiacus, commonly known as the desert candle, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, candle-like stems that can grow up to three meters in height. The plant produces yellow or orange flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by distinctive, spiky seed pods. It has a deep root system that allows it to survive in extreme drought conditions.

The plant is adapted to harsh environments and is often used in traditional medicine.
