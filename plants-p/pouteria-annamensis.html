Pouteria annamensis

Pouteria annamensis, commonly known as the Annam tamarind, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by edible, round fruits with a sweet and sour taste.

Its fruit is often used in traditional cuisines and has potential applications in pharmaceutical and cosmetic industries.
