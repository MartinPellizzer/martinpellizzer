Phlomis lychnitis

Phlomis lychnitis, commonly known as the candlestick phlomis, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It features upright, sparsely branched stems that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces showy, pink to purple flower clusters at the ends of its stems, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Phlomis lychnitis thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.
