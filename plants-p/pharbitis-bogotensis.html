Pharbitis bogotensis

Pharbitis bogotensis, commonly known as the Andean morning glory, is a climbing vine native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from violet to blue. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain seeds with a hard coat. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in some regions for its aesthetic appeal.
