Pongamia rigida

Pongamia rigida, commonly known as the rigid pongam, is a small evergreen tree native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is notable for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil through its root nodules, making it beneficial for soil enrichment. Its seeds produce a dark, oily pulp that has been used in traditional medicine and as a source of biofuel.

The tree is also valued for its hard, durable wood and its adaptability to various soil types.
