Populus scythica

Populus scythica, commonly known as the Siberian aspen, is a deciduous tree native to the cold regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and heart-shaped leaves that turn yellow in the autumn. The plant produces small, catkin-like flowers and is adapted to harsh, arctic and alpine environments. It plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.

Populus scythica is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
