Phaseolus ricciardianus

Phaseolus ricciardianus, commonly known as the yellow bean or Ricciardian bean, is a flowering plant species native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant produces yellow or green pods that contain small, edible beans used in traditional cuisines. Its leaves are trifoliate, and it thrives in warm, tropical climates with well-drained soil.

This species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and agricultural significance.
