Polygonum sagittatum var. gracilentum

Polygonum sagittatum var. gracilentum, commonly known as the arrowhead plant or arrowleaf plant, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It is characterized by its distinctive arrow-shaped leaves that are usually a vibrant green with a glossy texture. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in spikes during the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and is often found in wetlands, ditches, and along stream banks.

Its adaptability makes it a common sight in wetland ecosystems across its native range.
