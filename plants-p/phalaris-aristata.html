Phalaris aristata

Phalaris aristata, commonly known as purple grass or tall false brome, is a perennial grass native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can grow up to 1.5 meters in height, with narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces dense clusters of small, spike-like flower heads that range in color from purple to pink, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open fields, meadows, and along roadsides.

Its ornamental value and ability to stabilize soil make it a popular choice for landscaping and ecological restoration projects.
