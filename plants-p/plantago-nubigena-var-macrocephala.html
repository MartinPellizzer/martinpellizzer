Plantago nubigena var. macrocephala

Plantago nubigena var. macrocephala, commonly known as the giant plantain, is a variant of the plantain species native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its large, broad leaves that can reach up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces showy, pale green to yellowish inflorescences that are often mistaken for flowers but are actually spikes of small, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils and is often found in meadows, along stream banks, and in wetlands.

This variant is valued for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies for centuries.
