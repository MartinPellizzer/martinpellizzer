Potentilla × suberecta

Potentilla × suberecta, commonly known as the shrub cinquefoil, is a hybrid species within the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its compact, upright growth habit and produces clusters of small, yellow flowers. The plant typically has finely toothed, oval-shaped leaves that are covered in a soft, woolly pubescence. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens and borders.

Potentilla × suberecta is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardy nature in temperate climates.
