Plagianthus monoica

Plagianthus monoica, commonly known as the solitary plume, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its most distinctive feature is its single, elongated flower head that appears at the top of the stem.

Plagianthus monoica is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its unique and elegant appearance.
