Phlomis bruguieri

Phlomis bruguieri, commonly known as the Spanish thistle, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and North Africa.

It is characterized by its gray-green, fuzzy leaves and tall spikes of purple or pink flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is drought-tolerant, making it well adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their astringent properties.

The plant is also valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
