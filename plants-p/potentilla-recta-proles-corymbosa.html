Potentilla recta proles corymbosa

Potentilla recta proles corymbosa, commonly known as the clustered cinquefoil, is a perennial flowering plant native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are palmately divided into five leaflets, and it produces small, berry-like fruits that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans.

It is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to stabilize soil in wet areas.
