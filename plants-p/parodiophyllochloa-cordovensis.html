Parodiophyllochloa cordovensis

Parodiophyllochloa cordovensis, commonly known as the Cordovense grass, is a species of grass native to South America, particularly Argentina.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and narrow, linear leaves. The plant typically thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and shrublands. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets that are loosely arranged, and it produces small, wind-pollinated flowers.

Due to its ecological significance and ornamental potential, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and for erosion control.
