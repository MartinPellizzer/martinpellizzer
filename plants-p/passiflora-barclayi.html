Passiflora barclayi

Passiflora barclayi, commonly known as Barclay's passionflower, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, complex flowers that feature a unique combination of petals, sepals, and modified leaves. The plant produces a showy, five-petaled crown that is often adorned with intricate filaments and a central reproductive structure. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped with a velvety texture, and the plant thrives in humid, shaded environments.

Passiflora barclayi is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens.
