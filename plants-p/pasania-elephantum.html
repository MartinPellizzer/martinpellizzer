Pasania elephantum

Pasania elephantum, commonly known as the elephant tree, is a unique species native to the tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, woody trunk and sparse foliage, which helps it survive in arid environments. The plant has a slow growth rate and can live for several decades, making it a long-lived species. Its leaves are typically small and clustered near the base of the trunk, while its flowers are small and inconspicuous.

Pasania elephantum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in dry, rocky gardens.
