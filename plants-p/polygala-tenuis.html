Polygala tenuis

Polygala tenuis, commonly known as slender milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, delicate flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are alternate and often have a hairy texture, while the flowers are usually pink or purple in color.

Polygala tenuis is a perennial species that plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native environment.
