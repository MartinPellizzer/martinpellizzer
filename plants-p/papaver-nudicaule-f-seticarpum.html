Papaver nudicaule f. seticarpum

Papaver nudicaule f. seticarpum, commonly known as the hairy poppy, is a variant of the common poppy characterized by its distinctive hairy foliage and stems.

This plant is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in well-drained, sunny soils. Its flowers are typically bright red or orange, with a papery texture and a central stigma that contrasts with the petals. The leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine, soft hairs, giving the plant its "hairy" appearance.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and is also studied for its potential medicinal properties.
