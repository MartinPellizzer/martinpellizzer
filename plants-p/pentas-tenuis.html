Pentas tenuis

Pentas tenuis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved pentas, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its delicate, five-petaled flowers that typically range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are usually arranged oppositely along the stems. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its showy blooms.

The flowers are known to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance.
