Polygala violacea var. martiana

Polygala violacea var. martiana, commonly known as the violet milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia and the Middle East.

It typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, violet-blue flowers. The plant features pinnate leaves with numerous leaflets, and its flowers are often clustered in racemes. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.

The species is also of interest in ecological studies due to its specific habitat requirements and limited distribution.
