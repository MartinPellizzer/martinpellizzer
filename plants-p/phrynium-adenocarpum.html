Phrynium adenocarpum

Phrynium adenocarpum, commonly known as the giant arrowroot, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 1 meter in length. The plant produces clusters of yellowish-green flowers followed by orange or red berries containing seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often grown for its starchy roots, which are used in cooking.

The plant also has ornamental value due to its striking foliage.
