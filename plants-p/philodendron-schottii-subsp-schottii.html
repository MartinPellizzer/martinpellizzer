Philodendron schottii subsp. schottii

Philodendron schottii subsp.

schottii, commonly known as the Schott's philodendron, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of South America. It features heart-shaped, glossy green leaves that are typically dark green with a slightly waxy texture.

The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by red, fleshy fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in warm, humid environments with bright, indirect light.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental foliage and is a popular choice for indoor gardening.
