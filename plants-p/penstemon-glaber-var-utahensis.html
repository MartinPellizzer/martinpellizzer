Penstemon glaber var. utahensis

Penstemon glaber var. utahensis, commonly known as Utah beardtongue, is a perennial plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that range in color from purple to pink, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

This variety is valued for its drought tolerance and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
