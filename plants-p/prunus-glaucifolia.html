Prunus glaucifolia

Prunus glaucifolia, commonly known as the silverleaf cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its grayish-green leaves that give it its common name, and its small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, edible fruits that are typically consumed by birds and other wildlife. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
