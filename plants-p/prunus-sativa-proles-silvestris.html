Prunus sativa proles silvestris

Prunus sativa proles silvestris, commonly known as the wild almond, is a species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is a small to medium-sized deciduous tree or shrub with simple, alternate leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape. The flowers are typically white or pale pink, appearing in clusters, and the fruit is a drupe with a hard pit enclosed in a fleshy outer layer. This plant prefers well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in woodland edges or rocky areas.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has historical uses in traditional medicine and as a source of edible seeds.
