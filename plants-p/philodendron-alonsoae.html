Philodendron alonsoae

Philodendron alonsoae, commonly known as the Alfonso's philodendron, is a tropical climbing plant native to the rainforests of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its distinctive inflorescence consisting of a spadix and a spathe. The plant features heart-shaped, glossy, and dark green leaves that are often variegated with light green or white markings. It thrives in warm, humid environments with bright, indirect light and well-draining soil.

Philodendron alonsoae is valued for its ornamental appeal and ease of care, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.
