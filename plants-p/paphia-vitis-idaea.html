Paphia vitis-idaea

Paphia vitis-idaea, commonly known as the crowberry, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the northern regions of North America and Eurasia.

It features small, dark blue to black berries that are edible and often used in jams and wines. The plant has needle-like leaves that are densely packed, providing dense ground cover. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in boreal and tundra ecosystems.

The crowberry plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing food for wildlife.
