Polgidon bulbosum

Polgidon bulbosum, commonly known as the bulbous polgidon, is a member of the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is characterized by its underground bulb, which serves as a storage organ for nutrients. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white and pink, typically in late spring or early summer. Its foliage consists of strap-like leaves that emerge from the base of the bulb.

Polgidon bulbosum is native to South Africa and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.
