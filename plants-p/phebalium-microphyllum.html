Phebalium microphyllum

Phebalium microphyllum, commonly known as the small-leaved hakea, is a shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves that are typically less than 2 centimeters in length. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which are often followed by woody, cylindrical fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to dry, sunny conditions.

Phebalium microphyllum is often used in native gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
