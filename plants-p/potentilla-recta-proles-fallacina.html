Potentilla recta proles fallacina

Potentilla recta proles fallacina, commonly known as the false silverweed, is a low-growing perennial plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its silver-gray foliage and clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats. Its stems are typically prostrate, allowing it to spread horizontally and form dense mats.

The species is known for its hardiness and ability to withstand drought conditions.
