Psiadia salviifolia subsp. salviifolia

Psiadia salviifolia subsp.

salviifolia, commonly known as the Mexican blueweed, is a perennial herb native to Mexico and parts of Central America. It is characterized by its basal rosette of lanceolate, blue-green leaves with a woolly texture.

The plant produces small, blue to purple flowers arranged in dense clusters, which bloom in summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in dry, rocky habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional medicinal uses.
