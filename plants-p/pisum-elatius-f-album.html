Pisum elatius f. album

Pisum elatius f. album, commonly known as the white-flowered pea, is a horticultural form of the common pea.

It is characterized by its pale yellow to white flowers, which contrast with the typical green of the species. The plant is a climbing legume with trifoliate leaves and pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

This variety is valued in gardens for its unique floral color and ease of cultivation.
