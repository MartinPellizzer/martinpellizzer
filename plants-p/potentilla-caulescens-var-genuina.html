Potentilla caulescens var. genuina

Potentilla caulescens var. genuina, commonly known as the slender cinquefoil, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. The plant features a slender, upright stem with opposite, palmately lobed leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small, yellow, and clustered in dense inflorescences, blooming in late summer.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardiness and low maintenance requirements.
