Peschiera arcuata

Peschiera arcuata, commonly known as the wavy-leaf water plant, is a species of aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in Europe.

It is characterized by its narrow, wavy-edged leaves that are typically arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem. The plant thrives in slow-moving or still waters, often found in ponds, lakes, and wetlands. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, usually greenish-white in color, and it reproduces through both sexual and asexual means.

Peschiera arcuata plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for small organisms and contributing to water filtration.
