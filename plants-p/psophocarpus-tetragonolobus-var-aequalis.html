Psophocarpus tetragonolobus var. aequalis

Psophocarpus tetragonolobus var. aequalis, commonly known as the winged bean, is a leguminous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive quadrangular stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces pods containing edible seeds and a starchy, tuberous root known as the "taro bean.". It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is cultivated for its nutritional value.

The species is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it a beneficial crop in sustainable agriculture.
