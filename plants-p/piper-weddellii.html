Piper weddellii

Piper weddellii, commonly known as Weddell's pepper, is a tropical climbing shrub native to the Amazon rainforest.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many species of pepper plants. The plant produces small, green berries that turn red when ripe, and these berries contain the aromatic compound responsible for its distinctive flavor. The leaves are typically heart-shaped with a rough texture and are often used in traditional medicine.

Its botanical characteristics make it a unique and valuable species within its ecosystem.
