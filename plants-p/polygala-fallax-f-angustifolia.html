Polygala fallax f. angustifolia

Polygala fallax f. angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf milkvetch, is a flowering plant species native to parts of North America.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its slender, angular stems and small, clustered flowers. The leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

This subspecies is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
