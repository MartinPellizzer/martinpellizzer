Prunus serotina subsp. virens

Prunus serotina subsp.

virens, commonly known as the greenleaf black cherry, is a subspecies native to eastern North America. It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows in moist, well-drained soils.

The leaves are lance-shaped and often have a distinctive yellow-green hue, which gives it its common name. It produces small, dark purple fruits that are an important food source for wildlife.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological role in forest ecosystems.
