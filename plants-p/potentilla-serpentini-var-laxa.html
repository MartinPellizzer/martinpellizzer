Potentilla serpentini var. laxa

Potentilla serpentini var. laxa, commonly known as the laxa potentilla, is a flowering plant belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its low-growing habit and produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The leaves are typically oval-shaped with a rough texture and are arranged alternately along the stems. Native to specific regions in Europe, it thrives in well-drained soils and sunny to partially shaded environments.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and rockeries.
