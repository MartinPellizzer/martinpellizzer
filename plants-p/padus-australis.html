Padus australis

Padus australis, commonly known as the Australian blackwood, is a deciduous tree native to southern Australia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its dark, aromatic bark and elliptical leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, white flowers in spring, followed by edible red berries that are favored by birds and small mammals.

It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions and is often found in woodland and scrubland habitats.
