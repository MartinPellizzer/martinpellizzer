Papaver rhoeas var. strigosum

Papaver rhoeas var. strigosum, commonly known as the hairy poppy, is a variant of the common poppy characterized by its hairy stems and leaves.

It is native to Europe and parts of western Asia, often found in disturbed soils and fields. The plant produces vibrant red or orange flowers with a distinctive crown-like structure, blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate and typically have a rough, hairy texture, which helps in identifying the species.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
