Plantago brachyantha

Plantago brachyantha, commonly known as the narrow-leaved plantain, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to North America.

It features upright, branching stems that bear narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in dense clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers moist, well-drained environments.

This species is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and disturbed areas.
