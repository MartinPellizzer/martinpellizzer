Polygonatum multiflorum var. latifolium

Polygonatum multiflorum var. latifolium, commonly known as the broadleaf Solomon's seal, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern Asia.

It features long, arching stems with broad, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in a spiral pattern. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers in early summer, followed by red berries that mature in the fall. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and natural landscapes.
