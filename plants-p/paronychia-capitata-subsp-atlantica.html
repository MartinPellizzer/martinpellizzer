Paronychia capitata subsp. atlantica

Paronychia capitata subsp.

atlantica, commonly known as the Atlantic sandmat, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of the southeastern United States. It is a low-growing herbaceous perennial with a mat-forming habit, characterized by its small, fleshy, succulent leaves.

The plant produces clusters of tiny, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in sandy, well-drained soils and is often found in dune environments.

This subspecies is adapted to harsh coastal conditions, including salt spray and strong winds.
