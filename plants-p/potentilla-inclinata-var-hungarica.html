Potentilla inclinata var. hungarica

Potentilla inclinata var. hungarica, commonly known as the Hungarian cinquefoil, is a perennial shrub native to Central Europe.

It features slender, arching stems that bear small, yellow flowers with five petals, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has alternate, pinnately compound leaves composed of five to seven oval leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Its ornamental value comes from its bright yellow flowers and compact growth habit.
