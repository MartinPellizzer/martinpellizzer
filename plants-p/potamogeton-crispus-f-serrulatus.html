Potamogeton crispus f. serrulatus

Potamogeton crispus f. serrulatus, commonly known as the serrated curly pondweed, is a variant of the curly pondweed species.

It is a submerged aquatic plant characterized by its finely serrated leaf margins and slender, ribbon-like leaves. This plant typically grows in freshwater environments such as ponds, lakes, and slow-moving rivers. Its leaves are usually dark green and have a waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss.

It plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
