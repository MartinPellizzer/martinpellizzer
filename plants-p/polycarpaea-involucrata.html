Polycarpaea involucrata

Polycarpaea involucrata, commonly known as the African bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It features attractive, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in the summer months. The plant has alternate, ovate leaves that are often hairy and covered in fine pubescence. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is complemented by its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
