Plumeria ambigua

Plumeria ambigua, commonly known as the ambiguous plumeria, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of South America.

It is characterized by its showy, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to yellow and pink. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are usually ovate or elliptical in shape. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The bark and flowers have been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
