Phyllanthus norlindii

Phyllanthus norlindii, commonly known as the African moonseed, is a small shrub native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are often dispersed by wind. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its medicinal properties, it has been used in traditional African medicine for various ailments.
