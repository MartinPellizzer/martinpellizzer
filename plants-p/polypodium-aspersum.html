Polypodium aspersum

Polypodium aspersum, commonly known as the hairy shield fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Polypodiaceae.

It is characterized by its leathery, dark green fronds that are covered in fine, hair-like structures, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky areas. Its reproductive structures, known as sori, are clustered on the underside of the fronds and produce spores for propagation.

Polypodium aspersum is often found in temperate regions and is valued for its ornamental qualities in horticulture.
