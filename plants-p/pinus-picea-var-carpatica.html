Pinus picea var. carpatica

Pinus picea var. carpatica, commonly known as the Carpathian spruce, is a variant of the white spruce native to the Carpathian Mountains.

It is a coniferous evergreen tree characterized by its slender, pyramidal shape and dark blue-green needles. The tree produces small, cone-like structures that contain seeds, typical of the Pinaceae family. Its foliage is dense and provides habitat for various wildlife species.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
