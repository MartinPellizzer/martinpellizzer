Polygala kuriensis

Polygala kuriensis, commonly known as the Kuriensis milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and prefers arid or semi-arid climates. Its flowers are usually pale green to yellowish in color, and it produces small, bead-like fruits.

Polygala kuriensis is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and ecological adaptations.
