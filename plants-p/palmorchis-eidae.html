Palmorchis eidae

Palmorchis eidae, commonly known as the Eidae palm orchid, is a rare and specialized orchid species native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique morphology, featuring a short, stocky rhizome and a single, erect inflorescence bearing numerous small, delicate flowers. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, typically found in tropical rainforests. Its flowers are often white or pale green, with intricate patterns that aid in pollination by specific insect species.

Due to habitat destruction, Palmorchis eidae is considered endangered and is the subject of conservation efforts.
