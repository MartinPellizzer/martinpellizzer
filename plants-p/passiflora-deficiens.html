Passiflora deficiens

Passiflora deficiens, commonly known as the Brazilian passionflower, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that feature five sepals, five petals, and a distinctive crown of filaments. The plant produces blue or purple berries that are edible and contain seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in shaded or semi-shaded areas.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and ecological restoration projects.
