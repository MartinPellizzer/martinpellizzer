Piper sellowianum

Piper sellowianum, commonly known as the Sellow's pepper or Brazilian pepper, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces berries that are initially green and turn red when mature, often attracting various bird species. It is widely cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of spices, though its fruit is not typically used in cooking.

The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in coastal areas and lowland forests.
