Pilea langsonensis

Pilea langsonensis, commonly known as the Chinese money plant, is a species of flowering plant in the family Urticaceae.

It is native to China and is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that resemble small coins. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in umbels. It is often cultivated as a decorative houseplant due to its attractive foliage.

Pilea langsonensis prefers indirect light and well-draining soil, making it suitable for indoor environments.
