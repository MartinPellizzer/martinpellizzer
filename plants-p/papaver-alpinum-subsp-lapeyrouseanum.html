Papaver alpinum subsp. lapeyrouseanum

Papaver alpinum subsp.

lapeyrouseanum, commonly known as the Lapeyrouse's poppy, is a subspecies of the Alpine poppy. It is a herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, particularly in areas like the Tien Shan and Pamir Mountains.

The plant produces striking, cup-shaped flowers that are typically red or orange in color, with a distinctive yellow center. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils at high altitudes, often in alpine meadows.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
