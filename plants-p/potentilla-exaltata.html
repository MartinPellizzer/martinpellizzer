Potentilla exaltata

Potentilla exaltata, commonly known as the alpine cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils. The plant produces showy yellow flowers in late spring to early summer, which are clustered in groups of five. Its leaves are dark green, elliptical, and arranged alternately along the stems.

Potentilla exaltata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its hardiness and visual impact.
