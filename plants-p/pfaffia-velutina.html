Pfaffia velutina

Pfaffia velutina, commonly known as Brazilian ginseng, is a flowering plant native to the Atlantic Forest region of Brazil.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its upright, herbaceous stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, and its roots are the primary part used for medicinal purposes. Pfaffia velutina is valued for its potential adaptogenic properties and is often used in traditional medicine.

It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
