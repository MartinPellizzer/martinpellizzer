Paphiopedilum spicerianum

Paphiopedilum spicerianum, commonly known as the chocolate orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features a unique, showy flower with a pouch-like lip that is often deep red or brown in color. The plant has a short, thick rhizome and produces leaves that are typically broad and dark green. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil.

Its flowers bloom in late spring or early summer, adding a striking visual element to its natural habitat.
