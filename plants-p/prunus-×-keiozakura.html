Prunus × keiozakura

Prunus × keiozakura, commonly known as the Tokyo cherry.

It is a hybrid cherry tree resulting from the cross between Prunus serrulata and Prunus lannesiana. This species is renowned for its ornamental value, particularly for its showy pink to white flowers that bloom in early spring. The tree typically grows to a height of 8–10 meters and has a rounded, spreading form.

Its leaves are oval with a slightly serrated margin, and it produces small, edible fruits that are not typically consumed by humans.
