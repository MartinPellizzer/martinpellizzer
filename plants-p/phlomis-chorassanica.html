Phlomis chorassanica

Phlomis chorassanica, commonly known as the Persian fireweed, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Lamiaceae, which includes many aromatic plants known for their medicinal and ornamental value. The plant features tall, erect stems covered in soft, hairy foliage, and it produces showy clusters of pink to purple flowers in the summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly woolly texture, while its roots are thick and fibrous.

Phlomis chorassanica is adapted to dry, rocky environments and is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
