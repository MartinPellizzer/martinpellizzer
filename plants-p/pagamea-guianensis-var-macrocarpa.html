Pagamea guianensis var. macrocarpa

Pagamea guianensis var. macrocarpa, commonly known as the giant pagamea, is a deciduous tree native to the rainforests of South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and produces showy, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. The plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in humid, tropical environments. Its wood is valued for its strength and durability, making it useful in construction and furniture-making.

The species also plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
