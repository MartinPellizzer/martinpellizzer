Persea effusa var. areolata

Persea effusa var. areolata, commonly known as the areola persimmon, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that are usually dark green and have a leathery texture. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by fruits resembling persimmons, which are typically orange to red in color when ripe. The fruit has a sweet, sometimes astringent flavor and is often consumed fresh or used in culinary applications.

It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value as well as its edible fruit.
