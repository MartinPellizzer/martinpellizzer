Parochetus maculata

Parochetus maculata, commonly known as the spotted bellflower, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia and the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in sunny to partially shaded environments. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in a fine, hairy texture.

Parochetus maculata is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and botanical collections.
