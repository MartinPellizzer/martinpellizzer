Plectritis gibbosa

Plectritis gibbosa, commonly known as the hairy salsify, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellow flowers and hairy stems. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Plectritis gibbosa is an herbaceous perennial that blooms from late spring to early summer.
