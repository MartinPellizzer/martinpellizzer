Pleurothallis archidiaconi

Pleurothallis archidiaconi, commonly known as the Archidona orchid, is a species native to the cloud forests of Colombia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its delicate, pendulous inflorescences. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are usually small, with intricate patterns and a range of colors from white to pale yellow.

Due to its habitat specificity and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
