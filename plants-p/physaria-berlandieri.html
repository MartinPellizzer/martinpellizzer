Physaria berlandieri

Physaria berlandieri, commonly known as the desert primerose, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants. The plant produces bright yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It has succulent, fleshy leaves that help it store water, adapting to its arid environment.

Physaria berlandieri is often found in rocky soils and is well-suited for xeriscaping due to its low water requirements.
