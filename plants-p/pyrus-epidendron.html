Pyrus epidendron

Pyrus epidendron, commonly known as the Indian pear, is a deciduous tree native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, grey-barked trunk and alternate, simple, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters, followed by edible, sweet-tart berries that are typically harvested in the autumn.

Pyrus epidendron is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
