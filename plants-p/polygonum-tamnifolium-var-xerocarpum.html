Polygonum tamnifolium var. xerocarpum

Polygonum tamnifolium var. xerocarpum, commonly known as the desert smartweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It features upright, branched stems that are often hairy, and its leaves are lance-shaped with a rough texture. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from pink to red, typically blooming in late summer. Its seeds are enclosed in small, dry fruits, and the plant is well adapted to withstand drought conditions.

It is often found in disturbed soils and is a common component of dryland ecosystems.
