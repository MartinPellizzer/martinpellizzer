Piper griffithii

Piper griffithii, commonly known as Griffith's pepper, is a flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia, including parts of India, Malaysia, and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its slender, climbing vines and small, heart-shaped leaves. Its flowers are typically greenish-white and arranged in clusters, while the fruit is a small, elongated berry.

Piper griffithii is valued for its aromatic properties and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
