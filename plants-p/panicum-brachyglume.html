Panicum brachyglume

Panicum brachyglume, commonly known as little barley, is a grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, cylindrical inflorescence and small, bristly spikelets. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. Its leaves are narrow and hairy, and it produces small, wind-dispersed seeds.

It is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support local biodiversity.
