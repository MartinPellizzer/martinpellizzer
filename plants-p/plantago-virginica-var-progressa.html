Plantago virginica var. progressa

Plantago virginica var. progressa, commonly known as the Virginia plantain, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swales, and roadside ditches. The plant features erect, smooth stems that can reach up to 1 meter in height. Its leaves are broad, heart-shaped, and covered with fine, white hairs, giving them a fuzzy appearance.

The small, greenish-white flowers are clustered in dense spikes and bloom from late spring to early summer.
