Pappochroma paludicola

Pappochroma paludicola, commonly known as the swamp pappochroma, is a herbaceous plant native to wetland environments in tropical regions.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves that help it retain water in moist or swampy habitats. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. Its root system is adapted for anchoring in waterlogged soils and nutrient absorption.

Pappochroma paludicola plays a role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems and supporting local biodiversity.
