Prestonia falcatosepala

Prestonia falcatosepala, commonly known as the cat's claw vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its long, slender stems and opposite, ovate leaves with a pointed tip. The plant produces small, fragrant, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release numerous small, winged seeds.

Prestonia falcatosepala is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
