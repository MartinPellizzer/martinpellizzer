Pavonia urbaniana

Pavonia urbaniana, commonly known as the purple coneflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features showy, tubular purple to pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Pavonia urbaniana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like butterflies and bees.
