Pleurothallis patateensis

Pleurothallis patateensis, commonly known as the yellow orchid, is a species of orchid native to South America, particularly in regions of Ecuador and Peru.

It belongs to the Epidendroideae subfamily and is characterized by its compact, pseudobulbous structure and its ability to thrive in shaded, humid environments. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or greenish-yellow in color, often arranged in a raceme. It is a terrestrial orchid that prefers well-draining soil and is often found growing in cloud forests.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists alike.
