Picradenia canescens

Picradenia canescens, commonly known as the African sandalwood, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Rhamnaceae and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and simple, alternate leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by woody, oval fruits containing several seeds. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.

Due to its aromatic wood, it has traditional uses in medicine and as a source of incense.
