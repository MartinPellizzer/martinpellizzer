Pectis brevipedunculata

Pectis brevipedunculata, commonly known as the short-pedunculate pectis, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features small, yellowish flowers arranged in dense, spherical clusters called corymbs. Its leaves are typically narrow and linear, with a rough texture.

Pectis brevipedunculata is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments.
