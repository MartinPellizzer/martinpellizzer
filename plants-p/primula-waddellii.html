Primula waddellii

Primula waddellii, commonly known as Waddell's primrose, is a species of flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that typically range in color from white to pink. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in forest understories or rocky habitats. Its leaves are usually oval or ovate, with a slightly hairy texture, and it produces small, clustered flowers during the spring season.

Primula waddellii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate beauty.
