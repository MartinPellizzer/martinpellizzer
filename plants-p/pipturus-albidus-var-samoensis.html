Pipturus albidus var. samoensis

Pipturus albidus var. samoensis, commonly known as the Samoan white hakea, is a flowering plant native to the islands of Samoa.

It belongs to the Proteaceae family and is characterized by its dense, leathery foliage and showy, white to pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and lowland areas.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for horticultural use in tropical and subtropical regions.
