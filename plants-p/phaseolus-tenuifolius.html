Phaseolus tenuifolius

Phaseolus tenuifolius, commonly known as the horse bean or slender-leafed bean, is a leguminous plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its small, elongated pods that contain several seeds, often used as a food source. The plant has compound leaves with slender leaflets and produces small, yellow flowers. It thrives in warm climates and is cultivated for its nutritional value.

Phaseolus tenuifolius is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, benefiting other crops when grown in rotation.
