Paepalanthus heterophyllus

Paepalanthus heterophyllus, commonly known as the variable leaf puya, is a striking South American plant belonging to the Xanthorrhoeaceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, spire-like inflorescence that bears numerous small, brightly colored flowers, typically in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant is native to the Andean regions of Bolivia, Chile, and Peru, where it thrives in high-altitude environments. Its leaves are usually grass-like and vary in shape, which contributes to its common name.

Paepalanthus heterophyllus is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant blooms and unique appearance.
