Piper cumanense var. subvillosum

Piper cumanense var. subvillosum, commonly known as the velvet pipevine, is a tropical vine native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its dark green, velvety leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive texture. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers, which are followed by dark blue berries that contain the seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and unique appearance, it is cultivated in some botanical gardens and private collections.
