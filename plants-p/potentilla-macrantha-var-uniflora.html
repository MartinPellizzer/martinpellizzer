Potentilla macrantha var. uniflora

Potentilla macrantha var. uniflora, commonly known as the single-flower potentilla, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges, and its stems are covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a border plant.

Due to its ornamental value and hardiness, it is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms and low maintenance requirements.
