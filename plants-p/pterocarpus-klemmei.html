Pterocarpus klemmei

Pterocarpus klemmei, commonly known as the African rosewood, is a deciduous tree native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, dark bark and dense, aromatic wood. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by flat, pod-like fruits containing several seeds. Its leaves are bipinnate, consisting of numerous small, oval leaflets, and it thrives in well-drained soils with adequate sunlight.

Pterocarpus klemmei is valued for its hard, durable wood used in furniture and musical instruments.
