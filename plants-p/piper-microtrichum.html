Piper microtrichum

Piper microtrichum, commonly known as the small-hairy pepper plant, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, and thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant is characterized by its small, hairy leaves and clusters of tiny, greenish-white flowers. Its fruit is a small, three-lobed berry that turns red when mature.

Piper microtrichum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has some traditional medicinal uses in local communities.
