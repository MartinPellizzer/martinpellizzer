Pandanus beccarii

Pandanus beccarii, commonly known as the broad-leafed pandanus, is a tropical plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, fan-shaped leaves that can reach several meters in length. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically yellow or green, followed by spiky, woody fruits. It thrives in coastal and wetland environments, often growing in sandy or muddy soils.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional crafts and construction in its native regions.
