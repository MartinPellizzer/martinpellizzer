Pamphilia pedicellata var. intermedia

Pamphilia pedicellata var. intermedia, commonly known as the intermediate leafy nightshade, is a herbaceous perennial native to southern Europe.

It features distinctive, alternate leaves that are ovate to lanceolate in shape, often with a slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale green, blooming in late spring to early summer. Its fruit is a dry capsule that splits open to release small, elliptical seeds.

This variety is often found in rocky or limestone soils and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
