Panicum proliferum var. geniculatum

Panicum proliferum var. geniculatum, commonly known as the creeping millet, is a grass species characterized by its low-growing, rhizomatous growth habit.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces fluffy, feathery inflorescences that are typically pink to purplish in color. This plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as fields and roadsides. It is drought-tolerant and can spread rapidly through both seeds and underground runners.

The plant's botanical structure supports its role as a pioneer species in ecological succession.
