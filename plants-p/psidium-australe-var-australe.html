Psidium australe var. australe

Psidium australe var. australe, commonly known as the Australian guava, is a small evergreen shrub native to eastern Australia.

It features ovate to elliptical leaves that are dark green and glossy on top, with a lighter underside. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, yellow or orange berries. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial shade.

The fruit is often used in culinary applications and is valued for its aromatic flavor and nutritional content.
