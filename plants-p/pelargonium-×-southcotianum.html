Pelargonium × southcotianum

Pelargonium × southcotianum, commonly known as the Southcotian geranium, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Geraniaceae family.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and produces vibrant, showy flowers in shades of pink, red, and white. The leaves are typically rounded with a slightly hairy surface, and the plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and containers. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for bedding plants.

This species is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
