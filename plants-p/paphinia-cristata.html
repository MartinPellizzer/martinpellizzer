Paphinia cristata

Paphinia cristata, commonly known as the crown orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and striking, hairy leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, arranged in a loose cluster. Paphinia cristata thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its unique appearance and ornamental value make it a popular choice for cultivation in tropical gardens.
