Polygala obscura var. genuina

Polygala obscura var. genuina, commonly known as the narrowleaf milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae, which is characterized by its distinctive floral structures and often showy blossoms. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun. Its leaves are narrow and linear, and the flowers are usually small, clustered in spicate inflorescences.

It is a perennial herb that plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat.
