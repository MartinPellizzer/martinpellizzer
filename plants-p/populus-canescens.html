Populus canescens

Populus canescens, commonly known as the grey poplar, is a deciduous tree native to temperate regions of Asia and Europe.

It is characterized by its grayish-white bark and heart-shaped leaves that turn yellow in the fall. The plant produces small, yellowish catkins in early spring, which are its primary means of reproduction. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found near rivers and wetlands.

Populus canescens has been used in traditional medicine and is also valued for its rapid growth and ecological benefits.
