Panicum thurowii

Panicum thurowii, commonly known as Thurow's panicgrass, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences that are typically green to brown in color. It thrives in wetland environments and is often found in marshes and along the edges of ponds.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
