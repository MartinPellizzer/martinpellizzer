Pelea makahae

Pelea makahae, commonly known as the Hawaiian pelea, is a flowering plant native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and has adapted to the unique environmental conditions of Hawaii. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss.

Pelea makahae plays an important role in the local ecosystem and is considered a symbol of Hawaiian culture.
