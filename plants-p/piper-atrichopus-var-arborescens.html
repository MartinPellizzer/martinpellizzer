Piper atrichopus var. arborescens

Piper atrichopus var. arborescens, commonly known as the tree pepper, is a shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually ovate to elliptic in shape. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, followed by berries that turn red when mature. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has some traditional medicinal uses.

The species is adapted to tropical and subtropical climates, thriving in humid, well-drained soils.
