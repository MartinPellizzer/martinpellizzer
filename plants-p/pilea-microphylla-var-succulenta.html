Pilea microphylla var. succulenta

Pilea microphylla var. succulenta, commonly known as the Chinese money plant or pancake plant, is a compact, low-growing succulent native to China.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that are round or oval in shape, often with a waxy texture and a slight bluish tint. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers that appear on short stalks, though it is primarily cultivated for its ornamental foliage rather than its blooms. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Its unique appearance and ease of care make it a popular choice for indoor gardening.
