Phaseolus intonsus

Phaseolus intonsus, commonly known as the wild kidney bean, is a species native to Central and South America.

It is a climbing vine that produces small, green pods containing edible seeds. The plant belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small white or purple flowers. Its botanical features include trifoliate leaves and a fibrous root system.

It is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, growing in moist, well-drained soils.
