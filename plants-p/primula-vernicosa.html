Primula vernicosa

Primula vernicosa, commonly known as the glossy primrose, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe.

It features a basal rosette of lance-shaped, glossy green leaves with a waxy coating. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped, fragrant flowers in shades of white, yellow, and orange, typically blooming in early spring. It thrives in cool, moist environments and prefers partial to full shade.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and woodland settings.
