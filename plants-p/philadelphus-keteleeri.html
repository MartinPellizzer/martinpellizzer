Philadelphus keteleeri

Philadelphus keteleeri, commonly known as the Chinese mock orange, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant has opposite, simple, and ovate leaves that turn yellow in the fall. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil.

Philadelphus keteleeri is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
