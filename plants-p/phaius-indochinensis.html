Phaius indochinensis

Phaius indochinensis, commonly known as the Indochinese marsh orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It typically grows in wetland or swampy environments, where it thrives in moist, acidic soils. The plant features a tall, slender stem that bears a single, showy spike of flowers. Its blossoms are usually pale yellow to greenish-white, with intricate patterns that attract pollinators.

Phaius indochinensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in its native habitats.
