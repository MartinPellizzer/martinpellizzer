Pinalia pannea

Pinalia pannea, commonly known as the golden flame orchid, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow and orange flowers, which bloom in clusters during the warmer months. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in a spiral pattern around the stem. It thrives in humid, well-drained soil and requires indirect sunlight to flourish.

Its unique appearance makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and botanical collections.
