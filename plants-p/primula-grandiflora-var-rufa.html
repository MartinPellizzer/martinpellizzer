Primula grandiflora var. rufa

Primula grandiflora var. rufa, commonly known as the red-flowered primrose, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe.

It features upright, hairy stems that bear clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink to deep red. The leaves are basal, ovate, and covered with a dense layer of fine hairs. It prefers cool, moist environments and is often found in woodland or alpine habitats.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens for its early spring blooms.
