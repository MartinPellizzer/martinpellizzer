Penstemon deustus var. deustus

Penstemon deustus var. deustus, commonly known as the blue mist penstemon, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a fine, fuzzy layer, giving it a distinctive texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its showy flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to ecological biodiversity.
