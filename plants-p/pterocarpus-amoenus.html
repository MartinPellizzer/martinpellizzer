Pterocarpus amoenus

Pterocarpus amoenus, commonly known as the yellow mangostin, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its ornamental, fragrant flowers and dark, aromatic seeds. The plant produces woody pods that contain several seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are alternate, pinnately compound, and have a distinctive, glossy appearance.

The tree is valued for its timber and is also cultivated for its ornamental and medicinal properties.
