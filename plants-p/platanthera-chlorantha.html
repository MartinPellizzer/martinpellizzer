Platanthera chlorantha

Platanthera chlorantha, commonly known as the green twayblade, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant is native to moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and heathlands in Europe and parts of Asia. It has a symbiotic relationship with specific mycorrhizal fungi, which aid in nutrient absorption.

The species is noted for its delicate appearance and ecological significance in maintaining biodiversity in its native ecosystems.
