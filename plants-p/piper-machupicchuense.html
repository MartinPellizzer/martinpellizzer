Piper machupicchuense

Piper machupicchuense, commonly known as the Machu Picchu pepper, is a rare and endemic species native to the high-altitude regions of Peru.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes various pepper species, and is characterized by its small, elongated fruits. The plant typically grows in cloud forests, thriving in cool, moist environments with high humidity. Its leaves are usually heart-shaped and have a distinctive aromatic scent.

Due to its limited distribution and unique ecological niche, it is considered a valuable subject for botanical research and conservation efforts.
