Prunus jamasakura f. tanashiensis

Prunus jamasakura f. tanashiensis, commonly known as the Tanashi cherry, is a botanical variant of the Japanese cherry tree.

It is characterized by its small, delicate white flowers that bloom in early spring, often before the leaves appear. The tree has a compact, rounded shape with short, stout branches, making it well-suited for urban landscapes. Its foliage is dark green and turns a vibrant red in the fall, adding to its ornamental value.

This cultivar is prized for its aesthetic appeal and is often used in gardens and public spaces.
