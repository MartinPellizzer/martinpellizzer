Philodendron parvilobum

Philodendron parvilobum, commonly known as the heartleaf philodendron, is a tropical climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, waxy leaves that are typically a deep green with a glossy surface. The plant has aerial roots that allow it to climb and attach to surfaces, making it well adapted to its natural habitat. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires consistently moist, well-draining soil.

Philodendron parvilobum is popular as a houseplant due to its aesthetic appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
