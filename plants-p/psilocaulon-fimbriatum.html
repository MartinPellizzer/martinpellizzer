Psilocaulon fimbriatum

Psilocaulon fimbriatum, commonly known as the fringed psilocaulon, is a member of the Acanthaceae family.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its slender, branching stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The flowers are typically small and clustered in whorls, with distinctive blue or purple hues. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardeners seeking unique foliage.
