Penstemon spectabilis var. gilmanii

Penstemon spectabilis var. gilmanii, commonly known as Gilman's tall beardtongue, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, upright growth habit and showy, tubular flowers that range in color from lavender to pink. The plant produces bracts that are often more vibrant than the actual flowers, adding to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and native plant landscapes.

Its botanical features make it an important species for pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds.
