Prunus spinosa proles lucens

Prunus spinosa proles lucens, commonly known as the shining blackthorn, is a shrub native to Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is characterized by its dark, thorny branches and small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically dark green in color. The plant produces clusters of white or pale yellow flowers in early spring, followed by dark blue berries that are edible but have a tart flavor. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in hedgerows and woodland edges.

The species is known for its hardy nature and ability to tolerate various environmental conditions.
