Prunus cerasia

Prunus cerasia, commonly known as the sour cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Europe and western Asia.

It produces small, tart cherries that are widely used in cooking and baking. The plant has simple, ovate leaves with serrated edges and white or pinkish flowers that bloom in early spring. Its fruit is typically harvested in late summer and is characterized by its intense flavor and high acidity.

Prunus cerasia is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and utility.
