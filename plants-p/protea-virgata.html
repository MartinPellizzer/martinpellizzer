Protea virgata

Protea virgata, commonly known as the silver protea, is a striking South African plant belonging to the Proteaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescence composed of numerous white, petal-like bracts that enclose small, clustered flowers. The plant is native to the Western and Eastern Cape provinces, where it thrives in sandy, well-drained soils. It is often found in fynbos vegetation, which is adapted to the region's Mediterranean climate.

Protea virgata is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in local ecosystems by providing nectar for pollinators.
