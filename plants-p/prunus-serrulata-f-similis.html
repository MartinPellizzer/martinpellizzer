Prunus serrulata f. similis

Prunus serrulata f. similis, commonly known as the Japanese cherry blossom or some varieties may be referred to as the "Yoshino" cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its delicate, pink to white flowers that bloom in early spring, creating a spectacular display. The plant has ovate to elliptical leaves that are serrated along the edges, giving it its species name "serrulata.". It thrives in temperate climates and prefers well-drained soil.

This cultivar is widely cultivated for ornamental purposes in gardens and parks around the world.
