Phylica greyii

Phylica greyii, commonly known as the Grey's protea, is a striking South African plant belonging to the Proteaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescences composed of numerous small, tubular flowers arranged in a spherical or conical shape. The plant is native to the Western and Eastern Cape provinces, where it thrives in sandy, well-drained soils. Its flowers are typically pink or red, with a prominent central style that extends beyond the petals.

Phylica greyii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also valued for its ecological role in supporting local pollinators.
