Potamogeton paucifolius

Potamogeton paucifolius, commonly known as the few-leaved pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in North America.

It typically grows in shallow, slow-moving waters and has slender, ribbon-like leaves that are arranged spirally along its stems. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often submerged, followed by the development of small, canoe-shaped fruits. It plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.

Due to its ability to spread through vegetative reproduction, it can become invasive in some water bodies.
