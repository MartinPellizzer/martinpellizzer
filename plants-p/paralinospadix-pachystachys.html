Paralinospadix pachystachys

Paralinospadix pachystachys, commonly known as the giant arrowroot, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, arrow-shaped leaves and tall, cylindrical inflorescence. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or white in color. Its rhizomes are the primary part used, containing a starchy substance similar to taro.

This plant is often cultivated for its edible roots and is valued in traditional cuisines.
