Paphiopedilum × shipwayae

Paphiopedilum × shipwayae, commonly known as the Shipway's lady's slipper, is a hybrid orchid that exhibits a range of botanical characteristics typical of the Paphiopedilum genus.

It features a single, showy, slipper-shaped lip that is often brightly colored, which plays a key role in its pollination strategy. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte or lithophyte, attaching itself to trees or rocks in its native habitat. Its leaves are usually broad and leathery, adapted to conserve moisture in its natural environment.

The flowers are borne on a tall, erect stem and can produce multiple blooms, making it a popular choice among orchid enthusiasts.
