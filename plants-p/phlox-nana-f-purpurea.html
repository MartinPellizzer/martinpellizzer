Phlox nana f. purpurea

Phlox nana f. purpurea, commonly known as the dwarf garden phlox, is a compact, herbaceous perennial native to the northeastern United States.

It features dense clusters of showy, violet-pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has dark green, lance-shaped leaves that are slightly hairy, adding texture to the foliage. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained, fertile soil.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
