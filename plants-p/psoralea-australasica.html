Psoralea australasica

Psoralea australasica, commonly known as the Australian psoralea, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is often found in open habitats such as woodlands and grasslands. It has compound leaves and produces pods that contain several seeds.

Psoralea australasica is adapted to arid conditions and plays a role in local ecosystems by providing food for various insects and small animals.
