Piper discolor

Piper discolor, commonly known as the variegated pepper plant, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its striking variegated leaves, which display shades of white, green, and yellow. It produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into elongated, berry-like fruits.

Piper discolor is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and air-purifying properties.
