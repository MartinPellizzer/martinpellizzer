Pometia tomentosa var. ferruginea

Pometia tomentosa var. ferruginea, commonly known as the rusty pometia, is a variant of the pometia tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, grayish-brown bark and elliptical leaves with a rough texture. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by edible, fleshy fruits that are often used in local cuisines. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, typically in moist, well-drained soils.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential medicinal properties.
