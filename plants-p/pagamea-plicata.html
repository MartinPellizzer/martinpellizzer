Pagamea plicata

Pagamea plicata, commonly known as the zigzagged pagamea, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, rounded shape and fleshy, green leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white powder. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the summer months, adding a subtle ornamental appeal. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Its unique appearance and low maintenance make it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
