Philodendron simmondsii

Philodendron simmondsii, commonly known as the golden velvet philodendron, is a tropical climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It features heart-shaped, waxy leaves that are typically a deep green with a velvety texture. The plant produces striking yellow flowers that are followed by bright red berries, which are edible for humans but toxic to pets. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires consistent moisture and high humidity.

Its unique appearance and ornamental value make it a popular choice for indoor gardens and greenhouses.
