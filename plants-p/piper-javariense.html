Piper javariense

Piper javariense, commonly known as the Amazonian pepper plant, is a member of the Piperaceae family, which includes over 2,000 species of flowering plants.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly the Amazon basin, where it thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves and small, clustered greenish-white flowers that develop into berries. It is often found growing along riverbanks and in dense rainforest undergrowth.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and research settings.
