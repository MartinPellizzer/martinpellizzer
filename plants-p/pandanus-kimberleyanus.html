Pandanus kimberleyanus

Pandanus kimberleyanus, commonly known as the Kimberley pandanus, is a tropical plant native to northern Australia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and long, narrow leaves that form a rosette at the top. The plant produces inflorescences that bear small, fragrant flowers, which are typically yellow or green in color. Its foliage is often used in traditional crafts and basket weaving by Indigenous communities.

The species is adapted to coastal and wetland environments, thriving in high humidity and saline conditions.
