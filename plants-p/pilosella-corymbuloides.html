Pilosella corymbuloides

Pilosella corymbuloides, commonly known as the clustered goat's beard, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in meadows and grasslands. The plant features a basal rosette of hairy, spoon-shaped leaves and produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flower heads. Its flowers are typically surrounded by pale green to white bracts, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Pilosella corymbuloides is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
