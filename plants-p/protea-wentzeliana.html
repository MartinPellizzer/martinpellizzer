Protea wentzeliana

Protea wentzeliana, commonly known as the Wentzel's protea, is a striking South African plant belonging to the Proteaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescence composed of numerous pinkish-white bracts that resemble flowers. The plant is native to the Western Cape region and thrives in sandy, well-drained soils. It typically grows in shrubland and fynbos vegetation, often in areas with moderate rainfall.

Protea wentzeliana is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various insects.
