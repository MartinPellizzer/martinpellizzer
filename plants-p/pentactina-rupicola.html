Pentactina rupicola

Pentactina rupicola, commonly known as the rock pentactina, is a rare and hardy plant native to rocky habitats in southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact growth form and distinctive, waxy leaves that are adapted to reduce water loss in arid conditions. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink, adding a subtle ornamental value. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, Pentactina rupicola is considered a valuable species for botanical study and conservation efforts.
