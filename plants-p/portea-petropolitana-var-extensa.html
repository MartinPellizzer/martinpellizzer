Portea petropolitana var. extensa

Portea petropolitana var. extensa, commonly known as the Brazilian nettle, is a herbaceous plant native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It belongs to the Urticaceae family, characterized by its stinging hairs that can cause skin irritation upon contact. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and has alternate, ovate leaves with serrated edges. Its flowers are small and clustered in dense inflorescences, often greenish-white in color.

It is valued in traditional medicine for its potential anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties.
