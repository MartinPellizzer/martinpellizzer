Piper subrotundum

Piper subrotundum, commonly known as the round-leaved pepper or *Piper subrotundum*, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its small, round leaves and clusters of tiny, fragrant flowers. The plant produces small, peppery fruits that are used in traditional medicine and local cuisines. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in the understory of tropical rainforests.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and biodiversity conservation.
