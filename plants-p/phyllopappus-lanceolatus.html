Phyllopappus lanceolatus

Phyllopappus lanceolatus, commonly known as the lanceolate leaf euphorbia, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that are lance-shaped and often covered in a waxy cuticle to reduce water loss. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters, typically in shades of green or yellow. It thrives in arid environments and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Due to its unique appearance and resilience, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
