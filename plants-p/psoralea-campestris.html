Psoralea campestris

Psoralea campestris, commonly known as the field psoralea, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region and parts of Africa.

It is a low-growing annual herb that typically reaches heights of up to 30 centimeters. The plant features small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters, and its leaves are pinnately divided with a hairy texture. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fields.

The plant is also known for its medicinal properties, traditionally used in herbal remedies.
