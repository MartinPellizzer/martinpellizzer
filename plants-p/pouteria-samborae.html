Pouteria samborae

Pouteria samborae, commonly known as the African butterfruit, is a tropical plant native to Central and West Africa.

It is a small to medium-sized tree that can grow up to 10 meters in height, featuring smooth, greyish-brown bark and elliptical leaves with a glossy green upper surface. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, orange-colored fruits with a creamy, buttery pulp.

Its fruit is rich in nutrients and is often used in traditional cuisines and local markets.
