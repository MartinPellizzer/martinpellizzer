Potamogeton parmatus

Potamogeton parmatus, commonly known as the broadleaf pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in North America.

It has long, ribbon-like leaves that are typically dark green and can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are arranged in dense clusters, often emerging from the water surface. It plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various waterfowl and invertebrates.

Potamogeton parmatus is often found in shallow lakes, ponds, and slow-moving streams.
