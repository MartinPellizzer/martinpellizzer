Pavonia integrifolia

Pavonia integrifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf penta, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, featuring upright stems and lance-shaped leaves with smooth, entire margins. The plant produces showy, five-petaled flowers in shades of pink to red, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often found in moist, well-drained soils along stream banks and in woodland edges.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and natural landscapes.
