Pleioblastus sadawoanus

Pleioblastus sadawoanus, commonly known as the Japanese silvergrass, is a perennial grass native to East Asia.

It features narrow, silvery-blue leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in early spring, which are followed by fluffy seed heads. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
