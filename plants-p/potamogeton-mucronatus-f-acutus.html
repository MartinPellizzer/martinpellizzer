Potamogeton mucronatus f. acutus

Potamogeton mucronatus f. acutus, commonly known as the sharp-pointed pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in North America.

It has slender, ribbon-like leaves that are typically dark green and arranged in whorls around the stem. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often submerged, contributing to its role in aquatic ecosystems. It is an important food source for various aquatic herbivores and helps stabilize sediment in shallow waters.

Its presence is often an indicator of good water quality in lakes and rivers.
