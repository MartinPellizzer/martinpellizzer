Pterolobium exosum

Pterolobium exosum, commonly known as the African senna, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant has pinnately compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, arranged in racemes, and attract various pollinators.

The seeds are enclosed in long, slender pods that split open when mature to release the seeds.
