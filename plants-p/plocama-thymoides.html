Plocama thymoides

Plocama thymoides, commonly known as the creeping thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant has small, white or pale purple flowers that bloom in the spring and summer. Its leaves are often used in culinary applications due to their mild thyme-like fragrance.

Plocama thymoides thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable ground cover in rocky or dry landscapes.
