Pancratium parvum subsp. malabaricum

Pancratium parvum subsp.

malabaricum, commonly known as the Malabar yellow flower, is a flowering plant native to southern India and Sri Lanka. It is a herbaceous perennial that grows in coastal and sandy soils, often found in wetlands and marshy areas.

The plant produces striking yellow flowers with a strong, sweet fragrance, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it has a fibrous root system adapted to its moist habitat.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has traditional uses in local medicine.
