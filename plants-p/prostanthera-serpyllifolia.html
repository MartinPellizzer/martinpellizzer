Prostanthera serpyllifolia

Prostanthera serpyllifolia, commonly known as silverleaf mintbush, is a shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, silver-gray foliage and small, tubular flowers that are typically pale blue or violet. The plant belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, sunny habitats such as coastal heathlands.

Prostanthera serpyllifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinators.
