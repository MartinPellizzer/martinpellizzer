Pilocereus tetetzo var. cristata

Pilocereus tetetzo var. cristata, commonly known as the cristate night bloomer, is a unique cactus species native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its distinctive, flattened, ribbed stems that form a crown-like shape, giving it a striking appearance. The plant produces large, fragrant, white flowers that open at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as bats and moths. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its ornamental value and specific care requirements, it is a popular choice among cactus enthusiasts.
