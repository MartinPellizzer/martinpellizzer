Praxelis macrocarpa

Praxelis macrocarpa, commonly known as the large-fruited morning glory, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking blue or violet trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in the morning. The plant produces large, showy fruits that are typically about 5 to 10 centimeters in length. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a rough texture, while its stems are hairy and often trailing.

Praxelis macrocarpa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
