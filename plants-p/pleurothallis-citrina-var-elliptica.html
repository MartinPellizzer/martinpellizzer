Pleurothallis citrina var. elliptica

Pleurothallis citrina var. elliptica, commonly known as the white-veined orchid, is a species native to the cloud forests of Central and South America.

It is a small epiphytic orchid that thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces delicate, white flowers with greenish veining. It is prized for its elegant appearance and is often cultivated as a houseplant.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is relatively challenging to grow outdoors outside its native range.
