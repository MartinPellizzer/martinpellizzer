Pavonia rulingioides

Pavonia rulingioides, commonly known as the Mexican flame flower, is a perennial shrub native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its vibrant red to orange tubular flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture, and it thrives in full sun to partial shade. It prefers well-drained soil and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.

Pavonia rulingioides is also valued for its ability to tolerate drought and poor soil conditions.
