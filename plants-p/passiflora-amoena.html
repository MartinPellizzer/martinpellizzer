Passiflora amoena

Passiflora amoena, commonly known as the sweet passionflower, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful flowers that feature a complex arrangement of petals, sepals, and bracts. The plant produces edible berries that are rich in antioxidants and have a sweet, tropical flavor. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a velvety texture.

Passiflora amoena is often cultivated for ornamental purposes and is also used in traditional medicine.
