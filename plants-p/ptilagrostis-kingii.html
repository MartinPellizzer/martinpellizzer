Ptilagrostis kingii

Ptilagrostis kingii, commonly known as the King's hairgrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted foliage and delicate, hair-like leaves that give it its common name. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky outcrops. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typical of the grass family.

Ptilagrostis kingii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its elegant appearance.
