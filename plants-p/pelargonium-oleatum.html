Pelargonium oleatum

Pelargonium oleatum, commonly known as the lemon-scented geranium, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its fragrant, lemon-scented leaves and clusters of small, pink to red flowers. The plant is evergreen, with rounded, lobed leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is drought-tolerant once established.

Pelargonium oleatum is widely cultivated as an ornamental plant and is also used in aromatherapy due to its pleasant scent.
