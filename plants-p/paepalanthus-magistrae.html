Paepalanthus magistrae

Paepalanthus magistrae, commonly known as the Magistrae's cholla, is a species of cactus native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems covered in small, spiny tubercles. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, adding a striking visual contrast to its rugged appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires minimal water, making it well adapted to desert environments.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological significance, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists alike.
