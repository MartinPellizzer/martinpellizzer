Papaver radicatum subsp. gjaerevolli

Papaver radicatum subsp.

gjaerevolli, commonly known as the Gjaerevolli poppy, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe. It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant features a taproot system and lance-shaped leaves with a hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
