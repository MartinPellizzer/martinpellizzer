Ptilotus polystachyus

Ptilotus polystachyus, commonly known as the desert lily or silverleaf lily, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage, which helps reduce water loss in harsh environments. The plant produces showy, pink or purple flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it well-suited for xeriscaping.

Its unique appearance and adaptability make it a notable species in ecological and horticultural contexts.
