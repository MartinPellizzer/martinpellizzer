Pandanus palustris

Pandanus palustris, commonly known as the swamp pandanus, is a tropical plant native to wetland areas of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stem and long, narrow leaves that are often used in traditional crafts. The plant thrives in moist, swampy environments and is often found near water bodies. Its inflorescence consists of a branched cluster of small, fragrant flowers.

Pandanus palustris plays an important ecological role in its habitat by providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
