Pygeum steinii

Pygeum steinii, commonly known as the African wild almond, is a small evergreen tree native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Proteaceae family and is characterized by its dense, leathery leaves and showy, pale yellow to orange flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that contain hard, angular seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
