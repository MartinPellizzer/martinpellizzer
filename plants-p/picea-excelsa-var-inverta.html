Picea excelsa var. inverta

Picea excelsa var. inverta, commonly known as the Inverted White Spruce, is a rare variant of the white spruce native to specific regions of Canada.

It is characterized by its unique inverted cone shape, which sets it apart from typical coniferous forms. The plant exhibits dense, dark green foliage with a distinctive needle-like structure that provides insulation in cold climates. Its cones are small and often remain attached to the branches for an extended period.

This variety is of interest to botanists due to its genetic and morphological uniqueness.
