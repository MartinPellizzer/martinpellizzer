Paphiopedilum hartwegii

Paphiopedilum hartwegii, commonly known as the Hartweg's lady's slipper, is a terrestrial orchid native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It features a distinctive, pouch-like lip that is typically yellow or green with a red or purple border, which is a characteristic feature of the Paphiopedilum genus. The plant has a short, stocky stem and produces few, large, showy flowers with a velvety texture. It thrives in cool, humid environments and requires well-draining, acidic soil for optimal growth.

This species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
