Pycreus flavicomus

Pycreus flavicomus, commonly known as the golden-winged pycreus, is a rare and striking member of the Apiaceae family.

It is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces fluffy, spherical seed heads that are often mistaken for miniature pinecones. Native to specific regions in Southeast Asia, it thrives in moist, shaded environments.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered endangered and requires conservation efforts to ensure its survival.
