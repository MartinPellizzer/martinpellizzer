Phaseolus gonospermos var. oryzoides

Phaseolus gonospermos var. oryzoides, commonly known as the rice bean, is a leguminous plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its small, oval-shaped seeds that resemble rice grains, hence its common name. The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm, with trifoliate leaves and small white or purple flowers. Its pods are usually hairy and contain 2–5 seeds.

This variety is valued for its nutritional content and is cultivated in various regions for both food and agricultural purposes.
