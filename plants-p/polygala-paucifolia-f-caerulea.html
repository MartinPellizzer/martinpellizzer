Polygala paucifolia f. caerulea

Polygala paucifolia f. caerulea, commonly known as the blue-flowered polygala, is a rare and striking variant of the polygala genus.

It is characterized by its delicate, bluish-purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils in open woodland or scrub habitats. Its foliage consists of small, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

This ornamental plant is valued for its unique coloration and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
