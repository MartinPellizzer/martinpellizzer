Physematium obtusum subsp. occidentale

Physematium obtusum subsp.

occidentale, commonly known as the western bluntleaf sedge, is a perennial herb native to western North America. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spikelets. It is characterized by its blunt, rounded leaf tips and is often found in riparian zones.

This subspecies is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
