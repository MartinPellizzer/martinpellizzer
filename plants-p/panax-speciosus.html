Panax speciosus

Panax speciosus, commonly known as the Chinese ginseng, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to China, Korea, and Japan.

It belongs to the Araliaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, branched root system that is highly valued in traditional medicine. The plant typically grows to a height of 1-2 meters, featuring compound leaves with opposite arrangement and small, greenish-white flowers. Its roots contain various bioactive compounds, including ginsenosides, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

Panax speciosus is often cultivated for its therapeutic uses in enhancing vitality and supporting immune function.
