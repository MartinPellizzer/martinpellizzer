Pseudolycopodiella carnosa

Pseudolycopodiella carnosa, commonly known as the hairy clubmoss, is a small, evergreen fern-like plant belonging to the family Lycopodiaceae.

It is characterized by its compact, mat-forming growth habit and its scale-like leaves that are often covered in fine, hairy structures. The plant produces small, cone-like structures called strobili, which contain the reproductive organs. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically found in forest undergrowth or on rocky slopes.

Pseudolycopodiella carnosa is valued for its ornamental appearance and its role in maintaining ecological balance in its native habitats.
