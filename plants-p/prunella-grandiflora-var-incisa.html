Prunella grandiflora var. incisa

Prunella grandiflora var. incisa, commonly known as narrow-leaved selfheal, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and spikes of blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded areas such as woodlands and stream banks. It is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally in herbal remedies.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental flowers make it a popular choice for garden cultivation.
