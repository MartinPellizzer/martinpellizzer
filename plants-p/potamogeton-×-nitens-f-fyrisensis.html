Potamogeton × nitens f. fyrisensis

Potamogeton × nitens f. fyrisensis, commonly known as the Fyris River pondweed, is a hybrid aquatic plant native to Europe.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, elongated leaves that are typically arranged in a spiral pattern. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are clustered in spike-like inflorescences. It thrives in shallow, slow-moving freshwater environments such as lakes, ponds, and rivers.

Its morphology and growth habits make it an important component of aquatic ecosystems, providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
