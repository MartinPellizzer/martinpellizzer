Penstemon cyananthus subsp. longiflorus

Penstemon cyananthus subsp.

longiflorus, commonly known as the blue beard penstemon, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in mountainous regions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a fine, downy coating.

It is valued in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
