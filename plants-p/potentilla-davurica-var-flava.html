Potentilla davurica var. flava

Potentilla davurica var. flava, commonly known as the yellow cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its upright growth habit and dense foliage. The plant produces bright yellow flowers in clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a rough texture with serrated edges.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy and ornamental addition to gardens.
