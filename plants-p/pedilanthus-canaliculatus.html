Pedilanthus canaliculatus

Pedilanthus canaliculatus, commonly known as the candleplant, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and Central America.

It is characterized by its succulent, cylindrical stems that resemble candles, hence its common name. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in the spring. Its leaves are typically alternate, simple, and have a waxy texture.

Pedilanthus canaliculatus is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and drought tolerance.
