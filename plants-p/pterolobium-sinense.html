Pterolobium sinense

Pterolobium sinense, commonly known as the Chinese pterolobium, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing ability. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces yellow or white flowers in racemes.

The seeds are enclosed in elongated, fibrous pods, which are often used in traditional medicine.
