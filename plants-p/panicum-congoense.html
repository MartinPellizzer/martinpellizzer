Panicum congoense

Panicum congoense, commonly known as Congo panicgrass, is a grass species native to Central and Southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, blade-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in open woodlands and grasslands.

Due to its adaptability, it is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
