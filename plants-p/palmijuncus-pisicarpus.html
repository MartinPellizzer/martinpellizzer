Palmijuncus pisicarpus

Palmijuncus pisicarpus, commonly known as the giant water lily, is a striking aquatic plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, broad leaves that can reach up to three meters in diameter, floating on the surface of still or slow-moving waters. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers that are typically pink or white, which bloom at night and attract pollinators such as bats and insects. Its roots system helps stabilize the soil and improve water quality by filtering pollutants.

However, due to its rapid growth and invasive nature, it can disrupt local ecosystems if not managed properly.
