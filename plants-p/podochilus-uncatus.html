Podochilus uncatus

Podochilus uncatus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and small, delicate flowers that typically bloom in shades of white or pale yellow. The plant grows in shaded, moist environments, often in forest understories or along stream banks. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread slowly and form dense clusters.

Podochilus uncatus is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated as a container plant.
