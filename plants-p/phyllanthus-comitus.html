Phyllanthus comitus

Phyllanthus comitus, commonly known as the Indian cheesewood, is a small evergreen shrub native to tropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are often glossy and elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by orange-red berries that are a source of food for various animals.

It is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories and is valued for its medicinal properties in traditional medicine.
