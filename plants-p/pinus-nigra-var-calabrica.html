Pinus nigra var. calabrica

Pinus nigra var. calabrica, commonly known as the Italian stone pine, is a variant of the black pine species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, straight trunk and dense, dark green foliage that forms a conical shape. The tree produces edible pine nuts, which are highly valued in culinary traditions. Its bark is thick and fissured, providing a rugged appearance.

This plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous areas.
