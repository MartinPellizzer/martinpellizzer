Poa laxa f. racemosa

Poa laxa f. racemosa, commonly known as the tufted meadowgrass, is a flowering plant belonging to the grass family Poaceae.

It is a perennial species characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, erect stems. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, two-lobed flowers. It thrives in moist, temperate environments and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an important component of ecological and agricultural systems.
