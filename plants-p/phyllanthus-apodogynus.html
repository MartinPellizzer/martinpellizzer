Phyllanthus apodogynus

Phyllanthus apodogynus, commonly known as the African date palm, is a flowering plant in the family Phyllanthaceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate leaves that can reach several meters in length. It produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in dense clusters, followed by small, fleshy fruits that are edible.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in various cultures for food and medicine.
