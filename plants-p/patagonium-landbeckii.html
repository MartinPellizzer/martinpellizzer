Patagonium landbeckii

Patagonium landbeckii, commonly known as the Patagonian bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the southern regions of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, showy blue or purple flowers that bloom in the summer months. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It has small, narrow leaves that are adapted to the arid conditions of its native environment.

Patagonium landbeckii plays a role in local ecosystems by providing nectar for pollinators and habitat for small insects.
