Plantago hirtella var. veratrifolia

Plantago hirtella var. veratrifolia, commonly known as the leafy plantain, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in dense clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, along roadsides, and in wetlands.

This variety is valued for its medicinal properties and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
