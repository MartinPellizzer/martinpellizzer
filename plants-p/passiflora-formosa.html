Passiflora formosa

Passiflora formosa, commonly known as the beautiful passionflower, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, complex flowers that feature five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments. The plant produces edible berries that are rich in antioxidants and have a mild, sweet flavor. Its leaves are heart-shaped and often have a velvety texture.

Passiflora formosa is widely cultivated for ornamental purposes and is also valued in traditional medicine.
