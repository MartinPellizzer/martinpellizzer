Panicum reimarioides

Panicum reimarioides, commonly known as the Reimarioides grass, is a species of grass native to certain regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences that are typically green or reddish in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands or open woodlands.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
