Ptilotus lophotrichus

Ptilotus lophotrichus, commonly known as the spinifex grass, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of Australia.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its tufted, grass-like appearance with dense, spiky foliage. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. It is well adapted to harsh environments, with deep root systems and drought-resistant properties.

Its unique morphology and ecological role make it an important component of its native ecosystem.
