Paratrophis pendulina

Paratrophis pendulina, commonly known as the hanging mistplant, is a unique species of flowering plant native to the cloud forests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, pendulous inflorescences that dangle from its stems, creating a striking visual effect. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that emit a faint fragrance, attracting pollinators such as moths and beetles. Its leaves are usually narrow and elongated, adapted to capture moisture from the humid air.

Paratrophis pendulina thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found growing on tree trunks or in rocky crevices.
