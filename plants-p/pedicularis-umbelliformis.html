Pedicularis umbelliformis

Pedicularis umbelliformis, commonly known as the umbrella monkey orchid, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of Asia.

It belongs to the family Orobanchaceae and is characterized by its unique, spurred flowers that resemble a monkey's face. The plant is hemiparasitic, meaning it partially relies on other plants for nutrients through its root system. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows.
