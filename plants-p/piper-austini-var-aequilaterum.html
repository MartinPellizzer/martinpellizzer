Piper austini var. aequilaterum

Piper austini var. aequilaterum, commonly known as the Austini pepper or the three-lobed pepper, is a variant of the Piper austini species native to the rainforests of South America.

It is characterized by its unique trifoliate leaves, which are three-lobed and have a distinctive shape. The plant produces small, elongated fruits that are typically harvested when they turn from green to red. It is valued for its aromatic leaves and peppery flavor, often used in traditional cooking and herbal remedies.

The botanical features of this plant make it a notable species within the Piper genus.
