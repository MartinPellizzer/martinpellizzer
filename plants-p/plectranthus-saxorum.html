Plectranthus saxorum

Plectranthus saxorum, commonly known as the rock plectranthus, is a perennial herb native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its aromatic, woolly leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to purple, often blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental foliage make it a popular choice for both ornamental and medicinal uses.
