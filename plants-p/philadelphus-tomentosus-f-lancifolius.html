Philadelphus tomentosus f. lancifolius

Philadelphus tomentosus f. lancifolius, commonly known as the lance-leaved mock orange, is a compact shrub characterized by its lance-shaped, aromatic leaves.

It produces fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers in late spring to early summer, which are clustered in racemes. The plant is native to the eastern United States and thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun. It is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and pleasant fragrance.

The foliage remains dense and attractive throughout the growing season, making it a desirable addition to landscape design.
