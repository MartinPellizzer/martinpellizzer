Polygala bicornis

Polygala bicornis, commonly known as the two-horned milk-vetch, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, two-lobed flowers that resemble horns, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments. Its leaves are narrow and often arranged in opposite pairs, while its stems are erect and can reach heights of up to 1 meter.

The flowers are usually pale yellow or greenish in color and bloom during the warmer months of the year.
