Pilea mongolica

Pilea mongolica, commonly known as the Mongolian pilea, is a species of flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Urticaceae, which includes nettle plants, and is characterized by its upright growth habit and simple, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in the spring. It is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and relatively low maintenance requirements.

Pilea mongolica prefers well-drained soil and partial shade, making it suitable for indoor cultivation in temperate climates.
