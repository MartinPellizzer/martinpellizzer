Philodendron cymbispathum

Philodendron cymbispathum, commonly known as the giant marble vine, is a striking tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 3 feet in length, often with a waxy, glossy texture. The plant produces clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers that are followed by bright red berries. It thrives in humid, warm environments and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.

Its unique appearance and ornamental value make it a popular choice for indoor and outdoor gardens.
