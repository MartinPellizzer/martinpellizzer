Picea ascendens

Picea ascendens, commonly known as the Sitka spruce, is a tall evergreen conifer native to the coastal regions of Southeast Alaska and western Canada.

It is characterized by its slender, upward-curving needles and cone-shaped buds, which are typical of the pine family. The tree has a shallow root system that allows it to thrive in coastal environments with high winds and frequent rainfall. Its wood is highly valued for its strength and resistance to decay, making it important for construction and musical instruments.

The Sitka spruce also plays a crucial role in the ecosystems of its native habitats, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
