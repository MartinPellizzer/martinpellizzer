Phyllanthus anamalayanus

Phyllanthus anamalayanus, commonly known as the Malabar phyllanthus, is a flowering plant native to the Western Ghats of India.

It is a small shrub that typically grows in moist, shaded environments within tropical and subtropical regions. The plant features simple, opposite leaves with a leathery texture and a distinctive, slightly serrated margin. Its small, greenish-white flowers are clustered in dense inflorescences, and it produces small, spherical fruits that contain a single seed.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
