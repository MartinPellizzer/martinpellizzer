Paspalum sincoranum

Paspalum sincoranum, commonly known as the narrowleaf grass, is a perennial grass native to South America.

It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as wetlands and marshes. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
