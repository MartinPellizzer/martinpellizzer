Polygala amarella var. lanceolatum

Polygala amarella var. lanceolatum, commonly known as the lanceleaf milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia.

It typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in spikes, which bloom in late summer. It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is often found in arid or semi-arid regions.
