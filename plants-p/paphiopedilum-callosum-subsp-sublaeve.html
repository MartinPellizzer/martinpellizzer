Paphiopedilum callosum subsp. sublaeve

Paphiopedilum callosum subsp.

sublaeve, commonly known as the smooth-leafed lady's slipper orchid, is a rare and striking orchid native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its unique, pouch-shaped lip that is often a striking pink or red color.

The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its leaves are broad and smooth, contributing to its common name.

It blooms with showy, fragrant flowers that attract specific pollinators, making it an important species in ecological studies.
