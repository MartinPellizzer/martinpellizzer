Piper fimbriulatum

Piper fimbriulatum, commonly known as the fringed pepper or mountain pepper, is a tropical climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many pepper species. The plant is characterized by its slender, vine-like stems and heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive fringed margin. Its small, greenish-white flowers are followed by oval, peppery fruits that are used in traditional medicine.

The plant's botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and pharmacological studies.
