Phlomoides strigosa

Phlomoides strigosa, commonly known as the hairy phlomoides, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Lamiaceae, which includes many aromatic plants. The plant is characterized by its hairy, narrow leaves and spikes of small, pale blue or purple flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and rocky areas.

Phlomoides strigosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
