Potentilla rhyolitica var. rhyolitica

Potentilla rhyolitica var. rhyolitica, commonly known as the Rhyolite cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of small, yellowish-orange flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has finely divided, serrated leaves that are often covered in a powdery bloom, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to harsh, alpine environments.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
