Pleocnemia nesiotica

Pleocnemia nesiotica, commonly known as the golden mistflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant features slender, hairy stems and produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often covered in fine pubescence.

Pleocnemia nesiotica thrives in moist, woodland environments and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
