Phrynium bicolor

Phrynium bicolor, commonly known as the golden arrow plant, is a tropical perennial herb native to Central and South America.

It features broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often variegated with yellow and green patterns. The plant produces striking yellow-orange flowers that are clustered in panicles, adding ornamental value. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often used in ornamental horticulture.

Its foliage is also valued for its aesthetic appeal in tropical landscapes.
