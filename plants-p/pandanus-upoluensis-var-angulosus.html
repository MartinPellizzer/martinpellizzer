Pandanus upoluensis var. angulosus

Pandanus upoluensis var. angulosus, commonly known as the Tonga pandanus, is a species native to the Pacific Islands.

It is a tall, palm-like plant characterized by its long, narrow leaves that are often recurved and have a fibrous texture. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers that are followed by spiky, woody fruits. Its leaves are used in traditional crafts and thatching, while the fruits are sometimes consumed.

This plant thrives in coastal and wetland environments, adapting well to tropical climates.
