Pachytrophe obovata var. montana

Pachytrophe obovata var. montana, commonly known as the mountain pachytrophe, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It features lanceolate to obovate leaves that are often hairy and have a bluish-green coloration. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink, blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens in cooler climates.
