Ptilophyllum attenuatum

Ptilophyllum attenuatum, commonly known as the slender fern, is a species of fern native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped fronds that are often slightly curled at the tips. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its reproductive structures, known as sori, are located on the underside of the fronds and produce spores for propagation.

Ptilophyllum attenuatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with appropriate humidity and light conditions.
