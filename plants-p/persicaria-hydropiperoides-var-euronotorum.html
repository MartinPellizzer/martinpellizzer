Persicaria hydropiperoides var. euronotorum

Persicaria hydropiperoides var. euronotorum, commonly known as the European water pepper, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features slender, upright stems that are often covered in fine hairs, and its leaves are lance-shaped with a slightly serrated margin. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in dense clusters, typically blooming from late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist or wet environments such as marshes, stream banks, and damp woodlands.

Its root system is fibrous, and it is often found in nutrient-rich soils.
