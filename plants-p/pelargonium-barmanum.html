Pelargonium barmanum

Pelargonium barmanum, commonly known as the Barman's geranium, is a species of flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Geraniaceae family and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and showy, five-petaled flowers. The leaves are typically heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, giving them a velvety texture. It produces small, clustered flowers in shades of pink, red, and white, often blooming throughout the warmer months.

This plant is popular in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
