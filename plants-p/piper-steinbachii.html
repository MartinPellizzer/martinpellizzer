Piper steinbachii

Piper steinbachii, commonly known as the Steinbach's pepper vine, is a climbing plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species. The plant is characterized by its slender, woody stems and opposite, ovate leaves with a glossy surface. Its small, greenish-white flowers are followed by berries that turn red when mature.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has some traditional uses in folk medicine.
