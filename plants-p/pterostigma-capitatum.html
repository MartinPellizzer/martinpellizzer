Pterostigma capitatum

Pterostigma capitatum, commonly known as the crown-stigma plant, is a unique species belonging to the family Pterostigmataceae.

It is characterized by its distinctive crown-like structure at the base of its stem, which is formed by a dense cluster of modified leaves. The plant exhibits a moderate growth rate and thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically found in temperate regions. Its leaves are elongated and have a waxy surface, which helps in reducing water loss.

The flowers are small and inconspicuous, producing nectar that attracts various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
