Pongamia cassioides

Pongamia cassioides, commonly known as the Indian beech, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by elongated pods containing dark brown seeds. It is adapted to thrive in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in dry, rocky soils.

The tree is also valued for its hard wood and historical use in traditional medicine.
