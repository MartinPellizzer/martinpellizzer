Panicum trichopus

Panicum trichopus, commonly known as the hairy panicgrass, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and Australia.

It is a member of the grass family Poaceae and typically grows as a herbaceous perennial. The plant features slender, hair-covered stems and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Its leaves are narrow and often have a rough texture, contributing to its adaptability in various environments.

Panicum trichopus is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in both wet and dry conditions.
