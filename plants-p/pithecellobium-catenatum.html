Pithecellobium catenatum

Pithecellobium catenatum, commonly known as the catena acacia, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod-like fruits. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are often clustered in inflorescences. Its root system is known for its ability to fix nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and use in traditional medicine.
