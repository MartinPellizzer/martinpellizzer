Phalaris arundinacea var. genuina

Phalaris arundinacea var. genuina, commonly known as the common reed, is a perennial grass native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and long, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces dense clusters of small, spike-like inflorescences that contain both male and female flowers. It thrives in moist environments such as wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds.

Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to form dense stands, making it an important species in ecological restoration projects.
