Phyllostachys viridiglaucescens

Phyllostachys viridiglaucescens, commonly known as the green-blue bamboo, is a member of the grass family and is native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, bluish-green foliage and slender, upright culms that can grow up to several meters in height. This bamboo species is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its elegant appearance and adaptability to various soil types. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

Phyllostachys viridiglaucescens is also valued for its use in landscaping and as a decorative plant in gardens and parks.
