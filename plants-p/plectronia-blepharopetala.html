Plectronia blepharopetala

Plectronia blepharopetala, commonly known as the velvetleaf orchid, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, velvety petals that range in color from deep purple to dark red. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments and thrives in tropical climates. Its inflorescence consists of multiple showy flowers arranged in a spike, which attracts pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
