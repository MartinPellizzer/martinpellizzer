Polygonum × callmei

Polygonum × callmei, commonly known as the Chinese knotweed, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Polygonaceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive, red-veined, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers. The plant is native to China and is often found in moist, shady environments such as stream banks and forest floors. It produces small, triangular seeds that are dispersed by wind, contributing to its spread.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can become invasive if not managed carefully.
