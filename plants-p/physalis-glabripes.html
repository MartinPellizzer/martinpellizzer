Physalis glabripes

Physalis glabripes, commonly known as the smooth husk tomato, is a herbaceous annual plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its distinctive inflated calyces that enclose the fruit. The plant produces small, yellow-orange berries that are edible when fully ripe. Its leaves are ovate with a smooth texture, and it typically grows in dry, sandy soils.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
