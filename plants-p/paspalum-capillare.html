Paspalum capillare

Paspalum capillare, commonly known as silky paspalum, is a perennial grass species native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its fine, hair-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, thriving in moist to saturated soils. It is often found in temperate and subtropical regions, particularly in the southeastern United States and parts of South America.

Paspalum capillare plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
