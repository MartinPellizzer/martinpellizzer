Piper martinense

Piper martinense, commonly known as Martin's pepper, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its climbing habit and compound leaves with serrated edges. Its small, greenish-white flowers are followed by berries that turn red when mature.

Piper martinense is valued for its aromatic properties and is used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
