Poa adjarica

Poa adjarica, commonly known as the Adjara grass, is a perennial grass species native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and fine, blue-green foliage. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous areas. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers.

Poa adjarica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
