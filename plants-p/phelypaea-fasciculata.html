Phelypaea fasciculata

Phelypaea fasciculata, commonly known as the clustered water clover, is a aquatic plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its dense, floating leaves that are often arranged in clusters, giving it a clover-like appearance. The plant thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, such as ponds and marshes. Its leaves are typically small, oval, and have a smooth surface with a waxy coating.

Phelypaea fasciculata is often used in water gardens and as a decorative element in aquariums due to its aesthetic appeal and ability to provide habitat for aquatic life.
