Piper fuliginosum

Piper fuliginosum, commonly known as black pepper vine, is a climbing shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species. The plant is characterized by its dark green, glossy leaves and small, clustered flowers that develop into red, fleshy berries. Its fruit contains tiny, aromatic seeds that are used in traditional medicine and as a spice.

The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.
