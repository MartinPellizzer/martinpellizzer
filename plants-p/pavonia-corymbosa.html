Pavonia corymbosa

Pavonia corymbosa, commonly known as the scarlet flame bush, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 3 to 6 feet, featuring dense, upright growth habit. The plant produces showy, clustered red flowers that bloom in the summer months, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Pavonia corymbosa is valued in gardens for its vibrant color and ability to thrive in full sun to partial shade.
