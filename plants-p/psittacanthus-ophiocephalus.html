Psittacanthus ophiocephalus

Psittacanthus ophiocephalus, commonly known as the golden button fern, is a tropical epiphytic plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its compact, rosette-like growth habit. The plant features small, glossy, dark green leaves that are typically arranged in a tight spiral. It produces sporangia on the underside of its fronds, which are clustered in small, rounded structures called sorangia.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in humid, shaded environments.
