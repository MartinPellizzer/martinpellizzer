Podochrea incana

Podochrea incana, commonly known as the silverleaf acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to Africa.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray, papery bark and feathery, bipinnate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in spherical heads. It is often found in arid or semi-arid regions and is adapted to thrive in poor soil conditions.

Podochrea incana is also valued for its role in traditional medicine and as a source of browse for livestock.
