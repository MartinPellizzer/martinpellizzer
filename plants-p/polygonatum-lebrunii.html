Polygonatum lebrunii

Polygonatum lebrunii, commonly known as the Chinese Solomon's seal, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the Asparagaceae family and is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit and lanceolate, dark green leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, white, bell-shaped flowers during the summer months, followed by red or orange berries. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal uses, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
