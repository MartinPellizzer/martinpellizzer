Piper guatemalense

Piper guatemalense, commonly known as Guatemalan pepper or wild pepper, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species such as black pepper. The plant is characterized by its climbing habit, with opposite, ovate leaves that are often hairy. Its small, greenish-white flowers are followed by elongated, fleshy berries that contain the aromatic seeds.

The plant is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
