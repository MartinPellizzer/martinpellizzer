Passiflora sanctae-mariae

Passiflora sanctae-mariae, commonly known as the sacred passionflower, is a climbing vine native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, complex flowers that feature a crown of filaments and a central calyx. The plant produces blue or purple berries that are edible and often used in culinary and medicinal applications. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, requiring well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and ecological restoration projects.
