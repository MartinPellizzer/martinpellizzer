Pancratium tortifolium

Pancratium tortifolium, commonly known as the red mangosteen, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking red flowers and palm-like leaves, which are often used in traditional medicine. The plant thrives in coastal areas and is adapted to sandy soils and salt spray. Its fruit, which resembles a mango, contains a sweet, aromatic pulp surrounded by a thick, colorful rind.

Pancratium tortifolium is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in coastal ecosystems.
