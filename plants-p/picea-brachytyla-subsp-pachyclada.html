Picea brachytyla subsp. pachyclada

Picea brachytyla subsp.

pachyclada, commonly known as the dwarf spruce, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to the mountainous regions of western North America. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and short, needle-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color.

The plant produces small, cone-like structures that contain seeds, typical of the Pinaceae family. It thrives in cold, high-altitude environments and is often found in alpine and subalpine forests.

Its botanical features make it an important component of the ecosystems it inhabits.
