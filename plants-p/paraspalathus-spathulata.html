Paraspalathus spathulata

Paraspalathus spathulata, commonly known as the broad-leaved spathulate plant, is a member of the Malvaceae family.

It is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves and showy, spathate flowers that are often pink or purple in color. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, thriving in humid, well-drained soils. The plant's structure includes a woody stem with alternate leaves and a unique floral bract that encloses the actual flowers.
