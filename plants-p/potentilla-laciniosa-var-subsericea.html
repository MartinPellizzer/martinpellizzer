Potentilla laciniosa var. subsericea

Potentilla laciniosa var. subsericea, commonly known as the silverleaf potentilla, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which results from dense covering of fine, white hairs on the leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, adding ornamental value to its appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Its compact growth habit and drought tolerance make it a desirable plant for xeriscaping.
