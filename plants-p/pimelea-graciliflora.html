Pimelea graciliflora

Pimelea graciliflora, commonly known as the slender-flowered geebung, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Australia.

It is a shrub that typically grows in heathlands and dry sclerophyll forests, preferring well-drained soils. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, often appearing in spring. Its leaves are narrow and linear, with a leathery texture typical of many Australian shrubs.

Pimelea graciliflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its compact growth and seasonal blooms.
