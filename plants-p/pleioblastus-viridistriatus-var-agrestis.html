Pleioblastus viridistriatus var. agrestis

Pleioblastus viridistriatus var. agrestis, commonly known as the silver stripe bamboo, is a variant of the green-striated bamboo native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems that often exhibit a bluish-gray sheen. The leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a distinct silvery stripe running along the midrib. This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often used in landscaping and bonsai.

It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a versatile choice for garden settings.
