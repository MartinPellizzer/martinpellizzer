Pilosella fulgens

Pilosella fulgens, commonly known as the golden star flower, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features hairy stems and leaves, giving it a distinctive, rugged appearance. Its bright yellow flowers bloom in summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Pilosella fulgens is often found in meadows and open woodland areas, thriving in well-drained soils.
