Pyrus amphigenea

Pyrus amphigenea, commonly known as the Japanese whitebeam, is a deciduous tree native to Japan.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its small, white flowers that bloom in early spring. The tree produces small, edible berries that are typically yellow or orange in color. Its leaves are oval-shaped with serrated edges and turn vibrant red in the fall.

Pyrus amphigenea is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively disease-resistant.
