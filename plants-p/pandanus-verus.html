Pandanus verus

Pandanus verus, commonly known as the screw pine, is a tropical plant native to the Indo-Pacific region.

It is characterized by its distinctive spirally arranged leaves that form a dense, rosette-like structure. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its fibrous roots are often used in traditional crafts, and its leaves are utilized in thatching and weaving.

The plant thrives in coastal areas and is well adapted to saline and sandy soils.
