Pandanus calcis

Pandanus calcis, commonly known as the limestone pandanus, is a tropical plant native to the Indo-Pacific region.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and long, narrow leaves that are often used in traditional crafts. The plant thrives in coastal and limestone areas, where it adapts to saline and well-drained soils. Its reproductive structures include small, clustered inflorescences that produce fragrant flowers.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing coastal ecosystems.
