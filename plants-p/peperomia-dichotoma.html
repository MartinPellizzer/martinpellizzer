Peperomia dichotoma

Peperomia dichotoma, commonly known as the forked peperomia, is a compact, evergreen perennial native to tropical regions of South America.

It features a rosette of glossy, dark green, oval-shaped leaves that are often adorned with silver or white markings. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in shades of green and red, which are followed by elliptical, winged berries. It thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Its compact size and attractive foliage make it an excellent addition to shaded or semi-shaded garden beds.
