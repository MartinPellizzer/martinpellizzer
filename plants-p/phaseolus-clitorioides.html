Phaseolus clitorioides

Phaseolus clitorioides, commonly known as the climbing bean or velvet bean, is a tropical flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its vigorous climbing habit and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are followed by pod-like fruits containing edible seeds. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.

Due to its rapid growth and ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a garden plant.
