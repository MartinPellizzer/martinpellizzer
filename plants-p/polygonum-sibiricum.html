Polygonum sibiricum

Polygonum sibiricum, commonly known as Siberian knotweed, is a perennial herb native to the temperate regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its stout, reddish stems and heart-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, stream banks, and disturbed areas.

Polygonum sibiricum is known for its rapid growth and ability to spread through rhizomes, making it a common weed in various habitats.
