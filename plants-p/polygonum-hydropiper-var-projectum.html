Polygonum hydropiper var. projectum

Polygonum hydropiper var. projectum, commonly known as the spiked smartweed, is a flowering plant native to wetland environments in North America.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and distinctive, spiky inflorescence that bears small, greenish-white flowers. The plant thrives in moist soils and is often found in marshes, pond edges, and along streams. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are reddish and often branched.

It plays an important role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various insects and wildlife.
