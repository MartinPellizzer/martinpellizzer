Polygala nicaeensis var. tomentella

Polygala nicaeensis var. tomentella, commonly known as the hairy milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit and produces small, delicate flowers in shades of pink and white. The plant features hairy stems and leaves, which contribute to its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
