Pennisetum teneriffae

Pennisetum teneriffae, commonly known as the Canary Island grass, is a perennial grass native to the Canary Islands.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically green to bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences that are often pink or purple in hue, adding visual interest to its appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for ornamental gardening.

Due to its adaptability and aesthetic appeal, it is sometimes cultivated in warm climates as a decorative plant.
