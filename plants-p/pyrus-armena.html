Pyrus armena

Pyrus armena, commonly known as the Armenian pear, is a deciduous tree native to the Caucasus region.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its oval-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white flowers. The tree produces edible pears that are typically harvested in the autumn. It prefers well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous areas.

The plant is valued for its ornamental beauty and historical use in traditional medicine.
