Philadelphus serpyllifolius var. argyrocalyx

Philadelphus serpyllifolius var. argyrocalyx, commonly known as silverleaf mock orange, is a perennial shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its finely divided, silvery-gray leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it suitable for garden cultivation.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its showy blooms and unique foliage.
