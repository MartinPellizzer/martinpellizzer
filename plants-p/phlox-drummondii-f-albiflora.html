Phlox drummondii f. albiflora

Phlox drummondii f. albiflora, commonly known as the white-flowered garden phlox, is a cultivated form of the annual phlox species native to the southern United States.

This variety is characterized by its striking white to pale pink blooms, which contrast with its dark green foliage. It typically grows to a height of 12 to 24 inches and produces showy clusters of flowers in late spring to early summer. The plant thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking vibrant, low-maintenance ornamental plants.

Its botanical features make it a valuable addition to both wildflower gardens and landscape designs.
