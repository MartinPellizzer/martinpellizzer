Polygala globosa

Polygala globosa, commonly known as the globular milk-vetch, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive globular or spherical seed pods, which are a striking feature of the species. The plant typically grows in grassland and rocky habitats, preferring well-drained soils. Its flowers are small and usually pink or purple, arranged in spikes along the stem.

Polygala globosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
