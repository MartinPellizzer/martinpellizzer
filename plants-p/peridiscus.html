Peridiscus

Peridiscus, commonly known as the "spider plant," is a member of the Asparagaceae family.

It is native to southern Africa and is characterized by its rosette-shaped foliage composed of narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, white or greenish flowers that are followed by bright blue or purple berries. Peridiscus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of indoor environments.

It is also noted for its air-purifying properties and ease of care.
