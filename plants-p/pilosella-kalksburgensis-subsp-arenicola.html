Pilosella kalksburgensis subsp. arenicola

Pilosella kalksburgensis subsp.

arenicola, commonly known as the sand pilosella, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its small, yellowish to white composite flowers.

The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils, demonstrating a high degree of adaptability to harsh environmental conditions. Its leaves are usually hairy and narrow, aiding in water retention and reducing transpiration.

This subspecies is of interest to botanists due to its unique ecological niche and potential for studying plant resilience in dry climates.
