Potamogeton × nitens var. merloensis

Potamogeton × nitens var. merloensis, commonly known as the Merlo's pondweed, is a hybrid aquatic plant native to parts of South America.

It is characterized by its submerged, ribbon-like leaves that are typically green to reddish in color. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are clustered in spikelets, which are often submerged or just at the water's surface. It thrives in freshwater environments with moderate to high light levels and is often found in lakes, ponds, and slow-moving rivers.

Due to its rapid growth, it can play a significant role in aquatic ecosystems, both as a habitat for aquatic life and as a potential invasive species in non-native regions.
