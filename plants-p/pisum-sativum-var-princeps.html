Pisum sativum var. princeps

Pisum sativum var. princeps, commonly known as the Prince's pea, is a botanical variety of the common pea plant.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive, clustered flowers. The plant produces small, edible pods that contain round, yellow or green seeds. It thrives in temperate climates and is typically grown as an annual crop.

Its botanical features make it a valuable addition to both gardens and agricultural settings.
