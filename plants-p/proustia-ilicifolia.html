Proustia ilicifolia

Proustia ilicifolia, commonly known as the silverleaf pittosporum, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its dense, silvery-gray foliage and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant belongs to the family Pittosporaceae and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for coastal gardens.

Its leaves are evergreen, providing year-round visual interest in landscaping.
