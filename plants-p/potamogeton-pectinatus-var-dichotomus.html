Potamogeton pectinatus var. dichotomus

Potamogeton pectinatus var. dichotomus, commonly known as the forked pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, oval-shaped leaves that are often arranged in pairs. The plant produces tiny, green flowers that are typically clustered in short inflorescences. It thrives in shallow, slow-moving waters and plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.

Its presence is often an indicator of good water quality in its native habitats.
