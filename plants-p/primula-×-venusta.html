Primula × venusta

Primula × venusta, commonly known as the hybrid primrose, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and produces showy clusters of bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant typically blooms in early spring, often before the leaves fully develop, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking early-season color. Its foliage consists of heart-shaped, dark green leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of down.

Primula × venusta is well-suited for rock gardens, borders, and containers, thriving in cool, moist conditions with partial shade.
