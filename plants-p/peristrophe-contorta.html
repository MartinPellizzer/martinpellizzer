Peristrophe contorta

Peristrophe contorta, commonly known as the twisted peristrophe, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its slender, often twisted stems. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, pale green to brownish flowers. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a slightly rough texture.

Peristrophe contorta is typically found in open, sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.
