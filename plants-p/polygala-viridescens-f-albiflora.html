Polygala viridescens f. albiflora

Polygala viridescens f. albiflora, commonly known as the white-flowered greenleaf milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, giving it its distinguishing appearance. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
