Pyrrocoma setigera

Pyrrocoma setigera, commonly known as the slender pygmy grass, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its fine, needle-like leaves. The plant typically forms dense mats on rocky soils, often in mountainous or alpine environments. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are arranged in spikelets, typical of grasses.

Pyrrocoma setigera is adapted to harsh conditions, making it a resilient component of its ecosystem.
