Parodia spegazziniana

Parodia spegazziniana, commonly known as the Spegazzini's barrel cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of northern Argentina.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems that are typically ribbed and covered in white to pale blue spines. The plant produces yellow flowers during the warmer months, adding a striking contrast to its otherwise desolate appearance. It is a slow-growing cactus that thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and arid landscapes.
