Petronia

Petronia, commonly known as the desert bell, is a genus of flowering plants belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

These plants are native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa, the Arabian Peninsula, and parts of Asia. They are characterized by their showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. Petronia species are typically shrubs or subshrubs with small, leathery leaves adapted to withstand drought conditions.

They play an important role in stabilizing sandy soils and providing nectar for pollinators in their native habitats.
