Punica

Punica, commonly known as the pomegranate, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the region spanning from the Indus River to the Persian Gulf.

It is characterized by its bright red, spiny fruit that contains numerous seeds encased in a juicy, edible aril. The plant produces showy, brick-red flowers in spring, which are followed by the development of its distinctive fruit. Pomegranates are valued for their ornamental value and their use in culinary and medicinal applications.

The botanical name Punica granatum reflects its historical significance in ancient cultures.
