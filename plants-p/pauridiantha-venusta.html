Pauridiantha venusta

Pauridiantha venusta, commonly known as the golden bell vine, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is a climbing shrub with slender, woody stems and heart-shaped leaves. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance.
