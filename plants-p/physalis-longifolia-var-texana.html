Physalis longifolia var. texana

Physalis longifolia var. texana, commonly known as the Texas huskfruit, is a perennial shrub native to the southwestern United States.

It produces vibrant orange, lantern-like fruits that encase the edible seeds. The plant features hairy, ovate leaves with serrated edges and thrives in well-drained, sandy soils. It is often found in dry, open habitats such as prairies and rocky areas.

The fruit is typically harvested when fully mature, offering a sweet and tart flavor.
