Pupalia patula

Pupalia patula, commonly known as the common hibiscus, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features showy, five-petaled flowers that range in color from white to pink and red, typically blooming in summer. The plant has heart-shaped, dark green leaves that are often hairy to the touch. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Pupalia patula is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
