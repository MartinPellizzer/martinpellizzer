Pennisetum siguiriense

Pennisetum siguiriense, commonly known as the Brazilian grass or silky beard grass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces panicles of small, fluffy flowers that give it a soft, feathery appearance. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Pennisetum siguiriense is also valued for its ability to withstand drought and its use in erosion control.
