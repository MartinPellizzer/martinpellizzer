Polypodium alleganum

Polypodium alleganum, commonly known as the Allegheny leatherwood, is a fern native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its evergreen, leathery fronds that remain on the plant throughout the year. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The species is valued for its ornamental appearance and ecological role in forest ecosystems.
