Palaquium ternatense

Palaquium ternatense, commonly known as the Ternate sapote, is a tropical tree native to the Maluku Islands in Indonesia.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its elliptical, leathery leaves and yellowish-green flowers. The tree produces edible fruits that are similar in appearance to persimmons, with a sweet, sometimes tangy flavor. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The plant is valued not only for its fruit but also for its ornamental value and traditional uses in local communities.
