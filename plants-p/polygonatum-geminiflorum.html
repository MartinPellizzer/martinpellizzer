Polygonatum geminiflorum

Polygonatum geminiflorum, commonly known as the Chinese Solomon's seal, is a perennial herb native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Asparagaceae family and is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem.

Polygonatum geminiflorum is valued in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.
