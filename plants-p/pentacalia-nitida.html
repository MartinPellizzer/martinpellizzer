Pentacalia nitida

Pentacalia nitida, commonly known as the silverleaf pentacalia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in a fine, silvery-white pubescence. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, blooming in late summer. It is often grown as an ornamental plant for its delicate appearance and ease of cultivation.

Pentacalia nitida prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.
