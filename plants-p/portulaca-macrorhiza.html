Portulaca macrorhiza

Portulaca macrorhiza, commonly known as the large-flowered portulaca, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States and parts of Mexico.

It is a low-growing annual herb with fleshy, succulent stems and leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces vibrant, showy flowers that range in color from pink to red, typically blooming in summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its ability to store water in its leaves allows it to survive in arid environments.
