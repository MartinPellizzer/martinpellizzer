Panicum roxburghii

Panicum roxburghii, commonly known as kallu, is a perennial grass native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces spikelets that are typically golden or brown in color, contributing to its ornamental value. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it adaptable to various environmental conditions.

Panicum roxburghii is often used in landscaping and is also valued for its role in traditional medicine.
