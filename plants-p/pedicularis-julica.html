Pedicularis julica

Pedicularis julica, commonly known as the Alpine monkeyflower, is a flowering plant native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It is a member of the Orobanchaceae family, characterized by its parasitic growth habit and lack of chlorophyll. The plant produces striking pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its tubular flowers are adapted for pollination by bees and other insects.

It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils at high altitudes.
