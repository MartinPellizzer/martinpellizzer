Piper culebranum

Piper culebranum, commonly known as the snake vine, is a climbing shrub native to tropical regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes over 2,000 species of flowering plants. The plant is characterized by its long, twisting stems and heart-shaped leaves with a glossy surface. Its small, greenish-white flowers are clustered in spikes and are followed by small, fleshy berries.

Piper culebranum is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties.
