Plumaria vulgaris

Plumaria vulgaris, commonly known as the common plumaria, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its showy, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to violet. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

The flowers are primarily pollinated by birds, particularly sunbirds, making it an important component of local ecosystems.
