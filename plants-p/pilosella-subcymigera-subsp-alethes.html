Pilosella subcymigera subsp. alethes

Pilosella subcymigera subsp.

alethes, commonly known as the Alethian pearly everlasting, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of both ray and disk flowers.

The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and wastelands. It produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, and its leaves are covered in fine, white hairs, giving it a soft, silvery appearance.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate, long-lasting flowers.
