Potentilla schmakovii

Potentilla schmakovii, commonly known as the Schmakov's cinquefoil, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit. The plant features small, yellow flowers that bloom in summer, typically arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems. Its leaves are divided into five oval-shaped lobes, giving it the common name "cinquefoil.".

Potentilla schmakovii is adapted to rocky and arid environments, making it a hardy species suitable for xeriscaping and rock gardens.
