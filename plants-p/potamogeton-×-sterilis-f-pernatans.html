Potamogeton × sterilis f. pernatans

Potamogeton × sterilis f. pernatans, commonly known as the water milfoil, is a hybrid aquatic plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit and small, scale-like leaves that are typically green to reddish in color. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often submerged, and it thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments. It is an important component of wetland ecosystems, providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.

However, it can become invasive in certain areas, outcompeting native vegetation.
