Polygonum longisetum

Polygonum longisetum, commonly known as the long-hair knotweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its erect, slender stems that are often covered with long, spreading hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in dense clusters at the ends of its stems. Its leaves are lance-shaped with serrated edges and are typically covered with fine, soft hairs.

Polygonum longisetum is often found in moist environments such as stream banks and wet meadows.
