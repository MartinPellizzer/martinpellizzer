Pavonia coccinea

Pavonia coccinea, commonly known as the scarlet pavonia, is a species of flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces showy, red bract-like flowers that resemble petals. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its botanical features include opposite leaves with serrated edges and clusters of flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

Pavonia coccinea is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
