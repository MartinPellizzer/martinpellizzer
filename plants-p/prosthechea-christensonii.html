Prosthechea christensonii

Prosthechea christensonii, commonly known as the Christenson's orchid, is a species of epiphytic orchid native to South America.

It belongs to the family Orchidaceae and is characterized by its compact growth habit and intricate floral structure. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, often with a contrasting lip. Its pseudobulbs are short and clustered, and it thrives in humid, shaded environments.

Prosthechea christensonii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical greenhouses.
