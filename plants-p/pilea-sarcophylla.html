Pilea sarcophylla

Pilea sarcophylla, commonly known as the stonecrop pilea, is a succulent plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid conditions. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils, which contribute to its hardy nature. Its small, star-shaped flowers are usually greenish-white, adding a subtle aesthetic to its appearance.

Pilea sarcophylla is well-suited for indoor cultivation due to its low maintenance requirements and ability to adapt to various light conditions.
