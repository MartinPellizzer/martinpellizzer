Phaeocephalum plankii

Phaeocephalum plankii, commonly known as the silverleaf flax, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and clusters of small, white or pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. Its botanical features include fibrous roots and a creeping stem that can form dense mats.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
