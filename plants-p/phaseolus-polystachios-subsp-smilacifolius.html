Phaseolus polystachios subsp. smilacifolius

Phaseolus polystachios subsp.

smilacifolius, commonly known as the smilacifolous bean, is a climbing legume native to Mexico. It features palmately compound leaves with elongated, pointed leaflets that resemble the foliage of smilax plants.

The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in racemes, followed by elongated, bean-like pods containing several seeds. It is characterized by its rapid growth habit and ability to thrive in warm, sunny environments.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential as a food crop in traditional agriculture.
