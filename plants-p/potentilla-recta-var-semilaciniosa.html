Potentilla recta var. semilaciniosa

Potentilla recta var. semilaciniosa, commonly known as the silky cinquefoil, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its soft, silvery-gray foliage that is covered in fine, woolly hairs, giving it a distinctive and visually appealing appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Its unique botanical features make it a valuable addition to both ornamental and ecological landscapes.
