Polygonum hyssopifolium

Polygonum hyssopifolium, commonly known as the woolly knotweed, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features upright, branched stems that are often covered in dense, soft white hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The leaves are opposite, ovate, and also hairy, with a slightly serrated margin. It produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or purple, blooming from late spring to early autumn.

This plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or disturbed areas.
