Paepalanthus elatus

Paepalanthus elatus, commonly known as the silver leaf cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of South America.

It is a small, leafless plant that thrives in rocky and sandy soils, often found in coastal areas. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom during the warmer months, adding a striking visual contrast to its silver-gray stems. Its unique appearance and drought tolerance make it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

Due to its ecological importance and limited distribution, it is considered a vulnerable species in some regions.
