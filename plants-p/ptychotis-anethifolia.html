Ptychotis anethifolia

Ptychotis anethifolia, commonly known as the anise-leaved daisy, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and is characterized by its finely divided, anise-scented leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are typically clustered in a flat-topped inflorescence. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Ptychotis anethifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aromatic foliage and delicate blooms.
