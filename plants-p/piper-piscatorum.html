Piper piscatorum

Piper piscatorum, commonly known as the fish poison plant, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many species of flowering plants. The plant is characterized by its climbing habit and heart-shaped leaves. It produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in spikes.

The plant is notable for containing toxic compounds that have been traditionally used to stun fish for easier capture.
