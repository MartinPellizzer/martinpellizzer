Polystachya laxa

Polystachya laxa, commonly known as the loose-stemmed bamboo orchid, is a species of orchid native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and linear, grass-like leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in panicles, which bloom during the rainy season. Polystachya laxa is typically found in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices.

Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense colonies in suitable habitats.
