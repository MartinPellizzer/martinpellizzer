Protea melaleuca

Protea melaleuca, commonly known as the sugarbush, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Proteaceae family and is characterized by its showy, cone-like inflorescences that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant typically grows in coastal and mountainous regions, thriving in well-drained, sandy soils. Its leaves are leathery and evergreen, providing a dense canopy that supports local biodiversity.

The flowers attract a variety of pollinators, including birds and insects, contributing to its ecological importance.
