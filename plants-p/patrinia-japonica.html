Patrinia japonica

Patrinia japonica, commonly known as Japanese Patrinia, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It belongs to the family Lamiaceae, also known as the mint family, and is characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically white or pale purple, arranged in whorls along the stem. It is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and stream banks.

Patrinia japonica is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.
