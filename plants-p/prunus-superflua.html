Prunus superflua

Prunus superflua, commonly known as the Japanese cherry or sakura, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its slender branches and clusters of small, white or pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, round fruits known as cherries, which are typically red when mature. Prunus superflua thrives in temperate climates and prefers well-drained soil.

It is widely cultivated for ornamental purposes and is a symbol of renewal and the changing seasons.
