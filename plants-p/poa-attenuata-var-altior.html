Poa attenuata var. altior

Poa attenuata var. altior, commonly known as the tall meadowgrass, is a variant of the meadowgrass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is a perennial grass characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and fine, bluish-green foliage. The plant typically thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open meadows, grasslands, and along the edges of woodlands. Its inflorescence consists of a loose to dense panicle, bearing small, two-lobed spikelets.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators and soil stability.
