Phyllanthus chrysanthus var. micrantheoides

Phyllanthus chrysanthus var. micrantheoides, commonly known as the small-flowered phyllanthus, is a member of the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa, particularly found in countries like Kenya and Tanzania. The plant features small, yellowish-green flowers clustered in umbels, and its leaves are typically alternate and ovate. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and traditional herbal practices.
