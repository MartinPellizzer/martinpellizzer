Pithecellobium falconiense

Pithecellobium falconiense, commonly known as the Falconi's pithecellobium, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, and is often found in humid, lowland areas. The plant produces small, clustered pods that contain edible seeds, which are sometimes used in local cuisines. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three leaflets that are opposite in arrangement, and it has a woody stem with thorns.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential ecological benefits in nitrogen fixation.
