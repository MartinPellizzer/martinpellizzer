Paronychia nitida

Paronychia nitida, commonly known as the silverleaf carnation, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, mat-forming growth habit and produces showy, pink to red flowers. The leaves are narrow, silver-green, and covered in fine hairs, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

This species is also valued for its drought tolerance and ability to attract pollinators.
