Phoebe subtriplinervia

Phoebe subtriplinervia, commonly known as the Florida beech, is a small evergreen tree native to the southeastern United States.

It has simple, elliptical leaves that are glossy and dark green on top, with a lighter green underside. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers in clusters, followed by small, leathery fruits. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an important component of the southeastern forest ecosystem.
