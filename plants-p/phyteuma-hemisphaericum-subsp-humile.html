Phyteuma hemisphaericum subsp. humile

Phyteuma hemisphaericum subsp.

humile, commonly known as the dwarf globe thistle, is a low-growing perennial plant native to southern Europe. It features a rosette of silver-gray, hairy leaves that are lance-shaped and covered in fine, soft hairs.

The plant produces showy, globe-shaped blue or purple flowers in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained, sunny soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ease of cultivation, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.
