Potentilla fragaria var. micrantha

Potentilla fragaria var. micrantha, commonly known as the dwarf cinquefoil, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the northern regions of North America.

It features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has narrow, slightly hairy leaves that are arranged in a compact, mat-like form. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a ground cover.

Botanically, it is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and its ability to spread via stolons, forming dense colonies.
