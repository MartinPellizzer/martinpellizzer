Polycycnis escobariana

Polycycnis escobariana, commonly known as the Escobaria cactus, is a species of flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northwestern Mexico.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its globular, ribbed stems that store water for survival in arid environments. The plant produces yellow or orange flowers during the spring and summer months, adding a striking visual contrast to its green foliage. Its leaves are modified into spines, which help reduce water loss and deter herbivores.

Polycycnis escobariana is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
