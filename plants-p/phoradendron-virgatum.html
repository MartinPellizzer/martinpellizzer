Phoradendron virgatum

Phoradendron virgatum, commonly known as the silver mistflower, is a climbing plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves and clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant is a hemiparasite, meaning it partially relies on other plants for water and nutrients through its specialized roots. Its leaves are usually alternate and have a silvery appearance due to a dense covering of fine hairs.

The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forests and along stream banks.
