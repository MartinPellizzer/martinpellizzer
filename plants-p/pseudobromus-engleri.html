Pseudobromus engleri

Pseudobromus engleri, commonly known as the Engler's false brome, is a grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands and rocky areas.

Due to its ornamental value and hardiness, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
