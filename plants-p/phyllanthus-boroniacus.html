Phyllanthus boroniacus

Phyllanthus boroniacus, commonly known as the African buttonwood, is a small evergreen shrub native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves that are typically elliptical or ovate in shape. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in yellowish-green cyathia, typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. Its fruits are capsules that contain tiny seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Phyllanthus boroniacus is often found in rocky or coastal habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance.
