Pentagonia clementinensis

Pentagonia clementinensis, commonly known as the Clementine pentagonia, is a species of flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its distinctive pentagonal-shaped leaves, which give it its scientific name. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow in color. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is often cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and modest floral display.
