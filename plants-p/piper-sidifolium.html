Piper sidifolium

Piper sidifolium, commonly known as the swordleaf pepper or sidifolium pepper, is a tropical climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species such as black pepper. The plant is characterized by its elongated, lance-shaped leaves that give it its common name. It produces small, green berries that turn red when mature, containing the aromatic seeds used in traditional medicine and cuisine.

The plant's botanical features make it an interesting subject for both horticultural study and pharmacological research.
