Palaquium bancanum

Palaquium bancanum, commonly known as the banana tree of the Philippines, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and broad, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by oval, woody fruits containing a single seed. It thrives in tropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of timber.

Palaquium bancanum is also valued for its ability to withstand drought conditions once established.
