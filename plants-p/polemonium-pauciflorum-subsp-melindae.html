Polemonium pauciflorum subsp. melindae

Polemonium pauciflorum subsp.

melindae, commonly known as Melinda's bluebells, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States. It belongs to the family Polemoniaceae and is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to purple.

The plant typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and has lanceolate leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. Its flowering period occurs in late spring to early summer, and it is often found in shaded environments.

The subspecies is distinguished by its smaller flower clusters and slightly different leaf morphology compared to the main species.
