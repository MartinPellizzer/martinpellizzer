Paracryphia suaveolens

Paracryphia suaveolens, commonly known as the sweet-scented paracryphia, is a species of flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its evergreen, glossy leaves that are often fragrant when crushed. The plant produces small, white flowers that are typically clustered in terminal inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value and aromatic foliage, it is cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
