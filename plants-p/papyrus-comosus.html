Papyrus comosus

Papyrus comosus, commonly known as the giant papyrus, is a tall, reed-like plant native to the wetlands of Africa.

It has long, narrow leaves that can grow up to 5 meters in length, and it produces a distinctive, fluffy seed head at the top of its stem. The plant thrives in shallow water and is often found in marshy areas along rivers and lakes. Its fibrous stalks were historically used by ancient Egyptians for making paper and baskets.

The plant plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
