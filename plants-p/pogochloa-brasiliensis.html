Pogochloa brasiliensis

Pogochloa brasiliensis, commonly known as the Brazilian grass or *Pogochloa brasiliensis*, is a species of grass native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes many other grasses. The plant is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. It typically grows in open habitats such as grasslands and savannas.

Pogochloa brasiliensis is often used in ecological studies due to its role in soil stabilization and habitat provision for various small organisms.
