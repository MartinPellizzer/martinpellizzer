Pesomeria tetragona

Pesomeria tetragona, commonly known as the four-angled plectranthus, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its square stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. It produces small, tubular flowers that are typically violet or blue in color.

Pesomeria tetragona is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and fragrance.
