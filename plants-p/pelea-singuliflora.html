Pelea singuliflora

Pelea singuliflora, commonly known as the single-flowered pelea, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its solitary, showy flowers that typically bloom in the spring. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and is often found in sandy or rocky soils. It is a member of the Amaranthaceae family, which includes other plants with similar growth habits.

Pelea singuliflora is often cultivated for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
