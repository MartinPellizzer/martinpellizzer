Prunus × syriaca

Prunus × syriaca, commonly known as the European mountain ash or common ash, is a hybrid species of the Prunus genus.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically opposite and have serrated edges. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clusters, followed by small, edible berries that are often consumed by birds. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Prunus × syriaca is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
