Pentapogon densus

Pentapogon densus, commonly known as the dense five-lobed orchid, is a rare and specialized orchid species found in specific high-altitude habitats.

It is characterized by its compact, dense growth habit and distinctive floral structure with five lobes. The plant produces small, intricate flowers that are typically white or pale pink, adapted for specific pollinators. It thrives in cool, moist environments with well-drained soil, often in mountainous regions.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
