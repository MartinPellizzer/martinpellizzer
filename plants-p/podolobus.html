Podolobus

Podolobus, commonly known as the African violet, is a genus of flowering plants native to tropical Africa.

These plants are characterized by their showy, velvety flowers that come in a variety of colors, including purple, pink, and white. The leaves are typically rounded and glossy, often forming a rosette pattern at the base of the plant. Podolobus species are popular houseplants due to their ability to thrive in low-light conditions and their compact growth habit.

They are also valued for their ornamental appeal and ease of care.
