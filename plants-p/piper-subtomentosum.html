Piper subtomentosum

Piper subtomentosum, commonly known as the hairy pepper vine, is a climbing shrub native to tropical regions of Asia and Africa.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many species of pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and heart-shaped, alternate leaves. Its small, greenish-yellow flowers are followed by berries that turn red when mature.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in some cultures.
