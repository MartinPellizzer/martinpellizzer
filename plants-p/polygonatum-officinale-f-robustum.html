Polygonatum officinale f. robustum

Polygonatum officinale f. robustum, commonly known as the Chinese Solomon's seal, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to East Asia.

It features a rhizomatous stem system that stores nutrients and supports growth. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are arranged in a basal rosette, and it produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers in early summer. Its flowers are followed by red berries that mature in late summer or early autumn.

This species is valued in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.
