Pancratium tazetta

Pancratium tazetta, commonly known as the sea daffodil, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of the Mediterranean and parts of southern Europe.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, often with a strong, sweet fragrance. The plant has strap-like, narrow leaves that are typically blue-green in color. It thrives in well-drained sandy or rocky soils and is often found growing in coastal dunes or along the edges of the sea.

Pancratium tazetta is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
