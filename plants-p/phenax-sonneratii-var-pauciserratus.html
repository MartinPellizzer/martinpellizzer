Phenax sonneratii var. pauciserratus

Phenax sonneratii var. pauciserratus, commonly known as the narrow-toothed phenax, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its small, clustered inflorescences and distinctive, lanceolate leaves with few serrations. The plant produces pale yellow to orange flowers, which are typically arranged in dense clusters. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in humid lowland forests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and greenhouses.
