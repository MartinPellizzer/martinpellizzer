Pavetta oliveriana var. oliveriana

Pavetta oliveriana var. oliveriana, commonly known as Oliver's pavetta, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring opposite, ovate to elliptic leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are often borne in the axils of the leaves. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, winged seeds.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its foliage and floral display.
