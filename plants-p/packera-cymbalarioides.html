Packera cymbalarioides

Packera cymbalarioides, commonly known as the Alpine golden ragwort, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils at high elevations. Its leaves are lanceolate and covered in fine pubescence, while its yellow flowers bloom in late summer.

It plays a vital role in pollinator support and is often found in alpine meadows and tundra environments.
