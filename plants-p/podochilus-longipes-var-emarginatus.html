Podochilus longipes var. emarginatus

Podochilus longipes var. emarginatus, commonly known as the emarginated orchid, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial orchid with a distinctive appearance, featuring elongated, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces inflorescences with numerous small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom during the rainy season. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb moisture from the humid environment.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
