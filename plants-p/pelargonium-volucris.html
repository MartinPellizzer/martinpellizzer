Pelargonium volucris

Pelargonium volucris, commonly known as the bird's nest geranium, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive foliage, which forms a dense, rounded rosette resembling a bird's nest. The plant produces showy, five-petaled flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, typically blooming in summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Pelargonium volucris is also valued for its ornamental appeal and relatively low maintenance requirements.
