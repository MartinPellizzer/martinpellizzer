Plectocephalus americanus

Plectocephalus americanus, commonly known as the American hawksbeard, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its yellow, rayless composite flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in open habitats such as meadows and roadsides, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stem is erect and branching.

The species is known for its adaptability and is often found in disturbed soils.
