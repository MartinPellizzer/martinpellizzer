Pleurothallis amaliae

Pleurothallis amaliae, commonly known as the Amalia's orchid, is a species native to the cloud forests of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that often display a range of colors, including white, pink, and purple. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to tree bark and deriving moisture and nutrients from the air and rain.

Its intricate floral structure and specialized pollination mechanisms make it a fascinating subject in botanical studies.
