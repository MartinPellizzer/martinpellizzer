Pyrus malus var. parkmannii

Pyrus malus var. parkmannii, commonly known as the Parkmann's apple, is a botanical variant of the common apple.

It is a deciduous tree native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. The plant is characterized by its small, oval-shaped leaves and clusters of white flowers that bloom in spring. Its fruits are typically small, tart apples that are not commonly cultivated for commercial use.

This variety is of interest to botanists and horticulturists due to its genetic diversity and potential for breeding programs.
