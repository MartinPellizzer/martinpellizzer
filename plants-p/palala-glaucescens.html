Palala glaucescens

Palala glaucescens, commonly known as the silverleaf palala, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery down, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, pale green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, typically blooming during the rainy season. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Palala glaucescens is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been cultivated for its unique foliage.
