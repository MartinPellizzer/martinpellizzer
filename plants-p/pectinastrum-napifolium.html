Pectinastrum napifolium

Pectinastrum napifolium, commonly known as the silky fig, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pale green to yellowish in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.

The fruit is a small, fleshy berry that ripens to a red or orange color.
