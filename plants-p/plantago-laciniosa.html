Plantago laciniosa

Plantago laciniosa, commonly known as the broadleaf plantain, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It features a rosette of broad, oval-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, hair-like structures called trichomes. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in a dense spike that emerges from the center of the rosette. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their soothing and anti-inflammatory properties.

Botanically, it is characterized by its basal leaf arrangement and its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types.
