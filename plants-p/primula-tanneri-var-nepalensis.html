Primula tanneri var. nepalensis

Primula tanneri var. nepalensis, commonly known as the Nepal primrose, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayan regions of Nepal and northern India.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink and white. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments at high altitudes, often found in alpine meadows and rocky habitats. Its leaves are ovate to elliptical, with a hairy texture, and it produces small, clustered flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable climatic conditions.
