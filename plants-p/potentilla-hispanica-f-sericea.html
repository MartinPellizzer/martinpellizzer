Potentilla hispanica f. sericea

Potentilla hispanica f. sericea, commonly known as the silky cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe.

It is a low-growing shrub characterized by its finely hairy, silver-gray foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers in late spring to early summer, which are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardeners seeking drought-tolerant and visually appealing plants.
