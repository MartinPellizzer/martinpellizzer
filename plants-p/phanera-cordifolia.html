Phanera cordifolia

Phanera cordifolia, commonly known as the heartleaf phanera, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves, which are typically glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are often white or pale yellow, arranged in clusters. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening in tropical regions.
