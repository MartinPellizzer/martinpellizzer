Potentilla alpestris var. crocea

Potentilla alpestris var. crocea, commonly known as the alpine cinquefoil, is a perennial flowering plant native to mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky habitats, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are compound, consisting of five oval-shaped leaflets, and it is often found in high-altitude environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience in harsh conditions.
