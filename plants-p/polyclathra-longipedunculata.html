Polyclathra longipedunculata

Polyclathra longipedunculata, commonly known as the long-pedunculate stinkwood, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, dark bark and can grow up to 30 meters tall. The plant has opposite, simple, and ovate leaves that are usually dark green on top and lighter underneath. Its flowers are small and yellow, arranged in long, drooping clusters, and it produces small, fleshy fruits.

The plant is often used in traditional medicine and has ecological significance in its native habitats.
