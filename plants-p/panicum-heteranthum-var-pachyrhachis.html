Panicum heteranthum var. pachyrhachis

Panicum heteranthum var. pachyrhachis, commonly known as the broad-ribbed panicgrass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences that are typically greenish or reddish in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or grasslands.

Due to its ornamental appeal and adaptability, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
