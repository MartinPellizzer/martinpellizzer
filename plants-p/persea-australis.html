Persea australis

Persea australis, commonly known as the Australian laurel, is a species of flowering plant in the family Lauraceae.

It is native to southeastern Australia, where it typically grows in moist, temperate forests. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and small, fragrant white flowers. Its fruit is a smooth, oval berry that turns from green to yellow or red as it matures.

Persea australis is valued for its ornamental qualities and is also used in traditional Aboriginal medicine.
