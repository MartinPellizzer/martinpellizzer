Picris hispida

Picris hispida, commonly known as goat's beard, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its yellow flowers and hairy, bristly stems and leaves, which give it its common name. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky, or sandy soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and wastelands. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are clustered in loose inflorescences.

Picris hispida is also known for its medicinal properties, having been used traditionally in herbal remedies.
