Persicaria komarovii

Persicaria komarovii, commonly known as Komarov's knotweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, which includes plants known for their distinctive leaf shapes and often edible parts. The plant features upright stems that are often reddish in color and are covered with small, translucent glands. Its leaves are lance-shaped with a prominent midrib and are arranged alternately along the stem.

It produces small, greenish flowers in dense clusters, typically blooming in late summer.
