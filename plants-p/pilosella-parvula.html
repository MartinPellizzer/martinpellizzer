Pilosella parvula

Pilosella parvula, commonly known as the small hairy groundsel, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching a height of about 10 to 30 centimeters. Its leaves are hairy and alternate, while the flowers are yellow with pale green centers.

Pilosella parvula is often found in open habitats and is adapted to thrive in temperate climates.
