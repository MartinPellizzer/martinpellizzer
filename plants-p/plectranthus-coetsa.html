Plectranthus coetsa

Plectranthus coetsa, commonly known as the Cape verbena, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing stems and clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers. Its leaves are typically gray-green, with a slightly fuzzy texture, and it produces fragrant blooms throughout the growing season.

Plectranthus coetsa is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
