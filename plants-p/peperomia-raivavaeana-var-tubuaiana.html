Peperomia raivavaeana var. tubuaiana

Peperomia raivavaeana var. tubuaiana, commonly known as the Tubuaian peperomia, is a rare and endemic species native to the island of Tubua'i in French Polynesia.

It is a low-growing herbaceous plant with thick, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a waxy cuticle, an adaptation to its arid and coastal environment. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences with pale green to white flowers, which are typically pollinated by insects. Its unique morphology and limited distribution make it a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists.

Due to habitat loss and limited propagation, it is considered a vulnerable species requiring careful conservation efforts.
