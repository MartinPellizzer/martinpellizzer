Patrisa canescens

Patrisa canescens, commonly known as the silverleaf lily, is a flowering plant native to parts of South America.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage and showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of white and pink. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a fine, powdery coating that gives them their distinctive appearance.

Patrisa canescens is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
