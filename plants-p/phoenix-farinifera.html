Phoenix farinifera

Phoenix farinifera, commonly known as the Arabian date palm, is a tall, slender palm native to the deserts of the Arabian Peninsula.

It has a slender trunk with a smooth, grayish-brown bark and produces long, feather-like leaves that are bright green and covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant is well adapted to arid environments, with deep roots that allow it to access water from deep underground. It produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers that develop into edible date fruits.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical significance in desert cultures.
