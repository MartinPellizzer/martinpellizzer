Primula dejuniana

Primula dejuniana, commonly known as the Sierra Madre primrose, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in rocky or woodland habitats, preferring cool and moist environments. Its leaves are ovate and hairy, providing a textured appearance.

Primula dejuniana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
