Pterocactus kurtzei

Pterocactus kurtzei, commonly known as the Kutch pterocactus, is a species of cactus native to the arid regions of western India.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems that are covered in dense, white spines, which help to reduce water loss and protect against herbivores. The plant produces yellow flowers during the rainy season, adding a striking contrast to its otherwise desolate appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires minimal water, making it well adapted to its harsh environment.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological significance, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists alike.
