Portulaca grandiflora var. purpurea

Portulaca grandiflora var. purpurea, commonly known as the purple portulaca, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is a succulent species with fleshy, thick stems and leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. The plant produces vibrant, showy flowers in shades of red, pink, and purple, which bloom during the day and close at night. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun exposure, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Its rapid growth and drought tolerance make it an easy-to-care-for ornamental plant.
