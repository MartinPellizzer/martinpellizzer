Paracroton sterrhopodus

Paracroton sterrhopodus, commonly known as the African flame tree, is a tropical tree native to eastern Africa.

It is characterized by its large, showy orange-red flowers that bloom in clusters during the dry season. The plant has a straight, often buttressed trunk and broad, palmate leaves that are dark green and leathery. Its seeds are enclosed in woody pods and are dispersed by animals.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
