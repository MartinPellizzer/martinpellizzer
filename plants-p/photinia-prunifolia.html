Photinia prunifolia

Photinia prunifolia, commonly known as the redleaf photinia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its striking red to orange-red leaves, which provide vibrant seasonal color. The plant produces small, white flowers in spring, followed by red berries that are edible for birds. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often used in landscaping for its attractive foliage and hardy nature.
