Plectranthus poggeanus

Plectranthus poggeanus, commonly known as the Cape verbena, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant features square stems and opposite leaves, typical of the mint family. It produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that are usually blue or purple in color.

Plectranthus poggeanus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow in well-drained soil.
