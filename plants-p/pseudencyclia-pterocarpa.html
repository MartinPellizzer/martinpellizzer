Pseudencyclia pterocarpa

Pseudencyclia pterocarpa, commonly known as the broadleaf cycad, is a unique plant belonging to the Cycadaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, feathery leaves that can grow up to several meters in length. The plant produces cones, with male and female cones typically growing on separate plants. Its stems are thick and woody, often forming a trunk-like structure.

Native to parts of Southeast Asia, it thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with well-drained soil.
