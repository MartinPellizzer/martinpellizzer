Ptychosperma polystachyum

Ptychosperma polystachyum, commonly known as the giant cycad, is a member of the Cycadaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, feather-like leaves that can grow up to several meters in length. The plant produces large, cone-like structures that contain both male and female reproductive organs. It thrives in tropical climates with high humidity and well-drained soil.

Due to its slow growth and conservation status, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and conservatories.
