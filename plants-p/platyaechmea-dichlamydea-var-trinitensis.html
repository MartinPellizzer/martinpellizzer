Platyaechmea dichlamydea var. trinitensis

Platyaechmea dichlamydea var. trinitensis, commonly known as the Trinidadian star cactus, is a striking bromeliad native to the Caribbean.

It features rosettes of fleshy, dark green leaves that are often adorned with white or pale markings. The plant produces vibrant orange or red flowers in a distinctive star-shaped inflorescence, which attracts pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical and subtropical gardens.
