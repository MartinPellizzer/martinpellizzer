Phymatodes variabilis

Phymatodes variabilis, commonly known as the desert lily, is a perennial plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves and showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky soils. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to access water deep underground.

Phymatodes variabilis plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing nectar for pollinators in its ecosystem.
