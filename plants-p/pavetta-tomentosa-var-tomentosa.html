Pavetta tomentosa var. tomentosa

Pavetta tomentosa var. tomentosa, commonly known as the woolly pavetta, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves that give it a distinctive, velvety appearance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink, often with a subtle fragrance. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories or along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural study.
