Polygala campestris

Polygala campestris, commonly known as the field milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, usually greenish or yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and wastelands. It produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Polygala campestris is often used in traditional medicine and has been studied for its potential pharmacological properties.
