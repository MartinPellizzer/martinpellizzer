Primula barnardoana

Primula barnardoana, commonly known as the Barnardo's primrose, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of central and southern China.

It typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils at elevations ranging from 1,500 to 3,000 meters. The plant features basal rosettes of lanceolate, hairy leaves and produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, yellow, and orange. Its flowering period occurs in spring, usually between April and June.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms and adaptability to temperate climates.
