Phalaris nitida

Phalaris nitida, commonly known as silver hairgrass, is a perennial grass native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and dense clusters of tiny, silver-gray spikelets that give it its common name. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows, marshes, and along stream banks. Its fine, silky appearance makes it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping.

Phalaris nitida is known for its ability to thrive in partial shade and its tolerance to wet conditions.
