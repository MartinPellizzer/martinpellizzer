Pithecellobium laxiflorum

Pithecellobium laxiflorum, commonly known as the Mexican honey tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant produces small, fragrant yellow flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in diverse soil conditions.
