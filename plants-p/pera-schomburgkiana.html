Pera schomburgkiana

Pera schomburgkiana, commonly known as the Schomburgk's pera, is a rare and striking plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 centimeters in diameter. The plant produces striking yellow-orange flowers with a distinctive purple-pink corolla, which bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its rarity and ornamental value, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by plant enthusiasts.
