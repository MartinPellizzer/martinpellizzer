Polygonum debeauxii

Polygonum debeauxii, commonly known as the Beaux's knotweed, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of China.

It is characterized by its erect, slender stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or reddish in color, blooming in late summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Polygonum debeauxii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and floral display.
