Potameia vacciniifolia

Potameia vacciniifolia, commonly known as the silverleaf shrub, is a species of flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, silver-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, white hairs. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters during the warmer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Potameia vacciniifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
