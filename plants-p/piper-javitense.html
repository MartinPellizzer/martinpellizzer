Piper javitense

Piper javitense, commonly known as the Javitense pepper or Javitense long pepper, is a climbing vine belonging to the Piperaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil and Peru. The plant produces small, elongated berries that are used in traditional medicine and as a spice. Its leaves are often used in herbal remedies for their purported anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties.

The plant's botanical characteristics include opposite, ovate leaves and a distinctive, aromatic scent.
