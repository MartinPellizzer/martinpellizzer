Potamogeton × zizii subvar. lucescens

Potamogeton × zizii subvar. lucescens, commonly known as the yellow pondweed, is a hybrid aquatic plant characterized by its bright yellow flowers and slender, ribbon-like leaves.

It is typically found in shallow freshwater habitats such as lakes, ponds, and slow-moving streams. The plant has a submerged stem with nodes that produce both roots and shoots, allowing it to spread vegetatively. Its flowers are often clustered in dense, spike-like inflorescences, and it blooms during the summer months.

This species plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various waterfowl and invertebrates.
