Pedicularis gracilis subsp. macrocarpa

Pedicularis gracilis subsp.

macrocarpa, commonly known as the large-fruited lousewort, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia. It belongs to the Orobanchaceae family, which includes parasitic and semi-parasitic species.

This subspecies is characterized by its slender, erect stems and showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant is semi-parasitic, deriving some of its nutrients from the roots of neighboring plants.
