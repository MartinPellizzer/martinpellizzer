Pronephrium pentaphyllum

Pronephrium pentaphyllum, commonly known as the five-lobed bladderwort, is a carnivorous plant native to freshwater environments in Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, bladder-like structures that trap and digest aquatic insects. The plant has a submerged, branched stem with whorled leaves that aid in photosynthesis. Its unique morphology allows it to thrive in nutrient-poor waters by supplementing its nutrients through prey capture.

The species is an example of adaptive evolution in aquatic ecosystems.
