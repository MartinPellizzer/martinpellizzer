Phyllanthus toninensis

Phyllanthus toninensis, commonly known as the African date palm, is a small shrub native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by fleshy, orange-red fruits that are edible. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is cultivated in some regions for both horticultural and traditional uses.
