Physalis acutifolia

Physalis acutifolia, commonly known as the acute-leaved husk tomato, is a species of flowering plant in the nightshade family.

It is native to South America, particularly Argentina and Chile, and thrives in dry, arid environments. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into the characteristic inflated, papery fruit capsules. Its leaves are typically narrow and pointed, which gives it its common name.

The fruit, while not as widely cultivated as other Physalis species, is sometimes used in traditional medicine and as a food source in certain regions.
