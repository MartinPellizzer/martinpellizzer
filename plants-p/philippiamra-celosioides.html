Philippiamra celosioides

Philippiamra celosioides, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are velvety to the touch, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in spikes, followed by elliptical, winged fruits. It is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands.

Due to its rapid growth and invasive tendencies, it can become a problematic weed in agricultural and natural ecosystems.
