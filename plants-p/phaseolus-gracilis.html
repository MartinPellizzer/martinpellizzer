Phaseolus gracilis

Phaseolus gracilis, commonly known as the slender bean, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its climbing habit and small, pod-like fruits. The plant produces white or pink flowers and has trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets. Its seeds are typically small and oval, with a smooth surface, and are used in traditional cuisines.

Phaseolus gracilis is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it an important crop in sustainable agriculture.
