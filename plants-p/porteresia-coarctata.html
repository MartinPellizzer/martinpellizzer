Porteresia coarctata

Porteresia coarctata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved arrowroot, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a herbaceous perennial that grows in wet, swampy areas and thrives in humid climates. The plant features long, narrow leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by capsule fruits. Its rhizomes are edible and are used in traditional cuisines for their starchy texture.

The botanical characteristics of Porteresia coarctata make it an important species in both ecological and agricultural contexts.
