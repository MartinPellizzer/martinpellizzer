Plantago correae

Plantago correae, commonly known as the Correa's plantain, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and rocky crevices. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a rough, hairy texture and produces small, greenish-white flowers in late spring. Its seeds are encased in a distinctive, cap-like structure called an urn, which aids in wind dispersal.

This species is valued for its ecological role in supporting local insect populations and its potential medicinal properties.
