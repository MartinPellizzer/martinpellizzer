Phlox caryophylla

Phlox caryophylla, commonly known as the Carolina phlox, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features showy, clustered flowers that range in color from pink to purple, blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, with narrow, linear leaves that are aromatic when crushed. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable addition to native plant gardens and pollinator habitats.
