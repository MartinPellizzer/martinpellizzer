Primula stracheyi

Primula stracheyi, commonly known as the Strachey's primrose, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia, particularly in China and Nepal.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments at high altitudes, often in rocky or forested areas. Its leaves are oval-shaped with a rough texture, and it produces small, clustered flowers that bloom in early spring.

Primula stracheyi is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
