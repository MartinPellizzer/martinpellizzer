Pyrrhocactus jussieui

Pyrrhocactus jussieui, commonly known as the Jussieui cactus, is a unique member of the Cactaceae family characterized by its compact, globular shape and dense arrangement of cylindrical spines.

It originates from arid regions of South America, where it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun exposure. The plant features a thick, fleshy stem that stores water, allowing it to survive in drought conditions. Its flowers, which are typically yellow or orange, bloom in late spring and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its slow growth and ornamental appeal, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
