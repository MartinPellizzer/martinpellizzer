Physostegia virginiana var. obovata

Physostegia virginiana var. obovata, commonly known as the whorled pogostemon, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It features opposite, obovate leaves that are often hairy and have a slightly serrated margin. The plant produces spikes of showy, violet-blue flowers that bloom in late summer. Its root system is fibrous, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

It is often found in wetlands, stream banks, and other damp habitats.
