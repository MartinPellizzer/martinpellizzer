Potentilla rivalis var. millegrana

Potentilla rivalis var. millegrana, commonly known as the silver potentilla, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of central and eastern Europe.

It features small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has finely divided, silver-green leaves that give it a distinctive, ornamental appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a border plant.

Its compact growth habit and hardy nature make it a popular choice for gardeners seeking low-maintenance, decorative plants.
