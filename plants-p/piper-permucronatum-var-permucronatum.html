Piper permucronatum var. permucronatum

Piper permucronatum var. permucronatum, commonly known as the purple pepper plant, is a climbing shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, violet flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant produces small, edible berries that turn from green to purple as they mature. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value and unique fruit, it is often cultivated in tropical and subtropical regions.
