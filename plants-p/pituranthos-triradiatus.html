Pituranthos triradiatus

Pituranthos triradiatus, commonly known as the three-rayed pituranthos, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its striking, triangular-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are clustered in dense, spherical inflorescences, which bloom in the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is often cultivated in xeriscaped gardens.
