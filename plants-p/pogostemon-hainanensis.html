Pogostemon hainanensis

Pogostemon hainanensis, commonly known as Hainan betony, is a perennial herb native to southern China, particularly Hainan Island.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant features opposite, lanceolate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, two-lipped flowers in clusters. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
