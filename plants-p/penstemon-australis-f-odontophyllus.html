Penstemon australis f. odontophyllus

Penstemon australis f. odontophyllus, commonly known as the toothed-leaved penstemon, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, tubular flowers. The leaves are typically lance-shaped with a distinctive toothed margin, which gives the plant its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to arid environments.
