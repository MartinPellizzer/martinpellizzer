Potentilla speciosa var. discolor

Potentilla speciosa var. discolor, commonly known as the showy cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has compound leaves with five leaflets, which are typically oval and serrated. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its botanical features make it both ornamental and ecologically valuable in native habitats.
