Pandanophyllum palustre

Pandanophyllum palustre, commonly known as the swamp pandan, is a herbaceous plant native to wetland environments in Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Pandanaceae and is characterized by its aromatic, strap-like leaves that are often used in traditional cooking. The plant typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, thriving in marshy and swampy habitats. Its leaves contain essential oils that contribute to its distinctive fragrance.

Pandanophyllum palustre plays a role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems and providing habitat for various small organisms.
