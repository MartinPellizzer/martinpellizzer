Potamogeton gaudichaudii

Potamogeton gaudichaudii, commonly known as the Gaudichaud's pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater habitats in Southeast Asia.

It has long, narrow leaves that are typically arranged in whorls around the stem, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are pollinated by wind. The plant thrives in still or slow-moving waters and is often found in ponds, lakes, and wetlands. It plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.

Its presence can indicate good water quality due to its sensitivity to pollution.
