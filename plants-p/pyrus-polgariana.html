Pyrus polgariana

Pyrus polgariana, commonly known as the Polish pear, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Eastern Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers in the spring, followed by edible, sweet-tart pears in the autumn. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun.

Pyrus polgariana is also valued for its hardiness and ability to adapt to various climatic conditions.
