Phoebe tetragona

Phoebe tetragona, commonly known as the four-angled phoebe, is a small evergreen shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves that are typically four-angled in cross-section, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that are clustered in terminal panicles, followed by small, spherical berries. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories or along stream banks.

Phoebe tetragona is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
