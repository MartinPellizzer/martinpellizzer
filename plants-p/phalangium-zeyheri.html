Phalangium zeyheri

Phalangium zeyheri, commonly known as the Zeyher's scorpion plant, is a unique member of the Asparagaceae family.

It is native to South Africa and is characterized by its peculiar, scorpion-like appearance due to its elongated, segmented leaves. The plant produces small, white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in summer, adding a delicate touch to its otherwise spiky form. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Despite its intimidating look, it is a relatively low-maintenance plant that can be a striking addition to succulent gardens.
