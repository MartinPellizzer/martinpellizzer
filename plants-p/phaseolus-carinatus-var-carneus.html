Phaseolus carinatus var. carneus

Phaseolus carinatus var. carneus, commonly known as the thick-ribbed bean, is a species native to Central America and northern South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, ridged pods and kidney-shaped seeds that are typically white or yellow in color. The plant is an erect annual legume that grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. Its leaves are trifoliate, with each leaflet having a hairy surface, and it produces small, yellow flowers.

This variety is valued for its nutritional content and is often cultivated for both food and traditional use.
