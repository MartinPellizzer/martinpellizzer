Phalaenopsis elisabethae

Phalaenopsis elisabethae, commonly known as the Elizabeth's orchid, is a species of orchid native to the Philippines.

It is characterized by its long, pendant-like inflorescences that bear clusters of white or pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its leaves are broad and fleshy, adapted to store moisture in the tropical environment.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for horticultural purposes.
