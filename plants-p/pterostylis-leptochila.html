Pterostylis leptochila

Pterostylis leptochila, commonly known as the slender-leafed duck orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to southeastern Australia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and rocky crevices. The plant features a slender, wiry stem with a single, lance-shaped leaf. Its flowers are small, greenish-white, and have a distinctive labellum that resembles a duck's beak, giving it its common name.

Pterostylis leptochila is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is known for its specialized pollination mechanism involving specific insect species.
