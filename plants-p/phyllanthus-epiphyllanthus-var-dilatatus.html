Phyllanthus epiphyllanthus var. dilatatus

Phyllanthus epiphyllanthus var. dilatatus, commonly known as the broad-leaved phyllanthus, is a tropical plant characterized by its large, broad leaves that are often lobed or serrated.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is native to regions of Southeast Asia, including parts of India and Malaysia. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte on trees, utilizing its aerial roots to absorb moisture and nutrients from the air and rain. Its flowering occurs in clusters, producing small yellowish flowers that are followed by distinctive orange or red seed capsules.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
