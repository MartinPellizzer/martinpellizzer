Plerandra gabriellae

Plerandra gabriellae, commonly known as the Gabrielle's arum, is a tropical plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves and dramatic inflorescence. The plant features a spathe that is typically dark green with a white or pale pink interior, surrounded by a cluster of small, clustered flowers. Plerandra gabriellae thrives in humid, shady environments and is often found in rainforests.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
