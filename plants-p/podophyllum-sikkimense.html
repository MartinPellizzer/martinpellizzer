Podophyllum sikkimense

Podophyllum sikkimense, commonly known as the Sikkim podophyllum, is a perennial herb native to the Himalayan region of India.

It belongs to the family Podophyllaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, clustered, reddish-brown stems. The plant features simple, alternate leaves that are usually lanceolate in shape. It produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by red, berry-like fruits.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
