Plantago panormitana

Plantago panormitana, commonly known as the Panormitan plantain, is a species of flowering plant in the family Plantaginaceae.

It is native to the Mediterranean region, particularly found in parts of southern Europe and the Middle East. The plant is characterized by its rosette-forming leaves that are broad, lance-shaped, and covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. It produces small, greenish flowers arranged in a spike, which are typically wind-pollinated.

This species is often found in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to thrive in arid environments.
