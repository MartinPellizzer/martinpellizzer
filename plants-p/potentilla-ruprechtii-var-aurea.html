Potentilla ruprechtii var. aurea

Potentilla ruprechtii var. aurea, commonly known as the golden potentilla, is a compact, deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, adding a vibrant splash of color to gardens. The plant has finely divided, silvery-green leaves that provide an attractive foliage contrast. It is well-suited for rock gardens and dry, well-drained soils due to its drought tolerance.

Its ornamental value comes from both its showy flowers and its hardy, low-maintenance nature.
