Piper marginatum f. catalpifolium

Piper marginatum f. catalpifolium, commonly known as the Catalpa-leaved pepper plant, is a unique variant of the pepper plant characterized by its distinctive foliage.

This plant features elongated, lance-shaped leaves that resemble the leaves of the catalpa tree, giving it its common name. Botanically, it belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes various species of pepper plants. The leaves are typically glossy and have a slightly serrated edge, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

It is native to tropical regions and thrives in warm, humid climates with well-drained soil.
