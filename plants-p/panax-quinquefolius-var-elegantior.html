Panax quinquefolius var. elegantior

Panax quinquefolius var. elegantior, commonly known as the elegant ginseng, is a variant of American ginseng characterized by its distinctive foliage and root structure.

It is native to the eastern United States and thrives in rich, moist forest soils. The plant features compound leaves with five to seven leaflets, giving it a more refined appearance compared to the standard variety. Its roots are valued for their medicinal properties, similar to those of regular ginseng.

This variant is often cultivated for both ornamental and herbal purposes.
