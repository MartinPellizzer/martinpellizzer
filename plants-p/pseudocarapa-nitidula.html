Pseudocarapa nitidula

Pseudocarapa nitidula, commonly known as the glossy carapa, is a small evergreen tree native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant is notable for its smooth, dark green foliage and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a pod containing several seeds, and the tree is often found in moist, shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
