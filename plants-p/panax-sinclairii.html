Panax sinclairii

Panax sinclairii, commonly known as the Sinclair ginseng, is a rare and critically endangered species native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the Araliaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, fleshy roots and broad, lance-shaped leaves. The plant thrives in cool, humid climates with well-drained soil, often growing in shaded forest environments. Its botanical significance lies in its potential medicinal properties, though research on its chemical composition remains limited.

Due to habitat loss and overharvesting, conservation efforts are crucial for its survival.
