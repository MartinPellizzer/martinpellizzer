Primula roxburghii

Primula roxburghii, commonly known as the Chinese primrose, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Himalayas and parts of East Asia.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple, typically blooming in early spring. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are dark green and slightly hairy, and it thrives in cool, moist environments. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.

The species is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
