Peperomia carapasana

Peperomia carapasana, commonly known as the Carpathian peperomia, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Europe.

It features thick, waxy leaves that are typically dark green with a glossy surface, often adorned with distinctive veins. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters, which are usually white or pale green. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers indirect light, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Due to its slow growth and attractive foliage, it is valued for its ornamental appeal and ease of care.
