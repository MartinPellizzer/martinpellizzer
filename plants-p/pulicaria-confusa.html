Pulicaria confusa

Pulicaria confusa, commonly known as the common tansy, is a flowering plant native to Europe and western Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial herb that typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features gray-green, fuzzy leaves and produces yellow flowers in clusters during the summer months. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads.

Pulicaria confusa thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry grasslands and rocky habitats.
