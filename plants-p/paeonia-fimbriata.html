Paeonia fimbriata

Paeonia fimbriata, commonly known as the Japanese peony, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan and Korea.

It is characterized by its showy, double or semi-double flowers that range in color from white to deep pink. The plant has compound, pinnate leaves that are green and hairy on the underside. It typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters and prefers well-drained, fertile soil.

Paeonia fimbriata is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its spring blooms.
