Prunus mume var. grandiflora

Prunus mume var. grandiflora, commonly known as the Japanese flowering plum, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, fragrant flowers that bloom in early spring before the leaves appear. The plant produces small, edible fruits known as mume, which are used in traditional medicine and culinary applications. Its ornamental value is enhanced by its glossy dark green leaves and attractive bark.

Prunus mume var. grandiflora thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.
