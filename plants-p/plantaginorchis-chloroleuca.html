Plantaginorchis chloroleuca

Plantaginorchis chloroleuca, commonly known as the green-leaved orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to parts of Asia, including China and Japan.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, hairy texture. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Botanically, it belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is noted for its unique floral structure and ecological adaptations.
