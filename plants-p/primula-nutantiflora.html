Primula nutantiflora

Primula nutantiflora, commonly known as the hanging primrose, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of China.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in spring. The plant has a low-growing habit with hairy stems and broad, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are borne on long, drooping pedicels, giving it a cascading appearance.

This species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in shaded, moist environments.
