Penstemon guadalupensis subsp. ernestii

Penstemon guadalupensis subsp.

ernestii, commonly known as Ernest's beardtongue, is a perennial flowering plant native to the southwestern United States. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces showy, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to violet.

The plant typically thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it well-suited for arid and semi-arid environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its drought tolerance.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
