Phylica strigosa var. strigosa

Phylica strigosa var. strigosa, commonly known as the silverleaf phylica, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its narrow, silver-gray leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the cooler months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy environments.

Due to its drought tolerance, it is a popular choice for xeriscaping and coastal gardens.
