Paphiopedilum insigne var. kimballianum

Paphiopedilum insigne var. kimballianum, commonly known as the Kimball's velvet slipper orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features a single, dark green, lance-shaped leaf and produces striking, showy flowers with a distinctive slipper-shaped pouch. The flowers are typically pale yellow to green with red or purple markings, and they bloom during the summer months. This orchid thrives in shaded, humid environments and requires well-draining soil.

It is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated by enthusiasts for its unique appearance.
