Pithecellobium solomonense

Pithecellobium solomonense, commonly known as the Solomon Islands acacia, is a tropical shrub native to the Solomon Islands.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant features small, fragrant white or yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. Its root system is known for its ability to fix nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in its native habitat.
