Psoralea hypogaea

Psoralea hypogaea, commonly known as the African burr pea, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a small, annual legume that typically grows in dry, sandy soils. The plant features small, yellow flowers and produces distinctive, hairy pods that contain several seeds. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three small, oval-shaped leaflets.

Psoralea hypogaea is often found in disturbed areas and is adapted to arid environments.
