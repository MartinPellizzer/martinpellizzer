Polygonum amphibium f. serrulatum

Polygonum amphibium f. serrulatum, commonly known as the serrated smartweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its erect, branching stems and alternate leaves with serrated edges.

It typically grows in wetland habitats, including marshes, pond edges, and along streams, thriving in moist to water-saturated soils. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in dense clusters, which are often borne on slender pedicels. Its roots are rhizomatous, allowing it to spread vegetatively and form dense stands.

The species is notable for its adaptability to fluctuating water levels and its role in stabilizing aquatic and riparian ecosystems.
