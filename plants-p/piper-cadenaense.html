Piper cadenaense

Piper cadenaense, commonly known as the Amazonian pepper vine, is a climbing plant native to the rainforests of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into elongated, fleshy berries. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped with a rough texture and emit a mild peppery scent.

Piper cadenaense is valued for its potential medicinal properties and is often used in traditional herbal remedies.
