Peperomia riparia

Peperomia riparia, commonly known as the riparian peperomia, is a species of flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is a low-growing, evergreen perennial that typically forms dense mats of foliage. The plant features thick, waxy leaves that are often arranged in a rosette pattern, which helps it retain moisture in its natural habitat. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually greenish-white in color.

Peperomia riparia is well-suited for indoor cultivation due to its compact size and ability to thrive in bright, indirect light.
