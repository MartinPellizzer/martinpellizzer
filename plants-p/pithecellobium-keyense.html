Pithecellobium keyense

Pithecellobium keyense, commonly known as the Key Island acacia, is a shrub native to the Caribbean, particularly the Bahamas.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces pods that contain edible seeds, which are often used in traditional cuisine. It thrives in coastal areas and is adapted to saline and sandy soils.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for local wildlife.
