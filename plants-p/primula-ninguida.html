Primula ninguida

Primula ninguida, commonly known as the Ningua primrose, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and has lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. Its blooming period occurs in early spring, making it a valuable addition to woodland gardens.

The species is also noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its aesthetic appeal.
