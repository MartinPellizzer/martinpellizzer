Phreatia cylindrostachya

Phreatia cylindrostachya, commonly known as the cylindrical-stemmed orchid, is a tropical epiphytic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its erect, slender pseudobulbs and long, narrow leaves. The plant produces inflorescences with numerous small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom during the rainy season. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining substrates for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
