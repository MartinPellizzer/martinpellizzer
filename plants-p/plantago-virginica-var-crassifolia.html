Plantago virginica var. crassifolia

Plantago virginica var. crassifolia, commonly known as broadleaf plantain, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It is characterized by its broad, oval-shaped leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in a dense spike, which are borne on a leafy stem. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and wetlands.

This species is known for its medicinal properties and has been used historically for its healing benefits.
