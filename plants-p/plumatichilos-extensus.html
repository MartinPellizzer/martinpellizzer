Plumatichilos extensus

Plumatichilos extensus, commonly known as the broad-leafed sun-orchid, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, showy flowers with a broad, flat lip. The plant typically grows from a rhizome and produces clusters of bright yellow or orange flowers during the warm season. Its leaves are broad and fleshy, adapted to store water in its native environment.

The species is noted for its ecological importance as a food source for various pollinators.
