Petalochilus javanicus

Petalochilus javanicus, commonly known as the Java petal orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and yellow. The plant grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its roots are adapted to absorb moisture from the air and rain, allowing it to thrive in humid, tropical environments.

The orchid's unique floral structure and ornamental appeal make it a sought-after species in horticulture.
