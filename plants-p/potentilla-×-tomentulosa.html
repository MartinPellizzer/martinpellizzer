Potentilla × tomentulosa

Potentilla × tomentulosa, commonly known as the silver cinquefoil, is a low-growing perennial shrub native to North America.

It features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has hairy, silver-gray leaves that help it retain moisture in dry conditions. It typically grows in well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade.

Its compact form and ornamental foliage make it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.
