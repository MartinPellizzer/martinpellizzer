Phlomis cyclodon

Phlomis cyclodon, commonly known as the woolly phlomis, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to arid environments. The plant features gray-green, hairy leaves that help reduce water loss, and it produces showy, pink to purple flower clusters in the summer. Its blooming period attracts pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to local biodiversity.

The plant is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
