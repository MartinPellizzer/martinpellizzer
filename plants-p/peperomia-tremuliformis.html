Peperomia tremuliformis

Peperomia tremuliformis, commonly known as the trembling peperomia, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It features glossy, dark green, ovate leaves that are often tinged with silver or white veins, giving it a striking appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters, typically green or reddish, which are followed by elongated, spiny fruits. It thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Due to its compact size and ornamental foliage, it is often used as a decorative plant in both indoor and outdoor settings.
