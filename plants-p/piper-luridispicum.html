Piper luridispicum

Piper luridispicum, commonly known as the red-veined pepper plant, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its vibrant, red-veined leaves and small, peppery fruits. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil.

Piper luridispicum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of spicy compounds used in traditional medicine.
