Papaver orientale f. flavolactescens

Papaver orientale f. flavolactescens, commonly known as the Persian poppy with yellowish-white petals, is a hybrid form of the Oriental poppy.

It features large, showy flowers that range from pale yellow to cream, often with a slight pinkish tinge. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with lance-shaped leaves that are slightly hairy. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, adding a striking visual element to gardens.

The botanical characteristics of this variety make it a popular choice for ornamental cultivation.
