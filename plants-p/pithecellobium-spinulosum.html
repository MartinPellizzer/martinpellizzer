Pithecellobium spinulosum

Pithecellobium spinulosum, commonly known as the spiny poinsettia, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are often covered in fine, spiny hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are typically surrounded by colorful bracts, which are often mistaken for petals. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The species is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
