Potentilla radicosa

Potentilla radicosa, commonly known as silver cinquefoil, is a perennial flowering plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive silver-gray foliage and showy yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its root system is fibrous and often used in traditional herbal medicine.

Potentilla radicosa is also valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and low maintenance requirements.
