Passiflora octandra var. cochinchinensis

Passiflora octandra var. cochinchinensis, commonly known as the Vietnamese passionflower, is a climbing vine native to Southeast Asia.

It features striking, showy flowers with five sepals, five petals, and a distinctive crown of filaments surrounding the reproductive structures. The plant produces blue or purple berries that are edible and often used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a rough texture, while its stems are woody and covered in fine hairs.

This species is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance in its native habitats.
