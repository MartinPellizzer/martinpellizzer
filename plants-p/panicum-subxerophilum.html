Panicum subxerophilum

Panicum subxerophilum, commonly known as the desert panicgrass, is a drought-tolerant grass species native to arid and semi-arid regions.

It possesses narrow, grass-like leaves that are adapted to minimize water loss through transpiration. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to access water from deeper soil layers.

This species is often used in restoration projects due to its resilience and ability to stabilize soil in harsh environments.
