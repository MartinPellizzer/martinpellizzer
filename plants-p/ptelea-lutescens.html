Ptelea lutescens

Ptelea lutescens, commonly known as the yellow osier, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its slender, flexible branches that are often used in basket weaving. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by small, round fruits. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly hairy surface.

Ptelea lutescens thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in riparian zones and woodlands.
