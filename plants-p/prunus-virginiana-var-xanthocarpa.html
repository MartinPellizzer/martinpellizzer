Prunus virginiana var. xanthocarpa

Prunus virginiana var. xanthocarpa, commonly known as the yellow crabapple, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and clusters of fragrant, white to pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The fruit is a small, yellow or orange apple-like berry that ripens in the fall. The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens for its vibrant seasonal color.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a versatile addition to landscapes.
