Panicum polycomum

Panicum polycomum, commonly known as purpletop millet, is a warm-season grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and dense, spike-like inflorescences that range in color from green to purple. The plant thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it suitable for use in erosion control and ornamental landscaping. Its seeds are small and can be used as a food source for birds and small mammals.

Panicum polycomum is also valued for its drought tolerance and ability to grow in poor soil conditions.
