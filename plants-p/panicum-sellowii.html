Panicum sellowii

Panicum sellowii, commonly known as Sellow's panicgrass, is a species of grass native to South America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a rough texture.

It is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its adaptability and ability to stabilize soil.
