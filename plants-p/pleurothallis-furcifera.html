Pleurothallis furcifera

Pleurothallis furcifera, commonly known as the forked pleurothallis, is a species of orchid native to the cloud forests of Central and South America.

It is a small, epiphytic plant that typically grows on trees in humid, shaded environments. The plant features slender, wiry stems and produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pale yellow. Its inflorescence is often branched, giving it the common name "forked pleurothallis.".

Due to its delicate appearance and specific habitat requirements, it is a sought-after species among orchid enthusiasts.
