Potentilla varia var. argentea

Potentilla varia var. argentea, commonly known as silver potentilla, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its silvery-white undersides on the leaves, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its compact growth habit and drought tolerance.
