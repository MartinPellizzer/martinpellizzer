Phegopteris speciosissima

Phegopteris speciosissima, commonly known as the ornate water fern, is a species of aquatic fern native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its delicate, lacy fronds that are typically divided into numerous small, pointed segments. The plant thrives in shallow, freshwater environments such as ponds and streams, where it attaches to submerged or floating vegetation. Its reproductive structures, including sporangia, are borne on the underside of the fronds, contributing to its dispersal in aquatic ecosystems.

The ornate water fern plays a role in maintaining water quality and providing habitat for aquatic organisms.
