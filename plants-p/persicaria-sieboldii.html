Persicaria sieboldii

Persicaria sieboldii, commonly known as Siebold's knotweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to East Asia.

It features upright stems that are typically hairy and can grow up to 1.5 meters in height. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually pink or red, arranged in dense inflorescences. Its leaves are lance-shaped with serrated edges and are often covered in fine hairs.

Persicaria sieboldii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and blooms.
