Panicularia nervata f. major

Panicularia nervata f. major, commonly known as the large-veined panicled bellflower, is a variant of the panicled bellflower species native to Europe.

It is characterized by its elongated, paniculate inflorescence composed of numerous small, bell-shaped flowers. The leaves are typically elongated with prominent venation, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
