Panicum aequiglume

Panicum aequiglume, commonly known as the flatglume panicgrass, is a species of grass native to Africa.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and compact, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its flowers are small and clustered, with pale to brownish hues, and it produces small, dry seeds.

Panicum aequiglume is often used in ecological studies due to its adaptability and role in soil stabilization.
