Prostanthera saxicola var. major

Prostanthera saxicola var. major, commonly known as the large-leaved narrow-leaf mintbush, is a shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, tubular flowers in shades of purple and blue. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local wildlife.
