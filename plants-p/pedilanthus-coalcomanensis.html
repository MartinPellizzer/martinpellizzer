Pedilanthus coalcomanensis

Pedilanthus coalcomanensis, commonly known as the Mexican buttercup, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its showy, yellow-orange flowers. The plant has elliptical, glossy leaves and produces fleshy, red berries that contain toxic seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in rocky or disturbed soils.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal uses, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
