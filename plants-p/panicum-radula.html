Panicum radula

Panicum radula, commonly known as the slender panicgrass, is a grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous plant that typically grows in wetland habitats, such as marshes and swamps. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. Its flowering period occurs in the summer months, and it relies on wind for pollination.

Panicum radula plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various small organisms.
