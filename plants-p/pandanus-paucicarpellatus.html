Pandanus paucicarpellatus

Pandanus paucicarpellatus, commonly known as the few-chambered pandanus, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Pandanaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, fan-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its inflorescences are usually terminal and consist of a dense cluster of small, fragrant flowers.

The fruit is a spadix surrounded by a bract, which becomes fibrous and is often used in traditional crafts and as a source of fiber.
