Phyllanthus hypoleucus

Phyllanthus hypoleucus, commonly known as the cranesbill plant, is a flowering plant belonging to the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Americas. The plant is characterized by its alternate, simple leaves that are often glossy and have a distinct petiole. Its small, greenish-yellow flowers are clustered in cup-like structures called cyathia, typical of the Euphorbiaceae family.

The plant is often grown as an ornamental due to its attractive foliage and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
