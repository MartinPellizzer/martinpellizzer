Polygonum scandens var. dentatoalatum

Polygonum scandens var. dentatoalatum, commonly known as the curly dock, is a flowering plant in the Polygonaceae family.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its prostrate or climbing growth habit, with reddish stems and heart-shaped leaves that have serrated edges. The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in dense clusters, and it is often found in moist soils along stream banks and in wetlands.

It is valued for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally for its astringent and anti-inflammatory effects.
