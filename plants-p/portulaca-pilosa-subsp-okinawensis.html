Portulaca pilosa subsp. okinawensis

Portulaca pilosa subsp.

okinawensis, commonly known as the Okinawa sandmat, is a flowering plant native to the Ryukyu Islands of Japan. It is a low-growing annual herb with fleshy, succulent leaves and vibrant yellow flowers.

The plant thrives in sandy, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas and dry habitats. Its small, bead-like seeds are dispersed by wind, allowing it to colonize open spaces efficiently.

The species is characterized by its compact growth form and adaptability to arid environments.
