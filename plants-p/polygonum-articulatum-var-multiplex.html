Polygonum articulatum var. multiplex

Polygonum articulatum var. multiplex, commonly known as the multibract euphorbia, is a herbaceous plant characterized by its succulent stems and opposite, ovate leaves.

It produces small, clustered inflorescences with yellowish-green flowers, typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. The plant is native to parts of Asia and is often found in moist, disturbed habitats such as wetlands and stream banks. It has a sprawling growth habit and is sometimes used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive foliage.

The species is also noted for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its tolerance to partial shade.
