Pandanus capusii

Pandanus capusii, commonly known as the Madagascar pandanus, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Pandanaceae family and is characterized by its tall, narrow leaves that are often used in traditional crafts. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically yellow or green in color. Its roots and leaves are sometimes used in local medicine and culinary practices.

Pandanus capusii thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in coastal or swampy areas.
