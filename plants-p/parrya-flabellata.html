Parrya flabellata

Parrya flabellata, commonly known as the flabellate parrya, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves that are often clustered at the base of the plant. The plant produces small, white to pale green flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Parrya flabellata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local insect populations.
