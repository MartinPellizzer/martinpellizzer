Primula clarkei

Primula clarkei, commonly known as the Clarke's primrose, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its clustered, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky areas at high elevations. Its leaves are broad and ovate, with a hairy texture that helps protect it from cold temperatures.

Primula clarkei is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens in cooler climates.
