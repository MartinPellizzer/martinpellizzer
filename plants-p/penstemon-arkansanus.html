Penstemon arkansanus

Penstemon arkansanus, commonly known as the Arkansas beardtongue, is a perennial flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces showy, tubular flowers that range in color from purple to pink. The plant typically thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for xeriscaping and native gardens. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its drought tolerance.

Penstemon arkansanus is also valued for its role in supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
