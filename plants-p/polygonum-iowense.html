Polygonum iowense

Polygonum iowense, commonly known as Iowense knotweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the central United States.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, which includes plants known for their distinctive bracts and flowers. The plant typically grows in moist habitats such as wetlands, stream banks, and prairies. Its leaves are lance-shaped with a reddish or purple tint, and it produces small, clustered flowers in late summer.

Polygonum iowense is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for wildlife.
