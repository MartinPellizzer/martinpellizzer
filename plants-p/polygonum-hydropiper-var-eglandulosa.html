Polygonum hydropiper var. eglandulosa

Polygonum hydropiper var. eglandulosa, commonly known as water pepper, is a perennial herb native to wetland environments.

It features slender, upright stems that are often covered in small, glandular hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or reddish in color. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and have a slightly hairy texture.

It is adapted to moist soils and is often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.
