Potentilla × dolosa

Potentilla × dolosa, commonly known as the alpine cinquefoil, is a hybrid plant species native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its low-growing habit, with dense clusters of small, yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically has hairy stems and leaves that are divided into five oval-shaped lobes, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine meadows and tundra environments.

Due to its hardy nature and ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
