Primula × coronata

Primula × coronata, commonly known as the crown primrose, is a hybrid species within the Primulaceae family.

It is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant typically grows to a height of 10–30 cm, with hairy stems and broad, oval-shaped leaves. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in woodland areas or shaded gardens.

The flowers bloom in early spring, adding a vibrant touch to the landscape during the colder months.
