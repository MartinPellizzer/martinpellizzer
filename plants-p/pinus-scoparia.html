Pinus scoparia

Pinus scoparia, commonly known as the dwarf mountain pine, is a coniferous evergreen shrub native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, multi-stemmed growth habit and short, stiff needles that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces small, cone-like structures that contain its seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Pinus scoparia thrives in harsh, alpine environments, often growing at high elevations where few other plants can survive.

Its resilience to cold and poor soil conditions makes it an important component of mountain ecosystems.
