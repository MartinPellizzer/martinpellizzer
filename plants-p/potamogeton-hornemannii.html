Potamogeton hornemannii

Potamogeton hornemannii, commonly known as the hornemann's pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in Asia.

It has long, narrow leaves that are typically arranged in a spiral pattern along its stem. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually found in clusters near the water's surface. It plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.

Potamogeton hornemannii is often used in water quality assessments due to its sensitivity to environmental changes.
