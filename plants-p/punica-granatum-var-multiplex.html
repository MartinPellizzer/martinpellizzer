Punica granatum var. multiplex

Punica granatum var. multiplex, commonly known as the Armenian pomegranate, is a cultivar of the pomegranate tree known for its compact growth habit and ornamental value.

It produces vibrant red flowers in spring, followed by showy, orange-red fruits that are smaller than the typical pomegranate. The plant is native to the Caucasus region and thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil. It is often cultivated for its ornamental foliage and fruit, which can be used in cooking or as a decorative element.

This variety is also valued for its drought tolerance and ability to thrive in container gardens.
