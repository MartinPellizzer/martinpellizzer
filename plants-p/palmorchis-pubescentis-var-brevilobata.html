Palmorchis pubescentis var. brevilobata

Palmorchis pubescentis var. brevilobata, commonly known as the pubescent palm orchid, is a rare and delicate orchid species native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, palm-like leaves and small, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in the spring. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained, nutrient-rich soil. Its unique morphology and limited distribution make it a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists.

Due to habitat destruction, it is considered endangered and is often found in protected areas.
