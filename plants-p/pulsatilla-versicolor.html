Pulsatilla versicolor

Pulsatilla versicolor, commonly known as the peacock flower, is a member of the Ranunculaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia. The plant is characterized by its showy, compound flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. Its leaves are palmately divided, and it typically grows in rocky or alpine environments.

The plant produces a fleshy, spiny fruit that contains several seeds.
