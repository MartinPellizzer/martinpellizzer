Panicum pyrularium

Panicum pyrularium, commonly known as purple lovegrass, is a perennial grass native to Africa and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and produces fluffy, purple-tinted seed heads that give it its common name. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping for its drought tolerance and ability to stabilize soil.

However, it can become invasive in certain regions if not managed properly.
