Paepalanthus roraimae

Paepalanthus roraimae, commonly known as the Roraima cactus, is a unique plant native to the tepuis of Venezuela and Brazil.

It is a type of cacti belonging to the family Cactaceae, characterized by its spiny stems and ability to store water. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils at high altitudes. It produces small, yellow flowers that bloom during the dry season.

Due to its restricted habitat, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
