Poa annua var. villosa

Poa annua var. villosa, commonly known as hairy annual bluegrass, is a cool-season grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its fine, bluish-green foliage and tufted growth habit, with hairy nodes and leaves that give it its common name. The plant typically grows in lawns, gardens, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. It is an annual species, completing its life cycle within a single growing season.

Due to its aggressive growth and adaptability, it is often considered a weed in turfgrass settings.
