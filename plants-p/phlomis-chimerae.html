Phlomis chimerae

Phlomis chimerae, commonly known as the hybrid phlomis, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of central Asia.

It belongs to the family Lamiaceae, which includes many aromatic and medicinal plants. The plant is characterized by its upright growth habit, narrow lance-shaped leaves, and clusters of purple or pink tubular flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is occasionally cultivated in horticulture.
