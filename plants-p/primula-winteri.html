Primula winteri

Primula winteri, commonly known as the winter primrose, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of China.

It is characterized by its dense, low-growing habit and produces clusters of funnel-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in forested areas or along stream banks. Its leaves are typically broad and oval, with a slightly hairy texture.

Primula winteri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its early spring blooms.
