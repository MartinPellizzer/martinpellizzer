Pyrethrum mandianum

Pyrethrum mandianum, commonly known as the Tibetan pyrethrum, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its daisy-like yellow flowers and finely divided, fern-like leaves. The plant is valued for its natural pyrethrin compounds, which are used in organic pest control. It thrives in cool, temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Due to its ecological significance and medicinal potential, it is also of interest in botanical research.
