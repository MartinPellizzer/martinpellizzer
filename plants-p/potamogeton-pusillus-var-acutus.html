Potamogeton pusillus var. acutus

Potamogeton pusillus var. acutus, commonly known as the sharpleaf pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments across Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in shallow, slow-moving waters and has slender, branching stems that are often covered in small, narrow leaves. The plant produces small, green flowers that are clustered in spike-like inflorescences, which emerge above the water surface. Its leaves are usually sharp and pointed, giving it its common name, and it plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.

This species is often used in pond restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize sediments and improve water clarity.
