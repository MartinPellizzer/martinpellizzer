Platanthera brachycorythis

Platanthera brachycorythis, commonly known as the short-corned lady's tresses, is a perennial orchid native to North America.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green leaves and its distinctive, fragrant, white to pale pink flower spike that resembles a tresses of hair. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and wet meadows. It produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are pollinated by various insects, including bees and flies.

The species is relatively rare and is often found in association with specific mycorrhizal fungi that aid in nutrient uptake.
