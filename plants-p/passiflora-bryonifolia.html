Passiflora bryonifolia

Passiflora bryonifolia, commonly known as the Brazilian passionflower, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, complex flowers that feature a blend of blue, purple, and white petals, along with intricate filaments and a crown-like structure. The plant produces edible, tropical berries that are often used in beverages and desserts. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a rough texture, while its stems are woody and can grow up to several meters in length.

Passiflora bryonifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
