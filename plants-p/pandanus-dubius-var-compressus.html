Pandanus dubius var. compressus

Pandanus dubius var. compressus, commonly known as the narrow-leaf pandanus, is a tropical plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its narrow, sword-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, which are often used in traditional crafts. Its fibrous leaves are utilized for weaving baskets and mats, making it an important resource in local cultures.

The plant thrives in coastal and wetland environments, preferring well-drained, sandy soils.
