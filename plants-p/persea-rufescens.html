Persea rufescens

Persea rufescens, commonly known as the red-leaf persimmon, is a species of flowering plant in the Lauraceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of Central and South America, where it thrives in humid, warm climates. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are often reddish in color when young. It produces small, fragrant flowers followed by edible fruits that are similar in appearance to other persimmons.

The tree is valued for its ornamental qualities and its fruit, which is consumed by both humans and wildlife.
