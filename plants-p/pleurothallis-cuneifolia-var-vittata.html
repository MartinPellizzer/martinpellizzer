Pleurothallis cuneifolia var. vittata

Pleurothallis cuneifolia var. vittata, commonly known as the streaked pleurothallis, is a species of orchid native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, elongated leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture. The flowers are typically pale green or yellow with intricate patterns, and they bloom in clusters during the rainy season. This variety is noted for its compact growth habit and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in terrariums and as a houseplant.

It requires bright, indirect light and moderate humidity to thrive.
