Panicum unispicatum

Panicum unispicatum, commonly known as the narrowleaf panicgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, often found in open woodlands and disturbed areas.

Its ecological value includes providing habitat and forage for various wildlife species.
