Pentacalia diplostephioides

Pentacalia diplostephioides, commonly known as the Florida bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has slender, hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves that are often tinged with red or purple. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive and ecologically important species in its native habitat.
