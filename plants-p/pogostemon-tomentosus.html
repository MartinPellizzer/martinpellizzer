Pogostemon tomentosus

Pogostemon tomentosus, commonly known as the woolly pogostemon, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It features a square, hairy stem that is often covered in fine, white pubescence. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, arranged in whorls along the stem. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in a dense layer of soft, woolly hairs.

Pogostemon tomentosus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
