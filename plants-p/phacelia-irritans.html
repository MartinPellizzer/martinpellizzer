Phacelia irritans

Phacelia irritans, commonly known as the irritant phacelia, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its delicate, blue to purple flowers and hairy, glandular leaves that can cause skin irritation. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is part of the Boraginaceae family. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds.

Due to its irritant properties, it is often avoided by livestock and humans alike.
