Pilosocereus flavipulvinatus

Pilosocereus flavipulvinatus, commonly known as the yellow pulvinate cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that are covered in dense, white to pale yellow spines. The plant produces large, showy yellow flowers during the warmer months, which attract pollinators such as bats and insects. It has a deep root system that allows it to survive in harsh, dry environments.

The cactus is also notable for its ability to store water in its thick, fleshy stems, making it well adapted to desert conditions.
