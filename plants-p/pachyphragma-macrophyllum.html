Pachyphragma macrophyllum

Pachyphragma macrophyllum, commonly known as the giant desert cactus, is a striking plant native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its thick, ribbed stems that can grow up to several meters in height, storing water to survive in harsh, dry environments. The plant produces large, showy yellow flowers during the rainy season, attracting pollinators such as bees and bats. Its leaves are modified into spines, reducing water loss and providing protection from herbivores.

Pachyphragma macrophyllum is well adapted to extreme desert conditions, showcasing remarkable resilience and botanical uniqueness.
