Potamogeton augustani

Potamogeton augustani, commonly known as August's pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in Europe.

It has long, slender, ribbon-like leaves that are typically arranged in whorls around the stem. The plant produces small, green flowers that are clustered in spike-like inflorescences, which emerge from the water surface. It plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.

Potamogeton augustani is often found in still or slow-moving waters such as lakes and ponds.
