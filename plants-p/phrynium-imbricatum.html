Phrynium imbricatum

Phrynium imbricatum, commonly known as the African shield fern, is a striking tropical plant native to parts of Africa and the Caribbean.

It features broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy cuticle, giving them a distinctive, almost metallic sheen. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers that are borne on spadices, typical of the Araceae family. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense colonies in moist, shaded environments.

The leaves are also known for their ability to retain moisture, making the plant well-suited for humid climates.
