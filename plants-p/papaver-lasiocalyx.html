Papaver lasiocalyx

Papaver lasiocalyx, commonly known as the rough-hairy poppy, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive papery, often reddish or purple-colored petals and a rough, hairy texture on its stems and leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, adding a striking visual element to its natural habitat.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
