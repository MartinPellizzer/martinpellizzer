Pectis venezuelensis

Pectis venezuelensis, commonly known as the Venezuelan pectis, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Venezuela.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its leaves are usually narrow and lance-shaped, with a hairy texture, while its flowers are small and yellow, arranged in a compact cluster.

Pectis venezuelensis is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
