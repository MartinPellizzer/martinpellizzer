Peperomia glareosa

Peperomia glareosa, commonly known as the shining peperomia, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to Brazil.

It features thick, waxy leaves that are typically bright green with a glossy texture, often adorned with white or silver markings. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters, usually in shades of green or purple, which are followed by elongated, spiky fruits. It thrives in bright, indirect light and prefers well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Peperomia glareosa is relatively low maintenance and resistant to pests, contributing to its appeal among plant enthusiasts.
