Paspalum fastigiatum

Paspalum fastigiatum, commonly known as the silvergrass or velvetgrass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and narrow, silver-green leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are often adorned with white or pale blue flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

Paspalum fastigiatum is valued for its ornamental qualities and its ability to stabilize soil in riparian areas.
