Penstemon humilis subsp. typicus

Penstemon humilis subsp.

typicus, commonly known as the low larkspur, is a perennial herb native to the western United States. It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils in open habitats such as meadows and slopes.

The plant features slender, upright stems with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. Its showy, tubular flowers are typically blue to purple in color, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

It is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained conditions.
