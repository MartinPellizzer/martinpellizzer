Pitcairnia fimbriatobracteata

Pitcairnia fimbriatobracteata, commonly known as the fringed bract pitcairnia, is a species of flowering plant in the Bromeliaceae family.

It is native to the cloud forests of Ecuador and is characterized by its striking, rosette-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of trichomes. The plant produces showy inflorescences with brightly colored flowers, typically in shades of red, orange, or yellow. Its leaves are thick and succulent, adapted to store water in its native humid environment.

The species is appreciated for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
