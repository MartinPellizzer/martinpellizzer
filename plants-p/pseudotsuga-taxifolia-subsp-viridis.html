Pseudotsuga taxifolia subsp. viridis

Pseudotsuga taxifolia subsp.

viridis, commonly known as the Japanese white fir, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to eastern Asia. It is characterized by its slender, conical shape and needle-like leaves that are typically light green and slightly twisted.

The plant produces small, cylindrical cones that mature to a reddish-brown color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in mountainous regions with well-drained soils.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for horticultural use in gardens and landscapes.
