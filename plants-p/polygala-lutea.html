Polygala lutea

Polygala lutea, commonly known as the yellow milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has pinnately compound leaves and produces pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in dry, sunny environments and is often found in grasslands and rocky soils.

Polygala lutea is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
