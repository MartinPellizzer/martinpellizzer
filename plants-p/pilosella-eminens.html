Pilosella eminens

Pilosella eminens, commonly known as the alpine hawk's-beard, is a flowering plant native to mountainous regions of Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and herbaceous growth habit. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils and full sunlight. Its leaves are usually hairy and lance-shaped, while its yellow flowers are arranged in a single head with surrounding bracts.

Pilosella eminens is often found in alpine meadows and is adapted to cold, harsh climates.
