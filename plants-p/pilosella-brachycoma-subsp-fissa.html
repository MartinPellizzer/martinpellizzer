Pilosella brachycoma subsp. fissa

Pilosella brachycoma subsp.

fissa, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia. It typically grows in alpine and subalpine environments, thriving in well-drained, rocky soils.

The plant features a basal rosette of narrow, linear leaves and produces small, yellowish-green composite flowers arranged in a cluster. Its inflorescence is characterized by a short, compact head of florets, which are often surrounded by a fringe of pale yellow to white bracts. Pilosella brachycoma subsp.

fissa is known for its hardy nature and ability to withstand cold temperatures, making it a common sight in high-altitude meadows.
