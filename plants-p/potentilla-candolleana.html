Potentilla candolleana

Potentilla candolleana, commonly known as the Candolle's cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in rocky and alpine environments. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. It is characterized by its hardy nature and ability to thrive in cold, arid conditions.

Potentilla candolleana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
