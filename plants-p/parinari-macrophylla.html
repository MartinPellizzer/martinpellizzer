Parinari macrophylla

Parinari macrophylla, commonly known as the large-leaf parinari, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, glossy, elliptical leaves that can grow up to 20 centimeters in length. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by edible, fleshy berries. Its fruit is an important food source for various wildlife species.

The tree is also valued for its hard, durable wood, which is used in construction and furniture making.
