Patagonium incanum

Patagonium incanum, commonly known as the white thistle, is a perennial flowering plant native to the southern regions of South America.

It is characterized by its grayish-green, hairy leaves and spiny, purple flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in steppe and shrubland habitats. Its scientific name reflects its pale appearance, with "incanum" referring to the whitish down covering its stems and leaves.

Patagonium incanum plays a role in local ecosystems by providing food and shelter for various insects and small animals.
