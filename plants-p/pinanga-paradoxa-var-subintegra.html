Pinanga paradoxa var. subintegra

Pinanga paradoxa var. subintegra, commonly known as the giant bamboo palm, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, pinnately compound leaves that can reach several feet in length. The plant belongs to the Arecaceae family and is valued for its ornamental appeal in both indoor and outdoor settings. Its growth habit is clump-forming, with a slow growth rate that makes it suitable for container cultivation.

The leaves are typically a deep green, providing a lush, tropical appearance throughout the year.
