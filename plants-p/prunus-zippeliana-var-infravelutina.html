Prunus zippeliana var. infravelutina

Prunus zippeliana var. infravelutina, commonly known as the Chinese sand cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It is characterized by its slender, multi-stemmed growth habit and its small, ovate leaves that are typically pubescent beneath. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers in early spring, followed by edible, yellow or orange-red fruits. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has a preference for well-drained soils and full sun.

This variety is noted for its hardiness and ability to thrive in a range of environmental conditions.
