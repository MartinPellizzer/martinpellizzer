Pleurothallis furcatipetala

Pleurothallis furcatipetala, commonly known as the forked-petaled pleurothallis, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a small, epiphytic orchid that typically grows on trees in humid, shaded environments. The plant features intricate, lance-shaped leaves and produces delicate, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pale pink. Its flowers are characterized by their unique, bifurcated petals, which give the species its common name.

Due to its ornamental value and rarity, it is often sought after by orchid enthusiasts and conservationists.
