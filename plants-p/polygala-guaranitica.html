Polygala guaranitica

Polygala guaranitica, commonly known as the Guarani's milkweed, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Polygalaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and riverbanks. Its flowers are usually pale yellow or greenish-white, with a distinctive, spurred shape that attracts pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Polygala guaranitica is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
