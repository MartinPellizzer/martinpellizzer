Psidium piribebuiense

Psidium piribebuiense, commonly known as the Piribebu fruit tree, is a species native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree that typically grows up to 10 meters in height. The plant produces small, yellowish-green berries that are edible and have a sweet, slightly acidic flavor. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a smooth texture with a dark green upper surface.

The tree thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland and swampy areas.
