Paspalum orbiculatum var. potaroense

Paspalum orbiculatum var. potaroense, commonly known as the Potaro paspalum, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetland or riparian habitats.

Due to its adaptability, it is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
