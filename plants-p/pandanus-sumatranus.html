Pandanus sumatranus

Pandanus sumatranus, commonly known as the Natal pandanus, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, narrow leaves that are often used in traditional crafts and thatching. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically grouped in clusters at the ends of its stems. Its roots are sometimes used in herbal remedies, and its leaves are valued for their durability and aesthetic appeal.

Overall, Pandanus sumatranus plays an important ecological and cultural role in its native habitats.
