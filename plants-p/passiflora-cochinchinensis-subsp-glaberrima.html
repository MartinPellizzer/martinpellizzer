Passiflora cochinchinensis subsp. glaberrima

Passiflora cochinchinensis subsp.

glaberrima, commonly known as the glabrous passionflower, is a tropical vine native to Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers with five sepals, five petals, and a distinctive crown of filaments.

The plant produces edible, orange-red berries that are rich in nutrients. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped and covered with a fine layer of hair, which is reduced in this subspecies.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in herbal medicine.
