Pseudopilocereus robustus

Pseudopilocereus robustus, commonly known as the robust night bloomer, is a cacti species native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems covered in white spines and its ability to produce large, fragrant white flowers at night. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade. It is drought-tolerant and prefers warm, dry climates.

The flowers typically open in the evening and close by morning, attracting nocturnal pollinators.
