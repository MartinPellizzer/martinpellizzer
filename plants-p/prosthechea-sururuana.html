Prosthechea sururuana

Prosthechea sururuana, commonly known as the Sururuana orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It features a sympodial growth habit with a short, horizontal rhizome and produces pseudobulbs that are cylindrical and covered in a dense, fibrous sheath. The plant has linear, grass-like leaves that are arranged in two rows along the stem. Its flowers are small, fragrant, and typically white or pale yellow, arranged in a raceme.

Prosthechea sururuana is adapted to grow in shaded, humid environments and is often found in rocky or forested areas.
