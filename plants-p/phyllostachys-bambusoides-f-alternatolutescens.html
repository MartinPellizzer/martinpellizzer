Phyllostachys bambusoides f. alternatolutescens

Phyllostachys bambusoides f. alternatolutescens, commonly known as the golden bamboo or yellow stripe bamboo, is a varietal form of the common bamboo species.

It is characterized by its striking yellow-green stripes on the culms, which contrast with the green base, creating a unique visual effect. This bamboo is native to eastern Asia and thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil. It grows as a clump-forming plant, reaching heights of up to 6 meters, and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.

Its foliage is typically narrow and elongated, contributing to its aesthetic appeal in gardens and landscapes.
