Paraphlomis intermedia

Paraphlomis intermedia, commonly known as the broadleaf cowpea, is a perennial herb native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the family Lamiaceae, which includes many aromatic plants. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with a rough texture and a strong, spicy scent. Its flowers are small, tubular, and typically pink or purple, arranged in whorled clusters.

The plant is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
