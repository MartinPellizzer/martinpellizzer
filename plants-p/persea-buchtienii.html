Persea buchtienii

Persea buchtienii, commonly known as the Buchtien's persimmon, is a species of flowering plant in the family Lauraceae.

It is native to Mexico and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and small, fragrant flowers. The plant produces edible berries that are typically orange to reddish in color when mature. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and private collections.
