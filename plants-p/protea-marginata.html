Protea marginata

Protea marginata, commonly known as the silver protea, is a striking South African plant belonging to the Proteaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescence composed of numerous white to pale pink flower heads surrounded by a dense, silver-tinged bract. The plant typically grows in coastal and mountainous regions, thriving in well-drained, acidic soils. Its flowers are adapted for pollination by birds, particularly sunbirds, due to their nectar-rich structure.

Protea marginata is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique and elegant appearance.
