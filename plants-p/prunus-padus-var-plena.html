Prunus padus var. plena

Prunus padus var. plena, commonly known as the double white cherry, is a cultivar of the common bird cherry.

It is characterized by its dense, double white flowers that bloom in early spring before the leaves appear. The plant is a deciduous shrub or small tree with slender, branching stems and smooth, grey bark. Its flowers are highly fragrant and attract pollinators such as bees and birds.

It is often used in ornamental landscaping for its showy, early-season floral display.
