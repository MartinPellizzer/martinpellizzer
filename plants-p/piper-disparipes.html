Piper disparipes

Piper disparipes, commonly known as the slender pepper vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many species of pepper plants. The plant features heart-shaped leaves and produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that develop into elongated, woody berries. Its botanical structure is characterized by its twining habit and the presence of glandular hairs on its stems and leaves.

Piper disparipes is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
