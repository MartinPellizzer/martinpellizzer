Pittocaulon velatum var. velatum

Pittocaulon velatum var. velatum, commonly known as the narrowleaf pittocaulon, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southwestern United States.

It features slender, upright stems that are often hairy and bear opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly serrated margin. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive member of the Apiaceae family.
