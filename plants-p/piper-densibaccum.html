Piper densibaccum

Piper densibaccum, commonly known as the dense-leaved pepper or black pepper vine, is a climbing shrub native to tropical regions of Asia and Africa.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its dark green, glossy leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces small, round berries that mature from green to dark purple, containing the aromatic seeds used in spice blends. Its botanical features include a woody stem, opposite leaf arrangement, and a distinctive, pungent aroma.

It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its medicinal and culinary uses.
