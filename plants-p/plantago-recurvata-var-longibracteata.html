Plantago recurvata var. longibracteata

Plantago recurvata var. longibracteata, commonly known as the long bract plantain, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to regions of Asia, including China and Japan.

It features a basal rosette of lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, white hairs. The plant produces tall, slender flowering stems that bear clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. Its leaves are characterized by elongated bracts that are often mistaken for leaves, contributing to its unique appearance.

This species is valued for its medicinal properties and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
