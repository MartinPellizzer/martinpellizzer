Phaseolus pluriflorus

Phaseolus pluriflorus, commonly known as the scarlet runner bean, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its climbing habit and large, showy flowers that are typically red or pink in color. The plant produces long, cylindrical pods that contain edible beans, which are rich in nutrients. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for both ornamental and culinary purposes.
