Primula macrophylla var. lineariloba

Primula macrophylla var. lineariloba, commonly known as the narrow-leaf primrose, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of China.

It is characterized by its elongated, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in shaded woodland areas. Its foliage is evergreen, providing year-round interest, while its flowers bloom in early spring.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
