Pelargonium apetalum

Pelargonium apetalum, commonly known as the naked flower geranium, is a species native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its lack of petals, giving it its common name, and features small, greenish-white flowers. The plant has a compact growth habit with hairy leaves that are often lobed or serrated. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements, it is valued in horticulture for ornamental purposes.
