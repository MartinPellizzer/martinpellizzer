Pectis rubiacea

Pectis rubiacea, commonly known as the red thornweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and has hairy, sometimes prickly stems. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a rough texture, while its flowers are small and yellow, arranged in clusters.

Pectis rubiacea is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
