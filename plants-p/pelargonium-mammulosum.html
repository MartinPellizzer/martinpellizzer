Pelargonium mammulosum

Pelargonium mammulosum, commonly known as the woolly geranium, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that gives it a distinctive, fuzzy appearance. The plant produces showy, five-petaled flowers in shades of pink, red, and white, typically blooming in spring and summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Due to its ornamental value and easy care, it is widely cultivated in gardens around the world.
