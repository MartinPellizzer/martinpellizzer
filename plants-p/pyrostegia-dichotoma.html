Pyrostegia dichotoma

Pyrostegia dichotoma, commonly known as the flame tree or the Brazilian flame tree, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that can grow up to 6 meters tall. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy on the underside. Its most striking feature is its clusters of bright red or orange flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring.

The flowers are typically borne on long, slender pedicels and are followed by woody, capsule-like fruits.
