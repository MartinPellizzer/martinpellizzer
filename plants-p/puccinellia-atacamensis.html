Puccinellia atacamensis

Puccinellia atacamensis, commonly known as Atacama grass, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of South America, particularly the Atacama Desert.

It is a perennial grass species that thrives in extremely dry and saline environments, showcasing remarkable adaptations to harsh conditions. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain tiny flowers. It plays a role in stabilizing soil and preventing erosion in its native habitat.

Due to its resilience, it is sometimes cultivated for use in restoration projects in arid landscapes.
