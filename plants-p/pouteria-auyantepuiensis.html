Pouteria auyantepuiensis

Pouteria auyantepuiensis, commonly known as the "Yanapai" or "Pachira," is a species native to the Amazon rainforest, particularly in regions of Venezuela and Brazil.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 20 meters tall, with a broad, buttressed trunk and large, palmate leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by edible, round fruits that are rich in nutrients.

Its botanical significance lies in its role as a food source for local wildlife and its potential for use in traditional medicine.
