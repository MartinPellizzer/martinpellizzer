Patrinia palmata var. gibbosa

Patrinia palmata var. gibbosa, commonly known as the hairy patrinia, is a flowering plant native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and opposite, ovate leaves with hairy surfaces. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically borne in the axils of the leaves. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
