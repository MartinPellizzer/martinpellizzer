Piper atrobaccum

Piper atrobaccum, commonly known as black acuminate pepper, is a climbing shrub native to the rainforests of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many species of pepper plants. The plant produces small, oval-shaped berries that turn from green to black as they mature. These berries contain aromatic compounds responsible for the plant's distinctive flavor.

It is often cultivated for its use in traditional medicine and as a spice.
