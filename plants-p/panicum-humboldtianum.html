Panicum humboldtianum

Panicum humboldtianum, commonly known as the Humboldt panicgrass, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, wiry stems. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences that are often tinged with red or purple hues. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green coloration.

Panicum humboldtianum is adapted to grow in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in coastal or arid environments.
