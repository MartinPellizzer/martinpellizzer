Paspalum platyphyllum

Paspalum platyphyllum, commonly known as flatleaf grass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its flat, wide leaves and dense, tufted growth habit. The plant typically grows in wet or marshy areas, making it an important component of wetland ecosystems. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers.

Paspalum platyphyllum is often used in restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and prevent erosion.
