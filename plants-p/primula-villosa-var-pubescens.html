Primula villosa var. pubescens

Primula villosa var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy primrose, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it its scientific name. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, often blooming in early spring. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
