Poa gayana

Poa gayana, commonly known as the New Zealand hair grass, is a perennial grass species native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and fine, hair-like leaves that give it its common name. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and stream banks. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers.

Poa gayana plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
