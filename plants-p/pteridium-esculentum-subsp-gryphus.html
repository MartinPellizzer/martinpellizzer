Pteridium esculentum subsp. gryphus

Pteridium esculentum subsp.

gryphus, commonly known as the giant edible fern, is a member of the family Polypodiaceae. It is characterized by its large, lance-shaped fronds that can reach lengths of up to 2 meters.

The plant is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia and is often found in moist, shaded environments. Its rhizomes are edible and have been used in traditional cuisines.

The fronds are typically divided into segments with a distinct midrib and are often used as a vegetable in various culinary applications.
