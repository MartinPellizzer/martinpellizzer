Phaseolus vulgaris var. sarcusa

Phaseolus vulgaris var. sarcusa, commonly known as the scarlet runner bean, is a variant of the common bean species native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its vibrant red or orange flowers and elongated, edible pods that contain seeds used for food. The plant is a climbing vine with compound leaves and can grow up to several meters in height. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is valued for both its ornamental appeal and culinary uses.
