Papaver segetale

Papaver segetale, commonly known as field poppy, is an annual herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features distinctive bright red or orange flowers with a prominent central stigma, typically blooming from late spring to early autumn. The plant has smooth, lance-shaped leaves that are usually arranged alternately along the stem. Its flowers are pollinated primarily by insects, and it produces capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds.

Papaver segetale is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in agricultural fields.
