Parietaria debilis subvar. lanuginosa

Parietaria debilis subvar. lanuginosa, commonly known as the downy wall-rocket, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and waste areas. The plant features small, heart-shaped leaves covered in fine, white hairs, giving it a fluffy appearance. Its flowers are inconspicuous, consisting of tiny greenish-yellow blooms that appear in clusters.

It is known for its tolerance to poor soil conditions and its ability to spread through rhizomes.
