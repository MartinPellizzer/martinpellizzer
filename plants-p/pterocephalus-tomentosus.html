Pterocephalus tomentosus

Pterocephalus tomentosus, commonly known as the woolly antelope plant, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, gray-green foliage and produces distinctive yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. Its unique appearance makes it a popular choice for xeriscaping and rock gardens.

The woolly antelope plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
