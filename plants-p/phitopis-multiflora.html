Phitopis multiflora

Phitopis multiflora, commonly known as the Chinese sweetgum, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and oval-shaped leaves that turn vibrant shades of red and yellow in the fall. The plant produces small, spherical fruit clusters that contain spiny seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a common sight in temperate forests.

Its ornamental value has led to its cultivation in gardens and parks around the world.
