Plectranthus laetus

Plectranthus laetus, commonly known as the silverleaf plectranthus, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to South Africa.

It features opposite, hairy, ovate leaves that are often covered in a fine silvery down, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pale purple to blue in color, arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems. It is a member of the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs such as mint and basil.

Plectranthus laetus is often used in ornamental gardening for its attractive foliage and ease of cultivation.
