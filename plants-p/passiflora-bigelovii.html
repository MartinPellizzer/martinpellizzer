Passiflora bigelovii

Passiflora bigelovii, commonly known as the Bigelow passionflower, is a climbing vine native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its striking, complex flowers that feature a blend of blue, purple, and white hues, along with intricate filaments and a crown-like structure. The plant produces edible berries that are typically yellow or orange when mature, containing numerous seeds. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, often found in rocky or arid environments.

Passiflora bigelovii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
