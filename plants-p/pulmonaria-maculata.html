Pulmonaria maculata

Pulmonaria maculata, commonly known as the spotted lungwort, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It features basal rosettes of dark green, velvety leaves with distinctive white and purple spots. The plant produces tall spikes of bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink, lavender, and white, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shady woodland habitats and is often found in calcareous soils.

Pulmonaria maculata is valued in gardens for its ornamental foliage and early spring bloom.
