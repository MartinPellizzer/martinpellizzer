Passiflora lauana

Passiflora lauana, commonly known as the Brazilian passionflower, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, complex flowers that feature five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments resembling a crown of thorns. The plant produces edible, orange-red berries that are rich in vitamins and antioxidants. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped with a rough, hairy texture.

Passiflora lauana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported calming effects.
