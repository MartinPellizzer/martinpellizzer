Prunus spinosa f. semiglabra

Prunus spinosa f. semiglabra, commonly known as the smooth thorned cherry or European blackthorn, is a shrub native to Europe and parts of western Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its dense, thorny growth habit. The leaves are elliptical, with a glossy green upper surface and a lighter, sometimes hairy underside. Its small, dark purple-black berries are an important food source for birds and other wildlife.

The plant is often used in hedgerows and for wildlife habitat due to its hardy nature and ecological value.
