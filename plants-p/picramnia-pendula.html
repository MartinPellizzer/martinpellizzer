Picramnia pendula

Picramnia pendula, commonly known as the hanging picramnia, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features slender, arching stems that often hang from branches, giving it a unique, cascading appearance. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers in late spring, which are clustered in racemes. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a serrated margin.

Picramnia pendula is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in partial shade.
