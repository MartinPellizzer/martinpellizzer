Podocarpus barretoi

Podocarpus barretoi, commonly known as the African yellowwood, is a evergreen tree native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Podocarpaceae family and is characterized by its dense, aromatic foliage and distinctive yellowish-green leaves. The plant produces small, cone-like structures that contain its seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It is often found in coastal forests and is valued for its hard, durable wood.

Podocarpus barretoi plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, supporting a variety of wildlife.
