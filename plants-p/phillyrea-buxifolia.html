Phillyrea buxifolia

Phillyrea buxifolia, commonly known as the boxleaf Phillyrea, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features dense, glossy, dark green leaves that are oval in shape and often have a leathery texture. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers in clusters during the spring, followed by red, fleshy fruits that are edible for birds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Phillyrea buxifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to withstand harsh environmental conditions.
