Pilosella gorkense

Pilosella gorkense, commonly known as the Gorkha pearlyweed, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in cool and arid conditions. It produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that are surrounded by a dense layer of fine, white hairs.

Pilosella gorkense is notable for its delicate appearance and its ability to adapt to high-altitude environments.
