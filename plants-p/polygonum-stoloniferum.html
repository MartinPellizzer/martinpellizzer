Polygonum stoloniferum

Polygonum stoloniferum, commonly known as the red-rooted filbert, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, which includes buckwheats and sorrels. The plant is characterized by its upright, branched stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often tinged with red or purple. It produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-white in color.

Polygonum stoloniferum is known for its rhizomatous growth habit, allowing it to spread vegetatively and form dense colonies.
