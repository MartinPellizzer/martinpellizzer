Pseudoroegneria strigosa subsp. kanashiroi

Pseudoroegneria strigosa subsp.

kanashiroi, commonly known as the Kanashiro's feathergrass, is a grass species native to the mountainous regions of East Asia. It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and fine, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of short hairs.

This subspecies typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its inflorescence consists of a narrow spikelet cluster, which produces small, wind-pollinated flowers.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
