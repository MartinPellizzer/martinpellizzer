Phagnalon calycinum subsp. calycinum

Phagnalon calycinum subsp.

calycinum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved phagnalon, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to regions of Europe and western Asia. It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

The plant features slender, hairy stems and narrow, linear leaves that are often recurved at the edges. Its flowers are small and clustered in dense heads, typically appearing in shades of pink or purple.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
