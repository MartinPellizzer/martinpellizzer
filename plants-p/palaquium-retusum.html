Palaquium retusum

Palaquium retusum, commonly known as the round-leaved wax apple, is a small evergreen tree native to tropical regions of Asia and the Pacific.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and simple, elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by round, fleshy fruits resembling small apples. The fruit's pulp is sweet and acidic, often consumed fresh or used in traditional remedies.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential medicinal properties.
