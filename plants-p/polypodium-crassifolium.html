Polypodium crassifolium

Polypodium crassifolium, commonly known as the broadleaf huperzia, is a species of fern belonging to the family Huperziaceae.

It is characterized by its large, leathery, ovate leaves that are typically dark green and have a distinctive, rugged texture. The plant is native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas, often growing in moist, shaded environments. Its rhizome is thick and woody, which helps it to survive in various soil conditions.

Polypodium crassifolium is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to cultivate in shaded gardens.
