Pseudanamomis jambosoides

Pseudanamomis jambosoides, commonly known as the giant flame tree, is a tropical plant characterized by its large, showy red flowers that bloom in clusters.

It is native to Southeast Asia and thrives in warm, humid climates with well-drained soil. The plant has a sturdy, upright growth habit and can reach heights of up to 20 meters. Its leaves are compound, with long, slender leaflets that give it a feathery appearance.

The species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in landscaping due to its striking floral display.
