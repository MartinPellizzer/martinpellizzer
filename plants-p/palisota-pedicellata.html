Palisota pedicellata

Palisota pedicellata, commonly known as the Chinese nettle or broadleaf nettle, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its erect, hairy stems and opposite, heart-shaped leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are borne on short pedicels. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Palisota pedicellata is known for its stinging hairs, which can cause skin irritation upon contact.
