Phrynium vittatum

Phrynium vittatum, commonly known as the striped arrowroot, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows up to 1.5 meters tall, featuring broad, arrow-shaped leaves with a distinctive green and white striped pattern. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers arranged in spikes, followed by tuberous roots that are edible. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of starchy roots.

Phrynium vittatum is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
