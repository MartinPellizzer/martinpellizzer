Pennisetum somalense

Pennisetum somalense, commonly known as Somali finger grass, is a perennial grass native to the Horn of Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, pale-green flowers that are clustered in panicles, which are typically borne at the top of the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Pennisetum somalense is often used in traditional landscaping and has ornamental value due to its distinctive appearance.
