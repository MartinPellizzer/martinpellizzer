Pentaloncha humilis

Pentaloncha humilis, commonly known as the dwarf pentaloncha, is a small, herbaceous plant native to South America.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors or along stream banks. The plant features delicate, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small and clustered, usually in greenish-white or pale yellow hues.

Pentaloncha humilis is known for its ability to thrive in low-light conditions and is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value.
