Phreatia keysseri

Phreatia keysseri, commonly known as the Keyser's spider orchid, is a terrestrial orchid species native to the rainforests of New Guinea.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, spider-like appearance, with long, slender petals and a striking, elongated lip. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often near streams or in moist undergrowth. Its flowers are usually pale green or white, with intricate patterns that aid in pollination by specific insect species.

Phreatia keysseri is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in the ecological balance of its native habitat.
