Phaius terrestris

Phaius terrestris, commonly known as the golden iris, is a striking ornamental plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in wet, swampy environments, often near water bodies. The plant features broad, sword-shaped leaves that are dark green and glossy. Its most notable characteristic is its showy, golden-yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

Phaius terrestris is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with moist soil conditions.
