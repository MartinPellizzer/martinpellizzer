Paepalanthus speciosus var. attenuatus

Paepalanthus speciosus var. attenuatus, commonly known as the slender puya, is a striking South American plant belonging to the Xanthorrhoeaceae family.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces tall, showy inflorescences with clusters of small, often brightly colored flowers. The plant is native to the Andean regions of Peru and Bolivia, where it thrives in high-altitude environments. It is known for its ornamental value and is cultivated for its unique appearance and resilience.

The flowers typically bloom in late winter to early spring, adding a vibrant touch to its natural habitat.
