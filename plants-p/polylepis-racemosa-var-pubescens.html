Polylepis racemosa var. pubescens

Polylepis racemosa var. pubescens, commonly known as the Andean wax tree, is a shrub or small tree native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and distinctive, elongated inflorescences. The plant thrives in cold, arid environments and is adapted to the extreme conditions of the Andean mountains. Its leaves are covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in harsh climates.

This species plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
