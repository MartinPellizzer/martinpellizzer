Potentilla recta subsp. hirta

Potentilla recta subsp.

hirta, commonly known as hairy cinquefoil, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils.

The plant features wiry, hairy stems and basal leaves that are palmately divided into five lobes. Its flowers are small, yellow, and clustered in compact inflorescences, blooming in early summer.

It is valued in horticulture for its hardiness and ornamental appeal.
