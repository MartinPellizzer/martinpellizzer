Phyllanthus casticum var. kirganelia

Phyllanthus casticum var. kirganelia, commonly known as the Kirganelia phyllanthus, is a shrub native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically green when immature and turn red upon ripening. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, well-drained soils.

Its botanical features make it a subject of interest in both horticulture and ecological studies.
