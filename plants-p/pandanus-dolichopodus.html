Pandanus dolichopodus

Pandanus dolichopodus, commonly known as the long-fruit pandanus, is a tropical plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It is a member of the Pandanaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, feather-like leaves. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, yellowish flowers that eventually develop into elongated, fibrous fruits. Its roots are often used in traditional crafts, and its leaves are utilized in cooking and weaving.

The plant thrives in humid, coastal environments and is well adapted to sandy soils.
