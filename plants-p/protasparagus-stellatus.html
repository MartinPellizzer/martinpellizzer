Protasparagus stellatus

Protasparagus stellatus, commonly known as the star asparagus, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its prostrate growth habit and produces small, yellowish-white flowers clustered in racemes. The plant has small, scale-like leaves that are often mistaken for true leaves, and its stems are covered in fine, hair-like structures. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant.
