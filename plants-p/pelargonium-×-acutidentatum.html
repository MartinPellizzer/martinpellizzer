Pelargonium × acutidentatum

Pelargonium × acutidentatum, commonly known as the trailing geranium, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Geraniaceae family.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and vibrant, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with serrated edges, and it is native to South Africa. It is widely cultivated as an ornamental plant for gardens and containers due to its ability to thrive in various soil types and its tolerance to drought.

Pelargonium × acutidentatum is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
