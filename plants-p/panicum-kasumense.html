Panicum kasumense

Panicum kasumense, commonly known as Kasumense grass, is a species of grass native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny, wind-dispersed seeds.

Due to its adaptability, it is often used for soil stabilization and as a forage crop in certain regions.
