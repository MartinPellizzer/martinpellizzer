Paspalum praelongum

Paspalum praelongum, commonly known as the longawn paspalum, is a perennial grass native to South America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, erect stems that can reach heights of up to 1 meter. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with numerous tiny, two-lobed flowers. Its leaves are narrow and often have a bluish-green color, and it is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for various ecological and agricultural applications.

Paspalum praelongum is also valued for its ability to stabilize soil and prevent erosion in disturbed areas.
