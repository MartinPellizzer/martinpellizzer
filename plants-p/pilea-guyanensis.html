Pilea guyanensis

Pilea guyanensis, commonly known as the shield plant, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the family Urticaceae and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, white waxy coating. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers that appear in clusters. It is typically grown as a houseplant due to its attractive foliage and ease of care.

Pilea guyanensis prefers bright, indirect light and well-draining soil to thrive.
