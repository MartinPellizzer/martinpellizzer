Piper wabagense

Piper wabagense, commonly known as the Wabag mountain pepper, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of New Guinea.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species such as black pepper. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves and small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in spikes. It produces small, round berries that turn from green to red as they mature.

The plant is often found in humid, lowland forests and is valued for its aromatic properties.
