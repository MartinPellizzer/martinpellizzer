Pleurothallis tipuloides

Pleurothallis tipuloides, commonly known as the tipuloides orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Ecuador and Peru.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and pale yellow. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in cloud forests. Its roots are epiphytic, meaning it grows on other plants without being parasitic, and it requires well-draining, loose soil to flourish.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is a sought-after species among orchid enthusiasts.
