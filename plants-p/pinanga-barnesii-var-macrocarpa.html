Pinanga barnesii var. macrocarpa

Pinanga barnesii var. macrocarpa, commonly known as the giant bamboo palm, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can reach heights of up to 10 meters, forming a dense, bamboo-like structure. The plant produces large, paddle-shaped leaves that are often used in traditional medicine and ornamental horticulture. Its unique growth habit and aesthetic appeal make it a popular choice for indoor and outdoor cultivation.

However, it requires specific humidity and light conditions to thrive, making it a challenging but rewarding plant for experienced growers.
