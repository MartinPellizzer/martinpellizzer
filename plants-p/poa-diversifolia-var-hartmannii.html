Poa diversifolia var. hartmannii

Poa diversifolia var. hartmannii, commonly known as Hartmann's bentgrass, is a low-growing perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted foliage and small, spikelet-bearing inflorescences. The plant typically thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in grassland or woodland habitats. Its leaves are narrow and usually have a bluish-green hue, while the flowers are small and inconspicuous.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
