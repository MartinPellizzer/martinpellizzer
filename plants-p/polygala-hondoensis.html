Polygala hondoensis

Polygala hondoensis, commonly known as Hondo's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the island of Hondo in the Philippines.

It belongs to the Polygalaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, often spiral-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or coastal habitats, adapting to the harsh environmental conditions of its native region. Its small, delicate flowers are usually white or pale purple, and it produces capsule fruits that contain several seeds.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, Polygala hondoensis is considered a rare and potentially endangered species.
