Piper lucaeanum

Piper lucaeanum, commonly known as the Lucaean pepper, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to the Amazon rainforest and is characterized by its climbing habit and small, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by elongated, slender fruits. Its botanical features include a woody stem, opposite leaf arrangement, and a distinctive, pungent aroma.

It is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
