Polygala vulgaris var. caliptera

Polygala vulgaris var. caliptera, commonly known as the hairy false goat's beard, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, clustered flowers. The leaves are typically narrow and slightly hairy, giving the plant its common name. The flowers are usually pale yellow or greenish-white, with a distinctive structure that resembles a false beard.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and rocky areas.
