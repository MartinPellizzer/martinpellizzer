Platystyliparis speculifera

Platystyliparis speculifera, commonly known as the ghost orchid, is a rare and elusive orchid species native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique, elongated, and often curved inflorescence that resembles a ghostly silhouette, giving it its common name. The plant is epiphytic, meaning it grows on the surfaces of trees without deriving nutrients from them, relying instead on air, rain, and accumulated debris. Its flowers are typically white or pale in color, with intricate patterns that aid in pollination by specific insect species.

Due to habitat loss and its elusive nature, Platystyliparis speculifera is considered a critically endangered plant.
