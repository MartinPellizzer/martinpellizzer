Panicum chaseae

Panicum chaseae, commonly known as Chase's panicgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as wetlands and prairies. The plant has slender, cylindrical stems and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, two-lobed spikelets. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green color that helps it stand out in its environment.

Panicum chaseae is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
