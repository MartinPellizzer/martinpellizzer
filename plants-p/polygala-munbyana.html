Polygala munbyana

Polygala munbyana, commonly known as the Munby's milk-vetch, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its delicate, often pink or white flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. Its leaves are usually narrow and opposite, and it produces small, bead-like fruits that contain the seeds.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and somewhat endangered species in its native range.
