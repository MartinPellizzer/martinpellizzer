Phaseolus difformis

Phaseolus difformis, commonly known as the horsebean or sickle bean, is a climbing legume native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces elongated, curved pods containing several seeds, which are often used as a food source. It thrives in warm climates and is adapted to various soil types.

Botanically, it is notable for its ability to fix nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility.
