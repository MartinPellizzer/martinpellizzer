Pavonia spinifex var. oblongifolia

Pavonia spinifex var. oblongifolia, commonly known as the roughleaf pavonia, is a perennial shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features lance-shaped, hairy leaves that give it its common name, and it produces clusters of pink to red flowers throughout the summer. The plant is often found in moist, wooded areas and thrives in partial to full shade. Its showy blooms attract pollinators like bees and butterflies, making it a valuable addition to native gardens.

The species is known for its hardy nature and ability to tolerate a variety of soil conditions.
