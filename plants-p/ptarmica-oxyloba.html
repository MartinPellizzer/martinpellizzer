Ptarmica oxyloba

Ptarmica oxyloba, commonly known as the narrow-leaved cow-wheat, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its distinctive pod-like fruits and nitrogen-fixing ability. The plant features trifoliate leaves with serrated edges and produces small, yellowish-white flowers in clusters. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Ptarmica oxyloba plays a role in supporting local insect populations and is often found in conservation-focused habitats.
