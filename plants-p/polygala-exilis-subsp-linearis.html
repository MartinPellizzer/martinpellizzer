Polygala exilis subsp. linearis

Polygala exilis subsp.

linearis, commonly known as the slender milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its slender, often branching stems and small, greenish-white flowers arranged in racemes.

The leaves are linear and usually hairy, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in dry, sunny habitats such as rocky soils and open woodlands.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
