Passiflora spinosa

Passiflora spinosa, commonly known as the thorned passionflower, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, showy flowers with five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments. The plant produces edible berries that are often used in local cuisines. Its leaves are palmately lobed and covered with small, stiff spines.

Passiflora spinosa is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
