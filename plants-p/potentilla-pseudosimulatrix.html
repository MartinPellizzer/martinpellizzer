Potentilla pseudosimulatrix

Potentilla pseudosimulatrix, commonly known as the false sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Arctic and alpine regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its small, clustered flowers that typically bloom in shades of white or pink. The plant has slender, hairy stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are adapted to cold, harsh environments. It thrives in rocky soils and is often found in tundra and mountainous habitats.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and resilience.
