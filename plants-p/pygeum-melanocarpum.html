Pygeum melanocarpum

Pygeum melanocarpum, commonly known as the black-fruited African ebony, is a small evergreen tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Proteaceae family and is characterized by its dark, glossy leaves and small, clustered flowers. The tree produces hard, dark-colored fruits that contain seeds, which are dispersed by animals. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in moist, shaded habitats.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological importance, it is of interest in both horticulture and conservation efforts.
