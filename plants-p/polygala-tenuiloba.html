Polygala tenuiloba

Polygala tenuiloba, commonly known as narrowleaf milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Polygalaceae family and typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments. The plant features slender, often hairy stems and small, delicate leaves that are usually less than an inch in length. Its flowers are usually pink or purple and are arranged in clusters, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

It is adapted to drought conditions and plays a role in stabilizing sandy soils in its native habitat.
