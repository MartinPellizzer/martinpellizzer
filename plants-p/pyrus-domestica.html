Pyrus domestica

Pyrus domestica, commonly known as the common pear.

It is a deciduous tree native to Europe, western Asia, and northern Africa. The plant produces edible fruit with a sweet, sometimes tart flavor, and its leaves are alternate, simple, and ovate. The flowers are white or yellow and are typically pollinated by insects.

The fruit develops from an inferior ovary and is surrounded by a fleshy, edible pericarp.
