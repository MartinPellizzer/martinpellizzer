Platyrhiza quadricolor

Platyrhiza quadricolor, commonly known as the four-colored bladderwort, is a small aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in Asia.

It is characterized by its unique, inflated, bladder-like structures that aid in capturing small aquatic organisms. The plant typically grows in shallow, still waters and is known for its ability to float on the water's surface. Its leaves are small and often clustered around the base of the plant, while its flowering stems produce tiny, greenish-white flowers.

Platyrhiza quadricolor is valued in both ecological studies and ornamental gardening due to its distinctive appearance and ecological role.
