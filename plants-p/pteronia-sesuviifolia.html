Pteronia sesuviifolia

Pteronia sesuviifolia, commonly known as the coastal pteronia, is a succulent shrub native to southern Africa.

It features small, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white wax, which helps reduce water loss in arid environments. The plant produces clusters of tubular, pink to purple flowers during the summer months. It is well adapted to coastal and sandy soils, thriving in full sun.

Its unique appearance and hardy nature make it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.
