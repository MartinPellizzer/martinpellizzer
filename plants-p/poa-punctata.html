Poa punctata

Poa punctata, commonly known as the dotted meadowgrass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and narrow, bluish-green leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, two-lobed flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, stream banks, and wetlands.

Poa punctata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting insect and bird populations.
