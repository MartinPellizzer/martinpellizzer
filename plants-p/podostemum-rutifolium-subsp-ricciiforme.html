Podostemum rutifolium subsp. ricciiforme

Podostemum rutifolium subsp.

ricciiforme, commonly known as the velvetleaf water plant, is a semi-aquatic flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa. It is characterized by its small, opposite leaves that are often covered in a dense, velvety pubescence.

The plant typically grows in slow-moving or still freshwater habitats, such as riverbanks and ponds. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange, with five petals and a distinctive, spurred structure.

It plays an important ecological role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various invertebrates and small fish.
