Pilea appendicilata

Pilea appendicilata, commonly known as the velvet plant, is a species of flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that give it a velvety texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers that appear in clusters. It prefers bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Its compact growth habit and attractive foliage make it an ornamental plant with low maintenance requirements.
