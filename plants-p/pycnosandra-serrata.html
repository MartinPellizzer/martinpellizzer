Pycnosandra serrata

Pycnosandra serrata, commonly known as the serrated pycnosandra, is a climbing plant belonging to the Araceae family.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves with serrated edges and produces striking, spadix-like inflorescences. Native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia, it thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant is often found in lowland forests and is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in botanical gardens and ecological studies.
