Podanthum scoparium

Podanthum scoparium, commonly known as the silverleaf ironwood, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its dense, silver-gray foliage and small, inconspicuous flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, woody fruits that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing on rocky soils.

Its slow growth rate and drought tolerance make it a hardy, long-lived plant adapted to harsh conditions.
