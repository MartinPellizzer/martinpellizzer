Pulsatilla patens subsp. uralensis

Pulsatilla patens subsp.

uralensis, commonly known as the Siberian pasqueflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its showy, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow, typically blooming in early spring.

The plant has a basal rosette of hairy, spoon-shaped leaves that help it survive in cold, alpine environments. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in meadows and forest edges.

The species is noted for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for various ailments.
