Pleioblastus shibuyanus var. basihirsutus

Pleioblastus shibuyanus var. basihirsutus, commonly known as the Shibuya bamboo, is a clump-forming evergreen bamboo species native to Japan.

It features slender, gracefully arching stems covered in a dense layer of fine, white to pale yellow hairs, giving it a distinctive fuzzy appearance. The leaves are lance-shaped, dark green, and often have a bluish tinge, contributing to its ornamental value. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers well-drained, moist soil.

This variety is prized for its elegant form and is often used in ornamental gardening and landscape design.
