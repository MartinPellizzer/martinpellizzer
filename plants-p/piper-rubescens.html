Piper rubescens

Piper rubescens, commonly known as red pepper vine, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other species like the common pepper plant. The plant is characterized by its trailing growth habit and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. Its leaves are typically ovate with a rough texture and emit a mild peppery scent.

The fruit, which is a small, red, fleshy berry, is edible and often used in traditional medicine and culinary applications.
