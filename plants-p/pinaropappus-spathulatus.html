Pinaropappus spathulatus

Pinaropappus spathulatus, commonly known as the broad-leafed pinyon, is a shrub native to the arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States.

It belongs to the Pinaceae family, which includes various coniferous trees and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its broad, flat leaves that are arranged in a spiral pattern along the stems. Its small, cone-like structures contain the seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

This species is well adapted to harsh environments, exhibiting drought tolerance and a slow growth rate.
