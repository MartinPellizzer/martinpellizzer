Perdicium flexuosum

Perdicium flexuosum, commonly known as the flexible fiddlehead, is a rare and delicate fern species native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its coiled, fiddlehead-like fronds that unfurl into feather-like structures as it matures. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in cloud forests or along stream banks. Its reproductive structures, including sporangia, are typically located on the underside of its leaves.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, Perdicium flexuosum is considered a vulnerable species in conservation efforts.
