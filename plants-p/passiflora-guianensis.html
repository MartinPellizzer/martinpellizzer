Passiflora guianensis

Passiflora guianensis, commonly known as the giant passionflower, is a climbing vine native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that feature five prominent sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments surrounding the reproductive structures. The plant produces edible, tropical fruits known as passionfruits, which are rich in vitamins and antioxidants. Its leaves are typically large, heart-shaped, and have a velvety texture.

Passiflora guianensis thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in rainforests and along riverbanks.
