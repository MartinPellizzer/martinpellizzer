Puccinellia sachalinensis

Puccinellia sachalinensis, commonly known as the Sakhalin puccinellia, is a perennial grass species native to northeastern Asia.

It typically grows in wetland and saline environments, showcasing its adaptability to challenging soil conditions. The plant has slender, cylindrical stems and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. Its leaves are narrow and often covered with fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

This species is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
