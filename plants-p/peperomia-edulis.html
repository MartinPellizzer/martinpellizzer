Peperomia edulis

Peperomia edulis, commonly known as the edible peperomia, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its compact, rosette-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, powdery coating. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or red in color. Its leaves are edible and have a slightly peppery or citrus-like flavor, making it a unique ingredient in some regional cuisines.

Peperomia edulis thrives in well-drained soil and prefers indirect sunlight, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.
