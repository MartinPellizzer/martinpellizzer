Panicum schimperianum

Panicum schimperianum, commonly known as the Schimper's panicgrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in open habitats such as grasslands and rocky outcrops, thriving in well-drained soils. The plant features slender, erect stems with narrow, linear leaves that are often hairy along the edges. Its inflorescence consists of a loose cluster of spikelets, each bearing small, two-lobed florets.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
