Panicum karumiense

Panicum karumiense, commonly known as Karum Island grass, is a species of flowering plant in the grass family Poaceae.

It is native to the coastal regions of southern Africa, particularly around the island of Karum. The plant is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers.

Panicum karumiense is adapted to sandy, well-drained soils and is often found in dune environments.
