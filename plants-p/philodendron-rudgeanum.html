Philodendron rudgeanum

Philodendron rudgeanum, commonly known as the Rudge's philodendron, is a tropical climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It features heart-shaped, dark green leaves with a velvety texture and prominent veins. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by green, fleshy berries that turn red when mature. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires consistent moisture and high humidity.

Its unique foliage makes it a popular choice for indoor gardening and botanical collections.
