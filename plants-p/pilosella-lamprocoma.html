Pilosella lamprocoma

Pilosella lamprocoma, commonly known as the silverleaf pearly everlasting, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant features silver-gray foliage with a soft, fuzzy texture, and its flowers bloom in clusters of white to pale yellow. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Pilosella lamprocoma is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
