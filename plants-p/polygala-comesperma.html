Polygala comesperma

Polygala comesperma, commonly known as the narrowleaf milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its slender, often hairy stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. Its leaves are narrow and linear, and the flowers are usually pink or purple, with a distinctive spurred structure.

It plays a role in stabilizing soils and providing nectar for pollinators in its native range.
