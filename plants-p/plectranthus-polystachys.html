Plectranthus polystachys

Plectranthus polystachys, commonly known as the African violet, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its velvety, dark green leaves and clusters of tubular flowers that range in color from purple to white. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light to flourish. It is often cultivated as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and ease of care.

Plectranthus polystachys is also valued for its aromatic leaves, which can be used in traditional herbal remedies.
