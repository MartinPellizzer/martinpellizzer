Piper pastasanum

Piper pastasanum, commonly known as the West African pepper or wild pepper, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species such as black pepper. The plant is characterized by its climbing habit, with opposite, ovate leaves and small, greenish-white flowers. Its fruits are berries that turn red when mature, containing numerous tiny seeds.

The plant is often used in traditional medicine and has aromatic properties similar to other Piper species.
