Pelargonium salmoneum

Pelargonium salmoneum, commonly known as the salmon geranium, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its vibrant, salmon-pink flowers and heart-shaped leaves that are often variegated with white or green patterns. The plant typically grows as a trailing or bushy herb, reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters. It thrives in well-draining soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Pelargonium salmoneum is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its ornamental foliage and blossoms.
