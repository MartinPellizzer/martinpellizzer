Phyllanthus antillanus var. concolor

Phyllanthus antillanus var. concolor, commonly known as the Caribbean false mallow, is a shrub native to the Caribbean region.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by capsule fruits containing tiny, smooth seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas and disturbed habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional medicinal uses.
