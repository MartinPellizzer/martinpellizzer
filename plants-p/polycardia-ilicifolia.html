Polycardia ilicifolia

Polycardia ilicifolia, commonly known as the narrowleaf huckleberry, is a shrub native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It is characterized by its small, dark green leaves and clusters of bright red berries that ripen in the fall. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas. Its berries are edible and have a tart flavor, making them popular among wildlife and humans alike.

Botanically, it belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes other shrubs like blueberries and cranberries.
