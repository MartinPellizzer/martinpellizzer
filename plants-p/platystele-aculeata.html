Platystele aculeata

Platystele aculeata, commonly known as the spiny leafless orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a small, epiphytic orchid that grows on trees or rocks, relying on atmospheric moisture for nourishment. The plant is characterized by its simple, lance-shaped leaves and produces delicate, white or pale green flowers. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in cloud forests.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a threatened species in its native range.
