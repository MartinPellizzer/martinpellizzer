Polygonum panduriforme

Polygonum panduriforme, commonly known as the heartleaf knotweed, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive heart-shaped leaves that are often reddish in color. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically borne on slender stalks. Its stems are often reddish and can become woody with age.

Polygonum panduriforme is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and stream banks.
