Penstemon scariosus var. scariosus

Penstemon scariosus var. scariosus, commonly known as the scarious penstemon, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It typically grows in dry, rocky habitats such as grasslands and sagebrush steppe. The plant features upright stems with lance-shaped leaves that have a distinctive waxy coating. Its showy, tubular flowers range in color from blue to purple and bloom in late spring to early summer.

It is valued for its drought tolerance and ability to attract pollinators like bees and hummingbirds.
