Phoebe paraguariensis

Phoebe paraguariensis, commonly known as the Paraguay phoebe, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil, Paraguay, and Argentina.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels and cinnamon trees, and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and aromatic properties. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 6 meters, with dark green, glossy leaves and small white flowers that bloom in clusters.

Its fruits are small, dark berries that contain seeds, and the plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and medicinal uses in traditional medicine.
