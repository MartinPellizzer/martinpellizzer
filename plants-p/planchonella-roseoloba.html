Planchonella roseoloba

Planchonella roseoloba, commonly known as the red-veined hibiscus, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves with distinctive red veins, which give it its common name. The plant produces showy, five-petaled flowers that are typically pink or white, blooming during the warmer months. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and full sunlight.

Planchonella roseoloba is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
