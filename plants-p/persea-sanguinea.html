Persea sanguinea

Persea sanguinea, commonly known as the red mamey or red sapote, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central America and the Caribbean.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The tree produces small, round fruits with a thick, yellowish skin and a sweet, creamy pulp. Its botanical features include a smooth, pale bark and a tendency to grow in dense, humid forests.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and edible fruit, which is rich in nutrients.
