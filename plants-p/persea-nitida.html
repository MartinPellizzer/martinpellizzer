Persea nitida

Persea nitida, commonly known as the Mexican persimmon, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central America and parts of the Caribbean.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its glossy, lance-shaped leaves and fragrant, white flowers. The tree produces small, orange to red fruits that are edible when fully mature. Its botanical features include a smooth, gray bark and a dense canopy that provides shade.

Persea nitida is valued for its ornamental appeal and its fruit, which is often used in local cuisines.
