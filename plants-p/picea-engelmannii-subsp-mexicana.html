Picea engelmannii subsp. mexicana

Picea engelmannii subsp.

mexicana, commonly known as the Mexican fir, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to the mountainous regions of Mexico. It belongs to the family Pinaceae and is characterized by its conical shape and needle-like leaves.

The tree produces cones that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in temperate to subtropical climates and is often found in high-altitude forests.

The Mexican fir plays an important role in its ecosystem, providing habitat for various wildlife species.
