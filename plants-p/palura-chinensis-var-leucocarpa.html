Palura chinensis var. leucocarpa

Palura chinensis var. leucocarpa, commonly known as the white-fruited Chinese palura, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, dark green leaves and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant produces distinctive, fleshy, white berries that are edible and often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and medicinal properties.
