Pomaderris aspera

Pomaderris aspera, commonly known as rough pomaderris, is a shrub native to southern Australia.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its rough, often glandular, foliage. The leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, with a hairy texture. It produces small, white or pale yellow flowers in clusters, which are followed by small, round fruits.

This plant is well adapted to dry, sandy soils and is often found in heathlands and woodlands.
