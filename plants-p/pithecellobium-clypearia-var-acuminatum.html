Pithecellobium clypearia var. acuminatum

Pithecellobium clypearia var. acuminatum, commonly known as the acuminated pithecellobium, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces edible pods containing seeds, which are often used in local cuisines. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in secondary forests and disturbed areas.

Its growth habit makes it a useful plant for soil stabilization and reforestation efforts.
