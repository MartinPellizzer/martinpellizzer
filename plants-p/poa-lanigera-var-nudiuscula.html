Poa lanigera var. nudiuscula

Poa lanigera var. nudiuscula, commonly known as the slender meadow grass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows, grasslands, and riverbanks. The plant has slender, erect stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often hairy on the underside. Its inflorescence consists of a loose panicle of small, spikelet-like flowers.

This variety is characterized by its reduced leaf sheaths and is often found in association with other grasses in temperate climates.
