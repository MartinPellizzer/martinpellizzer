Papaver rubroaurantiacum

Papaver rubroaurantiacum, commonly known as the red-orange poppy, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, vibrant red and orange petals that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant produces capsules that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, often found in mountainous regions.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.
