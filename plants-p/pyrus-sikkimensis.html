Pyrus sikkimensis

Pyrus sikkimensis, commonly known as the Sikkim pear, is a deciduous tree native to the eastern Himalayas.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its broad leaves and white flowers that bloom in spring. The tree produces small, edible fruits that are typically harvested in late summer. It thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil and is often found at elevations between 1,500 and 3,000 meters.

Due to its ornamental value and fruit, it is cultivated in gardens and for local consumption.
