Potamogeton montanensis

Potamogeton montanensis, commonly known as mountain pondweed, is a freshwater aquatic plant native to North America.

It typically grows in shallow, slow-moving waters and is characterized by its long, narrow leaves that are often arranged in whorls. The plant produces small, green flowers that are clustered in spike-like inflorescences. It plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.

Potamogeton montanensis is also used in phytoremediation efforts due to its ability to absorb heavy metals from contaminated water.
