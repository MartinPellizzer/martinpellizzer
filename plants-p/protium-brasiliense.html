Protium brasiliense

Protium brasiliense, commonly known as Brazilian wild cinnamon, is a tropical tree native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae, which includes other aromatic plants like cinnamon and laurel. The plant is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers followed by oval, woody fruits. Its bark and leaves contain essential oils, giving it a distinct aromatic quality.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential medicinal properties.
