Pimelea ciliata subsp. ciliata

Pimelea ciliata subsp.

ciliata, commonly known as the silky pimelea, is a shrub native to southwestern Australia. It features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in spring, typically arranged in dense, spike-like clusters.

The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in heathlands and open woodlands.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
