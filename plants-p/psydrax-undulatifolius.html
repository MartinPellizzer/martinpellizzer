Psydrax undulatifolius

Psydrax undulatifolius, commonly known as the wavy-leaf psidium, is a small evergreen shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It features elliptical, wavy-edged leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, berry-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires a warm, humid climate.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruit, it is often cultivated in gardens and small-scale agriculture.
