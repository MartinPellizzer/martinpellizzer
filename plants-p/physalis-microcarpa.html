Physalis microcarpa

Physalis microcarpa, commonly known as the common groundcherry, is a herbaceous annual plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, and it produces small, yellow-orange fruits enclosed in a papery calyx. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and thrives in temperate climates. Its flowers are bell-shaped and usually pink or white, blooming in late summer.

The fruit, which is edible when ripe, is often used in culinary applications such as jams and sauces.
