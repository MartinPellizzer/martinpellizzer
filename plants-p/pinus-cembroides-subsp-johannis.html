Pinus cembroides subsp. johannis

Pinus cembroides subsp.

johannis, commonly known as the Texas fir, is a subspecies native to the south-central United States. It is a coniferous evergreen tree characterized by its needle-like leaves and cone-bearing structure.

The plant has a distinctive pyramidal shape with a dense crown, and its bark is thick and scaly, providing protection against fire and drought. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

The subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
