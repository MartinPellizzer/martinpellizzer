Prunus japonica var. sphaerica

Prunus japonica var. sphaerica, commonly known as the Japanese flowering cherry or some varieties as the globe cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pink flowers in early spring, which are followed by edible berries. Its leaves are oval and serrated, turning yellow in autumn.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
