Pithecellobium monadelphum

Pithecellobium monadelphum, commonly known as the velvet tamarind, is a small evergreen tree native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, clustered flowers. The plant produces edible pods containing seeds, which are often used in traditional cuisines.

Its growth habit and botanical features make it an important species in ecological and agricultural contexts.
