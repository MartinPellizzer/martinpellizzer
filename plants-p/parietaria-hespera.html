Parietaria hespera

Parietaria hespera, commonly known as the common wall-rocket, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and can be found along roadsides, in gardens, and on walls. Its leaves are usually heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its fruits are small, dry pods.

Parietaria hespera is known for its ability to thrive in various environmental conditions, making it a common weed in many regions.
