Polythecanthum cochinchinense

Polythecanthum cochinchinense, commonly known as the Vietnamese starfruit or carambola, is a tropical fruit-bearing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive five-pointed star shape when sliced, with a crisp, juicy flesh that ranges from sweet to tart in flavor. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth. Its leaves are large, heart-shaped, and have a slightly hairy texture, while its flowers are small and white, often clustered in groups.

The fruit is typically harvested when it turns a vibrant yellow or orange, indicating maturity.
