Pyrus sieboldii var. arborescens

Pyrus sieboldii var. arborescens, commonly known as the Japanese white pear, is a deciduous tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its ornamental white flowers that bloom in early spring before the leaves appear. The tree has a rounded, compact shape with smooth, gray bark that becomes fissured with age. Its leaves are broad, ovate, and turn a vibrant yellow in autumn.

This variety is valued for its aesthetic appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value.
