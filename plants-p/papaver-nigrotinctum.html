Papaver nigrotinctum

Papaver nigrotinctum, commonly known as black-stained poppy, is a rare and striking species of flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its dark purple to black flowers, which bloom in late summer and early autumn. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, dark seeds. Its leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, with a slightly hairy texture.

Papaver nigrotinctum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in specialized gardens due to its unique appearance.
