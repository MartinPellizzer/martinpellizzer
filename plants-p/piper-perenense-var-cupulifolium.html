Piper perenense var. cupulifolium

Piper perenense var. cupulifolium, commonly known as the cupulifolium pepper plant, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are often clustered at the base of the plant. The plant produces small, white or pale green flowers followed by elongated, slender fruits that turn red when mature. Its foliage is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is well-suited for cultivation in tropical and subtropical regions.
