Penstemon procerus var. tolmiei

Penstemon procerus var. tolmiei, commonly known as Tolmie's beardtongue, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and showy, tubular flowers that range in color from violet to pink. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils in open habitats such as meadows and slopes. It produces small, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

Tolmie's beardtongue is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its long blooming period and drought tolerance.
