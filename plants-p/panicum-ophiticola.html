Panicum ophiticola

Panicum ophiticola, commonly known as the firecracker grass, is a perennial grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive red-tipped, cylindrical inflorescences that resemble firecrackers, which are a striking feature during its flowering period. The plant typically grows in full sun and thrives in well-drained soils, making it adaptable to various environmental conditions. Its foliage is narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue that adds visual interest to landscapes.

Panicum ophiticola is often used in ornamental gardening for its vibrant color and drought tolerance.
