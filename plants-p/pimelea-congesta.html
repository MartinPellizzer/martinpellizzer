Pimelea congesta

Pimelea congesta, commonly known as the clustered bottlebrush, is a shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in heathlands and open forests.

Pimelea congesta is valued for its ornamental appeal and is relatively low maintenance once established.
