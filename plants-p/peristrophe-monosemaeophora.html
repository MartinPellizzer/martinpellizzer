Peristrophe monosemaeophora

Peristrophe monosemaeophora, commonly known as the narrow-leafed papyrus sedge, is a flowering plant native to wetland habitats in southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flower heads that are typically wind-pollinated, a common trait among Cyperaceae species. It thrives in shallow water or moist soil, playing a role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is often found in areas with consistent water levels and is of interest to botanists studying aquatic plant communities.
