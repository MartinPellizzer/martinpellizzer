Persea triplinervis var. valenzuelana

Persea triplinervis var. valenzuelana, commonly known as the Valenzuelan mango, is a tropical evergreen tree native to southern Mexico.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves with three prominent veins, giving it its scientific name. The tree produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow. Its fruit is a small, oval mango with a thin, smooth skin and sweet, aromatic flesh.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in warm, humid climates.
