Papaver nudicaule f. pygmaea

Papaver nudicaule f. pygmaea, commonly known as the dwarf poppy, is a compact variant of the silky poppy.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its small, delicate flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant typically grows to a height of only a few inches, making it ideal for rock gardens or as a border plant. Its foliage consists of finely divided, fern-like leaves that are covered in a soft, silky coating.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained soils with full sun.
