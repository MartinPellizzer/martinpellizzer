Paypayrola longifolia

Paypayrola longifolia, commonly known as the longleaf paypayrola, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, upright stems with narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers clustered in terminal racemes, which bloom in early summer. Its root system is fibrous and adapted to thrive in well-drained, sandy soils.

Paypayrola longifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
