Poterium gaillardotii

Poterium gaillardotii, commonly known as the mountain cactus, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of South America.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its spiny stems and clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in arid or semi-arid environments. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange and bloom during the warmer months.

Poterium gaillardotii is adapted to withstand extreme temperatures and limited water availability.
