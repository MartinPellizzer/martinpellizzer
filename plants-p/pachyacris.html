Pachyacris

Pachyacris, commonly known as the thick-horned grasshopper, is a genus of short-horned grasshoppers belonging to the family Acrididae.

These insects are characterized by their robust bodies and strong hind legs adapted for powerful jumps. They are primarily found in arid and semi-arid regions of the Americas, where they thrive in grassy and shrubland habitats. Pachyacris species typically have a cryptic coloration that helps them blend into their surroundings.

Their life cycle involves incomplete metamorphosis, with nymphs resembling adults but lacking wings.
