Paepalanthus bifidus var. exappendiculus

Paepalanthus bifidus var. exappendiculus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved puya, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its rosette-forming growth habit and long, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces striking, showy inflorescences composed of numerous small, bell-shaped flowers arranged in a spike. It thrives in high-altitude environments with cool, temperate climates and well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
