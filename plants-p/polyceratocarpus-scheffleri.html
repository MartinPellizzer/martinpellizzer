Polyceratocarpus scheffleri

Polyceratocarpus scheffleri, commonly known as the African mountain ebony, is a small evergreen tree native to eastern Africa.

It is characterized by its dark, dense wood and smooth, greyish-brown bark. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape and often have a waxy coating. It produces small, pale yellow flowers followed by small, round fruits.

This species is valued for its hardiness and is often grown in tropical and subtropical regions for ornamental purposes.
