Polygonum pergracile

Polygonum pergracile, commonly known as slender knotweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, which is characterized by its distinctive nodes and often triangular stems. The plant typically grows in moist environments such as wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped with a prominent midrib and are often covered in fine hairs.

Small, greenish-white flowers appear in clusters, and the plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to spread through rhizomes.
