Pteronia arcuata

Pteronia arcuata, commonly known as the silver cotyledon, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features fleshy, silver-green leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern, which helps it store water and reduce transpiration. The plant produces small, delicate flowers in shades of pink or white during the warmer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

Its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements make it a popular choice for xeriscaping.
