Pyrus diversifolia

Pyrus diversifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf pear, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that vary in shape, hence the species name "diversifolia.". The tree produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in early spring, followed by edible, sweet fruits in late summer. Its botanical features include a strong, straight trunk and a rounded crown.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit production.
