Potentilla haematochrus

Potentilla haematochrus, commonly known as the blood-red cinquefoil, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive red-orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant features compound leaves with five leaflets, often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
