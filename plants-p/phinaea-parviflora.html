Phinaea parviflora

Phinaea parviflora, commonly known as the small-flower phinaea, is a delicate flowering plant native to the temperate regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often covered in a fine layer of hair. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
