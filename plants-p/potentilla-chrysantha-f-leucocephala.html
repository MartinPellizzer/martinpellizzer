Potentilla chrysantha f. leucocephala

Potentilla chrysantha f. leucocephala, commonly known as the white-headed potentilla, is a flowering plant belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is a compact shrub characterized by its small, rounded leaves and clusters of showy white flowers. The flowers typically bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. This cultivar is prized for its ornamental value and is often used in garden landscapes for its delicate appearance.

It prefers well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it a versatile choice for various garden settings.
