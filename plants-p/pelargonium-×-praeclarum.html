Pelargonium × praeclarum

Pelargonium × praeclarum, commonly known as the scented geranium, is a hybrid plant in the Geraniaceae family.

It is characterized by its aromatic leaves that emit a variety of scents, such as citrus, mint, or lavender, depending on the cultivar. The plant typically has a trailing growth habit, making it suitable for hanging baskets or as a ground cover. Its flowers are small, clustered, and often pink or red, adding ornamental value.

Pelargonium × praeclarum thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, requiring regular watering and pruning to maintain its shape and fragrance.
