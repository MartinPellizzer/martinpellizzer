Parosela crassifolia

Parosela crassifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved parosela, is a flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are often violet or purple in color. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats.

Parosela crassifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with specific growing conditions.
