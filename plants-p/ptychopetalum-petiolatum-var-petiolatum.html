Ptychopetalum petiolatum var. petiolatum

Ptychopetalum petiolatum var. petiolatum, commonly known as the Brazilian rosewood, is a species native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 30 meters tall, characterized by its dark, dense wood and smooth, grayish-brown bark. The leaves are alternate, simple, and elliptical, with a glossy green upper surface and a lighter underside. Its flowers are small and clustered, producing fragrant, white or pale pink blooms that attract pollinators.

The plant is valued for its hard, heavy wood, which is highly sought after in musical instrument crafting.
