Passerina purpurea

Passerina purpurea, commonly known as the purple pasqueflower, is a striking early spring blooming plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is a member of the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, petal-like sepals and a central cluster of stamens. The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine environments, preferring well-drained, rocky soils. Its flowers, which range in color from white to deep purple, are pollinated primarily by bees and other early-season pollinators.

Passerina purpurea is known for its resilience to cold temperatures and its role in supporting local wildlife during the early spring months.
