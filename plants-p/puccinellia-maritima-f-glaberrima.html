Puccinellia maritima f. glaberrima

Puccinellia maritima f. glaberrima, commonly known as the glabrous saltbush, is a flowering plant belonging to the Poaceae family.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit and is typically found in coastal and saline environments. The plant exhibits reduced leaf hairs, giving it a smooth, glabrous appearance, which is a key distinguishing feature of this form. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dunes or salt marshes.

This subspecies is adapted to withstand high salinity and arid conditions, making it an important component of coastal vegetation.
