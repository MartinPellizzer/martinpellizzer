Potentilla × fraterna

Potentilla × fraterna, commonly known as the twinflower, is a hybrid shrub native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of small, pink or white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm and features dark green, oval-shaped leaves with serrated edges. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a versatile choice for rock gardens and borders.

Due to its ornamental value and hardiness, it is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and delicate blossoms.
