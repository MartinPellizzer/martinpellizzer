Papaver alpinum subvar. igneum

Papaver alpinum subvar. igneum, commonly known as the fiery poppy, is a striking plant native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its vibrant red to orange flowers, which bloom in late summer and early autumn. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes, where it thrives in full sun. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are sturdy and often branched.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its bold, fiery hues.
