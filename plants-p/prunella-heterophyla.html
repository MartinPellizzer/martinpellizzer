Prunella heterophyla

Prunella heterophyla, commonly known as the purple deadnettle, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface and clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from purple to pink. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and stream banks. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is known for its aromatic herbs and medicinal plants.

Prunella heterophyla is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and healing properties.
