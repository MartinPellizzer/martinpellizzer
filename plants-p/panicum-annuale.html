Panicum annuale

Panicum annuale, commonly known as annual panicgrass, is a flowering plant in the grass family, Poaceae.

It is an annual herb that typically grows in disturbed soils and is native to temperate regions of the Americas. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers, which are wind-pollinated. Its leaves are narrow and linear, with a bluish-green color, and it often forms dense colonies in open areas.

Panicum annuale is sometimes considered a weed but can also serve as forage for livestock in certain regions.
