Paepalanthus elongatus var. ciliatus

Paepalanthus elongatus var. ciliatus, commonly known as the silky pincushion cactus, is a species native to South America, particularly Argentina.

It is a low-growing, mat-forming cactus characterized by its cylindrical stems covered in soft, silky hairs. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, adding a delicate touch to its appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or arid landscapes.

Due to its unique appearance and relatively easy care requirements, it is a popular choice among cactus enthusiasts.
