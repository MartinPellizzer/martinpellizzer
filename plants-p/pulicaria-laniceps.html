Pulicaria laniceps

Pulicaria laniceps, commonly known as the woolly tansy, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing perennial with a mat-forming habit, featuring silver-gray foliage covered in dense, soft hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Its aromatic properties and ornamental value make it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
