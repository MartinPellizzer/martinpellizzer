Phyllostachys glauca

Phyllostachys glauca, commonly known as the silver bamboo, is a species of grass native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, hollow stems that are covered in a distinctive silvery-blue sheen. This ornamental bamboo is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal and is widely used in landscaping. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Phyllostachys glauca is a fast-growing plant that can reach heights of up to 10 feet, making it a popular choice for privacy screens and garden accents.
