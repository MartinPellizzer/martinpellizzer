Plantago refracta

Plantago refracta, commonly known as the narrowleaf plantain, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It features erect, branching stems that bear lance-shaped leaves with a rough, textured surface. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in dense clusters, which are borne on slender pedicels. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

Botanically, it belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and light conditions.
