Paspalum longiflorum

Paspalum longiflorum, commonly known as the long-flower grass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and produces dense, spike-like inflorescences that are often tinged with purple or red. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically green and covered in fine hairs. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions and full sun.

Paspalum longiflorum is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its aesthetic appeal and low maintenance requirements.
