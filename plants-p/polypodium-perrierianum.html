Polypodium perrierianum

Polypodium perrierianum, commonly known as the Perrier's cliff fern, is a species of fern native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped fronds that are typically less than 30 centimeters in length. The plant thrives in shaded, moist environments, often growing on rocky surfaces or in forest understories. Its reproductive structures, known as sori, are clustered on the underside of the fronds and release spores for propagation.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
