Phoradendron fosteri

Phoradendron fosteri, commonly known as the Foster's mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on various host trees, primarily in the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its evergreen leaves and small, white or yellowish flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces fleshy, red berries that are a food source for birds and other animals. Its seeds are dispersed by birds that consume the berries, facilitating its spread to new host trees.

Phoradendron fosteri plays a role in forest ecosystems by contributing to nutrient cycling and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
