Pyrus mercurius

Pyrus mercurius, commonly known as the mercury pear, is a rare and ancient species of pear native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its small, clustered flowers and small, edible fruits that are typically yellow or green in color. The plant thrives in cool, temperate climates and is often found in rocky or well-drained soils. Its leaves are typically oval-shaped with a smooth margin and a slightly hairy surface.

Due to its rarity and historical significance, Pyrus mercurius is of interest to botanists and conservationists alike.
