Protea witzenbergiana

Protea witzenbergiana, commonly known as the Witzenberg protea, is a striking evergreen shrub native to the Western Cape of South Africa.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescences composed of numerous overlapping bracts that resemble petals. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun, making it well-suited to coastal and mountainous regions. Its flowers come in shades of white, pink, and red, adding vibrant color to its natural habitat.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique and long-lasting blooms.
