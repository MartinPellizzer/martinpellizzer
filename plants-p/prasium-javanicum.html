Prasium javanicum

Prasium javanicum, commonly known as the Java senna, is a flowering plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly Indonesia, and thrives in tropical climates. The plant features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. Its seeds are encased in a hairy pod, and the plant is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Prasium javanicum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
