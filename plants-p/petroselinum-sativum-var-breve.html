Petroselinum sativum var. breve

Petroselinum sativum var. breve, commonly known as parsley, is a biennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its finely divided, aromatic leaves that are used widely in culinary applications. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that develop into small, round berries containing seeds. Its leaves contain essential oils, including apiol and limonene, which contribute to its distinctive flavor and aroma.

Parsley thrives in cool climates and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and culinary use.
