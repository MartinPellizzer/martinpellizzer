Pentameris

Pentameris, commonly known as the bluebell, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Lamiaceae.

It is characterized by its striking blue or purple flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in woodland areas and thrives in partial shade. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Pentameris is known for its aromatic properties and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
