Phyteuma canescens var. salicifolium

Phyteuma canescens var. salicifolium, commonly known as the silverleaf phyteuma, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Europe.

It features tall, slender stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, pale yellow to cream-colored flowers. The leaves are lance-shaped, with a silvery-white underside, giving the plant its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

This plant is valued in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
