Plectritis spathulata

Plectritis spathulata, commonly known as the narrowleaf ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and distinctive leaf shapes. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. Its small, purple to pink flowers bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Plectritis spathulata is often found in sandy or clay soils and is adapted to tolerate periodic flooding.
