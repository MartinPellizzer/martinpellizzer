Primulina macrodonta

Primulina macrodonta, commonly known as the large-leaf primulina, is a species of flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Gesneriaceae family and is characterized by its striking, velvety leaves and showy, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, thriving in the shaded understories of tropical rainforests. Its foliage is often a deep green with a waxy texture, and it produces vibrant orange or red flowers during the warmer months.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
