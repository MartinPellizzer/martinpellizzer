Primula bella subsp. indobella

Primula bella subsp.

indobella, commonly known as the Indochinese primrose, is a herbaceous perennial native to Southeast Asia. It features basal leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape, with a hairy surface.

The plant produces clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white to pink, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and shaded borders.
