Phrynium molle

Phrynium molle, commonly known as the soft phrynium, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters. The plant features broad, heart-shaped leaves with a waxy texture and a distinctive silver-green coloration. Its flowers are small, clustered in inflorescences, and are usually yellow or greenish-yellow.

Phrynium molle is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to humid, warm climates.
