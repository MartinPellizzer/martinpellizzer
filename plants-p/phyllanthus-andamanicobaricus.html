Phyllanthus andamanicobaricus

Phyllanthus andamanicobaricus, commonly known as the Andamanicobaricus phyllanthus, is a member of the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is a small shrub characterized by its simple, opposite leaves that are usually elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, clustered yellow flowers, which are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous tiny seeds. It is native to the Andaman and Nicobar Islands and thrives in tropical and subtropical regions.

Due to its limited distribution and ecological significance, it is considered a rare and endemic species.
