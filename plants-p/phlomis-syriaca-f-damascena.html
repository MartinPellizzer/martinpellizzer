Phlomis syriaca f. damascena

Phlomis syriaca f. damascena, commonly known as Damascene mullein, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features tall, upright stems that bear narrow, gray-green leaves with a fuzzy texture. The plant produces showy, clustered spikes of pale yellow to golden orange flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its ornamental value is complemented by its historical use in traditional medicine.
