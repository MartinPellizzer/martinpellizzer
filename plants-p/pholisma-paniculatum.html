Pholisma paniculatum

Pholisma paniculatum, commonly known as the desert broom.

It is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. This species is characterized by its erect, branching stems and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late spring. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in sandy or rocky soils.

The plant is adapted to withstand drought conditions and has a unique ability to photosynthesize at night, which helps it conserve water.
