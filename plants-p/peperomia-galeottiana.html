Peperomia galeottiana

Peperomia galeottiana, commonly known as the silver peperomia, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact rosette of fleshy, silver-green leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white hairs. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or reddish in color, appearing in the axils of the leaves. It thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Due to its slow growth and minimal maintenance requirements, it is well-suited for container cultivation.
