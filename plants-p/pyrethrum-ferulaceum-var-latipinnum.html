Pyrethrum ferulaceum var. latipinnum

Pyrethrum ferulaceum var. latipinnum, commonly known as the broad-leaved pyrethrum, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces pyrethrin, a natural insecticide, which is derived from its flower heads. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental and pest-control purposes.

Its botanical features make it both aesthetically pleasing and ecologically valuable.
