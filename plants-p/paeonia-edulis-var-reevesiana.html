Paeonia edulis var. reevesiana

Paeonia edulis var. reevesiana, commonly known as the Reeves' edible peony, is a variant of the peony genus native to China.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and red, often with a subtle fragrance. The plant produces fleshy, edible roots that are traditionally used in Chinese cuisine. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a suitable candidate for ornamental and edible gardening.

Its cultivation is valued both for its aesthetic appeal and its culinary uses.
