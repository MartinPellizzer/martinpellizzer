Primulina polycephala

Primulina polycephala, commonly known as the Shrimp Plant, is a tropical evergreen perennial native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Gesneriaceae family and is characterized by its striking, waxy, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant produces multiple flower clusters at the ends of its stems, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its leaves are typically ovate to elliptical, with a smooth, glossy surface.

Primulina polycephala thrives in bright, indirect light and requires consistent moisture to flourish.
