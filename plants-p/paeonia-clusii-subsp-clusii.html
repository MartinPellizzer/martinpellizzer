Paeonia clusii subsp. clusii

Paeonia clusii subsp.

clusii, commonly known as the Clusius peony, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Turkey and northern Iran. It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically range in color from white to deep pink, with a distinctive double or semi-double bloom structure.

The plant features robust, upright stems and heart-shaped, dark green leaves that are palmately lobed. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated for its beauty and fragrance.
