Primula geraniifolia

Primula geraniifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved primrose, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and rocky slopes. It is a perennial species that produces showy blooms in late spring to early summer.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for gardeners seeking to add ornamental value to woodland gardens.
