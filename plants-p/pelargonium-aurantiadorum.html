Pelargonium aurantiadorum

Pelargonium aurantiadorum, commonly known as the orange geranium, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its vibrant orange flowers and serrated, lobed leaves. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and containers.

Pelargonium aurantiadorum is a perennial species that prefers well-drained soil and is relatively drought-tolerant.
