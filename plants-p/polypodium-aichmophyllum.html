Polypodium aichmophyllum

Polypodium aichmophyllum, commonly known as the sharp-pointed polypody, is a species of fern belonging to the family Polypodiaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, strap-like fronds that are typically dark green and have a leathery texture. The plant produces sporangia on the underside of its fronds, which are clustered in small, round clusters called sori. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in rocky or forested areas.

Polypodium aichmophyllum is a hardy, evergreen plant that is commonly used in horticulture for its ornamental value.
