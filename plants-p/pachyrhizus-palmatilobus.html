Pachyrhizus palmatilobus

Pachyrhizus palmatilobus, commonly known as the broad-lobed peanut vine, is a climbing legume native to South America.

It is characterized by its palmately lobed leaves and distinctive yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces edible seeds encased in a fibrous pod, similar to other members of the Fabaceae family. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its botanical features make it an important species for both ecological and agricultural studies.
