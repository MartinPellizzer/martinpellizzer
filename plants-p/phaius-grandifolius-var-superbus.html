Phaius grandifolius var. superbus

Phaius grandifolius var. superbus, commonly known as the giant orchid, is a striking tropical orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, broad leaves that can reach up to 1 meter in length, giving it a dramatic appearance. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, often in clusters along the stem. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining, nutrient-rich soil.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and greenhouses.
