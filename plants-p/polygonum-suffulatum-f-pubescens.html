Polygonum suffulatum f. pubescens

Polygonum suffulatum f. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy knotweed, is a variant of the species Polygonum suffulatum, which belongs to the Polygonaceae family.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in moist environments such as wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks. Its leaves are often arranged alternately and have a heart-shaped base.

This plant is known for its ability to thrive in nutrient-rich soils and can sometimes become invasive in certain regions.
