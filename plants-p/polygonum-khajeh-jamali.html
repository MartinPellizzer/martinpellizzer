Polygonum khajeh-jamali

Polygonum khajeh-jamali, commonly known as the Persian knotweed, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, characterized by its distinctive arrow-shaped leaves and clusters of small, greenish flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky and gravelly soils, preferring sunny to partially shaded environments. Its stems are often reddish in color and can become woody with age.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
