Prunella grandiflora subsp. hastifolia

Prunella grandiflora subsp.

hastifolia, commonly known as narrow-leaved selfheal, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It features upright, branching stems that are often hairy, and it produces spikes of blue to purple flowers in the summer.

The leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a rough texture, and they are arranged oppositely along the stem. This plant thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

It is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional herbal remedies.
