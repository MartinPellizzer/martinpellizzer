Potamogeton × saxonicus

Potamogeton × saxonicus, commonly known as the saxonic water plantain, is a hybrid species within the Potamogetonaceae family.

It is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves that are typically submerged or floating in freshwater environments. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with minute flowers, which are often pollinated by wind. Its roots form dense mats that can stabilize sediments and provide habitat for aquatic organisms.

Potamogeton × saxonicus is native to Europe and is often found in slow-moving or still freshwater habitats.
