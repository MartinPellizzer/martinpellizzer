Phacelia oreopola subsp. simulans

Phacelia oreopola subsp.

simulans, commonly known as the Sierra phacelia, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America. It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes.

The plant produces striking blue to purple flowers in clusters, which attract a variety of pollinators including bees and butterflies. Its leaves are generally lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, aiding in water retention in arid environments.

The species is valued for its ecological role in supporting local wildlife and its ornamental appeal in native gardens.
