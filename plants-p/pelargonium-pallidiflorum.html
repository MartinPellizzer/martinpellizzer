Pelargonium pallidiflorum

Pelargonium pallidiflorum, commonly known as the paleflower geranium, is a species native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and clusters of small, pale pink to white flowers. The plant features rounded, velvety leaves that are often a deep green color. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

Pelargonium pallidiflorum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ease of cultivation.
