Pleurandra cistiflora

Pleurandra cistiflora, commonly known as the rock pear, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and produces small, white, fragrant flowers in clusters. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. Its fruit is a small, fleshy berry that contains several seeds.

Pleurandra cistiflora is often used in ornamental gardening for its attractive appearance and drought tolerance.
