Polygala acanthoclada var. intricata

Polygala acanthoclada var. intricata, commonly known as the intricate milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Polygalaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, fern-like foliage and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun conditions. Its flowers are usually pink or purple, and it blooms from spring to early summer.

Due to its ornamental value and limited distribution, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
