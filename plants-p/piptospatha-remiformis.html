Piptospatha remiformis

Piptospatha remiformis, commonly known as the slender false hellebore, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in a basal rosette. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in umbels, which bloom in early spring. Its rhizomatous root system allows it to spread vegetatively, making it somewhat invasive in certain areas.

Botanically, it belongs to the Araliaceae family and is characterized by its unique leaf morphology and medicinal properties.
