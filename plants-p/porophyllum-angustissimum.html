Porophyllum angustissimum

Porophyllum angustissimum, commonly known as narrowleaf fleabane, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. The plant features narrow, linear leaves and produces small, yellowish-white composite flowers that bloom in late summer. Its flowering heads are composed of numerous tiny florets, typical of the Asteraceae family.

Porophyllum angustissimum is adapted to arid conditions and is often found in disturbed or sandy soils.
