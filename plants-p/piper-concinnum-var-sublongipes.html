Piper concinnum var. sublongipes

Piper concinnum var. sublongipes, commonly known as the long-stemmed pepper plant, is a tropical vine native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many pepper species. The plant is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and heart-shaped leaves with a slightly serrated edge. Its small, greenish-yellow flowers are followed by elliptical, dark purple to black berries.

The berries are often used in traditional medicine and have a peppery, slightly spicy flavor.
