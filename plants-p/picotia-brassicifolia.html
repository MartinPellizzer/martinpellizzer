Picotia brassicifolia

Picotia brassicifolia, commonly known as the golden wax plant, is a member of the Euphorbiaceae family and is native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is a shrub-like plant characterized by its smooth, waxy leaves that are often a vibrant green or yellowish-green in color. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or yellow, followed by distinctive orange or red berries. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant in warmer climates.
