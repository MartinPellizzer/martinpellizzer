Plumeria domingensis

Plumeria domingensis, commonly known as the Dominican frangipani, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is a member of the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its showy, fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of white, yellow, and pink. The plant typically grows as a small tree or shrub, reaching heights of up to 6 meters, with smooth, pale green bark. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a waxy texture, while its flowers are borne in clusters at the ends of branches.

The plant is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its striking appearance and tropical aesthetic.
