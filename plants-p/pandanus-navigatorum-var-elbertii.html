Pandanus navigatorum var. elbertii

Pandanus navigatorum var. elbertii, commonly known as the Elbert's pandanus, is a variant of the pandanus plant native to certain regions of the Pacific.

It is characterized by its tall, narrow leaves that are often used in traditional crafts and thatching. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences with fragrant flowers, which are pollinated by insects and birds. Its roots system is robust, allowing it to thrive in coastal and sandy soils.

This variant is valued for both its ecological role and cultural significance in local communities.
