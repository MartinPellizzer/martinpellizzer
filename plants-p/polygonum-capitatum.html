Polygonum capitatum

Polygonum capitatum, commonly known as the clustered knotweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its upright stems and dense clusters of small, greenish-white flowers that appear in late summer. The plant has opposite, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Polygonum capitatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in wet areas.
