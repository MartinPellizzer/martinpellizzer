Pandanus gillespiei

Pandanus gillespiei, commonly known as the Gillespie's pandanus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Pandanaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of the Pacific, particularly found in parts of French Polynesia. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and long, narrow leaves that are often used in traditional crafts. It produces small, clustered inflorescences with yellowish flowers, which are followed by spiny, three-lobed fruits.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and cultural significance in the regions where it is found.
