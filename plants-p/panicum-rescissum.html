Panicum rescissum

Panicum rescissum, commonly known as cut panicgrass, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, spreading blades. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, two-lobed florets. It thrives in wetland environments and is often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

Due to its adaptability, it plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
