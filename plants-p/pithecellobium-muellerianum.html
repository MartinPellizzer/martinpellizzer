Pithecellobium muellerianum

Pithecellobium muellerianum, commonly known as the yellow mimosoid, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its feathery, bipinnate leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by bean-like pods containing edible seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories or along riverbanks.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential use in agroforestry systems.
