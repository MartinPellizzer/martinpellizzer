Phyllanthus austinii

Phyllanthus austinii, commonly known as Austin's spurge, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a low-growing perennial shrub characterized by its smooth, green stems and small, alternate leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which are followed by small, greenish-brown fruits. It thrives in wetland habitats and is often found in marshes and along the edges of ponds.

The species is notable for its adaptability to moist environments and its role in local ecosystems.
