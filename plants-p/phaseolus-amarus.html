Phaseolus amarus

Phaseolus amarus, commonly known as bitter snap bean, is a species of legume native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that give way to elongated pods containing several dark brown, kidney-shaped seeds. It is known for its bitter taste, which is due to the presence of certain alkaloids and tannins.

This plant is often grown as a traditional crop in tropical and subtropical regions.
