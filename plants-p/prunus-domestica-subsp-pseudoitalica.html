Prunus domestica subsp. pseudoitalica

Prunus domestica subsp.

pseudoitalica, commonly known as the Italian plum, is a subspecies of the European plum characterized by its small, yellow to orange fruits. It is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soils.

The plant typically grows as a small tree or shrub, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are ovate with a serrated margin, and it produces fragrant white flowers in early spring.

The subspecies is valued for its culinary uses, particularly in jams and preserves.
