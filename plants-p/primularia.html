Primularia

Primularia, commonly known as the "Primrose," is a genus of flowering plants belonging to the family Primulaceae.

These plants are typically herbaceous, with showy, often brightly colored flowers that bloom in early spring. They are native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere and are often found in woodland or meadow habitats. The leaves are usually broad and oval, with a smooth or hairy surface depending on the species.

Primularia species are valued for their ornamental appeal and are commonly cultivated in gardens.
