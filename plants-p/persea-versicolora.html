Persea versicolora

Persea versicolora, commonly known as the variable persimmon, is a small evergreen tree native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that vary in color from dark green to reddish-brown, depending on the season and light exposure. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by orange to red, fleshy fruits that are edible when fully ripe. Its fruit has a sweet, sometimes astringent flavor and is often used in local cuisines.

The tree is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in humid, tropical climates.
