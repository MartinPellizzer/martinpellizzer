Penstemon cardwellii

Penstemon cardwellii, commonly known as Cardwell's beardtongue, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is a member of the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its tall, spire-like inflorescence of tubular, two-lipped flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun, making it well-suited for arid environments. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces showy blooms in shades of purple, pink, and white.

Penstemon cardwellii is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
