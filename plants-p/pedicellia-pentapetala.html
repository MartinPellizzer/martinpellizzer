Pedicellia pentapetala

Pedicellia pentapetala, commonly known as the five-petaled cluster plant, is a flowering plant characterized by its unique inflorescence structure.

It features a compact, branched stem that bears small, clustered flowers. The plant typically has five petals per flower, which are often brightly colored to attract pollinators. Its leaves are usually narrow and arranged alternately along the stem.

This species is native to specific regions and thrives in moist, well-drained soils.
