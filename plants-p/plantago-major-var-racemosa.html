Plantago major var. racemosa

Plantago major var. racemosa, commonly known as the greater plantain, is a variant of the common plantain characterized by its tall, erect growth habit.

It features broad, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs, giving them a distinctive appearance. The plant produces long, branched inflorescences bearing small, greenish flowers that develop into capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It is a hardy perennial that thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.

This plant is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally for its healing benefits.
