Pilosocereus tuberculosus

Pilosocereus tuberculosus, commonly known as the Moon Cactus, is a cactus species native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its distinctive, rounded, and often spiny stems that form a dense, globular shape. The plant features a thick, fleshy stem that stores water, allowing it to survive in arid environments. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, appearing in clusters during the warmer months.

Pilosocereus tuberculosus is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
