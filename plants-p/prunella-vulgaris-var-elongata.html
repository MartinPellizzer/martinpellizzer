Prunella vulgaris var. elongata

Prunella vulgaris var. elongata, commonly known as long-stemmed selfheal, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features slender, upright stems that can grow up to 60 cm in height, covered in fine, hairy hairs. The plant produces small, blue to purple flowers arranged in dense clusters, typically blooming from June to August. Its leaves are lance-shaped, serrated, and have a slightly hairy texture.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, woodlands, and along stream banks.
