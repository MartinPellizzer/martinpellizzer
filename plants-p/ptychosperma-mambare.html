Ptychosperma mambare

Ptychosperma mambare, commonly known as the Mambare palm, is a species of flowering plant in the Arecaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including parts of Indonesia and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate leaves that can reach several meters in length. It produces small, clustered inflorescences with both male and female flowers, typical of many palm species.

The fruit is a small, fibrous drupe that contains a single seed, contributing to its propagation in natural habitats.
