Pentaschistis filiformis

Pentaschistis filiformis, commonly known as the slender false dandelion, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its leaves are linear and often covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are small and yellow, arranged in a cluster.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
