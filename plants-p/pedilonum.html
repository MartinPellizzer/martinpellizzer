Pedilonum

Pedilonum, commonly known as the velvet tamarind, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by elongated, woody pods containing edible seeds. The seeds are often used in traditional medicine and as a source of protein.

Its root system is extensive, allowing it to thrive in poor soil conditions.
