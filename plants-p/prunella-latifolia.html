Prunella latifolia

Prunella latifolia, commonly known as broadleaf heal-all, is a herbaceous plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its upright, branched stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Prunella latifolia is known for its medicinal properties, including anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial effects.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, woodlands, and along stream banks.
