Potamogeton × nitens f. lacuum

Potamogeton × nitens f. lacuum, commonly known as the lacustrine pondweed, is a hybrid aquatic plant that thrives in freshwater environments.

It is characterized by its long, ribbon-like leaves that are typically narrow and slightly curved. The plant produces small, green flowers that are clustered in spikes, which emerge from the water surface. It plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various water organisms.

Due to its adaptability, it is often found in still or slow-moving waters such as lakes and ponds.
