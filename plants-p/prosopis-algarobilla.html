Prosopis algarobilla

Prosopis algarobilla, commonly known as the Algarobilla tree, is a leguminous shrub or small tree native to the arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its deep root system, which allows it to thrive in poor soils and withstand drought conditions. The plant produces small, green pods that contain edible seeds, which are a traditional food source for both humans and animals. It also plays an important role in stabilizing soils and preventing erosion in desert environments.

Additionally, its wood is used for fuel and construction due to its durability and resistance to decay.
