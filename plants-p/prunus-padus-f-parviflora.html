Prunus padus f. parviflora

Prunus padus f. parviflora, commonly known as the dwarf cherry, is a compact form of the common bird cherry.

It is characterized by its small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The leaves are ovate and have a serrated margin, with a dark green upper surface and a lighter underside. This plant is typically found in moist, shady woodlands and thrives in temperate climates.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing food for pollinators.
