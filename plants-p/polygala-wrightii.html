Polygala wrightii

Polygala wrightii, commonly known as Wright's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, showy flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats, such as slopes and canyons, and is adapted to arid conditions. Its flowers are usually pink or purple and bloom in late spring to early summer.

The species is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
