Phaseolus strictus

Phaseolus strictus, commonly known as the scarlet runner bean, is a climbing legume native to the Americas.

It produces vibrant red or yellow flowers and elongated pods that contain edible seeds. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets and is characterized by its vigorous vining growth habit. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Phaseolus strictus is often cultivated for both its ornamental value and as a food source.
