Phacelia eisenii var. brandegeeana

Phacelia eisenii var. brandegeeana, commonly known as the Brandegee's phacelia, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, branching stems and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. The plant produces small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically hairy and have a glandular texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a common sight in arid and semi-arid regions.

This species is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.
