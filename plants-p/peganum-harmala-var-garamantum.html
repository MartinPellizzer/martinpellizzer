Peganum harmala var. garamantum

Peganum harmala var. garamantum, commonly known as the desert cowpea, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the Middle East and North Africa.

It is a shrub characterized by its woody stems and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces capsule fruits containing numerous small, hard seeds. It is adapted to survive in harsh, drought-prone environments with minimal water requirements.

Due to its chemical composition, it has been used traditionally in various medicinal and ritualistic practices.
