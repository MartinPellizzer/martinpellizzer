Pittosporum quinquevalvatum

Pittosporum quinquevalvatum, commonly known as the five-valved pittosporum, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant white flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant produces small, orange-red berries that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and seasonal blooms.

Pittosporum quinquevalvatum prefers well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it suitable for various garden environments.
