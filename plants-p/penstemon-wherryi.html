Penstemon wherryi

Penstemon wherryi, commonly known as Wherry's beardtongue, is a perennial flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces spikes of tubular, two-lipped flowers that range in color from pink to purple. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is well adapted to arid environments. It is often found in rocky soils and is a member of the Plantaginaceae family.

Penstemon wherryi is valued in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental appeal.
