Potamogeton densus f. setaceus

Potamogeton densus f. setaceus, commonly known as the dense pondweed.

It is a submerged aquatic plant characterized by its dense, ribbon-like leaves that are often arranged in whorls. The plant typically grows in freshwater habitats such as lakes, rivers, and ponds, where it forms dense underwater vegetation. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are usually borne in spike-like clusters, and it plays a role in providing habitat for aquatic organisms.

This variety is distinguished by its compact growth habit and its ability to thrive in nutrient-rich waters.
