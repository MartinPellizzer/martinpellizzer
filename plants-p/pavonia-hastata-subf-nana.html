Pavonia hastata subf. nana

Pavonia hastata subf.

nana, commonly known as the dwarf pavonia, is a compact, herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States. It features small, showy pink to red flowers that bloom in spikes during the summer months.

The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in a whorled pattern along the stems. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained, sandy soils.

Its compact size and ornamental flowers make it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.
