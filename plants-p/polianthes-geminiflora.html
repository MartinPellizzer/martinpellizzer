Polianthes geminiflora

Polianthes geminiflora, commonly known as the Mexican white lily, is a striking bulbous plant native to Mexico.

It features tall, slender stems that bear clusters of pure white, star-shaped flowers, which bloom in late summer to early autumn. The plant produces multiple flower buds at the base of each stem, giving it the species name "geminiflora," meaning "twinned flowers.". Its foliage consists of long, strap-like leaves that are often used in floral arrangements.

This plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking ornamental value.
