Peltogyne latifolia

Peltogyne latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf ironwood, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia and northern Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and sturdy, fibrous wood that is highly valued for construction and furniture-making. The plant has compound leaves with elongated, lance-shaped leaflets that provide shade and contribute to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Peltogyne latifolia plays an important ecological role by supporting diverse wildlife and contributing to soil stability in its native habitats.
