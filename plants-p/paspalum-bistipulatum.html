Paspalum bistipulatum

Paspalum bistipulatum, commonly known as the two-leaf grass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist or wetland environments, making it an important component of riparian ecosystems. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of spikelets, which are often arranged in a panicle-like structure.

Paspalum bistipulatum is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
