Pleurostachys graminifolia var. gracilis

Pleurostachys graminifolia var. gracilis, commonly known as the slender feathergrass, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its delicate, slender blades and fluffy, panicle-like inflorescences that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils along stream banks and wetlands. Its foliage is often used in floral arrangements due to its ornamental appeal.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small wildlife in its native environment.
