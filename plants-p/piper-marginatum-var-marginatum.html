Piper marginatum var. marginatum

Piper marginatum var. marginatum, commonly known as the creeping pepper plant, is a tropical vine native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and small, heart-shaped leaves with a waxy texture. The plant produces tiny, greenish-white flowers that develop into elliptical, red berries when mature. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory properties.

The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental appeal.
