Primula gemmifera subsp. monantha

Primula gemmifera subsp.

monantha, commonly known as the single-flowered primrose, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of China. It features basal rosettes of lanceolate, hairy leaves and produces solitary, showy flowers in shades of yellow to orange.

The flowers are typically borne on long, slender pedicels and have five petals with a distinctive, spurred lower petal. This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens and alpine settings.

It prefers cool, moist conditions and is adapted to high-altitude environments.
