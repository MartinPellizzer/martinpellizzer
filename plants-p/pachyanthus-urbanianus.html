Pachyanthus urbanianus

Pachyanthus urbanianus, commonly known as the Urbanian flame tree, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, fiery orange to red flowers that bloom in dense clusters during the rainy season. The plant has compound leaves composed of several oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces woody pods that contain flat, winged seeds. It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories or along riverbanks.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and as a landscape plant in warm climates.
