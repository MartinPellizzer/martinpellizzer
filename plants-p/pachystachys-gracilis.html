Pachystachys gracilis

Pachystachys gracilis, commonly known as the slender spike grass, is a perennial herb native to South America.

It features slender, upright stems that can grow up to 1.5 meters in height. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically blue or purple in color, arranged in dense spike-like inflorescences. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a slightly hairy texture.

Pachystachys gracilis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to well-drained, sunny environments.
