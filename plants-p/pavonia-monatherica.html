Pavonia monatherica

Pavonia monatherica, commonly known as the velvetleaf pavonia, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its showy, pink to red bracts that resemble flowers. The plant has palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides.

Pavonia monatherica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant foliage and unique floral structure.
