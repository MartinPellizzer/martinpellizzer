Pandanus papenooensis

Pandanus papenooensis, commonly known as the Papenooensis pandanus, is a tropical plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its tall, fibrous trunk and long, narrow leaves that are often used for thatching and weaving. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences with fragrant, yellowish flowers. Its roots are fibrous and can be used in traditional crafts.

The species is adapted to coastal and wetland environments, thriving in humid, saline conditions.
