Philoxerus humilis

Philoxerus humilis, commonly known as the Mexican bristlegrass, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its tufted growth habit. The plant features slender, wiry stems and small, spike-like inflorescences that produce tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Its leaves are narrow and often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Philoxerus humilis is adapted to arid environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.
