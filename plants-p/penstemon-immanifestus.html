Penstemon immanifestus

Penstemon immanifestus, commonly known as the blue penstemon, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, tubular blue to purple flowers. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It prefers well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and native plant landscapes.

Its foliage is lance-shaped and often covered in a fine, fuzzy texture, adding visual interest throughout the growing season.
