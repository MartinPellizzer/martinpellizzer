Plantago corvensis

Plantago corvensis, commonly known as the common plantain, is a perennial herb native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It features a rosette of broad, waxy, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces a tall, leafless flowering stem topped with a dense spike of small, greenish flowers. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found along roadsides, fields, and waste areas.

The plant is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally for its healing benefits.
