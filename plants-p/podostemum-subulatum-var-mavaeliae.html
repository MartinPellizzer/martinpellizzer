Podostemum subulatum var. mavaeliae

Podostemum subulatum var. mavaeliae, commonly known as the Mavaelia podostemum, is a rare aquatic plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, needle-like leaves that are adapted to grow on submerged rocks in fast-flowing streams. The plant produces tiny, delicate flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, and it is often found in clear, nutrient-rich waters. Its unique morphology allows it to thrive in highly specialized microhabitats.

Due to habitat destruction, this species is considered endangered and is of great interest to botanists and conservationists.
