Phlox philadelphica

Phlox philadelphica, commonly known as the annual phlox, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces showy clusters of pink to red flowers in the summer months. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy, and it thrives in full sun to partial shade. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly used in gardens and floral arrangements.

The species is also known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
