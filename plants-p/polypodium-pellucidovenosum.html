Polypodium pellucidovenosum

Polypodium pellucidovenosum, commonly known as the translucent-veined polypody, is a species of fern belonging to the family Polypodiaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, leathery fronds that exhibit a unique translucent appearance due to the thin, visible veins running through the leaf blades. This fern typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky crevices, where it thrives in humid conditions. Its rhizomes are often covered in a dense layer of rhizomorphs, which aid in nutrient absorption and water retention.

The plant's reproductive structures, including sori, are located on the underside of the fronds, contributing to its reproductive strategy in the understory of tropical and subtropical forests.
