Protea buekiana

Protea buekiana, commonly known as the Buekia protea, is a striking South African plant belonging to the Proteaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescences composed of numerous small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or red in color. The plant is native to the Western Cape region and thrives in well-drained, sandy soils. It is often found in fynbos vegetation, which is a unique biome known for its biodiversity.

Protea buekiana is valued not only for its ornamental appeal but also for its ecological importance in supporting local pollinators.
