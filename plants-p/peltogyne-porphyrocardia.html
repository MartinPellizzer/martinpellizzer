Peltogyne porphyrocardia

Peltogyne porphyrocardia, commonly known as the red-heart ironwood, is a member of the legume family Fabaceae.

It is a large, evergreen tree native to the tropical regions of Africa, particularly in countries like Nigeria and Cameroon. The tree is characterized by its thick, dark bark and dense, hard wood that is highly valued for its durability and strength. Its leaves are bipinnate, consisting of numerous small, oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces yellowish flowers followed by pod-like fruits.
