Pinguicula reichenbachiana

Pinguicula reichenbachiana, commonly known as the Mexican butterwort, is a carnivorous plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its rosette of fleshy, sticky leaves that trap and digest insects. The plant produces small, white, bell-shaped flowers during the growing season. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil.

Pinguicula reichenbachiana is a popular species among carnivorous plant enthusiasts due to its unique appearance and feeding habits.
