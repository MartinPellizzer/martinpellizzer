Prunus pseudocerasus f. humilis

Prunus pseudocerasus f. humilis, commonly known as the dwarf Chinese date or small-fruited loquat, is a compact, ornamental variety of the loquat plant.

It is characterized by its small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by edible yellow-orange fruits in late summer. The plant has smooth, dark green leaves and a compact growth habit, making it suitable for small gardens or container cultivation. It is native to China and thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil.

This cultivar is valued for its aesthetic appeal and mild, sweet fruit flavor.
