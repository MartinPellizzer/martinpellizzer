Piper marginatum var. catalpifolium

Piper marginatum var. catalpifolium, commonly known as the Catalpa-leaved pepper plant, is a unique variant of the creeping woodbine.

It is characterized by its distinctive foliage, which features broad, catalpa-like leaves with a heart-shaped base. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, typically in shades of white or pale yellow. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

This variant is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its aesthetic appeal.
