Pachycentria versteegii

Pachycentria versteegii, commonly known as the African giant rat-tail cactus, is a unique succulent native to South Africa.

It features a thick, columnar stem with a distinctive swollen base and a long, trailing root system. The plant produces clusters of small, yellow flowers during the summer months. Its leaves are modified into spines, and it has a fibrous, fleshy stem that stores water.

It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.
