Puccinellia coreensis

Puccinellia coreensis, commonly known as the Korean needlegrass, is a perennial grass native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist to wet habitats, such as marshes and wet meadows.

Its ecological importance includes stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
