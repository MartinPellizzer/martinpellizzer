Phaseolus minoomoo

Phaseolus minoomoo, commonly known as the Mexican bean or wild bean, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family.

It is native to Central America and is characterized by its climbing habit and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that develop into pods containing several seeds. Its botanical features include compound leaves and a fibrous root system typical of leguminous plants.

It is often found in moist, shaded environments and is valued for its potential in traditional agriculture.
