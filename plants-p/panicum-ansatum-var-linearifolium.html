Panicum ansatum var. linearifolium

Panicum ansatum var. linearifolium, commonly known as silky grass or silky millet, is a warm-season grass native to Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, linear leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically pale green to brown in color. It is often used as a forage crop and in erosion control due to its dense growth habit.

This variety is well adapted to dry climates and can thrive in poor soil conditions.
