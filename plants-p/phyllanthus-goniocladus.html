Phyllanthus goniocladus

Phyllanthus goniocladus, commonly known as the African date palm, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It is a small to medium-sized tree that can grow up to 10 meters tall, characterized by its pinnately compound leaves and clustered inflorescences. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by edible, fleshy fruits resembling dates. Its botanical features include a woody stem, fibrous roots, and a unique ability to produce both male and female flowers on the same plant.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in some African cultures.
