Psychrobatia pedata

Psychrobatia pedata, commonly known as the rock lily, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is a perennial herb with narrow, grass-like leaves and produces delicate, star-shaped flowers in shades of white and yellow. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats. Its blooming period typically occurs in late spring to early summer.

Psychrobatia pedata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
