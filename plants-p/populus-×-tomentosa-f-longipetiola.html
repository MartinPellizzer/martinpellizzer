Populus × tomentosa f. longipetiola

Populus × tomentosa f. longipetiola, commonly known as the Chinese poplar or longpetiolate poplar, is a unique hybrid species within the Populus genus.

It is characterized by its distinctive elongated petioles, which set it apart from other poplar varieties. The plant typically exhibits broad, heart-shaped leaves with a dense, woolly underside, contributing to its ornamental value. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to various soil types.

This variety is also noted for its rapid growth and ability to tolerate urban environments, making it a popular choice for landscaping.
