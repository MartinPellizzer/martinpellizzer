Physalis fauriei

Physalis fauriei, commonly known as the goldenberry or Inca berry, is a tropical shrub native to South America.

It produces small, orange-yellow fruits enclosed in a papery calyx, which dries to a reddish color. The plant is characterized by its hairy stems and broad, ovate leaves with serrated edges. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its edible fruits.

The fruit has a tart, slightly sweet flavor and is used in various culinary applications.
