Phaseolus vulgaris var. ceratonoides

Phaseolus vulgaris var. ceratonoides, commonly known as the scarlet runner bean, is a climbing legume native to the Americas.

It produces vibrant red or orange pods that are edible when young and tender. The plant has compound leaves with trifoliate arrangements and hairy stems. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink, and it thrives in warm, sunny climates.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and culinary use in various cuisines.
