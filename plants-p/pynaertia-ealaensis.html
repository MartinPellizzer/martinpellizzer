Pynaertia ealaensis

Pynaertia ealaensis, commonly known as the Ealaensis Pynaertia, is a rare flowering plant native to the tropical rainforests of Central America.

It is characterized by its striking, large, violet-pink petals that bloom during the rainy season. The plant has a short, woody stem with broad, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in a fine, silvery pubescence. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained, nutrient-rich soil.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered endangered and is protected in several conservation reserves.
