Pothuava pycnantha

Pothuava pycnantha, commonly known as the golden flame tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant produces small, woody fruits that contain several seeds. Its leaves are typically alternate, simple, and have a slightly serrated edge.

Pothuava pycnantha is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its striking floral display.
