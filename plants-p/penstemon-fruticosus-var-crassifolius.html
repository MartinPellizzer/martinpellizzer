Penstemon fruticosus var. crassifolius

Penstemon fruticosus var. crassifolius, commonly known as the thick-leaved penstemon, is a perennial plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its robust, woody stems and large, lance-shaped leaves that are thick and fleshy. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it a hardy choice for xeriscaping.

Its adaptability and ornamental value make it a popular plant in native gardens and dry landscapes.
