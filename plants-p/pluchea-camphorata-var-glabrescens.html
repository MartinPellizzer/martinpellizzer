Pluchea camphorata var. glabrescens

Pluchea camphorata var. glabrescens, commonly known as the glabrous camphorweed, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows as an annual or perennial herb with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small and yellow, arranged in clusters, and it produces small, oval-shaped fruits.

This species is known for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
