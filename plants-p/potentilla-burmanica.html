Potentilla burmanica

Potentilla burmanica, commonly known as Burman's cinquefoil, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its dense, sprawling growth habit. The plant features small, yellow flowers that bloom in summer, typically arranged in clusters along the stems. Its leaves are compound, with five to seven leaflets, and are covered in fine, soft hairs.

Potentilla burmanica is often found in rocky or alpine environments, where it thrives in well-drained soils.
