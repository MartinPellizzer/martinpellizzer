Piper fungiforme

Piper fungiforme, commonly known as the horned pepper, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many pepper species. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are followed by tiny, round berries. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

The plant is valued for its aromatic leaves and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
