Plectranthus strangulatus

Plectranthus strangulatus, commonly known as the African violet, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its velvety, dark green leaves and clusters of tubular, violet-blue flowers. The plant thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening. It is a member of the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and ornamental plants.

Due to its compact growth habit and attractive foliage, it is often used as a decorative plant in both indoor and outdoor settings.
