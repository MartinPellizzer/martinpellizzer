Potamogeton gramineus f. myriophyllus

Potamogeton gramineus f. myriophyllus, commonly known as the broad-leaf pondweed, is a aquatic plant characterized by its elongated, ribbon-like leaves that are typically broad and flat.

It is a submerged or floating-leaved perennial that thrives in freshwater environments such as lakes, ponds, and slow-moving streams. The plant has a robust root system that helps stabilize sediments and improve water quality. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, usually appearing in clusters, and it reproduces both sexually and vegetatively.

The species is often found in shallow waters with soft, muddy substrates.
