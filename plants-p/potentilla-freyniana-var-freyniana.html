Potentilla freyniana var. freyniana

Potentilla freyniana var. freyniana, commonly known as Freyn's cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its hairy stems and small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The leaves are typically divided into five oval-shaped lobes, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or arid environments.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardiness and drought tolerance.
