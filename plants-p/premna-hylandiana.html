Premna hylandiana

Premna hylandiana, commonly known as the silver vine, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves that are covered in fine, silvery hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by small, round berries. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Premna hylandiana is also known for its attractive foliage and is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant.
