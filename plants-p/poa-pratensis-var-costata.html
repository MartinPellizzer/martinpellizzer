Poa pratensis var. costata

Poa pratensis var. costata, commonly known as the ribbed meadowgrass, is a variant of the common meadowgrass species.

It is characterized by its distinctive ribbed or ridged leaf blades, which give it a unique appearance. This plant typically grows in moist, temperate regions and is often found in meadows and grasslands. It has a clump-forming growth habit and produces dense, tufted inflorescences.

The botanical features of Poa pratensis var. costata make it both ecologically and aesthetically significant in its native habitats.
