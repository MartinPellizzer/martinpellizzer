Penstemon jamesii subsp. typicus

Penstemon jamesii subsp.

typicus, commonly known as the bluecurls, is a perennial herb native to the western United States. It is characterized by its striking blue to violet tubular flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in mountainous regions. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces seed capsules that release small, wind-dispersed seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is adapted to dry, sunny conditions.
