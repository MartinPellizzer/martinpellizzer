Platystachys heptantha

Platystachys heptantha, commonly known as the seven-lobed flame bamboo, is a unique member of the grass family (Poaceae).

It is characterized by its striking, fiery red or orange inflorescences that resemble flames, making it a visually striking plant. Native to China, it thrives in temperate climates and prefers well-drained soil. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that bloom in late summer.

It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its vibrant color and distinctive appearance.
