Potentilla adscharica var. gymnosepala

Potentilla adscharica var. gymnosepala, commonly known as the Alaskan potentilla, is a flowering plant native to the northern regions of North America.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit and produces small, yellowish-white flowers in clusters. The plant has alternate, ovate leaves with hairy surfaces and is adapted to cold, dry climates. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and subarctic environments.

This variant is distinguished by its unique floral structure and is valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture.
