Primulina bullata

Primulina bullata, commonly known as the Bullata Caladium, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves that are often adorned with intricate patterns of white, red, and green. The plant belongs to the Araceae family and is typically grown as a decorative foliage plant. Its leaves are usually variegated, with a waxy texture and a glossy appearance.

Primulina bullata thrives in humid, shaded environments and is popular in indoor gardening for its ornamental value.
