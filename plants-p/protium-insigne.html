Protium insigne

Protium insigne, commonly known as the golden apple or Indian apple, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Sapotaceae and is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and large, glossy, elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by spherical, yellow fruits with a sweet, aromatic pulp. The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and potential economic value in agroforestry systems.
