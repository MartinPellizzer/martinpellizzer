Pygeum wightianum

Pygeum wightianum, commonly known as the Indian sumac, is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub native to the western Ghats of India.

It belongs to the Anacardiaceae family, which includes other plants like the cashew and poison ivy. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves, which are usually alternate and have a serrated margin. Its small, yellowish-green flowers are followed by red, fleshy fruits that contain several seeds.

Pygeum wightianum is often found in moist, shaded areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional medicinal uses.
