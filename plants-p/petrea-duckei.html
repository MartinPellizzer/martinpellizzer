Petrea duckei

Petrea duckei, commonly known as the golden trumpet tree, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, pendulous yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are opposite on the stems, and it produces small, woody fruits containing seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in disturbed habitats or along riverbanks.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscape design.
