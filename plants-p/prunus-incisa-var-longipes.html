Prunus incisa var. longipes

Prunus incisa var. longipes, commonly known as the long-peduncled Japanese apricot, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its slender, elongated peduncles that bear clusters of white or pink flowers in early spring. The leaves are ovate to elliptic, with a smooth texture and a slightly serrated margin. The fruit is a small, yellowish drupe that ripens in late summer.

This species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental flowers and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
