Portulaca granulatostellulata

Portulaca granulatostellulata, commonly known as the star-studded portulaca, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of South Africa.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy stems and leaves that are often covered in a fine, granular texture. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, typically blooming in the morning. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a hardy and drought-tolerant species.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is often cultivated in gardens and rockeries.
