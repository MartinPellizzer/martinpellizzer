Phylica obtusifolia

Phylica obtusifolia, commonly known as the Natal flame bush, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of bright red, flame-like flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, woody fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Phylica obtusifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
