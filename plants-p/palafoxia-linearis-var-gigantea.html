Palafoxia linearis var. gigantea

Palafoxia linearis var. gigantea, commonly known as the giant palmetto, is a distinctive plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial herb with long, narrow, grass-like leaves that can grow up to several feet in length. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in panicles, which are typically found at the base of the leaf blades. It thrives in wet, sandy soils and is often found in marshy areas or along the edges of swamps.

Its unique appearance and adaptability make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
