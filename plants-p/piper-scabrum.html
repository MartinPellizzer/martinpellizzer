Piper scabrum

Piper scabrum, commonly known as rough pepper or scabrous pepper, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes numerous species of pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and small, heart-shaped leaves with a rough, textured surface. Its flowers are unisexual, with male and female flowers occurring on separate plants, and it produces small, dark blue to black berries.

The plant is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
