Potentilla recta f. dichasialis

Potentilla recta f. dichasialis, commonly known as the dichasial potentilla, is a flowering plant belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine regions. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and usually have a slightly serrated edge.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and attractive appearance.
