Phragmipedium × roethianum

Phragmipedium × roethianum, commonly known as the hybrid orchid, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Phragmipedium kovachii and Phragmipedium sanderianum.

It is a terrestrial orchid native to the cloud forests of Central and South America. The plant features tall, erect stems with broad, elliptical leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine, white pubescence. Its flowers are showy, with a distinctive lip that is usually yellow or orange, and it produces a fragrant scent to attract pollinators.

This species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and ease of cultivation under proper conditions.
