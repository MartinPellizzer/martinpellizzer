Polygala angustifolia f. ramosa

Polygala angustifolia f. ramosa, commonly known as the slender milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to regions of China and Korea.

It is characterized by its slender, often branched stems and small, greenish-white flowers that appear in clusters. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to arid conditions. Its leaves are narrow and linear, contributing to its overall delicate appearance.

It is valued in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
