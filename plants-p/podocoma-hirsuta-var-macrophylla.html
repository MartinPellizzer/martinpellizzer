Podocoma hirsuta var. macrophylla

Podocoma hirsuta var. macrophylla, commonly known as the giant hairless ice plant, is a succulent species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, thick leaves that store water, adapted to arid environments. The plant produces clusters of small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow and orange during the warmer months. Its stems are often branched and can grow quite long, reaching up to several feet in height.

It is a hardy, low-maintenance plant suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
