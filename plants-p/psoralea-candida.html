Psoralea candida

Psoralea candida, commonly known as the white psoralea, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, yellow flowers and trifoliate leaves. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and thrives in full sun. It is often found in coastal areas and is adapted to arid conditions.

Psoralea candida is also known for its small, kidney-shaped seeds that are used in traditional medicine.
