Prunus germanica

Prunus germanica, commonly known as the German cherry or Prunus domestica subsp.

germanica, is a species of small to medium-sized deciduous tree native to Central Europe. It is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and oval-shaped leaves that turn yellow in autumn.

The plant produces small, white flowers in spring, followed by edible, sweet-tart cherries in summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit.

Prunus germanica is also valued in traditional medicine for its various health benefits.
