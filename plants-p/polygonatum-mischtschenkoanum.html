Polygonatum mischtschenkoanum

Polygonatum mischtschenkoanum, commonly known as the Siberian Solomon's Seal, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the forests of eastern Asia.

It features a rhizomatous root system and lance-shaped leaves that are arranged in a basal rosette. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers in early summer, followed by berries that mature to a dark purple color. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in woodlands and mountainous regions.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
