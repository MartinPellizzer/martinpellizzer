Pycreus fluminalis

Pycreus fluminalis, commonly known as the river pycreus, is a rare aquatic plant native to certain freshwater ecosystems.

It typically grows in shallow, flowing waters where it can anchor itself to submerged substrates. The plant features long, ribbon-like leaves that float on the water's surface, aiding in photosynthesis. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, often blooming in clusters near the water's edge.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered vulnerable to environmental changes and habitat loss.
