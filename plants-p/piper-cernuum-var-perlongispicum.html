Piper cernuum var. perlongispicum

Piper cernuum var. perlongispicum, commonly known as the long-spined pepper vine, is a climbing perennial shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its long, slender stems and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. The plant produces small, elongated berries that are typically green when unripe and turn red when mature. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
