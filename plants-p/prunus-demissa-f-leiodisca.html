Prunus demissa f. leiodisca

Prunus demissa f. leiodisca, commonly known as the smooth-leaved cherry, is a botanical variant of the Prunus demissa species.

It is characterized by its smooth, glossy leaves that are typically smaller than those of the typical form. The plant produces small, white to pink flowers in spring, followed by edible, sweet berries in summer. It is native to parts of Asia and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

This form is valued in horticulture for its compact growth habit and attractive foliage.
