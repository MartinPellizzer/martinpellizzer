Potentilla toyamensis

Potentilla toyamensis, commonly known as Toyama cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, five-petaled flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodland areas and stream banks. Its leaves are palmately divided, and it produces small, berry-like fruits that mature in the fall.

Potentilla toyamensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance in its native habitat.
