Parodia mairanana var. atra

Parodia mairanana var. atra, commonly known as the Black Parodia, is a unique variant of the Parodia genus native to the arid regions of South America.

This succulent is characterized by its dark, almost black, spiny stems that contrast sharply with its pale, woolly texture. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the warmer months, adding a striking visual contrast to its dark foliage. It is a compact, slow-growing cactus that thrives in well-drained soil and full sun.

Due to its distinctive appearance, it is a popular choice among collectors and enthusiasts of rare succulents.
