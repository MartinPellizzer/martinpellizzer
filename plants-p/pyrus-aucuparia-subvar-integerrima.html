Pyrus aucuparia subvar. integerrima

Pyrus aucuparia subvar. integerrima, commonly known as the European whitebeam, is a deciduous tree native to Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The leaves are typically oval-shaped with a rough texture and a slightly serrated margin. The fruit is a small, edible pome that turns from green to yellow or red as it matures.

This subvariety is often found in woodlands and is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance.
