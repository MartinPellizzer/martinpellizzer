Phlomis umbrosa var. typica

Phlomis umbrosa var. typica, commonly known as the shadow phlomis, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows in shaded or semi-shaded environments, such as woodland edges or rocky slopes. The plant features upright, branching stems that are often covered in soft, grayish-green leaves. Its showy, yellow flower clusters appear in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

It is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in partial shade.
