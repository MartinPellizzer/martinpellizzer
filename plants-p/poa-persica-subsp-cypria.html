Poa persica subsp. cypria

Poa persica subsp.

cypria, commonly known as the Cyprian Persian grass, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Cyprus. It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and fine, blue-green foliage.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, two-lobed florets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or limestone habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
