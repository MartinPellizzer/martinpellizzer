Philadelphus aromaticus

Philadelphus aromaticus, commonly known as the mock orange, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has opposite, simple, and ovate leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a versatile garden plant.

Philadelphus aromaticus is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
