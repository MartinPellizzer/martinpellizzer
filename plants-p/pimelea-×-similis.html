Pimelea × similis

Pimelea × similis, commonly known as the silky pimelea, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Australia.

It is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Pimelea prostrata and Pimelea rigida. The plant is characterized by its prostrate growth habit and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. Its leaves are narrow and covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and heathland environments.
