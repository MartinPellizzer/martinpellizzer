Plantago richardsonii

Plantago richardsonii, commonly known as Richardson's plantain, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a member of the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its rosette of broad, waxy leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in a spike, which bloom in the summer months. It thrives in dry, disturbed soils and is often found in prairies and along roadsides.

Richardson's plantain is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally for its healing benefits.
