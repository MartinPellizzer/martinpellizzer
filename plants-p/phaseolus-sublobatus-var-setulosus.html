Phaseolus sublobatus var. setulosus

Phaseolus sublobatus var. setulosus, commonly known as the hairy runner bean, is a climbing legume native to South America.

It features compound leaves with serrated edges and small, hairy stipules. The plant produces elongated, hairy pods that contain edible seeds, which are often used in traditional cuisines. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Its botanical significance lies in its role as a genetic resource for bean breeding programs.
