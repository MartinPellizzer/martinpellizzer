Psidium ovatifolium var. glabrum

Psidium ovatifolium var. glabrum, commonly known as the smooth-leaved guava, is a species native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its ovate, smooth, and glossy leaves that are typically dark green in color. The plant produces small, white flowers that develop into edible berries, which are similar in appearance to common guavas but have a distinct flavor. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential fruit production in suitable environments.
