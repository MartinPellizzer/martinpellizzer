Pleurothallis oricola

Pleurothallis oricola, commonly known as the orchid of the Andes, is a species native to the cloud forests of South America.

It is a small epiphytic orchid that thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant features delicate, narrow leaves and produces fragrant, small flowers in shades of white and pale pink. It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is known for its unique floral structure.

Due to its habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
