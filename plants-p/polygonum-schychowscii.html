Polygonum schychowscii

Polygonum schychowscii, commonly known as the Schychowksi knotweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to China.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, which is characterized by distinctive nodes along its stems. The plant features palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, and it produces small, clustered flowers in late summer. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Its ornamental value has led to its cultivation in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
