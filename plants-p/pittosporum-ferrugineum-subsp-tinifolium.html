Pittosporum ferrugineum subsp. tinifolium

Pittosporum ferrugineum subsp.

tinifolium, commonly known as the golden pittosporum, is a shrub native to New Zealand. It features glossy, dark green leaves that are elliptical in shape and often have a slight reddish tinge when young.

The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring, followed by orange-red berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

This subspecies is relatively hardy and adaptable to a range of environmental conditions.
