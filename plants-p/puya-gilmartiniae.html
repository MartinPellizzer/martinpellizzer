Puya gilmartiniae

Puya gilmartiniae, commonly known as the Gilmartin's torch, is a striking member of the Bromeliaceae family native to the Andean highlands of South America.

This plant is characterized by its tall, spire-like inflorescence that can reach up to 3 meters in height, adorned with vibrant red and orange bracts. The leaves are long, narrow, and arranged in a rosette formation, forming a water-holding structure typical of bromeliads. It thrives in cool, humid environments and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Puya gilmartiniae is notable for its longevity, often living for several decades before flowering and dying.
