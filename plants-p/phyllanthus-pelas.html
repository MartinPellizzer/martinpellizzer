Phyllanthus pelas

Phyllanthus pelas, commonly known as the African spurge, is a shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It features small, green, egg-shaped leaves that are often clustered at the base of the plant. The plant produces yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by small, round fruits containing seeds. It is characterized by its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions and its resistance to drought.

The plant has been used in traditional medicine for various ailments, though its botanical properties remain under scientific study.
