Plantago lanceolata var. lacustris

Plantago lanceolata var. lacustris, commonly known as the lake plantain, is a variant of the common plantain.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its lance-shaped leaves that are often slightly hairy. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers in dense spikes, which are typically borne on long, slender stalks. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wet meadows, marshes, and along the edges of lakes.

This variety is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
