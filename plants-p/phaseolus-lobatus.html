Phaseolus lobatus

Phaseolus lobatus, commonly known as the scarlet runner bean, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its vibrant red or purple flowers and edible pods. The plant typically grows as a climbing vine, reaching heights of up to 3 meters, with trifoliate leaves. Its seeds, which are enclosed within the pods, are often used in cooking and are a good source of protein.

The plant also has a symbiotic relationship with nitrogen-fixing bacteria, enhancing soil fertility.
