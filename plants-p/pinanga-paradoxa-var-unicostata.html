Pinanga paradoxa var. unicostata

Pinanga paradoxa var. unicostata, commonly known as the single-costate bamboo palm, is a unique variant of the bamboo palm native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and arching, feather-like leaves that give it a gracefully elegant appearance. The plant has a fibrous root system that helps it anchor in moist, well-drained soils. Its leaves are typically a deep green, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by berries.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to indoor environments.
