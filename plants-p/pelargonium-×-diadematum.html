Pelargonium × diadematum

Pelargonium × diadematum, commonly known as the diadem geranium, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its ornamental foliage, featuring deep green leaves with silver-white veins and a velvety texture. The plant produces clusters of small, five-petaled flowers in shades of pink, red, and white, typically blooming from spring to autumn. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and containers.

Its compact growth habit and vibrant blooms make it a versatile addition to both indoor and outdoor landscapes.
