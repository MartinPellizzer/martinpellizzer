Priva lamiifolia

Priva lamiifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf privet, is a shrub or small tree native to parts of Asia.

It features opposite, simple, and usually ovate or elliptic leaves that are glossy and dark green on top. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers in clusters, which are followed by small, spherical berries. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and adaptability to various soil types.

However, it can become invasive in certain regions if not managed properly.
