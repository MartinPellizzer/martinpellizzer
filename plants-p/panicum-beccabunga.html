Panicum beccabunga

Panicum beccabunga, commonly known as watergrass, is a perennial grass native to wetland environments.

It thrives in shallow water and moist soils, often found in marshes, ponds, and along the edges of lakes. The plant has slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense mats, which can help stabilize soil and prevent erosion.

Watergrass is also an important food source for various aquatic animals and birds.
