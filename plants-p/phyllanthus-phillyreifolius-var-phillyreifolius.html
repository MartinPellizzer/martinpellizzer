Phyllanthus phillyreifolius var. phillyreifolius

Phyllanthus phillyreifolius var. phillyreifolius, commonly known as the broadleaf phyllanthus, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It features opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, followed by elliptical, red or orange berries. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical characteristics make it a subject of interest in both horticulture and ecological studies.
