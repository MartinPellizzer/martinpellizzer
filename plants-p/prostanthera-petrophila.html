Prostanthera petrophila

Prostanthera petrophila, commonly known as the granite betony, is a flowering plant native to southern Australia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. This species is characterized by its small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to violet, typically arranged in dense clusters. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and inland regions.

Its leaves are fragrant and have a mint-like scent, contributing to its use in traditional herbal medicine.
