Piper longipedicellatum

Piper longipedicellatum, commonly known as the long-stalked pepper vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes various pepper species. The plant is characterized by its slender, woody vines and opposite, ovate leaves with slightly serrated edges. Its small, greenish-yellow flowers are followed by berries that mature to a dark purple color.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
