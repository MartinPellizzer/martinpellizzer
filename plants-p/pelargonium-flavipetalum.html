Pelargonium flavipetalum

Pelargonium flavipetalum, commonly known as the yellow-petaled geranium, is a species native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers, which are typically tubular and clustered in dense inflorescences. The leaves are usually rounded with a slightly hairy texture, and the plant tends to grow in a compact, bushy form. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its vibrant floral display.
