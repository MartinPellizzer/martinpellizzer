Prunus gongshanensis

Prunus gongshanensis, commonly known as the Gongshan cherry, is a rare and endemic species native to China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its small, red berries that are typically consumed by birds. The plant is known for its ornamental value due to its attractive pinkish-white flowers that bloom in spring. It thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil and partial sunlight.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a conservation concern.
