Paspalum taphrophyllum

Paspalum taphrophyllum, commonly known as the dwarf paspalum, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It typically grows in wetland environments, such as marshes and along the edges of water bodies. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It is characterized by its low-growing habit and ability to thrive in nutrient-poor soils.

Due to its hardiness, it is sometimes used in restoration projects for stabilizing soil in wetland areas.
