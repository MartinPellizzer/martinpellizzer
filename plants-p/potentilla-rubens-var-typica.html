Potentilla rubens var. typica

Potentilla rubens var. typica, commonly known as the red cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its bright red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It has palmately divided leaves and produces small, yellowish-green fruits.

This species is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
