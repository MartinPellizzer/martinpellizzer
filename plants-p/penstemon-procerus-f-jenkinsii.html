Penstemon procerus f. jenkinsii

Penstemon procerus f. jenkinsii, commonly known as the Jenkins' tall beardtongue, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, tubular flowers. The plant typically produces spikes of violet to pinkish-purple blooms that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance in garden settings.
