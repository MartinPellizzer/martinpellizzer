Pternandra coerulescens

Pternandra coerulescens, commonly known as the blue ghost plant, is a striking evergreen shrub native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its unique, waxy, blue-green leaves that are often tinged with a bluish hue, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators. It thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Due to its ornamental value and ease of care, it is widely cultivated in tropical and subtropical regions.
