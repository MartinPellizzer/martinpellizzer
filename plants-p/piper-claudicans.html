Piper claudicans

Piper claudicans, commonly known as the climbing pepper or wild pepper, is a tropical vine belonging to the Piperaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its small, greenish-yellow flowers and elongated, narrow leaves. The plant produces berries that are typically green when immature and turn red when mature, containing a single seed. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its aromatic leaves and fruits, it is sometimes used in traditional medicine and as a spice in local cuisines.
