Pisum sativum subvar. limitatum

Pisum sativum subvar. limitatum, commonly known as the dwarf pea, is a botanical variant of the common pea.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and shorter stems compared to the standard pea variety. The plant produces small, often wrinkled pods that contain fewer seeds. It is typically cultivated for its edible seeds and is well-suited for container gardening.

This subvariety is valued for its adaptability to various growing conditions and its use in both culinary and ornamental settings.
