Passerina rigida

Passerina rigida, commonly known as the rigid pasqueflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is a member of the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, early spring blooms. The plant has a short, hairy stem with palmately divided leaves that are typically bright green. Its flowers are usually pink or white, with five petals and numerous stamens.

Passerina rigida thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.
