Phyllolobium lasaense

Phyllolobium lasaense, commonly known as the Lasa leaf plant, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly found in regions of Argentina and Chile.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant features compound leaves with a distinctive lobed shape, and its flowers are typically small and clustered in inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological role, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and restoration projects.
