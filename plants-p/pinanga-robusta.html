Pinanga robusta

Pinanga robusta, commonly known as the giant bamboo or giant rattan, is a member of the Arecaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can grow up to 20 meters in height, forming a dense canopy. The plant produces large, feather-like leaves that are often used in traditional crafts and as roofing materials. Its roots and stems are valued for their strength and flexibility, making them useful in construction and furniture-making.

Pinanga robusta plays an important ecological role in its native habitats by providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
