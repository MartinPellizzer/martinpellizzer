Primulina latinervis

Primulina latinervis, commonly known as the silver stripe primula, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Gesneriaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves with distinctive silver or white veins. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, typically blooming during the summer months. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value, it is a popular choice for indoor gardening and shaded outdoor landscapes.
