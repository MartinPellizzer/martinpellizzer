Pyrus pyraster f. populifolia

Pyrus pyraster f. populifolia, commonly known as the Poplar-leaved Crabapple, is a horticultural form of the common crabapple.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that resemble those of poplar trees, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in spring, followed by edible fruits that are often used in jams and jellies. It thrives in temperate climates and is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.

This variety is relatively hardy and adaptable, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
