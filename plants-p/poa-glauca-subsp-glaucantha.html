Poa glauca subsp. glaucantha

Poa glauca subsp.

glaucantha, commonly known as the bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the northern hemisphere. It is characterized by its fine, blue-green foliage and compact growth habit.

The plant typically forms dense, low-growing turf and is often found in moist, well-drained soils. Its flowering stems bear small, spike-like inflorescences with pale blue to purple flowers.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native plant gardens.
