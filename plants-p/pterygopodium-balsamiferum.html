Pterygopodium balsamiferum

Pterygopodium balsamiferum, commonly known as the balsamroot, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its tall, woody stem and large, showy yellow flowers. The plant produces a sticky, aromatic resin that gives it its common name. Its leaves are deeply lobed and covered in a fine, white pubescence.

Pterygopodium balsamiferum is often found in dry, open habitats and is adapted to survive in arid conditions.
