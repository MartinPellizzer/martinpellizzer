Passiflora foetida var. salvadorensis

Passiflora foetida var. salvadorensis, commonly known as the Salvadoran passionflower, is a climbing vine native to Central America.

It is characterized by its striking, complex flowers that feature a mix of red, blue, and white petals, along with a crown of filaments. The plant produces edible berries that are often used in local cuisine and traditional medicine. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a rough texture, while its stems are woody and covered in fine hairs.

This species thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in humid, shaded environments.
