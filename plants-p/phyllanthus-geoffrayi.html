Phyllanthus geoffrayi

Phyllanthus geoffrayi, commonly known as Geoffray's spurge, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree with smooth, often reddish bark and alternate, simple, elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, followed by spherical, fleshy fruits. It is adapted to grow in dry, well-drained soils and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.

The plant contains various phytochemical compounds and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
