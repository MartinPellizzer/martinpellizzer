Panicum melananthum

Panicum melananthum, commonly known as black panicgrass, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is a member of the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its dark-colored inflorescence, which gives it its common name. The plant typically grows in open areas such as fields and roadsides, thriving in well-drained soils. Its small, spike-like clusters of flowers are often wind-pollinated, and it produces small, dry seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Black panicgrass is often considered a weed in agricultural settings due to its rapid growth and adaptability.
