Phyllanthus fimbriatus

Phyllanthus fimbriatus, commonly known as the Indian cheesewood, is a shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its small, clustered flowers and distinctive, fimbriated (fringed) leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories. Its leaves are used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits, and the plant has a compact, bushy growth habit.

The fruit is a small, three-lobed capsule that contains several seeds.
