Phlox drummondii var. typica

Phlox drummondii var. typica, commonly known as the common garden phlox, is a flowering plant native to the central and southern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of 12 to 24 inches, featuring showy, clustered flowers in shades of pink, red, or white. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs, and its stems are square in cross-section.

It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardeners due to its vibrant blooms and ease of cultivation.
