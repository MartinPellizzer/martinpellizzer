Pseuderanthemum carruthersii var. atropurpureum

Pseuderanthemum carruthersii var. atropurpureum, commonly known as the purple-flowered pseuderanthemum, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and striking, tubular flowers that range in color from purple to violet. The leaves are typically opposite, ovate, and have a glossy surface with a slightly serrated edge. This species thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to bloom throughout the year under proper care.
