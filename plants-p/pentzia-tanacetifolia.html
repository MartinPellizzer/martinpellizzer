Pentzia tanacetifolia

Pentzia tanacetifolia, commonly known as the Cape dandelion, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellow composite flowers and linear, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in coastal and sandy environments, thriving in well-drained soils. It produces small, oval-shaped fruits that contain seeds dispersed by wind.

Pentzia tanacetifolia is often used in coastal restoration projects due to its hardy nature and ability to stabilize sand dunes.
