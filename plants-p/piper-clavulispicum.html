Piper clavulispicum

Piper clavulispicum, commonly known as the Clavulispic pepper vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes numerous species of pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its slender, woody stems and heart-shaped leaves with a glossy surface. Its small, greenish-white flowers are followed by elongated, spiky fruits that contain several seeds.

Due to its unique morphology and potential medicinal properties, it is of interest to botanists and herbalists alike.
