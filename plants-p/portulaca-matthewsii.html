Portulaca matthewsii

Portulaca matthewsii, commonly known as the Matthew's moss rose, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is a low-growing, succulent species that thrives in hot, dry climates with minimal water requirements. The plant produces vibrant, showy flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, which bloom throughout the summer months. Its fleshy leaves are adapted to store water, allowing it to survive in harsh, desert-like conditions.

Portulaca matthewsii is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its drought tolerance and attractive floral display.
