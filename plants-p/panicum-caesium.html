Panicum caesium

Panicum caesium, commonly known as purpletop millet, is a warm-season grass native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, erect growth and distinctive purple-tinted foliage, which becomes more pronounced as the plant matures. The plant produces fluffy, light green to golden brown seed heads that are often used in ornamental landscaping. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it a drought-tolerant choice for gardens and restoration projects.

Its rapid growth and ability to suppress weeds make it a valuable plant for erosion control and wildlife habitat.
