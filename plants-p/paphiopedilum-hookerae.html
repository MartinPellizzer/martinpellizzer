Paphiopedilum hookerae

Paphiopedilum hookerae, commonly known as the Hooker's lady's slipper, is a rare and highly sought-after orchid native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, showy flowers with a unique slipper-shaped pouch that resembles a lady's shoe, a feature typical of the orchid genus Paphiopedilum. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are usually pale pink to white with contrasting green and red markings, and it blooms during the late spring or early summer.

Due to its rarity and beauty, Paphiopedilum hookerae is often cultivated in greenhouses and is protected in its natural habitat.
