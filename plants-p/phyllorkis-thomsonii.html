Phyllorkis thomsonii

Phyllorkis thomsonii, commonly known as Thomson's mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on the branches of eucalyptus trees in Australia.

It has small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a fine, white waxy coating. The plant produces small, white or pale green flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its berries are usually red or orange and contain sticky seeds that adhere to the bark of host trees.

This species plays an important ecological role by providing food for birds and other animals.
