Ptilagrostis macrospicula

Ptilagrostis macrospicula, commonly known as the giant feather grass, is a grass species native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and long, feathery plumes that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are densely packed with tiny, spikelet-based flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands and along riverbanks.

Its botanical features make it an important component of its native ecosystem.
