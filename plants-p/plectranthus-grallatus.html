Plectranthus grallatus

Plectranthus grallatus, commonly known as the silver bush or velvetleaf, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring dense, silvery-gray foliage. The leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine, soft hairs that give it a distinctive velvety appearance. It produces small, tubular flowers in clusters, usually in shades of purple or blue, which bloom from late winter to early spring.

This plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
