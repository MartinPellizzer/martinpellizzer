Pseudopteris ankaranensis

Pseudopteris ankaranensis, commonly known as the Ankaran fern, is a rare species native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the family Pteridaceae and is characterized by its distinctive fronds with a bipinnate structure. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often in forest understories. Its leaves are dimorphic, with fertile and sterile fronds differing in shape and size.

The species is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and limited distribution.
