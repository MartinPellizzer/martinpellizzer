Plantago leptophylla

Plantago leptophylla, commonly known as narrow-leaf plantain, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It features upright, hairy stems that bear narrow, lance-shaped leaves with prominent veins. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in dense, spike-like clusters during the summer months. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

Botanically, it belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette growth habit.
