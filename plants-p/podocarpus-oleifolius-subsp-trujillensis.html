Podocarpus oleifolius subsp. trujillensis

Podocarpus oleifolius subsp.

trujillensis, commonly known as the Trujillense Podocarpus, is a evergreen tree native to parts of Central America. It belongs to the Podocarpaceae family and is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and conical shape.

The leaves are typically lanceolate and have a leathery texture, while the plant produces small, spherical cones. This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping due to its adaptability and resistance to urban conditions.

It also plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
