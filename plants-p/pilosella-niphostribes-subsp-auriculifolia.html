Pilosella niphostribes subsp. auriculifolia

Pilosella niphostribes subsp.

auriculifolia, commonly known as the auriculate pilosella, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family. It is characterized by its small, daisy-like flowers with yellow disc florets and white to pale yellow ray florets.

The leaves are typically basal, with a distinctive auriculate (ear-shaped) base, giving the plant its common name. It is native to regions of Central and South America, where it thrives in open, sunny habitats.

This species is often found in disturbed soils and is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its delicate appearance.
