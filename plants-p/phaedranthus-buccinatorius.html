Phaedranthus buccinatorius

Phaedranthus buccinatorius, commonly known as the hooded bellflower, is a striking perennial plant native to South Africa.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically violet or blue in color, with a distinctive hood-like structure over the petals. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are arranged in a basal rosette, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its unique floral morphology and vibrant colors make it a notable addition to botanical collections.
