Physodium

Physodium, commonly known as the common physodium, is a herbaceous plant native to temperate regions.

It typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring upright stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually white or pale yellow, blooming in the summer months. Its root system is fibrous, and it thrives in well-drained soil.

Physodium is often found in meadows and along the edges of woodlands.
