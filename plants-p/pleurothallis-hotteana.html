Pleurothallis hotteana

Pleurothallis hotteana, commonly known as the Hotte's orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Peru and Ecuador.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its small, intricate flowers that often display a range of colors including white, pink, and purple. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its botanical features include slender stems, lance-shaped leaves, and a unique structure of the floral bracts that aid in pollination.

This orchid is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by enthusiasts for its delicate beauty.
