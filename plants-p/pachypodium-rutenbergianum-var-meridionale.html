Pachypodium rutenbergianum var. meridionale

Pachypodium rutenbergianum var. meridionale, commonly known as the Southern Madagascar moon cactus, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of southern Madagascar.

It is characterized by its thick, ribbed stems that store water, allowing it to survive in dry conditions. The plant produces striking, star-shaped flowers that are typically white or yellow in color. Its leaves are usually small and spiny, providing some protection from herbivores.

This variety is well-suited for cultivation in dry climates with minimal water requirements.
