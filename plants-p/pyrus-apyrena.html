Pyrus apyrena

Pyrus apyrena, commonly known as the Kashmir pear, is a deciduous tree native to the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its ornamental value due to its attractive foliage and showy white flowers. The tree produces small, sweet, and aromatic fruits that are typically eaten fresh or used in jams. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Pyrus apyrena is also valued for its ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
