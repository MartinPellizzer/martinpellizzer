Psydrax cudalorensis

Psydrax cudalorensis, commonly known as the Cudalore starfruit, is a flowering plant native to the Western Ghats of India.

It is characterized by its small, star-shaped yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically yellow when ripe, with a sweet and tangy flavor. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in forested areas.

Due to its ornamental value and unique fruit, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
