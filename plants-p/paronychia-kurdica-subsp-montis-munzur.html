Paronychia kurdica subsp. montis-munzur

Paronychia kurdica subsp.

montis-munzur, commonly known as the Munzur mountain saxifrage, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Turkey. It typically grows in rocky and alpine environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions.

The plant features basal leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of white hairs, which help reduce water loss. Its small, yellowish flowers are arranged in dense clusters, typical of the Caryophyllaceae family.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance in its native habitat.
