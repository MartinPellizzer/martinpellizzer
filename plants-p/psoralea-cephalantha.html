Psoralea cephalantha

Psoralea cephalantha, commonly known as the pink burr legume, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of small, pinkish to purplish flowers. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and is often found in dry, rocky habitats. Its fruit is a hairy, pod-like structure that contains several seeds.

Psoralea cephalantha is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
