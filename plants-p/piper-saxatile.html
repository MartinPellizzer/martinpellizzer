Piper saxatile

Piper saxatile, commonly known as the rock pepper or saxatile pepper, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many species of pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its climbing habit and has small, opposite leaves that are usually ovate to lanceolate in shape. It produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in spikes, and its fruits are berries that turn red when mature.

The plant is often found growing in rocky or disturbed habitats, and it is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
