Protea nubigena

Protea nubigena, commonly known as the cloud protea, is a striking South African plant belonging to the Proteaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescence composed of numerous white to pinkish bracts that resemble petals. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils in the Western Cape region of South Africa. Its flowers bloom in late winter to early spring, attracting pollinators such as birds and insects.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is a valued species in both horticulture and conservation efforts.
