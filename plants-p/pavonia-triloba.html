Pavonia triloba

Pavonia triloba, commonly known as the three-leaved paver or redroot, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, featuring opposite, trifoliate leaves with three leaflets. The plant produces showy, red or pink bract-like flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Pavonia triloba is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in native plant gardens.
