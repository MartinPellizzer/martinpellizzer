Phacelia bombycina

Phacelia bombycina, commonly known as the woolly phacelia, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy leaves and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. It is often found in open woodlands and grasslands.

Phacelia bombycina plays a role in supporting pollinators and is valued for its ornamental appeal.
