Populus cercidiphylla

Populus cercidiphylla, commonly known as the Chinese poplar, is a deciduous tree native to China and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Salicaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and heart-shaped leaves that turn yellow in the fall. The tree produces small, catkin-like flowers in the spring, followed by winged seeds that are dispersed by the wind. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.

Populus cercidiphylla is also noted for its rapid growth and adaptability to urban environments.
