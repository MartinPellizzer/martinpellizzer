Piper hirsutum var. longepilosum

Piper hirsutum var. longepilosum, commonly known as the long-haired pepper plant, is a tropical vine native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves. The plant produces small, green berries that turn red when mature, containing aromatic seeds used in traditional medicine. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and medicinal properties.

Its botanical features make it a unique subject of study in botany and pharmacognosy.
