Pleurothallis trimytera

Pleurothallis trimytera, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to the cloud forests of South America.

It is a small, epiphytic orchid that thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant produces delicate, fragrant flowers in shades of yellow and green, which bloom during the rainy season. Its roots are adapted to absorb moisture from the air and surrounding substrates.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is a subject of interest for botanists and orchid enthusiasts.
