Potentilla verna subsp. croceolata

Potentilla verna subsp.

croceolata, commonly known as the spring cinquefoil, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges.

The plant features bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, adding vibrant color to the landscape. Its leaves are palmately divided, with five to seven lobes, and the stems are covered in fine, glandular hairs.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, supporting pollinators and thriving in well-drained soils.
