Plectranthus macranthus

Plectranthus macranthus, commonly known as the African violet or blue violet, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and clusters of showy, violet-blue flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often covered in a soft, fuzzy texture.

Plectranthus macranthus thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.
