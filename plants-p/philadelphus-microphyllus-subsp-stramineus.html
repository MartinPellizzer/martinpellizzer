Philadelphus microphyllus subsp. stramineus

Philadelphus microphyllus subsp.

stramineus, commonly known as the yellow mock orange, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States. It features small, fragrant, white to yellow flowers that bloom in early summer.

The leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a smooth surface and a slightly hairy underside. The plant has a compact growth habit and is often used in gardens for its ornamental value.

It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.
