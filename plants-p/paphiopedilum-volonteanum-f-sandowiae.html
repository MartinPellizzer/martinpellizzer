Paphiopedilum volonteanum f. sandowiae

Paphiopedilum volonteanum f. sandowiae, commonly known as the Sandow's lady's slipper, is a rare and exquisite orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, showy flowers that resemble a slipper, with a deep pink or red lip and a contrasting white or yellow upper lip. The plant typically grows as a terrestrial or epiphytic orchid, often found in shaded, humid environments. Its leaves are broad and elliptical, providing a lush green backdrop to its vibrant blooms.

Due to its rarity and beauty, it is highly sought after by orchid enthusiasts and conservationists alike.
