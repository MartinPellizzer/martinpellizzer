Pachyptera umbelliformis

Pachyptera umbelliformis, commonly known as the umbrella bellflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, clustered inflorescence that resembles an umbrella, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Pachyptera umbelliformis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
