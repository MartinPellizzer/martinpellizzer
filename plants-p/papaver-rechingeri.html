Papaver rechingeri

Papaver rechingeri, commonly known as the Rechinger's poppy, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that range in color from red to orange, with a distinctive, saucer-shaped corona. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, thriving in well-drained soils. Its flowers bloom in the spring and are often visited by pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant appearance.
