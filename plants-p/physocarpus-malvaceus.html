Physocarpus malvaceus

Physocarpus malvaceus, commonly known as the red osier dogwood, is a deciduous shrub native to North America.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes plants like hibiscus and cotton. The plant is characterized by its showy, pink to red bract-like flowers that appear in late spring. Its leaves are ovate with serrated edges and turn vibrant yellow in the fall.

The shrub is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in moist, well-drained soils.
