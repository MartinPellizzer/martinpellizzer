Pulsatilla montana subsp. olympica

Pulsatilla montana subsp.

olympica, commonly known as the Olympic pasqueflower, is a member of the Ranunculaceae family. It is a herbaceous perennial that produces showy, pink to purple flowers with a distinctive, fluffy center.

Native to the Pacific Northwest of North America, it thrives in dry, open habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes. The plant has palmately divided, lobed leaves that are hairy and grayish-green in color.

It is notable for its early spring blooming and is considered a rare and endangered species in some regions.
