Pyrethrum catananche

Pyrethrum catananche, commonly known as the common pyrethrum, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant produces small, yellowish to reddish flowers that bloom in summer, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Pyrethrum catananche is also valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in natural pest control.
