Philadelphus rhombifolius

Philadelphus rhombifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaved mock orange, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its rhombic-shaped leaves and fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its flowers are composed of five petals and are often used in gardens for their ornamental value.

The shrub is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
