Piper pernigricans

Piper pernigricans, commonly known as black pepper vine, is a climbing shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species. The plant is characterized by its dark green, alternate leaves and small, greenish-white flowers. Its fruit, which resembles peppercorns, is used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

The plant's botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and medicinal contexts.
