Pithecellobium minutissimum

Pithecellobium minutissimum, commonly known as the dwarf pisonia, is a small, woody shrub native to tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom throughout the year. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are usually elliptical in shape and covered in fine hairs. Its fruit is a small, round pod that splits open to release tiny, fluffy seeds.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is often cultivated in gardens and as a hedge plant.
