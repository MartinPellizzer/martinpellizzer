Potentilla heuffeliana var. pseudochrysantha

Potentilla heuffeliana var. pseudochrysantha, commonly known as the golden potentilla, is a compact, flowering shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features upright stems covered in fine, silvery hairs, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow, ovate, and have a slightly serrated edge, contributing to its ornamental value.

This variety is valued in gardens for its hardiness and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
