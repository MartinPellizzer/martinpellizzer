Pyrus rushforthii

Pyrus rushforthii, commonly known as the Rushforth pear, is a species of flowering plant in the family Rosaceae.

It is native to the mountainous regions of central and southern China. The plant is characterized by its small, deciduous leaves and clusters of white or pale yellow flowers that appear in early spring. Its fruits are small pears, typically yellow or green, with a rough, edible skin.

The species is valued for its ornamental value and has been cultivated for its unique appearance and historical significance in horticulture.
