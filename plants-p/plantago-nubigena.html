Plantago nubigena

Plantago nubigena, commonly known as the cloud plant, is a flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that resembles a cloud, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in a spike, which are typically pollinated by wind. It thrives in high-altitude environments with cool, moist conditions.

Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.
