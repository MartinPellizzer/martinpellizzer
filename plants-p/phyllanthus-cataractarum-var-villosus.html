Phyllanthus cataractarum var. villosus

Phyllanthus cataractarum var. villosus, commonly known as the hairy stonecrop, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its fleshy, succulent leaves that are often covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, clustered yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its adaptability and ornamental value make it a popular choice for drought-tolerant landscaping.
