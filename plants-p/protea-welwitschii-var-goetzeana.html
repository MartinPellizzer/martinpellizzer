Protea welwitschii var. goetzeana

Protea welwitschii var. goetzeana, commonly known as the Goetze's king protea, is a striking South African flowering plant belonging to the Proteaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescence composed of numerous white to pinkish flowers arranged in a cylindrical shape. The plant is native to the coastal regions of KwaZulu-Natal and is adapted to sandy, well-drained soils. It thrives in full sun and is often found in coastal fynbos vegetation.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
