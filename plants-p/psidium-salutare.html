Psidium salutare

Psidium salutare, commonly known as the sacred guava, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, ovate leaves and small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces edible berries that are typically yellow or red when ripe, with a sweet and tangy flavor. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal uses, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and research settings.
