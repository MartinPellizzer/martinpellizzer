Potentilla alpestris f. septenata

Potentilla alpestris f. septenata, commonly known as the alpine cinquefoil, is a perennial shrub native to alpine regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, thriving in full sun and cool climates. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three leaflets that are serrated and hairy, contributing to its hardy nature.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing alpine soils.
