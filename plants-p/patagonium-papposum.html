Patagonium papposum

Patagonium papposum, commonly known as the Patagonian cottonbush, is a flowering plant native to the southern regions of South America.

It is characterized by its fluffy, white seed heads that resemble cotton, which give it its common name. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, adapting to harsh climatic conditions. Its leaves are usually narrow and covered in fine hairs, helping to reduce water loss.

Patagonium papposum plays a role in local ecosystems by providing food for various insects and birds.
