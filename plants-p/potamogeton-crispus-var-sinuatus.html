Potamogeton crispus var. sinuatus

Potamogeton crispus var. sinuatus, commonly known as the sinuous pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater ecosystems in North America.

It has long, ribbon-like leaves that are often curved and have a sinuous appearance, which gives it its scientific name. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically borne in clusters near the water surface. It thrives in shallow, slow-moving waters and plays a role in providing habitat for aquatic invertebrates and fish.

Its presence can indicate good water quality, as it is sensitive to pollution.
