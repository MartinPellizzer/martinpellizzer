Phaseolus vulgaris var. sanguinans

Phaseolus vulgaris var. sanguinans, commonly known as the blood bean, is a variety of common bean characterized by its striking red to purple pod and seed coloration.

This plant belongs to the Fabaceae family and is native to the Americas, particularly Central and South America. It typically grows as an annual herb with climbing stems, producing clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers. The pods are usually hairy and contain several seeds that are often used in local cuisines.

Its botanical features make it a notable ornamental and edible plant in tropical and subtropical regions.
