Palala furfuracea

Palala furfuracea, commonly known as the hairy palala, is a unique plant native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive hairy foliage and stems, which give it its common name. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as forests or undergrowth. Its leaves are usually broad and lance-shaped, with a rough texture.

Palala furfuracea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
