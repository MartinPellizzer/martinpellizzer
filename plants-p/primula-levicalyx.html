Primula levicalyx

Primula levicalyx, commonly known as the broad-leaf primrose, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of China.

It is characterized by its clustered, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically broad and slightly hairy. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in woodland or shaded areas.

Its flowers bloom in early spring, making it a valuable early-season nectar source for pollinators.
