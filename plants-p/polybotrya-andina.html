Polybotrya andina

Polybotrya andina, commonly known as the Andean polybotrya, is a species of flowering plant native to the Andes mountains.

It belongs to the family Polybotryaceae and is characterized by its unique, branched inflorescences. The plant typically grows in high-altitude regions, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are usually narrow and grass-like, while its flowers are small and clustered.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
