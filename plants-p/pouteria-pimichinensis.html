Pouteria pimichinensis

Pouteria pimichinensis, commonly known as the Mexican butterfruit, is a tropical tree native to Central America and parts of Mexico.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces a fruit that is typically pear-shaped and has a smooth, waxy skin with a mild, sweet aroma. The fruit's pulp is edible and has a creamy texture, often used in local cuisines.

It also has potential economic value due to its ornamental appeal and possible medicinal properties.
