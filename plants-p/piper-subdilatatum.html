Piper subdilatatum

Piper subdilatatum, commonly known as the broad-leafed pepper plant, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its heart-shaped, glossy leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that develop into elongated, cylindrical fruits. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its attractive foliage.

However, it is not typically used for culinary purposes compared to other pepper species.
