Picea glauca f. conica

Picea glauca f. conica, commonly known as the blue spruce tree, is a compact, coniferous evergreen with a distinctive conical shape.

It features dark blue, needle-like leaves that are short and stiff, giving it a dense, lush appearance. The tree produces small, cone-like structures that contain its seeds, typical of the Pinaceae family. It is native to North America and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.

This variety is well-suited for colder climates and is resistant to many common pests and diseases.
