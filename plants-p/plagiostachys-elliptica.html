Plagiostachys elliptica

Plagiostachys elliptica, commonly known as the narrow-leaf sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia.

It typically grows in wet or marshy environments, showcasing its adaptability to moist conditions. The plant features narrow, linear leaves that are often arranged in a dense, grass-like structure. Its flowering spikes produce small, greenish-brown flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences.

Plagiostachys elliptica is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in wetland restoration projects.
