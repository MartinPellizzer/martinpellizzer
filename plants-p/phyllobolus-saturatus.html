Phyllobolus saturatus

Phyllobolus saturatus, commonly known as the satiated leaf beetle, is a species of leaf beetle belonging to the family Chrysomelidae.

It is characterized by its vibrant green coloration, which helps it blend with the foliage of its host plants. The beetle's diet primarily consists of leaves from various plants in the family Fabaceae, such as legumes. Its larvae also feed on plant material, contributing to the plant's natural decomposition process.

This species plays a role in nutrient cycling within its ecosystem.
