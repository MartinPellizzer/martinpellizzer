Piper maranyonense

Piper maranyonense, commonly known as the Maranon pepper, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to the Amazon rainforest, specifically in regions of Peru and Colombia. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves and small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. It produces small, round berries that are used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
