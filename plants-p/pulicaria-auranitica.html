Pulicaria auranitica

Pulicaria auranitica, commonly known as the golden pulicaria, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial herb characterized by its hairy stems and small, yellowish-green leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in dry, sandy or rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and scrublands.

Pulicaria auranitica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and visual interest.
