Paysonia lasiocarpa subsp. berlandieri

Paysonia lasiocarpa subsp.

berlandieri, commonly known as the plains paysonia, is a flowering plant native to the central and western United States. It is characterized by its basal rosette of gray-green, woolly leaves and produces tall, sparsely branched stems with yellow flowers.

The subspecies is adapted to dry, rocky habitats and is often found in prairie and steppe environments. Its flowers are typically visited by bees and other pollinators, contributing to its ecological role in native plant communities.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appearance and is sometimes cultivated in arid garden settings.
