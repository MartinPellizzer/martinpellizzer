Pilosella obscuribracteum

Pilosella obscuribracteum, commonly known as the dark bract pilosella, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in grassy areas and along roadsides, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are hairy and often have a bluish-gray appearance, while the flowers are small and yellow.

Pilosella obscuribracteum is often found in disturbed habitats and can be an indicator of soil conditions.
