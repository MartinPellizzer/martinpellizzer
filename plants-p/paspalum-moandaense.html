Paspalum moandaense

Paspalum moandaense, commonly known as the Moanda grass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, erect stems. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, spikelet-like flowers. It thrives in moist to wet environments, often found in marshy areas and along riverbanks.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
