Palmijuncus unifarius

Palmijuncus unifarius, commonly known as the single-fruit palm, is a rare and unique species native to the island of Socotra.

It is characterized by its distinctive, solitary fruit that develops at the top of the plant, which is a striking feature in the botanical world. The plant has a short, stout trunk and produces pinnately compound leaves that are covered in a dense layer of spines. Its morphology reflects adaptations to the arid and harsh environment of its native habitat.

Due to its rarity and unique features, it is of significant interest to botanists and conservationists alike.
