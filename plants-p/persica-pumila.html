Persica pumila

Persica pumila, commonly known as the dwarf peach, is a small, deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically yellow or orange in color, similar to peaches but much smaller. Persica pumila is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate blooms and fruit.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for temperate climates.
