Podostemum ostenianum

Podostemum ostenianum, commonly known as the Brazilian water primrose, is a semi-aquatic plant native to South America.

It thrives in shallow freshwater environments, often growing along the edges of streams and rivers. The plant has small, lance-shaped leaves that are typically submerged or floating, with flowers that emerge above the water surface. Its reproductive strategy involves wind pollination, and it plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for microorganisms and small aquatic life.

Podostemum ostenianum is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appearance and ease of cultivation.
