Potentilla mieheorum

Potentilla mieheorum, commonly known as the Mieh's cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has palmate leaves with five to seven leaflets, and its stems are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to harsh alpine environments.

Potentilla mieheorum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
