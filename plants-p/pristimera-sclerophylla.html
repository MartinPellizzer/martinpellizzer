Pristimera sclerophylla

Pristimera sclerophylla, commonly known as the rock fig, is a small evergreen shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, leathery leaves that are adapted to conserve water in arid environments. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are often clustered in inflorescences. Its fruit is a small, fleshy drupe that ripens to a yellowish color.

Pristimera sclerophylla is often found growing in rocky or sandy soils and is well-suited to xerophytic conditions.
