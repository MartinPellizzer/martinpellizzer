Phaseolus altimontanus

Phaseolus altimontanus, commonly known as the Andean kidney bean, is a species native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is a climbing legume that produces small, yellow flowers and kidney-shaped seeds. The plant thrives in cool, temperate climates and is adapted to grow in rocky, well-drained soils. Its leaves are trifoliate, with each leaflet having a slightly hairy surface.

This species is valued for its edible seeds, which are rich in protein and nutrients.
