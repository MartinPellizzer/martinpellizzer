Pyrus pseudohupehensis

Pyrus pseudohupehensis, commonly known as the Chinese white pear, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and alternate, ovate leaves with serrated edges. The tree produces clusters of small, white flowers in early spring, followed by edible, sweet fruits that are similar to common pears.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit production in temperate climates.
