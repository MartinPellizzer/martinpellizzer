Paederota cochlearifolia

Paederota cochlearifolia, commonly known as the spiral-leaved bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive spiral arrangement of leaves, which gives it a unique and ornamental appearance. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Paederota cochlearifolia is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic value and easy maintenance.
