Phlegmariurus cumingii

Phlegmariurus cumingii, commonly known as the Cuming's haircap moss, is a species of moss belonging to the family Dicranaceae.

It is characterized by its dense, cushion-like growth form and dark green to blackish stems covered with short, appressed leaves. The leaves are typically ovate to lanceolate, with a distinct costa that extends beyond the base of the leaf. This moss thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and rocky crevices.

Its reproductive structures, including capsules, are borne on short, upright stalks and release spores when mature.
