Philadelphus koreanus var. robustus

Philadelphus koreanus var. robustus, commonly known as the Korean mock orange, is a deciduous shrub native to Korea.

It features showy, fragrant flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer, typically in shades of white or pale yellow. The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges and a woody structure that supports its upright growth habit. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its botanical characteristics make it both visually appealing and ecologically valuable in native habitats.
