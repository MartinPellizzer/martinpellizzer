Pandanus spondiophyllus

Pandanus spondiophyllus, commonly known as the narrow-leaf pandanus, is a tropical plant native to the Pacific islands.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and long, narrow leaves that are often used in traditional crafts. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences that bear fragrant, yellowish flowers. Its roots are sometimes used in local medicine for their purported healing properties.

Overall, it plays an important ecological and cultural role in its native habitats.
