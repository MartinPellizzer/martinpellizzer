Planchonella puberula

Planchonella puberula, commonly known as the hairy planchonella, is a tropical plant native to parts of Southeast Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It is a small to medium-sized tree or shrub that typically grows in humid, lowland forests. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are often covered in fine, short hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its small, yellowish flowers are followed by edible, citrus-like fruits that are used in traditional medicine.

The botanical features of Planchonella puberula make it an important species in its native ecosystems and for local cultural practices.
