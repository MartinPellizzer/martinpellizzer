Plantago tomentosa subsp. schlechtendaliana

Plantago tomentosa subsp.

schlechtendaliana, commonly known as the woolly plantain, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It features a rosette of broad, hairy, ovate leaves that are covered in fine white tomentum, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The plant produces erect flowering stems that bear dense clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, along roadsides, and in wetlands.

This subspecies is valued for its adaptability and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
