Pappostipa parodiana

Pappostipa parodiana, commonly known as the Parodiana grass, is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

Due to its ecological importance, it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
