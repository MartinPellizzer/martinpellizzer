Pelargonium revolutum

Pelargonium revolutum, commonly known as the Zulu geranium, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and vibrant, showy flowers that range from pink to red. The leaves are typically lobed and have a distinctive revolute margin, giving the plant its scientific name. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens and containers.

This species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
