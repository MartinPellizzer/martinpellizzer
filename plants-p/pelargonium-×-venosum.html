Pelargonium × venosum

Pelargonium × venosum, commonly known as the variegated geranium, is a hybrid species within the Geraniaceae family.

It is characterized by its striking variegated leaves, which often display combinations of white, yellow, and green patterns. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of pink, red, or white, typically during the summer months. It is a perennial herb that thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is widely cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
