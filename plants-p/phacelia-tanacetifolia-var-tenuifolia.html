Phacelia tanacetifolia var. tenuifolia

Phacelia tanacetifolia var. tenuifolia, commonly known as narrow-leaved blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, sky-blue flowers. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes. Its flowers bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native plant gardens.
