Potentilla garipensis

Potentilla garipensis, commonly known as the Turkish cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to Turkey.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit. The plant features small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are typically compound, with five leaflets, and it thrives in well-drained, sandy soils.

Potentilla garipensis is often found in mountainous regions and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
