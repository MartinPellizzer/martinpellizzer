Parodia paucicostata

Parodia paucicostata, commonly known as the "Cactus of the Desert," is a species of cactus native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems that are typically covered in white, fuzzy spines. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, adding a striking contrast to its green foliage. Parodia paucicostata is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements make it a favored plant among succulent enthusiasts.
