Pseudolysimachion vandasii

Pseudolysimachion vandasii, commonly known as the Vanda's pseudolysimachion, is a member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in Thailand and Malaysia. The plant features a sympodial growth habit with a pseudobulb that supports its foliage and flowering. Its leaves are elongated and strap-like, often with a waxy texture, and it produces fragrant, showy flowers in shades of white and yellow.

This species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
