Paropsia madagascariensis

Paropsia madagascariensis, commonly known as the Madagascar star orchid, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, star-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments within the rainforests of Madagascar. Its foliage consists of broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and valuable species in botanical collections.
