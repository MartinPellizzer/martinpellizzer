Proboscidea parviflora subsp. parviflora

Proboscidea parviflora subsp.

parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered proboscidea, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America. It is a herbaceous perennial with slender, upright stems and small, clustered flowers.

The plant produces white to pale blue flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often covered in fine hairs.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to high-altitude environments.
