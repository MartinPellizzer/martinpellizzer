Pilea longipes

Pilea longipes, commonly known as the Shingle Plant, is a species of flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit and its distinctive, elongated leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in the undergrowth of tropical forests.

Pilea longipes is valued both for its ornamental appeal and its ecological role in its native habitat.
