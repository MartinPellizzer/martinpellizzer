Peperomia diaphanoides var. vincentensis

Peperomia diaphanoides var. vincentensis, commonly known as the Vincent's peperomia, is a compact, low-growing plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It features fleshy, oval-shaped leaves that are typically green with a waxy texture, often displaying subtle variegation. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white or greenish flowers that emerge from leaf axils. It thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Its compact size and ornamental foliage make it suitable for use in hanging baskets or as a tabletop plant.
