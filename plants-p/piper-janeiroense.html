Piper janeiroense

Piper janeiroense, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper vine, is a climbing plant native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many species of pepper plants. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with a glossy surface and produces small, greenish-white flowers. Its fruits are small, round berries that turn red when mature, attracting various bird species.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in tropical gardens, though it can become invasive in certain environments.
