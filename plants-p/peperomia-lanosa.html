Peperomia lanosa

Peperomia lanosa, commonly known as the velvety peperomia, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Central and South America, where it thrives in shaded, humid environments. The plant is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are covered in a fine, velvety texture, giving it a unique appearance. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are typically greenish-white and appear in clusters.

Peperomia lanosa is often cultivated as a decorative houseplant due to its compact size and low maintenance requirements.
