Pervillaea venenata subsp. macrantha

Pervillaea venenata subsp.

macrantha, commonly known as the Mexican ironweed, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America. It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and robust, hairy stems.

The plant produces showy, purple to violet flower heads that bloom in late summer and early autumn. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. Pervillaea venenata subsp.

macrantha is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.
