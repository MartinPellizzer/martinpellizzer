Phoradendron latifolium

Phoradendron latifolium, commonly known as the mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches itself to the branches of host trees.

It lacks true roots and absorbs water and nutrients through its stem and leaves. The plant produces small, white or greenish flowers that are often inconspicuous. Its berries, which are typically red, are an important food source for birds and other wildlife.

Phoradendron latifolium plays a significant role in the ecological dynamics of its host environment.
