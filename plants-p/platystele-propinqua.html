Platystele propinqua

Platystele propinqua, commonly known as the narrow-leaved ghost orchid, is a rare and enigmatic member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is an epiphytic plant, meaning it grows on the surface of other plants without deriving nutrients from them. Native to the cloud forests of Central and South America, it thrives in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are narrow and strap-like, often appearing as small, ghostly projections from its host tree.

The plant produces delicate, white flowers with a unique structure that resembles a ghostly face, contributing to its ethereal appearance.
