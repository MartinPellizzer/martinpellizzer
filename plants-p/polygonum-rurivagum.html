Polygonum rurivagum

Polygonum rurivagum, commonly known as the wandering knotweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, which is characterized by its distinctive nodes and often swollen stems. The plant typically grows in moist environments such as wet meadows and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped with a prominent midrib, and it produces small, greenish flowers in clusters.

Polygonum rurivagum is known for its ability to spread vegetatively, making it both a resilient and sometimes invasive species.
