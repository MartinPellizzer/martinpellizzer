Plagiocarpus arnhemicus

Plagiocarpus arnhemicus, commonly known as the desert shield-leaf, is a perennial shrub native to the arid regions of northern Australia.

It is characterized by its small, thick, waxy leaves that help reduce water loss in its harsh environment. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in spring, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. Its root system is extensive and deep, allowing it to access groundwater in dry conditions.

Plagiocarpus arnhemicus plays a role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for local wildlife.
