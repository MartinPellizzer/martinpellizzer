Pleopeltis polypodioides var. knoblochiana

Pleopeltis polypodioides var. knoblochiana, commonly known as the Knobloch's rock fern, is a species of epiphytic fern native to Southeast Asia.

It typically grows on tree trunks and rocky surfaces in humid tropical environments. The plant has lanceolate, leathery leaves that are usually dark green and have a waxy coating. It produces small, spore-bearing structures called sori on the underside of its fronds.

This variety is characterized by its compact growth habit and is often found in shaded, moist forest habitats.
