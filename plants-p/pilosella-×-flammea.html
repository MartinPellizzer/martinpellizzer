Pilosella × flammea

Pilosella × flammea, commonly known as the flame hawkweed, is a hybrid species within the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage and bright yellow flowers that resemble dandelions. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides and meadows. It is native to Europe but has been introduced to other parts of the world.

Pilosella × flammea is an annual or biennial plant that can spread aggressively through seed dispersal.
