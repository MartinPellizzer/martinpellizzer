Pinus abchasica

Pinus abchasica, commonly known as the Abkhazian pine, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its slender, often twisted trunk and needle-like leaves that are typically 2 to 4 centimeters long. The tree produces cones that are usually less than 5 centimeters in length, containing winged seeds. It thrives in mountainous areas with well-drained soils and is adapted to cold climates.

The Abkhazian pine plays an important role in the local ecosystem and is valued for its timber and ornamental qualities.
