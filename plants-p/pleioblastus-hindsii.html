Pleioblastus hindsii

Pleioblastus hindsii, commonly known as the Hinds' bamboo, is a species of grass found primarily in China.

It is characterized by its slender, green culms that are often covered with a white, powdery bloom. This plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as along streams or in forest understories. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a smooth texture and a slightly pointed tip.

Pleioblastus hindsii is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often used in horticulture for its aesthetic appeal.
