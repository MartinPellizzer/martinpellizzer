Ptychosperma ridleyi

Ptychosperma ridleyi, commonly known as the Malabar palm, is a tropical plant native to the Western Ghats of India.

It is a tall, slender palm that can grow up to 15 meters in height, characterized by its large, pinnate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by black, spherical fruits. Its trunk is smooth and covered with a fibrous sheath, and it is often found in moist, shaded habitats.

Ptychosperma ridleyi is valued for its ornamental qualities and has some traditional uses in local communities.
