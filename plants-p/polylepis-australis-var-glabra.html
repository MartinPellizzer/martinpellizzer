Polylepis australis var. glabra

Polylepis australis var. glabra, commonly known as the smooth polylepis, is a shrub or small tree native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its smooth, often greyish bark and dense foliage. The plant typically grows in high-altitude environments, often in cloud forests or paramo ecosystems. Its leaves are generally simple and alternate, with a leathery texture.

Polylepis australis var. glabra plays an important ecological role in its habitat, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
