Pogonia punctata

Pogonia punctata, commonly known as the spotted pogonia, is a terrestrial orchid native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking, violet-colored flowers with distinctive yellow spots and a unique, spurred lip. The plant typically grows in moist, acidic woodlands and requires specific environmental conditions to thrive. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces a single, showy flower per stem.

Pogonia punctata is considered endangered due to habitat loss and over-collection.
