Passiflora naviculata

Passiflora naviculata, commonly known as the boat-shaped passionflower, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It is characterized by its unique, intricate flowers that feature five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments. The plant produces edible, orange-yellow berries that are rich in antioxidants. Its leaves are heart-shaped and often have a velvety texture.

Passiflora naviculata is valued in horticulture for its ornamental beauty and is also used in traditional medicine.
