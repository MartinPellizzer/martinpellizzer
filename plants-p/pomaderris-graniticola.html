Pomaderris graniticola

Pomaderris graniticola, commonly known as the granite pomaderris, is a small to medium-sized shrub native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, leathery leaves that are typically arranged in opposite pairs along the stems. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are usually white or pale yellow, appearing in clusters during the spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
