Prunus persica var. laevis

Prunus persica var. laevis, commonly known as the smooth peach, is a variety of peach characterized by its smooth, hairless skin.

It is native to China and is cultivated for its sweet, juicy fruits that are typically harvested in late summer. The plant is a deciduous tree with a broad, rounded crown and produces white flowers in early spring. Its leaves are ovate with a serrated margin and a deep green color.

The fruit contains a single large seed enclosed in a fuzzy, hairy outer layer.
