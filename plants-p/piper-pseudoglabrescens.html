Piper pseudoglabrescens

Piper pseudoglabrescens, commonly known as the roughleaf pepper, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many species of pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its rough, hairy leaves and small, clustered flowers. Its fruits are typically berries that contain aromatic seeds used in traditional medicine.

The botanical features of Piper pseudoglabrescens make it an interesting subject for studies in plant morphology and pharmacology.
