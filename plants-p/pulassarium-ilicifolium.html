Pulassarium ilicifolium

Pulassarium ilicifolium, commonly known as the silverleaf holly, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage that ranges from dark green to silvery-gray in color. The leaves are typically oval-shaped with a leathery texture and a waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss. It produces small, inconspicuous white flowers in the spring, followed by red berries that are attractive to birds.

The plant is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and hardiness in various soil conditions.
