Passiflora onychina

Passiflora onychina, commonly known as the velvet passionflower, is a striking tropical vine native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its dark green, velvety leaves and showy, complex flowers that feature a blend of blue, purple, and white petals. The plant produces edible, orange-red berries that are rich in antioxidants and have a sweet, tangy flavor. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Passiflora onychina is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its aesthetic appeal and ecological value.
