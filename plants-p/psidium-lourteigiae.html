Psidium lourteigiae

Psidium lourteigiae, commonly known as the Lourteig's guava, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are followed by edible, fleshy fruits. Its leaves are typically elliptical with a glossy surface and a serrated edge.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and potential as a fruit crop in suitable climates.
