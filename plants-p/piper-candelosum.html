Piper candelosum

Piper candelosum, commonly known as candle bush, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes numerous species of flowering plants. The plant is characterized by its slender, often branched stems and small, opposite leaves that are usually glossy and dark green. It produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by bright red berries, which are an important food source for birds and other wildlife.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in shaded, humid environments.
