Polycarena formosa

Polycarena formosa, commonly known as the coral tree, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, colorful inflorescences that resemble coral, hence its common name. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a waxy texture.

Polycarena formosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
