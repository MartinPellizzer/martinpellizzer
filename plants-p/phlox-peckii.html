Phlox peckii

Phlox peckii, commonly known as the Peck's phlox, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows to a height of 12 to 24 inches and features showy clusters of pink to purple flowers. The plant has opposite, linear to lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Phlox peckii is valued for its ornamental value and is often used in native plant gardens.
