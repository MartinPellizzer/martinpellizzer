Phaeostemma surinamensis

Phaeostemma surinamensis, commonly known as the Surinam ghostplant, is a unique aquatic plant native to the wetlands of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, ribbon-like leaves that float on the water's surface, often appearing translucent or ghostly due to their pale coloration. The plant thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, where it can form dense mats on the water's surface. Its reproductive structures are small and inconspicuous, typically producing tiny seeds that are dispersed by water currents.

Phaeostemma surinamensis plays a role in maintaining water quality by absorbing excess nutrients and providing habitat for small aquatic organisms.
