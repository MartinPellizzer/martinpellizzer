Phalaris setacea

Phalaris setacea, commonly known as the narrowleaf beakgrass, is a flowering plant native to Europe, North Africa, and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the grass family Poaceae and typically grows in wet or marshy habitats. The plant has slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences with tiny, pale green flowers. Its seeds are dispersed by wind, and it often forms dense colonies in its native environment.

Phalaris setacea is also sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its delicate appearance.
