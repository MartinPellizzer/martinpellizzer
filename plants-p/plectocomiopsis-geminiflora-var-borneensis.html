Plectocomiopsis geminiflora var. borneensis

Plectocomiopsis geminiflora var. borneensis, commonly known as the Bornean twin-flower, is a rare and unique plant native to the rainforests of Borneo.

It is characterized by its striking, paired flowers that bloom in a distinctive spiral arrangement along the stem. The plant has small, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in opposite pairs, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained, nutrient-rich soil.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a valuable species for botanical research and conservation efforts.
