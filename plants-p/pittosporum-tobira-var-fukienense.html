Pittosporum tobira var. fukienense

Pittosporum tobira var. fukienense, commonly known as the Chinese pittosporum, is a compact evergreen shrub native to eastern China.

It features glossy, dark green leaves that are elliptical in shape and emit a pleasant fragrance when touched. The plant produces small, white flowers with a strong, sweet scent, which appear in late spring to early summer. These flowers are followed by orange-red berries that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to a variety of garden conditions.
