Polypodium heterophyllum

Polypodium heterophyllum, commonly known as the variable huperzia, is a species of fern belonging to the family Huperziaceae.

It is characterized by its evergreen, leathery fronds that are typically divided into pairs of pinnae. The plant is native to various regions in Asia, including China, Japan, and the Himalayas, where it thrives in moist, shaded environments. Its rhizome is thick and creeping, allowing it to spread and form dense colonies.

Polypodium heterophyllum is often used in traditional medicine and is valued for its ornamental appearance in horticulture.
