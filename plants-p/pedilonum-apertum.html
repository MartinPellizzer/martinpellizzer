Pedilonum apertum

Pedilonum apertum, commonly known as the open-footed orchid, is a rare and delicate flowering plant native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, showy flowers that resemble small, open sandals. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as rainforests, where it relies on mycorrhizal fungi for nutrient absorption. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to anchor in the rich, organic soil of its natural habitat.

Due to habitat loss and over-collection, Pedilonum apertum is considered endangered, highlighting the need for conservation efforts.
