Phalacroloma annuum subsp. strigosum

Phalacroloma annuum subsp.

strigosum, commonly known as the hairy nightshade, is a member of the Solanaceae family. It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it its common name.

The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale green. Its fruits are berries that mature to a dark purple color.

This subspecies is native to parts of Central and South America and is often found in dry, open habitats.
