Petroselinum crispum f. variegatum

Petroselinum crispum f. variegatum, commonly known as variegated parsley, is a cultivar of parsley characterized by its distinctive leaf patterns.

Its foliage features a mix of green and white or yellow variegation, making it visually striking. The plant is a biennial herb that thrives in cool climates and well-drained soil. It produces small, yellow flowers that eventually develop into small, oval-shaped fruits.

This variety is often grown for ornamental purposes as well as for its mild parsley flavor in culinary applications.
