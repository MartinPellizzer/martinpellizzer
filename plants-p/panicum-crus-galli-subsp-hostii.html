Panicum crus-galli subsp. hostii

Panicum crus-galli subsp.

hostii, commonly known as red panicgrass, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States. It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and produces dense, fluffy panicles that are often tinged with red or purple hues.

The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats, such as marshes and along stream banks. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green coloration.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in wet conditions.
