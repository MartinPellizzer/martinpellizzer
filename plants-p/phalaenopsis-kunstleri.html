Phalaenopsis kunstleri

Phalaenopsis kunstleri, commonly known as the Kunstler's orchid, is a species of epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its long, pendant inflorescences that bear clusters of fragrant, showy flowers in various shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant has strap-like leaves that are typically dark green and arranged in a rosette pattern. It thrives in warm, humid environments with indirect light and requires well-draining potting media.

Phalaenopsis kunstleri is popular among orchid enthusiasts for its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
