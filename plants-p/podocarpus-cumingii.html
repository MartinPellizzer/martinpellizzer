Podocarpus cumingii

Podocarpus cumingii, commonly known as the Chinese podocarp or Cuming's podocarp, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to southern China and Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Podocarpaceae and is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and small, cone-like structures. The plant is typically found in humid, tropical or subtropical regions, growing in moist, well-drained soils. Its leaves are leathery and arranged spirally on the shoots, contributing to its ornamental value.

Podocarpus cumingii is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal and is used in horticulture and landscaping.
