Panicum fasciculiforme

Panicum fasciculiforme, commonly known as the narrow-leaf panicgrass, is a grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields. It has a fibrous root system and produces small, wind-pollinated flowers.

Panicum fasciculiforme is often used as a forage plant for livestock due to its high nutritional value.
