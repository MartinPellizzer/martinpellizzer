Primula yesomontana

Primula yesomontana, commonly known as the Yesomontan primrose, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often in forest understories or along stream banks. Its leaves are usually oval-shaped with a slightly hairy texture, and it produces small, clustered flowers that bloom in early spring.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
