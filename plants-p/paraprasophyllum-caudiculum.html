Paraprasophyllum caudiculum

Paraprasophyllum caudiculum, commonly known as the slender-stemmed paraprasophyllum, is a rare and specialized plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its elongated, cylindrical stem that stores water, a feature adapted to its harsh, dry environment. The plant produces small, delicate flowers in shades of yellow and orange, typically during the rainy season. Its leaves are reduced to scale-like structures, minimizing water loss through transpiration.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a vulnerable species with limited distribution.
