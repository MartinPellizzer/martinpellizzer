Piper relaxatum

Piper relaxatum, commonly known as the creeping pepper vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes over 3,000 species of flowering plants. The plant features heart-shaped, glossy leaves and produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into reddish berries. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can thrive in humid, shaded environments.

Its scientific name reflects its relaxed, sprawling growth habit.
