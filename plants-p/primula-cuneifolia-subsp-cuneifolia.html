Primula cuneifolia subsp. cuneifolia

Primula cuneifolia subsp.

cuneifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf primrose, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of East Asia. It typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors and rocky crevices.

The plant features lance-shaped, dark green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are usually pink or white, with five petals and a distinctive yellow throat.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its early spring blooms.
