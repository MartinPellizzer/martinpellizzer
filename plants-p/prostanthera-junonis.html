Prostanthera junonis

Prostanthera junonis, commonly known as the narrow-leaf mintbush, is a small shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, pale blue or purple flowers arranged in spikes. Prostanthera junonis is adapted to dry, sandy soils and is often found in heathlands and woodlands.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local wildlife.
