Petrosimonia sibirica var. bungeana

Petrosimonia sibirica var. bungeana, commonly known as the Siberian sandwort, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the cold regions of Central Asia and Siberia.

It features small, fleshy, blue-green leaves that are adapted to retain moisture in arid environments. The plant produces clusters of tiny, white or pale pink flowers during the summer months. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in alpine or steppe habitats.

Its botanical characteristics make it well-suited for xerophytic conditions and it is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant in gardens.
