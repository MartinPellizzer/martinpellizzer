Paphiopedilum burmanicum

Paphiopedilum burmanicum, commonly known as the Burman's slipper orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It features a pseudobulb with a single, fleshy leaf and produces a single spike bearing several showy, lip-like flowers. The flowers are typically pale yellow with a distinctive red or orange lip, and they bloom during the spring or early summer. This species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in greenhouses or as a potted plant.

It prefers cool, humid conditions and well-draining soil to thrive.
