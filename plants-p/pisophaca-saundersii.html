Pisophaca saundersii

Pisophaca saundersii, commonly known as the Saunders' sandwort, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically thrives in rocky, well-drained soils. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers during the spring and summer months. Its leaves are usually succulent and arranged in a rosette pattern, which helps it retain moisture in arid environments.

Pisophaca saundersii is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
