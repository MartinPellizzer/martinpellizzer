Polypodium trichomanes

Polypodium trichomanes, commonly known as the lady fern, is a species of fern native to the temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its delicate, bipinnate fronds that are typically bright green and have a feathery appearance. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in woodland areas and along stream banks. Its rhizome is creeping and produces multiple shoots, allowing it to spread vegetatively.

Polypodium trichomanes is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for various purposes.
