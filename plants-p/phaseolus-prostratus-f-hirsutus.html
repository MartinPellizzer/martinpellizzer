Phaseolus prostratus f. hirsutus

Phaseolus prostratus f. hirsutus, commonly known as the hairy runner bean, is a variant of the runner bean species native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its prostrate growth habit and hairy foliage, which distinguishes it from other Phaseolus species. The plant produces edible pods that are similar to common runner beans but often have a slightly different texture and flavor. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is valued for both its ornamental appeal and its culinary use in various cuisines.
