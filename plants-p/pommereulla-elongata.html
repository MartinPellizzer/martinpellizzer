Pommereulla elongata

Pommereulla elongata, commonly known as the long-stemmed pommereulla, is a unique aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in southern Africa.

It is characterized by its elongated, slender stems that can grow up to several meters in length, often floating on the water's surface. The plant produces small, delicate white flowers that bloom in early spring, adding a subtle ornamental value to its habitat. Its leaves are narrow and ribbon-like, arranged alternately along the stem, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Pommereulla elongata plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various invertebrates and small fish.
