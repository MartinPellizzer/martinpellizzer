Persea hypoglauca

Persea hypoglauca, commonly known as the Mexican persimmon, is a small evergreen tree native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that are dark green on top and pale underneath. The tree produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by orange to reddish fruits with a sweet, sometimes astringent flavor. The fruit is encased in a thick, leathery skin and is typically consumed when fully ripe.

This plant is valued not only for its edible fruit but also for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
