Primula viscosa subsp. commutata

Primula viscosa subsp.

commutata, commonly known as the sticky primrose, is a low-growing perennial herb native to parts of Asia. It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves that produce a sticky substance, which is a distinctive botanical feature.

The plant produces clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of yellow and orange, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in woodlands and rocky areas.

Its subspecies status highlights its unique adaptations and ecological niche within its native habitat.
