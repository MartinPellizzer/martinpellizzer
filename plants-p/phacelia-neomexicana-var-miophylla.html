Phacelia neomexicana var. miophylla

Phacelia neomexicana var. miophylla, commonly known as the New Mexico phacelia, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves that are often covered in fine, white pubescence. The plant produces clusters of small, blue to violet flowers that bloom in early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Its botanical features make it a valuable plant for pollinators and a subject of interest in ecological and botanical studies.
