Pyrostria hexasperma

Pyrostria hexasperma, commonly known as the six-ribbed bamboo, is a perennial plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its slender, hollow stems with six distinct ribs. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest understories. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a smooth texture and a bluish-green color.
