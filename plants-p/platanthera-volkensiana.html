Platanthera volkensiana

Platanthera volkensiana, commonly known as the Volkens' orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands. Its leaves are elongated and often arranged in a rosette pattern, while its flowers are usually white or pale yellow with a distinctive lip.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for horticultural purposes.
