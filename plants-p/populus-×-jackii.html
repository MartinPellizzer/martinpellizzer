Populus × jackii

Populus × jackii, commonly known as the hybrid poplar, is a deciduous tree native to the northern hemisphere.

It is a cross between Populus nigra and Populus tremula, resulting in a species with characteristics of both parents. The plant has smooth, gray bark and heart-shaped leaves that are typically 5-9 cm in length. It produces small, clustered flowers in the spring, followed by winged seeds that are dispersed by the wind.

Populus × jackii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and rapid growth rate.
