Photinia serratifolia var. tomentosa

Photinia serratifolia var. tomentosa, commonly known as the tomentosa photinia, is a compact shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its dense, upright growth habit and small, dark green leaves that are covered in a fine, grayish-white pubescence. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers in the spring, followed by red, fleshy berries that persist into winter. It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various soil types.

This variety is often used in landscaping for its dense foliage and seasonal color changes.
