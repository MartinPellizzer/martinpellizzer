Passiflora pusilla

Passiflora pusilla, commonly known as the dwarf passionflower, is a small, climbing vine native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, palmately lobed leaves and striking, five-petaled flowers that are often adorned with intricate filaments and a crown-like structure. The plant produces small, edible berries that are typically yellow or orange in color. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and unique floral structure, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
