Peperomia borucana

Peperomia borucana, commonly known as the Borucan Peperomia, is a compact, evergreen perennial native to the tropical regions of South America.

It features dense, fleshy, ovate leaves that are typically green with a waxy texture, often adorned with subtle variegation. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters, usually in shades of green or red, which are followed by tiny, berry-like fruits. It thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Its low maintenance and attractive foliage make it a desirable addition to both indoor and outdoor spaces.
