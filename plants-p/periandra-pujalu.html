Periandra pujalu

Periandra pujalu, commonly known as the Pujalu flame tree, is a tropical plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, flame-like red flowers that bloom in clusters during the wet season. The plant has compound leaves with elongated leaflets and produces small, spherical fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and requires a warm, humid climate.

Periandra pujalu is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant floral display.
