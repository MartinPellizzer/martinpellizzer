Pisum aphaca

Pisum aphaca, commonly known as the narrowleaf pea, is a flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and trifoliate leaves composed of three small, elliptical leaflets. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale purple, followed by pods containing several seeds. Native to the Mediterranean region, it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
