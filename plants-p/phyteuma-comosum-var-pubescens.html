Phyteuma comosum var. pubescens

Phyteuma comosum var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy false sunflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to southern Europe.

It features upright stems covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive textured appearance. The plant produces showy, daisy-like yellow flowers with dark central cones, blooming from late summer to early autumn. Its foliage consists of lance-shaped, hairy leaves that are often bluish-green in color.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.
