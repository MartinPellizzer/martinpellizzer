Platyclinis commelinifolia

Platyclinis commelinifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf marsh grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It typically grows in wetland environments, such as marshes and along the edges of water bodies. The plant has linear, narrow leaves that are often arranged in a basal rosette, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters. Its root system is fibrous, helping it anchor in moist soils and absorb nutrients efficiently.

Platyclinis commelinifolia plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
