Polygala densifolia

Polygala densifolia, commonly known as the dense-flowered milkwort, is a flowering plant native to regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that often cluster in dense inflorescences. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in semi-arid environments. Its leaves are usually narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, often white or pinkish flowers.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation efforts.
