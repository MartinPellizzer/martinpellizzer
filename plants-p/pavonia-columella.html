Pavonia columella

Pavonia columella, commonly known as the scarlet pavonia, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features showy, red or pink bract-like flowers that are clustered at the ends of its stems. The plant has slender, upright stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Pavonia columella is valued in gardens for its vibrant floral display and ability to attract pollinators.
