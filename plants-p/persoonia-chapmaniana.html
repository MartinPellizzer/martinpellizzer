Persoonia chapmaniana

Persoonia chapmaniana, commonly known as Chapman's ghost gum, is a small evergreen tree or shrub native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, white hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in spring and attract nectar-feeding insects. Its bark is smooth and greyish, often flaking in thin strips.

Persoonia chapmaniana is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient plant in harsh conditions.
