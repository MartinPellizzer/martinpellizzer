Panicum jardinii

Panicum jardinii, commonly known as the silver hair grass, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, silver-tinged leaves and delicate, feathery inflorescences that bloom in late summer. The plant thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening. It is drought-tolerant and low-maintenance, often used in native plant gardens and wildlife habitats.

Its unique appearance and ecological benefits make it a valuable addition to diverse landscapes.
