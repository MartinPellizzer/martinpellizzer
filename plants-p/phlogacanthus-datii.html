Phlogacanthus datii

Phlogacanthus datii, commonly known as the African senna, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its bipinnate leaves and yellow-orange flowers. The plant produces cylindrical pods that contain small, dark seeds. It is often found in dry savanna and woodland habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
