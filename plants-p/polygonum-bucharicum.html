Polygonum bucharicum

Polygonum bucharicum, commonly known as the Bukharan knotweed, is a perennial herb native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, characterized by its distinctive arrow-shaped leaves and clusters of small, pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, sandy soils and is often found in riverbanks and grasslands. Its stems are often hairy and can form dense colonies, making it a common weed in disturbed areas.

The plant is also noted for its ability to thrive in harsh environmental conditions.
