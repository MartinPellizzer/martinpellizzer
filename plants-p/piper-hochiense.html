Piper hochiense

Piper hochiense, commonly known as the Hoch's pepper, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species. The plant is characterized by its slender, vine-like stems and compound leaves with opposite arrangement. Its small, greenish-white flowers are clustered in spikes, and it produces small, round berries that are typically green when unripe.

This species is valued for its aromatic properties and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
