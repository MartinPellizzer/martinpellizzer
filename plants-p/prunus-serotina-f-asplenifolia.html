Prunus serotina f. asplenifolia

Prunus serotina f. asplenifolia, commonly known as the asplenifolius cherry, is a variant of the black cherry tree characterized by its distinctive foliage.

Its leaves are ovate to elliptic in shape, with a serrated margin and a dark green upper surface. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by dark red to black berries in the fall. It is typically found in moist, well-drained soils in woodland areas.

This form is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
