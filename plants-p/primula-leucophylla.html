Primula leucophylla

Primula leucophylla, commonly known as the white-leaf primrose, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of central and southern China.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The leaves are typically broad and have a distinctive silvery or white underside, which gives the plant its common name. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in rocky or forested areas.

This species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to bloom in early spring.
