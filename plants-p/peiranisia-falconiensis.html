Peiranisia falconiensis

Peiranisia falconiensis, commonly known as the Falcon Islands hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean, specifically the islands of the Bahamas.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically range in color from white to pink, with five petals and a prominent yellow stamen. The plant thrives in coastal areas and is adapted to salty air and sandy soils. It plays an important role in local ecosystems by providing nectar for pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds.
