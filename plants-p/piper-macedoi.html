Piper macedoi

Piper macedoi, commonly known as the Macedonian pepper plant, is a flowering vine native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species such as black pepper. The plant produces small, green berries that turn red when mature, containing aromatic seeds used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are often used in local cuisines for their spicy flavor.

The botanical structure of Piper macedoi features opposite, ovate leaves and a climbing habit with tendrils for support.
