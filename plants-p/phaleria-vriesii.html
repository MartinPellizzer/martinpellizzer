Phaleria vriesii

Phaleria vriesii, commonly known as the Vries's tamarind, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small evergreen tree or shrub that typically grows up to 5 meters in height. The plant features compound leaves with elongated leaflets and produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in panicles. Its fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release seeds, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The plant is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.
