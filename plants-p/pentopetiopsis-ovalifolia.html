Pentopetiopsis ovalifolia

Pentopetiopsis ovalifolia, commonly known as the oval-leaf pentopetiopsis, is a flowering plant native to the Andes region of South America.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments at high altitudes. Its botanical features include a fibrous root system and a short, upright stem.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in specialized gardens.
