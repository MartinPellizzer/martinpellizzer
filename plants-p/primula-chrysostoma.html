Primula chrysostoma

Primula chrysostoma, commonly known as the golden crown primrose, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, preferring well-drained soil and full sun. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in a dense layer of fine hairs.

Primula chrysostoma is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
