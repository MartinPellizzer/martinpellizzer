Polygala obovata var. latifolia

Polygala obovata var. latifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia, including China and the Korean Peninsula.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
