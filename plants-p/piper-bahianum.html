Piper bahianum

Piper bahianum, commonly known as Brazilian pepper or pimenta-do-brejo, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its small, clustered white flowers and glossy, dark green leaves. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible but often have a spicy or peppery taste. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and traditional medicine practices.
