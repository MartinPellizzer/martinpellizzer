Prosthechea chondylobulbon

Prosthechea chondylobulbon, commonly known as the yellow-tipped orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its erect, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of yellowish-green to brownish flowers with distinctive, elongated sepals and petals. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forests and grasslands.

Prosthechea chondylobulbon is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its unique floral display.
