Priva rhinanthifolia

Priva rhinanthifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved redshank, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of bright red, tubular flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils, often found in wet meadows and along riverbanks.

Priva rhinanthifolia is an important plant for pollinators, particularly bees, due to its nectar-rich flowers.
