Potentilla pseudorubens

Potentilla pseudorubens, commonly known as the red sandalwood or red potentilla, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a low-growing perennial shrub that typically reaches heights of 10 to 20 inches. The plant features finely divided, gray-green leaves and produces clusters of small, bright yellow flowers in the spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable addition to native plant gardens.
