Pyrus cydonia var. sylvestris

Pyrus cydonia var. sylvestris, commonly known as the wild pear, is a shrub or small tree native to Europe and western Asia.

It produces small, yellowish-green fruits that are typically harvested in late summer. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape with serrated edges. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in horticulture.
