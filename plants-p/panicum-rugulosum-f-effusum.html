Panicum rugulosum f. effusum

Panicum rugulosum f. effusum, commonly known as silky foxtail, is a grass species characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and distinctive silky, silver-tomentose leaves.

It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in open fields, roadsides, and agricultural areas. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are typically green to reddish in color. Its leaves are narrow and have a soft, fuzzy texture, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

It is native to the Americas and is adapted to a wide range of environmental conditions.
