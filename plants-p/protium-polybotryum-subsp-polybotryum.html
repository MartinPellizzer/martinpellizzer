Protium polybotryum subsp. polybotryum

Protium polybotryum subsp.

polybotryum, commonly known as the Philippine tamarind, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to the Philippines. It is characterized by its simple, alternate, and elliptic leaves that are usually leathery in texture.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in panicles, followed by woody, elongated fruits that split open to release seeds. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland to montane forest areas.

The species is valued for its timber and has some traditional medicinal uses.
