Piper warakabacoura

Piper warakabacoura, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper vine, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its climbing habit, with opposite, ovate leaves that are often hairy. It produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by dark blue berries.

The plant is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value, though it can become invasive in certain environments.
