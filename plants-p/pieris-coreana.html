Pieris coreana

Pieris coreana, commonly known as the Korean pieris, is a species of evergreen shrub native to Korea.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, leathery foliage. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped, white to pale pink flowers in early spring. Its leaves are typically oval with a slightly wavy margin and a glossy texture.

Pieris coreana thrives in acidic, well-drained soil and is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value.
