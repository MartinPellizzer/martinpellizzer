Pleurothallis peculiaris

Pleurothallis peculiaris, commonly known as the peculiar orchid, is a species native to the cloud forests of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, often fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees without deriving nutrients from them. Its flowers are usually small, with intricate patterns and colors that range from white to pale pink.

Pleurothallis peculiaris is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in shaded, humid environments.
