Phyllanthus myrtilloides subsp. myrtilloides

Phyllanthus myrtilloides subsp.

myrtilloides, commonly known as the small-leaved spurge, is a species of flowering plant in the family Euphorbiaceae. It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are usually arranged oppositely along the stem.

The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in cyathia, typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. It is native to parts of Africa and is often found in open, disturbed habitats such as fields and roadsides.
