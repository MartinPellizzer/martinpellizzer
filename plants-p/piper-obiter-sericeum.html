Piper obiter-sericeum

Piper obiter-sericeum, commonly known as the silky pepper plant, is a species of flowering plant in the Piperaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and small, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs. Its flowers are unisexual, with male and female flowers occurring on separate plants, and it produces small, round berries that are typically green when mature.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its unique foliage.
