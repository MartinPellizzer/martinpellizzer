Ptelea neglecta

Ptelea neglecta, commonly known as the slenderleaf sand cherry, is a deciduous shrub native to the southwestern United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, oval leaves. The plant produces clusters of white to pink flowers in early spring, which are followed by small, red berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Ptelea neglecta is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
