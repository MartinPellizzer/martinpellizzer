Petrocoptis lagascae

Petrocoptis lagascae, commonly known as the rock sedge, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with slender, cylindrical stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in mountainous or semi-arid environments. Its leaves are narrow and tough, adapted to withstand harsh environmental conditions.

Petrocoptis lagascae plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat and is of interest to botanists studying alpine flora.
