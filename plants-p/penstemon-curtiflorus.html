Penstemon curtiflorus

Penstemon curtiflorus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved beardtongue, is a perennial plant native to the western United States.

It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is found in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant features upright stems with lance-shaped leaves and produces tall spikes of tubular flowers that range in color from pink to purple. It is valued for its drought tolerance and ornamental appeal in gardens.

Penstemon curtiflorus is also an important nectar source for native bees and butterflies.
