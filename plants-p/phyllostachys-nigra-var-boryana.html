Phyllostachys nigra var. boryana

Phyllostachys nigra var. boryana, commonly known as the black bamboo, is a variety of bamboo characterized by its dark, almost black culms that darken with age.

It is native to China and is prized for its ornamental value and ease of cultivation. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are a deep green color, providing a striking contrast to its dark stems. It grows in clumps and can reach heights of up to 10 meters, making it a dramatic addition to gardens.

This bamboo is cold-hardy and thrives in full sun to partial shade, making it adaptable to a variety of climates.
