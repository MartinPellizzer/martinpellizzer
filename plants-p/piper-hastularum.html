Piper hastularum

Piper hastularum, commonly known as the Mexican pepper plant, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to Central America and parts of the southern United States. The plant is characterized by its trailing vines and small, heart-shaped leaves. It produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into bright red berries.

The berries are often used in traditional medicine and as a spice due to their pungent flavor.
