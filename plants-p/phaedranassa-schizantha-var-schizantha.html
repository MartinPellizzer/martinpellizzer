Phaedranassa schizantha var. schizantha

Phaedranassa schizantha var. schizantha, commonly known as the purple passionflower, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant purple and orange flowers, which are highly attractive to pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has compound leaves with five to seven lobes, and its stems are woody and tend to twine around supports. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

The flowers are not only ornamental but also have been used in traditional medicine for their potential health benefits.
