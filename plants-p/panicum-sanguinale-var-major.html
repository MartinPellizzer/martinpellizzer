Panicum sanguinale var. major

Panicum sanguinale var. major, commonly known as red panicgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, erect growth habit and its ability to thrive in full sun to partial shade. The plant produces feathery, reddish-brown inflorescences that are distinctive and often used in ornamental gardening. Its leaves are narrow and have a rough texture, contributing to its rustic appearance.

This variety is drought-tolerant and prefers well-drained soils, making it a hardy choice for landscaping in warmer climates.
