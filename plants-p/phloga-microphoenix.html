Phloga microphoenix

Phloga microphoenix, commonly known as the golden flame orchid, is a striking member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, where it thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant is characterized by its vibrant orange and yellow petals, which resemble a flame, giving it its common name. Its flowers are typically small but numerous, arranged in dense clusters that attract various pollinators.

Botanically, it has a complex root system and is known for its ability to store water, which helps it survive in its natural habitat.
