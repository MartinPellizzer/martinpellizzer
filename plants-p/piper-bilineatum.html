Piper bilineatum

Piper bilineatum, commonly known as the two-lined pepper or bilineate pepper, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many species of pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves with a distinct two-colored vein pattern, giving it its common name. It produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by elongated, fleshy berries.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its unique foliage and ability to thrive in humid, shaded environments.
