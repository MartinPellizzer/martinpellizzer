Prunus incisa f. serrata

Prunus incisa f. serrata, commonly known as the Japanese cherry plum.

This plant is a compact shrub or small tree native to Japan, characterized by its delicate pink flowers that bloom in spring. The leaves are ovate with serrated edges, giving it the specific epithet "serrata.". It produces small, edible fruits that are often used in jams and preserves.

The plant is valued in horticulture for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens and parks.
