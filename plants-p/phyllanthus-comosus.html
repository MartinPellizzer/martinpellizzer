Phyllanthus comosus

Phyllanthus comosus, commonly known as the Christmas star or starfruit plant, is a flowering shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, star-shaped fruits that are edible and used in various culinary applications. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in dense clusters, which develop into the characteristic star-shaped fruits. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a rough texture with prominent veins.

Phyllanthus comosus is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional medicine.
