Paracaryum gorganicum

Paracaryum gorganicum, commonly known as the golden spurge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Africa.

It features succulent, fleshy leaves that are typically green with a yellowish or golden hue, which gives it its common name. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, often blooming in the spring. It is adapted to arid environments, storing water in its leaves and stems to survive dry conditions.

Paracaryum gorganicum is often used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive appearance and hardy nature.
