Populus cathayana

Populus cathayana, commonly known as the Chinese poplar, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It belongs to the Salicaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive heart-shaped leaves and showy, fluffy flowers. The tree typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in temperate climates. It is often found in mountainous regions and is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance.

Populus cathayana plays a significant role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
