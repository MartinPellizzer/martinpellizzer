Pimpinella stocksii

Pimpinella stocksii, commonly known as the yellow bishop's hat, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers that resemble a bishop's hat, which bloom in late summer. The plant has feathery, finely divided leaves that are aromatic and often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Pimpinella stocksii is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to pollinators.
