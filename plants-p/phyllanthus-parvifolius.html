Phyllanthus parvifolius

Phyllanthus parvifolius, commonly known as the small-leaved phyllanthus, is a tropical shrub belonging to the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is native to regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific Islands, often found in moist, shaded environments. The plant features small, elliptical leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface, and it produces tiny, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters. Its fruit is a capsule that contains several seeds, which are dispersed by wind or water.

Phyllanthus parvifolius is also noted for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.
