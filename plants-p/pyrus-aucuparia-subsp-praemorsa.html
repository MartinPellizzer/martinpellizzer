Pyrus aucuparia subsp. praemorsa

Pyrus aucuparia subsp.

praemorsa, commonly known as the whitebeam, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually oval to elliptic in shape, with a smooth or slightly serrated margin.

The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clusters, which are followed by edible, sweet-tart berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or hedgerows.

This subspecies is notable for its ornamental value and historical use in horticulture and traditional medicine.
