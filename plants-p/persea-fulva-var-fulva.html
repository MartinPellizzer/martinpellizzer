Persea fulva var. fulva

Persea fulva var. fulva, commonly known as the yellow avocado or yellow custard apple, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its smooth, dark green, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, yellowish-green flowers. The fruit is a pear-shaped berry with a thick, yellow or orange skin and a creamy, aromatic flesh. It is valued for its edible fruit, which is rich in nutrients and has a unique, sweet, custard-like flavor.

The plant is also cultivated for its ornamental value and has cultural significance in various regions.
