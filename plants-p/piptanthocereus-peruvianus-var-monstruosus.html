Piptanthocereus peruvianus var. monstruosus

Piptanthocereus peruvianus var. monstruosus, commonly known as the Peruvian apple cactus, is a unique cactus species native to the Andes Mountains.

It is characterized by its swollen, fleshy stems that resemble apples, which store water and nutrients. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in the evening, adding a striking visual element to its appearance. Its leaves are modified into spines, which help reduce water loss and provide protection from herbivores.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in arid gardens for its unusual form and resilience.
