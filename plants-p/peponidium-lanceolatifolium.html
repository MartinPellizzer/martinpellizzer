Peponidium lanceolatifolium

Peponidium lanceolatifolium, commonly known as the lanceolate pumpkin.

This plant is a member of the Cucurbitaceae family, characterized by its trailing growth habit and large, lobed leaves. It produces yellow-orange flowers that give way to elongated, oval fruits with a rough, hairy surface. The fruit is typically harvested when it turns a deep orange color, indicating maturity.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions and is often cultivated for its edible fruit.
