Phyteuma pauciflorum f. simplex

Phyteuma pauciflorum f. simplex, commonly known as the simple-flowered phyteuma, is a compact perennial herb native to southern Europe.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink to purple. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders.

Its ornamental value lies in its delicate appearance and ease of cultivation.
