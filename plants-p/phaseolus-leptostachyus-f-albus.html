Phaseolus leptostachyus f. albus

Phaseolus leptostachyus f. albus, commonly known as the white runner bean, is a flowering plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, trailing vines and large, heart-shaped leaves. The flowers are typically white or pale pink, and the pods are elongated, containing edible seeds. This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often grown in gardens for both its beauty and culinary use.

It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil and full sun for optimal growth.
