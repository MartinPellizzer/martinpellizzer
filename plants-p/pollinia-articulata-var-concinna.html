Pollinia articulata var. concinna

Pollinia articulata var. concinna, commonly known as the slender pollinia, is a variant of the pollinia species characterized by its unique floral structure.

It is a flowering plant with small, clustered inflorescences that produce fragrant, tubular flowers. The plant is native to specific regions in South America and thrives in moist, shaded environments. Its pollination mechanism involves specialized pollinaria that adhere to the bodies of visiting insects.

The botanical features of this variant make it an interesting subject for study in plant morphology and pollination biology.
