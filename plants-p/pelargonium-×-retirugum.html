Pelargonium × retirugum

Pelargonium × retirugum, commonly known as the hybrid geranium, is a species that results from the crossbreeding of various pelargonium species.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, colorful flowers that bloom in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant typically has serrated, lobed leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden beds and containers.

Due to its ornamental value, it is widely cultivated for both decorative and medicinal purposes.
