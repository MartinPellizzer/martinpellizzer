Pholidia gibbifolia

Pholidia gibbifolia, commonly known as the roughleaf pittosporum, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its leathery, often rough-textured leaves and fragrant white or yellow flowers. The plant produces small, fleshy berries that are typically orange or red in color. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in coastal areas or disturbed soils.

Pholidia gibbifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping due to its hardy nature.
