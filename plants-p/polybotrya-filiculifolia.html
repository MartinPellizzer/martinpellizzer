Polybotrya filiculifolia

Polybotrya filiculifolia, commonly known as the feather-leaf lichen, is a foliose lichen that thrives in moist, shaded environments.

It features a richly branched thallus with a distinctive, leaf-like structure that resembles fern fronds. The plant's vegetative body is composed of a symbiotic association between algae and fungi, which allows it to absorb nutrients from the substrate. It is typically found on tree trunks and rocks in temperate forest regions.

Its slow growth and unique morphology make it an important indicator species for environmental health.
