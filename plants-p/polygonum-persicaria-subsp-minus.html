Polygonum persicaria subsp. minus

Polygonum persicaria subsp.

minus, commonly known as narrowleaf smartweed, is a herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere. It typically grows in moist soils along stream banks, wet meadows, and disturbed areas.

The plant features slender, ascending stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. Its small, clustered flowers are usually pink or red, blooming in late summer.

The species is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of wetland habitats.
