Phaseolus macfadyenii

Phaseolus macfadyenii, commonly known as the MacFadyen bean, is a climbing legume native to Mexico and Central America.

It produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, typically yellow or brown in color. The plant has compound leaves with three leaflets and is characterized by its hairy stems and flowers. It is adapted to grow in dry, tropical environments and is often found in disturbed habitats.

This species is of interest to botanists and horticulturists due to its unique morphology and potential for agricultural use.
