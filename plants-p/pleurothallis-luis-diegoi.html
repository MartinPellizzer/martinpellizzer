Pleurothallis luis-diegoi

Pleurothallis luis-diegoi, commonly known as Luis-Diego's orchid, is a species native to the cloud forests of Ecuador.

It is a small epiphytic orchid with intricate, lance-shaped leaves that are typically green with a waxy texture. The plant produces delicate, fragrant flowers in shades of white and yellow, often clustered in small inflorescences. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining substrates for optimal growth.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
