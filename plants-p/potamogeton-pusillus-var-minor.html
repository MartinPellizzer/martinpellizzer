Potamogeton pusillus var. minor

Potamogeton pusillus var. minor, commonly known as the small pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in North America.

It typically grows in shallow, slow-moving waters and is characterized by its small, narrow leaves arranged in whorls around the stem. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often submerged, followed by tiny, boat-shaped fruits. It plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.

Due to its rapid growth, it can become invasive in some water bodies if not managed properly.
