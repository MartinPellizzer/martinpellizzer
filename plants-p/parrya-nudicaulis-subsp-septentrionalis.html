Parrya nudicaulis subsp. septentrionalis

Parrya nudicaulis subsp.

septentrionalis, commonly known as the northern parrya, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is a low-growing perennial with small, succulent leaves that are typically clustered at the base.

The species produces white to pale pink flowers in late spring, which are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.

This subspecies is often found in mountainous regions and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological resilience.
