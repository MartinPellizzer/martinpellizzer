Pandanus undulifolius

Pandanus undulifolius, commonly known as the bottle palm, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, fan-shaped leaves that are often used in traditional crafts. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that develop into edible fruits, which are consumed both fresh and dried. Its unique morphology and cultural significance make it an important species in island ecosystems.

The leaves are also used for thatching and weaving, highlighting its versatility in traditional practices.
