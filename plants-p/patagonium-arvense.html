Patagonium arvense

Patagonium arvense, commonly known as the field gentian, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the family Gentianaceae and is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. It has lance-shaped leaves and produces small, tubular flowers that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Patagonium arvense is known for its adaptability to various environmental conditions.
