Polygonum floridum

Polygonum floridum, commonly known as the Florida smartweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its upright stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds.

Polygonum floridum is a member of the buckwheat family (Polygonaceae) and is known for its adaptability to various wetland environments.
