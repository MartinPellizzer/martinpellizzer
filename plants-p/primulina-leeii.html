Primulina leeii

Primulina leeii, commonly known as the Lee's gloxinia, is a species of flowering plant native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Gesneriaceae family and is characterized by its attractive, tubular flowers that come in shades of pink, red, and white. The plant typically grows as a rhizomatous perennial, with heart-shaped leaves that are glossy and dark green.

It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its vibrant blooms and ease of care.
