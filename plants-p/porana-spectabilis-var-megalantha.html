Porana spectabilis var. megalantha

Porana spectabilis var. megalantha, commonly known as the giant porana, is a striking plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, broad leaves that can reach up to 1 meter in length, often with a deep green color and prominent veins. The plant produces showy, clustered inflorescences with small, white to pale yellow flowers that attract pollinators. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and requires high humidity and indirect sunlight.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
