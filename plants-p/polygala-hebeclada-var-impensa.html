Polygala hebeclada var. impensa

Polygala hebeclada var. impensa, commonly known as the narrow-leaved milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Polygalaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in arid or semi-arid environments. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, and the flowers are usually pale yellow or greenish-white.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and delicate blooms.
