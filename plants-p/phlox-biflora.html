Phlox biflora

Phlox biflora, commonly known as the two-flowered phlox, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Polemoniaceae family and is characterized by its showy, clustered flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 12 to 24 inches and features narrow, grass-like leaves. Its flowers are usually pink to purple in color and are arranged in inflorescences that give it a distinctive appearance.

Phlox biflora thrives in partial shade to full sun and is often found in moist, woodland habitats.
