Pyrus salicifolia var. amygdaliformis

Pyrus salicifolia var. amygdaliformis, commonly known as the almond-leaved pear, is a variant of the pear tree characterized by its distinctive foliage.

Its leaves are elongated and lance-shaped, resembling those of the willow, with a fine, serrated edge. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in early spring, which are followed by edible, sweet fruits. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit yield.

This variety is relatively hardy and adaptable to various soil types, making it a popular choice for gardens and orchards.
