Pothuava

Pothuava, commonly known as the Indian nettle, is a herbaceous plant native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the Urticaceae family and is characterized by its stinging hairs that cause skin irritation upon contact. The plant typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, with opposite, ovate leaves that have serrated edges. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The flowering stems bear small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences.
