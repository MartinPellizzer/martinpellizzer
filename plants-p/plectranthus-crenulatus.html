Plectranthus crenulatus

Plectranthus crenulatus, commonly known as the African violet or Cape violet, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its velvety, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from purple to pink. The plant thrives in well-draining soil and requires bright, indirect light to flourish. It is often cultivated as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and ease of care.

Plectranthus crenulatus is also valued for its aromatic foliage, which can be used in herbal remedies.
