Peltogyne parvifolia var. glabra

Peltogyne parvifolia var. glabra, commonly known as the smooth-leaved ironwood, is a tropical tree native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, pale bark and small, elliptical leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the branches. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by hard, woody fruits containing several seeds. It is valued for its durable wood and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ecological and economic importance, it is also a subject of conservation interest.
