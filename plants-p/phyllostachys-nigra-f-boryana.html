Phyllostachys nigra f. boryana

Phyllostachys nigra f. boryana, commonly known as the black bamboo, is a variant of the black bamboo species native to China.

It is characterized by its dark, almost black culms that are often covered in a thin layer of white powder. The plant produces narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically a deep green color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full sunlight.

This ornamental bamboo is popular in gardens for its striking appearance and ease of cultivation.
