Panargyrus uniflorus

Panargyrus uniflorus, commonly known as the single-flower sungrass, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces a single, showy yellow flower at the top of its stem. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are adapted to conserve water in its harsh environment. Its flowers bloom during the dry season, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Panargyrus uniflorus is often used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements and ornamental value.
