Ptychosperma buabe

Ptychosperma buabe, commonly known as the Bornean palm, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia, particularly Borneo.

It is a large, herbaceous plant that can grow up to several meters in height, characterized by its broad, feather-like leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in a spike, followed by clustered, edible fruits that are used in local cuisine. Its root system is fibrous and adapted to thrive in wet, swampy environments.

Ptychosperma buabe plays an important ecological role in its habitat, providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
