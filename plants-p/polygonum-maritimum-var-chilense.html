Polygonum maritimum var. chilense

Polygonum maritimum var. chilense, commonly known as Chilean knotweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America, particularly Chile.

It is characterized by its robust, sprawling growth habit and its ability to thrive in coastal and saline environments. The plant features alternate, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, clustered flowers in shades of pink to purple. It is adapted to withstand harsh conditions, including salt spray and strong winds, making it a common sight along coastal dunes and rocky shores.

Its ecological importance includes stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various insects and small animals.
