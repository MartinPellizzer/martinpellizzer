Poa sudetica var. remota

Poa sudetica var. remota, commonly known as the remote mountain grass, is a variant of the sudetic grass species.

It is a perennial grass that typically grows in alpine and subalpine regions, characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It is adapted to cold climates and is often found in rocky or mountainous terrain.

Its botanical structure reflects its ecological niche, making it a resilient species in harsh environments.
