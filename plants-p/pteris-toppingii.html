Pteris toppingii

Pteris toppingii, commonly known as the Topping's water fern, is a species of fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that are typically arching or creeping in growth habit. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, making it a popular choice for tropical and subtropical gardens. Its spores are produced on the underside of the fronds, and it reproduces primarily through spore dispersal.

The leaves are often used in traditional medicine and horticulture due to their ornamental value and adaptability.
