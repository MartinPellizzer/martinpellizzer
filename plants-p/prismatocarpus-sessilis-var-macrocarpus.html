Prismatocarpus sessilis var. macrocarpus

Prismatocarpus sessilis var. macrocarpus, commonly known as the large-fruited prismo, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree characterized by its smooth, pale grey bark and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers followed by distinctive, elongated fruits that are typically yellow or orange when mature. Its botanical features make it a valuable species for ornamental horticulture and traditional medicine.

The fruit is edible and has a mild, sweet flavor, often used in local cuisines.
