Protium brasiliense var. subserratum

Protium brasiliense var. subserratum, commonly known as the Brazilian wild pepper, is a deciduous tree native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It belongs to the family Sapotaceae and is characterized by its smooth, grayish-bark and elliptical leaves with serrated margins. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by orange-red, fleshy berries containing a single seed.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
