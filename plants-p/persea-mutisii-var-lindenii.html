Persea mutisii var. lindenii

Persea mutisii var. lindenii, commonly known as the Mexican persimmon, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Central America.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and fragrant, white flowers. The plant produces small, orange-yellow fruits that are edible when fully ripe. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is valued for its ornamental and culinary uses.
