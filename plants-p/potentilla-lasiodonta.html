Potentilla lasiodonta

Potentilla lasiodonta, commonly known as the woolly cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its hairy, grayish stems and leaves that are typically arranged in a spiral pattern. The plant produces clusters of small, yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its foliage is often covered in a dense layer of fine, white hairs, giving it a woolly appearance.

Potentilla lasiodonta is adapted to rocky and arid environments, making it a hardy species found in alpine and subalpine habitats.
