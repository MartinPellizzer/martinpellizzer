Polygonum bodinieri

Polygonum bodinieri, commonly known as the Chinese knotweed, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It features rhizomatous growth and can form dense colonies, often invading disturbed soils. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, clustered flowers in late summer. Its botanical characteristics make it both ecologically significant and a potential weed in agricultural settings.

It is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
