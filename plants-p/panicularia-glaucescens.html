Panicularia glaucescens

Panicularia glaucescens, commonly known as blue panicgrass, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, grass-like leaves that are often tinged with a bluish hue. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along the edges of ponds.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and restoration projects.
