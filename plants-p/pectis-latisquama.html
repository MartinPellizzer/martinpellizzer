Pectis latisquama

Pectis latisquama, commonly known as the broad-scale pectis, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant is distinguished by its large, showy yellow flowers that bloom in summer. Its leaves are typically linear and arranged in a rosette pattern, and it often grows in rocky or sandy soils.
