Pithecellobium leucophyllum

Pithecellobium leucophyllum, commonly known as the white-leaf pithecellobium, is a species of legume native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its delicate, trifoliate leaves and small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, edible pods containing seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories or along riverbanks.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
