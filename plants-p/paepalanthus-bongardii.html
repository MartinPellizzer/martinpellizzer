Paepalanthus bongardii

Paepalanthus bongardii, commonly known as the Bongard's pincushion, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the family Cactaceae and is characterized by its unique, spiny, spherical stems that resemble a pincushion. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, adding a striking visual contrast to its dense, thorny structure. It is adapted to arid environments and thrives in well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and private collections.
