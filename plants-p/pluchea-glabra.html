Pluchea glabra

Pluchea glabra, commonly known as white cudweed, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as grasslands and wetlands. Its leaves are hairy and alternate, while the flowers are small and white or pale yellow.

Pluchea glabra is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
