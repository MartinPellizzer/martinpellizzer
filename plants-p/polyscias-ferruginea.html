Polyscias ferruginea

Polyscias ferruginea, commonly known as the rusty-leaf sycamore, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It features palmately lobed leaves that are typically dark green with a reddish or rusty-colored underside, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by orange to red berries that are attractive to birds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often used in ornamental horticulture for its aesthetic foliage.

Polyscias ferruginea is also valued for its hardy nature and ability to tolerate various soil types.
