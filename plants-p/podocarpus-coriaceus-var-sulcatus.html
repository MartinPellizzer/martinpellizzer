Podocarpus coriaceus var. sulcatus

Podocarpus coriaceus var. sulcatus, commonly known as the Bursera shrub, is a species native to tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its small, leathery leaves that are often arranged in whorls around the stems. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically greenish-yellow in color. Its fruit is a capsule that releases tiny seeds when mature.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping due to its hardy nature and ability to thrive in diverse soil conditions.
