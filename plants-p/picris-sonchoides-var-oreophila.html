Picris sonchoides var. oreophila

Picris sonchoides var. oreophila, commonly known as the alpine groundsel, is a flowering plant native to mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in cool, moist environments at high elevations. Its leaves are usually hairy and alternate, while the flowers are yellow with a central disc.

It plays a role in supporting local pollinators and is often found in meadows and slopes.
