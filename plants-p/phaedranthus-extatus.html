Phaedranthus extatus

Phaedranthus extatus, commonly known as the golden bellflower, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its striking yellow-orange flowers that resemble small bells, which bloom in late summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Its ornamental value and drought tolerance make it a favored plant among gardeners.
