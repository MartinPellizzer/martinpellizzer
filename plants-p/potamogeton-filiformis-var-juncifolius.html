Potamogeton filiformis var. juncifolius

Potamogeton filiformis var. juncifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaf pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in North America.

It has long, slender, ribbon-like leaves that are typically less than 1 millimeter wide, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually arranged in spikes, and it relies on water currents for pollination. It thrives in shallow, slow-moving waters and plays a vital role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.

Its presence is often an indicator of good water quality in its habitat.
