Pyrus megalocarpa

Pyrus megalocarpa, commonly known as the giant crabapple, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that bloom in spring, typically in shades of white or pink. The tree produces edible fruits, though they are generally smaller and less flavorful compared to other crabapple varieties. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a serrated margin.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
