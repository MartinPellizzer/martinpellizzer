Pleurothallis pellucida

Pleurothallis pellucida, commonly known as the glassy orchid, is a species native to the cloud forests of Central and South America.

It is a small, epiphytic orchid that typically grows on trees in humid, shaded environments. The plant features delicate, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, white or pale green flowers with a subtle fragrance. Its name derives from the translucent, waxy appearance of its petals and sepals.

Pleurothallis pellucida is valued in horticulture for its unique aesthetic and adaptability to indoor growing conditions.
