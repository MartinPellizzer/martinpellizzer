Phoebe erythropus

Phoebe erythropus, commonly known as the red-leaf phoebe, is a species of flowering plant native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae, which includes laurels and cinnamon trees. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that turn reddish in the fall, giving it its common name. It produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow.
