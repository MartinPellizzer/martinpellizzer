Pelea haleakalana

Pelea haleakalana, commonly known as the Haleakala pelea, is a rare Hawaiian plant species endemic to the island of Maui.

It is a flowering plant belonging to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its delicate, fragrant blossoms. The plant thrives in the unique microclimates of the Haleakala National Park, typically found in rocky crevices and shaded areas. Due to its limited habitat and threats from invasive species, it is considered endangered.

Conservation efforts are ongoing to protect and propagate this rare botanical treasure.
