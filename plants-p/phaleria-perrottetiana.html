Phaleria perrottetiana

Phaleria perrottetiana, commonly known as the Java laurel, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae and is characterized by its smooth, dark-barked trunk and dense, glossy green foliage. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by spherical, black berries. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in landscaping due to its hardy nature.
