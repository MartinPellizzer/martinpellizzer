Phlox pilosa subsp. longipilosa

Phlox pilosa subsp.

longipilosa, commonly known as the long-haired phlox, is a perennial flowering plant native to the eastern United States. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy clusters of pink to lavender flowers that bloom in early summer.

The plant features dense, gray-green foliage with long, soft hairs that give it its common name. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

Its botanical features make it an attractive and hardy addition to native plant gardens.
