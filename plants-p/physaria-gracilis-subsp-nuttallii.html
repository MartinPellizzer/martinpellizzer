Physaria gracilis subsp. nuttallii

Physaria gracilis subsp.

nuttallii, commonly known as Nuttall's bladderpod, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and capsule fruits.

The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and has hairy, erect stems. Its leaves are alternate and linear, often with a bluish-green hue.

The subspecies is distinguished by its slightly different morphology compared to the typical form of Physaria gracilis.
