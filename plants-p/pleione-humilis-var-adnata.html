Pleione humilis var. adnata

Pleione humilis var. adnata, commonly known as the Adnate Pleione, is a rare and elegant orchid native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

This terrestrial orchid features a pseudobulb-like structure with narrow, grass-like leaves and produces delicate, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink. It typically blooms in late winter to early spring, adding a touch of beauty to its natural habitat. The plant prefers cool, humid environments and is often found growing in shaded, forested areas.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is a subject of interest for botanists and orchid enthusiasts alike.
