Pinus carica

Pinus carica, commonly known as the Canary Island pine, is a species of pine native to the Canary Islands and Madeira.

It is a medium-sized evergreen tree with a straight trunk and a broad, umbrella-like crown. The leaves are needle-like, typically arranged in pairs, and have a bluish-green color with a white stomatal band. It produces cones that are woody and contain edible seeds, which are often used in traditional cuisine.

This plant is adapted to dry, coastal environments and is valued for its ornamental and ecological significance.
