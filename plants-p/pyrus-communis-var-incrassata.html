Pyrus communis var. incrassata

Pyrus communis var. incrassata, commonly known as the Crassifolia pear, is a variety of the common pear characterized by its thick, fleshy fruit.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia and is known for its large, juicy, and aromatic fruits. The plant typically grows as a small to medium-sized tree with dense, dark green leaves. It produces white flowers in the spring, which are followed by the distinctive, edible pears.

This variety is valued for both its ornamental appeal and its culinary uses.
