Prasophyllum canaliculatum

Prasophyllum canaliculatum, commonly known as the narrow-lobed leek orchid, is an endemic plant species found in the southern regions of Australia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its slender, grass-like foliage and small, greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in open, sandy or rocky habitats, often in heathlands or woodlands. It is a perennial species that relies on specific mycorrhizal fungi for nutrient uptake, which is a common trait among orchids.

Due to habitat loss and environmental changes, it is considered vulnerable and requires conservation efforts to ensure its survival.
