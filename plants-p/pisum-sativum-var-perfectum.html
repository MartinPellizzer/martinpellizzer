Pisum sativum var. perfectum

Pisum sativum var. perfectum, commonly known as the garden pea, is a variety of the pea plant that is widely cultivated for its edible pods and seeds.

It is a leguminous plant belonging to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its climbing habit and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, round pods that contain several seeds, which are rich in protein and nutrients. It thrives in cool climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, benefiting subsequent crops.
