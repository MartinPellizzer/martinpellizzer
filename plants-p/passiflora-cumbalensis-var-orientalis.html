Passiflora cumbalensis var. orientalis

Passiflora cumbalensis var. orientalis, commonly known as the Eastern Cumbal passionflower, is a climbing vine native to parts of South America.

It is characterized by its showy, complex flowers that feature a blend of blue, purple, and white petals, along with a distinctive crown of filaments. The plant produces edible berries that are often consumed raw or used in culinary applications. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, requiring well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and botanical collections.
