Pachyphyllum subbiflorum

Pachyphyllum subbiflorum, commonly known as the desert rose or false rose, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its fleshy, thick leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in dry environments. The plant produces striking, showy flowers in shades of pink and red, which bloom during the cooler months. Its growth habit is typically low and sprawling, with a rosette formation that helps reduce water loss.

Pachyphyllum subbiflorum is well-suited for rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental appeal.
