Prunus laurocerasus var. angustifolia

Prunus laurocerasus var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved cherry laurel, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Europe.

It features dark green, lance-shaped leaves that turn yellow in autumn. The plant produces small, white flowers in spring, followed by dark purple berries. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping.

However, it is toxic to humans and animals, requiring caution in cultivation.
