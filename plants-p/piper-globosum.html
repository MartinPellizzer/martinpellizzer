Piper globosum

Piper globosum, commonly known as the globular pepper or African pepper, is a climbing shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and small, greenish-white flowers. The plant produces tiny, peppery fruits that are often used in traditional medicine and cuisine. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and cultural contexts.
