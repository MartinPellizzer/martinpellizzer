Petasites versipilus

Petasites versipilus, commonly known as the yellow meadow saxifrage, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Saxifragaceae family.

It is characterized by its rosette of broadly ovate, hairy leaves that are typically yellow-green in color. The plant produces small, clustered flowers with five petals, usually pink or white, that bloom in early summer. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.

Petasites versipilus is well adapted to temperate climates and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
