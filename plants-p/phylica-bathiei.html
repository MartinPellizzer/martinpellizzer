Phylica bathiei

Phylica bathiei, commonly known as the Natal bottlebrush, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive cylindrical inflorescences, which resemble a bottlebrush due to their dense arrangement of small, red or pink flowers. The plant typically grows in coastal regions and is adapted to sandy soils and saline conditions. It is often found in the fynbos biome, where it contributes to the region's rich biodiversity.

Phylica bathiei is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
