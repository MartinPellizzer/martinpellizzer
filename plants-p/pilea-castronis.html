Pilea castronis

Pilea castronis, commonly known as the Chinese money plant, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to China.

It features rosettes of thick, waxy, rounded leaves that resemble coins, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers that appear in the spring. It thrives in well-draining soil and indirect light, making it a popular houseplant.

Its ability to propagate through leaf cuttings adds to its appeal among indoor gardeners.
