Pandanus rotumaensis

Pandanus rotumaensis, commonly known as the Rotuma pandanus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Pandanaceae.

It is native to the island of Rotuma in the South Pacific and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and long, narrow leaves. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences that bear yellowish-white flowers, which are followed by distinctive, spiky fruits. It thrives in coastal and lowland areas, often growing in sandy or limestone soils.

This species is valued for its ornamental appearance and has cultural significance in the local communities of Rotuma.
