Potentilla × okaensis

Potentilla × okaensis, commonly known as the Japanese cinquefoil, is a hybrid shrub native to Japan.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit. The plant features bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically oval-shaped with serrated edges, and it thrives in well-drained soils.

Potentilla × okaensis is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
