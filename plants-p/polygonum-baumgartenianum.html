Polygonum baumgartenianum

Polygonum baumgartenianum, commonly known as the Baumgarten's knotweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, which includes various species of knotweeds and buckwheats. The plant typically grows in moist environments such as wetlands and stream banks, where it thrives in partial to full shade. Its leaves are ovate to lanceolate, with a distinctive reddish or purple coloration on the underside, and it produces small, clustered greenish flowers.

Polygonum baumgartenianum is often found in riparian zones and is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits.
