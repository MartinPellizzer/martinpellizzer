Persea teneriffae

Persea teneriffae, commonly known as the Teneriffe persimmon, is a small evergreen tree native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers followed by edible, orange-red berries that are rich in nutrients. It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is also cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
