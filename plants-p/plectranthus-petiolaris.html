Plectranthus petiolaris

Plectranthus petiolaris, commonly known as the purple-leaf plectranthus, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant features opposite, ovate to lance-shaped leaves that are often purple or reddish in color, contrasting with its green veins. It produces small, tubular flowers that are usually pale purple or white, arranged in clusters.

Plectranthus petiolaris is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens or as a houseplant.
