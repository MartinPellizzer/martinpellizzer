Polygonatum canaliculatum var. ovatum

Polygonatum canaliculatum var. ovatum, commonly known as the ovate Solomon's seal, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern Asia.

It features slender, arching stems that bear clusters of small, greenish-white flowers in late spring. The plant produces oval-shaped berries that are typically reddish when mature. Its leaves are broad, lance-shaped, and have a smooth texture.

It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.
