Pilea craspedodroma

Pilea craspedodroma, commonly known as the Chinese money plant, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is native to China and is characterized by its rosette of thick, waxy, succulent leaves that resemble small coins. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. It is a popular houseplant due to its ease of care and attractive foliage.

Pilea craspedodroma prefers bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it suitable for indoor cultivation.
