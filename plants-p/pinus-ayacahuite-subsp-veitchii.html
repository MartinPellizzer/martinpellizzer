Pinus ayacahuite subsp. veitchii

Pinus ayacahuite subsp.

veitchii, commonly known as the Veitch's Mexican pine, is a rare and critically endangered subspecies native to a small region in central Mexico. It is a coniferous evergreen tree characterized by its long, slender needles and distinctive, often twisted cones.

The plant thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and requires a cool, temperate climate with moderate rainfall. Due to habitat loss and limited natural propagation, conservation efforts are crucial for its survival.

Its botanical significance lies in its unique morphology and role in the local ecosystem.
