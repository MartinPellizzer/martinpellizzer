Pelargonium parvipetalum

Pelargonium parvipetalum, commonly known as the small-petaled geranium, is a species native to South Africa.

It is a low-growing, mat-forming plant with fleshy, succulent leaves that are often hairy. The flowers are small, typically pink or purple, and appear in clusters during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
