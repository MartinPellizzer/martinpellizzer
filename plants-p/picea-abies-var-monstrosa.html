Picea abies var. monstrosa

Picea abies var. monstrosa, commonly known as the Norwegian giant spruce, is a variant of the Norway spruce characterized by its unusually large size and distinctive growth form.

It exhibits a slow growth rate, which contributes to its towering height and thick, dense foliage. The tree's needles are long, dark green, and often have a slight blue tinge, while its cones are typically larger than those of the standard Norway spruce. This variety is native to the mountainous regions of Scandinavia and is valued for its ornamental and ecological significance.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is a subject of interest in botanical conservation efforts.
