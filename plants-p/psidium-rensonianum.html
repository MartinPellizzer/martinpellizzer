Psidium rensonianum

Psidium rensonianum, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or red mombin, is a small evergreen tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and showy, fragrant white flowers. The tree produces small, round berries that turn from green to red when mature, containing a single seed. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and full sun exposure.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its edible fruits.
