Phrynium nicobaricum

Phrynium nicobaricum, commonly known as the Nicobar arrowhead, is a tropical plant native to the Andaman and Nicobar Islands.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows up to 1.5 meters in height, featuring broad, arrowhead-shaped leaves. The plant produces striking yellow or orange flowers that are clustered in panicles, often appearing in late spring to early summer. Its foliage is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory properties.

The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.
