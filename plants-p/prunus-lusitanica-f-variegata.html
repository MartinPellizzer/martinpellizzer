Prunus lusitanica f. variegata

Prunus lusitanica f. variegata, commonly known as the variegated Portuguese cherry, is a compact, deciduous shrub native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its striking variegated leaves, which display a combination of white, yellow, and green patterns. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers in early spring, followed by edible, dark red berries in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Due to its unique foliage, it is often used in landscape design to add visual interest.
