Primula oblanceolata

Primula oblanceolata, commonly known as the broad-leaved primrose, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its oval-shaped, often hairy leaves and clusters of bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically blooms in spring, producing shades of white, pink, and purple. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in rocky or forested areas.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
