Penthorum intermedium

Penthorum intermedium, commonly known as the common marsh bladdernut, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland areas of North America.

It features a basal rosette of heart-shaped, toothed leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clusters during the summer months. Its fruit is a three-lobed capsule that splits open to release seeds.

It plays an important ecological role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
