Polygonum pinetorum

Polygonum pinetorum, commonly known as the pine woods morning glory, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded areas such as pine woods and along stream banks. Its stems are often hairy and can climb or trail along the ground.

Polygonum pinetorum is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in woodland environments.
