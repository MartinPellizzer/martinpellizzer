Potentilla Nivea Subsp. Mischkinii

White cinquefoil, scientifically known as Potentilla nivea subsp. mischkinii, is a striking plant native to the high alpine regions of western North America. It is characterized by its delicate white flowers and silver-green leaves that shimmer in the sun. This subspecies thrives in harsh, rocky environments where few other plants can survive. Its beauty makes it a popular subject for botanists and nature enthusiasts alike. Despite its hardiness, it remains a rare and fragile species, highlighting the importance of conservation efforts in its natural habitat.
