Plumbago auriculata f. alba

Plumbago auriculata f. alba, commonly known as silverleaf plumbago, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring woody stems and lance-shaped leaves with a silvery underside. The plant produces clusters of small, star-shaped white flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its attractive foliage and long-lasting blooms, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
