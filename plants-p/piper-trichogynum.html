Piper trichogynum

Piper trichogynum, commonly known as the wahoo or wild pepper, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a climbing shrub that belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by yellowish fruits. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a rough texture.

The plant is known for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
