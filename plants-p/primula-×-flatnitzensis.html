Primula × flatnitzensis

Primula × flatnitzensis, commonly known as the flatnitz hybrid primrose, is a hybrid plant resulting from crosses between different Primula species.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers in a variety of colors, including white, pink, and purple. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking vibrant early-season color. It thrives in cool, moist environments and prefers partial to full shade, making it well-suited for woodland gardens or shaded borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long flowering period and ability to naturalize in suitable conditions.
