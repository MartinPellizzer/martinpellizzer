Piptadenia goetzei

Piptadenia goetzei, commonly known as the Brazilian trumpet tree, is a leguminous plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy yellow flowers that resemble trumpets, which bloom in clusters. The plant has compound leaves with long, slender leaflets and produces woody pods that contain flat, edible seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in forested areas.

Piptadenia goetzei is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
