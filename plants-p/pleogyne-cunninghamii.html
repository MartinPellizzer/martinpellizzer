Pleogyne cunninghamii

Pleogyne cunninghamii, commonly known as the Sydney pepper tree, is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree native to eastern Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and distinctive yellow flowers. The plant produces small, woody fruits that contain seeds, which are dispersed by animals. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and forested areas.

Pleogyne cunninghamii is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses by Indigenous communities.
