Phaseolus longepedunculatus var. angustifolius

Phaseolus longepedunculatus var. angustifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leafed runner bean, is a climbing legume native to South America.

It features slender, angular stems and heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive narrow foliage. The plant produces elongated pods that contain kidney-shaped seeds, which are typically white or yellow in color. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is valued for its edible beans and is often cultivated in home gardens.
