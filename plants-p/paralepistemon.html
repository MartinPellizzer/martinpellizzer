Paralepistemon

Paralepistemon, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its showy, blue to purple flower heads that resemble mist. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in desert or semi-desert environments. Paralepistemon has lance-shaped leaves and produces small, yellow disk flowers surrounded by blue ray florets.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.
