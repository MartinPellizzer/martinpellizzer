Pelargonium somalense

Pelargonium somalense, commonly known as the Somali geranium, is a flowering plant native to Somalia and neighboring regions.

It is characterized by its succulent, hairy leaves and showy, five-petaled flowers that range in color from pink to red. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to dry conditions. It belongs to the Geraniaceae family and is valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience.

Pelargonium somalense is often cultivated as a garden plant due to its hardiness and aesthetic value.
