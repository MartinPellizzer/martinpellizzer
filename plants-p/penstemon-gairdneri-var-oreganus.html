Penstemon gairdneri var. oreganus

Penstemon gairdneri var. oreganus, commonly known as the Oregon penstemon, is a flowering plant native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, rocky habitats such as cliffs and slopes. The plant features upright stems with lance-shaped leaves and produces showy, tubular flowers in shades of pink, purple, or white. It is valued for its ornamental beauty and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.

This variety is also noted for its ecological importance, providing nectar for pollinators like bees and butterflies.
