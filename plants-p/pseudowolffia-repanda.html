Pseudowolffia repanda

Pseudowolffia repanda, commonly known as the slender water fern, is a small aquatic plant belonging to the family Wolffia.

It is characterized by its tiny, floating leaves that are typically less than 1 centimeter in diameter. The plant lacks true roots and has a simple, branched stem system. It thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, often found in ponds and wetlands.

Pseudowolffia repanda plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for microorganisms and contributing to nutrient cycling.
