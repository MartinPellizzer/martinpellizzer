Prostanthera mulliganensis

Prostanthera mulliganensis, commonly known as the Mulligan's mint bush, is a small shrub native to the wet tropical rainforests of northeastern Australia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant features opposite, narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its small, tubular flowers are typically pale blue to purple and are arranged in dense clusters.

Prostanthera mulliganensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local biodiversity.
