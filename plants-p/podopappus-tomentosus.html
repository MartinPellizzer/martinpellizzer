Podopappus tomentosus

Podopappus tomentosus, commonly known as the woolly podopappus, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and daisy-like appearance. The plant features a low-growing, mat-forming structure with hairy, gray-green foliage. Its flowers are typically yellow and bloom in late spring to early summer.

Podopappus tomentosus is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and open plains.
