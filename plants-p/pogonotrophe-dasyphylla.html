Pogonotrophe dasyphylla

Pogonotrophe dasyphylla, commonly known as the woolly bladderwort, is a carnivorous plant native to wetlands and marshy areas.

It possesses small, bladder-like structures that trap and digest small aquatic organisms. The plant has finely divided, grass-like leaves that help in capturing prey. It thrives in nutrient-poor environments by supplementing its diet with captured prey.

Its unique botanical features make it an interesting subject for both ecological and botanical study.
