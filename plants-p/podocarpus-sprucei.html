Podocarpus sprucei

Podocarpus sprucei, commonly known as the silver fir or spruce pine, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Podocarpaceae and is characterized by its needle-like leaves and cone-bearing structure. The tree typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in mountainous areas with high humidity. Its foliage is dense and provides habitat for various wildlife species.

Podocarpus sprucei is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine and as a source of timber.
