Pseudoraphis minuta var. laevis

Pseudoraphis minuta var. laevis, commonly known as the silky pseudoraphis, is a succulent plant native to arid regions of Africa.

It features small, fleshy leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the warmer months. It is well adapted to dry environments, storing water in its thick leaves and stems.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and low maintenance requirements in xeriscaping.
