Pourouma scobina

Pourouma scobina, commonly known as the red-veined bamboo, is a member of the Poaceae family and is native to South America.

It is a tall, clump-forming grass that can reach heights of up to 10 meters. The plant is characterized by its striking red veins on its dark green leaves, which give it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

Its rapid growth and ornamental value make it a popular choice for landscaping in suitable environments.
