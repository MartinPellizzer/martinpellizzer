Pisum sativum var. procerum

Pisum sativum var. procerum, commonly known as the tall pea, is a cultivated variety of the common pea plant.

It is characterized by its elongated, upright growth habit and larger, more robust pods compared to standard peas. The plant typically produces a high yield of seeds, which are often used in culinary applications. Botanically, it belongs to the Fabaceae family and features compound leaves with small, stipitate leaflets.

Its flowers are usually white or pale purple, and it is an annual plant that thrives in temperate climates.
