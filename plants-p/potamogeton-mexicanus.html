Potamogeton mexicanus

Potamogeton mexicanus, commonly known as Mexican pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater habitats in Mexico.

It has long, narrow leaves that are typically arranged in whorls around the stem, and it produces small, green flowers that are pollinated by insects. The plant thrives in still or slow-moving waters and is often found in lakes, ponds, and wetlands. It plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.

Its presence can indicate good water quality due to its sensitivity to pollution.
