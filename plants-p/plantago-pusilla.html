Plantago pusilla

Plantago pusilla, commonly known as the common mouse-ear.

It is a small, herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa. The plant features rosettes of hairy, oval-shaped leaves that are typically less than 1 inch in length. It produces small, greenish flowers in a spike-like inflorescence, which are pollinated by wind.

Plantago pusilla is often found in disturbed soils and is known for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.
