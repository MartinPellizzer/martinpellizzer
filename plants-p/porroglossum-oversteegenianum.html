Porroglossum oversteegenianum

Porroglossum oversteegenianum, commonly known as the velvetleaf or velvetleaf morning glory, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, velvety hairs. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and violet, often with a fragrant scent. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and abandoned fields.

The plant is also known for its rapid growth and ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes.
