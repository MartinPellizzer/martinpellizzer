Piper parimanum

Piper parimanum, commonly known as the African pepper or black pepper tree, is a tropical plant native to West Africa.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces small, dark berries that are used in traditional medicine and as a spice. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in rainforest understories.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and cultural contexts.
