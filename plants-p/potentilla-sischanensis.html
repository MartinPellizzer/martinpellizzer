Potentilla sischanensis

Potentilla sischanensis, commonly known as the Sichuan cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of western China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit. The plant produces showy yellow flowers in early summer, which are clustered in inflorescences. Its leaves are palmately divided into five to seven lobes, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Potentilla sischanensis is adapted to rocky and well-drained soils, often found in alpine environments.
