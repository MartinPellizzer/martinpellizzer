Padus virens

Padus virens, commonly known as the green ash, is a deciduous tree native to Europe, western Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically 10 to 20 centimeters long and composed of 5 to 9 oval-shaped leaflets. The tree produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in catkins, which are wind-pollinated, and its fruits are small, dry drupes. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in riparian zones and along roadsides.

The bark is smooth and grey, becoming fissured with age, and the tree is known for its rapid growth and adaptability.
