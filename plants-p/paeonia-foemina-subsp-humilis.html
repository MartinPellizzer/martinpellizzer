Paeonia foemina subsp. humilis

Paeonia foemina subsp.

humilis, commonly known as the Siberian peony, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It features showy, double flowers in shades of pink, red, and white, which bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant has compound, bipinnate leaves that are finely divided and covered in a soft, fuzzy texture. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its botanical characteristics make it both ornamental and ecologically valuable in its native habitat.
