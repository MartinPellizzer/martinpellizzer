Piper diversipilum

Piper diversipilum, commonly known as the variable pepper vine, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, Colombia, and Ecuador. The plant is characterized by its trailing growth habit, with opposite, ovate leaves that are often hairy. Its small, greenish-white flowers are clustered in spikes, and it produces small, round berries that are typically yellow or orange when mature.

Piper diversipilum is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to various growing conditions.
