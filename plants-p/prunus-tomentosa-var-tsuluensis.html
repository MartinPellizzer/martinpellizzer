Prunus tomentosa var. tsuluensis

Prunus tomentosa var. tsuluensis, commonly known as the Tsuluensis cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It is characterized by its dense, silvery-white pubescent foliage that gives it a distinctive, woolly appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in early spring, followed by edible, sweet cherries in summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its unique appearance and ornamental value make it a sought-after plant in botanical collections.
