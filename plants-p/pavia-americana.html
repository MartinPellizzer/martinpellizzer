Pavia americana

Pavia americana, commonly known as the American pavia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a serrated margin.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often used in landscaping due to its attractive foliage and fragrant blossoms.
