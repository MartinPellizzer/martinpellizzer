Pithecellobium turbinatum

Pithecellobium turbinatum, commonly known as the turban pod or turban tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant produces yellow to orange flowers followed by elongated, curved pods that contain edible seeds. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.

The species is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it useful in ecological restoration projects.
