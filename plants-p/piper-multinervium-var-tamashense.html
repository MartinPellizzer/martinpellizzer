Piper multinervium var. tamashense

Piper multinervium var. tamashense, commonly known as the Tamashen pepper vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species, and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces tiny, berry-like fruits that are typically green when immature and turn red upon ripening. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and research settings.
