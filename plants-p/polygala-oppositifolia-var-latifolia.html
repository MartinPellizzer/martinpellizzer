Polygala oppositifolia var. latifolia

Polygala oppositifolia var. latifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to southern China.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional Chinese medicine.

The varietal form, latifolia, distinguishes it by its broader leaves compared to the typical species.
