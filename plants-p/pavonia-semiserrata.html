Pavonia semiserrata

Pavonia semiserrata, commonly known as the scarlet pimpernel, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States and parts of Mexico.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, showy flowers that range in color from white to deep red. The leaves are typically ovate to lanceolate, with a serrated edge, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often found in disturbed soils, such as along roadsides or in open woods.

The plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to bloom throughout the growing season, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
