Potentilla latipetiolata

Potentilla latipetiolata, commonly known as the broad-leaf cinquefoil, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features woody stems that are often branched and covered with small, glandular hairs. The plant produces clusters of yellow flowers with five petals, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a broad, ovate shape with serrated edges.

Potentilla latipetiolata is adapted to rocky and well-drained soils, making it a hardy plant in alpine and subalpine environments.
