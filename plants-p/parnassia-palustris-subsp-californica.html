Parnassia palustris subsp. californica

Parnassia palustris subsp.

californica, commonly known as the California marsh buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its delicate, white, cup-shaped flowers and broad, arrowhead-shaped leaves.

This subspecies thrives in wetland habitats, such as marshes and bogs, where it requires consistently moist soil. The plant has a shallow root system and is often found in association with other aquatic or semi-aquatic flora.

It plays a role in supporting local pollinators and contributes to the ecological diversity of its wetland environment.
