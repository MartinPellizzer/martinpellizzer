Piper brachypodon

Piper brachypodon, commonly known as the short-stalked pepper vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes various species of pepper plants. The plant features small, heart-shaped leaves and produces tiny, greenish-white flowers that develop into red, fleshy berries. Its botanical characteristics include a woody stem with adventitious roots and opposite leaf arrangement.

It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.
