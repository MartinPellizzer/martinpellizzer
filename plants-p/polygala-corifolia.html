Polygala corifolia

Polygala corifolia, commonly known as the mountain milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, pale yellow to greenish flowers. Polygala corifolia is often found in open woodlands and grasslands, contributing to the biodiversity of its native ecosystems.
