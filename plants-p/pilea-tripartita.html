Pilea tripartita

Pilea tripartita, commonly known as the Chinese money plant, is a species of flowering plant in the family Urticaceae.

It is native to China and is characterized by its round, rosette-shaped leaves that resemble coins. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers that are typically followed by red, berry-like fruits. It is a popular houseplant due to its ease of care and attractive foliage.

Pilea tripartita thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it suitable for indoor cultivation.
