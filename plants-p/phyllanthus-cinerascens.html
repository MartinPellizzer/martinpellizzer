Phyllanthus cinerascens

Phyllanthus cinerascens, commonly known as the African nightshade, is a shrub native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers and is known for its toxic properties, containing various alkaloids. It is sometimes used in traditional medicine, though its use is limited due to its potential toxicity.

The plant thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in woodland and savanna habitats.
