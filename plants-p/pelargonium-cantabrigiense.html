Pelargonium cantabrigiense

Pelargonium cantabrigiense, commonly known as the Cambridge geranium, is a species native to South Africa.

It is a perennial herb with opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces small, clustered flowers in shades of pink to purple, typically blooming in spring. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
