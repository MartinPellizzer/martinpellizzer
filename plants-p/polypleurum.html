Polypleurum

Polypleurum, commonly known as the silver fern, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Polygonaceae.

It is characterized by its distinctive, fern-like leaves that are often covered in a silvery or grayish pubescence. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream sides. Its flowers are small and clustered, often forming dense inflorescences.

Polypleurum species are valued for their ornamental appeal and are sometimes used in horticulture.
