Pithecellobium macrophyllum

Pithecellobium macrophyllum, commonly known as the giant velvetbean, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, leathery leaves and clusters of fragrant, yellowish-white flowers. The plant produces long, pod-like fruits that contain several edible, bean-like seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and potential as a food source, it is cultivated in some regions for both horticultural and agricultural purposes.
