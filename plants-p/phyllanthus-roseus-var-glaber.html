Phyllanthus roseus var. glaber

Phyllanthus roseus var. glaber, commonly known as the smooth roseleaf phyllanthus, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It features small, elliptical leaves that are typically smooth and glossy, with a deep green color. The plant produces tiny, yellowish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by small, round fruits. It is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests or along stream banks.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its attractive foliage.
