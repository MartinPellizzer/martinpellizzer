Pseudocannaboides

Pseudocannaboides, commonly known as the "false marijuana" plant, is a species within the Cannabaceae family.

It is characterized by its dioecious nature, with separate male and female plants. The plant typically features simple, alternate leaves with a serrated margin. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, often clustered in catkins.

While it shares some chemical similarities with Cannabis sativa, it does not contain significant levels of tetrahydrocannabinol (THC).
