Pyrus pyraster var. ovata

Pyrus pyraster var. ovata, commonly known as the oval-shaped crabapple, is a variant of the common crabapple species.

It is a deciduous tree or shrub native to Europe and parts of western Asia. The plant features oval-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and glossy on top, with a lighter underside. Its small, fragrant flowers bloom in spring, followed by edible, sour fruits that ripen in late summer.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and ecological benefits.
