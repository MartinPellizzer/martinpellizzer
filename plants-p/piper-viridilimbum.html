Piper viridilimbum

Piper viridilimbum, commonly known as the green-leaf pepper or green pepper plant, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, and thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant is characterized by its broad, glossy, dark green leaves and produces small, clustered green berries that turn red when mature. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has some traditional uses in local medicine.

The plant's botanical features make it a distinctive member of the Piper genus, with unique adaptations to its native ecosystem.
