Potentilla agrimonioides

Potentilla agrimonioides, commonly known as the creeping cinquefoil, is a low-growing perennial plant native to North America.

It features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has compound leaves with five oval leaflets, giving it its scientific name. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for native plant gardens and erosion control.
