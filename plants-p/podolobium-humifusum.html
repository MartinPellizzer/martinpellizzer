Podolobium humifusum

Podolobium humifusum, commonly known as the groundsel bush, is a perennial shrub native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in coastal and sandy soils, often in areas with high salinity. The plant has small, narrow leaves and produces yellow flowers in clusters. It is adapted to withstand harsh environmental conditions, including strong winds and salt spray.

Podolobium humifusum plays a role in stabilizing coastal dunes and providing habitat for local wildlife.
