Potamogeton crispus var. obtusifolius

Potamogeton crispus var. obtusifolius, commonly known as the broadleaf pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in Europe and Asia.

It has long, ribbon-like leaves that are broader at the tips, distinguishing it from other pondweed species. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are typically arranged in clusters, and it thrives in slow-moving or still waters. It plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.

Its presence can indicate good water quality and is often found in ponds, lakes, and slow rivers.
