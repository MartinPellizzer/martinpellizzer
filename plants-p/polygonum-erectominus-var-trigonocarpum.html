Polygonum erectominus var. trigonocarpum

Polygonum erectominus var. trigonocarpum, commonly known as the three-cornered gobernadora, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It features erect, branching stems that are often hairy, with lanceolate to ovate leaves that are usually arranged alternately along the stem. The plant produces small, clustered flowers in shades of pink to purple, which bloom in late summer. Its fruits are distinctive three-angled capsules, giving it its specific epithet *trigonocarpum*.

This species is often found in dry, disturbed habitats such as roadsides and grasslands.
