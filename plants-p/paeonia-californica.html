Paeonia californica

Paeonia californica, commonly known as the California peony, is a deciduous perennial native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its showy, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink and red, typically blooming in late spring. The plant features palmately compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its stems are often hairy. It thrives in well-drained soils and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

The California peony is also valued for its ornamental beauty and historical significance in local ecosystems.
