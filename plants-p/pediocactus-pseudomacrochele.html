Pediocactus pseudomacrochele

Pediocactus pseudomacrochele, commonly known as the giant red barrel cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its large, cylindrical stems that can grow up to several meters in height, often displaying a reddish or greenish hue. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, which are typically clustered at the top of the stem. Its thick, fleshy stems are adapted to store water, allowing it to thrive in harsh, dry environments.

The species is also notable for its slow growth rate and long lifespan, making it a unique and striking addition to desert landscapes.
