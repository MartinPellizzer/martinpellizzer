Polystichopsis puncticulata

Polystichopsis puncticulata, commonly known as the punctuated fern, is a species of fern native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its delicate, feather-like fronds that are typically bi- or tri-pinnate. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories. Its spores are produced in sori that are clustered and covered by a thin, membranous indusium.

The plant's botanical features make it a valuable subject for study in both horticulture and ecological research.
