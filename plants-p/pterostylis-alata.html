Pterostylis alata

Pterostylis alata, commonly known as the broad-leafed sun-orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, often yellow or greenish, floral structure with a prominent, spurred labellum. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as rainforests and understoreys. Its leaves are broad and strap-like, often with a waxy coating.

Pterostylis alata is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is known for its specialized pollination mechanisms involving insects.
