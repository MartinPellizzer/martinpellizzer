Pediomelum hypogaeum var. scaposum

Pediomelum hypogaeum var. scaposum, commonly known as the slender-stemmed groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit with hairy, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
