Potentilla wimanniana anomala

Potentilla wimanniana anomala, commonly known as the Wimann's cinquefoil, is a low-growing perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, typically from June to July. The plant has compound leaves composed of five to seven oval-shaped leaflets, which are covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
