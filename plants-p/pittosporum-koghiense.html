Pittosporum koghiense

Pittosporum koghiense, commonly known as the Koghi Pittosporum, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its evergreen foliage and fragrant white or cream-colored flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are leathery and glossy, with a distinctive aromatic scent when crushed.

The fruit is a small, woody capsule that contains several seeds.
