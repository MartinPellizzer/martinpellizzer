Phlox americana

Phlox americana, commonly known as the eastern phlox, is a herbaceous perennial native to eastern North America.

It features upright, branching stems that bear clusters of showy, fragrant flowers in shades of pink, red, or white. The plant has alternate, heart-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. It thrives in partial to full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

The flowers bloom in early to mid-summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
