Pachycentria fengii

Pachycentria fengii, commonly known as the Feng's thick-ribbed cactus, is a unique species native to arid regions of China.

It is characterized by its thick, ribbed stems that store water, allowing it to survive in dry environments. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the summer months, adding a striking visual contrast to its green, spiny structure. Its leaves are reduced to spines, which help minimize water loss and deter herbivores.

Pachycentria fengii is valued for its resilience and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant in drought-tolerant gardens.
