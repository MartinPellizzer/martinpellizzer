Piper jactatum

Piper jactatum, commonly known as the "shooting pepper," is a climbing vine native to the Amazon rainforest.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes numerous pepper species. The plant is characterized by its slender stems and small, heart-shaped leaves. Its fruit, a small, green berry, contains the plant's psychoactive alkaloid, which has been used traditionally by indigenous peoples.

Due to its unique properties, it is of interest to both botanists and ethnopharmacologists.
