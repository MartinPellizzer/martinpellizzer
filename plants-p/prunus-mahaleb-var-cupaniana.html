Prunus mahaleb var. cupaniana

Prunus mahaleb var. cupaniana, commonly known as the sweet cherry or sour cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its simple, ovate leaves that turn yellow in autumn, and its fragrant, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, sweet to tart cherries that are edible and used in culinary applications. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit.

This variety is closely related to other Prunus species and is valued for its hardiness and adaptability.
