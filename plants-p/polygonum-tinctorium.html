Polygonum tinctorium

Polygonum tinctorium, commonly known as Japanese knotweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit, which allows it to spread rapidly and aggressively. The plant features heart-shaped leaves and produces small, greenish flowers in clusters. It is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and environmental conditions.

Its botanical significance lies in its historical use as a dye plant and its ecological impact as an invasive species in non-native regions.
