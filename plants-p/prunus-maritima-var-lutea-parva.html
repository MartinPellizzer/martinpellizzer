Prunus maritima var. lutea-parva

Prunus maritima var. lutea-parva, commonly known as the dwarf yellow cherry, is a small shrub native to coastal regions of the southeastern United States.

It typically grows to a height of less than 1 meter, featuring dense, compact growth and dark green, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers in the spring, which are followed by bright red, edible fruits. Its leaves and fruits are often used in local cuisine and traditional medicine.

This variety is well adapted to sandy soils and salt spray, making it a hardy plant in coastal environments.
