Pelargonium × homophyllum

Pelargonium × homophyllum, commonly known as the ivy-leaved geranium, is a hybrid plant that exhibits a unique combination of traits from its parent species.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and dark green, heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink, red, or white, depending on the cultivar. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires bright, indirect light to flourish.

Due to its ornamental value and ease of care, it is a popular choice for hanging baskets and container gardening.
