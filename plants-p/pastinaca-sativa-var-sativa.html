Pastinaca sativa var. sativa

Pastinaca sativa var. sativa, commonly known as the common parsnip, is a biennial plant belonging to the Apiaceae family.

It is characterized by its long, slender, white or yellowish root that is often used as a vegetable. The plant has finely divided, feather-like leaves that are typically bluish-green in color. Its flowers are small and white, arranged in umbrella-like clusters called umbels.

The parsnip thrives in cool climates and is cultivated for its edible root, which is rich in nutrients like vitamin C and fiber.
