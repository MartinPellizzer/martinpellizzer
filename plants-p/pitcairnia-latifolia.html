Pitcairnia latifolia

Pitcairnia latifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved pitcairnia, is a striking bromeliad native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its broad, waxy, and often colorful leaves that form a rosette shape, which helps collect and retain water. The plant produces showy, spadix-like inflorescences that are typically pink or red, adding to its ornamental value. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in the understory of rainforests.

Due to its aesthetic appeal and unique structure, it is a popular choice for indoor gardening and botanical collections.
