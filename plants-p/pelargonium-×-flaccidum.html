Pelargonium × flaccidum

Pelargonium × flaccidum, commonly known as the ivy-leaved geranium, is a hybrid plant in the Geraniaceae family.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and heart-shaped, slightly lobed leaves that resemble ivy leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, five-petaled flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, typically blooming throughout the summer. Native to South Africa, it is widely cultivated as an ornamental plant for its attractive foliage and long-lasting blooms.

It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is commonly used in hanging baskets and containers.
