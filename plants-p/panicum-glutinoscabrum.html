Panicum glutinoscabrum

Panicum glutinoscabrum, commonly known as sticky foxtail, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Africa and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and dense, spike-like inflorescences that are covered in a sticky, waxy substance. The plant typically grows in dry, disturbed soils and is often found in grasslands and agricultural fields. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, while the seeds are small and dispersed by wind.

Panicum glutinoscabrum is sometimes considered a weed but has also been studied for its potential in ecological restoration projects.
