Pentacalia marinii

Pentacalia marinii, commonly known as the sea pentacalia, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of the Mediterranean.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils, thriving in saline environments. Its small, daisy-like flowers are usually white or pale blue, and it produces small, dry fruits called achenes.

Pentacalia marinii is often found in coastal dunes and is adapted to withstand strong winds and salt spray.
