Potamogeton serratus

Potamogeton serratus, commonly known as the serrated pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant found in freshwater environments.

It has long, slender stems that are often branched and bear small, oval-shaped leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces tiny, greenish flowers that are typically arranged in clusters near the water surface. It plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various water organisms.

Potamogeton serratus is widely distributed across temperate regions and is often used in wetland restoration projects.
