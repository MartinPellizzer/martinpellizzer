Pentatropis microphylla

Pentatropis microphylla, commonly known as the small-leaved five-branch pine, is a rare and endemic conifer native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes in South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit, with short, twisted branches and small, needle-like leaves. The plant produces distinctive cone-like structures that contain both male and female reproductive organs, typical of conifers. Its botanical significance lies in its unique morphology and adaptation to extreme environmental conditions.

Due to habitat loss and limited distribution, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
