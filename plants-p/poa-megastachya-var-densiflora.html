Poa megastachya var. densiflora

Poa megastachya var. densiflora, commonly known as the dense-flowered tussock grass, is a variant of the tall meadow grass species native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and elongated, narrow leaves that are typically bluish-green in color. The plant produces panicles of small, spike-like flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, stream banks, and forest edges.

This variant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for wildlife.
