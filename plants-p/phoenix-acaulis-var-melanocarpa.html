Phoenix acaulis var. melanocarpa

Phoenix acaulis var. melanocarpa, commonly known as the black-tailed date palm, is a rare and striking variant of the date palm.

It is characterized by its dark, almost black stems that contrast sharply with its bright green, fan-shaped leaves. The plant is native to the Arabian Peninsula and thrives in arid and semi-arid climates. It produces small, edible dates that are typically harvested in late summer.

Its unique appearance and limited distribution make it a notable species in botanical studies.
