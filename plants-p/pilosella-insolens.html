Pilosella insolens

Pilosella insolens, commonly known as the alpine mouse-ear, is a flowering plant native to mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky slopes, thriving in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are hairy and spoon-shaped, and the flowers are yellow with a central disk.

Pilosella insolens is often used in ecological studies due to its role in alpine ecosystems.
