Pseudotsuga douglasii var. caesia

Pseudotsuga douglasii var. caesia, commonly known as the silver fir, is a variety of the Douglas fir native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is a coniferous evergreen tree characterized by its slender, conical shape and silvery-blue needles. The tree produces both male and female cones, with the female cones being egg-shaped and maturing to release winged seeds. Its foliage is dense and provides habitat for various wildlife species.

The bark is thick and deeply furrowed, offering protection against fires and pests.
