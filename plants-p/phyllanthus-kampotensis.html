Phyllanthus kampotensis

Phyllanthus kampotensis, commonly known as Kampot star gooseberry, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia, particularly Cambodia.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its small, clustered yellow flowers and oval-shaped, edible fruits. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in moist, shaded environments.

Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a rough texture, while its fruits are used in traditional medicine and as a culinary ingredient.
