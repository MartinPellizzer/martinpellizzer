Phanerophlebia nobilis

Phanerophlebia nobilis, commonly known as the silverleaf magnolia, is a striking evergreen shrub or small tree native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, lance-shaped leaves that are typically silver-white on the underside, giving it its common name. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers that are usually pink or white, which bloom in clusters during the rainy season. Its bark is smooth and grayish, and it often has a dense, rounded crown.

Phanerophlebia nobilis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
