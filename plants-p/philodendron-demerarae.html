Philodendron demerarae

Philodendron demerarae, commonly known as the Demerara philodendron, is a tropical climbing plant native to South America.

It features heart-shaped, waxy leaves that are typically a deep green with a slightly variegated pattern. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by red, berry-like fruits that are edible for some animals. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires consistently moist, well-draining soil.

Its foliage makes it a popular choice for indoor gardening due to its aesthetic appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
