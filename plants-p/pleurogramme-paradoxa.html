Pleurogramme paradoxa

Pleurogramme paradoxa, commonly known as the paradoxical pleurogramme, is a rare and enigmatic fern native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique, deeply lobed fronds that resemble those of other ferns but with distinctive features that set it apart. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, often growing on rocky outcrops or in dense forest undergrowth. Its reproductive structures, including sporangia, are clustered in specialized clusters called sori, typical of many ferns.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a conservation concern.
