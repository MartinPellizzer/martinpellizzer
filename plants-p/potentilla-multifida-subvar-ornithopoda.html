Potentilla multifida subvar. ornithopoda

Potentilla multifida subvar. ornithopoda, commonly known as the ornate cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its finely divided, feather-like leaves that give it a delicate, lace-like appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or alpine settings.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
