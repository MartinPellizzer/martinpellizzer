Passiflora fuscinata

Passiflora fuscinata, commonly known as the yellow passionflower, is a climbing vine native to the Caribbean and northern South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow flowers, which feature a distinctive crown of filaments and a complex structure with five sepals, five petals, and a unique reproductive system. The plant produces edible, orange-red berries that are rich in antioxidants and have been used in traditional medicine. Its rapid growth habit and ornamental value make it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

The species is also noted for its ecological importance, providing nectar for pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds.
