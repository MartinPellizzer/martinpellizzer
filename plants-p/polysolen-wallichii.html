Polysolen wallichii

Polysolen wallichii, commonly known as the Himalayan bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its erect stems and showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are lanceolate and covered with a dense layer of fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in its harsh habitat.

Polysolen wallichii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens that mimic its natural environment.
