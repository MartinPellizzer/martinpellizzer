Pollinia geminata

Pollinia geminata, commonly known as the twin-pollen orchid, is a rare and specialized orchid species native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique reproductive structure, the pollinia, which are clustered in pairs and adapted for specific pollination mechanisms. The plant produces small, delicate flowers with intricate patterns that aid in attracting pollinators such as bees and wasps. Its botanical features include a sympodial growth habit and a fibrous root system typical of many orchid species.

Due to habitat destruction, Pollinia geminata is considered endangered, making conservation efforts crucial for its survival.
