Picea hudsonia

Picea hudsonia, commonly known as Hudson's pine, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to the northern regions of North America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and dense, pyramidal crown, with needle-like leaves that are blue-green in color. The tree produces small, woody cones that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in cool, moist climates and is often found in boreal and subarctic forests.

Its botanical features make it an important component of the ecosystem in its native habitat.
