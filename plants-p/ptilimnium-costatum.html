Ptilimnium costatum

Ptilimnium costatum, commonly known as the narrowleaf ptilimnium, is a small, herbaceous plant native to North America.

It typically grows in moist, sandy soils along the edges of ponds and wetlands. The plant features slender, upright stems with alternate, linear to lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small, white or pale pink, and are arranged in loose clusters at the ends of the stems.

Ptilimnium costatum is often found in disturbed or open habitats and is adapted to thrive in wet or semi-aquatic environments.
