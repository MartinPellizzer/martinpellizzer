Pseudocaryophyllus velutinus

Pseudocaryophyllus velutinus, commonly known as the velvetleaf sandmat, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Caryophyllaceae.

It is characterized by its hairy, velvety leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or white in color. It is native to regions of southern Africa and thrives in arid or semi-arid environments.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for study in ecological and horticultural contexts.
