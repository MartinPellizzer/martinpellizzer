Pulicaria scabra

Pulicaria scabra, commonly known as rough pulicaria, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is a low-growing perennial herb that typically reaches a height of about 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features gray-green, hairy leaves that are often covered in fine, rough texture, giving it its common name. Its small yellow flowers appear in clusters during the spring and summer months.

Pulicaria scabra is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments.
