Prunus × yedoensis var. grandiflora

Prunus × yedoensis var. grandiflora, commonly known as the Japanese cherry blossom, is a deciduous tree native to Japan.

It is prized for its showy, pink to white flowers that bloom in early spring, creating a spectacular floral display. The plant belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its slender branches and heart-shaped leaves. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is widely cultivated in parks and gardens around the world.
