Phacelia divaricata

Phacelia divaricata, commonly known as blue phacelia, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often covered in fine hairs.

Phacelia divaricata is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and as a nectar source for pollinators.
