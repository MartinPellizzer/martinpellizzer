Pedicularis lutescens subsp. brevifolia

Pedicularis lutescens subsp.

brevifolia, commonly known as the short-leaved yellow lousewort, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its hairy stems and yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils. It has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This subspecies is notable for its ecological role in supporting pollinators in its native environment.
