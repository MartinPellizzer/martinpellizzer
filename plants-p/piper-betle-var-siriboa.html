Piper betle var. siriboa

Piper betle var. siriboa, commonly known as Java betel, is a variant of the betel leaf plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a tropical evergreen shrub characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves used in traditional chewing practices. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth. Its leaves are often cultivated for their aromatic properties and cultural significance.

The botanical features of this variant include a robust stem, compound leaves, and small, inconspicuous flowers.
