Polygonum acutum

Polygonum acutum, commonly known as sharpleaf smartweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to North America.

It typically grows in wet or moist soils, often found in marshes, swales, and along stream banks. The plant features alternate, lance-shaped leaves with a serrated margin, and it produces small, clustered flowers that are usually pink or red in color. Its stems are often reddish and can become woody with age.

Polygonum acutum is known for its ability to thrive in wetland environments and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
