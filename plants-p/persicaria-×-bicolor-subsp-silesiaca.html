Persicaria × bicolor subsp. silesiaca

Persicaria × bicolor subsp.

silesiaca, commonly known as the redstem filago, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of Europe. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and distinctive foliage, which often displays a striking combination of green and red hues.

The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically arranged in clusters, and it is often found in moist, shady environments. It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental leaves and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.

This subspecies is also noted for its ecological benefits, providing habitat and nectar for pollinators.
