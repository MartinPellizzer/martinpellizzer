Physalis hirsuta var. repandodentata

Physalis hirsuta var. repandodentata, commonly known as the woolly groundcherry, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to South America.

It features hairy, sparsely foliated stems and produces clusters of yellow flowers that develop into inflated, red-orange berries. The leaves are alternate, ovate, and covered in fine, white pubescence. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often growing in dry, open habitats.

Its unique fruit structure and ornamental value make it a popular choice for botanical gardens and native plant collections.
