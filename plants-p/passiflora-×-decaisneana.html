Passiflora × decaisneana

Passiflora × decaisneana, commonly known as the purple passionflower, is a hybrid species native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking violet or purple flowers, which feature a complex structure with five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments. The plant is a climbing vine with heart-shaped leaves and produces edible berries that are rich in antioxidants. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its showy blooms.

Passiflora × decaisneana is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
