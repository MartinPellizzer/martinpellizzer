Paropsia integrifolia

Paropsia integrifolia, commonly known as the smooth-leafed paropsia, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its simple, entire leaves that are typically arranged oppositely along the stem. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in chaparral and woodland habitats.
