Pyrus ussuriensis var. acidula

Pyrus ussuriensis var. acidula, commonly known as the Ussuri pear, is a variant of the Ussuri pear tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its deciduous nature and produces small, sour fruits that are often used in traditional medicine and local cuisines. The plant typically grows in temperate climates and prefers well-drained, loamy soils. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a serrated margin, while its flowers are white and clustered in inflorescences.

The botanical features of this plant make it an important species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
