Polygonum setiferum

Polygonum setiferum, commonly known as the flameflower or red polygonum, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and vibrant, showy inflorescences that range from red to orange in color. The plant features simple, alternate leaves that are often lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Polygonum setiferum is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
