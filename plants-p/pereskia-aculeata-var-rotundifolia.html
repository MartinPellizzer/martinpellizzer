Pereskia aculeata var. rotundifolia

Pereskia aculeata var. rotundifolia, commonly known as the Roundleaf Cactus, is a succulent plant native to the Caribbean and northern South America.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems that are often mistaken for leaves, and it produces small, white, star-shaped flowers. The plant has a unique appearance with its rounded, broad leaves and spiny structures that provide protection. It thrives in sunny, well-drained environments and is often used in ornamental gardening.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for both horticultural study and ecological research.
