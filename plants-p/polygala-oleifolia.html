Polygala oleifolia

Polygala oleifolia, commonly known as the oliveleaf or oil plant, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that often have a distinctive, almost insect-like appearance. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 1 meter, with grayish-green leaves that are covered in a fine, powdery coating. Its flowers are usually pale green to yellowish in color and are arranged in spikes, blooming in spring.

Polygala oleifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
