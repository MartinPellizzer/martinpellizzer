Prasophyllum drummondii

Prasophyllum drummondii, commonly known as the Drummond's leek orchid, is an Australian native orchid belonging to the family Orchidaceae.

It is a terrestrial herb with a rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces a spike of greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in heathlands and open forests, preferring well-drained soils. It is found in Western Australia and is listed as vulnerable due to habitat loss and degradation.

Prasophyllum drummondii is notable for its unique floral structure and ecological significance in its native environment.
