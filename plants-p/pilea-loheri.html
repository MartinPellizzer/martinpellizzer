Pilea loheri

Pilea loheri, commonly known as the Chinese money plant, is a species of flowering plant in the family Araceae.

It is native to China and is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that resemble small coins. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers that are typically hidden beneath its leaves. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Pilea loheri is a popular houseplant due to its attractive foliage and relatively low maintenance requirements.
