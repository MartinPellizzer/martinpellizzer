Poa remota subsp. raduliformis

Poa remota subsp.

raduliformis, commonly known as the slender meadowgrass, is a grass species native to the northern hemisphere. It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and narrow, linear leaves.

The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. It produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences with inconspicuous flowers.

Botanically, it is distinguished by its unique leaf morphology and habitat preferences.
