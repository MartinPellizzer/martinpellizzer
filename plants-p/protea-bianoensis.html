Protea bianoensis

Protea bianoensis, commonly known as the white protea, is a striking South African plant belonging to the Proteaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescence composed of numerous white, petal-like bracts. The plant typically grows in coastal fynbos vegetation, thriving in well-drained, acidic soils. Its flowers are pollinated primarily by birds, particularly sunbirds, due to their nectar-rich structure.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique and elegant appearance.
