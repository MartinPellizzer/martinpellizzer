Poterium hermaphroditum

Poterium hermaphroditum, commonly known as the hermaphroditic poterium, is a unique plant species characterized by its ability to produce both male and female reproductive structures on the same individual.

It typically features small, clustered flowers that are often white or pale yellow in color. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, making it a common sight in woodland areas. Its botanical significance lies in its sexual dimorphism and the way it contributes to pollination dynamics in its ecosystem.

The species is also notable for its relatively simple morphology, which makes it a subject of interest in botanical studies.
