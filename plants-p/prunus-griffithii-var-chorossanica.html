Prunus griffithii var. chorossanica

Prunus griffithii var. chorossanica, commonly known as the Kashmir cherry or Chorossan cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Himalayan regions of India and Pakistan.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its ornamental pink to white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has elliptical leaves with serrated edges and produces small, sweet, edible fruits in the summer. It thrives in cool, moist climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible yield.

The species is also valued for its ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
