Platyschkuhria oblongifolia

Platyschkuhria oblongifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf yarrow, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of linear, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands. It is a perennial species with a short stem that branches near the top, supporting its compact growth habit.

The flowers bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
