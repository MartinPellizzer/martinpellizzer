Pilosella carcina

Pilosella carcina, commonly known as the Carcina mouse-ear, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its hairy, spoon-shaped leaves and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the summer. Native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa, it thrives in dry, rocky soils and open habitats. The plant is often found in grasslands and along the edges of forests.

Its name reflects its resemblance to the mouse ear, a feature that aids in its identification.
