Perula arborea

Perula arborea, commonly known as the tree hibiscus, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its woody stems and large, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

Perula arborea is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional medicinal uses.
