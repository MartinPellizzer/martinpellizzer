Pharetranthus ferrugineus

Pharetranthus ferrugineus, commonly known as the rusty pharetra, is a member of the Acanthaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa and is characterized by its hairy, reddish stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain environments.
