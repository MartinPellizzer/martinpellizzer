Piper willdenowii

Piper willdenowii, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces berries that are typically red or yellow when ripe, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value. However, it can become invasive in certain regions, outcompeting native vegetation.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
