Polystachya leonensis

Polystachya leonensis, commonly known as the Leon's spikegrass, is a member of the orchid family, Orchidaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Central and South America, where it thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant produces tall, slender inflorescences bearing numerous small, greenish-white flowers. Its leaves are elongated and grass-like, often arranged in a rosette pattern.

Polystachya leonensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique appearance.
