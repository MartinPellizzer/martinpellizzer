Pelargonium trichostemum

Pelargonium trichostemum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved geranium, is a species of flowering plant in the family Geraniaceae.

It is native to South Africa and is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and dark green, narrow leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, pink to red flowers that bloom in spring and summer. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and showy blooms.

Pelargonium trichostemum prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for gardens and containers.
