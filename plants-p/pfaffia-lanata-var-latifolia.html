Pfaffia lanata var. latifolia

Pfaffia lanata var. latifolia, commonly known as Brazilian ginseng, is a perennial herb native to the Amazon rainforest.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its hairy, lance-shaped leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is renowned for its medicinal properties, particularly its root, which contains bioactive compounds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical regions.

Due to its ecological and pharmacological significance, it is a subject of both traditional and modern botanical research.
