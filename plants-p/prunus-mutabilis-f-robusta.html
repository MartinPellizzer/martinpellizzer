Prunus mutabilis f. robusta

Prunus mutabilis f. robusta, commonly known as the robusta cherry, is a compact, bushy shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of the United States.

It features dark green, ovate leaves with a slightly serrated margin and produces small, showy flowers in shades of white, pink, and red. The plant is characterized by its robust growth habit and is often used in ornamental landscaping for its aesthetic appeal. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

The fruit, a small, tart cherry, is edible but not typically cultivated for commercial use.
