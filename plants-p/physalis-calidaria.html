Physalis calidaria

Physalis calidaria, commonly known as the warm-day husk or tropical husk, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the genus Physalis and is part of the nightshade family, Solanaceae. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that develop into distinctive orange or red, inflated calyces resembling tiny lanterns. Its leaves are simple, ovate, and covered with fine, white hairs.

The fruit is edible when mature, though it is often used in culinary applications in its native regions.
