Protea woodwardii

Protea woodwardii, commonly known as the silver protea, is a striking South African plant belonging to the Proteaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, fluffy, white to pinkish inflorescence, which resembles a cluster of small flowers. The plant is native to the Western and Eastern Cape provinces, where it thrives in sandy, well-drained soils. It is often found in fynbos vegetation, showcasing its adaptation to the region's Mediterranean climate.

The protea is not only ornamental but also plays a role in supporting local pollinators.
