Panicum sanguinale var. glabrum

Panicum sanguinale var. glabrum, commonly known as red panicgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and vibrant red to purple inflorescences that appear in late summer. The plant has smooth, glabrous leaves and stems, which distinguish it from other varieties of Panicum sanguinale. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, often found in open woodlands and disturbed sites.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for native plant gardens and ecological restoration projects.
