Ptilotrichum pyrenaicum

Ptilotrichum pyrenaicum, commonly known as the Pyrenean moss campion, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of the Iberian Peninsula and southern France.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit. The plant features small, pink or white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are typically opposite, linear, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Ptilotrichum pyrenaicum thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.
