Potentilla camillae

Potentilla camillae, commonly known as Camilla's cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are typically lobed and covered in fine hairs, and it thrives in well-drained, rocky soils.

Potentilla camillae is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to stabilize soil in arid environments.
