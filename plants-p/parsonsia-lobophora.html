Parsonsia lobophora

Parsonsia lobophora, commonly known as the golden trumpet vine, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia and the Pacific.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as birds and insects. The plant has woody stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for gardens and conservation efforts in its native habitats.
