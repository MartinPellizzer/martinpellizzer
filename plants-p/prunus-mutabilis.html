Prunus mutabilis

Prunus mutabilis, commonly known as the Mayflower or Persian lilac, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its showy, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink to deep red, depending on the stage of bloom. The plant produces small, dark purple or black berries that are edible but often used more for ornamental purposes. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a serrated edge, contributing to its overall aesthetic appeal.

Prunus mutabilis thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
