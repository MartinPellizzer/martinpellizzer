Pholidota uraiensis

Pholidota uraiensis, commonly known as the Urai tamarind, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and pod-like fruits. The plant is adapted to tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, well-drained soils. Its flowers are typically pale yellow or greenish, and it produces edible pods that contain seeds.

The tree is valued for its timber and has traditional uses in local medicine.
