Pilocereus chrysomallus

Pilocereus chrysomallus, commonly known as the golden star cactus, is a species of cacti native to South America.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems that are typically green with a slight reddish tinge. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun exposure.

Due to its striking appearance and unique flowering habit, it is a popular choice for succulent collectors and gardeners.
