Piper subfalcatum

Piper subfalcatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved pepper plant, is a tropical vine native to the rainforests of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many pepper species. The plant is characterized by its long, slender leaves and small, clustered flowers. Its fruits are initially green and turn red when mature, containing a spicy, peppery flavor.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and for use in traditional medicine.
