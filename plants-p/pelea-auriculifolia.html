Pelea auriculifolia

Pelea auriculifolia, commonly known as the earleaf pelea, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean islands.

It is characterized by its distinctive ear-shaped leaves, which give it its common name. The plant produces small, clustered yellow flowers that bloom during the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas.

Pelea auriculifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in tropical gardens.
