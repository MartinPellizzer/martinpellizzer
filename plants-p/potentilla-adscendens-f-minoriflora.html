Potentilla adscendens f. minoriflora

Potentilla adscendens f. minoriflora, commonly known as the dwarf flowered cinquefoil, is a compact, low-growing shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, typically in clusters of five. The plant has slender, wiry stems and hairy leaves that are typically less than 2 inches in length. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

This cultivar is valued for its hardiness and ornamental appeal in garden landscapes.
