Phalaris bulbosa var. hirtiglumis

Phalaris bulbosa var. hirtiglumis, commonly known as the hairy bulbous grass, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact, bulbous base and slender, grass-like leaves that are covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny blue or purple flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or dry habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
