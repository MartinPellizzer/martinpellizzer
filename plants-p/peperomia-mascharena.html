Peperomia mascharena

Peperomia mascharena, commonly known as the Mascharena pepper plant, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to Brazil.

It features thick, waxy leaves that are typically dark green with a slight reddish or purple tinge on the underside. The plant produces small, clustered flowers in shades of white or pale pink, which bloom during the spring and summer months. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Peperomia mascharena is popular as a houseplant due to its low maintenance and attractive foliage.
