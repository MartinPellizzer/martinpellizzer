Penstemon × jonesii

Penstemon × jonesii, commonly known as the Jones' beardtongue, is a hybrid perennial plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The plant produces bracts that resemble fuzzy, hair-like structures, giving it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

Penstemon × jonesii is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds.
