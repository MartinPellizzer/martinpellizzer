Pygeum diospyrophyllum

Pygeum diospyrophyllum, commonly known as the African persimmon, is a small evergreen tree native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, dark bark and alternate, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by orange-red, fleshy fruits that are edible.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and medicinal contexts.
