Papaver alpinum var. pseudobiconvex

Papaver alpinum var. pseudobiconvex, commonly known as the Alpine poppy, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It features striking, showy flowers with bright red petals that are typically arranged in a double or semi-double form. The plant has a fibrous root system and produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds. Its foliage consists of basal leaves that are palmately lobed and covered in a fine layer of white hairs.

This species is well adapted to cold, alpine environments and thrives in well-drained, rocky soils.
