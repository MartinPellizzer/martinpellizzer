Pulsatilla regeliana

Pulsatilla regeliana, commonly known as the Regel's pasqueflower, is a species of flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its showy, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in early spring. Pulsatilla regeliana is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking appearance.
