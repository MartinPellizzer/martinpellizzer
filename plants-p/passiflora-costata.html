Passiflora costata

Passiflora costata, commonly known as the yellow passionflower, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers with intricate patterns of red, blue, and white. The plant produces edible berries that are often used in culinary applications. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a rough texture.

Passiflora costata is valued in horticulture for its ornamental beauty and is also used in traditional medicine.
