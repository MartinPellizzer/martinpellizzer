Physostegia virginiana subsp. praemorsa

Physostegia virginiana subsp.

praemorsa, commonly known as the purple coneflower or swamp milkweed, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States. It features showy, tubular purple flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant typically grows in wet or moist habitats, including swamps, marshes, and along stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture, while its stems are erect and can reach up to 2 meters in height.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting native wildlife.
