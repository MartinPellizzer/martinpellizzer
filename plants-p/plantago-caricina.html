Plantago caricina

Plantago caricina, commonly known as broadleaf plantain, is a perennial herb native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It features a basal rosette of broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces upright flowering stalks topped with a dense cluster of small, greenish flowers. It is well adapted to grow in a variety of habitats, including lawns, roadsides, and disturbed soils.

Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their soothing and healing properties.
