Penstemon canosobarbatus

Penstemon canosobarbatus, commonly known as the silver-haired beardtongue, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage and showy, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to violet. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It is a perennial species that produces a basal rosette of leaves and flowering stems.

Penstemon canosobarbatus is valued in native plant gardens for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
