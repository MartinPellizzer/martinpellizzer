Primula firmipes subsp. flexilipes

Primula firmipes subsp.

flexilipes, commonly known as the flexible primrose, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Himalayas. It typically grows in high-altitude meadows and rocky areas, thriving in cool, moist environments.

The plant features upright stems with clustered, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. Its foliage consists of broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate blooms.
