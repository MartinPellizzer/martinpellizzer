Penstemon clevelandii var. angelicus

Penstemon clevelandii var. angelicus, commonly known as the Cleveland's beardtongue, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of tubular, violet-blue flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are hairy and gray-green in appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and native plant landscapes.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
