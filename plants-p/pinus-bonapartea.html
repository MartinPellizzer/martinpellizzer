Pinus bonapartea

Pinus bonapartea, commonly known as the Bonaparte pine, is a coniferous tree native to the mountainous regions of central and eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, straight trunk and long, needle-like leaves that are typically arranged in bundles of five. The tree produces cones that are often pendulous and can remain on the branches for several years before opening. It thrives in cool, moist climates and is often found in forested areas at high elevations.

The species is valued for its timber and has ecological significance in its native habitats.
