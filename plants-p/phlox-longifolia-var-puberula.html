Phlox longifolia var. puberula

Phlox longifolia var. puberula, commonly known as the woolly phlox, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its soft, fuzzy leaves and clusters of fragrant, pink to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It is a perennial species that produces showy blooms in late spring to early summer.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for xeriscaping and native garden plantings.
