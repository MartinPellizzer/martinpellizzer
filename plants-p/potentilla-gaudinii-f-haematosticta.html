Potentilla gaudinii f. haematosticta

Potentilla gaudinii f. haematosticta, commonly known as the red-stained potentilla, is a botanical variant characterized by its distinctive red pigmentation on the leaf margins and stems.

This form belongs to the Rosaceae family and is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. The leaves are typically narrow and serrated, with a dense, compact growth habit. It produces small, yellow flowers in early summer, which are clustered in inflorescences.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique coloration and hardy nature.
