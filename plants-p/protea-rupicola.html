Protea rupicola

Protea rupicola, commonly known as the rock protea, is a striking South African plant belonging to the Proteaceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, spiky inflorescence composed of numerous small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in fynbos vegetation. Its unique structure and vibrant blooms make it a popular choice for horticultural displays.

Protea rupicola is also valued for its ecological role, supporting various pollinators in its native habitat.
