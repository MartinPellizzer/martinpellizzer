Polygala adenophylla var. parvifolia

Polygala adenophylla var. parvifolia, commonly known as the small-leaved milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in sandy or rocky soils, often in arid or semi-arid environments. The plant features slender stems and small, often clustered, greenish-white flowers. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in tough conditions.
