Pyrus pannonica sanguinea

Pyrus pannonica sanguinea, commonly known as the crimson crabapple, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Carpathian Basin.

It is characterized by its ornamental value, featuring bright red flowers in spring and vibrant red or orange fruits in autumn. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are oval to elliptic in shape with a smooth margin. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for gardens and landscapes.

Due to its aesthetic appeal, it is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.
