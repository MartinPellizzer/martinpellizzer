Pimelea nivea var. micrantha

Pimelea nivea var. micrantha, commonly known as the white mistflower, is a small shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It features narrow, linear leaves that are typically covered in a fine, white powdery coating, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, fluffy white flowers that bloom in spring, adding a delicate touch to its foliage. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats such as heathlands and woodlands.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique visual appeal.
