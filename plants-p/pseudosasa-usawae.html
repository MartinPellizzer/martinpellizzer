Pseudosasa usawae

Pseudosasa usawae, commonly known as the Japanese bamboo or Usawa bamboo, is a species of grass belonging to the Poaceae family.

It is native to Japan and is characterized by its slender, hollow stems that grow in clusters. The plant typically reaches a height of about 1 to 2 meters and has long, narrow leaves that are often described as having a silky texture. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in mountainous regions.

Pseudosasa usawae is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional Japanese landscaping.
