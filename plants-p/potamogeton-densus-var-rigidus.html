Potamogeton densus var. rigidus

Potamogeton densus var. rigidus, commonly known as rigid pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in North America.

It is characterized by its dense, stiff, grass-like leaves that are typically arranged in whorls around the stem. The plant produces small, green flowers that are clustered in spikelets, which are often submerged or just below the water surface. It thrives in slow-moving to still waters with muddy or sandy bottoms.

This species plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
