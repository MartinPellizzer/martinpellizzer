Pseudoprosopis euryphylla subsp. euryphylla

Pseudoprosopis euryphylla subsp.

euryphylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaved mesquite, is a shrub or small tree native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically narrow and have a silvery underside.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by edible pods containing seeds. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing on rocky soils.

This species is well adapted to drought conditions and is an important plant in desert ecosystems.
