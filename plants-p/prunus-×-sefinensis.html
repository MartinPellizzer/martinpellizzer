Prunus × sefinensis

Prunus × sefinensis, commonly known as the Japanese cherry, is a hybrid species within the Prunus genus.

It is characterized by its ornamental value, particularly for its showy flowers that bloom in spring. The plant is native to Japan and is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal in gardens and landscapes. It typically grows as a small tree or shrub, reaching heights of up to 10 meters.

The leaves are simple, alternate, and have a serrated margin, while the fruit is a small, hard drupe.
