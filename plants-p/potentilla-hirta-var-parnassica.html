Potentilla hirta var. parnassica

Potentilla hirta var. parnassica, commonly known as the Parnassian cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, preferring well-drained soil and full sun. Its leaves are usually hairy and arranged in a basal rosette, contributing to its hardy nature.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinator populations.
