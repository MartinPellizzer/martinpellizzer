Pleurothallis prorepens

Pleurothallis prorepens, commonly known as the creeping pleurothallis, is a species of orchid native to the cloud forests of Central and South America.

It is a small, terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, moist environments. The plant features a rhizomatous growth habit and produces delicate, greenish-white flowers with a subtle fragrance. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it is often found in association with other epiphytic orchids.

Due to its delicate nature and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
