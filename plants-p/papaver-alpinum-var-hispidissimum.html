Papaver alpinum var. hispidissimum

Papaver alpinum var. hispidissimum, commonly known as the Alpine poppy, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It features striking, showy flowers that are typically red or orange in color, with a prominent central stigma. The plant has finely divided, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to cold climates.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in alpine gardens.
