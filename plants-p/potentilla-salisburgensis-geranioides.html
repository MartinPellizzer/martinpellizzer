Potentilla salisburgensis geranioides

Potentilla salisburgensis geranioides, commonly known as the Salzburg geranium, is a flowering plant native to central Europe.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, pink to red flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in woodland edges and meadows. Its leaves are palmately lobed, and it produces small, clustered flowers in the summer.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
