Psorothamnus

Psorothamnus, commonly known as the desert broom, is a genus of flowering plants native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

These shrubs typically have woody stems and small, often gray-green leaves that are adapted to reduce water loss in hot, dry climates. The flowers are usually yellow or cream-colored, clustered in dense, branched inflorescences, and bloom in spring. Psorothamnus species are drought-tolerant and play a role in stabilizing sandy soils in their native habitats.

Their ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various desert wildlife.
