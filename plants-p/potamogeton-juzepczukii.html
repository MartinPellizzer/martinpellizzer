Potamogeton juzepczukii

Potamogeton juzepczukii, commonly known as the Juzepczuk's pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the family Potamogetonaceae and is characterized by its long, narrow leaves that are often arranged in whorls around the stem. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are typically clustered in spike-like inflorescences. It thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments such as lakes and ponds.

Its presence is often an indicator of good water quality and is important for supporting aquatic ecosystems.
