Pueraria volkensii

Pueraria volkensii, commonly known as the African kudzu, is a climbing legume native to tropical Africa.

It features large, heart-shaped leaves and produces showy, purple or violet flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is characterized by its vigorous growth habit and extensive root system, which can spread rapidly through rhizomes. It thrives in moist, fertile soils and is often found in rainforests and along riverbanks.

Pueraria volkensii is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in some African cultures.
