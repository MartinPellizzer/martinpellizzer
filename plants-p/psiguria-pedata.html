Psiguria pedata

Psiguria pedata, commonly known as the walking fern, is a unique plant characterized by its creeping rhizomes that allow it to spread along the ground.

It features delicate, arching fronds that resemble the shape of a walking person, giving it its common name. The plant is native to tropical and subtropical regions, particularly in the Americas, and thrives in moist, shaded environments. Its leaves are typically light green and can grow up to several feet in length.

Psiguria pedata is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to form dense, carpet-like mats.
