Pedicularis tribuloides

Pedicularis tribuloides, commonly known as the spurred gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky habitats at high elevations. Its flowers have spurred petals that aid in pollination by specific insect species.

Pedicularis tribuloides is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance in its native environment.
