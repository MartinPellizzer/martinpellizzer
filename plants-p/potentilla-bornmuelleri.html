Potentilla bornmuelleri

Potentilla bornmuelleri, commonly known as the Bornmueller's cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are palmately divided, and it produces small, aggregate fruits known as achenes.

Potentilla bornmuelleri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and vibrant blooms.
