Prunella vulgaris subsp. hastifolia

Prunella vulgaris subsp.

hastifolia, commonly known as narrow-leaved selfheal, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in moist, grassy habitats such as meadows and woodland edges.

The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and have a slightly serrated edge. Its small, clustered blue or purple flowers bloom in late spring to early summer.

It is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional herbal remedies.
