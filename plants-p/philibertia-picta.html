Philibertia picta

Philibertia picta, commonly known as the painted filbert, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of central and southern South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring. Philibertia picta is often found in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in temperate climates.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it is not widely commercially grown.
