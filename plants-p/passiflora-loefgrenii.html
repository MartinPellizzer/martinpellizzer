Passiflora loefgrenii

Passiflora loefgrenii, commonly known as the Hawaiian passionflower, is a rare and endemic species native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It is a climbing vine with striking, velvety green leaves and showy, complex flowers that feature a blend of blue, purple, and white hues. The flowers are characterized by their intricate corona, which consists of numerous filaments surrounding the reproductive structures. This species is adapted to the unique tropical climate of Hawaii and thrives in moist, shaded environments.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable plant species.
