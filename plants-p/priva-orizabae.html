Priva orizabae

Priva orizabae, commonly known as the Orizaba privet, is a species of flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Oleaceae family and is characterized by its small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has opposite, simple leaves that are typically oval in shape and have a glossy surface. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for ornamental gardening.

Priva orizabae is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal and ability to adapt to various garden conditions.
