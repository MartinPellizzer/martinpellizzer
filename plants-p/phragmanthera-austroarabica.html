Phragmanthera austroarabica

Phragmanthera austroarabica, commonly known as the Arabian sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the Arabian Peninsula.

It is a low-growing shrub that typically reaches heights of about 30 centimeters. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are usually pink or purple in color, which bloom during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand high temperatures and limited water availability.

Its ecological role includes providing nectar for pollinators and stabilizing sandy soils in its native habitat.
