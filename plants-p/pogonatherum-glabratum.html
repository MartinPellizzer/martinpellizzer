Pogonatherum glabratum

Pogonatherum glabratum, commonly known as the smooth flat sedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It typically grows in wetland environments, including marshes and along the edges of water bodies. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. Its botanical features include a rhizomatous root system and slender, fibrous stems.

It is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support aquatic ecosystems.
