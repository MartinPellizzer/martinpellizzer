Pleocnemia macrodonta

Pleocnemia macrodonta, commonly known as the giant fern, is a striking member of the Blechnaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, leathery fronds that can grow up to several meters in length. The plant has a robust rhizome system that allows it to thrive in moist, shaded environments. Its spore-producing structures, called sori, are located on the underside of the fronds.

This species is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, where it contributes to the biodiversity of forest ecosystems.
