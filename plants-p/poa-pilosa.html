Poa pilosa

Poa pilosa, commonly known as silky bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to North America.

It is characterized by its fine, hair-like leaves that give it a soft, velvety appearance. The plant typically grows in open, sunny areas such as meadows and lawns. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny blue to purple flowers.

Poa pilosa is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to form dense, attractive turf.
