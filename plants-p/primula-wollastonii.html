Primula wollastonii

Primula wollastonii, commonly known as the Wollaston's primrose, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of central and southern China.

It typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils at high altitudes, often in alpine meadows. The plant features basal leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape, with a hairy texture. Its flowers are showy, with five petals that are usually pale yellow to orange, and they bloom in early spring.

Primula wollastonii is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and blooms.
