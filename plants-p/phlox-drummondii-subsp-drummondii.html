Phlox drummondii subsp. drummondii

Phlox drummondii subsp.

drummondii, commonly known as the common garden phlox, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States. It features showy, clustered flowers in shades of pink, red, and white, typically blooming in late spring to early summer.

The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

Its botanical structure supports its role as a nectar source for pollinators like bees and butterflies.
