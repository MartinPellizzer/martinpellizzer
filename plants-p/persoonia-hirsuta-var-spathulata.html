Persoonia hirsuta var. spathulata

Persoonia hirsuta var. spathulata, commonly known as the broad-leafed pear-plant, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It features small, white, bell-shaped flowers that are often clustered in dense inflorescences. The leaves are broad, ovate, and covered in fine, soft hairs, giving the plant a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to Mediterranean climates.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
