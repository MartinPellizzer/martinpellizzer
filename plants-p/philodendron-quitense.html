Philodendron quitense

Philodendron quitense, commonly known as the Ecuadorian philodendron, is a climbing plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its distinctive leaf morphology and inflorescence structure. The plant features heart-shaped leaves that are often variegated with white or yellow markings, making it visually striking. Its roots system is adapted for climbing, allowing it to ascend trees or other structures in its natural habitat.

The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically surrounded by a modified leaf called a spathe.
