Pultenaea obovata

Pultenaea obovata, commonly known as the broad-leaved bush-pea, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant features opposite, ovate to obovate leaves that are often hairy, and its flowers are typically yellow or orange, arranged in racemes. It produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Pultenaea obovata is often found in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to thrive in arid and semi-arid environments.
