Pyrus pyraster f. majoricarpa

Pyrus pyraster f. majoricarpa, commonly known as the large-fruited crabapple, is a cultivated form of the common crabapple.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers and edible, sweet-tart fruits. The plant is a deciduous shrub or small tree, typically reaching heights of 3 to 6 meters. Its leaves are ovate to elliptic, with a smooth margin and a glossy upper surface.

The fruit, which is typically yellow or red when ripe, is often used in culinary applications and ornamental gardening.
