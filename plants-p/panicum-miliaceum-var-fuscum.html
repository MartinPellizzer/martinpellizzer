Panicum miliaceum var. fuscum

Panicum miliaceum var. fuscum, commonly known as black millet, is a grass species native to warm climates.

It is characterized by its dark-colored grains and compact growth habit. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny seeds. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils.

Black millet is valued for its nutritional content and is used in traditional cuisines and animal feed.
