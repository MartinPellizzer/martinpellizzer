Papaver rhoeas proles intermedium

Papaver rhoeas proles intermedium, commonly known as the intermediate poppy, is a hybrid form of the common poppy.

It exhibits a mix of characteristics between the typical Papaver rhoeas and other species within the Papaver genus. This plant is known for its vibrant red or orange flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often found in disturbed soils such as roadsides and fields.

Its botanical significance lies in its role as a natural hybrid, showcasing genetic diversity within the Papaver family.
