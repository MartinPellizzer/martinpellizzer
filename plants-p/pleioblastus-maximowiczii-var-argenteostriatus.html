Pleioblastus maximowiczii var. argenteostriatus

Pleioblastus maximowiczii var. argenteostriatus, commonly known as the silver-striped bamboo, is a variant of the pleioblastus bamboo species native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its gracefully arching culms and distinctive silver-green stripes on its leaves. The plant has a clumping growth habit and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes. Its foliage tends to be more densely packed and narrower compared to other pleioblastus varieties.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is relatively cold-hardy, making it suitable for temperate climates.
