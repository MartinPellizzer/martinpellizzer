Paeonia mascula var. lusitanica

Paeonia mascula var. lusitanica, commonly known as the Lusitanian peony, is a herbaceous perennial native to southern Europe.

It features showy, single or semi-double flowers in shades of pink, red, and white, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has palmately compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its stems are often reddish in color. It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and historical significance in botanical gardens.
