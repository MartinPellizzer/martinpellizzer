Panicum nutans

Panicum nutans, commonly known as the nodding grass, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its drooping inflorescences, which give it its common name. The plant typically grows in wetland environments and is often found in marshy areas or along the edges of water bodies. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, spike-like clusters of flowers.

Panicum nutans is a member of the grass family (Poaceae) and is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil.
