Piper longibracteum

Piper longibracteum, commonly known as the long-bract pepper, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil and Peru. The plant is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and small, heart-shaped leaves. Its flowers are unisexual, with separate male and female flowers on the same plant.

The fruit is a small, elongated berry that contains a single seed, and the plant is valued for its ornamental and potential medicinal properties.
