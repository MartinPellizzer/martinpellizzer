Pouzolzia indica subvar. muralis

Pouzolzia indica subvar. muralis, commonly known as the wall pouzolzia, is a climbing plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family and is characterized by its slender, leafy stems that often grow along walls or rocky surfaces. The leaves are typically ovate to lanceolate, with a smooth or slightly pubescent surface. It produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, and its fruits are three-winged achenes.

This species is often used in traditional medicine and is valued for its ability to thrive in harsh, urban environments.
