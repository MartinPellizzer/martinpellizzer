Peutalis polystachya

Peutalis polystachya, commonly known as the golden bamboo, is a member of the grass family (Poaceae).

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and clusters of golden-yellow inflorescences that give it its common name. Native to tropical regions of South America, this plant thrives in warm, humid climates. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its striking color and elegant appearance.

Peutalis polystachya is also used in traditional medicine in some indigenous communities.
