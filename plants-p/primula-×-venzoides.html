Primula × venzoides

Primula × venzoides, commonly known as the Japanese primrose, is a hybrid species within the Primulaceae family.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant typically blooms in early spring, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking early-season color. Native to Japan and Korea, it thrives in cool, moist environments and is often cultivated in shaded garden beds.

Its foliage consists of broad, dark green leaves that provide a striking contrast to its vibrant blooms.
