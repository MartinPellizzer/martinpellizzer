Polypodium camptocarpum

Polypodium camptocarpum, commonly known as the rock fern, is a perennial fern native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its leathery, evergreen fronds. The plant typically grows on rocky surfaces or in shaded, moist environments, demonstrating its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its sporangia are clustered in sori on the underside of the fronds, a common feature in ferns.

Polypodium camptocarpum is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in challenging landscapes.
