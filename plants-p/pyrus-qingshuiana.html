Pyrus qingshuiana

Pyrus qingshuiana, commonly known as the Qingshuian pear, is a rare species native to China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves and white, fragrant flowers. The plant produces small, yellowish fruits that are typically eaten fresh or used in traditional Chinese medicine. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in mountainous regions with well-drained soil.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a conservation concern.
