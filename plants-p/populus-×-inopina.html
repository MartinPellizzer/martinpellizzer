Populus × inopina

Populus × inopina, commonly known as the narrow-leaf poplar, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Populus balsamifera and Populus deltoides.

It is characterized by its slender, elongated leaves that are typically 5–10 cm in length. The plant is native to the northern regions of North America, where it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers in the spring, followed by samaras that aid in its wind-dispersed reproduction.

Due to its rapid growth and adaptability, it is often used in reforestation and erosion control efforts.
