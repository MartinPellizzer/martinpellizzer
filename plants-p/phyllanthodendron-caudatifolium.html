Phyllanthodendron caudatifolium

Phyllanthodendron caudatifolium, commonly known as the long-leaved phyllanthodendron, is a tropical climbing plant belonging to the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is characterized by its elongated, lance-shaped leaves that often have a waxy surface and a prominent midrib. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or yellowish in color. Its fruits are capsule-like structures that contain numerous tiny seeds.

This species is native to regions of Southeast Asia and is often found in humid, lowland forests.
