Penstemon similis

Penstemon similis, commonly known as the similar beardtongue, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and showy, two-lipped flowers that range in color from pink to purple. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are opposite and lance-shaped, often covered in fine hairs.

Penstemon similis is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
