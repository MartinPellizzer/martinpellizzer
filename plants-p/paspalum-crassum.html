Paspalum crassum

Paspalum crassum, commonly known as broadleaf grass, is a perennial grass species native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its robust, coarse leaves and dense, tufted growth habit. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats, such as marshes and along riverbanks. Its flowering heads are spike-like and contain small, wind-pollinated flowers.

Paspalum crassum is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for wildlife.
