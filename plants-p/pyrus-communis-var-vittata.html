Pyrus communis var. vittata

Pyrus communis var. vittata, commonly known as the variegated pear, is a cultivar of the common pear tree characterized by its distinctive variegated foliage.

The leaves exhibit a striking combination of green, white, and yellow patterns, making it a popular ornamental plant. It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is native to Europe. The tree typically produces small, edible pears with a sweet and sometimes tart flavor.

While it is primarily grown for its aesthetic appeal, it can also yield fruit suitable for culinary use.
