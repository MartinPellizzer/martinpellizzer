Papaver glaucum

Papaver glaucum, commonly known as the blue poppy, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in early summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy, bluish coating, giving it a distinctive appearance. It prefers well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, thriving in cooler climates.

Papaver glaucum is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique color and form.
