Pimpinella decursiva

Pimpinella decursiva, commonly known as wild carrot or bishop's weed, is a biennial or perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features finely divided, fern-like leaves that are aromatic and often used in herbal medicine. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in umbrella-like clusters called umbels. Its seeds are small, oblong, and have a distinctive ridged surface.

Pimpinella decursiva is known for its medicinal properties, including antispasmodic and carminative effects.
