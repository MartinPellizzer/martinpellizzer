Podochilus sumatrensis

Podochilus sumatrensis, commonly known as the Sumatran orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that typically feature a vibrant mix of colors, including yellow, orange, and red. The plant grows as an epiphyte, often found on the trunks or branches of trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are typically arranged in racemes and can bloom multiple times throughout the year.

Podochilus sumatrensis plays an important role in its ecosystem, providing nectar for various pollinators such as bees and birds.
