Phlox pilosa subsp. ozarkana

Phlox pilosa subsp.

ozarkana, commonly known as Ozark fuzzy phlox, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States. It features erect, branching stems covered with fine, fuzzy hairs, giving it a distinctive, soft appearance.

The plant produces clusters of showy, pink to lavender flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered with a similar fuzzy texture, contributing to its overall ornamental appeal.

Ozark fuzzy phlox thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and open areas.
