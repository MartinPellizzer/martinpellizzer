Peperomia leptostachya

Peperomia leptostachya, commonly known as the silver peperomia, is a species native to Central and South America.

It is a compact, evergreen perennial plant characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that often display a silvery or grayish sheen. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in the spring. It thrives in bright, indirect light and prefers well-draining soil with moderate humidity.

Peperomia leptostachya is well-suited for indoor cultivation due to its low maintenance requirements and attractive foliage.
