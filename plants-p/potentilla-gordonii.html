Potentilla gordonii

Potentilla gordonii, commonly known as the Gordon's cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant features showy yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are compound, consisting of five oval-shaped leaflets, and the plant has a woody stem covered in fine hairs.

Potentilla gordonii is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
