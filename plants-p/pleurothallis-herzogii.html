Pleurothallis herzogii

Pleurothallis herzogii, commonly known as the Herzog's orchid, is a species native to the cloud forests of South America.

It is a terrestrial orchid with small, delicate flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and pink. The plant has a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its leaves are usually narrow and grass-like, adapted to the humid, shaded environment it inhabits.

This orchid is prized by horticulturists for its unique floral structure and ability to thrive in specific ecological conditions.
