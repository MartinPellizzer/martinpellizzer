Polystachya gerrardii

Polystachya gerrardii, commonly known as the Schomburgk's spike orchid, is a species of orchid native to the Caribbean and Central America.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and dense clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories or near streams. Its leaves are elongated and strap-like, often arranged in a rosette at the base of the plant.

Polystachya gerrardii is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in tropical gardens.
