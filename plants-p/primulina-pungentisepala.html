Primulina pungentisepala

Primulina pungentisepala, commonly known as the sharp-sepal primulina, is a species of flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Gesneriaceae family and is characterized by its striking, velvety leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its sepals are notably elongated and have a pungent or sharp appearance, giving it its scientific name.

The flowers, which bloom in spring, are often pink or red and attract various pollinators like bees and butterflies.
