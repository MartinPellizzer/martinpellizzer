Parmentiera alata

Parmentiera alata, commonly known as the winged bignay, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its distinctive winged stems and large, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by elongated, woody fruits containing edible seeds. Parmentiera alata is often found in dry, open habitats and is adapted to arid conditions.

It has cultural and medicinal significance in some indigenous communities.
