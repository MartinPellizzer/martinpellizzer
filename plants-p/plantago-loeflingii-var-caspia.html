Plantago loeflingii var. caspia

Plantago loeflingii var. caspia, commonly known as Caspian plantain, is a perennial herb native to the arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its rosette of broad, lance-shaped leaves with a hairy underside. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in dense spikes, which are borne on slender stalks. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

This species is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
