Polygonum pensylvanicum

Polygonum pensylvanicum, commonly known as Pennsylvania smartweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to eastern North America.

It typically grows in moist, disturbed soils along stream banks, wet meadows, and roadside ditches. The plant features upright stems that are often reddish or purplish in color, especially towards the tips. Its small, clustered flowers are usually pink or reddish, appearing in late summer to early fall.

The leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and have a rough texture, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
