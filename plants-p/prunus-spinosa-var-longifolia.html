Prunus spinosa var. longifolia

Prunus spinosa var. longifolia, commonly known as the longleaf blackthorn, is a shrub native to Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny branches and small, dark blue berries that ripen in late summer. The leaves are typically narrow and elongated, contributing to its distinctive appearance. This plant thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in hedgerows and woodland edges.

Its flowers, which appear in early spring, are white and clustered, adding to its ornamental value.
