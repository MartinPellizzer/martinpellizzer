Piptolepis imbricata

Piptolepis imbricata, commonly known as the rock cress, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a small, annual herb that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils. The plant features basal leaves that are linear to lanceolate in shape, and its flowers are small, yellow, and arranged in clusters. It produces a capsule fruit that contains numerous tiny seeds.

Piptolepis imbricata is adapted to arid environments and is often found in coastal or mountainous regions.
