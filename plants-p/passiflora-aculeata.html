Passiflora aculeata

Passiflora aculeata, commonly known as the Thorny Passionflower, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its distinctive, showy flowers with five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments surrounding the reproductive structures. The plant produces edible, fleshy berries that are yellow or orange when mature. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped with serrated edges and can have small thorns along the veins.

Passiflora aculeata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in some cultures for its medicinal properties.
