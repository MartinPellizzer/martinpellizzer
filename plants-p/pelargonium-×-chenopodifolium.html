Pelargonium × chenopodifolium

Pelargonium × chenopodifolium, commonly known as the ivy-leaf geranium, is a hybrid plant characterized by its trailing growth habit and distinctive heart-shaped, dark green leaves with a slightly serrated edge.

It produces clusters of small, two-lipped flowers in shades of pink, red, or white, which bloom throughout the growing season. The plant is native to South Africa and is often cultivated as a decorative trailing plant in hanging baskets or containers. It prefers well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking ornamental foliage and blooms.

Its adaptability and aesthetic appeal contribute to its widespread use in both indoor and outdoor settings.
