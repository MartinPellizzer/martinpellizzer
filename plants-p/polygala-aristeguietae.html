Polygala aristeguietae

Polygala aristeguietae, commonly known as the Aristeguieta's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of central Chile.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its delicate, branched stems and small, often blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. Its unique floral structure resembles that of a milkweed, with a distinctive spur-like appendage at the base of each petal.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and vulnerable species.
