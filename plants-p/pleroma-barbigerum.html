Pleroma barbigerum

Pleroma barbigerum, commonly known as the bird's nest orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique floral structure, with a pouch-like lip that resembles a bird's nest, hence its common name. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist forest environments, often on rocky or humus-rich soil. Its flowers are usually pale green to yellowish in color, with intricate patterns that aid in pollination by specific insects.

Pleroma barbigerum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
