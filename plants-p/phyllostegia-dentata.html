Phyllostegia dentata

Phyllostegia dentata, commonly known as the toothed mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its opposite, toothed leaves and showy, lavender-colored flowers that bloom in the summer. It typically grows in moist, wooded areas and is often found in the understory of forests.

Phyllostegia dentata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
