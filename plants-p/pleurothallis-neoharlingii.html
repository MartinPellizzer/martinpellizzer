Pleurothallis neoharlingii

Pleurothallis neoharlingii, commonly known as the Neoharling's pleurothallis, is a species of orchid native to the cloud forests of Central and South America.

It is a small, epiphytic orchid that typically grows on trees in humid, shaded environments. The plant features delicate, narrow leaves and produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pale pink. It is noted for its intricate floral structure and is often found in association with specific host trees.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
