Potentilla matsumurae

Potentilla matsumurae, commonly known as the Japanese cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit. The plant typically features small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. Its leaves are usually pinnately compound, with several leaflets arranged along the stem.

Potentilla matsumurae is often found in rocky or mountainous regions, thriving in well-drained soils.
