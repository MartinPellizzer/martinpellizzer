Pleurothallis morrisii

Pleurothallis morrisii, commonly known as the Morris's orchid, is a species of orchid native to the Andean cloud forests of South America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its small, intricate flowers that often display a range of colors including white, yellow, and green. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees and deriving moisture and nutrients from the air and rain.

Its botanical features include thin, wiry stems and lance-shaped leaves that are adapted to the humid, shaded environments of its natural habitat.
