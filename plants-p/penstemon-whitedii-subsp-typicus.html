Penstemon whitedii subsp. typicus

Penstemon whitedii subsp.

typicus, commonly known as the Whiteleaf beardtongue, is a perennial flowering plant native to the southwestern United States. It is characterized by its narrow, gray-green leaves and showy, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink.

The plant typically grows in rocky soils and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. It is part of the Plantaginaceae family and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance.

Its nectar-rich flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
