Paeonia sibirica

Paeonia sibirica, commonly known as the Siberian peony, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the cold climates of Siberia and the Russian Far East.

It features large, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and red, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has palmately compound leaves with five to seven leaflets, and its root system consists of thick, fleshy rhizomes. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy addition to gardens in temperate regions.

Paeonia sibirica is valued for its ornamental beauty and historical use in traditional medicine.
