Persicaria orientalis

Persicaria orientalis, commonly known as oriental knotweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to eastern Asia.

It features upright, slender stems that bear opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly serrated edge. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread rapidly, making it both a hardy ornamental and a potential invasive species in certain regions.
