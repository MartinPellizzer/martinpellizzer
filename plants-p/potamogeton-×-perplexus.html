Potamogeton × perplexus

Potamogeton × perplexus, commonly known as the hybrid water plant, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Potamogeton perfoliatus and Potamogeton praelatus.

It is a submerged aquatic plant characterized by its elongated, ribbon-like leaves that are often arranged in whorls around the stem. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are typically clustered in spikelets, which are borne on long, flexible stalks. It thrives in freshwater environments such as lakes, ponds, and slow-moving rivers, where it can form dense underwater vegetation.

Its botanical features make it an important component of aquatic ecosystems, providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
