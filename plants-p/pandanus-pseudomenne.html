Pandanus pseudomenne

Pandanus pseudomenne, commonly known as the false pandanus, is a member of the Pandanaceae family.

It is a shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia, including parts of Indonesia and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its stiff, sword-shaped leaves that are often used in traditional crafts and as a source of fiber. Its roots and leaves are also used in local cuisines for their aromatic properties.

The plant thrives in humid, tropical environments and is often found in coastal areas.
