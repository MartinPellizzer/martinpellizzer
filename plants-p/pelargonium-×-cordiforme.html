Pelargonium × cordiforme

Pelargonium × cordiforme, commonly known as the heartleaf geranium, is a hybrid species within the Geraniaceae family.

It is native to South Africa and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves, which give it its common name. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of pink, red, and white, typically during the summer months. It is a popular ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and blooms.

Pelargonium × cordiforme thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for gardens and containers.
