Paspalum obtusifolium

Paspalum obtusifolium, commonly known as broadleaf paspalum, is a perennial grass species native to parts of the Americas.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered with fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions.

Paspalum obtusifolium is often found in grasslands, disturbed areas, and along roadsides.
