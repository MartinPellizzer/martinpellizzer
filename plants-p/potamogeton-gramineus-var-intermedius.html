Potamogeton gramineus var. intermedius

Potamogeton gramineus var. intermedius, commonly known as the intermediate pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater ecosystems in North America.

It typically grows in shallow, slow-moving waters and has long, narrow leaves that are often ribbon-like in appearance. The plant produces small, green flowers that are clustered in spike-like inflorescences, which emerge from the water surface. It plays an important role in aquatic habitats by providing shelter and food for various aquatic organisms.

Its presence is often an indicator of good water quality and stable ecological conditions.
