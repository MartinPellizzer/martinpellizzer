Pycreus maximiliani

Pycreus maximiliani, commonly known as the Himalayan bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It features upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped flowers, typically in shades of blue or violet. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation efforts.
