Polygonum buchananii

Polygonum buchananii, commonly known as the Buchanan's knotweed, is a perennial herb native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, which is characterized by distinctive arrow-shaped leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as forest understories or along stream banks. Its leaves are often reddish or purple-tinged, and it produces tiny greenish-white flowers in late summer.

Polygonum buchananii is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
