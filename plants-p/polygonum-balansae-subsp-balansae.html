Polygonum balansae subsp. balansae

Polygonum balansae subsp.

balansae, commonly known as the Balansae knotweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and stream banks.

The plant features upright stems with heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small, clustered in spikes, and produce tiny, three-lobed seeds.

It is valued in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties.
