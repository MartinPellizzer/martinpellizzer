Phyllostachys nigra f. hanchiku

Phyllostachys nigra f. hanchiku, commonly known as the black bamboo or black bamboo variant, is a compact, ornamental bamboo species native to China.

It is characterized by its dark, almost black culms that are covered with a dense layer of fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a glossy dark green color that contrasts beautifully with the dark stems. This variety is prized for its elegant form and is often used in landscaping for its aesthetic appeal.

It thrives in well-drained soil and requires partial to full sunlight for optimal growth.
