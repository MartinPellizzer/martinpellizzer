Phyllanthus rigens

Phyllanthus rigens, commonly known as the rigid phyllanthus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Euphorbiaceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific Islands. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. It produces small, clustered yellow flowers, which are followed by distinctive, three-lobed capsules.

Phyllanthus rigens is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
