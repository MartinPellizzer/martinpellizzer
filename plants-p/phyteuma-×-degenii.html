Phyteuma × degenii

Phyteuma × degenii, commonly known as the hybrid bellflower, is a perennial plant native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to violet. The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm and prefers well-drained soil in full sun to partial shade.

Its foliage consists of lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy, and it is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ease of cultivation.
