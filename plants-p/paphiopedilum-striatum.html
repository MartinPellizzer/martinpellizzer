Paphiopedilum striatum

Paphiopedilum striatum, commonly known as the streaked orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique, intricate flower structure with a spurred lip that resembles a pouch. The plant typically grows as a terrestrial or epiphytic orchid, often found in shaded, humid environments. Its leaves are usually dark green and somewhat fleshy, adapted to retain moisture.

The flowers bloom in shades of white, pink, and purple, adding ornamental value to gardens and collections.
