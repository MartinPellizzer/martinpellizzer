Prostanthera incana

Prostanthera incana, commonly known as silver leaf mint bush, is a small shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which gives it a distinctive appearance and helps it reflect sunlight. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically white or pale blue, often arranged in clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to dry, sunny conditions.

Prostanthera incana is valued for its aromatic leaves and is often used in horticulture for its ornamental qualities.
