Polycarena pubescens

Polycarena pubescens, commonly known as the hairy polycarp, is a species of flowering plant in the family Apiaceae.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it its common name. The plant typically grows in moist, temperate environments and is often found in meadows and along stream banks. Its small, yellowish flowers are clustered in umbels, a typical feature of the Apiaceae family.

Polycarena pubescens is known for its aromatic foliage, which can be used in traditional herbal remedies.
