Panicum frederici

Panicum frederici, commonly known as Frederick's grass, is a perennial grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in wetland habitats, including marshes and swamps, and thrives in moist to wet soil conditions. The plant features slender, erect stems and produces fluffy, panicle-shaped inflorescences that are often pink or red in color. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue, and it is valued for its ornamental appeal in wetland restoration projects.

Panicum frederici plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
