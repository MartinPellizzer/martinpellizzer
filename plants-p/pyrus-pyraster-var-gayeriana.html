Pyrus pyraster var. gayeriana

Pyrus pyraster var. gayeriana, commonly known as the Gayer's whitebeam, is a variant of the whitebeam species native to parts of Europe.

It is a deciduous tree or shrub characterized by its small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by small, round fruits that are edible but not particularly flavorful. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or hedgerow environments.

The variety is noted for its ornamental value and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
