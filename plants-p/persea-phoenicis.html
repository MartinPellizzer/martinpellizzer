Persea phoenicis

Persea phoenicis, commonly known as the flame tree or fire tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, palmate leaves that are glossy and dark green. The tree produces showy, orange-red flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. Its fruit is a drupe, typically containing a single seed, and it thrives in warm, humid climates.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its striking floral display.
