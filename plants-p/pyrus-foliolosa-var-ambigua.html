Pyrus foliolosa var. ambigua

Pyrus foliolosa var. ambigua, commonly known as the ambiguous leaf pear, is a variant of the leaf pear species native to China.

It is characterized by its distinctive, deeply lobed leaves that resemble those of other pear species but with subtle variations in shape and arrangement. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in early spring, which are followed by edible, sweet fruits in late summer. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value as well as its fruit.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and somewhat elusive species in the botanical world.
