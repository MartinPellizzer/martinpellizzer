Pittosporum spathulifolium

Pittosporum spathulifolium, commonly known as the broad-leaved pittosporum, is a shrub or small tree native to New Zealand.

It features lance-shaped, dark green leaves that are often glossy and have a distinctive waxy coating. The plant produces fragrant, white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by small, orange-red berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and subalpine regions.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and parks for its attractive foliage and floral display.
