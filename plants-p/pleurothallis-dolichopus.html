Pleurothallis dolichopus

Pleurothallis dolichopus, commonly known as the long-stemmed pleurothallis, is a species of orchid native to the cloud forests of Central and South America.

It is a small, epiphytic orchid that typically grows on trees in humid, shaded environments. The plant produces delicate, pendulous flowers that are usually white or pale pink, with intricate patterns on the petals and sepals. It has a compact growth habit with short, fleshy roots and is relatively easy to care for in a humid, well-draining potting mix.

Due to its beauty and rarity, it is a sought-after species among orchid enthusiasts.
