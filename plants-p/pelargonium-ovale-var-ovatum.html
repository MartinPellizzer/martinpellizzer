Pelargonium ovale var. ovatum

Pelargonium ovale var. ovatum, commonly known as the Fat-leaf Geranium, is a compact, trailing plant native to South Africa.

It features large, rounded, velvety leaves that are typically a deep green with a slightly waxy texture. The plant produces small, showy flowers in shades of pink, red, and white, often in clusters at the ends of its stems. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This variety is popular in hanging baskets and containers due to its trailing habit and ornamental foliage.
