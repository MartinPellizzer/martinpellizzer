Pleurothallis spicata

Pleurothallis spicata, commonly known as the spike orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and elegant, spike-like inflorescences. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white, yellow, and green, often with intricate patterns. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value, it is a popular choice for indoor cultivation and botanical gardens.
