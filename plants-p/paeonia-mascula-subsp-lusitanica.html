Paeonia mascula subsp. lusitanica

Paeonia mascula subsp.

lusitanica, commonly known as the Iberian peony, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe. It features showy, single or double pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring.

The plant has palmately lobed leaves with a rough texture and a distinctive, aromatic scent. It prefers well-drained soil and partial shade, often growing in rocky or woodland habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
