Pedicularis comosa subsp. comosa

Pedicularis comosa subsp.

comosa, commonly known as the woolly lousewort, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Asia. It is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive, rugged appearance.

The plant produces striking, spurred flowers that are typically pink or red, often arranged in dense clusters. It thrives in cool, moist habitats such as alpine meadows and rocky slopes.

Due to its specialized habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
