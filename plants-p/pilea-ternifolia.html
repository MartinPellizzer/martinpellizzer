Pilea ternifolia

Pilea ternifolia, commonly known as the Chinese money plant, is a compact, evergreen perennial native to China.

It features rosettes of thick, waxy, heart-shaped leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers that appear in spring. It thrives in indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular houseplant.

Its ability to propagate through leaf cuttings adds to its appeal among plant enthusiasts.
