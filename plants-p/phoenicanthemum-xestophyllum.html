Phoenicanthemum xestophyllum

Phoenicanthemum xestophyllum, commonly known as the coastal sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and its ability to thrive in sandy, well-drained soils. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink and white, which bloom throughout the growing season. It is well adapted to coastal environments, tolerating salt spray and strong winds.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is often cultivated in coastal gardens and restoration projects.
