Pyrethrum abrotanifolium

Pyrethrum abrotanifolium, commonly known as wild chrysanthemum, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow to orange flower heads. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that bloom in late summer. It is often found in dry, rocky soils and is valued for its ornamental appeal.

Pyrethrum abrotanifolium is also used in traditional medicine and as a source of natural pesticides.
