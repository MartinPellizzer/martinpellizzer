Piriadacus erubescens

Piriadacus erubescens, commonly known as the red-flowered orchid, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, velvety red petals and a distinctive yellow lip. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers bloom in clusters and are known for their fragrant, spicy scent.

Piriadacus erubescens is valued both for its ornamental beauty and its cultural significance in traditional medicine.
