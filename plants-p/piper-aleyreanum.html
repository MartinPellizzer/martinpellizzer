Piper aleyreanum

Piper aleyreanum, commonly known as the Aleyrean pepper, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species such as black pepper. The plant is characterized by its climbing habit, with long, slender stems that often twine around supporting structures. Its leaves are typically ovate to elliptical in shape, with a glossy surface and a strong, aromatic scent.

The small, greenish-yellow flowers are followed by round, peppery fruits that are used in traditional medicine and culinary applications.
