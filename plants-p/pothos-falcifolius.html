Pothos falcifolius

Pothos falcifolius, commonly known as the arrowhead vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, waxy leaves that are typically bright green with a slightly variegated appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers followed by dark blue berries. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil.

Pothos falcifolius is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental foliage and ease of care.
