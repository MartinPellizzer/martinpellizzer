Pimelea colorans var. apiculata

Pimelea colorans var. apiculata, commonly known as the pink wattle, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Australia.

It is a shrub characterized by its narrow, grey-green leaves and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in sandy or well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or heathlands. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.

The species is also noted for its ability to thrive in coastal areas due to its tolerance of salt spray.
