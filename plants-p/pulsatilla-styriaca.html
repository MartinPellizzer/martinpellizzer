Pulsatilla styriaca

Pulsatilla styriaca, commonly known as the Styrian pasqueflower, is a perennial herb native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its early spring blooms of pale yellow to pink flowers. The plant features a short, hairy stem with palmate leaves that are deeply lobed. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine regions.

Pulsatilla styriaca is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
