Pyrus salatinii

Pyrus salatinii, commonly known as the Saltspring pear, is a species of flowering plant in the family Rosaceae.

It is native to the Pacific Northwest of North America, particularly found in coastal regions of British Columbia and Washington. The plant is characterized by its small, deciduous leaves and clusters of white flowers that bloom in early spring. Its fruit, a small pear-like berry, is edible and has a tart flavor.

Pyrus salatinii is often grown in gardens for its ornamental value and historical significance in local ecosystems.
