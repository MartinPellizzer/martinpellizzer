Potentilla grossa

Potentilla grossa, commonly known as the broadleaf cinquefoil, is a perennial shrub native to North America.

It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or streamside habitats. The plant features compound leaves with five to seven elliptic leaflets, and it produces clusters of yellow flowers in late spring to early summer. Its botanical characteristics include a woody stem, and it is often found in the eastern United States.

Potentilla grossa is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to pollinators.
