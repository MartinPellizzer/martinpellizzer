Pyrus canoplia

Pyrus canoplia, commonly known as the canoplia pear, is a species of flowering plant in the family Rosaceae.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, where it thrives in temperate climates. The plant produces small, ornamental flowers that bloom in spring, followed by edible fruits that are similar to common pears. Its leaves are broad and lobed, with a slightly hairy underside, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

The canoplia pear is valued both for its ornamental beauty and its potential as a fruit-bearing plant in cultivation.
