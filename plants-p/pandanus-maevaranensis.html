Pandanus maevaranensis

Pandanus maevaranensis, commonly known as the Mauvaranensis pandanus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Pandanaceae.

It is native to the island of Savai'i in Samoa and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and long, narrow leaves that are often used in traditional crafts. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences at the base of the leaves.

It thrives in coastal areas and is adapted to saline and humid conditions, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
