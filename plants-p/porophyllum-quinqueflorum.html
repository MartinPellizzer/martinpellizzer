Porophyllum quinqueflorum

Porophyllum quinqueflorum, commonly known as the five-flowered cudweed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, and it produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

Porophyllum quinqueflorum is often used in traditional medicine and is also valued for its ornamental appeal.
