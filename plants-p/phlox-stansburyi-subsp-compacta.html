Phlox stansburyi subsp. compacta

Phlox stansburyi subsp.

compacta, commonly known as the compact phlox, is a perennial flowering plant native to the southwestern United States. It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and produces showy clusters of pink to purple flowers in the summer.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery-white coating.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
