Phebalium ambiguum

Phebalium ambiguum, commonly known as the variable hakea, is a flowering plant native to southern Australia.

It belongs to the Proteaceae family and is characterized by its woody structure and dense, needle-like leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in spring, attracting various pollinators. Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
