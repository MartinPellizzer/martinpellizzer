Petroselinum crispum var. angustifolium

Petroselinum crispum var. angustifolium, commonly known as Italian parsley, is a biennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features finely divided, feathery leaves that are typically bright green and have a slightly curled appearance. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by small, round seeds. Its aromatic foliage is rich in essential oils, contributing to its distinctive flavor and culinary uses.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for both garden cultivation and commercial agriculture.
