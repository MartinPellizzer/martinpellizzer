Pentaptera pyrifolia

Pentaptera pyrifolia, commonly known as the Japanese maple, is a deciduous tree native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its ornamental foliage, which changes color with the seasons, displaying vibrant red, orange, and yellow hues in autumn. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are typically palmately lobed, with five to seven distinct lobes. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, appearing in clusters, while the fruit is a samara, a type of winged seed pod.

The tree thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping.
