Polypogon monspeliensis var. paniceus

Polypogon monspeliensis var. paniceus, commonly known as panic grass, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are often tinged with red or purple hues. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along the edges of water bodies.

Its botanical features make it a valuable plant for ecological restoration and wildlife habitat.
