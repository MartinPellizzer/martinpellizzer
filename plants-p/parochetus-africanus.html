Parochetus africanus

Parochetus africanus, commonly known as the African star grass, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, white or pale blue flowers arranged in dense clusters, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands and along riverbanks.

Parochetus africanus is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat.
