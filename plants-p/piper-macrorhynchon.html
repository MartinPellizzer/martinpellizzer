Piper macrorhynchon

Piper macrorhynchon, commonly known as the large-lobed pepper vine, is a climbing shrub native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many species of pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and small, clustered white flowers that develop into elongated, fleshy berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

The berries are typically consumed raw or used in traditional remedies, though the plant is not widely cultivated for commercial purposes.
