Peperomia balineorum

Peperomia balineorum, commonly known as the Balinese peperomia, is a compact, evergreen perennial native to the tropical regions of Indonesia.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that are typically green with a waxy texture, often displaying variegation or reddish veins. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters, usually green or reddish, which are followed by small, round berries. It thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Due to its low maintenance and attractive foliage, it is often cultivated as a decorative plant.
