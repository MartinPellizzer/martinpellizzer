Phlomoides ebracteolata

Phlomoides ebracteolata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved phlomoides, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pale blue or violet in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands.

Phlomoides ebracteolata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
