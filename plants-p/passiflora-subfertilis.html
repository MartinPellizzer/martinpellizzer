Passiflora subfertilis

Passiflora subfertilis, commonly known as the Brazilian passionflower, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that feature a complex arrangement of petals, sepals, and filaments. The plant produces edible, orange-red berries that are rich in antioxidants. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and ecological restoration projects.
