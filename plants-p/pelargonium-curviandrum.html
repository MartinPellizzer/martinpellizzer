Pelargonium curviandrum

Pelargonium curviandrum, commonly known as the curved-stem geranium, is a species native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, curved stems and small, clustered flowers that range from pink to purple in color. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are usually narrow and slightly hairy, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Pelargonium curviandrum is often cultivated for its unique appearance and adaptability to various garden conditions.
