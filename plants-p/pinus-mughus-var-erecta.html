Pinus mughus var. erecta

Pinus mughus var. erecta, commonly known as the upright blue spruce, is a variant of the blue spruce native to the mountainous regions of central Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, dark blue needles and a compact, upright growth habit. The plant produces cone-like structures that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Its foliage remains evergreen throughout the year, providing year-round visual interest.

This variety is often cultivated for ornamental purposes in gardens and landscapes due to its aesthetic appeal and hardy nature.
