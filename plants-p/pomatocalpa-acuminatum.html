Pomatocalpa acuminatum

Pomatocalpa acuminatum, commonly known as the acuminated orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and pink. The plant has a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its leaves are usually long and narrow, arranged in a rosette pattern around the base of the plant.

The orchid thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil for optimal growth.
