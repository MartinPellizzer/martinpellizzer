Pyrus taurica

Pyrus taurica, commonly known as the Caucasian whitebeam, is a deciduous tree native to the Caucasus region.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its small, white flowers and oval-shaped leaves. The tree produces small, edible fruits that are similar to apples but less commonly consumed. It is often found in mountainous areas and is adapted to harsh climates.

The bark is smooth and grayish, with a distinctive appearance that aids in its identification.
