Persicaria macrophylla

Persicaria macrophylla, commonly known as the broadleaf plantain, is a perennial herb native to temperate regions of Asia and Europe.

It features heart-shaped, broad leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving them a fuzzy texture. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in late summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, stream banks, and woodland edges.

Its robust growth and adaptability make it a common weed in agricultural and disturbed areas.
