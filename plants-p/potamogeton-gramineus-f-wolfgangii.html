Potamogeton gramineus f. wolfgangii

Potamogeton gramineus f. wolfgangii, commonly known as the Wolfgang's pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in Europe.

It belongs to the family Potamogetonaceae and is characterized by its long, ribbon-like leaves that are typically green to reddish in color. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually arranged in spike-like clusters. It thrives in shallow, slow-moving waters and plays a crucial role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various water organisms.

Its presence is often an indicator of good water quality in its native habitats.
