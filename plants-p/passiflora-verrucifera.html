Passiflora verrucifera

Passiflora verrucifera, commonly known as the Hawaiian passionflower, is a climbing vine native to the tropical regions of the Pacific.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and striking, showy flowers that feature a blend of blue, purple, and white hues. The plant produces edible, yellow-orange berries that are rich in nutrients. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is often cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
