Phaseolus reticulatus

Phaseolus reticulatus, commonly known as the scarlet runner bean, is a flowering plant in the legume family.

It is native to the Americas and is characterized by its climbing habit and vibrant red flowers. The plant produces elongated, cylindrical pods that contain edible beans, which are rich in protein and nutrients. Its leaves are trifoliate, with heart-shaped leaflets, and the plant thrives in warm, sunny climates.

It is often cultivated for both ornamental and culinary purposes.
