Psilotrichum cyathuloides

Psilotrichum cyathuloides, commonly known as the narrow-leaved clubmoss, is a small, evergreen fern-like plant belonging to the family Psilotaceae.

It is characterized by its diminutive, scale-like leaves and a creeping rhizome system that allows it to spread along the forest floor. Native to moist, shaded environments in temperate regions, it thrives in humid, nutrient-rich soils. The plant produces small, inconspicuous spore-bearing structures called sporangia, which are clustered in cup-like formations called cyathula.

Its unique morphology and ecological role make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
