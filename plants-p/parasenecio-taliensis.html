Parasenecio taliensis

Parasenecio taliensis, commonly known as the Taliensis fleabane, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern China.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories or along stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a central disc.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
