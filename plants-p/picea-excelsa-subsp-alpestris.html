Picea excelsa subsp. alpestris

Picea excelsa subsp.

alpestris, commonly known as the alpine fir, is a subspecies of the white spruce native to high-altitude regions of the Himalayas. It is a coniferous evergreen tree characterized by its slender, pyramidal shape and dark green, needle-like leaves.

The cones are typically small and cylindrical, maturing to a reddish-brown color. This subspecies thrives in cool, moist environments and is adapted to withstand harsh alpine conditions.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat for various alpine species and contributing to soil stabilization in mountainous regions.
