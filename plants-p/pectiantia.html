Pectiantia

Pectiantia, commonly known as the golden spurge, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Euphorbiaceae.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy stems and leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers clustered in a structure called a cyathium, which is typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. Pectiantia thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, making it a hardy species adapted to drought conditions.
