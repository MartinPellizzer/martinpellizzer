Plantago gentianoides var. tatarica

Plantago gentianoides var. tatarica, commonly known as Tatarian plantain, is a perennial herb native to Central Asia and parts of Eastern Europe.

It features lance-shaped leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface, and it produces small, blue to purple flowers arranged in a spike. The plant is adapted to grow in dry, rocky, or sandy soils and is often found in steppe and grassland habitats. It has a fibrous root system that helps it survive in harsh environments.

This variety is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
