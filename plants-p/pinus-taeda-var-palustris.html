Pinus taeda var. palustris

Pinus taeda var. palustris, commonly known as the pond pine, is a variant of the loblolly pine native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its ability to thrive in wet, swampy environments, making it a common species in poorly drained soils. The tree typically has a slender, often crooked trunk and exhibits a pyramidal shape when young, which tends to become more irregular with age. Its needles are longer than those of the typical loblolly pine, and it produces small, clustered cones.

This variety is valued for its ecological role in wetland ecosystems and its use in reforestation projects.
