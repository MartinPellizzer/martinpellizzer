Protea venosa

Protea venosa, commonly known as the silver protea, is a striking South African plant belonging to the Proteaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescence composed of numerous white to pale pink flowers arranged in a spherical or conical shape. The plant features a woody stem with a fibrous, spongy interior that provides structural support. Its leaves are typically narrow and linear, often covered in a fine, silvery-white coating that reflects light.

Protea venosa thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is adapted to the Mediterranean climate of its native region.
