Pavetta handenina

Pavetta handenina, commonly known as the Cape snowflake, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 meter, characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage. The plant produces small, white, star-shaped flowers that bloom in clusters during the winter months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
