Peschiera praeclara

Peschiera praeclara, commonly known as the splendid marsh orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to the wetlands of southern Europe.

It is characterized by its striking, pale purple to white flowers with intricate patterns that aid in pollination by insects. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and thrives in shaded, wetland environments. Its leaves are linear and grass-like, while its roots are fibrous and adapted to waterlogged conditions.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered a rare and endangered species in many regions.
