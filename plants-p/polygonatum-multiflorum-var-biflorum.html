Polygonatum multiflorum var. biflorum

Polygonatum multiflorum var. biflorum, commonly known as the twin-flowered Solomon's seal, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to East Asia.

It features slender, arching stems that bear clusters of small, white, bell-shaped flowers. The plant produces elliptical, dark green leaves arranged in a basal rosette. Its fruit is a red, fleshy berry that contains several seeds.

It thrives in shaded, moist woodland habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional medicinal uses.
