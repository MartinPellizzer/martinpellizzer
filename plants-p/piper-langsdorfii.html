Piper langsdorfii

Piper langsdorfii, commonly known as the Langsdorf's pepper plant, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species such as black pepper. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves and small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in clusters. It produces small, fleshy berries that contain the aromatic seeds used in traditional medicine and culinary applications.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and research settings.
