Peperomia selenophylla

Peperomia selenophylla, commonly known as the silver-leaf peperomia, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It features thick, waxy leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery or grayish pubescence, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters, typically green or reddish, which are followed by small, round berries. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Its unique foliage makes it a popular choice for indoor gardening and decorative purposes.
