Persica simonii

Persica simonii, commonly known as the Chinese sand pear.

It is a deciduous tree native to northern China, characterized by its small, oval-shaped leaves and clusters of white flowers in spring. The tree produces small, yellowish fruits that are typically harvested in late summer. It is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.

Persica simonii is adapted to arid and semi-arid climates, making it a resilient choice for landscaping in such environments.
