Pilea macropoda

Pilea macropoda, commonly known as the Chinese money plant, is a compact, trailing plant native to China.

It features thick, waxy, round leaves that resemble small coins, giving it its common name. The plant has a shallow root system and thrives in bright, indirect light. It is often grown as a houseplant due to its low maintenance and attractive foliage.

Pilea macropoda is known for its ability to spread easily through runners, making it a popular choice for hanging baskets or shelves.
