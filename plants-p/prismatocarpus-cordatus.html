Prismatocarpus cordatus

Prismatocarpus cordatus, commonly known as the heart-shaped mangosteen, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its elliptical, heart-shaped leaves that are glossy and dark green in color. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, followed by edible, segmented fruits. Its fruit has a thick, purple rind and a white, juicy pulp that is rich in nutrients.

The tree is valued for its ornamental beauty and is also cultivated for its medicinal and culinary uses.
