Potamogeton polygonifolius cordifolius

Potamogeton polygonifolius cordifolius, commonly known as the broad-leaf pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in Europe and parts of Asia.

It features long, slender stems that often branch and produce dense clusters of small, green, ovate leaves. The plant has a complex root system that helps stabilize sediments and improve water clarity. It blooms with small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically arranged in spike-like clusters.

This species plays a vital role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various water organisms.
