Plantago connivens

Plantago connivens, commonly known as the common plantain, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It features a rosette of broad, waxy leaves that are often ribbed and covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in a spike, which are pollinated by wind. It thrives in a variety of habitats, including roadsides, fields, and waste areas, due to its adaptability.

Its leaves are sometimes used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.
