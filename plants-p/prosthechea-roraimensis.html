Prosthechea roraimensis

Prosthechea roraimensis, commonly known as the Roraima heliconia, is a species of orchid native to the tepuis of Venezuela and Brazil.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescences that bear numerous yellow-orange flowers with a distinctive spiral arrangement. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments at high altitudes, often on rock faces or in crevices. Its leaves are broad and sword-shaped, adapted to capture moisture from the air.

Prosthechea roraimensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical greenhouses.
