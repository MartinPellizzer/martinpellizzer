Phaseolus nelsonii

Phaseolus nelsonii, commonly known as the scarlet runner bean, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its climbing habit and large, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant produces elongated, edible pods that contain seeds used for food and cultivation. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for both ornamental and agricultural purposes.
