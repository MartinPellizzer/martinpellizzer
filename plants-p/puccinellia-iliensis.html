Puccinellia iliensis

Puccinellia iliensis, commonly known as the Tien Shan grass, is a perennial grass species native to Central Asia.

It typically grows in arid and semi-arid regions, often found in steppes and mountainous areas. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It is adapted to tolerate drought and poor soil conditions.

Its ecological importance includes stabilizing soils and serving as forage for grazing animals.
