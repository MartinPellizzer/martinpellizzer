Paspalum tumidum

Paspalum tumidum, commonly known as the hairy paspalum, is a perennial grass species native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and dense, tufted inflorescences that resemble panicles. The leaves are typically narrow and have a hairy texture, which gives the plant its common name. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in grasslands, wetlands, and disturbed areas.

This plant plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
