Puccinellia triflora

Puccinellia triflora, commonly known as three-flowered glassworts, is a flowering plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is a member of the Amaranthaceae family and typically grows in saline or alkaline soils. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in dense spikes, and its leaves are often succulent and fleshy. It is often found in coastal areas, salt marshes, and other moist, saline environments.

Puccinellia triflora plays a role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for various insects and microorganisms.
