Prunus sogdiana

Prunus sogdiana, commonly known as the Sogdian plum, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are typically ovate to elliptic in shape. The plant produces small, clustered flowers in spring, followed by edible drupes that range in color from yellow to red. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in mountainous regions.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been introduced to other parts of the world for horticultural purposes.
