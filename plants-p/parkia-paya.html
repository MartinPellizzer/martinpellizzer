Parkia paya

Parkia paya, commonly known as the yellow flame tree, is a deciduous leguminous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking yellow inflorescences and pinnate leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant belongs to the Fabaceae family and is known for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, enriching the soil. Its seeds are enclosed in a fibrous pod and are used in various cultural practices.

The tree is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in tropical ecosystems.
