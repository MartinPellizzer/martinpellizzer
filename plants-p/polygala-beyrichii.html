Polygala beyrichii

Polygala beyrichii, commonly known as the Cape honeysuckle, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its showy, tubular flowers that resemble small honeybees. The plant typically grows as a shrub or subshrub, with woody stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy habitats.

The flowers are pollinated by insects, particularly bees, and produce small, capsule-like fruits.
