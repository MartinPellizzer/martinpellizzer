Piper asperifolium

Piper asperifolium, commonly known as the rough-leaved pepper vine, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species. The plant is characterized by its rough, serrated leaves and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. Its fruits are small berries that turn red when mature, and the plant is often grown as an ornamental due to its attractive foliage.

It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with high humidity and partial shade.
