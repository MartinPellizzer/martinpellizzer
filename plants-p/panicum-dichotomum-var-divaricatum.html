Panicum dichotomum var. divaricatum

Panicum dichotomum var. divaricatum, commonly known as the slender panicgrass, is a grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and fine, thread-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are often pinkish or purplish in hue, adding ornamental value to landscapes. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is drought-tolerant once established.

This variety is often used in native plant gardens for its ecological benefits and aesthetic appeal.
