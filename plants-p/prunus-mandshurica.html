Prunus mandshurica

Prunus mandshurica, commonly known as the Manchurian cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its simple, ovate leaves that are typically hairy on the underside. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers in early spring, followed by edible berries that are typically red or black when mature. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forested areas.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in herbal medicine.
