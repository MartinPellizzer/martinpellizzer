Panicum sanguinale

Panicum sanguinale, commonly known as red panicgrass, is a flowering plant species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, spike-like clusters of flowers. The plant produces pink to red bracts, which give it its common name and contribute to its ornamental value. It typically grows in well-drained soils and thrives in full sun.

Panicum sanguinale is often used in landscaping for its attractive appearance and drought tolerance.
