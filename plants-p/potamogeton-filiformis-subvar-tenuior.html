Potamogeton filiformis subvar. tenuior

Potamogeton filiformis subvar. tenuior, commonly known as the slender pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in North America.

It has long, thread-like leaves that are typically less than 1 millimeter in width, giving it a delicate appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually arranged in spike-like clusters. It thrives in shallow, slow-moving waters and is often found in lakes, ponds, and wetlands.

Its morphology and growth habits make it an important component of aquatic ecosystems, supporting a variety of aquatic life.
