Parsonsia momiensis

Parsonsia momiensis, commonly known as the Momi Island flame tree, is a flowering plant native to the South Pacific.

It is characterized by its striking red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters, adding vibrant color to its natural habitat. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are opposite in arrangement, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in coastal or lowland areas.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for horticultural displays in warm regions.
