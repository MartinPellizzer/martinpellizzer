Phyllanthus roseus

Phyllanthus roseus, commonly known as the roseleaf phyllanthus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and often have a waxy texture.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
