Pectis filipes var. filipes

Pectis filipes var. filipes, commonly known as the narrowleaf pectis, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in grasslands and rocky areas, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are narrow and linear, with a hairy texture, and it produces small yellow flowers arranged in clusters.
