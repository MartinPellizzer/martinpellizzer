Phlox glaberrima var. melampyrifolia

Phlox glaberrima var. melampyrifolia, commonly known as the Florida phlox, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in wetlands and swampy areas, thriving in moist, acidic soils. The plant features showy, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to red, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often covered in fine hairs.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in wetland habitats.
