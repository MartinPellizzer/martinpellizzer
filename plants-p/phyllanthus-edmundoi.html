Phyllanthus edmundoi

Phyllanthus edmundoi, commonly known as the Indian cheesewood, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small evergreen shrub that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. Its flowers are small and clustered in inflorescences, often with yellow or white petals.

The fruit is a small, three-lobed capsule that contains several seeds.
