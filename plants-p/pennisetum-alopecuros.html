Pennisetum alopecuros

Pennisetum alopecuros, commonly known as purple lovegrass, is a flowering plant native to Africa.

It is a member of the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and fluffy, purple-tinted inflorescences. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it adaptable to various environmental conditions.

Its ornamental value is due to its attractive, feathery appearance, which makes it a popular choice for landscaping.
