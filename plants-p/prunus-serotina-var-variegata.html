Prunus serotina var. variegata

Prunus serotina var. variegata, commonly known as the variegated black cherry, is a variant of the black cherry tree native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its distinctive variegated leaves that display white or yellow patterns against a dark green background. The plant belongs to the Rosaceae family and produces small, edible fruits that are typically harvested in the fall. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained, loamy soils.

This ornamental tree is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal and ecological benefits.
