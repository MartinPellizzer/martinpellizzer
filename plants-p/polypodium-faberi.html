Polypodium faberi

Polypodium faberi, commonly known as the Chinese shield fern, is a species of fern native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its large, leathery, and deeply divided fronds that can reach up to 1 meter in length. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky areas. Its rhizomes are thick and often covered in a dense layer of brown, velvety hair.

The fronds are evergreen and remain attached to the plant for several years before being replaced.
