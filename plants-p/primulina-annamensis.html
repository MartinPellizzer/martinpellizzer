Primulina annamensis

Primulina annamensis, commonly known as the Annam primula, is a species of flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its compact, rosette-forming growth habit. The plant produces small, showy flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in mountainous regions.

Due to its ornamental value and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
