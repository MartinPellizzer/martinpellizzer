Pedicularis × mantzii

Pedicularis × mantzii, commonly known as the hybrid Indian blanket flower, is a member of the Orobanchaceae family and is native to the mountainous regions of central Asia.

This herbaceous perennial produces showy, bilaterally symmetrical flowers that are typically orange-red with a contrasting yellow throat. The plant is characterized by its slender, erect stems and opposite, ovate leaves that are often hairy. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Pedicularis × mantzii is valued in horticulture for its vibrant floral display and is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental appeal.
