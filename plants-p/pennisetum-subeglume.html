Pennisetum subeglume

Pennisetum subeglume, commonly known as purple fountain grass, is a perennial grass native to Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and panicles of purple to pink bracts that give it a distinctive, feathery appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in dense, spike-like inflorescences. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Pennisetum subeglume is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as butterflies and bees.
