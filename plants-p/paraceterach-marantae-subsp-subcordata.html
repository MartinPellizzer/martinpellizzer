Paraceterach marantae subsp. subcordata

Paraceterach marantae subsp.

subcordata, commonly known as the subcordate arrowroot, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of South America. It belongs to the Marantaceae family, known for its ornamental value and unique foliage.

The plant features lanceolate to ovate leaves with a distinctive subcordate base, often adorned with striking patterns of veins and coloration. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is cultivated for its aesthetic appeal and potential medicinal properties.

Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense clumps in suitable conditions.
