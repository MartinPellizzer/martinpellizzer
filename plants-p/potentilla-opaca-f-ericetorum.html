Potentilla opaca f. ericetorum

Potentilla opaca f. ericetorum, commonly known as the heather potentilla, is a compact, low-growing shrub native to the British Isles.

It features dense clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has dark green, leathery leaves that are typically arranged in a compact, rounded form. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in heathland and moorland environments.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to garden conditions.
