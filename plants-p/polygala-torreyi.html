Polygala torreyi

Polygala torreyi, commonly known as Torrey's milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae, which is characterized by its distinctive floral structures and often fragrant blooms. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats, showcasing its adaptability to arid environments. Its leaves are alternate and pinnately compound, with small, lance-shaped leaflets.

The flowers are usually yellow or greenish-yellow, arranged in clusters, and are known for their unique, spurred shape.
