Pelargonium × chrysosplenium

Pelargonium × chrysosplenium, commonly known as the variegated geranium, is a hybrid plant that combines traits from different species of geraniums.

It is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that come in shades of red, pink, and white, often with contrasting markings. The plant typically has succulent, lobed leaves that are often variegated with white or yellow patterns. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for gardens and containers.

Due to its ornamental value and relatively easy care, it is widely cultivated for both decorative and medicinal purposes.
