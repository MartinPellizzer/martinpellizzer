Penstemon acuminatus f. roseus

Penstemon acuminatus f. roseus, commonly known as the rose-flowered beardtongue, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and striking, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to rose. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, attracting pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds.
