Piper subeburneum

Piper subeburneum, commonly known as the red-flowered pepper vine, is a climbing shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes various species of pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its small, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of bright red berries that are edible. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in the undergrowth of tropical forests.

The berries are used in traditional medicine and as a spice in some local cuisines.
