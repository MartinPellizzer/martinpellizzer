Pteris guineensis

Pteris guineensis, commonly known as the African water fern, is a species of fern native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its delicate, frond-like structures that resemble small, feathered leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests or near water sources. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The fronds are also sometimes cultivated as ornamental plants due to their aesthetic appeal.
