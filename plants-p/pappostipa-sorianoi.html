Pappostipa sorianoi

Pappostipa sorianoi, commonly known as the Sorianoi grass, is a rare and endemic grass species native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and small, spikelet-based inflorescences. The plant typically thrives in moist, shaded environments, such as forest understories or wetlands. Its leaves are narrow and often have a bluish-green hue, while its seeds are enclosed in distinctive, bristle-like structures.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a valuable subject for botanical research and conservation efforts.
