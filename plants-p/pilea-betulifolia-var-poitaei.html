Pilea betulifolia var. poitaei

Pilea betulifolia var. poitaei, commonly known as the Chinese money plant, is a compact, low-maintenance houseplant native to China.

It features round, glossy, waxy leaves that resemble small coins, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers that bloom in spring, followed by red berries. It thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it ideal for indoor cultivation.

Its unique appearance and ease of care have made it a popular choice among plant enthusiasts.
