Platanthera hondoensis

Platanthera hondoensis, commonly known as the Hondo orchid, is a rare and endemic orchid species native to Japan.

It is a terrestrial herb with a slender, leafless stem that produces a single spike of fragrant, greenish-white flowers. The plant thrives in shaded, moist habitats such as forests and mountainous regions. Its flowers have a distinctive, sweet scent that attracts nocturnal pollinators like moths.

Due to habitat loss and limited distribution, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
