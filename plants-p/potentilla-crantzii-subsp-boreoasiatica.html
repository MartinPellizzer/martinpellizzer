Potentilla crantzii subsp. boreoasiatica

Potentilla crantzii subsp.

boreoasiatica, commonly known as the northern potentilla, is a low-growing perennial shrub native to the Arctic and subarctic regions of Eurasia. It typically features small, narrow leaves and produces clusters of yellow flowers in late summer.

The plant thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to cold climates with long winters. It is often found in alpine tundra and dry grasslands.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important component of high-latitude ecosystems.
