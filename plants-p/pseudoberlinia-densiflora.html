Pseudoberlinia densiflora

Pseudoberlinia densiflora, commonly known as the African velvetwood, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and smooth, grey bark. The tree produces small, yellow flowers followed by long, narrow pods containing flat, bean-like seeds. It is often valued for its hard, durable wood and its ability to thrive in various soil types.

Additionally, it plays an important role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for wildlife.
