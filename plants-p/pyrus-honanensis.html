Pyrus honanensis

Pyrus honanensis, commonly known as the Honan pear, is a species of deciduous tree native to China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and ovate leaves with serrated edges. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers in spring, which are followed by edible pears that are typically sweet and aromatic.

It is often cultivated for its fruit and ornamental value, though it is less commonly found in commercial agriculture compared to other pear species.
