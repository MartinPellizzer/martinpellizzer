Peliosanthes serrulata

Peliosanthes serrulata, commonly known as the Chinese sandalwood.

It is a small evergreen tree native to southern China, characterized by its lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by small, woody fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in moist, shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value and aromatic properties, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and traditional medicine practices.
