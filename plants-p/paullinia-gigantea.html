Paullinia gigantea

Paullinia gigantea, commonly known as the giant soursop, is a tropical plant native to the Amazon basin.

It is a large shrub or small tree that can grow up to 10 meters tall, characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage. The plant produces large, yellow or green fruits with a spiny exterior and a sweet, aromatic pulp. Its leaves are typically large, alternate, and have a rough, textured surface.

Paullinia gigantea is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in local medicine.
