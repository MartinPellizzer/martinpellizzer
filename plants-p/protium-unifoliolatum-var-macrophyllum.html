Protium unifoliolatum var. macrophyllum

Protium unifoliolatum var. macrophyllum, commonly known as the large-leaf sandalwood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, leathery leaves that are usually elliptical in shape and have a single terminal leaflet. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically greenish-white in color, followed by woody fruits containing oval seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland evergreen forests.

The tree is valued for its aromatic wood, which is used in traditional medicine and as a source of essential oils.
