Panicum mistasypus

Panicum mistasypus, commonly known as the silverleaf panicgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, silver-gray leaves and compact, fluffy seed heads that resemble small clouds. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in open woodlands and disturbed areas. Its foliage is covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive silvery appearance.

Panicum mistasypus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in native plant gardens.
