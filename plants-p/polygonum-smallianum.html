Polygonum smallianum

Polygonum smallianum, commonly known as the smallianum knotweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, which is characterized by its distinctive nodes and often alternate leaves. The plant typically grows in moist environments such as wetlands and stream banks. Its stems are often reddish in color and can become woody with age.

The small, greenish flowers appear in clusters and are accompanied by bracts that are often mistaken for petals.
