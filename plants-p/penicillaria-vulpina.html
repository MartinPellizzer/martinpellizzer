Penicillaria vulpina

Penicillaria vulpina, commonly known as the foxglove penicillaria, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Apiaceae.

It is native to southern Europe and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in umbels. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in Mediterranean regions. Its foliage consists of finely divided, fern-like leaves that are aromatic when crushed.

Penicillaria vulpina is noted for its medicinal properties, though it contains toxic compounds and should be handled with care.
