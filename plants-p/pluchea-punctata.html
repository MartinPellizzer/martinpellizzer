Pluchea punctata

Pluchea punctata, commonly known as the spotted groundsel, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows as a herbaceous annual or perennial, often reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its leaves are alternate, ovate to lanceolate, and usually have a rough texture with prominent veins.

The flowers are yellow, with a central disk surrounded by ray florets, and the plant produces small, hairy seeds that are dispersed by wind.
