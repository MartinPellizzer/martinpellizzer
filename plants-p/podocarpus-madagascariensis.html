Podocarpus madagascariensis

Podocarpus madagascariensis, commonly known as the African yellowwood, is a medium-sized evergreen tree native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Podocarpaceae family and is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and reddish-brown bark. The plant produces small, spherical cones that contain edible seeds, which are an important food source for local wildlife. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in horticultural settings.
