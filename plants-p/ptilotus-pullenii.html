Ptilotus pullenii

Ptilotus pullenii, commonly known as the ghost flower, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its unique, spiky, white to pinkish flowers that resemble small ghosts, hence its common name. The plant is a member of the Amaranthaceae family and is often found in arid and semi-arid regions. It has a low-growing habit with succulent, fleshy leaves that help it conserve water.

The flowers bloom in late summer and early autumn, attracting various pollinators such as bees and birds.
