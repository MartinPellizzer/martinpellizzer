Plantago coronopus var. tlemceniana

Plantago coronopus var. tlemceniana, commonly known as the Algerian plantain, is a variant of the common plantain, native to North Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, calcareous soils along riverbanks and wet meadows. The plant features broad, lance-shaped leaves with a waxy coating, and it produces small, clustered flowers in late spring. It is valued for its medicinal properties, including anti-inflammatory and wound-healing benefits.

This variety is also noted for its adaptability to semi-arid conditions, making it a resilient plant in its native habitat.
