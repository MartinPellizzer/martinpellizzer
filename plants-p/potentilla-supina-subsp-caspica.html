Potentilla supina subsp. caspica

Potentilla supina subsp.

caspica, commonly known as the Caspian creeping cinquefoil, is a low-growing perennial shrub native to Central Asia. It typically features woody stems that root at the nodes, forming dense mats or cushions.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are compound, consisting of five oval leaflets, and are covered in fine hairs.

It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments.
