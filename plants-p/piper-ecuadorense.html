Piper ecuadorense

Piper ecuadorense, commonly known as the Ecuadorian pepper or Ecuadorian long pepper, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Ecuador and Peru. The plant is characterized by its climbing habit, with opposite, ovate leaves and small, greenish-white flowers. Its fruits are typically elongated and contain a single seed, which is used in traditional medicine.

The plant's botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and medicinal contexts.
