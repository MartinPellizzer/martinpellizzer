Podocarpus neriifolius var. bracteatus

Podocarpus neriifolius var. bracteatus, commonly known as the bracteate yellow-leaf podocarp, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Podocarpaceae family and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and small, clustered inflorescences. The leaves are typically narrow and glossy, with a yellowish-green hue, and the plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers. It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to various soil conditions.

The species is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
