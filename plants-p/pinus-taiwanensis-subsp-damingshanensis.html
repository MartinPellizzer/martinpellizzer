Pinus taiwanensis subsp.

damingshanensis, commonly known as the Daming Mountain pine, is a rare and endemic subspecies native to Taiwan. It is a coniferous evergreen tree characterized by its slender, conical shape and needle-like leaves.

The plant produces cones that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind, aiding in its propagation. It thrives in high-altitude areas with cool, moist climates, typically found in mountainous regions.
