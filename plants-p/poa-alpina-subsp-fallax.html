Poa alpina subsp. fallax

Poa alpina subsp.

fallax, commonly known as the alpine bentgrass, is a low-growing grass species native to mountainous regions. It thrives in cold, high-altitude environments, often found in alpine tundra and rocky slopes.

The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It is adapted to harsh conditions with a deep root system and a mat-forming growth habit.

Its botanical features make it an important component of alpine ecosystems.
