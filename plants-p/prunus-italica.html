Prunus italica

Prunus italica, commonly known as the Italian cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Europe.

It produces small, white flowers in early spring, which are followed by bright red, sweet-tart berries in the summer. The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal. Its fruits are edible and can be used in jams and desserts.

Prunus italica thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for Mediterranean climates.
