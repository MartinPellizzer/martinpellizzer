Plectranthus acaulis

Plectranthus acaulis, commonly known as the silver bush or silver leaf plant, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, silver-gray foliage and compact growth habit, making it a popular ornamental plant. The leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It produces small, tubular flowers in shades of purple or blue, typically in late spring to early summer.

This plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, requiring minimal water once established.
