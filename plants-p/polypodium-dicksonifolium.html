Polypodium dicksonifolium

Polypodium dicksonifolium, commonly known as the Dickson's fern, is a species of polypodioid fern native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped fronds that are typically bipinnate, with small, closely spaced pinnae. The plant produces sporangia on the underside of its fronds, which are clustered in sori covered by a protective indusium. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest undergrowth.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
