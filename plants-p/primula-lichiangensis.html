Primula lichiangensis

Primula lichiangensis, commonly known as the Yunnan primrose, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Yunnan Province in China.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that come in a range of colors, including white, pink, and purple. The plant typically grows in cool, moist environments at high altitudes, often in forested areas or rocky soils. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in a fine layer of hair, which helps to retain moisture in its native habitat.

Primula lichiangensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens that mimic its natural growing conditions.
