Pilea multicellularis

Pilea multicellularis, commonly known as the Chinese money plant, is a species of flowering plant in the family Araceae.

It is native to China and is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that resemble small, round coins. The plant produces clusters of small white flowers that are surrounded by a spadix, typical of the Araceae family. Pilea multicellularis is a perennial herb that thrives in moist, shady environments.

Its unique appearance and ease of care make it a popular houseplant.
