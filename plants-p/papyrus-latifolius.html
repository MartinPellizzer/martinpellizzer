Papyrus latifolius

Papyrus latifolius, commonly known as broadleaf papyrus, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its broad, sword-shaped leaves that can grow up to several feet in length. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that are clustered in spike-like inflorescences. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found near water bodies.

Its historical significance includes use in ancient Egypt for papermaking and as a decorative plant in gardens.
