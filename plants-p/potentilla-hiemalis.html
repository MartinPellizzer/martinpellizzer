Potentilla hiemalis

Potentilla hiemalis, commonly known as winter cinquefoil, is a low-growing perennial shrub native to the northern regions of North America.

It features small, white to yellowish flowers that bloom in early spring, typically from March to May. The plant has delicate, five-lobed leaves that are alternate and covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full to partial sunlight, often found in open woodlands or rocky slopes.

Its botanical characteristics make it a hardy and resilient plant adapted to cold climates.
