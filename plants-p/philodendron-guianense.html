Philodendron guianense

Philodendron guianense, commonly known as the Guianan philodendron, is a climbing plant native to the tropical rainforests of South America.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped, waxy leaves that are often variegated with white or yellow markings. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by berries that are typically dark red or black. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Its foliage makes it a popular choice for indoor gardening due to its aesthetic appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
