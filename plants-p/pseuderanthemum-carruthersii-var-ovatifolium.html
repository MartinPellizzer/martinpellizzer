Pseuderanthemum carruthersii var. ovatifolium

Pseuderanthemum carruthersii var. ovatifolium, commonly known as the ovate-leaf pseuderanthemum, is a species native to tropical regions of South America.

It is a member of the Acanthaceae family, characterized by its showy, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are often hairy and have a slightly serrated edge. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for shade gardens and tropical greenhouses.
