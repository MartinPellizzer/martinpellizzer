Protorhus namaquensis

Protorhus namaquensis, commonly known as the Namaqua protea, is a striking South African flowering plant belonging to the Proteaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescences composed of numerous white to pinkish flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant is native to the arid regions of the Western and Northern Cape provinces, where it thrives in well-drained, sandy soils. It has adapted to survive in drought conditions with a deep root system and waxy leaves that reduce water loss.

The Namaqua protea is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and botanical gardens.
