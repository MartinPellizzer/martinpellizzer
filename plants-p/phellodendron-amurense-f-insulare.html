Phellodendron amurense f. insulare

Phellodendron amurense f. insulare, commonly known as the Japanese tree peony, is a variant of the Amur corktree.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to East Asia, characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is known for its thick, corky bark that becomes prominent with age. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it suitable for ornamental gardening.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species in both horticulture and ecological studies.
