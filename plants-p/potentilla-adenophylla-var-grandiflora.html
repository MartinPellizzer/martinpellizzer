Potentilla adenophylla var. grandiflora

Potentilla adenophylla var. grandiflora, commonly known as the large-flowered cinquefoil, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features compound leaves with five to seven elliptical leaflets, often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces showy, bright yellow flowers in terminal clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy addition to rock gardens or alpine landscapes.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its vigorous growth and long flowering period.
