Populus simonii var. przewalskii

Populus simonii var. przewalskii, commonly known as the Przewalski's poplar, is a deciduous tree native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and distinctive, elongated buds. The plant is adapted to harsh environmental conditions, including drought and poor soil, making it a hardy species. It produces small, yellowish catkins in the spring, which are its primary means of reproduction.

This variety is of interest to botanists and conservationists due to its limited distribution and ecological significance.
