Polypodium plebeium var. palmense

Polypodium plebeium var. palmense, commonly known as the palm fern, is a species of fern characterized by its distinctive fronds that resemble a palm tree.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia, Africa, and the Americas. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky crevices. Its fronds are pinnately compound, with a slender, fibrous stem that often forms dense clusters.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
