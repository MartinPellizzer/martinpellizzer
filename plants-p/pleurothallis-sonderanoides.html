Pleurothallis sonderanoides

Pleurothallis sonderanoides, commonly known as the Sonder's pleurothallis, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its small, intricate flowers that often feature a unique coloration. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid forest environments. Its leaves are usually elongated and strap-like, adapted for efficient water absorption in its natural habitat.

Due to its delicate appearance and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and specialized orchid species.
