Pentalepis ecliptoides subsp. hirsuta

Pentalepis ecliptoides subsp.

hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy eclipta, is a flowering plant native to Australia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads.

The plant is distinguished by its hairy, glandular leaves and yellow disk flowers surrounded by pale yellow to brown ray florets. It typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal and inland regions.

The subspecies is notable for its adaptability to arid conditions and its role in supporting local pollinators.
