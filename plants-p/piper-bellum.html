Piper bellum

Piper bellum, commonly known as the black pepper plant, is a flowering vine native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its small, green, oval-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into the familiar black, white, and green peppercorns used in cooking. Its fruit, known as a drupe, contains the aromatic seed that is the primary spice derived from this plant.

Piper bellum thrives in humid, warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
