Potamogeton furcatus

Potamogeton furcatus, commonly known as the forked pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in North America.

It has long, ribbon-like leaves that are typically narrow and tapering, with a distinct forked shape at the base of its flowering structures. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are clustered in dense spikes, often found near the water's surface. It thrives in shallow, slow-moving waters and plays a crucial role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.

Its presence is often an indicator of good water quality in its habitat.
