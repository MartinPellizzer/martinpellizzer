Piper santaremense

Piper santaremense, commonly known as the Santarem pepper, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to the Amazon rainforest, particularly in Brazil, and is characterized by its climbing habit and small, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that develop into elongated, cylindrical berries. Its fruit is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties.

The plant's botanical features make it an important component of the biodiversity in its native habitat.
