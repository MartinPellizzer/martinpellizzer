Pentanema × transsilvanicum

Pentanema × transsilvanicum, commonly known as the Transylvanian pentanema, is a hybrid plant belonging to the family Primulaceae.

It is characterized by its semi-evergreen to deciduous habit, with lanceolate to ovate leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white to pink, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is native to parts of Europe, particularly the Carpathian region.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
