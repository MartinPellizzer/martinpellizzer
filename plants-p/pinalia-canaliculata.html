Pinalia canaliculata

Pinalia canaliculata, commonly known as the narrow-ribbed pincushion cactus, is a species of flowering plant native to the arid regions of South America.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its spiny stems and clusters of yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun conditions. Its unique appearance, with its tubular flowers and segmented stems, makes it a striking addition to desert landscapes.

Pinalia canaliculata is also valued for its ability to store water, making it well-adapted to its harsh environment.
