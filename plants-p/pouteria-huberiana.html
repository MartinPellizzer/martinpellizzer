Pouteria huberiana

Pouteria huberiana, commonly known as the Brazilian nut tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Amazon Basin.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and produces clusters of yellowish-green flowers. The tree is notable for its edible seeds, which are encased in a hard, woody shell and are commercially valuable as Brazil nuts. Pouteria huberiana thrives in humid, warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various wildlife species in its native region.
