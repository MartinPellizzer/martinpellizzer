Parabarium velutinum var. koratianum

Parabarium velutinum var. koratianum, commonly known as the Koratian velvetleaf, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are velvety to the touch, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in humid lowland forests.

The species is notable for its rapid growth and is sometimes considered an invasive weed in agricultural areas.
