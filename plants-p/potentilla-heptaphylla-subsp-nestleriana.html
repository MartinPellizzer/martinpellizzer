Potentilla heptaphylla subsp. nestleriana

Potentilla heptaphylla subsp.

nestleriana, commonly known as the Nestler's cinquefoil, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant has compound leaves composed of five to seven leaflets, which are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine plantings.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its compact growth habit and long blooming period.
