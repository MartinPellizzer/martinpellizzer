Passiflora weigendii

Passiflora weigendii, commonly known as the Weigend passionflower, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful flowers that feature a unique blend of violet, yellow, and red hues. The plant produces edible berries that are often used in culinary applications. Its foliage consists of deeply lobed leaves that provide dense shade.

Passiflora weigendii is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in warm, humid climates.
