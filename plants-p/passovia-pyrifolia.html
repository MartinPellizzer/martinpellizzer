Passovia pyrifolia

Passovia pyrifolia, commonly known as the Japanese white pine, is a species of conifer native to Japan.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and needle-like leaves that are typically light green in color. The plant produces small, yellowish cones that mature to a reddish-brown hue. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Due to its slow growth rate, it is often used as a specimen tree in landscaped settings.
