Pandanus lanutooensis

Pandanus lanutooensis, commonly known as the Talaud pandanus, is a tropical plant native to the Talaud Islands in Indonesia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and long, narrow leaves that are typically arranged in a spiral pattern. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences that bear fragrant, yellowish flowers. Its leaves are often used in traditional crafts and as thatching material.

The species is adapted to coastal and humid environments, thriving in well-drained sandy soils.
