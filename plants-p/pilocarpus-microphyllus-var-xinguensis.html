Pilocarpus microphyllus var. xinguensis

Pilocarpus microphyllus var. xinguensis, commonly known as the Xingu pepper tree, is a species native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree with smooth, grayish-brown bark and dense, spreading foliage. The leaves are alternate, simple, and elliptical, with a leathery texture and a distinct aromatic scent. It produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by red, fleshy berries that are edible but have a spicy, peppery flavor.

This plant is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional medicine for its stimulant and anticholinergic effects.
