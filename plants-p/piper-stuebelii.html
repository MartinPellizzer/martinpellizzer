Piper stuebelii

Piper stuebelii, commonly known as Stuebel's pepper, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species such as black pepper. The plant is characterized by its climbing habit, with opposite, ovate leaves that are often hairy. Its small, greenish-white flowers are clustered in spikes, and it produces small, round berries that turn red when mature.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
