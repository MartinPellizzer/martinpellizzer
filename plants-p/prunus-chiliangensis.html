Prunus chiliangensis

Prunus chiliangensis, commonly known as the Yunnan cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, red or yellow fruits that are edible but not widely cultivated for commercial use. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in mountainous regions with well-drained soil.

Prunus chiliangensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
