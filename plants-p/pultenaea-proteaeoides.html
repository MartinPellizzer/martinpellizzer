Pultenaea proteaeoides

Pultenaea proteaeoides, commonly known as the golden wattle, is a shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often hairy stems and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant produces small, woody pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and coastal areas.

Pultenaea proteaeoides is an important species in native gardens and plays a role in supporting local wildlife.
