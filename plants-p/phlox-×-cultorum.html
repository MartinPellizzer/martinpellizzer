Phlox × cultorum

Phlox × cultorum, commonly known as garden phlox, is a hybrid perennial plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its showy, clustered flowers that range in color from pink to purple, depending on the cultivar. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, featuring opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowering period occurs in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.
