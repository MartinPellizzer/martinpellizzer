Polygonum sumatranum

Polygonum sumatranum, commonly known as the Java knotweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, which includes plants known for their distinctive arrowhead-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, thriving in moist, nutrient-rich soils. Its leaves are often reddish or purple in color, and it produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters.

Polygonum sumatranum is sometimes used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
