Photinia maximowiczii

Photinia maximowiczii, commonly known as the red leaf photinia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant red to orange-red foliage, which becomes particularly striking in the autumn. The plant features simple, alternate leaves that are usually ovate to elliptical in shape. It produces small, white to pale yellow flowers in clusters, followed by red, fleshy berries that are edible for birds.

Photinia maximowiczii is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
