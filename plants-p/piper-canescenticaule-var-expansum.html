Piper canescenticaule var. expansum

Piper canescenticaule var. expansum, commonly known as the broad-stemmed pepper vine, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species. The plant is characterized by its slender, often sprawling stems and heart-shaped leaves with a slightly pubescent surface. Its small, greenish-white flowers are typically clustered in axillary inflorescences.

While not widely cultivated for its fruits, it is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and potential medicinal properties.
