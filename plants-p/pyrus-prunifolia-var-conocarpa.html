Pyrus prunifolia var. conocarpa

Pyrus prunifolia var. conocarpa, commonly known as the Mexican white pear, is a variant of the white pear tree native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its large, white flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by edible, sweet fruits. The plant has smooth, dark green leaves that are ovate in shape with a slightly serrated edge. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is valued for both its ornamental beauty and its culinary uses.
