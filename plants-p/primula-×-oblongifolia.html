Primula × oblongifolia

Primula × oblongifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf primrose, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Primulaceae family.

It is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves that are often a deep green with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, or purple, typically blooming in early spring. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in woodland areas or garden settings.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated for its attractive foliage and flowers.
