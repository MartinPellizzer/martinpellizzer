Polygonum lapathifolium proles vernicosum

Polygonum lapathifolium proles vernicosum, commonly known as the common knotweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, characterized by its distinctive arrow-shaped leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in moist soils along riverbanks, wetlands, and disturbed areas. Its stems are often reddish and slightly swollen, while the leaves are smooth and have a slightly bitter taste.

It is known for its rapid growth and can become invasive in certain environments.
