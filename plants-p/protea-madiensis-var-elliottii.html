Protea madiensis var. elliottii

Protea madiensis var. elliottii, commonly known as the Elliott's protea, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Proteaceae family and is characterized by its striking, cone-shaped inflorescence composed of numerous small, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to Mediterranean climates with wet winters and dry summers. Its flowers range in color from white to pink and are often visited by birds and insects.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
