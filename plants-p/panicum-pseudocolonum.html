Panicum pseudocolonum

Panicum pseudocolonum, commonly known as the narrowleaf panicgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically a vibrant green. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny florets, which are often wind-pollinated. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it a common sight in open fields and disturbed areas.

Its botanical features make it an important component of native plant communities and a potential candidate for restoration projects.
