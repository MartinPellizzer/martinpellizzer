Phlomoides regeliana

Phlomoides regeliana, commonly known as the Regel's phlomoides, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Asia.

It typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, showcasing adaptations to drought conditions. The plant features slender, upright stems covered in fine hairs, and its leaves are narrow and often grayish-green in color. It produces small, clustered flowers that are usually pale blue or purple, which bloom in summer.

The species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and resilience in challenging climates.
