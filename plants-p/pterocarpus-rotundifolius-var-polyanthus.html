Pterocarpus rotundifolius var. polyanthus

Pterocarpus rotundifolius var. polyanthus, commonly known as the purpleheart tree, is a tropical hardwood species native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its dark purple to reddish-brown heartwood, which becomes more intense with age. The leaves are typically alternate, simple, and elliptical in shape, with a smooth margin. The flowers are small, fragrant, and yellowish-white, often clustered in inflorescences.

This species is valued for its durable, high-quality timber used in furniture and flooring.
