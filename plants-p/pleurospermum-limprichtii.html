Pleurospermum limprichtii

Pleurospermum limprichtii, commonly known as the rock dandelion, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, gray-green leaves and yellow composite flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. Its flowering stems are often hairy and can reach heights of up to 30 centimeters.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
