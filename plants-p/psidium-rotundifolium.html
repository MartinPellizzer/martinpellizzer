Psidium rotundifolium

Psidium rotundifolium, commonly known as the round-leaf guava, is a small evergreen tree native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, ovate leaves with rounded tips. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, yellowish-orange berries. Its fruits are typically consumed fresh or used in culinary applications.

The plant is valued for its ornamental beauty and adaptability to various soil types.
