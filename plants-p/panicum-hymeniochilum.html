Panicum hymeniochilum

Panicum hymeniochilum, commonly known as the silky grass or silky panicgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-gray foliage that gives it a distinctive, almost silky appearance. The plant produces small, fluffy seed heads that are often seen in late summer and early autumn. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it a common sight in open fields and along roadsides.

Due to its ornamental value and ability to tolerate drought, it is sometimes used in landscaping and restoration projects.
