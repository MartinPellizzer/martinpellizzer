Primula chishuiensis

Primula chishuiensis, commonly known as the Chishui primrose, is a flowering plant native to China, specifically the Chishui River valley in Sichuan Province.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often in mountainous regions with cool climates. Its foliage consists of broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white hairs.

Primula chishuiensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also of interest to botanists due to its unique ecological adaptations.
