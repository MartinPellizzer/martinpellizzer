Polyalthia motleyana

Polyalthia motleyana, commonly known as the Motley's satinash, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves with alternate arrangement. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by small, round fruits. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The bark and leaves have been used in traditional medicine for their potential therapeutic properties.
