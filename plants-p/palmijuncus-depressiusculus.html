Palmijuncus depressiusculus

Palmijuncus depressiusculus, commonly known as the dwarf palm, is a small, compact plant native to tropical regions.

It belongs to the Arecaceae family, which includes most palms, and is characterized by its short, stout trunk and clustered fronds. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often in forest understories. Its leaves are usually narrow and arranged in a rosette pattern, contributing to its compact appearance.

Due to its unique morphology, it is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant in warm climates.
