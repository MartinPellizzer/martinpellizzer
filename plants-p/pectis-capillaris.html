Pectis capillaris

Pectis capillaris, commonly known as the silky pectis, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous small florets. The plant features slender, branched stems and narrow, silvery-gray leaves that are covered in fine hairs. Its showy, yellow flowers bloom in clusters, often attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Pectis capillaris is adapted to arid environments and is often found in rocky soils and open habitats.
