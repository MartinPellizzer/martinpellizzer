Pleioblastus pygmaeus

Pleioblastus pygmaeus, commonly known as the dwarf bamboo, is a species of grass-like plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically less than 10 centimeters in length. This plant thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories. Its growth habit is clumping, forming dense clusters that can reach heights of up to 60 centimeters.

Pleioblastus pygmaeus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
