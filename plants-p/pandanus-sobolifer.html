Pandanus sobolifer

Pandanus sobolifer, commonly known as the giant pandanus, is a flowering plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, fan-shaped leaves that can grow up to several meters in length. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences that bear both male and female flowers, typical of the Pandanus genus. Its root system is fibrous and well adapted to coastal and saline environments.

The plant is often used in traditional crafts and has ecological significance in its native habitats.
