Piper aberrans

Piper aberrans, commonly known as the Madagascar pepper or African pepper, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar and parts of Africa.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many pepper species. The plant is characterized by its climbing habit, with opposite, ovate leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers. Its fruit is a small, berry-like structure that turns red when mature.

Piper aberrans is valued for its aromatic leaves and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
