Prunus mutabilis f. augusta

Prunus mutabilis f. augusta, commonly known as the August cherry, is a unique variant of the Japanese maple.

It is characterized by its striking, multi-colored foliage that shifts from green to red, orange, and purple throughout the growing season. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in early spring, followed by edible berries that attract wildlife. It thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens.

Its botanical features make it a valuable addition to both landscape design and ecological habitats.
