Pleioblastus triangulata

Pleioblastus triangulata, commonly known as the Japanese forest grass, is a species of bamboo native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright culms that often form dense clumps, and its leaves are typically narrow and grass-like. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its aesthetic appeal. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are followed by cylindrical seed pods.

Pleioblastus triangulata is also valued for its ability to stabilize soil and prevent erosion in woodland areas.
