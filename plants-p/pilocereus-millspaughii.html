Pilocereus millspaughii

Pilocereus millspaughii, commonly known as the Moon Cactus, is a species of cactus native to South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that bloom at night, typically in shades of white, pink, or purple. The plant has a short, thick stem and is often grafted onto a different cactus species for structural support. Its leaves are modified into spines, and it thrives in well-draining soil with minimal water.

Pilocereus millspaughii is popular among succulent enthusiasts for its ornamental value and unique appearance.
