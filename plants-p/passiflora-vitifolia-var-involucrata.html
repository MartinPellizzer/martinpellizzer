Passiflora vitifolia var. involucrata

Passiflora vitifolia var. involucrata, commonly known as the purple crown passionflower, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking violet to purple bracts that surround the inflorescence, giving it its common name. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers with intricate structures typical of the Passifloraceae family. Its leaves are usually heart-shaped and have a velvety texture.

It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing as a vine or shrub.
