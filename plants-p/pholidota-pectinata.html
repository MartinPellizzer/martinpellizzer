Pholidota pectinata

Pholidota pectinata, commonly known as the velvet tamarind, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, dark-brown bark and elliptical leaves with a velvety texture. The plant produces yellowish-white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, bean-like fruits. The leaves and young fruits are used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

It thrives in tropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit production.
