Phyllanthus chacoensis

Phyllanthus chacoensis, commonly known as the Chaco phyllanthus, is a shrub native to South America, particularly found in the Chaco region of Paraguay and Argentina.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of wax. The plant produces small, clustered yellowish flowers, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds.

It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing drought resistance through its succulent leaves and deep root system.
