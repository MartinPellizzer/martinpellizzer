Polystichum hancockii var. proliferum

Polystichum hancockii var. proliferum, commonly known as the Hancock's shield fern, is a variant of the shield fern species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen fronds that form a compact, bushy growth habit. The plant features bipinnate leaves with a dark green, leathery texture and a distinctive, shield-like shape. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.

This fern is valued for its ornamental appeal and is occasionally cultivated in horticulture for its aesthetic qualities.
