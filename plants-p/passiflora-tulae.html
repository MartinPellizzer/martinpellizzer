Passiflora tulae

Passiflora tulae, commonly known as the Tula passionflower, is a climbing vine native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its striking, complex flowers that feature five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments. The plant produces edible, orange-yellow berries that are rich in nutrients. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil.

Passiflora tulae is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
