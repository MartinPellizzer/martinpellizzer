Polygala kajii

Polygala kajii, commonly known as the Japanese milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and delicate, feathery inflorescences. The plant produces small, usually white or pale yellow flowers that are clustered in spicate racemes. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or grassland habitats.

Polygala kajii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
