Pariana glauca

Pariana glauca, commonly known as the bluebush, is a shrub native to the arid regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and silver-blue foliage, which results from dense covering of fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring, adding a subtle ornamental value. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Its botanical features make it a valuable plant for ecological restoration and ornamental gardening in dry climates.
