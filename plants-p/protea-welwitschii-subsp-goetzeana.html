Protea welwitschii subsp. goetzeana

Protea welwitschii subsp.

goetzeana, commonly known as the Goetze's protea, is a striking evergreen shrub native to the Western Cape of South Africa. It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescences composed of numerous pale pink to white flowers arranged in a conical shape.

The plant features leathery, elliptical leaves that are usually covered in a dense layer of fine, white hairs. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
