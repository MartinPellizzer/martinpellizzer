Pleione coronaria

Pleione coronaria, commonly known as the crown orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and striking, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically produces showy, white or pale pink blooms during the spring season. It thrives in cool, humid environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and rarity, it is sometimes cultivated in specialized horticultural settings.
