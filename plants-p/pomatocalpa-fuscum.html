Pomatocalpa fuscum

Pomatocalpa fuscum, commonly known as the dark orchid, is a species of orchid native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dark purple to blackish flowers, which are often adorned with intricate patterns. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as forest floors or along stream banks. Its roots form symbiotic relationships with mycorrhizal fungi, aiding in nutrient absorption.

The orchid's morphology includes slender stems and broad, lance-shaped leaves that contribute to its overall aesthetic appeal.
