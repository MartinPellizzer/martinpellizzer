Potamogeton lucens var. lancifolius

Potamogeton lucens var. lancifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaved pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in North America.

It has long, slender, lance-shaped leaves that are typically smooth and slightly ribbed, aiding in its identification. The plant produces small, green flowers that are clustered in dense spike-like inflorescences, often floating at the water's surface. It thrives in shallow, slow-moving waters and plays a vital role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various waterfowl and invertebrates.

Its presence is often an indicator of good water quality and a healthy aquatic environment.
