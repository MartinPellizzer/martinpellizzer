Polygala virgata var. intermedia

Polygala virgata var. intermedia, commonly known as the intermediate milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in grassland and woodland habitats, often in well-drained soils. The plant features slender, branched stems with alternate, narrow, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are small, clustered in spicate inflorescences, and are usually pink or purple in color.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
