Podochilus dendrobioides

Podochilus dendrobioides, commonly known as the tree orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is a flowering plant belonging to the family Orchidaceae, characterized by its tall, slender stem and clusters of small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its flowers are usually yellow or greenish-yellow, with a distinctive three-lobed lip.

Podochilus dendrobioides is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its unique floral display.
