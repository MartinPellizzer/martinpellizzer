Passiflora umbilicata

Passiflora umbilicata, commonly known as the Mexican passionflower, is a climbing vine native to Central America and parts of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful flowers that feature five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments around the reproductive structures. The plant produces edible berries that are often used in local cuisines and traditional medicine. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a slightly serrated edge, while its stems are woody and can grow up to several meters in length.

Passiflora umbilicata is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
