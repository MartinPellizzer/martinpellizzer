Phyllorkis pusilla

Phyllorkis pusilla, commonly known as the small flame grass, is a small perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, upright stems that bear narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or red, blooming from late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or coastal landscapes.

Due to its compact size and ornamental flowers, it is often cultivated for garden use.
