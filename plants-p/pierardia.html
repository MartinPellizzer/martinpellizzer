Pierardia

Pierardia, commonly known as the Chinese mountain pepper, is a shrub native to China and Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Ericaceae and is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by red, berry-like fruits. Pierardia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional Chinese medicine.

Its botanical features make it well adapted to acidic, well-drained soils in woodland or mountainous regions.
