Pongamia lucida

Pongamia lucida, commonly known as the yellow pongam, is a leguminous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, light green to yellowish bark and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by distinctive, flat, brown pods containing several seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Pongamia lucida is valued for its ability to fix nitrogen and its potential use in agroforestry systems.
