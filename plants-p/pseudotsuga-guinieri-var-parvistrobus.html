Pseudotsuga guinieri var. parvistrobus

Pseudotsuga guinieri var. parvistrobus, commonly known as the dwarf hemlock, is a rare and small evergreen conifer native to the high elevations of the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its narrow, conical shape and short, dense needles that are dark green in color. The plant produces small, spherical cones that mature to a reddish-brown color, and it is typically found in cool, moist, and well-drained soils. Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered an endangered species.

Its botanical features make it a unique subject for ecological and taxonomic study.
