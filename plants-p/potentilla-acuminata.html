Potentilla acuminata

Potentilla acuminata, commonly known as the sharpleaf cinquefoil, is a flowering plant belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is native to North America and thrives in moist, acidic soils, often found in wetlands and along stream banks. The plant features small, clustered white or pink flowers that bloom in the spring and summer. Its leaves are typically oval-shaped with serrated edges, and it produces small, red berries in the fall.

Potentilla acuminata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
