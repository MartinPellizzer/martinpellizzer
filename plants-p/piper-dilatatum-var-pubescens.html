Piper dilatatum var. pubescens

Piper dilatatum var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy sarsaparilla, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many species of climbing plants. The plant is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves, which contribute to its common name. It produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, and its fruits are berries that contain several seeds.

This variant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has historical uses in traditional medicine.
