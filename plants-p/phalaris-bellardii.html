Phalaris bellardii

Phalaris bellardii, commonly known as the Bellard's lovegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the southern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and prairies.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and restoration projects.
