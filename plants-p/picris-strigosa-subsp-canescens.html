Picris strigosa subsp. canescens

Picris strigosa subsp.

canescens, commonly known as the hairy tansy, is a herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellow flowers and hairy stems.

The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered with fine, white hairs.

It is known for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
