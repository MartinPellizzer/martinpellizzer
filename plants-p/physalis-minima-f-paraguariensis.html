Physalis minima f. paraguariensis

Physalis minima f. paraguariensis, commonly known as the Jesuit's bread or Inca berry, is a small shrub native to South America.

It produces small, spherical fruits enclosed in a papery calyx, which turns red when mature. The plant is characterized by its hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in shaded areas.

The fruit is edible, with a tangy flavor, and has been used in traditional medicine and as a food source.
