Passiflora alata var. mauritiana

Passiflora alata var. mauritiana, commonly known as the Mauritius passionflower, is a climbing vine native to the Mascarene Islands.

It features striking, palmately lobed leaves that are often hairy and have a distinctive appearance. The flowers are showy, with a complex structure of five sepals, five petals, and numerous stamens, typically in shades of white, pink, and red. The fruit is a bright orange, edible berry that contains numerous seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated for its unique foliage and floral display.
