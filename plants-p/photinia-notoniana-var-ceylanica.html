Photinia notoniana var. ceylanica

Photinia notoniana var. ceylanica, commonly known as the Ceylon photinia, is a shrub or small tree native to Sri Lanka.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a slightly serrated margin. The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. Its fruits are small, red berries that contain seeds, which are dispersed by birds.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and seasonal blooms.
