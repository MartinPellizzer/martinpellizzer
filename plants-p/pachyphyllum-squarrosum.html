Pachyphyllum squarrosum

Pachyphyllum squarrosum, commonly known as the squarrosum cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems that store water, adapting to its harsh environment. The plant typically grows as a solitary shrub, with a short, sturdy trunk and sparse, spiny leaves. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange, blooming in the summer months.

Pachyphyllum squarrosum is a slow-growing plant that thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight.
