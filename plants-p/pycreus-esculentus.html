Pycreus esculentus

Pycreus esculentus, commonly known as the giant nightshade, is a member of the Solanaceae family.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its large, showy flowers and edible fruits, though the plant is toxic in many parts. The plant thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth. Its leaves are broad and lobed, while its stems are hairy and can grow quite tall.

The fruit, which resembles a small tomato, is typically harvested when it turns a deep red color.
