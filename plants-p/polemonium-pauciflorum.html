Polemonium pauciflorum

Polemonium pauciflorum, commonly known as the few-flowered Jacob's ladder, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Polemoniaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, cascading clusters of blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and has lance-shaped leaves that are arranged in a stair-step pattern. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds, aiding in its propagation.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is often cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
