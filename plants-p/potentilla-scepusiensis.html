Potentilla scepusiensis

Potentilla scepusiensis, commonly known as the Scepusian cinquefoil, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its small, yellowish flowers and pinnately compound leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its botanical features include hairy stems and small, elliptical leaves with serrated edges.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and specialized species within its native habitat.
