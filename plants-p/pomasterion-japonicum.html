Pomasterion japonicum

Pomasterion japonicum, commonly known as the Japanese pomelo tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family, which includes citrus plants, and is characterized by its fragrant, compound leaves and yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically green when mature, though they may turn yellow or red depending on the variety. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal uses, it is cultivated in gardens and traditional medicine practices.
