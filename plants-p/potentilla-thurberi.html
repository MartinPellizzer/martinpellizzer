Potentilla thurberi

Potentilla thurberi, commonly known as Thurber's cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a low-growing perennial shrub that typically reaches heights of 10 to 20 centimeters. The plant features bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are compound, consisting of five oval-shaped leaflets, and it thrives in well-drained soils.

Potentilla thurberi is often found in rocky habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
