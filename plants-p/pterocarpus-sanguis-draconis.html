Pterocarpus sanguis-draconis

Pterocarpus sanguis-draconis, commonly known as the bloodwood tree, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its dark, reddish-brown heartwood that is highly valued for its durability and aesthetic appeal. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by flat, elongated pods containing seeds. Its leaves are compound, with numerous small leaflets, and it thrives in tropical and subtropical climates.

The tree is also noted for its ecological importance, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
