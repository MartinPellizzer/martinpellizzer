Polyachyrus selinoides

Polyachyrus selinoides, commonly known as the yellow bellflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in open grasslands and rocky areas, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, while its stems are upright and branching.

The yellow bellflower is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
