Polygala martinellii

Polygala martinellii, commonly known as the Martinellii milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to China.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, delicate flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is known for its unique inflorescence structure. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Due to its limited distribution and specific ecological requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
