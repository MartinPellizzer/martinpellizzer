Piaranthus mammillaris

Piaranthus mammillaris, commonly known as the mammillary pincushion.

This succulent plant is native to South Africa and is characterized by its dense, spiky appearance with small, rounded tubercles. It features a compact, bushy growth habit and produces small, white or yellowish flowers during the summer months. The plant is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Its unique morphology and low maintenance requirements make it a popular choice among succulent enthusiasts.
