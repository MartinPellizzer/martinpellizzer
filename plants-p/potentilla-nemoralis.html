Potentilla nemoralis

Potentilla nemoralis, commonly known as the common cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in woodland edges, hedgerows, and rocky soils. The plant features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are palmately divided into five lobes, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Potentilla nemoralis is known for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
