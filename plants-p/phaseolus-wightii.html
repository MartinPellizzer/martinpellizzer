Phaseolus wightii

Phaseolus wightii, commonly known as the Indian kidney bean, is a climbing legume native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It produces elongated, bean-like pods that contain edible seeds used in various cuisines. The plant has compound leaves with trifoliate or pinnate structures, and its flowers are typically white or pale purple. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires a warm climate for optimal growth.

Phaseolus wightii is valued not only for its nutritional seeds but also for its role in traditional agricultural systems.
