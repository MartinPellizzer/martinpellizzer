Passiflora heyneana

Passiflora heyneana, commonly known as the yellow passionflower, is a tropical climbing vine native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow flowers, which feature intricate structures including a crown of stamens and a distinctive floral shield. The plant produces edible, orange-colored berries that are rich in antioxidants and often used in culinary applications. Its leaves are heart-shaped and can be used in traditional medicine for their purported calming effects.

Passiflora heyneana thrives in humid, warm climates and requires support for its vigorous growth.
