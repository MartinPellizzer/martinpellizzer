Pyrus coronaria var. fragrans

Pyrus coronaria var. fragrans, commonly known as the fragrant pear, is a cultivar of the European pear tree.

It is characterized by its ornamental value, featuring showy white flowers that emit a sweet fragrance. The plant produces small, edible fruits that are typically harvested in late summer. It thrives in temperate climates and prefers well-drained soil.

This variety is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal and mild, sweet fruit flavor.
