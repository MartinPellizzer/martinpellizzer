Potentilla iberica

Potentilla iberica, commonly known as the Iberian cinquefoil, is a perennial shrub native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to yellow. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in sunny, well-drained environments. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three leaflets that are often covered in fine hairs.

Potentilla iberica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects due to its resilience and ability to stabilize soils.
