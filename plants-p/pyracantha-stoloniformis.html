Pyracantha stoloniformis

Pyracantha stoloniformis, commonly known as the creeping firethorn, is a deciduous shrub native to southern China.

It is characterized by its thorny stems that grow along the ground, forming dense mats. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in the spring, followed by orange-red berries in the fall. Its leaves are elliptical, dark green, and slightly toothed.

It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental berries and prickly growth habit.
