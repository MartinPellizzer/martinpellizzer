Phelypaea reuteri

Phelypaea reuteri, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or yellowish in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in disturbed habitats.

Phelypaea reuteri is known for its ability to spread rapidly, making it a common weed in agricultural areas.
