Pinguicula villosa lusus ramosa

Pinguicula villosa lusus ramosa, commonly known as the hairy butterwort, is a carnivorous plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves that secrete digestive enzymes to trap and digest small insects. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often in rocky or peaty soils. Its flowers are usually small and white or pink, arranged in a dense inflorescence.
