Pogonia nana

Pogonia nana, commonly known as the dwarf pink twayblade, is a small terrestrial orchid native to the northern regions of North America.

It typically grows in moist, acidic bogs and pine forests, where it thrives in shaded environments. The plant has a slender, leafless stem that bears two or three narrow, grass-like leaves. Its flowers are small, pinkish-white, and have a distinctive, spurred lip that attracts pollinators.

Pogonia nana is a rare and endangered species, often requiring specific conservation efforts to ensure its survival.
