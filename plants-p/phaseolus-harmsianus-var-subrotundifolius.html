Phaseolus harmsianus var. subrotundifolius

Phaseolus harmsianus var. subrotundifolius, commonly known as the Mexican coffee bean plant, is a climbing legume native to Central America.

It features compound leaves with ovate to subrotund leaflets, giving it its specific variety name. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that develop into elongated pods containing kidney-shaped seeds. It is adapted to tropical and subtropical climates, thriving in well-drained soils.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical significance in traditional agriculture.
