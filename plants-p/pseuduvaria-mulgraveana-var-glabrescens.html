Pseuduvaria mulgraveana var. glabrescens

Pseuduvaria mulgraveana var. glabrescens, commonly known as the Mulgrave glabrescent pseuduvaria, is a species native to the tropical rainforests of northern Queensland, Australia.

It is a small, evergreen shrub characterized by its dense, leathery foliage and compact growth habit. The leaves are elliptical, glossy, and typically dark green, with a smooth, glabrous surface. It produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by bright red, fleshy berries.

This plant is well adapted to humid, shaded environments and is often found in understorey habitats.
