Polygonum × janoviense

Polygonum × janoviense, commonly known as the hybrid knotweed, is a perennial plant belonging to the Polygonaceae family.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers that appear in late summer. The plant produces egg-shaped, reddish-brown seeds that are dispersed by wind, contributing to its invasive potential in certain regions. Its leaves are typically arrow-shaped with a prominent midrib and a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

Polygonum × janoviense is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of light conditions.
