Potamogeton × tenuinervis

Potamogeton × tenuinervis, commonly known as the narrow-veined pondweed, is a hybrid aquatic plant that exhibits characteristics of its parent species, Potamogeton acutifolius and Potamogeton tenuifolius.

It is typically found in freshwater environments such as lakes, ponds, and slow-moving streams. The plant has long, slender leaves that are often ribbon-like in appearance, with a smooth or slightly serrated edge. Its flowers are small and greenish, arranged in spikes, and it produces small, three-lobed fruits.

Potamogeton × tenuinervis plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
