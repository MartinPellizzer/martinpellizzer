Piper turrialvanum

Piper turrialvanum, commonly known as the Turrialva pepper, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Colombia and Ecuador. The plant is characterized by its climbing habit, with opposite, ovate leaves that are often pubescent. Its small, greenish-yellow flowers are borne in clusters and are followed by elliptical, three-lobed berries.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its unique foliage.
