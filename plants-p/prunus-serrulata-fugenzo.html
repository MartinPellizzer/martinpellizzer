Prunus serrulata fugenzo

Prunus serrulata fugenzo, commonly known as the Japanese cherry blossom, is a deciduous tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its delicate, pink to white flowers that bloom in early spring, making it a symbol of renewal and transience in Japanese culture. The tree has a rounded crown and smooth, gray bark that becomes fissured with age. Its leaves are oval and turn a vibrant yellow in autumn, adding to its seasonal beauty.

The plant is often cultivated in gardens and parks for its ornamental value and cultural significance.
