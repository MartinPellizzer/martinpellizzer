Prunus cerasus subsp. aproniana

Prunus cerasus subsp.

aproniana, commonly known as the apricot apron tree, is a subspecies of the apricot that is native to the Caucasus region. It is characterized by its small, fragrant white flowers and yellow-orange fruits that ripen in late summer.

The plant typically grows as a small tree or shrub, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are elliptical with a slightly serrated margin, and the bark is smooth and grayish-brown.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated for its edible fruits.
