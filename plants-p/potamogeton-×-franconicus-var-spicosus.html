Potamogeton × franconicus var. spicosus

Potamogeton × franconicus var. spicosus, commonly known as the spiky pondweed, is a hybrid aquatic plant that exhibits characteristics of both its parental species.

It is typically found in freshwater habitats such as lakes, ponds, and slow-moving streams. The plant has long, narrow leaves that are often arranged in whorls around the stem. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, with stamens that may have spiky appendages, contributing to its common name.

It plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various water organisms.
