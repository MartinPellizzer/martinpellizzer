Panicularia syrtica

Panicularia syrtica, commonly known as the desert flax, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of Africa and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has fibrous roots that help it survive in dry, sandy soils, and its leaves are narrow and covered in a fine layer of hair to reduce water loss. It is often found in rocky habitats and is adapted to withstand high temperatures and low precipitation.

Panicularia syrtica plays a role in stabilizing desert soils and provides nectar for pollinators in its ecosystem.
