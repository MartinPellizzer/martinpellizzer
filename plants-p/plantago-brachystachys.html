Plantago brachystachys

Plantago brachystachys, commonly known as narrow-stalked plantain, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features upright, branched stems that bear oval, dark green leaves with prominent veins. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in dense spikes, which are typically arranged in two rows along the stem. It is often found in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and fields, and is adapted to thrive in dry, sunny conditions.

The species is valued for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally in herbal remedies.
