Penstemon confertus var. attenuatus

Penstemon confertus var. attenuatus, commonly known as the narrowleaf beardtongue, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils in open habitats such as meadows and slopes. The plant features slender, upright stems with lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. Its showy, tubular flowers are usually pink to purple in color, attracting pollinators like bees and hummingbirds.

It is valued in native plant gardens for its drought tolerance and ornamental appeal.
