Potentilla sojakii

Potentilla sojakii, commonly known as the desert cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit and produces showy yellow or orange flowers. The plant has small, elliptical leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, aiding in water conservation. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand drought and high temperatures.

Potentilla sojakii plays an important role in stabilizing sandy soils and supporting local pollinator populations.
