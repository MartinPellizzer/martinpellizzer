Protea scolopendriifolia

Protea scolopendriifolia, commonly known as the silver protea, is a striking South African plant belonging to the Proteaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescences composed of numerous small, tubular flowers arranged in a spherical or conical shape. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to Mediterranean climates with wet winters and dry summers. Its foliage consists of narrow, leathery leaves that are often covered in a silvery or whitish powdery coating.

Protea scolopendriifolia is a popular ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and ability to thrive in coastal and inland regions of South Africa.
