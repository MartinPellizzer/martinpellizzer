Pholidia laanii

Pholidia laanii, commonly known as the African star gooseberry, is a small shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, star-shaped yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces edible berries that are yellow or orange in color, often used in local cuisines. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid regions.

Pholidia laanii is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
