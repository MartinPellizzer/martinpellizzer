Potentilla × italica

Potentilla × italica, commonly known as the Italian cinquefoil, is a hybrid shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring upright stems and a dense, compact growth habit. The plant produces showy, bright yellow flowers in late spring to early summer, which are clustered in spherical inflorescences. Its leaves are alternate, serrated, and covered in fine hairs, giving them a slightly fuzzy texture.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.
