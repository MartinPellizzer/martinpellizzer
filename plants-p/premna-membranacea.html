Premna membranacea

Premna membranacea, commonly known as the silver vine, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with a distinctive silvery underside, giving it its common name. It produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters, followed by red berries that are attractive to birds.

The leaves are often used in traditional medicine and are also popular as a climbing plant in ornamental gardens.
