Pieris henryi

Pieris henryi, commonly known as the Chinese pieris, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its evergreen, leathery foliage. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped, white or pale pink flowers in early spring. Its leaves are elliptical and have a smooth texture, often with a bluish-green hue.

It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.
