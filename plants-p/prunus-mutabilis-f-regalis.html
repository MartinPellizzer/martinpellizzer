Prunus mutabilis f. regalis

Prunus mutabilis f. regalis, commonly known as the royal cherry tree, is a cultivar of the Prunus mutabilis species, which is native to Japan.

This ornamental tree is characterized by its striking pink to white flowers that change color as they age, a trait that gives it its common name. The leaves are elliptical, with a smooth texture and a glossy appearance, adding to its aesthetic appeal. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for temperate climates.

The tree is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and parks due to its beautiful blossoms and graceful form.
