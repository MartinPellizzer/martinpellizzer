Potamogeton pectinatus var. vaginatus

Potamogeton pectinatus var. vaginatus, commonly known as the narrow-leaf pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater habitats across Europe and parts of Asia.

It has long, slender, ribbon-like leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating, aiding in water retention. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are typically arranged in spike-like clusters, which develop into elongated, cylindrical fruits. It thrives in still or slow-moving water and plays a vital role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.

Its presence is often an indicator of good water quality and ecological health.
