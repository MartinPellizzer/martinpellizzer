Pappostipa ruiz-lealii

Pappostipa ruiz-lealii, commonly known as the Ruiz-Leal's grass, is a species of flowering plant in the Poaceae family.

It is native to South America, particularly found in regions of Argentina and Chile. This perennial grass is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-dispersed seeds.

It thrives in dry, open habitats and is often found in disturbed or rocky soils.
