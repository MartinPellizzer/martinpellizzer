Psylliostachys × afghanicus

Psylliostachys × afghanicus, commonly known as the Afghan millet, is a hybrid grass species native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and small, spikelet-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in dry, temperate climates and is adapted to poor soil conditions. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a fibrous texture that contributes to its resilience.

This species is valued for its potential in agricultural and ecological restoration applications.
