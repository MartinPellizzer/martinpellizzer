Palaquium sumatranum

Palaquium sumatranum, commonly known as the Sumatran wax apple, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and small, round fruits. The fruit has a thick, waxy skin and a sweet, tangy flavor, making it popular in local cuisines. It thrives in tropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value as well as its edible fruit.

The plant is also valued for its potential medicinal properties in traditional medicine.
