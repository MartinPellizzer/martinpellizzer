Phreatia curvata

Phreatia curvata, commonly known as the curved phreatia, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in humid, shaded environments within tropical forests. The plant features a pseudobulb that supports its foliage and flowers, which are usually small and greenish-white in color. Its roots are adapted to absorb moisture from the surrounding soil and air.

Phreatia curvata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in greenhouses or conservatories.
