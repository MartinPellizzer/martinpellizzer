Polypogon melillensis

Polypogon melillensis, commonly known as the Melilla grass, is a perennial grass species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal areas and arid landscapes.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
