Pulsatilla vulgaris var. anglica

Pulsatilla vulgaris var. anglica, commonly known as the English pasqueflower, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, purple or pinkish-yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has a short, thick rhizome and hairy, lance-shaped leaves that emerge after the flowers. It thrives in well-drained, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and rocky areas.

The species is noted for its early blooming habit and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
