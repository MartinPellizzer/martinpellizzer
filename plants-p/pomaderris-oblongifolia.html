Pomaderris oblongifolia

Pomaderris oblongifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved pomaderris, is a shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically glossy and arranged alternately along the stems. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers in clusters during the spring and summer months. It is often found in dry sclerophyll forests and is adapted to nutrient-poor soils.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in native gardens.
