Pinus uncinata var. miniata

Pinus uncinata var. miniata, commonly known as the dwarf mountain pine, is a rare and distinctive variant of the mountain pine.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and short, twisted branches, which give it a unique appearance. The leaves are needle-like, typically ranging from 1 to 2 centimeters in length, and are often covered with a fine, white bloom. This variety thrives in high-altitude environments, often growing in rocky or well-drained soils.

Its botanical features make it an important species for studying adaptation to harsh mountain climates.
