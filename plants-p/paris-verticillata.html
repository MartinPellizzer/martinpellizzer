Paris verticillata

Paris verticillata, commonly known as the lesser mouse-ear, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features small, white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has a rosette of basal leaves that are rounded and covered in fine hairs. Its roots are tuberous and often used in traditional medicine.

Paris verticillata thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.
