Pandanus monodon

Pandanus monodon, commonly known as the red pandanus, is a tropical plant native to the Indo-Pacific region.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and long, narrow leaves that are often bright green or reddish in color. The plant produces inflorescences that bear small, white flowers, which are followed by triangular, fibrous fruits. Its leaves are used in traditional crafts and as thatching material in some cultures.

The plant thrives in coastal areas and is adapted to saline environments.
