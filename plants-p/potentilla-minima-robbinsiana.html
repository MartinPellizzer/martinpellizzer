Potentilla minima robbinsiana

Potentilla minima robbinsiana, commonly known as the Robbins' cinquefoil, is a low-growing perennial shrub native to the western United States.

It typically reaches a height of 10 to 20 centimeters and features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has five leaflets, which are typically oval to elliptic in shape, and are covered in fine, soft hairs. Its root system is fibrous and helps it thrive in well-drained, rocky soils.

This species is often found in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and slopes.
