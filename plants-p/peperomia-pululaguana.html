Peperomia pululaguana

Peperomia pululaguana, commonly known as the Mexican peperomia, is a compact, low-growing plant native to Mexico.

It features oval, waxy leaves that are typically green with a slight reddish or purple hue on the underside. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in shades of white or pale pink, which are followed by tiny, round berries. It thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Due to its unique appearance and ease of care, it is often cultivated as a decorative plant.
