Phoebe longipes

Phoebe longipes, commonly known as the long-stalked phoebe, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Lauraceae family, which includes other aromatic plants such as laurel and cinnamon. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 10 meters. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a glossy, dark green upper surface.

Phoebe longipes produces small, fragrant flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by elliptical, dark blue berries.
