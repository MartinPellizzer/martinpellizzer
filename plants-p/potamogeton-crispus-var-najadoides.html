Potamogeton crispus var. najadoides

Potamogeton crispus var. najadoides, commonly known as the narrow-leaf pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater habitats across Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in shallow waters, forming dense underwater stands that provide habitat for various aquatic organisms. The plant has long, narrow leaves that are often slightly curled, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are pollinated by wind or water. Its rhizomes allow it to spread vegetatively, making it a common and sometimes invasive species in ponds and lakes.

Due to its ecological role, it is often used in wetland restoration projects.
