Pseuderanthemum congestum

Pseuderanthemum congestum, commonly known as the clustered pseuderanthemum, is a tropical flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences, often in shades of white, pink, or purple. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Pseuderanthemum congestum is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive floral display and compact size.
