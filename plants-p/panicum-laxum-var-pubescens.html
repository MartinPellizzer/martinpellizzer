Panicum laxum var. pubescens

Panicum laxum var. pubescens, commonly known as silky panicgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the southern United States.

It is characterized by its soft, pubescent foliage and drooping panicles that give it a graceful appearance. The plant typically grows in moist to wet soils, often found in marshy areas or along the edges of water bodies. Its botanical features include narrow, grass-like leaves and small, spikelet-bearing inflorescences.

It is valued in wetland restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for wildlife.
