Pinaropappus mojadanus

Pinaropappus mojadanus, commonly known as the Mojave pappus plant, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northwestern Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous small florets. The plant features narrow, linear leaves and produces small, daisy-like flowers that bloom in late spring. It is adapted to harsh, dry environments, with a deep root system to access water.

Its ecological role includes providing nectar for pollinators and serving as a food source for certain wildlife species.
