Papaver pilosum

Papaver pilosum, commonly known as the silky poppy, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and striking, bright orange to red flowers. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, dark seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun exposure.

Papaver pilosum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
