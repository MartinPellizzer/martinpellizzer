Psilostachys filipes

Psilostachys filipes, commonly known as the Chinese silver grass, is a perennial grass native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, silver-gray blades and fluffy, feathery inflorescences that appear in late summer. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens. Its delicate appearance and ease of care have led to its cultivation in various regions beyond its native range.

Psilostachys filipes is also valued for its ecological benefits, providing habitat and food for small insects and birds.
