Pseudaleioides thouarsii

Pseudaleioides thouarsii, commonly known as the Madagascar orchid, is a unique species native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the orchid family (Orchidaceae) and is characterized by its striking, often colorful flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in the humid rainforests of its native habitat. Its botanical features include long, slender stems and intricate, showy floral structures that attract pollinators.

Due to its rarity and specific ecological requirements, it is considered a conservation concern in its natural environment.
