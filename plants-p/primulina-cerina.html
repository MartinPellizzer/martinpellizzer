Primulina cerina

Primulina cerina, commonly known as the silver-tipped arrowroot, is a species of flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Gesneriaceae family and is characterized by its striking silver-green leaves with a waxy texture. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or red in color. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Due to its unique appearance and ease of care, it is popular among indoor gardeners.
