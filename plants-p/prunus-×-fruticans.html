Prunus × fruticans

Prunus × fruticans, commonly known as the wild cherry or black cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to North America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its slender, often multi-stemmed growth habit. The plant produces small, white flowers in early spring, which are followed by edible, dark purple to black berries. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have serrated edges, while its bark is smooth and grayish in younger trees.

Prunus × fruticans is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance, providing food for wildlife and habitats for various insects.
