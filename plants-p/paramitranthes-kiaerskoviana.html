Paramitranthes kiaerskoviana

Paramitranthes kiaerskoviana, commonly known as the Kiaerskov's goldenweed, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in sunny, arid environments. Its yellow-orange flowers bloom in the summer months, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
