Phalaris tuberosa

Phalaris tuberosa, commonly known as the tuberous beardgrass, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, grassy leaves and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant produces tuberous roots, which are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.

Phalaris tuberosa is a perennial species that blooms in the summer months, adding a subtle ornamental value to its natural environment.
