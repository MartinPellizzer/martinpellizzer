Prunus jenkinsii

Prunus jenkinsii, commonly known as the Jenkins cherry, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Chile, where it thrives in temperate climates. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers in early spring, which are followed by edible berries that are often consumed by birds and mammals. Its leaves are elliptical with a glossy surface and turn yellow in the autumn.

Prunus jenkinsii is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance in its native habitat.
