Potamogeton gramineus var. hybridus

Potamogeton gramineus var. hybridus, commonly known as the hybrid pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in North America.

It has long, flexible stems that often form dense underwater vegetation, providing habitat for various aquatic organisms. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically found in clusters near the water surface. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often with a slightly rough texture.

This species is important for maintaining water quality and supporting aquatic ecosystems.
