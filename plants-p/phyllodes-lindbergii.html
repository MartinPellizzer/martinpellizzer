Phyllodes lindbergii

Phyllodes lindbergii, commonly known as the Lindberg's phyllodine, is a species of acacia native to Australia.

It is characterized by its distinctive phyllodes, which are flattened, leaf-like structures that serve as the primary photosynthetic organs. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, adapting to harsh conditions with its drought-resistant features. Its small, yellow flowers appear in compact spikes, adding ornamental value to the plant.

Phyllodes lindbergii is often used in horticulture for its hardy nature and unique foliage.
