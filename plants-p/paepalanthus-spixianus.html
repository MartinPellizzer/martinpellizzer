Paepalanthus spixianus

Paepalanthus spixianus, commonly known as the Spix's cactus, is a species of flowering plant native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its spiny stems and vibrant yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its flowers are often pollinated by bees and other insects, contributing to its ecological role in its native habitat.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered vulnerable and requires conservation efforts to ensure its survival.
