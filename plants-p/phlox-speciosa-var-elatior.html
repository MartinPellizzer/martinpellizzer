Phlox speciosa var. elatior

Phlox speciosa var. elatior, commonly known as the showy phlox, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Polemoniaceae family and is characterized by its showy, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its foliage is often a deep green, and it produces attractive, aromatic leaves that complement its vibrant blooms.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value in gardens and its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
