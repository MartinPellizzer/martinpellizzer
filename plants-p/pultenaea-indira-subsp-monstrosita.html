Pultenaea indira subsp. monstrosita

Pultenaea indira subsp.

monstrosita, commonly known as the purple wattle, is a shrub native to southeastern Australia. It is characterized by its dense, feathery foliage and clusters of small, bright yellow flowers.

The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is often found in open woodlands and heathlands. Its leaves are narrow and leathery, and the flowers bloom in late summer to early autumn.

The subspecies monstrosita is distinguished by its more robust growth and distinctive floral structure compared to other subspecies of Pultenaea indira.
