Potamogeton pectinatus var. coronatus

Potamogeton pectinatus var. coronatus, commonly known as the crown pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater habitats in Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in slow-moving or still waters, such as lakes, ponds, and canals. The plant has long, slender stems that are often branched and bear small, narrow leaves arranged in whorls. Its flowers are unisexual, with male flowers usually occurring in clusters at the base of the stem and female flowers higher up.

This species plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
