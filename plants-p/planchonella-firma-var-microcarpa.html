Planchonella firma var. microcarpa

Planchonella firma var. microcarpa, commonly known as the small-fruited pandanus, is a species native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a small evergreen tree or shrub characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces small, fleshy berries that are often yellow or orange in color, which are an important food source for various wildlife. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal or lowland areas.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
