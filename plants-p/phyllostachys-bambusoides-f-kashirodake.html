Phyllostachys bambusoides f. kashirodake

Phyllostachys bambusoides f. kashirodake, commonly known as the Kashiro-dake bamboo, is a variegated form of the bamboo species native to Japan.

It is characterized by its striking white and green striped culms, which add a distinctive ornamental value to gardens and landscapes. The plant typically grows as a clump-forming bamboo, reaching heights of up to 6 meters under optimal conditions. Its foliage is narrow and grass-like, with a slightly glossy texture that enhances its visual appeal.

This variety is well-suited for temperate climates and requires well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight for optimal growth.
