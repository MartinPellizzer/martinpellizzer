Pantorrhynchus maroccanus f. leiocarpum

Pantorrhynchus maroccanus f. leiocarpum, commonly known as the Moroccan horned lichen, is a unique lichen species characterized by its intricate thallus structure and distinct coloration.

It belongs to the family Caloplacaceae and is known for its leathery, crust-like growth form that adheres tightly to rock surfaces. This lichen is of particular interest to botanists due to its limited distribution and specialized ecological requirements.
