Potamogeton distinctus

Potamogeton distinctus, commonly known as the distinct water milfoil, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in North America.

It has slender, branching stems that are often found in shallow lakes and slow-moving rivers. The plant produces small, green to reddish flowers that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. Its leaves are narrow and ribbon-like, with a smooth surface and no prominent veins.

Potamogeton distinctus plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
