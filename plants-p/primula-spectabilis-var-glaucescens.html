Primula spectabilis var. glaucescens

Primula spectabilis var. glaucescens, commonly known as the bluebell primrose, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of China.

It features basal leaves that are broadly ovate to elliptic in shape, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped flowers, typically in shades of blue, purple, or white, depending on the cultivar. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in woodland or alpine habitats.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and shaded borders in horticulture.
