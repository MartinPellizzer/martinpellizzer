Primula × polyantha

Primula × polyantha, commonly known as the polyantha primrose, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Primula vulgaris and Primula obconica.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of small, showy flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows to a height of 10–30 cm and prefers well-drained, moist soil in partial shade. Its foliage consists of broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

This species is popular in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in cool, temperate climates.
