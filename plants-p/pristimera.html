Pristimera

Pristimera, commonly known as the ghost flower, is a unique plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, lantern-like flowers that resemble floating spirits, hence its common name. The plant belongs to the Aizoaceae family, known for its adaptations to harsh environments. Pristimera has a succulent structure, allowing it to store water and survive in drought conditions.

Its flowers bloom at night, attracting specific pollinators and adding to its mysterious allure.
