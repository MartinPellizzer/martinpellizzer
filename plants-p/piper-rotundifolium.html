Piper rotundifolium

Piper rotundifolium, commonly known as the round-leaved pepper or Mexican pepper plant, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to Central America and parts of Mexico, where it thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. The plant is characterized by its rounded, glossy leaves and small, fragrant flowers that produce berries. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and for the spicy flavor of its fruits.

The plant prefers well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a suitable candidate for shaded garden spaces.
