Piper obumbratifolium

Piper obumbratifolium, commonly known as the shaded-leaf pepper, is a flowering plant native to the rainforests of Queensland, Australia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species. The plant is characterized by its dark green, glossy leaves that are often shaded by the dense canopy of its habitat. It produces small, white flowers followed by red, fleshy berries that are edible.

The botanical features of this plant make it an important component of its ecosystem and a subject of study in tropical botany.
