Poncirus polyandra

Poncirus polyandra, commonly known as the Chinese boxwood, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny foliage and small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically a glossy green. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by orange-red berries that are edible but often bitter. It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.

However, it is susceptible to certain pests and diseases, requiring careful maintenance.
