Plocama putorioides

Plocama putorioides, commonly known as the purple poppy or sweet willie, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It features delicate, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has slender, branching stems and heart-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight.

Plocama putorioides is often cultivated for its ornamental value and historical use in traditional medicine.
