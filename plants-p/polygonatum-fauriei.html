Polygonatum fauriei

Polygonatum fauriei, commonly known as the Chinese Solomon's seal, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Asparagaceae family and is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit. The plant features lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of small, white or greenish-white flowers. It thrives in shady, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its ornamental value and medicinal properties make it a notable species in horticulture and traditional medicine.
