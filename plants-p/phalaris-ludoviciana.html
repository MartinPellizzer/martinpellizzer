Phalaris ludoviciana

Phalaris ludoviciana, commonly known as the Louisiana panicgrass, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in wetland habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. The plant has slender, grass-like leaves and produces loose, spike-like clusters of small, greenish flowers. It is often found in areas with high moisture and is adapted to tolerate flooding.

Its presence is an indicator of healthy wetland ecosystems.
