Piper hirtovarium

Piper hirtovarium, commonly known as the hairy pepper vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species. The plant is characterized by its hairy stems and small, greenish-white flowers. Its fruits are berries that turn red when mature, and they are often used in traditional medicine.

The leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a mild peppery aroma.
