Pandanus odoratissimus var. suvaensis

Pandanus odoratissimus var. suvaensis, commonly known as the Suva pandanus, is a tropical plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its long, sword-shaped leaves that are often used in traditional crafts and ceremonies. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically clustered in branched inflorescences. Its roots and leaves are used in local medicine for their purported healing properties.

The Suva pandanus thrives in coastal and humid environments, often growing near water bodies.
