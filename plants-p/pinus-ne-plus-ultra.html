Pinus ne-plus-ultra

Pinus ne-plus-ultra, commonly known as the "extreme pine," is a rare and unusual species of conifer native to the high altitudes of the Andes in South America.

It is characterized by its needle-like leaves, which are typically longer and more rigid than those of other pine species. The tree produces distinctive cones that are often covered in a dense layer of resin, which helps protect the seeds from moisture and pests. Its growth is slow, and it thrives in cool, humid climates with well-drained soil.
