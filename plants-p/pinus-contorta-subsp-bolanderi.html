Pinus contorta subsp. bolanderi

Pinus contorta subsp.

bolanderi, commonly known as the Bolander white pine, is a subspecies native to the western United States. It is characterized by its slender, often contorted trunk and long, needle-like leaves.

The tree produces cones that are typically reddish-brown and contain edible seeds. It thrives in high-elevation environments, often in open or rocky areas.

This subspecies is adapted to cold, dry conditions and is an important component of subalpine and alpine ecosystems.
