Pellaea namaquensis

Pellaea namaquensis, commonly known as the Namaqua spleenworts, is a species of fern belonging to the family Pellaeaceae.

It is native to southern Africa, particularly found in the arid regions of the Western Cape. This plant is characterized by its compact growth habit and small, clustered fronds. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

The plant's spore-producing structures are located on the underside of its leaves, typical of many fern species.
