Phyllanthera lancifolia

Phyllanthera lancifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved phyllanthera, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its elongated, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest undergrowth. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The species is valued both for its ornamental appeal and its medicinal properties.
