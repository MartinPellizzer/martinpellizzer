Pomatocalpa orientale

Pomatocalpa orientale, commonly known as the oriental pink orchid, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking pink or purple floral spikes. The plant produces small, fragrant orchid flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense colonies in moist, shaded environments.

Pomatocalpa orientale is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is appreciated in horticultural gardens.
