Picea abies f. inverta

Picea abies f. inverta, commonly known as the Inverted Norway spruce, is a unique form of the Norway spruce characterized by its upward-curving needles.

This variation results in a distinctive, almost umbrella-like shape, which sets it apart from the typical conical form of the species. The foliage is dense and dark green, with a fine, silvery-white underside that gives it a striking appearance. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant in gardens and parks due to its aesthetic appeal.

The plant is relatively hardy and adaptable, making it suitable for a variety of climates and soil conditions.
