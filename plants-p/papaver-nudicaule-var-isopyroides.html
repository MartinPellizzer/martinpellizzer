Papaver nudicaule var. isopyroides

Papaver nudicaule var. isopyroides, commonly known as the slender poppy, is a variant of the meadow poppy native to Europe.

It is a herbaceous annual plant characterized by its delicate, nodding flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant features slender, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stem. Its flowers are borne on short pedicels and are often clustered at the tips of the branches.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
