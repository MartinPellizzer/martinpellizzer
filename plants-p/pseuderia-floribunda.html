Pseuderia floribunda

Pseuderia floribunda, commonly known as the clustered spider orchid, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its intricate, spider-like floral structure. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, with a distinctive, elongated spur. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists.
