Phaseolus lasiocarpus

Phaseolus lasiocarpus, commonly known as the hairy pod bean, is a species of legume native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its hairy, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. The plant typically grows as a climbing vine, reaching lengths of up to several meters. Its leaves are trifoliate, with each leaflet having a serrated edge.

The flowers are usually pale purple or white, and they bloom in clusters.
