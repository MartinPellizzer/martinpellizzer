Polygala lupulina

Polygala lupulina, commonly known as the hop-polygala, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia, including China and Japan.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that often have a distinctive, intricate structure. The plant typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and can reach heights of up to 1 meter. Its leaves are opposite and ovate, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

The fruit is a dry capsule that contains several small, elongated seeds.
