Paropsia gabonica

Paropsia gabonica, commonly known as the African golden orchid, is a species of orchid native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking yellow and green flowers, which bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or wooded areas and prefers well-drained soil. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

Paropsia gabonica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
