Puya ramosissima

Puya ramosissima, commonly known as the Queen of the Andes, is a striking plant native to the high altitudes of South America.

It is a member of the bromeliad family and is characterized by its tall, spire-like inflorescence composed of numerous vibrant red flowers. The plant produces a dense rosette of long, narrow leaves that can grow up to several meters in length. It thrives in cool, humid environments and is adapted to the harsh conditions of the Andean mountains.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
