Psiadia ageratoides

Psiadia ageratoides, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, sunny habitats such as wetlands and swamps. The plant features slender, branching stems covered in fine, silky hairs. Its most distinctive characteristic is the clusters of small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall.

Psiadia ageratoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
