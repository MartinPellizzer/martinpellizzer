Pancratium quitoense

Pancratium quitoense, commonly known as the Quito yellow flower, is a striking bulbous plant native to the Andean highlands of Ecuador.

It features a tall, erect stem with bright yellow, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has a fibrous root system and a bulb that stores nutrients for growth. Its leaves are linear and grass-like, often with a bluish-green hue.

Pancratium quitoense is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
