Pothos ecclesiae

Pothos ecclesiae, commonly known as the houseplant of the church, is a member of the Araceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and vigorous climbing habit. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by spadices, which are typical of the Araceae family. It thrives in low-light conditions and requires moderate watering.

Pothos ecclesiae is popular as an ornamental plant due to its hardiness and aesthetic appeal.
