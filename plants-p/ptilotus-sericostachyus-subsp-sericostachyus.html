Ptilotus sericostachyus subsp. sericostachyus

Ptilotus sericostachyus subsp.

sericostachyus, commonly known as the silky ptilotus, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of Australia. It is characterized by its striking, sparsely branched stems and clusters of small, pink to purple flowers.

The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it its common name. It is adapted to survive in dry, sandy soils and can thrive in full sun.

The silky ptilotus is often found in coastal dunes and is a popular ornamental plant due to its unique appearance.
