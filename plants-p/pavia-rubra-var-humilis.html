Pavia rubra var. humilis

Pavia rubra var. humilis, commonly known as the dwarf red pavia, is a low-growing perennial herb native to parts of East Asia.

It features small, clustered flowers that range in color from pink to red, typically blooming in early spring. The plant has slender, often hairy stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or shaded areas.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
