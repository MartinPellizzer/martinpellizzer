Plantago major var. glabra

Plantago major var. glabra, commonly known as broadleaf plantain, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It features a rosette of broad, oval-shaped leaves that are smooth and often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in dense spikes during the summer months. It is commonly found in lawns, gardens, and disturbed soils due to its adaptability.

Its leaves are used in traditional medicine for their healing properties.
