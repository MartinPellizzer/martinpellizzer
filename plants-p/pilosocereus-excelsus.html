Pilosocereus excelsus

Pilosocereus excelsus, commonly known as the Christmas cactus, is a species of cactus native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its segmented, branching stems that are covered in short, wooly hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces vibrant flowers in shades of red, pink, and orange, typically during the holiday season. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires bright, indirect light to flourish.

Due to its ornamental value, it is a popular houseplant worldwide.
