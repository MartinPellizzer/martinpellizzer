Panax ginseng f. angustifolius

Panax ginseng f. angustifolius, commonly known as narrow-leaf ginseng, is a botanical variant of the ginseng plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its narrow, elongated leaves and smaller, more compact roots compared to the standard Panax ginseng. This form is often cultivated for its medicinal properties, similar to other ginseng species. The plant thrives in cool, humid climates and requires well-drained, loamy soil.

Its botanical significance lies in its unique morphology and potential pharmacological applications.
