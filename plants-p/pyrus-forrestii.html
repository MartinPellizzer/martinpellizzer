Pyrus forrestii

Pyrus forrestii, commonly known as the Yunnan pear, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape. The tree produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters, followed by edible fruits that are similar to pears in texture but have a more acidic flavor. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to temperate climates.

The plant is also valued for its potential in horticultural and medicinal applications.
