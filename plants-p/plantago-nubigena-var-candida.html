Plantago nubigena var. candida

Plantago nubigena var. candida, commonly known as the white plantain, is a flowering plant belonging to the Plantaginaceae family.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a dense layer of fine, white hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually pale green to white in color. It is often found in moist, disturbed soils in temperate regions.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage.
