Primula buryana var. purpurea

Primula buryana var. purpurea, commonly known as the purple primrose, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its rosette of broad, dark green leaves and produces clusters of showy, violet-pink flowers in early spring. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and prefers partial to full shade. Its flowers are often visited by bees and other pollinators, contributing to its ecological significance.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and hardiness in temperate climates.
