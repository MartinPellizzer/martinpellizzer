Phyllostachys viridiglaucescens var. hinkulii

Phyllostachys viridiglaucescens var. hinkulii, commonly known as Hinkuli bamboo, is a variant of the greenish-blue bamboo species native to China.

It is characterized by its slender, erect culms that are often tinged with a striking greenish-blue hue, especially when young. The leaves are narrow and lanceolate, with a smooth texture and a vibrant, almost metallic sheen. This bamboo thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it suitable for both ornamental and landscaping purposes.

It is prized for its aesthetic appeal and ease of cultivation in temperate climates.
