Prunus jamasakura f. arakawaensis

Prunus jamasakura f. arakawaensis, commonly known as the Arakawa cherry, is a botanical variant of the Japanese cherry tree.

It is characterized by its distinctive pinkish-white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant is native to Japan and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and parks. It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is closely related to other Prunus species.

Its foliage turns vibrant red in the fall, adding to its aesthetic appeal.
