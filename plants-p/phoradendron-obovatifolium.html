Phoradendron obovatifolium

Phoradendron obovatifolium, commonly known as the Florida mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on various host trees, primarily in the southeastern United States.

It possesses small, ovate leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss in its often-dry habitat. The plant produces small, white or pale green flowers that are typically inconspicuous and are followed by berries that contain sticky seeds. Its parasitic nature allows it to absorb water and nutrients from its host, while still performing some photosynthesis.

This species plays a role in the ecosystem by providing food for birds and other animals that consume its berries.
