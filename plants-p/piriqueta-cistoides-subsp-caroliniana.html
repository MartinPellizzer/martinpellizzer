Piriqueta cistoides subsp. caroliniana

Piriqueta cistoides subsp.

caroliniana, commonly known as the Carolina rock cress, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the southeastern United States. It typically features small, rounded leaves and produces clusters of bright yellow flowers in early spring.

The plant thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats. It is well adapted to dry conditions and is considered a hardy, drought-tolerant species.

Its botanical structure reflects its evolutionary adaptation to rocky, arid environments.
