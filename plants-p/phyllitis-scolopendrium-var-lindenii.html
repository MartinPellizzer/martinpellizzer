Phyllitis scolopendrium var. lindenii

Phyllitis scolopendrium var. lindenii, commonly known as the Lindenberg's maidenhair fern, is a variant of the common maidenhair fern.

It is a perennial fern characterized by its delicate, lace-like fronds that are typically bright green. The plant is native to parts of Europe and is often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its reproductive structures, including sporangia, are located on the underside of the fronds.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
