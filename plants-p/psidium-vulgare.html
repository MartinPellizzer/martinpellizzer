Psidium vulgare

Psidium vulgare, commonly known as the common guava, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to the Americas.

It produces fragrant, white flowers that develop into edible berries with a sweet, aromatic flavor. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and ovate with a glossy surface.

The fruit is rich in vitamin C and is widely cultivated for both culinary and medicinal purposes.
