Phyllanthus hirtellus var. thymoides

Phyllanthus hirtellus var. thymoides, commonly known as thymeleaf phyllanthus, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its small, aromatic leaves that resemble those of thyme, hence its common name. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Its botanical features make it a subject of interest in both horticulture and ethnobotanical studies.
