Passiflora rubra

Passiflora rubra, commonly known as the purple passionflower, is a species native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is a climbing vine characterized by its vibrant purple and red flowers, which are adorned with intricate patterns and feathery sepals. The plant produces edible berries that are rich in antioxidants and have a sweet, tangy flavor. Its foliage consists of large, heart-shaped leaves with a rough, hairy texture.

Passiflora rubra is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its striking appearance and ecological importance as a food source for various pollinators.
