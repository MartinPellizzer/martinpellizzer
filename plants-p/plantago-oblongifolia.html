Plantago oblongifolia

Plantago oblongifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf plantain, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It features a rosette of broad, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in a dense spike, which are clustered in inflorescences at the tips of its stems. It is a member of the Plantaginaceae family and is often found in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and fields.

The leaves are used in traditional medicine for their soothing and healing properties.
