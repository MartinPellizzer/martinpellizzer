Pyrus × veitchii

Pyrus × veitchii, commonly known as the Veitch's pear, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Pyrus communis (European pear) and Pyrus pyrifolia (Asian pear).

It is a deciduous tree that typically grows to a height of 5–10 meters, featuring broad, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of white or pale yellow flowers in spring. The fruit is usually small, sweet, and has a crisp texture, making it suitable for both fresh consumption and culinary use.

This hybrid is valued for its ornamental qualities and adaptability to various climates, though it may require specific care to thrive.
