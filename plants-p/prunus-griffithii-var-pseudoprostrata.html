Prunus griffithii var. pseudoprostrata

Prunus griffithii var. pseudoprostrata, commonly known as the creeping cherry or Japanese snowbell, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its slender, prostrate growth habit and produces clusters of small, white, bell-shaped flowers in early spring. The leaves are elliptical, with a glossy green upper surface and a pale underside, and they turn red in the fall. The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens for its delicate floral display.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.
