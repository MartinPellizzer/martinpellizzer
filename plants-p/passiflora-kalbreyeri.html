Passiflora kalbreyeri

Passiflora kalbreyeri, commonly known as the Kalbreyer passionflower, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, complex flowers that feature five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments. The plant produces edible, orange-yellow berries that are rich in antioxidants. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped with a velvety texture.

Passiflora kalbreyeri is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its unique floral structure and vibrant appearance.
