Paspalum peckii

Paspalum peckii, commonly known as the Florida paspalum, is a grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in wetland environments and is often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

Paspalum peckii is an important component of wetland ecosystems, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
