Penstemon utahensis

Penstemon utahensis, commonly known as Utah beardtongue, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, tubular flowers. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, producing pink to purple flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Utah beardtongue thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for xeriscaping in arid regions.

Its adaptability and ornamental value make it a popular plant among gardeners and botanists alike.
