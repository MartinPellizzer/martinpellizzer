Primula obovata

Primula obovata, commonly known as the broad-ovate primrose, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It features upright stems with lanceolate to ovate leaves that are often hairy and have a waxy coating. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, typically blooming in spring. It prefers cool, moist environments and is often found in woodland or alpine habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
