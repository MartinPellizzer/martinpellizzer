Piper methysticum var. wichmannii

Piper methysticum var. wichmannii, commonly known as the Papuasian kava, is a tropical shrub native to the islands of the western Pacific.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and small, greenish-white flowers. The plant is cultivated primarily for its roots, which contain compounds used in traditional medicinal practices. Its botanical structure supports its role in cultural and pharmacological contexts.

The variety is distinguished by its specific geographic distribution and chemical composition compared to other kava varieties.
