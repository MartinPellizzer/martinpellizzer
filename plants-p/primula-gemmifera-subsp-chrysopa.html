Primula gemmifera subsp. chrysopa

Primula gemmifera subsp.

chrysopa, commonly known as the yellow primrose, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in early spring.

The leaves are typically broad and oval, with a slightly hairy texture. This subspecies is often found in rocky or well-drained soils at higher elevations.

It is valued in horticulture for its vibrant flowers and relatively easy cultivation.
