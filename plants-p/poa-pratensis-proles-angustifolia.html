Poa pratensis proles angustifolia

Poa pratensis proles angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf meadow grass, is a grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its slender, elongated leaves that are typically less than 2 mm wide, giving it a distinct appearance compared to other Poa species. This plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, pastures, and along stream banks. Its inflorescence consists of a dense, spike-like cluster of spikelets, which are usually pale green to brown in color.

Poa pratensis proles angustifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in maintaining ecological balance in grassland ecosystems.
