Panicum sublaetum

Panicum sublaetum, commonly known as narrowleaf panicgrass, is a perennial grass native to parts of North America.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and rocky soils. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and is often found in disturbed or marginal areas.

Its botanical features make it an important component of native plant communities in its range.
