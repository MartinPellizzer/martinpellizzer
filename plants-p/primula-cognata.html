Primula cognata

Primula cognata, commonly known as the alpine primrose, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple, depending on the variety. Its leaves are oval and slightly hairy, adapted to withstand harsh environmental conditions.

Primula cognata is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to bloom in early spring.
