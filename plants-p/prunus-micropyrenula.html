Prunus micropyrenula

Prunus micropyrenula, commonly known as the dwarf cherry or small-fruited cherry, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is a small deciduous shrub native to parts of East Asia, including China and Japan. The plant produces small, edible fruits that are typically red or orange in color. Its flowers are white or pink and appear in spring, followed by the development of its characteristic small fruits.

Prunus micropyrenula is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic beauty.
