Pycreus flavidus var. strictus

Pycreus flavidus var. strictus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved golden bell, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, upright stems that bear small, yellowish flowers clustered in bell-shaped inflorescences. The leaves are narrow and lanceolate, with a slightly hairy surface, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or dry habitats.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
