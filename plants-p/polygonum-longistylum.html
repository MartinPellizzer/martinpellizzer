Polygonum longistylum

Polygonum longistylum, commonly known as the long-stemmed knotweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, which includes plants known for their distinctive nodes and often triangular stems. The plant typically grows in moist, disturbed soils along stream banks and wetlands. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a reddish tint, while its flowers are small and clustered in dense, terminal spikes.

Polygonum longistylum is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in wetland environments.
