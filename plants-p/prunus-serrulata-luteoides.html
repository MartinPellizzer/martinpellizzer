Prunus serrulata luteoides

Prunus serrulata luteoides, commonly known as the Japanese cherry blossom, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its delicate, pale yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, preceding the emergence of its heart-shaped leaves. The plant belongs to the Rosaceae family and is known for its ornamental value, particularly in gardens and parks. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, requiring moderate watering during its growing season.

The species is also valued for its ecological role, providing food for pollinators and contributing to the aesthetic beauty of spring landscapes.
