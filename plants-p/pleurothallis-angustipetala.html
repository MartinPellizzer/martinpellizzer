Pleurothallis angustipetala

Pleurothallis angustipetala, commonly known as the narrow-leafed pleurothallis, is a species of orchid native to the cloud forests of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its slender, often pendulous stems and small, elongated leaves that are typically arranged in a spiral pattern. The flowers are usually white or pale pink, with intricate, delicate structures that are typical of the Pleurothallis genus. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained, acidic soil for optimal growth.

This orchid is appreciated for its ornamental value and is often cultivated by enthusiasts due to its unique and elegant appearance.
