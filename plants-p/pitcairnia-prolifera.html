Pitcairnia prolifera

Pitcairnia prolifera, commonly known as the "mother of thousands," is a bromeliad native to the Andes of South America.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped foliage and produces clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers. The plant is notable for its ability to propagate through offsets, which form along the edges of the leaves. It thrives in shaded, humid environments and is often found in rocky or forested areas.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for indoor and outdoor gardens.
