Prunus mutabilis f. reginae

Prunus mutabilis f. reginae, commonly known as the Queen's cherry, is a unique cultivar of the Prunus mutabilis species, also called the double-flowered cherry.

This plant is characterized by its striking, double flowers that change color throughout the blooming season, typically from white to pink and then to deep red. The leaves are ovate with a smooth margin and a glossy green hue. It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and seasonal interest.
