Passerina gymnostachya

Passerina gymnostachya, commonly known as the scarlet globemallow, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in dry, rocky soils and arid environments. The plant features showy, bright red or orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its drought tolerance.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
