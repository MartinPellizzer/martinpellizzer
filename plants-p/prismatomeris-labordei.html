Prismatomeris labordei

Prismatomeris labordei, commonly known as the Mexican blue orchid, is a rare and striking orchid species native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its vibrant blue-violet petals and a unique, intricate floral structure. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting specific pollinators such as bees and moths.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a conservation concern.
