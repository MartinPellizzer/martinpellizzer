Phalaris arundinacea var. vivipara

Phalaris arundinacea var. vivipara, commonly known as the viviparous reed, is a perennial grass native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems that can grow up to 2 meters in height, and its narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces spikelets that are often clustered at the base of the stem, and it is notable for its ability to produce live young shoots from the base, a trait known as viviparity. It thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils and is often found near wetlands, marshes, and stream banks.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, supporting various insect and bird species.
