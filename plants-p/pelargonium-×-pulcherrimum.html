Pelargonium × pulcherrimum

Pelargonium × pulcherrimum, commonly known as the geranium, is a popular flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its showy, five-petaled flowers that come in a wide range of colors, including red, pink, purple, and white. The plant typically has rounded, lobed leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a versatile choice for gardens and containers.

Pelargonium × pulcherrimum is also known for its aromatic foliage, which can be used in potpourris or as a natural air freshener.
