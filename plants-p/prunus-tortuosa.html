Prunus tortuosa

Prunus tortuosa, commonly known as the twisted cherry or Japanese cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its gnarled, twisted branches and small, oval leaves that turn yellow in autumn. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pink flowers in spring, followed by edible, red drupes. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Prunus tortuosa is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.
