Pelargonium rarum

Pelargonium rarum, commonly known as the rare geranium, is a species native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety leaves and showy, tubular flowers that range in color from red to purple. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters. Its foliage is often adorned with prominent veins, and it thrives in well-drained soil with ample sunlight.

Pelargonium rarum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
