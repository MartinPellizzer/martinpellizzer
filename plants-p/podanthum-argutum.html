Podanthum argutum

Podanthum argutum, commonly known as the narrow-leafed mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on the branches of host trees, primarily in tropical and subtropical regions.

It has small, elliptical leaves that are usually arranged in opposite pairs, and its flowers are typically greenish-white with four petals. The plant produces small, fleshy berries that are dispersed by birds, aiding in its propagation. Its root system forms haustoria, which penetrate the host's vascular system to absorb water and nutrients.

Podanthum argutum plays a role in local ecosystems by providing food for various bird species and contributing to the biodiversity of its habitat.
