Physalis fendleri

Physalis fendleri, commonly known as the desert tomatillo, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its hairy, spiny calyx that encases the fruit, which resembles a small, yellowish tomato. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its fruit is edible and often used in local cuisines, though it is less commonly cultivated than other Physalis species.

The plant's structure and growth habits make it an important component of its native ecosystem.
