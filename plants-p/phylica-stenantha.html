Phylica stenantha

Phylica stenantha, commonly known as the narrow-leaved phylica, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves and clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant is often found in rocky or sandy soils, thriving in arid and semi-arid environments. Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and birds, contributing to local biodiversity.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
