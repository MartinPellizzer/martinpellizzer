Palmijuncus petraeus

Palmijuncus petraeus, commonly known as the rock date palm, is a rare and ancient species native to the arid regions of the Arabian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its short, robust trunk and a crown of narrow, fan-shaped leaves that are adapted to conserve water. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences that yield edible dates, though it is less productive than other date palm varieties. Its resilience to extreme drought and high temperatures makes it well-suited for harsh desert environments.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
