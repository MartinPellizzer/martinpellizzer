Panicum indicum var. elatum

Panicum indicum var. elatum, commonly known as tall Indian grass, is a perennial grass native to the warmer regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can reach heights of up to 2 meters. The plant produces panicles of small, whitish to golden-green florets, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a common sight in prairies and disturbed areas.

This species is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
