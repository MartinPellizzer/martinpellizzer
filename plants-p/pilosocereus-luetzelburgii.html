Pilosocereus luetzelburgii

Pilosocereus luetzelburgii, commonly known as the Luetzelburg's candelabra cactus, is a species native to Brazil.

It is a slow-growing, columnar cactus that forms a branched structure resembling a candelabra. The plant features spiny, ribbed stems with areoles that bear white, fluffy spines. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, appearing at the tips of the branches during the warmer months.

It is adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water and well-draining soil.
