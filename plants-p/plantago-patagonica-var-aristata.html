Plantago patagonica var. aristata

Plantago patagonica var. aristata, commonly known as the Patagonian plantain, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Argentina and Chile.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its rosette of broad, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces spikes of small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences. It is often found in arid or semi-arid environments, thriving in well-drained soils.

This variety is notable for its adaptability and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
