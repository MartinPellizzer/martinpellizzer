Plantago jurtzevii

Plantago jurtzevii, commonly known as the Siberian plantain, is a perennial herb native to the cold climates of Central Asia.

It features a rosette of broad, hairy leaves that are often covered in fine, silvery pubescence. The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in dense spikes, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or steppe environments.

Its medicinal properties have been historically used in traditional medicine for their soothing and anti-inflammatory effects.
