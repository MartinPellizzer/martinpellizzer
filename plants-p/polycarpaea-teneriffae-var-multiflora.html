Polycarpaea teneriffae var. multiflora

Polycarpaea teneriffae var. multiflora, commonly known as the Canary Island geranium, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the Geraniaceae family and is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit. The plant produces clusters of small, pink to red flowers during the summer months. Its leaves are typically ovate with a slightly hairy surface, and it thrives in well-drained, sunny conditions.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance and resilience.
