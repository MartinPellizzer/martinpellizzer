Phaseolus schottii var. genuinus

Phaseolus schottii var. genuinus, commonly known as the yellow passionflower, is a climbing vine native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers with intricate patterns of red and purple, which are highly attractive to pollinators. The plant produces small, round, green pods that contain kidney-shaped seeds. Its leaves are heart-shaped with a velvety texture and a slightly hairy surface.

This species is valued both for its ornamental beauty and its potential use in traditional medicine.
