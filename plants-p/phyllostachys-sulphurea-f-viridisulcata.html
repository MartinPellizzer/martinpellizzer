Phyllostachys sulphurea f. viridisulcata

Phyllostachys sulphurea f. viridisulcata, commonly known as the green-striped bamboo, is a variant of the golden bamboo species.

It is characterized by its slender, upright culms that exhibit a distinctive green stripe along the stem. The leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, with a bright green coloration that contrasts with the lighter green of the stems. This bamboo thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for ornamental landscaping.

Its unique appearance and hardy nature make it a popular choice for gardeners and landscape designers.
