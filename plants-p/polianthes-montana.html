Polianthes montana

Polianthes montana, commonly known as the mountain amaryllis, is a striking bulbous plant native to Mexico.

It features showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and red, often with a distinctive red or purple stripe down the center. The plant produces strap-like, grassy leaves that are evergreen in mild climates and die back in colder regions. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for gardens and containers.

Polianthes montana is known for its long blooming period, typically from late spring to early summer.
