Piper amphibracteum

Piper amphibracteum, commonly known as the broad-bract pepper, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many species of pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves and small, greenish-white flowers clustered in axillary inflorescences. Its fruits are small, berry-like structures that mature to a dark purple color.

While not widely cultivated for spice, it holds ecological significance in its native habitats.
