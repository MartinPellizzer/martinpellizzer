Penstemon degeneri

Penstemon degeneri, commonly known as the narrowleaf beardtongue, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces tall spikes of tubular, two-lipped flowers that range in color from pink to purple, attracting pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping and native gardens.

Penstemon degeneri is also valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological benefits in supporting local wildlife.
