Priva peruviana

Priva peruviana, commonly known as the Peruvian privet, is a shrub native to the Andean regions of South America.

It features opposite, simple, and often hairy leaves that are typically oval to elliptic in shape. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in clusters, which are usually white or pale yellow. Its fruit is a small, fleshy drupe that contains a single seed.

Priva peruviana is often found in moist, shaded habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal.
