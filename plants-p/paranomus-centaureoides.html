Paranomus centaureoides

Paranomus centaureoides, commonly known as the narrowleaf star-thistle, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small stars. The plant has narrow, linear leaves and produces small, purple to pinkish flowers that bloom in summer. It is often found in dry, disturbed soils and can be invasive in some areas.

The species is known for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in arid environments.
