Pogonotrophe zeylanica

Pogonotrophe zeylanica, commonly known as the Ceylon velvet tamarind, is a tropical shrub native to Sri Lanka and southern India.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant produces small, edible pods that contain seeds surrounded by a fibrous pulp. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in local medicine.

Its growth habit and botanical features make it well adapted to humid, tropical climates.
