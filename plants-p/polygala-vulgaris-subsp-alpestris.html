Polygala vulgaris subsp. alpestris

Polygala vulgaris subsp.

alpestris, commonly known as the alpine milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It typically grows in dry, rocky, or alpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils.

The plant features slender stems and small, usually green or pale yellow flowers clustered in racemes. Its leaves are often linear or lance-shaped, and it produces pods that contain small, hard seeds.

This subspecies is adapted to harsh environments and is often found in high-altitude meadows and grasslands.
