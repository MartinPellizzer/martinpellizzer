Phyllanthus casticum var. glaber

Phyllanthus casticum var. glaber, commonly known as the smooth phyllanthus, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are often smooth to the touch. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in clusters, typically followed by capsule fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires moderate to high light exposure.

Its botanical features make it a subject of interest in both horticulture and ecological studies.
