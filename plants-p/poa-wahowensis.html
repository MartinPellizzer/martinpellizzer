Poa wahowensis

Poa wahowensis, commonly known as the Whangarei grass, is a perennial grass species native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, erect stems. The plant has narrow, blade-like leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

Poa wahowensis is often found in coastal and wetland habitats, contributing to the ecological diversity of these regions.
