Pharmacosycea angustifolia

Pharmacosycea angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf pharmacosycea, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories. Its botanical features include a fibrous root system and a branching stem covered in fine hairs.

It is valued for its potential medicinal properties, though further research is needed to confirm its therapeutic uses.
