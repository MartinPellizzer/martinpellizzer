Pandanus scopulorum

Pandanus scopulorum, commonly known as the rock pandanus, is a tropical plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and long, narrow leaves that are often spirally arranged. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences with fragrant, yellowish flowers. Its leaves are used in traditional crafts and as thatching material.

Pandanus scopulorum thrives in coastal and rocky habitats, often growing in well-drained soils.
