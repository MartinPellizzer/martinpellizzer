Prunus serrulata f. multiplex

Prunus serrulata f. multiplex, commonly known as the Japanese cherry blossom or Somei Yoshino, is a cultivar of the Prunus serrulata species.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, pale pink to white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant is a deciduous shrub or small tree, typically reaching heights of 5 to 10 meters. Its leaves are oval-shaped with finely serrated edges, and it produces edible, tart cherries in the summer.

This cultivar is widely cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its showy floral display.
