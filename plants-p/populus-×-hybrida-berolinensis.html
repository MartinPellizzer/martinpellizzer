Populus × hybrida berolinensis

Populus × hybrida berolinensis, commonly known as the Berlin poplar, is a hybrid species within the Populus genus.

It is characterized by its fast growth rate and adaptability to various soil conditions. The plant typically exhibits broad, heart-shaped leaves that are dark green on top and lighter in color underneath. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers in the spring, followed by winged seeds that are dispersed by the wind.

This species is often used in urban landscaping due to its ornamental value and ability to thrive in urban environments.
