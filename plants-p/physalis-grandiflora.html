Physalis grandiflora

Physalis grandiflora, commonly known as the giant Chinese lantern, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive orange-red, inflated calyces that enclose the small, yellow berries. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves. Its flowers are bell-shaped and usually pale yellow, which give way to the vibrant fruits.

The botanical features of Physalis grandiflora make it a popular ornamental plant in gardens and greenhouses.
