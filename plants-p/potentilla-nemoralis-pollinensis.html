Potentilla nemoralis pollinensis

Potentilla nemoralis pollinensis, commonly known as the alpine potentilla, is a low-growing shrub native to alpine and subalpine regions.

It features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has slender, upright stems covered in fine hairs, and its leaves are narrow and leathery, adapted to cold, high-altitude environments. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in mountain ecosystems.
