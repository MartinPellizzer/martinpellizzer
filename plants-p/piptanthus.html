Piptanthus

Piptanthus, commonly known as the Chinese peashrub, is a genus of flowering plants in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It consists of several species native to arid and semi-arid regions of Asia, particularly China, Mongolia, and Tibet. These plants are characterized by their small, pea-like flowers and compound leaves, which are often adapted to withstand harsh environmental conditions. The genus is notable for its ability to fix nitrogen through symbiotic relationships with rhizobia bacteria, making it valuable in soil enrichment.

Piptanthus species are also appreciated for their ornamental value and are sometimes cultivated in gardens for their hardy nature and aesthetic appeal.
