Persica vulgaris f. alba

Persica vulgaris f. alba, commonly known as the white peach.

This is a botanical variant of the peach tree characterized by its white or pale pink flowers and fruit. It is a flowering plant in the Rosaceae family, known for its ornamental value and edible fruit. The plant typically grows in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for both horticultural and ornamental purposes.
