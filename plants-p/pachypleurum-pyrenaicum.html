Pachypleurum pyrenaicum

Pachypleurum pyrenaicum, commonly known as the Pyrenean bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Pyrenees region.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and mountain slopes. The plant features upright stems covered in dense, hairy foliage. Its flowers are showy, bell-shaped, and usually blue or violet in color, blooming in late summer.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local pollinators.
