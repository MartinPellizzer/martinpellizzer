Persea chrysophylla

Persea chrysophylla, commonly known as the golden-leaf persea, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central America.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves that are typically a vibrant yellow-green in color. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are white or pale yellow, followed by edible fruits resembling small, smooth-coated cherries. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The tree is valued for its ornamental beauty and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
