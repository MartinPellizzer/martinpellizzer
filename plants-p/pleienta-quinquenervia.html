Pleienta quinquenervia

Pleienta quinquenervia, commonly known as the five-veined pleienta, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves with five prominent veins running from the base to the tip. The plant produces small, clustered white flowers that bloom during the rainy season. Its root system is fibrous and often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
