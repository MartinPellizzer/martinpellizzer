Pachystachys linearibracteata

Pachystachys linearibracteata, commonly known as the sword bamboo, is a member of the grass family (Poaceae) and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender culms that can grow up to several meters in height, with long, narrow leaves that are often bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, white to pale yellow flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested or swampy areas.

Due to its ornamental value and ease of cultivation, it is popular in horticulture and landscape design.
