Physaria pallida

Physaria pallida, commonly known as the pale bladderpod, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit and is often found in dry, sandy or gravelly soils. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and covered in a fine layer of white pubescence.

Physaria pallida is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes many flowering plants with four-petaled flowers.
