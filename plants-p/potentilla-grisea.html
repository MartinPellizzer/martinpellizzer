Potentilla grisea

Potentilla grisea, commonly known as gray cinquefoil, is a perennial flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage and showy yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, woodlands, and stream banks. It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is known for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in various environmental conditions.

The small, five-petaled flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
