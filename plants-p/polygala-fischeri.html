Polygala fischeri

Polygala fischeri, commonly known as the Fischer's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its small, showy flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. The plant has slender, branching stems and small, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing on rocky soils or in sandy areas.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists alike.
