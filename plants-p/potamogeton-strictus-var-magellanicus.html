Potamogeton strictus var. magellanicus

Potamogeton strictus var. magellanicus, commonly known as the Magellanic pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to the southern regions of South America.

It has long, slender, ribbon-like leaves that are typically dark green and arranged in whorls around the stem. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often submerged, followed by elongated, cylindrical fruits. It thrives in freshwater environments such as lakes, rivers, and marshes, often forming dense underwater vegetation.

This species plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
