Prunus conadenia

Prunus conadenia, commonly known as the desert almond, is a small, deciduous shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, multi-stemmed growth habit and small, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, white to pale pink flowers in early spring, followed by small, edible drupes that turn from green to red as they mature. Prunus conadenia is adapted to harsh desert conditions, with deep root systems that allow it to survive in low-water environments.

Its botanical features make it an important plant for ecological studies and a symbol of resilience in arid landscapes.
