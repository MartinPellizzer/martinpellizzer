Psoralea azurea

Psoralea azurea, commonly known as blue psoralea, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky outcrops. Its small, compound leaves are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Psoralea azurea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color.
