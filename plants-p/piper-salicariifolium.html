Piper salicariifolium

Piper salicariifolium, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a member of the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces small, yellowish-green fruits that are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in humid environments.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
