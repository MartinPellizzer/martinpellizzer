Piptanthocereus huilunchu

Piptanthocereus huilunchu, commonly known as the Huilunchu cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems that are covered in dense, white spines, which help reduce water loss and protect against herbivores. The plant produces large, showy flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color, blooming during the warmer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it well adapted to desert environments.
