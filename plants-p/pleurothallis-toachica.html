Pleurothallis toachica

Pleurothallis toachica, commonly known as the Toachica orchid, is a species native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a small, epiphytic orchid that thrives in humid, shaded forest environments. The plant features a compact growth habit with narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces delicate, fragrant flowers in shades of white and yellow. Its flowers are typically clustered in inflorescences and bloom during the warmer months.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and orchid enthusiasts alike.
