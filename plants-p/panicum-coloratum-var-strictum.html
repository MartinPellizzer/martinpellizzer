Panicum coloratum var. strictum

Panicum coloratum var. strictum, commonly known as the rigid bristlegrass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and dense, spike-like inflorescences that are often tinged with red or purple hues. The plant typically thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it suitable for dry or semi-arid environments. Its foliage is narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green coloration that adds visual interest to landscapes.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in native plant gardens and erosion control projects.
