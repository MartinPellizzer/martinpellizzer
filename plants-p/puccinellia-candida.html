Puccinellia candida

Puccinellia candida, commonly known as white needlegrass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in wet meadows, marshes, and saline soils, showcasing its adaptability to moist environments. The plant features slender, erect stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine white hairs. Its small, spike-like inflorescence produces tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

Puccinellia candida plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity in its native habitats.
