Populus bisattenuata

Populus bisattenuata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved poplar, is a deciduous tree native to central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, elongated leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in catkins, which are a key feature of its reproductive system. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in river valleys and moist soils.

Its wood is lightweight and has been used historically for various crafts and construction purposes.
