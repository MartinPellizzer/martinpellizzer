Phyllocactus phyllanthus var. boliviensis

Phyllocactus phyllanthus var. boliviensis, commonly known as the Bolivian phyllocactus, is a unique cactus species native to South America.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny stems that form a compact, globular shape. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers during the warmer months, adding a delicate touch to its otherwise rugged appearance. Its leaves are modified into spines, which help reduce water loss in arid environments.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and adaptability to indoor growing conditions.
