Paepalanthus argyrolinon

Paepalanthus argyrolinon, commonly known as the silver-haired pincushion cactus, is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its compact, spiny stems that resemble a pincushion, covered in dense, white, woolly hair. The plant produces yellow flowers during the dry season, which are clustered in inflorescences at the ends of the stems. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky soils.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
