Polygonum controversum

Polygonum controversum, commonly known as the narrowleaf smartweed, is a herbaceous annual plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, characterized by its distinctive arrow-shaped leaves and clusters of small, pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows in moist soils along stream banks, wetlands, and disturbed areas. Its stems are usually reddish and hairy, while its leaves are narrow and pointed.

It is often found in the eastern and central United States, where it plays a role in supporting local ecosystems.
