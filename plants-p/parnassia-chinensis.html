Parnassia chinensis

Parnassia chinensis, commonly known as the Chinese mountain buttercup, is a flowering plant native to Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive white, cup-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as mountain forests and stream sides. Its leaves are palmately divided, and the flowers often have a subtle fragrance.

Parnassia chinensis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
