Piper trichostylopse

Piper trichostylopse, commonly known as the narrow-leaved pepper plant, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and small, heart-shaped leaves. Its flowers are unisexual, with male and female flowers occurring on separate plants.

The fruit is a small, three-lobed berry that contains several seeds.
