Prunus serrulata f. virginalis

Prunus serrulata f. virginalis, commonly known as the Shidarezakura, is a rare form of the cherry blossom tree characterized by its double flowers.

It is native to Japan and is prized for its ornamental value, particularly during the spring blooming season. The plant produces clusters of white or pale pink flowers, which bloom before the leaves emerge, creating a striking visual display. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil and full sun for optimal growth.

This cultivar is often planted in gardens and parks for its aesthetic appeal and cultural significance.
