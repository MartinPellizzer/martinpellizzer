Pandanus usaramensis

Pandanus usaramensis, commonly known as the Usaramensis pandanus, is a tropical plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and long, narrow leaves that are typically arranged in a spiral pattern. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences that bear fragrant, yellowish flowers. Its leaves are often used in traditional crafts and thatching.

The species is adapted to coastal and sandy environments, making it resilient to salt spray and wind.
