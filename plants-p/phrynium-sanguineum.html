Phrynium sanguineum

Phrynium sanguineum, commonly known as the red-veined arrowleaf, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves with striking red veins against a dark green background. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Its foliage is also used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
