Physalis parviculea

Physalis parviculea, commonly known as the small-fruited groundcherry, is a species native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a herbaceous annual plant characterized by its hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green fruits enclosed in a papery, inflated calyx. Its flowers are typically white or pale green, with five petals and numerous stamens.

It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in disturbed soils and open habitats.
