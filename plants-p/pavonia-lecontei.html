Pavonia lecontei

Pavonia lecontei, commonly known as the Leconte's false mallow, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 3 feet. The plant produces showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are ovate to elliptical in shape, with a hairy texture, and it prefers well-drained soil in full sun.

Pavonia lecontei is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and open woodlands.
