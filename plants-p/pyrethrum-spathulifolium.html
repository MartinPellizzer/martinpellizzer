Pyrethrum spathulifolium

Pyrethrum spathulifolium, commonly known as the broad-leaved pyrethrum, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family and is known for its production of pyrethrins, which are natural insecticides. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in meadows and grasslands.

Due to its ornamental value and pesticidal properties, it is cultivated in gardens and for commercial use.
