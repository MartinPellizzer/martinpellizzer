Polygala eucosma

Polygala eucosma, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its showy, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that form a basal rosette.

Polygala eucosma is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
