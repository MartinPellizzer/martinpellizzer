Pilea anisophylla

Pilea anisophylla, commonly known as the Chinese money plant, is a species of flowering plant in the family Araceae.

It is native to China and is characterized by its round, fleshy leaves that resemble coins, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. It is a low-maintenance houseplant that thrives in indirect light and well-draining soil.

Pilea anisophylla is popular for its aesthetic appeal and air-purifying qualities.
