Passiflora viridescens

Passiflora viridescens, commonly known as the green passionflower, is a tropical vine native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, complex flowers that feature five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments. The plant produces edible berries that are typically green when mature, though they may turn yellow or red. Its leaves are heart-shaped and often have a waxy texture.

Passiflora viridescens is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported calming effects.
