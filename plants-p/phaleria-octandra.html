Phaleria octandra

Phaleria octandra, commonly known as the white-fruit tree, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and small, fragrant white flowers. The tree produces oval, smooth-skinned fruits that contain a single seed. Its leaves are aromatic and have been used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical gardens.
