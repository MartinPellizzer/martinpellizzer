Paepalanthus densifolius

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-forming leaves that are densely packed and often covered in fine, hair-like structures. The plant produces striking inflorescences with showy, brightly colored flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds. Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
