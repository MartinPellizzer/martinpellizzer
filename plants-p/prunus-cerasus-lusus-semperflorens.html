Prunus cerasus lusus semperflorens

Prunus cerasus lusus semperflorens, commonly known as the cherry blossom or sakura, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its small, sweet fruits and showy, pink or white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant belongs to the Rosaceae family and is closely related to other cherry species. Its flowers are typically produced in clusters and are highly valued for their aesthetic appeal in gardens and landscapes.

The leaves are oval-shaped with a smooth margin and turn vibrant shades of red or orange in the fall.
