Premna quadrifolia

Premna quadrifolia, commonly known as the four-leafed glory or Indian nettle, is a small evergreen shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its opposite, ovate to elliptic leaves that are often pubescent on the underside. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous tiny seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest undergrowth.

Its leaves are used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.
