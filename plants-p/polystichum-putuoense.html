Polystichum putuoense

Polystichum putuoense, commonly known as the Putuo fern, is a species of evergreen fern native to China.

It belongs to the family Dryopteridaceae and is characterized by its lanceolate, dark green fronds with a serrated margin. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky areas. Its spores are produced on the underside of the fronds in clusters called sori.

Polystichum putuoense is valued for its ornamental appearance and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
