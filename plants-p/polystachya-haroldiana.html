Polystachya haroldiana

Polystachya haroldiana, commonly known as the Harold's bamboo orchid, is a member of the orchid family (Orchidaceae) and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its clumping growth habit and long, slender stems that bear clusters of narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces inflorescences of small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late summer or early autumn. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Polystachya haroldiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in tropical and subtropical regions for horticultural purposes.
