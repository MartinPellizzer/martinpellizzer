Phaseolus coccineus subsp. formosus

Phaseolus coccineus subsp.

formosus, commonly known as the scarlet runner bean, is a tropical legume native to Central and South America. It is characterized by its striking red or orange flowers and elongated, edible pods.

The plant typically grows as a climbing vine, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are heart-shaped and compound, with three to five leaflets.

The subspecies formosus is distinguished by its more vibrant flower colors and slightly different growth habits compared to other Phaseolus species.
