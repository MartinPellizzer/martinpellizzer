Paepalanthus dichotomus var. glabrescens

Paepalanthus dichotomus var. glabrescens, commonly known as the silky puya, is a flowering plant native to the Andean region of South America.

It is characterized by its rosette growth habit and produces striking, spadix-like inflorescences that are often brightly colored. The plant has a short, sturdy stem and is adapted to high-altitude environments with cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are typically covered in a fine, silky layer of white or pale hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It is a member of the Bromeliaceae family and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
