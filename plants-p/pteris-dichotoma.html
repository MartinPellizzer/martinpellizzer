Pteris dichotoma

Pteris dichotoma, commonly known as the forked water fern, is a species of fern native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its distinctive fronds that split into two branches, giving it a unique, fern-like appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or near water sources. Its leaves are pinnately divided, with a light green color and a delicate, feathery texture.

Pteris dichotoma is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in humid conditions.
