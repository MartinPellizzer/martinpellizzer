Peperomia robusta

Peperomia robusta, commonly known as the velvet leaf peperomia, is a compact, evergreen perennial plant native to tropical regions of Central and South America.

It features thick, waxy, oval-shaped leaves that are typically dark green with a velvety texture, often adorned with reddish or purplish veins. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white or greenish flowers that appear in clusters, though it is primarily grown for its ornamental foliage rather than its blooms. It thrives in bright, indirect light and prefers well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Peperomia robusta is relatively low maintenance and can be propagated through stem cuttings or division.
