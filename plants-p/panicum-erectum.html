Panicum erectum

Panicum erectum, commonly known as tall panicgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its erect, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces fluffy, seed-bearing spikes that are often visible in late summer and early autumn. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it a common sight in open fields and along roadsides.

Due to its drought tolerance and ornamental appeal, it is sometimes used in landscaping and restoration projects.
