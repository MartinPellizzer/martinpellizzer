Pharbitis quinquefolia

Pharbitis quinquefolia, commonly known as morning glory, is a climbing vine native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and vibrant, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in the morning. The plant belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is known for its rapid growth habit. Its seeds are encased in a spiral-shaped pod, and the plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
