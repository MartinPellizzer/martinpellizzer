Pancratium recurvum

Pancratium recurvum, commonly known as the yellow rain lily, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of South America.

It is characterized by its striking yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late summer and early autumn. The plant features strap-like, narrow leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. Its bulbous root system allows it to store water and nutrients, aiding in its survival in tropical and subtropical climates.

Pancratium recurvum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
