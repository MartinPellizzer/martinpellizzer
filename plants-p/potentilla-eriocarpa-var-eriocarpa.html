Potentilla eriocarpa var. eriocarpa

Potentilla eriocarpa var. eriocarpa, commonly known as the woolly cinquefoil, is a perennial shrub native to North America.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that gives it a distinctive, soft appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its compact growth habit and long blooming period.
