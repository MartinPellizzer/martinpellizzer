Prasophyllum morrisii

Prasophyllum morrisii, commonly known as the Morris's leek orchid, is an endemic species native to the alpine regions of southeastern Australia.

It is a small, terrestrial orchid with a single, narrow leaf and a spike of pale green to white flowers. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in heathlands and open forests. Its flowers are typically arranged in a dense cluster and are pollinated by insects such as bees and flies.

Due to habitat loss and environmental changes, Prasophyllum morrisii is considered vulnerable and requires conservation efforts to ensure its survival.
