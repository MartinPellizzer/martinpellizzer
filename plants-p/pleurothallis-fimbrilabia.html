Pleurothallis fimbrilabia

Pleurothallis fimbrilabia, commonly known as the fringe-lipped pleurothallis, is a species of orchid native to the cloud forests of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its compact, epiphytic growth habit and its delicate, greenish-white flowers with hairy sepals. The plant typically grows on trees or rocks, thriving in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are usually narrow and leathery, adapted to retain moisture in its natural habitat.

Pleurothallis fimbrilabia is noted for its unique floral structure and is a subject of interest for orchid enthusiasts and botanists alike.
