Protea paludosa

Protea paludosa, commonly known as the marsh protea, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its unique, showy inflorescence that resembles a flower head composed of many small, petal-like bracts. The plant typically grows in wet, swampy areas and is adapted to moist, nutrient-poor soils. Its flowers are often pink or white and are pollinated by birds and insects.

Protea paludosa plays an important ecological role in its habitat by providing nectar and shelter for various wildlife species.
