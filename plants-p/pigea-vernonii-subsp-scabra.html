Pigea vernonii subsp. scabra

Pigea vernonii subsp.

scabra, commonly known as the rough-leaved pigeonwax, is a shrub native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its rough, scabrous leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and is typically found in coastal or sandy habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
