Pseudomariscus

Pseudomariscus, commonly known as the false marsh grass, is a genus of flowering plants belonging to the family Poaceae.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance and is often found in wetland or marshy environments. The plants typically have narrow, linear leaves and produce small, clustered inflorescences. Pseudomariscus species are adapted to moist soils and can thrive in areas with standing water.

They play a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
