Populus × fredroviensis

Populus × fredroviensis, commonly known as the hybrid poplar, is a deciduous tree that exhibits a range of botanical characteristics.

It is a natural hybrid between Populus tremula (aspen) and Populus alba (white poplar), resulting in a species with unique genetic diversity. The tree typically has smooth, gray bark and heart-shaped leaves that are often slightly serrated. It produces small, fluffy seeds that are dispersed by the wind, aiding in its spread.

Populus × fredroviensis is known for its rapid growth and adaptability to various soil types, making it a common ornamental and ecological plant.
