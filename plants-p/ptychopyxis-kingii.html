Ptychopyxis kingii

Ptychopyxis kingii, commonly known as the King's ptychopyxis, is a rare and endangered species native to the coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is a small, shrub-like plant with narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in a fine, white powdery coating. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and requires well-drained conditions to prevent root rot.

Due to habitat loss and limited distribution, conservation efforts are crucial for its survival.
