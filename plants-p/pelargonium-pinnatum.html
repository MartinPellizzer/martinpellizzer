Pelargonium pinnatum

Pelargonium pinnatum, commonly known as the African geranium, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and clusters of small, showy flowers that range in color from pink to red. The leaves are typically lobed or pinnately divided, giving the plant a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for hanging baskets and containers.

The plant is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
