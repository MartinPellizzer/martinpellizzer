Potamogeton × nitens f. intermedius

Potamogeton × nitens f. intermedius, commonly known as the intermediate pondweed, is a hybrid aquatic plant characterized by its morphological traits that blend features of its parent species.

It typically grows in freshwater environments such as lakes, ponds, and slow-moving streams, where it forms dense underwater vegetation. The plant has long, narrow leaves that are often arranged in whorls, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually submerged or floating. It plays a significant role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.

Due to its hybrid nature, it exhibits genetic variability, which can influence its adaptability to different environmental conditions.
