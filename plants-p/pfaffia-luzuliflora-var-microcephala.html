Pfaffia luzuliflora var. microcephala

Pfaffia luzuliflora var. microcephala, commonly known as the dwarf bluebush, is a perennial shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense foliage. The plant features small, clustered blue to purple flowers that bloom in the spring. Its leaves are typically blue-green in color, with a waxy coating that helps reduce water loss.

It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive appearance and drought tolerance.
