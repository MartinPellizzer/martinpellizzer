Polygala cuneata f. obovata

Polygala cuneata f. obovata, commonly known as the broad-leaf milkvetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the Polygalaceae family.

It is characterized by its obovate leaves that are often hairy and have a distinctive bluish-green hue. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in racemes, which bloom in early summer. It is typically found in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and delicate floral display.
