Pteronia trigona

Pteronia trigona, commonly known as the three-angled pteronia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, arranged in dense clusters. Its leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Pteronia trigona is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
