Pinanga limosa var. montana

Pinanga limosa var. montana, commonly known as the mountain pinanga, is a variant of the pinanga plant native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its clustered growth habit and slender, arching leaves. The leaves are typically elongated and have a waxy surface, which helps in reducing water loss in its native habitat. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories.

This variant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its unique foliage.
