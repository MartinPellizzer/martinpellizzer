Polygonum antihaemorrhoidale

Polygonum antihaemorrhoidale, commonly known as the hemorrhoid plant, is a herbaceous species belonging to the Polygonaceae family.

It is characterized by its upright stems and clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments and is native to parts of Asia. Its leaves are broad and heart-shaped, with a slightly hairy texture.

It is noted for its traditional use in herbal medicine to treat hemorrhoids and other inflammatory conditions.
