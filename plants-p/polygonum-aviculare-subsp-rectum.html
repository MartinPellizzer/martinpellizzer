Polygonum aviculare subsp. rectum

Polygonum aviculare subsp.

rectum, commonly known as the narrowleaf smartweed, is a low-growing herbaceous plant characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, clustered flowers. It typically has alternate, lance-shaped leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

The flowers are usually pink or red, arranged in dense, terminal spikes, and bloom during the summer months. This subspecies is often found in disturbed soils, such as roadsides, fields, and along streambanks.

It is a member of the Polygonaceae family, known for its distinctive bead-like fruits.
