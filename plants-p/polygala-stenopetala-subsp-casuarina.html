Polygala stenopetala subsp. casuarina

Polygala stenopetala subsp.

casuarina, commonly known as the Casuarina polygala, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Australia. It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, clustered flowers that typically bloom in spring.

The plant has narrow, linear leaves and produces distinctive, spike-like inflorescences. It is often found in coastal or sandy soils, thriving in well-drained environments.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
