Prunus × lannesiana f. longipedunculata

Prunus × lannesiana f. longipedunculata, commonly known as the long pedunculate cherry, is a unique form of the Prunus lannesiana species.

It is characterized by its elongated peduncles, which are the stalks supporting the flower clusters. This variety exhibits distinct floral morphology, with flowers that are often more showy and clustered than those of the typical species. Botanically, it is notable for its genetic diversity and is often found in hybrid forms.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated for its aesthetic qualities in gardens and landscapes.
