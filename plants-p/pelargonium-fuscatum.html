Pelargonium fuscatum

Pelargonium fuscatum, commonly known as the sandstone geranium, is a species of flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, mat-forming growth habit and gray-green leaves with a velvety texture. The plant produces showy, five-petaled flowers in shades of pink and purple, typically blooming in spring and summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Pelargonium fuscatum is also valued for its ability to tolerate drought and poor soil conditions.
