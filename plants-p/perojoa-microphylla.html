Perojoa microphylla

Perojoa microphylla, commonly known as the small-leaved perojoa, is a shrub native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and small, leathery leaves that are typically grayish-green in color. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by spherical, woody fruits. It is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments with minimal water availability.

Due to its unique adaptations, Perojoa microphylla is often used in xeriscaping and ecological restoration projects.
