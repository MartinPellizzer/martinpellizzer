Panicastrella capitata

Panicastrella capitata, commonly known as the clustered panic grass, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of Africa.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit. The plant features slender, cylindrical stems with tightly packed spikelets that give it a distinctive appearance. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often with a slight sheen.

Panicastrella capitata is typically found in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides and grasslands.
