Passiflora garckei subsp. pentaloba

Passiflora garckei subsp.

pentaloba, commonly known as the purple passionflower, is a species native to the tropical regions of Central and South America. It is characterized by its striking, five-lobed petals that are often a deep violet or purple color, giving it its common name.

The plant produces edible berries that are rich in antioxidants and have a sweet, tangy flavor. Its climbing habit involves tendrils that allow it to attach to supports, and it thrives in warm, humid climates with well-drained soil.

The flowers are also notable for their intricate structure, featuring a crown-like pattern of filaments around the reproductive organs.
