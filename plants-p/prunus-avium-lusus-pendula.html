Prunus avium lusus pendula

Prunus avium lusus pendula, commonly known as the weeping cherry tree, is a cultivar of the sweet cherry tree.

It is characterized by its cascading branches that create a distinctive, ornamental shape. The tree produces showy, pinkish-white flowers in early spring, followed by edible red cherries in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun for optimal growth.

This variety is popular in gardens for its aesthetic appeal and seasonal beauty.
