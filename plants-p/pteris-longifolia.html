Pteris longifolia

Pteris longifolia, commonly known as the long-leaf fern, is a species of fern native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that often have a feathery appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests or along stream banks. Its rhizome is creeping and produces both vegetative and reproductive structures.

Pteris longifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and indoor spaces.
