Pothomorphe peltata var. colombiana

Pothomorphe peltata var. colombiana, commonly known as the Colombian arrowleaf, is a strikingly ornamental plant native to the Andean regions of Colombia.

It features large, heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and deep green coloration, often adorned with silver or white veins. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or white, arranged in clusters. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in cloud forests.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is a sought-after species for botanical gardens and conservation efforts.
