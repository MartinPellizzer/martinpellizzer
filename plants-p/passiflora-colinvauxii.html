Passiflora colinvauxii

Passiflora colinvauxii, commonly known as the Colinvaux passionflower, is a species native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a climbing vine characterized by its striking, complex flowers with five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments. The plant produces blue or purple berries that are edible and contain a single seed. It thrives in humid, tropical environments and is often found in cloud forests.

Its unique floral structure and ornamental value make it a subject of interest in botanical gardens and conservation efforts.
