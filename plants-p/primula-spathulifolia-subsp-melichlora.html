Primula spathulifolia subsp. melichlora

Primula spathulifolia subsp.

melichlora, commonly known as the Melichloran primrose, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and rocky slopes.

The plant features lanceolate to spatulate leaves that are often covered in a fine, woolly pubescence. Its flowers are usually pale green to yellowish, with a distinct, showy corolla that attracts pollinators like bees and flies.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
