Potentilla anomalifolia

Potentilla anomalifolia, commonly known as the irregular-leaved cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of central Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, irregularly shaped leaves and clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its leaves are often covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in arid conditions.

Potentilla anomalifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and vibrant floral display.
