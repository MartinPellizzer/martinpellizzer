Papaver × harleyanum

Papaver × harleyanum, commonly known as the Harley hybrid poppy, is a botanical hybrid resulting from the cross between Papaver rhoeas (common poppy) and Papaver orientale (oriental poppy).

It is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that range in color from red to orange and yellow, often with a distinctive black or dark center. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring lance-shaped leaves that are slightly hairy. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.

The flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, adding a striking visual element to gardens and landscapes.
