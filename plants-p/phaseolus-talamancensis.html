Phaseolus talamancensis

Phaseolus talamancensis, commonly known as the Talamancan bean, is a species of flowering plant native to Central America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its climbing habit and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers followed by elongated, bean-like pods containing edible seeds. It is often grown for its nutritional value, as the seeds are rich in protein and essential nutrients.

Due to its adaptability and yield, it is a significant crop in regions with tropical and subtropical climates.
