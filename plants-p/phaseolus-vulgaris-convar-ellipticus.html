Phaseolus vulgaris convar. ellipticus

Phaseolus vulgaris convar. ellipticus, commonly known as the kidney bean.

This variety is characterized by its elongated, kidney-shaped seeds, which are typically smooth and have a distinctive mottled or speckled appearance. The plant is an annual legume with compound leaves and produces small, yellow flowers that develop into pods containing the edible seeds. It thrives in warm climates and is cultivated for its high protein content and nutritional value.

The ellipticus convariation is widely used in both culinary and agricultural contexts.
