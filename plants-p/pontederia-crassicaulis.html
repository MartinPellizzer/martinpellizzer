Pontederia crassicaulis

Pontederia crassicaulis, commonly known as pickerelweed, is a herbaceous perennial native to wetland environments in the Americas.

It features striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in dense spikes, typically from late spring to early fall. The plant has broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating, aiding in water repellence. Its roots are fibrous and help stabilize soil in aquatic habitats.

Pickerelweed is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ability to thrive in shallow water and its ecological benefits.
