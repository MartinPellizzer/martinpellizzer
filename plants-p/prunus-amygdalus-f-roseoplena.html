Prunus amygdalus f. roseoplena

Prunus amygdalus f. roseoplena, commonly known as the Persian almond or roseoplena almond, is a cultivar of the almond tree known for its ornamental value.

It is characterized by its distinctive pinkish-white flowers that bloom in early spring, adding a striking visual appeal to gardens. The plant is a deciduous shrub or small tree, typically reaching heights of 2 to 5 meters. Its leaves are simple, ovate, and have a slightly serrated margin, while the fruit is a drupe with a hard shell enclosing the edible seed.

This variety is valued for both its aesthetic beauty and its potential for nut production.
