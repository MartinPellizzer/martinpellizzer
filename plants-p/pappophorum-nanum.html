Pappophorum nanum

Pappophorum nanum, commonly known as the dwarf grass or small-headed grass, is a small, herbaceous plant native to arid and semi-arid regions.

It typically grows to a height of less than 10 centimeters, with slender, tufted stems and tiny, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, pale green or yellowish flowers in dense clusters, which are often surrounded by bracts. Its seeds are enclosed in a pappus, a feathery structure that aids in wind dispersal.

Pappophorum nanum is adapted to survive in harsh environments with low water availability and high temperatures.
