Pentanisia prunelloides var. latifolia

Pentanisia prunelloides var. latifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved pentanisia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in clusters, which are usually white or pale yellow in color. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and private collections.
