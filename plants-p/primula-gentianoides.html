Primula gentianoides

Primula gentianoides, commonly known as the gentian-like primrose, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers, which are typically tubular and arranged in clusters. The plant belongs to the Primulaceae family and is known for its ability to thrive in cool, moist environments. Its leaves are typically oval-shaped with a slightly hairy surface, and it produces showy blooms in late spring to early summer.

Primula gentianoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant colors.
