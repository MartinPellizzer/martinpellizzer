Plectranthus emanueli

Plectranthus emanueli, commonly known as the Emanuel's mint, is a species of flowering plant in the family Lamiaceae.

It is native to South Africa and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and spikes of small, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or herb, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its leaves are often hairy and have a strong mint-like scent, which is a defining feature of the genus Plectranthus.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fragrance.
