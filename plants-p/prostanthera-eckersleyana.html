Prostanthera eckersleyana

Prostanthera eckersleyana, commonly known as the Grey Leaved Mint Bush, is a shrub native to southern Australia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant features narrow, grey-green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pale blue or violet, arranged in dense spikes.

Prostanthera eckersleyana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in native gardens due to its hardiness and ability to attract pollinators.
