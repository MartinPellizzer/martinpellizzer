Pleurothallis nelsonii

Pleurothallis nelsonii, commonly known as the Nelson's orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its small, intricate flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its flowers are usually pale green to white in color, with a distinctive lip that is often marked with purple or red streaks.

Pleurothallis nelsonii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated by orchid enthusiasts for its unique and delicate appearance.
