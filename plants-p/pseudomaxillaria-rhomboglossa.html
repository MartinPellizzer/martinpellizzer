Pseudomaxillaria rhomboglossa

Pseudomaxillaria rhomboglossa, commonly known as the rhomboid-tongued orchid, is a species of orchid native to South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique rhomboid-shaped lip. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, often in cloud forests. Its flowers are small and clustered, with a distinctive coloration that ranges from white to pale pink.

Pseudomaxillaria rhomboglossa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
