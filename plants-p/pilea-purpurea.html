Pilea purpurea

Pilea purpurea, commonly known as the Chinese money plant, is a species of flowering plant in the family Araceae.

It is native to China and is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that are often tinged with purple or reddish hues. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers that are followed by bright red berries. It thrives in bright, indirect light and prefers well-draining soil.

Pilea purpurea is popular as a houseplant due to its attractive foliage and ease of care.
