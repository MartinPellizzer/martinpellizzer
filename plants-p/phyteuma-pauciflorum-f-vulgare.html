Phyteuma pauciflorum f. vulgare

Phyteuma pauciflorum f. vulgare, commonly known as the common phyteuma, is a flowering plant belonging to the Campanulaceae family.

It is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that typically range in color from pale blue to violet. The plant is native to southern Europe and is often found in grasslands and woodland edges. It produces small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds.

The foliage consists of basal leaves that are lance-shaped and often have a hairy texture.
