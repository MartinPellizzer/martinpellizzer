Phyllanthus peninsularis subsp. novogalicianus

Phyllanthus peninsularis subsp.

novogalicianus, commonly known as the Galician phyllanthus, is a shrub native to the Iberian Peninsula. It typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and is adapted to Mediterranean climates.

The plant has small, ovate leaves with a leathery texture and produces small yellow flowers. Its fruit is a capsule containing several tiny seeds.

It is often found in dry, open habitats and is of interest to botanists due to its limited distribution and unique ecological adaptations.
