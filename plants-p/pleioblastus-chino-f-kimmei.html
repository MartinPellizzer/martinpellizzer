Pleioblastus chino f. kimmei

Pleioblastus chino f. kimmei, commonly known as the Kimmei bamboo, is a compact ornamental grass native to China.

It features slender, arching culms that are often a striking silver-green color, especially in the winter months. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in early spring, followed by fluffy, white seed heads that add visual interest. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers well-drained soil.

This variety is prized for its elegant appearance and low maintenance requirements, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
