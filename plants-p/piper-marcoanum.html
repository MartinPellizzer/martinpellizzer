Piper marcoanum

Piper marcoanum, commonly known as Marco's pepper, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its climbing habit, with opposite, ovate leaves that have a glossy surface. Its small, greenish-white flowers are borne in clusters and are followed by elongated, slender berries.

Piper marcoanum is primarily valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
