Panicum deltoideum

Panicum deltoideum, commonly known as delta panicgrass, is a warm-season grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, bluish-green leaves. The plant produces fluffy, whitish to grayish seed heads that are often mistaken for dandelion fluff. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it a common sight in disturbed areas and along roadsides.

Due to its ornamental value and ability to stabilize soil, it is sometimes used in landscaping and erosion control.
