Potentilla myrioclada

Potentilla myrioclada, commonly known as the many-leafed cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit and small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has finely divided, feathery leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, arid environments.

Potentilla myrioclada is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
