Pelargonium × robustum

Pelargonium × robustum, commonly known as the African hybrid geranium, is a species native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant has serrated, velvety leaves that are often a deep green color. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is drought-tolerant once established.

Pelargonium × robustum is widely cultivated as an ornamental plant for its attractive foliage and flowers.
