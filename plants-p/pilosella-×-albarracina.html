Pilosella × albarracina

Pilosella × albarracina, commonly known as the alpine everlasting, is a hybrid plant in the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit and produces small, yellowish to white composite flowers. The plant is native to the mountainous regions of Europe and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats. Its foliage consists of narrow, hairy leaves that help reduce water loss in high-altitude environments.

Pilosella × albarracina is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and prolonged flowering period.
