Piper buchtienii var. charopampanum

Piper buchtienii var. charopampanum, commonly known as the Charopampanum pepper plant, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a climbing vine with dark green, heart-shaped leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces tiny, peppery fruits that are used in local cuisines for their sharp, spicy flavor. Botanically, it belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species.

Its morphology and chemical composition are similar to other Piper species, contributing to its culinary and medicinal uses.
