Pentapetes melanoxylon

Pentapetes melanoxylon, commonly known as blackwood, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Rhamnaceae and is characterized by its dark, dense wood and small, glossy leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by small, round berries. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in lowland forests.

Due to its hardy nature and ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and for reforestation purposes.
