Palmoglossum crassifolium

Palmoglossum crassifolium, commonly known as the broadleaf spurge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States.

It features a rosette of thick, fleshy, and often blue-green leaves that store water, aiding in its survival in dry environments. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in clusters during the spring and summer months. Its leaves are typically broad and have a waxy coating, which reduces water loss.

It is often found in rocky soils and is adapted to thrive in low-nutrient conditions.
