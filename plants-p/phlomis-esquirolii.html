Phlomis esquirolii

Phlomis esquirolii, commonly known as the Esquiroli's mullein, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It belongs to the family Lamiaceae, which includes many aromatic plants. The plant features tall, erect stems covered in soft, grayish hairs, and lance-shaped leaves that are also hairy. Its showy, yellowish-white flowers are clustered in spikes and bloom in late summer.

Phlomis esquirolii is adapted to rocky and dry habitats, making it a hardy species suitable for xeriscaping.
