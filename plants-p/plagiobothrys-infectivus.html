Plagiobothrys infectivus

Plagiobothrys infectivus, commonly known as the infectious bladderpod, is a member of the Brassicaceae family.

It is a small annual herb that typically grows in dry, disturbed soils across the western United States. The plant features hairy stems and small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a segmented silique, which splits open to release tiny seeds.
