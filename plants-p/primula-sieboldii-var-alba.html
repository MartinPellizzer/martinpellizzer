Primula sieboldii var. alba

Primula sieboldii var. alba, commonly known as the white primrose, is a flowering plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its showy, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and rock gardens. Its leaves are oval-shaped with a slightly hairy texture, and it produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small seeds.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
