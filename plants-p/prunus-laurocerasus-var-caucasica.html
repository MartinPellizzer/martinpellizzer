Prunus laurocerasus var. caucasica

Prunus laurocerasus var. caucasica, commonly known as the Caucasian cherry or Prunus laurocerasus 'Caucasica', is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Caucasus region.

It features glossy, dark green leaves that turn reddish in autumn, and it produces clusters of small, white flowers in spring. The plant is characterized by its aromatic, dark purple berries that are edible but often used in jams and preserves. It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various soil types.

This variety is closely related to the common laurel cherry and is often cultivated for its aesthetic and ecological benefits.
