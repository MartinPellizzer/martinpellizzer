Phlox caespitosa

Phlox caespitosa, commonly known as the tufted phlox, is a flowering plant native to eastern North America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, woodland environments. The plant features upright, grass-like foliage and produces clusters of showy, tubular flowers in shades of pink, red, or white. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Phlox caespitosa is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in partial shade.
