Pandanus malgassicus

Pandanus malgassicus, commonly known as the Madagascar pandanus, is a species of flowering plant in the pandan family.

It is native to Madagascar and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and long, narrow leaves. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences with yellowish flowers, which are followed by triangular, fibrous fruits. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in coastal or lowland areas.

The plant has traditional uses in local crafts and may have ecological significance in its native habitat.
