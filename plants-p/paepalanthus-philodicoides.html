Paepalanthus philodicoides

Paepalanthus philodicoides, commonly known as the golden cup plant, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Argentina and Chile.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its distinctive golden-yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant features a tall, branching stem with narrow, grass-like leaves, and it produces small, clustered flower heads. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
