Phlomis setifera

Phlomis setifera, commonly known as slender torchgrass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features tall, erect stems that can grow up to 1.5 meters in height, covered with fine, silky hairs. The plant produces dense clusters of small, yellowish flowers during the summer months, which are arranged in spike-like inflorescences. Its leaves are lance-shaped, often with a rough texture and a bluish-green coloration.

Phlomis setifera is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it a hardy plant suitable for rocky or dry garden environments.
