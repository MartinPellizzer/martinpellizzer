Persea borbonia subsp. humilis

Persea borbonia subsp.

humilis, commonly known as the dwarf avocado or lesser avocado, is a small evergreen tree native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America. It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its smooth, dark green leaves and fragrant flowers.

The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically less than 2 inches in diameter. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible fruit.

The subspecies is distinguished by its shorter stature compared to the typical P. borbonia.
