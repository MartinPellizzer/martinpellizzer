Paspalum fournierianum

Paspalum fournierianum, commonly known as the silvergrass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-gray foliage and compact growth habit, making it a popular ornamental plant. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typical of the Poaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in dry, open habitats.

Its drought tolerance and low maintenance requirements make it suitable for use in landscaping and erosion control.
