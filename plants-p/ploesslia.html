Ploesslia

Ploesslia, commonly known as the dwarf rock cress, is a small flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its low-growing habit, with compact rosettes of dark green, hairy leaves. The plant produces small, white or yellowish flowers with four petals, typically blooming in early spring. Ploesslia is often found in rocky or sandy soils in arctic and alpine regions.

It is a hardy species adapted to cold climates and is sometimes used in rock gardens.
