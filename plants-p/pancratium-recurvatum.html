Pancratium recurvatum

Pancratium recurvatum, commonly known as the yellow torch lily, is a striking flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, narrow leaves and its showy, yellow-orange flowers that resemble torches. The plant produces a distinctive, hollow, yellow flower spike that can reach up to 2 meters in height. It blooms during the summer months and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Pancratium recurvatum is also known for its medicinal properties, with various parts of the plant used in traditional medicine.
