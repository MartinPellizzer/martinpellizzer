Paphiopedilum insigne var. maximum

Paphiopedilum insigne var. maximum, commonly known as the Giant Paphiopedilum, is a striking orchid native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

This variant is distinguished by its large, showy flowers that can reach up to 12 inches in diameter, featuring a velvety purple and white coloration. The plant has a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, enabling it to thrive in humid, shaded environments. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb moisture from the surrounding soil.

It is prized by orchid enthusiasts for its ornamental value and unique floral structure.
