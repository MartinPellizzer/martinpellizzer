Pancratium herbertianum

Pancratium herbertianum, commonly known as Herbert's torchflower, is a striking bulbous plant native to southern Africa.

It features a tall, slender stem that bears clusters of bright yellow, torch-like flowers. The plant has strap-shaped, evergreen leaves that are often used in horticultural displays. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for coastal gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long-lasting blooms, which appear in late summer to early autumn.
