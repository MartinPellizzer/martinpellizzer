Pinanga tenella

Pinanga tenella, commonly known as the dwarf bamboo or miniature bamboo, is a species of flowering plant in the family Polypodiaceae.

It is a small, evergreen fern that typically grows to a height of around 30 cm. Native to Southeast Asia, it thrives in moist, shaded environments such as tropical rainforests. The plant has slender, wiry stems that form dense clumps, and its leaves are elongated with a smooth, glossy surface.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
