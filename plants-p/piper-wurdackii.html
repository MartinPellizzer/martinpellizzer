Piper wurdackii

Piper wurdackii, commonly known as the Wurdack's pepper or the Maluku pepper, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to the Maluku Islands in Indonesia and is characterized by its small, dark red berries that are used in traditional medicine. The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges and produces small, greenish-white flowers. It thrives in tropical climates with high humidity and is often cultivated for its medicinal properties.

The berries contain various bioactive compounds that have been studied for their potential health benefits.
