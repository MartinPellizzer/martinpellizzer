Papaver radicatum subsp. dahlianum

Papaver radicatum subsp.

dahlianum, commonly known as the Dahlian poppy, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region. It features striking, showy flowers with bright orange petals and a prominent black center, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant has a basal rosette of lance-shaped leaves that are covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. It produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

This species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
