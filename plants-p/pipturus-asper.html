Pipturus asper

Pipturus asper, commonly known as the roughleaf buckthorn, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as stream banks and woodlands. The plant has compound leaves that are usually 5 to 9 pairs of leaflets, which are elliptic to ovate in shape. Its small, greenish-yellow flowers appear in clusters and are followed by dark blue berries that are edible for birds.

The plant is characterized by its rough, textured bark and its ability to thrive in shaded environments.
