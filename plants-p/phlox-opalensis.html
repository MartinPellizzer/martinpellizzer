Phlox opalensis

Phlox opalensis, commonly known as the Opal phlox, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its compact, mounding growth habit and its showy clusters of pink to purple flowers. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Its ornamental value and drought tolerance contribute to its popularity among gardeners in arid regions.
