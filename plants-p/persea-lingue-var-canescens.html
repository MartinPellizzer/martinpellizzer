Persea lingue var. canescens

Persea lingue var. canescens, commonly known as the hairy spondias, is a variant of the spondias species native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, pale green leaves and small, yellowish-green fruits that are typically edible. The plant thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in lowland forests. Its botanical features include a short, smooth trunk and clusters of small, white flowers.

This variant is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential medicinal properties.
