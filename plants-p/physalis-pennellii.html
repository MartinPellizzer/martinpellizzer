Physalis pennellii

Physalis pennellii, commonly known as the Cape gooseberry, is a species of flowering plant in the nightshade family, Solanaceae.

It is native to South America and is characterized by its distinctive papery calyx that encloses the fruit. The fruit is a small, yellow-orange berry that turns red when ripe, often resembling a tiny lantern. The plant produces showy yellow flowers that develop into the characteristic fruit.

It is cultivated for its edible fruit, which has a tart, tangy flavor.
