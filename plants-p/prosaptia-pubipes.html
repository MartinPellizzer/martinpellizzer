Prosaptia pubipes

Prosaptia pubipes, commonly known as the hairy legume, is a flowering plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive hairy stems and leaves, which give it its common name. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It is typically found in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments.

Prosaptia pubipes plays a role in nitrogen fixation, contributing to soil fertility in its native range.
