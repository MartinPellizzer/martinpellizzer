Pentagonia bonifaziana

Pentagonia bonifaziana, commonly known as the golden trumpet tree, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has a woody stem with a distinctive pentagonal cross-section, giving it its genus name. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a velvety texture.

Pentagonia bonifaziana thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
