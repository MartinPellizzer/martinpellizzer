Phragmipedium caudatum var. wallisii

Phragmipedium caudatum var. wallisii, commonly known as the Wallisiana phragmipedium, is a species of orchid native to the cloud forests of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its pendulous, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of yellow and orange, with a distinctive red or orange lip. The plant has strap-like leaves that are often dark green and glossy, and it thrives in humid, shaded environments. It is a terrestrial orchid that prefers well-drained, acidic soils and requires consistent moisture.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
