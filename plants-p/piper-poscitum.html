Piper poscitum

Piper poscitum, commonly known as the "Mexican pepper vine," is a tropical climbing plant native to Central America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its long, trailing stems and small, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers followed by small, peppery fruits. It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardeners seeking a lush, cascading plant.
