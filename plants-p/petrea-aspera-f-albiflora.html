Petrea aspera f. albiflora

Petrea aspera f. albiflora, commonly known as the white-flowered pittosporum, is a hybrid form of the pittosporum genus native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and clusters of white, fragrant flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has a compact, rounded shape and is often used as an ornamental shrub in gardens. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its attractive appearance and hardy nature, it is popular in coastal and temperate climates.
