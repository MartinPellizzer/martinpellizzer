Picea elegantissima

Picea elegantissima, commonly known as the white spruce, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to the northern regions of North America.

It is characterized by its slender, pyramidal shape and dark green, needle-like leaves that are coated with a whitish bloom, giving it a distinctive appearance. The tree produces small, cone-like structures that contain the seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in cool, moist climates and is often found in boreal forests and mountainous areas.

Picea elegantissima is valued for its timber and its role in ecosystem stability.
