Pertya rigidula

Pertya rigidula, commonly known as the rigid thornbush, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub characterized by its dense, thorny branches and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces yellow or orange flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. Its leaves are usually small, oval, and covered in a fine layer of hair.

Pertya rigidula is adapted to arid environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.
