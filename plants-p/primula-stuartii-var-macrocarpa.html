Primula stuartii var. macrocarpa

Primula stuartii var. macrocarpa, commonly known as the large-fruited Stuart primrose, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features erect stems that bear opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves with a hairy texture. The plant produces clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, or purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its flowers are tubular and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant prefers well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats.
