Pogonarthria tuberculata

Pogonarthria tuberculata, commonly known as the hairy leaf bluemink, is a perennial grass native to Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves with a distinctive hairy texture. The plant produces small, pale blue flowers arranged in dense clusters, which are typical of the Poaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands and disturbed areas.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
