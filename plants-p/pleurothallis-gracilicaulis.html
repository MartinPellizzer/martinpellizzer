Pleurothallis gracilicaulis

Pleurothallis gracilicaulis, commonly known as the slender-stemmed orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, elongated stems and small, greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded forest environments. Its flowers are often short-lived but attract various pollinators such as bees and moths.

Due to its unique appearance and growth habits, it is a sought-after species among orchid enthusiasts.
