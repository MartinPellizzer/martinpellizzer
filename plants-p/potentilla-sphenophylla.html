Potentilla sphenophylla

Potentilla sphenophylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaved cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its small, upright growth habit and bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The leaves are narrow and serrated, typically arranged in a spiral pattern along the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in alpine and subalpine environments.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and vibrant floral display.
