Pinanga ternatensis var. papuana

Pinanga ternatensis var. papuana, commonly known as the Papuan bamboo palm, is a species of flowering plant in the Arecaceae family.

It is native to the lowland and montane rainforests of New Guinea and nearby islands. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender stems and pinnately compound leaves that are often used in traditional crafts. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in the understory of dense tropical forests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is of interest to both botanists and conservationists.
