Piptadenia africana

Piptadenia africana, commonly known as African locust bean, is a leguminous tree native to Africa.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow or orange flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces elongated pods containing edible seeds, which are used in traditional cuisine and medicine. It has a deep root system that helps prevent soil erosion and improves soil fertility.

Piptadenia africana is also valued for its timber and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.
