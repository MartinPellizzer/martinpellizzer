Pinus pseudostrobus subsp. protuberans

Pinus pseudostrobus subsp.

protuberans, commonly known as the Mexican bristle pine, is a subspecies native to Mexico and parts of Central America. It is a coniferous evergreen tree that typically grows in tropical and subtropical forests.

The plant features needle-like leaves that are arranged in fascicles of five, with a dark green color and a slightly resinous coating. Its cones are woody and contain seeds that are dispersed by wind.

The tree is valued for its hard, durable wood and plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat for various wildlife species.
