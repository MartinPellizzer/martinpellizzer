Pleurothallis krahnii

Pleurothallis krahnii, commonly known as the Krahn's orchid, is a species native to the cloud forests of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its small, intricate flowers that often display vibrant colors. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees and deriving moisture and nutrients from the air. Its leaves are usually narrow and strap-like, adapted to the humid, shaded environments it inhabits.

Pleurothallis krahnii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
