Passiflora foetida var. quinqueloba

Passiflora foetida var. quinqueloba, commonly known as the five-lobed passionflower, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, complex flowers that feature five prominent lobes on the corona. The plant produces edible berries that are often used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are palmately lobed and can be quite large, contributing to its ornamental value.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal and ecological benefits.
