Pleurothallis choerorhyncha

Pleurothallis choerorhyncha, commonly known as the pink-veined pleurothallis, is a species of orchid native to the cloud forests of Central and South America.

It is a small, terrestrial orchid with a short, wiry rhizome and a single, narrow leaf. The plant produces a single, elongated inflorescence bearing several small, white to pink flowers with distinctive veining. It thrives in cool, humid environments and requires well-drained, acidic soil.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is a sought-after species among orchid enthusiasts.
