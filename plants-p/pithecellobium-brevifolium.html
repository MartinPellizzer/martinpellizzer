Pithecellobium brevifolium

Pithecellobium brevifolium, commonly known as the dwarf pita, is a small evergreen shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, leathery foliage and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces edible, sweet, and fleshy pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires a warm, humid climate.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruit, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and for local consumption.
