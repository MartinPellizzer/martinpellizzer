Polygala subopposita

Polygala subopposita, commonly known as the narrow-leaved milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in sunny environments. The plant features small, usually pink or white flowers arranged in racemes, which bloom from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and opposite, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Polygala subopposita is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
