Pleurothallis galerita

Pleurothallis galerita, commonly known as the green-tipped orchid, is a species native to the cloud forests of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that typically bloom in shades of green and white. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot. Its leaves are usually narrow and grass-like, contributing to its overall appearance.

Pleurothallis galerita is prized by orchid enthusiasts for its unique coloration and relatively easy care requirements.
