Pelexia tomentosa

Pelexia tomentosa, commonly known as the woolly pelexia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that covers its stems and leaves, giving it a distinctive, soft appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in spring, adding a subtle ornamental value. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a container plant.

Due to its unique texture and low maintenance requirements, it is a popular choice among gardeners seeking drought-tolerant plants.
