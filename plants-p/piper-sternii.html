Piper sternii

Piper sternii, commonly known as the mountain pepper plant, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of Central and South America, particularly in the Andean foothills. The plant is characterized by its small, green, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of tiny, white flowers. It produces small, peppery fruits that are edible and have a mild, spicy flavor.

Piper sternii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and its potential medicinal properties.
