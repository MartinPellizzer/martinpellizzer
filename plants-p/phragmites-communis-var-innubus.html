Phragmites communis var. innubus

Phragmites communis var. innubus, commonly known as the common reed, is a tall, perennial grass that thrives in wetland environments.

It is characterized by its hollow, jointed stems and dense clusters of narrow, sword-shaped leaves. This plant plays a vital role in stabilizing shorelines and filtering water due to its extensive root system. It is often found in marshes, along lake shores, and in brackish tidal areas.

The plant produces fluffy, feathery seeds that are dispersed by the wind, aiding in its wide distribution.
