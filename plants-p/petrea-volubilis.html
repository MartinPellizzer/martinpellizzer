Petrea volubilis

Petrea volubilis, commonly known as the Brazilian jasmine or sweet pepper vine, is a climbing shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its trailing stems and fragrant, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, round, red berries that are edible and often used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Petrea volubilis thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, making it a popular ornamental plant in warm regions.
