Pelargonium × comptoniae

Pelargonium × comptoniae, commonly known as the Compton's geranium, is a hybrid pelargonium species native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense foliage, often featuring variegated leaves with a mix of green, white, and silver tones. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink, red, or white, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is widely cultivated in gardens across temperate regions.
