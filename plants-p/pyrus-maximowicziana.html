Pyrus maximowicziana

Pyrus maximowicziana, commonly known as the Korean pear or Japanese white pear, is a species of deciduous tree native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves and showy white flowers that appear in early spring. The tree produces small, sweet, yellow or green fruits that are edible and often used in culinary applications.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
