Polygala kalaboensis

Polygala kalaboensis, commonly known as the Kalabo polygala, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its delicate, often branched inflorescences. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. Its flowers are usually small and colorful, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
