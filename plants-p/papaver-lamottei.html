Papaver lamottei

Papaver lamottei, commonly known as the Lamotte poppy, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features striking, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from deep red to orange, often with a distinctive black center. The plant has finely dissected, fern-like leaves that are typically hairy and covered in a waxy cuticle. It blooms in late spring to early summer, producing capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Papaver lamottei is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant and unique floral display.
