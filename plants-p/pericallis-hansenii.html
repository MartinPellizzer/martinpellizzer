Pericallis hansenii

Pericallis hansenii, commonly known as the Hansen's pericallis, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its succulent, spiny stems and vibrant yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its flowers are tubular and often attract pollinators such as bees and moths.

Pericallis hansenii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in botanical gardens and by succulent enthusiasts.
