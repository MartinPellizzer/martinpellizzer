Pandanus parvicentralis

Pandanus parvicentralis, commonly known as the small-veined pandanus, is a tropical plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It is a member of the Pandanaceae family and is characterized by its pinnate leaves, which are often used in traditional crafts and cooking. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and thrives in humid, saline environments. Its fibrous leaves are harvested for their durability and aesthetic appeal.

Pandanus parvicentralis plays an important ecological role in stabilizing coastal soils and providing habitat for various species.
