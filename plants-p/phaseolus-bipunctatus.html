Phaseolus bipunctatus

Phaseolus bipunctatus, commonly known as the scarlet runner bean, is a member of the legume family Fabaceae.

It is a climbing vine that produces edible pods containing kidney-shaped beans. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets and is native to the Americas. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

The flowers are typically bright yellow or orange, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
