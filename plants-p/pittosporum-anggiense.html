Pittosporum anggiense

Pittosporum anggiense, commonly known as the Java Pittosporum, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its evergreen foliage and fragrant white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are glossy and lance-shaped, while the bark is smooth and greyish in color.

Pittosporum anggiense is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in horticulture for landscaping purposes.
