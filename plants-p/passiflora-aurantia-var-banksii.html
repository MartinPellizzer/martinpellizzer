Passiflora aurantia var. banksii

Passiflora aurantia var. banksii, commonly known as the Banks' passionflower, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its showy, colorful flowers that feature a unique blend of red, yellow, and orange hues. The plant produces edible, orange-colored berries that are rich in vitamin C. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped with a rough texture and a strong, aromatic scent.

This variety is valued for both its ornamental beauty and its ecological role in supporting pollinators.
