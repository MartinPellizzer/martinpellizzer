Pentopetia urceolata

Pentopetia urceolata, commonly known as the urn-shaped bellflower, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive urn-shaped flowers that are typically blue or violet in color. The plant grows as a herbaceous perennial, often found in moist, shaded environments. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a rough texture.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
