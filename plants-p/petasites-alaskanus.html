Petasites alaskanus

Petasites alaskanus, commonly known as the Alaska buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the northern hemisphere.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can reach up to 30 cm in diameter. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and wetlands.

Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense colonies.
