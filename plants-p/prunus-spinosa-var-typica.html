Prunus spinosa var. typica

Prunus spinosa var. typica, commonly known as the blackthorn, is a deciduous shrub native to Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and small, dark blue berries that ripen in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and glossy on top. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in hedgerows and woodland edges.

The berries are used in making jams and wines, and the plant has historical uses in herbal medicine.
