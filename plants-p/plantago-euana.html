Plantago euana

Plantago euana, commonly known as the narrow-leaved plantain, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in a spike, which are typically borne on slender stalks. It thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils and is often found in meadows, along stream banks, and in disturbed areas.

Plantago euana is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies for various ailments.
