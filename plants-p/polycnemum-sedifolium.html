Polycnemum sedifolium

Polycnemum sedifolium, commonly known as the sedifolium nettle, is a flowering plant belonging to the Urticaceae family.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and slender, hairy stems. The leaves are alternate, often with a wavy margin and a distinctive serrated edge. It produces small, greenish flowers arranged in clusters, typically in late spring to early summer.

This plant is often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.
