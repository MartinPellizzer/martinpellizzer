Papaver alpinum subf. hispidum

Papaver alpinum subf.

hispidum, commonly known as the Alpine poppy, is a member of the Papaveraceae family and is characterized by its striking red or orange flowers. It is native to the mountainous regions of Europe and is adapted to cold, alpine environments.

The plant typically grows in rocky soils and has a low, spreading growth habit. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its rugged appearance.

The subspecies hispidum is distinguished by its dense, hairy foliage and its ability to thrive in high-altitude conditions.
