Podocarpus macrophyllus var. albovariegatus

Podocarpus macrophyllus var. albovariegatus, commonly known as the Variegated Podocarpus, is a slow-growing evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves with striking white or yellow variegation patterns. The plant produces small, cone-like structures that contain seeds, typical of the Podocarpaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping.

Its compact growth habit and attractive foliage make it suitable for hedges, topiaries, and indoor cultivation.
