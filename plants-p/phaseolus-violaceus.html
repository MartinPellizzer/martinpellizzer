Phaseolus violaceus

Phaseolus violaceus, commonly known as the purple bean or violaceus bean, is a leguminous plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking violet to purple flowers and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces elongated, bean-like pods that contain edible seeds used in various cuisines. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Its botanical features make it a valuable crop for both ornamental and agricultural purposes.
