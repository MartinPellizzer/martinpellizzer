Perityle megalocephala var. intricata

Perityle megalocephala var. intricata, commonly known as the intricately branched perityle, is a rare flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its complex, branching structure and dense clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers. The leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, with a slightly hairy texture. This plant thrives in cool, humid environments and is often found in shaded forest understories.

Its unique morphology and limited distribution make it a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists.
