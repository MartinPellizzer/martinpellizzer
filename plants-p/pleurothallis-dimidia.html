Pleurothallis dimidia

Pleurothallis dimidia, commonly known as the narrow-leafed orchid, is a species native to the cloud forests of Central and South America.

It is a small epiphytic orchid that thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant has slender, wiry stems and produces delicate, greenish-white flowers with a faintly fragrant scent. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, adapted to capture moisture from the air.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is a popular choice among orchid enthusiasts and botanists.
