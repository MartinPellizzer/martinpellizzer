Potentilla verna var. frigida

Potentilla verna var. frigida, commonly known as the alpine cinquefoil, is a low-growing perennial plant native to alpine and arctic regions.

It features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, typically from April to June. The plant has wiry, branched stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to cold climates.

This variety is valued for its hardiness and is often used in alpine gardens or as a ground cover in harsh environments.
