Pyrus shirinensis

Pyrus shirinensis, commonly known as the Persian whitebeam, is a deciduous tree native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, which are followed by edible, sweet-tart berries.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and seasonal interest.
