Piper artanthe

Piper artanthe, commonly known as the Mexican pepper plant, is a climbing shrub native to Central America and northern South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper plants. The plant produces small, green berries that turn red when mature, and they are often used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are aromatic and contain essential oils, contributing to its use in herbal remedies.

The plant thrives in humid, tropical environments and is often found in forest understories.
