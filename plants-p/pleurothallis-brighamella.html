Pleurothallis brighamella

Pleurothallis brighamella, commonly known as the Brigham's orchid, is a species of orchid native to the cloud forests of Central and South America.

It is a small, epiphytic plant that typically grows on trees or rocks in humid, shaded environments. The plant produces delicate, fragrant flowers that are usually white or pale yellow, with intricate patterns on the petals. It has a short, fleshy rhizome and fibrous roots that help it absorb moisture from the air.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in the wild.
