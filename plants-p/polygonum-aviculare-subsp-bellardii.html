Polygonum aviculare subsp. bellardii

Polygonum aviculare subsp.

bellardii, commonly known as the common knotgrass, is a small annual herbaceous plant belonging to the Polygonaceae family. It typically grows in disturbed soils and moist environments, such as roadsides and fields.

The plant has slender, branched stems and small, greenish-white flowers that appear in clusters. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a waxy coating.

It is characterized by its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its rapid growth habit.
