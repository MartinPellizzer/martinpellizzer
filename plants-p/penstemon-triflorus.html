Penstemon triflorus

Penstemon triflorus, commonly known as the three-flowered beardtongue, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and striking spikes of tubular flowers that range in color from purple to pink. The plant produces showy, two-lipped flowers that attract a variety of pollinators, including bees and hummingbirds. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its drought-tolerant nature.

Penstemon triflorus is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
