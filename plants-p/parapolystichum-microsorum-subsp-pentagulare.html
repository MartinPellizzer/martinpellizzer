Parapolystichum microsorum subsp. pentagulare

Parapolystichum microsorum subsp.

pentagulare, commonly known as the five-lobed cliff fern, is a fern species native to Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit and fronds that are typically leathery and dark green.

The fronds are pinnately compound, with lanceolate pinnae that have a distinct, serrated margin. This subspecies is often found growing on rocky outcrops or in shaded, moist environments.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ecologically significant plant in its native habitat.
