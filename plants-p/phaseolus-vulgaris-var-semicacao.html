Phaseolus vulgaris var. semicacao

Phaseolus vulgaris var. semicacao, commonly known as the black bean.

This variety is a legume native to the Americas, characterized by its dark, glossy seeds that are rich in protein and dietary fiber. The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm, producing pods that contain 3–5 dark-colored beans. It thrives in warm climates with well-drained soil and is often cultivated for both culinary and nutritional purposes.

The botanical features of this plant make it a valuable crop in sustainable agriculture systems.
