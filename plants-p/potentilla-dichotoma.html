Potentilla dichotoma

Potentilla dichotoma, commonly known as the creeping cinquefoil, is a low-growing perennial shrub native to arctic and alpine regions.

It features a mat-forming growth habit with multiple stems that spread horizontally along the ground. The plant produces small, yellow flowers with five petals, typically blooming in late summer. Its leaves are compound, consisting of five oval-shaped leaflets, and are covered in fine, soft hairs.

Potentilla dichotoma is well adapted to cold climates and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.
