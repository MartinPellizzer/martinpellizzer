Pleuroplitis quartiniana var. plumbea

Pleuroplitis quartiniana var. plumbea, commonly known as the plumbea sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, thriving in moist, well-drained soils. The plant features slender, grass-like leaves that are often bluish-gray in color, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its inflorescence consists of small, clustered spikes that produce tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
