Panicum crus-galli var. rohlenae

Panicum crus-galli var. rohlenae, commonly known as the rohlenae lovegrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, wiry stems that often form dense clumps. The leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green coloration that becomes more pronounced under drought conditions. Its inflorescence consists of a panicle made up of spikelets that are loosely arranged, giving it a delicate, feathery appearance.

This plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in harsh climates.
