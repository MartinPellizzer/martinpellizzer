Potentilla schrenkiana

Potentilla schrenkiana, commonly known as the Schrenk's cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in dry, rocky habitats. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. Its leaves are palmately divided into five lobes, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Potentilla schrenkiana is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
