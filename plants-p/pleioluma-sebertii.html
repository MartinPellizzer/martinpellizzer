Pleioluma sebertii

Pleioluma sebertii, commonly known as the Sebert's orchid, is a tropical plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of yellow and orange. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its roots are adapted for absorbing moisture and nutrients from the air and rain.

Pleioluma sebertii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and greenhouses.
