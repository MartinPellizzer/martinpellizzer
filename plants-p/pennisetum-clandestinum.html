Pennisetum clandestinum

Pennisetum clandestinum, commonly known as purple lovegrass, is a perennial grass native to Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and distinctive purple-tinted foliage. The plant produces small, fluffy seeds that are dispersed by wind, aiding in its spread across various environments. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and open fields.

Pennisetum clandestinum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in some regions for forage or erosion control.
