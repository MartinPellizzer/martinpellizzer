Primula clevelandii var. clevelandii

Primula clevelandii var. clevelandii, commonly known as the Cleveland primrose, is a flowering plant native to California.

It is a member of the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It produces a basal rosette of narrow, lance-shaped leaves and is often found in coastal scrub habitats.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
