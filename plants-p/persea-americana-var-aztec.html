Persea americana var. aztec

Persea americana var. aztec, commonly known as the Aztec avocado, is a variant of the common avocado native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its small, elongated fruit with a smooth, thin skin that turns from green to dark purple as it matures. The fruit has a rich, creamy texture and a mild, nutty flavor, making it popular in traditional Mexican cuisine. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, requiring well-drained soil and consistent moisture.

This variety is also valued for its ornamental appeal, with attractive evergreen foliage.
