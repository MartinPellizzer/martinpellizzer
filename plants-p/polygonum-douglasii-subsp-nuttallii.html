Polygonum douglasii subsp. nuttallii

Polygonum douglasii subsp.

nuttallii, commonly known as the Nuttall's smartweed, is a perennial herb native to the western United States. It belongs to the Polygonaceae family and is characterized by its upright stems and lanceolate leaves.

The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink to red in color. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in wet meadows and streambanks.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and wetland restoration efforts.
