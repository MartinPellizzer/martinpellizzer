Pandanus belepensis

Pandanus belepensis, commonly known as the Belep pandanus, is a species of palm native to the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and long, pinnate leaves that are often used in traditional crafts. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into fibrous, spiky fruits. Its leaves are fibrous and have been utilized for weaving and thatching in various island cultures.

The plant thrives in coastal and wetland environments, adapting well to saline and humid conditions.
