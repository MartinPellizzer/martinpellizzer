Parmena grayana

Parmena grayana, commonly known as the mountain heather, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas.

It is a shrub that typically grows in rocky and arid environments, exhibiting a compact growth habit. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are usually pink or purple in color, blooming in the spring. Its leaves are narrow and evergreen, adapted to withstand cold and dry conditions.

Parmena grayana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens in cooler climates.
