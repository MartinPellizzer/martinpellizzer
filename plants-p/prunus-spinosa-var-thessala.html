Prunus spinosa var. thessala

Prunus spinosa var. thessala, commonly known as the Thessalian blackthorn, is a shrub native to the eastern Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dark, almost black, small berries that are typically harvested in late summer. The plant has thorny branches and small, ovate leaves that are often covered in a fine pubescence. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or semi-arid environments.

The berries are used in traditional medicine and can be fermented into a type of wine.
