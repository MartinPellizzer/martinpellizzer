Pleurothallis dentifera

Pleurothallis dentifera, commonly known as the toothed orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that often have a unique, tooth-like appearance on the petals. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that help it conserve moisture in its natural habitat.

Pleurothallis dentifera is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts for its intricate floral structure.
