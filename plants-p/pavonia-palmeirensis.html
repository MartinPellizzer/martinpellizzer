Pavonia palmeirensis

Pavonia palmeirensis, commonly known as the Brazilian peacock flower, is a flowering plant native to Brazil.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its striking, showy inflorescences. The plant features palmately lobed leaves and produces clusters of pink to red flowers that resemble peacock feathers. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Pavonia palmeirensis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
