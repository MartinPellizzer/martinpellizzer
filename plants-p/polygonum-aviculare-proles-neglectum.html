Polygonum aviculare proles neglectum

Polygonum aviculare proles neglectum, commonly known as common knotgrass, is a low-growing annual plant belonging to the buckwheat family (Polygonaceae).

It is characterized by its prostrate or ascending stems that produce small, greenish-white flowers throughout the growing season. The plant has alternate, ovate leaves with a hairy texture and often thrives in disturbed soils such as roadsides and fields. It is known for its ability to spread rapidly through both seeds and rhizomes, making it a common weed in various habitats.

Its botanical features make it well-adapted to a wide range of environmental conditions.
