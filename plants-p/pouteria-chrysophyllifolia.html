Pouteria chrysophyllifolia

Pouteria chrysophyllifolia, commonly known as the golden-leaf pouteria, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, glossy, elliptical leaves that are often a striking yellow-green color. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by edible, sweet, and fleshy fruits. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and its fruit, which is sometimes used in local cuisines.
