Packera cana f. eradiata

Packera cana f. eradiata, commonly known as the paleleaf fleabane, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks. Its leaves are broadly ovate to round, with a pale green color and a fuzzy texture.

The flowers, which bloom in late summer, are yellow and arranged in a flat-topped cluster, attracting various pollinators.
