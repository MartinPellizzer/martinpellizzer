Primula hookeriana

Primula hookeriana, commonly known as Hooker's primrose, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often in alpine meadows and forested areas. Its leaves are usually broad and oval, with a hairy texture that helps retain moisture.

Primula hookeriana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
