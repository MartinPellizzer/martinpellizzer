Potentilla erecta var. maurorum

Potentilla erecta var. maurorum, commonly known as the Maurorum cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its upright growth habit and dense foliage. The plant features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically narrow and serrated, with a dark green upper surface and a lighter underside.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its hardiness and visual interest.
