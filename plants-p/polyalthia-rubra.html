Polyalthia rubra

Polyalthia rubra, commonly known as the red flame tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking red flowers that bloom in clusters, often appearing before the leaves in the spring. The plant has compound leaves with elongated, lance-shaped leaflets and produces small, woody fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The tree is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
