Peperomia portulacoides var. pilosa

Peperomia portulacoides var. pilosa, commonly known as the hairy peperomia, is a compact, low-growing plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It features thick, fleshy, hairy leaves that are typically green with a reddish or purple tinge on the underside. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually green or reddish in color. It thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Due to its drought tolerance and ease of care, it is well-suited for novice plant growers.
