Plantago australis subsp. hartwegii

Plantago australis subsp.

hartwegii, commonly known as the Hartweg's plantain, is a perennial herb native to the western United States. It is characterized by its basal rosette of broad, lance-shaped leaves with a hairy surface.

The plant produces erect flowering stems topped with a dense cluster of small, greenish-white flowers. It thrives in moist environments such as wet meadows, streambanks, and marshes.

Its fibrous roots and robust growth make it a hardy plant adapted to wetland habitats.
