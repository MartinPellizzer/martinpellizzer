Platycaulos mahonii subsp. mahonii

Platycaulos mahonii subsp.

mahonii, commonly known as the Mahon's flat-leafed plant, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region. It features basal rosettes of broad, fleshy, dark green leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating.

The plant produces small, inconspicuous white or pale pink flowers during the spring season. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
