Phacelia magellanica var. heterophylla

Phacelia magellanica var. heterophylla, commonly known as the Magellan phacelia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and variable leaf morphology, which can range from lance-shaped to heart-shaped. The plant produces clusters of small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in Patagonian regions.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
