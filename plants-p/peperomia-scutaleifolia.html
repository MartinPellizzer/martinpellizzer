Peperomia scutaleifolia

Peperomia scutaleifolia, commonly known as the shield-leaf peperomia, is a compact, evergreen perennial native to tropical regions of South America.

It features glossy, waxy leaves that are typically oval or elliptical in shape, often with a slight sheen and a textured surface. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually greenish-white in color, emerging from the leaf axils. It thrives in bright, indirect light and prefers well-draining soil with moderate humidity.

Peperomia scutaleifolia is popular as a houseplant due to its attractive foliage and low maintenance requirements.
