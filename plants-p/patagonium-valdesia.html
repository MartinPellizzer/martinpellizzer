Patagonium valdesia

Patagonium valdesia, commonly known as the Patagonian lichen, is a unique plant that thrives in the extreme environments of southern South America.

It is a composite organism consisting of a fungal partner and algal or cyanobacterial symbionts, forming a lichen. This plant is adapted to cold, arid conditions and can survive in nutrient-poor soils. Its structure is typically crust-like or foliose, allowing it to cling to rocks and tree trunks.

Patagonium valdesia plays a crucial role in the ecological balance of its habitat by contributing to soil formation and providing habitat for microorganisms.
