Puccinellia bruggemannii

Puccinellia bruggemannii, commonly known as Bruggemann's needlegrass, is a perennial grass species native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It is adapted to thrive in dry, sandy soils and is often found in desert scrub and grassland habitats.

Puccinellia bruggemannii plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for grazing animals in its native range.
