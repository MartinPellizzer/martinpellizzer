Papaver arachnoideum

Papaver arachnoideum, commonly known as the spider lily, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, pale yellow to orange flowers with a distinctive, hairy, spider-like appearance on the petals. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, black seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers sunny to partially shaded environments.

Papaver arachnoideum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
