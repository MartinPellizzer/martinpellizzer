Pyrethrum germanicopolitanum

Pyrethrum germanicopolitanum, commonly known as the Germanic pyrethrum, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its daisy-like flowers with yellow centers and white petals. The plant produces fine, hair-like structures called trichomes, which contain pyrethrin, a natural insecticide. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Its botanical features make it both aesthetically pleasing and ecologically valuable.
