Phleum hostii

Phleum hostii, commonly known as the purple meadow grass, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and dense, tufted growth habit, often forming expansive clumps. The plant produces panicles of small, spikelet-like flowers that are typically greenish or purplish in color. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue that adds visual interest to gardens.

Phleum hostii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
