Phragmanthera nigritana

Phragmanthera nigritana, commonly known as the black nightshade, is a herbaceous plant belonging to the Solanaceae family.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and small, dark purple to black berries that contain toxic compounds. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and is native to regions of Africa and the Middle East. Its flowers are usually white or pale blue, and it is often found in dry, open habitats.

Due to its toxic properties, it should be handled with care.
