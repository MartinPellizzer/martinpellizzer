Poa brachyanthera

Poa brachyanthera, commonly known as the slender meadowgrass, is a species of grass native to the southern regions of South America.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and narrow, blade-like leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of silky hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, pale blue to purple flowers. It typically thrives in moist, temperate climates and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental appeal and ecological value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and restoration projects.
