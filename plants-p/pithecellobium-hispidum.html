Pithecellobium hispidum

Pithecellobium hispidum, commonly known as the hairy pod bean, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and hairy stems. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by elongated, pod-like fruits that contain edible seeds. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has potential uses in traditional medicine.

The species is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial in agroforestry systems.
