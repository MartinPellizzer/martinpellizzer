Patagonium eremophilum

Patagonium eremophilum, commonly known as the Patagonian eremophilum, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of southern South America.

It is characterized by its low-growing, succulent stems and small, pale green leaves adapted to conserve water. The plant produces clusters of tiny, yellowish flowers that bloom during the brief rainy season. It is well-suited to harsh, dry environments and is often found in rocky soils.

Its botanical features reflect its evolutionary adaptations to survive in extreme conditions.
