Paeonia arietina var. andersonii

Paeonia arietina var. andersonii, commonly known as the Anderson's peony, is a cultivated variant of the peony genus native to China.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically range from pink to red in color. The plant is a herbaceous perennial, known for its robust growth and ability to thrive in well-drained soil. It produces fragrant blooms in the spring, often with a subtle, sweet scent.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value in gardens and its historical significance in horticultural cultivation.
