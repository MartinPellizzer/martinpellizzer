Paphiopedilum exul

Paphiopedilum exul, commonly known as the slender lady's slipper, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features a unique floral structure with a pouch-like lip that serves as a pollination trap. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments with rich, well-drained soil. Its leaves are broad and grass-like, and it produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of pink and white.

Paphiopedilum exul is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
