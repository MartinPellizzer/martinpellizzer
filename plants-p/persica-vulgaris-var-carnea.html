Persica vulgaris var. carnea

Persica vulgaris var. carnea, commonly known as the pink peach.

This variety is a cultivar of the peach tree, characterized by its distinctive pinkish-pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces sweet, fleshy fruits with a delicate flavor, typically harvested in late summer. It thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil and full sunlight.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruit, it is widely cultivated in gardens and orchards.
