Pavetta breyeri var. glabra

Pavetta breyeri var. glabra, commonly known as the smooth pavetta, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, glabrous stems and leaves that are typically elliptic to ovate in shape. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are often borne on short pedicels. Its fruits are capsule-like, splitting open to release small, winged seeds.

This species is often found in moist, shaded habitats within forest understories.
