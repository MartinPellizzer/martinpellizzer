Premna regularis

Premna regularis, commonly known as the small-leaved glorybower, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Lamiaceae, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks.

Premna regularis is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
