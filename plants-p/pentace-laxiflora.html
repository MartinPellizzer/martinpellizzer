Pentace laxiflora

Pentace laxiflora, commonly known as the African yellowwood, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a small to medium-sized shrub or tree that typically grows in coastal and mountainous regions. The plant is characterized by its yellowish-green leaves and clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. It produces small, woody fruits that contain seeds dispersed by wind.

Pentace laxiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
