Piper hodgei

Piper hodgei, commonly known as the Hodge's pepper, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and small, heart-shaped leaves. It produces tiny greenish-white flowers that develop into elongated, peppery fruits.

Piper hodgei is often grown for its ornamental value and has mild culinary uses in some traditional cuisines.
