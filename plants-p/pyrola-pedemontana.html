Pyrola pedemontana

Pyrola pedemontana, commonly known as the Alpine wintergreen, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its low-growing, evergreen foliage. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or white, blooming in early spring. Its leaves are dark green and leathery, adapted to thrive in cool, moist, acidic soils.

Pyrola pedemontana is often found in alpine meadows and forested areas at high elevations.
