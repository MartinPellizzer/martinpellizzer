Pyrus communis var. lagenaria

Pyrus communis var. lagenaria, commonly known as the pear-shaped apple, is a variant of the common pear tree.

It is characterized by its distinctive, swollen, and often ribbed fruits that resemble a pear in shape. The plant typically grows in temperate climates and produces edible fruits with a sweet and sometimes tart flavor. Its botanical features include alternate, simple leaves and a branching habit that supports its growth.

This variety is valued for both its ornamental appeal and its culinary uses.
