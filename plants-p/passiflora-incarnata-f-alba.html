Passiflora incarnata f. alba

Passiflora incarnata f. alba, commonly known as the white passionflower, is a variant of the common passionflower.

It is characterized by its predominantly white flowers, which contrast with the typical pink and purple hues of the species. The plant is native to the southeastern United States and thrives in warm, humid climates. It features heart-shaped leaves and produces edible, though somewhat bland, berries.

The white flowers are often used in ornamental gardening due to their striking appearance.
