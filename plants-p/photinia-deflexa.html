Photinia deflexa

Photinia deflexa, commonly known as the weeping photinia, is a shrub or small tree native to China.

It is characterized by its distinctive pendulous branches that give it a weeping appearance. The leaves are ovate to elliptical, with a glossy green upper surface and a lighter, sometimes hairy underside. It produces small, white flowers in clusters, followed by red, fleshy berries that are attractive to birds.

This plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
