Parapetalifera serratifolia

Parapetalifera serratifolia, commonly known as the serrated leaf parapetalifera, is a unique plant species characterized by its distinctive serrated foliage.

It belongs to the family Parapetaliferae and is native to specific regions in Southeast Asia. The plant features elongated, lance-shaped leaves with sharp, tooth-like edges along the margins. Its flowers are small and clustered, often appearing in shades of white or pale yellow.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in tropical gardens.
