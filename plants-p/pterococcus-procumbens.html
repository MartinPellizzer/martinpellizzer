Pterococcus procumbens

Pterococcus procumbens, commonly known as the prostrate pterococcus, is a flowering plant species native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is a low-growing shrub characterized by its woody stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
