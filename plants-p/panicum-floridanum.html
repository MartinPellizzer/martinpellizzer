Panicum floridanum

Panicum floridanum, commonly known as Florida panicgrass, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and fine, silver-green leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces fluffy, whitish inflorescences that are typical of the grass family Poaceae. It thrives in wetland environments and is often found in marshes and along the edges of water bodies.

Due to its adaptability, it plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
