Phyllanthus affinis

Phyllanthus affinis, commonly known as the Indian cheesewood, is a small evergreen shrub native to tropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves that are usually elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, followed by distinctive, fleshy, orange-red fruits.

It is often found in moist, shaded areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional medicinal uses.
