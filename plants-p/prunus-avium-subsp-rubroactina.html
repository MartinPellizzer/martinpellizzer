Prunus avium subsp. rubroactina

Prunus avium subsp.

rubroactina, commonly known as the red-leaf cherry, is a subspecies of the sweet cherry tree characterized by its distinctive foliage. It is native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe, where it thrives in temperate climates.

The plant features oval, serrated leaves that turn a striking red or orange in the fall, adding ornamental value to landscapes. Its flowers are white and appear in clusters, followed by sweet, edible fruits similar to those of the common cherry.

This subspecies is valued both for its aesthetic appeal and its contribution to local ecosystems.
