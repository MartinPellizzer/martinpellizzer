Piper dilatatum f. magnifolium

Piper dilatatum f. magnifolium, commonly known as the giant pepper plant, is a variant of the piper species native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 cm in length, showcasing a glossy, deep green surface. The plant produces small, clustered white flowers followed by elongated, cylindrical fruits that turn from green to yellow as they mature. It thrives in humid, shady environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its striking foliage.

The species is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
