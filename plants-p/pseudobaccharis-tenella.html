Pseudobaccharis tenella

Pseudobaccharis tenella, commonly known as the slender pseudobaccharis, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its narrow, gray-green leaves and small yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal dunes and scrub habitats. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain seeds dispersed by wind.

Pseudobaccharis tenella is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for native wildlife.
