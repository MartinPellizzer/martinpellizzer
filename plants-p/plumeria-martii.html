Plumeria martii

Plumeria martii, commonly known as the white frangipani, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, waxy flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, which bloom in clusters at the ends of branches. The plant has simple, opposite, and ovate leaves that are usually light green and slightly hairy. Its bark is smooth and pale, often exuding a milky sap when cut.

Plumeria martii is well adapted to coastal and arid environments, making it a popular ornamental plant in tropical regions.
