Phlox carolina subsp. turritella

Phlox carolina subsp.

turritella, commonly known as the Carolina phlox, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States. It typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and features showy, fragrant flowers in shades of pink to purple.

The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves and produces capsule fruits containing numerous small seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, stream banks, and woodland edges.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
