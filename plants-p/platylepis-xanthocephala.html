Platylepis xanthocephala

Platylepis xanthocephala, commonly known as the yellow-headed platylepis, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit and is typically found in moist, tropical environments. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, which are often borne on short pedicels. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a glossy surface, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

The species is valued in horticulture for its hardiness and ability to thrive in shaded conditions.
