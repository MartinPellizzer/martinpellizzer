Pterocarpus richardii

Pterocarpus richardii, commonly known as the African rosewood, is a deciduous tree native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. The tree produces long, flat pods containing seeds, and its wood is highly valued for its rich, reddish color and fine grain.

It thrives in well-drained soils and requires a warm, humid climate for optimal growth.
