Platystylis atropurpurea

Platystylis atropurpurea, commonly known as the purple-veined orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Australia.

It features a slender, upright stem with a single, lance-shaped leaf that is often tinged with purple. The plant produces a spike of small, pale green flowers with prominent purple veins, which bloom during the spring and early summer. It prefers well-drained soils and is often found in sandy or rocky habitats.

Platystylis atropurpurea is a relatively rare species, making it a sought-after plant among orchid enthusiasts.
