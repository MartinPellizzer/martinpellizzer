Polygala glaucoides var. laotica

Polygala glaucoides var. laotica, commonly known as the Laotian milkweed, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine, soft hairs, while its flowers are usually pale green to yellowish in color.

This variant is distinguished from the typical form by its geographic distribution and slightly different floral morphology.
