Papaver × trilobum

Papaver × trilobum, commonly known as the Himalayan poppy, is a hybrid species native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, vibrant red or orange flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, with lance-shaped leaves that are hairy and dark green. Its flowers consist of a single, saucer-shaped corolla with a prominent central stigma, typical of the Papaveraceae family.

The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, often found in alpine environments.
