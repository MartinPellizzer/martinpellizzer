Piper grandispicum

Piper grandispicum, commonly known as the large-fruited pepper plant, is a member of the Piperaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil and Peru. The plant is characterized by its climbing habit, with heart-shaped leaves and small, clustered flowers. Its fruits are berries that turn from green to yellow or red when mature.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
