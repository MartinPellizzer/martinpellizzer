Potentilla nutans

Potentilla nutans, commonly known as the nodding cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its sprawling growth habit and clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The plant features compound leaves with five leaflets, and its flowers typically bloom in early summer. Potentilla nutans thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and disturbed sites.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing food for pollinators.
