Pandanus brevispathus

Pandanus brevispathus, commonly known as the short-pathed pandanus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Pandanaceae.

It is native to the Pacific Islands, particularly Hawaii, where it thrives in coastal and wetland habitats. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender stems and long, narrow leaves that are often used in traditional crafts. Its inflorescence consists of a branched cluster of small, fragrant flowers that attract various pollinators.

The plant plays an important ecological role in its native environment, contributing to local biodiversity and providing resources for wildlife.
