Pistacia chinensis var. falcata

Pistacia chinensis var. falcata, commonly known as the Chinese pistache, is a deciduous tree native to China and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are alternate and consist of 10-20 elliptical leaflets. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, followed by edible, reddish-brown drupes. Its bark is thick, fissured, and becomes flaky with age, providing a distinctive appearance.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
