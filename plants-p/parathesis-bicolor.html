Parathesis bicolor

Parathesis bicolor, commonly known as the two-colored parathesis, is a flowering plant native to specific regions in South America.

It is characterized by its strikingly contrasting leaf colors, with one side of the leaf being green and the other side silver or white. The plant produces small, delicate flowers that are typically white or pale yellow. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical or subtropical regions.

Due to its unique appearance, it is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant in botanical gardens.
