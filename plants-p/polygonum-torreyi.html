Polygonum torreyi

Polygonum torreyi, commonly known as the Torrey's knotweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southwestern United States.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, which includes plants commonly referred to as smartweeds or knotweeds. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and can be found in desert scrub and chaparral habitats. It has slender, erect stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

Its small, greenish-white flowers appear in clusters and are accompanied by bracts that are characteristic of the genus.
