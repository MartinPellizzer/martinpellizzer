Papaver amoenum

Papaver amoenum, commonly known as the Persian poppy, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features striking, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from deep red to orange and yellow, often with a contrasting black center. The plant has smooth, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

The flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, adding a dramatic visual impact to the landscape.
