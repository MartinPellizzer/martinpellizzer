Passiflora helleri

Passiflora helleri, commonly known as Heller's passionflower, is a climbing vine native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, complex flowers that feature five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments. The plant produces blue or purple berries that are edible and often used in culinary applications. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires support for its vigorous growth.

Passiflora helleri is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
