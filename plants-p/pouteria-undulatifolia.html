Pouteria undulatifolia

Pouteria undulatifolia, commonly known as the wavy-leaf soursop, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, undulating leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces fragrant, yellowish flowers that develop into edible, spiny fruits resembling a large, green, heart-shaped melon. Its botanical features include smooth, greyish bark and a dense canopy of foliage.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential medicinal properties.
