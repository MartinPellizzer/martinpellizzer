Plantago myosuros var. latifolia

Plantago myosuros var. latifolia, commonly known as broad-leaved plantain, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features broad, lance-shaped leaves with a waxy surface and prominent veins, and it produces small, greenish flowers in dense spikes. The plant is adapted to grow in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of habitats, including roadsides and meadows. It is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.

The species is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ease of cultivation.
