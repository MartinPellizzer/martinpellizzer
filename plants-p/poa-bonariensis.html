Poa bonariensis

Poa bonariensis, commonly known as the Buenos Aires grass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers, which are wind-pollinated. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in open fields, meadows, and along roadsides.

Due to its adaptability and ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and used in landscaping.
