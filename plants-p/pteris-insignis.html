Pteris insignis

Pteris insignis, commonly known as the golden fern, is a species of fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, fan-shaped fronds that are typically bright green with a subtle golden hue. The plant has a compact growth habit and thrives in moist, shaded environments. Its rhizomes are fibrous and help anchor it to rocky or forested substrates.

Pteris insignis is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance and ease of care.
