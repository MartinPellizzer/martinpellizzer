Peperomia wallichii

Peperomia wallichii, commonly known as the silver peperomia, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It features succulent, ovate leaves with a distinctive silvery-blue hue on the upper surface and a darker green underside. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers that appear in clusters during the warmer months. It thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Due to its slow growth and ornamental foliage, it is often cultivated as a decorative plant in both home and office settings.
