Pilea gracilior

Pilea gracilior, commonly known as the Chinese money plant, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to China.

It features heart-shaped leaves that are typically a glossy dark green, with a slightly waxy texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers that bloom in spring, followed by round, greenish-brown fruits. It thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor cultivation.

Pilea gracilior is noted for its ease of care and ability to adapt well to various indoor environments.
