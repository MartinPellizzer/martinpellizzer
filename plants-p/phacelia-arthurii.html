Phacelia arthurii

Phacelia arthurii, commonly known as Arthur's phacelia, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a herbaceous annual that typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. The plant produces clusters of small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in early summer. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often hairy, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Phacelia arthurii is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators.
