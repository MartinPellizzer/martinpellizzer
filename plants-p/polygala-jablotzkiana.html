Polygala jablotzkiana

Polygala jablotzkiana, commonly known as the Cape jasmine, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and has a shrub-like appearance with narrow, leathery leaves. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, and they are arranged in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic beauty.
