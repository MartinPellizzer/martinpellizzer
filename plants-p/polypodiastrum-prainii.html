Polypodiastrum prainii

Polypodiastrum prainii, commonly known as the Prain's polypody, is a fern native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its delicate, feather-like fronds. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its reproductive structures, including sporangia, are clustered on the underside of its leaves.

Polypodiastrum prainii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
