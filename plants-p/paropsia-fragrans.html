Paropsia fragrans

Paropsia fragrans, commonly known as the sweet-scented orchid, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, fragrant blooms. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, thriving in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, while its flowers are usually pink or white with a strong, sweet aroma.

Paropsia fragrans is valued both for its ornamental beauty and its use in traditional medicine.
