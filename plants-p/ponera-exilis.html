Ponera exilis

Ponera exilis, commonly known as the slender antplant, is a unique epiphytic plant native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It forms a dense mat of foliage that serves as a habitat for various small invertebrates, including ants. The plant's leaves are small, elongated, and covered with a fine layer of trichomes, which help in water retention and protection from herbivores. It reproduces through vegetative propagation, with new shoots emerging from the base of the parent plant.

The botanical structure of Ponera exilis is adapted to its epiphytic lifestyle, with specialized roots that absorb moisture and nutrients from the air and surrounding substrates.
