Plantago coronopus var. maritima

Plantago coronopus var. maritima, commonly known as sea plantain, is a coastal plant adapted to sandy and salty environments.

It has narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in a fine, white wax layer to reduce water loss. The plant produces small, greenish flowers in dense spikes, which are typically wind-pollinated. It is found along shorelines and dunes in temperate regions.

This variety is closely related to other Plantago species but is distinguished by its salt tolerance and specific habitat preferences.
