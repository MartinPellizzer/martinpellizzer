Pandanus multicarpelatus

Pandanus multicarpelatus, commonly known as the multi-fruited pandanus, is a tropical plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It is a member of the Pandanaceae family and is characterized by its tall, tree-like structure with long, narrow leaves. The plant produces multiple clusters of fruits, which are typically covered in a fibrous, spiky husk. Its roots system is robust, allowing it to thrive in moist, sandy soils.

The plant is often used for its ornamental value and has cultural significance in various island communities.
