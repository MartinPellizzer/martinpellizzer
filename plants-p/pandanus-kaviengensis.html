Pandanus kaviengensis

Pandanus kaviengensis, commonly known as the Buka pandanus, is a flowering plant native to the Pacific Islands, particularly found in Papua New Guinea.

It belongs to the Pandanaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, sword-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, clustered yellow flowers that are often used in traditional crafts and culinary practices. Its fibrous leaves are also utilized for weaving baskets and other items, showcasing its cultural and practical significance.

The species thrives in coastal and lowland areas, adapting well to humid tropical climates.
