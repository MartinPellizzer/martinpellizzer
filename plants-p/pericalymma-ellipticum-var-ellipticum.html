Pericalymma ellipticum var. ellipticum

Pericalymma ellipticum var. ellipticum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved pericalymma, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, elliptical leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the spring and summer seasons. It thrives in well-drained soils and is typically found in open woodlands or grasslands.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
