Panicum xanthophysum f. amplifolium

Panicum xanthophysum f. amplifolium, commonly known as the broadleaf panicgrass, is a variant of the panicum genus characterized by its distinctive foliage.

This plant is native to parts of eastern North America and thrives in full sun to partial shade conditions. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, often with a bluish-green hue, and are covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, fluffy seed heads that add a decorative touch to gardens and natural landscapes.

It is commonly used in ornamental horticulture for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
