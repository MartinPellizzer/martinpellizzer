Pleiospora buchananii

Pleiospora buchananii, commonly known as the false flax or false nettle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is native to South Africa and is characterized by its tall, erect growth habit and yellow flowers arranged in a composite head. The plant has alternate, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it a common sight in open grasslands and disturbed areas.

Pleiospora buchananii is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
