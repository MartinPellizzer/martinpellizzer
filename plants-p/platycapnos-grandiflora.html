Platycapnos grandiflora

Platycapnos grandiflora, commonly known as the large-flowered bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to violet, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are arranged in a basal rosette, and its stems are hairy and upright. It prefers well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a suitable addition to rock gardens or alpine settings.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its attractive floral display.
