Passiflora splendens

Passiflora splendens, commonly known as the brilliant passionflower, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that display a mix of blue, violet, and white petals, along with prominent filaments that resemble threads. The plant produces edible, orange-red berries that are rich in antioxidants. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires support for its vigorous growth.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for tropical gardens and as a houseplant.
