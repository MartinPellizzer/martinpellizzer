Pyrus breviramea

Pyrus breviramea, commonly known as the short-stemmed pear, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the mountainous regions of central and southern China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate, and ovate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in early spring, followed by edible, sweet-tart fruits that are similar to common pears.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible yield.
