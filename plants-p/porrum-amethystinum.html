Porrum amethystinum

Porrum amethystinum, commonly known as the amethyst bellflower, is a rare and striking plant native to the high altitudes of the Andes.

It is characterized by its vivid purple flowers, which bloom in clusters resembling small bells, and its striking silver-green foliage. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments with well-drained soil, making it a challenging species to cultivate outside its natural habitat. Its flowers are pollinated primarily by hummingbirds and certain species of moths.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, Porrum amethystinum is considered a vulnerable species in conservation efforts.
