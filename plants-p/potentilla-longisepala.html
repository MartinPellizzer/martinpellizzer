Potentilla longisepala

Potentilla longisepala, commonly known as the long-sepaled cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of small, yellow, five-petaled flowers. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves that are arranged alternately along the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in alpine and subalpine environments.

Potentilla longisepala is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
