Protasparagus stipulaceus

Protasparagus stipulaceus, commonly known as the stipule asparagus, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Asparagaceae.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant is native to southern Africa and thrives in well-drained, sandy soils. Its leaves are narrow and linear, with small stipules at the base of each leaf blade.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its attractive appearance and ability to form dense mats.
