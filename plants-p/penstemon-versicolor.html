Penstemon versicolor

Penstemon versicolor, commonly known as the variable beardtongue, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple and pink. The plant produces showy, two-lipped flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Penstemon versicolor thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

Its adaptability and ornamental value contribute to its significance in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
