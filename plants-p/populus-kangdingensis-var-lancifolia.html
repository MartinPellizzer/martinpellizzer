Populus kangdingensis var. lancifolia

Populus kangdingensis var. lancifolia, commonly known as the Kangding poplar, is a rare and endemic species native to the eastern Himalayas.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and its ability to thrive in high-altitude environments. The plant typically grows in moist, temperate forests and is often found in mountainous regions of China. Its bark is smooth and grayish, while its branches exhibit a distinctive, spreading growth habit.

This variant is of significant botanical interest due to its unique morphology and ecological adaptations.
