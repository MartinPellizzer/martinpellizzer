Potentilla tormentilla var. humifusa

Potentilla tormentilla var. humifusa, commonly known as creeping potentilla, is a low-growing shrub native to North America.

It features dense, mat-forming growth with small, oval leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of yellow flowers in late spring to early summer, which attract pollinators like bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

This variety is valued for its hardiness and ornamental appeal in garden settings.
