Prostanthera empetrifolia

Prostanthera empetrifolia, commonly known as the rock fig or empetrifolia mint, is a small shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its small, opposite, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are typically pale blue to purple and are borne in clusters, attracting various pollinators.

Prostanthera empetrifolia is well adapted to rocky and sandy soils, making it a resilient species in arid environments.
