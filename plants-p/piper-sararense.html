Piper sararense

Piper sararense, commonly known as the Sararenga pepper, is a tropical climbing vine native to the Amazon rainforest.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many pepper species. The plant produces small, yellowish-green berries that are used in traditional medicine by indigenous communities. Its leaves are often used for their aromatic properties and are sometimes brewed into a tea.

The plant's botanical structure includes aerial roots and a complex system of tendrils for support.
