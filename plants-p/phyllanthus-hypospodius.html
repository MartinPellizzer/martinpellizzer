Phyllanthus hypospodius

Phyllanthus hypospodius, commonly known as the African date tree, is a small evergreen shrub native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its dense, rounded crown and small, clustered leaves. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers that are followed by fleshy, red or orange fruits containing several seeds. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.
