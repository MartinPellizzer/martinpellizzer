Phalacrodiscus latifolius

Phalacrodiscus latifolius, commonly known as the broad-leaved phalacrodiscus, is a unique flowering plant native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy cuticle, which helps reduce water loss in its often humid and tropical habitat. The plant produces small, clustered flowers with pale yellow petals, which bloom during the rainy season. Its root system is fibrous and adapted to nutrient-poor soils, allowing it to thrive in moist, shaded environments.

Due to its limited distribution and specific ecological requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
