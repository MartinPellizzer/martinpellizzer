Prunus verecunda var. intermedia

Prunus verecunda var. intermedia, commonly known as the intermediate cherry, is a hybrid species within the Prunus genus.

It is characterized by its moderate growth habit and is typically found in temperate regions. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers in early spring, followed by edible berries that are often used in local cuisines. Its leaves are oval-shaped with a smooth texture and a slightly serrated edge.

This variety is valued for both its ornamental appeal and its ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
