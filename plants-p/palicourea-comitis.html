Palicourea comitis

Palicourea comitis, commonly known as the Brazilian beautyberry, is a member of the Rubiaceae family, which includes coffee and gardenias.

It is a shrub native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil, characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves with a glossy surface. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that transition into bright purple to blue berries, which are edible but have a mild, tangy flavor.

These berries are often used in local cuisine and traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
