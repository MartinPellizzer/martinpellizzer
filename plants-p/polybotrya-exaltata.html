Polybotrya exaltata

Polybotrya exaltata, commonly known as the tall rock orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its erect, branching stems and small, greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it often forms dense clusters in its natural habitat.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
