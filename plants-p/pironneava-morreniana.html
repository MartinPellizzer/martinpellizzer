Pironneava morreniana

Pironneava morreniana, commonly known as the Morren's pironneava, is a rare and striking orchid native to the high-altitude regions of Madagascar.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stem and large, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of violet and white. The plant belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is known for its intricate floral structure, which includes specialized pollination mechanisms. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires specific soil conditions to flourish.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered endangered and is the subject of conservation efforts.
