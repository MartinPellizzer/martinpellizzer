Potentilla recta subsp. sulphurea

Potentilla recta subsp.

sulphurea, commonly known as sulphur potentilla, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It is characterized by its yellow flowers and hairy stems, which are typical of the Potentilla genus.

The subspecies is distinguished by its sulfur-yellow inflorescences, which give it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
