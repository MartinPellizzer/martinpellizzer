Premna sambucina

Premna sambucina, commonly known as the common sycamore or false elder, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the family Lamiaceae and is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink, followed by blue-black berries. It thrives in sunny to partially shaded locations and is often found in woodland edges or hedgerows.

Premna sambucina is noted for its ornamental value and historical use in traditional medicine.
