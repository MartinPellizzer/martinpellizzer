Portulaca andicola

Portulaca andicola, commonly known as the Andean portulaca, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a low-growing annual herb that thrives in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant features fleshy, succulent leaves and vibrant, bright flowers that typically bloom in shades of red, yellow, or orange. It has adapted to survive in poor soil conditions and can tolerate high levels of sunlight.

Portulaca andicola is often used in landscaping due to its ornamental value and drought resistance.
