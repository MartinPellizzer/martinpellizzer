Phyllanthus microphyllinus

Phyllanthus microphyllinus, commonly known as the small-leaved phyllanthus, is a flowering plant belonging to the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia, including India, China, and Southeast Asia. The plant is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are densely packed along the stems. Its flowers are unisexual, with male and female flowers occurring on separate plants, and it produces small, fleshy fruits.

The species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional medicinal uses in some cultures.
