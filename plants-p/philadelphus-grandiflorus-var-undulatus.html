Philadelphus grandiflorus var. undulatus

Philadelphus grandiflorus var. undulatus, commonly known as the wavyleaf mock orange, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white, clustered flowers that bloom in early summer. The leaves are ovate with a wavy margin, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil.

This plant is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and hardiness.
