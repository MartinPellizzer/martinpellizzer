Pereskia vargasii

Pereskia vargasii, commonly known as the Vargas cactus, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems that are often mistaken for leaves, a feature common in many cacti. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom during the warmer months. Its structure is adapted to store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments.

Pereskia vargasii is also noted for its slow growth rate and ability to survive in poor soil conditions.
