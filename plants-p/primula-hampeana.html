Primula hampeana

Primula hampeana, commonly known as the Hampe's primrose, is a species of flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, bell-shaped flowers that typically range in color from white to pink. The plant has hairy, oval-shaped leaves that are usually arranged in a basal rosette. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in forested areas at high altitudes.

Due to its ornamental value and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
