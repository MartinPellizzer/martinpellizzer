Phusicarpos longifolius

Phusicarpos longifolius, commonly known as the longleaf honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, arching branches and small, elliptical leaves that are often tinged with red or purple in the fall. The plant produces fragrant, tubular flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, which bloom in late spring. Its berries, which are dark blue to black, are an important food source for birds and small mammals.

Phusicarpos longifolius thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and open areas.
