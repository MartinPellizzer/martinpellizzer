Polygala preslii

Polygala preslii, commonly known as Presli's milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, demonstrating a high degree of drought tolerance. Its flowers are usually pale green to yellowish, with five petals and a distinctive spurred structure.

Polygala preslii is valued in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
