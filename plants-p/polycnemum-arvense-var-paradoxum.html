Polycnemum arvense var. paradoxum

Polycnemum arvense var. paradoxum, commonly known as the paradoxical field sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and spike-like inflorescence.

It typically grows in moist, disturbed soils in temperate regions, often found in meadows, roadsides, and along streambanks. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are wind-pollinated, and its rhizomes allow it to spread vegetatively. It is a member of the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants.

Its adaptability to various environmental conditions makes it a common weed in agricultural and natural settings.
