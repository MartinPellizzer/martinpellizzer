Phalaris intermedia var. angustata

Phalaris intermedia var. angustata, commonly known as narrowleaf jointvetch, is a perennial grass-like plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features slender, grass-like leaves that are typically narrow and slightly hairy, with a bluish-green hue. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing tiny, greenish-white flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along streambanks.

Its botanical structure makes it adaptable to a variety of wetland habitats.
