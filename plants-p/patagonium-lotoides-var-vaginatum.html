Patagonium lotoides var. vaginatum

Patagonium lotoides var. vaginatum, commonly known as the Patagonian water shield, is a perennial aquatic plant native to the wetlands of southern South America.

It is characterized by its broad, floating leaves that are often covered in a waxy cuticle to reduce water loss. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in late summer, typically emerging from the water surface. Its roots form dense mats that help stabilize sediments and provide habitat for aquatic organisms.

This species is adapted to freshwater environments and plays a role in nutrient cycling within its ecosystem.
