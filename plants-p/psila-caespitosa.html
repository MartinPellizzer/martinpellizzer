Psila caespitosa

Psila caespitosa, commonly known as the tufted greenbottle, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in grasslands and open woodlands. The plant features a basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in summer. It is often found in sandy or disturbed soils and is adapted to dry conditions.

The species is notable for its association with the larvae of certain flies, which feed on its foliage.
