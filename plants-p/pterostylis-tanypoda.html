Pterostylis tanypoda

Pterostylis tanypoda, commonly known as the narrow-leaf greenhood, is a terrestrial orchid native to eastern Australia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as rainforests and understoreys. The plant features a slender, wiry stem with a single, narrow lance-shaped leaf. Its flowers are greenish-yellow with a distinctive hood-like structure, and they are often pollinated by thynnid wasps.

This species is part of the large and diverse Orchidaceae family, known for its specialized floral morphology and ecological interactions.
