Philyra

Philyra, commonly known as the moonflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its large, showy white flowers that open at night. The plant produces small, round berries that turn from green to red as they mature. Philyra thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Its unique nocturnal blooming habit makes it a popular choice for gardens seeking evening beauty.
