Piper galeatum

Piper galeatum, commonly known as the pepper plant or wild pepper, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its slender, climbing vines with opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by three-lobed, berry-like fruits that are aromatic and used in traditional medicine. Its leaves and fruits contain essential oils and have been used for their medicinal properties.

Piper galeatum thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in humid forests.
