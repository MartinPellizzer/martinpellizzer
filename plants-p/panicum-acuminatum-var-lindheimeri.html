Panicum acuminatum var. lindheimeri

Panicum acuminatum var. lindheimeri, commonly known as Lindheimer's panicgrass, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and produces narrow, grass-like leaves with a bluish-gray coloration. The plant typically blooms with small, spike-like inflorescences that bear delicate, feather-like flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and prairies.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for ecological studies and native plant landscaping.
