Pinalia pusilla

Pinalia pusilla, commonly known as the dwarf pincushion cactus, is a small, flowering cactus native to the deserts of southern Africa.

It features a compact, globular stem covered in dense, spiny areoles, which provide protection from herbivores and reduce water loss. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the cooler months, adding a delicate touch to its otherwise harsh appearance. Its succulent structure allows it to store water efficiently, adapting to arid environments.

Pinalia pusilla is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its unique shape and resilience.
