Panicum prostratum

Panicum prostratum, commonly known as prostrate panicgrass, is a low-growing grass species native to parts of North America.

It typically forms dense mats on the ground, with slender, hairless stems that lie flat on the soil surface. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, fluffy seeds. It thrives in dry, disturbed soils and is often found in open fields, along roadsides, and in sandy areas.

Due to its hardy nature, it is considered a weed in some agricultural settings but also serves as a valuable pioneer species in ecological succession.
