Pogoblephis missurica

Pogoblephis missurica, commonly known as the Missourian bog lichen, is a unique symbiotic organism composed of a fungus and algae or cyanobacteria.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as bogs and wetlands, where it forms a dense, crust-like growth on decaying wood or soil. The plant's structure is non-photosynthetic, relying on its algal partner for energy through photosynthesis. It plays a crucial role in nutrient cycling in its ecosystem by breaking down organic matter.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is often used as an indicator species for environmental health.
