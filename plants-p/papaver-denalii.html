Papaver denalii

Papaver denalii, commonly known as the Denali poppy, is a striking perennial plant native to the high-altitude regions of Alaska and the Yukon.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from deep red to orange, with a prominent, saucer-shaped corolla. The plant typically grows in rocky, alpine environments, thriving in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are sturdy and often reddish in hue.

Papaver denalii is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens that mimic its natural habitat.
