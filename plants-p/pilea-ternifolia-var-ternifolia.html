Pilea ternifolia var. ternifolia

Pilea ternifolia var. ternifolia, commonly known as the Chinese money plant, is a compact, evergreen perennial native to China.

It features rosettes of thick, fleshy, oval-shaped leaves that are typically a deep green with a slightly waxy texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually white or pale green, emerging from the center of the rosette. It thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Its unique leaf shape and low maintenance requirements contribute to its widespread cultivation.
