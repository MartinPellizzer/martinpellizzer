Piper niceforoi var. glabricaule

Piper niceforoi var. glabricaule, commonly known as the smooth-stemmed pepper plant, is a variant of the Piper niceforoi species native to the rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, glabrous stems and lanceolate leaves that are typically dark green in color. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that develop into peppercorns, which are used in traditional medicine and culinary applications. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained, fertile soil.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
