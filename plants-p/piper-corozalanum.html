Piper corozalanum

Piper corozalanum, commonly known as the Zambian pepper, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and small, greenish-white flowers. It produces small, dark blue berries that are edible and have a spicy flavor.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and traditional medicinal uses.
