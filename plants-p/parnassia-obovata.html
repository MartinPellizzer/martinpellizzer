Parnassia obovata

Parnassia obovata, commonly known as the broad-leaf white crowfoot, is a flowering plant native to mountainous regions of Asia and North America.

It is characterized by its showy, white, five-petaled flowers that resemble small crowfeet. The plant has a basal rosette of broadly ovate, waxy leaves that are often dotted with small, yellowish glands. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as meadows and forest floors.

Parnassia obovata is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
