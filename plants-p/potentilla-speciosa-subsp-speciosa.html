Potentilla speciosa subsp. speciosa

Potentilla speciosa subsp.

speciosa, commonly known as the showy cinquefoil, is a perennial shrub native to the western United States. It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are compound, consisting of five oval-shaped leaflets, and the stems are often covered in fine hairs.

This species is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of conditions.
