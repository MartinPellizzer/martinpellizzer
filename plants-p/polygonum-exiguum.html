Polygonum exiguum

Polygonum exiguum, commonly known as dwarf knotweed, is a low-growing perennial plant native to North America.

It typically reaches a height of only a few inches, with slender, spreading stems that form dense mats. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.

Polygonum exiguum thrives in moist, shady environments such as stream banks and wet meadows.
