Pterocarpus polyphyllus

Pterocarpus polyphyllus, commonly known as the Indian rosewood, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, dark reddish-brown timber, which is highly valued for its aesthetic and structural properties. The plant has compound leaves with multiple leaflets, and its flowers are small and yellowish-green. It produces distinctive, thick, woody pods that contain several seeds.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and for the quality of its wood.
