Pouteria rigida subsp. tomentosa

Pouteria rigida subsp.

tomentosa, commonly known as the woolly pouteria, is a small evergreen tree native to tropical regions of South America. It is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage, which gives it its common name.

The leaves are typically elliptical and have a leathery texture. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are usually white or pale yellow.

Its fruit is a capsule that contains several seeds, and it is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value.
