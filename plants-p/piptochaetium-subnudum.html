Piptochaetium subnudum

Piptochaetium subnudum, commonly known as the slender hairgrass, is a grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its fine, wiry leaves and small, spikelet-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. It has a C4 photosynthetic pathway, which allows it to efficiently utilize carbon dioxide under high-temperature conditions.

Its root system is fibrous, contributing to soil stabilization and nutrient cycling in its native environment.
