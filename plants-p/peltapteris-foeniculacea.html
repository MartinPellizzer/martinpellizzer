Peltapteris foeniculacea

Peltapteris foeniculacea, commonly known as the slender frond fern, is a prehistoric plant belonging to the order Marattiales.

It is characterized by its delicate, fern-like fronds that were typically long and narrow, with a feathery appearance. The plant thrived in swampy, moist environments during the late Paleozoic era. Its reproductive structures, including sporangia, were arranged in clusters on the underside of its fronds.

Fossil records indicate that Peltapteris foeniculacea played a significant role in the early development of forest ecosystems.
