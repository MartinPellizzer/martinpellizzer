Pneumatopteris truncata var. loyalii

Pneumatopteris truncata var. loyalii, commonly known as the Loyal's water fern, is a variant of the water fern genus Pneumatopteris.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that are typically submerged or partially submerged in aquatic environments. The plant exhibits a unique morphology with a short, creeping rhizome and small, clustered sporangia. It thrives in slow-moving freshwater habitats, often found in marshes and wetlands.

This variant is notable for its ecological role in aquatic ecosystems and its use in botanical studies.
