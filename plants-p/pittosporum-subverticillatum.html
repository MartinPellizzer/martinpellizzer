Pittosporum subverticillatum

Pittosporum subverticillatum, commonly known as the New Zealand pittosporum, is a shrub or small tree native to New Zealand.

It features dark green, leathery leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a distinctive white or yellowish midrib. The plant produces fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers in clusters, which are followed by small, orange-red berries. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The species is also valued for its role in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support local wildlife.
