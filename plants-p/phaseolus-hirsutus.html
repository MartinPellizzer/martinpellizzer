Phaseolus hirsutus

Phaseolus hirsutus, commonly known as the hairy pole bean, is a species of legume native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, as well as its elongated, pod-like fruits that contain edible seeds. The plant typically grows as a climbing vine, using its tendrils to support itself. Its flowers are usually white or pale purple, and it thrives in warm, tropical climates.

This plant is valued for its nutritional content and is often cultivated for both food and ornamental purposes.
