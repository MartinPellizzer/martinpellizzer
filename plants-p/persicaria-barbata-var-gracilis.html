Persicaria barbata var. gracilis

Persicaria barbata var. gracilis, commonly known as slender jointed knotweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of Asia.

It features upright stems that are often reddish in color and are covered with small, bract-like structures. The leaves are lance-shaped, with a hairy texture and a serrated edge. It produces small, white to pinkish flowers in clusters, which bloom in late summer.

This plant is often found in moist environments such as wet meadows and stream banks.
