Poikilacanthus flexuosus

Poikilacanthus flexuosus, commonly known as the zigzag mallow, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive, zigzagging stems and large, velvety leaves with a fuzzy texture. The plant produces showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy choice for gardens in warmer climates.

The botanical features of Poikilacanthus flexuosus make it both ornamental and ecologically valuable.
