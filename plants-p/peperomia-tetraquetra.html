Peperomia tetraquetra

Peperomia tetraquetra, commonly known as the four-veined peperomia, is a compact evergreen shrub native to Central and South America.

It features glossy, dark green leaves with a distinctive four-veined pattern on the underside, giving it its scientific name. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically white or pale green, followed by reddish berries. It thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Its low maintenance and attractive foliage make it a favored ornamental plant.
