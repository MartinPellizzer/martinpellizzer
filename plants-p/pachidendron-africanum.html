Pachidendron africanum

Pachidendron africanum, commonly known as the African pachyderm, is a unique plant species native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that help it retain moisture in harsh environments. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically green or yellowish in color. Its growth habit is shrub-like, with multiple stems emerging from a common base.

Pachidendron africanum plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for local wildlife.
