Pappostipa frigida

Pappostipa frigida, commonly known as the alpine feathergrass, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and produces narrow, gray-green leaves. The plant is adapted to cold climates and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky habitats. It blooms with small, inconspicuous flowers that are arranged in spikelets.

Pappostipa frigida is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in stabilizing soil in harsh environments.
