Philadelphus calvescens

Philadelphus calvescens, commonly known as the common mock orange, is a deciduous shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant has opposite, simple, and serrated leaves that are typically dark green in color. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.

Philadelphus calvescens is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and its ability to attract pollinators.
