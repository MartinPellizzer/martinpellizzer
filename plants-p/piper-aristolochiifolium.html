Piper aristolochiifolium

Piper aristolochiifolium, commonly known as the broadleaf pepper or the wild pepper, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species. The plant is characterized by its heart-shaped, dark green leaves and clusters of small, white flowers. It produces small, round fruits that are often used in traditional medicine.

The plant's botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and medicinal contexts.
