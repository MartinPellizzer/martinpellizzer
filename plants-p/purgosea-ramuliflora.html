Purgosea ramuliflora

Purgosea ramuliflora, commonly known as the fragrant forest fern, is a unique plant native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, feather-like fronds that emit a subtle, sweet aroma, especially during the evening hours. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained, nutrient-rich soil to flourish. Its reproductive system involves spores rather than seeds, and it plays a vital role in the ecological balance of its habitat.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered a rare and endangered species in the wild.
