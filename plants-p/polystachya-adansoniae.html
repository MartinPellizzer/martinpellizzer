Polystachya adansoniae

Polystachya adansoniae, commonly known as the giant orchid, is a striking tropical plant native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescences that can reach several meters in height, composed of numerous violet or blue-violet flowers. The plant has long, arching stems and broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, fuzzy texture. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its rarity and unique appearance, it is highly valued in horticulture and conservation efforts.
