Persicaria meisneriana

Persicaria meisneriana, commonly known as the velvetleaf or Mexican water spinach, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist or aquatic environments. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters. It has a fibrous root system and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds.

Its rapid growth makes it both a useful aquatic plant and a potential invasive species in non-native habitats.
