Primula obconica subsp. intanoensis

Primula obconica subsp.

intanoensis, commonly known as the Intano primrose, is a perennial herb native to the high-altitude regions of southern China. It features upright, hairy stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped flowers in shades of white to pale pink.

The leaves are broad, ovate, and often covered with a fine pubescence. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in rock gardens.
