Phylica axillaris var. pulchra

Phylica axillaris var. pulchra, commonly known as the Cape tulip, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular pink or red flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is often used in rock gardens due to its drought tolerance. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for coastal and Mediterranean climates.

The flowers attract pollinators such as birds and insects, contributing to its ecological value.
