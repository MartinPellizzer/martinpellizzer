Pachycymbium lugardii

Pachycymbium lugardii, commonly known as the African orchid, is a rare and striking member of the Orchidaceae family native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically bloom in late summer or early autumn. The plant features thick, fleshy leaves that help it store water in its native arid environment. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.

Due to its rarity and unique appearance, it is a sought-after species among orchid enthusiasts.
