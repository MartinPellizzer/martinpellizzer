Plumeria jamaicensis

Plumeria jamaicensis, commonly known as the Jamaican frangipani, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its fragrant, showy flowers that range in color from white to yellow and orange. The plant has simple, lance-shaped leaves and produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in tropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Plumeria jamaicensis is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
