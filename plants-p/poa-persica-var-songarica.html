Poa persica var. songarica

Poa persica var. songarica, commonly known as the Songarica grass, is a perennial grass species native to Central Asia.

It typically grows in temperate climates and is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green coloration.

It is often found in meadows, grasslands, and along riverbanks, contributing to soil stabilization and ecological diversity.
