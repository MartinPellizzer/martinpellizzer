Phoradendron flavescens var. glabriusculum

Phoradendron flavescens var. glabriusculum, commonly known as the Mexican mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on various host trees in the southwestern United States and Mexico.

It has small, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, whitish bloom, and its flowers are typically yellow or greenish-yellow. The plant produces small, fleshy berries that are a food source for birds and other wildlife. It is characterized by its lack of true roots and its ability to absorb water and nutrients from its host through specialized structures called haustoria.

This species is also notable for its role in the ecological relationships of its native habitats.
