Philodendron subhastatum

Philodendron subhastatum, commonly known as the "Crimson Shield" philodendron, is a tropical climbing plant native to the rainforests of Central and South America.

It features large, heart-shaped leaves with a striking red and green variegation pattern, which gives it its distinctive appearance. The plant has a robust stem that can climb using aerial roots, often reaching significant heights in its natural habitat. Its foliage is thick and waxy, adapting to high humidity and indirect light conditions.

Philodendron subhastatum is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
