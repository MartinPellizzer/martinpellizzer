Phoenix reclinata var. somalensis

Phoenix reclinata var. somalensis, commonly known as the Somali date palm, is a rare and resilient palm species native to the arid regions of Somalia.

It is characterized by its slender trunk and narrow, feather-like leaves that are adapted to withstand extreme drought conditions. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by edible dates, though it is less commercially cultivated than other date palm varieties. Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species facing threats from environmental degradation.

Its botanical significance lies in its unique adaptations to desert environments and its role in local ecosystems.
