Panicum urvilleanum

Panicum urvilleanum, commonly known as silver millet, is a grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and narrow, silver-gray leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, fluffy panicles of seeds that are dispersed by wind, aiding in its propagation. It is often cultivated as a forage crop and is adapted to grow in dry, poor soils.

Its resilience to drought and heat makes it an important plant in arid agricultural systems.
