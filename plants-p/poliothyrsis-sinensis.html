Poliothyrsis sinensis

Poliothyrsis sinensis, commonly known as the Chinese parasol tree, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It is characterized by its large, umbrella-shaped canopy and distinctive, showy white flowers that bloom in spring. The tree has compound leaves with long, slender leaflets and produces woody, spiky fruits that split open to release seeds. It thrives in temperate climates and is often planted for its ornamental value and shade-providing capabilities.

The plant is also valued for its ecological benefits, supporting various pollinators and providing habitat for wildlife.
