Prunus chamaecerasus f. salicifolia

Prunus chamaecerasus f. salicifolia, commonly known as the willowleaf cherry, is a compact, deciduous shrub native to Japan.

It is characterized by its narrow, willow-like leaves that are pale green and turn yellow in autumn. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers in early spring, followed by red or orange berries. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a ornamental plant.

Its unique foliage and seasonal color changes make it a popular choice for gardeners seeking visual interest throughout the year.
