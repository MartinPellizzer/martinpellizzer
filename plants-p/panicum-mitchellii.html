Panicum mitchellii

Panicum mitchellii, commonly known as Mitchell's panicgrass, is a warm-season grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and produces fluffy, silver-gray seed heads that resemble small clouds. The plant typically grows in sandy or disturbed soils and thrives in full sun. It is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.

Its foliage is narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-gray hue that adds visual interest to landscapes.
