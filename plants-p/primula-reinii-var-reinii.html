Primula reinii var. reinii

Primula reinii var. reinii, commonly known as the Alpine primrose, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its low-growing habit and showy, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically blooms in early spring, producing clusters of white or pinkish flowers that are often adorned with a distinctive purple or brown blotch. Its leaves are broadly ovate, with a waxy texture, and it thrives in cool, moist environments with partial shade.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens or alpine settings.
