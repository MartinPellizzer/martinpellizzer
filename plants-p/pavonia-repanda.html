Pavonia repanda

Pavonia repanda, commonly known as the scarlet penta, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces showy, five-petaled flowers in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant has alternate, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy surface and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.

Pavonia repanda is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
