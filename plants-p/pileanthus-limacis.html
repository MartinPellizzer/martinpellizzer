Pileanthus limacis

Pileanthus limacis, commonly known as the spiral fern, is a unique plant characterized by its distinctive spiral arrangement of leaves.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia. The plant features long, narrow fronds that curl into a spiral shape, creating an elegant and intricate appearance. Its rhizomes are fibrous and help anchor it to moist, shaded environments.

Pileanthus limacis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in humid, low-light conditions.
