Polygonum herniarioides var. illecebroides

Polygonum herniarioides var. illecebroides, commonly known as the redroot false nettle, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the eastern United States.

It features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often reddish at the base, and it produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters. The plant has a fibrous root system and thrives in moist, disturbed soils such as those found in wetlands or along stream banks. It is part of the Polygonaceae family, which is characterized by the presence of bracts and achenes as fruits.

Its botanical structure supports its role in ecological systems, providing habitat and food for various insects and wildlife.
