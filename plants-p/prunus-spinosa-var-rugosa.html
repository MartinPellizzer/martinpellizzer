Prunus spinosa var. rugosa

Prunus spinosa var. rugosa, commonly known as the rough-cherry or brambles, is a shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and oval, hairy leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces small, white or pink flowers in the spring, followed by edible berries that are often used in jams and wines. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is commonly found in hedgerows and woodland edges.

Its botanical features make it an important species for both ecological and culinary purposes.
