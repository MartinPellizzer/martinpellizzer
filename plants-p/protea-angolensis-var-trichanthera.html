Protea angolensis var. trichanthera

Protea angolensis var. trichanthera, commonly known as the Angolan protea, is a striking evergreen shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescences composed of numerous white to pinkish flowers that resemble a cluster of small proteas. The plant typically grows in coastal and mountainous regions, preferring well-drained, acidic soils. Its foliage consists of leathery, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery-white pubescence.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
