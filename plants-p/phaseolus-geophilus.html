Phaseolus geophilus

Phaseolus geophilus, commonly known as the creeping pole bean, is a herbaceous perennial native to Central and South America.

It produces small, yellow flowers and edible pods that contain small, round beans. The plant typically grows close to the ground, with sprawling stems that root at the nodes. Its botanical features include compound leaves with three leaflets and a fibrous root system.

It thrives in well-drained soil and is often used in traditional agriculture and as a forage crop.
