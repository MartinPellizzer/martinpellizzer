Peperomia parvipunctulata

Peperomia parvipunctulata, commonly known as the small-punctured peperomia, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It features dense, glossy, ovate leaves with a distinctive pattern of small, raised dots along the veins. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or red, which are followed by round, fleshy berries. It is well-suited for indoor cultivation due to its low maintenance and ability to thrive in indirect light.

Its unique leaf patterns make it a popular choice for collectors and indoor gardeners alike.
