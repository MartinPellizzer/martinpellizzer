Paspalum geminiflorum

Paspalum geminiflorum, commonly known as the twin-flower grass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and produces small, two-flowered spikelets that give it its common name. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks. Its flowering period occurs in the spring and summer, and it is known for its ability to thrive in tropical and subtropical climates.

Paspalum geminiflorum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in supporting local wildlife.
