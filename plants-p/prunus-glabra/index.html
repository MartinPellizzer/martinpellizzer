Prunus Glabra

Prunus glabra is a small, deciduous tree native to eastern North America. It is known for its simple, oval-shaped leaves and clusters of white or pink flowers that bloom in spring. The tree produces small, edible drupes that are often used in jams and preserves. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is commonly found in woodland areas. Prunus glabra is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological benefits to local wildlife.
