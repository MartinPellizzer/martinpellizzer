Paeonia caucasica

Paeonia caucasica, commonly known as the Caucasian peony, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and red, typically blooming in late spring. The plant has compound, bipinnate leaves that are arranged alternately along the stems. Its roots are thick and fleshy, adapted for storing nutrients and water.

Paeonia caucasica is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
