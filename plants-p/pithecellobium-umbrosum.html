Pithecellobium umbrosum

Pithecellobium umbrosum, commonly known as the dark pithecellobium, is a species of legume native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant produces elliptical, leathery pods that contain edible seeds, which are often used in local cuisines. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and nutritional benefits, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and sustainable agriculture projects.
