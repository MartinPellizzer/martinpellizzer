Palmifolium angustifolium

Palmifolium angustifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaf palm, is a tropical plant characterized by its long, slender leaves that grow in a fan shape.

It belongs to the Arecaceae family and is native to regions with warm, humid climates. The plant's leaves are typically a deep green, with a smooth, waxy surface that helps reduce water loss. It produces small, clustered flowers that eventually develop into edible fruits.

The plant is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its elegant appearance and adaptability to various growing conditions.
