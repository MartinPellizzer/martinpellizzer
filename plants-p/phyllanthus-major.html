Phyllanthus major

Phyllanthus major, commonly known as the Indian cheesewood, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its small, clustered leaves and yellow flowers. The plant produces capsules that contain seeds covered in a sticky, resinous substance. It is often found in dry, rocky soils and can thrive in arid environments.

Phyllanthus major is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
