Pilularia bokkeveldensis

Pilularia bokkeveldensis, commonly known as the Bokkeveld waterwheel, is a small aquatic plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its unique, spirally arranged leaves that form a whorl around the stem, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in still or slow-moving freshwater habitats, such as ponds and pools. Its reproductive structure consists of a floating fruit that splits open to release seeds when submerged.

Pilularia bokkeveldensis plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for small invertebrates and contributing to water quality through its biological activity.
