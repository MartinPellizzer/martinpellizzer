Polypodium beccarianum

Polypodium beccarianum, commonly known as the Beccarian clubmoss, is a fern-like plant belonging to the family Polypodiaceae.

It is characterized by its small, scale-like leaves and a creeping rhizome that produces sporangia. Native to tropical and subtropical regions, this plant thrives in moist, shaded environments. Its reproductive structures are clustered on the underside of the leaves, a feature typical of many ferns.

Polypodium beccarianum is often found in forest understories, contributing to the biodiversity of its habitat.
