Prunus mandshurica var. barbinervis

Prunus mandshurica var. barbinervis, commonly known as the Korean cherry or Manchurian cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to East Asia.

It features simple, ovate to elliptic leaves with a smooth margin and a slightly serrated edge. The flowers are white to pale pink, appearing in clusters, and bloom in early spring before the leaves emerge. The fruit is a small, dark red drupe that ripens in late summer.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its spring blossoms.
