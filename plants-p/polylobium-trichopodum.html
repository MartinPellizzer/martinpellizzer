Polylobium trichopodum

Polylobium trichopodum, commonly known as the three-lobed grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its distinctive trifoliate leaves.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as fields and roadsides, where it forms dense mats. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are wind-pollinated, and it produces lightweight seeds that are dispersed by the wind.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in stabilizing soil in ecological restoration projects.
