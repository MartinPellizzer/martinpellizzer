Pediomelum megalanthum var. retrorsum

Pediomelum megalanthum var. retrorsum, commonly known as the red sandalwood, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its woody stems and dense, leathery foliage. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink. Its bark is valued for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine.

Due to overharvesting, it is now classified as an endangered species.
