Piper entradense

Piper entradense, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper vine, is a climbing shrub native to the Amazon rainforest.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many species of pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves and small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in spikes. Its fruits are berries that turn red when mature, and they are often consumed by birds.

The plant is also valued for its potential medicinal properties in traditional medicine.
