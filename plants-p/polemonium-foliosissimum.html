Polemonium foliosissimum

Polemonium foliosissimum, commonly known as the showy bluebell, is a herbaceous perennial native to eastern North America.

It features lance-shaped leaves that are prominently veined and often have a waxy coating. The plant produces striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in early summer, typically in clusters at the ends of stems. It thrives in moist, woodland environments and is often found in rich, loamy soils.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable component of native plant gardens and ecological restoration projects.
