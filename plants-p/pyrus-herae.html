Pyrus herae

Pyrus herae, commonly known as the Herae pear, is a rare and ancient species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its small, ornamental leaves and clusters of white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, edible fruits that are typically yellow or green in color. Native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, it thrives in cool, temperate climates.

Due to its rarity and historical significance, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists alike.
