Prunus cocomilia var. puberula

Prunus cocomilia var. puberula, commonly known as the Mexican cherry or pink cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and oval-shaped leaves that turn yellow in the fall. The plant produces clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers in early spring, which are followed by red, fleshy fruits resembling cherries. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for ornamental planting in gardens.

The species is also valued for its adaptability to various climates and its historical use in traditional medicine.
