Physalis chenopodiifolia

Physalis chenopodiifolia, commonly known as the silverleaf physalis, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, silver-gray leaves and clusters of yellow flowers that develop into lantern-like fruits. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, making it well adapted to dry conditions. Its fruit, which is edible when ripe, has a sweet and tangy flavor.

The species is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its unique appearance and hardy nature.
