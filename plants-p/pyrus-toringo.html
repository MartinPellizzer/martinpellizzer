Pyrus toringo

Pyrus toringo, commonly known as the Japanese whitebeam, is a deciduous tree native to Japan.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and alternate, simple leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clusters, followed by small, round fruits that are typically red or yellow when mature.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to a variety of soil types, though it prefers well-drained conditions.
