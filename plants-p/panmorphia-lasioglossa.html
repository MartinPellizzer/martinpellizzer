Panmorphia lasioglossa

Panmorphia lasioglossa, commonly known as the velvetleaf orchid, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the orchid family (Orchidaceae) and is characterized by its striking, velvety leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous perennial, often reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange with a distinctive red or purple lip, and it thrives in moist, shaded environments.

Panmorphia lasioglossa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
