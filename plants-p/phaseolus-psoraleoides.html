Phaseolus psoraleoides

Phaseolus psoraleoides, commonly known as the yellow bean or yellow pinto bean, is a species of legume native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its yellow or cream-colored pods and small, edible seeds. The plant typically grows as an annual herb, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its flowers are usually pale yellow or greenish, and it thrives in warm, tropical climates.

The seeds are used in various culinary applications, though they are less commonly cultivated than other beans.
