Passiflora jatunsachensis

Passiflora jatunsachensis, commonly known as the Peruvian passionflower, is a species native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a climbing vine characterized by its striking, colorful flowers that resemble a crown, with intricate patterns of blue, purple, and white. The plant produces edible berries that are often used in traditional medicine and culinary practices. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a rough texture, while its stems are woody and covered in fine hairs.

Passiflora jatunsachensis is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance in its native habitat.
