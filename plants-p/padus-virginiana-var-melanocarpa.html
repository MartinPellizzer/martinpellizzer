Padus virginiana var. melanocarpa

Padus virginiana var. melanocarpa, commonly known as black chokeberry, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its dark purple to black berries that ripen in the fall. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and has compound leaves with five to seven leaflets. Its flowers are white and appear in clusters, followed by the edible, tart berries that are used in jams and wines.

The shrub is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing food for birds and wildlife.
