Panicum maximum var. laeve

Panicum maximum var. laeve, commonly known as Guinea grass, is a tropical grass species native to Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, erect growth habit and coarse, hairy leaves. The plant produces dense, spike-like inflorescences that bear small, two-lobed seeds. It is highly nutritious and is widely used as forage for livestock.

Due to its adaptability, it thrives in a variety of soil types and is resistant to drought.
