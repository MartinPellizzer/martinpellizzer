Pyrus montana var. longipes

Pyrus montana var. longipes, commonly known as the long-stemmed mountain ash, is a variant of the mountain ash tree native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows at high altitudes, often in rocky or alpine environments. The plant is characterized by its elongated, slender branches and clusters of small, white flowers that appear in early spring. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have serrated margins, turning yellow in the fall.

The fruit is a small, edible berry that is often consumed by birds and other wildlife.
