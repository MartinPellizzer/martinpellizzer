Peperomia increscens

Peperomia increscens, commonly known as the silver peperomia, is a compact, evergreen perennial native to the tropical regions of South America.

It features thick, waxy, silver-green leaves that are often covered in a fine, powdery coating, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers that emerge from the leaf axils. It is a popular houseplant due to its low maintenance and ability to thrive in indirect light.

Peperomia increscens prefers well-draining soil and moderate humidity, making it suitable for indoor cultivation.
