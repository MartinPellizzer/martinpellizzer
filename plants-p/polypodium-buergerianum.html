Polypodium buergerianum

Polypodium buergerianum, commonly known as the Buerger's shield fern, is a tropical fern native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, leathery, shield-shaped fronds. The plant typically grows on tree trunks or rocks in humid, shaded environments. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The fronds are usually evergreen and can reach lengths of up to 1 meter.
