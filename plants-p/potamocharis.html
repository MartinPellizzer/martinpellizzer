Potamocharis

Potamocharis, commonly known as the water clover, is a genus of aquatic plants belonging to the family Hydrocharitaceae.

These plants are characterized by their delicate, floating leaves that form a dense mat on the water surface. They thrive in freshwater environments such as ponds, lakes, and slow-moving streams. Potamocharis species are often used in ecological restoration projects due to their ability to improve water quality.

Their rapid growth and spreading habit make them both beneficial and potentially invasive in certain ecosystems.
