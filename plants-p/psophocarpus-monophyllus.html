Psophocarpus monophyllus

Psophocarpus monophyllus, commonly known as the evergreen cowpea, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and produces small, yellow flowers that develop into pods containing edible seeds. The plant thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth. It is often cultivated for its nutritional seeds, which are rich in protein and essential nutrients.

Additionally, the plant has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
