Philadelphus laxus

Philadelphus laxus, commonly known as the looseleaf phellodendron, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its fragrant, white to yellowish flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its bark is smooth and often peels in thin strips. It thrives in well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight, making it a suitable choice for garden landscapes.

The species is also valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in traditional medicine.
