Pelargonium × veniferum

Pelargonium × veniferum, commonly known as the sweet geranium, is a hybrid species within the Geraniaceae family.

It is characterized by its showy, fragrant flowers that range in color from pink to red, often with a distinctive white or yellow center. The plant typically has rounded, lobed leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a velvety texture. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and containers.

Pelargonium × veniferum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
