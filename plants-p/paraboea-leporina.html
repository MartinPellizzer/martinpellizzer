Paraboea leporina

Paraboea leporina, commonly known as the rabbit's foot fern, is a climbing epiphytic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Araliaceae and is characterized by its unique, fleshy, moss-like leaves that resemble rabbit feet. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by red berries, which are edible for some bird species. Its ornamental value makes it a popular houseplant, though it can be challenging to care for due to its specific environmental needs.
