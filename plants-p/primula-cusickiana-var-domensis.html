Primula cusickiana var. domensis

Primula cusickiana var. domensis, commonly known as the Domestica Primrose, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of central Asia.

It is characterized by its low-growing habit, with rosettes of dark green, hairy leaves that help it survive in harsh alpine conditions. The plant produces clusters of delicate, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine displays.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
