Phelipanche Olbiensis

Phelipanche olbiensis is a parasitic plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa. It belongs to the Orobanchaceae family and is known for its unique ability to attach to host plants and extract nutrients. This species is often found in arid and semi-arid regions where it thrives on the roots of various agricultural crops. Due to its invasive nature, it poses a significant threat to local ecosystems and farming industries. Understanding its biology and control methods is crucial for managing its impact on the environment.
