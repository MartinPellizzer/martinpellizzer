Polygala obscura var. macrophylla

Polygala obscura var. macrophylla, commonly known as the large-leaved milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to parts of China and Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its small, showy flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. The plant has compound leaves with elongated leaflets and produces small, cylindrical fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories or along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
