Paullinia rubiginosa subsp. setosa

Paullinia rubiginosa subsp.

setosa, commonly known as the hairy soursop, is a member of the Sapotaceae family. It is a small evergreen shrub characterized by its elliptical, dark green leaves with a hairy underside.

The plant produces yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by oval, yellowish fruits with a rough, bumpy surface. The fruit is edible, though its taste is often described as bland or slightly sour.

It is native to tropical regions of South America and is often found in moist, shaded forest understories.
