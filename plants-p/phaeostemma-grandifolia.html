Phaeostemma grandifolia

Phaeostemma grandifolia, commonly known as the large-flowered phaeostema, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its upright, branching stems and clusters of showy, yellow to orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has hairy, linear leaves that are typically arranged in opposite pairs along the stem. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Phaeostemma grandifolia is often found in open plains, along roadsides, and in disturbed areas.
