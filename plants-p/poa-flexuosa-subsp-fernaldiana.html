Poa flexuosa subsp. fernaldiana

Poa flexuosa subsp.

fernaldiana, commonly known as the Fernald's bentgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States. It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit and narrow, grass-like leaves.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers, typical of the Poaceae family. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an important component of native plant communities in its habitat.
