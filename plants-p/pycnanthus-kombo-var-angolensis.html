Pycnanthus kombo var. angolensis

Pycnanthus kombo var. angolensis, commonly known as the Angola pycnanthus, is a shrub native to central and southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Myrtaceae and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas. Its leaves are used in traditional medicine for their antifungal and antibacterial properties.

The fruit is a small, woody capsule that contains several seeds.
