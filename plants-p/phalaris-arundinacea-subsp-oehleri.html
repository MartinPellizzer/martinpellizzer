Phalaris arundinacea subsp. oehleri

Phalaris arundinacea subsp.

oehleri, commonly known as Oehleri reed, is a perennial grass species native to South America. It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, grass-like leaves.

The plant produces panicles of small, greenish flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in wetland environments and is often found in marshes and along riverbanks.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in aquatic habitats.
