Philadelphus roylei

Philadelphus roylei, commonly known as the Kashmir rose or Chinese mock orange, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has opposite, simple, and ovate leaves that are dark green on top and hairy underneath. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow in well-drained soil.

The species is also valued for its ability to thrive in a variety of climates and its historical use in traditional medicine.
