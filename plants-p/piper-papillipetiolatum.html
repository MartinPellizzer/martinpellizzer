Piper papillipetiolatum

Piper papillipetiolatum, commonly known as the hairy-leaf pepper, is a tropical climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species. The plant is characterized by its finely hairy, elongated leaves and small, greenish-yellow flowers. Its fruits are berries that mature to a dark purple color, containing several seeds.

Piper papillipetiolatum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has some traditional medicinal uses.
