Polygala kaessneri

Polygala kaessneri, commonly known as the Desert Caper, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in dense, cylindrical inflorescences. The plant has a succulent stem and leaves, which help it conserve water in its harsh environment. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is a popular choice for xeriscaping and rock gardens.
