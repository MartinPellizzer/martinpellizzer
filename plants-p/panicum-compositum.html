Panicum compositum

Panicum compositum, commonly known as foxtail millet, is an annual grass species native to the warmer regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, branched inflorescence that resembles a foxtail, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters and produces small, spikelet-based flowers. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a smooth texture, and it thrives in well-drained soils.

Panicum compositum is valued for its adaptability and is often cultivated as a staple grain in arid and semi-arid climates.
