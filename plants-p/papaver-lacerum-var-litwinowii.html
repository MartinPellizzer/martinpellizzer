Papaver lacerum var. litwinowii

Papaver lacerum var. litwinowii, commonly known as the Litwinowii poppy, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, vividly colored flowers that range from red to orange, often with a distinctive black or dark purple center. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, elongated seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, typically growing in alpine or rocky habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
