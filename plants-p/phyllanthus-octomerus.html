Phyllanthus octomerus

Phyllanthus octomerus, commonly known as the eight-merous phyllanthus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Euphorbiaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa and is characterized by its distinctive leaf structure with eight lobes. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences called cyathia, a typical feature of the Euphorbiaceae family. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
