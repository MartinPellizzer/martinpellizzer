Pithecellobium kubaryanum

Pithecellobium kubaryanum, commonly known as the Cuban velvet tamarind, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, yellow-orange flowers. The plant produces pods containing edible seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine and cuisine.

Its growth habit and adaptability make it a valuable species for ecological restoration in degraded tropical habitats.
