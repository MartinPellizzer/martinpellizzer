Philodendron tricostatum

Philodendron tricostatum, commonly known as the Three-ribbed Philodendron, is a tropical climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It features heart-shaped, glossy leaves that are typically dark green with a slightly waxy texture. The plant has a distinctive veining pattern, with three prominent ribs running from the base to the tip of the leaf. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually greenish-white in color.

Philodendron tricostatum thrives in bright, indirect light and requires consistently moist, well-draining soil.
