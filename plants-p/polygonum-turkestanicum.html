Polygonum turkestanicum

Polygonum turkestanicum, commonly known as the Turkestan knotweed, is a perennial herb native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family and is characterized by its stout, upright stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or red, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in river valleys and wetlands.

Polygonum turkestanicum is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
