Ptychosperma ambiguum

Ptychosperma ambiguum, commonly known as the ambiguous palm, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate leaves that can reach several meters in length. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers followed by edible, black-purple berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is also cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
