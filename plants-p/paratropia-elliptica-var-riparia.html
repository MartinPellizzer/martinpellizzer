Paratropia elliptica var. riparia

Paratropia elliptica var. riparia, commonly known as the river sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland and riparian habitats.

It typically grows in dense clusters, reaching heights of up to 1 meter, with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often submerged or partially submerged in water. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in early summer, which are clustered in spike-like inflorescences. Its rhizomatous root system helps stabilize soil and prevent erosion in aquatic environments.

It is an important component of wetland ecosystems, providing habitat and food for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
