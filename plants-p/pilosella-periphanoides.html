Pilosella periphanoides

Pilosella periphanoides, commonly known as the silky weed, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features soft, hairy leaves and produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. It thrives in moist, open habitats such as meadows and riverbanks.

Pilosella periphanoides is often found in disturbed soils and can spread readily through its seeds.
