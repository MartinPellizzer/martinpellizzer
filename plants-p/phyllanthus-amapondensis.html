Phyllanthus amapondensis

Phyllanthus amapondensis, commonly known as the African spurge, is a shrub native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its small, clustered leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant contains various bioactive compounds, including alkaloids and flavonoids, which have been studied for their medicinal properties. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and abandoned fields.

Due to its potential health benefits, it has gained attention in traditional and modern herbal medicine.
