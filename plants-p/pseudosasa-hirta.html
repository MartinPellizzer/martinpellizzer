Pseudosasa hirta

Pseudosasa hirta, commonly known as blue flag iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has narrow, sword-shaped leaves that are typically bluish-green in color. It thrives in wet or moist environments, often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

Pseudosasa hirta is an important species for wetland ecosystems, providing habitat and food for various wildlife.
