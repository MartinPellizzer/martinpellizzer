Potentilla recta f. microteropetala

Potentilla recta f. microteropetala, commonly known as the narrow-leaved cinquefoil, is a botanical variant characterized by its compact growth habit and distinctive foliage.

It features small, narrow leaves that are arranged in a spiral pattern along the stems, giving it a dense, mat-like appearance. The plant produces clusters of yellow flowers in late spring to early summer, which are typical of the Potentilla genus. It is native to regions of Europe and Asia, often found in rocky or dry habitats.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardiness in garden settings.
