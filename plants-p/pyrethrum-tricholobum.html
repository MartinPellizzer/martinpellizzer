Pyrethrum tricholobum

Pyrethrum tricholobum, commonly known as the narrowleaf pyrethrum, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and small, daisy-like yellow flowers. The plant is known for producing pyrethrins, natural insecticides used in organic pest control. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, often growing in rocky or arid environments.

Pyrethrum tricholobum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to pollinators.
