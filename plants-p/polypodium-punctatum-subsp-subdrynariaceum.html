Polypodium punctatum subsp. subdrynariaceum

Polypodium punctatum subsp.

subdrynariaceum, commonly known as the punctate holly fern, is a fern species native to Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped fronds with a distinctive pattern of small, round sori on the underside.

The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky areas. Its rhizomes are often covered in a thin, scaly layer, and the fronds are usually leathery and dark green.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in horticulture.
