Pleomele xiphophylla

Pleomele xiphophylla, commonly known as the swordleaf dracaena, is a tropical plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and elongated, sword-shaped leaves that are typically a deep green color. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in clusters, which are followed by red or orange berries. It thrives in humid, warm climates and requires well-draining soil.

Pleomele xiphophylla is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking foliage and resilience.
