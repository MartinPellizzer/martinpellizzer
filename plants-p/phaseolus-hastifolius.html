Phaseolus hastifolius

Phaseolus hastifolius, commonly known as the horse gram or cluster bean, is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pod-like fruits that contain several edible seeds. The plant is well adapted to dry climates and can thrive in poor soils, making it an important crop in arid regions. Its flowers are typically white or pale purple, and it produces a distinctive, clustered growth pattern.

The seeds are rich in protein and are used in various cuisines, particularly in South Asia.
