Papaver insignitum

Papaver insignitum, commonly known as the yellow poppy, is a herbaceous annual plant native to the western United States.

It features striking yellow flowers with a distinctive black center, blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves that are typically bluish-green in color. It grows up to 2 feet tall and prefers well-drained soils in full sun.

Papaver insignitum is often found in dry meadows and along rocky slopes, where it thrives in harsh, arid conditions.
