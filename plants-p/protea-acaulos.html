Protea acaulos

Protea acaulos, commonly known as the mountain protea, is a striking evergreen shrub native to the Western Cape of South Africa.

It is characterized by its large, showy flower heads that consist of a dense cluster of small, tubular flowers surrounded by modified leaves called bracts. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun, making it a popular choice for coastal gardens. Its unique floral structure and vibrant colors make it a standout species in the Proteaceae family.

The plant is also valued for its ecological importance, providing nectar for a variety of pollinators.
