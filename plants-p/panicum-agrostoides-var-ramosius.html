Panicum agrostoides var. ramosius

Panicum agrostoides var. ramosius, commonly known as the hairy panicgrass, is a perennial grass native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features slender, tufted stems with fine, hairy leaves that are often bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear delicate, pale green to brownish flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas.

Its adaptability makes it a common weed in agricultural fields and along roadsides.
