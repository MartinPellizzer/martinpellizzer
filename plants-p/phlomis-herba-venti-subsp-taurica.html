Phlomis herba-venti subsp. taurica

Phlomis herba-venti subsp.

taurica, commonly known as the Crimean mullein, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and large, gray-green, velvety leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces showy, clustered flowers in shades of yellow to orange, typically blooming in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
