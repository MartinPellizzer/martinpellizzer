Penstemon moffatii

Penstemon moffatii, commonly known as the Moffat's beardtongue, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to purple. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in arid or semi-arid environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

Penstemon moffatii is a perennial species that thrives in full sun and is well adapted to drought conditions.
