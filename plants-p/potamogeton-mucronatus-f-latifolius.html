Potamogeton mucronatus f. latifolius

Potamogeton mucronatus f. latifolius, commonly known as the broadleaf pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in North America.

It features long, ribbon-like leaves that are broad and flat, typically ranging from 1 to 3 centimeters in width. The plant produces small, green flowers that are arranged in spike-like clusters, and it reproduces both sexually and vegetatively through stolons. It thrives in shallow, slow-moving waters and plays a vital role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.

Its presence is often an indicator of good water quality and stable ecological conditions.
