Panicum sanguinale subvar. leptostachyon

Panicum sanguinale subvar. leptostachyon, commonly known as crimson grass or red panicum, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Asia and Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and dense, fluffy panicles that produce a striking red or pink hue. The plant typically grows in open habitats such as grasslands, woodlands, and disturbed soils. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green color that contrasts with the vibrant inflorescence.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping and erosion control.
