Phlox speciosa f. occidentalis

Phlox speciosa f. occidentalis, commonly known as the western showy phlox, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a deciduous perennial that thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil. The plant produces showy, fragrant clusters of pink to lavender flowers in the spring. Its foliage is lance-shaped and often covered in a fine, silvery-blue down.

It is valued in gardens for its ornamental beauty and ability to attract pollinators.
