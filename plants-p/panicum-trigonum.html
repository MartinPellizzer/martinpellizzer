Panicum trigonum

Panicum trigonum, commonly known as triangular panicgrass, is a species of grass native to the Americas.

It is a low-growing annual plant with narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically thrives in disturbed soils and can be found in fields, roadsides, and other open areas. Its seeds are dispersed by wind, contributing to its widespread distribution.

The species is often considered a weed but has some ecological value in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small invertebrates.
