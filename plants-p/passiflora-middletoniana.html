Passiflora middletoniana

Passiflora middletoniana, commonly known as the Middleton's passionflower, is a climbing vine native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful flowers that feature intricate structures such as the crown of filaments and a central corona. The plant produces edible, blue-black berries that are rich in antioxidants. Its leaves are heart-shaped and often have a velvety texture.

Passiflora middletoniana is valued in horticulture for its ornamental beauty and is also studied for its potential medicinal properties.
