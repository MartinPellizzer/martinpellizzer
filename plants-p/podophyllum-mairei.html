Podophyllum mairei

Podophyllum mairei, commonly known as the Maire's bladdernut, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the family Podophyllum, which includes several species with similar morphological characteristics. The plant is characterized by its large, broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. Its flowers are small and yellow, arranged in clusters, and it produces distinctive, berry-like fruits that contain toxic seeds.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal potential, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and research settings.
