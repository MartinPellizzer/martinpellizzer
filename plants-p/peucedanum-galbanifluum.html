Peucedanum galbanifluum

Peucedanum galbanifluum, commonly known as wild anise, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features finely divided, fern-like leaves and produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in umbels. The plant is known for its aromatic seeds, which have a licorice-like scent and are used in traditional medicine. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable species for both ecological and culinary purposes.
