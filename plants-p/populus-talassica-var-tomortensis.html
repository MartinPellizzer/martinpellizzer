Populus talassica var. tomortensis

Populus talassica var. tomortensis, commonly known as the Tomortensian poplar, is a rare and lesser-known variant of the poplar genus native to specific regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its narrow, elongated leaves and distinctive bark that exhibits a unique combination of colors, often ranging from grayish-brown to reddish tones. The plant typically grows in riparian zones and moist soils, adapting well to temperate to semi-arid climates. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, relying on wind pollination, while its seeds are dispersed by wind, allowing it to colonize new areas.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a species of conservation interest.
