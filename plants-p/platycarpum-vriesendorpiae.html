Platycarpum vriesendorpiae

Platycarpum vriesendorpiae, commonly known as the Vriesendorp's cycad, is a rare and ancient plant species belonging to the Cycadaceae family.

It is characterized by its stout, unbranched trunk and a crown of large, pinnately compound leaves. Native to South Africa, this plant thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid conditions. Its unique morphology and limited distribution make it a subject of interest in botanical research.

Due to its endangered status, conservation efforts are crucial for its survival.
