Pleurothallis heliconioides

Pleurothallis heliconioides, commonly known as the golden spike orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a small, epiphytic orchid that typically grows on trees in humid, shaded environments. The plant features slender, wiry stems and produces delicate, fragrant flowers in shades of yellow and green. Its leaves are elongated and strap-like, adapted for efficient photosynthesis in low-light conditions.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts for its unique appearance.
