Pyrola montana

Pyrola montana, commonly known as the mountain snowbell, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the northern regions of North America.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its evergreen leaves and showy white flowers. The plant typically grows in cool, moist environments such as forest understories and rocky slopes. Its small, bell-shaped flowers bloom in late spring and are pollinated by insects.

Pyrola montana is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used by indigenous peoples for various traditional remedies.
