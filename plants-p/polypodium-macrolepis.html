Polypodium macrolepis

Polypodium macrolepis, commonly known as the broad-scale huperzia, is a species of fern belonging to the family Huperziaceae.

It is characterized by its robust, evergreen rhizome and its distinctive, lanceolate leaves that are often covered in small, raised scales. The fronds are typically bipinnate, with a dense arrangement of pinnae that give the plant a lush, bushy appearance. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
