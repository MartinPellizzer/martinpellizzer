Polyphlebium herzogii

Polyphlebium herzogii, commonly known as the Herzog's spurge, is a member of the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its succulent leaves and striking yellow flowers. Native to southern Africa, it thrives in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant has a compact growth habit with fleshy stems and leaves that store water, aiding in its survival in dry conditions.

Its flowers are typically clustered in cyathia, a unique structure typical of the Euphorbiaceae family.
