Parkia gigantocarpa

Parkia gigantocarpa, commonly known as the giant legume tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, compound leaves and showsy, fragrant yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The tree produces elongated, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds embedded in a sticky, resinous pulp. Botanically, it belongs to the Fabaceae family, known for its nitrogen-fixing capabilities through symbiotic relationships with bacteria.

Its unique morphology and ecological role make it an important species in its native ecosystems.
