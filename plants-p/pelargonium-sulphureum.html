Pelargonium sulphureum

Pelargonium sulphureum, commonly known as the golden geranium, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow flowers and succulent, fleshy leaves. The plant belongs to the Geraniaceae family and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

Its unique coloration makes it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.
