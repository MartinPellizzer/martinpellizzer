Pauridiantha paucinervis

Pauridiantha paucinervis, commonly known as the slender-leaved pauridiantha, is a small evergreen shrub native to the mountainous regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves that are typically arranged in opposite pairs along the stems. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in clusters, often during the cooler months. Its foliage is dense and provides habitat for various small insects and birds.

Pauridiantha paucinervis is well adapted to rocky and well-drained soils, making it a resilient plant in its native environment.
