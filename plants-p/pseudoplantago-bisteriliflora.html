Pseudoplantago bisteriliflora

Pseudoplantago bisteriliflora, commonly known as the brittle-leaved plantain, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant is adapted to thrive in sandy or rocky soils, often found in disturbed or dry habitats. Its flowering period typically occurs in late spring to early summer, and it produces capsule fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds.

Due to its resilience and unique morphology, it is of interest to botanists studying plant adaptations to harsh environments.
