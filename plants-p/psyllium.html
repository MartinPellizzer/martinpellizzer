Psyllium

Psyllium, commonly known as isabgol, is a flowering plant native to regions of India and Pakistan.

It belongs to the Plantae kingdom and the Chenopodiaceae family. The plant is cultivated primarily for its seeds, which are rich in soluble fiber. The seeds are often used in traditional medicine and as a dietary supplement to aid digestion.

Psyllium's fibrous husks absorb water, forming a gel-like substance that can help regulate bowel movements.
