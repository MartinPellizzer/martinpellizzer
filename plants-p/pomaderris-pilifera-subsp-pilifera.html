Pomaderris pilifera subsp. pilifera

Pomaderris pilifera subsp.

pilifera, commonly known as the silky pomaderris, is a shrub native to southern Australia. It is characterized by its finely hairy, grey-green leaves that give it a distinctive appearance.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in clusters, which bloom in spring. It is typically found in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to coastal and semi-arid environments.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often used in native gardens.
