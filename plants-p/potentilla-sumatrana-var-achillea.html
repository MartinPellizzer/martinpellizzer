Potentilla sumatrana var. achillea

Potentilla sumatrana var. achillea, commonly known as the Sumatran cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories or along stream banks. Its leaves are palmately divided, with a hairy texture, and it produces small, berry-like fruits that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
