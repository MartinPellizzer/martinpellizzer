Polygonella articulata f. atrorubens

Polygonella articulata f. atrorubens, commonly known as the black-red polygonella, is a variant of the polygonella plant characterized by its distinct reddish-brown foliage.

It is a member of the Polygonaceae family, which includes plants known for their distinctive leaf shapes and often edible parts. This fern-like plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors or stream banks. Its leaves are narrow and often have a zigzag or undulating margin, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

The plant is valued in horticulture for its aesthetic qualities and ability to thrive in partially shaded conditions.
