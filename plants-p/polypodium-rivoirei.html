Polypodium rivoirei

Polypodium rivoirei, commonly known as the Rivoire's fern, is a species of fern native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often on rocky substrates or in forest undergrowth. Its reproductive structures, including sori, are located on the underside of the fronds.

Polypodium rivoirei is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
