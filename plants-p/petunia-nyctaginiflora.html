Petunia nyctaginiflora

Petunia nyctaginiflora, commonly known as the nightshade petunia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from purple to pink and white. The plant typically grows as an annual herb with trailing stems and opposite leaves that are ovate to lanceolate in shape. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardeners.

Its blooming period extends from late spring to early autumn, adding ornamental value to gardens and landscapes.
