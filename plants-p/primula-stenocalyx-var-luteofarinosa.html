Primula stenocalyx var. luteofarinosa

Primula stenocalyx var. luteofarinosa, commonly known as the yellow-hairy primrose, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of China.

It features lance-shaped leaves that are densely covered with fine, yellowish hairs, giving it a distinctive, fuzzy appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of yellow and white, typically blooming in early spring. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in forested areas or rocky slopes.

Due to its ornamental value and unique foliage, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
