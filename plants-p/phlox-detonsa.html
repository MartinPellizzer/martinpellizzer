Phlox detonsa

Phlox detonsa, commonly known as the blue phlox, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features showy, clustered flowers that range in color from blue to purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs, and it thrives in well-drained soil in full sun to partial shade. It is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.

Phlox detonsa is also valued for its ability to naturalize and form dense colonies in suitable conditions.
