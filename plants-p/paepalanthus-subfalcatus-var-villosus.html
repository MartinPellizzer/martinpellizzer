Paepalanthus subfalcatus var. villosus

Paepalanthus subfalcatus var. villosus, commonly known as the hairy sword fern, is a species native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a perennial plant characterized by its sword-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, hairy structures. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky areas. Its fronds are usually dark green and can reach lengths of up to 1 meter.

The varietal name 'villosus' refers to the hairy texture of its foliage, a distinctive botanical feature.
