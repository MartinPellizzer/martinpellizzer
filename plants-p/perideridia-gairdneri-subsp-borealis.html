Perideridia gairdneri subsp. borealis

Perideridia gairdneri subsp.

borealis, commonly known as the northern pattypan, is a perennial herb native to the northwestern United States and southern Canada. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as coniferous forests and stream banks.

The plant has a distinctive underground rhizome system that helps it survive in cooler climates. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often have a hairy texture.

The flowers are small, white or pale green, and clustered in dense, terminal inflorescences.
