Paspalum canum

Paspalum canum, commonly known as the rigid grass or slender paspalum, is a perennial grass species native to the Americas.

It typically grows in wetland and marshy environments, thriving in moist to saturated soils. The plant has slender, erect stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets that are arranged in a panicle, producing small, two-lobed flowers.

Paspalum canum is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for wildlife.
