Pachyanthus glaber

Pachyanthus glaber, commonly known as the velvet tamarind, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, dark bark and compound leaves with elongated, lance-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or greenish-yellow in color. Its fruit is a woody pod containing several flat, edible seeds.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in local medicine and cuisine.
