Polygonum aviculare subsp. aequale

Polygonum aviculare subsp.

aequale, commonly known as common knotgrass, is a low-growing perennial plant belonging to the buckwheat family, Polygonaceae. It is characterized by its prostrate or ascending stems that often root at the nodes, forming dense mats.

The leaves are ovate to elliptic, with a distinctive reddish or purple coloration on the underside, and the plant produces small, clustered flowers in late spring to early summer. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in lawns, roadsides, and waste areas.

This species is known for its rapid growth and ability to spread vegetatively, making it both a weed and a pioneer species in ecological succession.
