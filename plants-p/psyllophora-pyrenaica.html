Psyllophora pyrenaica

Psyllophora pyrenaica, commonly known as the Pyrenean psyllid, is a small, sap-sucking insect belonging to the family Psyllidae.

It is native to the Pyrenees mountain range and is often found on specific host plants such as Erica and Calluna. The plant's botanical significance lies in its role as a host for this insect, which can impact the health of the vegetation. Its presence is an indicator of the ecological balance in its native habitat.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a species of conservation concern.
