Potamogeton pectinatus f. ramulosus

Potamogeton pectinatus f. ramulosus, commonly known as the ramose pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater habitats across Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, narrow leaves that are often arranged in whorls. The plant produces tiny, inconspicuous flowers that are typically borne in clusters near the base of the stems. It thrives in slow-moving or still waters with muddy or sandy substrates.

This species plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
