Ptychosperma hospitum

Ptychosperma hospitum, commonly known as the giant pandanus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Pandanaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including parts of Indonesia and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feather-like leaves that can grow up to several meters in length. It produces small, clustered yellow flowers that are often followed by woody fruits containing seeds.

Ptychosperma hospitum is often used in traditional crafts and as a ornamental plant due to its striking appearance.
