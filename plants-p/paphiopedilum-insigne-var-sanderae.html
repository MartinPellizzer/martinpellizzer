Paphiopedilum insigne var. sanderae

Paphiopedilum insigne var. sanderae, commonly known as the Sanderae Paphiopedilum, is a rare and striking orchid native to the mountainous regions of Borneo.

It is characterized by its large, velvety petals and a unique, intricate lip that resembles a pouch, a feature typical of the orchid family. The plant typically grows as a terrestrial orchid, with a pseudobulb that stores water and nutrients. Its flowers are usually a mix of yellow, green, and red hues, making it highly sought after by orchid enthusiasts.

Due to its rarity and habitat-specific requirements, it is often cultivated in specialized greenhouses to ensure its survival.
