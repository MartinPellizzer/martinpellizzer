Pseudolaelia irwiniana

Pseudolaelia irwiniana, commonly known as the Irwin's orchid, is a flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that come in various colors, including purple, pink, and white. The plant typically grows in high-altitude environments, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to anchor it in rocky or mountainous terrain.

Pseudolaelia irwiniana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
