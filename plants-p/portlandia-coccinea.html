Portlandia coccinea

Portlandia coccinea, commonly known as the scarlet bellflower, is a strikingly colorful plant native to the southwestern United States.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range from red to orange, often with a yellow throat, making it a standout in gardens. The plant is a perennial herb with a fibrous root system and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, preferring arid to semi-arid environments.

Its unique floral structure and vibrant colors make it a valuable addition to xeriscapic landscaping.
