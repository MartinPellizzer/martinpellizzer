Polygonum metzianum

Polygonum metzianum, commonly known as the red polygonum, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in rocky or alpine environments. The plant produces showy, clustered inflorescences with bright red bracts that are often mistaken for flowers. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.

Polygonum metzianum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking color.
