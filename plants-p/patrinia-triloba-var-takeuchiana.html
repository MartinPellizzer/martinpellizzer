Patrinia triloba var. takeuchiana

Patrinia triloba var. takeuchiana, commonly known as Japanese patrinia, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and have a slightly serrated edge. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it a suitable choice for woodland gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its attractive foliage and delicate floral display.
