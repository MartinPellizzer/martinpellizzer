Prunus sativa proles rosiflora

Prunus sativa proles rosiflora, commonly known as the rose-flowered almond.

This plant is a variant of the almond tree, characterized by its ornamental rose-like flowers that bloom in early spring. It is a deciduous shrub or small tree with alternate, ovate leaves that turn yellow in autumn. The flowers are typically pink or white, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Its fruit is a drupe, containing a single seed enclosed in a hard shell.
