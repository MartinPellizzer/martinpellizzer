Platanus lobata

Platanus lobata, commonly known as the California sycamore, is a deciduous tree native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its large, palmate leaves that turn yellow in the fall. The tree produces distinctive, spherical fruit clusters that contain small, winged seeds. Its bark is thick, flaky, and often has a mottled appearance.

Platanus lobata is adapted to a variety of soil types and is often found in riparian zones and along riverbanks.
