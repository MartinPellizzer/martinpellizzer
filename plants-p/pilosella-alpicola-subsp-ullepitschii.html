Pilosella alpicola subsp. ullepitschii

Pilosella alpicola subsp.

ullepitschii, commonly known as the alpine pilosella, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dense, fluffy white or pale yellow flower heads.

The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils at high altitudes. Its leaves are usually narrow and covered with fine hairs, giving the plant a soft, fuzzy appearance.

This subspecies is notable for its adaptability to harsh, cold climates and is often found in alpine meadows and slopes.
