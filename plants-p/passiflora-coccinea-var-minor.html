Passiflora coccinea var. minor

Passiflora coccinea var. minor, commonly known as the lesser passionflower, is a tropical vine native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking red and orange flowers, which are adorned with intricate patterns and feathery filaments. The plant produces small, yellow-orange berries that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped with a velvety texture, and it thrives in warm, humid climates with well-drained soil.

This variant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is a popular choice for gardens and greenhouses.
