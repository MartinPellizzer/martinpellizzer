Psittacoglossum

Psittacoglossum, commonly known as the "parrot's tongue," is a strikingly unique plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is a member of the Bromeliaceae family, which includes many epiphytic plants that thrive in humid, tropical environments. The plant is characterized by its vibrant, tongue-shaped flowers that come in a variety of colors, including red, yellow, and orange. Its foliage consists of long, narrow leaves that form a rosette, creating a water-holding structure typical of bromeliads.

Psittacoglossum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is well-suited for indoor or greenhouse growing conditions.
