Phyllostachys kumasasa

Phyllostachys kumasasa, commonly known as the Japanese bamboo or black bamboo, is a species of grass in the grass family Poaceae.

It is native to eastern Asia, particularly Japan, and is characterized by its dark green to blackish culms that are often striped with lighter coloration. The plant typically grows as a clump-forming bamboo, reaching heights of up to 10 meters, with slender, hollow stems. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a glossy surface, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in the spring.

This bamboo is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic and structural qualities.
