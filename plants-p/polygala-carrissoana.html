Polygala carrissoana

Polygala carrissoana, commonly known as the Carrissoan milkweed, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 feet, featuring slender, branching stems covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually pale yellow to greenish-white in color, with five narrow petals. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

The species is valued for its ecological role, providing nectar for native pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
