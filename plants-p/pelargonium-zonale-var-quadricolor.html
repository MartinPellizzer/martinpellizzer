Pelargonium zonale var. quadricolor

Pelargonium zonale var. quadricolor, commonly known as the four-color geranium, is a striking ornamental plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its vibrant, multicolored flowers that typically display combinations of red, yellow, white, and purple. The plant features heart-shaped, dark green leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Its compact growth habit makes it a popular choice for containers, hanging baskets, and garden borders.
