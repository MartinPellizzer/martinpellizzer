Piper chalhuapuquianum

Piper chalhuapuquianum, commonly known as the Chalhuapuquian pepper, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly in Ecuador and Peru. The plant is characterized by its climbing habit and heart-shaped leaves with a rough texture. Its small, greenish-yellow flowers are followed by elongated, peppery fruits that are used in traditional medicine.

The botanical features of this plant make it an important species in the study of Piperaceae diversity.
