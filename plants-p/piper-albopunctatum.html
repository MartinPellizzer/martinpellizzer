Piper albopunctatum

Piper albopunctatum, commonly known as the white-spotted pepper plant, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its slender, climbing vines and small, heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive white or pale green spots. Its flowers are unisexual, with separate male and female flowers on the same plant, and it produces small, round berries that are typically green when mature.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its unique foliage.
