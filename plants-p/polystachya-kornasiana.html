Polystachya kornasiana

Polystachya kornasiana, commonly known as the Kornasian spikegrass, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like appearance with elongated, narrow leaves and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its inflorescence consists of multiple racemes, which are often adorned with tiny, fragrant blooms.

Due to its unique morphology and habitat preferences, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists alike.
