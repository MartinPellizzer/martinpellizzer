Passerina pubescens var. elliptica

Passerina pubescens var. elliptica, commonly known as the elliptical pasqueflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its showy, early spring blooms. The plant features hairy stems and leaves that are elliptical in shape, giving it its botanical name. It thrives in alpine environments and is adapted to cold climates with short growing seasons.

The flowers are typically bright yellow or orange, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
