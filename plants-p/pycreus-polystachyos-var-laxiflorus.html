Pycreus polystachyos var. laxiflorus

Pycreus polystachyos var. laxiflorus, commonly known as the looseflower pycreus, is a variant of the pycreus plant characterized by its loosely arranged floral clusters.

This plant belongs to the family Asteraceae and is native to specific regions in the Mediterranean. It typically grows as a herbaceous perennial, featuring narrow, grass-like leaves and small, daisy-like flowers. The looseflower variety is distinguished by its more open inflorescence compared to other variants of the species.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.
