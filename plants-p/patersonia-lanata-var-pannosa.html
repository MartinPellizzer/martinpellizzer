Patersonia lanata var. pannosa

Patersonia lanata var. pannosa, commonly known as the woolly patersonia, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and striking blue or purple flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or heathland environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It is a herbaceous perennial that attracts pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
