Phyllanthus singampattianus

Phyllanthus singampattianus, commonly known as the Singampatti phyllanthus, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Western Ghats of India.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit. The plant features small, elliptical leaves that are glossy and often covered in fine hairs. It produces yellowish-green flowers in clusters, followed by small, fleshy fruits that are edible.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
