Pilea

Pilea, commonly known as the Chinese money plant, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Araceae.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that are often round or shield-like in appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually green or white in color. Pilea species are native to Asia, Africa, and the Pacific Islands, and many are popular as indoor houseplants due to their ease of care.

They thrive in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making them suitable for a variety of growing conditions.
