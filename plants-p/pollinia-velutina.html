Pollinia velutina

Pollinia velutina, commonly known as the velvet spurge, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Euphorbiaceae.

It is characterized by its distinctive, fleshy, and often hairy structures called pollinia, which are specialized for seed dispersal. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are typically clustered in cyathia, a unique floral structure typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. Pollinia velutina is native to tropical and subtropical regions and thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for study in plant morphology and pollination mechanisms.
