Paeonia delavayi

Paeonia delavayi, commonly known as the Yunnan peony, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of western China.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from white to deep pink, often with intricate patterns. The plant produces palmately lobed leaves and has a woody root system typical of the Paeonia genus. It thrives in cool, humid climates and is valued for its ornamental beauty and medicinal properties.

Paeonia delavayi is also noted for its ability to produce a significant number of seeds, contributing to its natural propagation in the wild.
