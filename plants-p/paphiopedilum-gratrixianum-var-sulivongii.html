Paphiopedilum gratrixianum var. sulivongii

Paphiopedilum gratrixianum var. sulivongii, commonly known as the Sulivongii Lady's Slipper, is a rare and exquisite orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety petals and a unique slipper-shaped lip that is a hallmark of the Paphiopedilum genus. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest undergrowth where it relies on specific mycorrhizal fungi for nutrient uptake. Its flowers, which range in color from white to pale pink, bloom in late spring or early summer, adding a touch of elegance to its natural habitat.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is a sought-after species among orchid enthusiasts and conservationists alike.
