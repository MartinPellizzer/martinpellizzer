Protium octandrum

Protium octandrum, commonly known as the eight-angled tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Burseraceae and is characterized by its smooth, greyish-brown bark and long, elliptical leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by woody, spiny fruits containing aromatic seeds. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties.

The plant is also valued for its durable wood, which is used in construction and furniture-making.
