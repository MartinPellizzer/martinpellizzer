Platonia esculenta

Platonia esculenta, commonly known as the African breadfruit, is a tropical tree native to Africa.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves. The tree produces edible fruits that are rich in nutrients and have a starchy texture similar to bread. The fruit is typically harvested when it is fully mature and has a yellowish color.

It is often used in traditional cuisines and has potential for cultivation as a food crop.
