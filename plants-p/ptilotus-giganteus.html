Ptilotus giganteus

Ptilotus giganteus, commonly known as the giant spinifex, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is a member of the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit. The plant produces bright red or orange flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting various pollinators. Its leaves are small, narrow, and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in arid conditions.

Ptilotus giganteus is well adapted to sandy soils and is often found in coastal and inland dunes.
