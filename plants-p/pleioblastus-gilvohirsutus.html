Pleioblastus gilvohirsutus

Pleioblastus gilvohirsutus, commonly known as the golden-hairy bamboo, is a species of grass bamboo native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in panicles. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and landscapes for its delicate appearance and resilience.
