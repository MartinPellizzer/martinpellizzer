Pyrus lui

Pyrus lui, commonly known as the Chinese white pear, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and broad, ovate leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers in spring, which are followed by edible, sweet fruits in late summer. Pyrus lui is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic and culinary uses.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.
