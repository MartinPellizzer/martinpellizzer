Prenanthes serpentaria f. simplicifolia

Prenanthes serpentaria f. simplicifolia, commonly known as the simple-leaved ironweed, is a herbaceous perennial native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its upright stems and clusters of small, pale yellow to white flowers. The leaves are simple, lance-shaped, and often have a slightly hairy texture. It typically grows in moist, wooded areas and thrives in partial to full shade.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
