Piper baccans var. sancarlosianum

Piper baccans var. sancarlosianum, commonly known as the San Carlos pepper plant, is a variant of the Piper baccans species native to specific regions of South America.

It is a climbing vine characterized by its dark green, heart-shaped leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces small, berry-like fruits that are typically green when unripe and turn red when mature. These fruits are often used in traditional medicine and for their mild peppery flavor.

Its botanical features make it a unique subject of study in plant taxonomy and ethnobotany.
