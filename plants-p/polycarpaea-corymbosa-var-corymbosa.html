Polycarpaea corymbosa var. corymbosa

Polycarpaea corymbosa var. corymbosa, commonly known as the clustered polycarpaea, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, clustered inflorescences that bear small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or perennial herb, often found in moist, shaded environments. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and have a rough texture with serrated edges.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
