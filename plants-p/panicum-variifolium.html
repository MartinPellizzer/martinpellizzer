Panicum variifolium

Panicum variifolium, commonly known as variable panicgrass, is a species of grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its varied leaf morphology, with blades that can be either flat or folded, and a range of colors from green to reddish-brown. The plant typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in wetlands, ditches, and along roadsides. Its inflorescence consists of a loose panicle with spikelets that are often reddish in color.

Panicum variifolium plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various insects and small animals.
