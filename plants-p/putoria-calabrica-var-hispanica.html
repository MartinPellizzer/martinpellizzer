Putoria calabrica var. hispanica

Putoria calabrica var. hispanica, commonly known as the Spanish rock fig, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its small, fleshy leaves and produces edible, fig-like fruits. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments. It has a compact growth habit and is often found in coastal and mountainous areas.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
