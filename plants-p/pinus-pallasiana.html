Pinus pallasiana

Pinus pallasiana, commonly known as the Japanese black pine, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to Japan, Korea, and parts of China.

It is characterized by its dark, fissured bark and needle-like leaves that are typically 5 to 7 centimeters long. The tree produces cones that are cylindrical and often remain attached to the branches for several years. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.

The species is also valued for its resilience to harsh environmental conditions.
