Panicum ihosyense

Panicum ihosyense, commonly known as the Ihosyense grass, is a species of flowering plant native to certain regions of Africa.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences that are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in open, sunny habitats and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
