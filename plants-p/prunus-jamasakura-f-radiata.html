Prunus jamasakura f. radiata

Prunus jamasakura f. radiata, commonly known as the Radiata Cherry Blossom, is a unique floral variant of the Japanese cherry blossom.

It is characterized by its profuse and extended blooming period, which can last up to three weeks. The plant features delicate, semi-double pink flowers that bloom on short pedicels, creating a dense, radiant display. It is native to Japan and thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil.

This cultivar is highly valued for its ornamental beauty and is often planted in gardens and public spaces.
