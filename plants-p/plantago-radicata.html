Plantago radicata

Plantago radicata, commonly known as broadleaf plantain, is a perennial herb native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It features upright, leafy stems with broad, heart-shaped leaves that are rough to the touch. The plant produces small, greenish flowers in dense clusters, which are typically wind-pollinated. Its leaves contain mucilage, making them useful in traditional medicine for soothing inflamed tissues.

It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.
