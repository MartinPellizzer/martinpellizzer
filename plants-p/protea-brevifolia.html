Protea brevifolia

Protea brevifolia, commonly known as the short-leaved protea, is a striking South African plant belonging to the Proteaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, spherical inflorescence composed of numerous small, colorful flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in shrubland and fynbos vegetation, thriving in well-drained, acidic soils. Its leaves are narrow and leathery, providing a contrasting texture to the showy floral display.

Protea brevifolia is well adapted to the Mediterranean climate of its native region, with a dormant summer period and active growth during the cooler months.
