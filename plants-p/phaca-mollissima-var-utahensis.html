Phaca mollissima var. utahensis

Phaca mollissima var. utahensis, commonly known as the Utah velvetleaf, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy leaves that give it a velvety texture. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in riparian zones.

Its botanical features make it an important component of its native ecosystem.
