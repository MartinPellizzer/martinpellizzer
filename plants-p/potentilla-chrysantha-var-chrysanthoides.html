Potentilla chrysantha var. chrysanthoides

Potentilla chrysantha var. chrysanthoides, commonly known as golden potentilla, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes. It has a compact, shrub-like growth form with upright stems and small, lance-shaped leaves.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance.
