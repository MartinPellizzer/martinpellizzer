Pilea deltoidea var. domingensis

Pilea deltoidea var. domingensis, commonly known as the Mexican stone plant, is a compact, low-growing perennial native to Mexico.

It features heart-shaped, waxy leaves that are often variegated with white or silver markings, giving it a striking appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers in early spring, which are followed by round, fleshy fruits. It thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Its unique foliage and ease of care have made it a favored ornamental plant among plant enthusiasts.
