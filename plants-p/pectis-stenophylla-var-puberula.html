Pectis stenophylla var. puberula

Pectis stenophylla var. puberula, commonly known as the narrow-leaf pectis, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, hairy leaves and produces yellow ray flowers with central disk flowers. It is often found in grasslands and rocky habitats, thriving in full sun.

Its botanical structure supports its role in local ecosystems, providing nectar for pollinators.
