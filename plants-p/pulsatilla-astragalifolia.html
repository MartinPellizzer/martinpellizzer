Pulsatilla astragalifolia

Pulsatilla astragalifolia, commonly known as the Astragalus pasqueflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its showy, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its leaves are palmately divided, and it produces small, yellowish fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Pulsatilla astragalifolia is noted for its early blooming habit, often appearing in spring before many other plants.
