Ptelea

Ptelea, commonly known as the American privet, is a genus of flowering plants in the olive family, Oleaceae.

It consists of several species, with Ptelea corymbosa being the most well-known. These plants are shrubs or small trees native to North America, particularly the western United States. They are characterized by their opposite, simple, and often aromatic leaves.

The flowers are small, greenish-yellow, and clustered in dense cymes, followed by small, woody fruits.
