Peperomia cobana

Peperomia cobana, commonly known as the Cobana peperomia, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It features thick, waxy leaves that are often variegated with shades of green, white, and pink, giving it a striking appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or red, which are followed by capsule fruits containing the seeds. It thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Due to its compact size and attractive foliage, it is often used as a decorative plant in both home and office environments.
