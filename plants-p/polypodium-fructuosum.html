Polypodium fructuosum

Polypodium fructuosum, commonly known as the fruiting polypody, is a fern native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, feathery fronds that are typically less than a meter in length. The plant produces small, clustered sporangia on the underside of its leaves, which are responsible for its reproductive cycle. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and rocky crevices.

Polypodium fructuosum is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
