Papaver alpinum subvar. degenii

Papaver alpinum subvar. degenii, commonly known as the alpine poppy, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, where it thrives in well-drained soils. Its leaves are usually dark green and slightly hairy, and the stems are erect and often branched.

This subvariety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
