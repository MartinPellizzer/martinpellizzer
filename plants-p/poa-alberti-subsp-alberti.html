Poa alberti subsp. alberti

Poa alberti subsp.

alberti, commonly known as the Albert's bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States. It is characterized by its fine, blue-green foliage and dense, tufted growth habit.

The plant typically thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands and disturbed sites. Its inflorescence consists of a spike of small, two-rowed spikelets, which are usually pale green to blue-gray in color.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in native plant landscaping.
