Peperomia yungasana

Peperomia yungasana, commonly known as the Yungasana peppergrass, is a compact, low-growing shrub native to the cloud forests of Bolivia.

It features thick, waxy leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern, providing moisture retention in its humid, high-altitude habitat. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often clustered in spadices, typical of the Piperaceae family. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is well-suited for indoor cultivation due to its tolerance of lower light conditions.

Peperomia yungasana is valued for its ornamental foliage and air-purifying qualities, making it a popular choice for indoor gardens.
