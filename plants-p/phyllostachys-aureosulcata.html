Phyllostachys aureosulcata

Phyllostachys aureosulcata, commonly known as the golden bamboo or yellow bamboo, is a fast-growing species native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its striking golden-yellow culms that contrast with its dark green leaves. The plant belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is a member of the Bambusoideae subfamily. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its aesthetic appeal.

Its growth habit is clump-forming, making it suitable for landscaping in gardens and parks.
