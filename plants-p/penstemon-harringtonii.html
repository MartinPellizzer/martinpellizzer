Penstemon harringtonii

Penstemon harringtonii, commonly known as Harrington's beardtongue, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces showy, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to purple. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to arid environments. It has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer.

Penstemon harringtonii is valued in gardens for its drought tolerance and ornamental appeal.
