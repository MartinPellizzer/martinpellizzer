Pinus larix

Pinus larix, commonly known as the larch, is a coniferous tree belonging to the pine family.

It is characterized by its needle-like leaves, which turn golden yellow and shed annually, making it unique among pines. The tree produces cones that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It typically grows in temperate regions and thrives in well-drained soils.

Its botanical features make it an important species in forest ecosystems and a valued ornamental plant.
