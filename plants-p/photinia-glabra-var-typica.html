Photinia glabra var. typica

Photinia glabra var. typica, commonly known as the glossy leaf photinia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, elliptical leaves that are glossy green on the upper surface and lighter on the underside. The plant produces small, white to pink flowers in spring, followed by red, fuzzy fruits that are attractive to birds. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types.

The species is also valued for its use in landscaping and as a hedge plant.
