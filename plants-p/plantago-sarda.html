Plantago sarda

Plantago sarda, commonly known as the Sardinian plantain, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in a spike, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in coastal areas and mountainous regions.

Due to its adaptability, it is considered a hardy and resilient species within its native habitat.
