Polypodium feuillei f. trilobum

Polypodium feuillei f. trilobum, commonly known as the three-lobed wall fern, is a fern species native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, leathery, and often lobed fronds. The fronds are typically upright and can grow up to 30 centimeters in length, with a rugged texture. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

This fern is valued for its ornamental appearance and is sometimes cultivated as a houseplant.
