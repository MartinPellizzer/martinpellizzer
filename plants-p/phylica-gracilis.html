Phylica gracilis

Phylica gracilis, commonly known as the slender phylica, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Proteaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, feathery foliage and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and thrives in coastal regions. Its flowers are usually pink or red, and they bloom in spring and summer.

Phylica gracilis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
