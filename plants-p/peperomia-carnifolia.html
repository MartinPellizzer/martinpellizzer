Peperomia carnifolia

Peperomia carnifolia, commonly known as the "bloodleaf" plant, is a member of the Piperaceae family and is native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, dark red to purple leaves that resemble bloodstains, hence its common name. The plant has a compact, bushy growth habit with thick, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery pubescence. It produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers that appear in clusters during the spring and summer months.

Peperomia carnifolia thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.
