Primula agleniana var. thearosa

Primula agleniana var. thearosa, commonly known as the Alaskan primrose, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Alaska and western Canada.

It is a perennial herb with hairy, basal leaves that form a rosette and stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped flowers. The flowers are typically pink or purple, with five petals and a distinctive throat that is often darker in color. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to bloom in early spring.
