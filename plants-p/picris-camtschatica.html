Picris camtschatica

Picris camtschatica, commonly known as the Siberian wallflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disc flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils and full sunlight. Its yellow flowers bloom in late summer, and the plant is known for its hardy nature and ability to withstand harsh climatic conditions.

It is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
