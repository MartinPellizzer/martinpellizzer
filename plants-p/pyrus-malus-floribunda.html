Pyrus malus floribunda

Pyrus malus floribunda, commonly known as the common whitebeam, is a deciduous tree native to Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its dense, rounded crown and clusters of small, white flowers that appear in early spring. The leaves are typically oval with serrated edges and turn a striking yellow or orange in the fall. It produces small, edible fruits known as serviceberries, which are favored by both wildlife and humans.

This plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
