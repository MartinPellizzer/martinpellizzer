Pinus flexilis var. macrocarpa

Pinus flexilis var. macrocarpa, commonly known as the sugar pine, is a variant of the sugar pine native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, straight trunk and long, slender needles that grow in bundles of five. The tree produces large, edible cones that contain winged seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Its bark is thick and scaly, providing protection against fire and pests.

This species plays an important ecological role in forest ecosystems, offering habitat and food for various wildlife species.
