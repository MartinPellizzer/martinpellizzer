Pteris walkeri

Pteris walkeri, commonly known as the walking fern, is a species of fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, upright fronds that resemble walking legs, giving it its common name. The plant has a rhizomatous growth habit, allowing it to spread and form dense mats. Its leaves are typically divided into numerous small, pointed segments.

Pteris walkeri thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.
