Primula alta

Primula alta, commonly known as the alpine primrose, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky habitats at high altitudes. The plant features rosettes of narrow, hairy leaves and produces clusters of bell-shaped, fragrant flowers in shades of white, yellow, and pink. It is a perennial species that thrives in cool, moist environments with well-drained soil.

Due to its hardiness and ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens with similar growing conditions.
