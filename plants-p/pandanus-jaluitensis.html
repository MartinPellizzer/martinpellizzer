Pandanus jaluitensis

Pandanus jaluitensis, commonly known as the Jaluit pandanus, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and long, narrow leaves that are often used in traditional crafts. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences that bear fragrant, white flowers. Its fruit, a spiky, fibrous structure, contains seeds that are dispersed by wind and water.

This species plays an important ecological and cultural role in its native habitats.
