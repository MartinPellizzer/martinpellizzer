Prestoea megalochlamys

Prestoea megalochlamys, commonly known as the giant yellowwood, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. The plant has a sturdy, straight trunk and can grow up to 30 meters tall, making it a significant component of forest ecosystems. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a smooth texture, while its wood is prized for its durability and aesthetic qualities.

The species plays an important role in maintaining biodiversity and soil stability in its native habitats.
