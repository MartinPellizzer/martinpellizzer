Piper caudatum

Piper caudatum, commonly known as the curly hemp or curly pepper, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is a member of the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species. The plant is characterized by its long, trailing stems and small, heart-shaped leaves that often curl at the edges. It produces small green flowers that develop into red, elongated berries.

The plant is cultivated for its ornamental value and has some traditional uses in herbal medicine.
