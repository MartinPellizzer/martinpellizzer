Primula Viscosa Var. Latifolia

Primula viscosa var. latifolia is a unique variant of the common primrose known for its broad, velvety leaves and clusters of delicate, fragrant flowers. Native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe, this plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in rocky or forested areas. Its striking appearance makes it a popular choice among gardeners and botanists alike. The plant's ability to adapt to various soil types and its low maintenance requirements contribute to its widespread cultivation. Despite its beauty, it remains relatively obscure compared to other primrose species.
