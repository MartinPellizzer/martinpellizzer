Polygala alphonsii

Polygala alphonsii, commonly known as the Alphonse milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the Andaman and Nicobar Islands.

It is a small, herbaceous shrub that typically grows in coastal and sandy environments. The plant features slender, branching stems and small, clustered flowers that are usually pink or white in color. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Polygala alphonsii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in tropical gardens.
