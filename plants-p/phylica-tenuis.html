Phylica tenuis

Phylica tenuis, commonly known as the narrowleaf phylica, is a small shrub native to the coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in summer. The plant is well adapted to sandy and rocky soils, often growing in dune environments. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it a hardy coastal species.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and nectar for local pollinators.
