Piper artanthe var. parvispicum

Piper artanthe var. parvispicum, commonly known as the small-eyed pepper vine, is a tropical flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its slender, climbing vines and small, heart-shaped leaves with a glossy surface. The plant produces clusters of tiny, greenish-white flowers that develop into small, round berries. These berries are typically yellow or red when mature and contain a single seed.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has a mild, peppery flavor, making it suitable for culinary use in some regions.
