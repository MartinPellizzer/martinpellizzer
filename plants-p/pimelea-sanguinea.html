Pimelea sanguinea

Pimelea sanguinea, commonly known as the crimson pimelea, is a flowering plant native to southern Australia.

It is a low-growing shrub that typically reaches heights of up to 30 centimeters. The plant produces small, bright red flowers in clusters during the spring and summer seasons. Its leaves are narrow and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Pimelea sanguinea is often found in sandy or gravelly soils in open habitats such as heathlands and coastal areas.
