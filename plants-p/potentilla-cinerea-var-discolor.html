Potentilla cinerea var. discolor

Potentilla cinerea var. discolor, commonly known as silver cinquefoil, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray, fuzzy leaves that provide a striking visual contrast to its yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and alpine environments, thriving in full sun. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The variety discolor is distinguished by its variegated leaves, which display a mix of white and green hues.
