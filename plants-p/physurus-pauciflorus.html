Physurus pauciflorus

Physurus pauciflorus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved daisy.

It is a flowering plant native to southern Africa, characterized by its small, daisy-like flowers and narrow, linear leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments. It produces yellow flowers with a central disc of dark brown or black florets, and it is known for its hardy, drought-resistant nature.

Physurus pauciflorus is often used in rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its low water requirements and ornamental value.
