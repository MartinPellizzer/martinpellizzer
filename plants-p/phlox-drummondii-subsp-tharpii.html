Phlox drummondii subsp. tharpii

Phlox drummondii subsp.

tharpii, commonly known as the desert phlox, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant, showy flowers that bloom in shades of red, pink, and white.

The plant typically grows in well-drained soils and thrives in full sun, making it well-suited for arid and semi-arid environments. Its leaves are narrow and linear, and the stems are often hairy, providing a distinctive appearance.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and drought tolerance, making it a popular choice in xeriscaping.
