Potentilla candicans f. crocea

Potentilla candicans f. crocea, commonly known as the yellow cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and rocky soils. It has palmately divided leaves and produces small, aggregate fruits.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and hardy nature.
