Polygonum leptocarpum

Polygonum leptocarpum, commonly known as slender fruit smartweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, which includes plants commonly known as buckwheats and sedges. The plant is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, greenish-white flowers that appear in clusters. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a distinctive reddish or purple tint on the underside.

Polygonum leptocarpum is often found in moist soils along stream banks and in wet meadows.
