Pueraria harmsii

Pueraria harmsii, commonly known as the climbing peanut vine, is a leguminous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vigorous climbing habit and trifoliate leaves with a velvety texture. The plant produces yellow flowers followed by pods containing edible seeds similar to those of the peanut. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Pueraria harmsii is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, which enriches the soil.
