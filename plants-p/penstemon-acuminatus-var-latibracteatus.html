Penstemon acuminatus var. latibracteatus

Penstemon acuminatus var. latibracteatus, commonly known as the broad-bract penstemon, is a herbaceous perennial native to the western United States.

It features upright stems covered in lance-shaped, blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically hairy and arranged in a whorled pattern around the stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its showy flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to ecological biodiversity.
