Plastobrassica

Plastobrassica, commonly known as the golden spire, is a unique flowering plant characterized by its striking yellow, spire-like inflorescences.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many familiar plants such as cabbages and mustards. The plant is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in well-drained soils with full sun exposure. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, while its flowers consist of four petals arranged in a cross shape, typical of the Brassicaceae family.

Plastobrassica is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
