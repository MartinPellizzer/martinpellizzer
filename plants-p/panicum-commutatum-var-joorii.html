Panicum commutatum var. joorii

Panicum commutatum var. joorii, commonly known as the Joori Panicgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit, with slender, bluish-green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces dense, spike-like inflorescences that are typically golden to reddish in color, adding ornamental value to its appearance. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for xeriscaping and erosion control.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for both ecological restoration and ornamental gardening in suitable climates.
