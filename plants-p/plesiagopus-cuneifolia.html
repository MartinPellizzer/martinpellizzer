Plesiagopus cuneifolia

Plesiagopus cuneifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf plesiagopus, is a striking member of the Acanthaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa, particularly found in countries like Kenya and Tanzania. The plant is characterized by its elongated, narrow leaves that are often covered in a fine, white powdery coating. Its flowers are typically bright red or orange, clustered in dense spikes, adding a vibrant visual element to its appearance.

It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.
