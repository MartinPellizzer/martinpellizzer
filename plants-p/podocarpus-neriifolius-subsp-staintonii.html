Podocarpus neriifolius subsp. staintonii

Podocarpus neriifolius subsp.

staintonii, commonly known as the Stainton's satinwood, is a species of evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its dark green, glossy leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a leathery texture.

The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by woody cones that contain edible, sweet-smelling seeds. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland forests.

The subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its timber.
