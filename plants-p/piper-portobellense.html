Piper portobellense

Piper portobellense, commonly known as the Portobello pepper plant, is a species of flowering plant in the Piperaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant produces small, green, spiky fruits that mature to a bright red color. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its unique appearance.

The leaves are compound, with elongated, pointed leaflets that give the plant a distinctive look.
