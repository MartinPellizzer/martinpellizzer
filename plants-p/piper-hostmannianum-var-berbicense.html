Piper hostmannianum var. berbicense

Piper hostmannianum var. berbicense, commonly known as the Berbice pepper, is a tropical vine native to South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its climbing habit and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are followed by elongated, thin fruits. The berries are typically harvested when they turn red, and they are used in traditional medicine and as a spice.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and agricultural contexts.
