Polygala sieboldiana

Polygala sieboldiana, commonly known as the Siebold's milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in woodland or shrubland habitats and prefers well-drained soil. Its flowers are usually pale green or yellowish, with a distinctive structure that resembles a small, elongated capsule.

Polygala sieboldiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and delicate blooms.
