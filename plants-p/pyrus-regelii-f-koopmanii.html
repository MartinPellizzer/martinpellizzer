Pyrus regelii f. koopmanii

Pyrus regelii f. koopmanii, commonly known as the Koopman's whitebeam, is a rare and distinctive horticultural form of the species Pyrus regelii.

It is characterized by its white-veined, ovate leaves that are often prominently marked with a pale green to white midrib. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, which are followed by edible, sweet-tart fruits. It is typically found in temperate regions and is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardy nature.

This cultivar is often cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and historical horticultural significance.
