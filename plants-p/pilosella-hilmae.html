Pilosella hilmae

Pilosella hilmae, commonly known as the Hilma's pearly everlasting, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features small, daisy-like flowers with white petals and a yellow center, blooming in the summer months. The plant has a compact rosette of silver-gray leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in alpine meadows and rocky habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and unique appearance, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
