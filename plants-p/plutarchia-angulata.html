Plutarchia angulata

Plutarchia angulata, commonly known as the angular leaf plant, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its distinctive angular leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery pubescence. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements, it is a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
