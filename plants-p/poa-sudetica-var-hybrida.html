Poa sudetica var. hybrida

Poa sudetica var. hybrida, commonly known as the Alpine meadow grass, is a variant of the Poa sudetica species native to mountainous regions of Central Europe.

It is a perennial grass characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, wiry stems. The plant typically produces small, spikelet-like inflorescences with pale green to yellowish flowers. It thrives in well-drained, nutrient-poor soils and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky habitats.

Its botanical features make it an important component of high-altitude ecosystems.
