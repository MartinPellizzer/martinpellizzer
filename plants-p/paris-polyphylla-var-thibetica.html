Paris polyphylla var. thibetica

Paris polyphylla var. thibetica, commonly known as the Tibetan buttercup, is a perennial herb native to the Himalayan regions of China, India, and Nepal.

It features a short rhizome and produces clusters of bright yellow flowers with five petals, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant is characterized by its palmate, deeply lobed leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in mountainous forests and meadows.

Due to its medicinal properties, it has been traditionally used in folk remedies for various ailments.
