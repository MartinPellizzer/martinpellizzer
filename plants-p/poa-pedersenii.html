Poa pedersenii

Poa pedersenii, commonly known as the Pedersen's bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and narrow, bluish-green leaves. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences that contain both male and female flowers, typical of the Poaceae family. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species in its native habitat.
