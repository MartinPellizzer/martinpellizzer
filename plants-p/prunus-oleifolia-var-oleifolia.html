Prunus oleifolia var. oleifolia

Prunus oleifolia var. oleifolia, commonly known as the oliveleaf cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually oval or elliptic in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by edible, sweet-tart drupes. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
