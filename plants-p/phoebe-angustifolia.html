Phoebe angustifolia

Phoebe angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf phoebe, is a species of flowering plant in the family Lauraceae.

It is native to the eastern United States, where it typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical in shape with a pointed tip. Its small, fragrant flowers are clustered in umbels and are followed by dark, shiny berries.

Phoebe angustifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
