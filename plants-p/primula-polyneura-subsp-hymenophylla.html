Primula polyneura subsp. hymenophylla

Primula polyneura subsp.

hymenophylla, commonly known as the silver-leaf primrose, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of southern China. It is characterized by its narrow, silvery-gray leaves that are covered in a dense layer of fine, fuzzy hairs.

The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, typically blooming in early spring. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in shaded forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and unique foliage, it is cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
