Potentilla multifida f. subpalmata

Potentilla multifida f. subpalmata, commonly known as the palmate potentilla, is a variant of the multifida potentilla, characterized by its distinctive palmate leaf structure.

It is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of central and eastern Europe. The leaves are typically divided into several lobes, giving the plant a delicate, fern-like appearance. It produces small, yellowish flowers in early summer, which are clustered in dense inflorescences.

This plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine displays.
