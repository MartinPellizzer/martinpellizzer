Pyrus tengyuehensis

Pyrus tengyuehensis, commonly known as the Tengyuehensis pear, is a species of deciduous tree native to China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves and clusters of small, white flowers. The tree produces small, round pomes that are typically yellow or brown when mature. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical significance in traditional Chinese horticulture.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
