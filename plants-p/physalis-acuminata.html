Physalis acuminata

Physalis acuminata, commonly known as the Cape gooseberry or golden berry, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes tomatoes and potatoes, and is characterized by its hairy stems and lobed leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into inflated, orange-red calyces enclosing bright orange berries. These berries are edible, sweet, and often used in culinary applications.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has some medicinal uses in traditional medicine.
