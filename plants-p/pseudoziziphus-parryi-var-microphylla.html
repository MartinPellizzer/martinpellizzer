Pseudoziziphus parryi var. microphylla

Pseudoziziphus parryi var. microphylla, commonly known as the small-leaved Indian fig, is a deciduous shrub native to the southwestern United States.

It features small, ovate leaves that are typically less than 2 centimeters in length, giving it its varietal name "microphylla.". The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late spring, followed by edible, red, fleshy berries. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, thriving in well-drained soils.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species in ecological studies and traditional uses by indigenous communities.
