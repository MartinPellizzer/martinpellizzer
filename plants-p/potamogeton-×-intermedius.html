Potamogeton × intermedius

Potamogeton × intermedius, commonly known as the intermediate pondweed, is a hybrid aquatic plant that exhibits characteristics of both its parent species, Potamogeton perfoliatus and Potamogeton praelatus.

It is a submerged herb that typically grows in freshwater environments such as lakes, ponds, and slow-moving rivers. The plant has long, slender stems that are often branched and bear small, narrow leaves arranged in a whorled pattern. Its flowers are usually unisexual, with male and female flowers occurring on separate plants, and it produces small, inconspicuous fruits.

Potamogeton × intermedius plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
