Podocarpus gnidioides

Podocarpus gnidioides, commonly known as the Chinese podocarp, is a small evergreen tree native to southern China and Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Podocarpaceae family and is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and small, cone-like fruits. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in whorls around the stem. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and used in traditional medicine in some regions.
