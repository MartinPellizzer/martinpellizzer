Paullinia allenii

Paullinia allenii, commonly known as the Allen's soursop, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to the Caribbean and northern South America.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and fragrant, white flowers. The plant produces a fruit similar to a soursop, with a spiky exterior and a soft, creamy interior. Its botanical significance lies in its unique chemical composition, including high levels of antioxidants and alkaloids.

Paullinia allenii is also valued for its ornamental appeal and potential medicinal properties.
