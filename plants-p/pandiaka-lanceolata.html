Pandiaka lanceolata

Pandiaka lanceolata, commonly known as the lanceleaf pandiaka, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves, which are typically dark green and have a smooth or slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually white or pale yellow, often blooming during the warmer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories or along stream banks.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
