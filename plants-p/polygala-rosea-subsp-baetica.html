Polygala rosea subsp. baetica

Polygala rosea subsp.

baetica, commonly known as the Baetica milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula. It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its delicate, pinkish-white flowers arranged in clusters.

The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and has small, narrow leaves. It is known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species in some regions.
