Philodendron latipes

Philodendron latipes, commonly known as the creeping philodendron, is a tropical climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It features heart-shaped, dark green leaves that are often variegated with lighter green or white markings. The plant has a trailing growth habit, making it suitable for hanging baskets or shelves. Its stems can climb or trail, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by red berries.

It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires consistent moisture and humidity for optimal growth.
