Phoebe siamensis

Phoebe siamensis, commonly known as the Siam phoebe, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels and avocados. The plant is characterized by its evergreen foliage and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. Its leaves are typically elliptical with a glossy surface and a distinctive aromatic scent.

Phoebe siamensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to tropical and subtropical climates.
