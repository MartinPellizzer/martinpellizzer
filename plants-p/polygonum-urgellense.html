Polygonum urgellense

Polygonum urgellense, commonly known as the Urgellense knotweed, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, which includes plants known for their distinctive arrow-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, rocky habitats and features slender stems with reddish-brown hairs. Its leaves are lanceolate and often have a reddish tinge, while the flowers are small and clustered in inflorescences.

Polygonum urgellense is characterized by its ability to thrive in harsh alpine environments.
