Pygeum kingianum

Pygeum kingianum, commonly known as the African wild almond, is a small to medium-sized tree native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Proteaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, pinkish-white flowers. The tree produces small, hard, angular fruits that contain several seeds. Its bark is thick and fissured, often exuding a reddish sap when cut.

The plant is adapted to nutrient-poor soils and is often found in dry, coastal or mountainous regions.
