Paesia coriifolia

Paesia coriifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf holly or holly fern, is a perennial plant belonging to the family Berberidaceae.

It is characterized by its evergreen, leathery leaves that are typically broad and elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in spring, followed by red berries that are edible for birds. Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardeners seeking a hardy, low-maintenance plant.
