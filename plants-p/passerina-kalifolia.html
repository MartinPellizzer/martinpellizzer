Passerina kalifolia

Passerina kalifolia, commonly known as the California pasqueflower, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its showy, early spring blooms. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and has simple, trifoliate leaves. It produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in a dense head.
