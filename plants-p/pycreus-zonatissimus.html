Pycreus zonatissimus

Pycreus zonatissimus, commonly known as the zonated pycreus, is a unique member of the Arecaceae family, native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and pinnately compound leaves that are often adorned with distinct zonation patterns. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically yellow or white, followed by elongated, cylindrical fruits. Its growth habit makes it suitable for both ornamental and horticultural purposes in warm climates.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is relatively rare in the wild and is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
