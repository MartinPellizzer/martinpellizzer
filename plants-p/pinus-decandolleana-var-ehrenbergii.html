Pinus decandolleana var. ehrenbergii

Pinus decandolleana var. ehrenbergii, commonly known as the Ehrenberg's silver fir, is a variant of the Decandollean fir native to the high-altitude regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, conical shape and dark green, needle-like leaves that are typically arranged in whorls. The tree produces distinctive cones that are often reddish-brown and contain edible seeds. It thrives in cool, moist climates and is often found in mountainous forest ecosystems.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance in its native habitat.
