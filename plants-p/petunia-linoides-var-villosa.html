Petunia linoides var. villosa

Petunia linoides var. villosa, commonly known as the woolly petunia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive, fuzzy appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically violet or blue in color. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
