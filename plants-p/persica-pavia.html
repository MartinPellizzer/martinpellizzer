Persica pavia

Persica pavia, commonly known as the Pavia peach, is a species of flowering plant in the Prunus genus.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and is characterized by its showy pink or white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, yellowish fruits that are typically not edible for humans but are used in traditional medicine. Persica pavia thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
