Pescatoria lalindei

Pescatoria lalindei, commonly known as the silverleaf orchid, is a rare flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and clusters of small, pale greenish-white flowers. The plant possesses a fibrous root system that helps it survive in nutrient-poor soils. Its leaves are narrow and covered in a fine, silvery down, which aids in reflecting sunlight and reducing water loss.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species.
