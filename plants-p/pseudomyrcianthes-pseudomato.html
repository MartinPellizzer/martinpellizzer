Pseudomyrcianthes pseudomato

Pseudomyrcianthes pseudomato, commonly known as the Brazilian peppertree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Amazon rainforest.

It belongs to the family Myrtaceae and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and small, fragrant white flowers. The tree produces woody, orange-red fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by birds and mammals. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland forests and along riverbanks.

Due to its rapid growth and invasive tendencies, it is considered a problematic species in some regions.
