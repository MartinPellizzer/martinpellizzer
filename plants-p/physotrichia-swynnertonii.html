Physotrichia swynnertonii

Physotrichia swynnertonii, commonly known as Swynnerton's grass, is a flowering plant species native to Africa.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, wiry stems. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides and savannas.

Due to its limited distribution and specific ecological requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
