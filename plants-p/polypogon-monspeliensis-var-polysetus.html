Polypogon monspeliensis var. polysetus

Polypogon monspeliensis var. polysetus, commonly known as the broadleaf panicgrass, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its erect, grass-like growth habit and narrow, blade-like leaves that are typically hairy along the edges. The plant produces dense clusters of small, pale green to brownish flowers, which are arranged in spikelets. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds.

Its botanical features make it an important species for wetland ecosystems and a valuable plant for ecological restoration projects.
