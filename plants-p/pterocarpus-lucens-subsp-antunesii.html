Pterocarpus lucens subsp. antunesii

Pterocarpus lucens subsp.

antunesii, commonly known as the Malabar ironwood, is a deciduous tree native to the Western Ghats of India. It is characterized by its dark, dense wood that is highly valued for its strength and durability.

The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, fragrant yellow flowers. Its fruit is a woody pod containing several flat, elongated seeds.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in tropical rainforests.
