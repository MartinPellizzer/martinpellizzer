Potamogeton intramongolicus

Potamogeton intramongolicus, commonly known as the Mongolian pondweed, is a freshwater aquatic plant native to parts of Asia, particularly Mongolia and China.

It is characterized by its long, ribbon-like leaves that are typically submerged in water, and it produces small, green flowers that emerge from the water surface. This species is often found in shallow lakes, ponds, and slow-moving rivers, where it forms dense underwater vegetation. Potamogeton intramongolicus plays a vital role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various water organisms.
