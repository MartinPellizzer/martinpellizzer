Pouteria pubescens

Pouteria pubescens, commonly known as the Brazilian peach or yellow sapote, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and yellow, fleshy fruits with a sweet, creamy pulp. The plant produces small, white flowers that are often clustered together, and its bark is rough and fissured.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and is valued both for its edible fruit and its ornamental appeal.
