Panax nossibiensis

Panax nossibiensis, commonly known as the Brazilian ginseng, is a rare and endangered plant native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It belongs to the Araliaceae family and is characterized by its woody root system and compound leaves. The plant is valued for its medicinal properties, with roots containing compounds believed to have adaptogenic and immunostimulant effects. Due to habitat loss and overharvesting, it is critically endangered and protected under Brazilian law.

Its conservation is essential for maintaining biodiversity in the region.
