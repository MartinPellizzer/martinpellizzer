Prunus jamasakura f. profusa

Prunus jamasakura f. profusa, commonly known as the Japanese cherry blossom variant, is a cultivar of the Prunus jamasakura species.

It is characterized by its profuse flowering, with clusters of small, white-pink flowers appearing in early spring. The plant is a deciduous shrub or small tree, typically reaching heights of 3 to 5 meters. Its leaves are ovate with a serrated margin, and they turn a vibrant yellow in autumn.

This cultivar is prized for its ornamental value and is often planted in gardens and public spaces for its aesthetic appeal.
