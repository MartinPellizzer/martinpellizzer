Protea burchellii

Protea burchellii, commonly known as the Burchell's protea, is a striking South African plant belonging to the Proteaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescences composed of numerous tubular flowers arranged in a spherical or conical shape. The plant typically grows in coastal and mountainous regions, thriving in well-drained, sandy soils. Its flowers come in shades of white, pink, and red, often with a distinctive yellow or orange center.

Protea burchellii is well adapted to fire-prone environments, with a lignotuber allowing it to regenerate after bushfires.
