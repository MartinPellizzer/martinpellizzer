Paraphlomis lancidentata

Paraphlomis lancidentata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved blue flag, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of southern China.

It belongs to the family Lamiaceae, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a bluish-green hue and produces small, tubular flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is cultivated in some horticultural settings.
