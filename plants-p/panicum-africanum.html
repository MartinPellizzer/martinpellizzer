Panicum africanum

Panicum africanum, commonly known as African millet, is a grass species native to Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and small, bristly spikelets that contain its seeds. The plant is drought-tolerant and thrives in poor soil conditions, making it a hardy crop in arid regions. Its seeds are often used as a staple food in various African countries.

The plant also plays a role in traditional agriculture and can be used for thatching or livestock feed.
