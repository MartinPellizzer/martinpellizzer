Prenanthes purpurea var. querciformis

Prenanthes purpurea var. querciformis, commonly known as the oak-leaved water hemp, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It features heart-shaped to oak-like leaves that are typically lobed and have a rough texture. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters, which are borne on slender, branching stems. It thrives in moist environments such as wetlands and along stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species in ecological studies of wetland flora.
