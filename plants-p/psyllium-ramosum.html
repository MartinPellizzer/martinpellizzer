Psyllium ramosum

Psyllium ramosum, commonly known as the common psyllium, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the family Plantaginaceae and is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant produces a mucilaginous substance from its seeds, which is widely used in herbal remedies and as a dietary fiber supplement. Its growth is typically annual, and it thrives in well-drained soils with moderate moisture.

Psyllium ramosum is valued for its ability to improve digestive health and its role in traditional medicine.
