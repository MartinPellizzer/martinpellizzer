Pieris cubensis

Pieris cubensis, commonly known as the Cuban rhododendron, is a species of evergreen shrub native to the Caribbean, particularly Cuba and the Bahamas.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, leathery foliage and showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, often in coastal forests or rocky soils. Its leaves are elliptical with a pointed tip and are usually dark green, while its flowers range in color from white to pink.

Pieris cubensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens in tropical and subtropical regions.
