Piptatherum flavescens

Piptatherum flavescens, commonly known as yellow foxtail, is a grass species native to North America.

It is characterized by its fine, yellowish-green blades and produces slender, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in open areas, such as fields and roadsides, and thrives in full sun. It has a rhizomatous growth habit, allowing it to spread vegetatively.

The seeds are small and wind-dispersed, contributing to its ability to colonize new areas.
