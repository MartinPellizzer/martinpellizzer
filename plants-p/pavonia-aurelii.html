Pavonia aurelii

Pavonia aurelii, commonly known as the golden pavonia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its showy, bright yellow to orange-red bracts that surround its small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows as an erect herb with lance-shaped leaves and can reach heights of up to 2 meters. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Pavonia aurelii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
