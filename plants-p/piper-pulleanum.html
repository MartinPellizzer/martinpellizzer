Piper pulleanum

Piper pulleanum, commonly known as the Indian pepper vine, is a climbing shrub native to the tropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper plants such as black pepper. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are followed by three-lobed, berry-like fruits. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped with a rough texture and a strong, pungent aroma.

The plant is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
