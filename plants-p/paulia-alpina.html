Paulia alpina

Paulia alpina, commonly known as the alpine paulia, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a shrub that typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high elevations. The plant produces small, fragrant white or pink flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often covered in a fine pubescence.

Paulia alpina is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with well-drained soil.
