Pittosporum abyssinicum

Pittosporum abyssinicum, commonly known as the Abyssinian pittosporum, is a shrub or small tree native to Ethiopia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, dark green leaves and clusters of fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, red, berry-like fruits that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans. It thrives in well-drained soil and is often found in forested areas at moderate elevations.

Pittosporum abyssinicum is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
