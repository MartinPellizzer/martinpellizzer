Puccinellia kamtschatica

Puccinellia kamtschatica, commonly known as Kamtschatic grass, is a perennial grass species native to cold and temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats, such as marshes, wet meadows, and along riverbanks. Its inflorescence consists of a dense spikelet cluster, and it produces small, wind-pollinated flowers.

Puccinellia kamtschatica is often found in nutrient-poor soils and is adapted to withstand harsh environmental conditions.
