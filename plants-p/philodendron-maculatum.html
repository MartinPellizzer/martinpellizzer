Philodendron maculatum

Philodendron maculatum, commonly known as the Variegated Philodendron, is a climbing tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive variegated pattern of light green and white, often with a marbled appearance. The plant has aerial roots that help it climb and anchor itself to supports. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires consistently moist, well-draining soil.

Philodendron maculatum is popular as a houseplant due to its attractive foliage and relatively easy care requirements.
