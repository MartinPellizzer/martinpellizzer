Papaver rubroaurantiacum subsp. changaicum

Papaver rubroaurantiacum subsp.

changaicum, commonly known as the Changai poppy, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of northern China. It belongs to the family Papaveraceae and is characterized by its vibrant red and orange petals.

The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving at high altitudes. Its flowers are short-lived, often opening in the morning and closing by midday.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
