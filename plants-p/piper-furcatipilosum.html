Piper furcatipilosum

Piper furcatipilosum, commonly known as the forked-hair pepper, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil and Peru. The plant is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and small, opposite leaves with a slightly hairy texture. Its flowers are unisexual, with separate male and female flowers on the same plant, and it produces small, round berries.

The plant is valued for its aromatic leaves and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
