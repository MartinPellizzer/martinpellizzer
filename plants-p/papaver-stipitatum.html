Papaver stipitatum

Papaver stipitatum, commonly known as the stipitate poppy, is a species of flowering plant in the family Papaveraceae.

It is characterized by its striking, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from red to orange, often with a contrasting central stigma. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and is native to regions of southern Europe and western Asia. Its leaves are deeply lobed and have a distinctive stipitate base, giving it its common name.

Papaver stipitatum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also found in wildflower meadows.
