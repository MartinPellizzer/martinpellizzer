Primula deleiensis

Primula deleiensis, commonly known as the Khabarovsk primrose, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Far East of Russia.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or mountain meadows. It has lance-shaped leaves with a hairy texture and produces clusters of flowers in early spring.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
