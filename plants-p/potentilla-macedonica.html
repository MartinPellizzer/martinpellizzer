Potentilla macedonica

Potentilla macedonica, commonly known as the Macedonian potentilla, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Balkan region.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit with dense, sprawling stems. The plant produces small, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets, and it thrives in well-drained soils.

Potentilla macedonica is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in sunny, dry conditions.
