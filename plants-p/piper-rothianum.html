Piper rothianum

Piper rothianum, commonly known as the Java pepper or long pepper, is a flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly Indonesia, and thrives in tropical climates. The plant produces small, elongated berries that are used in traditional medicine and as a spice. Its leaves are often used in local cuisines for their aromatic properties.

The plant's botanical features include compound leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into the characteristic peppercorns.
