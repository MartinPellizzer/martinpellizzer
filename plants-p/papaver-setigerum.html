Papaver setigerum

Papaver setigerum, commonly known as the Persian poppy, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its striking red or orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has smooth, green, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Papaver setigerum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
