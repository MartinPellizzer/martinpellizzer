Potamochloa retzii

Potamochloa retzii, commonly known as the rivergrass or water reed, is a perennial grass species native to wetland and riparian habitats.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth and long, narrow leaves that are often submerged or floating. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers. It thrives in shallow water and is often found along riverbanks and in marshy areas.

Potamochloa retzii plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for aquatic organisms.
