Ptilotus spicatus var. leianthus

Ptilotus spicatus var. leianthus, commonly known as the "blue mistflower," is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has hairy, branching stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal areas.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long-lasting blooms and drought tolerance.
