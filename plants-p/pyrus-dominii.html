Pyrus dominii

Pyrus dominii, commonly known as the Mexican whitebeam, is a deciduous tree native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and alternate, simple leaves that are elliptical in shape. The tree produces small, white flowers in clusters, which are followed by small, round berries. It is often found in rocky soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

The plant has limited horticultural use but is of interest for its ecological role in native habitats.
