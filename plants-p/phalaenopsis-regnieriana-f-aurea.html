Phalaenopsis regnieriana f. aurea

Phalaenopsis regnieriana f. aurea, commonly known as the golden phalaenopsis orchid, is a hybrid orchid that exhibits striking golden-yellow floral hues.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia, where it typically grows as an epiphyte on trees. The plant features long, narrow leaves and produces clusters of fragrant, showy flowers that bloom repeatedly throughout the year. Its botanical characteristics include a pseudobulb structure and roots that are adapted for absorbing moisture from the air.

This variety is highly prized in horticulture for its aesthetic appeal and ease of cultivation.
