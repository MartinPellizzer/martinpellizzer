Pleioblastus hatsusimanus

Pleioblastus hatsusimanus, commonly known as the Shikoku bamboo, is a species of grass belonging to the Poaceae family.

It is native to Japan, specifically the Shikoku region, and is characterized by its slender, erect culms and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as mountain forests and stream sides. Its foliage is often a vibrant green, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in early summer.

This bamboo is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in horticulture and landscaping.
