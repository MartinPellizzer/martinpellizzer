Pterospartum scolopendrium

Pterospartum scolopendrium, commonly known as the Brazilian flame tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and striking, fiery orange to red flowers. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Its ability to fix nitrogen makes it beneficial for soil enrichment in ecological settings.
