Pterostylis jacksonii

Pterostylis jacksonii, commonly known as Jackson's duck orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its small, greenish-yellow flower spike that typically blooms in late winter to early spring. The plant has a short, leafless stem with a single, narrow ovary that develops into a capsule containing numerous tiny seeds. It thrives in shaded, moist environments such as rainforests and understoreys.

The species is notable for its unique floral structure and is part of the orchid family, Orchidaceae.
