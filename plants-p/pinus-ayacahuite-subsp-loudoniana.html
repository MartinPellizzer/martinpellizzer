Pinus ayacahuite subsp. loudoniana

Pinus ayacahuite subsp.

loudoniana, commonly known as the Mexican white pine, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to Mexico. It belongs to the Pinaceae family and is characterized by its slender, upward-curving needles and relatively small, oval cones.

The tree typically grows in mountainous regions, preferring well-drained soils and a temperate climate. It is valued for its timber and has been used in traditional medicine.

Due to habitat loss and overharvesting, it is considered vulnerable in some areas.
