Potamogeton natans var. parnassiifolius

Potamogeton natans var. parnassiifolius, commonly known as the Parnassian watermilfoil, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, opposite leaves that are typically narrow and elongated. The plant produces tiny, floating flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, often giving it a feathery appearance. It thrives in still or slow-moving waters with soft, muddy or sandy substrates.
