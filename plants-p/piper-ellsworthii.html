Piper ellsworthii

Piper ellsworthii, commonly known as the Ellsworth's pepper plant, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of Central America, particularly Belize and Guatemala. The plant is characterized by its climbing habit, with opposite, ovate leaves that are usually covered in a fine pubescence. Its small, greenish-white flowers are borne in clusters and are followed by small, three-lobed berries.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated as a houseplant or in botanical gardens.
