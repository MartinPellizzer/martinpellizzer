Pentacaena polycnemoides

Pentacaena polycnemoides, commonly known as the clustered sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It is a low-growing perennial herb with a mat-forming growth habit, characterized by its dense clusters of small, fleshy leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in winter, which are clustered in dense inflorescences at the ends of the stems. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Its botanical features make it an important component of the local flora in its native habitat.
