Podocarpus pungens

Podocarpus pungens, commonly known as the silver pine, is a evergreen conifer native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upward-curving needles that are covered in a fine, silvery bloom, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, spherical cones that mature to a reddish-brown color. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping.

Podocarpus pungens is also valued for its hardiness and ability to tolerate urban environments.
