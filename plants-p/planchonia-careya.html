Planchonia careya

Planchonia careya, commonly known as the breadfruit tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Pacific Islands.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and smooth, pale bark. The tree produces a fruit that is rich in nutrients and has been a staple food source for indigenous communities. Its flowering is typically followed by the development of a green, spiky fruit that ripens to a yellowish color.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental qualities and has cultural significance in many island societies.
