Prunus mahaleb var. cochleata

Prunus mahaleb var. cochleata, commonly known as the sweet cherry or sour cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and alternate, ovate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, white flowers in early spring, followed by edible fruits that range from red to black in color. The fruits are typically harvested in late summer and are used in culinary and medicinal applications.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular ornamental and fruit-bearing plant in gardens.
