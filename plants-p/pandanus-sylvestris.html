Pandanus sylvestris

Pandanus sylvestris, commonly known as the wild pandanus, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It is a member of the Pandanaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feather-like leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically clustered in terminal spikes. Its leaves are often used in traditional crafts and cooking, while its fruits are edible.

Pandanus sylvestris thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal or swampy areas.
