Phaseolus multiflorus

Phaseolus multiflorus, commonly known as the scarlet runner bean, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its vigorous climbing habit and large, showy flowers that range in color from white to deep red. The plant produces long, cylindrical pods that contain edible beans, which are often used in culinary applications. Botanically, it belongs to the Fabaceae family, known for its nitrogen-fixing ability through symbiotic bacteria in its root nodules.

Its foliage consists of trifoliate leaves, and it thrives in warm, sunny climates with well-drained soil.
