Passiflora semiciliosa

Passiflora semiciliosa, commonly known as the Brazilian passionflower, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, complex flowers that feature a unique blend of colors and structures, including a crown of filaments and a central corona. The plant produces edible, orange-colored berries that are rich in nutrients and often consumed by local communities. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

Passiflora semiciliosa is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential calming properties.
