Poa pratensis var. lejeunei

Poa pratensis var. lejeunei, commonly known as the Lejeune's bluegrass, is a variant of the common bluegrass species.

It is a cool-season perennial grass characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and dense foliage. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and pastures. Its leaves are narrow and slightly rolled, with a blue-green coloration that gives it its common name.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in turfgrass mixes.
