Praxelis missiona

Praxelis missiona, commonly known as the Mission praxelis, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters. It is adapted to survive in hot, dry climates with minimal water requirements.

Praxelis missiona is often used in xeriscaping due to its low maintenance and ornamental value.
