Pennisetum quartinianum

Pennisetum quartinianum, commonly known as purple muhly, is a perennial grass native to Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, silver-green leaves and striking purple-tinted inflorescences that appear in late summer. This plant thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for xeriscaping and drought-tolerant gardens. Its ornamental value comes from its feathery plumes and ability to attract birds and pollinators.

Pennisetum quartinianum is also valued for its low maintenance requirements and ability to stabilize soil in erosion-prone areas.
