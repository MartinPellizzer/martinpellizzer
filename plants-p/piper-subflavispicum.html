Piper subflavispicum

Piper subflavispicum, commonly known as the golden-flowered pepper plant, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to the rainforests of Southeast Asia, particularly Indonesia and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its small, yellowish flowers and heart-shaped leaves. It grows as an erect shrub, often reaching a height of up to 2 meters.

Its botanical features make it a subject of interest in both horticultural and ecological studies.
