Pimelandra griffithii

Pimelandra griffithii, commonly known as the golden flame plant, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking, tubular, orange-red flowers. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Pimelandra griffithii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and vibrant floral display.
