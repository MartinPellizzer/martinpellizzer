Primula vulgaris subsp. rubra

Primula vulgaris subsp.

rubra, commonly known as the common primrose, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its showy, clustered flowers that typically range in color from yellow to pink and red.

The plant has heart-shaped, hairy leaves that are often covered in a fine down. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This species is widely cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a symbol of spring.
