Persicaria setacea

Persicaria setacea, commonly known as the silky knotweed, is a flowering plant native to Asia and parts of Europe.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and slender, hairy stems that often appear in dense clusters. The plant produces small, pale pink to white flowers arranged in spikes, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, giving them a silky texture.

Persicaria setacea thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.
