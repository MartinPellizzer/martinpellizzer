Peliosanthes kenhilloides

Peliosanthes kenhilloides, commonly known as the Kenhill's orchid, is a rare and unique orchid species native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often in forest undergrowth where it can receive indirect sunlight. Its leaves are long and narrow, adapted to capture limited light, while its roots are specialized for nutrient absorption from the surrounding soil.

Due to habitat loss and over-collection, this species is considered endangered and is the subject of conservation efforts.
