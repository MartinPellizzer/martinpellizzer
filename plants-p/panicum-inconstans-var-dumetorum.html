Panicum inconstans var. dumetorum

Panicum inconstans var. dumetorum, commonly known as purple lovegrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and distinctive purple to pinkish inflorescences that appear in late summer. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides and grasslands, and it thrives in full sun. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green color, and the plant is often used in ornamental horticulture for its visual appeal.

It is also valued for its ability to stabilize soil and prevent erosion in arid environments.
