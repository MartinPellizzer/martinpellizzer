Paepalanthus argenteus var. viridis

Paepalanthus argenteus var. viridis, commonly known as the silverleaf puya, is a striking plant native to South America, particularly Argentina.

It is a member of the Xanthorrhoeaceae family and is characterized by its rosette of broad, silver-green leaves. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences with numerous small, white or pale yellow flowers. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and arid landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and unique appearance, it is often cultivated for its visual appeal and resilience.
