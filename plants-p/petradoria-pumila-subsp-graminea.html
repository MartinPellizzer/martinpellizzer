Petradoria pumila subsp. graminea

Petradoria pumila subsp.

graminea, commonly known as the slender petradoria, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its fine, grass-like leaves and small, clustered flowers that bloom in early spring.

The plant typically thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats. Its foliage is often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs, which helps to reduce water loss in arid environments.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soils in mountainous regions.
