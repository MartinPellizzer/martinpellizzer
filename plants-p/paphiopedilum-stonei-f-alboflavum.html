Paphiopedilum stonei f. alboflavum

Paphiopedilum stonei f. alboflavum, commonly known as the White-flowered Stone's Orchid, is a rare and prized orchid species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking white to pale yellow flowers with intricate patterns and a unique lip structure typical of the Paphiopedilum genus. The plant typically grows as a terrestrial or epiphytic orchid, preferring shady, humid environments with well-drained soil. Its foliage consists of broad, elliptical leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating.

Due to its beauty and rarity, it is highly sought after in horticultural circles and is often cultivated in controlled environments.
