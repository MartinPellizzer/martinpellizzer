Paulownia silvestrii

Paulownia silvestrii, commonly known as the wild paulownia, is a deciduous tree native to China and parts of East Asia.

It belongs to the family Paulowniaceae and is characterized by its rapid growth and large, heart-shaped leaves. The tree produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in early spring, which are typically pale purple or white. Its wood is lightweight and highly valued for its strength and workability, though it is not as commonly used as other paulownia species.

The plant is also noted for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions and its resilience to environmental stress.
