Porpax viridis

Porpax viridis, commonly known as the green porpax, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves that are typically a vibrant green color. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are usually white or pale yellow in hue. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical forests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
