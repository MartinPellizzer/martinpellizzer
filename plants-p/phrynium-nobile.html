Phrynium nobile

Phrynium nobile, commonly known as the noble arrowleaf, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often variegated with white and green patterns. The plant produces striking yellow flowers that are clustered in spikes, adding ornamental value to gardens. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Phrynium nobile is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
