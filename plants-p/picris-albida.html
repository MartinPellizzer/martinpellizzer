Picris albida

Picris albida, commonly known as white goatsbeard, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white hairs. It thrives in dry, sunny grasslands and is often found along roadsides and in disturbed soils.

Picris albida is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.
