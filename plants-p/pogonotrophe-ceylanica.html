Pogonotrophe ceylanica

Pogonotrophe ceylanica, commonly known as the Ceylon velvet tamarind, is a small, deciduous tree native to Sri Lanka.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and clustered yellow flowers. The plant produces small, edible pods containing seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. Its bark and leaves contain tannins and are valued for their astringent properties.

Pogonotrophe ceylanica thrives in tropical climates and is often found in moist, shaded forest environments.
