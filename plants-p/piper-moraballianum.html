Piper moraballianum

Piper moraballianum, commonly known as the Moraballianum pepper, is a flowering plant native to the Amazon rainforest.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes numerous species of pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its slender, climbing vines and small, heart-shaped leaves. Its flowers are unisexual, with separate male and female structures, and it produces small, round berries that are typically green when mature.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has limited use in traditional medicine.
