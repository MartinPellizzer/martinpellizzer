Parmentiera dressleri

Parmentiera dressleri, commonly known as the desert globemallow, is a shrub native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northwestern Mexico.

It features a woody stem with thick, fleshy leaves that help it conserve water in its harsh environment. The plant produces showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in the spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, winged seeds.

Parmentiera dressleri is adapted to thrive in sandy or rocky soils with minimal rainfall.
