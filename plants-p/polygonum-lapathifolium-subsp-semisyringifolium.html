Polygonum lapathifolium subsp. semisyringifolium

Polygonum lapathifolium subsp.

semisyringifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaf smartweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of Asia. It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, characterized by its distinctive arrow-shaped leaves and clusters of small, pinkish flowers.

The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along riverbanks. Its leaves are narrow and often have a slightly serrated edge, while the stems are slender and often reddish in color.

It is known for its ability to thrive in nutrient-poor soils and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
