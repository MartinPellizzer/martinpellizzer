Plectranthus gibbosus

Plectranthus gibbosus, commonly known as the woolly plectranthus, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy leaves that give it a velvety appearance. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically purple or blue in color. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Plectranthus gibbosus is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and can also be grown as a container plant.
