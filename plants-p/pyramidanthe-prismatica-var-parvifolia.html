Pyramidanthe prismatica var. parvifolia

Pyramidanthe prismatica var. parvifolia, commonly known as the small-leaved pyramidanthe, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its narrow, prism-like leaves that are typically smaller than those of the typical variety. The plant produces clusters of small, pale yellow flowers that bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its unique morphology and ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
