Polypodium carthusianum

Polypodium carthusianum, commonly known as the Carthusian brake, is a species of fern belonging to the family Polypodiaceae.

It is a terrestrial fern that typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky areas. The plant has a creeping rhizome with a dense network of roots, and its fronds are bipinnate, meaning they have two levels of division. The fronds are usually dark green and can reach lengths of up to 30 centimeters.

It is characterized by its distinctive, glossy appearance and is often found in temperate regions across the Northern Hemisphere.
