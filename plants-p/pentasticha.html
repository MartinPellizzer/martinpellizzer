Pentasticha

Pentasticha, commonly known as the mountain sandwort, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Caryophyllaceae.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming structure with dense clusters of small, white or pink flowers. The plant typically features opposite, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Pentasticha thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in alpine or arctic environments.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil in harsh, mountainous regions.
