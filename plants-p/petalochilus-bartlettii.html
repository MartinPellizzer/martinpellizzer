Petalochilus bartlettii

Petalochilus bartlettii, commonly known as the Bartlett's spike orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It typically grows in lowland forests and is characterized by its compact, upright growth habit. The plant produces a single spike of flowers, which are usually pale green to yellow in color. Its leaves are linear and grass-like, and it blooms during the wet season.

Petalochilus bartlettii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
