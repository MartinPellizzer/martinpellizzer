Panicum decompositum

Panicum decompositum, commonly known as the decomposed panicgrass, is a rare and lesser-known grass species native to specific regions of the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its delicate, grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or wetlands, where it thrives in the presence of organic matter. Its reproductive structures are clustered in loose, panicle-like formations, which contribute to its scientific name.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
