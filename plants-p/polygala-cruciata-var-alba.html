Polygala cruciata var. alba

Polygala cruciata var. alba, commonly known as the white-flowered milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has pinnately compound leaves with linear to lanceolate leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in sagebrush steppe and grassland habitats.

Its botanical features make it an important component of its native ecosystem.
