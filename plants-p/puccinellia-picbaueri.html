Puccinellia picbaueri

Puccinellia picbaueri, commonly known as the sand dropseed, is a flowering plant belonging to the grass family (Poaceae).

It is native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia, including parts of China, Kazakhstan, and Mongolia. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to harsh environmental conditions. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences with tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

Puccinellia picbaueri plays a role in stabilizing sandy soils and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
