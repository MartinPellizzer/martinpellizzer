Pleurothallis gnoma

Pleurothallis gnoma, commonly known as the gnoma orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and pink. The plant grows as an epiphyte, often found on trees in humid, shaded environments. Its roots are adapted to absorb moisture and nutrients from the air and surrounding substrates.

Pleurothallis gnoma is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
