Pyrus ochracea

Pyrus ochracea, commonly known as the yellow crabapple, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its oval, yellowish-green leaves that turn reddish-brown in the fall. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in early spring, followed by edible, sour fruits that are often used in jams and jellies. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens.

Pyrus ochracea is also valued for its ornamental value and historical use in traditional medicine.
