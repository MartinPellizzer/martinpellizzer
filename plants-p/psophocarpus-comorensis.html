Psophocarpus comorensis

Psophocarpus comorensis, commonly known as the Comorian velvet bean, is a climbing legume native to the Comoros Islands.

It produces distinctive, inflated pods that contain edible seeds, which are often used in traditional cuisines. The plant has compound leaves with trifoliate or pinnate structures, and its flowers are typically small and white or purple. It thrives in tropical climates and is adapted to grow in well-drained soils.

The species is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, making it useful in agricultural systems.
