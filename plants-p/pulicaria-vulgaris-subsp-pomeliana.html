Pulicaria vulgaris subsp. pomeliana

Pulicaria vulgaris subsp.

pomeliana, commonly known as the Mediterranean tansy, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and North Africa. It is a low-growing perennial herb with gray-green, hairy foliage and yellow-orange composite flowers.

The plant typically thrives in dry, sunny habitats such as rocky soils and coastal areas. Its flowers are arranged in flat-topped clusters, and the leaves are often aromatic, emitting a strong, bitter scent.

It is often used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported digestive and antiseptic properties.
