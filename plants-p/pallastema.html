Pallastema

Pallastema, commonly known as the false kaffir lime, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family, which includes citrus plants, and is characterized by its aromatic, fragrant leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by oval-shaped fruits. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The plant's botanical features make it a unique and valuable species in both horticulture and herbal practices.
