Poa iconia

Poa iconia, commonly known as the Iconic Bristlegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, wiry stems that often have a bluish-green hue. The leaves are narrow, rough, and covered with short, stiff hairs, giving the plant a distinctive texture. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of spikelets, which are typically tinged with purple or brown.

Poa iconia thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.
