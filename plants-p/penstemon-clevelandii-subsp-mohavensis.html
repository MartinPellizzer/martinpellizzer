Penstemon clevelandii subsp. mohavensis

Penstemon clevelandii subsp.

mohavensis, commonly known as the Mohave penstemon, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to purple.

The plant typically thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it well adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often covered in a fine layer of hair to reduce water loss.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting native pollinators.
