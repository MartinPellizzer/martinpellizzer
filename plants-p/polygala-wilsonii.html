Polygala wilsonii

Polygala wilsonii, commonly known as Wilson's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of western North America.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its delicate, often pink or white flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments. Its leaves are usually small and linear, and it produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Wilson's milkvetch is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens in suitable climates.
