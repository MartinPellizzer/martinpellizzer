Paspalum scrobiculatum var. bispicatum

Paspalum scrobiculatum var. bispicatum, commonly known as the two-awned paspalum, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and narrow, bluish-green leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear two awns, giving it its common name. It thrives in moist to wet environments, often found in marshy areas and along riverbanks.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in wetland habitats.
