Pteris marattiifolia

Pteris marattiifolia, commonly known as the giant water fern, is a species of fern native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its large, feathery fronds that can grow up to 2 meters in length. The plant has a distinctive rhizome system that allows it to thrive in moist, shaded environments. Its spores are produced on the underside of its fronds, which are typically arranged in a bipinnate pattern.

This plant is often used in ornamental horticulture due to its striking appearance and adaptability.
