Pinus mughus var. rufa

Pinus mughus var. rufa, commonly known as the red fir, is a variant of the Mughal fir native to the mountainous regions of western China.

It is characterized by its reddish-brown bark and needle-like leaves that are typically 2 to 4 centimeters long. The cones of this species are usually pendulous and can reach lengths of up to 15 centimeters. It thrives in cool, moist climates at high altitudes, often growing in dense forests.

The plant plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
