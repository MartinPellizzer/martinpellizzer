Paraphlomis seticalyx

Paraphlomis seticalyx, commonly known as the hairy root parsnip, is a perennial herb native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the family Lamiaceae, which includes many aromatic plants. The plant is characterized by its hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves with a slightly serrated edge. Its flowers are small and clustered in whorls, typically in shades of purple or pink.

The roots are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
