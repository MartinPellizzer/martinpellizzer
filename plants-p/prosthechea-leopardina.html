Prosthechea leopardina

Prosthechea leopardina, commonly known as the leopard orchid, is a species of epiphytic orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its dark green, leathery leaves and distinctive, showy flowers that often feature a contrasting color pattern resembling a leopard's spots. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in a raceme, which bloom during the rainy season. Prosthechea leopardina thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found growing on trees or rocks in tropical forests.

Its unique appearance and ecological role make it a notable species in orchid diversity.
