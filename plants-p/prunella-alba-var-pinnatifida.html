Prunella alba var. pinnatifida

Prunella alba var. pinnatifida, commonly known as narrow-leaved selfheal, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is characterized by its slender, opposite leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces small, pale blue or violet flowers arranged in whorled clusters, which bloom from late spring to early autumn. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland edges and meadows.

Its medicinal properties have been historically used in traditional herbal remedies.
