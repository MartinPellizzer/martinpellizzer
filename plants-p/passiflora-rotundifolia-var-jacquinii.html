Passiflora rotundifolia var. jacquinii

Passiflora rotundifolia var. jacquinii, commonly known as the Jacquin's passionflower, is a climbing vine native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, intricate flowers that feature five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments. The plant produces blue or purple berries that are edible and often used in local cuisines. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Its botanical features make it a notable species within the Passifloraceae family.
