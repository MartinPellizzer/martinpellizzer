Prostanthera cineolifera

Prostanthera cineolifera, commonly known as the narrow-leaved mintbush, is a shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically white or pale blue, arranged in dense clusters. Its leaves are narrow and covered in fine, glandular hairs that release a distinctive mint-like scent.

Prostanthera cineolifera is well adapted to dry, sandy soils and is often found in heathlands and coastal areas.
