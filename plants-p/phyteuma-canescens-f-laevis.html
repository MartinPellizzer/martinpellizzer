Phyteuma canescens f. laevis

Phyteuma canescens f. laevis, commonly known as the smooth phyteuma, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of bell-shaped flowers. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in a fine, silky hair, giving the plant a soft appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often used in gardens for its attractive, long-lasting blooms.
