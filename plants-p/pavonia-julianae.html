Pavonia julianae

Pavonia julianae, commonly known as the Julian's false mallow, is a perennial shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has oval, hairy leaves that are arranged oppositely along the stems. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soils.

Pavonia julianae is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and open woodlands.
