Pleuridium oxyloba

Pleuridium oxyloba, commonly known as the narrow-leafed pleuridium, is a member of the Cyperaceae family, commonly referred to as the sedge family.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream sides. Its flowering period occurs in the spring, producing tiny, inconspicuous blooms that are often overlooked.

The species is noted for its ecological importance in maintaining soil stability and providing habitat for various microorganisms.
