Phylacium

Phylacium, commonly known as the African violet, is a genus of flowering plants native to Africa.

These plants are characterized by their rosette-shaped leaves and showy, tubular flowers that come in a variety of colors. Phylacium species are typically evergreen and thrive in humid, well-drained soil. They are popular as ornamental plants due to their compact size and vibrant blooms.

The genus includes several species, with Phylacium africanum being one of the most commonly cultivated.
