Platanus orientalis var. hispanica

Platanus orientalis var. hispanica, commonly known as the Spanish plane tree, is a deciduous tree native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its large, palmate leaves that turn yellow in autumn before falling. The tree produces spherical, spiky fruits that contain small, winged seeds. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in urban and suburban landscapes.

Its sturdy trunk and broad canopy make it a notable feature in both natural and cultivated settings.
