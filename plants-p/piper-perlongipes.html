Piper perlongipes

Piper perlongipes, commonly known as the long-footed pepper vine, is a climbing shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes numerous pepper species. The plant is characterized by its slender, trailing stems and small, heart-shaped leaves with a glossy surface. Its flowers are unisexual, with male and female flowers occurring on separate plants, and it produces small, round berries that are typically green when immature.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
