Pappobolus hutchisonii

Pappobolus hutchisonii, commonly known as the Hutchison's pappobolus, is a unique member of the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive inflorescence, which consists of small, clustered flowers surrounded by bracts. The plant produces a type of fruit called a pappus, which is adapted for wind dispersal. Native to specific regions in southern Africa, it thrives in arid and semi-arid environments.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and taxonomic studies.
