Pelargonium caledonicum

Pelargonium caledonicum, commonly known as the Caledonian geranium, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Geraniaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, five-petaled flowers. The plant typically produces vibrant pink to red blooms during the summer months. Its leaves are palmately lobed and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Pelargonium caledonicum is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained soil.
