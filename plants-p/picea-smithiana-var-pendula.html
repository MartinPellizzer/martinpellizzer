Picea smithiana var. pendula

Picea smithiana var. pendula, commonly known as the weeping spruce, is a unique variant of the white spruce species.

It is characterized by its drooping branches that create a cascading effect, giving it a distinctive appearance. This evergreen conifer is native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas, where it thrives in cool, moist environments. The tree has needle-like leaves that are typically dark green and glossy, and it produces small, cone-like fruits.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for botanical gardens and landscapes.
