Plantago crassifolia var. trichopoda

Plantago crassifolia var. trichopoda, commonly known as the hairy plantain, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features rosettes of broad, lance-shaped leaves covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive, fuzzy appearance. The plant produces small, greenish flowers in dense clusters, which are typically borne on slender stalks. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and along stream banks.

This variant is notable for its adaptability and is used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
