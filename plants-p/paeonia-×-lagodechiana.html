Paeonia × lagodechiana

Paeonia × lagodechiana, commonly known as the Chinese peony, is a hybrid species within the genus Paeonia, native to China.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that can range in color from white to deep red, often with intricate patterns. The plant is a herbaceous perennial that produces thick, fleshy roots and has compound, bipinnate leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens.

Its botanical significance lies in its role as a parent species in the development of many modern peony cultivars.
