Polygala pedemontana var. guarensis

Polygala pedemontana var. guarensis, commonly known as the Guarense milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the Andean region of South America.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or open habitats at higher elevations. The plant features slender, branched stems and small, often clustered flowers with distinctive spurred petals. Its leaves are usually narrow and lance-shaped, adapted to reduce water loss in arid conditions.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral structure.
