Panicum hillebrandianum

Panicum hillebrandianum, commonly known as the Hillebrand's panicgrass, is a species of grass native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces panicles of small, spikelet-bearing flowers, which are typically green or brown in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and restoration projects.
