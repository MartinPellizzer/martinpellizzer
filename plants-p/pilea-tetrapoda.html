Pilea tetrapoda

Pilea tetrapoda, commonly known as the Chinese money plant, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to China.

It features rosettes of glossy, heart-shaped leaves that are typically a deep green with a slightly waxy texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers that appear in late spring or early summer. It thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Pilea tetrapoda is known for its ease of care and ability to adapt well to a variety of indoor environments.
