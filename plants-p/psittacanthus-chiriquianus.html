Psittacanthus chiriquianus

Psittacanthus chiriquianus, commonly known as the Guatemalan cycad, is a unique plant belonging to the Cycadaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of Central America, particularly Guatemala and El Salvador. This plant features a stout, unbranched trunk that is often covered in a fibrous, spongy layer. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with long, narrow leaflets that give it a distinctive appearance.

Psittacanthus chiriquianus is adapted to grow in dry, rocky soils and is often found in mountainous areas.
