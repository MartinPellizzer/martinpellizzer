Polygala curtissii

Polygala curtissii, commonly known as the Texas milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its flowers are usually pale yellow to greenish-white, with a distinctive spurred structure.

Polygala curtissii is often found in grasslands and open woodlands, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local pollinator populations.
