Pilosella × bifurca subsp. comata

Pilosella × bifurca subsp.

comata, commonly known as the hairy pearly everlasting, is a flowering plant in the Asteraceae family. It is characterized by its dense, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant has a mat-forming growth habit with hairy stems and leaves. Its subspecies comata is distinguished by its more pronounced hairy foliage compared to other forms.

It is native to Europe and is often found in dry meadows and grasslands.
