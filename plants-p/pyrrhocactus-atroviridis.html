Pyrrhocactus atroviridis

Pyrrhocactus atroviridis, commonly known as the black viridian cactus, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its columnar stems covered in dense, spiny areoles. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, adding a striking contrast to its dark green foliage. It is a slow-growing plant that thrives in well-drained soil and full sun.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is often cultivated as a rare ornamental plant.
