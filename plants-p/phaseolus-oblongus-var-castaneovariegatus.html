Phaseolus oblongus var. castaneovariegatus

Phaseolus oblongus var. castaneovariegatus, commonly known as the variegated castanea bean, is a lesser-known variant of the common lima bean.

It is characterized by its unique leaf patterns and a pod that often displays a striking combination of colors, including shades of brown and green. The plant typically grows as a climbing vine, reaching lengths of up to several meters. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, and the beans are edible when mature.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal as well as its culinary potential.
