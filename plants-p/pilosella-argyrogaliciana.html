Pilosella argyrogaliciana

Pilosella argyrogaliciana, commonly known as the silver-haired groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central and Southern Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, where it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its leaves are covered in fine, silvery hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance and contributing to its common name.

Pilosella argyrogaliciana is often found in meadows and open slopes, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
