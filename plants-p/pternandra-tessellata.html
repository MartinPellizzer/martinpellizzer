Pternandra tessellata

Pternandra tessellata, commonly known as the African boxwood, is a small evergreen shrub native to southern Africa.

It features dense, compact foliage with dark green, leathery leaves that are arranged in a unique, tessellated pattern. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers followed by red, fleshy berries that are edible for birds. It is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its attractive appearance and hardy nature.

Pternandra tessellata prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens and containers.
