Polygonum arenarium var. caesaraugustanum

Polygonum arenarium var. caesaraugustanum, commonly known as the Augustan sandpolygon, is a variant of the sandpolygon species native to arid and semi-arid regions.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves and small, clustered flowers that typically bloom in shades of red or purple. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand periods of drought. Its growth habit is usually low and spreading, forming dense mats that help stabilize sandy soils.

This variant is of interest in botanical studies due to its unique adaptations to harsh environments.
