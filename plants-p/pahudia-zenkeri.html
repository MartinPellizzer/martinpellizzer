Pahudia zenkeri

Pahudia zenkeri, commonly known as the Zambesi lily, is a striking plant native to southern Africa.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces showy, white to pale pink flowers with a distinctive, almost lantern-like shape. The plant features long, narrow leaves that are often covered in a fine, silky hair. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found near water sources such as rivers and lakes.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
