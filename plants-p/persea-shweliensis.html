Persea shweliensis

Persea shweliensis, commonly known as the Shweli persimmon, is a species of flowering plant in the Lauraceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, particularly Myanmar. The plant is characterized by its broad, glossy leaves and produces small, fragrant flowers. Its fruit, a smooth, orange-hued berry, is edible and has a sweet, sometimes astringent flavor.

Persea shweliensis is often found in lowland evergreen forests and is valued for its ornamental and culinary uses.
