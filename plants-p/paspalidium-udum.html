Paspalidium udum

Paspalidium udum, commonly known as the slender false brome, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in open woodlands and grasslands, preferring well-drained soils. The plant has slender, erect stems and small, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its inflorescence consists of a spike-like arrangement of small, two-lobed spikelets.

Paspalidium udum plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various insects and small animals.
