Pleurothallis matudana

Pleurothallis matudana, commonly known as the Matudana orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that often display a range of colors, including white, yellow, and green. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its botanical features include narrow, grass-like leaves and a compact growth habit, making it well-suited for cultivation in controlled conditions.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a valuable species in botanical gardens and conservation efforts.
