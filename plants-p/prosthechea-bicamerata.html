Prosthechea bicamerata

Prosthechea bicamerata, commonly known as the two-chambered bromeliad, is a terrestrial orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that form a water-holding cup, typical of bromeliads. The plant produces striking inflorescences with showy, fragrant flowers in shades of yellow and orange. Its roots are fibrous and adapted for anchoring in moist, shaded environments.

Prosthechea bicamerata is often found in humid forests and is valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture.
