Phaseolus vulgaris var. dimidiatus

Phaseolus vulgaris var. dimidiatus, commonly known as the runner bean, is a climbing legume native to the Americas.

It produces elongated, edible pods that are typically green when immature and turn yellow or red when mature. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces showy, butterfly-shaped flowers. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is often cultivated for both its edible beans and ornamental value.
