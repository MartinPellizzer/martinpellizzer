Pachycarpus orbicularis

Pachycarpus orbicularis, commonly known as the globe cactus, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its spherical shape and thick, fleshy stems that store water, allowing it to survive in dry conditions. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in the spring, adding a delicate touch to its rugged appearance. Its leaves are modified into spines, reducing water loss and deterring herbivores.

Overall, Pachycarpus orbicularis is a hardy, low-maintenance plant well-suited for xerophytic gardens.
