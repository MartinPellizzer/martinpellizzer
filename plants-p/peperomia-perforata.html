Peperomia perforata

Peperomia perforata, commonly known as the laceleaf peperomia, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its glossy, elongated leaves that are deeply veined and have a distinctive lace-like appearance. The plant typically grows as a compact shrub with short, thick stems and small, clustered flowers that are usually greenish-white in color. It thrives in bright, indirect light and prefers well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Peperomia perforata is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental foliage and relatively low maintenance requirements.
