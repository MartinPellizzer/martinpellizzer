Polemonium lanatum

Polemonium lanatum, commonly known as the downy bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its hairy, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of bell-shaped, blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in native plant gardens.
