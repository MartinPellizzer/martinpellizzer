Phyteuma corniculatum var. vulgare

Phyteuma corniculatum var. vulgare, commonly known as the common phyteuma, is a herbaceous perennial native to southern Europe.

It features tall, slender stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, pale yellow to cream-colored flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are slightly hairy and arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often found in meadows and woodland edges.

Its flowering period typically occurs in late spring to early summer.
