Pterotheca glaucescens

Pterotheca glaucescens, commonly known as the blueleaf pterotheca, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features slender, erect stems covered in fine, hairy textures, and its leaves are lance-shaped with a bluish-green hue. The plant produces small, white to pale blue flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the cooler months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
