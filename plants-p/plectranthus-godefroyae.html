Plectranthus godefroyae

Plectranthus godefroyae, commonly known as the Cape honeysuckle, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant thrives in warm, sunny conditions and is often used in ornamental gardening for its attractive foliage and blooms. It has lance-shaped leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs, and it is well-suited for container growing.

Plectranthus godefroyae is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
