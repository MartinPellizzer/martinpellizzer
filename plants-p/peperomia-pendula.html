Peperomia pendula

Peperomia pendula, commonly known as the hanging peperomia, is a compact, trailing plant native to tropical regions of Central and South America.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that are often waxy and have a distinctive dark green to reddish hue. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or reddish in color. It thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for hanging baskets or shelves.

Its low maintenance and ability to cascade make it an ideal indoor plant for adding visual interest.
