Pulegium erectum

Pulegium erectum, commonly known as blueweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe, North Africa, and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its square, hairy stems and opposite leaves that are lance-shaped and often hairy as well. The plant produces small, blue or purple flowers arranged in spikes, which bloom during the summer months. It is often found in moist environments such as meadows, ditches, and along stream banks.

Pulegium erectum is known for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
