Pygeum latifolium glabrius

Pygeum latifolium glabrius, commonly known as the broadleaf African teak, is a deciduous tree native to parts of Africa.

It is characterized by its large, elliptical leaves that are typically smooth and dark green on top. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by woody, capsule-like fruits containing flat, winged seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, well-drained soils.

This species is valued for its durable wood and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
