Physalis dentata

Physalis dentata, commonly known as the Indian gooseberry or amla, is a deciduous shrub native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is characterized by its hairy, yellow-orange fruits enclosed in a papery calyx. The plant produces small, elliptical leaves with serrated edges, and its flowers are typically greenish-white with five petals. The fruit, which is rich in vitamin C, ripens from green to a bright orange color.

It thrives in warm climates and is often cultivated for its nutritional and medicinal properties.
