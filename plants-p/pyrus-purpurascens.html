Pyrus purpurascens

Pyrus purpurascens, commonly known as the red-leaf pear, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its vibrant red foliage, which becomes more pronounced in the fall. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in the spring, followed by edible, sweet fruits that are often used in jams and jellies. Its leaves are typically oval with serrated edges and a distinctive reddish tinge.

Pyrus purpurascens is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
