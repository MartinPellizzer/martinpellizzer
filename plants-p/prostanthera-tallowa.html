Prostanthera tallowa

Prostanthera tallowa, commonly known as Tallowwood mint, is a small shrub native to eastern Australia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pale blue to purple in color. Its leaves are aromatic and have a mint-like scent, which is a common trait among plants in the mint family.

Prostanthera tallowa is often found in coastal heathlands and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
