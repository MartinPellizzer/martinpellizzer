Potamogeton × aemulans

Potamogeton × aemulans, commonly known as the hybrid water milfoil, is a submerged aquatic plant that exhibits characteristics of both its parental species, Potamogeton crispus and Potamogeton perfoliatus.

It is native to North America and is often found in freshwater habitats such as lakes, ponds, and slow-moving streams. The plant has long, narrow leaves that are often arranged in whorls, and it produces small, green flowers that are typically borne in clusters. Potamogeton × aemulans plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for various aquatic organisms and contributing to water clarity through its dense growth.

However, it can also become invasive in certain environments, leading to ecological concerns.
