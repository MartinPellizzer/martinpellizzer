Pandanus burmanicus

Pandanus burmanicus, commonly known as the Burmese pandanus, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Pandanaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and long, narrow leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers that are often used in traditional cuisine and crafts. Its leaves are widely utilized for weaving baskets and mats due to their durability and flexibility.

The plant thrives in humid, coastal environments and is often found in mangrove ecosystems.
