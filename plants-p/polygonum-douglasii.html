Polygonum douglasii

Polygonum douglasii, commonly known as Douglas's knotweed, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its upright stems and clusters of small, pinkish to reddish flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along streambanks.

Polygonum douglasii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat and nectar for pollinators.
