Platychaete mucronifolia

Platychaete mucronifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved flatweed, is a low-growing herbaceous plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It features slender, often prostrate stems that are covered in fine, soft hairs. The leaves are linear to lanceolate, with a pointed tip, and are typically arranged in a rosette or along the stems. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds.

It is adapted to survive in harsh conditions with limited water availability.
