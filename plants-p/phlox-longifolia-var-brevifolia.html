Phlox longifolia var. brevifolia

Phlox longifolia var. brevifolia, commonly known as the shortleaf phlox, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Caprifoliaceae family and is characterized by its showy, tubular flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers, which range in color from white to pink, are adapted for pollination by bees and other insects.

The compact growth habit and ornamental value make it a popular choice for gardens and natural landscapes.
