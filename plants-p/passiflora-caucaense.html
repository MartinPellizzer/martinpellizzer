Passiflora caucaense

Passiflora caucaense, commonly known as the Cauca passionflower, is a climbing vine native to the Andean regions of Colombia.

It is characterized by its striking, intricate flowers that feature five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments surrounding the reproductive structures. The plant produces edible, yellow-orange berries that are rich in antioxidants and nutrients. Its leaves are large, heart-shaped, and often have a waxy texture.

Passiflora caucaense is valued both for its ornamental beauty and its potential medicinal uses in traditional herbal practices.
