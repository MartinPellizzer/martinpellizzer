Psychilis bifida

Psychilis bifida, commonly known as the split-leaved psychilis, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive bifid (split) leaves, which are typically narrow and lance-shaped. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually white or pale pink in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Psychilis bifida is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
