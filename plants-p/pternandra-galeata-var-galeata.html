Pternandra galeata var. galeata

Pternandra galeata var. galeata, commonly known as the silver flame tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, palmate leaves that are often covered in a silvery or grayish pubescence. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, arranged in clusters. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The bark and leaves have been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
