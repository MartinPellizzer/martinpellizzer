Phyllanthus amarus var. baronianus

Phyllanthus amarus var. baronianus, commonly known as the African date tree or baron's milkweed, is a shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its small, green, succulent leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by fleshy, red or orange berries that contain seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it adaptable to various environmental conditions.

The plant's medicinal properties have been studied for their potential anti-inflammatory and antiviral effects.
