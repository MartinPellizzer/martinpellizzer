Populus nova

Populus nova, commonly known as the New World poplar, is a deciduous tree native to North America.

It belongs to the Salicaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces catkin-like flowers in early spring, which are essential for wind pollination. Its rapid growth rate makes it a valuable species for reforestation and erosion control.

Populus nova also has medicinal properties, with its bark historically used in traditional medicine.
