Phoradendron salicifolium

Phoradendron salicifolium, commonly known as the silky mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on various host trees, primarily in the southern United States.

It has small, oval-shaped leaves that are dark green and glossy, and its flowers are typically white or pale green, appearing in clusters. The plant produces small, fleshy berries that are usually red or orange, which are an important food source for birds.

Its parasitic nature allows it to draw water and nutrients from its host, while still performing photosynthesis to some extent.
