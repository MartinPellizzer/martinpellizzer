Phellodendron sachalinense f. longipes

Phellodendron sachalinense f. longipes, commonly known as the longipedic form of the Sakhalin tree.

It is a variant of the Phellodendron sachalinense, a deciduous tree native to the Russian Far East. The plant is characterized by its elongated, slender branches and distinctive bark that is thick and fissured. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a glossy upper surface.

This form is notable for its unique growth habit and is of interest in botanical studies due to its genetic and morphological characteristics.
