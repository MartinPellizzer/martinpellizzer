Palicourea copensis

Palicourea copensis, commonly known as Brazilian pepper tree or *Schinus terebinthifolius*, is a member of the Anacardiaceae family.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree native to the Caribbean and parts of South America. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves, which are opposite and often have a reddish or orange coloration. Its small, fragrant flowers are followed by bright red berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife.

However, it is considered an invasive species in many regions outside its native range.
