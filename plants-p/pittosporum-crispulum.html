Pittosporum crispulum

Pittosporum crispulum, commonly known as the silver pittosporum, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its silvery-white undersides on its leaves, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring, followed by red or orange berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value.

Pittosporum crispulum is also valued for its ability to provide habitat for native birds and insects.
