Papaver rhoeas chelidonioides

Papaver rhoeas chelidonioides, commonly known as the meadow poppy, is a hybrid plant that exhibits characteristics of both *Papaver rhoeas* and *Chelidonium majus*.

It is a herbaceous perennial with striking yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, fields, and along roadsides. Its leaves are palmately lobed, and the flowers have a distinctive saucer-like shape with a prominent central stigma.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
