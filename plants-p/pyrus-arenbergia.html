Pyrus arenbergia

Pyrus arenbergia, commonly known as the arenberg pear.

It is a deciduous tree native to the southeastern United States, particularly found in Georgia and Florida. The plant has smooth, gray-brown bark that becomes fissured with age. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and ovate with a serrated margin.

The fruit is a small, yellowish pear with a sweet, delicate flavor, often used in local cuisines.
