Peumus alba

Peumus alba, commonly known as the white peumus, is a deciduous shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are often glandular and emit a strong, spicy aroma. Its root system is fibrous and can spread widely, making it a hardy and persistent species.

Peumus alba is often found in moist, well-drained soils along streambanks and in forested areas.
