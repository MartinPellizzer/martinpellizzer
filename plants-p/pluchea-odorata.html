Pluchea odorata

Pluchea odorata, commonly known as sweetleaf or sweetbriar, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a cluster of small flowers. The plant features alternate, heart-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface, and its flowers are typically yellow with a central disk. Pluchea odorata is often found in moist, shaded areas such as forests and streambanks.

It has a creeping growth habit and produces small, oval fruits that contain seeds dispersed by wind.
