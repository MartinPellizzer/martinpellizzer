Portulaca lakhonensis

Portulaca lakhonensis, commonly known as the Thai moss rose, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a succulent species characterized by its fleshy, thick stems and leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid conditions. The plant produces vibrant, showy flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red, typically blooming in the morning and closing by midday. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Portulaca lakhonensis is also valued for its ornamental beauty and resilience in hot, dry climates.
