Phyllanthus graveolens var. genuinus

Phyllanthus graveolens var. genuinus, commonly known as the African spurge, is a perennial shrub native to southern Africa.

It features small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in dense clusters, typically blooming in spring. The plant has succulent, fleshy leaves that are often hairy and have a distinctive, somewhat unpleasant odor. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or arid environments.

Its botanical characteristics make it an interesting subject for horticultural study and ecological research.
