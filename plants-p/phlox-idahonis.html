Phlox idahonis

Phlox idahonis, commonly known as the Idaho phlox, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in dry, rocky soils and open habitats. The plant produces clusters of showy, fragrant flowers in shades of pink, purple, and white during the summer months. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are evergreen in some varieties.

Phlox idahonis is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in challenging environmental conditions.
