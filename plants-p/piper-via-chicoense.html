Piper via-chicoense

Piper via-chicoense, commonly known as the Chico pepper or Chico vine, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, and is often found in humid lowland forests. The plant is characterized by its climbing habit, with long, slender stems that can reach several meters in length. Its leaves are typically alternate, ovate to lanceolate in shape, and have a glossy surface.

The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by red, fleshy berries that are edible and have a spicy, peppery flavor.
