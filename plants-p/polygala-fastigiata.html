Polygala fastigiata

Polygala fastigiata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved milkvetch, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit. The plant features small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink, often with a distinctive spurred structure. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, and it thrives in well-drained, sandy soils.

Polygala fastigiata is often found in coastal and inland grasslands, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
