Pyrus ruficola

Pyrus ruficola, commonly known as the red-fruited pear.

It is a species of flowering plant in the family Rosaceae, native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. The plant is characterized by its small, reddish fruits that are typically harvested in late summer. Its leaves are elliptical with a smooth margin and a slightly pubescent underside.

Pyrus ruficola is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of edible fruit in certain regions.
