Potentilla glandulifera subsp. gaudinii

Potentilla glandulifera subsp.

gaudinii, commonly known as the Gaudini's cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its small, yellowish-orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its leaves are palmately divided into five to seven lobes, often covered with fine, glandular hairs.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its compact growth and colorful blooms.
