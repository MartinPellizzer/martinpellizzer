Piper piliovarium

Piper piliovarium, commonly known as the pilose pepper vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many species of pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its slender, woody stems and small, ovate leaves with a hairy texture. Its flowers are unisexual, with separate male and female flowers on the same plant, and it produces small, berry-like fruits.

The plant's botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and taxonomic studies.
