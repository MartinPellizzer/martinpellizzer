Pyrrhocactus chilensis

Pyrrhocactus chilensis, commonly known as the Chilean fire cactus, is a species native to South America, particularly Chile and Argentina.

It is a low-growing cactus with cylindrical stems that are often covered in spines. The plant produces bright yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and arid landscapes.
