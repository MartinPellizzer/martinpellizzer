Pseudocodon convolvulaceus subsp. forrestii

Pseudocodon convolvulaceus subsp.

forrestii, commonly known as the Forest's morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia. It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family, which includes many vines and flowering plants.

The species is characterized by its trumpet-shaped flowers, typically ranging in color from pale yellow to deep orange. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, shaded environments.
