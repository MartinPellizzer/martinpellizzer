Passiflora curva

Passiflora curva, commonly known as the curly passionflower, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, intricate flowers that feature five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments. The plant produces blue or purple berries that are edible and contain a sweet, fleshy pulp. Its leaves are heart-shaped with a velvety texture and are often used in traditional medicine.

Passiflora curva is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
