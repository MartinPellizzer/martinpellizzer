Phagnalon sordidum var. sordidum

Phagnalon sordidum var. sordidum, commonly known as the black sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils, often found in meadows, grasslands, and along stream banks. The plant has slender, grass-like stems with small, narrow leaves that are usually dark green in color. Its flowers are tiny and clustered in spike-like inflorescences, producing small, inconspicuous seeds.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
