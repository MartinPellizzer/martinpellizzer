Phyllanthus abnormis var. riograndensis

Phyllanthus abnormis var. riograndensis, commonly known as the Rio Grande phyllanthus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Euphorbiaceae.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil and Paraguay, and thrives in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves and small, clustered yellow flowers. It typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters.

The species is noted for its adaptability to various soil types and its ability to tolerate drought conditions.
