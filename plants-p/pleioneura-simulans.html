Pleioneura simulans

Pleioneura simulans, commonly known as the false orchid, is a species of flowering plant belonging to the orchid family, Orchidaceae.

It is native to certain regions of Southeast Asia, including parts of India and China. The plant is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and small, delicate flowers that typically bloom in the spring. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists alike.
