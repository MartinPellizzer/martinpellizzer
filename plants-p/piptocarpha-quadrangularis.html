Piptocarpha quadrangularis

Piptocarpha quadrangularis, commonly known as the four-angle goldenweed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and hairy stems. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have a rough texture.

The showy yellow flowers bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
