Panicum subaristatum

Panicum subaristatum, commonly known as the slender panicgrass, is a species of grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its fine, hair-like leaves and slender, erect stems that often grow in dense clusters. The plant typically blooms with small, fluffy panicles that produce light seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in swampy areas or along the edges of ponds.

Due to its adaptability, it is sometimes considered an invasive species in certain regions.
