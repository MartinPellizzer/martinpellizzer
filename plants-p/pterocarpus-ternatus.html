Pterocarpus ternatus

Pterocarpus ternatus, commonly known as the Java ebony or blackwood, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dark, heavy wood that is highly valued for its durability and fine grain. The plant has compound leaves with three leaflets, and its flowers are small and yellowish-green. It produces distinctive pod-like fruits that contain flat, hard seeds.

The tree is often found in lowland forests and is used in traditional medicine and furniture-making.
