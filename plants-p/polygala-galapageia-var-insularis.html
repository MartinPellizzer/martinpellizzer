Polygala galapageia var. insularis

Polygala galapageia var. insularis, commonly known as the Galápagos milkweed, is a flowering plant native to the Galápagos Islands.

It is a member of the Polygalaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, spindly stems and clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and is adapted to the arid, volcanic soils of its native habitat. It plays an important role in the local ecosystem, providing nectar for pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species.
