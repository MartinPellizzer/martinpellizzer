Plectronia longicauda

Plectronia longicauda, commonly known as the long-tailed plectronia, is a unique flowering plant native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, elongated inflorescence that resembles a tail, giving it its common name. The plant features small, clustered flowers with vibrant colors, often attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically broad and glossy, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Plectronia longicauda is often cultivated in botanical gardens for its unusual appearance and ecological significance.
