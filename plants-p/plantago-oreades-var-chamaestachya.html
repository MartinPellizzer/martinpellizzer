Plantago oreades var. chamaestachya

Plantago oreades var. chamaestachya, commonly known as the rock plantain, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its upright, hairy stems and rosettes of narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish flowers in dense clusters, which are typically borne on slender, upright peduncles. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments.

This variety is noted for its adaptability to harsh conditions and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
