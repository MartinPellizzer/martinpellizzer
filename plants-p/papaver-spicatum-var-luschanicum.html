Papaver spicatum var. luschanicum

Papaver spicatum var. luschanicum, commonly known as the Luschan's poppy, is a variant of the spiked poppy native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its striking red to orange flowers with a distinctive spiky center, which are borne on slender, upright stems. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to arid environments. Its foliage consists of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of hairs.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
