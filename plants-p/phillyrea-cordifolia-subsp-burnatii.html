Phillyrea cordifolia subsp. burnatii

Phillyrea cordifolia subsp.

burnatii, commonly known as the Burnat's boxwood, is a shrub native to the eastern Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage composed of small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically a deep green.

The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers in the spring, which are followed by spherical, red-brown berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or limestone habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its compact growth and foliage.
