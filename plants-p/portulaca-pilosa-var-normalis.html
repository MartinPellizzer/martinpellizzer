Portulaca pilosa var. normalis

Portulaca pilosa var. normalis, commonly known as the hairy portulaca, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions.

It is a low-growing annual herb with fleshy, succulent leaves and vibrant, showy flowers that range in color from pink to red. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it suitable for rocky or sandy gardens. Its small, cylindrical seed pods contain numerous seeds that are easily dispersed by wind.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in harsh environmental conditions.
