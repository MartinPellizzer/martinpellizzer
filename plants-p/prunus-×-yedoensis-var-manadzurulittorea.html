Prunus × yedoensis var. manadzurulittorea

Prunus × yedoensis var. manadzurulittorea, commonly known as the Japanese cherry blossom, is a cultivar of the Prunus genus native to Japan.

It is characterized by its delicate, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in early spring, making it a symbol of transience and beauty in Japanese culture. The plant typically grows as a small to medium-sized tree, reaching heights of up to 10 meters, with a rounded, spreading canopy. Its leaves are oval-shaped, with a glossy green upper surface and a lighter underside, providing a striking contrast during the foliage season.

The tree is well-suited to temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
