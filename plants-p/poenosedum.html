Poenosedum

Poenosedum, commonly known as the "ghost plant," is a genus of succulent plants native to Africa.

It is characterized by its unique, often translucent or waxy leaves that give it a ghostly appearance. The plants typically grow in arid or semi-arid environments, adapting to low-water conditions through thick, fleshy leaves that store moisture. Poenosedum species are often cultivated as ornamental plants due to their striking visual appeal and low maintenance requirements.

They produce small, star-shaped flowers in shades of white, yellow, or orange, adding to their aesthetic value.
