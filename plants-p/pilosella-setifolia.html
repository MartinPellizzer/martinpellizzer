Pilosella setifolia

Pilosella setifolia, commonly known as the slender leaf hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters. Its leaves are narrow, hairy, and often arranged in a rosette at the base, while its flowers are yellow with pale centers.

Pilosella setifolia is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions, making it a common sight in temperate regions.
