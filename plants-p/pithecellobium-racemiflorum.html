Pithecellobium racemiflorum

Pithecellobium racemiflorum, commonly known as the racemiflorous poinsettia, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod-like fruits. The plant produces showy, colorful bracts that are often mistaken for flowers, though they are actually modified leaves. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are typically green or white and are clustered in racemes.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value, especially during the holiday season.
