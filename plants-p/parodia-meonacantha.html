Parodia meonacantha

Parodia meonacantha, commonly known as the Moon Cactus, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is a small, globular plant with a short, thick stem and a dense covering of spines. The plant is known for its vibrant, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of yellow or orange. It is a member of the Cactaceae family and is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its unique appearance.

Parodia meonacantha is well adapted to arid environments, with thick, fleshy stems that store water efficiently.
