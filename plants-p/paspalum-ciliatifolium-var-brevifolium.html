Paspalum ciliatifolium var. brevifolium

Paspalum ciliatifolium var. brevifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaved paspalum, is a grass species native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its slender, narrow leaves with a hairy margin, which give it its scientific name. This plant typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, preferring moist, well-drained soils. It is often found in regions with a subtropical or temperate climate.

Its flowering spikes are usually small and inconspicuous, with tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.
