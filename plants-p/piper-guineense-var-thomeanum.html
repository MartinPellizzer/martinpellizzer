Piper guineense var. thomeanum

Piper guineense var. thomeanum, commonly known as African black pepper, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its small, dark purple berries that resemble black pepper. The plant has compound leaves with opposite arrangement and produces small, yellowish-green flowers. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

The berries are used in traditional medicine and as a spice, similar to its more well-known relative, black pepper.
