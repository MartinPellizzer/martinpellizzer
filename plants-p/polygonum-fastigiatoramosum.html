Polygonum fastigiatoramosum

Polygonum fastigiatoramosum, commonly known as the upright polygonum, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, which includes plants known for their distinctive nodes and often edible seeds. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring upright, branching stems covered in soft, hairy foliage. Its small, greenish-white flowers appear in dense clusters during the summer months.

The leaves are lance-shaped with a smooth margin and a reddish tint on the underside, contributing to its ornamental value in gardens.
