Philammos margusaricus

Philammos margusaricus, commonly known as the golden mistflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features showy, daisy-like yellow flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant has slender, branching stems and lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

Philammos margusaricus is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support local wildlife.
