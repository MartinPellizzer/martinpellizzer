Paronychia sinaica

Paronychia sinaica, commonly known as the Sinai sandwort, is a low-growing flowering plant native to the arid regions of the Middle East and North Africa.

It thrives in rocky and sandy soils, showcasing its adaptation to harsh, dry environments. The plant features small, white or pink flowers that bloom in early spring, adding a delicate touch to its landscape. Its leaves are narrow and fleshy, helping to conserve water in its arid habitat.

Paronychia sinaica is often found in coastal and desert areas, where it plays a role in stabilizing sandy soils.
