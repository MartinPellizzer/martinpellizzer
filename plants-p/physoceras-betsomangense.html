Physoceras betsomangense

Physoceras betsomangense, commonly known as the Betsomang rock orchid, is a rare and endemic plant species found in specific regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and unique floral structure. The plant typically grows on rocky outcrops, where it forms a symbiotic relationship with mycorrhizal fungi. Its flowers are usually small and pale in color, with intricate patterns that aid in pollination by specific insect species.

Due to habitat destruction and limited distribution, it is considered vulnerable and requires conservation efforts to ensure its survival.
