Potentilla germadensis

Potentilla germadensis, commonly known as the Transylvanian cinquefoil, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and is often found in mountainous regions. It has palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, and its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer.
