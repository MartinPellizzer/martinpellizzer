Piptadenia zenkeri

Piptadenia zenkeri, commonly known as the African locust bean tree, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The tree can grow up to 20 meters tall, with a broad, umbrella-shaped canopy. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, arranged in dense clusters, and it produces flat, woody pods containing edible seeds.
