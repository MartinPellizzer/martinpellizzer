Prunus jamasakura f. longipes

Prunus jamasakura f. longipes, commonly known as the long-stemmed Japanese cherry, is a unique cultivar of the Prunus jamasakura species.

It is characterized by its elongated, slender branches that give it a delicate and graceful appearance. The flowers are typically pinkish-white, appearing in early spring before the leaves emerge. This variety is prized for its ornamental value in gardens and parks, especially in regions with temperate climates.

It requires well-drained soil and partial shade to thrive, making it a popular choice for ornamental horticulture.
