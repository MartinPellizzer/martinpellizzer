Philadelphus pueblanus

Philadelphus pueblanus, commonly known as the Pueblan Mockorange, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has simple, opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges and gray-green foliage. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade.

Philadelphus pueblanus is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
