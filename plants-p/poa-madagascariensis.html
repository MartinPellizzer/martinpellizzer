Poa madagascariensis

Poa madagascariensis, commonly known as the Madagascar bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted foliage and compact growth habit, often forming dense mats on rocky or sandy soils. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny blue to violet flowers. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is adapted to high-altitude regions.

Due to its ornamental appeal and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
