Polygala puberula var. ovalis

Polygala puberula var. ovalis, commonly known as the oval spurred gentian, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, clustered flowers that typically range in color from blue to purple. The flowers have distinctive spurred structures, which are adaptations for pollination by specific insect species. The plant thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in coastal or inland areas with moderate rainfall.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
