Potentilla tiroliensis

Potentilla tiroliensis, commonly known as the Alpine cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its low-growing habit and showy yellow flowers. The plant typically thrives in well-drained soils and sunny alpine environments. Its leaves are usually compound, with five leaflets, and it produces small, round fruits called achenes.

Potentilla tiroliensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in alpine ecosystems.
