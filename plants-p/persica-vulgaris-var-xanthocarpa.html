Persica vulgaris var. xanthocarpa

Persica vulgaris var. xanthocarpa, commonly known as the yellow peach, is a botanical variant of the peach tree characterized by its vibrant yellow fruit.

The plant belongs to the Rosaceae family and is native to temperate regions of Asia. Its fruits are typically smaller than common peaches and have a distinct, sweet flavor. The plant exhibits typical peach tree morphology, with compound leaves and a branching structure.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and unique fruit characteristics.
