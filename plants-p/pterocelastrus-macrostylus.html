Pterocelastrus macrostylus

Pterocelastrus macrostylus, commonly known as the large-styled Chinese hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of small, white flowers. The plant produces bright red berries that are edible and often used in traditional Chinese medicine.

It thrives in temperate climates and is valued for its ornamental beauty and medicinal properties.
