Pteronia turbinata

Pteronia turbinata, commonly known as the Cape tulip, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, brightly colored flowers that resemble tulips. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and has a compact, bushy habit with narrow, grass-like leaves. Its flowers are usually pink or red and bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Pteronia turbinata is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and coastal landscapes.
