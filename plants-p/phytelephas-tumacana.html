Phytelephas tumacana

Phytelephas tumacana, commonly known as the Amazonian macaw palm, is a tall, slender palm native to the rainforests of South America.

It is characterized by its large, feather-like leaves and a single, unbranched trunk. The plant produces yellow flowers that develop into elongated, woody fruits containing a single seed. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in the understory of tropical forests.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local communities.
