Piper silvigaudens

Piper silvigaudens, commonly known as the forest pepper, is a flowering plant species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many species of pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its climbing habit, with long, slender stems that often twine around tree trunks and branches in its natural habitat. Its leaves are typically alternate, ovate, and have a glossy surface, while small greenish-white flowers are borne in clusters.

The fruit is a small, three-lobed berry that turns red when mature, and it is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
