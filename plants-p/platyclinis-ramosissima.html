Platyclinis ramosissima

Platyclinis ramosissima, commonly known as the Brazilian rosewood, is a small evergreen shrub native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has a compact growth habit with multiple branches, giving it a bushy appearance. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to tropical and subtropical climates.

Due to its slow growth and rarity, it is sometimes considered a conservation concern.
