Plerandra pachyphylla

Plerandra pachyphylla, commonly known as the thick-leafed arum, is a striking tropical plant native to the rainforests of Queensland, Australia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are thick and waxy, adapted to retain moisture in humid environments. The plant produces striking inflorescences with a spathe that resembles a flower, though the true flowers are small and clustered inside. It thrives in shady, moist conditions and is often found growing in the understorey of dense forests.

Plerandra pachyphylla is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and private collections.
