Primula cephalantha

Primula cephalantha, commonly known as the mountain primrose, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils and cool, moist conditions. The plant features a basal rosette of lance-shaped, hairy leaves and produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, yellow, or pink. Its flowering period occurs in early spring, making it an early bloomers in its native environment.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
