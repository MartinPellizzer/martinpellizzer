Phacelia crenulata var. ambigua

Phacelia crenulata var. ambigua, commonly known as the ambiguous leaf phacelia, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and heart-shaped, serrated leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of blue to purple flowers, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open meadows and along streambanks.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native plant gardens.
