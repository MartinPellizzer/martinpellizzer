Peperomia jaraguana

Peperomia jaraguana, commonly known as the Jaragua peperomia, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to Brazil.

It features thick, waxy leaves that are typically dark green with a metallic sheen, often adorned with subtle variegation. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters, which are usually green or reddish in color. It thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Due to its slow growth and attractive foliage, it is often cultivated as a decorative plant.
