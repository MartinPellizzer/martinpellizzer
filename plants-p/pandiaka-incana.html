Pandiaka incana

Pandiaka incana, commonly known as the white thistle, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage and tall, spiny stems that bear purple to pink flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a fuzzy texture and is often found in rocky or sandy soils. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it a hardy addition to gardens.

The flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological importance.
