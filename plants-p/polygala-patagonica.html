Polygala patagonica

Polygala patagonica, commonly known as the Patagonian milk-vetch, is a perennial herb native to South America, particularly Argentina and Chile.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a hairy texture, while its flowers are usually pink or purple.

Polygala patagonica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
