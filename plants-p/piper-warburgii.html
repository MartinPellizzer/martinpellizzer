Piper warburgii

Piper warburgii, commonly known as the West Indian pepper or Brazilian pepper tree, is a flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to the Caribbean and parts of South America, where it thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves, which are often used in traditional medicine. Its small, greenish-yellow flowers are clustered in spikes, and it produces small, round berries that are edible but have a peppery taste.

The species is also known for its rapid growth and invasive tendencies in certain regions.
