Panicum lachnanthum

Panicum lachnanthum, commonly known as silky panicgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its fine, silky hairs that cover the leaves and stems, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. Its inflorescence consists of a loose, open panicle with small, spikelet-like flowers.

Silky panicgrass plays an important role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
