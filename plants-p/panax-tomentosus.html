Panax tomentosus

Panax tomentosus, commonly known as the Japanese ginseng, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Araliaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, fleshy roots that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments and can reach a height of up to 1 meter. Its leaves are alternate, compound, and have a rough, hairy texture, while its flowers are small and greenish-white.

The botanical features of Panax tomentosus make it a valuable species in both ecological and medicinal contexts.
