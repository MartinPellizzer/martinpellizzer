Paspalum polychaetum

Paspalum polychaetum, commonly known as the hairy paspalum, is a perennial grass native to South America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and long, narrow leaves with a hairy texture. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist to wet environments, often found in marshy areas and along riverbanks.

Paspalum polychaetum is known for its adaptability and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
