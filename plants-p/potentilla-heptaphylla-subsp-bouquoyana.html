Potentilla heptaphylla subsp. bouquoyana

Potentilla heptaphylla subsp.

bouquoyana, commonly known as the Bouquoy's cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces showy yellow flowers in late summer.

The plant has compound leaves with five leaflets, which are typically oval and slightly hairy. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
