Potentilla Foersteriana Var. Brassii

Brass's cinquefoil, scientifically known as Potentilla foersteriana var. brassii, is a striking member of the Rosaceae family native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. This perennial plant is recognized for its vibrant yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, adding a splash of color to rocky and alpine environments. With its compact growth habit and attractive foliage, it is a favorite among horticulturists and garden enthusiasts. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a resilient choice for rock gardens and borders. Despite its beauty, it remains relatively obscure in popular gardening circles, despite its ornamental value and ecological importance.
