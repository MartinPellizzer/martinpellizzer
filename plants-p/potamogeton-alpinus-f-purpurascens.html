Potamogeton alpinus f. purpurascens

Potamogeton alpinus f. purpurascens, commonly known as the purple-stemmed pondweed, is a variant of the alpine pondweed.

It is a submerged aquatic plant characterized by its slender, cylindrical stems that often display a distinctive purplish coloration. The leaves are long and narrow, typically arranged in whorls around the stem, and they are often finely serrated. This plant thrives in freshwater environments such as lakes, ponds, and slow-moving streams.

It plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
