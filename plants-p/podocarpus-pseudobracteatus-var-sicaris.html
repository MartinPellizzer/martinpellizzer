Podocarpus pseudobracteatus var. sicaris

Podocarpus pseudobracteatus var. sicaris, commonly known as the Sicilian podocarp, is a slow-growing evergreen tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It features lanceolate leaves that are dark green and glossy on top, with a lighter underside. The plant produces small, spherical cones that contain seeds, typical of the Podocarpaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in horticultural settings.
