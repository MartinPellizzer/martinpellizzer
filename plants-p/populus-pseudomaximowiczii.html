Populus pseudomaximowiczii

Populus pseudomaximowiczii, commonly known as the Manchurian poplar, is a deciduous tree native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Salicaceae family and is characterized by its lanceolate leaves with a serrated margin. The tree produces small, inconspicuous flowers in catkins, which are typical of the willow family. Its bark is smooth and grayish-brown, often peeling in thin strips.

This species is valued for its rapid growth and adaptability to various soil types.
