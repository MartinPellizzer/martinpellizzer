Pittosporum discolor

Pittosporum discolor, commonly known as the variable pittosporum, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its aromatic, fragrant leaves and clusters of white or pinkish flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has dark green, leathery foliage that provides a striking contrast to its showy blossoms. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal.

Pittosporum discolor is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and birds.
