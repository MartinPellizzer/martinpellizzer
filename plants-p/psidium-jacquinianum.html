Psidium jacquinianum

Psidium jacquinianum, commonly known as the Brazilian guava, is a small evergreen tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its oval-shaped, dark green leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible berries similar to guavas. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial shade.

The fruit is often used in local cuisines and has medicinal properties in traditional remedies.
