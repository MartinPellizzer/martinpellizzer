Pulsatilla multifida

Pulsatilla multifida, commonly known as the pasque flower, is a herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its showy, showy yellow or orange flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has a short, stout stem with palmately divided, hairy leaves that are typically gray-green in color. It thrives in well-drained, calcareous soils and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky areas.

Pulsatilla multifida is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
