Plantago fonckii

Plantago fonckii, commonly known as the Fonck's plantain, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Africa and the Middle East.

It typically grows in arid or semi-arid regions, often in rocky or sandy soils. The plant features rosettes of broad, waxy leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white hairs. Its flowering stems bear small, greenish-white flowers arranged in dense clusters.

This species is valued for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally in herbal remedies.
