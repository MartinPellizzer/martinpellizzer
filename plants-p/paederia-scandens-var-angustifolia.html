Paederia scandens var. angustifolia

Paederia scandens var. angustifolia, commonly known as narrow-leaf paederia, is a climbing vine native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive veining pattern and small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. The plant produces small, round berries that are often red when mature, containing seeds dispersed by animals. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forests or along stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it an interesting subject for ecological studies and horticultural cultivation.
