Pyrrosia penangiana

Pyrrosia penangiana, commonly known as the Penang fern, is a species of epiphytic fern native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its leathery, evergreen fronds. The plant typically grows on trees or rocks in humid, shaded environments. Its fronds are pinnate, with lanceolate to ovate-shaped pinnae, and it produces small, clustered spore cases.

Pyrrosia penangiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical gardens.
