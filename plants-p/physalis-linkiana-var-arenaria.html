Physalis linkiana var. arenaria

Physalis linkiana var. arenaria, commonly known as the desert cape gooseberry, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, spiny calyces that enclose the fruit, which resembles a small, inflated balloon. The plant produces yellow-orange berries that are edible and often used in local cuisines. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Its botanical features make it a unique and resilient species in its native habitat.
