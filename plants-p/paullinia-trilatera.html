Paullinia trilatera

Paullinia trilatera, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Sapindaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are trifoliate, meaning they are divided into three parts. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by bright red, fleshy berries. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been introduced to other regions, sometimes becoming invasive.

The plant contains various bioactive compounds, making it of interest in traditional medicine and pharmacological research.
