Piper longivaginans

Piper longivaginans, commonly known as the long sheath pepper, is a climbing shrub native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many species of pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its elongated, sheathing leaves that wrap around the stems, providing structural support. Its small, greenish-yellow flowers are followed by berries that turn red when mature.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is occasionally used in traditional medicine.
