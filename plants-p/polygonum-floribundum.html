Polygonum floribundum

Polygonum floribundum, commonly known as the flowering knotweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It features upright stems that are often reddish in color and are covered with small, rounded leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, pinkish to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found near streams or in forested areas.

Polygonum floribundum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
