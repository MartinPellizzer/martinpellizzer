Plectranthus semayatensis

Plectranthus semayatensis, commonly known as the Malawi plectranthus, is a flowering plant native to eastern Africa.

It is characterized by its aromatic, opposite leaves and produces small, tubular flowers that are typically violet or purple in color. The plant is often grown as an ornamental due to its attractive foliage and fragrance. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

Plectranthus semayatensis is also valued for its potential medicinal properties in traditional herbal practices.
