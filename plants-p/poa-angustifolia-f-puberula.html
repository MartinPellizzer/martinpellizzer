Poa angustifolia f. puberula

Poa angustifolia f. puberula, commonly known as the pubescent narrowleaf bentgrass, is a variant of the narrowleaf bentgrass species.

It is characterized by its fine, grass-like foliage with a distinctive pubescent (hairy) texture on the leaf blades. This form typically grows in moist, temperate environments, often found in meadows and along stream banks. The plant has a compact growth habit and produces small, spike-like inflorescences.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural study.
