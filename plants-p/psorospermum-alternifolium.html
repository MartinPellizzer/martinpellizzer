Psorospermum alternifolium

Psorospermum alternifolium, commonly known as the Indian sandalwood, is a small evergreen tree native to the western Ghats of India.

It is characterized by its alternate, elliptical leaves that are glossy and dark green in color. The plant produces small, white flowers that are followed by woody, oval-shaped fruits. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in dry, well-drained soils.

Due to its aromatic wood, it has significant economic and medicinal value.
