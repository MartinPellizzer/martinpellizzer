Physalis clarionensis

Physalis clarionensis, commonly known as the Clarion gooseberry, is a species of flowering plant in the nightshade family, Solanaceae.

It is native to Mexico and is characterized by its distinctive inflated calyx that encloses the fruit. The plant produces small, yellow-orange berries that are edible when ripe. The leaves are alternate, ovate, and covered in fine hairs.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.
