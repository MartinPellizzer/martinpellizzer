Pellaea lozani

Pellaea lozani, commonly known as the Lozan's cliff brake, is a species of fern native to South America.

It belongs to the family Pellaeaceae and is characterized by its compact, rosette-like growth habit. The plant features dark green, leathery fronds with a distinctive, serrated margin. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as rocky crevices and forest floors.

Pellaea lozani is notable for its ability to withstand harsh environmental conditions, making it a resilient and ecologically important species.
