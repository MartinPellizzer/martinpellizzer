Paspalum dispar var. marahuacense

Paspalum dispar var. marahuacense, commonly known as the Marahuacan paspalum, is a perennial grass species native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically less than 10 cm in length. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with pale green to brown flowers, which are wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist to wet habitats, such as marshes and seasonally flooded areas, and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

This variety is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
