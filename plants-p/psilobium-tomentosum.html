Psilobium tomentosum

Psilobium tomentosum, commonly known as the hairy psilobium, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous traits and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant is distinguished by its hairy, ovate leaves and small, pea-like flowers that typically bloom in clusters. Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in horticultural settings.
