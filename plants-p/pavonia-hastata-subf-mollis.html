Pavonia hastata subf. mollis

Pavonia hastata subf.

mollis, commonly known as the soft-stemmed pavonia, is a perennial shrub native to the southeastern United States. It features slender, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often glandular-dotted.

The plant produces showy, pink to red bract-like flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or wetland habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
