Polygala eminensis

Polygala eminensis, commonly known as the Ethiopian milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the highlands of Ethiopia.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or semi-arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its flowers are usually pale yellow or greenish, with a distinctive structure that resembles a spurred petal.

Polygala eminensis is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and limited geographic distribution.
