Phoradendron diminutivum

Phoradendron diminutivum, commonly known as the dwarf mistletoe, is a parasitic plant that grows on various species of trees, primarily in the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its small, evergreen leaves and white or pink flowers that bloom in late winter or early spring. The plant produces small, fleshy berries that are a food source for birds and other animals. Its life cycle involves a complex relationship with its host tree, where it relies on the host for nutrients and structural support.

The plant's unique morphology and ecological role make it an interesting subject in botanical studies.
