Peperomia quadratifolia

Peperomia quadratifolia, commonly known as the four-leaf peperomia, is a compact evergreen shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It features oval, dark green leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern, with some leaves exhibiting a distinctive four-lobed shape. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers that appear in clusters during the summer months. It is well-suited for indoor cultivation due to its low maintenance requirements and ability to thrive in indirect light.

Its unique foliage makes it a popular choice for decorative plantings and terrariums.
