Protea cygnea

Protea cygnea, commonly known as the silver protea, is a striking South African plant belonging to the Proteaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescences that resemble a crown, composed of numerous small, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in coastal regions, thriving in well-drained, acidic soils. Its flowers are predominantly white or pale pink, with a central cluster of darker petals.

Protea cygnea is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
