Psilocaulon annuum

Psilocaulon annuum, commonly known as the annual psilocaulon, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its erect, branching stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces small, dry fruits that contain minute seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological niche, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
