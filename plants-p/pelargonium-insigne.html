Pelargonium insigne

Pelargonium insigne, commonly known as the Cape geranium, is a species of flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Geraniaceae family and is characterized by its succulent, hairy leaves and showy, five-petaled flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid conditions. It produces small, red or pink flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Pelargonium insigne is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance and drought tolerance.
