Pandanus furcatus var. penangensis

Pandanus furcatus var. penangensis, commonly known as the Penang screw pine, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, spirally arranged leaves that are often used in traditional crafts. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. Its roots are fibrous and can be used for making rope and mats.

This species thrives in coastal and brackish water environments, making it well adapted to saline conditions.
