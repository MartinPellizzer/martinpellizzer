Plagiotheca fallax

Plagiotheca fallax, commonly known as the false liverwort, is a small, terrestrial liverwort belonging to the family Porellaceae.

It is characterized by its prostrate growth habit and dense, closely packed leaves that form a cushion-like structure. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky crevices. Its leaves are usually green to reddish in color and have a smooth, waxy surface.

Plagiotheca fallax plays a role in soil stabilization and provides microhabitat for various invertebrates.
