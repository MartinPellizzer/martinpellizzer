Polygala rupestris f. angustifolia

Polygala rupestris f. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to rocky and arid environments.

It is characterized by its slender, angular stems and small, clustered flowers that range from white to pale pink. The leaves are typically narrow and linear, adapted to reduce water loss in its harsh habitat. It belongs to the family Polygalaceae, which includes many species with unique floral structures.

This plant is often found in limestone soils and is well-suited to dry, sunny conditions.
