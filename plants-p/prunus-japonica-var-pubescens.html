Prunus japonica var. pubescens

Prunus japonica var. pubescens, commonly known as the Japanese cherry plum, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and lance-shaped leaves that turn reddish in the fall. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pink flowers in early spring, which are followed by small, yellowish fruits. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens.

The variety 'pubescens' is distinguished by its hairy stems and leaves, adding to its unique botanical appearance.
