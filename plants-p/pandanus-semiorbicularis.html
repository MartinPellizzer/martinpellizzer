Pandanus semiorbicularis

Pandanus semiorbicularis, commonly known as the broad-leaf pandanus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Pandanaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including parts of Indonesia and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its large, broad, sword-shaped leaves that are often used in traditional crafts and as thatching material. It produces small, clustered inflorescences with yellowish flowers, which are pollinated by insects and birds.

The plant thrives in coastal areas and is often found growing near mangroves or in sandy soils.
