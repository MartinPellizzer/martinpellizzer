Polypodium pseudaustrale

Polypodium pseudaustrale, commonly known as the New Zealand tree fern, is a member of the Polypodiaceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and a crown of large, feather-like fronds. The plant is native to New Zealand and thrives in moist, shaded environments. Its rhizome is thick and fibrous, supporting the growth of the fronds.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in forest ecosystems.
