Prunus fortunensis

Prunus fortunensis, commonly known as the Chinese bird cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its ornamental value due to its showy white to pink flowers. The plant produces small, dark red berries that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal in gardens.

Prunus fortunensis is also valued for its use in traditional medicine.
