Penaea myrtoides

Penaea myrtoides, commonly known as the silky myrtle, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes eucalyptus and other aromatic plants. The plant is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs. Penaea myrtoides produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring, attracting various pollinators.

It is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and pleasant scent.
