Podocarpus nobilis

Podocarpus nobilis, commonly known as the satinwood or noble podocarp, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Podocarpaceae family and is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and cylindrical cones. The tree can grow up to 30 meters tall, with a straight trunk and a conical crown. Its leaves are leathery and arranged in spirals, and it produces small, woody seeds enclosed in fleshy, orange-red cones.

The plant is valued for its durable timber and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.
