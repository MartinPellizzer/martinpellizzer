Piper pervenulosum

Piper pervenulosum, commonly known as the creeping pepper vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes various species of pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its slender, trailing stems and small, ovate leaves with a glossy surface. Its flowers are unisexual, with separate male and female flowers on the same plant, and it produces small, round berries that are typically green when immature and turn red when mature.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
