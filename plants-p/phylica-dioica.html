Phylica dioica

Phylica dioica, commonly known as the Natal glorybower, is a small evergreen shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, showy yellow flowers that appear in clusters during the summer months. The plant has narrow, leathery leaves that are arranged in opposite pairs along the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for coastal gardens.

Phylica dioica is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and birds.
