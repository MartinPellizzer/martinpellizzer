Pinus densiflora f. erecta

Pinus densiflora f. erecta, commonly known as the upright form of the Japanese black pine, is a variant of the Japanese black pine characterized by its more upright growth habit.

This form exhibits dense foliage and a conical shape, making it visually distinct from the typical form of the species. It is native to Japan and thrives in well-drained, acidic soils. The plant produces long, slender needles that are typically dark green in color.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental tree due to its aesthetic appeal and hardiness.
