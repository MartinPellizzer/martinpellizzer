Phanera glauca subsp. tenuiflora

Phanera glauca subsp.

tenuiflora, commonly known as the narrow-flowered phanera, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia. It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in moist, tropical environments.

The plant features simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical in shape and have a smooth or slightly hairy surface. Its flowers are small and clustered, often with a pale blue or white color, and it produces woody capsules as fruit.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in horticultural settings.
