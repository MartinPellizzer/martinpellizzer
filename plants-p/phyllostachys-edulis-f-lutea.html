Phyllostachys edulis f. lutea

Phyllostachys edulis f. lutea, commonly known as the golden bamboo, is a variant of the common bamboo species characterized by its striking golden-yellow culms.

This ornamental bamboo is native to eastern Asia and is valued for its aesthetic appeal in gardens and landscapes. The plant exhibits a rapid growth rate, making it suitable for erosion control and as a living fence. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, contributing to its elegant appearance.

While it requires well-drained soil and full sun, it is relatively low maintenance once established.
