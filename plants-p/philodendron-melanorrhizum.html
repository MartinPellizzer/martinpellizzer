Philodendron melanorrhizum

Philodendron melanorrhizum, commonly known as the black-root philodendron, is a tropical climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its dark, almost black, rhizomes, which give it its common name. The plant features heart-shaped, waxy leaves that are typically a deep green with a glossy texture. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its unique appearance and ease of care, it is a popular choice for indoor gardening.
