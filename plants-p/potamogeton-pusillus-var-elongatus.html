Potamogeton pusillus var. elongatus

Potamogeton pusillus var. elongatus, commonly known as the slender pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments across Europe and parts of Asia.

It features long, narrow, ribbon-like leaves that are typically less than 1 millimeter in width, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually arranged in spikelets, which develop into elongated, cylindrical fruits. It thrives in slow-moving or still waters with muddy or sandy substrates, often forming dense underwater vegetation.

This species plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various waterfowl and invertebrates.
