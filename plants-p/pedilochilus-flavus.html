Pedilochilus flavus

Pedilochilus flavus, commonly known as the golden pedilochilus, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and striking yellow flowers with intricate patterns. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest undergrowth. Its leaves are broad and elliptical, often with a waxy texture.

Pedilochilus flavus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated by orchid enthusiasts for its vibrant color and unique floral structure.
