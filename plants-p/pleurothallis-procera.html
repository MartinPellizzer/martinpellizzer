Pleurothallis procera

Pleurothallis procera, commonly known as the Ecuadorian orchid, is a species native to the cloud forests of Ecuador.

It is a small epiphytic orchid that typically grows on trees or rocks in humid, shaded environments. The plant features delicate, narrow leaves and produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink. Its flowers are often clustered in a raceme and bloom during the warmer months.

Due to its limited habitat and specific environmental requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
