Polypodiodes pinnatifida

Polypodiodes pinnatifida, commonly known as the fan-leaf polypody, is a fern species characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds.

The plant has a rhizomatous growth habit, with creeping underground stems that allow it to spread across forest floors. Its leaves are pinnately divided, giving it a feathery appearance, and are typically bright green with a slightly glossy texture. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in temperate forests and along stream banks.

The plant produces sporangia on the underside of its fronds, contributing to its role in the ecological cycle of ferns.
