Pleurothallis quadrata

Pleurothallis quadrata, commonly known as the four-angled orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its compact, epiphytic growth habit. The plant produces small, delicate flowers that are typically greenish-white with a faintly colored lip. Its leaves are usually narrow and arranged in a rosette pattern, which helps in water retention.

Pleurothallis quadrata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in shaded, humid environments.
