Passiflora transversa

Passiflora transversa, commonly known as the Transverse Passionflower, is a climbing vine native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, intricate flowers that feature five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments surrounding the reproductive structures. The plant produces blue or purple berries that are typically consumed by birds and other animals, aiding in seed dispersal. Its leaves are ovate with a serrated margin and often have a hairy texture.

Passiflora transversa is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated as a decorative plant.
