Penstemon attenuatus var. attenuatus

Penstemon attenuatus var. attenuatus, commonly known as the slender beardtongue, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and spikes of tubular, pink to purple flowers. The leaves are linear and often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its drought-resistant nature. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

This species is valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
