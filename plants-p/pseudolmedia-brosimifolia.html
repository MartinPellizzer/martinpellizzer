Pseudolmedia brosimifolia

Pseudolmedia brosimifolia, commonly known as the brosima-like mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches to the branches of its host trees.

It lacks chlorophyll and relies on its host for water and nutrients, while producing its own sugars through photosynthesis using light absorbed by its leaves. The plant has small, oval-shaped leaves and produces white or yellowish flowers, followed by small, fleshy berries. It is typically found in tropical and subtropical regions, often in forest canopies.

Its unique parasitic relationship with host trees makes it an interesting subject for ecological studies.
