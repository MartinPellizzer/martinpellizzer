Picris saxatilis

Picris saxatilis, commonly known as rock chicory, is a flowering plant native to rocky and arid regions of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous small florets. The plant typically grows in crevices and on slopes, adapting to poor soil conditions and harsh climates. Its leaves are basal, often covered in fine hairs, and its bright yellow flowers bloom in early summer.

Picris saxatilis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used historically in traditional medicine.
