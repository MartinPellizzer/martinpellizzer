Pleurothallis brachiata

Pleurothallis brachiata, commonly known as the orchid spider, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is a small, epiphytic plant that typically grows on trees in humid, shaded environments. The plant has slender, wiry stems and produces delicate, greenish-white flowers with a subtle fragrance. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often arranged in a spiral pattern around the stem.

Pleurothallis brachiata is valued in horticulture for its unique appearance and ease of cultivation.
