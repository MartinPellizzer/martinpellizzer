Primula nivalis var. turkestanica

Primula nivalis var. turkestanica, commonly known as the Turkestan snowbell, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its erect stem and clusters of bell-shaped, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky habitats at high elevations. Its leaves are basal, lanceolate, and covered in a dense layer of fine, white hairs.

This variety is notable for its cold tolerance and is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value.
