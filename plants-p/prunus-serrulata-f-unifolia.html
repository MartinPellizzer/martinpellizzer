Prunus serrulata f. unifolia

Prunus serrulata f. unifolia, commonly known as the Japanese cherry blossom or sakura, is a cultivar of the Prunus serrulata species.

It is characterized by its delicate, single-layered flowers that bloom in early spring, often in shades of pink and white. The plant is a deciduous shrub or small tree, typically reaching heights of 5 to 10 meters. Its leaves are ovate with finely serrated edges, giving it the species name "serrulata.".

This variety is highly valued for its ornamental beauty and is widely cultivated in gardens and parks across Japan and other parts of the world.
