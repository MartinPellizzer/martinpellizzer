Peniculifera

Peniculifera, commonly known as the sea holly, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking blue or purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spiny fruits that contain seeds. Peniculifera is often cultivated for its ornamental value in coastal gardens.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it a hardy addition to maritime landscapes.
