Penthea melaleuca

Penthea melaleuca, commonly known as the narrow-leaved tea tree, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and small, white or pink flowers. The plant is known for its aromatic foliage and is often used in traditional bush tinctures. It thrives in well-drained soils and is commonly found in dry, open habitats.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological and horticultural contexts.
