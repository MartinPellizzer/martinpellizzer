Piper semitarium

Piper semitarium, commonly known as the creeping woodbine, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes over 2,000 species of flowering plants. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are typically glossy and dark green. It produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by berries that are toxic to humans and animals.

Its rapid growth habit makes it both a useful ornamental plant and a potential invasive species in certain environments.
