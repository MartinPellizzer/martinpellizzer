Palura paniculata var. pubescens

Palura paniculata var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy palura, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine, soft hairs, giving the plant its "hairy" appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

This species is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
