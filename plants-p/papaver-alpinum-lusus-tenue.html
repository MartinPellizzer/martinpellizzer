Papaver alpinum lusus tenue

Papaver alpinum lusus tenue, commonly known as the slender alpine poppy, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is a member of the Papaveraceae family, characterized by its showy, often brightly colored flowers. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky slopes, thriving in cool, high-altitude environments. Its flowers are usually pale yellow to orange, with a distinctive, spurred corona.

The slender stems and narrow leaves are adaptations to its harsh, windy habitat.
