Potentilla tabernaemontani subvar. grandiflora

Potentilla tabernaemontani subvar. grandiflora, commonly known as the large-flowered cinquefoil, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It features compound leaves with five oval leaflets and produces showy, bright yellow flowers in late spring to early summer. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings. Its ornamental value comes from its vibrant blooms and compact growth habit.

It is also valued for its hardiness and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
