Pterocaulon lorentzii

Pterocaulon lorentzii, commonly known as the Lorentz's pterocaulon, is a perennial herb native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in dense clusters, which bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is typically found in dry, open habitats.

Due to its limited distribution and specific ecological requirements, it is considered a rare and specialized species.
