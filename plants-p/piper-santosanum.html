Piper santosanum

Piper santosanum, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, greenish-white flowers. The plant produces small, red berries that are an important food source for various birds and mammals. It is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, growing in a variety of soil types.

Due to its ornamental value, it has been introduced to other parts of the world, sometimes becoming invasive.
