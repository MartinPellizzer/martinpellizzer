Potamogeton nanus

Potamogeton nanus, commonly known as the dwarf pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments across North America.

It has slender, branched stems that are often less than 10 cm in length, and its small, oval leaves are arranged alternately along the stem. The plant produces tiny, green flowers that are typically arranged in spike-like clusters, and it is often found in shallow, still waters.

Potamogeton nanus plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
