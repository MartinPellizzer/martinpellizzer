Polygala polymorpha

Polygala polymorpha, commonly known as the variable milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia and Australia.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its delicate, often branched stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. Its flowers are usually pale green to yellowish in color, with five petals that are often unequal in size.

Polygala polymorpha is known for its adaptability to arid conditions and is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value.
