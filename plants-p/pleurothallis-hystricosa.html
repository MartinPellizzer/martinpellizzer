Pleurothallis hystricosa

Pleurothallis hystricosa, commonly known as the spiny orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, often hairy or spiny floral structures. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are usually small and clustered, displaying a range of colors from white to pink and purple.

Pleurothallis hystricosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in greenhouses or as a houseplant.
