Petalostemon roseus

Petalostemon roseus, commonly known as the rose petal plant, is a flowering species native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its showy, rose-like petals that range in color from pink to deep red. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 1 meter, with small, elliptical leaves that are glossy and dark green. It blooms throughout the year, producing clusters of fragrant flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses for its aesthetic appeal.
