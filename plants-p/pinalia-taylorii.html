Pinalia taylorii

Pinalia taylorii, commonly known as the Taylor's pinyon, is a small evergreen shrub native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit, needle-like leaves, and small, edible cones that mature to a dark brown color. The plant is adapted to harsh, dry environments, with deep root systems that allow it to survive in nutrient-poor soils. It plays an important ecological role by providing shelter and food for local wildlife.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and unique foliage.
