Pyrola cordata

Pyrola cordata, commonly known as the heartleaf pyrola, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its evergreen foliage. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist forest understories, preferring acidic soils. Its leaves are oval and leathery, with a waxy coating that helps retain moisture.

Small white or pink flowers appear in late spring, followed by edible, red berries that are a food source for various wildlife.
