Plagiobothrys californicus var. ursinus

Plagiobothrys californicus var. ursinus, commonly known as the California milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, rocky soils and open habitats. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually pink to purple in color. Its leaves are compound, with thread-like leaflets, and it has a fibrous root system.

It is adapted to arid environments and is often found in chaparral and coastal sage scrub ecosystems.
