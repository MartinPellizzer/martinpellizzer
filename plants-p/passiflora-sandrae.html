Passiflora sandrae

Passiflora sandrae, commonly known as the Sandrae passionflower, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, intricate flowers that feature a blend of blue, purple, and white petals, along with prominent filaments and a crown-like structure. The plant produces small, edible berries that are typically yellow or orange when ripe. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil to flourish.

Passiflora sandrae is valued not only for its ornamental beauty but also for its ecological importance in supporting local pollinators.
