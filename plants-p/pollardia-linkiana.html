Pollardia linkiana

Pollardia linkiana, commonly known as the red-flowered orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, bright red petals and a compact, upright growth habit. The plant typically blooms during the rainy season, producing clusters of fragrant flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Botanically, it belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is valued for its aesthetic qualities in horticulture.
