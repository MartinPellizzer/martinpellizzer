Pouteria acuminata

Pouteria acuminata, commonly known as the Brazilian nut tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Amazon basin.

It is characterized by its large, compound leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into hard, woody fruits. The fruit contains a single large seed, which is the source of the commercially valuable Brazil nut. The tree can grow up to 30 meters tall and has a dense canopy that provides habitat for various wildlife species.

It plays an important ecological role in the rainforest and is also valued for its economic contribution.
