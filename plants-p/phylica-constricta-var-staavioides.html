Phylica constricta var. staavioides

Phylica constricta var. staavioides, commonly known as the Natal silver tree, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in summer. The plant is evergreen, with a rounded, shrub-like form that can grow up to 3 meters tall. Its leaves are narrow and leathery, providing a distinctive appearance.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in coastal conditions.
