Psidium benthamianum

Psidium benthamianum, commonly known as the Benthams' guava, is a small evergreen shrub or tree native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically green when unripe and turn yellow or red when mature. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires a warm, humid climate for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is occasionally cultivated in subtropical gardens.
