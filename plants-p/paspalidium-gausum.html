Paspalidium gausum

Paspalidium gausum, commonly known as the Gausum grass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions.

Paspalidium gausum is often found in grasslands and open areas, contributing to the ecological diversity of its habitat.
