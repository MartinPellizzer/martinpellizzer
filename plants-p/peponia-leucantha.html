Peponia leucantha

Peponia leucantha, commonly known as the white-flowered passionvine, is a climbing vine native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the family Passifloraceae and is characterized by its showy, white flowers with a distinctive crown of filaments. The plant produces small, edible fruits that are often used in local cuisines. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a rough texture, while its stems are woody and covered in fine hairs.

Peponia leucantha thrives in warm, humid environments and is often found in forest understories or along riverbanks.
