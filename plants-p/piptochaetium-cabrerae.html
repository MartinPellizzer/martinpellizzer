Piptochaetium cabrerae

Piptochaetium cabrerae, commonly known as the Cabrera's grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern South America.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted leaves and compact growth habit, often forming dense mats in its natural habitat. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. Its inflorescence consists of a narrow spike with small, two-lobed spikelets.

Piptochaetium cabrerae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its drought tolerance and visual interest.
