Pinus balfouriana var. austrina

Pinus balfouriana var. austrina, commonly known as the coastal Douglas fir, is a variant of the Douglas fir native to the Pacific coast of North America.

It is a coniferous evergreen tree characterized by its needle-like leaves and cone-bearing structure. The plant typically grows in coastal regions, where it thrives in moist, temperate climates. Its bark is thick and fissured, providing protection against environmental stressors.

This variant is notable for its adaptability to coastal conditions and its ecological importance in forest ecosystems.
