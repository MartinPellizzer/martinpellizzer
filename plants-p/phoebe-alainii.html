Phoebe alainii

Phoebe alainii, commonly known as the Alain's phoebe, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of the Pacific Islands.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes other aromatic plants such as laurel and cinnamon. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are typically elliptical in shape and have a smooth, waxy surface. Its small, fragrant flowers are usually greenish-white in color and appear in clusters, attracting pollinators like bees and flies.

The fruit is a small, spherical drupe that contains a single seed, contributing to its propagation in its native habitat.
