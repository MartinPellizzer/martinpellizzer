Primula laxiuscula

Primula laxiuscula, commonly known as the slender primrose, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of central and southern China.

It features upright, branched stems that bear clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. The leaves are lanceolate, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface, and are arranged alternately along the stem. This species thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in shaded woodland areas.

Its flowers bloom in early spring, adding a delicate touch of color to its natural habitat.
