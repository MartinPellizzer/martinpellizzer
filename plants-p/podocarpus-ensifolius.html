Podocarpus ensifolius

Podocarpus ensifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaved podocarp, is a evergreen coniferous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Podocarpaceae family and is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and cylindrical cones. The plant has needle-like leaves that are typically arranged in whorls around the stem. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland forests.

Podocarpus ensifolius is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine.
