Pultenaea psoraleoides

Pultenaea psoraleoides, commonly known as the silver wattle, is a shrub native to southern Australia.

It features slender, often hairy stems and small, bright yellow flowers that appear in clusters. The leaves are typically narrow and slightly curved, with a silvery or greyish underside. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and dry inland regions.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in gardens for its hardy nature.
