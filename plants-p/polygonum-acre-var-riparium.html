Polygonum acre var. riparium

Polygonum acre var. riparium, commonly known as river smartweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to wetland and riparian habitats.

It features erect, branching stems that are often reddish in color, and it produces small, clustered flowers in shades of pink to purple. The leaves are alternate, ovate to lanceolate, and have a distinctive waxy coating. It thrives in moist to aquatic environments and is often found along riverbanks and pond edges.

This plant plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for aquatic and terrestrial wildlife.
