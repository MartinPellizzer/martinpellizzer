Phyllostachys nigra var. muchisasa

Phyllostachys nigra var. muchisasa, commonly known as black bamboo, is a variant of the black bamboo species native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its dark, almost black culms that develop a striking purplish hue when exposed to sunlight. The plant is a clumping bamboo that grows in dense stands, reaching heights of up to 10 meters. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a glossy, dark green color.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping and traditional Chinese gardens.
