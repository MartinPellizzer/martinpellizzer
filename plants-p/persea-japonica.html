Persea japonica

Persea japonica, commonly known as the Japanese persimmon, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape. The tree produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by the development of orange to reddish fruits. The fruit is typically achenes, enclosed in a fleshy, sweet pulp, and is often consumed fresh or used in culinary applications.

It thrives in temperate climates and is valued for both its ornamental appeal and edible fruit.
