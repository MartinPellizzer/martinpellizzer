Primula gracilipes

Primula gracilipes, commonly known as the slender primrose, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of East Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, bell-like flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in cool, moist environments, such as forest understories or rocky slopes. It belongs to the Primulaceae family, which includes many other ornamental and medicinal plants.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat preferences, it is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value.
