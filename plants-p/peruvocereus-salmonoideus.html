Peruvocereus salmonoideus

Peruvocereus salmonoideus, commonly known as the salmon-colored cactus, is a species of cactus native to South America, particularly Peru and Ecuador.

It is a columnar cactus characterized by its smooth, pale green to yellowish stems that often have a salmon-colored tinge, especially under certain lighting conditions. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom during the warmer months, typically in the evening. It is adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water, making it suitable for xerophytic gardens.

Its slow growth rate and unique coloration make it a distinctive addition to succulent collections.
