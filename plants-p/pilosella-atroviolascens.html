Pilosella atroviolascens

Pilosella atroviolascens, commonly known as the black violet.

It is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its small, violet-colored flowers and hairy foliage. Native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa, it thrives in rocky or sandy soils. The plant typically grows to a height of about 10 to 30 centimeters, with slender stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves.

Its flowers bloom in early summer, producing both ray and disk florets that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
