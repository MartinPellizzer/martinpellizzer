Pulmonaria collina

Pulmonaria collina, commonly known as lungwort, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe.

It is characterized by its distinctive, spotted leaves that resemble lung tissue, giving it its common name. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink, white, and purple, typically blooming in early spring. It thrives in shady, moist environments and is often found in woodlands and rocky habitats.

Pulmonaria collina is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
