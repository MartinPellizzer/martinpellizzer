Pleurothallis wercklei

Pleurothallis wercklei, commonly known as the golden tassel orchid, is a species of orchid native to the cloud forests of Central and South America.

It is a small, epiphytic orchid that typically grows on trees or rocks in humid, shaded environments. The plant features delicate, linear leaves and produces intricate, pendulous inflorescences with small, fragrant flowers in shades of yellow and green. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered threatened in some regions, highlighting the need for conservation efforts.
