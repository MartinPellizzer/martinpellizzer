Pyrenacantha laetevirens

Pyrenacantha laetevirens, commonly known as the greenleaf pyrenacantha, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, clustered flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, fleshy fruits that are often consumed by local wildlife. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid environments.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
