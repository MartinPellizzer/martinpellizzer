Polemonium pectinatum

Polemonium pectinatum, commonly known as the white fringed gentian, is a herbaceous perennial native to eastern North America.

It features showy, blue to purple flowers with distinctive fringed petals that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and has lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges. It belongs to the family Polemoniaceae and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.

Its flowering period attracts various pollinators, including bees and butterflies.
