Platythelys latifolia

Platythelys latifolia, commonly known as the broad-leafed fern, is a species of fern characterized by its large, broad, and leathery fronds.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky crevices. The plant has a rhizomatous root system that helps it anchor and absorb nutrients from the soil. Its fronds are usually bi-directionally divided, with a distinctive, elegant appearance.

Platythelys latifolia is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often used in horticulture and landscape design.
