Phrynium chrysoleucum

Phrynium chrysoleucum, commonly known as the golden phrynium, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, broad leaves that are typically yellow to green in color. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in spikes. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil.

Phrynium chrysoleucum is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.
