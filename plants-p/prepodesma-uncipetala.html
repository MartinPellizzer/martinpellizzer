Prepodesma uncipetala

Prepodesma uncipetala, commonly known as the hairy false dandelion, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, shaded environments. The plant features alternate, lance-shaped leaves with hairy surfaces, and its flowers are small, yellow, and clustered in a head. It produces a capsule fruit that contains numerous tiny seeds.

The species is characterized by its distinctive morphology and is often found in forest understories.
