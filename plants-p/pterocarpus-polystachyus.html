Pterocarpus polystachyus

Pterocarpus polystachyus, commonly known as Indian rosewood, is a deciduous tree native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its dark, aromatic wood and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by elongated, woody pods containing flat, seed-like structures. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in dry deciduous forests.

Due to its valuable timber, it is also considered an important species for ecological and economic purposes.
