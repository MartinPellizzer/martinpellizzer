Phyllanthus effusus

Phyllanthus effusus, commonly known as the hairy phyllanthus, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, clustered yellow flowers, which are typically followed by distinctive, fleshy, greenish-yellow fruits. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for cultivation in warm climates.

Phyllanthus effusus is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
