Pleurothallis ramificans

Pleurothallis ramificans, commonly known as the branched orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and delicate, often fragrant flowers. The plant typically produces clusters of small, white or pale yellow blossoms that bloom during the warmer months. Its roots are epiphytic, allowing it to thrive on tree bark in humid, shaded environments.

Pleurothallis ramificans is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
