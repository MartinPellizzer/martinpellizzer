Plantago camtschatica

Plantago camtschatica, commonly known as the Siberian plantain, is a perennial herb native to the cold climates of Siberia and northern regions.

It features a rosette of broad, waxy leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in arid environments. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish flowers arranged in a spike, which are pollinated by wind. Its seeds are encased in a sticky, fleshy structure that aids in dispersal by adhering to animals or clothing.

This species is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional herbal remedies.
