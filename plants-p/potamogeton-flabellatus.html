Potamogeton flabellatus

Potamogeton flabellatus, commonly known as the fan-leaf pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in North America.

It has long, ribbon-like leaves that are often arranged in a fan-like pattern, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are typically clustered in spikes, and it blooms from late spring to early summer. It plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.

Potamogeton flabellatus is often found in shallow, slow-moving waters such as ponds and lakes.
