Pycreus globosus var. typicus

Pycreus globosus var. typicus, commonly known as the globular pycreus, is a flowering plant native to specific regions in South America.

It is characterized by its spherical, compact growth habit and produces small, clustered flowers in shades of white and pale yellow. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs, giving it a textured appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its unique morphology and limited distribution, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists alike.
