Pygeum tokangpengii

Pygeum tokangpengii, commonly known as the Philippine rosewood, is a rare and valuable tree species native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Proteaceae family and is characterized by its dense, aromatic wood and distinctive floral structures. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink, followed by hard, woody fruits. Due to its rarity and high demand, it is considered an endangered species and is protected in its native habitat.
