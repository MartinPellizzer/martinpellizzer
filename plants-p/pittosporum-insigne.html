Pittosporum insigne

Pittosporum insigne, commonly known as the silver tree, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage, which gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in late winter or early spring. Its bark is smooth and reddish-brown, often exfoliating to reveal a lighter inner layer.

Pittosporum insigne is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to various soil types.
