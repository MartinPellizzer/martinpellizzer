Piper umbriculum

Piper umbriculum, commonly known as the dark pipevine, is a climbing vine native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many species of flowering plants. The plant is characterized by its dark green, heart-shaped leaves and produces small, greenish-white flowers. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

The plant is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its foliage.
