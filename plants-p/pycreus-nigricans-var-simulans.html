Pycreus nigricans var. simulans

Pycreus nigricans var. simulans, commonly known as the false nightshade, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows to a height of about 1 meter, featuring erect, branched stems covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually white or pale purple, blooming from late spring to early autumn. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture.

The fruit is a capsule containing numerous small, black seeds, which are dispersed by wind.
