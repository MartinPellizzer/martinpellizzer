Passiflora neillii

Passiflora neillii, commonly known as the Neill's passionflower, is a climbing vine native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, complex flowers that feature a blend of blue, purple, and white petals, along with a crown of filaments. The plant produces edible berries that are often used in culinary applications. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Passiflora neillii is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
