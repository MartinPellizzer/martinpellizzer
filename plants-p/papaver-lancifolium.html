Papaver lancifolium

Papaver lancifolium, commonly known as the lancifoliate poppy, is a species of flowering plant in the family Papaveraceae.

It is native to Central Asia and is characterized by its striking, bright red to orange flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white hairs. It grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

The seeds of Papaver lancifolium are used in traditional medicine and are also a source of opium alkaloids.
