Piper perlongipedunculum

Piper perlongipedunculum, commonly known as the long-stemmed pepper vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piper genus, which includes many species of flowering plants in the family Piperaceae. The plant is characterized by its long, slender peduncles that bear clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. Its leaves are typically ovate with a smooth surface and a slightly glossy texture.

The plant produces small, aromatic berries that are often used in traditional medicine and culinary applications.
