Polystichum acrostichoides var. incisum

Polystichum acrostichoides var. incisum, commonly known as the incised wood fern, is a perennial fern native to the eastern United States.

It features a tall, upright rhizome with dark, leathery fronds that are deeply divided into narrow, lance-shaped segments. The fronds are typically bi- or tri-pinnate, with the pinnae bearing toothed or lobed margins. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

The plant produces spore-bearing clusters called sori on the underside of its fronds, contributing to its reproductive strategy.
