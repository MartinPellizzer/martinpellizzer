Porophyllum purpureum

Porophyllum purpureum, commonly known as the purple coneflower, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features showy purple to pink ray florets surrounding a central disk of tubular flowers, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically hairy and gray-green, adapted to thrive in arid environments.

Porophyllum purpureum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ecological benefits in native gardens.
