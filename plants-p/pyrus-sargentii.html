Pyrus sargentii

Pyrus sargentii, commonly known as Sargent's pear, is a species of flowering plant in the family Rosaceae.

It is native to eastern Asia, particularly China, and is characterized by its deciduous nature and ornamental value. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by edible fruits that are typically small and sweet. It is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal and is sometimes used in horticultural settings.

The leaves are simple, alternate, and have a smooth margin, contributing to its visual appeal.
