Piper paniculatum

Piper paniculatum, commonly known as the fragrant pepper plant, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its climbing habit and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that develop into clusters of elongated, cylindrical berries. These berries are edible and have a peppery, spicy flavor, making them a popular ingredient in local cuisines.

Its aromatic properties and ornamental value make it a sought-after plant in both horticulture and culinary applications.
