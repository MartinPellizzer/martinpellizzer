Pyrethrum sibiricum

Pyrethrum sibiricum, commonly known as Siberian pyrethrum, is a flowering plant native to the cold climates of Siberia and northern Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its daisy-like yellow flowers with a central dark brown disk. The plant produces pyrethrin compounds, which are natural insecticides used in organic pest control. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Its hardy nature allows it to survive in harsh, temperate environments.
