Polygonum duclouxii

Polygonum duclouxii, commonly known as the Clump-forming knotweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring upright stems that are often reddish in color. The plant has broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an important species for wetland ecosystems and a subject of study in plant ecology.
