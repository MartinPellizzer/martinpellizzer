Poissonia

Poissonia, commonly known as the "watermelon vine," is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and vibrant, showy flowers that range from yellow to red. The plant produces edible fruits that resemble watermelons in appearance, though they are typically smaller. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture.

Poissonia thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for both ornamental and culinary purposes.
