Phyllanthus delicatissimus

Phyllanthus delicatissimus, commonly known as the delicate phyllanthus, is a small shrub native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are often hairy and have a distinctive, slightly lobed appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clusters, followed by unique, cup-shaped seed capsules.

It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions, making it a resilient and adaptable species.
