Passiflora helleborifolia

Passiflora helleborifolia, commonly known as the white passionflower, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking white flowers that resemble the structure of a heart, with a central crown of filaments. The plant produces edible, tropical fruits that are often used in beverages and culinary applications. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a rough texture, while its stems are woody and covered in fine hairs.

Passiflora helleborifolia is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
