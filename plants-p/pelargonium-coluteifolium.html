Pelargonium coluteifolium

Pelargonium coluteifolium, commonly known as the broad-leaved geranium, is a species native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its large, velvety leaves that are often heart-shaped or rounded. The plant produces clusters of small, pink or red flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
