Platanthera dilatata var. leucostachys

Platanthera dilatata var. leucostachys, commonly known as the white-stemmed lady's tresses, is a perennial orchid native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem that bears a dense, spiraled arrangement of white to pale green flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and wet meadows. Its leaves are linear and arranged in a basal rosette, and it produces a single, elongated inflorescence.

The species is notable for its distinctive floral structure and is often found in association with specific mycorrhizal fungi.
