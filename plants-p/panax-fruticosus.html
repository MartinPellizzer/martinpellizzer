Panax fruticosus

Panax fruticosus, commonly known as wild ginseng, is a perennial plant native to the forests of East Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, branched root system and its palmately compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into red berries. It thrives in shaded, moist environments with well-drained soil.

Due to its medicinal properties, it is highly valued in traditional herbal medicine.
