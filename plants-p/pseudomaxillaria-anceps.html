Pseudomaxillaria anceps

Pseudomaxillaria anceps, commonly known as the narrow-leafed orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces delicate, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pale yellow, typically during the spring and summer seasons. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained, acidic soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
