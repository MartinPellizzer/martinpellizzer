Psidium guayaquilense

Psidium guayaquilense, commonly known as the yellow guava, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, ovate leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces edible berries that are typically yellow or orange when ripe, with a sweet and aromatic flavor. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit.

Psidium guayaquilense is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.
