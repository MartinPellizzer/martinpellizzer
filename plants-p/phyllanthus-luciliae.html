Phyllanthus luciliae

Phyllanthus luciliae, commonly known as the lucilia mallow, is a flowering plant species native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters. The plant features simple, alternate leaves that are usually ovate or elliptic in shape. Its flowers are small and clustered in inflorescences, often with five petals.

The fruit is a capsule that contains several seeds, which are dispersed by wind or animals.
