Polyalthia gigantifolia

Polyalthia gigantifolia, commonly known as the giant flame tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy red flowers that bloom in clusters, creating a striking visual display. The tree can grow up to 30 meters tall, with a broad, spreading canopy. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a waxy texture.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often planted in gardens and parks for its vibrant floral display.
