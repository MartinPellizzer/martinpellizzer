Pandanus dipsaceus

Pandanus dipsaceus, commonly known as the white pandanus, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its tall, narrow leaves that are often used in traditional thatching and basket weaving. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that eventually develop into fibrous, palm-like fruits. Its root system is robust, allowing it to thrive in moist, well-drained soils.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appearance and is sometimes cultivated for its aesthetic appeal.
