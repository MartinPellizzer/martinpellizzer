Paepalanthus subulatus

Paepalanthus subulatus, commonly known as the slender pincushion cactus, is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny stems that form a compact, cushion-like structure. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the warmer months, typically in spring or early summer. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, preferring well-drained soils.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptations, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant in dry gardens.
