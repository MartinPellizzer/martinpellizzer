Phyteuma tenerum f. hirsutum

Phyteuma tenerum f. hirsutum, commonly known as the hairy phyteuma, is a flowering plant native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and showy, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in moist meadows and woodland edges, preferring partial to full sun. Its flowers, which range in color from white to pale blue, bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in garden landscapes for its delicate appearance.
