Persicaria foliosa var. paludicola

Persicaria foliosa var. paludicola, commonly known as the swamp smartweed, is a herbaceous plant native to wetland environments in North America.

It features upright, slender stems that often grow in clusters, and its leaves are broad, ovate, and slightly hairy. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in dense, terminal clusters, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist to wet soils, making it a common sight in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

Its botanical characteristics make it well adapted to aquatic and semi-aquatic habitats.
