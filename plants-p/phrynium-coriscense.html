Phrynium coriscense

Phrynium coriscense, commonly known as the narrow-leaf arrowleaf, is a tropical plant native to parts of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, arrow-shaped leaves that are often variegated with yellow and green patterns. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous perennial, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters. Its flowers are clustered in inflorescences and are usually yellow or green, attracting various pollinators.

Phrynium coriscense is valued in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
