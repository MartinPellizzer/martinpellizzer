Prunus × donarium var. praecox

Prunus × donarium var. praecox, commonly known as the early cherry, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its small, sweet-tart fruits that ripen earlier than other cherry varieties. The plant typically grows as a deciduous shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are oval-shaped with serrated edges, and it produces white or pale pink flowers in spring.

The fruit is often used in jams, pies, and other culinary applications due to its distinct flavor.
