Pappophorum apertum var. vaginatum

Pappophorum apertum var. vaginatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved pappophorum, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, cylindrical stems and narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with feathery bracts, which aid in wind pollination. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and rocky outcrops.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping due to its delicate appearance.
