Polygala barthiana

Polygala barthiana, commonly known as the Barth's milkweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid regions. Its flowers are usually pink or white, with a distinctive spurred structure that attracts pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Polygala barthiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
