Piper irazuanum

Piper irazuanum, commonly known as the Irazu pepper, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to Central America, particularly found in the highlands of Costa Rica and Panama. The plant is characterized by its climbing habit, with long, slender stems that often trail along the ground or climb supports. Its small, greenish-white flowers are followed by the distinctive, elongated, and often spiny fruits.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
