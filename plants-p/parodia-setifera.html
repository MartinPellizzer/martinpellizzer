Parodia setifera

Parodia setifera, commonly known as the ghost cactus, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, spiny stems that can grow up to 2 meters in height. The plant produces small, white flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Its unique appearance and low maintenance make it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
