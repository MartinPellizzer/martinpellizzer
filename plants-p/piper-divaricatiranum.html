Piper divaricatiranum

Piper divaricatiranum, commonly known as the purple pepper vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many species of pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its slender, twining stems and small, heart-shaped leaves that are often a deep green with a slightly glossy texture. Its flowers are unisexual, with male and female flowers occurring on separate plants, and it produces small, round berries that turn from green to purple as they mature.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
