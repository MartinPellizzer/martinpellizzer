Pithecellobium lucyi

Pithecellobium lucyi, commonly known as the yellow jungle bean, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellow flowers. The plant produces pods that contain edible seeds, which are often used in local cuisines. Botanically, it is notable for its nitrogen-fixing ability, which benefits soil fertility.

Its growth habit and adaptability make it a valuable species in ecological and agricultural contexts.
