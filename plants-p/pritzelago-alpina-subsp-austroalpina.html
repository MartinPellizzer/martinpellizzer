Pritzelago alpina subsp. austroalpina

Pritzelago alpina subsp.

austroalpina, commonly known as the Alpine bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the alpine regions of Europe. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue.

The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes, often in mountainous areas. It has narrow, linear leaves and is adapted to cold, harsh environments.

This subspecies is often found in the Alps and other mountain ranges across Europe.
