Potentilla alba splendens

Potentilla alba splendens, commonly known as the white splendour cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia and the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its delicate, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, showcasing its hardy nature. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a silvery or fuzzy texture.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained soil.
