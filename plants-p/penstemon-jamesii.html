Penstemon jamesii

Penstemon jamesii, commonly known as the Sierra penstemon, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils in mountainous regions. It produces seed capsules that release small, wind-dispersed seeds.

Penstemon jamesii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
