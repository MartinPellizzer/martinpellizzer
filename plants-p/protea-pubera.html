Protea pubera

Protea pubera, commonly known as the silver protea, is a striking South African plant belonging to the Proteaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, fluffy, white or pale pink inflorescence that resembles a ball of cotton, which is actually a dense cluster of small flowers. The plant is native to the coastal regions of South Africa, particularly in the Western and Eastern Cape provinces. Its unique appearance makes it a popular ornamental plant, though it requires well-drained soil and a cool, dry climate to thrive.

The protea pubera plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing nectar for pollinators and supporting local biodiversity.
