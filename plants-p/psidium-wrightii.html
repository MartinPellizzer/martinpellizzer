Psidium wrightii

Psidium wrightii, commonly known as Wright's guava, is a small evergreen tree native to Mexico and Central America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its fragrant, white flowers and edible, yellow-orange fruits. The leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a glossy surface with serrated edges. The plant thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial shade.

Its fruit is often used in culinary applications and has a sweet, aromatic flavor.
