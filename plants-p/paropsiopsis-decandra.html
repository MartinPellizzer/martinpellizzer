Paropsiopsis decandra

Paropsiopsis decandra, commonly known as the velvetleaf or false nettle, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a velvety texture. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically borne on slender stalks. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forests or along stream banks.
