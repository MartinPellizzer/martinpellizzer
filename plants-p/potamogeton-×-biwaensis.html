Potamogeton × biwaensis

Potamogeton × biwaensis, commonly known as the Lake Biwa hybrid pondweed, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Potamogeton acutiflorus and Potamogeton crispus.

It is native to Japan, specifically found in the Lake Biwa region, where it thrives in freshwater environments. The plant is characterized by its narrow, ribbon-like leaves and small, clustered flowers that bloom in late summer. It plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.

Due to its hybrid nature, it exhibits a range of morphological variations that make it distinct from its parent species.
