Panicum bispiculatum

Panicum bispiculatum, commonly known as the two-branched grass, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Panicum bispiculatum is often used in ornamental gardening for its delicate appearance and adaptability to various soil conditions.
