Pyrus cashmiriana

Pyrus cashmiriana, commonly known as the Kashmir pear, is a deciduous tree native to the Himalayan region.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and alternate, ovate leaves. The tree produces clusters of small, white flowers in spring, followed by edible, sweet, and juicy fruits. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit production.

The plant is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional medicine.
