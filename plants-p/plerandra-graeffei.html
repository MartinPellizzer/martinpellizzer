Plerandra graeffei

Plerandra graeffei, commonly known as the Graeff's arum, is a striking tropical plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its distinctive inflorescence consisting of a spadix and a spathe. The plant features large, heart-shaped leaves that are typically a deep green with a glossy texture. Its flowers are unisexual, with male and female flowers occurring on separate plants.

Plerandra graeffei is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can thrive in humid, shaded environments.
