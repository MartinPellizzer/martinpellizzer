Penstemon hallii var. arizonicus

Penstemon hallii var. arizonicus, commonly known as the Arizona beardtongue, is a perennial flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that are typically pink to purple in color, attracting pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy choice for xeriscaping.

This variety is known for its drought tolerance and ability to bloom reliably in arid environments.
