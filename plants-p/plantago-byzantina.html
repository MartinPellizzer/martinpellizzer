Plantago byzantina

Plantago byzantina, commonly known as Byzantine plantain, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its rosette of broad, heart-shaped leaves covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in a spike-like inflorescence, which are borne on long, slender stalks. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

The species is valued for its hardy nature and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
