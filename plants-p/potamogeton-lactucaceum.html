Potamogeton lactucaceum

Potamogeton lactucaceum, commonly known as the water milfoil, is a freshwater plant belonging to the Potamogetonaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems that often grow submerged in shallow waters. The plant has small, opposite leaves that are typically narrow and ribbon-like in appearance. It produces tiny, greenish flowers that are clustered in spike-like inflorescences.

Potamogeton lactucaceum plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
