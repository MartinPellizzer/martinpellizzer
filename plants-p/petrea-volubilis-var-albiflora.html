Petrea volubilis var. albiflora

Petrea volubilis var. albiflora, commonly known as the whiteflower poinciana, is a fast-growing, evergreen vine native to South America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, fluffy white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value. It is relatively drought-tolerant once established, making it suitable for various climates.

Its rapid growth and attractive foliage make it a popular choice for trellises and vertical gardening.
