Plumbagella micrantha var. micrantha

Plumbagella micrantha var. micrantha, commonly known as the narrow-leaved plumbagin, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically reaches a height of about 10-20 centimeters. The plant produces small, blue or violet flowers that bloom in summer, and its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a border plant.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated for its delicate appearance.
