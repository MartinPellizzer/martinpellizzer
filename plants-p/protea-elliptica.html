Protea elliptica

Protea elliptica, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sugarbush, is a South African plant belonging to the Proteaceae family.

It is characterized by its elliptical, leathery leaves and striking, cone-shaped inflorescences that range in color from white to pink. The plant is adapted to nutrient-poor soils and is often found in fynbos vegetation. It produces small, nectar-rich flowers that attract pollinators like birds and insects.

Protea elliptica is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
