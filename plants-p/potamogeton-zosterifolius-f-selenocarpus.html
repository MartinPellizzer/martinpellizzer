Potamogeton zosterifolius f. selenocarpus

Potamogeton zosterifolius f. selenocarpus, commonly known as the silver-fruit pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to North America.

It has narrow, linear leaves that are typically arranged in whorls around the stem. The plant produces small, white flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its unique feature is the production of selenocarps, which are small, calcified fruits that help in seed dispersal.

This plant thrives in shallow, freshwater environments and plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
