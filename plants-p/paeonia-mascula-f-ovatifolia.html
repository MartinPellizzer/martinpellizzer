Paeonia mascula f. ovatifolia

Paeonia mascula f. ovatifolia, commonly known as the ovate-leaved peony, is a unique cultivar of the peony genus.

It is characterized by its broad, ovate-shaped leaves that differ from the typical foliage of the species. The plant produces large, showy flowers in shades of pink, red, and white, typically blooming in late spring. It is a herbaceous perennial that thrives in well-drained soil and full sun.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its visual appeal.
