Polemonium boreale var. villosissimum

Polemonium boreale var. villosissimum, commonly known as the hairy bluebell, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to northern North America.

It features slender, upright stems that are covered in fine, white hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a soft, hairy texture, contributing to its overall ornamental value.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.
