Prunus chamaecerasus sativa

Prunus chamaecerasus sativa, commonly known as the Japanese cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pink flowers in early spring, followed by edible berries that are used in traditional Japanese cuisine. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and turn vibrant shades of red and yellow in autumn.

The species is valued for its ecological benefits, providing food and habitat for various wildlife species.
