Prunus bakti

Prunus bakti, commonly known as the Bakti cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape. The plant produces small, white flowers in early spring, followed by edible drupes that are typically red or purple in color. Prunus bakti is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.

It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.
