Plectranthus diversus

Plectranthus diversus, commonly known as the narrow-leaf plectranthus, is a perennial herb native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to violet, typically blooming in summer. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is a popular choice for gardeners seeking a low-maintenance, drought-tolerant plant.
