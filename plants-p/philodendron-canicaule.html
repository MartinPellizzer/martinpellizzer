Philodendron canicaule

Philodendron canicaule, commonly known as the curly stalk philodendron, is a tropical climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, waxy leaves that are typically dark green with a slight sheen. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by red, berry-like fruits that are edible for birds. It thrives in humid, warm environments and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.

Its unique, curling stems make it a popular choice for indoor gardening and vertical displays.
