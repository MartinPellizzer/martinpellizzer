Phalaenopsis sanderiana subvar. marmorata

Phalaenopsis sanderiana subvar. marmorata, commonly known as the Sander's Phalaenopsis with marble-like markings, is a hybrid orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, long-lasting flowers that range from white to pale pink, often with intricate marbling patterns on the petals. The plant typically grows as a sympodial orchid, forming a compact rosette with broad, fleshy leaves. It prefers bright, indirect light and moderate humidity, making it a popular choice for indoor cultivation.

Its unique appearance and ease of care have made it a favorite among orchid enthusiasts.
