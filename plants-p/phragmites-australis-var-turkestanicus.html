Phragmites australis var. turkestanicus

Phragmites australis var. turkestanicus, commonly known as the Central Asian reed, is a variant of the common reed that is native to Central Asia.

It is a tall, perennial grass that typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of water bodies. The plant has hollow, cylindrical stems and can reach heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are long, narrow, and often have a rough texture.

This variant is notable for its ecological importance in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
