Pyrola asarifolia

Pyrola asarifolia, commonly known as the fragrant wintergreen, is a flowering plant native to the northern hemisphere.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is typically found in moist, acidic forest soils. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are usually white or pink in color. Its leaves are evergreen, leathery, and have a distinct aromatic scent when crushed.

Pyrola asarifolia is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
