Phaseolus subvolubilis

Phaseolus subvolubilis, commonly known as the common scarlet runner bean, is a climbing legume native to the Americas.

It produces vibrant red or orange flowers and elongated, edible pods that contain seeds used in culinary applications. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets and possesses tendrils that aid in its climbing growth habit. It thrives in warm climates and is often cultivated for both its ornamental value and nutritional benefits.

This species is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential health properties.
