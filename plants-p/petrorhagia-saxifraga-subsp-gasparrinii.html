Petrorhagia saxifraga subsp. gasparrinii

Petrorhagia saxifraga subsp.

gasparrinii, commonly known as the Gasparrini's rockrose, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is a low-growing shrub characterized by its compact, mat-forming growth habit and small, fleshy leaves.

The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in dry, sunny habitats.

Its botanical features make it well adapted to arid environments, with a high degree of drought tolerance.
