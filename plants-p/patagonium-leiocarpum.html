Patagonium leiocarpum

Patagonium leiocarpum, commonly known as the smooth-capped Patagonian grass, is a perennial herb native to the southern regions of South America.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow leaves. The plant typically grows in open, sunny areas such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its small, pale florets are arranged in dense clusters, and it produces lightweight seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Patagonium leiocarpum is adapted to harsh environmental conditions, making it a resilient component of its native ecosystem.
