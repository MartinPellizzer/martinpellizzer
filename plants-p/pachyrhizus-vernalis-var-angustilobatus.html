Pachyrhizus vernalis var. angustilobatus

Pachyrhizus vernalis var. angustilobatus, commonly known as the narrow-leaf vernal bean, is a herbaceous annual plant native to South America.

It features compound leaves with elongated, narrow leaflets and produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant is characterized by its cylindrical, tuberous roots that store nutrients, a feature common in the genus Pachyrhizus. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
