Pyrus tobani

Pyrus tobani, commonly known as the Toban pear, is a species of tree native to the island of Sardinia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its small, ornamental leaves and clusters of white flowers. The fruit is typically small, yellowish, and has a sweet, aromatic flavor. It is a rare and endangered species, with limited natural distribution.

Due to its unique characteristics, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists alike.
