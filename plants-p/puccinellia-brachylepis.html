Puccinellia brachylepis

Puccinellia brachylepis, commonly known as narrowleaf saltbush, is a perennial grass native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of hairs, aiding in water conservation. It thrives in saline and alkaline soils, making it an important species in stabilizing coastal and desert environments.

Its adaptability to harsh conditions makes it valuable for ecological restoration projects.
