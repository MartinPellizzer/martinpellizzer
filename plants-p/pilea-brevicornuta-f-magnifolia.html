Pilea brevicornuta f. magnifolia

Pilea brevicornuta f. magnifolia, commonly known as the Shingle Plant, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It features dense clusters of small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically a deep green with a slight sheen. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers that appear in spring, followed by red berries. It thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Its unique foliage and ease of care contribute to its popularity among plant enthusiasts.
