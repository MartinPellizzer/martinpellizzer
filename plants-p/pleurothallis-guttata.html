Pleurothallis guttata

Pleurothallis guttata, commonly known as the spotted pleurothallis, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a small, epiphytic plant that typically grows on trees in humid, shaded environments. The plant features delicate, linear leaves and produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink. Its flowers are often clustered in racemes and are known for their intricate, intricate structure.

Pleurothallis guttata is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
