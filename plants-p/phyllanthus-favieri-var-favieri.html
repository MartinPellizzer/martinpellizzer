Phyllanthus favieri var. favieri

Phyllanthus favieri var. favieri, commonly known as the Indian gooseberry or amla, is a shrub native to India and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its fruit is a bright orange, berry-like structure that contains a single seed.

This species is valued for its medicinal properties and is widely used in traditional Ayurvedic medicine.
