Phryne hispanica

Phryne hispanica, commonly known as the Spanish phryne, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, clustered leaves. The plant produces delicate, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, often with a slight purple tinge. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Phryne hispanica is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
