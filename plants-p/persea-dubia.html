Persea dubia

Persea dubia, commonly known as the Mexican persimmon, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central America and parts of the Caribbean.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and fragrant, white flowers. The tree produces small, orange to reddish fruits that are edible when fully ripe. Its botanical features include a smooth, greyish bark and a tendency to grow in dense, humid forests.

Persea dubia is valued for its ornamental appeal and its fruit, which is used in local cuisines and traditional medicine.
