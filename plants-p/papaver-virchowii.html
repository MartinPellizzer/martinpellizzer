Papaver virchowii

Papaver virchowii, commonly known as the Virchow's poppy, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its striking red or orange flowers with a dark central blotch, which resemble those of the opium poppy. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain small, elliptical seeds. It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Papaver virchowii is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
