Primula minima var. subacaulis

Primula minima var. subacaulis, commonly known as the dwarf primrose, is a compact, herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of China.

It features small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow, typically blooming in early spring. The plant has a low-growing habit with short, hairy stems and rosettes of narrow, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in rocky or shaded woodland areas.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine displays.
