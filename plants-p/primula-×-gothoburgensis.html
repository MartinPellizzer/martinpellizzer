Primula × gothoburgensis

Primula × gothoburgensis, commonly known as the Swedish primrose, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Primula vulgaris and Primula veris.

It is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant typically grows to a height of 15–30 cm and produces basal leaves that are heart-shaped with serrated edges. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its early spring blooms.
