Piper bejucalanum

Piper bejucalanum, commonly known as the wild pepper vine, is a climbing shrub native to tropical regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many species of flowering plants known for their aromatic properties. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by berries, which can be either green or red when mature. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The vine is characterized by its vigorous growth habit and ability to thrive in humid, shaded environments.
