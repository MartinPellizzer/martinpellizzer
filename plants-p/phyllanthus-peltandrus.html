Phyllanthus peltandrus

Phyllanthus peltandrus, commonly known as the broadleaf spurge, is a flowering plant native to Africa and parts of Asia.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are usually broad and ovate in shape. Its small, greenish-yellow flowers are clustered in dense inflorescences, and it produces small, round berries that are often yellow or orange.

The plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in disturbed soils.
