Panicularia nervata var. rigida

Panicularia nervata var. rigida, commonly known as rigid panicled bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It features upright, branching stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped flowers, typically blue to violet in color. The leaves are lanceolate, with prominent veins that give the plant its scientific name. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or wetland habitats.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, supporting various pollinators.
