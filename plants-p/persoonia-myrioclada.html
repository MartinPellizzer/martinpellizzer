Persoonia myrioclada

Persoonia myrioclada, commonly known as the many-branched ghost flower, is a flowering plant native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, pale green, leafless stems that bear clusters of small, white, lantern-shaped flowers. The plant thrives in sandy soils and is often found in coastal heathlands. It is adapted to arid conditions and has a unique ability to store water in its stems.

The flowers are pollinated by insects, particularly bees, and the plant produces small, woody fruits that contain seeds.
