Prosthechea boothiana

Prosthechea boothiana, commonly known as the Booth's orchid, is a species of epiphytic orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact, rosette-like growth habit and produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining substrates for optimal growth. Its roots are adapted to absorb moisture and nutrients from the air and surrounding surfaces.

Prosthechea boothiana is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
