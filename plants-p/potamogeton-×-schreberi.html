Potamogeton × schreberi

Potamogeton × schreberi, commonly known as the hybrid water milfoil, is a submerged aquatic plant that exhibits characteristics of both its parent species, Potamogeton crispus and Potamogeton natans.

It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in still or slow-moving freshwater environments such as lakes and ponds. The plant has long, narrow leaves that are typically arranged in whorls around the stem, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often submerged. Potamogeton × schreberi plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various invertebrates and fish.

Its presence can indicate good water quality, as it thrives in clean, well-oxygenated conditions.
