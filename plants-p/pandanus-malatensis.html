Pandanus malatensis

Pandanus malatensis, commonly known as the Malate pandanus, is a tropical plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It is a member of the Pandanaceae family and is characterized by its tall, fibrous stalks and large, feather-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically yellow or green in color. Its roots and leaves are often used in traditional crafts and for making thatch.

The species is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing coastal soils and providing habitat for various wildlife.
