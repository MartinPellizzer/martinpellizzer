Paloue bicolor var. velutina

Paloue bicolor var. velutina, commonly known as the velvetleaf paloue, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its striking bi-colored leaves, typically featuring a green upper surface and a pale or variegated underside. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually white or pale yellow, attracting various pollinators. Its leaves are often covered in a fine, velvety texture, giving it its common name.

Paloue bicolor var. velutina is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its unique foliage and modest flowering display.
