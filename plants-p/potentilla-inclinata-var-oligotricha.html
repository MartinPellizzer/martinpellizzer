Potentilla inclinata var. oligotricha

Potentilla inclinata var. oligotricha, commonly known as the slender cinquefoil, is a low-growing perennial shrub native to the western United States.

It typically thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine regions. The plant features small, five-petaled yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. Its leaves are compound, with multiple narrow leaflets, and the stems are often covered in fine, soft hairs.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in harsh environments.
