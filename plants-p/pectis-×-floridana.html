Pectis × floridana

Pectis × floridana, commonly known as the Florida pectis, is a hybrid perennial plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and foliage with a distinctive, often hairy texture. The plant typically grows in moist, sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a rough, serrated margin, while the flowers are small and yellow, arranged in clusters.

Pectis × floridana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local pollinators.
