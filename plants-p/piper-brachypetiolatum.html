Piper brachypetiolatum

Piper brachypetiolatum, commonly known as the short-stemmed pepper plant, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, and thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant is characterized by its small, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of tiny, greenish-white flowers. Its fruit, which resembles a small pepper, is typically harvested when mature for use in traditional medicine and culinary applications.

The plant's botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and ethnobotanical studies.
