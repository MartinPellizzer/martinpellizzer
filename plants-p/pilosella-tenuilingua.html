Pilosella tenuilingua

Pilosella tenuilingua, commonly known as the slenderleaf hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, and it produces small yellow flowers that bloom in the summer.

Pilosella tenuilingua is often considered a weed in agricultural areas due to its rapid growth and ability to spread easily.
