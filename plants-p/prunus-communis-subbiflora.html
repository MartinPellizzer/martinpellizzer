Prunus communis subbiflora

Prunus communis subbiflora, commonly known as the double-flowered cherry, is a cultivar of the common cherry tree.

It is characterized by its distinctive double blossoms, which consist of numerous small petals instead of the typical single bloom. The plant is native to Europe and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and parks. It produces small, edible fruits that are similar to those of the common cherry.

This variety is valued for its aesthetic appeal and is often used in landscaping and floral arrangements.
