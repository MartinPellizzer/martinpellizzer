Platanthera madagascariensis

Platanthera madagascariensis, commonly known as the Madagascan twayblade, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Madagascar.

It is a terrestrial orchid with a slender, often hairy stem and linear leaves. The plant produces delicate, greenish-white flowers with prominent lip petals, which are characteristic of the genus Platanthera. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically in montane forests.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered threatened and is of interest for conservation efforts.
