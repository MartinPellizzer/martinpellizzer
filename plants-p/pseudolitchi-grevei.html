Pseudolitchi grevei

Pseudolitchi grevei, commonly known as the Chinese tamarind, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and fragrant flowers. The tree produces small, yellowish fruits that are often used in traditional medicine. Its botanical features include smooth bark and a tendency to grow in tropical and subtropical climates.

It is valued for both its ornamental appeal and medicinal properties.
