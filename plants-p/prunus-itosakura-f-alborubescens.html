Prunus itosakura f. alborubescens

Prunus itosakura f. alborubescens, commonly known as the white-flowered cherry blossom, is a rare and ornamental variant of the Prunus itosakura species.

It is characterized by its striking white petals that contrast beautifully with its pinkish-red buds, creating a unique visual display during bloom. The plant is native to Japan and is often cultivated for its aesthetic value in gardens and parks. Its flowers typically appear in early spring, adding a fleeting yet mesmerizing beauty to the landscape.

The botanical features of this cultivar make it a prized specimen among horticulturists and botanists alike.
