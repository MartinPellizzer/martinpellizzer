Pothos quinquevenosus

Pothos quinquevenosus, commonly known as the five-veined ivy, is a tropical vine native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, waxy leaves that have five distinct veins, giving it its scientific name. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers, followed by orange-red berries that are toxic to humans and animals. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often used in ornamental horticulture.

Pothos quinquevenosus is a fast-growing plant that can be propagated through stem cuttings.
