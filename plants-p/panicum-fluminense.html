Panicum fluminense

Panicum fluminense, commonly known as river panicum, is a perennial grass native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces fluffy, golden-brown seed heads that are often seen in wetland and marshy environments. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found along riverbanks and in swampy areas.

Its botanical features make it an important component of aquatic and riparian ecosystems.
