Potamogeton vaginatus var. helveticus

Potamogeton vaginatus var. helveticus, commonly known as the European pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater habitats in Europe.

It has long, slender leaves that are typically whorled around the stem, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often submerged. The plant is characterized by its smooth, cylindrical stems and the presence of leaf sheaths that are often narrower than the leaves themselves. It thrives in slow-moving to still waters with moderate to high light levels and is an important component of aquatic ecosystems.

Potamogeton vaginatus var. helveticus plays a role in providing habitat for aquatic invertebrates and fish.
