Piriqueta integrifolia

Piriqueta integrifolia, commonly known as the smoothleaf piriqueta, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are entire, meaning they have no teeth or lobes, and are arranged oppositely along the stem.

The showy yellow flowers bloom in late spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
