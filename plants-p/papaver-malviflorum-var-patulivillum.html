Papaver malviflorum var. patulivillum

Papaver malviflorum var. patulivillum, commonly known as the broadleaf poppy, is a variant of the broadleaf poppy species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple, often with a distinctive, slightly nodding appearance. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, and the plant is known for its ornamental value in gardens.

The seeds of this plant are also used in traditional medicine for their potential analgesic properties.
