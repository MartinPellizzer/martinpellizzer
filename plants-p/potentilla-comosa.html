Potentilla comosa

Potentilla comosa, commonly known as the silky cinquefoil, is a flowering plant belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere, including parts of Europe, Asia, and North America. The plant is characterized by its low-growing habit, with dense clusters of small, yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. Its leaves are typically divided into five oval segments, giving it the common name "cinquefoil.".

Potentilla comosa is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and rocky soils, where it thrives in full sun to partial shade.
