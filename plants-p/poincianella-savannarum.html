Poincianella savannarum

Poincianella savannarum, commonly known as the pink trumpet tree, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive pink to red tubular flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as birds and insects. The tree has a slender trunk and a spreading crown, with bipinnate leaves that are finely divided. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in savannas and open woodlands.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its showy floral display.
