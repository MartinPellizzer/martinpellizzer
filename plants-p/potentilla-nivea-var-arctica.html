Potentilla nivea var. arctica

Potentilla nivea var. arctica, commonly known as Arctic cinquefoil, is a low-growing perennial shrub native to arctic and alpine regions.

It is characterized by its small, white to pale yellow flowers and hairy, silver-gray leaves that help reflect sunlight in cold climates. The plant thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to withstand harsh environmental conditions. It plays an important role in stabilizing soil in its native habitats.

Its botanical features make it a hardy species suitable for cultivation in similar environments.
