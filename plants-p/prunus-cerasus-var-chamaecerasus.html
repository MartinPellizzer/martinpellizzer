Prunus cerasus var. chamaecerasus

Prunus cerasus var. chamaecerasus, commonly known as the dwarf cherry, is a compact, shrub-like variant of the sweet cherry.

It features small, rounded leaves that are typically a deep green, and its flowers are white with a slight pinkish tinge. The plant produces small, sweet fruits that are often used in culinary applications. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often used in landscaping due to its hardy nature and aesthetic appeal.
