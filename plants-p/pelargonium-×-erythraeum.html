Pelargonium × erythraeum

Pelargonium × erythraeum, commonly known as the red-veined geranium, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Geraniaceae family.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety leaves with deep red veins against a dark green background. The plant produces clusters of small, five-petaled flowers in shades of pink, red, and purple, typically blooming in spring and summer. Native to South Africa, it thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden borders and containers.

Its ornamental value and adaptability contribute to its widespread cultivation in temperate climates.
