Ptiloria paniculata

Ptiloria paniculata, commonly known as the African firethorn, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub that can grow up to 2 meters in height, characterized by its dense, spiny foliage. The plant produces showy, orange-red flowers that bloom in winter, attracting pollinators such as birds and insects. Its leaves are elliptical and have a waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss in arid conditions.

Ptiloria paniculata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in harsh environments.
