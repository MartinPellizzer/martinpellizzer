Pandanus tectorius var. timorensis

Pandanus tectorius var. timorensis, commonly known as the Timor pandanus, is a tropical plant native to the eastern Indonesian island of Timor.

It is a member of the Pandanaceae family and is characterized by its long, narrow, sword-shaped leaves that are often used in traditional crafts. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and is adapted to saline and humid environments. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of small, fragrant flowers that attract pollinators like bees and birds.

The Timor pandanus is also valued for its ornamental qualities and has cultural significance in local communities.
