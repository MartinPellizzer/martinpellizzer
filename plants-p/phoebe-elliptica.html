Phoebe elliptica

Phoebe elliptica, commonly known as the elliptic phoebe, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Lauraceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like laurel and cinnamon. The plant is characterized by its elliptical, dark green leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Phoebe elliptica is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential medicinal properties.
