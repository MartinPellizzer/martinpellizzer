Pyrus miniata

Pyrus miniata, commonly known as the dwarf whitebeam, is a small deciduous tree or shrub native to Europe.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and distinctive white to pale pink flowers that appear in early spring. The leaves are typically oval-shaped with a serrated margin and a dark green upper surface. This plant is often found in woodland habitats and thrives in well-drained soils.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical significance in horticulture.
