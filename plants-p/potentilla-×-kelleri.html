Potentilla × kelleri

Potentilla × kelleri, commonly known as the Keller's cinquefoil, is a hybrid shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring upright stems and dense foliage. The plant produces small, yellow flowers in clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are compound, consisting of five oval-shaped leaflets, giving it the common name "cinquefoil.".

It is well adapted to rocky and arid environments, making it a resilient plant in harsh conditions.
