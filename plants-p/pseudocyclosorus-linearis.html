Pseudocyclosorus linearis

Pseudocyclosorus linearis, commonly known as the slender chain fern, is a member of the family Polypodiaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, linear fronds that grow in a compact, rosette-like formation. The plant produces spore-bearing structures called sori, which are typically located on the underside of the fronds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its unique morphology and reproductive features make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
