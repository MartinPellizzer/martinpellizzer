Piper machadoense

Piper machadoense, commonly known as the Machado's pepper, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its climbing habit and heart-shaped leaves. It produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are followed by elongated, peppery fruits.

This species is valued for its aromatic properties and is often used in traditional medicine.
