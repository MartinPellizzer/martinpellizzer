Phyllocactus macrocarpus

Phyllocactus macrocarpus, commonly known as the giant leaf cactus, is a unique member of the Cactaceae family native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, fleshy, paddle-shaped stems that can grow up to several meters in length. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, typically blooming in the spring. Its stems are covered in areoles, which are specialized structures that bear spines, flowers, and sometimes branches.

This plant is well adapted to arid environments, storing water in its thick, succulent stems.
