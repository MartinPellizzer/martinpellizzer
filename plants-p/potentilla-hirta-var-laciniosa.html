Potentilla hirta var. laciniosa

Potentilla hirta var. laciniosa, commonly known as the hairy cinquefoil, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has finely divided, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or alpine environments.

Its botanical characteristics make it an interesting subject for both horticultural and ecological study.
