Primula villosa var. apennina

Primula villosa var. apennina, commonly known as the Apennine primrose, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It features hairy stems and heart-shaped, dark green leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence. The plant produces clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and rockeries.
