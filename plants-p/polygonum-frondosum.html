Polygonum frondosum

Polygonum frondosum, commonly known as the fronded knotweed, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its upright stems and large, heart-shaped leaves that are often lobed or fronded. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, blooming in late summer. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as stream banks and forest floors.

Polygonum frondosum is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and flowering display.
