Pleurothallis sulcata

Pleurothallis sulcata, commonly known as the zigzag orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, twisted floral structure. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are small, fragrant, and often display a range of colors from white to pink.

Pleurothallis sulcata is appreciated for its ornamental value and is cultivated by orchid enthusiasts worldwide.
