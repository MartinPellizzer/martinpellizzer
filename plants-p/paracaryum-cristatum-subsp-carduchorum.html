Paracaryum cristatum subsp. carduchorum

Paracaryum cristatum subsp.

carduchorum, commonly known as the mountain holly, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of southern Africa. It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, leathery leaves that are often arranged in a compact, bushy form.

The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers during the summer months, which are followed by bright red berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to full sun to partial shade conditions.

Due to its ornamental value and resilience, it is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
