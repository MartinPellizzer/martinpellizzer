Pandanus thailandicus

Pandanus thailandicus, commonly known as the Thai pandanus, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, sword-shaped leaves that grow in a fan-like arrangement. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences at the base of the leaves. Its roots and leaves are often used in traditional medicine and local crafts.

Pandanus thailandicus thrives in humid, coastal environments and is well adapted to saline conditions.
