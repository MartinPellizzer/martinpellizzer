Patis coreana

Patis coreana, commonly known as the Korean bellflower, is a flowering plant native to Korea.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full sunlight. Patis coreana has lance-shaped leaves and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.

It is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
