Peristylus turneri

Peristylus turneri, commonly known as the Mexican rock cactus, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its compact, branching structure and is often found growing on rocky outcrops. The plant has thick, fleshy stems that store water, allowing it to survive in arid environments. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, appearing in the spring.

Peristylus turneri is a slow-growing plant that is relatively easy to care for, making it a popular choice for cactus enthusiasts.
