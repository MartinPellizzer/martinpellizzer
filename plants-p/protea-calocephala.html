Protea calocephala

Protea calocephala, commonly known as the white-headed protea, is a striking South African plant belonging to the Proteaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, white, spherical inflorescence that resembles a head, hence its common name. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces showy, pinkish-white flowers that attract pollinators such as birds and insects. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is typically found in fynbos vegetation.

Protea calocephala is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
