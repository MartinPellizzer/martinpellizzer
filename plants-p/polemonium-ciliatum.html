Polemonium ciliatum

Polemonium ciliatum, commonly known as the blue flax or Pennsylvania ironweed, is a flowering plant native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Polemonium genus in the family Polemoniaceae, and is characterized by its showy, blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and has opposite, ovate leaves with hairy surfaces.

Its root system is fibrous, and it is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
