Potamogeton lucens subsp. macrophyllus

Potamogeton lucens subsp.

macrophyllus, commonly known as the broad-leaf pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in North America. It is characterized by its long, narrow leaves that are often broad at the base, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The plant produces small, green flowers that are typically clustered in dense spikes, and it thrives in shallow, nutrient-rich waters. It plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.

Its presence is often an indicator of good water quality and stable environmental conditions.
