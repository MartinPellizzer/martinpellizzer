Potentilla coriandrifolia var. dumosa

Potentilla coriandrifolia var. dumosa, commonly known as the woolly cinquefoil, is a perennial shrub characterized by its dense, woolly foliage.

It features small, yellow, five-petaled flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves covered in fine, white hairs, giving it a soft, fuzzy appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardiness in temperate climates.
