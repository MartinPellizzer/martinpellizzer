Pagiantha dichotoma

Pagiantha dichotoma, commonly known as the forked pincushion, is a flowering plant native to the Cape region of South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, spherical inflorescence composed of numerous small, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

Pagiantha dichotoma is known for its resilience to drought and is often used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements.
