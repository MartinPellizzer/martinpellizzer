Pachystroma ilicifolium var. longifolium

Pachystroma ilicifolium var. longifolium, commonly known as the long-leaved pachystroma, is a perennial shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage with elongated, leathery leaves that are often aromatic. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers in terminal clusters, which are typically visited by pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native plant gardens.
