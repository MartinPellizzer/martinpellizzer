Paspalum eggertii

Paspalum eggertii, commonly known as Florida panicum, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, white to pale green flowers. It thrives in wetland environments and is often found in marshes and along the edges of ponds.

Paspalum eggertii is valued for its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife species.
