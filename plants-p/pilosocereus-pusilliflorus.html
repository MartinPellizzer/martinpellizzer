Pilosocereus pusilliflorus

Pilosocereus pusilliflorus, commonly known as the small-flowered cactus, is a species native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a columnar cactus with a distinctive appearance, featuring a tall, cylindrical stem covered in dense, white spines. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom at the ends of the stems, typically during the warmer months. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, requiring well-drained soil and minimal water.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological adaptations, it is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.
