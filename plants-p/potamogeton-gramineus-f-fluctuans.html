Potamogeton gramineus f. fluctuans

Potamogeton gramineus f. fluctuans, commonly known as floating pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in North America.

It has long, slender, grass-like leaves that are often found floating on the water surface or partially submerged. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in spikelets, and it reproduces both sexually and vegetatively. It plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.

However, it can become invasive in some water bodies, leading to ecological imbalances.
