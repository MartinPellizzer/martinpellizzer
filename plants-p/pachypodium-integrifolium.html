Pachypodium integrifolium

Pachypodium integrifolium, commonly known as the Madagascar moon cactus, is a succulent plant native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems that store water, allowing it to survive in arid environments. The plant features a single, upright stem with a crown of spiny, paddle-shaped leaves that are typically green or grayish in color. Its flowers are small and often yellow or white, emerging from the top of the stem during the warmer months.

Pachypodium integrifolium is well adapted to dry conditions and requires minimal watering, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping and indoor gardening.
