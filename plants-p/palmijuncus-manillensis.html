Palmijuncus manillensis

Palmijuncus manillensis, commonly known as the Philippine palm, is a rare and endemic species native to the Philippines.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and pinnate leaves that give it a distinctive palm-like appearance. The plant typically grows in humid, tropical environments, often in mountainous regions with well-drained soils. Its reproductive structures include small, clustered inflorescences that produce both male and female flowers.

Due to habitat loss and limited distribution, it is considered a vulnerable species requiring conservation efforts.
