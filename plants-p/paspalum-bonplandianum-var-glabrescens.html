Paspalum bonplandianum var. glabrescens

Paspalum bonplandianum var. glabrescens, commonly known as the glabrous paspalum, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist to wet environments, often found in marshy areas or along waterways.

Its dense growth makes it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
