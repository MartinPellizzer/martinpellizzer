Prunus jamasakura f. viridifolia

Prunus jamasakura f. viridifolia, commonly known as the green-leafed cherry blossom, is a variant of the Japanese cherry tree characterized by its distinctive emerald-green foliage.

This ornamental plant is prized for its vibrant spring blossoms, which contrast beautifully with its lush green leaves. It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is native to Japan, where it is often cultivated in gardens and parks. The plant typically grows to a moderate height, with a rounded canopy that provides both aesthetic and ecological value.

Its unique foliage makes it a popular choice for those seeking a more unusual ornamental cherry tree.
