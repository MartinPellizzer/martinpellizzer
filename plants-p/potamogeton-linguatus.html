Potamogeton linguatus

Potamogeton linguatus, commonly known as the tongue-shaped pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater habitats in North America.

It has long, slender leaves that are typically narrower at the base and broader towards the tip, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually arranged in spikes, and it reproduces both sexually and asexually through rhizomes. It plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.

Its presence is often an indicator of good water quality in its environment.
