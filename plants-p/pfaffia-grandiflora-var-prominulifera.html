Pfaffia grandiflora var. prominulifera

Pfaffia grandiflora var. prominulifera, commonly known as the Brazilian ginseng, is a flowering plant native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is characterized by its upright, branching stems and lanceolate leaves with a rough, hairy texture. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its root system is highly valued for its medicinal properties, containing compounds believed to have adaptogenic and anti-inflammatory effects.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in humid tropical regions.
