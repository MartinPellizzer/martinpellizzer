Piper aequale f. acutispicum

Piper aequale f. acutispicum, commonly known as the sharp-leaved pepper plant, is a variant of the Piper aequale species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its acuminate (sharply pointed) leaves, which are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in spikes, followed by elongated, slender fruits. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties.

The foliage and fruits contain alkaloids, making it a subject of interest in botanical and pharmacological research.
