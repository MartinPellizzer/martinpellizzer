Planchonella crebrifolia

Planchonella crebrifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf hibiscus, is a tropical plant native to regions of Southeast Asia and the Pacific.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree or shrub characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, often clustered in inflorescences. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, winged seeds.

Planchonella crebrifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in subtropical gardens.
