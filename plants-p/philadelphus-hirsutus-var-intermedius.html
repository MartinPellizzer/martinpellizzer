Philadelphus hirsutus var. intermedius

Philadelphus hirsutus var. intermedius, commonly known as the Intermediate Mockorange, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with hairy surfaces, which give it its species name "hirsutus.". It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained, moist soils.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and pleasant fragrance.
