Piper betle

Piper betle, commonly known as betel leaf, is a tropical evergreen vine native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its large, glossy, heart-shaped leaves. The plant is cultivated for its aromatic leaves, which are used in traditional rituals and as a stimulant. The leaves contain compounds like eugenol and methylated compounds that contribute to its unique flavor and medicinal properties.

It thrives in humid, tropical climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
