Parartocarpus triandrus

Parartocarpus triandrus, commonly known as the three-stamen fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to Madagascar and is characterized by its unique floral structure, which features three stamens. The plant produces small, fig-like fruits that are typically green when mature. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in dense forest understories.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
