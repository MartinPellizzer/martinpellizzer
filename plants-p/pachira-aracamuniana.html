Pachira aracamuniana

Pachira aracamuniana, commonly known as the money plant or elephant ear, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, waxy, heart-shaped leaves and thick, fleshy stems that store water, making it drought-tolerant. The plant produces yellow flowers followed by round, woody fruits containing several seeds. It thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Its ability to purify air and its striking appearance contribute to its popularity as a houseplant.
