Persicaria hirsuta var. glabrescens

Persicaria hirsuta var. glabrescens, commonly known as the soft-hairy knotweed, is a variant of the common knotweed species.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its upright growth habit and lance-shaped leaves. The leaves are typically hairy when young but become smooth as they mature, giving the plant its varietal name. It produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters, which are often borne on slender stalks.

This plant is often found in moist or wet environments, such as marshes and stream banks.
