Paradisea liliastrum var. lusitanica

Paradisea liliastrum var. lusitanica, commonly known as the Lusitanica lily, is a striking perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features showy, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, often with a subtle violet tinge. The plant produces tall, slender stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height, making it a dramatic addition to gardens. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are evergreen and provide a lush backdrop for its blooms.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in rock gardens or as a cut flower.
