Petrosedum rupestre subsp. erectum

Petrosedum rupestre subsp.

erectum, commonly known as the upright stonecrop, is a low-growing succulent native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It features fleshy, upright stems and small, clustered leaves that are typically gray-green in color.

The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the summer months, which are borne on short, erect stalks. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy choice for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Its drought tolerance and ornamental value make it a popular choice among succulent enthusiasts.
