Panicum

Panicum, commonly known as millet, is a genus of flowering plants in the grass family Poaceae.

It includes several species that are widely cultivated for their edible grains, which are important food sources in many parts of the world. Panicum species are typically annual or perennial grasses that thrive in dry and warm climates. They are characterized by their slender stalks, small spikelets, and often produce fluffy seeds that are dispersed by the wind.

These plants are also valued for their ornamental appeal and are used in landscaping and erosion control.
