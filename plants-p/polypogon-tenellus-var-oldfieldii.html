Polypogon tenellus var. oldfieldii

Polypogon tenellus var. oldfieldii, commonly known as Oldfield's slender grass, is a perennial grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as prairies, woodland edges, and disturbed soils. The plant has slender, erect stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often slightly hairy. Its inflorescence consists of a loose cluster of spikelets, which produce small, wind-pollinated flowers.

This variety is noted for its ecological importance in supporting local wildlife and maintaining soil stability in its native range.
