Psoralea mexicana

Psoralea mexicana, commonly known as the Mexican psoralea, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and pod-like fruits. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in open woodlands or grasslands. Psoralea mexicana is also valued for its ecological role in supporting local pollinators and soil stabilization.
