Pyragra

Pyragra, commonly known as the golden rain tree, is a deciduous tree native to Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, yellow, raceme-like flowers. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant floral display.

Pyragra thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
