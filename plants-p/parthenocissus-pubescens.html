Parthenocissus pubescens

Parthenocissus pubescens, commonly known as the hairy ivy or Virginia creeper, is a deciduous vine native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which typically have five leaflets that turn vibrant red in the fall. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by bright red berries in the autumn. It is often used in landscaping for its attractive foliage and vigorous climbing habit.

The hairy texture of its stems and leaves gives it its common name.
