Potentilla emilii-popii

Potentilla emilii-popii, commonly known as the Emili-poppy, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces showy, yellow to orange flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves that help it thrive in well-drained, rocky soils. It is often found in alpine meadows and is adapted to cold climates.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
