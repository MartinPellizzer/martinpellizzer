Pavetta zeyheri subsp. zeyheri

Pavetta zeyheri subsp.

zeyheri, commonly known as the Zeyher's pavetta, is a shrub native to southern Africa. It typically grows in coastal and mountainous regions, often in sandy or rocky soils.

The plant features simple, opposite leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. Its flowers are small, clustered in umbels, and typically have five white or pale pink petals.

The fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, winged seeds.
