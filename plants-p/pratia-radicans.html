Pratia radicans

Pratia radicans, commonly known as creeping bluemink, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the temperate regions of southern Africa.

It features a mat-forming growth habit with prostrate stems that root at the nodes, allowing it to spread across the ground. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically blue or violet in color, blooming in spring. Its leaves are ovate to lanceolate, with a hairy texture, and it thrives in well-drained soils.

Pratia radicans is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to stabilize soil in erosion-prone areas.
