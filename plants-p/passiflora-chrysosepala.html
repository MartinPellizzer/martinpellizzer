Passiflora chrysosepala

Passiflora chrysosepala, commonly known as the golden-sepaled passionflower, is a climbing vine native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that feature a unique blend of colors, including yellow and white petals. The plant produces edible berries that are often used in traditional medicine and cuisine. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are woody and can climb by means of tendrils.

This species is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
