Panicum beckmanniiforme

Panicum beckmanniiforme, commonly known as the Beckmann's panicgrass, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of South America.

It is a grass species characterized by its slender, erect growth habit and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant produces tiny, wind-pollinated flowers that develop into bristly, spikelet-like structures. It thrives in moist, tropical environments and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens in warm climates.
