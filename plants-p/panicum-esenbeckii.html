Panicum esenbeckii

Panicum esenbeckii, commonly known as silver panicum, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a warm-season grass that typically grows in wetland habitats, such as marshes and swamps. The plant features slender, silver-gray leaves and produces fluffy, spike-like inflorescences that are often seen in late summer. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in wet environments.

Botanically, it is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit and its ability to thrive in poorly drained soils.
