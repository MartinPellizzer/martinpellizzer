Poa psilolepis

Poa psilolepis, commonly known as the slender meadow grass, is a species of grass native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and slender, wiry leaves. The plant typically grows in open, moist habitats such as wetlands and streambanks. Its inflorescence consists of a compact cluster of spikelets, which are often tinged with purple.

Poa psilolepis plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for invertebrates.
