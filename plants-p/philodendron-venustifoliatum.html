Philodendron venustifoliatum

Philodendron venustifoliatum, commonly known as the velvet-leaf philodendron, is a tropical climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, velvety leaves that are often a deep green with a slight sheen. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically followed by orange-red berries. It thrives in warm, humid environments with bright, indirect light.

Due to its ornamental foliage, it is popular as a houseplant and in greenhouses.
