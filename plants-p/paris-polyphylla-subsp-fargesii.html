Paris polyphylla subsp. fargesii

Paris polyphylla subsp.

fargesii, commonly known as the Farges' yellow-flowered buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of China. It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, yellow flowers and trifoliate leaves.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded forest environments at high altitudes. Its rhizomatous root system allows it to thrive in well-drained, loamy soils.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
