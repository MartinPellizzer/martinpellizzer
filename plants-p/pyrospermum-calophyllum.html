Pyrospermum calophyllum

Pyrospermum calophyllum, commonly known as the golden torch tree, is a deciduous tree native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its smooth, greyish-brown bark and its striking, pyramidal shape. The tree produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color, which bloom in the spring. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and oval-shaped, with a glossy green surface.

Pyrospermum calophyllum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in warm, humid climates.
