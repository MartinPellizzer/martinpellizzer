Passiflora aurantia var. pubescens

Passiflora aurantia var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy yellow passionflower, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow flowers with intricate patterns of red and purple, and its hairy stems and leaves. The plant produces orange-yellow berries that are edible and contain a sweet, juicy pulp. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Its botanical features make it a unique and visually striking member of the Passifloraceae family.
