Pitcairnia kroemeri

Pitcairnia kroemeri, commonly known as the Kroemeri's pitcairnia, is a striking bromeliad native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, rosette-shaped leaves that form a water-holding cup, a feature common among bromeliads. The plant produces vibrant, long-lasting inflorescences that range in color from red to orange, attracting pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees. Its foliage is often adorned with intricate patterns and a waxy coating, enhancing its aesthetic appeal.

Pitcairnia kroemeri thrives in shaded, humid environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in tropical gardens.
