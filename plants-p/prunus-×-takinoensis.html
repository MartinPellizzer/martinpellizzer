Prunus × takinoensis

Prunus × takinoensis, commonly known as the Japanese cherry plum, is a hybrid species within the Prunus genus.

It is native to Japan and is characterized by its small, edible fruits that range in color from red to yellow. The plant typically grows as a deciduous shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its flowers, which appear in early spring, are pale pink to white and are followed by the development of its distinctive fruits.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
