Picrosia cabreriana

Picrosia cabreriana, commonly known as the Spanish fennel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like parsley and carrots. The plant features slender, hollow stems and finely divided, fern-like leaves that are often covered in a silvery or grayish bloom. Its small yellow flowers are arranged in umbels, typical of the Apiaceae family.

Picrosia cabreriana is valued for its aromatic qualities and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
