Piper pendentispicum var. furcatipilosum

Piper pendentispicum var. furcatipilosum, commonly known as the hairy forked pepper vine, is a climbing shrub native to the rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its long, trailing stems and compound leaves with serrated margins. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by clustered, fleshy berries that turn red when mature. Its leaves are often covered with fine, hairy glands, giving it a distinctive appearance.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated as a decorative plant in tropical gardens.
