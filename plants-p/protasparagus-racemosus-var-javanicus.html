Protasparagus racemosus var. javanicus

Protasparagus racemosus var. javanicus, commonly known as Java asparagus, is a climbing shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Asparagaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, slightly woody stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in racemes, followed by red or orange berries containing seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, shaded environments.

Due to its medicinal properties, it is valued in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
