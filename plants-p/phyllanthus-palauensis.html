Phyllanthus palauensis

Phyllanthus palauensis, commonly known as the Palau sandmat, is a flowering plant native to the island nation of Palau in the western Pacific.

It is a low-growing shrub characterized by its small, fleshy leaves and yellow flowers that bloom during the rainy season. The plant thrives in coastal and sandy habitats, often found in areas with high salinity and poor soil conditions. Its unique adaptations to its environment make it an important component of the local ecosystem.

Phyllanthus palauensis is also of interest to botanists due to its limited distribution and potential medicinal properties.
