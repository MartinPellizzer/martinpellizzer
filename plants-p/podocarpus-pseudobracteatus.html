Podocarpus pseudobracteatus

Podocarpus pseudobracteatus, commonly known as the Philippine podocarp or the dwarf podocarp, is a small evergreen tree native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the family Podocarpaceae and is characterized by its dense, needle-like foliage and small, cone-like reproductive structures. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often in mountainous regions with high humidity. It has a slow growth rate and is valued for its ornamental appeal and use in horticulture.

The species is also important in local ecosystems, providing habitat and contributing to forest biodiversity.
