Phyteuma attenuatum

Phyteuma attenuatum, commonly known as the slender phyteuma, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically blooms in late summer, producing pale blue to violet flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged alternately along the stem, and it prefers well-drained soils in full sun to partial shade.

Phyteuma attenuatum is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and easy maintenance.
