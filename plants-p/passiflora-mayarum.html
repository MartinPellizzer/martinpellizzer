Passiflora mayarum

Passiflora mayarum, commonly known as the purple passionflower, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its striking, complex flowers with five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments that resemble a crown of thorns. The plant produces edible, orange-red berries that are rich in vitamin C and used in various culinary applications. Its foliage consists of large, heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture.

Passiflora mayarum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported calming properties.
