Purgosea spicata

Purgosea spicata, commonly known as the spiked purgosea, is a rare flowering plant native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically found in forest understories.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered endangered and requires conservation efforts to ensure its survival.
