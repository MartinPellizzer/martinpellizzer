Philadelphus intermedius

Philadelphus intermedius, commonly known as the common mock orange, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has opposite, simple, and ovate leaves that are dark green on top and hairy underneath. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained, loamy soil.

Philadelphus intermedius is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
