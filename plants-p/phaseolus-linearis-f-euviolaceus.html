Phaseolus linearis f. euviolaceus

Phaseolus linearis f. euviolaceus, commonly known as the purple-leaf runner bean, is a variant of the common runner bean characterized by its distinctive violet or purple foliage.

This cultivar belongs to the Fabaceae family and is native to the Americas, particularly Central and South America. Its leaves are elongated and serrated, while the flowers are typically white or pale pink, adding ornamental value to gardens.

The plant produces edible beans that are similar to standard runner beans in taste and texture, making it both aesthetically pleasing and useful in cultivation.
