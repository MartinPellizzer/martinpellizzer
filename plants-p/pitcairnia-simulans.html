Pitcairnia simulans

Pitcairnia simulans, commonly known as the glassy air plant, is a species of bromeliad native to South America.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy, translucent coating, giving it its common name. The plant produces striking, long-lasting inflorescences that can reach several feet in height. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Pitcairnia simulans is popular as a houseplant due to its unique appearance and relatively low maintenance requirements.
