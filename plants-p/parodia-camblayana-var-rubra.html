Parodia camblayana var. rubra

Parodia camblayana var. rubra, commonly known as the red-tipped parodia, is a cactus species native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems that are typically green with red or pink spines. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and arid landscapes.

Its slow growth rate and unique appearance make it a desirable addition to succulent collections.
