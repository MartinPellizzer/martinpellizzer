Pyrrocoma paniculata var. virgata

Pyrrocoma paniculata var. virgata, commonly known as the Virgin's tears or white panicled bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, white to pale pink bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and stream banks. It has opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy texture and a short, branched stem.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
