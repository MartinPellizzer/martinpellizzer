Primula × affinis

Primula × affinis, commonly known as the hybrid primrose, is a perennial flowering plant that belongs to the Primulaceae family.

It is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Primula vulgaris and Primula veris, showcasing a combination of traits from both parent species. The plant typically features showy, clustered flowers in shades of yellow, orange, or white, which bloom in early spring. Its foliage consists of broadly ovate to heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery down.

Primula × affinis is well-suited for woodland gardens and prefers cool, moist, well-drained soil.
