Pergularia tomentosa var. virescens

Pergularia tomentosa var. virescens, commonly known as the green-hairy beggar-ticks, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves, which are often covered in fine, white to grayish pubescence. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are usually ovate to elliptic in shape, with a rough, textured surface.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.
