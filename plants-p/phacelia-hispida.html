Phacelia hispida

Phacelia hispida, commonly known as sticky phacelia, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It features hairy, branched stems and heart-shaped leaves with a rough texture. The plant produces small, blue to purple flowers in clusters, typically blooming from late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open areas such as grasslands and disturbed sites.

Phacelia hispida is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators.
