Piptomeris forrestii

Piptomeris forrestii, commonly known as the Forster's thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, hairy stems and prominent yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its leaves are deeply lobed and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

Piptomeris forrestii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
