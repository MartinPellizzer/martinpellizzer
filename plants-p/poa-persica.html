Poa persica

Poa persica, commonly known as Persian meadowgrass, is a species of grass native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and fine, soft leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.

Poa persica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping for its delicate appearance.
