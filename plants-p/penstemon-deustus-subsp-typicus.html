Penstemon deustus subsp. typicus

Penstemon deustus subsp.

typicus, commonly known as the narrowleaf penstemon, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and vibrant blue to purple tubular flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky soils and open habitats, such as meadows and grasslands. It produces showy blooms from late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.
