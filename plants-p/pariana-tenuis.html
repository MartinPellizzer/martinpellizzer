Pariana tenuis

Pariana tenuis, commonly known as the slender pariana, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its delicate, grass-like foliage and small, inconspicuous flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and is adapted to withstand periods of drought. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to access water from deeper underground.

Despite its modest appearance, Pariana tenuis plays an important role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and nectar for pollinators.
