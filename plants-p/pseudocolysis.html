Pseudocolysis

Pseudocolysis, commonly known as the false stem, is a botanical feature observed in certain plants where the stem appears to be formed by the fusion of leaf bases rather than a true stem.

This structure is typically seen in plants like the genus *Alocasia* and *Caladium*, where the leaves are tightly clustered at the base. The pseudocolysis serves as a support structure for the plant and can also store water and nutrients. It lacks the typical vascular bundles found in a true stem, distinguishing it from regular stems.

This adaptation is often a response to environmental conditions that limit the plant's ability to develop a conventional stem.
