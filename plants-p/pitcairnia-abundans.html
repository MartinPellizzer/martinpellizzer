Pitcairnia abundans

Pitcairnia abundans, commonly known as the Amazonian pineapple plant, is a striking bromeliad native to the rainforests of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, rosette-shaped leaves that form a central cup to collect rainwater, which serves as a habitat for various small organisms. The plant produces vibrant, spathiflorous flowers that are typically pink or red, adding to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Pitcairnia abundans is valued both for its aesthetic qualities and its ecological role in supporting biodiversity.
