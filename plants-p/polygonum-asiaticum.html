Polygonum asiaticum

Polygonum asiaticum, commonly known as Chinese knotweed, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, fertile soils along riverbanks and in wetlands. The plant features upright stems with lance-shaped leaves that are often reddish or purple in color. Its small, clustered flowers are usually greenish-white and appear in late summer.

Polygonum asiaticum is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
