Pseuderanthemum siamense

Pseuderanthemum siamense, commonly known as Siam's false jasmine, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and showy, tubular flowers. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink and white, which bloom in clusters along the stems. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a velvety texture.

It is often cultivated as a ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance and ability to thrive in humid conditions.
