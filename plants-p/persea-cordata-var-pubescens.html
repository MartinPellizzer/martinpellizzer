Persea cordata var. pubescens

Persea cordata var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy persimmon, is a species of flowering plant in the family Lauraceae.

It is native to parts of Central America and northern South America, where it thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. The plant is characterized by its elliptical leaves that are covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its flowers are small and clustered, typically greenish-white in color, and it produces edible fruit that is often sweet and astringent.

The plant is valued both for its ornamental qualities and its fruit, which can be used in culinary applications.
