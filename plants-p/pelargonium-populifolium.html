Pelargonium populifolium

Pelargonium populifolium, commonly known as the broadleaf geranium, is a species of flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white hair, giving them a silvery appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, pale pink to violet flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Due to its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements, it is widely cultivated in gardens around the world.
