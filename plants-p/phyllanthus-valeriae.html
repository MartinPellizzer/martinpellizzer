Phyllanthus valeriae

Phyllanthus valeriae, commonly known as the Valerian geranium, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces elliptical, waxy leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Phyllanthus valeriae is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
