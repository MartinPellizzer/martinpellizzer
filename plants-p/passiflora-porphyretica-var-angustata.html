Passiflora porphyretica var. angustata

Passiflora porphyretica var. angustata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf passionflower, is a tropical climbing vine native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, complex flowers with five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments surrounding the reproductive structures. The plant produces blue or violet berries that are edible and often consumed by birds and mammals. Its leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, with a rough, hairy texture.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
