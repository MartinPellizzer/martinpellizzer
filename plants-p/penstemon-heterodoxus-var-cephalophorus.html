Penstemon heterodoxus var. cephalophorus

Penstemon heterodoxus var. cephalophorus, commonly known as the Sierra Madre penstemon, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and striking blue to violet tubular flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and dry environments, showcasing its adaptability to arid conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, aiding in moisture retention.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a nectar source for pollinators.
