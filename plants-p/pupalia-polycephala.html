Pupalia polycephala

Pupalia polycephala, commonly known as the clustered pomegranate, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 3 meters in height, characterized by its dense, rounded growth habit. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by bright red, fleshy berries. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a leathery texture with a bluish-green hue.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant flowers and berries.
