Polygala amara subsp. brachyptera

Polygala amara subsp.

brachyptera, commonly known as the narrow-leaved milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It features slender, upright stems that bear opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a hairy texture.

The flowers are small and clustered in spicate inflorescences, typically in shades of pink or purple. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

The plant is notable for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
