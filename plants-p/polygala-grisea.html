Polygala grisea

Polygala grisea, commonly known as the gray milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Asia.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its slender, often branched stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. Its flowers are usually pale or bluish in color, and it produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Polygala grisea is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its unique appearance and hardy nature.
