Piper lacunosum

Piper lacunosum, commonly known as the lacunar pepper, is a tropical shrub native to the rainforests of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many species of pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves with a distinctive lobed structure and small, clustered greenish-white flowers. Its fruits are tiny, round berries that mature to a dark purple color.

The plant is valued for its aromatic leaves, which are used in traditional medicine and as a spice in some cuisines.
