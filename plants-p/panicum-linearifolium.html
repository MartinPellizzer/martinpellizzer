Panicum linearifolium

Panicum linearifolium, commonly known as slender panicgrass, is a perennial grass native to the southern United States.

It is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves and compact growth habit, often forming dense mats. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, spikelet-like flowers. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it a common sight in disturbed areas and along roadsides.

Due to its drought tolerance and ornamental appeal, it is sometimes used in landscaping and erosion control.
