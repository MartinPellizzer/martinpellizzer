Pyrus shannanensis

Pyrus shannanensis, commonly known as the Shannan pear, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and clusters of white flowers. The tree produces small, yellowish-green fruits that are typically harvested in late summer. It is valued for its ornamental features and has limited use in horticulture due to its specific growing requirements.

The plant is adapted to temperate climates and thrives in well-drained soils.
