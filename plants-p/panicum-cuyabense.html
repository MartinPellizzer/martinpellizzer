Panicum cuyabense

Panicum cuyabense, commonly known as the Cuyabeno grass, is a perennial grass species native to the Amazon rainforest region of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, linear leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. This plant thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils and is often found in wetland or swampy areas. It plays an important role in the local ecosystem by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.

Due to its ecological significance, it is also of interest for conservation and ecological restoration projects.
