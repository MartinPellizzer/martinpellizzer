Polypodium hirtiforme

Polypodium hirtiforme, commonly known as the hairy shield fern, is a member of the Polypodiaceae family.

It is a perennial fern that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky crevices. The plant features a creeping rhizome with upright, lanceolate fronds covered in fine, hairy structures. Its spore-producing structures, called sori, are located on the undersides of the fronds.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in shaded gardens.
