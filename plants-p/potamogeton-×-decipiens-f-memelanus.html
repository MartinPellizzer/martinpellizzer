Potamogeton × decipiens f. memelanus

Potamogeton × decipiens f. memelanus, commonly known as the Memel river pondweed, is a hybrid aquatic plant belonging to the Potamogetonaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves that are typically arranged in whorls around the stem. The plant thrives in freshwater environments, such as lakes, ponds, and slow-moving rivers, where it can form dense underwater vegetation. Its flowers are small and clustered, often appearing in spikes, and it reproduces both sexually and vegetatively.

This species plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
