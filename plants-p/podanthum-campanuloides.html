Podanthum campanuloides

Podanthum campanuloides, commonly known as the bell-shaped podanthum, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Campanulaceae and is characterized by its bell-shaped, tubular flowers that are typically blue or violet in color. The plant produces small, spherical fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden cultivation in humid climates.
