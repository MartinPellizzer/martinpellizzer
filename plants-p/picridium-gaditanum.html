Picridium gaditanum

Picridium gaditanum, commonly known as the Spanish broom, is a flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its legume fruits and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces yellow flowers in clusters. It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and rocky areas.

Its ornamental value and ability to stabilize soil make it both ecologically and aesthetically significant.
