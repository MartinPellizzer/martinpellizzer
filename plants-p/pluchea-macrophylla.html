Pluchea macrophylla

Pluchea macrophylla, commonly known as the broadleaf cudweed, is a perennial herb native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom from spring to early autumn. Its root system is fibrous, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

Pluchea macrophylla is often found in wetlands, stream banks, and disturbed areas, making it an important component of riparian ecosystems.
