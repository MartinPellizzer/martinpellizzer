Parasicyos

These plants are unique for their parasitic lifestyle, attaching themselves to host plants to derive nutrients. They lack true roots and instead use specialized structures called haustoria to extract water and minerals from their hosts. The leaves of Parasicyos are typically small and alternate, while the flowers are unisexual, with separate male and female structures.

Despite their parasitic nature, some species of Parasicyos are cultivated for their edible fruits, which resemble those of cucumbers.
