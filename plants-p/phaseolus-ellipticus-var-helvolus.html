Phaseolus ellipticus var. helvolus

Phaseolus ellipticus var. helvolus, commonly known as the scarlet runner bean, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its climbing habit and distinctive elongated, elliptical pods. The plant produces vibrant red or orange flowers, which are followed by long, slender pods containing edible seeds. Its botanical features include compound leaves with three leaflets and a fibrous root system that helps in nitrogen fixation.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and culinary use in various cuisines.
