Persica vulgaris var. albaplena

Persica vulgaris var. albaplena, commonly known as the white-flowered peach.

It is a cultivar of the peach tree characterized by its pale pink to white petals, which contrast with its yellow-orange fruits. The plant is native to China and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens. It produces small, sweet fruits that are typically harvested in the summer months.

This variety is often grown for both its aesthetic value and edible yield.
