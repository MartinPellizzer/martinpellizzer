Pinus pumilio var. mugho

Pinus pumilio var. mugho, commonly known as the dwarf mountain pine, is a compact evergreen conifer native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its slow growth rate and typically reaches a height of only 1 to 2 meters. The plant has short, stiff needles that are dark green in color, and it produces small, pale yellow cones. It thrives in cold, dry climates and is often found in alpine and subalpine environments.

Its unique appearance and resilience make it a popular ornamental plant in gardens and landscapes.
