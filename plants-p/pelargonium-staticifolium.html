Pelargonium staticifolium

Pelargonium staticifolium, commonly known as the sticky leaf geranium, is a species native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, sticky leaves that help reduce water loss in arid environments. The plant produces small, pink to red flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

Its unique foliage and compact growth habit make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
