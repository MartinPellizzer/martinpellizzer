Phoberos acuminatus

Phoberos acuminatus, commonly known as the slender ghost fern, is a species of fern native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, slender fronds that are often slightly curled at the tips. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky crevices. Its reproductive structures, including sori, are located on the underside of the fronds.

Phoberos acuminatus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
