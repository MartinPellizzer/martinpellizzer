Pleopeltis fossa

Pleopeltis fossa, commonly known as the Fossa Hymenophylla, is a species of epiphytic fern native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Hymenophyllaceae and is characterized by its delicate, translucent leaves that are often covered in a fine, waxy coating. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, typically found on tree trunks or rocks in tropical rainforests. Its small, clustered fronds are usually less than 10 centimeters in length, and it reproduces via spores rather than seeds.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a conservation concern in many regions.
