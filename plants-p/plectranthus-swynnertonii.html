Plectranthus swynnertonii

Plectranthus swynnertonii, commonly known as Swynnerton's plectranthus, is a perennial herb native to South Africa.

It features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance. The plant produces small, tubular flowers in clusters, typically in shades of purple or violet, which bloom during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

Due to its ornamental value and ease of cultivation, it is a popular choice among gardeners.
