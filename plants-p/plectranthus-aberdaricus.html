Plectranthus aberdaricus

Plectranthus aberdaricus, commonly known as the African violet, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces clusters of tubular flowers that range in color from purple to white, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Its foliage is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
