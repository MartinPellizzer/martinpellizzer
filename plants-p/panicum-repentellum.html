Panicum repentellum

Panicum repentellum, commonly known as the slender panicgrass, is a species of grass native to certain regions of North America.

It typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as fields, roadsides, and sandy soils. The plant has slender, upright stems and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are often tinged with red or purple. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a slight hairy texture.

Panicum repentellum is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small insects and birds.
