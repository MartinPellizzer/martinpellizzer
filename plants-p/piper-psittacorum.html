Piper psittacorum

Piper psittacorum, commonly known as the golden gooseberry, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its small, yellow-orange berries that are edible and have a tart flavor. The plant belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other species known for their aromatic properties. Its leaves are typically alternate and have a glossy appearance, while the stems are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and for the use of its fruits in traditional medicine.
