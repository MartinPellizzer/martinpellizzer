Penstemon euglaucus

Penstemon euglaucus, commonly known as the blue penstemon, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It features upright, branching stems covered in dense, blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically gray-green in color, providing a striking contrast to its vibrant floral display. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its nectar-rich flowers attract a variety of pollinators, including bees and butterflies.
