Pyrus zayuensis

Pyrus zayuensis, commonly known as the Yunnan pear, is a species of flowering plant in the family Rosaceae.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Yunnan Province in China, where it grows at high altitudes. The plant is characterized by its deciduous leaves and small, clustered flowers that bloom in spring. Its fruits are pears that are typically small and have a tart flavor.

Due to its limited distribution and unique habitat, Pyrus zayuensis is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
