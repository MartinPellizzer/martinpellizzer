Pandanus rhombocarpus

Pandanus rhombocarpus, commonly known as the red pandanus, is a tropical plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its long, narrow leaves that are typically green with red veins. The plant produces fragrant, spadix-like inflorescences that are often used in traditional crafts. Its roots and leaves have been used in various cultural and medicinal practices.

It thrives in coastal and sandy soils, often growing near the shoreline.
