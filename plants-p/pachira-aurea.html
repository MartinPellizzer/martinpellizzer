Pachira aurea

Pachira aurea, commonly known as the money plant or Brazilian money tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to South America.

It features thick, waxy, paddle-shaped leaves that are often variegated with yellow or white markings. The plant produces yellow, bell-shaped flowers that give way to woody, elongated fruits containing several seeds. It thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular houseplant.

Its ability to grow aerial roots and its association with good fortune have made it a favored ornamental plant worldwide.
