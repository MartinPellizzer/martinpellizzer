Peperomia inaequalifolia

Peperomia inaequalifolia, commonly known as the unequal-leaf peperomia, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its unique, waxy, and textured foliage, with leaves that have a striking contrast of colors and shapes. The plant typically features lance-shaped leaves that are often variegated with shades of green, white, and pink. It thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Peperomia inaequalifolia is also noted for its ability to produce small, inconspicuous flowers that appear in clusters during the warmer months.
