Phyteuma gaussenii

Phyteuma gaussenii, commonly known as the Gaussen's phyteuma, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems and showy, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically blooms in late summer, producing clusters of violet to blue-purple flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Phyteuma gaussenii is often found in alpine meadows and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
