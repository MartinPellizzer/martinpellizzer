Paraphlomis membranacea

Paraphlomis membranacea, commonly known as the membranous paraphlomis, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Lamiaceae, which includes many aromatic plants. The plant features square stems and opposite leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its small, tubular flowers are typically purple or pink, arranged in whorls along the stem.

It is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
