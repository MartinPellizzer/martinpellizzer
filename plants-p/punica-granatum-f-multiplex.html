Punica granatum f. multiplex

Punica granatum f. multiplex, commonly known as the mulberry-like pomegranate, is a botanical variant of the pomegranate plant.

It is characterized by its distinctive, elongated, and often branched fruit structure, which differs from the typical round pomegranate. The plant produces small, fleshy, red fruits that are often used in ornamental horticulture. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This cultivar is valued for its unique appearance and is commonly cultivated for decorative purposes.
