Philadelphus dubius

Philadelphus dubius, commonly known as the common mockorange, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It features fragrant, white to pinkish-yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has opposite, simple, and ovate leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. Its bark is smooth and reddish-brown, becoming fissured with age.

It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value.
