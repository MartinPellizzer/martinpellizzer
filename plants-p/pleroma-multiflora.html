Pleroma multiflora

Pleroma multiflora, commonly known as the red-flowered bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking red or pink bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and grows in moist, well-drained soils, often in woodland areas. It is a perennial herb that can reach heights of up to 2 feet.

Pleroma multiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
