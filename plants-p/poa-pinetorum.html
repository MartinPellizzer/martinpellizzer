Poa pinetorum

Poa pinetorum, commonly known as the pinegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the northern hemisphere.

It is characterized by its fine, slender blades and tufted growth habit, often forming dense mats in forested areas. This plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is commonly found in coniferous and mixed forests. Its inflorescence consists of a single, narrow spike that bears small, spikelet-like flowers.

Poa pinetorum plays an important ecological role by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
