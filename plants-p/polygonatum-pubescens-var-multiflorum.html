Polygonatum pubescens var. multiflorum

Polygonatum pubescens var. multiflorum, commonly known as the hairy Solomon's seal, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit and lance-shaped leaves with a hairy texture. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers in early summer. Its fruit is a red, fleshy berry that matures in late summer.

It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.
