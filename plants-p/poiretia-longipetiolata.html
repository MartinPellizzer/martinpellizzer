Poiretia longipetiolata

Poiretia longipetiolata, commonly known as the long-petioled poiretia, is a species of flowering plant in the family Araceae.

It is characterized by its striking, arrow-shaped leaves with long petioles that can reach several centimeters in length. The plant produces clusters of small, white to greenish flowers arranged in an inflorescence called a spadix, which is enclosed by a modified leaf called a spathe. It is native to tropical regions of South America and thrives in humid, shady environments.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated as a houseplant or in botanical gardens.
