Phyllanthus liesneri

Phyllanthus liesneri, commonly known as the African false mustard, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It is a small shrub characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are often clustered at the base of the plant. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, typically blooming during the dry season. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.

The species is notable for its potential medicinal uses, though further research is needed to confirm its therapeutic properties.
