Phaseolus adenanthus var. latifolius

Phaseolus adenanthus var. latifolius, commonly known as the broadleaf common bean, is a perennial legume native to South America.

It features compound leaves with broad, ovate leaflets and produces small, yellow flowers that develop into elongated, cylindrical pods. The plant is characterized by its ability to fix nitrogen through symbiotic relationships with rhizobia bacteria in its root nodules. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often grown for its edible seeds.

This variety is valued for its adaptability and nutritional content.
