Pseuderanthemum fasciculatum

Pseuderanthemum fasciculatum, commonly known as the clustered pseuderanthemum, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of pink and white, which bloom during the warmer months. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its vibrant blooms and compact size.
