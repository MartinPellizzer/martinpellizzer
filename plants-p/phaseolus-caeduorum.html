Phaseolus caeduorum

Phaseolus caeduorum, commonly known as the scarlet runner bean, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its climbing habit, with long, hairy stems that support its large, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces striking red or purple flowers that are followed by elongated pods containing edible seeds. Its botanical features include a fibrous root system and a complex vascular structure typical of legumes.

It is valued both for its ornamental appeal and as a source of nutritious beans.
