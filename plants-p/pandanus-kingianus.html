Pandanus kingianus

Pandanus kingianus, commonly known as the King Island pandanus, is a tropical plant native to the island of King Island in the Bass Strait, Australia.

It is a member of the Pandanaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stem and long, narrow leaves that are often used in traditional crafts. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically borne in clusters at the base of the leaves. Its fibrous leaves are harvested for making mats, baskets, and other woven items, highlighting its cultural and practical significance.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appearance and ecological role in its native habitat.
