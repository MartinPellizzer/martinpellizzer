Primula kitadakensis

Primula kitadakensis, commonly known as the Japanese primrose, is a species of flowering plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in woodland areas and is known for its ability to thrive in shaded environments. It has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of hair, giving it a soft, fuzzy appearance.

Primula kitadakensis is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
