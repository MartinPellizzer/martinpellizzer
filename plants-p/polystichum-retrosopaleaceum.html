Polystichum retrosopaleaceum

Polystichum retrosopaleaceum, commonly known as the retro-slope fern, is a species of tree fern native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnately compound fronds that can reach several meters in length. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically found in tropical and subtropical rainforests. Its fronds are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

The species is valued for both its ornamental appeal and ecological significance in its native habitat.
