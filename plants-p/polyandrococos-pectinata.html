Polyandrococos pectinata

Polyandrococos pectinata, commonly known as the pectinated polyandrous palm, is a unique species native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive pectinate (toothed) leaf margins, which give it its scientific name. The plant exhibits a complex reproductive system, with multiple male and female flowers on the same inflorescence. Its leaves are large and fan-shaped, often reaching several meters in diameter.

The species is of interest to botanists due to its unusual floral structure and potential ecological significance in its native habitat.
