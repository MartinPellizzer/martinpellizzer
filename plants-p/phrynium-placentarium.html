Phrynium placentarium

Phrynium placentarium, commonly known as the African arrowroot or swamp arrowroot, is a perennial herb native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its large, arrow-shaped leaves that can grow up to 1 meter in length. The plant produces striking yellowish-green spadices with small white flowers, typical of the arum family. Its rhizomes are edible and have been used traditionally for their starchy content.

Phrynium placentarium thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in wetlands and along riverbanks.
