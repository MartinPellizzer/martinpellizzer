Polygonatum multiflorum var. obtusifolium

Polygonatum multiflorum var. obtusifolium, commonly known as the blunt-leaf Solomon's seal, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of East Asia.

It features long, arching stems that bear clusters of small, greenish-white flowers in late spring. The leaves are broad, ovate, and have a blunt tip, giving the plant its varietal name. It thrives in shaded, moist woodland environments and has a rhizomatous root system.

The plant is valued in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits and is also cultivated for ornamental purposes.
