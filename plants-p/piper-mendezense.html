Piper mendezense

Piper mendezense, commonly known as the Mendez's pepper vine, is a climbing shrub native to the humid lowland forests of Central America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many species of pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves and small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in spikes. Its fruits are berry-like, turning from green to red as they mature, and are often used in traditional medicine.

The plant's botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies in tropical ecosystems.
