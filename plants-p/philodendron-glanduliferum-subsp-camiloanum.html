Philodendron glanduliferum subsp. camiloanum

Philodendron glanduliferum subsp.

camiloanum, commonly known as the Camiloanum Philodendron, is a tropical climbing plant native to Central and South America. It features heart-shaped, glossy leaves that are often variegated with light green and white markings.

The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically pollinated by insects. Its stems are sturdy and can climb using aerial roots, making it well-suited for hanging baskets or vertical gardens.

It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires consistent moisture and humidity to flourish.
