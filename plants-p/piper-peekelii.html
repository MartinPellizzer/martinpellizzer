Piper peekelii

Piper peekelii, commonly known as the Hawaiian pepper plant, is a member of the Piperaceae family.

It is native to the Hawaiian Islands and is characterized by its climbing habit and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by elliptical, yellowish berries. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in rainforest habitats.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species.
