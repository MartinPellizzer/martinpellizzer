Prunus communis proles congesta

Prunus communis proles congesta, commonly known as the European cherry or common cherry, is a species of shrub or small tree native to Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape. The plant produces clusters of white or pale pink flowers in early spring, followed by edible drupes that range in color from red to black. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit, which can be used in cooking and baking.

The species is also valued in horticulture for its adaptability to various soil types and climates.
