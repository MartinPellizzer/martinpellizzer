Pulsatilla slavica subsp. subslavica

Pulsatilla slavica subsp.

subslavica, commonly known as the Slavonic pasqueflower, is a herbaceous perennial plant belonging to the Ranunculaceae family. It is characterized by its showy, early spring blooms consisting of pinkish-white flowers with prominent yellow stamens.

The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine meadows, preferring well-drained, calcareous soils. Its leaves are palmately divided, and the plant produces a single, erect stem.

It is native to the Carpathian Mountains and is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological role in mountain ecosystems.
