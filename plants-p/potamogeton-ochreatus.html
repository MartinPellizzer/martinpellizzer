Potamogeton ochreatus

Potamogeton ochreatus, commonly known as the yellow pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in North America.

It is characterized by its long, slender stems and small, yellowish-green leaves that are often arranged in whorls. The plant produces tiny, inconspicuous flowers that are typically borne in clusters near the water's surface. It plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.

Potamogeton ochreatus is often found in lakes, ponds, and slow-moving rivers.
