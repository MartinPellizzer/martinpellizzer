Prunus ernestii

Prunus ernestii, commonly known as the Ernestii cherry, is a species of flowering shrub or small tree native to China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its slender branches and clusters of white or pinkish flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, edible fruits that are similar in appearance to cherries but are typically less sweet and more tart. It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive foliage and flowering display.

Prunus ernestii prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for gardens in temperate climates.
