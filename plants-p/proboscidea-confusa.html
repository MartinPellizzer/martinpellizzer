Proboscidea confusa

Proboscidea confusa, commonly known as the ghost flower, is a rare and enigmatic plant native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its unique, elongated, and often translucent petals that resemble ghostly apparitions. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that bloom at night, attracting specific pollinators such as moths. Its leaves are typically narrow and waxy, adapted to conserve water in its harsh environment.

Due to its rarity and ecological significance, Proboscidea confusa is considered a conservation concern.
