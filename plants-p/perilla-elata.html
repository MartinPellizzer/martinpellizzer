Perilla elata

Perilla elata, commonly known as Chinese coriander or rice pepper, is an herbaceous plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like mint and basil. The plant is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, oval-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. Its flowers are clustered in whorls and are typically pale purple or white in color.

Perilla elata is valued for its aromatic leaves and seeds, which are used in traditional cuisines and herbal medicine.
