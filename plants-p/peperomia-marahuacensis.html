Peperomia marahuacensis

Peperomia marahuacensis, commonly known as the Mexican pepper plant, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to Central America and is characterized by its compact, upright growth habit and thick, waxy leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. It prefers bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor cultivation.

Peperomia marahuacensis is relatively low maintenance and thrives in moderate temperatures with minimal watering.
