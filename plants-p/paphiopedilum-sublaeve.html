Paphiopedilum sublaeve

Paphiopedilum sublaeve, commonly known as the bare-veined orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dark green, glabrous leaves and produces showy, fragrant flowers with a distinctive lip. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest undergrowth. Its flowers are usually pink or white with a yellowish throat, and it blooms during the spring or early summer.

Paphiopedilum sublaeve is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
