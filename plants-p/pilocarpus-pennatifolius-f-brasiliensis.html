Pilocarpus pennatifolius f. brasiliensis

Pilocarpus pennatifolius f. brasiliensis, commonly known as the Brazilian jaborandi, is a shrub native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is characterized by its pinnately compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant contains alkaloids, particularly pilocarpine, which has medicinal uses. It thrives in humid, tropical climates with well-drained soil.

Due to its ecological and pharmacological significance, it is often studied for its potential in traditional and modern medicine.
