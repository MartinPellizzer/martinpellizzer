Paepalanthus viridulus

Paepalanthus viridulus, commonly known as the green panic grass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in panicles, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions.

Due to its hardy nature, it is often used in ornamental landscaping and erosion control.
