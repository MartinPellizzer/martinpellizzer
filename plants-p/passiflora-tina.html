Passiflora tina

Passiflora tina, commonly known as the Mexican passionflower, is a climbing vine native to Central America.

It is characterized by its striking, intricate flowers that feature five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments. The plant produces edible, orange-yellow berries that are rich in nutrients. Its leaves are heart-shaped and often have a velvety texture.

Passiflora tina is valued for its ornamental beauty and traditional medicinal uses.
