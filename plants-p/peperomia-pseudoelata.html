Peperomia pseudoelata

Peperomia pseudoelata, commonly known as the silver peperomia, is a compact, low-maintenance plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It features thick, waxy leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery or grayish coating, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or red, which appear in clusters along the stems. It thrives in bright, indirect light and prefers well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Its compact size and attractive foliage make it a popular choice for indoor gardening and container planting.
