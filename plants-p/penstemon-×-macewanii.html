Penstemon × macewanii

Penstemon × macewanii, commonly known as the Macewan beardtongue, is a hybrid perennial plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of tubular, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pink to purple. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its hardy nature and ornamental value contribute to its widespread cultivation in gardens across arid and semi-arid regions.
