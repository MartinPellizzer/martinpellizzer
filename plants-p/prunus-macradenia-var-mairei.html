Prunus macradenia var. mairei

Prunus macradenia var. mairei, commonly known as the Maire cherry, is a rare and ornamental species native to China.

It is characterized by its small, clustered flowers that bloom in early spring, typically in shades of white or pale pink. The plant produces small, edible fruits that are often consumed by birds and small mammals. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a slightly serrated margin.

This variety is valued for its aesthetic appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique floral display.
