Plectronia bibracteata

Plectronia bibracteata, commonly known as the red sandalwood, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or red in color. The plant produces woody fruits that contain seeds, which are dispersed by animals. Its leaves are alternate and simple, with a glossy surface.

Plectronia bibracteata is valued for its hard, durable wood, which has been used traditionally in furniture and carving.
