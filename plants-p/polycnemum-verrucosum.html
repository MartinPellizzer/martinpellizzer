Polycnemum verrucosum

Polycnemum verrucosum, commonly known as the hairy nettle, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Urticaceae family.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and serrated leaves, which are often covered in stinging hairs. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It produces small, greenish flowers arranged in clusters, and its root system is fibrous and rhizomatous.

Polycnemum verrucosum is often found in temperate regions of Europe and Asia.
