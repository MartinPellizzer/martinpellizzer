Priestleya tecta

Priestleya tecta, commonly known as the mountain rock cactus, is a species of flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a perennial cactus that typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils at high elevations. The plant features a compact, globular stem with spines and produces yellow flowers during the spring and early summer. Its botanical characteristics include a thick, succulent stem adapted for water storage and a shallow root system suited to arid environments.

Priestleya tecta is often found in desert and mountainous regions, where it thrives in full sun and minimal rainfall.
