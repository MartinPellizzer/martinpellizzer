Primula caucasica

Primula caucasica, commonly known as the Caucasian primrose, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Caucasus region.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and mountainous areas. The plant features rosettes of broad, oval leaves and produces clusters of bell-shaped, brightly colored flowers in shades of white, pink, and yellow. It blooms from late spring to early summer, adding vibrant color to its natural habitat.

Primula caucasica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
