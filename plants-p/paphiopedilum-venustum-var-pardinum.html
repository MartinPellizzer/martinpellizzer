Paphiopedilum venustum var. pardinum

Paphiopedilum venustum var. pardinum, commonly known as the Black Orchid, is a rare and striking orchid native to the mountainous regions of China.

It is characterized by its dark, velvety petals and sepals, which give it its distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows as a terrestrial orchid, often found in shaded, humid environments. Its flowers are relatively small but highly fragrant, with a unique coloration that sets it apart from other orchid varieties.

Due to its rarity and beauty, it is highly sought after by orchid enthusiasts and conservationists alike.
