Prunus serrulata f. classica

Prunus serrulata f. classica, commonly known as the Japanese cherry blossom, is a cultivar of the Prunus serrulata species, which is native to Japan.

It is characterized by its delicate, pale pink to white flowers that bloom in early spring, typically around March or April. The plant is a small deciduous tree, often reaching heights of 5 to 10 meters, with a rounded crown and smooth, reddish-brown bark. Its leaves are oval-shaped with serrated edges, giving it the species name "serrulata.".

The flower buds are tightly clustered, and the tree is well-known for its ornamental value, particularly in traditional Japanese gardens and during the annual hanami (flower viewing) season.
