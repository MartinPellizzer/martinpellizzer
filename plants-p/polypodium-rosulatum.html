Polypodium rosulatum

Polypodium rosulatum, commonly known as the rosette fern, is a tropical fern species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive rosette-shaped fronds that form a dense, compact cluster. The plant has a short, creeping rhizome from which the fronds emerge. Its leaves are typically dark green and have a leathery texture.

Polypodium rosulatum thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
