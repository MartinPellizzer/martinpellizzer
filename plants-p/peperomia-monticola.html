Peperomia monticola

Peperomia monticola, commonly known as the mountain peperomia, is a compact, evergreen perennial native to the mountainous regions of Central and South America.

It features glossy, ovate leaves that are often reddish or maroon on the underside, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers that are clustered in spikes, typically blooming in late spring or early summer. It thrives in well-draining soil and prefers indirect sunlight, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Its slow growth rate and ornamental foliage make it a versatile and low-maintenance houseplant.
