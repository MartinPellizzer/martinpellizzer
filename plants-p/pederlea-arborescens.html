Pederlea arborescens

Pederlea arborescens, commonly known as the tree fern, is a member of the Cyatheaceae family and is native to tropical regions of Asia and Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk covered with a dense layer of persistent, fibrous roots. The plant produces large, feather-like fronds that can grow up to several meters in length. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires consistent moisture and well-drained soil.

Due to its unique appearance and slow growth rate, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant in tropical gardens.
