Phaseolus viridis

Phaseolus viridis, commonly known as the common scarlet runner bean, is a herbaceous annual plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its climbing habit, with tendrils that allow it to ascend supports. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that develop into elongated, bean-like pods containing edible seeds. Its leaves are palmately compound, typically with three to five leaflets.

Phaseolus viridis is valued for its nutritional content and is cultivated in various regions for both culinary and agricultural purposes.
