Pseudorchis albida var. tricuspis

Pseudorchis albida var. tricuspis, commonly known as the white orchid or tricuspis orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, white flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests or rocky slopes. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, clustered inflorescences.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated for its beauty.
