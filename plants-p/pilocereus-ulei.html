Pilocereus ulei

Pilocereus ulei, commonly known as the red-pear cactus, is a species of cactus native to South America.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems that are typically red or reddish in color, often with a waxy coating. The plant produces large, showy flowers that are usually white or pale pink, which open at night. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, requiring minimal water and well-draining soil.

Pilocereus ulei is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in suitable conditions.
