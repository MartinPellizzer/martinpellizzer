Pyrus decora var. groenlandica

Pyrus decora var. groenlandica, commonly known as the Greenland serviceberry, is a variant of the American serviceberry.

It is a small to medium-sized deciduous tree native to northern regions of North America. The plant features white flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by edible berries that ripen in late summer. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a serrated margin.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing food for wildlife.
