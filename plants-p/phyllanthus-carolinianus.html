Phyllanthus carolinianus

Phyllanthus carolinianus, commonly known as the Carolina gooseberry, is a small evergreen shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features glossy, elliptical leaves that are typically arranged in whorls of three to five along the stems. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that give way to bright red, fleshy berries resembling gooseberries. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The plant thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.
