Piper irazuanum var. suborbiculatum

Piper irazuanum var. suborbiculatum, commonly known as the Irazu pepper or Irazu mountain pepper, is a tropical shrub native to Central America.

It is characterized by its small, elliptic to suborbiculate leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that develop into elongated, aromatic berries. The berries are typically harvested when fully mature and are used in traditional medicine and as a spice.

It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in cloud forests.
