Pistacia atlantica

Pistacia atlantica, commonly known as the Atlantic pistachio, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically divided into three to five leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by woody, spiny fruits containing edible seeds. Its bark is often fissured and grayish in color, and it is well adapted to arid and rocky environments.

Pistacia atlantica is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical significance in traditional medicine.
