Pseudoprospero firmifolium subsp. natalensis

Pseudoprospero firmifolium subsp.

natalensis, commonly known as Natal's firm-leaved pseudoprospero, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its robust, evergreen foliage.

The plant produces small, daisy-like yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. It typically grows in well-drained soils and is adapted to dry, sunny conditions.

Its subspecies status highlights its distinct ecological and morphological features compared to other members of the species.
