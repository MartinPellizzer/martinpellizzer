Poa cheelii

Poa cheelii, commonly known as the Cheel's bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its fine, blue-green blades and dense, tufted growth habit. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and montane regions. It produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences with pale blue to greenish flowers.

Poa cheelii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
