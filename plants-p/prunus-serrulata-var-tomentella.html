Prunus serrulata var. tomentella

Prunus serrulata var. tomentella, commonly known as the Japanese cherry or some varieties as the weeping cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. The plant produces clusters of white or pink flowers in early spring, which are highly attractive to pollinators. Its leaves are ovate with serrated edges and a hairy underside, giving it a distinctive texture.

The species is valued in horticulture for its aesthetic appeal and is often used in gardens and landscapes.
