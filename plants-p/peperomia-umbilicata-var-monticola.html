Peperomia umbilicata var. monticola

Peperomia umbilicata var. monticola, commonly known as the Mountain Peperomia, is a compact, evergreen perennial native to the humid forests of Central and South America.

It features thick, waxy leaves that are typically dark green with a silvery underside, giving it a striking visual contrast. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in shades of white or pale green, which are often hidden among the foliage. It thrives in bright, indirect light and prefers well-draining soil with moderate humidity.

Due to its compact size and attractive foliage, it is a popular choice for indoor gardening and rock gardens.
