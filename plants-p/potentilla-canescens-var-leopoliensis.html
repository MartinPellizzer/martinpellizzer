Potentilla canescens var. leopoliensis

Potentilla canescens var. leopoliensis, commonly known as the Leopoliensis cinquefoil, is a perennial shrub native to Central Asia.

It features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has finely divided, silver-green leaves that are covered in a powdery coating, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its compact growth habit and long flowering period.
