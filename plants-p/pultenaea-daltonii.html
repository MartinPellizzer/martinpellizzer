Pultenaea daltonii

Pultenaea daltonii, commonly known as Dalton's wattle, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. Its yellow flowers appear in spring, adding a vibrant splash of color to the landscape.

Pultenaea daltonii is also valued for its ecological role in supporting local wildlife and its potential use in horticulture.
