Pseudocentrum sylvicola

Pseudocentrum sylvicola, commonly known as the Philippine starling tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves and showy, clustered flowers. The plant produces small, fleshy berries that are typically orange or red in color. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland and montane forests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation areas.
