Phyllanthus scaber var. genuinus

Phyllanthus scaber var. genuinus, commonly known as the rough phyllanthus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves with a rough texture due to the presence of small, raised dots. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for tropical and subtropical gardens.
