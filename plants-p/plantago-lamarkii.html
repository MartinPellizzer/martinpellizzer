Plantago lamarkii

Plantago lamarkii, commonly known as the Lamark's plantain, is a perennial herb native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of broad, waxy, lance-shaped leaves that help reduce water loss. The plant produces small, greenish flowers in a spike-like inflorescence, which are wind-pollinated. It has a deep root system that allows it to thrive in nutrient-poor soils and withstand drought conditions.

Plantago lamarkii is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
