Phaeopappus depressus

Phaeopappus depressus, commonly known as the yellow pappus.

It is a flowering plant native to the western United States, typically found in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant has small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring, and its leaves are usually narrow and gray-green in color. It produces a distinctive pappus, which is a feathery structure that aids in seed dispersal.

The species is adapted to withstand drought conditions and thrives in well-drained soils.
