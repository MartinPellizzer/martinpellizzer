Platystyliparis aurita

Platystyliparis aurita, commonly known as the golden bell orchid, is a striking member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, where it thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant features elongated, pendulous inflorescences adorned with golden-yellow flowers that resemble small bells. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, often with a waxy coating to reduce water loss.
