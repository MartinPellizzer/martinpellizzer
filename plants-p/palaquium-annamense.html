Palaquium annamense

Palaquium annamense, commonly known as the Annam palm, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and glossy, elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by spherical, fleshy fruits that are orange-red in color. Its habitat includes tropical rainforests and is often found in lowland areas with high humidity.

Palaquium annamense is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its fruit and timber.
