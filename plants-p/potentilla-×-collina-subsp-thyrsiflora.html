Potentilla × collina subsp. thyrsiflora

Potentilla × collina subsp.

thyrsiflora, commonly known as the thrysoflora cinquefoil, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its slender, often branched stems and small, rounded leaves with a slightly hairy surface.

The plant produces clusters of bright yellow flowers in late summer, which are arranged in thyrse-like inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.

Its ornamental value is appreciated in gardens for its compact growth and vibrant floral display.
