Penstemon pubescens var. gracilis

Penstemon pubescens var. gracilis, commonly known as the slender beardtongue, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and narrow, hairy stems. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that are typically pink to purple in color, attracting pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy perennial suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its foliage is often covered in fine, soft hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
