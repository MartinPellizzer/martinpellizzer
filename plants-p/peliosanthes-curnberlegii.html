Peliosanthes curnberlegii

Peliosanthes curnberlegii, commonly known as the golden chalice, is a rare and striking plant native to the mountainous regions of southern China.

It is characterized by its unique, cup-shaped flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color, which give it its common name. The plant belongs to the Arecaceae family, making it a type of palm, though it is not commonly cultivated due to its specific environmental requirements. Its foliage consists of long, narrow leaves that are arranged in a fan-like pattern, contributing to its ornamental appeal.
