Piper viridicaule

Piper viridicaule, commonly known as green-stemmed pepper or green pepper vine, is a tropical climbing shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its slender, vine-like stems and small, heart-shaped leaves that are usually bright green. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers followed by small, round berries that turn red when mature. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has some traditional medicinal uses.

The plant thrives in humid, warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
