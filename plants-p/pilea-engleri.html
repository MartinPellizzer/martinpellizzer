Pilea engleri

Pilea engleri, commonly known as the Chinese money plant, is a compact, evergreen perennial shrub native to China.

It features thick, waxy leaves that are typically round or heart-shaped, with a deep green color and a slightly fuzzy texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually greenish-white in color, though they are not showy. It thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Pilea engleri is also noted for its ability to propagate easily through stem cuttings.
