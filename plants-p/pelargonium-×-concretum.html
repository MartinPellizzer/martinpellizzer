Pelargonium × concretum

Pelargonium × concretum, commonly known as the rock geranium, is a hybrid succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, thick leaves that are often gray-green in color, adapted to store water in arid environments. The plant produces small, clustered flowers in shades of red, pink, or white, typically blooming in summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

Due to its compact growth habit, it is often used as a decorative plant in both indoor and outdoor settings.
