Peristrophe angustifolia var. aureovariegata

Peristrophe angustifolia var. aureovariegata, commonly known as the golden variegated arrowleaf, is a striking tropical plant characterized by its narrow, arrow-shaped leaves with distinctive golden variegation.

Native to Southeast Asia, it thrives in warm, humid environments and requires bright, indirect light to maintain its vibrant leaf colors. The plant's foliage is often adorned with intricate patterns of yellow and green, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by winged fruits, which are typical of the Acanthaceae family.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in shaded garden settings.
