Pseudarthria gyrans

Pseudarthria gyrans, commonly known as the spiral grass, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive spiral arrangement of leaves along the stem. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are clustered in dense, spherical inflorescences. Its leaves are alternate and trifoliate, with each leaflet being narrow and slightly curved.

Pseudarthria gyrans is often found in grassland habitats and is valued for its ornamental appearance and potential ecological benefits.
