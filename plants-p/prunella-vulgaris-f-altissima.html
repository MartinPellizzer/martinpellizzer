Prunella vulgaris f. altissima

Prunella vulgaris f. altissima, commonly known as tall selfheal, is a variant of the common selfheal plant.

It is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa, characterized by its upright growth habit and taller stature compared to the typical form. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs, and it produces clusters of blue or purple flowers in late spring to early summer. It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and along roadsides.

This form is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional medicinal uses.
