Piper artanthopse

Piper artanthopse, commonly known as the velvetleaf pepper, is a tropical climbing vine native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species. The plant is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are covered in a fine, velvety texture. Its small, greenish-white flowers are followed by tiny, berry-like fruits that turn red when mature.

The plant is often found in moist, shaded environments and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
