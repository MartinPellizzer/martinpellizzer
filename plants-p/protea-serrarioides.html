Protea serrarioides

Protea serrarioides, commonly known as the serrated protea, is a striking South African plant belonging to the Proteaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescences composed of numerous small, tubular flowers arranged in a spherical or conical shape. The plant typically grows in fynbos vegetation, thriving in well-drained, acidic soils. Its flowers come in a range of colors, including white, pink, and red, and are pollinated by birds and insects.

Protea serrarioides is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
