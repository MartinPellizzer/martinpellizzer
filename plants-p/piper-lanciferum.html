Piper lanciferum

Piper lanciferum, commonly known as the lance-leaf pepper, is a tropical climbing vine belonging to the Piperaceae family.

It is characterized by its elongated, lance-shaped leaves and small, greenish-yellow flowers that develop into oval, red berries. The plant is native to Southeast Asia and is often found in humid, shaded environments within tropical forests. Its berries contain tiny seeds and are sometimes used in traditional medicine.

The plant's botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
