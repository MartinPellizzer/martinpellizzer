Pinalia crassicaulis

Pinalia crassicaulis, commonly known as the broad-stemmed pinalia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine pubescence. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in dense clusters, typically in shades of white or pale green. Its root system is fibrous and adapted to absorb moisture from the rocky soil in its natural habitat.

Pinalia crassicaulis is valued for its resilience and is often found in high-altitude grasslands and shrublands.
