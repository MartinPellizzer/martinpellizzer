Poecilocnemis macrostachya

Poecilocnemis macrostachya, commonly known as the giant bamboo grass, is a member of the Poaceae family, characterized by its tall, robust stalks and dense foliage.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions, where it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. The plant exhibits a clumping growth habit, forming large, upright tussocks that can reach significant heights. Its leaves are long and narrow, often with a slight sheen, and its inflorescence consists of clustered, spike-like structures.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic and structural presence.
