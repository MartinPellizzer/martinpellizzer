Pinus halepensis

Pinus halepensis, commonly known as the Aleppo pine, is a species of evergreen conifer native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its straight, tall trunk and needle-like leaves that grow in pairs. The plant produces cones that contain winged seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments.

The Aleppo pine plays an important role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
