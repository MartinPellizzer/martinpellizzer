Pandanus penetrans

Pandanus penetrans, commonly known as the swamp pandanus, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and long, narrow leaves that are often used in traditional crafts. The plant thrives in wet, swampy environments and is adapted to tolerate waterlogged soil. Its inflorescence consists of a branched cluster of small, fragrant flowers.

The fruit, which is a fibrous structure containing edible seeds, is an important food source for various wildlife species.
