Pancratium amancaes

Pancratium amancaes, commonly known as the yellow nightshade, is a striking bulbous plant native to the Cape region of South Africa.

It features showy, trumpet-shaped yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as moths and beetles. The plant has a fleshy, underground bulb that stores water and nutrients, allowing it to survive in arid conditions. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces capsule fruits containing numerous seeds.

Pancratium amancaes is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
