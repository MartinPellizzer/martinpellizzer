Prunus veitchii

Prunus veitchii, commonly known as the Veitch's cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It features smooth, gray bark and lance-shaped leaves that turn yellow in the fall. The plant produces clusters of white, fragrant flowers in early spring, followed by small, edible berries. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens.

Prunus veitchii prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.
