Prunus tikalana

Prunus tikalana, commonly known as the Tikalana cherry, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of Central America, particularly found in countries like Guatemala and Belize. The plant is characterized by its small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring, followed by edible cherries that are typically red when ripe. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a smooth texture, while its bark is smooth and grayish in color.

Prunus tikalana is often cultivated for both its ornamental value and the culinary use of its fruits.
