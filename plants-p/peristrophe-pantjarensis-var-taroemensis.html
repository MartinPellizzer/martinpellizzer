Peristrophe pantjarensis var. taroemensis

Peristrophe pantjarensis var. taroemensis, commonly known as the Taroemensis grass, is a perennial herb native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with tiny, greenish flowers that are typically wind-pollinated. Its leaves are narrow and have a smooth texture, making it suitable for ornamental and ecological landscaping.

This variant is notable for its adaptability to various soil types and its ability to thrive in both wet and dry conditions.
