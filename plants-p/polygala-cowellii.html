Polygala cowellii

Polygala cowellii, commonly known as Cowell's milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae, which is characterized by its distinctive floral structures. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, such as rocky soils and open woodlands. Its small, delicate flowers are usually white or pale pink, and it produces small, bean-like fruits.

Polygala cowellii is often found in regions with low rainfall and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.
