Pennisetum pennisetiforme

Pennisetum pennisetiforme, commonly known as fountain grass, is a perennial grass native to Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted, fountain-like growth habit and slender, arching blades that create a soft, flowing appearance. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are often pink or red in color, adding visual interest in gardens. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping.

Pennisetum pennisetiforme is also valued for its drought tolerance and ability to attract birds and beneficial insects.
