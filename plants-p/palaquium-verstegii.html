Palaquium verstegii

Palaquium verstegii, commonly known as the yellow mangostin, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its elliptical, glossy leaves and small, yellow-orange fruits. The plant thrives in tropical climates with high humidity and well-drained soil. Its fruits are edible, though the pulp is typically consumed with the seeds removed.

The tree is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its medicinal properties.
