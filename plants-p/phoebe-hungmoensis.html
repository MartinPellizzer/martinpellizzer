Phoebe hungmoensis

Phoebe hungmoensis, commonly known as the Guangxi phoebe, is a species of flowering plant in the family Lauraceae.

It is native to China, specifically the Guangxi region, where it grows in moist, shaded forest environments. The plant is characterized by its simple, elliptical leaves and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. Its bark is smooth and often exhibits a light brown or grayish color.

Phoebe hungmoensis is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in horticultural settings.
