Peperomia latibracteata

Peperomia latibracteata, commonly known as the broad-bracted peperomia, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Central and South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its compact, upright growth habit and thick, waxy leaves that are often green with a reddish or purple tinge. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are typically borne in clusters and are followed by distinctive, broad bracts.

Peperomia latibracteata is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental foliage and low maintenance requirements.
