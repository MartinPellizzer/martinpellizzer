Poinciana compressa

Poinciana compressa, commonly known as the flame tree, is a tropical flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, bright red or orange feathery inflorescences that appear during the dry season. The plant has compound leaves composed of multiple leaflets, and its flowers are arranged in dense, branched clusters. It thrives in warm climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental tree due to its vibrant blooms.

Poinciana compressa is also valued for its fast growth and ability to provide shade in urban landscapes.
