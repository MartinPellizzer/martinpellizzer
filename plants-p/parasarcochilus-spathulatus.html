Parasarcochilus spathulatus

Parasarcochilus spathulatus, commonly known as the broad-leafed leek orchid, is an epiphytic plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its leathery, strap-like leaves. The plant produces small, white or greenish flowers arranged in a spike, which bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found growing on trees or rocks in rainforest areas.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
