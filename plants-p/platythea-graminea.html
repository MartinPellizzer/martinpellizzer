Platythea graminea

Platythea graminea, commonly known as the grass-like flatleaf plant, is a member of the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants.

It is characterized by its flat, grass-like leaves that are typically narrow and arranged in a dense, tufted growth habit. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in spikelets, typical of the sedge family. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of streams.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects to stabilize soil and support local wildlife.
