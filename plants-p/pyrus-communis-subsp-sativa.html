Pyrus communis subsp. sativa

Pyrus communis subsp.

sativa, commonly known as the common pear, is a species of tree native to Europe and parts of western Asia. It is characterized by its broad ovate leaves and clusters of white flowers that bloom in spring.

The fruit, which is typically harvested in late summer, is a fleshy, edible pome with a sweet or sour taste depending on the variety. The tree prefers well-drained soil and full sun, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value as well as its fruit.

It is also known for its historical significance in horticulture and agriculture.
