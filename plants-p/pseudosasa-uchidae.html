Pseudosasa uchidae

Pseudosasa uchidae, commonly known as the Uchida bamboo, is a species of grass native to Japan.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its slender, cylindrical stems that grow in dense clumps. The plant typically reaches a height of 2 to 3 meters and has narrow, grass-like leaves with a bluish-green hue. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

The Uchida bamboo is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional Japanese crafts.
