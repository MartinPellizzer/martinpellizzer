Pyrus granulosa

Pyrus granulosa, commonly known as the Japanese whitebeam, is a deciduous tree native to Japan.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and oval-shaped leaves. The tree produces small, greenish-white flowers in early spring, followed by edible, sweet fruits known as nashi or Chinese pears. Pyrus granulosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.
