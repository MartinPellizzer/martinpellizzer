Philadelphus californicus

Philadelphus californicus, commonly known as the California bride's blush, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It features fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. The plant has opposite, simple, and ovate leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. It thrives in well-drained soils and partial to full sun, making it suitable for gardens in coastal regions.

Its ornamental value and ecological benefits make it a popular choice for native plant landscaping.
