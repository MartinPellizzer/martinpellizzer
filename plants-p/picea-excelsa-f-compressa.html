Picea excelsa f. compressa

Picea excelsa f. compressa, commonly known as the dwarf white spruce, is a compact variant of the white spruce species.

It is characterized by its dense, pyramidal growth habit and short, stout needles that are a vibrant green. This form is typically found in alpine or subalpine regions where it thrives in cooler, moist environments. The plant has a slow growth rate and is well adapted to harsh mountain climates.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for alpine wildlife.
