Passiflora santos-llatasii

Passiflora santos-llatasii, commonly known as the Santos-Llata's passionflower, is a rare and striking member of the Passifloraceae family.

Native to the Andean regions of South America, it is characterized by its vivid, intricate flowers with five prominent sepals and a crown of filaments. The plant produces a unique, edible fruit that is often used in local cuisines. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil to flourish.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
