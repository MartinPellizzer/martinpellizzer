Pyrus macrostipes

Pyrus macrostipes, commonly known as the large-fruited pear.

It is a species of flowering plant in the family Rosaceae, native to the eastern Himalayas. The plant is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and alternate, ovate leaves with serrated edges. Its flowers are white to pale yellow, and it produces large, edible pears that are typically harvested in late summer.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
