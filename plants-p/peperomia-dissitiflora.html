Peperomia dissitiflora

Peperomia dissitiflora, commonly known as the silver peperomia, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It features thick, waxy, silver-green leaves that are often covered in fine, fuzzy hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers that appear in clusters along the stems. It thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Peperomia dissitiflora is relatively low maintenance, requiring minimal watering and occasional pruning to maintain its shape.
