Parathelypteris gracilescens

Parathelypteris gracilescens, commonly known as the slender shield fern, is a species of fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped fronds that are often covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its rhizomes are creeping and produce clusters of small, greenish-brown spore-bearing structures called sori.

The botanical features of P. gracilescens make it a distinctive and ecologically important member of the fern family.
