Phalaridantha robinsoniana

Phalaridantha robinsoniana, commonly known as the Robinson's phalaris, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in wetland habitats such as marshes and swamps, where it thrives in moist, acidic soils. The plant features slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters during the spring and summer months. Its flowering stems are often branched and can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters.

This species is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
