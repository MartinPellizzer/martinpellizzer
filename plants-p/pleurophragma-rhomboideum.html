Pleurophragma rhomboideum

Pleurophragma rhomboideum, commonly known as the rhomboid pleurophragma, is a member of the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and legume pods.

It is a shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of Africa, particularly in countries like Kenya and Tanzania. The plant features small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in racemes, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three ovate to lanceolate leaflets, and the plant produces elongated, woody pods that contain several seeds.

The species is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing drought-resistant traits in its morphology and physiology.
