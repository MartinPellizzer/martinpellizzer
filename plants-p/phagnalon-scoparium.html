Phagnalon scoparium

Phagnalon scoparium, commonly known as the common phagnalon, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is native to Europe, North Africa, and parts of Asia, often found in dry, calcareous grasslands and rocky habitats. The plant features erect, branched stems covered in fine hairs, and its leaves are linear to lanceolate, often with a silvery-gray appearance due to dense pubescence. Small, yellowish flowers are arranged in compact heads, typical of the Asteraceae family, and bloom in the summer months.

It is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally in herbal remedies.
