Pitcairnia lindleyana

Pitcairnia lindleyana, commonly known as the Lindley's pitcairnia, is a species of bromeliad native to South America.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that form a central cup to collect rainwater. The plant produces striking, inflorescence with vibrant flowers that bloom in shades of red, orange, and yellow. It thrives in shaded, humid environments and is often found in tropical forests.

Its unique morphology and ornamental value make it a popular choice for botanical gardens and collections.
