Pittierothamnus elineolatus

Pittierothamnus elineolatus, commonly known as the silverleaf buckthorn, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage and small, dark berries that persist through winter. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and along canyon walls. Its leaves are narrow and have a distinctive waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
