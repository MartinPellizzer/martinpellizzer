Passiflora picturata

Passiflora picturata, commonly known as the painted passionflower, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful flowers that display intricate patterns of red, yellow, and blue. The plant produces edible berries that are often used in jams and beverages. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a velvety texture.

Passiflora picturata is valued in horticulture for its ornamental beauty and ecological benefits to pollinators.
