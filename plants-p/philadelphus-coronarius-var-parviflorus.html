Philadelphus coronarius var. parviflorus

Philadelphus coronarius var. parviflorus, commonly known as the small-flowered mock orange, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, typically in clusters along the stems. The plant has simple, ovate leaves with a smooth texture and a slightly hairy underside. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained, fertile soil.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and wildlife habitat.
