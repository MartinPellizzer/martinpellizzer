Paspalum chrysodactylon

Paspalum chrysodactylon, commonly known as golden finger grass, is a perennial grass native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its erect, slender stems and narrow, blade-like leaves that are often tinged with golden hues. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences with spikelets that are typically yellow or golden in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open areas, grasslands, and disturbed sites.

Paspalum chrysodactylon is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping or as a ground cover.
