Poecilanthe parviflora var. floribunda

Poecilanthe parviflora var. floribunda, commonly known as the fragrant flame vine, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and clusters of small, bright orange to red tubular flowers. The plant produces small, elliptical leaves that are opposite and often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, making it a valuable addition to gardens.
