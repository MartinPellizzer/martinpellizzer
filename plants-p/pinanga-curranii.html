Pinanga curranii

Pinanga curranii, commonly known as the Curran's banyan, is a tropical plant belonging to the Araceae family.

It is native to the Philippines and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 1 meter in length. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in a spadix, typical of the Araceae family. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists alike.
