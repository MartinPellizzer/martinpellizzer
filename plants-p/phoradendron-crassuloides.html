Phoradendron crassuloides

Phoradendron crassuloides, commonly known as the creeping mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches itself to the branches of host trees, primarily in the genus *Palo Verde*.

It lacks roots and absorbs water and nutrients through its specialized structures called haustoria, which penetrate the host's vascular system. The plant produces small, green leaves and white, fragrant flowers, which are often visited by insects for pollination. Its berries, which are dark purple to black, contain seeds that are dispersed by birds.

This species is native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico, where it thrives in arid and semi-arid environments.
