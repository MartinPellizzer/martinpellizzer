Protea obliqua

Protea obliqua, commonly known as the twisted protea, is a striking South African plant belonging to the Proteaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescences that consist of a central cone-like structure surrounded by colorful bracts. The plant typically grows in coastal fynbos vegetation, where it thrives in well-drained, acidic soils. Its flowers are pollinated by birds, particularly the sugarbird, which is attracted to its nectar.

Protea obliqua is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
