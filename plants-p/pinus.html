Pinus

Pinus, commonly known as pine, is a genus of coniferous evergreen trees and shrubs.

These plants are characterized by their needle-like leaves and cone-bearing reproductive structures. They are native to the Northern Hemisphere and are found in a variety of habitats, including forests, mountains, and coastal areas. Pinus species typically have a long lifespan and can grow to great heights, often reaching over 50 meters.

Their wood is valued for its strength and durability, making it a significant resource in forestry and construction.
