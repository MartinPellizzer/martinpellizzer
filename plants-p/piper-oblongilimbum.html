Piper oblongilimbum

Piper oblongilimbum, commonly known as the long-leaf pepper or Malabar long pepper, is a climbing shrub native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species such as black pepper. The plant produces small, oval-shaped berries that are harvested when mature for their peppery flavor. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a waxy surface.

The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is cultivated for both its aromatic fruits and ornamental value.
