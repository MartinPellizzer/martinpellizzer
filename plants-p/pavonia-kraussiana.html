Pavonia kraussiana

Pavonia kraussiana, commonly known as the Cape rose or red sandalwood, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring woody stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, which bloom from late winter to early spring. Its flowers are often visited by pollinators such as bees and birds, contributing to its ecological significance.

The species is known for its ornamental value and is cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms and hardy nature.
