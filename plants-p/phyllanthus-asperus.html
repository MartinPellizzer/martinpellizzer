Phyllanthus asperus

Phyllanthus asperus, commonly known as the rough phyllanthus, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its small, clustered leaves and yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain several seeds. It is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to withstand arid conditions.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and traditional medicine.
