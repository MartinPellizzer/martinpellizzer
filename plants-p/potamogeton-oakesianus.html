Potamogeton oakesianus

Potamogeton oakesianus, commonly known as Oakes' pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to North America.

It typically grows in freshwater environments such as lakes, rivers, and ponds, often forming dense underwater vegetation. The plant has long, narrow leaves that are usually arranged in whorls around the stem, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are pollinated by water. Its roots help stabilize sediment and improve water quality by reducing erosion.

Oakes' pondweed plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
