Pimenta citrifolia

Pimenta citrifolia, commonly known as graviola, is a tropical tree native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves and small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in clusters. The fruit, which is typically oblong and has a rough, green exterior, contains a single large seed and a sweet, aromatic pulp. The plant is also known for its ornamental value and its use in traditional medicine.

Its botanical features make it well-suited for cultivation in warm, humid climates.
