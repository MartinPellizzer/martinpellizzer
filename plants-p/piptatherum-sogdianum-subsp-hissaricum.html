Piptatherum sogdianum subsp. hissaricum

Piptatherum sogdianum subsp.

hissaricum, commonly known as the Central Asian needlegrass, is a perennial grass species native to Central Asia. It typically grows in arid and semi-arid regions, often found in steppes and grasslands.

The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It is characterized by its hardy nature and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.

This subspecies plays an important role in stabilizing soil and supporting local wildlife.
