Pimelea linifolia subsp. linoides

Pimelea linifolia subsp.

linoides, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bottlebrush, is a shrub native to southeastern Australia. It is characterized by its dense, cylindrical flower spikes that range in color from red to yellow, which appear in spring.

The leaves are narrow, linear, and covered in fine hairs, giving the plant a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal areas.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and ability to attract native pollinators.
