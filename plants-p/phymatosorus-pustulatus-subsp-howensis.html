Phymatosorus pustulatus subsp. howensis

Phymatosorus pustulatus subsp.

howensis, commonly known as the Howes' rock fern, is a fern native to Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its compact, upright growth habit and dark green, leathery fronds.

The fronds are pinnately compound, with serrated margins and a distinctive, rounded shape. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found on rocky or forested slopes.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in shady garden settings.
