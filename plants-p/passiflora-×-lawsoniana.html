Passiflora × lawsoniana

Passiflora × lawsoniana, commonly known as the purple passionflower, is a hybrid species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking violet to purple flowers, which feature intricate structures such as the crown and filaments. The plant is a vigorous climber with heart-shaped, dark green leaves that have a velvety texture. Its fruit, a small, edible berry, is typically green when unripe and turns yellow or orange when mature.

This species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated as a decorative vine.
