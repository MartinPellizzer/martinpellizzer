Pentarhopalopilia umbellulata

Pentarhopalopilia umbellulata, commonly known as the umbrella bellflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its daisy-like inflorescence. The plant features showy, yellow-orange ray florets surrounding a central disk of smaller, tubular flowers. It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
