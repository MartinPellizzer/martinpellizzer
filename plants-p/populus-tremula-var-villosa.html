Populus tremula var. villosa

Populus tremula var. villosa, commonly known as the downy birch, is a variant of the European white birch.

It is characterized by its hairy, or pubescent, stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive appearance. The leaves are typically oval-shaped with a serrated margin and a pale underside. This species is native to temperate regions of Europe and parts of Asia.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and river valleys.
