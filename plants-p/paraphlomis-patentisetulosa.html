Paraphlomis patentisetulosa

Paraphlomis patentisetulosa, commonly known as the broad-leaved blueflag, is a perennial herb native to southern China.

It belongs to the family Lamiaceae and is characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces clusters of blue to purple flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded areas.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden landscapes and wetland restoration projects.
