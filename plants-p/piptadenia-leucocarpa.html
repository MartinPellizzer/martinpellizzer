Piptadenia leucocarpa

Piptadenia leucocarpa, commonly known as white wood or Brazilian white wood, is a leguminous tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, light-colored bark and large, palmately lobed leaves. The plant produces showy yellow flowers followed by long, cylindrical pods that contain flat, winged seeds. It is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, growing in a variety of soil types.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated for horticultural purposes.
