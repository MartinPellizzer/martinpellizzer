Pleurothallis recurvata

Pleurothallis recurvata, commonly known as the recurved pleurothallis, is a species of orchid native to the cloud forests of Central and South America.

It is a small, epiphytic orchid that typically grows on trees or rocks in humid, shaded environments. The plant features delicate, spiraling inflorescences with small, fragrant flowers that are usually white or pale green. Its roots are adapted for absorbing moisture and nutrients from the air and surrounding substrate.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is often sought after by orchid enthusiasts and conservationists.
