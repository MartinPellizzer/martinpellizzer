Potentilla alchimilloides var. iminouakensis

Potentilla alchimilloides var. iminouakensis, commonly known as the Alaskan potentilla, is a flowering plant native to northern North America.

It is a low-growing shrub that typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This variety is adapted to harsh, alpine environments, thriving in well-drained, rocky soils.
