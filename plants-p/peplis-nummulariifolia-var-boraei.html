Peplis nummulariifolia var. boraei

Peplis nummulariifolia var. boraei, commonly known as the Arctic sandwort, is a low-growing, mat-forming plant native to arctic and alpine regions.

It features small, rounded, silver-green leaves that are adapted to withstand harsh, cold climates. The plant produces tiny, white or pale yellow flowers during the short growing season. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in tundra and mountainous environments.

Its botanical characteristics make it well-suited for survival in extreme northern climates.
