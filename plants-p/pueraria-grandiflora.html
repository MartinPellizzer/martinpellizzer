Pueraria grandiflora

Pueraria grandiflora, commonly known as the giant pueraria, is a climbing legume native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and showy white or purple flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is known for its vigorous growth and ability to form dense foliage, often reaching heights of several meters. Its roots contain compounds that have been studied for potential medicinal uses.

Pueraria grandiflora thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas or along riverbanks.
