Plantago gigas

Plantago gigas, commonly known as the giant plantain, is a flowering plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in a dense layer of fine, silky hairs. The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in a spike, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and wetlands.

The plant is valued for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally for its healing benefits.
