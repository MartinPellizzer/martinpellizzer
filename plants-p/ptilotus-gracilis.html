Ptilotus gracilis

Ptilotus gracilis, commonly known as the slender spinifex, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its compact, low-growing habit. The plant produces small, showy flowers that range in color from yellow to red, often appearing in dense clusters. It thrives in arid and sandy soils, making it well adapted to harsh environmental conditions.

Ptilotus gracilis is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its resilience and ability to stabilize soil.
