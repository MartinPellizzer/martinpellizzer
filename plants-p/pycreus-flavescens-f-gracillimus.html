Pycreus flavescens f. gracillimus

Pycreus flavescens f. gracillimus, commonly known as the slender yellow gentian, is a rare and striking member of the Gentianaceae family.

It is characterized by its delicate, blue to violet-blue flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a distinctive silver-white underside.

This subspecies is notable for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in botanical gardens for its beauty and rarity.
