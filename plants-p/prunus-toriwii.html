Prunus toriwii

Prunus toriwii, commonly known as the Japanese cherry or Japanese yuzu, is a small deciduous tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its ornamental white or pink flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by small, yellowish fruits. The plant belongs to the Rosaceae family and is closely related to other cherry species. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a serrated margin.

Prunus toriwii is often cultivated for its aesthetic value in gardens and landscapes.
