Phyllostegia odorata

Phyllostegia odorata, commonly known as the sweetleaf or sweet pepper bush, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves that are often fragrant when crushed. Its showy, tubular flowers are typically pink or purple and bloom in late spring to early summer.

Phyllostegia odorata is often found in moist, wooded areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
