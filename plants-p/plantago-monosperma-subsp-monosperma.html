Plantago monosperma subsp. monosperma

Plantago monosperma subsp.

monosperma, commonly known as the single-seeded plantain, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa. It typically grows in grasslands and rocky areas, preferring well-drained soils.

The plant has a rosette of broad, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are arranged in a dense spike, with each flower producing a single, elongated seed.

This subspecies is valued for its hardiness and is often used in traditional medicine.
