Phegopteris gymnogrammoides

Phegopteris gymnogrammoides, commonly known as the broad-lobed water fern, is a species of aquatic fern native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its large, floating fronds that are divided into broad, lobed segments, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, such as ponds and marshes. Its fronds are typically green and may develop a reddish tinge when exposed to full sun.

The reproductive structures, including sori, are located on the underside of the fronds, typical of many fern species.
