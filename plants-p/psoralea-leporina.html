Psoralea leporina

Psoralea leporina, commonly known as the hare's-foot psoralea, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a low-growing annual herb with small, yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has hairy, branched stems and produces small, bean-like pods that contain seeds. Its leaves are pinnately divided, giving it a delicate, fern-like appearance.

Psoralea leporina thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats.
