Phaseolus coccineus var. minuticicatricatus

Phaseolus coccineus var. minuticicatricatus, commonly known as the scarlet runner bean with small scars, is a variant of the scarlet runner bean.

It is characterized by its distinctive red pods and small, faint scars on the pod surface, which give it its specific name. The plant is a climbing legume that produces showy, butterfly-shaped flowers. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is often cultivated for both its ornamental value and edible beans.
