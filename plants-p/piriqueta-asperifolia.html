Piriqueta asperifolia

Piriqueta asperifolia, commonly known as the roughleaf piriqueta, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its hairy, rough-textured leaves and small, clustered yellow flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or coastal areas. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for study in plant morphology and ecology.
