Polystachya coelogynochila

Polystachya coelogynochila, commonly known as the golden spike orchid, is a species of orchid native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often in forest undergrowth. Its leaves are elongated and grass-like, and it produces numerous spikes of flowers that bloom during the wet season.

Polystachya coelogynochila is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
