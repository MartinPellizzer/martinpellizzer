Poilanedora

Poilanedora, commonly known as the velvet leaf.

It is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia, characterized by its thick, velvety leaves that give it its common name. The plant features a climbing habit and produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and yellow. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

Poilanedora thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
