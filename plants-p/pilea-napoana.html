Pilea napoana

Pilea napoana, commonly known as the Chinese money plant, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It features glossy, heart-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern, giving it a unique and attractive appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by the development of round, coin-like structures called "money bags.". It thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Pilea napoana is known for its ease of care and ability to purify the air, contributing to its popularity among plant enthusiasts.
