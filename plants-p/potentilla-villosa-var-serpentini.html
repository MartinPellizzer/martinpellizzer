Potentilla villosa var. serpentini

Potentilla villosa var. serpentini, commonly known as the Serpent's Tail, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its silver-gray, fuzzy foliage and clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine settings. Its unique, trailing growth habit and ornamental flowers make it a visually striking addition to gardens.

It is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
