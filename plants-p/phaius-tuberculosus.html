Phaius tuberculosus

Phaius tuberculosus, commonly known as the tubercled orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its robust rhizomes and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense, waxy coating. The plant produces striking, showy flowers in shades of yellow and orange, with a distinctive tuberculate (knob-like) pattern on the petals. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts for its unique appearance.
