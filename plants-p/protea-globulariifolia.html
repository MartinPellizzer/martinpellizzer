Protea globulariifolia

Protea globulariifolia, commonly known as the globular sugarbush, is a South African plant belonging to the Proteaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, spherical clusters of small, white to pink flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has leathery, elliptical leaves that are typically covered in a fine, white powdery coating. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in fynbos vegetation.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological importance, it is a popular ornamental plant in horticulture.
