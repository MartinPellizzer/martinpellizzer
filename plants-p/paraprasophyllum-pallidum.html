Paraprasophyllum pallidum

Paraprasophyllum pallidum, commonly known as the pale paraprasophyllum, is a rare and specialized plant species native to specific regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, mat-forming growth habit and features small, pale green to white leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of hair. The plant produces tiny, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-white in color, which bloom during the cooler months. Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered endangered and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.
