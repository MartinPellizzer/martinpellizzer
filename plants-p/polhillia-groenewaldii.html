Polhillia groenewaldii

Polhillia groenewaldii, commonly known as the Cape tulip, is a striking South African plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, often with a distinctive red or orange center. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and is native to the Western and Northern Cape provinces. It produces a single, tall flowering stem with several bracts that resemble petals, and its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves.

Polhillia groenewaldii is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and is well-suited to Mediterranean climates.
