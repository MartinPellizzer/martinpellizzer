Polypodium simulans

Polypodium simulans, commonly known as the common polypody, is a fern native to various regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen fronds that form a compact, mat-like growth. The plant typically grows on rocky surfaces or in shaded, moist environments, showcasing its adaptability to humid conditions. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine, highlighting its botanical and cultural significance.

The fronds are divided into numerous small, serrated segments, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
