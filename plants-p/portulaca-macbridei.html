Portulaca macbridei

Portulaca macbridei, commonly known as the MacBride's morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the family Portulacaceae and is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves and vibrant, showy flowers that open in the morning. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun, making it well adapted to arid environments. Its flowers are usually pink or red, and they attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Portulaca macbridei is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to bloom throughout the growing season.
