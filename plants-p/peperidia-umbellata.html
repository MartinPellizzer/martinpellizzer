Peperidia umbellata

Peperidia umbellata, commonly known as the umbrella bellflower, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, bell-shaped flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences that resemble an umbrella, giving it its common name. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Peperidia umbellata is often found in wetlands and along stream banks, where it contributes to the local biodiversity.
