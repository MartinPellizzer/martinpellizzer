Piper rugosibaccum

Piper rugosibaccum, commonly known as the rough-stemmed pepper plant, is a tropical species native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper plants such as black pepper. The plant is characterized by its woody stems covered in rough, ridged bark and compound leaves with serrated edges. Its small, greenish-white flowers are clustered in spikes, and it produces small, peppery berries that are used in traditional medicine.

The botanical features of Piper rugosibaccum make it an interesting subject for study in tropical botany and ethnobotany.
