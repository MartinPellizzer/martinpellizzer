Primula hirsuta f. pallida

Primula hirsuta f. pallida, commonly known as the pale primrose, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It features hairy, upright stems that bear clusters of small, pale yellow flowers with a distinctive greenish hue. The leaves are typically oval-shaped and covered in fine, soft hairs, contributing to its fuzzy appearance. This variety is prized for its delicate floral display and is often cultivated in gardens for ornamental purposes.

It prefers cool, moist environments and is relatively easy to grow in well-drained soil.
