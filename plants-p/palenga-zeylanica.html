Palenga zeylanica

Palenga zeylanica, commonly known as the Ceylon sandalwood, is a small evergreen tree native to Sri Lanka.

It is characterized by its aromatic, fragrant wood that is highly valued in traditional medicine and perfumery. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a smooth, waxy surface. It produces small, white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by dark blue-black berries.

The tree is often cultivated for its essential oils and is also used in religious and cultural practices.
