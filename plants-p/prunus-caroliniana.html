Prunus caroliniana

Prunus caroliniana, commonly known as the Carolina cherrybark oak, is a deciduous tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark that often has a flaky or scaly texture. The tree produces small, red or yellowish flowers in the spring, followed by edible, sweet-tart drupes. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have serrated edges.

The plant is adapted to a variety of soil types and is often found in mixed hardwood forests.
