Phyllostachys pierreana

Phyllostachys pierreana, commonly known as the black bamboo, is a species of grass in the bamboo family, Poaceae.

It is native to China and is characterized by its dark, almost black stems that are covered in a thick layer of hair. The plant typically grows to a height of 6 to 10 meters and forms clumps with slender, green culms. Its leaves are long, narrow, and often have a bluish-green hue.

This bamboo is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance and resilience.
