Piper subandinum

Piper subandinum, commonly known as the Andean pepper or wild pepper, is a climbing shrub native to the high altitudes of the Andes in South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many species of pepper plants. The plant produces small, green berries that turn red when mature, containing aromatic seeds used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are often used in local remedies for their purported medicinal properties.

The plant thrives in cool, humid climates and is adapted to rocky, mountainous terrain.
