Picris angustissima

Picris angustissima, commonly known as narrowleaf groundsel, is a herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in disturbed soils and along roadsides, reaching heights of up to 60 centimeters. The plant features narrow, linear leaves and produces yellow flowers in clusters at the end of its stems. It is characterized by its small, dandelion-like blooms and is often found in open, sunny habitats.

The species is also known for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal remedies.
