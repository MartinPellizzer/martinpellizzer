Pelargonium hypotrachys

Pelargonium hypotrachys, commonly known as the narrow-leaf geranium, is a species of flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its narrow, serrated leaves and vibrant clusters of small, showy flowers that range in color from pink to red. The plant typically grows in a compact shrub form, reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Pelargonium hypotrachys is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ease of cultivation.
