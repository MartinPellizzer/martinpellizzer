Paspalum virgatum var. platyaxis

Paspalum virgatum var. platyaxis, commonly known as the broad-axis paspalum, is a perennial grass native to South America.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that are typically green or brown. The plant is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit, allowing it to spread and form dense mats. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along water bodies.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in aquatic environments.
