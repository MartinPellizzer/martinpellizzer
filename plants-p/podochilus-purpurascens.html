Podochilus purpurascens

Podochilus purpurascens, commonly known as the purple-flowered orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a flowering plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family, characterized by its striking violet or purple petals and a unique floral structure. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its flowers are often fragrant and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Podochilus purpurascens is valued both for its ornamental beauty and its role in local ecosystems.
