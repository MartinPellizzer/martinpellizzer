Polygala heliostigma

Polygala heliostigma, commonly known as the sun mimic or golden bell, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Polygalaceae family and is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun, making it a hardy species well adapted to arid environments. Its flowers are often visited by bees and other pollinators, contributing to its ecological significance.
