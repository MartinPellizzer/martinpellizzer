Potentilla × inaperta

Potentilla × inaperta, commonly known as the hybrid cinquefoil, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces clusters of small, bright yellow flowers in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm, with hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often reddish beneath. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens and alpine gardens.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is widely cultivated in horticulture.
