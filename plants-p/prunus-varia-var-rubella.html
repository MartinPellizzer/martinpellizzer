Prunus varia var. rubella

Prunus varia var. rubella, commonly known as the red-leaf cherry, is a variant of the Prunus varia species, characterized by its striking red foliage.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to East Asia, particularly China. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in spring, followed by edible berries that ripen in summer. Its leaves turn vibrant shades of red and orange in autumn, making it a popular ornamental plant.

The species is valued for its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to various soil conditions.
