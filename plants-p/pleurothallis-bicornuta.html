Pleurothallis bicornuta

Pleurothallis bicornuta, commonly known as the two-horned pleurothallis, is a species of orchid native to the cloud forests of Central and South America.

It is a small, epiphytic plant that typically grows on trees in humid, shaded environments. The plant features delicate, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink. Its flowers are often clustered in inflorescences and are known for their intricate, intricate structure.

Pleurothallis bicornuta is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
