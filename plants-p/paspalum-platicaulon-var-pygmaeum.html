Paspalum platicaulon var. pygmaeum

Paspalum platicaulon var. pygmaeum, commonly known as the dwarf flat-stemmed grass, is a low-growing perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features narrow, flat leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant is adapted to well-drained soils and can thrive in full sun to partial shade. Its compact growth habit makes it suitable for ornamental use in gardens and landscapes.

It is also valued for its ability to stabilize soil and prevent erosion in marginal areas.
