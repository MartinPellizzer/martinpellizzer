Phalaris arechavaletae

Phalaris arechavaletae, commonly known as the Arechavaleta grass, is a species of flowering plant in the grass family Poaceae.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
