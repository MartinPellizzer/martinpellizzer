Peltogyne angustiflora

Peltogyne angustiflora, commonly known as the narrow-leaved ironwood, is a tropical tree native to regions of South America, particularly Brazil.

It is characterized by its dense, hard wood and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in racemes. Its fruit is a legume pod that contains several seeds, which are often dispersed by animals.

The tree is valued for its durable timber and plays an important ecological role in its native habitats.
