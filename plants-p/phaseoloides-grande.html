Phaseoloides grande

Phaseoloides grande, commonly known as the large-phaseoloid, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing ability. The plant features trifoliate leaves with palmately arranged leaflets and produces showy, butterfly-shaped flowers in shades of white and purple. Its fruit is a legume pod that contains several edible seeds.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in warm climates for garden use.
