Paullinia pubescens

Paullinia pubescens, commonly known as the velvet tamarind, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the Sapindaceae family and is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by edible, tart fruits resembling small, round oranges. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is valued for both its ornamental and medicinal properties.
