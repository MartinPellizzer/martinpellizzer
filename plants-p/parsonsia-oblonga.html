Parsonsia oblonga

Parsonsia oblonga, commonly known as the broadleaf nightshade, is a tropical shrub native to regions of Southeast Asia.

It features showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically yellow or orange with a red throat, blooming at night. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are usually ovate to elliptic in shape. Its fruit is a fleshy, red berry that contains several seeds.

Parsonsia oblonga is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has some traditional medicinal uses.
