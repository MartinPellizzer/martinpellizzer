Piper monteluctans

Piper monteluctans, commonly known as the Luctan pepper, is a climbing vine native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many species of pepper plants. The plant produces small, elongated fruits that are typically green when unripe and turn red when mature. These fruits are often used in local cuisines for their spicy flavor.

Botanically, it is characterized by its opposite leaves and distinctive, aromatic flowers.
