Pleurothallis carnosilabia

Pleurothallis carnosilabia, commonly known as the pink-lip orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its unique floral structure. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are notable for their pinkish-lavender petals and a distinctive, fleshy lip that gives it its common name.

This species is appreciated for its ornamental value and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
