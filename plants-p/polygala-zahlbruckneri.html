Polygala zahlbruckneri

Polygala zahlbruckneri, commonly known as the Zahler's milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the Polygalaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to drought conditions. Its flowers are often pink or purple and are visited by various pollinators, including bees and butterflies.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
