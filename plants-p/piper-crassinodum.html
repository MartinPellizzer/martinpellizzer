Piper crassinodum

Piper crassinodum, commonly known as the broad-node pepper vine, is a climbing shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes various species of pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and small, greenish-white flowers that develop into elongated, fleshy berries. Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for botanical studies and horticultural cultivation.

The plant's structure and growth habits are adapted to its natural habitat in humid, shaded environments.
