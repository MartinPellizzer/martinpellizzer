Pelargonium lappaceum

Pelargonium lappaceum, commonly known as the woolly geranium, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, fuzzy leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, pink or red flowers that bloom in summer. It is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its unique foliage and showy blooms.

Pelargonium lappaceum prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.
