Panicum lorentzianum

Panicum lorentzianum, commonly known as Lorentz's panicgrass, is a species of grass native to parts of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, pale green to golden florets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
