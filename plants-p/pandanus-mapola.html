Pandanus mapola

Pandanus mapola, commonly known as the Solomon Islands pandanus, is a tropical plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and long, narrow leaves that form a dense canopy. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. Its fibrous leaves are used by local communities for weaving baskets and thatch.

Pandanus mapola thrives in coastal areas and is adapted to salt spray and sandy soils.
