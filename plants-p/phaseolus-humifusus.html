Phaseolus humifusus

Phaseolus humifusus, commonly known as the scarlet runner bean, is a climbing legume native to the Americas.

It produces vibrant red or orange flowers and edible beans that are often used in culinary applications. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets and can grow up to several meters in length. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both agricultural and ecological contexts.
