Platystele microscopica

Platystele microscopica, commonly known as the minute leafless orchid, is a unique member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is a small, epiphytic plant that lacks leaves and has a specialized structure called a pseudobulb for storing nutrients. The plant produces minute, white flowers that are often hidden within its dense growth habit. It is native to the cloud forests of Central and South America, where it thrives in humid, shaded environments.

Due to its delicate nature and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
