Petunia integrifolia

Petunia integrifolia, commonly known as the violet petunia, is a species native to South America.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and vibrant, showy flowers that come in a variety of colors, including purple, blue, and white. The plant has simple, entire leaves that are typically arranged oppositely along the stems. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Petunia integrifolia is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
