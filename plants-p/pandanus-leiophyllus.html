Pandanus leiophyllus

Pandanus leiophyllus, commonly known as the smooth-leaf pandanus, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its large, narrow, and smooth-textured leaves that grow in a rosette formation. The plant produces yellow or orange inflorescences that contain small, fragrant flowers. Its roots and leaves are often used in traditional crafts and for medicinal purposes.

It thrives in humid, coastal environments and is well adapted to saline soils.
