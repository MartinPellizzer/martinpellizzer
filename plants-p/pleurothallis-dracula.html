Pleurothallis dracula

Pleurothallis dracula, commonly known as the vampire orchid, is a species of orchid native to the cloud forests of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its intricate, often dark-colored flowers that resemble a bat's face. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees and deriving moisture and nutrients from the air and rain. Its flowers are usually small and fragrant, blooming in clusters during the wet season.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is a subject of interest for botanists and orchid enthusiasts alike.
