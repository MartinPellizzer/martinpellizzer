Prunus eburnea

Prunus eburnea, commonly known as the white-flowered cherry or the white cherry, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It is characterized by its slender, elegant form and its showy white blossoms that appear in early spring before the leaves emerge. The tree has smooth, light grey bark and oval-shaped leaves that turn yellow in autumn. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and parks.

Prunus eburnea is relatively easy to grow and thrives in full sun to partial shade.
