Podocarpus macrophyllus

Podocarpus macrophyllus, commonly known as the Chinese podocarp or umbrella tree, is a evergreen coniferous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Podocarpaceae and is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and distinctive umbrella-like shape. The plant has scale-like leaves arranged in flat, spreading sprays, and produces small, cone-like structures that contain seeds. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in landscaping due to its adaptability and resilience.

The species is also valued in traditional medicine and for its durable wood.
