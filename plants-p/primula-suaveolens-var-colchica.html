Primula suaveolens var. colchica

Primula suaveolens var. colchica, commonly known as the Caucasian primrose, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus.

It features upright, hairy stems that bear clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and yellow. The leaves are typically basal, heart-shaped, and covered in fine pubescence. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in woodlands and meadows.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
