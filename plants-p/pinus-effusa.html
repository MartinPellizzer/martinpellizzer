Pinus effusa

Pinus effusa, commonly known as the Mexican white pine, is a coniferous tree native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, needle-like leaves that are typically 5 to 8 centimeters long. The tree produces cones that are often reddish-brown in color and contain edible seeds. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions.

Pinus effusa plays an important role in its native ecosystems, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
