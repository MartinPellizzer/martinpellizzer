Pinus kwangtungensis

Pinus kwangtungensis, commonly known as the Guangdong pine, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to southern China.

It is characterized by its slender, pyramidal shape and needle-like leaves that are typically 2 to 4 centimeters long. The tree produces cones that are approximately 5 to 7 centimeters in length, containing edible seeds that are a food source for various wildlife. It thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil and is often found in forested areas.

The plant is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been used in horticulture for its hardy nature and adaptability.
