Parnassia foliosa subsp. nummularia

Parnassia foliosa subsp.

nummularia, commonly known as the silverweed, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the northern hemisphere. It is characterized by its rosette of glossy, silver-green leaves that are typically round or kidney-shaped.

The plant produces small, white, bell-shaped flowers that are clustered in a terminal inflorescence. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its foliage is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to suppress weeds.
