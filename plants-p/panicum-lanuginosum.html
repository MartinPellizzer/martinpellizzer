Panicum lanuginosum

Panicum lanuginosum, commonly known as silky panicgrass, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a grass species characterized by its soft, silky hairs that cover its stems and leaves, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny florets. It typically grows in moist to wet soils in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems and a subject of study in ecological and botanical research.
