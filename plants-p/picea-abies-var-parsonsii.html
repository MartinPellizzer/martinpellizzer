Picea abies var. parsonsii

Picea abies var. parsonsii, commonly known as the Parson's fir, is a variant of the Norway spruce.

It is characterized by its slender, conical shape and dense foliage. The leaves are typically shorter and more closely packed compared to the standard Norway spruce. It is native to the mountainous regions of central Europe.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value in horticulture.
