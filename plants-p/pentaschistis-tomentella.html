Pentaschistis tomentella

Pentaschistis tomentella, commonly known as the woolly pentaschistis, is a flowering plant native to the Andes region of South America.

It is characterized by its small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink in color. The plant has hairy, glandular leaves that give it a distinctive, fuzzy appearance. It thrives in high-altitude environments and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats.

Due to its unique morphology and limited distribution, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
