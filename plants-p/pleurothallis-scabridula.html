Pleurothallis scabridula

Pleurothallis scabridula, commonly known as the rough-leaved orchid, is a species native to the cloud forests of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact, epiphytic growth habit. The plant features small, lanceolate leaves that are covered in a rough, scabrous texture, giving it its common name. Its flowers are typically small and clustered, often in shades of white or pale pink, with a delicate, fragrant aroma.

Due to its unique morphology and habitat, it is a sought-after species among orchid enthusiasts and botanists.
