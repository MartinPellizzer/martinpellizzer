Portulaca aurantiaca

Portulaca aurantiaca, commonly known as the moss rose, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is a succulent species with fleshy, ribbed leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. The plant produces vibrant, showy flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, typically blooming in summer. It prefers full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Its ability to survive drought conditions makes it a resilient and low-maintenance ornamental plant.
