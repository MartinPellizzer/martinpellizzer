Picris angustifolia subsp. morrisonensis

Picris angustifolia subsp.

morrisonensis, commonly known as Morrison's narrowleaf wormwood, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa. It typically grows in arid and semi-arid regions, often in rocky or sandy soils.

The plant features slender, branching stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a grayish-green hue. Its small yellow flowers are arranged in dense, terminal clusters, and it is known for its aromatic, sometimes bitter, foliage.

This subspecies is part of the Asteraceae family and is valued for its ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
