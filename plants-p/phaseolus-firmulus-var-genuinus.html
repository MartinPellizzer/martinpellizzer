Phaseolus firmulus var. genuinus

Phaseolus firmulus var. genuinus, commonly known as the common scarlet runner bean, is a leguminous plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its distinctive red or orange flowers and elongated, edible pods. The plant typically grows as an annual vine, reaching lengths of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are trifoliate, with each leaflet being ovate and hairy.

The seeds, which are enclosed within the pods, are often used in culinary applications and can be dried for storage.
