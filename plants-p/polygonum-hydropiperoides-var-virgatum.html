Polygonum hydropiperoides var. virgatum

Polygonum hydropiperoides var. virgatum, commonly known as the Virgin's tears or water pepper, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, characterized by its distinctive nodes and often zigzagging stems. The plant features small, greenish-white flowers arranged in dense, spike-like clusters. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture.

It thrives in moist environments such as wetlands and stream banks, making it a common wetland indicator species.
