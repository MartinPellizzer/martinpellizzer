Passiflora bicuspidata

Passiflora bicuspidata, commonly known as the two-lobed passionflower, is a climbing vine native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, complex flowers that feature a crown of white filaments and a central cluster of red and blue sepals. The plant produces edible berries that are often consumed by wildlife and humans alike. Its leaves are heart-shaped with a rough texture and are often used in traditional medicine.

Passiflora bicuspidata thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, requiring ample sunlight and well-drained soil.
