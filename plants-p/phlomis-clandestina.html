Phlomis clandestina

Phlomis clandestina, commonly known as the hidden phlomis, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the family Lamiaceae, which includes many aromatic plants such as mint and sage. The plant is characterized by its upright growth habit, gray-green foliage, and clusters of small, showy flowers that range in color from pink to purple. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in some areas.
