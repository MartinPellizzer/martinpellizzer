Psydrax attenuatus var. myrmecophilus

Psydrax attenuatus var. myrmecophilus, commonly known as the ant-loving strobilanthes, is a unique species within the Acanthaceae family.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular flowers that range in color from red to purple, attracting specific pollinators. The plant is native to Southeast Asia, particularly Malaysia and Indonesia, where it thrives in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a velvety texture, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

This variant is notable for its symbiotic relationship with certain ant species, which provide protection in exchange for shelter and food.
