Pouteria campechiana var. palmeri

Pouteria campechiana var. palmeri, commonly known as the Mexican mahogany, is a species native to Central America and northern South America.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree that can grow up to 20 meters tall, with a dense, symmetrical crown. The leaves are alternate, simple, and elliptic, with a glossy dark green upper surface and a lighter underside. Its flowers are small, white, and fragrant, typically appearing in clusters, while the fruit is a woody, spiny capsule that contains several seeds.

This plant is valued for its durable wood and is also used in traditional medicine.
