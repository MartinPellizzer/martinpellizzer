Pontederas

Pontederas, commonly known as pickerelweed, are aquatic plants native to the Americas.

They are characterized by their tall, erect stems and showy, spathaceous flowers that bloom in shades of purple or pink. The leaves are broad and heart-shaped, often floating on the water's surface or submerged in shallow waters. These plants play a vital role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.

They are also popular in ornamental gardening due to their striking appearance and ability to thrive in water gardens.
