Papaver cruciatum

Papaver cruciatum, commonly known as the cross-shaped poppy, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking, four-petaled flowers that resemble a cross, hence its common name. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sunlight. Its petals are usually a vibrant red or orange, and it produces a capsule fruit containing numerous seeds.

Papaver cruciatum is often found in dry, open habitats and is valued for its ornamental beauty.
