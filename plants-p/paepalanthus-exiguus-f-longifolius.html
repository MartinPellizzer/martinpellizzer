Paepalanthus exiguus f. longifolius

Paepalanthus exiguus f. longifolius, commonly known as the long-leaved peacock fern, is a fern species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, feathery fronds that are longer and more slender than those of the typical variety. The plant produces spore-bearing structures called sori, which are typically located on the underside of the fronds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained, acidic soil.

This fern is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its elegant, flowing appearance.
