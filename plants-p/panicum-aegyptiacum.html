Panicum aegyptiacum

Panicum aegyptiacum, commonly known as Egyptian millet, is a flowering plant native to Africa.

It is a member of the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its upright, cylindrical inflorescence. The plant produces small, spikelet-like flowers that are typically brown or reddish in color. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in disturbed areas or along roadsides.

Due to its hardiness and adaptability, it is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
