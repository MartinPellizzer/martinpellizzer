Phalaenopsis amabilis f. alba

Phalaenopsis amabilis f. alba, commonly known as the white phalaenopsis orchid, is a hybrid orchid species characterized by its striking white flowers.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia, including parts of Indonesia and the Philippines. The plant is known for its long-lasting blooms, which can persist for several months, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening. Its foliage consists of broad, dark green leaves that grow in a rosette pattern, and it thrives in bright, indirect light with moderate humidity.

This variety is particularly valued for its elegant appearance and ease of care.
