Polypodium cambricum subsp. australe

Polypodium cambricum subsp.

australe, commonly known as the Australian wall fern, is a fern species native to southeastern Australia. It is characterized by its creeping rhizome and the production of fertile fronds with sporangia on the underside.

The fronds are typically lanceolate or linear, with a smooth, glossy surface. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as rock crevices and forest floors.

This subspecies is notable for its adaptability to various soil types and its ability to form dense mats.
