Pleurothallis scaphipetala

Pleurothallis scaphipetala, commonly known as the "spoon-leaf orchid," is a species native to the cloud forests of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its elongated, strap-like leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, clustered in racemes. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained, acidic soil for optimal growth.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is a sought-after species among orchid enthusiasts.
