Polygonum rumicifolium

Polygonum rumicifolium, commonly known as red root or redroot pigweed, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a member of the Polygonaceae family, characterized by its distinctive arrow-shaped leaves and clusters of small, pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and fields, and is often found in moist or wet environments. It has a deep taproot system that allows it to thrive in various soil conditions.

The plant is sometimes used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
