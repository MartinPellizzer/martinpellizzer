Psidium munizianum

Psidium munizianum, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or granadilla, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, edible berries that are typically red or yellow when ripe, with a sweet and tangy flavor. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit production.

The plant's botanical features make it well-suited for both landscaping and agricultural purposes in suitable environments.
