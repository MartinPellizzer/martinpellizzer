Pelargonium × aquilinum

Pelargonium × aquilinum, commonly known as the African marigold, is a hybrid geranium species native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that range in color from yellow to orange and red, often with a distinctive central boss. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub with lobed, hairy leaves that are aromatic when crushed. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for garden borders and containers.

Pelargonium × aquilinum is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
