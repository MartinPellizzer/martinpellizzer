Pleiostachya pittieri

Pleiostachya pittieri, commonly known as the Pittier's panicgrass, is a species of grass native to the Caribbean, particularly found in Jamaica and other nearby islands.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like foliage and small, clustered inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. Its botanical features include narrow leaves and a flowering structure that consists of spikelets arranged in panicles.

Pleiostachya pittieri is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and ecological adaptations.
