Papaver kuvajevii

Papaver kuvajevii, commonly known as the Kuvajevi poppy, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, vividly colored flowers that range from red to orange, often with a distinctive black or dark-colored center. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in temperate to cold climates. Its leaves are usually deeply lobed and covered in a fine layer of hairs, while its stems are sturdy and often branched.

This species is valued not only for its ornamental beauty but also for its potential medicinal properties.
