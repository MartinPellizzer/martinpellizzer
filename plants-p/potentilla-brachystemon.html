Potentilla brachystemon

Potentilla brachystemon, commonly known as the narrowleaf cinquefoil, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has compound leaves with five to seven leaflets, which are typically narrow and lance-shaped. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens and alpine habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens in temperate climates.
