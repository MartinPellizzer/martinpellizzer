Potentilla fragiformis var. typica

Potentilla fragiformis var. typica, commonly known as the silverweed, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It features a mat-forming growth habit with numerous flowering stems that bear small, yellow, five-petaled flowers. The leaves are typically silver-white on the upper surface and dark green underneath, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine environments.

The plant is adapted to harsh conditions and plays a role in stabilizing soil in rocky and arid habitats.
