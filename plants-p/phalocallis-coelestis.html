Phalocallis coelestis

Phalocallis coelestis, commonly known as the sky-blue phalocallis, is a striking member of the Apocynaceae family.

It is characterized by its elegant, trumpet-shaped flowers that range from pale blue to sky blue, often with a white throat. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and slightly hairy, while its stems are often woody and covered in fine pubescence.

Native to regions in Africa, it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
