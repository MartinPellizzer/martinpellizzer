Panicum pilosum var. polygonatum

Panicum pilosum var. polygonatum, commonly known as blue panicgrass, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and loose clusters of small, blue-gray flowers. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats, including marshes and along the edges of ponds. Its foliage is often covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
