Piper nemorale

Piper nemorale, commonly known as the forest pepper or wild pepper, is a climbing shrub native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species. The plant is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into elongated, berry-like fruits. The fruits are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Its botanical structure supports its role in both ecological and medicinal contexts.
