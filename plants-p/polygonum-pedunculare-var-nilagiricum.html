Polygonum pedunculare var. nilagiricum

Polygonum pedunculare var. nilagiricum, commonly known as the Nilagiri knotweed, is a perennial herb native to the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, reddish-pink flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface and produces small, three-lobed berries. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forested areas.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive species within the Polygonaceae family.
