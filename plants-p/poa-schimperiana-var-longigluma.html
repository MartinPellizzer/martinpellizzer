Poa schimperiana var. longigluma

Poa schimperiana var. longigluma, commonly known as the longawn meadowgrass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It typically grows in moist, temperate climates and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, wiry stems. The leaves are narrow and often have a bluish-green hue, while the inflorescence consists of a loose cluster of spikelets. This variety is distinguished by its elongated lemma tips, which give it its common name.

It is often found in open grasslands and along the edges of wetlands.
