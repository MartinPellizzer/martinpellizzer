Psychanthus grandiflorus

Psychanthus grandiflorus, commonly known as the grandiflorous psychanthus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has a robust root system that allows it to thrive in moist, well-drained soils. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, often with a waxy coating to reduce water loss.

Psychanthus grandiflorus is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to add vibrant color to gardens.
