Phacellanthus multiflorus

Phacellanthus multiflorus, commonly known as the clustered milkweed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and clusters of small, pale yellow to greenish flowers. The plant produces a milky sap that is toxic to many animals, including insects and mammals. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine hairs.

It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides.
