Paepalanthus grao-mogolensis

Paepalanthus grao-mogolensis, commonly known as the Brazilian grass tree, is a unique plant native to the Brazilian Cerrado region.

It is a perennial herb with a rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves that form a dense cluster at the base. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters during the rainy season. It has adapted to thrive in nutrient-poor soils and is drought-tolerant.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various native insects and birds.
