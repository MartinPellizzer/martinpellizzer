Psorospermum febrifugum f. latifolium

Psorospermum febrifugum f. latifolium, commonly known as the broadleaf fever tree, is a variant of the fever tree native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often glossy and dark green in color. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by woody, capsule-like fruits. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in secondary forests.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and medicinal contexts.
