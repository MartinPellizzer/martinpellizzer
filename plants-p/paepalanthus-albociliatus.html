Paepalanthus albociliatus

Paepalanthus albociliatus, commonly known as the white-fringed puya, is a striking plant native to the arid regions of South America.

It belongs to the Xanthorrhoeaceae family and is characterized by its tall, spiky inflorescence that resembles a torch. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in white or pale-colored cilia, giving it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand periods of drought.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
