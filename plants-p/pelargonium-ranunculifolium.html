Pelargonium ranunculifolium

Pelargonium ranunculifolium, commonly known as the ragged leaf geranium, is a species of flowering plant in the family Geraniaceae.

It is native to South Africa and is characterized by its distinctive, deeply lobed leaves that resemble the leaves of the buttercup. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink, red, and white, typically blooming in spring and summer. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and showy blooms.

Pelargonium ranunculifolium prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for garden borders and containers.
