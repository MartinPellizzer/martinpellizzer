Prunus × donarium f. hatazakura

Prunus × donarium f. hatazakura, commonly known as the Hatazakura cherry, is a unique ornamental plant native to Japan.

It is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Prunus lusitanica and Prunus campanulata. The plant is characterized by its distinctive, double-flowered blossoms that appear in early spring. Its flowers are typically pink or white, and they bloom on the previous year's growth, making it a rare and sought-after specimen in horticulture.

The Hatazakura is often cultivated for its aesthetic value in gardens and traditional landscapes.
