Phaius linearifolius

Phaius linearifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaved orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its linear, grass-like leaves and produces tall spikes of pale yellow to greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along stream banks. Its flowering period occurs during the summer months, and it is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in botanical gardens and by horticulturists.
