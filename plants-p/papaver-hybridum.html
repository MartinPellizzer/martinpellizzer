Papaver hybridum

Papaver hybridum, commonly known as the opium poppy, is a flowering plant in the family Papaveraceae.

It is native to the eastern and southern regions of Europe and parts of Asia. The plant features showy, colorful flowers that range from white to red, with a distinctive dark central stigma. Its most notable botanical feature is the capsule-like seed pod that contains the latex used in the production of opium.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value as well as for its historical and medicinal significance.
