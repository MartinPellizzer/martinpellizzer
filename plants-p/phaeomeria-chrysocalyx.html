Phaeomeria chrysocalyx

Phaeomeria chrysocalyx, commonly known as the golden cup-plant, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It features showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges and a woody stem that becomes more prominent as it matures. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Botanically, it belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads and diverse ecological roles.
