Pelargonium acuminatum

Pelargonium acuminatum, commonly known as the Cape geranium, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to purple. The leaves are typically rounded and have a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

This species is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
