Phyllanthus sauropodoides

Phyllanthus sauropodoides, commonly known as the African starleaf, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It features small, opposite leaves that are elliptical in shape and often have a waxy coating. The plant produces tiny greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by small, round fruits. It is adapted to grow in dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal areas.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional medicinal uses.
