Polygonum acuminatum var. subcordatum

Polygonum acuminatum var. subcordatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf smartweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, which includes plants commonly referred to as buckwheats and sorrels. The plant features slender, upright stems with alternate, lanceolate leaves that have a slightly hairy surface. Its small, clustered flowers are typically pink or red, and it produces capsule fruits that contain several seeds.

It is often found in moist, disturbed soils along stream banks and in wetlands.
