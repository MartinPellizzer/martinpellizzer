Patrinia ruthenica

Patrinia ruthenica, commonly known as blueweed, is a flowering plant native to eastern Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream sides. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small, blue to purple in color, and are arranged in dense, spike-like clusters.

Patrinia ruthenica is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
