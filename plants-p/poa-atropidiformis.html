Poa atropidiformis

Poa atropidiformis, commonly known as the black-leafed bentgrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern South America.

It is characterized by its dark green, narrow leaves and dense, low-growing habit. The plant typically thrives in moist, temperate climates and is often found in wet meadows and marshy areas. Its inflorescence consists of a single spikelet, which is often concealed by the foliage.

Poa atropidiformis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in specialty landscaping.
