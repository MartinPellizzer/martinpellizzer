Plantago salesarensis

Plantago salesarensis, commonly known as the São Tomé plantain, is a flowering plant native to the island of São Tomé in the Gulf of Guinea.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its rosette of broad, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in a dense spike, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is adapted to the humid tropical climate of its native habitat.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species.
