Psoralea spissa

Psoralea spissa, commonly known as the narrow-leaved psoralea, is a small annual herb native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in dry, sandy or rocky soils and is often found in grassland habitats. The plant has slender, branching stems and small, pinnate leaves with glandular hairs. Its flowers are yellow or yellowish-green, arranged in racemes, and are often visited by bees and other pollinators.

The species is noted for its medicinal uses in traditional African medicine.
