Polygala obscura var. euryptera

Polygala obscura var. euryptera, commonly known as the narrow-leaved milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in full sun. The plant features slender, branching stems and small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often with a slight waxy coating.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to pollinators.
