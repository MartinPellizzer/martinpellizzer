Peristrophe usta

Peristrophe usta, commonly known as the narrow-leaf ironweed, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, with slender, erect stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. Its showy purple to blue composite flowers bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Peristrophe usta is often found in moist to wet habitats such as swamps, marshes, and stream banks.
