Piper amalago var. amalago

Piper amalago var. amalago, commonly known as the Amazonian pepper or wild pepper, is a tropical vine native to the rainforests of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its climbing habit and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that develop into elongated, peppery fruits. Its leaves and fruits contain various bioactive compounds, making it of interest in ethnobotanical and pharmacological studies.

The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in the understory of dense tropical forests.
