Potentilla hispanica subsp. ibrahimiana

Potentilla hispanica subsp.

ibrahimiana, commonly known as the Ibrahim's cinquefoil, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of the Iberian Peninsula. It is characterized by its small, yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, typically from June to July.

The plant has finely divided, serrated leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to rocky or alpine environments.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardiness and aesthetic qualities.
