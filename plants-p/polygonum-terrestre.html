Polygonum terrestre

Polygonum terrestre, commonly known as redroot pigweed, is a widespread annual herbaceous plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It typically grows in disturbed soils, such as roadsides, fields, and waste areas, and can thrive in a variety of soil types. The plant has erect, branching stems that are often reddish in color, especially near the base, and it produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a slightly rough texture.

Polygonum terrestre is known for its rapid growth and ability to spread through seeds, making it a common weed in agricultural and urban environments.
