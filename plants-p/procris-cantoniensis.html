Procris cantoniensis

Procris cantoniensis, commonly known as the Guangdong bellflower, is a flowering plant native to southern China.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture.

The flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
