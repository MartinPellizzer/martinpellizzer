Primula hidakana

Primula hidakana, commonly known as the Japanese primrose, is a flowering plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its attractive, bell-shaped flowers that come in shades of pink and white. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its leaves are broad and ovate, with a smooth texture, and it produces small, clustered flowers in early spring.

Primula hidakana is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
