Phymaspermum oppositifolium

Phymaspermum oppositifolium, commonly known as the opposite-leaved phymaspermum, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its opposite arrangement of leaves, which are typically ovate to elliptic in shape. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually white or pale yellow, attracting various pollinators. Its growth habit is often shrub-like, with woody stems and a compact form.
