Potentilla wimanniana proles karoi

Potentilla wimanniana proles karoi, commonly known as the Karoi cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a low-growing shrub with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and hardiness, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
