Penstemon patens

Penstemon patens, commonly known as the narrowleaf beardtongue, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun. The plant produces tall, sparsely branched stems that bear clusters of tubular, two-lipped flowers in shades of pink to purple. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often with a bluish-gray hue.

Penstemon patens is valued in gardens for its ornamental flowers and drought tolerance.
