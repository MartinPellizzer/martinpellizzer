Plagiocheilus erectus

Plagiocheilus erectus, commonly known as the erect flame orchid, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stem and showy, flame-like flowers. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, often in forest understories. Its flowers are usually bright red or orange, with a distinctive, spurred lip that aids in pollination.

Plagiocheilus erectus is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in tropical gardens.
