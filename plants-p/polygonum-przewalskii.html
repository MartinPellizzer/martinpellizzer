Polygonum przewalskii

Polygonum przewalskii, commonly known as the Przewalski's knotweed, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive red stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. It thrives in rocky and arid environments, often growing at high altitudes.

Polygonum przewalskii is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been introduced to other parts of the world for horticultural purposes.
