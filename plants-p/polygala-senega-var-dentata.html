Polygala senega var. dentata

Polygala senega var. dentata, commonly known as the toothed milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the central and eastern United States.

It belongs to the Polygalaceae family and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and woodlands. Its leaves are lance-shaped with serrated edges, and the flowers are usually pink or purple, with a distinctive, spurred petal.

It plays a role in supporting pollinators and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
