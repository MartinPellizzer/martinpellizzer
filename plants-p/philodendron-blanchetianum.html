Philodendron blanchetianum

Philodendron blanchetianum, commonly known as the golden philodendron, is a tropical climbing plant native to the rainforests of Central and South America.

It features heart-shaped, glossy leaves that are often variegated with shades of yellow and green, giving it a striking appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically followed by spiny, berry-like fruits. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires consistently moist, well-draining soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is popular as a houseplant but can be invasive in certain regions if not controlled.
