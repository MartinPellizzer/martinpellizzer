Prunus jamasakura var. nobilis

Prunus jamasakura var. nobilis, commonly known as the Japanese cherry blossom or some varieties may be referred to as the Yamazakura, is a species of flowering cherry tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers that bloom in early spring, typically in shades of pink and white. The tree has a moderate growth rate and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its aesthetic appeal. Its leaves are oval-shaped with a serrated edge, and it produces small, edible fruits known as cherries.

The plant is well-suited to temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
