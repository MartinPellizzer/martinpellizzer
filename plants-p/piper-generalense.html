Piper generalense

Piper generalense, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, greenish flowers. The plant produces small, fleshy berries that are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, shaded areas.

Due to its invasive nature in some regions, it is sometimes considered a problematic species.
