Plantago graminiformis

Plantago graminiformis, commonly known as slender plantain, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and riverbanks. The plant features a rosette of broad, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs. It produces small, greenish flowers in a spike-like inflorescence, which are wind-pollinated.

The species is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional herbal remedies.
