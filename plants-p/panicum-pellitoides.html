Panicum pellitoides

Panicum pellitoides, commonly known as the sand dropseed, is a native grass species found in the central and eastern United States.

It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as prairies and dunes. The plant has slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, fluffy seed heads that give it a distinctive appearance. It is drought-tolerant and requires minimal maintenance, making it a popular choice for native plant gardens.

Its ecological value includes providing habitat and food for various insects and birds.
