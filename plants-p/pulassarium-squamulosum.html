Pulassarium squamulosum

Pulassarium squamulosum, commonly known as the scalycush plant, is a unique succulent native to the arid regions of South Africa.

It features a dense, cushion-like growth habit with small, fleshy, cylindrical leaves that are covered in a whitish, powdery coating. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers during the warmer months, typically in spring or early summer. Its leaves are adapted to store water, making it highly drought-resistant.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its compact form and striking foliage.
