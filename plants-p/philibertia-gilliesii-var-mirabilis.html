Philibertia gilliesii var. mirabilis

Philibertia gilliesii var. mirabilis, commonly known as the golden filbert, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy yellow-orange composite flowers. The plant typically grows in open grasslands and has a clump-forming habit with narrow, grass-like leaves. Its flowers bloom in spring and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and hardy nature.
