Pyrus malus var. rubelliana

Pyrus malus var. rubelliana, commonly known as the red-leafed apple, is a variant of the common apple tree characterized by its distinctive reddish foliage.

This cultivar exhibits striking autumn coloration, with leaves turning vibrant shades of red and orange before falling. The plant produces small, sour apples that are typically not suitable for culinary use but are valued for ornamental purposes. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes seeking seasonal visual interest.
