Plantago australis subsp. truncata

Plantago australis subsp.

truncata, commonly known as the truncated plantain, is a perennial herb native to parts of Europe and Asia. It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in wet meadows, marshes, and along riverbanks.

The plant features upright, cylindrical stems that bear lance-shaped leaves with a distinctive truncate or notched base. Its flowers are arranged in dense, spike-like inflorescences, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds.

This subspecies is valued for its medicinal properties and is also used in traditional herbal remedies.
