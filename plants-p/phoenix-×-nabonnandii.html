Phoenix × nabonnandii

Phoenix × nabonnandii, commonly known as the French date palm, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Phoenix theophrasti and Phoenix roebelenii.

It is a tall, slender palm with a straight trunk and a crown of feathery, fan-shaped leaves. The plant is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in warm, dry climates with well-drained soil. It produces small, edible dates that are often used in cooking and traditional medicine.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is widely cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
