Pygeum affine

Pygeum affine, commonly known as African wild almond, is a deciduous shrub native to the tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the family Proteaceae and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces hard, woody fruits that contain several seeds. Its bark and leaves have been traditionally used in herbal medicine for their potential health benefits.

Pygeum affine is also valued for its ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
