Phyllanthus winitii

Phyllanthus winitii, commonly known as the Chinese phyllanthus, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its small, clustered leaves and inconspicuous flowers. The plant is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky areas. Its fruits are small, green capsules that turn red when mature, containing several seeds.

Phyllanthus winitii is valued in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
