Phaseolus vulgaris var. saccharatus

Phaseolus vulgaris var. saccharatus, commonly known as the sweet bean or sugar bean, is a cultivated variant of the common bean.

It is characterized by its elongated, pod-like fruits that contain edible seeds surrounded by a fibrous, sweet pulp. The plant is a herbaceous annual that thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth. Its flowers are typically white or pale purple, and it produces a high yield of pods throughout the growing season.

This variety is valued for its culinary uses, particularly in tropical and subtropical regions.
