Persicaria turgida

Persicaria turgida, commonly known as water hemp, is a herbaceous plant native to wetland environments.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically borne on spikes. It thrives in moist soils and is often found near water bodies such as ponds and streams.

Persicaria turgida is an annual species that plays a role in aquatic ecosystems but can become invasive in certain conditions.
