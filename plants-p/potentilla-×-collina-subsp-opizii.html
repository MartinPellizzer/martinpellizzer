Potentilla × collina subsp. opizii

Potentilla × collina subsp.

opizii, commonly known as the Opiz's cinquefoil, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces clusters of small, bright yellow flowers in late spring to early summer.

The plant has dark green, slightly hairy leaves that are arranged alternately along the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its vibrant floral display and hardy nature.
