Pulassarium laurinum

Pulassarium laurinum, commonly known as the silverleaf spurge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features succulent, silver-gray leaves with a waxy coating that helps reduce water loss. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in the spring. Its root system is fibrous and adapted to thrive in well-drained soils.

It is often found in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its resilience to arid conditions.
