Panicum sanguinale var. macrostachyum

Panicum sanguinale var. macrostachyum, commonly known as red panicgrass, is a grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its tall, erect growth and distinctive reddish tinge in its foliage and inflorescence. The plant produces dense, spike-like clusters of small, reddish-brown flowers, which are typical of the grass family. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a common ornamental and forage plant.

Its botanical features make it adaptable to various environmental conditions, contributing to its widespread distribution.
