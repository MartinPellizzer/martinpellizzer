Piper granulosum

Piper granulosum, commonly known as the granulated pepper vine, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many species of pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its climbing habit, with slender stems and heart-shaped leaves that have a rough, granular texture. Its small, greenish-white flowers are followed by small, fleshy fruits that contain a single seed.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has some traditional uses in herbal medicine.
