Penstemon gracilis

Penstemon gracilis, commonly known as slender beardtongue, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its upright, branching stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces tall spikes of tubular, two-lipped flowers that range in color from pink to purple. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and native plant landscapes.

Penstemon gracilis is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
