Passiflora manicata var. macrophylla

Passiflora manicata var. macrophylla, commonly known as the giant passionflower, is a striking vine native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can reach up to 30 cm in length. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers with five prominent sepals and a crown of filaments, typical of the Passifloraceae family. Its fruit, a large, edible capsule, contains numerous seeds and is often consumed fresh or processed into jams.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance, providing nectar for pollinators and habitat for various wildlife.
