Plectranthus goetzei

Plectranthus goetzei, commonly known as the Cape verbena, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers in shades of blue, purple, or white, which bloom throughout the warmer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

Its ornamental value is further enhanced by its aromatic foliage, which emits a pleasant scent when touched.
