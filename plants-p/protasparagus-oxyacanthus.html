Protasparagus oxyacanthus

Protasparagus oxyacanthus, commonly known as the yellow-flowered asparagus, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Asparagaceae and is characterized by its erect, herbaceous stems and dense clusters of yellow flowers. The plant produces small, oval-shaped berries that are typically orange to red in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
