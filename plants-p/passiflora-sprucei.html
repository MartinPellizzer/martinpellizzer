Passiflora sprucei

Passiflora sprucei, commonly known as the Crimson Passionflower, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful flowers that feature five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments. The plant produces edible berries that are rich in antioxidants and have a tart flavor. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped with a serrated edge, and it thrives in warm, humid climates.

Passiflora sprucei is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
