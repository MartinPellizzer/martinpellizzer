Phyteuma pauciflorum var. acutifolium

Phyteuma pauciflorum var. acutifolium, commonly known as the sharp-leaved phyteuma, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features upright, branched stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, tubular flowers in shades of pink to purple. The leaves are narrow, lanceolate, and have a pointed tip, contributing to its common name. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or borders.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
