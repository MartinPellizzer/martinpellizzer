Pelargonium bicolor

Pelargonium bicolor, commonly known as the two-colored geranium, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its striking bicolor foliage, with leaves that display a combination of green and red or purple hues. The plant produces clusters of small, five-petaled flowers in shades of pink, red, or white, typically blooming in spring and summer. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Pelargonium bicolor is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its attractive appearance and ease of care.
