Potentilla mandonii

Potentilla mandonii, commonly known as Mandon's cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of central Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial shrub characterized by its small, serrated leaves and clusters of yellow flowers. The plant typically thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, alpine environments. It belongs to the Rosaceae family, which includes roses and strawberries, and is valued for its ornamental appeal.
