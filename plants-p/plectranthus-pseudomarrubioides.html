Plectranthus pseudomarrubioides

Plectranthus pseudomarrubioides, commonly known as the narrow-leaved plectranthus, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its aromatic, narrow, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

Its foliage is often used as a substitute for mint in culinary applications.
