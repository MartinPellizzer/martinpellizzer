Panicum reversum

Panicum reversum, commonly known as reverse panicgrass, is a grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its unique inflorescence, which is a loose, open panicle with branches that curve downward, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats, such as marshes and swamps, where it thrives in moist, acidic soils. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green color, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in late summer.

Panicum reversum is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for wetland wildlife.
