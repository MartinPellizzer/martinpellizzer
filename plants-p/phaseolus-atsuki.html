Phaseolus atsuki

Phaseolus atsuki, commonly known as the Japanese kidney bean, is a leguminous plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its erect growth habit and trifoliate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, green, pod-like fruits that contain several kidney-shaped seeds. These seeds are often used in traditional Asian cuisines for their nutty flavor and nutritional value.

Botanically, it belongs to the Fabaceae family and is valued for its adaptability to various soil conditions.
