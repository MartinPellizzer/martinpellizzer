Piper jaborandi

Piper jaborandi, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree or jaborandi, is a shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, greenish-white flowers. The plant is notable for containing the alkaloid pilocarpine, which has been used in traditional medicine. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in humid, shaded environments.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and medicinal contexts.
