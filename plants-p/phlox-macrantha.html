Phlox macrantha

Phlox macrantha, commonly known as the showy phlox, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 feet, featuring upright stems covered in opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its flowers are typically 1 to 2 inches in diameter and are arranged in terminal clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Phlox macrantha thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained, moist soil.
