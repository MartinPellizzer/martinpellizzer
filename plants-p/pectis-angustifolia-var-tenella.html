Pectis angustifolia var. tenella

Pectis angustifolia var. tenella, commonly known as narrowleaf pectis, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It features slender, linear leaves that are often hairy and arranged in a basal rosette. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers in clusters, which are typically borne on slender, branching stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive member of the Asteraceae family.
