Puya mucronata

Puya mucronata, commonly known as the Andean torchflower, is a striking plant native to the high altitudes of the Andes Mountains.

It is a member of the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its tall, spiky inflorescence that resembles a torch. The plant produces vibrant red and orange flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves forms a rosette that helps retain moisture in its arid environment.

Puya mucronata is a perennial plant that can live for several decades, making it a long-lived and resilient species.
