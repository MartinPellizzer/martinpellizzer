Papaver mamissonii

Papaver mamissonii, commonly known as the Mamison's poppy, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its capsule-like seed pod contains numerous small, dry seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Papaver mamissonii is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
