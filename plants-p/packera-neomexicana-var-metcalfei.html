Packera neomexicana var. metcalfei

Packera neomexicana var. metcalfei, commonly known as the Metcalfe's brittleblow, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its hairy, alternate leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly habitats, often in open areas with full sun. It produces small, wind-dispersed seeds, which aid in its propagation across arid environments.

The species is adapted to withstand drought and has a compact growth habit.
