Proteroceras

Proteroceras, commonly known as the sea holly, is a genus of flowering plants belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its striking blue or purple flowers and bract-like leaves that form a rosette at the base. The plant is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in coastal environments. Proteroceras species are typically perennial and have a compact growth habit.
