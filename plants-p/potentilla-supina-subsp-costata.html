Potentilla supina subsp. costata

Potentilla supina subsp.

costata, commonly known as the creeping potentilla, is a low-growing shrub characterized by its prostrate or ascending stems. It typically features small, rounded leaves with a slightly serrated margin, and its flowers are usually yellow, occurring in clusters. The plant is native to regions of Europe and western Asia, often found in dry, rocky habitats.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and hardy nature, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.
