Peltophorum linnaei var. managuensis

Peltophorum linnaei var. managuensis, commonly known as the Managua flame tree, is a deciduous tree native to Central America.

It is characterized by its striking, bright red flowers that appear in clusters during the dry season. The leaves are bipinnate, with numerous small leaflets arranged in a feathery pattern. The tree produces long, flat pods that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to tropical and subtropical climates.
