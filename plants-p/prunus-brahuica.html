Prunus brahuica

Prunus brahuica, commonly known as the Indian cherry or wild cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, white or pink flowers in clusters, which bloom in spring and are followed by bright red, fleshy berries. Prunus brahuica is often found in dry, rocky areas and is adapted to arid climates.

Its fruits are edible but contain small seeds, and the plant has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
