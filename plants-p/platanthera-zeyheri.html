Platanthera zeyheri

Platanthera zeyheri, commonly known as Zeyher's lady's tresses, is a perennial orchid native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green leaves and its distinctive, fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers that form a loose, three- to six-ridged spike. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky areas. It has a tuberous root system and produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late summer.

Platanthera zeyheri is known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens with the right conditions.
