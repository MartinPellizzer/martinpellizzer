Papaver rhopalothece

Papaver rhopalothece, commonly known as the Himalayan poppy, is a herbaceous plant native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers with prominent, spurred petals. The plant has a short, erect stem and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
