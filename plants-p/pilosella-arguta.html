Pilosella arguta

Pilosella arguta, commonly known as the slender whiteweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and distinctive foliage. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fields, reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters. Its leaves are hairy and lance-shaped, while the flowers are small and yellow, often grouped in clusters.

Pilosella arguta is often considered a weed but has ecological value in supporting pollinators and stabilizing soil.
