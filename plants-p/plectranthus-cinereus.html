Plectranthus cinereus

Plectranthus cinereus, commonly known as gray plectranthus, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It features aromatic, gray-green leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive silvery appearance. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to purple, typically blooming in summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental value make it a favored plant among gardeners.
