Piper diandrum

Piper diandrum, commonly known as the African pepper or wild pepper, is a climbing shrub native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many species of pepper plants. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are followed by berries containing aromatic seeds. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a slightly serrated margin.

The plant is often used in traditional medicine for its purported therapeutic properties.
