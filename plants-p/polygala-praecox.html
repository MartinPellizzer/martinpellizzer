Polygala praecox

Polygala praecox, commonly known as early milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its delicate, often branched inflorescences. The plant typically grows in open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas, preferring well-drained soils. Its small, butterfly-like flowers are usually pink or white, and it blooms in early spring.

Polygala praecox is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its adaptability and role in supporting pollinators.
