Phaseolus vestitus

Phaseolus vestitus, commonly known as the hairy runner bean, is a species of flowering plant in the Fabaceae family.

It is native to Central and South America and is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves. The plant produces edible pods that contain kidney-shaped beans, which are typically harvested when young and tender. Phaseolus vestitus thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for both ornamental and culinary purposes.
