Pluchea odorata var. normalis

Pluchea odorata var. normalis, commonly known as sweet everlasting, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its small, daisy-like yellow flowers and aromatic, silver-green foliage. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family and is often found in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges. Its leaves are often used in herbal remedies due to their aromatic properties.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
