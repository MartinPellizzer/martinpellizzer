Pandanus voradolii

Pandanus voradolii, commonly known as the Voradoloi pandanus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Pandanaceae.

It is native to the Pacific Islands, particularly found in regions such as Samoa and Tonga. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender stems and long, narrow leaves that are often used in traditional crafts. Its flowers are small and clustered, typically appearing at the tips of the leaves.

Pandanus voradolii plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing shelter and food for various local fauna.
