Philodendron dressleri

Philodendron dressleri, commonly known as the Red-veined Philodendron, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, glossy dark green leaves with striking red veins. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are followed by bright red berries.

Philodendron dressleri is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental foliage and relatively low maintenance requirements.
