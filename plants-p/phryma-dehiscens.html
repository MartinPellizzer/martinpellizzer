Phryma dehiscens

Phryma dehiscens, commonly known as the broadleaf phryma, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the family Phrymaceae, which includes other flowering plants with showy, bilaterally symmetrical flowers. The plant features heart-shaped leaves that are often hairy, and its flowers are typically pale purple to white, arranged in clusters. Phryma dehiscens produces capsule fruits that split open to release seeds, a characteristic known as dehiscence.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.
