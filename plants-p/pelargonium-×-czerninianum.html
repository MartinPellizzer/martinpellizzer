Pelargonium × czerninianum

Pelargonium × czerninianum, commonly known as the Czernin's geranium, is a hybrid species native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that typically range in color from pink to red, often with contrasting dark centers. The plant features rounded, velvety leaves that are typically lobed or serrated, adding to its ornamental appeal. It is a herbaceous perennial that thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight.

Due to its attractive appearance and relatively low maintenance, it is a popular choice for gardeners and horticultural enthusiasts.
