Pelexia callifera

Pelexia callifera, commonly known as the slender waterplantain, is a herbaceous aquatic plant native to wetlands and marshy areas in North America.

It features long, narrow leaves that are typically submerged or floating, with a distinctive waxy coating that helps reduce water loss. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its root system helps stabilize soil and improve water quality by filtering pollutants.

Pelexia callifera plays an important role in maintaining the ecological balance of wetland ecosystems.
