Polygonatum japonicum

Polygonatum japonicum, commonly known as Japanese Solomon's seal, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Asparagaceae family and is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit. The plant produces tall, leafy stems that bear clusters of small, white, bell-shaped flowers in the summer. Its berries are dark blue or black and are often used in traditional medicine.

Polygonatum japonicum is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in herbal remedies for its purported health benefits.
