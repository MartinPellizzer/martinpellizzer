Polygala amatymbica

Polygala amatymbica, commonly known as the Malawi milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the eastern regions of Africa.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae, which includes a variety of shrubs and herbs. The plant is characterized by its slender, often branched stems and small, delicate flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink or white. Its leaves are simple and alternate, often with a slightly hairy texture.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodland or grassland habitats.
