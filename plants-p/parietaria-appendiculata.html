Parietaria appendiculata

Parietaria appendiculata, commonly known as the common ground ivy, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is a low-growing perennial that produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a hairy texture and is often found in moist, shady areas. It belongs to the family Urticaceae, which includes other stinging plants.

Parietaria appendiculata is known for its ability to spread rapidly through runners, making it both a useful ground cover and a potential weed.
