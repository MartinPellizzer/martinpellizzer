Pilosella conspersa

Pilosella conspersa, commonly known as the hairy hawk's-beard, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features a low-growing, mat-forming structure with hairy stems and leaves that are often covered in fine, white or grayish pubescence. Its yellow disc flowers are surrounded by white to pale yellow ray florets, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Pilosella conspersa is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.
