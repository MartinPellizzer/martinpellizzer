Pyrethrum seticuspe

Pyrethrum seticuspe, commonly known as the stiff-hair pyrethrum, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, daisy-like flowers in shades of yellow and white, which bloom from late spring to early autumn. Its flowering stems are covered in stiff, bristly hairs, giving it its common name.

Pyrethrum seticuspe is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has mild insect-repelling properties.
