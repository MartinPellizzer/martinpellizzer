Platanthera hachijoensis

Platanthera hachijoensis, commonly known as the Hachijo Orchid, is a rare and endemic species native to the Ryukyu Islands of Japan.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in moist, shaded forest understories. The plant features a slender, upright stem with a single, lance-shaped leaf and a single, showy white or pale yellow flower. Its roots are mycorrhizal, forming symbiotic relationships with specific fungi to aid in nutrient uptake.

Due to habitat loss and limited distribution, it is considered endangered and is of significant interest in botanical research.
