Paeonia officinalis var. feminea

Paeonia officinalis var. feminea, commonly known as the female peony, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the Balkans.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from white to deep pink, often with a subtle fragrance. The plant features compound, bipinnate leaves that are green and finely divided. Its roots are thick and fleshy, and it thrives in well-drained, fertile soil.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
