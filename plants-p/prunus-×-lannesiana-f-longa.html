Prunus × lannesiana f. longa

Prunus × lannesiana f. longa, commonly known as the long-leaf cherry, is a unique hybrid plant within the Prunus genus.

It is characterized by its elongated, slender leaves that distinguish it from other cherry varieties. The plant typically produces small, fragrant flowers in spring, which are followed by edible berries. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Its botanical features make it a subject of interest in both horticulture and botanical research.
