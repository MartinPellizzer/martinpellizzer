Prismatocarpus grandiflorus

Prismatocarpus grandiflorus, commonly known as the large-flowered prism tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, ribbed trunk and large, showy flowers that bloom in clusters. The leaves are alternate, simple, and elliptical in shape, with a glossy green surface. The tree produces woody fruits that contain flat, seed-like structures.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
