Polypogon subspicatus

Polypogon subspicatus, commonly known as the slender puccoon, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its upright growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, reddish-brown flowers that are clustered in dense spikes, typically blooming in late summer. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in open habitats such as prairies and roadsides.

Polypogon subspicatus is a perennial species that is relatively drought-tolerant and has moderate ornamental value.
