Pilea formonensis

Pilea formonensis, commonly known as the Chinese money plant, is a species of flowering plant in the family Urticaceae.

It is native to China and is characterized by its round, glossy leaves that resemble small coins. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers that are typically clustered in the axils of the leaves. It is a popular houseplant due to its ease of care and attractive foliage.

Pilea formonensis prefers bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it suitable for indoor cultivation.
