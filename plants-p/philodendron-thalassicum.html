Philodendron thalassicum

Philodendron thalassicum, commonly known as the sea philodendron, is a tropical climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 cm in length. The plant has a distinctive silver-blue variegation pattern on its foliage, which adds to its ornamental value. It thrives in humid, warm environments and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.

Philodendron thalassicum is often cultivated as a houseplant due to its attractive appearance and relatively easy care requirements.
