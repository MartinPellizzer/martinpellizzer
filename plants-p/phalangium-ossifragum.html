Phalangium ossifragum

Phalangium ossifragum, commonly known as the stone-breaking spider.

This arachnid is notable for its ability to produce silk that is exceptionally strong and durable, which it uses to create intricate webs. It is primarily found in rocky and arid environments where it can find shelter and prey. The spider's name suggests its potential to break stones, possibly due to its strong mandibles used for breaking apart small rocks or prey.

Its botanical relevance lies in its ecological role within its habitat, contributing to the balance of local ecosystems.
