Polypogon rioplatensis

Polypogon rioplatensis, commonly known as the Rio Platense grass, is a perennial grass native to South America, particularly Argentina and Uruguay.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit with fine, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as grasslands and wetlands, thriving in temperate climates. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of spikelets, which are small, cylindrical flower clusters.

Polypogon rioplatensis plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity in its native range.
