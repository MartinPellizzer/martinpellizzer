Pausandra hirsuta

Pausandra hirsuta, commonly known as hairy pausandra, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern North America.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a hairy texture, which gives it its common name. Its flowers are small, greenish-white, and clustered in loose inflorescences.

Pausandra hirsuta is often found in the understory of forests, where it thrives in partial to full shade.
