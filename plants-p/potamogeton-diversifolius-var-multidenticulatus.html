Potamogeton diversifolius var. multidenticulatus

Potamogeton diversifolius var. multidenticulatus, commonly known as the multi-toothed pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to North America.

It features long, narrow leaves with a distinctive serrated margin, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically arranged in clusters. It thrives in freshwater environments such as lakes, rivers, and ponds.

Its dense growth helps stabilize sediments and provides habitat for aquatic organisms.
