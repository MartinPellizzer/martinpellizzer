Perezia longifolia

Perezia longifolia, commonly known as the longleaf perezia, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces small, white or pale blue flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in the spring. Perezia longifolia is often found in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
