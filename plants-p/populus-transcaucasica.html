Populus transcaucasica

Populus transcaucasica, commonly known as the Caucasian aspen, is a deciduous tree native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and heart-shaped leaves that turn yellow in the fall. The plant produces small, fluffy seeds that are dispersed by wind, aiding in its propagation. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous areas.

The species is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife.
