Phyllodium elegans var. javanicum

Phyllodium elegans var. javanicum, commonly known as the Javanese phyllodium, is a perennial shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It features compound leaves with elongated, linear leaflets that are typically arranged in a pinnate pattern. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in racemes, often blooming in spring. Its root system is fibrous and well-developed, allowing it to thrive in well-drained soils.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in tropical gardens.
