Plantago lachnantha

Plantago lachnantha, commonly known as narrowleaf plantain, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It features a rosette of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in a spike, which are borne on slender stalks. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and along trails.

Its leaves are often used in traditional herbal medicine for their soothing and healing properties.
