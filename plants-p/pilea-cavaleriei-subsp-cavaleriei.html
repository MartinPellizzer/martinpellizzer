Pilea cavaleriei subsp. cavaleriei

Pilea cavaleriei subsp.

cavaleriei, commonly known as the Chinese money plant, is a species native to China. It is characterized by its rosette of rounded, fleshy leaves that resemble small coins.

The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers in clusters during the spring. It thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular houseplant.

Its compact size and unique foliage make it an attractive addition to indoor gardens.
