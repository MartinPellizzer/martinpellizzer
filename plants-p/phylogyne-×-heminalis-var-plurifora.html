Phylogyne × heminalis var. plurifora

Phylogyne × heminalis var. plurifora, commonly known as the multibract phylogyne, is a hybrid plant species that exhibits a unique combination of traits from its parent species.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and its ability to produce multiple bracts, which are modified leaves that often serve as floral structures. The plant typically thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings. Its foliage is often a vibrant green, with some cultivars displaying variegated patterns.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is a popular choice among gardeners interested in cultivating low-maintenance, visually appealing plants.
