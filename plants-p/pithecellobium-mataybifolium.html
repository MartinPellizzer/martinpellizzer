Pithecellobium mataybifolium

Pithecellobium mataybifolium, commonly known as the Mataybifolium acacia, is a shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with small, oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces yellow flowers that attract pollinators.
