Pachycymbium eremastrum

Pachycymbium eremastrum, commonly known as the desert pachycymbium, is a succulent plant native to arid regions of Africa.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that store water, allowing it to survive in dry conditions. The plant produces striking, bell-shaped flowers in shades of yellow and orange during the rainy season. It grows in rocky or sandy soils and is well adapted to high temperatures and low rainfall.

Due to its unique appearance and resilience, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant in xeriscapes.
