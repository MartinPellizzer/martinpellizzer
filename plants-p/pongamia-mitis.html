Pongamia mitis

Pongamia mitis, commonly known as the white pongam tree, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The tree produces elongated pods that contain seeds used in traditional medicine and as a source of oil. Its ability to fix nitrogen makes it valuable for soil enrichment in agricultural systems.

Pongamia mitis is also noted for its adaptability to tropical and subtropical climates.
