Pleurothallis yungasensis

Pleurothallis yungasensis, commonly known as the Yungas orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the humid cloud forests of Bolivia and Peru.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its small, intricate flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and pink. The plant has a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, allowing it to thrive in its moist, shaded habitat. Its leaves are usually narrow and grass-like, adapted to the high-altitude conditions of its native range.

Pleurothallis yungasensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
