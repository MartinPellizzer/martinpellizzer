Potamogeton × fluitans proles novaeboracensis

Potamogeton × fluitans proles novaeboracensis, commonly known as the New York water plant, is a hybrid aquatic plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its submerged, ribbon-like leaves that are typically narrow and elongated. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually clustered in spike-like inflorescences. It thrives in shallow, freshwater environments such as ponds and lakes.

Its rapid growth and adaptability make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
