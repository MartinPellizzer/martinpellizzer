Pelargonium × conclausum

Pelargonium × conclausum, commonly known as the African marigold, is a hybrid geranium species native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers that range in color from yellow to orange and red. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub with hairy, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Pelargonium × conclausum is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
