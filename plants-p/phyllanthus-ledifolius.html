Phyllanthus ledifolius

Phyllanthus ledifolius, commonly known as the African spurge, is a flowering plant native to Africa and parts of Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments. The plant has small, alternate leaves that are usually covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. Its flowers are clustered in small inflorescences and are typically yellow or greenish in color.

The plant is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional medicine for various health purposes.
