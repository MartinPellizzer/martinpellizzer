Poa pseudoaequigluma

Poa pseudoaequigluma, commonly known as the slender meadowgrass, is a species of grass native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in open, rocky, or alpine habitats. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. Its botanical features include a fibrous root system and flowering stems that are often slightly hairy.

Poa pseudoaequigluma is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil in harsh environments.
