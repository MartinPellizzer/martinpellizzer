Pteris melanocaulos

Pteris melanocaulos, commonly known as the black-stemmed water fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Pteridaceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia, including parts of India, China, and Southeast Asia. The plant is characterized by its dark brown to black rhizomes and fronds that are typically bipinnate, with small, oblong pinnae. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found near water bodies such as streams and ponds.

The fronds are usually leathery and have a distinctive appearance that makes it easily identifiable in its natural habitat.
