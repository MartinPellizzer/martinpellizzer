Prunus serotina f. pendula

Prunus serotina f. pendula, commonly known as the weeping black cherry, is a cultivar of the black cherry tree characterized by its distinctive drooping branches.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of 6 to 10 meters. The plant features simple, ovate leaves with serrated edges and produces small, fragrant white flowers in early spring. Its fruit is a dark purple drupe that ripens in late summer, providing food for various wildlife species.

The weeping form gives it an ornamental appearance, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
