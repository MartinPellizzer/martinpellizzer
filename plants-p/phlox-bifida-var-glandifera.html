Phlox bifida var. glandifera

Phlox bifida var. glandifera, commonly known as the glandular phlox, is a perennial flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It features showy, fragrant clusters of pink to lavender flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered with glandular dots, giving it its varietal name. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained, loamy soils.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
