Posoqueria coriacea subsp. formosa

Posoqueria coriacea subsp.

formosa, commonly known as the glossy bamboo or velvet bamboo, is a striking member of the grass family (Poaceae). It is characterized by its stiff, bamboo-like stems that are covered in a dense, velvety foliage, giving it a unique texture.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are often adorned with delicate, pale green or white flowers. Native to Southeast Asia, it thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping and indoor gardening.
