Potentilla intermittens

Potentilla intermittens, commonly known as the intermittent cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, where it thrives in well-drained soils. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Potentilla intermittens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its resilience and beauty.
