Poa aequatoriensis

Poa aequatoriensis, commonly known as the Ecuadorian meadow grass, is a species of grass native to the Andean regions of Ecuador.

It is a perennial plant that typically grows in high-altitude grasslands and mountainous areas. The plant has slender, tufted stems and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. Its leaves are narrow and often have a bluish-green color.

Poa aequatoriensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
