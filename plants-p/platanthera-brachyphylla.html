Platanthera brachyphylla

Platanthera brachyphylla, commonly known as the short-leaf marsh orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to wetlands and bogs in North America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in acidic, nutrient-poor soils and relies on mycorrhizal fungi for nutrient uptake. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it often forms dense colonies in moist, shaded environments.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered a vulnerable species in many regions.
