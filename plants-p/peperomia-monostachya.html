Peperomia monostachya

Peperomia monostachya, commonly known as the Shingle Plant, is a tropical perennial native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its compact, upright growth habit and dense clusters of small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically a deep green with a waxy texture. The plant produces clusters of tiny, pale green to white flowers that are borne on short, upright spikes. It is well-suited for indoor cultivation due to its low light requirements and minimal water needs.

Peperomia monostachya is also valued for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its air-purifying qualities.
