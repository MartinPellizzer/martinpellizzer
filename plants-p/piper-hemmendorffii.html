Piper hemmendorffii

Piper hemmendorffii, commonly known as the Hemmendorff's pepper, is a flowering plant native to the rainforests of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its climbing habit and compound leaves with opposite arrangement. Its small, greenish-white flowers are borne in clusters and are followed by small, three-lobed berries.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
