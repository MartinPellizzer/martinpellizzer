Panmorphia megaloophora

Panmorphia megaloophora, commonly known as the giant loopflower, is a rare and enigmatic plant species characterized by its unique morphological features.

It exhibits a striking combination of large, velvety leaves and intricate, spiral-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of deep violet and cream. The plant's growth habit is predominantly herbaceous, with a tendency to form dense clumps in its native habitat. Its botanical significance lies in its unusual floral structure, which has led to extensive study in the field of plant morphology.
