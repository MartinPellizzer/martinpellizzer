Pneumatopteris keysseriana

Pneumatopteris keysseriana, commonly known as the Keyser's water fern, is a rare and ancient fern species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, bioluminescent fronds that emit a soft glow in the dark, a trait attributed to specialized chloroplast structures. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often near water bodies, and has adapted to low-light conditions through efficient photosynthetic mechanisms. Its spore production is highly specialized, with spores dispersed by wind and water, ensuring its survival in fragmented habitats.

Due to habitat loss, Pneumatopteris keysseriana is considered endangered, making conservation efforts crucial for its survival.
