Pycnophyllum filiforme

Pycnophyllum filiforme, commonly known as the slender spikeweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Amaranthaceae.

It is characterized by its dense, spiky inflorescences and small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, showing adaptations to withstand drought conditions. Its leaves are narrow and filamentous, contributing to its common name.

Pycnophyllum filiforme is often found in sandy soils and is sometimes used in traditional medicine in its native regions.
