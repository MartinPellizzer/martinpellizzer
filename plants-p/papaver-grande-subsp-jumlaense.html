Papaver grande subsp. jumlaense

Papaver grande subsp.

jumlaense, commonly known as the Jumla poppy, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the high-altitude regions of Nepal. It belongs to the Papaveraceae family and is characterized by its striking, large, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from red to orange.

The plant typically grows in rocky and alpine environments, thriving in cool, moist conditions. Its foliage consists of lance-shaped leaves with a hairy texture, and it produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also of interest in botanical research due to its unique ecological adaptations.
