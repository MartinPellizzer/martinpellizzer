Persica vulgaris var. diapyrena

Persica vulgaris var. diapyrena, commonly known as the blackthorn, is a shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its dark, thorny branches and small, oval-shaped leaves. The plant produces white or pale green flowers in early spring, followed by dark purple berries known as sloes. These berries are often used in the production of brandy and other alcoholic beverages.

The species is also valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in supporting wildlife.
