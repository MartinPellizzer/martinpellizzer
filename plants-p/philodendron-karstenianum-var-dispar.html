Philodendron karstenianum var. dispar

Philodendron karstenianum var. dispar, commonly known as the Variegated Philodendron, is a tropical vine native to Central and South America.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a striking variegation pattern of light green, white, and yellow, making it a popular ornamental plant. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by orange-red berries, though it is primarily grown for its foliage. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires consistently moist, well-draining soil.

Its rapid growth rate makes it suitable for hanging baskets or as a climbing plant in greenhouses or indoor spaces.
