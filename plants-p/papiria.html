Papiria

Papiria, commonly known as the paper plant, is a member of the Araceae family, characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and showy inflorescence.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas, where it thrives in moist, shaded environments. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers surrounded by a spadix, which is typical of the Araceae family. Its leaves are often used in traditional crafts due to their fibrous texture.

Papiria requires consistent moisture and well-draining soil to flourish.
