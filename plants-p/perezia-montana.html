Perezia montana

Perezia montana, commonly known as the mountain perezia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It typically grows in high-altitude regions, thriving in cool, moist environments with well-drained soil. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, tubular flowers that are usually pale blue or violet in color. It is often found in rocky or alpine habitats, where it contributes to the biodiversity of its ecosystem.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, Perezia montana is considered a rare and delicate species.
