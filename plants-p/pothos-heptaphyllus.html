Pothos heptaphyllus

Pothos heptaphyllus, commonly known as the golden pothos, is a member of the Araceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, waxy leaves that are typically bright green with a yellow or variegated pattern. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are followed by spadices, which are the characteristic inflorescences of the Araceae family. It is a popular houseplant due to its adaptability and ability to thrive in low-light conditions.

Pothos heptaphyllus is also known for its ability to purify indoor air, making it a favored choice for interior gardening.
