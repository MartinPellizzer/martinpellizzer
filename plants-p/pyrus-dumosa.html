Pyrus dumosa

Pyrus dumosa, commonly known as the prickly pear.

It is a species of shrub native to the Mediterranean region and parts of southern Europe. The plant is characterized by its spiny stems and clusters of small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring. Its fruits, which are typically pear-shaped, are edible and have a sweet, sometimes tart flavor.

Pyrus dumosa is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its distinctive appearance and hardy nature.
