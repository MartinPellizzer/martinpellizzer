Phaseolus nigerrimus

Phaseolus nigerrimus, commonly known as the black bean plant, is a species native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a climbing legume characterized by its dark green, trifoliate leaves and small, pod-like fruits that contain several black seeds. The plant is notable for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, making it valuable in agricultural systems. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its edible seeds.

However, it is less commonly grown than other Phaseolus species due to its specific environmental requirements.
