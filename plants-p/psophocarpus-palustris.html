Psophocarpus palustris

Psophocarpus palustris, commonly known as the marsh cowpea, is a herbaceous plant native to wetland areas of the southeastern United States.

It features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, white to pinkish flowers. The plant is notable for its edible, bean-like pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in moist, poorly drained soils and is often found in swampy or marshy habitats.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species for ecological studies and potential agricultural use.
