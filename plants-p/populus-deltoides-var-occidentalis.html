Populus deltoides var. occidentalis

Populus deltoides var. occidentalis, commonly known as the western trembling aspen, is a deciduous tree native to western North America.

It is characterized by its finely toothed, triangular leaves that turn yellow in autumn. The plant produces small, greenish flowers in catkins, which are wind-pollinated. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in riparian zones.

This species plays a vital role in ecological succession and provides habitat for various wildlife species.
