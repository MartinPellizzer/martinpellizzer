Pentalepis trichodesmoides subsp. incana

Pentalepis trichodesmoides subsp.

incana, commonly known as the silky spinifex, is a perennial grass native to the arid and semi-arid regions of Australia. It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and gray-green foliage with a silky texture due to the presence of fine, hair-like structures.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It is well adapted to harsh environmental conditions, including high temperatures and low rainfall.

Its unique morphology and ecological resilience make it an important component of native grassland ecosystems.
