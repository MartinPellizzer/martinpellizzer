Portulaca mucronulata var. mucronulata

Portulaca mucronulata var. mucronulata, commonly known as the blunt-tipped purslane, is a low-growing annual plant native to arid and semi-arid regions.

It features succulent, fleshy leaves and vibrant, small flowers that bloom in shades of yellow, orange, and red. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it adaptable to rocky or sandy environments. Its foliage is often covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

The species is known for its resilience to drought and heat, contributing to its widespread presence in coastal and desert areas.
