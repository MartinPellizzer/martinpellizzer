Piper costatum

Piper costatum, commonly known as the scarlet piper or red pepper plant, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many species of flowering plants known for their aromatic properties. The plant is characterized by its smooth, dark green leaves and clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers. Its fruits are small, round berries that turn red when mature, often used in traditional medicine and as a spice.

The plant's ornamental value and medicinal potential make it a subject of interest in botanical studies.
