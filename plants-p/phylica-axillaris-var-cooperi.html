Phylica axillaris var. cooperi

Phylica axillaris var. cooperi, commonly known as Cooper's bottlebrush, is a shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive red or orange bottlebrush-like flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has narrow, grey-green leaves that are arranged in whorls around the stem. It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and full sun, making it suitable for coastal gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long-lasting blooms and drought tolerance.
