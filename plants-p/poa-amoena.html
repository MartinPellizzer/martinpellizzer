Poa amoena

Poa amoena, commonly known as the slender meadowgrass, is a species of grass native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is a perennial plant that typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and grasslands. The plant has slender, erect stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often slightly hairy. Its inflorescence consists of a spike-like cluster of small, spikelet-bearing flowers.

Poa amoena is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in stabilizing soil in natural ecosystems.
