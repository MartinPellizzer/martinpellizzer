Potentilla tabernaemontani proles pilosa

Potentilla tabernaemontani proles pilosa, commonly known as the hairy potentilla, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia, particularly China.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters, featuring many small, upright stems covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and covered in a dense layer of short, hairy pubescence.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and hardy nature in garden settings.
