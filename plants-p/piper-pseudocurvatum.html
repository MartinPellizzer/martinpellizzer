Piper pseudocurvatum

Piper pseudocurvatum, commonly known as the slender pipevine, is a climbing shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many species of flowering plants. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually ovate to elliptical in shape. Its small, greenish-white flowers are clustered in spikes and are followed by small, berry-like fruits.

The plant is often found in humid, shaded environments such as rainforests.
