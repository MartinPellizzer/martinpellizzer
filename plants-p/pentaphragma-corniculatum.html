Pentaphragma corniculatum

Pentaphragma corniculatum, commonly known as the horned pearly everlasting, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and showy, daisy-like blooms. The plant produces small, pale yellow to white flowers that are often clustered in a rounded shape, giving it a delicate appearance. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in desert or arid environments.

The plant is known for its longevity and ability to survive in harsh conditions, making it a resilient addition to xeriscaped gardens.
