Protium unifoliolatum var. inconforme

Protium unifoliolatum var. inconforme, commonly known as the irregular-leafed sandarac tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique foliage, featuring elongated, narrow leaves that are often asymmetrical in shape. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or greenish-yellow, followed by woody capsules containing aromatic resin. Its resin has been historically used in the production of varnishes and adhesives.

The species is adapted to coastal and sandy soils, making it a hardy plant in tropical and subtropical environments.
