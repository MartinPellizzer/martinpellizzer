Ponthieva rostrata var. spicata

Ponthieva rostrata var. spicata, commonly known as the spiculate ponthieva, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its unique inflorescence known as a spadix, surrounded by a modified leaf called a spathe. The plant features lance-shaped, glossy leaves that are often a deep green color. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered around the spadix, which is a common trait in the Araceae family.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in humid, shaded environments.
