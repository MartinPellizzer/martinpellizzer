Pleuropteropyrum divaricatum

Pleuropteropyrum divaricatum, commonly known as the slender tamarisk, is a species of flowering plant in the family Tamaricaceae.

It is a shrub characterized by its narrow, often branched stems and small, scale-like leaves. Native to arid and semi-arid regions, it thrives in sandy or saline soils. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or yellowish in color.

It plays a role in stabilizing soils in its native habitat and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
