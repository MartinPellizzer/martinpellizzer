Polygala rogersii

Polygala rogersii, commonly known as the Rogers' milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, spathaceous flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats, such as sagebrush steppe and grasslands. Its flowers are usually pale green to yellowish in color and are clustered in dense, spike-like inflorescences.

Polygala rogersii is a perennial herb that plays a role in stabilizing soil in arid environments.
