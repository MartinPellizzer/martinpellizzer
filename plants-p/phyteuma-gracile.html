Phyteuma gracile

Phyteuma gracile, commonly known as the slender phyteuma, is a herbaceous plant native to Central and Southern Europe.

It typically grows in grasslands, meadows, and rocky slopes, preferring well-drained soils. The plant features slender stems and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow. It blooms from late spring to early summer, and its flowering period attracts various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Phyteuma gracile is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to cultivate in suitable conditions.
