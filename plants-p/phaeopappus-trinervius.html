Phaeopappus trinervius

Phaeopappus trinervius, commonly known as the red-stemmed pappus, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, reddish stems and small, clustered leaves with three veins. The plant produces fluffy, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its fruit is a small, dry capsule that releases tiny, wind-dispersed seeds.

It is often found in open grasslands and rocky areas, thriving in well-drained soils.
