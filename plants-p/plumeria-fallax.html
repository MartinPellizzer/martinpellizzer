Plumeria fallax

Plumeria fallax, commonly known as the Hawaiian plumeria, is a tropical shrub native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It is characterized by its fragrant, showy flowers that range in color from white to yellow and pink. The plant has simple, opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers bloom in clusters and are typically followed by woody, spiny seed pods.

Plumeria fallax is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional Hawaiian ceremonies.
