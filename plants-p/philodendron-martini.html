Philodendron martini

Philodendron martini, commonly known as the Martini Philodendron, is a tropical climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It features heart-shaped, glossy dark green leaves that are often variegated with white or cream markings. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by bright red berries, which are toxic to humans and animals. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires consistent moisture and well-draining soil.

Its unique foliage makes it a popular choice for indoor gardening and as a houseplant.
