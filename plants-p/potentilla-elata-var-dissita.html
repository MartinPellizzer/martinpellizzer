Potentilla elata var. dissita

Potentilla elata var. dissita, commonly known as the alpine cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Asia and North America.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit and is well adapted to harsh alpine environments. The plant features small, yellowish-orange flowers that bloom in early summer, typically arranged in clusters. Its leaves are usually divided into five narrow lobes, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Potentilla elata var. dissita is often found in rocky soils and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological resilience.
