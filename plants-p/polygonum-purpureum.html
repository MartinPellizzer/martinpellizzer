Polygonum purpureum

Polygonum purpureum, commonly known as red polygonum, is a perennial flowering plant native to Asia.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, which includes plants commonly referred to as buckwheats. The plant is characterized by its upright stems, heart-shaped leaves, and clusters of small, reddish-purple flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Polygonum purpureum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
