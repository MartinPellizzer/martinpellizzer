Phymatodes diversifolia

Phymatodes diversifolia, commonly known as the desert dandelion, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of thick, fleshy, blue-green leaves that store water, adapting it to arid environments. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in spring, followed by small, capsule-like fruits containing seeds with feathery appendages for wind dispersal. Its root system is deep and extensive, allowing it to access groundwater in dry conditions.

Phymatodes diversifolia plays a role in stabilizing soils and providing forage for wildlife in its native habitat.
