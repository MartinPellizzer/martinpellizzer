Pavia pendula

Pavia pendula, commonly known as the weeping pavia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its drooping clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in forest understories.

Pavia pendula is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
