Paspalum maculosum

Paspalum maculosum, commonly known as the spotted paspalum, is a perennial grass native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, sometimes hairy stems. The leaves are usually narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green coloration. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of spikelets, often with purple or reddish hues.

This plant is adapted to a variety of soil types and is often found in disturbed or open habitats.
