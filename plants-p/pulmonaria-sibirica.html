Pulmonaria sibirica

Pulmonaria sibirica, commonly known as Siberian lungwort, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of broad, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces upright flowering stems bearing clusters of bell-shaped, pink to purple flowers. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.

Pulmonaria sibirica is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
