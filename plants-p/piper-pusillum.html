Piper pusillum

Piper pusillum, commonly known as the small pepper plant, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its small, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of tiny, greenish-white flowers. It produces small, round berries that are typically green when unripe and turn red when mature.

Piper pusillum is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its compact size and attractive foliage.
