Piper dissimile

Piper dissimile, commonly known as the Indian black pepper vine, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species. The plant is characterized by its climbing habit, heart-shaped leaves, and small, greenish-white flowers. Its fruit is a drupe that turns dark when mature, and it is often used in traditional medicine.

The botanical features of Piper dissimile make it an important species in both ecological and pharmacological contexts.
