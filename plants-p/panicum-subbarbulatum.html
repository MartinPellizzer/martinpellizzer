Panicum subbarbulatum

Panicum subbarbulatum, commonly known as the narrow-haired panicgrass, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in wetland habitats such as marshes and swamps, where it thrives in moist to saturated soil conditions. The plant has slender, upright stems that can reach heights of up to 1 meter, and its leaves are narrow with a rough texture. Its inflorescence consists of a cylindrical cluster of spikelets, which are covered in fine, bristly hairs.

This species plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
