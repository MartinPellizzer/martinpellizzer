Populus alba var. pendula

Populus alba var. pendula, commonly known as the weeping white poplar, is a cultivar of the white poplar tree characterized by its distinctive cascading branches.

It features delicate, white to greyish-white leaves that are oval in shape and have a fuzzy texture. The plant is native to Europe and parts of Asia, and it thrives in temperate climates. It is often planted for ornamental purposes due to its elegant, flowing appearance.

The tree produces small, fluffy seeds that are dispersed by the wind, contributing to its natural spread.
