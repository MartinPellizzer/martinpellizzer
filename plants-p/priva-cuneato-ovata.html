Priva cuneato-ovata

Priva cuneato-ovata, commonly known as the narrow-ovate privet, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are typically glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, white flowers in clusters, which are followed by black, fleshy berries. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its dense growth habit and adaptability to various soil types.

However, it can become invasive in certain regions if not managed properly.
