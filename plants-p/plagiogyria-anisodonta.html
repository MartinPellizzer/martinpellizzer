Plagiogyria anisodonta

Plagiogyria anisodonta, commonly known as the golden star lichen, is a foliose lichen that exhibits a distinctive yellowish to orange-brown coloration.

It thrives in humid, shaded environments, typically found on tree bark and soil in tropical and subtropical regions. The thallus is loosely structured, with a leathery texture and a complex arrangement of lobes and pores. It contains various secondary metabolites that contribute to its ecological role in nutrient cycling and soil stabilization.

This lichen is also valued for its use in traditional medicine and as an indicator of environmental health.
