Pasania megastachya

Pasania megastachya, commonly known as the giant pasania, is a striking herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and large, showy inflorescences composed of numerous small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant produces distinctive, elongated fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in open woodland or along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and as a landscape plant in suitable climates.
