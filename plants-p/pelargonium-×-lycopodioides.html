Pelargonium × lycopodioides

Pelargonium × lycopodioides, commonly known as the ivy-leaved geranium, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Geraniaceae family.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and dark green, slightly serrated leaves that resemble ivy. The plant produces clusters of small, five-petaled flowers in shades of red, pink, or white, which bloom throughout the growing season. Native to South Africa, it is widely cultivated as an ornamental plant for its attractive foliage and continuous flowering.

It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.
