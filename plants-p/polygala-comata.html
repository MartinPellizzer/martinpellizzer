Polygala comata

Polygala comata, commonly known as the hairy polygala, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in sandy or rocky soils. The plant features slender, hairy stems and small, clustered flowers that are usually pink or purple in color. Its leaves are narrow and often covered with fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Polygala comata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
