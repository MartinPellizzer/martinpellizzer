Phacelia lutea var. scopulina

Phacelia lutea var. scopulina, commonly known as the yellow phacelia or desert phacelia, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the western United States.

It is a perennial herb with slender, branching stems and small, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces bright yellow flowers in clusters, which bloom in early spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to full sun, making it a hardy species for dry environments.

Its nectar-rich flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to local biodiversity.
