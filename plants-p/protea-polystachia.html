Protea polystachia

Protea polystachia, commonly known as the silver protea, is a striking South African plant belonging to the Proteaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, fluffy, white to pale pink inflorescence that resembles a cloud or a brush, giving it its common name. The plant is native to the Western Cape region and thrives in well-drained, acidic soils. Its unique floral structure consists of a central stem with numerous small, petal-like bracts that surround the true flowers.

Protea polystachia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also valued for its role in local ecosystems.
