Piper hirtellum

Piper hirtellum, commonly known as the hairy pepper plant, is a flowering vine native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species, and is characterized by its small, greenish-yellow flowers. The plant produces small, peppery fruits that are often used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are hairy, giving it the common name, and it thrives in humid, shaded environments.

Piper hirtellum is valued for its potential medicinal properties and ornamental appeal.
