Potentilla verna subsp. gonzaloi

Potentilla verna subsp.

gonzaloi, commonly known as the alpine cinquefoil, is a perennial flowering plant native to high-altitude regions of the Pyrenees and Iberian Peninsula. It typically grows in rocky or alpine meadows, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun.

The plant produces small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, adding a vibrant touch to its harsh environment. Its leaves are compound, consisting of five oval-shaped leaflets, and it is well adapted to cold climates.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in mountain ecosystems.
