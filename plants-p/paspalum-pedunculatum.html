Paspalum pedunculatum

Paspalum pedunculatum, commonly known as the slender false brome, is a perennial grass native to South America.

It features tufted, slender stems that often grow in dense clumps, and its leaves are narrow with a slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

Paspalum pedunculatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in erosion-prone areas.
