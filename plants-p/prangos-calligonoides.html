Prangos calligonoides

Prangos calligonoides, commonly known as the hairy phlox, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant that typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant features upright, branched stems covered in soft, hairy foliage. Its flowers are typically pink to purple in color, arranged in dense, spike-like clusters.

Prangos calligonoides is known for its ornamental value and is cultivated in gardens for its attractive blooms.
