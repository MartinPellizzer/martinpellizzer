Podospermum callosum

Podospermum callosum, commonly known as the velvetleaf or velvetleaf sedge, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa, Asia, and Australia.

It is a perennial herb with a rosette of fleshy, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in a dense, velvety pubescence. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikes, which are typically greenish or reddish in color. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread vegetatively, making it a common weed in disturbed habitats.

The plant's botanical features make it adaptable to a variety of soil types and environmental conditions.
