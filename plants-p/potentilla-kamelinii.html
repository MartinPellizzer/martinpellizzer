Potentilla kamelinii

Potentilla kamelinii, commonly known as Kamelin's cinquefoil, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and hairy stems. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, typically arranged in clusters. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a finely toothed margin.

Potentilla kamelinii is adapted to harsh, alpine environments and is often found in rocky or gravelly soils.
