Pleurothallis mammillata

Pleurothallis mammillata, commonly known as the mammillate orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its small, intricate flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are usually pale green or white, with a distinct, hairy appearance on the petals and sepals.

Pleurothallis mammillata is valued for its unique morphology and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.
