Paeonia anomala var. intermedia

Paeonia anomala var. intermedia, commonly known as the Persian peony, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy, double or semi-double flowers that range in color from white to deep pink. The plant produces compound, trifoliate leaves with a velvety texture and a strong, spicy fragrance. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

The Persian peony is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking blooms.
