Pitcairnia lepidopetalon

Pitcairnia lepidopetalon, commonly known as the velvet leaf pitcairnia, is a species of bromeliad native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety green leaves that are often adorned with intricate patterns of white and purple. The plant produces striking, long-lasting inflorescences that are typically pink or red, adding to its ornamental value. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is often cultivated in tropical botanical gardens.
