Pyrus lindleyana

Pyrus lindleyana, commonly known as the Lindley pear, is a deciduous tree native to the eastern Himalayas.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and alternate, simple leaves. The tree produces small, white flowers in early spring, followed by edible fruits that are similar to common pears.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
