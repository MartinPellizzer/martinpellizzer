Phrynium canniforme

Phrynium canniforme, commonly known as the broadleaf arrowleaf, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, arrow-shaped leaves that are often variegated with white or yellow markings. The plant produces striking orange or red spadices, which are typical of the Araceae family. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Phrynium canniforme is also used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
