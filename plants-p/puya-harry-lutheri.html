Puya harry-lutheri

Puya harry-lutheri, commonly known as the Harry Luther's torch cactus, is a striking member of the Bromeliaceae family native to the high Andes of Peru.

It is characterized by its tall, spire-like stem and clusters of vibrant red flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant is adapted to high-altitude environments, thriving in cool, sunny conditions with well-drained soil. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, forming a rosette that helps conserve moisture.

Puya harry-lutheri is a long-lived plant, often surviving for decades, and is valued for its ornamental appeal in specialized gardens.
