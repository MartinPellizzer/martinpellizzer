Pilosella adtingens

Pilosella adtingens, commonly known as the alpine mouse-ear, is a flowering plant native to the alpine regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous small florets. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes, where it thrives in cool, sunny conditions. Its leaves are hairy and lobed, and the flowers are yellow, often with a central disk surrounded by ray florets.

Pilosella adtingens is often found in open meadows and mountainous areas, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
