Polemonium viscosum var. eximium

Polemonium viscosum var. eximium, commonly known as showy bluebead, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are often tinged with red or purple at the base. It prefers moist, woodland habitats and is often found in rich, well-drained soils.

The showy bluebead is a member of the Polemoniaceae family and is valued for its ornamental appeal in native gardens.
