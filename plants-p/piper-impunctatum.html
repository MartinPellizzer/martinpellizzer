Piper impunctatum

Piper impunctatum, commonly known as the scarlet piper or red pepper plant, is a tropical vine native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and heart-shaped leaves with a slightly pubescent surface. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into bright red, berry-like fruits. These fruits are edible and have a spicy, peppery flavor, making them a valuable crop in some regions.

It is often cultivated for both its ornamental value and its use in traditional medicine.
