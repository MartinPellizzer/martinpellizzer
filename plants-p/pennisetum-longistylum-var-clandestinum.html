Pennisetum longistylum var. clandestinum

Pennisetum longistylum var. clandestinum, commonly known as the secret grass, is a perennial grass native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, grass-like leaves that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant produces small, fluffy seeds that are dispersed by wind, aiding in its propagation. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, often found in open grasslands and disturbed areas.

Due to its unique appearance and growth habits, it is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant.
