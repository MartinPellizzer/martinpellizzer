Pinus elliottii

Pinus elliottii, commonly known as the slash pine, is a tall evergreen conifer native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its long, needle-like leaves that grow in bundles of two, and its distinctive pine cones that are typically yellow when mature. The tree produces a resinous sap that has been historically used for various purposes, including the production of turpentine. It thrives in wetland habitats and is often found in coastal areas and swamp forests.

The slash pine plays an important ecological role in its native range, providing habitat and contributing to soil stability.
