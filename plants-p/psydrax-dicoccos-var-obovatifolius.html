Psydrax dicoccos var. obovatifolius

Psydrax dicoccos var. obovatifolius, commonly known as the African dandelion, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and lanceolate leaves with a slightly hairy surface. The plant produces yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by distinctive seed capsules that release seeds with tufts of silky hair. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodland or grassland habitats.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in some African cultures.
