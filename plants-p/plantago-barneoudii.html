Plantago barneoudii

Plantago barneoudii, commonly known as the Barneoud's plantain, is a perennial herb native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its rosette of broad, fleshy leaves that store water, aiding in its survival in dry environments. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in a spike, which are pollinated primarily by wind. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to access deep soil moisture.

This species is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
