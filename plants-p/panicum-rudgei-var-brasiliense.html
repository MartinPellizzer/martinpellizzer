Panicum rudgei var. brasiliense

Panicum rudgei var. brasiliense, commonly known as Brazilian millet, is a perennial grass native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny grains, typical of the Poaceae family. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, often found in grasslands and disturbed areas.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential as a drought-tolerant forage crop.
