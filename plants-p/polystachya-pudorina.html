Polystachya pudorina

Polystachya pudorina, commonly known as the velvet-leafed orchid, is a species of orchid native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, velvety foliage and clusters of small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often in forest understories. Its roots are fibrous and adapted for nutrient absorption in poor soils.

The orchid's unique appearance and habitat make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
