Platylepis viscosa

Platylepis viscosa, commonly known as the sticky-leaved heath, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage that is covered in a fine, sticky resin, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological importance, it is a valuable component of its native ecosystems.
