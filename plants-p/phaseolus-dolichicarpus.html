Phaseolus dolichicarpus

Phaseolus dolichicarpus, commonly known as the long-seed bean, is a species of legume native to South America.

It is characterized by its elongated, narrow pods that contain small, round seeds. The plant typically grows as an annual herb with trifoliate leaves and produces flowers in shades of white or pink. It is valued for its nutritional content, being rich in protein and fiber.

The botanical features of Phaseolus dolichicarpus make it an important crop in certain regions for both human consumption and animal feed.
