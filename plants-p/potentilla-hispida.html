Potentilla hispida

Potentilla hispida, commonly known as rough cinquefoil, is a perennial flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it a rough texture. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or rocky areas.

Potentilla hispida is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to attract pollinators.
