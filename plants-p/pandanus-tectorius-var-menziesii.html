Pandanus tectorius var. menziesii

Pandanus tectorius var. menziesii, commonly known as the coconut pandanus, is a species native to the Pacific Islands.

It is a tall, slender tree that can grow up to 10 meters in height, with long, narrow leaves arranged in a spiral pattern. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers followed by fleshy, fibrous fruits that are used for various purposes. Its roots and leaves are often utilized in traditional crafts and as a source of fiber.

The plant thrives in coastal areas and is well adapted to salty, humid environments.
