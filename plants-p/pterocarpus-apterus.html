Pterocarpus apterus

Pterocarpus apterus, commonly known as the Indian rosewood, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its striking red or pink flowers that bloom during the rainy season. The tree produces flat, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. Its heartwood is highly valued for its dark color and fine grain, making it a prized material in traditional woodworking.

The plant also plays an important ecological role in its native habitats, providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
