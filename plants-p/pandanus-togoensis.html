Pandanus togoensis

Pandanus togoensis, commonly known as the Togo pandanus, is a tropical plant native to West Africa.

It is a member of the Pandanaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and long, narrow leaves. The plant produces inflorescences that bear small, fragrant flowers, which are often used in traditional crafts and culinary applications. Its fibrous leaves are utilized for making thatch, mats, and other woven goods.

The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and riverine habitats.
