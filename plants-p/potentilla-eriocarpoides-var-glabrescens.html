Potentilla eriocarpoides var. glabrescens

Potentilla eriocarpoides var. glabrescens, commonly known as the smooth potentilla, is a low-growing shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves that become glabrous, or smooth, with age. The plant produces small, yellow, five-petaled flowers that bloom in early summer. Its leaves are typically elliptical or ovate, with a serrated margin.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes.
