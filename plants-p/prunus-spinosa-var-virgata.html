Prunus spinosa var. virgata

Prunus spinosa var. virgata, commonly known as the silverthorn or white thorn, is a variant of the common blackthorn.

It is a deciduous shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia, characterized by its thorny stems and small, white flowers. The leaves are typically elliptical and have a silvery underside, giving the plant its distinctive appearance. It produces small, dark blue berries that are edible but often tart.

This plant is often found in hedgerows and woodland edges, thriving in well-drained soils.
