Papaver argemone f. vinosorubrum

Papaver argemone f. vinosorubrum, commonly known as the red-purple poppy, is a variant of the Argemone poppy characterized by its striking, velvety flowers.

The plant features showy, cup-shaped blooms that range in color from deep red to purple, with a distinctive orange or yellow center. It is native to regions of southern Europe and western Asia, where it thrives in well-drained soils. The leaves are lance-shaped and often have a waxy texture, while the stems are sturdy and upright.

This ornamental plant is often cultivated for its aesthetic value in gardens and landscapes.
