Pogonia vargasii

Pogonia vargasii, commonly known as the Vargas's bog orchid, is a rare and delicate flowering plant native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and small, greenish-white flowers. The plant thrives in wetland and swamp environments, where it relies on mycorrhizal fungi for nutrient uptake. Its limited distribution and habitat specificity make it highly vulnerable to environmental changes and human disturbance.

Conservation efforts are crucial to protect this unique species from extinction.
