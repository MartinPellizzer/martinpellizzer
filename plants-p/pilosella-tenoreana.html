Pilosella tenoreana

Pilosella tenoreana, commonly known as the Tuscan starflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features small, daisy-like flowers with white petals and a yellow center, blooming in late spring to early summer. Pilosella tenoreana typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
