Protea thyoides

Protea thyoides, commonly known as the silver protea, is a striking South African plant belonging to the Proteaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, fluffy, white to pale pink inflorescence that resembles a spherical cloud, giving it its common name. The plant is native to the Western and Eastern Cape provinces of South Africa, where it thrives in rocky, well-drained soils. Its flowers are pollinated primarily by birds, particularly sunbirds, which are attracted to its nectar.

The protea is also valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
