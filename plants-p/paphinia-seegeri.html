Paphinia seegeri

Paphinia seegeri, commonly known as the Seeger's paphiopedilum, is a rare and unique orchid species native to the Philippines.

It is characterized by its dark green, velvety leaves and striking, showy flowers with a distinctive lip. The plant produces fragrant, yellowish-white blooms that are often adorned with purple markings. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a conservation concern.
