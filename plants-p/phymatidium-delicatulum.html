Phymatidium delicatulum

Phymatidium delicatulum, commonly known as the delicate phymatidium, is a small, herbaceous plant native to the wetlands of South America.

It typically grows in dense clusters, with slender, wiry stems that often root at the nodes. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often covered in a fine, silky coating.

Phymatidium delicatulum is adapted to waterlogged soils and plays a role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems.
