Pultenaea sylvatica

Pultenaea sylvatica, commonly known as the silver wattle, is a flowering plant native to eastern Australia.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in heathlands and open forests. The plant features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. Its leaves are narrow and often have a silvery or greyish underside, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Pultenaea sylvatica is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and seasonal blooms.
