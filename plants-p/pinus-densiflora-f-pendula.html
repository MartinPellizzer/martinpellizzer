Pinus densiflora f. pendula

Pinus densiflora f. pendula, commonly known as the weeping Japanese black pine, is a unique variant of the Japanese black pine characterized by its cascading, drooping branches.

This form exhibits a distinctive pendulous growth pattern, creating a gracefully flowing appearance that sets it apart from the upright form of the species. Botanically, it retains the typical features of Pinus densiflora, including needle-like leaves and cone-bearing structures. The plant is native to Japan and thrives in well-drained soils with full sun exposure.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and parks due to its striking visual form.
