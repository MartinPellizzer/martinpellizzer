Pothomorphe umbellata

Pothomorphe umbellata, commonly known as the umbrella plant, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its rosette of fleshy, blue-green leaves that are covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and rockeries.
