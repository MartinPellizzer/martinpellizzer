Pterocactus skottsbergii

Pterocactus skottsbergii, commonly known as the Skottsberg's pterocactus, is a species of cactus native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a small, globular cactus with a short, thick stem and a dense covering of spines. The plant features areoles, which are specialized structures from which spines, flowers, and branches emerge. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange and bloom during the warmer months.

This cactus is adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water and well-draining soil to thrive.
