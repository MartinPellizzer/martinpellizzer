Potentilla cappadocica

Potentilla cappadocica, commonly known as Cappadocian cinquefoil, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its low-growing habit and clusters of small, yellow flowers. The plant typically thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes. Its foliage consists of palmately divided leaves, which are often covered in fine hairs.

Potentilla cappadocica is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
