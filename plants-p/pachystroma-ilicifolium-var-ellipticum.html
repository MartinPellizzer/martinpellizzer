Pachystroma ilicifolium var. ellipticum

Pachystroma ilicifolium var. ellipticum, commonly known as the elliptic leatherleaf, is a rare and unique plant native to specific regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, leathery foliage that resembles the leaves of the holly plant, hence its common name. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often in forest understories where it can thrive in low light conditions. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are usually green or reddish and are followed by small, capsule-like fruits.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a botanical curiosity and of interest to conservationists.
