Panicum milioides var. macrostachyum

Panicum milioides var. macrostachyum, commonly known as giant millet, is a warm-season grass native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its tall, erect growth habit and large, fluffy panicles that produce copious amounts of seeds. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for cultivation in arid and semi-arid climates. Its rapid growth and drought tolerance make it a valuable forage crop and soil stabilizer.

The species is also used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive, airy appearance.
