Primula calliantha subsp. kiuchiangensis

Primula calliantha subsp.

kiuchiangensis, commonly known as the Chihchiang primrose, is a species of flowering plant native to China. It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink and white.

The plant is perennial and thrives in cool, moist environments, often found in forested areas at higher elevations. Its leaves are typically oval and arranged in a basal rosette, while its stems are upright and bear clusters of flowers.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate beauty.
