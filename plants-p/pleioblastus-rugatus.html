Pleioblastus rugatus

Pleioblastus rugatus, commonly known as the Japanese silvergrass, is a species of grass native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-green foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant has a clumping growth habit and produces small, inconspicuous flowers in the spring. It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its aesthetic appeal and ease of care.

Pleioblastus rugatus is also valued in traditional landscaping and is relatively drought-tolerant once established.
