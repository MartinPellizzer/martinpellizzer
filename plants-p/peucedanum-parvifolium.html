Peucedanum parvifolium

Peucedanum parvifolium, commonly known as the small-leaved parsley.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. The plant features finely divided, fern-like leaves that are aromatic and often used in traditional medicine. Its flowers are small and white, arranged in umbels, typical of the Apiaceae family.

The seeds are used in some culinary and medicinal applications due to their pungent aroma and potential health benefits.
