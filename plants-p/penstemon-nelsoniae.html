Penstemon nelsoniae

Penstemon nelsoniae, commonly known as Nelson's beardtongue, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems and clusters of tubular, two-lipped flowers that range in color from pink to purple. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils in open habitats such as meadows and slopes. It is a perennial species that thrives in full sun and is adapted to arid environments.

Penstemon nelsoniae is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a nectar source for pollinators.
