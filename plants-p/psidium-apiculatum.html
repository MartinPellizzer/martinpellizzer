Psidium apiculatum

Psidium apiculatum, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or small passionfruit, is a small evergreen shrub or tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically yellow or orange when ripe, with a thin, leathery skin. The fruit contains a sweet, aromatic pulp surrounded by a fibrous, inedible membrane.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of edible fruit in tropical regions.
