Phoenix atlantica

Phoenix atlantica, commonly known as the Canary Island date palm, is a species native to the Canary Islands.

It is a tall, slender palm with a single trunk and a crown of feathery leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into edible date-like fruits. It thrives in warm, sunny climates with well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value and historical significance, it is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
