Poa novae-zelandiae var. subvestita

Poa novae-zelandiae var. subvestita, commonly known as the subvestita grass, is a native New Zealand plant belonging to the Poaceae family.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and narrow, bluish-green leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and lowland areas. Its inflorescence consists of dense, spike-like clusters of small, two-rowed flowers.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat.
