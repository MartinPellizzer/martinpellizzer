Pavonia fruticosa var. angustiloba

Pavonia fruticosa var. angustiloba, commonly known as narrowleaf wood rose, is a perennial shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features slender, upright stems and small, showy flowers that range in color from pink to red. The leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

This plant is often used in native gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
