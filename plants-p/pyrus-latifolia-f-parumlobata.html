Pyrus latifolia f. parumlobata

Pyrus latifolia f. parumlobata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved pear, is a cultivated form of the common pear tree.

It is characterized by its narrower, more elongated leaves compared to the typical broad-leaved variety. The plant produces small, sweet fruits that are often used in cooking and baking. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and edible yield.
