Pimenta racemosa var. terebinthina

Pimenta racemosa var. terebinthina, commonly known as Brazilian pepper tree, is a species native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are dark green on top and pale underneath. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers arranged in racemes, which are followed by red, fleshy berries. It is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been introduced to other regions, sometimes becoming invasive.

The plant contains essential oils and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
