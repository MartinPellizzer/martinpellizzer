Pandanus banahaensis

Pandanus banahaensis, commonly known as the Banahae pandanus, is a rare species native to the island of Banaha in the Solomon Islands.

It is a member of the Pandanaceae family, characterized by its tall, leafy stems and large, feathery leaves. The plant typically grows in coastal or swampy areas, thriving in humid and saline environments. Its leaves are often used in traditional crafts and have a distinct aromatic scent.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered endangered and requires conservation efforts to ensure its survival.
