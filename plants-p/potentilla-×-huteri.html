Potentilla × huteri

Potentilla × huteri, commonly known as the hybrid cinquefoil, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features wiry, arching stems that produce clusters of small, yellow flowers in early summer. The plant has palmately divided leaves with five to seven leaflets, giving it a distinctive appearance. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens due to its hardiness and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.

Potentilla × huteri is also valued for its role in ecological restoration projects.
