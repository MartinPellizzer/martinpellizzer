Piper hylophilum

Piper hylophilum, commonly known as the black pepper plant, is a flowering vine native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its climbing habit and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, green berries that turn from red to black as they mature. The dried fruit, known as pepper, is the primary part used for its pungent flavor and medicinal properties.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is closely related to other pepper species.
