Phragmotheca mambitana

Phragmotheca mambitana, commonly known as the African orchid, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests or wetlands. Its roots are specialized for absorbing nutrients from the soil, and it has a complex system of aerial roots.

The flowers often have a strong fragrance and are pollinated by insects and birds.
