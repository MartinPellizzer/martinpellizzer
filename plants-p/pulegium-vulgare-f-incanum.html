Pulegium vulgare f. incanum

Pulegium vulgare f. incanum, commonly known as hairy pulegium, is a flowering plant belonging to the Lamiaceae family.

It is characterized by its hairy, gray-green foliage and small, tubular flowers that bloom in clusters. Native to Europe and parts of Asia, this plant thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodland edges and stream banks. The leaves are often used in traditional herbal medicine for their aromatic and medicinal properties.

Its distinctive appearance and ecological role make it a notable species within its native habitats.
