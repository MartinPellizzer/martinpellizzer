Panicularia latifolia

Panicularia latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf panicled grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It features slender, erect stems that bear whorled clusters of narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in paniculate inflorescences, which are characteristic of the Poaceae family. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along streambanks.

Due to its delicate appearance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens for ornamental purposes.
