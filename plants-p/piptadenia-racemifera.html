Piptadenia racemifera

Piptadenia racemifera, commonly known as the Brazilian bean or yellow copaiba, is a deciduous tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant produces elongated pods containing flat, seed-rich seeds that are used in traditional medicine. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in forested areas.

Its bark and leaves have been used for their medicinal properties, including anti-inflammatory and analgesic effects.
