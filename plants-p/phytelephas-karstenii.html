Phytelephas karstenii

Phytelephas karstenii, commonly known as the Amazonian butter tree, is a species of palm native to the rainforests of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate leaves that can reach several meters in length. The plant produces edible fruits that are similar in appearance to coconut, containing a creamy, nutty flesh. Its flowers are small and clustered in inflorescences, typical of many palm species.

The plant plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing food for various wildlife and contributing to the biodiversity of the rainforest.
