Prunus spinosa subvar. genuina

Prunus spinosa subvar. genuina, commonly known as the common blackthorn, is a shrub native to Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and small, dark purple berries that ripen in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a serrated margin and can grow up to 3 meters in height. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in hedgerows and woodland edges.

The berries are used in cooking and traditional medicine, and the plant is also important for wildlife, providing food for birds and insects.
