Polygonum vacciniifolium var. medium

Polygonum vacciniifolium var. medium, commonly known as the medium cranberry bush, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, characterized by its distinctive nodes and stipules. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that resemble cranberries, which are typically red when mature. Its leaves are ovate to elliptic, with a glossy surface and a reddish hue during the growing season.

It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in wetlands or along streambanks.
