Polemonium pulcherrimum f. montrosense

Polemonium pulcherrimum f. montrosense, commonly known as the Montrose form of the Eastern Bleeding Heart, is a variant of the species native to the eastern United States.

This subspecies is characterized by its distinctive pink to red flowers that bloom in early summer, typically in shaded woodland environments. The foliage consists of heart-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full shade.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
