Pimpinella verticillata

Pimpinella verticillata, commonly known as the clustered cowwheat, is a herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to theApiaceae family, which includes carrots and parsley, and is characterized by its finely divided leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and streambanks. Its fruits are small, dry schizocarps that release two one-seeded halves when mature.

Pimpinella verticillata is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory properties.
