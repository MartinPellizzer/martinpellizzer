Pentas monbassana

Pentas monbassana, commonly known as the flame flower, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant, five-petaled flowers that come in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth. Its foliage consists of opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy texture.

Pentas monbassana is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a decorative plant in indoor settings.
