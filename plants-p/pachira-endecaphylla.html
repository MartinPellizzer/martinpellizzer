Pachira endecaphylla

Pachira endecaphylla, commonly known as the Mexican money plant, is a species of flowering plant in the Araceae family.

It is native to Mexico and is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves and clusters of small, white flowers. The plant produces berries that contain seeds, which are dispersed by animals. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full shade.

Pachira endecaphylla is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and ease of care.
