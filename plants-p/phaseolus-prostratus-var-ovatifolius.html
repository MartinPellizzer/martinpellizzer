Phaseolus prostratus var. ovatifolius

Phaseolus prostratus var. ovatifolius, commonly known as the broadleaf runner bean, is a vigorous climbing legume native to Central and South America.

It features large, heart-shaped leaves that are ovate and often have a velvety texture. The plant produces showy, butterfly-shaped flowers in shades of pink or white, which are followed by pods containing edible beans. It thrives in warm climates and is valued for its ornamental appeal as well as its edible yield.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for both gardeners and agricultural growers.
