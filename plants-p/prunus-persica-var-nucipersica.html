Prunus persica var. nucipersica

Prunus persica var. nucipersica, commonly known as the Persian peach, is a botanical variant of the peach tree characterized by its distinct morphological features.

This variety typically exhibits a compact growth habit with a dense canopy, making it well-suited for smaller spaces. Its leaves are usually broader and more rounded compared to standard peaches, and the flowers are often more fragrant. The fruit is generally smaller, with a thicker skin and a sweeter, more aromatic pulp.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and unique fruit characteristics.
