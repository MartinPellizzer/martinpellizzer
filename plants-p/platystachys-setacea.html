Platystachys setacea

Platystachys setacea, commonly known as the silverleaf honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its silvery-white undersides on its lance-shaped leaves, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in clusters, typically in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and partial to full sun, making it suitable for gardens and landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal and ecological benefits.
