Pourouma guianensis subsp. venezuelensis

Pourouma guianensis subsp.

venezuelensis, commonly known as the Venezuelan areca palm, is a tall, slender palm native to the tropical regions of South America. It features a smooth, gray trunk with a single crown of large, pinnate leaves that can reach several meters in length.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in branched inflorescences, which are followed by elongated, three-angled fruits containing a single seed. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local communities.
