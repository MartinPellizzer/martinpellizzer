Phialanthus grandifolius

Phialanthus grandifolius, commonly known as the African star gooseberry, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage with elliptical, leathery leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, followed by bright red, fleshy berries that are edible when ripe. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Due to its ornamental value and unique appearance, it is cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
