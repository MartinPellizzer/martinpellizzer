Pombalia verbenacea

Pombalia verbenacea, commonly known as the Cape verbena, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and thrives in well-drained sandy soils. It has small, opposite leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

Pombalia verbenacea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens for its drought tolerance and ability to attract pollinators.
