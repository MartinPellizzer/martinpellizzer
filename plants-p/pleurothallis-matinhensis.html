Pleurothallis matinhensis

Pleurothallis matinhensis, commonly known as the Matinhensis orchid, is a species of orchid native to South America, particularly found in the Amazon rainforest.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and pink. The plant is epiphytic, meaning it grows on other plants without being parasitic, and it thrives in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are usually narrow and elongated, adapted to capture moisture from the air.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is a subject of interest for botanists and orchid enthusiasts alike.
