Panicum rigidulum subsp. pubescens

Panicum rigidulum subsp.

pubescens, commonly known as the hairy panicgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the southeastern United States. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and narrow, hairy leaves that give it its subspecies name.

The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as swampy areas and roadside ditches. Its inflorescence consists of dense, cylindrical spikelets that are often tinged with red or purple.

It plays a role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
