Potentilla caulescens var. grandiflora

Potentilla caulescens var. grandiflora, commonly known as the large-flowered cinquefoil, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features upright, woody stems that are often covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces showy, bright yellow flowers with five petals, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are compound, consisting of five oval-shaped leaflets, and are arranged alternately along the stems.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
