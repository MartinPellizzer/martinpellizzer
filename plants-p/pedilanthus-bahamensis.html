Pedilanthus bahamensis

Pedilanthus bahamensis, commonly known as the Bahamian candleplant, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean islands.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves and showy, yellow-orange flowers. The plant produces a milky sap that is toxic and can cause skin irritation. It thrives in coastal areas and is often found in sandy soils.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species.
