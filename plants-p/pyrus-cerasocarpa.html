Pyrus cerasocarpa

Pyrus cerasocarpa, commonly known as the sweet cherry crabapple, is a small deciduous tree native to eastern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its oval, glossy leaves and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in early spring. The fruit is a small, round apple-like berry that turns red to dark red when mature, often with a sweet and tart flavor. The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in horticulture for its decorative qualities.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for gardens and landscapes.
