Pilosella papyrodes

Pilosella papyrodes, commonly known as the woolly pappus dandelion, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant is distinguished by its hairy, grayish-green leaves and a fluffy, white pappus that aids in seed dispersal. It typically grows in open, disturbed areas such as roadsides and meadows.

Pilosella papyrodes is a perennial herb that thrives in temperate climates.
