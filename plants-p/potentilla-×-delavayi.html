Potentilla × delavayi

Potentilla × delavayi, commonly known as the Delavay's cinquefoil, is a hybrid shrub native to the Himalayas and parts of China.

It is characterized by its dense, sprawling growth habit and produces clusters of small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its leaves are usually compound, with five to seven leaflets that are serrated and covered in fine hairs.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its hardiness and colorful display.
