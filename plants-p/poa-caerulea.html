Poa caerulea

Poa caerulea, commonly known as blue grass, is a flowering plant belonging to the Poaceae family.

It is characterized by its fine, blue-green blades and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. Native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere, it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. The plant is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and along stream banks.

Its delicate appearance makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
