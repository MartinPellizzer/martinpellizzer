Pyrus spectans

Pyrus spectans, commonly known as the showy pear, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its clusters of white to pale yellow flowers that appear in early spring, often before the leaves emerge. The plant produces small, edible fruits that are similar to pears but are typically less commonly cultivated. Pyrus spectans thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for rocky or arid landscapes.

Its ornamental value lies in its striking floral display and its ability to adapt to a variety of environmental conditions.
