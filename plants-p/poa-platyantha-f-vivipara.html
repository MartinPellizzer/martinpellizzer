Poa platyantha f. vivipara

Poa platyantha f. vivipara, commonly known as the viviparous bluegrass, is a variant of the bluegrass species native to the eastern United States.

This plant is characterized by its tufted growth habit and fine, bluish-green leaves that are often slightly hairy. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers. One notable botanical feature is its vivipary, a reproductive strategy where the seeds germinate while still attached to the parent plant.

This unique trait allows for rapid propagation and establishment in suitable habitats.
