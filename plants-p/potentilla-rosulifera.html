Potentilla rosulifera

Potentilla rosulifera, commonly known as the Rosulate Potentilla, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit and its clusters of small, bright yellow flowers. The plant features compound leaves with numerous leaflets arranged in a rosette pattern, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in alpine and rocky environments.

Potentilla rosulifera is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and visual interest.
