Philadelphus kochianus

Philadelphus kochianus, commonly known as the Chinese mock orange, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has opposite, simple, and usually ovate leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for garden cultivation.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its pleasant aroma and showy blossoms.
