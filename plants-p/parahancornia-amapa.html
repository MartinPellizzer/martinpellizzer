Parahancornia amapa

Parahancornia amapa, commonly known as the Amapa prickly pear, is a species of cactus native to the Amazon Basin.

It is characterized by its spiny stems and clusters of yellow flowers that bloom during the dry season. The plant has paddle-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, adapting to the humid and tropical environment. It produces edible fruits similar to other cacti, which are sometimes consumed by local communities.

Parahancornia amapa plays a role in the ecological balance of its habitat, providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
