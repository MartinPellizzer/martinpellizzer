Polygonum plebeium var. effusum

Polygonum plebeium var. effusum, commonly known as the softleaf smartweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to North America.

It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swales, and stream banks. The plant features alternate, lanceolate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a soft texture. Its small, clustered flowers are usually pink or red, appearing in late summer to early fall.

The species is notable for its ability to thrive in waterlogged soils and its role in supporting local biodiversity.
