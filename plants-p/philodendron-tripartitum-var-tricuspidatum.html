Philodendron tripartitum var. tricuspidatum

Philodendron tripartitum var. tricuspidatum, commonly known as the Tricuspidatum Philodendron, is a tropical climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It features large, heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive three-lobed structure, giving it its scientific name. The foliage is typically a deep green with a waxy texture, and the plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are followed by red berries. It thrives in warm, humid environments with bright, indirect light.

This variety is popular among houseplant enthusiasts due to its striking appearance and relatively easy care requirements.
