Philoxerus ferrugineus

Philoxerus ferrugineus, commonly known as the rusty sand dollar, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its succulent, fleshy stems and leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or red, followed by edible, fleshy fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky soils and crevices.

Due to its adaptability, it plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for desert wildlife.
