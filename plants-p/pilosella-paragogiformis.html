Pilosella paragogiformis

Pilosella paragogiformis, commonly known as the creeping pilosella, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit and is native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia. The plant features small, yellowish-green flowers that are typically clustered in dense heads, with bracts that are hairy and lobed. Pilosella paragogiformis thrives in dry, rocky, or gravelly soils and is often found in open habitats such as meadows and scrublands.

Its botanical structure is adapted to withstand arid conditions, making it a resilient species in its native range.
