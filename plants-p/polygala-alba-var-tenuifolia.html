Polygala alba var. tenuifolia

Polygala alba var. tenuifolia, commonly known as the slender milk-vetch, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, pale green to white flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, and it produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

It is often found in coastal areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
