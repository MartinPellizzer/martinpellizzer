Paphiopedilum glaucophyllum

Paphiopedilum glaucophyllum, commonly known as the blue-veined orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique, showy flowers with a distinctive blue-green coloration on the petals and sepals. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments and has a pseudobulbous structure that stores water and nutrients. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb moisture from the forest floor.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in greenhouses or as a houseplant.
