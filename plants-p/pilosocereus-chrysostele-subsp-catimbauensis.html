Pilosocereus chrysostele subsp. catimbauensis

Pilosocereus chrysostele subsp.

catimbauensis, commonly known as the Catimbaua cactus, is a species native to Brazil. It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its cylindrical, ribbed stems that are typically covered with white, woolly spines.

The plant produces large, showy yellow flowers that bloom in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as bats and moths. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing on rocky soils in coastal regions.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
