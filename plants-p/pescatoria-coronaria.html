Pescatoria coronaria

Pescatoria coronaria, commonly known as the crown fern, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Cyperaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and small, clustered flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in wetlands or along stream banks. Its leaves are narrow and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Pescatoria coronaria is noted for its ability to thrive in wet environments and is often used in ecological restoration projects.
