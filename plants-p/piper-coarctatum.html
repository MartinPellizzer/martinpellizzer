Piper coarctatum

Piper coarctatum, commonly known as the narrowleaf pepper plant, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and small, opposite leaves with a serrated margin. Its flowers are unisexual, with separate male and female flowers on the same plant, and it produces small, green berries that mature to a red color.

Piper coarctatum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
