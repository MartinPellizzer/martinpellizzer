Philoxerus canescens

Philoxerus canescens, commonly known as the silverleaf tree, is a tropical plant native to parts of South America.

It is a member of the Malvaceae family and is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, silvery hairs. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. Due to its unique appearance and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists alike.
