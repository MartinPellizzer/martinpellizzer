Pouzolzia frondosa var. fulgens

Pouzolzia frondosa var. fulgens, commonly known as the flameleaf pouzolzia, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking, lobed leaves that range from green to red or orange, depending on the season and light exposure. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are followed by capsule-like fruits containing seeds. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in wetlands or along streambanks.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for native plant gardens and ecological restoration projects.
