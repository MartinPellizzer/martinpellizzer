Paspalum humboldtianum

Paspalum humboldtianum, commonly known as the Humboldt grass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It is often found in coastal and sandy environments, thriving in well-drained soils.

Paspalum humboldtianum is valued for its erosion control capabilities and is sometimes used in restoration projects.
