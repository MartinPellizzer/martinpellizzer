Piper platyphyllum

Piper platyphyllum, commonly known as the broadleaf pepper or flat-leaf pepper, is a tropical climbing shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many species of pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves and small, clustered white flowers. Its fruits are tiny, three-lobed berries that mature to a dark purple color.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has some traditional medicinal uses in local communities.
