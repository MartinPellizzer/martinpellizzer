Phaseolus melanospermus

Phaseolus melanospermus, commonly known as black-seed bean or dark-seeded kidney bean, is a species of legume native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its dark-colored seeds, which are typically elongated and kidney-shaped. The plant produces small, yellow flowers and has compound leaves with three leaflets. It thrives in warm, tropical climates and is often grown for its edible seeds.

The plant is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it a beneficial crop in agricultural systems.
