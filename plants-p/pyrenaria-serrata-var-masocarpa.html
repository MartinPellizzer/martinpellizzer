Pyrenaria serrata var. masocarpa

Pyrenaria serrata var. masocarpa, commonly known as the serrated pyrenaria, is a variant of the pyrenaria genus native to tropical regions.

It is characterized by its serrated leaf margins and distinctive fruit morphology. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, often in forest understories. Its leaves are usually broad and lance-shaped, with a glossy surface.

The fruit, which is the basis of its varietal name, is notable for its thick, fleshy pericarp and is often used in local cuisines.
