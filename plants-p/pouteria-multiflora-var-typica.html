Pouteria multiflora var. typica

Pouteria multiflora var. typica, commonly known as the Brazilian nut tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is characterized by its large, palmate leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into elongated, woody pods. The tree's most notable feature is its edible seeds, commonly referred to as Brazil nuts, which are encased in a hard shell. The plant thrives in humid, nutrient-rich soils and requires a warm, stable climate.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
