Pilosella glaucescens subsp. pseudauriculoides

Pilosella glaucescens subsp.

pseudauriculoides, commonly known as the narrow-leaf mouse-eared chickweed, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It features small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, typically from April to June.

The plant has hairy, narrow leaves that are often spoon-shaped at the base, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats.

This subspecies is part of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads.
