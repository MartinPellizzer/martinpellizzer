Polygonum hydropiper f. vayredae

Polygonum hydropiper f. vayredae, commonly known as the water pepper, is a variant of the water pepper plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist or wet environments such as marshes and along stream banks. The plant features small, greenish-white flowers arranged in dense clusters, which are borne on slender, erect spikes. Its leaves are alternate, ovate to lanceolate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

The species is noted for its ability to thrive in waterlogged soils, making it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
