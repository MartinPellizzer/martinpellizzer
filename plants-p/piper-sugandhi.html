Piper sugandhi

Piper sugandhi, commonly known as Indian black pepper, is a climbing vine native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, peppery fruits. The plant produces small, dark-colored berries that are used in traditional medicine and as a spice. Its leaves are also used in Ayurvedic practices for their medicinal properties.

The botanical features of Piper sugandhi include its opposite, ovate leaves and its dioecious flowering pattern.
