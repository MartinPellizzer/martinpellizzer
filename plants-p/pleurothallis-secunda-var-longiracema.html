Pleurothallis secunda var. longiracema

Pleurothallis secunda var. longiracema, commonly known as the long pedicellate pleurothallis, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pale yellow. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in the understory of rainforests. Its inflorescence is notable for its elongated pedicels, which give the variety its common name.

The leaves are usually elliptical and arranged in two rows along the stem, contributing to its unique appearance.
