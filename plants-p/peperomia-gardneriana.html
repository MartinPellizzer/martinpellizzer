Peperomia gardneriana

Peperomia gardneriana, commonly known as the Gardner's pepper plant, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of Central and South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its compact, rosette-like growth habit and small, waxy, ovate leaves that are typically green with a reddish or purple tinge on the underside. It produces tiny, inconspicuous white or green flowers that are clustered in spikes, and it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage.

Peperomia gardneriana prefers bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.
