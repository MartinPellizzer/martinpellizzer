Potentilla thyraica

Potentilla thyraica, commonly known as the mountain cinquefoil, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. The plant features small, yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, adding a vibrant touch to its natural habitat. Its leaves are palmately divided, with a hairy texture that helps reduce water loss in arid conditions.

Potentilla thyraica is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
