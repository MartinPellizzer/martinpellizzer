Philadelphus intectus

Philadelphus intectus, commonly known as the Chinese Mockorange, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has simple, opposite, ovate leaves that are usually dark green and hairy underneath. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it adaptable to various garden conditions.

Philadelphus intectus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and pleasant fragrance.
