Potamogeton torquatus

Potamogeton torquatus, commonly known as the twisted pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in North America.

It is characterized by its long, ribbon-like leaves that are typically arranged in a spiral pattern around the stem. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often clustered in dense inflorescences near the water surface. Potamogeton torquatus plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.

It is also used in phytoremediation efforts due to its ability to absorb nutrients and heavy metals from the water.
