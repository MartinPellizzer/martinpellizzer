Poa bergii var. chubutensis

Poa bergii var. chubutensis, commonly known as the Chubut grass, is a perennial grass species native to the Patagonian region of South America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, wiry culms that often form dense mats on the ground. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with numerous tiny spikelets, typical of the Poaceae family. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, adapting to poor soil conditions and low precipitation.

This grass plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for grazing animals in its native habitat.
