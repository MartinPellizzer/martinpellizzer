Plinia pedunculata

Plinia pedunculata, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or *Eugenia uniflora*, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and showy, pinkish-white flowers. The plant produces small, red, edible fruits that are often used in jams and desserts. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is valued for its ornamental appeal and culinary uses.

Plinia pedunculata is also appreciated for its ability to adapt to various soil types and its relatively low maintenance requirements.
