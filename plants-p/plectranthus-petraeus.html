Plectranthus petraeus

Plectranthus petraeus, commonly known as the Cape verbena, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows in rocky or coastal areas, thriving in well-drained soils. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant blue or violet flowers, which bloom throughout the year in mild climates. Its leaves are often grayish-green and have a distinctive, slightly hairy texture.

Plectranthus petraeus is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
