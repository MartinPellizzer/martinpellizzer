Polycnemum fontanesii

Polycnemum fontanesii, commonly known as the Italian water clover, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Polygonaceae, which includes species known for their distinctive leaf structures. The plant features a creeping rhizome with opposite, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It produces small, greenish flowers arranged in clusters, which are pollinated by insects.

Polycnemum fontanesii is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of water bodies, where it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils.
