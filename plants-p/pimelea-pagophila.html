Pimelea pagophila

Pimelea pagophila, commonly known as the snow pimelea, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is a low-growing shrub that typically reaches a height of about 30 centimeters. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the spring and summer months. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and the plant is adapted to grow in sandy or rocky soils.

Pimelea pagophila is often found in heathlands and is a characteristic species of the South West Australian biodiversity hotspot.
