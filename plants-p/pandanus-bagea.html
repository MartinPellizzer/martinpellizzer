Pandanus bagea

Pandanus bagea, commonly known as the Bagea pandanus, is a flowering plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It belongs to the Pandanaceae family and is characterized by its large, sword-shaped leaves that are often used in traditional crafts and cuisine. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically clustered in spikes, which are pollinated by insects and birds.

Its roots and leaves are utilized in various cultural practices, while its hardy nature allows it to thrive in coastal and wetland environments.
