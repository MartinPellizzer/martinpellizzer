Prunus serrulata f. megalantha

Prunus serrulata f. megalantha, commonly known as the Japanese cherry blossom or yozakura, is a striking variant of the Prunus serrulata species.

It is characterized by its exceptionally large, showy flowers that can reach up to 5 centimeters in diameter, making it a standout among cherry blossom varieties. The plant is native to Japan and is closely associated with the cultural significance of cherry blossoms in traditional Japanese festivals. Its flowers typically bloom in early spring, adding a fleeting yet breathtaking display of pink and white blossoms.

The botanical features of this variety include its smooth, pink-tinged bark and its ability to thrive in temperate climates with well-drained soil.
