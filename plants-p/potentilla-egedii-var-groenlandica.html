Potentilla egedii var. groenlandica

Potentilla egedii var. groenlandica, commonly known as Greenland potentilla, is a low-growing shrub native to Arctic and alpine regions of North America and Eurasia.

It features small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. The plant has wiry, often hairy stems and elliptical leaves with a leathery texture adapted to cold climates. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in tundra and rocky habitats.

Its hardy nature makes it a resilient plant in harsh, high-altitude environments.
