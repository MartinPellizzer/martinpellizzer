Piptadenia gonoacantha

Piptadenia gonoacantha, commonly known as the yellow wood or African yellowwood, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, pale bark and compound leaves. The plant produces yellow-orange flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by flat, pod-like fruits containing several seeds. It is often found in savanna and woodland habitats, and its wood is valued for its durability and use in construction.

The plant also has traditional medicinal uses in various African cultures.
