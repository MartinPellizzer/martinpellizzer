Pariana velutina

Pariana velutina, commonly known as the velvet pariana, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its soft, velvety foliage, which gives it its common name. The plant produces small, clustered white or pale yellow flowers that bloom during the warmer months. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a smooth texture.

Pariana velutina is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and greenhouses.
