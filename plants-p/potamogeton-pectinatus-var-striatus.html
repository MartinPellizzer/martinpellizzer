Potamogeton pectinatus var. striatus

Potamogeton pectinatus var. striatus, commonly known as the striped pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its long, ribbon-like leaves that are typically green with a distinct yellow or white stripe running along the midrib. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often submerged, and it thrives in shallow, slow-moving waters with muddy or sandy substrates. It plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various water organisms.

Its presence can indicate good water quality and is often used in ecological assessments.
