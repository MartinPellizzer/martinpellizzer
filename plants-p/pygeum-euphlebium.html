Pygeum euphlebium

Pygeum euphlebium, commonly known as the African wild almond, is a deciduous tree native to the dry regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the Proteaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, pale yellow flowers. The plant produces small, hard capsules that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid climates.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological significance, it is often found in savanna and rocky habitats.
