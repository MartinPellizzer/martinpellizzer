Piper paludis

Piper paludis, commonly known as the swamp pepper vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to wetland areas of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper plants, and is characterized by its slender, vine-like structure. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are typically clustered in spikes, and its leaves are opposite, ovate, and slightly hairy. Piper paludis is adapted to moist, humid environments and thrives in swampy or marshy habitats.

Its botanical features make it an important species in the study of wetland flora and ecological adaptation.
