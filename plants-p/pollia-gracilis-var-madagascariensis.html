Pollia gracilis var. madagascariensis

Pollia gracilis var. madagascariensis, commonly known as the Madagascar orchid, is a species native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, spiral-shaped inflorescence. The plant produces striking, colorful flowers that are often used in traditional ceremonies. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated for horticultural purposes.
