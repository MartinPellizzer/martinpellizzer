Pfaffia pulverulenta var. microdonta

Pfaffia pulverulenta var. microdonta, commonly known as Paraguay tea or yerba mate, is a species native to South America, particularly Brazil and Paraguay.

It is a shrub that produces small, yellowish flowers and red berries, which contain the stimulant compound caffeine. The plant is characterized by its aromatic leaves, which are traditionally used to make a stimulating beverage. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in humid, shaded environments.

Due to its medicinal and stimulant properties, it has been widely cultivated and studied for its potential health benefits.
