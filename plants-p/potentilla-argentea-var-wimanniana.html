Potentilla argentea var. wimanniana

Potentilla argentea var. wimanniana, commonly known as silver potentilla, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its silvery-white, woolly foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy perennial suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its compact growth habit and drought tolerance.
