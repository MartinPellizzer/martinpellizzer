Pilea amplistipulata

Pilea amplistipulata, commonly known as the broad-leaf pilea, is a species of flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, heart-shaped leaves that are typically bright green with a slightly waxy texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers that appear in clusters during the warmer months. It is a popular houseplant due to its ease of care and attractive foliage.

Pilea amplistipulata prefers indirect light and well-draining soil, making it suitable for indoor cultivation.
