Poa pratensis var. colorata

Poa pratensis var. colorata, commonly known as the colorful meadow grass, is a variant of the common bentgrass.

It is characterized by its vibrant, often variegated foliage that displays shades of green, white, and sometimes pink or red. This plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks. Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping and garden use.

The plant is perennial, with a clump-forming growth habit and produces small, spike-like inflorescences.
