Pogostemon patchouly

Pogostemon patchouly, commonly known as patchouli, is a perennial herb native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its dark green, aromatic leaves and small, pale purple flowers. The plant is renowned for its essential oil, which is extracted from the dried leaves and has a distinct, earthy fragrance. Patchouli thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil.

It is often cultivated for its medicinal and aromatic properties.
