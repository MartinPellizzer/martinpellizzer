Potentilla halleri

Potentilla halleri, commonly known as Haller's cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows to a height of about 30 centimeters. The plant produces small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are compound, consisting of five oval-shaped leaflets, which give it the common name "cinquefoil.".

Potentilla halleri is adapted to rocky and well-drained soils, often found in alpine environments.
