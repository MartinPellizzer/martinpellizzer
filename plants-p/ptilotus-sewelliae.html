Ptilotus sewelliae

Ptilotus sewelliae, commonly known as the "bottlebrush bush," is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, dense clusters of pink or red bracts that resemble bottle brushes, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically visited by bees and other pollinators. It is a shrub that thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it well-suited to harsh, semi-arid environments.

Its unique appearance and adaptability make it a notable species in Australian flora.
