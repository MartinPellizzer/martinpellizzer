Pogostemon reflexus

Pogostemon reflexus, commonly known as the reflexed pogostemon, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its square, hairy stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, pale blue or purple flowers arranged in whorled clusters, which bloom in the summer months. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or stream banks.

Pogostemon reflexus is valued for its aromatic leaves and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
