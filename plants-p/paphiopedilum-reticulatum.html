Paphiopedilum reticulatum

Paphiopedilum reticulatum, commonly known as the netted lady's slipper, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique, intricate floral structure with a pouch-shaped lip that is often adorned with a network of veins. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments and has long, strap-like leaves. Its flowers are usually pale yellow to greenish-white, with a contrasting purple or reddish lip.

Paphiopedilum reticulatum is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated for horticultural purposes.
