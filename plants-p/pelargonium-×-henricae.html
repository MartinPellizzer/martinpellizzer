Pelargonium × henricae

Pelargonium × henricae, commonly known as the chocolate geranium, is a hybrid species within the Geraniaceae family.

It is characterized by its dark, almost black foliage and small, fragrant flowers that range in color from pink to red. The plant is native to South Africa and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Its compact growth habit makes it suitable for container gardening and borders.
