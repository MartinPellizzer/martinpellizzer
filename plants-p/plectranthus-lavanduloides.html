Plectranthus lavanduloides

Plectranthus lavanduloides, commonly known as the purple verbena plant, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its aromatic, gray-green leaves and clusters of lavender-pink flowers that bloom in summer. The plant belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is known for its strong, pleasant fragrance. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance and ease of care.

Plectranthus lavanduloides prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.
