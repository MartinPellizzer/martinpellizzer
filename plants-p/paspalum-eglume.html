Paspalum eglume

Paspalum eglume, commonly known as spikegrass, is a perennial grass native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its slender, spike-like inflorescence that gives it its common name. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spikelet-based flowers. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in disturbed areas or along roadsides.

Paspalum eglume is sometimes used in ecological restoration due to its ability to stabilize soils and provide habitat for wildlife.
