Pelargonium × baldinianum

Pelargonium × baldinianum, commonly known as the scented geranium, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Geraniaceae family.

It is characterized by its aromatic foliage, which emits a strong, often spicy or citrus-like scent when touched. The plant typically features broad, velvety leaves with a variety of colors, including pink, red, and white, and small, showy flowers in shades of pink or red. It is native to South Africa and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its fragrance and visual appeal.

Pelargonium × baldinianum thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and indoor spaces.
