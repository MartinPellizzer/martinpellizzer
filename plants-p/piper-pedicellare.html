Piper pedicellare

Piper pedicellare, commonly known as the red pepper plant, is a climbing shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its small, clustered flowers and oval-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, red berries that are often used in traditional medicine and as a spice. Its botanical structure includes aerial roots that help it climb and support its growth in dense vegetation.

The leaves are typically lance-shaped with a smooth margin and a slightly glossy surface.
