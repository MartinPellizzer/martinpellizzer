Phyllostachys pubescens f. rigida

Phyllostachys pubescens f. rigida, commonly known as the rigid bamboo, is a variant of the silver bamboo species.

It is characterized by its stiff, upright growth habit and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery down. This plant is native to eastern Asia and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes. The rigid bamboo thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it adaptable to various climates.

Its unique appearance and hardy nature make it a popular choice for both ornamental and functional landscaping.
