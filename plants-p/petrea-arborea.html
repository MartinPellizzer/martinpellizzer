Petrea arborea

Petrea arborea, commonly known as the Brazilian jasmine or coral tree, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that can grow up to 10 meters tall, featuring a dense, spreading canopy. The plant is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves and clusters of fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in spring. Its flowers are borne on long, slender pedicels and are often used in traditional medicine.

Petrea arborea is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its showy blooms.
