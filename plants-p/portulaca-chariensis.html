Portulaca chariensis

Portulaca chariensis, commonly known as the Mexican portulaca, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of Mexico.

It is a low-growing annual herb with succulent, fleshy leaves and vibrant, showy flowers that bloom in shades of red, yellow, and orange. The plant thrives in hot, dry conditions and is well adapted to withstand drought due to its thick, waxy leaves. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds, facilitating its dispersal.

Portulaca chariensis is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its colorful blooms and resilience in challenging environments.
