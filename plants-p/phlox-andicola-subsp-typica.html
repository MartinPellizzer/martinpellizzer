Phlox andicola subsp. typica

Phlox andicola subsp.

typica, commonly known as the Andicola phlox, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink.

The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, producing nectar that attracts pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for native gardens.

Its foliage is aromatic and often has a slightly fuzzy texture, adding visual interest throughout the growing season.
