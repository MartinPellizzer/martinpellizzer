Psidium guajava f. cujavillus

Psidium guajava f. cujavillus, commonly known as the Brazilian guava, is a botanical variant of the common guava.

It is characterized by its small, smooth, and often red or yellow fruit, which is typically smaller than the standard guava. The plant thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soils and full sunlight. It produces fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by the edible fruit.

This variety is valued for its unique flavor and is often used in culinary and medicinal applications.
