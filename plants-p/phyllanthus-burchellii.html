Phyllanthus burchellii

Phyllanthus burchellii, commonly known as the African gooseberry, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its small, yellowish-green flowers and elliptical, waxy leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, red, fleshy berries that are edible and have a sour taste. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides.

Phyllanthus burchellii is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional African medicine.
