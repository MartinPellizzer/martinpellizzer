Pintoa chilensis var. glandulosa

Pintoa chilensis var. glandulosa, commonly known as the Chilean pintoa, is a perennial shrub native to South America, particularly Chile.

It is characterized by its glandular-stemmed structure, which gives it its botanical variant name "glandulosa.". The plant typically grows in coastal and temperate regions, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are usually narrow and covered with fine hairs, while its flowers are small and clustered.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience to coastal conditions.
