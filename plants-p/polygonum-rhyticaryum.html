Polygonum rhyticaryum

Polygonum rhyticaryum, commonly known as the red-rooted smartweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, characterized by its distinctive arrow-shaped leaves and clusters of small, pinkish flowers. The plant is known for its reddish stems and roots, which give it its common name. It thrives in moist soil conditions and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

Polygonum rhyticaryum plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
