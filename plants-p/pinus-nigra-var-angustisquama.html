Pinus nigra var. angustisquama

Pinus nigra var. angustisquama, commonly known as the narrow-leaved stone pine, is a variety of the stone pine native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, needle-like leaves that are typically arranged in pairs. The tree produces cones that are elongated and often remain attached to the branches for several years. It is adapted to rocky and well-drained soils, making it a resilient species in arid environments.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in landscaping and forestry.
