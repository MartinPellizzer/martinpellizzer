Prunus ssiori

Prunus ssiori, commonly known as the Japanese cherry blossom, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, five-petaled flowers that bloom in early spring, often in shades of pink and white. The plant belongs to the Rosaceae family and is closely related to other stone fruits like peaches and plums. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and turn yellow in the fall before shedding.

The tree is valued for its ornamental beauty and cultural significance in Japan and other parts of Asia.
