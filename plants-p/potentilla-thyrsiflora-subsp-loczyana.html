Potentilla thyrsiflora subsp. loczyana

Potentilla thyrsiflora subsp.

loczyana, commonly known as the Hungarian cinquefoil, is a perennial shrub native to the Carpathian region. It typically grows in rocky or calcareous soils and is characterized by its upright growth habit.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in dense, conical inflorescences, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are compound, with five leaflets that are usually serrated and hairy.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and attractive foliage.
