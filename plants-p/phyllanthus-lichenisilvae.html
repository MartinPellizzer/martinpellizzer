Phyllanthus lichenisilvae

Phyllanthus lichenisilvae, commonly known as the forest lichen-leaf phyllanthus, is a shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its small, clustered leaves and yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and undergrowth. Its leaves are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, inconspicuous fruits.

It is valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
