Psidium musarum

Psidium musarum, commonly known as the small passionfruit, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its fragrant, star-shaped flowers. The plant produces small, oval fruits that are typically green when unripe and turn yellow or orange when mature. The fruit's interior contains a juicy, aromatic pulp surrounded by a fibrous layer.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has limited use in culinary applications due to its tart flavor.
