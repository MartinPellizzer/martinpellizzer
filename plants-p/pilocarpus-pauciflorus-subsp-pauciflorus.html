Pilocarpus pauciflorus subsp. pauciflorus

Pilocarpus pauciflorus subsp.

pauciflorus, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to South America. It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are usually alternate and pinnate, with small, oval-shaped leaflets.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in spikes, followed by dark purple, fleshy berries. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in coastal areas and lowland forests.

The species is notable for its aromatic foliage and has been used in traditional medicine for its stimulant properties.
