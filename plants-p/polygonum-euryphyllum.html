Polygonum euryphyllum

Polygonum euryphyllum, commonly known as broadleaf knotweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, which includes other familiar plants like buckwheat and sorrel. The plant is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. It typically grows in moist environments such as wetlands and stream banks.

Polygonum euryphyllum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in wet conditions.
