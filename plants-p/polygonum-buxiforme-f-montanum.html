Polygonum buxiforme f. montanum

Polygonum buxiforme f. montanum, commonly known as the mountain boxleaf knotweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and dark green, glossy leaves that resemble those of boxwood. The plant produces small, clustered flowers in shades of pink to red, typically blooming in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it adaptable to a variety of garden conditions.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is often cultivated in rock gardens and as a ground cover.
