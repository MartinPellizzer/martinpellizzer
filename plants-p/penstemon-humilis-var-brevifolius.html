Penstemon humilis var. brevifolius

Penstemon humilis var. brevifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaved beardtongue, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The leaves are lance-shaped and typically shorter than those of the typical variety, giving it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or dry habitats.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is a popular choice for native plant gardens.
