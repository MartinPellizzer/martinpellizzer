Petopentia

Petopentia, commonly known as the golden star orchid, is a species of orchid native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking yellow and green foliage and its delicate, star-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has a sympodial growth habit, with new shoots emerging from the base of the old ones. Petopentia prefers well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for greenhouse cultivation.

Its unique appearance and relatively easy care requirements make it a favored ornamental plant among horticulturists.
