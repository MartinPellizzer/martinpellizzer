Pimelea imbricata var. villifera

Pimelea imbricata var. villifera, commonly known as the narrow-leaved riceflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and its striking yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The leaves are narrow and linear, often covered in fine hairs, giving the plant a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in heathlands and open woodlands.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
