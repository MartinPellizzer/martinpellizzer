Phaca elata

Phaca elata, commonly known as the tall phaca, is a herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows to a height of up to 1.5 meters, featuring slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. Its flowers are small and yellow, arranged in dense clusters, and bloom from late winter to early spring.

Phaca elata is often found in grasslands and along riverbanks, thriving in well-drained soils.
