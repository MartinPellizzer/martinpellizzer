Plantago akkensis subsp. akkensis

Plantago akkensis subsp.

akkensis, commonly known as the Akkadian plantain, is a perennial herb native to the Middle East and parts of Central Asia. It is characterized by its rosette of broad, lance-shaped leaves with a rough, hairy texture.

The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in a spike, which are borne on a slender stem. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid regions.

This subspecies is valued for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally in herbal remedies.
