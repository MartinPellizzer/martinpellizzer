Pteronia glandulosa

Pteronia glandulosa, commonly known as the glandular pteronia, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its upright, grass-like foliage and showy, clustered inflorescences. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple, depending on the variety. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.

Pteronia glandulosa is also noted for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
