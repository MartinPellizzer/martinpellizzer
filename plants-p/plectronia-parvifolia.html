Plectronia parvifolia

Plectronia parvifolia, commonly known as the small-leaved plectronia, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, small leaves that are typically arranged oppositely along the stems. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are usually white or pale yellow, which bloom in clusters. Plectronia parvifolia thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical features make it a subject of interest in both horticulture and ecological studies.
