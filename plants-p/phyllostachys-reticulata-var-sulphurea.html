Phyllostachys reticulata var. sulphurea

Phyllostachys reticulata var. sulphurea, commonly known as the golden bamboo, is a variant of the common bamboo species characterized by its striking yellowish-green foliage.

This plant is a member of the grass family (Poaceae) and is native to eastern Asia, particularly China. It exhibits a clumping growth habit and can reach heights of up to 10 meters, making it a prominent feature in ornamental landscaping. The leaves are lance-shaped and have a smooth texture, while the culms are hollow and segmented.

It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
