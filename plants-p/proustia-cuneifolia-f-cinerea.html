Proustia cuneifolia f. cinerea

Proustia cuneifolia f. cinerea, commonly known as the silverleaf proustia, is a rare variant of the proustia genus native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive silvery-gray foliage, which results from a dense covering of fine, soft hairs. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically borne in clusters along the stems. It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its limited distribution and unique appearance, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts of rare succulents.
