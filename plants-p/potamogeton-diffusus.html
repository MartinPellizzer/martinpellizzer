Potamogeton diffusus

Potamogeton diffusus, commonly known as the broadleaf pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in North America.

It has long, slender stems that can grow up to several meters in length, with small, opposite leaves that are typically green and slightly pointed. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are clustered in spike-like inflorescences, which emerge from the water surface during the summer months. It plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various waterfowl and invertebrates.

Its presence is often an indicator of good water quality in lakes and slow-moving streams.
