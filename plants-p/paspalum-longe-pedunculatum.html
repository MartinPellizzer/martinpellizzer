Paspalum longe-pedunculatum

Paspalum longe-pedunculatum, commonly known as the long pedunculate grass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and long, drooping inflorescences that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green coloration.

Paspalum longe-pedunculatum is valued for its ornamental qualities and its ability to stabilize soil in wetland environments.
