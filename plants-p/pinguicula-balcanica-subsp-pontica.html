Pinguicula balcanica subsp. pontica

Pinguicula balcanica subsp.

pontica, commonly known as the Pontic butterwort, is a carnivorous plant native to parts of Eastern Europe. It is characterized by its rosette of glandular, sticky leaves that trap and digest small insects.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as bogs and rocky soils. Its flowers are usually small and yellow, with five petals arranged symmetrically.

This subspecies is notable for its adaptability to various wetland habitats and its role in local ecosystems.
