Penstemon maguirei

Penstemon maguirei, commonly known as Maguire's beardtongue, is a perennial flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces striking, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to purple. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for xeriscaping.

Penstemon maguirei is also valued for its drought tolerance and ability to enhance native plant gardens.
