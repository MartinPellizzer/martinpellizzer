Pavonia mattogrossensis var. minor

Pavonia mattogrossensis var. minor, commonly known as the dwarf pavonia, is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or red in color, which bloom during the warmer months. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
