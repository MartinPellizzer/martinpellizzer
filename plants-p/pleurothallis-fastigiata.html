Pleurothallis fastigiata

Pleurothallis fastigiata, commonly known as the zigzag orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a small, epiphytic orchid that typically grows on trees or rocks in humid, shaded environments. The plant features a compact, branching structure with elongated, narrow leaves that help it capture moisture from the air. Its flowers are usually small and clustered, often in shades of white, green, or yellow, and are arranged in a zigzag pattern along the stems.

This unique growth form and floral arrangement make it a distinctive and sought-after species among orchid enthusiasts.
