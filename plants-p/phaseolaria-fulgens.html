Phaseolaria fulgens

Phaseolaria fulgens, commonly known as the golden phaseolus, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its striking yellow-orange flowers. The plant produces small, bean-like pods that contain edible seeds, which are often used in traditional cuisines. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three oval-shaped leaflets, and it thrives in warm, sunny environments.

Phaseolaria fulgens is also valued for its ornamental appeal due to its vibrant floral display.
