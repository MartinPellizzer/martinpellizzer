Papaver interjectum

Papaver interjectum, commonly known as the lesser poppy, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Papaveraceae family and is characterized by its showy, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from red to orange. The plant typically grows in meadows, woodland edges, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are alternate, palmately lobed, and often have a hairy texture.

Papaver interjectum is known for its brief but striking floral display, typically blooming in late spring to early summer.
