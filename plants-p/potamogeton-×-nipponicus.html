Potamogeton × nipponicus

Potamogeton × nipponicus, commonly known as the Japanese pondweed, is a hybrid aquatic plant native to East Asia.

It is a perennial species that typically grows in freshwater habitats such as lakes, ponds, and slow-moving rivers. The plant has long, narrow leaves that are often submerged, with some floating leaves at the surface. It produces small, greenish flowers that are clustered in spike-like inflorescences.

Potamogeton × nipponicus plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for various water organisms and contributing to water quality through its dense vegetation.
