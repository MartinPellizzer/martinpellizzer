Potamogeton curtisii

Potamogeton curtisii, commonly known as Curtis's pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant found in freshwater environments.

It has long, narrow leaves that are typically arranged in whorls around the stem. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in spikes, and it is often found in shallow, still waters. It plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.

Its presence is often an indicator of good water quality.
