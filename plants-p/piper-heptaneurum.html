Piper heptaneurum

Piper heptaneurum, commonly known as the seven-nerved pepper vine, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many species of pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its slender, vine-like stems and compound leaves with seven nerves, giving it its scientific name. Its small, greenish-white flowers are followed by small, round berries that are typically yellow or red when ripe.

The plant is often used in traditional medicine for its purported medicinal properties.
