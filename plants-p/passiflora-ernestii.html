Passiflora ernestii

Passiflora ernestii, commonly known as Ernest's passionflower, is a climbing vine native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful flowers that feature five sepals, five petals, and a crown-like structure of filaments. The plant produces edible, orange-yellow berries that are rich in nutrients. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped with a rough texture and dark green color.

Passiflora ernestii is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
