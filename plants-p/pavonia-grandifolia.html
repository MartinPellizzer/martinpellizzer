Pavonia grandifolia

Pavonia grandifolia, commonly known as the largeflower false mallow, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It is often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides, demonstrating adaptability to various environments.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardeners seeking native plants.
