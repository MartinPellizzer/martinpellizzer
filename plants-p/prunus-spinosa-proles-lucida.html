Prunus spinosa proles lucida

Prunus spinosa proles lucida, commonly known as the white thorned cherry, is a deciduous shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its sharp, thorned stems and small, white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, dark red berries that are edible but often tart. Its leaves are oval-shaped with serrated edges, and it thrives in well-drained soils.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to wildlife.
