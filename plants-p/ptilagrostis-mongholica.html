Ptilagrostis mongholica

Ptilagrostis mongholica, commonly known as the Mongolian hairgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the grasslands of Mongolia and adjacent regions.

It is characterized by its fine, hair-like leaves and produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats and is adapted to withstand harsh environmental conditions. It plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies in arid and semi-arid regions.
