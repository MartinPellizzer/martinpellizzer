Pittosporum pulgarense

Pittosporum pulgarense, commonly known as the New Zealand pittosporum, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of 3 to 6 meters. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath, and it produces fragrant, white or pale yellow flowers in clusters. The fruit is a small, round berry that turns red when mature, containing several seeds.

It is often found in coastal and lowland areas, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun.
