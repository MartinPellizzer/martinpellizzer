Paspalum loliaceum

Paspalum loliaceum, commonly known as the silver grass or silver needle grass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Asia and Australia.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-blue foliage and slender, often hairy stems. The plant typically grows in wet or marshy environments, making it a common sight in wetlands and along water bodies. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets that are often clustered in panicles, giving it a delicate, feathery appearance.

Paspalum loliaceum is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in moist habitats.
