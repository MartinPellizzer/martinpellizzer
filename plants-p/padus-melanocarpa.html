Padus melanocarpa

Padus melanocarpa, commonly known as blackthorn, is a deciduous shrub native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its dark, thorny stems and small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically a deep green. The plant produces white or pale pink flowers in early spring, which are followed by dark blue-black berries in the fall. These berries are rich in antioxidants and have been used in traditional medicine and culinary applications.

Its hardy nature makes it adaptable to a variety of soil types and climates.
