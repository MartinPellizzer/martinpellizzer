Pfaffia fruticulosa var. diffusa

Pfaffia fruticulosa var. diffusa, commonly known as curcuma, is a perennial shrub native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is characterized by its woody stems and dark green, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by orange or red berries containing numerous seeds. It thrives in humid, tropical climates with well-drained soil.

Due to its medicinal properties, it is often cultivated for use in traditional and modern herbal remedies.
