Pomaderris polyantha

Pomaderris polyantha, commonly known as the white-flowered pomaderris, is a shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves that are usually covered in fine, white hairs. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers in clusters, which bloom in spring. It prefers well-drained soils and is often found in open forests and woodlands.

Pomaderris polyantha is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
