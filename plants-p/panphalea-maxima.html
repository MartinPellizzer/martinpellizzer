Panphalea maxima

Panphalea maxima, commonly known as the giant panphalea, is a striking plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that can reach up to 15 centimeters in diameter. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous perennial, often reaching heights of over two meters. Its foliage consists of broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating.

Panphalea maxima is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in warm, humid climates.
