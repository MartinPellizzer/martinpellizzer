Polygala brasiliensis

Polygala brasiliensis, commonly known as the Brazilian milkweed, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its flowers are usually pale yellow or greenish-white, and it produces small, hairy fruits that contain seeds with silky hairs.

Polygala brasiliensis is often used in traditional medicine and is also a host plant for certain butterfly species.
