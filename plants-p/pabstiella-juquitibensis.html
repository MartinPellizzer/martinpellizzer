Pabstiella juquitibensis

Pabstiella juquitibensis, commonly known as the Juquitibense orchid, is a rare and endemic species native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique floral structure and vibrant coloration. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments within the Atlantic Forest biome. It features a pseudobulb with narrow, strap-like leaves and produces intricate, fragrant flowers.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered endangered and requires conservation efforts to ensure its survival.
