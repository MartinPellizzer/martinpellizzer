Pleurothallis mornicola

Pleurothallis mornicola, commonly known as the morning glory orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to the cloud forests of Central and South America.

It is a small, epiphytic orchid that typically grows on trees or rocks in humid, shaded environments. The plant produces delicate, fragrant flowers that are often white or pale pink, with intricate patterns on the petals. Its foliage consists of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern.

Pleurothallis mornicola is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated by enthusiasts due to its unique appearance and ease of care.
