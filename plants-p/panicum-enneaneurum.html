Panicum enneaneurum

Panicum enneaneurum, commonly known as the nine-veined grass, is a species of grass native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences, which are typical of the grass family Poaceae. Its flowering period usually occurs during the late summer and early fall.

Panicum enneaneurum is often found in open, disturbed habitats such as fields and roadsides.
