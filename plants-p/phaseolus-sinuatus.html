Phaseolus sinuatus

Phaseolus sinuatus, commonly known as the scarlet runner bean, is a climbing legume native to the Americas.

It produces vibrant red or yellow flowers and edible beans that are often used in culinary applications. The plant has compound leaves with a distinctive sinuate or wavy margin, which is a key identifying feature. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Its botanical significance lies in its role in both traditional agriculture and ecological systems.
