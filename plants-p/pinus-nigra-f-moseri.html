Pinus nigra f. moseri

Pinus nigra f. moseri, commonly known as the Moser's black pine, is a rare and distinctive variant of the black pine.

It is characterized by its dark, almost black bark and dense, conical crown. The foliage consists of short, stiff needles that are typically a deep green color. This subspecies is native to the mountains of southern Europe, particularly in regions with a Mediterranean climate.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and specialized collections.
