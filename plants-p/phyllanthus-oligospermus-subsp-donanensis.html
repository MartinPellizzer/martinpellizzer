Phyllanthus oligospermus subsp. donanensis

Phyllanthus oligospermus subsp.

donanensis, commonly known as the Brazilian pear, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Brazil. It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are often elliptical or ovate in shape.

The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by small, fleshy berries. It thrives in moist, tropical environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
