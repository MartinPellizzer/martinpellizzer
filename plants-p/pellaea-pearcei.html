Pellaea pearcei

Pellaea pearcei, commonly known as the pearce's rock fern, is a perennial fern native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and small, leathery, evergreen leaves that form dense, mat-like clusters. The plant produces spore-bearing leaves, or sporophylls, that are distinct from the vegetative fronds. Pellaea pearcei thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments.

Its unique morphology and adaptation to harsh conditions make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
