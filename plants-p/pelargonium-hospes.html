Pelargonium hospes

Pelargonium hospes, commonly known as the Cape geranium, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, showy flowers that range in color from pink to purple. The leaves are typically rounded and slightly hairy, with a fragrant scent when crushed. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

This species is known for its resilience and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
