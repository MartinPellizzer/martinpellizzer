Piper perbarbatum

Piper perbarbatum, commonly known as the black pepper plant, is a climbing shrub native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves and small, greenish-white flowers. The plant produces berries that mature to a dark red color, containing the aromatic seeds used as a spice. Its botanical features include a woody stem, compound leaves, and a unique reproductive system involving both male and female flowers on the same plant.

The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
