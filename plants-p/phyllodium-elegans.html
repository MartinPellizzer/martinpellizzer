Phyllodium elegans

Phyllodium elegans, commonly known as the elegant phyllodium, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its unique, narrow, leaf-like structures called phyllodes. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, showcasing adaptations to drought conditions. Its small, pea-like flowers are usually pale yellow or greenish-white, and it produces pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Phyllodium elegans is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
