Pleurothallis rinkei

Pleurothallis rinkei, commonly known as the silver moon orchid, is a species of orchid native to the cloud forests of Central and South America.

It is a small, epiphytic plant that typically grows on trees in humid, shaded environments. The plant produces delicate, white or pale green flowers that are often clustered in small inflorescences. Its leaves are narrow and strap-like, adapted for efficient water absorption in its natural habitat.

Due to its striking appearance and rarity, it is highly sought after by orchid enthusiasts and conservationists alike.
