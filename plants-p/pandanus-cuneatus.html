Pandanus cuneatus

Pandanus cuneatus, commonly known as the narrow-leaf pandanus, is a tropical plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and long, narrow leaves that are often used in traditional crafts. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically grouped in spikes, followed by fibrous fruits. It thrives in coastal and wetland areas, often growing in sandy or muddy soils.

Its unique appearance and cultural significance make it an important species in the region's flora.
