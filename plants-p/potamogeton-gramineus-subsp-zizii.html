Potamogeton gramineus subsp. zizii

Potamogeton gramineus subsp.

zizii, commonly known as the narrow-leaved pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in Europe and parts of Asia. It has long, narrow leaves that are typically arranged in whorls around the stem, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often submerged.

This species thrives in slow-moving to still waters, such as lakes, ponds, and canals, where it can form dense underwater vegetation. Its rhizomes allow it to spread vegetatively, contributing to the structure of aquatic ecosystems.

The plant plays a role in water filtration and provides habitat for various aquatic organisms.
