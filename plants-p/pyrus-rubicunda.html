Pyrus rubicunda

Pyrus rubicunda, commonly known as the red-berried pear, is a species of flowering plant in the genus Pyrus.

It is native to eastern Asia, particularly China, where it grows in forested areas and mountainous regions. The plant produces small, yellowish-green leaves and is characterized by its distinctive red or reddish-purple fruits. Its flowers are typically white or pale yellow, and it blooms in the spring.

Pyrus rubicunda is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its edible fruits.
