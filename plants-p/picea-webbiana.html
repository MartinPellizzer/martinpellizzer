Picea webbiana

Picea webbiana, commonly known as the Webb's spruce, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to the Himalayas.

It typically grows at high elevations, often in cold and moist environments. The tree has needle-like leaves that are dark green and arranged in spirals along the branches. Its cones are typically small and woody, containing seeds that are dispersed by wind.

The plant has a distinctive appearance and is valued for its ornamental qualities in horticulture.
