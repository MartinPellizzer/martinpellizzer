Paronychia nivea var. capitata

Paronychia nivea var. capitata, commonly known as the white sand verbena, is a low-growing, mat-forming plant native to arid regions of the southwestern United States.

It features small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in summer, arranged in dense clusters at the tips of the stems. The plant has succulent, fleshy leaves that help it retain moisture in dry environments. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Its botanical structure is adapted to withstand drought and high temperatures, making it a resilient species in harsh climates.
