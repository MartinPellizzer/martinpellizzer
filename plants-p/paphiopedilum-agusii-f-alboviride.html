Paphiopedilum agusii f. alboviride

Paphiopedilum agusii f. alboviride, commonly known as the white-veined lady's slipper, is a rare and distinctive orchid species native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique, intricate flower structure with a prominent, pouch-like lip that is often marked with white veins. The plant typically grows as a terrestrial orchid, preferring shaded, humid environments with well-drained soil. Its foliage consists of broad, strap-like leaves that are usually dark green in color.

This orchid is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated by enthusiasts due to its rarity and elegance.
