Pandanus leucanthus

Pandanus leucanthus, commonly known as the white-flowered pandanus, is a tropical plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, palm-like leaves that are often used in traditional crafts. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, which bloom in clusters at the tips of the leaves. Its roots and leaves are sometimes used in local cuisines and medicinal preparations.

The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and requires a warm, humid climate to flourish.
