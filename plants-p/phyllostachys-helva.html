Phyllostachys helva

Phyllostachys helva, commonly known as the golden bamboo, is a fast-growing grass species native to China.

It is characterized by its slender, golden-yellow culms that add a striking visual contrast to its green leaves. This bamboo thrives in temperate climates and prefers well-drained soil. It is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its aesthetic appeal and ability to form dense clumps.

Phyllostachys helva is also valued for its role in erosion control and as a sustainable material in crafts and construction.
