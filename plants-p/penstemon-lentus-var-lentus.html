Penstemon lentus var. lentus

Penstemon lentus var. lentus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved beardtongue, is a perennial plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and tubular, two-lipped flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its drought-tolerant nature. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

This species is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
