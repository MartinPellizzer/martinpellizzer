Pisonia riedeliana var. oxyphylla

Pisonia riedeliana var. oxyphylla, commonly known as the red-berried pisonia, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean islands.

It is characterized by its showy, clustered pink or red flowers that bloom in the summer months. The plant produces small, fleshy berries that are typically red when mature, which are an important food source for local wildlife. It thrives in coastal areas and is often found in sandy or limestone soils.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in tropical ecosystems.
