Phoradendron lindemanii

Phoradendron lindemanii, commonly known as the Lindeman's mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches to the branches of host trees, primarily in the genus *Quercus*.

It lacks chlorophyll and relies on its host for water and nutrients, while producing its own carbohydrates through photosynthesis using the light it captures. The plant has small, scale-like leaves and produces white or yellowish berries that are consumed by various bird species. Its seeds are dispersed by birds that eat the berries, facilitating its spread to new host trees.

This species plays a role in ecosystem dynamics by influencing host tree growth and providing food for wildlife.
