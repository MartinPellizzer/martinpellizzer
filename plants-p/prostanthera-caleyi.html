Prostanthera caleyi

Prostanthera caleyi, commonly known as the pink matted mint, is a perennial shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant features small, tubular pink or purple flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and summer seasons. Its leaves are typically narrow and covered in a soft, velvety texture, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Prostanthera caleyi is often cultivated in gardens for its fragrant foliage and ability to attract pollinators.
