Picea obovata var. fennica

Picea obovata var. fennica, commonly known as the Finnish white spruce, is a variant of the white spruce native to northern regions of Europe and Asia.

It is a coniferous evergreen tree characterized by its slender, pyramidal shape and dark green, needle-like leaves. The foliage is typically dense and arranged in spirals along the branches. Its cones are small, woody, and contain two seeds, typical of the Pinaceae family.

This variety is adapted to cold climates and is often found in boreal forests and mountainous areas.
