Passiflora mixta var. mixta

Passiflora mixta var. mixta, commonly known as the mixed passionflower, is a species native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, complex flowers that feature a blend of blue, purple, and white petals, along with intricate filaments and a crown-like structure. The plant typically grows as a climbing vine, featuring heart-shaped leaves and tendrils for support. It produces edible berries that are rich in antioxidants and often used in culinary applications.

This species is valued both for its ornamental beauty and its ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
