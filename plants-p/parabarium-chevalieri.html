Parabarium chevalieri

Parabarium chevalieri, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, velvety leaves and clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows as an annual herb, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. It thrives in moist, fertile soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and agricultural fields.

Its botanical features make it both ecologically significant and a potential weed in cultivated areas.
