Podocarpus japonicus

Podocarpus japonicus, commonly known as Japanese podocarp, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to Japan and parts of Korea.

It belongs to the family Podocarpaceae and is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and small, cone-like fruits. The plant typically grows in mountainous regions and thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil. It has a slow growth rate and can reach heights of up to 15 meters in its natural habitat.

Due to its ornamental value, it is also cultivated in gardens and parks for its aesthetic appeal and resilience.
