Pelargonium × russelianum

Pelargonium × russelianum, commonly known as the ivy-leaved geranium, is a hybrid plant in the Geraniaceae family.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and heart-shaped, dark green leaves that often have a velvety texture. The plant produces small, clustered flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, which bloom throughout the growing season. It is widely cultivated as an ornamental plant for its attractive foliage and long-lasting blooms.

Native to South Africa, it thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight.
