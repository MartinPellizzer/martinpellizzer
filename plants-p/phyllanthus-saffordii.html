Phyllanthus saffordii

Phyllanthus saffordii, commonly known as the Safford spurge, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant has small, simple leaves that are usually opposite or alternate in arrangement. Its flowers are unisexual, with separate male and female flowers on the same plant, and it produces small, fleshy fruits.

The plant is adapted to withstand drought and has a deep root system to access water from deeper soil layers.
