Picea magnifica var. macrocarpa

Picea magnifica var. macrocarpa, commonly known as the giant sequoia, is a towering conifer native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its massive size, with some trees reaching heights of over 300 feet and diameters exceeding 15 feet. The tree has needle-like leaves that are dark green and arranged in spirals along the branches. Its cones are woody and contain large, edible seeds.

The bark is thick, furrowed, and provides protection against fire and pests.
