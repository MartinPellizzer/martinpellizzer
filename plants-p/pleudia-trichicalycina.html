Pleudia trichicalycina

Pleudia trichicalycina, commonly known as the woolly pleudia, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and clusters of small, pale green to white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and thrives in partial to full shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine, white hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The flowers bloom in late spring and early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
