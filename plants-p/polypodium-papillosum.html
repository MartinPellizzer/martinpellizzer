Polypodium papillosum

Polypodium papillosum, commonly known as the hairy shield fern, is a species of fern native to tropical regions of Asia and the Pacific.

It is characterized by its large, leathery fronds that are often covered in fine, hairy structures, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky crevices. Its sporangia are clustered in sori on the underside of the fronds, a common reproductive feature in ferns.

Polypodium papillosum is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
