Pouteria fulva

Pouteria fulva, commonly known as the yellow spondias, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its smooth, grayish-brown bark and oval-shaped leaves that are alternate and slightly serrated. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into edible, orange-red berries. Its fruits are rich in nutrients and are often used in traditional cuisines.

The tree is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types.
