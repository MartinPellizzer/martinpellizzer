Polyanthes

Polyanthes, commonly known as the "mock orchid," is a genus of flowering plants in the orchid family, Orchidaceae.

These plants are native to the Mediterranean region and parts of southern Europe, characterized by their delicate, fragrant flowers. Polyanthes typically have small, showy blooms in shades of white, pink, and yellow, often arranged in clusters. They are perennial herbs with strap-shaped leaves and are often grown as ornamental plants in gardens.

The genus includes several species, such as Polyanthes tuberosa, which is particularly popular for its ornamental value.
