Potentilla newberryi

Potentilla newberryi, commonly known as Newberry's cinquefoil, is a low-growing perennial shrub native to the western United States.

It features small, clustered yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. The plant has slender, wiry stems and lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Botanically, it is characterized by its compact growth habit and its role in stabilizing soil in arid environments.
