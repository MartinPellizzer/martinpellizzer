Pinalia ventricosa

Pinalia ventricosa, commonly known as the mountain bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Andes.

It is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in cool, moist environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a dense layer of fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

Pinalia ventricosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
