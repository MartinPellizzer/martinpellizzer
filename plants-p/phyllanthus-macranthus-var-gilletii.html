Phyllanthus macranthus var. gilletii

Phyllanthus macranthus var. gilletii, commonly known as the giant phyllanthus, is a variant of the phyllanthus genus native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree characterized by its large, elliptical leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by spherical, red fruits that are edible. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires a warm, humid climate.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and traditional herbal practices.
