Polygala salicina

Polygala salicina, commonly known as the sweet piper or silky piper, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its delicate, feathery inflorescences. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and is often found in sandy or rocky soils. Its small, tubular flowers are usually pale green or white, and it produces small, fleshy fruits that contain seeds.

Polygala salicina is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
