Peucedanum heracleoides

Peucedanum heracleoides, commonly known as horse's parsley, is a biennial or perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features finely divided, feathery leaves that are often mistaken for those of other umbelliferous plants. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in umbrella-like clusters, typical of the Apiaceae family. Its seeds are small and oblong, with a distinctive ridged surface.

The plant contains various bioactive compounds, including volatile oils and alkaloids, which contribute to its medicinal and aromatic properties.
