Pandanus palkilensis

Pandanus palkilensis, commonly known as the Palkil Island pandanus, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Pandanaceae family and is characterized by its robust, fibrous leaves that are often used in traditional crafts and as thatching material. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences with inconspicuous flowers, which are followed by triangular, spiky fruits. Its roots and leaves contain aromatic compounds, making it valuable in both culinary and medicinal applications.

Pandanus palkilensis thrives in coastal and wetland environments, adapting well to saline and humid conditions.
