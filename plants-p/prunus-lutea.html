Prunus lutea

Prunus lutea, commonly known as the yellow cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, often preceding the leaves. The plant produces small, sweet, yellowish fruits known as cherries, which are edible. Its leaves are typically oval with a smooth margin and a glossy texture.

Prunus lutea is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing food for birds and insects.
