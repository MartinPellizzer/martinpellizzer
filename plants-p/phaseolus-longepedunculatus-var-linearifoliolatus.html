Phaseolus longepedunculatus var. linearifoliolatus

Phaseolus longepedunculatus var. linearifoliolatus, commonly known as the narrow-leaf runner bean, is a variant of the runner bean species native to South America.

It is characterized by its elongated peduncles and narrow, linearly arranged folioli, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces edible pods that are typically harvested when young and tender. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and culinary uses in traditional cuisines.
