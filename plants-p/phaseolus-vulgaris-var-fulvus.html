Phaseolus vulgaris var. fulvus

Phaseolus vulgaris var. fulvus, commonly known as the golden bean or yellow kidney bean, is a variety of the common bean plant.

It is characterized by its bright yellow to orange seeds, which are typically smaller than standard kidney beans. This variety thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth. The plant produces upright vines that bear clusters of white or pale pink flowers.

It is valued for its nutritional content and is often used in culinary applications across various cuisines.
