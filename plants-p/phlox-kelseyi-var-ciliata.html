Phlox kelseyi var. ciliata

Phlox kelseyi var. ciliata, commonly known as the Sierra phlox, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils in mountainous regions. The plant features showy, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to purple, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its leaves are often hairy and have a distinctive, slightly lobed shape.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in harsh, alpine environments.
