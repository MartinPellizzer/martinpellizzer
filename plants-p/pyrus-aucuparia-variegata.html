Pyrus aucuparia variegata

Pyrus aucuparia variegata, commonly known as the variegated crabapple, is a cultivar of the crabapple tree native to Europe.

It is characterized by its ornamental foliage, which features striking white and green variegation on the leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in spring, followed by edible, tart apples in the fall. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

This variety is often cultivated for its aesthetic value in gardens and landscapes.
