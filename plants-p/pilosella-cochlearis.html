Pilosella cochlearis

Pilosella cochlearis, commonly known as the small white cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, with hairy stems and leaves that are usually oval or lance-shaped. It thrives in grassy areas and open woodlands, often in well-drained soils.

Pilosella cochlearis is typically found in temperate climates and is known for its delicate appearance and ecological importance to pollinators.
