Plantago coriacea var. pilosula

Plantago coriacea var. pilosula, commonly known as hairy plantain, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It features a rosette of broad, waxy, lance-shaped leaves that are densely covered with fine, soft hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in a spike, which are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous tiny seeds. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found along roadsides, fields, and waste areas.

This species is known for its medicinal properties and has been used historically in traditional herbal remedies.
