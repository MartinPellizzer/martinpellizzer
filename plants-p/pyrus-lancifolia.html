Pyrus lancifolia

Pyrus lancifolia, commonly known as the Chinese white pear, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves. The tree produces small, white flowers in early spring, followed by edible, sweet fruit. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit production.

The bark is smooth and grayish-brown, becoming fissured with age.
