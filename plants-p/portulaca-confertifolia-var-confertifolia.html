Portulaca confertifolia var. confertifolia

Portulaca confertifolia var. confertifolia, commonly known as the dense-stemmed purslane, is a succulent plant native to arid and semi-arid regions.

It features fleshy, prostrate stems that often branch out densely, forming a low-growing mat. The leaves are small, oval, and covered in a waxy coating to reduce water loss. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, with five petals, and bloom in response to sunlight.

This species is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it a common weed in disturbed areas.
