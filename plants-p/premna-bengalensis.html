Premna bengalensis

Premna bengalensis, commonly known as Indian nettle or Bengal premna, is a deciduous shrub native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the family Verbenaceae and is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves with serrated margins. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in racemes, followed by small, elliptical berries. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forests and along stream banks.

Premna bengalensis is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional systems of medicine.
