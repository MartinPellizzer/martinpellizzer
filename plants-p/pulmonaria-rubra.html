Pulmonaria rubra

Pulmonaria rubra, commonly known as lungwort, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of lance-shaped leaves that are often spotted with purple or red, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink, red, and white, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in moist, shady woodland areas and is known for its ornamental value in gardens.

Pulmonaria rubra is also valued for its medicinal properties, historically used to treat respiratory ailments.
