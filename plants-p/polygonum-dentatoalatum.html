Polygonum dentatoalatum

Polygonum dentatoalatum, commonly known as the toothed knotweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, which includes plants known for their distinctive arrowhead-shaped leaves. The plant features opposite, serrated leaves that are typically green with a slightly hairy texture. It produces small, clustered flowers that are usually pink or red, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Polygonum dentatoalatum is often found in moist, shady environments like stream banks and woodland edges.
