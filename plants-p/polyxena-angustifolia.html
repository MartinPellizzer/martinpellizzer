Polyxena angustifolia

Polyxena angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf polyxena, is a perennial herb native to the temperate regions of the northern hemisphere.

It features slender, upright stems that bear alternate, lance-shaped leaves with a distinctive silvery underside. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, typically blooming in early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is often cultivated in gardens and natural habitats.
