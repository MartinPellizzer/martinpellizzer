Potentilla supina f. villosiuscula

Potentilla supina f. villosiuscula, commonly known as the woolly trailing cinquefoil, is a low-growing shrub characterized by its soft, hairy stems and leaves.

It typically features small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant is native to regions of Europe and western Asia, often found in rocky or sandy soils. Its foliage is usually covered in fine, white pubescence, giving it a distinctive appearance.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and adaptability to various garden conditions.
