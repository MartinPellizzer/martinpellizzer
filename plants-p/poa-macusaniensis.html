Poa macusaniensis

Poa macusaniensis, commonly known as the Death Valley grass, is a perennial grass species native to the Atacama Desert in Chile.

It is adapted to extreme arid conditions, with deep root systems that allow it to access water from deep underground. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It is highly drought-resistant and can survive in soils with very low nutrient content.

Due to its unique adaptations, it plays an important role in stabilizing the fragile desert ecosystem.
