Pothos gracillimus

Pothos gracillimus, commonly known as the silver pothos, is a climbing vine native to Southeast Asia.

It features heart-shaped leaves that are typically variegated with silver and green hues. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers, though it is more prized for its ornamental foliage than its blooms. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Pothos gracillimus is a popular choice for indoor gardening due to its ease of care and ability to thrive in a variety of conditions.
