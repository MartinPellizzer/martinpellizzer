Pelargonium galpinii

Pelargonium galpinii, commonly known as the Galpin's geranium, is a species of flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink and white. The leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a slightly serrated edge. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

This plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
