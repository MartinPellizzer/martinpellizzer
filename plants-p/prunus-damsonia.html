Prunus damsonia

Prunus damsonia, commonly known as the Damson plum, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It produces small, dark purple to black fruits that are typically harvested in late summer. The plant has simple, ovate leaves with serrated edges and clusters of small, white flowers in the spring. Its fruits are valued for their culinary uses and are often used in jams and preserves.

The plant is also cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive spring blossoms and autumn foliage.
