Pseuderucaria teretifolia var. parviflora

Pseuderucaria teretifolia var. parviflora, commonly known as the narrow-leaf pseuderucaria, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to specific regions of South America.

It features slender, erect stems covered in fine hairs, and its leaves are elongated, narrow, and often slightly hairy. The flowers are small, clustered in dense inflorescences, and typically have a pale yellow to greenish hue. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The plant has limited cultivation outside its native range due to its specific ecological requirements.
