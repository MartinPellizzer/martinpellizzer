Phyteuma scheuchzeri var. scorzonerifolium

Phyteuma scheuchzeri var. scorzonerifolium, commonly known as the Alpine phyteuma, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It features upright stems covered in narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of yellow to orange, typically blooming in late summer. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments.

This variety is closely related to other members of the Campanulaceae family, sharing similar floral structures and growth habits.
