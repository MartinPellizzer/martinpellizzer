Passiflora lehmannii

Passiflora lehmannii, commonly known as the Lehmann's passionflower, is a climbing vine native to South America, particularly found in regions of Brazil and Peru.

It is characterized by its striking, complex flowers that feature a blend of purple, blue, and white petals, along with a crown of filaments resembling a crown of thorns. The plant produces edible, orange-yellow berries that are rich in antioxidants and have a tangy flavor. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a velvety texture, while its stems are woody and twining.

Passiflora lehmannii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported calming effects.
