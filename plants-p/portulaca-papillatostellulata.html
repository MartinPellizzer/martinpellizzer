Portulaca papillatostellulata

Portulaca papillatostellulata, commonly known as the star-pointed moss rose, is a species of flowering plant in the family Portulacaceae.

It is native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa, where it thrives in sandy or gravelly soils. The plant is characterized by its fleshy, succulent leaves and small, star-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to red. It produces capsules that contain numerous tiny seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Portulaca papillatostellulata is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its drought tolerance and attractive appearance.
