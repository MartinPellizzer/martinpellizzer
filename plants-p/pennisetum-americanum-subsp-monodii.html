Pennisetum americanum subsp. monodii

Pennisetum americanum subsp.

monodii, commonly known as giant miscanthus, is a tall, perennial grass native to the southeastern United States. It features narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color and can grow up to 6 feet in height.

The plant produces feathery, silver-green plumes during the fall, which contribute to its ornamental value. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for moist to wet conditions.

This species is often used in landscaping for its attractive appearance and ability to stabilize soil in wet areas.
