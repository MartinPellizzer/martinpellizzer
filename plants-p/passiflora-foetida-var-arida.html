Passiflora foetida var. arida

Passiflora foetida var. arida, commonly known as the desert passionflower, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its striking, complex flowers with five sepals, five petals, and a crown-like structure of filaments. The plant produces edible, orange-yellow berries that are adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments. Its leaves are usually thick and waxy, reducing water loss through transpiration.

It plays an important ecological role in supporting pollinators and wildlife in its native habitats.
