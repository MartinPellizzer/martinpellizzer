Paeonia × moutan var. carnea-plena

Paeonia × moutan var. carnea-plena, commonly known as the double peony, is a hybrid deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its large, showy, double blooms that come in a variety of colors, including red, pink, and white. The plant has compound, trifoliate leaves that are dark green and velvety in texture. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, requiring a cool climate for optimal growth.

The flowers are often used in traditional Chinese medicine and as ornamental plants in gardens.
