Paepalanthus ensifolius

Paepalanthus ensifolius, commonly known as the swordleaf cactus, is a species of flowering plant native to the arid regions of South America.

It is a low-growing, spiny cactus that typically forms dense mats on the ground. The plant features long, narrow leaves that are often mistaken for stems, a characteristic adaptation to reduce water loss in its harsh environment. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange and appear in clusters during the rainy season.

Paepalanthus ensifolius is well adapted to survive in extreme conditions with minimal water availability.
