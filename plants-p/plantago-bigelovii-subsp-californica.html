Plantago bigelovii subsp. californica

Plantago bigelovii subsp.

californica, commonly known as California plantain, is a perennial herb native to the western United States. It features broad, oval-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a dense layer of fine hairs.

The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in a spike, which are borne on slender stalks. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and roadsides.

Its fibrous root system helps stabilize soil, making it an important plant in ecological restoration efforts.
