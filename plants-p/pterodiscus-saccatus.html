Pterodiscus saccatus

Pterodiscus saccatus, commonly known as the bladderpod, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive inflated pods that resemble bladders. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in coastal or inland areas. Its small, yellow flowers are clustered in racemes, and the leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has also been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
