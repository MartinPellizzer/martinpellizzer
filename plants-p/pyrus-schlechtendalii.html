Pyrus schlechtendalii

Pyrus schlechtendalii, commonly known as the Schlechtendal pear, is a deciduous tree native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and smooth, gray bark. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, which are followed by edible, sweet-tart pears in late summer. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a slightly serrated margin, contributing to its ornamental value.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental appeal and fruit, though it is less commonly grown than other pear varieties.
