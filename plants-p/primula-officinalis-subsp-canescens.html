Primula officinalis subsp. canescens

Primula officinalis subsp.

canescens, commonly known as the downy primrose, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream sides.

The plant features basal leaves that are lance-shaped and covered in fine, white hairs, giving it a woolly appearance. Its flowers are usually pale yellow to cream, with five petals and a distinctive central boss.

It is valued in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
