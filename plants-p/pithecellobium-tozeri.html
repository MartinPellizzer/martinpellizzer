Pithecellobium tozeri

Pithecellobium tozeri, commonly known as the Tozer's acacia, is a tropical shrub native to the Pacific Islands.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by flat, pod-like fruits. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine, and the plant is adapted to grow in coastal and sandy soils.

Pithecellobium tozeri is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, which benefits soil fertility in its native habitats.
