Pinguicula agnata

Pinguicula agnata, commonly known as the Mexican butterwort, is a carnivorous plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its sticky, glandular leaves that trap and digest small insects. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as rocky crevices or forest floors. Its flowers are small and usually pink or purple, adding to its ornamental appeal.

Pinguicula agnata is a low-maintenance plant that thrives in cool, humid conditions with proper drainage.
