Pinus cembra var. pygmaea

Pinus cembra var. pygmaea, commonly known as the Pygmy Fir, is a rare and dwarf variant of the Swiss Fir.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit, typically reaching only a few meters in height. The plant features needle-like leaves that are short and densely packed, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in cool, moist environments, often found in high-altitude regions.

Its botanical significance lies in its unique morphology and ecological niche within alpine ecosystems.
