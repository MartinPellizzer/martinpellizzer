Piper heterobracteum

Piper heterobracteum, commonly known as the heterobracteum pepper, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species. The plant is characterized by its small, clustered flowers and distinctive bracts that differ in size and shape. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a slightly aromatic scent.

It is often grown for its ornamental value and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
