Poeppigia excelsa

Poeppigia excelsa, commonly known as the Mexican nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its dark green, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, star-shaped flowers that bloom in the evening. The plant produces violet to purple berries that are edible but have a bitter taste. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Poeppigia excelsa is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
