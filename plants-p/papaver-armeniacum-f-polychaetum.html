Papaver armeniacum f. polychaetum

Papaver armeniacum f. polychaetum, commonly known as the Armenian poppy, is a botanical variant characterized by its distinctive floral morphology.

It features showy, vibrant red or orange flowers with a prominent central stigma, typical of the Papaveraceae family. The plant has lanceolate, often hairy leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun, making it a hardy perennial in suitable climates.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking blooms.
