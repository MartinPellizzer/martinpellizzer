Pharbitis hispida var. imberbis

Pharbitis hispida var. imberbis, commonly known as velvetleaf, is a tropical annual herb native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, velvety hairs, giving it a distinctive texture. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by elongated, cylindrical fruits containing several seeds. It is often found in disturbed soils and is considered a weed in agricultural settings.

Its rapid growth and adaptability make it a notable species in ecological and agricultural contexts.
