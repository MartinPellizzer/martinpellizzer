Pabstiella decurva

Pabstiella decurva, commonly known as the bent-stemmed orchid, is a species of orchid native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, often curved stems and small, greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments such as forest understories. Its leaves are linear and arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem.

Botanically, it is noted for its unique floral structure and is part of the Orchidaceae family.
