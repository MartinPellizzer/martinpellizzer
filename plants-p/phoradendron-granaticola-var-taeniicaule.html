Phoradendron granaticola var. taeniicaule

Phoradendron granaticola var. taeniicaule, commonly known as the creeping mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on the branches of host trees, primarily in the tropics.

It has small, oval-shaped leaves that are usually opposite or whorled, and it produces small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant relies on its host for water and some nutrients, while it photosynthesizes for additional energy. Its berries are typically red or orange, containing seeds that are dispersed by birds.

This species is notable for its ability to form dense clusters on its host, sometimes leading to the weakening of the host tree.
