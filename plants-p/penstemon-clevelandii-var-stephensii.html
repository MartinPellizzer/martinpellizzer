Penstemon clevelandii var. stephensii

Penstemon clevelandii var. stephensii, commonly known as Stephens' penstemon, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun, making it well-suited for xeriscaping. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, attracting pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds.
