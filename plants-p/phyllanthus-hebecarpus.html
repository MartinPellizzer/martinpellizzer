Phyllanthus hebecarpus

Phyllanthus hebecarpus, commonly known as the African spurge, is a flowering plant native to Africa and parts of the Middle East.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant features small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, and its leaves are alternate, simple, and usually ovate or elliptical in shape. The fruit is a capsule that splits open to release tiny seeds.

It is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and antiviral properties.
