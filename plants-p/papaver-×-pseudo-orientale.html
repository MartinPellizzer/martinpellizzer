Papaver × pseudo-orientale

Papaver × pseudo-orientale, commonly known as the Persian poppy, is a hybrid species that exhibits a range of botanical characteristics.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant with showy, cup-shaped flowers that come in various colors, including red, orange, and yellow. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and features distinctive, lobed leaves that are often hairy. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.

The flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, adding vibrant color to gardens and landscapes.
