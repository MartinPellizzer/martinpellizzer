Panicum fauriei var. latius

Panicum fauriei var. latius, commonly known as the broadleaf panicgrass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that can reach lengths of up to 1 meter. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences that are typically green to reddish in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for ornamental and erosion control purposes.

Due to its hardy nature, it is often used in landscaping and as a component in native plant gardens.
