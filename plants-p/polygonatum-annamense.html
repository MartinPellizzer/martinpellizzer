Polygonatum annamense

Polygonatum annamense, commonly known as the Annam snake plant, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Asparagaceae family and is characterized by its upright, sword-shaped leaves that are typically green with a bluish tinge. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers in clusters during the spring or early summer. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in low-light conditions.

Polygonatum annamense is also valued in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
