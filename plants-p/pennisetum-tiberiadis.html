Pennisetum tiberiadis

Pennisetum tiberiadis, commonly known as the Tiberian grass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically green to bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences that are often pink or purple, adding ornamental value to gardens. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for landscaping in warm climates.

Pennisetum tiberiadis is also valued for its ability to tolerate drought and poor soil conditions.
