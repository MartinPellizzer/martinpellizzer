Potentilla thibetica

Potentilla thibetica, commonly known as Tibetan cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Tibet and adjacent areas in China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky slopes, thriving in cool, high-altitude environments. Its leaves are palmately divided, and the stems are often covered with fine hairs.

Potentilla thibetica is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been used in traditional medicine for various health purposes.
