Pilosella Officinarum Subsp. Pseudopilosella

Pilosella officinarum subsp. pseudopilosella is a lesser-known member of the Asteraceae family that thrives in rocky and alpine environments. This subspecies is distinguished by its compact growth habit and dense, woolly foliage that helps it survive in harsh climates. It is often found in mountainous regions across Europe and parts of Asia, where it adapts to poor soil conditions and extreme weather. Despite its resilience, it remains a rare and underappreciated plant in botanical studies. Its unique appearance and ecological significance make it a fascinating subject for further research and conservation efforts.
