Piper pseudopeculiare

Piper pseudopeculiare, commonly known as the pseudopeculiar pepper, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its climbing habit and compound leaves with serrated margins. Its small, greenish-yellow flowers are clustered in spikes and are followed by small, berry-like fruits.

Piper pseudopeculiare is primarily valued for its ornamental appeal and is occasionally used in traditional medicine.
