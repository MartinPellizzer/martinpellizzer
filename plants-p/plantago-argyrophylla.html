Plantago argyrophylla

Plantago argyrophylla, commonly known as silverleaf plantain, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its silver-gray, velvety leaves that are covered in fine, dense hairs, which help reduce water loss in dry environments. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers arranged in a spike, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.

Its leaves are sometimes used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.
