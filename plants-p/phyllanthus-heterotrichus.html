Phyllanthus heterotrichus

Phyllanthus heterotrichus, commonly known as the African buttonweed, is a shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its small, green, button-like leaves that are often clustered at the ends of its branches. The plant produces tiny, yellow flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences, followed by small, round fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.

Phyllanthus heterotrichus is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional medicine for various health purposes.
