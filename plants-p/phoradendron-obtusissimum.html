Phoradendron obtusissimum

Phoradendron obtusissimum, commonly known as the mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches to the branches of host trees, primarily in the Americas.

It lacks roots and absorbs water and nutrients through its stems, which are embedded into the host's bark. The plant produces small, white or yellowish flowers and red berries that are often consumed by birds, aiding in seed dispersal. Its leaves are usually opposite and have a waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss.

Despite its parasitic nature, Phoradendron obtusissimum plays a role in forest ecosystems by providing food for various wildlife species.
