Philodendron amplectens

Philodendron amplectens, commonly known as the climbing philodendron, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, dark green leaves that are often variegated with white or cream markings. The plant has a vigorous climbing habit, using its aerial roots to attach to supports as it grows. Its stems are typically reddish-brown and can reach significant lengths in suitable conditions.

Philodendron amplectens thrives in bright, indirect light and requires consistent moisture and humidity to flourish.
