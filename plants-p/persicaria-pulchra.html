Persicaria pulchra

Persicaria pulchra, commonly known as the beautiful knotweed, is a flowering plant native to Asia.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, which includes other familiar plants like buckwheat and sorrel. The plant features upright stems covered in soft, hairy foliage, and it produces small, clustered flowers in shades of pink or white. Persicaria pulchra is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens due to its attractive appearance and ease of growth.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full sunlight.
