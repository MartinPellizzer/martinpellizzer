Pitcairnia tillandsioides

Pitcairnia tillandsioides, commonly known as the silver pine bromeliad, is a striking epiphytic plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It features a rosette of narrow, silver-green leaves that are covered in a dense layer of trichomes, which help it retain moisture in its arid habitat. The plant produces tall, spathiform inflorescences with showy, violet to purple flowers that attract pollinators like hummingbirds. Its unique leaf structure allows it to thrive in low-light conditions by maximizing water absorption from air and rain.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to indoor environments.
