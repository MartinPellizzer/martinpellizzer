Plantago coronopus subsp. commutata

Plantago coronopus subsp.

commutata, commonly known as the lesser plantain, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters and features a basal rosette of lanceolate, hairy leaves.

The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in dense spike-like inflorescences, which are borne on slender stalks. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

The species is characterized by its ability to thrive in disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas.
