Pandanus ceratophorus

Pandanus ceratophorus, commonly known as the hairy pandanus, is a flowering plant native to the Indo-Pacific region.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, sword-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, hairy textures. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or green in color. It thrives in coastal areas and is often found growing near mangroves or in sandy soils.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its unique foliage.
