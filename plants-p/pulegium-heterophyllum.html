Pulegium heterophyllum

Pulegium heterophyllum, commonly known as Indian coleus, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It is a member of the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces clusters of small, tubular flowers in shades of blue, purple, and white. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and is often used in ornamental gardening.

Additionally, it has been traditionally used in herbal medicine for its purported antispasmodic and anti-inflammatory properties.
