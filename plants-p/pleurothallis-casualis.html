Pleurothallis casualis

Pleurothallis casualis, commonly known as the casual orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Epidendroideae subfamily and is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that typically bloom in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot. Its leaves are usually elongated and covered in a fine, soft hair, which helps in retaining moisture.

Pleurothallis casualis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in a suitable environment.
