Plectritis magna var. nitida

Plectritis magna var. nitida, commonly known as the silver hairgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-gray foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and streambanks. It produces small, greenish-white flowers in dense spikes during the summer months.

Its foliage is often used in ornamental gardening due to its unique color and texture.
