Pimelea menkeana

Pimelea menkeana, commonly known as the Menke's bottlebrush, is a flowering plant native to Western Australia.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 meter, characterized by its dense, cylindrical inflorescences that resemble bottlebrushes. The flowers are usually yellow or orange in color and are borne in clusters along the stems. The plant has small, leathery leaves that are arranged in a spiral pattern around the stems.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and heathlands.
