Prunus damascena xanthocarpa

Prunus damascena xanthocarpa, commonly known as the yellow-fruited cherry, is a hybrid species that combines traits from Prunus damascena and Prunus xanthocarpa.

It is characterized by its small, yellowish fruits that are typically harvested in late summer. The plant produces fragrant white flowers in early spring, which are often used in perfumery and culinary applications. Its leaves are oval-shaped with a smooth texture and a moderate growth rate.

This hybrid is valued for its ornamental appeal and unique fruit characteristics.
