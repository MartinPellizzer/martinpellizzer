Pemphis punctata

Pemphis punctata, commonly known as the swamp mallow, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds, thriving in moist, acidic soils. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and produces showy pink to red flowers in the summer. Its flowering stems are often hairy, and the plant is known for its ability to tolerate waterlogged conditions.

Pemphis punctata plays an important role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
