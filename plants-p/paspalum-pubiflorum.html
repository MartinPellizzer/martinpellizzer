Paspalum pubiflorum

Paspalum pubiflorum, commonly known as the silky grass or silvergrass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and narrow, silver-gray leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikes, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

Paspalum pubiflorum is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in aquatic environments.
