Polygala viminalis

Polygala viminalis, commonly known as the willow-leaf milk-vetch, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is characterized by its slender, willow-like leaves. The plant produces small, delicate flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, often arranged in clusters. It belongs to the family Polygalaceae, which includes many species with specialized floral structures.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and natural habitats.
