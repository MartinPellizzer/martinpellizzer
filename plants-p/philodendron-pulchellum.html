Philodendron pulchellum

Philodendron pulchellum, commonly known as the golden velvet philodendron, is a tropical climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It features heart-shaped, waxy leaves that are typically dark green with a velvety texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are followed by bright orange berries. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires consistent moisture and high humidity.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in greenhouses.
