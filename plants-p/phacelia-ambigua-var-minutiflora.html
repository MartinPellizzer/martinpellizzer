Phacelia ambigua var. minutiflora

Phacelia ambigua var. minutiflora, commonly known as the small-flowered phacelia, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a herbaceous annual with slender, branched stems and small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in early summer. The leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open areas such as meadows and disturbed sites.

This plant is valued for its role in supporting pollinators and its use in restoration projects.
