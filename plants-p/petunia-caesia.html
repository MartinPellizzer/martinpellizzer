Petunia caesia

Petunia caesia, commonly known as the violet petunia, is a species native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a flowering plant in the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its clusters of small, violet-blue flowers. The plant typically grows as an annual or perennial herb, reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers bloom in spring and summer.

Petunia caesia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and fragrance.
