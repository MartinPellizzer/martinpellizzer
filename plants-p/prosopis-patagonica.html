Prosopis patagonica

Prosopis patagonica, commonly known as the Patagonian mesquite, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pod-like fruits. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it resilient to drought conditions. It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.

Its wood is also valued for its strength and durability in traditional uses.
