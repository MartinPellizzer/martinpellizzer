Planchonia tetraptera

Planchonia tetraptera, commonly known as the four-winged pandanus, is a tropical plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It belongs to the Pandanaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, feathery leaves that are often used in traditional crafts and cuisine. The plant grows as a shrub or small tree, typically reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are long, narrow, and have a fibrous texture, making them suitable for weaving.

The plant thrives in coastal areas and is often found in sandy soils near the shoreline.
