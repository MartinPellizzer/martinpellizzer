Plectranthus puberulentus

Plectranthus puberulentus, commonly known as the woolly plectranthus, is a perennial herb native to South Africa.

It features hairy, gray-green foliage that gives it a distinctive, fuzzy appearance. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that range in color from purple to blue, typically blooming in summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and containers.

Plectranthus puberulentus is also valued for its aromatic leaves, which are often used in traditional medicine.
