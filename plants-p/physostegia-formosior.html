Physostegia formosior

Physostegia formosior, commonly known as the showy bluebeard.

It is a perennial herb native to eastern North America, characterized by its striking spikes of violet-blue flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it thrives in moist, woodland environments. It belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is known for its ornamental value in gardens.

Physostegia formosior is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
