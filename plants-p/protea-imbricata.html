Protea imbricata

Protea imbricata, commonly known as the Cape sugarbush, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Proteaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, showy inflorescences composed of numerous small, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in coastal fynbos vegetation and prefers well-drained, acidic soils. Its flowers are pollinated by birds, particularly sunbirds, and it produces woody, cone-like seed pods.

Protea imbricata is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens.
