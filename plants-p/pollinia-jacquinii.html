Pollinia jacquinii

Pollinia jacquinii, commonly known as the Jacquin's orchid, is a unique orchid species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive pollinia, which are compact masses of pollen that are transferred as a single unit during pollination. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to tree branches in its natural habitat. Its flowers are small and often clustered, displaying a range of colors from white to pale yellow.

The botanical structure of Pollinia jacquinii reflects adaptations for specific pollinators, making it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural study.
