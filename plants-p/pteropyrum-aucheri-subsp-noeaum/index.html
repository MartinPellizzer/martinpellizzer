Pteropyrum Aucheri Subsp. Noeaum

Pteropyrum aucheri subsp. noeaum is a lesser-known but intriguing member of the Apiaceae family. Native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus, this plant thrives in cool, moist environments. Its distinctive appearance includes finely divided leaves and small, clustered flowers that bloom in late spring. Though not widely cultivated, it holds value for botanists and gardeners interested in rare flora. Understanding its ecology and distribution can enhance conservation efforts in its natural habitat.
