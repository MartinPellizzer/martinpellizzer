Piper dilatatifolium

Piper dilatatifolium, commonly known as the broad-leaf pepper, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil and Peru. The plant is characterized by its large, ovate leaves and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. Its fruits are peppery and used in local cuisines for their distinctive flavor.

The plant's botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and culinary contexts.
