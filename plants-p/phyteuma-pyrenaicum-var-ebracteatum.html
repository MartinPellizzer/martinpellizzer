Phyteuma pyrenaicum var. ebracteatum

Phyteuma pyrenaicum var. ebracteatum, commonly known as the Pyrenean phyteuma, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Pyrenees region.

It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, pale yellow to orange flowers. The leaves are lanceolate, with a hairy surface, and are arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its distinctive floral display.
