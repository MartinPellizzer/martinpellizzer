Pteroceras ciliatum

Pteroceras ciliatum, commonly known as the hairy bellflower, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia, particularly China and the Himalayas.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors. The plant features showy, bell-shaped flowers that are usually blue or purple in color, with hairy stems and leaves. Its leaves are lanceolate and covered in fine, silky hairs, contributing to its common name.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
