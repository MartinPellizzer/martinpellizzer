Penstemon laricifolius var. exilifolius

Penstemon laricifolius var. exilifolius, commonly known as the slender-leaf beardtongue, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and spikes of tubular, pink to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly habitats, thriving in full sun to partial shade. It is well adapted to arid environments and is often found in mountainous regions.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
