Phaseolus jaliscanus

Phaseolus jaliscanus, commonly known as the Jalisco bean, is a species native to Mexico, particularly the state of Jalisco.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its climbing habit and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, elongated pods that contain several seeds, which are typically used as food.

Botanically, it is notable for its genetic diversity and potential role in crop improvement due to its adaptability to various environmental conditions.
