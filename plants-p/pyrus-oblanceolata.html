Pyrus oblanceolata

Pyrus oblanceolata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf pear.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China, characterized by its slender, elongated leaves that are often bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by edible, sweet-sour fruits that ripen in late summer. Its botanical features include a smooth, greyish bark and a preference for well-drained soils in temperate climates.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional Chinese medicine.
