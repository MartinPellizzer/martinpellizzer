Prunus × kohigan

Prunus × kohigan, commonly known as the Japanese apricot, is a hybrid deciduous shrub or small tree native to Japan.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its showy, single or double pink to white flowers that bloom in early spring before the leaves emerge. The plant produces small, yellow-orange fruits that are edible but often tart and not widely cultivated for consumption. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Prunus × kohigan is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in traditional Japanese gardens.
