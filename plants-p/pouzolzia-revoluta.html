Pouzolzia revoluta

Pouzolzia revoluta, commonly known as the revolute pouzolzia, is a member of the Polygonaceae family, characterized by its unique leaf morphology.

It is a perennial shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas, particularly in Mexico and Central America. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with a distinctive revolute margin, giving it its common name. Its small, greenish-white flowers are clustered in inflorescences, and it produces small, three-lobed fruits.

Pouzolzia revoluta is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories and rocky slopes.
