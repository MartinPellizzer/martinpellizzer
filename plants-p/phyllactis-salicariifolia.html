Phyllactis salicariifolia

Phyllactis salicariifolia, commonly known as the bladderworts, is a genus of aquatic and semi-aquatic plants characterized by their unique adaptations for underwater life.

These plants typically have small, bladder-like structures called bladders, which are used to trap and digest small aquatic organisms. They are often found in shallow, still or slow-moving freshwater habitats such as ponds and ditches. Phyllactis salicariifolia is known for its simple, submerged leaves and its ability to thrive in nutrient-poor environments.

The genus plays an important ecological role in aquatic ecosystems by contributing to nutrient cycling and providing habitat for microorganisms.
