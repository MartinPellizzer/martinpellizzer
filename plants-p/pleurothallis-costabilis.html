Pleurothallis costabilis

Pleurothallis costabilis, commonly known as the Costa Rican orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of Central America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact, epiphytic growth habit. The plant typically produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and yellow, which bloom during the rainy season. Its leaves are usually arranged in a rosette pattern and are covered in a fine, fuzzy texture.

Pleurothallis costabilis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in shaded, humid environments.
