Poecilostachys bakeri

Poecilostachys bakeri, commonly known as the Baker's bluemouth, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its narrow, blue-green leaves and produces small, pale blue to purple flowers in late spring. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its foliage remains attractive throughout the growing season, making it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping.

Poecilostachys bakeri is also valued for its ability to stabilize soil and support local wildlife.
