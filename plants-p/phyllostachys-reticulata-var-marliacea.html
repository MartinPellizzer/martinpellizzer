Phyllostachys reticulata var. marliacea

Phyllostachys reticulata var. marliacea, commonly known as the Black Bamboo, is a striking variant of the giant bamboo species.

It is characterized by its dark, almost black culms that contrast sharply with its bright green, lance-shaped leaves. This variety is native to China and thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil. It grows rapidly, often reaching heights of 10 to 15 feet, making it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping.

The plant is cold-hardy and requires minimal maintenance once established.
