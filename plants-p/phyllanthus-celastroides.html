Phyllanthus celastroides

Phyllanthus celastroides, commonly known as the golden mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that thrives on various host trees in tropical and subtropical regions.

It possesses small, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and its flowers are typically greenish-yellow with five petals. The plant produces small, fleshy fruits that contain a single seed, which is dispersed by birds. It is notable for its ability to photosynthesize partially while also drawing nutrients from its host.

Its botanical characteristics make it an interesting subject in ecological and parasitic plant studies.
