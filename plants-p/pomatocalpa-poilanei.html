Pomatocalpa poilanei

Pomatocalpa poilanei, commonly known as the Poilane's orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique floral structure, featuring a resupinate ovary and a distinctive labellum. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its flowers are usually small, fragrant, and come in a range of colors, including white, yellow, and green.

Pomatocalpa poilanei is often prized for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
