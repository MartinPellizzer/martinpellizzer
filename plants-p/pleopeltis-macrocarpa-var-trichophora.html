Pleopeltis macrocarpa var. trichophora

Pleopeltis macrocarpa var. trichophora, commonly known as the hairy-veined rock fern, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial fern characterized by its rhizomatous growth and fronds that are typically leathery and dark green. The varietal name "trichophora" refers to the hairy or pubescent veins on its leaves. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found growing on rocky substrates.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ornamental species within the family Polypodiaceae.
