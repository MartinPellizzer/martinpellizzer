Polypodium muscosum

Polypodium muscosum, commonly known as the moss fern, is a species of epiphytic fern that thrives in moist, shaded environments.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its dense, moss-like appearance. The plant has a rhizomatous structure with small, scale-like leaves that form a carpet-like growth. It is often found growing on tree trunks or rocks in tropical and subtropical regions.

Polypodium muscosum plays a role in maintaining soil moisture and providing habitat for microorganisms.
