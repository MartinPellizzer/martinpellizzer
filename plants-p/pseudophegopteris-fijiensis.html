Pseudophegopteris fijiensis

Pseudophegopteris fijiensis, commonly known as the Fiji cliff fern, is a unique species of fern native to the tropical regions of the South Pacific, particularly Fiji.

It is characterized by its robust, leathery fronds that are often clustered at the base of rocky outcrops or on cliff faces. The plant has a creeping rhizome that allows it to anchor itself in nutrient-poor, well-drained soils. Its fronds are typically dark green and have a distinctive, serrated edge.

This fern is adapted to harsh, sun-exposed environments and plays a role in stabilizing rocky habitats through its dense growth.
