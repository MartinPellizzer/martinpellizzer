Prunus padus var. pendula

Prunus padus var. pendula, commonly known as the weeping cherry or weeping almond, is a cultivar of the common cherry tree.

It is characterized by its distinctive cascading branches that bear clusters of small, white flowers in early spring. The plant is native to Europe and parts of western Asia, and it thrives in temperate climates. Its foliage turns vibrant shades of red and orange in the autumn, adding ornamental value throughout the year.

This tree is often planted in gardens and parks for its aesthetic appeal and seasonal beauty.
