Pseudolysimachion subincanovelutinum

Pseudolysimachion subincanovelutinum, commonly known as the velvet-hair false lysimachia, is a flowering plant characterized by its dense, soft, and hairy foliage.

It typically features small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, often blooming in late summer. The plant has a compact growth habit, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a ground cover. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped with a velvety texture, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade.
