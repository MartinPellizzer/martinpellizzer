Polygonum tenuicaule var. nanum

Polygonum tenuicaule var. nanum, commonly known as dwarf polygonum, is a low-growing herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia.

It typically reaches a height of only a few centimeters, making it a compact and hardy species. The plant features small, opposite leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces clusters of tiny, reddish or greenish flowers. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Due to its delicate appearance and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens for ornamental purposes.
