Plantago atrofusca

Plantago atrofusca, commonly known as black plantain, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It features a rosette of broad, dark green leaves that are covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in a spike, which are followed by oval, dark brown seeds. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats.

Botanically, it is characterized by its robust root system and its ability to adapt to harsh environmental conditions.
