Polygonum sachalinense

Polygonum sachalinense, commonly known as the Sakhalin knotweed, is a perennial herb native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, characterized by its distinctive nodes and stipules. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments, such as marshes and stream banks. Its leaves are alternate, ovate to lanceolate, and often have a waxy coating.

The flowers are small and clustered in inflorescences, with a unique structure that includes bracts and stamens.
