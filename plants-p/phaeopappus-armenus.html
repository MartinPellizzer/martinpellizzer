Phaeopappus armenus

Phaeopappus armenus, commonly known as the purple pappus, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually pink or purple in color, which bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, giving them a slightly fuzzy texture.

The plant is adapted to well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy choice for rock gardens or coastal landscapes.
