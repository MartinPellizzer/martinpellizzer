Pterocereus foetidus

Pterocereus foetidus, commonly known as the stinking moon cactus, is a cactus species native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its segmented, spiny stems and nocturnal flowering habit. The plant produces large, fragrant, white flowers that bloom at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators. Its stems are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

The plant is relatively easy to care for, making it a popular choice for cactus enthusiasts.
