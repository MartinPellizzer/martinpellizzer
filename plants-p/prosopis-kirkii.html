Prosopis kirkii

Prosopis kirkii, commonly known as the African mesquite, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Africa.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are often finely divided and arranged alternately along the stems. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are followed by elongated, bean-like pods containing seeds. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, making it an important species in desert ecosystems.

The plant is also valued for its hard wood and ability to fix nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility.
