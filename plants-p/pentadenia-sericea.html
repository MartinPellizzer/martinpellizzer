Pentadenia sericea

Pentadenia sericea, commonly known as silky pentadenia, is a small evergreen shrub native to the coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, silver-gray foliage, which gives it a distinctive and ornamental appearance. The plant produces small, white, five-petaled flowers that bloom in spring, adding visual interest to its overall structure. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy coastal environments.

Due to its hardy nature and aesthetic appeal, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it is primarily valued for its ecological role in its native habitat.
