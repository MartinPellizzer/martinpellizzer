Papaver dubium var. dielsianum

Papaver dubium var. dielsianum, commonly known as the Diels' poppy, is a lesser-known variant of the common poppy.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Europe, particularly found in regions of Spain and Italy. The plant features striking, bright red flowers with a distinctive, saucer-shaped corona. Its leaves are typically lobed and covered in a fine layer of silky hairs.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
