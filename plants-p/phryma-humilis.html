Phryma humilis

Phryma humilis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved phryma, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the family Phrymaceae, which includes other species with showy, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a hairy texture, while its flowers are violet to purple in color.

Phryma humilis is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
