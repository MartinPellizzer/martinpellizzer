Pleuropteropyrum ajanense var. divaricatum

Pleuropteropyrum ajanense var. divaricatum, commonly known as the mountain huckleberry, is a shrub native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense, dark green foliage. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink, followed by edible berries that are often used in local cuisines. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in its native habitat.
