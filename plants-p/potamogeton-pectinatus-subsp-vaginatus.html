Potamogeton pectinatus subsp. vaginatus

Potamogeton pectinatus subsp.

vaginatus, commonly known as the narrow-leaf pondweed, is a aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in Europe and Asia. It is characterized by its slender, ribbon-like leaves that are often arranged in whorls around the stem.

The plant produces small, green flowers that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences near the water surface. Its rhizomes form extensive underwater networks, allowing it to colonize shallow, still waters.

It plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
