Parathesis macrophylla

Parathesis macrophylla, commonly known as the giant water lily, is a striking aquatic plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, broad leaves that can reach up to 3 meters in diameter, which float on the water's surface. The plant's roots are anchored in the mud at the bottom of slow-moving or still water bodies. Its flowers are showy, with white petals and yellow stamens, blooming during the day and closing at night.

Parathesis macrophylla plays an important role in its ecosystem by providing habitat for various aquatic organisms and contributing to water filtration.
