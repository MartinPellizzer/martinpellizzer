Polygonum schugnanicum

Polygonum schugnanicum, commonly known as the Schugnan knotweed, is a perennial herb native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, characterized by its distinctive nodes and often triangular stems. The plant typically grows in moist, rocky habitats and has heart-shaped leaves with a rough, hairy texture. Its small, clustered flowers are usually pink or red, and it produces small, three-lobed berries.

Polygonum schugnanicum is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
