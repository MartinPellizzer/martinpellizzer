Polygala linkiana

Polygala linkiana, commonly known as the Malayan milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Polygalaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, delicate flowers. The plant typically grows in open woodlands and grasslands, preferring well-drained soils. Its flowers are usually pale green or yellowish, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits.

Polygala linkiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and flowering habit.
