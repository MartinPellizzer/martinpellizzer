Pilosella alticeps

Pilosella alticeps, commonly known as the alpine everlasting, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky slopes, thriving in cool, high-altitude environments. Its leaves are hairy and grayish-green, while its flowers are small and yellow, forming a cluster that resembles a dandelion.

Pilosella alticeps is known for its hardy nature and ability to withstand harsh climatic conditions.
