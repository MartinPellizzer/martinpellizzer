Persea thunbergii

Persea thunbergii, commonly known as the Chinese evergreen or the Brazilian pepper tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant, yellowish-white flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically orange or red when ripe. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

Persea thunbergii thrives in warm, humid climates and is relatively drought-tolerant once established.
