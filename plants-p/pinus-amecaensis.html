Pinus amecaensis

Pinus amecaensis, commonly known as the Ameca pine, is a species of evergreen conifer native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, straight trunk and long, needle-like leaves that are typically arranged in bundles of five. The tree produces cone-shaped seed structures that mature over a period of several years. It thrives in temperate to subtropical climates and is often found in mountainous regions.

The botanical features of Pinus amecaensis make it an important component of its native ecosystems.
