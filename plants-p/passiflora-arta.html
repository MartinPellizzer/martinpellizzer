Passiflora arta

Passiflora arta, commonly known as the Artic passionflower, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, complex flowers that feature a blend of red, blue, and white petals, along with a crown of filaments. The plant produces edible, tropical fruits known as passionfruits, which are rich in vitamins and antioxidants. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a velvety texture.

Passiflora arta thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for both ornamental and culinary purposes.
