Prunus jacquemontii var. bifrons

Prunus jacquemontii var. bifrons, commonly known as the twin-flowered Japanese cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its distinctive pink flowers that appear in pairs, giving it its common name. The plant has simple, oval-shaped leaves that turn yellow in autumn. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular ornamental plant.

The fruit is a small, dark purple drupe that ripens in late summer.
