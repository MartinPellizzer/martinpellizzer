Polygala sibirica

Polygala sibirica, commonly known as Siberian milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the temperate regions of Asia.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are alternate and pinnate, with small, rounded leaflets.

The flowers are usually pink or purple and are followed by elongated, bean-like pods that contain the seeds.
