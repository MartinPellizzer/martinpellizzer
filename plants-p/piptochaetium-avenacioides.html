Piptochaetium avenacioides

Piptochaetium avenacioides, commonly known as the needlegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the southern United States and parts of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves with a bluish-green color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, two-lobed seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open areas such as prairies and disturbed sites.

Botanically, it is noted for its adaptability to various environmental conditions and its role in ecological restoration projects.
