Polygonum tristachyum

Polygonum tristachyum, commonly known as the tri-stamen knotweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, which includes plants known for their distinctive arrowhead-shaped leaves. The plant features slender, erect stems that are often covered in fine hairs, and its leaves are typically alternate and lanceolate. It produces small, clustered flowers that are usually pink or red, arranged in inflorescences called cyathia.

Polygonum tristachyum is often found in moist environments such as stream banks and wet meadows.
