Pterocephalus fruticulosus

Pterocephalus fruticulosus, commonly known as the hairy globemallow, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Mexico.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. The plant features a woody stem with numerous branches and produces showy, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. Its leaves are alternate, hairy, and have a distinctive lobed shape.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its drought tolerance and unique appearance.
