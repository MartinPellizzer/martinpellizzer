Phaseolus cuernavacanus

Phaseolus cuernavacanus, commonly known as the Cuernavaca bean, is a species native to Mexico.

It is a climbing legume characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, hairy pods. The plant produces yellow flowers and is adapted to warm, humid climates. It is known for its edible seeds, which are used in traditional cuisines.

Botanically, it is closely related to common beans and belongs to the Fabaceae family.
