Pouteria dominigensis subsp. dominigensis

Pouteria dominigensis subsp.

dominigensis, commonly known as the Dominican mahogany, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Caribbean, particularly Dominica. It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its dark, aromatic wood and broad, leathery leaves.

The plant produces small, white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by oval, yellowish fruits containing a single large seed. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its valuable timber, it is also considered an important species for conservation efforts.
