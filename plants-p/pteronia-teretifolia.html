Pteronia teretifolia

Pteronia teretifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved pteronia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and clusters of small, pink to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.

Pteronia teretifolia is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional African medicine.
