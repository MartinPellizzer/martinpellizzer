Paphinia vermiculifera

Paphinia vermiculifera, commonly known as the veined paphiopedilum, is a rare and highly sought-after orchid native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique, spurred lip and intricate veining pattern on its leaves, which give it its common name. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments and requires well-draining, acidic soil to thrive. Its flowers, which bloom in shades of pink and white, are long-lasting and attract specific pollinators.

Due to its rarity and ornamental value, Paphinia vermiculifera is often cultivated in greenhouses and conservation programs.
