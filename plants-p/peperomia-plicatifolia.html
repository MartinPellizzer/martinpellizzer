Peperomia plicatifolia

Peperomia plicatifolia, commonly known as the zigzag peperomia, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its unique, crinkled, and wavy leaf margins, which give it its common name. The leaves are typically a deep green with a slightly glossy texture and can grow up to 6 inches in length. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers in shades of white or pale pink, which are followed by red or purple berries.

This plant is well-suited for indoor cultivation due to its low maintenance requirements and ability to thrive in bright, indirect light.
