Panicum micrognostum

Panicum micrognostum, commonly known as dwarf panicgrass, is a low-growing grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted foliage and small, spikelet-based inflorescences. The plant typically thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks. Its botanical features include narrow, grass-like leaves and a compact growth habit.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small invertebrates in its native environment.
