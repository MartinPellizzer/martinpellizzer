Protea leucoblepharis

Protea leucoblepharis, commonly known as the white-eyed protea, is a striking South African plant belonging to the Proteaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescence composed of numerous small, white flowers surrounded by a cluster of petal-like bracts. The plant is native to the Western Cape region and thrives in well-drained, acidic soils. It produces a distinctive, spherical head of flowers that can reach up to 30 cm in diameter.

Protea leucoblepharis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is a popular choice in gardens and floral arrangements.
