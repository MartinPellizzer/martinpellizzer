Pelargonium micropetalum

Pelargonium micropetalum, commonly known as the small-petaled geranium, is a species of flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Geraniaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy flowers. The plant features serrated leaves and produces clusters of small, five-petaled flowers in shades of pink and red. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is often cultivated as a decorative plant.
