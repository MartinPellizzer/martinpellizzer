Pulegium aromaticum

Pulegium aromaticum, commonly known as blueweed or pulegium, is a flowering plant belonging to the Lamiaceae family.

It is native to Europe, North Africa, and parts of Asia, and it thrives in moist, temperate climates. The plant produces small, tubular blue or purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are aromatic, often used in herbal medicine for their antispasmodic and expectorant properties.

The plant is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic benefits.
