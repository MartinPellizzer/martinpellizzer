Polygala albowiana

Polygala albowiana, commonly known as the narrow-leaf milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its slender, often hairy stems and small, delicate leaves. The plant produces clusters of bright yellow or greenish flowers, which are typically arranged in racemes. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and shrublands.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
