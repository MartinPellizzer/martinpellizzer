Potentilla svanetica

Potentilla svanetica, commonly known as the Svaneti cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the Caucasus region.

It is a low-growing perennial shrub that typically reaches a height of about 30 centimeters. The plant features bright yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are palmately divided, with a rough texture and a distinctive silver-green color.

Potentilla svanetica thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.
