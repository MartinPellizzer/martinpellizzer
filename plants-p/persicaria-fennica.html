Persicaria fennica

Persicaria fennica, commonly known as the Finnish knotweed, is a perennial herb native to northern Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, nutrient-rich soils along riverbanks and wetlands. The plant features upright stems that are often reddish or purple in color, and it produces small, clustered flowers in late summer. Its leaves are broad and heart-shaped, with a slightly hairy texture.

Persicaria fennica is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in wet conditions.
