Phrygilanthus corymbosus

Phrygilanthus corymbosus, commonly known as the clustered flame tree, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, bright red or orange flowers that appear in dense clusters, giving it its common name. The plant has a woody stem with smooth, greyish bark and produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular ornamental plant in warmer climates.

Its rapid growth and showy blooms make it a notable species in botanical gardens and landscapes.
