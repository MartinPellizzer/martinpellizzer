Plantago brasiliensis

Plantago brasiliensis, commonly known as Brazilian plantain, is a species of flowering plant in the plantain family.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and thrives in moist, shaded environments. The plant features broad, lance-shaped leaves with a waxy surface and produces small, greenish flowers arranged in a spike. Its seeds are encased in a sticky, helmet-like structure that aids in seed dispersal.

This plant is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
