Poranthera corymbosa var. genuina

Poranthera corymbosa var. genuina, commonly known as the California fuchsia, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its showy, tubular red or orange flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant has hairy, glandular leaves and produces capsule fruits containing numerous small, dark seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to full sun, making it a popular choice for drought-tolerant gardens.

Its nectar-rich flowers attract pollinators such as hummingbirds and butterflies.
