Passiflora suberosa subsp. litoralis

Passiflora suberosa subsp.

litoralis, commonly known as the coastal passionflower, is a climbing vine native to coastal regions of South America. It is characterized by its hairy, woody stems and heart-shaped leaves with serrated edges.

The plant produces striking flowers with five sepals, five petals, and a unique crown of filaments around the reproductive structures. Its fruit is a rounded, fleshy capsule that turns red when mature, containing numerous seeds.

This species thrives in humid, coastal environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.
