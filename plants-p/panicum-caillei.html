Panicum caillei

Panicum caillei, commonly known as the Caille's panicgrass, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean region.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, wiry stems. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences that are typically green to reddish in color. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a prominent midrib and a slightly hairy texture.

Panicum caillei is often found in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides and dry grasslands.
