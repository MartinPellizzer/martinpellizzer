Plectranthus australis f. eximius

Plectranthus australis f. eximius, commonly known as the narrow-leaf mint, is a hybrid form of the common plectranthus.

It is a perennial herb native to South Africa, characterized by its slender, aromatic leaves and clusters of small, tubular flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for both garden beds and container cultivation. Its foliage is often used in traditional medicine and as a culinary herb, owing to its mint-like aroma.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various growing conditions.
