Pseudomyrcianthes rosea

Pseudomyrcianthes rosea, commonly known as the pink sylph, is a flowering plant native to the Amazon rainforest.

It belongs to the family Myrtaceae and is characterized by its fragrant, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has small, elliptical leaves that are glossy and dark green in color. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found near water sources.
