Prasophyllum australe

Prasophyllum australe, commonly known as the southern leek orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to southeastern Australia.

It is a small plant with a rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces a single spike of pale green to yellow flowers. The species is adapted to grow in sandy or gravelly soils in open habitats. It is often found in heathlands and woodlands, and it blooms between September and November.

Prasophyllum australe is listed as vulnerable due to habitat loss and fragmentation.
