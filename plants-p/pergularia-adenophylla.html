Pergularia adenophylla

Pergularia adenophylla, commonly known as the hairy leaf dandelion, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a small, herbaceous perennial that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils. The plant features alternate, hairy leaves and produces yellow flowers that bloom in spring. It is known for its ability to thrive in arid environments due to its drought-resistant characteristics.

The species is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional African medicine.
