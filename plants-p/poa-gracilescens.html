Poa gracilescens

Poa gracilescens, commonly known as slender meadowgrass, is a species of grass native to the temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically less than 2 millimeters wide. This plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, streambanks, and wetlands. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in dense clusters, which are wind-pollinated.

Poa gracilescens is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
