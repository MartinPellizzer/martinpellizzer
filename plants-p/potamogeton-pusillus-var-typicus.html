Potamogeton pusillus var. typicus

Potamogeton pusillus var. typicus, commonly known as the small pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments across Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in shallow, slow-moving waters and is characterized by its slender, thread-like leaves that are often arranged in whorls around the stem. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually submerged, followed by small, oval-shaped fruits. It plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various invertebrates and fish.

Due to its widespread distribution and ecological significance, it is often used in water quality assessments and habitat restoration projects.
