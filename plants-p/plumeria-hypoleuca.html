Plumeria hypoleuca

Plumeria hypoleuca, commonly known as the white frangipani, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, fragrant white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has simple, narrow leaves and produces woody, branching stems that are often used in traditional crafts. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, requiring full sun and well-drained soil.

The flowers are often used in religious ceremonies and as ornamental plants in gardens.
