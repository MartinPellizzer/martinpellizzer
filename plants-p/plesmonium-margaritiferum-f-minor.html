Plesmonium margaritiferum f. minor

Plesmonium margaritiferum f. minor, commonly known as the dwarf white bellflower, is a compact, low-growing perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features delicate, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale blue, blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has smooth, narrow leaves that are arranged alternately along the stems. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This cultivar is prized for its ornamental value and is often used in rock gardens or as a border plant.
