Paspalum adpressum

Paspalum adpressum, commonly known as flatgrass, is a perennial grass native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit and narrow, flat leaves. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats, such as marshes and swamps, and is often found in coastal areas. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers.

Paspalum adpressum is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
