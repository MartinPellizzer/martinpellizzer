Palmeria scandens var. hirsuta

Palmeria scandens var. hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy palmeria, is a climbing shrub native to the Caribbean.

It features small, ovate leaves with a hairy texture that gives it its common name. The plant produces clusters of tiny, white, five-petaled flowers that bloom in the summer. It thrives in humid, tropical environments and is often found growing along coastal areas.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardeners seeking a unique and hardy plant.
