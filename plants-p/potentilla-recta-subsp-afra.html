Potentilla recta subsp. afra

Potentilla recta subsp.

afra, commonly known as African cinquefoil, is a shrub native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant has pinnately compound leaves with serrated edges, and its stems are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and hardiness, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
