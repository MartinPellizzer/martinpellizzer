Polygonum macropterum

Polygonum macropterum, commonly known as giant knotweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Asia.

It features erect, often woody stems that can grow up to 2 meters in height. The plant has alternate, heart-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. Its small, greenish-white flowers are clustered in dense, cylindrical inflorescences.

Polygonum macropterum is known for its rapid growth and has been introduced to other regions, sometimes becoming invasive.
