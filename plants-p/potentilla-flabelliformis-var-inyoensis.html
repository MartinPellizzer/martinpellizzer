Potentilla flabelliformis var. inyoensis

Potentilla flabelliformis var. inyoensis, commonly known as the Inyo potentilla, is a rare flowering plant native to the Mojave Desert in California.

It is a low-growing shrub characterized by its silvery-gray foliage and small, yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant has adapted to arid conditions with thick, waxy leaves that reduce water loss. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky habitats.

Due to its limited distribution and sensitivity to environmental changes, it is considered a species of conservation concern.
