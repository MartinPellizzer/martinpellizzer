Piptanthus nepalensis var. sericopetalus

Piptanthus nepalensis var. sericopetalus, commonly known as the silver-petaled burr bush, is a deciduous shrub native to the Himalayan regions of Nepal and northern India.

It features compound leaves with a distinctive silver or grayish-white underside, giving it a unique appearance. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, typical of the Fabaceae family. It thrives in mountainous areas with well-drained soils and is often found in subalpine and alpine zones.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation projects.
