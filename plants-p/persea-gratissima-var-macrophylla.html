Persea gratissima var. macrophylla

Persea gratissima var. macrophylla, commonly known as the Mexican white sapote, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central America.

It is characterized by its large, glossy, ovate leaves that can grow up to 30 cm in length. The plant produces fragrant, white flowers followed by large, smooth, yellow or orange fruits with a sweet, creamy flesh. Its leaves and fruits are used in traditional medicine and culinary applications.

The tree thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
