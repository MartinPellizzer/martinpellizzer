Polystichum setillosum

Polystichum setillosum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf fern, is a species of evergreen fern native to the forests of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Dryopteridaceae and is characterized by its slender, upright fronds that are divided into lance-shaped pinnae. The plant thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in mountainous regions. Its spore-producing structures, called sori, are typically located on the underside of the fronds.

Polystichum setillosum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
