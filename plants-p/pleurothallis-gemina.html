Pleurothallis gemina

Pleurothallis gemina, commonly known as the golden pleurothallis, is a species of orchid native to the cloud forests of Central and South America.

It is a small, epiphytic orchid that typically grows on trees, drawing moisture and nutrients from the air and rain. The plant features delicate, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of fragrant, yellowish-green flowers. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining potting media.

Due to its striking appearance and relatively easy care, it is a popular choice among orchid enthusiasts.
