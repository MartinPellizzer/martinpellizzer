Pallenis spinosa var. gymnesica

Pallenis spinosa var. gymnesica, commonly known as the Gymnesian spiny centaury, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its spiny, woody stems and small, blue or purple flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant thrives in rocky and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are typically narrow and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

This variety is often found in coastal areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance.
