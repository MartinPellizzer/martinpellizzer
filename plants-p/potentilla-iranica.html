Potentilla iranica

Potentilla iranica, commonly known as Persian cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Iran.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit with multiple stems. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the summer months, attracting pollinators such as bees. Its leaves are typically trifoliate, with three leaflets that are hairy and serrated.

Potentilla iranica is adapted to rocky and arid environments, showcasing its resilience to harsh climatic conditions.
