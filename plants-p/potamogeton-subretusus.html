Potamogeton subretusus

Potamogeton subretusus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in North America.

It has long, narrow leaves that are typically arranged in whorls around the stem, and it produces small, green flowers that are clustered in spike-like structures. The plant thrives in shallow, slow-moving waters and plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.

Its rhizomes help stabilize sediment, contributing to water clarity and preventing erosion.
