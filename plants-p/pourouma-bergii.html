Pourouma bergii

Pourouma bergii, commonly known as the giant bamboo palm, is a tall, perennial herb native to the Amazon rainforest.

It belongs to the family Arecaeae and is characterized by its massive, clustered inflorescences that can reach several meters in height. The plant has long, narrow leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern, and it produces small, greenish flowers that develop into edible, starchy fruits. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in wet lowland forests.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological role, it is an important species in both botanical research and traditional Amazonian practices.
