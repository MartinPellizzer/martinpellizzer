Portulaca parvifolia

Portulaca parvifolia, commonly known as the dwarf portulaca, is a low-growing annual plant native to southern Africa.

It features succulent, fleshy leaves and produces small, vibrant flowers in shades of red, yellow, and orange. The plant thrives in hot, dry climates and is often found in rocky or sandy soils. It has a shallow root system, allowing it to survive in arid conditions with minimal water.

Portulaca parvifolia is widely cultivated as a ornamental plant due to its drought tolerance and colorful blooms.
