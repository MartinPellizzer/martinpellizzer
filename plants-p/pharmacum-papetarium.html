Pharmacum papetarium

Pharmacum papetarium, commonly known as the golden spire, is a rare flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Andes.

It features striking, elongated yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has a woody stem with a fibrous, bark-like exterior. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a slightly hairy surface.

The botanical significance of Pharmacum papetarium lies in its unique chemical compounds, which have been studied for potential medicinal applications.
