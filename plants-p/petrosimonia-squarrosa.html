Petrosimonia squarrosa

Petrosimonia squarrosa, commonly known as the woolly petrosimonia, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of Central Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial shrub characterized by its hairy, fleshy stems and leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky or sandy soils.

Due to its unique adaptations, it is considered a valuable species for ecological studies in harsh climates.
