Pittosporum terminalioides f. halophilum

Pittosporum terminalioides f. halophilum, commonly known as the coastal pittosporum, is a variant of the species that exhibits notable adaptations to saline environments.

It is characterized by its dense, leathery foliage which helps reduce water loss in coastal conditions. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, often found in coastal heathlands and dunes. Its flowers are small, fragrant, and white to pale yellow, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds.

This form is particularly valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing coastal soils.
