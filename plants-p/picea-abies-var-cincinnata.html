Picea abies var. cincinnata

Picea abies var. cincinnata, commonly known as the dwarf Norway spruce, is a compact variant of the Norway spruce native to mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its slow growth rate and dense, rounded form, making it well-suited for rock gardens and alpine settings. The foliage consists of dark green, needle-like leaves that are slightly twisted and emit a subtle pine scent. It produces small, cone-like seed structures that mature in the second year, contributing to its reproductive cycle.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in harsh, cold climates.
