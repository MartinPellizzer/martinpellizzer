Pseudobombax simplicifolium

Pseudobombax simplicifolium, commonly known as the pink hibiscus mealybug tree, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are typically green with a waxy coating. The plant produces showy, pink or red flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking appearance.

The tree is also known for its ability to host the pink hibiscus mealybug, a common pest in tropical regions.
