Picea fortunei

Picea fortunei, commonly known as the Chinese white fir, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, conical shape and dark green, needle-like leaves. The tree produces small, spherical cones that mature to a reddish-brown color. It thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil and moderate rainfall.

Picea fortunei is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in reforestation efforts.
