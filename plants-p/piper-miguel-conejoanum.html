Piper miguel-conejoanum

Piper miguel-conejoanum, commonly known as the Miguel-Conejoanum pepper plant, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piper genus, which includes numerous species of flowering plants known for their aromatic properties. The plant produces small, elongated berries that are typically green when unripe and turn red when mature. These berries are often used in traditional medicine and culinary applications due to their pungent flavor.

Its botanical characteristics include compound leaves with serrated edges and a climbing habit that allows it to thrive in humid, shaded environments.
