Potentilla argentea var. angustisecta

Potentilla argentea var. angustisecta, commonly known as silver cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its finely divided, silver-gray leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry meadows and rocky areas.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
