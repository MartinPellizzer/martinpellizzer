Pterocymbium tinctorium var. glabrifolium

Pterocymbium tinctorium var. glabrifolium, commonly known as the glabrous-leafed turmeric, is a member of the Zingiberaceae family.

It is characterized by its smooth, lance-shaped leaves and stems that often have a reddish hue. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, and its rhizomes are used for their medicinal and dyeing properties. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with high humidity and well-drained soil.

This variant is valued for its unique botanical features and traditional applications.
