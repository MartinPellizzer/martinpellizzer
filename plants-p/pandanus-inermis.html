Pandanus inermis

Pandanus inermis, commonly known as the Tahitian pandanus, is a tropical plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It is a member of the Pandanaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, feather-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically yellow or green in color. Its roots and leaves are often used in traditional cooking and crafts.

The plant thrives in humid, coastal environments and is well adapted to saline conditions.
