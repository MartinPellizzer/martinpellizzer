Pleioblastus chino f. variegatus

Pleioblastus chino f. variegatus, commonly known as the Variegated Chinese Bambboo, is a cold-hardy bamboo species native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its striking variegated foliage, featuring white and green stripes on its leaves. This plant typically grows in clumps and can reach heights of up to 6 feet, with slender, gracefully arching stems. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Its unique appearance and adaptability make it a favored plant for both landscape design and indoor cultivation.
