Passiflora trintae

Passiflora trintae, commonly known as the Trinidad passionflower, is a climbing vine native to the Caribbean islands, particularly Trinidad and Tobago.

It is characterized by its striking, intricate flowers that display a mix of purple, yellow, and white petals, along with a crown-like structure of filaments. The plant produces edible berries that are often used in local cuisine and traditional medicine. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a velvety texture, while its stems are woody and covered in fine hairs.

Passiflora trintae plays an important role in the local ecosystem by providing food and shelter for various insects and birds.
