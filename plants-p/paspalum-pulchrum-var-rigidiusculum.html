Paspalum pulchrum var. rigidiusculum

Paspalum pulchrum var. rigidiusculum, commonly known as the rigid false brome, is a perennial grass native to South America.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color and have a distinct, slightly hairy texture. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, white to pale blue flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or disturbed areas.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native plant gardens.
