Polygonum monticola

Polygonum monticola, commonly known as mountain knotweed, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It is a member of the Polygonaceae family, characterized by its distinctive bracts and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or disturbed soils and thrives in temperate climates. Its stems are often reddish or purple, and it produces small, greenish-white flowers in late summer.

Polygonum monticola is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
