Poa polyphylla

Poa polyphylla, commonly known as the Sierra Nevada tufted grass, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and distinctive silver-gray foliage. The plant typically grows in high-elevation meadows and rocky areas, thriving in cool, moist environments. Its inflorescence consists of dense, spike-like clusters of small, two-rowed flowers.

Poa polyphylla plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
