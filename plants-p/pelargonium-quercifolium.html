Pelargonium quercifolium

Pelargonium quercifolium, commonly known as the oak-leaf geranium, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its large, palmate leaves that resemble the shape of an oak leaf, giving it its common name. The plant produces clusters of small, pink to red flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

Pelargonium quercifolium is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
