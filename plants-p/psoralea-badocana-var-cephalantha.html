Psoralea badocana var. cephalantha

Psoralea badocana var. cephalantha, commonly known as the mountain psoralea, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes.

It is a small shrub that typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, thriving in cool and dry conditions. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with small, oval-shaped leaflets.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens or alpine settings.
