Plectranthus laurifolius

Plectranthus laurifolius, commonly known as African violet, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It features opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are often glossy and dark green, with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers in shades of purple, pink, or white, typically blooming in summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers bright, indirect light.

Plectranthus laurifolius is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and ease of care.
