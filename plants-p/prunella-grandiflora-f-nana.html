Prunella grandiflora f. nana

Prunella grandiflora f. nana, commonly known as dwarf self-vein, is a compact, low-growing perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features upright, hairy stems that bear small, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface. The plant produces clusters of blue to violet flowers in late spring to early summer, which are attractive to pollinators. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and suitability in woodland gardens.
