Pinus taeda var. alopecuroidea

Pinus taeda var. alopecuroidea, commonly known as the southern yellow pine variant with a distinctive foliage pattern.

This plant is a variant of the southern yellow pine, characterized by its unique leaf arrangement and reduced foliage. It is native to the southeastern United States, where it thrives in well-drained soils. The leaves are typically shorter and more sparse compared to the standard variety, giving it a more open appearance.

It is valued for its ecological role and potential use in horticulture due to its ornamental features.
