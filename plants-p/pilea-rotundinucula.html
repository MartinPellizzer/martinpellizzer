Pilea rotundinucula

Pilea rotundinucula, commonly known as the rounded-nut siltplant, is a unique species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit and its distinctive, rounded, nut-like structures on its stems. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are followed by tiny, berry-like fruits. Its leaves are typically small, ovate, and have a smooth texture.

Pilea rotundinucula is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its unusual appearance and ability to thrive in shaded environments.
