Pyrus malus fruticosa

Pyrus malus fruticosa, commonly known as the shrub apple, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring thorny branches and alternate, simple leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters, followed by edible, apple-like fruits that are yellow or red in color. It is native to regions of Central Asia and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit production.

The shrub is adapted to a variety of soil types and is relatively drought-tolerant once established.
