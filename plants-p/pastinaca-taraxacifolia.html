Pastinaca taraxacifolia

Pastinaca taraxacifolia, commonly known as the dandelion root chicory, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of dark green, hairy leaves. The plant produces yellow composite flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its roots are thick and fleshy, often used in traditional medicine and as a vegetable.

The species is known for its ability to thrive in disturbed soils and its resilience to various environmental conditions.
