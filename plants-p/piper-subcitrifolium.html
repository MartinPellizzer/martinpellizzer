Piper subcitrifolium

Piper subcitrifolium, commonly known as the yellow-leafed pepper or citronleaf pepper, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many pepper species. The plant is characterized by its broad, waxy leaves that range from light green to yellowish in color. Its small, green berries are typically harvested when mature for use in traditional medicine and culinary applications.

The plant's botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and ethnobotanical contexts.
