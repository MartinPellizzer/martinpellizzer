Platytaenia lasiocarpa var. glabrior

Platytaenia lasiocarpa var. glabrior, commonly known as the smooth-hairy mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches to the stems of its host trees.

It belongs to the family Loranthaceae and is characterized by its flattened, leathery leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant produces small, fleshy berries that are dispersed by birds, aiding in its propagation. Its leaves are usually glabrous or only slightly hairy, distinguishing it from other varieties of Platytaenia.

This species is often found in forested areas where it establishes a symbiotic relationship with its host, drawing nutrients and water through its haustoria.
