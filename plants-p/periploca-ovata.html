Periploca ovata

Periploca ovata, commonly known as the white mullein, is a flowering plant belonging to the Scrophulariaceae family.

It is native to southern Europe, North Africa, and western Asia, often found in dry, rocky areas. The plant features tall, hairy stems that can grow up to 2 meters in height, with large, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, white hairs. Its showy, pale yellow flowers bloom in late summer, attracting various pollinators.

Periploca ovata is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally for its soothing effects on respiratory ailments.
