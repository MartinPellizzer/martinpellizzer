Potentilla porphyrii

Potentilla porphyrii, commonly known as the purple cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant features palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy perennial suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long-lasting blooms and drought tolerance.
