Pandanus perrieri

Pandanus perrieri, commonly known as the New Caledonian pandanus, is a species of flowering plant in the genus Pandanus.

It is native to the island of New Caledonia and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and long, pinnate leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically borne in dense, branched clusters. It thrives in coastal and lowland areas with well-drained soil and is adapted to humid, tropical climates.

The species is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and ecological significance in its native habitat.
