Piper heptandrum

Piper heptandrum, commonly known as the seven-stamen pepper, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil and Peru. The plant is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and small, heart-shaped leaves. Its flowers are typically greenish-white and are borne in clusters, while the fruit is a small, elongated berry.

Piper heptandrum is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional herbal remedies.
