Polypodium fraxinifolium var. fraxinellum

Polypodium fraxinifolium var. fraxinellum, commonly known as the golden fern, is a variant of the common polypody.

It is a small, evergreen fern characterized by its delicate, frond-like leaves that are typically golden to bronze in color. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories. Its fronds are pinnately divided, with a leathery texture and a distinctive, intricate pattern of veins.

This fern is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in shaded garden settings.
