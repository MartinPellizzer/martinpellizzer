Piper lauterbachii

Piper lauterbachii, commonly known as the Lauterbach's pepper, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many species of pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its climbing habit, with slender stems that often twine around supports. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a glossy surface, while the flowers are small and greenish-white in color.

The fruit is a small, three-lobed berry that turns red when mature, containing several seeds.
