Plectranthus rugosiformis

Plectranthus rugosiformis, commonly known as the roughleaf plectranthus, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its hairy, rough-textured leaves and clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from purple to white. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and containers.

Plectranthus rugosiformis is also valued for its ornamental appeal and mild aromatic properties.
