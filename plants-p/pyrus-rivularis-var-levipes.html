Pyrus rivularis var. levipes

Pyrus rivularis var. levipes, commonly known as the river pear or slender pear, is a variant of the river pear species native to parts of Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows near water bodies, such as rivers and streams. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are usually ovate to elliptic in shape, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. Its flowers are small and clustered, producing a fragrant aroma, and it bears edible fruits that are similar to common pears.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to moist environments.
