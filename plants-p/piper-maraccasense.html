Piper maraccasense

Piper maraccasense, commonly known as the Amazonian pepper or *Piper maraccasense*, is a climbing vine native to the rainforests of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many species of pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and small, greenish-yellow flowers that develop into elongated, peppery fruits. The fruits are typically harvested when they turn red, and they are used in traditional medicine and as a spice.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and ethnobotanical contexts.
