Piper quadrifolium

Piper quadrifolium, commonly known as the four-leaf pepper, is a tropical climbing vine native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its opposite, ovate to elliptical leaves that often have four lobes, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by elliptical, dark purple to black berries. It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is occasionally used in traditional medicine.
