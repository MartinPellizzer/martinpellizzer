Phaseolus amplus

Phaseolus amplus, commonly known as the broadleaf bean, is a species native to the Andean region of South America.

It is a climbing legume characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and showy, violet-blue flowers. The plant produces elongated pods containing edible seeds that are often used in traditional cuisines. Botanically, it belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is known for its nitrogen-fixing capabilities.

Its growth habit and morphological features make it an important species in both ecological and agricultural contexts.
