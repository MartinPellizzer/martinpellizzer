Penstemon wislizeni

Penstemon wislizeni, commonly known as the narrowleaf penstemon, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, upright stems and showy, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments. It has lance-shaped leaves that are usually narrow and covered in fine hairs.

Penstemon wislizeni is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators like bees and hummingbirds.
