Persicaria wallichii var. pubescens

Persicaria wallichii var. pubescens, commonly known as the pubescent knotweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the Himalayas.

It features upright, slender stems that are covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The leaves are lance-shaped, with a slightly hairy surface, and they often have a bluish-green hue. Its flowers are small, clustered in dense spikes, and typically bloom in shades of pink or white.

This plant thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in mountainous regions.
