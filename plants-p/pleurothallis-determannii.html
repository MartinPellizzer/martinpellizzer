Pleurothallis determannii

Pleurothallis determannii, commonly known as the Determann's orchid, is a species native to the cloud forests of Central America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact, sympodial growth habit. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, often with a greenish tinge. It thrives in high humidity and requires well-drained, acidic soil.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is a sought-after species among orchid enthusiasts.
