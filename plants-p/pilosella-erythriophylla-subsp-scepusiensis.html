Pilosella erythriophylla subsp. scepusiensis

Pilosella erythriophylla subsp.

scepusiensis, commonly known as the Scepusian whiteweed, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia. It is a perennial herb with a low-growing habit, characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves.

The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in dense, spherical clusters. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as meadows and rocky areas.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive member of the Asteraceae family.
