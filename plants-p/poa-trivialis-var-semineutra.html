Poa trivialis var. semineutra

Poa trivialis var. semineutra, commonly known as the common meadow grass, is a variant of the widely distributed Poa trivialis species.

It is a tufted perennial grass with slender, wiry stems and narrow, blade-like leaves. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences that contain both male and female flowers, making it a dioecious species. It thrives in a variety of habitats, including meadows, lawns, and disturbed soils, and is adapted to temperate climates.

Its botanical features make it an important component of grassland ecosystems.
