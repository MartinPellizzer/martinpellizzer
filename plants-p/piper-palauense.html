Piper palauense

Piper palauense, commonly known as the Palau pepper, is a climbing vine native to the island nation of Palau in the western Pacific.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper plants such as black pepper. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by bright red, berry-like fruits that are edible. Its leaves are ovate with a glossy surface and emit a mild peppery aroma.

Piper palauense is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional use in local cuisine.
