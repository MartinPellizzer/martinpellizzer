Potentilla nivea var. pallidior

Potentilla nivea var. pallidior, commonly known as the pale snow willow, is a low-growing shrub native to the Arctic and alpine regions of Eurasia.

It features small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in the summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has slender, often hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine, white pubescence. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to cold, harsh environments.

Its botanical characteristics make it a hardy and resilient plant suited to extreme climates.
