Pleurothallis cycesis

Pleurothallis cycesis, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and striking yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in the understory of forests. Its flowers are highly fragrant, attracting pollinators such as bees and moths.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated by enthusiasts but remains relatively rare in the wild.
