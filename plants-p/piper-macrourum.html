Piper macrourum

Piper macrourum, commonly known as the long-tongued pepper, is a flowering plant native to the rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species. The plant is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and heart-shaped leaves. It produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into elongated, thin fruits.

The fruit is often used in traditional medicine and as a spice, though it is less commonly consumed than other pepper varieties.
