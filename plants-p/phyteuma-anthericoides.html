Phyteuma anthericoides

Phyteuma anthericoides, commonly known as the narrow-leaved phyteuma, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically blooms in late summer, producing pale blue to violet flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often with a slightly hairy surface.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens and borders.
