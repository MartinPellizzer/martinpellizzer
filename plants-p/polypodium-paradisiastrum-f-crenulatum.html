Polypodium paradisiastrum f. crenulatum

Polypodium paradisiastrum f. crenulatum, commonly known as the crested polypody, is a fern native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, finely divided fronds that often exhibit a wavy or curled margin, giving it a unique appearance. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte on trees or as a terrestrial plant in moist, shaded environments. Its leaves are usually leathery and dark green, with a rough texture.

This fern is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in shaded gardens or as a houseplant.
