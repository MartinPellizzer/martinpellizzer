Passiflora lobata

Passiflora lobata, commonly known as the lobed passionflower, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, lobed leaves and showy, fragrant flowers that feature a complex structure with five sepals, five petals, and numerous stamens. The plant produces edible, orange-yellow berries that are rich in antioxidants and nutrients. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Passiflora lobata is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
