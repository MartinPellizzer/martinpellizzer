Pelargonium citrosum

Pelargonium citrosum, commonly known as the lemon-scented geranium, is a species of flowering plant in the family Geraniaceae.

It is native to South Africa and is characterized by its fragrant, lemon-scented leaves and showy, five-petaled flowers. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub with hairy stems and serrated foliage. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and aromatic properties.

Pelargonium citrosum is also used in herbal remedies and as a natural insect repellent.
