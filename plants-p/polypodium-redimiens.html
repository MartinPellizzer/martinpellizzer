Polypodium redimiens

Polypodium redimiens, commonly known as the red-veined shield fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Polypodiaceae.

It is characterized by its intricate, lace-like fronds that are often adorned with distinct red veins, giving it its common name. Native to tropical and subtropical regions, this fern thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant typically grows on rocky surfaces or in moist soil, exhibiting a creeping growth habit.

Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine, highlighting its ecological and cultural significance.
