Panicum zenkowskii

Panicum zenkowskii, commonly known as the Zankowski panicgrass, is a flowering plant species native to parts of Asia, including China and Japan.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit with fine, thread-like leaves. The plant produces small, pale green to brown spikelets that are arranged in dense clusters along the stem. It is often found in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
