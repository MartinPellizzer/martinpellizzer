Physalis walteri

Physalis walteri, commonly known as the Walter's groundcherry, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its hairy, yellow-orange calyces that enclose the fruit. The plant produces small, edible berries that are often used in culinary applications. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, preferring well-drained soils.

Physalis walteri is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to pollinators.
