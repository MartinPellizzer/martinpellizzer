Psittacanthus pilanthus

Psittacanthus pilanthus, commonly known as the golden soursop, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are ovate to elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by berries that are yellow when mature. Its bark and leaves are used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland tropical forests.
