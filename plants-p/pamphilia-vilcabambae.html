Pamphilia vilcabambae

Pamphilia vilcabambae, commonly known as the Vilcabamba datura, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes nightshade plants, and is characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in high-altitude environments, often in rocky or mountainous terrain. Due to its habitat and appearance, it is sometimes referred to as the "moonflower" in local communities.
