Puya lasiopoda

Puya lasiopoda, commonly known as the Andean torch, is a striking plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It belongs to the bromeliad family and is characterized by its tall, spiky inflorescence that resembles a torch. The plant produces showy, colorful bracts that can range from red to orange, attracting pollinators such as hummingbirds and insects. Puya lasiopoda is adapted to grow in cool, dry environments, often found in cloud forests and alpine meadows.

Its unique structure and vibrant appearance make it a notable species in botanical gardens and conservation efforts.
