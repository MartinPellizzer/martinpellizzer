Premna sterculiifolia var. cordata

Premna sterculiifolia var. cordata, commonly known as heartleaf Premna, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It features heart-shaped leaves that are typically opposite or whorled, with a smooth or slightly serrated margin. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by small, fleshy berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The leaves are used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties.
