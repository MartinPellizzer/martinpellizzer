Pandanus tectorius var. borneensis

Pandanus tectorius var. borneensis, commonly known as the Bornean pandanus, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia, particularly Borneo.

It is a member of the Pandanaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and long, narrow leaves that are often used in traditional crafts. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. It thrives in tropical climates and is often found growing near water sources such as rivers and swamps.

The leaves and stems are utilized in various cultural and practical applications, including thatching and as a natural dye source.
