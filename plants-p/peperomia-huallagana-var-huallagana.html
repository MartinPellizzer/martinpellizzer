Peperomia huallagana var. huallagana

Peperomia huallagana var. huallagana, commonly known as the Huallagana pepper plant, is a species native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a compact, evergreen shrub with thick, fleshy leaves that are often adorned with a waxy coating. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or red, followed by tiny, berry-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires moderate to bright indirect light.

Due to its ornamental foliage, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in shaded garden environments.
