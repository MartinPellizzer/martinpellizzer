Phyllanthus urceolatus

Phyllanthus urceolatus, commonly known as the shield flower or urceolatus plant, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its rosette-like arrangement of fleshy, oval-shaped leaves that form a dense, shield-like structure. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, often blooming during the warmer months. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its unique leaf shape and ability to thrive in well-drained soil.

The plant is also used in traditional medicine in some cultures for its purported health benefits.
