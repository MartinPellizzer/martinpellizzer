Penstemon campanulatus subsp. chihuahuensis

Penstemon campanulatus subsp.

chihuahuensis, commonly known as the Chihuahua bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Chihuahua region of northern Mexico. It is characterized by its bell-shaped, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple, often with a contrasting throat.

The plant typically grows in rocky or well-drained soils, often in open habitats such as grasslands or along riverbanks. It produces showy, clustered blooms during the spring and early summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant flowers and adaptability to arid conditions.
