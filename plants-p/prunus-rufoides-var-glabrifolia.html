Prunus rufoides var. glabrifolia

Prunus rufoides var. glabrifolia, commonly known as the smoothleaf cherry, is a shrub or small tree native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its smooth, glabrous bark and elliptical leaves that are typically dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers in early spring, which are followed by edible, sweet berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in chaparral and woodland habitats.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
