Piptochaetium panicoides f. subpapillosum

Piptochaetium panicoides f. subpapillosum, commonly known as the papillate grass, is a variant of the panicoid grass species characterized by its distinctive papillate (bumpy) structures on the leaf sheaths.

This plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in disturbed or open habitats. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a prominent midrib and a hairy texture. The inflorescence consists of a loose panicle, bearing small, spikelet-like flowers.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
