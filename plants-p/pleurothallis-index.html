Pleurothallis index

Pleurothallis index, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a species of orchid native to the cloud forests of Central and South America.

It is a small, epiphytic plant that typically grows on trees and rocks in humid, shaded environments. The plant produces delicate, fragrant flowers in shades of yellow and green, often with intricate patterns on the petals. It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its sympodial growth habit and numerous, short-lived inflorescences.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a conservation concern in many regions.
