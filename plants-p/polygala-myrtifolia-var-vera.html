Polygala myrtifolia var. vera

Polygala myrtifolia var. vera, commonly known as the myrtle-leaved milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, clustered flowers. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a leathery texture, often with a bluish-green hue. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in coastal or mountainous regions.

The plant produces small, yellow or greenish flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
