Passiflora bernaccii

Passiflora bernaccii, commonly known as the Madagascar passionflower, is a climbing vine native to Madagascar.

It features showy, five-petaled flowers with a distinctive crown of filaments, characteristic of the Passifloraceae family. The plant produces edible, orange-yellow berries that are rich in nutrients. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a rough texture, while its stems are woody and covered in fine hairs.

Passiflora bernaccii thrives in tropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
