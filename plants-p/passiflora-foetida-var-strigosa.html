Passiflora foetida var. strigosa

Passiflora foetida var. strigosa, commonly known as the rough passionflower, is a climbing vine native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its dark green, velvety leaves and striking, intricate flowers that feature a mix of blue, purple, and white hues. The plant produces edible, orange-red berries that are often used in traditional medicine and cuisine. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

Its unique floral structure and ecological significance make it an important species in its native habitats.
