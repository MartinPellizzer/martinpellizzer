Potentilla matsumurae var. matsumurae

Potentilla matsumurae var. matsumurae, commonly known as the Japanese cinquefoil, is a perennial shrub native to Japan.

It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. The plant features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a slightly serrated edge.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardiness and low maintenance requirements.
