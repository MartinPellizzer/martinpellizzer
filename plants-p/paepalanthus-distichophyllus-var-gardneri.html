Paepalanthus distichophyllus var. gardneri

Paepalanthus distichophyllus var. gardneri, commonly known as the Gardner's arrowleaf, is a unique plant species native to the Andean region of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, arrow-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, adding to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in mountainous regions with well-drained soil.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
