Polygala steudneri

Polygala steudneri, commonly known as the desert milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of North America.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that often appear in clusters. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is well adapted to drought conditions. Its leaves are usually narrow and gray-green, reducing water loss through transpiration.

The species is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soils and providing nectar for pollinators.
