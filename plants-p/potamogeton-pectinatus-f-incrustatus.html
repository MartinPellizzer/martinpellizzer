Potamogeton pectinatus f. incrustatus

Potamogeton pectinatus f. incrustatus, commonly known as the crusty pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant characterized by its unique morphological features.

It typically grows in freshwater environments such as lakes, rivers, and slow-moving streams. The plant has long, ribbon-like leaves that are often covered with a waxy coating, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its flowers are small and clustered in spike-like inflorescences, which are usually submerged or just at the water's surface.

This subspecies is known for its ecological importance in providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
