Ponerorchis monophylla

Ponerorchis monophylla, commonly known as the single-leaf bladder orchid, is a unique member of the Orchidaceae family native to Australia.

It is characterized by its single, fleshy, leaf-like structure that serves as a photosynthetic organ, adapting to its arid environment. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in spring, often in clusters. Its specialized root system helps it absorb moisture from the dry soil, and it is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its rarity and specific ecological requirements, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
