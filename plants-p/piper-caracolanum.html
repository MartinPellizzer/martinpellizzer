Piper caracolanum

Piper caracolanum, commonly known as the Caracolana pepper or Caracolana vine, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species. The plant is characterized by its trailing growth habit and small, greenish-white flowers. Its leaves are typically ovate with a slightly waxy texture.

The fruit is a small, round berry that turns red when mature, often used in local cuisines for its mild peppery flavor.
