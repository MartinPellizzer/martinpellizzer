Pedicularis verticillata subsp. tangutica

Pedicularis verticillata subsp.

tangutica, commonly known as the Tangut lousewort, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of Asia. It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and showy, spurred flowers that typically bloom in shades of yellow and red.

The plant thrives in alpine and subalpine environments, often growing in rocky or gravelly soils. Its flowers are adapted for pollination by insects, particularly bees and flies.

The subspecies is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking appearance.
