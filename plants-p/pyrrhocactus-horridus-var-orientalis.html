Pyrrhocactus horridus var. orientalis

Pyrrhocactus horridus var. orientalis, commonly known as the Oriental Thorned Cactus, is a succulent plant native to arid regions of Asia.

It features spiny, cylindrical stems that are typically green or reddish in color, adapted for water storage and sunlight reflection. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the summer months, adding a striking contrast to its rugged appearance. Its thick, fleshy leaves are modified into spines, reducing water loss and deterring herbivores.

This variety is prized for its unique morphology and resilience in harsh environments.
