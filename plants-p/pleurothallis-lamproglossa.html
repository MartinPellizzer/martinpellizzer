Pleurothallis lamproglossa

Pleurothallis lamproglossa, commonly known as the long-tongued pleurothallis, is a species of orchid native to the cloud forests of Central and South America.

It is a small, epiphytic plant that thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant produces delicate, fragrant flowers with intricate, often colorful petals and sepals. Its root system is specialized for absorbing moisture and nutrients from the air and surrounding substrates.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is a subject of interest for botanists and orchid enthusiasts alike.
