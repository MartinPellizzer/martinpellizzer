Polygonum roseoviride

Polygonum roseoviride, commonly known as the rose-green knotweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to East Asia.

It features upright stems that are often reddish or purplish, covered with fine hairs. The leaves are ovate to lanceolate, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface, and they display a striking combination of green and rose-colored hues. Its small, greenish-white flowers are clustered in dense, cylindrical inflorescences, and it produces small, three-lobed berries.

This plant is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and stream banks.
