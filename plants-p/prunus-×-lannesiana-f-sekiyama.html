Prunus × lannesiana f. sekiyama

Prunus × lannesiana f. sekiyama, commonly known as the Japanese cherry blossom, is a cultivar of the Prunus genus.

It is characterized by its delicate, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant is a hybrid between Prunus serrula and Prunus lannesiana, resulting in a unique combination of traits. It is widely cultivated in Japan for ornamental purposes, particularly in parks and along streets.

The botanical features of this plant include its small, oval leaves and its tendency to produce copious amounts of blossoms, making it a symbol of spring and renewal.
