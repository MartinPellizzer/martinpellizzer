Pellaea holstii

Pellaea holstii, commonly known as the Holst's maidenhair fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Pellaeaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, feather-like fronds that are typically dark green and have a leathery texture. Native to South America, particularly Chile and Argentina, it thrives in moist, shaded environments. The plant produces small, inconspicuous spore-bearing structures called sori on the underside of its fronds.

Its unique appearance and adaptability make it a popular choice for indoor gardening and rock gardens.
