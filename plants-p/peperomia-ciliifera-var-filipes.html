Peperomia ciliifera var. filipes

Peperomia ciliifera var. filipes, commonly known as the creeping peperomia, is a compact, low-growing plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It features succulent, fleshy leaves that are often covered in fine, hair-like structures, giving it a distinctive texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers that are typically borne on short, erect spikes. Its leaves are usually arranged in a rosette pattern, and the stems can spread along the ground, forming a dense mat.

It thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.
