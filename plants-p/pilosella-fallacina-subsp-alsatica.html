Pilosella fallacina subsp. alsatica

Pilosella fallacina subsp.

alsatica, commonly known as the alpine pilosella, is a flowering plant native to the alpine regions of Europe. It is a low-growing perennial herb with a rosette of narrow, hairy leaves.

The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in a dense cluster at the top of the stem. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments. Pilosella fallacina subsp.

alsatica is often found in mountain meadows and is a characteristic species of alpine vegetation.
