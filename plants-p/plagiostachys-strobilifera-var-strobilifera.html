Plagiostachys strobilifera var. strobilifera

Plagiostachys strobilifera var. strobilifera, commonly known as the mountain bamboo, is a perennial herb native to the Himalayan regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that often form dense clumps, and its leaves are elongated with a slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences that are typically greenish or reddish in color. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in forested areas at high elevations.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation projects.
