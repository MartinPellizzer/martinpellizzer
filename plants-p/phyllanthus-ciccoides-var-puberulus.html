Phyllanthus ciccoides var. puberulus

Phyllanthus ciccoides var. puberulus, commonly known as the hairy false crosswort, is a flowering plant belonging to the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is characterized by its small, clustered flowers and hairy foliage, which give it its common name. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in moist, shaded environments. Its leaves are alternate and ovate, with a rough texture due to the presence of fine hairs.

It is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its unique appearance and adaptability to various soil conditions.
