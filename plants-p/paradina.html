Paradina

Paradina, commonly known as the Christmas cactus, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Cactaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its segmented stems and showy, bilaterally symmetrical flowers that bloom in winter. Paradina species typically have thick, fleshy leaves and produce vibrant flowers in shades of red, pink, and white.

These plants are popular as ornamental houseplants due to their attractive appearance and ability to thrive in indoor environments.
