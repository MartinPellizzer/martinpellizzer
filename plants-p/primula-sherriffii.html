Primula sherriffii

Primula sherriffii, commonly known as Sherriff's primrose, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that typically range in color from white to pink. The plant features a basal rosette of narrow, hairy leaves and produces clusters of flowers on short pedicels. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows.

Due to its ornamental value and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
