Picea parryana glauca-pendula

Picea parryana glauca-pendula, commonly known as the blue-weeping white spruce, is a unique variant of the white spruce species native to North America.

This plant is characterized by its striking blue-gray foliage, which gives it a distinctive appearance in both forest and garden settings. The pendulous branches create a weeping effect, adding an ornamental quality to its overall form. It thrives in cooler climates and well-drained soils, making it suitable for specific horticultural applications.

Due to its unique color and shape, it is often cultivated for ornamental purposes in landscaping and botanical gardens.
