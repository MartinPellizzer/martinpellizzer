Passiflora penduliflora

Passiflora penduliflora, commonly known as the hanging passionflower, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It features striking, showy flowers with five sepals, five petals, and a unique crown of filaments. The plant produces edible, orange-yellow berries that are rich in antioxidants. Its leaves are heart-shaped and often have a velvety texture.

Passiflora penduliflora thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
