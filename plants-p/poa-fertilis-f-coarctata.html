Poa fertilis f. coarctata

Poa fertilis f. coarctata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved meadow grass, is a variant of the common meadow grass species.

It is a perennial grass that typically grows in temperate regions, often found in meadows and grasslands. The plant has slender, compressed stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often slightly hairy. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences with numerous tiny flowers.

This subspecies is valued for its role in stabilizing soil and supporting biodiversity in grassland ecosystems.
