Pelargonium ensatum

Pelargonium ensatum, commonly known as the Cape geranium, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Geraniaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, clustered flowers. The plant typically features palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, and its flowers are usually pink or red, with five petals. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Pelargonium ensatum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
