Petrophyes brachycaulon var. canariae

Petrophyes brachycaulon var. canariae, commonly known as the Canary Island rock heath, is a low-growing shrub native to the Canary Islands.

It thrives in rocky, coastal environments and is adapted to withstand strong winds and salt spray. The plant features compact, dense foliage with small, leathery leaves that help reduce water loss. Its flowers are typically pink or purple, appearing in clusters during the spring and summer months.

This variety is valued for its resilience and is often used in coastal landscaping.
