Phyllanthus arachnodes

Phyllanthus arachnodes, commonly known as the leafy spurge, is a shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its alternate, simple, and often lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in cyathia, a unique structure typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. Its fruits are capsule-like structures that release tiny seeds when mature.

The plant is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
