Polypodium serra

Polypodium serra, commonly known as the serrate holly fern, is a species of polypodioid fern native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its feathery, compound fronds that are often serrated along the margins. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky crevices. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine, and the fronds are sometimes used as ornamental plants.

Polypodium serra is known for its ability to thrive in humid conditions and its distinctive, delicate appearance.
