Paris christii

Paris christii, commonly known as the Christ's Paris or the White Paris, is a rare and endangered flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It is characterized by its delicate, white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in shaded, humid environments. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and a short, upright stem.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat destruction, it is considered a conservation priority.
