Primula officinalis subf. cordata

Primula officinalis subf.

cordata, commonly known as the heart-leaved primrose, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It features basal rosettes of ovate to heart-shaped leaves that are often hairy and have a distinct veining pattern.

The plant produces showy, funnel-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, or purple, typically blooming in early spring. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in woodland or meadow habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
