Potentilla agapantha

Potentilla agapantha, commonly known as the silver bush, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, silver-gray foliage that reflects light, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, white flowers in clusters during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal areas.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is a popular choice for landscaping in arid regions.
