Pisum sativum var. alosti

Pisum sativum var. alosti, commonly known as the Alost pea, is a variety of the common pea plant.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and smaller, more rounded pods compared to standard garden peas. The plant produces edible seeds that are often used in culinary applications. Botanically, it belongs to the Fabaceae family, known for its nitrogen-fixing ability through symbiotic bacteria in its root nodules.

This variety is valued for its adaptability to various soil conditions and its potential in sustainable agriculture.
