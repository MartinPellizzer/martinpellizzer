Piper javanicum

Piper javanicum, commonly known as Java pepper or long pepper, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its climbing habit and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, green berries that turn red when mature, containing aromatic seeds used in traditional medicine and cuisine. Its essential oils, rich in piperine, contribute to its distinctive spicy flavor and medicinal properties.

Piper javanicum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a spice in various regional dishes.
