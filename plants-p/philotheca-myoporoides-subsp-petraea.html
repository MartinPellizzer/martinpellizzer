Philotheca myoporoides subsp. petraea

Philotheca myoporoides subsp.

petraea, commonly known as the coastal myopor, is a perennial shrub native to southern Australia. It typically grows in coastal heathlands and sandy soils, thriving in well-drained conditions.

The plant features narrow, linear leaves and produces small, white or pale pink flowers in spring. It is often used in coastal landscaping due to its hardiness and ornamental value.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and nectar for local wildlife.
