Poicillopsis oblongata

Poicillopsis oblongata, commonly known as the oblong flatworm, is a species of flatworm belonging to the phylum Platyhelminthes.

It is characterized by its elongated, flattened body and lacks a true circulatory system. This organism is typically found in freshwater environments, where it feeds on small invertebrates. Its reproductive system is complex, with both male and female reproductive organs present in the same individual.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological role, it is of interest to researchers studying aquatic ecosystems.
