Psidium grandifolium var. albidum

Psidium grandifolium var. albidum, commonly known as the white-leafed guava, is a variant of the common guava characterized by its pale green to white foliage.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree native to the tropical regions of Central and South America. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by small, round fruits with a mild flavor. It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for cultivation in warm climates.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruit, it is often grown in gardens and for commercial purposes.
