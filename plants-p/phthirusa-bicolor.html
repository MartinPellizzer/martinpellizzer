Phthirusa bicolor

Phthirusa bicolor, commonly known as the two-colored orchid, is a species of flowering plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its strikingly contrasting colors, with green and white or yellow markings on its petals and sepals. Native to tropical regions, this plant thrives in warm, humid environments with well-draining soil. Its flowers are typically small but numerous, arranged in a spike-like inflorescence.

Phthirusa bicolor is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ease of cultivation under suitable conditions.
