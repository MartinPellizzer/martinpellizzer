Phragmites communis var. stenophyllus

Phragmites communis var. stenophyllus, commonly known as narrow-leaved common reed, is a variant of the common reed found in wetland habitats.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and tall, dense growth that often forms colonies in marshes and along water bodies. This plant plays a crucial role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial species. It is also used in phytoremediation due to its ability to absorb pollutants from water.

Its flowering stems produce fluffy seeds that are dispersed by wind, aiding in its spread across wetland environments.
