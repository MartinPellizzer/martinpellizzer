Polygala commutata

Polygala commutata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. The plant has slender stems and small, clustered leaves that are often hairy. Its flowers are usually pink or purple and are arranged in spike-like clusters.

Polygala commutata is characterized by its unique seed pods that split open to release its seeds.
