Platycapnos tenuiloba subsp. tenuiloba

Platycapnos tenuiloba subsp.

tenuiloba, commonly known as the slenderleaf bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of central and eastern Europe. It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in alpine or subalpine habitats.

The plant features slender, upright stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are bell-shaped, with five petals, and are usually pale blue to purple in color.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
