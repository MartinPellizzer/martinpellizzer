Panicum fasciculatum var. fuscum

Panicum fasciculatum var. fuscum, commonly known as black panicum, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and dark, almost black-colored foliage. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, spikelet-like flowers. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, often found in disturbed areas or along roadsides.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
