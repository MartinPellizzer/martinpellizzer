Podocarpus neriifolius var. annamiensis

Podocarpus neriifolius var. annamiensis, commonly known as the Annamite podocarp, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and small, scale-like leaves that are typically arranged in opposite pairs. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by woody cones that contain edible seeds. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland and montane forests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
