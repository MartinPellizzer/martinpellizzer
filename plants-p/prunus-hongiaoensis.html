Prunus hongiaoensis

Prunus hongiaoensis, commonly known as the Hongkong cherry, is a deciduous tree native to southern China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in early spring. The tree produces small, round fruits that are typically red when ripe, though they are not widely consumed by humans. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its attractive foliage and seasonal blooms.

The plant is also valued in traditional Chinese medicine for its potential health benefits.
