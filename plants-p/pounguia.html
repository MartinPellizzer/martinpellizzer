Pounguia

Pounguia, commonly known as the white-flowered orchid, is a tropical plant native to the island of Madagascar.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its striking white petals and a single, prominent yellow lip. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers bloom in clusters and are known for their delicate, fragrant aroma.

Pounguia is valued both for its ornamental beauty and its cultural significance in local traditions.
