Patagonium racemosum

Patagonium racemosum, commonly known as the Patagonian cactus, is a flowering plant native to the southern regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Cactaceae and is characterized by its spiny stems and clusters of yellow flowers. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, with thick, fleshy stems that store water. Its flowers are typically short-lived, opening in the morning and closing by midday.

Patagonium racemosum plays a role in local ecosystems by providing food and shelter for various desert animals.
