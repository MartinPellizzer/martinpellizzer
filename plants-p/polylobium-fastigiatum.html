Polylobium fastigiatum

Polylobium fastigiatum, commonly known as the narrow-leafed willow.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in catkins, which are typical of the Salicaceae family. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in riparian zones and wetlands.

Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their astringent properties.
