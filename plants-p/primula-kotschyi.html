Primula kotschyi

Primula kotschyi, commonly known as the Kotschyi primrose, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features upright stems with lance-shaped, hairy leaves that are typically dark green in color. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, or purple, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and unique appearance, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
