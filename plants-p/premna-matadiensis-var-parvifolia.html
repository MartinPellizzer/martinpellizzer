Premna matadiensis var. parvifolia

Premna matadiensis var. parvifolia, commonly known as the small-leaved glorybower, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It features opposite, ovate to elliptic leaves that are usually hairy on the underside. The plant produces small, fragrant, white or pale yellow flowers in clusters during the summer months. Its inflorescences are typically terminal and have a distinctive, bell-shaped appearance.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
