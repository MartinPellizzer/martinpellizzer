Panke

Panke, commonly known as the Indian almond, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are typically elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by oval, woody fruits containing single seeds. Panke is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

Its botanical name is Terminalia chebula, and it belongs to the family Combretaceae.
