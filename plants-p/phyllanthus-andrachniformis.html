Phyllanthus andrachniformis

Phyllanthus andrachniformis, commonly known as the African spurge, is a shrub native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its small, clustered leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, bead-like fruits that contain the seeds. It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in arid conditions.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and traditional medicine.
