Poa trivialiformis

Poa trivialiformis, commonly known as the slender meadowgrass, is a species of grass native to the temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and slender, bluish-green blades that often form dense mats. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, pastures, and along stream banks. Its inflorescence consists of a loose panicle of small, spikelet-bearing branches.

Poa trivialiformis is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in stabilizing soil in ecological restoration projects.
