Panicum sanguinale var. porranthum

Panicum sanguinale var. porranthum, commonly known as the red panicgrass, is a grass species native to Australia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically reddish in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and adaptable member of the Poaceae family.
