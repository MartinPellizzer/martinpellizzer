Pleurothallis sextonii

Pleurothallis sextonii, commonly known as the Sexton's orchid, is a species native to the cloud forests of Central America.

It is a small, epiphytic orchid that thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant produces delicate, fragrant flowers in shades of white and green, often with a subtle purple hue. Its roots are adapted to absorb moisture from the air and decaying plant matter.

This orchid is valued for its unique appearance and is often cultivated by enthusiasts for its ornamental qualities.
