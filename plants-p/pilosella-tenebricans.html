Pilosella tenebricans

Pilosella tenebricans, commonly known as the shadowy pilosella, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its small, dandelion-like yellow flowers and fuzzy, hairy leaves. Native to regions of Europe and western Asia, it thrives in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and grasslands. The plant's structure includes a basal rosette of leaves and a flowering stem that bears a single flower head.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and often overlooked member of the composite plant family.
