Polygonum hydropiperoides subsp. opelousanum

Polygonum hydropiperoides subsp.

opelousanum, commonly known as Opelousas smartweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States. It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, which includes many species of flowering plants known for their distinctive bract structures.

The plant typically grows in wetland habitats, such as marshes and along the edges of ponds, where it thrives in moist to aquatic conditions. Its leaves are alternate, ovate to lanceolate, and often have a reddish or purple tinge, while the flowers are small and clustered in dense, spike-like inflorescences.

It plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various insects and wildlife.
