Pelargonium inodorum

Pelargonium inodorum, commonly known as the sweet geranium, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and vibrant, showy flowers that range in color from pink to red and white. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically lobed and have a slightly rough texture, while the stems are sturdy and often hairy.

Pelargonium inodorum is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its aesthetic appeal and ease of care.
