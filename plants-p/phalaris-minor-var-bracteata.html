Phalaris minor var. bracteata

Phalaris minor var. bracteata, commonly known as the bracted bottlebrush grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and distinctive spikelets that resemble bottle brushes. The plant produces small, two-lobed flowers that are typically green or pale blue in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and rocky areas.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden cultivation in suitable climates.
