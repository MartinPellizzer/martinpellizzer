Paspalum yaguaronense

Paspalum yaguaronense, commonly known as Yaguaron grass, is a perennial grass native to South America, particularly found in Argentina and Uruguay.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and narrow, blade-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetland or marshy habitats.

Due to its ecological importance, it plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
