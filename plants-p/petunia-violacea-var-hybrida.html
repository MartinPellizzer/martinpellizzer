Petunia violacea var. hybrida

Petunia violacea var. hybrida, commonly known as the violet petunia, is a hybrid cultivar prized for its vibrant, bi-colored flowers that often feature a mix of purple and white.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other flowering plants like tomatoes and tobacco. The plant typically grows as an annual, reaching heights of 6 to 12 inches with trailing or bushy growth habits. Its flowers are tubular at the base and expand into a five-petaled shape, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is cultivated for ornamental purposes in gardens and containers due to its adaptability and showy blooms.
