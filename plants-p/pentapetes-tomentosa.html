Pentapetes tomentosa

Pentapetes tomentosa, commonly known as the velvet tree, is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves that give it a velvety texture, and its small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces small, round fruits that are edible and often consumed raw or cooked. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in moist, shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is cultivated in gardens and traditional medicine practices.
