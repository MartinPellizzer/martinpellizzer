Prunus serrulata f. shidarezakura

Prunus serrulata f. shidarezakura, commonly known as the Yoshino cherry, is a cultivar of the Japanese cherry tree.

It is characterized by its delicate, pale pink flowers that bloom in early spring, creating a stunning display across landscapes. The tree has a slender, upright growth habit with smooth, gray bark that becomes flaky with age. Its leaves are oval-shaped and turn a vibrant green in summer, providing aesthetic value throughout the year.

This variety is highly valued in ornamental horticulture, especially in parks and gardens for its seasonal beauty.
