Phoradendron sessilifolium

Phoradendron sessilifolium, commonly known as the mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches to the branches of host trees, primarily in the southeastern United States.

It lacks true roots and absorbs water and nutrients through its stems, which are embedded into the host's bark. The plant produces small, white or greenish flowers and red, fleshy berries that are dispersed by birds. Its leaves are thick, waxy, and sessile, meaning they are attached directly to the stem without a stalk.

This plant plays an important ecological role by providing food for various bird species and contributing to the biodiversity of forest ecosystems.
