Poa longipedunculata var. viridescens

Poa longipedunculata var. viridescens, commonly known as the green-stemmed meadow grass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and grasslands. The plant features slender, erect stems that are often tinged with a bluish-green hue, giving it its common name. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a slight sheen, and it produces small, spike-like inflorescences.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
