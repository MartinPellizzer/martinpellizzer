Pleurothallis pidax

Pleurothallis pidax, commonly known as the golden rosette orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in humid, shaded environments within cloud forests. The plant forms dense, compact rosettes of narrow, dark green leaves and produces striking yellow flowers with a distinctive, fragrant scent. Its flowers are often clustered in small inflorescences and bloom during the wet season.

Pleurothallis pidax is valued for its ornamental appeal and is relatively easy to cultivate under the right conditions.
