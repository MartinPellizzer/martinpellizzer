Physalis joe-diasii

Physalis joe-diasii, commonly known as the Joe-Diasii huskfruit, is a species of flowering plant in the nightshade family, Solanaceae.

It is native to the Andean regions of South America, particularly Ecuador and Peru. The plant produces small, orange-hued fruits enclosed in a papery, lantern-like calyx, which gives it its common name. Its foliage consists of ovate, hairy leaves with a distinctive grayish-green color.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been cultivated for its unique fruiting structure.
