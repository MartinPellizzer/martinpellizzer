Paepalanthus barkleyi

Paepalanthus barkleyi, commonly known as the barkley's cactus, is a species of flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a small, leafless cactus that typically grows in rocky or arid environments. The plant produces striking yellow flowers during the warmer months, which attract various pollinators. Its structure consists of cylindrical stems covered in spines, adapted to reduce water loss in its harsh habitat.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat threats, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
