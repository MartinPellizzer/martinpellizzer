Piper aduncum var. brachyarthrum

Piper aduncum var. brachyarthrum, commonly known as the short-stemmed pepper plant, is a member of the Piperaceae family.

It is a climbing shrub characterized by its small, ovate leaves and clusters of tiny, greenish-white flowers. The plant produces small, round berries that are typically green when unripe and turn red when mature. It is native to tropical regions of South America and is often found in humid, shaded environments.

Due to its unique morphology and potential medicinal properties, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists alike.
