Plantago neumannii

Plantago neumannii, commonly known as the Neumann's plantain, is a perennial herb native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in a spike, typically blooming in the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along streambanks.

Due to its adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated as a medicinal plant for its potential health benefits.
