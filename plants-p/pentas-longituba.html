Pentas longituba

Pentas longituba, commonly known as the flame flower, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant, tubular flowers that range in color from red to orange and yellow. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy on the underside. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The flowers are pollinated primarily by birds and insects, contributing to its ecological significance.
