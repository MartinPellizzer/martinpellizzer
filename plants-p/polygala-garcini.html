Polygala garcini

Polygala garcini, commonly known as the Garcini's milkweed, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

The flowers, which are usually pink or purple, are notable for their unique structure and attract various pollinators.
