Penstemon secundiflorus

Penstemon secundiflorus, commonly known as the Sierra penstemon, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and open habitats, such as meadows and slopes. It produces small, lance-shaped leaves and is adapted to arid environments.

Penstemon secundiflorus is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, supporting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
