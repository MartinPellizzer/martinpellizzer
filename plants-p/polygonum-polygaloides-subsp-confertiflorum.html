Polygonum polygaloides subsp. confertiflorum

Polygonum polygaloides subsp.

confertiflorum, commonly known as the denseflower smartweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the eastern United States. It typically grows in wetland areas, marshes, and along stream banks, preferring moist to saturated soils.

The plant features erect, branched stems that are often reddish in color, and its leaves are alternate, lanceolate, and have a slightly toothed margin. Its flowers are small, clustered in dense inflorescences, and are usually pink to reddish in color, blooming from late summer to early fall.

It is a member of the Polygonaceae family, characterized by its distinctive nodes and bead-like leaves.
