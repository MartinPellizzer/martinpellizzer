Polemonium caeruleum var. occidentale

Polemonium caeruleum var. occidentale, commonly known as the western bluebell, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range from blue to purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and arranged alternately along the stem. It prefers moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and natural landscapes.
