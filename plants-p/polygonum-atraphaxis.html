Polygonum atraphaxis

Polygonum atraphaxis, commonly known as desert knotweed, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the Americas.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is well adapted to harsh environmental conditions. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually pink or red in color, and it has slender, branching stems that can form dense mats. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, which helps reduce water loss in hot, dry climates.

Polygonum atraphaxis is often used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements and ornamental value.
