Pulsatilla hybrida

Pulsatilla hybrida, commonly known as the pasque flower, is a member of the Ranunculaceae family and is native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, showy, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are palmately divided, and the plant is known for its early spring blooming habit.

Pulsatilla hybrida is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also studied for its potential medicinal properties.
