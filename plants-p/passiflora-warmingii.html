Passiflora warmingii

Passiflora warmingii, commonly known as the Warming's passionflower, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, complex flowers that feature a blend of red, yellow, and blue hues, along with intricate filaments and a crown-like structure. The plant produces edible, tropical berries that are often used in culinary applications. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil to flourish.

Passiflora warmingii is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
