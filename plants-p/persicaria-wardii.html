Persicaria wardii

Persicaria wardii, commonly known as the Ward's knotweed, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs, giving it a slightly fuzzy texture. Persicaria wardii thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or alpine environments.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ecologically important species in its native habitat.
