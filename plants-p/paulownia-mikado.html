Paulownia mikado

Paulownia mikado, commonly known as the Japanese princess tree, is a deciduous tree native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its rapid growth and large, heart-shaped leaves that turn yellow in the fall. The tree produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in the spring, which are typically pale purple or white. Its wood is lightweight and highly valued for its use in furniture and musical instruments.

The plant is also known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and climates.
