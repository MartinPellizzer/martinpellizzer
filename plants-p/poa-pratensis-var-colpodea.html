Poa pratensis var. colpodea

Poa pratensis var. colpodea, commonly known as the meadow grass or colpodea bentgrass, is a variant of the common bentgrass species.

It is characterized by its fine, dense foliage and a low-growing habit, making it suitable for turfgrass applications. The plant typically thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and grassy areas. Its leaves are narrow and slightly rolled, with a bluish-green coloration.

This variety is valued for its ability to form a dense, uniform lawn and its tolerance to moderate foot traffic.
