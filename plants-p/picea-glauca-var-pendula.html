Picea glauca var. pendula

Picea glauca var. pendula, commonly known as the blue spruce weeping form, is a cultivar of the blue spruce tree characterized by its distinctive cascading branches.

This variety features a dense, pyramidal shape with long, drooping shoots that give it a weeping appearance. The foliage consists of dark blue, needle-like leaves that are short and stiff, providing a striking visual contrast. It is native to North America and thrives in cold climates, making it a popular ornamental tree in gardens and parks.

Due to its unique form and hardy nature, it is often used for landscape design in temperate regions.
