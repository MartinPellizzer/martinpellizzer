Pterocaulon sphacelatum

Pterocaulon sphacelatum, commonly known as the narrow-leafed pterocaulon, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its small, narrow leaves and clusters of yellowish-white flowers. The plant has a deep root system that allows it to survive in dry conditions, making it drought-tolerant. Its flowers are typically arranged in dense inflorescences, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits.

The species is of interest in ecological studies due to its adaptation strategies in harsh environments.
