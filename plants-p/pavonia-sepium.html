Pavonia sepium

Pavonia sepium, commonly known as the redroot false nettle, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, disturbed soils along stream banks and in woodland edges. The plant features upright stems that are often reddish in color, and its leaves are ovate with a rough, hairy texture. It produces clusters of small, pink to red flowers that bloom in the spring and summer.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in native plant gardens.
