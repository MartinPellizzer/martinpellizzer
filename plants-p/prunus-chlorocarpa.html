Prunus chlorocarpa

Prunus chlorocarpa, commonly known as the green-leaf cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its elliptical, dark green leaves that are often coated with a waxy layer, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers in early spring, followed by edible berries that are typically yellow or green when mature. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to local wildlife.
