Parodia schumanniana

Parodia schumanniana, commonly known as the Schumann's pincushion cactus, is a species of cactus native to South America, specifically Brazil.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems that are often covered in white, woolly spines. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the summer months, which are typically borne at the ends of the stems. Parodia schumanniana is a slow-growing species that thrives in well-draining soil and full sun.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
