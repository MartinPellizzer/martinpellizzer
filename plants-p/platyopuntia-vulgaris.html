Platyopuntia vulgaris

Platyopuntia vulgaris, commonly known as the common prickly pear, is a species of cactus native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its flat, paddle-shaped stems that are covered in sharp spines and clusters of small, white flowers. The plant produces edible fruits known as tunas, which are typically yellow or red in color. Its succulent stems store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments.

The plant plays an important role in local ecosystems by providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
