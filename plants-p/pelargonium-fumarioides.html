Pelargonium fumarioides

Pelargonium fumarioides, commonly known as the candle bush, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of small, pink to red flowers that resemble candles, hence its common name. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for coastal gardens.

Pelargonium fumarioides is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
