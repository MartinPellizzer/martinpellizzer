Plinia formosa

Plinia formosa, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or araçá-do-brejo, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to Brazil and thrives in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant produces small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by bright red berries. Its leaves are elliptical, glossy, and have a slightly serrated edge.

The fruit is edible, with a sweet and tangy flavor, and is often used in local cuisines and traditional medicine.
