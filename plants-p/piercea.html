Piercea

Piercea, commonly known as the golden saxifrage, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Saxifragaceae.

It consists of several species, most of which are found in temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere. These plants typically have rosettes of fleshy, often hairy leaves and produce clusters of small, yellow or orange flowers. They are adapted to rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils.

The genus is notable for its ornamental value and ability to tolerate harsh growing conditions.
