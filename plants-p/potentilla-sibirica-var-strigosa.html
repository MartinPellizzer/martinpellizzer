Potentilla sibirica var. strigosa

Potentilla sibirica var. strigosa, commonly known as the Siberian cinquefoil, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features slender, often hairy stems and small, palmate leaves that are typically covered with fine pubescence. The plant produces clusters of yellow flowers in the summer, which are borne on short pedicels. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to cold, alpine environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardiness in harsh climates.
