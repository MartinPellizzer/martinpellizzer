Pabstiella atrata

Pabstiella atrata, commonly known as the black-leafed orchid, is a rare and delicate orchid species native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dark, velvety leaves and small, pale flowers that bloom in late winter. The plant prefers cool, humid environments and is often found growing in shaded, rocky habitats. Its unique morphology and limited distribution make it a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists.
