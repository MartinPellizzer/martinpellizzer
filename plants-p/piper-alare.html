Piper alare

Piper alare, commonly known as the white pepper plant, is a flowering vine native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its small, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of tiny white flowers. The plant is cultivated for its fruit, which contains the seeds used to produce white pepper. Its growth habit involves trailing or climbing along supports, and it thrives in warm, humid climates.

The botanical features of Piper alare make it a valuable species in both traditional medicine and spice production.
