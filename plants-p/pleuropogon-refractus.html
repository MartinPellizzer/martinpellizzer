Pleuropogon refractus

Pleuropogon refractus, commonly known as the narrowleaf panicgrass, is a species of grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as sandy soils and disturbed areas. It has a clump-forming growth habit and is often found in grasslands and woodland edges.

Its botanical features make it a valuable component of native plant communities and a subject of ecological study.
