Potentilla fragarifolia

Potentilla fragarifolia, commonly known as sweet william or garden sorrel, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, showy flowers that bloom in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant typically reaches heights of 12 to 24 inches and produces yellowish-green leaves with a slightly sour taste. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardeners.

Potentilla fragarifolia is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
