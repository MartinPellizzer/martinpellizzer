Pelargonium oxalidifolium

Pelargonium oxalidifolium, commonly known as the oxalid leaf geranium, is a species of flowering plant in the Geraniaceae family.

It is native to South Africa and is characterized by its distinctive, heart-shaped leaves with a serrated edge. The plant produces small, clustered flowers in shades of pink, red, and white, which bloom throughout the growing season. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Pelargonium oxalidifolium is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and vibrant blooms.
