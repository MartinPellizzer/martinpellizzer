Philadelphus lewisii

Philadelphus lewisii, commonly known as Lewis's mock orange, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It features fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has compound leaves with ovate to lanceolate leaflets and produces small, red or reddish-brown fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy and adaptable species.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable plant for both ornamental and ecological purposes.
