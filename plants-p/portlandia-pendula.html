Portlandia pendula

Portlandia pendula, commonly known as the weeping rockrose, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing shrub that typically reaches a height of about 30 centimeters. The plant features small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring. Its foliage consists of narrow, leathery leaves that are arranged in a compact, mat-like form.

Portlandia pendula is well adapted to rocky and dry environments, making it a hardy choice for xeriscaping.
