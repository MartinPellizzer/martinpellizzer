Primula pulchella subsp. compsantha

Primula pulchella subsp.

compsantha, commonly known as the alpine primrose, is a herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in cool, moist environments.

The plant features basal leaves that are ovate to lanceolate in shape, with a hairy surface. Its flowers are usually pale blue or white, arranged in a cluster at the top of the stem.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in alpine ecosystems.
