Piptadenia minutiflora

Piptadenia minutiflora, commonly known as the Brazilian cedar or red cedar, is a deciduous tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, fragrant flowers. The tree produces small, woody pods that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is valued for its durable, aromatic wood.

The plant is also used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
