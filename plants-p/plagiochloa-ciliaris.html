Plagiochloa ciliaris

Plagiochloa ciliaris, commonly known as the fringe grass, is a perennial grass native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and narrow, linear leaves that are often tinged with silver or gray. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in dense, spike-like clusters, which are typically wind-pollinated. Plagiochloa ciliaris thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, ditches, and along stream banks.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various invertebrates and birds.
