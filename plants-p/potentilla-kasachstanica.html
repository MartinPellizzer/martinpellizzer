Potentilla kasachstanica

Potentilla kasachstanica, commonly known as the Altai cinquefoil, is a perennial flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are compound, with multiple leaflets that are often covered in fine hairs.

Potentilla kasachstanica is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
