Pharmacum

Pharmacum, commonly known as the medicinal herb, is a plant native to temperate regions and is valued for its diverse medicinal properties.

It typically features lance-shaped leaves and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant contains various bioactive compounds, including alkaloids and flavonoids, which contribute to its therapeutic uses. Pharmacum thrives in well-drained soil and requires moderate sunlight for optimal growth.

Its botanical significance lies in its historical use in traditional medicine and modern pharmacological research.
