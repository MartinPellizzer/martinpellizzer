Pecteilis malintana

Pecteilis malintana, commonly known as the Mexican sunflower, is a herbaceous plant native to Mexico and Central America.

It is characterized by its vibrant orange to yellow ray flowers and dark green, heart-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters and thrives in full sun. Its flowers bloom from summer to fall, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Botanically, it belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.
