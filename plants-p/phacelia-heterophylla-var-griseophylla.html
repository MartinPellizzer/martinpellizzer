Phacelia heterophylla var. griseophylla

Phacelia heterophylla var. griseophylla, commonly known as gray-leaf phacelia, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its gray-green foliage and clusters of blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It has hairy stems and leaves that vary in shape depending on environmental conditions.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and role in supporting pollinators.
