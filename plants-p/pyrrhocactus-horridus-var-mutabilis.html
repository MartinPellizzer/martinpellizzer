Pyrrhocactus horridus var. mutabilis

Pyrrhocactus horridus var. mutabilis, commonly known as the variable barbed cactus, is a succulent plant native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its spiny, segmented stems that can vary in color from green to reddish hues. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the warmer months, adding a striking contrast to its rugged appearance. Its leaves are typically reduced to spines, a feature that helps it conserve water in arid environments.

This variant is noted for its morphological variability, making it a subject of interest for botanists and cactus enthusiasts alike.
