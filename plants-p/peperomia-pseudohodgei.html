Peperomia pseudohodgei

Peperomia pseudohodgei, commonly known as the Hodge's Peperomia, is a compact, evergreen perennial native to South America.

It features thick, waxy leaves that are typically green with a subtle variegation, often displaying shades of silver or white. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters, which are usually green or reddish in color. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Peperomia pseudohodgei is a popular choice for indoor gardening due to its low maintenance and attractive foliage.
