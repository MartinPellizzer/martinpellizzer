Pilea imparifolia

Pilea imparifolia, commonly known as the Chinese money plant, is a herbaceous perennial native to China.

It is characterized by its rosette of thick, waxy, heart-shaped leaves that are typically green with a slight reddish tinge. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers that appear on short stalks above the foliage. It prefers indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular houseplant.

Its unique leaf shape and low maintenance requirements make it a favored choice for indoor gardening.
