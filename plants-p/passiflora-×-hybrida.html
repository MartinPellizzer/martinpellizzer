Passiflora × hybrida

Passiflora × hybrida, commonly known as the passionflower vine, is a hybrid species within the Passifloraceae family.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that feature a complex arrangement of petals, sepals, and filaments. The plant is native to the Americas and is widely cultivated for its ornamental value and edible fruits. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires support as a climbing vine.

Its botanical features include opposite leaves and a unique reproductive structure known as a gynostemon.
