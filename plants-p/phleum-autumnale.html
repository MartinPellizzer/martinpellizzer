Phleum autumnale

Phleum autumnale, commonly known as autumn grass or field grass, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, leafy stems. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers, which are typically greenish or pale yellow in color. Phleum autumnale is often found in meadows, pastures, and disturbed soils, and it plays a role in soil stabilization and as forage for livestock.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological and agricultural contexts.
