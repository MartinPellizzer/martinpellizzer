Poa filipes

Poa filipes, commonly known as slender meadow grass, is a species of grass found in temperate regions.

It is characterized by its fine, hair-like leaves and compact growth habit. The plant typically grows in open, sunny areas such as meadows and grasslands. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of spikelets, which are small, cylindrical flower clusters.

Poa filipes is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support local wildlife.
