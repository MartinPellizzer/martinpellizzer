Poa × herjedalica

Poa × herjedalica, commonly known as the Herjedalen bluegrass, is a hybrid grass species resulting from the cross between Poa annua and Poa trivialis.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and fine, bluish-green foliage. This plant typically thrives in temperate climates and is often found in meadows and along roadsides. Its leaves are narrow and have a slightly hairy texture, and it produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences.

Poa × herjedalica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping and turfgrass mixtures.
