Primula cortusoides var. sieboldii

Primula cortusoides var. sieboldii, commonly known as Siebold's primrose, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern Asia.

It features upright, hairy stems that bear clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink and white. The leaves are ovate to elliptical, with a hairy texture and a bluish-green hue. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in woodland or rocky habitats.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
