Piper yessupianum

Piper yessupianum, commonly known as the Yessup's pepper vine, is a climbing plant native to the rainforests of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many species of pepper plants. The plant produces small, green berries that turn red when ripe, and these berries are used in traditional medicine by local communities. Its leaves are often used for their aromatic properties, and the plant thrives in humid, shaded environments.

The botanical features of Piper yessupianum make it an important species in both ecological and ethnobotanical contexts.
