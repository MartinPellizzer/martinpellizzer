Pyrus kingdonii

Pyrus kingdonii, commonly known as the Kingdon's pear, is a deciduous tree native to the mountainous regions of western China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves and showy white flowers that appear in early spring. The tree produces small, yellowish fruits that are typically not edible for humans but are important for local wildlife.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in botanical gardens for its aesthetic appeal and ecological significance.
