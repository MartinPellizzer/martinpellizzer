Platylobium parviflorum

Platylobium parviflorum, commonly known as the small-flowered flat-lipped orchid, is a flowering plant native to Western Australia.

It belongs to the family Orchidaceae and is characterized by its small, pale yellow to cream-colored flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in heathlands and coastal areas. It has narrow, linear leaves and produces a spike of tiny, clustered flowers.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and threatened species.
