Piper aequilaterum

Piper aequilaterum, commonly known as the flat-leaved pepper or broadleaf pepper, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species such as black pepper. The plant is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves and small, greenish-white flowers that grow in clusters. Its fruit is a small, three-lobed berry that turns from green to red when mature.

Piper aequilaterum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in some indigenous cultures.
