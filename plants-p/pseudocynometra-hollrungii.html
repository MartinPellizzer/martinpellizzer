Pseudocynometra hollrungii

Pseudocynometra hollrungii, commonly known as the Hainan ironwood, is a tropical tree native to southern China and parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, hard wood and evergreen foliage, making it a valuable species in local ecosystems. The plant belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain seeds. It typically grows in lowland rainforests and is adapted to humid, warm climates.

Due to its ecological and economic importance, it is sometimes threatened by habitat loss and overharvesting.
