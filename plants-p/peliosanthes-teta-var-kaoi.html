Peliosanthes teta var. kaoi

Peliosanthes teta var. kaoi, commonly known as the Kaoi yew, is a rare and unique variant of the yew family.

It is characterized by its evergreen foliage, which consists of narrow, leathery leaves that are typically dark green in color. The plant produces small, red berries that are toxic to humans and animals. It is native to a limited geographic region in Southeast Asia, where it thrives in moist, shaded environments.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a conservation concern.
