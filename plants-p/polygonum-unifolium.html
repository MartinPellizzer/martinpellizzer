Polygonum unifolium

Polygonum unifolium, commonly known as prairie smoke, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that bear clusters of feathery, pinkish-red bracts resembling smoke. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as prairies and disturbed soils.

Its unique appearance and ecological role make it a notable species in native plant gardens.
