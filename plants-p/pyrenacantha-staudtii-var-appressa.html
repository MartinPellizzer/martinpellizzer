Pyrenacantha staudtii var. appressa

Pyrenacantha staudtii var. appressa, commonly known as the narrow-leaved pyrenacantha, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its small, narrow leaves and clusters of white or pale yellow flowers. The plant produces small, spherical fruits that are typically red or orange when mature. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
