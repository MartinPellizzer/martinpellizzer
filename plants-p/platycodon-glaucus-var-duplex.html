Platycodon glaucus var. duplex

Platycodon glaucus var. duplex, commonly known as the double-flowered bellflower, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, double-layered blue or purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a bluish-green hue and a fibrous root system. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
