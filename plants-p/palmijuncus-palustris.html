Palmijuncus palustris

Palmijuncus palustris, commonly known as the marsh palm, is a unique plant native to wetland environments.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like foliage and a short, unbranched stem. The plant thrives in moist, nutrient-poor soils and is often found in marshes and along the edges of ponds. Its reproductive structures are small and inconspicuous, producing wind-pollinated flowers.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and sensitive indicator of wetland health.
