Prunus cerasifera f. subglobosa-drupacea

Prunus cerasifera f. subglobosa-drupacea, commonly known as the globe cherry or subglobous cherry, is a cultivar of the cherry tree characterized by its rounded, compact growth habit.

It produces small, dark red to black fruits that are typically edible and have a sweet, tart flavor. The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal, featuring glossy leaves and showy flowers in spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for gardens and landscapes.

This variety is often used as a decorative shrub or small tree due to its dense, rounded form.
