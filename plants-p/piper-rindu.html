Piper rindu

Piper rindu, commonly known as the Rindu pepper or wild long pepper, is a tropical climbing shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species such as black pepper. The plant produces small, elongated berries that are harvested when ripe, typically in clusters. The berries contain aromatic compounds that contribute to its distinctive flavor and medicinal properties.

It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated for both culinary and traditional uses.
