Pithecellobium alexandri var. troyanum

Pithecellobium alexandri var. troyanum, commonly known as the Troyanum pithecellobium, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod-like fruits. The plant produces small, yellow to orange flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its fruit is a flat, elongated pod containing edible seeds that are often used in traditional cuisines.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential nutritional benefits.
