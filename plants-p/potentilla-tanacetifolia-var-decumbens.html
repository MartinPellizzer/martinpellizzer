Potentilla tanacetifolia var. decumbens

Potentilla tanacetifolia var. decumbens, commonly known as the creeping cinquefoil, is a low-growing perennial plant native to North America.

It features small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has alternate, ovate leaves with serrated edges and a hairy texture. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wet meadows and along stream banks.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in supporting pollinators.
