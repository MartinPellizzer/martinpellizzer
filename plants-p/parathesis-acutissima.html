Parathesis acutissima

Parathesis acutissima, commonly known as the sharp-leafed parathesis, is a species of flowering plant native to the temperate regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a fine layer of white pubescence. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in clusters, which are usually pale yellow to greenish-white in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and traditional herbal practices.
