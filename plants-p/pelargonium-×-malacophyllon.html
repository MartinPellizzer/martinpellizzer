Pelargonium × malacophyllon

Pelargonium × malacophyllon, commonly known as the ivy-leaf geranium, is a hybrid plant in the Geraniaceae family.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and dark green, heart-shaped leaves that resemble ivy leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, double flowers in shades of pink, red, and white, which bloom throughout the growing season. It is native to South Africa and is widely cultivated as an ornamental plant for its attractive foliage and flowers.

Pelargonium × malacophyllon thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for hanging baskets and containers.
