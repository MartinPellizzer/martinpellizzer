Petrocarvi

Petrocarvi, commonly known as caraway, is a biennial or annual herbaceous plant native to Europe and western Asia.

It features a taproot system and produces slender, branched stems that grow up to 1 meter in height. The plant has alternate, pinnately compound leaves with a strong, aromatic scent. Its flowers are small, white or pale yellow, and arranged in umbels, typical of the Apiaceae family.

The seeds, known as caraway seeds, are widely used in culinary and medicinal applications.
