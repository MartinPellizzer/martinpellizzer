Prunus serrulata wasinowo

Prunus serrulata wasinowo, commonly known as the Wasinowo cherry, is a species native to Japan.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree characterized by its delicate, single-layered flowers that bloom in early spring. The leaves are elliptical with serrated edges, giving it the species name "serrulata.". The plant produces small, red fruits that are edible but not widely cultivated for consumption.

It is often appreciated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
