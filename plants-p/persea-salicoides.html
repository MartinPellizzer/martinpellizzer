Persea salicoides

Persea salicoides, commonly known as the Mexican persimmon, is a small evergreen tree native to Central America and parts of Mexico.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its smooth, dark green leaves and fragrant, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces small, orange to reddish fruits that are edible when fully ripe. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with well-drained soil and partial shade.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and its fruit, which is used in traditional cuisine and medicine.
