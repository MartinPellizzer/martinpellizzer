Portulaca marginata

Portulaca marginata, commonly known as the margined portulaca, is a low-growing annual plant native to southern Africa.

It features succulent, fleshy leaves that are typically green with reddish or purple margins. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in summer, adding a delicate touch to rocky or sandy soils. It thrives in full sun and is drought-tolerant, making it well-suited for arid environments.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental appearance make it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
