Protium australasicum

Protium australasicum, commonly known as the Australian breadfruit, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia and northern Australia.

It belongs to the Burseraceae family and is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and elliptical leaves with a glossy upper surface. The tree produces small, fragrant flowers followed by edible, orange to yellow fruits that resemble small breadfruits.

Its seeds are enclosed in a fibrous, three-chambered capsule, and the plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential medicinal properties.
