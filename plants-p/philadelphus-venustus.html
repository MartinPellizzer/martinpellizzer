Philadelphus venustus

Philadelphus venustus, commonly known as the sweet bush or mock orange, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has opposite, ovate to elliptic leaves that are typically dark green and slightly hairy on the underside. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained, moist soil.

Philadelphus venustus is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and pleasant scent.
