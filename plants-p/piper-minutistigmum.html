Piper minutistigmum

Piper minutistigmum, commonly known as the small-stigma pepper, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many species of pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its small, dark green leaves and clusters of tiny, greenish-white flowers. Its fruits are small, round berries that turn red when mature, and they are used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The plant's botanical features make it an interesting subject for both horticultural study and ethnobotanical research.
