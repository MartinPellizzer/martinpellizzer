Petunia thymifolia f. gracilis

Petunia thymifolia f. gracilis, commonly known as the slender thyme-leaved petunia, is a compact, trailing plant characterized by its small, dark green leaves and clusters of vibrant blue or violet flowers.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is native to South America, particularly Brazil. The plant features aromatic foliage that resembles thyme, giving it its common name. It thrives in well-draining soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for hanging baskets and containers.

Its delicate appearance and long blooming period make it a favored ornamental plant in gardens.
