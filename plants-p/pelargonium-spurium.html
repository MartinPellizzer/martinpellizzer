Pelargonium spurium

Pelargonium spurium, commonly known as the African geranium, is a species of flowering plant in the Geraniaceae family.

It is native to southern Africa and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, five-petaled flowers. The leaves are typically lobed or serrated, and the plant produces small, round fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Pelargonium spurium is also valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various garden conditions.
