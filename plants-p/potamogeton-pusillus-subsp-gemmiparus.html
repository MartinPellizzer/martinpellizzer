Potamogeton pusillus subsp. gemmiparus

Potamogeton pusillus subsp.

gemmiparus, commonly known as the gemmiparous pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in shallow, slow-moving waters and is characterized by its slender, cylindrical stems and small, opposite leaves.

The plant reproduces both sexually and asexually, with the latter involving the formation of gemmae, which are small vegetative propagules that can detach and establish new colonies. Its leaves are usually smooth and have a slightly waxy surface, helping it to remain buoyant in water.

This species plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various invertebrates and fish.
