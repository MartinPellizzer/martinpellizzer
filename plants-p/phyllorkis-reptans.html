Phyllorkis reptans

Phyllorkis reptans, commonly known as the creeping phyllorkis, is a member of the Scrophulariaceae family.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its trailing growth habit and small, opposite leaves. The plant produces showy, two-lipped flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is cultivated in gardens and naturalized areas.
