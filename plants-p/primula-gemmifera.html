Primula gemmifera

Primula gemmifera, commonly known as the gemmed primrose, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, evergreen foliage and produces clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant typically blooms in spring and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky habitats. Primula gemmifera is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its delicate beauty.

It is also notable for its ability to form dense mats, which help stabilize soil in its natural environment.
