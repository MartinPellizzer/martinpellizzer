Psidium spathulatum

Psidium spathulatum, commonly known as the Brazilian guava or spathulate guava, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its small, fragrant white flowers and edible, yellowish fruits. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are elliptical, glossy, and arranged alternately along the branches.

The fruit is often used in local cuisines and has a sweet, aromatic flavor similar to that of guava.
