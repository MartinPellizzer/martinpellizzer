Podochilus klossii

Podochilus klossii, commonly known as the Kloss's orchid, is a rare and endangered species native to the Indonesian archipelago.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, intricate floral structure. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid tropical environments. Its flowers are small and delicate, often displaying a blend of white and green hues with subtle patterns.

Due to habitat loss and limited natural distribution, conservation efforts are crucial for its survival.
