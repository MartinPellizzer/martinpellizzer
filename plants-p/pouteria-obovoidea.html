Pouteria obovoidea

Pouteria obovoidea, commonly known as the Brazilian mango or obovoide mango, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is a member of the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its large, glossy, elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by edible, sweet fruits with a smooth, yellow skin. The tree is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated for its aesthetic appeal.
