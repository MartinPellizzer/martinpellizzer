Parodia catamarcensis var. riojensis

Parodia catamarcensis var. riojensis, commonly known as the Rioja Cactus, is a succulent native to Argentina.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems that are often covered in white spines. The plant produces yellow flowers during the warmer months, typically in spring or early summer. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-draining soil, making it suitable for rock gardens or arid landscapes.

Its compact size and unique appearance make it a popular choice for collectors and gardeners interested in cacti.
