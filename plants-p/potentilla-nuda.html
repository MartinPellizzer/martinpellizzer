Potentilla nuda

Potentilla nuda, commonly known as the naked potentilla, is a low-growing perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features small, yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has slender, wiry stems and small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or alpine environments.

Potentilla nuda is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and attractive floral display.
