Piper crispatimargine

Piper crispatimargine, commonly known as the curly-margin pepper vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, woody stems and heart-shaped leaves with serrated, wavy margins. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by round, peppery fruits that are edible. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and mild peppery flavor.

Due to its unique appearance and fragrance, it is a popular choice for ornamental gardens and herbal use.
