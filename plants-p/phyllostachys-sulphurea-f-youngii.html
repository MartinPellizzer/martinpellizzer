Phyllostachys sulphurea f. youngii

Phyllostachys sulphurea f. youngii, commonly known as the golden bamboo, is a variant of the yellow bamboo species native to China.

It is characterized by its striking golden-yellow culms that contrast with its green leaf blades. This ornamental bamboo is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal in gardens and landscapes. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires partial to full sunlight.

The plant is relatively cold-hardy and can be grown in USDA hardiness zones 6 to 9.
