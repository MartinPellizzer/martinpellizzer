Passiflora caerulea var. glauca

Passiflora caerulea var. glauca, commonly known as the blue passionflower, is a climbing vine native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its striking blue and white flowers with intricate patterns resembling a crown, which are highly attractive to pollinators. The plant produces edible, blue-black berries that are rich in antioxidants and often used in jams and beverages. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a velvety texture, while its stems are woody and covered in fine hairs.

This species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to cultivate in warm climates.
