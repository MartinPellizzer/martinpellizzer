Potentilla apennina subsp. stojanovii

Potentilla apennina subsp.

stojanovii, commonly known as the Stojanov's cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the Balkan region. It is a perennial shrub characterized by its upright growth habit and small, palmately lobed leaves.

The plant produces clusters of yellow flowers in the summer, which are typical of the Potentilla genus. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or alpine environments.
