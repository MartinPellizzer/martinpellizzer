Phelypaea pinetorum

Phelypaea pinetorum, commonly known as the pine forest fern, is a species of fern native to the forests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, feather-like fronds that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in the understory of tropical and subtropical forests. Its spores are dispersed by wind, allowing it to colonize new areas within its habitat.

Phelypaea pinetorum plays a role in maintaining forest biodiversity and soil stability in its native ecosystems.
