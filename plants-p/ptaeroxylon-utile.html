Ptaeroxylon utile

Ptaeroxylon utile, commonly known as the yellowwood tree, is a species native to South Africa.

It is a large, evergreen tree that can grow up to 25 meters tall, characterized by its smooth, yellowish bark. The leaves are opposite, simple, and elliptical, with a glossy upper surface and a lighter underside. It produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in terminal umbels, followed by woody, cylindrical fruits containing several seeds.

The tree is valued for its durable wood and is also used in traditional medicine.
