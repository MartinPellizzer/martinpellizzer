Polygonum dregei

Polygonum dregei, commonly known as the desert knotweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its robust, succulent stems that form a dense mat or low shrub, often with reddish or purple hues. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or red, blooming in late summer. Its leaves are narrow and fleshy, adapted to conserve water in dry environments.

Polygonum dregei is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
