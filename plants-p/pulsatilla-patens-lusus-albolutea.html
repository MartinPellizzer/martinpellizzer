Pulsatilla patens lusus albolutea

Pulsatilla patens lusus albolutea, commonly known as the white-flowered pasqueflower, is a member of the Ranunculaceae family.

It is characterized by its showy, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine regions, preferring well-drained, rocky soils. Its leaves are palmately divided and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

The species is noted for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
