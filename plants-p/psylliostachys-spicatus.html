Psylliostachys spicatus

Psylliostachys spicatus, commonly known as the spiculate sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, triangular stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically arranged in three rows. The plant produces small, greenish or brownish flowers in spike-like clusters, which are borne on the upper part of the stems. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

Psylliostachys spicatus plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various insects and small aquatic organisms.
