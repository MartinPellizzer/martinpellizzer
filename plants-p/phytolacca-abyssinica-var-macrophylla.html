Phytolacca abyssinica var. macrophylla

Phytolacca abyssinica var. macrophylla, commonly known as the Abyssinian beggar's tick, is a shrub native to Ethiopia and parts of East Africa.

It features large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, followed by red, berry-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The species is notable for its toxic properties, with various parts containing compounds that can be harmful to humans and animals.
