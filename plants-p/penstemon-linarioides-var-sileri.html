Penstemon linarioides var. sileri

Penstemon linarioides var. sileri, commonly known as the Sierra penstemon, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, spire-like inflorescences that bear numerous tubular flowers, typically in shades of blue or purple. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is adapted to grow in rocky or sandy soils. It thrives in full sun and is often found in alpine or montane environments.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
