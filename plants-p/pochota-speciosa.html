Pochota speciosa

Pochota speciosa, commonly known as the giant prickly pear, is a species of cactus native to Central America.

It is characterized by its large, spiny stems and clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant produces edible fruits, although they are typically consumed by animals rather than humans. Its thick, fleshy stems store water, allowing it to survive in arid environments.

Pochota speciosa plays an important ecological role in its native habitat by providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
