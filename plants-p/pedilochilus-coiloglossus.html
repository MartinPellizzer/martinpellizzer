Pedilochilus coiloglossus

Pedilochilus coiloglossus, commonly known as the golden beard orchid, is a species of orchid native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking yellow and green floral structure, with a distinctive, elongated lip that resembles a beard. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest understories where it can receive indirect sunlight. Its flowers bloom during the warmer months, adding a vibrant touch to its natural habitat.

Pedilochilus coiloglossus is valued not only for its ornamental beauty but also for its ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
