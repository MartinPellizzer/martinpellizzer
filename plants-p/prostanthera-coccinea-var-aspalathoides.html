Prostanthera coccinea var. aspalathoides

Prostanthera coccinea var. aspalathoides, commonly known as the red-flowered mint bush, is a compact shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It features small, glossy, oval-shaped leaves that are aromatic when crushed, typical of the Lamiaceae family. The plant produces clusters of tubular, red or pink flowers that bloom from late winter to early spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

Due to its ornamental value and fragrant foliage, it is often cultivated in gardens for both its beauty and medicinal properties.
