Pseudopogonatherum speciosum

Pseudopogonatherum speciosum, commonly known as the ornate grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often adorned with fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Pseudopogonatherum speciosum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
