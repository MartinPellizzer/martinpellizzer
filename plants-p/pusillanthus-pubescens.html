Pusillanthus pubescens

Pusillanthus pubescens, commonly known as the hairy pusillanthus, is a small, herbaceous plant native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, pubescent leaves and small, clustered flowers that typically bloom in the spring. The plant grows in moist, shaded environments, often in forest understories or along stream banks. Its botanical features include a short stem, alternate leaves with serrated edges, and white to pale yellow flowers.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
