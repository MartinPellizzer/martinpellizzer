Phaseolus gonospermus

Phaseolus gonospermus, commonly known as the Andean runner bean, is a species of flowering plant native to the Andes region of South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its climbing habit and compound leaves. The plant produces elongated pods that contain edible seeds, similar to other beans in the Phaseolus genus. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Due to its genetic diversity, it is of interest to botanists and agricultural researchers.
