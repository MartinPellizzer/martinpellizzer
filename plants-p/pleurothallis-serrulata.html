Pleurothallis serrulata

Pleurothallis serrulata, commonly known as the serrated pleurothallis, is a species of orchid native to the cloud forests of Central and South America.

It is a small, epiphytic orchid that typically grows on trees, absorbing moisture and nutrients from the air and rain. The plant features delicate, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and yellow. It thrives in humid, shaded environments with high air circulation.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is a popular choice among orchid enthusiasts.
