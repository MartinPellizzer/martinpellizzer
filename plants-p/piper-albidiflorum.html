Piper albidiflorum

Piper albidiflorum, commonly known as white-flowered pepper vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species. The plant is characterized by its slender, woody stems and heart-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface. Its most distinctive feature is the small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters.

The plant is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive foliage and unique floral display.
