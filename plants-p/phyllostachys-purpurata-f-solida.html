Phyllostachys purpurata f. solida

Phyllostachys purpurata f. solida, commonly known as the solid bamboo, is a variant of the purple bamboo species native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and thick, sturdy culms that resist splitting. The leaves are typically a vibrant green, with a slight purplish tinge, especially in cooler weather. This bamboo is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping for its structural form.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it adaptable to a variety of garden settings.
