Piriqueta ionidioides

Piriqueta ionidioides, commonly known as the Mexican firecracker vine, is a climbing plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the family Fabaceae and is characterized by its striking red and orange flowers that resemble firecrackers. The plant has trifoliate leaves and produces pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in warm, sunny environments and is often found in dry or semi-arid regions.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain areas.
