Photinia serratifolia var. serratifolia

Photinia serratifolia var. serratifolia, commonly known as the serrated photinia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia.

It features dense, upright growth with ovate to lance-shaped leaves that have serrated edges. The plant produces small, white flowers in clusters during spring, followed by red, fleshy berries in the fall. Its foliage changes color in autumn, offering ornamental value, and it is often cultivated for its attractive form and seasonal interest.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.
