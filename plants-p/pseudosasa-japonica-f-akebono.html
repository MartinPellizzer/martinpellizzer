Pseudosasa japonica f. akebono

Pseudosasa japonica f. akebono, commonly known as the Akebono bamboo, is a compact, clump-forming ornamental grass native to Japan.

It features striking, narrow, dark green leaves that turn a vibrant golden yellow in the fall, adding seasonal interest to the landscape. The plant produces small, pale green flowers in late summer, which are followed by fluffy, white seed heads. It thrives in partial to full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for gardens and landscapes.

Its compact size and elegant appearance make it a popular choice for container gardening and small spaces.
