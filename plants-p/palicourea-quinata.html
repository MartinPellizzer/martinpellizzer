Palicourea quinata

Palicourea quinata, commonly known as the purple passion vine, is a climbing shrub native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves that are often tinged with purple or reddish hues on the underside. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, arranged in clusters. Its fruit is a small, fleshy berry that turns from green to yellow or orange as it matures.

Palicourea quinata is valued in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
