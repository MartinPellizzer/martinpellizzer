Pleioblastus boshyuensis

Pleioblastus boshyuensis, commonly known as the Japanese forest grass, is a species of bamboo native to Japan.

It is characterized by its slender, upright culms that are often covered in a fine, powdery bloom. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or brown in color. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in mountainous regions.

Its foliage is finely textured, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
