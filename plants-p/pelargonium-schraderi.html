Pelargonium schraderi

Pelargonium schraderi, commonly known as the Schrader's geranium, is a species of flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or red in color. The leaves are usually rounded with a slightly hairy surface, and the plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for hanging baskets and containers.
