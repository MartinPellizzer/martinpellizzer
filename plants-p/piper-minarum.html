Piper minarum

Piper minarum, commonly known as the black pepper plant, is a flowering vine native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its small, dark purple to black berries that contain the spice known as black pepper. The plant thrives in tropical climates with high humidity and well-drained soil. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a slightly serrated margin.

Piper minarum is cultivated for its aromatic fruits, which are used extensively in global cuisine and traditional medicine.
