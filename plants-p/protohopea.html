Protohopea

Protohopea, commonly known as the African violet, is a genus of flowering plants native to Africa.

These plants are characterized by their rosettes of fleshy, velvety leaves and showy, tubular flowers that come in a variety of colors. Protohopea species are typically herbaceous and thrive in well-drained soil with moderate light exposure. They are popular houseplants due to their ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.

The genus includes several species, such as Protohopea simplex and Protohopea plicata, which are prized for their compact growth habit and vibrant blooms.
