Primula lepida

Primula lepida, commonly known as the fairy bells, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It is a perennial herb that produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant typically grows in damp, shaded environments such as forests and rocky areas. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a fine, silvery down.

Primula lepida is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
