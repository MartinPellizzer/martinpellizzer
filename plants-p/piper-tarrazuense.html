Piper tarrazuense

Piper tarrazuense, commonly known as the Tarrazu pepper, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to the humid highlands of Costa Rica and is characterized by its small, elongated berries that turn from green to red as they mature. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a glossy surface, while the stems are covered in fine hairs.

The fruit is often used in local cuisines for its mild, peppery flavor and is also valued for its ornamental appeal.
