Persicaria hastatotriloba f. viridans

Persicaria hastatotriloba f. viridans, commonly known as the green-flowered smartweed, is a variant of the smartweed species characterized by its distinct foliage and floral structure.

This plant typically features lance-shaped leaves with a waxy texture and a unique trilobed leaf base, which distinguishes it from other Persicaria species. The inflorescence consists of small, clustered flowers that are usually greenish in color, a trait that sets it apart from the more common red or pink blooms of other varieties. It thrives in moist environments such as wetlands and stream banks, where it can form dense stands.

The plant is often found in temperate regions and is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits.
