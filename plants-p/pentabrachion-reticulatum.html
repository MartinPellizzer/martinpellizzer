Pentabrachion reticulatum

Pentabrachion reticulatum, commonly known as the net-veined fern, is a species of fern characterized by its delicate, lace-like fronds.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is native to tropical and subtropical regions. The plant features a rhizomatous growth habit with numerous aerial roots that help in nutrient absorption. Its intricate reticulate venation pattern is a defining botanical feature.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
