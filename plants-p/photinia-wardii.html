Photinia wardii

Photinia wardii, commonly known as the Chinese photinia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that turn bright red in the autumn. The plant produces small, white flowers in the spring, followed by red, fleshy berries that are attractive to birds. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes for its vibrant seasonal color changes.
