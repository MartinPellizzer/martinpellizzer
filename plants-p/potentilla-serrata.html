Potentilla serrata

Potentilla serrata, commonly known as the serrated cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its small, yellow flowers and serrated leaf margins. The plant typically grows in meadows, woodlands, and rocky soils, thriving in well-drained conditions. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a rough texture, while its stems are generally hairy.

Potentilla serrata is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for various purposes.
