Poa halleridis

Poa halleridis, commonly known as Haller's bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and produces slender, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes. Its leaves are narrow and blade-like, with a bluish-green hue that gives it its common name.

Poa halleridis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic and ecological benefits.
