Pentarrhinum abyssinicum

Pentarrhinum abyssinicum, commonly known as the Abyssinian five-lobed plant, is a member of the Rubiaceae family.

It is native to Ethiopia and is characterized by its striking, five-lobed leaves that are often hairy and have a distinct veining pattern. Due to its unique morphology and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
