Piper suberythrocarpum

Piper suberythrocarpum, commonly known as the red-fruited pepper plant, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and heart-shaped leaves. Its small, greenish-white flowers are followed by bright red berries that are edible.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
