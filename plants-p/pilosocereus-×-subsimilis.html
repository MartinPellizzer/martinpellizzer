Pilosocereus × subsimilis

Pilosocereus × subsimilis, commonly known as the Christmas cactus, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between *Pilosocereus gounellei* and *Schlumbergera truncata*.

It is characterized by its segmented, fleshy stems that are often adorned with spines and bloom with vibrant, showy flowers during the holiday season. The plant is native to Brazil and thrives in warm, humid environments with well-draining soil. It requires bright, indirect light and moderate watering to flourish.

Its unique appearance and seasonal blooming make it a popular choice for indoor gardening.
