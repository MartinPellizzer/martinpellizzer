Paspalum barbatum var. scabrum

Paspalum barbatum var. scabrum, commonly known as rough hairgrass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and hairy, rough-textured leaves. The plant typically thrives in wet or moist environments, such as marshes and roadside ditches. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets that are often clustered in panicles, and it produces small, wind-dispersed seeds.

This variety is notable for its adaptability to various soil types and its ability to tolerate periodic flooding.
