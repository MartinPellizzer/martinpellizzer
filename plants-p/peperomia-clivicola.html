Peperomia clivicola

Peperomia clivicola, commonly known as the cliff peperomia, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It features small, waxy leaves that are often arranged in a rosette pattern, providing a dense, foliage-rich appearance. The plant produces delicate, tubular flowers in shades of pink or white, which bloom in spring or early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers bright, indirect light, making it suitable for indoor cultivation.

Peperomia clivicola is valued for its ornamental appeal and relatively low maintenance requirements.
