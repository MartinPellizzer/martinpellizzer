Pimelea gnidia var. pulchella

Pimelea gnidia var. pulchella, commonly known as the pink and white pimelea, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its compact shrub form and is often found in heathlands and open forests. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pink to white, typically blooming in spring. Its leaves are narrow and evergreen, providing a dense foliage cover.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive floral display.
