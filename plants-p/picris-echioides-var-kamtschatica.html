Picris echioides var. kamtschatica

Picris echioides var. kamtschatica, commonly known as the Kamtschatka goldenweed, is a herbaceous plant native to the Russian Far East and parts of Siberia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and leaves with a distinctive, often hairy texture. The plant typically grows in moist, temperate environments, such as meadows and riverbanks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and can be either simple or lobed, while the flowers are yellow and arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence.

The species is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
