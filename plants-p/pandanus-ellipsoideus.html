Pandanus ellipsoideus

Pandanus ellipsoideus, commonly known as the broad-leaf pandanus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Pandanaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of the Indo-Pacific, including parts of Australia, Papua New Guinea, and the Solomon Islands. The plant is characterized by its large, stiff, sword-shaped leaves that can grow up to several meters in length. Its inflorescence consists of a branched cluster of small, fragrant flowers, which are often used in traditional crafts and cuisine.

The plant thrives in coastal and wetland environments, often forming dense thickets in mangrove swamps and sandy shores.
