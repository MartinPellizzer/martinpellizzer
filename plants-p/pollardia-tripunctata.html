Pollardia tripunctata

Pollardia tripunctata, commonly known as the three-spotted orchid, is a species of orchid native to parts of Africa.

It is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and pink. The plant has a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it prefers well-drained, sandy soils.

Pollardia tripunctata is often found in open woodland or grassland habitats.
