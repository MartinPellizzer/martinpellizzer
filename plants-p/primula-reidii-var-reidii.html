Primula reidii var. reidii

Primula reidii var. reidii, commonly known as the Reid's primrose, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of China.

It features a compact rosette of broad, waxy, dark green leaves that are slightly hairy. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in cool, moist environments and prefers partial to full shade.

This species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
