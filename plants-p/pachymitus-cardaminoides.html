Pachymitus cardaminoides

Pachymitus cardaminoides, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant has a robust root system and can thrive in moist, shaded environments. It is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and wetland conservation.
