Potentilla bifurca var. glaucescens

Potentilla bifurca var. glaucescens, commonly known as the blue-green potentilla, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of central Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its slender, arching stems and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow. The leaves are typically oval-shaped with a waxy, bluish-green coating that gives the plant its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardy nature in cooler climates.
