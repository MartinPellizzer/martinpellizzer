Persea densiflora

Persea densiflora, commonly known as the dense-flowered persimmon, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central America and parts of the Caribbean.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are typically arranged in a spiral pattern along the stems. The tree produces small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible berries that are rich in nutrients. Its fruit is often consumed fresh or used in culinary applications.

Persea densiflora is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in subtropical gardens.
