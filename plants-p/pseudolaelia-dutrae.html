Pseudolaelia dutrae

Pseudolaelia dutrae, commonly known as the Dutra's orchid, is a species native to Brazil, specifically the state of Rio de Janeiro.

It is a terrestrial orchid that thrives in humid, shaded environments within the Atlantic Forest biome. The plant features a sympodial growth habit, with a short rhizome and a few leaves that are lanceolate in shape. Its flowers are typically pale green to yellow, with a distinctive three-lobed lip that is often adorned with intricate patterns.

Pseudolaelia dutrae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also of interest to botanists due to its unique ecological adaptations.
