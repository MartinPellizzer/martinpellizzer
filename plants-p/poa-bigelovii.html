Poa bigelovii

Poa bigelovii, commonly known as the Bigelow's bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its fine, blue-green foliage and forms dense, low-growing mats. The plant typically thrives in dry, open habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes. Its flowering stems bear small, spike-like clusters of blue to purple flowers.

Poa bigelovii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in native plant gardens.
