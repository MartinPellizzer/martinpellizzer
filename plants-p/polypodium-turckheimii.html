Polypodium turckheimii

Polypodium turckheimii, commonly known as the Turckheim's shield fern, is a species of polypodioid fern native to South America.

It is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit and produces shield-shaped fronds that are typically evergreen. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its spores are released from sori located on the underside of the fronds.

The botanical features of Polypodium turckheimii make it a valuable species for both ecological and horticultural studies.
