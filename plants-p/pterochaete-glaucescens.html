Pterochaete glaucescens

Pterochaete glaucescens, commonly known as the blue-green panicgrass, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial grass that typically grows in wetlands and marshy areas, thriving in moist, acidic soils. The plant has slender, erect stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a waxy, bluish-green coating. Its inflorescence consists of a loose cluster of spikelets, each bearing small, two-lobed flowers.

Pterochaete glaucescens is known for its ability to tolerate flooding and is often found in coastal and tidal habitats.
