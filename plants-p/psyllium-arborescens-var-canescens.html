Psyllium arborescens var. canescens

Psyllium arborescens var. canescens, commonly known as the gray psyllium, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its woody stems and small, inconspicuous flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces a fibrous seed coat that is rich in mucilage, making it valuable for medicinal and industrial uses. Its growth habit includes both shrub-like and tree-like forms, depending on environmental conditions.

The leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered with a fine, grayish pubescence, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
