Pachysandra axillaris var. kouytchensis

Pachysandra axillaris var. kouytchensis, commonly known as the Kouytchensis pachysandra, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It features dense, dark green foliage with a glossy texture, making it an attractive ground cover plant. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers in spring, followed by red berries that are a food source for birds. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to suppress weeds.

Its compact growth habit and adaptability make it suitable for rock gardens and woodland settings.
