Pultenaea hispidula

Pultenaea hispidula, commonly known as the hairy pultenaea, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, pea-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to red. The plant typically grows in heathlands and coastal areas, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are hairy, hence the species name "hispidula," and it produces pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Pultenaea hispidula is often used in native gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
