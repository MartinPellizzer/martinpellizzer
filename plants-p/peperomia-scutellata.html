Peperomia scutellata

Peperomia scutellata, commonly known as the shieldleaf peperomia, is a compact, evergreen perennial native to tropical regions of South America.

It features glossy, dark green, heart-shaped leaves that are often marked with silver or white veins, giving it a striking appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters, typically in shades of red or purple, which are followed by tiny, round berries. It thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Due to its slow growth and minimal care requirements, it is well-suited for beginners and space-limited environments.
