Paphiopedilum hirsutissimum

Paphiopedilum hirsutissimum, commonly known as the hairy paphiopedilum, is a terrestrial orchid native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique, hairy leaves and intricate, lip-shaped petals that are often adorned with intricate patterns. The plant typically produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white, yellow, and pink, which bloom during the spring and summer seasons. It thrives in shaded, humid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts for its beauty and resilience.
