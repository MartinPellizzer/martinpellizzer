Paraboea eburnea

Paraboea eburnea, commonly known as the silver vine, is a climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, woody stems and heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery pubescence. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by red, fleshy berries. It thrives in humid, tropical environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its attractive foliage and ornamental value, it is cultivated in some regions as a decorative plant.
