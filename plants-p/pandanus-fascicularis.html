Pandanus fascicularis

Pandanus fascicularis, commonly known as the narrow-leaf pandanus, is a tropical plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It is a member of the Pandanaceae family and is characterized by its long, narrow leaves that are often used in traditional crafts. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its inflorescence consists of a tall, spiky structure that bears small, fragrant flowers.

The plant is adapted to coastal and sandy soils, making it a common species in coastal vegetation.
