Persea humboldtii

Persea humboldtii, commonly known as the Humboldt's avocado, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central America and parts of the Caribbean.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant, white flowers. The tree produces small, fleshy fruits that are typically harvested when they are fully mature and have a smooth, dark skin. The fruit contains a single large seed and is valued for its creamy, rich flesh.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
