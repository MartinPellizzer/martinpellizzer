Parrya maidantalica

Parrya maidantalica, commonly known as the Sierra Nevada lily, is a striking alpine plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, showy white or pinkish flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or open meadow habitats at high elevations. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it is often found in the Sierra Nevada and other mountain ranges.

Parrya maidantalica is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in alpine ecosystems.
