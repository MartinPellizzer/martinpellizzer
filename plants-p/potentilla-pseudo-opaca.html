Potentilla pseudo-opaca

Potentilla pseudo-opaca, commonly known as the white potentilla, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has slender, wiry stems and small, oval-shaped leaves with a hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in alpine or rocky habitats.

Botanically, it belongs to the Rosaceae family, which includes other familiar plants like roses and strawberries.
