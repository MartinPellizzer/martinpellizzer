Potentilla anserina var. aurantiaca

Potentilla anserina var. aurantiaca, commonly known as the golden cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant features compound leaves with five leaflets, which are typically oval and slightly hairy. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or alpine environments.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is commonly cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and hardy nature.
