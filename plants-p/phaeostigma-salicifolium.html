Phaeostigma salicifolium

Phaeostigma salicifolium, commonly known as the willow-leaved phaeostigma, is a flowering plant native to parts of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its willow-like leaves that are narrow and lance-shaped, with a smooth, dark green upper surface. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically pollinated by insects. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or grasslands.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
