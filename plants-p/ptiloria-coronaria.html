Ptiloria coronaria

Ptiloria coronaria, commonly known as the crown orchid, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and has a compact, rosette-like growth form. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, while its flowers are clustered in a crown-like arrangement, giving it its common name.

Ptiloria coronaria is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is appreciated for its unique appearance and subtle fragrance.
