Pentagonia coa

Pentagonia coa, commonly known as the red-veined or purple-veined pentagonia, is a striking member of the Malvaceae family.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its large, velvety leaves with distinctive purple or red veins. The plant produces showy, five-petaled flowers that are typically pink or red, adding to its ornamental appeal. Pentagonia coa thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Its unique foliage and vibrant blooms make it a popular choice for tropical gardens and indoor cultivation.
