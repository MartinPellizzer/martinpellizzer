Piptadenia latifolia

Piptadenia latifolia, commonly known as Brazilian peach or pitahaya, is a tropical leguminous plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves and showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces fleshy, edible fruits that are often consumed fresh or used in culinary applications. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit yield.

The plant's ability to fix nitrogen makes it beneficial for soil enrichment in agricultural settings.
