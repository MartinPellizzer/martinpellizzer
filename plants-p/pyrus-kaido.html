Pyrus kaido

Pyrus kaido, commonly known as the Japanese pear, is a deciduous tree native to Japan.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its attractive, sparsely branched structure. The tree produces small, white flowers in early spring, followed by edible fruits that are typically harvested in autumn. Its leaves are oval-shaped with a smooth margin and a glossy green surface.

Pyrus kaido is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and historical significance in Japanese culture.
