Panicum chapadense

Panicum chapadense, commonly known as Chapada grass, is a perennial grass native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces fluffy, spike-like inflorescences that are often used in ornamental gardening. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for dry climates.

Its adaptability and drought tolerance make it a valuable plant for ecological restoration projects.
