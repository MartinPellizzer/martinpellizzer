Persicaria lapathifolia subsp. africana

Persicaria lapathifolia subsp.

africana, commonly known as African knotweed, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa. It features upright stems that are often reddish in color and covered with fine hairs.

The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or white, blooming in late summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly serrated edge, with a rough texture.

This species is adapted to moist environments and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.
