Potentilla recta subsp. viscidula

Potentilla recta subsp.

viscidula, commonly known as sticky cinquefoil, is a perennial shrub native to temperate regions of Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive, sticky texture.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value.
