Pyrus lidjiangensis

Pyrus lidjiangensis, commonly known as the Lijiang pear, is a rare species native to China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its deciduous leaves and showy white flowers. The plant produces small, edible fruits that are typically harvested in late summer. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in mountainous regions with well-drained soil.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a valuable species for botanical research and conservation efforts.
