Polystachya lacroixiana

Polystachya lacroixiana, commonly known as the lacroix's bamboo orchid, is a member of the orchid family (Orchidaceae) and is native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers that bloom during the rainy season. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest understories or along stream banks. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense colonies, making it a notable species in its native habitat.

The botanical interest in Polystachya lacroixiana lies in its unique morphology and ecological adaptation to its environment.
