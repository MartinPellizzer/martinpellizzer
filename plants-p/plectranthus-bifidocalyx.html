Plectranthus bifidocalyx

Plectranthus bifidocalyx, commonly known as the narrow-leaved plectranthus, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, tubular flowers that bloom in shades of purple and pink. The plant is often grown as an ornamental due to its attractive foliage and ability to thrive in a variety of conditions. It is a member of the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs.

Plectranthus bifidocalyx is also valued for its use in traditional medicine and as a source of essential oils.
