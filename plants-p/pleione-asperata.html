Pleione asperata

Pleione asperata, commonly known as the rough pleione, is a species of orchid native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces showy, fragrant flowers with a distinctive coloration. The plant typically blooms in late winter or early spring, adding a unique touch to the landscape during the colder months. Its leaves are broad and leathery, adapted to retain moisture in its natural habitat.

Pleione asperata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in a controlled environment.
