Piper auritilaminum

Piper auritilaminum, commonly known as the golden pipevine, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many species of flowering plants. The plant is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. It produces dark blue or black berries that are edible but should be consumed with caution.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and its ability to thrive in shaded environments.
