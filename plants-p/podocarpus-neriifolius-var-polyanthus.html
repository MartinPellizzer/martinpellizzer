Podocarpus neriifolius var. polyanthus

Podocarpus neriifolius var. polyanthus, commonly known as the variegated satinwood, is a species of evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves with striking white or yellow variegation, which adds visual interest to gardens. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by spherical, fleshy berries that are red when ripe. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often used as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage.

Podocarpus neriifolius var. polyanthus is also valued for its durability and is sometimes used in landscaping for its dense, compact growth habit.
