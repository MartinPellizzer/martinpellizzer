Piper semperflorens

Piper semperflorens, commonly known as the everblooming pepper plant, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom continuously throughout the year. The plant produces small, red, peppercorn-like fruits that are edible and have a mild, spicy flavor. It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant or for its culinary uses.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for both gardeners and those interested in exotic plants.
