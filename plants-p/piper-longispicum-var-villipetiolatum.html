Piper longispicum var. villipetiolatum

Piper longispicum var. villipetiolatum, commonly known as the long-stemmed pepper plant, is a tropical species belonging to the Piperaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, vine-like growth habit and elongated, lanceolate leaves that are often pubescent. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, followed by small, berry-like fruits that turn red when mature. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is native to parts of Southeast Asia.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in tropical gardens.
