Prunus divaricata subsp. turcomanica

Prunus divaricata subsp.

turcomanica, commonly known as the Turkestan cherry, is a shrub or small tree native to Central Asia. It is characterized by its slender, often branched stems and small, oval leaves that are typically hairy on the underside.

The flowers are usually white or pale pink, appearing in clusters, and the fruit is a small, reddish drupe. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky or sandy soils.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience to harsh climatic conditions.
