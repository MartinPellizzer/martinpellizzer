Pelargonium malacoides

Pelargonium malacoides, commonly known as the Malacoides geranium, is a species of flowering plant in the Geraniaceae family.

It is native to South Africa and is characterized by its distinctive, often variegated foliage. The plant produces showy, five-petaled flowers in shades of pink, red, and white, typically blooming in spring and summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Its ornamental value and relatively low maintenance requirements contribute to its appeal among gardeners.
