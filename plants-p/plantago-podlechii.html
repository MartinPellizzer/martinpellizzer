Plantago podlechii

Plantago podlechii, commonly known as the Polish plantain, is a perennial herb native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its rosette of broad, waxy leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in dense spikes, which are typically borne on slender stems. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, along roadsides, and in disturbed areas.

The species is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
