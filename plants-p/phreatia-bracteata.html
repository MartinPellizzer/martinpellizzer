Phreatia bracteata

Phreatia bracteata, commonly known as the pitcher orchid, is a tropical epiphytic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique pitcher-shaped inflorescence that resembles a hunting trap. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found growing on trees or rocks in tropical rainforests. Its flowers are typically green or yellowish, with a strong, sweet scent to attract pollinators.

Phreatia bracteata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for horticultural purposes.
