Persicaria duclouxii var. hypoleuca

Persicaria duclouxii var. hypoleuca, commonly known as white-veined smartweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, which includes other familiar plants like buckwheat and sorrel. The plant is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, white or pale pink flowers that appear in clusters. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped with distinct white veins, giving it a striking appearance.

It prefers moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.
