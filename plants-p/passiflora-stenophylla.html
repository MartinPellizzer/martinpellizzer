Passiflora stenophylla

Passiflora stenophylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaf passionflower, is a species native to Central and South America.

It is a climbing vine that produces striking, colorful flowers with intricate structures. The plant features palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, and its flowers have a unique arrangement of sepals, petals, and filaments. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in humid, shaded environments.

The fruit, which develops from the flower, is edible and contains small, seed-like structures.
