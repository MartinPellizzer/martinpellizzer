Ponthieva ephippium

Ponthieva ephippium, commonly known as the velvetleaf or velvetleaf morning glory, is a tropical vine belonging to the Convolvulaceae family.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, smooth seeds. It is native to Central and South America and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fields.

Due to its rapid growth and invasive potential, it is considered a weed in many regions.
