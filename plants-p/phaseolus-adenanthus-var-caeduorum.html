Phaseolus adenanthus var. caeduorum

Phaseolus adenanthus var. caeduorum, commonly known as the Mexican coffee bean plant, is a climbing legume native to Central America.

It produces small, green, bean-like fruits that mature to a dark brown color. The plant is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and showy, yellow-orange flowers. It thrives in warm, tropical climates and is often grown for ornamental purposes.

The seeds of this variety are not typically used for coffee but are sometimes cultivated for their unique appearance.
