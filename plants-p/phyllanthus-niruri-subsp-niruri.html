Phyllanthus niruri subsp. niruri

Phyllanthus niruri subsp.

niruri, commonly known as stonebreaker or cheeseweed, is a small herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Americas. It typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and along stream banks.

The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy surface and produces small, greenish-yellow flowers clustered in axillary inflorescences. Its fruits are small, capsule-like structures that contain several tiny seeds.

The plant is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional medicine for various health purposes.
