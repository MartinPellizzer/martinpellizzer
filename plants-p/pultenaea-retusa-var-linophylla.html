Pultenaea retusa var. linophylla

Pultenaea retusa var. linophylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaved pultenaea, is a shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often branching stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are usually pink or red, arranged in clusters along the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and dry forest habitats.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
