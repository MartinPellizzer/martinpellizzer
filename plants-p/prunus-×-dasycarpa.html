Prunus × dasycarpa

Prunus × dasycarpa, commonly known as the Japanese apricot, is a hybrid species within the Prunus genus.

It is characterized by its ornamental value, featuring showy, fragrant flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows as a small tree or shrub, with smooth, gray bark and oval-shaped leaves. Its fruit is a small, yellow or orange drupe that ripens in late summer.

The botanical features of Prunus × dasycarpa make it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
