Pultenaea villifera var. glabrescens

Pultenaea villifera var. glabrescens, commonly known as the silky pultenaea, is a shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and small, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs. The flowers are typically pale yellow or cream, arranged in dense, spike-like inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open forests and heathlands.

This variety is distinguished by its reduced foliage and soft, glabrous appearance compared to the typical form.
