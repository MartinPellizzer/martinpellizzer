Persoonia rufiflora

Persoonia rufiflora, commonly known as the red-flowered geebung, is a species native to Australia, particularly found in the eastern and southern regions.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in heathlands and sclerophyll forests. The plant is characterized by its small, leathery leaves and striking red or pink flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. Its inflorescence consists of dense clusters of flowers, which are often visited by nectar-feeding birds and insects.

Persoonia rufiflora is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
