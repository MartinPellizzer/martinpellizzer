Pithecellobium malinoense

Pithecellobium malinoense, commonly known as the Malinoense Pithecellobium, is a tropical shrub native to the Pacific Islands.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces edible pods that contain small, sweet fruits similar to beans. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in coastal and lowland areas.

Due to its ornamental value and potential nutritional benefits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and for local use.
