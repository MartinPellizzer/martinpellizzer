Pavetta indica var. tomentosa

Pavetta indica var. tomentosa, commonly known as the tomentose pavetta, is a shrub native to parts of Asia, including India and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, oval-shaped leaves that are densely covered in fine, white hairs, giving the plant a fuzzy appearance. The flowers are typically white or pale pink, with five petals and a distinctive tubular corolla. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
