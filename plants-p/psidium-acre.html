Psidium acre

Psidium acre, commonly known as the sour sop or custard apple, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Caribbean and Central America.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and fragrant, yellowish-green flowers. The fruit is distinctive, with a spiky exterior and a creamy, aromatic pulp that contains numerous seeds. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
