Pelargonium elandsmontanum

Pelargonium elandsmontanum, commonly known as the Elandsmontana geranium, is a species native to South Africa.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, five-petaled flowers. The leaves are typically rounded and slightly hairy, with a distinctive blue-green coloration. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens and containers.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
