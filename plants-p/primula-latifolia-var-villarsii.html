Primula latifolia var. villarsii

Primula latifolia var. villarsii, commonly known as the broadleaf primrose, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe.

It is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves that are typically hairy and have a distinctive silvery-green coloration. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink, white, and purple, which bloom in early spring. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in woodland or rocky habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and delicate blossoms.
