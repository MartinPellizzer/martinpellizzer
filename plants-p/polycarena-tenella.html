Polycarena tenella

Polycarena tenella, commonly known as the slender water milfoil, is a small aquatic plant native to North America.

It typically grows in still or slow-moving freshwater environments such as ponds and lakes. The plant has finely divided, ribbon-like leaves that are usually arranged in whorls around the stem. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often submerged, followed by tiny, floating fruits.

Polycarena tenella plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for microorganisms and invertebrates.
