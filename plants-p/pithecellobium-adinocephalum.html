Pithecellobium adinocephalum

Pithecellobium adinocephalum, commonly known as the small-headed pithecellobium, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as under forest canopies. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange, with a distinctive spurred structure.

The fruit is a legume that contains several edible seeds, making it a valuable resource in some regions.
