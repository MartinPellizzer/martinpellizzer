Pilocereus exerens

Pilocereus exerens, commonly known as the night blooming cereus, is a species of cactus native to South America.

It is characterized by its spiny stems and is typically found in arid or semi-arid environments. The plant produces large, showy white flowers that open at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. Its flowers emit a sweet fragrance, which enhances its appeal during the evening hours.

Pilocereus exerens is relatively easy to care for, making it a popular choice for cactus enthusiasts.
