Piper bisacuminatum

Piper bisacuminatum, commonly known as the black pepper plant, is a tropical vine belonging to the Piperaceae family.

It is native to regions of Southeast Asia, including India and Indonesia, and thrives in warm, humid climates. The plant is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and small, clustered flowers that give way to the familiar black peppercorns used globally in cuisine and medicine. Its fruit, which turns from green to red to black as it matures, is the primary part used for its pungent flavor and medicinal properties.

The plant's botanical features make it an important species in both agricultural and ecological contexts.
