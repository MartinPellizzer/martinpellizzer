Piper pseudoperuvianum

Piper pseudoperuvianum, commonly known as the Peruvian pepper plant, is a climbing vine native to the Amazon rainforest.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves and small, clustered flowers. Its fruit is a small, three-lobed berry that turns red when mature.

It is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties.
