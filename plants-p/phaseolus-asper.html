Phaseolus asper

Phaseolus asper, commonly known as the rough pole bean, is a species of legume native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its hairy, often pubescent stems and leaves, which give it its common name. The plant produces elongated pods that contain small, angular seeds. It is typically grown as a climbing plant, requiring support for its vigorous growth.

Botanically, it is valued for its nutritional content and is cultivated in various regions for both food and agricultural purposes.
