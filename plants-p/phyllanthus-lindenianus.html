Phyllanthus lindenianus

Phyllanthus lindenianus, commonly known as the Lindenberg's spurge, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows up to 3 meters in height, featuring simple, alternate leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, followed by spherical, fleshy fruits containing several seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories or along stream banks.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for potential medicinal properties.
