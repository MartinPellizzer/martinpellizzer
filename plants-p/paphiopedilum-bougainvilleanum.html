Paphiopedilum bougainvilleanum

Paphiopedilum bougainvilleanum, commonly known as the Bougainvillean orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the Philippines.

It features a short, fleshy rhizome and produces a single, hairy pseudobulb with a single, upright, leathery leaf. The plant blooms with a single, showy, yellow-green flower that has a distinctive, lip-like structure. It prefers shaded, humid environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its flowers are highly fragrant and attract various pollinators, including bees and flies.
