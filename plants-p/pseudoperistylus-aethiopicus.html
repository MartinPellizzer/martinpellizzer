Pseudoperistylus aethiopicus

Pseudoperistylus aethiopicus, commonly known as the African orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that typically feature a combination of white, yellow, and green hues. The plant grows as an epiphyte, often found on trees in humid, shaded environments. Its roots are specialized for absorbing moisture and nutrients from the air and surrounding substrates.

The orchid's unique floral structure and ecological adaptations make it a notable species within the Orchidaceae family.
