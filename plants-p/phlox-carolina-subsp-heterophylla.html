Phlox carolina subsp. heterophylla

Phlox carolina subsp.

heterophylla, commonly known as the Carolina phlox, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States. It typically grows to a height of 12 to 24 inches and features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy.

The plant produces clusters of showy, tubular flowers in shades of pink, red, or white, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It prefers moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for wetland gardens.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species for ecological studies and native plant landscaping.
