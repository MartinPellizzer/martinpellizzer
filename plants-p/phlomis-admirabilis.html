Phlomis admirabilis

Phlomis admirabilis, commonly known as the showy phlomis, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of showy, yellowish-orange flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has lance-shaped, gray-green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy addition to rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Phlomis admirabilis is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
