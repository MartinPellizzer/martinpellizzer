Patagonium latistipula

Patagonium latistipula, commonly known as the broad-tipped Patagonian grass, is a perennial herb native to the grasslands of southern South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and dense, grass-like foliage. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typical of the Poaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to the temperate to cold climates of its native range.

Patagonium latistipula plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for local wildlife.
