Piper manzanillanum

Piper manzanillanum, commonly known as the Mexican pepper plant, is a tropical vine belonging to the Piperaceae family.

It is native to Central America and northern South America, where it thrives in humid, warm climates. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that develop into three-lobed, reddish berries. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a slightly peppery aroma, which is characteristic of the genus Piper.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in local medicine.
