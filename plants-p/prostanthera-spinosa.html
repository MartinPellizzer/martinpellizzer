Prostanthera spinosa

Prostanthera spinosa, commonly known as the spiked mintbush, is a shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface, and it produces small, fragrant white or pale blue flowers. Prostanthera spinosa is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract native pollinators.

It is also used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.
