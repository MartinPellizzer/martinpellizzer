Phyllostachys montana

Phyllostachys montana, commonly known as the bamboo of the mountains, is a species of grass belonging to the Poaceae family.

It is native to East Asia, particularly China, where it grows in mountainous regions. This plant is characterized by its tall, slender culms that can reach heights of up to 10 meters. Its leaves are long, narrow, and often have a slightly wavy edge.

Phyllostachys montana is valued for its ornamental qualities and is also used in traditional Chinese medicine.
