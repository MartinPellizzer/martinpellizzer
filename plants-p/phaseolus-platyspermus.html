Phaseolus platyspermus

Phaseolus platyspermus, commonly known as the broad-seeded bean, is a species native to Central and South America.

It is a herbaceous annual plant belonging to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and climbing habit. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that give way to pods containing flat, broad seeds. It thrives in warm, tropical climates and is cultivated for its edible seeds, which are rich in protein.

Due to its genetic diversity, it is also of interest in plant breeding and conservation efforts.
