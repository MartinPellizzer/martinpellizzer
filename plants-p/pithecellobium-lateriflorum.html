Pithecellobium lateriflorum

Pithecellobium lateriflorum, commonly known as the broad-leaved acacia, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant has bipinnate leaves with small, oval leaflets and produces yellow to orange flowers that bloom in clusters. Its root system is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

The plant is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, making it useful in agroforestry systems.
