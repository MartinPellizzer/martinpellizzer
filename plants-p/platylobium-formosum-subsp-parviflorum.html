Platylobium formosum subsp. parviflorum

Platylobium formosum subsp.

parviflorum, commonly known as the small-flowered golden wattle, is a low-growing shrub native to Western Australia. It features dense clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting various pollinators.

The plant has narrow, linear leaves and is typically found in sandy or gravelly soils. It is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showing drought tolerance.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in native gardens and restoration projects.
