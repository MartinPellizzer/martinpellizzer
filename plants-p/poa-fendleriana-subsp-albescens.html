Poa fendleriana subsp. albescens

Poa fendleriana subsp.

albescens, commonly known as the white hair grass, is a perennial grass species native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America. It is characterized by its slender, gray-green leaves and dense, compact growth habit.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear white to pale blue flowers, which are often covered in fine, hairy structures. It is well adapted to drought conditions and thrives in rocky or sandy soils.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in harsh environments.
