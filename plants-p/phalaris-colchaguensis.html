Phalaris colchaguensis

Phalaris colchaguensis, commonly known as the South American bottlebrush grass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical flower spikes that resemble bottlebrushes, which are typically pink to purple in color. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is often found in moist, well-drained soils in temperate regions. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.

Phalaris colchaguensis is also known for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
