Pterothrix perotrichoides

Pterothrix perotrichoides, commonly known as the velvetleaf or velvetleaf fern, is a tropical plant belonging to the family Polypodiaceae.

It is characterized by its dense, velvety fronds that are typically dark green in color. The plant produces small, clustered spore capsules on the undersides of its leaves, which are often covered in a fine, hairy texture. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.

Its unique appearance and growth habit make it a popular specimen in botanical gardens and conservation efforts.
