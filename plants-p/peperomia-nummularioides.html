Peperomia nummularioides

Peperomia nummularioides, commonly known as the Dollar Plant, is a compact, evergreen perennial native to South America.

It features rounded, waxy leaves that are typically green with a slight reddish or purple hue on the underside. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually green or brown, which are not showy but are important for its reproductive cycle. It thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Its compact size and attractive foliage make it ideal for container gardening and as a houseplant.
