Pterocarpus kuhlmannii

Pterocarpus kuhlmannii, commonly known as the African bloodwood, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its thick, fissured bark and dense, aromatic wood. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by flat, leathery pods containing seeds. It thrives in dry to moist savannas and is often found in regions with seasonal rainfall.

The plant is valued for its hard, durable wood and its ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
