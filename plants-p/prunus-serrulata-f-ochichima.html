Prunus serrulata f. ochichima

Prunus serrulata f. ochichima, commonly known as the Japanese cherry blossom or sakura, is a cultivar of the Prunus serrulata species.

It is characterized by its pale pink to white flowers that bloom in early spring, creating a stunning visual display. The plant is a deciduous shrub or small tree, typically reaching heights of 3 to 10 meters. Its leaves are elliptical, with finely serrated edges, and turn vibrant red or orange in the fall.

This ornamental plant is highly valued for its beauty and is often planted in gardens and along streets for its seasonal floral display.
