Phaseolus rufus

Phaseolus rufus, commonly known as the red phaseolus, is a species of legume native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and trifoliate leaves with reddish hues. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that develop into elongated, bean-like pods. The seeds are typically dark brown to black in color and are often used in traditional cuisines.

It is valued for its nutritional content and ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it an important crop in sustainable agriculture.
