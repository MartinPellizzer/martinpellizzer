Paspalum chapadense

Paspalum chapadense, commonly known as Brazilian grass or chapada grass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often found in open woodlands and grasslands. Its rhizomatous root system allows it to form extensive mats, contributing to soil stabilization.

Paspalum chapadense is also valued for its ornamental appeal and use in landscaping.
