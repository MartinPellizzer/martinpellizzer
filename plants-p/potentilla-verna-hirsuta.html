Potentilla verna hirsuta

Potentilla verna hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy cinquefoil, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its hairy stems and small, yellowish-white flowers. The plant typically thrives in moist, rocky or sandy soils and is often found in woodland edges or meadows. Its leaves are palmately divided into five lobes, and it produces fruit in the form of a dry, hairy capsule.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and floral display.
