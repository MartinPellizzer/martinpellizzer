Peltapteris moorei

Peltapteris moorei, commonly known as the Moore's peltapteris, is a fossilized plant from the Late Devonian period.

It belongs to the order Peltapteridales and is characterized by its complex frond-like structures. The plant exhibits a well-preserved, dichotomously branched morphology with a central axis and lateral appendages. Its leaves are typically pinnately divided, suggesting a sophisticated vascular system for nutrient transport.

Fossil records of Peltapteris moorei provide valuable insights into the evolution of early land plants and their adaptation to terrestrial environments.
