Pollinia leptostachys

Pollinia leptostachys, commonly known as the slender-stalked pollinia plant, is a unique orchid species characterized by its specialized pollination structures.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is native to specific regions in Southeast Asia. The plant's most distinctive feature is its pollinia, which are compact masses of pollen enclosed in a cohesive structure. These pollinia are adapted for transfer by insect vectors, particularly bees and wasps.

Its botanical significance lies in its role in understanding the evolution of orchid pollination mechanisms.
