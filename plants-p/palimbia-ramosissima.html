Palimbia ramosissima

Palimbia ramosissima, commonly known as the hairy palimbia, is a shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, branching structure and small, elliptical leaves with a hairy texture. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by round, fleshy fruits. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed or secondary vegetation.

Due to its adaptability, it is sometimes used in reforestation efforts in its native habitat.
