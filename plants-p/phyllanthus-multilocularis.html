Phyllanthus multilocularis

Phyllanthus multilocularis, commonly known as the African nightshade, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It features small, greenish-white flowers and produces berries that turn red when mature. The plant has a woody stem with opposite leaves that are oval and slightly hairy. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in coastal areas.

The berries are toxic to humans and animals, making it a plant to be handled with care.
