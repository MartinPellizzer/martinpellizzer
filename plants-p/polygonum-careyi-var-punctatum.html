Polygonum careyi var. punctatum

Polygonum careyi var. punctatum, commonly known as the spotted knotweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia.

It features upright stems that are often reddish in color and bear opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a distinctive purplish or reddish sheen. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in dense clusters at the tips of the stems. It is known for its ability to thrive in moist environments, such as wetlands and streambanks.

Its ornamental value comes from its striking foliage and adaptability to various soil conditions.
