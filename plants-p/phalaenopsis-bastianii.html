Phalaenopsis bastianii

Phalaenopsis bastianii, commonly known as the Bornean phalaenopsis, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, humid environments with well-draining soil. The plant produces large, showy flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, often with intricate patterns resembling moth wings. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb moisture from the surrounding soil.

This orchid is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated for horticultural purposes.
