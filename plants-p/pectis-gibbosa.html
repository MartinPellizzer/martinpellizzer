Pectis gibbosa

Pectis gibbosa, commonly known as the hairy pincushion, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in coastal and sandy soils. The plant produces dense, spiny heads of yellow flowers, which are borne on long, slender stalks. Its leaves are narrow, hairy, and often arranged in a rosette pattern.

Pectis gibbosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
