Pseudolysimachion sajanense

Pseudolysimachion sajanense, commonly known as the Sajan mountain bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of eastern Russia.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are lanceolate and often covered in a fine pubescence.
