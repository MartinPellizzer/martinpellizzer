Philodendron populneum

Philodendron populneum, commonly known as the golden philodendron, is a tropical climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It features heart-shaped leaves that are typically bright green with a metallic sheen, often adorned with yellow or white veins. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are followed by berries containing toxic seeds. It thrives in warm, humid environments with bright, indirect light.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for indoor gardening.
