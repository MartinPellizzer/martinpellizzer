Primula mollis

Primula mollis, commonly known as the woolly primrose, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features dense, soft, hairy foliage that gives it its common name, and its flowers are typically pink or white, with five petals. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows. It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to bloom early in the growing season.

Primula mollis is also known for its ability to tolerate shade, making it a versatile choice for garden planting.
