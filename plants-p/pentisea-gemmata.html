Pentisea gemmata

Pentisea gemmata, commonly known as the gemmed pentisea, is a rare and unusual plant species native to the Andean region of South America.

It is a succulent shrub characterized by its compact, fleshy stems and clusters of small, star-shaped flowers. The plant produces a unique type of vegetative propagation through gemmae, which are tiny, self-contained plantlets that can develop into new individuals. Its botanical features make it a subject of interest for botanists studying adaptation to high-altitude environments.

Due to its limited distribution and specific ecological requirements, it is considered a vulnerable species.
