Populus × petrowskiana

Populus × petrowskiana, commonly known as the Siberian poplar, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Populus alba and Populus tremula.

It is a deciduous tree characterized by its slender, often weeping form and heart-shaped leaves that turn yellow in autumn. The plant produces small, fluffy seeds that are dispersed by the wind, aiding in its spread across open areas. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Botanically, it is notable for its rapid growth and adaptability to various environmental conditions.
