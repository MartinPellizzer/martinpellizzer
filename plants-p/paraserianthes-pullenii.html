Paraserianthes pullenii

Paraserianthes pullenii, commonly known as the African wattle, is a deciduous leguminous tree native to sub-Saharan Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its feathery, bipinnate leaves and yellowish-green flowers. The tree is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types, making it useful for reforestation and soil stabilization. Its seeds are encased in pods and can remain viable for several years, aiding in natural regeneration.

The plant also has a symbiotic relationship with nitrogen-fixing bacteria, enhancing soil fertility.
