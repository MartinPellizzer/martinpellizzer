Pyrus pannonica subpyraster

Pyrus pannonica subpyraster, commonly known as the Pannonian pear, is a subspecies of the pear tree native to the Pannonian Basin.

It is characterized by its large, juicy, yellow to orange fruits that are typically harvested in late summer. The plant has a spreading habit with a dense canopy, making it suitable for both ornamental and edible purposes. Its leaves are broad and lobed, often with a slight hairy texture, and its flowers are white with a subtle fragrance.

This subspecies is valued for its hardiness and adaptability to various soil types.
