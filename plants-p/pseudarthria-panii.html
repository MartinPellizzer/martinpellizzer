Pseudarthria panii

Pseudarthria panii, commonly known as the African grass or false arrowhead, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and nitrogen-fixing ability. The plant features slender, erect stems and trifoliate leaves with a distinctive arrowhead shape. Its small, pea-like flowers are typically white or pale blue, and it produces pods that contain several seeds.

Pseudarthria panii is often used in traditional medicine and as a forage plant for livestock.
