Plectranthus cyanophyllus

Plectranthus cyanophyllus, commonly known as blue-leaf plectranthus, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its striking blue-green foliage and produces small, tubular flowers that are typically lavender or purple in color. The plant is a perennial herb with a compact growth habit, often used in gardens for its ornamental value. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Plectranthus cyanophyllus is also valued for its aromatic leaves, which can be used in herbal teas or as a culinary herb.
