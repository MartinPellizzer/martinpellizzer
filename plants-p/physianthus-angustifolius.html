Physianthus angustifolius

Physianthus angustifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaved physianthus, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous perennial, featuring narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its small, yellowish flowers are arranged in dense clusters, making it an attractive addition to gardens.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, though it can tolerate partial shade.
