Periploca humboldtiana

Periploca humboldtiana, commonly known as the Humboldt's periploca, is a shrub native to the coastal regions of South America.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its woody stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pink in color. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in coastal dunes and scrub habitats.

Due to its ecological importance and ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
