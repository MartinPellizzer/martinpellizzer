Psychanthus papilionaceus

Psychanthus papilionaceus, commonly known as the flame vine, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its striking, bright orange to red flowers that resemble butterflies. The plant produces pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets, and it thrives in warm, humid climates.

The botanical features of Psychanthus papilionaceus make it a popular ornamental plant in gardens and landscapes.
