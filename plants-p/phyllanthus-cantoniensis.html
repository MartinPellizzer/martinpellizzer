Phyllanthus cantoniensis

Phyllanthus cantoniensis, commonly known as Chinese holly or Guangdong holly, is a flowering plant native to southern China.

It is a small evergreen shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 3 meters. The plant features simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. Its small, white flowers are clustered in dense inflorescences, and it produces small, red berries that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional Chinese medicine.
