Pleioblastus variegatus var. tanakae

Pleioblastus variegatus var. tanakae, commonly known as the Tanaka's variegated bamboo, is a clump-forming grass native to Japan.

It features slender, arching culms with a distinctive variegated pattern of light and dark green on its leaves. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters and has a dense, compact growth habit. Its foliage is often adorned with white or silver markings, adding ornamental value.

It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
