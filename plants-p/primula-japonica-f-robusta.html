Primula japonica f. robusta

Primula japonica f. robusta, commonly known as the Japanese primrose, is a striking hybrid plant characterized by its large, showy flowers.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is native to Japan, where it thrives in cool, moist environments. The plant features thick, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating, aiding in water retention. Its flowers, which can be white, pink, or purple, are borne in clusters and bloom in early spring.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly grown in gardens and as a houseplant.
