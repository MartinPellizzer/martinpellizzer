Prunus × saihoziensis

Prunus × saihoziensis, commonly known as the Japanese cherry blossom, is a hybrid species within the Prunus genus.

It is native to Japan and is celebrated for its ornamental value, particularly during the spring when it blooms with clusters of white or pink flowers. The plant is a small to medium-sized deciduous tree, typically reaching heights of 5 to 10 meters. Its leaves are ovate with serrated edges, and the fruit is a small, hard cherry that turns red when mature.

The botanical features of Prunus × saihoziensis make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and cultural events.
