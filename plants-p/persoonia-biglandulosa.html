Persoonia biglandulosa

Persoonia biglandulosa, commonly known as the rough-leaved pear.

It is a shrub native to eastern Australia, characterized by its woody stems and serrated, hairy leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in spikes, which appear in spring. Its fruits are woody capsules that contain seeds, often dispersed by wind.

Persoonia biglandulosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and resilience.
