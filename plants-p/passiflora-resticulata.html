Passiflora resticulata

Passiflora resticulata, commonly known as the clustered passionflower, is a climbing vine native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, complex flowers that feature five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments surrounding the reproductive structures. The plant produces edible berries that are often used in local cuisines and traditional medicine. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped with a rough texture, and it thrives in warm, humid climates.

Passiflora resticulata is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
