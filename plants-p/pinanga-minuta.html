Pinanga minuta

Pinanga minuta, commonly known as the small-leafed bamboo or dwarf bamboo, is a species of grass-like plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its compact growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest understories. Pinanga minuta has a rhizomatous root system that allows it to spread and form dense clumps.

Its botanical features make it an important component of tropical and subtropical ecosystems.
