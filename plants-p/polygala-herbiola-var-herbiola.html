Polygala herbiola var. herbiola

Polygala herbiola var. herbiola, commonly known as the narrowleaf milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its slender, herbaceous stems and small, intricate flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. Its leaves are alternate and lance-shaped, often with a hairy texture.

It produces small, yellowish or greenish flowers that are clustered in spikes, attracting pollinators like bees and flies.
