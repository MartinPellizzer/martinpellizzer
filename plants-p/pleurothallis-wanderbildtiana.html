Pleurothallis wanderbildtiana

Pleurothallis wanderbildtiana, commonly known as the Wanderbildt's orchid, is a species native to the cloud forests of South America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its small, delicate flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its flowers are usually white or pale yellow, with a distinctive lip that aids in pollination.

It requires high humidity and indirect light to thrive, making it a popular choice among orchid enthusiasts.
