Pelargonium × torrefactum

Pelargonium × torrefactum, commonly known as the tobacco plant, is a species native to South Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring thick, aromatic leaves with a distinctive grayish-green color. The plant produces small, pink or red flowers that bloom in clusters, often attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are known for their strong, pungent scent, which is used in traditional medicine and as a natural insect repellent.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is cultivated in gardens for its visual appeal.
