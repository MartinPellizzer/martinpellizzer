Potentilla recta proles astracanica

Potentilla recta proles astracanica, commonly known as the Astracanica cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees. It is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
