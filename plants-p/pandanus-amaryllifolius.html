Pandanus amaryllifolius

Pandanus amaryllifolius, commonly known as the sweet-scented pandanus, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, fibrous trunk and broad, sword-shaped leaves that are often used in traditional cuisine. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences that eventually develop into orange or red fruits. Its leaves are widely utilized in cooking, particularly in Malaysian and Indonesian dishes, for their aromatic properties.

The plant thrives in humid, coastal environments and is often cultivated for both ornamental and culinary purposes.
