Polystachya leucosepala

Polystachya leucosepala, commonly known as the white-sepaled orchid, is a species of orchid native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its elegant, upright growth habit and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has strap-like leaves that are typically green with a waxy texture. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.

Polystachya leucosepala is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
