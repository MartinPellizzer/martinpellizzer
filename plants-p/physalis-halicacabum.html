Physalis halicacabum

Physalis halicacabum, commonly known as the common ground cherry, is a herbaceous annual plant native to the Americas.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its inflated, papery calyx that encloses the fruit. The fruit, which is typically yellow or orange, is edible and resembles small tomatoes in appearance. The plant produces small, white flowers that develop into the distinctive fruiting structures.

It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and waste areas.
