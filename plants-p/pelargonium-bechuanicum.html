Pelargonium bechuanicum

Pelargonium bechuanicum, commonly known as the Bechuanian geranium, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a low-growing perennial shrub that typically reaches heights of up to 30 centimeters. The plant features succulent, hairy leaves that are often arranged in a rosette pattern. Its flowers are usually pink or red, with five petals and a distinctive scent.

It thrives in well-drained soil and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.
