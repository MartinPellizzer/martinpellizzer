Pimpinella saxifraga var. seseliifolia

Pimpinella saxifraga var. seseliifolia, commonly known as the rock parsley, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It features finely divided, fern-like leaves that are typically bluish-green in color, with a hairy texture. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers in umbel-like clusters, which bloom in early summer. It thrives in rocky or well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive and ornamental species within the Apiaceae family.
