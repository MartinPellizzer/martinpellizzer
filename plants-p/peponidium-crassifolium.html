Peponidium crassifolium

Peponidium crassifolium, commonly known as the thick-leaved melon, is a tropical plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, broad leaves that are typically thick and waxy, adapted to retain moisture in humid environments. The plant produces yellow flowers that give way to round, green fruits resembling small melons. Its botanical structure includes a robust stem with hairy nodes and a fibrous root system.

Peponidium crassifolium is often cultivated for its edible fruits, which have a sweet and aromatic flavor.
