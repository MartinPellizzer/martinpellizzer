Pericaulon perfoliatum

Pericaulon perfoliatum, commonly known as the perforate sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the wetlands and marshes of eastern North America.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves that are often perforated or have a distinctive leaf sheath structure. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish flowers arranged in dense clusters, which are typically wind-pollinated. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense colonies in moist soil.

Pericaulon perfoliatum plays an important role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
