Pollia bracteata

Pollia bracteata, commonly known as the African violet, is a tropical plant native to Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety bracts that resemble petals, which are actually modified leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically blue or violet in color. It thrives in humid, well-drained soil and requires indirect sunlight.

Pollia bracteata is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and ease of care.
