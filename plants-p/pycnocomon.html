Pycnocomon

Pycnocomon, commonly known as the sea holly, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its dense, spiky bracts that enclose the small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and saline environments, showing a high tolerance for salt and wind. Its leaves are usually narrow and covered in a waxy layer to reduce water loss.

Pycnocomon is often found in Mediterranean regions and is valued for its ornamental appeal.
