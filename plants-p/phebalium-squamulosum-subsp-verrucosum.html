Phebalium squamulosum subsp. verrucosum

Phebalium squamulosum subsp.

verrucosum, commonly known as the rough-hairy hakea, is a shrub native to southeastern Australia. It is characterized by its dense, woody structure and small, leathery leaves covered in fine, scale-like hairs.

The plant produces yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in spikes, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to coastal and heathland environments.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
