Protea paulina

Protea paulina, commonly known as the Paulina protea, is a striking South African plant belonging to the Proteaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescences composed of numerous tubular flowers arranged in a spherical or conical shape. The plant typically grows in coastal fynbos vegetation, thriving in well-drained, acidic soils. Its flowers range in color from white to pink and are often visited by birds and insects for nectar.

Protea paulina is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in local ecosystems and horticulture.
