Piper maternale

Piper maternale, commonly known as the "mother plant," is a member of the Piperaceae family, native to tropical regions of South America.

It is a climbing vine characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and small, greenish-white flowers. The plant produces small, berry-like fruits that are typically green when unripe and turn red when mature. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
