Potentilla gordonii var. gordonii

Potentilla gordonii var. gordonii, commonly known as Gordon's cinquefoil, is a low-growing perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features wiry, branched stems that are often covered in soft, grayish-white hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, bright yellow flowers during the summer months, which are typically borne on slender pedicels. Its leaves are compound, consisting of five oval to lance-shaped leaflets with serrated edges.

It thrives in well-drained, sandy or rocky soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.
