Pithecellobium brassii

Pithecellobium brassii, commonly known as the yellow velvet tamarind, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant produces small, yellow, edible pods that are often used in local cuisines. Its leaves are trifoliate, with each leaflet being elliptical in shape and covered in a fine, velvety texture.

The plant is valued not only for its edible fruits but also for its ornamental appeal and potential medicinal uses.
