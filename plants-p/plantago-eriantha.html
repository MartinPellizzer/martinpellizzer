Plantago eriantha

Plantago eriantha, commonly known as the silky plantain, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its erect, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in a spike, which are borne on long, slender stalks. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in coastal and inland regions.

The species is known for its hardy nature and is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant.
