Pelargonium tinctum

Pelargonium tinctum, commonly known as the violet geranium, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, showy flowers that range in color from purple to violet. The leaves are typically rounded and slightly hairy, giving the plant a distinctive appearance. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a potted plant.
