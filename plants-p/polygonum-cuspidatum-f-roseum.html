Polygonum cuspidatum f. roseum

Polygonum cuspidatum f. roseum, commonly known as the pink flowered Japanese knotweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to East Asia.

It features upright, hollow stems that are often reddish in color and bear clusters of small, pink to purple flowers. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are typically reddish underneath, contributing to its ornamental value. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Due to its rapid growth and invasive potential, it is considered a noxious weed in many regions.
