Petrosedum thartii

Petrosedum thartii, commonly known as the Sierra Valley stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its dense, low-growing rosettes of fleshy, blue-green leaves that store water, aiding in its survival in arid environments. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink or white during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
