Pilea clandestina

Pilea clandestina, commonly known as the Chinese money plant, is a species of flowering plant in the family Urticaceae.

It is native to southern China and is characterized by its round, fleshy leaves that resemble coins. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers that are typically pollinated by insects. It is a popular indoor plant due to its ease of care and aesthetic appeal.

Pilea clandestina thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil.
