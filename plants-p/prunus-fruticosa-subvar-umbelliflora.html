Prunus fruticosa subvar. umbelliflora

Prunus fruticosa subvar. umbelliflora, commonly known as the dwarf cherry or Japanese snowbell, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It features small, ovate leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces clusters of white, bell-shaped flowers in early spring, which are followed by small, red berries. It thrives in partial to full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular ornamental plant.

Its compact size and ornamental flowers make it suitable for rock gardens and small spaces.
