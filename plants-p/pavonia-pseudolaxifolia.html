Pavonia pseudolaxifolia

Pavonia pseudolaxifolia, commonly known as the scarlet pavonia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, featuring upright stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, bright red or pink flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its botanical characteristics include showy inflorescences and a fibrous root system, making it well-suited for moist, well-drained soils.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its vibrant floral display.
