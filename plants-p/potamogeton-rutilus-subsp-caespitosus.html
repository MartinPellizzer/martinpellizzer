Potamogeton rutilus subsp. caespitosus

Potamogeton rutilus subsp.

caespitosus, commonly known as the tufted pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater habitats in North America. It is characterized by its slender, cylindrical stems and small, opposite leaves that are usually less than 1 cm in length.

The plant produces tiny, green flowers that are clustered in spikes, typically appearing in early summer. It thrives in shallow, slow-moving waters and plays a vital role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.

Its dense growth can also influence water clarity and nutrient cycling in its environment.
