Potentilla rupestris f. villosa

Potentilla rupestris f. villosa, commonly known as the hairy stonecrop, is a low-growing perennial plant native to rocky and arid regions.

It features succulent, fleshy leaves that are densely covered in fine, white hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, bright yellow flowers during the summer months. It thrives in full sun and well-draining soil, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its hardy nature and ornamental value make it a popular choice for drought-tolerant landscaping.
