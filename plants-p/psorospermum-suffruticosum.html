Psorospermum suffruticosum

Psorospermum suffruticosum, commonly known as the Indian hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant features lance-shaped leaves and produces showy, five-petaled flowers in shades of yellow and orange. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release seeds.

Psorospermum suffruticosum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
