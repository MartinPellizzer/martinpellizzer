Petunia parviflora var. glabriuscula

Petunia parviflora var. glabriuscula, commonly known as the glabrous petunia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that typically range in color from white to pale blue. The plant has hairy stems and leaves, though the variety 'glabriuscula' is noted for its reduced pubescence. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.

This variety is often used in ornamental gardening due to its compact growth habit and attractive blooms.
