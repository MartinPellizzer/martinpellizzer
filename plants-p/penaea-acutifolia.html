Penaea acutifolia

Penaea acutifolia, commonly known as the sharpleaf hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 6 meters. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

Penaea acutifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
