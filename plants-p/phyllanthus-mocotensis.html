Phyllanthus mocotensis

Phyllanthus mocotensis, commonly known as the Malagasy phyllanthus, is a shrub native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are often pubescent. The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in clusters, followed by capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is adapted to the unique ecological conditions of its native habitat.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and potentially endangered species.
