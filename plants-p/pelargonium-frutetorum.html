Pelargonium frutetorum

Pelargonium frutetorum, commonly known as the lemon-scented geranium, is a species of flowering plant in the family Geraniaceae.

It is native to South Africa and is characterized by its fragrant, lemon-scented leaves and showy, pink or red flowers. The plant typically grows as a trailing or shrub-like herb, with opposite, oval-shaped leaves that are often hairy. It is commonly cultivated as a ornamental plant for its attractive foliage and pleasant aroma.

Pelargonium frutetorum is also valued for its use in aromatherapy and as a natural pest repellent.
