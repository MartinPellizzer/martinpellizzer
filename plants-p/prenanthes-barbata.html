Prenanthes barbata

Prenanthes barbata, commonly known as the hairy arrowhead, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its arrowhead-shaped leaves and distinctive spadix inflorescence. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats, such as marshes and swamps, where it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils. Its flowers are small and clustered, with a unique structure that includes a female flower at the base and male flowers along the spadix.

Prenanthes barbata plays an important ecological role by providing food and habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
