Prunus × lannesiana f. mazakura

Prunus × lannesiana f. mazakura, commonly known as the Japanese cherry blossom or sakura, is a cultivar of the Prunus genus.

It is characterized by its delicate pink to white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant is a hybrid between Prunus lannesiana and Prunus incisa, resulting in unique floral displays. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

The foliage turns vibrant shades of red and orange in the fall, adding to its ornamental value.
