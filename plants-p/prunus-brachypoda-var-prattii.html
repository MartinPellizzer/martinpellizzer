Prunus brachypoda var. prattii

Prunus brachypoda var. prattii, commonly known as the dwarf cherry, is a compact shrub native to the western United States.

It features small, oval leaves that are typically a deep green, with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces delicate white flowers in early spring, followed by small, sweet-tart red berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and partial to full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a container plant.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its compact size and seasonal color changes.
