Pleioblastus longqishanensis

Pleioblastus longqishanensis, commonly known as the Longqishan bamboo, is a species native to China, particularly found in the mountainous regions of Zhejiang Province.

It is a clumping bamboo that typically grows to a height of 2 to 4 meters, with slender, green culms that have a smooth, slightly ridged surface. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually pollinated by wind, and its leaves are long, narrow, and have a slightly waxy texture.

This bamboo is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its graceful appearance and hardy nature.
