Polygonatum melleum

Polygonatum melleum, commonly known as yellow false dragonhead, is a perennial herb native to parts of Asia and Europe.

It belongs to the Asparagaceae family and is characterized by its creeping rhizomes and clustered, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. Its yellowish-green flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, and the leaves are lance-shaped with a smooth margin.

The plant is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
