Pimelea novae-zeylandiae

Pimelea novae-zeylandiae, commonly known as the New Zealand bottlebrush, is a shrub native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its dense, cylindrical inflorescences that resemble bottle brushes, which are typically red or pink in color. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are adapted for pollination by birds, particularly honeyeaters. Its leaves are narrow and evergreen, providing a fine texture to the plant's overall appearance.

Pimelea novae-zeylandiae thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and forested areas.
