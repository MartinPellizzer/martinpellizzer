Persicaria × fennica

Persicaria × fennica, commonly known as the hybrid knotweed, is a perennial plant that belongs to the Polygonaceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems that can grow up to 2 meters in height, often producing clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface, and it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils. It is often found in wetlands, stream banks, and other damp environments.

Due to its rapid growth and spreading habit, it can become invasive in certain regions.
