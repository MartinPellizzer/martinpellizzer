Pycreus oakfortensis

Pycreus oakfortensis, commonly known as the Fortis Oak, is a deciduous tree native to the temperate regions of the northern hemisphere.

It is characterized by its broad, lobed leaves that turn vibrant shades of red and yellow in autumn. The tree produces small, inconspicuous flowers in the spring, followed by acorn-like fruits that are an important food source for various wildlife. Its deep root system helps prevent soil erosion, making it valuable in ecological restoration projects.

The bark of Pycreus oakfortensis is thick and fissured, providing habitat for a variety of insects and fungi.
