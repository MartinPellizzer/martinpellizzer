Prunus jamasakura elegans

Prunus jamasakura elegans, commonly known as the Japanese cherry blossom, is a deciduous tree native to Japan.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is renowned for its ornamental value, particularly its showy white to pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has a compact, rounded shape with smooth, greyish-brown bark and elliptical leaves that turn yellow in autumn. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil and full sun for optimal growth.

The tree is often cultivated in gardens and public spaces for its aesthetic appeal and cultural significance.
