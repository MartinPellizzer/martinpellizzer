Pluchea scabrida

Pluchea scabrida, commonly known as roughleaf sunflower, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows as an annual or perennial herb, often found in moist to wet habitats such as swamps and riverbanks. Its leaves are rough and hairy, and the flowers are yellow with a central disk, attracting various pollinators.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
