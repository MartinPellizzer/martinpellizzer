Pleurothallis piraquarensis

Pleurothallis piraquarensis, commonly known as the Piraquarensis orchid, is a species native to the cloud forests of Ecuador.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and intricate floral structure. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, with intricate patterns on the petals and sepals. It thrives in high humidity and partial shade, often growing as an epiphyte on trees.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
