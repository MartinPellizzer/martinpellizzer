Polemonium caeruleum var. racemosum

Polemonium caeruleum var. racemosum, commonly known as the clustered bluebeard, is a herbaceous perennial native to eastern North America.

It features upright, branching stems that bear clusters of showy, blue to purple flowers. The foliage consists of lance-shaped, dark green leaves that are opposite along the stem. It thrives in moist, woodland habitats and prefers partial to full shade.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
