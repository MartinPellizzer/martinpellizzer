Pithecellobium pauciflorum

Pithecellobium pauciflorum, commonly known as the few-flowered pithecellobium, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces edible pods that contain seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as forest edges and clearings.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
