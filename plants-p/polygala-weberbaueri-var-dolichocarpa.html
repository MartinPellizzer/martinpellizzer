Polygala weberbaueri var. dolichocarpa

Polygala weberbaueri var. dolichocarpa, commonly known as the long-fruited milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its slender, often branched stems and small, delicate flowers. The leaves are typically alternate and pinnately compound, with elongated leaflets. The plant produces distinctive, elongated pods that contain small, kidney-shaped seeds.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
