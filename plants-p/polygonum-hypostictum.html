Polygonum hypostictum

Polygonum hypostictum, commonly known as the Chinese knotweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to East Asia.

It features rhizomatous growth and can form dense colonies, making it a common invasive species in wetlands and disturbed soils. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are often reddish or purple-tinged, and it produces small, greenish flowers in clusters. It thrives in moist environments and is often found along riverbanks and in marshy areas.

Its rapid growth and ability to spread through underground stems contribute to its ecological impact.
