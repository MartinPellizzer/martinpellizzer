Pimelea laevigata var. monticola

Pimelea laevigata var. monticola, commonly known as the mountain white everlasting, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in heathlands and open forests, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, and the stems are often woody and branched.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
