Polygala polygama var. ramulosa

Polygala polygama var. ramulosa, commonly known as the slender milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its slender, often branching stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are usually narrow and slightly hairy, adapting to the harsh conditions of its habitat.

The flowers, which are usually white or pale pink, are arranged in dense racemes and attract various pollinators.
