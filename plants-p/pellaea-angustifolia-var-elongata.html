Pellaea angustifolia var. elongata

Pellaea angustifolia var. elongata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved cliff fern, is a variant of the narrowleaf maidenhair fern.

It is a perennial fern native to regions of South America, particularly Argentina and Chile. The plant is characterized by its slender, elongated fronds that are typically dark green and slightly curled. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found growing on cliffs or in rocky crevices.

Its botanical features make it an attractive and hardy addition to rock gardens and shaded landscapes.
