Pygmea

Pygmea, commonly known as the dwarf azalea, is a compact evergreen shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that come in a range of colors, including pink, red, and white. The plant has dark green, leathery leaves that provide a striking contrast to its vibrant blooms. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

Pygmea is popular in gardens for its ornamental value and relatively low maintenance requirements.
