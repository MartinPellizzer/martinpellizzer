Polemonium pulcherrimum var. delicatum

Polemonium pulcherrimum var. delicatum, commonly known as the delicate Jacob's ladder, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Polemoniaceae family and is characterized by its cascading clusters of blue to purple flowers. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy, and it thrives in partial to full shade. It is a herbaceous perennial that prefers moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland habitats.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and conservation efforts.
