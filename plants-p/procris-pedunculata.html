Procris pedunculata

Procris pedunculata, commonly known as the yellow star moss, is a species of liverwort belonging to the family Peltasporaceae.

It is characterized by its dense, cushion-like growth habit and bright yellow to orange-green coloration. The plant forms a dense mat of thalloid structures, which are covered in small, rounded lobes. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically found in forest floors or rocky crevices.

Procris pedunculata plays a role in soil stabilization and provides microhabitat for various invertebrates.
