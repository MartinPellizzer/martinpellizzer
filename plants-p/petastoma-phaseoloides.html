Petastoma phaseoloides

Petastoma phaseoloides, commonly known as the scarlet runner bean, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its climbing habit and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces showy, scarlet flowers that are followed by elongated, bean-like pods containing edible seeds. It thrives in warm climates and is often cultivated for both its ornamental value and nutritional benefits.

The species is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential health properties.
