Potentilla atrisanguinea

Potentilla atrisanguinea, commonly known as black blood cinquefoil, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its dark purple to black stems and leaves with a distinctive five-lobed shape. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
