Pyrus wrightiana

Pyrus wrightiana, commonly known as the Wright's pear, is a deciduous tree native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its attractive, rounded crown and smooth, gray bark. The tree produces small, white flowers in spring, followed by edible, sweet fruit that ripens in late summer. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a slightly serrated margin.

Pyrus wrightiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic and ecological benefits.
