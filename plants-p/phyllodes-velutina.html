Phyllodes velutina

Phyllodes velutina, commonly known as the silky wattle, is a shrub native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, hair-covered phyllodes that resemble leaves, which are actually modified stems. The plant produces yellow flowers in spikes during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

Phyllodes velutina is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
