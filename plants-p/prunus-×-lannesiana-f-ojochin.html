Prunus × lannesiana f. ojochin

Prunus × lannesiana f. ojochin, commonly known as the Mexican cherry, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its small, dark red to black fruits that are typically edible. The plant is native to Mexico and thrives in warm, temperate climates. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a slightly serrated margin.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit production.
