Pithecellobium sonorae

Pithecellobium sonorae, commonly known as the Sonoran pithecellobium, is a shrub native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow. The plant produces edible, pod-like fruits that are often used in traditional medicine by indigenous communities. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand drought and high temperatures.

Its ecological importance includes providing food for various wildlife species and stabilizing sandy soils in desert environments.
