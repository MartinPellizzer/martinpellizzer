Potentilla norvegica subsp. genuina

Potentilla norvegica subsp.

genuina, commonly known as the Norwegian cinquefoil, is a low-growing perennial shrub native to northern Europe. It features wiry, branched stems and produces clusters of small, bright yellow flowers in late spring to early summer.

The plant has palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, and its root system is fibrous and rhizomatous. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine regions.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
