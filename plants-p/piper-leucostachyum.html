Piper leucostachyum

Piper leucostachyum, commonly known as the white-stemmed pepper plant, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including parts of India, Indonesia, and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, clustered white or greenish-white flowers. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a slightly serrated edge.

The fruit is a small, fleshy berry that contains several seeds, and the plant is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value.
