Plantago major var. nana

Plantago major var. nana, commonly known as the dwarf plantain, is a compact variant of the common plantain.

It is a perennial herb native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa, characterized by its rosette-forming leaves that are broad and slightly lobed. The plant produces small, greenish flowers in a dense spike, which are wind-pollinated. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and gardens.

Its leaves are used in traditional medicine and as a food source for various animals.
