Perdicium lactucoides

Perdicium lactucoides, commonly known as the milk thistle, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its distinctive purple flowers and spiny, lobed leaves. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers. Its most notable feature is the milky sap that exudes from the stems and leaves when damaged.

Perdicium lactucoides is often cultivated for its potential medicinal properties, particularly in liver health.
