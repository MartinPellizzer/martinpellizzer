Passiflora sanguinolenta

Passiflora sanguinolenta, commonly known as the blood passionflower, is a striking tropical vine native to South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant red bracts that resemble petals, giving it a dramatic appearance. The plant produces showy, complex flowers with a unique structure that includes a crown of filaments and a central corona. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped with a rough texture and a strong, distinctive odor.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in warm climates for its visual impact.
