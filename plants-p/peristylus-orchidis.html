Peristylus orchidis

Peristylus orchidis, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a rare and striking member of the orchid family.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, where it thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant features delicate, greenish-yellow flowers with intricate patterns that resemble the golden hues of sunlight. Its roots are adapted to absorb nutrients from the forest floor, often growing in symbiosis with specific fungi.

The botanical study of Peristylus orchidis offers insights into the ecological relationships and conservation needs of rare orchid species.
