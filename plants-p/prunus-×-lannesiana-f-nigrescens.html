Prunus × lannesiana f. nigrescens

Prunus × lannesiana f. nigrescens, commonly known as the black cherry plum, is a compact, ornamental shrub or small tree characterized by its dark, almost black bark and dense, rounded form.

It produces small, fragrant flowers in spring, followed by edible, sweet-tart fruits that are often used in culinary applications. The leaves are typically dark green and turn a striking red or purple in the fall, adding seasonal interest. It is native to the eastern United States and thrives in full sun to partial shade, preferring well-drained soil.

This variety is valued for its aesthetic appeal and adaptability in landscaping.
