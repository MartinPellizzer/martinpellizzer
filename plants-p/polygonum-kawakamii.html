Polygonum kawakamii

Polygonum kawakamii, commonly known as Kawakami's knotweed, is a perennial herb native to Japan.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, which includes plants commonly referred to as buckwheats and knotweeds. The plant is characterized by its upright stems, lance-shaped leaves, and small, clustered flowers that bloom in late summer. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or stream banks.

Polygonum kawakamii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
