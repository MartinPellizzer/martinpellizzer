Poinsettia prunifolia

Poinsettia prunifolia, commonly known as the "Christmas star," is a strikingly ornamental plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its vibrant red bracts, which are modified leaves that surround small, inconspicuous yellow flowers. The plant thrives in warm, humid environments and requires bright, indirect light to maintain its color intensity. Its foliage is often variegated with shades of green, white, and pink, adding to its visual appeal.

Poinsettia prunifolia is popular as a holiday plant due to its association with Christmas and its ability to bloom during the winter months.
