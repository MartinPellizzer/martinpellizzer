Primula wawushanica

Primula wawushanica, commonly known as the Wawushan primrose, is a perennial herb native to China.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and mountainous regions. Its leaves are typically oval and arranged in a basal rosette, while its stems are usually short and hairy.

Primula wawushanica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
