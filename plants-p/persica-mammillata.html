Persica mammillata

Persica mammillata, commonly known as the hairy peach.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Africa. The plant is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and showy, pink to white flowers that bloom in spring. Its fruits are small, yellow peaches with a hairy skin and a sweet, aromatic flavor.

Persica mammillata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and fragrance.
