Phyllostachys kwangsiensis

Phyllostachys kwangsiensis, commonly known as the Guangxi bamboo, is a species of grass in the family Poaceae.

It is native to southern China, particularly Guangxi Province, and is characterized by its tall, slender culms and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant is often found in moist, well-drained soils in forested areas or along stream banks. It is a perennial herb that can grow up to several meters in height, making it a prominent feature in its natural habitat.

Its rapid growth rate and ability to form dense stands make it both ecologically significant and useful for erosion control.
