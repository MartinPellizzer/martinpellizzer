Pteronia candollei

Pteronia candollei, commonly known as the Cape reed, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in coastal and sandy environments, thriving in well-drained soils. Its foliage is narrow and grass-like, often forming dense stands.

Pteronia candollei is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping due to its hardy nature.
