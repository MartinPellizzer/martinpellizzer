Plantago eriostachya

Plantago eriostachya, commonly known as the narrow-leaved plantain, is a perennial herb native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It features basal rosettes of narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a hairy underside. The plant produces dense clusters of small, greenish-white flowers on slender stems, which are borne in a spike-like inflorescence. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their soothing properties.

The species is adapted to grow in a variety of habitats, including lawns, roadsides, and disturbed soils.
