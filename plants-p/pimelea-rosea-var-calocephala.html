Pimelea rosea var. calocephala

Pimelea rosea var. calocephala, commonly known as the pink waratah, is a shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It features showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are usually covered in a fine, silvery-white down. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens.

The flowers are often used in floral arrangements due to their vibrant color and longevity.
