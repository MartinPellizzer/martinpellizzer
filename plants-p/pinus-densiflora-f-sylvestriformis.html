Pinus densiflora f. sylvestriformis

Pinus densiflora f. sylvestriformis, commonly known as the Japanese black pine in its typical form, is a variant of the Japanese white pine.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and a compact, bushy growth habit. This form is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes. The needles are short and closely arranged, giving the plant a lush, textured appearance.

It is relatively hardy and adaptable, making it a popular choice for bonsai and decorative planting.
