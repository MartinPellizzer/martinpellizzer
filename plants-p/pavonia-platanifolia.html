Pavonia platanifolia

Pavonia platanifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf pavonia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring upright stems and opposite leaves. The leaves are broad and ovate, often with a slightly hairy texture, and they have a distinctive, waxy appearance. Its flowers are clustered in dense inflorescences, usually in shades of pink or red, and bloom during the warmer months.

The plant is known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
