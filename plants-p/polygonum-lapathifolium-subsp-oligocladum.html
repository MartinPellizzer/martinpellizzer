Polygonum lapathifolium subsp. oligocladum

Polygonum lapathifolium subsp.

oligocladum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved smartweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of Asia. It belongs to the buckwheat family, Polygonaceae, and is characterized by its upright growth habit and lance-shaped leaves.

The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in dense clusters, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist soils and is often found in wetlands, stream banks, and other aquatic or semi-aquatic habitats.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and wetland conservation.
