Pinguicula lippoldii

Pinguicula lippoldii, commonly known as the Lippold's butterwort, is a carnivorous plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its rosette of fleshy, glandular leaves that secrete a sticky substance to trap small insects. The plant produces delicate, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in moist, nutrient-poor environments, often found in rocky or boggy areas.

Pinguicula lippoldii is a popular specimen among carnivorous plant enthusiasts due to its unique appearance and fascinating predatory behavior.
