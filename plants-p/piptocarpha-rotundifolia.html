Piptocarpha rotundifolia

Piptocarpha rotundifolia, commonly known as roundleaf alkali sacaton, is a perennial grass native to the western United States.

It typically grows in saline or alkaline soils, often found in wetlands and marshes. The plant has rounded, bluish-gray leaves and produces small, spike-like flower clusters. It plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for wildlife.

Its botanical characteristics make it well adapted to harsh, arid environments.
