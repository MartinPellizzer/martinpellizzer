Persea multinervia

Persea multinervia, commonly known as the Mexican persimmon, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central America and parts of the Caribbean.

It is characterized by its broad, dark green leaves with prominent veins, which contribute to its scientific name "multinervia.". The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by edible, orange to reddish fruits with a sweet, sometimes astringent flavor. Its fruit is often consumed fresh or used in culinary applications.

Persea multinervia is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in warm climates for its aesthetic and edible qualities.
