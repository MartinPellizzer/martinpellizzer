Pseudocaryophyllus glaziovianus

Pseudocaryophyllus glaziovianus, commonly known as the Glaziovian sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of Mexico.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit. The plant features succulent, fleshy leaves that help it conserve water in dry environments. Its small, pink or white flowers bloom in clusters, adding ornamental value.

It is often cultivated in xeriscapes due to its drought tolerance and aesthetic appeal.
