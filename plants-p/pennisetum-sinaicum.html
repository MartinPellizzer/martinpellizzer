Pennisetum sinaicum

Pennisetum sinaicum, commonly known as the Sinai grass, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the Middle East and North Africa.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, blade-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny florets, typical of the grass family. It is well adapted to drought conditions, making it a resilient species in harsh environments.

Pennisetum sinaicum is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
