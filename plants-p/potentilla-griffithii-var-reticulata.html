Potentilla griffithii var. reticulata

Potentilla griffithii var. reticulata, commonly known as the reticulate cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayas.

It is a low-growing shrub characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and clusters of small, bright yellow flowers. The plant thrives in high-altitude environments, often found in rocky or alpine regions. Its flowers bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and visual interest.
