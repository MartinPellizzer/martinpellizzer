Polemonium caeruleum var. pulcherrimum

Polemonium caeruleum var. pulcherrimum, commonly known as the showy bluebell, is a flowering plant native to the northeastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged oppositely along the stem. It thrives in moist, shady woodland habitats and is often found in moist soils.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
