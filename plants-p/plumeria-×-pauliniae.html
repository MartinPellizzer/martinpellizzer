Plumeria × pauliniae

Plumeria × pauliniae, commonly known as the frangipani hybrid, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, waxy flowers that come in shades of white, yellow, and pink, often with a distinctive golden eye. The plant has simple, lance-shaped leaves that are alternate and often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are typically borne in clusters at the ends of branches, and it is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens and as a cut flower.

The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.
