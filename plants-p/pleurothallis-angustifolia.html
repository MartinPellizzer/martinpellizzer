Pleurothallis angustifolia

Pleurothallis angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a small, epiphytic orchid that typically grows on trees in humid, shaded environments. The plant features delicate, narrow leaves and produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink. It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its intricate floral structure.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is often sought after by orchid enthusiasts and conservationists.
