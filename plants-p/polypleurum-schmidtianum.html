Polypleurum schmidtianum

Polypleurum schmidtianum, commonly known as the Schmidt's polypody, is a fern native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped fronds. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest undergrowth. Its rhizome is creeping and produces numerous small, oblong spore cases.

It is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in humid conditions.
