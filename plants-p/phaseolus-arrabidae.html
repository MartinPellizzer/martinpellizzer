Phaseolus arrabidae

Phaseolus arrabidae, commonly known as the Arrabida bean, is a species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a climbing legume that produces pods containing kidney-shaped seeds. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, yellow flowers. It thrives in temperate climates and is often grown for its edible seeds.

The botanical features of Phaseolus arrabidae make it an important crop in traditional agriculture.
