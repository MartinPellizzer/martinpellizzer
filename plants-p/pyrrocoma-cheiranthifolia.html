Pyrrocoma cheiranthifolia

Pyrrocoma cheiranthifolia, commonly known as the rock cress, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many species with small, four-petaled flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to harsh, alpine environments. Its leaves are usually basal, arranged in a rosette, and its flowers are often bright yellow or white.

Pyrrocoma cheiranthifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in rock gardens.
