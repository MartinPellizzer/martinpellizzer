Polygala tetrasepala

Polygala tetrasepala, commonly known as the four-lobed milkweed, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink or white. The plant has slender, branching stems and small, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are notable for their unique structure, with four sepals and four petals, giving it the specific epithet "tetrasepala.".

This species is often found in moist, open habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
