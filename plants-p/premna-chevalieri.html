Premna chevalieri

Premna chevalieri, commonly known as the Madagascar nettle, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the family Lamiaceae and is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves with a hairy texture. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale purple in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest undergrowth.

Premna chevalieri is notable for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional medicine for various ailments.
