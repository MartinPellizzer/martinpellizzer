Persea flavifolia

Persea flavifolia, commonly known as the yellow-flowered persea, is a small evergreen tree native to the tropical regions of Central America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, avocados, and cinnamon trees. The plant is characterized by its simple, elliptical leaves that are glossy and dark green in color. Its flowers are small, yellow, and clustered in panicles, often appearing before the leaves in early spring.

The fruit is a small, smooth, yellow berry that ripens in the summer months.
