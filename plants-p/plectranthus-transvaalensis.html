Plectranthus transvaalensis

Plectranthus transvaalensis, commonly known as Transvaal plectranthus, is a perennial herb native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It produces small, tubular flowers that are typically lavender or purple in color.

Plectranthus transvaalensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and fragrance.
