Parodia procera

Parodia procera, commonly known as the Queen of the Night cactus, is a species native to the deserts of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, spindly stems that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant produces large, night-blooming flowers that are typically white or pale pink. Its stems are covered in white, woolly hairs that help to reduce water loss in arid environments.

Parodia procera is a popular choice for cactus enthusiasts due to its striking appearance and unique blooming habits.
