Proserpinaca palustris var. australis

Proserpinaca palustris var. australis, commonly known as the Australian marsh pennywort, is a variant of the marsh pennywort species native to southern regions of Australia.

It is a small, aquatic plant characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are typically submerged or floating on the water surface. The plant thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, often found in wetlands and ponds. Its simple, slender stems and small white flowers contribute to its ornamental value in aquatic gardens.

This variety is adapted to tolerate fluctuating water levels and is often used in water feature landscaping.
