Phlox abdita

Phlox abdita, commonly known as the mountain phlox, is a herbaceous perennial native to the Appalachian Mountains.

It features showy, fragrant flowers that range in color from pink to white, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged oppositely along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and woodland settings.

Its ornamental value and adaptability contribute to its appeal in both natural and cultivated landscapes.
