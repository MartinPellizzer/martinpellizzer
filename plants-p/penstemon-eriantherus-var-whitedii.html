Penstemon eriantherus var. whitedii

Penstemon eriantherus var. whitedii, commonly known as White's penstemon, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and lance-shaped leaves that are typically hairy and gray-green in color. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that range in color from pale pink to white, often with a distinctive white throat. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

Its botanical features make it well adapted to arid environments and suitable for native plant gardens.
