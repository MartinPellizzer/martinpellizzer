Pavonia paniculata var. corymbosa

Pavonia paniculata var. corymbosa, commonly known as the clustered pavonia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its showy, pink to red bract-like flowers that appear in clusters. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub with trifoliate leaves and produces seed pods that split open to release its seeds.

It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often found in disturbed soils along roadsides and in open woodlands.
