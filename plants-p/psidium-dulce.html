Psidium dulce

Psidium dulce, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or cherimoya, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that can grow up to 10 meters tall, featuring glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant white flowers. The plant produces a large, pear-shaped fruit with a thick, leathery rind and a creamy, sweet flesh containing numerous black seeds. Its botanical characteristics include a woody stem, opposite leaf arrangement, and a unique floral structure with five petals.

The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
