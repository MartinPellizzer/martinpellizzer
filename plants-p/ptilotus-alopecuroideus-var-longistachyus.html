Ptilotus alopecuroideus var. longistachyus

Ptilotus alopecuroideus var. longistachyus, commonly known as the long-stemmed spinifex, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Australia.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its tufted, grass-like foliage and distinctive, spiky inflorescences. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-white in color, which bloom during the cooler months. It is well adapted to harsh, dry environments, with a deep root system and reduced leaf surface to minimize water loss.

This species is often found in sandy or rocky soils and is an important component of the native flora in its habitat.
