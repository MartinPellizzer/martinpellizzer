Phylica floccosa

Phylica floccosa, commonly known as the woolly phylica, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Phylica floccosa is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique texture and visual appeal.
