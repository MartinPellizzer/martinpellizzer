Poa novae-zelandiae var. desiliens

Poa novae-zelandiae var. desiliens, commonly known as the slender tussock grass, is a perennial grass species native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and is often found in moist, well-drained soils in mountainous regions. The plant has slender, wiry stems and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. Its leaves are narrow and often have a bluish-green hue, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for native invertebrates.
