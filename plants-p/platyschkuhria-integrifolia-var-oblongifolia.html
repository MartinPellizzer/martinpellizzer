Platyschkuhria integrifolia var. oblongifolia

Platyschkuhria integrifolia var. oblongifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf false nettle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It features lanceolate to elliptic leaves with smooth margins and a distinctive reddish stem. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in early spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it identifiable by its unique leaf shape and growth habit.
