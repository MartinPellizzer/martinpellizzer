Parsonsia squamuligera

Parsonsia squamuligera, commonly known as the golden trumpet vine, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its striking yellow-orange tubular flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as birds and insects. The plant has heart-shaped, glossy leaves and produces woody, climbing stems that can grow up to several meters in length. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, often found in coastal and inland areas.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscaping.
