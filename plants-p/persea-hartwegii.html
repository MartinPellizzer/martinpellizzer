Persea hartwegii

Persea hartwegii, commonly known as the Mexican persimmon, is a small evergreen tree native to Mexico and Central America.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and fragrant flowers. The tree produces small, orange-yellow fruits that are edible when fully ripe. Its botanical features include a smooth, bark-like trunk and a dense canopy of foliage.
