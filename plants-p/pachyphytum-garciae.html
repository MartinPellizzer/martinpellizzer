Pachyphytum garciae

Pachyphytum garciae, commonly known as the Garcia's thickleaf, is a succulent native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its fleshy, thick leaves that store water, typical of plants in arid environments. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Its unique appearance makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and indoor collections.
