Prunus leveilleana var. pendula

Prunus leveilleana var. pendula, commonly known as the weeping cherry or weeping almond, is a unique variant of the Prunus leveilleana species.

It is characterized by its pendulous branches that cascade downward, creating a striking, ethereal appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in the spring, typically pink or white, which add to its ornamental value. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.

This cultivar is often used in gardens and landscapes for its aesthetic appeal and seasonal beauty.
