Pavonia spiciformis

Pavonia spiciformis, commonly known as the spiked pavonia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant produces clusters of small, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are simple, ovate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Pavonia spiciformis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens for its vibrant blooms and hardy nature.
