Pithecellobium stuhlmannii

Pithecellobium stuhlmannii, commonly known as the yellow pithecellobium, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are often visited by bees and other pollinators. Its seeds are enclosed in hairy, elongated pods that mature in the dry season.

The plant is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in some indigenous communities.
