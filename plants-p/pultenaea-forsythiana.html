Pultenaea forsythiana

Pultenaea forsythiana, commonly known as the yellow wattle, is a shrub native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in spring, adding a vibrant splash of color to the landscape. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are arranged alternately along the stems. It is often found in dry, sandy soils and is well adapted to coastal and inland environments.

Pultenaea forsythiana is a valuable component of native flora, supporting local wildlife and contributing to the ecological diversity of its habitat.
