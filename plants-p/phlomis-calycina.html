Phlomis calycina

Phlomis calycina, commonly known as narrowleaf mullein, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features tall, slender stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height, often branching near the top. The leaves are lance-shaped, with a rough, hairy texture, and the plant produces showy clusters of yellow to orange flowers in summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Its botanical characteristics make it both ornamental and ecologically valuable.
