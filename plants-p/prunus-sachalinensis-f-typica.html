Prunus sachalinensis f. typica

Prunus sachalinensis f. typica, commonly known as the Sakhalin cherry, is a shrub or small tree native to the Russian Far East and parts of northeastern China.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and clusters of white or pale pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The fruit is a small, sweet, edible cherry that ripens in late summer. The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its spring blossoms.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.
