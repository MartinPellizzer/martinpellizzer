Phaseolus hirtus

Phaseolus hirtus, commonly known as hairy bean, is a leguminous plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it its common name. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several edible seeds. It is often cultivated for its nutritional value, as the seeds are rich in protein and essential nutrients.

Phaseolus hirtus thrives in warm climates and is commonly grown in tropical and subtropical regions.
