Polypodium macrodon

Polypodium macrodon, commonly known as the giant water fern, is a fern species native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its large, floating fronds that can reach lengths of up to 3 meters. The plant thrives in slow-moving freshwater environments, such as ponds and streams. Its fronds are divided into numerous small, finger-like segments called pinnae.

Polypodium macrodon plays a vital role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
