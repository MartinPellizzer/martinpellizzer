Plantago arechavaletae

Plantago arechavaletae, commonly known as the Arechavaleta plantain, is a species of flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the family Plantaginaceae and is characterized by its rosette of broad, fleshy leaves. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are clustered in a spike, which bloom in the summer months. It thrives in rocky and coastal environments, showcasing its adaptability to arid conditions.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species within its native range.
