Porophyllum platyphyllum

Porophyllum platyphyllum, commonly known as the broadleaf fleabane, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are broadly ovate with a rough, hairy texture, and its flowers are yellow with a central dark center.

Porophyllum platyphyllum is known for its resilience to drought and its role in supporting local pollinator populations.
