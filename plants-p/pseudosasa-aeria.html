Pseudosasa aeria

Pseudosasa aeria, commonly known as the silver bamboo, is a member of the grass family Poaceae.

It is native to eastern Asia, particularly China, and is characterized by its slender, hollow stems that are often covered in a silvery sheen. The plant forms dense clumps and can grow up to several meters in height, making it a striking ornamental plant. Its leaves are long, narrow, and slightly twisted, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Pseudosasa aeria is often cultivated for its aesthetic value and is also used in traditional Chinese medicine.
