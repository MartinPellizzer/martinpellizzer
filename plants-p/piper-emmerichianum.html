Piper emmerichianum

Piper emmerichianum, commonly known as the Emmerich's pepper, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes numerous species of pepper plants. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by elongated, berry-like fruits. Its leaves are typically alternate, ovate, and have a rough texture.

The plant is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
