Pavonia serrata

Pavonia serrata, commonly known as the scarlet pavonia, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its vibrant red to purple flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Pavonia serrata is valued for its ornamental beauty and is cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
