Pyrus halliana var. obtusiloba

Pyrus halliana var. obtusiloba, commonly known as the narrow-leaf pear, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically hairy on the underside. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters, which are followed by edible, sweet-tart berries. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable choice for ornamental gardening.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various garden conditions.
