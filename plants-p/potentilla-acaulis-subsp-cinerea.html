Potentilla acaulis subsp. cinerea

Potentilla acaulis subsp.

cinerea, commonly known as silver cinquefoil, is a low-growing perennial plant characterized by its silver-gray foliage. It has upright, wiry stems that bear small, yellow flowers in late spring to early summer.

The leaves are typically divided into five leaflets, which are covered in a fine, powdery bloom that gives the plant its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

This subspecies is native to Europe and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.
