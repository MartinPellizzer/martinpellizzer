Plantago coronopus var. cupanii

Plantago coronopus var. cupanii, commonly known as the narrow-leaf plantain, is a variant of the common plantain species.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, disturbed soils along roadsides, riverbanks, and wet meadows. It produces capsules that contain numerous small seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

This variety is often used in herbal medicine for its purported healing properties.
