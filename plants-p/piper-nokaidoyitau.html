Piper nokaidoyitau

Piper nokaidoyitau, commonly known as the Nokaidoyitau pepper, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, particularly found in the forests of Borneo. The plant is characterized by its climbing habit, with long, slender stems that support its compound leaves. Its small, greenish-white flowers are followed by the production of small, peppery fruits.

The plant is valued for its aromatic leaves and fruits, which are used in traditional medicine and local cuisine.
