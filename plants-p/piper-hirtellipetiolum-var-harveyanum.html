Piper hirtellipetiolum var. harveyanum

Piper hirtellipetiolum var. harveyanum, commonly known as Harvey's pepper plant, is a tropical species native to Central America.

It is characterized by its aromatic, dark green leaves and small, clustered white flowers. The plant produces small, berry-like fruits that are typically harvested when they turn red. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of medicinal compounds.
