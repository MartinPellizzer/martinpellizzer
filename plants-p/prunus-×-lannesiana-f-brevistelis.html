Prunus × lannesiana f. brevistelis

Prunus × lannesiana f. brevistelis, commonly known as the dwarf cherry, is a compact, ornamental shrub characterized by its small, rounded form and dense growth habit.

It is a natural hybrid between Prunus lannesiana and Prunus sargentii, exhibiting traits of both parent species. The plant typically features dark green, glossy leaves and produces clusters of small, white or pink flowers in early spring. Its fruit is a small, dark red drupe, though it is not typically edible.

This variety is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
