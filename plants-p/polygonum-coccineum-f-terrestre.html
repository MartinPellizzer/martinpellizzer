Polygonum coccineum f. terrestre

Polygonum coccineum f. terrestre, commonly known as the red pigweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its prostrate growth habit and bright red stems that become more pronounced as the plant matures. The leaves are alternate, ovate, and often covered in fine hairs, while the small pink to red flowers appear in clusters at the ends of the stems. This plant thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and other open areas.

It plays a role in soil stabilization and serves as a food source for various insects and wildlife.
