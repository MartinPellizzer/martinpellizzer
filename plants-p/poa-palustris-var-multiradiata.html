Poa palustris var. multiradiata

Poa palustris var. multiradiata, commonly known as the marsh bentgrass, is a variant of the common bentgrass species.

It is a low-growing perennial grass that typically thrives in wet, marshy environments. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It is characterized by its ability to tolerate waterlogged soil conditions.

This variant is often found in wetlands and is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and supporting biodiversity.
