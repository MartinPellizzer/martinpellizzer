Prunella laciniata var. subintegra

Prunella laciniata var. subintegra, commonly known as the narrow-leaved heal-all, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features slender, upright stems that bear opposite, lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, blue-violet flowers in whorls along the stem, typically blooming from late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and disturbed areas.

The species is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally in herbal remedies.
