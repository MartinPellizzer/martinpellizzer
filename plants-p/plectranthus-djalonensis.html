Plectranthus djalonensis

Plectranthus djalonensis, commonly known as the African mint, is a perennial herb native to West Africa.

It is characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves, which are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically violet or purple in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

Due to its aromatic properties, it is sometimes used in traditional medicine and as a culinary herb.
