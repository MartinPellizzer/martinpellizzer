Primula sachalinensis

Primula sachalinensis, commonly known as the Sakhalin primrose, is a perennial herb native to the Far East, including parts of Russia, Japan, and Korea.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and rocky slopes. The plant features lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. Its blooming period occurs in late spring to early summer, and it is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.

The species is also of interest in botanical studies due to its unique adaptations to its native habitat.
