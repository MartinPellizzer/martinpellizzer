Polypodium peruvianum

Polypodium peruvianum, commonly known as the Peruvian water fern, is a species of fern native to the Andes region of South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that give it a lacy appearance. This plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

The plant plays an important ecological role in its native habitat by contributing to soil stability and providing habitat for various microorganisms.
