Phlomis flavescens

Phlomis flavescens, commonly known as yellow beardtongue, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features upright, hairy stems that can grow up to 1 meter in height. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, typically blooming in summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Phlomis flavescens is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it a hardy addition to rock gardens and dry landscapes.
