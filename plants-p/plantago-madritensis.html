Plantago madritensis

Plantago madritensis, commonly known as the Spanish plantain, is a perennial herb native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in a spike, which are typically pollinated by insects. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and rocky areas.

Due to its medicinal properties, it has been traditionally used in herbal remedies.
