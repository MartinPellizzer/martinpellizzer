Protea aulax

Protea aulax, commonly known as the silver protea, is a striking South African plant belonging to the Proteaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescence composed of numerous white to cream-colored flowers arranged in a spherical or conical shape. The plant typically grows in coastal and mountainous regions, thriving in well-drained, acidic soils. Its leaves are narrow and leathery, often covered in a fine, silvery down that gives it its common name.

Protea aulax is an important component of fynbos vegetation and is valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture.
