Piscidia piscipula

Piscidia piscipula, commonly known as the yellow glorybower, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to the Caribbean and parts of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators such as bees and birds. The plant has compound leaves with elliptical leaflets and produces small, woody pods that contain seeds. It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often used in ornamental gardening due to its vibrant floral display.

The species is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal remedies.
