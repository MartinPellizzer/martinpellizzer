Phaseolus cochleatus var. violaceus

Phaseolus cochleatus var. violaceus, commonly known as the purple runner bean, is a variety of the common bean genus native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its vibrant purple to violet-colored pods and seeds, which are often used in culinary applications. The plant is a climbing vine with compound leaves and produces small, yellow flowers. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is valued for both its ornamental appeal and nutritional content.
