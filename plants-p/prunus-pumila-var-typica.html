Prunus pumila var. typica

Prunus pumila var. typica, commonly known as the dwarf Japanese cherry, is a compact shrub native to Japan.

It features small, oval leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces delicate white or pink flowers in early spring, which are followed by small, red berries. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a border plant.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its early blooming habit and compact growth form.
