Passiflora platyceras

Passiflora platyceras, commonly known as the flat-seeded passionflower, is a species native to Central and South America.

It is a climbing vine that produces striking, showy flowers with intricate structures. The plant features five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments surrounding the reproductive organs. Its fruit is a red, fleshy berry that contains numerous small, black seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
