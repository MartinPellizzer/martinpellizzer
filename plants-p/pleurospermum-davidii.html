Pleurospermum davidii

Pleurospermum davidii, commonly known as the Chinese bellflower, is a perennial herb native to China.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its hairy stems and showy blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its drought tolerance.

The flowers bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
