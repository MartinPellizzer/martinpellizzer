Pagamea macrophylla

Pagamea macrophylla, commonly known as the large-leaf pagamea, is a tropical plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, glossy leaves that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-draining soil.

Pagamea macrophylla is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
