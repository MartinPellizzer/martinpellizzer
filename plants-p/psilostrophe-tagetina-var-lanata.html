Psilostrophe tagetina var. lanata

Psilostrophe tagetina var. lanata, commonly known as the woolly goldenrod, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy leaves that give it a distinctive, fuzzy appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in panicles, which bloom in late summer to early autumn. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along streambanks.

Its botanical features make it a valuable plant for ecological restoration and wetland habitats.
