Phlomis seticalycina

Phlomis seticalycina, commonly known as the silky phlomis, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, rocky soils and sunny habitats. The plant features upright stems covered in soft, silver-gray hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its flowers are usually pink or purple, arranged in dense clusters at the ends of the stems.

The leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine, silky hairs, contributing to its common name.
