Piper longipilosum

Piper longipilosum, commonly known as the long-hair pepper, is a flowering plant species native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes various pepper species. The plant is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and small, opposite leaves with a hairy texture. Its flowers are unisexual, with separate male and female structures, and it produces small, three-lobed berries.

The plant is valued for its aromatic properties and is used in traditional medicine and as a spice in some regions.
