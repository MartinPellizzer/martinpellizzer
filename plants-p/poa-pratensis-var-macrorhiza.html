Poa pratensis var. macrorhiza

Poa pratensis var. macrorhiza, commonly known as the large-rooted meadow grass, is a variant of the common meadow grass.

It is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere. The plant is characterized by its robust root system and dense foliage, which contribute to its use in turfgrass and erosion control. It typically grows in moist to wet soils, often found in wet meadows and along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological restoration projects.
