Pithecellobium stipellatum

Pithecellobium stipellatum, commonly known as the velvet tamarind, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, yellow-orange flowers. The plant produces small, edible pods that contain seeds, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value and potential medicinal uses.

Its growth habit and botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural study.
