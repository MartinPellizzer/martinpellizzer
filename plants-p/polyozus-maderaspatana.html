Polyozus maderaspatana

Polyozus maderaspatana, commonly known as the Indian rosewood, is a large evergreen tree native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is characterized by its dark, aromatic wood and dense canopy of lanceolate leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by woody, capsule-like fruits. Its bark is thick and fissured, often exuding a resinous sap.

The plant is valued for its timber and has traditional uses in medicine and craftsmanship.
