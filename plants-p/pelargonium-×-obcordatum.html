Pelargonium × obcordatum

Pelargonium × obcordatum, commonly known as the ivy-leaved geranium, is a hybrid species native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and heart-shaped, dark green leaves with a velvety texture. The plant produces small, clustered flowers in shades of red, pink, or white, which bloom throughout the growing season. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for hanging baskets and containers.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is widely cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
