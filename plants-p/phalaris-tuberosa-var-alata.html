Phalaris tuberosa var. alata

Phalaris tuberosa var. alata, commonly known as the winged tuberous grass, is a perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, winged seed pods that give it its common name. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and along riverbanks. Its tuberous roots are edible and have been used in traditional cuisine.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal due to its delicate, grass-like foliage and unique seed structures.
