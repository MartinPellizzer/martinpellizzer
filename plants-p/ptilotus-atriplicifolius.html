Ptilotus atriplicifolius

Ptilotus atriplicifolius, commonly known as the silverleaf mistflower, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage and small, pale purple or white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has a compact, bushy growth habit and is often found in sandy or rocky soils. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped with a hairy surface, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental qualities and ability to thrive in arid conditions.
