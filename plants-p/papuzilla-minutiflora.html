Papuzilla minutiflora

Papuzilla minutiflora, commonly known as the tiny flowered papuzilla, is a small, herbaceous plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It features delicate, small flowers that are typically white or pale pink, arranged in clusters at the ends of its slender stems. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are adapted to conserve water in its dry environment. It blooms during the cooler months, often in spring or early summer, and relies on insect pollination for reproduction.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
