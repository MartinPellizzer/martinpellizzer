Piper stipulare

Piper stipulare, commonly known as the stipulate pepper vine, is a climbing shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and small, greenish-white flowers that develop into red, fleshy berries. Its stems are often covered in fine hairs, and the leaves have distinct stipules at their bases.

Piper stipulare is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its foliage and unique fruiting structures.
