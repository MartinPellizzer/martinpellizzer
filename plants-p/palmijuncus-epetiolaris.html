Palmijuncus epetiolaris

Palmijuncus epetiolaris, commonly known as the narrow-leaved palm, is a rare and unique plant native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, unbranched stem and narrow, elongated leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant exhibits a palm-like structure but lacks the typical fan-shaped leaves of most palms, instead featuring elongated, linear foliage. It thrives in humid, tropical environments and is often found in lowland forests with rich, well-drained soil.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
