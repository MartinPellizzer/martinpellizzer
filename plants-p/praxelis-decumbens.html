Praxelis decumbens

Praxelis decumbens, commonly known as the creeping bellflower, is a low-growing perennial plant native to South America.

It features prostrate stems that root at the nodes, allowing it to spread across the ground. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine hairs.

Praxelis decumbens is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to form dense mats.
