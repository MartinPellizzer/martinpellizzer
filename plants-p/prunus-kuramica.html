Prunus kuramica

Prunus kuramica, commonly known as the Kuramica cherry, is a small deciduous tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape, with a smooth margin and a glossy green upper surface. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers in early spring, which are followed by small, round, reddish-purple fruits. Prunus kuramica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic features.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it adaptable to a variety of garden settings.
