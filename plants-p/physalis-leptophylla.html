Physalis leptophylla

Physalis leptophylla, commonly known as the slender-leafed groundcherry, is a species of flowering plant in the nightshade family, Solanaceae.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its hairy stems and small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that are encased in a papery calyx, similar to other physalis species. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

Due to its unique appearance and potential medicinal properties, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists.
