Prinos spicatus

Prinos spicatus, commonly known as the spiked prinos, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in arid or semi-arid environments. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that are clustered in spikes, giving it its common name. It has small, leathery leaves that are arranged oppositely along the stems.

Prinos spicatus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
