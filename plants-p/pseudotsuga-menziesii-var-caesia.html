Pseudotsuga menziesii var. caesia

Pseudotsuga menziesii var. caesia, commonly known as the white fir, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its conical shape, smooth gray bark, and needle-like leaves that are bluish-green in color. The cones are small, woody, and typically hang downward, containing winged seeds. This variety is adapted to a variety of soil types and is often found in mountainous regions.

It plays an important role in forest ecosystems and is valued for its timber and ornamental qualities.
