Pithecellobium curvicarpum

Pithecellobium curvicarpum, commonly known as the narrow-fruit acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically pinnate with small, elliptical leaflets. The plant produces yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by elongated, woody pods containing edible seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in disturbed or open habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
