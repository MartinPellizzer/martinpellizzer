Phragmites occidentalis

Phragmites occidentalis, commonly known as western common reed, is a tall, perennial grass that thrives in wetland environments.

It is characterized by its hollow, jointed stems and narrow, blade-like leaves. This plant is often found in marshes, shores, and other moist habitats across North America. It plays a vital role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.

Its rhizomes form dense colonies, making it an important part of wetland ecosystems.
