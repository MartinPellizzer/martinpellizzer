Potentilla tommasiniana

Potentilla tommasiniana, commonly known as the Tommasini cinquefoil, is a low-growing perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has compound leaves composed of five to seven oval-shaped leaflets, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Botanically, it belongs to the Rosaceae family, sharing characteristics with other members of the genus Potentilla.
