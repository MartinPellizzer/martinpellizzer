Phyllanthus wingfieldii

Phyllanthus wingfieldii, commonly known as the wingfield's spurge, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss in arid conditions. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. It is a member of the Euphorbiaceae family, which includes many other spurge species.
