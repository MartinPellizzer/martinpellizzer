Pyrethrum peucedanifolium

Pyrethrum peucedanifolium, commonly known as the blue pyrethrum, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves and thrives in well-drained soil with full sun exposure. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of natural insecticides.

Pyrethrum peucedanifolium is also valued in horticulture for its ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
