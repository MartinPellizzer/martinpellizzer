Primula buryana var. buryana

Primula buryana var. buryana, commonly known as the alpine primrose, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its low-growing habit, with rosettes of hairy, oval-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped, violet or blue flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky soils.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and conservation efforts.
