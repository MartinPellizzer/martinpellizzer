Pseudopodospermum suberosum

Pseudopodospermum suberosum, commonly known as the rough-stemmed pseudopodospermum, is a shrub native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its woody stems and small, clustered leaves. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. It thrives in dry, arid environments and is adapted to withstand periods of drought.

The species is of interest in botanical studies due to its unique morphology and ecological adaptations.
