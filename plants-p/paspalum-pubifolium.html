Paspalum pubifolium

Paspalum pubifolium, commonly known as the hairy leaf paspalum, is a perennial grass native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, hairy leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces small, clustered spikelets with inconspicuous flowers, typical of the grass family. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in marshy areas or along waterways.

Paspalum pubifolium is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings due to its aggressive growth habit.
