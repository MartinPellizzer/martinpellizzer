Poa microstachya

Poa microstachya, commonly known as the slender meadow grass, is a species of grass native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and slender, wiry stems that often form dense mats. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny, two-awned spikelets. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, wetlands, and along stream banks.

Poa microstachya is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil and supporting wildlife.
