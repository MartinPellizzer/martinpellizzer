Protea elliottii var. angustifolia

Protea elliottii var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sugarbush, is a species native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and produces clusters of pink to reddish bracts that resemble flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to Mediterranean climates with dry summers. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens.

The species plays a role in supporting local wildlife and is part of the fynbos biome.
