Piper griseopubens var. revocabile

Piper griseopubens var. revocabile, commonly known as the gray pubescent pepper plant, is a variant of the Piper griseopubens species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its small, elongated leaves that are covered in fine, grayish pubescence, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, green berries that mature to a dark purple or black color, typical of many Piper species. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in the understory of tropical rainforests.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and less-studied botanical specimen.
