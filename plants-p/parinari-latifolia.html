Parinari latifolia

Parinari latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf parinari, is a tropical tree native to Africa, Southeast Asia, and the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its smooth, grayish-brown bark and large, palmate leaves that can grow up to 30 centimeters in diameter. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, followed by edible, fleshy fruits that are yellow when ripe. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory and antidiabetic properties.

The plant is also valued for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in disturbed soils, making it useful for reforestation efforts.
