Podocarpus caesius

Podocarpus caesius, commonly known as the silver podocarp, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage that often has a silvery underside, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, spherical cones that mature to a reddish color, containing winged seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in coastal or mountainous regions.

Podocarpus caesius is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
