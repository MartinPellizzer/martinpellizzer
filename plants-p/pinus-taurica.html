Pinus taurica

Pinus taurica, commonly known as the Turkish pine, is a species of evergreen conifer native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its needle-like leaves, which are typically dark green and arranged in clusters of five. The plant produces cones that are woody and can remain on the tree for several years. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments.

Pinus taurica is often used in landscaping due to its ornamental value and hardy nature.
