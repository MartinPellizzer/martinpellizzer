Polypodium nigrocinctum

Polypodium nigrocinctum, commonly known as the black-banded huperzia, is a species of fern belonging to the family Huperziaceae.

It is characterized by its distinctive dark green, leathery fronds that are often adorned with a black or dark brown band along the midrib. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as forests and rocky areas, where it can thrive in humid conditions. Its sporangia are clustered in sori on the underside of the fronds, a common reproductive feature in ferns.

Polypodium nigrocinctum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appearance and is relatively easy to care for in the right conditions.
