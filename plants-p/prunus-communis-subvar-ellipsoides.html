Prunus communis subvar. ellipsoides

Prunus communis subvar. ellipsoides, commonly known as the oval cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree characterized by its elliptical, glossy leaves and clusters of white or pink flowers.

It produces small, round fruits that are typically red or black when mature, containing a single stone. The plant is native to parts of Europe and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, and it is relatively hardy in temperate climates.

The species is known for its compact growth habit and is suitable for use in hedging or as a specimen plant.
