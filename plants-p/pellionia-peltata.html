Pellionia peltata

Pellionia peltata, commonly known as velvetleaf, is a tropical flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, velvety leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the summer months. Pellionia peltata thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated as a houseplant.
