Potamogeton perfoliatus var. richardsonii

Potamogeton perfoliatus var. richardsonii, commonly known as the Richardson's pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to North America.

It has long, narrow leaves that are often spirally arranged around the stem. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically found in clusters near the water surface. It thrives in shallow, freshwater environments such as ponds and lakes.

Its dense growth helps stabilize sediments and provides habitat for aquatic organisms.
