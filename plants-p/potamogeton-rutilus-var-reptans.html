Potamogeton rutilus var. reptans

Potamogeton rutilus var. reptans, commonly known as the thread-leaved pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in North America.

It has long, slender, ribbon-like leaves that are typically less than 1 millimeter wide, giving it its characteristic appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually found in clusters near the water's surface. It thrives in shallow, slow-moving waters and is often found in lakes, ponds, and streams.

Its dense growth can provide habitat for aquatic organisms and contribute to water quality by stabilizing sediments.
