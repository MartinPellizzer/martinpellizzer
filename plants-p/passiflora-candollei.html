Passiflora candollei

Passiflora candollei, commonly known as the Candolle's passionflower, is a climbing vine native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, complex flowers that feature five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments surrounding the reproductive structures. The plant produces edible, orange-yellow berries that are rich in nutrients and often consumed by local communities. Its foliage consists of heart-shaped leaves with a smooth texture and a slightly glossy surface.

Passiflora candollei is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
