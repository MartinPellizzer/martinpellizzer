Pachystela ovatostipulata

Pachystela ovatostipulata, commonly known as the broad-leafed mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on the branches of eucalyptus trees in Australia.

The plant produces small, white or pale green flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, followed by small, fleshy berries that serve as a food source for birds. Its root system forms haustoria, which penetrate the host tree to absorb water and nutrients.

Despite its parasitic nature, Pachystela ovatostipulata plays a role in the ecosystem by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
