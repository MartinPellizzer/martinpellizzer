Parastrephia lucida

Parastrephia lucida, commonly known as the shining star vine, is a climbing plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Violaceae and is characterized by its slender, twining stems and clusters of small, star-shaped flowers. The leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a glossy surface, contributing to its common name. The flowers are typically pale purple or white, with five petals and a distinctive spurred structure.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in shaded gardens or as a houseplant.
