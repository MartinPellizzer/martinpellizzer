Persea trollii

Persea trollii, commonly known as the Mexican persimmon, is a small evergreen tree native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its dark green, glossy leaves and fragrant flowers. The plant produces small, orange-red fruits that are edible when fully ripe. The fruit has a sweet, honey-like flavor and is often used in culinary applications.
