Piper halesiifolium

Piper halesiifolium, commonly known as the Halesia-leaved pepper plant, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes various pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its elongated, lance-shaped leaves that resemble those of the Halesia tree, giving it its common name. It produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in spikes, and its fruits are three-lobed berries containing a single seed.

This plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
