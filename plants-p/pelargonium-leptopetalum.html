Pelargonium leptopetalum

Pelargonium leptopetalum, commonly known as the narrow-petaled geranium, is a species of flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of pink, red, and white. The leaves are typically heart-shaped with a slightly hairy surface, and the plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and blooms.

Pelargonium leptopetalum is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
