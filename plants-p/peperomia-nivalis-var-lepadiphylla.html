Peperomia nivalis var. lepadiphylla

Peperomia nivalis var. lepadiphylla, commonly known as the snow peperomia, is a compact, low-growing plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It features thick, fleshy, dark green leaves that are often covered in a fine, powdery coating, giving it a unique appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white or greenish flowers, typically in clusters, which are followed by small, round berries. It is a succulent-like plant that thrives in well-draining soil and requires moderate light and water.

Its slow growth rate makes it an ideal choice for indoor gardening and container cultivation.
