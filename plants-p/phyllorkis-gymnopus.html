Phyllorkis gymnopus

Phyllorkis gymnopus, commonly known as the broad-leaved phyllorkis, is a member of the Myrtaceae family.

It is a small shrub characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, typically blooming in spring. Its leaves are broad and ovate, with a smooth, waxy surface.

Native to Australia, it thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and temperate regions.
