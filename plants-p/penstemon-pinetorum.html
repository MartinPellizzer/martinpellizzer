Penstemon pinetorum

Penstemon pinetorum, commonly known as the white beard scale penstemon, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of tubular, white to pale lavender flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is well adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are narrow and gray-green, often covered in a powdery coating.

Penstemon pinetorum is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
