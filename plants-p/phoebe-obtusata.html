Phoebe obtusata

Phoebe obtusata, commonly known as the blunt-leaf phoebe, is a species of flowering plant in the family Lauraceae.

It is native to the southeastern United States, particularly Florida and the Gulf Coast. The plant is characterized by its simple, ovate leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. It produces small, fragrant flowers that are usually white or pale green, followed by small, round berries.

Phoebe obtusata is often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and swampy areas.
