Pecteilis xanthantha

Pecteilis xanthantha, commonly known as the yellow pecteilis, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellow-orange composite flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, sandy soils along stream banks and in wetlands. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often with a slight hairy texture.

Pecteilis xanthantha is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
