Phlomis mauritanica

Phlomis mauritanica, commonly known as the Moroccan mullein, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features tall, erect stems covered in soft, woolly hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces showy clusters of yellow to orange flowers during the summer months. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a silvery-gray underside due to dense pubescence.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.
