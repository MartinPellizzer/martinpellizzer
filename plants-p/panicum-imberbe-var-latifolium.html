Panicum imberbe var. latifolium

Panicum imberbe var. latifolium, commonly known as broadleaf panicgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its wide, flat leaves and dense, spike-like inflorescences that produce small, wind-dispersed seeds. The plant typically grows in full sun and thrives in well-drained soils, making it adaptable to various habitats. Its foliage is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its attractive, grass-like appearance.

This variety is also valued for its role in stabilizing soil and supporting local wildlife.
