Panicum sanguinale var. blepharanthum

Panicum sanguinale var. blepharanthum, commonly known as crimson dropseed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit. The plant produces showy, pink to red inflorescences that resemble small, delicate plumes, adding ornamental value to gardens. Its foliage is narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue that contrasts nicely with its vibrant flowers.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for native plant gardens.
