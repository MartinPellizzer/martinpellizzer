Piper rohrii var. crassum

Piper rohrii var. crassum, commonly known as the broad-leaved pepper plant, is a variant of the Piper rohrii species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, broad leaves and small, clustered white flowers. The plant produces small, peppery berries that are used in traditional medicine and local cuisines. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often cultivated for its aromatic foliage and medicinal properties.

The variant 'crassum' distinguishes it from other forms of Piper rohrii through its distinctive leaf morphology.
