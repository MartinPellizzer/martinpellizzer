Panicum cayoense

Panicum cayoense, commonly known as the Cayo grass, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean region.

It is a member of the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, leaf-like blades. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and along riverbanks.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in tropical gardens.
