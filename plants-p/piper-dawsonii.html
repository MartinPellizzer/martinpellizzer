Piper dawsonii

Piper dawsonii, commonly known as Dawson's pepper, is a climbing vine native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper plants. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by berry-like fruits containing aromatic seeds. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a rough texture.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its unique foliage.
