Pyrus castaneifolia

Pyrus castaneifolia, commonly known as the Chinese white pear, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves that are often covered in a fine, rusty-colored pubescence. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, which are followed by edible, sweet, yellow or white fruits. The fruit is typically harvested in late summer and is valued for its culinary and medicinal uses.

It is also cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive foliage and seasonal display.
