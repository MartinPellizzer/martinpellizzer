Passiflora sparrei

Passiflora sparrei, commonly known as the California passionflower, is a climbing vine native to the western United States.

It features striking, showy flowers with five sepals, five petals, and a unique crown of filaments that surround the reproductive structures. The plant produces edible, orange-yellow berries that are rich in vitamin C. Its leaves are heart-shaped with a waxy texture and are often used in traditional medicine.

Passiflora sparrei thrives in sunny to partially shaded environments and is an important species for pollinators like bees and butterflies.
