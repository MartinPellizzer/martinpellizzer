Potentilla cinerea trifoliata

Potentilla cinerea trifoliata, commonly known as the silverleaf potentilla, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its grayish-silver foliage, which gives it a distinctive appearance and helps it retain moisture in arid environments. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are trifoliate, meaning they are divided into three leaflets, which is a notable botanical feature.

It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
