Paronychia polygonifolia proles velucensis

Paronychia polygonifolia proles velucensis, commonly known as the velvetleaf sandmat, is a low-growing, mat-forming plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It features small, fleshy, ovate leaves that are typically covered in a fine, velvety pubescence, which helps reduce water loss in its harsh environment. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, often appearing in late spring to early summer. Its root system is extensive and fibrous, allowing it to anchor firmly in sandy or rocky soils.

This species is well adapted to drought conditions and is often found in disturbed or open habitats.
