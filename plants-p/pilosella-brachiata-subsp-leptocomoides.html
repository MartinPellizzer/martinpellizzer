Pilosella brachiata subsp. leptocomoides

Pilosella brachiata subsp.

leptocomoides, commonly known as the slender-leaved goat's beard, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It features a rosette of narrow, hairy leaves that are typically blue-green in color.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in a dense, brush-like inflorescence, which bloom in early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in alpine meadows.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive and ornamental species within the Asteraceae family.
