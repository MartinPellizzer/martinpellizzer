Phaseolus tumidus

Phaseolus tumidus, commonly known as the inflated bean, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its unique, inflated pods that resemble small, rounded balloons. The plant produces small, white or purple flowers and has compound leaves with oval-shaped leaflets. Its seeds are typically pale yellow to brown and are used in traditional cuisines.

The plant is valued not only for its edible seeds but also for its ornamental appeal.
