Passiflora granadilla

Passiflora granadilla, commonly known as the granadilla or yellow passionflower, is a tropical vine native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, intricate flowers that feature a mix of blue, purple, and yellow hues. The plant produces edible, bright orange berries that are rich in vitamin C and have a tangy flavor. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped and covered in fine, silky hairs.

Passiflora granadilla is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its showy blooms and is also valued in traditional medicine.
