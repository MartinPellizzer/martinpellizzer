Phragmipedium caricinum

Phragmipedium caricinum, commonly known as the golden phragmipedium, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking golden-yellow flowers that bloom in late summer or early autumn. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded forest environments. Its leaves are broad and dark green, often with a waxy texture that helps retain moisture.

The orchid's unique floral structure and vibrant color make it a popular choice among orchid enthusiasts.
