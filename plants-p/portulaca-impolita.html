Portulaca impolita

Portulaca impolita, commonly known as the common purslane, is an annual flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features fleshy, succulent leaves and bright yellow flowers that bloom throughout the growing season. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and gardens. Its stems and leaves are coated with a waxy layer that helps reduce water loss, making it drought-tolerant.

It is also known for its edible leaves and seeds, which are used in various cuisines.
