Pelargonium × lasiocaulon

Pelargonium × lasiocaulon, commonly known as the ivy-leaved geranium, is a hybrid plant characterized by its trailing growth habit and distinctive heart-shaped, velvety leaves.

It produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, which bloom throughout the growing season. Native to South Africa, this plant thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight. It is widely cultivated as a decorative plant for hanging baskets and containers due to its cascading foliage.

The species is also valued for its adaptability to various climates and its ability to attract pollinators.
