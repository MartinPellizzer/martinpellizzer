Pavonia stellata

Pavonia stellata, commonly known as star cluster or starry pavonia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its showy, clustered inflorescences. The plant produces small, five-petaled flowers that range in color from white to pink, often appearing in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a hairy texture, while its stems are erect and often branched.

Pavonia stellata is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
