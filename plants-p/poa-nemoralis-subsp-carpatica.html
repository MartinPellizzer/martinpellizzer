Poa nemoralis subsp. carpatica

Poa nemoralis subsp.

carpatica, commonly known as the Carpathian bentgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the Carpathian Mountains. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and stream banks.

The plant has slender, tufted stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its inflorescence consists of a single, dense spikelet that bears small, two-rowed flowers.

This subspecies is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
