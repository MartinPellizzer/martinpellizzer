Panicum lacteum

Panicum lacteum, commonly known as millet grass, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, spike-like inflorescences. The leaves are typically narrow and grass-like, with a smooth texture. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that are clustered in dense spikes.

It is often used in traditional medicine and as a source of forage for livestock.
