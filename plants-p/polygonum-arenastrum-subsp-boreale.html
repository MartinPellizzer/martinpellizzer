Polygonum arenastrum subsp. boreale

Polygonum arenastrum subsp.

boreale, commonly known as the Arctic knotweed, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the Arctic and alpine regions of the Northern Hemisphere. It thrives in harsh, cold environments, often found on rocky slopes and gravelly soils.

The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or red, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often covered in fine hairs to reduce water loss.

This subspecies is adapted to extreme conditions, showcasing resilience and specialized botanical features.
