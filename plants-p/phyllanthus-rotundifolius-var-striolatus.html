Phyllanthus rotundifolius var. striolatus

Phyllanthus rotundifolius var. striolatus, commonly known as the narrow-leaf sand pear, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves with a distinctive striated pattern along the midrib. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its fruit is a capsule containing several small, hard seeds.

It is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments.
