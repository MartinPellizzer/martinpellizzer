Podocarpus longifoliolatus

Podocarpus longifoliolatus, commonly known as the long-leaved podocarp, is a medium-sized evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves that are often leathery and have a bluish-green hue. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by woody cones containing edible seeds. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland forests.

Its dense foliage makes it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping in warm regions.
