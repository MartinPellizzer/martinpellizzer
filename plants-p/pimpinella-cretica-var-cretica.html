Pimpinella cretica var. cretica

Pimpinella cretica var. cretica, commonly known as Cretan cowparsley, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its finely divided, feathery leaves and small white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or calcareous soils and is often found in coastal areas and mountainous regions. It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes other aromatic herbs like parsley and fennel.

The plant is also noted for its historical use in traditional medicine.
