Primula austrolisteri

Primula austrolisteri, commonly known as the New Zealand primrose, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and rock crevices. Its foliage consists of broad, oval leaves that are often covered in a fine, silky hair.

Primula austrolisteri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate beauty.
