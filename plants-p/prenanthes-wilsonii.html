Prenanthes wilsonii

Prenanthes wilsonii, commonly known as Wilson's waterleaf, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, arrow-shaped leaves and showy, white to pale green flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowers are often clustered in umbels and are accompanied by a spadix, a common feature in the Araceae family.

Prenanthes wilsonii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
