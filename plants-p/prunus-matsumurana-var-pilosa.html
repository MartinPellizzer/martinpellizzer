Prunus matsumurana var. pilosa

Prunus matsumurana var. pilosa, commonly known as the Japanese white cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its smooth, grey-barked trunk and dense, upright growth habit. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers in early spring, which are highly ornamental. Its leaves are ovate with serrated edges and turn yellow in autumn.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
