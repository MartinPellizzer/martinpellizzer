Prunus dussii

Prunus dussii, commonly known as the Desert peach, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and ovate, dark green leaves that turn red in the fall. The plant produces showy, white or pink flowers in the spring, followed by edible, yellow or orange fruits that are highly valued by both wildlife and humans. Prunus dussii is adapted to arid environments, with deep roots that allow it to survive in harsh, dry conditions.

Its botanical features make it an important species in desert ecosystems and a symbol of resilience in challenging climates.
