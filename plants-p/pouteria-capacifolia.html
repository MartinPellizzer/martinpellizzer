Pouteria capacifolia

Pouteria capacifolia, commonly known as the Brazilian nut tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is characterized by its large, smooth, greyish bark and can grow up to 30 meters tall. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by large, woody pods containing the edible seeds known as Brazil nuts. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and ovate with a pointed tip.

The plant plays a significant ecological role in its habitat and is also valued for its economic importance.
