Pyrus × robusta

Pyrus × robusta, commonly known as the European whitebeam, is a hybrid species within the genus Pyrus, native to Europe.

It is characterized by its small, white to pale yellow flowers that appear in clusters, typically in early spring. The plant produces small, round fruits that are generally edible but not particularly flavorful. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland areas or along riverbanks.

The leaves are simple, alternate, and have a serrated edge, contributing to its ornamental value in gardens.
