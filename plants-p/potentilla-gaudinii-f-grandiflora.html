Potentilla gaudinii f. grandiflora

Potentilla gaudinii f. grandiflora, commonly known as the large-flowered cinquefoil, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features showy, bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has compound leaves with five to seven leaflets, which are typically oval and serrated. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or alpine settings.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its compact growth habit and long-lasting blooms.
