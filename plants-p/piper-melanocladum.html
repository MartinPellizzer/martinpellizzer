Piper melanocladum

Piper melanocladum, commonly known as black-stemmed pepper, is a tropical vine native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species. The plant is characterized by its dark green, waxy leaves and small, yellowish flowers that develop into berries. Its fruits are typically harvested when they turn from green to red, indicating maturity.

The plant is often cultivated for its aromatic leaves and potential medicinal properties.
