Pandanus serpentinicus

Pandanus serpentinicus, commonly known as the serpentined pandanus, is a tropical plant native to the Indo-Pacific region.

It is characterized by its large, sword-shaped leaves that grow in a rosette pattern, often reaching lengths of up to 2 meters. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences, which are then followed by edible, fibrous fruits. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to thrive in nutrient-poor, coastal soils.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional uses in various Pacific island cultures.
