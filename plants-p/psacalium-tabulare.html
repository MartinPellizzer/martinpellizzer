Psacalium tabulare

Psacalium tabulare, commonly known as the flatleaf spurge, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and the Arabian Peninsula.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments. The plant features alternate, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small and yellow, arranged in clusters, and it produces toxic, milky sap that can cause skin irritation.

Psacalium tabulare is often found in rocky soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.
