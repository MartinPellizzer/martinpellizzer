Pergularia purpurea

Pergularia purpurea, commonly known as the purple pogostemon, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia, particularly China and India.

It is a member of the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its square, hairy stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically purple or pink in color, which bloom in clusters. Pergularia purpurea thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for various purposes.
