Pulmonaria denticulata

Pulmonaria denticulata, commonly known as the toothed lungwort, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of lance-shaped leaves with toothed margins. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped, pinkish-purple flowers in early spring. It thrives in moist, shady woodlands and prefers well-drained, loamy soil.

Pulmonaria denticulata is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting early pollinators.
