Paphiopedilum dodyanum

Paphiopedilum dodyanum, commonly known as the Dodyan's lady's slipper, is a rare and exquisite orchid native to the mountainous regions of Vietnam.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety petals and a unique, pouch-like lip that resembles a lady's slipper, a feature typical of the orchid family. The plant thrives in cool, humid environments and requires well-draining, acidic soil for optimal growth. It blooms during the spring and summer months, producing fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a conservation concern in its native range.
