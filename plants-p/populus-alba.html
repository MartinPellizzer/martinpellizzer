Populus alba

Populus alba, commonly known as the white poplar, is a deciduous tree native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its white, fuzzy underside on its leaves, which gives it a distinctive appearance. The tree produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in catkins, which are wind-pollinated. Its bark is pale and often has a flaky, peeling texture.

Populus alba is often found near water sources and can thrive in a variety of soil types.
