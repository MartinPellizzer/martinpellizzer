Potentilla leschenaultiana var. concolor

Potentilla leschenaultiana var. concolor, commonly known as the smooth potentilla, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense foliage, with leaves that are typically smooth and oval in shape. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers in early summer, which are attractive to pollinators. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Due to its ornamental value and hardiness, it is a popular choice for gardeners in temperate climates.
