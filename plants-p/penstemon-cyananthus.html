Penstemon cyananthus

Penstemon cyananthus, commonly known as bluecurls, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple tubular flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and dry habitats, such as cliffs and slopes. It has hairy, branched stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves.

Penstemon cyananthus is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a nectar source for pollinators.
