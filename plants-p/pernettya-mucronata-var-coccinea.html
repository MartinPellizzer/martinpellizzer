Pernettya mucronata var. coccinea

Pernettya mucronata var. coccinea, commonly known as the red-flowered pernettya, is a flowering plant native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in moist, shaded forest environments. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to red, with the varietal name "coccinea" referring to its reddish hues. Its leaves are elliptical and have a smooth texture, while its fruits are small, fleshy berries that are edible for birds.

This species plays an important ecological role in its native habitat by supporting local wildlife and contributing to forest biodiversity.
