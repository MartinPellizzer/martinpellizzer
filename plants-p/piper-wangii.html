Piper wangii

Piper wangii, commonly known as the Wangi pepper or Wangi piper, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, particularly Indonesia and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its climbing habit, with slender stems and heart-shaped leaves. Its small, green berries are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The botanical study of Piper wangii contributes to the understanding of Piperaceae diversity and its ecological roles in tropical ecosystems.
