Platanthera fuscescens

Platanthera fuscescens, commonly known as the brown twayblade, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of North America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and small, greenish-yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands. It has a symbiotic relationship with specific mycorrhizal fungi, which are essential for its nutrient uptake.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered a threatened species in many regions.
