Pterisanthes pedata

Pterisanthes pedata, commonly known as the mountain hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It is characterized by its striking, large, showy flowers that typically range in color from white to pink. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it thrives in well-drained, rocky soils. It is a perennial shrub that can grow up to several meters in height.

The flowers bloom in the summer months and attract a variety of pollinators, including bees and butterflies.
