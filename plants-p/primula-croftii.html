Primula croftii

Primula croftii, commonly known as the Croft's primrose, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of China.

It is characterized by its vibrant purple or pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or well-drained soils and thrives in cool, moist environments. Its leaves are oval-shaped with a slightly hairy texture, and it is often found in alpine meadows.

Primula croftii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its delicate, showy blossoms.
