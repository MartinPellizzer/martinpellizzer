Prismatocarpus decurrens

Prismatocarpus decurrens, commonly known as the narrow-leaf tamarind, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its elongated, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stems. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by elongated, woody fruits that contain edible seeds. Its bark is smooth and often exudes a resinous substance, which has been used traditionally in various applications.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some medicinal uses in traditional medicine.
