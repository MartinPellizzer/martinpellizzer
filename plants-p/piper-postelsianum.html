Piper postelsianum

Piper postelsianum, commonly known as the Java pepper or wild pepper, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a glossy appearance.

The small, greenish-white flowers are borne in clusters and are followed by small, fleshy berries that turn red when mature.
