Pandanus croceus

Pandanus croceus, commonly known as the golden pandanus, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stem and large, fan-shaped leaves that are typically a vibrant green. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into fibrous, orange-colored fruits. Its leaves are often used in traditional crafts and as thatching materials.

The golden pandanus is adapted to grow in coastal and wetland environments, thriving in high humidity and saline conditions.
