Prunus chamaecerasus

Prunus chamaecerasus, commonly known as the dwarf cherry or Japanese flowering cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its dense, rounded growth habit and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers in early spring, which are followed by red or black berries. Its leaves are ovate to elliptic, with a smooth margin and a glossy green upper surface.

Prunus chamaecerasus thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it suitable for gardens and landscapes.
