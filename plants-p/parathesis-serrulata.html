Parathesis serrulata

Parathesis serrulata, commonly known as the serrated parathesis, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves with finely serrated edges. The plant produces small, clustered white or pale yellow flowers that bloom during the summer months. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest undergrowth.

Its ornamental value makes it a sought-after plant for botanical gardens and private collections.
