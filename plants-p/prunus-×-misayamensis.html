Prunus × misayamensis

Prunus × misayamensis, commonly known as the Japanese cherry blossom, is a hybrid species native to Japan.

It is characterized by its delicate, pink or white flowers that bloom in early spring, making it a symbol of renewal and beauty. The plant belongs to the Rosaceae family and is closely related to other cherry species such as Prunus serrulata and Prunus lannesiana. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have serrated edges, while the fruit is a small, yellow drupe.

The botanical features of Prunus × misayamensis make it a popular ornamental plant in gardens and public spaces.
