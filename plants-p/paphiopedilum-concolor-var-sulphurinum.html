Paphiopedilum concolor var. sulphurinum

Paphiopedilum concolor var. sulphurinum, commonly known as the Yellow Lady's Slipper, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking yellow and green flowers with a distinctive slipper-shaped pouch. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often in forest undergrowth. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, and it produces a single, showy flower per stem.

This variety is prized for its unique coloration and is a popular choice among orchid enthusiasts.
