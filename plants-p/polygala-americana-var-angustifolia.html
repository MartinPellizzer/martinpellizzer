Polygala americana var. angustifolia

Polygala americana var. angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as woodlands and wet meadows. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces delicate, spicate inflorescences. Its flowers are usually pink or purple and have a unique, spurred appearance.

It plays a role in supporting local pollinators and is often found in ecological restoration projects.
