Pyrus sohayakiensis

Pyrus sohayakiensis, commonly known as the Sohayaki pear, is a species of flowering plant in the family Rosaceae.

It is native to the mountainous regions of central Asia, particularly in parts of China and the Tibetan Plateau. The plant is characterized by its deciduous leaves, which are alternate and ovate in shape, with a rough, hairy texture. Its flowers are typically white or pale yellow, arranged in clusters, and bloom in early spring.

The fruit, a small pear-like apple, is edible and has a sweet, aromatic flavor.
