Pyrus crataegifolia

Pyrus crataegifolia, commonly known as the Chinese white pear, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and alternate, pinnately compound leaves. The plant produces small, white flowers in early spring, followed by edible, yellow or green fruits that ripen in late summer.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit, though it is less commonly grown for commercial purposes compared to other pear species.
