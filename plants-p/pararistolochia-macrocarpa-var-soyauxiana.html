Pararistolochia macrocarpa var. soyauxiana

Pararistolochia macrocarpa var. soyauxiana, commonly known as the giant Chinese knotweed, is a climbing vine native to southern China.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and distinctive, complex, and often fragrant flowers that resemble knots. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow berries that mature to a dark purple or black color. It is a member of the Aristolochiaceae family, known for its unique floral structures and chemical compounds.

However, it is considered invasive in some regions due to its rapid growth and ability to outcompete native vegetation.
