Parosela plumosa

Parosela plumosa, commonly known as the silky parosela, is a flowering plant native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its soft, feathery foliage and clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in high-altitude grasslands. Its leaves are covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Parosela plumosa is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable climatic conditions.
