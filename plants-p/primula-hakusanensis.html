Primula hakusanensis

Primula hakusanensis, commonly known as the Hakusan primrose, is a perennial herb native to Japan.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and mountainous regions. Its leaves are oval and slightly hairy, with a broad base that clasps the stem.

Primula hakusanensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
