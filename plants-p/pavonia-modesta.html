Pavonia modesta

Pavonia modesta, commonly known as the Mexican bushcrack, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring woody stems and opposite leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of red, pink, or white, which bloom in the summer months. Its flowers are arranged in racemes and are attractive to pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Pavonia modesta is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
