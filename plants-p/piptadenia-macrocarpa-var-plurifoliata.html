Piptadenia macrocarpa var. plurifoliata

Piptadenia macrocarpa var. plurifoliata, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or *Piptadenia plurifoliata*, is a deciduous tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are often deeply lobed and can reach up to 30 cm in length. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by elongated, woody pods containing edible, red, fleshy berries. These berries are rich in nutrients and are often consumed by both wildlife and humans.

The plant is valued for its ornamental beauty and is also used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
