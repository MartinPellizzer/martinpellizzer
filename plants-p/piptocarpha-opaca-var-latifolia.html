Piptocarpha opaca var. latifolia

Piptocarpha opaca var. latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf grey gooseweed, is a perennial herb native to the arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its broad, waxy leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in its harsh environment. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in dense, spherical clusters, which are typically visited by pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas or along roadsides.

Its compact growth habit and drought tolerance make it a hardy plant adapted to extreme conditions.
