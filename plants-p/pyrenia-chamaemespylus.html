Pyrenia chamaemespylus

Pyrenia chamaemespylus, commonly known as the creeping thyme, is a low-growing perennial plant native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, small leaves and clusters of pink or white flowers. The plant typically thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as ground cover. Its compact growth habit and ornamental value make it a popular choice in horticulture.

Pyrenia chamaemespylus is also valued for its medicinal properties, with historical use in herbal remedies.
