Pseudotrillium rivale

Pseudotrillium rivale, commonly known as the river trillium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern North America.

It typically grows in moist, wooded areas and thrives in partial to full shade. The plant features a single, waxy, white or pale pink flower that emerges from a rosette of heart-shaped leaves. Its flowers are often visited by pollinators such as bees and flies.

Pseudotrillium rivale is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in forest ecosystems.
