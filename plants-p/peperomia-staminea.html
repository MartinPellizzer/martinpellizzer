Peperomia staminea

Peperomia staminea, commonly known as the silver peperomia, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its compact, low-growing habit and thick, waxy leaves that often have a silvery or variegated appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or reddish in color. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Peperomia staminea is a popular choice for indoor gardening due to its ease of care and attractive foliage.
