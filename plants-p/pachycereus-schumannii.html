Pachycereus schumannii

Pachycereus schumannii, commonly known as the Schumann's prickly pear, is a cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its thick, ribbed stems that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. The plant produces large, showy flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. Its fruit, known as a tuna, is edible and has a sweet, tangy flavor.

This species is often cultivated for ornamental purposes and for its edible fruit.
