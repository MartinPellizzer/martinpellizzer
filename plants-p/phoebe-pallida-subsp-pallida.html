Phoebe pallida subsp. pallida

Phoebe pallida subsp.

pallida, commonly known as the pale phoebe, is a small evergreen shrub native to the southeastern United States. It typically grows in moist, acidic soils along stream banks and in forest understories.

The plant features elliptic to ovate leaves with a smooth, waxy surface and a bluish-green color. Its small, fragrant white flowers appear in clusters and are followed by dark blue berries.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to local wildlife.
