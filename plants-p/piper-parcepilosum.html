Piper parcepilosum

Piper parcepilosum, commonly known as the hairy pepper vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes various species of pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and small, opposite leaves with a rough texture. Its flowers are unisexual, with separate male and female flowers on the same plant, and it produces small, round berries that are typically green when mature.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in some indigenous cultures.
