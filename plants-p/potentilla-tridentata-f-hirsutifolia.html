Potentilla tridentata f. hirsutifolia

Potentilla tridentata f. hirsutifolia, commonly known as the hirsute cinquefoil, is a variant of the three-toothed cinquefoil.

It is a low-growing perennial shrub characterized by its hairy stems and leaves. The plant typically features small, yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in ecological and horticultural contexts.
