Papaver dubium var. arenarium

Papaver dubium var. arenarium, commonly known as the sand poppy, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its delicate, yellowish-white petals and a distinctive, rounded seed capsule. The plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in coastal areas and arid environments. It produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in early spring.

This variety is noted for its resilience to harsh conditions and its role in supporting local pollinator populations.
