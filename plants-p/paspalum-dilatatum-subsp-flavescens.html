Paspalum dilatatum subsp. flavescens

Paspalum dilatatum subsp.

flavescens, commonly known as yellow paspalum, is a perennial grass native to South America. It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and produces small, pale yellow to golden inflorescences.

The plant typically thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green coloration.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil in wet environments.
