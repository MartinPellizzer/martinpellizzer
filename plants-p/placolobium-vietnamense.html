Placolobium vietnamense

Placolobium vietnamense, commonly known as Vietnamese false indigo, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along stream banks. It has a taproot system and produces small, legume-like pods that contain seeds.

Due to its ornamental value and potential ecological benefits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
