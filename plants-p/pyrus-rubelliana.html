Pyrus rubelliana

Pyrus rubelliana, commonly known as the red-leafed pear, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its vibrant red foliage, which becomes more pronounced in the fall. The plant produces small, white flowers in the spring, followed by edible, sweet berries that attract wildlife. Its leaves are typically oval with a serrated edge and a glossy texture.

Pyrus rubelliana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can thrive in a variety of soil types.
