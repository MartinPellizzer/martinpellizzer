Poa howellii var. microsperma

Poa howellii var. microsperma, commonly known as the Howell's bluegrass, is a low-growing perennial grass native to the western United States.

It typically thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks. The plant features fine, bluish-green foliage and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. Its flowers are wind-pollinated, a common trait among grasses, and the seeds are small and numerous.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native plant gardens.
