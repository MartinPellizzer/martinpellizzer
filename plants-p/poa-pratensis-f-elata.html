Poa pratensis f. elata

Poa pratensis f. elata, commonly known as the tall meadow grass, is a variant of the common bentgrass.

It is characterized by its elongated, slender blades and a more upright growth habit compared to the typical form. This grass species is often found in moist, well-drained soils in temperate regions. Its flowering stems are usually taller, reaching up to 60 cm in height, and bear spikelets that are loosely arranged.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping and turfgrass mixtures.
