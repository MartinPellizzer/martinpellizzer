Pavetta catophylla var. pubescens

Pavetta catophylla var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy pavetta, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It features small, elliptical leaves that are covered in fine, pubescent hairs, giving it a distinctive textured appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers, which are typically arranged in umbels. Its fruit is a capsule that contains numerous small, black seeds.

This species is often found in moist, shaded habitats such as forest understories and rocky areas.
