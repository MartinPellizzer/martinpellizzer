Pyrus × parkmannii

Pyrus × parkmannii, commonly known as the Chinese white pear, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Pyrus bretschneideri and Pyrus ussuriensis.

It is a deciduous tree that typically grows to a height of 5–10 meters, featuring a broad, rounded crown and grayish-brown bark. The plant produces small, white flowers in spring, followed by edible, sweet, and aromatic fruits in late summer. It is native to China and is cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit production.

The leaves are alternate, simple, and have a serrated margin, contributing to its aesthetic appeal.
