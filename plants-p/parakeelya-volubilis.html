Parakeelya volubilis

Parakeelya volubilis, commonly known as the climbing pomegranate, is a tropical shrub or vine native to Southeast Asia.

It features opposite, ovate leaves with a smooth texture and a slightly pubescent underside. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pale yellow or greenish-white. Its fruit is a small, round berry that turns red when ripe, similar to the pomegranate.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has some traditional medicinal uses.
