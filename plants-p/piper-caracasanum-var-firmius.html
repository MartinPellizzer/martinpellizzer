Piper caracasanum var. firmius

Piper caracasanum var. firmius, commonly known as the purple pepper plant, is a tropical species belonging to the Piperaceae family.

It is characterized by its aromatic, dark purple to black berries that are often used in traditional medicine and culinary applications. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters, with pinnately compound leaves. Its fruits develop in clusters and are known for their pungent, peppery flavor.

The plant thrives in humid, warm climates and is native to regions of South America, particularly Brazil.
