Pseudocassine

Pseudocassine, commonly known as the false cassia, is a genus of flowering plants in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to various regions in Africa, Asia, and Australia, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy yellow or orange flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or tree, with some species reaching heights of up to 10 meters. Pseudocassine species are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The seeds of some species are also used as a spice or dye.
