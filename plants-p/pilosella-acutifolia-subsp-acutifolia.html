Pilosella acutifolia subsp. acutifolia

Pilosella acutifolia subsp.

acutifolia, commonly known as the sharp-leaved mouse-ear, is a flowering plant in the Asteraceae family. It is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage and small, daisy-like yellow flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils, often in alpine or subalpine regions. It has narrow, linear leaves that are sharply pointed, giving it its common name.

This subspecies is native to Europe and parts of western Asia.
