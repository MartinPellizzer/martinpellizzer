Polypodium hombersleyi

Polypodium hombersleyi, commonly known as the Hombersley's shield fern, is a species of evergreen fern native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its large, leathery, shield-shaped fronds that can grow up to 1 meter in length. The plant typically thrives in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices, where it can retain humidity. Its rhizome is thick and creeping, allowing it to spread and form dense colonies.

The fronds are bi-lobed and have a distinctive, glossy appearance, making it a notable ornamental plant in horticulture.
