Plocama pendula

Plocama pendula, commonly known as the weeping plocama, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features long, drooping clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are typically dark green and slightly hairy. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its unique cascading form makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening in suitable climates.
