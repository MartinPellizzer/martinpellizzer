Podalyria oleifolia

Podalyria oleifolia, commonly known as the oil plant or narrowleaf oil plant, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink, followed by elongated pods that contain the oily seeds. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three oval-shaped leaflets, and the plant is often found in dry, open habitats.

The seeds are valued for their high oil content, which has been used traditionally in various applications.
