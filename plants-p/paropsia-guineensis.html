Paropsia guineensis

Paropsia guineensis, commonly known as the African violet, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Gesneriaceae family and is characterized by its compact rosette of velvety, dark green leaves. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers in shades of purple, pink, and white, often with a contrasting throat. It thrives in humid, well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light.

Paropsia guineensis is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
