Phragmanthera crassicaulis

Phragmanthera crassicaulis, commonly known as the broad-stemmed phragmanthera, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its thick, succulent stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

Phragmanthera crassicaulis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
