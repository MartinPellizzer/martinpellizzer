Panicum oligosanthes var. helleri

Panicum oligosanthes var. helleri, commonly known as Heller's panicgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as wetlands, marshes, and roadside ditches. The plant features slender, tufted stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its inflorescence consists of a dense, spike-like cluster of small, two-lobed spikelets.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting native wildlife.
