Perularia fuscescens

Perularia fuscescens, commonly known as the dark green bugloss, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to purple, typically blooming in spring. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white wax. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Perularia fuscescens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
