Potamogeton × suecicus f. abundans

Potamogeton × suecicus f. abundans, commonly known as the common pondweed, is a hybrid aquatic plant that thrives in freshwater environments.

It is characterized by its long, slender stems and small, spirally arranged leaves that are typically submerged. The plant produces tiny, green flowers that are clustered in spikelets, often found near the water's surface. It plays a vital role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.

Its rapid growth makes it both a valuable ecological component and a potential weed in managed water bodies.
