Pycnandra acuminata

Pycnandra acuminata, commonly known as the black bean tree, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree that can grow up to 10 meters in height. The plant is characterized by its dark, glossy leaves and produces small, white flowers that are followed by woody, bean-like pods. The tree is notable for its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions and is often found in secondary forests.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and traditional medicine.
