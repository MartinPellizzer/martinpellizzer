Pleurothallis vide-vallis

Pleurothallis vide-vallis, commonly known as the "ghost orchid," is a member of the orchid family (Orchidaceae) and is native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is a small, epiphytic plant that typically grows on trees or rocks in humid, shaded environments. The plant is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped leaves and small, white or pale green flowers that bloom in late summer. Its name, "vide-vallis," is derived from Latin, meaning "see the valley," likely referring to its subtle appearance.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
