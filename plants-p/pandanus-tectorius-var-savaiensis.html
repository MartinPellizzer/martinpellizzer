Pandanus tectorius var. savaiensis

Pandanus tectorius var. savaiensis, commonly known as the Savai ªi pandanus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Pandanaceae.

It is native to the islands of Samoa and nearby regions in the South Pacific. This plant is characterized by its long, narrow, leaf-like structures that are often used in traditional crafts and as thatching materials. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically yellow or green, followed by fibrous fruits.

It thrives in coastal and wetland areas, preferring well-drained soil and full sun exposure.
