Piper maculatum

Piper maculatum, commonly known as the spotted pepper plant, is a flowering shrub native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves with distinctive white or yellow spots. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into red, fleshy berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The berries are edible but should be consumed in moderation due to their mild peppery flavor.
