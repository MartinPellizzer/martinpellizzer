Paepalanthus irwinii

Paepalanthus irwinii, commonly known as the Irwin's pincushion cactus, is a species of flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny stems that form a spherical or cushion-like shape. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the summer months, which are clustered at the top of the stems. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for arid or semi-arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
