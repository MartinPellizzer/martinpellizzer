Parapteroceras erosulum

Parapteroceras erosulum, commonly known as the narrow-leafed orchid, is a flowering plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and delicate, spurred flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and pale pink. Native to the wet tropical regions of Southeast Asia, this species thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant's root system is fibrous, and it often grows in dense clusters on forest floors or along stream banks.

Its unique floral structure and ecological adaptations make it an important component of its native ecosystems.
