Prunus domestica var. apyrena

Prunus domestica var. apyrena, commonly known as the Apyrena plum, is a botanical variant of the common European plum.

It is characterized by its small, round fruits that are typically less than 2 centimeters in diameter. The plant produces small, white flowers in the spring, which are followed by the development of its unique, sweet-tart fruit. It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that thrives in temperate climates.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its use in culinary applications.
