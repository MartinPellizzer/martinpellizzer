Peperomia obtusifolia var. petiolata

Peperomia obtusifolia var. petiolata, commonly known as the wax plant or broadleaf peperomia, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It features thick, waxy, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, powdery coating, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters, typically in shades of red or purple, which are followed by small, round berries. It thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Its slow growth rate and ornamental foliage make it a desirable plant for both decorative and low-maintenance purposes.
