Prunus domestica maliformis

Prunus domestica maliformis, commonly known as the Maliformis plum, is a cultivar of the European plum.

It is characterized by its distinctive, often twisted or irregular growth habit, which gives it a unique appearance. The plant produces small, dark purple fruits that are typically harvested in late summer. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities as well as its edible fruit.
