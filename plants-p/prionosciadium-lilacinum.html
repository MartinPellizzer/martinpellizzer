Prionosciadium lilacinum

Prionosciadium lilacinum, commonly known as the lilac-flowered cowpea, is a flowering plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is characterized by its delicate, lilac-colored flowers and trifoliate leaves. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in disturbed soils or along roadsides. It produces small, elongated pods that contain edible seeds, which are used in various traditional cuisines.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.
