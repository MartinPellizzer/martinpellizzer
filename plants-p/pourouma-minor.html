Pourouma minor

Pourouma minor, commonly known as the red bamboo palm, is a member of the Arecaceae family and is native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, pinnately compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by edible, round fruits that contain a single seed. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in humid lowland forests.

Due to its ornamental value and unique appearance, it is cultivated in some botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
