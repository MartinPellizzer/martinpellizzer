Paeonia japonica f. pilosa

Paeonia japonica f. pilosa, commonly known as the Japanese peony with hairy leaves, is a cultivar characterized by its distinctive foliage and floral features.

This plant is a herbaceous perennial that belongs to the芍药科 (Paeoniaceae) family, native to Japan. Its leaves are covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a unique textured appearance. The flowers are typically large, with a range of colors from pink to white, and bloom in late spring.

It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
