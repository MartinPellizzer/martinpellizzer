Piper lunare

Piper lunare, commonly known as moon pepper, is a climbing shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes over 2,000 species of flowering plants. The plant produces small, bright yellow berries that are edible and have a spicy, peppery flavor. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The plant's name is derived from the color of its fruits, which resemble the color of the moon.
