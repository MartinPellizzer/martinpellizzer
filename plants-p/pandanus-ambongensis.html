Pandanus ambongensis

Pandanus ambongensis, commonly known as the Bismarck palm, is a species of flowering plant in the family Pandanaceae.

It is native to the Pacific islands, including parts of Papua New Guinea and the Solomon Islands. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and long, pinnate leaves that can reach several meters in length. Its inflorescences are clustered at the base of the leaves and produce small, fragrant flowers.

The plant is often used in traditional crafts and has cultural significance in the regions where it grows.
