Picea canadensis f. parva

Picea canadensis f. parva, commonly known as the dwarf Canadian spruce, is a compact variety of the white spruce.

It is characterized by its short, dense branches and small, needle-like leaves that are typically bluish-green in color. This form is naturally occurring and is often found in shaded or rocky environments. It is a slow-growing tree that can reach heights of up to 5 meters.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in challenging conditions.
