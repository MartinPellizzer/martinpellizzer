Phalaenopsis amabilis var. rosenstromii

Phalaenopsis amabilis var. rosenstromii, commonly known as the Rosenstrom's Phalaenopsis, is a tropical orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its long, pendant inflorescences that bear clusters of fragrant, waxy flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant has broad, dark green leaves that are often marked with silver or gray veins, giving it a distinctive appearance. It is a sympodial orchid, meaning it grows in pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is commonly cultivated as a houseplant due to its ability to thrive in controlled environments.
