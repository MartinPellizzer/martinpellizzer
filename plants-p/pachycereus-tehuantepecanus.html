Pachycereus tehuantepecanus

Pachycereus tehuantepecanus, commonly known as the Tehuantepec cactus, is a columnar cactus native to southern Mexico.

It is characterized by its thick, ribbed stems that can grow up to 10 meters in height. The plant has spiny areoles and produces yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. It is adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water.

Its structure provides habitat for various desert animals and insects.
