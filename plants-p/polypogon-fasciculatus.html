Polypogon fasciculatus

Polypogon fasciculatus, commonly known as narrowleaf bottlebrush grass, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and distinctive, bottlebrush-like inflorescences that are typically reddish-brown in color. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and prairies.

Its unique appearance and ecological role make it an important species in native plant communities.
