Physalis digitalifolia

Physalis digitalifolia, commonly known as the Cape gooseberry or goldenberry, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant orange, lantern-shaped fruit that encloses the seeds. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. Its leaves are typically hairy and have a distinctive, lobed shape.

Physalis digitalifolia is often cultivated for its edible fruit, which has a tart, sweet flavor and is used in various culinary applications.
