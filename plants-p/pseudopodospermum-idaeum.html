Pseudopodospermum idaeum

Pseudopodospermum idaeum, commonly known as the Cape chestnut, is a shrub native to South Africa.

It belongs to the family Proteaceae and is characterized by its woody stems and leathery, elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by woody, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds. It is adapted to nutrient-poor soils and is often found in fynbos vegetation.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has some traditional uses in local communities.
