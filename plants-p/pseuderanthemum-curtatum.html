Pseuderanthemum curtatum

Pseuderanthemum curtatum, commonly known as the pink-stemmed pseuderanthemum, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its slender, often reddish stems and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, arranged in clusters at the leaf axils. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in some horticultural settings for its attractive foliage and flowering habit.
