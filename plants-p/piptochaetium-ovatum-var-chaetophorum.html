Piptochaetium ovatum var. chaetophorum

Piptochaetium ovatum var. chaetophorum, commonly known as the hairy fingergrass, is a perennial grass native to parts of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and narrow, grass-like leaves with a distinctive hairy texture. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas.

Its adaptability makes it a common weed in agricultural settings.
