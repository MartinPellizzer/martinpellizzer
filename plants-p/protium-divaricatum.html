Protium divaricatum

Protium divaricatum, commonly known as the West Indian satinwood, is a tropical tree native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the family Sapotaceae and is characterized by its smooth, dark bark and elliptical leaves with a leathery texture. However, due to habitat loss, it is considered a threatened species in some regions.
