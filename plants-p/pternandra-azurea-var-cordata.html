Pternandra azurea var. cordata

Pternandra azurea var. cordata, commonly known as the blue pterandra, is a strikingly beautiful plant native to tropical regions.

It is characterized by its vibrant blue flowers, which are clustered in dense inflorescences. The leaves are typically heart-shaped, giving the plant its scientific name "cordata," meaning "heart-like.". It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and requires partial to full sunlight.

This plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a decorative foliage plant.
