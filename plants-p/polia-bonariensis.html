Polia bonariensis

Polia bonariensis, commonly known as the Brazilian orchid, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its elegant, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, often in the undergrowth of tropical forests. Its leaves are broad and strap-like, and it produces clusters of small, colorful blossoms.

Polia bonariensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in botanical gardens and greenhouses worldwide.
