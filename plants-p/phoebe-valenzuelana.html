Phoebe valenzuelana

Phoebe valenzuelana, commonly known as the Valenzuelan phoebe, is a species of flowering plant in the family Lauraceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of Central and South America, particularly in countries like Mexico, Guatemala, and Costa Rica. The plant is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, white flowers that bloom in clusters. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as rainforests and along riverbanks.

Phoebe valenzuelana is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine by indigenous communities.
