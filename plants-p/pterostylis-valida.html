Pterostylis valida

Pterostylis valida, commonly known as the Valid's hooded orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to southeastern Australia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as understorey vegetation in rainforests. The plant features a single, erect stem with a single, hooded flower that is often pale green or yellowish in color. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb nutrients from the surrounding soil.

The orchid is part of the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its unique floral structure and specialized pollination mechanisms.
