Passiflora ciliata var. hibiscifolia

Passiflora ciliata var. hibiscifolia, commonly known as the Hibiscifoliate passionflower, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves with serrated edges and clusters of striking, colorful flowers that resemble a crown. The flowers typically feature five sepals, five petals, and a distinctive crown of filaments around the reproductive structures. It produces edible, orange-yellow berries that are rich in nutrients and used in traditional medicine.

This plant thrives in humid, warm climates and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its aesthetic appeal.
