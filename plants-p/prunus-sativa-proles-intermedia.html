Prunus sativa proles intermedia

Prunus sativa proles intermedia, commonly known as the intermediate almond.

This plant is a hybrid species that exhibits characteristics of both Prunus dulcis (sweet almond) and Prunus amygdalus var. fuscocata (bitter almond). It is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to regions with temperate climates. The flowers are typically pink or white, and the fruit is a drupe containing a single seed.

It is cultivated for its edible nuts, which have a unique flavor profile between sweet and bitter almonds.
