Pholidota ventricosa

Pholidota ventricosa, commonly known as the Chinese lantern plant, is a perennial shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, inflated, orange-red fruits that resemble small lanterns, which are often used in ornamental gardening. The plant has palmately lobed leaves and produces small, inconspicuous white flowers. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its vibrant, showy fruits.

The species is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional Chinese medicine.
