Protasparagus angusticladus

Protasparagus angusticladus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved asparagus, is a member of the Asparagaceae family.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, narrow leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters, which are typically followed by small, berry-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or arid environments.

Due to its unique morphology and limited distribution, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
