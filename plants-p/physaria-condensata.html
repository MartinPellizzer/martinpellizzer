Physaria condensata

Physaria condensata, commonly known as the yellow sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It has a compact, mat-forming growth habit with hairy, oval-shaped leaves.

Physaria condensata is adapted to arid environments and is often found in sandy or gravelly soils.
