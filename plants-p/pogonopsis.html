Pogonopsis

Pogonopsis, commonly known as the beard grass, is a genus of flowering plants belonging to the grass family, Poaceae.

These plants are typically found in arid and semi-arid regions, where they adapt to harsh environmental conditions. Pogonopsis species are characterized by their dense, hairy foliage that resembles a beard, giving them their common name. They often grow in low, compact clumps and have narrow, grass-like leaves.

The flowers of Pogonopsis are small and clustered, usually appearing in spikelets that are borne on slender stalks.
