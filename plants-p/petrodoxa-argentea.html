Petrodoxa argentea

Petrodoxa argentea, commonly known as the silverleaf petrodoxa, is a flowering plant native to parts of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which gives it its common name. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Petrodoxa argentea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
