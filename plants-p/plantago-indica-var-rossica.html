Plantago indica var. rossica

Plantago indica var. rossica, commonly known as Russian plantain, is a variant of the common plantain species.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. The plant produces small, pale greenish flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically borne on slender stalks. It is native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia and is often found in disturbed soils such as roadsides and fields.

This species is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
