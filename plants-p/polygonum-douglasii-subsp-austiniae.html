Polygonum douglasii subsp. austiniae

Polygonum douglasii subsp.

austiniae, commonly known as Austin's smartweed, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States. It belongs to the buckwheat family (Polygonaceae) and is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, pinkish-red flowers.

The plant typically grows in dry, disturbed soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands. Its leaves are lance-shaped with a prominent midrib, and it produces small, three-angled fruits that are dispersed by wind.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
