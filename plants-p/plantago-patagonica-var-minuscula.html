Plantago patagonica var. minuscula

Plantago patagonica var. minuscula, commonly known as the Patagonian plantain, is a flowering plant native to the southern regions of South America.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters. It belongs to the plantain family (Plantaginaceae) and is characterized by its compact growth habit.

This variety is distinguished by its smaller size compared to other varieties of Plantago patagonica.
