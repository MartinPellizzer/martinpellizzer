Phyllocladus alpinus

Phyllocladus alpinus, commonly known as the mountain pine or alpine pine, is a unique evergreen conifer native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive foliage, which consists of scale-like leaves arranged in dense, overlapping clusters. The plant's cones are small and woody, typically appearing in clusters at the ends of the branches. Its bark is thick and fissured, providing protection against harsh alpine conditions.

This species plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, contributing to soil stabilization and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
