Piptochaetium erianthum

Piptochaetium erianthum, commonly known as the silky bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its fine, bluish-green foliage and tufted inflorescences that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in prairies and open woodlands. Its leaves are narrow and covered with short, silky hairs, contributing to its common name.

Piptochaetium erianthum is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in ecological restoration projects.
