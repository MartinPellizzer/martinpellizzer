Potentilla argyrops

Potentilla argyrops, commonly known as silverleaf cinquefoil, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its silvery-white undersides on its leaves, which give it its common name. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its foliage is often used in traditional medicine for its astringent properties.

Potentilla argyrops thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to harsh, alpine environments.
