Pera distichophylla var. martiana

Pera distichophylla var. martiana, commonly known as the Martiana fern, is a rare and delicate plant native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its finely divided, bi-pinnate leaves that give it a lacy appearance. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in cloud forests or along stream banks. Its reproductive structures are small and inconspicuous, typical of many fern species.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered endangered and requires conservation efforts to ensure its survival.
