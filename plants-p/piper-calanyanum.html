Piper calanyanum

Piper calanyanum, commonly known as the Calayan pepper, is a climbing vine native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are followed by elongated, peppery fruits. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped with a rough texture and a strong, pungent aroma.

The plant is often cultivated for its edible fruits, which are used in local cuisine and traditional medicine.
