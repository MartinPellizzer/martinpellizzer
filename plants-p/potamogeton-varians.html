Potamogeton varians

Potamogeton varians, commonly known as variable pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments across Europe, Asia, and North America.

It typically grows in shallow waters, forming dense underwater vegetation that provides habitat for various aquatic organisms. The plant has long, narrow leaves that are often ribbon-like in appearance, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are either male or female. It plays an important role in water ecosystems by stabilizing sediments and supporting fish and invertebrate populations.

However, it can become invasive in some regions, altering native plant communities and affecting water quality.
