Pachydesmia haughtii

Pachydesmia haughtii, commonly known as the Haught's thickleaf plant, is a succulent species native to arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that store water, adapting to its harsh, dry environment. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its flowers are small and often greenish-white, appearing in clusters during the rainy season.

Due to its unique morphology and limited distribution, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
