Pilosella niphostribes subsp. lachnocephala

Pilosella niphostribes subsp.

lachnocephala, commonly known as the woolly cinquefoil, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It is characterized by its dense, white to grayish-white woolly covering on the stems and leaves, which gives it a distinctive appearance.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and rocky areas.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
