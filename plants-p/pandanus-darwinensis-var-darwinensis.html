Pandanus darwinensis var. darwinensis

Pandanus darwinensis var. darwinensis, commonly known as the Darwin's pandanus, is a flowering plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It belongs to the Pandanaceae family and is characterized by its tall, narrow leaves that can grow up to several meters in length. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences with both male and female flowers, typical of many monocot species. Its roots are fibrous and often used in traditional crafts and as a source of fiber.

This variant is distinguished by its robust growth and adaptability to coastal and sandy soils.
