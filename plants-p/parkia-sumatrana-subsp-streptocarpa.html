Parkia sumatrana subsp. streptocarpa

Parkia sumatrana subsp.

streptocarpa, commonly known as the Java bean tree, is a tropical leguminous plant native to Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its compound leaves and distinctive pod fruits that contain edible seeds.

The plant belongs to the Fabaceae family and is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland forests.

Its seeds are used in traditional medicine and as a source of protein in some regions.
