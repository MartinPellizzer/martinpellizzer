Pleudia bariensis

Pleudia bariensis, commonly known as the Bariensis Pleudia, is a rare flowering plant native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, large, waxy leaves that are often a vibrant green with subtle variegation. The plant produces small, clustered white flowers that bloom during the rainy season, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained, nutrient-rich soil.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered endangered and is protected in several conservation reserves.
