Persea decursinervis

Persea decursinervis, commonly known as the Mexican persimmon, is a species of flowering plant in the family Lauraceae.

It is native to Mexico and parts of Central America, where it typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves that are glossy and dark green in color. It produces small, fragrant flowers followed by edible, orange to reddish fruits.

The fruit is often consumed raw or used in culinary applications, and the plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal.
