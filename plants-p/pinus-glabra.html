Pinus glabra

Pinus glabra, commonly known as the smooth pine, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and slender, drooping branches. The needles are short, typically 2 to 4 inches long, and grow in bundles of two. It produces cone-shaped fruits that mature within 12 to 18 months.

This species is often found in moist, well-drained soils in forested areas.
