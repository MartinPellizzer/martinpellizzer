Polygala bosniaca

Polygala bosniaca, commonly known as the Bosnian milkweed, is a flowering plant native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and clusters of small, delicate flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or forested areas and is adapted to temperate climates. Its flowers are usually white or pale yellow, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Polygala bosniaca is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
