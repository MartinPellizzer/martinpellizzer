Polygala rotundifolia

Polygala rotundifolia, commonly known as the round-leaf milkvetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Polygalaceae.

It is native to southern Africa, particularly South Africa, where it thrives in rocky and sandy soils. The plant is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that typically range in color from white to pink, and it produces distinctive, spirally arranged seed pods. It is often found in fynbos vegetation, which is adapted to the region's Mediterranean climate.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
