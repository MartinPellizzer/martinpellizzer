Prunus × koidzumii

Prunus × koidzumii, commonly known as the Japanese cherry or Yoshino cherry, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Prunus serrula and Prunus lusitanica.

It is native to Japan and is renowned for its showy, pink or white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant is a small to medium-sized deciduous tree, typically reaching heights of 8 to 15 meters. Its leaves are ovate with serrated edges and turn a vibrant yellow in autumn.

The fruit is a small, dark purple drupe that contains a single seed.
