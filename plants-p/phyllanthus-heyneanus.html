Phyllanthus heyneanus

Phyllanthus heyneanus, commonly known as the African date tree, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, followed by small, round fruits that resemble dates. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has some traditional medicinal uses.

The species is also valued for its ability to thrive in dry conditions, making it adaptable to various environments.
