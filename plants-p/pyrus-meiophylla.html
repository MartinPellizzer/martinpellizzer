Pyrus meiophylla

Pyrus meiophylla, commonly known as the Chinese white pear, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its pale green, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers in early spring, followed by white to yellowish fruit that ripens in autumn. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for cultivation in temperate climates.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional Chinese horticulture.
