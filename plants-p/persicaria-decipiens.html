Persicaria decipiens

Persicaria decipiens, commonly known as the false smartweed, is a herbaceous annual plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, which includes plants known for their distinctive bracts and often edible seeds. The plant typically grows in moist to wet environments such as marshes, ditches, and along stream banks. It features simple, alternate leaves with a smooth or slightly toothed margin, and its flowers are small, clustered in dense, spherical heads.

The seeds are enclosed in a fleshy, three-lobed pericarp, which can be dispersed by water or animals.
