Ponthieva lilacina

Ponthieva lilacina, commonly known as the lilac orchid, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, fragrant flowers that range in color from pale pink to deep purple. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its leaves are long and narrow, often with a waxy texture, and it produces small, clustered inflorescences.

Ponthieva lilacina is valued in horticulture for its ornamental beauty and unique floral structure.
