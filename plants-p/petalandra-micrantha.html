Petalandra micrantha

Petalandra micrantha, commonly known as the small-flowered magnolia, is a deciduous tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and heart-shaped leaves that turn yellow in the fall. The plant produces fragrant, white flowers with numerous stamens, typically blooming in early spring before the leaves emerge. Its fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release seeds.

Petalandra micrantha is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens and parks.
