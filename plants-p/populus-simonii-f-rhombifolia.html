Populus simonii f. rhombifolia

Populus simonii f. rhombifolia, commonly known as the rhombifoliate poplar, is a unique variant of the poplar tree characterized by its distinctive rhombic-shaped leaves.

This ornamental plant is native to China and is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal in gardens and landscapes. The leaves are typically larger and more elongated compared to the standard Populus simonii, giving it a striking visual presence. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers in the spring, followed by samaras that aid in seed dispersal.

The plant prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a versatile choice for various horticultural applications.
