Phellodendron amurense var. molle

Phellodendron amurense var. molle, commonly known as the Amur mahogany or soft phellodendron, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and palmately compound leaves with five to seven leaflets. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers followed by small, woody fruits containing a single seed. It is valued for its hard, durable wood and has been used in traditional Chinese medicine.

The species is also noted for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its adaptability to different climatic conditions.
