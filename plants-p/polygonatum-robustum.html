Polygonatum robustum

Polygonatum robustum, commonly known as the yellow-flowered Solomon's seal, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to East Asia.

It features upright, slender stems that bear whorled leaves and clusters of yellow-green flowers. The plant produces small, red berries that ripen in late summer. It thrives in shaded, moist woodland environments and is often found in temperate regions.

Polygonatum robustum is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
