Psilocaulon corallinum

Psilocaulon corallinum, commonly known as the coral-like lichen, is a unique composite organism formed by a symbiotic relationship between a fungus and a green alga.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically on decaying wood or stone. The plant exhibits a branched, coral-like structure that gives it its common name. Its color ranges from yellowish to reddish, depending on environmental conditions.

Psilocaulon corallinum plays an important ecological role in nutrient cycling and soil stabilization in its habitat.
