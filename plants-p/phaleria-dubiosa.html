Phaleria dubiosa

Phaleria dubiosa, commonly known as the white-veined evergreen, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Lauraceae family, which includes laurel trees and cinnamon plants. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves with prominent white veins. It produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow.

Phaleria dubiosa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
