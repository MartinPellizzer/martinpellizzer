Pandanus tectorius var. javanicus

Pandanus tectorius var. javanicus, commonly known as the pandanus palm or hibiscus tree, is a species native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a tall, slender palm with long, sword-shaped leaves that are often used in traditional crafts and thatching. The plant produces small, clustered flowers followed by fibrous, coconut-like fruits. Its roots and leaves are used in various cultural and medicinal practices.

It thrives in coastal and wetland areas, contributing to the ecological diversity of its habitat.
