Pastinaca mantegazziana

Pastinaca mantegazziana, commonly known as the Italian burdock, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features a thick, reddish-brown root that is often used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are large, heart-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are small and yellowish, arranged in clusters.

Pastinaca mantegazziana thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in woodland edges and along roadsides.
