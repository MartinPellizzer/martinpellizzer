Piper puerense

Piper puerense, commonly known as the Puer tea plant, is a climbing vine native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper plants. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by berries that are used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are often used to make a type of tea, which is believed to have stimulant properties.

The botanical characteristics of Piper puerense include its aromatic foliage and unique fruit structure.
