Picris squarrosa

Picris squarrosa, commonly known as the squarrose wormwood, is a herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its upright, branched stems and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has serrated, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in dry, sunny environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.

Picris squarrosa is known for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
