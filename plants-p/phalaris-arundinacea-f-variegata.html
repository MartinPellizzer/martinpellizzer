Phalaris arundinacea f. variegata

Phalaris arundinacea f. variegata, commonly known as the variegated reed grass, is a decorative grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and striking variegated leaves that display shades of green, white, and yellow. This ornamental grass thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers moist, well-drained soil. It is often used in landscaping for its aesthetic appeal and ability to provide texture in garden settings.

The plant is relatively low maintenance and can be grown in USDA hardiness zones 4 through 8.
