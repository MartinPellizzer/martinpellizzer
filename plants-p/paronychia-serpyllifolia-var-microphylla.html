Paronychia serpyllifolia var. microphylla

Paronychia serpyllifolia var. microphylla, commonly known as mouse ear chickweed, is a low-growing perennial plant characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit.

It features small, spoon-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs, giving the plant a fuzzy appearance. The flowers are small and white, arranged in clusters, and bloom in early spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in lawns, gardens, and rocky areas.

This variety is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family and is known for its hardy nature and ability to spread easily.
