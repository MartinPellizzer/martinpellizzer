Potentilla glandulosa f. incisa

Potentilla glandulosa f. incisa, commonly known as the narrow-leaved cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes. Its leaves are finely divided and have a distinctive serrated edge, contributing to its ornamental value.
