Poa aksuensis

Poa aksuensis, commonly known as the Aksu grass, is a species of flowering plant in the grass family Poaceae.

It is native to Central Asia, particularly found in regions of Xinjiang, China. This perennial herb typically grows in dry, mountainous areas and is adapted to cold and arid conditions. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat and is of interest for its ecological and potential agricultural significance.
