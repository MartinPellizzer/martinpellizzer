Paracaryum nigrum

Paracaryum nigrum, commonly known as black pepper.

It is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia. The plant produces small, dark red berries that are harvested and dried to create the spice known as black pepper. The fruit contains a single seed, which is used in both culinary and medicinal applications.

Its botanical characteristics include compound leaves and a climbing habit, adapting to its natural forest environment.
