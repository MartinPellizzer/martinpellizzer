Phlox setacea var. amoena

Phlox setacea var. amoena, commonly known as the Amoena phlox, is a perennial flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of showy, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to red. The plant features narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained, slightly acidic soil.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
