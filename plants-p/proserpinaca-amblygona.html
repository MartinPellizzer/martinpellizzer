Proserpinaca amblygona

Proserpinaca amblygona, commonly known as the narrowleaf watercress, is a flowering plant native to wetland environments in southern Africa.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other cruciferous species. The plant typically grows in shallow water or damp soil, characterized by its slender, elongated leaves and small white or yellow flowers. Its seeds are often dispersed by water, aiding in its spread across aquatic habitats.
