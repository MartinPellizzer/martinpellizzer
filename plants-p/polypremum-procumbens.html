Polypremum procumbens

Polypremum procumbens, commonly known as the creeping polypody, is a fern native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its prostrate growth habit, with rhizomes that spread along the ground. The plant features small, lanceolate leaves that are typically less than 10 cm in length. Its reproductive structures include sporangia that produce spores for propagation.

Polypremum procumbens thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories.
