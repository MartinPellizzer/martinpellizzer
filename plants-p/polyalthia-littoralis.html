Polyalthia littoralis

Polyalthia littoralis, commonly known as the coastal flame tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, fiery orange-red flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as birds and insects. The plant thrives in coastal areas and is often found in mangrove ecosystems, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and preventing erosion. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and elliptical, with a smooth surface and a glossy texture.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens and urban landscapes for its vibrant floral display.
