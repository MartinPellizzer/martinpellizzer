Pyrus cydonia var. major

Pyrus cydonia var. major, commonly known as the white mulberry, is a deciduous tree native to Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in early spring. The tree produces edible berries that are typically pink to red in color when mature. It is often cultivated for its fruit, which is used in traditional medicine and as a food source.

The plant is also valued for its role in silviculture, providing food for silkworms.
