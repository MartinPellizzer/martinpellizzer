Persea tabacifolia

Persea tabacifolia, commonly known as the cigar tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and fragrant, white flowers. The plant produces edible berries that are often used in traditional medicine. Its bark and leaves contain compounds with potential medicinal properties.

The cigar tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance in its native habitat.
