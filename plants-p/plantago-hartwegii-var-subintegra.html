Plantago hartwegii var. subintegra

Plantago hartwegii var. subintegra, commonly known as the smoothleaf plantain, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It features broad, lance-shaped leaves that are smooth and slightly hairy on the underside. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in dense clusters during the summer months. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their anti-inflammatory properties.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is commonly found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.
