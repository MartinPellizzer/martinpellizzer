Phyteuma spicatum var. caerulescens

Phyteuma spicatum var. caerulescens, commonly known as blue phyteuma, is a herbaceous perennial native to southern Europe.

It features tall, slender stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue to purple. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically hairy and arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and wildflower displays.
