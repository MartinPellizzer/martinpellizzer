Potamogeton crispus var. gemmifer

Potamogeton crispus var. gemmifer, commonly known as the gemmiferous water plantain, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of tiny, greenish-white flowers that are typically borne on short, lateral branches. It plays an important ecological role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various invertebrates and fish.

Due to its ability to spread through vegetative reproduction, it can form dense stands in still or slow-moving waters.
