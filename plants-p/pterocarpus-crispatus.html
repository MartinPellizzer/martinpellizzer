Pterocarpus crispatus

Pterocarpus crispatus, commonly known as the African rosewood, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The tree produces hard, dark wood that is highly valued for its durability and aesthetic qualities. Its seeds are encased in flat, leathery pods, and the plant has a symbiotic relationship with nitrogen-fixing bacteria.

Pterocarpus crispatus is also known for its medicinal properties, with various parts of the plant used in traditional medicine.
