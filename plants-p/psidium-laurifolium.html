Psidium laurifolium

Psidium laurifolium, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or laurifolium guava, is a small evergreen tree native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, bright red berries. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit.

The leaves are also used in traditional medicine for their potential health benefits.
