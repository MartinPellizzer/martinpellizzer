Pulsatilla albana var. violacea

Pulsatilla albana var. violacea, commonly known as the violet anemone, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its showy, violet-colored flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant features a short, upright stem with palmate leaves that are deeply lobed and covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are typically five-petaled and have a distinctive, fluffy center, resembling a small anemone.

Pulsatilla albana var. violacea is adapted to rocky and well-drained soils, often growing in alpine or subalpine environments.
