Piper laetispicum

Piper laetispicum, commonly known as the "happy pepper," is a tropical climbing vine native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many pepper species. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are followed by elliptical, reddish berries. Its leaves are heart-shaped with a slightly serrated margin and emit a mild peppery scent.

Piper laetispicum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
