Potentilla clevelandii

Potentilla clevelandii, commonly known as Cleveland's cinquefoil, is a perennial shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its small, yellow, five-petaled flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant has dark green, serrated leaves and a compact growth habit. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Botanically, it belongs to the Rosaceae family and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
