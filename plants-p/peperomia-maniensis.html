Peperomia maniensis

Peperomia maniensis, commonly known as the Manisens peperomia, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, particularly Indonesia. This plant is characterized by its compact, rosette-like growth habit and thick, waxy leaves that help it retain moisture. The leaves are typically oval or elliptic in shape, with a glossy surface and a variety of colors ranging from green to reddish tones.

Peperomia maniensis is often cultivated as a decorative indoor plant due to its attractive appearance and low maintenance requirements.
