Poliomintha longiflora var. longiflora

Poliomintha longiflora var. longiflora, commonly known as the long-flowered poliomintha, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant features square, hairy stems and opposite leaves that are typically gray-green in color. Its showy, tubular flowers are usually violet or purple, and they bloom in late spring to early summer.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.
