Penstemon pachyphyllus var. congestus

Penstemon pachyphyllus var. congestus, commonly known as the dense-leaved beardtongue, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to purple. The plant features thick, waxy leaves that help it retain moisture in arid environments. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its botanical features make it well adapted to drought and high-altitude conditions.
