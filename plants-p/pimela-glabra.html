Pimela glabra

Pimela glabra, commonly known as the smooth pimela, is a shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, glabrous stems and oval-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, clustered yellow flowers that are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and woodland edges.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes used in horticulture for its ornamental value.
