Persica vulgaris var. tristis

Persica vulgaris var. tristis, commonly known as the wild peach or bitter peach, is a shrub or small tree native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and white or pink flowers. The fruit is typically small, with a thick, bitter skin and a yellow or orange flesh. It is often considered a wild relative of the domesticated peach and is used in horticultural breeding programs.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits.
