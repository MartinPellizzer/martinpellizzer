Panax bernieri

Panax bernieri, commonly known as the Chinese ginseng or the red ginseng, is a perennial herb native to China and Korea.

It belongs to the Araliaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, tuberous roots that are highly valued in traditional medicine. The plant typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring pinnately compound leaves and small greenish-white flowers. Its roots contain various bioactive compounds, including ginsenosides, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

The plant is often cultivated for its roots, which are harvested after several years of growth.
