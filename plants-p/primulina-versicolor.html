Primulina versicolor

Primulina versicolor, commonly known as the variegated primula, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Gesneriaceae family and is characterized by its strikingly variegated foliage with shades of green, white, and yellow. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink, red, and white, often in clusters. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value, it is popular in both indoor and greenhouse cultivation.
