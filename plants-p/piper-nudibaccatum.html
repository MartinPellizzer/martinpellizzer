Piper nudibaccatum

Piper nudibaccatum, commonly known as the climbing pepper or wild pepper, is a tropical vine native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Piper genus within the Piperaceae family, which includes many species of pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its long, trailing stems and small, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are unisexual, with separate male and female flowers on the same plant, and it produces small, berry-like fruits that are typically green when mature.

The plant is often cultivated for its aromatic leaves and potential medicinal properties.
