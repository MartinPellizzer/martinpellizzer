Petunia axillaris subsp. parodii

Petunia axillaris subsp.

parodii, commonly known as the Parodii petunia, is a flowering plant native to South America. It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and produces clusters of vibrant, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of purple and blue.

The plant has hairy stems and leaves that are typically opposite and oval in shape. It thrives in warm, sunny conditions and is often cultivated as a decorative plant in gardens and containers.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental horticulture.
