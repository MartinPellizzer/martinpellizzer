Phyllanthus echinospermus

Phyllanthus echinospermus, commonly known as the spiny-fruit phyllanthus, is a shrub native to tropical Africa and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves with a rough, sometimes spiny texture. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, followed by unique, spiny, spherical fruits. It is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories or along stream banks.

The species is notable for its medicinal uses in traditional herbal remedies.
