Panicum densiflorum

Panicum densiflorum, commonly known as denseflower panicgrass, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, spike-like inflorescence that bears numerous small, spikelet-like flowers. The plant typically grows in full sun and thrives in moist, well-drained soils, often found in wetlands or along stream banks. Its foliage is narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue that adds ornamental value to landscapes.

Due to its adaptability and aesthetic appeal, it is sometimes used in native plant gardens and ecological restoration projects.
