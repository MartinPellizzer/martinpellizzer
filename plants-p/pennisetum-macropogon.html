Pennisetum macropogon

Pennisetum macropogon, commonly known as bloodgrass, is a perennial grass native to Africa.

It features tall, slender stems with striking red-tipped leaves that create a dramatic visual effect. The plant produces feathery, purple-pink plumes that add ornamental value to gardens. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for landscaping.

Its botanical characteristics include narrow, lance-shaped leaves and a clumping growth habit.
