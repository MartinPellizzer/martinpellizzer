Populus × hybrida

Populus × hybrida, commonly known as the hybrid poplar, is a deciduous tree that belongs to the Salicaceae family.

It is characterized by its rapid growth rate and distinctive heart-shaped leaves that turn yellow in the fall. The tree produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish or yellowish in color. Its bark is smooth and light gray, becoming fissured with age.

Populus × hybrida is widely cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in reforestation and phytoremediation projects.
