Potamogeton × nitens f. merloensis

Potamogeton × nitens f. merloensis, commonly known as the Merlo's pondweed, is a hybrid plant species belonging to the family Potamogetonaceae.

It is characterized by its submerged aquatic growth habit and elongated, narrow leaves that are typically arranged in whorls around the stem. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often clustered in spike-like inflorescences. It thrives in freshwater environments such as lakes, ponds, and slow-moving streams.

Due to its hybrid nature, it exhibits a range of morphological variations, making it a subject of interest in botanical studies.
