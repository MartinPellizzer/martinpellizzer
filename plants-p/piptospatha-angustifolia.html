Piptospatha angustifolia

Piptospatha angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf piptospatha, is a member of the Fabaceae family and is native to certain regions of South America.

It is a shrub characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pale yellow to greenish in color. Its fruits are legumes that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Piptospatha angustifolia is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid conditions.
