Passiflora parahybensis

Passiflora parahybensis, commonly known as the Brazilian passionflower, is a climbing vine native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that feature a complex arrangement of petals, sepals, and a distinctive crown of filaments. The plant produces edible berries that are often consumed by both humans and wildlife. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a velvety texture, while its stems are woody and twining.

Passiflora parahybensis plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, supporting various pollinators and serving as a food source for animals.
