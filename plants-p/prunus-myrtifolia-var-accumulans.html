Prunus myrtifolia var. accumulans

Prunus myrtifolia var. accumulans, commonly known as the Mexican white ash, is a deciduous tree native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and compound leaves that are alternate and pinnately arranged. The tree produces small, white to pale yellow flowers in early spring, followed by small, round, green fruits that mature to a dark purple color. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable plant for arid and semi-arid climates.

Its ornamental value comes from its attractive foliage and adaptability to various environmental conditions.
