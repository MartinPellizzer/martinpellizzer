Pontia pallens

Pontia pallens, commonly known as the pale winter cabbage.

It is a biennial plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its rosette of silvery-gray, hairy leaves. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers in a raceme during the spring. Its leaves contain glucosinolates, which are defensive compounds common in the mustard family.

It is often found in disturbed soils and can be a weed in agricultural settings.
