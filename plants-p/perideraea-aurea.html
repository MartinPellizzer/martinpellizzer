Perideraea aurea

Perideraea aurea, commonly known as the golden star fern, is a unique and striking plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, feather-like fronds that exhibit a vibrant golden hue, especially under sunlight. The plant's botanical structure consists of a creeping rhizome with clustered, small, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.

Perideraea aurea is prized for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated as a decorative plant in greenhouses and shaded gardens.
