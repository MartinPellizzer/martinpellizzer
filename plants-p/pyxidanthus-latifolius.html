Pyxidanthus latifolius

Pyxidanthus latifolius, commonly known as the broadleaf pyxidanthus, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves and showy, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in shaded or semi-shaded environments. Its botanical features include a woody stem and small, capsule-like fruits that contain seeds.

It is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive foliage and blooms.
