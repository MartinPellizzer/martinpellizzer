Polygonum sagittatum f. viridialbum

Polygonum sagittatum f. viridialbum, commonly known as the yellow arrowhead, is a variant of the arrowhead plant characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers and heart-shaped leaves.

It is a herbaceous perennial native to North America, particularly found in wetland and marsh environments. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, with slender stems that often root at the nodes. Its leaves are alternate, arrowhead-shaped, and usually have a glossy green surface.

The inflorescence consists of small, clustered flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.
