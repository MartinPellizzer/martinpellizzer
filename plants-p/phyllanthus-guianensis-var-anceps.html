Phyllanthus guianensis var. anceps

Phyllanthus guianensis var. anceps, commonly known as the Guianese phyllanthus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Phyllanthaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil and Guyana. The plant is characterized by its small, clustered leaves and yellowish-white flowers that bloom in axillary clusters. It typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters.

The leaves are usually elliptical or ovate, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface, and the plant is often found in moist, shaded environments.
