Pygeum phaeostictum

Pygeum phaeostictum, commonly known as the yellow or golden acacia, is a flowering plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is native to parts of Africa, particularly South Africa, where it thrives in arid and semi-arid regions. The plant is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters. Its leaves are bipinnate, consisting of numerous small leaflets, and it produces pods that contain seeds.

The plant is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits, though its botanical features are more notable for their structural adaptations to harsh environments.
