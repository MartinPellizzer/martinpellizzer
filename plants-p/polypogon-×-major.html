Polypogon × major

Polypogon × major, commonly known as the tall flat sedge, is a hybrid grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical inflorescence and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats such as marshes and swampy areas. It has a fibrous root system and is often found in association with other wetland vegetation.

Due to its adaptability, it plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
