Piper leptophyllum

Piper leptophyllum, commonly known as the slender-leaved pepper plant, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Central and South America, where it typically grows in humid, shaded environments. The plant is characterized by its small, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of tiny, fragrant flowers. Its fruit, a small, elongated berry, contains several seeds and is used in traditional medicine.

Piper leptophyllum is valued for its aromatic properties and has been used in herbal remedies for various health purposes.
