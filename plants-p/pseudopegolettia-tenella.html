Pseudopegolettia tenella

Pseudopegolettia tenella, commonly known as the Mexican rock cactus, is a member of the Cactaceae family and is native to Mexico.

It is a small, leafless plant that typically grows on rocky surfaces, showcasing adaptations to arid environments. The plant features a short, cylindrical stem covered in areoles, from which spines and flowers emerge. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange, blooming in the spring, and are relatively short-lived.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
