Pterochrosia vexillaria

Pterochrosia vexillaria, commonly known as the flag-leaf orchid, is a small, terrestrial orchid native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats. The plant features a slender, branching stem with narrow, grass-like leaves. Its most distinctive characteristic is its small, delicate flowers that are usually pale green or white with a yellowish tint.

Pterochrosia vexillaria is known for its unique floral structure and is appreciated for its ornamental value in botanical gardens.
