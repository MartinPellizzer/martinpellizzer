Pentagonia woronovii

Pentagonia woronovii, commonly known as the Brazilian bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Atlantic rainforests of Brazil.

It is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to violet. The plant typically grows as an erect shrub, reaching heights of up to 2 meters, with opposite, ovate leaves that are often hairy. Its flowers bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Pentagonia woronovii is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique appearance.
