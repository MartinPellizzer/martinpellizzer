Passiflora jaenensis

Passiflora jaenensis, commonly known as the Jamaican passionflower, is a climbing vine native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful flowers that display a complex arrangement of petals, sepals, and modified leaves. The plant produces edible, tropical berries that are rich in antioxidants and have a mild, sweet flavor. Its rapid growth habit makes it suitable for ornamental use in gardens and greenhouses.

Passiflora jaenensis is also valued for its ecological role, providing nectar for pollinators and habitat for various wildlife species.
