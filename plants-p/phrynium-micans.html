Phrynium micans

Phrynium micans, commonly known as the shining arrowroot, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that features large, heart-shaped leaves with a smooth, waxy surface. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers that are typically borne on tall, leafless stalks. Its rhizomes are used in traditional medicine and as a starch source.

The plant thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in humid tropical forests.
