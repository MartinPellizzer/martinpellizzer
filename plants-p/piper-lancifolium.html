Piper lancifolium

Piper lancifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaved pepper plant, is a tropical vine native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its elongated, lance-shaped leaves that are often glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by black, peppercorn-like berries. It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species.

Its botanical features make it a valuable plant in both traditional medicine and horticulture.
