Prunus serotina var. eximia

Prunus serotina var. eximia, commonly known as the black cherry, is a deciduous tree native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its smooth, dark bark and alternate, simple leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape. The tree produces small, yellow-green flowers in early spring, which are followed by edible, dark red to black berries. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained, loamy soils and full sun.

This species is often found in forests, along stream banks, and in open woodlands.
