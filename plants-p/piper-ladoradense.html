Piper ladoradense

Piper ladoradense, commonly known as the Ladorado pepper or Ladorado long pepper, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil and Peru. The plant is characterized by its climbing habit, with dark green, opposite leaves and small, clustered flowers. Its fruits are elongated, red berries that contain aromatic seeds used in traditional medicine.

The plant's botanical features make it a unique subject of study in both horticulture and ethnobotany.
