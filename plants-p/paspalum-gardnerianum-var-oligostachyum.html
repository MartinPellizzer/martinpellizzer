Paspalum gardnerianum var. oligostachyum

Paspalum gardnerianum var. oligostachyum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved paspalum, is a perennial grass native to South America.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and compact growth habit. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, inconspicuous flowers. It thrives in wetland environments and is often found in marshy areas or along the edges of water bodies.

Its rhizomatous root system allows it to spread vegetatively and stabilize soil in moist habitats.
