Pulsatilla montana var. serbica

Pulsatilla montana var. serbica, commonly known as the Serbian pasqueflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its showy, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant has palmate leaves with three to five lobes, and it produces a distinctive, hairy seed head after flowering. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and protected areas.
