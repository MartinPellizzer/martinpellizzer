Prunus × lannesiana f. koshioyama

Prunus × lannesiana f. koshioyama, commonly known as the Koshioyama cherry, is a unique botanical variant of the Prunus genus.

It is a hybrid species, resulting from the cross between Prunus lannesiana and Prunus × yedoensis. This plant is characterized by its striking, double-flowered blossoms that appear in early spring. The leaves are typically dark green and oval-shaped, providing a lush backdrop to the ornamental flowers.

It is primarily cultivated as an ornamental plant in gardens and parks due to its aesthetic appeal.
