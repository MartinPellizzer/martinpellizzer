Piper arthantopse

Piper arthantopse, commonly known as the narrow-leaf pepper, is a tropical climbing vine belonging to the Piperaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, aerial stems and opposite, ovate leaves with a glossy surface. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that develop into three-winged berries. Native to Southeast Asia, it thrives in humid, shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens.
