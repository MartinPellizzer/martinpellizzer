Pseudoscabiosa grosii

Pseudoscabiosa grosii, commonly known as the alpine scabious, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun. The plant features hairy stems and basal leaves that are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are blue to violet in color, arranged in dense, spherical clusters, and bloom from late spring to early summer.

Pseudoscabiosa grosii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
