Pisonia triandra

Pisonia triandra, commonly known as the flame tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a large, deciduous tree that can grow up to 30 meters tall, characterized by its striking red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters. The leaves are simple, alternate, and have a smooth surface with a glossy appearance. Its fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release small, hairy seeds.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant floral display.
