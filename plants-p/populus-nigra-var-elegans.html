Populus nigra var. elegans

Populus nigra var. elegans, commonly known as the Weeping Willow, is a deciduous tree native to Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its long, drooping branches that create a cascading effect, giving it a distinctive and ornamental appearance. The tree has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are bright green in summer and turn yellow in autumn. It produces small, fluffy seeds that are dispersed by the wind, aiding in its propagation.

This species is often planted in gardens and along waterways for its aesthetic value and ability to thrive in moist soils.
