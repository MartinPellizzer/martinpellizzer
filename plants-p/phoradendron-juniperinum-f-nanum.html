Phoradendron juniperinum f. nanum

Phoradendron juniperinum f. nanum, commonly known as the dwarf mistletoe, is a parasitic plant that grows on various species of oak trees.

It produces small, white, fleshy berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. The plant has a unique life cycle, relying on the seeds dispersed by birds to infect new host trees. Its leaves are typically small and scale-like, and it lacks true roots, instead anchoring itself to the host tree with haustoria.

The plant is native to parts of the southern United States and is an example of a hemiparasite that partially depends on its host for survival.
