Phylica tuberculata

Phylica tuberculata, commonly known as the Natal flame bush, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its bright red or orange flowers that bloom in winter, attracting pollinators such as birds and insects. The plant has small, leathery leaves and is often used in gardens for its ornamental value. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for coastal and dry areas.

The genus Phylica is known for its unique floral structures and ecological importance in its native habitats.
