Pueraria lobata var. thomsonii

Pueraria lobata var. thomsonii, commonly known as the Chinese kudzu, is a vigorous climbing vine native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically trifoliate or pinnately lobed, and its distinctive purple flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to form dense thickets, making it both ecologically significant and potentially invasive. Its roots produce a starch-like substance that has been used in traditional medicine and as a food source.

The plant thrives in moist, fertile soils and is often found in woodland areas or along streambanks.
