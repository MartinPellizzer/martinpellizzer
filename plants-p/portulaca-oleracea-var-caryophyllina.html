Portulaca oleracea var. caryophyllina

Portulaca oleracea var. caryophyllina, commonly known as the moss rose or Indian spinach, is a flowering plant native to Africa, Asia, and Australia.

It is a succulent species with fleshy, paddle-shaped leaves and vibrant, showy flowers that come in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for rock gardens and containers. It produces small, edible seeds that are used in some traditional cuisines.

The plant's ability to store water in its leaves allows it to survive in arid environments.
