Piper amalago

Piper amalago, commonly known as the wild pepper or Malagao pepper, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species like black pepper. The plant produces small, green berries that turn red when ripe, containing aromatic seeds used in traditional medicine and cuisine. Its leaves are often used in herbal remedies due to their medicinal properties.

The plant thrives in tropical climates and is valued for both its culinary and therapeutic applications.
