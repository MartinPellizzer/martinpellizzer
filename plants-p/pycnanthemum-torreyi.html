Pycnanthemum torreyi

Pycnanthemum torreyi, commonly known as sweet peppergrass, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its aromatic, yellow-green flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, rich soils in woodland or wetland habitats. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a distinctive, slightly hairy texture.

Sweet peppergrass is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional herbal remedies.
