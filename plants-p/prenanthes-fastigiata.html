Prenanthes fastigiata

Prenanthes fastigiata, commonly known as the tall water shield, is a herbaceous plant native to wetland habitats in North America.

It features narrow, elongated leaves that are often submerged or floating, adapted to aquatic environments. The plant produces small, white flowers arranged in a spike, which bloom from July to September. Its roots are fibrous and help stabilize the soil in shallow water areas.

Prenanthes fastigiata plays a role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
