Platylepis geluana

Platylepis geluana, commonly known as the Arctic willow, is a low-growing shrub native to the Arctic and subarctic regions.

It is characterized by its small, leathery leaves and clusters of yellowish-green flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant is adapted to cold climates and can survive in permafrost conditions. It plays a vital role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for Arctic wildlife.

Its botanical features make it a unique example of plant adaptation to extreme environments.
