Prunus mutabilis f. magnifica

Prunus mutabilis f. magnifica, commonly known as the Double-flowered Cherry, is a striking ornamental plant celebrated for its unique floral characteristics.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is native to Japan, where it has been cultivated for centuries. The plant is noted for its profuse display of double flowers, which consist of multiple layers of petals, giving it a lush, full appearance. It typically grows as a small to medium-sized tree, reaching heights of up to 10 meters, with dense, dark green foliage.

This variety is often planted in gardens for its ornamental value and is prized for its early spring blooms that add vibrant color to the landscape.
