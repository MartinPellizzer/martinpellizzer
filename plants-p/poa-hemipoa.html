Poa hemipoa

Poa hemipoa, commonly known as the New Zealand hairgrass, is a perennial grass species native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and produces delicate, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its leaves are narrow and hair-like, contributing to its common name.

Poa hemipoa plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
