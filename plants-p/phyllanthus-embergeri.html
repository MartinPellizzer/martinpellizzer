Phyllanthus embergeri

Phyllanthus embergeri, commonly known as the African spurge, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is a small shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant features simple, opposite leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. Its flowers are small and yellow, arranged in dense clusters, and it produces small, round fruits.

The plant is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
