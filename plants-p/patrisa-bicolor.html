Patrisa bicolor

Patrisa bicolor, commonly known as the two-colored bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking bi-colored petals, typically featuring a blue or purple upper part and a white or yellow lower part. The plant produces bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Patrisa bicolor is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ease of cultivation.
