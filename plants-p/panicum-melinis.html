Panicum melinis

Panicum melinis, commonly known as millet grass, is a flowering plant in the grass family Poaceae.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in dry, disturbed soils and is often found in agricultural fields or along roadsides. It produces small, wind-pollinated flowers that develop into three-angled, hairy seeds.

Panicum melinis is an annual plant that is adaptable to various environmental conditions and is sometimes considered a weed in cultivated areas.
