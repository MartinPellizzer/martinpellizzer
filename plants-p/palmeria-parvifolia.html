Palmeria parvifolia

Palmeria parvifolia, commonly known as the small-leaved palmeria, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its small, rounded leaves and clusters of pale pink or white flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or coastal environments, showing a degree of salt tolerance. It is a perennial herb that produces small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds.

Its compact size and ornamental flowers make it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.
