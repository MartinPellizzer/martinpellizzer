Primula auricula var. balbisii

Primula auricula var. balbisii, commonly known as the Balbis's auricula, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive heart-shaped leaves and showy, bell-like flowers that range in color from pink to white. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.

The variety is closely related to other members of the Primula genus, sharing similar botanical features.
