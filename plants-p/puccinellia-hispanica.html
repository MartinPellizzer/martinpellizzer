Puccinellia hispanica

Puccinellia hispanica, commonly known as Spanish needlegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows in dry, sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in coastal areas and inland dunes. The plant has slender, erect stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets that are arranged in dense, cylindrical clusters, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Puccinellia hispanica is adapted to harsh environmental conditions and plays a role in stabilizing coastal and sandy soils.
