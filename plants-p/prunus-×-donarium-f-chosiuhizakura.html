Prunus × donarium f. chosiuhizakura

Prunus × donarium f. chosiuhizakura, commonly known as the Choshiuhizakura cherry blossom, is a cultivar of the Prunus genus, characterized by its ornamental value.

It is a hybrid species, resulting from the cross between Prunus lusitanica and Prunus pendula, and is prized for its delicate pink flowers. The plant is typically a small to medium-sized tree, with a compact growth habit and a profuse bloom in early spring. Its flowers are known for their subtle fragrance and are often used in ornamental horticulture.

The leaves are dark green and provide a striking contrast to the blossoms, making it a popular choice for gardens and public spaces.
