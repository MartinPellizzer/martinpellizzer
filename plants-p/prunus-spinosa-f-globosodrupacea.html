Prunus spinosa f. globosodrupacea

Prunus spinosa f. globosodrupacea, commonly known as the globular thorned cherry, is a compact, shrub-like form of the common blackthorn.

It is characterized by its dense, spherical growth habit and small, dark purple berries that appear in late summer. The plant features thorny stems and small, oval leaves with a leathery texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in hedgerows or woodland edges.

This form is prized for its ornamental value and its ability to provide habitat for wildlife.
