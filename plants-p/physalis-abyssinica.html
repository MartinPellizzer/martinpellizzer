Physalis abyssinica

Physalis abyssinica, commonly known as the Abyssinian groundcherries, is a flowering plant native to Ethiopia.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its hairy stems and yellow-orange flowers. The plant produces small, orange-red berries enclosed in a papery calyx, which are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid conditions.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and pharmacological studies.
