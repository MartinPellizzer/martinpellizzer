Plectranthus vettiveroides

Plectranthus vettiveroides, commonly known as the honey plant or sweet senna, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces small, fragrant yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its root system is fibrous, and it prefers well-drained soil in full sun to partial shade.

The plant is often used in traditional medicine and as an ornamental due to its aesthetic appeal and aromatic properties.
