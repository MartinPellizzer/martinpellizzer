Polygonum strigosum

Polygonum strigosum, commonly known as the hairy knotweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, which is characterized by its distinctive nodes and often triangular stems. The plant features small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, and its leaves are typically covered in fine, hairy texture. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Polygonum strigosum is known for its ability to spread vegetatively through rhizomes, making it a persistent weed in some ecosystems.
