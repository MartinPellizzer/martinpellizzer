Pappea

Pappea, commonly known as the African glorybower, is a genus of flowering plants native to southern Africa.

It consists of several species, most notably Pappea aurantiaca, which is known for its striking red and orange flowers. The plant is characterized by its woody stems and broad, leathery leaves. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky or sandy soils.

Pappea is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
