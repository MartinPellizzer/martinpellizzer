Pandanus tritosphaericus

Pandanus tritosphaericus, commonly known as the narrow-leaf pandanus, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and long, narrow leaves that are often used in traditional crafts. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into three-lobed, fibrous fruits. Its roots and leaves are sometimes utilized in local medicine and as a source of fiber.

The species is well adapted to coastal and wetland environments, thriving in humid, saline conditions.
