Paeonia × suffruticosa f. banksii

Paeonia × suffruticosa f. banksii, commonly known as the Banks' peony, is a hybrid shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its dense, bushy growth habit and its ability to produce large, showy flowers in shades of pink, red, and white. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, with petals that are often ruffled or notched. Its foliage consists of dark green, compound leaves that are arranged alternately along the stems.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is commonly cultivated in gardens for its visual appeal.
