Phaius rumphii

Phaius rumphii, commonly known as the white orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and large, showy white flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant has broad, strap-like leaves and produces a single spike of flowers that can reach up to 30 centimeters in length. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil.

Phaius rumphii is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and tropical landscapes.
