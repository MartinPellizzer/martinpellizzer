Piper schenckii

Piper schenckii, commonly known as the Schenck's pepper or the Amazonian pepper, is a flowering plant native to the rainforests of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species such as black pepper. The plant is characterized by its climbing habit, with slender stems and heart-shaped leaves. It produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into small, round berries.

The fruit contains a single seed and is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
