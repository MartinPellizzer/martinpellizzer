Persea curvifolia

Persea curvifolia, commonly known as the Mexican persimmon, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central America and parts of Mexico.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant flowers. The tree produces small, orange to reddish fruits that are edible when fully ripe. Its botanical features include a smooth, grayish bark and a tendency to grow in dense, humid forests.

Persea curvifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and its fruit, which is used in both culinary and traditional medicinal applications.
