Pyrola forrestiana

Pyrola forrestiana, commonly known as the Forrester's wintergreen, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, commonly known as the heath or heather family. The plant features showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or white, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are evergreen, leathery, and arranged in a rosette pattern, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Pyrola forrestiana thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in shaded forest environments.
