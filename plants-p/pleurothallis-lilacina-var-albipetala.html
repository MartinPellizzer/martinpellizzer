Pleurothallis lilacina var. albipetala

Pleurothallis lilacina var. albipetala, commonly known as the white-petaled pleurothallis, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are predominantly white with a subtle lilac hue, and it produces small, greenish-white pseudobulbs.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated for its unique floral display.
