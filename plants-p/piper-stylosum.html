Piper stylosum

Piper stylosum, commonly known as the slender pipevine, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Piperaceae, which includes many species of flowering plants. The plant is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. It produces small, fleshy berries that are often consumed by birds.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal potential, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
