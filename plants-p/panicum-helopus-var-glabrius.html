Panicum helopus var. glabrius

Panicum helopus var. glabrius, commonly known as the smooth panicgrass, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like foliage and produces small, fluffy seed heads that are often wind-dispersed. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its leaves are narrow and smooth, with a bluish-green hue, and it blooms during the dry season.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in stabilizing soil in arid environments.
