Pleurothallis parahybunensis

Pleurothallis parahybunensis, commonly known as the Parahyba orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a small epiphytic orchid that typically grows on trees in humid, shaded forest environments. The plant features delicate, linear leaves and produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pale pink. It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique floral structure and subtle beauty.

Due to its habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native range.
