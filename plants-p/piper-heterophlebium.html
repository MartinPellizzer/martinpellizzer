Piper heterophlebium

Piper heterophlebium, commonly known as the variable vein pepper, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil and Peru. The plant is characterized by its climbing habit and pinnately compound leaves with serrated margins. Its small, greenish-white flowers are clustered in terminal inflorescences, and it produces small, dark berries as fruit.

Despite its ornamental potential, it is not widely cultivated for commercial purposes.
