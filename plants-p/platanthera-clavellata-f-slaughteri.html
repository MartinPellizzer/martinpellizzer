Platanthera clavellata f. slaughteri

Platanthera clavellata f. slaughteri, commonly known as the Slaughter's spottylip, is a rare orchid species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, greenish-white flowers with distinctive yellowish-brown markings. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and swampy areas. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem.

Due to habitat loss and limited distribution, it is considered a threatened species requiring conservation efforts.
