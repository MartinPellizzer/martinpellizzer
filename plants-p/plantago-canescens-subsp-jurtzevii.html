Plantago canescens subsp. jurtzevii

Plantago canescens subsp.

jurtzevii, commonly known as the Siberian plantain, is a perennial herb native to Central Asia. It features a rosette of broad, gray-green, hairy leaves that are often covered in a waxy cuticle.

The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in a spike, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in steppe and grassland habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
