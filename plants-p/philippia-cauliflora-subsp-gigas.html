Philippia cauliflora subsp. gigas

Philippia cauliflora subsp.

gigas, commonly known as the giant cauliflorous philippia, is a striking plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its unique cauliflorous growth habit, where flowers and fruits develop directly on the trunk and branches rather than at the ends of stems.

The plant produces large, showy flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, attracting a variety of pollinators. Its leaves are broad and glossy, contributing to its ornamental value in both natural and cultivated settings.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a valuable species for botanical research and conservation efforts.
