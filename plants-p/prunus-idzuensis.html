Prunus idzuensis

Prunus idzuensis, commonly known as the Japanese apricot, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its ornamental value, featuring showy, single or double pink to white flowers in early spring. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that turn yellow in autumn, adding to its seasonal appeal. It produces small, yellowish fruits that are edible but not commonly consumed.

Prunus idzuensis is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic beauty and is relatively easy to care for.
