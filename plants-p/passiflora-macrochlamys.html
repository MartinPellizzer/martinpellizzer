Passiflora macrochlamys

Passiflora macrochlamys, commonly known as the giant passion flower, is a striking tropical vine native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that feature a complex arrangement of petals, sepals, and bracts, often in shades of red, yellow, and white. The plant produces edible berries that are typically orange or yellow when ripe, containing a sweet, fleshy pulp. Its foliage consists of large, heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture, and it thrives in warm, humid climates.

Passiflora macrochlamys is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a decorative plant.
