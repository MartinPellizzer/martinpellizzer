Pfaffia tuberosa f. rubella

Pfaffia tuberosa f. rubella, commonly known as the red-rooted devil's backbone, is a unique variant of the Pfaffia genus native to South America.

This plant is characterized by its striking red roots and its distinctive, spiny stems that resemble a backbone. It thrives in tropical and subtropical environments, often growing in shaded, moist soils. The plant is known for its medicinal properties, with traditional uses in herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.

Its botanical features make it a notable species within the Amaranthaceae family.
