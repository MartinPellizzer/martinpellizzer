Paronychia chionaea subsp. kemaliya

Paronychia chionaea subsp.

kemaliya, commonly known as the white-flowered stonecrop, is a low-growing perennial plant native to southern Africa. It features succulent, fleshy leaves that are typically blue-gray in color, adapted to arid environments.

The plant produces small, star-shaped white or pale blue flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Its compact growth habit and drought tolerance make it a popular choice for landscaping in dry climates.
