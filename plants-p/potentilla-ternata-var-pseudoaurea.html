Potentilla ternata var. pseudoaurea

Potentilla ternata var. pseudoaurea, commonly known as the golden potentilla, is a low-growing shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has slender, woody stems that are often covered in fine, soft hairs. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets with serrated edges.

This variety is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained, rocky soils.
