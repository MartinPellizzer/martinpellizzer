Persicaria spectabilis

Persicaria spectabilis, commonly known as the showy polygon, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, which includes plants commonly referred to as smartweeds. This species is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, pinkish to reddish flowers that bloom in late summer. The leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often have a distinctive reddish or purple hue on the underside.

Persicaria spectabilis is often found in moist to wet soils in wetlands, stream banks, and other riparian habitats.
