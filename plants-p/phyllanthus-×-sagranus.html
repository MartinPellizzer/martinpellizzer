Phyllanthus × sagranus

Phyllanthus × sagranus, commonly known as the hybrid stonecrop, is a member of the Phyllanthaceae family.

It is a succulent plant characterized by its fleshy, thick leaves and compact growth habit. Native to regions of southern Africa, it thrives in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences.

Its botanical features make it well adapted to drought conditions, with specialized structures for water storage and reduced transpiration.
