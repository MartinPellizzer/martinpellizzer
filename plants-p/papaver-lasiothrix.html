Papaver lasiothrix

Papaver lasiothrix, commonly known as the woolly poppy, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its striking purple to pink flowers and its hairy, glandular stems and leaves, which give it its common name. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils. It produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, dark seeds.

Papaver lasiothrix is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
