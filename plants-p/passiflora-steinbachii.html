Passiflora steinbachii

Passiflora steinbachii, commonly known as the purple passionflower, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful flowers that feature a unique blend of purple, blue, and white hues. The plant produces edible berries that are often used in culinary applications. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a velvety texture.

Passiflora steinbachii is also valued for its ornamental beauty and is cultivated in gardens for its visual appeal.
