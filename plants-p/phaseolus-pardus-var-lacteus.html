Phaseolus pardus var. lacteus

Phaseolus pardus var. lacteus, commonly known as the white runner bean, is a variety of the common runner bean.

It is characterized by its pale green to white pods, which are typically longer and thinner than those of other varieties. The plant is a climbing legume with compound leaves and produces small, yellow flowers. It thrives in warm climates and is often cultivated for its edible beans.

This variety is valued for its mild flavor and high yield in agricultural settings.
