Paspalum longifolium var. hirsutum

Paspalum longifolium var. hirsutum, commonly known as hairy paspalum, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its hairy leaves and compact growth habit, making it well-suited for ornamental and erosion control purposes. The plant typically grows in moist to wet soils, often found in marshy areas or along riverbanks. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets that are often clustered in dense, cylindrical structures.

It is valued for its adaptability and ability to thrive in a variety of wetland environments.
