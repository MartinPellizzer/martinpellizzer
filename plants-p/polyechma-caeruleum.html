Polyechma caeruleum

Polyechma caeruleum, commonly known as the blue-flowered phlox, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features showy, clustered blue or violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its fragrant blooms and ability to attract pollinators.
