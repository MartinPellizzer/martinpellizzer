Plateilema palmeri

Plateilema palmeri, commonly known as Palmer's ghost flower, is a unique desert plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its small, delicate, and often translucent flowers that appear during the evening, a trait that aids in attracting specific pollinators. The plant has a low-growing, mat-forming habit, with narrow, fleshy leaves that help it conserve water in arid conditions. Its flowers are typically pale green or white, with a subtle fragrance that becomes more noticeable at night.

Plateilema palmeri is adapted to extreme environmental conditions, showcasing remarkable resilience in its native habitat.
