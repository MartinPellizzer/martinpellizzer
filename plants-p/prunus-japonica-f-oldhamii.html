Prunus japonica f. oldhamii

Prunus japonica f. oldhamii, commonly known as the Japanese cherry plum or oldhamii cherry plum, is a compact, ornamental shrub native to Japan.

It is characterized by its small, oval-shaped leaves that are dark green and glossy, providing a striking visual contrast against its clusters of fragrant, white flowers. The plant produces small, edible fruits that are typically yellow or red in color, though they are not commonly consumed by humans. It is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value, particularly during the spring when it blooms profusely.

This variety is relatively hardy and adaptable, making it suitable for a variety of garden environments.
