Piper annulatispicum

Piper annulatispicum, commonly known as the ringed pepper vine, is a climbing shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, woody stems that often have a distinctive ringed appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by elongated, fleshy berries that turn red when mature. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped with a glossy surface and are often used in traditional medicine.

Piper annulatispicum is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some reported medicinal properties.
