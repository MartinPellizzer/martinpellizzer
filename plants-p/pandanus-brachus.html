Pandanus brachus

Pandanus brachus, commonly known as the narrow-leaf pandanus, is a tropical plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and long, narrow leaves that are often used in traditional crafts. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that develop into fibrous, spiky fruits. Its roots and leaves are also used in local medicine and for making thatch.

Pandanus brachus thrives in coastal areas and is adapted to saline and sandy soils.
