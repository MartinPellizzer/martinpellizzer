Piper brevifolium

Piper brevifolium, commonly known as the short-leaved pepper plant, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its small, ovate leaves and clusters of tiny, greenish-white flowers. It produces small, peppery fruits that are used in traditional medicine and cuisine.

Its botanical features make it an important species for both ecological and ethnobotanical studies.
