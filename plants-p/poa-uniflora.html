Poa uniflora

Poa uniflora, commonly known as the single-flower grass, is a species of flowering plant in the grass family Poaceae.

It is native to the mountainous regions of South America, particularly in Chile and Argentina. This plant is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and small, spike-like inflorescences. The leaves are typically narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green coloration.

Poa uniflora is often found in rocky or alpine environments, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions.
