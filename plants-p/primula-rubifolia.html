Primula rubifolia

Primula rubifolia, commonly known as the red-leaved primrose, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia.

It features lance-shaped, dark green leaves with a reddish tinge on the underside, giving it its common name. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink to white, typically blooming in early spring. It thrives in cool, moist environments and prefers well-drained, acidic soil.

Primula rubifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and botanical collections.
