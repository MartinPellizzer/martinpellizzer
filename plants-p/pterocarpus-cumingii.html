Pterocarpus cumingii

Pterocarpus cumingii, commonly known as the African rosewood, is a deciduous tree native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its straight, dark-colored heartwood that is highly valued for its durability and aesthetic appeal. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by flat, pod-like fruits containing several seeds. Its leaves are bipinnate, consisting of numerous small leaflets, and it thrives in well-drained soils with adequate sunlight.

Due to its ornamental and timber value, Pterocarpus cumingii is often cultivated in horticultural settings.
