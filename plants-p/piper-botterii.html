Piper botterii

Piper botterii, commonly known as the Mexican pepper plant, is a tropical shrub native to Central America and parts of Mexico.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many pepper species. The plant is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves and small, greenish-white flowers that develop into red, peppercorn-like berries. These berries are edible and have a spicy, peppery flavor similar to black pepper.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of culinary spice.
