Protea lepidocarpodendron

Protea lepidocarpodendron, commonly known as the silver-leaf protea, is a striking South African plant belonging to the Proteaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescence composed of numerous small, tubular flowers arranged in a spherical or conical shape. The plant is evergreen, with leathery, silvery-gray foliage that gives it its common name. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in fynbos vegetation.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and botanical collections worldwide.
