Phoebe poiretiana

Phoebe poiretiana, commonly known as the Mexican ash, is a deciduous tree native to central Mexico.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and simple, opposite leaves. The tree produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are followed by oval, woody fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open woodlands.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
