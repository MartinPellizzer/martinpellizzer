Patagonium sentis

Patagonium sentis, commonly known as the Southern Patagonian Flax, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It belongs to the Linaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and delicate, blue to purple flowers. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in mountainous regions of Chile and Argentina. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a slightly hairy texture.

Patagonium sentis plays a role in local ecosystems by providing nectar for pollinators and habitat for small invertebrates.
