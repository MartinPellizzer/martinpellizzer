Ptiloria

Ptiloria, commonly known as the "Lemon Tree" or "Kaffir Lime," is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, narrow leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into round, green citrus fruits. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot. Its leaves are often used in culinary applications for their aromatic properties, and the fruit is valued for its tangy flavor.

Ptiloria is also cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive foliage and fragrant presence.
