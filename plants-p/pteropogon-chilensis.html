Pteropogon chilensis

Pteropogon chilensis, commonly known as the Chilean pteropogon, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Chile and Argentina.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that are often brightly colored. The plant typically grows in rocky or coastal areas, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are usually narrow and grass-like, while its flowers are clustered in spikes.
