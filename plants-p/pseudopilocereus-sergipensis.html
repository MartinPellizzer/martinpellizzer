Pseudopilocereus sergipensis

Pseudopilocereus sergipensis, commonly known as the Sergipense cactus, is a flowering plant native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its spiny stems and clusters of white or pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky environments and is adapted to arid conditions. Its structure includes areoles, which are specialized structures from which spines, flowers, and branches emerge.

Pseudopilocereus sergipensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in suitable climates.
