Pinus horizontalis

Pinus horizontalis, commonly known as the horizontal pine, is a rare and unique conifer native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive horizontal branching pattern, which gives it a distinctive, almost whimsical appearance. The plant has needle-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color, providing a striking contrast to its rugged, twisted form. It produces small, pale cones that mature to a reddish-brown color, and it is well adapted to rocky, arid environments.

Due to its limited distribution and ecological significance, Pinus horizontalis is considered a valuable species for conservation efforts.
