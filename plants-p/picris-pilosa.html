Picris pilosa

Picris pilosa, commonly known as yellow stargrass, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow and arranged in a cluster.

It is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
