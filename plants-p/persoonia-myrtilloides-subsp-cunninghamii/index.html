Persoonia Myrtilloides Subsp. Cunninghamii

Persoonia myrtilloides subsp. cunninghamii is a unique plant native to the coastal regions of southeastern Australia. It belongs to the Proteaceae family and is known for its striking appearance and ecological significance. This subspecies is characterized by its small, dark blue to purple berries and dense, leathery foliage. It thrives in sandy soils and is often found in heathlands and coastal dunes. Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and vulnerable species.
