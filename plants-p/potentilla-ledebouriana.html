Potentilla ledebouriana

Potentilla ledebouriana, commonly known as the alpine cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in rocky and alpine environments, where it thrives in well-drained soils. The plant produces small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are compound, consisting of five oval-shaped leaflets, and are covered in a fine layer of white hairs.

Potentilla ledebouriana is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and aesthetic appeal.
