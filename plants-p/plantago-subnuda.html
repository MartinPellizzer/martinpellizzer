Plantago subnuda

Plantago subnuda, commonly known as the naked plantain, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in a spike, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, along stream banks, and in disturbed habitats.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes used in traditional medicine and as a ground cover in ecological restoration projects.
