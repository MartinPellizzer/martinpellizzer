Piper guacimonum

Piper guacimonum, commonly known as guacimo, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other species such as the pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves and small, greenish-white flowers that are typically clustered in spikes. It produces small, round berries that turn from green to red as they mature.

The plant is often found in humid, shaded environments within the understory of tropical forests.
