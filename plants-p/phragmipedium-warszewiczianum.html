Phragmipedium warszewiczianum

Phragmipedium warszewiczianum, commonly known as the Warszewicz's phragmipedium, is a terrestrial orchid native to the rainforests of South America.

It is characterized by its erect, leafy stems and large, showy flowers that typically bloom in late winter or early spring. The plant produces pendulous, cream to yellowish flowers with a distinctive lip that is often darker in color. It thrives in shaded, humid environments with well-drained, acidic soil.

Due to its beauty and rarity, it is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
