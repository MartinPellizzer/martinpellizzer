Passiflora foetida var. mayarum

Passiflora foetida var. mayarum, commonly known as the Mayan passionflower, is a climbing vine native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, complex flowers with five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments that resemble a crown of thorns. The plant produces dark blue to black berries that are edible and have a mildly tart flavor. Its leaves are large, heart-shaped, and have a rough texture.

This species is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
