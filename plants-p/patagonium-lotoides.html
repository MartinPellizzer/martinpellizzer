Patagonium lotoides

Patagonium lotoides, commonly known as the Patagonian water lily, is a unique aquatic plant native to the southern regions of South America.

It thrives in freshwater environments such as lakes and slow-moving rivers, where it plays a vital role in maintaining ecological balance. The plant features broad, floating leaves that support a dense mat of vegetation, providing habitat for various aquatic organisms. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink, with a distinctive, waxy appearance that aids in pollination.

Patagonium lotoides is notable for its ability to tolerate cold temperatures, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
