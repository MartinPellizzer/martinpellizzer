Philodendron mathewsii

Philodendron mathewsii, commonly known as the Mathews' philodendron, is a climbing tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It features heart-shaped, dark green leaves with a velvety texture and prominent veins. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by orange-red berries that are toxic to humans and animals. It thrives in warm, humid environments with indirect light and well-draining soil.

Its unique foliage makes it a popular choice for indoor gardening and tropical plant collections.
