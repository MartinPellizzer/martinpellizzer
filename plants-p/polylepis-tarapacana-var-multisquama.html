Polylepis tarapacana var. multisquama

Polylepis tarapacana var. multisquama, commonly known as the Andean wax tree, is a unique species native to the high Andes of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, thick, waxy leaves that help it withstand the harsh, cold, and arid conditions of its environment. This plant is part of the Rosaceae family and is known for its ability to thrive at high altitudes, often growing above the tree line. Its bark is thick and fissured, providing protection against strong winds and temperature fluctuations.
