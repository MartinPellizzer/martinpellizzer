Plantago scorzonerifolia

Plantago scorzonerifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf plantain, is a perennial herb native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in moist, disturbed soils such as roadsides, meadows, and wetlands. The plant has lance-shaped, hairy leaves that are often ribbed and have a slightly waxy texture. Its small, greenish flowers are arranged in dense, spike-like clusters and bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant is known for its ability to thrive in challenging environments and is often used in traditional herbal medicine.
