Psoralea venusta

Psoralea venusta, commonly known as the showy psoralea, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, showy flowers that are typically pink or violet in color. The plant has trifoliate leaves and produces pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grassland and rocky habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
