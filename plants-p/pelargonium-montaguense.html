Pelargonium montaguense

Pelargonium montaguense, commonly known as the mountain geranium, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces small, clustered flowers in shades of pink or red. The leaves are typically rounded with a slightly hairy texture, and the plant is often found in rocky or sandy soils. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
