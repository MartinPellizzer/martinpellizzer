Pseudoheterocaryum macrocarpum

It is characterized by its large, irregularly shaped cap that can reach up to 30 centimeters in diameter. The cap is usually pale yellow to brown in color and has a distinct, wrinkled texture. The stem is thick and hollow, often with a faintly visible veil that becomes a ring near the base.

This fungus is typically found in moist, forested areas and is known for its distinctive appearance and potential toxicity.
