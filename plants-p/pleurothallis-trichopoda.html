Pleurothallis trichopoda

Pleurothallis trichopoda, commonly known as the hairy pleurothallis, is a species of orchid native to the cloud forests of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and the presence of short, hairy structures on its stems and leaves, which give it its common name. The plant typically produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and yellow, often arranged in clusters. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained, acidic soil for optimal growth.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is a subject of interest for both botanists and orchid enthusiasts.
