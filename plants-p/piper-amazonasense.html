Piper amazonasense

Piper amazonasense, commonly known as the Amazonian pepper or Amazonian pipevine, is a climbing shrub native to the rainforests of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many species of pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a three-lobed, fleshy berry that contains several seeds.

Piper amazonasense is often found in humid, shaded environments and plays a role in the ecological dynamics of its native habitat.
