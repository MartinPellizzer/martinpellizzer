Packera bernardina

Packera bernardina, commonly known as the Bernard's rocknet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish to white flowers arranged in a composite head, typical of the aster family. It thrives in rocky, alpine environments and is often found at high elevations.

Packera bernardina is adapted to cold climates and is a common component of alpine vegetation.
