Pelargonium parvulum

Pelargonium parvulum, commonly known as the dwarf geranium, is a compact, herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features small, rounded leaves with a slightly hairy surface and produces clusters of five-petaled, pink or red flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of about 10-15 centimeters, making it ideal for rock gardens or containers. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Pelargonium parvulum is known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated for its vibrant blooms and compact growth habit.
