Petunga glomerata

Petunga glomerata, commonly known as the clustered petunga, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dense clusters of small, yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or along stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture.

Petunga glomerata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
