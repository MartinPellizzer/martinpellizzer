Panicum benthamii

Panicum benthamii, commonly known as the Bentham's panicgrass, is a perennial grass species native to Australia.

It typically grows in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils, often in arid or semi-arid regions. The plant features slender, upright stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its inflorescence consists of a dense, cylindrical cluster of spikelets, which are characteristic of the grass family.

Panicum benthamii is adapted to withstand drought conditions, making it a resilient plant in its native environment.
