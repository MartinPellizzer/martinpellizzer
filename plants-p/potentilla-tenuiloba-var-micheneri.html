Potentilla tenuiloba var. micheneri

Potentilla tenuiloba var. micheneri, commonly known as the Michener's narrowleaf cinquefoil, is a low-growing perennial shrub native to the western United States.

It features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer, typically from May to June. The plant has slender, often hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves that are typically less than 2 inches in length. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in open habitats such as meadows and slopes.

Its botanical characteristics make it well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.
