Prunus jamasakura var. verecunda

Prunus jamasakura var. verecunda, commonly known as the Japanese cherry blossom variant, is a deciduous tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its delicate pink and white flowers that bloom in early spring, making it a symbol of transience and beauty in Japanese culture. The tree has small, oval-shaped leaves that turn yellow in autumn, adding to its seasonal appeal. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is often cultivated in gardens and parks for its ornamental value.
