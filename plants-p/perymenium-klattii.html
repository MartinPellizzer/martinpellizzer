Perymenium klattii

Perymenium klattii, commonly known as the Klatt's mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on the branches of specific host trees.

It possesses a distinctive appearance with small, fleshy leaves and produces small, white or pale green flowers. The plant relies on its host for water and nutrients, while it provides some sugars through photosynthesis. Its seeds are dispersed by birds that consume its berries.

Perymenium klattii is found in tropical and subtropical regions, often in forested areas with high humidity.
