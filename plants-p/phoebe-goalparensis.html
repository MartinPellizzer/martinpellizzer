Phoebe goalparensis

Phoebe goalparensis, commonly known as the Florida phoebe, is a small evergreen tree native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows to a height of 10 to 20 feet and has a rounded, dense canopy. The leaves are oval-shaped, leathery, and dark green with a glossy surface. It produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by blue-black berries.

This species is well adapted to coastal and wetland environments, making it a valuable component of local ecosystems.
