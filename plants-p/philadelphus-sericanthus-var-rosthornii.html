Philadelphus sericanthus var. rosthornii

Philadelphus sericanthus var. rosthornii, commonly known as the Rosthorn's mock orange, is a deciduous shrub native to the Balkans.

It features showy, fragrant white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has opposite, simple, ovate leaves with serrated edges and a smooth, greyish-bark trunk. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it suitable for gardens and landscapes.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and historical significance in horticulture.
