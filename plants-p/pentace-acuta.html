Pentace acuta

Pentace acuta, commonly known as the acute pentace, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Pentaceae and is characterized by its small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a rough texture.

The acute pentace is adapted to thrive in humid, tropical environments and is often found in forest understories.
