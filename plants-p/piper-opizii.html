Piper opizii

Piper opizii, commonly known as the Sikkim pepper or Himalayan long pepper, is a flowering plant native to the eastern Himalayas.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves and small, greenish-yellow flowers that develop into elongated, spiky fruits. The fruits are harvested when mature and used in traditional medicine and as a spice.

Its botanical features make it distinct from other pepper species, contributing to its ecological and medicinal significance in its native region.
