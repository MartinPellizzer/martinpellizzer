Piper matanganum

Piper matanganum, commonly known as the Java pepper or long pepper, is a climbing vine native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species such as black pepper. The plant produces small, spherical berries that are harvested when mature, typically in the dry season. The fruit contains a single seed and is used both as a spice and in traditional medicine.

Its botanical features include opposite, ovate leaves and a distinctive, aromatic scent.
