Pinus × rigitaeda

Pinus × rigitaeda, commonly known as the cross between Pinus rigida and Pinus taeda, is a hybrid conifer native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its needle-like leaves, which are typically longer and more slender than those of its parent species. The tree produces cones that mature over a period of several years, with the female cones often appearing reddish-brown in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in mixed hardwood forests.

Due to its hybrid nature, it exhibits a range of morphological variations, making it a subject of interest in botanical studies.
