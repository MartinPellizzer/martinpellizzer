Pandanus mesos

Pandanus mesos, commonly known as the narrow-leaf pandanus, is a tropical plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its long, narrow leaves that are typically arranged in a spiral pattern around the stem. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences that bear both male and female flowers. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to grow in sandy or coastal soils.

The plant is often used in traditional crafts and for its ornamental value in landscaping.
