Phyllanthus griffithii

Phyllanthus griffithii, commonly known as the Indian gooseberry or amla, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by bright orange, fleshy fruits. It is well-known for its high vitamin C content and is widely used in traditional medicine and as a culinary ingredient.

The plant thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental and medicinal value.
