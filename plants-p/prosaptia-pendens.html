Prosaptia pendens

Prosaptia pendens, commonly known as the hanging sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It typically grows in wet or marshy environments, showcasing its adaptability to humid and waterlogged conditions. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in spikelets. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense vegetation in its habitat.

Prosaptia pendens plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
