Polysphaeria parvifolia var. glabra

Polysphaeria parvifolia var. glabra, commonly known as the smooth-leaved starwort, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its small, star-shaped flowers that typically bloom in late spring or early summer. The leaves are usually smooth and glabrous, giving the plant its varietal name. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands or along stream banks.

The plant is of interest to botanists due to its unique floral structure and ecological adaptations.
