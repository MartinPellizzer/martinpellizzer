Potentilla heptaphylla var. perrobusta

Potentilla heptaphylla var. perrobusta, commonly known as the broad-leaved potentilla, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features woody stems that are often branched and covered with a layer of fine, soft hairs. The leaves are ovate to elliptic in shape, with a hairy surface and a serrated margin. Its flowers are small, yellowish, and clustered in corymbs, blooming in late spring to early summer.

This plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or alpine environments.
