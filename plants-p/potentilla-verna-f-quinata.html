Potentilla verna f. quinata

Potentilla verna f. quinata, commonly known as the five-leaf cinquefoil, is a low-growing perennial shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, typically from March to May. The plant has palmate leaves divided into five lobes, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in alpine or rocky habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardiness in cooler climates.
