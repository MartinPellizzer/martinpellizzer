Phyllanthus madeirensis

Phyllanthus madeirensis, commonly known as the Madeira spurge, is a flowering plant native to the island of Madeira.

It is a shrub characterized by its simple, opposite leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in shaded or semi-shaded areas.

Phyllanthus madeirensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
