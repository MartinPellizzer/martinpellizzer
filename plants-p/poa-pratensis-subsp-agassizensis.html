Poa pratensis subsp. agassizensis

Poa pratensis subsp.

agassizensis, commonly known as the Agassiz's bluegrass, is a member of the Poaceae family and is native to mountainous regions of Central Asia. This subspecies is characterized by its fine, tufted grass blades and a dense, low-growing habit.

It typically thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with blue-green glumes, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

It is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
