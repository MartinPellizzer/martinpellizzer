Phaeopappus libanoticus

Phaeopappus libanoticus, commonly known as the Lebanon phaeopappus, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the eastern Mediterranean.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by composite flower heads and often aromatic properties. The plant is distinguished by its yellowish to reddish flowers and hairy, lobed leaves. It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and potentially endangered species.
