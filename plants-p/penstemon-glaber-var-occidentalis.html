Penstemon glaber var. occidentalis

Penstemon glaber var. occidentalis, commonly known as the western beardtongue, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils in open habitats. The plant features tubular, two-lipped flowers that are usually pink to purple in color, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It is valued in gardens for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
