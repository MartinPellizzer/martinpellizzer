Prunus × lannesiana f. kinryu

Prunus × lannesiana f. kinryu, commonly known as the Japanese cherry blossom or sakura, is a cultivar of the Prunus genus, specifically a hybrid between Prunus lannesiana and Prunus × yedoensis.

It is characterized by its delicate, pale pink to white flowers that bloom in early spring, creating a visually stunning display. The plant is a deciduous tree with a rounded canopy and smooth, grey bark that becomes more rugged with age. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

This cultivar is highly valued for ornamental purposes in gardens and public spaces throughout Japan and other parts of the world.
