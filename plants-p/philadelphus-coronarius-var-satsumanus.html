Philadelphus coronarius var. satsumanus

Philadelphus coronarius var. satsumanus, commonly known as the Satsuma Mock Orange, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant has simple, opposite, ovate leaves with serrated margins. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often used in gardens for its pleasant fragrance and showy blooms.
