Phylica squamosa

Phylica squamosa, commonly known as the silverleaf phylica, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows in coastal and sandy soils, often found in the Western and Eastern Cape provinces. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in spring, followed by distinctive seed pods. Its leaves are covered in fine, silvery hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Phylica squamosa is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
