Pultenaea daphnoides

Pultenaea daphnoides, commonly known as the silver wattle, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its feathery, bipinnate leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are clustered in cylindrical spikes, typically blooming in spring. It is often found in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.
