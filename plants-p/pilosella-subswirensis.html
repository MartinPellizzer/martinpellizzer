Pilosella subswirensis

Pilosella subswirensis, commonly known as the slenderleaf hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters. It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils.

Pilosella subswirensis is noted for its adaptability and is often found in regions with temperate climates.
