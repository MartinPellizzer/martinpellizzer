Phyllanthus obfalcatus

Phyllanthus obfalcatus, commonly known as the broadleaf spurge, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny foliage and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has thick, succulent stems and leaves that are typically oval or lance-shaped. It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of light conditions.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and resilient species within its native habitat.
