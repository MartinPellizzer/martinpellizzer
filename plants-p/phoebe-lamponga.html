Phoebe lamponga

Phoebe lamponga, commonly known as the red-flowered phoebe, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking red flowers, which bloom in clusters and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has glossy, dark green leaves that provide a lush backdrop to its vibrant blossoms. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

Botanically, it belongs to the family Lauraceae, which includes other aromatic plants like laurel and cinnamon.
