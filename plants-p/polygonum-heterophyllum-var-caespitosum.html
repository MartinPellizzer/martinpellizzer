Polygonum heterophyllum var. caespitosum

Polygonum heterophyllum var. caespitosum, commonly known as the tufted smartweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It typically grows in moist to wet environments such as marshes, wet meadows, and along stream banks. The plant features alternate, ovate leaves with hairy surfaces and produces small, clustered flowers in late spring to early summer. Its stems are often reddish and slightly hairy, and it is known for its ability to thrive in disturbed soils.

The species is part of the buckwheat family (Polygonaceae) and is characterized by its distinctive, short, tufted growth habit.
