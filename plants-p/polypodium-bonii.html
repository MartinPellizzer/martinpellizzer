Polypodium bonii

Polypodium bonii, commonly known as the Chinese water fern, is a fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, pinnate fronds that are often used in traditional Chinese medicine. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories. Its reproductive structures, called sori, are located on the underside of the fronds.

Polypodium bonii is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
