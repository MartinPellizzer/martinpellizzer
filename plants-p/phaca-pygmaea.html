Phaca pygmaea

Phaca pygmaea, commonly known as the dwarf phaca, is a small, herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and stream banks. The plant features delicate, grass-like leaves and produces tiny, inconspicuous flowers that are often overlooked. Its botanical classification places it in the family Polygonaceae, which includes other familiar plants like buckwheat.
