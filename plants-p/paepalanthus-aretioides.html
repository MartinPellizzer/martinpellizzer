Paepalanthus aretioides

Paepalanthus aretioides, commonly known as the Arete or Arete cactus, is a species of flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a small, spineless cactus that typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, adding a striking visual element to its arid habitat. It is characterized by its compact, rounded shape and its ability to thrive in harsh, dry conditions.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is often cultivated in arid gardens and botanical collections.
