Poa janaensis

Poa janaensis, commonly known as the Janaensis bluegrass, is a species of grass native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and its ability to thrive in cool, moist environments. The plant typically produces small, spike-like inflorescences with purple or blue flowers, which are a distinctive feature. Its leaves are narrow and often have a bluish-green hue, contributing to its ornamental value.

Poa janaensis is often found in alpine meadows and is adapted to high-altitude conditions.
