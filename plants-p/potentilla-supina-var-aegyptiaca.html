Potentilla supina var. aegyptiaca

Potentilla supina var. aegyptiaca, commonly known as the Egyptian cinquefoil, is a low-growing perennial shrub native to arid regions of Africa.

It features small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in clusters, typically from spring to early summer. The plant has wiry, sprawling stems and hairy leaves that are often covered in fine, soft pubescence. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments.

Its botanical characteristics make it a resilient plant suitable for xeriscaping and erosion control in suitable climates.
