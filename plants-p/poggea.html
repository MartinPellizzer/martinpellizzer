Poggea

Poggea, commonly known as the "ghost flower," is a rare and elusive plant belonging to the nightshade family.

It is characterized by its small, white, star-shaped flowers that open only at night, giving it its mysterious reputation. The plant is native to the mountainous regions of South America, particularly in Chile and Argentina. Poggea thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in shaded, forested areas.

Due to its rarity and unique blooming habits, it is of great interest to botanists and conservationists.
