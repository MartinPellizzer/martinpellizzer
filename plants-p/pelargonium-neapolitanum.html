Pelargonium neapolitanum

Pelargonium neapolitanum, commonly known as the Neapolitan geranium, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink and white. The leaves are typically rounded and have a slightly hairy texture. This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.

It prefers well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight.
