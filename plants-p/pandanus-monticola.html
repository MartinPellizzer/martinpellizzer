Pandanus monticola

Pandanus monticola, commonly known as the mountain pandanus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Pandanaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including parts of Indonesia and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and long, narrow leaves that are often used in traditional crafts. It produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences at the tips of the leaves.

The plant is adapted to grow in coastal and wetland environments, where it thrives in moist, sandy soils.
