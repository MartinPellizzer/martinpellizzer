Pyrus komarovii

Pyrus komarovii, commonly known as the Komarov's pear, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia, particularly China.

It is characterized by its broad ovate leaves that are typically dark green on top and lighter in color underneath. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by edible fruits that are similar in appearance to common pears. Its botanical features make it a valuable species for both ornamental and potential agricultural purposes.

Pyrus komarovii is also noted for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and climates.
