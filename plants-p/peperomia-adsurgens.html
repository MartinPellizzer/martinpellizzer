Peperomia adsurgens

Peperomia adsurgens, commonly known as the climbing peperomia, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its trailing stems and dense, velvety foliage that ranges from green to reddish-brown. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or white, followed by unique, elongated berries. It thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Its ability to climb or trail makes it versatile for use in hanging baskets or as a shelf plant.
