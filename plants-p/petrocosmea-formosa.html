Petrocosmea formosa

Petrocosmea formosa, commonly known as the showy rock orchid, is a species of flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that often display vibrant colors such as pink, red, and purple. The plant typically grows in rocky or crevice habitats, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its morphology includes a sympodial growth pattern with fleshy roots, and it produces small, elliptical leaves.

Petrocosmea formosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
