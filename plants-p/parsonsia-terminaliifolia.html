Parsonsia terminaliifolia

Parsonsia terminaliifolia, commonly known as the flame tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, bright red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters, often resembling flames. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are usually green and glossy. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release seeds, aiding in its dispersal.

Parsonsia terminaliifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and parks.
