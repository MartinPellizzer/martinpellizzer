Poa tremuloides

Poa tremuloides, commonly known as the trembling bentgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and fine, delicate blades that often sway in the wind. The plant typically thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes. Its leaves are usually blue-green in color, and it produces small, inconspicuous spikelets that contain the seeds.

Poa tremuloides is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
