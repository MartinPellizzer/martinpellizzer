Paeonia wittmanniana var. tomentosa

Paeonia wittmanniana var. tomentosa, commonly known as the woolly peony, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of central Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, grayish foliage and showy, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with broad, palmately divided leaves. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, and the plant is known for its ornamental value in gardens.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.
