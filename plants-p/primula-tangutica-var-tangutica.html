Primula tangutica var. tangutica

Primula tangutica var. tangutica, commonly known as the Tangut primrose, is a perennial flowering plant native to the high altitudes of Tibet and western China.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its upright stems and clusters of bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, producing showy flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. It prefers cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists alike.
