Plantago lutulenta

Plantago lutulenta, commonly known as yellow plantain, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It features a rosette of broad, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers in dense, spike-like inflorescences during the summer months. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their mild laxative properties.

The plant is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
