Petroselinum sativum convar. foliosum

Petroselinum sativum convar. foliosum, commonly known as parsley, is a biennial or perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its finely divided, aromatic leaves that are widely used in culinary applications. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that develop into spherical, dry fruits containing numerous tiny seeds. Its leaves are rich in essential oils, which contribute to its distinctive flavor and medicinal properties.

Parsley thrives in cool climates and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for both home gardens and commercial cultivation.
