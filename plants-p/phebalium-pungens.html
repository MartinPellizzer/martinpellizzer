Phebalium pungens

Phebalium pungens, commonly known as the stinging wattle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant features small yellow flowers arranged in spikes, which bloom in spring. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three oval-shaped leaflets, and the stems are often armed with stiff, glandular hairs.

The plant is known for its defensive mechanism, as the hairs can cause skin irritation.
