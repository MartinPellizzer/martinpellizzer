Piper cahuapanasense

Piper cahuapanasense, commonly known as the Cahuapanasense pepper plant, is a species native to the Amazon rainforest of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many species of flowering plants known for their aromatic properties. The plant is characterized by its climbing habit, with long, slender stems that often trail along the forest floor. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a rough, textured surface.

The fruit, a small berry, contains the aromatic compound responsible for its distinctive flavor.
