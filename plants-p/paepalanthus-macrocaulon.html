Paepalanthus macrocaulon

Paepalanthus macrocaulon, commonly known as the giant palm-like plant, is a striking member of the Arecaceae family native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate leaves that can reach several meters in length. The plant produces large, showy inflorescences with numerous small white flowers, which are typically pollinated by insects. Its unique morphology and growth habit make it a notable species in botanical gardens and conservation efforts.

However, it is also considered vulnerable due to habitat loss and over-collection.
