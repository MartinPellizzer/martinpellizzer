Penstemon campanulatus var. campanulatus

Penstemon campanulatus var. campanulatus, commonly known as the blue mist penstemon, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of blue to violet bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry slopes, preferring full sun and well-drained soil. Its foliage consists of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

This species is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
