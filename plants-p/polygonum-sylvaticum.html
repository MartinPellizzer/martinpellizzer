Polygonum sylvaticum

Polygonum sylvaticum, commonly known as redroot smartweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to North America.

It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, stream banks, and wet meadows. The plant features upright stems that are often reddish in color, especially near the base, and lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. Its small, clustered flowers are usually pink to reddish-purple and bloom in late summer.

The plant is known for its ability to thrive in nutrient-rich soils and is sometimes used in wetland restoration projects.
