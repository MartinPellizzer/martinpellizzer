Pleuropterantha

Pleuropterantha, commonly known as the rock orchid, is a genus of flowering plants in the orchid family.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and striking, often colorful flowers that bloom in spring. These plants typically grow in rocky or crevice habitats, showcasing their adaptability to harsh environments. The leaves are usually narrow and grass-like, while the flowers have a unique structure with a spurred lip.

Pleuropterantha species are found primarily in regions of South America, particularly in the Andes.
