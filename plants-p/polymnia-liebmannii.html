Polymnia liebmannii

Polymnia liebmannii, commonly known as the Liebmann's polymnia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America.

It belongs to the family Polygonaceae, which includes plants commonly known as buckwheats. The plant features upright stems with lance-shaped leaves that have a distinctive reddish coloration. It produces small, greenish flowers arranged in clusters, and it is often found in moist, shaded environments.

Polymnia liebmannii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
