Potamogeton obtusifolius f. insolitus

Potamogeton obtusifolius f. insolitus, commonly known as the narrow-leaf pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater ecosystems in Europe and parts of Asia.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are arranged in spike-like clusters, and it thrives in shallow, slow-moving waters. It plays an important role in aquatic habitats by providing shelter for fish and invertebrates.
