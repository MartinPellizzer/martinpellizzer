Piper pilipedunculum

Piper pilipedunculum, commonly known as the red-veined pepper, is a tropical climbing vine native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many species of pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves with prominent red veins, which give it its common name. Its small, clustered flowers are typically greenish-white, and it produces small, peppery fruits that are used in local cuisines.

The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental and culinary value.
