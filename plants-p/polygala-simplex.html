Polygala simplex

Polygala simplex, commonly known as the narrow-leaved milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that typically range in color from white to pale pink. The plant grows as a herbaceous annual or perennial, often found in rocky or sandy soils. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, and it produces small, bean-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Polygala simplex is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
