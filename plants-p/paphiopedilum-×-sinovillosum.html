Paphiopedilum × sinovillosum

Paphiopedilum × sinovillosum, commonly known as the Chinese velvet orchid, is a hybrid orchid native to China.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety petals and a unique, intricate lip that resembles a pouch, a feature common in many orchid species. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its flowers are usually a mix of white, pink, and purple hues, adding to its ornamental appeal.

It prefers humid, warm environments with bright, indirect light for optimal growth.
