Piper papulaecaule

Piper papulaecaule, commonly known as the slender pipevine, is a species of flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes over 3,000 species of mostly woody plants. The plant is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and small, heart-shaped leaves. It produces clusters of tiny, greenish-white flowers that are typically pollinated by insects.

The fruit is a small, three-lobed capsule that contains several seeds.
