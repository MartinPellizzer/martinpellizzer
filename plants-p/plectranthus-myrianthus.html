Plectranthus myrianthus

Plectranthus myrianthus, commonly known as the purple-leaf plectranthus, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its aromatic, opposite leaves that are often a striking shade of purple or violet. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pale blue or purple, arranged in clusters. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and containers.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its compact growth habit and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
