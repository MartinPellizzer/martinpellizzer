Primula amethystina subsp. brevifolia

Primula amethystina subsp.

brevifolia, commonly known as the short-leaved primrose, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its compact growth habit, with short, sturdy stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white pubescence.

The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, blooming in early spring. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is cultivated in gardens and conservation efforts.
