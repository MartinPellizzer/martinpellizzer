Phyllanthus suffultus

Phyllanthus suffultus, commonly known as the African nightshade, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its small, greenish-yellow flowers. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in savanna regions.

The leaves are alternate and have a rough, slightly hairy texture.
