Pleurothallis tectosepala

Pleurothallis tectosepala, commonly known as the hooded pleurothallis, is a species of orchid native to the cloud forests of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its intricate, often fragrant flowers that are typically small to medium in size. The plant produces pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, aiding its survival in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are usually elliptical and arranged in a rosette pattern, contributing to its compact growth habit.

Pleurothallis tectosepala is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and unique floral structure.
