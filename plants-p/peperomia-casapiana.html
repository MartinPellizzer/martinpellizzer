Peperomia casapiana

Peperomia casapiana, commonly known as the silver peperomia, is a compact evergreen shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It features glossy, dark green leaves with a distinctive silvery or grayish underside, giving it a striking visual appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white or pale green flowers, typically emerging from the leaf axils. It thrives in bright, indirect light and prefers well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Due to its low maintenance requirements and attractive foliage, it is widely appreciated as a decorative plant.
