Philibertia subnivea

Philibertia subnivea, commonly known as the snow-white filbert, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a perennial herb with a short, stout stem and basal leaves that are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. Its fruit is a follicle containing several nut-like seeds, which are edible and used in local cuisines.

Philibertia subnivea thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments.
