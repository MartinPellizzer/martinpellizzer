Podocarpus rivularis

Podocarpus rivularis, commonly known as the river podocarp, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Podocarpaceae family and is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and small, cone-like fruits. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along riverbanks. Its leaves are leathery and have a distinctive, aromatic scent.

Podocarpus rivularis plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
