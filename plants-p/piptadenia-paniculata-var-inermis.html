Piptadenia paniculata var. inermis

Piptadenia paniculata var. inermis, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or *Piptadenia inermis*, is a deciduous tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, red or orange flowers. The plant is known for its rapid growth and is often used in reforestation projects due to its ability to thrive in various soil types. Its fruit is a legume pod containing edible seeds, which are rich in nutrients.

The tree also plays an important role in supporting local biodiversity and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
