Primula malvacea subsp. rosthornii

Primula malvacea subsp.

rosthornii, commonly known as the Rosthorn's primrose, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa. It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant has ovate to lanceolate leaves with hairy undersides, and it produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small seeds.

It is often found in coastal or mountainous regions where it thrives in well-drained conditions.
