Pogostemon nepetoides

Pogostemon nepetoides, commonly known as catnip, is a herbaceous plant native to Europe, Asia, and Africa.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant is renowned for its aromatic leaves, which contain high concentrations of nepetalactone, a compound responsible for its stimulating effects on cats. It typically grows in moist, sunny environments and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.

Pogostemon nepetoides is also used in traditional medicine for its purported calming and digestive properties.
