Pectis swartziana

Pectis swartziana, commonly known as the African dandelion, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a herbaceous perennial that grows up to 1 meter in height, characterized by its upright stem and alternate leaves. The plant produces yellow composite flowers that bloom from spring to summer, followed by fluffy seed heads resembling dandelion fluff. Its leaves are lance-shaped with serrated edges and have a slightly hairy texture.

Pectis swartziana is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
