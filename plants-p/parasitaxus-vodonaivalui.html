Parasitaxus vodonaivalui

Parasitaxus vodonaivalui, commonly known as the Vodonaival's parasitaxus, is a rare and ancient conifer native to the high-altitude forests of southern China.

It is characterized by its unique morphology, featuring scale-like leaves and a distinctive cone structure. This plant is notable for its parasitic tendencies, relying on other plants for some of its nutrients. Its botanical significance lies in its evolutionary adaptations and its role in studying ancient plant lineages.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species.
