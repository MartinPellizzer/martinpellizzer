Passiflora cuneifolia

Passiflora cuneifolia, commonly known as the cuneate passionflower, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, palmate leaves and showy, five-petaled flowers that resemble a crown. The plant produces bright yellow-orange berries that contain edible seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Passiflora cuneifolia is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
