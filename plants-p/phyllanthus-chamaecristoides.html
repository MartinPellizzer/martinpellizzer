Phyllanthus chamaecristoides

Phyllanthus chamaecristoides, commonly known as the creeping phyllanthus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is a low-growing shrub characterized by its small, opposite leaves and clusters of yellow flowers. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed or open habitats.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes used in horticulture for ground cover.
