Polygonum reynoutria f. colorans

Polygonum reynoutria f. colorans, commonly known as the redflower knotweed, is a variant of the invasive species *Polygonum reynoutria*, which is native to Asia but has spread to various parts of the world.

This plant is characterized by its robust growth, rhizomatous root system, and its ability to thrive in a wide range of soil conditions. The foliage is typically heart-shaped with a smooth texture, and the leaves often exhibit a reddish or purple hue, especially under certain environmental conditions. The flowers are small, clustered in dense inflorescences, and range in color from white to pink, adding ornamental value to the plant.

Due to its aggressive growth, it is considered a noxious weed in many regions, posing a threat to native ecosystems.
