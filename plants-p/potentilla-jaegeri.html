Potentilla jaegeri

Potentilla jaegeri, commonly known as Jaeger's cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its small, showy yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters and features compound leaves with five leaflets. Its root system is fibrous and adapted to thrive in well-drained, rocky soils.

Potentilla jaegeri is often found in alpine meadows and is valued for its ornamental qualities in horticulture.
