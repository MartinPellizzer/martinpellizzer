Pleurothallis cynocephala

Pleurothallis cynocephala, commonly known as the "monkey head orchid," is a species of orchid native to the cloud forests of Central and South America.

It is a small to medium-sized epiphytic orchid, typically growing on trees or rocks in humid, shaded environments. The plant features delicate, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white, yellow, and green. Its flowers are often clustered in a spike and are known for their unique, intricate structure.

Pleurothallis cynocephala is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
