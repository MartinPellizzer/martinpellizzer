Primula flabellifera

Primula flabellifera, commonly known as the broad-leaf primrose, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments at high altitudes. It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is valued for its ornamental appeal.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
