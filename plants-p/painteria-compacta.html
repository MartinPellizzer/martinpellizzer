Painteria compacta

Painteria compacta, commonly known as the dwarf painteria, is a striking flowering plant native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant, showy flowers that range from red to orange in color. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that attract pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees. Its leaves are typically dark green and glossy, providing a striking contrast to its colorful blooms.

Painteria compacta is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its unique appearance and ability to thrive in warm, humid environments.
