Pineda

Pineda, commonly known as the Mexican pine, is a coniferous tree native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Pinaceae family and is characterized by its long, needle-like leaves and cone-bearing structure. The tree can grow up to 30 meters tall, with a straight trunk and a conical crown. Its bark is thick and scaly, providing protection against wildfires and pests.

The plant plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, offering shelter and food for various wildlife species.
