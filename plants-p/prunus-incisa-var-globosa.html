Prunus incisa var. globosa

Prunus incisa var. globosa, commonly known as the Japanese flowering cherry or some sources refer to it as the globe cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its rounded, globular form and is known for its early blooming pink or white flowers that appear before the leaves. The plant has simple, ovate leaves that turn yellow in autumn, adding to its ornamental value. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Due to its compact size and showy display, it is often used in landscape design for its aesthetic appeal.
