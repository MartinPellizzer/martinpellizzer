Phyllostachys edulis f. subconvexa

Phyllostachys edulis f. subconvexa, commonly known as the subconvex bamboo, is a variant of the common bamboo species, characterized by its unique leaf shape.

It is a perennial grass native to East Asia, particularly China, and thrives in temperate climates. The plant features slender, hollow stems that grow in clusters, and its leaves are broad and slightly curved at the base. It is valued for ornamental purposes and is often used in landscaping due to its aesthetic appeal and hardiness.

The subconvexa form exhibits a distinct leaf morphology compared to other bamboo varieties, making it a distinctive choice for gardeners and botanists alike.
