Phyllanthus fuertesii

Phyllanthus fuertesii, commonly known as the Mexican spurge, is a shrub native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family, which is known for its diverse and often toxic species. The plant features small, green leaves with a slightly waxy texture and produces yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters. It is typically found in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to withstand arid conditions.

The plant is also noted for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal practices.
