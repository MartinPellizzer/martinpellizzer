Polygonum lorinseri

Polygonum lorinseri, commonly known as the Lorinser's knotweed, is a perennial herb native to specific regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, which includes plants commonly referred to as smartweeds or knotweeds. The plant is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers that appear in late summer. Its leaves are typically ovate to lanceolate, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

Polygonum lorinseri is often found in moist, disturbed soils and is adapted to temperate climates.
