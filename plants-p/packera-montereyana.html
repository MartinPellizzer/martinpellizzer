Packera montereyana

Packera montereyana, commonly known as Monterey golden aster, is a perennial herb native to California and southern Oregon.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its bright yellow composite flowers. The plant typically grows in moist meadows and open woodlands, preferring cool, humid environments. Its leaves are basal, heart-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, while its flowering stems are usually unbranched.

It plays an important ecological role by providing nectar for pollinators and serving as a habitat for various invertebrates.
