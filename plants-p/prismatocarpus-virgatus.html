Prismatocarpus virgatus

Prismatocarpus virgatus, commonly known as the slender pandanus, is a member of the Pandanaceae family.

It is a shrub or small tree native to the Pacific Islands, including Hawaii and Tahiti. The plant is characterized by its narrow, sword-like leaves that are often used in traditional crafts and cooking. Its inflorescence consists of small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters.

The fruit is a drupe, typically containing a single seed, and is an important food source for various wildlife species.
