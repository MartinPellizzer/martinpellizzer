Poa violacea var. viridiaurea

Poa violacea var. viridiaurea, commonly known as the green-leafed bluegrass, is a variant of the bluegrass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its fine, grass-like leaves that are typically a vibrant green, with a slight bluish tinge. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences bearing purple to blue flowers, which bloom in the spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and meadows.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and restoration projects.
