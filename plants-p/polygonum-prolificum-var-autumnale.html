Polygonum prolificum var. autumnale

Polygonum prolificum var. autumnale, commonly known as autumnal knotweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of Asia.

It features upright, slender stems that often branch near the base, forming dense clusters. The leaves are alternate, lanceolate, and have a slightly hairy surface, often tinged with red or purple in the fall. Its small, clustered flowers appear in late summer to early autumn, producing a distinctive pink or reddish color.

This plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in moist, disturbed soils.
