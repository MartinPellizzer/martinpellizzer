Pleurothallis barboselloides

Pleurothallis barboselloides, commonly known as the Barbozel's orchid, is a species of orchid native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a small, epiphytic plant that typically grows on trees in humid, shaded forest environments. The plant features delicate, lance-shaped leaves and produces fragrant, greenish-white flowers with intricate patterns. It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is known for its unique floral structure and subtle coloration.

Due to its habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native range.
