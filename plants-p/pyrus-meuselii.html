Pyrus meuselii

Pyrus meuselii, commonly known as the Meusel's pear, is a small deciduous tree native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, elongated leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The tree produces small, sweet-tart pears that ripen in late summer. It is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and unique fruit.

Pyrus meuselii is relatively cold-hardy and can thrive in a variety of soil types.
