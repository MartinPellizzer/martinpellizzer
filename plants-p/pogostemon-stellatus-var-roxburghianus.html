Pogostemon stellatus var. roxburghianus

Pogostemon stellatus var. roxburghianus, commonly known as the star-leaved pogostemon, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive star-shaped leaves, which are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, pale blue to violet flowers arranged in spikes, typically blooming in summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its aromatic leaves and ornamental value make it a popular choice for horticultural use in tropical gardens.
