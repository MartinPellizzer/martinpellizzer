Pulsatilla vulgaris subsp. anglica

Pulsatilla vulgaris subsp.

anglica, commonly known as the English pasqueflower, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe. It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its showy, yellowish to pale pink flowers that appear in early spring.

The plant has a short, thick rhizome and hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in well-drained, alkaline soils and is often found in grasslands and rocky habitats.

The flowers are pollinated by insects, and the plant is known for its ornamental value in gardens.
