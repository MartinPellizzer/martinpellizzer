Polypodium taenitis

Polypodium taenitis, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wall fern, is a species of epiphytic fern native to Southeast Asia.

It typically grows on tree trunks and rocks in humid, shaded environments. The plant has elongated, lance-shaped fronds with a distinctive, serrated margin. Its sporangia are clustered in sori along the margins of the fronds.

Polypodium taenitis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical gardens.
