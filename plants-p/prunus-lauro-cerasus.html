Prunus-lauro-cerasus

Prunus-lauro-cerasus, commonly known as the cherry laurel, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Europe and western Asia.

It features glossy, dark green leaves that are elliptical in shape and often have a leathery texture. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers in early spring, which are followed by dark purple berries. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping.

However, its berries are toxic to humans and animals, requiring caution in cultivation.
