Passiflora foetida var. variegata

Passiflora foetida var. variegata, commonly known as the variegated passionflower, is a climbing shrub native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its striking, variegated leaves that display a mix of green, white, and sometimes yellow or cream patterns. The plant produces showy, complex flowers with five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments, typical of the Passifloraceae family. Its fruit is a red, fleshy berry that contains inedible seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and floral display.
