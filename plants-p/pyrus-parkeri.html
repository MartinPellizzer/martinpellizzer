Pyrus parkeri

Pyrus parkeri, commonly known as the parker pear, is a deciduous tree native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves and clusters of white flowers that bloom in early spring. The tree produces small, edible fruits that are typically yellow or orange when ripe. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for cultivation in temperate climates.

Pyrus parkeri is valued for its ornamental beauty and historical significance in horticulture.
