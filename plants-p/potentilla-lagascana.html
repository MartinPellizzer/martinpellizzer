Potentilla lagascana

Potentilla lagascana, commonly known as the Spanish cinquefoil, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit. The plant features yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, typically from June to July. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with five to seven leaflets, and are covered in fine hairs.

Potentilla lagascana thrives in dry, sunny habitats such as rocky soils and open woodlands.
