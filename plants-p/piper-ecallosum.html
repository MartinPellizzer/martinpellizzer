Piper ecallosum

Piper ecallosum, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes over 3,000 species of flowering plants. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves, which are opposite and have a serrated margin. It produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in spikes, followed by dark blue berries.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been introduced to other regions, sometimes becoming an invasive species.
