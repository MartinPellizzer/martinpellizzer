Pithecellobium confusum

Pithecellobium confusum, commonly known as the confused pithecolobium, is a small evergreen shrub native to tropical regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces edible pods that contain seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories or along riverbanks.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
