Primula sibirica var. integrifolia

Primula sibirica var. integrifolia, commonly known as the Siberian primrose, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features a rosette of basal leaves that are broadly ovate with entire margins, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, typically blooming in early spring. It thrives in well-drained, moist soils and prefers partial to full shade, making it suitable for woodland gardens.

Its foliage and flowers are both ornamental, contributing to its popularity in horticulture.
