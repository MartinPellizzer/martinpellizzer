Plagius virgatus

Plagius virgatus, commonly known as the pink sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically reaches heights of 10 to 20 centimeters. The plant features small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often covered in a fine layer of white hairs.

Plagius virgatus thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it a hardy species suited to arid environments.
