Prunus padus var. borealis

Prunus padus var. borealis, commonly known as the northern bird cherry, is a variant of the bird cherry species native to northern regions of Europe and Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized deciduous tree or shrub that typically grows in moist, temperate climates. The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers in early spring, followed by edible, yellowish fruits that are preferred by birds and some wildlife. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have serrated edges, contributing to its ornamental value.

The wood is strong and durable, making it useful for various crafts and construction purposes.
