Polygonum indicum

Polygonum indicum, commonly known as Indian knotweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Asia.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, which includes many species with distinctive arrowhead-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring slender stems and small, clustered flowers that bloom in summer. Its leaves are alternate, lanceolate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

The plant is characterized by its ability to thrive in moist environments, such as wetlands and stream banks.
