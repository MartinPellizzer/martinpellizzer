Primula leucops

Primula leucops, commonly known as the white primrose, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its showy, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant typically grows in cool, moist environments and prefers well-drained soil. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in a fine layer of pubescence.

Primula leucops is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a cut flower.
