Pinus ayacahuite subsp. strobiformis

Pinus ayacahuite subsp.

strobiformis, commonly known as the Mexican red pine, is a subspecies native to central Mexico. It is a medium to tall evergreen conifer with slender, straight trunks and dense, pyramidal crowns.

The needles are typically arranged in bundles of five, with a dark green color and a slight blue-gray hue on the underside. Its cones are woody, elongated, and often remain attached to the branches for many years.

This subspecies is adapted to a variety of soil types and is often found in mountainous regions with moderate rainfall.
