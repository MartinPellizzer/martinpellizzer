Phanera japonica

Phanera japonica, commonly known as the Japanese flame tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Japan and Korea.

It is characterized by its showy, bright red or orange inflorescences that appear in late spring to early summer. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape and are usually dark green in color. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens.

The species is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
