Papaver chelidoniifolium f. tenuisectum

Papaver chelidoniifolium f. tenuisectum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf poppy, is a variant of the broadleaf poppy characterized by its slender, elongated leaves.

This plant belongs to the Papaveraceae family and is known for its striking, bright yellow to orange flowers. The foliage is typically finely divided, giving it a delicate appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This cultivar is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and botanical collections.
