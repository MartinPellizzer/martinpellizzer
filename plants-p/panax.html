Panax

Panax, commonly known as ginseng, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Araliaceae.

It is native to eastern Asia, with the most well-known species being Panax ginseng, which is widely cultivated for its medicinal roots. The plant typically grows as a perennial herb with fleshy, tuberous roots that are rich in bioactive compounds. Its leaves are compound, with a distinctive palmate shape, and it produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by red berries.

The genus includes several species, some of which are used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
