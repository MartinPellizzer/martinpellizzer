Phanera glabrifolia var. maritima

Phanera glabrifolia var. maritima, commonly known as the coastal phanera, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silky hair. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to salt spray and coastal winds.

This variety is often found in wetland or brackish environments along the shorelines.
