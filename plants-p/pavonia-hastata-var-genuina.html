Pavonia hastata var. genuina

Pavonia hastata var. genuina, commonly known as the Mexican bushcrickets, is a flowering plant native to parts of Central America and Mexico.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of showy, pink to red bract-like flowers that resemble small, rounded leaves. The plant produces small, oval fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Its unique floral structure and vibrant color make it a standout in the landscape.
