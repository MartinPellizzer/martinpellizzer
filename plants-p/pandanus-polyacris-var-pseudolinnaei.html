Pandanus polyacris var. pseudolinnaei

Pandanus polyacris var. pseudolinnaei, commonly known as the narrow-leaf pandanus, is a tropical plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender leaves that are typically narrow and linear in shape. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers arranged in panicles, which are often borne on long, branching stems. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to thrive in wet, swampy environments.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional crafts in its native regions.
