Panicum albemarlense

Panicum albemarlense, commonly known as the white panicgrass, is a species of grass native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and pale green to white-colored foliage. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as fields and roadsides. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny grains.

Due to its limited distribution and specific ecological requirements, it is considered a rare and less studied species within the Panicum genus.
