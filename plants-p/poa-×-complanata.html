Poa × complanata

Poa × complanata, commonly known as the flatleaf bluegrass, is a naturally occurring hybrid between Poa annua and Poa trivialis.

It is a perennial grass that typically grows in temperate regions, often found in lawns, meadows, and disturbed soils. The plant has narrow, flat leaves that are typically blue-green in color, giving it a distinctive appearance. It produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences with wind-pollinated flowers.

Due to its adaptability and aesthetic appeal, it is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
