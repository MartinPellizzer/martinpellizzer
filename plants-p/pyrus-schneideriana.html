Pyrus schneideriana

Pyrus schneideriana, commonly known as the Schneider's pear, is a small deciduous tree native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and ovate, dark green leaves that turn yellow in autumn. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by edible, sweet-tart pears that ripen in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value and unique fruit, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
