Phoebe kwangsiensis

Phoebe kwangsiensis, commonly known as the Guangxi phoebe, is a species of flowering plant in the family Lauraceae.

It is native to southern China, particularly the Guangxi region, and is typically found in moist, shaded environments within forests. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical in shape and have a glossy surface. It produces small, fragrant flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by small, dark berries.

Phoebe kwangsiensis is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
