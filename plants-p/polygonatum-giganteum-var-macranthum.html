Polygonatum giganteum var. macranthum

Polygonatum giganteum var. macranthum, commonly known as the giant Solomon's seal, is a striking perennial plant native to eastern Asia.

It features tall, arching stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height, adorned with large, elliptical leaves that are dark green and glossy. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped, white or pale yellow flowers in late spring, followed by red berries that ripen in the fall. It thrives in shady, moist environments and is often used in ornamental gardens for its dramatic foliage and floral display.

Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread slowly, making it a valuable addition to woodland landscapes.
