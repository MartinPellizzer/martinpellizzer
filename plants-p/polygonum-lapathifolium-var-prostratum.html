Polygonum lapathifolium var. prostratum

Polygonum lapathifolium var. prostratum, commonly known as prostrate knotweed, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Asia.

It features rhizomatous growth and produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in dense clusters. The leaves are typically heart-shaped with a hairy texture and are often covered in fine pubescence. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

This plant is known for its rapid spread and can become invasive in certain ecosystems.
