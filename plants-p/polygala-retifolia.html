Polygala retifolia

Polygala retifolia, commonly known as the slender milk-vetch, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Polygalaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, often pink or white flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are usually narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, bean-like pods that contain the seeds.

Polygala retifolia is often found in open woodlands and along coastal areas.
