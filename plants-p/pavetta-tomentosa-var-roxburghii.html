Pavetta tomentosa var. roxburghii

Pavetta tomentosa var. roxburghii, commonly known as the woolly pavetta, is a shrub native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically covered in a whitish, woolly coating. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its aesthetic appeal.
