Pharbitis laciniata

Pharbitis laciniata, commonly known as the leafy morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and vibrant, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of purple and blue. The plant is a climbing vine with tendrils that allow it to ascend supports or other structures. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Pharbitis laciniata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
