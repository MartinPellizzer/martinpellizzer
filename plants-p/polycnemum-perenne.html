Polycnemum perenne

Polycnemum perenne, commonly known as field sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges and related grass-like plants. The plant has triangular stems and produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters called spikelets. Its leaves are typically narrow and grass-like, with a distinct midrib.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, wetlands, and along stream banks.
