Phoradendron quadrangulare var. gracile

Phoradendron quadrangulare var. gracile, commonly known as the slender mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on the branches of various host trees.

It has small, opposite, ovate leaves that are usually covered in a fine, white pubescence. The plant produces small, white to pale green flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. Its berries are small, round, and often turn red when mature, containing sticky seeds that adhere to birds for dispersal.

This species is native to the southeastern United States and is often found in forested areas.
