Platanus vulgaris var. vitifolia

Platanus vulgaris var. vitifolia, commonly known as the California sycamore, is a deciduous tree native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that turn yellow in the fall. The bark is thick, flaky, and ranges from gray to white, providing a distinctive appearance. It produces spherical, fuzzy fruits that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

This species is adapted to a variety of soil types and is often found in riparian zones and urban landscapes.
