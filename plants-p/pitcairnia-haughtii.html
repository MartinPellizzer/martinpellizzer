Pitcairnia haughtii

Pitcairnia haughtii, commonly known as the showy pitcairnia, is a species of flowering plant in the Bromeliaceae family.

It is native to the humid forests of South America, particularly in regions of Brazil, Colombia, and Ecuador. The plant is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that form a water-holding cup, a common feature among bromeliads. Its inflorescence consists of vibrant red or orange flowers that bloom in late summer or early autumn.

Pitcairnia haughtii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical and subtropical gardens.
