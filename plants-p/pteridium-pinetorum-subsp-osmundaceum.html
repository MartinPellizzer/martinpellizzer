Pteridium pinetorum subsp. osmundaceum

Pteridium pinetorum subsp.

osmundaceum, commonly known as the osmundaceum fern, is a fern species native to certain regions of the Americas. It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its delicate, lace-like fronds.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its spore-producing structures, called sori, are clustered on the underside of its leaves.
