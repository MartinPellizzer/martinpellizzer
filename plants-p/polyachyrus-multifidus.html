Polyachyrus multifidus

Polyachyrus multifidus, commonly known as the multifid polyachyrus, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Araceae, which includes plants such as philodendrons and peace lilies. The plant features a thick, rhizomatous stem with broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often variegated in color. Its inflorescence consists of a spadix surrounded by a modified leaf called a spathe, typical of the Araceae family.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its striking foliage and unique floral structure.
