Pastinaca lehmanniana

Pastinaca lehmanniana, commonly known as Lehmann's burdock, is a species of flowering plant in the sunflower family.

It is native to South America, particularly Argentina and Chile, and thrives in temperate climates. The plant features large, heart-shaped leaves with a rough texture and produces purple flowers that bloom in the summer. Its roots are thick and fibrous, often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Overall, it is a hardy plant with both ornamental and medicinal value.
