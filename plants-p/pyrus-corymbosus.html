Pyrus corymbosus

Pyrus corymbosus, commonly known as the American hazelnut, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces edible nuts enclosed in a spiny husk, which are typically harvested in the fall. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Pyrus corymbosus is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a food source for wildlife.
