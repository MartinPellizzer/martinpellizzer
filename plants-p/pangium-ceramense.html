Pangium ceramense

Pangium ceramense, commonly known as the Ceramese mangosteen, is a tropical plant native to the Maluku Islands of Indonesia.

It belongs to the family Myrtaceae and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and fragrant flowers. The plant produces edible, segmented fruits that are similar in appearance to the mangosteen but have a distinct flavor. Its botanical features include smooth, leathery leaves and small, white flowers that bloom in clusters.

Pangium ceramense is often cultivated for its ornamental value and for its unique fruit, which is appreciated in local cuisines.
