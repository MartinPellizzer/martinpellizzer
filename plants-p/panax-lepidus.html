Panax lepidus

Panax lepidus, commonly known as the Japanese ginseng, is a rare and endangered species native to the mountainous regions of Japan.

It belongs to the Araliaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, hairy leaves and yellowish flowers. The plant is known for its medicinal properties, though it is less commercially exploited than other ginseng species. It thrives in cool, shaded environments and requires specific soil conditions for optimal growth.

Due to its rarity and ecological significance, conservation efforts are crucial for its survival.
