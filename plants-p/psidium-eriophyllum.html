Psidium eriophyllum

Psidium eriophyllum, commonly known as the silverleaf guava, is a small evergreen shrub or tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that have a silvery underside, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, yellow or orange berries. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit.

Psidium eriophyllum is also valued for its ability to tolerate poor soil conditions and its use in traditional medicine.
