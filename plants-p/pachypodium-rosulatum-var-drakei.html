Pachypodium rosulatum var. drakei

Pachypodium rosulatum var. drakei, commonly known as the Drake's bottle tree, is a succulent plant native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its thick, bottle-shaped trunk that stores water, allowing it to survive in arid conditions. The plant features sparse, spiny foliage and produces small, white flowers during the wet season. Its unique morphology makes it well-adapted to dry, rocky environments.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking appearance and resilience.
