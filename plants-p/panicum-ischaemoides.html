Panicum ischaemoides

Panicum ischaemoides, commonly known as silverleaf panicgrass, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage, which results from fine, dense hairs covering the leaves. The plant produces small, fluffy panicles that bloom in late summer, adding a light, airy appearance to the landscape. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it adaptable to various environmental conditions.

Its ornamental value and low maintenance requirements make it a popular choice for native plant gardens.
