Polythecium splendidum

Polythecium splendidum, commonly known as the golden spire orchid, is a striking flowering plant native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stem and clusters of vibrant yellow flowers that bloom during the rainy season. The plant has lance-shaped, glossy leaves that help it thrive in humid, shaded environments. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb nutrients from the rich, organic soil of its habitat.

Polythecium splendidum is valued not only for its ornamental beauty but also for its ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
