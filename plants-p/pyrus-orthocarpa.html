Pyrus orthocarpa

Pyrus orthocarpa, commonly known as the common whitebeam, is a deciduous tree native to Europe and parts of western Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its small, white flowers and oval-shaped leaves. The tree produces edible berries that are typically yellow or orange when mature. Its bark is smooth and grayish-brown, with a tendency to peel in thin layers.

Pyrus orthocarpa is often found in woodland areas and thrives in well-drained soils.
