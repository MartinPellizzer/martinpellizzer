Pandanus halmaherensis

Pandanus halmaherensis, commonly known as the Halmahera pandanus, is a flowering plant native to the Maluku Islands of Indonesia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and long, sword-shaped leaves that are often used in traditional crafts. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences with fragrant, yellowish flowers. Its roots and leaves have been traditionally used for medicinal purposes by local communities.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in coastal environments.
