Psidium willemetianum

Psidium willemetianum, commonly known as the Willemetian guava, is a tropical plant belonging to the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and thrives in warm, humid climates. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that develop into edible, berry-like fruits. Its leaves are opposite, elliptical, and have a glossy appearance.

The fruit is typically yellow or red when ripe and is valued for its aromatic flavor and nutritional content.
