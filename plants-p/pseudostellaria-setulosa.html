Pseudostellaria setulosa

Pseudostellaria setulosa, commonly known as the hairy starwort, is a low-growing perennial herb native to temperate regions of Asia.

It features a basal rosette of narrow, hairy leaves and produces small, white to pale pink flowers arranged in clusters. The plant is characterized by its slender, branching stems and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and rocky areas. It has a fibrous root system and is adapted to thrive in well-drained soils.

Pseudostellaria setulosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
