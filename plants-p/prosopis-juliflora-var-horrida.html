Prosopis juliflora var. horrida

Prosopis juliflora var. horrida, commonly known as the screwbean mesquite, is a shrub or small tree native to the arid and semi-arid regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its twisted, thorny stems and small, bean-like pods that contain seeds. The plant has small, compound leaves that are adapted to reduce water loss in hot, dry climates. It produces yellowish-white flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds.

This species is known for its hardiness and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions, making it a common invasive species in some parts of the world.
