Physurus roseus var. angustioflius

Physurus roseus var. angustioflius, commonly known as the narrow-leafed roseum.

This variant is characterized by its slender, elongated leaves that are typically a vibrant green with a slight reddish tinge on the underside. It produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers during the spring and summer months. The plant is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in well-drained soil with full sun exposure.

It is a hardy perennial that is often used in rock gardens and as a decorative plant in coastal landscapes.
