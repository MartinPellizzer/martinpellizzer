Poivrea albiflora

Poivrea albiflora, commonly known as the white-flowered privet, is a deciduous shrub native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It features small, opposite leaves that are typically oval or elliptical in shape. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers in the spring, which are followed by dark blue berries in the fall. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.

Poivrea albiflora is also valued for its use in hedges and as a decorative plant in gardens.
