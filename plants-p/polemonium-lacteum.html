Polemonium lacteum

Polemonium lacteum, commonly known as the milkweed of the polemonium genus, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to North America.

It features basal leaves that are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, with a distinctive flowering stem that bears clusters of bell-shaped, white or pale pink flowers. The plant produces a milky sap, which is a characteristic feature of the genus. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Its flowers attract various pollinators, including bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance.
