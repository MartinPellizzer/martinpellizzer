Pseuderanthemum huegelii

Pseuderanthemum huegelii, commonly known as the mountain morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its trailing growth habit and vibrant, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of blue, purple, and white. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Pseuderanthemum huegelii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to grow in well-drained, sandy soils.
