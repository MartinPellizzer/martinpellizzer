Panicum divaricatum var. lanatum

Panicum divaricatum var. lanatum, commonly known as silky finger grass, is a perennial grass native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its soft, silky hairs that cover the leaves and stems, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as lawns, roadsides, and disturbed soils. Its inflorescence consists of a dense, spike-like cluster of small, spikelets that are often wind-pollinated.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping due to its fine texture and ability to thrive in various soil conditions.
