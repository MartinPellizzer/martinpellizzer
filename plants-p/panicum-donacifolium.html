Panicum donacifolium

Panicum donacifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaf panicgrass, is a species of grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats, such as marshes and swamps, where it thrives in moist, sandy soils. Its botanical features include a fibrous root system and a tall, erect stem that can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters.

It plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
