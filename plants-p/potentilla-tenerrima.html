Potentilla tenerrima

Potentilla tenerrima, commonly known as the silver potentilla, is a low-growing shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray leaves, which are covered in fine, dense hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellow, five-petaled flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Potentilla tenerrima is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
