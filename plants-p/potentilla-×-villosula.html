Potentilla × villosula

Potentilla × villosula, commonly known as the softleaf cinquefoil, is a hybrid shrub belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy foliage and produces clusters of yellow flowers in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It is native to regions of western North America, including parts of the United States and Canada.

Potentilla × villosula is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
