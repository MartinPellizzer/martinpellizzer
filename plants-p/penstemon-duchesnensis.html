Penstemon duchesnensis

Penstemon duchesnensis, commonly known as the Sierra penstemon, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils in open habitats such as meadows and slopes. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing drought tolerance and efficient water use.

The Sierra penstemon plays an important role in supporting pollinators, particularly bees and butterflies, due to its nectar-rich flowers.
