Phlomis woroschilovii

Phlomis woroschilovii, commonly known as the Woroschilov's mullein, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and large, woolly leaves. The plant produces showy, yellowish to orange flowers in spikes, which bloom in summer. It thrives in rocky or dry soils and is often found in alpine environments.

Phlomis woroschilovii is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
