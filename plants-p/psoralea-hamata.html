Psoralea hamata

Psoralea hamata, commonly known as the hairy psoralea, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, yellowish flowers and hairy foliage. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is often found in shrublands and grasslands. Its seeds are enclosed in pods and are adapted for dispersal by wind.

Psoralea hamata is also known for its medicinal properties, with some traditional uses in herbal remedies.
