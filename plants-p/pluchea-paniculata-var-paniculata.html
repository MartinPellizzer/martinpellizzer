Pluchea paniculata var. paniculata

Pluchea paniculata var. paniculata, commonly known as the panicled ironweed, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, characterized by its erect stem and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in paniculate inflorescences, which bloom in late summer to early autumn. Its foliage is often covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a slightly fuzzy appearance.

The species is known for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine for various ailments.
