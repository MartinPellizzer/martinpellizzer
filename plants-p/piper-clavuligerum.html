Piper clavuligerum

Piper clavuligerum, commonly known as the wild pepper or Mexican pepper plant, is a climbing shrub native to Central America and parts of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species. The plant is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into elongated, berry-like fruits. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in shaded, moist environments.

The plant is also noted for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional medicine for various health purposes.
