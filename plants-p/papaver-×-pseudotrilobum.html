Papaver × pseudotrilobum

Papaver × pseudotrilobum, commonly known as the Persian poppy, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Papaver rhoeas and Papaver orientale.

It is characterized by its striking, large, and brightly colored flowers, typically in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant is native to regions of Central Asia and the Middle East, where it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun. Its botanical features include smooth, green, lobed leaves and a tall, branching stem.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
