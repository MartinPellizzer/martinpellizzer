Papaver lapponicum subsp. occidentale

Papaver lapponicum subsp.

occidentale, commonly known as the western poppy, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the northern regions of North America. It is characterized by its showy, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and red, often with a distinctive yellow center.

The plant typically grows in open, rocky, or sandy habitats, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its leaves are palmately lobed and often have a slightly hairy texture, while its stems are usually unbranched and covered in fine hairs.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
