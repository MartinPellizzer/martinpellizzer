Phyllanthus scaber var. angustifolius

Phyllanthus scaber var. angustifolius, commonly known as narrow-leaved sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa and the Middle East.

It is a low-growing shrub characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and small, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, followed by small, round fruits that contain a single seed. It is adapted to survive in harsh environments with minimal water, making it a resilient species in desert ecosystems.

This plant is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.
