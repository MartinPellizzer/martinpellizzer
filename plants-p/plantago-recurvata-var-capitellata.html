Plantago recurvata var. capitellata

Plantago recurvata var. capitellata, commonly known as the clustered plantain, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It features a basal rosette of broad, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. The plant produces a spike of small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered at the top of the stem. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in wetlands, ditches, and along stream banks.

The species is valued for its medicinal properties and is sometimes used in herbal remedies.
