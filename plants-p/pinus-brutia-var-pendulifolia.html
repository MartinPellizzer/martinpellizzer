Pinus brutia var. pendulifolia

Pinus brutia var. pendulifolia, commonly known as the weeping Turkish pine, is a unique variant of the Turkish pine, characterized by its distinctive weeping habit.

It is a coniferous evergreen tree native to the eastern Mediterranean region, particularly Turkey and Cyprus. The plant features long, drooping branches that create a cascading effect, giving it an ornamental appearance. Its foliage consists of needle-like leaves that are dark green and slightly twisted.

This variety is valued for its aesthetic appeal in horticulture and landscape design.
