Panicum dimidiatum

Panicum dimidiatum, commonly known as the half-panic grass, is a flowering plant species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences that are often adorned with silky, hairy structures. It is typically found in moist soils near water bodies and is adapted to wetland environments.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and wetland restoration projects.
