Pabstiella pauloensis

Pabstiella pauloensis, commonly known as the Brazilian star orchid, is a rare epiphytic orchid native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, star-shaped flowers that bloom in a vibrant array of colors. The plant typically grows on trees or rocks, thriving in humid, shaded environments. Its roots are adapted to absorb moisture and nutrients from the air and rain.

Due to habitat loss, Pabstiella pauloensis is considered endangered, making conservation efforts essential for its survival.
