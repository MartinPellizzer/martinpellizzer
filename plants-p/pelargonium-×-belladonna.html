Pelargonium × belladonna

Pelargonium × belladonna, commonly known as the common geranium, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Geraniaceae family.

It is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that come in a variety of colors such as red, pink, and purple. The plant typically has heart-shaped leaves with a slightly serrated edge, and it thrives in well-drained soil and full sun. It is widely cultivated as an ornamental plant for gardens and container planting.

Pelargonium × belladonna is also known for its ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
