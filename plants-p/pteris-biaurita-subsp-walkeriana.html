Pteris biaurita subsp. walkeriana

Pteris biaurita subsp.

walkeriana, commonly known as the Walker's water fern, is a species of fern native to Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that are typically arching or pendulous.

The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its rhizomes are creeping and produce numerous sporangia on the undersides of the fronds.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in water gardens or as a houseplant.
