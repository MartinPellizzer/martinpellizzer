Poa trivialis var. angustifolia

Poa trivialis var. angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf meadow grass, is a variant of the common meadow grass.

It is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed areas such as meadows, roadsides, and pastures. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences.

Its botanical characteristics make it adaptable to a variety of soil types and environmental conditions.
