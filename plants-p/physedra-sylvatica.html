Physedra sylvatica

Physedra sylvatica, commonly known as the marsh holly.

It is a perennial herb native to wetland habitats in Europe and parts of Asia. The plant features slender, erect stems that are often covered in fine hairs. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and have a slightly waxy texture.

The small, inconspicuous flowers are typically greenish-white and appear in clusters, followed by bladder-like fruits that release seeds when disturbed.
