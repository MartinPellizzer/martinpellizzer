Phreatia vitellina

Phreatia vitellina, commonly known as the yellow lady's slipper, is a tropical orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically have a yellow or orange coloration with intricate patterns. The plant grows epiphytically on trees in humid, shaded environments, relying on rainfall for hydration. Its roots form a symbiotic relationship with mycorrhizal fungi, aiding in nutrient absorption.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
