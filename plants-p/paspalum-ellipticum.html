Paspalum ellipticum

Paspalum ellipticum, commonly known as slender grass or narrowleaf paspalum, is a perennial grass species native to the Americas.

It typically grows in wetland environments, including marshes and swampy areas, and is often found in regions with high humidity. The plant has slender, erect stems and narrow, elongated leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its small, spike-like inflorescence produces numerous tiny flowers, which are usually wind-pollinated.

Paspalum ellipticum is valued for its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife species.
