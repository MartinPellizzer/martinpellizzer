Primula farinosa subvar. albiflora

Primula farinosa subvar. albiflora, commonly known as the white-flowered primrose, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that are predominantly white, with some varieties exhibiting pale pink or lavender hues. The plant has hairy, broad leaves that are typically arranged in a basal rosette, and it thrives in cool, moist environments. It blooms in early spring, producing clusters of flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
