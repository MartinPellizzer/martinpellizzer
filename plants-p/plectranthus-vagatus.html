Plectranthus vagatus

Plectranthus vagatus, commonly known as the woolly plectranthus, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly gray-green foliage and produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from purple to blue. The plant is a member of the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and container planting.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its compact growth habit and long blooming season.
