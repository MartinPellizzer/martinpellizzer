Papaver pyrenaicum subsp. rhaeticum

Papaver pyrenaicum subsp.

rhaeticum, commonly known as the Rhaetic poppy, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It typically grows in alpine and subalpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils and sunny exposures.

The plant produces striking, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from pale pink to deep red, with a distinctive dark central blotch. Its leaves are palmately lobed and covered in a fine, silky pubescence.

The species is notable for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and resilience.
