Piper silvarum var. minus

Piper silvarum var. minus, commonly known as the lesser forest pepper, is a tropical climbing plant native to the rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces small, peppery fruits that are used in traditional medicine and as a spice in some regions. Its growth habit involves twining around tree trunks and branches, making it a common understory plant in humid, shaded environments.

The botanical features of this variety include its aromatic foliage and the presence of piperine-like compounds in its fruits.
