Polypodium cucullatum

Polypodium cucullatum, commonly known as the shield fern, is a species of fern native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its distinctive, shield-shaped fronds that are often covered in a dense layer of fine, silky hairs. The plant typically grows on tree trunks or rocks in moist, shaded environments. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The shield fern is valued both for its ornamental appearance and its ecological role in forest ecosystems.
