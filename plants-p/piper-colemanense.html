Piper colemanense

Piper colemanense, commonly known as Coleman's pepper, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species such as black pepper. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves and small, greenish-white flowers that grow in clusters. Its fruit is a small, fleshy berry that contains several seeds.

Despite its botanical interest, it is not widely cultivated for commercial use.
