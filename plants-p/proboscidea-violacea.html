Proboscidea violacea

Proboscidea violacea, commonly known as the violet morning glory, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and striking violet to purple flowers that bloom in the morning. The plant belongs to the Convolvulaceae family, which includes other climbing vines. Its flowers are typically trumpet-shaped, with five petals that open in response to sunlight.
