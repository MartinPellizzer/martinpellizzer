Podanthum persicum var. asperum

Podanthum persicum var. asperum, commonly known as the Persian sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Iran.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of small, delicate blue or purple flowers. The leaves are typically narrow and covered in fine hairs, giving them a rough texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
