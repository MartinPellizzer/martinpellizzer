Polygonum aviculare proles monspeliense

Polygonum aviculare proles monspeliense, commonly known as the common knotgrass, is a low-growing annual herb belonging to the Polygonaceae family.

It is characterized by its prostrate or ascending stems that are often hairy and bear small, triangular leaves. The plant produces clusters of tiny, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences called scorpioid cymes. It thrives in disturbed soils and is commonly found in fields, roadsides, and waste areas.

Its seeds are dispersed by wind, contributing to its widespread distribution across temperate regions.
