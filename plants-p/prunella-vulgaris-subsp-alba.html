Prunella vulgaris subsp. alba

Prunella vulgaris subsp.

alba, commonly known as white dead-nettle, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa. It features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are hairy and have a distinctive white or pale underside.

The plant produces small, two-lipped flowers in shades of pink to white, typically arranged in whorls along the stems. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

White dead-nettle is often found in disturbed soils and is known for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal remedies.
