Phyllodes mackoyana

Phyllodes mackoyana, commonly known as the Mackoy's wattle, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and nitrogen-fixing ability. The plant features phyllodes, which are flattened, leaf-like stems that perform photosynthesis. It produces yellow flowers in late winter to early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds.

The species is adapted to arid conditions and is often found in sandy soils.
