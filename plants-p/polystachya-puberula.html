Polystachya puberula

Polystachya puberula, commonly known as the hairy polystachya, is a species of orchid native to tropical regions of Africa and southern Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and dense clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories or rocky crevices. Its leaves are usually covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive textured appearance.

Polystachya puberula is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
