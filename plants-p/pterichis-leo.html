Pterichis leo

Pterichis leo, commonly known as the lion's tail, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is characterized by its distinctive, pendulous clusters of orange-yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are arranged in a spiral pattern around the stem. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Its unique appearance and ornamental value make it a notable species in horticulture.
