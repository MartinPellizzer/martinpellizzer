Pulsatilla aurea

Pulsatilla aurea, commonly known as the showy pulsatilla, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow, often with a purple or brownish tinge at the base. The plant produces a distinctive, spiny, and hairy fruit known as a follicle, which contains the seeds. Its leaves are palmately divided, with a soft, hairy texture, and it thrives in well-drained, rocky soils.

Pulsatilla aurea is often found in alpine meadows and is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance.
