Pseudalepyrum minimum

Pseudalepyrum minimum, commonly known as the dwarf pseudalepyrum, is a small shrub native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense, evergreen foliage. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink, which bloom in spring. Its leaves are narrow and leathery, adapted to withstand harsh environmental conditions.

Pseudalepyrum minimum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and resilience in rocky soils.
