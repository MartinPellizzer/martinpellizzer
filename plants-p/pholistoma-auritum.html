Pholistoma auritum

Pholistoma auritum, commonly known as the ear-shaped liverwort, is a small, terrestrial liverwort belonging to the family Porellaceae.

It is characterized by its rosette-like growth habit and has leafy shoots that are often clustered at the base. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest floors or on rocky substrates.

This plant is known for its simple morphology and is used in ecological studies to assess environmental conditions.
