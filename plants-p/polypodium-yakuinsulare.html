Polypodium yakuinsulare

Polypodium yakuinsulare, commonly known as the Yaku Island polypody, is a species of fern native to Japan.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its delicate, leathery fronds. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The fronds are usually bipinnate, with a distinct, glossy appearance that aids in its identification.
