Pinus scipioniformis

Pinus scipioniformis, commonly known as the Spanish stone pine, is a species of conifer native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its slow growth and distinctive, thick, scaly bark that provides protection against fire and extreme weather. The plant produces long, slender cones that hang from branches, containing edible seeds known as pine nuts. Its needles are dark green and arranged in bundles of five, contributing to its ornamental value.

This species plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, supporting various wildlife species.
