Phalaenopsis sanderiana var. alba

Phalaenopsis sanderiana var. alba, commonly known as the White Sander Orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its elongated, lance-shaped leaves and its striking white flowers with a subtle pinkish hue. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, long-lasting blooms that can remain open for several months. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires moderate humidity and well-draining potting media.

This variety is prized for its elegance and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.
