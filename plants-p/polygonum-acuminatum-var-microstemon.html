Polygonum acuminatum var. microstemon

Polygonum acuminatum var. microstemon, commonly known as the small-stamen smartweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist or wet habitats such as marshes, ditches, and stream banks. The plant has alternate, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small and clustered in spike-like inflorescences, with distinct stamens that give it its specific epithet.

It is a member of the Polygonaceae family, known for its distinctive nodes and often edible seeds.
