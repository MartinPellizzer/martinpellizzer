Persicaria belophylla

Persicaria belophylla, commonly known as the silverleaf knotweed, is a herbaceous perennial native to eastern Asia.

It features upright, slender stems that are often covered in a silvery-white pubescence, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or purple, blooming in late summer. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a distinctive, slightly fuzzy texture.

Persicaria belophylla is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and can thrive in moist, shady environments.
