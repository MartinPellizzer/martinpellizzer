Potentilla andersonii

Potentilla andersonii, commonly known as Anderson's cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a low-growing perennial shrub that typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features small, five-petaled yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are arranged in a spiral pattern along the stems and are typically oval to elliptical in shape.

Potentilla andersonii is adapted to rocky and dry habitats, often found in alpine or subalpine regions.
