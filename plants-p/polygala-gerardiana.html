Polygala gerardiana

Polygala gerardiana, commonly known as the Gerard's milk-vetch, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Polygalaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, delicate flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and thrives in arid or semi-arid environments. Its flowers are usually pale green or yellowish, and it produces small, hairy pods that contain seeds.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
