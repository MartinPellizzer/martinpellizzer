Polygonum patulum subsp. pulchellum

Polygonum patulum subsp.

pulchellum, commonly known as the slender knotweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the eastern United States. It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and stream banks.

The plant has slender, branching stems that are often reddish in color, and its leaves are alternate, lanceolate, and covered in fine hairs. It produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters, which are pollinated by insects.

The species is characterized by its rapid growth and ability to thrive in nutrient-rich, waterlogged soils.
