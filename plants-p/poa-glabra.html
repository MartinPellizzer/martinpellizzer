Poa glabra

Poa glabra, commonly known as the smooth meadow grass, is a species of grass native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is a perennial plant that typically grows in open, disturbed areas such as meadows, fields, and along roadsides. The plant has slender, leafy stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are smooth to the touch, as indicated by its scientific name. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences containing numerous tiny flowers.

Poa glabra is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support local wildlife.
