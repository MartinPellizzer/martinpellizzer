Phyllogonum

Phyllogonum, commonly known as the leaf-gonad plant, is a unique species characterized by its distinctive botanical features.

It possesses elongated, spirally arranged leaves that are often covered in a fine, waxy cuticle. The plant's most notable feature is its reproductive structure, which resembles a cluster of leaf-like appendages surrounding the flower. These structures are not true leaves but modified floral organs that play a role in pollination.

Phyllogonum thrives in moist, shaded environments and is native to specific regions in Southeast Asia.
