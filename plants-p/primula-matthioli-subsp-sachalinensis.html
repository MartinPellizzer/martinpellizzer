Primula matthioli subsp. sachalinensis

Primula matthioli subsp.

sachalinensis, commonly known as the Sakhalin primrose, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Russian Far East and northeastern China. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and rocky areas.

The plant features oval, dark green leaves and produces clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink and white. Its floral structure is characteristic of the Primulaceae family, with five petals and a prominent corona.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
