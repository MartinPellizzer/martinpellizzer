Potentilla moesiaca

Potentilla moesiaca, commonly known as the Carpathian cinquefoil, is a perennial shrub native to the Carpathian Mountains.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its dense, low-growing habit with multiple stems. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, typically from June to July. Its leaves are palmately divided, with five to seven lobes, and are covered in fine hairs.

Potentilla moesiaca thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments.
