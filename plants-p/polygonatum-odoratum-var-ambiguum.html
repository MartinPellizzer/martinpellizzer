Polygonatum odoratum var. ambiguum

Polygonatum odoratum var. ambiguum, commonly known as the fragrant Solomon's seal, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern Asia.

It features slender, arching stems that bear clusters of small, greenish-white flowers with six petals. The plant produces red berries that are edible but typically not consumed by humans. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged alternately along the stem.

It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in woodland areas.
