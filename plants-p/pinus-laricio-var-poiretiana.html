Pinus laricio var. poiretiana

Pinus laricio var. poiretiana, commonly known as the Corsican pine, is a variant of the Corsican pine species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a coniferous evergreen tree characterized by its slender, straight trunk and needle-like leaves. The cones are typically small and woody, containing seeds that are dispersed by wind. This variety is adapted to rocky and mountainous terrain, thriving in well-drained soils.

Its botanical features make it an important component of the Mediterranean forest ecosystems.
