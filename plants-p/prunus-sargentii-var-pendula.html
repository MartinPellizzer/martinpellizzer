Prunus sargentii var. pendula

Prunus sargentii var. pendula, commonly known as the weeping cherry or weeping almond, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It is characterized by its pendulous branches that create a cascading effect, giving it a distinctive, ornamental appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in early spring, typically pink or white, which are followed by small, edible drupes. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

Its botanical features make it both aesthetically pleasing and ecologically valuable.
