Potamogeton dunicola

Potamogeton dunicola, commonly known as the dune pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to North America.

It typically grows in shallow, freshwater environments such as lakes, ponds, and slow-moving streams. The plant has long, narrow leaves that are arranged in a spiral pattern along its stem. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often submerged or just at the water's surface.

Potamogeton dunicola plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
