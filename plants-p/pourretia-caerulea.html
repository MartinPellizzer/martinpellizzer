Pourretia caerulea

Pourretia caerulea, commonly known as the blue star, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking blue-violet flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and prefers partial to full shade. Its foliage consists of opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence.

Pourretia caerulea is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in shaded conditions.
