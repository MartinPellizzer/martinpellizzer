Polypodium panamense

Polypodium panamense, commonly known as the Panamanian holly fern, is a species of polypodioid fern native to Central and South America.

It typically grows in humid, shaded environments such as forests and rocky crevices. The plant has a rhizomatous growth habit, with fronds that are pinnately compound and often have a leathery texture. Its spores are produced on the undersides of the fronds in sporangia clusters.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
