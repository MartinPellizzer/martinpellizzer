Papaver robustum

Papaver robustum, commonly known as the robust poppy, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its striking red or orange petals that bloom in late summer. The plant produces a capsule fruit that contains numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a hardy species in temperate climates.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
