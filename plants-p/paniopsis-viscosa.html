Paniopsis viscosa

Paniopsis viscosa, commonly known as the sticky paniopsis, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its dense, glandular hairs that give its stems and leaves a sticky texture. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale green in color. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and plant enthusiasts alike.
