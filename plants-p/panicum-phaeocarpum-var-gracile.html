Panicum phaeocarpum var. gracile

Panicum phaeocarpum var. gracile, commonly known as slender panicgrass, is a grass species native to parts of Asia and Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats, such as marshes and along waterways. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green coloration.

It is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for wildlife.
