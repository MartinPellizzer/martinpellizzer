Pleurothallis sarcopetala

Pleurothallis sarcopetala, commonly known as the rock orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the Andes of South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and small, often pale green or yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or disturbed soils, thriving in high-altitude environments. Its pseudobulbs are short and stout, supporting the plant's survival in harsh conditions.

The species is valued for its unique appearance and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.
