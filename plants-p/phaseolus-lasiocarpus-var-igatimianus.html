Phaseolus lasiocarpus var. igatimianus

Phaseolus lasiocarpus var. igatimianus, commonly known as the Iguatimian runner bean, is a climbing legume native to South America.

It features compound leaves with three leaflets and produces elongated, hairy pods that contain edible seeds. The plant is characterized by its vigorous growth habit and ability to thrive in tropical and subtropical climates. Its flowers are typically white or pale purple, and it is cultivated for both its ornamental value and edible beans.

The variety is notable for its unique pod morphology and adaptability to different soil conditions.
