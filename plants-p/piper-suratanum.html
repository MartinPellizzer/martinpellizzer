Piper suratanum

Piper suratanum, commonly known as Suratanum pepper or Indian pepper, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species like black pepper. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that develop into elongated, reddish berries. The berries are used in traditional medicine and as a spice, valued for their pungent flavor.

Its botanical structure includes opposite, ovate leaves and a woody stem with adventitious roots.
