Pyrenaria grandiflora

Pyrenaria grandiflora, commonly known as the large-flowered pyrenaria, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, large, showy flowers that typically bloom in the spring. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical significance lies in its unique floral structure and ecological role in supporting local pollinators.
