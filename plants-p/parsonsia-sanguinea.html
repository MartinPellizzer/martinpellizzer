Parsonsia sanguinea

Parsonsia sanguinea, commonly known as the red-flowered bellflower, is a species of flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia and the Pacific.

It is characterized by its striking red or orange tubular flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as birds and insects. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, with lance-shaped leaves that are opposite in arrangement. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release seeds, and it thrives in well-drained soils with full sun exposure.

Parsonsia sanguinea is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
