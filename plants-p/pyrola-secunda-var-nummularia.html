Pyrola secunda var. nummularia

Pyrola secunda var. nummularia, commonly known as the pennywhistle, is a low-growing evergreen herbaceous plant native to the northern regions of North America.

It features broad, leathery, waxy leaves that are typically oval or rounded in shape. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are usually pink or white, blooming in early spring. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in coniferous or mixed forests.

This variety is notable for its distinctive leaf morphology and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in shady gardens.
