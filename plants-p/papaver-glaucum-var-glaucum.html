Papaver glaucum var. glaucum

Papaver glaucum var. glaucum, commonly known as the blue poppy, is a striking perennial herb native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating, giving them a silvery appearance. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and botanical collections worldwide.
