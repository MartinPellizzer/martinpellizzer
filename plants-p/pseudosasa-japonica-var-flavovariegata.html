Pseudosasa japonica var. flavovariegata

Pseudosasa japonica var. flavovariegata, commonly known as the golden variegated bamboo, is a striking ornamental grass native to East Asia.

It features slender, upright culms with a distinctive yellow-green variegation pattern along the leaf blades. The plant typically grows in clumps, reaching heights of up to 2 meters, and thrives in partial shade to full sun. Its foliage remains fresh and vibrant throughout the growing season, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

It is relatively low maintenance and resistant to pests and diseases, making it an excellent addition to both residential and commercial settings.
