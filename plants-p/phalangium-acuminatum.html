Phalangium acuminatum

Phalangium acuminatum, commonly known as the slender harvestman, is a type of arachnid belonging to the family Phalangodidae.

It is characterized by its elongated, slender body and long legs, which are often longer than its body. This species is typically found in moist, humid environments such as under rocks, logs, or in leaf litter. Its botanical associations are limited, as it does not interact directly with plants in a photosynthetic or symbiotic manner.

Instead, it plays a role in the decomposition of organic matter, contributing to soil health in its habitat.
