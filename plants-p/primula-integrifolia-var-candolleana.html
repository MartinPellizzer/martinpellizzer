Primula integrifolia var. candolleana

Primula integrifolia var. candolleana, commonly known as the Candolleana primrose, is a species of flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of western China.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of bell-shaped, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow. The leaves are typically broad, heart-shaped, and have a smooth, glossy surface. This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.

It prefers cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows at high elevations.
