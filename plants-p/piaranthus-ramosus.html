Piaranthus ramosus

Piaranthus ramosus, commonly known as the ramose pigmyweed, is a small, flowering plant native to arid regions of southern Africa.

It typically grows close to the ground, with a compact, mat-like growth habit. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, often during the dry season. Its leaves are fleshy and succulent, adapted to store water in its native environment.

Piaranthus ramosus is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is well-suited to drought conditions.
