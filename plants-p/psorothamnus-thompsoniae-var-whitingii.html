Psorothamnus thompsoniae var. whitingii

Psorothamnus thompsoniae var. whitingii, commonly known as Thompson's greasewood, is a shrub native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its small, needle-like leaves and yellowish-green flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant is adapted to harsh, dry environments and has a deep root system to access groundwater. It plays a role in stabilizing soils in desert ecosystems.

Its botanical features make it an important component of desert flora.
