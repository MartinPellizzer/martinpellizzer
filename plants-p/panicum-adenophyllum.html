Panicum adenophyllum

Panicum adenophyllum, commonly known as the silver panicum, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is a member of the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its upright growth habit and distinctive silver-gray foliage. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, two-lobed flowers. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, often found in open habitats such as prairies and disturbed areas.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and restoration projects.
