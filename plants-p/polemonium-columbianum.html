Polemonium columbianum

Polemonium columbianum, commonly known as the Columbia bluebell, is a herbaceous perennial native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It features delicate, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its flowers attract pollinators like bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance in its native habitat.
