Potentilla nitida var. edentata

Potentilla nitida var. edentata, commonly known as the smooth cinquefoil, is a low-growing shrub native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It features small, rounded leaves that are typically smooth or slightly hairy, depending on the climate. The plant produces clusters of bright yellow flowers in early summer, which are attractive to pollinators. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or alpine settings.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental flowers make it a popular choice for gardeners seeking hardy, drought-tolerant plants.
