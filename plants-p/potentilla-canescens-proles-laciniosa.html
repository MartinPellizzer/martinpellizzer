Potentilla canescens proles laciniosa

Potentilla canescens proles laciniosa, commonly known as the silky cinquefoil, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its finely hairy, silver-gray leaves and clusters of small, yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows in alpine or subalpine habitats, often on rocky slopes or in meadows. It has a compact growth habit and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.

The species is also noted for its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions and its resistance to harsh environmental conditions.
