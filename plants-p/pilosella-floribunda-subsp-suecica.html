Pilosella floribunda subsp. suecica

Pilosella floribunda subsp.

suecica, commonly known as the Swedish pearly everlasting, is a flowering plant native to northern Europe. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense foliage.

The plant produces small, daisy-like flowers with white petals and a yellow center, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and grassy slopes. Pilosella floribunda subsp.

suecica is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in floral arrangements.
