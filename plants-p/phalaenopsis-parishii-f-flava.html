Phalaenopsis parishii f. flava

Phalaenopsis parishii f. flava, commonly known as the yellow phalaenopsis, is a rare and striking orchid species native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its elongated, lance-shaped leaves and its distinctive yellow flowers with a subtle greenish tinge. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to tree trunks or branches in its natural habitat. It produces clusters of fragrant, long-lasting blooms that can remain open for several weeks.

Due to its rarity and ornamental value, it is highly sought after by orchid enthusiasts and conservationists alike.
