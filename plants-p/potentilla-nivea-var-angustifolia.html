Potentilla nivea var. angustifolia

Potentilla nivea var. angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf white cinquefoil, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its slender, angular stems and small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The leaves are narrow and serrated, typically arranged in a spiral pattern along the stems. This plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in alpine and subalpine environments.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in stabilizing soil in rocky habitats.
