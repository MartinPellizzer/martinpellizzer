Pilea capitata

Pilea capitata, commonly known as the Chinese money plant, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to China.

It features rosettes of thick, fleshy, paddle-shaped leaves that are typically green with a reddish tinge on the underside. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers in late spring, followed by spherical, red berries that contain seeds. It thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular indoor plant.

Its unique foliage and ease of care contribute to its popularity among plant enthusiasts.
