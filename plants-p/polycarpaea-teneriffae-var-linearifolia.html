Polycarpaea teneriffae var. linearifolia

Polycarpaea teneriffae var. linearifolia, commonly known as the Canary Islands false mallow, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is characterized by its slender, linear leaves and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to the island's Mediterranean climate. It is often found in coastal and mountainous regions, where it contributes to local biodiversity.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species.
