Penstemon laetus subsp. sagittatus

Penstemon laetus subsp.

sagittatus, commonly known as the arrowleaf beardtongue, is a perennial herb native to the western United States. It is characterized by its lanceolate, arrow-shaped leaves and spikes of violet to purple flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils in open habitats such as meadows and slopes. It is well adapted to full sun and has a compact, upright growth habit.

Its flowers attract pollinators like bees and butterflies, making it a valuable addition to native plant gardens.
