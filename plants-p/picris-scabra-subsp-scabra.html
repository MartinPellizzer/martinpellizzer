Picris scabra subsp. scabra

Picris scabra subsp.

scabra, commonly known as common sandwort, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family. It is a low-growing perennial herb characterized by its narrow, linear leaves and small yellow flowers arranged in composite heads.

Native to Europe, it thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in disturbed habitats. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
