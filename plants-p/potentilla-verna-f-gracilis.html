Potentilla verna f. gracilis

Potentilla verna f. gracilis, commonly known as the slender creeping cinquefoil, is a flowering plant belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is a low-growing perennial shrub that typically reaches heights of 5 to 15 centimeters. The plant features slender, wiry stems that form a dense mat, often spreading across rocky or gravelly ground. Its small, white to pale yellow flowers appear in clusters during late spring to early summer.

The leaves are compound, consisting of five oval-shaped leaflets, and are covered in fine hairs, giving the plant a distinctive, rugged appearance.
