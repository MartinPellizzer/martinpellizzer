Platanthera extremiorientalis

Platanthera extremiorientalis, commonly known as the Eastern Asian lady's tresses, is a perennial orchid native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, spreading inflorescence of small, white to pale pink flowers that resemble a tresses of hair. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and rocky crevices. Its roots form a symbiotic relationship with mycorrhizal fungi, which aid in nutrient absorption.

The species is noted for its delicate appearance and is often found in temperate and subtropical regions.
