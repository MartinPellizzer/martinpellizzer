Polyalthia capillata

Polyalthia capillata, commonly known as the silky tree or silk-cotton tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, pale grey bark and large, ovate leaves with a velvety texture. The plant produces clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its fruit is a woody capsule containing fluffy, cotton-like seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Polyalthia capillata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
