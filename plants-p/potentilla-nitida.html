Potentilla nitida

Potentilla nitida, commonly known as silverweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its finely hairy, silver-gray foliage that reflects light, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges.

Potentilla nitida is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping for its drought tolerance and low maintenance requirements.
