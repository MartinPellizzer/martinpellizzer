Phyllanthus caudatus var. caudatus

Phyllanthus caudatus var. caudatus, commonly known as the cat's tail plant, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, elongated, cat-like leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, followed by red, fleshy fruits that are edible. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional medicinal uses in some cultures.
