Prunus × lannesiana f. praecox

Prunus × lannesiana f. praecox, commonly known as the early-flowering cherry, is a compact, ornamental shrub or small tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its profuse display of delicate, single or double pink to white flowers that appear early in the spring, often before the leaves emerge. The foliage is dark green and glossy, providing a striking contrast to the blossoms. This variety is prized for its ornamental value in gardens and public spaces.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it adaptable to a variety of landscape conditions.
