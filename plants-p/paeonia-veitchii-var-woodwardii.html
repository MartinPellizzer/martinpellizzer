Paeonia veitchii var. woodwardii

Paeonia veitchii var. woodwardii, commonly known as the Woodward peony, is a deciduous perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of China.

It is characterized by its large, showy, double-flowered blooms that range in color from white to deep pink. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with thick, fleshy rhizomes that store nutrients. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces distinctive, spiny capsules as fruit.

The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
