Potentilla recta var. pedatifrons

Potentilla recta var. pedatifrons, commonly known as the narrowleaf cinquefoil, is a perennial shrub native to the western United States.

It features small, five-petaled yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant has slender, branched stems and hairy leaves with a distinctive narrow, elongated shape. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or dry habitats.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable plant for ecological restoration and ornamental gardening.
