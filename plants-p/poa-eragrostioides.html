Poa eragrostioides

Poa eragrostioides, commonly known as the narrowleaf bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern South America.

It typically grows in open habitats such as grasslands, meadows, and disturbed areas. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves with a bluish-green hue, and it produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It is well adapted to temperate climates and can thrive in a variety of soil types.

Poa eragrostioides is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its hardiness and ability to stabilize soil.
