Phyllodium insigne

Phyllodium insigne, commonly known as the golden wattle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically oval and slightly hairy. The plant produces yellow flowers in late summer, which are clustered in spherical heads. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal regions.

Phyllodium insigne is a symbol of Australia and is widely cultivated for its ornamental value.
