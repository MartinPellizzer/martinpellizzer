Pseudocherleria imbricata

Pseudocherleria imbricata, commonly known as the narrow-leafed chenopod, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its small, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in dry environments. The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in spring. Its fibrous roots enable it to thrive in poor soils and withstand drought conditions.

Pseudocherleria imbricata plays a role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for native wildlife.
