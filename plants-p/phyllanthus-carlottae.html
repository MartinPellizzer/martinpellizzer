Phyllanthus carlottae

Phyllanthus carlottae, commonly known as the Carolinian spurge, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or wetland habitats. The plant features opposite, elliptic leaves with a smooth margin and produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters. Its fruits are capsules that release tiny seeds when mature.

The plant is characterized by its simple, unbranched stems and is often found in shaded or partially shaded environments.
