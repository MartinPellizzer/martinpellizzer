Pleuropteropyrum Iturupense

Iturup sandwort, scientifically known as Pleuropteropyrum iturupense, is a rare and unique flowering plant native to the remote islands of the Russian Far East. This small, low-growing species thrives in harsh coastal environments, where it has adapted to strong winds and saline conditions. Its delicate white flowers bloom briefly during the summer months, making it a striking yet elusive sight in its natural habitat. Despite its beauty, the plant remains little studied due to its limited distribution and challenging growing conditions. Conservation efforts are crucial to protect this rare species from the threats of climate change and habitat loss.
