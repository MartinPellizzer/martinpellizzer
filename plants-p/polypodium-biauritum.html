Polypodium biauritum

Polypodium biauritum, commonly known as the two-auricle fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Polypodiaceae.

It is characterized by its distinctive fronds that have two prominent auricles, or ear-like structures, at the base. Native to tropical and subtropical regions, this fern thrives in humid, shaded environments. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The plant's intricate morphology makes it a subject of interest in both botanical research and horticultural cultivation.
