Pycnandra fastuosa

Pycnandra fastuosa, commonly known as the golden shower tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in summer, giving it its common name. The plant has opposite, simple, and often hairy leaves that are elliptical in shape. Its bark is smooth and pale, and it produces small, round, yellowish fruits.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
