Peperomia subemarginulata

Peperomia subemarginulata, commonly known as the silver mohair plant, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery-white or grayish pubescence, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers that are typically hidden beneath the foliage. It thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Due to its slow growth and low maintenance requirements, it is well-suited for container cultivation.
