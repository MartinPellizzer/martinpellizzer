Polygonum biaristatum

Polygonum biaristatum, commonly known as the narrowleaf smartweed, is a herbaceous plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and lance-shaped leaves that are typically hairy along the edges. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually pink or red, blooming from late summer to early fall. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in wetlands, ditches, and along stream banks.

Polygonum biaristatum is a member of the Polygonaceae family, known for its distinctive bracts and seeds that are dispersed by wind.
