Piper chanekii

Piper chanekii, commonly known as the New Guinea pepper vine, is a climbing shrub native to the lowland forests of New Guinea.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many species of pepper plants. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are typically clustered in spikes, followed by small, round berries. Its leaves are heart-shaped with a glossy surface and are often used in traditional medicine.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has potential medicinal properties.
