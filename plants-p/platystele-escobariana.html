Platystele escobariana

Platystele escobariana, commonly known as the Escobaria marsh orchid, is a rare and specialized orchid species native to the humid cloud forests of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that typically bloom in the wet season. The plant is epiphytic, meaning it grows on the surface of trees or other plants without deriving nutrients from them. Its roots are adapted to absorb moisture and nutrients from the air and rain, which is crucial for its survival in its high-altitude habitat.

Due to habitat loss and limited distribution, Platystele escobariana is considered endangered and is of significant interest to botanists and conservationists.
