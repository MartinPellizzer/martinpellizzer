Perrottetia wichmaniorum

Perrottetia wichmaniorum, commonly known as the mountain hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It is characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 6 meters. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and often have a rough texture.

Perrottetia wichmaniorum thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to tropical and subtropical climates.
