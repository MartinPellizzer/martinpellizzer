Polygonum dolichopodum

Polygonum dolichopodum, commonly known as the long-footed knotweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, which is characterized by its distinctive nodes and often triangular stems. The plant features small, clustered greenish-white flowers that bloom in late summer, and its leaves are typically lance-shaped with a rough texture. Polygonum dolichopodum is adapted to grow in moist, disturbed soils and can be found in meadows and along stream banks.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
