Phacelia heterophylla subsp. virgata

Phacelia heterophylla subsp.

virgata, commonly known as the Sierra phacelia, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its variable leaf morphology, with leaves ranging from lance-shaped to ovate, depending on the growth environment.

The plant produces clusters of small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and restoration projects.
