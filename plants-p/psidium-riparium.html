Psidium riparium

Psidium riparium, commonly known as the river guava, is a tropical shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are typically elliptical or ovate in shape. The plant produces small, white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by edible, yellow or orange berries. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils near water bodies, making it a common species in riparian habitats.

The fruit is often used in local cuisines and has a mild, sweet flavor.
