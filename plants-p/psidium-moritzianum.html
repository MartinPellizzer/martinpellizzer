Psidium moritzianum

Psidium moritzianum, commonly known as the Moritz's guava, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its fragrant, white flowers and edible, yellow or red berries. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments and thrives in well-drained, loamy soil. Its leaves are alternate, elliptical, and have a glossy surface, while its fruit is often used in culinary applications.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential economic value in local markets.
