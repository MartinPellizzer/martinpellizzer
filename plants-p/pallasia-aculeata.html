Pallasia aculeata

Pallasia aculeata, commonly known as the prickly pears, is a flowering plant belonging to the Cactaceae family.

It is characterized by its spiny stems and clusters of vibrant, colorful flowers that bloom in the summer. Native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico, it thrives in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant produces small, edible fruits that are often used in local cuisines.

Its structure and adaptations make it well-suited for survival in harsh desert conditions.
