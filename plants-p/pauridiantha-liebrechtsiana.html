Pauridiantha liebrechtsiana

Pauridiantha liebrechtsiana, commonly known as the Cape orchid, is a rare and endemic species native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its unique floral structure includes a spurred lip, which is a distinguishing feature of many orchid species.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a conservation concern.
