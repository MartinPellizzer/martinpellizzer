Polygala tenuifolia var. uncinata

Polygala tenuifolia var. uncinata, commonly known as the Chinese milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to China.

It is characterized by its slender, often hairy stems and small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters. The plant has compound leaves with thread-like leaflets, and its roots are used in traditional Chinese medicine. It thrives in dry, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or mountainous regions.

The species is valued for its medicinal properties and is also used in ornamental gardening.
