Pithecellobium standleyi

Pithecellobium standleyi, commonly known as the yellow velvet tamarind, is a shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its leguminous properties and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant produces distinctive yellow or orange flowers followed by pod-like fruits containing edible seeds. Its leaves are trifoliate, with small, oval-shaped leaflets, and the bark is often covered in a thick, corky layer.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in some indigenous communities.
