Polygonum chanetii

Polygonum chanetii, commonly known as the red-rooted knotweed, is a perennial herb native to the northeastern United States.

It is characterized by its upright stems that are often reddish in color, especially near the base. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in dense clusters during the summer months. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture.

Polygonum chanetii is often found in moist, disturbed soils along streambanks and in wetlands.
