Phoradendron heredianum

It lacks chlorophyll and relies on its host for water and nutrients, while producing its own carbohydrates through photosynthesis using its small, scale-like leaves. The plant's berries are often consumed by birds, which then disperse the seeds to new hosts. Its life cycle involves a complex relationship with both its host and the animals that aid in its propagation.

Phoradendron heredianum plays a significant ecological role in forest ecosystems, contributing to biodiversity through its interactions with other organisms.
