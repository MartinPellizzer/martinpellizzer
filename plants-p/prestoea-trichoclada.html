Prestoea trichoclada

Prestoea trichoclada, commonly known as the white mahoe, is a tropical tree native to the Caribbean and northern South America.

It is characterized by its smooth, pale bark and large, palmate leaves that are typically bright green and leathery. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by elongated, woody fruits containing numerous seeds. Its wood is highly valued for its durability and fine grain, making it important in local economies.

The plant plays a significant role in the ecosystems of its native habitats, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
