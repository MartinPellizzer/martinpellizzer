Plumeria lancifolia var. microphylla

Plumeria lancifolia var. microphylla, commonly known as the small-leaf frangipani, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and showy, fragrant flowers that come in shades of yellow, orange, and pink. The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is well-suited to warm, humid climates. It thrives in full sun and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

The flowers are typically produced in the summer months, adding a striking visual element to gardens and landscapes.
