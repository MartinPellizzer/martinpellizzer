Parilia

Parilia, commonly known as the red sandalwood, is a small evergreen tree native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dark, aromatic wood that is highly valued for its durability and rich color. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape and often have a glossy surface. It produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by small, round fruits.

The tree is often cultivated for its ornamental value and for the production of essential oils.
