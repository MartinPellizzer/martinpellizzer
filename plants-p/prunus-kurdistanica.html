Prunus kurdistanica

Prunus kurdistanica, commonly known as the Kurdistan cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the mountainous regions of Turkey, Iran, and parts of the Caucasus.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its simple, ovate leaves and white or pinkish flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, sweet, edible fruits similar to cherries, which are often consumed fresh or used in local cuisines.

Prunus kurdistanica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
