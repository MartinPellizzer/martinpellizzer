Phaseolus oblongus var. zebrinus

Phaseolus oblongus var. zebrinus, commonly known as the zebra bean, is a tropical legume native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, narrow, and elongated pods that display a striking zebra-like pattern of alternating light and dark green stripes. The plant produces small, white or pale purple flowers and has compound leaves with trifoliate structures. The seeds are typically small, round, and range in color from white to yellow or brown.

It is valued for its edible beans, which are rich in protein and often used in traditional cuisines.
