Phyllanthus moi

Phyllanthus moi, commonly known as the African butterbean, is a shrub native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are often glossy and elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers clustered in inflorescences, followed by fruit capsules that release tiny, round seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in savannah and woodland habitats.

The plant is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional African medicine.
