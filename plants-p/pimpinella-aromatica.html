Pimpinella aromatica

Pimpinella aromatica, commonly known as bishop's weed, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant produces small, oval seeds that are often used in culinary and medicinal applications. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable choice for gardens and herb collections.

Pimpinella aromatica is also valued for its aromatic properties and historical use in traditional medicine.
