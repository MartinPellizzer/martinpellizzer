Phyllanthus salviifolius var. genuinus

Phyllanthus salviifolius var. genuinus, commonly known as the slender leaf phyllanthus, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It features small, opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in axillary clusters, followed by yellowish fruits that are capsules. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories or along stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable species for ecological studies and potential medicinal applications.
