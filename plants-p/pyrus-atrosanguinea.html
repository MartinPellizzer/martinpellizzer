Pyrus atrosanguinea

Pyrus atrosanguinea, commonly known as the black blood pear, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, including parts of China and the Russian Far East. The plant is characterized by its dark purple to black fruit, which is covered in a thick, waxy coating. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a bluish-green hue with hairy undersides.

Pyrus atrosanguinea is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been cultivated for its unique fruit appearance.
