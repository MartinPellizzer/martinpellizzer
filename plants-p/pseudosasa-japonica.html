Pseudosasa japonica

Pseudosasa japonica, commonly known as Japanese bamboo, is a perennial grass native to East Asia.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its slender, hollow stems that grow in dense clumps. The plant typically reaches heights of 1 to 3 meters and has narrow, linear leaves that are often a bright green color. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers in early summer, followed by the development of elongated, cylindrical seed pods.

Pseudosasa japonica is often used in ornamental gardening for its elegant appearance and hardy nature.
