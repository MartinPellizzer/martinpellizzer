Polygonum hispidum var. glabrescens

Polygonum hispidum var. glabrescens, commonly known as the soft-haired knotweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia.

It features upright stems that are initially hairy but become smoother as the plant matures. The leaves are lance-shaped, with a rough texture and often have a reddish or purple tinge on the underside. It produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or red, blooming in late summer.

This plant is often found in moist environments such as stream banks and wet meadows.
