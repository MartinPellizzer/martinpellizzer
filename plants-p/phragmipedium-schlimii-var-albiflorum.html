Phragmipedium schlimii var. albiflorum

Phragmipedium schlimii var. albiflorum, commonly known as the Whiteflowered Paphiopedilum, is a rare and striking orchid native to the cloud forests of South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy white flowers with a distinctive lip that often has a yellow or greenish tint. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are broad and leathery, adapted to retain moisture in its natural habitat.

This variety is highly sought after by orchid enthusiasts for its beauty and rarity.
