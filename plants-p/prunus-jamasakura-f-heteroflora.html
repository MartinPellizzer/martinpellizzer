Prunus jamasakura f. heteroflora

Prunus jamasakura f. heteroflora, commonly known as the Japanese cherry blossom variant, is a cultivar of the Prunus jamasakura species.

It is characterized by its distinct floral morphology, featuring flowers with a mix of white and pink hues. The plant is a deciduous tree that typically grows to a moderate height, with a rounded crown and smooth, gray bark. Its blossoms appear in early spring, adding a striking visual display to gardens and natural landscapes.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its aesthetic contribution to spring scenery.
