Pyrus rotundifolia var. decipiens

Pyrus rotundifolia var. decipiens, commonly known as the false winter apple, is a variant of the round-leaved pear.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States. The plant features elliptical to ovate leaves that are often lobed, and it produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring. Its fruit is a small, yellow or red berry that ripens in late summer.

The species is notable for its ornamental value and adaptability to various soil types.
