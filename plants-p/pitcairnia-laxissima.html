Pitcairnia laxissima

Pitcairnia laxissima, commonly known as the showy pitcairnia, is a species of bromeliad native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of white or silver hairs, giving it a striking appearance. The plant produces vibrant, long-lasting inflorescences that can reach up to two meters in height, featuring clusters of small, colorful flowers. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is well-suited for indoor cultivation due to its adaptability.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for botanical gardens and collectors.
