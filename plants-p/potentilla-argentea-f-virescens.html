Potentilla argentea f. virescens

Potentilla argentea f. virescens, commonly known as silver cinquefoil, is a variant of the silverweed species native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its finely divided, silver-hairy leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and rocky areas.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
