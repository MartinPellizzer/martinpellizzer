Petroselinum crispum var. erfurtense

Petroselinum crispum var. erfurtense, commonly known as German parsley, is a variety of parsley characterized by its curly, dark green leaves and robust flavor.

It is native to Central Europe and is often used in traditional cuisine for its aromatic properties. The plant grows as an annual herb, reaching heights of up to 50 cm, with slender stems and finely divided leaves. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for garden cultivation.

Its botanical features make it a valuable addition to both culinary and ornamental gardens.
