Phlogacanthus vitellinus

Phlogacanthus vitellinus, commonly known as the yellow bellflower, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its showy yellow or orange bell-shaped flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has lance-shaped leaves and thrives in warm, humid environments. It is often found in forest understories and along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening in subtropical climates.
