Persea bullata

Persea bullata, commonly known as the bulletwood or bullet tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, dark bark and glossy, elliptical leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers followed by oval, fleshy fruits that are typically yellow or orange when ripe. Persea bullata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has a rich history of use in traditional medicine.

Its botanical features make it well-suited for warm, humid climates and partial shade.
