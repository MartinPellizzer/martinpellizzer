Pogostemon plectranthoides

Pogostemon plectranthoides, commonly known as the narrow-leaved pogostemon, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, tubular flowers arranged in spikes, typically blooming in shades of pink or purple. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its aromatic leaves and ornamental flowers make it a popular choice for shade gardens.
