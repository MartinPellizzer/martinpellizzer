Paeonia officinalis var. microcarpa

Paeonia officinalis var. microcarpa, commonly known as the dwarf peony, is a compact variety of the peony plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its small, rounded foliage and clusters of fragrant, double flowers that range in color from white to deep red. The plant is typically deciduous, shedding its leaves in the autumn, and it thrives in well-drained soil with full sun exposure. It is valued for its ornamental beauty and historical use in traditional medicine.

The variety is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal and hardy nature.
