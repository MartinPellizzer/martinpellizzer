Pilosella ziziana subsp. leptophylla

Pilosella ziziana subsp.

leptophylla, commonly known as the slender-leaved hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit.

The plant features small, yellow composite flowers arranged in dense clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrowly elliptic, with a hairy surface that gives the plant its species name "leptophylla.".

This subspecies is adapted to rocky and alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils and full sunlight.
