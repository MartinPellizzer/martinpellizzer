Pisonia calafia

Pisonia calafia, commonly known as the California pisonia, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a small shrub that typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, showcasing adaptability to harsh conditions. The plant produces clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in the spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

The fruit is a small, round capsule that splits open to release tiny, wind-dispersed seeds.
