Podochilus imitans

Podochilus imitans, commonly known as the mimic orchid, is a species of flowering plant in the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its striking, often colorful flowers that resemble other orchid species. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid tropical environments. Its flowers are bilaterally symmetrical, with a distinctive lip that aids in pollination by insects.

Podochilus imitans is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and greenhouses.
