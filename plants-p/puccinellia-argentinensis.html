Puccinellia argentinensis

Puccinellia argentinensis, commonly known as the Argentine bulrush, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It typically grows in wetland and marshy environments, showcasing its adaptability to moist conditions. The plant features slender, cylindrical stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its small, spike-like inflorescence contains numerous tiny flowers, which are wind-pollinated.

This species plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wetland organisms.
