Potentilla pratensis

Potentilla pratensis, commonly known as meadow cinquefoil, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its low-growing habit and showy yellow flowers. The plant typically features compound leaves with five leaflets, which give it its common name. It thrives in moist, open habitats such as meadows and riverbanks.

Potentilla pratensis is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support local biodiversity.
