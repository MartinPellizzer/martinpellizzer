Pellaea flavens

Pellaea flavens, commonly known as the golden maidenhair fern, is a species of fern native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its delicate, bright yellow to orange fronds that contrast with its dark green rachises. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and rocky crevices. Its fronds are pinnately divided and often have a distinctive, almost hair-like appearance.

Pellaea flavens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
