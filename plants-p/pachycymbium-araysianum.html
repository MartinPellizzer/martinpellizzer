Pachycymbium araysianum

Pachycymbium araysianum, commonly known as the Malabar arrowroot, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of India and Sri Lanka.

It belongs to the family Araceae and is characterized by its large, arrow-shaped leaves and striking inflorescences. The plant produces a starchy tuberous root that is used as a food source in local cuisines. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in dense tropical forests.

Due to its ornamental value and edible properties, it is sometimes cultivated for both horticultural and culinary purposes.
