Phyllanthus gigantifolius

Phyllanthus gigantifolius, commonly known as the giant-leaf phyllanthus, is a shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can reach up to 30 cm in length. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by red, fleshy fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires a warm, humid climate.

The species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional medicinal uses in various cultures.
