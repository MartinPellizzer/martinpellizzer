Pachira trinitensis

Pachira trinitensis, commonly known as the Trinidadian money plant, is a species of flowering plant in the Araceae family.

It is native to the Caribbean, particularly Trinidad and Tobago, and is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and waxy, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers that are followed by bright red berries. It is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its attractive foliage and ability to thrive in low-light conditions.

Pachira trinitensis is also valued for its cultural significance in some regions of the Caribbean.
