Pittosporum trilobum

Pittosporum trilobum, commonly known as the golden leatherleaf, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its dark green, leathery leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a glossy appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring, followed by orange-red berries. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and seasonal blooms.

Pittosporum trilobum is also valued for its ability to tolerate a range of soil conditions and its use in landscaping.
