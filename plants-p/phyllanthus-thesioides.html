Phyllanthus thesioides

Phyllanthus thesioides, commonly known as the Indian cheesewood, is a small evergreen shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, followed by distinctive, three-lobed capsules as its fruit. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories or on rocky slopes.

Due to its medicinal properties, it is also cultivated for use in traditional herbal remedies.
