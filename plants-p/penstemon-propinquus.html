Penstemon propinquus

Penstemon propinquus, commonly known as the plains penstemon, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and rocky slopes. The plant features showy, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in arid environments.

Penstemon propinquus is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
