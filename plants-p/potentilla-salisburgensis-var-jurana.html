Potentilla salisburgensis var. jurana

Potentilla salisburgensis var. jurana, commonly known as the Salzburg potentilla, is a low-growing shrub native to the Alpine regions of Central Europe.

It features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has slender, woody stems covered in fine hairs, and its leaves are narrow, elliptical, and slightly hairy. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine meadows and slopes.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to withstand harsh mountain conditions.
