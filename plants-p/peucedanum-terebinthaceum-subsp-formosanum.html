Peucedanum terebinthaceum subsp. formosanum

Peucedanum terebinthaceum subsp.

formosanum, commonly known as the Formosan cowparsley, is a herbaceous plant belonging to the Apiaceae family. It is native to eastern Asia, particularly Taiwan, where it thrives in moist, shaded environments.

The plant features finely divided, fern-like leaves and produces small, white to greenish-yellow flowers arranged in umbels. Its fruits are slender, ribbed schizocarps, typical of the Apiaceae family.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
