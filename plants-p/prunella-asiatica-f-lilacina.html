Prunella asiatica f. lilacina

Prunella asiatica f. lilacina, commonly known as the lilac-flowered selfheal, is a flowering plant belonging to the mint family, Lamiaceae.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its square, hairy stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale lilac, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Native to regions of Asia, including China and the Himalayas, it thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

It is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
