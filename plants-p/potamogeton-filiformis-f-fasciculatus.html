Potamogeton filiformis f. fasciculatus

Potamogeton filiformis f. fasciculatus, commonly known as the clustered threadstem pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in North America.

It is characterized by its slender, thread-like leaves that grow in dense clusters, often forming long, flexible stems. The plant typically thrives in slow-moving or still waters, such as lakes and ponds, where it can anchor itself to the substrate. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are usually borne in clusters and are adapted for underwater pollination.

This variety is important for aquatic ecosystems, providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
