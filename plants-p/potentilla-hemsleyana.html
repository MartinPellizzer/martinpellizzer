Potentilla hemsleyana

Potentilla hemsleyana, commonly known as the Hemsley's cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy yellow flowers. The plant typically blooms in summer, producing clusters of small, five-petaled flowers. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets, and it thrives in well-drained soils.

Potentilla hemsleyana is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
