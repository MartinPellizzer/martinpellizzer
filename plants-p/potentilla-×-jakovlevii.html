Potentilla × jakovlevii

Potentilla × jakovlevii, commonly known as the Jacob's potentilla, is a hybrid shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and produces clusters of small, yellow flowers in late spring to early summer. The plant has finely divided, gray-green leaves that are often covered in a powdery bloom, giving it a distinctive appearance. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is a popular choice for gardeners in temperate climates.
