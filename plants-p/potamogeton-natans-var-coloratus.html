Potamogeton natans var. coloratus

Potamogeton natans var. coloratus, commonly known as the red-stemmed pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in North America.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems that often display a reddish hue, especially in deeper water or during certain seasons. The plant produces small, green, egg-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in whorls around the stem. Its flowers are unisexual, with male and female flowers occurring on separate plants, a feature common in many pondweeds.

This species plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
