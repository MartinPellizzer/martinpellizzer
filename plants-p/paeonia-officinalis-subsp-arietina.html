Paeonia officinalis subsp. arietina

Paeonia officinalis subsp.

arietina, commonly known as the Armenian peony, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Caucasus region. It is characterized by its tall, upright stems and large, showy flowers that range in color from white to deep pink.

The plant produces large, heart-shaped leaves that are typically green with a slightly rough texture. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Its botanical significance lies in its historical use in traditional medicine and its ornamental value.
