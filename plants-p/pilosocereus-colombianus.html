Pilosocereus colombianus

Pilosocereus colombianus, commonly known as the Colombian cactus or the white-spined cactus, is a species native to the Andes regions of Colombia.

It is a spineless cactus with a cylindrical stem that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant produces white or pale yellow flowers during the dry season, which are often nocturnal in bloom. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires minimal water, making it well adapted to arid environments.

Its unique appearance and ecological role make it an important species in its native habitat.
