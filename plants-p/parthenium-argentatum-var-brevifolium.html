Parthenium argentatum var. brevifolium

Parthenium argentatum var. brevifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaved feverfew, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States.

It is characterized by its small, daisy-like white flowers and hairy, narrow leaves. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family and is known for its medicinal properties. It is often used in traditional medicine for its anti-inflammatory and analgesic effects.

However, it can be invasive in certain regions and may cause skin irritation in some individuals.
