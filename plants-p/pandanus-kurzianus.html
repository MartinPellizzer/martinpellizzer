Pandanus kurzianus

Pandanus kurzianus, commonly known as the dwarf pandanus, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its rosette-like arrangement of long, narrow, fibrous leaves. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences that bear yellowish-green flowers. Its roots are fibrous and often used in traditional crafts.

It thrives in well-drained soil and requires ample sunlight to flourish.
