Physalis pensylvanica var. lanceolata

Physalis pensylvanica var. lanceolata, commonly known as the lanceleaf husk or wild tomato, is a species of flowering plant in the nightshade family.

It is characterized by its hairy, egg-shaped fruits that enclose the seeds, resembling small, inflated capsules. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and has lance-shaped leaves with a slightly toothed margin. It produces yellowish-white flowers that bloom in the summer months.

This variant is native to the eastern United States and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.
