Peperomia radicosa

Peperomia radicosa, commonly known as the root peperomia, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its compact, upright growth habit and its ability to thrive in low-light conditions. Its leaves are typically ovate to lanceolate in shape, with a waxy surface that helps reduce water loss.

Peperomia radicosa produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often clustered in spikes, contributing to its ornamental value in indoor gardening.
