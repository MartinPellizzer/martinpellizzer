Petrophytum cinerascens

Petrophytum cinerascens, commonly known as the desert lily, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its tall, branching stems and showy, white to pale pink flowers. The plant is adapted to arid environments, featuring succulent leaves and deep root systems to conserve water. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Petrophytum cinerascens plays an important ecological role in desert ecosystems by providing nectar for pollinators and habitat for small animals.
