Piper tetraphyllum

Piper tetraphyllum, commonly known as the four-leaf pepper, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil and Peru. The plant is characterized by its small, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of tiny, peppery fruits. It grows as a shrub or small tree, typically reaching heights of up to 3 meters.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and for research purposes.
