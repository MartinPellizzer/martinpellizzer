Pyrus dilecta

Pyrus dilecta, commonly known as the Japanese whitebeam, is a deciduous tree native to Japan.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its oval, dark green leaves that turn yellow in autumn. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers in spring, followed by edible, round berries that are typically green when mature. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can thrive in a variety of soil types.

Pyrus dilecta is relatively low maintenance and suitable for smaller gardens due to its compact size.
