Pandanus nullumiae

Pandanus nullumiae, commonly known as the Nullumia pandanus, is a species of tree from the Pandanaceae family.

It is native to the island of Nullumia in the Pacific Ocean and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feather-like leaves. The plant thrives in humid, coastal environments and is often found in tropical rainforests. Its leaves are used in traditional crafts and for thatching, while its fruit is edible and has a mild, sweet flavor.

Due to its limited range, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
