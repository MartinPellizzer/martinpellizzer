Pleiospora obovata

Pleiospora obovata, commonly known as the oblong spore fungus, is a species of ascomycete fungus characterized by its distinctive spore morphology.

It produces dark, oblong-shaped spores that are typically found in soil and decaying organic matter. The fungus is often associated with plant debris and can be found in various habitats, including forest floors and grasslands. Its reproductive structures are essential for its survival and propagation in natural ecosystems.

Due to its unique spore shape, it is sometimes used in taxonomic studies to identify related fungal species.
