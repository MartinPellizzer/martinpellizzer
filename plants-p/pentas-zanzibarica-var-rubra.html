Pentas zanzibarica var. rubra

Pentas zanzibarica var. rubra, commonly known as the red pentas, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of East Africa.

It is characterized by its showy, clustered flowers that range in color from pink to deep red, often with a contrasting white or yellow center. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves and thrives in full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes. It is typically propagated through cuttings and is known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

This ornamental plant is well-suited for warm climates and is often used in ornamental horticulture.
