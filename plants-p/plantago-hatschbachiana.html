Plantago hatschbachiana

Plantago hatschbachiana, commonly known as the Alpine plantain, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils at high altitudes, often in alpine meadows and grasslands. The plant has broad, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clusters. It is adapted to cold climates and can withstand harsh winter conditions.

This species is valued for its medicinal properties and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
