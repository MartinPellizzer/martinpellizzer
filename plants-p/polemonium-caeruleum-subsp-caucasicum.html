Polemonium caeruleum subsp. caucasicum

Polemonium caeruleum subsp.

caucasicum, commonly known as the Caucasian bluebells, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Caucasus region. It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically blue to purple in color, blooming in early summer.

The plant has lanceolate leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in moist, woodland habitats and prefers partial to full shade.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden cultivation in temperate climates.
