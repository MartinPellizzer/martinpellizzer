Prostanthera cuneata

Prostanthera cuneata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf mint bush, is a shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant features small, tubular flowers that are typically pale purple to blue in color, arranged in dense spikes. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often with a minty fragrance when crushed.

Prostanthera cuneata is adapted to dry, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and heathland habitats.
