Peperomia fluviatilis

Peperomia fluviatilis, commonly known as the river peperomia, is a compact, low-growing plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It features fleshy, oval-shaped leaves that are typically green with a waxy texture, often adorned with subtle variegation. The plant produces small, clustered flowers in shades of white or pale pink, which bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor cultivation.

Due to its compact size and ornamental appeal, it is often used in terrariums and as a decorative foliage plant.
