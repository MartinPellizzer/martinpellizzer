Phlox divaricata subsp. divaricata

Phlox divaricata subsp.

divaricata, commonly known as woodland phlox, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern North America. It typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and thrives in rich, well-drained soils.

The plant features erect, branching stems covered with opposite, lance-shaped leaves. Its showy clusters of pink to purple flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native gardens.
