Pachycentria junghuhniana

Pachycentria junghuhniana, commonly known as the giant flame tree, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy red flowers that bloom in clusters, creating a dramatic visual impact. The plant has a thick, trunk-like stem with a rough, bark-like surface, which gives it its scientific name. Its leaves are large, simple, and alternate, often reaching up to 30 centimeters in length.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in warm climates for its vibrant floral displays.
