Prunus × subhirtella f. autumnalis

Prunus × subhirtella f. autumnalis, commonly known as the Autumnalis cherry, is a hybrid deciduous shrub or small tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its striking coppery-orange foliage that turns vibrant red in the fall, making it a popular ornamental plant. The plant produces small, pinkish-white flowers in early spring, followed by dark red or black berries. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained, fertile soil.

Its compact growth habit and seasonal color changes make it a valuable addition to gardens and landscapes.
