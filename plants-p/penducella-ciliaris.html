Penducella ciliaris

Penducella ciliaris, commonly known as the silverleaf penducella, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its silver-gray leaves and clusters of small, pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in coastal regions. It has a compact growth habit and is well adapted to arid conditions.

Penducella ciliaris is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
