Pandanus celebicus

Pandanus celebicus, commonly known as the Moluccan pandanus, is a tropical plant native to the Maluku Islands in Indonesia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, fan-shaped leaves that are often used in traditional crafts. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers that eventually develop into edible fruits. Its fibrous leaves are also utilized for making thatch and baskets.

Pandanus celebicus thrives in humid, coastal environments and is often found growing near the shoreline.
