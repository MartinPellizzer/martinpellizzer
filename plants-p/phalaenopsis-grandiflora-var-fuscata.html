Phalaenopsis grandiflora var. fuscata

Phalaenopsis grandiflora var. fuscata, commonly known as the dark-fuscated moth orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dark green, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of fragrant, long-lasting flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. The flowers typically bloom once a year, often during the winter months, and can last for several weeks. This variety is prized for its elegant appearance and is popular in horticulture for indoor cultivation.

It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires moderate humidity and well-draining potting media.
