Prunus × donarium f. sirayuki

Prunus × donarium f. sirayuki, commonly known as the Sirayuki cherry, is a unique hybrid plant originating from Japan.

It is characterized by its double pink flowers that bloom in early spring, adding a striking visual element to gardens. The plant is a compact shrub, typically reaching heights of 1 to 2 meters, with dense, dark green foliage. Its botanical features include small, oval leaves and a short, sturdy stem.

The Sirayuki cherry is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in temperate climates for its beauty.
