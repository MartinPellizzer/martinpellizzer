Pyrus toringo var. integrifolia

Pyrus toringo var. integrifolia, commonly known as the Japanese white pear, is a variant of the Toringo pear native to Japan.

It is characterized by its smooth, often white or pale yellow skin and its sweet, juicy flesh. The leaves are typically entire, without teeth or lobes, giving it the species name "integrifolia.". It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value as well as its edible fruit.

This variety is valued for its cold hardiness and ability to adapt to various soil conditions.
