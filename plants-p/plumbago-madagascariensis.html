Plumbago madagascariensis

Plumbago madagascariensis, commonly known as the Madagascar plumbago, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows up to 2 meters in height, featuring woody stems and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces clusters of sky-blue or white flowers during the summer months, which are highly attractive to pollinators. Its root system is fibrous and adapted to the well-drained soils of its native habitat.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is occasionally cultivated in gardens outside its native range.
