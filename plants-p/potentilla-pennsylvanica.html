Potentilla pennsylvanica

Potentilla pennsylvanica, commonly known as Pennsylvania Cinquefoil, is a perennial flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its showy, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full sunlight. Its leaves are palmately divided into five oval-shaped leaflets, and it has a compact, bushy growth habit.

Pennsylvania Cinquefoil is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
