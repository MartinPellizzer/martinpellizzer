Poa sieberiana var. spithamea

Poa sieberiana var. spithamea, commonly known as the Spithamean bluegrass, is a variant of the bluegrass genus native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny flowers, which are wind-pollinated. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine or subalpine meadows.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
