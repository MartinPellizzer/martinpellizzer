Pyrus commixta f. angustissima

Pyrus commixta f. angustissima, commonly known as the narrow-leaved pear, is a rare and distinctive cultivar of the pear tree.

It is characterized by its narrow, elongated leaves and smaller, often clustered fruit compared to typical pear species. This variety is typically found in specific regions with suitable climate conditions, often in temperate zones. Its botanical features make it a subject of interest for horticulturists and botanists.

Due to its limited distribution and unique morphology, it is considered a valuable specimen in botanical gardens and conservation efforts.
