Poa oligantha

Poa oligantha, commonly known as the slender meadow grass, is a flowering plant species native to South America.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its delicate, tufted growth habit. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences that are typically green to brown in color. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

Poa oligantha is often found in open grasslands and along stream banks, where it plays a minor role in local ecosystems.
