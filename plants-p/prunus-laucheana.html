Prunus laucheana

Prunus laucheana, commonly known as the Lauche cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and small, fragrant flowers. The plant produces small, dark red berries that are edible but often have a bitter taste. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Prunus laucheana is also valued for its ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
