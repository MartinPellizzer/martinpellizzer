Protea aristata

Protea aristata, commonly known as the silver protea, is a striking South African plant belonging to the Proteaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescences composed of numerous white to pale pink flowers arranged in a spherical or conical shape. The plant typically grows in fynbos vegetation, thriving in well-drained, acidic soils and full sun exposure. Its unique floral structure attracts a variety of pollinators, including birds and insects.

Protea aristata is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its long-lasting blooms.
