Prosopis nandubey

Prosopis nandubey, commonly known as the Andean mesquite, is a shrub or small tree native to South America, particularly Argentina and Chile.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pod-like fruits. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it resilient to drought conditions. Its roots have a symbiotic relationship with nitrogen-fixing bacteria, enhancing soil fertility.

The plant is also valued for its wood and as a source of fodder for livestock.
