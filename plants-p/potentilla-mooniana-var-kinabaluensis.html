Potentilla mooniana var. kinabaluensis

Potentilla mooniana var. kinabaluensis, commonly known as the Kinabalu potentilla, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Borneo.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and dense, shrub-like form. The plant produces showy, yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically narrow and elliptical, with a slightly hairy texture.

This variety is particularly adapted to high-altitude environments, thriving in cool, moist conditions.
