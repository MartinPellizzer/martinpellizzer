Phoradendron buchtienii

Phoradendron buchtienii, commonly known as the Buchtien's mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on the branches of host trees, primarily in tropical and subtropical regions.

It lacks chlorophyll and relies on its host for water and nutrients, while producing its own sugars through photosynthesis using the light it captures. The plant has small, leathery leaves and produces white or yellow flowers that are often visited by insects for pollination. Its berries are typically red or black and contain seeds that are dispersed by birds.

This species plays an important ecological role in its habitat by supporting biodiversity and contributing to the nutrient cycle.
