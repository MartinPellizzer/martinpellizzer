Phreatia pumilio

Phreatia pumilio, commonly known as the small phreatia orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in humid, shaded environments within lowland forests. The plant features a sympodial growth habit, with multiple pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its flowers are small and usually white or pale yellow, arranged in a raceme.

Phreatia pumilio is known for its ability to thrive in moist, well-drained soils and is often found near streams or in swampy areas.
