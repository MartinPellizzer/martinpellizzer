Pinus sylvestris var. sibirica

Pinus sylvestris var. sibirica, commonly known as the Siberian pine, is a variant of the Scots pine native to the cold climates of Siberia and northern Europe.

It is a coniferous evergreen tree characterized by its needle-like leaves and cone-bearing structure. The tree typically grows in well-drained soils and can reach heights of up to 25 meters. Its bark is thick and fissured, providing protection against harsh environmental conditions.

The Siberian pine is also valued for its timber and has cultural significance in various regions.
