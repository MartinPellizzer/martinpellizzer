Penstemon attenuatus var. hyacinthinus

Penstemon attenuatus var. hyacinthinus, commonly known as the hyacinth penstemon, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of showy, hyacinth-like blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It is a perennial species that is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and xeriscaping.
