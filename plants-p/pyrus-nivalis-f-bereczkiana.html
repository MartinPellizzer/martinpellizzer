Pyrus nivalis f. bereczkiana

Pyrus nivalis f. bereczkiana, commonly known as the Snow Pear, is a cold-hardy ornamental fruit tree native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by edible fruits that are typically small and sour. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for gardens in temperate climates. Its compact size and ornamental value make it a popular choice for landscape design.

The f. bereczkiana form is a cultivated variant known for its improved cold resistance and unique floral display.
