Poa pratensis subsp. hilaea

Poa pratensis subsp.

hilaea, commonly known as hilaceous meadow grass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Europe and Asia. It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and fine, bluish-green leaves.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with pale green to brownish flowers. It thrives in moist, temperate climates and is often found in meadows and grasslands.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in landscaping and ecological restoration projects.
