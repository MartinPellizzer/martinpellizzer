Panopsis megistosperma

Panopsis megistosperma, commonly known as the giant panopsis, is a striking member of the Bromeliaceae family.

It is native to the Andean regions of South America, where it thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant is characterized by its large, broad leaves that form a rosette, often with a waxy coating to retain moisture. Its inflorescence consists of vibrant, long-lasting flowers that range from red to orange, attracting various pollinators.

Due to its ornamental value and unique appearance, it is a popular choice for botanical gardens and specialized collections.
