Pithecellobium tuerckheimii

Pithecellobium tuerckheimii, commonly known as the Tuerckheim's mountain bean, is a small, evergreen shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often in mountainous areas with high humidity. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several small, oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces small, yellowish flowers.

The fruit is a legume that splits open to release its seeds, contributing to its role in ecological systems.
