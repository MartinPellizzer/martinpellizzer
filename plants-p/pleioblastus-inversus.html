Pleioblastus inversus

Pleioblastus inversus, commonly known as the reverse bamboo, is a unique species of grass native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its striking silver-green foliage and a distinctive reverse growth pattern, where the leaves curve inward rather than outward. This plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its elegant, cascading appearance. It prefers well-drained soil and partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a container plant.

The plant is also valued for its ability to thrive in humid climates and its relatively low maintenance requirements.
