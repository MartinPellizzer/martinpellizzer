Phillyrea angustifolia subsp. baui

Phillyrea angustifolia subsp.

baui, commonly known as the narrow-leaved Phillyrea, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region. It features small, elliptical leaves that are typically dark green and glossy on the upper surface.

The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by small, woody fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for rocky or coastal areas.

The leaves and flowers contain volatile oils, which contribute to its aromatic properties and potential medicinal uses.
