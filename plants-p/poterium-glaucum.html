Poterium glaucum

Poterium glaucum, commonly known as the bluebush, is a shrub native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, dense growth habit and gray-green foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, pale blue flowers that bloom in clusters, adding a subtle ornamental value. It is highly drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Poterium glaucum is also valued for its ability to stabilize soil and prevent erosion in harsh environments.
