Potentilla leuconota var. victoriae

Potentilla leuconota var. victoriae, commonly known as the Victoria cinquefoil, is a variant of the cinquefoil genus native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is a low-growing perennial shrub characterized by its bright yellow flowers and finely divided, fern-like leaves. The plant typically thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight. Its flowers bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in wetland environments.
