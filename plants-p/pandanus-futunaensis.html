Pandanus futunaensis

Pandanus futunaensis, commonly known as the Futuna pandanus, is a species of flowering plant in the genus Pandanus.

It is native to the island of Futuna in the South Pacific, specifically in the Kingdom of Tonga. The plant is characterized by its long, narrow, and fibrous leaves that grow in a rosette pattern. It produces small, clustered inflorescences that bear yellowish flowers, which are followed by fibrous, spiky fruits.

This species is often used for its fibrous leaves, which have traditional and practical applications in the region.
