Pleiarina lasiandra

Pleiarina lasiandra, commonly known as the velvetleaf or velvetleaf daisy, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant is noted for its dark green, velvety leaves and yellow to orange flower heads that bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for gardens in temperate climates.

Pleiarina lasiandra is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
