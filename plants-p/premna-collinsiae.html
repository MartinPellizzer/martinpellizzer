Premna collinsiae

Premna collinsiae, commonly known as Collins' premna, is a species of flowering plant in the family Lamiaceae.

It is native to parts of East Africa, including Kenya and Tanzania, where it typically grows in moist, shaded forest understories. The plant is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. Its small, tubular flowers are usually purple or violet in color and are arranged in clusters.

Premna collinsiae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its aesthetic and ecological significance.
