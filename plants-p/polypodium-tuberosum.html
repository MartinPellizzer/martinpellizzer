Polypodium tuberosum

Polypodium tuberosum, commonly known as the tuberous polypody, is a species of fern native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit, with tuber-like structures that store nutrients. The plant typically has slender, lance-shaped fronds that are often clustered at the base. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its reproductive structures include sporangia that release spores, contributing to its propagation in suitable habitats.
