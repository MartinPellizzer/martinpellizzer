Paspalum nudatum

Paspalum nudatum, commonly known as the slender false brome, is a perennial grass species native to the southern United States and parts of South America.

It typically grows in wetland habitats, such as marshes and swampy areas, where it thrives in moist, acidic soils. The plant has slender, erect stems that can reach up to 1 meter in height, with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets that are clustered along the stem, producing small, wind-pollinated flowers.

Paspalum nudatum is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife species.
