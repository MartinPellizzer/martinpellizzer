Papaver orientale var. paucifoliatum

Papaver orientale var. paucifoliatum, commonly known as the Turkish poppy, is a striking perennial plant native to the eastern Mediterranean region.

It features showy, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from deep red to orange and yellow, with a prominent black center. The plant has a compact growth habit, with few leaves and a tall, slender stem that supports its vibrant blooms. Its foliage is typically narrow and lance-shaped, contributing to its elegant appearance.

This variety is valued in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
