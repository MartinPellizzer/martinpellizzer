Potentilla purdomii

Potentilla purdomii, commonly known as the Purdom's cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of central Asia.

It is a low-growing shrub that typically reaches heights of about 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer, arranged in clusters of five. Its leaves are palmately divided into five leaflets, which are serrated and covered in fine hairs.

Potentilla purdomii thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, alpine environments.
