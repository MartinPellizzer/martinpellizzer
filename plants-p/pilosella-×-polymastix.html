Pilosella × polymastix

Pilosella × polymastix, commonly known as the hairy groundsel, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and yellow composite flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides and meadows. It is native to Europe and has been introduced to other parts of the world, including North America.

Pilosella × polymastix is often considered a weed due to its rapid growth and ability to colonize various environments.
