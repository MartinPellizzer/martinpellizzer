Paracaryum glandulosum

Paracaryum glandulosum, commonly known as the sticky leaf plant, is a unique species native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its glossy, waxy leaves that are covered in fine, sticky hairs, giving it a distinctive texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers that bloom in spring. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

Paracaryum glandulosum is a hardy plant that thrives in well-drained soil and full sun.
