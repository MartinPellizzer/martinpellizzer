Polypodium nitidissimum

Polypodium nitidissimum, commonly known as the glossy polypody, is a fern species native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its finely divided, leathery fronds that are typically bright green and covered with a waxy coating. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories. Its reproductive structures, including sori, are located on the underside of the fronds.

Polypodium nitidissimum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in horticulture.
