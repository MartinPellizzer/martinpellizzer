Potentilla freyniana f. plena

Potentilla freyniana f. plena, commonly known as the double-flowered potentilla, is a compact, deciduous shrub native to the southwestern United States.

It features dense clusters of small, showy flowers that are typically double, with numerous petals giving it a full, ornamental appearance. The plant has upright, branching stems that are covered in fine, silvery hair, enhancing its visual texture. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its bloom period extends from late spring to early summer, adding vibrant color to gardens throughout the growing season.
