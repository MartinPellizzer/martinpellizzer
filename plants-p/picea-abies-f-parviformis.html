Picea abies f. parviformis

Picea abies f. parviformis, commonly known as the dwarf Norway spruce, is a compact form of the Norway spruce, characterized by its slow growth and dense, rounded shape.

It features short, stout shoots and small, needle-like leaves that are typically bluish-green in color. This form is often cultivated as an ornamental plant in gardens and landscapes due to its compact size and aesthetic appeal. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, thriving in temperate climates.

The plant is resistant to many common pests and diseases, making it a low-maintenance choice for gardeners.
