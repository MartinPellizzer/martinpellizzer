Phyllanthus collinsiae var. collinsiae

Phyllanthus collinsiae var. collinsiae, commonly known as Collins' spurge, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of southern Africa.

It is a shrub characterized by its small, clustered leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. It has a compact growth habit and is often found in coastal or mountainous areas.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and resilience in challenging conditions.
