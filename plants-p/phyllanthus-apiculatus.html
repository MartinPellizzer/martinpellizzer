Phyllanthus apiculatus

Phyllanthus apiculatus, commonly known as the African spurge, is a flowering plant native to Africa and parts of Asia.

It is a small shrub that typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are often followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a rough texture, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Phyllanthus apiculatus is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional remedies for various ailments.
