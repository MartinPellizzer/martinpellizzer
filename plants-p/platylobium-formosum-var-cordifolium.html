Platylobium formosum var. cordifolium

Platylobium formosum var. cordifolium, commonly known as the heart-leaf false heath, is a flowering plant native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its small, clustered flowers that are typically yellow or cream in color. The leaves are broad and heart-shaped, giving the plant its specific epithet "cordifolium.". It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or sandy areas.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
