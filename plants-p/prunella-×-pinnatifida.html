Prunella × pinnatifida

Prunella × pinnatifida, commonly known as the narrow-leaved selfheal, is a hybrid plant belonging to the mint family (Lamiaceae).

It is characterized by its slender, opposite leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces spikes of blue to purple flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and meadows.

Prunella × pinnatifida is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
