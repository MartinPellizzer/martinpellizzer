Pandanus brongniartii

Pandanus brongniartii, commonly known as the giant pandanus, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, palm-like leaves that can grow up to several meters in length. The plant produces fragrant, yellowish flowers that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. Its leaves are often used in traditional crafts and as thatching material.

The species is well adapted to coastal and wetland environments, thriving in humid, saline conditions.
