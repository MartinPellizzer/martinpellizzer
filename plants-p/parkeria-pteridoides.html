Parkeria pteridoides

Parkeria pteridoides, commonly known as the African water fern, is a species of aquatic fern native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its delicate, floating leaves that form a rosette shape on the water surface. The plant has long, thin rhizomes that anchor it to submerged substrates, allowing it to spread across water bodies. Its fronds are typically green and have a feathery appearance, resembling the structure of true ferns.

Parkeria pteridoides plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for small aquatic organisms and contributing to water filtration.
