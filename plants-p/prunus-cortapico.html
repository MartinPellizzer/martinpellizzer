Prunus cortapico

Prunus cortapico, commonly known as the Cortapico cherry, is a species native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized tree that typically grows up to 10 meters in height. The plant produces small, white or pink flowers in the spring, followed by edible drupes that are often used in local cuisines. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a slightly serrated edge.

The bark is smooth and grayish in color, becoming fissured with age.
