Piper sepium var. guacimonum

Piper sepium var. guacimonum, commonly known as the Guacimonum pepper plant, is a variant of the Piper sepium species native to Central and South America.

It is a climbing vine characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and small, peppery fruits that are often used in local cuisines. The plant thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, requiring high humidity and well-drained soil. Its botanical features include aromatic leaves and a distinct, pungent flavor, making it a valuable ornamental and culinary plant.

This variety is also noted for its potential medicinal properties in traditional herbal practices.
