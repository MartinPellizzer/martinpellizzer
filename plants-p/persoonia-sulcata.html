Persoonia sulcata

Persoonia sulcata, commonly known as the serrated burr-persoonia, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is a shrub that typically grows in coastal and sandy soils, often found in the southern regions of Western Australia. The plant features distinctive, woody fruits that are covered in sharp, spiny bracts, which help in seed dispersal by attaching to animals. Its flowers are small and yellowish, arranged in clusters, and it blooms during the autumn season.

Persoonia sulcata is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
