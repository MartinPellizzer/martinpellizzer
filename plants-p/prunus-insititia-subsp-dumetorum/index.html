Prunus Insititia Subsp. Dumetorum

Blackthorn, scientifically known as Prunus insititia subsp. dumetorum, is a hardy shrub native to temperate regions of the northern hemisphere. It is recognized for its sharp, thorny branches and its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions. The plant produces small, dark purple berries that ripen in late summer and are often used in traditional medicine. Despite its tough exterior, the Blackthorn plays an important role in supporting local wildlife and ecosystems. Its resilience and adaptability make it a notable species in both natural and cultivated landscapes.
