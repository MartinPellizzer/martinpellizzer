Pandanus paracalensis

Pandanus paracalensis, commonly known as the narrow-leaf pandanus, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Pandanaceae family and is characterized by its long, narrow leaves that are often used in traditional crafts. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and wetlands, where it thrives in humid, saline conditions. Its roots are fibrous and help stabilize the soil, making it an important plant in coastal ecosystems.

The plant's inflorescence consists of small, clustered flowers that are pollinated by insects and birds.
