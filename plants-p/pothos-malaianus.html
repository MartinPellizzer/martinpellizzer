Pothos malaianus

Pothos malaianus, commonly known as the Malaianum devil's ivy, is a tropical climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped, waxy leaves that are typically a deep green with a slight variegation. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers that are followed by bright yellow berries. It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often cultivated as a decorative houseplant due to its attractive foliage and air-purifying properties.

Pothos malaianus is also known for its ability to climb using adventitious roots that attach to surfaces.
