Polygala americana

Polygala americana, commonly known as the American milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and rocky soils. The plant features slender, branched stems and small, clustered flowers that are usually pink or purple in color. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, and it produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Polygala americana is valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and its adaptability to challenging environments.
