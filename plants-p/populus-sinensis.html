Populus sinensis

Populus sinensis, commonly known as the Chinese poplar, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It belongs to the Salicaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and heart-shaped leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish catkins in the spring, which are its primary means of reproduction. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in river valleys and mountainous regions.

Populus sinensis is valued for its fast growth and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
