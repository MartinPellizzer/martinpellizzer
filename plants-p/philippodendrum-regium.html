Philippodendrum regium

Philippodendrum regium, commonly known as the royal philodendron, is a tropical climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 3 feet in length. The plant has a thick, fibrous stem that supports its vigorous growth as it climbs towards light. Its leaves are typically a deep green with a waxy surface, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by bright red berries.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking foliage and ability to thrive in indoor environments.
