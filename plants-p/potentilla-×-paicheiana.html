Potentilla × paicheiana

Potentilla × paicheiana, commonly known as the golden potentilla, is a hybrid shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and produces showy, golden-yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant features small, leathery leaves that are typically arranged in a spiral pattern along the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Due to its ornamental value and hardiness, it is widely cultivated in gardens across temperate climates.
