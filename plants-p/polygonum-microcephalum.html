Polygonum microcephalum

Polygonum microcephalum, commonly known as the small-headed knotweed, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, which includes plants known for their distinctive arrowhead-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in moist environments such as wetlands and stream banks. Its small, clustered flowers are usually pink or red, and it produces small, three-angled berries.

It is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
