Panios

Panios, commonly known as the "sea bean," is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant produces elongated, bean-like pods that contain several seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. Panios thrives in coastal areas and is adapted to saline and well-drained soils.

Its ability to fix nitrogen makes it an important plant for soil enrichment in agricultural settings.
