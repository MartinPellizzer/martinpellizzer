Plantago barbata var. puberula

Plantago barbata var. puberula, commonly known as the hairy plantain, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to regions of Europe and Asia.

It features upright, slender stems that bear lance-shaped leaves with a rough, hairy texture. The plant produces small, clustered greenish-white flowers in early summer, which are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous tiny seeds. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, along roadsides, and in cultivated fields.

Its fibrous root system helps stabilize soil, making it a useful plant for erosion control.
