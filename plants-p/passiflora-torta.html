Passiflora torta

Passiflora torta, commonly known as the twisted passionflower, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It is characterized by its unique, intricate flowers that feature five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments. The plant produces dark blue or black berries that are edible but often have a tart flavor. Its leaves are palmately lobed and can be used in traditional medicine.

Passiflora torta thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
