Polygala vahliana

Polygala vahliana, commonly known as the yellow milkweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in coastal or mountainous regions.

Polygala vahliana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine.
