Petasites deschmannii

Petasites deschmannii, commonly known as the giant buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 1 meter in diameter, and its showy, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in alpine or subalpine habitats. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense colonies.

The species is notable for its medicinal properties, with historical use in traditional remedies for respiratory ailments.
