Polygala carphoides

Polygala carphoides, commonly known as the golden mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows primarily in the southeastern United States.

It relies on a network of host trees, such as oaks and hickories, for water and nutrients while performing photosynthesis to some extent. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are often clustered in spikes, and it is pollinated by insects. Its seeds are dispersed by birds that consume the fleshy, red berries.

The species is of interest to botanists due to its unique parasitic relationship and ecological role in forest ecosystems.
