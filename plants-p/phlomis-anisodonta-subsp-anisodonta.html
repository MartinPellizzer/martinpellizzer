Phlomis anisodonta subsp. anisodonta

Phlomis anisodonta subsp.

anisodonta, commonly known as the narrow-leaved phlomis, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun.

The plant features erect, branched stems and lance-shaped leaves with a rough, hairy texture. Its showy, purple to pink flowers bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

It is often used in dry gardens due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
