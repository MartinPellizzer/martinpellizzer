Palisota gracilior

Palisota gracilior, commonly known as the slender bamboo grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in loose panicles. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Palisota gracilior is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat for various insects and birds.
