Panicum latifolium var. australe

Panicum latifolium var. australe, commonly known as Australian flat sedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to Australia.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically arranged in a dense, mat-like growth form. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in wet or seasonally waterlogged soils, often found in swampy or marshy habitats.

This variant is characterized by its broad, flat leaves and is often used in wetland restoration projects.
