Penstemon spectabilis var. subviscosus

Penstemon spectabilis var. subviscosus, commonly known as the sticky penstemon, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and often have a sticky or glandular surface, which is a distinguishing feature of the subspecies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

This plant is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
