Polypodium lauterbachii

Polypodium lauterbachii, commonly known as the Lauterbach's polypody, is a small, evergreen fern native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and rocky crevices. The plant has a creeping rhizome with a dense, tufted foliage of lance-shaped, dark green leaves. Its fronds are usually less than 30 cm in length and have a leathery texture.

Polypodium lauterbachii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in shady garden settings.
