Pentake

Pentake, commonly known as the five-lobed orchid, is a rare and exotic plant native to the high-altitude regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, five-lobed petals that resemble the shape of a star, giving it its common name. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and requires well-drained, acidic soil to flourish. Its blooming period occurs during the late spring to early summer months, producing fragrant flowers that attract various pollinators.

Due to its limited distribution and unique morphology, the pentake is considered a valuable subject in botanical research and conservation efforts.
