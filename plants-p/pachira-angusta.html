Pachira angusta

Pachira angusta, commonly known as the Mexican water tree, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its thick, woody trunk and large, glossy, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers that develop into woody, spiny fruits containing seeds. It thrives in warm, humid environments and prefers well-draining soil.

Pachira angusta is often cultivated as a houseplant due to its attractive appearance and relatively low maintenance requirements.
