Phymatodes banerjiana

Phymatodes banerjiana, commonly known as the Indian sandalwood, is a deciduous tree native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is characterized by its aromatic wood, which is highly valued in traditional medicine and perfumery. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape and often have a rough texture. It produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by woody fruits containing small, hard seeds.

The tree thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in dry, well-drained soils.
