Paspalum eruciforme

Paspalum eruciforme, commonly known as the silver grass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, silver-gray blades and panicles that produce small, pale blue or green flowers. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and along riverbanks. Its fibrous root system helps stabilize soil, making it useful in erosion control.

Paspalum eruciforme is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to provide habitat for various wildlife species.
