Pseudocerastium stellarioides

Pseudocerastium stellarioides, commonly known as the star-like cinquefoil, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Rosaceae and is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit. The plant features small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in clusters, typically from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate to elliptic, and often have a hairy texture.
