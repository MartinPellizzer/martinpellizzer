Potentilla dealbata

Potentilla dealbata, commonly known as silver potentilla, is a deciduous shrub native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It features wiry, thornless stems and is characterized by its white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The leaves are alternate, ovate, and covered in a fine layer of silvery hairs, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly used in gardens and landscaping.
