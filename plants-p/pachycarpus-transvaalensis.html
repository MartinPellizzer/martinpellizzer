Pachycarpus transvaalensis

Pachycarpus transvaalensis, commonly known as the Transvaal barberry, is a shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny branches and small, dark green leaves. The plant produces clusters of yellow flowers in the spring, which are followed by red, berry-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal areas.

Its hardy nature and ornamental features make it a popular choice for ornamental horticulture.
