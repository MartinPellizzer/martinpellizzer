Plantago niederleinii

Plantago niederleinii, commonly known as the Niederlein's plantain, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in a spike, which are typically pollinated by insects. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and along riverbanks.

Due to its medicinal properties, it has been used traditionally for treating skin conditions and respiratory ailments.
