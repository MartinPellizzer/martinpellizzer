Poa nepalensis var. nipponica

Poa nepalensis var. nipponica, commonly known as Japanese mountain grass, is a perennial grass species native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its fine, wiry leaves and compact growth habit, often forming dense mats in alpine and subalpine regions. The plant typically grows in well-drained soils and is adapted to cool, moist climates. Its inflorescence consists of a panicle of spikelets, which are often tinged with purple or reddish hues.

This variant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing mountainous terrain.
