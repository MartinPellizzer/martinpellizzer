Piper sperdinum

Piper sperdinum, commonly known as the Malabar pepper or wild long pepper, is a climbing vine native to the tropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into elongated, curved berries containing aromatic seeds. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped with a rough texture and a strong, pungent scent.

The plant is often cultivated for its medicinal properties and as a spice in traditional cuisines.
