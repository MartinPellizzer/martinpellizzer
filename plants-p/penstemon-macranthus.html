Penstemon macranthus

Penstemon macranthus, commonly known as tall beardtongue, is a herbaceous perennial native to the western United States.

It features upright stems that can grow up to 2 to 3 meters tall, adorned with narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers in shades of pink, purple, or red, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and native plant landscapes.

Its nectar-rich flowers attract bees, butterflies, and hummingbirds, contributing to ecological biodiversity.
