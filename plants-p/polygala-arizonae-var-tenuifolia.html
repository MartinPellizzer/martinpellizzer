Polygala arizonae var. tenuifolia

Polygala arizonae var. tenuifolia, commonly known as the Arizona milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils in arid environments, such as deserts and rocky areas. Its leaves are narrow and linear, adapted to reduce water loss in hot, dry climates.
