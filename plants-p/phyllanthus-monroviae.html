Phyllanthus monroviae

Phyllanthus monroviae, commonly known as African milk bush, is a shrub native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its small, green, rounded leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, and its fruits are small, three-lobed capsules that contain seeds. It is adapted to grow in dry, sandy soils and is often found in woodland or scrub habitats.

The plant has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
