Persicaria franciscana

Persicaria franciscana, commonly known as the Franciscan knotweed, is a herbaceous perennial native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and small, clustered flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant produces tiny, greenish-white flowers that are typically arranged in dense clusters at the tips of the stems. Its leaves are ovate to lanceolate, with a rough texture and prominent veins.

Persicaria franciscana is often found in moist, open habitats such as meadows and streambanks.
