Pentacalia polymera

Pentacalia polymera, commonly known as the coral vine, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is a climbing plant with slender, wiry stems that can reach lengths of up to 10 meters. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or red in color, arranged in dense inflorescences. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly waxy texture.

Pentacalia polymera is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in gardens and as a climbing plant in shaded areas.
