Pyrethrum partheniifolium var. latilobum

Pyrethrum partheniifolium var. latilobum, commonly known as the broad-lobed pyrethrum, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, daisy-like yellow flowers. The plant produces pyrethrin, a natural insecticide, which is derived from its flower heads. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it suitable for gardens and ornamental use.

This variety is valued both for its aesthetic appeal and its historical use in pest control.
