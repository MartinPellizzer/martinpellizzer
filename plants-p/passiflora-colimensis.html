Passiflora colimensis

Passiflora colimensis, commonly known as the Mexican passionflower, is a climbing vine native to Central America.

It is characterized by its striking, complex flowers that feature a blend of red, blue, and yellow hues. The plant produces edible, orange-yellow berries that are rich in nutrients. Its leaves are heart-shaped with a rough texture and are often used in traditional medicine.

Passiflora colimensis thrives in humid, tropical environments and is valued for both its ornamental beauty and ecological importance.
