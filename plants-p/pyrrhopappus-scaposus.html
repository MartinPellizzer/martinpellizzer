Pyrrhopappus scaposus

Pyrrhopappus scaposus, commonly known as the slenderleaf golden aster, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are narrow and hairy, while the flowers are yellow and bloom in late summer.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
