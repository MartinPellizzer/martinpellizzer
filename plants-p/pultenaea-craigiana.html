Pultenaea craigiana

Pultenaea craigiana, commonly known as Craig's wattle, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Australia.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring narrow, dark green leaves. The plant produces bright yellow flowers in late summer to autumn, which are clustered in racemes. It is part of the legume family, Fabaceae, and has a symbiotic relationship with nitrogen-fixing bacteria.

Pultenaea craigiana is often found in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to coastal and inland habitats.
