Potentilla incerta

Potentilla incerta, commonly known as the uncertain cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to regions of Asia and Europe.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy yellow flowers. The plant typically blooms in the summer months, producing clusters of small, bright yellow petals. Its leaves are compound, consisting of five oval-shaped leaflets, and it thrives in well-drained soils.

Potentilla incerta is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
