Paepalanthus accrescens var. glabrescens

Paepalanthus accrescens var. glabrescens, commonly known as the smooth-leaved puya, is a striking plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a member of the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its rosette of grass-like leaves that form a water-holding structure. The plant produces striking inflorescences with brightly colored, showy flowers that attract pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees. It thrives in high-altitude environments with cool temperatures and well-drained soils.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
