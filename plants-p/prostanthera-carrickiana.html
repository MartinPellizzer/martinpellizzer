Prostanthera carrickiana

Prostanthera carrickiana, commonly known as Carrick's mint, is a small shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in heathlands and open forests. Its small, tubular flowers are usually pink or purple, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits.

Prostanthera carrickiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional Indigenous medicine.
