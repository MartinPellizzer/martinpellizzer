Parahebe giulianettii

Parahebe giulianettii, commonly known as the New Zealand bellflower, is a perennial shrub native to New Zealand.

It features evergreen foliage with glossy, dark green leaves that are narrow and lance-shaped. The plant produces striking, bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue or violet, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers a sunny to partially shaded location.

Due to its ornamental value and unique appearance, it is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
