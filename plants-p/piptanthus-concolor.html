Piptanthus concolor

Piptanthus concolor, commonly known as the Chinese peashrub, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant produces pods that contain edible seeds, which are often used in traditional Chinese cuisine. It thrives in arid and semi-arid climates, making it an important species in ecological restoration projects.

Its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil also contributes to soil fertility in degraded landscapes.
