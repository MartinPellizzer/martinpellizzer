Pyrus malus var. astracanica

Pyrus malus var. astracanica, commonly known as the Astracanica apple, is a botanical variant of the domesticated apple.

It is characterized by its distinctive fruit shape, often more elongated and conical compared to typical apples. The plant is native to the Caucasus region and exhibits a high degree of genetic diversity. It is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical significance in horticultural studies.

Its botanical features make it a subject of interest for both conservation and breeding programs.
