Pellionia viridis

Pellionia viridis, commonly known as the green pellionia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves that are typically bright green and glossy. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white or greenish flowers that are clustered in terminal inflorescences. Pellionia viridis thrives in shaded, humid environments and is often used in ornamental horticulture for its attractive foliage.

It is a perennial plant that prefers well-drained, moist soil and can be grown as a ground cover or in hanging baskets.
