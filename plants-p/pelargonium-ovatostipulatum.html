Pelargonium ovatostipulatum

Pelargonium ovatostipulatum, commonly known as the broad-stipule geranium, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Geraniaceae family and is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves with prominent stipules. The plant produces small, clustered flowers in shades of pink or red, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or containers.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
