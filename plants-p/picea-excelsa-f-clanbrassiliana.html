Picea excelsa f. clanbrassiliana

Picea excelsa f. clanbrassiliana, commonly known as the Clanbrassiliana white spruce, is a rare and distinctive variant of the white spruce.

It is characterized by its narrow, conical shape and dense foliage, which gives it a compact appearance. The leaves are typically shorter and more closely packed compared to the typical white spruce, contributing to its unique morphology. It is native to specific regions in the Andes, where it thrives in high-altitude environments.

Due to its limited distribution and specific ecological requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
