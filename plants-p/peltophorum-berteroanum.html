Peltophorum berteroanum

Peltophorum berteroanum, commonly known as the Indian peacock tree, is a deciduous tree native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is characterized by its striking, peacock-like flower clusters that bloom in spring. The plant has pinnately compound leaves and produces woody pods that contain seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The tree is also valued for its rapid growth and ability to provide shade in urban landscapes.
