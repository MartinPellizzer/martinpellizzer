Polypodium harpeodes

Polypodium harpeodes, commonly known as the Harpe's shield fern, is a member of the Polypodiaceae family and is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It is a terrestrial fern that typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky crevices. The plant has a rhizomatous growth habit, with a dense, evergreen frond that can reach up to 30 centimeters in length. Its fronds are pinnate, with lanceolate to ovate pinnae, and the sori are arranged in two rows along the margins of the pinnae.

Polypodium harpeodes is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in shaded garden settings.
