Potentilla alpestris gracilior

Potentilla alpestris gracilior, commonly known as the slender alpine cinquefoil, is a low-growing perennial plant native to alpine and subalpine regions of Europe.

It features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer, typically in clusters of five. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves that are adapted to withstand cold and windy conditions. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine displays.
