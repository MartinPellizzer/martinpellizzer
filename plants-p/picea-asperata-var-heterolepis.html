Picea asperata var. heterolepis

Picea asperata var. heterolepis, commonly known as the Chinese fir, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, conical shape and dark green, needle-like leaves that are typically arranged in two rows. The tree produces small, cylindrical cones that mature to a reddish-brown color, containing winged seeds. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in mountainous regions with well-drained soils.

This species is valued for its timber and is also used in ornamental horticulture.
