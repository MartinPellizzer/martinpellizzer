Poikilacanthus pochutlensis

Poikilacanthus pochutlensis, commonly known as the Mexican flame tree, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its striking, flame-like orange and red flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges and can grow up to 10 meters tall. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The fruit is a woody capsule that contains several seeds.
