Phyllanthus hortensis

Phyllanthus hortensis, commonly known as the garden phyllanthus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is a small shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, and its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a slightly rough texture. It is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and is often used in traditional medicine.

The plant's botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental and medicinal purposes in its native habitat.
