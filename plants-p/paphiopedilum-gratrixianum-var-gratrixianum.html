Paphiopedilum gratrixianum var. gratrixianum

Paphiopedilum gratrixianum var. gratrixianum, commonly known as the Gratrixian lady's slipper, is a tropical orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers with a distinctive slipper-shaped pouch. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are broad and glossy, often arranged in a rosette pattern.

It blooms in late spring to early summer, producing fragrant flowers that attract specific pollinators.
