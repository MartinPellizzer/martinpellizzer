Pogostemon heyneanus

Pogostemon heyneanus, commonly known as the Indian balm, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving them a fuzzy texture. Its small, tubular flowers are typically purple or blue, arranged in whorled clusters at the leaf axils.

Pogostemon heyneanus is valued for its medicinal properties, particularly for its anti-inflammatory and analgesic effects.
