Potamogeton plantagineus subsp. fluitans

Potamogeton plantagineus subsp.

fluitans, commonly known as the floating pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere. It typically grows in still or slow-moving freshwater environments such as lakes, ponds, and wetlands.

The plant has long, slender stems that are often fully submerged, with small, oval-shaped leaves arranged in whorls. It produces tiny, greenish flowers that are clustered in spike-like inflorescences, and it plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.

Its presence can indicate good water quality and is sometimes used as a bioindicator in ecological assessments.
