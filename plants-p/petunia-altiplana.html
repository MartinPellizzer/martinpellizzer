Petunia altiplana

Petunia altiplana, commonly known as the Andean petunia, is a species native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces clusters of small, trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant typically blooms in shades of violet, blue, and white, adapting to the cool and dry conditions of its native habitat. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Petunia altiplana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and resilience in challenging environments.
