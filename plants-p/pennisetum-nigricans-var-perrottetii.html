Pennisetum nigricans var. perrottetii

Pennisetum nigricans var. perrottetii, commonly known as blackfoot daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial grass that produces clusters of small, pink to purple flowers during the summer months. The plant is characterized by its dark, almost black stems, which give it its common name. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Its drought tolerance and ability to attract pollinators make it a valuable addition to native plant landscapes.
