Poliomintha dendritica

Poliomintha dendritica, commonly known as the creeping poliomintha, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the western United States.

It features small, opposite leaves that are typically elliptical or ovate in shape. The plant produces clusters of tiny, tubular flowers that are usually pink or purple in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

Poliomintha dendritica is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, supporting various pollinators.
