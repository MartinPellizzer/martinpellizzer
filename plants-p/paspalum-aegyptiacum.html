Paspalum aegyptiacum

Paspalum aegyptiacum, commonly known as Egyptian grass, is a perennial grass species native to Africa and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, blade-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions and is often found in disturbed areas or along roadsides.

Paspalum aegyptiacum is also used in some regions for forage or as a ornamental plant due to its hardy nature.
