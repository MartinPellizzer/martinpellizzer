Ponera albida

Ponera albida, commonly known as the white ant plant, is a unique species belonging to the Araceae family.

It is characterized by its striking white, waxy leaves and a distinctive inflorescence that resembles a flower. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its foliage is not only aesthetically pleasing but also plays a role in its photosynthetic process.

Ponera albida is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its visual appeal and adaptability to indoor conditions.
