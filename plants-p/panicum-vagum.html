Panicum vagum

Panicum vagum, commonly known as the slender panicgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as swamps, marshes, and along stream banks. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces fluffy, panicle-like inflorescences that are often pinkish or reddish in color. It is characterized by its ability to thrive in wet conditions and its tolerance to periodic flooding.

Panicum vagum plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
