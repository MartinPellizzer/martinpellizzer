Pyrenaria khasiana

Pyrenaria khasiana, commonly known as the Khasi orchid, is a flowering plant native to the northeastern regions of India.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded forest environments, often on tree trunks or rocks. Its leaves are broad and glossy, and it produces small, clustered inflorescences.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
