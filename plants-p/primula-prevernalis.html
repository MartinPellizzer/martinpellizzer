Primula prevernalis

Primula prevernalis, commonly known as the early primrose, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Primulaceae family and typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, showy flowers in shades of yellow and white, which bloom in early spring. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in a fine, hairy texture.

Primula prevernalis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is an important early nectar source for pollinators.
