Pyrus semiustulata

Pyrus semiustulata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved pear.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. The plant features narrow, elongated leaves that are often covered in a fine pubescence. Its flowers are typically white or pale yellow, appearing in clusters, and it produces small, edible fruits.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
