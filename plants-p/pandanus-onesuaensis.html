Pandanus onesuaensis

Pandanus onesuaensis, commonly known as the New Caledonian pandanus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Pandanaceae.

It is native to the island of New Caledonia in the South Pacific and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically clustered in spike-like inflorescences. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in coastal or wetland areas.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has limited use in traditional practices.
