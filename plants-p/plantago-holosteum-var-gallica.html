Plantago holosteum var. gallica

Plantago holosteum var. gallica, commonly known as the greater plantain, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features broad, lance-shaped leaves with a distinctive ribbed texture and a waxy surface. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in dense spikes, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found along roadsides, fields, and waste areas.

This species is known for its medicinal properties and has been used historically for its healing abilities.
