Pelargonium × dysgenon

Pelargonium × dysgenon, commonly known as the ivy-leaved geranium, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Geraniaceae family.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and heart-shaped, slightly serrated leaves that resemble ivy leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, five-petaled flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, which bloom throughout the growing season. It is widely cultivated as an ornamental plant for hanging baskets and containers due to its attractive foliage and flowers.

Pelargonium × dysgenon prefers well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it a versatile choice for gardeners.
