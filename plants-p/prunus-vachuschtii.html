Prunus vachuschtii

Prunus vachuschtii, commonly known as the Japanese cherry, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its ornamental value, particularly during spring when it produces clusters of pink or white flowers. The plant belongs to the Rosaceae family and is known for its heart-shaped leaves and smooth, grey bark. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.

The fruit, which is a small, sweet drupe, is edible but less commonly consumed compared to its ornamental significance.
