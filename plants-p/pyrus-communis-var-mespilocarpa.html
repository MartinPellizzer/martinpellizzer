Pyrus communis var. mespilocarpa

Pyrus communis var. mespilocarpa, commonly known as the Chinese white pear, is a cultivar of the common pear tree.

It is characterized by its large, white fruits with a smooth, thin skin and a sweet, juicy flesh. The plant typically grows as a deciduous tree with broad, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of yellow-green flowers in spring. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is prized for its fruit quality and is often cultivated for both ornamental and culinary purposes.
