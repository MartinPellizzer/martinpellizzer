Phaseolus oblongus var. truncatus

Phaseolus oblongus var. truncatus, commonly known as the scarlet runner bean, is a climbing legume native to the Americas.

It produces vibrant red or orange flowers that give way to elongated, cylindrical pods. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets and is characterized by its vigorous vining habit. It thrives in warm climates and requires full sun for optimal growth.

This variety is valued for its edible beans, which are rich in protein and commonly used in culinary dishes.
