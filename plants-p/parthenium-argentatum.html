Parthenium argentatum

Parthenium argentatum, commonly known as the silverleaf nightshade, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its silvery-white undersides on its leaves, which give it its common name. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the summer months. It is an annual herb that thrives in dry, disturbed soils and is often found in arid environments.

Parthenium argentatum is known for its allelopathic properties, which can inhibit the growth of other plants around it.
