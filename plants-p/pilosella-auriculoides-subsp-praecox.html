Pilosella auriculoides subsp. praecox

Pilosella auriculoides subsp.

praecox, commonly known as the early pilosella, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae. It is characterized by its small, yellowish to white composite flowers that appear in clusters.

The plant has basal leaves that are usually hairy and spoon-shaped, giving it a distinctive appearance. It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in grasslands and open habitats.
