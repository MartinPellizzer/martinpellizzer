Ptychogyne flexuosa

Ptychogyne flexuosa, commonly known as the zigzag sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of water bodies, where it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. The plant has slender, grass-like leaves that are often curved or zigzagged, giving it its common name. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are clustered in dense, cylindrical inflorescences, and it produces small, three-lobed fruits.

Ptychogyne flexuosa plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
