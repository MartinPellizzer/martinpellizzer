Pilocarpus racemosus subsp. viridulus

Pilocarpus racemosus subsp.

viridulus, commonly known as green pilocarpus, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Brazil. It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters.

The plant produces small, round fruits that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in humid, shaded environments.

Its leaves are rich in alkaloids, making it a subject of interest in botanical and pharmacological studies.
