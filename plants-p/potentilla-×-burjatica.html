Potentilla × burjatica

Potentilla × burjatica, commonly known as the Afghan cinquefoil, is a hybrid shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces clusters of bright yellow, five-petaled flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. Its foliage consists of small, rounded leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

Potentilla × burjatica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardiness and visual interest.
