Pariti sterculiifolium

Pariti sterculiifolium, commonly known as the African senna, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in dry, rocky soils. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are compound, with small, oval-shaped leaflets that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
