Pinckneya bracteata

Pinckneya bracteata, commonly known as the pinkroot, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant has a distinctive, branched root system that is often harvested for its medicinal properties. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Pinckneya bracteata thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas.
