Pelargonium × papyraceum

Pelargonium × papyraceum, commonly known as the paper plant, is a species native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its delicate, papery leaves that are often pale green or white, giving it a unique appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink, red, or white, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light to flourish.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in gardens with suitable conditions.
