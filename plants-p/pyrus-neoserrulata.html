Pyrus neoserrulata

Pyrus neoserrulata, commonly known as the Japanese white pear, is a deciduous tree native to Japan.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and alternate, pinnately compound leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in spring, followed by edible, sweet fruits that are similar to apples.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a fruit-bearing plant in temperate climates.
