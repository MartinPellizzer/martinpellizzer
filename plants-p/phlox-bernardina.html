Phlox bernardina

Phlox bernardina, commonly known as Bernard's phlox, is a perennial flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Polemoniaceae family and is characterized by its showy, clustered flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and features narrow, grass-like leaves. Its flowers are usually pink to purple in color, with five petals and a distinctive spurred shape.

Phlox bernardina thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and natural landscapes.
