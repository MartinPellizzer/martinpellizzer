Pouteria obscura

Pouteria obscura, commonly known as the black sapote, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and produces small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. The fruit is a rough, greenish-yellow berry that turns dark when ripe, containing a single large, creamy seed. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
