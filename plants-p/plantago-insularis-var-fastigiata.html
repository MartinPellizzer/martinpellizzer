Plantago insularis var. fastigiata

Plantago insularis var. fastigiata, commonly known as the island plantain, is a perennial herb native to certain island regions.

It features upright, narrow leaves that are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in a spike, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal or rocky habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local insect populations.
