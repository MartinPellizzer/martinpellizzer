Peperomia pterocaulis

Peperomia pterocaulis, commonly known as the silver mitten plant, is a compact, low-growing evergreen shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It features thick, fleshy, waxy leaves that are typically gray-green in color, with a distinctive mitten-like shape. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually green or reddish, followed by tiny, round berries. It thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Its unique foliage and ease of care contribute to its popularity among plant enthusiasts.
