Phyllocactus crenatus

Phyllocactus crenatus, commonly known as the zigzag cactus, is a unique member of the Cactaceae family native to South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, undulating, leaf-like stems that form a zigzag pattern, which helps it conserve water in arid environments. The plant produces small, white or yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It is a spineless cactus, making it an attractive and low-maintenance choice for indoor and outdoor gardening.

Its adaptability to various light conditions and drought tolerance make it a popular ornamental plant.
