Piper humorigaudens

Piper humorigaudens, commonly known as the yellow pepper vine, is a climbing shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many species of pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its slender, woody stems and heart-shaped leaves with a slightly rough texture. Its small, yellow flowers are clustered in spikes and are followed by small, round berries that turn red when mature.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
