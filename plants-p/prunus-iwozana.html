Prunus iwozana

Prunus iwozana, commonly known as the Iwozana cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-branched structure and oval-shaped leaves that turn red in autumn. The plant produces small, white flowers in early spring, followed by edible, sweet fruits that are similar to cherries. Prunus iwozana thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for gardens and landscapes.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its fruit and floral display.
