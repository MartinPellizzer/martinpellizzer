Pithecoctenium vitalba var. extratropicum

Pithecoctenium vitalba var. extratropicum, commonly known as the tropical fig-leaf tree, is a variant of the species native to tropical regions.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense, velvety pubescence. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences that develop into edible fig-like fruits. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in lowland forests.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in both horticultural and ecological contexts.
