Philodendron wurdackii

Philodendron wurdackii, commonly known as the Wurdackii philodendron, is a tropical climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It features heart-shaped, glossy leaves that are typically dark green with a slightly waxy texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are followed by bright red berries, which are an important food source for birds. It thrives in humid, warm environments with bright, indirect light.

Its unique foliage makes it a popular choice for indoor gardening and as a houseplant.
