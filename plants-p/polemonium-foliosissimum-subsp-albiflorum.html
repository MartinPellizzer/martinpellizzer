Polemonium foliosissimum subsp. albiflorum

Polemonium foliosissimum subsp.

albiflorum, commonly known as the whiteflower bluebell, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States. It features slender, upright stems that bear lance-shaped, dark green leaves with prominent veins.

The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale pink, blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, wooded areas and is often found in rich, well-drained soils.

Its foliage and floral structure make it a distinctive and ornamental addition to woodland gardens.
