Piper taboganum var. rugosum

Piper taboganum var. rugosum, commonly known as the rough-skinned pepper plant, is a tropical vine native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its woody stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that develop into elongated, fleshy berries. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties.

The varietal name 'rugosum' refers to the rough, textured surface of its leaves.
