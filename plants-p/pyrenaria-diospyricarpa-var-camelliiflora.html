Pyrenaria diospyricarpa var. camelliiflora

Pyrenaria diospyricarpa var. camelliiflora, commonly known as the Camellia-flowered persimmon, is a unique variant of the persimmon species.

It is characterized by its striking, camellia-like flowers that bloom in shades of red and pink, adding ornamental value to gardens. The plant produces small, edible fruits that are typically orange to reddish in color when mature. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is prized for both its aesthetic appeal and its culinary uses.
