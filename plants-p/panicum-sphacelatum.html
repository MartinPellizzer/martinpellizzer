Panicum sphacelatum

Panicum sphacelatum, commonly known as silver panicum, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is a perennial grass that typically grows in moist to wet habitats, such as marshes and swamps. The plant features slender, silver-haired stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. Its inflorescence consists of dense, spike-like clusters of small, greenish to brownish flowers.

Panicum sphacelatum plays a role in stabilizing wetland soils and provides habitat for various small animals and insects.
