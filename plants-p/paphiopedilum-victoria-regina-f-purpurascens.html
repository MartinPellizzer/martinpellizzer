Paphiopedilum victoria-regina f. purpurascens

Paphiopedilum victoria-regina f. purpurascens, commonly known as the Victoria Regina Purple Flame orchid, is a rare and striking orchid species native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its intricate, spurred lip and vibrant purple-pink floral display. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its leaves are broad and leathery, providing structural support and aiding in moisture retention.

This cultivar is highly sought after by orchid enthusiasts for its unique appearance and challenging cultivation requirements.
