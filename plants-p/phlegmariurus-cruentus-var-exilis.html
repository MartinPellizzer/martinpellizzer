Phlegmariurus cruentus var. exilis

Phlegmariurus cruentus var. exilis, commonly known as the slender blood moss, is a variety of the blood moss species native to the moist, shaded environments of tropical and subtropical regions.

It is a type of liverwort, specifically a thallose hepatic, characterized by its flat, ribbon-like structure and vibrant red or purple pigmentation. The plant thrives in humid, nutrient-rich substrates such as decaying wood or mossy rock surfaces. Its botanical features include a dense, compact growth form and the presence of specialized structures called gemmae for vegetative reproduction.

This variety is often found in association with other epiphytic and lithophytic plants in forest ecosystems.
