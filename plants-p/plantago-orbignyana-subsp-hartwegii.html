Plantago orbignyana subsp. hartwegii

Plantago orbignyana subsp.

hartwegii, commonly known as Hartweg's plantain, is a perennial herb native to South America, particularly found in regions of Argentina and Chile. It is characterized by its basal rosette of broad, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs.

The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in dense spikes, which bloom during the spring and early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands or along riverbanks.

This subspecies is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
