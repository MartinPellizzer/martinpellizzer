Primula cortusoides var. amoena

Primula cortusoides var. amoena, commonly known as the amoena primrose, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe.

It features dense clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow, often with a pinkish tinge. The plant has hairy, ovate leaves that are typically arranged in a basal rosette, and it thrives in cool, moist environments. It is often found in woodland areas and rocky soils, preferring partial shade to full sun.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
