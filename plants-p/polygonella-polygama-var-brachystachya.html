Polygonella polygama var. brachystachya

Polygonella polygama var. brachystachya, commonly known as the narrow-stalked mountain moss, is a variant of the Polygonella genus within the Cyperaceae family.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its dense, cushion-like growth form and small, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as rocky slopes and streambanks. Its flowering structures, known as spikelets, are compact and contain numerous tiny flowers.

This variety is notable for its adaptability to high-altitude and arid conditions, making it a resilient species in mountainous regions.
