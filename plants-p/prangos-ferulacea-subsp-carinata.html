Prangos ferulacea subsp. carinata

Prangos ferulacea subsp.

carinata, commonly known as the Carinate Ferulacea, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It features upright stems with lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and have a distinctive ribbed structure.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in dense, terminal clusters, which bloom in the summer months. Its root system is fibrous and can reach considerable depth, allowing it to thrive in well-drained, rocky soils.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and flowering habit.
