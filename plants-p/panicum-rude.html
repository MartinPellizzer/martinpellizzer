Panicum rude

Panicum rude, commonly known as rough panicgrass, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It is characterized by its coarse, hairy leaves and spikes of pale green to brownish flowers. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and fields, and thrives in full sun. It has a fibrous root system and can spread through both seeds and rhizomes.

Its drought tolerance and ability to colonize open areas make it an important pioneer species in ecological succession.
