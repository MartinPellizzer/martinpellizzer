Paspalum scrobiculatum var. bispiculatum

Paspalum scrobiculatum var. bispiculatum, commonly known as the bispiculate paspalum, is a perennial grass native to South America.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant is characterized by its distinctive two-keeled lemma, which gives it its specific epithet "bispiculatum.". It thrives in wetland environments and is often found in marshy areas or along water edges.

This species is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
