Pleurothallis subreniformis

Pleurothallis subreniformis, commonly known as the "butterfly orchid," is a species of orchid native to the cloud forests of Central and South America.

It is a small, epiphytic plant that thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant features delicate, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink. It is characterized by its unique, butterfly-like appearance, which gives it its common name.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native range.
