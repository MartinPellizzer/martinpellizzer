Pittosporum viridiflorum var. kruegeri

Pittosporum viridiflorum var. kruegeri, commonly known as the Krueger's Pittosporum, is a shrub or small tree native to New Zealand.

It features glossy, dark green leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a leathery texture. The plant produces clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in spring, followed by dark blue, edible berries. It thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it suitable for coastal gardens.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract native birds.
