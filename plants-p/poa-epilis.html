Poa epilis

Poa epilis, commonly known as the silky bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the northern regions of North America.

It is characterized by its fine, blue-green foliage and dense, tufted growth habit. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full to partial sunlight. Its slender, cylindrical seed heads are often tinged with purple or pink, adding visual interest to its appearance.

Poa epilis is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to form dense, attractive lawns.
