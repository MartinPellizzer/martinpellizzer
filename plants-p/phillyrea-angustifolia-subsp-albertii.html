Phillyrea angustifolia subsp. albertii

Phillyrea angustifolia subsp.

albertii, commonly known as the Algerian Phillyrea, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its small, leathery leaves that are typically dark green and glossy.

The plant produces small, white or yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences. It is adapted to dry, rocky soils and is often found in coastal areas.

This subspecies is noted for its slow growth and long lifespan, making it a resilient plant in harsh environments.
