Pelargonium × cartilagineum

Pelargonium × cartilagineum, commonly known as the African ice plant, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, thick leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid conditions. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and red, typically blooming in late winter to early spring. Its scientific name reflects its hybrid origin, as it is a cross between two species of geranium.

Pelargonium × cartilagineum is popular as a decorative plant due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
