Paepalanthus consanguineus

Paepalanthus consanguineus, commonly known as the red-bellied spider plant, is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that are typically dark green with a reddish or purplish tint on the underside. The plant produces small, white, spider-like flowers that bloom in summer. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and ease of care.

Paepalanthus consanguineus is adapted to grow in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.
