Peperomia tetraphylla f. protractifolia

Peperomia tetraphylla f. protractifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaf peperomia, is a compact, low-growing perennial plant native to tropical regions.

It features ovate to lance-shaped leaves that are often glossy and have a slightly waxy texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in shades of greenish-white, which are typically borne on short, erect spikes. Its foliage is characterized by a broad, spreading habit, making it suitable for container gardening or as a ground cover.

It thrives in bright, indirect light and prefers well-draining soil with moderate moisture levels.
