Pyrus osteomeles

Pyrus osteomeles, commonly known as the Japanese whitebeam, is a deciduous tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and oval-shaped leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. The tree produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in early spring, followed by edible, sweet-tart fruits known as "kazinoko.". Its foliage turns a striking red or orange in the fall, adding ornamental value to gardens.

Pyrus osteomeles is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to various soil types.
