Poincianella palmeri

Poincianella palmeri, commonly known as the Palmer's poinciana, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its striking, fern-like leaves and clusters of bright red flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, making it well adapted to desert conditions. Its rapid growth and ornamental value make it a popular choice for landscaping in dry regions.

Poincianella palmeri is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen, which improves soil fertility.
