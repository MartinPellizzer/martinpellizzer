Phyllanthus anisophyllioides

Phyllanthus anisophyllioides, commonly known as the African buttertree, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It is a small to medium-sized shrub that typically grows in sandy soils along coastal areas. The plant has alternate, simple, and often elliptical leaves that are usually pubescent. Its flowers are small and greenish-white, arranged in clusters, and it produces small, fleshy, yellow fruits.

The species is noted for its ecological importance and has been used in traditional medicine.
