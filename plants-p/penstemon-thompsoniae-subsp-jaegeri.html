Penstemon thompsoniae subsp. jaegeri

Penstemon thompsoniae subsp.

jaegeri, commonly known as Thompson's beardtongue, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, tubular flowers that range in color from purple to pink.

The plant typically thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves, and it produces distinctive, hair-like bracts beneath the flowers.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
