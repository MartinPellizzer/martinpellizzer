Pityopsis graminifolia var. aequilifolia

Pityopsis graminifolia var. aequilifolia, commonly known as the narrowleaf golden aster, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and clusters of yellow to orange flowers. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as prairies and dry meadows. It is known for its delicate appearance and is often found in the central and western United States.

The variety aequilifolia is distinguished by its narrower leaves compared to the typical form of Pityopsis graminifolia.
