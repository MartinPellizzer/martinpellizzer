Peponia kilimandscharica var. holstii

Peponia kilimandscharica var. holstii, commonly known as the Kilimanjaro melon, is a variant of the species native to the high-altitude regions of East Africa.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its sprawling growth habit and large, lobed leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers that are followed by orange to red, fleshy fruits resembling small melons. The fruit is edible, with a sweet and aromatic flavor, and is often used in local cuisines.

It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun exposure for optimal growth.
