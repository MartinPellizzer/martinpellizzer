Panisea bia

Panisea bia, commonly known as the golden bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters and prefers well-drained soil. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture.

Panisea bia blooms in late spring to early summer, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
