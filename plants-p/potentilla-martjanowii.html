Potentilla martjanowii

Potentilla martjanowii, commonly known as the Karakorum potentilla, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically narrow and hairy, adapted to withstand harsh alpine conditions.

Potentilla martjanowii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens and alpine settings.
