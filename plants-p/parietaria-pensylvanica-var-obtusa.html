Parietaria pensylvanica var. obtusa

Parietaria pensylvanica var. obtusa, commonly known as the broad-leaved lungwort, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, broad leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in the summer months. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland areas and streambanks.

Its leaves are often used in traditional herbal medicine for their potential respiratory benefits.
