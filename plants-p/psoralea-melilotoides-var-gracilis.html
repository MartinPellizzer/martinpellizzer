Psoralea melilotoides var. gracilis

Psoralea melilotoides var. gracilis, commonly known as slender melilot, is a perennial legume native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in dense spikes. The plant has trifoliate leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and open habitats.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential ecological benefits in supporting pollinators.
