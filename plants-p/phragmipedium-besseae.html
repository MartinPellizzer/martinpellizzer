Phragmipedium besseae

Phragmipedium besseae, commonly known as the Bessa's slipper orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the cloud forests of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, elongated flower spike that bears a single, showy orchid flower with a distinctive pouch-like lip. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments and thrives in well-drained, nutrient-rich soils. Its leaves are broad and strap-like, often forming a dense rosette at the base.

Phragmipedium besseae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts for its unique and elegant blooms.
