Primula balbisii subsp. hungarica

Primula balbisii subsp.

hungarica, commonly known as the Hungarian primrose, is a flowering plant native to the Carpathian Basin. It is characterized by its low-growing habit, with hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves.

The plant produces clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink and white. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate blooms.
