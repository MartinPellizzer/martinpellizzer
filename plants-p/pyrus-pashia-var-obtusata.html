Pyrus pashia var. obtusata

Pyrus pashia var. obtusata, commonly known as the blunt-leaved whitebeam, is a variant of the whitebeam tree native to the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves that are typically leathery and have a smooth margin. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters, which are followed by edible, yellowish fruits. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in mountainous regions with well-drained soils.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in its native habitat.
