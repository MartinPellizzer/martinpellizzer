Pleurothallis solium

Pleurothallis solium, commonly known as the white-veined orchid, is a species native to the cloud forests of Central and South America.

It is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, often fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are usually elongated and have a waxy surface to reduce water loss.

Pleurothallis solium is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
