Potentilla effusa var. rupincola

Potentilla effusa var. rupincola, commonly known as the alpine cinquefoil, is a perennial flowering plant native to mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun. The plant produces clusters of small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. Its leaves are compound, consisting of five oval-shaped leaflets, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It is valued in horticulture for its hardiness and ornamental value in rocky gardens.
