Polygonum perforatum

Polygonum perforatum, commonly known as Indian spinach or kangkong, is a flowering plant native to Asia.

It is a member of the Polygonaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, greenish flowers. The plant is often cultivated for its edible leaves, which are rich in nutrients such as vitamins A and C. It thrives in moist, tropical environments and is commonly found in wetlands and along waterways.

Its rapid growth and ability to spread through rhizomes make it both a useful crop and a potential weed.
