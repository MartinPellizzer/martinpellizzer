Pachygone columbica

Pachygone columbica, commonly known as the Columbian thickleaf, is a unique plant species native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that help it retain moisture in its arid habitat. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink, adding to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native range.
