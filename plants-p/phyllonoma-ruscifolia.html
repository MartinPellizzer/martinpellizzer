Phyllonoma ruscifolia

Phyllonoma ruscifolia, commonly known as the leafy spurge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America.

It features a rosette of basal leaves that are typically fleshy and covered in a waxy cuticle. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in dense clusters at the end of its stems. Its leaves are characterized by their narrow, lance-shaped form and a distinctive, almost succulent texture.

Phyllonoma ruscifolia is adapted to grow in dry, rocky environments and is often found in arid regions.
