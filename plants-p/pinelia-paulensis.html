Pinelia paulensis

Pinelia paulensis, commonly known as the Brazilian yew, is a flowering plant native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is a small evergreen tree or shrub that typically grows to a height of 3 to 5 meters. The plant features dark green, leathery leaves and produces small, inconspicuous white flowers. Its fruit is a capsule that contains numerous tiny seeds.

Pinelia paulensis is valued for its ornamental qualities and has some traditional medicinal uses.
