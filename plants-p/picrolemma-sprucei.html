Picrolemma sprucei

Picrolemma sprucei, commonly known as the spruce's picrolemma, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Polygonaceae.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and distinctive, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-white in color, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Native to the mountainous regions of Central America, it thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and unique appearance, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and by plant enthusiasts.
