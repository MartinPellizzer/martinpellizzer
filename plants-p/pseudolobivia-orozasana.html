Pseudolobivia orozasana

Pseudolobivia orozasana, commonly known as the Orózaña's pseudolobivia, is a unique cactus species native to the arid regions of northern Argentina.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems covered in white, woolly spines. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, adding a striking contrast to its rugged appearance. It is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments with minimal water requirements.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat threats, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
