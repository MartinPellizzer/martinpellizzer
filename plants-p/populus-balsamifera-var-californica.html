Populus balsamifera var. californica

Populus balsamifera var. californica, commonly known as the California poplar, is a deciduous tree native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its smooth, light-gray bark and heart-shaped leaves with a serrated margin. The tree produces small, catkin-like flowers in early spring, followed by fluffy, white seed heads that are dispersed by the wind. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in riparian zones along rivers and streams.

This variety is notable for its rapid growth and adaptability to various environmental conditions.
