Paspalum carinatum

Paspalum carinatum, commonly known as the spikegrass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions.

Paspalum carinatum is often used in landscaping and erosion control due to its hardiness and aesthetic appeal.
