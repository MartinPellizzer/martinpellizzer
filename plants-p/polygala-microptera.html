Polygala microptera

Polygala microptera, commonly known as the small-leaved milkweed or lesser milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its slender stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in grassy areas and along roadsides, preferring well-drained soils. Its flowers are usually pale pink to white, with five petals and a distinctive spurred structure.

It is often used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.
