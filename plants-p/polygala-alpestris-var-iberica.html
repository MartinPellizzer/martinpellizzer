Polygala alpestris var. iberica

Polygala alpestris var. iberica, commonly known as the Iberian milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in alpine or subalpine regions. The plant features slender stems and small, clustered flowers that are usually pale yellow or greenish in color. Its leaves are narrow and often hairy, adapted to withstand the harsh conditions of its habitat.

It plays a role in local ecosystems by providing nectar for pollinators and stabilizing soil in its native environment.
