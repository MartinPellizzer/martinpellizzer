Physalis timorensis

Physalis timorensis, commonly known as the Timor waxapple, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia, particularly Indonesia.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its distinctive inflated calyx that persists around the fruit. The plant produces small, yellow-orange berries enclosed in a papery, lantern-like structure. Its leaves are ovate with a rough texture, and it thrives in tropical and subtropical climates.

The fruit is often used in local cuisines and has traditional medicinal uses in some regions.
