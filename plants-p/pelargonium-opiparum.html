Pelargonium opiparum

Pelargonium opiparum, commonly known as the sweet geranium, is a species of flowering plant in the Geraniaceae family.

It is native to South Africa and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and vibrant, showy flowers. The plant produces clusters of small, pink to red flowers that bloom throughout the growing season. Its leaves are typically rounded and covered in fine hairs, giving them a soft, velvety texture.

Pelargonium opiparum is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its ornamental value and adaptability to various growing conditions.
