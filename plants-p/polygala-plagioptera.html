Polygala plagioptera

Polygala plagioptera, commonly known as the narrow-leaved milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its slender, often hairy stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. Its flowers are usually pale yellow to greenish in color, and the leaves are narrow and opposite.

It is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and shrublands.
