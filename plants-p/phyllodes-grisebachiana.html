Phyllodes grisebachiana

Phyllodes grisebachiana, commonly known as the Grey-leafed Acacia, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its unique phyllodes, which are flattened, leaf-like structures that serve as photosynthetic organs. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, typically during the warmer months. Its phyllodes are often covered in a fine, greyish pubescence, giving it its common name.

It is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in harsh climates.
