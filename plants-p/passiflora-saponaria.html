Passiflora saponaria

Passiflora saponaria, commonly known as the soapberry passionflower, is a climbing vine native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its striking, intricate flowers that feature five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments. The plant produces small, round, yellowish fruits that contain a single seed. Its leaves are palmately lobed, and the plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The name "saponaria" refers to the soap-like substance found in its fruits, which can be used as a natural detergent.
