Poa nemoralis var. loiseaui

Poa nemoralis var. loiseaui, commonly known as the Loiseau's sheep's fescue, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands, woodland edges, and disturbed soils. The plant has slender, upright stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often hairy along the edges. Its inflorescence consists of a loose cluster of spikelets, each bearing small, two-rowed flowers.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
