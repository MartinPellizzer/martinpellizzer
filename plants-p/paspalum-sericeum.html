Paspalum sericeum

Paspalum sericeum, commonly known as silky paspalum, is a perennial grass native to South America.

It is characterized by its fine, silky texture and silver-gray foliage, which gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It is often found in moist, well-drained soils in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
