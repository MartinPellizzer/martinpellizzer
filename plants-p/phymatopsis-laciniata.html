Phymatopsis laciniata

Phymatopsis laciniata, commonly known as the Texas spiderwort, is a perennial flowering plant native to the southern United States.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of small, showy blue or purple flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and prairies. It is adapted to withstand drought conditions and has a deep root system.

Phymatopsis laciniata is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally by Native American tribes.
