Pilosocereus chrysostele subsp. chrysostele

Pilosocereus chrysostele subsp.

chrysostele, commonly known as the golden star cactus, is a species native to Mexico. It is a columnar cactus with a distinctive appearance, featuring a tall, narrow stem covered in dense, white to pale blue spines.

The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as bats and moths. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, requiring well-drained soil and minimal water.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in cactus gardens.
