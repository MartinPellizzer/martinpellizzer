Potentilla subvahliana

Potentilla subvahliana, commonly known as the Himalayan cinquefoil, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central and Southern Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its small, showy yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky slopes, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun. It has alternate, pinnately compound leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

Potentilla subvahliana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and floral display.
