Polygonum filicaule var. alpestre

Polygonum filicaule var. alpestre, commonly known as alpine polygonum, is a variant of the common polygonum species characterized by its slender, upright growth habit.

It is native to alpine regions of Europe and Asia, where it thrives in rocky, well-drained soils. The plant features lanceolate leaves that are typically hairy and have a bluish-green hue. Its flowers are small, clustered in loose inflorescences, and are usually pink or purple in color.

It is often used in rock gardens due to its hardy nature and ornamental appeal.
