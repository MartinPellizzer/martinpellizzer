Physaria pachyphylla

Physaria pachyphylla, commonly known as the thickleaf bladderpod, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northwestern Mexico.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that store water, adapting it to arid environments. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard plants.

Physaria pachyphylla is often found in rocky soils and is a common sight in desert landscapes.
