Persicaria lapathifolia subsp. lanata

Persicaria lapathifolia subsp.

lanata, commonly known as woolly knotweed, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and stems, which give it a distinctive, fuzzy appearance.

The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or red, arranged in dense inflorescences. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a notable species within the Polygonaceae family.
