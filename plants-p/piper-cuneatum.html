Piper cuneatum

Piper cuneatum, commonly known as the Indian pepper or hill pepper, is a climbing shrub native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many species of pepper plants. The plant produces small, green berries that turn red when mature, containing aromatic seeds used in traditional medicine and cuisine. Its leaves are often used in herbal remedies due to their medicinal properties.

The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is valued for both its ornamental and practical uses.
