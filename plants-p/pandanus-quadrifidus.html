Pandanus quadrifidus

Pandanus quadrifidus, commonly known as the four-lobed pandanus, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It is a member of the Pandanaceae family and is characterized by its long, sword-shaped leaves that are often used in traditional crafts and cuisine. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its flowers are small and clustered, often producing a fragrant, white or yellowish inflorescence.

The fruit, known as a syncarp, is a cluster of berries that can be used in local culinary practices.
