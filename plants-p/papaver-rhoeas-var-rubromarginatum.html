Papaver rhoeas var. rubromarginatum

Papaver rhoeas var. rubromarginatum, commonly known as the red-edged poppy, is a variant of the common poppy characterized by its distinctive red margins on the petals.

This plant is native to Europe and is often found in disturbed soils such as roadsides and fields. It produces striking flowers with bright orange to red petals that contrast with the dark red veins. The plant is an annual herb with a short stem and is known for its showy, papery blooms.

It is cultivated for ornamental purposes and has historical uses in medicine and dye production.
