Pseudocucumis naudinianus

Pseudocucumis naudinianus, commonly known as the Naudin's false cucumber, is a flowering plant belonging to the Cucurbitaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, and thrives in humid, warm climates. The plant produces small, green, cucurbit-like fruits that are typically harvested when immature. Its leaves are broad and lobed, and it has a climbing habit, often using tendrils to support itself.

The species is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and potential ecological roles in its native habitat.
