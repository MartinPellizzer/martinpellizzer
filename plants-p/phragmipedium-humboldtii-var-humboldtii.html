Phragmipedium humboldtii var. humboldtii

Phragmipedium humboldtii var. humboldtii, commonly known as the Humboldt's phragmipedium, is a species of orchid native to the Andes of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, long-lasting orchid flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. The plant features a pseudobulb and produces multiple racemes of fragrant blooms during the growing season. It thrives in cool, humid environments with partial shade and well-draining soil.

This orchid is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated by enthusiasts for its beauty and unique appearance.
