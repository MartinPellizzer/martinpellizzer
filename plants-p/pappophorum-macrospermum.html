Pappophorum macrospermum

Pappophorum macrospermum, commonly known as the large-seeded grass or the broad-seeded grass, is a species of flowering plant in the grass family Poaceae.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and dense, spike-like inflorescences that bear small, wind-dispersed seeds. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands, where it thrives in temperate climates. Its seeds are equipped with a pappus, a feathery structure that aids in wind dispersal, enhancing its ability to spread over long distances.

The botanical features of Pappophorum macrospermum make it an important component of grassland ecosystems.
