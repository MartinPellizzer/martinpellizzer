Piper pubens

Piper pubens, commonly known as the hairy pepper vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Piperaceae, which includes many species of pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its pubescent (hairy) stems and leaves, which give it its common name. Its small, greenish-white flowers are followed by elongated, narrow fruits that contain the aromatic seeds.

The plant is often grown for its ornamental value and has some traditional uses in folk medicine.
