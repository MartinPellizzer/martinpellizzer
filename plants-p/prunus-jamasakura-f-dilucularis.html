Prunus jamasakura f. dilucularis

Prunus jamasakura f. dilucularis, commonly known as the dilucularis cherry, is a unique variation of the Japanese cherry species.

It is characterized by its distinctive, rounded leaves that give it a compact, bushy appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink, typically blooming in early spring. Its foliage remains lush throughout the growing season, providing ornamental value year-round.

This cultivar is prized in horticulture for its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to various garden settings.
