Plectreca corymbosa

Plectreca corymbosa, commonly known as the clustered bellflower, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia and the Mediterranean.

It is characterized by its clustered inflorescences of bell-shaped flowers, typically in shades of blue or violet. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are arranged oppositely along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Plectreca corymbosa is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
