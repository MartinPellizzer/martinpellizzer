Paronychia pauciflora

Paronychia pauciflora, commonly known as the few-flowered sandwort, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of South Africa.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically forms a dense mat of foliage near the ground. The plant produces small, white or pink flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and summer months. Its leaves are small, fleshy, and adapted to conserve water in dry conditions.

Paronychia pauciflora is often found in rocky soils and is well-suited to xerophytic environments.
