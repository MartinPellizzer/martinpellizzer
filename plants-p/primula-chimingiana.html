Primula chimingiana

Primula chimingiana, commonly known as the Chimingian primrose, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of China.

It features rosettes of dark green, velvety leaves and produces clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink, white, and purple. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and prefers well-drained, fertile soil. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and rockeries.

The flowers bloom in early spring, adding a vibrant touch to the landscape during the cooler months.
