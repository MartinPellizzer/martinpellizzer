Pogostemon rogersii

Pogostemon rogersii, commonly known as the California pogostemon, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants such as mint and basil. The plant features square, hairy stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly serrated edge. Its small, tubular flowers are typically violet or purple, arranged in whorls along the stem.

Pogostemon rogersii is often found in moist, rocky habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
