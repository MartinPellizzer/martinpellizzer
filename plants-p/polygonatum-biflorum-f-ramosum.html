Polygonatum biflorum f. ramosum

Polygonatum biflorum f. ramosum, commonly known as the branched Solomon's seal, is a variant of the Solomon's seal plant native to eastern Asia.

It is a herbaceous perennial that features long, arching stems with lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in clustered umbels, which are typically borne on long, slender pedicels. Its rhizomatous root system is used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

It thrives in shady, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.
