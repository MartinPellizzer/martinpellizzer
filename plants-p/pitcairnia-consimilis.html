Pitcairnia consimilis

Pitcairnia consimilis, commonly known as the similar pitcairnia, is a species of bromeliad native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped foliage that forms a central cup to collect rainwater. The plant produces striking, inflorescence with colorful, long-lasting flowers that attract pollinators such as hummingbirds. It thrives in shaded, humid environments and is often found in cloud forests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
