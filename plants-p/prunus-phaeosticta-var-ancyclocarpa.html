Prunus phaeosticta var. ancyclocarpa

Prunus phaeosticta var. ancyclocarpa, commonly known as the red sand cherry, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in early spring. The leaves are ovate with a serrated margin and turn a striking red or orange in the fall. The fruit is a small, yellowish drupe that ripens in late summer.

This plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
