Peperomia parasitica

Peperomia parasitica, commonly known as the parasitic peperomia, is a unique species within the Piperaceae family.

It is a hemiparasitic plant, meaning it derives some of its nutrients from a host plant while also performing photosynthesis. Its small, succulent leaves are often clustered at the base, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often found in shaded, moist environments.

Due to its parasitic nature, it has adapted to thrive in nutrient-poor soils by relying on host plants for essential nutrients.
