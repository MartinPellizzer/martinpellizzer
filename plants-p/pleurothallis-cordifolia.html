Pleurothallis cordifolia

Pleurothallis cordifolia, commonly known as the heart-shaped orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact, epiphytic growth habit. The plant produces small, delicate flowers that are typically white or pale pink, with a distinctive heart-shaped ovary. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining substrates for optimal growth.

Due to its unique morphology and ornamental value, it is a popular choice among orchid enthusiasts.
