Poa chrysantha

Poa chrysantha, commonly known as the golden meadowgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and produces dense, golden-yellow inflorescences that bloom in late summer. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue, and it has a fibrous root system that helps stabilize soil.

Poa chrysantha is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
