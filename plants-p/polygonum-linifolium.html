Polygonum linifolium

Polygonum linifolium, commonly known as silky knotweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the eastern United States.

It features slender, creeping stems that root at the nodes, forming dense mats on moist soil. The leaves are lance-shaped, with a soft, silky texture due to fine hairs covering their surfaces. It produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters during the summer months.

This plant is often found in wetlands, streambanks, and other moist habitats, contributing to soil stabilization and providing habitat for various insects.
