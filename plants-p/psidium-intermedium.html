Psidium intermedium

Psidium intermedium, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or pitanga, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, edible berries that are typically yellow or red when ripe, containing a single seed. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit yield.

The botanical features of Psidium intermedium make it an important species in both ecological and agricultural contexts.
