Piper recurvatum

Piper recurvatum, commonly known as the recurved pepper vine, is a climbing shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many species of pepper plants. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are followed by berries, which can turn red when mature. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped with a rough texture and a distinct aromatic scent.

Piper recurvatum is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
