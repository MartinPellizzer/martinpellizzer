Parodia claviceps

Parodia claviceps, commonly known as the red globe cactus, is a species of cactus native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its globular shape and vibrant red or pink spines, which give it a striking appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers during the warmer months, typically in spring or early summer. Its thick, fleshy stems store water, allowing it to survive in harsh, dry environments.

Parodia claviceps is a popular choice for rock gardens and indoor cactus collections due to its compact size and ornamental value.
