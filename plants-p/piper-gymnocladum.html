Piper gymnocladum

Piper gymnocladum, commonly known as the African pepper or wild pepper, is a tropical shrub native to Africa.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many species of pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its opposite, simple leaves that are usually elliptical in shape. Its small, greenish-white flowers are clustered in spikes, and it produces small, round berries that turn red when mature.

The plant is often used in traditional medicine and as a spice in some regions.
