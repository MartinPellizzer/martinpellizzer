Parsonsia radicans

Parsonsia radicans, commonly known as the climbing bellflower, is a tropical vine native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its long, trailing stems and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant produces small, round fruits that contain seeds enclosed in a fleshy, red aril. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in some regions for its striking floral display.
