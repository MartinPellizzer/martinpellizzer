Philoxerus vermicularis var. aggregatus

Philoxerus vermicularis var. aggregatus, commonly known as the clustered root-veined orchid, is a rare and delicate orchid species native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its unique root system that forms dense, interwoven clusters, which help anchor it in rocky or forested soils. The plant produces small, often inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish or yellowish in color. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires careful cultivation due to its specific ecological needs.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered endangered and is of interest to botanists and conservationists alike.
