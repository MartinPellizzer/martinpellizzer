Papaya cucumerina

Papaya cucumerina, commonly known as the horned papaya, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and a distinctive, horn-like protrusion on its fruit. The plant produces yellow-orange fruits with a sweet, fibrous pulp and black, edible seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

The fruit is often used in culinary applications and traditional medicine due to its nutritional and medicinal properties.
