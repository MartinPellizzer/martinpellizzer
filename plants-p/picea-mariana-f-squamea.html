Picea mariana f. squamea

Picea mariana f. squamea, commonly known as the white spruce, is a subspecies of the spruce tree native to North America.

It is characterized by its dense, conical shape and needle-like leaves that are typically bluish-green in color. The cones are small and woody, containing winged seeds that are dispersed by the wind. This subspecies is well adapted to cold climates and can thrive in a variety of soil types, including those that are poorly drained.

It plays an important ecological role in boreal and subarctic forests, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
