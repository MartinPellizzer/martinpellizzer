Piper laevifolium

Piper laevifolium, commonly known as the broad-leaved pepper plant, is a tropical vine native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species such as black pepper. The plant is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves and small, greenish-white flowers that are often clustered in spikes. Its fruits are small, round berries that turn from green to red as they mature.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its foliage rather than its fruit.
