Polypodiodes falcipinnula

Polypodiodes falcipinnula, commonly known as the narrow-pinnate polypody, is a fern belonging to the family Polypodiaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, feather-like fronds that are typically narrow and deeply divided. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its rhizome is creeping and produces adventitious roots, aiding in its ability to anchor and absorb nutrients.

The spore-bearing structures, called sori, are located on the underside of the fronds, contributing to its reproductive strategy.
