Pisum sativum var. floridum

Pisum sativum var. floridum, commonly known as the Florida pea, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a variant of the common pea, characterized by its trailing growth habit and clusters of small, pea-shaped flowers. The plant produces edible pods that contain several seeds, which are typically used in culinary applications. Botanically, it is distinguished by its hairy stems, compound leaves, and its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil through symbiotic bacteria.

This variety is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive blooms and adaptability to warm climates.
