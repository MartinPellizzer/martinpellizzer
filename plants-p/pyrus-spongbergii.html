Pyrus spongbergii

Pyrus spongbergii, commonly known as the Spongberg pear, is a rare and ornamental species native to China.

It is characterized by its small, clustered leaves and showy, white flowers that appear in early spring. The plant produces small, edible fruits that are typically consumed fresh or used in jams. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it suitable for garden cultivation.

Due to its limited distribution and unique appearance, it is often sought after by horticulturists and plant enthusiasts.
