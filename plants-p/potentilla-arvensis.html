Potentilla arvensis

Potentilla arvensis, commonly known as field cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North America.

It is a low-growing perennial herb that typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer, often in clusters of five. Its leaves are compound, consisting of five oval to lance-shaped leaflets.

Potentilla arvensis thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas.
