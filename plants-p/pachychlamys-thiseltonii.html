Pachychlamys thiseltonii

Pachychlamys thiseltonii, commonly known as the yellow-stemmed orchid, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking yellow stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces fragrant, yellowish-green flowers with a distinctive hooded lip. It typically grows in shady, moist environments such as forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in horticulture, though it remains relatively rare in the wild.
