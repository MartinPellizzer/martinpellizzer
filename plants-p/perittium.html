Perittium

Perittium, commonly known as the bluebell or perittium bellflower, is a flowering plant belonging to the Campanulaceae family.

It is characterized by its striking blue or purple bell-shaped flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in woodland areas and is often found in moist, well-drained soils. Perittium has lance-shaped leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain seeds.

It is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
