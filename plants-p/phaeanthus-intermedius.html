Phaeanthus intermedius

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like foliage and showy, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and blooms in the spring. Its flowers are usually white or pale blue and are arranged in dense, terminal clusters.
