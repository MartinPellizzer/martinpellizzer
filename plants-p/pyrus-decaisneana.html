Pyrus decaisneana

Pyrus decaisneana, commonly known as the De Cailliez pear, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its ornamental value, featuring showy white flowers in spring. The tree produces small, edible fruits that are typically not cultivated for commercial use. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a serrated margin.

Pyrus decaisneana is often grown in gardens for its aesthetic appeal and ecological benefits.
