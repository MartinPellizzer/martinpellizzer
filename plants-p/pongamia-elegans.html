Pongamia elegans

Pongamia elegans, commonly known as the Indian beech, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and compound leaves that are alternate and pinnate. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by bean-like pods containing flattened, dark brown seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Pongamia elegans is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it an important plant in ecological restoration projects.
