Piper cartagoanum

Piper cartagoanum, commonly known as the Cartago pepper or wild black pepper, is a flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to Central America and is characterized by its climbing habit and dark green, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, clustered white flowers that give way to elongated, dark purple berries. Its fruits are used in traditional medicine and as a spice, similar to black pepper.

The botanical features of Piper cartagoanum make it an important species in both ecological and economic contexts.
