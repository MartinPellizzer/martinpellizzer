Prunus mahaleb f. pendula

Prunus mahaleb f. pendula, commonly known as the weeping cherry or weeping almond, is a cultivar of the Prunus mahaleb species.

It is characterized by its weeping growth habit, with long, cascading branches that create a distinctive, ornamental appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in early spring, typically in shades of pink or white. Its leaves are oval-shaped and turn a striking yellow in the fall.

This ornamental tree is often used in gardens for its aesthetic appeal and is relatively easy to care for.
