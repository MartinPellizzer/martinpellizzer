Pseudolaelia medinensis

Pseudolaelia medinensis, commonly known as the Medinensis orchid, is a species native to the Canary Islands.

It is a terrestrial orchid with a compact, rhizomatous growth habit. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers with yellow markings, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
