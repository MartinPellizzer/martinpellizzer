Pyrus taihangshanensis

Pyrus taihangshanensis, commonly known as the Taihangshan pear, is a rare and endemic species native to China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its small, elongated leaves and yellowish-white flowers. The plant produces small, sweet fruits that are typically harvested in late summer. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in mountainous regions with well-drained soil.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a valuable species for botanical research and conservation efforts.
