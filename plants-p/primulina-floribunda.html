Primulina floribunda

Primulina floribunda, commonly known as the golden flame begonia, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, bright orange to yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, making it a popular ornamental plant. The leaves are typically heart-shaped and glossy, providing a lush green contrast to the vibrant blooms. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

This plant is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal in gardens and indoor settings.
