Primula hidakana f. hidakana

Primula hidakana f. hidakana, commonly known as the Hidaka primrose, is a flowering plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as mountain forests and stream sides. It has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of hair.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate beauty.
