Phyllobolus lesliei

Phyllobolus lesliei, commonly known as the Leslie's leaf beetle, is a species of leaf beetle native to the tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its elongated, oval-shaped body and a coloration that ranges from green to brown, often resembling the leaves it inhabits. The beetle's primary diet consists of various species of plants, particularly those in the families Rubiaceae and Euphorbiaceae. It plays a role in the ecological dynamics of its habitat through both herbivory and as a food source for other organisms.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a relatively rare species.
