Pleurothallis expansa

Pleurothallis expansa, commonly known as the broad-leafed orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, often fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid forest environments. Its leaves are usually broad and elliptical, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Pleurothallis expansa is valued in horticulture for its unique floral structure and adaptability to various growing conditions.
