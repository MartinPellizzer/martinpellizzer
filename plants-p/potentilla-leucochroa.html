Potentilla leucochroa

Potentilla leucochroa, commonly known as the white-flowered cinquefoil, is a flowering plant belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, including countries such as Kazakhstan and Mongolia. The plant is characterized by its slender stems and clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are typically divided into five oval-shaped leaflets, giving it the common name "cinquefoil.".

Potentilla leucochroa is often found in rocky or alpine habitats and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments.
