Polygala crinita

Polygala crinita, commonly known as the featherleaf milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, delicate leaves that give it a feathery appearance. The plant produces showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Polygala crinita is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and floral display.
