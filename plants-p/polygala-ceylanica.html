Polygala ceylanica

Polygala ceylanica, commonly known as the Ceylon milk-vetch, is a herbaceous plant native to Sri Lanka.

It belongs to the Polygalaceae family and is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories. Its leaves are opposite and lance-shaped, while the flowers are usually pale green or white with a distinctive spurred structure.

Polygala ceylanica is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
