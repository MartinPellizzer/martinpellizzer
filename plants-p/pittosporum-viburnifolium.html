Pittosporum viburnifolium

Pittosporum viburnifolium, commonly known as the New Zealand whitebeam, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to New Zealand.

It features compound leaves that are often lobed or serrated, with a distinctive silvery underside. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers in clusters during the spring, followed by red, berry-like fruits. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forest margins and rocky areas.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in native ecosystems.
