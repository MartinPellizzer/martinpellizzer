Pycnanthemum arkansanum

Pycnanthemum arkansanum, commonly known as sweet peppergrass, is a perennial herb native to the central and eastern United States.

It features aromatic, lance-shaped leaves with a distinctive sweet, peppery scent when crushed. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically blue to purple in color, arranged in dense clusters at the top of the stems. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in prairies, swamps, and along stream banks.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in herbal medicine.
