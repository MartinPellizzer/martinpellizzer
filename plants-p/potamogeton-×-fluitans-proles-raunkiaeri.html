Potamogeton × fluitans proles raunkiaeri

Potamogeton × fluitans proles raunkiaeri, commonly known as the floating pondweed.

This hybrid plant is a combination of Potamogeton fluitans and P. raunkiaeri, exhibiting characteristics of both parent species. It is a submerged aquatic plant that thrives in freshwater environments, often found in ponds and slow-moving rivers. Its leaves are long and ribbon-like, with a smooth surface and a midrib that is often visible.

The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically found in clusters at the water's surface.
