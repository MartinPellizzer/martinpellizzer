Pleurothallis kelloggii

Pleurothallis kelloggii, commonly known as the Kellogg's pleurothallis, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact, epiphytic growth habit. The plant typically produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and yellow, which bloom during the wet season. Its leaves are usually narrow and grass-like, adapted to its humid, shaded habitat.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is a subject of interest for botanists and orchid enthusiasts.
