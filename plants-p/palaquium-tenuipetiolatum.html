Palaquium tenuipetiolatum

Palaquium tenuipetiolatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf cheesefruit, is a small evergreen tree native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, elongated leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stems. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by edible, spherical fruits with a mild, sweet flavor. The fruit's pericarp is thin and fleshy, containing a single seed.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and for the consumption of its fruit in local cuisines.
