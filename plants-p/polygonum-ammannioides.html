Polygonum ammannioides

Polygonum ammannioides, commonly known as the Ammann's knotweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, which includes other familiar plants like buckwheat and sorrel. The plant is characterized by its erect, branching stems and small, clustered flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are typically oval to lance-shaped with a distinctive reddish or purple hue on the underside.

Polygonum ammannioides is often found in moist or wetland habitats, thriving in environments with high humidity and poor soil conditions.
