Pernettya myrtilloides

Pernettya myrtilloides, commonly known as the dwarf blueberry, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the boreal forests of North America.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its small, dark blue berries that are typically edible. The plant thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and requires a cool, moist environment to flourish. Its leaves are usually elliptical, with a glossy green upper surface and a lighter underside.

The dwarf blueberry is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local wildlife.
