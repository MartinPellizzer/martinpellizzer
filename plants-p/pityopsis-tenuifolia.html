Pityopsis tenuifolia

Pityopsis tenuifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf golden aster, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its delicate, yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in open, sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It is often found in prairies and disturbed areas, playing a role in supporting local pollinators.
