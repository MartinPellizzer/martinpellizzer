Payena grandistipula

Payena grandistipula, commonly known as the large-stippled payena, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 20 meters in height, characterized by its thick, buttressed roots and broad, palmate leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in panicle inflorescences, followed by woody, elongated fruits containing several seeds. Its bark and leaves have been used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

The species is valued for its ecological role in supporting biodiversity in its native habitats.
