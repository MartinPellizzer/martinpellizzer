Pleurothallis superbiens

Pleurothallis superbiens, commonly known as the elegant orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, pendulous flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, with a subtle fragrance that attracts pollinators.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in greenhouses and botanical gardens.
