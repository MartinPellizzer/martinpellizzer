Pithecellobium recordii

Pithecellobium recordii, commonly known as the velvet tamarind, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, yellowish flowers. The plant produces edible pods that contain small, sweet, and acidic fruits, which are often consumed fresh or used in culinary applications. It thrives in tropical climates and is often grown for its ornamental value as well as its edible produce.

The plant also has nitrogen-fixing properties, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
