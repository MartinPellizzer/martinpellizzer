Potentilla monspeliensis var. norvegica

Potentilla monspeliensis var. norvegica, commonly known as the Norwegian cinquefoil, is a perennial shrub native to northern Europe.

It is characterized by its small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine regions. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with five leaflets, and it has a compact, bushy growth habit.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardiness and floral display.
