Polygonum sagittatum f. sericeum

Polygonum sagittatum f. sericeum, commonly known as the silky arrowhead, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its distinctive arrow-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a soft, velvety texture. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

Its fibrous roots and rhizomes allow it to spread vegetatively, making it a common sight in wetland ecosystems.
