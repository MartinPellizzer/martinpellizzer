Populus × gileadensis

Populus × gileadensis, commonly known as the Trembling Aspen or Quaking Aspen, is a hybrid deciduous tree native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-stemmed trunk and heart-shaped leaves that tremble in the wind, giving it its common name. The plant belongs to the Salicaceae family and is known for its rapid growth and ability to colonize disturbed areas. Its bark is smooth and gray, becoming fissured with age, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in early spring.

Populus × gileadensis is also notable for its role in forest ecosystems, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
