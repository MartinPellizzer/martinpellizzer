Pelargonium discipes

Pelargonium discipes, commonly known as the woolly geranium, is a species of flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that gives it a distinctive, almost fuzzy appearance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or red in color. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Pelargonium discipes is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its unique texture and vibrant blooms.
