Psilochloa pilgerana

Psilochloa pilgerana, commonly known as the Pilger's grass, is a perennial grass species native to South America, particularly found in Argentina and Chile.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit with narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas, showcasing adaptations to arid conditions. Its small, spike-like inflorescence contains numerous tiny florets, which are typical of many grass species.

Psilochloa pilgerana is of interest to botanists due to its ecological role and potential for use in restoration projects.
