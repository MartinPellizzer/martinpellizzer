Parodia mammulosa

Parodia mammulosa, commonly known as the "Mammillaria mammulosa," is a species of cactus native to northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stem covered in white, woolly hairs. The plant produces white to pale pink flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and ease of care, it is a popular choice for cactus enthusiasts.
