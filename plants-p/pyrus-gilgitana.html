Pyrus gilgitana

Pyrus gilgitana, commonly known as the Gilgit pear, is a species of pear native to the Himalayan regions of Pakistan and India.

It is a deciduous tree that typically grows at high altitudes, often in mountainous areas with cold climates. The plant produces small, round fruits that are usually yellow or green when mature. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a serrated margin, while its flowers are white and appear in clusters.

The tree is valued for its ornamental qualities and has some traditional uses in local communities.
