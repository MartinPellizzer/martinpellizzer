Prunus translucens

Prunus translucens, commonly known as the translucent cherry, is a rare and delicate flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, branching structure and small, pale pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The leaves are oval-shaped with a smooth texture and a light green hue, while the bark is thin and reddish-brown. This species is known for its unique translucent appearance, particularly in its petals, which seem to refract light in a subtle manner.

Due to its limited distribution and fragile nature, Prunus translucens is considered a rare and endangered species.
