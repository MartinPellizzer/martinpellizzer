Poivrea phaneropetala var. kaudernii

Poivrea phaneropetala var. kaudernii, commonly known as the Kaudernii firethorn, is a shrub native to the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny branches and clusters of bright red berries that persist through winter. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers in early spring, which are followed by the ornamental berries. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a hedge.

Its hardy nature and winter interest make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening in temperate climates.
