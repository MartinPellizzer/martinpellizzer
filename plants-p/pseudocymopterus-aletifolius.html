Pseudocymopterus aletifolius

Pseudocymopterus aletifolius, commonly known as the feather-leaved cymopterus, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces small, clustered white or pale yellow flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its unique foliage and modest floral display, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts of rare plants.
