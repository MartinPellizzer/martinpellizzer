Pyrrhocactus andicola var. robustus

Pyrrhocactus andicola var. robustus, commonly known as the robust pyrrhocactus, is a succulent plant native to South America, particularly found in regions of Brazil and Peru.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers during the warmer months, adding ornamental value. Its leaves are typically reduced to spines, a common adaptation in desert plants to minimize water loss.

This variety is valued for its resilience and ability to flourish with minimal care in suitable growing conditions.
