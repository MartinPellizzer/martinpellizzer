Panicum cristagalli

Panicum cristagalli, commonly known as the crested grass, is a species of grass native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its distinctive, tufted inflorescence that gives it a crested appearance. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and disturbed soils. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like clusters of flowers.

Panicum cristagalli is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its unique visual appeal.
