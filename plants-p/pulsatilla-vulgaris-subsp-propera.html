Pulsatilla vulgaris subsp. propera

Pulsatilla vulgaris subsp.

propera, commonly known as the meadow anemone, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its showy, pink to purple flower heads that resemble a crown of anemones.

The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows, grasslands, and woodland edges. Its leaves are palmately divided, with a hairy surface that helps reduce water loss.

The flowers bloom in early spring, often before the leaves fully emerge, and are pollinated by insects such as bees and flies.
