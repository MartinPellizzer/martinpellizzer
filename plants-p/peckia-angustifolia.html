Peckia angustifolia

Peckia angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf peckia, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and small, clustered yellow flowers. Native to the southeastern United States, it thrives in wetland and swampy environments. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and is often found in moist, sunny habitats.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
