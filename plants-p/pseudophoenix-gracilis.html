Pseudophoenix gracilis

Pseudophoenix gracilis, commonly known as the slender palm, is a small, tree-like plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Arecaceae family and is characterized by its slender, unbranched trunk and a crown of feathery, pinnate leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and is adapted to thrive in humid tropical climates.

Its unique morphology and limited distribution make it a subject of interest in botanical studies.
