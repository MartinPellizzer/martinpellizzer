Pimenta officinalis var. tabasco

Pimenta officinalis var. tabasco, commonly known as Tabasco pepper.

This variant is a cultivar of the pimento tree, native to Central America, and is characterized by its small, fiery red fruits. The plant is a shrub that can grow up to several meters in height, with dark green, glossy leaves. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

The fruits are used extensively in the production of hot sauce, particularly the famous Tabasco brand.
