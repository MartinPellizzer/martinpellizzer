Pilosella fennica subsp. fulvescens

Pilosella fennica subsp.

fulvescens, commonly known as the Finnish hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to northern Europe. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tufted, hairy stems and yellow composite flowers.

The subspecies is distinguished by its reddish-brown stems and leaves that are often covered in fine, white hairs. It typically grows in open, moist habitats such as meadows and wetlands.

The plant is relatively small, reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters, and is often found in the boreal and subarctic regions.
