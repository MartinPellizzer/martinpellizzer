Phaseolus sinensis

Phaseolus sinensis, commonly known as the Chinese bean or evergreen bean, is a climbing legume native to Southeast Asia.

It produces small, pale green pods that contain edible seeds, which are often used in traditional cuisines. The plant has trifoliate leaves and produces small, yellow flowers typical of the Fabaceae family. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often grown for its nutritional value.

Its botanical features make it an important crop in tropical and subtropical regions.
