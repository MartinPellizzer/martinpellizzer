Pandanus novohibernicus

Pandanus novohibernicus, commonly known as the New Ireland pandanus, is a tropical plant native to the island of New Ireland in the Bismarck Archipelago.

It is characterized by its long, narrow leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces large, branched inflorescences that bear small, fragrant flowers, which are often used in traditional crafts and ceremonies. Its roots and leaves are also utilized in local communities for various purposes, including construction and medicinal applications.

The species is adapted to coastal environments and is often found growing in sandy or rocky soils near the shoreline.
