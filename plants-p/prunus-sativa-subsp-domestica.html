Prunus sativa subsp. domestica

Prunus sativa subsp.

domestica, commonly known as the domesticated plum, is a species of fruit tree native to Europe and parts of Asia. It produces small, sweet to tart fruits that are widely consumed fresh or used in cooking.

The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges and produces small, fragrant flowers in the spring. Its fruit develops from the flower and typically ripens in late summer.

The tree is valued for its ornamental beauty and its contribution to both culinary and agricultural practices.
