Phlomis glabrata

Phlomis glabrata, commonly known as narrowleaf phlomis, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the family Lamiaceae, which includes many aromatic plants. The plant is characterized by its upright growth habit, narrow lance-shaped leaves, and spikes of pink to purple flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Its foliage and flowers are often used in traditional herbal medicine for their purported health benefits.
