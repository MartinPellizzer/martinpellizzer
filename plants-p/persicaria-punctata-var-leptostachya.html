Persicaria punctata var. leptostachya

Persicaria punctata var. leptostachya, commonly known as slender-stalked smartweed, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It features upright, branching stems that are often covered in fine hairs, and its leaves are lance-shaped with a slightly rough texture. The plant produces small, pink to red flowers in dense clusters, typically blooming from late summer into early autumn. It thrives in moist to wet soils, often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in aquatic environments.
