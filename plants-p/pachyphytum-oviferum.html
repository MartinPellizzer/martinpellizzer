Pachyphytum oviferum

Pachyphytum oviferum, commonly known as the moon cactus, is a succulent native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. The plant produces small, pale green flowers that bloom in the spring. Its leaves are typically blue-gray in color, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique look and low maintenance requirements.
