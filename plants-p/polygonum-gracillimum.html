Polygonum gracillimum

Polygonum gracillimum, commonly known as slender knotweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to eastern Asia.

It features slender, upright stems that are often reddish in color, and it produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or white. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving them a slightly fuzzy texture. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Polygonum gracillimum is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in wetland habitats.
