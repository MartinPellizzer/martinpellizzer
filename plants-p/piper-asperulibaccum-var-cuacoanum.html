Piper asperulibaccum var. cuacoanum

Piper asperulibaccum var. cuacoanum, commonly known as the Cuacoanum pepper, is a member of the Piperaceae family, native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, climbing vines and small, heart-shaped leaves with a glossy surface. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that develop into elongated, peppery berries. Its botanical features include a pungent aroma and a spicy flavor, making it a unique species within the Piper genus.

It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in the understory of tropical rainforests.
