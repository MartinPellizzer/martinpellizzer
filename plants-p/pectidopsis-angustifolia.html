Pectidopsis angustifolia

Pectidopsis angustifolia, commonly known as the narrowleaf pectidopsis, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its botanical features include a basal rosette of leaves and flowering stems that rise from the center, producing clusters of greenish-white flowers.

Pectidopsis angustifolia is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and open plains.
