Potentilla arvernensis

Potentilla arvernensis, commonly known as the Arvernes cinquefoil, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its dense clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has hirsute (hairy) stems and leaves that are typically divided into five oval-shaped lobes. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
