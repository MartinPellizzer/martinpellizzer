Pilosella salebrosa

Pilosella salebrosa, commonly known as the hairy pearly everlasting, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk florets. The plant is distinguished by its dense, white to pale yellow flowers and hairy, silver-green foliage. It thrives in rocky, alpine environments and is often found in meadows and grasslands at high altitudes.

Pilosella salebrosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
