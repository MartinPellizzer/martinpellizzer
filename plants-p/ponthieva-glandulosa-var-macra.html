Ponthieva glandulosa var. macra

Ponthieva glandulosa var. macra, commonly known as the Macra thornapple, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which are often glandular and covered in fine, sticky hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late summer. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, spherical seeds.

This species is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and hardy nature.
