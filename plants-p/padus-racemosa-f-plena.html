Padus racemosa f. plena

Padus racemosa f. plena, commonly known as the white-flowered rowan.

This cultivar is a variation of the common rowan, characterized by its abundant white flowers that appear in dense clusters. It is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern North America. The leaves are oval with serrated edges, and the fruit is a small, orange-red berry.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
