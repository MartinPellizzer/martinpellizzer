Pyrus elenchus

Pyrus elenchus, commonly known as the narrowleaf pear, is a species of flowering plant in the family Rosaceae.

It is native to parts of Europe and western Asia, typically found in moist, wooded areas. The plant is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in early spring. Its fruits are small pears that are generally not cultivated for food but are of interest to botanists.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
