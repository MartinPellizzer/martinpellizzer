Piper flavispicum

Piper flavispicum, commonly known as the yellow spicewood, is a tropical vine native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes over 2,000 species of flowering plants. The plant is characterized by its slender stems, heart-shaped leaves, and small, greenish-white flowers. Its fruits are capsules that contain numerous tiny seeds.

Piper flavispicum is often found in humid, lowland forests and is used in traditional medicine for its potential anti-inflammatory properties.
