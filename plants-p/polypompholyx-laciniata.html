Polypompholyx laciniata

Polypompholyx laciniata, commonly known as the fringed polypompholyx, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, finely divided leaves that give it a feathery appearance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its unique morphology, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists alike.
