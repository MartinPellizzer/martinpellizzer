Pincenectia recurvata

Pincenectia recurvata, commonly known as the recurved pincenectia, is a rare flowering plant native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy beneath. The plant produces delicate, white to pale yellow flowers clustered in terminal inflorescences. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically in mountainous forests.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered endangered and is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
