Prunus apyrena

Prunus apyrena, commonly known as the Syrian cherry or Prunus apyrena, is a species of shrub or small tree native to the Middle East and parts of southern Europe.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and showy, white to pink flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, sweet-tart fruits known as cherries, which are edible and often used in culinary applications.

Prunus apyrena is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
