Pachira macrantha

Pachira macrantha, commonly known as the golden shower tree, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its palm-like appearance, with a thick, unbranched trunk and large, compound leaves. The plant produces showy, yellow flowers that hang in clusters, giving it its common name. These flowers are followed by elongated, woody fruits containing several seeds.

Pachira macrantha is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and relatively easy care requirements.
