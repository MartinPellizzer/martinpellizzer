Piper perforatum

Piper perforatum, commonly known as wild pepper, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in spikes, followed by small, round berries. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their aromatic and medicinal properties.

The plant is characterized by its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its resilience to drought conditions.
