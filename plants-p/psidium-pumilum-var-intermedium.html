Psidium pumilum var. intermedium

Psidium pumilum var. intermedium, commonly known as the dwarf pineapple guava, is a compact shrub native to South America.

It produces small, edible fruits that resemble miniature pineapples, with a sweet and tangy flavor. The plant has glossy, dark green leaves and a shallow root system, making it suitable for container gardening. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and unique fruit, though it is less commonly cultivated than other guava species.
