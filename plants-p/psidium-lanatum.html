Psidium lanatum

Psidium lanatum, commonly known as the wax apple or Brazilian guava, is a small evergreen tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its smooth, dark green leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. The plant produces small, white flowers that are followed by round, green fruits with a waxy skin. The fruit matures to a yellow or red color and has a sweet, aromatic flavor.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible fruit.
