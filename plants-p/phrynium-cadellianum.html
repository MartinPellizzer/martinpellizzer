Phrynium cadellianum

Phrynium cadellianum, commonly known as the Cadellian arrowleaf, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of Asia and Australia.

It is characterized by its large, arrow-shaped leaves that are often variegated with yellow and green patterns. The plant produces striking orange or red bracts that enclose its small, inconspicuous flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Phrynium cadellianum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
