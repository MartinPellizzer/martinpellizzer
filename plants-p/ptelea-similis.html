Ptelea similis

Ptelea similis, commonly known as the red maple, is a deciduous tree native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its distinctive red flowers and vibrant autumn foliage. The plant belongs to the Aceraceae family and is known for its ornamental value in gardens and natural landscapes. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and often have serrated edges.

Ptelea similis thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is adaptable to a variety of light conditions.
