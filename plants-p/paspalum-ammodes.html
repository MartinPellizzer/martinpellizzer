Paspalum ammodes

Paspalum ammodes, commonly known as the slender paspalum, is a perennial grass native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and other moist environments, making it an important component of aquatic ecosystems. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a prominent midrib, and it produces small, wind-pollinated flowers.

Paspalum ammodes is often used in restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife species.
