Phlomis tschimganica

Phlomis tschimganica, commonly known as the Tschimgan wormwood, is a perennial herb native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its upright, branched stems and gray-green foliage. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in dense, spherical heads, typical of the sunflower family. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in mountainous regions.

Phlomis tschimganica is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in herbal medicine.
