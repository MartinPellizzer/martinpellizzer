Pilosella sciadophora subsp. humidicola

Pilosella sciadophora subsp.

humidicola, commonly known as the wetland pilosella, is a flowering plant native to wet or moist habitats in Europe. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its small, clustered yellow flowers and hairy stems.

The subspecies is distinguished by its ability to thrive in humid environments, often found in meadows and along riverbanks. Its leaves are typically narrow and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This plant plays a role in supporting local pollinators and maintaining ecological balance in its native range.
