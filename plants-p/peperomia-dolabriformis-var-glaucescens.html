Peperomia dolabriformis var. glaucescens

Peperomia dolabriformis var. glaucescens, commonly known as the Blue-veined Peperomia, is a compact, low-maintenance plant native to tropical regions.

It features thick, waxy leaves with a distinctive blue-green hue and prominent veins, giving it a unique visual appeal. The plant typically grows to a height of about 12 inches, making it ideal for tabletop displays or hanging baskets. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Its foliage is often used in ornamental horticulture for its aesthetic value and ease of care.
