Phlox ingrum

Phlox ingrum, commonly known as the showy phlox, is a perennial flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Polemoniaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, featuring lance-shaped leaves and clusters of tubular, often pink or purple flowers. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscaping.

Phlox ingrum is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
