Pterostylis whitei

Pterostylis whitei, commonly known as the White's hooded orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its unique floral structure, featuring a single, hood-like petal that encloses the reproductive organs. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and understoreys. Its flowers are usually pale green or white, with a distinctive, narrow lip that aids in pollination.

Pterostylis whitei is a small plant, generally reaching heights of less than 20 centimeters.
