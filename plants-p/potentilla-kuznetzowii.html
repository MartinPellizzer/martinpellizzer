Potentilla kuznetzowii

Potentilla kuznetzowii, commonly known as the Kuznetsov's cinquefoil, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its woody stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-orange flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or rocky habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and hardiness, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens in temperate climates.
