Pandanus insularis

Pandanus insularis, commonly known as the island pandanus, is a flowering plant native to the Indo-Pacific region.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and long, narrow leaves that are often used in traditional crafts. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers arranged in a branched inflorescence, which are pollinated by insects and birds. Its fruit is a fibrous, spiky structure that contains edible seeds.

Pandanus insularis plays an important ecological role in coastal and wetland habitats.
