Peperomia coriacea

Peperomia coriacea, commonly known as the roughleaf peperomia, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of hair, giving them a textured appearance. It produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers that are typically clustered in spikes.

Peperomia coriacea is a popular houseplant due to its low maintenance requirements and attractive foliage.
