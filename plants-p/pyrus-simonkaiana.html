Pyrus simonkaiana

Pyrus simonkaiana, commonly known as the Simonkaian pear, is a species of flowering plant in the family Rosaceae.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, particularly in Kazakhstan and Kyrgyzstan. The plant is characterized by its deciduous leaves and clusters of white flowers that bloom in spring. Its fruits are small, yellow pomes that ripen in late summer.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
