Piper hydrocotylefolium

Piper hydrocotylefolium, commonly known as the water pepper plant, is a tropical species belonging to the Piperaceae family.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are often submerged or floating on the surface of water. The plant typically grows in wetland environments, such as marshes and along the edges of streams. Its small, greenish flowers are clustered in spikes and are followed by small, three-lobed fruits.

The plant is noted for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
