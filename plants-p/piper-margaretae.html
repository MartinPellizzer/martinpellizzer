Piper margaretae

Piper margaretae, commonly known as the Margaretae pepper or the Guatemalan pepper, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to Central America and is characterized by its small, greenish-yellow flowers and elongated, slender fruits. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a serrated margin, while the stems are often hairy.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and has also been used in traditional medicine for its purported therapeutic properties.
