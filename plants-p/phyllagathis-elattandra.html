Phyllagathis elattandra

Phyllagathis elattandra, commonly known as the narrow-leafed myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes well-known plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence. It produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, followed by edible, berry-like fruits.

This species is valued for its aromatic properties and is often used in traditional medicine and as a source of essential oils.
