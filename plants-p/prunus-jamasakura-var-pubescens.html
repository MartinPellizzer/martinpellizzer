Prunus jamasakura var. pubescens

Prunus jamasakura var. pubescens, commonly known as the Japanese cherry blossom variant with pubescent leaves, is a deciduous tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its delicate pink flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by heart-shaped leaves that turn yellow in autumn. The leaves are covered in fine, soft hairs, giving the plant its varietal name. It thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

This variety is highly valued for its ornamental beauty and is often planted in gardens and along streets for its seasonal floral display.
