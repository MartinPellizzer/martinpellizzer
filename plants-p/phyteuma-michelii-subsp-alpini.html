Phyteuma michelii subsp. alpini

Phyteuma michelii subsp.

alpini, commonly known as the Alpine phyteuma, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the alpine regions of Europe. It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environmental conditions.

The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of yellow to orange, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are lanceolate and covered in fine hairs, aiding in water retention.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with well-drained soil.
