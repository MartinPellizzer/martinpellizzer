Paeonia × suffruticosa f. papaveracea

Paeonia × suffruticosa f. papaveracea, commonly known as the Persian peony, is a hybrid perennial plant belonging to the芍药属 (Paeonia) family.

It is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit and is native to regions of Central Asia, including parts of Iran and Afghanistan. The plant produces showy, double flowers in shades of pink, red, and white, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its foliage consists of deeply lobed, dark green leaves, and it is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.

The Persian peony is relatively hardy and thrives in well-drained soil with full sun to partial shade.
