Psoralea bituminosa subsp. plumosa

Psoralea bituminosa subsp.

plumosa, commonly known as the silky psoralea, is a shrub native to Australia. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, pinnately compound leaves.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in spikes, which are typically borne on the upper parts of the stems. Its seeds are enclosed in pods and are often used in traditional Aboriginal medicine.

The species is adapted to dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal and inland regions of Australia.
