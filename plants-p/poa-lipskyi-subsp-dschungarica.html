Poa lipskyi subsp. dschungarica

Poa lipskyi subsp.

dschungarica, commonly known as the Dzungarian bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to Central Asia. It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and narrow, bluish-green leaves.

The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as steppes and rocky slopes. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences with numerous short, awned florets.

This subspecies is adapted to arid conditions and is often found in regions with low precipitation and high solar exposure.
