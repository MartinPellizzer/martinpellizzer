Phlomis tuvinica

Phlomis tuvinica, commonly known as Tuvinian mullein, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Lamiaceae, which includes many aromatic herbs. The plant features upright stems covered in soft, woolly hairs, and its leaves are lance-shaped with a downy texture. It produces clusters of small, pale yellow to greenish flowers that bloom in summer.

Phlomis tuvinica is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments.
