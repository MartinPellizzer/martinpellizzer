Potentilla heptaphylla var. macropetala

Potentilla heptaphylla var. macropetala, commonly known as the large-leaved cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit and produces clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. The leaves are palmately divided into five to seven lobes, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in alpine and subalpine environments.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.
