Phelipanche caesia

Phelipanche caesia, commonly known as the common morning glory, is a flowering plant belonging to the Convolvulaceae family.

It is characterized by its trumpet-shaped, violet to blue flowers that open in the morning and close by midday. The plant is an annual or biennial herb with slender, twining stems that climb using tendrils. Its leaves are alternate, heart-shaped, and have a rough texture.

Native to Europe and parts of Asia, it is often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides.
