Paurolepis

Paurolepis, commonly known as the African violet, is a genus of flowering plants native to Africa.

These plants are characterized by their fleshy, thick leaves and showy, tubular flowers that come in a variety of colors. The leaves are typically rosette-shaped, which helps the plant conserve moisture in its native arid environments. Paurolepis species are popular as houseplants due to their ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.

They thrive in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil.
