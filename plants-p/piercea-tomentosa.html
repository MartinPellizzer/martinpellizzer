Piercea tomentosa

Piercea tomentosa, commonly known as the woolly piercea, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly gray foliage that covers its compact, shrub-like form. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in spring, adding a subtle ornamental value. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements, it is often cultivated in rock gardens and as a decorative plant in arid landscapes.
