Potentilla pensylvanica var. japonica

Potentilla pensylvanica var. japonica, commonly known as Japanese cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of bright yellow flowers in the summer. The plant has finely divided, serrated leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or borders.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardiness in temperate climates.
