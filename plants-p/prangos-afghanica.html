Prangos afghanica

Prangos afghanica, commonly known as the Afghan pincushion, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and showy, spherical flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its flowers are usually pink or purple, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Prangos afghanica is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
