Potamogeton × undulatus var. cooperi

Potamogeton × undulatus var. cooperi, commonly known as Cooper's pondweed, is a hybrid aquatic plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its elongated, ribbon-like leaves that are often slightly undulating, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in shallow, freshwater habitats such as ponds and lakes, where it forms dense underwater vegetation. Its flowers are small and clustered in spike-like inflorescences, and it plays a role in providing habitat for aquatic life.

However, it can become invasive in some regions, outcompeting native species and altering ecosystem dynamics.
