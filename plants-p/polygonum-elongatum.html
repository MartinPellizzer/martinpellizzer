Polygonum elongatum

Polygonum elongatum, commonly known as the elongated knotweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, which includes other familiar plants like buckwheat and sorrel. The plant is characterized by its upright stems and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a distinctive veining pattern.

Polygonum elongatum is often found in moist environments such as stream banks and wet meadows.
