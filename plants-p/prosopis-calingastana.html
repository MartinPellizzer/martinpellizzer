Prosopis calingastana

Prosopis calingastana, commonly known as the Argentine mesquite, is a leguminous shrub or small tree native to South America.

It belongs to the genus Prosopis, which includes several species adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. The plant has compound leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, yellowish flowers followed by elongated, woody pods. It is well-suited for reforestation and soil stabilization due to its deep root system and drought tolerance.

The seeds are edible and have been used by indigenous communities for food and traditional medicine.
