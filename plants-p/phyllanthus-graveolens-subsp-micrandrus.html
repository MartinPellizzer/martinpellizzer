Phyllanthus graveolens subsp. micrandrus

Phyllanthus graveolens subsp.

micrandrus, commonly known as the fragrant phyllanthus, is a shrub native to parts of Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators. Its fruit is a small, three-lobed capsule that contains several seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
