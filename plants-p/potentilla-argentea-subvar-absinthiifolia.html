Potentilla argentea subvar. absinthiifolia

Potentilla argentea subvar. absinthiifolia, commonly known as silver cinquefoil, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its silver-white, hairy leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in alpine and rocky habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is cultivated in gardens and used in traditional herbal medicine.
