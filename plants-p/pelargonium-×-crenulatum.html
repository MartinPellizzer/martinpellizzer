Pelargonium × crenulatum

Pelargonium × crenulatum, commonly known as the ivy-leaf geranium, is a hybrid plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and heart-shaped, waxy leaves that resemble ivy leaves, giving it its common name. The plant produces clusters of small, five-petaled flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, typically blooming from spring to autumn. It thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it a popular choice for hanging baskets and containers.

Its ornamental value and ease of care contribute to its widespread cultivation in gardens and landscapes.
