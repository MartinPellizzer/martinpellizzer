Potamogeton × lundii

Potamogeton × lundii, commonly known as the hybrid water milfoil, is a submerged aquatic plant that exhibits characteristics of both its parent species, Potamogeton perfoliatus and Potamogeton crispus.

It typically grows in freshwater environments such as lakes, ponds, and slow-moving rivers, where it forms dense underwater vegetation. The plant has slender, branching stems and small, opposite leaves that are often serrated or toothed. Its flowers are unisexual, with male and female flowers occurring on separate plants, a feature common in many pondweeds.

Potamogeton × lundii plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
