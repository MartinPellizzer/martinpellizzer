Philadelphus sharpianus var. parviflorus

Philadelphus sharpianus var. parviflorus, commonly known as the small-flowered mock orange, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features fragrant, white, tubular flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has opposite, simple, and ovate leaves that are dark green and hairy underneath. It typically grows to a height of 3 to 6 feet and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This variety is valued for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a range of soil conditions.
