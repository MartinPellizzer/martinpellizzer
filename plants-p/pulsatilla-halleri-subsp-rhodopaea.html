Pulsatilla halleri subsp. rhodopaea

Pulsatilla halleri subsp.

rhodopaea, commonly known as the red poppy, is a member of the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking pink to red flowers. It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils.

The plant features basal leaves that are palmately lobed, and its stems are usually unbranched with a hairy texture. It produces follicles that contain numerous small, brown seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

This subspecies is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia and is noted for its ornamental value in gardens.
