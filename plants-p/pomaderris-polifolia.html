Pomaderris polifolia

Pomaderris polifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leafed honeysuckle, is a shrub native to southern Australia.

It features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in clusters, typically white or pale yellow, which bloom in spring. Its fruit is a small, round drupe that turns red when mature.

Pomaderris polifolia is often found in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.
