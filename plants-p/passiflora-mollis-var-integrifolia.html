Passiflora mollis var. integrifolia

Passiflora mollis var. integrifolia, commonly known as the silky passionflower, is a tropical vine native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its velvety, dark green foliage and striking, complex flowers with five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments. The plant produces edible, tropical berries that are rich in antioxidants and have a mild, sweet flavor. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and medicinal properties.

The species is also valued in traditional medicine for its calming effects.
