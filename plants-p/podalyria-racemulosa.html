Podalyria racemulosa

Podalyria racemulosa, commonly known as the clustered bluebell, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its upright growth habit and dense clusters of blue to purple flowers. The plant produces small, bean-like pods that contain several seeds, typical of the Fabaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and rocky areas.

Podalyria racemulosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
