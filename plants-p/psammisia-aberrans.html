Psammisia aberrans

Psammisia aberrans, commonly known as the sandspurry, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing perennial that thrives in sandy or gravelly soils. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in early spring. Its leaves are succulent and fleshy, adapted to retain moisture in arid environments.

Psammisia aberrans is often used in rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
