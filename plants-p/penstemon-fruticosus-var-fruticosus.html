Penstemon fruticosus var. fruticosus

Penstemon fruticosus var. fruticosus, commonly known as the common penstemon, is a perennial shrub native to the western United States.

It typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. The plant features upright, woody stems with lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its showy, tubular flowers come in shades of red, purple, and pink, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

It is valued in gardens for its drought tolerance and ornamental appeal.
