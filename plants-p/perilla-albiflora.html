Perilla albiflora

Perilla albiflora, commonly known as white-flowered perilla, is an herbaceous plant belonging to the Lamiaceae family.

It is native to East Asia and is characterized by its slender, aromatic stems and small, white or pale pink flowers. The leaves are typically oval-shaped with a slightly serrated edge and emit a mild, mint-like fragrance when crushed. This plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in herbal medicine.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full sunlight.
