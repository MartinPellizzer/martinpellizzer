Panicum brizanthum var. polystachyum

Panicum brizanthum var. polystachyum, commonly known as purple lovegrass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and produces panicles of purple to pink flowers that bloom in the spring and summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves with a bluish-green color and is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its attractive appearance. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for dry or semi-arid environments.

Its fibrous root system helps prevent soil erosion, contributing to its ecological value in certain habitats.
