Passiflora callacallensis

Passiflora callacallensis, commonly known as the Peruvian passionflower, is a climbing vine native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, complex flowers with five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments that resemble a crown. The plant produces edible, orange-yellow berries that are rich in vitamin C. Its leaves are palmately lobed and have a velvety texture.

Passiflora callacallensis is valued for its ornamental beauty and traditional medicinal uses.
