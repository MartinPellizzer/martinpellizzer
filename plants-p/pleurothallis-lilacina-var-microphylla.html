Pleurothallis lilacina var. microphylla

Pleurothallis lilacina var. microphylla, commonly known as the miniature lilac orchid, is a species native to the cloud forests of Central and South America.

It is a small, epiphytic orchid with delicate, lance-shaped leaves and fragrant, lilac-colored flowers. The plant typically grows on trees or rocks in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are often clustered in small inflorescences, and the plant is noted for its compact size and ornamental appeal.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is relatively rare and often cultivated by enthusiasts.
