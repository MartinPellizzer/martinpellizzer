Panicum massaiense

Panicum massaiense, commonly known as Massai grass, is a perennial grass species native to eastern Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves with a bluish-green color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in savanna and grassland habitats.

Due to its hardiness and ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and used for erosion control in some regions.
