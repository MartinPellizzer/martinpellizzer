Plantago amblodes

Plantago amblodes, commonly known as the narrow-leaved plantain, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its broad, ribbed leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in a spike, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in a variety of habitats, including meadows, roadsides, and disturbed soils.

Plantago amblodes is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
