Panax trifolius var. roseus

Panax trifolius var. roseus, commonly known as the rose-flowered ginseng, is a variant of the ginseng genus native to specific regions of Asia.

This plant is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three leaflets arranged in a fan-like pattern. The flowers are typically pink or rose-colored, giving it its common name. It thrives in shaded, moist forest environments and is often found in mountainous regions.

The plant is valued for its medicinal properties, similar to other ginseng species, though its specific uses and chemical composition may vary.
