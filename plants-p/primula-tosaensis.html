Primula tosaensis

Primula tosaensis, commonly known as the Tosa primrose, is a flowering plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and stream banks. Its leaves are broad and heart-shaped, with a smooth texture.

Primula tosaensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
