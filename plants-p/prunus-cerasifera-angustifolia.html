Prunus cerasifera angustifolia

Prunus cerasifera angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and lance-shaped leaves that are dark green on top and pale underneath. The plant produces small, white flowers in early spring, followed by edible, dark red berries in the summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping.

Its botanical features make it both aesthetically pleasing and ecologically valuable in gardens and natural habitats.
