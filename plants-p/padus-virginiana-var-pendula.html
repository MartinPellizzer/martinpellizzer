Padus virginiana var. pendula

Padus virginiana var. pendula, commonly known as the weeping white crabsapple, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its pendulous branches that droop gracefully, giving it a distinctive, ornamental appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers in early spring, followed by red or yellow berries in the fall. Its leaves are ovate to elliptical, with serrated edges, and turn vibrant red or orange in autumn.

This variety is often cultivated for its aesthetic value in gardens and landscapes.
