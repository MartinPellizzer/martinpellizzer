Phaseolus looseri var. subrotundifolius

Phaseolus looseri var. subrotundifolius, commonly known as the broad-leaved runner bean, is a variant of the runner bean species native to South America.

It is characterized by its broad, rounded leaves and vigorous climbing habit, making it suitable for trellises or supports. The plant produces elongated, edible pods that are typically green when harvested, though they may turn yellow or red depending on the variety. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, and it thrives in warm, sunny conditions with well-drained soil.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and culinary use in various cuisines.
