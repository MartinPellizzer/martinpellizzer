Pterococcus corniculatus

Pterococcus corniculatus, commonly known as the horned acacia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a small, evergreen shrub that typically grows in coastal areas and sandy soils. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a woody pod that splits open to release small, hard seeds.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local communities.
