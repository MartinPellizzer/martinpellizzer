Persea lige

Persea lige, commonly known as the Mexican persimmon, is a small evergreen tree native to Mexico and Central America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its elliptical, dark green leaves with a glossy surface. The tree produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by orange to reddish fruits in the fall. The fruit has a sweet, sometimes astringent flavor and is often consumed fresh or used in culinary applications.

Its ornamental value and edible fruit make it a valued plant in both horticulture and agriculture.
