Poterium mauritanicum var. saxatile

Poterium mauritanicum var. saxatile, commonly known as the saxatile poterium, is a perennial flowering plant native to the arid regions of North Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and grayish-blue foliage. The plant produces small, daisy-like flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, blooming during the warmer months. It is well adapted to dry environments, with succulent leaves that store water and reduce transpiration.

This variety is often found in rocky or sandy soils, thriving in full sun conditions.
