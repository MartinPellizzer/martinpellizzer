Portulaca saxifragoides

Portulaca saxifragoides, commonly known as the stonecress or desert purslane, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions.

It is a succulent species characterized by its fleshy, thick leaves and vibrant yellow flowers. The plant thrives in hot, dry environments and is adapted to conserve water through its thick cuticle and succulent tissues. It typically grows in rocky soils and is often found in disturbed areas or along roadsides.

Portulaca saxifragoides is a low-growing plant that blooms throughout the summer months.
