Phaseolus reflexopilosus

Phaseolus reflexopilosus, commonly known as the hairy runner bean, is a herbaceous annual plant native to South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its hairy stems and compound leaves. The plant produces elongated, pod-like fruits that contain edible seeds, often used in culinary applications. Its growth habit includes trailing or climbing vines, making it suitable for trellises or support structures.

The hairy reflexopilosus variety is noted for its ornamental value and adaptability to various soil conditions.
