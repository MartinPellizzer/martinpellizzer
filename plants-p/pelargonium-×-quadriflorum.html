Pelargonium × quadriflorum

Pelargonium × quadriflorum, commonly known as the four-flowered geranium, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Geraniaceae family.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of red, pink, and white. The leaves are usually rounded or heart-shaped, with a slightly hairy texture, and they often have a reddish or purple hue. This plant is native to South Africa and is widely cultivated as an ornamental due to its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to various growing conditions.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for gardens and containers.
