Peperomia argentea

Peperomia argentea, commonly known as the silver peperomia, is a compact, evergreen perennial native to tropical regions of South America.

It features rosettes of fleshy, silver-gray leaves that are covered in a fine, waxy coating, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers on short stalks, which are typically hidden among the foliage. It thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Due to its low maintenance and attractive foliage, it is often used as a decorative houseplant.
