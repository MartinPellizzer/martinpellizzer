Plantago euryphylla

Plantago euryphylla, commonly known as broadleaf plantain, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features a rosette of broad, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving them a fuzzy appearance. The plant produces small, greenish flowers in a spike-like inflorescence, which are wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and disturbed areas.

Plantago euryphylla is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally for its healing benefits.
