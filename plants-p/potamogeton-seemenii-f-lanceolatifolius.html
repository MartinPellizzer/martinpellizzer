Potamogeton seemenii f. lanceolatifolius

Potamogeton seemenii f. lanceolatifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaf pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in Asia.

It features long, ribbon-like leaves that are typically narrow and lanceolate in shape, which help reduce water resistance. The plant produces small, green flowers that are clustered in spike-like inflorescences, typical of the Potamogeton genus. It thrives in shallow to moderately deep waters with soft to muddy substrates.

This variant is often found in lakes, rivers, and wetlands, contributing to aquatic ecosystem stability by providing habitat and oxygen.
