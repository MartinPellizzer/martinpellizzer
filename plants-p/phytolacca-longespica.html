Phytolacca longespica

Phytolacca longespica, commonly known as the Chinese pokeweed, is a herbaceous plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, upright stems and large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers followed by bright red berries that are toxic to humans and animals. It thrives in moist, fertile soils and is often found in woodland areas or along roadsides.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in both ecological and medicinal contexts.
