Pentagonocarpus cordatus

Pentagonocarpus cordatus, commonly known as the heartleaf mangrove, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive heart-shaped leaves and its ability to thrive in saline and brackish water environments. The plant has a robust root system that helps stabilize coastal soils and prevent erosion. Its flowers are small and clustered, producing fruits that are dispersed by water.

This species plays a vital role in mangrove ecosystems by providing habitat for various marine and terrestrial organisms.
