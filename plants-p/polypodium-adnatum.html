Polypodium adnatum

Polypodium adnatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf huperzia, is a species of fern belonging to the family Huperziaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, upright fronds that are typically less than 30 centimeters in length. The plant has a creeping rhizome that produces sporangia on the undersides of its leaves. It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia, including India, China, and Southeast Asia.

Polypodium adnatum thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.
