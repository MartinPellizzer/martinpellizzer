Panicum fastigiatum

Panicum fastigiatum, commonly known as purpletop grass, is a perennial grass native to the eastern United States.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves with a distinctive purple tinge along the midribs, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, fluffy seed heads that are often used in ornamental gardening. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Its upright growth habit and drought tolerance make it a valuable addition to native plant landscapes.
