Pagiantha cerifera

Pagiantha cerifera, commonly known as the candlewax plant, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its evergreen shrub form, with small, leathery leaves that have a waxy texture. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in late spring. Its unique appearance and aromatic foliage make it a popular ornamental plant.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, thriving in coastal climates.
