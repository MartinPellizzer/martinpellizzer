Piper calvescens var. pedunculatum

Piper calvescens var. pedunculatum, commonly known as the black pepper plant, is a tropical vine native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dark green, glossy leaves and small, clustered white flowers that develop into peppercorns. The plant produces tiny, round berries that turn from green to black as they mature, containing the aromatic compound piperine. It thrives in warm, humid climates with well-drained soil and requires support for its climbing habit.

This species is widely cultivated for its culinary and medicinal uses.
