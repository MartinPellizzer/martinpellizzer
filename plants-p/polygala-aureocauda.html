Polygala aureocauda

Polygala aureocauda, commonly known as the golden-tailed milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has pinnately compound leaves and produces small, hairy fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in desert scrub and grassland habitats.

Polygala aureocauda is an important plant for pollinators and is also valued for its ornamental appeal.
