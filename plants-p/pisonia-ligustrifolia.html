Pisonia ligustrifolia

Pisonia ligustrifolia, commonly known as the beach hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its showy, pink to red bracts that surround small, yellow flowers. The plant has woody stems and opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly rough texture. It thrives in sandy or limestone soils and is often found in tropical and subtropical coastal areas.

Pisonia ligustrifolia is well adapted to salt spray and can tolerate moderate drought conditions.
