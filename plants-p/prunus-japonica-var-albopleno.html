Prunus japonica var. albopleno

Prunus japonica var. albopleno, commonly known as the white-flowered Japanese apricot, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its smooth, reddish-brown bark and oval, dark green leaves that turn yellow in autumn. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in early spring before the leaves emerge, typically in shades of white or pale pink. Its fruit is a small, yellowish drupe that ripens in late summer.

It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its aesthetic appeal and hardy nature.
