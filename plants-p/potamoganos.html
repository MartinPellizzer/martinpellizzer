Potamoganos

Potamoganos, commonly known as the water spinach or kangkong, is a herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its succulent, edible leaves and hollow stems. The plant thrives in moist environments and is often cultivated in water or wet soil conditions. Its leaves are commonly used in various cuisines, particularly in Asian countries.
