Phaeopappus spectabilis

Phaeopappus spectabilis, commonly known as the showy pappus, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and distinctive bracts. The plant produces showy, orange to yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically narrow and gray-green, with a fuzzy texture.

The plant thrives in rocky soils and is often found in open habitats like meadows and slopes.
