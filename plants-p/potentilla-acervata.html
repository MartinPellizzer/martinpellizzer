Potentilla acervata

Potentilla acervata, commonly known as the clustered cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in moist, rocky habitats such as stream banks and meadows. The plant features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are compound, consisting of five oval-shaped leaflets, and it has a compact, bushy growth habit.

Potentilla acervata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in native plant gardens.
