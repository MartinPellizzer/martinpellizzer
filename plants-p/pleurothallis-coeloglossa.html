Pleurothallis coeloglossa

Pleurothallis coeloglossa, commonly known as the red-lipped orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers with a distinctive red lip. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are usually small to medium in size, with a complex structure typical of orchids, featuring three sepals and three petals.

Pleurothallis coeloglossa is appreciated for its ornamental value and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
