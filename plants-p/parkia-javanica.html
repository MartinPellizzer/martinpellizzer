Parkia javanica

Parkia javanica, commonly known as the Java flame tree, is a tropical leguminous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking orange to red flowers that bloom in clusters, often resembling flames. The plant has pinnate leaves and produces flat, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its vibrant floral display.

The tree also plays a role in nitrogen fixation, contributing to soil fertility.
