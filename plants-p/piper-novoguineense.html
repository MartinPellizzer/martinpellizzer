Piper novoguineense

Piper novoguineense, commonly known as the New Guinea pepper vine, is a climbing plant native to the tropical regions of New Guinea.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper plants such as black pepper. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a glossy surface and produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into elongated, fleshy berries.

Its botanical characteristics make it a notable species in horticultural and ecological studies due to its unique morphology and growth habits.
