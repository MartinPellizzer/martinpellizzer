Pleurothallis miqueliana

Pleurothallis miqueliana, commonly known as the Miquel's orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Ecuador and Peru.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its compact growth habit and delicate, fragrant flowers. The plant typically produces small, greenish-white or pale yellow blooms that are clustered in inflorescences. It thrives in shaded, humid environments and requires well-drained, acidic soil for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated by enthusiasts but remains relatively rare in the horticultural trade.
