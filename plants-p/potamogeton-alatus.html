Potamogeton alatus

Potamogeton alatus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments across Europe and Asia.

It features long, narrow leaves that are typically arranged in a spiral pattern along its stem. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in dense spikes, which emerge from the water surface during the summer months. Its rhizomes help anchor it in muddy substrates, and it plays a vital role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.

Potamogeton alatus is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize sediments and improve water quality.
