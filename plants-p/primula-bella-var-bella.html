Primula bella var. bella

Primula bella var. bella, commonly known as the bell primrose, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. The foliage consists of ovate, dark green leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It prefers cool, moist environments and is often grown in gardens for its ornamental value.

This variety is valued for its early blooming habit and ability to thrive in partially shaded conditions.
