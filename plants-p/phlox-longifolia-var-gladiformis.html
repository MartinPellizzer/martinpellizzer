Phlox longifolia var. gladiformis

Phlox longifolia var. gladiformis, commonly known as the longstem phlox, is a perennial flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of showy, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its foliage consists of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery down.

This variety is well-suited for woodland gardens and prefers partial to full shade with moist, well-drained soil.
