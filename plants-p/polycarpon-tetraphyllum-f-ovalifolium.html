Polycarpon tetraphyllum f. ovalifolium

Polycarpon tetraphyllum f. ovalifolium, commonly known as the oval-leafed four-leafed knotweed, is a variant of the species Polycarpon tetraphyllum.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its oval-shaped leaves, which are typically arranged in a rosette formation. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in umbels, typical of the Polygonaceae family. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a notable species for ecological studies and botanical gardens.
