Penaea nitida

Penaea nitida, commonly known as the slender mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on the branches of various host trees, primarily in tropical and subtropical regions.

It has small, oval-shaped leaves that are usually clustered in whorls, and its flowers are typically white or pale green, arranged in clusters. The plant produces small, fleshy berries that are dispersed by birds, aiding in its propagation. Penaea nitida has a symbiotic relationship with its host, drawing water and nutrients while also providing shelter for certain insects and birds.

Its presence can indicate a healthy ecosystem, as it thrives in diverse forest environments.
