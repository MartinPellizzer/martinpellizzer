Pleurothallis tortilis

Pleurothallis tortilis, commonly known as the twisted pleurothallis, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact, often epiphytic growth habit and its delicate, spiraling inflorescence. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pale yellow, which bloom during the wet season. Its leaves are usually narrow and arranged in a spiral pattern, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Pleurothallis tortilis is valued in horticulture for its unique morphology and ornamental appeal.
