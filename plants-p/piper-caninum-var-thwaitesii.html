Piper caninum var. thwaitesii

Piper caninum var. thwaitesii, commonly known as the thwaitesii pepper plant, is a variant of the wild pepper species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a climbing shrub characterized by its small, glossy leaves and clusters of tiny, greenish-white flowers. The plant produces small, dark purple berries that are used in traditional medicine and as a spice. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and medicinal contexts.
