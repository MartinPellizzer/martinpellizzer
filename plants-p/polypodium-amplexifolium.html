Polypodium amplexifolium

Polypodium amplexifolium, commonly known as the clasping fern, is a species of epiphytic fern native to tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive fronds that wrap around tree trunks, giving it its common name. The plant has a rhizome that is covered with a layer of brown, velvety tissue. Its leaves are typically long and narrow, with a serrated margin and a bright green color.

Polypodium amplexifolium thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.
