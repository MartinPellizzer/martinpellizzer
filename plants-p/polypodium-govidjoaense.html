Polypodium govidjoaense

Polypodium govidjoaense, commonly known as the Goodyera fern, is a species of epiphytic fern native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped fronds. The plant typically grows on trees or rocks in humid, shaded environments. Its sporangia are clustered in sori on the underside of the fronds, a common feature in ferns.

Polypodium govidjoaense is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in tropical gardens.
