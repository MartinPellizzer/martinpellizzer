Pelargonium verbasciflorum

Pelargonium verbasciflorum, commonly known as the woolly geranium, is a species of flowering plant in the Geraniaceae family.

It is native to South Africa and is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and showy, showy pink or purple flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters, and thrives in well-drained soil and full sun. Its unique appearance makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

The flowers bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
