Pterandra egleri

Pterandra egleri, commonly known as the golden shower tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, pendulous clusters of yellow flowers that bloom throughout the year. The plant has compound leaves composed of several oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Pterandra egleri is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a decorative plant.
