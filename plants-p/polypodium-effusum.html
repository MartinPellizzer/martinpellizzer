Polypodium effusum

Polypodium effusum, commonly known as the broad-leaf maidenhair fern, is a species of fern native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its large, leathery fronds that are typically divided into numerous pinnae. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories. Its rhizomes are creeping and can form dense colonies.

Polypodium effusum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
