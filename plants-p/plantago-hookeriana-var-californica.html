Plantago hookeriana var. californica

Plantago hookeriana var. californica, commonly known as the California plantain, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It features rosettes of broad, oval, hairy leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces small, greenish flowers in a spike-like inflorescence, which are wind-pollinated. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal sage scrub and grassland habitats.

This species is valued for its adaptability and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
