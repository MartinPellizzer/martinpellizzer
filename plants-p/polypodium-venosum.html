Polypodium venosum

Polypodium venosum, commonly known as the veined shield fern, is a member of the family Polypodiaceae.

It is a terrestrial fern characterized by its wiry, creeping rhizomes and dense, evergreen foliage. The fronds are typically leathery and have a distinctive veined appearance, which gives the plant its common name. It thrives in shaded, moist environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
