Plantago compressiscapa

Plantago compressiscapa, commonly known as the narrow-leaved plantain, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in a spike, which are typically borne on slender stalks. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its fibrous root system helps stabilize soil, making it a valuable plant in ecological restoration efforts.
