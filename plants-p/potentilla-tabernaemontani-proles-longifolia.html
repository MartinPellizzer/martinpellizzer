Potentilla tabernaemontani proles longifolia

Potentilla tabernaemontani proles longifolia, commonly known as the longleaf potentilla, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of central and eastern Europe.

It typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring upright stems covered in fine, grayish hairs. The plant produces small, yellow flowers clustered in spherical inflorescences, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are elliptic to ovate, with a pointed tip and a smooth margin, often slightly hairy underneath.

This species thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or forested areas.
