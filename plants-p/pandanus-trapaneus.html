Pandanus trapaneus

Pandanus trapaneus, commonly known as the giant pandanus, is a tropical plant native to the Indo-Pacific region.

It is characterized by its large, sword-shaped leaves that can grow up to several meters in length. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, yellowish flowers that are often used in traditional crafts and rituals. Its roots and leaves are also utilized in local cuisines and medicinal practices.

The species is well adapted to coastal and saline environments, making it a hardy and resilient plant.
