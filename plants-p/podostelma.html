Podostelma

Podostelma, commonly known as water moccasins, are aquatic plants that thrive in fast-flowing freshwater environments.

They are characterized by their small, delicate leaves that are often submerged or partially submerged in water. These plants typically have rhizomes that anchor them to rocks or other substrates in streams and rivers. Podostelma species are known for their ability to tolerate low light conditions and high water currents.

They play an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
