Polemonium scopulinum

Polemonium scopulinum, commonly known as bluebells, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Polemoniaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to purple bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and has lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture. Its flowers bloom in early spring, often before the canopy of trees fully develops.

Polemonium scopulinum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
