Panicum chambeshii

Panicum chambeshii, commonly known as the Chambeshii grass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Africa.

It typically grows in open woodlands and grasslands, thriving in well-drained soils. The plant is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, linear leaves. Its inflorescence consists of a panicle made up of small spikelets, which are often tinged with red or purple.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping due to its drought tolerance and low maintenance requirements.
