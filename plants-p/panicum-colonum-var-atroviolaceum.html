Panicum colonum var. atroviolaceum

Panicum colonum var. atroviolaceum, commonly known as black velvet grass, is a perennial grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit and dark purple to black foliage that gives it its striking appearance. The plant produces slender, spike-like inflorescences that are often tinged with red or purple hues. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscape use in moist garden settings.
