Pleurothallis popayanensis

Pleurothallis popayanensis, commonly known as the Popayan orchid, is a species native to South America, particularly Colombia.

It is a small epiphytic orchid that grows on trees in humid, shaded environments. The plant has slender, dark green leaves and produces delicate, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink. It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its intricate floral structure.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
