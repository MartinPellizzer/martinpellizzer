Podosperma pedunculare

Podosperma pedunculare, commonly known as the European mountain ash or the common whitebeam, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are elliptic to ovate in shape. The plant produces small, white to pink flowers in clusters, followed by small, inedible berries that ripen to a dark red color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodlands and rocky areas.

Podosperma pedunculare is valued for its ornamental qualities and its ability to tolerate various soil conditions.
