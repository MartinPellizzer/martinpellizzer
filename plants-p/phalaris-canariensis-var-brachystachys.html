Phalaris canariensis var. brachystachys

Phalaris canariensis var. brachystachys, commonly known as the Canary grass, is a flowering plant belonging to the grass family Poaceae.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, blue-green blades that are often used in ornamental landscaping. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny florets, typical of grass species. It is native to Europe and has been introduced to various regions as a decorative and erosion-control plant.

Due to its adaptability, it thrives in a range of soil types and can tolerate both moist and dry conditions.
