Platyraphium billardierei

Platyraphium billardierei, commonly known as the narrow-leafed mountain grass, is a perennial herb native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pale green to brown in color, which bloom during the spring and summer months. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and woodland edges.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
