Podochilus thymifolius

Podochilus thymifolius, commonly known as the thyme-leaved rock orchid, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact, epiphytic growth habit. The plant features narrow, aromatic leaves that resemble those of thyme, giving it its common name. Its flowers are small, greenish-yellow, and often clustered in inflorescences.

It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found growing on tree trunks or rocks in tropical forests.
