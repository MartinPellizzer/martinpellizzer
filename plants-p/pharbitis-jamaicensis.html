Pharbitis jamaicensis

Pharbitis jamaicensis, commonly known as the Jamaican morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central and South America.

It is an annual vine that produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of blue, purple, and white. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and can grow up to several meters in length, climbing or trailing along supports. Its seeds are enclosed in a spiral-shaped capsule, which opens to release the seeds when mature.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has historical uses in traditional medicine.
