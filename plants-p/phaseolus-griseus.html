Phaseolus griseus

Phaseolus griseus, commonly known as the green bean or yardlong bean, is a leguminous plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its long, slender pods that range in color from green to yellow, depending on the variety. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, with trifoliate leaves and small white or purple flowers. Its seeds are edible when young and are often used in various cuisines around the world.

The plant is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it a beneficial crop in sustainable farming practices.
