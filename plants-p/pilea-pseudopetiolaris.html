Pilea pseudopetiolaris

Pilea pseudopetiolaris, commonly known as the Chinese money plant, is a member of the Araceae family.

It is native to China and is characterized by its round, thick, succulent leaves that resemble small coins. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers that are followed by bright yellow berries. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Pilea pseudopetiolaris is popular as a houseplant due to its attractive foliage and relatively easy care requirements.
