Prunus pumila var. depressa

Prunus pumila var. depressa, commonly known as the dwarf Japanese cherry, is a compact shrub native to Japan.

It is characterized by its small, rounded leaves and clusters of delicate pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows to a height of less than 1 meter, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a ground cover. Its foliage turns a vibrant red in the fall, adding ornamental value to the landscape.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial shade, thriving in temperate climates.
