Pyrus upsaliensis

Pyrus upsaliensis, commonly known as the Upsala pear, is a deciduous tree native to the forests of northern Europe.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and alternate, palmate leaves. The plant produces small, white flowers in the spring, which are followed by edible, yellowish pears that ripen in late summer. It prefers moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded woodland environments.

Pyrus upsaliensis is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical significance in traditional medicine.
