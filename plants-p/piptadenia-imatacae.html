Piptadenia imatacae

Piptadenia imatacae, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or *Piptadenia* tree, is a tropical leguminous plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically pinnate with small, oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces showy, yellow-orange flowers that are clustered in racemes, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its fruits are pod-like structures that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind or animals.

The tree is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in local medicine.
