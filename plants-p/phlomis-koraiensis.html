Phlomis koraiensis

Phlomis koraiensis, commonly known as Korean mullein, is a flowering plant native to the Korean Peninsula.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant features tall, erect stems covered in soft, woolly hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its flowers are typically pale blue or purple, arranged in dense spikes at the top of the stems.

Phlomis koraiensis is often found in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments.
