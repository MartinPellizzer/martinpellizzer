Phymatopteris hastata f. longisquamata

Phymatopteris hastata f. longisquamata, commonly known as the long-scale maidenhair fern, is a fern species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that are typically arching and gracefully cascading. The plant features distinctive, elongated, and often hairy scales on its rhizomes and fronds, which contribute to its unique appearance. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in woodland areas with rich, organic soil.

This fern is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
