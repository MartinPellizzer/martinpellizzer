Poa angustifolia f. setacea

Poa angustifolia f. setacea, commonly known as the hairy narrowleaf grass, is a variant of the narrowleaf bluegrass.

It is characterized by its fine, grass-like leaves that are typically narrow and slightly hairy, giving it a distinctive texture. This plant is often found in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks. It has a clump-forming growth habit and produces small, spike-like inflorescences.

The botanical features of this form make it notable for its ornamental value and ecological adaptability.
