Philodendron ventricosum

Philodendron ventricosum, commonly known as the giant arrowroot or swamp philodendron, is a tropical climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 inches in length. The plant has a distinctive swollen, tuberous stem that stores water and nutrients, aiding in its survival in wet, humid environments. Its leaves are typically bright green with a waxy texture, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by yellowish berries.

Philodendron ventricosum thrives in partial shade and requires high humidity and well-draining soil for optimal growth.
