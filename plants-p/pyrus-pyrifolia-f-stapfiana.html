Pyrus pyrifolia f. stapfiana

Pyrus pyrifolia f. stapfiana, commonly known as the Chinese white pear, is a cultivar of the Chinese pear species.

It is characterized by its white, sweet, and crisp fruit, which is typically harvested in late summer. The plant is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 10 meters tall, with a rounded crown and dark green leaves. Its flowers are small and white, appearing in clusters, and it is often cultivated for both its fruit and ornamental value.

This variety is well-suited to temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
