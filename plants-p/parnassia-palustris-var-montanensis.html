Parnassia palustris var. montanensis

Parnassia palustris var. montanensis, commonly known as the mountain buttercup, is a variant of the marsh buttercup native to alpine and subalpine regions.

It is characterized by its bright yellow, buttercup-like flowers and has narrow, lance-shaped leaves. This plant thrives in moist, rocky soils and is often found in mountain meadows and stream banks. It is a perennial herb that blooms in late summer, producing small, showy flowers.

The plant has a shallow root system and is adapted to the harsh conditions of high-altitude environments.
