Pterocarpus quercinus

Pterocarpus quercinus, commonly known as the ironwood tree, is a tropical species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, hard wood that is highly valued for its strength and durability. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by flat, pod-like fruits containing several seeds. Its leaves are typically bipinnate, with numerous small leaflets arranged in a feathery pattern.

Pterocarpus quercinus is often found in lowland rainforests and is known for its ability to thrive in humid, tropical climates.
