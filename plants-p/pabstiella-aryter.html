Pabstiella aryter

Pabstiella aryter, commonly known as the silver leaf orchid, is a rare and striking member of the orchid family.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and delicate, silver-blue foliage that reflects light in a unique way. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pale purple, typically blooming in late spring. Native to the high-altitude regions of South America, it thrives in cool, moist environments with well-drained soil.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, Pabstiella aryter is considered a conservation concern.
