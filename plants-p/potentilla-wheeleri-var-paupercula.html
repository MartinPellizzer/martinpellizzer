Potentilla wheeleri var. paupercula

Potentilla wheeleri var. paupercula, commonly known as the Wheeler's cinquefoil, is a low-growing perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has finely divided, serrated leaves that are typically covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments.

Its botanical characteristics make it a hardy and resilient plant adapted to harsh, high-altitude conditions.
