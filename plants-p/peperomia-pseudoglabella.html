Peperomia pseudoglabella

Peperomia pseudoglabella, commonly known as the velvet peperomia, is a compact, evergreen perennial plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It features fleshy, dark green leaves with a velvety texture and a striking reddish or purple underside. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or yellow, which appear in clusters during the growing season. It is well-suited for indoor cultivation due to its low light requirements and drought tolerance.

Its unique foliage makes it a popular choice for tabletop displays and shaded garden settings.
