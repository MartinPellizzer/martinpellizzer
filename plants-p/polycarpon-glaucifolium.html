Polycarpon glaucifolium

Polycarpon glaucifolium, commonly known as the bluish-leaved chickweed, is a low-growing herbaceous plant characterized by its glaucous, waxy leaves that give it a distinctive silvery appearance.

It typically features small, white flowers with five petals, which bloom in early spring. The plant is often found in moist, disturbed soils in woodland or meadow habitats. It has a fibrous root system and spreads primarily through stolons, forming dense mats.

Its foliage is often used in traditional herbal medicine for its mild diuretic properties.
