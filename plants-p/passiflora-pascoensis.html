Passiflora pascoensis

Passiflora pascoensis, commonly known as the Pasco passionflower, is a tropical vine native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, complex flowers that feature five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments resembling a crown of thorns. The plant produces edible berries that are often used in local cuisines and traditional medicine. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a rough texture.

Passiflora pascoensis thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in cloud forests.
