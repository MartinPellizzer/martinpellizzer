Parkia bahiae

Parkia bahiae, commonly known as the Java candlewood, is a tropical leguminous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, elongated, candle-like pods that hang in clusters from the branches. The plant has bipinnate leaves, with small, ovate leaflets arranged in two rows. It produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, contributing to its ornamental value.

The tree is often cultivated for its decorative appearance and is also valued for its timber and traditional uses.
