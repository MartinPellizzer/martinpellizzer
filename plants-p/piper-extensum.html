Piper extensum

Piper extensum, commonly known as the broadleaf pepper or longleaf pepper, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, Colombia, and Peru. The plant is characterized by its climbing habit, with long, slender stems that can reach several meters in length. Its leaves are alternate, ovate to lanceolate in shape, and often have a glossy surface.

The fruit is a small, elongated berry that contains the aromatic seeds used in traditional medicine and culinary applications.
