Pectis ramosissima

Pectis ramosissima, commonly known as the branched pectis, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble single blooms. The plant features slender, branching stems covered in fine, hairy foliage. Its yellow flowers typically bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Pectis ramosissima is adapted to arid environments and thrives in well-drained soils.
