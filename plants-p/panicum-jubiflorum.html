Panicum jubiflorum

Panicum jubiflorum, commonly known as purpletop millet, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and dense, panicle-like inflorescences that produce numerous small, purple flowers. The plant is often found in open, disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields, thriving in full sun. Its fibrous roots help prevent soil erosion, making it a valuable plant in ecological restoration projects.

The seeds are edible and have been used by indigenous peoples for food and medicinal purposes.
