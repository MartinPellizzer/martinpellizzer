Potentilla fruticosa var. vulgaris

Potentilla fruticosa var. vulgaris, commonly known as common cinquefoil, is a deciduous shrub native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It features multiple stems covered in small, yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant has alternate, pinnately compound leaves with five leaflets, giving it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges.

Its botanical characteristics make it a hardy and adaptable plant suitable for various ecological and ornamental purposes.
