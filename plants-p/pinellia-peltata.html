Pinellia peltata

Pinellia peltata, commonly known as the broad-leaf pinellia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the family Araceae and is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit. The plant features broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often mottled with white or light green patterns. Its flowering structure consists of a spadix enclosed by a spathe, typical of the Araceae family.

Pinellia peltata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in herbal medicine.
