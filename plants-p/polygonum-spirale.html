Polygonum spirale

Polygonum spirale, commonly known as the spiral knotweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive spiraling arrangement of leaves, which gives it its common name. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in moist, fertile soils and is often found near streams or in wetlands.

Polygonum spirale is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.
