Pseudocynometra schefferi

Pseudocynometra schefferi, commonly known as the African ebony tree, is a tropical tree native to Central and Eastern Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its dark, dense wood that is highly valued for its durability and rich color. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in drooping clusters, followed by elongated, pod-like fruits.

Its leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils in tropical rainforests.
