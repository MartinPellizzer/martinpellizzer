Pterocarpus berteroi

Pterocarpus berteroi, commonly known as African rosewood, is a tropical tree native to regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its dark, dense wood that is highly valued for its aesthetic and structural properties. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by pods containing flat, fibrous seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in secondary forests and along riverbanks.

The tree has a slow growth rate and is considered a valuable species for both timber and ecological purposes.
