Poa thouarsiana

Poa thouarsiana, commonly known as the New Zealand hairgrass, is a perennial grass species native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its fine, hair-like leaves and produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands. It has a fibrous root system and is often found in coastal and subalpine regions.

Poa thouarsiana plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
