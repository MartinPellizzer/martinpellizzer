Parsonsia neoebudica

Parsonsia neoebudica, commonly known as the red-veined parsonia, is a striking tropical plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, often with intricate red veins. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to several meters. Its leaves are usually large, broad, and have a glossy appearance.

Parsonsia neoebudica is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
