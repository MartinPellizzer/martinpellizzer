Pseudaraeococcus sessiliflorus

Pseudaraeococcus sessiliflorus, commonly known as the sessile-flower scale plant, is a species of scale insect that belongs to the family Coccidae.

It is characterized by its small, sessile flowers that are typically clustered on the stems of its host plants. The plant is often found on woody plants, where it feeds on sap, causing various symptoms such as leaf discoloration and deformation. Its botanical significance lies in its role as a pest in horticultural and agricultural settings.

Due to its specialized host range and unique floral morphology, it is of interest in both entomological and botanical studies.
