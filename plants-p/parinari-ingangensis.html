Parinari ingangensis

Parinari ingangensis, commonly known as the African satinash, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and simple, alternate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by fleshy, orange-red berries that are an important food source for various wildlife. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in secondary forests and along riverbanks.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation areas.
