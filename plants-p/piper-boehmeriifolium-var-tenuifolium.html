Piper boehmeriifolium var. tenuifolium

Piper boehmeriifolium var. tenuifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaf pepper plant, is a tropical species native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its slender, elongated leaves that are typically less than 2 inches wide. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that develop into dark purple to black berries. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their aromatic and potentially medicinal properties.

The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
