Perityle crassifolia var. robusta

Perityle crassifolia var. robusta, commonly known as the robust perityle, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its robust growth habit and thick, succulent leaves that help it retain moisture in arid environments. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale blue, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for both horticultural study and ecological research.
