Passiflora cookii

Passiflora cookii, commonly known as Cook's passionflower, is a climbing vine native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, complex flowers that feature five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments surrounding the reproductive structures. The plant produces blue or violet berries that are edible and contain a single seed. Its leaves are heart-shaped with a rough texture and are often used in traditional medicine.

Passiflora cookii thrives in humid, shaded environments and is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance.
