Ptilotus alopecuroideus f. rubriflorus

Ptilotus alopecuroideus f. rubriflorus, commonly known as the red-flowered spinifex, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of Australia.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, spiky foliage and showy, red-orange flowers. The plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to withstand drought and high temperatures. Its flowers are typically small and clustered, forming dense inflorescences that bloom during the warmer months.

This species is also noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its striking appearance.
