Primula obconica subsp. obconica

Primula obconica subsp.

obconica, commonly known as the broad-leaf primrose, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe. It features upright, hairy stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple.

The leaves are broadly ovate with a wavy margin and a hairy surface, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in woodland or shaded gardens.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in horticulture for its early spring blooms.
