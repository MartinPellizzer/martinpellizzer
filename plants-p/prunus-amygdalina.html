Prunus amygdalina

Prunus amygdalina, commonly known as the almond cherry or sweet cherry, is a deciduous tree belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in temperate climates. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in spring, which are followed by edible drupes containing a single seed. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a smooth texture with a serrated margin.

Prunus amygdalina is valued for its ornamental beauty and is also cultivated for its sweet, nutritious fruit.
