Pinanga polymorpha

Pinanga polymorpha, commonly known as the giant bamboo or giant leaved bamboo, is a striking member of the grass family (Poaceae).

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including parts of Indonesia and the Philippines. This plant is characterized by its large, elongated leaves that can reach lengths of up to three meters. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

Pinanga polymorpha is valued not only for its ornamental appeal but also for its ecological importance in supporting local biodiversity.
