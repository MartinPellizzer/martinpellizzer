Phalangium exuviatum

Phalangium exuviatum, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are usually covered in a fine, velvety texture. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats.

Phalangium exuviatum is also known for its ability to tolerate a wide range of environmental conditions.
