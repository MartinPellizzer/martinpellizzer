Polygonum caducifolium

Polygonum caducifolium, commonly known as the annual smartweed, is a herbaceous plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its alternate, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom from late spring to early autumn. Its stems are erect and can grow up to 1 meter in height, often branching near the base.

Polygonum caducifolium is commonly found in disturbed soils, such as along roadsides and in fields, and is known for its rapid growth and adaptability.
