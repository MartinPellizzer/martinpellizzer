Potentilla autaretica

Potentilla autaretica, commonly known as the alpine potentilla, is a low-growing shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and small, often yellow or white flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has dark green, leathery leaves that are adapted to cold and arid conditions. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes.

Potentilla autaretica is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardiness in harsh environments.
