Polypodium ursipes

Polypodium ursipes, commonly known as the bear's foot fern, is a species of epiphytic fern native to the tropical regions of Asia and the Pacific.

It is characterized by its distinctive, leathery fronds that resemble the shape of a bear's footprint, hence its common name. The plant typically grows on trees or rocks in humid, shaded environments, and its rhizomes are often covered in a dense, fibrous layer. Polypodium ursipes produces sporangia on the underside of its fronds, which release spores for reproduction.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appearance and ability to thrive in a variety of conditions.
