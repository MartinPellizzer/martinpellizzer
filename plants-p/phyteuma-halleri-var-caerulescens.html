Phyteuma halleri var. caerulescens

Phyteuma halleri var. caerulescens, commonly known as the blue phyteuma, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flower heads, which are borne on tall, slender stems. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are linear and grass-like, and it is often found in alpine or subalpine regions.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens and its ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
