Pediocactus gielsdorfianus

Pediocactus gielsdorfianus, commonly known as the Giersdorf's cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of northern Mexico.

It is a low-growing, mound-forming cactus with cylindrical stems that are typically green or reddish in color. The plant produces clusters of small, yellow flowers during the spring and early summer. It has a shallow root system adapted to drought conditions and requires well-draining soil.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is often cultivated by succulent enthusiasts.
