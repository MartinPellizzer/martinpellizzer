Paspalum malacophyllum var. elongatum

Paspalum malacophyllum var. elongatum, commonly known as the elongated paspalum, is a perennial grass native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and elongated leaf blades that can reach up to 30 cm in length. The plant produces small, pale green to brown spikelets that are often clustered at the tips of the stems. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetland or riparian habitats.

This variant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in supporting local wildlife.
