Polygala pappeana

Polygala pappeana, commonly known as the desert milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Polygalaceae family and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, spiny leaves. The plant produces delicate, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in sandy or rocky soils.

Polygala pappeana is adapted to withstand drought and high temperatures, making it a resilient species in desert ecosystems.
