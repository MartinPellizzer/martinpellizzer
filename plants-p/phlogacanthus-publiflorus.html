Phlogacanthus publiflorus

Phlogacanthus publiflorus, commonly known as the showy phlogacanthus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescences that consist of numerous small, tubular flowers arranged in a spike. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are usually opposite and have a rough, pubescent surface.

The flowers are often visited by pollinators such as bees and birds, contributing to its ecological significance in its native habitat.
