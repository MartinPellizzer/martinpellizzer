Peristylus lacertifer var. taipoensis

Peristylus lacertifer var. taipoensis, commonly known as the Taipo rock fern, is a rare and unique fern species native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, lace-like fronds that are often adorned with small, spore-producing structures called sori. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, typically found in rocky or forested areas with well-drained soil. Its botanical features include a creeping rhizome and bipinnate leaves that contribute to its ornamental appeal.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a vulnerable species in its natural range.
