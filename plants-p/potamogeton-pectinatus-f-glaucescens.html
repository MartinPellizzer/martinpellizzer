Potamogeton pectinatus f. glaucescens

Potamogeton pectinatus f. glaucescens, commonly known as the bluish pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, cylindrical stems and long, narrow leaves that are often covered in a bluish waxy coating. The plant produces small, green flowers that are typically clustered in spike-like inflorescences. It thrives in still or slow-moving waters with soft, muddy or sandy substrates.

Due to its morphological features, it is often used in ecological studies to assess water quality and aquatic ecosystem health.
