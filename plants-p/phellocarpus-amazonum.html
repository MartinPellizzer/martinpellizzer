Phellocarpus amazonum

Phellocarpus amazonum, commonly known as the Amazonian balsa, is a deciduous tree native to the Amazon rainforest.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and smooth, light-colored bark. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by round, woody fruits containing several seeds. Its most notable feature is its lightweight, buoyant wood, which has been historically used for shipbuilding and flotation devices.

The plant plays an important ecological role in its habitat, providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
