Panicum brownianum

Panicum brownianum, commonly known as Brown's panicgrass, is a species of grass native to certain regions of the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial plant characterized by its tall, slender stalks and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as wet meadows and pond edges. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a smooth texture and a bluish-green color.

Panicum brownianum plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small invertebrates in its native environment.
