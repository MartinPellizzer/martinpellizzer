Psoralea aromatica

Psoralea aromatica, commonly known as sweet psoralea, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant produces small, yellowish to brownish pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its aromatic properties, it has been used in traditional medicine and as a source of essential oils.
