Pleurothallis cassidata

Pleurothallis cassidata, commonly known as the Cassidate Orchid, is a species of orchid native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a small, epiphytic plant that typically grows on trees in humid, shaded environments. The plant produces delicate, greenish-white flowers with prominent, showy lips. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that help it conserve moisture.

Botanically, it belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is notable for its intricate floral structure and adaptation to its specific ecological niche.
