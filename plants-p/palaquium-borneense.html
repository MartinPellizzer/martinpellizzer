Palaquium borneense

Palaquium borneense, commonly known as the Bornean persimmon, is a small evergreen tree native to the rainforests of Borneo.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces edible berries that are typically orange to red in color when mature. It thrives in humid, tropical climates with well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value and limited distribution, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
