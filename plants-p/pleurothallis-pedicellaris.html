Pleurothallis pedicellaris

Pleurothallis pedicellaris, commonly known as the pink-veined orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its slender, epiphytic growth habit. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink with intricate veining. Its roots are adapted to absorb moisture and nutrients from the air and surrounding substrate.

Pleurothallis pedicellaris is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in shaded, humid environments.
