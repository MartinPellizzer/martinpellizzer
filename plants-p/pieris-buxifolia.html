Pieris buxifolia

Pieris buxifolia, commonly known as the Japanese pieris, is a species of evergreen shrub native to Japan.

It is characterized by its dense, leathery foliage that remains green throughout the year. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped white flowers in early spring, which are followed by dark blue berries. It thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its botanical features make it a valuable plant for both landscape design and ecological habitats.
