Pelargonium homonymum

Pelargonium homonymum, commonly known as the African geranium, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of small, pink or white flowers. The plant has fuzzy, lobed leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

Pelargonium homonymum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
