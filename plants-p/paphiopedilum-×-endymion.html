Paphiopedilum × endymion

Paphiopedilum × endymion, commonly known as the Endymion phalaenopsis, is a hybrid orchid that exhibits a blend of characteristics from its parent species.

It is a terrestrial orchid with a sympodial growth habit, featuring a pseudobulb that supports its foliage and flowers. The plant produces large, showy flowers with a distinctive lip that often has a unique coloration and pattern. Its botanical features include broad, elliptical leaves and a compact inflorescence with multiple blooms.

This hybrid is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its long-lasting, intricate floral display.
