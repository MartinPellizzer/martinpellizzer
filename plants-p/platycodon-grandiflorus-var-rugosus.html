Platycodon grandiflorus var. rugosus

Platycodon grandiflorus var. rugosus, commonly known as the rough bellflower, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The plant has rough, hairy leaves that are typically lance-shaped and arranged oppositely along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often found in rocky or mountainous habitats.

The flowers bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
