Photinia lancilimba var. turbinata

Photinia lancilimba var. turbinata, commonly known as the turbinated photinia, is a shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its elliptical, glossy leaves that are typically dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers in clusters, which are followed by red, fleshy berries. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The varietal name 'turbinata' refers to the turbinate shape of its fruit, which is a distinctive feature of this botanical variant.
