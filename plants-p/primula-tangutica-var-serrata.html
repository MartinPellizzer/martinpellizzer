Primula tangutica var. serrata

Primula tangutica var. serrata, commonly known as the serrated primrose, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of western China.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges and clusters of bell-shaped, violet to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in cool, temperate climates. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.

The species is also of interest to botanists due to its unique floral structure and ecological adaptations.
