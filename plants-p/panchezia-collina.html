Panchezia collina

Panchezia collina, commonly known as the mountain panchezia, is a flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in high-altitude grasslands and rocky slopes. The plant features slender, upright stems with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white pubescence. Its flowers are small, clustered in dense inflorescences, and are usually pale blue or purple in color.

Panchezia collina is adapted to cold, arid environments and is often found in areas with strong sunlight exposure.
