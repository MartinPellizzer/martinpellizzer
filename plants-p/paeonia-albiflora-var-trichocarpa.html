Paeonia albiflora var. trichocarpa

Paeonia albiflora var. trichocarpa, commonly known as the white peony or tree peony, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of China.

It is characterized by its tall, upright growth habit and is often cultivated for its showy, fragrant white or pale pink flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges and produces small, reddish-purple buds that open into large, butterfly-like petals. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

This variety is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional Chinese medicine.
