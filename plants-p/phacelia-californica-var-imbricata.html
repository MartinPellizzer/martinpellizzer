Phacelia californica var. imbricata

Phacelia californica var. imbricata, commonly known as the California phacelia, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its hairy, branched stems and clusters of blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. This variety is adapted to dry, sunny environments and is often found in rocky soils and open woodlands.

It plays an important role in supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
