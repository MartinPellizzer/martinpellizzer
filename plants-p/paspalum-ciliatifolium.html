Paspalum ciliatifolium

Paspalum ciliatifolium, commonly known as the silky paspalum, is a perennial grass native to South America.

It is characterized by its fine, soft foliage and slender, erect growth habit. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers. It thrives in moist to wet environments, often found in marshy areas and along water bodies.

Its adaptability makes it a common species in wetland ecosystems.
