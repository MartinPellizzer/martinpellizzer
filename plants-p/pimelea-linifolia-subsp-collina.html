Pimelea linifolia subsp. collina

Pimelea linifolia subsp.

collina, commonly known as the coastal bottlebrush, is a shrub native to the southern coast of Western Australia. It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and dense clusters of bright red or pink flowers that bloom in spring.

The leaves are narrow, linear, and often covered in fine hairs, giving the plant a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it well-suited to coastal environments.

The plant's unique floral structure and adaptability to harsh coastal conditions make it an important species in its native habitat.
