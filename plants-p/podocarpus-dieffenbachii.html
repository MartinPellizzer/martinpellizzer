Podocarpus dieffenbachii

Podocarpus dieffenbachii, commonly known as the silver pine, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to New Zealand.

It features needle-like leaves that are typically dark green and glossy, with a silvery-blue underside. The plant produces small, cone-like structures that contain seeds, typical of the conifer family. It thrives in temperate climates and is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value.

The species is also valued for its durability and is sometimes used in landscaping and as a windbreak.
