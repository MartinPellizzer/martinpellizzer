Plantago maritima var. californica

Plantago maritima var. californica, commonly known as the California plantain, is a perennial herb native to the coastal regions of California.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in coastal dunes and salt marshes.

This variety is adapted to tolerate saline conditions and is an important component of coastal ecosystems.
