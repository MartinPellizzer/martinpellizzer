Papaver rhoeas var. hoffmanianum

Papaver rhoeas var. hoffmanianum, commonly known as the Hoffman's poppy, is a variant of the common poppy.

It is characterized by its striking red petals and a distinctive, elongated seed capsule. This plant is native to Europe and is often found in disturbed soils and open fields. The flowers typically bloom in the summer months, adding vibrant color to the landscape.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in the Papaveraceae family.
