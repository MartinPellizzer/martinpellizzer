Polyalthia pulchrinervia

Polyalthia pulchrinervia, commonly known as the fragrant flame tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with prominent, showy veins that give it its scientific name. The tree produces small, fragrant white flowers that are often clustered in panicles, followed by woody, ornamental fruits. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Its rapid growth and striking foliage make it a popular choice for landscaping in warm regions.
