Panicum vacillans

Panicum vacillans, commonly known as the wavering grass, is a perennial grass species native to certain regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, blade-like leaves that often exhibit a bluish-green hue. The plant produces small, fluffy panicles of flowers that are typically pale green to brown in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands or disturbed areas.

Due to its adaptability, it is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
