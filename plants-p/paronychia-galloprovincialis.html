Paronychia galloprovincialis

Paronychia galloprovincialis, commonly known as the Galician thyme, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its small, pink or white flowers bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Paronychia galloprovincialis is often used in traditional medicine for its antiseptic and anti-inflammatory properties.
