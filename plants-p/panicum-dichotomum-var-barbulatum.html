Panicum dichotomum var. barbulatum

Panicum dichotomum var. barbulatum, commonly known as the bristle panicgrass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, linear leaves with a rough, bristly texture along the margins. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas.

Its unique appearance and adaptability make it a notable species in ecological and ornamental contexts.
