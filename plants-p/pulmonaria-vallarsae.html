Pulmonaria vallarsae

Pulmonaria vallarsae, commonly known as the Valdres lungwort, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the alpine regions of southern Norway.

It belongs to the Boraginaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of hairy, kidney-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pink to purple, typically blooming in early spring. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is adapted to cold climates.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
