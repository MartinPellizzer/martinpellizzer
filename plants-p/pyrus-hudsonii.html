Pyrus hudsonii

Pyrus hudsonii, commonly known as Hudson's pear, is a deciduous tree native to North America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and oval-shaped leaves. The tree produces small, yellow-green flowers in early spring, followed by edible, sweet-tart pears in late summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in riparian areas.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
