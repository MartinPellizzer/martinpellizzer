Pavetta termitaria var. pubescens

Pavetta termitaria var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy pavetta, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its small, opposite leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence. The plant produces clusters of white or pale yellow flowers, which are typically borne in terminal inflorescences. Its fruit is a small, capsule that splits open to release tiny seeds.

The species is noted for its ecological importance, providing habitat and food for various insect species.
