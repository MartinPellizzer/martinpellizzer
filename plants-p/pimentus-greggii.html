Pimentus greggii

Pimentus greggii, commonly known as Gregg's pinyon, is a species of evergreen shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Pinaceae family, which includes pines and other conifers, and is characterized by its needle-like leaves and cone-bearing structure. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, adapting to harsh conditions with its deep root system and water-conserving features. Its small, edible seeds are an important food source for local wildlife, including birds and small mammals.

The plant's hardy nature and unique appearance make it a notable species in its native ecosystem.
