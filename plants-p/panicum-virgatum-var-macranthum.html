Panicum virgatum var. macranthum

Panicum virgatum var. macranthum, commonly known as switchgrass, is a native North American grass species valued for its ornamental and ecological benefits.

It is characterized by its tall, upright growth habit and long, narrow leaves that are typically bluish-green in color. The plant produces dense, panicle-like flower heads that range in color from pink to red, depending on the variety and environmental conditions. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it suitable for a variety of landscape applications.

This grass is also an important component of native prairie ecosystems and is often used in restoration projects.
