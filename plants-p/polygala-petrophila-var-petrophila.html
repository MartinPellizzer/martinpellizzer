Polygala petrophila var. petrophila

Polygala petrophila var. petrophila, commonly known as the mountain bottlebrush, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, bottle-shaped flowers that are often red or pink in color. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or scrub habitats. It produces small, green leaves that are opposite along the stems, and its flowering period usually occurs in the spring.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
