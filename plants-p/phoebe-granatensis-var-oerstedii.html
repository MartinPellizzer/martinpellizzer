Phoebe granatensis var. oerstedii

Phoebe granatensis var. oerstedii, commonly known as the Oersted's phoebe, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in humid, lowland forests and thrives in partial to full shade. Its leaves are dark green and glossy, with a distinct aromatic scent when crushed.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its timber.
