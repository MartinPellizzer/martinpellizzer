Plagianthus spicatus

Plagianthus spicatus, commonly known as the spike plumeveld, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dense, spike-like inflorescence composed of numerous small yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in grassland habitats and is often found in areas with well-drained soils. It is a perennial herb that can reach heights of up to 1 meter, with narrow, grass-like leaves.

Plagianthus spicatus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in horticulture for its distinctive floral display.
