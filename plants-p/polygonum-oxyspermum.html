Polygonum oxyspermum

Polygonum oxyspermum, commonly known as the sharpseed knotweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, which includes plants known for their distinctive arrow-shaped leaves. The plant features slender stems that often grow in dense clusters, and its leaves are typically lanceolate with a smooth or slightly serrated edge. Small, greenish-white flowers appear in clusters during the summer months, and the plant produces small, round berries that are edible but have a sharp, acidic taste.

It is often found in moist, disturbed soils and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
