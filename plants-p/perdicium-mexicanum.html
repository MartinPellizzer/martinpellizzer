Perdicium mexicanum

Perdicium mexicanum, commonly known as the Mexican partridge pea, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and yellow-orange flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. It produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Perdicium mexicanum is valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it useful in ecological restoration projects.
