Pecteilis parageniculata

Pecteilis parageniculata, commonly known as the silverleaf spurge, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that are typically silver or blue-gray in color, which help it retain moisture in arid environments. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters during the warmer months. It is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its striking appearance and drought tolerance.

However, it can be invasive in some regions and requires careful management to prevent spread.
