Panicum ramulosum

Panicum ramulosum, commonly known as the slender panicgrass, is a perennial grass native to the southern United States and parts of Mexico.

It is characterized by its fine, wiry leaves and produces small, fluffy seeds that are dispersed by wind. The plant typically grows in dry, disturbed soils and is often found in grasslands and along roadsides. It has a clump-forming habit and can reach heights of up to 1 meter.

Due to its drought tolerance and ornamental appeal, it is sometimes used in landscaping and erosion control.
