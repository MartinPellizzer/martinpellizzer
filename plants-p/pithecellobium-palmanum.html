Pithecellobium palmanum

Pithecellobium palmanum, commonly known as the silky acacia, is a small evergreen tree native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, branching structure and pinnately compound leaves with long, silky hairs. The plant produces small, fragrant yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by elongated, pod-like fruits containing edible seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and dry forest ecosystems.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in tropical gardens.
