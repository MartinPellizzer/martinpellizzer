Pakaraimaea dipterocarpacea subsp. nitida

Pakaraimaea dipterocarpacea subsp.

nitida, commonly known as the Pearly Padauk, is a tropical tree native to the mountain forests of Guyana and northern South America. It is characterized by its distinctive pearly white or pale pink flowers that appear in clusters, often blooming during the dry season.

The plant belongs to the Dipterocarpaceae family, which is primarily found in Southeast Asia, making this subspecies a notable exception in its geographic distribution. Its leaves are typically alternate and simple, with a glossy surface, and the wood is valued for its durability and aesthetic qualities.

This subspecies is also known for its ecological importance in supporting local biodiversity.
