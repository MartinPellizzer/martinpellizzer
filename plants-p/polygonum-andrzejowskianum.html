Polygonum andrzejowskianum

Polygonum andrzejowskianum, commonly known as the Andrzejowski's knotweed, is a perennial herb native to eastern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, characterized by its distinctive arrow-shaped leaves and clusters of small, greenish flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, disturbed soils along riverbanks and in wet meadows. Its stems are usually reddish and covered with short, stiff hairs.

It is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and astringent properties.
