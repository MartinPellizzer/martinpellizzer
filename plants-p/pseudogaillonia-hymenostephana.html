Pseudogaillonia hymenostephana

Pseudogaillonia hymenostephana, commonly known as the Himalayan snowbell, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its delicate, bell-shaped white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery down. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists alike.
