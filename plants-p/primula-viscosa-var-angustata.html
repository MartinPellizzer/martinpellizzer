Primula viscosa var. angustata

Primula viscosa var. angustata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sticky primrose, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves that secrete a sticky substance, which is a distinctive botanical feature. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of yellow and orange, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats.

Its unique morphology and ecological adaptations make it a notable species within the Primulaceae family.
