Ptychandra clemensiae

Ptychandra clemensiae, commonly known as the mountain thyme, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Andes in South America.

It is a small shrub with aromatic, glandular leaves that emit a strong, pungent odor. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in late summer. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in rocky or mountainous terrain.

Due to its unique habitat and limited distribution, Ptychandra clemensiae is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
