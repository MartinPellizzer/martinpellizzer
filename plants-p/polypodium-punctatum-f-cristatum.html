Polypodium punctatum f. cristatum

Polypodium punctatum f. cristatum, commonly known as the cristate holly fern, is a unique variant of the common holly fern.

It is characterized by its distinctive, crisped fronds that give it a decorative appearance. This fern is native to parts of Asia and thrives in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped with a serrated margin.

The plant is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ease of cultivation.
