Primula alcalina

Primula alcalina, commonly known as the alkaline primrose, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that typically range in color from white to pink. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in a fine, velvety texture.

Primula alcalina is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and botanical collections.
