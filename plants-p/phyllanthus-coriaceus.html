Phyllanthus coriaceus

Phyllanthus coriaceus, commonly known as the hardleaf spurge, is a shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific.

It is characterized by its dense, leathery leaves that are typically oval or elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed or open habitats.

Phyllanthus coriaceus is also noted for its medicinal properties, used in traditional medicine for various ailments.
