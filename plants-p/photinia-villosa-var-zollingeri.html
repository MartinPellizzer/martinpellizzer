Photinia villosa var. zollingeri

Photinia villosa var. zollingeri, commonly known as the Zollinger's flameleaf photinia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It is characterized by its striking red or orange-red leaves that turn vibrant shades of red in the fall. The plant produces small white flowers in the spring, which are followed by red, fleshy berries. Its compact growth habit and colorful foliage make it a popular ornamental plant in gardens.

It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil.
