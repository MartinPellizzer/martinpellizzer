Persea willdenovii

Persea willdenovii, commonly known as the Mexican persimmon, is a deciduous tree native to Central America and parts of Mexico.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its elongated, elliptical leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. The tree produces small, fragrant flowers followed by orange to reddish fruits that are edible when fully mature. Its bark is smooth and grayish, often developing fissures with age.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and its fruit, which has a sweet, honey-like flavor when ripe.
