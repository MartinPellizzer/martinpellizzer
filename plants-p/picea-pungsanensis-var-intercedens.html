Picea pungsanensis var. intercedens

Picea pungsanensis var. intercedens, commonly known as the Korean white fir, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to the mountainous regions of South Korea.

It belongs to the family Pinaceae and is characterized by its slender, conical shape and dark green, needle-like leaves. The tree produces cones that are typically pendulous and contain seeds enclosed in woody scales. It thrives in cool, moist climates and is often found in mixed forests at higher elevations.

This variant is notable for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
