Phoradendron randiae var. orientale

Phoradendron randiae var. orientale, commonly known as the oriental mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on various host trees, primarily in tropical and subtropical regions.

It has a distinctive appearance with smooth, leathery leaves and small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant produces small, fleshy berries that are a food source for birds and other animals. Its seeds are dispersed by birds that eat the berries, allowing the plant to spread to new host trees.

This species plays an important ecological role in its native habitats by contributing to the biodiversity and nutrient cycling of forest ecosystems.
