Prunus baldshuanica f. heteromera

Prunus baldshuanica f. heteromera, commonly known as the Chinese cherry plum or a variant of the Japanese apricot, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant white to pale pink flowers that bloom in early spring before the leaves emerge. The leaves are ovate to elliptical, with a smooth margin and a glossy green upper surface. The fruit is a small, yellowish drupe that ripens in late summer.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its spring blossoms and autumn foliage.
