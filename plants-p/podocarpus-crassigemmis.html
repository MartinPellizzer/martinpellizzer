Podocarpus crassigemmis

Podocarpus crassigemmis, commonly known as the thick-leaved podocarp, is a member of the Podocarpaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is a evergreen coniferous tree characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and thick, leathery leaves. The plant produces small, cone-like structures that contain seeds, typical of conifers. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland forests.

Podocarpus crassigemmis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in horticulture and reforestation projects.
