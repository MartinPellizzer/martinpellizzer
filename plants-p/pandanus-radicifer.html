Pandanus radicifer

Pandanus radicifer, commonly known as the red pandanus, is a tropical plant native to the Indo-Pacific region.

It is characterized by its tall, narrow leaves that are often used in traditional crafts and thatching. The plant produces fragrant, orange-colored fruits that are edible and have a sweet, citrus-like flavor. Its root system is fibrous and can be used in making rope and other textiles.

Pandanus radicifer thrives in coastal areas and is often found growing near mangroves and saltwater environments.
