Ptarmica ageratifolia subsp. serbica

Ptarmica ageratifolia subsp.

serbica, commonly known as the Serbica geranium, is a perennial herb native to the Balkan region. It features upright stems with opposite, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces clusters of small, pink to purple flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and partial shade, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens or woodland settings.

Its botanical characteristics make it an attractive and resilient addition to native plant collections.
