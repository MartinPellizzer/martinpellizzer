Pseuderanthemum albocoeruleum subsp. robustum

Pseuderanthemum albocoeruleum subsp.

robustum, commonly known as the robust pseuderanthemum, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America. It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, tubular flowers.

The plant typically produces white to pale blue flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a waxy texture, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

The species is valued in horticulture for its vibrant blooms and adaptability to various growing conditions.
