Plantago coronopus var. columnae

Plantago coronopus var. columnae, commonly known as the slender plantain, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its upright, slender growth habit. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in dense clusters, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, often with a waxy coating.

This variant is distinguished by its more elongated form compared to other varieties of Plantago coronopus.
