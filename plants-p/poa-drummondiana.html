Poa drummondiana

Poa drummondiana, commonly known as Drummond's bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to North America.

It is characterized by its fine, blue-green foliage and forms dense, low-growing sods. The plant typically thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in prairies and open woodlands. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets that are clustered in panicles, and it produces small, wind-dispersed seeds.

Poa drummondiana plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
