Pteris bambusoides

Pteris bambusoides, commonly known as the bamboo fern, is a species of fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright fronds that resemble bamboo in appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along stream banks. Its rhizomes are creeping and produce clusters of small, lance-shaped leaves.

Pteris bambusoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
