Pleurothallis fuegi var. echinata

Pleurothallis fuegi var. echinata, commonly known as the Fuegian echinatella, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to the southern Andes.

It is a small, terrestrial orchid with narrow, grass-like leaves and produces delicate, greenish-white flowers with prominent, spiny sepals. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments at high altitudes. It is characterized by its unique floral structure, which includes a short, narrow column and a spurred lip.

This variety is relatively rare and is of interest to orchid enthusiasts due to its distinctive appearance and limited distribution.
