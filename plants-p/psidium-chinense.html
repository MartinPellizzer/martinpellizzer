Psidium chinense

Psidium chinense, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or custard apple, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant, white flowers. The plant produces a fruit with a thick, leathery skin that turns from green to yellow or red when ripe. The fruit contains a smooth, creamy pulp with numerous small, hard seeds.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and is also used in traditional medicine and as a source of edible fruit.
