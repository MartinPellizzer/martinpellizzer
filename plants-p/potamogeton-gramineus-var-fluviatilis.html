Potamogeton gramineus var. fluviatilis

Potamogeton gramineus var. fluviatilis, commonly known as the river pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in North America.

It typically grows in shallow, slow-moving waters such as ponds, lakes, and rivers. The plant has long, slender stems that are often branched and bear small, narrow leaves arranged in a spiral pattern. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, typically appearing in clusters, and it produces small, three-lobed fruits.

This species plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various waterfowl and invertebrates.
