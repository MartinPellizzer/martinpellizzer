Polygala socotrana

Polygala socotrana, commonly known as Socotra's milkweed, is a unique plant endemic to the island of Socotra.

It belongs to the Polygalaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, spiny, leaf-like structures that are actually modified stems. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are adapted for pollination by specific insect species. It thrives in arid and rocky environments, showcasing its resilience to the harsh conditions of its native habitat.

Due to its limited distribution and ecological significance, it is considered a vulnerable species.
