Potentilla reptans var. floribunda

Potentilla reptans var. floribunda, commonly known as creeping potentilla, is a low-growing, mat-forming shrub native to Europe and western Asia.

It features small, rounded leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs, and it produces clusters of bright yellow flowers in late spring to early summer. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full to partial sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a ground cover. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soil in erosion-prone areas.

Its botanical characteristics make it a hardy and versatile plant for various garden settings.
