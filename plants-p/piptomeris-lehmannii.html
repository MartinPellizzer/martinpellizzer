Piptomeris lehmannii

Piptomeris lehmannii, commonly known as the Lehmann's piper, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly found in Brazil and Paraguay.

The plant is characterized by its climbing habit, with opposite, ovate leaves that are often glandular and emit a strong scent. Its small, greenish-white flowers are clustered in spikes and are typically pollinated by insects.

The plant is valued for its medicinal properties, with various parts used in traditional herbal remedies.
