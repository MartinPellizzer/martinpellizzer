Piper riocauchosanum

Piper riocauchosanum, commonly known as the Rio Cauchosanum pepper plant, is a species of flowering vine in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to the Amazon rainforest of South America, particularly in Ecuador and Peru. The plant is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and small, clustered green berries that turn red when ripe. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found growing along riverbanks and in dense undergrowth.

Its botanical features make it a unique subject for ecological and horticultural study.
