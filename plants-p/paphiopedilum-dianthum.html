Paphiopedilum dianthum

Paphiopedilum dianthum, commonly known as the pink lady's slipper, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique, slipper-shaped flower with a distinctive pink and white coloration. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments and has a fibrous root system. Its leaves are broad and grass-like, often forming a rosette at the base.

This species is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated for its striking floral display.
