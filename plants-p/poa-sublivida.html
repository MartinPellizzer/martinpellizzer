Poa sublivida

Poa sublivida, commonly known as the silver grass, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, silver-gray blades that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in high-altitude environments, where it tolerates cold temperatures and poor soil conditions. Its inflorescence consists of a dense, spike-like cluster of small, spikelet-bearing stems.

Poa sublivida is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
