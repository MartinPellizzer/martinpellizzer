Pectinopitys standleyi

Pectinopitys standleyi, commonly known as the Standley's pinyon, is a species of conifer native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Pinaceae family and is characterized by its small, edible seeds enclosed in woody cones. The tree typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and can reach heights of up to 10 meters. Its foliage consists of needle-like leaves that are arranged in spirals along the branches.

The plant plays a role in local ecosystems by providing food for wildlife and contributing to soil stability.
