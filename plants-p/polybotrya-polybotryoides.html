Polybotrya polybotryoides

Polybotrya polybotryoides, commonly known as the silver fern, is a unique plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, fern-like leaves that are covered in a dense, silvery-white coating of fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in loose inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and prefers a sunny to partially shaded environment.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is often cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
