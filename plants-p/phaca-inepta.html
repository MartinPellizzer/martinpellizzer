Phaca inepta

Phaca inepta, commonly known as the ghost flower, is a rare and enigmatic plant native to the remote regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its delicate, pale blue or white flowers that appear during the early morning hours. The plant has a short lifespan, blooming only once before dying, which makes it a subject of fascination for botanists. Its unique morphology includes small, lance-shaped leaves and a lack of prominent stems.

Due to its elusive nature and limited distribution, Phaca inepta is considered a critically endangered species.
