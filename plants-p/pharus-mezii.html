Pharus mezii

Pharus mezii, commonly known as the desert bellflower, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its distinctive bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant has small, narrow leaves that help reduce water loss in hot, dry environments. It typically grows in rocky soils and is adapted to withstand long periods of drought.

Pharus mezii plays an important role in supporting local pollinators and maintaining biodiversity in its ecosystem.
