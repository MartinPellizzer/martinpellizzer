Psoralea pinnata var. latifolia

Psoralea pinnata var. latifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved psoralea, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves with broad, pinnate foliage. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in racemes, which are typically borne on slender stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or grasslands.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural study.
