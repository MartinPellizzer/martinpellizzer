Pimelea imbricata var. baxteri

Pimelea imbricata var. baxteri, commonly known as the Baxter's Heath, is a flowering plant native to Western Australia.

It is a low-growing shrub with dense, compact foliage and produces small, yellowish-green flowers in late winter to spring. The plant features narrow, leathery leaves that are arranged in a compact, mat-like form. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in heathlands and coastal areas.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and is appreciated in native gardens for its resilience and aesthetic appeal.
