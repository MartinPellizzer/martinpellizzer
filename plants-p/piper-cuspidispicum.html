Piper cuspidispicum

Piper cuspidispicum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved pepper plant, is a climbing vine native to the rainforests of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Piper genus, which includes many species of flowering plants in the family Piperaceae. The plant is characterized by its narrow, elongated leaves and small, greenish-white flowers that develop into peppery fruits. Its botanical features make it an important species in traditional medicine and local cuisines.

The plant's structure and growth habits reflect its adaptation to humid, shaded environments.
