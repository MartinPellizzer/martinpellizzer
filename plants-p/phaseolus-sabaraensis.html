Phaseolus sabaraensis

Phaseolus sabaraensis, commonly known as the Sabara bean, is a climbing legume native to South America.

It produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are typically consumed as a food source. The plant has compound leaves with trifoliate arrangements and is characterized by its twining habit. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in shaded, moist environments.

The species is valued for its nutritional content and has been cultivated by indigenous communities for traditional use.
