Pilea subintegerrima

Pilea subintegerrima, commonly known as the Chinese money plant, is a species of flowering plant in the family Araceae.

It is native to China and is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that are thick and fleshy, often with a waxy texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers that are surrounded by a modified leaf called a spathe. It is a popular indoor plant due to its ease of care and attractive foliage.

Pilea subintegerrima thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it suitable for a variety of indoor environments.
