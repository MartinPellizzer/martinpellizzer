Panicum ichnodes

Panicum ichnodes, commonly known as red panicgrass, is a warm-season grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and slender, grass-like leaves that are typically green with a reddish tinge. The plant produces small, fluffy seeds that are dispersed by wind, contributing to its ability to colonize disturbed soils. It thrives in full sun and is drought-tolerant, making it a common weed in agricultural fields and along roadsides.

Its botanical features make it a resilient plant adapted to a variety of environmental conditions.
