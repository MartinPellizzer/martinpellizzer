Poa gilgitica

Poa gilgitica, commonly known as Gilgit grass, is a species of grass native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, wiry stems. The plant typically produces small, spike-like inflorescences with tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in cold, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes.

Due to its ecological significance, it plays a vital role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity in its native habitat.
