Phyllocalyx calycinus

Phyllocalyx calycinus, commonly known as the golden bell tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has smooth, greyish-brown bark and elongated, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release small, hairy seeds.
