Phaseolus carinatus

Phaseolus carinatus, commonly known as the scarlet runner bean, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its climbing habit, with tendrils that help it ascend supports. The plant produces vibrant orange or red flowers, followed by elongated, bean-like pods that contain edible seeds. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a distinctive, slightly hairy texture.

This species is valued both for its ornamental appeal and as a source of nutritious legumes.
