Polygala andrachnoides

Polygala andrachnoides, commonly known as the Andean milkweed, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Polygalaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, often pink or white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are clustered in racemes.

It plays a role in local ecosystems by providing nectar for pollinators like bees and butterflies.
