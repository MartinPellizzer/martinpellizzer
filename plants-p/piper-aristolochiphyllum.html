Piper aristolochiphyllum

Piper aristolochiphyllum, commonly known as the Indian pipe or ghost plant, is a rare and unusual member of the Piper family.

It is a hemiparasitic plant, meaning it partially relies on other plants for nutrients through its roots. The plant is characterized by its pale, almost translucent leaves that give it a ghostly appearance. It typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and wetlands.

Its unique botanical features make it a subject of interest in botanical studies and conservation efforts.
