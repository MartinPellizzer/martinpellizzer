Primula obconica subsp. begoniiformis

Primula obconica subsp.

begoniiformis, commonly known as the begonia-like primula, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of southern China. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and large, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple.

The leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in shaded forest understories.

This subspecies is valued in horticulture for its beauty and adaptability to garden conditions.
