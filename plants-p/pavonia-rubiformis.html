Pavonia rubiformis

Pavonia rubiformis, commonly known as the red-leaf pavonia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking red-tinged leaves and clusters of small, pink to red flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories. It belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes hibiscus and cotton.

Pavonia rubiformis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant foliage.
