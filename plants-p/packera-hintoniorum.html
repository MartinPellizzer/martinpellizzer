Packera hintoniorum

Packera hintoniorum, commonly known as Hinton's groundsel, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant typically grows in moist meadows and open woodlands, thriving in cool, temperate climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow and arranged in a flat-topped cluster.

Packera hintoniorum is valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and its ornamental appeal in native plant gardens.
