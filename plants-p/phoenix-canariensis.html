Phoenix canariensis

Phoenix canariensis, commonly known as the Canary Island date palm, is a striking evergreen tree native to the Canary Islands.

It features a tall, slender trunk with a smooth, grayish-brown bark and a crown of feathery, fan-shaped leaves. The plant is adapted to arid and coastal environments, making it highly drought-tolerant. Its leaves are long, narrow, and have a silvery-blue hue due to a waxy coating.

This species is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its elegant appearance and resilience.
