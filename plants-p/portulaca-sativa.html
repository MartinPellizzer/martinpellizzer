Portulaca sativa

Portulaca sativa, commonly known as purslane, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing annual herb with fleshy, succulent leaves and small, yellow flowers. The plant thrives in hot, dry climates and is often found in sandy or rocky soils. Its leaves are edible and rich in omega-3 fatty acids, making it a nutritious addition to diets.

Purslane is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to suppress weeds in gardens.
