Padus perulata

Padus perulata, commonly known as the white thornapple, is a deciduous shrub native to parts of South America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces small, round fruits that turn from green to yellow or red as they mature. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a rough texture.

Padus perulata is adapted to a variety of soil types and can thrive in both full sun and partial shade.
