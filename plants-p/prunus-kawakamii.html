Prunus kawakamii

Prunus kawakamii, commonly known as the Kawakamii cherry, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to Japan and is characterized by its small, pinkish-white flowers and dark red fruits. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are ovate with a serrated margin, and the bark is smooth and reddish-brown.

Prunus kawakamii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate blossoms and edible fruits.
