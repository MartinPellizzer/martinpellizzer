Poa meyenii

Poa meyenii, commonly known as quinoa grass, is a flowering plant native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny grains, which are harvested for their nutritional value. Its leaves are narrow and often have a bluish-green hue, and it thrives in high-altitude environments with cool temperatures.

Poa meyenii is cultivated for both its ornamental value and its potential as a food source.
