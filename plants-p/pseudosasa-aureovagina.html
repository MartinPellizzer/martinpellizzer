Pseudosasa aureovagina

Pseudosasa aureovagina, commonly known as golden-veined bamboo, is a species of grass belonging to the Poaceae family.

It is native to eastern Asia, particularly China, where it thrives in temperate climates. The plant is characterized by its slender, upright stems and long, narrow leaves with a distinctive golden-yellow midrib. It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its aesthetic appeal and hardy nature.

Pseudosasa aureovagina is also valued in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
