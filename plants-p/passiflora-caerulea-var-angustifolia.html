Passiflora caerulea var. angustifolia

Passiflora caerulea var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf passionflower, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking blue flowers with intricate patterns of red, purple, and white. The plant produces edible, blue-black berries that are rich in antioxidants. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a rough texture, while its stems are covered in fine hairs.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine for its calming properties.
