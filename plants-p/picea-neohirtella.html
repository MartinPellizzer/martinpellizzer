Picea neohirtella

Picea neohirtella, commonly known as the dwarf white spruce, is a species of conifer native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree, typically reaching heights of up to 10 meters. The tree has slender, cylindrical cones and dark green, needle-like leaves that are often covered in a whitish bloom. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in subalpine and alpine zones.

The plant plays an important ecological role in providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
