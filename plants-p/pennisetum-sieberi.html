Pennisetum sieberi

Pennisetum sieberi, commonly known as blue fescue, is a perennial grass native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its dense, silver-blue foliage and compact growth habit, making it a popular ornamental plant. The plant typically grows to a height of 12 to 24 inches and thrives in well-drained soil with full sun exposure. Its foliage remains evergreen throughout the year, providing year-round visual interest in gardens.

Pennisetum sieberi is also valued for its drought tolerance and ability to form attractive clumps that resist invasive weeds.
