Pterocarpus glaber

Pterocarpus glaber, commonly known as the African rosewood, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, dark bark and lance-shaped leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers followed by long, flat pods containing hard, dark seeds. Its wood is highly valued for its dense, aromatic timber used in furniture and musical instruments.

The plant is also known for its medicinal properties, with various parts used in traditional medicine.
