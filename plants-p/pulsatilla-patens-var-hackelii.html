Pulsatilla patens var. hackelii

Pulsatilla patens var. hackelii, commonly known as the Hackel's pasqueflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its showy, early spring blooms of pale yellow to pink petals. The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine habitats, thriving in well-drained, rocky soils. Its leaves are palmately divided and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are usually unbranched and somewhat hairy.

The flowers are pollinated by insects, and the plant produces follicles that contain numerous small, dark seeds.
