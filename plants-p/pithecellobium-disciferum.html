Pithecellobium disciferum

Pithecellobium disciferum, commonly known as the yellow velvet tamarind, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant produces small, edible pods that contain seeds surrounded by a fleshy, sweet pulp. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and for its edible fruits, which are used in local cuisines.

Additionally, it has potential medicinal uses and is valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, which benefits soil fertility.
