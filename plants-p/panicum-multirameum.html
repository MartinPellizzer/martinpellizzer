Panicum multirameum

Panicum multirameum, commonly known as the multibranch panicgrass, is a grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and long, slender blades that often curve downward. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny, two-lobed flowers. It thrives in moist, sandy soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
