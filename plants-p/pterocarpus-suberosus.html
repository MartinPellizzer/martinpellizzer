Pterocarpus suberosus

Pterocarpus suberosus, commonly known as the African rosewood, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its thick, corky bark that gives it its scientific name. The tree produces small, yellow flowers followed by flat, elongated pods containing seeds. Its wood is highly valued for its rich, reddish color and fine grain, making it a sought-after material in furniture and musical instrument crafting.

The plant also plays an important ecological role in its native habitats, providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
