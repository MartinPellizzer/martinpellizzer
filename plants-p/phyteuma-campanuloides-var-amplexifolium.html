Phyteuma campanuloides var. amplexifolium

Phyteuma campanuloides var. amplexifolium, commonly known as the broadleaf bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to central and eastern Europe.

It features basal rosettes of broad, heart-shaped leaves that wrap around the stem, giving it its varietal name "amplexifolium.". The plant produces tall, branched stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, blue to purple flowers. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained, loamy soils.

This species is valued in gardens for its ornamental flowers and ease of cultivation.
