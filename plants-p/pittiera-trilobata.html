Pittiera trilobata

Pittiera trilobata, commonly known as the three-lobed pittiera, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its evergreen foliage. The plant features small, elliptical leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. It produces clusters of white, bell-shaped flowers in late spring, followed by bright red berries in the fall.

Pittiera trilobata is often found in acidic, well-drained soils in woodland habitats.
