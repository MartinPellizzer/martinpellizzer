Pteris bavazzanoi

Pteris bavazzanoi, commonly known as the Bavazzano's water fern, is a species of fern native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Pteridaceae and is characterized by its delicate, leathery fronds that are typically bi- or tri-pinnate. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its spores are produced on the underside of the fronds, and it reproduces through sexual reproduction via spore dispersal.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
