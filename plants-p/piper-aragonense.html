Piper aragonense

Piper aragonense, commonly known as the Aragon pepper or Aragon vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species such as black pepper. The plant produces small, green berries that turn red when mature, and the fruits are used in traditional medicine and as a spice. Its leaves are often used in local cuisines for their aromatic properties.

Piper aragonense thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in the understory of tropical forests.
