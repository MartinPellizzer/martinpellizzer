Papaver orientale var. lateritium

Papaver orientale var. lateritium, commonly known as the Persian poppy, is a striking perennial plant native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from deep red to orange and yellow. The plant features a robust, upright stem with smooth, waxy leaves that are typically lance-shaped. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its botanical beauty and unique appearance make it a valued addition to both ornamental and ecological landscapes.
