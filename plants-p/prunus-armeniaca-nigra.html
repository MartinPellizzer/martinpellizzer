Prunus armeniaca nigra

Prunus armeniaca nigra, commonly known as the black cherry, is a deciduous tree native to parts of Asia and the Middle East.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its dark, glossy leaves and showy pink to white flowers in spring. The tree produces small, dark purple to black fruits that are rich in antioxidants and have been used in traditional medicine. Its bark is rough and fissured, with a distinctive aromatic scent.

The plant is valued for its ornamental beauty and historical use in both culinary and medicinal applications.
