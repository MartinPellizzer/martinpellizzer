Paphiopedilum villosum

Paphiopedilum villosum, commonly known as the hairy paphiopedilum, is a tropical orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its velvety, hairy leaves and distinctive, showy flowers with a unique lip structure. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers bloom in shades of white, pink, and purple, often with a contrasting yellow or green throat.

Paphiopedilum villosum is prized for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to cultivate under the right conditions.
