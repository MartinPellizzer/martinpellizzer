Pilosella eglandulosa

Pilosella eglandulosa, commonly known as the smooth pearly everlasting, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in summer, and its leaves are typically narrow and covered in fine, soft hairs.

Pilosella eglandulosa is often found in open habitats such as meadows and disturbed soils, and it is known for its drought tolerance and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
