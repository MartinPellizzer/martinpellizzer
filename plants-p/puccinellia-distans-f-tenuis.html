Puccinellia distans f. tenuis

Puccinellia distans f. tenuis, commonly known as slender saltbush, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Poaceae.

It is a perennial grass that typically grows in saline or coastal environments. The plant is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and small, spike-like inflorescences. It is well adapted to arid and salty conditions, making it a common species in coastal dunes and salt marshes.

Its ecological importance lies in its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various small organisms.
