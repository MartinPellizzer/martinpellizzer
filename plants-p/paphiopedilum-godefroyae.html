Paphiopedilum godefroyae

Paphiopedilum godefroyae, commonly known as the Godefroy's lady's slipper, is a rare and exotic orchid native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique, intricate flower structure with a pouch-like lip that resembles a slipper. The plant typically grows as a terrestrial orchid, often found in shaded, humid environments with well-drained soil. Its flowers are usually a mix of white, green, and yellow, with a distinctive golden throat.

Paphiopedilum godefroyae is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts due to its rarity and beauty.
