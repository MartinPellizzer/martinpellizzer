Phyllanthus calocarpus

Phyllanthus calocarpus, commonly known as the golden apple tree, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree that can grow up to 10 meters in height. The plant has simple, alternate, ovate to elliptical leaves with a glossy green upper surface and a lighter underside. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, followed by small, round, yellow fruits that are edible.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and has traditional uses in herbal medicine.
