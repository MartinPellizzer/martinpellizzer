Pleurothallis melanochthoda

Pleurothallis melanochthoda, commonly known as the black-stemmed orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the cloud forests of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, dark green stems and small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments and requires well-draining soil to thrive. Its flowers are usually white or pale yellow, with a distinctive lip that is often darker in color.

Pleurothallis melanochthoda is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
