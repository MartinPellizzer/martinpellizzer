Polypodium sarcopum

Polypodium sarcopum, commonly known as the rock fern, is a member of the Polypodiaceae family and is native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is a perennial evergreen fern characterized by its dense, leathery fronds that form a compact, cushion-like structure. The plant typically grows on rocky substrates or in shaded, moist environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its rhizomes are thick and wiry, providing structural support and aiding in nutrient absorption.

Polypodium sarcopum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
