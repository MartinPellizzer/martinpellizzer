Pyrola grandiflora

Pyrola grandiflora, commonly known as the large-flowered wintergreen, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the northern regions of North America.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its evergreen leaves and showy, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist forest floors, preferring acidic soils. Its flowers, which bloom in early spring, are often pink or white and are pollinated by insects.

Pyrola grandiflora is also valued for its aromatic leaves and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
