Premna odorata var. detergibilis

Premna odorata var. detergibilis, commonly known as the soapberry plant, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves that are often hairy on the underside. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by spherical, berry-like fruits that are yellow when mature. The fruit contains a single seed surrounded by a fleshy, sticky pulp.

It is notable for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its use in traditional medicine and as a source of natural soap.
