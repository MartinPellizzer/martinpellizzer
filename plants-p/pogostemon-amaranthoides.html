Pogostemon amaranthoides

Pogostemon amaranthoides, commonly known as the red-flowered pogostemon, is a herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic and medicinal plants. The plant is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and striking red or pink flowers that bloom in whorls along the stems. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Pogostemon amaranthoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
