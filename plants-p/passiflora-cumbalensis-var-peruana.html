Passiflora cumbalensis var. peruana

Passiflora cumbalensis var. peruana, commonly known as the Peruvian passionflower, is a climbing vine native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, complex flowers that feature five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments surrounding the reproductive structures. The plant produces edible, tropical berries that are often used in local cuisines. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped with a velvety texture and are often used in traditional medicine.

This species is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance in its native habitat.
