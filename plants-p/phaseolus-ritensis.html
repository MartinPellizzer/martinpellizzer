Phaseolus ritensis

Phaseolus ritensis, commonly known as the Andean runner bean, is a species native to the Andes region of South America.

It is a climbing vine that produces edible beans with a mild, nutty flavor. The plant features compound leaves with three to five leaflets and produces small, yellow flowers. Its fruit pods are typically elongated and can grow up to 20 centimeters in length.

Phaseolus ritensis is valued for its nutritional content and is cultivated for both food and ornamental purposes.
