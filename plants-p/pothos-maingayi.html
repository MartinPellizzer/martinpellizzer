Pothos maingayi

Pothos maingayi, commonly known as the golden pothos, is a climbing vine native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, dark green leaves that often have a slight variegation of yellow or gold. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers, though it is more celebrated for its foliage than its blooms. It thrives in bright, indirect light and is relatively easy to care for, making it a popular houseplant.

Pothos maingayi is also known for its ability to purify indoor air, enhancing its appeal as a decorative and beneficial plant.
