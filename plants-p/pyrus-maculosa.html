Pyrus maculosa

Pyrus maculosa, commonly known as the spotted pear, is a species of flowering plant in the genus Pyrus.

It is native to parts of Asia, including China and Japan, and is characterized by its ornamental value due to its distinctive spotted leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in the spring, followed by edible fruits that are similar to common pears. Pyrus maculosa is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal and can thrive in a variety of soil types.

It prefers full sun to partial shade and is relatively low maintenance once established.
