Prunus jamasakura var. chikusiensis

Prunus jamasakura var. chikusiensis, commonly known as the Chikusi cherry, is a variety of Prunus jamasakura, a species native to Japan.

It is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that bloom in early spring, typically in shades of pink and white. The plant is a deciduous shrub or small tree, often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes. Its foliage turns a striking red or orange in the autumn, adding to its aesthetic appeal.

The Chikusi cherry is also valued for its use in traditional Japanese culture, including in festivals and as a symbol of seasonal beauty.
