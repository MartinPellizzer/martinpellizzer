Phragmipedium humboldtii var. exstaminodium

Phragmipedium humboldtii var. exstaminodium, commonly known as the Humboldt's phragmipedium, is a species of orchid native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, large, showy flowers that are typically greenish-yellow with reddish-brown markings. The plant produces pendulous inflorescences with numerous blooms, making it a popular choice among orchid enthusiasts. It thrives in shaded, humid environments and requires well-drained, acidic soil.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated for its beauty and unique floral structure.
