Paris polyphylla var. violacea

Paris polyphylla var. violacea, commonly known as the violet-flowered Paris, is a striking member of the Melanthiaceae family native to the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its tall, grass-like stems and clusters of delicate, violet-blue flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces small, dark blue berries that contain toxic compounds, making it potentially harmful to humans and animals. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense colonies in moist, shaded woodland areas.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal uses, it is sometimes cultivated, though caution is necessary due to its toxic properties.
