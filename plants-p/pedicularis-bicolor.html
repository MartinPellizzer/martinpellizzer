Pedicularis bicolor

Pedicularis bicolor, commonly known as the two-colored lousewort, is a flowering plant native to the alpine regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the genus Pedicularis, which includes over 400 species of flowering plants. The plant is characterized by its striking, spurred flowers that range in color from pink to red, often with a contrasting throat. It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils at high altitudes, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions.

The species is notable for its unique morphology and is often found in mountainous habitats across the northern hemisphere.
