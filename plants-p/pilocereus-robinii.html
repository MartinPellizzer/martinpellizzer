Pilocereus robinii

Pilocereus robinii, commonly known as the night blooming cereus, is a cacti species native to South America.

It is a low-growing plant with cylindrical stems that are often covered in fine, white spines. The plant produces large, showy flowers that open at night, typically in shades of white or pale pink. Its flowers have a strong, sweet fragrance and are pollinated by moths and bats.

Pilocereus robinii is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water once established.
