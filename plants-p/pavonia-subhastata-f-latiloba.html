Pavonia subhastata f. latiloba

Pavonia subhastata f. latiloba, commonly known as the broad-leaved pavonia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, pink to red bract-like flowers. The leaves are ovate to elliptic, often with a hairy texture, and the plant produces small, clustered inflorescences. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is adapted to moist, well-drained soils.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in native plant gardens.
