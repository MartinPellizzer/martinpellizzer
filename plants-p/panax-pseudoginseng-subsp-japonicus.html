Panax pseudoginseng subsp. japonicus

Panax pseudoginseng subsp.

japonicus, commonly known as Japanese ginseng, is a species of plant in the Araliaceae family. It is native to Japan and parts of eastern Asia, where it thrives in forested areas with acidic soil.

The plant is characterized by its distinctive, palmately compound leaves and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. Its roots, which are the primary part used in traditional medicine, are rich in saponins and other bioactive compounds.

Due to its medicinal properties, it is often cultivated for use in herbal remedies and health supplements.
