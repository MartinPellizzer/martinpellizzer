Pinus armandi subsp. farjonii

Pinus armandi subsp.

farjonii, commonly known as the Farjon's Chinese red pine, is a subspecies native to China. It is a tall evergreen conifer characterized by its slender, upward-curving needles and distinctive, elongated cones.

The tree typically grows in mountainous regions with well-drained soils, preferring a temperate climate. Its foliage is dense and dark green, providing ornamental value in horticultural settings.

The subspecies is notable for its genetic diversity and ecological significance in its native habitat.
