Pleurothallis andreettae

Pleurothallis andreettae, commonly known as the Andreetta's orchid, is a species native to the cloud forests of Ecuador.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its small, intricate flowers that typically feature a vibrant color palette. The plant has a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that support its foliage and flowering structure. Its flowers are often adorned with intricate patterns and are pollinated by specific insects, showcasing a specialized ecological relationship.

Due to its habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
