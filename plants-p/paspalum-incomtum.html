Paspalum incomtum

Paspalum incomtum, commonly known as the slender paspalum, is a perennial grass native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers. It thrives in moist to wet environments, often found in marshy areas and along riverbanks.

Its botanical features make it an important species in wetland ecosystems and a potential candidate for ecological restoration projects.
