Paneion buckleyanum var. maius

Paneion buckleyanum var. maius, commonly known as the giant paneion, is a striking herbaceous plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, lance-shaped leaves that can reach lengths of up to 30 centimeters. The plant produces showy, violet-blue flowers arranged in dense clusters, which bloom during the cooler months. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense colonies in its natural habitat.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens for its ornamental value.
