Paspalum selloi

Paspalum selloi, commonly known as the Sello's grass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist to wet environments, such as marshes and riparian areas. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of spikelets, which are often reddish in color.

Paspalum selloi is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in wetland restoration projects.
