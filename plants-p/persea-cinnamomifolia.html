Persea cinnamomifolia

Persea cinnamomifolia, commonly known as the Mexican persimmon, is a small evergreen tree native to Mexico and Central America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and fragrant, yellowish-green flowers. The tree produces small, orange-red fruits that are edible when fully mature, though they are typically astringent until ripe. Its bark is aromatic and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various soil types.
