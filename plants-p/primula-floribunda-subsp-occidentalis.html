Primula floribunda subsp. occidentalis

Primula floribunda subsp.

occidentalis, commonly known as the western primrose, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to western North America. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

The plant features rosettes of broad, ovate leaves and produces clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. It blooms from late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
