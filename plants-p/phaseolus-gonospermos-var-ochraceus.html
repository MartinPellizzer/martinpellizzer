Phaseolus gonospermos var. ochraceus

Phaseolus gonospermos var. ochraceus, commonly known as the yellow-seeded scarlet runner bean, is a variant of the scarlet runner bean native to South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow seeds and robust, climbing vines that produce large, showy flowers. The plant thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth. Its leaves are heart-shaped and often have a hairy texture, while its pods are typically reddish-purple when mature.

This variety is valued for both its ornamental appeal and edible beans, making it a popular choice for gardeners and farmers alike.
