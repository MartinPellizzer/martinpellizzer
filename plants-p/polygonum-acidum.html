Polygonum acidum

Polygonum acidum, commonly known as sour dock, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, which includes other familiar plants like buckwheat and sorrel. The plant is characterized by its alternate, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small, greenish-white, and clustered in dense, rounded inflorescences.

Polygonum acidum is typically found in moist soils along stream banks and in disturbed areas.
