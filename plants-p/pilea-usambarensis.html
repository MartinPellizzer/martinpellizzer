Pilea usambarensis

Pilea usambarensis, commonly known as the African velvetleaf, is a flowering plant native to eastern Africa.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped, waxy leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers that are surrounded by a modified leaf called a spathe. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in moist woodlands and along riverbanks.

Pilea usambarensis is valued for its ornamental foliage and is sometimes cultivated as a houseplant.
