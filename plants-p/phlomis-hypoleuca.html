Phlomis hypoleuca

Phlomis hypoleuca, commonly known as narrowleaf mullein, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and large, gray-green, fuzzy leaves. The plant produces showy, pink to purple flowers in late summer, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its botanical features make it both ornamental and ecologically valuable in its native habitat.
