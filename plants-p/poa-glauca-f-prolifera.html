Poa glauca f. prolifera

Poa glauca f. prolifera, commonly known as the bluegrass meadow grass, is a variant of the bluegrass species native to North America.

It is characterized by its fine, bluish-green leaves and compact growth habit, making it a popular choice for lawns and ornamental use. The plant typically grows in temperate climates and thrives in well-drained soils. It reproduces through both seeds and vegetative means, such as rhizomes, which contribute to its ability to spread and form dense mats.

Its botanical features make it well-suited for low-maintenance landscaping and ecological restoration projects.
