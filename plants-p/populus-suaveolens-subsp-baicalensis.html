Populus suaveolens subsp. baicalensis

Populus suaveolens subsp.

baicalensis, commonly known as the Siberian poplar, is a deciduous tree native to the cold climates of eastern Asia. It belongs to the Salicaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive yellowish-green underside.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in catkins, which appear in early spring before the leaves emerge. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found near rivers and lakes in its natural habitat.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to tolerate harsh environmental conditions.
