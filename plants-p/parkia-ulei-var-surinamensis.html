Parkia ulei var. surinamensis

Parkia ulei var. surinamensis, commonly known as the Surinam legume or the African ironwood, is a deciduous tree native to tropical regions of Africa and South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy yellow flowers. The tree produces elongated pods containing flat, fibrous seeds. It is valued for its durable wood, which is used in construction and furniture-making.

The plant also has traditional medicinal uses in some cultures.
