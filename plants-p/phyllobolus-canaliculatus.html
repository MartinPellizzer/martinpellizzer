Phyllobolus canaliculatus

Phyllobolus canaliculatus, commonly known as the channel-veined leaf beetle, is a species of leaf beetle characterized by its distinctive leaf-like appearance.

It belongs to the family Chrysomelidae and is native to regions in Africa, particularly in countries like Kenya and Tanzania. The beetle's body is flattened and resembles a leaf, which helps it blend into its environment. Its coloration is typically green or brown, with intricate patterns that mimic the veins of its host plant.

This camouflage is a key adaptation for avoiding predation and thriving in its natural habitat.
