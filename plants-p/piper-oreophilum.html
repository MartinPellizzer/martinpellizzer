Piper oreophilum

Piper oreophilum, commonly known as the mountain pepper plant, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to the high-altitude regions of South America, particularly in the Andes. The plant is characterized by its small, elongated leaves and clusters of tiny, greenish-white flowers. It produces small, peppercorn-like fruits that are typically used in traditional medicine.

The botanical features of Piper oreophilum make it an important species in both ecological and pharmacological contexts.
