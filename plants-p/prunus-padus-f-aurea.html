Prunus padus f. aurea

Prunus padus f. aurea, commonly known as the golden-flowered bird cherry, is a cultivar of the bird cherry tree.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers that appear in early spring, contrasting beautifully with its dark green foliage. The plant is native to Europe and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks. It has small, edible fruits that mature into dark red drupes in late summer.

This ornamental tree is valued for its aesthetic appeal and its ability to attract pollinators.
