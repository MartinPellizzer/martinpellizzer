Peripleura virgata

Peripleura virgata, commonly known as the silverleaf sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or wetlands. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery bloom, giving it its common name. Its flowering spikes are usually borne on tall, slender stems, producing small, inconspicuous flowers.

Peripleura virgata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
