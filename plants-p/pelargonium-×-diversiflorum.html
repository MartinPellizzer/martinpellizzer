Pelargonium × diversiflorum

Pelargonium × diversiflorum, commonly known as the multibract geranium, is a hybrid plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, showy flowers that range in color from pink to red. The leaves are typically rounded with a slightly hairy texture, and the plant produces bract-like structures that resemble small leaves around the flower clusters. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its vibrant blooms and ability to thrive in well-drained soil.

This species is also valued for its use in gardens and as a cut flower due to its long-lasting flowers.
