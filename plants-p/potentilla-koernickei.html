Potentilla koernickei

Potentilla koernickei, commonly known as the Koernick's cinquefoil, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, where it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun exposure. The plant is characterized by its upright stems and small, clustered yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate, pinnately compound, and covered in a fine layer of silky hairs.

Potentilla koernickei is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and attractive floral display.
