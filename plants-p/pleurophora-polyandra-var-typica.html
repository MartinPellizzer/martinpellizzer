Pleurophora polyandra var. typica

Pleurophora polyandra var. typica, commonly known as the purple-veined orchid, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its striking violet and green veined petals. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments such as tropical forests. Its inflorescence consists of a spike bearing several showy flowers with a distinctive lip.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
