Prunus communis monstr. albiplena

Prunus communis monstr.

albiplena, commonly known as the white-flowered cherry, is a cultivar of the common cherry tree. It is characterized by its double white blossoms that appear in early spring, adding a striking visual display.

The plant has small, oval leaves that turn yellow in the fall. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

This variety is also valued for its fragrant flowers and modest fruit production.
