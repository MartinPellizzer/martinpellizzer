Pleurothallis mystax

Pleurothallis mystax, commonly known as the hair-leaf orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, hairy leaf structure that gives it its common name. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in the understory of forests. Its flowers are small and usually greenish-white, with a subtle fragrance.

Pleurothallis mystax is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts for its distinctive foliage.
