Phyllanthus puber

Phyllanthus puber, commonly known as the hairy phyllanthus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Euphorbiaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa and is characterized by its small, green, hairy leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in umbels, typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The plant has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
