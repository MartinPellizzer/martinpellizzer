Prunus emarginata var. crenulata

Prunus emarginata var. crenulata, commonly known as the Japanese cherry plum, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and oval-shaped leaves with a distinctive wavy margin. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in spring, followed by edible, sweet fruits that are often used in jams and preserves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its seasonal display of blossoms and fruit.
