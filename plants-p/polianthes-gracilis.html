Polianthes gracilis

Polianthes gracilis, commonly known as the slender pineapple flower, is a striking tropical plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its compact, rosette-like growth habit. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences with clusters of fragrant, white to pale pink flowers. Its leaves are narrow, strap-like, and often have a waxy texture.

Polianthes gracilis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
