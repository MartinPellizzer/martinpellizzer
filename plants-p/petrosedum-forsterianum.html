Petrosedum forsterianum

Petrosedum forsterianum, commonly known as the Forster's stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the Crassulaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, thick leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, or purple, typically during the spring or early summer. It is often used in rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its low maintenance and ornamental value.

Petrosedum forsterianum is also valued for its ability to stabilize soil in rocky or degraded landscapes.
