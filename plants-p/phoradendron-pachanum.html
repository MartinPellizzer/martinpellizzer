Phoradendron pachanum

Phoradendron pachanum, commonly known as the mistletoe of the Andes, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches to the branches of host trees, primarily in the Andean region.

It lacks roots and absorbs water and nutrients through its leaves, which are covered in a dense layer of spongy, white tissue. The plant produces small, white or yellowish berries that are a food source for various birds and mammals. Its life cycle involves birds dispersing its seeds, which then germinate on the bark of host trees.

This unique relationship with its host is a key feature of its botanical ecology.
