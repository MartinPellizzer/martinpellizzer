Papaver radicatum f. latifolium

Papaver radicatum f. latifolium, commonly known as the broadleaf poppy, is a variant of the common poppy characterized by its broader, more robust leaves.

It is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe. The plant produces striking, showy flowers that are typically bright red or orange with a black center. Its leaves are usually oval or lance-shaped and have a rough, hairy texture.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
