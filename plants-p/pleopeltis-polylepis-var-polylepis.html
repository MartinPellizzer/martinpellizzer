Pleopeltis polylepis var. polylepis

Pleopeltis polylepis var. polylepis, commonly known as the giant tree fern, is a striking member of the Polypodiaceae family.

It is characterized by its massive, tree-like form with a central trunk and a crown of large, feathery fronds. The plant thrives in humid, tropical environments, often found in shaded, moist forests. Its fronds are typically bioluminescent, displaying a vibrant green hue with intricate patterns.

This variety is notable for its slow growth and long lifespan, making it a valuable addition to botanical collections and conservation efforts.
