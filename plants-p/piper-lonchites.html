Piper lonchites

Piper lonchites, commonly known as the long-leaf pepper, is a member of the Piperaceae family, which includes various pepper species.

It is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil and Peru. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that develop into elongated, slender fruits. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a slightly serrated edge.

The plant is valued for its aromatic properties and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
