Poa nemoralis proles firmula

Poa nemoralis proles firmula, commonly known as the firm-leaf wood sedge, is a flowering plant belonging to the grass family Poaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically dark green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Poa nemoralis proles firmula is often found in temperate regions and is valued for its ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
