Paspalum longicuspe

Paspalum longicuspe, commonly known as the long-beaked paspalum, is a perennial grass native to South America.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and dense, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, thriving in moist, nutrient-rich soils. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense mats, which can stabilize soil and prevent erosion.

Paspalum longicuspe is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
