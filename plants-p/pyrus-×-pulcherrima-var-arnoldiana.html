Pyrus × pulcherrima var. arnoldiana

Pyrus × pulcherrima var. arnoldiana, commonly known as the Arnoldian crabapple, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its ornamental value, featuring showy flowers and small, edible fruits. The plant typically grows as a deciduous shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 6 meters. Its leaves are ovate with serrated edges, and it produces clusters of white or pink blossoms in early spring.

The fruit, while not typically consumed by humans, is used in ornamental horticulture and can be processed for jams or jellies.
