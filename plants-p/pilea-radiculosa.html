Pilea radiculosa

Pilea radiculosa, commonly known as the Chinese money plant, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It features rounded, waxy leaves that are typically green with a slight sheen, often arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers that appear in clusters during the spring or summer. It thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Pilea radiculosa is also noted for its ability to propagate easily through stem cuttings, enhancing its appeal among plant enthusiasts.
