Phoradendron falcifrons

Phoradendron falcifrons, commonly known as the sickle-leaf mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on various host trees in the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It has small, oval-shaped leaves that are often clustered at the tips of its branches, and its flowers are typically white or pale green. The plant produces small, fleshy berries that are a food source for birds and other animals.

Its parasitic nature allows it to draw water and nutrients from its host, while still performing photosynthesis to some extent.
