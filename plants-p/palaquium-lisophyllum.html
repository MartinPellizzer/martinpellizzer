Palaquium lisophyllum

Palaquium lisophyllum, commonly known as the white sapote, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central America and the Caribbean.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and glossy, elliptical leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers that give way to spherical fruits with a thick, sweet, white pulp. Its fruit is edible and has a flavor similar to a combination of a pear and a custard apple.

Palaquium lisophyllum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of nutritious fruit.
