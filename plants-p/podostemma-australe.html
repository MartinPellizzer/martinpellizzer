Podostemma australe

Podostemma australe, commonly known as the Australian podostem, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is a small, herbaceous species that typically grows in wetland or riparian habitats. The plant is characterized by its distinctive, small, opposite leaves and its unique, spiral arrangement of flowers. Its flowers are usually greenish-white in color and are borne on short, erect stalks.

Podostemma australe is often found in still or slow-moving water, where it plays a role in the local ecosystem.
