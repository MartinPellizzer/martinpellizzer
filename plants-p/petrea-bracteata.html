Petrea bracteata

Petrea bracteata, commonly known as the Brazilian flame tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, bright red to orange flowers that bloom in clusters, often resembling flames. The plant has pinnate leaves composed of several oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Petrea bracteata is also known for its ability to produce small, round, red or yellow berries that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans.
