Phoebe pittieri

Phoebe pittieri, commonly known as the Mexican ash, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Mexico.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae, which includes other aromatic plants like cinnamon and avocado. The tree has smooth, dark-gray bark and simple, alternate, elliptical leaves that are glossy on top and lighter underneath. It produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by dark purple berries, which are an important food source for local wildlife.

Phoebe pittieri is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
