Picea pungens f. glauca-pendula

Picea pungens f. glauca-pendula, commonly known as the blue spruce weeping form, is a variant of the blue spruce native to the Rocky Mountains.

It is characterized by its distinctive blue-gray foliage and cascading, weeping branches that give it a unique, ornamental appearance. The leaves are needle-like, four-sided, and have a waxy coating that enhances their blue hue. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

This cultivar is often used in landscaping for its aesthetic appeal and hardy nature.
