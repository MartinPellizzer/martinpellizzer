Prunus wattii

Prunus wattii, commonly known as the Watt's cherry, is a small deciduous tree native to China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and showy, pinkish-white flowers. The tree produces small, round drupes that are typically red or black when mature. It prefers well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
