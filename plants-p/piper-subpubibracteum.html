Piper subpubibracteum

Piper subpubibracteum, commonly known as the pubescent pepper vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species. The plant has heart-shaped, glossy leaves and produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into three-chambered berries. Its stems are covered in fine, short hairs, giving it a subpubescent appearance.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has potential medicinal uses in traditional practices.
