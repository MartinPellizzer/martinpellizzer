Poa buckleyana var. sandbergii

Poa buckleyana var. sandbergii, commonly known as Sandberg's bluegrass, is a cool-season grass native to North America.

It is characterized by its fine, bluish-green foliage and tufted growth habit, forming dense, low-growing turf. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves with a prominent midrib and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in ornamental landscaping for its aesthetic appeal.

This variety is particularly valued for its ability to withstand drought and maintain color throughout the growing season.
