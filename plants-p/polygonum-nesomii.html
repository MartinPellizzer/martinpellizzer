Polygonum nesomii

Polygonum nesomii, commonly known as the Neomii buckthorn, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is a member of the Polygonaceae family, which includes plants such as buckwheat and sorrel. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. It produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Its leaves are alternate and lance-shaped, often with a reddish tinge, contributing to its ornamental value in dry landscapes.
