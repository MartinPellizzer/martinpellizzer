Phyllanthus umbricola

Phyllanthus umbricola, commonly known as the dark-leaved phyllanthus, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It features small, elliptical leaves that are typically dark green with a glossy texture. The plant produces tiny, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by small, spherical fruits. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in horticultural settings.
