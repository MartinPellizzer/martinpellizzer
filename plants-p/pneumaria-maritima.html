Pneumaria maritima

Pneumaria maritima, commonly known as sea lungwort, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of the northern hemisphere.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in saline or brackish environments, such as salt marshes and tidal flats. The plant features succulent, lobed leaves and produces small, pink or purple flowers arranged in clusters. Its root system is adapted to tolerate high salinity and fluctuating water levels.

Pneumaria maritima plays an important ecological role in stabilizing coastal soils and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
