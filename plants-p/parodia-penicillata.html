Parodia penicillata

Parodia penicillata, commonly known as the "Penicillate Cactus," is a species native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its compact, globular shape with ribbed stems. The plant produces yellow flowers during the summer months, which are typically borne on short, erect stems. Its leaves are reduced to spines, and it thrives in well-drained soil with minimal water.

Parodia penicillata is a popular choice for rock gardens and indoor cactus collections due to its hardy nature and attractive appearance.
