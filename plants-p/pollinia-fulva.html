Pollinia fulva

Pollinia fulva, commonly known as the yellow orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow flowers and unique pollination mechanism involving pollinia. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as rainforests. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, and it produces small, greenish-brown fruits.

Pollinia fulva is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
