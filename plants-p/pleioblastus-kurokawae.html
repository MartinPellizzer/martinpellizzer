Pleioblastus kurokawae

Pleioblastus kurokawae, commonly known as the black bamboo, is a species of grass in the family Poaceae.

It is native to Japan and is characterized by its dark green to almost black culms that are often covered in a fine, whitish bloom. The plant grows in dense clumps and produces small, inconspicuous flowers in early summer. Its foliage is narrow and grass-like, with a slightly rough texture.

This bamboo is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
