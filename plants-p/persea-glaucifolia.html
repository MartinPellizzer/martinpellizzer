Persea glaucifolia

Persea glaucifolia, commonly known as the silverleaf persimmon, is a small evergreen tree native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that often have a waxy, silver-tinged underside. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by edible, orange-red berries in the fall. Its fruit is typically harvested when fully ripe, and it is valued for both its ornamental appeal and its edible yield.

The tree thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with well-drained soil and partial shade.
