Primula rhodochroa var. geraldinae

Primula rhodochroa var. geraldinae, commonly known as the Geraldine primrose, is a rare and striking variant of the primula genus native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that range from deep pink to almost crimson, often with a distinctive white or pale yellow throat. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in cool, moist environments with well-drained soil. Its foliage consists of broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery down.

This variety is highly valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in specialized gardens and botanical collections.
