Potamogeton purpurascens

Potamogeton purpurascens, commonly known as the purple spikegrass, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in North America.

It features long, slender stems that are often reddish in color, particularly at the nodes, giving it its characteristic appearance. The plant produces small, green to reddish flowers that are clustered in spike-like inflorescences. Its leaves are narrow and ribbon-like, arranged alternately along the stem.

Potamogeton purpurascens plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
