Primula tanupoda

Primula tanupoda, commonly known as the Tanupoda primrose, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that come in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky slopes. Its leaves are usually heart-shaped and covered in a fine, hairy texture.

Primula tanupoda is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens for its beauty and ecological significance.
