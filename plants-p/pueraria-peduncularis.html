Pueraria peduncularis

Pueraria peduncularis, commonly known as the Chinese kudzu or climbing peanut vine, is a vigorous vine native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of small, white to pale purple flowers. The plant produces cylindrical, pod-like fruits that contain several small, hard seeds. It thrives in moist, fertile soils and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Due to its rapid growth, it can become invasive in certain environments.
