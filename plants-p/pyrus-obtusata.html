Pyrus obtusata

Pyrus obtusata, commonly known as the Japanese whitebeam, is a deciduous tree native to Japan.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and alternate, simple leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in spring, which are followed by edible, round berries that turn from green to yellow or red as they mature.

Pyrus obtusata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adaptable to a variety of soil types.
