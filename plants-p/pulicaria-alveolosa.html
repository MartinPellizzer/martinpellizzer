Pulicaria alveolosa

Pulicaria alveolosa, commonly known as the woolly groundsel, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that covers the stems and leaves, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the summer months, typically from June to August. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as slopes and grasslands.

Pulicaria alveolosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique texture and resilience.
