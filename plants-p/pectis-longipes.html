Pectis longipes

Pectis longipes, commonly known as the long-stemmed pectis, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features slender, branching stems and small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters. Its leaves are typically narrow and linear, with a slightly hairy texture.

Pectis longipes is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments.
