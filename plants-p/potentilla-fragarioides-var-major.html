Potentilla fragarioides var. major

Potentilla fragarioides var. major, commonly known as the tall cinquefoil, is a perennial shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has pinnately compound leaves with five to seven leaflets, and its stems are typically covered in fine, white hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy and adaptable species.

The tall cinquefoil is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
