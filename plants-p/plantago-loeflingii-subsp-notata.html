Plantago loeflingii subsp. notata

Plantago loeflingii subsp.

notata, commonly known as the narrowleaf plantain, is a perennial herb native to the western United States. It features upright, slender stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in dense spikes during the summer months. It thrives in dry, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and gravelly areas.

Its botanical characteristics make it well adapted to arid environments and suitable for use in native plant gardens.
