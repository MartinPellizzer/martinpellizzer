Phyllanthus multilocularis var. pubescens

Phyllanthus multilocularis var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy mulberry, is a deciduous shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, clustered leaves that are hairy on the underside, giving it its varietal name. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by fleshy, red berries that are edible. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, shaded environments.

The hairy mulberry is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in herbal medicine.
