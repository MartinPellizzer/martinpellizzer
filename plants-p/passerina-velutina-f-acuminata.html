Passerina velutina f. acuminata

Passerina velutina f. acuminata, commonly known as the velvetleaf pasqueflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its striking violet to purple flowers, which bloom in early spring. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens with suitable climate conditions.
