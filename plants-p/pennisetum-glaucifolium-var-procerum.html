Pennisetum glaucifolium var. procerum

Pennisetum glaucifolium var. procerum, commonly known as blue foxtail, is a variant of the blue oatgrass species native to the United States.

It is characterized by its striking blue-gray foliage and panicle-shaped inflorescence that turns golden in maturity. This grass thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping. Its drought tolerance and low maintenance requirements contribute to its adaptability in various climates.

The plant is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators and its use in native plant gardens.
