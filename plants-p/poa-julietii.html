Poa julietii

Poa julietii, commonly known as Juliet's Meadowgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-blue foliage and compact growth habit, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full to partial sunlight, preferring cooler climates.

Due to its aesthetic appeal and low maintenance requirements, it is often used in rock gardens and as a ground cover.
