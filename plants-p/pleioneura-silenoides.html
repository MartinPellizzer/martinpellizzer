Pleioneura silenoides

Pleioneura silenoides, commonly known as the silvery bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue, often with a silvery hue on the inner petals. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as forest understories, where it thrives in cool and humid conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

The species is valued in horticulture for its unique floral display and adaptability to woodland gardens.
