Passiflora bacabensis

Passiflora bacabensis, commonly known as the Bacab Passionflower, is a climbing vine native to Central America.

It is characterized by its striking, complex flowers that feature five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments. The plant produces edible, orange-yellow berries that are typically consumed fresh or used in culinary applications. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Passiflora bacabensis is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
