Pariana microstachya

Pariana microstachya, commonly known as the small-stalked pariana, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and typically herbaceous growth habit. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in compact clusters. It thrives in high-altitude environments with cool, moist conditions.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
