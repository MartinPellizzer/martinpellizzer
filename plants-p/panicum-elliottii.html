Panicum elliottii

Panicum elliottii, commonly known as Elliott's panicgrass, is a warm-season grass native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along riverbanks. The plant has slender, erect stalks with small, spikelet-based flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often with a bluish-green hue.

Panicum elliottii plays an important role in wetland ecosystems by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
