Portulaca kermesina var. lutea

Portulaca kermesina var. lutea, commonly known as the yellow moss rose, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the Americas.

It is a low-growing annual herb with fleshy, succulent stems and leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, often appearing in clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its ability to store water in its leaves allows it to survive in dry conditions, showcasing its adaptation to harsh environments.
