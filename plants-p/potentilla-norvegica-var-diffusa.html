Potentilla norvegica var. diffusa

Potentilla norvegica var. diffusa, commonly known as the Norwegian cinquefoil, is a low-growing perennial shrub native to northern Europe.

It features wiry, sprawling stems and bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has finely divided, feather-like leaves that are often covered in a soft, fuzzy layer. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value.
