Piper marginatum var. firmum

Piper marginatum var. firmum, commonly known as the Java pepper or long pepper, is a climbing shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are usually arranged in a spiral pattern along the stems. The plant produces small, green berries that turn red when ripe, containing the aromatic seeds used in culinary and medicinal applications. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

The plant is valued for its pungent, spicy flavor and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
