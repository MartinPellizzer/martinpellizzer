Pilea leptogramma

Pilea leptogramma, commonly known as the Chinese money plant, is a species of flowering plant in the family Araceae.

It is native to China and is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that are thick and succulent, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers that are followed by bright red berries. It is often grown as a decorative houseplant due to its attractive foliage and ease of care.

Pilea leptogramma prefers bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it suitable for indoor cultivation.
