Prunus × lannesiana f. conspicua

Prunus × lannesiana f. conspicua, commonly known as the double-flowered cherry, is a cultivar of the Prunus genus that exhibits distinctive botanical features.

It is characterized by its large, double flowers that consist of numerous small petals, giving it a lush and ornamental appearance. The plant typically grows as a deciduous shrub or small tree, with ovate to elliptical leaves that are dark green and glossy. It produces edible fruits, though the flavor is often less pronounced compared to other cherry varieties.

This cultivar is valued in horticulture for its showy blooms and is often used in ornamental gardening.
