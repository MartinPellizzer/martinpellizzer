Planchonella linggensis var. garberi

Planchonella linggensis var. garberi, commonly known as the Garber's pandanus, is a rare and lesser-known variant of the Planchonella genus.

It is native to specific regions of Southeast Asia, particularly parts of Indonesia and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its unique foliage, featuring broad, lance-shaped leaves with a waxy texture and a distinct aromatic scent. It thrives in tropical climates with high humidity and well-drained soil.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
