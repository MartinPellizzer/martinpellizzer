Pilosella venosa

Pilosella venosa, commonly known as the hairy fleabane, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it its common name. The plant produces small, daisy-like yellow flowers that bloom in summer. It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in grassy areas and along roadsides.

Pilosella venosa is generally hardy and thrives in well-drained soils.
