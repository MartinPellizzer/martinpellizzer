Potentilla morisonii

Potentilla morisonii, commonly known as Morrison's cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its small, showy yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils. It has compound leaves with five leaflets and is often found in mountainous regions.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
