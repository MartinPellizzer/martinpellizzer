Phaseolus trichocarpus

Phaseolus trichocarpus, commonly known as the scarlet runner bean, is a climbing legume native to the Americas.

It produces vibrant red or orange flowers and edible pods that contain kidney-shaped seeds. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets and is often cultivated for its ornamental and culinary value. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This species is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, which benefits soil fertility.
