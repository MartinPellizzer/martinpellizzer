Parkia truncata

Parkia truncata, commonly known as the African locust bean tree, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy yellow flowers. The tree produces pods containing edible seeds that are used in traditional cuisines. It also has a deep root system that helps prevent soil erosion.

Its flowers are pollinated by insects, and it plays an important role in local ecosystems.
