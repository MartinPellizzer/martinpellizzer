Polygonum truellum

Polygonum truellum, commonly known as redroot pigweed, is an annual herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, characterized by its distinctive nodes and simple, alternate leaves. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and fields, and can reach heights of up to 1 meter. Its small, greenish-white flowers appear in dense clusters, and it produces small, three-lobed seeds.

Polygonum truellum is often considered a weed but has historical uses in traditional medicine.
