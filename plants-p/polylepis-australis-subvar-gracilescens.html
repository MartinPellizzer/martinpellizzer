Polylepis australis subvar. gracilescens

Polylepis australis subvar. gracilescens, commonly known as the slender polylepis, is a shrub or small tree native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its narrow, elongated leaves and dense, compact growth habit. The plant thrives in high-altitude environments, often found in cloud forests and paramo ecosystems. It produces small, fluffy flowers that are typically white or pale pink, and it is adapted to the cool, humid conditions of its native habitat.

Due to habitat loss and climate change, this subvariety is considered vulnerable and requires conservation efforts to ensure its survival.
