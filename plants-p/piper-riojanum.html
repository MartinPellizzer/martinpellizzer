Piper riojanum

Piper riojanum, commonly known as the Rioja pepper or the Amazonian pepper, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species such as black pepper. The plant is characterized by its slender, climbing vines and heart-shaped leaves. Its small, greenish-white flowers are followed by small, round berries that turn red when mature.

The berries are often used in traditional medicine and as a spice due to their pungent flavor.
