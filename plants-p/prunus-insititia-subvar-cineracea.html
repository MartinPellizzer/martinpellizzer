Prunus insititia subvar. cineracea

Prunus insititia subvar. cineracea, commonly known as the black-cherry or blackthorn, is a shrub or small tree native to North America.

It is characterized by its dark, often thorned branches and small, dark purple berries that ripen in late summer. The leaves are typically oval-shaped with serrated edges and a glossy texture. The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or open fields.
