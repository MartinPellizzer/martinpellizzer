Paspalum palmeri

Paspalum palmeri, commonly known as Palmer's grass, is a perennial grass species native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides.

Due to its hardiness and low maintenance requirements, it is sometimes used in landscaping and erosion control in dry regions.
