Prunus avium var. pendula

Prunus avium var. pendula, commonly known as the weeping cherry or weeping almond, is a cultivar of the sweet cherry tree.

It is characterized by its graceful, cascading branches that drape downward, creating a distinctive umbrella-like shape. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers in early spring, followed by edible pinkish-red cherries in late summer. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil and full sun for optimal growth.

This ornamental tree is widely cultivated for its aesthetic appeal and seasonal beauty.
