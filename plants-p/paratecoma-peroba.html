Paratecoma peroba

Paratecoma peroba, commonly known as Brazilian rosewood, is a tropical tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its dense, dark-colored wood. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, yellowish flowers. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in rainforests.

The tree is valued for its hard, durable wood, which is used in high-quality musical instruments and furniture.
