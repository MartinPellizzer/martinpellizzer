Phlox stansburyi subsp. eustansburyi

Phlox stansburyi subsp.

eustansburyi, commonly known as the Eustansbury phlox, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States. It is characterized by its erect stems and clusters of showy, pink to lavender flowers that bloom in the spring.

The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun. Its foliage consists of narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
