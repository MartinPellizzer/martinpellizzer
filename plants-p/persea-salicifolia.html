Persea salicifolia

Persea salicifolia, commonly known as the Mexican persimmon, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central America and parts of Mexico.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves, which are glossy and emit a mild fragrance. The tree produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by yellow-orange berries resembling persimmons. Its fruit is edible, though typically consumed when fully ripe, and it has a sweet, sometimes astringent flavor.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its fruit in warm climates.
