Penstemon wardii

Penstemon wardii, commonly known as Ward's beardtongue, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and spikes of showy, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to purple. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in mountainous regions. It produces small, oval-shaped seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Penstemon wardii is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in dry, well-drained conditions.
