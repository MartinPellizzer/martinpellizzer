Putterlickia saxatilis

Putterlickia saxatilis, commonly known as the rock spurge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to rocky and arid environments.

It typically grows in dry, sunny areas with well-drained soils, often found in mountainous regions. The plant features succulent, fleshy leaves that help it retain moisture in harsh conditions. Its flowers are small and yellow, often clustered in racemes, and bloom in the spring.

Putterlickia saxatilis is adapted to withstand drought and temperature extremes, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
