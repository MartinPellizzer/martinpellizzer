Populus simonii

Populus simonii, commonly known as the Chinese poplar, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It belongs to the Salicaceae family and is characterized by its slender, often multi-trunked form. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers in catkins, which are followed by oval, fuzzy seeds. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a serrated margin, typically turning yellow in autumn.

Populus simonii is adapted to a variety of soil types and is often found in mountainous regions.
