Peperomia marchionensis

Peperomia marchionensis, commonly known as the Brazilian peperomia, is a compact, evergreen perennial plant native to Brazil.

It features thick, waxy leaves that are typically oval or lance-shaped, with a deep green color and prominent veins. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually greenish-white in color, which appear in clusters during the growing season. It thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Peperomia marchionensis is relatively low-maintenance and drought-tolerant, requiring minimal water and pruning.
