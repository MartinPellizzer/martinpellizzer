Petunia sellowiana

Petunia sellowiana, commonly known as the Sellow's petunia, is a species native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a herbaceous annual plant characterized by its trailing growth habit and vibrant, showy flowers. The flowers are typically blue or violet, with five petals and a distinctive shape that resembles a funnel. The plant thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking colorful, low-maintenance ornamental plants.

Its botanical features make it both aesthetically pleasing and ecologically valuable in native habitats.
