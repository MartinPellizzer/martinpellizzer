Plectranthus verticillatus

Plectranthus verticillatus, commonly known as the purple heart plant, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its striking violet to purple tubular flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has opposite, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a velvety texture. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for gardens and containers.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental value make it a favored ornamental plant in both tropical and temperate climates.
