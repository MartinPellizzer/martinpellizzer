Phlomis spectabilis

Phlomis spectabilis, commonly known as the showy blazingstar, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, upright growth habit and vibrant, showy flowers. The plant features narrow, gray-green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and its flowers are typically pink to purple in color, appearing in dense clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

Phlomis spectabilis is also valued for its ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
