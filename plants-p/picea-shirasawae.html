Picea shirasawae

Picea shirasawae, commonly known as the Japanese white pine, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to Japan.

It belongs to the family Pinaceae and is characterized by its slender, conical shape and needle-like leaves. The tree produces small, cone-like structures that contain its seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in mountainous regions.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
