Polemonium acutiflorum f. lacteum

Polemonium acutiflorum f. lacteum, commonly known as the white-flowered Jacob's ladder, is a hybrid form of the native woodland plant Polemonium acutiflorum.

It is characterized by its pale to white flowers, which contrast with the deep green foliage. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a zigzag pattern along the stem.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in woodland gardens.
