Pentadenia hypocyrtantha

Pentadenia hypocyrtantha, commonly known as the narrow-leafed ironwood, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the family Rutaceae and is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its hard, dense wood has been used traditionally by Indigenous Australians for tool-making and as a source of fuel.
