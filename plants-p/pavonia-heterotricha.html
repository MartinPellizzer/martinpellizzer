Pavonia heterotricha

Pavonia heterotricha, commonly known as the redroot false mallow, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant features showy, pink to red bract-like flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a hairy texture, while the stems are often reddish in color.

Pavonia heterotricha is often found in moist, sunny habitats such as stream banks and woodlands.
