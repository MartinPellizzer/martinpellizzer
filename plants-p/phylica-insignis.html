Phylica insignis

Phylica insignis, commonly known as the Natal flame bush, is a shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and striking clusters of red, flame-like flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has small, leathery leaves that are arranged in whorls around the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for coastal gardens.

Phylica insignis is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract birds and insects.
