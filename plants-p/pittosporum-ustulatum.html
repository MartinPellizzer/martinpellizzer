Pittosporum ustulatum

Pittosporum ustulatum, commonly known as the flame tree, is a shrub or small tree native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its distinctive, bright red or orange flowers that bloom in spring, adding vibrant color to the landscape. The plant has glossy, dark green leaves that are elliptical in shape and often have a slightly serrated edge. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

The fruit is a small, woody capsule that splits open to release seeds, contributing to its ecological role in native habitats.
