Potentilla wimanniana silesiaca

Potentilla wimanniana silesiaca, commonly known as the Silesian cinquefoil, is a perennial shrub native to Central Europe.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and dense foliage. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are typically trifoliate, with three oval-shaped leaflets.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or open woodland habitats.
