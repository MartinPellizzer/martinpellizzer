Piper pullibaccum

Piper pullibaccum, commonly known as the fragrant pepper vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species. The plant is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and small, clustered flowers that develop into peppercorn-like berries. Its vines can grow up to several meters long, often climbing trees or shrubs in its natural habitat.

The berries are used in traditional medicine and as a spice, similar to black pepper.
