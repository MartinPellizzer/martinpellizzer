Phreatia acuminata

Phreatia acuminata, commonly known as the acuminata orchid, is a tropical epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its long, narrow leaves and clustered inflorescences. The plant typically grows on tree trunks or branches in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are usually white or pale yellow with a distinctive, spurred lip.

Phreatia acuminata is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in greenhouses or as a houseplant.
