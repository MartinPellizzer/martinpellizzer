Phyteuma orbiculare subsp. fistulosum

Phyteuma orbiculare subsp.

fistulosum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved phyteuma, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe. It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of yellow to orange.

The leaves are lance-shaped and often have a hairy texture, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
