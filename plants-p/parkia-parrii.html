Parkia parrii

Parkia parrii, commonly known as the white flame tree, is a tropical leguminous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and distinctive yellow-orange flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces long, flat pods that contain edible seeds, which are often used in traditional cuisine. Parkia parrii thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Its growth habit makes it a popular choice for shade trees in urban landscapes.
