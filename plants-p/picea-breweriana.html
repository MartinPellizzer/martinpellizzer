Picea breweriana

Picea breweriana, commonly known as Brewer's spruce, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It typically grows in moist, temperate rainforest environments, often reaching heights of 15 to 30 meters. The tree has slender, pyramid-shaped crowns and dark green, needle-like leaves that are arranged spirally along the branches. Its cones are small and cylindrical, containing seeds that are dispersed by wind.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays an important role in the ecological diversity of its habitat.
