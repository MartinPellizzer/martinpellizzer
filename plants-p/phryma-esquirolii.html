Phryma esquirolii

Phryma esquirolii, commonly known as the Mexican phryma, is a herbaceous plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the family Phrymaceae and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as along stream banks or in forest understories. Its flowers are usually pink or purple and bloom in late spring to early summer.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its distinctive foliage and floral display.
