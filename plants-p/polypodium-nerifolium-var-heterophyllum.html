Polypodium nerifolium var. heterophyllum

Polypodium nerifolium var. heterophyllum, commonly known as the variable holly fern, is a variant of the holly fern species native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, leathery fronds that are typically divided into narrow, lance-shaped segments. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical forests. Its leaves are evergreen and can grow up to several feet in length, making it a striking addition to shaded gardens.

This variety is noted for its variability in leaf shape and size, contributing to its common name.
