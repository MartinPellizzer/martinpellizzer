Ptilotrichum cappadocicum

Ptilotrichum cappadocicum, commonly known as the Cappadocian spurge, is a flowering plant native to Turkey and parts of the Middle East.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, woolly leaves and yellowish-green flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are often clustered in cyathia, a feature typical of the Euphorbiaceae family.

Ptilotrichum cappadocicum is also noted for its medicinal properties, traditionally used in herbal remedies.
