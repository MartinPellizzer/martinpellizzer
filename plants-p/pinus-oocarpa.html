Pinus oocarpa

Pinus oocarpa, commonly known as the Mexican pine, is a tall evergreen conifer native to Central America.

It is characterized by its long, slender needles that grow in bundles of five, and its cones are typically pendulous and woody. The tree can reach heights of up to 30 meters and has a straight, cylindrical trunk with a smooth, grayish bark. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry to semi-arid regions.

The species is valued for its timber and plays an important ecological role in its native habitats.
