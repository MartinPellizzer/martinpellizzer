Pelargonium × malvaceum

Pelargonium × malvaceum, commonly known as the velvetleaf geranium, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Geraniaceae family.

It is characterized by its broad, velvety leaves that are typically a deep green with a slight sheen. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from pink to red, often blooming in late spring to early summer. It is native to South Africa and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal.

The species is also noted for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its relatively low maintenance requirements.
