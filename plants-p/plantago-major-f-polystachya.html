Plantago major f. polystachya

Plantago major f. polystachya, commonly known as the common plantain, is a variant of the broadleaf plantain.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its rosette of broad, oval-shaped leaves that are hairy on the upper surface. The plant produces tall, branched flowering stems that bear numerous small, greenish flowers. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in lawns, gardens, and disturbed areas.

This plant is notable for its medicinal uses and its ability to tolerate urban environments.
