Passiflora nubicola

Passiflora nubicola, commonly known as the cloud passionflower, is a climbing vine native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, intricate flowers that feature a unique blend of colors and structures, including a crown of filaments and a central cluster of stamens. The plant produces edible, fleshy fruits that are typically yellow or orange in color. Its leaves are usually alternate and have a lobed or pinnate shape, often with a velvety texture.

Passiflora nubicola thrives in cool, humid environments and is often found in cloud forests or mountainous areas.
