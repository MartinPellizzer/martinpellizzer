Piper caducibracteum

Piper caducibracteum, commonly known as the Madagascar pepper plant, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Piper genus in the family Piperaceae, which includes many species of pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its slender, climbing vines and small, heart-shaped leaves. It produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are followed by small, fleshy berries.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated as a houseplant.
