Plantago hypolasia

Plantago hypolasia, commonly known as lesser plantain, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It features a basal rosette of broad, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in dense spikes, which are borne on slender stems. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and along stream banks.

Its medicinal properties have been traditionally used for treating wounds and skin irritations.
