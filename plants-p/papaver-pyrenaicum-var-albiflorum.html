Papaver pyrenaicum var. albiflorum

Papaver pyrenaicum var. albiflorum, commonly known as the white-flowered Pyrenean poppy, is a striking perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of the Iberian Peninsula.

It features striking white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer, contrasting with its dark green, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds, typical of the Papaver genus. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or alpine habitats.

Its ornamental value makes it a sought-after addition to rock gardens and wildflower displays.
