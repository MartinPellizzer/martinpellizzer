Piptomeris capitata

Piptomeris capitata, commonly known as the golden cup-moss, is a small, terrestrial liverwort found in moist, shaded environments.

It has a distinctive rosette-like growth pattern with dense, overlapping leaves that form a cup-shaped structure. The plant's leaves are typically bright yellow to orange in color, giving it its common name. It thrives in cool, humid climates and is often found on rocks or in forest floors.

Piptomeris capitata plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for microorganisms.
