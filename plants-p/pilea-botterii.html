Pilea botterii

Pilea botterii, commonly known as the Chinese money plant, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to China.

It features rosettes of glossy, round, fleshy leaves that resemble coins, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers that appear in the spring. It thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular houseplant.

Pilea botterii is known for its ease of care and ability to propagate through leaf cuttings.
