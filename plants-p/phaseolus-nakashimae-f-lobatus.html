Phaseolus nakashimae f. lobatus

Phaseolus nakashimae f. lobatus, commonly known as the lobate common bean, is a botanical variant of the common bean species.

It is characterized by its distinctive lobed leaves and small, pod-like fruits that contain edible seeds. This plant is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia and is often cultivated for its nutritional value. The leaves are typically trifoliate, with the central leaflet being larger and more elongated than the lateral ones.

It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
