Pterocarpus polyarthrus

Pterocarpus polyarthrus, commonly known as the African rosewood, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its dark, aromatic wood that is highly valued for its beauty and durability. The plant has bipinnate leaves and produces yellowish flowers that bloom in summer. Its fruit is a legume containing flat, winged seeds.

The tree is often found in dry savannas and is known for its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
