Peperomia guadaloupensis var. emarginulata

Peperomia guadaloupensis var. emarginulata, commonly known as the Guadaloupe peperomia, is a species native to the tropical regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its compact, low-growing habit and thick, fleshy leaves that are often variegated with silver or white markings. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or reddish in color. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires bright, indirect light to flourish.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ease of care, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.
