Plumbago capensis f. alba

Plumbago capensis f. alba, commonly known as white plumbago, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub characterized by its slender, branching stems and opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces clusters of small, white, tubular flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

The foliage remains evergreen, providing year-round interest in the landscape.
