Piper heterocarpum

Piper heterocarpum, commonly known as the broadleaf pepper or wild pepper, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Piperaceae, which includes many species of pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and small, greenish-white flowers that develop into berry-like fruits. Its fruits are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The plant's botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and ethnobotanical studies.
