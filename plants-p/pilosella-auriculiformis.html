Pilosella auriculiformis

Pilosella auriculiformis, commonly known as the auriculate pilosella, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and daisy-like appearance. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and grasslands. Its leaves are hairy and have auriculate (ear-shaped) bases, giving it its common name.

Pilosella auriculiformis produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the summer months.
