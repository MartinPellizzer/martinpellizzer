Pentanisia sericocarpa

Pentanisia sericocarpa, commonly known as the silky-fruited pentanisia, is a member of the Ericaceae family.

It is a small shrub characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and distinctive, woolly-textured fruits. The plant typically grows in moist, acidic soils in temperate regions, often in shaded forest understories. Its flowers are small, bell-shaped, and usually pink or white, appearing in clusters.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
