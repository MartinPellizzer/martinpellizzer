Pandanus echinodermops

Pandanus echinodermops, commonly known as the spiny pandanus, is a tropical plant native to parts of Southeast Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It is a member of the Pandanaceae family and is characterized by its long, narrow leaves that are often covered in small, spiny projections. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are used in traditional crafts and for thatching, while its fruits are edible and have a mild, sweet flavor.

The plant thrives in humid, coastal environments and is often found in mangrove or brackish water habitats.
