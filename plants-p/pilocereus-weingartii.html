Pilocereus weingartii

Pilocereus weingartii, commonly known as the Moon Cactus, is a cactus species native to South America.

It is characterized by its bright, colorful flowers that bloom at night, often in shades of yellow, orange, and red. The plant has a distinctive swollen, globular stem that resembles a ball, with spines covering its surface. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Its unique appearance and nocturnal flowering make it a popular ornamental plant among cactus enthusiasts.
