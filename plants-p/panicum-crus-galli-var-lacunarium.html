Panicum crus-galli var. lacunarium

Panicum crus-galli var. lacunarium, commonly known as red panicgrass, is a grass species native to warm temperate and tropical regions.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and inflorescences that consist of spikelets arranged in a panicle. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and waste areas. Its leaves are narrow and hairy, with a distinctive reddish tinge, especially under stress conditions.

It is a perennial grass that thrives in full sun and is adaptable to a range of soil types.
