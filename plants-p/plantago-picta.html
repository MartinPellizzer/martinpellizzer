Plantago picta

Plantago picta, commonly known as the painted plantain, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves that often display a striking pattern of silver or white markings. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-yellow in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and along stream banks.

The species is valued for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally in herbal remedies.
