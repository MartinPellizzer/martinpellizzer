Phyllanthus obliquus

Phyllanthus obliquus, commonly known as the African spurge, is a flowering plant native to Africa and parts of Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant has small, simple leaves that are usually opposite or alternate in arrangement. Its flowers are unisexual, with separate male and female flowers on the same plant.

The fruit is a small, dry capsule that contains several seeds.
