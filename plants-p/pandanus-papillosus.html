Pandanus papillosus

Pandanus papillosus, commonly known as the hairy pandanus, is a tropical plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It is a member of the Pandanaceae family and is characterized by its long, narrow leaves that are covered in fine, hair-like structures. The plant produces small, clustered yellowish flowers that are often used in traditional crafts and cooking. Its roots and leaves are also utilized in local cultures for their aromatic properties.

Pandanus papillosus thrives in coastal areas and is often found growing near the shoreline.
