Parodia carapariana

Parodia carapariana, commonly known as the "Cactus of the Desert," is a species of cactus native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems that are often covered in white, woolly hairs, which help to reduce water loss. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the warmer months, typically in spring or early summer. It is a slow-growing species that thrives in well-draining soil and full sun.

Due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements, it is a popular choice for desert gardens and cactus enthusiasts.
