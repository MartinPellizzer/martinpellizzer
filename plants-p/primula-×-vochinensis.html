Primula × vochinensis

Primula × vochinensis, commonly known as the hybrid primrose, is a perennial flowering plant belonging to the Primulaceae family.

It is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Primula vulgaris and Primula veris, combining characteristics of both parent species. The plant typically features showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, which bloom in early spring. Its foliage consists of broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white down.

Primula × vochinensis is well-suited for woodland gardens and prefers partial shade with moist, well-drained soil.
