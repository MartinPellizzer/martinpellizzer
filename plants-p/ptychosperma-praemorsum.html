Ptychosperma praemorsum

Ptychosperma praemorsum, commonly known as the black mangrove, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to coastal regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dark, almost black bark and its ability to thrive in saline and waterlogged soils. The plant has small, oval-shaped leaves that are alternate and often covered in a waxy coating. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by dark purple, berry-like fruits.

Ptychosperma praemorsum plays an important ecological role in stabilizing coastal ecosystems and providing habitat for various species.
