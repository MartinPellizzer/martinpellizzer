Polygonum setaceum var. tonsum

Polygonum setaceum var. tonsum, commonly known as the slender knotweed, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It features slender, upright stems that are often covered with fine, bristly hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in dense clusters, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy surface, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This species is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or along stream banks.
