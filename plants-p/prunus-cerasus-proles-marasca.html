Prunus cerasus proles marasca

Prunus cerasus proles marasca, commonly known as the marasca cherry, is a small deciduous tree native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It produces small, yellowish-red fruits that are highly tart and typically harvested in late summer. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and small, white flowers that bloom in early spring. The marasca cherry is closely related to other cherry species in the Prunus genus.

It is valued in horticulture for its unique flavor and historical use in the production of jams and liqueurs.
