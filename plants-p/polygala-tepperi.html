Polygala tepperi

Polygala tepperi, commonly known as the Cape Schanck polygala, is a flowering plant native to southern Australia.

It is a small, herbaceous perennial that typically grows in coastal heathlands and sandy soils. The plant produces delicate, spirally arranged flowers that are often pink or purple in color. Its leaves are narrow and linear, and it is known for its ornamental value in gardens.

Polygala tepperi is also valued for its ecological role in supporting local pollinators.
