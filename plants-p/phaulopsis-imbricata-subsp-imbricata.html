Phaulopsis imbricata subsp. imbricata

Phaulopsis imbricata subsp.

imbricata, commonly known as the red sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, clustered flowers that range in color from pink to red.

The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils, often in arid or semi-arid environments. Its leaves are linear and often covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

The species is known for its drought tolerance and ability to thrive in full sun.
