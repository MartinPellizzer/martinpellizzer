Paphiopedilum platyphyllum

Paphiopedilum platyphyllum, commonly known as the flat-leafed orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features a unique, often hairy stem and produces showy, spurred flowers in shades of yellow and green. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments and has a sympodial growth habit. Its leaves are broad and often have a waxy texture, aiding in water retention.

This orchid is prized for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to cultivate under suitable conditions.
