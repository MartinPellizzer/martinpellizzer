Procris quinquenervia

Procris quinquenervia, commonly known as the five-veined orchid, is a species of orchid native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its elegant, lance-shaped leaves and striking, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink and white. The plant has a complex root system that helps it thrive in moist, shaded environments. Its flowers are pollinated primarily by bees and other insects, contributing to its ecological significance.

Procris quinquenervia is often cultivated for its ornamental value in botanical gardens and private collections.
