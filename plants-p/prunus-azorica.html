Prunus azorica

Prunus azorica, commonly known as the Azorean cherry or cherry laurel, is a species of shrub or small tree native to the Azores.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy white or pink flowers. The plant produces small, edible cherries that are typically harvested in late summer. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Prunus azorica is also valued for its aromatic wood and has been used in traditional medicine.
