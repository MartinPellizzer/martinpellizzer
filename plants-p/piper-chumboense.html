Piper chumboense

Piper chumboense, commonly known as the Amazonian pepper or Chumbo pepper, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to the rainforests of South America, particularly Brazil, Peru, and Colombia. The plant is characterized by its climbing habit, with elongated stems that twine around supporting structures. Its leaves are typically ovate to elliptic in shape, with a glossy surface and a strong, pungent odor.

The fruit, a small, dark purple berry, contains the aromatic seeds used in traditional medicine and cuisine.
