Pogostemon benghalensis

Pogostemon benghalensis, commonly known as the Indian balm or Benghal balm, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, clustered flowers that bloom in shades of purple or pink. The plant is often cultivated for its medicinal properties, with the leaves used in traditional medicine to treat ailments such as inflammation and respiratory issues. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is commonly found in wetlands and forested areas.

The plant's essential oils are also used in aromatherapy and as a natural remedy for various health conditions.
