Ponthieva poitaei

Ponthieva poitaei, commonly known as the Madagascar star cactus, is a unique succulent native to Madagascar.

It features a short, thick stem with dense clusters of small, star-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of yellow and orange. The plant has spiny, fleshy leaves that help it store water, adapting to its arid environment. It thrives in full sun and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is often cultivated in controlled environments.
