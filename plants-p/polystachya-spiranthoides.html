Polystachya spiranthoides

Polystachya spiranthoides, commonly known as the spiral spike orchid, is a species of orchid native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, evergreen growth habit and produces clusters of fragrant, spiral-shaped inflorescences. The flowers are typically white or pale yellow, with a distinctive, elongated spur. It thrives in shaded, humid environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts for its unique floral structure.
