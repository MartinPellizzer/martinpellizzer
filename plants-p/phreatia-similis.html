Phreatia similis

Phreatia similis, commonly known as the purple-stemmed orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky areas. The plant has a distinctive violet or purple stem, which is often covered in a layer of fine, hairy tissue. Its leaves are broad and elliptical, with a leathery texture, and it produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink.

Phreatia similis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
