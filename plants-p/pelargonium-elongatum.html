Pelargonium elongatum

Pelargonium elongatum, commonly known as the tall geranium, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and elongated stems, which can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of pink, red, and white, typically blooming in spring and summer. Its leaves are usually lobed and have a slightly hairy texture, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Pelargonium elongatum is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant flowers and adaptability to various growing conditions.
