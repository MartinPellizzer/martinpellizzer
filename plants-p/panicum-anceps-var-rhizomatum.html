Panicum anceps var. rhizomatum

Panicum anceps var. rhizomatum, commonly known as purple lovegrass, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and dense, spike-like inflorescences that turn a striking purple hue as they mature. The plant has rhizomes, which allow it to spread vegetatively and form dense mats, making it effective for erosion control. Its foliage is narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green coloration that adds ornamental value to landscapes.

It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it a versatile choice for native plant gardens.
