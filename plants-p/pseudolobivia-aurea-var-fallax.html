Pseudolobivia aurea var. fallax

Pseudolobivia aurea var. fallax, commonly known as the Golden Pseudolobivia, is a rare and striking cactus native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems that are covered in dense, golden-yellow spines, giving it a distinctive and ornamental appearance. The plant produces vibrant orange to red flowers during the warmer months, which are often short-lived but highly attractive to pollinators. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it well-suited for xerophytic gardens.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is often cultivated in controlled environments to ensure its survival.
