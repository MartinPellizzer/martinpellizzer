Pasania hypoglauca

Pasania hypoglauca, commonly known as the blue-veined ivy, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and small, glossy, dark green leaves with prominent blue veins. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white or pale yellow flowers, which are often followed by orange-red berries. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often used in hedges or as a ground cover.

Pasania hypoglauca is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to tolerate a range of environmental conditions.
