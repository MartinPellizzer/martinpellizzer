Pandanus korthalsii var. bengkuluensis

Pandanus korthalsii var. bengkuluensis, commonly known as the Bengkulu pandanus, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and long, narrow leaves that are typically arranged in a spiral pattern. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences with yellowish-white flowers, which are followed by fibrous, spiky fruits. It thrives in wet, coastal environments and is often found in mangrove or swamp areas.

This variant is notable for its ornamental value and ecological importance in its native habitat.
