Polygonum palustre

Polygonum palustre, commonly known as marsh knotweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland habitats in North America.

It features erect, slender stems that are often reddish in color and bear small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters. The plant has opposite, heart-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in bogs, marshes, and along stream banks.

Polygonum palustre plays an important role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
