Pennisetum tristachyum var. galeottianum

Pennisetum tristachyum var. galeottianum, commonly known as the Galeottian lovegrass, is a perennial grass native to southern Africa.

It features slender, upright stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that have a bluish-green hue. The plant produces panicles of small, fluffy flowers that are typically pink or purplish in color. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to tolerate drought conditions.
