Pyrus montana var. rehderi

Pyrus montana var. rehderi, commonly known as the Rehder's mountain ash, is a variant of the mountain ash tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy clusters of white flowers that appear in early spring. The plant produces small, edible berries that are typically red or yellow when mature. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have serrated edges.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
