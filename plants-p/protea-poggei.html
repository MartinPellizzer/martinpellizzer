Protea poggei

Protea poggei, commonly known as the Mountain Protea, is a striking South African plant belonging to the Proteaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescences that resemble a cluster of flowers, though they are actually modified leaves called bracts. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils in the Western and Eastern Cape provinces. Its flowers come in a range of colors, including white, pink, and red, and are often pollinated by birds and insects.

Protea poggei is an important species in the local ecosystem and is also cultivated for its ornamental value.
