Pinanga densifolia

Pinanga densifolia, commonly known as the dense-leaf bamboo, is a species of flowering plant in the family Araceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including parts of Indonesia and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its clustered growth habit and possesses long, narrow, and often densely packed leaves. Its inflorescence consists of a spadix surrounded by a spathe, typical of the Araceae family.

Pinanga densifolia is often used in ornamental horticulture due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to various growing conditions.
