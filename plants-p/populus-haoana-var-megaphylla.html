Populus haoana var. megaphylla

Populus haoana var. megaphylla, commonly known as the giant trembling aspen, is a variant of the poplar tree characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves.

It is native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia, particularly in China, where it thrives in temperate climates. The plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to form dense stands through vegetative propagation. Its bark is smooth and gray, while its leaves are broad and often have a slightly serrated edge.

This species plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
