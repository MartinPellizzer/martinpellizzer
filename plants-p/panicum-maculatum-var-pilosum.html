Panicum maculatum var. pilosum

Panicum maculatum var. pilosum, commonly known as purpletop grass, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It features tall, upright stems that bear panicles of purple-tinted flowers, which are most prominent in late summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves with a bluish-green hue and a fine, silky texture. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, often found in prairie and meadow habitats.

This ornamental grass is valued for its attractive appearance and ability to provide habitat for wildlife.
