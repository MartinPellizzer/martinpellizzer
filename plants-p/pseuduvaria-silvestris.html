Pseuduvaria silvestris

Pseuduvaria silvestris, commonly known as the wild persimmon, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its alternate, simple, and often hairy leaves. Pseuduvaria silvestris is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
