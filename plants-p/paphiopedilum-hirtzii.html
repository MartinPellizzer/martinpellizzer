Paphiopedilum hirtzii

Paphiopedilum hirtzii, commonly known as the Hirtz's lady's slipper, is a rare and exquisite orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique, pouch-like lip that resembles a slipper, a distinctive feature among orchids. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often on tree trunks or rocks in the undergrowth of tropical forests. Its flowers are usually pale green to yellowish with intricate patterns, and it produces small, dark brown seeds.

Paphiopedilum hirtzii is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated by enthusiasts due to its rarity and striking appearance.
