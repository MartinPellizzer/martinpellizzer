Panicum capillare var. barbipulvinatum

Panicum capillare var. barbipulvinatum, commonly known as the barbed beardgrass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and distinctive barbed awns that give it its common name. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green coloration.

It is often used in ornamental gardening due to its unique appearance and drought tolerance.
