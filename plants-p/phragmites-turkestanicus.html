Phragmites turkestanicus

Phragmites turkestanicus, commonly known as the Turkestan reed, is a tall, perennial grass species native to Central Asia.

It typically grows in wetland environments such as marshes, lakes, and riverbanks, where it thrives in moist to water-saturated soils. The plant forms dense stands with tall, hollow stems that can reach heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are long, narrow, and often have a rough, fibrous texture.

The plant plays an important ecological role in stabilizing shorelines and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
