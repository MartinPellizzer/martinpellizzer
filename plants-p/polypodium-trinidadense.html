Polypodium trinidadense

Polypodium trinidadense, commonly known as the Trinidad fern, is a species of evergreen fern native to the Caribbean.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its delicate, feather-like fronds. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in tropical ecosystems.
