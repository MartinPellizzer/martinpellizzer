Polemonium campanuloides

Polemonium campanuloides, commonly known as the bellflower lungwort, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern North America.

It features basal rosettes of lance-shaped, bluish-green leaves with prominent veins and a fuzzy texture. The plant produces striking bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue, purple, or white, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, woodland habitats and is often found in shaded, well-drained soils.

Polemonium campanuloides is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
