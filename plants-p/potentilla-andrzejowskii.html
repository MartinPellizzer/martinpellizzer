Potentilla andrzejowskii

Potentilla andrzejowskii, commonly known as the Andrzejowski cinquefoil, is a perennial flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. Its foliage consists of trifoliate leaves, and it produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Potentilla andrzejowskii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardiness and low maintenance requirements.
