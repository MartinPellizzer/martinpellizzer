Poa pulchella

Poa pulchella, commonly known as the beautiful bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its fine, blue-green blades and forms dense, mat-like growth patterns. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowering stems bear small, spike-like clusters of flowers, which produce wind-pollinated seeds.

Poa pulchella is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in wetland habitats.
