Piper expolitum

Piper expolitum, commonly known as the black pepper vine, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species. The plant is characterized by its climbing habit, with opposite, ovate leaves and small, greenish-white flowers. Its fruit, a drupe, contains a single seed and is often used in traditional medicine.

The plant's botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
