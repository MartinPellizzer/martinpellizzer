Piper obliquum var. sprucei

Piper obliquum var. sprucei, commonly known as the Spruce's pepper vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its slender, twining stems and opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in spikes, followed by elongated, fleshy berries. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties.

The species is valued both for its ornamental appeal and its cultural significance in indigenous communities.
