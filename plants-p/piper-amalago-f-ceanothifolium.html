Piper amalago f. ceanothifolium

Piper amalago f. ceanothifolium, commonly known as the Ceanothifolium pepper plant, is a unique variant of the Piper amalago species.

It is characterized by its striking, broad, and lance-shaped leaves that resemble those of the ceanothus plant, giving it its specific epithet. The plant is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, and thrives in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties.

The plant's botanical interest lies in its morphological distinctiveness and potential pharmacological applications.
