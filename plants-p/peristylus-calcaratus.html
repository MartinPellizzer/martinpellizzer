Peristylus calcaratus

Peristylus calcaratus, commonly known as the rock cress, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a small annual herb that typically grows in rocky or calcareous soils. The plant features basal leaves that are pinnately lobed and small, white flowers that bloom in early spring. It is part of the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants.

Peristylus calcaratus is often found in dry, sunny habitats and is adapted to withstand harsh environmental conditions.
