Polygonum lapathifolium var. paui

Polygonum lapathifolium var. paui, commonly known as the purple false nettle, is a perennial herb native to parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, lance-shaped leaves with a reddish underside. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in ecological studies and traditional herbal medicine.
