Pandanus sinensis

Pandanus sinensis, commonly known as the Chinese screw pine, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, unbranched trunk and pinnate leaves that are often used in traditional crafts. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that develop into fibrous, spiny fruits. Its roots are fibrous and can be used to make ropes and mats.

The plant thrives in coastal areas and is often found in wetlands or saline soils.
