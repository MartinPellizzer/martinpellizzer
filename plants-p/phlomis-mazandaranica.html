Phlomis mazandaranica

Phlomis mazandaranica, commonly known as the Mazandaran germander, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Iran.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants known for their medicinal properties. The plant features upright stems covered in soft, hairy foliage and produces clusters of pink to purple flowers. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly serrated edge, while its flowers are typically arranged in dense, terminal spikes.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.
