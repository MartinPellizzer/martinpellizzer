Polygonum podlechii

Polygonum podlechii, commonly known as the Himalayan knotweed, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, which includes plants known for their distinctive nodes and often edible stems. The plant typically grows in moist, rocky habitats and can reach heights of up to 1 meter. Its leaves are lance-shaped with a reddish tint, and it produces small, greenish flowers in clustered inflorescences.

Polygonum podlechii is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
