Penstemon menziesii var. newberryi

Penstemon menziesii var. newberryi, commonly known as Newberry's beardtongue, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, tubular flowers that range in color from purple to pink. The plant typically thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves, and it produces seed capsules that disperse its seeds by wind.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to arid environments.
