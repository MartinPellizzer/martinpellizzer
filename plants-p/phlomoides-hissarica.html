Phlomoides hissarica

Phlomoides hissarica, commonly known as the Hissar false flax, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, rocky or sandy soils. The plant features slender stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. Its small, clustered flowers are usually pale blue or purple, blooming in summer.

It is often found in steppe and semi-desert environments.
