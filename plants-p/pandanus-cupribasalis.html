Pandanus cupribasalis

Pandanus cupribasalis, commonly known as the red pandanus, is a tropical plant native to the Indo-Pacific region.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and long, narrow leaves that are often tinged with red or pink hues. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and birds. Its fibrous leaves are used in traditional crafts and basket making.

The species is adapted to coastal and sandy environments, making it resilient to salt spray and wind.
