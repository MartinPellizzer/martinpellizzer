Paronychia brasiliana var. pubescens

Paronychia brasiliana var. pubescens, commonly known as the Brazilian sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a low-growing perennial shrub characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically thrives in well-drained sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in coastal or arid regions. Its flowers, which range from white to pink, bloom in spring and summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience in challenging environments.
