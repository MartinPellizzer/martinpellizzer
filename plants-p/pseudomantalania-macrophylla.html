Pseudomantalania macrophylla

Pseudomantalania macrophylla, commonly known as the giant pandanus, is a striking plant native to the island of Madagascar.

It is characterized by its large, sword-shaped leaves that can grow up to several meters in length. The plant produces large, clustered inflorescences with showy, yellowish flowers that attract pollinators. Its robust root system allows it to thrive in coastal and wetland environments.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological importance, it is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.
