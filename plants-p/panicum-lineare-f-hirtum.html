Panicum lineare f. hirtum

Panicum lineare f. hirtum, commonly known as hairy panicgrass, is a grass species characterized by its erect, cylindrical inflorescence and hairy foliage.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas and thrives in well-drained soils. The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters, with narrow, linear leaves that are densely covered in fine hairs. Its flowering period occurs during the warm season, producing small, spike-like clusters of pale to brownish flowers.

The species is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its distinctive appearance and adaptability to various environmental conditions.
