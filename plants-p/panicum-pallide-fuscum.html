Panicum pallide-fuscum

Panicum pallide-fuscum, commonly known as the pale-fusc panic grass, is a species of grass native to various parts of Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and produces narrow, pale to brownish spikelets. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as grasslands and agricultural fields. Its foliage is often covered in fine hairs, giving it a soft, textured appearance.

It is a low-maintenance plant that thrives in full sun and well-drained soils.
