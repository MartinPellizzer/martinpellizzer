Prunella grandiflora var. pyrenaica

Prunella grandiflora var. pyrenaica, commonly known as the Pyrenean dead-nettle, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of the Iberian Peninsula.

It features slender, hairy stems that often grow in dense clusters, and its leaves are lance-shaped with a distinctive purple underside. The plant produces small, two-lipped blue or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.

Prunella grandiflora var. pyrenaica is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
