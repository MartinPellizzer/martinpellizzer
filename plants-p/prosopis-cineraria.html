Prosopis cineraria

Prosopis cineraria, commonly known as the Indian date palm or tamarisk, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the arid and semi-arid regions of the Indian subcontinent.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are often grayish-green and covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by pods containing edible seeds. It is highly drought-tolerant and thrives in poor soils, making it an important species in desert ecosystems.

Prosopis cineraria is also valued for its role in preventing soil erosion and providing fodder for livestock.
