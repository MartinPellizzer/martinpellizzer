Papaver alpinum var. julicum

Papaver alpinum var. julicum, commonly known as the Alpine poppy, is a flowering plant native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers that bloom in the summer months. The plant typically grows in rocky or mountainous areas, where it thrives in well-drained soil and full sun. Its leaves are palmately lobed and covered in a dense layer of fine hairs.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens that mimic its natural habitat.
