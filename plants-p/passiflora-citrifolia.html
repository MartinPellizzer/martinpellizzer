Passiflora citrifolia

Passiflora citrifolia, commonly known as the yellow passionflower, is a climbing vine native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, complex flowers that feature five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments. The plant produces edible, yellow-orange fruits that are rich in vitamin C and have a tangy, citrus-like flavor. Its leaves are heart-shaped and emit a strong, aromatic scent.

Passiflora citrifolia is often cultivated for ornamental purposes and in some regions for its fruit.
