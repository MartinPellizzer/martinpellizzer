Podolepis aristata subsp. affinis

Podolepis aristata subsp.

affinis, commonly known as the slender podolepis, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Africa. It typically grows in open, rocky habitats at elevations ranging from 1,500 to 3,000 meters.

The plant features slender, upright stems with small, narrow leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of hair. Its flowers are arranged in dense, spike-like clusters and are usually white or pale green, giving it a subtle appearance.

The species is characterized by its unique leaf morphology and is of interest to botanists studying African flora.
