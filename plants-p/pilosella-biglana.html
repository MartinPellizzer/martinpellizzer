Pilosella biglana

Pilosella biglana, commonly known as the two-leaved groundsel, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and grasslands. It has basal leaves that are usually lobed or divided, and its flowers are yellow with a central disk.

Pilosella biglana is often found in disturbed soils and is considered a pioneer species in ecological succession.
