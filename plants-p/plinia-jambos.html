Plinia jambos

Plinia jambos, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or araçá, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is a member of the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and showy, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, red, edible fruits that are rich in vitamin C. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Plinia jambos is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of fruit in tropical regions.
