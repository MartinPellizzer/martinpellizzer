Prunus ampla

Prunus ampla, commonly known as the Chinese white cherry, is a deciduous tree native to China and parts of Korea.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and clusters of white, fragrant flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, red fruits that are edible but not widely cultivated for consumption. Prunus ampla is often grown for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.
