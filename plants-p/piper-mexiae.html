Piper mexiae

Piper mexiae, commonly known as the Mexican pepper plant, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to Mexico and is characterized by its small, green, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces tiny, yellowish-white flowers that are clustered in spikes, which are typically borne in the axils of the leaves. Piper mexiae is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has some traditional uses in herbal medicine.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial shade, making it suitable for tropical and subtropical climates.
