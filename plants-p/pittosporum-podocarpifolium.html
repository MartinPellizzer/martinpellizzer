Pittosporum podocarpifolium

Pittosporum podocarpifolium, commonly known as the silver-leaf pittosporum, is a shrub or small tree native to New Zealand.

It features lance-shaped leaves that are typically silver-white underneath, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are white or pale yellow, followed by rounded, berry-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and parks worldwide.
