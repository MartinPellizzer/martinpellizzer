Piper pavimentifolium

Piper pavimentifolium, commonly known as the pavement-leaf pepper, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that develop into elongated, cylindrical fruits. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in the understory of tropical forests.

The leaves and fruits are used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
