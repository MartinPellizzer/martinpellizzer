Penstemon hirsutus f. albiflorus

Penstemon hirsutus f. albiflorus, commonly known as the white-flowered tall beardtongue, is a cultivar of the tall beardtongue species native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its striking white to pale lavender flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 2 to 4 feet, featuring upright stems and lance-shaped leaves with a hairy texture. Its flowers are tubular and attract pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds.

This cultivar is well-suited for rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and low maintenance requirements.
