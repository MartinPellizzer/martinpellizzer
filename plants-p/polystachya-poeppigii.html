Polystachya poeppigii

Polystachya poeppigii, commonly known as the mountain orchid, is a species of orchid native to the Andes Mountains.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and large, showy flowers that are typically violet or purple in color. The plant produces a single inflorescence with numerous racemes, each bearing several flowers. It thrives in high-altitude environments with cool temperatures and well-drained soil.

Polystachya poeppigii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
