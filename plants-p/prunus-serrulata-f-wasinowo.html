Prunus serrulata f. wasinowo

Prunus serrulata f. wasinowo, commonly known as the Wasinowo cherry, is a compact, ornamental shrub or small tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its dense, rounded growth habit and its profuse display of pale pink to white flowers in early spring. The leaves are oval, serrated, and turn a striking red or orange in the fall. This form is a natural variant of the Japanese cherry, valued for its aesthetic appeal in gardens.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for smaller garden spaces.
