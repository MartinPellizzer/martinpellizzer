Phyllanthus meyerianus

Phyllanthus meyerianus, commonly known as the Meyer's phyllanthus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters. The plant features simple, opposite leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. Its small white or yellowish flowers are clustered in dense inflorescences, and it produces small, round fruits that contain a single seed.

The plant is often found in disturbed areas and is adapted to a variety of soil types.
