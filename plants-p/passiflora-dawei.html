Passiflora dawei

Passiflora dawei, commonly known as the Guangxi passionflower, is a climbing vine native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers with five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments. The plant produces edible berries that are typically yellow or orange when ripe. Its leaves are heart-shaped with a rough texture and a strong scent.

Passiflora dawei is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its vibrant floral display.
