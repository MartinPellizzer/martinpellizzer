Pleonotoma inaequalis

Pleonotoma inaequalis, commonly known as the unequal pleonotoma, is a species of leaf beetle belonging to the family Chrysomelidae.

It is characterized by its elongated, oval body and distinctive coloration, often featuring a metallic green or bronze sheen. The beetle's larvae feed on the leaves of specific host plants, primarily within the Asteraceae family. Its wings are typically hardened and covered in fine, sculptured patterns that aid in camouflage.

This species is often found in temperate regions and plays a role in the ecological dynamics of its habitat.
