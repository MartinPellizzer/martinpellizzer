Pankea

Pankea, commonly known as the golden cup plant, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has a distinctive cup-shaped inflorescence, which gives it its common name. It thrives in moist, sunny environments and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

Pankea is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinators.
