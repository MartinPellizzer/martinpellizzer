Phymatodes albosquamata

Phymatodes albosquamata, commonly known as the white-scaled phymatode, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy stems and leaves that are covered in a white, powdery coating. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the spring. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky soils.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptations, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists.
