Pinus douglasiana

Pinus douglasiana, commonly known as the Douglas fir, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to western North America.

It is characterized by its long, slender needles and distinctive cones that hang downward. The tree can grow quite tall, reaching heights of up to 100 meters, and has a conical or pyramidal shape. Its bark is thick and furrowed, providing protection against wildfires.

The Douglas fir is valued for its durable wood and plays an important role in forest ecosystems.
