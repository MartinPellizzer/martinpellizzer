Psiadia auriculata

Psiadia auriculata, commonly known as the auriculate psiadia, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its small, daisy-like flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces yellow-orange florets arranged in composite heads. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, often growing in rocky or coastal areas.

Psiadia auriculata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
