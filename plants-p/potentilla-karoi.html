Potentilla karoi

Potentilla karoi, commonly known as Karoi cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in mountainous regions. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three leaflets that are elliptical in shape.

Potentilla karoi is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and attractive appearance.
