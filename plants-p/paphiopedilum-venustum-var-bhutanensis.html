Paphiopedilum venustum var. bhutanensis

Paphiopedilum venustum var. bhutanensis, commonly known as the Bhutanese Venus Slipper, is a rare orchid native to the Himalayan regions of Bhutan.

It is characterized by its unique, slipper-shaped pouch that serves as a specialized structure for trapping pollinators. The plant typically grows as a terrestrial or epiphytic species, often found in shaded, humid forest environments. Its flowers are usually a mix of white and pink, with intricate patterns that aid in pollination.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated by enthusiasts due to its rarity and aesthetic appeal.
