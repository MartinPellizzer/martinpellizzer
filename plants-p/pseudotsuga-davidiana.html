Pseudotsuga davidiana

Pseudotsuga davidiana, commonly known as the David's hemlock, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the family Pinaceae and is characterized by its slender, conical shape and dark green, needle-like leaves. The tree produces small, cylindrical cones that contain seeds surrounded by a papery wing. It thrives in cool, moist climates and is often found in forested areas at high elevations.

The bark is thick and scaly, providing protection against environmental stressors.
