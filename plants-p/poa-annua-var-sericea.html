Poa annua var. sericea

Poa annua var. sericea, commonly known as silky bluegrass, is a cool-season grass characterized by its fine, silver-green foliage.

It is native to Europe and Asia but has been introduced to various parts of the world, including North America. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Its leaves are covered in a fine layer of silky hairs, which give it its common name.

It is often found in disturbed soils and is used in turfgrass mixes for its fine texture and durability.
