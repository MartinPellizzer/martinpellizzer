Pseudo-iris

Pseudo-iris, commonly known as the "false iris," is a genus of flowering plants in the family Iridaceae.

It is native to regions of the Northern Hemisphere, including parts of Europe, Asia, and North America. The plant typically features showy, violet or blue flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer. Its foliage consists of grass-like leaves, and it often grows in rocky or sandy soils.

Pseudo-iris species are valued for their ornamental appeal and are often cultivated in gardens for their vibrant blooms.
