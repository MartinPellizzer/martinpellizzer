Pinus pumilio var. cinnamomea

Pinus pumilio var. cinnamomea, commonly known as the cinnamon pine, is a variant of the whitebark pine native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive reddish-brown bark, which gives it its common name. The tree typically grows at high altitudes, often in rocky or alpine environments. Its cones are small and woody, containing seeds that are dispersed by wind.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in its native habitat.
