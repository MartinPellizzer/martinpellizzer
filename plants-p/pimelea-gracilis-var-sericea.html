Pimelea gracilis var. sericea

Pimelea gracilis var. sericea, commonly known as the silky geebung, is a low-growing shrub native to the south-western region of Western Australia.

It features narrow, linear leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, bright yellow flowers that are clustered in dense, spherical inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in heathlands and open woodlands.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
