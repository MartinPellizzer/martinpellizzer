Podanthum cichoriiforme

Podanthum cichoriiforme, commonly known as the silverleaf groundsel, is a low-growing perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It features basal rosettes of silver-gray, woolly leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in early spring, adding a subtle floral display. Its succulent-like leaves help it retain moisture in arid environments.

It is often used in rock gardens or as a ground cover due to its ornamental appearance and drought tolerance.
