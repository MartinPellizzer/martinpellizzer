Passerina laevigata

Passerina laevigata, commonly known as the smooth pasqueflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the family Ranunculaceae and is characterized by its showy, early spring blooms. The plant typically grows in open, rocky habitats and has a rosette of basal leaves that are smooth and lance-shaped. Its flowers are usually pink or purple, with five petals and numerous stamens.
