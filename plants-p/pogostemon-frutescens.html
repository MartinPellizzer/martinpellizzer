Pogostemon frutescens

Pogostemon frutescens, commonly known as Java mint, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It features square, hairy stems and opposite leaves that are usually lance-shaped and aromatic. The plant produces small, tubular flowers in clusters, typically violet or blue in color. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Java mint is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
