Pyrus domugledica

Pyrus domugledica, commonly known as the Hungarian whitebeam, is a deciduous tree native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its small, white to pale yellow flowers that appear in early spring. The tree has elliptical leaves that are typically dark green on top and lighter in color underneath. Its fruit is a small, round pome that ripens in late summer.

Pyrus domugledica is often found in forest margins and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
