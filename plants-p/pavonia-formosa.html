Pavonia formosa

Pavonia formosa, commonly known as the showy pavonia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet. The plant features striking, showy flowers that range in color from pink to red, often with a distinctive yellow center. Its leaves are ovate and slightly hairy, and it thrives in full sun to partial shade.

Pavonia formosa is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
