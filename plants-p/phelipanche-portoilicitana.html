Phelipanche portoilicitana

Phelipanche portoilicitana, commonly known as the Portuguese morning glory, is an annual herbaceous plant belonging to the Convolvulaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, trailing stems and heart-shaped leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically pink or purple, blooming in the morning. It is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in well-drained soils.

Due to its invasive potential, it is considered a weed in some agricultural areas.
