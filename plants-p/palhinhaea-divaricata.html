Palhinhaea divaricata

Palhinhaea divaricata, commonly known as the slender leaf vine, is a climbing plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its thin, wiry stems and small, alternate leaves that are often elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological studies and horticultural research.
