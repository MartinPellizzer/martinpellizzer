Plinia stictophylla

Plinia stictophylla, commonly known as the red-fruited myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its small, aromatic leaves and showy white or pink flowers. The plant produces small, red berries that are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Plinia stictophylla is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
