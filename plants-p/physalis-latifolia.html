Physalis latifolia

Physalis latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf huskfruit, is a perennial shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy calyces that enclose its small, yellow-orange berries. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. Its leaves are broad and ovate, often with a slightly hairy texture.

The fruit is edible when fully ripe and has a tangy, slightly sweet flavor.
