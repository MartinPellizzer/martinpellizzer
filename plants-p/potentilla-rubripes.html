Potentilla rubripes

Potentilla rubripes, commonly known as the redstem cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands. Its leaves are palmately divided into five lobes, and it produces small, red fruits that are dispersed by birds.

Potentilla rubripes is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in supporting local wildlife.
