Pilea urticella

Pilea urticella, commonly known as the stinging nettle plant, is a member of the Urticaceae family.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves covered in fine hairs that can cause a stinging sensation upon contact with the skin. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and is native to parts of Asia and Africa. It produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters.

Pilea urticella is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
