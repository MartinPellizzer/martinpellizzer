Phaseolus bertonii f. albus

Phaseolus bertonii f. albus, commonly known as the white horsebean, is a flowering plant species native to Central America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its pod-like fruits and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces white or pale yellow flowers. Its fruit is a cylindrical pod that contains several rounded seeds.

This variety is distinguished by its white seed coat, which sets it apart from other forms of Phaseolus bertonii.
