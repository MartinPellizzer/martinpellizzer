Papaver libanoticum

Papaver libanoticum, commonly known as the Lebanon poppy, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking red or orange petals and a prominent central stigma, typical of the Papaveraceae family. The plant produces capsules that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or arid environments.

Its ornamental value has made it a popular choice for garden cultivation.
