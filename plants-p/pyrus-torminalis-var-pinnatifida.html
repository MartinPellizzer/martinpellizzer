Pyrus torminalis var. pinnatifida

Pyrus torminalis var. pinnatifida, commonly known as the Chinese white pear, is a variant of the European pear species.

It is characterized by its finely serrated, pinnatifid leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in early spring, followed by fruits that ripen in late summer. The fruit is typically white or yellow, with a crisp, juicy texture and a mild, sweet flavor.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible fruit in temperate climates.
