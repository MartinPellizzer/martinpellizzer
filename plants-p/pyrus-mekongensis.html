Pyrus mekongensis

Pyrus mekongensis, commonly known as the Mekong pear, is a species of flowering plant in the family Rosaceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Cambodia, Laos, and Vietnam. The plant is characterized by its deciduous nature, producing small, yellowish-green flowers in the spring. Its fruit, a small pear-like berry, is edible but not widely cultivated for commercial purposes.

The species is of interest to botanists due to its unique adaptations to tropical and subtropical environments.
