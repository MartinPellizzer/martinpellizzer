Papaver lateritium

Papaver lateritium, commonly known as the rock poppy, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking red or orange petals and a distinctive, bulbous seed capsule. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils, thriving in well-drained environments. Its flowers bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been cultivated in gardens for its vibrant display.
