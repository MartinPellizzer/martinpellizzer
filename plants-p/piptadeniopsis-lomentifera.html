Piptadeniopsis lomentifera

Piptadeniopsis lomentifera, commonly known as the hairy pod senna, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with serrated edges and yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces elongated, hairy pods that contain lens-shaped seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and traditional medicine.
