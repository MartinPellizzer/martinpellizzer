Phoebe pubescens

Phoebe pubescens, commonly known as the hairy phoebe, is a small evergreen shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in the spring. The leaves are typically oval-shaped with a hairy texture, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to provide habitat for various wildlife species.
