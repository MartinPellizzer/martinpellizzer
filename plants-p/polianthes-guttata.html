Polianthes guttata

Polianthes guttata, commonly known as the spotted lily, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its striking, spotted leaves and showy, fragrant flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and prefers well-drained soil and full sun. Its foliage is often variegated with white or yellow spots, adding visual interest to gardens.

Polianthes guttata is a perennial that can be propagated through seeds or division, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and arid landscapes.
