Pavonia cochensis

Pavonia cochensis, commonly known as the scarlet pavonia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet. The plant features showy, bright red flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a rough texture, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Pavonia cochensis is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and ability to thrive in partial shade.
