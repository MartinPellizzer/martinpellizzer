Protea sulphurea

Protea sulphurea, commonly known as the yellow protea, is a striking South African plant belonging to the Proteaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, showy yellow flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant typically grows in coastal regions and is adapted to nutrient-poor soils, showcasing its resilience. Its inflorescence consists of a central cone surrounded by bracts that resemble petals.

Protea sulphurea plays an important role in local ecosystems and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
