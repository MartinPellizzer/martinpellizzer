Persicaria clarkei

Persicaria clarkei, commonly known as Clarke's knotweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, which includes plants such as buckwheat and sorrel. The plant is characterized by its upright stems, lance-shaped leaves, and small, clustered flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Persicaria clarkei is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
