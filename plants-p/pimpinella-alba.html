Pimpinella alba

Pimpinella alba, commonly known as white hogweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Apiaceae family.

It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, and it typically grows in moist, shaded environments. The plant features finely divided, fern-like leaves and produces small, white flowers arranged in umbrella-like clusters. Its seeds are enclosed in small, hairy fruits that mature in late summer.

Pimpinella alba is noted for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
