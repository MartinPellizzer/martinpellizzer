Picea jezoensis f. kamtchatkensis

Picea jezoensis f. kamtchatkensis, commonly known as the Kamchatka spruce, is a subspecies of the Japanese white pine.

It is a coniferous evergreen tree native to the coastal regions of the Kamchatka Peninsula in Russia. The tree is characterized by its slender, pyramidal shape and needle-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. It produces small, cone-like structures that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

This subspecies is adapted to cold, humid climates and is often found in mountainous and coastal areas.
