Portulaca lakshminarasimhaniana

Portulaca lakshminarasimhaniana, commonly known as the Lakshminarasimhan's moss rose, is a species of flowering plant in the family Portulacaceae.

It is native to the arid regions of southern India, particularly in Tamil Nadu. The plant is characterized by its fleshy, succulent leaves and vibrant, showy flowers that bloom in shades of red, yellow, and orange. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its unique appearance and ornamental value, it is increasingly cultivated in botanical gardens and as a decorative plant.
