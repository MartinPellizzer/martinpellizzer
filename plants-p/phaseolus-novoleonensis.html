Phaseolus novoleonensis

Phaseolus novoleonensis, commonly known as the wild kidney bean, is a species native to Central America and the Caribbean.

It is a climbing vine that produces small, green pods containing kidney-shaped seeds. The plant is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and hairy stems, typical of the Phaseolus genus. It thrives in warm, tropical climates and is often found in moist, shaded environments.

Due to its genetic diversity, it is of interest to botanists and breeders for potential agricultural use.
