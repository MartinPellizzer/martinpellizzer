Phyllostachys edulis f. rigida

Phyllostachys edulis f. rigida, commonly known as the stiff bamboo, is a variant of the bamboo species Phyllostachys edulis.

It is characterized by its rigid, upright growth habit and thick, sturdy culms. This plant is native to East Asia and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and structural strength. The leaves are typically narrow and pointed, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscaping.
