Psidium paraense

Psidium paraense, commonly known as the Brazilian guava, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically green when unripe and turn yellow or red when mature. The fruit has a sweet, aromatic flavor and is often used in culinary and medicinal applications.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and agricultural contexts.
