Pycnanthemum rosmarinoides

Pycnanthemum rosmarinoides, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its aromatic, silver-gray foliage and clusters of blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is known for its strong, herbaceous scent. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for native gardens.

Its nectar attracts pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to ecological biodiversity.
