Paullinia splendida f. chrysocarpa

Paullinia splendida f. chrysocarpa, commonly known as the golden fruit passionvine, is a botanical variant of the passionflower genus.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow fruits, which are distinct from the typical red or orange fruits of the species. The plant is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, and thrives in humid, warm climates. Its leaves are large, heart-shaped, and often have a glossy appearance.

The plant is valued not only for its ornamental qualities but also for its potential medicinal properties.
