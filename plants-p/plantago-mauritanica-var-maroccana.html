Plantago mauritanica var. maroccana

Plantago mauritanica var. maroccana, commonly known as the Moroccan plantain, is a perennial herb native to North Africa.

It is characterized by its rosette of broad, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in a spike, which are pollinated by wind. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils.

This variety is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional remedies for various ailments.
