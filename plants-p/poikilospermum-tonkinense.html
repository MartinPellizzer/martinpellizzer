Poikilospermum tonkinense

Poikilospermum tonkinense, commonly known as the Vietnamese starfruit or *Averrhoa carambola*, is a tropical plant belonging to the Oxalidaceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive five-pointed, star-shaped fruit, which is typically yellow when ripe. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible fruit.

The fruit is rich in vitamins and is widely consumed in Southeast Asia.
