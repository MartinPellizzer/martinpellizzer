Primula alpicola var. alba

Primula alpicola var. alba, commonly known as the alpine primrose, is a flowering plant native to high-altitude regions of the Alps.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and hairy leaves adapted to cold, windy environments. The flowers are typically white or pale yellow, with five petals and a showy appearance that attracts pollinators. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine meadows and slopes.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological role in mountain ecosystems.
