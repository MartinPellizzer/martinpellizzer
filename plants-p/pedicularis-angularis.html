Pedicularis angularis

Pedicularis angularis, commonly known as the angular monkeyflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its erect, sparsely branched stems and lanceolate, hairy leaves. The plant produces showy, spurred flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, rocky habitats and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments.

Pedicularis angularis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with well-drained soil and partial shade.
