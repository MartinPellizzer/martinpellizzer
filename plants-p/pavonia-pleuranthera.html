Pavonia pleuranthera

Pavonia pleuranthera, commonly known as the narrow-leaved pavonia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, often hairy stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, sandy soils and is often found in open woodlands or along stream banks. Its leaves are alternate, ovate to lanceolate, and have a distinctive pubescent surface.

The flowers are usually pink to red, with five petals, and the plant produces small, flat pods that contain several seeds.
