Phyllanthus hexadactylus

Phyllanthus hexadactylus, commonly known as the six-finger plant, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, palmately lobed leaves that resemble fingers, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clusters, followed by orange-red fruits that contain numerous seeds. It is often found in moist, shaded areas and is used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Its unique morphology and ecological role make it an interesting subject in botanical studies.
