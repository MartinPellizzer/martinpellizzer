Pinus sylvestris var. virgata

Pinus sylvestris var. virgata, commonly known as the silver fir, is a variant of the European silver fir.

It is a coniferous evergreen tree native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia. The tree has needle-like leaves that are typically silver-white underneath, giving it its common name. It produces cone-like structures that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often planted in gardens and parks.
