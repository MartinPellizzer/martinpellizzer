Psoralea acanthocarpa

Psoralea acanthocarpa, commonly known as the rough-seeded psoralea, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect, branching stems and small, pea-like flowers. The plant produces distinctive, woody pods that contain several small, kidney-shaped seeds. It typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is often found in grassland and savanna habitats.

Psoralea acanthocarpa is known for its ability to fix nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility in its native environment.
