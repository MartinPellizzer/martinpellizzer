Parthenocissus chinensis

Parthenocissus chinensis, commonly known as the Chinese ivy, is a climbing vine native to eastern Asia.

It features heart-shaped leaves that turn vibrant red in the fall, making it a popular ornamental plant. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are not particularly showy but are followed by red berries. Its tendrils allow it to climb and spread along walls, fences, and trellises.

Parthenocissus chinensis is well adapted to temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
