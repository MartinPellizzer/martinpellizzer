Pyrus sellii

Pyrus sellii, commonly known as the Kashmir pear, is a deciduous tree native to the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its small, ornamental flowers and edible fruits. The leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a smooth or slightly hairy surface. The fruit is typically small, round, and has a sweet, aromatic flavor.

It is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated for its fruit in high-altitude regions.
