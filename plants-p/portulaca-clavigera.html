Portulaca clavigera

Portulaca clavigera, commonly known as the spiked portulaca, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a succulent species with fleshy, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white wax. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the morning and close by midday. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for arid environments.

Its unique appearance and ability to store water in its leaves make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
