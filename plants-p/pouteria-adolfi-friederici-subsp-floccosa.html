Pouteria adolfi-friederici subsp. floccosa

Pouteria adolfi-friederici subsp.

floccosa, commonly known as the silky soursop, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America. It is characterized by its large, palmate leaves that are covered in a dense, silky white pubescence.

The plant produces large, fragrant, yellowish-green fruits with a rough, spiny exterior. Its flowers are typically unisexual, with separate male and female flowers on the same plant.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has potential uses in traditional medicine.
