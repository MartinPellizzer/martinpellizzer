Piper venulosissimum

Piper venulosissimum, commonly known as the Venulose Pepper Vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes numerous species of pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its slender, woody stems and opposite, ovate leaves with a glossy surface. Its small, greenish-white flowers are followed by elongated, fleshy fruits that turn red when mature.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in warm, humid climates.
