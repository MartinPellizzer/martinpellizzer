Prunus serrulata f. robusta

Prunus serrulata f. robusta, commonly known as the Japanese cherry blossom, is a variant of the Prunus serrulata species, which is native to East Asia.

This form is characterized by its larger, more robust flowers compared to the typical species, often displaying a striking pink or white coloration. It is a deciduous tree that produces showy blooms in early spring, making it a popular ornamental plant. The leaves are ovate with serrated edges, and the tree can grow up to 10 meters tall.

It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated in gardens and public spaces for its aesthetic value.
