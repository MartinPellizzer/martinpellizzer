Phyteuma michelii var. betonicifolium

Phyteuma michelii var. betonicifolium, commonly known as the betonicifolium phyteuma, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of bell-shaped, blue to purple flowers. The leaves are typically lanceolate and have a distinctive, somewhat hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is occasionally cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
