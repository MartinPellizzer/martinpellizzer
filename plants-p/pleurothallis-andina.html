Pleurothallis andina

Pleurothallis andina, commonly known as the Andean pleurothallis, is a species of orchid native to the cloud forests of the Andes.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and often has small, clustered inflorescences. The flowers are typically white or pale green, with intricate patterns that aid in pollination by specific insects. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and requires high humidity and well-draining soil.

This orchid is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical and subtropical gardens.
