Poa pratensis var. macrostachya

Poa pratensis var. macrostachya, commonly known as the tall meadow grass, is a variant of the common bentgrass.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and long, narrow leaves that are typically bluish-green in color. This grass species is native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia and is often found in meadows and grasslands. It produces inflorescences that are spike-like and contain small, clustered flowers.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and for erosion control.
