Panicum alabamense

Panicum alabamense, commonly known as Alabama panicgrass, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in moist to wet soils, often found in swampy areas and along riverbanks. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces panicles of small, spikelet-like flowers. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in wet conditions.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species in wetland ecosystems.
