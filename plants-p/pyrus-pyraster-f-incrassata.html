Pyrus pyraster f. incrassata

Pyrus pyraster f. incrassata, commonly known as the thick-leaved pear, is a variant of the European pear species.

It is characterized by its thick, leathery leaves and is native to southern Europe. The plant typically grows in woodland and scrub habitats, preferring well-drained soils. It produces small, yellowish flowers in early spring, followed by edible, sweet fruits.

This form is of interest to botanists and horticulturists due to its unique morphological traits.
