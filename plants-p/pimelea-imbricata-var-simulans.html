Pimelea imbricata var. simulans

Pimelea imbricata var. simulans, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bottlebrush, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its distinctive cylindrical flower spikes that are typically red or pink in color. The plant has narrow, linear leaves and is often found in heathland and coastal environments. It belongs to the family Myrtaceae and is known for its ornamental value in gardens.

The species is relatively drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils.
