Piptomeris carduacea

Piptomeris carduacea, commonly known as the common burrweed, is an annual flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and alternate leaves. The plant produces small, spiny fruits that are adapted for wind dispersal, aiding in its spread across various habitats. It typically grows in disturbed soils and can be found in fields, roadsides, and along fences.

Its botanical features make it a common weed in agricultural and urban environments.
