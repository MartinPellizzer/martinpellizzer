Phyllanthus evrardii

Phyllanthus evrardii, commonly known as the African buttonwillow, is a small shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, rounded growth habit and small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white pubescence. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Phyllanthus evrardii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is occasionally cultivated in gardens for its compact form and flowering display.
