Potentilla recta subsp. leucotricha

Potentilla recta subsp.

leucotricha, commonly known as the white-haired cinquefoil, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its slender, often hairy stems and small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early summer.

The leaves are typically compound, consisting of five oval-shaped leaflets with a slightly serrated edge. This subspecies is adapted to rocky and well-drained soils, often growing in alpine or subalpine environments.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.
