Panicum fusiforme

Panicum fusiforme, commonly known as slender panicgrass, is a grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, cylindrical spikelets that are typically less than 2 cm in length. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in panicles. It thrives in moist to wet soil conditions and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

Panicum fusiforme is an important component of wetland ecosystems, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
