Platyspermum scapigerum

Platyspermum scapigerum, commonly known as the scarlet bower vine, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its climbing habit and produces clusters of bright red, tubular flowers that attract pollinators. The leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a waxy texture. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The plant is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
