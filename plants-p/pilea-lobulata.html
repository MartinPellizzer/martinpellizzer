Pilea lobulata

Pilea lobulata, commonly known as the African violet, is a compact evergreen perennial native to Tanzania.

It features glossy, dark green leaves with a slightly waxy texture and small, showy flowers that bloom in shades of violet, pink, and white. The plant thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot. It is popular as a houseplant due to its attractive foliage and ease of care.

Pilea lobulata is also known for its ability to propagate through leaf cuttings, making it a favored choice for indoor gardening.
