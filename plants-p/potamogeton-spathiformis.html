Potamogeton spathiformis

Potamogeton spathiformis, commonly known as the broad-leaved pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in North America.

It has long, ribbon-like leaves that are typically green and slightly hairy on the underside. The plant produces small, green flowers that are arranged in spikes, and it reproduces both sexually and vegetatively through runners. It plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.

Potamogeton spathiformis is often found in shallow, slow-moving waters such as ponds and lakes.
