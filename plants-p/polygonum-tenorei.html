Polygonum tenorei

Polygonum tenorei, commonly known as the Tenerife knotweed, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is a member of the Polygonaceae family, which includes plants known for their distinctive bracts and small flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a reddish hue, while its flowers are clustered in dense, showy inflorescences.

Polygonum tenorei is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in its native habitat.
