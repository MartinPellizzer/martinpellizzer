Polystichum montevidense f. genuina

Polystichum montevidense f. genuina, commonly known as the Montevidense fern, is a variant of the common holly fern.

It is a deciduous plant native to South America, specifically found in regions of Uruguay and southern Brazil. The plant features dark green, leathery fronds that are deeply divided, giving it a distinctive, elegant appearance. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often used in ornamental horticulture.

Its botanical significance lies in its unique morphology and ecological adaptations.
