Pentaschistis aurea subsp. pilosogluma

Pentaschistis aurea subsp.

pilosogluma, commonly known as the hairy-stemmed golden bell, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its yellow-orange flowers that bloom in clusters, often resembling bells, hence its common name.

The plant has hairy stems and leaves that are typically oval in shape. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, shaded environments.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for horticultural gardens and landscape design.
