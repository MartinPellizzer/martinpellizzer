Piper teapense

Piper teapense, commonly known as the tea pepper or Indian pepper, is a climbing shrub native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many species of pepper plants. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are followed by berries containing aromatic seeds. Its leaves are typically ovate with a slightly wavy margin and a glossy surface.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
