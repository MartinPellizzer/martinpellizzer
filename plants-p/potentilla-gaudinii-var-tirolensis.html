Potentilla gaudinii var. tirolensis

Potentilla gaudinii var. tirolensis, commonly known as the Alpine cinquefoil, is a low-growing perennial shrub native to the alpine regions of Central Europe.

It features small, yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has compound leaves with five leaflets, which are typically covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments.

Its botanical characteristics make it a hardy and resilient plant suited to harsh mountain climates.
