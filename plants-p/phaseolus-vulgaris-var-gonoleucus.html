Phaseolus vulgaris var. gonoleucus

Phaseolus vulgaris var. gonoleucus, commonly known as the black bean.

This variety is characterized by its dark, almost black seeds that are typically oval in shape. It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is native to the Americas. The plant produces compound leaves and is often cultivated for its high-protein seeds.

It is valued for its nutritional content and is used in various culinary traditions around the world.
