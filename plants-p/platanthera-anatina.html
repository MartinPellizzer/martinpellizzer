Platanthera anatina

Platanthera anatina, commonly known as the narrow-leaved twayblade, is a perennial orchid native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant is typically found in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and wet meadows. It has a symbiotic relationship with mycorrhizal fungi, which aid in nutrient uptake.

Platanthera anatina is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is known for its delicate appearance and ecological significance.
