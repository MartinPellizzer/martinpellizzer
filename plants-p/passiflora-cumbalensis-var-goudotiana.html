Passiflora cumbalensis var. goudotiana

Passiflora cumbalensis var. goudotiana, commonly known as the Goudot's passionflower, is a species native to South America, particularly Colombia.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers with five prominent sepals and a crown of filaments resembling a crown. The plant typically grows as a climbing vine with heart-shaped leaves and produces edible, red berries. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in humid, shaded environments.

The botanical features of this variety make it a valuable subject for both ornamental gardening and botanical research.
