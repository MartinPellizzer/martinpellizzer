Poterium sanguisorba var. spachianum

Poterium sanguisorba var. spachianum, commonly known as red dock, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, characterized by its distinctive arrow-shaped leaves and clusters of small, reddish flowers. The plant is known for its striking crimson stems and foliage, which are often used in ornamental gardening. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fields.

Its historical use in traditional medicine and as a dye source adds to its botanical significance.
