Paphiopedilum villosum f. aureum

Paphiopedilum villosum f. aureum, commonly known as the golden velvet orchid, is a rare and striking hybrid within the Paphiopedilum genus.

It is characterized by its dense, velvety foliage and showy, golden-yellow flowers with a distinctive lip. The plant is native to Southeast Asia, particularly Thailand and Malaysia, where it thrives in humid, shaded environments. It prefers well-draining, acidic soil and requires moderate to high humidity for optimal growth.

Due to its rarity and ornamental value, it is often cultivated in greenhouses and botanical gardens.
