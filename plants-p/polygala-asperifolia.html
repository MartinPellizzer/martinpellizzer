Polygala asperifolia

Polygala asperifolia, commonly known as the roughleaf milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, delicate flowers that typically range in color from white to pale pink. The plant has small, hairy leaves that help reduce water loss in its dry habitat. It produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Polygala asperifolia is often found in rocky soils and is adapted to survive in low-nutrient environments.
