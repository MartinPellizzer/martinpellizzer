Persea macrophylla

Persea macrophylla, commonly known as the Mexican mahogany or avocado tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central America and Mexico.

It is characterized by its large, leathery, elliptical leaves that are glossy and dark green on top. The tree produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or greenish-yellow in color. Its fruit is a large, pear-shaped berry with a rough, green exterior and a creamy, nutty flesh containing a single large seed.

The plant is valued for its ornamental beauty, timber, and the edible fruit it produces.
