Phyteuma spicatum var. halleri

Phyteuma spicatum var. halleri, commonly known as the Haller's phyteuma, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features upright, branched stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, often violet or blue-purple flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically hairy and arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in rocky or alpine environments.

This variant is notable for its ornamental value and is cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
