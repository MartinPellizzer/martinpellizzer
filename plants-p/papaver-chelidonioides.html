Papaver chelidonioides

Papaver chelidonioides, commonly known as the yellow poppy, is a flowering plant native to central and eastern Europe.

It is characterized by its bright yellow petals and a distinctive, rounded seed pod that splits open when mature. The plant typically grows in meadows and woodland edges, preferring well-drained soil. It produces numerous small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in early summer.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
