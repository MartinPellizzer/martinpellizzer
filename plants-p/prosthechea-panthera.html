Prosthechea panthera

Prosthechea panthera, commonly known as the panther orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, vividly colored flowers that resemble the pattern of a panther's coat, giving it its common name. The plant produces aerial pseudobulbs and linear leaves, typical of many orchid species. Its flowers are borne on elongated inflorescences and are often fragrant, attracting various pollinators.

Prosthechea panthera is appreciated for its ornamental value and is cultivated by orchid enthusiasts for its unique appearance.
