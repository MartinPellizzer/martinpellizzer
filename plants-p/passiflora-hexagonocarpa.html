Passiflora hexagonocarpa

Passiflora hexagonocarpa, commonly known as the hexagon leaf passionflower, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive hexagonal-shaped leaves and showy, fragrant flowers that resemble a crown. The plant produces edible, orange-yellow berries that are often consumed raw or used in culinary applications. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value and unique botanical features, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and as a decorative plant.
