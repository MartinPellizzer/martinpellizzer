Phyllanthus antillanus var. pedicellaris

Phyllanthus antillanus var. pedicellaris, commonly known as the Jamaican star gooseberry, is a shrub native to the Caribbean.

It is characterized by its small, yellowish-green berries that are often clustered in star-like formations. The plant has small, elliptical leaves with a slightly waxy texture. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in coastal areas and disturbed soils.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine.
