Poa serpentum

Poa serpentum, commonly known as the snake grass, is a species of grass native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, dark green blades that often form dense, low-growing mats. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an important component of ecological systems in its native habitats.
