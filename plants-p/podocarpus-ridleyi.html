Podocarpus ridleyi

Podocarpus ridleyi, commonly known as the Malayan podocarp, is a slow-growing evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Podocarpaceae family and is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and small, cone-like fruits. The tree typically reaches heights of up to 30 meters and has a straight, cylindrical trunk with smooth, grey bark. Its leaves are leathery and arranged in a spiral pattern along the branches, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Podocarpus ridleyi is often cultivated for its aesthetic value and is used in landscaping and reforestation efforts.
