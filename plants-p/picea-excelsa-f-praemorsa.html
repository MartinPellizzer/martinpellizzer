Picea excelsa f. praemorsa

Picea excelsa f. praemorsa, commonly known as the giant white spruce, is a rare and distinctive variant of the white spruce tree.

It is characterized by its exceptionally tall stature, often reaching heights of over 60 meters, making it one of the tallest conifers in the world. The foliage is dense and needle-like, with a silvery-blue hue that gives it a striking appearance. It thrives in cool, moist environments, typically in mountainous regions with well-drained soils.

This subspecies is notable for its ecological importance and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.
