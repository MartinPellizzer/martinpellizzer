Prunella × giraudiasii

Prunella × giraudiasii, commonly known as the hybrid selfheal, is a perennial flowering plant belonging to the mint family, Lamiaceae.

It is a natural hybrid between Prunella vulgaris (common selfheal) and Prunella grandiflora (large-flowered selfheal). The plant typically grows to a height of 20–60 cm, featuring square stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are usually blue to purple, arranged in whorls along the stem, and bloom from late spring to early autumn.

Prunella × giraudiasii is often found in disturbed soils and open habitats, such as roadsides and meadows.
