Prunus catalaunensis

Prunus catalaunensis, commonly known as the Caucasian cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers in spring, which are followed by edible drupes that are typically red or yellow when mature. It prefers well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous areas.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has some historical use in traditional medicine.
