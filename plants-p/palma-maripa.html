Palma maripa

Palma maripa, commonly known as the pata de vaca, is a tropical plant native to South America, particularly Brazil and Peru.

It belongs to the Arecaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feather-like leaves. The plant thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in coastal areas and wetlands. Its trunk is used in traditional crafts, and its leaves are sometimes used for thatching.

The plant plays an important ecological role in its native habitat by providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
