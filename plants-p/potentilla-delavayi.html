Potentilla delavayi

Potentilla delavayi, commonly known as Delavay's cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit. The plant features small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are typically compound, consisting of five to seven oval-shaped leaflets.

Potentilla delavayi is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its hardiness and adaptability.
