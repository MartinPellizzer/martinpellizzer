Pilosella pseudofidalgoana subsp. vasconica

Pilosella pseudofidalgoana subsp.

vasconica, commonly known as the Vasconian goat's beard, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Iberian Peninsula. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and clusters of small, yellowish flower heads.

The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine pubescence, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
