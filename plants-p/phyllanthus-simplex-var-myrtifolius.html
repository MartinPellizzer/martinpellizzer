Phyllanthus simplex var. myrtifolius

Phyllanthus simplex var. myrtifolius, commonly known as the myrtle-leaved phyllanthus, is a flowering plant belonging to the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is characterized by its small, clustered flowers and distinctive, glossy, elliptical leaves that resemble those of myrtle. The plant is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa and is often found in moist, shaded environments. The species is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value due to its unique foliage and compact growth habit.
