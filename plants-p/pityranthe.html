Pityranthe

Pityranthe, commonly known as the redroot sunflower, is a genus of flowering plants in the sunflower family, Asteraceae.

These plants are native to the Americas and are characterized by their showy, daisy-like flowers and distinctive red or purple stems. Many species of Pityranthe are annuals, often found in disturbed soils and open habitats. They have small, alternate leaves and produce seeds in a composite flower head.

The genus includes several species, some of which are cultivated for their ornamental value.
