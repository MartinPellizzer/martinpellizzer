Panicum boivinii

Panicum boivinii, commonly known as Boivin's panicgrass, is a flowering plant native to Africa.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences that are typically green or brown in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
