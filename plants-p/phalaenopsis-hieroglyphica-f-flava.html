Phalaenopsis hieroglyphica f. flava

Phalaenopsis hieroglyphica f. flava, commonly known as the Yellow Phalaenopsis, is a hybrid orchid species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its elongated, lance-shaped leaves that are typically a deep green with a subtle yellowish tinge. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of yellow and white, often with a distinctive pattern resembling hieroglyphics, hence its scientific name. It thrives in warm, humid environments with bright, indirect light.

This variety is popular among orchid enthusiasts for its ornamental value and ease of cultivation.
