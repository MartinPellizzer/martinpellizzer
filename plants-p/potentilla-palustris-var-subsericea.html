Potentilla palustris var. subsericea

Potentilla palustris var. subsericea, commonly known as the swamp cinquefoil, is a low-growing perennial shrub native to wetland habitats in North America.

It features wiry, ascending stems that are often covered with fine, glandular hairs. The leaves are alternate, ovate to elliptic, and have a serrated margin with a distinctive pubescent underside. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers in late spring to early summer.

It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in bogs, marshes, and along the edges of ponds.
