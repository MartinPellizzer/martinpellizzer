Pinalia bambusifolia

Pinalia bambusifolia, commonly known as the bamboo fern, is a unique plant characterized by its delicate, feather-like fronds that resemble bamboo in structure.

It is a member of the Polypodiaceae family and is native to tropical regions of Asia. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often found in forest understories. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread slowly and form dense clusters.

The leaves are usually bi-colored, with a pale green upper surface and a darker underside, adding to its ornamental appeal.
