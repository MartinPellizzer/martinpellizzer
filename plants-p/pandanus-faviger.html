Pandanus faviger

Pandanus faviger, commonly known as the narrow-leaved pandanus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Pandanaceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its narrow, sword-shaped leaves that are often used in traditional crafts. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences with yellowish flowers, which are typically pollinated by insects. Its roots and leaves are sometimes used in local cultures for their aromatic properties.

The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal or wetland areas.
