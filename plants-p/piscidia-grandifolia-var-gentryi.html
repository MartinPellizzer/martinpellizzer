Piscidia grandifolia var. gentryi

Piscidia grandifolia var. gentryi, commonly known as Gentry's coral tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central America.

It is characterized by its distinctive coral-red flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. The plant has compound leaves with elongated leaflets and produces woody pods that contain seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in coastal or disturbed habitats.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for horticultural use in warm climates.
