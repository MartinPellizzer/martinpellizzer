Plantago oliganthos var. typica

Plantago oliganthos var. typica, commonly known as the narrow-leaved plantain, is a perennial herb native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in grasslands, meadows, and along roadsides. The plant features narrow, linear leaves that are arranged in a rosette, and it produces small, greenish flowers in a dense spike. Its medicinal properties have been historically used in traditional remedies for respiratory and skin conditions.

The species is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in various environmental conditions.
