Pollinia brevifolia

Pollinia brevifolia, commonly known as the short-leaved pollinia, is a unique plant with specialized floral structures.

It belongs to the orchid family and is characterized by its compact, clustered inflorescence. The plant produces pollinia, which are dry, powdery masses of pollen that are adapted for specific pollination mechanisms. Its leaves are short and broad, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically found in tropical and subtropical regions.
