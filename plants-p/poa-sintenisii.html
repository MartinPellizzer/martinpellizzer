Poa sintenisii

Poa sintenisii, commonly known as the Sintenis bentgrass, is a perennial grass species native to South America, particularly found in the Andes region.

It is characterized by its low-growing, tufted habit and fine, soft leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of hair. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers, typical of the Poaceae family. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in mountainous regions with well-drained soils.

Due to its limited distribution and unique morphology, Poa sintenisii is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
