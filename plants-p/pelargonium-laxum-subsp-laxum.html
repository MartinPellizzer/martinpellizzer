Pelargonium laxum subsp. laxum

Pelargonium laxum subsp.

laxum, commonly known as the slender geranium, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa. It features slender, upright stems and produces clusters of small, pink to red flowers.

The leaves are typically narrow and slightly hairy, with a distinctive, serrated margin. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable plant for rock gardens or containers.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various garden conditions.
