Polypodium cochleare

Polypodium cochleare, commonly known as the ear-shaped fern, is a species of epiphytic fern native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, leathery, and often curled fronds. The plant typically grows on trees or rocks, relying on atmospheric moisture for nourishment. Its fronds are usually dark green and have a unique, curling appearance that resembles a spiral or ear shape.

Polypodium cochleare thrives in humid environments and is often found in forest understories where it receives indirect sunlight.
