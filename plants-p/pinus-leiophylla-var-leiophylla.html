Pinus leiophylla var. leiophylla

Pinus leiophylla var. leiophylla, commonly known as the smoothleaf ponderosa pine, is a variant of the ponderosa pine native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its long, slender needles and a conical shape that becomes more rounded with age. The tree produces large, woody cones that release seeds when mature, contributing to its reproductive strategy. Its bark is thick and fissured, providing protection against wildfires.

This species is adapted to dry, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.
