Potentilla argentea subvar. magyarica

Potentilla argentea subvar. magyarica, commonly known as the Hungarian silver cinquefoil, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Europe.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and rocky areas.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive appearance.
