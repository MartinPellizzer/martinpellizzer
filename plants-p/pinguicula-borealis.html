Pinguicula borealis

Pinguicula borealis, commonly known as the northern butterwort, is a carnivorous plant native to Arctic and alpine regions.

It possesses specialized leaves that secrete a sticky mucilage to trap small insects. The plant's leaves are typically broad and fleshy, adapted to retain moisture in cold, nutrient-poor environments. It blooms with small, delicate flowers in shades of white or pale pink.

Pinguicula borealis plays a unique role in its ecosystem by supplementing its nutrient intake through insect capture.
