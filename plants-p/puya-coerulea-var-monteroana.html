Puya coerulea var. monteroana

Puya coerulea var. monteroana, commonly known as the blue puya, is a striking member of the bromeliad family native to the Andes of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, upright stem and striking blue-violet bracts that surround its flowering spikes. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers in shades of blue and purple, which attract pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, requiring minimal water once established.

This ornamental plant is valued for its unique appearance and adaptability to various garden environments.
