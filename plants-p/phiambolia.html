Phiambolia

Phiambolia, commonly known as the golden bellflower, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes nightshades, and is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant typically grows in shrubland and forest edges, preferring well-drained soils and full to partial sunlight. Phiambolia has small, oval-shaped leaves and produces berries that contain toxic compounds, making it important to handle with care.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens in temperate climates.
