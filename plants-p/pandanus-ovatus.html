Pandanus ovatus

Pandanus ovatus, commonly known as the screw pine, is a tropical plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its distinctive spiraling leaves that form a crown-like structure around the trunk. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are borne in dense clusters at the base of the leaves. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to grow in coastal and sandy soils.

The plant is often used in traditional crafts and is also valued for its ornamental appearance.
