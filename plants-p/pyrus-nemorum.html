Pyrus nemorum

Pyrus nemorum, commonly known as the common whitebeam, is a deciduous tree native to Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its small, white flowers that appear in early spring. The tree has a rounded crown and produces small, edible berries that are typically yellow or red. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have serrated edges, contributing to its ornamental value.

Pyrus nemorum is often found in woodlands and is adapted to a variety of soil types.
