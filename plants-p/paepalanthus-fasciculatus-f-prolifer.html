Paepalanthus fasciculatus f. prolifer

Paepalanthus fasciculatus f. prolifer, commonly known as the clustered fairy fan.

This plant is a succulent species native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its compact, rosette-forming growth habit. Its leaves are typically fleshy and arranged in a dense, fan-like pattern, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months.

It is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
