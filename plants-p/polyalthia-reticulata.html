Polyalthia reticulata

Polyalthia reticulata, commonly known as the red-flowered flame tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, bright red flowers that bloom in dense clusters, often giving the tree a fiery appearance. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape and are often covered with fine hairs. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The tree's bark is smooth and greyish, and it can grow up to 20 meters tall.
