Pipturus argenteus

Pipturus argenteus, commonly known as silverleaf tree or silverleaf acacia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree with a dense canopy and smooth, silvery-gray bark. The leaves are compound, consisting of several pairs of oval, leathery leaflets that give the plant its distinctive appearance. It produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, followed by flat, bean-like pods.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
