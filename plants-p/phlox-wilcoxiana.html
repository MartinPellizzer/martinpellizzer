Phlox wilcoxiana

Phlox wilcoxiana, commonly known as the Wilcox phlox, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its vibrant pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered inflorescences.

Phlox wilcoxiana is often found in desert scrub and chaparral habitats.
