Polygonum adoense

Polygonum adoense, commonly known as the redroot knotweed, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its reddish stems and heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Polygonum adoense is known for its rapid growth and ability to spread through rhizomes, making it a common invasive species in certain regions.
