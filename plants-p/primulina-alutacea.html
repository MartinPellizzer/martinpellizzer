Primulina alutacea

Primulina alutacea, commonly known as the silver ponytail plant, is a species of flowering plant native to the rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Gesneriaceae family and is characterized by its striking silver-gray foliage and cascading stems that bear small, tubular flowers. The leaves are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs, giving them a distinctive, almost metallic appearance. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires consistent moisture and well-draining soil.

This plant is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
