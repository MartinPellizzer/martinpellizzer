Penstemon shockleyi

Penstemon shockleyi, commonly known as Shockley's beardtongue, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is a perennial species that thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and open woodlands. The plant produces striking tubular flowers that are typically blue to purple in color, attracting pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it has a compact growth habit.

Penstemon shockleyi is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting native wildlife.
