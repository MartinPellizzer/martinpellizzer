Prunus × donarium f. senriko

Prunus × donarium f. senriko, commonly known as the Japanese apricot or the cherry plum, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its showy, single or semi-double flowers that bloom in spring, often in shades of pink or white. The plant typically grows as a small tree or shrub, with smooth, reddish bark and oval-shaped leaves that turn yellow in autumn. Its fruits are small, yellow plums that are edible but not commonly cultivated for commercial use.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often used in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
