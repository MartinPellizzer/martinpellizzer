Ptarmica japonica

Ptarmica japonica, commonly known as Japanese yarrow, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its finely divided, feathery leaves and clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. It is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.

Ptarmica japonica is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in partial shade.
