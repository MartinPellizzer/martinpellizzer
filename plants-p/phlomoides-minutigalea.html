Phlomoides minutigalea

Phlomoides minutigalea, commonly known as the dwarf phlomoides, is a small annual herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, such as grasslands and rocky soils. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, pale blue or purple flowers clustered in dense spikes. It is characterized by its compact size and adapted to survive in harsh, dry conditions.

Phlomoides minutigalea is often found in disturbed habitats and is considered a rare or endangered species in some regions.
