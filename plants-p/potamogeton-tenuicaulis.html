Potamogeton tenuicaulis

Potamogeton tenuicaulis, commonly known as narrow-stemmed pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It features long, slender, flexible stems that are often branched and bear small, oval leaves arranged in whorls. The plant produces tiny, inconspicuous flowers that are typically found in clusters near the water surface. It thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments such as lakes, ponds, and wetlands.

Potamogeton tenuicaulis plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
