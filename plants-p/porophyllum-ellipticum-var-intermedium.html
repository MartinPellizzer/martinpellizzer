Porophyllum ellipticum var. intermedium

Porophyllum ellipticum var. intermedium, commonly known as the narrowleaf blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils, and it produces small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in the summer months.

Its leaves are narrow and elliptical, with a rough, hairy texture, and the plant is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
