Polygonum tomentosum

Polygonum tomentosum, commonly known as the hairy knotweed, is a perennial herb native to Asia.

It is characterized by its stout, upright stems that are often covered in fine, woolly hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in late summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly rough texture.

Polygonum tomentosum is often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.
