Pommereulla royleana

Pommereulla royleana, commonly known as the Kashmir rose, is a perennial shrub native to the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its fragrant pink or white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has lance-shaped leaves and produces small, fleshy fruits that are edible. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in mountainous regions.

Due to its ornamental value and fragrance, it is cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
