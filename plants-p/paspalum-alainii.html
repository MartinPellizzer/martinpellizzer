Paspalum alainii

Paspalum alainii, commonly known as Alain's grass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along riverbanks. The plant has slender, tufted shoots and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue.

Paspalum alainii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in wetland environments.
