Phaseolus dalzellii

Phaseolus dalzellii, commonly known as the Dalzell's bean, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to Central and South America, particularly found in regions of Mexico and Central America. The plant is characterized by its climbing habit, with trifoliate leaves and small, yellowish-white flowers. Its pods contain edible seeds that are similar to common beans but are less widely cultivated.

This species is of interest to botanists and agricultural researchers due to its potential for genetic diversity and adaptability to various environmental conditions.
