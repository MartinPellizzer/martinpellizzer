Phaius pictus

Phaius pictus, commonly known as the painted orchid, is a striking tropical orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that display a vivid array of colors, including red, orange, and yellow, often with intricate patterns. The plant typically grows in wet, shaded environments, such as marshes and swampy forests. Its leaves are broad and strap-like, providing a lush green backdrop to its colorful blooms.

Phaius pictus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
