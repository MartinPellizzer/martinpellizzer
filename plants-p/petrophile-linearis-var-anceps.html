Petrophile linearis var. anceps

Petrophile linearis var. anceps, commonly known as the narrow-leaf bottlebrush, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the family Myrtaceae and is characterized by its distinctive cylindrical flower spikes. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in coastal or inland regions. It features narrow, linear leaves and produces vibrant red or pink flowers during the spring and summer seasons.
