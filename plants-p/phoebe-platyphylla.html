Phoebe platyphylla

Phoebe platyphylla, commonly known as the flat-leaf phoebe, is a species of flowering plant in the family Lauraceae.

It is native to the eastern United States, particularly found in the coastal regions of South Carolina and Georgia. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical in shape and have a smooth, waxy surface. It produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by small, dark blue berries.

The plant prefers moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetland or riparian habitats.
