Peperomia campinasana var. suboppositifolia

Peperomia campinasana var. suboppositifolia, commonly known as the Campinas peperomia, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to Brazil.

It features dense, dark green foliage with a velvety texture and lance-shaped leaves that are oppositely arranged along the stems. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers that emerge from the leaf axils, typically in the spring. It thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Its compact growth habit and attractive foliage make it a desirable ornamental plant.
