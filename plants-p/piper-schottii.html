Piper schottii

Piper schottii, commonly known as Schott's pepper or black pepper vine, is a climbing shrub native to tropical regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces small, dark purple berries that contain the aromatic seeds used as a spice. Its vines can grow up to several meters long, often climbing trees or other structures in its natural habitat.

The botanical features of Piper schottii make it an important species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
