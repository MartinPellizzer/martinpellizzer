Polygala scorpioides

Polygala scorpioides, commonly known as the scorpion's tail, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, spiky inflorescence that resembles a scorpion's tail, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, delicate flowers in shades of pink and white, which are clustered along the central spike. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Polygala scorpioides is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
