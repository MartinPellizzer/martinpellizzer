Packera pseudaurea

Packera pseudaurea, commonly known as the false golden aster, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant typically grows in moist meadows and open woodlands, preferring cool, temperate climates. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are small, yellowish, and clustered in a rounded head.

Packera pseudaurea plays a role in supporting local pollinators and contributes to the biodiversity of its native habitat.
