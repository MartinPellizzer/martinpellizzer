Protea brassicifolia

Protea brassicifolia, commonly known as the Cape protea, is a striking South African plant belonging to the Proteaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescence composed of numerous tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant is native to the fynbos biome, where it thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and full sun exposure. Its unique floral structure attracts a variety of pollinators, including birds and insects.

Protea brassicifolia is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and resilience.
