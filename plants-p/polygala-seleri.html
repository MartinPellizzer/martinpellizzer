Polygala seleri

Polygala seleri, commonly known as the Seler's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its slender, often branched stems and small, delicate flowers. The plant typically grows in grasslands and rocky areas, thriving in well-drained soils. Its flowers are usually yellow or greenish-yellow, with a distinctive structure that resembles a spur, aiding in pollination by insects.

Polygala seleri is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and adaptability to various environmental conditions.
