Pleioblastus gramineus

Pleioblastus gramineus, commonly known as the Japanese silver grass, is a species of grass native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-blue foliage that appears in dense, tufted clumps. The plant produces fluffy, white plumes in the fall, which contribute to its ornamental value. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

Due to its hardiness and low maintenance requirements, it is often used in rock gardens and as a border plant.
