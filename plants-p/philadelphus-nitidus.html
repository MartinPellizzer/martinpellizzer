Philadelphus nitidus

Philadelphus nitidus, commonly known as the glossy mock orange, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features opposite, ovate leaves that are typically glossy and dark green, with a slightly hairy underside. The plant produces fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers in clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Philadelphus nitidus is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
