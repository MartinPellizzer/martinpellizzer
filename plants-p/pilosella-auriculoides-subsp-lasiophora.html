Pilosella auriculoides subsp. lasiophora

Pilosella auriculoides subsp.

lasiophora, commonly known as the woolly mouse-ear, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa. It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, rocky soils and open habitats.

The plant features a basal rosette of silver-gray, woolly leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers in the spring. Its subspecies, lasiophora, is distinguished by its more densely hairy foliage compared to other varieties.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in harsh conditions.
