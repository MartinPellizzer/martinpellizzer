Prunus jamasakura subvar. glabra

Prunus jamasakura subvar. glabra, commonly known as the smooth-flowered Japanese cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its smooth, glabrous bark and clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a smooth margin, turning bright red in the fall. This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and parks.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sun exposure.
