Polygonum louci

Polygonum louci, commonly known as the Sierra polygonum, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in late summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along streambanks.

Polygonum louci is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local pollinators.
