Potentilla gageodoensis

Potentilla gageodoensis, commonly known as the Gageod's cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are compound, consisting of five oval-shaped leaflets, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Potentilla gageodoensis is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and aesthetic appeal.
