Ponerorchis kinoshitae

Ponerorchis kinoshitae, commonly known as the Kinoshita's ghost orchid, is a rare and enigmatic orchid species native to Japan.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, delicate flowers that resemble ghostly apparitions. The plant is epiphytic, growing on tree trunks or branches, and thrives in shaded, humid environments. Its small, white or pale pink flowers are often clustered in a loose inflorescence, and it has lance-shaped leaves with a waxy coating.

Due to its rarity and habitat specificity, it is considered a critically endangered species.
