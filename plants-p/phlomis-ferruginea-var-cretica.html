Phlomis ferruginea var. cretica

Phlomis ferruginea var. cretica, commonly known as the Cretan fireweed, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its erect, hairy stems and clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has lance-shaped, gray-green leaves with a rough texture, and it thrives in well-drained, rocky soils. It is often found in dry, sunny habitats such as coastal areas and limestone slopes.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to pollinators.
