Pleopeltis iteophylla

Pleopeltis iteophylla, commonly known as the velvet fern, is a species of fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its soft, velvety fronds that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and can reach lengths of up to 30 centimeters.

Pleopeltis iteophylla is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
