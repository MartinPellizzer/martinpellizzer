Ptelea cuspidata

Ptelea cuspidata, commonly known as the prickly ash or white ash, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are opposite and have serrated edges, and its small, yellowish-green flowers that appear in clusters. The plant produces small, dark red berries that are a food source for various wildlife. It is often found in moist, well-drained soils along stream banks and in woodland edges.

Botanically, it is part of the Rutaceae family, which includes citrus plants, and is known for its aromatic, sometimes prickly, foliage.
