Prunus armeniaca var. pendula

Prunus armeniaca var. pendula, commonly known as the weeping cherry or weeping almond, is a cultivar of the almond tree characterized by its cascading branches that drape downward.

It produces showy clusters of pink or white flowers in early spring, followed by small, edible fruits. The plant is native to the Middle East and has been cultivated for centuries for its ornamental value. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil and full sun.

Due to its delicate appearance, it is often planted in gardens and parks as a decorative feature.
