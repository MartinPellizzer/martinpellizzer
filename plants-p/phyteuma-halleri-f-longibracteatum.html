Phyteuma halleri f. longibracteatum

Phyteuma halleri f. longibracteatum, commonly known as the long-bract phyteuma, is a flowering plant belonging to the Campanulaceae family.

It is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pale blue to purple. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils in alpine or subalpine regions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while the bracts are elongated and prominent.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
