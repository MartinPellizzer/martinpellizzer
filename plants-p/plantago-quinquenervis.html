Plantago quinquenervis

Plantago quinquenervis, commonly known as the five-veined plantain, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Plantaginaceae and is characterized by its broad, oval-shaped leaves with five prominent veins. The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in a dense spike, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in a variety of habitats, including meadows, roadsides, and disturbed soils, and is often found in temperate climates.

The plant is known for its medicinal properties and has been used historically in traditional herbal remedies.
