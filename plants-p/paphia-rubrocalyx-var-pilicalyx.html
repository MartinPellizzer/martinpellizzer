Paphia rubrocalyx var. pilicalyx

Paphia rubrocalyx var. pilicalyx, commonly known as the red-flowered paphiopedilum, is a rare and striking orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its velvety, deep red petals and sepals, which contrast beautifully with its greenish-yellow lip. The plant typically grows as a terrestrial orchid, often found in shaded, humid forest environments. Its flowers bloom in late spring or early summer, producing a fragrant scent that attracts pollinators.

Due to its rarity and ornamental value, it is highly sought after by horticulturists and conservationists alike.
