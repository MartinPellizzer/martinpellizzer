Panicum lindheimeri subvar. pilosum

Panicum lindheimeri subvar. pilosum, commonly known as Lindheimer's panicgrass, is a perennial grass native to the southern United States.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves with a distinctive silvery-blue hue due to the fine, fuzzy hairs covering its foliage. The plant produces small, spikelike inflorescences that bear delicate, feather-like flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy ornamental plant.

Its unique appearance and adaptability make it a valuable addition to native plant gardens.
