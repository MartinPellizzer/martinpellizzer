Passiflora herthae

Passiflora herthae, commonly known as Herth's passionflower, is a climbing vine native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is characterized by its striking, intricate flowers that display a mix of blue, purple, and white hues, often with a crown-like structure at the center. The plant produces edible berries that are typically yellow or orange when ripe, containing a sweet, juicy pulp. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a rough texture, while its stems are woody and covered in fine hairs.

Passiflora herthae is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
