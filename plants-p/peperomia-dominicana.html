Peperomia dominicana

Peperomia dominicana, commonly known as the Dominican peperomia, is a compact, evergreen perennial plant native to the Caribbean island of Hispaniola.

It features thick, waxy leaves that are typically lance-shaped or ovate, often with a reddish or maroon hue on the underside. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually greenish-white in color, arranged in clusters along the stems. It thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Peperomia dominicana is relatively low-maintenance and drought-tolerant, suitable for both novice and experienced plant enthusiasts.
