Papaver clavigerum

Papaver clavigerum, commonly known as the spiked poppy, is a member of the Papaveraceae family.

It is characterized by its striking, crimson-red flowers with a distinctive, elongated style that resembles a spike. The plant typically grows in rocky or disturbed soils in mountainous regions. Its leaves are palmately lobed and covered in fine hairs.

The seeds are small, dry, and often dispersed by wind, contributing to its natural distribution.
