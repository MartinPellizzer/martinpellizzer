Plectranthus lilacinus

Plectranthus lilacinus, commonly known as the lilac plectranthus, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its aromatic, opposite leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to purple, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. It is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and fragrant properties.

Plectranthus lilacinus prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.
