Papaver holophyllum

Papaver holophyllum, commonly known as the Himalayan poppy, is a striking plant native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stem and vibrant red flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has narrow, elongated leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white hairs. Its flowers are typically solitary and have a distinctive, showy appearance with prominent stamens.

Papaver holophyllum is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking visual appeal.
