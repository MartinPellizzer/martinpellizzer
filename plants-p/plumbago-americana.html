Plumbago americana

Plumbago americana, commonly known as blue plumbago, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, featuring woody stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of bright blue or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Plumbago americana is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
