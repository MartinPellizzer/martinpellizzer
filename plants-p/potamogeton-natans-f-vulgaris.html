Potamogeton natans f. vulgaris

Potamogeton natans f. vulgaris, commonly known as the broadleaf pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater habitats across Europe, Asia, and North America.

It has long, flexible stems that grow horizontally along the bottom of lakes and ponds, often forming dense underwater vegetation. The plant produces small, green to yellowish flowers that are typically clustered in spikes, and it is characterized by its broad, elliptical leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. Potamogeton natans f. vulgaris plays a vital role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
