Plectranthus kameba

Plectranthus kameba, commonly known as the Kameba plant, is a species native to South Africa.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its square, hairy stems and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically violet or purple in color, arranged in clusters. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Plectranthus kameba is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in a garden setting.
