Passiflora chinensis

Passiflora chinensis, commonly known as Chinese passionflower, is a climbing vine native to China.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful flowers that display a blend of blue, purple, and white petals. The plant produces edible berries that are often used in traditional Chinese medicine. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a rough texture, while its stems are hairy and woody.

Passiflora chinensis is valued for its ornamental beauty and medicinal properties.
