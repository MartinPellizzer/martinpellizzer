Polygala vulgaris subsp. nicaeensis

Polygala vulgaris subsp.

nicaeensis, commonly known as the Nicaean milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is characterized by its slender, branching stems.

The plant produces small, pinkish-white flowers arranged in spicate inflorescences, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and often have a slightly hairy texture.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
