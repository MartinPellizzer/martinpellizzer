Pseudocaryophyllus burkartianus

Pseudocaryophyllus burkartianus, commonly known as the Burkart's ghostflower, is a rare and striking plant native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family, characterized by its succulent leaves and showy flowers. The plant produces small, pink or purple flowers that bloom during the cooler months, typically in the winter. Its leaves are fleshy and adapted to store water, reflecting its ability to thrive in harsh, dry environments.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
