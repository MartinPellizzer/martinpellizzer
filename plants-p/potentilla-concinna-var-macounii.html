Potentilla concinna var. macounii

Potentilla concinna var. macounii, commonly known as the Macoun's cinquefoil, is a perennial shrub native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in open woods or along streambanks. The plant features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a finely toothed margin, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often used in native plant gardens.
