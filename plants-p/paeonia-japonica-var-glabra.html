Paeonia japonica var. glabra

Paeonia japonica var. glabra, commonly known as the Japanese peony, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan and Korea.

It is characterized by its smooth, glabrous stems and large, showy flowers that range from white to pink and red. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters and produces heart-shaped leaves with a serrated margin. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, adding vibrant color to gardens.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal in traditional landscaping.
