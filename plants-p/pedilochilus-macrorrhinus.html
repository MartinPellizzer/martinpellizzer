Pedilochilus macrorrhinus

Pedilochilus macrorrhinus, commonly known as the large-lobed orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that typically feature a deep purple or violet coloration. The plant has a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its leaves are usually broad and fleshy, adapted to thrive in humid, shaded environments.

Pedilochilus macrorrhinus is often cultivated for its ornamental value in tropical gardens and greenhouses.
