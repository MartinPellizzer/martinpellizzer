Polygonum cespitosum var. longisetum

Polygonum cespitosum var. longisetum, commonly known as the hairy polygonum, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia.

It features upright, branched stems that are often covered in fine, hairy glands. The leaves are lance-shaped, with a waxy surface and prominent veins, and they are typically arranged alternately along the stem. Its small, clustered flowers are usually pink or red, appearing in late summer to early autumn.

This plant is often found in moist environments such as wetlands, streambanks, and marshy areas.
