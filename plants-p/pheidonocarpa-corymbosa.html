Pheidonocarpa corymbosa

Pheidonocarpa corymbosa, commonly known as the clustered hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that come in shades of red, yellow, and orange. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in coastal areas or near water sources.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscaping.
