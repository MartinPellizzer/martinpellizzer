Pimpinella tragium var. balearica

Pimpinella tragium var. balearica, commonly known as the Balearic cow-wheat, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, characterized by its umbellate inflorescences and aromatic foliage. The plant typically grows in rocky or calcareous soils and is found in coastal areas and mountainous regions. Its leaves are pinnately divided, and the flowers are small, white or pale yellow, arranged in umbrella-like clusters.

This variety is often used in traditional herbal medicine for its potential health benefits.
