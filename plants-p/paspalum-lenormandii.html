Paspalum lenormandii

Paspalum lenormandii, commonly known as the slender paspalum, is a grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically arranged in spikelets. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions.

Paspalum lenormandii is often found in disturbed areas and is considered a weed in some agricultural settings.
