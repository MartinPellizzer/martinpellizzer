Potentilla argentea var. multifida

Potentilla argentea var. multifida, commonly known as silver cinquefoil, is a perennial shrub native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its finely divided, silver-gray leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, yellow flowers in the summer, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy addition to gardens.

Its ornamental value comes from its striking foliage and long blooming period.
