Picris manginiana

Picris manginiana, commonly known as the African groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. The plant features a basal rosette of gray-green, hairy leaves and produces yellow flowers arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence. Its scientific name reflects its classification within the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its distinctive appearance.
