Passiflora foetida var. sanctae-martae

Passiflora foetida var. sanctae-martae, commonly known as the sacred passionflower, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, intricate flowers that feature five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments surrounding a central cluster of stamens. The plant produces edible, orange-yellow berries that are highly aromatic and contain numerous seeds. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped with a rough texture and a strong, sometimes unpleasant odor.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has cultural significance in some regions.
