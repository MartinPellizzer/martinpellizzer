Portulaca oleracea var. haageana

Portulaca oleracea var. haageana, commonly known as the common purslane, is an annual flowering plant native to tropical and warm temperate regions.

It is characterized by its fleshy, succulent leaves and small, yellow flowers that bloom in the sunlight. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and gardens. It has a widespread distribution and is considered a weed in many parts of the world.

The species is also valued for its edible leaves and seeds, which are used in various cuisines.
