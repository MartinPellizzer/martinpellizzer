Psychotria bahiensis var. stylosa

Psychotria bahiensis var. stylosa, commonly known as the Brazilian Psychotria, is a species native to the Atlantic rainforests of Brazil.

It is a small shrub characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically borne on short pedicels. Its fruit is a globose capsule that splits open to release minute seeds.

Psychotria species, including this variant, are often found in humid, shaded environments and are part of the Rubiaceae family.
