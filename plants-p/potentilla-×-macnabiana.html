Potentilla × macnabiana

Potentilla × macnabiana, commonly known as the Macnab's cinquefoil, is a hybrid shrub native to the western United States.

It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. The plant produces clusters of small, golden-yellow flowers in the summer, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its foliage is dark green and often has a slightly serrated edge, adding visual interest to gardens.

Potentilla × macnabiana is valued for its ornamental beauty and adaptability to various garden conditions.
