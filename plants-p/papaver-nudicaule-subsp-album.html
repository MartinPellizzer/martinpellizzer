Papaver nudicaule subsp. album

Papaver nudicaule subsp.

album, commonly known as the white poppy, is a flowering plant native to central and southern Europe. It is characterized by its delicate, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in early spring.

The plant has a basal rosette of lance-shaped leaves and produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This subspecies is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
