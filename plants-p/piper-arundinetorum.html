Piper arundinetorum

Piper arundinetorum, commonly known as the arrowleaf poisonwood, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many species of flowering plants known for their aromatic properties. The plant is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and small, greenish-white flowers that appear in clusters. It is often found in moist, shaded areas of tropical forests.

The plant contains toxic compounds, making it potentially harmful to humans and animals.
