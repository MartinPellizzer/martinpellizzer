Polypodium consimile

Polypodium consimile, commonly known as the similar holly fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Polypodiaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped fronds that are typically bipinnate, with a distinctive appearance that resembles the leaves of holly. Native to tropical and subtropical regions, this plant thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky crevices. Its rhizome is creeping and produces both roots and new fronds, allowing it to spread gradually.

The plant is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and can be found in various botanical gardens and collections.
