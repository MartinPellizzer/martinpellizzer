Polyphlebium pyxidiferum

Polyphlebium pyxidiferum, commonly known as the "cup-bearing moss," is a species of liverwort belonging to the family Porellaceae.

It is characterized by its dense, cushion-like growth form and is typically found in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. It reproduces through spores, which are released from specialized structures called cupules, giving it its common name.

This moss is often used in ecological studies due to its sensitivity to environmental changes.
