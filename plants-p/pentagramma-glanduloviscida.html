Pentagramma glanduloviscida

Pentagramma glanduloviscida, commonly known as the stickyleaf pentagramma, is a species of flowering plant in the family Pentagrammaceae.

It is native to the temperate regions of South America, particularly in Chile and Argentina. The plant is characterized by its small, ovate leaves that are often covered in glandular hairs, giving them a sticky texture. It produces small, white to greenish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences.

The species is adapted to grow in moist, shaded environments, often in forest understories.
