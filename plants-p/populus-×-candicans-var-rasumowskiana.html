Populus × candicans var. rasumowskiana

Populus × candicans var. rasumowskiana, commonly known as the white poplar, is a deciduous tree native to parts of Asia and Europe.

It is characterized by its white to gray bark that exfoliates to reveal a lighter inner layer. The leaves are broadly ovate with serrated margins and a heart-shaped base. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers in catkins, which are typically wind-pollinated.

This species is often found in moist, well-drained soils along riverbanks and in open woodlands.
