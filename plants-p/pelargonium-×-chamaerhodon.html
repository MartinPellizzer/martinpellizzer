Pelargonium × chamaerhodon

Pelargonium × chamaerhodon, commonly known as the ivy-leaved geranium, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Geraniaceae family.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and heart-shaped, dark green leaves with a velvety texture. The plant produces clusters of small, two-lipped flowers in shades of pink, red, or white, depending on the cultivar. Native to South Africa, it thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight.

It is widely cultivated as an ornamental plant for hanging baskets and containers due to its compact form and continuous blooming.
