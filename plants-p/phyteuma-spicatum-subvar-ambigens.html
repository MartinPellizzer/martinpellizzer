Phyteuma spicatum subvar. ambigens

Phyteuma spicatum subvar. ambigens, commonly known as the purple tansy, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to central and eastern Europe.

It features tall, slender stems that bear clusters of small, tubular, violet-blue flowers. The leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

This subvariety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
