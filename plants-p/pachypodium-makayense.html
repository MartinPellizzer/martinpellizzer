Pachypodium makayense

Pachypodium makayense, commonly known as the Madagascar bottle tree, is a succulent plant native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its thick, swollen stem that stores water, allowing it to survive in arid conditions. The plant has small, spiny leaves and produces yellow flowers during the dry season. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered vulnerable and requires conservation efforts to ensure its survival.
