Phaseoloides maingayi

Phaseoloides maingayi, commonly known as the African bean or African broad bean, is a leguminous plant native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pod-like fruits that contain edible seeds. The plant belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is known for nitrogen-fixing capabilities through symbiotic bacteria in its root nodules. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its nutritional seeds.

The plant's botanical features make it an important species in both traditional agriculture and ecological studies.
