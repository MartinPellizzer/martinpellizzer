Pentaphylax racemosa

Pentaphylax racemosa, commonly known as the mountain holly, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a shrub that typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils at higher elevations. The plant features small, elliptical leaves with a leathery texture and dark green color. Its showy, white to pale yellow flowers are arranged in racemes and bloom in the spring.

The berries that follow are bright red and contain several seeds, contributing to its ecological importance in its native habitat.
