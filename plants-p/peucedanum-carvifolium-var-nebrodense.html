Peucedanum carvifolium var. nebrodense

Peucedanum carvifolium var. nebrodense, commonly known as the Nebrodan cowparsley, is a perennial herb native to the alpine regions of the Balkans.

It is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes, often in mountainous areas. It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs such as parsley and carrots.

Its leaves and roots have been traditionally used in folk medicine for their purported medicinal properties.
