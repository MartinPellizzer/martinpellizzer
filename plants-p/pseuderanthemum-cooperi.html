Pseuderanthemum cooperi

Pseuderanthemum cooperi, commonly known as the Cooper's pseuderanthemum, is a member of the Acanthaceae family and is native to tropical regions of South America.

It is a small, herbaceous plant characterized by its opposite, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, tubular flowers. The flowers are typically white or pale blue and are arranged in dense inflorescences at the ends of the stems. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in humid forests.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with appropriate conditions.
