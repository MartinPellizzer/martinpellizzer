Papaver gorodkovii

Papaver gorodkovii, commonly known as the Karakachan poppy, is a flowering plant native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It is characterized by its striking, bright red petals that surround a central cluster of stamens. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, preferring well-drained soils. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, adding a vivid splash of color to the landscape.

The species is also noted for its medicinal properties, with some traditional uses in herbal remedies.
