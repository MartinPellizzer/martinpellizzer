Pittosporum anamallayense

Pittosporum anamallayense, commonly known as the Anamallay hills pittosporum, is a small evergreen shrub or tree native to the Western Ghats of India.

It is characterized by its dark green, leathery leaves and clusters of fragrant, white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, round berries that are typically yellow or orange when mature. It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

Due to its ornamental value and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
