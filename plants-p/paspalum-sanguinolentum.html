Paspalum sanguinolentum

Paspalum sanguinolentum, commonly known as the blood grass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive red-tinged inflorescence, which gives it its common name. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as wetlands and grassy areas. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green coloration.

Paspalum sanguinolentum is often used in ornamental gardening due to its visual appeal and adaptability to various soil conditions.
