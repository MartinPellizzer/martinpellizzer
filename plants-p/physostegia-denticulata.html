Physostegia denticulata

Physostegia denticulata, commonly known as the toothed beardtongue, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is characterized by its hairy stems and showy, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to purple. The plant typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and produces small, lance-shaped leaves with finely toothed edges.

It is valued in native plant gardens for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators like bees and butterflies.
