Phlomidopsis tuberosa

Phlomidopsis tuberosa, commonly known as the African potato, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are the primary part used for culinary and medicinal purposes. The plant has lance-shaped leaves and produces small, white or pale purple flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grassland and rocky habitats.

Due to its nutritional and medicinal value, it is widely cultivated and used in traditional medicine.
