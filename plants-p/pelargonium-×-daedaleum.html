Pelargonium × daedaleum

Pelargonium × daedaleum, commonly known as the rock pelargonium, is a hybrid plant in the Geraniaceae family.

It is native to South Africa and is characterized by its compact, mound-like growth habit. The plant features succulent, dark green leaves with a rough texture and prominent veins. It produces clusters of small, pale pink to white flowers that bloom in spring.

Due to its drought tolerance and ornamental appeal, it is often used in rock gardens and as a container plant.
