Parechites thunbergii

Parechites thunbergii, commonly known as the Thunberg's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, greenish flowers in spike-like clusters. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense colonies.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in wet conditions.
