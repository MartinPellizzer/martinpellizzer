Paphiopedilum hirsutissimum f. saccopetalum

Paphiopedilum hirsutissimum f. saccopetalum, commonly known as the hairy-leafed lady's slipper orchid, is a unique hybrid with distinctive botanical features.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its velvety, hairy leaves and intricate, lip-shaped petals. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte or lithophyte, thriving in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are usually pale yellow to greenish-white with a prominent pouch-like structure, which is a hallmark of the slipper orchid genus.

This cultivar is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated for its exotic appearance.
