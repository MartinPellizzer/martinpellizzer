Plectranthus phyllopodus

Plectranthus phyllopodus, commonly known as the broad-leaf plectranthus, is a herbaceous perennial native to South Africa.

It features opposite, ovate to elliptic leaves that are often hairy and have a distinctive, somewhat waxy texture. The plant produces small, tubular flowers in clusters, typically in shades of purple or violet, which bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade, making it a versatile addition to gardens.

Its aromatic leaves and ornamental value make it a popular choice for both ornamental and medicinal purposes.
