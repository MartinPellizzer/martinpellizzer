Phlox speciosa var. lanceolata

Phlox speciosa var. lanceolata, commonly known as the lanceleaf garden phlox, is a perennial flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It features showy, semi-double pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has lance-shaped, dark green leaves that are slightly hairy beneath. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained, moist soil.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
