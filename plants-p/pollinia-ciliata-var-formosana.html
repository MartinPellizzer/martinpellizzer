Pollinia ciliata var. formosana

It is characterized by its unique, elongated pollinia that are covered in fine, silky hairs, which aid in pollen adhesion to insect vectors. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist forest environments, where it thrives in the understory of subtropical and temperate forests. Its flowers are small and clustered, producing a subtle fragrance that attracts specific pollinators.
