Poa chushualana

Poa chushualana, commonly known as the Chushualana grass, is a perennial grass species native to South America, particularly found in the Andean regions of Peru and Bolivia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, wiry stems that often form dense mats on rocky or alpine slopes. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is adapted to high-altitude conditions with limited soil nutrients.

Poa chushualana plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat and is of interest to botanists studying alpine flora.
