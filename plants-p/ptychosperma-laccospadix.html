Ptychosperma laccospadix

Ptychosperma laccospadix, commonly known as the black palm, is a species of cycad native to New Guinea.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate leaves that can reach several meters in length. The plant produces distinctive, elongated cones that are typically dark green to black in color. Its botanical features include a robust root system and a unique reproductive structure that supports its role in tropical ecosystems.

Ptychosperma laccospadix is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
