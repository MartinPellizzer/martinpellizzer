Physospermum aquilegiifolium subsp. cornubiense

Physospermum aquilegiifolium subsp.

cornubiense, commonly known as the Cornish bellflower, is a rare and endemic species found primarily in the UK. It is a low-growing perennial herb with distinctive bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale purple.

The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a rough texture and is typically found in damp, shaded habitats such as woodland floors and limestone soils. Its botanical features make it a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists due to its limited distribution and ecological significance.
