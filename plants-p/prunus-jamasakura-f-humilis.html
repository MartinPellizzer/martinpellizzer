Prunus jamasakura f. humilis

Prunus jamasakura f. humilis, commonly known as the Japanese cherry blossom dwarf form, is a compact variant of the Prunus jamasakura species.

It is characterized by its small stature and delicate white to pale pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant is native to Japan and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and parks. Its foliage is dark green and provides a striking contrast to the blossoms.

This variety is particularly valued for its early flowering and graceful appearance.
