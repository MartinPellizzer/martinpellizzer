Prestonia ipomaeifolia

Prestonia ipomaeifolia, commonly known as the purple morning glory, is a climbing vine native to the Caribbean and parts of South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, velvety leaves and striking violet to purple flowers that bloom in the morning. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, round seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
