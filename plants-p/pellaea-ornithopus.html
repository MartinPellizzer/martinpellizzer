Pellaea ornithopus

Pellaea ornithopus, commonly known as the silver feather fern, is a species of fern native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that resemble bird feathers, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and rocky crevices. Its rhizomes are covered in a dense layer of fine, silvery hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Pellaea ornithopus is a perennial plant that thrives in humid conditions and is often found in the understory of forests.
