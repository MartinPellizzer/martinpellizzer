Pluchea paniculata var. gardneri

Pluchea paniculata var. gardneri, commonly known as Gardner's blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, blue to purple flower heads. The plant produces fluffy, white to pale blue seeds that are dispersed by wind, aiding in its propagation. It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as wetlands and prairies.

The leaves are usually alternate, ovate, and have a slightly hairy surface.
