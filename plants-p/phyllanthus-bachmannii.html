Phyllanthus bachmannii

Phyllanthus bachmannii, commonly known as Bachmann's spurge, is a shrub native to parts of Africa and the Arabian Peninsula.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family, characterized by its milky sap and often hairy foliage. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, showing adaptations to drought conditions. It is sometimes used in traditional medicine, though its botanical properties remain under study.
