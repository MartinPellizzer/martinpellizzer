Primula yargongensis

Primula yargongensis, commonly known as the Yarlung Valley primrose, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayan regions of China.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant typically blooms in early spring, adapting to the cool, high-altitude environments where it thrives. Its leaves are broad and heart-shaped, with a velvety texture, and it is often found in moist, shaded habitats.

Due to its limited distribution and ecological significance, it is considered a rare and valuable species in botanical studies.
