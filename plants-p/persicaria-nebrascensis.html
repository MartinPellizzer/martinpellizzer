Persicaria nebrascensis

Persicaria nebrascensis, commonly known as the Nebraska knotweed, is a flowering plant native to the central United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist to wet soils along stream banks and wet meadows. The plant features upright stems with opposite, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its small, greenish-white flowers are clustered in dense spikes, and it blooms from late summer into early fall.

Persicaria nebrascensis is valued for its ornamental qualities and its role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems.
