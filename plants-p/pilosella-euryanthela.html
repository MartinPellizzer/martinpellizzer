Pilosella euryanthela

Pilosella euryanthela, commonly known as the narrow-leaved whiteweed, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in dense, spherical heads. The plant has hairy stems and leaves that are typically narrow and linear. It is native to Europe and parts of western Asia, often found in open, disturbed habitats.

Pilosella euryanthela is a short-lived perennial that spreads through rhizomes and is often considered a weed in agricultural settings.
