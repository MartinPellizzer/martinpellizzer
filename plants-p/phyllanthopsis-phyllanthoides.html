Phyllanthopsis phyllanthoides

Phyllanthopsis phyllanthoides, commonly known as the African waterleaf, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It is a small, herbaceous annual that typically grows to a height of about 30 centimeters. The plant features simple, alternate leaves that are often lobed or trifoliate, with a rough texture. It produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by small, round fruits.

The plant is often found in moist, disturbed soils and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
