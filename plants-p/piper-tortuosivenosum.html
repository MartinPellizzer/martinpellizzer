Piper tortuosivenosum

Piper tortuosivenosum, commonly known as the waxy begonia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, waxy leaves that often display vibrant colors such as red, purple, and green. The plant produces small, clustered white flowers that are typically borne on short pedicels. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for indoor gardening and tropical landscapes.
