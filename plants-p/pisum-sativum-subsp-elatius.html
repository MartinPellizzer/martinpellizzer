Pisum sativum subsp. elatius

Pisum sativum subsp.

elatius, commonly known as the tall pea, is a subspecies of the common pea that exhibits distinct botanical features. It is characterized by its taller growth habit compared to the standard pea, often reaching heights of up to 2 meters.

The plant produces elongated, slender pods that contain several seeds, typically with a pale green coloration. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three leaflets, and it has a climbing habit with tendrils that aid in support.

This subspecies is adapted to a variety of climates and is often cultivated for both ornamental and edible purposes.
