Protium elegans

Protium elegans, commonly known as the elegant tallow tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Burseraceae and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in axillary inflorescences, followed by oval, woody fruits containing a single seed. Its leaves and bark contain compounds with potential medicinal properties, making it of interest in ethnobotanical research.

The tree is also valued for its hard, durable wood used in construction and furniture-making.
