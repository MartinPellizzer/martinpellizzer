Polygala sphaerocephala

Polygala sphaerocephala, commonly known as the globe-headed milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive spherical flower heads, which are composed of numerous small, pale yellow to white flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. Its leaves are usually narrow and linear, and it produces small, cylindrical seed pods that contain several seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
