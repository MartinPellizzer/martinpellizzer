Plagiostigma pumila

Plagiostigma pumila, commonly known as the dwarf plagiostigma, is a small, herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in arid or semi-arid environments. The plant features small, narrow leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence. Its flowers are usually small and yellow, arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems.

Plagiostigma pumila is known for its resilience to drought and is often used in rock gardens due to its compact growth habit.
