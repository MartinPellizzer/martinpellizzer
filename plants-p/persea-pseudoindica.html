Persea pseudoindica

Persea pseudoindica, commonly known as the Mexican persimmon, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central America and parts of Mexico.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its glossy, lance-shaped leaves and fragrant flowers. The tree produces small, orange to reddish fruits that are edible when fully mature. The fruit has a sweet, honey-like flavor and is often consumed fresh or used in culinary applications.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
