Panicum pangerangense

Panicum pangerangense, commonly known as the Pangerang grass, is a species of grass native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, blade-like leaves. The plant produces small, fluffy seeds that are dispersed by wind, aiding in its propagation. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and marshy areas.

Due to its adaptability, it plays an important role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
