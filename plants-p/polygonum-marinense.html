Polygonum marinense

Polygonum marinense, commonly known as sea blite, is a halophytic plant adapted to saline environments.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family and is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves that store water. The plant typically grows in coastal areas, salt marshes, and other moist, saline soils. Its small, greenish flowers are clustered in dense inflorescences, and it produces small, round seeds.

Polygonum marinense plays a role in stabilizing coastal soils and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
