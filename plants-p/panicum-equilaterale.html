Panicum equilaterale

Panicum equilaterale, commonly known as the common panicgrass, is a grass species native to various regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in open habitats such as fields and roadsides.

Due to its adaptability, it is considered a weed in some agricultural settings.
