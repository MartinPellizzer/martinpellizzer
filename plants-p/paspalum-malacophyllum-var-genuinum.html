Paspalum malacophyllum var. genuinum

Paspalum malacophyllum var. genuinum, commonly known as the narrowleaf paspalum, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It typically grows in wetland and marshy environments, preferring moist, acidic soils. The plant has slender, erect stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets that are clustered in dense, cylindrical spikes.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
