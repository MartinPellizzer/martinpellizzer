Potentilla crantzii var. typica

Potentilla crantzii var. typica, commonly known as the creeping cinquefoil, is a low-growing perennial shrub native to the western United States.

It features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has compound leaves with five oval leaflets, and its stems are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its hardy nature and ornamental value make it a popular choice for native plant gardens.
