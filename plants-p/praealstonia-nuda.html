Praealstonia nuda

Praealstonia nuda, commonly known as the naked alstonia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils. The plant produces small, white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, often appearing in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a rough, hairy texture.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
