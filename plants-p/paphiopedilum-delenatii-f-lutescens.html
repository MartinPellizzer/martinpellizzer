Paphiopedilum delenatii f. lutescens

Paphiopedilum delenatii f. lutescens, commonly known as the golden-flowered slipper orchid, is a rare and striking hybrid within the Paphiopedilum genus.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow petals and a unique, pouch-like lip that resembles a slipper, a trait typical of the slipper orchid family. This plant is native to Southeast Asia, where it thrives in humid, shaded environments with high air circulation. Its flowers are often fragrant and bloom during the warmer months, adding to its ornamental appeal.

Due to its rarity and specific care requirements, it is a prized specimen among orchid enthusiasts.
