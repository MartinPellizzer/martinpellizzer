Pandanus whitmeeanus

Pandanus whitmeeanus, commonly known as the giant pandanus, is a tropical plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, feathery leaves that can reach several meters in length. The plant produces fragrant, yellowish-white flowers that are clustered in branched inflorescences. Its roots are often used in traditional crafts, while its leaves are utilized for thatching and weaving.

The species is well adapted to coastal and brackish water environments, making it a resilient component of mangrove ecosystems.
