Pelargonium antidysentericum subsp. antidysentericum

Pelargonium antidysentericum subsp.

antidysentericum, commonly known as the dysentery geranium, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa. It features upright stems with opposite, palmately lobed leaves that are typically hairy and have a distinctive reddish or purple hue.

The plant produces small, clustered flowers in shades of pink to red, which bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
