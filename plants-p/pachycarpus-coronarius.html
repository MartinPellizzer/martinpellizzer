Pachycarpus coronarius

Pachycarpus coronarius, commonly known as the crownfruit plant, is a flowering shrub native to the Canary Islands.

It is characterized by its dense, rounded growth habit and produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has thick, fleshy leaves that help it conserve water in its arid native environment. Its fruit, which resembles a small apple, is edible and has a mild, sweet flavor.

Pachycarpus coronarius is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and unique appearance.
