Pleurothallis intonsa

Pleurothallis intonsa, commonly known as the white-veined orchid, is a species native to the cloud forests of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact, epiphytic growth habit. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow with intricate veining. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining, loose soil.

Due to its delicate appearance and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
