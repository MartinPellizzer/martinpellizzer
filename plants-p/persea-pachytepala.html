Persea pachytepala

Persea pachytepala, commonly known as the Mexican persimmon, is a small evergreen tree native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like laurel and avocado. The tree produces small, glossy leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. Its fruit is a yellow or orange berry that turns soft and sweet when ripe.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its edible fruit.
