Paeonia × suffruticosa var. purpurea

Paeonia × suffruticosa var. purpurea, commonly known as the Japanese peony, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its thick, woody stems and large, showy flowers that range from pink to deep purple. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters and produces compound, trifoliate leaves. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, often with a sweet, spicy fragrance.

The botanical features of this variety make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
