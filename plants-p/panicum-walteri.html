Panicum walteri

Panicum walteri, commonly known as Walter's panicgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks. The plant has slender, erect stems that can reach heights of up to 1 meter, with narrow, grass-like leaves. Its inflorescence consists of a panicle made up of numerous small spikelets, which are often tinged with red or purple.

Panicum walteri plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
