Pyrus horti-edeni

Pyrus horti-edeni, commonly known as the Eden pear, is a rare and ornamental species native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in early spring. The tree produces small, edible pears that are typically harvested in late summer. It thrives in temperate climates and prefers well-drained, loamy soil.

Due to its rarity and unique appearance, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by plant enthusiasts.
