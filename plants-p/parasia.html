Parasia

Parasia, commonly known as the parasitic orchid, is a genus of flowering plants that exhibits unique botanical characteristics.

These plants are hemiparasitic, meaning they derive some nutrients from their host plants while also performing photosynthesis. They typically have small, delicate leaves and produce intricate, often fragrant flowers. Parasia species are commonly found in tropical and subtropical regions, often growing on trees or shrubs.

Their specialized root systems allow them to establish symbiotic relationships with specific host plants.
