Piper gracile

Piper gracile, commonly known as the slender pepper plant, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Central and South America, where it thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant produces small, green berries that turn red when ripe, containing aromatic seeds used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are often used in local cuisines for their pungent flavor.

Piper gracile is valued for both its ornamental appeal and its medicinal properties.
