Primula parryi var. brachyantha

Primula parryi var. brachyantha, commonly known as the Sierra primrose, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in cool, moist environments. Its leaves are broad and ovate, often with a slightly hairy texture, and it produces small, clustered inflorescences.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
