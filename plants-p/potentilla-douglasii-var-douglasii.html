Potentilla douglasii var. douglasii

Potentilla douglasii var. douglasii, commonly known as Douglas' cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a low-growing perennial shrub with yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant features compound leaves with five leaflets, which are typically oval and slightly hairy. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and rocky areas.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable plant for ecological restoration and native gardens.
