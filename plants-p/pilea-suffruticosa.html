Pilea suffruticosa

Pilea suffruticosa, commonly known as the creeping pilea, is a perennial flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, low-growing habit and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. The plant features thick, waxy leaves that are often succulent, adapted to retain moisture in arid conditions. It produces small, rounded fruits that contain seeds, aiding in its propagation.

Pilea suffruticosa is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its attractive foliage and ease of care.
