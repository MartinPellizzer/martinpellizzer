Pogonitis montana

Pogonitis montana, commonly known as the mountain pogonitis, is a species of moss belonging to the family Pottiaceae.

It is characterized by its dense, cushion-like growth form and is typically found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky slopes. The plant has a dark green to reddish-brown stem with a distinct, often hairy base. Its leaves are elongated and gradually tapering, with a smooth or slightly serrated margin.

Pogonitis montana is well adapted to harsh, high-altitude conditions and plays a role in soil stabilization and moisture retention in its habitat.
