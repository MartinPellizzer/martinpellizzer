Prunus rufa var. trichantha

Prunus rufa var. trichantha, commonly known as the hairy red cherry, is a variant of the red cherry tree characterized by its distinctive hairy foliage.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is native to parts of China. The plant typically grows as a small to medium-sized deciduous tree, reaching heights of up to 10 meters. Its leaves are ovate with a serrated margin and are covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a unique appearance.

The flowers are usually pink to white, appearing in early spring before the leaves fully develop.
