Plantago hakusanensis f. viridescens

Plantago hakusanensis f. viridescens, commonly known as the green-veined plantain, is a variant of the plantain species native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. The leaves often display a distinctive greenish tint, particularly along the veins, which sets it apart from other plantain varieties. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in a spike, typical of the Plantaginaceae family.

This plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or grassland habitats.
