Pinus webbiana

Pinus webbiana, commonly known as the Webb's pine, is a species of coniferous tree native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, straight trunk and long, needle-like leaves that are typically grouped in bundles of two. The tree produces cones that are often reddish-brown in color and contain edible seeds. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appearance and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
