Pavonia revoluta

Pavonia revoluta, commonly known as the scarlet pimpernel, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 feet. The plant features showy, bright red flowers that bloom in clusters, often in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are ovate to lanceolate, with hairy surfaces and a slightly serrated edge.

Pavonia revoluta thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often found in disturbed soils along roadsides and in open woodlands.
