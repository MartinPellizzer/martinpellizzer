Panicum nitidum var. viride

Panicum nitidum var. viride, commonly known as green panicgrass, is a grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, erect growth habit and narrow, elongated leaves that are typically a vibrant green color. The plant produces fluffy, cylindrical inflorescences that are often mistaken for rice or other grasses. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a common ornamental and forage grass.

Due to its rapid growth and adaptability, it is sometimes considered an invasive species in certain regions.
