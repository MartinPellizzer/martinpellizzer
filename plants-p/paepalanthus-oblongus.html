Paepalanthus oblongus

Paepalanthus oblongus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved arrowroot, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-forming leaves that are narrow and often covered in a waxy coating. Due to its ornamental value and unique foliage, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
