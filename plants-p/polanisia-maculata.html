Polanisia maculata

Polanisia maculata, commonly known as the spotted polanisia, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive spotted leaves and clusters of small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest understories. Its leaves are often heart-shaped with a velvety texture, and the flowers are usually white or pale pink.

Polanisia maculata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
