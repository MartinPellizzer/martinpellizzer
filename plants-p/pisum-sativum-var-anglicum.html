Pisum sativum var. anglicum

Pisum sativum var. anglicum, commonly known as the English pea, is a subspecies of the common pea plant.

It is characterized by its smooth, green pods that contain edible, round seeds. The plant is typically grown as an annual crop in temperate climates. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires a cool growing season for optimal pod development.

The English pea is widely cultivated for its nutritional value and use in various culinary applications.
