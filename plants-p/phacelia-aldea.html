Phacelia aldea

Phacelia aldea, commonly known as evening primrose, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It features delicate, showy flowers that bloom in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant has slender, branching stems and lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. Its root system is fibrous, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Phacelia aldea is often found in disturbed soils and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
