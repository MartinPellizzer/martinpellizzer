Persicaria foliosa var. nikaii

Persicaria foliosa var. nikaii, commonly known as Nikai's leafy knotweed, is a variant of the species Persicaria foliosa, which belongs to the Polygonaceae family.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of silky hairs. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its small, greenish-white flowers appear in clusters and are followed by elongated, bead-like fruits.

This variant is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its attractive foliage.
