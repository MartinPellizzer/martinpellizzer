Potentilla taurica var. tauriciformis

Potentilla taurica var. tauriciformis, commonly known as the Tauric potentilla, is a perennial shrub native to the Caucasus region.

It typically grows to a height of about 30-60 cm, featuring upright stems covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces small, bright yellow flowers in clusters during the summer months. Its leaves are alternate, ovate to elliptical, and have a slightly serrated edge.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or dry habitats.
