Piper douglasii

Piper douglasii, commonly known as Douglas's pepper, is a climbing shrub native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many species of pepper plants. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are followed by berries containing a single seed. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage.
