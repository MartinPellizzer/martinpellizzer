Physostelma wrayi

Physostelma wrayi, commonly known as the Florida starflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its star-shaped flowers, which are typically yellow or orange, and its lanceolate, hairy leaves. The plant thrives in moist, sandy soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Physostelma wrayi is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in supporting local pollinators.
