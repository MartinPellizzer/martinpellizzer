Primulina confertiflora

Primulina confertiflora, commonly known as the broad-flowered primula, is a species of flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Gesneriaceae and is characterized by its compact growth habit and attractive, showy flowers. The plant typically features dark green, velvety leaves and produces clusters of tubular or funnel-shaped blooms in shades of pink, red, or white. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated as a decorative houseplant.

Due to its ornamental value, it is popular among plant enthusiasts for its beauty and ease of care.
