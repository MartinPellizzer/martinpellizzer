Pteroglossa hilariana

Pteroglossa hilariana, commonly known as the golden star orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers that resemble stars, which bloom during the rainy season. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments within the undergrowth of tropical forests. Its leaves are elongated and slightly hairy, adapted to thrive in humid conditions.

Pteroglossa hilariana is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
