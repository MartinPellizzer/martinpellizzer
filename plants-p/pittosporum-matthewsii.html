Pittosporum matthewsii

Pittosporum matthewsii, commonly known as the Stewart Island Pittosporum, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white to cream-colored flowers that bloom in spring. The leaves are elliptical, glossy, and have a smooth texture, often with a slight waxy coating. This species thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is typically found in coastal and lowland forest areas.

It plays an important ecological role in supporting local wildlife and is valued for its ornamental qualities in horticulture.
