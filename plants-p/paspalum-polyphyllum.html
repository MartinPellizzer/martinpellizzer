Paspalum polyphyllum

Paspalum polyphyllum, commonly known as the common paspalum, is a perennial grass native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers. It is often found in moist or wetland habitats, thriving in environments with high humidity.

Paspalum polyphyllum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping or as a ground cover.
