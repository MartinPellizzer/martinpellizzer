Pelargonium suburbanum subsp. suburbanum

Pelargonium suburbanum subsp.

suburbanum, commonly known as the suburban geranium, is a species native to southern Africa. It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils.

The plant features showy, five-petaled flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, which bloom during the summer months. Its leaves are usually rounded with a slightly hairy texture, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The subspecies is characterized by its compact growth habit and adaptability to various garden conditions.
