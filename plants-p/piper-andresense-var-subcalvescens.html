Piper andresense var. subcalvescens

Piper andresense var. subcalvescens, commonly known as the Andes pepper or Andean pepper, is a variant of the Piper andresense species native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a climbing vine characterized by its small, elongated leaves and clusters of tiny, greenish-white flowers. The plant produces small, round berries that turn from green to red as they mature. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has some traditional uses in local medicine.

Its botanical features make it well adapted to the humid, temperate climates of the Andes.
