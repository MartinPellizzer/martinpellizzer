Pyrus hohenesteri

Pyrus hohenesteri, commonly known as the Hohenester's pear, is a species of flowering plant in the family Rosaceae.

It is native to the eastern Himalayas, specifically found in regions of India and Nepal. The plant is characterized by its deciduous nature, with lance-shaped leaves that turn yellow in autumn. Its flowers are small, white, and clustered in inflorescences, and it produces small, edible fruits that are similar to pears.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
