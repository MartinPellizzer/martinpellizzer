Pinus excelsa var. peuce

Pinus excelsa var. peuce, commonly known as the Serbian pine, is a tall evergreen conifer native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It is characterized by its long, slender needles and distinctive, pendulous cones. The tree can grow up to 40 meters in height, making it one of the tallest pine species. Its bark is thick and fissured, providing protection against forest fires.

The plant thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soils, often found in mountainous regions.
