Passiflora uleana

Passiflora uleana, commonly known as the purple passionflower, is a climbing vine native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its striking, complex flowers that feature five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments surrounding the reproductive structures. The plant produces edible, orange-yellow berries that are rich in antioxidants and often used in culinary applications. Its leaves are trifoliate, with a velvety texture and a strong, aromatic scent.

Passiflora uleana is also valued for its ornamental beauty and has been used in traditional medicine for its sedative and calming properties.
