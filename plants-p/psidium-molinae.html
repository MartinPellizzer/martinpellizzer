Psidium molinae

Psidium molinae, commonly known as the Molina's guava, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to southern Mexico and is characterized by its small, edible berries that are typically yellow or red when mature. The plant has simple, ovate leaves with a smooth surface and emits a fragrant aroma. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

The flowers are white and fragrant, attracting various pollinators such as bees and birds.
