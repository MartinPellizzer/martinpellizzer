Plantago pachyphylla var. hawaiensis

Plantago pachyphylla var. hawaiensis, commonly known as the Hawaiian broadleaf plantain, is a perennial herb native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that are adapted to arid conditions, making it well-suited for dry environments. The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in dense clusters, which are typical of the Plantaginaceae family. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to anchor firmly in rocky or sandy soils.

This species plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for native insects and small animals.
