Pappophorum vincentianum

Pappophorum vincentianum, commonly known as Vincent's grass, is a perennial herb native to the Caribbean islands.

It features slender, erect stems that can grow up to 1 meter in height, often with a fibrous root system. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in loose clusters, followed by feathery, wind-dispersed seeds. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, adapted to thrive in tropical and subtropical environments.

It is often found in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides and forest edges.
