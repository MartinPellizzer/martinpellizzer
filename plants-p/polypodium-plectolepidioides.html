Polypodium plectolepidioides

Polypodium plectolepidioides, commonly known as the broad-leafed shield fern, is a species of evergreen fern native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its large, leathery, shield-shaped fronds. The plant typically grows on rocks or tree trunks, thriving in humid, shaded environments. Its rhizomes are often covered in a dense, dark brown layer of rhizome scales.

The fronds are bi-pinnate, with a distinctive arrangement of pinnae that give it a unique appearance.
