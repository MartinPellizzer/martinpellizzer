Pancratium occidentale

Pancratium occidentale, commonly known as the yellow rain lily, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of South America.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. The plant has strap-like, grassy leaves and produces a tall, slender stem. It thrives in sandy, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas.

Pancratium occidentale is also valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
