Parosela trochilina

Parosela trochilina, commonly known as the mountain orchid, is a species of orchid native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, spurred flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and pink. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments, often found in cloud forests and alpine meadows. Its roots are adapted to absorb nutrients from the rocky substrates where it grows.

Parosela trochilina is valued for its ornamental beauty and plays a role in the ecological balance of its native habitat.
