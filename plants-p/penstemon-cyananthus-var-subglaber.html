Penstemon cyananthus var. subglaber

Penstemon cyananthus var. subglaber, commonly known as the blue curl penstemon, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It features upright stems with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces striking blue to violet tubular flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its flowers are adapted for pollination by hummingbirds and bees, showcasing specialized nectar guides.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance in rocky or well-drained soils.
