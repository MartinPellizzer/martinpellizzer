Phlomoides labiosa

Phlomoides labiosa, commonly known as the pinkstem false foxglove, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Phlomoides labiosa is adapted to dry, rocky environments and is often found in desert scrub and grassland habitats.

It is a member of the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is known for its aromatic foliage and ecological importance to native pollinators.
