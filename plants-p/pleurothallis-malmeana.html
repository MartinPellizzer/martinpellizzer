Pleurothallis malmeana

Pleurothallis malmeana, commonly known as the Malme's orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a small to medium-sized orchid with a compact, pseudobulbous structure and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces intricate, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, with a distinctive lip that is often marked with purple or red. It thrives in shaded, humid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Pleurothallis malmeana is appreciated for its ornamental value and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
