Pleurothallis tribuloides

Pleurothallis tribuloides, commonly known as the spiky orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its compact growth habit and intricate floral structures. The plant typically produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and yellow, often arranged in a spike-like inflorescence. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained, acidic soil for optimal growth.

Due to its unique appearance and relatively easy care requirements, it is a popular choice among orchid enthusiasts.
