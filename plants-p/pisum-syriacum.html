Pisum syriacum

Pisum syriacum, commonly known as Syrian pea, is a flowering plant in the Fabaceae family.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and is characterized by its climbing habit and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, pea-like pods that contain several seeds. It is often cultivated for its edible seeds, which are rich in protein.

Pisum syriacum is also valued for its ornamental flowers and its role in traditional herbal medicine.
