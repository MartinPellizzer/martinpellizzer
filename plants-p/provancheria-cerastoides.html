Provancheria cerastoides

Provancheria cerastoides, commonly known as the common spurge, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and Africa.

It is an annual herb with a distinctive milky sap that is toxic to humans and animals. The plant features upright stems that are often branched and covered in fine hairs. Its small, yellow flowers are clustered in dense, spherical inflorescences called cymes.

The leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a rough texture with prominent veins.
