Passiflora recurva

Passiflora recurva, commonly known as the curly passionflower, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, intricate floral structure with five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments surrounding the reproductive organs. The plant produces yellow-orange flowers that are often fragrant and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its fruits are edible berries that turn red when mature, containing seeds surrounded by a fleshy pulp.

Passiflora recurva is valued for its ornamental beauty and is cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
