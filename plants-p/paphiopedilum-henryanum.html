Paphiopedilum henryanum

Paphiopedilum henryanum, commonly known as the Henryan's lady's slipper, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive slipper-shaped pouch, which is a key feature of the Paphiopedilum genus. The plant typically grows as a terrestrial or epiphytic orchid, with dark green, leathery leaves and a compact, showy flower. Its flowers are usually pale yellow to greenish-white with intricate patterns, and they bloom once a year.

This orchid is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated for horticultural purposes.
