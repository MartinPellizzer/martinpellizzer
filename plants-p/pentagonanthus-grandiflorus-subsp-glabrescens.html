Pentagonanthus grandiflorus subsp. glabrescens

Pentagonanthus grandiflorus subsp.

glabrescens, commonly known as the smooth-flowered pentagonanthus, is a species of flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its pentagonal-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers.

The subspecies glabrescens is distinguished by its smooth, hairless foliage compared to other subspecies. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest undergrowth.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens.
