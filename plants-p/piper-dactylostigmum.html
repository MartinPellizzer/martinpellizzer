Piper dactylostigmum

Piper dactylostigmum, commonly known as the Jamaican pepper or wild pepper, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species. The plant is characterized by its woody vines and small, heart-shaped leaves. Its fruit, a tiny, dark blue berry, contains the plant's psychoactive compound, piperine.

It is often found growing in tropical rainforests and is used in traditional medicine and as a spice in some regions.
