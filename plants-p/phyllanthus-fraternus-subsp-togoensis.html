Phyllanthus fraternus subsp. togoensis

Phyllanthus fraternus subsp.

togoensis, commonly known as the Togo milkweed, is a shrub native to West Africa. It is characterized by its small, opposite leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, and it is adapted to grow in dry, sandy soils. It is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.

The species is noted for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
