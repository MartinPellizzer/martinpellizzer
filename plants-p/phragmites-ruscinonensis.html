Phragmites ruscinonensis

Phragmites ruscinonensis, commonly known as the Ruscinian reed, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Eastern Europe and Western Asia.

It typically grows in wetland habitats such as marshes, lakeshores, and riverbanks, where it thrives in moist to aquatic conditions. The plant forms dense stands with tall, hollow stems that can reach heights of up to 2 meters. Its leaves are long, narrow, and often have a rough texture, while its inflorescence consists of a cluster of spikelets.

Phragmites ruscinonensis plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
