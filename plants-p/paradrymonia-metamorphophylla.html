Paradrymonia metamorphophylla

Paradrymonia metamorphophylla, commonly known as the ghost fern, is a unique fern species native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, delicate fronds that resemble translucent ghostly shapes, giving it its common name. The plant exhibits a remarkable ability to change the shape and color of its fronds throughout its life cycle, a trait known as metamorphosis. Its leaves are typically bioluminescent under certain conditions, adding to its ethereal appearance.

This rare plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is highly valued for its ornamental and medicinal properties.
