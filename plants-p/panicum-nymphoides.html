Panicum nymphoides

Panicum nymphoides, commonly known as the silky panicgrass, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and fine, silky hairs covering its foliage. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in panicles, which are often wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and restoration projects.
