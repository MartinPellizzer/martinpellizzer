Phoebe scortechinii

Phoebe scortechinii, commonly known as the Florida phoebe, is a small evergreen tree native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows to a height of 10 to 20 feet, featuring a rounded crown and smooth, gray bark. The leaves are simple, alternate, and elliptical, with a waxy texture and dark green color. It produces small, fragrant flowers in clusters, followed by red berries that are an important food source for birds.

This species is well adapted to wetland environments and is often found in swamps and along riverbanks.
