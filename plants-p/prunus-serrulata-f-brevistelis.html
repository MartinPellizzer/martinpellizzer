Prunus serrulata f. brevistelis

Prunus serrulata f. brevistelis, commonly known as the dwarf cherry or compact weeping cherry, is a compact, weeping form of the Japanese cherry.

It features delicate, pale pink to white flowers that bloom in early spring, adding ornamental value to gardens. The plant has a rounded, spreading habit with short, drooping branches that create a soft, cascading effect. Its foliage is dark green and provides a striking contrast to the blossoms.

It is well-suited for small spaces and is often used in bonsai or as a decorative shrub in ornamental horticulture.
