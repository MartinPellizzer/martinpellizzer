Premna orangeana

Premna orangeana, commonly known as the orange premna, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, which bloom in the summer months. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical forests.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardeners seeking a shade-tolerant, decorative plant.
