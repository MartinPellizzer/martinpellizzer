Piper sarapiquinum

Piper sarapiquinum, commonly known as the Amazonian pepper or wild pepper, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species such as black pepper. The plant is characterized by its slender, climbing vines and small, heart-shaped leaves. Its fruit, which is a small, yellow or red berry, contains the aromatic compound responsible for its distinctive flavor.

Piper sarapiquinum is often used in traditional medicine and as a spice in local cuisines.
