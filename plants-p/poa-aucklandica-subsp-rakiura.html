Poa aucklandica subsp. rakiura

Poa aucklandica subsp.

rakiura, commonly known as the Rakiura tussock grass, is a native New Zealand plant belonging to the grass family Poaceae. It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, wiry leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs.

This subspecies is endemic to the Stewart Island and adjacent southern islands, thriving in moist, rocky habitats. It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for invertebrates.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and vulnerable species.
