Phyllanthus brothersonii

Phyllanthus brothersonii, commonly known as the Madagascar starleaf, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its distinctive star-shaped leaves, which are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its unique morphology and restricted habitat, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
