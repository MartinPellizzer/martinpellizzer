Potentilla argentea var. grandiceps

Potentilla argentea var. grandiceps, commonly known as silver cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the Arctic and subarctic regions.

It is characterized by its silver-white underside leaves and clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It is a low-growing perennial shrub that is often found in alpine and tundra environments.

Its hardy nature makes it well adapted to cold climates and harsh conditions.
