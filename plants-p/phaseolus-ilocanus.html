Phaseolus ilocanus

Phaseolus ilocanus, commonly known as the Ilocano bean, is a flowering plant native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes known for their nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain edible seeds used in local cuisine. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three oval-shaped leaflets, and it thrives in tropical and subtropical climates.

Phaseolus ilocanus is an important crop for indigenous communities in the region.
