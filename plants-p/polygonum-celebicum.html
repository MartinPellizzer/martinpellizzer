Polygonum celebicum

Polygonum celebicum, commonly known as the Celebes knotweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, which includes species known for their distinctive nodes and often triangular stems. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped with a smooth margin, and it produces small, greenish flowers in clusters.

Polygonum celebicum is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
