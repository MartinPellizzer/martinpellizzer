Passiflora incarnata var. integriloba

Passiflora incarnata var. integriloba, commonly known as the purple passionflower, is a climbing vine native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking, intricate flowers that feature five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments surrounding the reproductive structures. The plant produces edible berries that are typically purple or orange in color, containing a sweet, juicy pulp. Its leaves are alternate, heart-shaped, and often have a velvety texture.

This species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its calming properties.
