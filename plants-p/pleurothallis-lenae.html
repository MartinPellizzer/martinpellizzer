Pleurothallis lenae

Pleurothallis lenae, commonly known as the Andean orchid, is a species native to the cloud forests of South America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its small, intricate flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees without deriving nutrients from them. Its flowers are usually pale green or white, with a distinctive lip that aids in pollination.

Pleurothallis lenae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by enthusiasts for its unique appearance.
