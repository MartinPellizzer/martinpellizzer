Polygala rivularis

Polygala rivularis, commonly known as the river milk-vetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, sandy soils along stream banks and in wetlands. The plant features slender, branching stems and small, greenish-white flowers arranged in spikelike clusters. Its leaves are opposite, narrow, and often covered with fine hairs.

Polygala rivularis plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various insects and small animals.
