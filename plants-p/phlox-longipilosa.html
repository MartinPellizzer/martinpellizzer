Phlox longipilosa

Phlox longipilosa, commonly known as the long-haired phlox, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its showy, fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of pink and white. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full sun. Its foliage is covered in fine, hair-like structures, giving it its common name.

Phlox longipilosa is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
