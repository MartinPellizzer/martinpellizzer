Phyllota humilis

Phyllota humilis, commonly known as the Mexican sunflower, is a perennial herb native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and striking yellow-orange flower heads that resemble sunflowers. The plant features palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, and its flowers are composite, consisting of both ray and disc florets. Phyllota humilis thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
