Polystichum atuntzeense

Polystichum atuntzeense, commonly known as the Atuntze fern, is a species of evergreen fern native to the Andes Mountains in South America.

It belongs to the family Dryopteridaceae and is characterized by its tall, upright fronds with a dark green, leathery texture. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often found in cloud forests at high elevations. Its spore-producing structures, called sori, are located on the underside of the fronds, contributing to its reproductive strategy.

This fern is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in the ecological diversity of its native habitat.
