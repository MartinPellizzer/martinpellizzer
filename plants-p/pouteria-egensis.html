Pouteria egensis

Pouteria egensis, commonly known as the Amazonian sapote, is a tropical tree native to the rainforests of South America.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and broad, lobed leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that give way to edible, orange-colored fruits with a creamy, sweet flesh. Its fruit is rich in nutrients and has been traditionally used in local cuisines.

The tree also plays a role in the ecosystem by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
