Podochilus effusus

Podochilus effusus, commonly known as the velvet orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its soft, velvety leaves and clusters of fragrant, pinkish-white flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers bloom in clusters and are known for their subtle fragrance.

Podochilus effusus is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ease of cultivation.
