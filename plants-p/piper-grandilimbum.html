Piper grandilimbum

Piper grandilimbum, commonly known as the large-leaved pepper vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many species of pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and small, clustered flowers that develop into dark blue to black berries. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

The fruit contains essential oils and alkaloids, contributing to its aromatic and medicinal properties.
