Psidium hians var. truncatum

Psidium hians var. truncatum, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or pitanga, is a small evergreen shrub or tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its small, glossy leaves and showy white or pink flowers. The plant produces small, red, fleshy fruits that are edible and often used in culinary and medicinal applications.

It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is valued for its ornamental appeal and fruit yield.
