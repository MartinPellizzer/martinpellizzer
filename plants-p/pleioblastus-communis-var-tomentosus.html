Pleioblastus communis var. tomentosus

Pleioblastus communis var. tomentosus, commonly known as the silver bamboo, is a clumping bamboo species native to East Asia.

It features slender, arching culms covered in a dense, silvery-white pubescence that gives it a distinctive appearance. The leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and have a soft, matte texture. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and is often used in ornamental landscaping for its aesthetic appeal.

This variety is valued for its compact growth habit and ability to tolerate a range of soil conditions.
