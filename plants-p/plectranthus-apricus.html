Plectranthus apricus

Plectranthus apricus, commonly known as the lemon verbena plant, is a shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its aromatic, lemon-scented leaves that are often used in herbal teas and aromatherapy. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Plectranthus apricus is also valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to attract beneficial insects.
