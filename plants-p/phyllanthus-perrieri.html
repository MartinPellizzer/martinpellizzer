Phyllanthus perrieri

Phyllanthus perrieri, commonly known as the African spurge, is a shrub native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves and small yellow flowers. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in dry conditions.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and traditional medicine.
