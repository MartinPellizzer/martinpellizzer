Polycarpaea corymbosa var. grandiflora

Polycarpaea corymbosa var. grandiflora, commonly known as the large-flowered polycarpaea, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa.

It is characterized by its showy, clustered inflorescences that bear numerous small, white to pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests or along stream banks. Its leaves are opposite, ovate to elliptic, and have a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive floral display.
