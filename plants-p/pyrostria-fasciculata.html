Pyrostria fasciculata

Pyrostria fasciculata, commonly known as the rock fig, is a shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces edible fig-like fruits that are often consumed by birds and other wildlife. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Pyrostria fasciculata is often used in ornamental gardening due to its hardiness and attractive appearance.
