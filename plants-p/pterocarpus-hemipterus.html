Pterocarpus hemipterus

Pterocarpus hemipterus, commonly known as the Malacca ebony, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dark, dense wood that is highly valued for its strength and durability. The plant has compound leaves with trifoliate or pinnate arrangements, and its flowers are small and yellowish-green. The tree produces woody pods containing flat, oblong seeds.

It thrives in humid climates and is often found in lowland and hill forest regions.
