Pandanus nandiensis

Pandanus nandiensis, commonly known as the Nandi palm, is a tropical plant native to eastern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feather-like leaves that grow in a rosette pattern. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or green, followed by edible fruits resembling pineapples. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas or near rivers.

The Nandi palm is valued for its ornamental beauty and has cultural significance in some regions.
