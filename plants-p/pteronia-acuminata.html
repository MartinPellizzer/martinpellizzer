Pteronia acuminata

Pteronia acuminata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved pteronia, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and spikes of small, tubular flowers. The plant typically blooms in spring and summer, producing vibrant pink or purple flowers. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and hardiness, it is often cultivated in Mediterranean climates.
