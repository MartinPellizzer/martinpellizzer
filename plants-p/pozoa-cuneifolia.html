Pozoa cuneifolia

Pozoa cuneifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf pozoa, is a member of the Bromeliaceae family, native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a small, evergreen herb that typically grows in shaded, humid environments. The plant features narrow, strap-like leaves that are often arranged in a rosette pattern, which helps to collect and retain moisture. Its flowers are usually small and clustered, producing a distinctive, fragrant scent that attracts pollinators.

Due to its ornamental value and unique foliage, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
