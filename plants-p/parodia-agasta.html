Parodia agasta

Parodia agasta, commonly known as the Agasta Cactus, is a species of cactus native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stem and spiny, ribbed structure, which helps it conserve water in its natural habitat. The plant produces yellow flowers during the warmer months, adding a striking contrast to its green foliage. It is a slow-growing plant that thrives in well-draining soil and full sunlight.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is a popular choice for succulent collectors and desert gardeners.
