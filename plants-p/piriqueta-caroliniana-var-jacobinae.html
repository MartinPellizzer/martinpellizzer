Piriqueta caroliniana var. jacobinae

Piriqueta caroliniana var. jacobinae, commonly known as Jacob's piriqueta, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered inflorescences of white or pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in wetland environments, such as marshes and swamps, where it thrives in moist, sandy soils. Its reproductive strategy involves wind pollination, and it plays a role in supporting local insect populations.
