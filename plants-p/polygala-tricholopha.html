Polygala tricholopha

Polygala tricholopha, commonly known as the narrow-leaved milk-vetch, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its slender, often hairy stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. Its flowers are usually pale green or white, and it produces small, round pods that contain seeds.

It is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its delicate appearance.
