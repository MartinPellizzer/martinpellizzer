Potentilla salwinensis var. parviflora

Potentilla salwinensis var. parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, yellowish-orange flowers. The plant typically features palmately divided leaves and produces small, clustered flowers in the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in forested or rocky habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and limited distribution, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
