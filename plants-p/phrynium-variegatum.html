Phrynium variegatum

Phrynium variegatum, commonly known as the variegated phrynium, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often variegated with white or yellow markings. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous perennial, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters. Its foliage is popular in ornamental gardening due to its striking appearance.

It prefers moist, well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it suitable for shaded garden areas.
