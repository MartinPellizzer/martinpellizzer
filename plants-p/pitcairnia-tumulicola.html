Pitcairnia tumulicola

Pitcairnia tumulicola, commonly known as the Turpentine Pitcairnia, is a species of bromeliad native to the cloud forests of Ecuador.

It is characterized by its striking, spiky inflorescence that can reach up to 2 meters in height. The plant typically grows on tree trunks or branches, exhibiting an epiphytic growth habit. Its leaves are long, narrow, and covered in a dense layer of trichomes, which help in water retention.

The flowers are usually pink or purple, and the plant is noted for its ecological importance in supporting various insect and bird species.
