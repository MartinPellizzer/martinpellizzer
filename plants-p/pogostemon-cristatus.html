Pogostemon cristatus

Pogostemon cristatus, commonly known as the crested pogostemon, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its distinctive, rosette-like arrangement of leaves and its spikes of small, tubular flowers. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or stream banks.

Its foliage is often used in traditional medicine for its purported therapeutic properties.
