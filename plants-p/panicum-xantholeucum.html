Panicum xantholeucum

Panicum xantholeucum, commonly known as yellow panicgrass, is a flowering plant in the grass family Poaceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific Islands. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its inflorescence consists of a panicle, which is a branched cluster of small flowers, typically yellow or green in color.

Panicum xantholeucum is often found in disturbed soils and is used in some regions for forage or traditional medicine.
