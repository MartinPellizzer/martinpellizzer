Prunus incisa var. gracilis

Prunus incisa var. gracilis, commonly known as the slender cherry or Japanese slender cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its slender, arching branches and small, oval leaves that turn yellow in autumn. The plant produces clusters of white or pale pink flowers in early spring, followed by small, red berries in summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping.

Its compact growth habit and seasonal interest make it a valuable addition to gardens and natural landscapes.
