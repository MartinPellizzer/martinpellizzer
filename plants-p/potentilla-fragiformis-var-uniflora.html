Potentilla fragiformis var. uniflora

Potentilla fragiformis var. uniflora, commonly known as the single-flowered cinquefoil, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Arctic and alpine regions of North America.

It features small, yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, typically from July to August. The plant has wiry, branched stems covered in fine, soft hairs. Its leaves are alternate, with finely divided, thread-like lobes that are also hairy.

It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in tundra and subalpine habitats.
