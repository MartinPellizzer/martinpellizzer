Protea pauciflora

Protea pauciflora, commonly known as the few-flowered protea, is a South African native plant belonging to the Proteaceae family.

It is characterized by its striking, cone-shaped inflorescence that consists of numerous small, tubular flowers surrounded by modified leaves called bracts. The plant typically grows in coastal fynbos vegetation, thriving in well-drained, acidic soils. It produces bright yellow to orange flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and birds.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological importance, it is a popular choice for horticultural cultivation and conservation efforts.
