Phillyrea ellipticifolia subsp. bordoyana

Phillyrea ellipticifolia subsp.

bordoyana, commonly known as the Canary Island Phillyrea, is a shrub native to the Canary Islands. It is characterized by its small, elliptical, dark green leaves that are usually glossy and arranged alternately along the stems.

The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and summer months. Its fruit is a small, woody capsule that contains several seeds.

This subspecies is adapted to the dry, rocky environments of its native habitat and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.
