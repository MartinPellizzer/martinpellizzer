Portulaca oleracea subsp. rausii

Portulaca oleracea subsp.

rausii, commonly known as the Indian spinach or moss rose, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions. It is an annual herb with succulent, fleshy stems and leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating.

The plant produces small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in summer, and it is adapted to thrive in dry, sandy soils. Its leaves are edible and are used in various cuisines, particularly in South Asia.

The species is also known for its ability to survive in harsh environmental conditions due to its drought-resistant properties.
