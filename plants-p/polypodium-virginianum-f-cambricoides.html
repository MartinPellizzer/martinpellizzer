Polypodium virginianum f. cambricoides

Polypodium virginianum f. cambricoides, commonly known as the broad-leaf maidenhair fern, is a fern species characterized by its distinctive, broad, leathery fronds.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is native to eastern North America. The fronds are typically bipinnate, with a unique, elegant appearance that features a central rachis and closely spaced pinnae. This fern prefers moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in both horticulture and ecological studies.
