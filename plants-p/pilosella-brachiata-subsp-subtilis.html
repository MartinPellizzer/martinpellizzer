Pilosella brachiata subsp. subtilis

Pilosella brachiata subsp.

subtilis, commonly known as the slender leaf hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and ray florets.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are narrow and often covered in fine hairs, giving it a soft, fuzzy appearance.

It produces small yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months, attracting various pollinators.
