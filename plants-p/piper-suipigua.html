Piper suipigua

Piper suipigua, commonly known as the Amazonian pepper or wild pepper, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many species of pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves and small, clustered flowers that are typically green or yellowish in color. Its fruit, a small berry, contains the aromatic compounds responsible for its distinctive flavor.

Piper suipigua is often used in traditional medicine and has potential applications in modern pharmacology.
