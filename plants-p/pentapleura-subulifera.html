Pentapleura subulifera

Pentapleura subulifera, commonly known as the needleleaf pentapleura, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, needle-like leaves that are typically arranged in whorls around the stem. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

Due to its unique morphology and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
