Phanera glauca var. gandhiana

Phanera glauca var. gandhiana, commonly known as the silverleaf phanera, is a flowering plant native to parts of India and Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree with lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy, silvery coating. The flowers are typically white or pale pink, arranged in clusters, and bloom during the rainy season. The plant is characterized by its aromatic foliage and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and has traditional uses in local medicine.
