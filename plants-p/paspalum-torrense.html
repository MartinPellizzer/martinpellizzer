Paspalum torrense

Paspalum torrense, commonly known as the Torrens paspalum, is a perennial grass species native to South America, particularly Argentina and Chile.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, wiry stems that often form dense mats. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are typically green to reddish in color, containing numerous tiny florets. It is adapted to thrive in arid and semi-arid environments, making it a common species in dry grasslands and coastal areas.

Paspalum torrense is also valued for its use in erosion control and as a forage crop for livestock.
