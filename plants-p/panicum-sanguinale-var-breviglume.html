Panicum sanguinale var. breviglume

Panicum sanguinale var. breviglume, commonly known as red panicgrass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves with a reddish tinge, particularly along the midrib, which gives it its common name. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open areas such as roadsides and disturbed sites.

Its botanical characteristics make it adaptable to various environmental conditions, contributing to its widespread distribution.
