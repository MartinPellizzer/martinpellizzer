Pilocereus brunnowianus

Pilocereus brunnowianus, commonly known as the Brunnow's night bloomer, is a species of cactus native to South America.

It is characterized by its spiny stems and nocturnal blooming habits, with large, fragrant flowers that open at night. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its flowers are often pale yellow or white, and it is valued for both ornamental and medicinal purposes.

Pilocereus brunnowianus is a popular choice for xeriscaping due to its low water requirements and striking appearance.
