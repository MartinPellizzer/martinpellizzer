Primula sieboldii var. patens

Primula sieboldii var. patens, commonly known as the Japanese primrose, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Japan.

It features upright, slender stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, tubular flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant has broad, ovate leaves that are typically hairy and arranged in a basal rosette. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in shaded forest undergrowth.

The flowers bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
