Parahyparrhenia tridentata

Parahyparrhenia tridentata, commonly known as the three-toothed grass, is a perennial grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and compact growth habit, often forming dense mats on the ground. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems, supporting various wildlife species.
