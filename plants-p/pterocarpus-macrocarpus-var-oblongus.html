Pterocarpus macrocarpus var. oblongus

Pterocarpus macrocarpus var. oblongus, commonly known as the African shea tree, is a tropical tree native to Africa, particularly found in countries like Ghana and Nigeria.

It is characterized by its large, oblong leaves and produces woody pods containing seeds surrounded by a fatty, edible substance. The tree is valued for its hard, durable wood used in furniture and construction, as well as for its seeds, which are a source of shea butter.

It thrives in moist, tropical climates and plays an important role in local ecosystems and economies.
