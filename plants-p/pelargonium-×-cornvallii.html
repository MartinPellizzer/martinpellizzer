Pelargonium × cornvallii

Pelargonium × cornvallii, commonly known as the Cornish geranium, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Geraniaceae family.

It is native to the British Isles and is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, five-petaled flowers. The leaves are typically rounded with a slightly serrated edge, and the plant produces vibrant blooms in shades of red, pink, and white. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

This plant is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
