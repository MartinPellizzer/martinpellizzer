Prunus × lannesiana f. juzukakezakura

Prunus × lannesiana f. juzukakezakura, commonly known as the Japanese cherry blossom or some varieties as the "Yoshino" cherry, is a cultivar of the Prunus genus.

It is characterized by its delicate, early-blooming flowers that appear in shades of pink and white. The plant is a small to medium-sized deciduous tree with a rounded crown and smooth, gray bark. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

This cultivar is widely appreciated for its ornamental value, particularly during the spring blossom season.
