Peutalis divergens

Peutalis divergens, commonly known as the spreading bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its striking blue or violet bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in rocky or alpine regions.

Peutalis divergens is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
