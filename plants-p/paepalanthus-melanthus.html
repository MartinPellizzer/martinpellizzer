Paepalanthus melanthus

Paepalanthus melanthus, commonly known as the purple passionflower, is a striking member of the Passifloraceae family.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. The plant is characterized by its vibrant purple and white flowers, which feature intricate structures including a crown of filaments and a unique, spurred calyx. Its leaves are large, heart-shaped, and often have a velvety texture.

The botanical interest of Paepalanthus melanthus lies in its complex floral morphology and its role in supporting local pollinators.
