Primula calycantha

Primula calycantha, commonly known as the calycanthus, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its showy, fragrant flowers that range in color from pink to red, often with a distinctive yellow throat. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and features narrow, grass-like leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens.

The flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, adding ornamental value to the landscape.
