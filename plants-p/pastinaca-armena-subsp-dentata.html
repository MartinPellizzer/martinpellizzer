Pastinaca armena subsp. dentata

Pastinaca armena subsp.

dentata, commonly known as the Armenian burdock, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus. It features a thick, fleshy root that is often harvested for its edible qualities.

The plant has palmate leaves with lobed margins, and it produces small, yellowish flowers in clusters. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in rocky or forested areas.

Its botanical characteristics make it both a useful wild plant and a subject of botanical study.
