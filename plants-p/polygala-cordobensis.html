Polygala cordobensis

Polygala cordobensis, commonly known as the Cordoba milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Argentina.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its delicate, often branched stems and small, showy flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. Its flowers are usually pale pink or white, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Polygala cordobensis is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and ecological adaptations.
