Pouteria dasystyla var. abaetensis

Pouteria dasystyla var. abaetensis, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or araçá, is a tropical plant native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is a small to medium-sized tree characterized by its smooth, grey bark and elliptical leaves with a glossy upper surface. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, red, fleshy berries. Its fruit is highly valued for its sweet, juicy pulp and is often consumed fresh or used in culinary applications.

The plant also plays an important ecological role in supporting local wildlife and maintaining biodiversity in its habitat.
