Patagonium berteroi

Patagonium berteroi, commonly known as the Andean morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the high Andes of South America.

It is characterized by its striking violet-blue flowers that bloom in the early morning. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces long, coiling vines that can climb or trail along the ground. It is adapted to high-altitude environments with cool temperatures and strong sunlight.

Patagonium berteroi is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens in temperate regions.
