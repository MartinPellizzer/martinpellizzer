Platylophus

Platylophus, commonly known as the narrow-lobed helleborine, is a genus of flowering plants belonging to the orchid family, Orchidaceae.

It is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit and the presence of showy, often greenish or reddish flowers. The plants typically thrive in shaded, moist environments, such as woodlands and stream banks. Platylophus species are native to various regions in Asia, Africa, and the Americas.

Their botanical significance lies in their role as indicator species for certain ecological conditions and their potential medicinal uses.
