Phyllanthus spectabilis

Phyllanthus spectabilis, commonly known as the showy phyllanthus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, palmate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in dense clusters, which bloom during the wet season. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories or along riverbanks.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
