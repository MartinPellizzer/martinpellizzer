Pastinaca tricuspidata

Pastinaca tricuspidata, commonly known as the three-lobed burdock, is a perennial plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves with three distinct lobes, and it produces tall, spiny stems that bear purple or pink flower heads. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along streambanks. Its roots are thick and fibrous, and the plant is known for its ability to tolerate wet conditions.

It plays a role in supporting local wildlife and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
