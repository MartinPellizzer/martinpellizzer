Phaseolus perennis

Phaseolus perennis, commonly known as the everlasting bean, is a perennial legume native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its erect, branching stems and trifoliate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that develop into pods containing several oval, brown seeds. It is adapted to tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in dry, disturbed habitats.

Due to its hardy nature, it has been cultivated for both ornamental and traditional purposes.
