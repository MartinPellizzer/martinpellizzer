Parodia macednosa

Parodia macednosa, commonly known as the Macedonian candelabra cactus, is a species native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, branching stems that bear clusters of small, white flowers. The plant has a spiny, cylindrical stem structure with areoles that produce both spines and flowers. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
