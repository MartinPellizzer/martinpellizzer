Pastinaca sativa

Pastinaca sativa, commonly known as wild carrot or Queen Anne's lace, is a biennial herbaceous plant native to Europe, Western Asia, and North Africa.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes other familiar plants like parsley and dill. The plant features a taproot that is often harvested for culinary or medicinal purposes. Its feathery, umbrella-like flowers are white with a single pink or purple flower in the center.

The leaves are finely divided and aromatic, releasing a strong scent when crushed.
