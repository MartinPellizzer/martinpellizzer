Persicaria dichopoda

Persicaria dichopoda, commonly known as twinleaf knotweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to eastern North America.

It typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy on the underside. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters during the summer months, which are borne on slender, ascending pedicels. Its root system is fibrous and rhizomatous, allowing it to spread vegetatively and form dense colonies.

Persicaria dichopoda is often found in moist, disturbed habitats such as stream banks and wet meadows.
