Pyrenaria serrata var. kunstleri

Pyrenaria serrata var. kunstleri, commonly known as the Kunstler's serrated pyrenaria, is a rare and distinctive plant species native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its serrated leaves and clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as along stream banks or in dense undergrowth. Its botanical features include a slender stem and lanceolate foliage with finely toothed margins.

Due to its limited distribution and unique morphology, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
