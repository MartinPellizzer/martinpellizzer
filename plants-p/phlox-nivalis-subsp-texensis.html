Phlox nivalis subsp. texensis

Phlox nivalis subsp.

texensis, commonly known as the Texas snowplant, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the southern United States. It is characterized by its dense clusters of fragrant, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in early spring.

The plant typically thrives in shaded, moist environments such as woodland floors and rocky slopes. Its leaves are narrow and evergreen, providing year-round foliage.

It is well adapted to acidic soils and is often found in areas with high humidity and cool temperatures.
