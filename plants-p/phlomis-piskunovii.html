Phlomis piskunovii

Phlomis piskunovii, commonly known as the Balkan plumeless verbena, is a perennial herb native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It belongs to the family Lamiaceae, which includes many aromatic plants. The plant features upright, branched stems covered in fine hairs and produces clusters of small, pale blue to purple flowers. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a rough texture.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy addition to rock gardens and dry landscapes.
