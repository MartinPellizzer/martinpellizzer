Phaius warpuri

Phaius warpuri, commonly known as the Warburton's marsh orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and large, showy flowers that typically bloom in late summer. The plant prefers wet or swampy habitats and is often found in forested areas with high humidity. Its leaves are elongated and grass-like, while the flowers are usually purple or violet in color.

Phaius warpuri plays an important ecological role in its native environment, supporting various pollinators and contributing to local biodiversity.
