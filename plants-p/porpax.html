Porpax

Porpax, commonly known as the common mallow, is a herbaceous plant belonging to the Malvaceae family.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves and showy, pink or purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is found in various parts of Europe and Asia. Porpax has a fibrous root system and produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small seeds.

It is often used in traditional medicine for its purported soothing properties.
