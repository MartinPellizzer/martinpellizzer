Pilea nummulariifolia

Pilea nummulariifolia, commonly known as the coin plant, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It features rounded, waxy leaves that resemble small coins, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers that bloom in spring. It thrives in bright, indirect light and prefers well-draining soil.

Pilea nummulariifolia is popular as a houseplant due to its attractive foliage and low maintenance requirements.
