Pteris excelsa var. inaequalis

Pteris excelsa var. inaequalis, commonly known as the unequal fern, is a variant of the common water fern.

It is a tropical fern characterized by its delicate, lace-like fronds that are typically bioluminescent in certain conditions. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its fronds are typically bipinnate, with a distinctive asymmetrical pattern that gives it its common name.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture and is often cultivated as a decorative plant.
