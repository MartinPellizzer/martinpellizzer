Phacelia californica f. immunda

Phacelia californica f. immunda, commonly known as the California phacelia, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. The plant features small, clustered blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are usually narrow and lance-shaped, with a hairy texture.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is often used in native plant gardens.
