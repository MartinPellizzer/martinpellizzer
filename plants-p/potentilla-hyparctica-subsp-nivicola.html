Potentilla hyparctica subsp. nivicola

Potentilla hyparctica subsp.

nivicola, commonly known as the alpine potentilla, is a low-growing shrub native to mountainous regions of Europe. It thrives in alpine and subalpine habitats, typically found in rocky or gravelly soils at high elevations.

The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow, dark green, and often covered in a fine layer of silky hairs.

This subspecies is valued for its hardiness and ability to withstand harsh environmental conditions.
