Phacelophrynium cylindricum

Phacelophrynium cylindricum, commonly known as the cylindrical phacelophrynium, is a member of the Araceae family, characterized by its unique botanical features.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa and is known for its striking, elongated leaves that can grow up to several feet in length. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in a spadix, typical of the Araceae family, which is enclosed by a modified leaf called a spathe. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense clumps in moist, shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in tropical gardens and greenhouses.
