Persica vulgaris f. sanguinea

Persica vulgaris f. sanguinea, commonly known as the blood-red peach.

This is a cultivar of the peach tree known for its striking red-pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant features a deciduous habit with alternate, ovate leaves that are dark green and glossy. Its flowers are showy and attract pollinators, contributing to its ornamental value.

While it is primarily grown for its floral display, it can also produce small, tart fruits.
