Panicum laevicaule

Panicum laevicaule, commonly known as silky panicgrass, is a low-growing, perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It features slender, cylindrical stems covered in soft, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are typically light green to brown in color. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
