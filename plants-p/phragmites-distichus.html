Phragmites distichus

Phragmites distichus, commonly known as narrowleaf common reed, is a tall, perennial grass that thrives in wetland environments.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. This plant forms dense stands that can dominate wetland ecosystems, often outcompeting other vegetation. Its rhizomes spread horizontally, allowing it to colonize large areas quickly.

Phragmites distichus plays a crucial role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
