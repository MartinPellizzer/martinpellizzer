Plectranthus leiperi

Plectranthus leiperi, commonly known as the Cape hybrid geranium, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a herbaceous perennial with opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are often hairy and have a slightly aromatic scent. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers in shades of pink, purple, or white, typically blooming from spring to autumn. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden borders and containers.

Its adaptability and ornamental value contribute to its use in both ornamental horticulture and traditional medicine.
