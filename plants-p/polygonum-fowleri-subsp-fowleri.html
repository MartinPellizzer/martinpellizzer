Polygonum fowleri subsp. fowleri

Polygonum fowleri subsp.

fowleri, commonly known as the red-rooted beggar-ticks, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to North America. It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks.

The plant features upright stems that are often reddish in color, and its leaves are lance-shaped with a slightly hairy texture. It produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by distinctive, three-lobed seed capsules.

The species is notable for its adaptability to wet environments and its role in supporting local wildlife.
