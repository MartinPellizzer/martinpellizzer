Pleopeltis angustatodecurrens

Pleopeltis angustatodecurrens, commonly known as the narrow-leaf rock fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Polypodiaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped fronds that are typically less than 30 centimeters in length. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found on rock surfaces or in forest understories. Its rhizomes are wiry and creeping, allowing it to spread along rocky substrates.

The leaves are usually glossy and have a delicate, feathery appearance, making it a distinctive member of the fern family.
