Panicum cristatum

Panicum cristatum, commonly known as the crested grass, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its distinctive, tufted inflorescences that resemble a crown, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as wetlands and prairies. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue, and it produces small, delicate spikelets that are wind-pollinated.

Panicum cristatum is often used in ornamental gardening for its unique appearance and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
