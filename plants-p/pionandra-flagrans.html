Pionandra flagrans

Pionandra flagrans, commonly known as the flame flower, is a tropical plant native to regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular flowers that range in color from red to orange, often resembling flames. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are opposite, elliptical, and have a smooth, glossy surface.

Pionandra flagrans thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
