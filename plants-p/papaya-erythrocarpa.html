Papaya erythrocarpa

Papaya erythrocarpa, commonly known as the red-fruited papaya, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a herbaceous plant that typically grows in tropical and subtropical climates. The plant features large, lobed leaves and produces yellow flowers that develop into the characteristic red, fleshy fruit. The fruit is rich in enzymes like papain, which is used in food processing and medicine.

Its botanical structure reflects adaptations to its humid, warm environment, supporting its growth in diverse ecological settings.
