Potentilla hispanica var. agrimonioides

Potentilla hispanica var. agrimonioides, commonly known as Spanish cinquefoil, is a perennial shrub native to southern Europe.

It typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in full sun. The plant produces showy yellow flowers in early summer, which are clustered in cymes. Its leaves are compound, consisting of five oval leaflets, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It is valued in horticulture for its hardiness and ornamental value in gardens.
