Pleopeltis polylepis

Pleopeltis polylepis, commonly known as the giant tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate fronds that can reach several meters in length. The plant has a distinctive crown of fertile fronds that produce spores, contributing to its reproductive cycle. Its rhizome system is robust, allowing it to anchor firmly in moist, shaded environments.

This species is often found in tropical rainforests, where it thrives in high humidity and partial shade.
