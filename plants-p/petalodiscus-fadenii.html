Petalodiscus fadenii

Petalodiscus fadenii, commonly known as the velvetleaf petalodiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine, velvety hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the early morning. Its seeds are enclosed in fleshy, berry-like structures that aid in seed dispersal by animals.

Petalodiscus fadenii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
