Pyrus centralasiatica

Pyrus centralasiatica, commonly known as the Central Asian pear, is a deciduous tree native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves and fragrant white flowers. The tree produces pome fruits that are typically sweet and juicy, with a texture similar to other pear varieties. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This species is valued both for its ornamental appeal and its edible fruit.
