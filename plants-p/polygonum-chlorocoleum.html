Polygonum chlorocoleum

Polygonum chlorocoleum, commonly known as the blue false indigo, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, which includes plants known for their distinctive leaf structures and sometimes edible parts. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a bluish-green hue and produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pale blue or purple. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and along streambanks.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species for ecological studies and traditional herbal uses.
