Plantago evacina

Plantago evacina, commonly known as narrowleaf plantain, is a perennial herb native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It features upright, hairy stems that bear lance-shaped leaves with a waxy coating. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in dense clusters, which are borne on slender, branching stalks. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their mild astringent properties.

Botanically, it belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and fibrous root system.
