Papaver betonicifolium

Papaver betonicifolium, commonly known as the betonicifolium poppy, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its delicate, blue to violet flowers and heart-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in sunny conditions. It belongs to the Papaveraceae family, which includes other well-known species like the opium poppy.

Its flowers bloom in early spring, adding a splash of color to its natural habitat.
