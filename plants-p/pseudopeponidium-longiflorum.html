Pseudopeponidium longiflorum

Pseudopeponidium longiflorum, commonly known as the long-flowered false cucumber, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is a climbing vine characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and yellow-orange flowers with long, slender petals. The plant produces small, green, edible fruits that turn orange when mature. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in tropical rainforests.

Its botanical features make it a unique addition to both horticultural collections and ecological studies.
