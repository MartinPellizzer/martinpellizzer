Pleurothallis insularis

Pleurothallis insularis, commonly known as the island orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact, epiphytic growth habit. The plant typically features slender, branching stems and produces small, delicate flowers in shades of white and yellow. Its leaves are usually narrow and arranged in a rosette pattern, which helps it conserve moisture in its natural habitat.

Pleurothallis insularis is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts for its unique floral display.
