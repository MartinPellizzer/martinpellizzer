Polygonum leptocladum

Polygonum leptocladum, commonly known as slender-stemmed knotweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, which includes plants commonly referred to as buckwheats and sorrels. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its slender stems are often reddish in color, and it produces small, clustered flowers in late summer.

The leaves are lance-shaped with a distinctive waxy coating, contributing to its unique botanical appearance.
