Papiliopsis

Papiliopsis, commonly known as the "flowering sea grass," is a genus of flowering plants found primarily in coastal regions.

These plants are characterized by their delicate, grass-like appearance and their ability to thrive in saline environments. Papiliopsis species often have small, clustered flowers that are adapted to withstand coastal winds and salt spray. They play an important role in stabilizing sand dunes and preventing erosion.

Due to their unique adaptations, they are of interest to botanists studying halophytes.
