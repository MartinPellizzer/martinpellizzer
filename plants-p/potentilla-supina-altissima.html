Potentilla supina altissima

Potentilla supina altissima, commonly known as the tall trailing cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a low-growing shrub that typically reaches heights of up to 30 centimeters, with sprawling stems that often lie close to the ground. The plant features small, yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are palmately divided into five ovate lobes, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Potentilla supina altissima is well adapted to rocky and arid environments, showcasing its resilience in harsh conditions.
