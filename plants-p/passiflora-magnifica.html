Passiflora magnifica

Passiflora magnifica, commonly known as the magnificent passionflower, is a striking climbing vine native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that display a complex arrangement of petals, sepals, and filaments. The plant produces vibrant, often bi-color blooms in shades of red, yellow, and white. Its fruit, a woody capsule, contains inedible, hairy seeds.

Passiflora magnifica is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in warm climates.
