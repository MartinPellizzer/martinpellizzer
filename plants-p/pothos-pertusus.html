Pothos pertusus

Pothos pertusus, commonly known as the creeping pothos, is a tropical vine native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are typically bright green with a waxy texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers, though it is primarily grown for its foliage rather than its blooms. It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often used as a decorative houseplant.

Its ability to climb and trail makes it suitable for hanging baskets or shelves.
