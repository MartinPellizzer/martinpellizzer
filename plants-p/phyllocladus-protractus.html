Phyllocladus protractus

Phyllocladus protractus, commonly known as the narrowleaf tea tree, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, elongated leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white wax. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by woody, cone-like structures that contain the seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to dry, sunny conditions.

The species is notable for its aromatic foliage and historical use in traditional medicine.
