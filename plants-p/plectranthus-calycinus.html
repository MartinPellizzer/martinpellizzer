Plectranthus calycinus

Plectranthus calycinus, commonly known as the Cape verbena, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with a hairy texture and produces clusters of small, tubular flowers in shades of blue, purple, and white. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens and containers.

Plectranthus calycinus is also valued for its aromatic foliage and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
