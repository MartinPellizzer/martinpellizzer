Panicum polycladum

Panicum polycladum, commonly known as red panicgrass, is a warm-season grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and bright red to bronze-colored foliage, which becomes more pronounced in the fall. The plant produces small, fluffy seed heads that are dispersed by wind, aiding in its spread. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it a common ornamental and erosion control plant.

Its drought tolerance and ability to form a dense ground cover make it a valuable plant in xeriscaping and wildlife habitats.
