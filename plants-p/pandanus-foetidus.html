Pandanus foetidus

Pandanus foetidus, commonly known as the stinking pandanus, is a flowering plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It is a member of the Pandanaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sword-shaped leaves that are often used in traditional crafts. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. Its fruit is a fibrous structure that contains a single seed, which is dispersed by wind.

Pandanus foetidus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and its utility in local cultures.
