Phlox amplexicaulis

Phlox amplexicaulis, commonly known as the clasping phlox, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features showy, clustered flowers that range in color from pink to red, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has distinctive foliage with leaves that wrap around the stems, giving it a unique, compact appearance. It prefers moist, well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for wetland gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its fragrant blooms and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
