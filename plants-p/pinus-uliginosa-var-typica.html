Pinus uliginosa var. typica

Pinus uliginosa var. typica, commonly known as the black spruce, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to North America.

It is characterized by its dark green, needle-like leaves and conical shape, typically growing in cold, boreal climates. The tree produces cones that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind, aiding in its propagation. It thrives in wet, acidic soils, often found in peat bogs and swampy areas.

This species plays a vital role in forest ecosystems, providing habitat for various wildlife species.
