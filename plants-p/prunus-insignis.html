Prunus insignis

Prunus insignis, commonly known as the Chilean cherry or the purple cherry, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the Andes mountains of Chile and is characterized by its small, dark purple to black fruits. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a rough texture, while its flowers are white or pale pink.

Prunus insignis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
