Primula maccalliana

Primula maccalliana, commonly known as the MacCall's primrose, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and has lance-shaped leaves with a hairy texture. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.

The species is also noted for its ability to thrive in cooler climates, making it a popular choice for alpine gardens.
