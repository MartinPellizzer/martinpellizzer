Panicum catumbense

Panicum catumbense, commonly known as catumbense grass, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces panicles of small, spikelet-like flowers, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as savannas and disturbed areas.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes used in landscaping or as a forage crop in certain regions.
