Podocarpus madagascariensis subsp. procerus

Podocarpus madagascariensis subsp.

procerus, commonly known as the Madagascan podocarp, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to Madagascar. It belongs to the Podocarpaceae family and is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and slender, cylindrical cones.

The plant is typically found in humid, tropical rainforests, where it thrives in shaded, moist environments. Its wood is dense and durable, making it valuable for construction and furniture-making.

The subspecies procerus is distinguished by its elongated leaves and unique cone morphology compared to other Podocarpus species.
