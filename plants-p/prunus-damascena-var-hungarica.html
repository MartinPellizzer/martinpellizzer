Prunus damascena var. hungarica

Prunus damascena var. hungarica, commonly known as the Hungarian cherry or Hungarian sweet cherry, is a cultivar of the sweet cherry tree.

It is native to the Carpathian Basin and is characterized by its compact growth habit and early fruiting. The plant produces small, dark red to black fruits with a sweet, aromatic flavor. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires a cold winter to break dormancy and produce fruit.

This variety is valued for its ornamental value and is often grown in gardens for its beauty and edible yield.
