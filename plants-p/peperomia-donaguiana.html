Peperomia donaguiana

Peperomia donaguiana, commonly known as the velvetleaf peperomia, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub with compact foliage, and its leaves are usually dark green with a glossy appearance. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or red, and it thrives in bright, indirect light with well-draining soil.

Peperomia donaguiana is popular as a houseplant due to its low maintenance and ornamental appeal.
