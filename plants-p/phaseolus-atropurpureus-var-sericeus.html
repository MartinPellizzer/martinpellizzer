Phaseolus atropurpureus var. sericeus

Phaseolus atropurpureus var. sericeus, commonly known as the purple runner bean, is a climbing legume native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its vibrant purple stems and hairy, bean-like pods that turn green as they mature. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale purple flowers, which are followed by edible pods containing kidney-shaped seeds. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and culinary use in various cuisines.
