Pimelea acra

Pimelea acra, commonly known as the narrow-leafed candlebush, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Australia.

It is a shrub that typically grows in heathlands and dry sclerophyll forests, thriving in well-drained soils. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in spring. Its leaves are narrow and often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its characteristic appearance.

Pimelea acra is known for its resilience to drought and is often found in coastal and inland regions.
