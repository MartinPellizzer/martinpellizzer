Penaea ruscifolia

Penaea ruscifolia, commonly known as the silverleaf hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a silvery or grayish pubescence. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red, which bloom throughout the growing season. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Penaea ruscifolia is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
