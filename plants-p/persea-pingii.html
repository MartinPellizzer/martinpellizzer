Persea pingii

Persea pingii, commonly known as the Chinese persimmon, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae and is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves and fragrant flowers. The tree produces small, orange to red fruits that are edible when fully ripe. Its fruit is rich in nutrients and has a sweet, sometimes astringent flavor.

Persea pingii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit production in subtropical regions.
