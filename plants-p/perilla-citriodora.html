Perilla citriodora

Perilla citriodora, commonly known as lemon basil, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, lemon-scented leaves and small white or purple flowers. The plant belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes other herbs like mint and lavender. Its leaves are often used in cooking, especially in Thai and Vietnamese cuisines, and it is also valued for its ornamental qualities.

The plant prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.
