Phaleria octandra var. laurifolia

Phaleria octandra var. laurifolia, commonly known as the white betel tree, is a species of flowering plant in the family Lauraceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its evergreen leaves and fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows as a small to medium-sized tree, reaching heights of up to 10 meters. Its leaves are glossy and dark green, while its fruit is a small, yellowish drupe.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in some cultures.
