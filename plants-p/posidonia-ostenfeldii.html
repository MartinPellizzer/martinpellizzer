Posidonia ostenfeldii

Posidonia ostenfeldii, commonly known as the New Zealand seagrass, is a marine flowering plant that thrives in coastal waters of the South Pacific.

It forms dense underwater meadows that stabilize sediments and provide habitat for a variety of marine life. The plant has long, ribbon-like leaves that can grow up to several meters in length. It reproduces both sexually through flowers and asexually via rhizomes, which allow it to spread across the seafloor.

Posidonia ostenfeldii plays a crucial role in maintaining the health of marine ecosystems by improving water quality and supporting biodiversity.
