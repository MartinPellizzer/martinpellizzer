Pleurothallis pachypus

Pleurothallis pachypus, commonly known as the thick-leafed pleurothallis, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact, epiphytic growth habit and its ability to thrive in shaded, humid environments. The plant typically produces small, intricate flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, often arranged in a raceme. Its leaves are thick and fleshy, adapted to store water in its native habitat.

Pleurothallis pachypus is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
