Papaver divergens

Papaver divergens, commonly known as the broadleaf poppy, is a species of flowering plant in the family Papaveraceae.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia and is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant has distinctive broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white hairs. Its capsule fruit contains numerous small, dark seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Papaver divergens is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
