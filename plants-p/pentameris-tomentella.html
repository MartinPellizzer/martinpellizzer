Pentameris tomentella

Pentameris tomentella, commonly known as the hairy pentameris, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and thickets. The plant features small, white to pale pink flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in the spring. Its leaves are ovate to elliptic in shape and covered in a dense layer of fine, fuzzy hairs.

The species is notable for its ecological role in supporting local insect populations and its historical use in traditional herbal medicine.
