Passiflora condorita

Passiflora condorita, commonly known as the Andean passionflower, is a climbing vine native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes.

It is characterized by its striking, intricate flowers that feature a blend of blue, purple, and white petals, along with prominent red filaments. The plant produces edible, orange-yellow berries that are rich in nutrients. Its leaves are large, heart-shaped, and have a velvety texture.

Passiflora condorita is valued not only for its ornamental beauty but also for its potential medicinal properties.
