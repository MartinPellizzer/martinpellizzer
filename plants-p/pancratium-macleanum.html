Pancratium macleanum

Pancratium macleanum, commonly known as the Maclean's yellow bulbous plant, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn. The plant produces a bulbous stem that stores nutrients, allowing it to survive in arid conditions. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often with a bluish hue.

Pancratium macleanum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental flowers and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
