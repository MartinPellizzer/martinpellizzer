Pangium rumphii

Pangium rumphii, commonly known as the tropical apple tree, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree that can grow up to 10 meters in height. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by edible, round fruits resembling apples in appearance. The leaves are simple, alternate, and have a glossy, dark green surface.

It thrives in tropical climates with high humidity and well-drained soil.
