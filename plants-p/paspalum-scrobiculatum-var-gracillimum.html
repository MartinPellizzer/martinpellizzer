Paspalum scrobiculatum var. gracillimum

Paspalum scrobiculatum var. gracillimum, commonly known as slender paspalum, is a grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted foliage and slender, erect stems that often form dense clumps. The plant typically grows in moist to wet environments, such as marshes and swampy areas. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green coloration.

It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers, contributing to its ornamental value in wetland gardens.
