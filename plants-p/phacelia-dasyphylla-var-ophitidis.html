Phacelia dasyphylla var. ophitidis

Phacelia dasyphylla var. ophitidis, commonly known as the mountain phacelia, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or open habitats, often at higher elevations. Its flowering period occurs in the spring and early summer.

The species is valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and its use in restoration projects.
