Pedicularis jurana

Pedicularis jurana, commonly known as the yellow monkey orchid, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the genus Pedicularis, which includes approximately 450 species of flowering plants, many of which are found in the temperate and subarctic regions of the Northern Hemisphere. The plant is characterized by its striking yellow-orange flowers that resemble the face of a monkey, hence its common name. It thrives in rocky, alpine environments and is often found growing in open, sunny habitats.
