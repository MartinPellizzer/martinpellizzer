Pinus maximartinezii

Pinus maximartinezii, commonly known as the Mexican white pine, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, upward-curving needles that are typically 5 to 7 centimeters long. The tree produces male and female cones, with the female cones developing into woody seed capsules that release winged seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
