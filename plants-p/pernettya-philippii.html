Pernettya philippii

Pernettya philippii, commonly known as the Philippi's cowberry, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the temperate regions of the northern hemisphere.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its small, leathery leaves and clustered flowers. The plant produces bright red or orange berries that are edible but often have a tart flavor. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories or rocky habitats.

Pernettya philippii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
