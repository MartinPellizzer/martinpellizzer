Pseudosasa hamadae

Pseudosasa hamadae, commonly known as the Japanese black bamboo, is a clump-forming grass species native to Japan.

It features slender, dark green culms that are often streaked with black, giving it a striking appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by oval, pale green to yellowish fruits. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and landscapes for its elegant foliage and structural form.
