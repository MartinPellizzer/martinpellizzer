Pleurothallis boliviana

Pleurothallis boliviana, commonly known as the Bolivian spider orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a small, terrestrial orchid with delicate, often hairy leaves and a single, slender stem that produces a cluster of small, fragrant flowers. The flowers are typically white or pale yellow with intricate patterns, and they bloom during the dry season. This species is adapted to high-altitude environments and requires well-drained soil and bright, indirect light.

It is appreciated by orchid enthusiasts for its unique appearance and relatively easy care requirements.
