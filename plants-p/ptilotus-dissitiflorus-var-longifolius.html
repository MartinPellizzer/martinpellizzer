Ptilotus dissitiflorus var. longifolius

Ptilotus dissitiflorus var. longifolius, commonly known as the long-leaved spinifex, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, typically appearing in late winter to early spring. Its leaves are elongated and often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

It is adapted to arid environments and is often found in sandy or coastal regions.
