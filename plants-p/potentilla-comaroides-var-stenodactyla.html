Potentilla comaroides var. stenodactyla

Potentilla comaroides var. stenodactyla, commonly known as the narrowleaf cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in clusters of five, giving it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in native plant gardens.
