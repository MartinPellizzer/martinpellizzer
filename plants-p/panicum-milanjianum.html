Panicum milanjianum

Panicum milanjianum, commonly known as red panicgrass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and dense, feathery inflorescences that produce small, red bracts. The plant thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it adaptable to various environmental conditions. Its rapid growth and ability to form dense stands make it both a useful forage crop and a potential weed.

Botanically, it belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes many other grasses found worldwide.
