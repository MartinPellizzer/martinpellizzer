Potentilla parvifolia var. hypoleuca

Potentilla parvifolia var. hypoleuca, commonly known as the white-flowered cinquefoil, is a low-growing perennial shrub native to the western United States.

It features small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has slender, wiry stems and small, oval-shaped leaves with a finely toothed margin. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable addition to native plant gardens and ecological restoration projects.
