Paphiopedilum insigne f. sanderae

Paphiopedilum insigne f. sanderae, commonly known as the Sanderae Cymbidium, is a rare and highly sought-after orchid species native to the Philippines.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety petals and a unique, spurred lip that sets it apart from other orchid varieties. The plant typically grows as a terrestrial orchid, often found in shaded, humid forest understories. Its flowers, which can range in color from white to pale pink, bloom in late summer or early autumn.

Due to its rarity and beauty, it is a prized specimen among orchid enthusiasts and conservationists alike.
