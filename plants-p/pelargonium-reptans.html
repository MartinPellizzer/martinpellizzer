Pelargonium reptans

Pelargonium reptans, commonly known as the creeping geranium, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and produces clusters of small, pink or red flowers. The leaves are typically rounded and have a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

This species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
