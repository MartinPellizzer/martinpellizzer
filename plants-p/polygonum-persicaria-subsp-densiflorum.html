Polygonum persicaria subsp. densiflorum

Polygonum persicaria subsp.

densiflorum, commonly known as red bryony, is a flowering plant characterized by its upright growth habit and dense clusters of small, reddish-pink flowers. It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, which includes plants known for their distinctive bracts and often edible seeds.

The subspecies is distinguished by its more compact inflorescence and darker flower color compared to the typical P. persicaria. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in wetlands, ditches, and along stream banks.

This plant is also noted for its ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes, making it a common weed in various habitats.
