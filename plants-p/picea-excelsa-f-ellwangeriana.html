Picea excelsa f. ellwangeriana

Picea excelsa f. ellwangeriana, commonly known as the Ellwangerian white spruce, is a variant of the white spruce native to the high altitudes of the Andes in South America.

This subspecies is characterized by its slender, conical shape and dense foliage, with silvery-white undersides on its needles. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is adapted to the harsh conditions of mountainous regions. The plant has small, cone-like seed structures that disperse windborne seeds, aiding in its propagation.

Its unique appearance and ecological significance make it a notable species in its native habitat.
