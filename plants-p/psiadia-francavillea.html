Psiadia francavillea

Psiadia francavillea, commonly known as the French Island dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers that develop into fluffy, white seed heads, typical of the Asteraceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Psiadia francavillea is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
