Potamogeton berchtoldii var. tenuissimus

Potamogeton berchtoldii var. tenuissimus, commonly known as slender pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in North America.

It features long, slender, ribbon-like leaves that are typically less than 1 millimeter wide, giving it a delicate appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually arranged in clusters, and it reproduces both sexually and vegetatively. It thrives in shallow, slow-moving waters and plays a role in providing habitat for aquatic invertebrates and fish.

Its presence is often an indicator of good water quality in its native habitats.
