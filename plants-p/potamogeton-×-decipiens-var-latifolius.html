Potamogeton × decipiens var. latifolius

Potamogeton × decipiens var. latifolius, commonly known as the broadleaf pondweed, is a hybrid aquatic plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its broad, elongated leaves that are typically arranged in whorls around the stem. This plant thrives in freshwater environments such as lakes, ponds, and slow-moving rivers. Its flowers are small and green, often clustered in dense spikes, and it produces small, three-lobed seeds.

The plant plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various water organisms.
