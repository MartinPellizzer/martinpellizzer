Poa coloradensis

Poa coloradensis, commonly known as the Colorado bluegrass, is a perennial grass native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its fine, blue-green blades and forms dense, mat-like growth patterns. The plant typically grows in open, sunny areas such as meadows and disturbed soils. It has a fibrous root system that helps stabilize soil and prevent erosion.

Poa coloradensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native plant landscapes.
