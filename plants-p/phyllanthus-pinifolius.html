Phyllanthus pinifolius

Phyllanthus pinifolius, commonly known as the pineleaf phyllanthus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa, Asia, and Australia.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows up to 3 meters in height, featuring compound leaves with serrated margins. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by small, round fruits containing a single seed. Its leaves are characterized by their needle-like appearance, giving it the common name "pineleaf.".

Phyllanthus pinifolius is often found in dry, open habitats and is used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
