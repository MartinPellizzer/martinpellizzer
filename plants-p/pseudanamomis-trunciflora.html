Pseudanamomis trunciflora

Pseudanamomis trunciflora, commonly known as the truncated-flowered pseudanamomis, is a rare and specialized plant species native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique leaf morphology, featuring truncate (cut-off) margins that distinguish it from related species. The plant produces small, clustered flowers with a distinctive coloration that aids in pollination by specific insect species. Its growth habit is typically shrub-like, with woody stems and dense foliage.

Due to habitat loss and limited distribution, it is considered endangered and requires conservation efforts to ensure its survival.
