Philadelphus floridus

Philadelphus floridus, commonly known as the Florida mock orange, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are ovate to elliptical in shape. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

The Florida mock orange is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
