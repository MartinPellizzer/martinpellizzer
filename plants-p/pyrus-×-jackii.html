Pyrus × jackii

Pyrus × jackii, commonly known as the Japanese white pear.

It is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Pyrus pyrifolia (Chinese white pear) and Pyrus communis (European pear). This plant is characterized by its large, white to yellowish fruit with a smooth skin. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

The tree typically reaches heights of 4–6 meters and produces fruit in late summer to early autumn.
