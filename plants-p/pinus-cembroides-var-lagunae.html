Pinus cembroides var. lagunae

Pinus cembroides var. lagunae, commonly known as the Mexican cliffrose or desert cliffrose, is a variant of the alligator juniper.

It is a shrub or small tree native to the Chihuahuan Desert in Mexico and the southwestern United States. The plant is characterized by its dense, needle-like leaves and small, clustered cones. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Its botanical features make it a resilient species capable of surviving in harsh desert conditions.
