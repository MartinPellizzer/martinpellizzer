Paepalanthus columbiensis

Paepalanthus columbiensis, commonly known as the Columbia pincushion cactus, is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its dense, spiny stems that resemble a pincushion. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. It is typically found in rocky or sandy soils in arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
