Phaseoloides speciosum

Phaseoloides speciosum, commonly known as the showy phaseoloid, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that typically bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has compound leaves with pinnate arrangement, and its stems are often hairy. It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is known for its leguminous plants capable of nitrogen fixation.

The fruit is a legume pod that contains several seeds, contributing to its role in ecological systems.
