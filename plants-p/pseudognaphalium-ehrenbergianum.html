Pseudognaphalium ehrenbergianum

Pseudognaphalium ehrenbergianum, commonly known as the Mexican everlasting or blue everlasting, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

The plant produces small, blue to purple flowers that are tightly packed in compact, spherical clusters. It is a perennial herb that thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Its foliage is typically gray-green with a woolly texture, contributing to its drought tolerance and ornamental appeal.
