Piper barbatum var. andicola

Piper barbatum var. andicola, commonly known as the Andean pepper plant, is a variant of the genus Piper native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a climbing shrub characterized by its small, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces small, peppery fruits that are used in traditional Andean cuisine. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in cloud forests.

Its botanical significance lies in its unique adaptations to high-altitude environments and its role in local biodiversity.
