Philodendron ecordatum

Philodendron ecordatum, commonly known as the velvetleaf philodendron, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of Central and South America.

It features heart-shaped, glossy leaves that are often covered in a velvety texture, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers, which are typically followed by orange-red berries. It thrives in warm, humid environments with bright, indirect light.

Philodendron ecordatum is popular as a houseplant due to its attractive foliage and relatively easy care requirements.
