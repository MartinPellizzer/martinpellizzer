Potentilla sylvestris var. pubescens

Potentilla sylvestris var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it its botanical name. The plant produces clusters of small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It typically grows in moist, woodland areas and thrives in partial to full shade.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
