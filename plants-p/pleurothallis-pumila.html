Pleurothallis pumila

Pleurothallis pumila, commonly known as the dwarf pleurothallis, is a species of orchid native to the cloud forests of Central and South America.

It is a small, terrestrial orchid that typically grows close to the ground, often in shaded, humid environments. The plant features delicate, narrow leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually white or pale green. It is known for its ability to thrive in low-light conditions and requires consistent moisture in its habitat.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological niche, it is a subject of interest for botanists and orchid enthusiasts alike.
