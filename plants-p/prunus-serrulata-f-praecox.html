Prunus serrulata f. praecox

Prunus serrulata f. praecox, commonly known as the early cherry or Japanese early cherry, is a distinct form of the Japanese cherry blossom.

It is characterized by its early blooming habit, producing delicate pink flowers before the full-sized Prunus serrulata. The plant is a deciduous shrub or small tree, typically reaching heights of 3 to 6 meters. Its leaves are oval-shaped with serrated edges, and the bark is smooth and reddish-brown.

It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its beautiful floral display.
