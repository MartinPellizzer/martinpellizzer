Pancheria engleriana var. potamophila

Pancheria engleriana var. potamophila, commonly known as the river pancheria, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found near rivers and streams. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a velvety texture, while its stems are often hairy.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with appropriate humidity and light conditions.
