Physalis cinerascens var. spathulifolia

Physalis cinerascens var. spathulifolia, commonly known as the broad-leafed groundcherry, is a herbaceous plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes tomatoes and potatoes, and is characterized by its hairy stems and oval-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that develop into inflated, lantern-like fruits encasing orange or yellow seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
