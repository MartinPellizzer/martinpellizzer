Polygonum pseudoarenarium

Polygonum pseudoarenarium, commonly known as the sand polygon or desert knotweed, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in sandy or rocky soils, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. The plant features slender, branched stems and small, clustered leaves that help reduce water loss. Its flowers are usually pink or purple, appearing in late summer, and are clustered in dense inflorescences.

Polygonum pseudoarenarium is valued for its resilience and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects in degraded landscapes.
