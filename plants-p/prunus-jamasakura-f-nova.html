Prunus jamasakura f. nova

Prunus jamasakura f. nova, commonly known as the Japanese cherry blossom variant, is a cultivar of the Prunus jamasakura species, which is native to Japan.

It is characterized by its delicate, early-blooming flowers that typically appear in late winter to early spring. The plant is a deciduous tree with a compact growth habit and small, ovate leaves that turn yellow in autumn. Its flowers are pale pink to white, with five petals, and are highly valued for their ornamental beauty.

This cultivar is often planted in gardens and public spaces for its aesthetic appeal and cultural significance.
