Polygala disperma

Polygala disperma, commonly known as the scattered pogostemon, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and thrives in full sun. Its flowers are usually pale green or yellowish, with a distinctive hooded structure.

It is often found in coastal and inland regions, contributing to the biodiversity of its native habitats.
