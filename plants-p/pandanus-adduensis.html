Pandanus adduensis

Pandanus adduensis, commonly known as the Addu pandanus, is a species of palm native to the Maldives.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and long, narrow leaves that are often used in traditional crafts. The plant thrives in coastal areas and is adapted to saline and sandy soils. It produces small, fragrant flowers that develop into edible fruits.

The Addu pandanus plays an important role in the local ecosystem and cultural practices of the region.
