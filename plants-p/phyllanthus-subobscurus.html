Phyllanthus subobscurus

Phyllanthus subobscurus, commonly known as the dark-leaved spurge, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and riverbanks. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are usually dark green with a glossy surface. Its small, yellowish-green flowers are clustered in dense inflorescences, and it produces small, spherical berries that contain the seeds.

Phyllanthus subobscurus is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
