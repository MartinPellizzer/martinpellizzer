Pandanus intraconicus

Pandanus intraconicus, commonly known as the narrow-leaf pandanus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Pandanaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including parts of Indonesia and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender leaves that are often used in traditional crafts and as a source of fiber. Its flowers are small and clustered, typically appearing in panicles at the tips of the leaves.

The plant thrives in humid, coastal environments and is often found growing near mangroves or in sandy soils.
