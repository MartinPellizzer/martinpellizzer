Piper griseocaule

Piper griseocaule, commonly known as the gray-stemmed pepper plant, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its slender, grayish stems and small, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into round, peppery fruits. It is often cultivated for its aromatic foliage and potential medicinal properties.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for horticultural and ethnobotanical studies.
