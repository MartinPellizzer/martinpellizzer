Phlomoides setigera

Phlomoides setigera, commonly known as the slender phlomis, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant features slender, upright stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its small, pale blue to purple flowers are clustered in whorls along the stem, blooming in late spring to early summer.

Phlomoides setigera is typically found in rocky soils and arid environments, making it well adapted to drought conditions.
