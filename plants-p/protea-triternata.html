Protea triternata

Protea triternata, commonly known as the three-part protea, is a striking South African plant belonging to the Proteaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescences composed of numerous petal-like bracts that surround small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in shrubland and fynbos vegetation, thriving in nutrient-poor soils. Its flowers come in a range of colors, from white to pink and red, making it a popular ornamental plant.

Protea triternata plays an important ecological role, providing nectar for pollinators such as birds and insects.
