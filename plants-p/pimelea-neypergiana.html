Pimelea neypergiana

Pimelea neypergiana, commonly known as the Narrow-leaved Riceflower, is an evergreen shrub native to the south-western region of Western Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers during the spring season. The plant is adapted to sandy soils and can thrive in coastal and heathland environments. It is also known for its ability to withstand drought conditions due to its deep root system.

Pimelea neypergiana is a popular ornamental plant in gardens due to its attractive appearance and hardy nature.
