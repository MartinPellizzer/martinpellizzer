Podochilus rupicola

Podochilus rupicola, commonly known as the rock orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It typically grows on rocky substrates and is characterized by its compact, often clustered growth habit. The plant features slender, erect stems that bear several lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are small and usually yellow or greenish-yellow, with a distinct lip that is often darker in color.

Podochilus rupicola is adapted to thrive in shaded, humid environments with good drainage.
