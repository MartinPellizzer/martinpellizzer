Phacelia simplex

Phacelia simplex, commonly known as blue phacelia, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its delicate, blue to violet flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant has slender, branching stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in open grasslands and rocky areas.

Phacelia simplex is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators.
