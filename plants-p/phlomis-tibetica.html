Phlomis tibetica

Phlomis tibetica, commonly known as Tibetan mullein, is a perennial herb native to the high altitudes of the Tibetan Plateau.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its tall, hairy stems and large, gray-green leaves. The plant produces showy yellow flowers in clusters, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, arid environments.

Phlomis tibetica is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and soothing properties.
