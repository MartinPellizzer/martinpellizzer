Piptatherum fedtschenkoi

Piptatherum fedtschenkoi, commonly known as the Mongolian grass, is a perennial grass species native to Central Asia.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as steppes and semi-deserts. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It is adapted to arid conditions with deep root systems for water absorption.

This species plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local ecosystems.
