Pinus armena var. parviflora

Pinus armena var. parviflora, commonly known as the Armenian pine, is a variant of the Armenian pine species native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus.

It is a coniferous evergreen tree characterized by its needle-like leaves and cone-bearing structure. The plant typically grows in temperate climates with well-drained soils and sufficient sunlight. Its cones are small and often remain closed for an extended period before releasing seeds.

This species plays a significant ecological role in its native habitat, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
