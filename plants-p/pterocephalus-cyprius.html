Pterocephalus cyprius

Pterocephalus cyprius, commonly known as the Cyprus lily, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking violet-blue flowers that bloom in summer, typically in clusters at the top of its slender stems. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is often found in rocky or dry habitats. It belongs to the Amaryllis family and is valued for its ornamental beauty.

However, it is considered endangered due to habitat loss and over-collection.
