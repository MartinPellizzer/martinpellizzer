Polygala kagerensis

Polygala kagerensis, commonly known as the Kager's milk-vetch, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid regions. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and potentially endangered species.
