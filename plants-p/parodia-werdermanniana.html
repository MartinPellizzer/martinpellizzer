Parodia werdermanniana

Parodia werdermanniana, commonly known as the Werdermann's cactus, is a compact, globular cactus native to southern Africa.

It features a ribbed, spiny stem with prominent areoles that bear white to yellow flowers. The plant is well adapted to arid environments, with a thick, fleshy stem that stores water. It produces small, delicate blooms during the cooler months, typically in spring or early summer.

Its slow growth rate and unique appearance make it a popular choice for rock gardens and indoor collections.
