Pandanus sierraleonensis

Pandanus sierraleonensis, commonly known as the Sierra Leone pandanus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Pandanaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of West Africa, particularly Sierra Leone. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, palm-like leaves that are often used in traditional crafts. Its inflorescence consists of a branched cluster of small, fragrant flowers, which are typically greenish-yellow in color.

This species plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
