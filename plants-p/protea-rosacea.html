Protea rosacea

Protea rosacea, commonly known as the pink sugarbowl, is a striking South African plant belonging to the Proteaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescence that resembles a sugar bowl, with numerous small, pinkish-white flowers clustered around a central stem. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to the Mediterranean climate of the region. Its flowers are pollinated by insects, particularly bees, and the plant is often found in fynbos vegetation.

Protea rosacea is valued for its ornamental beauty and is a symbol of the unique biodiversity of South Africa.
