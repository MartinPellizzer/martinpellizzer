Phragmipedium schlimii var. manzurii

Phragmipedium schlimii var. manzurii, commonly known as the Manzur's phragmipedium, is a rare and striking orchid native to the cloud forests of Ecuador.

It features elongated, lance-shaped leaves that are dark green and glossy, often with a waxy coating. The plant produces pendulous inflorescences with showy, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink with a distinctive yellow lip. It thrives in high humidity and partial shade, requiring well-draining, acidic soil.

This variant is prized by orchid enthusiasts for its unique floral structure and rarity in cultivation.
