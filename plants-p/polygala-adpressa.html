Polygala adpressa

Polygala adpressa, commonly known as the narrowleaf milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is a low-growing perennial herb that typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant produces small, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink, often clustered in dense spikes. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it thrives in sandy or gravelly soils.

Polygala adpressa is adapted to withstand drought and is often found in disturbed or open habitats.
