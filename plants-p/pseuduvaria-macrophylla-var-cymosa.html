Pseuduvaria macrophylla var. cymosa

Pseuduvaria macrophylla var. cymosa, commonly known as the clustered pseuduvaria, is a variant of the pseuduvaria genus native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, elongated leaves that are typically arranged in a clustered or whorled pattern along the stems. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are usually white or pale yellow, which are followed by elliptical, fleshy fruits. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in the study of tropical flora and horticulture.
