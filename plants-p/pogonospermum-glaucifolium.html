Pogonospermum glaucifolium

Pogonospermum glaucifolium, commonly known as the blueleaf pogonospermum, is a perennial herb native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its glaucous, waxy leaves that reflect sunlight and reduce water loss. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in the summer months. Its root system is deep and extensive, allowing it to access groundwater in dry environments.

Pogonospermum glaucifolium is adapted to harsh climates and plays a role in stabilizing sandy soils in its native habitat.
