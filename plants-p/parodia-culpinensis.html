Parodia culpinensis

Parodia culpinensis, commonly known as the Culpin's pincushion cactus, is a species native to Mexico.

It is a small, globular cactus with a distinctive appearance, featuring a dense covering of white to pale green spines. The plant has a short, thick stem that remains close to the ground, and its flowers are typically yellow or orange, appearing in the spring. It is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water once established.

Parodia culpinensis is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its unique and compact form.
