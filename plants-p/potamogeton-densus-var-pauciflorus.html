Potamogeton densus var. pauciflorus

Potamogeton densus var. pauciflorus, commonly known as the dense pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater habitats in North America.

It is characterized by its dense, branching stems and small, elongated leaves that are typically dark green in color. The plant produces few flowers, which are small and clustered, often appearing in late summer. It plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.

Its presence is often an indicator of good water quality in lakes and slow-moving rivers.
