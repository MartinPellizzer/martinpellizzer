Phyllanthus womersleyi

Phyllanthus womersleyi, commonly known as the Womersley's spurge, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its small, smooth, oval-shaped leaves that are typically arranged oppositely on the stems. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which are followed by small, round, greenish fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and dry forests.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional medicinal uses in local communities.
