Polystachya maculata subsp. maculata

Polystachya maculata subsp.

maculata, commonly known as the speckled orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to parts of Southeast Asia. This terrestrial herbaceous plant is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers.

It typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors or mountainous regions. The plant has linear to lanceolate leaves and produces a unique inflorescence with numerous tiny flowers arranged in a spike.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
