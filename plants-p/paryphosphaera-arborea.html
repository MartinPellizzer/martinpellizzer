Paryphosphaera arborea

Paryphosphaera arborea, commonly known as the golden bark tree, is a unique tropical plant characterized by its striking, peeling bark that reveals layers of golden-yellow tissue beneath.

It is native to the humid lowland forests of Southeast Asia, where it thrives in warm, moist conditions. The plant has a slow growth rate and can reach heights of up to 20 meters, forming a tall, slender trunk with a distinct, fluted appearance. Its leaves are broad and glossy, and it produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters.

Due to its ornamental value and rarity, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
