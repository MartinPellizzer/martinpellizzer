Piriqueta caroliniana var. integrifolia

Piriqueta caroliniana var. integrifolia, commonly known as the Carolina piriqueta, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It features slender, upright stems that can grow up to 2 meters tall, often branching near the base. The plant produces small, white to pale blue flowers arranged in dense, spike-like clusters. Its leaves are lance-shaped with smooth, entire margins, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.
