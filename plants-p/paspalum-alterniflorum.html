Paspalum alterniflorum

Paspalum alterniflorum, commonly known as the alternate-flowered grass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist to wet habitats, such as marshes and wetlands, and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Paspalum alterniflorum is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
