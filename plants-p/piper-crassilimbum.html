Piper crassilimbum

Piper crassilimbum, commonly known as the thick-leaved pepper plant, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes various pepper species. The plant is characterized by its large, thick, and glossy elliptical leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. Its small, greenish-yellow flowers are clustered in spikes, and it produces small, dark berries that are edible.

The plant is valued for its aromatic leaves and has been used in herbal remedies for its potential medicinal properties.
