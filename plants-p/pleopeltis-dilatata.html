Pleopeltis dilatata

Pleopeltis dilatata, commonly known as the broad-leafed rock fern, is a tropical fern native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its large, leathery, lance-shaped fronds. The plant typically grows on rocky surfaces or in shaded, moist environments. Its rhizomes are often covered in a dense, hairy layer that aids in water retention.

The fronds are usually bipinnate, with small, clustered sporangia on the underside of the pinnae.
