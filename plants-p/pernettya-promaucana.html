Pernettya promaucana

Pernettya promaucana, commonly known as the Kamchatka cowberry, is a shrub native to the Russian Far East and parts of northern Asia.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries. The plant produces small, bright red berries that are edible and often used in local cuisine. Its leaves are typically oval-shaped with a leathery texture, and it thrives in acidic, well-drained soils.

Pernettya promaucana is adapted to cold climates and can be found in forested areas and tundra-like environments.
