Polemonium flavum

Polemonium flavum, commonly known as the yellow Jacob's ladder, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Polemoniaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, cascading clusters of bright yellow flowers. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy to the touch. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a notable species for both ecological and ornamental purposes.
