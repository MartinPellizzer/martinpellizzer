Prasophyllum odoratum var. album

Prasophyllum odoratum var. album, commonly known as the white leek orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the temperate regions of southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and dense clusters of white or pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in heathlands and open forests, preferring well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are long, narrow, and grass-like, often forming a basal rosette.

The species is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
