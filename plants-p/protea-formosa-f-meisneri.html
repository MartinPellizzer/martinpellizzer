Protea formosa f. meisneri

Protea formosa f. meisneri, commonly known as the Meisner's protea, is a striking South African plant belonging to the Proteaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescences composed of numerous petal-like bracts that can range in color from white to pink and even red. The plant is native to the Western Cape region and thrives in well-drained, acidic soils. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and floral arrangements.

The species is also valued for its ecological role, providing nectar for pollinators such as birds and insects.
