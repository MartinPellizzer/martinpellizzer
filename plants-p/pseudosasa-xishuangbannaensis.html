Pseudosasa xishuangbannaensis

Pseudosasa xishuangbannaensis, commonly known as the Xishuangbanna bamboo, is a species native to southern China, particularly in Yunnan Province.

It is a clumping bamboo that typically grows in moist, shaded environments within tropical and subtropical forests. The plant has slender, culms that are often used in traditional crafts and construction due to their strength and flexibility. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a smooth, glossy surface, contributing to its ornamental value.

The plant plays an important ecological role in its native habitat by providing habitat for various wildlife species.
