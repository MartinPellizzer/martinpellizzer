Peperomia jalcaensis

Peperomia jalcaensis, commonly known as the Jalcaensis Peperomia, is a species native to the Andes region of South America.

It is a compact, evergreen shrub characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are often variegated with shades of green, white, and pink. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires bright, indirect light to flourish.

Peperomia jalcaensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and relatively low maintenance requirements, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.
