Phyllanthus holostylus

Phyllanthus holostylus, commonly known as the African starleaf, is a small shrub native to tropical and southern Africa.

It is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by spherical, fleshy fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or savanna habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal uses, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
