Potamogeton obtusifolius f. muticus

Potamogeton obtusifolius f. muticus, commonly known as the mute pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater habitats in Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves that are typically smooth and have a blunt tip, distinguishing it from other pondweed species. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually submerged, contributing to its "mute" appearance. It thrives in still or slow-moving waters with soft, muddy or sandy substrates.

This species plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
