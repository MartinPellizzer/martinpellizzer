Pimpinella austriaca var. dissecta

Pimpinella austriaca var. dissecta, commonly known as the Austrian cow parsley, is a biennial plant native to Central Europe.

It features finely dissected, fern-like leaves that are often bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in umbels, typical of the Apiaceae family. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks.
