Plagiopetalum esquirolii var. quadrangulum

Plagiopetalum esquirolii var. quadrangulum, commonly known as the quadrangular plagiopetalum, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive quadrangular (four-sided) stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

Due to its ornamental value and unique morphology, it is a sought-after plant among succulent enthusiasts.
