Pithecellobium matudae

Pithecellobium matudae, commonly known as the Matudae's pithecellobium, is a species of legume native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, clustered flowers that are typically yellow or yellow-green in color. The plant produces small, edible pods that contain several seeds, which are often used in local cuisines. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, well-drained soils.

The species is valued not only for its culinary uses but also for its role in ecological systems as a nitrogen-fixing plant.
