Persicaria cusickii

Persicaria cusickii, commonly known as Cusick's smartweed, is a flowering plant native to the central and eastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swales, and along stream banks. The plant produces small, pink to reddish flowers arranged in dense clusters, which bloom from late summer into early fall. Its leaves are alternate, ovate to elliptic, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Persicaria cusickii is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in wetland ecosystems.
