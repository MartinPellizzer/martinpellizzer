Primula tanneri subsp. nepalensis

Primula tanneri subsp.

nepalensis, commonly known as the Nepalese primrose, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayan regions of Nepal and northern India. It is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its low-growing habit and clustered rosettes of dark green, hairy leaves.

The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in forest understories or rocky slopes.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists alike.
