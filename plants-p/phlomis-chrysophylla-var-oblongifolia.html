Phlomis chrysophylla var. oblongifolia

Phlomis chrysophylla var. oblongifolia, commonly known as the yellow leaf phlomis, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It features erect, branched stems covered in soft, hairy foliage. The leaves are oblong and often have a yellowish-green hue, giving the plant its common name. It produces showy, yellow flowers in summer, which are clustered in dense, rounded inflorescences.

This plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable addition to rock gardens and dry landscapes.
