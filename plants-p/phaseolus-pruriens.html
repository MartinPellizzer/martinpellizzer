Phaseolus pruriens

Phaseolus pruriens, commonly known as velvet bean, is a climbing legume native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its hairy, purplish stems and trifoliate leaves with small, yellow flowers. The plant produces elongated pods containing edible seeds, though the beans are often used as a source of protein in traditional cuisines. It is also noted for its medicinal properties, including the presence of alkaloids that have been studied for their potential health benefits.

The plant thrives in warm, tropical climates and is often cultivated for both its nutritional and medicinal value.
