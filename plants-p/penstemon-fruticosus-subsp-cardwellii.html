Penstemon fruticosus subsp. cardwellii

Penstemon fruticosus subsp.

cardwellii, commonly known as the Cardwell's penstemon, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces showy, tubular flowers that range in color from violet to blue.

The plant typically thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it well-suited for arid and semi-arid environments. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery down.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
