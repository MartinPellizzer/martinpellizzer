Plagiospermum sinense

Plagiospermum sinense, commonly known as the Chinese false arum, is a herbaceous plant native to China.

It belongs to the Araceae family, which is characterized by its distinctive inflorescence known as a spadix, surrounded by a modified leaf called a spathe. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands. Its leaves are usually broad and arrow-shaped, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

The flowers are small and clustered, and the plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
