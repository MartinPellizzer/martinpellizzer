Pleuroplitis

Pleuroplitis, commonly known as the golden cup plant, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its distinctive yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has a sprawling growth habit with hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves. Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, making it a valuable addition to native gardens.

The plant is also known for its ability to thrive in moist, woodland environments.
