Pinus mughus var. leptoclada

Pinus mughus var. leptoclada, commonly known as the slender pine, is a rare and distinctive variant of the Mughal pine.

It is characterized by its slender, elongated cones and narrow, needle-like leaves. The plant is native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas, where it thrives in cool, moist environments. Its cones are typically pendulous and can remain on the tree for several years before opening.

This variety is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and ecological adaptations.
