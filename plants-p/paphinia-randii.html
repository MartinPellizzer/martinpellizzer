Paphinia randii

Paphinia randii, commonly known as the Red-Bellied Paphiopedilum, is a terrestrial orchid native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that typically feature a deep red or orange lip with a contrasting white or yellow center. The plant grows from a pseudobulb and produces a single, upright inflorescence with several blooms. Its leaves are elliptical and dark green, often with a waxy texture.

Paphinia randii is valued in horticulture for its beauty and is relatively easy to cultivate under the right conditions.
