Primula firmipes subsp. subansirica

Primula firmipes subsp.

subansirica, commonly known as the alpine primrose, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its rosette-like growth habit and produces clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink and white.

The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is typically found at high altitudes. Its leaves are usually basal, hairy, and have a broad, ovate shape.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in alpine gardens.
