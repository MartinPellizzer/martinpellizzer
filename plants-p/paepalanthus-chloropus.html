Paepalanthus chloropus

Paepalanthus chloropus, commonly known as the green fairy dandelion, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow flowers and hairy leaves. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing adaptations to drought conditions. Its scientific name reflects its chlorophyll-rich leaves and the presence of a specialized structure called a "fairy dandelion" in its flower head.

It is also noted for its medicinal properties in traditional South American medicine.
