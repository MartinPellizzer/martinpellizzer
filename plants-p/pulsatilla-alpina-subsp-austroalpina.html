Pulsatilla alpina subsp. austroalpina

Pulsatilla alpina subsp.

austroalpina, commonly known as the alpine pasqueflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the alpine regions of Europe. It features a rosette of hairy, basal leaves that are typically gray-green in color.

The plant produces showy, purple or pink flowers with five petals, often appearing in late spring. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments.

This subspecies is closely related to other members of the Ranunculaceae family and is valued for its ornamental and ecological significance.
