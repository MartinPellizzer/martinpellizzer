Pleonotoma glaberrima

Pleonotoma glaberrima, commonly known as the smooth pleonotoma, is a species of aquatic snail belonging to the family Pleuroceridae.

It is characterized by its elongated, slender shell that is typically smooth and lacks prominent sculpturing. The shell is usually pale in color, often with a slight iridescence, and can reach lengths of up to 2 centimeters. This species is typically found in freshwater environments such as streams and rivers with moderate to fast-flowing water.

Its diet consists mainly of detritus and algae, which it scrapes from surfaces using its radula.
