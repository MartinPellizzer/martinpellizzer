Piptochaetium brevifolium

Piptochaetium brevifolium, commonly known as the short-leaved hairgrass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit, with narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically less than 10 cm in length. The plant produces small, spikelet-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny florets, typical of the grass family Poaceae. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas.

Due to its resilience and ornamental appeal, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
