Panicum italicum var. californicum

Panicum italicum var. californicum, commonly known as California foxtail, is a perennial grass native to the western United States.

It typically grows in dry, disturbed soils and is often found in grasslands, open woodlands, and along roadsides. The plant produces tall, slender spikes of bristly, awned florets that give it a distinctive appearance. Its foliage is narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue when fresh.

It is adapted to arid environments and is often used in restoration projects due to its resilience and ability to stabilize soil.
