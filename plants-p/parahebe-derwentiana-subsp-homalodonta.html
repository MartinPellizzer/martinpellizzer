Parahebe derwentiana subsp. homalodonta

Parahebe derwentiana subsp.

homalodonta, commonly known as the Derwent hebe, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to New Zealand. It features dense, compact foliage with lance-shaped leaves that are typically a deep green color.

The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue or violet, which bloom in spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardy nature in temperate climates.
