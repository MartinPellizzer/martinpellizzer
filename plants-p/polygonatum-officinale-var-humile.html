Polygonatum officinale var. humile

Polygonatum officinale var. humile, commonly known as the lesser Solomon's seal, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern Asia.

It features a rhizomatous root system and tall, unbranched stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in racemes during late spring to early summer. Its berries are dark red and contain several seeds, which are dispersed by birds.

It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in woodlands and forest understories.
