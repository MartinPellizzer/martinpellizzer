Pinus hugelii

Pinus hugelii, commonly known as the Mexican red pine, is a species of evergreen conifer native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and long, needle-like leaves that are typically 5 to 10 centimeters in length. The plant produces cones that are reddish-brown and contain edible seeds, which are a food source for various wildlife species. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions with a temperate climate.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and landscapes.
