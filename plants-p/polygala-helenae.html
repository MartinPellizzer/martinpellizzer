Polygala helenae

Polygala helenae, commonly known as the Helen's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its delicate, often pink or white flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments. Its leaves are usually narrow and linear, and it produces small, pod-like fruits that contain seeds.

Polygala helenae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
