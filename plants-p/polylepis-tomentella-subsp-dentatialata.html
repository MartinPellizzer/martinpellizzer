Polylepis tomentella subsp. dentatialata

Polylepis tomentella subsp.

dentatialata, commonly known as the Andean velvet tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes. It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and distinctive, serrated leaves.

The plant thrives in cool, temperate climates and is often found in cloud forests at elevations between 2,500 and 4,000 meters. Its flowers are small and clustered, producing pale yellow or greenish blooms that attract pollinators.

This subspecies is valued for its ecological role and traditional uses by local communities.
