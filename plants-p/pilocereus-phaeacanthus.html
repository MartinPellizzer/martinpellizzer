Pilocereus phaeacanthus

Pilocereus phaeacanthus, commonly known as the night blooming cactus, is a species of cactus native to South America.

It is characterized by its spiny, cylindrical stems and is known for its large, fragrant flowers that open at night. The plant thrives in arid environments and requires minimal water, making it well adapted to desert conditions. Its flowers are typically white or pale yellow and emit a sweet scent to attract nocturnal pollinators.

The plant is popular among succulent enthusiasts for its unique appearance and blooming habits.
