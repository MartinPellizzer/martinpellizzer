Potentilla macrotoma

Potentilla macrotoma, commonly known as the large-flowered cinquefoil, is a species of flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of central and eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its showy, yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun. Its leaves are compound, consisting of five oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces small, red or black berries in the fall.

Potentilla macrotoma is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
