Potamogeton × nitens var. innominatus

Potamogeton × nitens var. innominatus, commonly known as the narrow-leaf pondweed, is a hybrid aquatic plant characterized by its slender, ribbon-like leaves.

It typically grows in shallow, freshwater environments such as ponds and lakes, where it forms dense underwater stands. The plant has small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually submerged, and it reproduces both sexually and vegetatively through rhizomes. Its leaves are usually dark green and slightly ribbed, with a smooth texture.

This variant is often found in regions with temperate climates and is an important component of aquatic ecosystems.
