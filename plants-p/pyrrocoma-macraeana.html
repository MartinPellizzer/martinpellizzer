Pyrrocoma macraeana

Pyrrocoma macraeana, commonly known as the Macrae's pyrrocoma, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, characterized by its dense, compact growth habit. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the cooler months of the year. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, adapted to reduce water loss in its harsh environment.

The species is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and limited distribution.
