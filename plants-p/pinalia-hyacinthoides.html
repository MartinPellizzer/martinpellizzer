Pinalia hyacinthoides

Pinalia hyacinthoides, commonly known as the bluebell of the Mediterranean, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pale blue to violet. The plant typically grows in woodland areas and thrives in partial shade with well-drained soil. Its leaves are lance-shaped and aromatic, often emitting a subtle fragrance.

Pinalia hyacinthoides is known for its early spring blooming habit, making it a valuable addition to natural landscapes.
