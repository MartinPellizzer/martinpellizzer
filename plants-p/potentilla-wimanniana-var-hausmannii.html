Potentilla wimanniana var. hausmannii

Potentilla wimanniana var. hausmannii, commonly known as the Hausmann's cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a low-growing shrub that typically reaches heights of about 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features small, five-petaled flowers that range in color from white to yellow, often appearing in clusters. Its leaves are trifoliate, meaning they are divided into three leaflets, and are covered in a fine, silvery hair.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and attractive foliage.
