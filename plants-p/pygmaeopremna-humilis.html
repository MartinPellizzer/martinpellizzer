Pygmaeopremna humilis

Pygmaeopremna humilis, commonly known as the dwarf premna, is a small shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, opposite leaves. The leaves are typically ovate to elliptic in shape, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. It produces small, tubular flowers that are usually pink or white, arranged in clusters at the leaf axils.

This plant is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories.
