Phelipanche caesia var. borealis

Phelipanche caesia var. borealis, commonly known as the northern common tomentary morning glory, is a flowering plant native to northern regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is a herbaceous annual that produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of pink to purple. The plant is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, and it thrives in disturbed soils and open habitats. It is often found in meadows, along roadsides, and in fields.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and its ability to spread rapidly in suitable conditions.
