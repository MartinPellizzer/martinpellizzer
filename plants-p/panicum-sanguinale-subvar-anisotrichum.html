Panicum sanguinale subvar. anisotrichum

Panicum sanguinale subvar. anisotrichum, commonly known as red panicgrass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and narrow, linear leaves that are often tinged with red or purple at the base. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it adaptable to a variety of environments.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its vibrant coloration and drought tolerance.
