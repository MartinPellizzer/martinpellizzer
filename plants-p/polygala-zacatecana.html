Polygala zacatecana

Polygala zacatecana, commonly known as the Zacatecan milkweed, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. Its flowers are usually pink or purple, with a distinctive spurred petal that aids in pollination by insects.

Polygala zacatecana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local pollinators.
