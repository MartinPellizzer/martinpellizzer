Pimelea schlechtendaliana

Pimelea schlechtendaliana, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bottlebrush, is a shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its distinctive cylindrical flower spikes, which are typically bright yellow or orange in color. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are arranged oppositely along the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in heathlands and dry forests.

Pimelea schlechtendaliana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
