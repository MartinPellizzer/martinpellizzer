Panicum acutiflorum

Panicum acutiflorum, commonly known as the slender panicgrass, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a member of the grass family (Poaceae) and typically grows in moist, open habitats such as wetlands and stream banks. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered inflorescences that are often pink or reddish in color. It is characterized by its rapid growth and ability to thrive in wet conditions, making it a common sight in riparian zones.

Due to its ecological importance, it is often used in wetland restoration projects.
