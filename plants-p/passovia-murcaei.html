Passovia murcaei

Passovia murcaei, commonly known as the Murcaei's ghostflower, is a rare and enigmatic plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes.

It is characterized by its delicate, translucent leaves and striking, bioluminescent flowers that emit a faint glow at night. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments with well-drained soil, often growing in rocky crevices. Its unique adaptations allow it to survive in extreme conditions, making it a subject of interest for botanists.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, Passovia murcaei is considered endangered and is protected in its native range.
