Ptilotus helichrysoides

Ptilotus helichrysoides, commonly known as the golden wattle, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is a shrub that typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is part of the Fabaceae family. The plant is characterized by its bright yellow flowers, which are clustered in globular inflorescences. Its leaves are small, hairy, and often arranged in a spiral pattern along the stems.

Ptilotus helichrysoides is also notable for its historical significance as the floral emblem of Australia.
