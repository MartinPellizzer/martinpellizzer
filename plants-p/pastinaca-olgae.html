Pastinaca olgae

Pastinaca olgae, commonly known as the Canary Islands burdock, is a perennial herb native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, hollow stems and large, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in composite flower heads, typical of the daisy family. Its roots are thick and fleshy, often used in traditional medicine.

Pastinaca olgae thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to the unique climate of its native habitat.
