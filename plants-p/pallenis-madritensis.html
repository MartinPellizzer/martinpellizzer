Pallenis madritensis

Pallenis madritensis, commonly known as the Spanish bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to arid conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.
