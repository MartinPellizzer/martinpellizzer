Pleurothallis neodubbeldamii

Pleurothallis neodubbeldamii, commonly known as the "Dubbeldam's pleurothallis," is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to the tropical regions of South America.

This small epiphytic orchid typically grows on trees in humid, shaded environments. It features delicate, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink. The plant is noted for its intricate floral structure and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts for its ornamental value.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is relatively rare in the wild.
