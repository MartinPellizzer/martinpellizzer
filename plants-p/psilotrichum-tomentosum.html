Psilotrichum tomentosum

Psilotrichum tomentosum, commonly known as the velvet fern, is a unique fern species characterized by its soft, hairy fronds.

It belongs to the family Psilotaceae and is native to South America, particularly Brazil. The plant has a distinctive appearance with its pale green, velvety leaves that are covered in fine, white trichomes. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
