Phalaris canariensis f. bracteata

Phalaris canariensis f. bracteata, commonly known as the Canary grass with bracts, is a variant of the Canary grass species.

It is a perennial grass characterized by its distinctive bracteate inflorescence, which features specialized leaf-like structures at the base of the flower cluster. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal or wetland habitats. Its foliage is narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green coloration that adds ornamental value.

This form is valued in horticulture for its unique appearance and adaptability to various environmental conditions.
