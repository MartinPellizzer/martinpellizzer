Poa bomiensis

Poa bomiensis, commonly known as the Bomien grass, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of southern China.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and narrow, bluish-green leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers, typical of the Poaceae family. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
