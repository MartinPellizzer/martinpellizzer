Pollinia imberbis var. willdenoviana

Pollinia imberbis var. willdenoviana, commonly known as the Willdenow's barren pollinia, is a variant of the orchid genus Pollinia.

It is characterized by its unique, elongated pollinia that lack the typical sticky structures found in most orchids. This plant is native to certain regions of South America, particularly Brazil, where it thrives in humid tropical environments. The flowers are usually small and green, with a subtle fragrance that attracts specific pollinators.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a delicate and less commonly cultivated species in botanical collections.
