Peucedanum hirsutiusculum var. subglabrum

Peucedanum hirsutiusculum var. subglabrum, commonly known as the smooth-hairy parsley, is a herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features finely divided, fern-like leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in umbels, which bloom in early summer. Its fruit is a slender, dry schizocarp with two mericarps, typical of the Apiaceae family.
