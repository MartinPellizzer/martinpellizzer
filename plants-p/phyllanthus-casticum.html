Phyllanthus casticum

Phyllanthus casticum, commonly known as the Indian gooseberry or amla, is a deciduous shrub native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a glossy, dark green surface. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by oval-shaped fruits that turn from green to bright orange when mature. The fruit is rich in vitamin C and has been used in traditional medicine for its antioxidant and therapeutic properties.

It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its nutritional value and medicinal uses.
