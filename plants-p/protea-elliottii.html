Protea elliottii

Protea elliottii, commonly known as Elliott's protea, is a striking South African plant belonging to the Proteaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescences that resemble a cluster of flowers, though they are actually a single flower with many petal-like structures. The plant typically grows in coastal and mountainous regions, thriving in well-drained, acidic soils. Its foliage consists of long, narrow, leathery leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery down.

Elliott's protea is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique and vibrant floral display.
