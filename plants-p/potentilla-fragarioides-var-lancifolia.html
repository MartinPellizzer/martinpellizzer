Potentilla fragarioides var. lancifolia

Potentilla fragarioides var. lancifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved cinquefoil, is a perennial shrub native to North America.

It features compound leaves with five to nine elliptical, lance-shaped leaflets that are typically hairy beneath. The plant produces small, yellow flowers in clusters during the summer months, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native plant gardens.
