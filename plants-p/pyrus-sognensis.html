Pyrus sognensis

Pyrus sognensis, commonly known as the Sogn apple or Sogn pear, is a deciduous tree native to the mountainous regions of southern Norway.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its small, rounded leaves and clusters of white flowers that bloom in early spring. The tree produces small, yellowish-green fruits that are typically not cultivated for commercial use but are valued for their ornamental appeal.

Pyrus sognensis is adapted to harsh alpine conditions, making it a resilient species in its natural habitat.
