Papaver armeniacum var. pilgerianum

Papaver armeniacum var. pilgerianum, commonly known as the Armenian poppy, is a species native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its striking red, cup-shaped flowers and hairy, lobed leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or mountainous areas and prefers well-drained soil. It blooms in late spring to early summer, adding vibrant color to its natural habitat.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
