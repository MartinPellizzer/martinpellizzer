Prunus armeniaca var. typica

Prunus armeniaca var. typica, commonly known as the common apricot, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Asia.

It produces showy, fragrant flowers in spring, which are typically white or pale pink. The fruit, an apricot, develops from these flowers and is characterized by its fuzzy skin and sweet, aromatic flesh. The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and its edible fruit, which is rich in nutrients.

It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
