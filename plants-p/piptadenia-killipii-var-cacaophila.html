Piptadenia killipii var. cacaophila

Piptadenia killipii var. cacaophila, commonly known as the red-veined cacao tree, is a tropical legume native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves with prominent red veins, and its compound inflorescences that bear small, yellow flowers. The plant produces pods that contain astringent, bitter seeds used in traditional medicine. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in secondary forests.

Its ecological importance includes nitrogen fixation and serving as a food source for various wildlife species.
