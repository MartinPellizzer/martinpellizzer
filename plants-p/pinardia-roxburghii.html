Pinardia roxburghii

Pinardia roxburghii, commonly known as the Kashmir pine, is a coniferous tree native to the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its tall, straight trunk and needle-like leaves that are arranged in bundles of five. The tree produces cones that are typically reddish-brown in color and contain seeds used for propagation. It thrives in cool, moist climates and is often found in mountainous regions.

The plant plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
