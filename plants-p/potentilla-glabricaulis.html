Potentilla glabricaulis

Potentilla glabricaulis, commonly known as the smoothstem cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a member of the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit. The plant features slender, hairless stems and produces clusters of small, yellow flowers in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a slightly serrated edge.

Potentilla glabricaulis is adapted to rocky and arid environments, making it a resilient species in harsh conditions.
