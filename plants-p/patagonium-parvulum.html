Patagonium parvulum

Patagonium parvulum, commonly known as the Patagonian dwarf cactus, is a small, succulent plant native to the arid regions of southern South America.

It features a compact, branching structure with fleshy, spiny stems that store water, allowing it to survive in harsh, dry environments. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the brief growing season, typically in spring. Its leaves are reduced to spines, minimizing water loss through transpiration.

Patagonium parvulum plays a role in local ecosystems by providing shelter and food for various desert organisms.
