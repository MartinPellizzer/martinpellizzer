Pseudotsuga japonica

Pseudotsuga japonica, commonly known as the Japanese hemlock, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to Japan.

It belongs to the family Pinaceae and is characterized by its slender, pyramidal shape and dark green, needle-like leaves. The tree produces cones that are typically reddish-brown and contain seeds covered in a thin, membranous wing. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in mountainous regions.

Due to its ornamental value, it is also cultivated in gardens and parks around the world.
