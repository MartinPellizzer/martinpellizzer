Pinalia erecta

Pinalia erecta, commonly known as the upright pinalia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, erect growth habit and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow in color. Pinalia erecta is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and greenhouses.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
