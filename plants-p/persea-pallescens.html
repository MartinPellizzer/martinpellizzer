Persea pallescens

Persea pallescens, commonly known as the white-leaf persimmon, is a small evergreen tree native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes other aromatic trees like laurel and avocado. The plant is characterized by its broad, shiny, light green leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a small, round berry that turns from green to yellow when mature.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its edible fruit.
