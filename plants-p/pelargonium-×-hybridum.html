Pelargonium × hybridum

Pelargonium × hybridum, commonly known as the geranium, is a hybrid plant species within the Geraniaceae family.

It is characterized by its showy, five-petaled flowers that come in a variety of colors, including red, pink, and white. The plant typically has upright stems and broad, lobed leaves that are often hairy or velvety in texture. It is widely cultivated for ornamental purposes in gardens and as houseplants.

Pelargonium × hybridum thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it a versatile choice for different growing conditions.
