Piper adenandrum

Piper adenandrum, commonly known as the Indian pepper vine, is a climbing shrub native to tropical regions of Asia and Africa.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many species of pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves and small, clustered white flowers that develop into three-lobed berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

The berries are edible but have a mild, peppery flavor, making it a valuable plant in traditional cuisines.
