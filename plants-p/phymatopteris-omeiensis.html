Phymatopteris omeiensis

It is a small, terrestrial fern that typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often on rocks or soil. The plant has a simple, upright frond structure with a distinct, narrow shape. Its spore-producing structures, called sori, are located on the underside of the fronds.

This species is native to China and is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and ecological preferences.
