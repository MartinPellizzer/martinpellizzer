Polygala sancti-georgii var. sancti-georgii

Polygala sancti-georgii var. sancti-georgii, commonly known as the Saint George's milkvetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the Polygalaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, clustered flowers that typically range in color from white to pale pink. The plant produces small, bean-like pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Native to the Mediterranean region, it thrives in rocky and arid environments.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and traditional herbal medicine.
