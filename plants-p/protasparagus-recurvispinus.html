Protasparagus recurvispinus

Protasparagus recurvispinus, commonly known as the curly-spined asparagus, is a unique member of the Asparagaceae family.

It is characterized by its wiry, spiny stems and small, clustered leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces tiny, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or white in color. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers sunny to partially shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental features and hardy nature, it is often cultivated in gardens and rocky landscapes.
