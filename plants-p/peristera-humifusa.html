Peristera humifusa

Peristera humifusa, commonly known as the creeping bellflower, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue. The plant thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats. It is adapted to cold climates and can tolerate harsh environmental conditions.

Its delicate appearance makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine displays.
