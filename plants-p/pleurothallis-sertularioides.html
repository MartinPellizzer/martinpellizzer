Pleurothallis sertularioides

Pleurothallis sertularioides, commonly known as the "serpent's tail orchid," is a species native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Ecuador and Peru.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its intricate, often spiral-shaped inflorescence. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded forest environments. Its flowers are small, fragrant, and come in a variety of colors, including white, yellow, and pink.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is a subject of interest for both botanists and orchid enthusiasts.
