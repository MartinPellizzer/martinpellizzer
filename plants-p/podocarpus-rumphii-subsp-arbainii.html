Podocarpus rumphii subsp. arbainii

Podocarpus rumphii subsp.

arbainii, commonly known as the Philippine podocarp, is a member of the Podocarpaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia. It is a medium to large evergreen tree that can grow up to 30 meters tall, characterized by its dense, dark green foliage.

The leaves are leathery, lanceolate, and arranged spirally along the branches. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by cone-like structures that contain its seeds.

This species is valued for its timber and is also used in horticulture for its ornamental qualities.
