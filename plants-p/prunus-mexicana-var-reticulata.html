Prunus mexicana var. reticulata

Prunus mexicana var. reticulata, commonly known as the Mexican wild cherry or reticulate cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Mexico.

It features ovate to elliptic leaves with a serrated margin and a reticulate (net-like) venation pattern on the underside. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers in early spring, followed by edible, dark red to black berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or limestone habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in traditional medicine.
