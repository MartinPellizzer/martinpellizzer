Protea bruniades

Protea bruniades, commonly known as the white bruniades protea, is a striking South African plant belonging to the Proteaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescence composed of numerous white to pale pink flowers arranged in a cylindrical shape. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and is native to the Western Cape region of South Africa. Its foliage consists of narrow, leathery leaves that are often covered in a fine, white powdery coating.

Protea bruniades is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various insects.
