Polygonum linicola

Polygonum linicola, commonly known as the slender knotweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, which is characterized by its distinctive nodes and often triangular stems. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as stream banks and forest floors. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a rough texture, while its small flowers are clustered in dense spikes.

Polygonum linicola is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
