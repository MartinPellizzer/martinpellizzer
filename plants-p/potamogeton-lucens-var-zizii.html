Potamogeton lucens var. zizii

Potamogeton lucens var. zizii, commonly known as the Zizian water milfoil, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in shallow, slow-moving waters and is characterized by its finely divided, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in spikelets, often submerged or just below the water surface. It plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and sometimes endangered species in certain regions.
