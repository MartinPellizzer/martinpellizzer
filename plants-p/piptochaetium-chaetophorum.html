Piptochaetium chaetophorum

Piptochaetium chaetophorum, commonly known as the silky hair grass, is a species of grass native to Africa and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its fine, wiry leaves and slender, erect growth habit. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. It is often found in arid or semi-arid regions due to its drought-resistant properties.

The seeds of Piptochaetium chaetophorum are small and adapted for dispersal by wind, contributing to its ability to colonize new areas.
