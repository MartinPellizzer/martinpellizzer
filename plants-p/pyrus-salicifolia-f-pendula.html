Pyrus salicifolia f. pendula

Pyrus salicifolia f. pendula, commonly known as the weeping whitebeam, is a striking ornamental tree characterized by its cascading branches that drape like a waterfall.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is a cultivar of the common whitebeam, Pyrus salicifolia. The plant features small, white flowers that appear in early spring, followed by edible berries in the fall. Its foliage turns a vibrant yellow in autumn, adding seasonal interest to the landscape.

This tree is well-suited for gardens and parks, offering both aesthetic value and ecological benefits.
