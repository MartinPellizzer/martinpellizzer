Passiflora foetida var. hastata

Passiflora foetida var. hastata, commonly known as the hairy passionflower, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves with a rough texture. The plant produces striking, complex flowers with blue and white petals, often accompanied by red filaments. Its fruit is a bright yellow, edible berry that ripens in late summer.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported calming effects.
