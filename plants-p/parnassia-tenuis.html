Parnassia tenuis

Parnassia tenuis, commonly known as the slender mountain buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of North America.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and alpine meadows. The plant features a basal rosette of narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, white or pale yellow flowers with five petals. Its flowers are often clustered in a loose inflorescence and are pollinated by various insects.

The species is noted for its delicate appearance and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value.
