Phellandrium globulosum

Phellandrium globulosum, commonly known as the globular honeysuckle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, glandular leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces spherical, fleshy fruits that are edible for some wildlife. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in heathland and coastal areas.

Phellandrium globulosum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local fauna.
