Polygala macrantha

Polygala macrantha, commonly known as the large-flowered milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, often blue or violet, spurred flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. Its leaves are alternate and pinnate, with small, oval-shaped leaflets.

Polygala macrantha is often found in open vegetation types such as grasslands and shrublands.
