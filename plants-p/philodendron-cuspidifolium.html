Philodendron cuspidifolium

Philodendron cuspidifolium, commonly known as the spiky-leaf philodendron, is a tropical climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a pointed tip, giving it its scientific name. The plant has a woody stem and can grow quite large under suitable conditions. Its foliage is often a deep green with a slightly glossy appearance.

It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires indirect light for optimal growth.
