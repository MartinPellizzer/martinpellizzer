Piper pisoense

Piper pisoense, commonly known as the Piso pepper or Piso vine, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil and Peru. The plant is characterized by its climbing habit, with elongated stems that can reach several meters in length. Its leaves are typically ovate to lanceolate in shape, with a glossy green surface and a distinct aromatic scent.

The fruit is a small, three-lobed berry that contains several seeds, and the plant is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
