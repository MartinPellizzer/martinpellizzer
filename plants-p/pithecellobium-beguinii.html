Pithecellobium beguinii

Pithecellobium beguinii, commonly known as the golden shower tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of yellow flowers that resemble small showers. The plant produces small, edible pods that contain seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties.
