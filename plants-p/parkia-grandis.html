Parkia grandis

Parkia grandis, commonly known as the African satinash, is a tropical leguminous tree native to Africa.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and produces distinctive yellowish-orange flowers. The tree bears pods containing edible seeds that are often used in traditional dishes. Its bark and leaves have been used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Parkia grandis is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in various soil conditions.
