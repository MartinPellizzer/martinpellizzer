Pteris sulphurea var. alba

Pteris sulphurea var. alba, commonly known as the white shield fern, is a variant of the common water fern.

It is characterized by its pale, almost white fronds that contrast with its dark, leathery rhizomes. The plant is native to Southeast Asia and thrives in moist, shaded environments. Its fronds are typically pinnate, with a delicate, feathery appearance.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value in humid indoor or outdoor gardens.
