Padus virginiana var. rubra

Padus virginiana var. rubra, commonly known as the red chokecherry.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern North America, characterized by its dark red to black fruits that are edible for birds and small mammals. The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges and produces white to pink flowers in early spring. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers moist, well-drained habitats.

The species is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
