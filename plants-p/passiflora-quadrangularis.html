Passiflora quadrangularis

Passiflora quadrangularis, commonly known as the passion fruit vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive quadrangular stems and compound leaves with five to seven leaflets. The plant produces striking flowers with a complex structure, featuring a crown of filaments and a central cluster of stamens. Its fruit, which is typically yellow or orange, contains a juicy, seed-filled pulp that is widely used in culinary applications.

The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires support for its vigorous growth.
