Pleurothallis crocodiliceps

Pleurothallis crocodiliceps, commonly known as the crocodile-headed orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to the cloud forests of Central and South America.

This epiphytic plant typically grows on trees and thrives in humid, shaded environments. Its most distinctive feature is the elongated, crocodile-like shape of its sepals and petals, which give it its common name. The flowers are usually small, white or pale yellow, and bloom during the rainy season.

It is appreciated for its unique appearance and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
