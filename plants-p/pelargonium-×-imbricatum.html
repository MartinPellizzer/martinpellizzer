Pelargonium × imbricatum

Pelargonium × imbricatum, commonly known as the zonal geranium, is a hybrid species within the Geraniaceae family.

It is native to South Africa and is characterized by its distinctive zonation patterns on the leaves, which are often in shades of green, red, or purple. The plant produces vibrant flowers in shades of pink, red, and purple, typically in clusters at the ends of the stems. It is a popular ornamental plant due to its compact growth habit and long blooming period.

Pelargonium × imbricatum thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it suitable for both container gardening and in-ground planting.
