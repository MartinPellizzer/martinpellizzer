Pachycereus pringlei f. calvus

Pachycereus pringlei f. calvus, commonly known as the Sonoran giant cardon cactus, is a distinctive variety of the cardon cactus native to the Sonoran Desert.

It is characterized by its massive, columnar stems that can grow up to 15 meters in height, often with a grayish-green color and a rough, scaly texture. The plant has spiny areoles and produces large, yellow flowers that bloom in the evening. It is adapted to arid environments with minimal rainfall, storing water in its thick, fleshy stems.

This plant plays an important ecological role in its habitat, providing shelter and food for various desert species.
