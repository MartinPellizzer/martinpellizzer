Piper boehmeriifolium var. tonkinense

Piper boehmeriifolium var. tonkinense, commonly known as Vietnamese pepper or long pepper, is a tropical vine native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves with elongated, serrated leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by elongated, cylindrical fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its aromatic and medicinal properties.

The plant's botanical features make it distinct within its genus and valuable in traditional herbal medicine.
