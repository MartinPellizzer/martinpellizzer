Prunus demissa var. nuttallii

Prunus demissa var. nuttallii, commonly known as the Nuttall's cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the western United States.

It typically grows to a height of 3 to 10 feet and features ovate to elliptic leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers in early spring, followed by edible, sweet-tart red drupes. It prefers well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it adaptable to various garden environments.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
