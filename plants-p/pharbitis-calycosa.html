Pharbitis calycosa

Pharbitis calycosa, commonly known as morning glory, is a flowering plant belonging to the Convolvulaceae family.

It is characterized by its heliotropic flowers that open in the morning and close at dusk. The plant has climbing stems with tendrils that help it ascend supports. Its leaves are alternate, heart-shaped, and often have a velvety texture.

Pharbitis calycosa produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in a variety of colors, including blue, purple, and white.
