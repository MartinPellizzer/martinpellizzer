Pleopeltis bradeorum

Pleopeltis bradeorum, commonly known as the Brade's rock fern, is a species of epiphytic fern native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its compact, dense foliage and rhizome system that allows it to thrive on tree trunks and rocks. The fronds are typically leathery and dark green, with a distinctive, intricate venation pattern. It prefers humid, shaded environments and is often found in cloud forests.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is a popular specimen among fern enthusiasts and botanists.
