Piper mediocre

Piper mediocre, commonly known as the common pepper plant, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of Central and South America. The plant is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and small, greenish-white flowers. It produces small berries that contain the aromatic compound piperine, responsible for its spicy flavor.

This plant is often cultivated for both ornamental and culinary purposes.
