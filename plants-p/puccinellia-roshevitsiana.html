Puccinellia roshevitsiana

Puccinellia roshevitsiana, commonly known as the Roshevitsian spikegrass, is a perennial grass species native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its low-growing habit, with slender, cylindrical stems and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically produces pale blue to purple flowers, which are clustered in dense, spike-like structures. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to withstand drought and high temperatures.

Puccinellia roshevitsiana plays a role in stabilizing soil in degraded landscapes and is of interest for ecological restoration projects.
