Phyllanthus pancherianus

Phyllanthus pancherianus, commonly known as the Indian gooseberry or amla, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by bright red, fleshy berries.

It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its edible fruits, which are rich in vitamin C. The plant also has traditional medicinal uses in Ayurvedic practices.
