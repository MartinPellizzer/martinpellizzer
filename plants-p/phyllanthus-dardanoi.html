Phyllanthus dardanoi

Phyllanthus dardanoi, commonly known as Dardanian gooseberry, is a shrub native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is characterized by its small, clustered greenish-yellow flowers. The plant produces small, round berries that are typically green when immature and turn red when mature. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in dry, rocky soils.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
