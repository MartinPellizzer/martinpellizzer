Pholidia exilifolia

Pholidia exilifolia, commonly known as the slender-leaf hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and the Arabian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its slender, often hairy leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Pholidia exilifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and blooms.
