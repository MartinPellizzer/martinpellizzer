Penstemon glaber var. glaber

Penstemon glaber var. glaber, commonly known as the tall beardtongue, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The plant features narrow, gray-green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its nectar-rich flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological value.
