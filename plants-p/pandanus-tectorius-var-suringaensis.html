Pandanus tectorius var. suringaensis

Pandanus tectorius var. suringaensis, commonly known as the Suringa pandanus, is a tropical plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its long, narrow, and often spirally arranged leaves that are typically green with a waxy texture. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences that bear both male and female flowers, which are essential for its reproductive cycle. It thrives in coastal and wetland environments, often growing in sandy or muddy soils.

This variant is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional uses in local cultures.
