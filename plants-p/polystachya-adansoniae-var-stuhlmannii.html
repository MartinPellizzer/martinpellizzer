Polystachya adansoniae var. stuhlmannii

Polystachya adansoniae var. stuhlmannii, commonly known as the Stuhlmann's spikegrass, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and long, slender spikes of small, white to pale pink flowers. The plant typically thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories or along stream banks. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often arranged in a basal rosette.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
