Potentilla intricata

Potentilla intricata, commonly known as the intricate cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, five-petaled yellow flowers and its finely divided, fern-like leaves. The plant typically grows in alpine or subalpine habitats, where it thrives in well-drained, rocky soils. Its small stature and intricate foliage make it a distinctive species within the Rosaceae family.

Potentilla intricata is often found in open meadows and slopes, where it contributes to the biodiversity of high-altitude ecosystems.
