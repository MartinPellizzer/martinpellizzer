Protea falcata

Protea falcata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sugarbush, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Proteaceae family, which is known for its showy, often colorful inflorescences. The plant produces cylindrical, yellow to orange flower heads that are surrounded by modified leaves called bracts. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in fynbos vegetation.

Protea falcata is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in local ecosystems.
