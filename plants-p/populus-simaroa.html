Populus simaroa

Populus simaroa, commonly known as the Andean poplar, is a deciduous tree native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and heart-shaped leaves that are typically 5-10 centimeters in length. The plant produces catkin-like inflorescences, which are its primary means of seed dispersal. It thrives in temperate to subtropical climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Populus simaroa plays an important role in local ecosystems, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
