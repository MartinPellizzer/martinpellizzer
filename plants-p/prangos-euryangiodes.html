Prangos euryangiodes

Prangos euryangiodes, commonly known as the blue poppy, is a striking perennial plant native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its vivid blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has basal leaves that are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in cool, moist environments and requires well-drained soil.

Prangos euryangiodes is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and botanical collections.
