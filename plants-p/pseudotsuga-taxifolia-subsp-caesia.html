Pseudotsuga taxifolia subsp. caesia

Pseudotsuga taxifolia subsp.

caesia, commonly known as the Japanese white fir, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to Japan. It is characterized by its slender, conical shape and needle-like leaves that are blue-green in color.

The tree produces small, spherical cones that contain winged seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in cool, moist climates and is often found in mountainous regions.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for both ecological and ornamental purposes.
