Polygala virgata var. decora

Polygala virgata var. decora, commonly known as the decorated milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in dry, rocky habitats. The plant produces small, delicate flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, arranged in clusters. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, and it has a fibrous root system.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in arid conditions.
