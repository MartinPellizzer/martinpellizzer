Plectranthus pilifer

Plectranthus pilifer, commonly known as the woolly plectranthus, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage that gives it a distinctive, almost fuzzy appearance. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically purple or violet in color, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Plectranthus pilifer is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for.
