Pongamia tetraptera

Pongamia tetraptera, commonly known as the four-winged pongam, is a leguminous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and distinctive four-winged pods that contain seeds. The plant is adapted to tropical and subtropical climates and thrives in well-drained soils. It has a deep root system that helps in soil stabilization and nutrient cycling.

The tree is also valued for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, making it useful in agroforestry systems.
