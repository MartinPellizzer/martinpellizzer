Peperomia moritzii

Peperomia moritzii, commonly known as the Moritz's pepper plant, is a compact, evergreen perennial shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It features thick, waxy, oval-shaped leaves that are typically dark green with a subtle sheen, often adorned with red or purple veins. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually green or reddish in color, which are followed by tiny, round berries. It thrives in bright, indirect light and prefers well-draining soil with moderate humidity.

Peperomia moritzii is prized for its ornamental foliage and ease of care, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.
