Plantago psyllia

Plantago psyllia, commonly known as mouse-eared chickweed, is a low-growing herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features small, oval-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance. The plant produces tiny, greenish-white flowers in clusters, which are typically arranged in a spike-like structure. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and along stream banks.

Botanically, it belongs to the Plantaginaceae family, which includes other species known for their medicinal and ecological importance.
