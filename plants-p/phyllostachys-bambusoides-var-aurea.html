Phyllostachys bambusoides var. aurea

Phyllostachys bambusoides var. aurea, commonly known as the golden bamboo, is a variant of the bamboo species native to China.

It is characterized by its striking golden-yellow leaves that contrast with its green stems. This plant is a clumping bamboo, forming dense stands that can grow up to 6 meters in height. The leaves are narrow and elongated, with a slightly hairy texture.

It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
