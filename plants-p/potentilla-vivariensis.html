Potentilla vivariensis

Potentilla vivariensis, commonly known as the vivarion cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in rocky and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

Potentilla vivariensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with well-drained soil and full sun.
