Platyopuntia ianthinantha

Platyopuntia ianthinantha, commonly known as the silver nightshade cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its flat, paddle-shaped stems that are covered in a dense layer of white, woolly hair, which helps to reduce water loss in its harsh environment. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. Its succulent nature allows it to store water efficiently, making it well adapted to drought conditions.

The plant is often used in xeriscaping due to its low maintenance and aesthetic appeal.
