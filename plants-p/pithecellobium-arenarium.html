Pithecellobium arenarium

Pithecellobium arenarium, commonly known as the sand pithecellobium, is a shrub native to the arid regions of northern Mexico and the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its small, clustered flowers and leathery, evergreen leaves that help it conserve water in dry environments. The plant produces small, edible pods that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind and animals. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to full sun, making it a resilient species in harsh climates.

Its botanical features make it an important component of desert ecosystems.
