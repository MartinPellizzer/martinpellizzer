Puccinellia fauriei

Puccinellia fauriei, commonly known as the Faurie's needlegrass, is a perennial grass native to arid and semi-arid regions of Asia.

It typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to drought conditions. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It is often found in desert margins and is used in ecological restoration projects.

Its botanical features make it an important component of dryland ecosystems.
