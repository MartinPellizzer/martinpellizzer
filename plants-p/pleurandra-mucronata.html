Pleurandra mucronata

Pleurandra mucronata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved arrowroot, is a herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its arrow-shaped leaves and striking yellow spatula-shaped bracts. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically hidden within the spathe. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Pleurandra mucronata is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
