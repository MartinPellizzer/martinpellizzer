Pterospartum tridentatum subsp. cantabricum

Pterospartum tridentatum subsp.

cantabricum, commonly known as the Cantabrian rock rose, is a shrub native to the Iberian Peninsula. It is characterized by its small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in spring.

The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and is adapted to Mediterranean climates. Its leaves are narrow and often have a slightly hairy texture.

It is a relatively rare subspecies, found primarily in the Cantabrian Mountains.
