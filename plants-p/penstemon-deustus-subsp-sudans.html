Penstemon deustus subsp. sudans

Penstemon deustus subsp.

sudans, commonly known as the Sudan penstemon, is a perennial herb native to the arid and semi-arid regions of Sudan. It features upright, branching stems that can grow up to 1.5 meters tall, covered in dense, soft hairs.

The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that are typically violet to purple in color, with five petals and a distinctive two-lipped structure. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to hot, dry climates.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in xeriscapes and rock gardens.
