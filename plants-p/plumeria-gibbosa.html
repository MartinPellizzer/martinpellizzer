Plumeria gibbosa

Plumeria gibbosa, commonly known as the frangipani tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, showy flowers that range in color from white to yellow and pink. The plant has simple, opposite, and often hairy leaves that are elliptical in shape. Its bark is smooth and grayish, and it produces woody capsules containing numerous seeds.

Plumeria gibbosa is widely cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
