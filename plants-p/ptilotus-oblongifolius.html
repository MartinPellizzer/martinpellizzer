Ptilotus oblongifolius

Ptilotus oblongifolius, commonly known as the ghost flower, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its unique, tubular flowers that resemble small, ghost-like shapes, hence its common name. The plant typically grows in sandy soils and is often found in coastal areas. It produces showy, pink or red flowers that bloom in spring and summer.

The leaves are usually narrow and grass-like, and the plant is known for its ornamental value in gardens.
