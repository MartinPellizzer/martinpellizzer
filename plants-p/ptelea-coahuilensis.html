Ptelea coahuilensis

Ptelea coahuilensis, commonly known as Coahuila privet, is a shrub native to the Chihuahuan Desert in northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and small, white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, black berries that are an important food source for local wildlife. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Ptelea coahuilensis plays a significant role in stabilizing sandy soils and supporting biodiversity in its native habitat.
