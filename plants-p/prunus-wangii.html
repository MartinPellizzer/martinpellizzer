Prunus wangii

Prunus wangii, commonly known as the Wangi cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its slender branches and small, oval-shaped leaves. The plant produces white or pale pink flowers in early spring, followed by small, sweet-tart berries. Prunus wangii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.
