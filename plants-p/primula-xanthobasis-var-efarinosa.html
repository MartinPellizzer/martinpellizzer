Primula xanthobasis var. efarinosa

Primula xanthobasis var. efarinosa, commonly known as the yellow primrose variant, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of China.

It features hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, powdery coating, giving it its variant name "efarinosa," which translates to "dust-like.". The plant produces clusters of bright yellow flowers with five petals, blooming in early spring. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in forested areas or rocky slopes.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive and ornamental addition to gardens and botanical collections.
