Puccinellia suecica var. angustifolia

Puccinellia suecica var. angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf sea blush, is a perennial grass species native to coastal regions of northern Europe.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, pinkish to reddish flowers that bloom in summer, typically arranged in dense, spike-like clusters. It thrives in saline and sandy soils, often found in dune environments and coastal grasslands.

This variety is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing coastal dunes and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
