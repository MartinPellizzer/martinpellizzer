Pentaschistis thunbergii

Pentaschistis thunbergii, commonly known as the Japanese false indigo, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It features slender, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in clusters, typically in the summer months. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in shaded environments.

Botanically, it belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its unique floral structure and adaptability to various soil types.
