Pteris megaphylla

Pteris megaphylla, commonly known as the giant water fern, is a species of fern native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its large, feathery fronds that can grow up to several feet in length. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found near streams or in dense forests. Its reproductive structures, including sori, are located on the underside of the fronds.

Pteris megaphylla plays an important ecological role in its habitat by contributing to soil stability and providing shelter for various organisms.
