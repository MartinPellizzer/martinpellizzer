Plectranthus buchananii

Plectranthus buchananii, commonly known as the African violet or Malva grandiflora, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and produces clusters of violet or purple flowers. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers partial to full sun exposure.

Plectranthus buchananii is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and showy blooms.
