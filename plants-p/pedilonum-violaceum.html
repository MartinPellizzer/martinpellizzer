Pedilonum violaceum

Pedilonum violaceum, commonly known as the violet-footed orchid, is a species of flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking violet-colored petals and sepals. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist forest environments, where it thrives in the understory of dense vegetation. Its flowers are small but fragrant, attracting various pollinators such as bees and moths.

Due to its habitat specificity, Pedilonum violaceum is considered a rare and endangered species, highlighting the importance of conservation efforts in preserving its natural environment.
