Praxelis peduncularis

Praxelis peduncularis, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 10 meters tall, featuring dark green, oval-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by red, fleshy berries. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in disturbed soils.

Its botanical characteristics make it both ecologically significant and potentially invasive in certain regions.
