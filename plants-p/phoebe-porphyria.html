Phoebe porphyria

Phoebe porphyria, commonly known as the purple phoebe, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a member of the Lauraceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like laurel and cinnamon. The plant is characterized by its dark green, glossy leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. It produces small, dark purple berries that are edible but not particularly palatable.

Phoebe porphyria thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas.
