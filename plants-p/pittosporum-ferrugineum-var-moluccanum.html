Pittosporum ferrugineum var. moluccanum

Pittosporum ferrugineum var. moluccanum, commonly known as the Moluccan Pittosporum, is a shrub or small tree native to the Moluccas in Indonesia.

It is characterized by its dark green, glossy leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a slightly serrated margin. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by orange-red berries. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and prefers well-drained soil.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and floral display.
