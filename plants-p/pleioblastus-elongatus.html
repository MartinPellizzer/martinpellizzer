Pleioblastus elongatus

Pleioblastus elongatus, commonly known as the elongated bamboo, is a species of grass-like plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and can reach heights of up to 3 meters. Its foliage is often a deep green, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in the spring.

Pleioblastus elongatus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping and traditional Chinese gardens.
