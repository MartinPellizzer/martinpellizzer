Pachypleuria lepida

Pachypleuria lepida, commonly known as the silverleaf pachypleura, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features fleshy, oval-shaped leaves that are covered in a fine, silvery pubescence, which helps reduce water loss in arid conditions. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers during the summer months, adding a subtle ornamental value. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Its compact growth habit makes it an ideal choice for rock gardens or container planting.
