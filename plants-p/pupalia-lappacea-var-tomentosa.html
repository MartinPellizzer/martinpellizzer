Pupalia lappacea var. tomentosa

Pupalia lappacea var. tomentosa, commonly known as the woolly poppy, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its hairy, grayish foliage and showy, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are palmately lobed, and the stems are covered in a dense layer of fine, white hairs.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively drought-tolerant once established.
