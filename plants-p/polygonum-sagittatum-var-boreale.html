Polygonum sagittatum var. boreale

Polygonum sagittatum var. boreale, commonly known as the northern arrowhead, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to northern regions of North America.

It typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. The plant features arrowhead-shaped leaves that are alternate and often have a distinct veining pattern. Its flowers are small and clustered in spikes, typically appearing in shades of pink to red.

This variety is notable for its adaptability to cold climates and its role in supporting local wildlife.
