Primula × ternovania

Primula × ternovania, commonly known as the hybrid primrose, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant typically grows to a height of 10–30 cm and features broad, hairy leaves that are arranged in a basal rosette. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and botanical collections worldwide.
