Petrocoptis pyrenaica subsp. wiedmannii

Petrocoptis pyrenaica subsp.

wiedmannii, commonly known as the Pyrenean rock-cress, is a member of the Brassicaceae family. It is a low-growing perennial herb characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and small, white or pale yellow flowers.

Native to the Pyrenees region, it thrives in rocky and alpine environments. The plant has narrow, linear leaves and produces pods that contain several seeds.

Its botanical features make it well adapted to harsh, high-altitude conditions.
