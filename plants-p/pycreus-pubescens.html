Pycreus pubescens

Pycreus pubescens, commonly known as the hairy pycreus, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Acanthaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, and thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a hairy texture, giving it its common name. Its flowers are typically pink or red, arranged in spikes, and are attractive to pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
