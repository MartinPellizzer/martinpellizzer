Peperomia turbinata

Peperomia turbinata, commonly known as the Turban Peperomia, is a compact, evergreen perennial native to tropical regions of South America.

It features fleshy, dark green, waxy leaves that are often shaped like turbans, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters, typically in shades of white or green, which are followed by reddish-brown seed pods. It thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Due to its slow growth and attractive foliage, it is often used as a decorative houseplant.
