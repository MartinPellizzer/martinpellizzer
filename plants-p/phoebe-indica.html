Phoebe indica

Phoebe indica, commonly known as the Indian phoebe, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels and cinnamon trees. The plant features broad, glossy leaves and produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species in ecological studies and horticulture.
