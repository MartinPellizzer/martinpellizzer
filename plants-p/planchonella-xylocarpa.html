Planchonella xylocarpa

Planchonella xylocarpa, commonly known as the black hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Pacific and Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized tree that typically grows up to 10 meters in height, with smooth, greyish-brown bark. The plant produces fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, often accompanied by distinctive, heart-shaped leaves. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, brown seeds.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in some Pacific cultures.
