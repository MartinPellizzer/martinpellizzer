Pilosella cymiflora subsp. tubulata

Pilosella cymiflora subsp.

tubulata, commonly known as the tubular pilosella, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads.

The plant typically grows in open grasslands and rocky areas, preferring well-drained soils. It produces small, yellow to pale orange flowers arranged in cymes, which bloom in spring.

The subspecies is distinguished by its tubular florets and hairy stems, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
