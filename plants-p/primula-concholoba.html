Primula concholoba

Primula concholoba, commonly known as the Chinese primrose, is a flowering plant native to China and is part of the Primulaceae family.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of bell-shaped, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. The leaves are typically ovate to elliptical, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value.

The plant is also valued in traditional Chinese medicine for its purported health benefits.
