Pyrus niitakayamensis

Pyrus niitakayamensis, commonly known as the Japanese white pear, is a deciduous tree native to Japan.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its ornamental white flowers and edible fruit. The plant typically grows to a height of 5-10 meters, featuring alternate, ovate leaves with a serrated margin. Its fruit, a small pear-shaped apple, is usually harvested in late summer and is valued for its sweet taste and culinary use.

The species is also appreciated in horticulture for its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to various soil conditions.
