Pitcairnia palmeri

Pitcairnia palmeri, commonly known as Palmer's pitcairnia, is a bromeliad native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that form a water-holding structure, typical of many bromeliads. The plant produces striking, long-lasting inflorescences with vibrant red and orange flowers. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in cloud forests.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for indoor gardening and botanical collections.
