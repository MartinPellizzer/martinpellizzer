Pulsatilla nemorosa

Pulsatilla nemorosa, commonly known as the pasque flower, is a herbaceous perennial native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its showy, showy, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows, woodlands, and alpine regions. Its leaves are palmately divided, and the plant produces a single, erect stem.

Pulsatilla nemorosa is known for its early spring blooming, often appearing before the leaves of many other plants.
