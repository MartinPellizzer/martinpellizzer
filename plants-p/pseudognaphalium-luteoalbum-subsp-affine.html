Pseudognaphalium luteoalbum subsp. affine

Pseudognaphalium luteoalbum subsp.

affine, commonly known as the yellow-flowered false dandelion, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, silver-green leaves.

The plant produces small, yellowish to white composite flowers arranged in a head, which are typically visited by various pollinators. It thrives in dry, open habitats and is often found in grasslands and rocky areas.

The subspecies affine is distinguished by its slightly different floral morphology compared to other subspecies of Pseudognaphalium luteoalbum.
