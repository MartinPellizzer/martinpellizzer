Pseudoraphis spinescens var. depauperata

Pseudoraphis spinescens var. depauperata, commonly known as the sparse spiny agave, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its short, narrow leaves that are typically blue-green in color and covered in a dense layer of white, powdery bloom. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences bearing small, greenish-white flowers. It is adapted to survive in hot, dry environments with minimal water requirements.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental foliage make it a popular choice for xeriscaping and rock gardens.
