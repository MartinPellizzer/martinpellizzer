Primula longifolia

Primula longifolia, commonly known as the long-flowered primrose, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It features erect, hairy stems that bear clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. The leaves are ovate to lanceolate, with a hairy surface and a serrated margin. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in woodland or alpine habitats.

The plant is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its early spring blooms.
