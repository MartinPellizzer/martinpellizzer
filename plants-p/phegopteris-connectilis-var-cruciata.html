Phegopteris connectilis var. cruciata

Phegopteris connectilis var. cruciata, commonly known as the cross-veined water fern, is a variant of the common water fern.

It is a fern that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as wetlands and stream banks. The plant features fronds with a distinctive cross-veining pattern, which is a key identifying characteristic. Its rhizomes are often submerged or partially submerged in water, allowing it to absorb nutrients from the aquatic environment.

The species is known for its adaptability to various water conditions and is often found in temperate regions.
