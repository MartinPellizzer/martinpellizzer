Phlox collina

Phlox collina, commonly known as the eastern phlox, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features upright stems covered in dense clusters of showy, tubular flowers that bloom in shades of pink, red, and white. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its foliage consists of opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, white powdery coating.

Phlox collina is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to attract pollinators such as butterflies and bees.
