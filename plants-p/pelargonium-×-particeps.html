Pelargonium × particeps

Pelargonium × particeps, commonly known as the hybrid geranium, is a popular ornamental plant characterized by its showy, clustered flowers.

It is a cross between species of the genus Pelargonium, resulting in a variety that exhibits a range of flower colors, including pink, red, and white. The plant typically has rounded, velvety leaves and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal in gardens and as a potted plant.

Pelargonium × particeps is relatively easy to care for, requiring well-draining soil and moderate watering.
