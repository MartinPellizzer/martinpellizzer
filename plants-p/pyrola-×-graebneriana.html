Pyrola × graebneriana

Pyrola × graebneriana, commonly known as the Graebner's wintergreen, is a hybrid evergreen shrub native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes blueberries and heathers, and is characterized by its dense, moss-like foliage. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pink, which bloom in early spring. Its leaves are dark green and remain evergreen throughout the year, and it thrives in acidic, well-drained soils.

Pyrola × graebneriana is often found in shaded forest understories and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
