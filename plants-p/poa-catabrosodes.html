Poa catabrosodes

Poa catabrosodes, commonly known as the tussock bentgrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern South America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and dense, low-growing foliage. The plant typically thrives in moist, temperate climates and is often found in meadows and grasslands. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, with a bluish-green coloration.

Poa catabrosodes is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping and restoration projects.
