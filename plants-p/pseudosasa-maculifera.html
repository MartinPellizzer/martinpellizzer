Pseudosasa maculifera

Pseudosasa maculifera, commonly known as the spotted bamboo, is a perennial grass native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, often zigzagging stems and distinctive leaf patterns. The leaves are typically narrow and have a bluish-green hue with white or silver markings. This plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in mountainous regions.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional landscaping.
