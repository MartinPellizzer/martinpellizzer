Prunus floridula

Prunus floridula, commonly known as the Florida cherry, is a small evergreen shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and glossy dark green leaves. The plant produces small, white or pink flowers in the spring, followed by edible, sweet-tart berries that are popular among birds and wildlife. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable ornamental plant for coastal and sandy soils.

Its hardy nature and adaptability make it a valuable addition to native plant gardens.
