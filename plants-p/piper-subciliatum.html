Piper subciliatum

Piper subciliatum, commonly known as the pink pepper plant, is a climbing shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves with serrated edges and small, clustered flowers. Its fruit, a tiny berry, changes color from green to red as it matures.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of spices.
