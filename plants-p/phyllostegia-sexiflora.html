Phyllostegia sexiflora

Phyllostegia sexiflora, commonly known as the six-flowered phyllostegia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves and showy, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

The species is notable for its ornamental value and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
