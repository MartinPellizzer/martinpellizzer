Poivrea intermedia

Poivrea intermedia, commonly known as the common broom.

It is a flowering shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia, characterized by its slender, branching stems and clusters of yellow flowers. The plant produces small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in pairs along the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for various garden conditions.

Poivrea intermedia is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
