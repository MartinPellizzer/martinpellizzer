Patagonium coronilloides var. glaucum

Patagonium coronilloides var. glaucum, commonly known as the Glaucous Patagonian Cactus, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of southern South America.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems that store water, allowing it to survive in dry environments. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom during the warmer months. Its leaves are typically reduced to spines, which help reduce water loss and deter herbivores.

This variety is distinguished by its bluish-gray foliage, which reflects sunlight and helps it retain moisture.
