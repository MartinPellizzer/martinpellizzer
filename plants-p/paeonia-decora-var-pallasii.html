Paeonia decora var. pallasii

Paeonia decora var. pallasii, commonly known as the Pallas' peony, is a species of herbaceous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, often with a distinctive red eye in the center. The plant features compound leaves with serrated edges and a robust root system that helps it thrive in well-drained soils. It blooms in late spring to early summer, adding ornamental value to gardens.

This variety is valued for its beauty and is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal in horticulture.
