Pseudogynoxys cummingii

Pseudogynoxys cummingii, commonly known as the Mexican sunflower, is a species of flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single bloom. The plant typically grows as an annual herb with erect, branching stems and hairy foliage. Its yellow-orange flower heads consist of numerous small florets arranged in a disc shape, with prominent bracts.

Pseudogynoxys cummingii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
