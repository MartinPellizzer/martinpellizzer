Paracaryum leventshikii

Paracaryum leventshikii, commonly known as the African cycad, is a member of the Cycadaceae family and is native to southern Africa.

It is a slow-growing, evergreen plant characterized by its stout, unbranched trunk and large, pinnate leaves. The plant produces cones rather than flowers, with male and female cones occurring on separate plants. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires minimal water, making it adaptable to arid environments.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
