Piper abutilifolium

Piper abutilifolium, commonly known as the giant pepper vine, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many species of pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. Its fruits are elongated berries that turn red when mature, containing a single seed.

The botanical features of Piper abutilifolium make it a notable species in the study of tropical flora.
