Paepalanthus argillicola var. pilosus

Paepalanthus argillicola var. pilosus, commonly known as the silky clay plant, is a perennial herb native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, wiry stems and small, clustered leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces clusters of yellow flowers during the dry season, which are adapted to attract specific pollinators. It thrives in well-drained, clay-rich soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its unique adaptations, it is an important species in its native ecosystem and is often studied for its resilience in harsh environments.
