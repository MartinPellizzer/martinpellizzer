Phyllostachys edulis f. purpurescens

Phyllostachys edulis f. purpurescens, commonly known as purple bamboo, is a variant of the common bamboo species characterized by its striking purple foliage.

This ornamental plant belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is native to eastern Asia. It features slender, hollow stems that grow in clusters, often reaching heights of several meters. The leaves are narrow and have a distinctive purplish hue, which becomes more intense in cooler weather.

It is widely cultivated for its aesthetic value in gardens and as a decorative plant in landscaping.
