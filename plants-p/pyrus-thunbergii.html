Pyrus thunbergii

Pyrus thunbergii, commonly known as the Japanese whitebeam, is a deciduous tree native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its small, white flowers and smooth, dark green leaves. The tree produces small, yellow or red berries that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans. It thrives in temperate climates and is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value.

Pyrus thunbergii is also valued for its ability to tolerate urban environments and air pollution.
