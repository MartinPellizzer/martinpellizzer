Phyllanthus caudatus var. pubescens

Phyllanthus caudatus var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy crassno.

This variant is a member of the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, hairy foliage. The leaves are typically alternate, ovate to elliptic in shape, and covered with fine pubescence. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in cyathia, typical of the Euphorbiaceae family.

The plant is native to tropical and subtropical regions and is often found in moist, shaded environments.
