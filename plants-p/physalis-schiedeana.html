Physalis schiedeana

Physalis schiedeana, commonly known as the Brazilian lantern or golden berry, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its vibrant orange, lantern-shaped fruits. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in open woods or along stream banks. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with five petals.

The fruit, which matures in late summer, is edible and has a sweet-tart flavor, often used in culinary applications.
