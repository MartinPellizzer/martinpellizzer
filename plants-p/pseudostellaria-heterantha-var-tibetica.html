Pseudostellaria heterantha var. tibetica

Pseudostellaria heterantha var. tibetica, commonly known as the Tibetan starwort, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Tibetan Plateau.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit. The plant features slender, branched stems covered in fine hairs and produces small, white or pale pink flowers clustered in umbels. Its leaves are typically opposite, ovate to lanceolate, and have a slightly hairy surface.

This variant is adapted to cold, arid environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes.
