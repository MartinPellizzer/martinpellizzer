Prunus persica var. uninensis

Prunus persica var. uninensis, commonly known as the Japanese white peach, is a variety of peach native to Japan.

It is characterized by its smooth, white flesh and relatively small, round fruits. The plant typically grows as a deciduous shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and ovate with a serrated margin.

The flowers are white with a slight pinkish tinge and bloom in early spring before the fruit develops.
