Parodia perplexa

Parodia perplexa, commonly known as the ghost cactus, is a species of cactus native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its unique, spindly stems that resemble twisted branches, giving it a ghostly appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the warmer months, typically in spring or early summer. Its thick, fleshy stems are adapted to store water, allowing it to thrive in harsh, dry environments.

Parodia perplexa is a popular choice among succulent enthusiasts due to its striking appearance and low maintenance requirements.
