Pleioblastus pandus

Pleioblastus pandus, commonly known as the golden bamboo, is a species of grass in the family Poaceae.

It is native to eastern Asia, including China, Japan, and Korea, and is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often a striking yellow-green color, especially in the spring. It is a clump-forming bamboo that can reach heights of up to 3 meters, making it suitable for ornamental landscaping.

Pleioblastus pandus is also valued for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions and its relatively low maintenance requirements.
