Patropyrum tauschii subsp. salinum

Patropyrum tauschii subsp.

salinum, commonly known as the saltbush, is a perennial shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions. It is characterized by its small, narrow leaves that are adapted to reduce water loss in saline environments.

The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. It has a deep root system that allows it to access water from deeper soil layers.

This subspecies is notable for its ability to thrive in high-salinity soils, making it an important plant in ecological restoration projects.
