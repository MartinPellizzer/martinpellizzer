Pyrus hondoensis var. lasiogyna

Pyrus hondoensis var. lasiogyna, commonly known as the Hondo pear, is a rare variant native to Japan.

It is characterized by its distinctive, rough-textured leaves and small, yellowish-green fruits. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often in mountainous regions. Its flowers are clustered and have a subtle fragrance, attracting pollinators like bees.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a valuable species for botanical study and conservation efforts.
