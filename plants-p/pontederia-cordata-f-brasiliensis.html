Pontederia cordata f. brasiliensis

Pontederia cordata f. brasiliensis, commonly known as the Brazilian water hyacinth, is a variant of the water hyacinth native to South America.

It is a semi-aquatic plant characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves and showy, violet-blue flowers that bloom in dense clusters. The plant thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, often forming dense mats on the water surface. Its rapid growth rate makes it an effective indicator of nutrient-rich water conditions.

However, it can become invasive if not managed, impacting local ecosystems.
