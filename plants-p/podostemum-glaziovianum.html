Podostemum glaziovianum

Podostemum glaziovianum, commonly known as the Glaziovian water primrose, is a unique aquatic plant found in fast-flowing streams and rivers in tropical regions.

It is characterized by its small, floating leaves that are often anchored to rocks or submerged substrates. The plant has specialized structures called hydroids, which help it attach to surfaces in turbulent waters. Its flowers are typically small and clustered, with bright colors that attract pollinators.

Podostemum glaziovianum plays an important ecological role in its habitat by providing shelter for aquatic organisms and contributing to water quality regulation.
