Pelargonium abrotanifolium

Pelargonium abrotanifolium, commonly known as the African geranium, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of small, five-petaled flowers in shades of red, pink, and white. The leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

This species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
