Polystachya virescens

Polystachya virescens, commonly known as the green-stemmed bamboo orchid, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and dense clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as forest understories, and thrives in humid climates. Its leaves are elongated and lance-shaped, often with a waxy coating that helps retain moisture.

Polystachya virescens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique appearance.
