Phycoschoenus isoetifolia

Phycoschoenus isoetifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved water starwort, is a member of the plantain family (Plantaginaceae).

It is a submerged aquatic plant characterized by its slender, ribbon-like leaves that are often arranged in a spiral pattern along its stem. This species typically grows in shallow, freshwater environments such as ponds, lakes, and slow-moving streams. Its leaves are usually narrow and have a waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss in aquatic settings.

Phycoschoenus isoetifolia plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various invertebrates and small fish.
