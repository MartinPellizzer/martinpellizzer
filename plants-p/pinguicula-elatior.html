Pinguicula elatior

Pinguicula elatior, commonly known as the greater butterwort, is a carnivorous plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its glandular, sticky leaves that trap small insects for nutrition. The plant typically grows in moist, nutrient-poor environments such as bogs and wetlands. Its flowers are usually pink or purple and are borne on tall, erect stems.

Pinguicula elatior is a perennial herb that thrives in cool, humid conditions.
