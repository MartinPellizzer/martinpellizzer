Polystichum aculeatum var. travancoricum

Polystichum aculeatum var. travancoricum, commonly known as the Travancore holly fern, is a variant of the holly fern native to the Western Ghats of India.

It is a perennial evergreen fern characterized by its dark green, leathery fronds with serrated margins. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and rocky crevices. Its fronds are pinnate, with a dense arrangement of small, spore-producing pinnae.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in shaded garden settings.
