Patrinia villosa subsp. punctifolia

Patrinia villosa subsp.

punctifolia, commonly known as the spotted beardtongue, is a perennial herb native to China. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and mountainous regions.

The plant features slender, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture. Its flowers are pink to purple, arranged in dense clusters, and bloom in the summer months.

It is valued in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits and is also cultivated as an ornamental plant.
