Philadelphus microphyllus subsp. typicus

Philadelphus microphyllus subsp.

typicus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved mock orange, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States. It features small, ovate leaves that are typically less than 2 inches long, giving it its "microphyllus" designation.

The plant produces fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers in clusters during late spring to early summer. Its botanical characteristics include compound cymes and stipitate glands on the leaves.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in partial shade.
