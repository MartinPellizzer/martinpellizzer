Populus simonii f. brachychaeta

Populus simonii f. brachychaeta, commonly known as the short-branch poplar, is a unique horticultural form of the poplar tree native to China.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and shorter, more densely packed branches compared to the typical Populus simonii. The leaves are heart-shaped, with a matte green upper surface and a lighter, sometimes whitish underside. This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and parks due to its attractive appearance and adaptability to various soil conditions.

It is also valued for its ability to thrive in urban environments, making it a popular choice for landscaping.
