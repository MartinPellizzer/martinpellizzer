Psoralea zeyheri

Psoralea zeyheri, commonly known as the Cape Flats psoralea, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a small, herbaceous annual that typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the warmer months. Its leaves are narrow and often hairy, adapting to arid conditions.

It is part of the legume family, Fabaceae, and has the potential to fix nitrogen in the soil.
