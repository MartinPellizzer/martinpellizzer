Physosiphon deregularis

Physosiphon deregularis, commonly known as the irregular siphon plant, is a unique species within the Physosiphon genus.

It is characterized by its elongated, tubular leaves that often curve and twist, forming intricate spirals. The plant exhibits a specialized reproductive structure called a siphon, which facilitates the transfer of nutrients between its symbiotic partners. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Due to its rare occurrence and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a conservation concern in many areas.
