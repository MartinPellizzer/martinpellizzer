Primula villosa

Primula villosa, commonly known as the hairy primrose, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and clusters of bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and meadows. It is a perennial species that thrives in cool climates and is often found in alpine environments.

The flowers bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
