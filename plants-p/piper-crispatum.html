Piper crispatum

Piper crispatum, commonly known as the curly pipevine, is a tropical climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Piperaceae and is characterized by its finely serrated, dark green leaves that are often curled or crinkled. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are typically clustered in spikes, followed by elliptical, blue-black berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and traditional herbal practices.
