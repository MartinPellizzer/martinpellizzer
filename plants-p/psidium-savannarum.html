Psidium savannarum

Psidium savannarum, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or pitanga, is a small evergreen tree native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, white flowers. The plant produces edible berries that range in color from yellow to red, depending on the variety and ripeness. These berries are rich in antioxidants and are often used in culinary and medicinal applications.

Psidium savannarum thrives in well-drained soils and requires a warm, humid climate for optimal growth.
