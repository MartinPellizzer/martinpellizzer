Pyrus albopilosa

Pyrus albopilosa, commonly known as the white-haired pear, is a species of flowering plant in the family Rosaceae.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, particularly in countries like Kazakhstan and Uzbekistan. The plant is characterized by its distinctive white or pale hairy foliage, which gives it its common name. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by edible, sweet fruits that are similar to common pears.

Pyrus albopilosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and its potential for cultivation in suitable climates.
