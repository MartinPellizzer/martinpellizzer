Pitcairnia weddelliana

Pitcairnia weddelliana, commonly known as the Weddell's pitcairnia, is a species of flowering plant in the Bromeliaceae family.

It is native to the Andes mountains of South America, particularly in Ecuador and Peru. This evergreen perennial is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that form a water-holding cup, a common feature among bromeliads. The plant produces striking, long-lasting inflorescences with bright red or orange flowers.

It thrives in shaded, humid environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal.
