Perplexia microcephala

Perplexia microcephala, commonly known as the tiny-headed wonder, is a unique species of flowering plant characterized by its small, rounded leaves and delicate, pale yellow flowers.

It is native to the high-altitude regions of South America, where it thrives in cool, moist environments. The plant has a short growth cycle, blooming once a year before entering a period of dormancy. Its leaves are covered in a fine, waxy coating that helps reduce water loss in its arid habitat.

Due to its rarity and specific ecological requirements, Perplexia microcephala is considered a conservation concern.
