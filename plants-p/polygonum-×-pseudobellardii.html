Polygonum × pseudobellardii

Polygonum × pseudobellardii, commonly known as the hybrid persicaria, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Polygonaceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces showy, clustered flowers in shades of pink to red, which bloom in late summer. It is native to North America and thrives in moist, sunny habitats such as wet meadows and stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is often cultivated in gardens and wetland restoration projects.
