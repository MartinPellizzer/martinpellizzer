Polygala senega var. rosea

Polygala senega var. rosea, commonly known as the rose-flowered milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the central and eastern United States.

It is characterized by its delicate, pink to rose-colored flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and thrives in open habitats such as prairies and woodland edges. Its leaves are opposite and pinnately compound, with small, lance-shaped leaflets.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in native plant gardens.
