Pandanus comatus

Pandanus comatus, commonly known as the hairy pandanus, is a tropical plant native to the Indo-Pacific region.

It is characterized by its long, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine, hair-like structures. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its inflorescence consists of a branched cluster of small, fragrant flowers.

The fruit is a spiky, fibrous structure that contains edible seeds, often used in traditional foods and crafts.
