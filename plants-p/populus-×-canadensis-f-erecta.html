Populus × canadensis f. erecta

Populus × canadensis f. erecta, commonly known as the upright Canadian poplar, is a hybrid species within the Populus genus.

It is characterized by its tall, straight growth habit and broad, heart-shaped leaves that are typically a vibrant green during the growing season. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in catkins, which are pollinated by wind. It is native to North America and thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

This variety is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its striking appearance and rapid growth rate.
