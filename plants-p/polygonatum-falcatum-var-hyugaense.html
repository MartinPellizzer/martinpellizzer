Polygonatum falcatum var. hyugaense

Polygonatum falcatum var. hyugaense, commonly known as the Hyuga snake plant, is a variant of the Solomon's seal genus native to Japan.

It is characterized by its long, narrow, grass-like leaves that can grow up to 1.5 meters in length. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in late spring, followed by red berries in the fall. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in mountainous regions.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in traditional Japanese gardens.
