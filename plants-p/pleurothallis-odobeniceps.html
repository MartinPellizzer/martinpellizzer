Pleurothallis odobeniceps

Pleurothallis odobeniceps, commonly known as the "elephant tusk orchid," is a species of orchid native to the cloud forests of Central and South America.

It is a small, epiphytic plant that thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant produces delicate, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow in color. Its roots are adapted to absorb moisture from the air and decaying organic matter.

Pleurothallis odobeniceps is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
