Phlox kelseyi var. costata

Phlox kelseyi var. costata, commonly known as the Costate phlox, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of showy, pink to lavender flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in a fine, white to grayish pubescence, giving the plant a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

This variety is noted for its compact size and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
