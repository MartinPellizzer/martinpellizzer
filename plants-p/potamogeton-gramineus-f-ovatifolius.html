Potamogeton gramineus f. ovatifolius

Potamogeton gramineus f. ovatifolius, commonly known as the broadleaf pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in North America.

It is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves that are typically arranged in whorls around the stem. The plant produces small, green flowers that are clustered in spikes, and it thrives in shallow, nutrient-rich waters. Its rhizomes help stabilize sediment and provide habitat for aquatic invertebrates.

This plant plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by contributing to water clarity and supporting biodiversity.
