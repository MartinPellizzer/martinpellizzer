Polystachya valentina

Polystachya valentina, commonly known as the Valentine's orchid, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Central and South America.

It is a terrestrial orchid characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers. The plant typically blooms in the spring and is noted for its delicate, fragrant blossoms. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and requires well-drained soil.

Polystachya valentina is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
