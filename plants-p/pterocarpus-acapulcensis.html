Pterocarpus acapulcensis

Pterocarpus acapulcensis, commonly known as the Honduras rosewood, is a tropical tree native to Central America and parts of the Caribbean.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive, fragrant flowers and thick, dark bark. The tree produces flat, pod-like fruits that contain flat, winged seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Its heartwood is highly valued for its rich color and durability, making it a prized material in furniture and musical instrument crafting.
