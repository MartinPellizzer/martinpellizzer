Piper levilimbum

Piper levilimbum, commonly known as the African pepper or black pepper vine, is a climbing shrub native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its long, twisting stems and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are followed by bright red, fleshy berries containing elliptical seeds. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their aromatic and analgesic properties.

The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
