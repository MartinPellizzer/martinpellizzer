Primula denticulata subsp. hoffmeisteri

Primula denticulata subsp.

hoffmeisteri, commonly known as the Hoffmeister's primrose, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It features rosettes of dark green, hairy, and slightly toothed leaves that form a dense basal cushion.

The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, or lilac, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and unique appearance, it is cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
