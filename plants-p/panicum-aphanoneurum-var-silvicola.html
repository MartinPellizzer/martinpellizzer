Panicum aphanoneurum var. silvicola

Panicum aphanoneurum var. silvicola, commonly known as the forest panicgrass, is a perennial grass native to eastern North America.

It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as swamps, marshes, and forest understories. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are often reddish or purplish in color. It is characterized by its low-growing habit and ability to thrive in shaded environments.

This species plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
