Pulicaria armena

Pulicaria armena, commonly known as Armenian groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in rocky and gravelly soils, thriving in temperate and alpine climates. Its leaves are grayish-green with a woolly texture, and its flowers are yellow with white ray florets.

It is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory properties.
