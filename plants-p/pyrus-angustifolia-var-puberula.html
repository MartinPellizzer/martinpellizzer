Pyrus angustifolia var. puberula

Pyrus angustifolia var. puberula, commonly known as the pubescent narrowleaf pear, is a variant of the narrowleaf pear species.

It is characterized by its slender, elongated leaves that are covered in fine, soft hairs, giving them a fuzzy texture. The plant typically grows in temperate regions and prefers well-drained soils. Its flowers are small and greenish-white, appearing in clusters, and it produces edible fruit that is often used in culinary applications.

This variety is valued for both its ornamental appeal and its fruit-bearing qualities.
