Puccinellia laurentiana

Puccinellia laurentiana, commonly known as Laurentian rush, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It typically grows in wetland and marshy environments, preferring moist to saturated soils. The plant has slender, cylindrical stems that often form dense clumps, and its leaves are narrow and grass-like. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

This species plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wetland organisms.
