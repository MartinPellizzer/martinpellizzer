Pilosella iserana subsp. subconfinium

Pilosella iserana subsp.

subconfinium, commonly known as the Alpine pearly everlasting, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the alpine regions of Europe. It is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit and produces small, white to pale yellow flowers with a pearly appearance.

The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are covered in fine, white hairs, giving it a silvery appearance. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine meadows and mountainous areas.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate, long-lasting flowers.
