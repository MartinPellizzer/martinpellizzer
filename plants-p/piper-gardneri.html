Piper gardneri

Piper gardneri, commonly known as the Gardner's pepper plant, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its climbing habit, with slender stems and heart-shaped leaves. Its small, greenish-white flowers are followed by tiny, peppery fruits that are edible.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of mild spices.
