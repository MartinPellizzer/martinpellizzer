Panicum hirstii

Panicum hirstii, commonly known as Hirst's panicgrass, is a species of grass native to the tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, wiry leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, two-lobed flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
