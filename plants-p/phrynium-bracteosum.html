Phrynium bracteosum

Phrynium bracteosum, commonly known as the broadleaf arrowroot, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is a herbaceous perennial with broad, arrow-shaped leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in a spadix, typical of the Araceae family. Its rhizomes are edible and have a starchy texture similar to arrowroot.

The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental and culinary value.
