Piper filiforme

Piper filiforme, commonly known as the slender pepper vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes various species of pepper plants. The plant features small, opposite, ovate leaves that are usually glossy and dark green. Its flowers are unisexual, with male and female flowers occurring on separate plants, and it produces small, berry-like fruits.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of aromatic leaves used in traditional medicine.
