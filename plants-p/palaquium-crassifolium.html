Palaquium crassifolium

Palaquium crassifolium, commonly known as the broadleaf palaquium, is a small evergreen tree native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its thick, leathery leaves that are typically elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by oval, fleshy fruits containing a single seed. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in the understorey of tropical rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation projects.
