Palala geminata

Palala geminata, commonly known as the blackwood tree, is a deciduous tree native to parts of southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Fabaceae and is characterized by its dark, dense bark and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by elongated pods containing flat, dark seeds. It is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is valued for its hard, durable wood.

Palala geminata plays an important role in local ecosystems and has traditional uses in various indigenous communities.
