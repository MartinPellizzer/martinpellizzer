Psidium dichotomum

Psidium dichotomum, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or pitanga, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to South America.

It produces small, yellow to red berries that are edible and have a sweet, tangy flavor. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. Its flowers are white or pale green and are clustered in small inflorescences.

The fruit is often used in culinary applications and traditional medicine due to its nutritional and medicinal properties.
