Panicum indicum

Panicum indicum, commonly known as Indian millet, is an annual grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, cylindrical spikelets and fine, thread-like leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, disturbed soils and is well adapted to arid environments. It produces small, pale green to brown flowers that develop into hard, edible seeds.

Panicum indicum is valued for its nutritional content and is used as a traditional food crop in many parts of Asia.
