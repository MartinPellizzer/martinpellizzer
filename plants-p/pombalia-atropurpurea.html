Pombalia atropurpurea

Pombalia atropurpurea, commonly known as the purple pombalia, is a striking perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant purple foliage and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine displays. Its leaves are broad and velvety, adding a unique texture to its appearance.

Pombalia atropurpurea is also valued for its ornamental qualities and its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
