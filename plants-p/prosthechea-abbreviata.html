Prosthechea abbreviata

Prosthechea abbreviata, commonly known as the short-stemmed orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and possesses a pseudobulb that supports its survival in shaded environments. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that bloom during the wet season. Its leaves are elliptical and arranged in a rosette pattern, aiding in moisture retention.

Prosthechea abbreviata is often found in humid, forested areas where it thrives in the understory with filtered sunlight.
