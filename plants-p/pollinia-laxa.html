Pollinia laxa

Pollinia laxa, commonly known as the lax pollinia orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its unique pollinia, which are masses of pollen grains that are loosely attached to a sticky base. These pollinia are typically found in clusters and are adapted for specific pollination mechanisms. The plant is native to certain regions of South America and prefers moist, shaded environments.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for both horticultural study and conservation efforts.
