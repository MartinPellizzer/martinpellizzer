Padus avium var. rufoferruginea

Padus avium var. rufoferruginea, commonly known as the red-stemmed bird cherry, is a variant of the bird cherry species native to Europe.

It is characterized by its distinctive reddish-brown stems and small, yellowish-white flowers that appear in clusters. The leaves are typically oval with a serrated edge and a slightly hairy texture. This plant prefers well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or open areas.

It produces small, red berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife.
