Poa attenuata var. desertorum

Poa attenuata var. desertorum, commonly known as the desert bluegrass, is a perennial grass native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its fine, blue-green foliage and compact growth habit, which allows it to thrive in harsh, dry environments. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. Its deep root system enables it to access moisture from deeper soil layers, making it drought-tolerant.

This species is often found in rocky soils and is a key component of native grassland ecosystems in desert regions.
