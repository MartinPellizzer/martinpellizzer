Polia

Polia, commonly known as the polia vine, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Malvaceae and is characterized by its long, trailing stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that are often clustered in racemes. Its fruits are capsule-like structures that release seeds when mature.

Polia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
