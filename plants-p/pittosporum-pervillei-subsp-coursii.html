Pittosporum pervillei subsp. coursii

Pittosporum pervillei subsp.

coursii, commonly known as the New Caledonia pittosporum, is a shrub or small tree native to the island of New Caledonia. It is characterized by its aromatic, dark green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that are typically clustered in loose inflorescences. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, winged seeds.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and floral display.
