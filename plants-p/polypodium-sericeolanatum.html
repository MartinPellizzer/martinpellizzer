Polypodium sericeolanatum

Polypodium sericeolanatum, commonly known as the silky cliff fern, is a fern species native to various regions in Asia, including China, Japan, and India.

It is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped fronds covered in fine, silky hairs, which give it its common name. The plant typically grows on rocky surfaces or in shaded, moist environments, often found in mountainous regions. Its reproductive structures, called sori, are located on the underside of the fronds and produce spores for propagation.

This fern is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
