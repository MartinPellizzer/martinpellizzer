Polypodium superficiale var. semilinearis

Polypodium superficiale var. semilinearis, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wall fern, is a variant of the common wall fern native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is a perennial fern characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that often grow in dense clusters on rocky or shaded surfaces. The plant thrives in humid environments and requires well-drained, moist soil to flourish. Its leaves are typically leathery and have a distinctive linear shape, contributing to its ornamental value.

This variant is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to shaded garden settings.
