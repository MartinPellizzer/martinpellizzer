Polygonum sphaerostachyum

Polygonum sphaerostachyum, commonly known as the globe thimbleweed, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its spherical, clustered inflorescences that give it its common name. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are alternate, ovate to elliptic, and often have a waxy coating.

The flowers are small and greenish-white, arranged in dense, globe-like clusters at the ends of the stems.
