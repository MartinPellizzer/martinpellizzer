Pleurothallis kaynagata

Pleurothallis kaynagata, commonly known as the Kaynagata orchid, is a species native to the cloud forests of Ecuador.

It is a small, epiphytic orchid that typically grows on trees in humid, shaded environments. The plant has narrow, linear leaves and produces delicate, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink. It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its sympodial growth pattern.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
