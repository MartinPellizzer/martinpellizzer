Poa halmaturina

Poa halmaturina, commonly known as Halmaturina grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences, which contain the flowers necessary for reproduction. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as plains and rocky areas.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
