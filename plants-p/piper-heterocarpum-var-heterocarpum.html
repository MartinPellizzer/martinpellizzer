Piper heterocarpum var. heterocarpum

Piper heterocarpum var. heterocarpum, commonly known as the West Indian pepper or wild pepper, is a perennial shrub native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its slender, aromatic leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces small, black berries that are used in traditional medicine and as a spice. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in moist, shaded areas of forests.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and ethnobotanical contexts.
