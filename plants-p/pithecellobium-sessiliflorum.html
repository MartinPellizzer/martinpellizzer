Pithecellobium sessiliflorum

Pithecellobium sessiliflorum, commonly known as the sessile-flower acacia, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant features small, yellowish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, often resembling those of acacia species. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three leaflets that are often hairy and have a distinctive shape.

The plant is adapted to grow in dry, well-drained soils and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
