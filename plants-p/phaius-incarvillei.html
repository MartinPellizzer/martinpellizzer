Phaius incarvillei

Phaius incarvillei, commonly known as the incarvillea orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink, red, or purple. The plant features a rhizomatous growth habit and produces broad, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in warm, humid environments with well-draining soil.

Phaius incarvillei is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and greenhouses.
