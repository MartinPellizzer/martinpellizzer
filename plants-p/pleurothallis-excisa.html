Pleurothallis excisa

Pleurothallis excisa, commonly known as the cut-leaf orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, narrow leaves that are often cut or frayed at the edges. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are small and usually white or pale green, with a delicate, fragrant scent.

Pleurothallis excisa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical gardens and greenhouses.
