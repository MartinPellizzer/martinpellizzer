Panicum rugosum

Panicum rugosum, commonly known as rough panicgrass, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is a member of the grass family, Poaceae, and typically grows in open, sunny areas such as prairies and disturbed soils. The plant has narrow, rough-textured leaves and produces small, clustered inflorescences that are often reddish or purplish in color. It is often used in restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and thrive in harsh conditions.

Panicum rugosum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various environmental conditions.
