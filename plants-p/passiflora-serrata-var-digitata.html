Passiflora serrata var. digitata

Passiflora serrata var. digitata, commonly known as the serrated passionflower, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, palmate leaves with serrated edges and clusters of showy, violet-blue flowers. The plant produces edible, orange-yellow berries that are often consumed fresh or used in culinary applications. Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and greenhouses.

The species is also valued for its potential medicinal properties in traditional herbal practices.
