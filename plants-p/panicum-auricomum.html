Panicum auricomum

Panicum auricomum, commonly known as golden panicum, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of golden-yellow inflorescences that give it a striking appearance. The plant produces small, wind-pollinated flowers that develop into three-angled, hairy seeds. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, often found in prairies and disturbed areas.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
