Plantago montana var. carpatica

Plantago montana var. carpatica, commonly known as the Carpathian plantain, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Carpathian Mountains.

It features upright, hairy stems that bear lance-shaped leaves with prominent veins. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in dense clusters, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in natural habitats.
