Pelargonium keeromsbergense

Pelargonium keeromsbergense, commonly known as Keeromsberg geranium, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink and white. The leaves are typically rounded with a slightly hairy texture, and the plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and containers.

This species is noted for its resilience and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
