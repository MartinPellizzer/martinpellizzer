Poterium minus

Poterium minus, commonly known as the lesser poterium, is a small, herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features delicate, grass-like leaves that are usually less than 10 centimeters in length. Its flowering stems produce tiny, inconspicuous flowers that are often overlooked.

Poterium minus is known for its ability to thrive in nutrient-poor soils and is often found in association with other moisture-loving plants.
