Passiflora × buonapartea

Passiflora × buonapartea, commonly known as the Napoleon passionflower, is a hybrid species native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, complex flowers that feature five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments surrounding the reproductive structures. The plant produces edible, fleshy berries that are typically yellow or orange in color. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
