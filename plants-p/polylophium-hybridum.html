Polylophium hybridum

Polylophium hybridum, commonly known as the hybrid sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and a dense, compact growth habit. The plant typically produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are clustered in inflorescences. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds.

Due to its adaptability, it is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects to stabilize soil and support biodiversity.
