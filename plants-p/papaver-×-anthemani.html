Papaver × anthemani

Papaver × anthemani, commonly known as the hybrid poppy, is a botanical hybrid resulting from the cross between Papaver rhoeas (common poppy) and Papaver orientale (oriental poppy).

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from red to orange and yellow. The plant typically grows to a height of 60–120 cm and prefers well-drained soil in full sun. Its petals are often papery and delicate, with a slightly wrinkled texture.

It is widely cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its striking appearance and ease of cultivation.
