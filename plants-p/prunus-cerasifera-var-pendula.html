Prunus cerasifera var. pendula

Prunus cerasifera var. pendula, commonly known as the weeping cherry or weeping almond, is a deciduous tree characterized by its cascading branches that create a distinctive, elegant appearance.

It features small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by edible, sweet red fruits in the summer. The plant is native to southern Europe and western Asia, and it thrives in well-drained soil with full sun exposure. It is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for.

The leaves turn a vibrant yellow in the fall, adding to its seasonal appeal.
