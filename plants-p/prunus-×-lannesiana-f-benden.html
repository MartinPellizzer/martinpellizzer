Prunus × lannesiana f. benden

Prunus × lannesiana f. benden, commonly known as the Japanese apricot, is a botanical variety characterized by its ornamental value and unique floral display.

This plant is a hybrid of Prunus mandshurica and Prunus serrulata, showcasing traits from both parent species. It typically produces showy, single or double pink to white flowers in early spring before the leaves emerge. The foliage is dark green and dense, providing a striking contrast to the blossoms.

It is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal and is relatively easy to care for in temperate climates.
