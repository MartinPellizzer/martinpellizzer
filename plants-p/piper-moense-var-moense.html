Piper moense var. moense

Piper moense var. moense, commonly known as the sweet pepper plant, is a species native to tropical regions of South America.

It is a climbing vine characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces small, green berries that turn red when ripe, which are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires support for its vining growth habit.

The berries contain alkaloids and are sometimes used in culinary and medicinal applications.
