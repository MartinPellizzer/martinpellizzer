Pterolepis trichotoma

Pterolepis trichotoma, commonly known as the hairy pterolepis, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Polygonaceae.

It is characterized by its upright, branched stems covered in fine, hairy pubescence. The plant produces small, clustered flowers with five petals, typically in shades of pink or white. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Pterolepis trichotoma is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local insect populations.
