Poa annua f. latisquamea

Poa annua f. latisquamea, commonly known as the broad-scaled annual bluegrass, is a variant of the annual bluegrass species.

It is characterized by its broad, flat leaf blades and distinctive, often pale blue-green foliage. This form is typically found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of climates. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with numerous tiny flowers.

It is often considered a weed in turfgrass settings due to its rapid growth and adaptability.
