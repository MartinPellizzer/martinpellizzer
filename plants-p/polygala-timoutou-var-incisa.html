Polygala timoutou var. incisa

Polygala timoutou var. incisa, commonly known as the narrow-leaved milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Polygalaceae family and is characterized by its slender, often branched stems and small, delicate flowers. The leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, with a hairy texture. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in open grasslands and rocky habitats.

The plant is known for its fragrant flowers, which attract various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
