Parinari polyandra subsp. floribunda

Parinari polyandra subsp.

floribunda, commonly known as the red-flowered parinari, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to tropical regions of Africa. It is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and clusters of small, bright red flowers that bloom in dense inflorescences.

The plant produces elliptical, woody fruits that contain a single seed, which is typically dispersed by animals. It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often found in secondary forests and along riverbanks.

The species is valued for its ornamental value and has some traditional uses in local communities.
