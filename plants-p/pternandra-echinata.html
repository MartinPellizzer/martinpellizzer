Pternandra echinata

Pternandra echinata, commonly known as the spiked pternandra, is a tropical plant native to parts of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its woody stem and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or yellowish in color. Its fruit is a capsule that contains several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Pternandra echinata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
