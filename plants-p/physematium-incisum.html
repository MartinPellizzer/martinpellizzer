Physematium incisum

Physematium incisum, commonly known as the cutleaf liverwort, is a non-vascular plant belonging to the family Porellaceae.

It is characterized by its intricate, lobed leaves that are often deeply incised, giving it a distinctive appearance. This liverwort typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its structure consists of a thallus with differentiated leaves and stems, and it reproduces via spores rather than seeds.

The plant plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for microorganisms in its ecosystem.
