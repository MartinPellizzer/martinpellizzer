Pyrus maulei

Pyrus maulei, commonly known as the Maule pear, is a deciduous tree native to central Chile.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves and clusters of small, white flowers. The tree produces edible pears that are typically harvested in the autumn, and its fruit is valued for its sweet, aromatic flavor. Pyrus maulei is often grown in temperate climates and is adapted to well-drained soils.

It is also noted for its ornamental value, with its attractive bark and seasonal foliage.
