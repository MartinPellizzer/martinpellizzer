Parthenocissus semicordata var. rubrifolia

Parthenocissus semicordata var. rubrifolia, commonly known as the red-leaf ivy.

This variant is a climbing vine native to eastern Asia, characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that turn vibrant red in the fall. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by red or orange berries. It thrives in partial to full shade and is often used in ornamental landscaping for its attractive foliage.

Its adaptability makes it a popular choice for trellises and vertical gardening.
