Panicum rostellatum

Panicum rostellatum, commonly known as the slender panicgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds, thriving in moist to wet soil conditions. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense colonies, making it an important component of wetland ecosystems.

It is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife species.
