Panicularia fluitans var. septentrionalis

Panicularia fluitans var. septentrionalis, commonly known as the northern floating panicled grass, is a aquatic plant native to northern regions.

It thrives in shallow, slow-moving freshwater environments such as ponds and lakes. The plant has long, slender leaves that float on the water's surface, supported by hollow stems. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of small, panicled flowers that bloom in the summer.

This variety is adapted to cold climates and is often found in regions with temperate to subarctic conditions.
