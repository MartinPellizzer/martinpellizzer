Palaquium stellatum

Palaquium stellatum, commonly known as the star apple tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and small, white flowers. The fruit is a yellowish, smooth, round berry that ripens to a reddish color, often containing a single large seed. It is valued for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

The plant thrives in humid, tropical climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
