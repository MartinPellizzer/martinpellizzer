Pleurothallis rhynchoglossa

Pleurothallis rhynchoglossa, commonly known as the hooked-lip pleurothallis, is a species of orchid native to the cloud forests of Central and South America.

It is a small, epiphytic orchid that thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces delicate, fragrant flowers with a distinctive hooked lip. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink, with a contrasting yellow or orange lip.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is a popular choice among orchid enthusiasts and botanists.
