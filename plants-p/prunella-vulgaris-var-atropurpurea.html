Prunella vulgaris var. atropurpurea

Prunella vulgaris var. atropurpurea, commonly known as purple dead-nettle, is a flowering plant in the Lamiaceae family.

It is characterized by its square, hairy stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often purple-tinged on the underside. The plant produces small, two-lipped flowers in whorls along the stem, typically in shades of purple or blue. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodlands, meadows, and along roadsides.

This species is known for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
