Pollinia fimbriata

Pollinia fimbriata, commonly known as the fringed pollinia orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique floral structure, which includes specialized pollinia that are fused into a single mass. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to tree trunks or branches in humid forest environments. Its flowers are usually small and clustered, with a complex arrangement that facilitates pollination by specific insect species.

The plant's botanical significance lies in its role in orchid pollination systems and its ornamental value in horticulture.
