Petalostelma

Petalostelma, commonly known as the glassy vine, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, twining stems and small, translucent, waxy leaves that give it its common name. The flowers are typically small and greenish-white, with five petals that are often mistaken for leaves. Petalostelma is often found growing on trees or shrubs in humid, shaded environments.

It is valued in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
