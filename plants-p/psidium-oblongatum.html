Psidium oblongatum

Psidium oblongatum, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or pitanga, is a small evergreen tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its elliptical, glossy leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant produces small, edible berries that are typically red when ripe, though some varieties may have yellow or orange fruits. These berries are rich in antioxidants and are often used in culinary applications or as a source of natural dye.

The plant thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is valued for both its ornamental and nutritional properties.
