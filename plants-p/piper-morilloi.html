Piper morilloi

Piper morilloi, commonly known as the Morillo pepper plant, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to the humid forests of South America, particularly in Ecuador and Peru. The plant produces small, elongated berries that are used in traditional medicine and as a spice. Its leaves are often used in local cuisines for their pungent flavor.

The botanical characteristics of Piper morilloi include its climbing habit, opposite leaves, and small, yellowish-green flowers.
