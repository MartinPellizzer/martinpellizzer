Phyllobolus knolfonteinensis

Phyllobolus knolfonteinensis, commonly known as the Knolfontein leaf beetle, is a species of leaf beetle native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, elongated body and metallic green coloration, which provides camouflage in its natural habitat. The plant it inhabits, typically found in arid regions, offers the beetle both shelter and a source of food. Its life cycle involves laying eggs on the underside of leaves, where the larvae feed on plant tissue before pupating.

This species plays a role in the ecological balance of its environment by contributing to nutrient cycling through its feeding habits.
