Phalaris canariensis var. subcylindrica

Phalaris canariensis var. subcylindrica, commonly known as the Canary grass, is a perennial grass species native to the Canary Islands.

It is characterized by its slender, cylindrical stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and is adapted to sandy, well-drained soils. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

This variety is often used in landscaping for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in challenging environments.
