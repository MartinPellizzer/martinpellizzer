Pomataphytum pocillatum

Pomataphytum pocillatum, commonly known as the silver leaf saxifrage, is a low-growing succulent plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It features small, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery or whitish coating, which helps to reflect sunlight and reduce water loss. The plant produces delicate, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink or white during the cooler months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its slow growth and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native range.
