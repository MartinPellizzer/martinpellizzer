Potamogeton monoginus

Potamogeton monoginus, commonly known as the narrowleaf pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater habitats across North America.

It has long, slender stems that are often branched and bear small, narrow leaves arranged alternately along the stem. The plant produces tiny, spike-like flowers that are typically green or brown in color, and it reproduces both sexually and asexually through vegetative propagation. It plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.

Potamogeton monoginus is often found in slow-moving water such as lakes, ponds, and wetlands.
