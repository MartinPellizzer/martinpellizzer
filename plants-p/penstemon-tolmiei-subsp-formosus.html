Penstemon tolmiei subsp. formosus

Penstemon tolmiei subsp.

formosus, commonly known as the Sierra penstemon, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils in high-elevation environments.

The plant features showy, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to purple, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in arid conditions.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
