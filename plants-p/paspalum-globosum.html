Paspalum globosum

Paspalum globosum, commonly known as the globular paspalum, is a perennial grass native to South America.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and compact, spherical inflorescences that give it its common name. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetland or riparian habitats. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green coloration.

Paspalum globosum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping for its unique appearance.
