Passiflora cabedelensis

Passiflora cabedelensis, commonly known as the Mexican passionflower, is a climbing vine native to Mexico and Central America.

It is characterized by its striking, complex flowers that feature a blend of blue, purple, and white petals, along with intricate filaments and a crown-like structure. The plant produces edible, orange-yellow berries that are rich in antioxidants and have a tangy flavor. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a velvety texture, while its stems are woody and covered in fine hairs.

Passiflora cabedelensis is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its beautiful blooms and is also valued in traditional medicine.
