Pseudoliparis multiflora

Pseudoliparis multiflora, commonly known as the many-flowered sea holly, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of blue or violet flowers that bloom throughout the summer months. The plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in dune environments. It has fibrous roots and succulent, fleshy leaves that help it retain moisture in arid conditions.

Pseudoliparis multiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in coastal gardens.
