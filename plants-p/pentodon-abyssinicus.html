Pentodon abyssinicus

Pentodon abyssinicus, commonly known as the Abyssinian pentagon, is a unique plant species native to the highlands of Ethiopia.

It is characterized by its distinctive pentagonal-shaped leaves, which are often covered in a fine, waxy coating. The plant thrives in cool, shaded environments and requires well-drained, acidic soil to flourish. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, typically appearing in clusters during the spring months.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
