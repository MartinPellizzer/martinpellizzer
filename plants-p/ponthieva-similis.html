Ponthieva similis

Ponthieva similis, commonly known as the slender bamboo, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest understories, and can reach heights of up to 3 meters. Its culms are hollow and jointed, a typical feature of many grass species.

Ponthieva similis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
