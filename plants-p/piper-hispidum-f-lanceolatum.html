Piper hispidum f. lanceolatum

Piper hispidum f. lanceolatum, commonly known as the hairy pepper plant, is a variant of the Piper hispidum species, which belongs to the Piperaceae family.

It is characterized by its lanceolate-shaped leaves, which are typically hairy and have a rough texture. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that develop into clustered, elongated berries. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in humid environments with well-drained soil.

This plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated for its unique foliage.
