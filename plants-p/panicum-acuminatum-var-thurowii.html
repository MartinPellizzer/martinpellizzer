Panicum acuminatum var. thurowii

Panicum acuminatum var. thurowii, commonly known as Thurow's Panicgrass, is a variant of a grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, bristly seeds. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is often found in open habitats such as fields and dunes. It has a compact growth habit and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Its botanical features make it a subject of interest in ecological and botanical studies.
