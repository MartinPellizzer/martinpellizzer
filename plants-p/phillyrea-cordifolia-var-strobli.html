Phillyrea cordifolia var. strobli

Phillyrea cordifolia var. strobli, commonly known as the Strobli Phillyrea, is a deciduous shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features lanceolate to ovate leaves that are typically dark green and glossy, with a leathery texture. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers arranged in racemes, which bloom in early spring. Its fruit is a small, fleshy capsule that splits open to release seeds.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to well-drained soils.
