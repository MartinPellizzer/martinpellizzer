Pluchea subumbellata

Pluchea subumbellata, commonly known as the golden crownbeard, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant features showy yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, often surrounded by pale green bracts. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Pluchea subumbellata is often found in disturbed habitats and is valued for its ornamental qualities in gardens.
