Paronychia amani var. amani

Paronychia amani var. amani, commonly known as the Amani buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of East Africa.

It is a low-growing perennial that produces bright yellow flowers resembling buttercups, typically blooming in spring. The plant has a rosette of hairy, lance-shaped leaves that help it retain moisture in its arid habitat. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly areas.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
