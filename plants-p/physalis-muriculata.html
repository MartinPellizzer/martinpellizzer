Physalis muriculata

Physalis muriculata, commonly known as the rough bladderfruit, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, spiny calyces that enclose the fruit, which is a type of berry. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats and is often found in disturbed areas. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange, and it produces small, yellow-orange fruits that are edible when ripe.

The plant is valued for its ornamental features and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
