Potentilla argentea f. angustisecta

Potentilla argentea f. angustisecta, commonly known as silver cinquefoil, is a flowering plant characterized by its finely divided, silver-white leaves that reflect light and give it a distinctive appearance.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is native to regions of Europe and Asia. The plant produces clusters of small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or alpine environments.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and landscape design.
