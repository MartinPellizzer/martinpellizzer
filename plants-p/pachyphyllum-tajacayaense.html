Pachyphyllum tajacayaense

Pachyphyllum tajacayaense, commonly known as the Tajacaya stonecress, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of northern Chile.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that store water, allowing it to survive in harsh, dry environments. The plant produces small, white or yellow flowers during the cooler months, typically in spring. It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other cruciferous plants.

Due to its unique adaptations, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant in xeriscaped gardens.
