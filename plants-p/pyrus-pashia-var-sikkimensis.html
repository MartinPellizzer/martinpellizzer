Pyrus pashia var. sikkimensis

Pyrus pashia var. sikkimensis, commonly known as the Sikkim pear, is a variant of the pear tree native to the eastern Himalayas.

It is characterized by its deciduous nature, producing attractive yellowish-green leaves during the growing season. The plant typically grows in moist, temperate climates at high altitudes, often found in forested areas. Its fruit, which is small and yellow, is edible and has a sweet, slightly tart flavor.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
