Pseuderanthemum exaequatum

Pseuderanthemum exaequatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved pseuderanthemum, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its erect, branched stems and opposite, narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically white or pale pink, arranged in clusters at the ends of the branches. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in tropical gardens.
