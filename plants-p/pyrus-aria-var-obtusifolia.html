Pyrus aria var. obtusifolia

Pyrus aria var. obtusifolia, commonly known as the Japanese whitebeam, is a deciduous tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray-brown bark and alternate, simple, ovate to elliptic leaves that are often rounded at the base. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clusters, followed by small, round, red-brown fruits. It is typically found in temperate forest areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.

The variety obtusifolia is distinguished by its obtuse leaf tips, setting it apart from other variants of Pyrus aria.
