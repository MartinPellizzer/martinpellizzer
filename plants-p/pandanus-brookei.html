Pandanus brookei

Pandanus brookei, commonly known as the Brooke's pandanus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Pandanaceae.

It is native to the Pacific Islands, including parts of Micronesia and Papua New Guinea. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and long, narrow leaves that are often used in traditional crafts. It produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences at the base of the leaves.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has cultural significance in the regions where it is found.
