Phleum phalaroides var. lobatum

Phleum phalaroides var. lobatum, commonly known as the broad-leaved oatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves and panicle-like inflorescences that bear small, spikelet-based flowers. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as meadows, marshes, and along stream banks. Its leaves are often hairy and have a rough texture, while the stems are erect and can reach heights of up to 1 meter.

This variety is notable for its ecological importance, providing food for various insects and birds.
