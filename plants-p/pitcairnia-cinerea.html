Pitcairnia cinerea

Pitcairnia cinerea, commonly known as the silver pitcairnia, is a species of bromeliad native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that are covered in a fine, silvery pubescence, giving it its common name. The plant produces striking white to pale purple flowers that emerge from the center of the rosette. It thrives in shaded, humid environments and is often found growing on rocky outcrops or in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is a subject of interest for both horticulturists and botanists.
