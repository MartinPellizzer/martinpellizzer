Pachycentria macrorhiza

Pachycentria macrorhiza, commonly known as the giant centipede plant, is a striking member of the Araceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of the Americas and is characterized by its large, waxy leaves that can grow up to several feet in length. The plant produces showy, spadix-like inflorescences surrounded by a modified leaf called a spathe. It thrives in humid, warm environments and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.

Its unique appearance makes it a popular choice for indoor gardening and botanical collections.
