Psidium hagelundianum

Psidium hagelundianum, commonly known as the Hagelundian guava, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically green when unripe and turn yellow or red when mature. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a slightly serrated margin.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and for its edible fruit, which has a tart flavor.
