Pachypodium rosulatum var. delphinense

Pachypodium rosulatum var. delphinense, commonly known as the Blue Ghost Tree, is a succulent shrub native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems that store water, allowing it to survive in arid environments. The plant produces clusters of small, blue or purple flowers during the rainy season. Its leaves are typically small and may be absent during periods of drought.

The species is valued for its unique appearance and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.
