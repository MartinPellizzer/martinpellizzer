Polygala exserta

Polygala exserta, commonly known as the narrow-leaved milk-vetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the Polygalaceae family.

It is native to southern Africa, where it typically grows in rocky or sandy soils. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters. It is often found in arid or semi-arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions.

The species is noted for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
