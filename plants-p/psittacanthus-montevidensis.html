Psittacanthus montevidensis

Psittacanthus montevidensis, commonly known as the Montevidense arrowroot, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil and Paraguay.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its unique inflorescence known as a spadix, surrounded by a spathe. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or yellow in color. Its rhizomes are often harvested for their starchy roots, which are used in local cuisines.

The species is valued not only for its edible parts but also for its ornamental appeal in tropical gardens.
