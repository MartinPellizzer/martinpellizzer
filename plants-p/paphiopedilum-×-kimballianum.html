Paphiopedilum × kimballianum

Paphiopedilum × kimballianum, commonly known as the Kimball's lady's slipper, is a hybrid orchid that exhibits a combination of traits from its parent species.

It is characterized by its unique, slipper-shaped pouch that serves as a pollination trap, a feature typical of the Paphiopedilum genus. The plant typically produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of pink, white, and purple, often with intricate patterns. It thrives in warm, humid environments with bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is widely cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
