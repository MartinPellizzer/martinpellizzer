Passiflora peltata

Passiflora peltata, commonly known as the giant purple passionflower, is a striking climbing vine native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and showy, five-petaled flowers that are often a vibrant purple or red. The flowers emit a sweet fragrance and are pollinated primarily by birds and insects. Its fruits, which are edible, develop into bright orange, fleshy berries containing numerous seeds.

This plant is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
