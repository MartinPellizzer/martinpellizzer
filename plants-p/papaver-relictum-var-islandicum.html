Papaver relictum var. islandicum

Papaver relictum var. islandicum, commonly known as the Icelandic poppy, is a rare and ancient species native to Iceland.

It is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers, which bloom in late summer. The plant is a herbaceous perennial with a short stem and palmately lobed leaves. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Due to its limited distribution and conservation status, it is considered a valuable species in botanical studies.
