Pygeum gardneri

Pygeum gardneri, commonly known as the African wild almond, is a small deciduous tree native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Proteaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and simple, alternate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by woody pods containing oval seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and inland forested areas.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens.
