Panamanthus panamensis

Panamanthus panamensis, commonly known as the Panamanian palm, is a species of flowering plant in the family Arecaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of Central America, particularly Panama. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnately compound leaves. It thrives in humid, tropical climates with well-drained soil.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological importance, it is often found in rainforests and is valued for both ornamental and ecological purposes.
