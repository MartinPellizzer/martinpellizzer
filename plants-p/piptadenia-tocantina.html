Piptadenia tocantina

Piptadenia tocantina, commonly known as the Brazilian ironwood, is a tree native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is characterized by its dense, hard wood that is highly valued for its strength and durability. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruits are legumes that contain seeds which are dispersed by animals.

The tree plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, supporting various wildlife species.
