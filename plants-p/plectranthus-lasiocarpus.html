Plectranthus lasiocarpus

Plectranthus lasiocarpus, commonly known as the woolly plectranthus, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, gray-green foliage and produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance and ease of care.

Plectranthus lasiocarpus is also valued for its aromatic leaves, which can be used in herbal teas or as a natural air freshener.
