Peperomia hemionitidifolia

Peperomia hemionitidifolia, commonly known as the silver peperomia, is a compact, evergreen perennial plant native to Southeast Asia.

It features thick, waxy leaves that are typically gray-green with a metallic sheen, giving it its common name. The plant has a low-growing, trailing habit, making it ideal for hanging baskets or as a ground cover. Its foliage is often variegated, with contrasting white or silver markings.

Peperomia hemionitidifolia thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.
