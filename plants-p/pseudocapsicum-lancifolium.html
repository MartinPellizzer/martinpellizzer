Pseudocapsicum lancifolium

Pseudocapsicum lancifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaved pseudocapsicum, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes nightshades, and is characterized by its elongated, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, white or yellowish flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. Its fruit is a capsule that contains several seeds, and it is often found in dry, open habitats.

Due to its unique morphology and limited distribution, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
