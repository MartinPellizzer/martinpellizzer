Peristrophe kotschyana

Peristrophe kotschyana, commonly known as the velvetleaf or Kotschy's peristrophe, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are covered in a fine, velvety pubescence. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale purple. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Peristrophe kotschyana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage.
