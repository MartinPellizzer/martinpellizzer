Pseudanthus pauciflorus

Pseudanthus pauciflorus, commonly known as the few-flowered pseudanthus, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in grasslands and open areas, thriving in well-drained soils. Its small, clustered flowers are often yellow or brown, and it produces achenes as its fruit.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
