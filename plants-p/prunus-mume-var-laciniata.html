Prunus mume var. laciniata

Prunus mume var. laciniata, commonly known as the Japanese apricot or fragrant apricot, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its finely dissected, lace-like leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in early spring before the leaves emerge. The fruits are small, yellow or orange berries that ripen in late summer.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
