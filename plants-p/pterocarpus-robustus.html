Pterocarpus robustus

Pterocarpus robustus, commonly known as the African rosewood, is a tropical tree native to regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dark, dense wood that is highly valued for its aesthetic and structural properties. The plant has compound leaves with long, slender leaflets and produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a legume pod containing flat, winged seeds.

Pterocarpus robustus is also known for its aromatic properties and is used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
