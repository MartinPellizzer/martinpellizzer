Potentilla thyrsiflora f. parvifolia

Potentilla thyrsiflora f. parvifolia, commonly known as the dwarf thrysoflora potentilla, is a compact shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features small, ovate leaves that are densely arranged along the stems, giving it a dense, bushy appearance. The plant produces clusters of golden-yellow flowers in late summer, which are typical of the Potentilla genus. Its foliage is often covered in a fine layer of white pubescence, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This variety is valued in horticulture for its ornamental qualities and adaptability to rocky or well-drained soils.
