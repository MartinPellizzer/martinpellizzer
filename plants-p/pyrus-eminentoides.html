Pyrus eminentoides

Pyrus eminentoides, commonly known as the Chinese white pear, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces white flowers in early spring, followed by edible, sweet fruits that ripen in late summer. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit production.

Pyrus eminentoides prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for temperate climates.
