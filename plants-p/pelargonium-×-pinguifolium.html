Pelargonium × pinguifolium

Pelargonium × pinguifolium, commonly known as the elephant's ear geranium, is a hybrid plant characterized by its large, velvety, heart-shaped leaves.

The leaves are typically a deep green with a waxy texture, and they can grow up to 15 cm in diameter. It produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of pink, red, or white, which bloom from late spring to early autumn. The plant is native to South Africa and is often cultivated as an ornamental due to its striking foliage.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for gardens and containers.
