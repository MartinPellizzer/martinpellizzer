Primula sinensis var. filicifolia

Primula sinensis var. filicifolia, commonly known as the Japanese primrose or Chinese primrose, is a flowering plant native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its low-growing habit and clusters of bell-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of pink, white, and yellow. The foliage consists of broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often a deep green color. This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is commonly cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.

It prefers cool, moist conditions and is well-suited for partial shade environments.
