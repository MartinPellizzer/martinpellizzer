Pectis febrifuga

Pectis febrifuga, commonly known as the feverfew plant, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its daisy-like flowers and hairy stems. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and can reach heights of up to 1 meter. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often have a strong, bitter aroma.

Pectis febrifuga is known for its medicinal properties, particularly in traditional African medicine.
