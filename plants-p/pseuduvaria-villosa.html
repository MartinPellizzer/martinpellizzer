Pseuduvaria villosa

Pseuduvaria villosa, commonly known as the hairy pseuduvaria, is a flowering plant species native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its woody stems and dense, hairy foliage that gives it its common name. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow in color. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in shaded, moist environments.

Pseuduvaria villosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
