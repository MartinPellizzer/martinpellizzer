Piper sessilifolium

Piper sessilifolium, commonly known as the broad-leaved pepper vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are followed by elongated, fleshy berries. Its leaves are broad, ovate, and often have a slightly waxy texture.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its unique foliage.
