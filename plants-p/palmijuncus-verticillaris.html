Palmijuncus verticillaris

Palmijuncus verticillaris, commonly known as the spiral palm, is a unique plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive spiral arrangement of leaves, which gives it a striking visual appearance. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are narrow and elongated, often with a waxy coating that helps reduce water loss.

The spiral pattern of its foliage is not only aesthetically pleasing but also serves a functional role in maximizing light absorption.
