Pleurothallis turrialbae

Pleurothallis turrialbae, commonly known as the Turrialba orchid, is a species native to Costa Rica.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact, epiphytic growth habit. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink, typically during the dry season. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining, organic substrates.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
