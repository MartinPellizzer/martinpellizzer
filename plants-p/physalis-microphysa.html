Physalis microphysa

Physalis microphysa, commonly known as the dwarf groundcherry, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its hairy, yellow-orange calyx that encases the small, orange berries. The plant typically grows to a height of about 10-30 centimeters, with narrow, lance-shaped leaves and small, white flowers. Its fruits are edible when fully ripe and have a tangy, slightly sweet flavor.

The plant is often cultivated as an ornamental and for its unique fruiting structure.
