Persea rugosa var. tomentella

Persea rugosa var. tomentella, commonly known as the woolly persimmon, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Central America.

It is characterized by its hairy, tomentose leaves and yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, orange to red fruits that are edible when fully ripe. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit.

The species is also valued for its ability to tolerate shade and its use in traditional medicine.
