Plectocephalus bulbosus

Plectocephalus bulbosus, commonly known as the African bellflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its bulbous root system and produces striking blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It is often found in coastal regions and is adapted to arid conditions.

The flowers are tubular and attract various pollinators, including bees and birds.
