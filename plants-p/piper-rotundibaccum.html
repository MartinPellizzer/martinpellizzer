Piper rotundibaccum

Piper rotundibaccum, commonly known as the round-leaved pepper vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species. The plant is characterized by its heart-shaped, glossy leaves and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruits are berries that turn from green to red when mature, containing seeds used in traditional medicine.

The plant's botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural study.
