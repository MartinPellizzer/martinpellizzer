Penstemon cyananthus var. cyananthus

Penstemon cyananthus var. cyananthus, commonly known as the blue curl penstemon, is a species native to the western United States.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its upright growth habit and striking blue to violet flowers. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are typically hairy and arranged in a basal rosette. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its showy blooms attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological value.
