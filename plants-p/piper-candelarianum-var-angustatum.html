Piper candelarianum var. angustatum

Piper candelarianum var. angustatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf pepper plant, is a tropical vine native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes various pepper species. The plant is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. Its small, greenish-white flowers are followed by elliptical, dark blue berries that are edible.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in shaded gardens or as a houseplant.
