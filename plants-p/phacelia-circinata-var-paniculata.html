Phacelia circinata var. paniculata

Phacelia circinata var. paniculata, commonly known as the panicled phacelia, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, hairy leaves and its tall, branched flowering stems. The plant produces showy, blue to purple flower clusters that bloom in early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas.

Phacelia circinata var. paniculata is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators.
