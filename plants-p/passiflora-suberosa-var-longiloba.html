Passiflora suberosa var. longiloba

Passiflora suberosa var. longiloba, commonly known as the silky passionflower, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, intricate flowers that feature a crown of filaments and a central cluster of stamens. The plant produces blue or purple berries that are edible and often consumed by various wildlife species. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped with a velvety texture, and the stems are covered in a soft, downy layer.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
