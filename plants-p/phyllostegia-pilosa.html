Phyllostegia pilosa

Phyllostegia pilosa, commonly known as the hairy phyllostegia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Phyllostegia pilosa is often found in moist, wooded areas and is valued for its ornamental qualities.
