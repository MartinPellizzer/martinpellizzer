Pleurothallis barrowii

Pleurothallis barrowii, commonly known as the Barrow's orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and yellow. The plant is epiphytic, meaning it grows on the surface of trees or other plants without being parasitic. Its leaves are usually clustered and fleshy, adapted to retain moisture in its natural habitat.

Pleurothallis barrowii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
