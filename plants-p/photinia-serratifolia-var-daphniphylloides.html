Photinia serratifolia var. daphniphylloides

Photinia serratifolia var. daphniphylloides, commonly known as the Daphniphylloides photinia, is a variant of the photinia plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are elliptical in shape and have serrated edges. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by red berries that are edible for birds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and prefers well-drained soil.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in shaded environments.
