Persicaria puritanorum

Persicaria puritanorum, commonly known as the New England arrowhead, is a herbaceous perennial native to the northeastern United States.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive arrowhead shape, often with a reddish or purple tinge on the underside. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as stream banks and wetlands.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable species for wetland restoration and ecological studies.
