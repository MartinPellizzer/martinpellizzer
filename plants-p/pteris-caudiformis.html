Pteris caudiformis

Pteris caudiformis, commonly known as the curlleaf water fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Pteridaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, feathery fronds that are often curled at the tips, giving it a distinctive appearance. Native to tropical and subtropical regions, this plant thrives in moist, shaded environments. Its rhizomes are creeping and produce clusters of small, spore-bearing leaves.

Pteris caudiformis is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and ease of care.
