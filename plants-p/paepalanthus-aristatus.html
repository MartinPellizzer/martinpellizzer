Paepalanthus aristatus

Paepalanthus aristatus, commonly known as the arrowhead cactus, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly in regions of Brazil, Argentina, and Paraguay.

It is a member of the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its spiny stems and clusters of small, white or yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, adapting to harsh conditions with its succulent structure. Its flowers are often visited by pollinators such as bees and birds, contributing to its ecological role in its native habitat.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture due to its unique appearance and resilience.
