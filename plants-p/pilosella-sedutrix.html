Pilosella sedutrix

Pilosella sedutrix, commonly known as the seductive pearly everlasting, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous small florets. The plant features slender, hairy stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine white hairs. Its showy yellow flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Pilosella sedutrix is often found in dry, open habitats like meadows and rocky slopes.
