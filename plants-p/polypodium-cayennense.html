Polypodium cayennense

Polypodium cayennense, commonly known as the Cayenne polypody, is a species of fern belonging to the family Polypodiaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that are typically less than 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces sporangia on the underside of its leaves, which are clustered in small clusters called sori. It thrives in shaded, moist environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Polypodium cayennense is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its aesthetic appeal.
