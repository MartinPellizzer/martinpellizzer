Polygala gerrardii

Polygala gerrardii, commonly known as the Indian whorltongue, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, spirally arranged leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in whorls along the stem. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in open woodlands and prairies.

Its unique morphology and ecological adaptations make it an important species in its native habitat.
