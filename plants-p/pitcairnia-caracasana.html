Pitcairnia caracasana

Pitcairnia caracasana, commonly known as the Caracas pitcairnia, is a species of flowering plant in the Bromeliaceae family.

It is native to the humid tropical regions of South America, particularly Venezuela and Colombia. This plant is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that form a water-holding cup, a feature common among many bromeliads. The flowers are typically borne on a central spike and can range in color from white to pink.

It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in cloud forests.
