Phyllanthus capillaris var. stuhlmanii

Phyllanthus capillaris var. stuhlmanii, commonly known as the Stuhlman's spurge, is a variant of the spurge plant native to certain regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and distinctive, narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its latex is milky and can be toxic, a common feature among plants in the Euphorbiaceae family.

It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal or arid environments.
