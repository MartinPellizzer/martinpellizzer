Polygonatum fuscum

Polygonatum fuscum, commonly known as black bamboo or Chinese bamboo, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Asparagaceae family.

It is native to eastern Asia, particularly China, Japan, and Korea, and thrives in moist, shaded forest environments. The plant features a rhizomatous root system and produces clusters of narrow, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are small, greenish-white, and clustered in panicles, typically blooming in late spring to early summer.

Polygonatum fuscum is valued in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits and is also cultivated as an ornamental plant.
