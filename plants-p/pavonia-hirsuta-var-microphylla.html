Pavonia hirsuta var. microphylla

Pavonia hirsuta var. microphylla, commonly known as the small-leaved hirsute pavonia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces showy, pink to red bract-like flowers. The plant features small, ovate leaves with a hairy texture, which contribute to its common name. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
