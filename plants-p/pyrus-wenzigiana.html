Pyrus wenzigiana

Pyrus wenzigiana, commonly known as the Wenzig's pear, is a species of flowering plant in the family Rosaceae.

It is native to China and is characterized by its deciduous nature, with heart-shaped leaves and clusters of white flowers in spring. The plant produces small, edible fruits that are typically yellow or orange in color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and somewhat endangered species in the wild.
