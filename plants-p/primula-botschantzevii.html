Primula botschantzevii

Primula botschantzevii, commonly known as the Botchanzev's primrose, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant has heart-shaped, hairy leaves that are typically arranged in a basal rosette. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows.

Due to its ornamental value and unique floral structure, it is cultivated in specialty gardens and botanical collections.
