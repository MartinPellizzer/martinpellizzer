Panicum pallidum

Panicum pallidum, commonly known as the pale panicgrass, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its slender, pale green foliage and delicate, fluffy seed heads. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats, such as marshes and swamp edges, where it thrives in moist, acidic soils. Its small, wind-pollinated flowers develop into light brown to beige seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Panicum pallidum plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
