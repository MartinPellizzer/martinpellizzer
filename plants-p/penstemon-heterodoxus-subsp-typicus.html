Penstemon heterodoxus subsp. typicus

Penstemon heterodoxus subsp.

typicus, commonly known as the narrowleaf penstemon, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and tubular, two-lipped flowers that range in color from blue to violet.

The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils in open, sunny habitats. It produces showy blooms from late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its drought tolerance and low maintenance requirements.
