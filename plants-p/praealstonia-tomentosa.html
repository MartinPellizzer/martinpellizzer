Praealstonia tomentosa

Praealstonia tomentosa, commonly known as the woolly praealstonia, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that covers its stems and leaves, giving it a distinctive, fuzzy appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring, adding a touch of color to its otherwise muted appearance. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and low maintenance, it is a popular choice for succulent enthusiasts and xeriscaping.
