Prangos macedonica

Prangos macedonica, commonly known as the Macedonian thistle, is a perennial plant native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It is characterized by its robust, upright stems and large, silvery-green leaves with a rough, hairy texture. The plant produces showy purple or violet flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or arid environments.

Prangos macedonica is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in local herbal medicine.
