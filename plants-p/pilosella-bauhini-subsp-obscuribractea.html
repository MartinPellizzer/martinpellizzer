Pilosella bauhini subsp. obscuribractea

Pilosella bauhini subsp.

obscuribractea, commonly known as the dark bract pilosella, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads.

The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are hairy and alternate, with a distinctive silvery-gray appearance.

The subspecies is distinguished by its dark, reduced bracts and small, yellowish flower heads.
