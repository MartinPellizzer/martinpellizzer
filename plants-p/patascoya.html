Patascoya

Patascoya, commonly known as the Mexican potato, is a tuberous plant native to the Andes region of South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which also includes tomatoes and peppers. The plant produces edible tubers that are typically round or elongated in shape. These tubers are rich in carbohydrates and have a slightly sweet, nutty flavor.

Patascoya is often used in traditional dishes and is valued for its versatility in cooking.
