Phoenicaulis cheiranthoides subsp. glabra

Phoenicaulis cheiranthoides subsp.

glabra, commonly known as the smooth-flowered phoenicea, is a member of the Brassicaceae family. It is characterized by its glabrous (smooth) stems and leaves, which lack hair.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in racemes, typical of the family. It is native to southern Africa and thrives in dry, rocky habitats.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and relatively rare species within its native range.
