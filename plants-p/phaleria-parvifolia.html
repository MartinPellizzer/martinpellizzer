Phaleria parvifolia

Phaleria parvifolia, commonly known as the small-leaved phaleria, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and small, fragrant flowers. The plant produces yellowish-green berries that are edible but often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and aromatic properties.

Phaleria parvifolia is also valued for its essential oils, which are used in perfumes and aromatherapy.
