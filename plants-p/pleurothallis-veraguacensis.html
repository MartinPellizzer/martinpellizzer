Pleurothallis veraguacensis

Pleurothallis veraguacensis, commonly known as the Veragua orchid, is a species native to Central America, particularly found in Panama and Colombia.

It is a small epiphytic orchid that typically grows on trees in humid, shaded environments. The plant features delicate, narrow leaves and produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and yellow. It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is part of the genus Pleurothallis, which includes over 1,000 species.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and orchid enthusiasts alike.
