Psophocarpus grandiflorus

Psophocarpus grandiflorus, commonly known as the giant hyacinth bean, is a climbing legume native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It features compound leaves with three to five leaflets and produces showy, violet-blue flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant bears pod-like fruits that contain several edible, kidney-shaped seeds. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes grown for its nitrogen-fixing ability in agricultural settings.

Its botanical characteristics make it a notable species in both horticulture and ecological studies.
