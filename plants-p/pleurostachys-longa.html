Pleurostachys longa

Pleurostachys longa, commonly known as the longleaf pleurostachys, is a member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and elongated, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers arranged in a raceme, typically in shades of white and yellow. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is native to parts of Central and South America.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in tropical gardens.
