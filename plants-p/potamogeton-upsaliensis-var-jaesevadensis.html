Potamogeton upsaliensis var. jaesevadensis

Potamogeton upsaliensis var. jaesevadensis, commonly known as the Jaesevadensis pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in Europe.

It typically grows in shallow, slow-moving waters and is characterized by its narrow, ribbon-like leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are often clustered in dense spikes. It plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.

Its presence is often used as an indicator of water quality and ecological health.
