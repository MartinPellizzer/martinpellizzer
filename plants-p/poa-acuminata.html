Poa acuminata

Poa acuminata, commonly known as slender meadow grass, is a cool-season grass native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its fine, slender blades and tufted growth habit, often forming dense mats in moist, well-drained soils. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, producing small, spike-like inflorescences with pale green to blue-gray florets. Its rhizomatous and stolonal growth allows it to spread and colonize open areas effectively.

Poa acuminata is often found in meadows, pastures, and along stream banks, contributing to soil stabilization and ecological diversity.
