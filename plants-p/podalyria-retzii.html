Podalyria retzii

Podalyria retzii, commonly known as the alpine bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is a perennial herb with a rosette of basal leaves and produces striking blue to violet flowers in the summer. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in alpine or subalpine environments. Its flowers are tubular and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Podalyria retzii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
