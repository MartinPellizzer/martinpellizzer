Persicaria trigonocarpa

Persicaria trigonocarpa, commonly known as the three-angled knotweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its triangular stems and heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in late summer. Persicaria trigonocarpa thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits, providing habitat for various insects and birds.
