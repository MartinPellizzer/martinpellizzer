Prunus × saihoziensis f. plena

Prunus × saihoziensis f. plena, commonly known as the double-flowered cherry, is a cultivar of the Prunus genus, characterized by its densely packed, double flowers that give it a more ornamental appearance compared to single-flowered varieties.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is native to Japan, where it has been cultivated for its aesthetic value. The plant typically grows as a small to medium-sized tree, reaching heights of up to 6 meters, with dark green, oval-shaped leaves.

Its flowers, which bloom in early spring, are pink to reddish in color and provide a striking visual display.
