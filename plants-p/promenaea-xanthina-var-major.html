Promenaea xanthina var. major

Promenaea xanthina var. major, commonly known as the golden trumpet plant, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow-orange tubular flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees. The plant belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is known for its ornamental value in gardens. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

The species is relatively easy to care for, thriving in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.
