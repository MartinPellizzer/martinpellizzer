Plumeria clusioides

Plumeria clusioides, commonly known as the frangipani, is a tropical flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, fragrant flowers that come in shades of white, yellow, and pink. The plant has simple, lance-shaped leaves and produces woody, branching stems. It thrives in warm, sunny climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The flowers are used in traditional ceremonies and have a sweet, honey-like fragrance.
