Polypogon × elongatus

Polypogon × elongatus, commonly known as the elongated barnyard grass, is a hybrid grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stature and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in agricultural fields and wetlands. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typical of the grass family.

Due to its aggressive growth habit, it is considered a weed in many cultivated areas.
