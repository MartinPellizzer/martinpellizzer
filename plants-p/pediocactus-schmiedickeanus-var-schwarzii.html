Pediocactus schmiedickeanus var. schwarzii

Pediocactus schmiedickeanus var. schwarzii, commonly known as the Schwarz's prickly pear, is a rare and unique cactus species native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its compact, globular shape and dense clusters of spiny, fleshy stems. The plant produces vibrant yellow to orange flowers during the spring and summer months. Its leaves are typically reduced to spines, and it has a thick, water-storing stem that helps it survive in arid environments.

This variety is highly sought after by collectors due to its rarity and distinctive appearance.
