Papilionanthe × pseudotaiwaniana

Papilionanthe × pseudotaiwaniana, commonly known as the Taiwan orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its elegant, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant features a sympodial growth habit with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, supporting its epiphytic or terrestrial growth. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and arranged in a rosette, while the inflorescence consists of a raceme of showy, butterfly-like flowers.

This species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses for its aesthetic appeal.
