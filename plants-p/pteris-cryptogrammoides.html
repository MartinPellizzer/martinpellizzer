Pteris cryptogrammoides

Pteris cryptogrammoides, commonly known as the cryptogrammoides water fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Pteridaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that are typically green and slightly curled when young. The plant produces sporangia on the underside of its fronds, which are clustered in sori, a common feature among ferns. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its unique leaf structure and reproductive features make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
