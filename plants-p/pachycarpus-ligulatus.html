Pachycarpus ligulatus

Pachycarpus ligulatus, commonly known as the narrow-leaf pachycarpus, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves and stems, which are adapted to store water in arid environments. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in the spring. Its leaves are often narrow and pointed, with a waxy surface that reduces water loss.

Pachycarpus ligulatus is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is well-suited to semi-desert conditions.
