Pappophorum sinaicum

Pappophorum sinaicum, commonly known as the Sinai pappus grass, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the Sinai Peninsula.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences with feathery bristles that aid in wind pollination. Its leaves are narrow and often covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in its harsh environment.

Pappophorum sinaicum is adapted to thrive in sandy soils and is often found in coastal and desert areas.
