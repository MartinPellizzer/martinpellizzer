Phaseolus lunatus var. macrocarpus

Phaseolus lunatus var. macrocarpus, commonly known as the giant kidney bean, is a variant of the common bean species native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its large, round, and edible seeds that are typically white or pale yellow in color. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale purple flowers, which develop into pods containing the seeds. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is valued for its nutritional content and is cultivated for both human consumption and animal feed.
