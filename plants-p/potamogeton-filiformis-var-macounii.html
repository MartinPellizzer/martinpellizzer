Potamogeton filiformis var. macounii

Potamogeton filiformis var. macounii, commonly known as the slender pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its long, thread-like leaves that are typically less than 1 mm in width. This variety thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, such as ponds and lakes. Its small, green flowers are clustered in spike-like inflorescences, and it plays a role in providing habitat for aquatic invertebrates.

The plant is often used in restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize sediments and improve water quality.
