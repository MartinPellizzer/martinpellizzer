Polypodium davalliaceum

Polypodium davalliaceum, commonly known as the broad-leaf maidenhair fern, is a species of epiphytic fern native to tropical regions of Asia and Africa.

It is characterized by its large, leathery, bipinnate fronds that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found growing on trees or rocks. Its rhizomes are thick and fleshy, allowing it to store moisture and nutrients.

The fronds are typically dark green and have a distinctive, elegant appearance that makes it a popular ornamental plant.
