Piptadenia laxipinna

Piptadenia laxipinna, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or yellow trumpet tree, is a tropical leguminous plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting a variety of pollinators. The tree has a straight trunk and can grow up to 20 meters tall, with pinnate leaves composed of several leaflets. Its fruit is a pod containing seeds that are dispersed by wind, contributing to its natural spread.

The plant is valued for its ornamental beauty and is also used in traditional medicine.
