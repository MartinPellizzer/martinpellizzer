Plinia yasuniana

Plinia yasuniana, commonly known as the Yasuni myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to the Amazon rainforest, specifically in Ecuador and Peru, where it grows in humid, tropical environments. The plant is characterized by its small, aromatic leaves and clusters of white or pinkish flowers. Its fruits are small, fleshy berries that are typically eaten by birds and other wildlife.

Due to its restricted habitat, Plinia yasuniana is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
