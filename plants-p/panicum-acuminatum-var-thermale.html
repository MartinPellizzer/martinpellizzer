Panicum acuminatum var. thermale

Panicum acuminatum var. thermale, commonly known as the thermophilic panicgrass, is a variant of the panicgrass species native to warmer climates.

This plant is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, grass-like leaves that are adapted to thrive in high-temperature environments. It typically grows in open, sunny areas such as grasslands and disturbed soils. The inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of spikelets, which are often reddish in color.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soil in arid or semi-arid regions.
