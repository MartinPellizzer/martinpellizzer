Pelargonium spathulatum

Pelargonium spathulatum, commonly known as the shield-leaf geranium, is a species of flowering plant in the family Geraniaceae.

It is native to South Africa and is characterized by its broad, rounded leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of pink, red, and white, which bloom throughout the growing season. It is a perennial herb that thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Its ornamental value and ease of cultivation have made it a favored plant among gardeners.
