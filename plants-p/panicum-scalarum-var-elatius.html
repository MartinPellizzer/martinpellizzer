Panicum scalarum var. elatius

Panicum scalarum var. elatius, commonly known as tall bristlegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and dense, cylindrical inflorescences that resemble a tuft of bristles. The plant typically grows in full sun and thrives in well-drained soils, making it adaptable to various environmental conditions. Its foliage is narrow and grass-like, with a distinctive blue-green hue that adds visual interest to landscapes.

This variety is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
