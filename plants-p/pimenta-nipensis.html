Pimenta nipensis

Pimenta nipensis, commonly known as the Japanese pepper tree, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to the Ryukyu Islands of Japan and is characterized by its fragrant, white flowers and glossy, dark green leaves. The plant produces small, red, peppery fruits that are used in traditional cuisine and herbal medicine. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in coastal areas with well-drained soil.

Pimenta nipensis is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
