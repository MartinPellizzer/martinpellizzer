Piper pseudoglabrifolium

Piper pseudoglabrifolium, commonly known as the glossy leaf pepper, is a tropical shrub native to the rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species, and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow fruits that are often used in traditional medicine and local cuisines. Its foliage is dense and glossy, making it an attractive ornamental plant in humid climates.

The species is also valued for its potential medicinal properties, though further research is needed to confirm its therapeutic uses.
