Phaius mannii

Phaius mannii, commonly known as the Malayan orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded forest environments, often near streams or in swampy areas. The plant features broad, grass-like leaves and produces striking, pale yellow to orange flowers with a distinctive, hooded lip. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.

The species is also noted for its ability to thrive in humid conditions, making it a popular choice for tropical gardens.
