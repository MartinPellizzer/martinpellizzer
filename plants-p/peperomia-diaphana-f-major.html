Peperomia diaphana f. major

Peperomia diaphana f. major, commonly known as the Variegated Peperomia, is a compact, evergreen perennial native to tropical regions.

It features glossy, ovate leaves with a striking combination of white, green, and pink hues, creating a visually appealing foliage. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters, typically in shades of green or red, which are followed by bright red berries. It thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Its unique leaf patterns and low maintenance requirements make it a favored ornamental plant.
