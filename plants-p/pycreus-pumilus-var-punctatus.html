Pycreus pumilus var. punctatus

Pycreus pumilus var. punctatus, commonly known as the speckled pycreus, is a small, herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and dark green leaves with distinctive white or pale green spots. The plant produces small, showy flowers in shades of violet and blue, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and woodland settings.

Due to its ornamental value and unique foliage, it is often cultivated by gardeners interested in rare and exotic plants.
