Pristiglottis aneytyumensis

Pristiglottis aneytyumensis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved spirogyra, is a species of aquatic plant belonging to the order Spirogyrales.

It is characterized by its long, ribbon-like leaves that are typically narrow and ribbon-shaped. The plant is found in freshwater environments, such as ponds and ditches, where it thrives in still or slow-moving water. It plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for microorganisms and small aquatic life.

Its simple structure and ability to reproduce through fragmentation make it an important component of freshwater biodiversity.
