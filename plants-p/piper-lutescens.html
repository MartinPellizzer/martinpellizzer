Piper lutescens

Piper lutescens, commonly known as golden pepper or yellow pepper, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many pepper species. The plant produces small, yellow berries that are often used in traditional medicine and as a spice. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a distinct, peppery aroma.

The botanical features of Piper lutescens make it an interesting subject for both horticultural and pharmacological study.
