Pulsatilla millefoliata

Pulsatilla millefoliata, commonly known as the meadow anemone, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, usually pink or white flowers. The plant features a short, thick rhizome and produces palmately divided, lobed leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers typically bloom in early spring, and the plant is known for its ability to thrive in moist, well-drained soils.

Pulsatilla millefoliata is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and rocky areas.
