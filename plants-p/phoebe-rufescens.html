Phoebe rufescens

Phoebe rufescens, commonly known as the red-leaf phoebe, is a species of flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in moist, well-drained soils along stream banks and in wetlands. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that turn reddish in the fall, and it produces small, fragrant white flowers in the spring. Its botanical characteristics include opposite leaf arrangement and clustered inflorescences.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits in supporting local wildlife.
