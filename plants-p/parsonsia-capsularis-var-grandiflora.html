Parsonsia capsularis var. grandiflora

Parsonsia capsularis var. grandiflora, commonly known as the large-flowered parsonsia, is a tropical flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, showy orange to red flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are opposite, elliptical, and have a smooth, glossy surface.

The fruit is a capsule that splits open to release seeds, aiding in its dispersal.
