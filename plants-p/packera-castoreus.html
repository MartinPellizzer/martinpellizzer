Packera castoreus

Packera castoreus, commonly known as the castoreus ragwort, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in composite heads, which are arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence. It thrives in moist, open habitats such as meadows and streambanks.

Packera castoreus is often found in the northern United States and southern Canada.
