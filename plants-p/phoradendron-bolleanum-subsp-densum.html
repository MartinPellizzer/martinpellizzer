Phoradendron bolleanum subsp. densum

Phoradendron bolleanum subsp.

densum, commonly known as the dense-stemmed mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on a variety of host trees. It is native to the Caribbean, particularly found in countries like Cuba and the Bahamas.

The plant has small, oval-shaped leaves and produces white or pale yellow flowers. Its berries are typically red or orange, containing seeds that are dispersed by birds.

This species plays an important ecological role in its native habitat by providing food for wildlife and contributing to the structure of forest canopies.
