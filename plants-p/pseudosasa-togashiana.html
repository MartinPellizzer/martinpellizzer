Pseudosasa togashiana

Pseudosasa togashiana, commonly known as the Japanese bamboo or Japanese black bamboo, is a perennial grass native to Japan.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender culms that can reach up to 6 meters in height. The plant has narrow, dark green leaves that are often described as having a metallic sheen. It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its elegant appearance and ability to form dense clumps.

Pseudosasa togashiana prefers moist, well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.
