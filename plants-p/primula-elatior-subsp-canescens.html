Primula elatior subsp. canescens

Primula elatior subsp.

canescens, commonly known as the hairy primrose, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its upright stems and clusters of bell-shaped, violet or purple flowers that bloom in early spring.

The subspecies is distinguished by its densely hairy leaves and stems, which give it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and prefers cool, moist conditions.

This plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is a member of the Primulaceae family.
