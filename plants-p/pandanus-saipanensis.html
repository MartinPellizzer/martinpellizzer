Pandanus saipanensis

Pandanus saipanensis, commonly known as the Saipan pandanus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Pandanaceae.

It is native to the island of Saipan in the Northern Mariana Islands and is characterized by its distinctive, stiff, sword-like leaves. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are often used in traditional crafts and have a strong, aromatic scent.

The plant plays an important role in the local ecosystem and is a symbol of the cultural heritage of the Northern Mariana Islands.
