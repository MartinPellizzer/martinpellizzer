Passiflora cephaleima

Passiflora cephaleima, commonly known as the helmet passionflower, is a climbing vine native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, showy flowers that feature a crown-like structure of filaments surrounding a central cluster of stamens. The plant produces edible berries that are typically yellow or orange when ripe. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a serrated margin.

Passiflora cephaleima is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported calming effects.
