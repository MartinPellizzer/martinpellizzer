Plumbago grandiflora

Plumbago grandiflora, commonly known as blue plumbago, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a shrub that produces clusters of bright blue or purple flowers, which bloom throughout the growing season. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are dark green and slightly hairy. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

Plumbago grandiflora is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
