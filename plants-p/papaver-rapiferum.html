Papaver rapiferum

Papaver rapiferum, commonly known as the meadow poppy, is a species of flowering plant in the family Papaveraceae.

It is native to Europe and western Asia and is characterized by its striking yellow to orange flowers. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 60 cm. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and covered in a fine layer of white hairs.

The flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, producing a unique scent that attracts pollinators like bees and butterflies.
