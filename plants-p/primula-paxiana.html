Primula paxiana

Primula paxiana, commonly known as the Sierra Nevada primrose, is a flowering plant native to the high elevations of the Sierra Nevada mountain range in California.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, rocky soils among alpine meadows. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and lavender, often with a distinctive purple throat. Its foliage consists of broad, oval leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence.

Primula paxiana is adapted to cold climates and is often found blooming in late spring to early summer.
