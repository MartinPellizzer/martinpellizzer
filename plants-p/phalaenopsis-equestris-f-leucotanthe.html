Phalaenopsis equestris f. leucotanthe

Phalaenopsis equestris f. leucotanthe, commonly known as the white phalaenopsis orchid, is a hybrid orchid species characterized by its striking white flowers.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia, where it thrives in tropical climates. The plant features long, slender pseudobulbs and broad, lance-shaped leaves that are typically dark green. Its flowers are pendulous and have a velvety texture, often resembling moth wings, which gives the species its common name.

This variety is popular among orchid enthusiasts for its elegance and relatively easy care requirements.
