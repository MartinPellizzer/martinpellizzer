Pseudopachystela lastoursvillensis

Pseudopachystela lastoursvillensis, commonly known as the Lastoursville ghost orchid, is a rare and enigmatic orchid species native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its unique, elongated flower spike that bears a single, delicate orchid flower with a distinctive white and green coloration. The plant is epiphytic, growing on tree branches rather than in soil, and thrives in humid, shaded environments. Its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements make it highly vulnerable to environmental changes.

Due to its rarity and ecological significance, it is considered a priority for conservation efforts.
