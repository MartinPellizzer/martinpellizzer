Podocarpus nagi var. koshuensis

Podocarpus nagi var. koshuensis, commonly known as the Koshu pine, is a variant of the Japanese white pine.

It is a coniferous evergreen tree characterized by its needle-like leaves and cones. Native to Japan, it thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forested areas. The plant has a slow growth rate and is valued for its ornamental and ecological significance.

Its botanical features make it a unique subject in both horticulture and ecological studies.
