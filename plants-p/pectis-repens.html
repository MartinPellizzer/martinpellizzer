Pectis repens

Pectis repens, commonly known as the creeping pectis, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and the Middle East.

It is a low-growing annual herb characterized by its hairy stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, which are borne on slender pedicels. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in sandy or rocky soils.

The species is known for its adaptability to harsh conditions and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
