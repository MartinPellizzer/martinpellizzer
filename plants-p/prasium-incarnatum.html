Prasium incarnatum

Prasium incarnatum, commonly known as the red-flowered prasium, is a striking member of the Apiaceae family.

It is characterized by its bright red flowers, which contrast vividly with its green, fern-like foliage. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as woodland areas or stream banks. Its leaves are compound, consisting of multiple narrow, lance-shaped leaflets.

Prasium incarnatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its visual impact.
