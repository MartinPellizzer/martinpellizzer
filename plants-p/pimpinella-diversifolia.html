Pimpinella diversifolia

Pimpinella diversifolia, commonly known as the diverseleaf cowparsnip, is a member of the Apiaceae family, characterized by its umbellate inflorescence and aromatic foliage.

It is native to temperate regions of Asia and is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. The plant features compound leaves with serrated margins and small, white flowers arranged in complex umbels. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread vegetatively, making it a persistent component of its habitat.

The plant contains various bioactive compounds, contributing to its traditional medicinal uses in some cultures.
