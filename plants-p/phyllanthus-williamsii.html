Phyllanthus williamsii

Phyllanthus williamsii, commonly known as the African date palm, is a shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its pinnately compound leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, followed by fleshy, orange-red berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires a warm, humid climate.
