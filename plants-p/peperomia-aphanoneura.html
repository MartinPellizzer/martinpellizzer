Peperomia aphanoneura

Peperomia aphanoneura, commonly known as the ghost peperomia, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to the tropical rainforests of South America.

It features dense, glossy dark green foliage with a velvety texture, and its leaves are often marked with subtle, ghost-like patterns, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers that emerge from the leaf axils, followed by elliptical, reddish-brown berries. It thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Peperomia aphanoneura is relatively low maintenance, requiring minimal watering and occasional fertilization during the growing season.
