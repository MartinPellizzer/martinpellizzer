Polygala vulgaris subvar. alpigena

Polygala vulgaris subvar. alpigena, commonly known as the alpine milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, often at elevations above 1,000 meters. The plant has slender, erect stems and small, clustered flowers that are usually pale blue or purple in color. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a hairy texture.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in rock gardens or alpine settings.
