Pyrus cathayensis

Pyrus cathayensis, commonly known as the Chinese white pear, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and alternate, ovate leaves. The tree produces small, white flowers in spring, followed by edible, sweet fruits that are similar to pears. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit production in temperate climates.

Pyrus cathayensis is also valued in traditional Chinese medicine for its purported health benefits.
