Pachygone odorifera

Pachygone odorifera, commonly known as the fragrant thickstem plant, is a perennial herb native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, succulent stems that store water, allowing it to thrive in humid, shaded environments. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, often emitting a subtle, pleasant fragrance. Its leaves are broad and waxy, with a smooth texture and a slightly aromatic scent when crushed.

Pachygone odorifera is valued in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties.
