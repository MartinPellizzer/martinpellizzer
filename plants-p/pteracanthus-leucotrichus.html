Pteracanthus leucotrichus

Pteracanthus leucotrichus, commonly known as the white-haired pteracanthus, is a rare flowering plant native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, slender stems and small, clustered white flowers that bloom during the wet season. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture, giving it its common name. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in dense forest undergrowth.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered endangered and is the subject of conservation efforts.
