Padbruggea dasyphylla

Padbruggea dasyphylla, commonly known as the blueberry hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its attractive, showy blue or violet flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has woody stems and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. Its leaves are alternate and ovate, with a rough texture.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for coastal gardens.
