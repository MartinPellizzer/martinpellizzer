Pseudibatia ciliata

Pseudibatia ciliata, commonly known as the fringed gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers that are typically arranged in a dense, spike-like inflorescence. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories. Its leaves are lance-shaped with finely toothed margins, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Pseudibatia ciliata is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
