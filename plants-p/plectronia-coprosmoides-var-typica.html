Plectronia coprosmoides var. typica

Plectronia coprosmoides var. typica, commonly known as the velvetleaf plectronia, is a shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It features small, ovate leaves with a velvety texture and a dark green upper surface. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom during the wet season. Its fruit is a three-lobed capsule that contains numerous tiny seeds.

This species is often found in moist, shaded habitats such as forest understories and along riverbanks.
