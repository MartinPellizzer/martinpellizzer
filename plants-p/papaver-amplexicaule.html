Papaver amplexicaule

Papaver amplexicaule, commonly known as the clasping poppy, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers and heart-shaped leaves that clasp the stem. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Papaver amplexicaule is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and natural landscapes.
