Piper aristolochioides

Piper aristolochioides, commonly known as the Indian pipe or ghost plant, is a unique flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Asia and Africa.

It belongs to the Piper genus within the family Piperaceae, which includes many species of flowering plants. The plant is characterized by its pale, almost translucent appearance, with elongated, tubular flowers that are often mistaken for fungi. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical features make it an intriguing subject for both ecological and horticultural study.
