Pyrus florifera

Pyrus florifera, commonly known as the flowering pear, is a deciduous tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and heart-shaped leaves that turn yellow in the fall. The tree produces small, greenish-white flowers in early spring, which are followed by edible, yellow fruits. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular ornamental species.

Due to its rapid growth and adaptability, it is often used in landscaping and reforestation efforts.
