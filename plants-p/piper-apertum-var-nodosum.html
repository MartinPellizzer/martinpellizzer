Piper apertum var. nodosum

Piper apertum var. nodosum, commonly known as the nodular pepper vine, is a climbing shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, warty, or nodular stems, which give it its botanical name. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by oval, edible berries that turn red when mature. It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species.

The leaves are typically alternate, ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture.
