Poa maia

Poa maia, commonly known as the Maia grass, is a species of flowering plant in the grass family Poaceae.

It is native to New Zealand and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and fine, delicate leaves. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences that are typically greenish or brownish in color. Poa maia thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and lowland areas.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for native invertebrates.
