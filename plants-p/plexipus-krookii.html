Plexipus krookii

Plexipus krookii, commonly known as the Krook's ghost fern, is a rare and enigmatic fern species native to the dense, humid forests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, lace-like fronds that exhibit a striking bioluminescent glow under certain conditions, a feature that has fascinated botanists and researchers. The plant thrives in shaded, moist environments, often growing on the trunks of ancient trees or in the undergrowth of tropical rainforests. Its unique reproductive structures, including specialized spore-producing organs, contribute to its ecological significance.

Due to habitat loss and limited distribution, Plexipus krookii is considered a critically endangered species, making conservation efforts essential for its survival.
