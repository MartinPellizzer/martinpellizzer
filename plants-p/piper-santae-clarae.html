Piper santae-clarae

Piper santae-clarae, commonly known as the Santa Clara pepper or the Mexican pepper, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It belongs to the Piper genus within the Piperaceae family, which includes various species of pepper plants. The plant produces small, yellowish-green berries that are often used in traditional medicine and culinary applications. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a mild, peppery aroma.

The botanical characteristics of Piper santae-clarae make it distinct from other pepper species due to its unique morphology and chemical composition.
