Parapodium simile

Parapodium simile, commonly known as the featherleaf sage, is a perennial herb native to the arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its delicate, feathery foliage and clusters of small, blue or violet flowers. The plant has a branched stem that grows close to the ground, and its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy. Parapodium simile thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it a hardy choice for xeriscaping.

Its botanical features make it an attractive and low-maintenance plant for gardeners in dry climates.
