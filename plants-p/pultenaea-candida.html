Pultenaea candida

Pultenaea candida, commonly known as white wattle, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the family Fabaceae and is characterized by its slender, often twining stems and small, clustered flowers. The flowers are typically white or pale yellow, with a distinctive papilionaceous structure. The plant produces small, elliptical pods that contain several seeds.
