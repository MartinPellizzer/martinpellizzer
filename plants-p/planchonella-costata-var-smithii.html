Planchonella costata var. smithii

Planchonella costata var. smithii, commonly known as the Smith's roughshank, is a tropical plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized tree or shrub characterized by its rough, fissured bark and elliptical leaves with a serrated margin. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by edible, round fruits that are typically yellow or orange when ripe. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
