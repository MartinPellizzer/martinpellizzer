Polygonum longipilum

Polygonum longipilum, commonly known as the long-hair knotweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, which includes plants commonly referred to as buckwheats and knotweeds. The plant is characterized by its upright growth habit, with slender, branching stems that often bear reddish tints. Its leaves are alternate, ovate to lanceolate in shape, and typically have a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

The flowers are small and clustered in dense, cylindrical inflorescences, often appearing in late summer to early autumn.
