Phthirusa castillana

Phthirusa castillana, commonly known as the Castilian thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and striking purple or pink flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its fibrous roots help it retain moisture, while its hairy stems provide protection against herbivores.

The Castilian thistle is also valued for its medicinal properties, traditionally used to treat various ailments.
