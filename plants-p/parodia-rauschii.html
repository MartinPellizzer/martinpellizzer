Parodia rauschii

Parodia rauschii, commonly known as the "Pineapple Cactus," is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems covered in dense, white to pale green spines. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. It is a slow-growing species that thrives in well-draining soil and full sun.

Due to its compact size and ornamental appeal, it is often cultivated as a decorative houseplant or in rock gardens.
