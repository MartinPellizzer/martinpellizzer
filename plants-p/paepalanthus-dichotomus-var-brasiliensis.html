Paepalanthus dichotomus var. brasiliensis

Paepalanthus dichotomus var. brasiliensis, commonly known as the Brazilian torch cactus, is a species of flowering plant native to Brazil.

It is a small, erect cactus with a distinctive appearance, featuring a green, cylindrical stem and spiny, leaf-like structures. The plant produces yellowish-orange flowers that bloom in clusters during the dry season. It is adapted to arid environments and has a shallow root system to absorb moisture efficiently.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and resilience in harsh conditions.
