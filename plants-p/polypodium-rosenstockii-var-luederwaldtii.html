Polypodium rosenstockii var. luederwaldtii

Polypodium rosenstockii var. luederwaldtii, commonly known as the Luederwaldt's shield fern, is a tropical fern native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its compact, upright growth habit. The fronds are typically leathery and have a distinctive, shield-like shape, with serrated margins. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained, acidic soil.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
