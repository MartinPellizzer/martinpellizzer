Papualthia auriculata

Papualthia auriculata, commonly known as the auriculate papualthia, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in cooler, high-altitude environments. Its leaves are often lobed and have a distinctive ear-like shape, giving it its common name.

Papualthia auriculata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with suitable climate conditions.
