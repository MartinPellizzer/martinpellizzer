Phlomis mongolica

Phlomis mongolica, commonly known as Mongolian mullein, is a perennial herb native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, hairy stems and large, woolly leaves that can reach up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces showy, yellow flowers in summer, which are clustered in dense, spike-like inflorescences. It thrives in dry, rocky or sandy soils and is often found in steppe and semi-desert environments.

Phlomis mongolica is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in herbal medicine.
