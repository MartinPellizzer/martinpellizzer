Pavonia macrophylla

Pavonia macrophylla, commonly known as the large-leaf paveronia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, red or pink bract-like flowers that are often mistaken for petals. The plant typically grows as a shrub with woody stems and large, heart-shaped leaves. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Pavonia macrophylla is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
