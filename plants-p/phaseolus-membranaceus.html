Phaseolus membranaceus

Phaseolus membranaceus, commonly known as the scarlet runner bean, is a climbing legume native to the Americas.

It produces vibrant red or yellow flowers and elongated, edible pods that contain kidney-shaped seeds. The plant thrives in warm climates and requires full sunlight for optimal growth. Its leaves are palmately lobed, and the stems are hairy and twining.

This species is valued for both its ornamental appeal and culinary use in various cuisines.
