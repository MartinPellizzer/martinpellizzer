Pyrus lanceolata

Pyrus lanceolata, commonly known as the lanceleaf pear, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia and is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces edible pomes that are typically small and have a mild, sweet flavor. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value as well as its fruit.

Pyrus lanceolata is a deciduous shrub or small tree, typically reaching heights of up to 5 meters.
