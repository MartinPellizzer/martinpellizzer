Potentilla cernavodae

Potentilla cernavodae, commonly known as the Cernavoda cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the Carpathian region of Romania.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit. The plant features yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, typically arranged in clusters of five, giving it its common name. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and covered in a dense layer of fine hairs.

Potentilla cernavodae is adapted to rocky and sandy soils, often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.
