Pleioblastus maximowiczii

Pleioblastus maximowiczii, commonly known as the Japanese forest grass, is a clump-forming bamboo native to eastern Asia.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically light green to bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are followed by elongated, cylindrical seed pods. It thrives in shady, moist environments and is often used in ornamental landscaping.

Its slow growth rate and ability to form dense clumps make it a popular choice for garden borders and rock gardens.
