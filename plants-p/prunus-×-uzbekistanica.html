Prunus × uzbekistanica

Prunus × uzbekistanica, commonly known as the Uzbek cherry, is a hybrid species belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is native to Central Asia, particularly Uzbekistan, and is characterized by its small, sweet fruits. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are oval-shaped with serrated edges, and it produces fragrant white or pink flowers in early spring.

The Uzbek cherry is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also cultivated for its edible fruit.
