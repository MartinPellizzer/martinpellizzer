Phyllocactus × triumphans

Phyllocactus × triumphans, commonly known as the hybrid Christmas cactus, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Schlumbergera truncata and Schlumbergera×kochii.

It is characterized by its segmented, flattened stems that resemble leaves, which are green on the upper surface and red or purple on the underside. The plant produces vibrant, showy flowers in shades of red, pink, and white during the holiday season, typically blooming in late autumn to early winter. It prefers bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor cultivation.

Its unique appearance and seasonal blooming make it a favored ornamental plant in many households.
