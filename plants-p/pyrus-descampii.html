Pyrus descampii

Pyrus descampii, commonly known as the Corsican pear, is a small deciduous tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, elongated leaves and clusters of small, white flowers. The plant produces edible pears that are typically small and have a sweet, aromatic flavor. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Due to its ornamental value and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and valuable species in botanical collections.
