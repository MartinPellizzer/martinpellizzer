Passiflora glandulosa var. canaliculata

Passiflora glandulosa var. canaliculata, commonly known as the Brazilian passionflower, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that feature a unique blend of colors, including blue, purple, and white. The plant produces edible berries that are often used in traditional medicine and culinary applications. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped with a rough texture and are covered in small, glandular hairs.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
