Prunus cochinchinensis

Prunus cochinchinensis, commonly known as the Chinese date tree, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and alternate, ovate leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers in spring, which are followed by edible, sweet fruits resembling dates. Its fruit is rich in nutrients and has been used in traditional medicine for centuries.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated for its timber.
