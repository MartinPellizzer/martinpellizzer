Potentilla × tikhomirovii

Potentilla × tikhomirovii, commonly known as the Tikhomirov's cinquefoil, is a hybrid shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and produces clusters of bright yellow flowers in late spring to early summer. The plant has dark green, serrated leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and by horticultural enthusiasts.
