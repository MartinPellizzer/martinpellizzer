Pleione odoratissima

Pleione odoratissima, commonly known as the fragrant pleione, is a species of orchid native to the mountainous regions of China.

It is characterized by its elegant, showy flowers that typically bloom in late winter or early spring. The plant produces pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, allowing it to survive in its cool, shaded habitat. Its flowers are fragrant, with a delicate scent that attracts pollinators such as bees and moths.

This orchid is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
