Prunus × oneyamensis

Prunus × oneyamensis, commonly known as the Japanese cherry plum, is a hybrid species within the Prunus genus.

It is native to Japan and is characterized by its small, edible fruits that are typically pink or red when ripe. The plant is a deciduous shrub or small tree, often reaching heights of up to 5 meters. It produces fragrant white or pink flowers in early spring, which are followed by the fruiting period in summer.

Prunus × oneyamensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic beauty.
