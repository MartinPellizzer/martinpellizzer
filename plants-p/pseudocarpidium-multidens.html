Pseudocarpidium multidens

Pseudocarpidium multidens, commonly known as the many-toothed pseudocarpidium, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Myrtaceae.

It is characterized by its small, clustered inflorescences and fragrant, white to pinkish flowers. The plant produces small, fleshy fruits that are typically eaten by various bird species. It thrives in tropical and subtropical regions, often growing in moist, well-drained soils.

The leaves are simple, alternate, and have a serrated margin, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
