Panicum neohumbertii

Panicum neohumbertii, commonly known as the Neohumbert's panicgrass, is a species of grass native to the tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, fluffy seeds that are dispersed by wind, aiding in its propagation. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, often found in savanna and grassland habitats.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated as a ornamental plant or for erosion control.
