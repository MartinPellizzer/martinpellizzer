Phlebochilus clavigerus

Phlebochilus clavigerus, commonly known as the purple tansy, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces yellow flowers arranged in a flat-topped cluster. Its flowers are rich in volatile oils, which contribute to its strong, aromatic scent.

The plant is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties.
