Pelargonium karooicum

Pelargonium karooicum, commonly known as the Karoo geranium, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, upright stems and opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from pink to red, typically blooming in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value, it is a popular choice for low-maintenance gardening.
