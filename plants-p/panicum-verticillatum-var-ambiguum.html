Panicum verticillatum var. ambiguum

Panicum verticillatum var. ambiguum, commonly known as the ambiguous panicgrass, is a perennial grass native to parts of the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, hairy stems that often produce clusters of small, greenish or reddish spikelets. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats, such as marshes and swampy areas, where it thrives in moist, acidic soils. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a slightly hairy texture, and it produces delicate, feathery inflorescences during the summer months.

This species is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support local wildlife.
