Pleiogynium cerasiferum var. genuinum

Pleiogynium cerasiferum var. genuinum, commonly known as the red-flowered hibiscus, is a tropical shrub characterized by its striking red or pink flowers.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia, particularly Malaysia and Indonesia. The plant features palmate leaves with five to seven lobes and produces showy, five-petaled flowers that bloom in clusters. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant floral display.

The fruit is a capsule that contains numerous small, hairy seeds.
