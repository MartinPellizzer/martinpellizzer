Passiflora sublanceolata

Passiflora sublanceolata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf passionflower, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful flowers that feature a unique structure with five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments. The plant produces edible berries that are often used in culinary applications. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped with a smooth or slightly toothed margin.

Passiflora sublanceolata is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic and ecological benefits.
