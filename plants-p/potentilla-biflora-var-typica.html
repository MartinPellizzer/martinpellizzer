Potentilla biflora var. typica

Potentilla biflora var. typica, commonly known as the twinflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, yellow flowers that bloom in pairs, giving the plant its common name. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are palmately divided, and the stems are often hairy.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its charming appearance.
