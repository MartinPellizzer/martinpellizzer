Phyllanthus maderaspatensis var. maderaspatensis

Phyllanthus maderaspatensis var. maderaspatensis, commonly known as the Indian gooseberry or amla, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are elliptic to ovate in shape and have a leathery texture. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by edible, yellow, berry-like fruits. The leaves and fruits are rich in vitamin C and are widely used in traditional medicine and culinary applications.

It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its medicinal and nutritional value.
