Phyla reptans

Phyla reptans, commonly known as the creeping plant, is a member of the Lamiaceae family.

It is characterized by its prostrate growth habit, with stems that lie close to the ground and often root at the nodes. The leaves are typically opposite, ovate, and covered in a fine pubescence. Its flowers are small, tubular, and usually arranged in whorls along the stems.

This plant is often found in moist, shaded environments and is valued for its ornamental appeal and medicinal properties.
