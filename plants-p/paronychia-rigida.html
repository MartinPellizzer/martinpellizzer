Paronychia rigida

Paronychia rigida, commonly known as rigid sandmat, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a low-growing perennial herb with fleshy, succulent stems and leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces small, white to pale purple flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Its botanical features make it an attractive and drought-tolerant addition to arid landscapes.
