Pelargonium recurvatum

Pelargonium recurvatum, commonly known as the zigzag geranium, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and distinctive zigzag-shaped stems. The plant produces small, showy flowers in shades of pink, red, and white, typically blooming in summer. Its leaves are opposite, with a rough texture and a serrated margin.

Pelargonium recurvatum is often cultivated as a decorative plant in hanging baskets or containers due to its attractive foliage and flowers.
