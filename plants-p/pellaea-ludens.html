Pellaea ludens

Pellaea ludens, commonly known as the silver fern, is a species of fern native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive, silver-gray fronds that are covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and rocky areas. Its rhizomes are creeping and produce clustered fronds that are often used in horticultural displays.

The botanical features of Pellaea ludens make it a popular choice for gardeners seeking a unique and ornamental fern.
