Piper pringlei

Piper pringlei, commonly known as Pringle's pepper vine, is a climbing shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes various species of pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and small, greenish-white flowers that develop into elongated, fleshy fruits. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in the understory of rainforests.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
