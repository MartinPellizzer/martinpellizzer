Pandanus grallatus

Pandanus grallatus, commonly known as the red pandanus, is a tropical plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, feather-like leaves that can grow up to several meters in length. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in dense, branched clusters. Its leaves are often used in traditional crafts and thatching, while its fruits are edible and used in local cuisines.

The plant thrives in humid, coastal environments and is adapted to saline and sandy soils.
