Parsonsia bureavi

Parsonsia bureavi, commonly known as the red-flowered parsonsia, is a striking tropical shrub native to the Pacific Islands.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family, characterized by its showy, tubular flowers and milky sap. The plant produces vibrant orange to red flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators like birds and insects. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and arranged oppositely along the stems.

Parsonsia bureavi is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is found in coastal and forested areas across its native range.
