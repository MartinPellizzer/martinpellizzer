Pentanema germanicum

Pentanema germanicum, commonly known as German mountainflower, is a perennial herb native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils in mountainous regions. The plant has slender, hairy stems and small, clustered flowers that are usually white or pale pink. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.

It is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions.
