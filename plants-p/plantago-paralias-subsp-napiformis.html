Plantago paralias subsp. napiformis

Plantago paralias subsp.

napiformis, commonly known as the broadleaf plantain, is a perennial herb native to temperate regions of Europe and western Asia. It features a rosette of broad, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in a dense layer of fine hairs.

The plant produces small, greenish flowers in a spike-like inflorescence, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found along roadsides, fields, and waste areas.

The plant is known for its medicinal properties and has been used historically for its soothing effects on skin irritations.
