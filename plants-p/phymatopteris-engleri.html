Phymatopteris engleri

Phymatopteris engleri, commonly known as the Engle's wallriven fern, is a species of polypodioid fern native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and rocky crevices. The plant features a rhizomatous growth habit with a dense, clustered frond structure. Its fronds are usually dark green and have a leathery texture, with a serrated or lobed margin.

The spores are produced in sori that are clustered on the underside of the fronds, typical of the Polypodiaceae family.
