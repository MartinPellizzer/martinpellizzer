Pediomelum canescens

Pediomelum canescens, commonly known as the desert dandelion, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its hairy, grayish stems and basal rosettes of narrow, blue-green leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring, followed by distinctive, oval-shaped seed pods. It is adapted to survive in dry environments with deep root systems and drought tolerance.

Pediomelum canescens is often found in sandy soils and is considered a valuable plant for stabilizing desert landscapes.
