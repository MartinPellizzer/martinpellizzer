Philadelphus hirsutus var. gracilis

Philadelphus hirsutus var. gracilis, commonly known as the slender false mock orange, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features compound leaves with 5 to 9 elliptical leaflets that are hairy on the underside. The plant produces fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable addition to native plant gardens and wildlife habitats.
