Pectis propetes var. holochaeta

Pectis propetes var. holochaeta, commonly known as the hairy pectis, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and leaves with a distinctive, often hairy texture. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and thrives in arid or semi-arid environments. The flowers are yellow and produce small, wind-dispersed seeds, aiding in its propagation across open habitats.
