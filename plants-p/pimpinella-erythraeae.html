Pimpinella erythraeae

Pimpinella erythraeae, commonly known as the Red Sea cow parsley, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of the Red Sea.

It is characterized by its slender stems and small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in umbels. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves that are often covered in a fine pubescence. It thrives in saline and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh coastal conditions.

Pimpinella erythraeae plays a role in local ecosystems and is of interest to botanists studying halophytic flora.
