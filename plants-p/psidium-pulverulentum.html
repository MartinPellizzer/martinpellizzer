Psidium pulverulentum

Psidium pulverulentum, commonly known as the powdery guava, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its small, fragrant white flowers and elliptical, smooth, pale green leaves. Its fruit is typically small, with a thin skin and a sweet, aromatic pulp.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of edible fruit.
