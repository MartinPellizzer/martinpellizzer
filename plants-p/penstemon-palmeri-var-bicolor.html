Penstemon palmeri var. bicolor

Penstemon palmeri var. bicolor, commonly known as the Palmer's beardtongue, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive two-toned flowers, which typically feature purple upper lips and white lower lips. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a fine layer of white hairs, aiding in water conservation.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
