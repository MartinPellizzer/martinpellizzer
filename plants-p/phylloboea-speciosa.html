Phylloboea speciosa

Phylloboea speciosa, commonly known as the golden leaf orchid, is a rare and exotic plant native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, elongated leaves that display a vibrant golden-green coloration, which becomes more pronounced under direct sunlight. The plant produces delicate, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pale yellow, often with intricate patterns resembling lace. It thrives in humid environments with well-draining soil and requires partial shade to prevent leaf scorching.

Due to its unique appearance and limited natural range, it is highly sought after by botanical enthusiasts and conservationists alike.
