Phaleria revoluta

Phaleria revoluta, commonly known as the white betel tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae and is characterized by its smooth, pale bark and large, lance-shaped leaves. The tree produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into oval, woody fruits containing a single seed. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appearance and is sometimes cultivated for its aromatic properties.
