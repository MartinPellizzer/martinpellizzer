Polygonum campanulatum var. fulvidum

Polygonum campanulatum var. fulvidum, commonly known as the yellow bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a slightly rough texture. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
