Plantago wulfenii

Plantago wulfenii, commonly known as Wulfen's plantain, is a flowering plant species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the family Plantaginaceae and is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves and dense clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. The plant is often found in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and fields, where it can thrive in poor soil conditions. It produces capsule fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

This species is valued for its medicinal properties and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
