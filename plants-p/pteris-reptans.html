Pteris reptans

Pteris reptans, commonly known as the common maidenhair fern, is a species of fern characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds.

It belongs to the family Pteridaceae and is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia, Africa, and Australia. The plant has a rhizomatous growth habit, with creeping underground stems that allow it to spread and form dense mats. Its fronds are typically bi- or tri-pinnate, with a distinctive blackish vein pattern on the underside.

Pteris reptans thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value.
