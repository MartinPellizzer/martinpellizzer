Penstemon campanulatus var. chihuahuensis

Penstemon campanulatus var. chihuahuensis, commonly known as the Chihuahua bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its bell-shaped, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to violet, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically gray-green in color, adapted to reduce water loss in its dry habitat. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for xeriscaping.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and drought tolerance, making it a popular choice in native plant gardens.
