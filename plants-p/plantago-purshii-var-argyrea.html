Plantago purshii var. argyrea

Plantago purshii var. argyrea, commonly known as silverleaf plantain, is a perennial herb native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It is characterized by its silver-gray leaves covered in fine, dense hairs, which help reduce water loss in arid environments. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish flowers in a spike-like inflorescence, which are wind-pollinated. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and dry meadows.

The leaves are sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine for their mild astringent properties.
