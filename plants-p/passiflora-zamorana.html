Passiflora zamorana

Passiflora zamorana, commonly known as the Zamora passionflower, is a climbing vine native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, complex flowers that feature five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments surrounding the reproductive structures. The plant produces edible berries that are often consumed raw or used in jams and beverages. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are woody and twining.

Passiflora zamorana is valued not only for its ornamental beauty but also for its ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
