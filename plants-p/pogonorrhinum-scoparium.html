Pogonorrhinum scoparium

Pogonorrhinum scoparium, commonly known as the coastal spurge, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in sandy or calcareous soils along the shoreline. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters.
