Pothos decursivus

Pothos decursivus, commonly known as the creeping pothos, is a tropical vine native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, waxy leaves that are typically bright green with a slightly variegated pattern. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by orange-red berries, though it is primarily grown for its ornamental foliage rather than its fruit. It thrives in bright, indirect light and is relatively low-maintenance, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Pothos decursivus is also known for its ability to purify the air and is often used in interior design to enhance both aesthetics and air quality.
