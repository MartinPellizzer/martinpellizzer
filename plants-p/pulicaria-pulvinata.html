Pulicaria pulvinata

Pulicaria pulvinata, commonly known as the woolly cinquefoil, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features silver-gray foliage covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive woolly appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in early spring. Its leaves are alternate and have a distinctive five-lobed shape, typical of the genus Pulicaria.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.
