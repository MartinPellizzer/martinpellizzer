Paspalum pallidum

Paspalum pallidum, commonly known as the silvergrass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-gray foliage and compact growth habit. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks. Its inflorescence consists of slender, spike-like clusters of small, pale flowers.

Paspalum pallidum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
