Protium heptaphyllum var. brasiliense

Protium heptaphyllum var. brasiliense, commonly known as Brazilian wild cashew, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Amazon basin.

It belongs to the Burseraceae family and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and compound leaves with elliptical leaflets. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers followed by fleshy, orange-red fruits that contain a single seed.
