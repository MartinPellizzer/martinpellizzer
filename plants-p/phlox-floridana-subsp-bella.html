Phlox floridana subsp. bella

Phlox floridana subsp.

bella, commonly known as the Florida bell phlox, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States. It features showy, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in late spring to early summer.

The plant has opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for native gardens.

Its floral structure attracts pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance.
