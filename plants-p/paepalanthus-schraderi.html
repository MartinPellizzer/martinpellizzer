Paepalanthus schraderi

Paepalanthus schraderi, commonly known as the Schrader's pincushion cactus, is a species of flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its dense, spiny stems that resemble a pincushion. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom during the dry season, typically in the summer months. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, adapting to poor soil conditions and high temperatures.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by cactus enthusiasts.
