Psoralea odoratissima

Psoralea odoratissima, commonly known as the sweet pea shrub, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its aromatic, small, pink or purple flowers. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable choice for gardens in warmer climates.

Its fragrant blooms attract pollinators like bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological value.
