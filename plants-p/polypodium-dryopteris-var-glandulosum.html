Polypodium dryopteris var. glandulosum

Polypodium dryopteris var. glandulosum, commonly known as the glandular wood fern, is a variant of the common wood fern.

It is a perennial fern native to parts of Asia, including China and Japan. The plant features fronds that are typically bipinnate, with a distinctive, often glossy appearance. Its leaves are characterized by the presence of glandular dots, which give it its varietal name.

This fern thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
