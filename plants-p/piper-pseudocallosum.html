Piper pseudocallosum

Piper pseudocallosum, commonly known as the false black pepper, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species. The plant is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and heart-shaped leaves. It produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that develop into elongated, berry-like fruits.

The botanical features of Piper pseudocallosum make it a distinctive member of its family with potential medicinal and ornamental uses.
