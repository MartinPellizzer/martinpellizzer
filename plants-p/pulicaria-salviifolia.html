Pulicaria salviifolia

Pulicaria salviifolia, commonly known as Woolly Groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its hairy, gray-green leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It is a low-growing perennial that forms dense mats, making it suitable for rock gardens.

Its botanical features make it a hardy and drought-tolerant species well adapted to Mediterranean climates.
