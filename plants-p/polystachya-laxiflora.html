Polystachya laxiflora

Polystachya laxiflora, commonly known as the slender-flowered polystachya, is a member of the orchid family, Orchidaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa and is characterized by its delicate, pendulous inflorescences. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte or terrestrial herb, often found in shaded, humid environments. Its flowers are usually small and white or pale green, with a distinctive, spurred lip.

Polystachya laxiflora is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is relatively easy to cultivate under the right conditions.
