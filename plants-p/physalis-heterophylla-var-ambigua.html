Physalis heterophylla var. ambigua

Physalis heterophylla var. ambigua, commonly known as the variable physalis, is a species of flowering plant in the nightshade family, Solanaceae.

It is characterized by its distinctive inflated calyx that encloses the fruit, which resembles a small lantern. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are usually alternate and vary in shape, contributing to its common name.

The fruit, which is edible when ripe, is often used in culinary applications in its native regions.
