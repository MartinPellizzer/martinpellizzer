Prunus serrulata var. glabra

Prunus serrulata var. glabra, commonly known as the Japanese cherry or Somei Yoshino, is a deciduous tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its smooth, pale bark and oval-shaped leaves that turn vibrant shades of red and orange in autumn. The tree produces clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers in early spring, which are highly ornamental. It thrives in temperate climates and is often planted in urban areas for its aesthetic value.

The species is also valued for its ecological benefits, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
