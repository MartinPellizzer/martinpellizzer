Plectrurus

Plectrurus, commonly known as the thread-tailed gecko, is a genus of lizards belonging to the family Gekkonidae.

These small, agile reptiles are characterized by their elongated bodies and distinctive, thread-like tails. They are primarily found in the tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific Islands. Plectrurus species typically inhabit rocky areas, forest floors, and tree trunks, where they rely on their excellent climbing abilities.

Their botanical significance lies in their role as indicators of ecosystem health and their contribution to local biodiversity.
