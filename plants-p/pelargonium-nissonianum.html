Pelargonium nissonianum

Pelargonium nissonianum, commonly known as the Nisson's geranium, is a species of flowering plant in the family Geraniaceae.

It is native to South Africa and is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, five-petaled flowers. The leaves are typically rounded with a slightly hairy surface, and the plant produces vibrant blooms in shades of red, pink, and white. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal in gardens and landscapes.
