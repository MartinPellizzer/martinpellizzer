Prospero pulchellum

Prospero pulchellum, commonly known as the silverleaf orchid, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, silver-green foliage and delicate, fragrant blooms. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often near streams or in dense undergrowth. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb moisture from the humid air.

The flowers, which bloom in late spring, come in shades of white and pale pink, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
