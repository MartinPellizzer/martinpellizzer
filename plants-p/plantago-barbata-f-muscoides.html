Plantago barbata f. muscoides

Plantago barbata f. muscoides, commonly known as the musk plantain, is a variant of the common plantain.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit. The leaves are typically broad, lance-shaped, and covered with a fine layer of white pubescence. It produces small, greenish flowers in spikes, which are often concealed by the dense foliage.

This subspecies is adapted to moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.
