Penstemon coloradoensis subsp. sileri

Penstemon coloradoensis subsp.

sileri, commonly known as the Colorado penstemon, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, tubular flowers.

The subspecies sileri typically features blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds.
