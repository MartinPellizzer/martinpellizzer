Piaranthus geminatus

Piaranthus geminatus, commonly known as the twin-flower or false dandelion, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a small, herbaceous perennial that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils. The plant features delicate, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters of two, giving it its common name. Its leaves are usually basal, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.

Piaranthus geminatus is known for its ability to thrive in arid environments and is often found in open, sunny habitats.
