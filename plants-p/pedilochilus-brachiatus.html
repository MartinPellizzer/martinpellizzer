Pedilochilus brachiatus

Pedilochilus brachiatus, commonly known as the branched lip orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its terrestrial growth habit and produces striking, showy flowers with a distinctive lip. The plant typically features a rhizomatous stem and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are usually pink or purple and bloom during the warmer months.

This orchid is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
