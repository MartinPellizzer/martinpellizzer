Pyrus pannonica orbiculata

Pyrus pannonica orbiculata, commonly known as the Pannonian pear, is a species of flowering plant in the family Rosaceae.

It is native to the Pannonian Basin in Central Europe and parts of the Balkans. The plant is characterized by its round, often lobed leaves and clusters of white flowers that appear in spring. Its fruit, a pear-like berry, is typically yellow or orange when ripe and is edible.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been cultivated for both its fruit and its use in horticulture.
