Papaya chilensis

Papaya chilensis, commonly known as the Chilean papaya, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Caricaceae family and is characterized by its large, lobed leaves and yellow-orange fruits. The plant typically grows in moist, tropical climates and thrives in well-drained soil. Its fruit is edible, with a sweet and musky flavor, and is often consumed raw or used in culinary applications.

The plant also has ornamental value due to its attractive foliage and unique fruit shape.
