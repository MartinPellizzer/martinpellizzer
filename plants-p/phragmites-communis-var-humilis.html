Phragmites communis var. humilis

Phragmites communis var. humilis, commonly known as the tall reed, is a variant of the common reed that is typically found in wetland environments.

It is a tall, perennial grass that can grow up to 3 meters in height, with narrow, grass-like leaves and dense, cylindrical flower spikes. This plant thrives in shallow water or moist soil, often forming dense stands that stabilize shorelines. Its rhizomes spread extensively, allowing it to dominate wetland ecosystems.

The plant plays a crucial role in water filtration and provides habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
