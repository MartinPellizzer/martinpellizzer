Piloisia curassavica

Piloisia curassavica, commonly known as the Caribbean flame tree, is a tropical plant native to the Caribbean and parts of South America.

It is characterized by its striking orange-red flowers that bloom in clusters, often appearing in late summer and autumn. The plant has compound leaves composed of several oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces small, round fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Piloisia curassavica is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
