Papaver radicatum var. hispidum

Papaver radicatum var. hispidum, commonly known as the hispid poppy, is a variant of the European poppy characterized by its hairy stems and petals.

It is a herbaceous perennial native to southern Europe, particularly found in rocky and arid environments. The plant produces striking red or orange flowers with a distinctive, saucer-shaped corolla. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine, stiff hairs.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
