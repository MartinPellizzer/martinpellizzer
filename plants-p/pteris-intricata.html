Pteris intricata

Pteris intricata, commonly known as the Chinese water fern, is a species of fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that give it a lacy appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine, and it is valued for its ornamental qualities.

Pteris intricata reproduces through spores and is a member of the family Pteridaceae.
