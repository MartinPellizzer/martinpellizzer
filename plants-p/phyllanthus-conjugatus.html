Phyllanthus conjugatus

Phyllanthus conjugatus, commonly known as the crassula or false uzara, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, succulent foliage and small, greenish-white flowers. The plant has a dense, bushy growth habit with fleshy, opposite leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It is adapted to arid environments and can thrive in well-drained soils.

Phyllanthus conjugatus is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance and drought tolerance.
