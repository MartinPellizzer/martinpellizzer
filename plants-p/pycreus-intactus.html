Pycreus intactus

Pycreus intactus, commonly known as the desert star cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a small, globular cactus with a ribbed structure that helps it store water efficiently in its succulent stems. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, adding a striking visual contrast to its green foliage. Its leaves are modified into spines, which provide protection from herbivores and reduce water loss through transpiration.

Overall, Pycreus intactus is well adapted to harsh desert environments, showcasing remarkable resilience and unique botanical features.
