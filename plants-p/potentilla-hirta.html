Potentilla hirta

Potentilla hirta, commonly known as the hairy cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to northern regions of North America and Eurasia.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in moist, open habitats such as wet meadows and riverbanks. The plant features small, five-petaled yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are usually hairy and have a distinctive five-lobed shape, giving it its common name.

Potentilla hirta is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
