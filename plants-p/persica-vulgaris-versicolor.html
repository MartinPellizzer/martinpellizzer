Persica vulgaris versicolor

Persica vulgaris versicolor, commonly known as the variable peach.

This plant is a variant of the peach tree, characterized by its diverse flower colors, ranging from white to pink and red. It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is native to temperate regions of Asia. The flowers are typically borne in clusters and are an important source of nectar for pollinators.

The fruit, though less commonly cultivated than standard peaches, is valued for its ornamental appeal and unique coloration.
