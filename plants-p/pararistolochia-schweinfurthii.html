Pararistolochia schweinfurthii

Pararistolochia schweinfurthii, commonly known as the African birthwort, is a climbing vine native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Aristolochiaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped, aromatic leaves. The plant produces unique, intricate flowers that are often mistaken for leaves, with a complex structure that resembles a pipe or a horn. Its roots contain compounds that have been traditionally used in herbal medicine, though they are toxic.

The species is notable for its ecological role in supporting certain insect pollinators.
