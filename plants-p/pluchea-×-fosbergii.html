Pluchea × fosbergii

Pluchea × fosbergii, commonly known as Fosberg's ironweed, is a hybrid perennial plant native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its erect, branched stems and dense clusters of small, yellow to orange flower heads. The plant produces fluffy, white to grayish pappus structures that aid in wind dispersal of its seeds. Its foliage is typically lance-shaped with hairy surfaces, and it thrives in moist, shaded environments.

Pluchea × fosbergii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local insect populations.
