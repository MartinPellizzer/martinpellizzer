Physalis ignota

Physalis ignota, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or golden gooseberry, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its vibrant orange, lantern-shaped fruits. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by the development of its distinctive, papery fruit husks. Its leaves are typically broad and ovate, with a slightly hairy texture.

Physalis ignota is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible fruits, which are rich in vitamin C.
