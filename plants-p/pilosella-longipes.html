Pilosella longipes

Pilosella longipes, commonly known as the long-stemmed pilosella, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters. The plant produces finely hairy leaves that are typically lance-shaped and have a slightly rough texture. Native to Europe and parts of Asia, it thrives in meadows and grassy areas.

Pilosella longipes is often found in open habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
