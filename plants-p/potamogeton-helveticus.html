Potamogeton helveticus

Potamogeton helveticus, commonly known as the European pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to temperate regions of Europe.

It has long, slender leaves that are typically arranged in whorls around the stem, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often submerged or just at the water's surface. The plant thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, such as ponds and lakes. It plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various water organisms.

However, it can become invasive in some regions, outcompeting native species and altering aquatic habitats.
