Poa callosa

Poa callosa, commonly known as the rough meadowgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its coarse, hairy leaves and dense, tufted growth habit. The plant typically thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine environments. Its flowering stems bear spikelets with bracts that are often hairy, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Poa callosa plays a vital role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat and is valued for its ornamental and ecological significance.
