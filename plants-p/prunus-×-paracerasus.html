Prunus × paracerasus

Prunus × paracerasus, commonly known as the Japanese cherry or yamabuki, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Japan.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its ornamental value, particularly during spring when it blooms with clusters of white or pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 3–6 meters and features alternate, ovate leaves with serrated edges. Its fruit is a small, hard drupe that ripens in late summer, containing a single seed.

Prunus × paracerasus is widely cultivated for its aesthetic appeal in gardens and landscapes.
