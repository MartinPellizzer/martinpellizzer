Polypodium multifolium

Polypodium multifolium, commonly known as the many-leaved wall fern, is a species of fern native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its lush, dark green fronds that are typically divided into multiple segments, giving it a feathery appearance. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often growing on rocky surfaces or in forest undergrowth. Its rhizome system allows it to spread and form dense colonies, making it a common sight in humid climates.

Polypodium multifolium is valued both for its ornamental appeal and its ecological role in stabilizing soil in its natural habitat.
