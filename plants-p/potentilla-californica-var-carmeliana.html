Potentilla californica var. carmeliana

Potentilla californica var. carmeliana, commonly known as Carmel potentilla, is a low-growing, evergreen shrub native to coastal areas of California.

It features wiry, arching stems covered in dense clusters of small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has dark green, oval-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine, silvery hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy choice for coastal gardens.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and ability to tolerate salt spray and coastal conditions.
