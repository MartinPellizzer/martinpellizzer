Phylica axillaris var. hirsuta

Phylica axillaris var. hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy lepidote, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Proteaceae family and is characterized by its dense, wiry foliage and showy, red or pink inflorescences. The plant is typically found in coastal and sandy environments, thriving in well-drained soils. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its distinctive appearance.
