Pyrus godiva

Pyrus godiva, commonly known as the Godiva pear, is a deciduous tree native to Europe.

It produces small, round pears that are typically harvested in late summer. The plant has a shallow root system and thrives in well-drained soil. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and oval-shaped with a serrated edge.

The flowers are white and borne in clusters, attracting pollinators like bees.
