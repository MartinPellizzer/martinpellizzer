Polygonum lapathifolium var. secondaireanum

Polygonum lapathifolium var. secondaireanum, commonly known as the redstem filaree, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, stream banks, and woodland edges. The plant has slender, upright stems that are reddish in color, especially when mature. Its leaves are lance-shaped with a hairy surface and a reddish or purple tinge along the veins.

The flowers are small, clustered in spikes, and range in color from white to pink, blooming from late spring to early autumn.
