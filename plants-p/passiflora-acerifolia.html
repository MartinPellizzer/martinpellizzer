Passiflora acerifolia

Passiflora acerifolia, commonly known as the mapleleaf passionflower, is a climbing vine native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, maple-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces striking flowers with five sepals, five petals, and a unique crown of filaments surrounding the reproductive structures. Its fruit is a woody, spiny capsule that contains edible, sweet-smelling seeds.

Passiflora acerifolia is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated for its medicinal properties.
