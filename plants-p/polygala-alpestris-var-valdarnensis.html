Polygala alpestris var. valdarnensis

Polygala alpestris var. valdarnensis, commonly known as the Valdarno milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of central Italy.

It belongs to the Polygalaceae family and is characterized by its slender stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils. Its flowers are usually pink or purple, and it produces distinctive, spiral-shaped seed pods.

This variety is of interest to botanists due to its limited distribution and ecological adaptations.
