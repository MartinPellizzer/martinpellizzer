Pauletia longifolia

Pauletia longifolia, commonly known as the longleaf pauletia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery pubescence. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, which bloom during the warmer months. Pauletia longifolia thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it a suitable candidate for xeriscaping in arid environments.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its delicate foliage and subtle floral display.
