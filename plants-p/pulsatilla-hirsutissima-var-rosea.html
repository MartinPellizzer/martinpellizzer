Pulsatilla hirsutissima var. rosea

Pulsatilla hirsutissima var. rosea, commonly known as the roseate pasque flower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, rose-pink to lavender-colored flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has hairy, gray-green foliage and produces small, woody capsules containing numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained, alkaline soils and is often found in rocky or alpine environments.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with specific horticultural care.
