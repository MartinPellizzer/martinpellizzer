Panicum maximum var. glaucum

Panicum maximum var. glaucum, commonly known as Guinea grass, is a perennial grass species native to Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, erect growth habit and narrow, bluish-green leaves. The plant produces dense, spike-like inflorescences that are often pale green to brown in color. It is highly adaptable and thrives in a variety of soil types, making it a common pasture grass.

Due to its high nutritional value, it is widely used in livestock farming.
