Pleurothallis nakatae

Pleurothallis nakatae, commonly known as the Nakata's orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical rainforests of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its small, intricate flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and green. The plant is epiphytic, meaning it grows on the surface of trees or other plants without deriving nutrients from them. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is often considered a conservation concern.
