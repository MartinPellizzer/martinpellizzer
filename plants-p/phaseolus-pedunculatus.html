Phaseolus pedunculatus

Phaseolus pedunculatus, commonly known as the scarlet runner bean, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its climbing habit, with tendrils that allow it to ascend supports. The plant produces vibrant red or orange flowers, which are followed by elongated pods containing edible seeds. Its botanical features include compound leaves and a fibrous root system.

It thrives in warm climates and is widely cultivated for both ornamental and culinary purposes.
