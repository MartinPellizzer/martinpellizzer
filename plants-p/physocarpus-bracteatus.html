Physocarpus bracteatus

Physocarpus bracteatus, commonly known as the Chinese beautyberry, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its clusters of vibrant purple berries that appear in late summer. The plant has compound leaves that are typically ovate to elliptic in shape. It thrives in partial to full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

The berries are not only visually striking but also serve as a food source for various birds and wildlife.
