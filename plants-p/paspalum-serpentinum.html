Paspalum serpentinum

Paspalum serpentinum, commonly known as mountain paspalum, is a perennial grass native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or mountainous regions.

Paspalum serpentinum is adapted to harsh environmental conditions, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
