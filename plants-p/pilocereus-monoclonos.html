Pilocereus monoclonos

Pilocereus monoclonos, commonly known as the Moon Cactus, is a cactus species native to South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive appearance, featuring a swollen, colorful "skirt" and a narrow, green stem. The plant is a hybrid, often combining the vibrant flowers of *Schlumbergera* with the stem of *Pilocereus* species. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires bright, indirect sunlight.

Due to its ornamental value, it is popular among cactus enthusiasts for its unique and colorful display.
