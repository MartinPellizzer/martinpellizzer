Prunus ilicifolia var. integrifolia

Prunus ilicifolia var. integrifolia, commonly known as the Pacific madrone, is a deciduous tree native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its smooth, reddish bark and clusters of white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has elliptical leaves with entire margins, which are dark green on top and pale underneath. It produces small, red berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife.

The species is adapted to coastal and mountainous regions, thriving in well-drained soils and full sunlight.
