Pimpinella × intermedia

Pimpinella × intermedia, commonly known as the hybrid angelica, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Apiaceae family.

It is a naturally occurring hybrid between Pimpinella polychroma and Pimpinella lapathifolia, characterized by its compound leaves and small white or yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream sides. It produces small, oval fruits that are ridged and contain several seeds.

Botanically, it is notable for its aromatic foliage and its role in ecological systems as a food source for various insects.
