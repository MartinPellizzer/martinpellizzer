Prunus × fruticosa f. taizanfukun

Prunus × fruticosa f. taizanfukun, commonly known as the Taizanfukun cherry, is a compact, ornamental shrub native to Japan.

It features small, oval leaves that turn vibrant red in the fall, adding visual interest to gardens. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers in early spring, followed by dark red, edible berries. It is well-suited for container gardening and can thrive in USDA hardiness zones 6-8.

Its unique foliage and seasonal color changes make it a popular choice for ornamental horticulture.
