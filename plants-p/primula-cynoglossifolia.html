Primula cynoglossifolia

Primula cynoglossifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaf primrose, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It features a basal rosette of broad, ovate to elliptic leaves that are often hairy and have a leathery texture. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, or purple, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in forest understories or rocky habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
