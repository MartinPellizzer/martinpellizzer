Polygala hoehneana

Polygala hoehneana, commonly known as the Hoehne's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of South America.

It belongs to the Polygalaceae family and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, delicate flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to survive in dry, nutrient-poor environments. Its flowers are usually pale green or white, and it produces small, dry fruits that contain seeds.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
