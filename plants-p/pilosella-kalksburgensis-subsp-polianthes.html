Pilosella kalksburgensis subsp. polianthes

Pilosella kalksburgensis subsp.

polianthes, commonly known as the Kalksburg bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous small florets.

The plant features basal and stem leaves that are typically lanceolate and covered in fine, soft hairs. It produces showy yellow to orange flower heads in the summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

This subspecies is notable for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
