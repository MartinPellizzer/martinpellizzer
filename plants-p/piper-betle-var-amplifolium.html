Piper betle var. amplifolium

Piper betle var. amplifolium, commonly known as large-leaf betel, is a variant of the betel leaf plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often used in traditional practices and for their aromatic properties. The plant belongs to the Piperaceae family and is closely related to the common betel (Piper betle). Its leaves are typically harvested for their stimulant properties and are used in various cultural and medicinal contexts.

The botanical features of this variant include its robust growth habit and the presence of essential oils in its foliage.
