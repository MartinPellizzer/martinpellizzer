Phacelia humilis var. dudleyi

Phacelia humilis var. dudleyi, commonly known as Dudley's phacelia, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the western United States.

It features slender stems and small, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rocky or arid environments.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental flowers make it a valuable addition to native plant gardens.
