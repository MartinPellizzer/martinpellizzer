Piper accedens

Piper accedens, commonly known as the black pepper plant, is a flowering vine native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its small, dark berries that contain the spice known as black pepper. The plant produces small, white flowers followed by green, unripe berries that turn red when mature. The berries are harvested and dried to form the familiar black pepper used in cooking and medicine.

It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
