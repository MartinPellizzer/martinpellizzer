Pyrus subdanubialis

Pyrus subdanubialis, commonly known as the Danube pear, is a species of pear native to Europe.

It is characterized by its small, oval-shaped fruits that typically ripen in late summer. The plant produces white flowers in early spring, which are followed by the development of its edible fruit. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in riverine environments.

The leaves are broad and have a rough texture, contributing to its distinctive botanical appearance.
