Phaca debilis

Phaca debilis, commonly known as the weak phaca, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, small yellow flowers and hairy, narrow leaves. Native to South America, particularly Brazil, it thrives in moist, shaded environments. The plant has a short lifespan and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological studies in its native habitat.
