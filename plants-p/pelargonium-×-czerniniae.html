Pelargonium × czerniniae

Pelargonium × czerniniae, commonly known as the Czernin's geranium, is a hybrid perennial plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Geraniaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, five-petaled flowers. The plant typically produces vibrant blooms in shades of red, pink, and white, which appear in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are usually rounded with a slightly serrated edge, and the plant prefers well-drained soil and full sun.

Pelargonium × czerniniae is often cultivated for ornamental purposes in gardens and as a decorative plant.
