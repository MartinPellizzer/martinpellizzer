Poa fischeri

Poa fischeri, commonly known as Fischer's bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue that gives it its common name.

Poa fischeri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
