Prunus × lannesiana subf. albida

Prunus × lannesiana subf.

albida, commonly known as the white-flowered cherry, is a hybrid species within the Prunus genus. It is characterized by its showy, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in early spring.

The plant typically grows as a deciduous shrub or small tree, with smooth, gray bark and ovate leaves that turn yellow in autumn. It thrives in temperate climates and prefers well-drained soil.

This cultivar is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
