Persicaria hartwrightii

Persicaria hartwrightii, commonly known as Hartwright's knotweed, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers arranged in dense clusters, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Persicaria hartwrightii is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits, providing habitat and nectar for various pollinators.
