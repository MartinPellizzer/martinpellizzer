Phyllanthus bupleuroides var. bupleuroides

Phyllanthus bupleuroides var. bupleuroides, commonly known as the small-leaved phyllanthus, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are typically arranged oppositely along the stems. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, often in the axils of the leaves. Its fruit is a small, smooth, three-lobed capsule that contains several seeds.

This species is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along stream banks.
