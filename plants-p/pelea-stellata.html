Pelea stellata

Pelea stellata, commonly known as star pelea, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its star-shaped flowers, which are typically white or pale blue in color. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Pelea stellata is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
