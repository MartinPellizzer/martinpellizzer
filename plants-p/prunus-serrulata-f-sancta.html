Prunus serrulata f. sancta

Prunus serrulata f. sancta, commonly known as the Japanese cherry blossom or sakura, is a cultivar of the Prunus serrulata species.

It is characterized by its delicate, pink or white flowers that bloom in early spring, creating a striking visual display. The plant is deciduous, shedding its leaves in the fall, and thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil. It is often cultivated in gardens and parks for its ornamental value.

The f. sancta form is particularly noted for its compact growth and profuse flowering.
