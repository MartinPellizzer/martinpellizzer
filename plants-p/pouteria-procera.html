Pouteria procera

Pouteria procera, commonly known as the Brazil nut tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Amazon Basin.

It is characterized by its large, compound leaves and produces clusters of small, yellow flowers. The tree's most notable feature is its hard-shelled, woody fruit, which contains multiple seeds known as Brazil nuts. The plant thrives in humid, nutrient-rich soils and requires a warm, stable climate.

It plays a vital ecological role in the Amazon rainforest, supporting a wide range of wildlife.
