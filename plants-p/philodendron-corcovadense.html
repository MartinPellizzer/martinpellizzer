Philodendron corcovadense

Philodendron corcovadense, commonly known as the red-veined philodendron, is a tropical climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves with striking red veins that contrast with its dark green foliage. The plant typically has a vigorous growth habit and can reach significant heights when given proper support. Its leaves are often used in ornamental horticulture due to their aesthetic appeal.

It prefers bright, indirect light and well-draining soil to thrive.
