Panicum laxum var. variegatum

Panicum laxum var. variegatum, commonly known as the variegated dropseed, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It features slender, arching stems that produce delicate, feathery flower plumes, which add ornamental value to gardens. The leaves are narrow and bluish-green, often with a striking variegation pattern of white or cream markings. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soils, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a border plant.

This plant is drought-tolerant and low-maintenance, contributing to its popularity among native plant enthusiasts.
