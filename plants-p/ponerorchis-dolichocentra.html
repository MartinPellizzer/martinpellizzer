Ponerorchis dolichocentra

Ponerorchis dolichocentra, commonly known as the long-stalked marsh orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is a terrestrial herb that typically grows in wet meadows and marshy areas. The plant features a slender, unbranched stem that supports a single, elongated flower spike. Its flowers are usually greenish-yellow with a distinctive, spurred lip.

Ponerorchis dolichocentra is characterized by its delicate appearance and specialized pollination mechanisms.
