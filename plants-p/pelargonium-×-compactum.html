Pelargonium × compactum

Pelargonium × compactum, commonly known as the ivy-leaved geranium, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Geraniaceae family.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and heart-shaped, dark green leaves with a velvety texture. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink, red, or white, depending on the cultivar. Native to South Africa, it is widely cultivated as an ornamental plant for its attractive foliage and long-lasting blooms.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it suitable for containers, hanging baskets, and garden beds.
