Podalyria thunbergiana

Podalyria thunbergiana, commonly known as Thunberg's bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features slender, upright stems that bear opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a rough, hairy texture. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue to purple, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is often cultivated in gardens and natural habitats.
