Pteronia incana

Pteronia incana, commonly known as the silver pterony, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its soft, silvery-gray foliage. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink or white, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Its drought tolerance and ornamental appeal make it a valuable addition to xeriscaped gardens.
