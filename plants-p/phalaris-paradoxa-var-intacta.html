Phalaris paradoxa var. intacta

Phalaris paradoxa var. intacta, commonly known as the paradox grass, is a perennial grass species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its distinctive, slender blades and compact, fountain-like growth habit. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Due to its ornamental value and unique appearance, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
