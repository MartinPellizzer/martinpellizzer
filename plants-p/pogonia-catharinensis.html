Pogonia catharinensis

Pogonia catharinensis, commonly known as the Carolinian bog orchid, is a rare and delicate orchid species native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in wet, acidic bogs and swampy areas, where it thrives in the moist, nutrient-poor soil. The plant features a slender stem with a single, showy orchid flower that is usually pale pink or white with intricate patterns. Its leaves are grass-like and arranged along the stem, contributing to its overall herbaceous appearance.

Due to habitat loss and environmental changes, it is considered endangered and requires conservation efforts to ensure its survival.
