Potentilla Puberula Var. Viridis

Potentilla puberula var. viridis is a lesser-known yet striking member of the rose family known for its unique foliage and modest flowers. This variant is characterized by its velvety green leaves that stand out against its typical gray-green foliage. Native to rocky and arid regions, it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its hardy nature makes it a valuable addition to drought-tolerant gardens. Despite its beauty, it remains underappreciated in horticultural circles.
