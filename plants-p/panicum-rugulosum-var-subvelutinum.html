Panicum rugulosum var. subvelutinum

Panicum rugulosum var. subvelutinum, commonly known as silky foxtail, is a grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and narrow, silky-hairy leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces spikelets that are loosely arranged, with bracts that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in full sun and is commonly found in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and fields.

Due to its ornamental value and ability to stabilize soil, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and restoration projects.
