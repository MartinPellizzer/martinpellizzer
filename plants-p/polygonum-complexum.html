Polygonum complexum

Polygonum complexum, commonly known as the common smartweed, is a flowering plant in the Polygonaceae family.

It is characterized by its upright, branched stems and alternate, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or red, which appear in late summer to early autumn. Polygonum complexum is often found in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and fields, and is adaptable to a variety of soil types.

It is a perennial herb that can spread through both seeds and rhizomes.
