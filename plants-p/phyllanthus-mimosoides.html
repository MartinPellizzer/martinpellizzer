Phyllanthus mimosoides

Phyllanthus mimosoides, commonly known as the African buttertree, is a shrub native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically yellow or green, followed by distinctive capsule fruits. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories or along riverbanks.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
