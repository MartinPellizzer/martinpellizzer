Piper cutucuense

Piper cutucuense, commonly known as the Cutucu pepper, is a flowering plant native to the Amazon rainforest.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species. The plant produces small, green berries that turn red when mature, containing the spicy compound responsible for its flavor. It is often cultivated for its unique taste and potential medicinal properties.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species in both ecological and agricultural contexts.
