Poranthera corymbosa var. sparsifolia

Poranthera corymbosa var. sparsifolia, commonly known as the sparsely leafy sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to western North America.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in summer. The leaves are narrow and often spaced apart, giving the plant a sparse appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal scrub and dunes.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
