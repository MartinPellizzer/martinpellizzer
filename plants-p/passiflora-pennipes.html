Passiflora pennipes

Passiflora pennipes, commonly known as the purple passionflower, is a climbing vine native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, intricate flowers that feature five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments surrounding the reproductive structures. The plant produces small, round, yellowish-green berries that are edible and often used in culinary applications. Its leaves are heart-shaped with a rough texture and are often used in traditional medicine.

Passiflora pennipes thrives in humid, warm climates and requires support for its climbing growth habit.
