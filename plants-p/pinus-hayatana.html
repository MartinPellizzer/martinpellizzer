Pinus hayatana

Pinus hayatana, commonly known as the Japanese white pine, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its slender, pyramidal shape and long, needle-like leaves that are typically two to three inches in length. The tree produces cones that range from 1.5 to 3 inches in length, containing seeds surrounded by a papery wing. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is also used in traditional Japanese gardens.
