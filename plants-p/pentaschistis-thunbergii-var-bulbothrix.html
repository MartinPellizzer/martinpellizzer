Pentaschistis thunbergii var. bulbothrix

Pentaschistis thunbergii var. bulbothrix, commonly known as the Japanese yew, is a species of flowering plant in the family Taxaceae.

It is a small evergreen shrub characterized by its needle-like leaves and small, reddish berries. The plant is native to Japan and parts of Korea, where it thrives in shaded, moist environments. It has a slow growth rate and is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value.

The botanical features of this variety include its distinct bark and the presence of toxic compounds in its foliage.
