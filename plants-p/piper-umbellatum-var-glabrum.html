Piper umbellatum var. glabrum

Piper umbellatum var. glabrum, commonly known as the smooth pepper plant, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its smooth, hairless stems and leaves that are typically ovate to lanceolate in shape. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by spherical, red berries that are edible and have a peppery flavor. It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and culinary uses.

The variety glabrum is distinguished by its lack of pubescence, making it visually distinct from other varieties of Piper umbellatum.
