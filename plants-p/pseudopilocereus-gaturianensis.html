Pseudopilocereus gaturianensis

Pseudopilocereus gaturianensis, commonly known as the Gaturian night bloomer, is a cactus species native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems that are typically covered in white to gray spines. The plant produces large, fragrant white flowers that open at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its striking appearance and unique flowering behavior, it is a popular choice for collectors and xerophytic gardens.
