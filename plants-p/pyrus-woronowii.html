Pyrus woronowii

Pyrus woronowii, commonly known as the Korean pear or Japanese white pear, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its alternate, simple, and serrated leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, which are followed by edible, sweet, and juicy fruits. The fruit has a white, crisp flesh and is often used in culinary applications.

It is valued for its ornamental value and fruit production in suitable climates.
