Pseudechinolaena camusiana

Pseudechinolaena camusiana, commonly known as the Camus' spikeweed, is a member of the Poaceae family, characterized by its grass-like morphology.

The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny florets. It is adapted to withstand drought conditions, with deep root systems that help it survive in nutrient-poor environments.
