Polygala vulgaris var. adriatica

Polygala vulgaris var. adriatica, commonly known as the Adriatic milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in coastal areas and open woodlands. Its leaves are narrow and hairy, and it produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
