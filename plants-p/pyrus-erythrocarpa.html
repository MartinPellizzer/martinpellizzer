Pyrus erythrocarpa

Pyrus erythrocarpa, commonly known as the red-fruited pear.

It is a deciduous tree native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, particularly in parts of Kazakhstan and Kyrgyzstan. The plant is characterized by its smooth, gray-barked trunk and lance-shaped leaves that turn yellow in autumn. Its most distinctive feature is its bright red, fleshy fruits, which are edible but not widely cultivated.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
