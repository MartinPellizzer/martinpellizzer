Prunus × donarium f. temari

Prunus × donarium f. temari, commonly known as the Japanese cherry blossom, is a hybrid species within the Prunus genus.

It is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers that bloom in early spring, typically in shades of white and pink. The plant is a deciduous tree or shrub, often reaching heights of 5 to 10 meters. Its leaves are elliptical, with a glossy dark green upper surface and a lighter underside.

The fruit is a small, hard drupe that matures in late summer, though it is not typically consumed by humans.
