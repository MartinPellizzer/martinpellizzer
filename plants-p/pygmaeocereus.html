Pygmaeocereus

Pygmaeocereus, commonly known as the miniature cactus, is a genus of flowering plants in the cactus family, Cactaceae.

These plants are native to arid and semi-arid regions of South America, particularly in countries like Argentina and Chile. They are characterized by their small, spiny stems and the ability to store water in their thick, fleshy tissues. Pygmaeocereus species typically produce clusters of small, white or yellow flowers during the warmer months.

Due to their compact size and unique appearance, they are popular among cactus enthusiasts and are often cultivated as ornamental plants.
