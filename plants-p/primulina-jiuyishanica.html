Primulina jiuyishanica

Primulina jiuyishanica, commonly known as the Jiuyishan primula, is a rare and endemic species native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant, showy flowers. The plant typically features broad, waxy leaves and clusters of bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink and white. It thrives in cool, humid environments and is often found in shaded, forested areas.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a conservation concern.
