Piper teysmannii

Piper teysmannii, commonly known as the Teyssmann's pepper, is a flowering plant native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species. The plant is characterized by its climbing habit and compound leaves with opposite arrangement. Its small, greenish-white flowers are clustered in spikes, and it produces small, round berries that are typically green when unripe.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated for its unique foliage.
