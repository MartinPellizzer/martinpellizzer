Picea davidiana

Picea davidiana, commonly known as David's fir, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to the mountainous regions of western China.

It is characterized by its slender, pyramidal shape and dark green, needle-like leaves that are typically arranged in spirals. The tree produces cones that are elongated and cylindrical, containing winged seeds. It thrives in cool, moist climates and is often found at high altitudes in forested areas.

The wood of Picea davidiana is valued for its strength and durability, making it a useful resource in various applications.
