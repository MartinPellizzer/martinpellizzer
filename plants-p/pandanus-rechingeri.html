Pandanus rechingeri

Pandanus rechingeri, commonly known as the giant pandanus, is a tropical plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its large, sword-shaped leaves that can grow up to several meters in length. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically grouped in dense clusters. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to thrive in wet, coastal environments.

The plant is often used in traditional crafts and has cultural significance in many island communities.
