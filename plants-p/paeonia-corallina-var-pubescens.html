Paeonia corallina var. pubescens

Paeonia corallina var. pubescens, commonly known as the pubescent coral peony, is a striking perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of China.

It is characterized by its vibrant, coral-pink flowers that bloom in early spring, often with a subtle fragrance. The plant features large, palmate leaves that are covered in fine, soft hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Due to its ornamental value and unique botanical features, it is a sought-after species among horticulturists and plant enthusiasts.
