Pachychilus speciosus

Pachychilus speciosus, commonly known as the showy pachychilus, is a striking member of the Bromeliaceae family native to South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, colorful bracts that resemble flowers, which are actually modified leaves. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, anchoring itself to trees in humid tropical forests. Its rosette-shaped leaves are thick and waxy, adapted to store water in its native environment.

Pachychilus speciosus is prized in horticulture for its ornamental value and unique appearance.
