Plantago caspica

Plantago caspica, commonly known as the Caspian plantain, is a perennial herb native to Central Asia.

It features a rosette of broad, lance-shaped leaves that are hairy and covered in a dense layer of fine pubescence. The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in a spike, which are borne on slender stalks. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments.

The species is valued for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally in herbal remedies.
