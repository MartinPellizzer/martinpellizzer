Portulaca philippii

Portulaca philippii, commonly known as the Mexican portulaca, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is a low-growing, succulent species that thrives in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant produces vibrant yellow or orange flowers that bloom in the morning and close by midday. Its fleshy leaves and stems store water, allowing it to survive in dry conditions.

Portulaca philippii is often used in ornamental gardening due to its resilience and attractive appearance.
