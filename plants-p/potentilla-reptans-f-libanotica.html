Potentilla reptans f. libanotica

Potentilla reptans f. libanotica, commonly known as the creeping cinquefoil, is a low-growing shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has dark green, serrated leaves and a sprawling growth habit, often forming dense mats on the ground. It thrives in well-drained soils and is commonly found in rocky or sandy habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardiness in temperate climates.
