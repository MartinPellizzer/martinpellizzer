Poa glaucifolia

Poa glaucifolia, commonly known as the bluegrass clover, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, bluish-green leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with pale blue to white flowers, which bloom in the spring and early summer. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes.

Poa glaucifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
