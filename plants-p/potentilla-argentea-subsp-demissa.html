Potentilla argentea subsp. demissa

Potentilla argentea subsp.

demissa, commonly known as silver cinquefoil, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia. It features small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant has finely divided, silver-hairy leaves that help reflect sunlight and reduce water loss. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky habitats.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardeners seeking drought-tolerant, hardy plants.
