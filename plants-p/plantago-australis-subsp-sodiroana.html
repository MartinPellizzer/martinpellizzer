Plantago australis subsp. sodiroana

Plantago australis subsp.

sodiroana, commonly known as the New Zealand plantain, is a perennial herb native to New Zealand. It is characterized by its rosette of broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs.

The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in a spike, which are borne on a tall stem. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, riverbanks, and coastal areas.

This subspecies is notable for its adaptability and ecological importance in supporting local biodiversity.
