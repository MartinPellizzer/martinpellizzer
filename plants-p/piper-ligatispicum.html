Piper ligatispicum

Piper ligatispicum, commonly known as the golden pepper or wild pepper, is a climbing shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes over 2,000 species of flowering plants. The plant is characterized by its small, yellowish-green berries that are often used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a distinctive, peppery aroma.

Piper ligatispicum is valued for its medicinal properties and is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental appeal.
