Phyteuma × adulterinum

Phyteuma × adulterinum, commonly known as the hybrid sunflower or false sunflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like yellow to orange composite flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in full sun to partial shade.
