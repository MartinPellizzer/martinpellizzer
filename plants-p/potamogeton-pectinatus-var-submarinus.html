Potamogeton pectinatus var. submarinus

Potamogeton pectinatus var. submarinus, commonly known as the submerged pondweed, is a aquatic plant native to freshwater environments.

It is characterized by its slender, ribbon-like leaves that are typically submerged in water. The plant produces small, green flowers that emerge from the water surface, and it often forms dense mats in shallow waters. It plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.

This variety is adapted to low light conditions and can thrive in a range of water depths.
