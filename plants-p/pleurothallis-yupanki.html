Pleurothallis yupanki

Pleurothallis yupanki, commonly known as the Andean orchid, is a species native to the cloud forests of the Andes in South America.

It is a small, epiphytic orchid that thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant features intricate, delicate flowers with a wide range of colors, often in shades of white, pink, and purple. Its foliage consists of narrow, leathery leaves that help it retain moisture in its natural habitat.

Pleurothallis yupanki is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
