Pisum sativum subsp. hortense

Pisum sativum subsp.

hortense, commonly known as garden pea, is a cultivated subspecies of the pea plant. It is characterized by its erect growth habit and pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

The plant typically has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces flowers in shades of white, violet, or blue. Its botanical features include a fibrous root system and a stem that is often hairy.

This subspecies is widely grown for its edible seeds, which are rich in protein and nutrients.
