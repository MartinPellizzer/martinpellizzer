Penstemon deamii

Penstemon deamii, commonly known as Deam's beardtongue, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its upright stems and spikes of tubular, two-lipped flowers that range in color from pink to purple. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. Its leaves are narrow and gray-green, often covered in a fine layer of hair.

Penstemon deamii is valued in gardens for its drought tolerance and attractive blooms.
