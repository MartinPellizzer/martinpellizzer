Pinus sinensis var. densata

Pinus sinensis var. densata, commonly known as the Chinese white pine, is a variety of the Chinese pine native to southern China.

It is characterized by its dense, compact foliage and slender, upward-curving needles that are typically 5 to 8 centimeters long. The tree produces cones that are cylindrical and often persist on the branches for several years. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.

This variety is also valued for its timber and has been used in traditional Chinese architecture and crafts.
