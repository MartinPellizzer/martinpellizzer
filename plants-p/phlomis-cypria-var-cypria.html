Phlomis cypria var. cypria

Phlomis cypria var. cypria, commonly known as Cyprus thistle, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features erect, branched stems covered in soft, grayish-green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces showy, pink to purple flower heads that are surrounded by narrow, spiny bracts. Its botanical characteristics include a fibrous root system and a preference for well-drained, sunny soils.

It is often found in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to arid environments.
