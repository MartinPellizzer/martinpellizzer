Prunus cerasus var. jenensis

Prunus cerasus var. jenensis, commonly known as the Jenense Cherry, is a variety of sweet cherry native to Central Europe.

It is characterized by its small, dark red fruits with a firm, juicy flesh and a sweet, aromatic flavor. The plant typically grows as a small to medium-sized tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are oval-shaped with a pointed tip and a smooth, dark green upper surface.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for both its beauty and edible fruit.
