Pulsatilla nigella

Pulsatilla nigella, commonly known as the meadow anemone, is a flowering plant belonging to the Ranunculaceae family.

It is characterized by its showy, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, often with a yellow center. The plant typically grows in moist meadows and woodland areas, preferring well-drained soil. Its leaves are palmately divided, and the plant is known for its short stature, usually reaching heights of less than 30 centimeters.

Pulsatilla nigella is also noted for its role in supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
