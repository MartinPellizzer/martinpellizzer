Palaquium petiolare

Palaquium petiolare, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or yellow mangosteen, is a tropical evergreen tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant white flowers. The tree produces small, yellow fruits that are edible and have a sweet, tangy flavor. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit production.

The plant is also valued for its hard, heavy wood used in traditional craftsmanship.
