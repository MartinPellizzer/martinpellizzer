Poa sandvicensis

Poa sandvicensis, commonly known as the Hawaiian grass, is a perennial grass species native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and narrow, bluish-green leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and lowland habitats.

Poa sandvicensis plays a significant role in the ecological dynamics of Hawaii's native ecosystems.
