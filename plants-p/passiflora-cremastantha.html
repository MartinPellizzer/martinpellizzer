Passiflora cremastantha

Passiflora cremastantha, commonly known as the coral passionflower, is a climbing vine native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, coral-pink bracts that resemble petals, giving it its common name. The plant produces showy, complex flowers with a unique structure featuring a crown of filaments and a central cluster of stamens. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped with a rough texture, and it thrives in tropical and subtropical climates.

Passiflora cremastantha is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
