Piper clementis

Piper clementis, commonly known as the Clement's pepper plant, is a member of the Piperaceae family, which includes various pepper species.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, and thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant produces small, green to red berries that are used in traditional medicine and cuisine. Its leaves are often used as a spice, similar to black pepper, and contain essential oils with potential medicinal properties.

Piper clementis is valued for its aromatic foliage and unique fruit, making it a notable species in botanical studies.
