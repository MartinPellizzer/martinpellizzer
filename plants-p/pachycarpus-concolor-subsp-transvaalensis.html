Pachycarpus concolor subsp. transvaalensis

Pachycarpus concolor subsp.

transvaalensis, commonly known as the Transvaal barberton, is a shrub native to South Africa. It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink.

The plant has a compact growth habit and is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. It produces small, woody fruits that contain several seeds.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its hardiness and drought tolerance.
