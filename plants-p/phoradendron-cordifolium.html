Phoradendron cordifolium

Phoradendron cordifolium, commonly known as the heartleaf mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on various host trees in the southeastern United States.

It has small, heart-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in pairs along the stems. The plant produces small, white or greenish flowers that are often clustered together. Its berries, which are usually red or yellow, contain the seeds that are dispersed by birds.

The botanical structure of Phoradendron cordifolium reflects its parasitic lifestyle, with reduced roots and specialized structures for attaching to host plants.
