Potamogeton porteri

Potamogeton porteri, commonly known as the Florida pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to the southeastern United States.

It has long, slender stems that are often branched and bear small, oval-shaped leaves. The plant produces tiny, inconspicuous flowers that are typically arranged in clusters near the water surface. It thrives in freshwater environments such as lakes, ponds, and slow-moving streams.
