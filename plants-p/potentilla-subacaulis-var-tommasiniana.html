Potentilla subacaulis var. tommasiniana

Potentilla subacaulis var. tommasiniana, commonly known as the Alpine cinquefoil, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its compact, mat-forming habit and small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The leaves are typically divided into five oval-shaped lobes, giving it the common name "cinquefoil.". It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in mountainous habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in alpine ecosystems.
