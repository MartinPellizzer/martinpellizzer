Phoenix humilis var. pedunculata

Phoenix humilis var. pedunculata, commonly known as the Mexican fan palm, is a small, ornamental palm native to Mexico.

It features a slender trunk with a smooth, grayish-brown bark and produces multiple fan-shaped leaves that are bright green and stiff. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for warm climates. It is often cultivated as a decorative plant in gardens and landscapes due to its attractive appearance and relatively low maintenance requirements.

However, it is important to note that it can be invasive in certain regions if not managed properly.
