Persicaria odorata f. conspicua

Persicaria odorata f. conspicua, commonly known as the fragrant knotweed, is a variant of the species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, fuzzy texture. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or red, adding visual interest to gardens. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is cultivated in various regions for its aesthetic appeal and ecological benefits.
