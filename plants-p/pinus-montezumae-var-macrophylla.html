Pinus montezumae var. macrophylla

Pinus montezumae var. macrophylla, commonly known as the Mexican bristlecone pine, is a variety of the Mexican pine native to high-altitude regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and dense, needle-like foliage. The plant produces cones that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Its leaves are long and thin, typically measuring up to 5 centimeters in length.

This species is adapted to cold, dry environments and is often found in mountainous regions with well-drained soil.
