Plectranthus saxatilis

Plectranthus saxatilis, commonly known as the rock plectranthus, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and lance-shaped, aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically purple or blue in color, arranged in clusters. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Plectranthus saxatilis is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
