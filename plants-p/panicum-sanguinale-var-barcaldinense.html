Panicum sanguinale var. barcaldinense

Panicum sanguinale var. barcaldinense, commonly known as Barcaldine lovegrass, is a perennial grass native to Australia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces small, pale green spikelets that are clustered in loose panicles, giving it a delicate appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
