Paphiopedilum macfarlanei

Paphiopedilum macfarlanei, commonly known as the Macfarlane's lady's slipper, is a rare and striking orchid native to the mountainous regions of Borneo.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers with a distinctive slipper-shaped pouch, which is a defining feature of the Paphiopedilum genus. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to tree bark in its natural habitat. Its flowers are usually pale pink to white with intricate patterns, and it blooms during the late winter or early spring.

Due to habitat loss and over-collection, it is considered endangered and is protected in many regions.
