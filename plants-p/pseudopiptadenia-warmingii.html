Pseudopiptadenia warmingii

Pseudopiptadenia warmingii, commonly known as the Brazilian wisteria, is a climbing legume native to South America.

It features compound leaves with elongated, lance-shaped leaflets and produces striking clusters of violet to purple flowers. The plant belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its nitrogen-fixing ability through symbiotic bacteria in its root nodules. Its rapid growth habit makes it both a valuable ornamental and a potential invasive species in certain regions.

The seeds are enclosed in elongated, pod-like fruits that split open to release the seeds.
