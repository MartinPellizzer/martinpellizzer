Polycodium macilentum

Polycodium macilentum, commonly known as the slender polycodium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to certain regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Polygonaceae, which includes plants known for their distinctive leaf shapes and often edible rhizomes. The plant features slender, erect stems covered with soft, hairy foliage that helps it retain moisture in arid environments. Its small, greenish-white flowers are arranged in clusters and bloom during the spring and early summer.

Polycodium macilentum is often found in moist, shaded areas such as woodlands and stream banks.
