Polygala clavistyla

Polygala clavistyla, commonly known as the narrow-leaved milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in sandy or rocky soils, often found in arid or semi-arid regions. The plant features slender, branching stems and small, clustered flowers that are usually pale green or yellowish in color. Its leaves are narrow and linear, contributing to its drought-resistant characteristics.

Polygala clavistyla is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and modest blooms.
