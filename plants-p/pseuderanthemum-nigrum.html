Pseuderanthemum nigrum

Pseuderanthemum nigrum, commonly known as black-eyed Susan, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its showy, yellow flowers with dark centers. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often covered in fine hairs.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
