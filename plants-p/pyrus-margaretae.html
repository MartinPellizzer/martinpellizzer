Pyrus margaretae

Pyrus margaretae, commonly known as the Margaretae pear, is a species of flowering plant in the family Rosaceae.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, particularly in countries like Kazakhstan and Uzbekistan. The plant is characterized by its deciduous leaves, which are oval-shaped with serrated edges, and its small, white flowers that bloom in early spring. The fruit, a small pear-like berry, is typically green when mature and is edible, though not widely cultivated for commercial use.

It plays an important role in local ecosystems and is of interest to botanists and horticulturists.
