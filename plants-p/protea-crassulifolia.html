Protea crassulifolia

Protea crassulifolia, commonly known as the hard-leaved protea, is a striking South African plant belonging to the Proteaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, leathery foliage and large, showy inflorescences that resemble a cluster of flowers. The plant is native to the Western Cape region and thrives in well-drained, sandy soils. It is often found in fynbos vegetation, where it contributes to the unique biodiversity of the area.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and botanical collections around the world.
