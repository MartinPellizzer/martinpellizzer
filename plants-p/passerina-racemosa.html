Passerina racemosa

Passerina racemosa, commonly known as the clustered pasqueflower, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its showy, early spring blooms. The plant typically grows in open, rocky habitats and has basal leaves that are palmately lobed. Its flowers are usually pink to purple and are clustered in racemes, giving it its scientific name.

Passerina racemosa is adapted to cold climates and is often found in alpine or subalpine regions.
