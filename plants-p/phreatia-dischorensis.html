Phreatia dischorensis

Phreatia dischorensis, commonly known as the Dischorensis orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, moist environments within lowland forests. The plant features a pseudobulb and produces showy, fragrant flowers in a range of colors including white, yellow, and orange. Its roots are adapted to absorb moisture from the surrounding soil, and it plays a role in the ecological balance of its habitat.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered vulnerable and requires conservation efforts to ensure its survival.
