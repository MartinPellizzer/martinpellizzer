Petrocoptis crassifolia subsp. guarensis

Petrocoptis crassifolia subsp.

guarensis, commonly known as the Guarense rock-cress, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Andean regions of South America. It typically grows in rocky or mountainous habitats, thriving in well-drained soils.

The plant features basal leaves that are thick and fleshy, adapted to conserve moisture in arid conditions. Its small, white or pale yellow flowers are clustered in racemes and bloom in spring. Petrocoptis crassifolia subsp.

guarensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in rock gardens.
