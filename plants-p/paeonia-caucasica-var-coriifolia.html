Paeonia caucasica var. coriifolia

Paeonia caucasica var. coriifolia, commonly known as the Caucasian peony, is a deciduous shrub native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with finely toothed margins, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces showy, single or double flowers in shades of pink, red, and white during late spring to early summer. It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it suitable for garden cultivation.

Its ornamental value and relatively hardy nature make it a sought-after addition to botanical gardens and landscaped areas.
