Phlomis megalantha var. pauciflora

Phlomis megalantha var. pauciflora, commonly known as the narrow-leaved plumevetch, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features gray-green, lance-shaped leaves with a hairy texture, and it produces tall, branched inflorescences of pale purple to pink flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or limestone habitats. Its flowering period typically occurs in late spring to early summer.

It is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
