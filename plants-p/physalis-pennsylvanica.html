Physalis pennsylvanica

Physalis pennsylvanica, commonly known as the Eastern lantern flower, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its distinctive papery calyx that encloses the fruit. The plant produces small, yellowish-green berries that turn red when mature, often referred to as "Chinese lanterns.". It typically grows in moist, open woodlands and thrives in partial shade.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
