Pieris ovalifolia var. denticulata

Pieris ovalifolia var. denticulata, commonly known as the denticulate Japanese pieris, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan.

It is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves with finely serrated edges. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped white flowers in early spring, which are followed by dark blue berries. It thrives in partial shade and well-drained, acidic soil.

This variety is valued for its ornamental foliage and seasonal blooms in garden settings.
