Phyllanthus crassifolius

Phyllanthus crassifolius, commonly known as the thick-leaved phyllanthus, is a shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific.

It is characterized by its dense, leathery leaves that are typically elliptical or ovate in shape. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, followed by small, round berries that are often red when mature. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

This species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional medicinal uses.
