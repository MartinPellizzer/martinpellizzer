Planchonella argentea

Planchonella argentea, commonly known as silver glory, is a tropical plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized tree that can grow up to 15 meters tall, characterized by its smooth, silver-gray bark. The leaves are alternate, simple, and ovate, with a glossy upper surface and a lighter underside. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by round, fleshy berries.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
