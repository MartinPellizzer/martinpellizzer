Potentilla × gilgitica

Potentilla × gilgitica, commonly known as the Gilgit cinquefoil, is a hybrid shrub native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and hairy leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees. Its adaptability to cold and arid conditions makes it a resilient species in alpine environments.

The botanical features of Potentilla × gilgitica make it an important component of the mountain flora in its native habitat.
