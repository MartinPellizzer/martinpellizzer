Philodendron placidum

Philodendron placidum, commonly known as the velvet leaf philodendron, is a tropical climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It features heart-shaped, velvety leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot. Its aerial roots allow it to climb and attach to surfaces, making it a popular choice for indoor hanging baskets or vertical gardens.

Philodendron placidum is relatively low maintenance, making it a favored houseplant for beginners.
