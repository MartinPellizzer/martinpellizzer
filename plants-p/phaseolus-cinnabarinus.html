Phaseolus cinnabarinus

Phaseolus cinnabarinus, commonly known as the scarlet runner bean, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its vibrant red flowers and edible beans. The plant produces long, cylindrical pods that contain kidney-shaped seeds, which are often used in cooking. Its leaves are trifoliate, with each leaflet being oval and serrated.

The scarlet runner bean is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it a useful crop in sustainable agriculture.
