Palafoxia linearis var. leucophylla

Palafoxia linearis var. leucophylla, commonly known as the white-leaf palafoxia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in open woods or along stream banks. The plant features slender, linear leaves that are often covered in fine white hairs, giving it its common name. Its small, clustered yellow flowers bloom in the summer, attracting various pollinators.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native plant gardens.
