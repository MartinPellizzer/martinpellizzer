Parasenecio dasythyrsus

Parasenecio dasythyrsus, commonly known as the woolly cudweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features dense, woolly hair covering its stems and leaves, giving it a distinctive appearance. It typically grows in grasslands and rocky areas, thriving in dry to semi-arid conditions.

Its flowers are small and yellow, often clustered in a head-like structure, and bloom during the warmer months.
