Pyrus sphaerocarpa

Pyrus sphaerocarpa, commonly known as the globe pear, is a deciduous tree native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its rounded, spherical fruit that is typically harvested when it reaches maturity. The plant features simple, alternate leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape, with a smooth margin. It produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in the spring, which are followed by the distinctive globose fruits.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its unique fruit characteristics.
