Portulaca erythraeae

Portulaca erythraeae, commonly known as the Ethiopian moss rose, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of Africa and the Middle East.

It produces vibrant, showy flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, often resembling small roses. The plant has fleshy, succulent leaves that help it store water, adapting to its drought-prone environment. It thrives in full sun and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Its short blooming period typically occurs in the summer months.
