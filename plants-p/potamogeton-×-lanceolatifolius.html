Potamogeton × lanceolatifolius

Potamogeton × lanceolatifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaved pondweed, is a hybrid aquatic plant that occurs naturally in freshwater environments.

It is characterized by its long, narrow leaves that are typically arranged in whorls around the stem. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are clustered in spike-like structures, and it is often found in shallow waters. Its rhizomes help it anchor in muddy substrates, allowing it to spread vegetatively.

Potamogeton × lanceolatifolius plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for various invertebrates and fish.
