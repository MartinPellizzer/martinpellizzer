Phyllocactus anguliger

Phyllocactus anguliger, commonly known as the angular phyllocactus, is a member of the Cactaceae family native to South America.

It is characterized by its unique, angular, rib-like stems that are typically green and covered in fine, white hairs. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, adding a subtle ornamental value. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light to flourish.

Due to its distinctive appearance, it is often cultivated as a decorative houseplant.
