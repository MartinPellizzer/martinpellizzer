Pyrus bulgarica

Pyrus bulgarica, commonly known as the Bulgarian pear, is a deciduous tree native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves and clusters of white flowers that bloom in spring. The tree produces small, yellow or green fruits that are typically harvested in late summer. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for cultivation in temperate climates.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and has historical significance in traditional medicine.
