Piper sambuanum

Piper sambuanum, commonly known as long pepper, is a climbing vine native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its small, peppercorn-like berries. The plant produces yellowish flowers that develop into elongated, slender fruits. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a slightly glossy surface.

The plant is often cultivated for its aromatic fruits, which are used in traditional medicine and culinary applications.
