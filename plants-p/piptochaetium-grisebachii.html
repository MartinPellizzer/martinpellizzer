Piptochaetium grisebachii

Piptochaetium grisebachii, commonly known as the grey-haired grass, is a perennial grass species native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves with a distinctive greyish hue on the underside. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It is adapted to survive in harsh environments with low rainfall and poor soil conditions.

Piptochaetium grisebachii plays a role in stabilizing soils and providing forage for livestock in its native habitats.
