Pechuelia

Pechuelia, commonly known as the Mexican sunflower, is a genus of flowering plants in the sunflower family, Asteraceae.

These plants are native to Mexico and are characterized by their vibrant, bright orange to red flowers that resemble those of the common sunflower. Pechuelia species typically have narrow, lance-shaped leaves and are often cultivated for their ornamental value in gardens. They thrive in full sun and well-drained soil, making them suitable for arid and semi-arid climates.

Some species are also used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
