Plantago media subsp. brutia

Plantago media subsp.

brutia, commonly known as the broad-leaved plantain, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to temperate regions of Europe and western Asia. It features rosettes of broad, ribbed, and sometimes hairy leaves that are often covered in a waxy cuticle.

The plant produces erect flowering stems with small, greenish-white flowers arranged in dense spikes. It is adapted to grow in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained, nutrient-rich soils.

This species is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides, fields, and waste areas.
