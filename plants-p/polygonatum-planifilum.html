Polygonatum planifilum

Polygonatum planifilum, commonly known as the flat-leaf Solomon's seal, is a perennial herb native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Asparagaceae family and is characterized by its upright, slender stems and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. The plant produces berries that mature to a red color, which are edible but often used in traditional medicine. Its rhizomes are also utilized in herbal remedies for their purported health benefits.

Polygonatum planifilum thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in woodland areas.
