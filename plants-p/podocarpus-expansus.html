Podocarpus expansus

Podocarpus expansus, commonly known as the Chinese podocarp, is a species of evergreen coniferous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Podocarpaceae family and is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and small, cone-like fruits. The tree typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in mountainous areas with high humidity. Its foliage is used in traditional medicine and as a source of timber, while its ornamental value makes it popular in horticulture.

The plant has a slow growth rate and can live for several decades, contributing to its ecological and economic significance.
