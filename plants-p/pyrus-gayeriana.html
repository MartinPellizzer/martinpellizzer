Pyrus gayeriana

Pyrus gayeriana, commonly known as the Gayer pear, is a species of flowering plant in the family Rosaceae.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, particularly in countries like Kazakhstan and Uzbekistan. The plant is characterized by its deciduous nature, with heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in early spring. Its fruit, a small pear-like berry, is edible and has a sweet, aromatic flavor.

Pyrus gayeriana is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique appearance and historical significance.
