Pavonia hastata f. cymbalaria

Pavonia hastata f. cymbalaria, commonly known as the pink sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, often with a slightly hairy texture. This plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it adaptable to arid and semi-arid environments.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for xeriscaping and native plant gardens.
