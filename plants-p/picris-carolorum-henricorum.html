Picris carolorum-henricorum

Picris carolorum-henricorum, commonly known as the lesser St. John's wort, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small clusters of flowers. The plant typically grows in grassy areas and along roadsides, reaching heights of up to 60 centimeters. Its yellow flowers bloom in summer, and it has narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

It is often used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory properties.
