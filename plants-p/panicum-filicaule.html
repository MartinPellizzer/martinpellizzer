Panicum filicaule

Panicum filicaule, commonly known as the slender panicgrass, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and panicle-shaped inflorescence. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as wetlands and disturbed soils. Its small, wind-pollinated flowers are often inconspicuous, and it produces lightweight seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Panicum filicaule is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for wildlife.
