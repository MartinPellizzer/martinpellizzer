Plantago tildeniae

Plantago tildeniae, commonly known as Tilden's plantain, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of South America.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its broad, oval leaves and dense clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky stream banks. Its seeds are dispersed by wind, and it plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
