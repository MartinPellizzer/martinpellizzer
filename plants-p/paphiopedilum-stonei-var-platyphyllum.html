Paphiopedilum stonei var. platyphyllum

Paphiopedilum stonei var. platyphyllum, commonly known as the flat-leafed lady's slipper, is a rare and striking orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers with a distinctive slipper-shaped pouch, which is a unique feature among orchids. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, often in mountainous regions with well-drained soil. Its leaves are broad and flat, contributing to its common name, and it produces fragrant blooms that attract specific pollinators.

Due to its rarity and horticultural appeal, it is highly sought after by orchid enthusiasts.
