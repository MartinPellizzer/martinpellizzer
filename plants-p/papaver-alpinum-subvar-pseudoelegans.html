Papaver alpinum subvar. pseudoelegans

Papaver alpinum subvar. pseudoelegans, commonly known as the alpine poppy, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It features showy, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and red, often with a dark central blotch. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically hairy and arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine meadows at high altitudes.

The flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
