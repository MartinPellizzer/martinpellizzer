Pueraria subspicata

Pueraria subspicata, commonly known as the climbing peanut vine, is a perennial leguminous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vigorous climbing habit and trifoliate leaves with heart-shaped leaflets. The plant produces long, cylindrical pods containing edible seeds, which are rich in protein and nutrients. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in disturbed soils or along forest edges.

Its rapid growth makes it both a valuable forage crop and a potential invasive species in certain regions.
