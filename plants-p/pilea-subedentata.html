Pilea subedentata

Pilea subedentata, commonly known as the Shingle Plant, is a species of flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and glossy, dark green leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white wax. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers that appear in clusters during the spring and summer months. It prefers bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Its unique appearance and ease of care have made it a favored ornamental plant among plant enthusiasts.
