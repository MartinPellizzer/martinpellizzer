Pseudojumellea melanosticta

Pseudojumellea melanosticta, commonly known as the black-stained pseudojumellea, is a rare and unique member of the Cactaceae family.

It is characterized by its spiny, cylindrical stems that are often covered in a dark, almost black, waxy coating. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom during the cooler months, typically in spring or early summer. Its native habitat is limited to specific regions in South America, where it thrives in arid and semi-arid conditions.
