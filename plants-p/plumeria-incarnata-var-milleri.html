Plumeria incarnata var. milleri

Plumeria incarnata var. milleri, commonly known as the miller's frangipani, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its fragrant, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and yellow. The plant produces large, paddle-shaped leaves and has a thick, aromatic bark. It thrives in warm, sunny climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The flowers are typically borne on long, slender stems and bloom during the dry season.
