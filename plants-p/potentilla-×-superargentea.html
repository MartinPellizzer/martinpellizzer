Potentilla × superargentea

Potentilla × superargentea, commonly known as silver weigela, is a hybrid shrub that exhibits striking botanical features.

It is characterized by its dense, upright growth habit and showy, cone-shaped flower clusters that bloom in early summer. The leaves are typically ovate to elliptical, with a glossy, dark green upper surface and a silvery-white underside, contributing to its ornamental appeal. The plant is native to the eastern United States and thrives in well-drained soils with full sun.

Its adaptability and aesthetic value make it a popular choice for gardens and landscaping.
