Pleurothallis scleropus

Pleurothallis scleropus, commonly known as the hard-shelled pleurothallis, is a species of orchid native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a small, epiphytic plant that thrives in humid, shaded environments within cloud forests. The plant features narrow, dark green leaves and produces delicate, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pale yellow. Its unique morphology includes a thickened, leathery stem that aids in water retention.

Due to its specialized habitat, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
