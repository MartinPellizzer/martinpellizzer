Pelargonium × lasiophyllum

Pelargonium × lasiophyllum, commonly known as the ivy-leaved geranium, is a hybrid plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink, red, or white. It is widely cultivated as a decorative plant for hanging baskets and containers due to its attractive foliage and long blooming period.

Pelargonium × lasiophyllum thrives in well-drained soil and requires moderate watering to prevent root rot.
