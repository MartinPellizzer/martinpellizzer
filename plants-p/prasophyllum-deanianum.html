Prasophyllum deanianum

Prasophyllum deanianum, commonly known as the Dean's leek orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to southern Australia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its grass-like leaves and small, greenish-white flowers. This plant typically grows in heathlands and open woodlands, thriving in well-drained soils. It is a perennial species, often flowering in spring, and is known for its delicate appearance.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered vulnerable in some regions.
