Pedicularis goniantha

Pedicularis goniantha, commonly known as the golden monkey orchid, is a striking member of the Orobanchaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of China, where it thrives in cool, moist environments. The plant is characterized by its vibrant yellow-orange flowers, which resemble the fur of a golden monkey. Its unique morphology includes spurred petals and a distinctive lip that aids in pollination by specific insects.

Pedicularis goniantha is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is a symbol of resilience in challenging alpine habitats.
