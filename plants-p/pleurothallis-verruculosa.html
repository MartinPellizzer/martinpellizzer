Pleurothallis verruculosa

Pleurothallis verruculosa, commonly known as the疣状万代兰, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and the presence of small, hairy structures on its stems, which give it its specific epithet "verruculosa.". The plant produces delicate, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, often with a slight greenish or reddish tinge. It thrives in shaded, humid environments and is often found growing epiphytically on trees in the understory of rainforests.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is a subject of interest for botanists and orchid enthusiasts alike.
