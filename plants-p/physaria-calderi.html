Physaria calderi

Physaria calderi, commonly known as the Calaveras bladderpod, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants. The plant is characterized by its compact, mat-forming growth habit and produces small, yellow flowers. Its leaves are alternate and pinnately lobed, and it thrives in dry, rocky habitats.

Physaria calderi is known for its ability to store water in its fleshy stems and leaves, adapting to arid environments.
