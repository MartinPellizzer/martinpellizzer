Pandanus callmanderiana

Pandanus callmanderiana, commonly known as the Madagascar pandanus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Pandanaceae.

It is native to Madagascar and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, sword-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences with yellowish-green flowers, which are followed by fibrous, spiky fruits. It thrives in coastal and wetland environments, often growing in saline or brackish soils.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and has traditional uses in local communities.
