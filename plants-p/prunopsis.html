Prunopsis

Prunopsis, commonly known as the false cherry or prunus-like plant, is a genus of flowering plants in the rose family, Rosaceae.

It consists of several species, most of which are shrubs or small trees characterized by their simple, alternate leaves and showy, often fragrant flowers. The flowers typically have five petals and are arranged in clusters, while the fruits are small, fleshy drupes. Prunopsis species are native to various regions in the Northern Hemisphere, including parts of Asia, Europe, and North America.

They are often cultivated for their ornamental value and have historical uses in traditional medicine.
