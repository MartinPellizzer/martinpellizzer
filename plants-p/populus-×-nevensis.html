Populus × nevensis

Populus × nevensis, commonly known as the European aspen.

It is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between white poplar (Populus alba) and black poplar (Populus nigra). This tree is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and heart-shaped leaves that turn yellow in the fall. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found near rivers and in wetlands.

Populus × nevensis plays an important role in riparian ecosystems and is valued for its rapid growth and adaptability.
