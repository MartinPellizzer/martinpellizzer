Pleurothallis recurvipetala

Pleurothallis recurvipetala, commonly known as the recurving lip orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a small epiphytic orchid that typically grows on tree branches or rocks in humid, shaded environments. The plant is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers that often have a recurved lip, which is a distinctive feature in many orchid species. Its leaves are usually narrow and arranged in a rosette pattern, and it produces multiple flower spikes during the growing season.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is a sought-after plant among orchid enthusiasts.
