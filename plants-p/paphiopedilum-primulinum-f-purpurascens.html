Paphiopedilum primulinum f. purpurascens

Paphiopedilum primulinum f. purpurascens, commonly known as the Purple-flowered Yellow Lady's Slipper, is a rare and striking orchid species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant purple petals and a distinctive yellow lip that resembles a slipper, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often on tree trunks or in rocky crevices. Its flowers are relatively small but highly ornamental, with a complex structure that includes a specialized lip for pollination.

This orchid is valued for its unique appearance and is often cultivated by enthusiasts due to its rarity and beauty.
