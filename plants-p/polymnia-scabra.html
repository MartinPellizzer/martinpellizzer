Polymnia scabra

Polymnia scabra, commonly known as the roughleaf polymnia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the family Polygonaceae, which includes plants commonly known as buckwheats. The plant is characterized by its rough, serrated leaves and small, clustered white flowers that bloom in the spring. It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as wetlands and forest edges.

Polymnia scabra is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support local wildlife.
