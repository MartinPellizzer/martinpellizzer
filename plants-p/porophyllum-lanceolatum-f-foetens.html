Porophyllum lanceolatum f. foetens

Porophyllum lanceolatum f. foetens, commonly known as the stinking pearly everlasting, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and robust, hairy stems. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in dense clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped, gray-green, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

The plant is adapted to arid environments and is often found in rocky soils and open woodlands.
