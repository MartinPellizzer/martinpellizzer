Pygeum parreauanum

Pygeum parreauanum, commonly known as the African sterculia, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Sterculiaceae and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and large, palmately lobed leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by woody pods containing oval seeds. It is often found in moist, well-drained soils in forested areas.

The tree is valued for its timber and has traditional uses in local medicine.
