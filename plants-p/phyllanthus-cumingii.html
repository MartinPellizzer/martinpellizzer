Phyllanthus cumingii

Phyllanthus cumingii, commonly known as the Indian cheesewood, is a small evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. The species is adapted to tropical and subtropical climates and thrives in well-drained soils.
