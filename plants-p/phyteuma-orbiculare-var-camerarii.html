Phyteuma orbiculare var. camerarii

Phyteuma orbiculare var. camerarii, commonly known as the Camerarii physostegia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Europe.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically produces clusters of violet-blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are lanceolate and arranged in a basal rosette, with hairy surfaces that help in moisture retention.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.
