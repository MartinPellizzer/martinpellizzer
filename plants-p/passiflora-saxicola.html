Passiflora saxicola

Passiflora saxicola, commonly known as the rock passionflower, is a species of flowering plant native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its unique, intricate flowers that feature five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments surrounding the reproductive structures. The plant typically grows on rocky outcrops and has adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments. Its leaves are usually small and leathery, and it produces edible berries that are sometimes used in traditional medicine.

Passiflora saxicola is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
