Punica granatum

Punica granatum, commonly known as the pomegranate, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the region of Persia.

It is characterized by its thick, red, spiny bark and vibrant, orange-red flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces bright red, granular fruits that contain numerous seeds surrounded by a juicy, tart aril. Pomegranates are valued for their ornamental appeal and their fruit, which is rich in antioxidants and used in culinary and medicinal applications.

The plant thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil to flourish.
