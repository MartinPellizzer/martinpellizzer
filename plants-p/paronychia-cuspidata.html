Paronychia cuspidata

Paronychia cuspidata, commonly known as the sharp-leaved sandwort, is a low-growing, mat-forming perennial plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It features small, fleshy, green leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern, adapted to conserve water in its harsh environment. The plant produces clusters of tiny, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in well-drained sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in rocky areas or along coastal dunes.

Paronychia cuspidata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in xeriscapes due to its drought tolerance.
