Pleopeltis picta

Pleopeltis picta, commonly known as the pictured pleopeltis, is a species of epiphytic fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, often colorful fronds that display intricate patterns and variations in coloration. The plant typically grows on trees or rocks in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with lance-shaped pinnae that are often adorned with spore-producing sori.

Pleopeltis picta is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various indoor growing conditions.
