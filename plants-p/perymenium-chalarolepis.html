Perymenium chalarolepis

Perymenium chalarolepis, commonly known as the golden star orchid, is a unique member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, where it thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant is characterized by its striking yellow petals and a central white or yellowish star-shaped structure, which gives it its common name. Its roots are adapted to absorb nutrients from decaying organic matter in the forest floor.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a conservation concern in its native range.
