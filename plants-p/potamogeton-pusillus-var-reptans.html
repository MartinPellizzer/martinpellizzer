Potamogeton pusillus var. reptans

Potamogeton pusillus var. reptans, commonly known as the creeping pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in shallow, slow-moving waters and is characterized by its slender, ribbon-like leaves that are often ribbon-like in shape. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually borne in clusters near the water surface. It plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.

Its rhizomes allow it to spread vegetatively, contributing to its ability to form dense underwater mats.
