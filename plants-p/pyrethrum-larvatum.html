Pyrethrum larvatum

Pyrethrum larvatum, commonly known as the African pyrethrum, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its daisy-like yellow flowers and finely divided, fern-like leaves. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its botanical name reflects its use in traditional medicine and as a source of natural insecticides.

Pyrethrum larvatum thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.
