Pinus uncinata var. castanea

Pinus uncinata var. castanea, commonly known as the Castanea pine, is a rare and endemic variant of the serotinous pine found primarily in the Pyrenees.

It is characterized by its distinctive, twisted needles and cones that remain closed until disturbed by fire or mechanical damage. The plant exhibits a unique adaptation to its harsh alpine environment, with a slow growth rate and dense foliage that helps retain heat. Its cones are typically reddish-brown and contain seeds that are dispersed by wind after the cones open.

This variant plays a crucial ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for specialized alpine flora and fauna.
