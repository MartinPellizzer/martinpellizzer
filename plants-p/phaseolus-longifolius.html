Phaseolus longifolius

Phaseolus longifolius, commonly known as the long-leaved bean, is a species native to Central and South America.

It is a climbing legume characterized by its elongated, lance-shaped leaves and small, pale green pods. The plant produces yellow flowers and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions. It is valued for its edible beans, which are rich in protein and nutrients.

Due to its adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated as a food crop in warm climates.
