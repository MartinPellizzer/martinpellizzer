Prunus maximowiczii f. rotundifolia

Prunus maximowiczii f. rotundifolia, commonly known as the round-leafed cherry, is a shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and dark green, elliptical leaves that are often slightly hairy underneath. The plant produces small, pale pink to white flowers in spring, followed by edible, sweet-tart berries. It prefers well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for gardens and landscapes.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various climates.
