Penstemon canescens

Penstemon canescens, commonly known as the gray beard scale, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems and clusters of tubular, two-lipped flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The plant has hairy, grayish-green leaves that are typically narrow and linear, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Penstemon canescens is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, supporting pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds.
