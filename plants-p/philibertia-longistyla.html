Philibertia longistyla

Philibertia longistyla, commonly known as the long-styled filbert, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single bloom. The plant features narrow, linear leaves and produces yellow to orange composite flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its unique floral structure includes a long style, which is a distinguishing feature of the species.

Philibertia longistyla is often found in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade.
