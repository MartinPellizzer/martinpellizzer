Petrosedum × elaverinum

Petrosedum × elaverinum, commonly known as the hybrid stonecrop, is a low-growing succulent plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features fleshy, upright stems covered in dense clusters of tiny, star-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant is adapted to rocky and well-drained soils, thriving in full sun to partial shade. Its foliage is typically gray-green, with some varieties displaying variegated patterns.

Petrosedum × elaverinum is often used in rock gardens and as a ground cover due to its hardiness and ornamental value.
