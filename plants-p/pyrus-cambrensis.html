Pyrus cambrensis

Pyrus cambrensis, commonly known as the Welsh pear, is a rare species of flowering plant native to the UK.

It is characterized by its small, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces edible fruits that are typically small and yellowish-green in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas.
