Paspalum malacophyllum var. linearifolium

Paspalum malacophyllum var. linearifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaved paspalum, is a grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, linear leaves and compact growth habit. The plant typically thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal dunes or disturbed areas. Its flowering stems bear small, spike-like inflorescences, and it produces small, wind-pollinated flowers.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing sandy soils.
