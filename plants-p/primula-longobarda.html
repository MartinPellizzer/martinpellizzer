Primula longobarda

Primula longobarda, commonly known as the Lombard primrose, is a species of flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of northern Italy.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky soils, thriving in cool, moist environments. Its foliage consists of broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery down.

Primula longobarda is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its early spring blooms.
