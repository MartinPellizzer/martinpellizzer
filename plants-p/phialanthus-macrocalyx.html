Phialanthus macrocalyx

Phialanthus macrocalyx, commonly known as the large-leaved phialanthus, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its broad, glossy leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces woody capsules that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and rocky habitats.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
