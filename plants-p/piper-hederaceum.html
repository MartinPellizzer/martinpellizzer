Piper hederaceum

Piper hederaceum, commonly known as the creeping Indian pepper, is a perennial vine native to tropical regions of Asia and Africa.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper plants, and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and small, fragrant flowers. The plant produces tiny berries that are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits. Its rapid growth habit makes it both a useful ground cover and a potential weed in agricultural settings.

The leaves and fruits contain various bioactive compounds, contributing to its significance in ethnobotanical practices.
