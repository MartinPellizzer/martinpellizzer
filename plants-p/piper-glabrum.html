Piper glabrum

Piper glabrum, commonly known as the smooth pepper vine, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes various species of pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its slender, twining stems and heart-shaped, glossy leaves. Its small, greenish-white flowers are followed by berries that mature to a dark purple color.

Piper glabrum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has some traditional medicinal uses.
