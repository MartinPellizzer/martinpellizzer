Potamogeton membranaceus

Potamogeton membranaceus, commonly known as the narrow-leaf pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in North America.

It has long, slender, ribbon-like leaves that are typically less than 1 millimeter thick, allowing it to float or rest on the bottom of water bodies. The plant produces small, green flowers that are arranged in spike-like clusters, and it reproduces both sexually and asexually through rhizomes. It plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.

Its presence is often an indicator of good water quality in ponds and lakes.
