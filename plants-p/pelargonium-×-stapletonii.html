Pelargonium × stapletonii

Pelargonium × stapletonii, commonly known as the Cape vermeil.

It is a hybrid geranium species native to South Africa, characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and vibrant, showy flowers. The plant produces clusters of small, five-petaled flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, which bloom throughout the growing season. Its leaves are typically rounded with a slightly hairy texture, and the plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and containers.

Pelargonium × stapletonii is drought-tolerant and prefers well-drained soil, making it a versatile choice for various climates.
