Polypodiodes amoena

Polypodiodes amoena, commonly known as the delicate fern, is a member of the Polypodiaceae family.

It is a small, epiphytic fern that typically grows on trees in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant features delicate, finely divided fronds that are often covered in a thin layer of moss-like tissue. Its rhizomes are short and branched, allowing it to anchor itself to its host.

The spore-producing structures, called sori, are located on the underside of the fronds, contributing to its reproductive strategy.
