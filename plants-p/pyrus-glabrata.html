Pyrus glabrata

Pyrus glabrata, commonly known as the smooth pear.

It is a species of flowering plant in the family Rosaceae, native to the eastern United States. The plant is characterized by its smooth, often reddish bark and clusters of small, white flowers that appear in early spring. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a serrated margin.

The fruit is a small, edible pear that ripens in late summer, providing food for various wildlife species.
