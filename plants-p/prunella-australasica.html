Prunella australasica

Prunella australasica, commonly known as the Australasian dead-nettle, is a flowering plant native to Australia and New Zealand.

It is a low-growing perennial herb that typically reaches a height of about 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features opposite, ovate to lance-shaped leaves with hairy surfaces, and it produces small, pale blue to purple flowers in whorled clusters. Prunella australasica is characterized by its glandular hairs and its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types, including sandy and rocky soils.

It is often found in open habitats such as roadsides, grasslands, and disturbed areas.
