Prunus × donarium f. ariake

Prunus × donarium f. ariake, commonly known as the Ariake cherry, is a botanical hybrid native to Japan.

It is a compact shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of 3–5 meters. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in early spring. Its fruits are small, sweet cherries that ripen in late summer.

The Ariake cherry is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic and edible qualities.
