Philadelphus uniflorus

Philadelphus uniflorus, commonly known as the single-flower mock orange, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white, solitary flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are dark green and glossy on top, with a lighter underside. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained, fertile soil.

Philadelphus uniflorus is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and its ability to attract pollinators.
