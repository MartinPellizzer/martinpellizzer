Polygala leptalea var. australiensis

Polygala leptalea var. australiensis, commonly known as the golden mistflower, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its delicate, feathery inflorescences that resemble mist or smoke. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in coastal or inland areas. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in dense, cylindrical spikes.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
