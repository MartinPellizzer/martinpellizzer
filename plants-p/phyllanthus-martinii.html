Phyllanthus martinii

Phyllanthus martinii, commonly known as the African star apple, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Africa and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, rounded canopy and small, glossy, dark green leaves. The plant produces small, white or yellowish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its fruit is a round, fleshy berry that turns from green to yellow or red when mature.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various health benefits.
