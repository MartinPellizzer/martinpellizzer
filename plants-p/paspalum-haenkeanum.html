Paspalum haenkeanum

Paspalum haenkeanum, commonly known as silvergrass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-gray foliage and compact growth habit. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks. Its inflorescence consists of dense, spike-like clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers.

Paspalum haenkeanum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soil in aquatic environments.
