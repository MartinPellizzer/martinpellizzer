Physocarpus ramaleyi

Physocarpus ramaleyi, commonly known as the red osier dogwood, is a deciduous shrub native to North America.

It is characterized by its dense, upright growth habit and its showy clusters of white flowers that appear in late spring. The plant produces small, dark blue berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. Its stems are notable for their bright red color, which becomes more pronounced in the winter months.

Physocarpus ramaleyi is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and its ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
