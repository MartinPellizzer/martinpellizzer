Pteropepon deltoideus var. peruvianus

Pteropepon deltoideus var. peruvianus, commonly known as the Peruvian delta cucumber, is a variant of the delta cucumber native to South America.

It is a member of the Cucurbitaceae family, characterized by its trailing growth habit and lobed leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that give way to elongated, cucurbit-like fruits. Its botanical features include a hairy stem and a distinct, angular shape in its mature fruit.

This variety is valued for its unique flavor and is often cultivated for both culinary and ornamental purposes.
