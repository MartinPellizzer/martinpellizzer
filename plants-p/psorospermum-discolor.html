Psorospermum discolor

Psorospermum discolor, commonly known as the yellow flame tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are opposite and consist of several elliptical leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by distinctive, woody, spiny fruits. It thrives in humid climates and is often found in secondary forests or along riverbanks.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in tropical regions.
