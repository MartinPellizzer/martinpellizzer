Pterochilus plantagineus

Pterochilus plantagineus, commonly known as the red sandalwood, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 3 meters tall, characterized by its aromatic, reddish-brown wood. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and have a rough texture with a distinctive aromatic scent.

The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in harsh climates.
