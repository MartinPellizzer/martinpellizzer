Pandanus novohibernicus var. praeacutus

Pandanus novohibernicus var. praeacutus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved pandanus, is a species native to the tropical regions of the Pacific.

It is characterized by its narrow, elongated leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, often followed by edible, spiky fruits. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its use in traditional crafts and cuisine.
