Phlox roemeriana var. elata

Phlox roemeriana var. elata, commonly known as the tall phlox, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its erect, branching stems that can grow up to 2 meters in height. The plant produces showy clusters of pink to purple flowers in late spring to early summer. Its foliage consists of lance-shaped, dark green leaves that are arranged oppositely along the stems.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in partial to full sun.
