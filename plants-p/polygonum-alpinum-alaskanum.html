Polygonum alpinum alaskanum

Polygonum alpinum alaskanum, commonly known as the Alaskan knotweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the alpine regions of Alaska and northern Canada.

It typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils at high elevations, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. The plant has slender, erect stems that are often reddish in color, and it produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters during the summer months. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in its harsh environment.

Polygonum alpinum alaskanum is valued for its hardiness and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its ornamental appeal.
