Physurus hyphaematicus

Physurus hyphaematicus, commonly known as the bloodroot fern, is a rare and enigmatic plant species characterized by its striking crimson roots and delicate, lace-like fronds.

It thrives in shaded, moist environments, typically found in temperate rainforests and mountainous regions. The plant's unique vascular system allows it to absorb nutrients efficiently from the soil, contributing to its survival in nutrient-poor habitats. Its roots contain compounds that have been studied for potential medicinal properties.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a conservation concern.
