Philoxerus conicus

Philoxerus conicus, commonly known as the cone shell, is a species of sea snail belonging to the family Fasciolariidae.

It is characterized by its elongated, conical shell that is often brightly colored with intricate patterns. The shell is typically spiral and has a smooth, glossy surface. This marine gastropod is found in tropical and subtropical waters around the world.

Its botanical features are primarily reflected in its shell morphology, which is both aesthetically striking and functionally adapted to its marine environment.
