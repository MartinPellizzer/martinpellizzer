Pelargonium × broughtoniae

Pelargonium × broughtoniae, commonly known as the Broughtonia geranium, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Geraniaceae family.

It is native to South Africa and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and vibrant, showy flowers. The plant produces clusters of small, five-petaled blooms in shades of pink, red, and white, typically during the warm seasons. Its foliage consists of rounded, velvety leaves that are often variegated with silver or white markings.

Pelargonium × broughtoniae is popular in horticulture for its ornamental value and adaptability to container gardening.
