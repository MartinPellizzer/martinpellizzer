Phoradendron duidanum var. hymenaeifolium

Phoradendron duidanum var. hymenaeifolium, commonly known as the silverleaf mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on a variety of host trees.

It has small, leathery, elliptical leaves that are typically covered in a dense layer of silvery-white wax, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, white or pale green flowers that are usually clustered in dense inflorescences. Its berries are typically red or yellow and contain seeds that are dispersed by birds.

This species is native to parts of Central and South America and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.
