Paxistima myrtifolia

Paxistima myrtifolia, commonly known as the Christmas cactus, is a succulent plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and vibrant, showy flowers that bloom in winter. The plant has a unique branching pattern with jointed stems that can be easily propagated through stem cuttings. Its leaves are typically opposite and oval-shaped, with a waxy texture that helps reduce water loss.

Paxistima myrtifolia thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.
