Potentilla wimanniana var. johanniniana

Potentilla wimanniana var. johanniniana, commonly known as the Johanninian cinquefoil, is a low-growing perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves that are typically covered in a soft, silvery pubescence. It thrives in well-drained, sandy or gravelly soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in rock gardens or alpine settings.
