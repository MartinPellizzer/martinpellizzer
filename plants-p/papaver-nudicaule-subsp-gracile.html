Papaver nudicaule subsp. gracile

Papaver nudicaule subsp.

gracile, commonly known as the slender poppy, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It features delicate, nodding flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, often with a slight bluish tinge.

The plant has a short, hairy stem and produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This subspecies is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and rockeries.
