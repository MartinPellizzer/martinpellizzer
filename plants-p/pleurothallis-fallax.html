Pleurothallis fallax

Pleurothallis fallax, commonly known as the false orchid, is a species of orchid native to the cloud forests of Central and South America.

It is a small, epiphytic plant that typically grows on trees or rocks in humid, shaded environments. The plant features delicate, narrow leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually white or pale green. It belongs to the orchid family (Orchidaceae) and is part of the genus Pleurothallis, which includes over 1,000 species.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts for its unique botanical characteristics.
