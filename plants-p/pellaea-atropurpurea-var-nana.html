Pellaea atropurpurea var. nana

Pellaea atropurpurea var. nana, commonly known as the dwarf purple fern, is a compact, low-growing fern native to the southeastern United States.

It features delicate, lace-like fronds that are typically less than 10 inches tall, with a dark purple hue on the underside. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and has a rhizomatous growth habit. Its leaves are bipinnate, with numerous small, rounded pinnae that give it a soft, feathery appearance.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in woodland gardens.
