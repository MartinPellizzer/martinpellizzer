Phaius tonkinensis

Phaius tonkinensis, commonly known as the Tonkin orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often in forest understories. Its leaves are broad and grass-like, and it produces large, fragrant inflorescences.

Phaius tonkinensis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
