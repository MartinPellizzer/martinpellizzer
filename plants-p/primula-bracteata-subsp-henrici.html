Primula bracteata subsp. henrici

Primula bracteata subsp.

henrici, commonly known as the Henrici's primrose, is a species native to the mountainous regions of China. It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in cool, moist environments such as forest understories or rocky slopes.

The plant features basal leaves that are oval to lance-shaped with a hairy surface, and it produces clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, or purple. Its blooming period occurs in spring, and it is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.

The subspecies is characterized by its distinct bracts and compact growth habit, making it a unique addition to the Primula genus.
