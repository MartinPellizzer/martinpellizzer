Psidium obovatum

Psidium obovatum, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or obovate guava, is a small evergreen shrub or tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its oval, leathery leaves that are often pubescent on the underside. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that give way to edible, red or yellow berries. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is valued for its ornamental appeal and fruit production.

The fruit is rich in vitamin C and is used in culinary and medicinal applications.
