Phanera excurrens

Phanera excurrens, commonly known as the creeping phanera, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in moist, shaded environments. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its small, fragrant flowers are usually white or pale yellow and are clustered in terminal inflorescences.

Phanera excurrens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
