Potentilla nivea var. subviridis

Potentilla nivea var. subviridis, commonly known as the white cinquefoil, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its finely divided, silver-green leaves that reflect light, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
