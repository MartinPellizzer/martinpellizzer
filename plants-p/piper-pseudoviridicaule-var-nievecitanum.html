Piper pseudoviridicaule var. nievecitanum

Piper pseudoviridicaule var. nievecitanum, commonly known as the Andean pepper plant, is a species native to the cloud forests of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, climbing habit and heart-shaped leaves with a glossy, dark green surface. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into elongated, spiny fruits. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory properties.

Botanically, it belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species known for their aromatic and medicinal uses.
