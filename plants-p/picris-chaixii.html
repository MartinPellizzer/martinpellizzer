Picris chaixii

Picris chaixii, commonly known as the Persian groundsel, is a flowering plant native to regions of the Middle East and Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and has narrow, linear leaves with a rough texture. Its yellow flowers bloom in the summer, producing small seeds that are dispersed by wind.

It is valued in traditional medicine for its potential anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties.
