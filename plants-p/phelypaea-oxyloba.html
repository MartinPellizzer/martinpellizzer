Phelypaea oxyloba

Phelypaea oxyloba, commonly known as the narrow-leaf water clover, is a species of aquatic plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its delicate, floating leaves that are typically divided into small, lobed segments. The plant thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, often found in ponds and marshes. Its roots are adapted for anchoring in soft substrates, while its leaves aid in photosynthesis at the water's surface.

Phelypaea oxyloba plays a role in water filtration and provides habitat for small aquatic organisms.
