Potamogeton foliosus var. genuinus

Potamogeton foliosus var. genuinus, commonly known as the narrow-leaf pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater ecosystems in North America.

It has long, slender leaves that are typically arranged in whorls around the stem, and it produces small, green flowers that are clustered in spike-like structures. The plant thrives in slow-moving or still waters, often found in lakes, ponds, and wetlands. Its rhizomes allow it to spread vegetatively, contributing to the formation of dense underwater vegetation.

It plays an important role in providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
