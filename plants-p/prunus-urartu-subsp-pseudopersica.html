Prunus urartu subsp. pseudopersica

Prunus urartu subsp.

pseudopersica, commonly known as the Caucasian cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Caucasus region. It features lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy on the underside, and it produces small, white or pinkish flowers in early spring.

The plant bears edible, tart berries that are typically consumed fresh or used in jams and preserves. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or mountainous environments.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
