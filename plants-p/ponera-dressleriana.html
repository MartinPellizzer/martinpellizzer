Ponera dressleriana

Ponera dressleriana, commonly known as the Dressler's ponera, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful flowers that bloom in clusters, often in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, such as rainforests, where it thrives on the forest floor or as an epiphyte on trees. Its leaves are usually broad and fleshy, adapted to store water in its native habitat.

The orchid is prized by horticulturists for its ornamental value and unique floral structure.
