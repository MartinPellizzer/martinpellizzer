Pediomelum subacaule

Pediomelum subacaule, commonly known as the slender pediomelum, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and small, daisy-like flowers. The plant typically has hairy stems and leaves that are linear to lanceolate in shape. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it is not widely distributed outside its native range.
