Pseudopanax crassifolius var. coriaceus

Pseudopanax crassifolius var. coriaceus, commonly known as the silver pine, is a unique evergreen tree native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its distinctive, scale-like leaves that are thick and leathery, giving it a silvery appearance. The plant has a slow growth rate and can live for several centuries, making it a long-lived species. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by winged seeds that are dispersed by the wind.

The botanical features of this plant make it an important component of New Zealand's native forest ecosystems.
