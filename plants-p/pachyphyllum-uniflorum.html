Pachyphyllum uniflorum

Pachyphyllum uniflorum, commonly known as the single-flower thickleaf cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems that store water, enabling it to survive in dry environments. The plant produces a single, showy yellow flower at the top of its stem, typically during the warm season. Its leaves are reduced to spines, which help reduce water loss and deter herbivores.

Pachyphyllum uniflorum is often cultivated for its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
