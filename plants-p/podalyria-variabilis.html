Podalyria variabilis

Podalyria variabilis, commonly known as the variable podalyria, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly found in regions of Argentina and Chile.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect, branched stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in the spring. Podalyria variabilis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to well-drained soils in full sun.
