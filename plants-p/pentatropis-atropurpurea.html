Pentatropis atropurpurea

Pentatropis atropurpurea, commonly known as the purpleleaf pentatropis, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its opposite, ovate to elliptical leaves that turn a striking purple or reddish hue in the fall. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers in panicles during late spring to early summer. Its bark is typically smooth and grayish-brown, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

It is often found in woodland edges and along stream banks, contributing to the ecological diversity of its habitat.
