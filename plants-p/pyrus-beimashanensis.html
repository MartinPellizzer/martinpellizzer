Pyrus beimashanensis

Pyrus beimashanensis, commonly known as the Beimashan pear, is a species of flowering plant in the family Rosaceae.

It is native to China and is characterized by its deciduous nature, with simple, alternate leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in the spring, followed by edible fruits that are typically pear-shaped and have a sweet, aromatic flavor. It is often found in mountainous regions and is valued for both its ornamental and culinary uses.

The species is relatively rare and is of interest to botanists due to its unique ecological and genetic traits.
