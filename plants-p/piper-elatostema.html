Piper elatostema

Piper elatostema, commonly known as the small-leaved pepper plant, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil and Peru. The plant is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves and clusters of tiny, greenish-white flowers. It produces small, round berries that are typically green when immature and turn red when ripe.

Piper elatostema is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
