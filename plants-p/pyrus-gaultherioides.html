Pyrus gaultherioides

Pyrus gaultherioides, commonly known as the alpine hawthorn, is a small deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its clusters of white, fragrant flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by small, edible berries. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery pubescence. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, alpine environments.

Pyrus gaultherioides is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
