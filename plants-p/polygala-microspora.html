Polygala microspora

Polygala microspora, commonly known as the small-spored milkvetch, is a flowering plant species belonging to the family Polygalaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, clustered flowers that typically range in color from white to pale yellow. The plant produces small, leathery fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds. Native to arid and semi-arid regions, it is adapted to survive in dry conditions with minimal water requirements.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural study.
