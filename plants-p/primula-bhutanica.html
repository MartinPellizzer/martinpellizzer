Primula bhutanica

Primula bhutanica, commonly known as the Bhutan primrose, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayan region, particularly Bhutan and parts of India.

It is characterized by its delicate, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and mountain meadows. It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological significance.

Its foliage consists of broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery down.
