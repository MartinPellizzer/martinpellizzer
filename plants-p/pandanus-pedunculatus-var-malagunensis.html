Pandanus pedunculatus var. malagunensis

Pandanus pedunculatus var. malagunensis, commonly known as the Malagasy screw pine, is a tropical plant native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its robust, fibrous roots and its distinctive, spirally arranged leaves that are narrow and strap-like. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences with yellowish flowers, which are followed by woody, spiky fruits. It thrives in coastal and sandy soils, often growing near the shoreline.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appearance and traditional uses in local crafts and construction.
