Pennisetum lechleri

Pennisetum lechleri, commonly known as the red grass or blood grass, is a perennial grass native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, reddish-pink inflorescences that bloom in summer. The plant grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun. Its foliage is narrow and grass-like, with a distinctive coloration that changes throughout the growing season.

Pennisetum lechleri is often cultivated for ornamental purposes in gardens and landscapes.
