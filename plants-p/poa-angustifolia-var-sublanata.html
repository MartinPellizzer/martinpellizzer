Poa angustifolia var. sublanata

Poa angustifolia var. sublanata, commonly known as the narrowleaf bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features slender, grass-like leaves that are typically narrow and slightly hairy, with a bluish-green hue. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny florets. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.

This variety is noted for its fine texture and is sometimes cultivated as a ornamental grass in gardens.
