Phillyrea cordifolia subsp. sanmiguelis

Phillyrea cordifolia subsp.

sanmiguelis, commonly known as San Miguel Phillyrea, is a shrub native to the Iberian Peninsula. It features small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and have a leathery texture.

The plant produces clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers during the spring and summer months. These flowers are followed by small, woody fruits that contain one or two seeds.

It is well adapted to dry, rocky soils and is often found in Mediterranean climates.
