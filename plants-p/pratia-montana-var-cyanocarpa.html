Pratia montana var. cyanocarpa

Pratia montana var. cyanocarpa, commonly known as the blue-fruited pratia, is a species native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial herb that typically thrives in moist, shaded environments. The plant is characterized by its simple, lance-shaped leaves and small, white to pale pink flowers. Its most distinctive feature is the blue-black, fleshy berries that develop in clusters, which are often used in traditional medicine.

The plant's botanical significance lies in its unique fruit coloration and potential medicinal properties.
