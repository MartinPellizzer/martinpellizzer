Pogostemon paludosus

Pogostemon paludosus, commonly known as swamp basil, is a perennial herb native to wetland environments in Southeast Asia.

It features square, hairy stems and opposite, ovate leaves with a distinct aromatic scent. The plant produces small, white or pale blue flowers in whorled clusters, typically blooming in summer. Its roots are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory properties.

Pogostemon paludosus thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils and is often found in marshes and along riverbanks.
