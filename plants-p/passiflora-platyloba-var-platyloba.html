Passiflora platyloba var. platyloba

Passiflora platyloba var. platyloba, commonly known as the flat-lobed passionflower, is a tropical climbing vine native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, intricate flowers that display a blend of purple, blue, and white hues, with prominent filaments and a crown of stamens. The plant produces edible, orange-yellow berries that are rich in nutrients and often consumed by wildlife. Its leaves are typically large, heart-shaped, and have a velvety texture.

This species thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
