Polemonium racemosum

Polemonium racemosum, commonly known as the clustered bluebead, is a herbaceous perennial native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the family Polemoniaceae and is characterized by its showy, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and has opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves. Its flowers are clustered in racemes, giving it its species name, and it is often found in shaded to partially shaded environments.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
