Pimenta samanensis

Pimenta samanensis, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a species native to the Caribbean and parts of South America.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree that can grow up to 10 meters in height. The plant has glossy, dark green leaves and produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a bright red drupe, which is often used in culinary and medicinal applications.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental qualities and ability to thrive in coastal and tropical environments.
