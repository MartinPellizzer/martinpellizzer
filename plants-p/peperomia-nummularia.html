Peperomia nummularia

Peperomia nummularia, commonly known as the "coin plant," is a compact, evergreen shrub native to Central and South America.

It features thick, waxy, round leaves that resemble coins, giving it its common name. The plant has a shallow root system and thrives in well-draining soil, making it ideal for indoor cultivation. It prefers bright, indirect light and moderate humidity, with minimal watering to avoid root rot.

Its compact growth habit and attractive foliage make it a popular choice for tabletop displays and small spaces.
