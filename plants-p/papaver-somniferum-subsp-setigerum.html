Papaver somniferum subsp. setigerum

Papaver somniferum subsp.

setigerum, commonly known as the Syrian poppy, is a herbaceous annual plant native to the eastern Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its striking red or orange petals and a distinctive, elongated seed capsule.

The plant produces a milky latex sap, which contains alkaloids such as morphine and codeine. Its flowers typically bloom in late spring to early summer, and it thrives in well-drained soils.

This subspecies is notable for its ornamental value and historical significance in the cultivation of opium.
