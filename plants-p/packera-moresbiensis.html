Packera moresbiensis

Packera moresbiensis, commonly known as Moresby's brittlestem, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of New Zealand.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine habitats, thriving in cool, moist environments. Its leaves are narrow and often covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are small and yellow, arranged in a cluster.

Packera moresbiensis is valued for its ecological role in supporting local pollinators and its resilience to harsh environmental conditions.
