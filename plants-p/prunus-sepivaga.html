Prunus sepivaga

Prunus sepivaga, commonly known as the Japanese maple, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, ornamental foliage that displays vibrant colors during the growing season and in autumn. The plant has alternate, simple, and often serrated leaves that are typically reddish or green in color. It produces small, clustered flowers in spring, followed by edible fruits that are similar to cherries.

Prunus sepivaga thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
