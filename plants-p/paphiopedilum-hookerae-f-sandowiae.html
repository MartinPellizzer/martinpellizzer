Paphiopedilum hookerae f. sandowiae

Paphiopedilum hookerae f. sandowiae, commonly known as the Sandow's lady's slipper, is a rare and unique orchid species native to the mountainous regions of Borneo.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, pouch-like lip that resembles a slipper, a feature typical of the Paphiopedilum genus. The plant produces striking flowers with a mix of white, pink, and purple hues, often with a contrasting green or yellow throat. It thrives in shaded, humid environments and requires well-draining, acidic soil for optimal growth.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a conservation concern and is often cultivated in specialized horticultural settings.
