Paspalum exaltatum

Paspalum exaltatum, commonly known as tall paspalum, is a perennial grass native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in wet or disturbed areas.

Paspalum exaltatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping.
