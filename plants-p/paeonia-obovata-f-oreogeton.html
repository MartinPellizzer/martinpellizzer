Paeonia obovata f. oreogeton

Paeonia obovata f. oreogeton, commonly known as the Chinese peony or the double-flowered peony, is a botanical variant of the peony species native to China.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink, red, and white, with a double petal structure. The plant features broad, heart-shaped leaves that are dark green and glossy, providing a striking contrast to its vibrant blossoms. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated for its floral display in horticultural settings.
