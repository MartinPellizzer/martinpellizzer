Protium medianum

Protium medianum, commonly known as the Philippine sandalwood, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae, which includes other aromatic plants like cinnamon and avocado. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptic or ovate in shape. It produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by small, round drupes.

The tree is valued for its fragrant wood, which is used in traditional crafts and as a source of essential oils.
