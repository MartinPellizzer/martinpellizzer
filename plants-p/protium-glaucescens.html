Protium glaucescens

Protium glaucescens, commonly known as the green ash or white ash, is a flowering plant species native to tropical regions of Asia and Africa.

It is a small to medium-sized tree that typically grows up to 15 meters tall, with a straight trunk and smooth, pale green bark. The leaves are alternate, simple, and ovate, often with a glossy surface and a waxy texture. Its flowers are small and clustered in catkins, and it produces small, spherical fruits that are dispersed by wind.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
