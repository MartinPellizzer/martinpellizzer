Piper peltaphyllum

Piper peltaphyllum, commonly known as the broad-leafed pepper plant, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes various species of pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. It produces small, peppery fruits that are used in traditional medicine and local cuisines.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and ethnobotanical studies.
