Paneion pratense

Paneion pratense, commonly known as the meadow bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue or violet flowers that bloom in summer. Paneion pratense is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
