Potamogeton × decipiens var. salicifolius

Potamogeton × decipiens var. salicifolius, commonly known as the willow-leaved pondweed, is a hybrid aquatic plant characterized by its elongated, willow-like leaves.

It typically grows in shallow, freshwater habitats such as lakes, ponds, and slow-moving streams. The plant has a submerged stem with whorled leaves that are usually narrow and pointed. It produces small, greenish flowers that are clustered in spike-like inflorescences.

This variety is often found in temperate regions and plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
