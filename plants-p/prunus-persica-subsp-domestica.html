Prunus persica subsp. domestica

Prunus persica subsp.

domestica, commonly known as the domestic peach, is a deciduous tree native to southwest Asia. It produces showy pink or white flowers in early spring, followed by fuzzy, sweet fruits that ripen in late summer.

The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

The fruit is widely cultivated for its edible flesh and is used in various culinary and commercial applications.
