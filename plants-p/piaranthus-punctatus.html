Piaranthus punctatus

Piaranthus punctatus, commonly known as the African starwort, is a small, herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its delicate, star-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of white and pink, often with a distinctive purple or red throat. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and thrives in full sun, making it a hardy species adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it is often found in coastal or inland regions with well-drained soil.

Piaranthus punctatus is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
