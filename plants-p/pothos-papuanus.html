Pothos papuanus

Pothos papuanus, commonly known as the Papuan devil's ivy, is a tropical vine native to New Guinea.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped, waxy leaves that are typically dark green with a glossy texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by red berries, which are an important food source for local wildlife. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often used in traditional medicine by indigenous communities.

Pothos papuanus is a hardy plant that can adapt to various growing conditions, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.
