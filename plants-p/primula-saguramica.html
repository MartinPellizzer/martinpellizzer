Primula saguramica

Primula saguramica, commonly known as the Sagurama primrose, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its showy, clustered flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink and white. The plant features oval, dark green leaves with a slightly hairy texture, and it thrives in cool, moist environments. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a decorative plant in pots.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native range.
