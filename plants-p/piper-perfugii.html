Piper perfugii

Piper perfugii, commonly known as the Christmas pepper or the Christmas tree pepper, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its small, ornamental berries that resemble tiny Christmas ornaments. The plant produces clusters of bright red fruits that are often used in festive decorations due to their visual appeal. Botanically, it has opposite, ovate leaves and a climbing or shrub-like growth habit.

The plant is also valued for its potential medicinal properties, though its primary use remains ornamental.
