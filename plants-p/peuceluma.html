Peuceluma

Peuceluma, commonly known as the Mexican moonflower, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and Central America.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its large, white, fragrant flowers that open at night. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 10 feet. Its leaves are ovate and glossy, while its fruit is a fleshy, orange-red berry.

Peuceluma is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
