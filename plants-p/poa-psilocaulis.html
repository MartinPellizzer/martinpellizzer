Poa psilocaulis

Poa psilocaulis, commonly known as the ghost grass, is a species of grass native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its slender, pale green blades that often appear translucent, giving it a ghostly appearance. The plant typically grows in damp, shaded environments such as forest floors and rocky areas. It has a fibrous root system and produces small, inconspicuous flowers.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
