Polygonum fertile

Polygonum fertile, commonly known as redroot pigweed, is a flowering plant in the family Polygonaceae.

It is characterized by its upright, branched stems and heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive reddish tint. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. It is native to North America and is often found in disturbed soils such as roadsides and fields.

Polygonum fertile is known for its adaptability and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
