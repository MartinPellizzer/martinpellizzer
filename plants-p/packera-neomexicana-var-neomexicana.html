Packera neomexicana var. neomexicana

Packera neomexicana var. neomexicana, commonly known as the New Mexico leopard's ear, is a herbaceous perennial native to the western United States.

It features heart-shaped, velvety leaves that are covered in fine, white hairs, giving it a distinctive, fuzzy appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural study.
