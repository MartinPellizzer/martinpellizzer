Primula kichanensis

Primula kichanensis, commonly known as the Kichan primrose, is a rare and delicate flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of northern Japan.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often in forest understories or rocky areas. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in a fine, fuzzy texture, contributing to its unique appearance.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
