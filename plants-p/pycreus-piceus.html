Pycreus piceus

Pycreus piceus, commonly known as the black spruce, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to North America.

It is characterized by its dark, fissured bark and needle-like leaves that are typically dark green and slightly twisted. The tree produces small, cone-like structures that contain its seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Pycreus piceus thrives in cold, boreal climates and is often found in swampy or poorly drained soils.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat for various wildlife species and playing a role in carbon sequestration.
