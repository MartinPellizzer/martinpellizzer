Panicum columbianum var. siccanum

Panicum columbianum var. siccanum, commonly known as the Colombian panicgrass, is a perennial grass native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny florets, typical of the grass family. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it adaptable to various environments.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
