Phaseolus sempervirens

Phaseolus sempervirens, commonly known as the evergreen bean or green bean vine, is a climbing legume native to the Americas.

It produces small, green, pod-like fruits that contain edible seeds, often used in traditional cuisines. The plant has compound leaves with trifoliate or pinnate structures, and its flowers are typically white or pale pink. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its nutritional value.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal due to its lush foliage and persistent green color.
