Pseudonephelium confine

Pseudonephelium confine, commonly known as the sweet-scented wild fig, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small evergreen shrub that typically grows to a height of about 2 to 3 meters. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by edible, pear-shaped fruits. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a leathery texture with a distinctive aroma.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its fragrant foliage and fruit.
