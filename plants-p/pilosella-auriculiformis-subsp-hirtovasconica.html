Pilosella auriculiformis subsp. hirtovasconica

Pilosella auriculiformis subsp.

hirtovasconica, commonly known as the hairy leaf cat's ear, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe. It is characterized by its small, daisy-like flowers with white petals and yellow centers, typically blooming in early summer.

The subspecies is distinguished by its hairy leaves and stems, which give it a distinctive textured appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

This plant is also noted for its ability to tolerate dry conditions, making it a resilient species in its native environment.
