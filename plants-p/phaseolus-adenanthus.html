Phaseolus adenanthus

Phaseolus adenanthus, commonly known as the desert bean or desert phaseolus, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a shrub-like species characterized by its woody stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a legume pod containing several small, edible seeds.

Phaseolus adenanthus is adapted to arid environments and has limited agricultural use compared to other beans in the genus.
