Prunus tomentosa var. heteromera

Prunus tomentosa var. heteromera, commonly known as the woolly cherry or Chinese sand cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It is characterized by its dense, grayish-white pubescent bark and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in early spring. The leaves are ovate to elliptic, with a hairy underside, and the plant produces small, yellowish-red berries in late summer. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional Chinese medicine.

The plant prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it adaptable to a variety of garden settings.
