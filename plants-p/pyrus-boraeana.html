Pyrus boraeana

Pyrus boraeana, commonly known as the Arctic pear, is a species of flowering plant native to the Arctic regions of Europe and Asia.

It is a small, deciduous tree or shrub that typically grows in rocky or mountainous terrain. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by edible, sweet-tart berries. Its leaves are narrow and elliptical, with a slightly serrated edge.

Pyrus boraeana is adapted to cold climates and is considered a hardy, resilient species.
