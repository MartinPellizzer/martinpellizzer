Pterocarpus thyrsiflorus

Pterocarpus thyrsiflorus, commonly known as the African rosewood, is a tropical tree native to regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, dark-colored wood, which is highly valued for its beauty and durability. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, clustered yellow flowers. Its fruit is a woody pod that splits open to release flat, winged seeds.

The tree is often found in moist, well-drained soils and can grow up to 30 meters tall.
