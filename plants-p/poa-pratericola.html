Poa pratericola

Poa pratericola, commonly known as the meadow grass or prater grass, is a species of grass native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial plant that typically forms dense mats in meadows and open woodlands. The plant has slender, cylindrical stems and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers. Its leaves are narrow and often have a bluish-green hue, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Poa pratericola is valued for its ability to stabilize soil and support biodiversity in grassland ecosystems.
