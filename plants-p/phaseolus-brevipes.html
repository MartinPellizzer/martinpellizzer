Phaseolus brevipes

Phaseolus brevipes, commonly known as the short-stemmed bean, is a species native to Central and South America.

It is a climbing legume with trifoliate leaves and small, yellow flowers. The plant produces small, edible pods that contain kidney-shaped seeds. It is adapted to a variety of soil types and thrives in warm, tropical climates.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and less commonly cultivated species.
