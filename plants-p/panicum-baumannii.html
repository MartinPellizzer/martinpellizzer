Panicum baumannii

Panicum baumannii, commonly known as dwarf panicgrass, is a species of grass native to the Americas.

It is a low-growing perennial plant characterized by its slender, spreading stems and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically thrives in disturbed soils and can be found in open areas such as roadsides and fields. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue, and it produces wind-pollinated flowers.

Panicum baumannii is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support local wildlife.
