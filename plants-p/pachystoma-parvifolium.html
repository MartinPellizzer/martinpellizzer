Pachystoma parvifolium

Pachystoma parvifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaved stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its fleshy, thick leaves that store water, enabling it to survive in dry conditions. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow or white, typically blooming in late spring or early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its hardy nature, it is often used in landscaping to add texture and color to drought-tolerant gardens.
