Peperomia quitensis

Peperomia quitensis, commonly known as the Andean peperomia, is a perennial evergreen shrub native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that are often glossy and variegated. The plant typically grows in rocky or forested areas, preferring well-drained soil and partial shade. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are usually green or reddish and appear in clusters.

Peperomia quitensis is valued for its ornamental foliage and is relatively easy to care for, making it a popular houseplant.
