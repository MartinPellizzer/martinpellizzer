Poa celsa

Poa celsa, commonly known as the slender meadow grass, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows, grasslands, and wetlands. The plant has slender, upright stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its inflorescence consists of a spike-like cluster of small, spikelet-bearing branches.

Poa celsa is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
