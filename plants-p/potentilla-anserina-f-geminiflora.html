Potentilla anserina f. geminiflora

Potentilla anserina f. geminiflora, commonly known as the twin-flowered silverweed, is a hemicryptophyte with a low-growing, mat-forming habit.

It features silver-gray, hairy leaves that are typically opposite and lanceolate in shape. The plant produces clusters of small, yellow, five-petaled flowers that bloom in early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments.

This variety is noted for its unique floral structure, with two flowers emerging from a single stem.
