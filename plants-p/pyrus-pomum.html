Pyrus pomum

Pyrus pomum, commonly known as the apple tree, is a species of deciduous tree native to central and eastern Europe.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and showy, pink or white flowers. The tree produces pomaceous fruits, which are typically sweet and juicy, containing a core of seeds surrounded by soft, fibrous tissue. Apple trees are widely cultivated for their fruit, which is used in culinary, medicinal, and commercial applications.

They thrive in temperate climates and require well-drained soil for optimal growth.
