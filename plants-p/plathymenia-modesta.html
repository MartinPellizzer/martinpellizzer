Plathymenia modesta

Plathymenia modesta, commonly known as the African mahogany, is a small to medium-sized tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, also known as the legume or bean family, and is characterized by its bipinnate leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The tree produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

It is valued for its durable, aromatic wood, which is used in furniture and construction, and for its ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
