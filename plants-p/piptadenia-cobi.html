Piptadenia cobi

Piptadenia cobi, commonly known as the Brazilian pequi tree, is a deciduous tree native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellowish flowers. The tree produces a large, woody pod that contains edible seeds, which are used in local cuisine. Its bark and leaves have also been used in traditional medicine for their various therapeutic properties.

The plant is valued for its ecological and economic importance in its native habitat.
