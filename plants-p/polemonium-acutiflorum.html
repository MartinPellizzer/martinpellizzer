Polemonium acutiflorum

Polemonium acutiflorum, commonly known as the sharpflower phlox, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern North America.

It typically grows in moist, wooded areas and has lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces striking clusters of pink to purple flowers in late spring to early summer. Its stems are erect and often branched, supporting the showy inflorescences.

Polemonium acutiflorum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a nectar source for pollinators.
