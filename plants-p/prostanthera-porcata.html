Prostanthera porcata

Prostanthera porcata, commonly known as the hairy leaved mintbush, is a shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by aromatic leaves and square stems. The plant features grey-green, hairy foliage and produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple. It is often found in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to coastal and heathland environments.

Prostanthera porcata is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local wildlife.
