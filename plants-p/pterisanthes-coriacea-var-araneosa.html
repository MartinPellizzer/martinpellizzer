Pterisanthes coriacea var. araneosa

Pterisanthes coriacea var. araneosa, commonly known as the hairy nettle, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa.

It is characterized by its rough, hairy stems and leaves that are often covered in fine, stinging hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically borne on slender pedicels. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland or rocky areas.

The species is notable for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
