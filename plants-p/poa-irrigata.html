Poa irrigata

Poa irrigata, commonly known as the irrigated grass or water grass, is a species of flowering plant in the grass family Poaceae.

It is native to the western United States, particularly in areas with moist soils and high elevations. The plant is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and slender, leafy blades that often form dense mats. Poa irrigata typically blooms with small, spike-like inflorescences that produce numerous tiny flowers.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
