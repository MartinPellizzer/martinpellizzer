Paphiopedilum chamberlainianum var. primulinum

Paphiopedilum chamberlainianum var. primulinum, commonly known as the Primulinum lady's slipper, is a rare and distinctive orchid native to the Philippines.

It is characterized by its unique, intricate flower structure with a pouch-like lip that resembles a slipper, a feature common in the genus Paphiopedilum. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded forest environments. Its flowers are usually pale yellow to greenish-white with subtle markings, and it blooms during the rainy season.

Due to habitat loss and over-collection, it is considered endangered and is often cultivated in conservation programs.
