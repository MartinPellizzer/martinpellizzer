Pyrus amoena

Pyrus amoena, commonly known as the sweet white pear, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its elegant, slender branches and heart-shaped leaves that turn yellow in autumn. The tree produces clusters of small, white flowers in early spring, followed by sweet, aromatic fruit. The fruit is typically small, with a yellowish skin and a juicy, sweet flesh.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental beauty and edible fruit.
