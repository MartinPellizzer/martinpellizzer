Pulsatilla patens subsp. asiatica

Pulsatilla patens subsp.

asiatica, commonly known as the Asian pasqueflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to parts of Asia. It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its showy, early spring blooms with numerous yellow stamens.

The plant typically grows in alpine or rocky habitats, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are palmately divided, and the flowers are surrounded by a crown of sepals.

It is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
