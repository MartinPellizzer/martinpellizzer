Phoebe superba

Phoebe superba, commonly known as the noble phoebe, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 3 to 6 feet, featuring dense, broad leaves that are often a deep green. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers in clusters during the spring, which are followed by blue-black berries. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or swampy areas.

Phoebe superba is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
