Pardanthus dichotomus

Pardanthus dichotomus, commonly known as the two-branched grass, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a small, perennial herb that typically grows in grassland habitats. The plant produces compact, spike-like inflorescences with tiny, white or pale blue flowers. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often arranged in pairs along the stem.

Pardanthus dichotomus is adapted to arid conditions and plays a role in stabilizing soils in its native environment.
