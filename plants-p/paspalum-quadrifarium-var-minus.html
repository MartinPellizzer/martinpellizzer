Paspalum quadrifarium var. minus

Paspalum quadrifarium var. minus, commonly known as the slender false brome, is a grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, pale green to brown flowers. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions.

This species is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soils and support local wildlife.
