Prunus incisa var. urceolata

Prunus incisa var. urceolata, commonly known as the Japanese cherry or urceolata cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its distinctive, urn-shaped buds that give it its scientific name, and its leaves are ovate with a serrated margin. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers in early spring, followed by bright red berries in summer. It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.

The species is also notable for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its tolerance to urban environments.
