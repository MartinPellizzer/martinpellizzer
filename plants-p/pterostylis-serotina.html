Pterostylis serotina

Pterostylis serotina, commonly known as the late-flowering greenhood, is an orchid native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its unique greenish-brown flower spike, which typically blooms in late summer to autumn. The plant produces a single, hood-like flower with a distinctive lip that is often pale green or yellowish. It grows in moist, shaded habitats such as rainforests and understoreys.

Pterostylis serotina is a terrestrial orchid with a short, rhizomatous root system and is pollinated by insects, particularly thrips.
