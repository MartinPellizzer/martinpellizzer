Piper malindangense

Piper malindangense, commonly known as the Malindang pepper, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to the Philippines, specifically the island of Mindanao, where it grows in humid, tropical rainforests. The plant is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and small, heart-shaped leaves. Its fruits are peppercorns that are used in local culinary and medicinal practices.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat loss, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
