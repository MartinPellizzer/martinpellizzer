Psidium speciosum

Psidium speciosum, commonly known as the beautyberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, bright purple berries that appear in the fall. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are typically glossy and dark green. Its flowers are small, white, and clustered in umbels, adding to its ornamental appeal.

Psidium speciosum is valued in gardens for its vibrant fruit and seasonal interest.
