Polypodium tetragonum

Polypodium tetragonum, commonly known as the four-angled fern, is a species of epiphytic fern native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its distinctive, four-angled rhizome and feathery, bipinnate fronds. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, often growing on trees or rocks. Its fronds are typically dark green and have a leathery texture.

Polypodium tetragonum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appearance and ability to thrive in controlled indoor conditions.
