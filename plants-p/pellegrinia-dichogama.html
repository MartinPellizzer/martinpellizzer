Pellegrinia dichogama

Pellegrinia dichogama, commonly known as the Cape Myrtle, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its showy, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to red. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 2 meters, with lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens in warm climates.

The flowers are pollinated primarily by birds, particularly sunbirds, which are attracted to their nectar-rich blooms.
