Ptelea trifoliata var. lutescens

Ptelea trifoliata var. lutescens, commonly known as the yellow-leafed angelica tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are trifoliate, with three leaflets that are typically yellow-green in color. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in clusters during the spring, followed by elliptical, woody fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
