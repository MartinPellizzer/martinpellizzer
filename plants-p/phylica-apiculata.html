Phylica apiculata

Phylica apiculata, commonly known as the silver tree, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its silvery-white, felt-like leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring, followed by red, fleshy fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Phylica apiculata is valued for its ornamental beauty and is also used in traditional medicine.
