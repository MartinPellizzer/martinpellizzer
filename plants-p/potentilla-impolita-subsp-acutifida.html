Potentilla impolita subsp. acutifida

Potentilla impolita subsp.

acutifida, commonly known as the sharp-fruited cinquefoil, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It features compound leaves with five elliptical leaflets, often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers in clusters during the summer months. Its fruits are small, dry capsules that release seeds upon maturity.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to harsh, alpine environments.
