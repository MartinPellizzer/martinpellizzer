Potentilla umbrosa subsp. decrescens

Potentilla umbrosa subsp.

decrescens, commonly known as the shade-loving cinquefoil, is a perennial shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of North America. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and rocky slopes.

The plant features small, dark green leaves that are typically arranged in a spiral pattern around the stems. Its flowers are usually white or pale yellow, appearing in clusters at the ends of the branches.

It has a compact growth habit and is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
