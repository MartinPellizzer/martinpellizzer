Potamogeton heterophyllus f. myriophyllus

Potamogeton heterophyllus f. myriophyllus, commonly known as the broad-leaf pondweed, is a freshwater aquatic plant characterized by its morphological variability.

It typically exhibits long, ribbon-like leaves that can vary in width depending on the water depth and light conditions. The plant is often found in shallow to moderately deep waters of lakes, rivers, and ponds. Its flowering stems bear small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in spike-like inflorescences.

This subspecies is known for its adaptability to different aquatic environments and plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various organisms.
