Paspalum chrysites

Paspalum chrysites, commonly known as golden paspalum, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and slender, upright stems. The plant produces small, golden-yellow inflorescences that are often clustered at the base of the plant. It thrives in moist to wet environments, such as marshes and along riverbanks.

Its rhizomatous root system allows it to spread vegetatively, making it an effective colonizer of disturbed or wetland habitats.
