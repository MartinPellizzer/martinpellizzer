Prunus serrulata f. longipes

Prunus serrulata f. longipes, commonly known as the Japanese cherry blossom or some varieties may be referred to as the weeping cherry, is a unique form of the Prunus serrulata species.

It is characterized by its pendulous branches that droop gracefully, giving it a weeping appearance. The plant produces delicate, fragrant flowers in shades of pink and white during the spring. It is native to Japan and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes in gardens and parks.

The leaves are oval-shaped and turn a vibrant yellow in the autumn, adding to its aesthetic appeal.
