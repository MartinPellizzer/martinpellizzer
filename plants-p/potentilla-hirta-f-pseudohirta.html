Potentilla hirta f. pseudohirta

Potentilla hirta f. pseudohirta, commonly known as the hairy cinquefoil, is a perennial flowering plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and dense, hairy foliage. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are typically oval or elliptical with a rough, hairy texture.

It is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and disturbed soils, and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
