Pilosella angustella

Pilosella angustella, commonly known as narrowleaf mouse-ear, is a flowering plant in the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its small, hairy leaves and yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters. Native to Europe and parts of Asia, it thrives in grassy areas and open habitats. The plant has a low-growing habit and is often found in disturbed soils.

It is an important nectar source for various pollinators.
