Pseudopiptadenia bahiana

Pseudopiptadenia bahiana, commonly known as Brazilian wisteria, is a climbing legume native to Brazil.

It features compound leaves with elongated leaflets and produces clusters of fragrant, violet-blue flowers. The plant is characterized by its woody stem and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its seeds are enclosed in pods that split open to release the seeds.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
