Pseudotsuga douglasii var. pumila

Pseudotsuga douglasii var. pumila, commonly known as the dwarf Douglas fir, is a compact evergreen conifer native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is characterized by its slow growth and dense, rounded form, making it well-suited for rock gardens and small spaces. The plant features scale-like leaves that are dark green on top and whitish underneath, with a fragrant resinous scent. Its cones are small and typically hang downward, containing edible seeds.

This variety is highly valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
