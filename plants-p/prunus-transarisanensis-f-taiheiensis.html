Prunus transarisanensis f. taiheiensis

Prunus transarisanensis f. taiheiensis, commonly known as the Taiheiensis cherry, is a rare and ornamental cherry tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its striking white flowers that bloom in early spring, adding a delicate beauty to gardens and landscapes. The plant has a compact growth habit, making it suitable for smaller spaces or as a decorative shrub. Its leaves are dark green and glossy, providing a striking contrast to the pale blossoms.

This variety is highly valued for its aesthetic appeal and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes in horticulture.
