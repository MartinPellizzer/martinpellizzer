Primula × variabilis subsp. flagellicaulis

Primula × variabilis subsp.

flagellicaulis, commonly known as the flagellate primrose, is a hybrid species within the Primulaceae family. It is characterized by its distinctive, elongated, and often hairy stems, which give it its subspecific name "flagellicaulis.".

The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped flowers, typically in shades of white, pink, or purple, which bloom in early spring. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in woodland or shaded garden settings.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated for its attractive foliage and floral display.
