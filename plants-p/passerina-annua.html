Passerina annua

Passerina annua, commonly known as the annual pasqueflower, is a herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its showy, early spring blooms. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows and roadsides. It has simple, alternate leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into follicles.

Passerina annua is an important early nectar source for pollinators like bees and butterflies.
