Poterium agrimonioides

Poterium agrimonioides, commonly known as the silverleaf nightshade, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring gray-green foliage and showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant produces small, fleshy berries that are often eaten by birds, aiding in seed dispersal.

Botanically, it belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and peppers.
