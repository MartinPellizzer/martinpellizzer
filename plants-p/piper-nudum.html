Piper nudum

Piper nudum, commonly known as the slender pepper vine, is a climbing shrub native to tropical regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many species of pepper plants. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by small, round berries. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a rough texture.

The plant is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine.
