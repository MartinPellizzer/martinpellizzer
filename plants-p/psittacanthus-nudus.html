Psittacanthus nudus

Psittacanthus nudus, commonly known as the naked leaf fern, is a tropical plant belonging to the family Polypodiaceae.

It is characterized by its simple, unbranched fronds that lack the typical pinnae found in many ferns, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces sporangia on the underside of its leaves, which are clustered in sori, a feature common among ferns. It thrives in humid, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its unique morphology and reproductive structures make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
