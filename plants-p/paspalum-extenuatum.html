Paspalum extenuatum

Paspalum extenuatum, commonly known as slender paspalum, is a grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and slender, elongated leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along streambanks. Its flowers are small and clustered in panicles, which are often hidden within the dense foliage.

Paspalum extenuatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in wetland environments.
