Polypodium argentinum

Polypodium argentinum, commonly known as the Argentine holly fern, is a species of fern native to South America.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its dense, evergreen fronds that form a compact, bushy growth. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky areas. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The fronds are divided into small, lance-shaped segments, giving it a distinctive appearance.
