Polygonum paniculatum var. rude

Polygonum paniculatum var. rude, commonly known as the red stemmed filaree, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Asia.

It features slender, upright stems that turn reddish-purple with age, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in paniculate inflorescences, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its ornamental value.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often used in landscaping for its attractive foliage and seasonal color.
