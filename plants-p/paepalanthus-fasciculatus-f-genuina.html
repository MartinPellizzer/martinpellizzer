Paepalanthus fasciculatus f. genuina

Paepalanthus fasciculatus f. genuina, commonly known as the narrow-leaved puya, is a unique bromeliad native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact, rosette-forming structure with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a waxy cuticle. The plant produces striking inflorescences with brightly colored, tubular flowers that attract specific pollinators. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires minimal water, making it well adapted to its harsh environment.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
