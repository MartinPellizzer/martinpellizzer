Phalaenopsis pantherina

Phalaenopsis pantherina, commonly known as the panther orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dark green, strap-like leaves and clusters of long, slender, white or pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees or other surfaces in its natural habitat. It produces flowers that are often fragrant and can last for several months.

Phalaenopsis pantherina is prized for its ornamental value and is commonly cultivated as a houseplant.
