Portulaca cuneifolia

Portulaca cuneifolia, commonly known as the narrowleaf purslane, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is a succulent species characterized by its fleshy, ribbed stems and leaves that are typically narrow and lance-shaped. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the morning and close by midday. It thrives in hot, dry environments and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides.

Portulaca cuneifolia is also known for its ability to tolerate poor soil conditions and is sometimes used in ornamental gardening.
