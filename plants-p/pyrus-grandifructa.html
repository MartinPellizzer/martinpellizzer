Pyrus grandifructa

Pyrus grandifructa, commonly known as the Chinese white pear, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy white flowers that bloom in early spring. The tree produces pome fruits with a crisp, sweet flesh and a white to yellowish color. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value as well as its edible fruit.

The species is also valued in traditional Chinese medicine for its purported health benefits.
