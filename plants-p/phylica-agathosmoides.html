Phylica agathosmoides

Phylica agathosmoides, commonly known as the silver bush, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, silver-gray foliage and small, showy flowers that range from white to pink. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it drought-tolerant and suitable for xeriscaping. It produces small, winged fruits that are dispersed by wind, aiding in its propagation.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in traditional medicine.
