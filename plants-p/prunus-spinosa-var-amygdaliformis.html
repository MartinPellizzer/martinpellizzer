Prunus spinosa var. amygdaliformis

Prunus spinosa var. amygdaliformis, commonly known as the almond-shaped blackthorn, is a variant of the common blackthorn.

It is a deciduous shrub native to Europe and parts of western Asia. The plant is characterized by its thorny stems and small, dark purple berries that ripen in late summer. Its leaves are typically oval with serrated edges, and it produces white flowers in early spring.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and historical use in hedgerows.
