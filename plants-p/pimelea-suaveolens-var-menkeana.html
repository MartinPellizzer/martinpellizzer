Pimelea suaveolens var. menkeana

Pimelea suaveolens var. menkeana, commonly known as the sweet-smelling pimelea, is a shrub native to southern Australia.

It features narrow, linear to lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in dense clusters, typically blooming in spring. It is well adapted to dry, sandy soils and is often found in heathlands and coastal areas.

The species is noted for its aromatic foliage and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
