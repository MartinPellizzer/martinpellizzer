Prunus mutabilis var. dilatata

Prunus mutabilis var. dilatata, commonly known as the Variable Cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its showy, showy flowers that range from white to pink to red, depending on the age of the bloom. The leaves are ovate to elliptical, with a smooth margin and a glossy green surface. The fruit is a small, dark red drupe that matures in late summer.

This plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its seasonal display.
