Paspalum paludosum

Paspalum paludosum, commonly known as swamp paspalum, is a perennial grass species native to wetland environments in the Americas.

It thrives in moist, often saturated soils and is commonly found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. The plant has slender, erect stems and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny florets. Its leaves are narrow and often have a bluish-green hue, and it is adapted to tolerate prolonged flooding.

Paspalum paludosum plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
