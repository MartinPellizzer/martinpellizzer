Psathyrostachys dagestanica

Psathyrostachys dagestanica, commonly known as Dagestan wild rye, is a grass species native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and narrow, blade-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny florets. It is adapted to rocky and mountainous environments, thriving in well-drained soils.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
