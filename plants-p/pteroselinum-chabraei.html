Pteroselinum chabraei

Pteroselinum chabraei, commonly known as Kashmiri coriander, is a herbaceous plant native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes other aromatic herbs like parsley and dill. The plant is characterized by its finely divided, feathery leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers that appear in umbrella-like clusters. Its aromatic leaves are often used in culinary applications, adding a distinct flavor to various dishes.

Additionally, it is valued for its medicinal properties, with traditional uses in treating digestive and respiratory ailments.
