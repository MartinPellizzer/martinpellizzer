Palisota laxiflora

Palisota laxiflora, commonly known as the broadleaf arrowhead, is a herbaceous perennial native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features heart-shaped leaves that are often glossy and arranged alternately along the stem. The plant produces small, white or pale green flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the warmer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Palisota laxiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, supporting various pollinators and aquatic life.
