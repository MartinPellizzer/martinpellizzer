Patrinia scabra

Patrinia scabra, commonly known as rough Patrinia, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the family Lamiaceae, which includes many aromatic plants such as mint and sage. The plant is characterized by its hairy, square stems and opposite leaves with serrated edges. Its small, tubular flowers are typically blue or purple and bloom in summer.

Patrinia scabra is often used in traditional Chinese medicine for its purported health benefits.
