Pelargonium vitifolium

Pelargonium vitifolium, commonly known as the grape-leaved geranium, is a species of flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that resemble grape leaves, hence its common name. The plant produces clusters of small, pink to red flowers that bloom in summer. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and showy blooms.

Pelargonium vitifolium prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for gardens and containers.
