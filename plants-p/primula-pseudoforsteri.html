Primula pseudoforsteri

Primula pseudoforsteri, commonly known as the Forster's primrose, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern China.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often in forest understories or rocky areas. It produces small, oval leaves that are usually hairy and arranged in a basal rosette.

Primula pseudoforsteri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
