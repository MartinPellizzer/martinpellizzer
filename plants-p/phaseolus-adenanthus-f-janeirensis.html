Phaseolus adenanthus f. janeirensis

Phaseolus adenanthus f. janeirensis, commonly known as the Janeirensis bean, is a variant of the common bean species native to South America.

It is characterized by its small, kidney-shaped seeds and hairy stems. The plant typically grows as an annual herb, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its leaves are trifoliate, with each leaflet being ovate and slightly serrated.

This variety is valued for its adaptability to various soil conditions and its use in traditional cuisines.
