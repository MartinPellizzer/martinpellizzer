Pandanus odorus

Pandanus odorus, commonly known as the sweet-scented pandanus, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, fibrous leaves that form a rosette shape, often reaching several meters in length. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. Its aromatic properties make it valuable in traditional medicine and as a source of essential oils.

The fruit, known as a "breadfruit," is edible and widely used in various cuisines.
