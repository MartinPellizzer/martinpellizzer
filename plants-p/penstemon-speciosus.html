Penstemon speciosus

Penstemon speciosus, commonly known as the showy penstemon, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, tubular flowers. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, producing vibrant purple or pink flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Penstemon speciosus is also valued for its drought tolerance and ability to enhance biodiversity in native plant landscapes.
