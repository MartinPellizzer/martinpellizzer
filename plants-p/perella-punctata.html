Perella punctata

Perella punctata, commonly known as the spotted bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of East Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture and a distinctive pattern of spots on its petals. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Perella punctata is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and botanical collections.
