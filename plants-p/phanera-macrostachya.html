Phanera macrostachya

Phanera macrostachya, commonly known as the large-stalked phanera, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in summer. The plant produces woody capsules as fruit, which contain numerous seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for ecological studies and horticultural interest.
