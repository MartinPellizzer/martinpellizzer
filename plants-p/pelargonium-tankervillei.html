Pelargonium tankervillei

Pelargonium tankervillei, commonly known as the Tankerville geranium, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Geraniaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, five-petaled flowers. The leaves are typically lobed and have a slightly hairy texture, while the flowers range in color from pink to red. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and relatively low maintenance requirements.
