Potamogeton acuminatus

Potamogeton acuminatus, commonly known as the sharp-pointed pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in North America.

It has long, narrow leaves that are typically arranged in whorls around the stem, and its flowers are small and green, often occurring in clusters. The plant thrives in shallow waters with soft sediment, and it plays a role in stabilizing aquatic habitats.

Its presence is often an indicator of good water quality and healthy wetland ecosystems.
