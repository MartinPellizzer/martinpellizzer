Pandanus flabelliformis

Pandanus flabelliformis, commonly known as the broad-leaf pandanus, is a tropical plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its long, narrow leaves that are often arranged in a fan-like pattern, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its fibrous roots and sturdy stems make it adaptable to coastal and sandy soils.

It is often used in traditional crafts and as a ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal.
