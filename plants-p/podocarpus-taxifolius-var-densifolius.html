Podocarpus taxifolius var. densifolius

Podocarpus taxifolius var. densifolius, commonly known as the dense-leaved lacebark, is a slow-growing evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and exfoliating bark that reveals a mottled pattern of white and green. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are glossy and arranged in whorls around the stem. It produces small, cone-like structures that contain its seeds, typical of the Podocarpaceae family.

This species is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and air-purifying qualities.
