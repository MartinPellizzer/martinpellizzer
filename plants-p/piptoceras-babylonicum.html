Piptoceras babylonicum

Piptoceras babylonicum, commonly known as the Babylonian fig, is a flowering plant belonging to the Moraceae family.

It is characterized by its alternate, simple, and often lobed leaves that are usually green and slightly hairy. The plant produces small, clustered greenish-white flowers that are followed by edible, round, green fruits. The fruit turns yellow or red when mature and is often consumed by both humans and animals.

It is native to regions of the Middle East and is valued for its ornamental and culinary uses.
