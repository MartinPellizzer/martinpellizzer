Pisonia georgina

Pisonia georgina, commonly known as the Georgina hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its showy, pale pink to white flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting birds and insects. The plant has palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, and its stems are often covered in soft, white hairs. It thrives in coastal areas and is often found in sandy or limestone soils.

Pisonia georgina is known for its rapid growth and is sometimes used in ornamental gardening.
