Paracaryum rugulosum var. cyclhymenium

Paracaryum rugulosum var. cyclhymenium, commonly known as the cyclical waxy fern, is a unique variant of the species characterized by its distinctive leaf morphology.

The plant exhibits a compact growth habit with fronds that are densely covered in a waxy cuticle, giving them a glossy appearance. Its leaves are typically arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem, with a slightly curled margin that contributes to its ornamental value. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, making it a popular choice for tropical and subtropical gardens.

Botanically, it is notable for its genetic variation, which results in the cyclical pattern of its leaf development.
