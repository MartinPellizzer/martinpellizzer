Phaius halconensis

Phaius halconensis, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking golden-yellow flowers and broad, strap-like leaves. The plant typically grows in wet, shaded environments such as swampy forests and along riverbanks. It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is known for its showy, long-lasting blooms.

The botanical features of Phaius halconensis make it a popular ornamental plant in horticulture.
