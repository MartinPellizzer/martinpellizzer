Phyllagathis ternata

Phyllagathis ternata, commonly known as the three-leaf palaquium, is a small evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three small, oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers that are often clustered in inflorescences. Its fruit is a woody capsule that contains several seeds.

Phyllagathis ternata is typically found in moist, shaded forest understories and is valued for its ornamental foliage.
