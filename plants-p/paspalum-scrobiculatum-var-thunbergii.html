Paspalum scrobiculatum var. thunbergii

Paspalum scrobiculatum var. thunbergii, commonly known as Thunberg's paspalum, is a perennial grass native to South America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetland or marshy habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in supporting local wildlife.
