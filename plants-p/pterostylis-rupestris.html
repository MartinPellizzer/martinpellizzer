Pterostylis rupestris

Pterostylis rupestris, commonly known as the rock orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Australia.

It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in coastal or inland regions. The plant features a slender, wiry stem with a single or few leaves, and it produces a single spike of small, greenish or brownish flowers. Its flowers are characterized by their unique morphology, with a lip that is often curved and adorned with intricate markings.

Pterostylis rupestris is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is adapted to thrive in arid or semi-arid environments.
