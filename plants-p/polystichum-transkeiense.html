Polystichum transkeiense

Polystichum transkeiense, commonly known as the Transkei shield fern, is a species of evergreen fern native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green, leathery fronds that are typically arranged in a compact, shield-like shape. The plant produces spore-bearing structures called sori on the underside of its fronds, which are clustered in groups known as sorus. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forested areas.

Polystichum transkeiense is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
