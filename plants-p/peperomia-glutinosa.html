Peperomia glutinosa

Peperomia glutinosa, commonly known as the sticky peperomia, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that often have a sticky texture, which is a natural defense mechanism against herbivores. The plant typically grows as a compact shrub with upright stems and small, ovate leaves that are usually green with a slight reddish or purple hue on the underside. It produces tiny, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or reddish, followed by small, berry-like fruits.

Peperomia glutinosa is well-suited for indoor cultivation due to its low maintenance requirements and ability to thrive in bright, indirect light.
