Pseudolaelia diamantinensis

Pseudolaelia diamantinensis, commonly known as the Diamond Mountain orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and unique floral structure. The plant produces small, showy flowers with a distinctive violet-blue coloration, often adorned with white markings. It thrives in shaded, humid environments and is typically found in rocky or forested areas.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
