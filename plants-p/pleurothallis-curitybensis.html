Pleurothallis curitybensis

Pleurothallis curitybensis, commonly known as the Curitype Cattleya, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and attractive, fragrant flowers. The plant typically produces small, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of white or pale pink flowers with a distinctive, spurred lip. It thrives in shaded, humid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is a sought-after species among orchid enthusiasts.
