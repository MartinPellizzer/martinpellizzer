Pseudotsuga taxifolia f. caesia

Pseudotsuga taxifolia f. caesia, commonly known as the blue spruce, is a variant of the Japanese white fir characterized by its striking bluish-gray foliage.

This form exhibits a dense, conical shape with needle-like leaves that are shorter and more sharply pointed than those of the typical species. The leaves are coated with a waxy cuticle, contributing to their distinctive color and enhanced drought resistance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular ornamental tree in temperate climates.

Its unique appearance and hardiness make it a valuable addition to gardens and landscapes.
