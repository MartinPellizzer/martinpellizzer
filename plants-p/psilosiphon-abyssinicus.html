Psilosiphon abyssinicus

Psilosiphon abyssinicus, commonly known as the Abyssinian siphon, is a rare and specialized plant native to the high-altitude regions of Ethiopia.

It is characterized by its unique, spirally twisted stems and small, inconspicuous leaves adapted to the harsh, arid conditions of its habitat. The plant produces tiny, pale yellow flowers that bloom in the early morning, followed by the development of distinctive, elongated fruits. It has a deep root system that allows it to access moisture from deep within the soil.

Due to its limited distribution and specific ecological requirements, it is considered a vulnerable species.
