Plantago gibbosa

Plantago gibbosa, commonly known as the broadleaf plantain, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features a rosette of broad, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces small, greenish flowers in a spike that bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their soothing properties.

Botanically, it belongs to the family Plantaginaceae and is characterized by its robust growth and adaptability to various soil types.
