Polypleurella micranthera

Polypleurella micranthera, commonly known as the narrow-leaved rock-heath, is a small shrub native to the wet tropical regions of Australia.

It belongs to the family Myrtaceae, which includes eucalyptus and tea trees. The plant is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and small, clustered white or pale pink flowers. It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in coastal or inland heathlands.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
