Polygonatum falcatum var. trichosanthum

Polygonatum falcatum var. trichosanthum, commonly known as the Chinese Solomon's seal, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It features long, arching stems with lanceolate leaves that are often hairy on the underside. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters, which are followed by red berries. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its rhizomes are used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
