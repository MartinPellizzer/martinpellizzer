Plantago leiocephala

Plantago leiocephala, commonly known as the narrow-leaved plantain, is a perennial herb native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It features rosettes of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, greenish flowers in dense spikes. The plant is known for its fibrous roots and is often found in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and fields. It has a long history of use in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.

Botanically, it belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
