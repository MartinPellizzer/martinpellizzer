Polygala ficalhoana

Polygala ficalhoana, commonly known as the Ficalho's milkweed, is a flowering plant native to the island of Socotra.

It belongs to the Polygalaceae family and is characterized by its unique, spurred flowers that resemble those of milkweeds. The plant typically grows in rocky and arid environments, showcasing its adaptation to the harsh conditions of its native habitat. Its foliage is usually small and succulent, aiding in water retention.

The species is of interest to botanists due to its distinct morphology and endemic status.
