Peperomia pilaloensis

Peperomia pilaloensis, commonly known as the Pilaloensis Peperomia, is a rare and unique species native to the mountainous regions of Ecuador.

It is a compact, low-growing plant with thick, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a fine, powdery coating. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or reddish in color, arranged in clusters. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.
