Potentilla hirta var. cernavodae

Potentilla hirta var. cernavodae, commonly known as the Transylvanian cinquefoil, is a perennial shrub native to the Carpathian Mountains.

It features small, yellow, five-petaled flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant has dark green, serrated leaves covered in fine, soft hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or alpine environments.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive and hardy member of the Rosaceae family.
