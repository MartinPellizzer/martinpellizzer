Pseudomaxillaria vestita

Pseudomaxillaria vestita, commonly known as the naked maxillaria, is a species of orchid native to South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in the understory of tropical forests. Its leaves are linear and arranged in a rosette, and it has a pseudobulb structure typical of many orchids.

The flowers bloom in summer and are known for their delicate appearance and subtle fragrance.
