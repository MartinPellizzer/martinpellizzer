Passiflora pacifica

Passiflora pacifica, commonly known as the Hawaiian passionflower, is a climbing vine native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that feature a complex arrangement of petals, sepals, and bracts. The plant produces blue or purple berries that are edible and contain small, hard seeds. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped with a rough texture and are often used in traditional Hawaiian medicine.

Passiflora pacifica plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, supporting various pollinators and wildlife.
