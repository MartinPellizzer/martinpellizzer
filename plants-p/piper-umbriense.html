Piper umbriense

Piper umbriense, commonly known as the Umbrian pepper plant, is a flowering vine native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species such as black pepper. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by elongated, fleshy fruits containing aromatic seeds. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a slightly rough texture.

Piper umbriense is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its unique foliage and fragrance.
