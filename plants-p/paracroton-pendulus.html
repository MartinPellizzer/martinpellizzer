Paracroton pendulus

Paracroton pendulus, commonly known as the weeping croton, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its drooping, pendulous branches that bear clusters of bright yellow-orange flowers. The plant has palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in humid, warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Paracroton pendulus is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.
