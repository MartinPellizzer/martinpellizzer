Panax pseudoginseng var. major

Panax pseudoginseng var. major, commonly known as the American ginseng, is a perennial herb native to the forests of North America.

It belongs to the Araliaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive fleshy, yellowish root system. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring compound leaves with three to five leaflets. Its flowers are small, greenish-white, and clustered in umbels, while its fruit is a red, fleshy berry.

The root is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional herbal remedies.
