Polystichum pygmaeum

Polystichum pygmaeum, commonly known as the dwarf wood fern, is a species of fern native to the northern hemisphere.

It is characterized by its compact, upright fronds that are typically less than 30 centimeters in length. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories. Its leaves are bi- to tri-pinnate, with a distinctive dark green color and a leathery texture.

Polystichum pygmaeum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in shaded garden settings.
