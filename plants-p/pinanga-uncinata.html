Pinanga uncinata

Pinanga uncinata, commonly known as the hooked pinanga, is a species of cycad native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its stout, unbranched trunk and large, pinnately compound leaves. The plant produces cones, with male and female cones typically occurring on separate plants. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine and as a source of fiber.

The species is adapted to tropical climates and thrives in well-drained, acidic soils.
