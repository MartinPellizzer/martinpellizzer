Passiflora maguirei

Passiflora maguirei, commonly known as the Maguire's passionflower, is a tropical climbing vine native to the Caribbean.

It is characterized by its striking, complex flowers that feature a mix of blue, purple, and white petals, along with vibrant crimson sepals. The plant produces edible berries that are typically yellow or orange when ripe. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Passiflora maguirei is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
