Pinus sylvestris cretacea

Pinus sylvestris cretacea, commonly known as the Cretaceous pine, is a fossilized species of pine that dates back to the Cretaceous period.

It is characterized by its ancient cone structures and needle-like leaves, which are preserved in sedimentary rock layers. This plant is significant in paleobotanical studies due to its role in understanding ancient ecosystems and climate conditions. Its morphology provides insights into the evolution of coniferous plants.

While it no longer exists in its original form, its fossilized remains offer valuable data for reconstructing prehistoric environments.
