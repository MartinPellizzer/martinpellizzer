Pereskia saipinensis

Pereskia saipinensis, commonly known as the Saipan pereskia, is a shrub native to the Mariana Islands.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems that are covered with a layer of whitish, waxy tissue. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the summer months. Its leaves are alternate, elliptical, and have a slightly serrated edge.

Pereskia saipinensis is adapted to coastal environments and is often found growing on rocky substrates.
