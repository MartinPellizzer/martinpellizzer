Phaseolus vulgaris var. ferrugineus

Phaseolus vulgaris var. ferrugineus, commonly known as the rust bean, is a variety of common bean characterized by its distinctive rust-colored seed coat.

It is native to the Americas and is often cultivated for its edible seeds, which are used in various cuisines. The plant typically grows as an annual herb, reaching heights of up to 2 meters, with compound leaves and clusters of small white or pale pink flowers. Its seeds are rich in protein and are used in traditional dishes, while the plant itself is also valued for its ornamental features.

The variety is relatively drought-tolerant, making it suitable for cultivation in a range of climates.
