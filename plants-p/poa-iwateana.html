Poa iwateana

Poa iwateana, commonly known as the Iwate grass, is a species of flowering plant in the grass family Poaceae.

It is native to Japan, specifically the island of Honshu, where it grows in moist, shaded environments. The plant is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, blue-green blades. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, two-lobed spikelets.

Poa iwateana is often found in forest understories and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in its native habitat.
