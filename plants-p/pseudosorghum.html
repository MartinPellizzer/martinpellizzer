Pseudosorghum

Pseudosorghum, commonly known as false sorghum, is a genus of flowering plants in the grass family, Poaceae.

It comprises several species, most of which are native to Africa and parts of Asia. These plants are typically annual or perennial herbs with tall, erect stems and narrow leaves. Pseudosorghum species often grow in arid or semi-arid environments, showcasing their adaptability to harsh conditions.

They are sometimes cultivated for forage or as ornamental plants due to their distinctive appearance.
