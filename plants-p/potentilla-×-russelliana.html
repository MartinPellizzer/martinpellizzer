Potentilla × russelliana

Potentilla × russelliana, commonly known as the Russell's cinquefoil, is a hybrid shrub belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and produces showy, yellow or orange flowers in early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 feet and features small, rounded leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Botanically, it is notable for its ability to hybridize with other Potentilla species, contributing to its genetic diversity.
