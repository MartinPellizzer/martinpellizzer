Psilurus nardoides

Psilurus nardoides, commonly known as the narrowleaf psilurus, is a member of the Apiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic foliage and umbellate flowers.

It is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, where it thrives in rocky and well-drained soils. It is often found in alpine meadows and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and traditional herbal practices.
