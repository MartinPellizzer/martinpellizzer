Polymnia aspera

Polymnia aspera, commonly known as the roughleaf polymnia, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the family Polygonaceae, which includes plants commonly known as buckwheats. The plant is characterized by its rough, hairy leaves and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodland areas and stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
