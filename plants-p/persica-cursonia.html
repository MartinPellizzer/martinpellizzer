Persica cursonia

Persica cursonia, commonly known as the Kashmir pear, is a deciduous tree native to the Himalayan regions of India and Pakistan.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, grey-barked trunk and oval-shaped leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers in early spring, which are followed by round, fuzzy fruits resembling pears.

Persica cursonia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also cultivated for its edible fruits, which have a sweet and aromatic flavor.
