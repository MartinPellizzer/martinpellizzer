Prosthechea christyana

Prosthechea christyana, commonly known as the Christmas orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, pendulous inflorescences that bear showy, fragrant flowers with intricate patterns. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are usually clustered at the base and are often covered with a dense layer of trichomes.

Prosthechea christyana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in greenhouses or as a houseplant.
