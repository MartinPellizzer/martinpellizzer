Pinalia obvia

Pinalia obvia, commonly known as the common pinalia, is a flowering plant native to the temperate regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters and prefers well-drained soil in full sun. It is a perennial species that thrives in moist, fertile environments.

The flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, making it an important plant in local ecosystems.
