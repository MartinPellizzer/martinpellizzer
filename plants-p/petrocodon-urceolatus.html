Petrocodon urceolatus

Petrocodon urceolatus, commonly known as the bell-shaped petrocodon, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pale blue or violet in color. The plant grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments. Its leaves are usually narrow and succulent, helping to conserve water.

Petrocodon urceolatus is often found in mountainous areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture.
