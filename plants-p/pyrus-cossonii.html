Pyrus cossonii

Pyrus cossonii, commonly known as the Cosson's pear, is a species of flowering plant in the family Rosaceae.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and is characterized by its deciduous nature, producing small, yellowish flowers in early spring. The plant has oval-shaped leaves with a serrated margin and smooth, dark green upper surface. Its fruit, a small pear-like berry, is typically yellow when ripe and contains a single seed.

Pyrus cossonii is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive foliage and seasonal blooms.
