Pedicularis filiculiformis var. filiculiformis

Pedicularis filiculiformis var. filiculiformis, commonly known as the fern-leaved lousewort, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves and its striking, spurred flowers that range in color from yellow to red. The plant typically grows in alpine or subalpine habitats, often on rocky slopes or in meadows. It is a perennial herb that thrives in cool, moist environments with well-drained soil.

The species is notable for its unique floral structure and its role in supporting pollinators in its native ecosystems.
