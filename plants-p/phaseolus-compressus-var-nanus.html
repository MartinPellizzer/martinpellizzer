Phaseolus compressus var. nanus

Phaseolus compressus var. nanus, commonly known as the dwarf common bean, is a compact variety of the common bean plant.

It is a herbaceous annual legume native to the Americas, characterized by its small stature and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain edible seeds, which are often used in traditional cuisines. It thrives in warm climates and is cultivated for its nutritional value and adaptability to various soil types.

Its botanical features make it a valuable crop for both agricultural and ecological purposes.
