Poterium sanguisorba subsp. virescens

Poterium sanguisorba subsp.

virescens, commonly known as red dock, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its upright stems that are often reddish in color, especially when mature.

The leaves are alternate, heart-shaped, and have a rough, hairy texture. It produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in dense clusters, which bloom in summer.

This plant thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in fields, along roadsides, and in wetlands.
