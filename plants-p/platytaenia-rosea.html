Platytaenia rosea

Platytaenia rosea, commonly known as the roseleaf stinkhorn, is a unique fungal plant that exhibits interesting botanical features.

It is a basidiomycete fungus that produces a distinctive, foul-smelling cap that resembles a rose in appearance. The fruiting body typically emerges from a fleshy, white base that resembles a stem. The spore-bearing surface is covered with a slimy, sticky layer that aids in spore dispersal.

This fungus is often found in decaying organic matter, contributing to the decomposition process in forest ecosystems.
