Pycnanthus marchalianus

Pycnanthus marchalianus, commonly known as the Marchalian's pycnanthus, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Myrtaceae and is characterized by its small, leathery leaves and showy white or pink flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically eaten by birds and other wildlife.

It is often found in lowland rainforests and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance.
