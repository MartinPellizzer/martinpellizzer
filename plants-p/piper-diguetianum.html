Piper diguetianum

Piper diguetianum, commonly known as the Guiana pepper, is a climbing vine native to the rainforests of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species such as black pepper. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by elongated, slender fruits that turn red when mature. The fruits are typically used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties.

Its botanical structure includes opposite, ovate leaves with prominent veins and a climbing habit supported by adventitious roots.
