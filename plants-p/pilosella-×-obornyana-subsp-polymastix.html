Pilosella × obornyana subsp. polymastix

Pilosella × obornyana subsp.

polymastix, commonly known as the hairy leaf groundsel, is a hybrid perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It features a low-growing habit with basal leaves that are densely covered in white to grayish hairs.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in compact clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its foliage is often used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory properties.

The species is well-adapted to rocky and dry soils, making it a common sight in disturbed or marginal habitats.
