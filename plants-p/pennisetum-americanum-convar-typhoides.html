Pennisetum americanum convar. typhoides

Pennisetum americanum convar. typhoides, commonly known as switchgrass, is a perennial grass native to the central and eastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. This plant thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained soils and full sun exposure. It is a drought-tolerant species that plays an important role in ecological restoration and bioenergy production.

Its flowering spikes, which appear in late summer, add ornamental value to landscapes.
