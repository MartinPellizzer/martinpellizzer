Primula glabra subsp. glabra

Primula glabra subsp.

glabra, commonly known as the smooth primrose, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It features upright, hairy stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped flowers in shades of yellow to orange.

The leaves are typically oval, with a smooth texture and a slightly waxy coating. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
