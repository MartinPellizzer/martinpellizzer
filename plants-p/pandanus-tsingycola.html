Pandanus tsingycola

Pandanus tsingycola, commonly known as the Madagascar pandanus, is a flowering plant native to the island of Madagascar.

It belongs to the Pandanus genus, which includes a variety of tropical plants known for their distinctive, often palm-like appearance. The plant features long, narrow leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern, and it produces small, fragrant flowers that develop into edible fruits. Pandanus tsingycola thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in coastal or lowland areas.

Its unique morphology and ecological role make it an important species in Madagascar's biodiversity.
