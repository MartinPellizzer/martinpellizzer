Potentilla coccinea

Potentilla coccinea, commonly known as the scarlet cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its bright red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are palmately compound, consisting of five oval-shaped leaflets.

Potentilla coccinea is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
