Phyllanthus jardinii

Phyllanthus jardinii, commonly known as the African date tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its pinnately compound leaves with small, opposite leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by orange-red, fleshy fruits resembling dates.

Its botanical features make it an important species in traditional medicine and horticulture.
