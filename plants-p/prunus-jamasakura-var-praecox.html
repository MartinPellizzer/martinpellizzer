Prunus jamasakura var. praecox

Prunus jamasakura var. praecox, commonly known as the early cherry or early flowering cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and is prized for its early blooming pink or white flowers that appear before the leaves. The plant has small, ovate leaves that turn yellow in autumn, adding to its ornamental value. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for gardens and landscapes.

This variety is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal and is a popular choice in ornamental horticulture.
