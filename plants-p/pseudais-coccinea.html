Pseudais coccinea

Pseudais coccinea, commonly known as the red flame orchid, is a strikingly colorful orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It features vibrant red petals and a contrasting yellow lip, which contribute to its ornamental appeal. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers bloom in clusters, often emitting a subtle fragrance that attracts pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Botanically, it belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its specialized root system that absorbs moisture and nutrients from the air.
