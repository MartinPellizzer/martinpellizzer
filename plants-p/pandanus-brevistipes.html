Pandanus brevistipes

Pandanus brevistipes, commonly known as the short-stemmed pandanus, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, palm-like leaves that are often used in traditional crafts. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and is adapted to saline and sandy soils. Its inflorescence consists of small, clustered flowers that are pollinated by insects and birds.

The fruit, which is a fibrous structure containing seeds, is an important resource for both wildlife and humans.
