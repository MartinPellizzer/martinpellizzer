Plantago trichophylla

Plantago trichophylla, commonly known as the hairy plantain, is a species of flowering plant in the family Plantaginaceae.

It is characterized by its broad, oval-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, hairy textures. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are clustered in dense spikes, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Native to regions of Europe and Asia, it thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.

This plant is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally in herbal remedies.
