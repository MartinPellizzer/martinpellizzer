Paepalanthus beyrichianus

Paepalanthus beyrichianus, commonly known as the golden cup-moss, is a striking plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a member of the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its dense, spiky rosettes of grass-like leaves that form a water-holding cup. The plant produces vibrant yellow-orange inflorescences, which are highly attractive to pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds. Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
