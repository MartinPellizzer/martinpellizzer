Piptadenia cebil

Piptadenia cebil, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or pitahaya tree, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, pinkish-white flowers. The tree produces edible, red, fleshy fruits that are rich in nutrients and have a sweet, tropical flavor. Its seeds are encased in a hard, woody pulp and are often used in traditional medicine.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated for its fruit and timber.
