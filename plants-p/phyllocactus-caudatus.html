Phyllocactus caudatus

Phyllocactus caudatus, commonly known as the bearded cactus, is a unique species native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, columnar stems that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant produces clusters of bright yellow flowers during the summer months, which are followed by small, round fruits. Its leaves are modified into spines, reducing water loss in its harsh environment.

Phyllocactus caudatus is often cultivated for its striking appearance and resilience in dry conditions.
