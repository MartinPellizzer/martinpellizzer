Plantago major var. lanceolata

Plantago major var. lanceolata, commonly known as the narrowleaf plantain, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features lance-shaped leaves that are arranged in a rosette, with a smooth, waxy surface. The plant produces small, greenish flowers in dense spikes during the summer months. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.

This species is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally for its healing benefits.
