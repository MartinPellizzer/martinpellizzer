Paphiopedilum armeniacum

Paphiopedilum armeniacum, commonly known as the Armenian lady's slipper, is a terrestrial orchid native to the mountainous regions of Armenia and Turkey.

It features a distinctive slipper-shaped lip that is typically yellow with purple markings, contributing to its striking appearance. The plant produces dense, dark green leaves and has a short, stocky stem that bears a single, showy flower. It thrives in cool, humid environments and requires well-drained, humus-rich soil.

This species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
