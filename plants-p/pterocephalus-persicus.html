Pterocephalus persicus

Pterocephalus persicus, commonly known as the Persian ironweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and typical sunflower-like appearance. The plant produces tall, sparsely branched stems with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are typically blue to violet in color, forming dense clusters at the top of the plant.

Pterocephalus persicus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
