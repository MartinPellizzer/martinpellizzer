Paepalanthus praedensatus

Paepalanthus praedensatus, commonly known as the desert sunflower, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellow-orange composite flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has a short, erect stem with narrow, linear leaves adapted to minimize water loss. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in desert or semi-desert environments.

Due to its drought tolerance, it plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for desert wildlife.
