Pleurothallis bufonis

Pleurothallis bufonis, commonly known as the golden pleurothallis, is a species of orchid native to the cloud forests of Central and South America.

It is a small, epiphytic plant that thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant features delicate, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of fragrant, yellowish-green flowers. Its flowers are typically short-lived but attract a variety of pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds.

Due to its striking appearance and rarity, it is highly sought after by orchid enthusiasts and conservationists alike.
