Pandanus pweleensis

Pandanus pweleensis, commonly known as the Malagasy pandanus, is a tropical plant native to Madagascar.

It is a member of the Pandanaceae family and is characterized by its large, sword-shaped leaves that are often used in traditional crafts. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and lowland forests, thriving in humid, warm climates. Its inflorescence consists of clustered yellow flowers that are pollinated by insects and birds.

The plant's unique morphology and ecological role make it an important species in its native habitat.
