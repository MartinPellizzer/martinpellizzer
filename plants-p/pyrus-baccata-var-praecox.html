Pyrus baccata var. praecox

Pyrus baccata var. praecox, commonly known as the early whitebeam, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Europe.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and small, white flowers that appear early in the spring. The plant produces edible berries that mature to a dark red or black color, though they are typically not consumed by humans.

It thrives in woodland edges and sunny clearings, preferring well-drained soils and moderate climates.
