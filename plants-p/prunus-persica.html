Prunus persica

Prunus persica, commonly known as the peach tree, is a deciduous fruit tree native to Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and alternate, ovate leaves. The tree produces showy, pinkish-white flowers in the spring, which are followed by fuzzy, yellow-orange fruits. The fruit contains a single hard pit surrounded by a fleshy, sweet pulp.

Prunus persica is cultivated for its edible fruit, which is rich in vitamins and antioxidants.
