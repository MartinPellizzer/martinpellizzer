Pavonia clarkii

Pavonia clarkii, commonly known as the Clark's hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in wetlands and along stream banks. The plant features showy, pink to red bract-like flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a rough texture.

Pavonia clarkii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
