Piptanthus forrestii

Piptanthus forrestii, commonly known as the Chinese peashrub, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant is notable for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky or sandy soils.

Piptanthus forrestii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological contributions in its native habitat.
