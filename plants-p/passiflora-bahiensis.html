Passiflora bahiensis

Passiflora bahiensis, commonly known as the Brazilian passionflower, is a climbing vine native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its striking, complex flowers that feature five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments. The plant produces edible, orange-colored berries that are rich in antioxidants. Its leaves are heart-shaped and often have a velvety texture.

Passiflora bahiensis is valued in horticulture for its ornamental beauty and is also used in traditional medicine.
