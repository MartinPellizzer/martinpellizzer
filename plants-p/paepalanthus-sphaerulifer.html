Paepalanthus sphaerulifer

Paepalanthus sphaerulifer, commonly known as the globular pincushion cactus, is a species of flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, spherical clusters of small, spiny stems that form a compact, cushion-like structure. The plant produces yellow flowers during the summer months, which are clustered at the tips of the stems. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, requiring well-drained soil and full sun exposure.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by cactus enthusiasts.
