Parodia microsperma var. lohaniana

Parodia microsperma var. lohaniana, commonly known as the Lohan's ghost cactus, is a compact, globular cactus native to Mexico.

It features a pale green to blue-gray body with a smooth, waxy surface that helps reduce water loss. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in spring, adding a striking contrast to its muted coloration. It is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Its unique appearance and low maintenance make it a favored specimen among succulent enthusiasts.
