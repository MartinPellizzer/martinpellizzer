Picea abies f. curvifolia

Picea abies f. curvifolia, commonly known as the curved foliage fir, is a botanical variant of the Norway spruce.

It is characterized by its distinctive, curved needle arrangement, which sets it apart from typical spruce species. The plant exhibits a coniferous structure with dense, pyramidal crowns and is native to specific regions in Europe. Its needles are short, stiff, and often have a bluish-green hue.

This form is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and unique foliage pattern.
