Philodendron ovatoluteum

Philodendron ovatoluteum, commonly known as the heartleaf philodendron, is a tropical climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It features heart-shaped, waxy leaves that are typically deep green with a slightly variegated pattern. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by bright red berries, which are a key part of its reproductive cycle. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Its foliage makes it a popular choice for indoor gardening due to its aesthetic appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
