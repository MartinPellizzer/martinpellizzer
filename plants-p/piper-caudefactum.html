Piper caudefactum

Piper caudefactum, commonly known as the black pepper vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves with a glossy surface. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that develop into elongated, dark purple berries. Its fruit contains a single seed and is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

The plant's rapid growth and ornamental foliage make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening in warm climates.
