Phanera longiseta

Phanera longiseta, commonly known as the long-stalked phanera, is a flowering plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and compound leaves with elongated leaflets. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pale yellow or greenish in color. It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia and is often found in moist, shaded environments.

Phanera longiseta is valued for its ornamental appeal and is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens.
