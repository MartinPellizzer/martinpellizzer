Protea pinifolia

Protea pinifolia, commonly known as the pineleaf protea, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Proteaceae family and is characterized by its unique, cone-like inflorescence composed of numerous small, brightly colored flowers. The plant typically grows in coastal fynbos vegetation, where it thrives in well-drained, acidic soils. Its leaves are needle-like, giving it the common name "pineleaf," and it produces striking white to pink flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring.

Protea pinifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and resilience.
