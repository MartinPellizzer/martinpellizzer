Phalaris minor f. bracteata

Phalaris minor f. bracteata, commonly known as the bracted beakgrass, is a flowering plant belonging to the grass family Poaceae.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, two-lipped flowers. It is native to Europe and is often found in moist, calcareous soils in meadows and along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
