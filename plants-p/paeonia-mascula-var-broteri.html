Paeonia mascula var. broteri

Paeonia mascula var. broteri, commonly known as the Broteri peony, is a rare and distinctive variety of peony native to the mountainous regions of China.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically display a striking combination of red and white petals. The plant features a thick, woody root system and thick, hairy stems that help it survive in its native habitat. Its leaves are compound, with lance-shaped leaflets that are arranged alternately along the stem.

This variety is highly valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by plant enthusiasts.
