Passiflora maximiliana var. acutiloba

Passiflora maximiliana var. acutiloba, commonly known as the Mexican passionflower, is a climbing vine native to Mexico and Central America.

It is characterized by its striking, palmate leaves and showy, colorful flowers that feature a unique structure with filaments resembling a crown. The plant produces edible berries that are typically yellow or orange when ripe. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for both horticulture and ecological studies.
