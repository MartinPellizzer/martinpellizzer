Parinari laxiflora var. lata

Parinari laxiflora var. lata, commonly known as the broad-leaved parinari, is a tropical tree species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, ovate leaves that are usually glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by woody, egg-shaped fruits. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The species is valued for its hardy nature and potential use in reforestation efforts.
