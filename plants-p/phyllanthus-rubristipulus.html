Phyllanthus rubristipulus

Phyllanthus rubristipulus, commonly known as the red-stippled phyllanthus, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It features small, ovate leaves with a distinctive red or purple margin, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release tiny, wind-dispersed seeds.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
