Piper jumayense

Piper jumayense, commonly known as the Jumayense pepper, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to Central America, particularly found in countries like Guatemala and Honduras. The plant is characterized by its climbing habit, with small, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of tiny, greenish-white flowers. Its fruit, which is a small, elongated berry, contains aromatic seeds used in traditional medicine.

The plant's botanical features make it distinct within the Piper genus, with its unique morphology and chemical composition.
