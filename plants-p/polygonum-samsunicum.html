Polygonum samsunicum

Polygonum samsunicum, commonly known as the Caucasian knotweed, is a perennial herb native to the Caucasus region.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, characterized by its distinctive arrow-shaped leaves and clusters of small, pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its stems are often reddish and covered with fine hairs, while its leaves are smooth and slightly serrated.

Polygonum samsunicum is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
