Pinalia earine

Pinalia earine, commonly known as the silverleaf pinalia, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its silvery-green foliage, which helps it conserve water in its harsh environment. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its root system is deep and extensive, allowing it to access moisture from lower soil layers.

Pinalia earine is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
