Pyrus × monstrosa

Pyrus × monstrosa, commonly known as the weeping crabapple, is a hybrid deciduous tree that exhibits striking ornamental features.

It is characterized by its cascading branches that create a weeping appearance, with small, fragrant flowers in spring followed by edible fruits. The plant is native to the United States and is often cultivated for its aesthetic value in gardens and landscapes. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, requiring moderate water and pruning to maintain its shape.

Its botanical interest lies in its hybrid origin and distinctive growth form.
