Pedicularis × johannis

Pedicularis × johannis, commonly known as the "Himalayan Beardtongue," is a hybrid species native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the family Scrophulariaceae, characterized by its glandular-hairy stems and showy, tubular flowers. The flowers are typically pink to purple in color and are borne in clusters at the ends of the stems. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes.

Due to its ornamental value and unique morphology, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and by plant enthusiasts.
