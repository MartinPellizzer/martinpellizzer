Prunus spinosa var. subcinera

Prunus spinosa var. subcinera, commonly known as the blackthorn, is a shrub native to Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and small, dark blue berries that ripen in late summer. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, whitish bloom. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in hedgerows and woodland edges.

The berries are a food source for birds and are used in making jams and liqueurs.
