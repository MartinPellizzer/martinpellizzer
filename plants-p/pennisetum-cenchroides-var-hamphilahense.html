Pennisetum cenchroides var. hamphilahense

Pennisetum cenchroides var. hamphilahense, commonly known as the African feather grass, is a perennial grass species native to eastern and southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and feathery, silver-green plumes that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its rapid growth and ability to form dense stands also make it useful for erosion control and as a forage plant.
