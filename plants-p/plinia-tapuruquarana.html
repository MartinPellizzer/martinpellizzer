Plinia tapuruquarana

Plinia tapuruquarana, commonly known as the Pernambuco araçá, is a species of flowering plant native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, edible berries that are often used in local cuisine and traditional medicine. Its leaves are elliptical and glossy, with a strong aromatic scent.

Plinia tapuruquarana is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
