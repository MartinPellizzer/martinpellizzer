Picris glaucescens

Picris glaucescens, commonly known as blueweed, is an annual flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features slender, hairy stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small and yellow, arranged in clusters, and it produces small, dry fruits called cypselae.

It is often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides, thriving in dry, sunny conditions.
