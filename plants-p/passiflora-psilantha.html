Passiflora psilantha

Passiflora psilantha, commonly known as the slender passionflower, is a species native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a climbing vine that produces striking, showy flowers with five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments. The plant's botanical features include a slender stem with tendrils and heart-shaped leaves with a waxy surface. Its flowers are typically white or pale blue, with a distinctive central cluster of stamens.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical and subtropical gardens.
