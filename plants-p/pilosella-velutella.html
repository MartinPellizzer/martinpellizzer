Pilosella velutella

Pilosella velutella, commonly known as the downy cat's-paw, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small clusters of flowers. The plant features hairy, lobed leaves and produces yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, often forming dense colonies.

Pilosella velutella is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
