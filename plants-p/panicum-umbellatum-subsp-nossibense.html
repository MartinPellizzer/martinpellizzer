Panicum umbellatum subsp. nossibense

Panicum umbellatum subsp.

nossibense, commonly known as the Natal grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, linear leaves that are typically blue-green in color.

The plant produces inflorescences of spikelets that are arranged in a panicle, giving it a fluffy appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and along riverbanks.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping due to its drought tolerance and attractive foliage.
