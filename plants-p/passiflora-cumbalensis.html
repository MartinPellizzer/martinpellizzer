Passiflora cumbalensis

Passiflora cumbalensis, commonly known as the Cumbal passionflower, is a species native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a climbing vine that produces striking, showy flowers with intricate structures. The plant features palmate leaves and is characterized by its unique reproductive organs, including a crown of stamens and a feathery pistil. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in cloud forests.

Due to its ornamental value and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
