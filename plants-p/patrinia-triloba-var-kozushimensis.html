Patrinia triloba var. kozushimensis

Patrinia triloba var. kozushimensis, commonly known as the Japanese Patrinia, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and have a slightly rough texture. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its flowering stems are usually erect and can reach heights of up to 30 centimeters.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
