Phaseolus mungo

Phaseolus mungo, commonly known as the black gram or urad dal, is a leguminous plant native to South and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its climbing habit, with slender stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, green, pod-like fruits that mature to a dark, wrinkled black or brown color. Its seeds are rich in protein and are widely used in Indian and other Asian cuisines.

Botanically, it belongs to the Fabaceae family and is valued for its nutritional content and agricultural significance.
