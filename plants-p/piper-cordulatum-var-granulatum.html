Piper cordulatum var. granulatum

Piper cordulatum var. granulatum, commonly known as the granulate pepper plant, is a tropical climbing shrub native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, granular, waxy leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by small, round berries that turn from green to red as they mature.

Its aromatic leaves and fruits are used in culinary and medicinal applications, and it thrives in humid, shaded environments with well-drained soil.
