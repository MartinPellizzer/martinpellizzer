Piper celtidiforme

Piper celtidiforme, commonly known as the West Indian pepper or tropical pepper, is a climbing shrub native to the Caribbean and Central America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many pepper species. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are followed by elongated, berry-like fruits. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly serrated margin.

The plant is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
