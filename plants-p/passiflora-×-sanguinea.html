Passiflora × sanguinea

Passiflora × sanguinea, commonly known as the purple passionflower, is a hybrid species native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful inflorescences that display a mix of red, purple, and white hues. The plant produces edible berries that are rich in antioxidants and have a tart, slightly sweet flavor. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Passiflora × sanguinea is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its aesthetic appeal and is also used in traditional medicine.
