Pictetia microphylla

Pictetia microphylla, commonly known as the small-leaved pictetia, is a flowering plant native to the Western Cape region of South Africa.

It is a small shrub that typically grows in coastal fynbos vegetation. The plant features narrow, linear leaves and produces clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers. It is known for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in well-drained, sandy soils.

Pictetia microphylla is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
