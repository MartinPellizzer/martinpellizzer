Paspalum arundinaceum

Paspalum arundinaceum, commonly known as tallgrass paspalum, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, grass-like leaves. This plant typically grows in moist or wet environments, such as marshes and riparian areas. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers.

Paspalum arundinaceum is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for wildlife.
