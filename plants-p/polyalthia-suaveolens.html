Polyalthia suaveolens

Polyalthia suaveolens, commonly known as the sweet-scented flame tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, fiery red flowers that bloom in clusters, often attracting a variety of pollinators. The plant has pinnately compound leaves and produces small, woody fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The tree's fragrant flowers contribute to its popularity in horticultural settings.
