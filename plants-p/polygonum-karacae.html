Polygonum karacae

Polygonum karacae, commonly known as Karacae knotweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, which is characterized by its distinctive swollen nodes and often triangular stems. The plant typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and can be found in areas such as riverbanks and meadows. Its leaves are alternate, ovate to lanceolate, and often have a reddish tint, while the flowers are small and clustered in dense inflorescences.

Polygonum karacae is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
