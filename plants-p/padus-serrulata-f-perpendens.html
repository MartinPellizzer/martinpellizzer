Padus serrulata f. perpendens

Padus serrulata f. perpendens, commonly known as the slender blackthorn, is a shrub native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its small, dark purple to black berries that ripen in late summer. The plant has dark green, oval-shaped leaves with serrated edges, and its flowers are small, white, and clustered in racemes. It prefers well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or hedgerows.

This variety is notable for its compact growth habit and ornamental value in gardens.
