Pseudocytisus integrifolius subsp. glabrescens

Pseudocytisus integrifolius subsp.

glabrescens, commonly known as the smooth broom, is a shrub native to southern Europe. It features slender, branching stems covered in fine, hairy pubescence that becomes smoother with age.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a smooth, glossy surface.

It prefers well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or rocky habitats.
