Palaquium pinnatinervium

Palaquium pinnatinervium, commonly known as the pinnate-veined palaquium, is a small evergreen tree native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves with pinnate venation. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences, followed by fleshy, orange-red berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists.
