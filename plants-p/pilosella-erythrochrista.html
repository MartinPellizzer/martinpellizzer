Pilosella erythrochrista

Pilosella erythrochrista, commonly known as the red-christed pilosella, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage and small, yellowish-white flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, grassy areas and is native to regions of Europe and western Asia. Its flowers bloom in the summer months, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Pilosella erythrochrista is often found in meadows and along stream banks, where it thrives in well-drained soil.
