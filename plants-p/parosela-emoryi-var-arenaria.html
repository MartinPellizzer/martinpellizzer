Parosela emoryi var. arenaria

Parosela emoryi var. arenaria, commonly known as the desert parosela, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its small, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink, often blooming in late spring. The plant has adapted to harsh environments with features such as hairy leaves and deep root systems to conserve water. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in desert scrub and chaparral habitats.

Its botanical significance lies in its role as a food source for local pollinators and its resilience in extreme climates.
