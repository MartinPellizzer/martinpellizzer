Pulsatilla × borosiana

Pulsatilla × borosiana, commonly known as the hybrid pasqueflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Ranunculaceae family.

It is characterized by its showy, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and yellow, typically blooming in early spring. The plant has a short, stout stem with basal leaves that are palmately divided, and it produces follicles containing numerous seeds. Native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, it thrives in well-drained, rocky soils.

Due to its ornamental value and limited natural range, it is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
