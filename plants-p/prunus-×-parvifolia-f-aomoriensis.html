Prunus × parvifolia f. aomoriensis

Prunus × parvifolia f. aomoriensis, commonly known as the Aomori cherry, is a unique cultivar of the Prunus genus.

It is characterized by its small, oval leaves that are typically a glossy green, providing an attractive contrast to its delicate pink to white flowers. The plant is native to Japan and is prized for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal and is a popular choice for ornamental planting in spring.
