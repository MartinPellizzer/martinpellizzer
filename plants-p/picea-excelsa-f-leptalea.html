Picea excelsa f. leptalea

Picea excelsa f. leptalea, commonly known as the slender white spruce, is a botanical variant of the white spruce species.

It is characterized by its narrow, conical shape and relatively slow growth rate compared to other spruce varieties. The foliage is dense and typically has a silvery-white appearance due to the presence of fine, wax-coated needles. This form is often found in alpine or subalpine regions where it adapts to harsh environmental conditions.

Its botanical significance lies in its ecological role and potential for use in reforestation projects in high-altitude areas.
