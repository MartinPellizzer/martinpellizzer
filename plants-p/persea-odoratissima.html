Persea odoratissima

Persea odoratissima, commonly known as the fragrant persimmon, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central America and the Caribbean.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and showy white flowers that emit a strong, sweet fragrance. The tree produces small, orange-red fruits that are edible and have a sweet, honey-like flavor when ripe. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its aromatic properties.
