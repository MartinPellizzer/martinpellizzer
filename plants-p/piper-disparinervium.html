Piper disparinervium

Piper disparinervium, commonly known as the veined pepper plant, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil and Peru. The plant is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and heart-shaped leaves with prominent veins. Its small, greenish-white flowers are borne in clusters and are followed by small, round berries.

Piper disparinervium is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has potential uses in traditional medicine.
