Picea asperata var. notabilis

Picea asperata var. notabilis, commonly known as the Himalayan fir, is a variant of the asperata fir native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

This coniferous tree is characterized by its dark green, needle-like leaves that are rough to the touch, giving it its species name "asperata.". The cones of this plant are typically pendulous and can reach lengths of up to 10 centimeters. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in subalpine and alpine regions.

The tree plays a significant role in the ecological systems of its native habitat, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
