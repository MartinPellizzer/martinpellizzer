Polygonum punctatum var. mucronatum

Polygonum punctatum var. mucronatum, commonly known as the spiked smartweed, is a herbaceous annual plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its upright, branched stems and small, greenish-white flowers that appear in clusters. The leaves are lance-shaped with prominent veins and often have a slightly hairy texture. This plant thrives in moist soils, often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of water bodies.

It plays a role in providing habitat and food for various insects and wildlife.
