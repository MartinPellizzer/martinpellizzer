Podotheca pollackii

Podotheca pollackii, commonly known as the yellow bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces showy, trumpet-shaped yellow flowers. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

The fruit is a capsule that releases small, wind-dispersed seeds.
