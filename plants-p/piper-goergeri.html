Piper goergeri

Piper goergeri, commonly known as the Java pepper or long pepper, is a climbing vine native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its small, peppercorn-like berries. The plant produces elongated, dark red fruits that are used in traditional medicine and as a spice. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a glossy surface.

The plant thrives in tropical climates and requires a humid environment for optimal growth.
