Pappophorum virens

Pappophorum virens, commonly known as the green-headed grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically green in color. The plant produces small, greenish flowers in dense clusters at the ends of its stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands and along riverbanks.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
