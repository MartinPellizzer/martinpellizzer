Potentilla tabernaemontani subvar. quinata

Potentilla tabernaemontani subvar. quinata, commonly known as the alpine cinquefoil, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It features small, yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, typically arranged in clusters of five. The plant has slender, upright stems and hairy leaves that are often oval or lance-shaped. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to cold, alpine environments.

This subvariety is notable for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its hardiness and visual appeal.
