Psychanthus oppositifolius

Psychanthus oppositifolius, commonly known as the opposite-leaved psychanthus, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its opposite arrangement of leaves, which are typically ovate to lanceolate in shape. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are often clustered in terminal inflorescences. Its flowering period generally occurs during the wet season, and it is adapted to thrive in humid, shaded environments.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
