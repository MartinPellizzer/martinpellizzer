Psyllium annuum

Psyllium annuum, commonly known as the common plantain, is a flowering plant in the family Plantaginaceae.

It is native to Europe, North Africa, and parts of Asia, and it thrives in temperate climates. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers and has broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. Its seeds, which are rich in soluble fiber, are commonly used as a laxative and in dietary supplements.

The plant is also valued for its ability to grow in poor soil conditions, making it a hardy and versatile species.
