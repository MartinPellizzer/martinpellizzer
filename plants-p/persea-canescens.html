Persea canescens

Persea canescens, commonly known as the Mexican persimmon, is a small evergreen tree native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that are often covered in a fine, whitish pubescence. The tree produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by orange to reddish fruits that are edible when fully ripe. The fruit has a sweet, sometimes astringent flavor and is often used in local cuisines.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
