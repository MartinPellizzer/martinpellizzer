Pyrus tushanensis

Pyrus tushanensis, commonly known as the Tushan pear, is a rare species native to China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its small, clustered flowers and edible fruits. The plant typically grows in mountainous regions and is adapted to temperate climates. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a serrated margin.

The fruit is a small, yellowish pear that ripens in late summer.
