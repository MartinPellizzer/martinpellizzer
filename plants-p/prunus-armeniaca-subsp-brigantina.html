Prunus armeniaca subsp. brigantina

Prunus armeniaca subsp.

brigantina, commonly known as the Armenian apricot, is a subspecies of the apricot tree native to the Caucasus region. It is characterized by its small, yellowish-orange fruits that are typically harvested in late summer.

The plant has a moderate growth habit and produces fragrant white flowers in early spring. Its leaves are oval-shaped with a slightly serrated margin, and the bark is smooth and grayish in color.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and is also used in traditional medicine.
