Polygala vulgaris subsp. multicaulis

Polygala vulgaris subsp.

multicaulis, commonly known as the common milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry grasslands and rocky soils.

The plant features slender, branched stems and small, clustered flowers that are usually pale yellow or greenish in color. Its leaves are alternate and often have a hairy texture, contributing to its ability to thrive in arid conditions.

The species is known for its ecological importance, providing nectar for pollinators and serving as a food source for various herbivores.
