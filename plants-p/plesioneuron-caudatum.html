Plesioneuron caudatum

Plesioneuron caudatum, commonly known as the long-tailed plesioneuron, is a unique member of the fern family.

It is characterized by its elongated, drooping fronds that can reach several meters in length. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, typically found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its reproductive structures are borne on specialized sporangia, which are clustered along the fronds.

The botanical features of Plesioneuron caudatum make it an interesting subject for study in both ecological and evolutionary contexts.
