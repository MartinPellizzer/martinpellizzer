Prunus mutabilis f. suaveolens

Prunus mutabilis f. suaveolens, commonly known as the sweet-scented cherry, is a cultivar of the Prunus mutabilis species, which is native to China.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white to pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows as a small tree or shrub, reaching heights of up to 6 meters. Its leaves are ovate with a slightly serrated margin, and the fruit is a small, sweet, edible cherry.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and aromatic foliage.
