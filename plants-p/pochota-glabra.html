Pochota glabra

Pochota glabra, commonly known as the smooth pochota, is a climbing plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Leguminosae family and is characterized by its smooth, glabrous stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by elongated, pod-like fruits containing several seeds. Pochota glabra is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used traditionally in some indigenous communities.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive member of the legume family with ecological and cultural significance.
