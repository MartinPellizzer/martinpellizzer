Parsonsia spiralis

Parsonsia spiralis, commonly known as the spiral glorybower, is a striking tropical shrub native to the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its vibrant orange to red flowers that bloom in clusters, often with a distinctive spiral arrangement of petals. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are usually arranged in a whorled pattern around the stem. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Parsonsia spiralis is also noted for its ability to attract pollinators such as birds and bees.
