Pisonia suspensa

Pisonia suspensa, commonly known as the beach hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its woody structure and showy, pink or red flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with a rough texture and a strong, pleasant fragrance. It thrives in sandy soils and is often found in dune environments, where it plays a role in stabilizing coastal ecosystems.

Pisonia suspensa is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
