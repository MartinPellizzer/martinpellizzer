Piptadenia macrocarpa var. genuina

Piptadenia macrocarpa var. genuina, commonly known as the Brazilian peach tree, is a deciduous tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The tree produces large, fleshy fruits that are edible and have a sweet, peach-like flavor. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental and culinary value.

The plant is also valued for its potential medicinal properties.
