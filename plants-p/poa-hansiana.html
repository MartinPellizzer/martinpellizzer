Poa hansiana

Poa hansiana, commonly known as the Hansen's bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the Pacific Northwest of the United States.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and produces small, spikelet-bearing inflorescences. The plant typically thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and meadows. Its foliage is fine and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue that gives it its common name.

Poa hansiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in native plant communities.
