Pelargonium aromaticum

Pelargonium aromaticum, commonly known as the sweet geranium, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its aromatic, velvety leaves and clusters of small, showy flowers that range in color from pink to red. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub, reaching heights of up to 3 feet. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and containers.

The fragrance of its leaves and flowers makes it a valuable addition to aromatic herb gardens.
