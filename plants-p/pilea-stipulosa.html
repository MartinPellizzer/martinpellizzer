Pilea stipulosa

Pilea stipulosa, commonly known as the Shingle Plant, is a species of flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Araceae and is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit. The plant features heart-shaped, glossy leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white wax, giving it a unique appearance. Pilea stipulosa produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-white in color.

It is a popular houseplant due to its ease of care and attractive foliage.
