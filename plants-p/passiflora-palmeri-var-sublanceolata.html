Passiflora palmeri var. sublanceolata

Passiflora palmeri var. sublanceolata, commonly known as the Palmer's passionflower, is a rare and distinctive species native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its striking, complex flowers with five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments surrounding the reproductive structures. The leaves are typically ovate to elliptic in shape, with a smooth or slightly toothed margin. This variety exhibits a unique combination of morphological traits that distinguish it from other Passiflora species.

Its botanical significance lies in its ecological role and potential medicinal properties.
