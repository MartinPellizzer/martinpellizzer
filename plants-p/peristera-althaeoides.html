Peristera althaeoides

Peristera althaeoides, commonly known as the althaeoid dandelion, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, silver-green leaves and bright yellow composite flowers. Native to southern Europe and western Asia, it thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. The plant produces a pappus-covered seed that aids in wind dispersal.

Its botanical features make it an important component of its native ecosystems.
