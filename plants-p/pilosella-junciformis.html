Pilosella junciformis

Pilosella junciformis, commonly known as the tufted cat's-paw, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in open habitats such as grasslands and woodland edges. The plant features small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, with distinctive, hairy leaves that give it its common name. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads.

Pilosella junciformis is often used in rock gardens due to its hardy nature and ornamental value.
