Phyllanthus macrocalyx

Phyllanthus macrocalyx, commonly known as the large-flowered phyllanthus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Phyllanthaceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific Islands. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and open woodlands.

The plant is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value or used in traditional medicine.
