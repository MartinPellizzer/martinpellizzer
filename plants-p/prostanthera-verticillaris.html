Prostanthera verticillaris

Prostanthera verticillaris, commonly known as the clustered mintbush, is a perennial shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It features small, aromatic, white to pale purple flowers that bloom in clusters, typically from spring to autumn. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It is well adapted to dry, sandy soils and is often found in heathlands and open woodlands.

Prostanthera verticillaris is also valued for its fragrant properties and is sometimes used in herbal remedies.
