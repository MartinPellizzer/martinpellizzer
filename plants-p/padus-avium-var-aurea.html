Padus avium var. aurea

Padus avium var. aurea, commonly known as the golden bird cherry.

This variant of the bird cherry tree is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers, which bloom in early spring. The leaves are typically oval and have a smooth texture, while the bark is smooth and light gray in color. It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that thrives in temperate climates.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
