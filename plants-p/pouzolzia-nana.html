Pouzolzia nana

Pouzolzia nana, commonly known as dwarf pouzolzia, is a small shrub native to the Canary Islands.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense foliage, typically reaching heights of less than 30 centimeters. The plant features small, dark green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces tiny, inconspicuous white flowers. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to the dry, sunny conditions of its native habitat.

Pouzolzia nana is often found in coastal areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience.
