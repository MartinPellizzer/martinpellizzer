Piper breviamentum

Piper breviamentum, commonly known as the short-stemmed pepper plant, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piper genus, which includes many species of flowering plants in the family Piperaceae. The plant is characterized by its small, clustered leaves and produces tiny, berry-like fruits that are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Due to its unique botanical features, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists alike.
