Pyrus autumnalis var. culta

Pyrus autumnalis var. culta, commonly known as the cultivated pear tree, is a variety of the common pear species.

It is characterized by its moderate growth habit and ornamental value, with attractive foliage and showy white flowers in spring. The fruit is typically smaller and less sweet compared to other pear varieties, making it more suitable for ornamental purposes than culinary use. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This cultivar is often used in landscaping for its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to various garden settings.
