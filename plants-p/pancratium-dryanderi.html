Pancratium dryanderi

Pancratium dryanderi, commonly known as the dryander's torchflower, is a striking bulbous plant native to southern Africa.

It features tall, strap-like leaves and produces showy, yellow-orange inflorescences that resemble torches. The plant is adapted to arid environments, with a deep root system that allows it to survive in dry conditions. Its flowers bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds.

Pancratium dryanderi is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow in well-drained soil.
