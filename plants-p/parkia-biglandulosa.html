Parkia biglandulosa

Parkia biglandulosa, commonly known as the African flame tree, is a tropical leguminous plant native to Africa and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, flame-like inflorescences that display vibrant red and orange hues. The plant produces flat, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds embedded in a fibrous pulp. Its leaves are bipinnate, consisting of multiple small leaflets, and it has a deep root system that helps stabilize soil.

Parkia biglandulosa is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its showy flowers and ecological benefits.
