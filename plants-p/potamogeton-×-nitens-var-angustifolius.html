Potamogeton × nitens var. angustifolius

Potamogeton × nitens var. angustifolius, commonly known as narrow-leaved pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant characterized by its slender, linear leaves that are typically less than 1 cm wide.

It is native to freshwater habitats in North America and is often found in slow-moving water or still ponds. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in spikes, which are usually submerged or partially emergent. Its rhizomes form dense underwater mats, contributing to water clarity by stabilizing sediments.

This species plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
