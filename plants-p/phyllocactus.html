Phyllocactus

Phyllocactus, commonly known as the candlestick cactus, is a genus of succulent plants native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that resemble candlesticks, often covered in a waxy cuticle to reduce water loss. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. Phyllocactus species are adapted to arid environments and require minimal water and well-draining soil.

They are popular among succulent enthusiasts for their unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
