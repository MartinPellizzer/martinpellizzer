Perlaria

Perlaria, commonly known as the sacred lotus, is a water-loving plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Nymphaeaceae family and is characterized by its large, showy flowers that bloom above the water surface. The plant has broad, floating leaves that help it photosynthesize efficiently in aquatic environments. Its roots anchor it in the mud at the bottom of ponds and lakes.

The botanical features of perlaria make it an important species in both ecological and cultural contexts.
