Piptadenia peregrina var. falcata

Piptadenia peregrina var. falcata, commonly known as the Brazilian cecropia, is a tropical tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and distinctive, pendulous flowers. The plant produces elongated pods that contain seeds surrounded by a fibrous, cotton-like material. It is often found in disturbed habitats and is valued for its rapid growth and ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.

The species is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
