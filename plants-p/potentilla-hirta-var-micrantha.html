Potentilla hirta var. micrantha

Potentilla hirta var. micrantha, commonly known as the hairy cinquefoil, is a low-growing perennial shrub native to the Arctic and subarctic regions.

It features small, yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, typically arranged in clusters of five. The plant has hairy stems and leaves with a rough texture, adapted to harsh, cold environments. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in tundra and alpine habitats.

Its botanical characteristics make it a hardy species with ecological importance in its native range.
