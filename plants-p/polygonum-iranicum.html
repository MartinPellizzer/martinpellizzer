Polygonum iranicum

Polygonum iranicum, commonly known as Persian knotweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the temperate regions of the Middle East and Central Asia.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, which includes plants known for their distinctive arrow-shaped leaves and clusters of small, often pink or red flowers. The plant typically grows in moist soils along riverbanks, meadows, and woodland edges. Its leaves are usually heart-shaped with a smooth or slightly toothed margin, and it produces erect, branched stems that bear dense inflorescences.

Polygonum iranicum is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
