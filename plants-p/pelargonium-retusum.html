Pelargonium retusum

Pelargonium retusum, commonly known as the African geranium, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of pink, red, and white. The leaves are typically rounded with a slightly waxy texture, and the plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for gardens and containers.

Pelargonium retusum is also valued for its ability to thrive in dry conditions, showcasing its adaptability to arid environments.
