Prunus mutabilis f. diversipes

Prunus mutabilis f. diversipes, commonly known as the Variable Cherry, is a unique cultivar of the Prunus genus.

This plant is characterized by its strikingly variable flower colors, which can range from white to pink to red depending on environmental conditions and the age of the flowers. The leaves are typically ovate with a smooth margin and a glossy dark green surface. It produces small, edible fruits that are similar to cherries but are often used more for ornamental purposes.

The plant thrives in temperate climates and is valued for its aesthetic appeal in gardens and landscapes.
