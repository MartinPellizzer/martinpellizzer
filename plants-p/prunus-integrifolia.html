Prunus integrifolia

Prunus integrifolia, commonly known as the Japanese cherry or Japanese yuzu, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, reddish bark and lance-shaped leaves that are entire, giving it its species name. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in early spring, followed by edible berries that are used in traditional medicine and culinary applications. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit.

Prunus integrifolia is also valued for its role in ecological systems, providing food for wildlife and supporting biodiversity.
