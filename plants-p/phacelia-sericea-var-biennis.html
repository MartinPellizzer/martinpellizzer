Phacelia sericea var. biennis

Phacelia sericea var. biennis, commonly known as the silverleaf phacelia, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its nectar-rich flowers attract a variety of pollinators, including bees and butterflies.
