Pyrus puellarum

Pyrus puellarum, commonly known as the dwarf pear, is a small deciduous tree or shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It typically grows to a height of about 2 to 5 meters, featuring a compact, rounded form with dense foliage. The leaves are alternate, simple, and ovate, with a serrated margin and a glossy dark green upper surface. Its flowers are white and borne in clusters, followed by small, edible pears that are usually yellow or green in color.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic and ecological benefits.
