Pyrus kopetdagensis

Pyrus kopetdagensis, commonly known as the Kopetdagan pear, is a species of tree native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its deciduous nature, producing small, oval-shaped fruits. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid regions, adapting to harsh environmental conditions. Its leaves are alternate and ovate, with a rough texture, while its flowers are white and fragrant.

The fruit is edible, though it is not widely cultivated for commercial purposes.
