Persicaria fauriei

Persicaria fauriei, commonly known as the Faurie knotweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to eastern Asia.

It features upright stems that are often reddish in color, and its leaves are ovate to lanceolate, with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in dense clusters, typically blooming in late summer. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodlands or along stream banks.

Persicaria fauriei is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in wetland habitats.
