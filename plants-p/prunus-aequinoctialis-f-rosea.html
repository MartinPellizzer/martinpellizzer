Prunus aequinoctialis f. rosea

Prunus aequinoctialis f. rosea, commonly known as the pink-flowered Japanese cherry, is a cultivar of the Prunus aequinoctialis species, which is native to Japan.

It is characterized by its striking pink flowers that bloom in early spring, adding a vibrant splash of color to gardens and natural landscapes. The plant is a deciduous shrub or small tree, typically reaching heights of 3 to 6 meters. Its leaves are oval-shaped with a smooth margin and turn a brilliant red in the fall.

The botanical features of this cultivar make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening due to its aesthetic appeal and seasonal interest.
