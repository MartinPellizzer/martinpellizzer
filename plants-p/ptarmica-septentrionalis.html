Ptarmica septentrionalis

Ptarmica septentrionalis, commonly known as the northern sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the northern regions of North America.

It typically grows in wetland habitats, such as bogs and marshes, where it thrives in moist, acidic soils. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers in early summer. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense colonies.

Ptarmica septentrionalis plays an important role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
