Polypodium hemionitideum

Polypodium hemionitideum, commonly known as the hemionite fern, is a species of epiphytic fern native to the mountainous regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped fronds that are typically divided into small, serrated segments. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and often grows on tree trunks or rocks in tropical and subtropical climates. Its rhizomes are fibrous and help anchor it to its substrate while absorbing moisture and nutrients from the air.

Polypodium hemionitideum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
