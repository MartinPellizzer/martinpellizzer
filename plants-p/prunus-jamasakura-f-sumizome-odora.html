Prunus jamasakura f. sumizome-odora

Prunus jamasakura f. sumizome-odora, commonly known as the fragrant form of the Japanese cherry blossom, is a botanical variant of the Prunus jamasakura species.

It is characterized by its delicate pink flowers that bloom in early spring, often preceding the more commonly recognized Prunus serrulata. The plant is native to Japan and is cultivated for its ornamental value, particularly in gardens and parks. Its leaves are oval-shaped with a smooth texture and a dark green hue.

The 'sumizome-odora' cultivar is distinguished by its subtle, sweet fragrance, which adds an extra sensory dimension to its aesthetic appeal.
