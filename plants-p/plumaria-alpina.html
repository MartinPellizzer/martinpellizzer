Plumaria alpina

Plumaria alpina, commonly known as the alpine plum or alpine orchid, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stem and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky slopes, thriving in cool, moist environments. Its flowers, which are usually pink or white, bloom in late spring to early summer.

Plumaria alpina is adapted to high-altitude conditions, with a short growing season and specialized root systems to withstand harsh weather.
