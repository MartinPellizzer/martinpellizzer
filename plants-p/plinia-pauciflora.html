Plinia pauciflora

Plinia pauciflora, commonly known as the few-flowered myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its small, aromatic leaves and showy white or pink flowers. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, such as forest understories, and prefers well-drained, acidic soils. Its fruit is a small, fleshy berry that contains several seeds, and it plays a role in local ecosystems by providing food for birds and small mammals.

Plinia pauciflora is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
