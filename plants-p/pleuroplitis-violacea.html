Pleuroplitis violacea

Pleuroplitis violacea, commonly known as the violet-leaf pleurothallis, is a member of the orchid family, Orchidaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of Central and South America, where it thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant is characterized by its small, delicate leaves that often display a striking violet or purple hue. Its flowers are typically inconspicuous, with a greenish-white color and a subtle fragrance.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in the wild.
