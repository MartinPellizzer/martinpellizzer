Potentilla Utahensis

Utah cinquefoil, scientifically known as Potentilla utahensis, is a rare and delicate flowering plant native to the arid regions of Utah. This species is part of the rose family and is characterized by its bright yellow blooms and finely divided leaves. Found primarily in dry, rocky habitats, it thrives in environments with minimal moisture and intense sunlight. Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, Utah cinquefoil is considered endangered and requires conservation efforts to ensure its survival. Its unique beauty and ecological importance make it a valuable subject for botanical study and environmental protection.
