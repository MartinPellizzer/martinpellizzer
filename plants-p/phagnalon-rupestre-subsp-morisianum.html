Phagnalon rupestre subsp. morisianum

Phagnalon rupestre subsp.

morisianum, commonly known as the Morisian rock everlasting, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region. It typically grows in rocky or limestone soils, thriving in sunny, well-drained environments.

The plant features small, tightly packed blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its leaves are narrow and often covered in a fine layer of white hairs, which help reduce water loss.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often used in rock gardens and dry landscapes.
