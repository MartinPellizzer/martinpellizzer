Poa howellii

Poa howellii, commonly known as Howell's bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its fine, blue-green foliage and dense, tufted growth habit. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along streambanks. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, and it produces small, spike-like inflorescences.

Poa howellii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in native landscapes.
