Polypogon imberbis

Polypogon imberbis, commonly known as the hairless panicgrass, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as wet meadows and marshes. The plant has slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are usually hairless. Its inflorescence consists of a single, spike-like cluster of small, two-lobed flowers.

Polypogon imberbis is characterized by its ability to thrive in wet environments and its distinctive floral structure.
