Passiflora translinearis

Passiflora translinearis, commonly known as the linear leaf passionflower, is a species native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, intricate flowers that feature a crown of filaments and a central cluster of stamens. The plant produces edible, tropical fruits that are often used in culinary applications. Its leaves are typically narrow and linear, giving it its common name.

Passiflora translinearis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow in warm climates.
