Prunus × makinoana

Prunus × makinoana, commonly known as the Japanese cherry or Makino cherry, is a hybrid species native to Japan.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its small, ornamental flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are oval-shaped with serrated edges, and it produces small, dark red fruits in the fall.

This species is widely cultivated for its aesthetic value in gardens and landscapes.
