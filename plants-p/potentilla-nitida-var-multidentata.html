Potentilla nitida var. multidentata

Potentilla nitida var. multidentata, commonly known as the silverleaf potentilla, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its finely divided, silver-gray leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, yellow flowers in early summer, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Its hardy nature and ornamental value make it a popular choice in horticulture.
