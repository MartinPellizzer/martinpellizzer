Polygonum convolvulus f. puberulum

Polygonum convolvulus f. puberulum, commonly known as the hairy bladder campion, is a variant of the common bladder campion.

It is a flowering plant in the family Polygonaceae, characterized by its prostrate growth habit and hairy stems. The leaves are ovate to lanceolate, with a rough texture, and the flowers are small, pinkish, and clustered in racemes. This subspecies is often found in disturbed soils and along pathways, thriving in temperate climates.

Its hairy foliage distinguishes it from other forms of Polygonum convolvulus.
