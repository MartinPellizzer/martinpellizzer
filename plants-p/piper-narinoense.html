Piper narinoense

Piper narinoense, commonly known as the Narino pepper or Colombian pepper, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to the Andean regions of Colombia and Ecuador, where it grows in humid, tropical forests. The plant is characterized by its trailing vines and heart-shaped leaves, which are often used in traditional medicine. Its small, green berries contain a spicy compound similar to capsaicin, giving it a peppery flavor.

Due to its unique properties, it is sometimes cultivated for both ornamental and medicinal purposes.
