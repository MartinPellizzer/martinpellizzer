Polygala petenensis

Polygala petenensis, commonly known as the Mexican milkweed, is a flowering plant native to Central America and parts of the southern United States.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its showy, spathaceous inflorescence that resembles a single flower. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a velvety texture, while its flowers are usually pink or purple.

Polygala petenensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
