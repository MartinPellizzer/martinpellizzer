Picris eichleri

Picris eichleri, commonly known as the common tansy, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is native to Europe, North Africa, and parts of Asia, and is characterized by its yellow-orange composite flowers and feathery leaves. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and can be found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas. It has a biennial or short-lived perennial life cycle, and its seeds are dispersed by wind.

Due to its medicinal properties, it has been historically used in herbal remedies, though it contains toxic compounds that require careful handling.
