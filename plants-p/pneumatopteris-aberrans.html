Pneumatopteris aberrans

Pneumatopteris aberrans, commonly known as the aberrant water fern, is a species of aquatic fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, floating leaves that are typically divided into narrow, ribbon-like segments. The plant thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, often found in ponds and marshes. Its reproductive structures, including sori, are located on the underside of the fronds, facilitating spore dispersal.

The botanical features of Pneumatopteris aberrans make it an important species in the study of aquatic plant ecology and evolution.
