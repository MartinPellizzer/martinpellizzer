Paratropia eurhyncha

Paratropia eurhyncha, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety pubescence. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters. Its fruit is a dry, three-lobed capsule that contains several seeds.

Paratropia eurhyncha is known for its rapid growth and is considered an invasive species in many areas where it is introduced.
