Phyllanthus symplocoides

Phyllanthus symplocoides, commonly known as the Indian gooseberry or amla, is a deciduous shrub native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are often elliptical or ovate in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by bright orange to red berries. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

The plant is widely cultivated for its nutritional and medicinal properties.
