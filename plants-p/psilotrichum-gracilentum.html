Psilotrichum gracilentum

Psilotrichum gracilentum, commonly known as the slender whisk fern, is a fern-like plant belonging to the family Psilotaceae.

It is characterized by its simple, unbranched stems and small, scale-like leaves. The plant lacks true roots, leaves, and vascular tissue, which is a unique feature among ferns. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often in tropical and subtropical regions.

Psilotrichum gracilentum is considered a primitive plant, providing insights into early plant evolution.
