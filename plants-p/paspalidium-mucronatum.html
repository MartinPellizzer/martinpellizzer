Paspalidium mucronatum

Paspalidium mucronatum, commonly known as the narrowleaf grass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It typically grows in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas, often forming dense stands. The plant has slender, wiry stems and narrow, grass-like leaves with a pointed tip. Its inflorescence consists of a spike-like cluster of small, two-lobed spikelets.

Paspalidium mucronatum is adapted to a variety of soil types and is often found in regions with moderate to high rainfall.
