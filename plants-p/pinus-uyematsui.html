Pinus uyematsui

Pinus uyematsui, commonly known as the Japanese white pine, is a species of coniferous evergreen tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its slender, often twisted trunk and its long, needle-like leaves that are typically bluish-green in color. The tree produces cones that are cylindrical and slightly curved, containing small, oval seeds. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in mountainous regions with well-drained soils.

Pinus uyematsui is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional Japanese gardens.
