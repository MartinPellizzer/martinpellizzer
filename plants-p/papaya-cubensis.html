Papaya cubensis

Papaya cubensis, commonly known as the Cuban papaya, is a tropical fruiting plant belonging to the Caricaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, broad leaves and a distinctive, soft, yellow-orange fruit that ripens quickly. The fruit contains a central cavity filled with a sweet, edible pulp and black seeds. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

It is cultivated for its nutritional value and is rich in enzymes like papain, which aid in digestion.
