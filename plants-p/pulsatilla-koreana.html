Pulsatilla koreana

Pulsatilla koreana, commonly known as the Korean pasqueflower, is a perennial herb native to Korea.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its showy, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils in alpine or subalpine regions. Its leaves are palmately divided, and it produces follicles as its fruit type.

Pulsatilla koreana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
