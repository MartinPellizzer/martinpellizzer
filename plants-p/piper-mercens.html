Piper mercens

Piper mercens, commonly known as the golden pepper plant, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes over 3,000 species of flowering plants. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves and small, greenish-white flowers that develop into peppery fruits. The fruits are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for both horticultural study and ecological research.
