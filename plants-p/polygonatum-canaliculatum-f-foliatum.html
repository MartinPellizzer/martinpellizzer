Polygonatum canaliculatum f. foliatum

Polygonatum canaliculatum f. foliatum, commonly known as the leafy Solomon's seal, is a variant of the Solomon's seal plant characterized by its distinct foliage.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern Asia, particularly China. The plant features long, arching stems with broad, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern. It produces small, greenish-white flowers in early summer, followed by red berries that ripen in the fall.

This variety is valued for its ornamental foliage and is often cultivated in shaded garden environments.
