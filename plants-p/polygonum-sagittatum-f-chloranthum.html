Polygonum sagittatum f. chloranthum

Polygonum sagittatum f. chloranthum, commonly known as the yellow arrowhead, is a variant of the arrowhead plant characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in wetlands and along the edges of ponds and streams. The plant features heart-shaped leaves that are often submerged or floating, contributing to its aquatic habitat. Its flowers are clustered in spikes and bloom in late spring to early summer.

This variant is notable for its striking coloration and is often used in wetland restoration projects.
