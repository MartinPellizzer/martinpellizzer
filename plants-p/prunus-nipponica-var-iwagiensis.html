Prunus nipponica var. iwagiensis

Prunus nipponica var. iwagiensis, commonly known as the Iwagi cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its delicate, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in early spring, often before the leaves emerge. The leaves are ovate with a finely toothed margin and turn vibrant shades of red or orange in the fall. This variety is prized for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for ornamental horticulture.
