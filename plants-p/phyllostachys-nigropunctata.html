Phyllostachys nigropunctata

Phyllostachys nigropunctata, commonly known as the black dot bamboo, is a species of grass belonging to the Bambusoideae subfamily.

It is native to eastern Asia, particularly China, where it thrives in temperate climates. The plant is characterized by its dark green, slender culms that are often speckled with black dots, giving it its common name. It is a clump-forming bamboo that can reach heights of up to 10 meters, making it a striking ornamental plant.

Due to its hardiness and aesthetic appeal, it is widely cultivated in gardens and landscapes for its visual impact and ecological benefits.
