Paepalanthus schwackeanus var. glabrescens

Paepalanthus schwackeanus var. glabrescens, commonly known as the smooth swamp cactus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Cactaceae.

It is native to South America, particularly found in regions of Brazil and Argentina. The plant is characterized by its dense, cushion-like growth habit and features small, spiny stems covered with a waxy coating. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, appearing in clusters and blooming during the dry season.

This variety is adapted to wetland environments and plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and nectar for pollinators.
