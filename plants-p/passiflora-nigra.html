Passiflora nigra

Passiflora nigra, commonly known as Black Passionflower, is a climbing vine native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dark purple to black flowers, which are adorned with intricate patterns and a unique structure that includes a crown of filaments. The plant produces edible berries that are typically dark purple when ripe, though they are not widely cultivated for consumption. Its foliage consists of large, heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture.

Passiflora nigra is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is also studied for its potential medicinal properties.
