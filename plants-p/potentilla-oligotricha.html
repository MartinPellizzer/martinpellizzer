Potentilla oligotricha

Potentilla oligotricha, commonly known as the narrowleaf potentilla, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its small, clustered flowers that typically bloom in shades of white or pink. The plant has slender, hairy stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Due to its hardy nature and ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
