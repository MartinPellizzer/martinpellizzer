Pisonia uleana

Pisonia uleana, commonly known as the flame tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to the Caribbean and parts of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, bright red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters, often resembling flames. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are typically elliptical or ovate in shape. Its fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release small, winged seeds.

Pisonia uleana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can thrive in well-drained soils with full sun exposure.
