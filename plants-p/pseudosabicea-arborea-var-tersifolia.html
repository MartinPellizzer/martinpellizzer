Pseudosabicea arborea var. tersifolia

Pseudosabicea arborea var. tersifolia, commonly known as the hairy-leafed pseudosabicea, is a shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its lanceolate leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, typically blooming during the wet season. Its leaves are usually opposite and have a leathery texture, which helps it retain moisture in its native habitat.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique foliage.
