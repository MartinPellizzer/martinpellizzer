Ptychosperma seemannii

Ptychosperma seemannii, commonly known as the African giant candlestick, is a tall, palm-like plant native to tropical Africa.

It features a slender, unbranched trunk with a smooth, greyish-brown bark and large, pinnate leaves that can reach up to 3 meters in length. The plant produces small, white flowers followed by black, spherical berries that contain the seeds. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to grow in well-drained soils with full sun exposure.

Ptychosperma seemannii is also valued for its use in traditional medicine and as a source of fiber.
