Pachycladon exilis

Pachycladon exilis, commonly known as the slender leaf cycad, is a unique cycad species native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its narrow, elongated leaves that are arranged in a dense, feather-like pattern. The plant produces distinctive cones, with male and female cones typically growing on separate plants. Its slow growth rate and specific habitat requirements make it a rare and ecologically significant species.

Due to habitat loss and over-collection, Pachycladon exilis is considered endangered in its natural range.
