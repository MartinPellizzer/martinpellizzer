Plocama bucharica

Plocama bucharica, commonly known as the Turkish bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Turkey and the Caucasus region.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to violet, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds. Plocama bucharica thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens or woodland borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its attractive foliage and long-lasting blooms.
