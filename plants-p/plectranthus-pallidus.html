Plectranthus pallidus

Plectranthus pallidus, commonly known as the pale plectranthus, is a herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are typically grayish-green in color. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are usually pale purple or white, arranged in clusters. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Plectranthus pallidus is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its attractive foliage and ease of care.
