Papaver × nicholsonii

Papaver × nicholsonii, commonly known as the hybrid poppy, is a botanical hybrid resulting from the cross between Papaver rhoeas (common poppy) and Papaver orientale (oriental poppy).

It is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that typically range in color from red to orange, with a distinctive papery petals that curl as they age. The plant is herbaceous, growing from 30 to 60 centimeters in height, and features a hairy stem with alternate, lobed leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often found in gardens and disturbed habitats.

The seeds are contained in a capsule that splits open to release the seeds, facilitating its spread in the wild.
