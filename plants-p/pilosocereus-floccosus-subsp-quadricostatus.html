Pilosocereus floccosus subsp. quadricostatus

Pilosocereus floccosus subsp.

quadricostatus, commonly known as the white candelabra cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of South America. It is characterized by its tall, columnar stems that are covered in dense, white, woolly hairs.

The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers that bloom at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators. It is a slow-growing cactus that thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water.

Due to its striking appearance and unique adaptations, it is a popular choice for cactus enthusiasts and xeriscaping.
