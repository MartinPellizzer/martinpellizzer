Parsonsia grandiflora

Parsonsia grandiflora, commonly known as the flame lily, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its vibrant orange to red tubular flowers, which are typically clustered in racemes at the ends of its branches. The plant is a member of the Apocynaceae family, known for its showy blooms and often toxic sap. Its leaves are usually opposite, elliptical, and have a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

Parsonsia grandiflora thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
