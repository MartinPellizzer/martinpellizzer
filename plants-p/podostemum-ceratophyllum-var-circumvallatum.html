Podostemum ceratophyllum var. circumvallatum

Podostemum ceratophyllum var. circumvallatum, commonly known as the rock dodder, is a unique aquatic plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its small, delicate leaves and specialized roots that anchor it to rocky substrates in fast-flowing streams. The plant thrives in clear, oxygen-rich waters and is often found in shaded, rocky habitats. Its flowers are typically small and clustered, with a distinctive appearance that resembles a crown.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species in its native range.
