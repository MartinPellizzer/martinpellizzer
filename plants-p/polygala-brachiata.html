Polygala brachiata

Polygala brachiata, commonly known as the slender milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, delicate flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and mountainous regions. Its leaves are opposite and lance-shaped, often with a hairy texture.

The flowers, which are usually pink or white, are arranged in clusters and attract various pollinators like bees and butterflies.
