Polygonum korshinskyanum

Polygonum korshinskyanum, commonly known as the Korshinsky's knotweed, is a flowering plant species belonging to the Polygonaceae family.

It is native to regions of Asia, including China, Korea, and Japan, where it thrives in moist, shady environments. The plant features striking red or purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are heart-shaped with a smooth texture and a slightly waxy surface.

Polygonum korshinskyanum is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in partial shade.
