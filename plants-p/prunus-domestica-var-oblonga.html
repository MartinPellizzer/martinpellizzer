Prunus domestica var. oblonga

Prunus domestica var. oblonga, commonly known as the oval black cherry, is a variant of the common cherry tree.

It is characterized by its elongated, oval-shaped fruits that are typically darker in color compared to standard cherries. The plant produces small, dark purple leaves that are slightly pointed at the tips. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and its edible fruits.
