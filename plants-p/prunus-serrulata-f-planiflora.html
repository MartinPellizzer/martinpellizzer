Prunus serrulata f. planiflora

Prunus serrulata f. planiflora, commonly known as the flatleaf cherry, is a cultivar of the Japanese cherry tree.

It is characterized by its broad, flat leaves that contrast with the typical narrow leaves of the species. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in spring, often white or pale pink. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly planted in gardens.

This variety is relatively hardy and adapts well to a range of soil conditions.
