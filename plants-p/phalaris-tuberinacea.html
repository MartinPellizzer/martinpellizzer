Phalaris tuberinacea

Phalaris tuberinacea, commonly known as the tuberous phalaris, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, cylindrical stems that often bear small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant produces tuberous roots that serve as storage organs, enabling it to survive in dry conditions. Its leaves are typically narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green coloration.

Phalaris tuberinacea is often found in grasslands and disturbed soils, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
