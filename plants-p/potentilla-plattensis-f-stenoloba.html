Potentilla plattensis f. stenoloba

Potentilla plattensis f. stenoloba, commonly known as the narrow-leaved potentilla, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features small, oval leaves that are typically covered in a fine layer of white hairs, giving them a silvery appearance. The plant produces clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its compact growth habit and ornamental foliage make it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine displays.

It prefers well-drained soils and full sun, thriving in cooler, arid environments.
