Panicum oligochaete

Panicum oligochaete, commonly known as little barley, is a flowering plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is a member of the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny florets, typical of grasses. Its leaves are narrow and often have a bluish-green hue, and it is adapted to thrive in well-drained soils.

Panicum oligochaete is often found in disturbed areas and can serve as a pioneer species in ecological succession.
