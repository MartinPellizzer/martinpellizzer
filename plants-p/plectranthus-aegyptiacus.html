Plectranthus aegyptiacus

Plectranthus aegyptiacus, commonly known as Egyptian hedge.

It is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region and parts of North Africa. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with a velvety texture and a strong, mint-like aroma. It produces small, tubular flowers in shades of purple and pink, typically blooming in summer.

Plectranthus aegyptiacus is often used in ornamental gardening and has been historically valued for its medicinal properties.
