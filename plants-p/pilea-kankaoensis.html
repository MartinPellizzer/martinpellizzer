Pilea kankaoensis

Pilea kankaoensis, commonly known as the Chinese money plant, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to China.

It features rosettes of glossy, heart-shaped leaves that are dark green with a waxy texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers that bloom in spring, followed by red berries. It thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular houseplant.

Its unique leaf shape and low maintenance requirements contribute to its popularity among indoor gardeners.
