Phragmites laxiflorus

Phragmites laxiflorus, commonly known as the slender flower reed, is a tall, perennial grass species native to wetland environments.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and small, clustered flower heads that bloom in late summer. This plant thrives in moist soils and is often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. Its rhizomes form dense colonies, which can help stabilize soil and prevent erosion.

However, it is also considered an invasive species in some regions due to its rapid spread and competitive growth.
