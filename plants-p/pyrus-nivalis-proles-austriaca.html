Pyrus nivalis proles austriaca

Pyrus nivalis proles austriaca, commonly known as the Austrian white pear, is a cultivar of the common pear tree.

It is characterized by its pale, almost white fruit that ripens in late summer. The plant is deciduous, with serrated leaves that turn yellow in autumn. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and mild, sweet fruit flavor.
