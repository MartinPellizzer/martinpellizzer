Philibertia hypoleuca

Philibertia hypoleuca, commonly known as the white-leafed dandelion, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, silvery-green leaves. The plant produces small, yellow composite flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in grasslands and open woodlands.

Philibertia hypoleuca is adapted to arid conditions and is known for its medicinal properties in traditional African medicine.
