Panicum crus-galli f. mixtum

Panicum crus-galli f. mixtum, commonly known as millet grass or a type of foxtail millet, is a grass species belonging to the Poaceae family.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, branched inflorescences that resemble a foxtail. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and is often found in agricultural fields or along roadsides. Its seeds are small and can be dispersed by wind, contributing to its widespread distribution.

It is valued for its adaptability and is sometimes used as a forage crop or in traditional landscaping.
