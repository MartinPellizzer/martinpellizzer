Phragmites communis var. berlandieri

Phragmites communis var. berlandieri, commonly known as the western common reed, is a tall, perennial grass that thrives in wetland environments.

It is characterized by its dense growth habit and hollow, jointed stems that can reach heights of up to 3 meters. The plant produces fluffy, feathery plumes that are highly visible during the flowering season. It plays a crucial role in stabilizing shorelines and providing habitat for various wildlife species.

Botanically, it is distinguished by its rhizomatous growth and its ability to form large, monotypic stands in moist soils.
