Parodia microsperma var. rubriflora

Parodia microsperma var. rubriflora, commonly known as the red-flowered parodia, is a compact cactus native to Mexico.

It features a short, rounded stem with a smooth, pale green surface that often develops a reddish tinge when exposed to sunlight. The plant produces small, spiny stems that bear clusters of vibrant red flowers during the spring and summer months. Its foliage is adapted to arid conditions, with thick, fleshy stems that store water efficiently.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and ease of care, making it a popular choice for succulent enthusiasts.
