Potentilla bidens

Potentilla bidens, commonly known as common cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North America.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges. The plant has a sprawling growth habit with multiple stems that often root at the nodes. Its flowers are small, yellow, and clustered in groups of five, giving it the common name.

The leaves are palmately divided into five lobes, which are usually hairy and have a serrated edge.
