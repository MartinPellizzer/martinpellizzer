Plicosepalus meridianus

Plicosepalus meridianus, commonly known as the desert bellflower, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, pleated, overlapping petals that give it its scientific name. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and thrives in full sun, showcasing a compact, shrub-like form. Its flowers are usually pale blue or purple, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits.

Plicosepalus meridianus is adapted to withstand drought and extreme temperatures, making it a resilient species in harsh environments.
