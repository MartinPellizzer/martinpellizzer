Petunia × versicolor

Petunia × versicolor, commonly known as the multicolored petunia, is a hybrid flowering plant that combines traits from both *Petunia integrifolia* and *Petunia axillaris*.

It is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that come in a wide range of colors, including red, pink, white, and purple. The plant is an annual or tender perennial, typically growing to a height of 10–30 cm with trailing or bushy growth habits. Its flowers are borne in clusters and are known for their strong fragrance, which attracts pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant thrives in full sun and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking colorful, low-maintenance ornamental plants.
