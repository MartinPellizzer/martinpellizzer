Penstemon barrettiae

Penstemon barrettiae, commonly known as Barrett's beardtongue, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to purple. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds. It prefers well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscapes.

Penstemon barrettiae is also valued for its drought tolerance and low maintenance requirements.
