Platycoryne paludosa

Platycoryne paludosa, commonly known as the swamp flatleaf, is a herbaceous plant native to wetland environments in Southeast Asia.

It typically grows in marshy areas and is characterized by its broad, flat leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually green or pale yellow, arranged in clusters at the base of the leaves. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb nutrients from waterlogged soils.

Platycoryne paludosa plays a role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems and providing habitat for various microorganisms.
