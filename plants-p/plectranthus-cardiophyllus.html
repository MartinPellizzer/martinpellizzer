Plectranthus cardiophyllus

Plectranthus cardiophyllus, commonly known as the African violet, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves and clusters of small, showy flowers that bloom in a variety of colors. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and prefers bright, indirect light. It is often cultivated as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and ease of care.

Plectranthus cardiophyllus is also valued for its ability to purify the air and its use in traditional medicine.
