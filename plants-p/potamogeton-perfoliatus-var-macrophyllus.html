Potamogeton perfoliatus var. macrophyllus

Potamogeton perfoliatus var. macrophyllus, commonly known as the broad-leaf pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in North America.

It is characterized by its elongated, ribbon-like leaves that are often broad and thick, with a waxy surface that aids in buoyancy. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically arranged in spikes, and it thrives in shallow, slow-moving waters. Its rhizomes form dense underwater mats, contributing to water clarity by stabilizing sediments.

This species plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
