Plantago radicata var. monticola

Plantago radicata var. monticola, commonly known as mountain plantain, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It features a basal rosette of broad, heart-shaped leaves with a hairy surface. The plant produces tall, flowering stems topped with dense clusters of small, greenish flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or alpine environments.

Its medicinal properties have been historically used for their healing and anti-inflammatory benefits.
