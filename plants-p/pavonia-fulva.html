Pavonia fulva

Pavonia fulva, commonly known as the scarlet false mallow, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features showy, bright red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves and can grow up to 3 meters in height. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Pavonia fulva is often used in native gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
