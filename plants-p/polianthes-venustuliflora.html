Polianthes venustuliflora

Polianthes venustuliflora, commonly known as the Mexican bellflower, is a striking member of the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is native to Mexico and is characterized by its elegant, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant produces a cluster of narrow, strap-like leaves that are often bluish-green in appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Polianthes venustuliflora is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its showy blooms.
