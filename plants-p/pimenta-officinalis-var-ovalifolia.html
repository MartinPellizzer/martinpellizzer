Pimenta officinalis var. ovalifolia

Pimenta officinalis var. ovalifolia, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to South America.

It features small, oval-shaped leaves that are glossy and arranged alternately along the branches. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, followed by red, fleshy berries that are edible. Its botanical characteristics include a robust root system and adaptability to various soil types, making it a resilient species.

However, it is considered an invasive species in some regions due to its rapid growth and competitive nature.
