Piper pendulifolium

Piper pendulifolium, commonly known as the hanging-leaf pepper, is a tropical climbing shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species. The plant is characterized by its long, hanging leaves that give it its common name. Its small, greenish-white flowers are followed by tiny, berry-like fruits.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has some traditional medicinal uses.
