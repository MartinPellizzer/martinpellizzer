Pteridium decompositum

Pteridium decompositum, commonly known as the decomposed fern, is a rare and enigmatic fern species native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, often translucent fronds that exhibit a unique decomposition-like appearance, which contributes to its common name. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, typically within tropical rainforests. Its reproductive structures, including sporangia, are clustered in sori on the underside of its leaves.

Due to its limited distribution and fragile habitat requirements, Pteridium decompositum is considered a vulnerable species.
