Phyllanthus stipulatus

Phyllanthus stipulatus, commonly known as the African spurge, is a flowering plant native to Africa and parts of Asia.

It is a small shrub that typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by small, bead-like fruits. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and often have stipules at the base, giving it its scientific name.

Phyllanthus stipulatus is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional medicine for various ailments.
