Picris strigosa

Picris strigosa, commonly known as rough hawk's-beard, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant features stiff, hairy stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence. Its bright yellow flowers bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

It is typically found in dry grasslands, wastelands, and along roadsides, thriving in disturbed soils.
