Pseudalepyrum ciliatum

Pseudalepyrum ciliatum, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a velvety texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or yellow in color. Its fruits are capsule-like structures that contain numerous tiny seeds.

Pseudalepyrum ciliatum is often found in disturbed soils and is considered a weed in agricultural settings.
