Potentilla biflora var. bifloriformis

Potentilla biflora var. bifloriformis, commonly known as the golden cinquefoil, is a low-growing perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, typically in clusters of five, giving it its common name. The plant has finely divided, feathery leaves that are covered in a soft, silvery down, enhancing its ornamental appeal. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Its compact growth habit and vibrant floral display make it a valuable addition to native plant gardens.
