Pyrus communis var. sabonia

Pyrus communis var. sabonia, commonly known as the Sabona pear, is a cultivated variety of the common pear.

It is characterized by its small, round fruits with a thin, smooth skin that ranges in color from yellow to reddish-brown. The fruit is typically harvested in late summer and is prized for its sweet, aromatic flavor and firm texture. This variety is well-suited to temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

It is often used in both fresh consumption and culinary applications due to its versatility and taste.
