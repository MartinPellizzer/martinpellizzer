Polypodium echinatum

Polypodium echinatum, commonly known as the hairy shield fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Polypodiaceae.

It is characterized by its distinctive, shield-shaped fronds that are covered in fine, hairy structures. Native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas, it thrives in moist, shaded environments. The plant has a rhizomatous growth habit, allowing it to spread and form dense colonies.

Its unique morphology and ecological adaptations make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
