Pterocarpus gummifer

Pterocarpus gummifer, commonly known as the gumwood tree, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its sturdy, aromatic wood and distinctive leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by pods containing flat, dark seeds. It thrives in tropical climates and is often found in lowland forests.

The plant is valued for its durable timber and has traditional uses in various cultures.
