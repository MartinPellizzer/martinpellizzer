Paepalanthus cumbricola

Paepalanthus cumbricola, commonly known as the Andean sunflower, is a species of flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant is known for its yellow-orange ray flowers and is often found in high-altitude grasslands. It thrives in cool, temperate climates and is adapted to the harsh conditions of its mountainous habitat.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
