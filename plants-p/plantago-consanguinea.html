Plantago consanguinea

Plantago consanguinea, commonly known as the common plantain, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It features broad, waxy leaves that are often ribbed and can reach up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in dense spikes, which are typically borne on long, slender stalks. It thrives in a variety of habitats, including roadsides, meadows, and disturbed soils, due to its adaptability and resilience.

Botanically, it belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its extensive root system and ability to spread through both seeds and vegetative reproduction.
