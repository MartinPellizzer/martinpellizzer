Pisonia byrsonimifolia

Pisonia byrsonimifolia, commonly known as the coral tree, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its striking, bright red or orange bract-like flowers that resemble coral, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in panicles, and its leaves are typically simple and ovate. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often growing as a shrub or small tree.

Pisonia byrsonimifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping in warm climates.
