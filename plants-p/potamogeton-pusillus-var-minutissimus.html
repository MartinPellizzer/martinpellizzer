Potamogeton pusillus var. minutissimus

Potamogeton pusillus var. minutissimus, commonly known as the minute pondweed, is a small aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in North America.

It is characterized by its tiny, narrow leaves that are typically less than 1 millimeter in width. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often submerged, and it reproduces both sexually and asexually through rhizomes. It thrives in shallow, slow-moving waters and is an important part of aquatic ecosystems.

Its presence can indicate good water quality due to its sensitivity to pollution.
