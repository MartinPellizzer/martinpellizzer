Panicum paludosum

Panicum paludosum, commonly known as marsh panicgrass, is a perennial grass species native to wetland habitats in the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in shallow water or moist soils, often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces slender, spike-like inflorescences that bloom in late summer. Its ability to thrive in saturated conditions makes it an important component of wetland ecosystems.

Panicum paludosum plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
