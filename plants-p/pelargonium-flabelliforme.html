Pelargonium flabelliforme

Pelargonium flabelliforme, commonly known as the flabelliform geranium, is a species of flowering plant in the family Geraniaceae.

It is native to South Africa and is characterized by its distinctive, palmately lobed leaves that resemble a fan or a fan-shaped structure. The plant produces showy, clustered flowers in shades of pink, red, and white, which bloom throughout the growing season. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is widely cultivated in gardens around the world.
