Pelargonium × irregulare

Pelargonium × irregulare, commonly known as the irregular geranium, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Geraniaceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive irregularly shaped leaves, which give it its common name. The plant produces showy, five-petaled flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, typically blooming in summer. It is a popular choice for garden use due to its ornamental value and adaptability to various growing conditions.

Pelargonium × irregulare is often cultivated as an annual or tender perennial in temperate climates.
