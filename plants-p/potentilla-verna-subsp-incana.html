Potentilla verna subsp. incana

Potentilla verna subsp.

incana, commonly known as the hairy cinquefoil, is a low-growing perennial plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere. It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which are typically divided into five oval lobes.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands, meadows, and rocky slopes.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and traditional herbal medicine.
