Philadelphus ernestii

Philadelphus ernestii, commonly known as the Ernestii Mockorange, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has simple, opposite, ovate leaves that turn yellow in the fall. It typically grows to a height of 6 to 10 feet and has a spreading, rounded form.

Philadelphus ernestii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens for its pleasant scent and showy blooms.
