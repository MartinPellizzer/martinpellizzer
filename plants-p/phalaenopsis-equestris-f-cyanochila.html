Phalaenopsis equestris f. cyanochila

Phalaenopsis equestris f. cyanochila, commonly known as the blue-lip phalaenopsis, is a rare and striking orchid species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its elongated, lance-shaped leaves and its distinctive flowers, which feature a unique blue-purplish coloration on the lip. The plant typically produces clusters of white or pale yellow flowers with a prominent, colorful lip that is its most notable feature. It thrives in warm, humid environments with indirect light and well-draining potting mix.

This orchid is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated for its beauty and resilience.
