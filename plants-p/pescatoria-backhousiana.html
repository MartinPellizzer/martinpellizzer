Pescatoria backhousiana

Pescatoria backhousiana, commonly known as the coastal backhousia, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of southern Australia.

It is a shrub that typically grows in sandy or rocky soils along the shoreline. The plant produces small, fragrant white or pink flowers during the spring and summer months. Its leaves are narrow and evergreen, providing a dense canopy that helps stabilize coastal dunes.

Pescatoria backhousiana is well adapted to salt spray and strong winds, making it an important species in coastal ecosystems.
