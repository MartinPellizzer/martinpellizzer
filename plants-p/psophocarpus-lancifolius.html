Psophocarpus lancifolius

Psophocarpus lancifolius, commonly known as the spearleaf cowpea, is a climbing legume native to tropical regions of Asia.

It features elongated, lance-shaped leaves with a distinctive serrated margin. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers followed by pods containing edible, kidney-shaped seeds. It is valued for its nutritional content and is often cultivated as a food crop.

The species is also noted for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, improving soil fertility.
