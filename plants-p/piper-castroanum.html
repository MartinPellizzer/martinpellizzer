Piper castroanum

Piper castroanum, commonly known as Castro's pepper, is a tropical vine native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper plants such as black pepper. The plant produces small, green berries that turn red when mature, containing a peppery, aromatic seed. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a rough texture, while its stems are hairy and woody.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and for the spice derived from its fruits.
