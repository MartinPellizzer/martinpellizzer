Physaliastrum sinicum

Physaliastrum sinicum, commonly known as the Chinese starwort, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. The plant features a creeping rhizome with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. Its small, greenish-white flowers are clustered in dense, spherical inflorescences.

Physaliastrum sinicum is valued in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties.
