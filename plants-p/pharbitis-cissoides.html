Pharbitis cissoides

Pharbitis cissoides, commonly known as the cissus vine, is a climbing plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and distinctive, red-tinged flowers. The plant thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth. Its rapid growth habit makes it suitable for ornamental use in gardens and as a ground cover.

The leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
