Plantago dalmatica

Plantago dalmatica, commonly known as Dalmatian plantain, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in a spike, which bloom in the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and disturbed areas.

This species is valued for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally for its healing benefits.
