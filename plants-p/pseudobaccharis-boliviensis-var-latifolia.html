Pseudobaccharis boliviensis var. latifolia

Pseudobaccharis boliviensis var. latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf pseudobaccharis, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Bolivia and Argentina.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and scrublands. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, with a rough texture, and it produces small yellow to orange flowers in clusters.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
