Potamogeton lanciformis

Potamogeton lanciformis, commonly known as the lanceleaf pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant found in freshwater environments.

It has long, narrow leaves that are typically arranged in whorls around the stem. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often submerged, followed by elongated, cylindrical fruits. It plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.

Its presence is often an indicator of good water quality in ponds and lakes.
