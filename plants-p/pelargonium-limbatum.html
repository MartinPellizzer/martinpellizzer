Pelargonium limbatum

Pelargonium limbatum, commonly known as the broad-leaf geranium, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its rounded, lobed leaves and clusters of small, showy flowers that range in color from pink to purple. The plant typically grows as a herbaceous perennial, often reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long blooming period, which extends from spring through summer.
