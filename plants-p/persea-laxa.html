Persea laxa

Persea laxa, commonly known as the Mexican persimmon, is a small evergreen tree native to Mexico and Central America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and fragrant, white flowers. The tree produces small, orange-red fruits that are edible when fully ripe. The fruit has a sweet, honey-like flavor and is often consumed fresh or used in culinary applications.

It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is valued for its ornamental and edible qualities.
