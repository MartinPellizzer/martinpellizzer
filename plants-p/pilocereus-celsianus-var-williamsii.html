Pilocereus celsianus var. williamsii

Pilocereus celsianus var. williamsii, commonly known as the Williams' night bloomer, is a species of cactus native to South America.

It is characterized by its spiny, cylindrical stems and produces large, fragrant flowers at night. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, requiring minimal water and full sun exposure. Its flowers, which open in the evening, are typically white or pale pink and attract nocturnal pollinators such as moths.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and unique blooming behavior.
