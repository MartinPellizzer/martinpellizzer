Planchonella cotinifolia var. pubescens

Planchonella cotinifolia var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy broad-leaf hibiscus, is a tropical shrub native to the Indo-Pacific region.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped, pubescent leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces showy, five-petaled flowers in shades of yellow to orange, which bloom during the wet season. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, winged seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in warm, humid climates.
