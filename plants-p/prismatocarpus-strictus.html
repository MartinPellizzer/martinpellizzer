Prismatocarpus strictus

Prismatocarpus strictus, commonly known as the narrow-leafed pandanus, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Pandanaceae and is characterized by its stiff, narrow leaves that are often used in traditional crafts and as a flavoring in cuisine. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers followed by clustered fruits that are edible. Its root system is fibrous, and it thrives in well-drained soils with ample sunlight.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and practical uses in local communities.
