Paphiopedilum haynaldianum

Paphiopedilum haynaldianum, commonly known as the Haynald's lady's slipper, is a rare and captivating orchid native to the mountainous regions of southern China.

It is characterized by its unique slipper-shaped pouch, which is a distinctive feature of the Paphiopedilum genus. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often on tree trunks or rocks in its natural habitat. Its flowers are usually pale yellow with a deep red lip, and it blooms once a year, making it a sought-after species among orchid enthusiasts.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered vulnerable and requires careful conservation efforts.
