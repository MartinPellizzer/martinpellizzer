Piptadenia psilostachya

Piptadenia psilostachya, commonly known as the Brazilian pequi tree, is a tropical legume native to South America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and showy yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The tree produces woody pods containing edible seeds, which are rich in oil and used in traditional cuisine. Its rapid growth and adaptability make it valuable for reforestation and agroforestry systems.

The plant also plays an important role in supporting local biodiversity.
