Pyrostria bibracteata

Pyrostria bibracteata, commonly known as the flame tree, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, fiery red or orange flowers that appear in clusters during the rainy season. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a smooth surface. Its fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release seeds.

Pyrostria bibracteata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adaptable to a variety of soil types.
