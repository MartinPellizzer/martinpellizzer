Prunus bifrons var. bifrons

Prunus bifrons var. bifrons, commonly known as the Japanese flowering cherry or some sources refer to it as the white-flowered cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its slender, multi-branched form and is noted for its early spring blossoms, which appear before the leaves. The flowers are typically white or pale pink, with five petals, and are borne in clusters along the branches. The leaves are ovate to elliptic, with a smooth margin and a glossy green surface.

This plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and parks.
