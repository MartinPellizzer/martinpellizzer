Plantago hudsoniana

Plantago hudsoniana, commonly known as Hudson's plantain, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows, wetlands, and along stream banks. The plant has a basal rosette of broad, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. It produces small, greenish-white flowers in a spike-like inflorescence during the summer months.

The species is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing food for various insects and wildlife.
