Polygonum equisetiforme var. controversum

Polygonum equisetiforme var. controversum, commonly known as the narrowleaf smartweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to North America.

It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swales, and along stream banks. The plant features upright stems that are often reddish in color, and it produces small, clustered flowers in late summer. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

The species is part of the Polygonaceae family, known for its distinctive bracts and edible seeds in some species.
