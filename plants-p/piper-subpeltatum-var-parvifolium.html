Piper subpeltatum var. parvifolium

Piper subpeltatum var. parvifolium, commonly known as the small-leaved pepper plant, is a tropical vine native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its slender stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by berries that turn from yellow to red as they mature. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties.

Its foliage is often used in traditional herbal remedies.
