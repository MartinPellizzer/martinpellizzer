Potentilla tanacetifolia

Potentilla tanacetifolia, commonly known as the meadow cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist meadows and along stream banks. The plant features bright yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, arranged in clusters of five. Its leaves are palmately divided, with a finely toothed edge, and the stems are often covered in fine hairs.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in herbal medicine.
