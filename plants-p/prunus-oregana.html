Prunus oregana

Prunus oregana, commonly known as the Oregon grape, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of bright yellow flowers that appear in early spring. The plant produces dark blue, edible berries that are often used in jams and wines. It thrives in well-drained soils and is commonly found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Botanically, it belongs to the Rosaceae family and is valued for its ornamental and ecological significance.
