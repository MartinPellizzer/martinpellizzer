Piper mansericheanum

Piper mansericheanum, commonly known as the Manu pepper, is a climbing vine native to the Amazon rainforest.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many species of pepper plants. The plant produces small, green berries that turn red when ripe, and they are used in traditional medicine by indigenous communities. The leaves are often used as a spice and have a pungent, peppery flavor.

It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
