Protea lorea

Protea lorea, commonly known as the silver protea, is a striking South African plant belonging to the Proteaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescences that resemble a cluster of flowers, with a central cone-like structure surrounded by numerous petal-like bracts. The plant is native to the Western Cape region and thrives in well-drained, acidic soils. It produces white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late summer to autumn.

Protea lorea is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also valued for its ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
