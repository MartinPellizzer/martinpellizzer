Pleiochasia dichotoma

Pleiochasia dichotoma, commonly known as the forked bamboo, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its distinctive branching pattern, where the stems split into two at each node. The plant has long, slender leaves that are often used in traditional medicine and as a decorative element.

It thrives in humid environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and resilience.
