Potentilla speciosa var. straussii

Potentilla speciosa var. straussii, commonly known as the Strauss's cinquefoil, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, showy yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has finely divided, serrated leaves that are typically covered in a dense layer of white, woolly hair, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is often cultivated in gardens and natural habitats.
