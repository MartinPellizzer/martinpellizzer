Pectinaria borealis

Pectinaria borealis, commonly known as the Arctic pectinaria, is a flowering plant native to the Arctic regions of North America and Eurasia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants. The plant is characterized by its small, white or pale yellow flowers and hairy, succulent leaves adapted to cold climates. It thrives in moist, rocky soils and is often found in tundra and alpine environments.

Pectinaria borealis plays a role in stabilizing soil in its harsh habitat and provides food for local pollinators.
