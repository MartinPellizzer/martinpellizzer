Panicum brizanthum var. lasiochloa

Panicum brizanthum var. lasiochloa, commonly known as the silky panic grass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and long, slender leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces fluffy, panicle-like inflorescences that are typically pale to dark brown in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for ornamental use in gardens and landscapes.

Its adaptability and drought tolerance contribute to its popularity as a low-maintenance ornamental grass.
