Potentilla libanotica

Potentilla libanotica, commonly known as the Lebanon cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the Middle East and parts of southern Europe.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its drought tolerance.

Potentilla libanotica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and attractive foliage.
