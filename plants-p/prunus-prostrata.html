Prunus prostrata

Prunus prostrata, commonly known as the Japanese snowbell, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan.

It features small, bell-shaped white flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by dark purple berries. The plant has a compact, spreading growth habit with slender, arching branches. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a smooth, glossy surface.

Prunus prostrata is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in partial shade.
