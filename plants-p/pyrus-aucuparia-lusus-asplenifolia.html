Pyrus aucuparia lusus asplenifolia

Pyrus aucuparia lusus asplenifolia, commonly known as the asplenifoliated crabapple, is a rare variant of the common crabapple.

It is characterized by its distinctive foliage, which resembles the shape of the fern genus Asplenium, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in temperate climates and thrives in well-drained soil. Its flowers are small and white, appearing in early spring before the leaves fully emerge.

This unique hybrid is of interest to botanists and horticulturists due to its unusual leaf morphology and potential for ornamental use.
