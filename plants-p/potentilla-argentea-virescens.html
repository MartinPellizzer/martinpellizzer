Potentilla argentea virescens

Potentilla argentea virescens, commonly known as silverleaf cinquefoil, is a low-growing perennial plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features silver-white, hairy leaves that give it its common name, and it produces yellow flowers in early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It is often found in alpine or subalpine regions due to its cold-hardy nature.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and flowers.
