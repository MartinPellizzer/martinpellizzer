Patagonium arenicola

Patagonium arenicola, commonly known as the Patagonian sandplant, is a halophyte adapted to grow in saline and arid environments.

It is native to the coastal regions of southern South America, particularly in Patagonia. The plant has a low, creeping growth habit with small, succulent leaves that help reduce water loss. Its flowers are typically small and clustered, often producing a distinctive yellow or greenish color.

Patagonium arenicola plays a vital role in stabilizing sandy soils and supporting local ecosystems in its harsh habitat.
