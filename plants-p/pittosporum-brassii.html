Pittosporum brassii

Pittosporum brassii, commonly known as the New Zealand white-flowered pittosporum, is a shrub or small tree native to New Zealand.

It features lance-shaped leaves that are dark green on top and silvery white underneath, giving it a striking appearance. The plant produces fragrant, white flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. Its fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release seeds.

Pittosporum brassii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to a range of soil types in temperate climates.
