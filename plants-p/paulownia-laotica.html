Paulownia laotica

Paulownia laotica, commonly known as the Lao paulownia, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Paulowniaceae family and is characterized by its rapid growth and distinctive heart-shaped leaves. The tree produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in the spring, which are typically pale blue or purple in color. Its wood is highly valued for its lightweight and strength, making it useful in construction and crafting.

However, due to overharvesting and habitat loss, it is considered vulnerable in some regions.
