Pentas involucrata

Pentas involucrata, commonly known as the velvet flower or sweet pitcher, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, five-petaled flowers that range in color from pink to red, often with a velvety texture. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and often have a slightly hairy surface.

Pentas involucrata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms and ability to thrive in warm climates.
