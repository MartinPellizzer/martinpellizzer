Prunus hosseusii

Prunus hosseusii, commonly known as the Hosseus cherry, is a species of shrub or small tree native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are often hairy on the underside. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers in early spring, followed by edible berries that are typically red or black in color.

Prunus hosseusii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
