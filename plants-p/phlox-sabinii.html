Phlox sabinii

Phlox sabinii, commonly known as the New England phlox, is a perennial flowering plant native to the northeastern United States.

It features showy, clustered flowers that range in color from pink to white, blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained, moist soil.

Phlox sabinii is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
