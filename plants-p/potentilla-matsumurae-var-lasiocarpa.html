Potentilla matsumurae var. lasiocarpa

Potentilla matsumurae var. lasiocarpa, commonly known as the hairy potentilla, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of East Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are typically ovate to elliptic in shape and covered with fine, soft hairs.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and attractive foliage.
