Padus retusa

Padus retusa, commonly known as the white thorn or European whitebeam, is a deciduous tree native to Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and small, white flowers that appear in clusters. The tree produces small, red berries that are edible for birds and other wildlife. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a serrated margin, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Padus retusa is often found in woodlands and along stream banks, where it plays a role in supporting local biodiversity.
