Primula × schottii

Primula × schottii, commonly known as the hybrid primrose, is a species resulting from the cross between Primula vulgaris and Primula obconica.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central and Southern Europe. The plant produces showy, funnel-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, typically blooming in early spring. Its foliage consists of broad, heart-shaped leaves that are dark green and slightly hairy.

Primula × schottii is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in cool, moist environments.
