Potentilla impolita f. borbasiana

Potentilla impolita f. borbasiana, commonly known as the rough potentilla, is a flowering plant belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers. The plant has hairy, rough-textured leaves that are typically oval to elliptic in shape. It is native to regions of Europe and is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value.
