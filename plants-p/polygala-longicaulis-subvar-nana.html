Polygala longicaulis subvar. nana

Polygala longicaulis subvar. nana, commonly known as the dwarf long-stemmed milkvetch, is a compact, herbaceous perennial native to arid and semi-arid regions of China.

It features small, clustered flowers that range in color from pink to purple, typically blooming in spring. The plant has slender, branched stems and narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

This subvariety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in xeriscaped gardens.
