Panicum interruptum

Panicum interruptum, commonly known as interrupted panicgrass, is a species of grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, spikelet-based inflorescences. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and other moist environments, showcasing its adaptability to humid conditions. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue that becomes more prominent during the growing season.

Panicum interruptum plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various small organisms in its native ecosystem.
