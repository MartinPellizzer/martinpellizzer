Polygonum rottboellioides var. rottboellioides

Polygonum rottboellioides var. rottboellioides, commonly known as the redroot hogweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features tall, slender stems that can grow up to 2 meters in height, often reddish in color. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs and produce small, greenish-white flowers in clusters. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in wet meadows and along stream banks.

Due to its rapid growth and invasive potential, it is sometimes considered a weed in certain regions.
