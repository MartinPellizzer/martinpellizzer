Pilea khasiana

Pilea khasiana, commonly known as the Khasi pilea, is a herbaceous plant native to the northeastern regions of India.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and compact growth habit. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often found in forest understories. Its leaves are usually a deep green with a waxy texture, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers.

Despite its modest appearance, Pilea khasiana is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological significance in its native habitat.
