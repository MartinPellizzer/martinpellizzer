Penstemon angustifolius var. dulcis

Penstemon angustifolius var. dulcis, commonly known as the sweetleaf penstemon, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of tubular, two-lipped flowers in shades of pink to purple. The leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often covered in a fine, fuzzy layer that helps reduce water loss. This variety is well adapted to rocky and arid environments, thriving in full sun and well-drained soils.

Its nectar-rich flowers attract a variety of pollinators, including bees and butterflies, making it a valuable addition to native gardens.
