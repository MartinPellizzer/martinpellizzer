Phyllodes oppenheimiana

Phyllodes oppenheimiana, commonly known as the Oppenheim's wattle, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant features phyllodes, which are flattened, leaf-like stems that perform photosynthesis. It produces yellow flowers in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds.

The species is adapted to arid environments and is often used in ecological restoration projects.
