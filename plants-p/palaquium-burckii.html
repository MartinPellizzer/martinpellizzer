Palaquium burckii

Palaquium burckii, commonly known as the Burck's palaquium, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and elliptical leaves with a leathery texture. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by spherical fruits containing a single large seed.

Its fruit is valued for its edible pulp, which has a sweet, tangy flavor, and the tree is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
