Pteris edulis

Pteris edulis, commonly known as the edible water fern, is a species of fern native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Pteridaceae and is characterized by its delicate, frond-like leaves that are often used in traditional cuisines. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found near streams or in humid forests. Its leaves are harvested for their tender fronds, which are used in various culinary preparations.

The plant's botanical structure includes a rhizomatous stem and bipinnate leaves, making it easily distinguishable from other fern species.
