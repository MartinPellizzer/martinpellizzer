Phyllanthus concolor var. ellipticus

Phyllanthus concolor var. ellipticus, commonly known as the elliptic leaf spurge, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It features elliptical, glossy leaves that are typically arranged oppositely along its stems. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clusters, which are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, shaded environments.

Its botanical characteristics make it a notable species in the Euphorbiaceae family.
