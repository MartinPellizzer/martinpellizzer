Passiflora bolstadii

Passiflora bolstadii, commonly known as the Bolstad's passionflower, is a species native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a climbing vine that produces striking, showy flowers with intricate structures. The flowers feature a unique arrangement of tepals, stamens, and a distinctive crown of filaments. The plant is known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
