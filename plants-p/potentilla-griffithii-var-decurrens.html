Potentilla griffithii var. decurrens

Potentilla griffithii var. decurrens, commonly known as the Kashmir rose or decurrent potentilla, is a perennial shrub native to the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its dense, rose-like flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine regions, preferring well-drained soils and full sun. Its foliage is dark green and densely arranged, contributing to its ornamental value.

It is valued in horticulture for its hardiness and ability to thrive in cooler climates.
