Pinzona calineoides

Pinzona calineoides, commonly known as the Mexican sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is a member of the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, which bloom in the summer months. It has adapted to survive in dry, sandy soils with minimal water availability.
