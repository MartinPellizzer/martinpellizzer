Pleurothallis bicristata

Pleurothallis bicristata, commonly known as the two-ridge orchid, is a species of orchid native to the cloud forests of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact, epiphytic growth habit. The plant produces small, delicate flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, with a distinctive two-lobed lip. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining, acidic soil.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts for its ornamental value.
