Plantago arborescens var. palmensis

Plantago arborescens var. palmensis, commonly known as the palmleaf plantain, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, palmate leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in spikes, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its leaves are edible and have been used for their nutritional and medicinal properties in various cultures.
