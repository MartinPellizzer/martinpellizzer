Peperomia chachapoyasensis

Peperomia chachapoyasensis, commonly known as the Chachapoyas peperomia, is a rare and unique species native to the cloud forests of northern Peru.

It is a compact, evergreen shrub with thick, waxy leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often surrounded by specialized structures called bracts. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its limited natural range and habitat destruction, it is considered a vulnerable species in the wild.
