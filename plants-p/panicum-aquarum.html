Panicum aquarum

Panicum aquarum, commonly known as water panicgrass, is a flowering plant species native to wetland environments.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like appearance with narrow leaves and small, spikelet-based flowers. The plant typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, thriving in aquatic or semi-aquatic habitats. Its seeds are dispersed by water, aiding in its spread across wetland areas.

Panicum aquarum plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for aquatic organisms.
