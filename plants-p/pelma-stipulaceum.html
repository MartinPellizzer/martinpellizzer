Pelma stipulaceum

Pelma stipulaceum, commonly known as the silverleaf fern, is a tropical plant characterized by its delicate, feathery fronds.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is native to regions with high humidity and warm climates. The plant features small, scale-like structures called stipules along its stems, which give it its common name. Its leaves are typically bioluminescent under certain conditions, a unique trait among ferns.

Pelma stipulaceum thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.
