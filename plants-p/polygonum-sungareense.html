Polygonum sungareense

Polygonum sungareense, commonly known as the Siberian knotweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the colder regions of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, riverbanks, and marshy areas. The plant features upright stems that are often reddish in color, and its leaves are lance-shaped with a rough texture. It produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in dense clusters, which bloom in late summer.

Polygonum sungareense is known for its ability to thrive in harsh environmental conditions, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
