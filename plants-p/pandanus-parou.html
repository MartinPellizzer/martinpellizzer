Pandanus parou

Pandanus parou, commonly known as the pandanus tree or screw pine, is a tropical plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its distinctive, spirally arranged leaves that are often used in traditional crafts and thatching. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. Its root system is fibrous and adapted to grow in coastal and sandy soils.

The fruit, which resembles a pine cone, contains edible seeds that are used in local cuisines.
