Potentilla verna var. salisburgensis

Potentilla verna var. salisburgensis, commonly known as the alpine cinquefoil, is a low-growing perennial shrub native to mountainous regions of Europe.

It features small, yellow, five-petaled flowers that bloom in early spring, typically from March to May. The plant has slender, woody stems covered with fine, soft hairs, and its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky habitats.

This variety is distinguished by its compact growth habit and is valued for its ornamental appeal in garden settings.
