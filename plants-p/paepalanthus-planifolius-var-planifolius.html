Paepalanthus planifolius var. planifolius

Paepalanthus planifolius var. planifolius, commonly known as the flat-leafed puya, is a striking plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a member of the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its tall, grass-like appearance with long, flat leaves that form a rosette. The plant produces striking inflorescences with bright red or orange flowers that attract pollinators such as hummingbirds. Due to its unique appearance and ecological importance, it is often cultivated for both its aesthetic value and conservation significance.
