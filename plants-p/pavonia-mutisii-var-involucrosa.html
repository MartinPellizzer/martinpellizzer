Pavonia mutisii var. involucrosa

Pavonia mutisii var. involucrosa, commonly known as the velvetleaf pavonia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking pink to red flowers that bloom in clusters, often with a velvety texture. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub, reaching heights of up to 2 meters. Its leaves are ovate to lanceolate, with a rough, hairy surface.

It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides.
