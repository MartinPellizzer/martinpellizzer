Palura pilosa f. leucocarpa

Palura pilosa f. leucocarpa, commonly known as the white-fruited palura, is a variant of the palura plant native to certain regions of South America.

It is a shrub characterized by its hairy, woody stems and large, palmate leaves. The plant produces distinctive, clustered fruit that is white or pale yellow in color, distinguishing it from other forms of Palura. Its botanical features make it notable for its unique morphology and ecological adaptations.

It is often found in dry, open habitats and is valued for its ornamental qualities.
