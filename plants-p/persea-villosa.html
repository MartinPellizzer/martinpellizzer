Persea villosa

Persea villosa, commonly known as the hairy persimmon, is a species of flowering plant in the family Lauraceae.

It is native to Mexico and Central America, where it typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant is characterized by its large, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs. Its fruit is a fleshy, orange to reddish berry that contains a single seed.

Persea villosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and has also been used in traditional medicine.
