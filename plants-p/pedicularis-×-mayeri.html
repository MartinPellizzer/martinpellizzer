Pedicularis × mayeri

Pedicularis × mayeri, commonly known as the yellow monkey orchid, is a hybrid plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the genus Pedicularis, which includes various species of flowering plants known as louse plants due to their unique, often bizarre flower shapes. The plant produces vivid yellow flowers with a distinctive, spurred structure that resembles a monkey's face, contributing to its common name. It thrives in alpine and subalpine environments, preferring well-drained soils and full sunlight.

Pedicularis × mayeri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking blooms.
