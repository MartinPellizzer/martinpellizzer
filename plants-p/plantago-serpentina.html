Plantago serpentina

Plantago serpentina, commonly known as snake plant or mother of thousands, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa, South America, and the Caribbean.

It is characterized by its upright, sword-shaped leaves that are often marked with silver or gray veins. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. It is a hardy species that thrives in a variety of soil types and is relatively drought-tolerant.

Botanically, it belongs to the family Plantaginaceae and is known for its ability to spread through rhizomes and seeds.
