Pygeum

Pygeum, commonly known as African plum or false acacia, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Proteaceae.

It is native to southern Africa and includes several species, the most well-known being Pygeum africanum. The plant is characterized by its woody shrubs or small trees with compound leaves and small, fragrant flowers. Its bark and leaves have been used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Pygeum is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental qualities and adaptability to various soil conditions.
