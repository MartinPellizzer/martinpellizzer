Piper ictum

Piper ictum, commonly known as the black pepper plant, is a flowering vine native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, greenish-white flowers. The plant produces small berries that turn from green to red as they mature, with the unripe fruit being the source of the spice black pepper. The botanical structure of Piper ictum supports its role in traditional medicine and culinary use.

Its growth habit includes climbing stems and aerial roots, which help it attach to supporting structures in its natural habitat.
