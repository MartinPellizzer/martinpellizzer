Patosia clandestina

Patosia clandestina, commonly known as the secret nightshade, is a rare flowering plant native to the dense, humid undergrowth of tropical rainforests.

It is characterized by its striking, violet-blue flowers that bloom at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths and bats. The plant has dark green, broad leaves with a slightly velvety texture, and its stems are covered in fine, white hairs. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and requires high humidity and well-drained soil.

Due to its elusive nature and limited distribution, Patosia clandestina is considered a botanical curiosity and is often studied for its unique ecological adaptations.
