Pleurothallis ascera

Pleurothallis ascera, commonly known as the ascending pleurothallis, is a species of orchid native to South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and small, delicate flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often found in the understory of tropical forests. Its leaves are usually ovate and arranged in a rosette pattern, contributing to its unique appearance.

The flowers, which are typically white or pale yellow, bloom during the wet season and are pollinated by various insects.
