Pandanus carrii

Pandanus carrii, commonly known as the Carri's pandanus, is a flowering plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its tall, narrow leaves that are typically green with a waxy texture. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually yellow or orange in color. It thrives in coastal and sandy soils, often growing near the shoreline.

Pandanus carrii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional crafts.
