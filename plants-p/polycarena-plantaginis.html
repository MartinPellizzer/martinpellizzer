Polycarena plantaginis

Polycarena plantaginis, commonly known as the common plantain, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to temperate regions of Europe, Asia, and North America.

It belongs to the family Plantaginaceae and is characterized by its broad, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-white in color, appearing in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found along roadsides, in meadows, and in urban environments.

Polycarena plantaginis is known for its medicinal properties and has been used historically for its anti-inflammatory and healing effects.
