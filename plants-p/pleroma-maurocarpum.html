Pleroma maurocarpum

Pleroma maurocarpum, commonly known as the black-carpet orchid, is a rare and striking orchid species native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dark, velvety leaves and clusters of small, fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of purple and violet. The plant thrives in shaded, humid environments, often found in dense forest undergrowth. Its unique morphology and limited distribution make it a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists.

Due to habitat loss, Pleroma maurocarpum is considered endangered, highlighting the need for its protection.
