Pleurothallis cardiophylla

Pleurothallis cardiophylla, commonly known as the heart-leaf orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique heart-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are small and often resemble the shape of a heart, contributing to its common name.

Pleurothallis cardiophylla is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for horticultural purposes.
