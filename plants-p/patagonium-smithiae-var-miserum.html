Patagonium smithiae var. miserum

Patagonium smithiae var. miserum, commonly known as the Patagonian thistle, is a perennial shrub native to the high-altitude regions of southern South America.

It is characterized by its spiny, gray-green foliage and yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky or sandy soils. It has a deep root system that allows it to survive in harsh, nutrient-poor conditions.

Due to its unique adaptations, it plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for local wildlife.
