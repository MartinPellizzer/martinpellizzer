Parastranthus euphrasioides

Parastranthus euphrasioides, commonly known as the yellow bellvile, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellow, daisy-like flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has narrow, linear leaves and is often found in rocky or sandy soils. It is a perennial herb that thrives in full sun and is relatively drought-tolerant.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
