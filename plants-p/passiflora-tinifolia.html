Passiflora tinifolia

Passiflora tinifolia, commonly known as the silky passionflower, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, intricate flowers that feature five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments surrounding the reproductive structures. The plant produces edible, orange-yellow berries that are often consumed by birds and mammals. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped with a velvety texture, and it thrives in humid, shaded environments.

Passiflora tinifolia is valued not only for its ornamental beauty but also for its ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
