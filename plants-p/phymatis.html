Phymatis

Phymatis, commonly known as the ghost flower, is a rare and enigmatic plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It belongs to the genus Phymatidae and is characterized by its unique, translucent petals that appear almost ethereal under sunlight. The plant's leaves are succulent and adapted to store water, reflecting its ability to thrive in arid environments. Its blooming period is brief, typically occurring at night, which adds to its mysterious allure.

Due to its rarity and habitat specificity, Phymatis is considered a conservation concern.
