Pachystoma gracile

Pachystoma gracile, commonly known as the slender stonecrop, is a low-growing succulent plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It features fleshy, ribbed leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in dry conditions. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow or orange, typically during the summer months. Its compact growth habit makes it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Pachystoma gracile is well adapted to poor soils and full sun exposure.
