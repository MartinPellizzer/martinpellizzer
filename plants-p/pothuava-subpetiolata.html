Pothuava subpetiolata

Pothuava subpetiolata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf potho, is a climbing or trailing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped, dark green leaves with a glossy surface. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically followed by spathaceous inflorescences. It thrives in humid environments and is often cultivated as a houseplant due to its ornamental value.

Pothuava subpetiolata is also valued for its ability to purify indoor air.
