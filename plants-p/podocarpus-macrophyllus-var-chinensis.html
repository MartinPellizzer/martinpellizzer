Podocarpus macrophyllus var. chinensis

Podocarpus macrophyllus var. chinensis, commonly known as the Chinese podocarp or Chinese ilex, is a broadleaf evergreen shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia.

It features dense, dark green, leathery leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a glossy texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by woody, cone-like structures that contain edible, fleshy berries. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

The species is well adapted to a variety of soil types and can thrive in both sunny and partially shaded environments.
