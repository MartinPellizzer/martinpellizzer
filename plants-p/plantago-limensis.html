Plantago limensis

Plantago limensis, commonly known as the Andean plantain or Andean groundsel, is a perennial herb native to the high Andes of South America.

It typically grows in rocky, alpine environments at elevations ranging from 3,000 to 5,000 meters. The plant has erect, branching stems covered in dense, grayish-green foliage. Its small, white to yellowish flowers are arranged in dense clusters at the tips of the stems.

It is characterized by its succulent leaves and is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
