Phyllanthus niruroides

Phyllanthus niruroides, commonly known as the Indian cheesewood, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is a small shrub that typically grows up to 1.5 meters tall, featuring opposite, ovate leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, followed by small, spherical fruits that contain a single seed. It is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along stream banks.

The species is noted for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
