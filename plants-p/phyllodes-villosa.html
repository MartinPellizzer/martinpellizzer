Phyllodes villosa

Phyllodes villosa, commonly known as the silky wattle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its feathery, green, and hairy phyllodes that resemble leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which appear in spring and summer. It thrives in sandy or loamy soils and is often found in coastal and inland regions.

Phyllodes villosa is adapted to dry conditions and is a hardy plant that can tolerate light frost.
