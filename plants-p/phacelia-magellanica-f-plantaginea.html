Phacelia magellanica f. plantaginea

Phacelia magellanica f. plantaginea, commonly known as the Magellan plantain, is a perennial herb native to South America.

It belongs to the Boraginaceae family and is characterized by its hairy, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, pale blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, temperate environments, often found in grasslands and along stream banks. The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential in ecological restoration projects.
