Persea rufotomentosa

Persea rufotomentosa, commonly known as the rough-leaved avocado, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its dark green, rough-textured leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are typically followed by oval, green fruits. The fruit has a thick, leathery skin and contains a single large seed.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and is also used in traditional medicine.
