Persea bournei

Persea bournei, commonly known as the Mexican persimmon, is a small evergreen tree native to southern Mexico and Central America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and fragrant, white flowers. The tree produces small, orange to reddish fruits that are edible when fully ripe. Its botanical features include a smooth, grayish bark and a tendency to grow in tropical and subtropical climates.

Persea bournei is valued for its ornamental appeal and its fruit, which is rich in nutrients.
