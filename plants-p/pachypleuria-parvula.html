Pachypleuria parvula

Pachypleuria parvula, commonly known as the dwarf pleuroneurium, is a small, perennial herb native to parts of southern Africa.

It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments. The plant has small, narrow leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern, and it produces tiny, inconspicuous flowers that are usually greenish or white in color. It is not widely cultivated but is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and ecological adaptations.
