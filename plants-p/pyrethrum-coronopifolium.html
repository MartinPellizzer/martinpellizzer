Pyrethrum coronopifolium

Pyrethrum coronopifolium, commonly known as the crown-leaved pyrethrum, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers. The plant produces natural pyrethrins, which are potent insecticides used in organic pest control. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun exposure.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is cultivated in gardens and for commercial use.
