Phaius tankervilleae var. tankervilleae

Phaius tankervilleae var. tankervilleae, commonly known as the Tankerville's ginger, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a herbaceous perennial with broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often glossy and dark green. The plant produces striking, fragrant inflorescences with pale yellow to white flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and requires partial to full shade.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and horticultural collections.
