Pilocarpus longipes

Pilocarpus longipes, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is evergreen, with a dense, bushy growth habit and can reach heights of up to 10 meters. Its leaves are opposite, elliptical, and have a distinctive aromatic scent when crushed.

The fruit is a small, woody capsule that contains several seeds.
