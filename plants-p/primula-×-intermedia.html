Primula × intermedia

Primula × intermedia, commonly known as the hybrid primrose, is a flowering plant belonging to the Primulaceae family.

It is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Primula vulgaris and Primula veris, showcasing a blend of characteristics from both parents. The plant typically produces showy, clustered flowers in shades of white, yellow, and pink, which bloom in early spring. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value.

Its foliage consists of broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, downy texture.
