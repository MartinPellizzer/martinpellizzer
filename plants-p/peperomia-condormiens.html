Peperomia condormiens

Peperomia condormiens, commonly known as the Condor Mountains peperomia, is a compact, low-growing perennial plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It features thick, waxy leaves that are typically rounded or ovate, often with a slightly notched or serrated edge. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in shades of green or white, which are borne on short, upright spikes. It is well-suited for indoor cultivation due to its ability to thrive in low light and minimal watering.

Its foliage is prized for its aesthetic appeal and air-purifying qualities.
