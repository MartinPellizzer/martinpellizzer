Patagonium renjifoanum

Patagonium renjifoanum, commonly known as the Andean bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Andes in South America.

It is characterized by its bell-shaped, violet-blue flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in cool, temperate climates. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often covered in a fine layer of white hairs.

Patagonium renjifoanum is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
