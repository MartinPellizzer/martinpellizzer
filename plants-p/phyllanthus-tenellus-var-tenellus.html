Phyllanthus tenellus var. tenellus

Phyllanthus tenellus var. tenellus, commonly known as the small-leaf gooseberry, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and Asia.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 3 meters, featuring small, elliptical leaves with a glossy green upper surface. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, followed by small, round fruits that turn red when mature. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, well-drained soils in forested areas.

The species is notable for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
