Potentilla adenophylla var. eglandulosa

Potentilla adenophylla var. eglandulosa, commonly known as the glandular potentilla, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its small, rose-like flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant features slender stems covered in glandular hairs, which give it its specific epithet "eglandulosa.". Its leaves are typically narrow and slightly hairy, adapted to withstand harsh alpine conditions.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with well-drained soil and full sun.
