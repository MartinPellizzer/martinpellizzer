Pachira macrocalyx

Pachira macrocalyx, commonly known as the giant pachira or water spinach, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and distinctive, showy yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. Its roots are edible and have been used in traditional cuisines.

Pachira macrocalyx is also valued for its ability to purify water, making it useful in aquatic environments.
