Pouteria peduncularis

Pouteria peduncularis, commonly known as the Brazilian star apple, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Amazon Basin.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and large, elliptical leaves. The tree produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by edible, star-shaped fruits with a sweet, creamy pulp. The fruit is highly valued for its flavor and is often consumed fresh or used in culinary applications.

Pouteria peduncularis is also known for its ecological importance in supporting local biodiversity.
