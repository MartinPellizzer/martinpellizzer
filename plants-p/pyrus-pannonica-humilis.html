Pyrus pannonica humilis

Pyrus pannonica humilis, commonly known as the Hungarian whitebeam, is a small deciduous tree or shrub native to the Carpathian Basin.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, elongated leaves and small white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces edible berries that are typically consumed by birds and small mammals. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forested areas or along riverbanks.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
