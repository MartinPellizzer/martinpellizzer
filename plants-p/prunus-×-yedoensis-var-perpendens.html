Prunus × yedoensis var. perpendens

Prunus × yedoensis var. perpendens, commonly known as the Japanese cherry blossom, is a variety of Prunus species native to Japan.

It is characterized by its delicate, pink to white flowers that bloom in early spring, creating a spectacular display. The plant is a deciduous tree with a rounded canopy and smooth, grey bark. Its flowers are borne in clusters and are highly fragrant, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds.

The leaves are oval-shaped, with a glossy green surface that turns yellow in autumn.
