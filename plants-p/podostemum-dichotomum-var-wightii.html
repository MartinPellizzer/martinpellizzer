Podostemum dichotomum var. wightii

Podostemum dichotomum var. wightii, commonly known as the Wight's split-leaved water plant, is a unique aquatic plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its small, opposite leaves that are often divided into two lobes, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant thrives in slow-moving freshwater environments, such as streams and rivers, where it attaches to rocks with its root system. Its flowers are typically small and inconspicuous, with a short pedicel, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits.

This species is of interest to botanists due to its specialized adaptations to aquatic habitats and its role in local ecosystems.
