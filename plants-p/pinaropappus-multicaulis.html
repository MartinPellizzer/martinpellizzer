Pinaropappus multicaulis

Pinaropappus multicaulis, commonly known as the desert flax, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its fibrous, branching stems and narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in spring, followed by fluffy, seed-bearing capsules. Its fibrous stalks are valued for their historical use in making textiles and ropes.

Pinaropappus multicaulis is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments with minimal water availability.
