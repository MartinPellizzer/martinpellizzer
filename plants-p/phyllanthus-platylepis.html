Phyllanthus platylepis

Phyllanthus platylepis, commonly known as the broad-leaf phyllanthus, is a member of the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is a shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia, including India, China, and Malaysia. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. Its small, clustered flowers are usually yellow or greenish-yellow, and it produces capsule fruits containing numerous seeds.

The plant is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and forest edges.
