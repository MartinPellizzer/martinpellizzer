Pleurothallis versicolor

Pleurothallis versicolor, commonly known as the variable pleurothallis, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, often fragrant flowers that display a range of colors. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded forest environments. Its leaves are usually narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, clustered inflorescences.

Due to its striking appearance and rarity, it is highly sought after by orchid enthusiasts and conservationists alike.
