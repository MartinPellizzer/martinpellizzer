Polystichum × flagellare

Polystichum × flagellare, commonly known as the silver fern, is a hybrid fern that exhibits a combination of characteristics from its parent species.

It is native to the temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere and thrives in shaded, moist environments. The plant features lance-shaped, silver-veined leaves that are often described as having a "flagellate" or whip-like appearance, giving it its scientific name. Its fronds are typically evergreen, providing year-round visual interest in gardens.

This fern is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in shaded woodland gardens.
