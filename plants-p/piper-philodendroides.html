Piper philodendroides

Piper philodendroides, commonly known as the creeping pipevine, is a tropical vine native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many species of flowering plants. The plant features heart-shaped, glossy leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence. It produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in spikes, and its fruits are three-winged berries.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to climb structures in shaded environments.
