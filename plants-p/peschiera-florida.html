Peschiera florida

Peschiera florida, commonly known as the Italian bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to southern Europe.

It produces striking blue or violet flowers that bloom in the summer months. The plant has lance-shaped leaves and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.

The botanical name reflects its historical association with the town of Peschiera in Italy.
