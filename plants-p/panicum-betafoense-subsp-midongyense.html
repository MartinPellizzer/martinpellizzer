Panicum betafoense subsp. midongyense

Panicum betafoense subsp.

midongyense, commonly known as the Madagascar grass, is a perennial grass species native to Madagascar. It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit.

The plant typically has narrow, linear leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in open grasslands and disturbed soils.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
