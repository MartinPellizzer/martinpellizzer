Petalodiscus mimosoides

Petalodiscus mimosoides, commonly known as the silky acacia, is a small, deciduous tree native to the tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its feathery, bipinnate leaves that give it a delicate, fern-like appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in dense, globular clusters, which are followed by long, slender pods. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires a warm, humid climate.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is often cultivated in gardens and as a shade tree in tropical landscapes.
