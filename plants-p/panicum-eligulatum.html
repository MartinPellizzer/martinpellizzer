Panicum eligulatum

Panicum eligulatum, commonly known as silverleaf panicgrass, is a warm-season grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, silver-gray leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, fluffy seed heads that are often used in ornamental landscaping due to their visual appeal. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it suitable for xeriscaping and erosion control.

Panicum eligulatum is also valued for its ability to attract wildlife, including birds and insects.
