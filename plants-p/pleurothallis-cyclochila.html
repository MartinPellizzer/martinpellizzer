Pleurothallis cyclochila

Pleurothallis cyclochila, commonly known as the spiral-leaf orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to the cloud forests of Central and South America.

This epiphytic plant typically grows on trees and has small, delicate leaves that are arranged in a spiral pattern, giving it its common name. It produces clusters of fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom during the warmer months. The plant prefers high humidity and indirect light, making it a popular choice for tropical terrariums and greenhouses.

Its unique appearance and relatively easy care requirements make it a sought-after species among orchid enthusiasts.
