Polygonatum acuminatifolium

Polygonatum acuminatifolium, commonly known as the sharp-leaved Solomon's seal, is a perennial herb native to eastern Asia.

It features long, arching stems that bear opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a pointed tip. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in racemes, followed by red berries that ripen in late summer. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and traditional herbal medicine.
