Philadelphus caucasicus

Philadelphus caucasicus, commonly known as the Caucasian mock orange, is a deciduous shrub native to the Caucasus region.

It features fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has opposite, simple, and ovate leaves with a slightly hairy surface. It typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters and prefers well-drained soil.

The bark is smooth and grayish, becoming fissured with age, and the plant is often used in ornamental gardening for its pleasant fragrance and showy blooms.
