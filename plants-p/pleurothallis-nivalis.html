Pleurothallis nivalis

Pleurothallis nivalis, commonly known as the snow orchid, is a species of orchid native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a small, epiphytic plant that thrives in high-altitude cloud forests, often growing on tree branches or rocks. The plant produces delicate, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, with a unique, intricate structure. It has a short, rhizomatous stem and lance-shaped leaves that are adapted to conserve moisture.

Due to its habitat and appearance, it is a prized plant among orchid enthusiasts and botanists alike.
