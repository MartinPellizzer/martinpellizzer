Prosthechea vespa

Prosthechea vespa, commonly known as the wasp orchid, is a species of epiphytic orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, wasp-like appearance, with dark green leaves and elongated, narrow pseudobulbs. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of yellow and green, often resembling the appearance of a wasp. Prosthechea vespa thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining substrates for optimal growth.

Its unique morphology and ecological adaptations make it a notable species in the orchid family.
