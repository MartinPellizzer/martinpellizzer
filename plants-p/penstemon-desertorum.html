Penstemon desertorum

Penstemon desertorum, commonly known as the desert penstemon, is a perennial flowering plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States.

It features upright, hairy stems and produces showy, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The plant is adapted to hot, dry environments and has a deep root system for water absorption. Its foliage consists of narrow, gray-green leaves that help reduce water loss.

Penstemon desertorum is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
