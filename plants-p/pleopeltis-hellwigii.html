Pleopeltis hellwigii

Pleopeltis hellwigii, commonly known as the Hellwig's rock fern, is a tropical fern native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its compact, evergreen foliage. The plant typically grows on rock surfaces or in shaded, humid environments, showcasing its lithophytic growth habit. Its fronds are usually leathery and have a distinctive, intricate pattern of veins.

Pleopeltis hellwigii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in greenhouses or as a houseplant.
