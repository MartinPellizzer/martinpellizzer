Pyrus spinosa var. diapulidis

Pyrus spinosa var. diapulidis, commonly known as the prickly pear.

This variant of the common pear is a shrub native to southern Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its thorny stems and small, greenish-yellow flowers. The plant produces edible berries that are typically harvested in the autumn.

It is often cultivated for both ornamental purposes and as a source of fruit in certain regions.
