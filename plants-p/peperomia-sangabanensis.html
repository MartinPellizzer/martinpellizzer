Peperomia sangabanensis

Peperomia sangabanensis, commonly known as the Philippine peperomia, is a species of flowering plant native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the family Piperaceae and is characterized by its compact, evergreen foliage and small, clustered inflorescences. The plant typically grows as a shrub or herb, with lance-shaped leaves that are often glossy and dark green. It prefers bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a suitable houseplant.

Peperomia sangabanensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ease of care in indoor environments.
