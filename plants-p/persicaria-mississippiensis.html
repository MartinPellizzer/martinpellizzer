Persicaria mississippiensis

Persicaria mississippiensis, commonly known as the Mississippi polygon, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features upright stems that can grow up to 2 meters tall, covered with small, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of tiny, pinkish-white flowers in late summer, which are borne on slender, branched inflorescences. It thrives in wet or moist soils, often found in swampy areas and along streambanks.

Its rapid growth and ability to spread vegetatively make it an effective plant for erosion control in wetland environments.
