Phaseolus vulgaris var. savianus

Phaseolus vulgaris var. savianus, commonly known as the Savian bean, is a variety of common bean native to the Andean region of South America.

It is characterized by its small, elongated, and usually yellow or green pods, which contain edible seeds. The plant is an annual legume that thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth. It produces clusters of white or pale purple flowers, which are typical of the Phaseolus genus.

This variety is valued for its nutritional content and is often cultivated for both culinary and agricultural purposes.
