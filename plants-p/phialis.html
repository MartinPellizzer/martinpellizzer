Phialis

Phialis, commonly known as the ghost flower, is a unique plant native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its translucent, waxy leaves that resemble ghostly apparitions, giving it its mystical reputation. The plant produces small, pale flowers that bloom at night, adding to its enigmatic appearance. Botanically, it belongs to the family Araceae, which includes other plants like taro and calla lilies.

Due to its rarity and unusual features, it is a subject of interest in both botanical studies and folklore.
