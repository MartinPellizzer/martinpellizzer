Potentilla jucunda

Potentilla jucunda, commonly known as the creeping cinquefoil, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. The plant has wiry, spreading stems and is often found in rocky or sandy soils. Its leaves are typically divided into five oval lobes, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Potentilla jucunda is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
