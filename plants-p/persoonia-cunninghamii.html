Persoonia cunninghamii

Persoonia cunninghamii, commonly known as the silver-leafed geebung, is a shrub native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, silver-green leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in spikes, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy habitats.

Persoonia cunninghamii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
