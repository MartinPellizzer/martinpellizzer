Prosorus cyanospermus

Prosorus cyanospermus, commonly known as the blue spore plant, is a unique species characterized by its striking blue pigmentation.

This coloration is primarily due to the presence of specialized pigments called cyanosperms, which are not commonly found in other plant species. The plant exhibits a compact growth habit with small, clustered leaves that are covered in a waxy cuticle. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in temperate forest understories.

Its botanical significance lies in its potential role in ecological studies due to its unusual pigmentation and adaptive traits.
