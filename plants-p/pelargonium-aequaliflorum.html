Pelargonium aequaliflorum

Pelargonium aequaliflorum, commonly known as the equal-flowered geranium, is a species native to South Africa.

It is a perennial herb with upright, branching stems that are often hairy. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of pink, red, or white, typically blooming in spring and summer. Its leaves are ovate to lance-shaped, with serrated edges and a slightly woolly texture.

Pelargonium aequaliflorum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
