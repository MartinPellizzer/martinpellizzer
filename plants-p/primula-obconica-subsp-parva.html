Primula obconica subsp. parva

Primula obconica subsp.

parva, commonly known as the dwarf primrose, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It features compact, low-growing plants with showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple.

The leaves are broadly ovate to elliptic, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky soils.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its delicate blooms.
