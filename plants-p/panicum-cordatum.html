Panicum cordatum

Panicum cordatum, commonly known as the heartleaf panicgrass, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It features slender, grass-like leaves with a distinctive heart-shaped base, which gives it its common name. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences that are often clustered at the tips of the stems. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along the edges of ponds.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable plant for ecological studies and wetland restoration projects.
