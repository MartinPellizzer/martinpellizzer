Passiflora glabrata

Passiflora glabrata, commonly known as the smooth passionflower, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, complex flowers that feature five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments. The plant produces edible, orange-yellow berries that are rich in nutrients. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered with a smooth, waxy surface.

Passiflora glabrata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
