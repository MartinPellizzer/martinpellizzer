Passiflora vitifolia

Passiflora vitifolia, commonly known as the grapeleaf passionflower, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, complex flowers that feature five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments. The leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a rough, hairy texture. It produces small, blue-black berries that are edible but not widely cultivated for consumption.

This species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and is often grown as a decorative plant in gardens.
