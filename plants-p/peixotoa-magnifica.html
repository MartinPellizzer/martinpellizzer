Peixotoa magnifica

Peixotoa magnifica, commonly known as the grandiose beauty, is a striking member of the Malvaceae family, native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from deep red to vibrant pink, often with intricate patterns. The plant features palmate leaves with a velvety texture and a strong, pleasant fragrance. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil to flourish.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant in tropical regions.
