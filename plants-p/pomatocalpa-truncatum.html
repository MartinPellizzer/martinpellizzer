Pomatocalpa truncatum

Pomatocalpa truncatum, commonly known as the truncated spider orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the southern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and small, greenish-yellow flowers with a distinctive, recurved lip. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as prairies and rocky soils. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often arranged in a rosette at the base of the plant.

The species is notable for its unique floral structure and is often found in association with specific mycorrhizal fungi.
