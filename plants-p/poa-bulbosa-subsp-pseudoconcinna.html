Poa bulbosa subsp. pseudoconcinna

Poa bulbosa subsp.

pseudoconcinna, commonly known as the slender bulbous grass, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States. It is characterized by its low-growing habit, with slender, grass-like leaves and small, spike-like inflorescences.

The plant produces bulbous structures that help it survive in harsh, dry environments. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes.

Its botanical features make it an important component of alpine and steppe ecosystems.
