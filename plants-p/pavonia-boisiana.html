Pavonia boisiana

Pavonia boisiana, commonly known as the redroot false mallow, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, featuring upright stems covered in fine hairs. The plant produces showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Pavonia boisiana thrives in full sun to partial shade and is adaptable to various soil types, making it a popular choice for native gardens.
