Piper hispidum f. patulipilum

Piper hispidum f. patulipilum, commonly known as the hairy pepper plant, is a variant of the genus Piper native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it its scientific name. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that develop into berries, typical of the Piper genus. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in the understory of tropical forests.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for taxonomic study and ecological research.
