Prunus × lannesiana f. benitoranowo

Prunus × lannesiana f. benitoranowo, commonly known as the Benito Ranowo cherry, is a compact, ornamental shrub or small tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its dense, upright growth habit and produces clusters of small, white flowers in spring. The plant has dark green, glossy leaves that provide a striking contrast to its delicate blossoms. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.

Due to its unique form and floral display, it is a prized addition to horticultural collections.
