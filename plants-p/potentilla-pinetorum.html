Potentilla pinetorum

Potentilla pinetorum, commonly known as the pine mountain cinquefoil, is a flowering plant belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Europe and parts of western Asia. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in forested areas with acidic conditions. It produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The leaves are usually compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets, and the plant is often used in ornamental gardening for its hardiness and aesthetic appeal.
