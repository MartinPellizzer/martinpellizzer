Pulicaria dentata

Pulicaria dentata, commonly known as the toothed wormwood, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features a low-growing, mat-forming structure with gray-green, fuzzy leaves that have serrated edges. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Pulicaria dentata is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
