Pouteria garciniifolia

Pouteria garciniifolia, commonly known as the Brazilian nut tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is characterized by its large, glossy, elliptical leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into elongated, woody fruits. The fruit contains a single large, edible seed, which is the source of the Brazil nut. The tree can grow up to 40 meters tall and has a dense, buttressed root system.

It plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
