Parrycactus alamosanus

Parrycactus alamosanus, commonly known as the Alamos cactus, is a species of cactus native to the Sonoran Desert.

It is a small, spineless cactus that typically grows to a height of about 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant has a short, cylindrical stem that is often covered in a white, powdery coating. It produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, which are typically clustered at the ends of the stems.

This species is adapted to arid environments and is capable of storing water in its stem to survive periods of drought.
