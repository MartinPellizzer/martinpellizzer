Pothos grandispathus

Pothos grandispathus, commonly known as the giant arrowhead plant, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, waxy leaves that can grow up to 12 inches in length. The plant thrives in humid, warm environments and requires indirect sunlight for optimal growth. Its aerial roots allow it to climb and anchor itself to surfaces, making it a popular choice for hanging baskets or trellises.

Pothos grandispathus is also valued for its air-purifying qualities and ability to thrive in low-light conditions.
