Phyllodes strobilifera

Phyllodes strobilifera, commonly known as the Woolly Phyllodyne, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and distinctive, cylindrical flower spikes. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are clustered in dense, cylindrical inflorescences. Its leaves are modified into phyllodes, which are flat, leaf-like structures adapted for water conservation.

It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing its resilience to drought and heat.
