Passiflora sicyoides

Passiflora sicyoides, commonly known as the Hawaiian passionflower, is a climbing vine native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It is characterized by its striking, palmately lobed leaves and showy, five-petaled flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. The plant produces edible, yellow-orange berries that are rich in nutrients and have a tropical flavor. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

Passiflora sicyoides plays an important ecological role by providing food for native birds and insects.
