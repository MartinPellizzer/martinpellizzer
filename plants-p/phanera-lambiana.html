Phanera lambiana

Phanera lambiana, commonly known as the African moonflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a deciduous shrub that produces large, fragrant white flowers at night, which open in response to moonlight. The plant has compound leaves with lance-shaped leaflets and is adapted to arid environments. Its flowers are pollinated by moths and emit a strong scent to attract them.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its nocturnal blooms.
