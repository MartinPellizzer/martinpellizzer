Prostanthera stenophylla

Prostanthera stenophylla, commonly known as narrow-leaf mintbush, is a perennial shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal and inland regions. Its narrow, lance-shaped leaves are covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to purple.

Prostanthera stenophylla is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional Indigenous medicine.
