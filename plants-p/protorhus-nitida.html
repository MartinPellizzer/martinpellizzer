Protorhus nitida

Protorhus nitida, commonly known as the whitebark tree, is a deciduous tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, white bark that becomes fissured with age. The tree has simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a glossy upper surface. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by spherical, woody fruits containing several seeds.

Protorhus nitida is often found in rocky or coastal areas and is adapted to tolerate saline and arid conditions.
