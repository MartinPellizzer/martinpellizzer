Passiflora miersii

Passiflora miersii, commonly known as the Miers' passionflower, is a species native to South America, particularly found in regions of Brazil and Peru.

It is a climbing vine characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and striking, complex flowers that feature five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments. The plant produces edible, orange-yellow berries that are rich in nutrients and have a sweet, tangy flavor.

It thrives in humid, tropical environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and medicinal properties.
