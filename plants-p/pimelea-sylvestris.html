Pimelea sylvestris

Pimelea sylvestris, commonly known as the mountain tea tree, is a shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It typically grows in sandy or loamy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. The plant features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. Its leaves are narrow and evergreen, providing year-round foliage.

Pimelea sylvestris is drought-tolerant and well-suited for rock gardens or coastal landscapes.
