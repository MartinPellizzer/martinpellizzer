Piper papantlense

Piper papantlense, commonly known as the Mexican pepper plant, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper plants such as the common black pepper. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves and small, greenish-white flowers. It typically grows in humid, tropical environments and is often found in shaded forest understories.

The fruit, which is a small, three-lobed berry, is used in traditional medicine and for its aromatic properties.
