Prunus pseudoadenopoda

Prunus pseudoadenopoda, commonly known as the Chinese dwarf plum, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in early spring, followed by edible drupes that are typically yellow or red in color. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has some traditional medicinal uses in Chinese herbal medicine.

The species is relatively hardy and adaptable to a variety of soil conditions.
