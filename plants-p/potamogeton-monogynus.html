Potamogeton monogynus

Potamogeton monogynus, commonly known as the narrow-leaf pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in North America.

It has long, slender, grass-like leaves that are typically less than 2 millimeters wide. The plant produces small, unisexual flowers, with male flowers usually occurring in clusters at the base of the stem and female flowers solitary at the tips. It plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.

Potamogeton monogynus is often found in shallow lakes, ponds, and slow-moving streams.
