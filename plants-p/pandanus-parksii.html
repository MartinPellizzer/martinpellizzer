Pandanus parksii

Pandanus parksii, commonly known as the Hawaiian pandanus, is a tropical plant native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, fan-shaped leaves that are often used in traditional crafts. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that develop into fibrous, spiky fruits. Its leaves are fibrous and have been historically used for making thatch and rope.

Pandanus parksii thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and lowland areas.
