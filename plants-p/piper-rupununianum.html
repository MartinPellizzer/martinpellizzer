Piper rupununianum

Piper rupununianum, commonly known as the Bornean black pepper, is a climbing vine native to the rainforests of Borneo.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that develop into elongated, dark purple to black berries. Its fruit is rich in essential oils and has been used traditionally for its pungent flavor and medicinal properties.

The plant's botanical characteristics make it a valuable resource in both culinary and pharmacological contexts.
