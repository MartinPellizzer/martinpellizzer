Pourouma napoensis

Pourouma napoensis, commonly known as the Napo river bamboo, is a tall, clump-forming grass native to the Amazon Basin.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its slender, bamboo-like stems that can grow up to 10 meters in height. The plant has narrow, elongated leaves with a smooth surface and is often found in wetland and riparian habitats. It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.

Due to its rapid growth and adaptability, it is also considered a potential candidate for sustainable biomass production.
