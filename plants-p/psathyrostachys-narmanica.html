Psathyrostachys narmanica

Psathyrostachys narmanica, commonly known as the narrowleaf wild rice, is a grass species native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in wet or moist environments, often found in marshes or along riverbanks.

Due to its ecological importance and potential agricultural value, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
