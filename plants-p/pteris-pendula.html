Pteris pendula

Pteris pendula, commonly known as the hanging fern, is a species of fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, feathery fronds that drape gracefully from its rhizomes. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky crevices. Its leaves are bi-lobed and can reach lengths of up to 30 centimeters.

Pteris pendula is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
