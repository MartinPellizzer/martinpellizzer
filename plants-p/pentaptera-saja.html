Pentaptera saja

Pentaptera saja, commonly known as the five-winged plant, is a unique species native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive five-winged leaves, which are broad and often have a leathery texture. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest understories or near water bodies. Its flowers are small and clustered, usually in greenish-white or pale yellow hues.

The botanical features of Pentaptera saja make it an interesting subject for study in plant morphology and ecology.
