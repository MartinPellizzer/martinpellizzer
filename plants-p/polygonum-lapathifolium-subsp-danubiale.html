Polygonum lapathifolium subsp. danubiale

Polygonum lapathifolium subsp.

danubiale, commonly known as the Danube knotweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, characterized by its distinctive arrow-shaped leaves and clusters of small, greenish flowers.

The plant typically grows in moist habitats such as riverbanks, marshes, and wet meadows. Its stems are often reddish or purple, and it produces capsules that contain numerous tiny seeds.

It is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil in wet environments.
