Pelargonium englerianum

Pelargonium englerianum, commonly known as the Engler's geranium, is a species native to South Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows to a height of about 30 centimeters. The plant features heart-shaped, serrated leaves and produces clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Pelargonium englerianum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and adaptability to various garden conditions.
