Protasparagus transvaalensis

Protasparagus transvaalensis, commonly known as the Transvaal asparagus, is a perennial shrub native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asparagaceae family and is characterized by its dense, wiry stems and small, scale-like leaves. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, white flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its hardiness and ornamental value, it is a popular choice among gardeners in arid climates.
