Pediocactus simpsonii var. hermannii

Pediocactus simpsonii var. hermannii, commonly known as the Hermann's cactus, is a species of flowering cactus native to the southwestern United States and northwestern Mexico.

It is characterized by its dense, cylindrical stems that are typically green to blue-green in color. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires full sun exposure.

Due to its slow growth rate and specific habitat requirements, it is often cultivated in arid environments or as a collector's plant.
