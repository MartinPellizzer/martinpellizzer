Pelargonium oxyphyllum

Pelargonium oxyphyllum, commonly known as the sweet geranium, is a species of flowering plant in the family Geraniaceae.

It is native to South Africa and is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that range in color from pink to red. The plant features upright stems and hairy leaves, which are often tinged with red or purple. It is a perennial herb that thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.

The flowers are not only ornamental but also attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
