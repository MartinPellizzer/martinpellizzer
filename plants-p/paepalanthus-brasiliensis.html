Paepalanthus brasiliensis

Paepalanthus brasiliensis, commonly known as the Brazilian torch cactus, is a species of cactus native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny stems and clusters of bright yellow flowers. The plant is adapted to arid environments and thrives in full sun. Its flowers bloom during the dry season, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds.

The cactus has a compact growth habit and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
