Prunus spinosa subsp. euspinosa

Prunus spinosa subsp.

euspinosa, commonly known as the common blackthorn, is a shrub native to Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its dark, almost black berries that ripen in late summer.

The plant has thorny branches and small, oval-shaped leaves that are glossy green on top and pale underneath. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained, acidic conditions.

This species is often found in hedgerows and woodland edges, contributing to biodiversity in these habitats.
