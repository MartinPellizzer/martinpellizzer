Phyllanthus pulcherrimus

Phyllanthus pulcherrimus, commonly known as the beautiful phyllanthus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its small, clustered flowers and glossy, dark green leaves. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories or along riverbanks.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various health purposes.
