Phoradendron lamprophyllum

Phoradendron lamprophyllum, commonly known as the silverleaf mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on various host trees in the Americas.

It has a distinctive appearance with small, leathery leaves that are often covered in a whitish, powdery coating. The plant produces small, white or yellowish flowers that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. Its berries are usually red or orange and contain sticky seeds that adhere to the bark of host trees.

This species plays an important ecological role by providing food for birds and other animals while also influencing the health of its host trees.
