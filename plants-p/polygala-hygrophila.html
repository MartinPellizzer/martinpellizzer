Polygala hygrophila

Polygala hygrophila, commonly known as the swamp milk-vine, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant thrives in moist, shady environments, often found in wetlands and along stream banks. Its leaves are opposite, ovate to elliptic, and often have a slightly hairy surface.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable humidity and drainage.
