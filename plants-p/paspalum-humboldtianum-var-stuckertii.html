Paspalum humboldtianum var. stuckertii

Paspalum humboldtianum var. stuckertii, commonly known as the Stuckert's paspalum, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various ecological conditions.
