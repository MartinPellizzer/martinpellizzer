Phyllanthus senensis

Phyllanthus senensis, commonly known as the Indian gooseberry or amla, is a deciduous shrub native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a smooth, glossy surface. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by bright orange to red berries. Its fruits are rich in vitamin C and have been used in traditional medicine for centuries.

The plant thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for both ornamental and medicinal purposes.
