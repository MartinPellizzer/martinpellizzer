Pinus pinaster subsp. pinaster

Pinus pinaster subsp.

pinaster, commonly known as the maroon pine, is a subspecies native to the western Mediterranean region. It is a tall evergreen conifer with needle-like leaves that are typically 5 to 10 centimeters long.

The tree produces cones that are initially green but turn brown as they mature. Its wood is valued for its strength and durability, making it useful in construction and furniture-making.

The plant plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
