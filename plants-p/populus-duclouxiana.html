Populus duclouxiana

Populus duclouxiana, commonly known as the Ducloux's poplar, is a species of flowering plant in the family Salicaceae.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, particularly in countries like Kazakhstan and Kyrgyzstan. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and broad, heart-shaped leaves that are typically gray-green in color. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers in catkins, which are typical of the Salicaceae family.

Populus duclouxiana is often found in riparian zones and is adapted to harsh, high-altitude environments.
