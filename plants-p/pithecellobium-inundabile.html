Pithecellobium inundabile

Pithecellobium inundabile, commonly known as the water pithecellobium, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically alternate and composed of several ovate to elliptical leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by fruit that resembles a legume, containing edible seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetland or riparian habitats.

Due to its adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes or as a source of forage.
