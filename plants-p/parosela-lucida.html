Parosela lucida

Parosela lucida, commonly known as the shining parosela, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Campanulaceae and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and clusters of bell-shaped flowers. The leaves are typically lanceolate and covered in a fine, waxy coating that gives them a glossy appearance. The flowers, which range in color from white to pale blue, bloom in late spring or early summer.

This plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded forest understories.
