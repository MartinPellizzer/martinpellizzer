Pittosporum fissicalyx

Pittosporum fissicalyx, commonly known as the New Zealand Pittosporum, is a shrub or small tree native to New Zealand.

It features elliptical, dark green leaves that are leathery in texture and have a distinctive waxy coating. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are clustered in umbels, typically blooming in spring. These flowers are followed by small, yellowish berries that mature in autumn.

Pittosporum fissicalyx is often found in coastal and lowland forests, where it thrives in moist, well-drained soils.
