Picris nuristanica var. chinensis

Picris nuristanica var. chinensis, commonly known as Chinese groundsel, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia, including China and Nepal.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and medicinal properties. The plant typically grows in mountainous regions and has hairy, lance-shaped leaves. Its yellow flowers bloom in the summer and are followed by small, oval-shaped seeds.

It is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties.
