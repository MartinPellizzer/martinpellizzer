Pluchea microcephala

Pluchea microcephala, commonly known as slenderleaf false dandelion, is a herbaceous annual plant native to the Americas.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant typically grows in moist environments such as wetlands, stream banks, and marshes. Its small, yellowish flowers are followed by fluffy, white seeds that are dispersed by the wind.

The leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and covered with fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
