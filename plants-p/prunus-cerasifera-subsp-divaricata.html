Prunus cerasifera subsp. divaricata

Prunus cerasifera subsp.

divaricata, commonly known as the cherry laurel or European cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Europe. It features dark green, glossy leaves that turn yellow in autumn, and it produces small, white, fragrant flowers in spring. The plant is characterized by its spreading, often irregular growth habit and its ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.

It is also known for its ornamental value and is sometimes used in landscaping for its aesthetic appeal.
