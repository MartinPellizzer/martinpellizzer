Potentilla flabellifolia subsp. matsumurae

Potentilla flabellifolia subsp.

matsumurae, commonly known as the Matsumura's Cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to East Asia. It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically divided into five to seven lobes, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the spring, often arranged in clusters. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and blooms.
