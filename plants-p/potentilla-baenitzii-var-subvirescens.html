Potentilla baenitzii var. subvirescens

Potentilla baenitzii var. subvirescens, commonly known as the greenleaf potentilla, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia, particularly China.

It is a shrub characterized by its upright growth habit and dense, compact foliage. The leaves are typically oval to elliptical in shape, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface, and they tend to retain a fresh green color throughout the growing season. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow, appearing in late spring to early summer.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
