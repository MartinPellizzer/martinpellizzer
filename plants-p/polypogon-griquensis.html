Polypogon griquensis

Polypogon griquensis, commonly known as the Griquas grass, is a perennial grass native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in grasslands and open woodlands, preferring well-drained soils. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in such conditions.

Its botanical features make it an important component of local ecosystems and potentially valuable for ecological restoration projects.
