Parodia prolifera

Parodia prolifera, commonly known as the "Proliferating Cactus," is a species of flowering cactus native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, globular shape and dense clusters of small, ribbed stems. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the warmer months, typically in spring or early summer. Its foliage is adapted to arid environments, with thick, fleshy stems that store water.

Parodia prolifera is popular among succulent enthusiasts for its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
