Phaca incana

Phaca incana, commonly known as the hairy phaca, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features slender, hairy stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. It produces small yellow flowers that are clustered in spherical heads.

Phaca incana is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments.
