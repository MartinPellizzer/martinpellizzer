Paepalanthus parvifolius

Paepalanthus parvifolius, commonly known as the small-leaved puya, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Xanthorrhoeaceae family and is characterized by its rosette growth habit and showy inflorescences. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that are clustered in dense, spike-like structures. It is adapted to high-altitude environments and thrives in well-drained, rocky soils.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
