Prunus serrulata var. densiflora

Prunus serrulata var. densiflora, commonly known as the Japanese cherry blossom or some varieties as the mountain cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its dense, showy clusters of white or pink flowers that bloom in early spring, making it a popular ornamental plant. The leaves are oval-shaped with serrated edges, and the bark is smooth and reddish-brown when young. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, often found in forested areas.

This variety is closely related to other Prunus species and is valued for its aesthetic appeal and ecological benefits.
