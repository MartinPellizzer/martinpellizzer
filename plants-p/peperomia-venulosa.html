Peperomia venulosa

Peperomia venulosa, commonly known as the veiny peperomia, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and distinctive, waxy, veined leaves that range in color from deep green to variegated shades. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in spikes, though they are not showy. It thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Its unique foliage makes it a desirable ornamental plant for both novice and experienced growers.
