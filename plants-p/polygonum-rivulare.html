Polygonum rivulare

Polygonum rivulare, commonly known as water pepper, is a flowering plant in the family Polygonaceae.

It is characterized by its prostrate growth habit and small, oval leaves that are often reddish in color. The plant produces tiny, greenish-white flowers that appear in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in moist, wet environments such as marshes, stream banks, and roadside ditches.

Polygonum rivulare is known for its ability to spread rapidly through rhizomes, making it both a common weed and an indicator of wetland habitats.
