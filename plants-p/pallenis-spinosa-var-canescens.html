Pallenis spinosa var. canescens

Pallenis spinosa var. canescens, commonly known as the hairy pallenis, is a variant of the pallenis plant characterized by its distinctive hairy foliage.

This plant is native to specific regions in southern Africa, where it thrives in arid and semi-arid environments. Its leaves are typically narrow and covered in fine, white hairs, which help reduce water loss in harsh conditions. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers during the warmer months, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its unique adaptations, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens for its ornamental value and ecological significance.
