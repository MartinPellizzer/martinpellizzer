Papaver macrostomum var. dalechianum

Papaver macrostomum var. dalechianum, commonly known as the Dalechianum poppy, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features striking, large, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from deep red to orange, with a distinctive blackish center. The plant has smooth, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in rocky or alpine environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
