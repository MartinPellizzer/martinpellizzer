Phragmipedium chapadense

Phragmipedium chapadense, commonly known as the Chapada orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the Amazon basin.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and large, showy flowers that typically bloom in late summer. The plant produces pendulous inflorescences with numerous flowers, each featuring a distinctive lip that is often brightly colored. Its leaves are broad and ovate, and it thrives in humid, shaded environments.

Phragmipedium chapadense is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
