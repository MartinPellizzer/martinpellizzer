Piptatherum songaricum var. kokanicum

Piptatherum songaricum var. kokanicum, commonly known as the Kokan grass, is a perennial grass species native to the temperate regions of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along riverbanks. The plant has slender, upright stems and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny florets. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a slight sheen, and it is often used in traditional landscaping and erosion control.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in supporting local wildlife.
