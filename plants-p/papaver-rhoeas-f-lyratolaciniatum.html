Papaver rhoeas f. lyratolaciniatum

Papaver rhoeas f. lyratolaciniatum, commonly known as the lyrated scarlet poppy, is a botanical variant of the common poppy.

It is characterized by its striking, large, and showy flowers with a deep red color and intricate, lyrated petal patterns. The plant typically grows in sunny, well-drained soils and is native to Europe. Its foliage consists of lobed, hairy leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white wax.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
