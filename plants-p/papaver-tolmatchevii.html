Papaver tolmatchevii

Papaver tolmatchevii, commonly known as the Tolmachev's poppy, is a species of flowering plant in the family Papaveraceae.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, particularly Kazakhstan and Russia. The plant is characterized by its striking, bright red flowers with a dark central stigma, which bloom in late summer. It produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds, typical of the Papaver genus.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
