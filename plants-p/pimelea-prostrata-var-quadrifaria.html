Pimelea prostrata var. quadrifaria

Pimelea prostrata var. quadrifaria, commonly known as the prostrate bottlebrush, is a low-growing shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It features dense clusters of bright red or orange flowers that bloom in spring, attracting nectar-feeding birds and insects. The plant has wiry, ascending stems with small, elliptical leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a ground cover.

Its ornamental value and ecological benefits make it a popular choice in native landscaping.
