Pediocactus pseudomacrochele var. krainzianus

Pediocactus pseudomacrochele var. krainzianus, commonly known as the Krainz's hedgehog cactus, is a compact, spineless cactus native to the arid regions of northern Mexico.

It features a globular to cylindrical stem that is typically greenish-gray in color, often covered with a whitish coating. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, which are clustered near the apex. It is well adapted to dry environments, storing water in its thick, fleshy stems.

Due to its slow growth and specific habitat requirements, it is a sought-after species among cactus enthusiasts.
