Parthenium argentatum f. marioloides

Parthenium argentatum f. marioloides, commonly known as the silverleaf nightshade, is a variant of the silverleaf plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage, which is covered in fine hairs, and its small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant is an annual or biennial herb that thrives in arid and semi-arid environments. It is often found in disturbed soils and can be invasive in certain regions.

Its botanical features make it distinguishable from other species in the genus Parthenium.
