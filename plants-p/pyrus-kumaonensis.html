Pyrus kumaonensis

Pyrus kumaonensis, commonly known as the Kumaon pear, is a deciduous tree native to the Himalayas, specifically found in the Indian states of Uttarakhand and Himachal Pradesh.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves and showy white flowers that bloom in early spring. The tree produces small, yellowish-green fruits that ripen in late summer, known for their sweet and aromatic flavor.

It thrives in cool, moist climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value as well as its edible fruit.
