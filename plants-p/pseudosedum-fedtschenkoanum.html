Pseudosedum fedtschenkoanum

Pseudosedum fedtschenkoanum, commonly known as the Fedtschenko's stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, low-growing form and fleshy, thick leaves that store water, typical of succulents. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow or orange, usually during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
