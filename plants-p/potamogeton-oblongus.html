Potamogeton oblongus

Potamogeton oblongus, commonly known as the broadleaf pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to North America.

It has long, narrow leaves that are typically arranged in whorls around the stem. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are clustered in dense spikes, which emerge from the water surface. It thrives in shallow, freshwater environments such as lakes and ponds.

Its dense growth helps stabilize sediments and provides habitat for aquatic organisms.
