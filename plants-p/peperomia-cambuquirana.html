Peperomia cambuquirana

Peperomia cambuquirana, commonly known as the Brazilian peperomia, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It features thick, waxy leaves that are typically green with a slight reddish or purple tinge on the underside. The plant produces small, clustered flowers in shades of white or pale pink, which bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in bright, indirect light and prefers well-draining soil with moderate humidity.

Peperomia cambuquirana is relatively low-maintenance, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.
