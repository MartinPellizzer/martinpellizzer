Polygonum jurii

Polygonum jurii, commonly known as the Chinese knotweed, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, characterized by its distinctive arrow-shaped leaves and clusters of small, pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its stems are often reddish and can become woody with age, while its leaves are smooth and have a slightly succulent texture.

Polygonum jurii is valued in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
