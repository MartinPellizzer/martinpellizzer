Polygonum uniflorum

Polygonum uniflorum, commonly known as the one-flowered knotweed, is a perennial herb native to parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, which includes plants known for their distinctive leaf shapes and flowering structures. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as stream banks and forest floors. Its leaves are narrow and tapering, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

The small, clustered flowers appear in late summer and are usually greenish-white in color.
