Populus brabantica

Populus brabantica, commonly known as Brabant poplar, is a deciduous tree native to parts of Europe.

It belongs to the Salicaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and catkin flowers. The tree typically grows in moist, fertile soils along riverbanks and wetlands. It produces small, winged seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Populus brabantica is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
