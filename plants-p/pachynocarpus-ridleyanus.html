Pachynocarpus ridleyanus

Pachynocarpus ridleyanus, commonly known as the Malabar banyan, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Western Ghats of India.

It belongs to the family Moraceae and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and distinctive, pendulous fruit clusters. The plant exhibits a unique growth pattern with aerial roots that can develop into additional trunks, giving it a banyan tree appearance. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in dense tropical forests.

The fruit is edible, with a fibrous outer layer and a sweet, starchy interior.
