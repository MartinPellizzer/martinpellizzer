Primula malvacea var. intermedia

Primula malvacea var. intermedia, commonly known as the hybrid primrose, is a herbaceous perennial that exhibits a unique blend of characteristics from its parent species.

It features heart-shaped, velvety leaves with a distinctive silvery-blue underside, contributing to its ornamental appeal. The plant produces clusters of showy, five-petaled flowers in shades of pink, white, and lavender, typically blooming in early spring. It thrives in cool, moist environments and prefers partial to full shade, making it suitable for woodland gardens.

Its botanical significance lies in its role as a hybrid species, showcasing the genetic diversity within the Primula genus.
