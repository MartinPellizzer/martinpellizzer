Pittosporum enkianthoides

Pittosporum enkianthoides, commonly known as the silverleaf pittosporum, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to New Zealand.

It features lance-shaped leaves that are covered in a fine, silvery-white pubescence, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, yellowish-white flowers in late winter to early spring, followed by small, bright red berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value.

The species is also valued for its ability to attract native birds and insects.
