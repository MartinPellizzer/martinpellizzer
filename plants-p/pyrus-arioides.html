Pyrus arioides

Pyrus arioides, commonly known as the African white pear, is a deciduous tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and lance-shaped leaves that turn yellow in autumn. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers in spring, which are followed by edible, yellowish fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires a sunny to partially shaded location.

Due to its ornamental value and fruit, it is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
