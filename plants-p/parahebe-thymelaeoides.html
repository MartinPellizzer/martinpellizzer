Parahebe thymelaeoides

Parahebe thymelaeoides, commonly known as the New Zealand bellflower, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to New Zealand.

It features lance-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and slightly hairy. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue or purple, which bloom in spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is often cultivated in gardens in temperate climates.
