Polygonum cognatum subsp. cognatum

Polygonum cognatum subsp.

cognatum, commonly known as red polygonum, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Polygonaceae, which includes species known for their distinctive leaf shapes and clusters of small flowers.

The plant is characterized by its upright stems, heart-shaped leaves, and vibrant red or pink flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in supporting local wildlife.
