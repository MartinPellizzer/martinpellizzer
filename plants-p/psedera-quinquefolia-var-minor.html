Psedera quinquefolia var. minor

Psedera quinquefolia var. minor, commonly known as the lesser five-leafed bellflower, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its small, bell-shaped flowers that typically bloom in shades of blue or purple. The plant has five leaflets, which give it its scientific name, and it thrives in moist, shady environments. It is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

This variety is less common than its parent species and is valued for its ornamental qualities in gardens.
