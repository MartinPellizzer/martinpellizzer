Paspalum plicatulum var. microspermum

Paspalum plicatulum var. microspermum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf paspalum, is a perennial grass native to parts of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks. Its seeds are small and have a distinctive, compressed shape, which aids in wind dispersal.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
