Pedilochilus kermesinostriatus

Pedilochilus kermesinostriatus, commonly known as the Kermesinostris orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the high-altitude regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its erect, hairy stems and lanceolate leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture. The plant produces striking, spurred orchid flowers with a distinctive coloration, typically featuring a deep violet or purple hue. Its flowers are usually arranged in a raceme and bloom during the cooler months, making it a sought-after species among orchid enthusiasts.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and delicate plant, often found in cloud forests and montane grasslands.
