Penstemon staticifolius

Penstemon staticifolius, commonly known as the stickyleaf beardtongue, is a perennial plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its hairy, sticky leaves and showy, tubular flowers that range in color from purple to pink. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It is well adapted to arid environments and is often found in mountainous regions.

Penstemon staticifolius is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
