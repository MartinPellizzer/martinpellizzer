Phaseolus rubescens

Phaseolus rubescens, commonly known as the scarlet runner bean, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its climbing habit and showy, pink or red flowers. The plant produces elongated, edible pods that contain kidney-shaped seeds. It thrives in warm climates and is often cultivated for both its ornamental value and its nutritious beans.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for gardens and agricultural settings.
