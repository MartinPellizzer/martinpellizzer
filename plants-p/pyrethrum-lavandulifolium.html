Pyrethrum lavandulifolium

Pyrethrum lavandulifolium, commonly known as lavender pyrethrum, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, lance-shaped leaves and small, daisy-like yellow flowers. The plant is known for its high concentration of pyrethrins, which are natural insecticides used in organic pest control. Its foliage emits a strong, pleasant fragrance similar to lavender, contributing to its ornamental value.

Pyrethrum lavandulifolium thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and herbal cultivation.
