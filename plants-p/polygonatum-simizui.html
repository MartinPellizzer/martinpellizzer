Polygonatum simizui

Polygonatum simizui, commonly known as the Japanese Solomon's seal, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Asparagaceae family and is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit. The plant features lance-shaped leaves and produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in late spring. Its berries are red and edible, often consumed in traditional medicine.

Polygonatum simizui is valued for its ornamental appeal and medicinal properties in traditional Chinese and Japanese medicine.
