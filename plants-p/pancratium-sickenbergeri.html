Pancratium sickenbergeri

Pancratium sickenbergeri, commonly known as the Sickenberger's yellow nutmeg, is a rare and striking bulbous plant native to South Africa.

It features showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring, often in clusters at the top of its narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces distinctive red, fleshy berries that contain the seeds, which are dispersed by animals. It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
