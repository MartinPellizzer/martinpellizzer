Pilosocereus pentaedrophorus

Pilosocereus pentaedrophorus, commonly known as the five-ribbed cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of South America.

It is a columnar cactus characterized by its thick, ribbed stems and spiny areoles. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom during the warmer months, typically in spring or early summer. Its structure is adapted to conserve water, with a deep root system and reduced leaf surface.

Pilosocereus pentaedrophorus is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking appearance and resilience in dry conditions.
