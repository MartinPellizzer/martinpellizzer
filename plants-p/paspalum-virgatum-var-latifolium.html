Paspalum virgatum var. latifolium

Paspalum virgatum var. latifolium, commonly known as broadleaf paspalum, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its wide, flat leaves and dense, tufted growth habit. The plant typically grows in wetland areas, marshes, and along the edges of ponds and lakes. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers.

This species is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for wildlife.
