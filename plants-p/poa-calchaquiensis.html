Poa calchaquiensis

Poa calchaquiensis, commonly known as the Calchaqui grass, is a species of flowering plant in the grass family Poaceae.

It is native to the high Andean regions of Argentina and Chile, where it thrives in cold, arid environments. The plant is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and fine, blue-green foliage. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bloom in late spring or early summer.

Poa calchaquiensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing mountainous soils.
