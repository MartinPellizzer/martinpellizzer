Phyteuma corniculatum subsp. charmelii

Phyteuma corniculatum subsp.

charmelii, commonly known as the Charmer's bell, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe. It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow, often with a distinctive red or purple stripe down the center.

The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils in open habitats such as cliffs and slopes. It has narrow, linear leaves that are arranged in a basal rosette, and its flowering period occurs in late spring to early summer.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
