Peyotl xochimilcensis

Peyotl xochimilcensis, commonly known as the Mexican morning glory, is a climbing vine native to Central America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and striking blue or violet flowers that bloom in the early morning. The plant contains alkaloids, including lysergic acid amide (LAA), which contribute to its psychoactive properties. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Due to its cultural and historical significance, it is often cultivated in traditional and horticultural settings.
