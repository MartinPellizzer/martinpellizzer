Pappostipa speciosa var. parva

Pappostipa speciosa var. parva, commonly known as the dwarf silky grass, is a low-growing perennial grass native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its fine, silky hairs that cover the leaves and inflorescence, giving it a soft, delicate appearance. The plant produces small, spike-like clusters of flowers that are typically green to reddish in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
