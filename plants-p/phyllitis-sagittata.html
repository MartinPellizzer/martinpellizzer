Phyllitis sagittata

Phyllitis sagittata, commonly known as the arrowhead plant, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments in North America.

It features heart-shaped leaves that are typically alternate and have a distinctive arrowhead shape, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

Phyllitis sagittata plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
