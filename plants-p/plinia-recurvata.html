Plinia recurvata

Plinia recurvata, commonly known as the recurved huckleberry, is a species of flowering plant in the Ericaceae family.

It is native to the temperate regions of South America, particularly in Chile and Argentina. The plant is characterized by its small, evergreen leaves and produces clusters of edible berries that are typically red or purple in color. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas or along stream banks.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
