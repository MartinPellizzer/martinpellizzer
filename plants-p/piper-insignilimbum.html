Piper insignilimbum

Piper insignilimbum, commonly known as the purple-veined pepper plant, is a species native to the rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is a climbing vine that belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves with distinct purple veins, giving it its common name. It produces small, green berries that mature to a dark purple color.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its unique foliage.
