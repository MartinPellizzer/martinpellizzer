Peponidium densiflorum

Peponidium densiflorum, commonly known as the denseflower melon, is a member of the Cucurbitaceae family.

It is a climbing vine characterized by its hairy stems and large, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that are densely arranged along the stems. Its fruit is a spherical melon with a thick, rough rind and a sweet, aromatic pulp.

Peponidium densiflorum is native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.
