Pyrus bressensis

Pyrus bressensis, commonly known as the Bressan pear, is a deciduous tree native to the eastern Alps.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves and clusters of white flowers that bloom in spring. The tree produces small, yellowish fruits that ripen in late summer. It is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.

Due to its limited natural range, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
