Phylica tortuosa

Phylica tortuosa, commonly known as the zigzag phylica, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in coastal or inland regions. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in dense clusters, which are followed by distinctive, spiny seed pods. Its leaves are narrow and grayish-green, often covered in fine hairs.

Phylica tortuosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
