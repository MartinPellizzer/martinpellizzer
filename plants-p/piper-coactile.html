Piper coactile

Piper coactile, commonly known as the black pepper plant, is a climbing vine native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves with a rough texture. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into dark purple to black berries, which are the source of the spice black pepper. The berries contain a single seed, which is used in various culinary and medicinal applications.

Its aromatic properties make it a valuable plant in both traditional and modern contexts.
