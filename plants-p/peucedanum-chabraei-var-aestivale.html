Peucedanum chabraei var. aestivale

Peucedanum chabraei var. aestivale, commonly known as summer cow Parsley, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic foliage and umbellate flowers. The plant features finely divided, fern-like leaves and produces small white to greenish flowers in clustered umbels. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and historical use in traditional medicine, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
