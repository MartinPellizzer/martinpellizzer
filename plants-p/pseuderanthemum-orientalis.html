Pseuderanthemum orientalis

Pseuderanthemum orientalis, commonly known as the oriental false heather, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense foliage. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically white or pale pink, often arranged in clusters. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in tropical gardens for its attractive appearance and resilience.
