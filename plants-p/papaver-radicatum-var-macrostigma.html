Papaver radicatum var. macrostigma

Papaver radicatum var. macrostigma, commonly known as the large-seeded poppy, is a herbaceous perennial native to the western United States.

It features showy, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from red to orange, with a distinctive yellow or orange center. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are often dispersed by wind. Its foliage consists of lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats such as meadows and rocky areas.
