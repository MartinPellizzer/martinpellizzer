Panicum laxum var. vestitum

Panicum laxum var. vestitum, commonly known as the velvet panicgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a fine, silky hairs, giving it a soft, velvety texture. The plant produces panicles of small, greenish-white flowers that are loosely arranged, typical of the Panicum genus. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often found in moist to wet soils in wetlands or along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is sometimes used in wetland restoration projects.
