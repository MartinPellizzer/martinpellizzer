Pyrus sieboldii var. calocarpa

Pyrus sieboldii var. calocarpa, commonly known as the Japanese white pear, is a deciduous tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and heart-shaped leaves that are dark green on the upper surface and lighter underneath. The plant produces small, clustered white flowers in early spring, which are followed by sweet, yellowish fruit resembling pears. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular ornamental and fruit-bearing species in temperate climates.

The variety is noted for its ornamental value and its potential for cultivation as a fruit tree.
