Passiflora triloba

Passiflora triloba, commonly known as the tri-lobed passionflower, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, intricate flowers that feature a crown of filaments and a central corona, which are often brightly colored. The plant produces blue or purple berries that contain edible seeds. Its leaves are typically trifoliate, with three deeply lobed leaflets.

Passiflora triloba is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its potential calming properties.
