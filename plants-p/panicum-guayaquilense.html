Panicum guayaquilense

Panicum guayaquilense, commonly known as Guayaquil grass, is a species of grass native to South America, particularly Ecuador and Colombia.

It is a perennial plant characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered panicles of flowers that are typically greenish or brownish in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
