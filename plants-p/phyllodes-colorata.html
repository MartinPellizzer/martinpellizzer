Phyllodes colorata

Phyllodes colorata, commonly known as the yellow wattle, is a species of acacia native to Australia.

It is characterized by its flattened, leaf-like phyllodes that are typically bright yellow in color. The plant produces small, yellow flowers in spring, which are clustered in spherical heads. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands.

The yellow wattle is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
