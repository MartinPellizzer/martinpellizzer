Philadelphus discolor

Philadelphus discolor, commonly known as the white-flowered mock orange, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.

Philadelphus discolor is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
