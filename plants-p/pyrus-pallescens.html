Pyrus pallescens

Pyrus pallescens, commonly known as the pale pear.

It is a deciduous tree native to the western United States, particularly found in California and Oregon. The plant is characterized by its slender, often multi-stemmed growth habit and small, ovate leaves with a pale green underside. Its flowers are white and appear in clusters, followed by small, yellowish fruits that are edible but not commonly cultivated.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native habitats.
