Pleurothallis catoxys

Pleurothallis catoxys, commonly known as the yellow-tipped orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, pendulous inflorescences. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are small, fragrant, and often display a combination of yellow and green hues.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is a sought-after species among orchid enthusiasts.
