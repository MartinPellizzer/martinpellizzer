Phyllanthus phillyreifolius var. parvifolius

Phyllanthus phillyreifolius var. parvifolius, commonly known as the small-leaved spurge, is a species of flowering plant in the family Euphorbiaceae.

It is characterized by its small, ovate leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stems. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in cyathia, a unique floral structure typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa and is often found in dry, open habitats.

The plant contains various bioactive compounds and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported therapeutic properties.
