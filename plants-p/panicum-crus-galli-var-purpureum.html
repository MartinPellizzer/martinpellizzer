Panicum crus-galli var. purpureum

Panicum crus-galli var. purpureum, commonly known as purple lovegrass, is a perennial grass native to Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and loose, fluffy panicles that turn a striking purple hue as they mature. The plant produces small, wind-pollinated flowers, and its leaves are narrow and often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is commonly found in open grasslands and disturbed areas.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
