Potentilla guenneri

Potentilla guenneri, commonly known as the Sierra potentilla, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a low-growing shrub that typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Potentilla guenneri is adapted to rocky and well-drained soils, often found in alpine and subalpine environments.
