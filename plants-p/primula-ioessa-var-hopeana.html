Primula ioessa var. hopeana

Primula ioessa var. hopeana, commonly known as the Hope's primrose, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes in South America.

It is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and produces clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. The leaves are typically oval and covered in a dense layer of fine, fuzzy hairs, which help protect the plant from cold and intense sunlight. This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens or alpine settings.

It prefers well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, thriving in cooler climates.
