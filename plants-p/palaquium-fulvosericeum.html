Palaquium fulvosericeum

Palaquium fulvosericeum, commonly known as the red-leaf palaquium, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that turn reddish in color when mature. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by edible, fleshy fruits resembling small apples. Its bark is thick and deeply fissured, often exuding a milky sap when damaged.

It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland forests.
