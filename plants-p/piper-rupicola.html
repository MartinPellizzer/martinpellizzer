Piper rupicola

Piper rupicola, commonly known as the rock pepper, is a climbing shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many species of pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its slender, woody stems and opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy texture. Its small, greenish-white flowers are followed by small, round berries that turn red when mature.

The plant is often found growing in rocky or mountainous areas, where it thrives in well-drained soils and partial shade.
