Pelargonium × dilatatum

Pelargonium × dilatatum, commonly known as the ivy-leaved geranium, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Geraniaceae family.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and heart-shaped, slightly lobed leaves that resemble ivy leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, two-lipped flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, which bloom throughout the growing season. Native to South Africa, it thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.

It is widely cultivated as an ornamental plant for gardens and container gardening due to its attractive foliage and long-lasting blooms.
