Phyllostachys pubescens f. aureovariegata

Phyllostachys pubescens f. aureovariegata, commonly known as the golden bamboo or variegated bamboo, is a variant of the silver bamboo species.

It is characterized by its striking variegated foliage, featuring shades of yellow, white, and green on its leaves. This plant is a clumping bamboo that grows in dense stands, often reaching heights of up to 10 feet. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping.

Due to its aesthetic appeal and relatively low maintenance, it is widely cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
