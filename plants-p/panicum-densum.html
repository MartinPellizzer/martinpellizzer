Panicum densum

Panicum densum, commonly known as dense panicgrass, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in wetland habitats such as marshes and swamps, where it thrives in moist to saturated soil conditions. The plant has slender, upright stems with dense clusters of small, spikelet-like flowers, which are often tinged with red or purple hues. Its foliage is narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green coloration that becomes more pronounced during the growing season.

Panicum densum plays an important role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
