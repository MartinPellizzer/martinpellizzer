Prionolepis

Prionolepis, commonly known as the African violet, is a genus of flowering plants native to tropical regions of Africa.

These plants are characterized by their rosettes of fleshy, velvety leaves and showy, often colorful flowers. Prionolepis species typically thrive in humid, well-drained soil and require bright, indirect light for optimal growth. The flowers are usually borne on tall, leafless stems and come in a variety of colors including purple, blue, and white.

They are popular as houseplants due to their ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
