Phyllanthus klotzschianus var. linearis

Phyllanthus klotzschianus var. linearis, commonly known as the narrow-leaf phyllanthus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows up to 3 meters in height, characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in dense clusters, which are followed by spherical, woody fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in moist, shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is cultivated in some regions for horticultural and traditional use.
