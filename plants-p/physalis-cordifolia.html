Physalis cordifolia

Physalis cordifolia, commonly known as the heartleaf nightshade, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States and parts of Mexico.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of bright orange, lantern-like fruits. The plant belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes tomatoes, potatoes, and peppers. Its flowers are typically yellow with five petals, and it thrives in warm, sunny climates.

The fruits are edible when fully ripe but contain toxic alkaloids in their unripe state.
