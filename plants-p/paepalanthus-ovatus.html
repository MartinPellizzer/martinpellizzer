Paepalanthus ovatus

Paepalanthus ovatus, commonly known as the silver leaf cactus, is a species of flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is characterized by its cylindrical, ribbed stems that are covered in a dense layer of white, waxy hair, which helps reduce water loss in its arid habitat. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months, adding a striking contrast to its silvery foliage. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Due to its unique appearance and low maintenance, it is a popular choice for desert gardens and rockeries.
