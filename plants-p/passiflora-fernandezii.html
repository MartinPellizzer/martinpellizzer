Passiflora fernandezii

Passiflora fernandezii, commonly known as the Fernandez's passionflower, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly found in regions of Argentina and Chile.

It belongs to the Passifloraceae family and is characterized by its striking, complex flowers that feature five sepals, five petals, and a distinctive crown of filaments. The plant produces edible berries that are often used in local cuisines and traditional medicine. Its growth habit includes climbing vines that can reach several meters in length, supported by tendrils that help it attach to structures.

Passiflora fernandezii is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty and ecological benefits.
