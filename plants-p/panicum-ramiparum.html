Panicum ramiparum

Panicum ramiparum, commonly known as silky panicgrass, is a herbaceous annual plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features slender, upright stems that often branch near the base, supporting narrow, grass-like leaves with a silvery-blue hue due to fine pubescence. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing tiny, two-lobed flowers that are typically pale green to brown in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a common weed in agricultural fields and disturbed areas.

Its rapid growth and prolific seed production contribute to its invasive potential in certain environments.
