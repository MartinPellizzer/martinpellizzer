Psidium anceps

Psidium anceps, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or pitanga, is a small evergreen shrub or tree native to South America.

It produces small, edible berries that are typically red when ripe, with a sweet and tangy flavor. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are usually oval or elliptical in shape. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often grown for its ornamental value as well as its fruit.

The flowers are white or pale pink and are borne in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds.
