Portulaca ramosissima

Portulaca ramosissima, commonly known as moss rose, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and parts of Mexico.

It is a low-growing annual plant with succulent, fleshy stems and leaves that are typically green or reddish in color. The plant produces vibrant, showy flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, which bloom throughout the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Its ability to store water in its stems and leaves allows it to survive in arid conditions.
