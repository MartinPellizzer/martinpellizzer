Pothos curtisii

Pothos curtisii, commonly known as the Curtis's Pothos, is a tropical vine native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, waxy leaves that are typically a deep green with a slightly variegated pattern. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by red berries, though it is primarily cultivated for its ornamental foliage rather than its fruits. It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often used as a houseplant due to its adaptability and ease of care.

Pothos curtisii is a member of the Araceae family, which includes other popular houseplants like the peace lily.
