Polygonum persicaria subsp. rechingeri

Polygonum persicaria subsp.

rechingeri, commonly known as the red-rooted knotweed, is a perennial herb native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It typically grows in moist, disturbed habitats such as stream banks and woodland edges.

The plant features upright stems that are often reddish in color, especially near the base. Its leaves are heart-shaped with a serrated margin and a smooth upper surface.

It produces small, clustered flowers that are usually pink or reddish, blooming in late summer.
