Penstemon richardsonii var. curtiflorus

Penstemon richardsonii var. curtiflorus, commonly known as the short-flowered beardtongue, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of tubular, two-lipped flowers that are typically pink to purple in color. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance in gardens.
