Pycreus flavescens f. minor

Pycreus flavescens f. minor, commonly known as the lesser yellow brome, is a grass species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, erect growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The inflorescence consists of spikelets that are typically yellowish-green in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as meadows and rocky areas.

This plant is of interest in botanical studies due to its morphological variation and ecological adaptability.
