Piper excelsum subsp. psittacorum

Piper excelsum subsp.

psittacorum, commonly known as the psittacorum pepper plant, is a member of the Piperaceae family and is native to the rainforests of New Guinea. It is characterized by its climbing habit, with long, slender stems that can reach several meters in length.

The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by elongated, cylindrical berries that turn red when mature. Its leaves are alternate, ovate to elliptic in shape, and have a slightly rough texture.

The species is noted for its unique aromatic properties and is sometimes used in traditional medicinal practices.
