Piper avellanum var. angustifolium

Piper avellanum var. angustifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaf sycamore or sycamore pepper, is a climbing vine native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its slender, twining stems and dark green, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly serrated margin. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by clusters of elongated, reddish berries. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their aromatic properties.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal remedies.
