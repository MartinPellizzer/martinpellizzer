Prunus jamasakura f. rotunda

Prunus jamasakura f. rotunda, commonly known as the rounded Japanese cherry, is a cultivar of the Prunus jamasakura species.

It is characterized by its distinctive rounded form, which sets it apart from the more typical weeping shape of its parent species. The plant produces clusters of pale pink to white flowers in early spring, adding ornamental value to gardens. Its leaves are ovate and have a smooth texture, turning a vibrant red in the autumn.

This variety is prized for its unique shape and seasonal floral display, making it a popular choice in horticulture.
