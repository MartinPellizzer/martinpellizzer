Pleurospermum corydalifolium

Pleurospermum corydalifolium, commonly known as Chinese angel's trumpets, is a flowering plant native to China.

It is characterized by its large, showy yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and arranged alternately along the stems. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a suitable choice for gardens in temperate climates.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
