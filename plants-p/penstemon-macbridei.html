Penstemon macbridei

Penstemon macbridei, commonly known as the MacBride's beardtongue, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to purple. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It prefers well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for xeriscaping in arid environments.

Penstemon macbridei is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native plant gardens.
