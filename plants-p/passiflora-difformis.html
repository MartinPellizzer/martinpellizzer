Passiflora difformis

Passiflora difformis, commonly known as the variable passionflower, is a climbing vine native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its intricate, showy flowers that feature five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments. The plant produces blue or purple berries that are edible and often used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped with a rough texture and a strong, somewhat unpleasant odor.

Passiflora difformis thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in shaded or semi-shaded areas.
