Protea urundinensis

Protea urundinensis, commonly known as the yellow protea, is a striking plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Proteaceae family, which is known for its diverse and often showy floral structures. The plant features a tall, upright stem with a large, yellowish inflorescence composed of numerous small, tubular flowers. Its blooms are typically surrounded by modified leaves called bracts, which give it a distinctive appearance.

Protea urundinensis is adapted to nutrient-poor soils and is often found in fynbos vegetation, showcasing its ecological significance in its native habitat.
