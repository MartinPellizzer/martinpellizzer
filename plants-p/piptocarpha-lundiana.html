Piptocarpha lundiana

Piptocarpha lundiana, commonly known as the desert dandelion, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble dandelions. The plant has silver-gray foliage covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in its harsh environment. Its flowers are typically yellow and bloom in late spring to early summer.

Piptocarpha lundiana is adapted to thrive in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in desert scrub habitats.
