Polypodium marantae

Polypodium marantae, commonly known as the Marantai fern, is a species of fern native to tropical regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped fronds that are typically bright green with a glossy texture. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories. Its rhizomes are fibrous and can form dense clumps, contributing to its ornamental value in horticulture.

Polypodium marantae is valued for its aesthetic appeal and is sometimes cultivated as a houseplant or in botanical gardens.
