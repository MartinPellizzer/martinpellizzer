Pedilonum pentagonum

Pedilonum pentagonum, commonly known as the five-sided sandal plant, is a unique species native to arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive pentagonal leaves that resemble small sandals, giving it its common name. The plant has a compact, low-growing habit with thick, fleshy stems that store water, aiding in its survival in dry environments. Its flowers are small, yellowish-green, and clustered in dense inflorescences, typically blooming during the rainy season.

Pedilonum pentagonum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in succulent gardens for its unique appearance.
