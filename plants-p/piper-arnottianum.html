Piper arnottianum

Piper arnottianum, commonly known as the Arnott's pepper, is a climbing vine native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper plants such as black pepper. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into elongated, berry-like fruits. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a slightly rough texture, while the stems are woody and can reach several meters in length.

The plant is valued for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
