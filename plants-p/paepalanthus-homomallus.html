Paepalanthus homomallus

Paepalanthus homomallus, commonly known as the smooth-hairless puya, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Andean region of South America.

It belongs to the family Xanthorrhoeaceae and is characterized by its rosette growth habit and dense, fibrous root system. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered inflorescences with yellowish-green flowers. It thrives in high-altitude environments and is adapted to cool, dry climates.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
