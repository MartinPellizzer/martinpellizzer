Pseudosasa

Pseudosasa, commonly known as false bamboo, is a genus of flowering plants in the grass family, Poaceae.

It consists of several species native to East Asia, particularly China, Korea, and Japan. These plants are characterized by their clumping growth habit and elongated, sword-shaped leaves. They are often used in horticulture for their ornamental value and ability to thrive in shaded environments.

Pseudosasa species are also valued for their use in traditional medicine and as a source of durable, flexible materials.
