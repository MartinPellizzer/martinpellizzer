Panicum vigoratum

Panicum vigoratum, commonly known as black panicgrass, is a perennial grass native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces dense, spike-like inflorescences that are often tinged with purple or black. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically green with a slight sheen. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it a common weed in agricultural fields.

Due to its rapid growth and adaptability, it is both a valuable forage crop and a challenging weed to control.
