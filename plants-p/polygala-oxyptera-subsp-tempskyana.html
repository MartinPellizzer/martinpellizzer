Polygala oxyptera subsp. tempskyana

Polygala oxyptera subsp.

tempskyana, commonly known as the Tempskyana milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia. It belongs to the Polygalaceae family and is characterized by its slender, often branched stems and small, clustered flowers.

The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. Its flowers are usually pale green to yellowish in color, and it produces small, elongated seed pods.

This subspecies is of interest in botanical studies due to its unique adaptations and limited distribution.
