Pyrus pyraster var. nyaradyana

Pyrus pyraster var. nyaradyana, commonly known as the Nyaradyan crabapple, is a variant of the common crabapple native to Central Asia.

It is a deciduous tree characterized by its small, tart fruits and ornamental white flowers that bloom in early spring. The leaves are typically oval-shaped with a smooth margin and a slightly serrated edge. This variety is often cultivated for its aesthetic value in gardens and for its use in traditional medicine.

It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.
