Phyllanthus dumetosus

Phyllanthus dumetosus, commonly known as the common phyllanthus, is a shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters and has opposite, elliptical leaves with a slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, followed by small, round fruits that are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species in both ecological and medicinal contexts.
