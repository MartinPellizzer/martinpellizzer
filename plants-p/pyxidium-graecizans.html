Pyxidium graecizans

Pyxidium graecizans, commonly known as the Greek bellflower, is a flowering plant belonging to the Campanulaceae family.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in rocky, well-drained soils. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped blue or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lanceolate and often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Pyxidium graecizans is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
