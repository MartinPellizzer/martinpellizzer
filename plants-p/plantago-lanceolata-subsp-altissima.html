Plantago lanceolata subsp. altissima

Plantago lanceolata subsp.

altissima, commonly known as the tall plantain, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia. It features long, narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and arranged in a rosette pattern.

The plant produces small, greenish flowers in dense clusters, which are borne on tall, erect stems. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas.

This subspecies is notable for its adaptability and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
