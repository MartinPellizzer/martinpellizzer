Pholidota chinensis

Pholidota chinensis, commonly known as the Chinese flame tree, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, bright red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. The plant has compound leaves composed of several elliptical leaflets, and it produces small, woody fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The tree is also valued in traditional medicine for its various therapeutic properties.
