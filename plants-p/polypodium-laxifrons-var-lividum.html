Polypodium laxifrons var. lividum

Polypodium laxifrons var. lividum, commonly known as the silver-veined holly fern, is a variant of the broad-leaf holly fern.

It is a perennial fern native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas. The plant features fronds with a distinctive silvery or bluish-green coloration on the underside, giving it its common name. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and can grow up to several feet in length.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
