Painteria

Painteria, commonly known as the "painter's flower," is a striking plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that come in a range of colors, including red, pink, and purple. The plant belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is known for its unique, trumpet-shaped blooms. Painteria thrives in cool, high-altitude environments and requires well-drained soil and partial sunlight.

Its ornamental value has made it a popular choice for gardeners seeking to add visual interest to their landscapes.
