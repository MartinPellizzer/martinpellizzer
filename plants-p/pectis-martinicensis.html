Pectis martinicensis

Pectis martinicensis, commonly known as the Martinique pectis, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean islands.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces showy, yellow flowers with a central disk surrounded by ray florets, typical of the family. It is often found in coastal areas and sandy soils, thriving in tropical and subtropical climates.

Its structure and floral morphology make it an important species for ecological studies in its native habitat.
