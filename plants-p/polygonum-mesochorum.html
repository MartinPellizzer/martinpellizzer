Polygonum mesochorum

Polygonum mesochorum, commonly known as the water pepper, is a flowering plant in the buckwheat family, Polygonaceae.

It is native to parts of Europe, Asia, and North Africa, and is often found in wet or marshy habitats. The plant has alternate, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, greenish flowers in clusters. Its roots are sometimes used in traditional medicine for their diuretic properties.

The plant is also known for its ability to thrive in moist environments, making it a common weed in wetland areas.
