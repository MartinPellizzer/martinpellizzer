Paspalidium utowanaeum

Paspalidium utowanaeum, commonly known as the southern sandgrass, is a perennial grass native to the coastal plains of southeastern Australia.

It typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in dune environments. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It is adapted to withstand salt spray and wind, making it a hardy species in coastal regions.

Its ecological importance includes stabilizing dunes and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
