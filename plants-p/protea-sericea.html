Protea sericea

Protea sericea, commonly known as the silver protea, is a striking South African plant belonging to the Proteaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescence composed of numerous white to pinkish flowers surrounded by a dense cluster of bracts. The plant is native to the Western and Eastern Cape provinces, where it thrives in sandy or gravelly soils. Its foliage consists of narrow, leathery leaves that are covered in a fine, silvery down, giving it its common name.

Protea sericea is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also an important species in local ecosystems.
