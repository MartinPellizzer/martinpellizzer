Persea wallichii

Persea wallichii, commonly known as the Indian persimmon, is a deciduous tree native to the eastern Himalayas.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae and is characterized by its simple, alternate, ovate leaves with a glossy upper surface. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by oval, orange-red fruits with a sweet, astringent flavor. Its bark is smooth and grayish-brown, often with shallow fissures, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and edible fruit, making it a significant species in both horticulture and local ecosystems.
