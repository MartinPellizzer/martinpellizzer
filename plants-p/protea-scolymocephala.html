Protea scolymocephala

Protea scolymocephala, commonly known as the silver protea, is a striking South African plant belonging to the Proteaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescences that resemble a cluster of small flowers, typically in shades of white, pink, and red. The plant thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in fynbos vegetation. Its unique floral structure attracts a variety of pollinators, including birds and insects.

Protea scolymocephala is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens across its native range.
