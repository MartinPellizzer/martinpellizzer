Pleurothallis indecora

Pleurothallis indecora, commonly known as the elegant orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, often fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are usually narrow and strap-like, adapted for efficient water retention.

The flowers, which can be white, pink, or purple, are small but intricately structured, showcasing the genus's typical floral morphology.
