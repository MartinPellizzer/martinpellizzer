Pentopetia boinensis

Pentopetia boinensis, commonly known as the bamboo grass, is a species of flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the grass family, Poaceae, and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow leaves. The plant typically grows in wet, tropical environments and is often found in swampy or marshy areas. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in spikelets, and it plays a role in stabilizing wetland soils.

Its rapid growth and adaptability make it both ecologically significant and sometimes invasive in non-native regions.
