Pilosella atrobulbosa

Pilosella atrobulbosa, commonly known as the black bulbous mouse-ear, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant features a basal rosette of hairy, spoon-shaped leaves and produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas, and is often found growing in disturbed soils.

Pilosella atrobulbosa is also known for its medicinal properties, with some traditional uses in herbal medicine.
