Protea revoluta

Protea revoluta, commonly known as the silver protea, is a striking South African plant belonging to the Proteaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescence composed of numerous white to pinkish bracts that surround a central cone of small flowers. The plant is native to the Western and Eastern Cape provinces, where it thrives in well-drained, sandy soils. Its foliage consists of narrow, leathery leaves that are often covered in a silvery-white waxy coating.

Protea revoluta is widely cultivated for its ornamental value and is also an important species in the conservation of South Africa's unique flora.
