Pluchea camphorata

Pluchea camphorata, commonly known as camphor weed, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its small, yellow flowers and finely divided leaves. The plant is known for its aromatic properties, with a strong camphor-like scent. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

Due to its invasive potential, it is considered a problematic weed in many areas.
