Periploca reticulata

Periploca reticulata, commonly known as the net-veined periploca, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its woody stems and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of light conditions, making it adaptable to different environments.

Its ornamental value and medicinal potential have made it of interest in horticulture and traditional medicine.
