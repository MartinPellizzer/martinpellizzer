Pterocarpus marsupium subsp. marsupium

Pterocarpus marsupium subsp.

marsupium, commonly known as Indian satinwood, is a deciduous tree native to the Indian subcontinent. It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its dark brown, aromatic heartwood, which is highly valued for its durability and fine grain.

The plant has pinnate leaves and produces yellowish-green flowers followed by pod-like fruits containing flat, seed-like structures. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, well-drained soils in forested areas.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is also cultivated for traditional uses in Ayurvedic medicine.
