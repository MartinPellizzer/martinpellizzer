Procris paludosa

Procris paludosa, commonly known as the marsh bellflower, is a herbaceous plant native to wetland habitats in North America.

It features showy, blue to violet flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs, aiding in moisture retention. Its root system is fibrous, allowing it to thrive in saturated soils.

Procris paludosa plays a vital role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems and supporting local biodiversity.
