Potamogeton × versicolor

Potamogeton × versicolor, commonly known as the hybrid water milfoil, is a submerged aquatic plant native to North America.

It is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Potamogeton perfoliatus and Potamogeton richardsonii. The plant has long, narrow leaves that are often arranged in whorls around the stem, and it produces small, greenish flowers. It thrives in slow-moving freshwater environments such as lakes, ponds, and streams.

Potamogeton × versicolor plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
