Parsonsia francii

Parsonsia francii, commonly known as the golden flame tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, creating a vibrant display. The plant has compound leaves with elongated, lance-shaped leaflets. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Parsonsia francii is also noted for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
