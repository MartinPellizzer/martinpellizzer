Phoebe effusa

Phoebe effusa, commonly known as the spreading phoebe, is a species of flowering plant in the family Lauraceae.

It is native to the southeastern United States, particularly in areas with moist, acidic soils. The plant features evergreen foliage with lance-shaped leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. It produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale green, followed by dark blue berries.

Phoebe effusa is often found in woodlands and along stream banks, where it thrives in partial shade to full sun.
