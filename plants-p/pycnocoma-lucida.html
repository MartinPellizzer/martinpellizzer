Pycnocoma lucida

Pycnocoma lucida, commonly known as the shining pycnocoma, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and clusters of small, showy flowers that bloom in shades of purple and blue. The plant thrives in high-altitude environments, often found in rocky or alpine habitats. Its leaves are typically thick and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in its arid surroundings.

Pycnocoma lucida is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with specific climate requirements.
