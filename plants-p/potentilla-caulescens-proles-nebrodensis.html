Potentilla caulescens proles nebrodensis

Potentilla caulescens proles nebrodensis, commonly known as the Nebroden Alps potentilla, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Alps.

It is a low-growing shrub with upright, often branched stems that are covered in dense, grayish-white hairs. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a serrated edge, contributing to its rugged appearance.

This species is adapted to rocky and well-drained soils, thriving in alpine and subalpine environments.
