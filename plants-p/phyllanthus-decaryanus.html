Phyllanthus decaryanus

Phyllanthus decaryanus, commonly known as the African spurge, is a perennial shrub native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its small, clustered leaves and yellowish flowers. The plant produces small, smooth, green fruits that contain seeds. It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in dry to semi-arid conditions.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
