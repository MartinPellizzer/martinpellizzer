Pelargonium × amaryllidis

Pelargonium × amaryllidis, commonly known as the hybrid geranium, is a popular ornamental plant characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers.

It is a hybrid between Pelargonium hortorum and Pelargonium × domesticum, resulting in a diverse range of cultivars with varying flower colors and foliage patterns. The plant typically produces clusters of five-petaled flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, which bloom throughout the growing season. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Pelargonium × amaryllidis is often used in garden beds, containers, and as hanging plants due to its compact growth habit and long blooming period.
