Prunus glandulosa f. albiplena

Prunus glandulosa f. albiplena, commonly known as the white-flowered cherry, is a cultivar of the Prunus glandulosa species, which is native to Japan.

It is characterized by its showy, white flowers that bloom in early spring, often preceding the foliage. The plant has a compact, rounded growth habit with small, ovate leaves that are usually a glossy green. Its botanical features include glandular hairs on the stems and leaves, which give it its scientific name.

This ornamental plant is valued for its aesthetic appeal in gardens and is often used in floral arrangements.
