Pilosocereus rosae

Pilosocereus rosae, commonly known as the rose cactus, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems that are covered in white, woolly spines, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces vibrant pink or red flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in arid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Pilosocereus rosae is a popular choice for succulent gardens due to its ornamental value and relatively low maintenance requirements.
