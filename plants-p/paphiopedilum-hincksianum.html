Paphiopedilum hincksianum

Paphiopedilum hincksianum, commonly known as the Hinck's lady's slipper, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features a distinctive slipper-shaped pouch that serves as a unique pollination mechanism. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments with rich soil. Its flowers are usually pale yellow with brownish markings, and it produces showy, fragrant blooms.

Paphiopedilum hincksianum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
