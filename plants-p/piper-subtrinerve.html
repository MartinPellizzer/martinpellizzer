Piper subtrinerve

Piper subtrinerve, commonly known as the three-veined pepper, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes various pepper species. The plant is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, with three distinct veins running from the base to the tip. Its small, greenish-white flowers are followed by elliptical berries that turn red when mature.

The plant is valued for its aromatic leaves and is often used in traditional medicine and culinary applications.
