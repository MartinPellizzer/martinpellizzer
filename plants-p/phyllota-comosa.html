Phyllota comosa

Phyllota comosa, commonly known as the silky phyllota, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the Leguminosae family and is characterized by its delicate, feathery foliage and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows from a short, woody stem and has compound leaves with numerous small, hairy leaflets. It is adapted to survive in dry conditions, storing water in its stems and roots.

Phyllota comosa is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it an important plant in soil enrichment.
