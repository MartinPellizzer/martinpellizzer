Pavetta olivifolia

Pavetta olivifolia, commonly known as the oliveleaf pavetta, is a shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It typically grows to a height of about 1 to 3 meters, featuring dense, evergreen foliage with lance-shaped leaves that are often glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, fragrant white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by small, berry-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a suitable candidate for ornamental gardening in suitable climates.

Its botanical characteristics make it an interesting subject for both ecological studies and horticultural applications.
