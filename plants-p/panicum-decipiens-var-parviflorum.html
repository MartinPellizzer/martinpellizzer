Panicum decipiens var. parviflorum

Panicum decipiens var. parviflorum, commonly known as the dwarf false panicgrass, is a grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its low-growing habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often hairy along the edges. The plant produces small, pale green or reddish spikelets that are clustered at the ends of the stems. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along the edges of ponds.

Due to its delicate appearance and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
