Palala sylvestris

Palala sylvestris, commonly known as the forest palala, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the temperate rainforests of South America.

It features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a dense layer of fine hairs, providing protection against moisture loss. The plant produces small, clustered white flowers during the spring and early summer, which are pollinated by insects. Its fibrous root system helps stabilize the soil in its natural habitat.

Palala sylvestris is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in forest ecosystems.
