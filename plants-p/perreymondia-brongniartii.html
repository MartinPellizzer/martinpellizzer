Perreymondia brongniartii

Perreymondia brongniartii, commonly known as the African moonflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its large, white, night-blooming flowers that emit a strong, sweet fragrance. The plant typically grows as a shrub with woody stems and elliptical, dark green leaves. Its flowers open at night and close during the day, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique blooming habit.
