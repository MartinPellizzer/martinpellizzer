Pyrrosia davidii

Pyrrosia davidii, commonly known as the David's rock fern, is a species of epiphytic fern native to eastern Asia.

It typically grows on rocks or trees in humid, shaded environments. The plant features delicate, lance-shaped fronds with a serrated edge. Its spore-producing structures, called sori, are located on the underside of the fronds.

Pyrrosia davidii is valued for its ornamental appearance and is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
