Paspalum galapageium

Paspalum galapageium, commonly known as Galápagos grass, is a perennial grass native to the Galápagos Islands.

It thrives in coastal and volcanic soils, often found in dry, open habitats. The plant has slender, erect stems and small, spike-like inflorescences that produce numerous tiny flowers. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green color.

Paspalum galapageium plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for local wildlife.
