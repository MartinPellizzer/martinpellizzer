Pelargonium egregium

Pelargonium egregium, commonly known as the giant geranium, is a striking evergreen shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its large, velvety leaves that can grow up to 30 cm in length, often with a reddish or purple tinge. The plant produces showy, clustered flowers in shades of pink, red, or white, typically blooming in spring and summer. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is widely cultivated in warm climates.
