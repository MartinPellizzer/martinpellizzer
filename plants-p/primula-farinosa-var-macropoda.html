Primula farinosa var. macropoda

Primula farinosa var. macropoda, commonly known as the large-flowered primrose, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, basal leaves that form a rosette and its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils and cool, moist conditions. Its flowers bloom in early spring, adding a vibrant touch to mountainous landscapes.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
