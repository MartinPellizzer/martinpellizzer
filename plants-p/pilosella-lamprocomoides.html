Pilosella lamprocomoides

Pilosella lamprocomoides, commonly known as the silverleaf pyrethrum, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its daisy-like flowers with white petals and a yellow center. The plant has a compact growth habit and produces small, hairy leaves that give it its common name. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.

Pilosella lamprocomoides is also noted for its potential in horticultural use, particularly in rock gardens and as a ground cover.
