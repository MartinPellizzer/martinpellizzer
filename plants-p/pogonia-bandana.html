Pogonia bandana

Pogonia bandana, commonly known as the showy lady's tresses, is a species of orchid native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, spiraled arrangement of dark green leaves and its striking, pinkish-purple flower spikes. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and swamps. It produces small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The orchid is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its beauty.
