Pinus ikedae

Pinus ikedae, commonly known as the Ikedae pine, is a rare and endangered species native to the Ryukyu Islands of Japan.

It is a coniferous evergreen tree characterized by its slender, upright trunk and dense, needle-like foliage. The plant has scale-like leaves that are typically arranged in a spiral pattern along the branches. It produces small, cone-shaped seed structures that contain winged seeds dispersed by wind.

Due to habitat loss and limited natural range, Pinus ikedae is of significant interest in botanical conservation efforts.
