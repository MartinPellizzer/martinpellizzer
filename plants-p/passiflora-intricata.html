Passiflora intricata

Passiflora intricata, commonly known as the intricate passionflower, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its complex, showy flowers that feature five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments surrounding the reproductive structures. The plant produces edible, tropical fruits known as passionfruits, which are rich in vitamins and antioxidants. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped with a velvety texture, and it thrives in warm, humid climates with well-drained soil.

Passiflora intricata is also valued for its ornamental beauty and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
