Prunus × lannesiana f. fruticosa

Prunus × lannesiana f. fruticosa, commonly known as the dwarf cherry or compact cherry, is a compact, shrub-like form of the Prunus × lannesiana species.

It features small, rounded leaves and produces clusters of white or pink flowers in spring. The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens for its aesthetic value. It is a hybrid species, resulting from crosses between Prunus cerasifera and Prunus lusitanica.

This variety is relatively hardy and can thrive in a range of soil conditions, making it a versatile choice for landscaping.
