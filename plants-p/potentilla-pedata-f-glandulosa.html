Potentilla pedata f. glandulosa

Potentilla pedata f. glandulosa, commonly known as the glandular potentilla, is a flowering plant belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit and produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The leaves are typically oval-shaped with a hairy texture, and the plant is often found in moist, calcareous soils. This variety is distinguished by its glandular structures on the stems and leaves, which contribute to its unique botanical features.

It is often used in ornamental gardening for its compact growth and delicate floral display.
