Pelargonium grossularioides var. anceps

Pelargonium grossularioides var. anceps, commonly known as the Cape gooseberry geranium, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves that are often tinged with red or purple hues. The plant produces small, orange to red berries that resemble the fruit of the cape gooseberry, hence its common name. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens and containers.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
