Pseudolysimachion rotundum f. roseum

Pseudolysimachion rotundum f. roseum, commonly known as the pink-flowered round-leaved pseudolysimachion, is a unique cultivar characterized by its striking pink or rose-colored flowers.

It belongs to the family Apiaceae, which includes parsley and carrots, and is native to southern Europe. The plant features rounded, aromatic leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that bloom in late spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a suitable addition to rock gardens or borders.
