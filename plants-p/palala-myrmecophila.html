Palala myrmecophila

Palala myrmecophila, commonly known as the giant antplant, is a unique epiphytic plant native to the tropical rainforests of South America.

It is characterized by its large, waxy leaves that provide shelter for colonies of ants. These ants, in turn, protect the plant from herbivores and competing plants by aggressively defending it. The plant forms a symbiotic relationship with its ant inhabitants, which is a rare and fascinating ecological interaction.

Its structure and function make it an important component of the rainforest ecosystem.
