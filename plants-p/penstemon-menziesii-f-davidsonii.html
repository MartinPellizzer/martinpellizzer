Penstemon menziesii f. davidsonii

Penstemon menziesii f. davidsonii, commonly known as the Davidson's beardtongue, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a perennial species characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to purple. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces seed capsules that disperse its seeds via wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping and native gardens.

This subspecies is noted for its ornamental value and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
