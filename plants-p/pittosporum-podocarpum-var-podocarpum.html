Pittosporum podocarpum var. podocarpum

Pittosporum podocarpum var. podocarpum, commonly known as the New Zealand Pittosporum, is a shrub or small tree native to New Zealand.

It features dark green, elliptical leaves that are glossy and have a fragrant aroma when crushed. The plant produces clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in spring, followed by edible, black, berry-like fruits. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and lowland forest areas.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in native New Zealand ecosystems.
