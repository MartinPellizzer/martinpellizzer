Potentilla bipinnatifida

Potentilla bipinnatifida, commonly known as the double-pinnate cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its finely divided, bipinnatifid leaves that give it a delicate, fern-like appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in alpine or rocky habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
