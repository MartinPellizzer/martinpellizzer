Phalaris amethystina

Phalaris amethystina, commonly known as purple grass or amethyst grass, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and spikes of purple to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its ornamental value comes from its attractive, long-lasting inflorescences, which are often used in floral arrangements.

Phalaris amethystina is also valued in ecological landscaping for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
