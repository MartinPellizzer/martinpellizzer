Prunus jamasakura f. formosissima

Prunus jamasakura f. formosissima, commonly known as the Japanese cherry blossom or sakura, is a cultivar of the Prunus jamasakura species.

It is characterized by its delicate, white or pink flowers that bloom in early spring, creating a striking visual display. The plant is a small to medium-sized deciduous tree, typically reaching heights of 5 to 10 meters. Its leaves are elliptical, with a smooth texture and a dark green hue during the growing season.

The botanical features of this cultivar make it a popular ornamental plant in gardens and public spaces throughout Japan and other parts of the world.
