Pinardia carinata

Pinardia carinata, commonly known as the horned orchid, is a striking member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its distinctive, horn-shaped flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained, nutrient-rich soil.

Pinardia carinata is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and private collections.
