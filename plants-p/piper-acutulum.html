Piper acutulum

Piper acutulum, commonly known as the narrowleaf Indian pepper, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia and Africa.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, opposite leaves with serrated edges. Its flowers are unisexual, with separate male and female flowers on the same plant, and it produces small, berry-like fruits.

Piper acutulum is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties.
