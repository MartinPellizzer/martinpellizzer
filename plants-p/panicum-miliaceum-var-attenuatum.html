Panicum miliaceum var. attenuatum

Panicum miliaceum var. attenuatum, commonly known as slender proso millet, is a grass species native to warm temperate and tropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, linear leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny grains, which are wind-pollinated. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for cultivation in arid environments.

This variety is valued for its use in traditional agriculture and as a forage crop.
