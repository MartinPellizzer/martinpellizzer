Picris hieracioides var. leteae

Picris hieracioides var. leteae, commonly known as the summer everlasting, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features wiry, branching stems covered in fine, hairy hairs, and it produces small, yellowish-green composite flowers arranged in clusters. The plant is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves that are often grayish-green in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, sunny habitats.

The flowers attract various pollinators, including bees and butterflies, making it an important plant in ecological gardens.
