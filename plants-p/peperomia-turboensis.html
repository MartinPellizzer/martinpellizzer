Peperomia turboensis

Peperomia turboensis, commonly known as the Turbo peperomia, is a compact, low-growing plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It features thick, waxy leaves that are typically dark green with a glossy texture, often adorned with subtle variegation. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually green or red, clustered in compact inflorescences. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires bright, indirect light to flourish.

Due to its compact size and attractive foliage, it is a popular choice for indoor gardening and container planting.
