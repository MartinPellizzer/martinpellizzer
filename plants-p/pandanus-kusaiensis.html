Pandanus kusaiensis

Pandanus kusaiensis, commonly known as the Kusaiensis pandanus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Pandanaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including parts of Indonesia and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender stems and long, narrow leaves that are often used in traditional crafts and as a source of fiber. It produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically clustered in panicles, contributing to its ornamental value.

The plant thrives in humid, coastal environments and is often found growing near mangroves or in swampy areas.
