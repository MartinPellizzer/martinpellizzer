Polygonum emersum

Polygonum emersum, commonly known as water smartweed, is a flowering plant native to wetland habitats across North America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, often found in marshes, stream banks, and ditches. The plant features alternate, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink. Its stems are often reddish and can become woody with age, while its roots form dense colonies that help stabilize soil.

Polygonum emersum is also known for its ability to thrive in nutrient-rich environments and is sometimes considered an invasive species in certain regions.
