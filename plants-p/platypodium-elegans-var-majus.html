Platypodium elegans var. majus

Platypodium elegans var. majus, commonly known as the giant candleplant, is a striking member of the Araceae family.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescence that consists of a spadix surrounded by a colorful spathe. Native to the tropical regions of Central and South America, this plant thrives in warm, humid environments. Its leaves are typically broad and glossy, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

It is often cultivated as a houseplant or in shaded outdoor gardens due to its preference for indirect light and consistent moisture.
