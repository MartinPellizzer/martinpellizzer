Pilosella phlebophylla

Pilosella phlebophylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaved pearly everlasting, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and foliage covered in fine, white hairs. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. Its leaves are narrow and often have a slightly hairy texture, while its flowers are small and white, arranged in dense clusters.

Pilosella phlebophylla is known for its resilience and ability to thrive in marginal environments.
