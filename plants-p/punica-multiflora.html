Punica multiflora

Punica multiflora, commonly known as the Chinese date tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It produces small, red, fleshy fruits that are edible and often used in traditional medicine. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape. Its flowers are typically pink to red, appearing in clusters, and it blooms in the spring.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and its fruit's nutritional and medicinal properties.
