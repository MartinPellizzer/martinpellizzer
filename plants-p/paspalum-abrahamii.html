Paspalum abrahamii

Paspalum abrahamii, commonly known as Abraham's grass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in a variety of habitats, including wetlands and disturbed soils, and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Paspalum abrahamii is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in marginal environments.
