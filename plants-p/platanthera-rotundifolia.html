Platanthera rotundifolia

Platanthera rotundifolia, commonly known as the round-leaf twayblade, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its small, greenish-white flowers that are typically arranged in a spike and lack showy petals. The plant has slender, grass-like leaves that are rounded at the base, giving it its common name. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and wet meadows.

Platanthera rotundifolia is often found in temperate regions and is known for its symbiotic relationship with mycorrhizal fungi.
