Poa cenisia var. diffusa

Poa cenisia var. diffusa, commonly known as the silver hair grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern South America.

It is characterized by its fine, silvery-blue foliage and tufted growth habit, which gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in grassland and open woodland habitats. Its inflorescence consists of a narrow, spike-like cluster of small, two-ranked spikelets.

Poa cenisia var. diffusa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its visual interest.
