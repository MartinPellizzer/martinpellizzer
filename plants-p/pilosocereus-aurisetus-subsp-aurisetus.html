Pilosocereus aurisetus subsp. aurisetus

Pilosocereus aurisetus subsp.

aurisetus, commonly known as the Moon Cactus, is a cactus species native to Mexico. It is characterized by its short, thick stems covered in white, woolly spines.

The plant typically grows in rocky, arid environments and requires well-draining soil. It produces yellow flowers during the summer months, adding a striking contrast to its green stems.

Due to its unique appearance and slow growth, it is a popular choice for indoor gardening.
