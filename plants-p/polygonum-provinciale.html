Polygonum provinciale

Polygonum provinciale, commonly known as the provincial knotweed, is a herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, which includes species known for their distinctive leaf shapes and clusters of small flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as stream banks and forest floors. Its leaves are lance-shaped with a prominent midrib, and it produces tiny, greenish-white flowers in dense spikes.

Polygonum provinciale is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
