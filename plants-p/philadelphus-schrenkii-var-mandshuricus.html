Philadelphus schrenkii var. mandshuricus

Philadelphus schrenkii var. mandshuricus, commonly known as the Siberian mock orange, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia.

It features fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged oppositely along the stems. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardiness in colder climates.
