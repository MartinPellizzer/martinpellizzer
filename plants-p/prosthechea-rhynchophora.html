Prosthechea rhynchophora

Prosthechea rhynchophora, commonly known as the narrow-leafed orchid, is a species of epiphytic orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect pseudobulbs and linear, grass-like leaves. The plant produces pendulous inflorescences bearing small, fragrant flowers with a distinctive yellow-green coloration. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining substrates for optimal growth.

Prosthechea rhynchophora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical greenhouses.
