Popowia riparia

Popowia riparia, commonly known as the river popowia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a shrub that typically grows near water bodies, showcasing its adaptability to moist environments. The plant features simple, alternate leaves that are often elliptical in shape. Its small, clustered flowers are usually white or pale pink, attracting various pollinators.

Popowia riparia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable humidity levels.
