Paphiopedilum praestans subsp. wilhelminae

Paphiopedilum praestans subsp.

wilhelminae, commonly known as the Wilhelmina's lady's slipper, is a terrestrial orchid native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia. It features a broad, velvety leaf and produces a single, showy flower with a distinctive pouch-shaped lip.

The plant is characterized by its dark green foliage and pale yellow to orange flowers with a deep red or purple lip. It thrives in shaded, humid environments and requires well-drained, nutrient-rich soil.

This subspecies is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated for its unique and elegant floral display.
