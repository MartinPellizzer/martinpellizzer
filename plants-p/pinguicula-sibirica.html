Pinguicula sibirica

Pinguicula sibirica, commonly known as the Siberian butterwort, is a carnivorous plant native to cold regions of Asia.

It possesses sticky, glandular leaves that trap and digest small insects, providing it with essential nutrients in nutrient-poor environments. The plant typically grows in moist, rocky habitats and is adapted to withstand harsh climatic conditions. Its flowers are usually pink or purple, and it blooms in late spring or early summer.

Pinguicula sibirica is a small, low-growing plant with a unique ability to thrive in extreme environments.
