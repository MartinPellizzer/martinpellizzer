Poa purpurea

Poa purpurea, commonly known as purple mountain grass, is a species of grass native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and dense, tufted growth habit. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are often purple or pink in color, giving it its common name. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows.

Poa purpurea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its visual interest.
