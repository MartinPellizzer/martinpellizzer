Panicum glabripes

Panicum glabripes, commonly known as silver panicum, is a grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage, which results from dense pubescence covering the leaves. The plant produces small, fluffy seeds that are dispersed by wind, aiding in its propagation. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for native plant gardens and erosion control.
