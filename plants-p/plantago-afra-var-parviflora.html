Plantago afra var. parviflora

Plantago afra var. parviflora, commonly known as narrow-leaved plantain, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and is often found in grasslands and along roadsides. It produces capsules that contain numerous small seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

This variety is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
