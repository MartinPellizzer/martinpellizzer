Piper gatunense var. cocleanum

Piper gatunense var. cocleanum, commonly known as the Cocleanum pepper plant, is a member of the Piperaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of Central and South America, particularly found in the understory of humid forests. The plant is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and lance-shaped leaves with a glossy surface. Its small, greenish-white flowers are typically clustered in spikes, and it produces small, round berries that turn from green to yellow as they mature.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
