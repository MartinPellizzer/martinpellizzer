Phalerocarpus Hispidulus

Hispidulus sycamore, scientifically known as Phalerocarpus hispidulus, is a unique member of the Sapotaceae family native to Southeast Asia. This tree is distinguished by its rough, hairy bark and thick, leathery leaves that provide a striking visual contrast. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in lowland forests and riverine habitats. The plant is valued for its durable wood and has been used in traditional medicine for centuries. Its ecological importance supports a variety of wildlife, making it a key species in its native ecosystems.
