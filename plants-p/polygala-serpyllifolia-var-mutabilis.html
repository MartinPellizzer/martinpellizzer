Polygala serpyllifolia var. mutabilis

Polygala serpyllifolia var. mutabilis, commonly known as the variable ladies' tresses, is a flowering plant in the family Polygalaceae.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and dense, spike-like inflorescences composed of small, white or pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges. Its leaves are linear and often hairy, and it is known for its variable flower coloration, which can change as the flowers age.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
