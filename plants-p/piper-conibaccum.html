Piper conibaccum

Piper conibaccum, commonly known as the wild pepper plant, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many species of pepper plants. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by dark purple berries that are edible but have a spicy flavor. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The botanical characteristics of Piper conibaccum make it an important species in both ecological and medicinal contexts.
