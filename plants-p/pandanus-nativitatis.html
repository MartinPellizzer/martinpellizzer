Pandanus nativitatis

Pandanus nativitatis, commonly known as the Christmas Island pandanus, is a unique palm-like plant native to the island of Christmas Island in the Indian Ocean.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feather-like leaves that can reach lengths of up to 3 meters. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically green or yellowish in color, which eventually develop into fibrous, cone-like fruits. It thrives in coastal areas and is adapted to the humid, tropical climate of its habitat.

Due to its restricted distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
