Polygala angulata var. angustifolia

Polygala angulata var. angustifolia, commonly known as narrow-leaved milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It features slender, angular stems that often grow in open, rocky habitats. The plant produces small, pink to purple flowers arranged in spikes, typical of the Polygala genus. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It is adapted to arid conditions and is often found in grasslands and sagebrush steppe.
