Piper striatinervosum

Piper striatinervosum, commonly known as the striated nerve vine, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many species of flowering plants. The plant is characterized by its slender stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves with prominent veins. Its small, greenish-white flowers are clustered in spikes, and it produces small, berry-like fruits.

It is often found in humid, shaded environments such as rainforests.
