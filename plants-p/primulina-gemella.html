Primulina gemella

Primulina gemella, commonly known as the twin-leaf caladium, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves that come in a variety of colors, including red, orange, and white. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or white, which are followed by berries. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Primulina gemella is often grown as an ornamental plant for its vibrant foliage.
