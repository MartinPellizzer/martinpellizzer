Potentilla subjuga

Potentilla subjuga, commonly known as the subglossy cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are compound, consisting of five oval-shaped leaflets, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Potentilla subjuga is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to cultivate.
