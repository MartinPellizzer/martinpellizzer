Phragmites maximus

Phragmites maximus, commonly known as giant reed, is a tall, perennial grass that thrives in wetland environments.

It forms dense stands along the edges of lakes, rivers, and marshes, often dominating the landscape. The plant has long, cylindrical stems that can grow up to 6 meters in height, with narrow, grass-like leaves. Its flowering spikes are usually pink or purplish, appearing in late summer.

Phragmites maximus plays a crucial role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
