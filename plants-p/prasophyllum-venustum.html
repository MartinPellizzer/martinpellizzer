Prasophyllum venustum

Prasophyllum venustum, commonly known as the golden leek orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the southern regions of Australia.

It typically grows in heathlands and open forests, favoring well-drained soils. The plant features a slender, grass-like stem with a single, elongated leaf and a spike of small, pale yellow to golden green flowers. Its floral structure is characteristic of the orchid family, with intricate petals and sepals that aid in pollination by insects.

Prasophyllum venustum is a rare and endangered species, requiring conservation efforts to protect its habitat.
