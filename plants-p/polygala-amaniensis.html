Polygala amaniensis

Polygala amaniensis, commonly known as the Amani milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to Tanzania.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid conditions. Its flowers are usually pale yellow or greenish-white, and it produces pods that contain small, round seeds.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
