Papaver rhoeas proles caudatifolium

Papaver rhoeas proles caudatifolium, commonly known as the caudate-leaved poppy, is a variant of the common poppy characterized by its distinctive, elongated leaf shapes.

It is a herbaceous annual plant native to Europe and parts of Asia, typically growing in disturbed soils and open fields. The plant produces striking red or orange flowers with a prominent central stigma, which are highly visible and attract pollinators. Its foliage is often more elongated and lobed compared to the standard Papaver rhoeas, giving it a unique appearance.

This botanical variant is of interest in horticulture and botanical studies due to its ornamental value and genetic variability.
