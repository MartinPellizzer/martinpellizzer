Prosthechea fortunae

Prosthechea fortunae, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its pendulous, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its leaves are broad and fleshy, adapted to thrive in humid, shaded environments.

Prosthechea fortunae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in greenhouses or as a houseplant.
