Pariana radiciflora

Pariana radiciflora, commonly known as the root-flowered pariana, is a unique plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its fibrous, underground root system that gives it its common name. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale green, which bloom during the rainy season. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a waxy texture, adapting to the humid environment.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, Pariana radiciflora is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
