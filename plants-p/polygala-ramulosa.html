Polygala ramulosa

Polygala ramulosa, commonly known as the slender milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its slender, often hairy stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. Its flowers are usually pink or purple and bloom in spring, attracting various pollinators.

It is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
