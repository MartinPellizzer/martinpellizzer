Persicaria alata

Persicaria alata, commonly known as winged burweed, is a flowering plant native to temperate regions of Asia and North America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, winged seeds that are adapted for wind dispersal. The plant typically grows in moist soils along streams, wetlands, and marshes. Its flowering period occurs in late summer, producing clusters of small, white or pinkish flowers.

Persicaria alata is often considered a weed in agricultural and garden settings due to its rapid growth and invasive tendencies.
