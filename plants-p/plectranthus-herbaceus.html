Plectranthus herbaceus

Plectranthus herbaceus, commonly known as the African violet, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its upright, herbaceous stems and opposite, ovate leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces small, tubular flowers in shades of purple, blue, and white, often arranged in clusters. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.

Plectranthus herbaceus is popular as a houseplant due to its attractive foliage and ease of care.
