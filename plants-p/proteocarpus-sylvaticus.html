Proteocarpus sylvaticus

Proteocarpus sylvaticus, commonly known as the wild pear, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and showy white or pink flowers. The plant produces small, edible fruits that are often consumed by wildlife and humans alike. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained, sandy or loamy soils.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and natural landscapes.
