Puya minima

Puya minima, commonly known as the miniature puya, is a small, flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its compact rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces delicate, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white and pink, which bloom in late summer. Puya minima is adapted to high-altitude environments and requires well-drained soil and full sunlight.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens for its ornamental value.
