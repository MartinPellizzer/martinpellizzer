Pothos pedatus

Pothos pedatus, commonly known as the creeping pothos, is a tropical climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It features heart-shaped, waxy leaves that are typically bright green with a slightly variegated pattern. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are followed by yellowish berries. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires indirect light for optimal growth.

Pothos pedatus is popular as a houseplant due to its ease of care and attractive foliage.
