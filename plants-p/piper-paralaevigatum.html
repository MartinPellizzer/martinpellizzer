Piper paralaevigatum

Piper paralaevigatum, commonly known as the roughleaf pepper or wild pepper, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its rough, waxy leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces small, green berries that turn red when mature, which are often used in traditional medicine. It is typically found in humid, shaded environments such as rainforests and is valued for its potential medicinal properties.

The plant's botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and pharmacological studies.
