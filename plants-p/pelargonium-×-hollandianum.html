Pelargonium × hollandianum

Pelargonium × hollandianum, commonly known as the zonal geranium, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Geraniaceae family.

It is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that come in a wide range of colors, including red, pink, purple, and white. The leaves are typically serrated and often feature distinct zones of color, giving the plant its common name. Native to South Africa, it is widely cultivated as an ornamental plant for its aesthetic appeal and ease of care.

This species is popular in gardens and as a potted plant due to its adaptability to various growing conditions.
