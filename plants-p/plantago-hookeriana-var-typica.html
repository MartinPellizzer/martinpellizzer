Plantago hookeriana var. typica

Plantago hookeriana var. typica, commonly known as Hooker's plantain, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its rosette of broad, lance-shaped leaves with a rough, hairy texture. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in dense clusters during the spring and summer seasons. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or rocky habitats.

Its leaves are used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory and healing properties.
