Papyrus elegans

Papyrus elegans, commonly known as the slender papyrus, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and other grass-like plants. The plant features tall, slender stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height, with narrow, grass-like leaves. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets that produce small, inconspicuous flowers.

Papyrus elegans is often found in moist, swampy areas and is adapted to thrive in wetland environments.
