Pyrus communis var. cerasocarpa

Pyrus communis var. cerasocarpa, commonly known as the sweet crabapple, is a cultivar of the common apple tree.

It is characterized by its small, sweet, and edible fruits that are typically red or yellow in color. The plant has a compact growth habit with attractive, glossy leaves that turn vibrant shades of red and orange in autumn. Its flowers are white and fragrant, appearing in early spring before the leaves emerge.

This variety is often cultivated for ornamental purposes as well as for its flavorful fruit used in jams and culinary applications.
