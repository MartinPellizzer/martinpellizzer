Pulegium pulegium

Pulegium pulegium, commonly known as narrowleaf pennyroyal, is a perennial herb native to Europe, Asia, and Africa.

It features slender, upright stems and small, opposite leaves that are often glandular and aromatic. The plant produces tiny, pale blue or white flowers arranged in spikes, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It is often found in moist, shaded areas such as woodlands and stream banks.

The essential oils extracted from its leaves have been used traditionally for their medicinal properties.
