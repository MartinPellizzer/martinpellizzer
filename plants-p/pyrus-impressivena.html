Pyrus impressivena

Pyrus impressivena, commonly known as the impressively shaped pear.

This species is characterized by its distinctive leaf morphology, featuring a unique pattern of venation and a slightly serrated margin. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, which are followed by edible fruits that ripen in late summer. Its leaves are typically ovate to elliptical in shape, with a smooth upper surface and a hairy underside.

Pyrus impressivena is often found in moist, temperate regions and is valued for both its ornamental and culinary attributes.
