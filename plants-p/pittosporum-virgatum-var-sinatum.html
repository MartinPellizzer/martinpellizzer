Pittosporum virgatum var. sinatum

Pittosporum virgatum var. sinatum, commonly known as the silver pittosporum, is a shrub native to New Zealand.

It features lance-shaped, dark green leaves with a silvery underside, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring, followed by orange-red berries. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adaptable to a variety of soil types.

However, it can become invasive in some regions if not managed properly.
