Plectranthus saccatus var. longitubus

Plectranthus saccatus var. longitubus, commonly known as the long-tubed plectranthus, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It features opposite, ovate to lance-shaped leaves that are typically hairy and have a slightly serrated edge. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are usually pale purple to pink in color, arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and ease of cultivation.
