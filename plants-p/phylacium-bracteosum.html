Phylacium bracteosum

Phylacium bracteosum, commonly known as the bractean sunflower, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its large, showy yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, often resembling sunflowers. The plant has a woody stem with thick, fleshy leaves that are typically arranged in a spiral pattern. Its fruit is a capsule that contains numerous small, dry seeds.

Phylacium bracteosum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
