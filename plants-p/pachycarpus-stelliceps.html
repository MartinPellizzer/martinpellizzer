Pachycarpus stelliceps

Pachycarpus stelliceps, commonly known as the star-headed cactus, is a unique succulent native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems that store water, allowing it to survive in dry environments. The plant produces striking, star-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of yellow and orange, adding a vibrant touch to its desert habitat. Its leaves are modified into spines, reducing water loss and providing protection from herbivores.

Pachycarpus stelliceps is well adapted to extreme conditions, making it a resilient and fascinating example of desert flora.
