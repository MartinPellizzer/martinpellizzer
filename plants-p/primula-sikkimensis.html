Primula sikkimensis

Primula sikkimensis, commonly known as the Sikkim primrose, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayan regions of India, Nepal, and Bhutan.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces clusters of bell-shaped, brightly colored flowers, typically in shades of white, pink, or purple. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and forested areas at high altitudes. Its leaves are broad, heart-shaped, and covered in a fine layer of silky hairs.

Primula sikkimensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also studied for its potential medicinal properties.
