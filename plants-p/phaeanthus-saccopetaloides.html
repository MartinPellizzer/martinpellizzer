Phaeanthus saccopetaloides

Phaeanthus saccopetaloides, commonly known as the scarlet bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is characterized by its striking red or orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves and produces showy, bell-like flowers that are clustered in inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or coastal landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and unique floral structure.
