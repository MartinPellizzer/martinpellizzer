Piper fulvescens var. geraense

Piper fulvescens var. geraense, commonly known as the Geraense pepper, is a climbing shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, small, and often spicuous berries. The plant produces small, heart-shaped leaves with a rough texture and emits a pungent odor. Its fruits are typically yellow or orange when ripe and are used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The botanical features of this plant make it a subject of interest in both ecological and pharmacological studies.
