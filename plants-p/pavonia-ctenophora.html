Pavonia ctenophora

Pavonia ctenophora, commonly known as the silky pavonia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its delicate, silky-hairy foliage. The plant produces small, clustered pink to red flowers that bloom in the summer months. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or swampy areas.

Pavonia ctenophora is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
