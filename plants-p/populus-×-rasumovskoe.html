Populus × rasumovskoe

Populus × rasumovskoe, commonly known as the Siberian poplar, is a hybrid deciduous tree belonging to the Salicaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, often weeping form and heart-shaped leaves that are typically light green in color. The tree produces small, yellowish catkins in early spring, which are its primary means of pollination. It is native to parts of Eastern Europe and Asia, where it thrives in temperate climates.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is often planted in gardens and urban landscapes.
