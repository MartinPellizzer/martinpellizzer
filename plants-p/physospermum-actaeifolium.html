Physospermum actaeifolium

Physospermum actaeifolium, commonly known as the fairy lantern or ghost flower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its delicate, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in sunny, well-drained environments. Its leaves are palmately lobed, and the plant is known for its ephemeral blooming period, often appearing in spring.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
