Pancratium teneriffae

Pancratium teneriffae, commonly known as the Canary Island yellow daffodil, is a striking flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant features strap-like leaves that are narrow and grass-like in appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it suitable for coastal environments.

Pancratium teneriffae is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also valued for its ecological role in supporting local pollinators.
