Philadelphus microphyllus var. occidentalis

Philadelphus microphyllus var. occidentalis, commonly known as the western mock orange, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It features small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in early summer, typically in clusters along the branches. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are dark green and glossy on top, with a lighter underside. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for gardens and landscapes.

Its ornamental value comes from its pleasant fragrance and showy blooms, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
