Parathesis lanceolata

Parathesis lanceolata, commonly known as the lanceleaf parathesis, is a herbaceous plant native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It features elongated, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged alternately along its stem. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually white or pale yellow, which bloom during the warm season. Its roots are fibrous and often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Parathesis lanceolata thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
