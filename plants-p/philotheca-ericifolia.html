Philotheca ericifolia

Philotheca ericifolia, commonly known as the heathphilotheca, is a shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its small, oval-shaped leaves that are densely covered with fine, hair-like structures, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in heathlands and rocky areas.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value.
