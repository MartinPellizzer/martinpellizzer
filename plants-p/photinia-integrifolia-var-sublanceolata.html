Photinia integrifolia var. sublanceolata

Photinia integrifolia var. sublanceolata, commonly known as the narrow-lanceleaf photinia, is a shrub or small tree native to eastern Australia.

It features elliptic to narrow-lanceolate leaves that are usually glabrous and have a smooth margin. The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers in clusters, which are followed by red, fleshy berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value.

The species is characterized by its compact growth habit and seasonal color changes in its foliage.
