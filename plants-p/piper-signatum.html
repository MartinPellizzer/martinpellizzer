Piper signatum

Piper signatum, commonly known as the golden piper or sacred pepper, is a climbing shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, woody stems and heart-shaped leaves with a glossy surface. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by oval, yellow-orange fruits that contain aromatic seeds. Its botanical significance lies in its unique chemical composition, which includes various alkaloids and essential oils.

Piper signatum is often cultivated for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
