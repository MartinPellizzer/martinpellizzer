Pollia papuana

Pollia papuana, commonly known as the papuan orchid, is a striking tropical plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its floral structure includes three sepals and three petals, with a distinctive lip that aids in pollination.

Pollia papuana is valued not only for its ornamental beauty but also for its cultural significance in traditional practices.
