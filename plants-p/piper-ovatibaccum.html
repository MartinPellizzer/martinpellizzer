Piper ovatibaccum

Piper ovatibaccum, commonly known as the broad-leafed pepper vine, is a climbing shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes various species of pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and small, greenish-white flowers that develop into elongated, fleshy berries. Its foliage is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory properties.

The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
