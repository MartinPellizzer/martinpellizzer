Passiflora podlechii

Passiflora podlechii, commonly known as the Ecuadorian passionflower, is a climbing vine native to the Andean region of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, complex flowers with five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments that surround the reproductive structures. The plant produces edible berries that are typically yellow or orange when ripe, containing numerous seeds. Its leaves are palmately lobed and have a rough, hairy texture.

Passiflora podlechii is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in tropical and subtropical gardens.
