Pelargonium conradieae

Pelargonium conradieae, commonly known as the Conradie's geranium, is a species of flowering plant in the Geraniaceae family.

It is native to South Africa, specifically the Western Cape province, where it grows in rocky, well-drained soils. The plant is characterized by its upright growth habit, with hairy stems and small, rounded leaves. Its flowers are typically pink or red, arranged in compact clusters, and bloom during the summer months.

Pelargonium conradieae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
