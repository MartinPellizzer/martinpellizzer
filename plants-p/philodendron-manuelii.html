Philodendron manuelii

Philodendron manuelii, commonly known as the Manuel's philodendron, is a climbing plant native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It features heart-shaped, glossy green leaves that are often adorned with a waxy bloom, giving it a striking appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by round, green fruits that turn red when mature. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires consistently moist, well-draining soil.

Philodendron manuelii is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental foliage and relatively easy care requirements.
