Penstemon linarioides subsp. compactifolius

Penstemon linarioides subsp.

compactifolius, commonly known as the compact leaf beardtongue, is a perennial herb native to the western United States. It features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are densely packed, giving it a compact appearance.

The plant produces spikes of showy, two-lipped flowers in shades of pink to purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its drought tolerance and ornamental value make it a valuable addition to native plant gardens.
