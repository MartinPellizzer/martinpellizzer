Paraeremostachys ghorana

Paraeremostachys ghorana, commonly known as the Afghan feather grass, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and fine, feathery foliage that gives it a delicate appearance. The plant produces small, spikelet-like inflorescences that are typically green or pale brown in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
