Prosopis mantaroensis

Prosopis mantaroensis, commonly known as the Mantaro mesquite, is a shrub or small tree native to the Andean regions of Peru.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically pinnate and have a greyish-green color. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by pods that contain edible seeds. It is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showing resistance to drought and poor soil conditions.

The species plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soils and providing forage for livestock in its native habitat.
