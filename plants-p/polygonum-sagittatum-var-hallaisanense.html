Polygonum sagittatum var. hallaisanense

Polygonum sagittatum var. hallaisanense, commonly known as Hallaisan knotweed, is a variant of the arrowleaf plantain native to Korea.

It is characterized by its distinctive arrow-shaped leaves and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as stream banks and forest floors. It has a rhizomatous root system that allows it to spread vegetatively.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological role, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and natural habitats.
