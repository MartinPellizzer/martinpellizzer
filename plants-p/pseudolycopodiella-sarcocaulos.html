Pseudolycopodiella sarcocaulos

Pseudolycopodiella sarcocaulos, commonly known as the rock spikerush, is a member of the Lycopodiaceae family and is characterized by its small, clustered growth habit.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as rocky crevices and stream banks. The plant has slender, wiry stems that are often reddish in color and bear small, scale-like leaves. Its sporophylls are arranged in dense clusters and produce spores in specialized structures called sporangia.

This plant is adapted to nutrient-poor soils and is often found in temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.
