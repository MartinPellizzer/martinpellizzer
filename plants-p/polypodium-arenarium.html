Polypodium arenarium

Polypodium arenarium, commonly known as the sand fern, is a member of the Polypodiaceae family and is characterized by its small, delicate fronds.

It is typically found growing on rocky or sandy substrates, often in arid or semi-arid environments. The plant has a rhizomatous growth habit, with creeping stems that produce sporangia on the undersides of its leaves. Its leaves are usually linear to lanceolate in shape and have a leathery texture.

Polypodium arenarium is adapted to survive in harsh conditions, making it a resilient and hardy species.
