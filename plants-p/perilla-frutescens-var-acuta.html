Perilla frutescens var. acuta

Perilla frutescens var. acuta, commonly known as Shiso, is an aromatic herb native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves that are often purple-veined and have a distinct minty or camphor-like fragrance. The plant is typically grown for its leaves, which are used in culinary and medicinal applications. It thrives in warm climates and prefers well-drained soil.

The leaves can be used fresh or dried, adding a unique flavor to dishes and traditional remedies.
