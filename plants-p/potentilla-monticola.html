Potentilla monticola

Potentilla monticola, commonly known as mountain potentilla, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. The plant features small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer, adding a vibrant touch to its natural habitat. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its rugged appearance.

Potentilla monticola is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often used in rock gardens or as a border plant in horticultural settings.
