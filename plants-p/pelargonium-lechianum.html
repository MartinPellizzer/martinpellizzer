Pelargonium lechianum

Pelargonium lechianum, commonly known as the lemon-scented geranium, is a species of flowering plant in the family Geraniaceae.

It is native to South Africa and is characterized by its fragrant, lemon-scented leaves and showy, five-petaled flowers. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub, reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters. Its leaves are ovate to lanceolate in shape and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Pelargonium lechianum is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its pleasant fragrance and vibrant floral display.
