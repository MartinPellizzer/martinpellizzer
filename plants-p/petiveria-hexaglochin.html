Petiveria hexaglochin

Petiveria hexaglochin, commonly known as wild tobacco or Mexican tobacco, is a perennial shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its aromatic, glandular leaves that emit a strong, pungent odor. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically white or pale purple, arranged in clusters. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in disturbed soils and open areas.

Petiveria hexaglochin has been used traditionally for its medicinal properties, including anti-inflammatory and antispasmodic effects.
