Pinus tabuliformis var. brevifolia

Pinus tabuliformis var. brevifolia, commonly known as the short-leaved Chinese pine, is a variant of the Chinese pine native to northern China.

It is a coniferous evergreen tree characterized by its short, stiff needles and distinctive bark that is thick and fissured. The plant produces cones that are typically reddish-brown and contain winged seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions with a temperate climate.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is also used in reforestation efforts due to its hardiness and adaptability.
