Pyrus communis var. pompejana

Pyrus communis var. pompejana, commonly known as the Pompeian pear, is a cultivar of the common pear tree.

It is characterized by its large, elongated fruits that typically have a yellowish-orange skin with a slight blush of red. The fruit is known for its sweet and aromatic flavor, making it a prized variety for both culinary and ornamental purposes. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is also valued for its ornamental appeal, with attractive foliage and a compact growth habit.
