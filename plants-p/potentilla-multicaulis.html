Potentilla multicaulis

Potentilla multicaulis, commonly known as the many-stemmed cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the Arctic and alpine regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is a low-growing perennial shrub with multiple stems that often form dense mats. The plant features small, yellow flowers clustered in umbels, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are typically divided into five oval lobes, giving it the common name "cinquefoil.".

Potentilla multicaulis thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, harsh environments.
