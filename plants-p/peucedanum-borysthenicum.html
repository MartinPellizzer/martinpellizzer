Peucedanum borysthenicum

Peucedanum borysthenicum, commonly known as the Crimean cowparsley, is a perennial herb native to the steppes of southern Ukraine and Russia.

It features slender, branching stems and finely divided, fern-like leaves that are aromatic and slightly hairy. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in umbels, typical of the Apiaceae family. Its seeds are small, ovoid, and often have a pungent scent.

It is valued in traditional medicine for its potential anti-inflammatory and digestive properties.
