Pachira pseudofaroensis

Pachira pseudofaroensis, commonly known as the Amazonian money plant, is a tropical species native to South America.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its large, waxy, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers that are followed by bright red berries. It thrives in warm, humid environments and prefers well-draining soil.

Pachira pseudofaroensis is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and ease of care.
