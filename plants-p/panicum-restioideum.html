Panicum restioideum

Panicum restioideum, commonly known as the narrowleaf false grass, is a member of the Poaceae family, characterized by its grass-like appearance.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in wet or swampy areas, often found in coastal regions. The plant has narrow, linear leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers. It is adapted to tolerate waterlogged soils and is often used in wetland restoration projects.

Its ecological importance lies in providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
