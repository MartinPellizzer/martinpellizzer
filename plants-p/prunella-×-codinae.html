Prunella × codinae

Prunella × codinae, commonly known as the purple deadnettle, is a flowering plant in the family Lamiaceae.

It is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Prunella vulgaris and Prunella laciniata. The plant is characterized by its square stems, opposite leaves, and clusters of small, two-lipped flowers. It typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and is found in various parts of Europe and Asia.

Prunella × codinae is often used in traditional medicine and as a decorative plant in gardens.
