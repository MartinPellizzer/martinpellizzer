Portulaca grandiflora var. microphylla

Portulaca grandiflora var. microphylla, commonly known as the moss rose, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and fleshy, succulent leaves that are small and densely packed. The plant produces vibrant, showy flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, which bloom throughout the summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Its ability to tolerate drought conditions makes it a low-maintenance ornamental plant.
