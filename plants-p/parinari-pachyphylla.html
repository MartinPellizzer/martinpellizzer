Parinari pachyphylla

Parinari pachyphylla, commonly known as the thick-shelled parinari, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its large, leathery leaves and distinctive, smooth, thick bark. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers followed by round, fleshy fruits that are edible for some animals. It thrives in humid, lowland tropical forests and is often found in secondary growth areas.

Due to its ecological importance and ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
