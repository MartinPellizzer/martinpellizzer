Pourouma bolivarensis

Pourouma bolivarensis, commonly known as the Bolivian areca, is a member of the Marantaceae family and is native to the Amazon rainforests of South America.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its tall, clustered stalks and large, elongated leaves that can reach up to 2 meters in length. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or white, arranged in panicles. Its rhizomatous root system allows it to thrive in moist, shaded environments.

It is often used in traditional medicine and has ornamental value due to its striking foliage.
