Poa anceps var. condensata

Poa anceps var. condensata, commonly known as the dense bentgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and dense, compact foliage that forms a mat-like structure. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas and lowland meadows. Its leaves are narrow, slightly hairy, and have a bluish-green color, while its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of spikelets.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in turfgrass and landscaping applications.
