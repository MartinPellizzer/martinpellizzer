Podophyllum trilobum

Podophyllum trilobum, commonly known as the American belladonna, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces dark blue to black berries that are toxic to humans and animals. Its leaves are palmately lobed, with three to five deep lobes, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and streambanks.
