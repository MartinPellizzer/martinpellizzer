Pelargonium ferulaceum var. polycephalum

Pelargonium ferulaceum var. polycephalum, commonly known as the Cape marigold, is a species native to South Africa.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and showy, yellow-orange flowers. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and is adapted to sandy, well-drained soils. It produces small, round fruits that contain several seeds.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is often cultivated in gardens and coastal landscapes.
