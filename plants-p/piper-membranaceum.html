Piper membranaceum

Piper membranaceum, commonly known as the ghost pepper or bhut jolokia, is a flowering plant native to India and Nepal.

It belongs to the Piper genus in the Piperaceae family, which includes other chili pepper species. The plant is characterized by its slender, vine-like structure and small, green, oval leaves. Its flowers are unisexual, with separate male and female flowers on the same plant.

The fruit is a small, yellowish berry that turns red when mature, containing a single seed.
