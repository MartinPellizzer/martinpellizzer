Prunus × donarium subsp. speciosa

Prunus × donarium subsp.

speciosa, commonly known as the Japanese cherry or Yamazakura, is a species of flowering shrub or small tree native to Japan. It is characterized by its ornamental value, particularly during spring when it produces clusters of pale pink to white flowers.

The plant belongs to the Rosaceae family and is known for its delicate, fragrant blossoms that bloom on old wood. It typically grows to a height of 4–8 meters and has oval, glossy leaves that turn yellow in autumn.

This species is widely cultivated for its aesthetic appeal in gardens and as a symbol of renewal and hope in Japanese culture.
