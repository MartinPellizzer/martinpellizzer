Piper baptisianum

Piper baptisianum, commonly known as the Amazonian pepper or soursop pepper, is a tropical vine native to the rainforests of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its climbing habit and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, green, berry-like fruits that turn yellow when mature. Its botanical features include a fibrous root system and a woody stem with adventitious roots.

The leaves are typically alternate and have a glossy surface with a strong, pungent aroma.
