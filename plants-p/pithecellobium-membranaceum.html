Pithecellobium membranaceum

Pithecellobium membranaceum, commonly known as the paper leaf acacia, is a small evergreen shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, papery leaves that are often mistaken for paper due to their thin, translucent texture. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by elongated, bean-like pods. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires a warm, humid climate to flourish.

Due to its ornamental value and unique foliage, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a landscape plant.
