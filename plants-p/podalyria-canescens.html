Podalyria canescens

Podalyria canescens, commonly known as the silverweed, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage, which results from dense covering of fine, soft hairs. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by clusters of fluffy, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Podalyria canescens is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in harsh environments.
