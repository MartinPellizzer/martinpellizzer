Psychotria tortilis

Psychotria tortilis, commonly known as the African coffee tree, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters in height, with dark green, elliptical leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, white flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, followed by red, fleshy berries containing seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests and along riverbanks.

Psychotria tortilis is also known for its use in traditional medicine and as a source of caffeine in some local communities.
