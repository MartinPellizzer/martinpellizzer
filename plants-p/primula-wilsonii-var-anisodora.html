Primula wilsonii var. anisodora

Primula wilsonii var. anisodora, commonly known as the Wilson's primrose, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of China.

It features basal leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape, often with a hairy texture. The plant produces clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, or purple, typically blooming in early spring. It thrives in cool, moist environments and prefers partial to full shade.

Due to its ornamental value and unique floral structure, it is cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
