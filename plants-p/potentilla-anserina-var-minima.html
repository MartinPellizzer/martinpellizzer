Potentilla anserina var. minima

Potentilla anserina var. minima, commonly known as the alpine cinquefoil, is a low-growing perennial plant native to alpine and subalpine regions of Europe.

It features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has a mat-forming growth habit with hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are adapted to cold, windy environments. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

This variety is notable for its hardiness and ability to withstand harsh mountain conditions.
