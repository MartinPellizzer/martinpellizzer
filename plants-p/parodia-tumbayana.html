Parodia tumbayana

Parodia tumbayana, commonly known as the Tumbaya cactus, is a species of cactus native to Argentina.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its cylindrical stems that are often ribbed and covered in white spines. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, which are typically short-lived but striking in appearance. Parodia tumbayana is a slow-growing plant that thrives in well-draining soil and full sun exposure.

It is popular among cactus enthusiasts for its unique shape and relatively low maintenance requirements.
