Populus suaveolens var. baicalensis

Populus suaveolens var. baicalensis, commonly known as the Siberian poplar, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-stemmed form and heart-shaped leaves that are typically 5-lobed. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers in catkins, which are borne in clusters. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found near rivers or in wetlands.

This variant is notable for its adaptability to cold climates and its historical use in traditional medicine.
