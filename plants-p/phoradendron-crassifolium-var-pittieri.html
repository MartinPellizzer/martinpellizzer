Phoradendron crassifolium var. pittieri

Phoradendron crassifolium var. pittieri, commonly known as the Pittier's mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches to the branches of host trees, primarily in the genus *Ficus*.

It has small, leathery, elliptical leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces small, white or pale green flowers, followed by fleshy, berry-like fruits that are an important food source for birds. It is native to parts of Central and South America, where it thrives in tropical and subtropical climates.

Its botanical characteristics reflect its adaptation to a parasitic lifestyle while contributing to the ecological dynamics of its host environment.
