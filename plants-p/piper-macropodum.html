Piper macropodum

Piper macropodum, commonly known as the giant betel vine, is a climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many species of pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and can grow up to 30 meters in length. Its fruits are small, round berries that contain a single seed.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in some regions.
