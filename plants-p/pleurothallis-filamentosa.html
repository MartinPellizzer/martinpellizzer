Pleurothallis filamentosa

Pleurothallis filamentosa, commonly known as the filamentous pleurothallis, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a small, terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, humid environments within cloud forests. The plant features delicate, linear leaves and produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and yellow. It is characterized by its unique inflorescence, which often consists of multiple clustered blooms.

Due to its habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
