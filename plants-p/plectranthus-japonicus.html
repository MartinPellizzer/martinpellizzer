Plectranthus japonicus

Plectranthus japonicus, commonly known as Japanese mint or Japanese spearmint, is a perennial herb native to Japan.

It features aromatic, opposite leaves that are often gray-green in color, with a slightly fuzzy texture. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically purple or pink, arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a versatile choice for gardens.

Its fragrance and ornamental value make it popular in both ornamental horticulture and traditional medicine.
