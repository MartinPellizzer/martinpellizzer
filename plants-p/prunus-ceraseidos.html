Prunus ceraseidos

Prunus ceraseidos, commonly known as the sweet cherry tree, is a deciduous tree belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the temperate regions of Europe and parts of Asia. The plant is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves, which are typically dark green and serrated along the edges. Its flowers, which bloom in spring, are white or pale pink and are followed by small, red fruits that ripen to a deep red or black color.

The plant is valued for its ornamental beauty and its edible fruits, which are widely cultivated for both culinary and commercial purposes.
