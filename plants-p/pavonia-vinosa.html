Pavonia vinosa

Pavonia vinosa, commonly known as the scarlet pavonia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, featuring upright stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, clustered pink to red flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Pavonia vinosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly used in gardens and landscapes.
