Potamogeton macrocarpus

Potamogeton macrocarpus, commonly known as the broad-fruit pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater habitats in North America.

It has long, slender stems that are often branched and bear small, oval-shaped leaves. The plant produces distinctive, inflated, green to brown fruits that are adapted for floating and dispersal. It plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.

Potamogeton macrocarpus is often found in slow-moving or still waters such as lakes, ponds, and wetlands.
