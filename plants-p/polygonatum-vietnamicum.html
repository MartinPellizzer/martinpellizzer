Polygonatum vietnamicum

Polygonatum vietnamicum, commonly known as Vietnamese Solomon's seal, is a perennial herb native to Vietnam.

It belongs to the family Asparagaceae and is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit. The plant produces long, slender leaves and clusters of small, white flowers in the summer. Its berries are red and edible, often used in traditional medicine.

Polygonatum vietnamicum is valued for its ornamental appeal and medicinal properties in local herbal practices.
