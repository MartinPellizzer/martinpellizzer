Pityothamnus obovatus

Pityothamnus obovatus, commonly known as the broad-leafed thornbush, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and oval-shaped leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that appear in clusters, followed by woody, cylindrical fruits. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing on rocky soils.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes used for erosion control in degraded landscapes.
