Podocarpus excelsus

Podocarpus excelsus, commonly known as the silver pine, is a evergreen conifer native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, cylindrical trunk and dense, dark green foliage. The plant produces small, cone-like structures that contain its seeds, typical of coniferous species. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Podocarpus excelsus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in horticulture and reforestation efforts.
