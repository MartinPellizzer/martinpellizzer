Primula vulgaris subsp. komarovii

Primula vulgaris subsp.

komarovii, commonly known as the Komarov's primrose, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that typically range in color from white to pale pink, with a distinctive yellow throat.

The plant has lance-shaped, hairy leaves that are arranged in a basal rosette, and it produces a single flowering stem that bears several flower buds. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
