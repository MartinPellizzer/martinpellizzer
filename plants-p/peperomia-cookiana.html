Peperomia cookiana

Peperomia cookiana, commonly known as the Cook's peperomia, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It features glossy, ovate leaves that are typically dark green with prominent veins, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters. The plant is characterized by its dense, upright growth habit and its ability to thrive in low-light conditions. It is often cultivated as a decorative houseplant due to its attractive foliage and ease of care.

Peperomia cookiana is also valued for its air-purifying properties and resilience to indoor environments.
