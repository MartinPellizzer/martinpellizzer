Peperomia dumeticola

Peperomia dumeticola, commonly known as the creeping peperomia, is a low-growing perennial plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It features compact, fleshy leaves that are often arranged in a rosette pattern, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in shades of green or white. The plant is well adapted to shady, humid environments and thrives in well-draining soil. It is popular as a houseplant due to its ease of care and attractive foliage.

Peperomia dumeticola is also valued for its ability to tolerate low light conditions and its compact growth habit.
