Parsonsia blepharophylla

Parsonsia blepharophylla, commonly known as the Christmas bell, is a striking flowering plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its vibrant orange or red tubular flowers that resemble bells, which bloom during the winter months. The plant produces fleshy, berry-like fruits that are edible for certain bird species. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Its unique floral structure and seasonal blooming make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
