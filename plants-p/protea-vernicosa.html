Protea vernicosa

Protea vernicosa, commonly known as the yellow protea, is a striking South African plant belonging to the Proteaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescence composed of numerous bracts that resemble petals. The plant typically grows in fynbos vegetation, thriving in well-drained, acidic soils. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange, with a distinctive waxy texture and a central cone-like structure.

Protea vernicosa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and plays a role in local ecosystems by providing nectar for pollinators.
