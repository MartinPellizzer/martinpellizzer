Prunus jamasakura f. longissima

Prunus jamasakura f. longissima, commonly known as the Japanese cherry blossom variant with elongated petals, is a cultivar of the Prunus jamasakura species.

It is characterized by its distinctive, elongated and slender petals that give it a more delicate appearance compared to standard cherry blossoms. This variety is native to Japan and is often cultivated for its ornamental value during the spring blooming season. The flowers are typically pale pink to white, with a subtle fragrance.

It is a popular choice for ornamental gardens and public parks due to its aesthetic appeal and seasonal display.
