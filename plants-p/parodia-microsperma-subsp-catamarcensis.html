Parodia microsperma subsp. catamarcensis

Parodia microsperma subsp.

catamarcensis, commonly known as the Catamarcensis Cactus, is a rare and compact cactus native to Argentina. It features a short, cylindrical stem with a ribbed structure that helps it store water efficiently.

The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers during the warmer months, typically in spring. It is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water and well-draining soil.

Due to its limited distribution and unique characteristics, it is considered a valuable species in botanical studies.
