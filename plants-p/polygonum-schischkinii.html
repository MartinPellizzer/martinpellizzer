Polygonum schischkinii

Polygonum schischkinii, commonly known as the Schischkin's knotweed, is a species of flowering plant in the buckwheat family, Polygonaceae.

It is native to Central Asia and is characterized by its erect, herbaceous stems and clusters of small, reddish or greenish flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in alpine or steppe environments. Its leaves are ovate to lanceolate, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface, and it produces small, three-lobed fruits.

Polygonum schischkinii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and seasonal color.
