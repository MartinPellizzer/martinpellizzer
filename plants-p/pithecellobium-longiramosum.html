Pithecellobium longiramosum

Pithecellobium longiramosum, commonly known as the long-stemmed carambola, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces edible fruits that are similar in appearance to star fruit, though they are less commonly consumed.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value as well as its fruit.
