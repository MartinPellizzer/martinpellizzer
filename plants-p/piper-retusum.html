Piper retusum

Piper retusum, commonly known as the rough pepper vine, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia and the Pacific.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species such as black pepper. The plant is characterized by its trailing growth habit, with heart-shaped leaves and small, greenish-white flowers. Its fruit, a small berry, contains aromatic seeds used in traditional medicine and cuisine.

The botanical features of Piper retusum make it an important species in both ecological and cultural contexts.
