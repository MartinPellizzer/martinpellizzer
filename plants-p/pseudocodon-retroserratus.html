Pseudocodon retroserratus

Pseudocodon retroserratus, commonly known as the backward-serrated caryophyll, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Caryophyllaceae.

It is characterized by its distinctive leaves with backward-facing serrations along the margins. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or white in color. It is native to the mountainous regions of Central and South America.

Pseudocodon retroserratus is often found in rocky or well-drained soils and is adapted to temperate climates.
