Plumeria inodora

Plumeria inodora, commonly known as the night-blooming cereus, is a tropical plant native to the Caribbean and Central America.

It is characterized by its large, waxy, fragrant flowers that open at night and close by dawn. The plant has simple, lance-shaped leaves and produces a milky sap when damaged. It thrives in warm, sunny climates with well-draining soil.

Plumeria inodora is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
