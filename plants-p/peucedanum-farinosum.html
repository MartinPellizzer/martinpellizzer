Peucedanum farinosum

Peucedanum farinosum, commonly known as the farinaed cowparsley, is a member of the Apiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic foliage and umbellate flowers.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia, where it thrives in rocky and well-drained soils. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring finely divided leaves and small, white to greenish flowers that bloom in late spring. Its seeds are small and dry, often dispersed by wind, contributing to its natural distribution.

The plant contains various bioactive compounds, making it of interest in traditional medicine and phytochemical research.
