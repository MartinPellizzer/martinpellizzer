Pyrus torminalis

Pyrus torminalis, commonly known as the whitebeam, is a deciduous tree native to Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its small, white flowers that bloom in spring. The tree produces edible berries that are typically consumed by birds and other wildlife. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a rough, textured surface.

Pyrus torminalis is often found in woodlands and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
