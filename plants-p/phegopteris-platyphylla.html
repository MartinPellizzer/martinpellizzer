Phegopteris platyphylla

Phegopteris platyphylla, commonly known as the broad-leafed water fern, is a species of aquatic fern native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its large, broad, leathery fronds that are typically divided into multiple lobes. The plant thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, such as ponds and wetlands. Its rhizomes are often submerged, anchoring the plant and facilitating nutrient absorption.

Phegopteris platyphylla plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for small organisms and contributing to water quality regulation.
