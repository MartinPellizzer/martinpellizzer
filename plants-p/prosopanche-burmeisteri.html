Prosopanche burmeisteri

Prosopanche burmeisteri, commonly known as the Burmeister's broom.

It is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of South America, particularly in Argentina and Chile. The plant produces striking yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. It has a succulent, branching structure that helps it conserve water in its harsh environment.

Its botanical classification places it in the family Fabaceae, which includes legumes.
