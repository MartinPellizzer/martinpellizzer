Penstemon heterander

Penstemon heterander, commonly known as the narrowleaf beardtongue, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and striking, tubular flowers that range in color from purple to pink. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It prefers well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, contributing to its elegant appearance.
