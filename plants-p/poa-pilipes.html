Poa pilipes

Poa pilipes, commonly known as the slender meadowgrass, is a species of grass native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its fine, wiry leaves and tufted inflorescence, which consists of slender spikelets. The plant typically grows in open, moist habitats such as meadows, pastures, and along stream banks. Poa pilipes is often found in nutrient-poor soils and is adapted to cool, temperate climates.

Its fibrous root system helps stabilize soil, making it an important component of grassland ecosystems.
