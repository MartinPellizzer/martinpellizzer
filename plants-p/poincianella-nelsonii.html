Poincianella nelsonii

Poincianella nelsonii, commonly known as the Nelson's poinciana, is a small evergreen shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It features delicate, fern-like leaves that are often aromatic and emit a pleasant scent. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
