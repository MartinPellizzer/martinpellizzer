Polemonium dubium

Polemonium dubium, commonly known as the white Jacob's ladder, is a herbaceous perennial native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Polemoniaceae family and is characterized by its cascading clusters of white or pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist woodlands and along stream banks, preferring partial to full shade. Its foliage consists of opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy to the touch.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, supporting various pollinators.
