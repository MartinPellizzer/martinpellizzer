Pultenaea incurvata

Pultenaea incurvata, commonly known as the sickle-bush, is a shrub native to southern Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its typical pea-like flowers and nitrogen-fixing ability. The plant features slender, often curved stems and small, leathery leaves that are typically grey-green in color. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange, appearing in clusters and attracting nectar-feeding birds and insects.

Pultenaea incurvata is well adapted to dry, sandy soils and is often found in heathlands and coastal areas.
