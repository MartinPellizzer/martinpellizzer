Paspalum furcatum var. filiforme

Paspalum furcatum var. filiforme, commonly known as slender paspalum, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its fine, hair-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. This plant thrives in wetland and marshy environments, often forming dense mats that help stabilize soil. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in wetland restoration.

Its adaptability to waterlogged conditions makes it an important species in aquatic plant communities.
