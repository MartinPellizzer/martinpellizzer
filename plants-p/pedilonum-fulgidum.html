Pedilonum fulgidum

Pedilonum fulgidum, commonly known as the golden bell vine, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, often with a metallic sheen. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically yellow or orange when mature. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a waxy texture.

Pedilonum fulgidum is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a climbing plant.
