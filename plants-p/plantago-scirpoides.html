Plantago scirpoides

Plantago scirpoides, commonly known as narrowleaf plantain, is a flowering plant belonging to the Plantaginaceae family.

It is native to parts of Europe, Asia, and Africa, and is often found in moist grasslands and along stream banks. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. Its small, greenish-white flowers are arranged in spikes and bloom in late spring to early summer.

It is a perennial herb that can reach heights of up to 60 centimeters.
