Poa caesia var. aspera

Poa caesia var. aspera, commonly known as the rough bluegrass, is a variant of the bluegrass species native to North America.

It is characterized by its coarse, rough-textured leaves and compact growth habit. This plant typically thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open fields and meadows. Its foliage is generally a deep blue-green color, which contributes to its ornamental value.

It is valued for its durability and is sometimes used in turfgrass mixes for its resilience.
