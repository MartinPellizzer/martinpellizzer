Polygala salviniana

Polygala salviniana, commonly known as the narrowleaf milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It typically grows in open habitats such as grasslands, sand dunes, and rocky soils. The plant features slender, branching stems and small, often pale green or yellowish leaves. Its flowers are clustered in spikelike inflorescences and are usually pink to purple in color.

The species is adapted to drought conditions, with deep root systems that help it survive in harsh environments.
