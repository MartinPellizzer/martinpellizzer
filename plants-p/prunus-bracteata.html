Prunus bracteata

Prunus bracteata, commonly known as the Chinese sand pear, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically yellow or orange when ripe. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has a moderate growth rate.

Prunus bracteata is relatively drought-tolerant and prefers well-drained soil.
