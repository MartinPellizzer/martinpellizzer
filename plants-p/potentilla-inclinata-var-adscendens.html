Potentilla inclinata var. adscendens

Potentilla inclinata var. adscendens, commonly known as the ascending potentilla, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is a low-growing shrub that typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features wiry, ascending stems that are often covered in fine hairs. Its leaves are small, ovate, and have a slightly serrated edge, while its flowers are yellow and clustered in dense, spherical inflorescences.

This variety is adapted to rocky and dry environments, making it a hardy species found in alpine and subalpine habitats.
