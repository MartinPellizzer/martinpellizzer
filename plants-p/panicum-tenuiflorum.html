Panicum tenuiflorum

Panicum tenuiflorum, commonly known as slenderflower panicgrass, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States.

It is a member of the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its delicate, thread-like flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as prairies and disturbed soils. It has slender, erect stems and small, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and restoration projects.
