Potamogeton × nitens subsp. curvifolius

Potamogeton × nitens subsp.

curvifolius, commonly known as the curled-leaf pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to North America. It is characterized by its elongated, ribbon-like leaves that often curl at the tips, giving it its common name.

The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically arranged in spike-like clusters. It thrives in shallow, freshwater environments such as lakes and slow-moving rivers.

Its morphology and growth habits make it an important component of aquatic ecosystems.
