Pilosella auriculoides subsp. pseudotanythrix

Pilosella auriculoides subsp.

pseudotanythrix, commonly known as the auriculate pilosella, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family. It is characterized by its small, yellowish to whitish flowers arranged in dense, spherical heads.

The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine regions, preferring rocky or gravelly soils. Its leaves are usually hairy and lobed, giving it a distinctive appearance.

This subspecies is often found in mountainous areas of Europe and parts of western Asia.
