Pleurothallis hamiltonii

Pleurothallis hamiltonii, commonly known as the Hamilton's orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and pink. The plant is epiphytic, meaning it grows on the surface of trees or other structures without deriving nutrients from them. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Its unique floral structure and subtle coloration make it a prized species among orchid enthusiasts.
