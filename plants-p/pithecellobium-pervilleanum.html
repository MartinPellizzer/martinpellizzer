Pithecellobium pervilleanum

Pithecellobium pervilleanum, commonly known as the velvet tamarind, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant produces small, edible pods that contain seeds surrounded by a sticky, resinous pulp. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has some traditional uses in local medicine.

The plant thrives in well-drained soil and requires a warm, humid climate for optimal growth.
