Protea strobus

Protea strobus, commonly known as the sugar bush, is a South African plant belonging to the Proteaceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive, rounded, yellowish-white flower heads that resemble a cluster of small flowers. The plant typically grows in coastal regions and has a shrub-like appearance with woody stems. Its leaves are thick, leathery, and often covered in a waxy coating to reduce water loss.

Protea strobus plays an important role in local ecosystems and is also cultivated for ornamental purposes.
