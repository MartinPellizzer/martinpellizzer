Piptostigma goslineanum

Piptostigma goslineanum, commonly known as the Gosline's thorny acacia, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its woody stems and small, clustered leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers that are followed by small, woody pods containing seeds. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes used in traditional medicine and as a source of fuel.
