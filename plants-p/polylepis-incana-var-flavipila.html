Polylepis incana var. flavipila

Polylepis incana var. flavipila, commonly known as the yellow-flowered polylepis, is a shrub or small tree native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers and grayish-barked trunk. The plant thrives in high-altitude environments, typically between 2,500 and 4,000 meters above sea level. Its leaves are usually small, elliptical, and covered in a fine layer of white hairs.

This species is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various Andean wildlife species.
