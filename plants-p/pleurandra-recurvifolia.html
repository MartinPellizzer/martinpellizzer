Pleurandra recurvifolia

Pleurandra recurvifolia, commonly known as the recurved pleurandra, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that curve backward, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in clusters, typically white or pale yellow, which bloom in the spring. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Its foliage is prized for its aesthetic value and is used in traditional landscaping and horticulture.
