Phlomis brevibracteata

Phlomis brevibracteata, commonly known as narrow-bracted blazingstar, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and gray-green, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces tall, branched flowering stems that bear showy, yellow-orange flower heads in late summer. Its flowers are typically composed of many small, tubular florets surrounded by a series of bracts.

Phlomis brevibracteata is adapted to dry, rocky soils and is often found in prairies and open woodlands.
