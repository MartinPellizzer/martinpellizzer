Piper rivi-vetusti

Piper rivi-vetusti, commonly known as the old river pepper, is a tropical climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many species of pepper plants. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are followed by elongated, fleshy fruits containing aromatic seeds. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a rough texture, while the stems are woody and vine-like.

The plant is often cultivated for its peppery fruits, which are used in traditional cuisines and herbal remedies.
