Pogonarthria fleckii

Pogonarthria fleckii, commonly known as the flecked grass, is a perennial grass species native to Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and narrow, linear leaves with a distinctive silver or grayish sheen. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and disturbed areas.

Pogonarthria fleckii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping due to its drought tolerance and low maintenance requirements.
