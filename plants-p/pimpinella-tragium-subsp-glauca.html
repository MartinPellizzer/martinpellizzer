Pimpinella tragium subsp. glauca

Pimpinella tragium subsp.

glauca, commonly known as blue bittercress, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It is a herbaceous annual or biennial species characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, lance-shaped leaves.

The flowers are white with four petals and are arranged in clusters, typically blooming in early spring. The plant produces small, round, greenish-brown seeds that are dispersed by wind.

It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and waste areas.
