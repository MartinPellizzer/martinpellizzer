Phalaenopsis bellina f. murtoniana

Phalaenopsis bellina f. murtoniana, commonly known as the Murton's phalaenopsis, is a hybrid orchid species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its elegant, long-lasting flowers that typically bloom in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant has a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that support its foliage and flowers. Its leaves are broad and elliptical, often arranged in a fan-like pattern.

This orchid prefers indirect light and consistent moisture, making it a popular choice for indoor cultivation.
