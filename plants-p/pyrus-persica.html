Pyrus persica

Pyrus persica, commonly known as the European pear.

It is a deciduous tree native to Europe and parts of Asia, characterized by its broad, ovate leaves and clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers. The tree produces pomes, which are fleshy fruits with a crisp, juicy texture and a sweet, sometimes aromatic flavor. Its fruit is widely cultivated for both fresh consumption and culinary use.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical significance in horticulture.
