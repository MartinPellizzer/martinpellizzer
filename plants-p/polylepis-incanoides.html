Polylepis incanoides

Polylepis incanoides, commonly known as the Andean cloud forest tree, is a species native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes.

It is characterized by its small, papery leaves and distinctive, elongated cones. This tree thrives in cool, moist climates and is often found in cloud forests at elevations between 2,500 and 4,000 meters. Its bark is thick and fissured, providing protection against harsh environmental conditions.

Polylepis incanoides plays a crucial role in maintaining the ecological balance of its habitat.
