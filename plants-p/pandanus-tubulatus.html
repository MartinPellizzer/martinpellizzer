Pandanus tubulatus

Pandanus tubulatus, commonly known as the tubular pandanus, is a flowering plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and long, narrow leaves that are often used in traditional thatching. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically yellow or green in color. Its roots and leaves have been used for various practical purposes by local communities.

The species is well adapted to coastal and wetland environments, thriving in humid, tropical climates.
