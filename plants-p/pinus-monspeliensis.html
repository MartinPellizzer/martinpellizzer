Pinus monspeliensis

Pinus monspeliensis, commonly known as the Syrian pine, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to the eastern Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, pyramidal shape and long, needle-like leaves that grow in bundles of five. The tree produces cone-like structures that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Its bark is thick and fissured, providing protection against fire and drought.

This species is often found in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions.
