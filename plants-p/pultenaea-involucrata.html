Pultenaea involucrata

Pultenaea involucrata, commonly known as the golden pultenaea, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is a shrub that typically grows in coastal and heathland areas, characterized by its dense, compact growth habit. The plant produces small, bright yellow flowers that are clustered in racemes, blooming in spring and summer. Its leaves are narrow and leathery, often with a slightly waxy texture, and it is adapted to thrive in well-drained soils.

Pultenaea involucrata is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
