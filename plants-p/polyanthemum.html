Polyanthemum

Polyanthemum, commonly known as the everlasting flower, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its showy, composite flower heads that resemble daisies, composed of numerous small florets. The plant is native to various regions in the Mediterranean and parts of Asia, where it thrives in well-drained soils. Polyanthemum species are often cultivated for their ornamental value and are used in gardens for their long-lasting blooms.

They are also known for their resilience and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
