Pedicularis robusta

Pedicularis robusta, commonly known as the spurred gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue or purple flowers with distinctive spurred petals. The plant typically grows in alpine or subalpine habitats, often in rocky or moist soils. Its tubular flowers are adapted for pollination by insects such as bees and butterflies.

Pedicularis robusta is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
