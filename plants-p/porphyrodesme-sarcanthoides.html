Porphyrodesme sarcanthoides

Porphyrodesme sarcanthoides, commonly known as the red-veined orchid, is a striking epiphytic orchid native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant green leaves with distinctive red veins, which give it its common name. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant, white to pale pink flowers that bloom during the wet season. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining, organic-rich substrates.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by plant enthusiasts.
