Passiflora foetida var. ellisonii

Passiflora foetida var. ellisonii, commonly known as the Ellison's passionflower, is a striking climbing vine native to the southeastern United States.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and distinctive, showy flowers that have a complex structure with five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments. The plant produces edible, orange-red berries that are favored by wildlife and some humans. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Its botanical characteristics make it a notable species in the Passifloraceae family.
