Polygonatum gentilianum

Polygonatum gentilianum, commonly known as the Solomon's seal, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Asia.

It belongs to the Asparagaceae family and is characterized by its arching stems and bell-shaped flowers. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in a raceme, followed by dark blue to black berries. Its leaves are lance-shaped and form a rosette at the base, contributing to its ornamental value in gardens.

Polygonatum gentilianum is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
