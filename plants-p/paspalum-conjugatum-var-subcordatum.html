Paspalum conjugatum var. subcordatum

Paspalum conjugatum var. subcordatum, commonly known as the narrowleaf paspalum, is a perennial grass native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically less than 1 cm wide. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with numerous tiny flowers, which are often arranged in clusters. It thrives in wetland environments and is adapted to tolerate waterlogged soils.

This species is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife.
