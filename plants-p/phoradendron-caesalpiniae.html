Phoradendron caesalpiniae

Phoradendron caesalpiniae, commonly known as the yellow shower tree, is a woody vine native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its climbing habit and the production of small, yellowish-green flowers that appear in clusters. The plant has compound leaves with opposite arrangement and is often found growing on trees in tropical rainforests. Its flowers are pollinated by insects, and it produces small, round fruits that contain seeds.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its unique appearance.
