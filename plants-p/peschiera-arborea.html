Peschiera arborea

Peschiera arborea, commonly known as the tree pechiera, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, often branched stems and lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stem. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are usually white or pale yellow, clustered in loose inflorescences. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

The plant's botanical features make it a desirable species for ornamental gardening and ecological restoration projects.
