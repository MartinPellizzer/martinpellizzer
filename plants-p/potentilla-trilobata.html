Potentilla trilobata

Potentilla trilobata, commonly known as the three-lobed cinquefoil, is a perennial shrub native to North America.

It typically grows in moist, sunny habitats such as stream banks and wet meadows. The plant features small, yellow, five-petaled flowers that bloom in early summer. Its leaves are compound, with three leaflets that are usually lobed or notched.

Potentilla trilobata is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support local wildlife.
