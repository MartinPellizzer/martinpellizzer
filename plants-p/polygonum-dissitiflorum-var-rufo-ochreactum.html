Polygonum dissitiflorum var. rufo-ochreactum

Polygonum dissitiflorum var. rufo-ochreactum, commonly known as the red-ochre knotweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia.

It features upright stems covered in dense, glandular hairs, giving it a distinctive fuzzy appearance. The leaves are broadly ovate to rhombic, with a rough texture and a reddish tint on the underside. Its small, clustered flowers are typically greenish-white and appear in late summer.

This plant is often found in moist environments such as marshes and stream banks.
