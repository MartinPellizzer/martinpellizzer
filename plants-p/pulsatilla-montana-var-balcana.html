Pulsatilla montana var. balcana

Pulsatilla montana var. balcana, commonly known as the Balkan pasqueflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its showy, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow. The plant typically grows in rocky or calcareous soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine regions. Its foliage consists of palmately divided leaves, and it produces follicles as its fruit type.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its early spring blooms.
