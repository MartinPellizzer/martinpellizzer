Prenanthes obtusa

Prenanthes obtusa, commonly known as roughleaf water dropwort, is a herbaceous plant native to North America.

It typically grows in moist, shady environments such as stream banks and woodland edges. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a rough texture and small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in clusters. Its root system is fibrous and adapted to absorb nutrients from wet soils.

Prenanthes obtusa is often found in riparian zones and is a member of the Apiaceae family, which includes carrots and parsley.
