Piriqueta cistoides var. bracteolata

Piriqueta cistoides var. bracteolata, commonly known as the bracteolate rockrose, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a shrub characterized by its compact growth habit and dense foliage of small, leathery leaves. The plant produces showy, pink to red flowers in clusters during the spring and summer months. Its flowers are typically borne on short, branching stems and are adapted for pollination by bees and other insects.

This variety is noted for its drought tolerance and ability to thrive in rocky, well-drained soils.
