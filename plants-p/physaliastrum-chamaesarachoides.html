Physaliastrum chamaesarachoides

Physaliastrum chamaesarachoides, commonly known as the sandverbena, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its prostrate growth habit and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically features hairy leaves and produces inconspicuous, greenish-white blooms. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments.

Due to its resilience and ornamental potential, it is occasionally cultivated in xeriscaped gardens.
