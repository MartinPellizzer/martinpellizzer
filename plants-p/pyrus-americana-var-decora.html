Pyrus americana var. decora

Pyrus americana var. decora, commonly known as the showy serviceberry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its ornamental white flowers that appear in early spring, followed by edible berries in late summer. The plant has pinnately compound leaves that turn vibrant shades of red and orange in the fall. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often used in landscaping for its aesthetic value.

The species is also valued for its ecological benefits, providing food for birds and pollinators.
