Pogostemon parviflorus var. hispidus

Pogostemon parviflorus var. hispidus, commonly known as the hairy pogostemon, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It features lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, hairy textures, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, pale blue to purple flowers arranged in whorled clusters along the stems. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical characteristics make it an interesting subject for both horticultural and ecological studies.
