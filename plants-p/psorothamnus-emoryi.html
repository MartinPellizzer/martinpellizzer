Psorothamnus emoryi

Psorothamnus emoryi, commonly known as Emory's greasewood, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its gray-green foliage and small, spiny leaves that help reduce water loss in arid environments. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers that bloom in the spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to harsh, desert conditions.

Psorothamnus emoryi plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various desert wildlife.
