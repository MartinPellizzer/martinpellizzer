Pouteria wurdackii

Pouteria wurdackii, commonly known as the Brazilian nut tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is characterized by its large, compound leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into elongated, woody pods. The tree's most notable feature is its edible seed, commonly referred to as the Brazil nut, which is encased in a hard shell. The plant thrives in humid, nutrient-rich soils and requires a warm, tropical climate for optimal growth.

It plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
