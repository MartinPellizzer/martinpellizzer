Polypodium flavopunctatum var. pinnatifidum

Polypodium flavopunctatum var. pinnatifidum, commonly known as the golden dot huperzia, is a rare and distinctive fern species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its finely divided, pinnatifid (broadly divided) fronds that are often covered in a dense layer of golden-yellow to orange-brown spots. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its rhizomes are thick and creeping, allowing it to form dense colonies over time.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists alike.
