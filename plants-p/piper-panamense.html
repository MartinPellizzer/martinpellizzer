Piper panamense

Piper panamense, commonly known as the Panamanian pepper plant, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to Central and South America, particularly found in the tropical regions of Panama and Colombia. The plant is characterized by its climbing habit, with opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its small, greenish-yellow flowers are clustered in spikes, and it produces small, peppery fruits that are used in traditional medicine.

The botanical features of Piper panamense make it an important species for both ecological and medicinal research.
