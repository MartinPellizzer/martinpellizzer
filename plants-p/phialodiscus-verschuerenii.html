Phialodiscus verschuerenii

Phialodiscus verschuerenii, commonly known as the African violet, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Gesneriaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-forming leaves and showy, tubular flowers. The plant typically produces violet or purple flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its ornamental value and ease of care contribute to its widespread cultivation as a houseplant.
