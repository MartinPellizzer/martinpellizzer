Poa buchtienii

Poa buchtienii, commonly known as the Buchtien's bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, hairless blades that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, Poa buchtienii is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
