Polygonum × difficile

Polygonum × difficile, commonly known as the hardy knotweed, is a hybrid perennial plant belonging to the Polygonaceae family.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and distinctive, arrow-shaped leaves that are often reddish in color. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range from green to reddish-purple, typically blooming in late summer. It is native to temperate regions of Asia and has been introduced to other parts of the world.

Due to its vigorous growth and ability to spread through rhizomes, it can become invasive in certain environments.
