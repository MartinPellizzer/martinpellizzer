Pyrus ailanthoides

Pyrus ailanthoides, commonly known as the Chinese white ash, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellowish-green flowers. The tree produces small, apple-like fruits that are typically edible but not widely cultivated for human consumption. It is often found in moist, well-drained soils and can thrive in a variety of climates.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally planted in gardens and parks.
