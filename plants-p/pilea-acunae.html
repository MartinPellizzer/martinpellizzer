Pilea acunae

Pilea acunae, commonly known as the Chinese money plant, is a species of flowering plant in the family Araceae.

It is native to China and is characterized by its round, waxy leaves that resemble silver coins. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers that are followed by spathes, typical of the Araceae family. It is a compact, evergreen perennial that thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil.

Pilea acunae is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental foliage and ease of care.
