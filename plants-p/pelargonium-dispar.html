Pelargonium dispar

Pelargonium dispar, commonly known as the spotted geranium, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Geraniaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, lobed leaves with a serrated margin. The plant produces clusters of small, pink to red flowers that bloom in summer. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soil.

Pelargonium dispar is also noted for its resilience to drought and mild frost.
