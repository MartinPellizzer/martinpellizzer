Primula renifolia

Primula renifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf primrose, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It features basal leaves that are broad and heart-shaped, with a velvety texture, and produces clusters of bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and prefers partial to full shade. Its flowers bloom in early spring, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and woodland plantings.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is relatively easy to cultivate in suitable conditions.
