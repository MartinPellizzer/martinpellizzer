Paeonia wittmanniana

Paeonia wittmanniana, commonly known as the Wittmann's peony, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces large, showy flowers with five to eight petals. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring compound leaves with serrated edges. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, displaying shades of pink, red, or white.

The plant is valued for its ornamental beauty and is also studied for its potential medicinal properties.
