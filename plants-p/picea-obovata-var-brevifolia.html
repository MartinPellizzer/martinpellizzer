Picea obovata var. brevifolia

Picea obovata var. brevifolia, commonly known as the Japanese white pine, is a variant of the white pine species native to Japan.

It is characterized by its narrow, conical shape and needle-like leaves that are short and broad, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, cone-like structures that contain seeds, typical of coniferous trees. Its foliage is often a vibrant green, and it thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
