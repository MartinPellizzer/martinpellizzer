Piper rubripes

Piper rubripes, commonly known as the red pepper plant, is a species of flowering vine native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper plants such as the common black pepper. The plant produces small, bright red berries that are used in traditional medicine and as a spice. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a peppery scent, while its stems are woody and can grow up to several meters in length.

The plant thrives in humid, warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
