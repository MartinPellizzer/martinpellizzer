Primula intricata subsp. subcordata

Primula intricata subsp.

subcordata, commonly known as the subcordate primrose, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of central Asia. It features basal leaves that are heart-shaped with a rounded base, and its flowers are typically violet or purple, clustered in cymes.

The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows. It has a compact growth habit and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.

Its botanical characteristics make it a subject of interest in both horticulture and botanical research.
