Primula hirsuta subf. nivea

Primula hirsuta subf.

nivea, commonly known as the snowy primrose, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that typically range in color from white to pale pink.

The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes. Its flowers bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable growing conditions.
