Portulaca oleracea var. violacea

Portulaca oleracea var. violacea, commonly known as violet portulaca, is an annual flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its fleshy, succulent leaves and vibrant violet or purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a common weed in gardens and cultivated fields. Its botanical features include erect stems and small, spiny leaves that help reduce water loss.

The species is widely used in traditional medicine and as a vegetable in some cuisines.
