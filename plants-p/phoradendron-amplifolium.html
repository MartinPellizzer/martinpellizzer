Phoradendron amplifolium

Phoradendron amplifolium, commonly known as the giant mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on various host trees in the southeastern United States.

It has thick, fleshy leaves that are typically clustered at the ends of branches, and its flowers are small, greenish-white, and often clustered in inflorescences. The plant produces small, white or yellowish berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife.

Its unique growth habit and ecological role make it a notable species in forest ecosystems.
