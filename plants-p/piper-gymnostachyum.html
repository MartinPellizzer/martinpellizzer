Piper gymnostachyum

Piper gymnostachyum, commonly known as the white pepper plant, is a tropical vine belonging to the Piperaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and small, greenish-white flowers. The plant produces berries that turn from green to dark red as they mature. The fruit is used to extract the spice known as white pepper, which is a key ingredient in many cuisines.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both horticulture and the spice trade.
