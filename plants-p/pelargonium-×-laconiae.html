Pelargonium × laconiae

Pelargonium × laconiae, commonly known as the lemon-scented geranium, is a hybrid plant that exhibits a range of botanical characteristics.

It is a member of the Geraniaceae family and is known for its aromatic foliage, which emits a citrus-like scent when crushed. The plant typically has rounded, lobed leaves that are covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, clustered flowers in shades of pink, red, or white. It is a perennial herb that thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardeners.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its vibrant blooms and pleasant fragrance, which also attract beneficial insects.
