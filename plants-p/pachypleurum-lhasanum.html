Pachypleurum lhasanum

Pachypleurum lhasanum, commonly known as the Lhasa bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Tibetan Plateau.

It is characterized by its robust, succulent stems and showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant thrives in high-altitude environments with cool temperatures and well-drained soil. Its leaves are thick and fleshy, adapted to conserve water in arid conditions.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is a symbol of resilience in harsh climates.
