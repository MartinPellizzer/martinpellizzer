Piper griseovenosum

Piper griseovenosum, commonly known as gray veined pepper or wild pepper, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species. The plant is characterized by its dark green, veined leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. Its fruit, a small, round berry, contains the aromatic seeds used in traditional medicine and cuisine.

The plant thrives in humid, tropical environments and is often found in rainforests.
