Philotheca basistyla

Philotheca basistyla, commonly known as the narrow-leaved greenbark, is a small evergreen shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in late winter to early spring, which are clustered in umbels. Its bark is rough and fissured, with a distinctive greenish hue.

Philotheca basistyla is typically found in dry, sandy soils in open forest or heathland environments.
