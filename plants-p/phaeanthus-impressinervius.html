Phaeanthus impressinervius

Phaeanthus impressinervius, commonly known as the impressively veined phaeanthus, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink. The leaves are typically opposite and have a distinctive, intricate venation pattern that gives the plant its scientific name. It thrives in humid, tropical environments and is often found in shaded forest understories.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
