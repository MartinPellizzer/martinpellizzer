Persicaria sieboldii var. sericea

Persicaria sieboldii var. sericea, commonly known as silky smartweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to East Asia.

It features upright stems that are often covered with fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or red, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly serrated edge, and they are often covered with a soft, downy texture.

This variety is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in moist to wet soil conditions.
