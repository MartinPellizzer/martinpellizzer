Peperomia australis

Peperomia australis, commonly known as the Australian pepper plant, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Australia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its compact, upright growth habit. The plant features broad, waxy leaves that are typically green with a slight reddish or purple tinge on the underside. It produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers that are clustered in spikes.

Peperomia australis is well adapted to dry conditions and is often grown as a houseplant due to its low maintenance requirements.
