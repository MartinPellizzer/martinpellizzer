Penstemon ambiguus var. foliosus

Penstemon ambiguus var. foliosus, commonly known as the leafy beardtongue, is a perennial plant native to the western United States.

It features upright, branched stems that bear clusters of tubular, two-lipped flowers in shades of pink to purple. The leaves are lance-shaped and slightly hairy, contributing to its ornamental value in gardens. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

This variety is noted for its drought tolerance and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
