Plantago mitchellii

Plantago mitchellii, commonly known as Mitchell's plantain, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows, stream banks, and woodland edges. The plant features a basal rosette of broad, heart-shaped leaves with a rough, hairy texture. Its flowering stems bear dense clusters of small, greenish-white flowers that develop into capsule fruits containing numerous seeds.

This species is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional herbal remedies.
