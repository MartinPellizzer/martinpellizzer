Petunia serpyllifolia

Petunia serpyllifolia, commonly known as creeping petunia, is a low-growing annual plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It features small, delicate flowers that are typically blue, violet, or white, blooming in clusters along its stems. The plant has hairy, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence, giving it a soft texture. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its compact growth habit and vibrant flowers make it an ornamental plant with ecological benefits for pollinators.
