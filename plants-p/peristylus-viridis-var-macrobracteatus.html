Peristylus viridis var. macrobracteatus

Peristylus viridis var. macrobracteatus, commonly known as the green peristylus with large bracts, is a unique variant of the peristylus genus.

It is characterized by its vibrant green foliage and distinctive, elongated bracts that are larger than those of the typical species. The plant exhibits a compact growth habit, making it suitable for small garden spaces or container cultivation. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and arranged in a rosette pattern, adding to its ornamental appeal.

This variety is native to specific regions in South America and is valued for its aesthetic qualities in horticultural settings.
