Phylica pearsonii

Phylica pearsonii, commonly known as the pearson's bottlebrush, is a shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, cylindrical flower spikes that resemble bottle brushes, typically ranging in color from red to yellow. The plant belongs to the Proteaceae family and is well adapted to dry, sandy soils. It produces small, leathery leaves and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Phylica pearsonii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine.
