Potentilla caulescens var. doerfleri

Potentilla caulescens var. doerfleri, commonly known as the Doerfler's cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its upright, slender stems and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves that are often hairy, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to cool, high-altitude environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and attractive foliage.
