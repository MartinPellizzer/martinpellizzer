Potamogeton alpinus f. angustifolius

Potamogeton alpinus f. angustifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaved pondweed, is a aquatic plant characterized by its slender, elongated leaves that are typically less than 1 cm wide.

It thrives in freshwater environments, particularly in shallow, nutrient-rich waters with soft sediment. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are clustered in dense spikes, often floating at the water's surface. Its leaves are often ribbon-like and can be found submerged or floating.

This subspecies is adapted to cold climates and is often found in alpine or subalpine regions.
