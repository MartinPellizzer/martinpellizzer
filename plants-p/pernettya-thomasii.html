Pernettya thomasii

Pernettya thomasii, commonly known as Thomas's cowberry, is a small evergreen shrub native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its clustered growth habit and small, leathery leaves. The plant produces bell-shaped, white to pink flowers in the spring, which are followed by bright red, juicy berries that are edible for both humans and wildlife.

Pernettya thomasii thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in subalpine and alpine habitats.
