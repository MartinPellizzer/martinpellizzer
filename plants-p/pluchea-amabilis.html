Pluchea amabilis

Pluchea amabilis, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its hairy, ovate leaves and small yellow flowers. The plant is often found in moist, disturbed habitats such as roadsides and riverbanks. It has a creeping growth habit and produces seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Pluchea amabilis is also noted for its use in traditional medicine in some cultures.
