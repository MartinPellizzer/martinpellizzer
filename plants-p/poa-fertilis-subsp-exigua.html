Poa fertilis subsp. exigua

Poa fertilis subsp.

exigua, commonly known as the slender meadow grass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere. It typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows, roadsides, and forest edges.

The plant has slender, upright stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its inflorescence consists of a loose cluster of spikelets, each containing several small, two-rowed flowers.

This subspecies is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.
