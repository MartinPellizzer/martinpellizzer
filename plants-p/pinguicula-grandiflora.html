Pinguicula grandiflora

Pinguicula grandiflora, commonly known as the large-flowered butterwort, is a carnivorous plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its rosette of fleshy, sticky leaves that trap and digest small insects. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in moist, acidic environments and requires consistent humidity to flourish.

Its unique adaptations make it a fascinating subject for both botanists and plant enthusiasts.
