Piper apollinaris

Piper apollinaris, commonly known as the Apollinaris pepper or the sacred pepper, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its small, elongated fruits that resemble peppercorns. The plant produces a pungent, spicy flavor due to the presence of piperine, a compound also found in black pepper. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value as well as its medicinal properties.

The leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a glossy appearance, contributing to its aesthetic appeal in gardens.
