Paronychia sessiliflora var. brevicuspis

Paronychia sessiliflora var. brevicuspis, commonly known as the narrow-cusped sandmat, is a low-growing perennial plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It features a mat-forming growth habit with small, fleshy, blue-green leaves that are often covered in fine, white hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its succulent leaves help it store water, enabling it to thrive in dry conditions.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance in landscaping.
