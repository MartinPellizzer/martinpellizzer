Paepalanthus kanaii

Paepalanthus kanaii, commonly known as the Kanai's pincushion cactus, is a unique plant native to the arid regions of northern Chile.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny clusters of modified stems that resemble a pincushion, providing protection against herbivores. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the spring, adding a splash of color to its desert habitat. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires minimal water, making it well adapted to harsh, dry environments.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat threats, it is considered a vulnerable species.
