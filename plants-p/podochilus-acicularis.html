Podochilus acicularis

Podochilus acicularis, commonly known as the needle pod orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and small, delicate flowers that typically bloom in clusters. The plant grows in shady, humid environments, often in forest understories or on tree trunks. Its unique morphology and specific habitat requirements make it a subject of interest in botanical studies.

Podochilus acicularis is also valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture.
