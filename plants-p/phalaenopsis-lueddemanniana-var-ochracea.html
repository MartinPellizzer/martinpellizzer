Phalaenopsis lueddemanniana var. ochracea

Phalaenopsis lueddemanniana var. ochracea, commonly known as the Orange Phalaenopsis, is a hybrid orchid prized for its vibrant, long-lasting blooms.

It features showy, waxy flowers in shades of orange and yellow, often with a contrasting throat. The plant typically grows on tree trunks or branches in its native habitat, which includes parts of Southeast Asia. It has a sympodial growth habit, producing new shoots from the base of the plant.

This variety is relatively easy to care for, making it a popular choice for both novice and experienced orchid growers.
