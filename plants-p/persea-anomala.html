Persea anomala

Persea anomala, commonly known as the avocado, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its dark green, glossy leaves and fragrant white flowers. The tree produces a large, pear-shaped fruit that contains a single large seed surrounded by a rich, creamy pulp. The fruit's high fat content, primarily monounsaturated fats, makes it nutritionally significant.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species in both horticulture and global agriculture.
