Polypodium dolichopterum

Polypodium dolichopterum, commonly known as the long-creeping polypody, is a species of fern belonging to the family Polypodiaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that are typically less than 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces small, spore-bearing structures called sori on the underside of its leaves. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Polypodium dolichopterum is primarily epiphytic, growing on tree trunks or rocks rather than in soil.
