Pennisetum dichotomum var. scabrum

Pennisetum dichotomum var. scabrum, commonly known as hairy finger grass, is a perennial grass native to Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and slender, wiry stems that often branch dichotomously. The leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, giving the plant its common name. It produces small, fluffy panicles of flowers that are typically pink to purple in color.

This plant is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
