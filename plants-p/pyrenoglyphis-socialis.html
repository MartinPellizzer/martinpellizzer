Pyrenoglyphis socialis

Pyrenoglyphis socialis, commonly known as the social spore plant, is a unique species characterized by its clustered growth pattern and specialized spore structures.

It belongs to the family Pyrenoglyphidaceae and is notable for its ability to produce highly resilient, social spores that can remain viable for extended periods. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically found in temperate forest understories. Its leaves are broad and dark green, with a waxy surface that aids in moisture retention.

The reproductive structures, known as pyrenocarps, are clustered at the base of the plant and release spores in response to specific environmental stimuli.
