Polygala viridescens var. sanguinea

Polygala viridescens var. sanguinea, commonly known as the red-flowered milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia.

It features slender, branched stems that often grow in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink to reddish in color, giving it its common name. Its leaves are narrow and alternate, often with a slight hairy texture.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
