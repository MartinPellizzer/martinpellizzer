Poa cenisia subsp. fontqueri

Poa cenisia subsp.

fontqueri, commonly known as the Fontqueri bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States. It is characterized by its fine, blue-green foliage and tufted growth habit, which gives it a distinctive appearance.

The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats, such as slopes and open woodlands. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and restoration projects.
