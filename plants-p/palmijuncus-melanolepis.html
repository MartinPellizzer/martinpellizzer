Palmijuncus melanolepis

Palmijuncus melanolepis, commonly known as the black-leaved palm, is a unique plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and dark green, leathery leaves that give it its distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by small, red berries. It is adapted to survive in dry conditions, with deep roots that allow it to access underground water sources.

Due to its striking appearance and ecological significance, it is often found in rocky outcrops and dry savannah habitats.
