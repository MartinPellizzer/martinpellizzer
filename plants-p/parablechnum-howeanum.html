Parablechnum howeanum

Parablechnum howeanum, commonly known as the New Zealand tree fern, is a member of the Cyatheaceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feather-like fronds that can reach up to 3 meters in length. The plant is native to New Zealand and thrives in moist, shaded environments. Its rhizome system supports the growth of the fronds, which are used for both ornamental and traditional purposes.

The plant plays an important ecological role in its native habitat by providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
