Pyrus insignoides

Pyrus insignoides, commonly known as the Japanese whitebeam, is a deciduous tree native to Japan.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and oval-shaped leaves that are dark green on top and pale underneath. The tree produces small, white flowers in spring, which are followed by edible, yellowish-green fruits.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and adaptability to various soil types.
