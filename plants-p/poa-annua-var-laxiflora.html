Poa annua var. laxiflora

Poa annua var. laxiflora, commonly known as the slender annual bluegrass, is a cool-season grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted blades and small, spike-like inflorescences that produce numerous tiny seeds. The plant typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in lawns, meadows, and along roadsides. It has a shallow root system and is known for its rapid growth and ability to establish quickly in open areas.

Due to its adaptability, it is both a common weed in agricultural settings and a valued ornamental grass in some landscaping applications.
