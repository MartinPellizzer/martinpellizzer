Palala laurifolia

Palala laurifolia, commonly known as the mountain hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically range in color from white to pink and red. The plant has glossy, dark green leaves that are often arranged oppositely along the stems. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Palala laurifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its medicinal properties.
