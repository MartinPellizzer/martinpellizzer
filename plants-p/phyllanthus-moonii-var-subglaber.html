Phyllanthus moonii var. subglaber

Phyllanthus moonii var. subglaber, commonly known as the African moonseed, is a member of the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is a small, shrub-like plant native to southern Africa, characterized by its simple, opposite leaves and small yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
