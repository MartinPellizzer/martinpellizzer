Picrodendron arboreum

Picrodendron arboreum, commonly known as the red mangrove, is a tropical tree native to the coastal regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, fibrous roots that help stabilize shorelines and filter water. The plant thrives in brackish or saline environments, making it an important component of mangrove ecosystems. Its small, fragrant flowers are typically white or pale pink, and it produces dark red or black berries.

Picrodendron arboreum plays a crucial role in coastal protection and provides habitat for various marine and terrestrial species.
