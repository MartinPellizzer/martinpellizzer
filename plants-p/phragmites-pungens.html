Phragmites pungens

Phragmites pungens, commonly known as the narrow leaf common reed, is a tall, perennial grass that thrives in wetland environments.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, blade-like leaves that are often covered in a fine, silky hair. This plant plays a vital role in stabilizing soil and filtering water in marshes and along shorelines. It produces dense clusters of small, pale green to brownish spikelets that mature into fluffy seeds.

Phragmites pungens is also known for its ability to form extensive colonies, which can dominate wetland ecosystems.
