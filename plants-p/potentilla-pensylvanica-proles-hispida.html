Potentilla pensylvanica proles hispida

Potentilla pensylvanica proles hispida, commonly known as the hispid potentilla, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a low-growing shrub characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers in clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are typically oval to elliptical in shape and have a rough texture due to the presence of fine hairs.

This species is often found in moist, open habitats such as woodlands and stream banks.
