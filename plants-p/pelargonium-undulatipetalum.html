Pelargonium undulatipetalum

Pelargonium undulatipetalum, commonly known as the undulating petal geranium, is a species native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, undulating petal edges that give it a unique appearance. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub with rounded, velvety leaves. Its flowers are usually pink or red, blooming in summer, and it prefers well-drained soil and full sun.

It is a popular choice for rock gardens and containers due to its ornamental value and hardiness.
