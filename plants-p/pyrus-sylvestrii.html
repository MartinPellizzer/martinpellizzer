Pyrus sylvestrii

Pyrus sylvestrii, commonly known as the wild pear, is a deciduous tree native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and white, fragrant flowers that appear in early spring. The tree produces small, sweet fruits that are typically harvested in late summer. It is often found in woodland areas and thrives in well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value and historical significance, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and conservation efforts.
