Pistacia × hybrida

Pistacia × hybrida, commonly known as the Persian ironwood, is a hybrid species of the genus Pistacia, native to the Middle East and Central Asia.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 15 meters tall, characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and smooth, greyish bark. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by edible, resinous fruits known as pistachios. Its leaves are alternate and pinnately compound, typically consisting of 5–9 leaflets.

Pistacia × hybrida is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also cultivated for its nut production.
