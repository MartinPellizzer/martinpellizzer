Potamogeton crispus f. angustifolius

Potamogeton crispus f. angustifolius, commonly known as narrow-leaved pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments across Europe and parts of Asia.

It has slender, linear leaves that are typically narrower than the standard variety of Potamogeton crispus, giving it a distinct appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are clustered in spike-like inflorescences, which emerge from the water surface. It thrives in slow-moving or still waters with muddy or sandy substrates.

Due to its rapid growth, it can become invasive in certain aquatic ecosystems, altering habitat structure and affecting water quality.
