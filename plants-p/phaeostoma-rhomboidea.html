Phaeostoma rhomboidea

Phaeostoma rhomboidea, commonly known as the rhomboid false hellebore, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features a basal rosette of lanceolate, dark green leaves with a waxy coating. Its flowers are small, greenish-yellow, and clustered in umbels, blooming in early spring.

Phaeostoma rhomboidea is characterized by its toxic properties, containing compounds such as protoanemonin, which can be harmful if ingested.
