Psorodendron fremontii

Psorodendron fremontii, commonly known as the Fremont's psorodendron, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its small, needle-like leaves and produces yellowish-green flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and along canyon walls. Its botanical features include a woody stem with a rough, fissured bark and small, clustered inflorescences.

It is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for desert wildlife.
