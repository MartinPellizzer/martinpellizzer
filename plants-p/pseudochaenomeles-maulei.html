Pseudochaenomeles maulei

Pseudochaenomeles maulei, commonly known as the Maule rose, is a deciduous shrub native to Chile.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its fragrant, pink to white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has narrow, leathery leaves and produces small, red fruits that are edible but not widely consumed.

It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
