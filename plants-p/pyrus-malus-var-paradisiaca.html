Pyrus malus var. paradisiaca

Pyrus malus var. paradisiaca, commonly known as the Persian apple or paradise apple, is a cultivar of the domesticated apple.

It is characterized by its large, sweet, and aromatic fruits with a distinctive red or yellow coloration. The plant is a deciduous tree with alternate, simple, and serrated leaves. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is prized for its culinary use and ornamental value in gardens.
