Pisum sativum var. solovjevae

Pisum sativum var. solovjevae, commonly known as the Solovjeva pea, is a rare botanical variant of the common pea.

It is characterized by its distinct morphological features, including unique pod and seed morphology. This variety is native to specific regions in Eastern Europe and is of interest to botanists and horticulturists. Its genetic makeup offers insights into the diversity within the Pisum genus.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a valuable subject for conservation and research.
