Piper cupreatum var. longum

Piper cupreatum var. longum, commonly known as the long-leaved pepper plant, is a tropical vine native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its long, slender leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces tiny, round berries that are typically green when immature and turn red when ripe. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has mild peppery flavors, making it useful in some culinary applications.

Its growth habit and foliage make it a popular choice for indoor and outdoor gardening.
