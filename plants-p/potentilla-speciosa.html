Potentilla speciosa

Potentilla speciosa, commonly known as the showy cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks. The plant features compound leaves with five leaflets and produces showy, yellow to orange flowers in late spring to early summer. Its flowers are often clustered in inflorescences and attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native plant gardens.
