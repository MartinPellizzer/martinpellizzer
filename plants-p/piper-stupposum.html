Piper stupposum

Piper stupposum, commonly known as the bitter pepper or wild pepper, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other species of pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its aromatic, pungent fruits that are often used in traditional medicine and as a spice. Its leaves are also used in herbal remedies due to their medicinal properties.

The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters.
