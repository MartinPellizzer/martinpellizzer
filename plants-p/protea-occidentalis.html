Protea occidentalis

Protea occidentalis, commonly known as the King Protea, is a striking South African plant belonging to the Proteaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescence composed of numerous petal-like bracts that surround a central cluster of small, tubular flowers. The plant is native to the Western Cape region and thrives in nutrient-poor, sandy soils. Its flowering period typically occurs during the summer months, from November to March.

Protea occidentalis is a popular ornamental plant due to its vibrant colors and long-lasting blooms.
