Plectranthus hosseusii

Plectranthus hosseusii, commonly known as the Cape speckled geranium, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant features square stems and opposite leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its small, tubular flowers are typically purple or blue, arranged in clusters at the ends of the branches.

It is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
