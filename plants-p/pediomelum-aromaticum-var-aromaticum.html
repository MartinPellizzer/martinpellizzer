Pediomelum aromaticum var. aromaticum

Pediomelum aromaticum var. aromaticum, commonly known as sweet everlasting, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces clusters of small, fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant has aromatic leaves that emit a pleasant scent when crushed, contributing to its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is often cultivated in gardens and herbal collections.
