Polygala sibirica subsp. bhutanica

Polygala sibirica subsp.

bhutanica, commonly known as the Bhutanese milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the Himalayan regions of Bhutan and northern India. It belongs to the Polygalaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, clustered flowers.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories and mountainous regions. Its leaves are lanceolate and often have a hairy texture, while its flowers are usually pink or white with a distinctive spurred structure.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
