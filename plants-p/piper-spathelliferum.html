Piper spathelliferum

Piper spathelliferum, commonly known as the white-veined pepper plant, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves with prominent white veins and small, clustered flowers. Its fruits are berries that turn from green to red as they mature.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its aromatic foliage.
