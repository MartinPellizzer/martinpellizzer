Pandanus scabribracteatus

Pandanus scabribracteatus, commonly known as the rough-bract pandanus, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It is a member of the Pandanaceae family and is characterized by its tall, narrow leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically clustered in spike-like inflorescences. Its fibrous roots and sturdy stems make it well adapted to coastal and wetland environments.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appearance and has traditional uses in various cultures.
