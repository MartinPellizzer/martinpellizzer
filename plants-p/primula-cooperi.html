Primula cooperi

Primula cooperi, commonly known as Cooper's primrose, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of central China.

It is characterized by its upright stems and clusters of bell-shaped, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant typically blooms in early spring, adding a splash of color to its natural habitat. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in a fine layer of downy hair, giving them a soft texture.

Primula cooperi is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in cooler climates.
