Pleurothallis acutissima

Pleurothallis acutissima, commonly known as the acute-angled pleurothallis, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its small, delicate flowers and epiphytic growth habit. The plant typically grows on trees or rocks in humid, shaded environments, where it absorbs moisture and nutrients from the air. Its leaves are usually narrow and grass-like, adapted to conserve water in its natural habitat.

The flowers, which are often white or pale pink, are relatively short-lived but attract various pollinators such as bees and moths.
