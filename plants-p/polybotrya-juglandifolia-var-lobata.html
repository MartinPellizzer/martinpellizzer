Polybotrya juglandifolia var. lobata

Polybotrya juglandifolia var. lobata, commonly known as the broad-leafed polybotrya, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often lobed, giving it its varietal name "lobata.". The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by woody, capsule-like fruits. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies of tropical flora.
