Pleurothallis setigera

Pleurothallis setigera, commonly known as the feather-veined orchid, is a species native to the cloud forests of Central and South America.

It is a small, epiphytic orchid that typically grows on trees in humid, shaded environments. The plant features slender, branching stems and produces delicate, greenish-white flowers with intricate veining. Its leaves are usually narrow and arranged in a rosette pattern, aiding in water retention.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is a sought-after species among orchid enthusiasts.
