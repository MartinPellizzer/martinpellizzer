Psychotria xiriricana

Psychotria xiriricana, commonly known as the sacred plant of the Shipibo people, is a member of the Rubiaceae family.

It is native to the Amazon rainforest and is characterized by its dark green, elliptical leaves and small, white flowers. The plant contains psychoactive alkaloids, including dimethyltryptamine (DMT), which contribute to its traditional use in spiritual ceremonies. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Due to its cultural and potential medicinal significance, it is often protected in its natural habitat.
