Pulsatilla magadanensis

Pulsatilla magadanensis, commonly known as the Magadan pasqueflower, is a member of the Ranunculaceae family.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in alpine and subarctic regions. The plant produces showy, cup-shaped flowers that are usually pink or white in color. Its leaves are palmately lobed and covered in a dense layer of silky hairs.

Pulsatilla magadanensis is adapted to cold climates and is often found in rocky or well-drained soils.
