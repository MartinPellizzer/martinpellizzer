Petrophile pedunculata

Petrophile pedunculata, commonly known as the slender rock heath, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit and is typically found in rocky or sandy soils. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers clustered in dense inflorescences, which bloom during the spring and summer seasons. Its foliage consists of narrow, linear leaves that are adapted to conserve water in its arid or semi-arid habitat.

Petrophile pedunculata is often used in native garden landscapes due to its ornamental value and resilience to drought.
