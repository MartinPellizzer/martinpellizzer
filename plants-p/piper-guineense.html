Piper guineense

Piper guineense, commonly known as African pepper or guinea pepper, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves and small, greenish-white flowers. Its fruit is a small, yellowish berry that contains several seeds.

Piper guineense is often cultivated for its aromatic leaves and traditional medicinal uses.
