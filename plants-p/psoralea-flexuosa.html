Psoralea flexuosa

Psoralea flexuosa, commonly known as the zigzag psoralea, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive zigzagging stem. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in dense clusters, which are often visited by pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are compound, with multiple pairs of small, oval-shaped leaflets.

Psoralea flexuosa is typically found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.
