Phyteuma persicifolium var. alpestre

Phyteuma persicifolium var. alpestre, commonly known as the alpine phyteuma, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pale yellow to orange, often with a distinctive red or purple throat. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at elevations above 1,000 meters. Its leaves are lance-shaped and slightly hairy, adapting to the harsh alpine environment.

It is valued in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained, sunny conditions.
