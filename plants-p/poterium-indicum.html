Poterium indicum

Poterium indicum, commonly known as the Indian chervil or wild cumin, is an annual herb native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes plants like parsley and carrots, and is characterized by its finely divided, feathery leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in umbels, typical of the family. Its seeds are often used in traditional medicine and as a spice in some cuisines.

Poterium indicum thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fields.
