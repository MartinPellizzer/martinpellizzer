Passiflora mixta var. bicoronata

Passiflora mixta var. bicoronata, commonly known as the bicoronate passionflower, is a tropical climbing vine native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, complex flowers with five sepals, five petals, and a crown-like structure formed by its filaments. The plant produces edible, tropical berries that are often used in culinary and medicinal applications. Its leaves are large, heart-shaped, and have a velvety texture.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
