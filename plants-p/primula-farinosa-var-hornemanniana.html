Primula farinosa var. hornemanniana

Primula farinosa var. hornemanniana, commonly known as the hornemannian primrose, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has hairy, glandular leaves that are often covered in a fine, powdery coating, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained, alkaline soils and prefers partial to full shade in its natural habitat.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens and alpine settings.
