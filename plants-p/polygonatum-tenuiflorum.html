Polygonatum tenuiflorum

Polygonatum tenuiflorum, commonly known as threadleaf Solomon's seal, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern Asia.

It features slender, arching stems that bear opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a distinctively narrow, thread-like appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in late spring, which are clustered in umbels. Its rhizomatous root system allows it to spread and form dense colonies in woodland habitats.

It is valued in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits and is also cultivated for ornamental purposes.
