Pedicellia

Pedicellia, commonly known as the pedicel, is a slender stalk that supports a single flower in many flowering plants.

It serves as a structure that elevates the flower above the leaf axil, facilitating pollination by making the flower more accessible to pollinators. The pedicel often contains vascular tissues that transport water and nutrients to the flower. In some species, the pedicel may also be modified to aid in seed dispersal.

Its presence and structure can vary significantly depending on the plant family and ecological adaptations.
