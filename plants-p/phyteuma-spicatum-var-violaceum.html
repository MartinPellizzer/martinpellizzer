Phyteuma spicatum var. violaceum

Phyteuma spicatum var. violaceum, commonly known as the purple spike gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central and Southern Europe.

It features tall, slender stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped flowers, typically ranging in color from violet to deep purple. The plant is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained, sandy or rocky soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This species is valued in gardens for its ornamental value and is often used in rock gardens or as a border plant.
