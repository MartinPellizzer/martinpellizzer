Panicum inconspicuum

Panicum inconspicuum, commonly known as the inconspicuous panicgrass, is a low-growing perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It typically reaches a height of 10 to 30 centimeters and forms dense, mat-like growths. Its foliage is narrow and grass-like, with small, delicate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces feathery, light green to brownish seed heads during the summer months.

It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides.
