Piptadenia leptostachya

Piptadenia leptostachya, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or *pau-rosa*, is a tropical leguminous tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically pinnate with several pairs of elliptic leaflets. The plant produces showy, fragrant yellow flowers arranged in long, drooping racemes. Its fruit is a pod that splits open to release seeds, and the tree is often cultivated for its ornamental value and timber.

Piptadenia leptostachya also has traditional uses in herbal medicine.
