Pimpinella magna var. rubens

Pimpinella magna var. rubens, commonly known as the red angelica, is a biennial or perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features finely divided, feathery leaves that are often aromatic and can be used in herbal remedies. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers arranged in umbrella-like clusters, typical of the Apiaceae family. Its roots are thick and fleshy, often harvested for medicinal purposes.

The variety 'rubens' is distinguished by its reddish stems and leaves, adding to its ornamental value.
