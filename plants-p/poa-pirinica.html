Poa pirinica

Poa pirinica, commonly known as the Pyrenean bentgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its low-growing habit, with slender, wiry stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Poa pirinica thrives in cool, moist environments, often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes.

Its botanical features make it an important component of high-altitude ecosystems.
