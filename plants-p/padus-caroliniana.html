Padus caroliniana

Padus caroliniana, commonly known as the Carolina hornbeam, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It has smooth, gray bark that becomes fissured with age, and its leaves are alternate, simple, and elliptic with a pointed tip. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, followed by small, red berries that are edible for birds.

It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in swamps, stream banks, and wetlands.
