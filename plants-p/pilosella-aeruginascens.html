Pilosella aeruginascens

Pilosella aeruginascens, commonly known as the blueweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its blue or bluish-green flowers and is native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its leaves are usually hairy and lance-shaped, while the stems are often erect and branched.

Pilosella aeruginascens is known for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
