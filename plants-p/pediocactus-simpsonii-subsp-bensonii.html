Pediocactus simpsonii subsp. bensonii

Pediocactus simpsonii subsp.

bensonii, commonly known as Benson's prickly pear, is a succulent cactus native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It features a short, spiny stem that often branches into multiple offsets, and its leaves are modified into spines.

The plant produces yellow or orange flowers during the spring and summer months. It has a compact growth habit and is well adapted to arid environments.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
