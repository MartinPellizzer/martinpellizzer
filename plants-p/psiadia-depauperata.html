Psiadia depauperata

Psiadia depauperata, commonly known as the poorflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing annual herb with small, inconspicuous yellow flowers. The plant has narrow, linear leaves and produces small, dry fruits called capsules. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, often found in rocky or arid environments.

Psiadia depauperata is noted for its simple morphology and ecological adaptability.
