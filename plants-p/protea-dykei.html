Protea dykei

Protea dykei, commonly known as the Dyke's protea, is a striking South African flowering plant belonging to the Proteaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescence composed of numerous white to pinkish flowers arranged in a dense, rounded head. The plant is native to the Eastern Cape province and thrives in sandy, well-drained soils. Its floral structure includes a central stem with bracts that resemble petals, and it blooms during the summer months.

Protea dykei is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in local ecosystems by providing nectar for pollinators.
