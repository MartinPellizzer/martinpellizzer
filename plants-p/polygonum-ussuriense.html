Polygonum ussuriense

Polygonum ussuriense, commonly known as the Ussuri knotweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, characterized by its distinctive nodes and hollow stems. The plant typically grows in moist environments such as riverbanks and wet meadows. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often have a reddish tint, while the flowers are small and clustered in dense inflorescences.

Polygonum ussuriense is known for its robust growth and adaptability to various soil conditions.
