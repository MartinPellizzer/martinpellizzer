Pectidium punctatum

Pectidium punctatum, commonly known as the spotted pectis, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is native to regions of South America, particularly Brazil, and thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. The plant features narrow, linear leaves and produces small, yellow flowers arranged in dense clusters. Its scientific name reflects the presence of distinct markings on its foliage.

Pectidium punctatum is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its delicate appearance and adaptability to various growing conditions.
