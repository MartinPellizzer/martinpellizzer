Protea argyrea

Protea argyrea, commonly known as the silver protea, is a striking South African plant belonging to the Proteaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescences that resemble a cluster of flowers, with a central white or pale yellow bloom surrounded by pink to red bracts. The plant typically grows in coastal regions, where it thrives in well-drained, acidic soils. Its flowers are pollinated by birds, particularly sunbirds, which are attracted to its nectar.

Protea argyrea is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique and vibrant appearance.
