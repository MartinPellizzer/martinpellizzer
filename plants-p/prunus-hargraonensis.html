Prunus hargraonensis

Prunus hargraonensis, commonly known as the Hargray's cherry, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to China and is characterized by its small, oval leaves and clusters of white or pale pink flowers. The plant produces small, edible fruits that are similar to cherries but are less commonly consumed by humans. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its attractive spring blossoms.

Prunus hargraonensis is also valued in traditional Chinese medicine for its potential health benefits.
