Prunus × donarium f. benden

Prunus × donarium f. benden, commonly known as the Japanese apricot, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its ornamental value, featuring showy, single or double pink to white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows as a small tree or shrub, with ovate to elliptical leaves that are glossy green on top and pale underneath. Its fruit is a small, yellow stone fruit that ripens in late summer, though it is not typically eaten due to its tart flavor.

The plant is valued for its aesthetic appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its spring blossoms.
