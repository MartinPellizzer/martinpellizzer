Pisum umbellatum

Pisum umbellatum, commonly known as the clustered pea or umbrella pea, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and clusters of small, pea-like flowers that bloom in shades of white or pale purple. The plant produces lens-shaped pods containing several seeds, which are typically harvested when mature. Pisum umbellatum is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and flowering display.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a versatile choice for gardens and landscapes.
