Paulownia thyrsoidea

Paulownia thyrsoidea, commonly known as the emperor paulownia, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It is characterized by its rapid growth, reaching heights of up to 20 meters, and its heart-shaped leaves that are large and dark green. The tree produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in clusters, typically in early spring before the leaves emerge. Its wood is prized for its lightweight and strength, making it valuable for furniture and musical instruments.

The plant is also noted for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its resilience to environmental stress.
