Polyalthia beamaniorum

Polyalthia beamaniorum, commonly known as the Beaman's flame tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the western Ghats of India.

It is characterized by its striking, clustered inflorescences that produce bright red or orange flowers, which are highly attractive to pollinators. The plant has compound leaves with alternate, pinnate arrangement, and its bark is smooth and pale in color. It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often found in moist forest areas.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and as a shade tree in tropical regions.
