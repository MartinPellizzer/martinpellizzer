Pterocarpus scandens

Pterocarpus scandens, commonly known as the climbing shea tree, is a leguminous plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its woody, climbing stem and pinnately compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by flat, pod-like fruits containing small, hard seeds. It is valued for its durable wood and is often used in traditional medicine.

Its growth habit and ecological role make it an important species in its native habitats.
