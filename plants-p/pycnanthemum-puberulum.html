Pycnanthemum puberulum

Pycnanthemum puberulum, commonly known as roughhead goldenrod, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a member of the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its erect, herbaceous stems and clusters of small, yellow to golden-orange flowers. The plant typically grows in moist to wet soils in open woodlands and prairies. Its leaves are lance-shaped, hairy, and often have a strong, aromatic scent.

Pycnanthemum puberulum is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
