Prunus sargentii var. intermedia

Prunus sargentii var. intermedia, commonly known as the Sargent cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and showy clusters of white or pink flowers in spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans. Its leaves are ovate to elliptical, with serrated edges, and turn yellow in the fall.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping for its seasonal interest and hardiness.
