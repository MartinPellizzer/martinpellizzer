Persicaria lonchophylla

Persicaria lonchophylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaf knotweed, is a perennial herb native to Asia.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive waxy coating, and its flowers are small, clustered in dense spikes. The plant typically grows in moist environments such as wet meadows and stream banks. It has a rhizomatous root system that allows it to spread vegetatively.

Its botanical characteristics make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural study.
