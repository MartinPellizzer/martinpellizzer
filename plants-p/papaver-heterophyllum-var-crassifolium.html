Papaver heterophyllum var. crassifolium

Papaver heterophyllum var. crassifolium, commonly known as the broad-leaved poppy, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive, broad, lance-shaped leaves and showy, red to orange-colored flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in dry, open habitats. It belongs to the family Papaveraceae and is known for its ornamental value in gardens.

The flowers are followed by seed pods that split open to release the seeds, aiding in its propagation.
