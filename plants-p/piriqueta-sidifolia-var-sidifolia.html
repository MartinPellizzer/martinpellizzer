Piriqueta sidifolia var. sidifolia

Piriqueta sidifolia var. sidifolia, commonly known as the rock spurge, is a perennial flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy stems and leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in drought conditions. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring, adding a subtle ornamental value to its rocky habitat. It prefers well-drained soils and full sun exposure, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Due to its hardy nature, it is often used in landscaping to stabilize soil and prevent erosion in dry environments.
