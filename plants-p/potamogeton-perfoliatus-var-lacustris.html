Potamogeton perfoliatus var. lacustris

Potamogeton perfoliatus var. lacustris, commonly known as the lake pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments.

It has long, slender stems that often branch and bear small, oval-shaped leaves arranged in whorls. The plant produces tiny, greenish flowers that are typically clustered in spike-like inflorescences. It thrives in shallow, slow-moving waters and plays a vital role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.

Its presence is often an indicator of good water quality in lakes and ponds.
