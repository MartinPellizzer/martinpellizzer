Pleurothallis arctata

Pleurothallis arctata, commonly known as the snow orchid, is a species of orchid native to the cloud forests of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its small, intricate flowers that often resemble miniature orchids. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its leaves are usually narrow and evergreen, and it produces fragrant blooms during the cooler months.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is a subject of interest for botanists and orchid enthusiasts alike.
