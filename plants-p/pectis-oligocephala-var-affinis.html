Pectis oligocephala var. affinis

Pectis oligocephala var. affinis, commonly known as the narrow-headed pectis, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous tiny florets. The plant produces showy yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its linear, grass-like leaves are adapted to minimize water loss in its harsh environment.

The species is often found in rocky outcrops and sandy soils, showcasing its resilience to drought and heat.
