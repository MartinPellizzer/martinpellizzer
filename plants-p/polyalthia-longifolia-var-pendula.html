Polyalthia longifolia var. pendula

Polyalthia longifolia var. pendula, commonly known as the weeping flame of the forest, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its drooping, pendulous inflorescences that give it a distinctive, ornamental appearance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by elongated, woody fruits. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often cultivated for its aesthetic value in gardens and landscapes.

The leaves are alternate, simple, and ovate, with a glossy surface that enhances its visual appeal.
