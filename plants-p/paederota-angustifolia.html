Paederota angustifolia

Paederota angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It features small, clustered leaves that are typically narrow and lance-shaped, adapted to reduce water loss in harsh environments. The plant produces tiny, white or pinkish flowers arranged in dense clusters, which bloom in early spring. Its root system is fibrous and shallow, allowing it to thrive in well-drained soils.

Paederota angustifolia plays a role in stabilizing sandy soils and providing habitat for small invertebrates.
