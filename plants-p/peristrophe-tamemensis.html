Peristrophe tamemensis

Peristrophe tamemensis, commonly known as the African feather grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and delicate, feathery panicles that give it a soft, airy appearance. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky outcrops. Its foliage is narrow and grass-like, with small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets.

Peristrophe tamemensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping for its elegant, wind-swaying foliage.
