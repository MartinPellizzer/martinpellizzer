Paspalum riparium

Paspalum riparium, commonly known as river paspalum, is a perennial grass species native to the wetlands and riverbanks of South America.

It is characterized by its clumping growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typically green or reddish in color. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in marshy areas or along the edges of water bodies.

Paspalum riparium is valued for its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
