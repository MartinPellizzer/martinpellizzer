Pimelea declivis

Pimelea declivis, commonly known as the mountain banksia, is a shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves and distinctive yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils, often in coastal or mountainous regions. It is a member of the Proteaceae family, which includes many other flowering plants with showy blooms.

Pimelea declivis is well adapted to dry conditions and is often found in open vegetation types.
