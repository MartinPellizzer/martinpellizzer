Potentilla fruticosa f. gynodynamica

Potentilla fruticosa f. gynodynamica, commonly known as the flowering cinquefoil, is a shrub characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and vibrant yellow flowers.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is native to various regions in Europe and Asia. The plant features small, oval leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or rocky habitats.

The cultivar is valued for its ornamental value and resilience in challenging environments.
