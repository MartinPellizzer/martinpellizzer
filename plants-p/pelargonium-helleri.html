Pelargonium helleri

Pelargonium helleri, commonly known as the ivy-leaved geranium, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and heart-shaped, serrated leaves that resemble ivy leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, pink or red flowers throughout the growing season. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Pelargonium helleri is popular in gardens and as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and ease of care.
