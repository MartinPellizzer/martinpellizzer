Polystichum decoratum subsp. habanense

Polystichum decoratum subsp.

habanense, commonly known as the Habanero fern, is a species of fern native to the tropical regions of the Americas. It belongs to the family Dryopteridaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, lance-shaped fronds with a dark green color and a metallic sheen.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky crevices. Its fronds are bipinnate, with small, closely spaced pinnae that give it a dense, feathery appearance.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
