Pitcairnia schiedeana

Pitcairnia schiedeana, commonly known as the Schiede's pitcairnia, is a species of bromeliad native to the Andes of South America.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that form a central tank to collect rainwater. The plant produces striking inflorescences with colorful, showy flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds. It thrives in shaded, humid environments and is often found in cloud forests.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for both horticultural collections and botanical research.
