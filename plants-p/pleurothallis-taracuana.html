Pleurothallis taracuana

Pleurothallis taracuana, commonly known as the Taracuana orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a small, epiphytic orchid that typically grows on trees in humid, shaded environments. The plant features delicate, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and yellow. It is noted for its unique floral structure and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in the wild.
