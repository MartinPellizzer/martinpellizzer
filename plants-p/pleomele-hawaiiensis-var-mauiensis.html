Pleomele hawaiiensis var. mauiensis

Pleomele hawaiiensis var. mauiensis, commonly known as Maui's bamboo, is a unique variant of the pleomele plant native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of narrow, grass-like leaves that give it a bamboo-like appearance. This plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in the understory of tropical rainforests. Its growth habit makes it an important component of Hawaii's native vegetation.

However, it is considered rare and is often threatened by habitat loss and invasive species.
