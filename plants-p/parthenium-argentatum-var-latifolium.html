Parthenium argentatum var. latifolium

Parthenium argentatum var. latifolium, commonly known as the greater white coneflower, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States.

It is characterized by its broad, silver-white leaves and showy yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant is often found in prairies and open woodlands, thriving in well-drained soils. It is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
