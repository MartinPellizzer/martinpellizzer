Podocarpus elatus

Podocarpus elatus, commonly known as the Christmas pine, is a species of coniferous evergreen tree native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, glossy green foliage and distinctive, cone-like structures that resemble pine cones. The plant has a slow growth rate and can reach heights of up to 20 meters in its natural habitat. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value.

The tree plays an important ecological role in providing habitat and stabilizing soils in native forests.
