Pleopeltis brownii

Pleopeltis brownii, commonly known as the Brown's rock fern, is a species of epiphytic fern native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its dense, clustered growth habit on trees or rocks. The plant has lance-shaped, leathery leaves that are typically dark green and covered in a fine, hairy layer. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological niche, it is a popular specimen in botanical gardens and conservation efforts.
