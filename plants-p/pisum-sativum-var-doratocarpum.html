Pisum sativum var. doratocarpum

Pisum sativum var. doratocarpum, commonly known as the purple-seed pea, is a botanical variant of the common pea.

It is characterized by its distinctive purplish seed coat, which sets it apart from the typical green-seeded varieties. The plant belongs to the Fabaceae family and is cultivated for both ornamental and culinary purposes. It exhibits a climbing habit, with tendrils that help it ascend supports.

Its flowers are usually white or pale blue, and it produces pods containing several seeds.
