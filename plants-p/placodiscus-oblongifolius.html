Placodiscus oblongifolius

Placodiscus oblongifolius, commonly known as the African hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically have five to seven petals and are often brightly colored. The leaves are broad, ovate, and have a smooth or slightly serrated edge. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The plant is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
