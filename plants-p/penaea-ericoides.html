Penaea ericoides

Penaea ericoides, commonly known as the heath-like plant, is a species of flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its small, leathery leaves. The plant produces clusters of white or pink flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Penaea ericoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
