Phaius berneysii

Phaius berneysii, commonly known as the Berneys' ginger, is a striking tropical orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and large, showy flowers that bloom in shades of pink and white. The plant features broad, strap-like leaves that are often tinged with red or purple at the edges. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Phaius berneysii is valued in horticulture for its ornamental beauty and unique floral structure.
