Pachygenium viride

Pachygenium viride, commonly known as the green thickstem, is a unique plant characterized by its dense, fibrous stem and waxy leaves.

It is native to the arid regions of southern Africa, where it has adapted to thrive in harsh, dry conditions. The plant's leaves are typically small and clustered near the base, reducing water loss through transpiration. Its fibrous stem provides structural support and stores moisture, allowing it to survive prolonged droughts.

Pachygenium viride is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
