Potamogeton proteus zizii

Potamogeton proteus zizii, commonly known as the Zizii pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater ecosystems in Europe and parts of Asia.

It has long, narrow leaves that are typically arranged in whorls around the stem, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often submerged. The plant is characterized by its ability to thrive in shallow, slow-moving waters, where it forms dense underwater vegetation. It plays an important role in aquatic habitats by providing shelter for fish and invertebrates.

However, it can become invasive in certain regions, outcompeting native species and altering water quality.
