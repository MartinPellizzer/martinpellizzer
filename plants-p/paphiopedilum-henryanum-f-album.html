Paphiopedilum henryanum f. album

Paphiopedilum henryanum f. album, commonly known as the White Lady's Slipper, is a rare and striking orchid native to the mountainous regions of China.

It is characterized by its large, showy white flowers with a distinctive slipper-shaped pouch, which is a unique feature of the Paphiopedilum genus. The plant has a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, allowing it to thrive in its natural habitat. Its foliage consists of dark green, strap-like leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern.

This orchid is highly valued in horticulture for its beauty and rarity, making it a sought-after species among plant enthusiasts.
