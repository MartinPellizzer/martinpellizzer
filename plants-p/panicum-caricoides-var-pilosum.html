Panicum caricoides var. pilosum

Panicum caricoides var. pilosum, commonly known as silky grass or silky panicum, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and feathery, silky-haired inflorescences that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments, such as marshes and along stream banks. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green color, and it produces small, wind-pollinated flowers.

This species is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for wildlife.
