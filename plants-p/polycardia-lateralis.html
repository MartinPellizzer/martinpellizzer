Polycardia lateralis

Polycardia lateralis, commonly known as the side-leaf polycardia, is a unique plant species characterized by its distinctive foliage.

It features elongated, lance-shaped leaves that grow in a staggered pattern along its stems. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. It is native to specific regions in Southeast Asia and thrives in humid, shaded environments.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
