Paspalum compressicaule

Paspalum compressicaule, commonly known as narrowleaf grass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, compressed stem and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in wetland environments, such as marshes and swamps, where it thrives in moist to water-saturated soils. Its flowering inflorescence consists of spikelets that are often concealed within leaf sheaths, making it less conspicuous.

Paspalum compressicaule plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wetland organisms.
