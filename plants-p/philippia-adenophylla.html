Philippia adenophylla

Philippia adenophylla, commonly known as the velvetleaf sandmat, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its hairy, fleshy stems and leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its succulent leaves and stems store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments.

Philippia adenophylla is often found in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to full sun conditions.
