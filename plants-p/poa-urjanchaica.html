Poa urjanchaica

Poa urjanchaica, commonly known as the Mongolian bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the cold climates of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its fine, blue-green foliage and tufted growth habit, which allows it to thrive in harsh, alpine environments. The plant typically grows in open, rocky, or sandy soils and is adapted to low temperatures and limited moisture. Its rhizomatous root system helps it stabilize soil and prevent erosion in its natural habitat.

Poa urjanchaica plays an important ecological role in maintaining the biodiversity of its native grassland ecosystems.
