Planchonella euphlebia

Planchonella euphlebia, commonly known as the broad-leaved sandarac tree, is a tropical plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree that typically grows in coastal or lowland forest areas. The plant has simple, elliptical leaves that are usually dark green and glossy. Its small, fragrant flowers are followed by woody capsules that contain the seeds.

The tree is valued for its aromatic resin, which has been traditionally used in various cultural and medicinal practices.
