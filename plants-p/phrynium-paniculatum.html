Phrynium paniculatum

Phrynium paniculatum, commonly known as the broadleaf arrowroot, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters. The plant features large, heart-shaped leaves that are often variegated with white and green patterns. Its inflorescence consists of clusters of small, greenish-white flowers arranged in panicles.

The plant is known for its ability to thrive in moist, well-drained soils and is often used in ornamental gardening.
