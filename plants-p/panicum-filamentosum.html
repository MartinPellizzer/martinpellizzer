Panicum filamentosum

Panicum filamentosum, commonly known as silky panicgrass, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its delicate, thread-like foliage and produces fluffy, seed-bearing spikes during the summer months. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as wetlands and along stream banks. It has a shallow root system and is often used in restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and wetland conservation.
