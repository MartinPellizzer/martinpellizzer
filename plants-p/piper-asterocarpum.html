Piper asterocarpum

Piper asterocarpum, commonly known as the star-seed pepper, is a tropical climbing vine native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many pepper species. The plant is characterized by its small, aromatic berries that are often used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a slightly rough texture.

The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is valued for its medicinal properties.
