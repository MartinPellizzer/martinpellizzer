Pithecellobium vaillantii

Pithecellobium vaillantii, commonly known as the yellow velvet tamarind, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant produces small, edible pods that contain seeds surrounded by a fibrous pulp. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has some traditional uses in local medicine.

The species is also valued for its ability to thrive in poor soils, making it useful for land rehabilitation.
