Pandanus copelandii var. panchoi

Pandanus copelandii var. panchoi, commonly known as the Panchoi pandanus, is a variant of the pandanus genus native to tropical regions of the Pacific.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and long, narrow leaves that often have a waxy texture. The plant produces large, spadix-like inflorescences that are typically yellow or orange in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires ample sunlight to grow optimally.

This species is often used in traditional crafts and as a ornamental plant due to its distinctive appearance.
