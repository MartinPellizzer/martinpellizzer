Phanerophlebia aurita

Phanerophlebia aurita, commonly known as the golden-leafed phanerophlebia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking golden-yellow leaves and small, fragrant white flowers. The plant typically grows in tropical rainforests, thriving in humid and shaded environments. Its leaves are thick and waxy, which helps it retain moisture in its native habitat.

Phanerophlebia aurita is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
