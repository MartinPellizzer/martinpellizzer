Polygala bennae

Polygala bennae, commonly known as Benn's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces yellowish-green blooms that are typically arranged in loose inflorescences. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky or sandy soils.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
