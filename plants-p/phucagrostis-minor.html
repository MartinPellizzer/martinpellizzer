Phucagrostis minor

Phucagrostis minor, commonly known as the lesser phragmites, is a perennial grass species native to wetland environments.

It typically grows in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds and lakes. The plant has tall, slender culms that are often densely clustered, forming dense stands. Its leaves are narrow and blade-like, with a distinct midrib and a fibrous sheath at the base.

Phucagrostis minor plays an important ecological role by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wetland organisms.
