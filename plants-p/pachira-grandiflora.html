Pachira grandiflora

Pachira grandiflora, commonly known as the giant water hyacinth, is a herbaceous aquatic plant native to South America.

It features broad, heart-shaped leaves that float on the water's surface, often with a waxy coating to reduce water loss. The plant produces clusters of showy, violet-blue flowers that bloom above the water, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its roots are submerged, absorbing nutrients from the water, which can sometimes lead to ecological imbalances if it proliferates unchecked.

Pachira grandiflora is often cultivated in ponds and water gardens for its ornamental value and ability to purify water.
