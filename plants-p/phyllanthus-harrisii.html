Phyllanthus harrisii

Phyllanthus harrisii, commonly known as the African spurge, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its small, clustered flowers and smooth, often reddish stems. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a waxy coating.
