Phyllanthus weinlandii

Phyllanthus weinlandii, commonly known as the Mexican dwarf date palm, is a small evergreen shrub native to Mexico and Central America.

It features compound leaves with multiple pairs of lance-shaped leaflets, giving it a distinctive, palm-like appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by red, fleshy fruits resembling dates. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for cultivation in warm, arid climates.

Phyllanthus weinlandii is often used in ornamental gardening due to its compact size and attractive foliage.
