Poa insignis

Poa insignis, commonly known as the alpine bluegrass, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its fine, grass-like leaves and small, blue-green flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes. Poa insignis is adapted to high-altitude conditions, with a compact growth habit that helps it withstand strong winds and freezing temperatures.

Its ecological importance lies in its role as a food source for grazing animals and its contribution to soil stabilization in its habitat.
