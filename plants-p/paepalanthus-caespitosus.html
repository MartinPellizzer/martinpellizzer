Paepalanthus caespitosus

Paepalanthus caespitosus, commonly known as the spiny pincushion cactus, is a unique member of the Cactaceae family native to South America.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny clusters of stems that form a cushion-like structure. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the warmer months, which are typically followed by red, fleshy fruits. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky or sandy soils.

Due to its striking appearance and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant.
