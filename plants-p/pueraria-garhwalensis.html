Pueraria garhwalensis

Pueraria garhwalensis, commonly known as the Garhwal Kud, is a climbing legume native to the Himalayan regions of India.

It features pinnately compound leaves with an odd number of leaflets and produces purple or violet pea-like flowers. The plant is characterized by its extensive root system and the presence of tubers, which are used in traditional medicine. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forested areas.

Its botanical significance lies in its potential medicinal properties and ecological role in the region.
