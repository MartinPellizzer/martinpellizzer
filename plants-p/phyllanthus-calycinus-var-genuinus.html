Phyllanthus calycinus var. genuinus

Phyllanthus calycinus var. genuinus, commonly known as the Indian gooseberry or amla, is a shrub native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is characterized by its small, glossy, elliptical leaves and yellowish-green flowers that appear in clusters. The plant produces small, round fruits that turn from green to orange-red when mature. The fruit is rich in vitamin C and contains various bioactive compounds.

It is widely cultivated for its medicinal and nutritional properties.
