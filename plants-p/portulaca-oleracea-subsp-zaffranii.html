Portulaca oleracea subsp. zaffranii

Portulaca oleracea subsp.

zaffranii, commonly known as the golden purslane, is a succulent plant native to warm temperate and tropical regions. It features fleshy, thick leaves and vibrant yellow flowers that bloom throughout the growing season.

The plant is characterized by its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions and withstand drought, making it a hardy species. Its flowers are often used in ornamental gardening, and the leaves are edible, though less commonly consumed than other portulaca varieties.

This subspecies is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal remedies.
