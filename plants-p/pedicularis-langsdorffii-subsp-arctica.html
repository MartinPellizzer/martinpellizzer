Pedicularis langsdorffii subsp. arctica

Pedicularis langsdorffii subsp.

arctica, commonly known as the Arctic lousewort, is a flowering plant native to Arctic and subarctic regions. It is characterized by its erect, hairy stems and small, spurred flowers that range in color from yellow to red.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in cold, high-altitude environments. Its flowers are adapted for pollination by insects, and it is often found in tundra and alpine habitats.

This subspecies is notable for its resilience to harsh climatic conditions.
