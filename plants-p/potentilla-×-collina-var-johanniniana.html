Potentilla × collina var. johanniniana

Potentilla × collina var. johanniniana, commonly known as the Johanniniana cinquefoil, is a hybrid shrub native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its dense, rounded growth habit and produces clusters of small, yellow flowers in early summer. The plant features compound leaves with five to seven leaflets, which are typically gray-green in color and covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine settings.

This variety is valued for its hardiness and ornamental appeal in horticultural landscapes.
