Piper reitzii

Piper reitzii, commonly known as the "ghost pepper" or "naga jolokia," is a climbing vine native to India and Nepal.

It belongs to the Piper genus in the family Piperaceae, which includes many species of pepper plants. The plant produces small, green, berry-like fruits that mature to a bright red color. These fruits are known for their extreme heat, with a Scoville heat unit rating that can exceed 1 million.

The botanical structure of Piper reitzii includes simple, alternate leaves and a complex reproductive system with both male and female flowers.
