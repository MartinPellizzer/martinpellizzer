Pastinaca lasiocarpa

Pastinaca lasiocarpa, commonly known as the hairy bur marigold, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its yellow-orange flowers and hairy, lobed leaves. The plant typically grows in wetlands and along the edges of water bodies. Its seeds are enclosed in a spiky, bur-like structure that aids in seed dispersal.

Botanically, it belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.
