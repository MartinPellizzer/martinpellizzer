Plectranthus micranthus

Plectranthus micranthus, commonly known as the narrow-leaf plectranthus, is a herbaceous perennial native to South Africa.

It features opposite, narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine pubescence. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically purple or violet in color, arranged in whorled clusters. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a versatile choice for gardens.

Due to its ornamental value and ease of care, it is popular among gardeners and horticulturists.
