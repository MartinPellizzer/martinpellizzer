Penstemon strictus subsp. strictoformis

Penstemon strictus subsp.

strictoformis, commonly known as the rigid penstemon, is a perennial herb native to the western United States. It is characterized by its upright, sparsely branched stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its botanical features make it an important species for pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds.
