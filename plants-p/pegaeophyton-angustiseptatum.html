Pegaeophyton angustiseptatum

Pegaeophyton angustiseptatum, commonly known as the narrow-septate marine alga, is a fossilized organism from the Silurian period.

It is characterized by its complex thallus structure composed of multiple filaments. The plant exhibits a distinctive septate morphology, with narrow, branching cells arranged in a reticulate pattern. It is considered an early example of a photosynthetic organism adapted to marine environments.

Its study provides insights into the evolution of early plant life and early ecosystems.
