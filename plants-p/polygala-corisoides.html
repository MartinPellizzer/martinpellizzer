Polygala corisoides

Polygala corisoides, commonly known as the narrow-leaved milkweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Polygalaceae family and typically grows in sandy or rocky soils. The plant features slender, erect stems and produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters. It is often found in coastal and inland regions with a Mediterranean climate.

Its ecological importance includes providing nectar for pollinators and serving as a host plant for certain butterfly species.
