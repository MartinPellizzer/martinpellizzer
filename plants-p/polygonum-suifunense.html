Polygonum suifunense

Polygonum suifunense, commonly known as the Japanese knotweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family and is characterized by its stout, bamboo-like stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters during the summer months. It is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types.

However, it is also considered an invasive species in many regions outside its native range.
