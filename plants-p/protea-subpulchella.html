Protea subpulchella

Protea subpulchella, commonly known as the silver protea, is a striking South African plant belonging to the Proteaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescences that resemble a cluster of white flowers, although they are actually composed of many small, individual florets. The plant thrives in the fynbos biome, where it adapts to nutrient-poor soils and Mediterranean climates. Its leaves are typically narrow and leathery, providing structural support and reducing water loss.

The protea subpulchella is also notable for its role in attracting pollinators such as birds and insects.
