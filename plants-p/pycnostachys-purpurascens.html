Pycnostachys purpurascens

Pycnostachys purpurascens, commonly known as the purple-flowered pycnostachys, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, elongated inflorescences that bear clusters of vibrant purple and pink flowers. The plant thrives in humid, warm environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot. Its foliage consists of broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often variegated with white or silver markings.

This species is popular in ornamental horticulture due to its showy appearance and ability to add color to shaded gardens.
