Pimelea prostrata f. parvifolia

Pimelea prostrata f. parvifolia, commonly known as the small-flowered prostrate pimelea, is a low-growing shrub native to southern Australia.

It features small, narrow leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive texture. The plant produces clusters of tiny, yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting various pollinators. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in heathlands and coastal areas.

Due to its compact growth habit and ornamental value, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscaping.
