Penstemon secundiflorus var. secundiflorus

Penstemon secundiflorus var. secundiflorus, commonly known as the narrowleaf beardtongue, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils in open habitats such as meadows and slopes. The plant features upright stems with opposite, narrow, lance-shaped leaves. Its showy, tubular flowers are usually pink to purple, with a distinctive blue or violet throat, attracting pollinators like bees and hummingbirds.

It is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
