Phaseolus peduncularis var. parabolica

Phaseolus peduncularis var. parabolica, commonly known as the parabolica bean, is a climbing legume native to the Americas.

It produces small, pod-like fruits that contain edible seeds, similar to other Phaseolus species. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and bears white or pale pink flowers. It thrives in warm, tropical climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential as a food crop.
