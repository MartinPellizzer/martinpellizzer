Polygonatum pubescens subvar. australe

Polygonatum pubescens subvar. australe, commonly known as the Australian yellow flag, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southeastern Australia.

It features rhizomatous growth and produces clusters of yellowish-green flowers in late winter to early spring. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its pubescent appearance. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable species for ecological restoration and ornamental gardening.
