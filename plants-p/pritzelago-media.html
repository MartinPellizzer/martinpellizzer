Pritzelago media

Pritzelago media, commonly known as the common thyme, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its small, fragrant leaves and pink or white flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has a creeping growth habit and often forms dense mats, making it suitable for ground cover. Its aromatic properties make it popular in culinary and medicinal uses.

Botanically, it belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is known for its aromatic oils and medicinal compounds.
