Prunus × lannesiana f. sobanzakura

Prunus × lannesiana f. sobanzakura, commonly known as the Japanese cherry blossom or sakura, is a cultivar of the Prunus genus.

It is characterized by its delicate, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in early spring, creating a stunning visual display. The plant is a hybrid between Prunus lannesiana and Prunus serrulata, resulting in a variety that is highly valued for its ornamental beauty. It typically grows as a small tree or shrub, reaching heights of up to 10 meters.

The leaves are ovate with serrated edges, and the bark is smooth and grayish-brown when mature.
