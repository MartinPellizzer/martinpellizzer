Prunus communis subuniflora

Prunus communis subuniflora, commonly known as the common cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and oval-shaped leaves that turn yellow in autumn. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers in spring, which are followed by edible red or black berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in both culinary and medicinal contexts.
