Prunus communis subvar. platyphylla

Prunus communis subvar. platyphylla, commonly known as the flat-leaf cherry, is a variant of the common cherry tree characterized by its broad, flat leaves.

It is native to parts of Europe and is often found in temperate climates. The plant produces small, white or pink flowers in spring, followed by edible, sweet cherries in summer. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and fruit production.

The subvariety is generally hardy and adaptable to a range of soil types.
