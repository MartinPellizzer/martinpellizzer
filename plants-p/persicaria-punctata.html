Persicaria punctata

Persicaria punctata, commonly known as the spotted knotweed, is a herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of Asia and Europe.

It features upright, slender stems that are often reddish in color and bear alternate, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters during the summer months, which are followed by tiny, three-lobed seed capsules. Persicaria punctata thrives in moist soils and is often found in wetlands, streambanks, and woodland edges.

Its rapid growth and ability to spread through rhizomes make it an invasive species in some areas.
