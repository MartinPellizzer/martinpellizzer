Pleurothallis ascendens

Pleurothallis ascendens, commonly known as the ascending pleurothallis, is a species of orchid native to the cloud forests of Central and South America.

It is a small, epiphytic plant that thrives in humid, shaded environments, often growing on trees or rocks. The plant features delicate, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pale yellow. Its flowers are typically arranged in a raceme and bloom during the rainy season.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is a sought-after species among orchid enthusiasts.
