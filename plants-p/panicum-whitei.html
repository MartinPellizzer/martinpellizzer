Panicum whitei

Panicum whitei, commonly known as white panicgrass, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the grass family (Poaceae) and typically grows in moist, open habitats such as wetlands and disturbed soils. The plant features slender, cylindrical stems and produces fluffy, white or pale pink seed heads that are characteristic of the Panicum genus. It is often used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive, feathery appearance and its ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.

Additionally, it plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small insects and birds.
