Pseudotsuga nobilis

Pseudotsuga nobilis, commonly known as the Douglas fir, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to western North America.

It is characterized by its tall, conical shape and needle-like leaves that are flattened and dark green on top. The tree produces cones that hang downward, containing edible seeds surrounded by a papery wing. Its bark is thick, scaly, and reddish-brown, providing protection against fire and pests.

The Douglas fir is valued for its strong, durable wood and plays an important role in forest ecosystems.
