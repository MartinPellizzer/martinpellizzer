Potentilla adscendens var. baumgarteniana

Potentilla adscendens var. baumgarteniana, commonly known as the Baumann's cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, often at high altitudes. The plant features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are compound, with five leaflets that are serrated and covered in fine hairs.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
