Phoebe erectifolia

Phoebe erectifolia, commonly known as the upright-leaf phoebe, is a species of flowering plant in the Lauraceae family.

It is native to parts of Southeast Asia, including regions of Malaysia and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its simple, elliptical leaves that are arranged alternately along the stems. It produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by small, dark berries.

Phoebe erectifolia is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories.
