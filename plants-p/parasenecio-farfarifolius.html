Parasenecio farfarifolius

Parasenecio farfarifolius, commonly known as the farfarifolius groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. Its leaves are broad, lobed, and often covered in a fine, white down.

The flowers, which bloom in spring, are yellow and attract various pollinators like bees and butterflies.
