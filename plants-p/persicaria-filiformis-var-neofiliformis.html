Persicaria filiformis var. neofiliformis

Persicaria filiformis var. neofiliformis, commonly known as the slender knotweed, is a herbaceous perennial native to eastern Asia.

It features slender, erect stems that are often reddish in color and covered with fine hairs. The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often with a slightly hairy texture.

It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.
