Persea scoparia

Persea scoparia, commonly known as the desert avocado or Mexican avocado, is a small evergreen tree native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in the spring. The fruit is a smooth, oval berry with a thick, leathery skin and a creamy, edible pulp. The plant thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in dry, rocky soils.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and its fruit, which is used in local cuisines.
