Pepinia corallina

Pepinia corallina, commonly known as the coral peperomia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its compact, succulent-like foliage and small, clustered inflorescences. The plant typically features thick, waxy leaves that are often variegated with shades of green, white, and pink. It thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Pepinia corallina is valued for its ornamental appeal and relatively low maintenance requirements.
