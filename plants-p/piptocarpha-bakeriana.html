Piptocarpha bakeriana

Piptocarpha bakeriana, commonly known as the Baker's brittlebrush, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage and small, tubular flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, with deep roots that allow it to survive in dry conditions.
