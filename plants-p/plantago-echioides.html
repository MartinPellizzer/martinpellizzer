Plantago echioides

Plantago echioides, commonly known as narrowleaf plantain, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It features upright, grass-like stems with lance-shaped leaves that have a rough, textured surface. The plant produces small, greenish flowers in dense clusters at the tips of the stems. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.

Its leaves are used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.
