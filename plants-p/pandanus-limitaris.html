Pandanus limitaris

Pandanus limitaris, commonly known as the narrow-leaf pandanus, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its long, narrow leaves that are often used in traditional crafts and as thatching material. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and is adapted to salt spray and sandy soils. It produces small, clustered flowers that are followed by spiky, fibrous fruits.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing coastal environments.
