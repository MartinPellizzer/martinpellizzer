Plantago fuscescens

Plantago fuscescens, commonly known as the brown plantain, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in disturbed habitats. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are covered in a dense layer of fine hairs, giving them a velvety texture. Its small, greenish-white flowers are clustered in loose spikes and bloom in the summer months.

The plant is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally for its soothing effects on the digestive system.
