Phelypaea gigantea

Phelypaea gigantea, commonly known as the giant water fern, is a striking aquatic plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, floating fronds that can reach up to 3 meters in length, making it one of the largest ferns in the world. The plant thrives in slow-moving freshwater environments, often found in lakes and rivers. Its fronds are composed of numerous small, lance-shaped pinnae, which help in photosynthesis and provide habitat for aquatic life.

Phelypaea gigantea plays an important ecological role by stabilizing water bodies and supporting diverse aquatic ecosystems.
