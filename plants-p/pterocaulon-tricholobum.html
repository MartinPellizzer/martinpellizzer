Pterocaulon tricholobum

Pterocaulon tricholobum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf pterocaulon, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in dense clusters, which are typically borne on short pedicels. Its foliage is often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive, fuzzy appearance.

Pterocaulon tricholobum is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, thriving in well-drained soils and full sunlight.
