Prostanthera marifolia

Prostanthera marifolia, commonly known as the mountain mint bush, is a shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its small, pale blue to purple flowers are arranged in whorled clusters along the stems.

Prostanthera marifolia is well adapted to dry, rocky habitats and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.
