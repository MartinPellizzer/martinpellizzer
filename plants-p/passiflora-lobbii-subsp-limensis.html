Passiflora lobbii subsp. limensis

Passiflora lobbii subsp.

limensis, commonly known as the Limen Passionflower, is a climbing vine native to the tropical regions of South America. It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that feature a complex arrangement of petals, sepals, and bracts.

The plant produces edible berries that are typically yellow or orange when ripe. It thrives in humid, warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This subspecies is valued for both its ornamental beauty and its ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
