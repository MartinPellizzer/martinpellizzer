Pleioblastus viridistriatus var. kongosanensis

Pleioblastus viridistriatus var. kongosanensis, commonly known as the Korean bamboo, is a species of grass that belongs to the Poaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of South Korea and is characterized by its slender, green culms with a distinctive light green stripe running down the center. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments and forms dense clumps, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening. Its foliage is finely textured and can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters, adding a graceful touch to landscapes.

This variety is also valued for its ability to thrive in partial shade and its low maintenance requirements.
