Phaenopyrum pruinosum

Phaenopyrum pruinosum, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a perennial shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its soft, velvety foliage, which gives it its common name. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, usually greenish-yellow, and it produces capsule fruits containing numerous seeds.

Phaenopyrum pruinosum is often found in disturbed areas and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
