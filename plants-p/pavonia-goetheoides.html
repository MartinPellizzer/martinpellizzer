Pavonia goetheoides

Pavonia goetheoides, commonly known as the Goethe's wood rose, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, featuring upright stems covered in fine hairs. The plant produces showy, pink to red bract-like flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly rough texture, while its root system is fibrous and shallow.

Pavonia goetheoides is well adapted to moist, sunny environments and is often found in wetlands and along streambanks.
