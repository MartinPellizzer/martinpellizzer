Prunus americana var. rosea

Prunus americana var. rosea, commonly known as the American cherry or roseleaf cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its ornamental pink to rose-red flowers that bloom in early spring before the leaves emerge. The plant has simple, alternate, ovate leaves that turn yellow in the fall. Its fruits are small, dark red drupes that ripen in late summer.

It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often used in landscaping for its aesthetic appeal.
