Phyllodes monophylla

Phyllodes monophylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its long, narrow phyllodes, which are modified leaves that serve as the primary photosynthetic structures. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. Its flowers are yellow and appear in clustered spikes, adding ornamental value.

The species is also valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for native wildlife.
