Pteromimosa

Pteromimosa, commonly known as the silver wattle, is a genus of flowering plants in the legume family.

It is native to Australia and is characterized by its distinctive, fern-like leaves and clusters of small, yellow flowers. The plant is often found in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. Its seeds are encased in pods that split open to release the seeds, aiding in its dispersal.

Pteromimosa species are also valued for their ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native landscapes.
