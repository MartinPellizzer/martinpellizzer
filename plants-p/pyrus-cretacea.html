Pyrus cretacea

Pyrus cretacea, commonly known as the Cretaceous pear, is a fossil plant species that lived during the Cretaceous period.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its woody structure and simple, alternate leaves. The plant exhibits features typical of early angiosperms, including a vascular system and reproductive structures. Fossilized remains of Pyrus cretacea provide valuable insights into the evolution of flowering plants.

Its morphology suggests it may have been a small tree or shrub adapted to the warm, coastal environments of the Cretaceous era.
