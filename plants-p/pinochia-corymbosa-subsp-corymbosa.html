Pinochia corymbosa subsp. corymbosa

Pinochia corymbosa subsp.

corymbosa, commonly known as the mountain holly, is a shrub native to the high-altitude regions of South America. It typically grows in rocky or mountainous terrain, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure.

The plant features small, elliptical leaves with a glossy green surface and produces clusters of white flowers in the spring. Its fruit is a small, fleshy berry that ripens to a red color, attracting various bird species.

This subspecies is noted for its hardiness and ability to thrive in harsh environmental conditions.
