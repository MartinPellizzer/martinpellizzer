Poecilolepis ficoidea

Poecilolepis ficoidea, commonly known as the fig-leaf fern, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, glossy, heart-shaped leaves that resemble a fig leaf, giving it its common name. The plant has a rhizomatous growth habit, allowing it to spread and form dense foliage. Its fronds are typically evergreen, providing a lush appearance throughout the year.

It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often used as an ornamental plant in tropical gardens.
