Prunus pygmaea

Prunus pygmaea, commonly known as the dwarf cherry or Japanese dwarf cherry, is a small deciduous shrub or tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit, typically reaching heights of 1 to 3 meters. The plant features small, ovate leaves that turn vibrant red or orange in the fall, adding ornamental value. Its flowers, which appear in early spring, are pink to white and arranged in clusters, attracting pollinators.

Prunus pygmaea is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal and hardy nature.
