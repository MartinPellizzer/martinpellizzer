Penstemon davidsonii var. ellipticus

Penstemon davidsonii var. ellipticus, commonly known as the Davidson's beardtongue, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces spikes of tubular, two-lipped flowers that range in color from purple to pink. The leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine, silky hairs, contributing to its drought-tolerant nature. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, supporting native pollinators.
