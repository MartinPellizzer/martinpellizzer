Primula jesoana var. pubescens

Primula jesoana var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy primrose, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves that give it a distinctive texture. The plant produces clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of yellow, white, or pink, typically blooming in early spring. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in woodland or rocky habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and unique appearance, it is cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
