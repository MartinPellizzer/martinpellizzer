Panicum sanguinale var. aegyptiacum

Panicum sanguinale var. aegyptiacum, commonly known as Egyptian lovegrass, is a perennial grass native to Africa and parts of the Middle East.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces fluffy, pinkish to reddish seed heads that are visually striking. The plant is often found in dry, sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It is drought-tolerant and has a clumping growth habit, making it suitable for ornamental use in gardens.

The botanical characteristics of this plant make it an interesting subject for both ecological and horticultural study.
