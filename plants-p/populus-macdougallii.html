Populus macdougallii

Populus macdougallii, commonly known as the Macdougall's poplar, is a species of willow native to the western United States.

It is a deciduous tree that typically grows in moist, well-drained soils along streambanks and in riparian zones. The plant has distinctive heart-shaped leaves with a rough texture and a grayish-green underside. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, appearing in catkins, while its bark is smooth and light gray.

Populus macdougallii plays an important ecological role in stabilizing riverbanks and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
