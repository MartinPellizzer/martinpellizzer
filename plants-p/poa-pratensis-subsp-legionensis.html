Poa pratensis subsp. legionensis

Poa pratensis subsp.

legionensis, commonly known as the Legion grass, is a member of the Poaceae family and is native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is a perennial grass characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves.

The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and along riverbanks. Its inflorescence consists of a single, dense spike that bears small, two-lobed spikelets.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping and ecological restoration projects.
