Potentilla thuringiaca subsp. nestleriana

Potentilla thuringiaca subsp.

nestleriana, commonly known as the Thuringian cinquefoil, is a low-growing perennial shrub native to Central Europe. It typically features small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant has hairy, reddish stems and lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
