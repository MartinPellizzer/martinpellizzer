Panicum atropurpureum

Panicum atropurpureum, commonly known as bloodgrass, is a perennial grass native to Africa.

It is characterized by its striking reddish-purple foliage, which becomes more intense under stress or during the dry season. The plant produces slender, spike-like inflorescences that are often used in ornamental gardening. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Due to its drought tolerance and aesthetic appeal, it is a popular choice for low-maintenance landscapes.
