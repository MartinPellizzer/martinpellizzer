Polypogon monspeliensis var. intermedius

Polypogon monspeliensis var. intermedius, commonly known as the intermediate panicgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It features slender, upright stems and narrow, grass-like leaves with a bluish-green hue. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in wetland habitats and is often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important component of wetland ecosystems and a subject of study in ecological and botanical research.
