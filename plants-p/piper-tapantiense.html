Piper tapantiense

Piper tapantiense, commonly known as the Tapanti pepper, is a member of the Piperaceae family, native to the tropical regions of Central America.

It is a climbing vine characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and small, greenish-yellow flowers. The plant produces small, red berries that are used in traditional medicine and as a spice. Its botanical features include a fibrous root system and opposite leaf arrangement.

It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in the understory of tropical rainforests.
