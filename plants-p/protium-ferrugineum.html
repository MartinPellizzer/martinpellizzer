Protium ferrugineum

Protium ferrugineum, commonly known as the red ironwood, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dark, dense wood that is highly valued for its strength and durability. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical in shape. It produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in spikes, followed by woody, spiny fruits.

The tree is often found in lowland rainforests and is known for its ecological importance in supporting local biodiversity.
