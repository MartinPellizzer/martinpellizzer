Paphiopedilum schlimii

Paphiopedilum schlimii, commonly known as the Schlim's lady's slipper, is a rare and exquisite orchid native to the mountainous regions of China.

It is characterized by its distinctive pouch-shaped lip, which is a defining feature of the Paphiopedilum genus. The plant typically grows as a terrestrial orchid with a single stem and produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. Its leaves are usually elliptical and arranged in a rosette pattern, contributing to its elegant appearance.

Due to its rarity and horticultural value, it is often cultivated in greenhouses and is a prized specimen among orchid enthusiasts.
