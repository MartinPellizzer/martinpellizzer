Paspalum erectum

Paspalum erectum, commonly known as tall paspalum, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and dense, tufted foliage. The plant typically produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas.

Paspalum erectum is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in erosion-prone regions.
