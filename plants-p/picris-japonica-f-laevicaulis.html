Picris japonica f. laevicaulis

Picris japonica f. laevicaulis, commonly known as the smoothleaf goatherb, is a variant of the common goatherb.

It is a low-growing perennial herb with slender, ascending stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically visited by various pollinators. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

Its smooth, hairless foliage distinguishes it from other forms of Picris japonica.
