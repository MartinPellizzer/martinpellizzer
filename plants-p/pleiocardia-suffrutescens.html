Pleiocardia suffrutescens

Pleiocardia suffrutescens, commonly known as the rock fig, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its small, fleshy leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers. The plant is adapted to arid and rocky environments, showcasing a succulent structure to store water. Its fruit is a small, berry-like structure that ripens to a red color.

It plays an important role in local ecosystems by providing food for various wildlife species.
