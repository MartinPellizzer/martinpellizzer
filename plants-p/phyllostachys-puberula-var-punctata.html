Phyllostachys puberula var. punctata

Phyllostachys puberula var. punctata, commonly known as the spotted bamboo, is a variant of the silver bamboo species.

It is characterized by its slender, green culms that are covered with a fine, silky layer of hair. The plant exhibits a distinctive pattern of dark green spots along the length of its stems, which gives it its common name. It is a clump-forming bamboo that grows in dense stands, often reaching heights of up to 10 meters.

This ornamental bamboo is valued for its aesthetic appeal and is commonly used in landscaping and garden design.
