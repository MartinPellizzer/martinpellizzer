Poa glutinosa

Poa glutinosa, commonly known as sticky bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and narrow, bluish-green leaves that are often coated with a sticky substance. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences containing numerous tiny flowers, which are wind-pollinated. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, pastures, and disturbed areas.

Due to its hardiness and ability to form dense mats, it is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
