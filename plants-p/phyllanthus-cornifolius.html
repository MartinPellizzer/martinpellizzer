Phyllanthus cornifolius

Phyllanthus cornifolius, commonly known as the horned plant or hornleaf, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, sharply pointed leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically pollinated by insects. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in shaded, moist environments.

Phyllanthus cornifolius is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional medicine.
