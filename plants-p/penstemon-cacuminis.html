Penstemon cacuminis

Penstemon cacuminis, commonly known as the blue penstemon, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet tubular flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats such as cliffs and slopes, showing a preference for well-drained soils. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often covered in fine hairs, and it is known for its drought tolerance.

Penstemon cacuminis plays an important role in supporting pollinators like bees and butterflies in its native ecosystem.
