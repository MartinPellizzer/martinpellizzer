Plantago aristata var. nuttallii

Plantago aristata var. nuttallii, commonly known as Nuttall's plantain, is a variant of the common plantain species native to North America.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its erect, branched stems and broad, oval-shaped leaves with a rough, hairy texture. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in dense, spike-like clusters, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist to wet habitats such as meadows, marshes, and stream banks.

This species is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional herbal remedies.
