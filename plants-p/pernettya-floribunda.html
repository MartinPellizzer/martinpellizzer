Pernettya floribunda

Pernettya floribunda, commonly known as the fragrant snowberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia.

It produces small, white, fragrant flowers in late spring, which are followed by bright red berries in the autumn. The plant has alternate, ovate leaves that are typically covered in a fine, silvery down. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is often cultivated in gardens and natural habitats.
