Polygala sinaloensis

Polygala sinaloensis, commonly known as the Sinaloan milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid conditions. Its flowers are usually pale green to yellowish in color, and it produces small, bean-like pods that contain its seeds.

Polygala sinaloensis is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and ecological adaptations.
