Psychanthus trichocalyx

Psychanthus trichocalyx, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in a dense layer of fine, velvety hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in spikes, and it is often found in moist, shaded environments. Psychanthus trichocalyx is also known for its ability to thrive in disturbed soils and is sometimes considered an invasive species in certain regions.

Its botanical features make it a notable plant in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
