Pinalia striolata

Pinalia striolata, commonly known as the streaked pincushion cactus, is a species of flowering plant native to the arid regions of South America.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its cylindrical, segmented stems covered in spines. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, adding a striking visual contrast to its green, spiky structure. Pinalia striolata is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments with minimal water, storing moisture in its thick, fleshy stems.

Its unique appearance and ability to thrive in extreme conditions make it a notable species in botanical studies.
