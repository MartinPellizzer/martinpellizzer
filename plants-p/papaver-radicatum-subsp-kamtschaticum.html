Papaver radicatum subsp. kamtschaticum

Papaver radicatum subsp.

kamtschaticum, commonly known as the Kamtschatic mountain poppy, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia. It is characterized by its striking red to orange petals and a distinctive, elongated seed capsule.

The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils. Its botanical features include a fibrous root system and lanceolate leaves with a waxy surface.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant flowers.
