Pentisea amplexans

Pentisea amplexans, commonly known as the climbing cissus, is a vigorous climbing plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It features heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow. The plant thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found growing along forest edges or in disturbed areas. Its stems are woody and can climb by means of tendrils or by wrapping around supports.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
