Prostanthera incisa var. sieberi

Prostanthera incisa var. sieberi, commonly known as the narrow-leaf mint bush, is a flowering plant native to eastern Australia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and rocky areas. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a distinctive minty fragrance when crushed.

The small, tubular flowers are usually pale blue to purple and appear in clusters, attracting various pollinators.
