Parathesis tonana

Parathesis tonana, commonly known as the golden mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces bright yellow blooms in late summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

Parathesis tonana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
