Potamogeton paramoanus

Potamogeton paramoanus, commonly known as the highland pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to the Andean region of South America.

It thrives in freshwater environments such as lakes, ponds, and streams, often in areas with moderate to high water flow. The plant has long, narrow leaves that are typically arranged in whorls around the stem, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often submerged. It plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.

Its presence is often an indicator of good water quality and stable ecological conditions.
