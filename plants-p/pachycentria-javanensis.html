Pachycentria javanensis

Pachycentria javanensis, commonly known as the Javanese thick-leafed fern, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its large, leathery fronds. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, such as forest understories. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The leaves are arranged in a compact, rosette-like pattern, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
