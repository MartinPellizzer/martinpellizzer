Piper mitifolium

Piper mitifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaf pepper, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many species of pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its slender, compound leaves and small, greenish-white flowers. It produces small, round berries that are typically green when unripe and turn red when mature.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has some traditional medicinal uses in indigenous communities.
