Pteropyrum aucheri subsp. ericoides

Pteropyrum aucheri subsp.

ericoides, commonly known as the dwarf snowdrop, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It features small, white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in early spring, typically from March to April.

The plant has dark green, leathery leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern, contributing to its dense, compact appearance. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and hardiness, it is often cultivated in gardens and alpine settings.
