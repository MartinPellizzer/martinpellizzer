Pyrus ussuriensis var. macrostipes

Pyrus ussuriensis var. macrostipes, commonly known as the large-fruited Siberian pear, is a variant of the Siberian pear native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its large, edible fruits that are typically yellow or orange in color when ripe. The plant produces small, white flowers in early spring, which are followed by the development of its distinctive fruits. The leaves are broad and oval, with a smooth texture and a slightly serrated edge.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and its fruit, which can be used in cooking or preserves.
