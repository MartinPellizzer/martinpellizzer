Prunus lusitanica f. myrtifolia

Prunus lusitanica f. myrtifolia, commonly known as the myrtle-leaved cherry, is a compact shrub native to southern Europe and North Africa.

It features small, elliptical leaves that are glossy and resemble those of myrtle, giving it its common name. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers in spring, followed by edible, tart berries. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adaptable to a variety of soil types.

This variety is also valued for its use in hedging and as a landscape plant due to its hardiness and compact growth habit.
