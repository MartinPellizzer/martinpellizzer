Paepalanthus implicatus

Paepalanthus implicatus, commonly known as the Andean sunflower, is a flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its vibrant yellow to orange flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in high-altitude regions, often in rocky or sandy soils, and thrives in cool, temperate climates. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves, and it is well adapted to the harsh environmental conditions of its native habitat.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its ornamental value.
