Picris sonchoides var. villarsii

Picris sonchoides var. villarsii, commonly known as the hairy sowthistle, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and can be found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas. Its leaves are hairy and have a distinctive lobed shape, while its yellow flowers bloom in the summer.

It is often considered a weed but has historical uses in traditional medicine.
