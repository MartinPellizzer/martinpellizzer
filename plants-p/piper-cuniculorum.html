Piper cuniculorum

Piper cuniculorum, commonly known as the "rabbit pepper," is a climbing vine native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by elongated, slender fruits. The fruits are typically harvested when they turn from green to red, and they have a mild, peppery flavor.

It is often used in traditional medicine and as a spice in regional cuisines.
