Panicum pappophorum var. tenerius

Panicum pappophorum var. tenerius, commonly known as velvet grass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear fluffy, wind-dispersed seeds, aiding in its propagation. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas or along roadsides.

Its ornamental value comes from its soft, velvety texture and ability to form dense clumps.
