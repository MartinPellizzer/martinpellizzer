Paronychia setigera subvar. subglabra

Paronychia setigera subvar. subglabra, commonly known as the smoothleaf sandmat, is a low-growing perennial plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States.

It has a mat-forming habit with small, fleshy, succulent leaves that are typically covered in a fine layer of white hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in clusters during the spring and summer months. Its leaves are generally smoother and less hairy compared to other varieties of Paronychia setigera.

It is well adapted to harsh, dry environments and is often found in rocky soils and sandy areas.
