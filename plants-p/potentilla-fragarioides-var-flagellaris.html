Potentilla fragarioides var. flagellaris

Potentilla fragarioides var. flagellaris, commonly known as the trailing potentilla, is a low-growing shrub native to North America.

It features slender, creeping stems that often root at the nodes, forming dense mats. The plant produces small, white to pink flowers in clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a slightly serrated margin.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or rocky areas.
