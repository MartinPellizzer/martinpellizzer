Pelargonium canaliculatum

Pelargonium canaliculatum, commonly known as the narrow-ribbed geranium, is a species of flowering plant in the family Geraniaceae.

It is native to South Africa and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and showy, five-petaled flowers. The plant typically produces bright pink to purple flowers that bloom throughout the growing season. Its leaves are heart-shaped with prominent veins and a slightly hairy texture.

Pelargonium canaliculatum is often cultivated as a decorative plant for gardens and hanging baskets due to its ornamental value.
