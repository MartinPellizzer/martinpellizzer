Potamogeton vaseyi

Potamogeton vaseyi, commonly known as the Vasey's pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to North America.

It typically grows in freshwater environments such as ponds, lakes, and slow-moving rivers. The plant has long, ribbon-like leaves that are often arranged in a spiral pattern along its stem. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are pollinated by water currents.
