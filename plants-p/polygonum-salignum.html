Polygonum salignum

Polygonum salignum, commonly known as salt grass, is a flowering plant native to arid and saline environments.

It belongs to the family Polygonaceae and is characterized by its succulent, often hairy leaves that help it retain water. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish or reddish in color. It thrives in coastal areas and salt marshes, where it plays a vital role in stabilizing soil and preventing erosion.

Polygonum salignum is well adapted to high salinity and can tolerate both drought and flooding conditions.
