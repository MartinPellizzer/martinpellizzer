Pelargonium scaposum

Pelargonium scaposum, commonly known as the Cape geranium, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, showy flowers that range in color from pink to purple. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture, and it produces small, rounded fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Pelargonium scaposum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
