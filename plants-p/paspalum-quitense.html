Paspalum quitense

Paspalum quitense, commonly known as the Andean grass, is a perennial grass species native to South America, particularly found in Argentina, Chile, and Peru.

It thrives in temperate to subtropical climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain tiny flowers. It is characterized by its ability to tolerate both drought and flooding conditions, making it adaptable to various environments.

Paspalum quitense is also valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in stabilizing soil in erosion-prone areas.
