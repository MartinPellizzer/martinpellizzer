Pongamia glabra var. minor

Pongamia glabra var. minor, commonly known as the lesser pongam, is a small leguminous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces pods containing seeds that are often used in traditional medicine and as a source of oil. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in disturbed soils.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological and agricultural studies.
