Piper rhomboidale

Piper rhomboidale, commonly known as the rhomboid pepper or the winged pepper, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species such as black pepper. The plant is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and small, greenish-white flowers that develop into elliptical berries. The berries are typically harvested when they turn red and are used in traditional medicine and as a spice.

It thrives in tropical climates and requires well-drained soil and partial shade for optimal growth.
