Panicum hoffmannseggii

Panicum hoffmannseggii, commonly known as Hoffmannsegg's panicgrass, is a perennial grass species native to Central Asia.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. The plant has slender, erect stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its inflorescence consists of a loose panicle made up of small spikelets, which are usually pale green to brown in color.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
