Paepalanthus piscatorum var. piscatorum

Paepalanthus piscatorum var. piscatorum, commonly known as the water papyrus, is a herbaceous plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is characterized by its tall, reed-like stems and large, arrowhead-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats, such as marshes and along the edges of lakes and rivers. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in terminal spikes, and it is often used in traditional medicine.

Its botanical features make it well adapted to aquatic or semi-aquatic environments.
