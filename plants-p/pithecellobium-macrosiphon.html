Pithecellobium macrosiphon

Pithecellobium macrosiphon, commonly known as the large-beaked pithecellobium, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and distinctive, elongated flower clusters. The plant produces small, edible pods that contain seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. Its growth habit includes both woody and herbaceous forms, depending on environmental conditions.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and nitrogen-fixing capabilities.
