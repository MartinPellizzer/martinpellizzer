Paris chinensis

Paris chinensis, commonly known as the Chinese lantern plant, is a perennial herb native to China.

It belongs to the family Melanthiaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, showy red berries that resemble small lanterns. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments and has a rhizomatous root system. Its leaves are lance-shaped and alternate along the stem, while its flowers are small and greenish-white.

The plant is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine.
