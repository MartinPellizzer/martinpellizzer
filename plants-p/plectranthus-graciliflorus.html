Plectranthus graciliflorus

Plectranthus graciliflorus, commonly known as slenderflower plectranthus, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and small, tubular flowers that are typically violet or purple in color. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Plectranthus graciliflorus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow in temperate climates.
