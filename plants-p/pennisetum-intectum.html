Pennisetum intectum

Pennisetum intectum, commonly known as purple top millet, is a flowering plant native to Africa.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its upright growth habit and striking purple-tinted foliage. The plant produces small, fluffy seed heads that are often used in ornamental gardening. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for drought-tolerant landscapes.

Pennisetum intectum is also valued for its ecological benefits, providing food for birds and insects.
