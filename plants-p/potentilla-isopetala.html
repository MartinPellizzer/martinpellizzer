Potentilla isopetala

Potentilla isopetala, commonly known as the alpine potentilla, is a low-growing perennial shrub native to alpine regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It features small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has slender, woody stems and is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit. Its leaves are typically oval-shaped with finely toothed margins and a hairy texture.

Potentilla isopetala is well adapted to cold, rocky environments and is often found in mountainous areas.
