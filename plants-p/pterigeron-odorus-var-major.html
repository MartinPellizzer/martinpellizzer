Pterigeron odorus var. major

Pterigeron odorus var. major, commonly known as the sweet violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and clusters of small, fragrant purple flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive scent, which is often described as sweet and spicy. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its delicate appearance and historical use in herbal medicine.
