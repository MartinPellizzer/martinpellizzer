Phyllanthus arabicus

Phyllanthus arabicus, commonly known as the Arabian spurge, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the Middle East and North Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring woody stems and small, alternate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It has adapted to harsh environments with its succulent leaves and deep root system, allowing it to survive in dry conditions.

Phyllanthus arabicus is also noted for its medicinal properties, used in traditional herbal remedies for various ailments.
