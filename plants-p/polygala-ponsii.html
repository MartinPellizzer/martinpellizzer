Polygala ponsii

Polygala ponsii, commonly known as the Chinese milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to China and parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, delicate flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in mountainous regions. Its flowers are usually pale pink or white, and it produces small, bead-like fruits that contain seeds.

Polygala ponsii is valued in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
