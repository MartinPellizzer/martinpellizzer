Plantago albicans f. minor

Plantago albicans f. minor, commonly known as the lesser plantain, is a variant of the common plantain species.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in disturbed soils such as roadsides and fields. The plant has broad, lance-shaped leaves with a distinctive hairy underside. Its flowers are small, greenish-white, and clustered in dense spikes.

It is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional herbal remedies.
