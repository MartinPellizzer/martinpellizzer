Pittosporum glabratum var. neriifolium

Pittosporum glabratum var. neriifolium, commonly known as the black-leaf pittosporum, is a shrub native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its dark, glossy leaves that are elliptical in shape and often have a reddish tint when young. The plant produces fragrant, white flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring, followed by small, dark blue berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers a sunny to partially shaded environment.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens and landscaping.
