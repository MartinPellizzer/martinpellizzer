Polygala sancti-georgii var. oblanceolata

Polygala sancti-georgii var. oblanceolata, commonly known as the St. George's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Polygalaceae family and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, clustered flowers. The leaves are typically narrow and oblanceolate, giving the plant its specific epithet. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
