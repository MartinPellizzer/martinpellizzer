Polygonum araucanum

Polygonum araucanum, commonly known as the Chilean knotweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America, particularly Chile and Argentina.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, which is characterized by its distinctive nodes and often triangular stems. The plant features small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, and its leaves are typically lance-shaped with a hairy texture. Polygonum araucanum is adapted to grow in moist, temperate climates and is often found in riverbanks and wetlands.

It has been used traditionally for its medicinal properties, including anti-inflammatory and antiseptic effects.
