Palaquium jambos

Palaquium jambos, commonly known as the wild mango or jambos tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by edible, fleshy berries that resemble mangoes in appearance. Its fruit is valued for its flavor and is often used in local cuisines.

The tree is also appreciated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in humid, tropical climates.
