Piper cincinnatum

Piper cincinnatum, commonly known as the narrow-leafed pepper plant, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil and Peru. The plant is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and small, heart-shaped leaves. It produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that develop into three-lobed berries.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and its use in traditional medicine.
