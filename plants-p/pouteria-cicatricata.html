Pouteria cicatricata

Pouteria cicatricata, commonly known as the Brazilian nut tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is characterized by its large, glossy leaves and produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The tree's most notable feature is its hard-shelled fruit, which contains edible seeds known as Brazil nuts. These seeds are rich in nutrients, particularly selenium, and are commercially valuable.

The plant plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, supporting various wildlife species.
