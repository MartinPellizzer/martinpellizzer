Peperomia candolleana

Peperomia candolleana, commonly known as the Candolleana peperomia, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit. The plant features small, ovate leaves that are often covered in a fine, powdery coating, giving them a unique texture. Its flowers are typically small and inconspicuous, arranged in dense spikes, and it is often grown as a decorative houseplant due to its attractive foliage.

Peperomia candolleana prefers bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it suitable for indoor cultivation.
