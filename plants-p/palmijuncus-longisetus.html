Palmijuncus longisetus

Palmijuncus longisetus, commonly known as the long-hair palm, is a unique member of the Arecaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, unbranched trunk and a crown of long, narrow leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences with both male and female flowers, typical of many palms. Its leaves are often covered in fine, hairy structures, which may aid in water retention in arid environments.

This species is native to specific regions in Africa and is of interest to botanists due to its morphological adaptations.
