Pterostylis lepida

Pterostylis lepida, commonly known as the ghost orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, pale green stems and small, inconspicuous flowers that are often hidden among the foliage. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as rainforests and swampy areas. Its flowers are usually green or pale yellow, with a distinctive lip that is often darker in color.

Pterostylis lepida is a rare and delicate species, requiring specific environmental conditions to thrive.
