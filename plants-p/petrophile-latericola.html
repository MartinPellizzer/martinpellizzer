Petrophile latericola

Petrophile latericola, commonly known as the red sandstone lilly pilly, is a shrub native to the arid regions of Western Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, white to pinkish flowers that appear in clusters. The plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to low rainfall conditions. Its leaves are narrow and leathery, providing efficient water retention.

It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
