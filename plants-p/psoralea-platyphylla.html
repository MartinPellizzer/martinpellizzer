Psoralea platyphylla

Psoralea platyphylla, commonly known as the flat-leaf psoralea, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in open, sandy or rocky soils and is often found in grassland habitats. The plant features slender, branched stems and small, opposite leaves that are usually oval or lance-shaped. Its flowers are clustered in dense inflorescences and are typically yellow or pale green, with a distinctive, spurred petal.

Psoralea platyphylla is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally in some African cultures for various health purposes.
