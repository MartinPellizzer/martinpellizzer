Potentilla sulphurea f. perglandulosa

Potentilla sulphurea f. perglandulosa, commonly known as the sulphur cinquefoil, is a botanical variant characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers and glandular leaves.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is native to certain regions of Europe and Asia. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its foliage is often covered in small, sticky glands, which contribute to its unique appearance.

The flowers bloom in early summer, adding a striking visual element to its natural habitat.
