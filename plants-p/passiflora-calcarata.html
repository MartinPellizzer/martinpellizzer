Passiflora calcarata

Passiflora calcarata, commonly known as the rock passionflower, is a climbing vine native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that feature five prominent petals and a crown-like structure of filaments. The plant produces small, round, yellowish fruits that are edible but not widely cultivated. Its leaves are heart-shaped with a rough texture, and it thrives in temperate to subtropical climates.

Passiflora calcarata is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
