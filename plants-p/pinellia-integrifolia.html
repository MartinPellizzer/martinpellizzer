Pinellia integrifolia

Pinellia integrifolia, commonly known as the Chinese wild ginger, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It belongs to the family Araceae and is characterized by its tuberous rhizomes and broad, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers that are typically enclosed in a spadix, a common feature among arum family members. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in mountainous regions.

Pinellia integrifolia has been used in traditional Chinese medicine for its purported medicinal properties.
