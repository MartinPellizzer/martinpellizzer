Phaseolus vulgaris f. satureigriseus

Phaseolus vulgaris f. satureigriseus, commonly known as the grey saturei bean, is a botanical variant of the common bean.

It is characterized by its unique greyish-green foliage and pods, which distinguish it from typical varieties. The plant belongs to the Fabaceae family and is native to the Americas. It is primarily cultivated for its edible seeds, which are used in various culinary applications.

Due to its ornamental features, it is also appreciated in garden settings.
