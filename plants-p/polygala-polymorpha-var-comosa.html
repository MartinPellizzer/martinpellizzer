Polygala polymorpha var. comosa

Polygala polymorpha var. comosa, commonly known as the hairy polygala, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers that are often surrounded by soft, hairy foliage, giving it its common name. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid conditions.

The species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
