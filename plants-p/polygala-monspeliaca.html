Polygala monspeliaca

Polygala monspeliaca, commonly known as the desert milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of southern Europe and North Africa.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that often resemble those of the genus Millettia. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to survive in drought conditions. It has narrow, linear leaves and produces yellow or greenish-yellow blooms.

Polygala monspeliaca is often used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements and ornamental value.
