Panicum gonatodes

Panicum gonatodes, commonly known as the narrowleaf panicgrass, is a grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, two-lobed spikelets. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, often found in open woodlands and disturbed sites.

Its botanical features make it an important component of native plant communities and a potential candidate for ecological restoration projects.
