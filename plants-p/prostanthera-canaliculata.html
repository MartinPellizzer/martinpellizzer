Prostanthera canaliculata

Prostanthera canaliculata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf mintbush, is a shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It features aromatic, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, clustered white or pale blue flowers, which bloom in spring and summer. It is well adapted to dry conditions and is often found in heathlands and rocky areas.

Prostanthera canaliculata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
