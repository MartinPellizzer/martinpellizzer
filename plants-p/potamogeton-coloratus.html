Potamogeton coloratus

Potamogeton coloratus, commonly known as the redstem pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems that are often reddish in color, especially near the nodes. The leaves are narrow, ribbon-like, and typically arranged in whorls around the stem. It produces small, green flowers that are clustered in spikes, and it plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.

This plant thrives in shallow, freshwater environments and is often found in lakes, ponds, and slow-moving streams.
