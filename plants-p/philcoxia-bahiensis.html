Philcoxia bahiensis

Philcoxia bahiensis, commonly known as the ghost plant, is a unique succulent native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its small, pale green leaves that are adapted to store water, allowing it to survive in arid conditions. The plant belongs to the family Aizoaceae and is known for its ability to photosynthesize through its leaves rather than stems. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for xerophytic gardens.

Its striking appearance and low maintenance make it a popular choice for succulent enthusiasts.
