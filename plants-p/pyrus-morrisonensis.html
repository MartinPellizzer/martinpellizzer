Pyrus morrisonensis

Pyrus morrisonensis, commonly known as Morrison's pear, is a rare and critically endangered species native to the mountainous regions of southern China.

It is characterized by its small, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by edible, sweet fruits that mature in late summer. The plant typically grows in cool, moist environments, often in forested areas with rich, well-drained soil. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a glossy surface, while its bark is smooth and grayish in color.

Due to habitat loss and overharvesting, conservation efforts are crucial for its survival.
