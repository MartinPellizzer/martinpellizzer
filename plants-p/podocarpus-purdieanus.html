Podocarpus purdieanus

Podocarpus purdieanus, commonly known as the Pukaki pine, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the family Podocarpaceae and is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and conical shape. The tree produces small, spherical cones that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in cool, moist climates and is often found in mountainous regions.

Its hardy nature makes it a valuable ornamental and ecological plant in temperate environments.
