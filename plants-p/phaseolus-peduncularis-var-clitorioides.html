Phaseolus peduncularis var. clitorioides

Phaseolus peduncularis var. clitorioides, commonly known as the climbing bean or clitoria bean, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its twining vines and clusters of small, butterfly-like flowers that are typically blue or violet in color. The plant produces elongated, bean-like pods containing edible seeds, which are often used in traditional cuisines. Its rapid growth habit and ornamental flowers make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and greenhouses.

The species is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, which benefits soil fertility in agricultural settings.
