Pavonia urens var. glabrescens

Pavonia urens var. glabrescens, commonly known as the silky pavonia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its slender, erect stems and clusters of small, showy pink to red flowers. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine, silky hairs, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in native plant gardens.
