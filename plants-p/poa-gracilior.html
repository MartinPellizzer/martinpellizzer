Poa gracilior

Poa gracilior, commonly known as slender meadowgrass, is a species of grass native to various regions of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows, wetlands, and grassy slopes. The plant has slender, erect culms with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often slightly hairy. Its inflorescence consists of a loose to dense cluster of spikelets, which are characteristic of the Poaceae family.

Poa gracilior is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
