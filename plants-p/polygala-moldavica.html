Polygala moldavica

Polygala moldavica, commonly known as the Moldavian milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to southeastern Europe and western Asia.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. The plant features slender, branched stems and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink. Its leaves are narrow and alternate, often with a slight hairy texture.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
