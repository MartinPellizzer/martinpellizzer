Paronychia pulvinata var. longiaristata

Paronychia pulvinata var. longiaristata, commonly known as the long-stalked cushion plant, is a succulent shrub native to southern Africa.

It features dense, mat-forming growth with fleshy, cylindrical stems that often branch at the base. The leaves are typically small, cylindrical, and covered in a fine, powdery coating, which helps reduce water loss. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers in terminal clusters, often blooming in late summer.

This variety is well adapted to arid conditions, making it a hardy choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
