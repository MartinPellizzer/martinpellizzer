Polycarpaea caboverdeana

Polycarpaea caboverdeana, commonly known as Cape Verde morning glory, is a climbing vine native to the Cape Verde Islands.

It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in coastal areas and disturbed soils.

Its rapid growth and ornamental flowers make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening in tropical regions.
