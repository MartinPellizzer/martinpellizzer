Pyrus matsumurana

Pyrus matsumurana, commonly known as the Japanese white pear, is a deciduous tree native to Japan.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in early spring, followed by edible, sweet fruits that ripen in late summer. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and culinary uses.

Pyrus matsumurana thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
