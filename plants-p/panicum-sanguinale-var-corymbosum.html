Panicum sanguinale var. corymbosum

Panicum sanguinale var. corymbosum, commonly known as crimson lovegrass, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and the Americas.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces dense, fluffy panicles that are often pink to red in color. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is adapted to thrive in well-drained soils. It is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its attractive appearance and drought tolerance.

The species is also valued for its role in soil stabilization and erosion control.
