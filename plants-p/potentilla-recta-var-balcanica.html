Potentilla recta var. balcanica

Potentilla recta var. balcanica, commonly known as the Balkan cinquefoil, is a perennial shrub native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in sunny, well-drained environments. The plant features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its leaves are compound, consisting of five oval-shaped leaflets, giving it a distinctive appearance.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardiness and aesthetic appeal.
