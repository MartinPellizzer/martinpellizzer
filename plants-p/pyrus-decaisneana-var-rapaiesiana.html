Pyrus decaisneana var. rapaiesiana

Pyrus decaisneana var. rapaiesiana, commonly known as the Chinese white pear, is a variant of the pear tree native to China.

It is characterized by its deciduous nature, producing small, white flowers in spring followed by edible fruits. The leaves are broad and ovate, with a smooth texture and a slightly serrated edge. The fruit is typically small, with a pale yellow or white flesh that is tender and juicy.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and mild, sweet flavor, making it suitable for both cultivation and culinary use.
