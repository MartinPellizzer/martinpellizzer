Phyllodes allouia

Phyllodes allouia, commonly known as the Allouia phyllodes, is a shrub native to arid regions of Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its unique, flattened, leaf-like structures called phyllodes. These phyllodes are often mistaken for leaves but function similarly, aiding in water conservation in dry climates. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

It is well adapted to harsh environments and plays a role in stabilizing sandy soils.
