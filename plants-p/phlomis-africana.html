Phlomis africana

Phlomis africana, commonly known as African torchflower, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Lamiaceae, which includes many aromatic plants. The plant is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, bright yellow to orange-red flowers that bloom in summer. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Phlomis africana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
