Piper deliciasanum

Piper deliciasanum, commonly known as the "pepper plant" or "delicious pepper," is a flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, and thrives in humid, warm climates. The plant produces small, green berries that mature to a bright red color, containing the aromatic compound piperine, responsible for its spicy flavor. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties.

The plant's ornamental value and culinary uses have contributed to its popularity in both horticulture and gastronomy.
