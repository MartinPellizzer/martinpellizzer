Phacelia divaricata var. continentis

Phacelia divaricata var. continentis, commonly known as the Sierra phacelia, is a flowering plant native to western North America.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence. The plant produces clusters of small, blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
