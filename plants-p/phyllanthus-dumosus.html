Phyllanthus dumosus

Phyllanthus dumosus, commonly known as the shrub mallow, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its dense, bushy growth habit and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically has smooth, green leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It produces yellow or white flowers that bloom in clusters, often attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Phyllanthus dumosus is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
