Pomatocalpa expansum

Pomatocalpa expansum, commonly known as the broad-leafed marsh orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and large, showy flowers with a distinctive labellum. The plant typically grows in wet, swampy habitats and thrives in humid environments. Its roots are specialized for absorbing nutrients from the waterlogged soil.

The orchid is notable for its intricate floral structure and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.
