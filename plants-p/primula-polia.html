Primula polia

Primula polia, commonly known as the polia primrose, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as woodlands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in a fine, fuzzy texture, while its stems are erect and often branched.

Primula polia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its early spring blooms.
