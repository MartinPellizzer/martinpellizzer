Pilosella viridicans

Pilosella viridicans, commonly known as the greenleaf mouse-ear, is a flowering plant in the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its small, hairy leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. Native to Europe and parts of Asia, it thrives in meadows and grassy areas. The plant has a compact growth habit and is often found in open, sunny habitats.

It is not typically cultivated but is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and ecological role.
