Potamogeton perfoliatus f. rotundifolius

Potamogeton perfoliatus f. rotundifolius, commonly known as the round-leaved pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, rounded leaves that are often arranged in whorls around the stem. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or yellow-green in color. It plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.

Its growth is often influenced by water clarity, nutrient levels, and light availability.
