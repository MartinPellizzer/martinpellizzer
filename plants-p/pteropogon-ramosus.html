Pteropogon ramosus

Pteropogon ramosus, commonly known as the hairy sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and narrow leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils, often in wetlands or along stream banks. Its inflorescence consists of small, clustered flowers that are usually green or brown in color.

Pteropogon ramosus is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in wetland environments.
