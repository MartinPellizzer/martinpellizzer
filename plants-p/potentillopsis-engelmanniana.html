Potentillopsis engelmanniana

Potentillopsis engelmanniana, commonly known as Engelmann's cinquefoil, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and along stream banks. The plant features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its leaves are pinnately divided, with five to seven leaflets, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often used in native plant gardens.
