Pseudocyperus scirpinus

Pseudocyperus scirpinus, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedges, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland and marshy areas in tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems. The plant typically grows in dense clusters, with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often slightly curved. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are arranged in spikelets, and it plays an important role in stabilizing wetland soils.

Pseudocyperus scirpinus is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to thrive in waterlogged conditions.
