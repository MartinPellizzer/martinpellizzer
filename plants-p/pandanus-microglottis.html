Pandanus microglottis

Pandanus microglottis, commonly known as the narrow-leaf pandanus, is a flowering plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and long, narrow leaves that are often used in traditional crafts. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences at the top of the stem. Its roots are sometimes used in local medicine for their purported healing properties.

The species is well adapted to coastal and wetland environments, thriving in humid, tropical climates.
