Pyrus brandisii

Pyrus brandisii, commonly known as the Brandis pear, is a species of flowering plant in the family Rosaceae.

It is native to the eastern Himalayas, including regions of India, Nepal, and Bhutan. The plant is characterized by its deciduous nature, with simple, alternate leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape. It produces small, greenish-white flowers in early spring, followed by edible fruits that are typically pear-shaped and have a mild, sweet flavor.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and its contribution to local biodiversity.
