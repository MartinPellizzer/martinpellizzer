Potentilla laevigata

Potentilla laevigata, commonly known as the smooth cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a low-growing perennial shrub that typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are palmately divided into five oval-shaped lobes, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The smooth cinquefoil thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or rocky areas.
