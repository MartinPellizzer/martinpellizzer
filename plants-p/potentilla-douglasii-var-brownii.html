Potentilla douglasii var. brownii

Potentilla douglasii var. brownii, commonly known as Brown's cinquefoil, is a low-growing perennial shrub native to the western United States.

It features bright yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, typically from May to June. The plant has compound leaves with five leaflets, which are usually hairy and have a distinctive grayish-green color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or open habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in native plant gardens.
