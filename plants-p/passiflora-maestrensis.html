Passiflora maestrensis

Passiflora maestrensis, commonly known as the Ecuadorian passionflower, is a climbing vine native to the Andean regions of Ecuador.

It is characterized by its striking, intricate flowers that feature five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments resembling a crown of thorns. The plant produces small, edible berries that are typically yellow or orange in color. Its leaves are heart-shaped with a rough texture and often have a distinct scent.

Passiflora maestrensis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated for its unique floral display.
