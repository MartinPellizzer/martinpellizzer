Persea philippinensis

Persea philippinensis, commonly known as the Philippine persimmon, is a small evergreen tree native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae and is characterized by its glossy, lance-shaped leaves and fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, orange-red fruits that are edible and have a sweet, astringent flavor. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruit, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and home landscapes.
