Primula cylindrica

Primula cylindrica, commonly known as the cylindrical primrose, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its upright, cylindrical flower stalks that bear clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils and cool climates. Its leaves are usually basal, forming a rosette, and are covered in a fine, white pubescence.

Primula cylindrica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate, early spring blooms.
