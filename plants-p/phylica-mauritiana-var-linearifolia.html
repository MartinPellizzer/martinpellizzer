Phylica mauritiana var. linearifolia

Phylica mauritiana var. linearifolia, commonly known as the linear-leaved bottlebrush, is a shrub native to the coastal regions of South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, cylindrical inflorescences that resemble bottlebrushes, which are typically red or orange in color. The leaves are narrow and linear, giving the plant its varietal name, and they are arranged opposite along the stems. This species thrives in sandy soils and is often found in dune environments, where it plays a role in stabilizing coastal ecosystems.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
