Phoebe campechiana

Phoebe campechiana, commonly known as the Mexican beech, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Central America and parts of Mexico.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae, which includes other aromatic trees and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a leathery texture. It produces small, yellowish flowers followed by dark blue berries that are edible for some bird species.

Phoebe campechiana is often found in moist, shaded environments such as montane forests and is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance.
