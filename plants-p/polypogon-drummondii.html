Polypogon drummondii

Polypogon drummondii, commonly known as Drummond's grass, is a species of flowering plant in the grass family Poaceae.

It is native to North America and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It is often found in dry, open habitats such as prairies and disturbed soils.

Polypogon drummondii is adapted to thrive in arid conditions and is considered an important component of native grassland ecosystems.
