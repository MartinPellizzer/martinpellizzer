Phyllostachys reticulata var. castillonii

Phyllostachys reticulata var. castillonii, commonly known as the Castillonii bamboo, is a variant of the giant bamboo species characterized by its striking silver-green culms with a distinctive reticulated pattern.

It is native to China and is prized for its ornamental value in horticulture. The plant typically grows in clumps and can reach heights of up to 10 meters, making it a dramatic addition to gardens. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, contributing to its elegant appearance.

This variety is relatively cold-hardy and thrives in well-drained soil, making it suitable for a variety of climates.
