Pourouma cuspidata

Pourouma cuspidata, commonly known as the areca palm, is a species of flowering plant in the Arecaceae family.

It is native to South America, particularly the Amazon Basin, and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into edible, fleshy berries. These berries are an important food source for various animals and are also used in traditional practices by indigenous communities.

Its rapid growth and adaptability make it a significant component of tropical rainforest ecosystems.
