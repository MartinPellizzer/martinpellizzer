Pyrus spectabilis var. kaido

Pyrus spectabilis var. kaido, commonly known as the Japanese white pear, is a variant of the white pear tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its ornamental value, featuring showy white flowers that bloom in early spring. The tree produces small, sweet fruits that are edible but less commonly cultivated for commercial use. Its leaves are broad and dark green, providing a striking visual contrast during the growing season.

This variety is often grown in gardens for its aesthetic appeal and mild fragrance.
