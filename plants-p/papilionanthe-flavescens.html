Papilionanthe flavescens

Papilionanthe flavescens, commonly known as the yellow-lipped orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking yellow and green flowers, which resemble the shape of a butterfly. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its leaves are long and strap-like, and it produces small, fragrant blossoms.

This orchid is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in tropical gardens.
