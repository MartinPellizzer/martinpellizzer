Panicum keyense

Panicum keyense, commonly known as Key panicgrass, is a grass species native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers.

Panicum keyense is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its attractive appearance and adaptability.
