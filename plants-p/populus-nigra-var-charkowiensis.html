Populus nigra var. charkowiensis

Populus nigra var. charkowiensis, commonly known as the Charkowian black poplar, is a variant of the black poplar native to Eastern Europe.

It is characterized by its dark, fissured bark and heart-shaped leaves with a serrated margin. The plant typically grows along riverbanks and moist soils, exhibiting a fast growth rate. It produces small, yellowish catkins in early spring, which are its primary means of reproduction.

This variety is valued for its ecological role and historical use in forestry and landscaping.
