Protium heptaphyllum

Protium heptaphyllum, commonly known as the seven-leaved tamanu, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the family Sapotaceae and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that are typically arranged in whorls of seven, giving it its common name. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by spherical, woody fruits containing a single seed. Its bark and leaves have been used traditionally for their medicinal properties, including anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial effects.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its unique foliage.
