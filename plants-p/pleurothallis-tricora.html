Pleurothallis tricora

Pleurothallis tricora, commonly known as the three-lobed orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact, sympodial growth habit. The plant produces small, delicate flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, often with intricate patterns on the petals. It thrives in shaded, humid environments and requires well-draining, acidic soil for optimal growth.

Pleurothallis tricora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
