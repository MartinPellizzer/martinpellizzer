Psoralea oligophylla

Psoralea oligophylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaf psoralea, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, compound leaves. The plant produces yellowish to brownish pea-like flowers, which are typically clustered in racemes. It is often found in sandy or rocky soils in arid and semi-arid regions.

Psoralea oligophylla is adapted to thrive in low-nutrient environments and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
