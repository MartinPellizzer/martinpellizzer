Phyllobolus roseus

Phyllobolus roseus, commonly known as the rose leaf bug, is a species of leaf insect native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its elongated, leaf-like body that mimics the appearance of rose leaves, providing excellent camouflage in its natural habitat. The plant has a robust stem with broad, lance-shaped leaves that are typically a vibrant green color. It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

The leaves are not only visually striking but also serve as a means of protection from predators.
