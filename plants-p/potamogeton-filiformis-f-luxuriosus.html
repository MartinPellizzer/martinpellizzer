Potamogeton filiformis f. luxuriosus

Potamogeton filiformis f. luxuriosus, commonly known as the luxuriant filamentous pondweed, is a subspecies of the pondweed genus Potamogeton.

It is characterized by its long, slender, and often branching stems that float or root in shallow water. The leaves are narrow and ribbon-like, typically arranged in whorls around the stem. This plant thrives in freshwater environments such as lakes, ponds, and slow-moving rivers.

It plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
