Poterium spinosum var. crispum

Poterium spinosum var. crispum, commonly known as the hairy pincushion cactus, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features a compact, spiny structure with dense, woolly covering that helps reduce water loss. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the warmer months, adding a decorative touch to its rugged appearance. Its leaves are modified into spines, while the stems store water, allowing it to thrive in arid conditions.

This variety is prized for its unique texture and ability to adapt to dry environments.
