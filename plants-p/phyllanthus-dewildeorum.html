Phyllanthus dewildeorum

Phyllanthus dewildeorum, commonly known as the Dewilde's spurge, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family, which is characterized by its milky sap and often showy inflorescences. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub, reaching heights of up to 1 meter, with lance-shaped leaves that are glossy and dark green. Its flowers are small and clustered in cyathia, which are cup-like structures typical of the Euphorbiaceae family.

The plant is often found in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or savanna habitats.
