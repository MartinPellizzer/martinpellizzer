Pimelea paludosa var. mucronata

Pimelea paludosa var. mucronata, commonly known as the swamp pimelea, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Australia.

It is a low-growing shrub characterized by its narrow, needle-like leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant thrives in wetland or swampy environments, often found in coastal areas with sandy or muddy soils. Its flowering period typically occurs in spring, and it plays a role in supporting local insect populations.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal in native gardens.
