Piper tabernillanum var. tabernillanum

Piper tabernillanum var. tabernillanum, commonly known as the West African black pepper, is a climbing shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, peppery fruits that are used in traditional medicine and culinary applications. The plant produces small, dark purple berries that contain the essential oil responsible for its distinctive flavor and medicinal properties. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species in both ecological and economic contexts.
