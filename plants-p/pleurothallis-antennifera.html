Pleurothallis antennifera

Pleurothallis antennifera, commonly known as the feather-antler orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, pendulous inflorescences. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are small and often white or pale green, with intricate patterns that aid in pollination.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is a subject of interest for botanists and orchid enthusiasts alike.
