Prunus insititia var. subsylvestris

Prunus insititia var. subsylvestris, commonly known as the wild cherry or sand cherry, is a shrub or small tree native to North America.

It is characterized by its alternate, ovate leaves with serrated edges and a hairy texture. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, followed by edible, sweet-tart drupes. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or along stream banks.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
