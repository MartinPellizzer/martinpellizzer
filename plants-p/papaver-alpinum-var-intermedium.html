Papaver alpinum var. intermedium

Papaver alpinum var. intermedium, commonly known as the Alpine poppy, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It features showy, bright red or orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves that are typically hairy and covered in a waxy layer. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine meadows.

Due to its striking appearance, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine landscapes.
