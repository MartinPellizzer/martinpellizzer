Polyactium arenarium

Polyactium arenarium, commonly known as the sand dune aster, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of the United States.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and clusters of small, showy flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant thrives in sandy, well-drained soils and is often found in dune ecosystems. It plays a vital role in stabilizing sand dunes and providing habitat for various insects and small animals.

Due to its ecological importance, it is considered a valuable component of coastal plant communities.
