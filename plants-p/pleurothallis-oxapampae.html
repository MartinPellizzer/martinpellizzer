Pleurothallis oxapampae

Pleurothallis oxapampae, commonly known as the Oxapampa orchid, is a species native to the highlands of Peru.

It is a small, terrestrial orchid that typically grows in moist, shaded environments. The plant features delicate, green leaves and produces intricate, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pale yellow. It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is known for its unique morphology and ecological adaptations.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
