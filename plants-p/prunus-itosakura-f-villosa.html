Prunus itosakura f. villosa

Prunus itosakura f. villosa, commonly known as the hairy Japanese cherry, is a cultivar of the Prunus itosakura species, which belongs to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its hairy or pubescent leaves and stems, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers in early spring, which are followed by edible, sweet cherries. It is native to Japan and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its aesthetic appeal.

This variety thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
