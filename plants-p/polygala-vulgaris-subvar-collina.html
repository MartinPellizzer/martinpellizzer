Polygala vulgaris subvar. collina

Polygala vulgaris subvar. collina, commonly known as the roughleaf milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun. The plant features slender, branched stems and small, clustered flowers that are usually pink or purple in color. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, contributing to its rough texture.

It is often found in dry, open habitats and is valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators.
