Plantago aliena

Plantago aliena, commonly known as the common plantain, is a perennial herb native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It features broad, heart-shaped leaves with a rough texture and prominent veins. The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in a spike, which develop into capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and gardens.

Botanically, it is characterized by its robust growth and adaptability to various environmental conditions.
