Phyteuma corniculatum

Phyteuma corniculatum, commonly known as the horned poppy, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Central Asia.

It features striking, bright orange to red flowers that resemble horns, giving it its common name. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its vibrant blooms and distinctive seed heads.
