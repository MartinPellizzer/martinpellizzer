Prunus × persicoides

Prunus × persicoides, commonly known as the Chinese white pear, is a hybrid species within the Rosaceae family.

It is native to China and is characterized by its smooth, gray-barked trunk and broad, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in spring, followed by white or yellowish fruit that ripens in late summer. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit, which can be used in cooking.

This species is also valued in traditional Chinese medicine for its purported health benefits.
