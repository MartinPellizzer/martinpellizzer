Pilgerodendron uviferum

Pilgerodendron uviferum, commonly known as the Andean cedar, is a conifer native to the high-altitude regions of southern South America.

It is characterized by its slender, evergreen trunk and long, needle-like leaves that are arranged in spirals. The plant produces small, cone-like structures that contain its seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in cool, moist climates and is often found in cloud forests at elevations above 2,500 meters.

Due to its unique ecological role and slow growth rate, it is considered a valuable and vulnerable species in its native habitat.
