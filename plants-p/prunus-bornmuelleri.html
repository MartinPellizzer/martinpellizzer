Prunus bornmuelleri

Prunus bornmuelleri, commonly known as the Bornmueller cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and produces small, white flowers in early spring. The plant has elliptical leaves that are dark green on top and pale underneath, and it bears small, red or black berries in the autumn. Prunus bornmuelleri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its spring blossoms and autumn fruit.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it adaptable to a variety of climates.
