Potentilla corymbosa

Potentilla corymbosa, commonly known as the showy cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, featuring woody stems and alternate, palmately compound leaves. The plant produces showy, yellow flowers in clusters during the summer months, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soils, making it a versatile choice for gardens and natural landscapes.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species in ecological restoration projects.
