Phoebe angustata

Phoebe angustata, commonly known as narrowleaf phoebe, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Lauraceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like laurel and cinnamon. The plant typically grows as a small tree or shrub, reaching heights of up to 10 meters. Its leaves are elongated and have a smooth, glossy surface, while its fragrant flowers are small and clustered in panicles.

The fruit is a drupe, containing a single seed enclosed in a fleshy, red pulp.
