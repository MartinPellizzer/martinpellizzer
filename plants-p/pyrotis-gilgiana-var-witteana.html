Pyrotis gilgiana var. witteana

Pyrotis gilgiana var. witteana, commonly known as the Witteana pyrotis, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking, tubular flowers that range in color from red to orange. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 1 meter, with lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

The flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
