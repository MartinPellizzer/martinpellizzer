Prunella vulgaris subsp. laciniata

Prunella vulgaris subsp.

laciniata, commonly known as cut-leaved selfheal, is a perennial herb native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa. It features slender, upright stems that are often hairy, and its leaves are deeply lobed or cut into narrow segments, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The plant produces spikes of small, blue to purple flowers from late spring to early autumn. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its medicinal properties have been historically used in traditional remedies for various ailments.
