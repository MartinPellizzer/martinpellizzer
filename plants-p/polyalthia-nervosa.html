Polyalthia nervosa

Polyalthia nervosa, commonly known as the white flame tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, pale grey bark and its striking clusters of white, waxy flowers that bloom in summer. The leaves are simple, alternate, and have a serrated margin, while the fruits are small, woody capsules containing several seeds. It thrives in humid climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The plant is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated for its timber.
