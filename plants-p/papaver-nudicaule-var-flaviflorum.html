Papaver nudicaule var. flaviflorum

Papaver nudicaule var. flaviflorum, commonly known as the yellow poppy, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, often with a prominent black center. The plant produces smooth, hairy stems and has palmately lobed leaves that are typically green with a slightly velvety texture. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine displays.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated for its vibrant, early spring blooms.
