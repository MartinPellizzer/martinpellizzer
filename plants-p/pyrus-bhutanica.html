Pyrus bhutanica

Pyrus bhutanica, commonly known as the Bhutanese pear, is a deciduous tree native to the Himalayan region of Bhutan.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its broad, lobed leaves and clusters of small, white flowers. The tree produces edible fruit that is typically harvested in late summer, known for its sweet and aromatic flavor. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found at elevations between 1,500 and 3,000 meters.

Due to its ornamental value and fruit production, it is cultivated in both horticultural and ecological settings.
