Prunus communis f. macrocarpa

Prunus communis f. macrocarpa, commonly known as the large-fruited cherry, is a cultivated form of the common cherry.

It is characterized by its notably larger fruits compared to the typical Prunus communis. The plant is a deciduous shrub or small tree that produces sweet, juicy berries. It thrives in temperate climates and is often grown for its ornamental value and edible fruits.

The leaves are typically oval-shaped with a smooth margin and a glossy green surface.
