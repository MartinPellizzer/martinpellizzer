Pleurothallis welteri

Pleurothallis welteri, commonly known as the Welter's orchid, is a species of orchid native to the cloud forests of Central and South America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that often display a variety of colors. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees and deriving moisture and nutrients from the air and rain. Its leaves are usually narrow and arranged in a rosette pattern, and it produces a single spike of flowers during the growing season.

Pleurothallis welteri is appreciated for its ornamental value and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
