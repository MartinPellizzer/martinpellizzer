Picramnia locuples

Picramnia locuples, commonly known as the red sandalwood tree, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree that typically grows up to 15 meters in height. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a smooth, glossy surface. Its flowers are small and white, arranged in clusters, and it produces woody, capsule-like fruits containing several seeds.

The tree is valued for its hard, aromatic wood, which has been traditionally used in furniture and carving.
