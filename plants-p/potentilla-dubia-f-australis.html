Potentilla dubia f. australis

Potentilla dubia f. australis, commonly known as the Southern Bushwillow, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its dense, sprawling growth habit. The plant features small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are typically narrow and grey-green, adapted to arid conditions.

It is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is well-suited to dry, sunny environments.
