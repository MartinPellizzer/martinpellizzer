Parasenecio sadoensis

Parasenecio sadoensis, commonly known as the Sado Island ragwort, is a flowering plant native to Japan, specifically found on the island of Sado.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in coastal or mountainous regions. It has slender stems and hairy leaves, and its flowers are usually yellow or pale yellow in color.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species within its native habitat.
