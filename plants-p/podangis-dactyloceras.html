Podangis dactyloceras

Podangis dactyloceras, commonly known as the spiral orchid, is a rare and unique orchid species native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive spiral-shaped inflorescence, which sets it apart from other orchid species. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink, often arranged in a helical pattern along the stem. Its roots are adapted to absorb nutrients from the rich, moist soil of its natural habitat.

Due to habitat loss and over-collection, Podangis dactyloceras is considered endangered and is protected in many regions.
