Piranhea trifoliolata

Piranhea trifoliolata, commonly known as the three-leaf piranha plant, is a unique species native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three leaflets arranged in a fan-like pattern. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink, adding to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and requires partial to full sunlight for optimal growth.

Due to its distinctive foliage and compact size, it is often cultivated as a decorative plant in gardens and greenhouses.
