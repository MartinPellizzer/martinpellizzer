Piper curtipetiolum

Piper curtipetiolum, commonly known as the golden spice plant, is a member of the Piperaceae family, native to tropical regions of South America.

It is a climbing vine characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces small, berry-like fruits that are typically green when immature and turn red when mature. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of aromatic leaves used in traditional medicine.

The plant thrives in humid, warm climates and requires partial shade for optimal growth.
