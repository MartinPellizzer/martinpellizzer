Papaver simplicifolium

Papaver simplicifolium, commonly known as the simpleleaf poppy, is a species of flowering plant in the family Papaveraceae.

It is native to regions of Europe and parts of Asia, typically found in meadows and grasslands. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and showy, brightly colored flowers that bloom in spring. Its flowers are usually pink to red in color, with a distinctive crown-like structure.

The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, smooth seeds.
