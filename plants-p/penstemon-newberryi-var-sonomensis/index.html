Penstemon Newberryi Var. Sonomensis

Penstemon newberryi var. sonomensis is a unique and rare plant native to the mountainous regions of Washington state. This variety of penstemon is known for its striking purple and blue flowers that bloom in late spring. It thrives in rocky soils and requires well-drained conditions to flourish. The plant is often found in alpine meadows and is a valuable species for ecological studies. Its limited distribution makes it a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists alike.
