Pongamia pinnata var. hannii

Pongamia pinnata var. hannii, commonly known as the Indian beech or pongam tree, is a tropical leguminous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are pinnately arranged and often have a distinctive reddish or yellowish coloration. The plant produces small, yellow flowers followed by elongated, woody pods containing oily seeds. These seeds are rich in bioactive compounds and are used in traditional medicine and as a source of biofuel.

It is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it useful in soil enrichment and agroforestry systems.
