Piper lanceolatum var. mucronatum

Piper lanceolatum var. mucronatum, commonly known as the African black pepper, is a climbing shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces peppery fruits that are often used in traditional medicine and as a spice. Its botanical features include a woody stem, opposite leaf arrangement, and small, greenish-white flowers.

The varietal form, mucronatum, is distinguished by its acute, sharp tip on the leaf blade.
