Potentilla alsatica

Potentilla alsatica, commonly known as alpine potentilla, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is a low-growing shrub that typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features wiry stems and small, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are yellow, with five petals, and bloom in early summer.

Potentilla alsatica thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, alpine environments.
