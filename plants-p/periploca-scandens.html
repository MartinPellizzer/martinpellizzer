Periploca scandens

Periploca scandens, commonly known as the climbing periploca, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia and Africa.

It is a woody vine that typically grows along rocky surfaces or in shrublands. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are usually white or pale yellow. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

The fruit is a capsule that releases small, winged seeds when mature.
