Pourouma sapida

Pourouma sapida, commonly known as the "sweet areca" or "sugarcane palm," is a member of the Arecaceae family and is native to South America, particularly the Amazon Basin.

It is a tall, slender palm that can grow up to 20 meters in height, with pinnate leaves that are often used in traditional crafts. The plant is valued for its edible fruits, which have a sweet, juicy pulp similar to that of a date. Its fibrous trunk is also used for construction and thatching in some indigenous communities.

The plant thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.
