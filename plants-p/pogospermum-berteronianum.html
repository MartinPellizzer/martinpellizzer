Pogospermum berteronianum

Pogospermum berteronianum, commonly known as the African milkweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and opposite, ovate leaves with a hairy texture. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in grassland habitats.

Pogospermum berteronianum is known for its milky sap, which can be toxic if ingested.
