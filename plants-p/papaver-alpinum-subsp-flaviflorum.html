Papaver alpinum subsp. flaviflorum

Papaver alpinum subsp.

flaviflorum, commonly known as the Alpine poppy, is a flowering plant native to the alpine regions of Europe. It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers that contrast with its green, lance-shaped leaves.

The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes. It produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, dark seeds.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with well-drained soil.
