Payena sessilis

Payena sessilis, commonly known as the African ironwood, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, dark bark and dense, hard wood that is highly valued for its strength and durability. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are often elliptical in shape and have a smooth upper surface. It produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by small, woody fruits containing a single seed.

Payena sessilis is adapted to dry conditions and plays an important ecological role in its native habitats.
