Pelargonium glechomoides

Pelargonium glechomoides, commonly known as the creeping geranium, is a species of flowering plant in the family Geraniaceae.

It is native to South Africa and is characterized by its prostrate growth habit and hairy, oval-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or as a ground cover.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
