Pulsatilla vulgaris var. propera

Pulsatilla vulgaris var. propera, commonly known as the meadow anemone, is a perennial herb native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It features a short, thick rhizome and produces showy, cup-shaped flowers in shades of pink or white. The plant has palmate, lobed leaves that are hairy and typically basal. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and woodland edges.

The flowers bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
