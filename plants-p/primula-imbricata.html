Primula imbricata

Primula imbricata, commonly known as the alpine primrose, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Asia, particularly in the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils at high altitudes, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are typically basal, rosette-forming, and covered in a dense layer of fine hairs.

Primula imbricata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in alpine gardens for its resilience and beauty.
