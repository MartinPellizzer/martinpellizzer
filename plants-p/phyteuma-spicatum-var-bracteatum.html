Phyteuma spicatum var. bracteatum

Phyteuma spicatum var. bracteatum, commonly known as the bracteated phyteuma, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of bell-shaped flowers. The leaves are lance-shaped and slightly hairy, while the bracts are prominent and often mistaken for petals. This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens and its ability to thrive in well-drained soils.

It typically blooms in late summer, adding a splash of color to the landscape.
