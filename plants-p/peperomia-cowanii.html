Peperomia cowanii

Peperomia cowanii, commonly known as the Shiny Peperomia, is a compact, evergreen perennial plant native to South America.

It features thick, waxy leaves that are typically dark green with a glossy texture, often adorned with silver or white veins. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters, usually in shades of green or red, which are not particularly showy. It thrives in bright, indirect light and prefers well-draining soil with moderate moisture.

Peperomia cowanii is popular as a houseplant due to its low maintenance and attractive foliage.
