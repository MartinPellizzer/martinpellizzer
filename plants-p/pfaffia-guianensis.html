Pfaffia guianensis

Pfaffia guianensis, commonly known as curare plant, is a climbing shrub native to the rainforests of South America.

It belongs to the family Araceae and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and distinctive spadix inflorescence. The plant is known for producing a toxic sap that has been historically used in the preparation of curare, a paralytic agent. Its botanical significance lies in its unique chemical composition and traditional medicinal uses.

Pfaffia guianensis is also valued for its potential in modern pharmacological research due to its bioactive compounds.
