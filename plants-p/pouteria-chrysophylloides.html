Pouteria chrysophylloides

Pouteria chrysophylloides, commonly known as the golden-leaf pouteria, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are typically a vibrant green, sometimes with a golden tint on the underside. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are usually white or pale yellow, followed by edible fruits resembling small, round cherries. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists alike.
