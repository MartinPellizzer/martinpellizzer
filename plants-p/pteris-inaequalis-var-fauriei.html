Pteris inaequalis var. fauriei

Pteris inaequalis var. fauriei, commonly known as the Faurie's water fern, is a variant of the common water fern, characterized by its distinctive fronds and adaptations to aquatic environments.

It is a member of the family Pteridaceae and is native to regions in Asia, including China and Japan. The plant features bipinnate fronds with a serrated margin, and its rhizomes are often submerged in water. It thrives in moist, shaded areas and is often found in ponds, ditches, and wetlands.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in aquatic ecosystems.
