Paspalum villosissimum

Paspalum villosissimum, commonly known as the silky paspalum, is a perennial grass native to South America.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and narrow, bluish-green leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers, typical of the Poaceae family. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along waterways.

Due to its ornamental value and ability to stabilize soil, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and restoration projects.
