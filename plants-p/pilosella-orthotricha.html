Pilosella orthotricha

Pilosella orthotricha, commonly known as the hairy leaf groundsel, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of hairy, gray-green leaves and produces small, yellowish flower heads that bloom in the summer. Native to Europe and parts of Asia, this species thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. The plant's structure includes a short, stout stem with leaves that are often covered in fine, white hairs.

Its flowers are typically arranged in compact clusters, contributing to its ornamental appeal in horticultural settings.
