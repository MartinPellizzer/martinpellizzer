Parthenium pumilum

Parthenium pumilum, commonly known as the smallleaf fleabane, is a small annual herbaceous plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. The plant features narrow, hairy leaves and produces small, yellowish to white composite flowers that attract various pollinators. Its flowering period usually occurs in late spring to early summer.

Parthenium pumilum is adapted to arid environments and is often found in regions with low precipitation.
