Plectranthus secundiflorus

Plectranthus secundiflorus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved plectranthus, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are usually covered in a fine layer of hairs. Its flowers are small, tubular, and typically arranged in clusters, blooming in shades of purple or violet.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
