Potamogeton pectinatus subvar. vaginatus

Potamogeton pectinatus subvar. vaginatus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater ecosystems.

It is characterized by its long, narrow leaves that are often ribbon-like in appearance, and it typically grows in shallow, slow-moving waters. The plant produces small, green flowers that are clustered in spikelets, and it is often found in wetlands, lakes, and rivers. Its rhizomes help stabilize the sediment, contributing to the ecological structure of its habitat.

This species plays an important role in providing habitat for aquatic invertebrates and fish.
