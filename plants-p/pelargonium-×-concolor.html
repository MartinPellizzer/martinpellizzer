Pelargonium × concolor

Pelargonium × concolor, commonly known as the spotted geranium, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Geraniaceae family.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves with distinctive red or purple spots and margins. The plant produces clusters of small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Native to South Africa, it thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight.

Pelargonium × concolor is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its attractive foliage and flowering habits.
