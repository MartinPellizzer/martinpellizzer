Poa cheesemanii

Poa cheesemanii, commonly known as the Cheeseman's bentgrass, is a perennial grass species native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and dark green, narrow leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, two-lobed flowers. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in mountainous regions.

Poa cheesemanii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in native grassland ecosystems.
