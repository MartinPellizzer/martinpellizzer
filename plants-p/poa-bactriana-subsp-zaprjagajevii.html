Poa bactriana subsp. zaprjagajevii

Poa bactriana subsp.

zaprjagajevii, commonly known as the Kyzylkum grass, is a perennial grass species native to Central Asia. It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves.

The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in sandy or gravelly soils. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences with pale blue to greenish flowers.

This subspecies is adapted to withstand harsh environmental conditions, making it an important component of steppe and desert ecosystems.
