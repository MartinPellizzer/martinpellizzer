Penstemon × princeps

Penstemon × princeps, commonly known as the tall beardtongue, is a hybrid perennial plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and showy, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to purple. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun, making it well-suited for xeriscaping. Its foliage consists of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of hair.

Penstemon × princeps is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
