Plazia argentea

Plazia argentea, commonly known as the silver leaf plant, is a perennial shrub native to the temperate regions of southern Europe.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which results from dense covering of fine, white hairs on the leaves. The plant produces small, clustered white flowers in the spring, which are followed by spherical, blue-black berries. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Due to its attractive appearance and low maintenance requirements, Plazia argentea is often used in landscape design and as a decorative plant in gardens.
