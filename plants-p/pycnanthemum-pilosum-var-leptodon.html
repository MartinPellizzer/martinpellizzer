Pycnanthemum pilosum var. leptodon

Pycnanthemum pilosum var. leptodon, commonly known as slenderleaf goldenrod, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist to wet soils in open woodlands and along stream banks. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, yellowish-green flowers in late summer. It is characterized by its distinctive, aromatic foliage and is often used in native plant gardens.

This variety is part of the Asteraceae family, which includes many other flowering plants.
