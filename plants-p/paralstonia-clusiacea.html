Paralstonia clusiacea

Paralstonia clusiacea, commonly known as the flame tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, bright red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters, making it a popular ornamental plant. The plant belongs to the family Clusiaceae, also known as the guttiferae family. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a smooth texture, while its bark is thick and often has a reddish hue.

Paralstonia clusiacea thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its vibrant floral display.
