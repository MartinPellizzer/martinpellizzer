Paeonia kavachensis var. coriifolia

Paeonia kavachensis var. coriifolia, commonly known as the Kashmir peony, is a rare and ornamental plant native to the Himalayan region of India.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and red, often with a distinctive double bloom. The plant has lance-shaped, dark green leaves that are glossy and arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in cool, moist climates and prefers well-drained, loamy soil.

Due to its limited distribution and ornamental value, it is considered a valuable species in botanical gardens and conservation efforts.
