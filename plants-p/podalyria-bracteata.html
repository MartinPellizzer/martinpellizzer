Podalyria bracteata

Podalyria bracteata, commonly known as the bracteate podalyria, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and showy, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in the Andes region. It produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
