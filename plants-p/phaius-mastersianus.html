Phaius mastersianus

Phaius mastersianus, commonly known as the Masters' marsh orchid, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and showy, violet-blue flowers. The plant typically grows in wetlands and marshy areas, where it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils. Its leaves are elongated and grass-like, while its inflorescence consists of a raceme of fragrant, spurred flowers.

Phaius mastersianus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and wetland restoration projects.
