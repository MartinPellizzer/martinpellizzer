Papaver alpinum subvar. roseolum

Papaver alpinum subvar. roseolum, commonly known as the alpine poppy, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Central Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that range in color from white to deep red, often with a distinctive red or purple blotch at the base. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils and full sunlight. Its leaves are palmately lobed and covered in a fine, silky layer of hairs.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
