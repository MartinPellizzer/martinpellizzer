Pelargonium fulgidum

Pelargonium fulgidum, commonly known as the silver-leaf geranium, is a species of flowering plant in the family Geraniaceae.

It is native to South Africa and is characterized by its striking silver-gray foliage and showy, five-petaled flowers that range in color from pink to red. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub, reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters, and thrives in well-drained soil with full sun exposure. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and ease of care.

Pelargonium fulgidum is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
