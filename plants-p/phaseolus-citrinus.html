Phaseolus citrinus

Phaseolus citrinus, commonly known as the yellow bean or golden bean, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to the Americas and is characterized by its yellow pods and small, edible seeds. The plant typically grows as an annual vine, reaching heights of up to 3 meters, with trifoliate leaves and yellow flowers. Its botanical features include a fibrous root system and a climbing habit, which are adaptations for its native habitat.

The seeds are often used in traditional cuisines and have been cultivated for their nutritional value.
