Pachycarpus schumannii

Pachycarpus schumannii, commonly known as the Schumann's thick-leafed plant, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, leathery leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss in arid environments. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in spring. Its fruit is a small, spherical capsule that splits open to release seeds.

This species is well adapted to dry conditions and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.
