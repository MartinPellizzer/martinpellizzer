Piper viridistachyum

Piper viridistachyum, commonly known as green-stemmed pepper plant, is a tropical vine belonging to the Piperaceae family.

It is native to regions of Southeast Asia, including parts of India and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its slender, green stems and opposite, heart-shaped leaves. It produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that develop into elongated, cylindrical fruits.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in some local cultures.
