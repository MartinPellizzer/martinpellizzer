Primula mandarina

Primula mandarina, commonly known as the orange primrose, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of southern China.

It features upright stems that bear clusters of fragrant, orange-yellow flowers resembling small mandarins, hence its common name. The leaves are oval, dark green, and slightly hairy, with a leathery texture. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.

The plant is also valued in traditional Chinese medicine for its purported health benefits.
