Polygonum longipes

Polygonum longipes, commonly known as the long-stemmed knotweed, is a perennial herb native to regions of Asia, including China and Nepal.

It is characterized by its stout, somewhat woody stems that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically borne on elongated inflorescences. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a rough texture, with prominent veins.

Polygonum longipes is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or stream banks.
