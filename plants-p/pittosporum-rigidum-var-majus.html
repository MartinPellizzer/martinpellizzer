Pittosporum rigidum var. majus

Pittosporum rigidum var. majus, commonly known as the broadleaf pittosporum, is a shrub or small tree native to New Zealand.

It features lance-shaped, dark green leaves that are often glossy and have a leathery texture. The plant produces fragrant, white flowers that are clustered in terminal panicles, which bloom in spring. The fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release small, black seeds.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental qualities and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types.
