Panicum retiglume

Panicum retiglume, commonly known as the narrow-leaved panic grass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features slender, erect stems that can grow up to 1.5 meters in height, with narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences, which are typically arranged in a panicle structure. Its leaves are characterized by a distinctive, narrow shape and a slightly rough texture.

Panicum retiglume is often found in disturbed soils and is adapted to thrive in full sun conditions.
