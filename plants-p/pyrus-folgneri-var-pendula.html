Pyrus folgneri var. pendula

Pyrus folgneri var. pendula, commonly known as the weeping pear, is a cultivar of the European pear tree characterized by its cascading branches that droop gracefully from the main trunk.

It features oval, dark green leaves that provide a lush backdrop to its clusters of white, fragrant flowers in early spring. The plant produces small, edible pears that are typically harvested in late summer or early autumn. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil and full sun for optimal growth.

This ornamental tree is often used in gardens for its aesthetic appeal and mild fragrance.
