Pilosella × dutartrei

Pilosella × dutartrei, commonly known as the creeping bellflower, is a hybrid species within the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit and produces small, daisy-like flowers with yellow centers and white petals. The plant typically thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks. Its leaves are usually hairy, giving it a distinctive appearance, and it is known for its ability to spread vegetatively through stolons.

Pilosella × dutartrei is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
