Peperomia nigro-oculata

Peperomia nigro-oculata, commonly known as the Black-Eyed Peperomia, is a compact, low-growing plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It features thick, waxy leaves that are typically dark green with a striking black or dark purple central vein, giving it its distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually green or reddish in color, which are not particularly showy. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Peperomia nigro-oculata is a popular choice for indoor gardening due to its ornamental foliage and ease of care.
