Primula chionantha subsp. melanops

Primula chionantha subsp.

melanops, commonly known as the silver-tipped primrose, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. This subspecies is characterized by its striking, pale violet to white flowers with a distinctive silvery sheen on the petals.

The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, preferring well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade. Its foliage consists of broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery down.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is occasionally cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
