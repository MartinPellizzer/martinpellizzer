Potentilla gaudinii proles sarajevensis

Potentilla gaudinii proles sarajevensis, commonly known as the Sarajevo potentilla, is a low-growing shrub native to the Balkan region.

It features small, rounded leaves and produces clusters of yellow flowers in late spring to early summer. The plant thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to temperate climates. It is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats, growing at elevations between 1,200 and 2,000 meters.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species within its native range.
