Potentilla alpestris var. typicabaldensis

Potentilla alpestris var. typicabaldensis, commonly known as the alpine cinquefoil, is a hardy shrub native to mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in alpine and subalpine habitats, thriving in well-drained, rocky soils. The plant features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its leaves are alternate, palmately lobed, and covered in fine, soft hairs.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience in harsh environmental conditions.
