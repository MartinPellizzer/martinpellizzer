Picea excelsa f. semierosa

Picea excelsa f. semierosa, commonly known as the slender white spruce, is a subspecies of the white spruce native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its narrow, conical shape and silvery-white bark that becomes more pronounced with age. The foliage consists of sharp, needle-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color, providing a striking contrast against the white bark. This subspecies is adapted to high-altitude environments, thriving in cool, moist conditions with well-drained soil.

Its unique appearance and ecological significance make it a notable species in alpine and subalpine ecosystems.
