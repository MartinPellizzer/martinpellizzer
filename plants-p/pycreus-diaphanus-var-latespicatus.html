Pycreus diaphanus var. latespicatus

Pycreus diaphanus var. latespicatus, commonly known as the golden bell orchid, is a rare and striking member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to the subtropical regions of Southeast Asia, particularly Thailand and Malaysia, where it thrives in shaded, humid environments. The plant is characterized by its elongated, pendant inflorescences that bear clusters of golden-yellow, bell-shaped flowers. Its leaves are lanceolate and often have a waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss in its natural habitat.

Due to its rarity and ornamental value, it is highly sought after by horticulturists and conservationists alike.
