Potentilla durangensis

Potentilla durangensis, commonly known as the Durango cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Mexico.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit. The plant typically features small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the spring, attracting pollinators such as bees. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Potentilla durangensis is adapted to rocky and well-drained soils, thriving in high-altitude environments.
