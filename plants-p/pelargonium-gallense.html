Pelargonium gallense

Pelargonium gallense, commonly known as the African geranium, is a species of flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant features palmate leaves with five to seven lobes and produces showy, clustered flowers in shades of pink, red, and white. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and containers.

Pelargonium gallense is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various growing conditions.
