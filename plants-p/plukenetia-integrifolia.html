Plukenetia integrifolia

Plukenetia integrifolia, commonly known as the African date palm, is a tropical shrub native to Africa and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its palm-like appearance, with feather-like leaves and small, yellow fruits that resemble dates. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in coastal areas and riverine forests. Its fruits are edible and have a sweet, nutty flavor, making them a valuable food source.

The plant also has historical significance in traditional medicine and agriculture.
