Patagonium parvifolium

Patagonium parvifolium, commonly known as the small-leaved Patagonian plant, is a flowering species native to the southern regions of South America.

It typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to the harsh climate of Patagonia. The plant features small, narrow leaves and produces clusters of tiny, white or pale yellow flowers. It is often found in open grasslands and along the edges of shrublands.

Due to its limited distribution and specific environmental requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
