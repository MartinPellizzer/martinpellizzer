Pyrrosia lanuginosa

Pyrrosia lanuginosa, commonly known as the flannel fern, is a species of epiphytic fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its soft, velvety fronds covered in fine, hairy structures that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows on trees or rocks in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are leathery and often have a bluish-green hue.

Pyrrosia lanuginosa is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in low-light conditions.
