Piper tamayoanum

Piper tamayoanum, commonly known as the Tamayo's pepper, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of Central and South America, particularly found in countries like Colombia and Ecuador. The plant is characterized by its vine-like growth habit, with compound leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. Its small, greenish-yellow flowers are followed by berries that contain the aromatic seeds used in culinary and medicinal applications.

The plant's botanical significance lies in its potential pharmacological properties and its role in local ecosystems.
