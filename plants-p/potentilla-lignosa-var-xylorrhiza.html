Potentilla lignosa var. xylorrhiza

Potentilla lignosa var. xylorrhiza, commonly known as the shrub cinquefoil, is a perennial plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its woody stems and compound leaves with five leaflets. The plant produces yellow flowers in late spring to early summer, which are clustered in racemes. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Its botanical features make it a hardy and adaptable species suitable for xeriscaping and native plant gardens.
