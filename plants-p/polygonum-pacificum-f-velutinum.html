Polygonum pacificum f. velutinum

Polygonum pacificum f. velutinum, commonly known as the velvetleaf smartweed, is a variant of the common smartweed.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its soft, velvety leaves that give it its common name. The plant typically grows in moist soils along streambanks and wetlands, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its small, pinkish to reddish flowers appear in dense clusters during the summer months.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and its ability to thrive in a variety of wetland environments.
