Proboscidea parviflora var. hohokamiana

Proboscidea parviflora var. hohokamiana, commonly known as the Hohokamian bottle-gourd, is a variant of the bottle-gourd plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its elongated, cylindrical fruit that can grow up to several feet in length. The plant is a member of the Cucurbitaceae family, which includes other gourd species. Its leaves are large, heart-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.
