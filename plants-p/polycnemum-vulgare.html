Polycnemum vulgare

Polycnemum vulgare, commonly known as common bladderwrack, is a perennial seaweed belonging to the family Fucaceae.

It is characterized by its dark brown, leathery fronds that are often covered in small, bladder-like structures called bladders, which help it float. This plant thrives in intertidal zones along rocky coastlines, where it attaches itself to rocks using a holdfast. It plays an important role in marine ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various marine organisms.

Polycnemum vulgare is also used in traditional medicine and as a source of alginates for industrial applications.
