Piper puberulum

Piper puberulum, commonly known as the hairy pepper vine, is a climbing shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers. Its fruits are small berries that turn red when mature, and they are often used in traditional medicine.

The plant's botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and pharmacological studies.
