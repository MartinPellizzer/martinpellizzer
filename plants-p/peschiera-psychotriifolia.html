Peschiera psychotriifolia

Peschiera psychotriifolia, commonly known as the ghost flower, is a rare and enigmatic plant species native to the alpine regions of South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, translucent petals that resemble ghostly apparitions, hence its common name. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that bloom during the late summer months. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a waxy coating that helps reduce water loss in its high-altitude habitat.

Due to its rarity and unique morphology, Peschiera psychotriifolia is of great interest to botanists and conservationists.
