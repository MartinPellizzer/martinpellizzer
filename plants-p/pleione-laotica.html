Pleione laotica

Pleione laotica, commonly known as the Laotian pleione, is a rare and striking orchid native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, upright stem and large, showy flowers that typically bloom in spring. The plant has broad, lance-shaped leaves and produces intricate, fragrant blooms with a mix of white and pink hues. It thrives in cool, shaded environments with high humidity, often growing at elevations above 1,500 meters.

Due to habitat loss and over-collection, it is considered endangered and is protected in several countries.
