Prunus padus f. rufoferruginea

Prunus padus f. rufoferruginea, commonly known as the rusty-hued wild cherry, is a botanical variant of the common wild cherry.

It is characterized by its distinctive reddish-brown bark and leaves that often display a rusty hue. This subspecies is native to parts of Europe and is typically found in moist, wooded areas. The flowers are small and white, appearing in early spring, followed by edible, dark red berries.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
