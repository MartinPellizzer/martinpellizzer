Peuce rubra

Peuce rubra, commonly known as the red peuce, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking red flowers that bloom in clusters, adding a vibrant touch to its natural habitat. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest understories, where it can thrive. Its leaves are broad and slightly lobed, with a smooth texture that contrasts with its showy blooms.

Peuce rubra is valued not only for its ornamental appeal but also for its potential medicinal properties in traditional herbal practices.
