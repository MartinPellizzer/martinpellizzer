Panicum sabiense

Panicum sabiense, commonly known as the African grass or red panicgrass, is a species of grass native to Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces dense, fluffy panicles that are often bright red or pink in color. The plant is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it suitable for ornamental use in gardens. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a fibrous texture, and it is often used in landscaping for its aesthetic appeal.

Panicum sabiense is also valued for its ability to stabilize soil and prevent erosion in arid environments.
