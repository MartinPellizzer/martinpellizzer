Pothos miniatus

Pothos miniatus, commonly known as the golden pothos, is a tropical vine native to Southeast Asia.

It features heart-shaped, waxy leaves that are typically bright green with a subtle golden variegation. The plant is known for its ability to thrive in low-light conditions and requires minimal care, making it a popular houseplant. Its stems can climb or trail, and it produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers.

Pothos miniatus is also noted for its air-purifying qualities and ability to remove toxins from indoor environments.
