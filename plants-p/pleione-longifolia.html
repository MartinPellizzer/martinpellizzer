Pleione longifolia

Pleione longifolia, commonly known as the long-flowered pleione, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its sympodial growth habit and produces showy, fragrant flowers with a distinctive spurred lip. The plant typically grows at elevations between 1,500 and 3,000 meters, where it thrives in cool, moist environments. Its leaves are broad and grass-like, and it relies on mycorrhizal fungi for nutrient uptake.

The species is appreciated for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in shaded, humid gardens.
