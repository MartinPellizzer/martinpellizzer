Pseudoglycine

Pseudoglycine, commonly known as the false glycine, is a flowering plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale purple. Pseudoglycine is native to parts of Asia and is often found in moist, shaded environments.

It is valued in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
