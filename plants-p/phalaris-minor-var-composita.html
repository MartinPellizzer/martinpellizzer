Phalaris minor var. composita

Phalaris minor var. composita, commonly known as the slender false brome, is a perennial grass native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and can be found in meadows, dunes, and along roadsides. The plant has slender, upright stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often hairy. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets arranged in a panicle, producing small, pale green to brown flowers.

It plays a role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for various insects and small animals.
