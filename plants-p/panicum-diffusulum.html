Panicum diffusulum

Panicum diffusulum, commonly known as the slender panicgrass, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, sandy soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks. The plant has thin, wiry stems and small, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its inflorescence consists of a loose cluster of spikelets, which produce tiny, wind-dispersed seeds.

Due to its habitat preferences, it plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
