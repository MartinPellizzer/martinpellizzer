Pabstiella robertoi

Pabstiella robertoi, commonly known as the Robert's pabstiella, is a rare and distinctive member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its unique morphology, featuring a compact, sympodial growth habit with a short rhizome. The plant produces striking, often colorful flowers that are typically borne on short pedicels. Its leaves are usually elliptical or ovate, with a smooth surface and a waxy texture.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, Pabstiella robertoi is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
