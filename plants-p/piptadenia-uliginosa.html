Piptadenia uliginosa

Piptadenia uliginosa, commonly known as the black beauty or black bean tree, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, compound leaves and showy yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces long, slender pods containing dark, glossy seeds that are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in moist, fertile soils and is often found in lowland tropical forests.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some economic importance in local communities.
