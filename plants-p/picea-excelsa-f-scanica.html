Picea excelsa f. scanica

Picea excelsa f. scanica, commonly known as the Scandinavian tall spruce, is a subspecies of the white spruce native to northern Europe.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and conical shape, often reaching heights of over 30 meters. The foliage consists of needle-like leaves that are dark green and arranged in spirals along the branches. It thrives in cool, moist climates and is typically found in boreal forests.

This subspecies is noted for its slow growth and resilience to harsh environmental conditions.
