Pittiera longipedunculata

Pittiera longipedunculata, commonly known as the long pedunculate pittiera, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows at elevations between 2,000 and 4,000 meters, where it thrives in cool, moist environments. Its leaves are dark green, leathery, and arranged in whorls around the stem, while its flowers are usually pink or white, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Pittiera longipedunculata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique appearance.
