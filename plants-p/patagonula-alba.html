Patagonula alba

Patagonula alba, commonly known as the white patagonian dandelion, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of silver-green leaves and bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in high-altitude regions. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.

Patagonula alba is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional medicine for its anti-inflammatory effects.
