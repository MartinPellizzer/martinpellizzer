Pyrus nivalis f. tokajensis

Pyrus nivalis f. tokajensis, commonly known as the Tokaj white pear, is a botanical variant of the European white pear.

It is characterized by its small, white flowers and round, yellowish fruits that ripen in late summer. This form is native to the Tokaj region in Hungary, where it has been cultivated for centuries. The plant is typically deciduous, with slender, upright branches and a compact growth habit.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical significance in viticulture and horticulture.
