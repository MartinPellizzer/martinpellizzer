Picris japonica subsp. japonica

Picris japonica subsp.

japonica, commonly known as Japanese groundsel, is a flowering plant native to East Asia. It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

The plant features basal rosettes of lance-shaped leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers in clusters. Its flowering period occurs from late spring to early autumn, and it is often found in the understory of temperate forests.

The species is characterized by its hairy stems and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
