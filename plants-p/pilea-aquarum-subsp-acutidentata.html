Pilea aquarum subsp. acutidentata

Pilea aquarum subsp.

acutidentata, commonly known as the Water Cabbage. This plant is a herbaceous species native to tropical regions, characterized by its broad, waxy leaves that are often submerged or floating in water.

It belongs to the Araceae family, which is known for its unique inflorescence structures. The leaves are typically heart-shaped and can grow quite large, contributing to its aesthetic appeal in aquatic environments.

It is often cultivated as a decorative plant in ponds and water gardens.
