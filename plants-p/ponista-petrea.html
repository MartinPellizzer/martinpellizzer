Ponista petrea

Ponista petrea, commonly known as the silver leaf plant, is a species native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, silver-green leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its leaves are used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties.
