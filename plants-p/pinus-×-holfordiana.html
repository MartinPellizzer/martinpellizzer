Pinus × holfordiana

Pinus × holfordiana, commonly known as the Holford's pine, is a hybrid conifer resulting from the cross between Pinus nigra and Pinus sylvestris.

It is characterized by its needle-like leaves, which are typically arranged in bundles of two, and its distinctive cones that mature to a reddish-brown color. The tree is native to the Pyrenees region and thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soils. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance and hardy nature.

The species is also valued for its ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
