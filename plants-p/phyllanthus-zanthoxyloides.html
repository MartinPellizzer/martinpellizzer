Phyllanthus zanthoxyloides

Phyllanthus zanthoxyloides, commonly known as the golden flame tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, opposite, ovate leaves that are usually dark green with a slightly waxy texture. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, followed by orange to red fruits that are capsule-like in structure. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in coastal areas or near rivers.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
