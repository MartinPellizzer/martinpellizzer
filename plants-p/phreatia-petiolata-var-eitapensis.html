Phreatia petiolata var. eitapensis

Phreatia petiolata var. eitapensis, commonly known as the Eitapensis orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial orchid characterized by its sympodial growth habit and elongated, fleshy pseudobulbs. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pale yellow, typically during the rainy season. It thrives in humid, shaded environments with well-drained soil.

Its botanical significance lies in its unique morphology and ecological adaptation to its native habitat.
