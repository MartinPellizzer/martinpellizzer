Pinanga auriculata

Pinanga auriculata, commonly known as the ear-shaped fern, is a species of flowering plant in the family Araceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its distinctive, palm-like leaves that are often adorned with a waxy coating. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically hidden within a spadix, a common feature in the Araceae family. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.

Pinanga auriculata is valued both for its ornamental appeal and its ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
