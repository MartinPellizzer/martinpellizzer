Polygonatum biflorum subvar. ellipsoidale

Polygonatum biflorum subvar. ellipsoidale, commonly known as the elliptic Solomon's seal, is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its smooth, cylindrical stems and lanceolate leaves.

It produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers in late spring, which are followed by red, berry-like fruits. The plant is native to eastern Asia and thrives in shaded, moist woodland environments. Its rhizomatous root system allows it to spread vegetatively, forming dense colonies.

The elliptic shape of its leaves and fruits gives it its scientific name, reflecting its distinctive morphology.
