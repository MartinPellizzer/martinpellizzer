Passiflora mixta var. subquinqueloba

Passiflora mixta var. subquinqueloba, commonly known as the Mexican passionflower, is a climbing vine native to Central America.

It features striking, palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, often with a distinctive waxy coating. The flowers are showy, with five sepals, five petals, and a complex crown of filaments. It produces edible berries that are typically yellow or orange when ripe.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and fragrance.
