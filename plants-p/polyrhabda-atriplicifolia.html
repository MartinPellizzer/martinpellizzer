Polyrhabda atriplicifolia

Polyrhabda atriplicifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leafed sunflower, is a herbaceous plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers in a composite head, typical of the Asteraceae family, which attract various pollinators. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in dry, open habitats.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and native landscaping.
