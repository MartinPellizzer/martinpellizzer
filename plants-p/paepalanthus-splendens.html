Paepalanthus splendens

Paepalanthus splendens, commonly known as the splendour cactus, is a species native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a member of the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its striking, vibrant flowers that bloom in shades of red, yellow, and orange. The plant has a short, cylindrical stem covered in spines and produces fleshy, ribbed leaves that help it retain water in arid environments. It thrives in full sun and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its unique appearance and seasonal blooming make it a visually appealing addition to botanical collections.
