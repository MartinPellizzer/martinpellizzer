Protea rhodantha

Protea rhodantha, commonly known as the red-stemmed protea, is a striking member of the Proteaceae family native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, branched stems and large, showy inflorescences that resemble a cluster of flowers. The plant produces vibrant pink to red bracts that enclose numerous small, tubular flowers, which are typically white or pale pink. It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Protea rhodantha is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens and floral arrangements.
