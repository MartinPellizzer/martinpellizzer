Phthirusa theloneura

Phthirusa theloneura, commonly known as the white-flowered orchid, is a species of orchid native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in a spiral pattern around the stem. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained, acidic soil for optimal growth.

Phthirusa theloneura is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
