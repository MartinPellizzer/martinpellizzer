Ptarmica olympica

Ptarmica olympica, commonly known as the Olympic mountain mint, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically white or purple, arranged in whorled clusters. Its leaves are often hairy and have a strong mint-like scent when crushed.

Ptarmica olympica is often found in rocky or grassy habitats at higher elevations.
