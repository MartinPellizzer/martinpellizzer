Phaseoloides piscidia

Phaseoloides piscidia, commonly known as the water bean vine, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is a member of the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod-like fruits. The plant typically grows as a climbing vine, often found in moist, shaded environments. Its flowers are small and usually white or pale yellow, while the seeds are enclosed in elongated pods.

The species is noted for its ability to thrive in wet conditions, making it a common weed in agricultural areas.
