Phaseolus scaberulus

Phaseolus scaberulus, commonly known as the rough-pod bean, is a species of legume native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, spiny pods and small, kidney-shaped seeds. The plant typically grows as an annual herb, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its leaves are trifoliate, with each leaflet having a rough, textured surface.

Phaseolus scaberulus is often found in disturbed habitats and is adapted to thrive in tropical and subtropical climates.
