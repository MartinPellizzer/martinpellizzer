Pinus sylvestris var. novocaesariensis

Pinus sylvestris var. novocaesariensis, commonly known as the Scots pine, is a variant of the Scots pine species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a coniferous evergreen tree characterized by its long, needle-like leaves and cones. The tree typically grows in a variety of habitats, including forests, open landscapes, and mountainous regions. It has a slow growth rate and can live for several centuries, making it an important component of many ecosystems.

The bark is thick and scaly, providing protection against environmental stressors.
