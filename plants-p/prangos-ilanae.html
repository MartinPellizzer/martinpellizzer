Prangos ilanae

Prangos ilanae, commonly known as the Ilanae thistle, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and woolly, silver-gray foliage. The plant typically grows in rocky and arid environments, showcasing adaptations to harsh, dry climates. Its flowers are usually pale blue to purple, and it produces small, spiny fruits that aid in seed dispersal.

Prangos ilanae is valued for its ornamental appeal and has limited uses in traditional medicine.
