Primula auricula var. bauhinii

Primula auricula var. bauhinii, commonly known as the auricled primrose, is a hybrid variety of the primrose family.

It is characterized by its distinctive, heart-shaped leaves that resemble small ears, giving it its common name. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, violet-blue flowers with yellow centers, typically blooming in early spring. Native to the mountainous regions of Central Europe, it thrives in cool, moist environments.

It is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
