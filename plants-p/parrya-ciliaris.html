Parrya ciliaris

Parrya ciliaris, commonly known as the Sierra foothill dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. It produces a basal rosette of hairy, spoon-shaped leaves and has a flowering period in the spring.

The species is notable for its adaptability to arid conditions and its role in supporting local pollinator populations.
