Peperomia guamana

Peperomia guamana, commonly known as the Guamana Peperomia, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It features thick, waxy leaves that are typically dark green with a glossy surface, often adorned with subtle variegation. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters, which are usually greenish-white in color. It thrives in well-draining soil and prefers bright, indirect light with moderate humidity.

Peperomia guamana is a popular choice for indoor gardening due to its low maintenance and attractive foliage.
