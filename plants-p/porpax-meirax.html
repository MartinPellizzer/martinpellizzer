Porpax meirax

Porpax meirax, commonly known as the golden bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, typically blooming in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along its stem. Its root system is fibrous, allowing it to thrive in well-drained, rocky soils.

Porpax meirax is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and botanical collections.
