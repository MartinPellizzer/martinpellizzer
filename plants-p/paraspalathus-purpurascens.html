Paraspalathus purpurascens

Paraspalathus purpurascens, commonly known as the purple-flowered spathaceous plant, is a unique member of the Araceae family.

It is characterized by its striking purple bracts that enclose the small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors or stream banks. Its morphology includes a short, thick rhizome and a single, leafy stem.

The inflorescence consists of a spathe and a spadix, typical of the Araceae family, with the spathe often displaying vibrant colors to attract pollinators.
