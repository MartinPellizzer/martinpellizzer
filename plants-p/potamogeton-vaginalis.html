Potamogeton vaginalis

Potamogeton vaginalis, commonly known as the narrow-leaf pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in North America.

It has long, slender leaves that are typically less than 1 millimeter wide, arranged in whorls around the stem. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually found in clusters near the water's surface. Its roots form dense mats that help stabilize sediments and provide habitat for aquatic organisms.

Potamogeton vaginalis plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by contributing to water clarity and supporting biodiversity.
