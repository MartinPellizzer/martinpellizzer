Potentilla passifloca

Potentilla passifloca, commonly known as the pink potentilla, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit. The plant produces showy, pink to reddish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are typically oval-shaped with a slightly serrated edge, and the plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight.

Potentilla passifloca is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
