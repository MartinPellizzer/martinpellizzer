Poa gracillima

Poa gracillima, commonly known as slender meadow grass, is a low-growing perennial grass species native to southern South America.

It typically forms dense, mat-like clumps with slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spikelet-bearing inflorescences that are often inconspicuous, contributing to its ability to thrive in open, disturbed habitats. It is adapted to a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions.

Poa gracillima plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small invertebrates in its native environment.
