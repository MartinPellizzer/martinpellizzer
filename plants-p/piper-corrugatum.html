Piper corrugatum

Piper corrugatum, commonly known as the zigzag pepper, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species. The plant is characterized by its finely toothed leaves and small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a small, dark purple drupe that contains a single seed.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and for its use in traditional medicine.
