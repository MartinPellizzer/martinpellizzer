Potentilla verna var. minima

Potentilla verna var. minima, commonly known as the dwarf cinquefoil, is a low-growing perennial plant native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It typically reaches heights of no more than 10 centimeters, with small, rounded leaves arranged in a compact rosette. The plant produces clusters of bright yellow flowers in early spring, which are borne on slender, upright stems. Its foliage is covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive texture.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens and alpine settings.
