Phyllorkis angustifolia

Phyllorkis angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved phyllorkis, is a perennial herb native to Australia.

It features slender, upright stems and linear, grass-like leaves that are typically narrow and pointed. The plant produces small, white or pale blue flowers arranged in spikes, which bloom during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or grassy areas.

Phyllorkis angustifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
