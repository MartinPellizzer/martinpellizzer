Plectranthus riparius

Plectranthus riparius, commonly known as the African violet, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, velvety foliage and clusters of small, tubular flowers that bloom in shades of purple, blue, and white. The plant typically grows as a shrub or subshrub, reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters. It prefers well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
