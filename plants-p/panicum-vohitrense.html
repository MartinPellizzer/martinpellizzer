Panicum vohitrense

Panicum vohitrense, commonly known as the Madagascar grass, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments. It belongs to the grass family, Poaceae, and is often used in traditional landscaping due to its ornamental value.

Its botanical features make it a unique species within the Panicum genus.
