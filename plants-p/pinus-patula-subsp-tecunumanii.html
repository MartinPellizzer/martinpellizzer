Pinus patula subsp. tecunumanii

Pinus patula subsp.

tecunumanii, commonly known as the Mexican white pine, is a subspecies native to central Mexico. It is a medium-sized evergreen conifer with slender, drooping branches and needle-like leaves.

The cones are typically small and woody, containing two to three seeds. This subspecies is characterized by its rapid growth and is often used in reforestation efforts.

Its botanical features make it well-adapted to a variety of soil types and climates.
