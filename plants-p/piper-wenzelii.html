Piper wenzelii

Piper wenzelii, commonly known as Wenzel's pepper, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper plants such as black pepper. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into elongated, three-lobed berries. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped with a glossy surface and a fragrant aroma.

The plant is often grown for its ornamental value and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
