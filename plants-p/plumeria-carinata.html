Plumeria carinata

Plumeria carinata, commonly known as the sacred frangipani, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink and yellow. The plant has palm-like leaves that are opposite and deeply veined. Its flowers bloom in clusters at the ends of branches, often in the summer months.

Plumeria carinata is widely cultivated for ornamental purposes and holds cultural significance in various regions.
