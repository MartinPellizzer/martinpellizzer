Papaver somniferum var. luteum

Papaver somniferum var. luteum, commonly known as the Turkish poppy, is a variant of the opium poppy characterized by its bright yellow flowers.

It is native to the eastern Mediterranean and thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil. The plant produces capsule-like fruits that contain latex, which can be processed to extract alkaloids such as morphine and codeine. Its botanical features include showy, cup-shaped petals and a taproot system.

Due to its association with opium production, it is regulated in many countries.
