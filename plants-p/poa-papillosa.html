Poa papillosa

Poa papillosa, commonly known as the silky bluegrass, is a cool-season grass native to North America.

It is characterized by its fine, bluish-green foliage and dense, tufted growth habit. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and grasslands.

Poa papillosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to form dense turf in suitable conditions.
