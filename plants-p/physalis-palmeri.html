Physalis palmeri

Physalis palmeri, commonly known as the desert tomatillo, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its inflated, spiny calyx that encloses the fruit. The plant produces small, yellowish-orange berries that are edible when fully ripe. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in sandy soils.
