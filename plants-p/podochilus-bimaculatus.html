Podochilus bimaculatus

Podochilus bimaculatus, commonly known as the two-spotted orchid, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact, epiphytic growth habit. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink, often with two distinct dark spots on the petals. Its leaves are narrow and strap-like, adapted for climbing and photosynthesis in shaded environments.

Podochilus bimaculatus is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ease of cultivation.
