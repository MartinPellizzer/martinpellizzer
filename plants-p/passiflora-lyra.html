Passiflora lyra

Passiflora lyra, commonly known as the giant sweet William or the sacred lotus of the Andes, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and striking, intricate flowers that display a mix of blue, purple, and white hues. The plant produces edible, fleshy berries that are often consumed raw or used in culinary applications. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Passiflora lyra is also valued for its ornamental beauty and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
