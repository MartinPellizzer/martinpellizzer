Pleurothallis calvariola

Pleurothallis calvariola, commonly known as the Calvaria orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a small, epiphytic orchid that thrives in humid, shaded environments within the canopy of rainforests. The plant produces delicate, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pale pink, often arranged in clusters. Its roots are adapted to absorb moisture and nutrients from the air and surrounding substrates.

Pleurothallis calvariola is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
