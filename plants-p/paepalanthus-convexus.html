Paepalanthus convexus

Paepalanthus convexus, commonly known as the cup-like cactus, is a unique species native to South America, particularly in regions of Brazil, Bolivia, and Paraguay.

It is characterized by its spherical, ribbed stem that resembles a cup or bowl, which is adapted to store water in arid environments. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom during the rainy season, adding a striking visual contrast to its green, spiny structure. Its leaves are modified into spines, reducing water loss and deterring herbivores.

Paepalanthus convexus is often found in rocky or sandy soils, thriving in full sun and exhibiting a slow growth rate.
