Papaya rochefortii

Papaya rochefortii, commonly known as the rock papaya, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and produces yellow-orange fruits with a distinctive, sweet flavor. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth. Its fruit is often used in culinary applications, while the leaves are also consumed as a vegetable.

Papaya rochefortii is valued for its nutritional content and adaptability to various growing conditions.
