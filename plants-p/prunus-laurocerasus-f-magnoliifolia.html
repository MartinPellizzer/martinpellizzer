Prunus laurocerasus f. magnoliifolia

Prunus laurocerasus f. magnoliifolia, commonly known as the Variegated Cherry Laurel, is a decorative shrub characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves with striking white and yellow variegation.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is native to the western Mediterranean region. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers in spring, followed by dark blue berries that are edible but not particularly flavorful.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for hedges and garden landscapes.
