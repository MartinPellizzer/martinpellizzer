Plumeria obtusa var. typica

Plumeria obtusa var. typica, commonly known as the frangipani, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, waxy flowers that come in a variety of colors including white, yellow, and pink. The plant has simple, opposite, and ovate leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. Its bark is smooth and light gray, and it produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is widely used in floral arrangements and traditional ceremonies.
