Polyspora multinervis

Polyspora multinervis, commonly known as the net-veined moss, is a species of moss belonging to the family Pottiaceae.

It is characterized by its dense, cushion-like growth form and intricate, net-like vein patterns in its leaves. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically found in forests and rocky areas. Its spore capsules are borne on short, erect stalks and are often surrounded by a peristome, a structure that aids in spore dispersal.

Polyspora multinervis plays a vital role in soil stabilization and moisture retention in its native habitats.
