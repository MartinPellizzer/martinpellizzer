Plantago pachyphylla var. muscicola

Plantago pachyphylla var. muscicola, commonly known as the thick-leaved plantain, is a perennial herb native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that store water, an adaptation to its harsh, dry environment. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in a dense spike, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its resilience, it is sometimes used in xeriscaping and as a ground cover in drought-prone areas.
