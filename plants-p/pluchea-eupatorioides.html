Pluchea eupatorioides

Pluchea eupatorioides, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features showy, blue-violet flowers that bloom in the summer, attracting pollinators such as butterflies and bees. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves that are often covered in a soft, downy texture. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Pluchea eupatorioides is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native plant gardens.
