Potentilla erecta f. stenopetala

Potentilla erecta f. stenopetala, commonly known as the narrow-petaled erect cinquefoil, is a flowering plant characterized by its compact growth habit and dense foliage.

It features small, five-lobed leaves that are typically arranged in a spiral pattern along the stems. The flowers are usually white or pale yellow, with five petals and numerous stamens, contributing to its ornamental appeal. This variety is native to regions of Europe and Asia, often found in rocky or sandy soils.

It is valued in gardens for its hardiness and ability to thrive in full sun to partial shade.
