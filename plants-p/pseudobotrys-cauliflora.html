Pseudobotrys cauliflora

Pseudobotrys cauliflora, commonly known as the cauliflower orchid, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique inflorescence, where flowers and buds develop directly on the stems, a trait known as cauliflory. The plant produces striking white or pinkish flowers that bloom in clusters, often resembling cauliflower. Its leaves are broad and glossy, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Pseudobotrys cauliflora thrives in warm, humid environments and is often cultivated for its aesthetic value in gardens and greenhouses.
