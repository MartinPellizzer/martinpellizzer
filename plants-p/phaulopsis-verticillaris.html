Phaulopsis verticillaris

Phaulopsis verticillaris, commonly known as the whorled false nettle, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, shaded areas such as woodland floors and stream banks. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters. Its root system is fibrous, and it is often found growing in colonies.
