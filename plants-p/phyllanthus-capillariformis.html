Phyllanthus capillariformis

Phyllanthus capillariformis, commonly known as the hairy phyllanthus, is a flowering plant belonging to the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia, including India, China, and Southeast Asia. The plant is characterized by its small, clustered leaves and yellowish-green flowers that are often surrounded by bracts. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

The species is noted for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional medicine for various health purposes.
