Pandanus nakanaiensis

Pandanus nakanaiensis, commonly known as the Nakanai pandanus, is a species of flowering plant in the Pandanaceae family.

It is native to the Pacific islands, particularly the Solomon Islands and Papua New Guinea. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender stems and long, narrow leaves that are often used in traditional crafts. It produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically clustered in branched inflorescences.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in local cultures.
