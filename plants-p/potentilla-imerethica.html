Potentilla imerethica

Potentilla imerethica, commonly known as the Armenian cinquefoil, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus and Central Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its dense, sprawling growth habit and small, bright yellow flowers. The plant typically thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats. Its leaves are palmately lobed, and the plant is known for its hardy nature and ability to withstand harsh climatic conditions.

Potentilla imerethica is also valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional uses in herbal medicine.
