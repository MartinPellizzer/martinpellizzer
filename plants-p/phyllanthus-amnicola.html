Phyllanthus amnicola

Phyllanthus amnicola, commonly known as the Himalayan spurge, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayan regions of India and Nepal.

It is a shrub that typically grows in rocky and mountainous areas, often at high altitudes. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and usually have a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

Phyllanthus amnicola is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional Ayurvedic medicine.
