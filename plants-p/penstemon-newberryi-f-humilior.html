Penstemon newberryi f. humilior

Penstemon newberryi f. humilior, commonly known as the lowland penstemon, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, tubular flowers. The plant typically produces blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds.
