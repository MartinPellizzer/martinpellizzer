Polycarpaea arida

Polycarpaea arida, commonly known as the desert milkweed, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of Africa and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy stems and leaves that help it conserve water in harsh environments. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pale yellow or greenish-white, adapted for pollination by insects. Its milky sap, which is toxic to some animals, serves as a defense mechanism against herbivores.

Polycarpaea arida plays a role in stabilizing sandy soils and providing limited forage for certain wildlife.
