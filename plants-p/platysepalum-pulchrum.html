Platysepalum pulchrum

Platysepalum pulchrum, commonly known as the velvetleaf orchid, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its striking, velvety leaves and showy inflorescences. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte or terrestrial herb, often found in humid tropical environments. Its flowers are usually pale yellow to greenish-white, with a distinctive three-lobed lip.

Platysepalum pulchrum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in botanical gardens and private collections.
