Primula × tristis

Primula × tristis, commonly known as the hybrid primrose, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe.

It typically grows to a height of 15–30 cm, featuring basal rosettes of broadly ovate, hairy leaves. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped, tubular flowers in shades of white, pink, or purple, which bloom in early spring. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in woodland or shaded garden settings.

Primula × tristis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in horticulture for its delicate floral display.
