Primula falcifolia

Primula falcifolia, commonly known as the sickleleaf primrose, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, elongated, sickle-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or white in color. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in shaded forest habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and unique foliage, it is cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
