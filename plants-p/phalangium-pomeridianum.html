Phalangium pomeridianum

Phalangium pomeridianum, commonly known as the afternoon phalangium, is a rare flowering plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, clustered flowers that bloom in shades of yellow and orange. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous minute seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sunlight to flourish.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species.
