Pharseophora involucrata

Pharseophora involucrata, commonly known as the purple coneflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It features showy, tubular violet-blue flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. The plant has alternate, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its root system consists of fibrous roots that help stabilize the soil.

Pharseophora involucrata is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
