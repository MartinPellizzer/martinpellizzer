Polygonatum agglutinatum

Polygonatum agglutinatum, commonly known as the sticky Solomon's seal, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Asparagaceae family and is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit. The plant features arching stems with narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. Its berries are typically red or yellow and contain several seeds.

Polygonatum agglutinatum is often found in woodland areas and is valued for its ornamental and medicinal properties.
