Prunus incisa f. tokinosu

Prunus incisa f. tokinosu, commonly known as the Tokinoshu cherry, is a compact, ornamental shrub native to Japan.

It features small, oval leaves that are glossy and dark green, with a slight serration along the edges. The plant produces delicate, pinkish-white flowers in early spring, which are followed by small, red fruits. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it suitable for garden borders or containers.

Its compact size and ornamental foliage make it a popular choice for ornamental horticulture.
