Piper tutuilae

Piper tutuilae, commonly known as the Vanuatu pepper, is a flowering plant native to the islands of Vanuatu in the South Pacific.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper plants such as black pepper. The plant is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and heart-shaped leaves with a slightly serrated edge. Its small, greenish-white flowers are followed by small, round berries that mature to a dark purple color.

The fruit is used in traditional medicine and as a spice, reflecting its cultural and economic significance in the region.
