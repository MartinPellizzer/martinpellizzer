Pandanus androcephalanthos

Pandanus androcephalanthos, commonly known as the narrow-leaved pandanus, is a species native to the Indo-Pacific region.

It is a flowering plant belonging to the pandan family, Pandanaceae, and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and long, narrow leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically yellow or greenish in color. It thrives in coastal and wetland environments, often growing in shallow water or on sandy soils.

Its unique appearance and adaptability make it a notable species in tropical ecosystems.
