Potentilla aurea f. minor

Potentilla aurea f. minor, commonly known as the dwarf gold cinquefoil, is a compact, low-growing shrub native to North America.

It features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer, typically from June to July. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves that are covered in a soft, silvery down, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a ground cover.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and ease of cultivation in gardens.
