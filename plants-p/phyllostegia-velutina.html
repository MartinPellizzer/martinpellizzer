Phyllostegia velutina

Phyllostegia velutina, commonly known as velvetleaf phyllodinea, is a perennial shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves that are covered in a soft, velvety pubescence, giving it its common name. Its flowers are typically pink to purple, arranged in whorled clusters, and bloom in the summer months.

Phyllostegia velutina is often found in moist, disturbed soils along streambanks and roadsides.
