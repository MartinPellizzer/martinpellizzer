Polygonum lapathifolium

Polygonum lapathifolium, commonly known as heartleaf smartweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to North America.

It features lance-shaped leaves that are often tinged with red or purple at the base, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in late summer. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in marshy areas or along stream banks.

Polygonum lapathifolium is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in wetland restoration projects.
