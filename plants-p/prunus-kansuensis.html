Prunus kansuensis

Prunus kansuensis, commonly known as the Kansu cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its slender branches and clusters of white or pink flowers. The plant produces small, dark red to black berries that are edible but often have a tart flavor. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive spring blossoms.

The leaves are simple, alternate, and turn yellow in autumn, contributing to its seasonal aesthetic.
