Petaloma mouriri

Petaloma mouriri, commonly known as the Brazilian starfruit tree, is a tropical plant native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its small, star-shaped fruits that resemble miniature starfruits. The plant has simple, alternate leaves with a smooth, waxy surface. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The fruit is edible, though its flavor is more tart than that of the common starfruit.
