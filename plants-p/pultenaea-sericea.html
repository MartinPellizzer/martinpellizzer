Pultenaea sericea

Pultenaea sericea, commonly known as silky pultenaea, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is a shrub that typically grows in heathlands and dry sclerophyll forests. The plant is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and small, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs. Its flowers are usually pale yellow or cream, arranged in clusters, and bloom during the spring and summer seasons.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and flowers.
