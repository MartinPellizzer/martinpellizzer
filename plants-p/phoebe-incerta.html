Phoebe incerta

Phoebe incerta, commonly known as the Florida phoebe, is a small evergreen shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in wetland areas, such as swamps and marshes, where it thrives in moist, acidic soils. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are elliptical in shape and often have a bluish-green hue. Its small, white flowers appear in clusters and are followed by dark blue berries that are attractive to birds.

Phoebe incerta is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
