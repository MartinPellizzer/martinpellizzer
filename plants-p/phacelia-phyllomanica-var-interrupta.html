Phacelia phyllomanica var. interrupta

Phacelia phyllomanica var. interrupta, commonly known as the Sierra phacelia, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats such as slopes and meadows. It is a perennial species that thrives in full sun and well-drained soils.

Its botanical features make it an important part of local ecosystems and a subject of interest in botanical studies.
