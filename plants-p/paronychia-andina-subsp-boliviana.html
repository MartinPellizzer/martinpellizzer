Paronychia andina subsp. boliviana

Paronychia andina subsp.

boliviana, commonly known as the Andean mouse-ear cress, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America. It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many economically important crops such as mustard and cabbage.

The plant is characterized by its small, basal rosette of fleshy, hairy leaves and produces clusters of tiny, yellow flowers. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky soils.
