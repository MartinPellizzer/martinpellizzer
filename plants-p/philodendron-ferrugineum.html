Philodendron ferrugineum

Philodendron ferrugineum, commonly known as the rusty-veined philodendron, is a tropical climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It features heart-shaped, glossy leaves with distinctive rust-colored veins, giving it its common name. The plant has a robust climbing habit, using aerial roots to attach to supports. It thrives in warm, humid environments with bright, indirect light.

Its foliage makes it a popular choice for indoor gardening due to its ornamental appeal.
