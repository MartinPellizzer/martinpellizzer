Panicum hordeoides

Panicum hordeoides, commonly known as broomgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the southern United States.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, drooping inflorescences that resemble a broom. The plant produces small, spikelet-based flowers that are typically wind-pollinated. Broomgrass is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in full sun conditions.

Its fibrous roots and dense growth make it a valuable plant for erosion control and wildlife habitat.
