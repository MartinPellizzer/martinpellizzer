Protea bruniodes

Protea bruniodes, commonly known as the yellow protea, is a striking South African plant belonging to the Proteaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescence composed of numerous white to yellow flowers arranged in a cylindrical shape. The plant is native to the Western and Eastern Cape provinces, where it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun. Its unique floral structure attracts pollinators such as birds and insects, playing a vital role in its ecosystem.

Due to its ornamental value, it is also cultivated in gardens and botanical collections worldwide.
