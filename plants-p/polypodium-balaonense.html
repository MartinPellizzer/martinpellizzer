Polypodium balaonense

Polypodium balaonense, commonly known as the Balaon fern, is a species of polypodioid fern native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its evergreen, leathery fronds that are typically divided into lanceolate segments. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories where it attaches to rocks or tree trunks. Its spore-producing structures, called sori, are clustered on the underside of the fronds, contributing to its reproductive strategy.

This fern is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in the ecological balance of its native habitat.
