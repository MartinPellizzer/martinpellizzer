Phyllostegia lantanoides var. konahuanuiana

Phyllostegia lantanoides var. konahuanuiana, commonly known as the Hawaiian flame tree, is a shrub or small tree native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It is characterized by its opposite, elliptical leaves that are often red-veined and have a distinctive waxy coating. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink, followed by orange or red fruits that split open to release seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in coastal areas or disturbed soils.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
