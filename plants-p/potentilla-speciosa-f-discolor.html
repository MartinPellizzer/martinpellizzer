Potentilla speciosa f. discolor

Potentilla speciosa f. discolor, commonly known as the white-flowered potentilla, is a variant of the showy potentilla, characterized by its pale or white flowers that contrast with its dark green foliage.

This shrub is native to the western United States, particularly in regions with rocky soils and sunny exposures. It features small, rounded leaves and produces clusters of five-petaled flowers in early summer. The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.

Botanically, it belongs to the Rosaceae family and is known for its hardy, drought-tolerant nature.
