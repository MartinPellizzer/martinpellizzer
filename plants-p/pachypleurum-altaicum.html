Pachypleurum altaicum

Pachypleurum altaicum, commonly known as the highland bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the alpine regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, sturdy stems that can reach up to 1.5 meters in height. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped flowers that are typically blue or purple in color, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, with a slightly hairy texture.

Pachypleurum altaicum is adapted to cold climates and is often found in rocky or gravelly soils at high elevations.
