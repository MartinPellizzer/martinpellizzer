Primula tenella var. flagellaris

Primula tenella var. flagellaris, commonly known as the flagellate primrose, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features a low-growing rosette of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and requires full to partial sunlight.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is cultivated in gardens and rockeries in temperate climates.
