Picrasma quassioides var. dasycarpa

Picrasma quassioides var. dasycarpa, commonly known as the rough-fruited quassia, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with serrated edges and yellowish-green foliage. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by woody, spiny fruits containing several seeds. It is often found in humid, lowland forests and is valued for its medicinal properties.

The bark and roots contain compounds used in traditional medicine for their anti-inflammatory and antimalarial effects.
