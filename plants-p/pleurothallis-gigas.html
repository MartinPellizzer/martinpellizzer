Pleurothallis gigas

Pleurothallis gigas, commonly known as the giant pleurothallis, is a species of orchid native to the cloud forests of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its long, slender stems and large, showy flowers that can reach up to 10 centimeters in length. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees and absorbing moisture and nutrients from the air. Its flowers are often fragrant and come in a variety of colors, including white, yellow, and pink.

Pleurothallis gigas is valued for its ornamental qualities and is relatively easy to cultivate under the right conditions.
