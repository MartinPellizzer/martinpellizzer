Podochilus crotalinus

Podochilus crotalinus, commonly known as the Crotalaria orchid, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Orchidaceae and is characterized by its striking, often brightly colored flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte or terrestrial species, with fleshy leaves and a compact, branching structure. Its flowers are usually borne in clusters and feature a unique lip that aids in pollination by insects.

Podochilus crotalinus is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
