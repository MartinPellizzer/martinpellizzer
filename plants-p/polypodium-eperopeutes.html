Polypodium eperopeutes

Polypodium eperopeutes, commonly known as the Eperopeutes fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Polypodiaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped fronds that are typically bi- or tri-pinnate, giving it a finely divided appearance. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its rhizome is slender and creeping, allowing it to spread along the forest floor.

The spores are produced on the underside of the fronds in clusters, contributing to its reproductive strategy.
