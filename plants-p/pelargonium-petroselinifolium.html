Pelargonium petroselinifolium

Pelargonium petroselinifolium, commonly known as the parsley-leaved geranium, is a species of flowering plant in the family Geraniaceae.

It is native to South Africa and is characterized by its aromatic, parsley-like leaves that are finely divided and fern-like in appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, pink to red flowers that bloom in summer. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and ability to thrive in a variety of conditions.

Pelargonium petroselinifolium is also used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
