Passiflora piligera

Passiflora piligera, commonly known as the silky passionflower, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, intricate flowers that feature a mix of blue, purple, and white petals, along with prominent red filaments. The plant produces edible berries that are often consumed raw or used in jams and beverages. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Passiflora piligera is also valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
