Pseudobombax septenatum

Pseudobombax septenatum, commonly known as the Mexican trumpet tree, is a tropical flowering plant native to Central America and parts of South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically pink or red in color. The plant produces woody, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in lowland forests and along riverbanks.

The leaves are alternate, simple, and have a slightly hairy surface, contributing to its distinctive botanical appearance.
