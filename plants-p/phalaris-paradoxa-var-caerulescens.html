Phalaris paradoxa var. caerulescens

Phalaris paradoxa var. caerulescens, commonly known as the blue false brome, is a perennial grass native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue-green foliage and slender, spike-like inflorescences that appear in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and restoration projects.
