Prunus × lannesiana f. lucida

Prunus × lannesiana f. lucida, commonly known as the white-flowered cherry, is a botanical variant of the Prunus genus.

It is characterized by its striking white petals that contrast with its pinkish-brown bark. This plant is typically found in temperate regions and thrives in well-drained soil. It produces small, edible fruits that are often used in jams and preserves.

The plant is valued for its ornamental beauty and is commonly planted in gardens and parks.
