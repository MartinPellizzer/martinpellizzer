Parathesis rekoi

Parathesis rekoi, commonly known as the golden mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, daisy-like flowers that bloom in clusters, often in shades of yellow and white. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem, and it thrives in moist, shady environments. Parathesis rekoi is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a decorative plant in indoor settings.

Its botanical features make it a unique addition to both natural and cultivated landscapes.
