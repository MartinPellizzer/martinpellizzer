Prunus itosakura f. rosea

Prunus itosakura f. rosea, commonly known as the pink-flowered Japanese apricot, is a cultivar of the Prunus itosakura species, which is native to Japan.

This ornamental plant is characterized by its showy, single or semi-double pink flowers that bloom in early spring before the leaves emerge. The foliage is typically dark green, providing a striking contrast to the pale pink blossoms. It is a compact shrub or small tree, often grown for its aesthetic value in gardens and landscapes.

The plant is relatively low maintenance and thrives in well-drained soil with full sun exposure.
