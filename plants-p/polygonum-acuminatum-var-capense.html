Polygonum acuminatum var. capense

Polygonum acuminatum var. capense, commonly known as Cape knotweed, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in moist, sandy soils along riverbanks and in coastal areas. The plant features slender, ascending stems with opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are often hairy. Its flowers are small, clustered in dense, cylindrical spikes, and are usually pink to reddish in color.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
