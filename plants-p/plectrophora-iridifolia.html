Plectrophora iridifolia

Plectrophora iridifolia, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, blue-violet flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are arranged in a basal rosette, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Plectrophora iridifolia is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
