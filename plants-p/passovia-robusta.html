Passovia robusta

Passovia robusta, commonly known as the robust passionflower, is a climbing vine native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and showy, fragrant flowers that resemble those of the passionfruit. The plant produces bright red or orange flowers with intricate patterns, often featuring a central crown-like structure. Its fruits are typically fleshy and edible, though they are less commonly consumed than those of other passionflower species.

Passovia robusta thrives in warm, humid climates and requires support for its vigorous growth.
