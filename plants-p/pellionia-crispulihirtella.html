Pellionia crispulihirtella

Pellionia crispulihirtella, commonly known as the woolly pellionia, is a low-growing herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It features small, ovate leaves that are covered in fine, velvety hairs, giving it a soft, fuzzy appearance. The plant produces clusters of tiny, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in the spring and summer months. Pellionia crispulihirtella thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its unique foliage and delicate flowers make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening in shaded areas.
