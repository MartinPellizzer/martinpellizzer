Paraphlomis oblongifolia

Paraphlomis oblongifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved blue flag, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of China.

It features rhizomatous growth and lanceolate to ovate leaves that are typically dark green and slightly hairy. The plant produces small, blue to purple flowers arranged in spikes, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
