Pelargonium × lateripes

Pelargonium × lateripes, commonly known as the zonal geranium, is a hybrid plant in the Geraniaceae family.

It is characterized by its velvety, fuzzy leaves that often display distinct color zones, typically in shades of green, silver, and pink. The plant produces showy, clustered flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, which bloom throughout the growing season. Native to South Africa, it is widely cultivated as an ornamental plant for its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to various growing conditions.

Its compact growth habit makes it suitable for container gardening and garden beds.
