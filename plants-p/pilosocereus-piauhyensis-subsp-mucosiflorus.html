Pilosocereus piauhyensis subsp. mucosiflorus

Pilosocereus piauhyensis subsp.

mucosiflorus, commonly known as the Piauhyensis Cereus, is a species of cactus native to Brazil. It is characterized by its cylindrical stem that is often covered in white or pale spines.

The plant produces large, showy flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, requiring well-drained soil and full sun exposure.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and cactus enthusiasts alike.
