Pennisetum nigricans

Pennisetum nigricans, commonly known as black beard grass, is a perennial grass native to Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and dense, dark purple to black inflorescences that give it its striking appearance. The plant typically grows in open, sunny areas and is often found in disturbed soils. It has a fibrous root system and produces small, spike-like clusters of flowers.

Pennisetum nigricans is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping and erosion control.
