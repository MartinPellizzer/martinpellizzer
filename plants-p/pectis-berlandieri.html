Pectis berlandieri

Pectis berlandieri, commonly known as the desert dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing adaptations to drought and heat. Pectis berlandieri is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in rocky or sandy habitats.
