Panicum helopus

Panicum helopus, commonly known as the narrowleaf panicgrass, is a species of grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, sandy soils along stream banks and wetlands. It has a fibrous root system and is often found in disturbed or open habitats.

Panicum helopus is valued for its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife species.
