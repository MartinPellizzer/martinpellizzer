Phaseolus ricciardianus var. macrocarpus

Phaseolus ricciardianus var. macrocarpus, commonly known as the giant scarlet runner bean, is a climbing legume native to South America.

It produces large, vibrant red pods that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets and produces showy yellow flowers. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and edible beans, which are rich in nutrients.
