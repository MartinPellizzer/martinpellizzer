Poa capillaris

Poa capillaris, commonly known as slender meadow grass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows, marshes, and along stream banks. The plant has slender, tufted culms with fine, soft leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of hairs. Its inflorescence consists of a narrow panicle bearing small, spikelet-like flowers.

Poa capillaris plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
