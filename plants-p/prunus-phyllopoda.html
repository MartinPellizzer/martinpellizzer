Prunus phyllopoda

Prunus phyllopoda, commonly known as the desert peach, is a small, deciduous shrub native to arid regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, branching structure and small, elliptical leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink, typically blooming in spring. Its fruit, a small, yellowish drupe, is edible but not as widely cultivated as other peach species.

Prunus phyllopoda is well adapted to drought conditions, making it a resilient plant in harsh environments.
