Pseudovigna

Pseudovigna, commonly known as the cluster bean or garden cowpea, is a genus of flowering plants in the legume family, Fabaceae.

These plants are characterized by their trifoliate leaves and small, pod-like fruits that contain edible seeds. Pseudovigna species are often cultivated for their seeds, which are used in various cuisines around the world. The plants are also valued for their ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making them beneficial in crop rotation.

They thrive in warm climates and are relatively drought-tolerant, making them suitable for a variety of agricultural settings.
