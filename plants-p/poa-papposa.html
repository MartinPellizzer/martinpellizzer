Poa papposa

Poa papposa, commonly known as the silky bluegrass, is a perennial grass native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It is characterized by its fine, blue-green blades and tufted seed heads that give it a fluffy appearance. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and grasslands. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, contributing to its distinctive look.

Poa papposa is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
