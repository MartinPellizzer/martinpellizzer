Piper barbinerve

Piper barbinerve, commonly known as the narrow-veined pepper vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes various species of pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its slender, twining stems and small, opposite leaves with prominently veined undersides. Its flowers are unisexual, with separate male and female flowers on the same plant, and it produces small, berry-like fruits.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in some regions.
