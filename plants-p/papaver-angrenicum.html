Papaver angrenicum

Papaver angrenicum, commonly known as the Angren poppy, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Papaveraceae family and is characterized by its striking red or orange petals and a prominent black or brown central stigma. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is known for its ornamental value. It produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species in some regions.
