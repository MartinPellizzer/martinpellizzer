Piper abditum

Piper abditum, commonly known as the hidden pepper, is a tropical vine native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many species of pepper plants. The plant produces small, green berries that turn red when mature, containing the aromatic seeds used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are often used in herbal remedies due to their medicinal properties.

The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is characterized by its climbing habit and distinctive foliage.
