Plantago × mixta

Plantago × mixta, commonly known as the hybrid plantain, is a perennial herbaceous plant that belongs to the Plantaginaceae family.

It is a natural hybrid between Plantago lanceolata and Plantago ovata, characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves with prominent veins. The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in dense spikes, which are typically wind-pollinated. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their mucilage content, which has soothing properties.

Plantago × mixta is commonly found in disturbed soils and is adaptable to various environmental conditions.
