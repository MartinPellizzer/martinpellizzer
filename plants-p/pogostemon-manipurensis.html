Pogostemon manipurensis

Pogostemon manipurensis, commonly known as the Manipur pogostemon, is a flowering plant native to the northeastern region of India.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream sides. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a distinct mint-like fragrance.

Pogostemon manipurensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has potential medicinal uses in traditional medicine.
