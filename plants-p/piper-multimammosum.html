Piper multimammosum

Piper multimammosum, commonly known as the mammillary pepper, is a tropical climbing vine native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many pepper species. The plant is characterized by its small, clustered flowers and distinctive, fleshy, mammillary ( nipple-like) fruits. Its leaves are typically alternate and ovate, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in local medicine.
