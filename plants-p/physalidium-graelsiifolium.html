Physalidium graelsiifolium

Physalidium graelsiifolium, commonly known as the leafy bladderpod, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Brassicaceae.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and alternate, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are often glandular-dotted. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in racemes, which are followed by inflated, bladder-like fruits that contain several seeds. Native to southern Africa, it thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats.

Its unique morphology and ecological adaptations make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
