Probletostemon elliottii

Probletostemon elliottii, commonly known as Elliott's honeysuckle, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 3 to 6 feet, featuring opposite, elliptic leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. The plant produces fragrant, tubular flowers in shades of yellow to orange, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its fruit is a small, red berry that ripens in the fall and is an important food source for birds and small mammals.

The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and wetlands.
