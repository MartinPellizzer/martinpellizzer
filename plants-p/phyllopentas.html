Phyllopentas

Phyllopentas, commonly known as the five-leaf plant, is a unique species characterized by its distinctive leaf morphology.

The plant features leaves that are divided into five distinct lobes, giving it a striking, almost symmetrical appearance. It belongs to the family Phyllopentaceae and is native to specific regions in Southeast Asia. The leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Its botanical structure makes it an interesting subject for both horticultural and scientific study.
