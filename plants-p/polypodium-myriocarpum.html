Polypodium myriocarpum

Polypodium myriocarpum, commonly known as the many-seeded polypody, is a fern belonging to the family Polypodiaceae.

It is characterized by its finely divided, feather-like fronds that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces small, clustered sporangia on the underside of its leaves, which are responsible for spore dispersal. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Polypodium myriocarpum is valued for its ornamental appearance and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
