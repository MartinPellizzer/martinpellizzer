Pseudocaryophyllus uniflorus

Pseudocaryophyllus uniflorus, commonly known as the single-flower caryophyllaceae, is a flowering plant belonging to the Caryophyllaceae family.

It is characterized by its simple, erect growth habit and produces small, clustered flowers with five petals. The plant typically has smooth, fleshy leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and specialized species within its native range.
