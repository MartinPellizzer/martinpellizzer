Pleurothallis nymphalis

Pleurothallis nymphalis, commonly known as the moon orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that often resemble miniature orchids. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, with a unique, almost lunar appearance that gives it its common name.

Pleurothallis nymphalis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
