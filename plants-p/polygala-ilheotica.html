Polygala ilheotica

Polygala ilheotica, commonly known as the Ilheus polygala, is a flowering plant native to the island of Ilheus in Brazil.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, delicate flowers. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and is adapted to sandy soils and saline conditions. Its flowers are usually white or pale blue, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits.

Due to its restricted distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
