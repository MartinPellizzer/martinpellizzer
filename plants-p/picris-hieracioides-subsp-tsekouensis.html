Picris hieracioides subsp. tsekouensis

Picris hieracioides subsp.

tsekouensis, commonly known as the Tsekouen hawk's-beard, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the eastern Mediterranean. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets.

The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, thriving in dry and sunny environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and hairy, while the flowers are yellow with a distinctive central disk.

This subspecies is noted for its ornamental value and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.
