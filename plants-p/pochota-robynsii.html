Pochota robynsii

Pochota robynsii, commonly known as the Mexican chocolate tree, is a member of the Fabaceae family and is native to Mexico.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 15 meters tall, featuring compound leaves and yellow flowers. The tree produces pods containing edible seeds, which are used to make a type of chocolate. Its botanical characteristics include a woody structure and the ability to thrive in tropical and subtropical climates.

Pochota robynsii is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
