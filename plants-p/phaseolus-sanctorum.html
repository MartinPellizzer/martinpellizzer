Phaseolus sanctorum

Phaseolus sanctorum, commonly known as the sacred bean, is a species of flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect, branching stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that give way to elongated pods containing kidney-shaped seeds. Its botanical significance lies in its traditional use in religious and ceremonial contexts by indigenous communities.

The plant is also valued for its nutritional content, including protein and essential minerals.
