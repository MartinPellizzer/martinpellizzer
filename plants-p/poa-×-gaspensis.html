Poa × gaspensis

Poa × gaspensis, commonly known as the Gaspé bluegrass, is a hybrid grass species native to eastern Canada.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted foliage and a bluish-green coloration due to its dense, compact growth habit. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and along stream banks. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny, wind-dispersed seeds.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and restoration projects.
