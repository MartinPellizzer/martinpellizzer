Pappostipa nana

Pappostipa nana, commonly known as the dwarf pappus grass, is a small perennial grass native to the northern regions of North America.

It typically grows in wet, marshy environments and is often found in coastal areas. The plant has slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, wind-dispersed seeds. Its flowering period occurs in the summer months, and it is characterized by its compact growth habit.

Pappostipa nana plays a role in stabilizing soil in wetland ecosystems.
