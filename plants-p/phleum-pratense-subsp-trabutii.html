Phleum pratense subsp. trabutii

Phleum pratense subsp.

trabutii, commonly known as the Trabut's timothy, is a perennial grass species native to southern Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands.

Its botanical features make it an important component of grassland ecosystems.
