Panicum furcellatum

Panicum furcellatum, commonly known as the narrowleaf panicgrass, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and narrow, linear leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and disturbed areas.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
