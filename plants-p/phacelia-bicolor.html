Phacelia bicolor

Phacelia bicolor, commonly known as the twinflower phacelia, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its striking blue and white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes. It has hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often tinged with purple.

Phacelia bicolor is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
