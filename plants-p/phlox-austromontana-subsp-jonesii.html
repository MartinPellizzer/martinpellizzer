Phlox austromontana subsp. jonesii

Phlox austromontana subsp.

jonesii, commonly known as Jones' phlox, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its showy, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink and are typically borne on slender, erect stems.

The plant has lance-shaped to oval leaves that are often hairy, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and native plant landscapes.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and horticultural use in arid regions.
