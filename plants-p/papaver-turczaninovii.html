Papaver turczaninovii

Papaver turczaninovii, commonly known as Turczaninov's poppy, is a species of flowering plant in the family Papaveraceae.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, particularly in Kazakhstan and Kyrgyzstan. The plant is characterized by its striking, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from red to orange, often with a contrasting black or dark center. It produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, dark seeds.

Papaver turczaninovii is typically found growing in rocky or alpine environments, where it thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight.
