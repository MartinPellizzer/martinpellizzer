Pyrus communis subsp. cordata

Pyrus communis subsp.

cordata, commonly known as the heartleaf pear, is a subspecies of the common pear characterized by its distinctive heart-shaped leaves. It is native to Central Asia and has been cultivated for its ornamental value and edible fruit.

The leaves are typically darker green and have a more pronounced heart shape compared to the common pear. It produces small, yellow fruits that are generally not as flavorful as those of the common pear.

This subspecies is often grown in gardens for its aesthetic appeal and hardy nature.
