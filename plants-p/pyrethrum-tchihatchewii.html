Pyrethrum tchihatchewii

Pyrethrum tchihatchewii, commonly known as the Tchihatchew pyrethrum, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its daisy-like flowers with yellow centers and white petals. The plant produces fine, hair-like structures called pharagocysts, which contain natural insecticides known as pyrethrins. These compounds make it highly effective against a variety of pests, making the plant valuable in organic pest control.

It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.
