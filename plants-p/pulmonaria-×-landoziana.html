Pulmonaria × landoziana

Pulmonaria × landoziana, commonly known as the woodland lungwort, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Pulmonaria officinalis and Pulmonaria grandiflora.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe, characterized by its basal rosette of velvety, lobed leaves. The plant produces showy, clustered flowers in shades of pink, purple, and white, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in moist, shady woodland habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.

Pulmonaria × landoziana is also noted for its ability to naturalize and form dense colonies over time.
