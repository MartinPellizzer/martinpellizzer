Populus nigra var. sinensis

Populus nigra var. sinensis, commonly known as the Chinese black poplar, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It belongs to the Salicaceae family and is characterized by its tall, straight trunk and broad, heart-shaped leaves. The tree produces small, fluffy seeds that are dispersed by the wind, aiding in its spread. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found near rivers and lakes.

The bark is dark and fissured, providing a distinctive appearance throughout the year.
