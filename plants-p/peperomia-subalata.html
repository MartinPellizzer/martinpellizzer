Peperomia subalata

Peperomia subalata, commonly known as the silver peperomia, is a compact, evergreen perennial plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It features glossy, ovate leaves with a striking silvery-white to pale green coloration on the upper surface, often contrasting with a darker green underside. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers that appear in clusters during the spring and summer months. It is well-suited for indoor cultivation due to its low light requirements and minimal water needs.

Peperomia subalata thrives in well-draining soil and prefers moderate to high humidity for optimal growth.
