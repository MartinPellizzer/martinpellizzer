Pelargonium × reformatum

Pelargonium × reformatum, commonly known as the zonal geranium, is a hybrid shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its velvety, fuzzy leaves and showy, double flowers in a range of colors including red, pink, and white. The plant is widely cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a potted plant. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Pelargonium × reformatum is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
